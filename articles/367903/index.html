Why do kids and adults mock robots?

A group of Japanese researchers from Ryukoku University, Tokai University and the ATR Intelligent Robotics and Communication Laboratories Robotics Laboratories investigated child aggression toward a humanoid robot in a supermarket ( pdf ). During the observations, the following behavior of the children was noted: they blocked the path of the robot, covered his eyes with his hands, hit the head, pushed, threw foreign objects at the robot and kicked them.



Children are present in many public places where robots work and will work: supermarkets, hospitals, schools, museums, etc. Therefore, appropriate measures of protection should be provided for robots, the authors of the study believe.

According to scientists, the psychological motives of children's aggression towards other children and animals were previously studied. These are several motives, including a sense of dominance and a desire to join the group. The authors of the study tried to understand whether these motifs are applicable to robots during the aggression of children. The question is whether a humanoid robot is perceived as a human-like creature, or at least as a living creature, in principle.

Other studies have previously shown that the robot causes empathy, if most similar to humans. The researchers checked whether this parameter affects the level of child aggression to the car.

To test their theses, the scientists organized a series of interviews with children who mocked the robot in the supermarket. A total of 28 children were surveyed. They spoke about the reasons for their behavior: 22% mocked the robot out of curiosity, 35% did it for fun, 17% of actions were caused by the example of other children.

As it turned out, half of the children believed that the robot perceives their mocking behavior. This can be regarded as a manifestation of empathy for the robot.

The authors of the study conclude that a humanoid robot does not always lead to a decrease in the level of aggression towards it. On the contrary, increased empathy of people can lead to increased levels of aggression, as is the case with children in a supermarket.

Sabotage sometimes included cases of direct aggression: kicking with a foot, hitting a baseball bat, drawing with a marker on a camera lens, shoving and hitting with a fist. However, for the most part, sabotage consisted of passive aggression: robots were locked in the basement, shifted from the planned route, and most often minimized their use.

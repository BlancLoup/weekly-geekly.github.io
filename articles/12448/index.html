Why does a blogger rarely write?

Based on: What Stops you from Writing?
Free translation: dimok

Most bloggers are undoubtedly aware of the importance of regular posts, but writing regularly is not easy.

Talk about why posts are becoming less frequent, and how to handle it.

1. Set aside until the last.

On the Internet, it is very easy to drag out time: read endless feeds, visit forums, watch videos, etc. It eats up a huge amount of time that could be spent writing a blog post and many other useful things. What to do?

* Put the blog in the first place. Only when write a post, allow yourself a little wander around the network.
* Allocate time. For example, from 10 to 12.
* Disable Internet. The most effective places for writing are those where there is no network. See also: When and how to write a to-do list .

2. What would I write about?

Coming up with a title is often the hardest part of a post. We all want something fresh, interesting and definitely in the blog topic. If you are stuck at this stage, I can advise the following:

* Write the text, and you will think up heading later.
* Look at the statistics. What requests do you receive, what interests people, what are the most popular posts? Expand these topics.
* Look at your subject from a different angle. Follow the fashion trends: ‚ÄúSEO tips from Harry Potter‚Äù. But do not overdo it!
* What would you like to know? Often the best posts are trying to answer your own questions (for example, Blogging and personal effectiveness ). Even if no one reads this article, at least you yourself will be in the black.

3. Not enough time.

This is apparently the scourge of the modern world. We always have little time, we always rush, and often the lack of time is an excuse. This item has much in common with the first.

* We all have enough time - we need to prioritize. If you get rid of unimportant tasks, then the time for a blog will be more.
* Free up a couple of hours: at least do not check statistics and email at this time.
* We have the same time 24 hours a day as Einstein, Bill Gutz, Kevin Mitnick, Berezovsky and others had.

4. I am not a writer.

And who is good to write? Get started now and improve your skills. The more you write, the better you will get.

Do not worry about the exceptional perfection of your articles or what people will say. Practice and write soon will be easier and articles will get better.

All good blogging!

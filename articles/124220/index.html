A few theses on e-reading

Last summer, quite unexpectedly, I got the idea to purchase an e-book based on E-Ink. Fortunately, at that time a good friend was in the states, Amazon and Barnes & Nobles lowered the prices of their offspring, and there was no problem to ask for help. Everything merged into a fleeting decision to purchase something from the manufacturers mentioned. Paradoxically, my eyes focused on the Nook, and not on the Kindle. I had some hostility towards the giant, and the second color display seemed like an interesting solution. Of course, I will not review the device, because there are already so many of them. And in my message I will try to reflect all the thoughts and ideas that have been formed during this short period of using the Nook-a and monitoring issues in general. I tried to break the whole text into separate theses with a few comments.

E-Ink is only suitable for fiction.


I bought my reading room just for reading artist. Any attempt to read something professional did not end there. For many reasons: due to the lack of such literature in the right format, due to the inconvenient navigation of devices based on E-Ink, a small screen. A lot of things can be included here. For an artist, there are no problems, just the text is easily formatted and there are no problems with its display.

Opinion about reading from a book reader can only be a posteriori.


A person should try to read at least one e-book on a book reader. Only after that a more or less reasonable attitude towards reading from the device screen can be formed. This conclusion was not without reason. Showing and demonstrating the capabilities of Nook-a people far from using any progressive electronic devices and from the words negatively responding to any reading from the screen, I was pleasantly surprised by their positive assessment of use. First of all, I mean the older, conservative generation. Well, I did not expect a flattering assessment. Plus, after about half a year, the nearest circle also wanted similar devices for themselves, and the first purchases have already been made.

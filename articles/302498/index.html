"IT nomads": why companies still recruit saboteurs

While experienced professionals confidently plow the expanses of the IT market, beginners move in rushes. And if at first they move from one interview to another, then in the future some of them continue to run - only from one company to another. It is no secret that large IT companies are constantly expanding and contracting. In this regard, they recruit juniors, often by inertia. So, the last one can only catch the wave.

This will mainly be about software developers, but much is true for other positions.

Junior Consumer Attitudes


It is interesting that at the interview stage these people try to make a positive impression by hook or by crook. Some even agree to work "for food" as an intern - if only the prospect was. However, immediately after taking office, they, under any pretext, evade independent work (even on learning tasks): too difficult, not enough time, a new team, an unusual situation. Thus, they manage not only to overload the curator with this measure, but also to distract other employees from their work.

When these employees' own tasks begin to subside, and the curator's patience ends, the junior remains alone, alone with the training project.

What options does the "junior saboteur" have in this situation:

  • Continue to work, developing what the curator and good colleagues started? - Hardly.
  • Ask the management to extend the trial period, since you now have to work alone? - Option.
  • Are you offended and complaining to your senior management? - A good idea.

The Great Combinator chooses a combination of the last two options and simultaneously begins to look for another job. After all, it is possible that the relationship between the leadership and the curator will deteriorate, and the unfortunate, abandoned junior will be to blame for this.

Probably, these tasks are not particularly interesting for him, the team is not friendly, and in general there are better companies in the market. Therefore, it is necessary to leave, the hero concludes, forgetting that in the end the company did not bring benefits, but harm (lost profits and sagging tasks of colleagues). He does not despair and continues to search, and the company has to look for a new junior from all the cases.

Of course, a milder scenario is also possible: an intern can sit on the company's neck, prevent colleagues from working and feed the leadership with breakfast until he is expelled. Sometimes, for some reason, the curator and other commanding persons are not eager to admit that they took the saboteur to work and nursed him for six months. Maybe they consider themselves too advanced leaders to make such mistakes. And in some large companies very few people pay attention to such incidents. After all, the junior's salary is a penny, compared with the amount the company earns.

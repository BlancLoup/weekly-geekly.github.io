Weekly assembly Vivaldi 1.0.167.2

Hello!

In Russia today is a day off, but in the offices of Vivaldi, located in Europe and in the USA, work continues non-stop. Today we have prepared the next weekly build, in which we fixed the errors that you found since the release of TP3.

In addition to working on bugs, we improved the shortcut function. Now the choice of the required combinations has become easier, and we also added the ability to assign several combinations to one action.

And one more important message to calm in advance the adherents of absolute privacy in the network. Starting with this assembly, we begin to collect some statistical information. In order to better understand who our user is, we want to find out what hardware and OS you have, what country you live in, and what language you prefer for the interface. In this way, we will be able to better focus on your requests and set priorities for correcting errors and adding new features.

How it will work. When you first launch this version of the Vivaldi browser, an individual ID will be generated for your browser version. Then, once every 24 hours, the browser will send (if it works, of course) via the secure HTTPS protocol to our servers the following information:

  • Unique ID
  • Browser User Agent Data
  • CPU architecture
  • Screen resolution
  • Browser interface language


These data, of course, are intended only for us - the statistics are impersonal and do not represent commercial value.

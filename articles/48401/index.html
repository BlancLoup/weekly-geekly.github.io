Manage utorrent downloads from ICQ

It so happens that he added many downloads to utorrent and went to work, but at that time several series of your favorite TV series were still released, they were automatically picked up by RSS and downloaded together with the others, and you realize that by your coming home a new series of your favorite TV series is simple Do not have time to download, if you do not stop several previous downloads.


Of course, there is a simple way out of this situation, to use in utorrent'e WebUl and view downloads, as well as manage them through the browser in the mobile. But what about those who have mobile Internet is too expensive (for me it is ~ $ 1 for 1 Mb)? It turns out there is a way out, this is the management of downloads and their viewing through ICQ.

To do this, we need a mobile phone with an installed ICQ client, a uTorrent ICQ Bot program, and of course utorrent itself with a configured WebUl (you can read about how to configure this in skurudo in a post ).

The program interface is not intricately simple and clear:


ICQ settings - a number that will be perceived by our teams and a password from it accordingly;
WebUl settings - settings used in utorrent for remote access;
Admin UIN - the number from which our bot will receive commands;

Subsequently, it is enough just to save the settings and bring the bot online. The bot perceives 4 types of commands:

list - a list of all active downloads with their numbers will be shown;
pause ‚Ññ - pauses download with the number;
stop ‚Ññ - will stop the download with the number;

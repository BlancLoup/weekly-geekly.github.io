Samsung Electronics Introduces Complete 3D Home Solution



Samsung Electronics has become the first company on the market that launches a complete solution for creating a 3D home theater: 3D LED, LCD and plasma TVs, home 3D theaters and 3D Blu-ray players.

In addition to the release of 3D-enabled products, the company is actively involved in the development of 3D television. In cooperation with Russian companies General Satellite and Platform HD, Samsung launched the first joint 3D broadcasting on the territory of Russia and Eastern Europe ( link to the news).

The line of 3D high-definition TVs in 2010 (and the first models were introduced in 2007) includes LED TVs of the 7000, 8000 and 9000 series, plasma panels of the 7000 series and LCD TVs of the 750 series. Together with the Samsung 3D Blu-ray Player BD-C6900 or the HT-C6730 Home Cinema System and special glasses, TVs from the 2010 model range will fully convey the full depth, color and clarity of Full HD 3D images.

The new LED TVs of the 7000, 8000 and 9000 series are equipped with a built-in 3D processor, 200Hz technology, and are distinguished by advanced interactive features, environmental friendliness and a superb ultra-slim design. The lineup includes three series of 3D LED-TVs with screen diagonals from 40 to 65 inches.
Among the LCD models of 2010, the 750 series TVs are equipped with 3D technology. Thanks to the Hyper Real EngineTM processor, the image quality, contrast and color rendition have increased significantly. In addition, all Samsung LCD TVs have much lower power consumption and are EnergyStar 4.0 compliant.

Samsung Series 7000 plasma TVs offer users a combination of design, quality and functionality. They feature innovative technologies such as Real Black Filter and Motion Judder Canceller (MJC), which provide unprecedented picture quality. And Samsung CinemaSmoothTM technology allows you to play Blu-ray content at 24 frames per second in quality comparable to the most modern cinemas.

Most products with 3D technology support will appear on the Russian market in April 2010.

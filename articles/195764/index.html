Mobile applications in the service of the city authorities

Many people believe that a mobile application is something in between a social network with a geo-fiber and a button for posting photos of food and cats. Of course, there is also professional software, but it is several times smaller compared to entertainment content. But, nevertheless, a mobile application is a very powerful tool that can help in different life situations. In this article I would like to talk about how mobile technologies can solve the many problems of residents of specific cities and regions.

In the beginning, I would like to mention the Open Government Data initiative, the essence of which is that the governments and local councils spread their data in open access so that anyone could use them. This is necessary to ensure the transparency of government activities and significantly increases the awareness of citizens. On datacatalogs.org you can find a list of open data catalogs worldwide. Five results are available for Russia :

  • budget.gov.ru. Russia open budget / open spending portal
  • data.mos.ru. Moscow city administration open data portal, Russia.
  • data.openpolice.ru. Data portal for the Open Police project, Russia
  • OpenGovData Russia. Catalog OpenGovData.ru is a public data catalog published by the Russian Government
  • DataGov.ru. CKAN community site, Russia

Kazakhstan


Last year, a hackathon called Smart Astana was held in Astana . Participants developed applications on the city theme, which then had to be integrated into the IT infrastructure of the city. This year the initiative also continues to exist .

The problems in different cities are approximately the same, so the ideas of such events are also quite similar - the participants came up with solutions to search for travel companions, track and optimize urban transport routes, simplify utility bills and develop tourist guides.

Russia


Recently, the Moscow App Contest'13 competition was announced for the best application that solves urban tasks and makes the everyday life of metropolitan residents simpler and more enjoyable. Winners will be announced soon.



Official applications of the Moscow government are located on the page dit.mos.ru/apps , currently 7 mobile applications are available:

  • Transport of Moscow. Sending free messages to car owners, an interactive map, checking the imposed traffic police fines in Moscow and the facts of car evacuation, background information for car enthusiasts and pedestrians.
  • Mobile reception. 14 typical and a number of seasonal applications (not removed garbage, ice in the yard, pits on the roads, etc.), all messages are reviewed within 8 business days. Tracking the status of the message.
  • State services of Moscow. Interactive map of urban infrastructure.
  • Parking Moscow. Parking search, interactive map, parking management.
  • Housing and communal services of Moscow. Information about the payment of utilities, plans to turn off the hot water, enter the readings of water meters, interactive map.
  • Mobile tourist portal of Moscow. Interesting places, routes and events of Moscow, an interactive map with indication of city objects and a catalog of places of interest, reference and contact information on hotels in Moscow, a poster of the most interesting events and a brief description of sightseeing routes.
  • Get to know Moscow. Interactive map showing interesting urban objects, background information, historical houses of the city.

Ukraine


In Ukraine, such initiatives are in their infancy, although something is even happening somewhere .

For example, in Odessa, QR codes will be placed on all objects of cultural heritage, with the help of which you can get a link to Wikipedia describing this historic place.



But I want more initiatives. In addition, fast and cheap Internet in Ukraine (50-100 MB unlimited without problems can be obtained for $ 8-12 per month), so the potential for the development of web and mobile services is obvious (although there are still difficulties with mobile Internet).

That is why we decided to conduct a hackathon on a city theme in the very heart of Ukraine - the city of Vinnytsia, where the city authorities happily decided to support this idea.

Our task is to help establish a close connection between the IT community and city councils, provide technical expertise for implementation and various web services, APIs and materials, as well as implement as many interesting ideas and projects that will help improve the lives of ordinary citizens.

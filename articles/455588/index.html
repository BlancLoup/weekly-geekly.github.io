That is, a means and medium of communication between different teams was needed.

"We need a community!"


At this stage, I came to the company. She came to a new corporate culture, to a team that had never dealt with such things before, but understood their necessity.

I was given the task of creating a community, deciding what to do with all these people, with their motivation, with what goals and objectives it is necessary to set for them, what problems to solve.

And why do people need a community?


A community is a group of people united voluntarily and by common interest (professional / non-professional basis). Man is a social being, generally rather weak. It just so happened that mother nature did not give us special obvious physical abilities, such as super speed jaguar or jaws like sharks, so for survival people were united in groups. Genetic memory is a beautiful thing, so let's use it.

Let us see what primarily gives a person such an association:

  1. Sense of security.
  2. A sense of shared responsibility.
  3. Feeling that you are not alone.

People are best united by hatred and profit. Common problems that are revealed over time, and joint responsibility with a sense of strength and support (" I am not the only one suffering, we are many, and we can solve all problems together "). First, people start hating something together, and then change something.

Therefore, when you build a professional community, you need to identify problems and give people a clear understanding of why participation in the community is beneficial for them. Your role here is the most important - to structure, promote and support. Otherwise it will not work.

Instruments


Technically, we gather people for meetings and events. We have a list of technology and related technologies, or we do not have a list, but we know how to collect it.

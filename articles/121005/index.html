Glasses with HD-camera broadcast video to the Network

Points for lifeblogging is about to launch a startup ZionEyez (CTO of the company is one of the developers of the famous Flip miniature video camera). According to the promised characteristics, the hidden HD camera will record 1280x720 video at 30 fps in the built-in 8 GB flash memory.



Moreover, the video can be broadcast in real time on an iPhone or Android device (Bluetooth 3.0 or WiFi 802.11n), from where it is directly uploaded to the video hosting service using the special Eyez application and can be viewed (for example, Qik and LiveStream ).

The camera lens should be completely invisible to the observer.



The developers promise that the battery in the arch is enough for three hours of shooting. Charging is done through the microUSB connector. Through it, you can copy the recorded video to a computer.



A very handy thing for recording long videos, when it's really inconvenient to hold a video camera all the time. For example, you can record lectures at the university or important events (wedding, etc.).



Preliminary orders for points are accepted until July 31 for $ 150, then the price rises to $ 200. And this is not just "pre-orders". The startup is funded under the terms of the Kickstarter Venture Fund , that is, anyone can invest as little as $ 1 in the production of these points. If you invest $ 150 - you get yourself points. But you can donate just one dollar for a good idea.

It remains an open question whether a startup will be able to realize the stated specifications. They say that for this they need only $ 55 thousand investment. However, they collected the necessary amount yesterday , that is, 52 days before the final date. At this moment the amount of funds collected is $ 60,351, and the counter continues to tick.

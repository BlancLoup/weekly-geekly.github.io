The last but one project is for phone studios . The task is to get a new amateur point of view without the use of a professional camera crane, as well as to take photographs with active movement with turns and displacements in height. Implementing the idea helped the stabilizer of the smartphone (without it would be tight) and a bit of different pieces of hardware.
The design uses two options for holding the phone:
- long boom: 2.5 meters (optimal for transportation in the car);
- short handle with a rotating axis.



The smartphone is mounted on the top or bottom of the bent rod set.



The phone takes a horizontal position, since the center of gravity of the structure is located well below the point of suspension, which is a ball joint.



People who saw an unfamiliar mounting mechanism first ask: ‚ÄúWhy does the phone not fall off the ball, is it a magnet?‚Äù No, everything is held only by gravity and a small rim above the ball. And safety rope just in case of fire.
Surprise is also turning the camera. Initially, the rotation was supposed to use rope thrust, attached to the two holes in the plate above the ball. But it turned out that everything rotates easily when you turn the ball around the axis. This interesting effect was applied further.

Long rod: chrome-plated steel pipe: L 2500 mm, D 25 mm, wall thickness 0.7 mm. At the end of the pipe - carbon tube D 19 mm, L protrusion. 56 mm. The tube is attached to the ball H 22 mm, D 30 mm from the ball valve VT.218.N.05. To protect against damage, it is covered with foam material.
Short handle with a rotating axis: part of a hollow handle from a Bosh electric drill GSB 13 RE Professional.
Axis: a stud with a length of 190 mm and a diameter of 8 mm with thread along the entire length. The thread in the lower part, protruding from the body by 25 mm, is closed with a heat shrinkable tube for easy rotation.
The distance from the top of the handle to the bottom of the ball is 30 mm. Carbon tube "planted" on the nuts M8. Bearings 628 RS CRAFT fasten the self-locking nuts. Under bearings, a shrink tube with a diameter of 10 mm is placed on the axle to remove a small gap. The gap between the outer side of the bearing and the inner part of the handle is removed with the help of three self-adhesive tapes glued along the length of the handle.

What is the problem of freelancing as such? The need to seek and the need to negotiate. Hiring a person in the state, the employer does it once and for a long time. On freelancing, this becomes a routine that takes a significant share of the time and effort intended to solve the problem.

The absence of a clear pricing policy generates a primitive market broth in which everyone gurgles who is in that much: customers take prices from the ceiling, freelancers drop prices below the baseboard, and then they meet and agree on four of them: each with each other and his own personal toad. In general, it is a constant bargaining, and the constant bargaining is a constant stress.

Search orders - a separate story. Coupled with the need to negotiate, the need for constant search creates a natural bewilderment: what about working, actually, when?

Kwork, being the Russian equivalent of a successful Fiverr service, following its prototype, changes the paradigm of the freelancer's relationship with the customer: instead of scouring for clientele, the performer works on systematization and delivery of his services. Customers come to kwork like a store, already knowing what they need. The care of the artist - to make his product look more attractive than others. This is a great model for experienced freelancers who have already worked on their record books - now the record book can work for them.

By itself, the need to structure your skills is a fat plus Quark, because it hasn't hurt anyone yet to understand what exactly you can do and how much this costs.

About DisplayPort 1.2

The VESA working group has published some details on the new DisplayPort 1.2 video interface standard.

The current version of DisplayPort 1.1, which was adopted in April 2007, supports a resolution of 2560x1600 pixels. The new DisplayPort 1.2 will be able to hold as many as two monitors with this resolution on one connector. The maximum DisplayPort resolution is 1.2 - 3840x2160 pixels. And for the image with a 30-bit color depth and a frequency of 60 Hz.

In addition, this standard will allow displaying HD images at a frequency of 120 frames per second or maintaining two images at the hardware level for stereoscopic technologies requiring this. Which are implemented, for example, in the ViewSonic FuHzion monitor and GeForce 3D Vision glasses from NVIDIA.

So far, however, even version 1.1 is not very common. This video interface was used only in several monitors. And in particular, Apple began to use DisplayPort in its laptops and monitors.

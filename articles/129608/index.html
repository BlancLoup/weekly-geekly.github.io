Mount for a laptop on the monitor (rear)

In the standard VESA slots that almost all monitors have. Not difficult. Reliably Handsomely.
With pictures and drawings.




Idea


Many people use laptops at home. Many with an external monitor. Some, like me, barely wear it anywhere. Almost all the time, the laptop lonely collects dust, taking up space next to the monitor tied to it with ropes of wires.
At the same time, the mass of the monitors has a standard (VESA) rear connector for mounting on the wall, which almost no one uses. It looks like four holes for screws in the very center of the back cover. In fact, the holes in plastic are holes in plastic, and the thread is in an iron frame inside the case, which will bear the load if you hang the monitor on the wall.
Without thinking twice (almost half a year, then one thing, then another, aha), superficially googling the Internet and not finding anything similar, I decided to combine one with another, since the idea was completely unsophisticated, even for a man who never did anything with his hands, but he was familiar with the computer .

Stage One - Project


VESA specifications were found to be absolutely commonplace - 75x75, 100x100 and 125x125 (almost never found on monitors, only for huge TVs). Own holes in the monitor of the Samsung P2450H (100x100), measurements of the laptop (MacBook Pro 15) are measured, the length is divided in half, added to half the distance between the holes, and so on, completely trivial calculations for the layout already estimated in the mind.
The model is drawn in an autocade, a three-dimensional model, a flat scan of the workpiece to be cut out and a three-projection drawing. All 1 to 1 and where necessary with the size. (files of drawings at the end of the article).

A small collection of answers to obvious questions:


Is the laptop warming up?

Not heated at all. I checked my monitor - it is completely cold behind me, though not a LED. Well, besides this, the laptop is 1.5 centimeters away from the monitor. Plus, the ventilation holes of the macbook are strictly behind and in this position they are obtained from above, where hot air tends from it. In general, with this full order.

Did the monitor lean back under the weight of the laptop back losing balance?

My - did not lean back, although I was afraid of this, but he surprisingly remained absolutely still, although he possesses the whole range of branded Samsung "features" - the play of plastic parts, and the bumpiness, if you touch it.

Do not pull a heavy laptop mount from the monitor?

Not exactly to throw up. The VESA mount is a mount in an iron frame inside the monitor that is slightly visible in the holes and which holds almost all the insides on it.

And how to turn off and on if the button is inside?

In sleep mode, keep it up. All from the keyboard. It's a laptop ... Although this is certainly the most ambiguous moment, if it hangs, it will be necessary to remove, open, turn off, turn on, remove ... But it hangs twice in two years, the work is quite feasible.

Time Lapse Photo Motion on Samsung NX100

One of the coolest pieces that the NX100 does is slow-motion photography (in English time lapse photo motion), which allows you to create the illusion of a greater speed of the processes that are taking place. To understand how it is done and how it is done, look at this very beautiful video with views of Moscow from Roman Russia operator Roman Lesovoy. Among other things, Roman told in more detail and showed how this video was mounted in Adobe After Effects.



We introduced the new mirrorless camera Samsung NX100 on the Russian market in September last year. Briefly about it: the matrix - 14.6 megapixels; memory - up to 32GB; display - 3 inches (AMOLED); may increase to ISO 6,400; GPS10 geotagging weight - 282 g (without battery and memory card)

Here's another curious fact about it: when creating the design of the NX100 camera, Samsung specialists were inspired by the look of a dew drop on a green leaf


And here is another example of slow-motion photography using the same camera - the clip of the popular indie band OK Go to the song Last Leaf.



On a few pieces of toasted bread, a real dramatic story unfolds. Samsung helped OK Go to realize his idea by providing the NX100 and professionals. By the way, this team itself has become world famous thanks to social networks - their Here It Goes Again video hit (where members of the group dance on eight treadmills without stopping) watched 60 million times, thanks to which he received the title of the most popular online music hit.

In general, look. And do not forget that all this is filmed using a camera weighing 282 grams.

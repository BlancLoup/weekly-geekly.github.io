Gym complex


The training complex is quite complex and atmospheric - many computers are connected to one network, flight data and video communication channels are displayed on the monitors. Spacecraft layout


The Martian manned spacecraft is assembled from several modules of the ISS, the VASIMR engine and the Martian module from the BBC film Space Odyssey .
The spacecraft turned out to be very long, it is difficult to control, and the relatively small thrust of the electric jet engine means that the maneuvers will be carried out much longer than in the case of using chemical engines. These factors seriously complicate the flight.

Flight plan


The flight begins in Earth orbit. The Martian ship cannot independently start from the surface, it is going to orbit from separate modules launched on its launch vehicles. After assembly and arrival of the crew, the ship accelerates to the Moon (due to the low thrust of the engines, this occurs in several stages) and goes into the Moon‚Äôs orbit. In low circumlunar orbit, the crew enters the landing module, lands on the moon near the Riphean Mountains (the ancient name of the Ural Mountains), takes off from the moon, and fits in with the ship. Flight results


After five and a half hours of real time and 15 days of the simulator, despite some technical difficulties and psychological fatigue from communicating with journalists, the flight was successfully completed in a circular orbit of the Earth.

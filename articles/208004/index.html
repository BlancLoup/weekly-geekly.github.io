Full analysis of Apple Mac Pro 2013

In mid-2013, the long-awaited Mac Pro was announced at WWDC. The novelty had an absolutely unique design and original layout of elements. Apple made a very interesting page (by the way, on HTML5) for a new computer, but the insides were not described in such detail. The updated computer went on sale in December 2013 and quickly became scarce. Finally, he got yes iFixit, and today we look at the most interesting computer in 2013.



This Mac Pro has the following characteristics.


  • Quad-Core Intel Xeon E5 with 10 MB L3 cache and Turbo Boost up to 3.9 GHz
  • 12 GB (three 4 GB modules) of 1866 MHz DDR3 ECC memory
  • Dual AMD FirePro D300 graphics processors with VR GB 2 GDDR5 each
  • 256 GB PCIe-based flash storage
  • 802.11ac Wi-Fi and Bluetooth 4.0
  • Price $ 2999


While many compare the Mac Pro with the trash can, it looks more like an aluminum soda can.


External interfaces:
  • 3.5 mm headset
  • Four USB 3.0 ports
  • Six Thunderbolt 2
  • Two Gigabit Ethernet ports
  • HDMI 1.4





No screws, just move the lock and lift the housing cover. Watch this interesting video on how to make a case for Mac Pro, fascinates.


Immediately you can see the entire filling of the new computer. Most places are occupied by two video cards.


Their symmetry is broken only by SSD, which is mounted on one of the video cards.


On both sides of the interfaces are symmetrically strap memory.


RAM is easy to replace.


4 GB DDR3L SDRAM (x3) Elpida EBJ04EG8BFWB-JS-F.

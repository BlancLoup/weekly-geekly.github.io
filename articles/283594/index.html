In China, they put kneeling sales managers who did not fulfill the plan.


Photo taken by Chu Koo, distributed by CEN news agency

See those bent backs on the bridge? That is how the sales managers in an unknown (for now) company of Xiamen City, Fujian Province of China, were punished for lack of diligence in their work.

It is unpleasant to miss your own goals, but we are accustomed to the fact that the maximum penalty: the absence of a salary bonus or, in the worst case, an unpleasant conversation with the boss.

Pedestrians, who recorded a kind of punishment, said that these people were not only forced to kneel on the bridge, asking for an apology - there were papers in front of them with inscriptions telling them exactly what each of them had done. With the mention of the name and age.

In front of Chen Liu, 43, lay a sign with a handwritten sentence: ‚ÄúI did not cope with my sales plan.‚Äù

Before Ming Chu, 39, lay a piece of paper with the text: "I have to kneel on this bridge for an hour, as punishment for unfulfilled work."

After one of the passers-by took a picture of the unfortunate salespeople and put the pictures on the net - the story began to spread viral. Of course, most users were outraged by the fact that an unknown company thus humiliates its own employees.

According to eyewitnesses, one of the guilty did not withstand public punishment, and, having decided that he had had enough of him, he stood up with a shout, crumpled and threw away a sign before retiring. The remaining three remained in place, without noticing the demarche colleagues.

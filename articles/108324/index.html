Game Connection, Lyon 2010



Continuing the topic of international gaming conferences and exhibitions, I want to tell you about the event called Game Connection, which our company visited last week.

Briefly about the event


Several times a year, Game Connection gathers representatives of various companies from the gaming industry for b2b parties. The slogan of the event is simple as a strike with a hammer about a snnu: We give [key] players of the [industry], you make deals.

How it works

Participants negotiate upcoming meetings over the course of several weeks through the GC Meeting System. And then for three days these meetings are held together at the event itself in separate rooms or at tables in the main hall. GC is a great opportunity to meet most of your partners in one place. The same, in fact, occurs at other conferences, but on the sidelines and unorganized. Reports on the GC was not, and as far as I know, does not happen.

International contingent

Previously, GCs consisted mainly of console gamers. This time everything is a little: developers / publishers of MMO, studios with products for PSN and XBL, developers for iPad, iPhone (though not many), developers of social network applications, monetizers, a couple of teams brought their 3D engines. Attended Google Ireland. Some companies visited the exhibition as a business-visitor. Full list of participants

From the new and interesting

Turbulenz offered a system representing something in between OnLive , Steam and Quake Live . By installing a small browser plugin, users are able to play modern three-dimensional single-player and multiplayer games. General catalog of games and monetization system is attached.

There was also one payment system , offering users to link their account in any of the European banks to the IVR and make payments on a call by entering the password and the order number by dialing.

On the day before the conference, everyone was taken on a tour of the Bandai Namco and EA offices. In BN, everything is very nicely decorated: large spaces, huge desktops, many figurines of game characters, posters and other souvenirs. Even after 6 pm, half the employees are at work. EA in the office did not drive. Only showed a presentation on what they are cool and a couple of trailers (there was no insider). From 30 minutes of a dull story, I highlighted one thought: every year, EA publishes fewer games and earns more and more from them.

This novelty for me is over.

Php Inspections (EA Extended): choose a strategy for 2016

Happy new year, habrovchane!

The year 2015 was very eventful for the PHP world: this is the long-awaited release of version 7, the major update of many frameworks, and many promising RFCs.

For Php Inspections (EA Extended) the past year was also very intense: the project saw the light, matured very quickly and was able to make the PHP world a little bit better ( symfony2 , symfony 1.5 , PHP CS Fixer ).

Let me remind you that Php Inspections (EA Extended) is a separate plugin that expands the capabilities of standard analysis in PhpStorm and Idea Ultimate. Early announcements on Habr√©: one , two , three .

Next (under the cut): a few polls and the opportunity to influence the choice of strategy for 2016.


From the very beginning, the project solved a single task: to make code review and training people less time consuming (and not so painful). It turned out very well and was able to help many teams.

Nevertheless, I do not want to move blindly, but to get feedback and be able to somehow manage the development of the project.

As a result, at the moment the following scenarios have been formed:
  • focus on community building (rare releases);
  • focus on the commercial version (the ability to automatically replace the code);
  • specialization in high-load projects.

Infographics. Close-up



Infographics in every day gaining new positions in design, in the media, in blogs Why does Infographic design get such distribution?



Why infographics?

First, this is an excellent visualization of the data, and as you know, 90% of the information a person receives through sight and only 10% through the rest of his senses. Secondly, simple and original visualization is much clearer and better absorbed. I think it can go on indefinitely.

Infographics are different because they mean reference or illustrated information provided by various visualization methods ‚Äî using graphs, charts, histograms, mental maps (mind maps), timelines (one of the basic principles of animated infographics), etc. .

We should also mention such a thing as information density (data density). By this criterion, infographics can be divided into two types:

  • "Unsaturated" . Simple, non-concentrated - built around several digits
  • "Concentrated" Dense, complex - built (usually) as a combination of an image and a number of numbers.

Examples of complex infographics




Through various ways of visualization, skillful authors manage to colorfully and simply present complex and boring numbers. Infographics are universal in terms of their scope - economics, politics, sports and of course, people, we are with you. Creating an infographic is not an easy process, as it goes through the following steps:

  1. Formulation of the goal of creating infographics and definition of the audience. Information design being developed is usually intended for a target audience.
  2. Collect a certain amount of data, material on the topic . The data is presented in various formats - textual content, graphics, video materials, tables of tables and so on ...
  3. Analytics and information processing The collected material must be analyzed and processed, lead to one denominator - usually these are unformed graphs, histograms ...
  4. Building an accessible visualization, layout . All material is assembled, given in a beautiful clear view. The format is selected (depending on the goals and the amount of data) - presentation, slide-cast, single-page picture, video.

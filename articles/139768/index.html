Time.is: atomic time synchronization



Not so long ago, Time.is started working - an excellent service that shows the approximate desynchronization of your computer clock with international atomic time (the error of Ajax is usually 0.02-0.1 s), also shows the time in 7 million places in the world and compares the time in different places.

Additionally, there are a number of nice bonuses: a calendar with the number of the week and the day number of the year, a directory of sunrises and sunsets for each location, integration with Google Maps, widgets for third-party sites.

There is even a special design for full-screen mode in the browser. And note that the appearance of a new second on the Time.is server occurs exactly at the beginning of this second. Some operating systems do not even know how to synchronize computer time with such accuracy.

Beautiful science from "Electro-L"

Six months ago, I wrote about the Russian meteorological satellite Electro-L, which has been successfully operating in geostationary orbit for more than two years. Habra liked the material, and I offered to those who wish to monitor its activities through the Vkontakte group. Then I promised that I would share here the materials that we collect. I think it's time to fulfill the promise.

The Electro-L spacecraft was built at the NPO im. S.A. Lavochkin (the company now also has a VK group ), launched by the Zenit rocket, with the Fregat booster block (RB also Made in Lavochka) two and a half years ago. It creates images of the Earth with details up to 1 km per pixel every 30 minutes. Frames are produced in ten spectral ranges, two of which are visible ‚Äî green and blue, followed by near infrared, and seven at different wavelengths of the middle and far infrared ranges. These observations allow meteorologists to observe the movement of clouds at various altitudes. But the first three channels create still beautiful photos.

When mixing the three RGB ranges, instead of red, they put near infrared, so the original is an unusual picture, where instead of green - orange. But this light scheme allows you to get more information about the earth's surface. To return the green in the pictures have to shaman in Photoshop.

I consider it a great omission that neither Roskosmos nor Roshydromet make any effort to share this beauty with their people. Fortunately, almost all the images are in the public domain on the FTP server of the Roskosmos division, the Scientific Center for Earth Operational Monitoring (OMZ).



Taking advantage of the openness of information, I decided to show by personal example what the meaning of the work of the Roskosmos press service was.

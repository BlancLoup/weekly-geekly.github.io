general description


Sinterit Lisa is a 3D printer from the Polish company Sinterit, which prints three-dimensional parts using SLS technology. This means that objects are formed in it in layers, by sintering the polymer powder with a laser beam.


Since the support itself is the powder surrounding the forming model, there are no special supports and jumpers between the elements of the printed part.


Due to this, the shape of the parts can be anything, including parts one into another and mobile connections, and their number in the working chamber of the printer is limited only by the dimensions of this chamber.

Material savings


As a result of the screening, up to 70% of the powder remaining in the chamber after printing is available. The printer can use sifter powder mixed with new, in a proportion of up to 50%, while maintaining print quality. If the proportion of recycled material is exceeded, surface quality may decline.

A powder sifter, sandblasting machine and 6 kg powder containers are supplied separately and can be ordered with the printer.


Materials



PA materials are based on polyamide, a special case of which is widely known nylon. Flexa materials are based on thermoplastic polyurethane.

PA 11: the most common polymer for SLS, is distinguished by its small particle size, which gives surfaces a greater smoothness.

PA 12: ruggedized polyamide that can withstand loads up to 41 megapascals and a 13% elongation to break.

Flexa: a material of increased softness and elasticity. Read below for a description of an interesting example of its use.

Detected a network of cyber espionage, operating in 40 countries

Kaspersky Lab announces the discovery of a global cyber espionage network operating in 40 countries and affecting more than 350 important sites. The main tool for carrying out these attacks was the malware NetTraveler, so named after the line found in the source codes ‚ÄúNetTraveler Is Running!‚Äù. This trojan is designed to steal confidential data, is able to register keystrokes, work with the file system, conduct a search among office documents (DOC, XLS, RTF, PPT, PDF). In addition, he is interested in projects Corel Draw, AutoCAD and various configuration files.
The largest number of infections was found in Mongolia, followed by Russia and India. In general, the attacks were recorded in 40 countries of the world, including Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, China, Tajikistan, South Korea, Spain, Germany, the USA, Canada, the UK, Greece and others.
It is noteworthy that this spy network specializes in infecting government agencies, embassies, enterprises of the oil and gas industry, research institutes and military contractors. At which specific sites in Russia traces of NetTraveler infection were detected are not reported ...

The first activity of the network was discovered in 2004, and the largest number of infections accounted for 2010-2013.

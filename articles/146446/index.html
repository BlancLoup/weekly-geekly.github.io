Luxoft Personnel presents a study of the Ukrainian IT labor market for 2011 and the 1st half of 2012

The study of the Ukrainian labor market was prepared by the analytical department of the recruiting agency Luxoft Personnel together with analysts of the career portal it.rabota.ua .

Due to changes in the global economy in recent years, the growth and development of companies, the information technology market remains one of the dominant areas.

In 2011, there was a significant increase compared with 2010, but, in 2012, according to analysts, a radical growth of the market is not expected, and this is evidenced by the unstable economic situation in Europe. The consequences for the IT industry cannot be foreseen, although the IT market in Ukraine already has experience of the crisis of 2008‚Äì2009, when the industry held out, the tactics and tools used in such situations are already known.

The growth sector is obliged to two main driving factors: the expansion of the general penetration of IT technologies in the business processes of companies and the daily life of people, the tendency to outsource the internal functions of IT and IT infrastructure to independent companies.

At the moment, the IT market in Ukraine is growing and will continue to grow. There are many technological changes in the world every day, and, accordingly, professionals are needed to support these changes. In this regard, the demand for IT professionals will continue to be high.

According to analysts, in 2012 the growth of the Ukrainian IT market will be 15-20%, which is an order of magnitude less than the 30% growth over the past year. This is due to the second wave of the crisis in the Western market. In general, the situation on the labor market in 2012 promises to be very prosperous for specialists in the IT sphere. Experts explain the high demand by two factors. First, the emergence of new technologies in IT, and secondly, the specialists of large companies often work not only on the domestic market, but also on the foreign one.

Key points:


  1. In 2011, the total number of vacancies in the IT industry increased by 2 times compared with the fourth quarter of 2010.
  2. According to employers' forecasts, growth trends in demand for IT professionals will continue in 2012.
  3. There is a tendency to grow professionals from young professionals within the company. In 2011, the number of vacancies for entry-level specialists increased by 2.7 times, well ahead of the trends for programmers with extensive experience. Experts predict a similar increase in 2012.
  4. 80% of all vacancies for programmers and testers are posted by companies in the software development industry and, accordingly, only 20% are companies from other industries.
  5. One third of all vacancies posted by companies in the software industry are for .Net and Java programmers.
  6. The most active growth in demand in 2011 among software developers was observed for web-programmers. The number of vacancies for them increased by 2.8 times.
  7. 25% of software development jobs are for large companies with more than 500 employees. In 2012, this figure, according to analysts, will grow, given the rather aggressive policy of recruiting these companies in the IT market.
  8. There is a shortage of Java and .Net programmers - less than one candidate for a place.
  9. The number of proposals for directors and heads of departments in the field of IT is increasing very actively. In the fourth quarter of 2011, the number of vacancies increased by 3 times compared with the fourth quarter of 2010.
  10. The most active growth in the number of vacancies (by 3.7 times) was observed for specialists in business process automation systems (ERP, CRM, etc.). Presumably in 2012, the demand for specialists of this profile will only continue to grow: the process of introducing automation systems is only gaining momentum.
  11. The growth of salaries of software developers in 2011 amounted to an average of 15-20%.


REVIEW OF EARNINGS OF SOFTWARE DEVELOPERS


In the world every day there are many technological changes, and, accordingly, we need specialists to support these changes. In this regard, the demand for IT professionals will continue to be high, which means that the main trends in pay will also remain unchanged. The projected wage inflation in Ukraine will remain at the same level, and the task of the large IT companies in Ukraine is to make every effort to keep it.

BRIGHT TRENDS 2012


Dynamics of the number of vacancies in the field of information technology for 6 quarters (01.10.2010 - 31.03.2012)


According to the portal it.rabota.ua, the growth rate of the number of vacancies in the IT sphere practically repeated the trends in the labor market as a whole. In the first quarter of 2012, the total number of vacancies in this area increased by 2 times compared with the fourth quarter of 2010.

Dynamics of the number of vacancies for software developers for 6 quarters (01.10.2010 - 31.03.2012)


Once again, we are convinced that web programming technologies are developing by leaps and bounds, only in the first quarter of this year, the number of web programming vacancies increased by 6% compared to the 4th quarter of 2011, and compared to 2010, the increase was almost 3 times.

Dynamics of the number of vacancies for IT directors (CTO / CIO) / Heads of IT departments for 6 quarters (10/01/2010 - 03/31/2012)

The number of proposals for directors and heads of departments in the field of information technology is increasing very actively, significantly ahead of the trends of the entire IT sphere. In the 1st quarter of 2012, the number of vacancies increased by 3.2 times compared to the 4th quarter of 2010.

Dynamics of the number of vacancies for specialists in automation systems for 6 quarters (10/01/2010 - 03/31/2012)


In the 1st quarter of 2012, the number of vacancies offered by employers to specialists in automation systems increased 3.9 times compared to the 4th quarter of 2010, which once again confirms the need to introduce automated enterprise systems and this is the first step towards building long-term relationships with customers.

The trial ended between the Ukrainian division of Google and the company "GOU OGLE"

The trial, which unfolded last year between the Ukrainian division of Google and the company Gou Ogle, ended. Google tried to defend the rights to the Google.ua domain, which was owned by the company GOU OGLE, which hosted a dating site.

On February 3, 2009, the Economic Court of Kiev ordered Google SEA to stop using the registered trademark of the corporation in the domain name Google.ua.ua, and register the domain zone (Hostmaster) to remove the delegation from the disputed domain. However, the respondent did not agree with this decision and filed an appeal. In a law office representing the interests of the defendant, it was clarified that GOU OGLE companies since 2006 have owned the Google and Go Ogle trademarks registered in Ukraine for classes 9 (scientific instruments, computers, cash and counting machines) and 45 (social, security services, marriage agencies).

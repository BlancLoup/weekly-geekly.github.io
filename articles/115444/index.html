An example of technical specifications on the site. Save time and nerves

The site is always a compromise between the developer and the owner, each of which is a professional in their field.

There are many points of view on how the site should be: the programmer has one, the designer has another, the internet marketer has the third, the owner ...

In fact, the point of view - only one - the end user of the resource. And it is precisely this that must first be taken into account. Naturally, taking into account the convenience of site maintenance by the administrator.

What are the advantages of a well-written technical assignment:
  • unequivocal understanding by all participants of the project of how and what should work
  • saving of time on development and all specifications
  • the opportunity to assess the complexity of the project and think out the subtle points before starting work
  • opportunity after completion of the work to track whether everything was done as intended
  • opportunity to calculate the final cost of the project before starting work
  • opportunity to distribute responsibility of both parties



Example site concept: narod.ru/disk/7515328001/konc_orbita.doc.html (doc, 3.5MB)

What does the given example site concept file of one of the regional companies include (a piece that the future owner of the site can independently make):
1. Analysis of the target audience.
2. The need for CA for certain materials.
3. Actions that visitors should perform on the site.
Based on paragraphs 1-3, we understand what should be the structure of the site, what should be the texts, and the location of navigation elements.

4. Sophisticated texts for the site pages.
5. Drawn page templates (not design, but already a guide to action for the designer) with written texts.
6. Text explanations for page layouts.
7. A brief analysis of the statistics of visits to the old version of the site.
8. Basic requirements for the admin site.

What does not include:
1. Site optimization issues.
2. Issues of attracting an audience.
3. Design requirements.
4. Requirements for hosting.
5. Audit of the old version of a site and sites of competitors.

In fact, this is not even a part of the TZ, but a piece of the general concept of one of the sites, which we thought out together with the customer. It is written in free form and serves as the main document necessary for understanding how a resource should work.

As a result, according to the technical task, which was written on the basis of this concept, one of the studios of our city made a site. Some moments the customer and the performer reviewed in the process.
Very interesting your opinion on the content of the document.

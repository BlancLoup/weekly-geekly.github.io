Review of literature read in two years

Hi Habra! When I first started doing web development, I couldn‚Äôt even imagine how much literature I needed to read and how much knowledge I needed to become a normal specialist. In addition, the sphere is constantly changing, and knowledge becomes obsolete.

For me, the problem was that I do not like reading and reading to be regarded solely as an inevitable work that must be done in order to extract material from the printed form. But it so happened that to improve their skills have to read. I set the standard for myself - one book per month. On the one hand, it is not enough, on the other - a lot (if you really stick to the plan). Well, since I have to read under compulsion, it should give some benefit. Each book is a torment, and I see no reason to spend time on literature, which will not give me anything after. Therefore, every time I get into the book shop and holding another book in my hands, I ask myself the question: ‚ÄúWhy ?! What is the use of these pieces of paper ?! What am I doing here? ‚Äù So that you do not waste time on books that seem useless to me, I decided to write a short review of two years of reading literature and other sources of knowledge found. All the material below is somehow related to web development and its various aspects. Below are only those books that I read. Those books that "did not master" / did not finish reading (for example, about NodeJS and a couple of frameworks) I do not cite.

Book Evaluation Criteria:

  • The amount of code. The more code in the book, the worse it is. This means that the author cites meaningless program listings instead of describing the algorithm of the words.
  • Book thickness The thicker the book, the worse. Most likely, it will be difficult to read to the end. Why did she get fat?
    • Because there is a lot of water.
    • Because the author does not know how to express his thoughts briefly and clearly.
    • Because the author works in any university, so it‚Äôs not serious to write a thin book: colleagues from the department will laugh, so he blows up with meaningless inserts.
  • Illustrations. The fewer illustrations, the worse the book. The same information, presented in graphic and textual form, is easier to perceive in graphic form. The depth of processing information also plays a role. Text with an illustration is perceived better than text without an illustration. The presence of high-quality illustrations shows that the author worked on the book "with the soul."
  • Design and layout. Good design speeds up reading and allows you to better assimilate information. This item includes compliance with the rules of typography, layout and structuring of information.
  • A good book is easy to read: the eyes should not get tired, and the pages should be pleasant to the touch (in this regard, glossy publications for designers, of course, apart from the competition). High-quality design, again, shows that the book worked "with the soul."
  • Ideas, not methods. A good book should teach that it will not become obsolete in the next few years, or even more. For everything else, articles in the relevant publications are suitable. Why pay 1000 rubles. for the book about the API of another fashionable framework, which everyone will forget about in six months? It is better to read about the ideas that the authors embodied in the framework, about what they wanted, how they achieved it, what they came to. How the architecture was designed and why.

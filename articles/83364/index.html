Now tips


To many, they seem obvious or strained, but remember for yourself how often you have to deal with their violation. So, as it should be:

1) Aesthetics. Whatever you say, it is very important. Of course, you only need to do this if everything else is in order. This can also include cleanliness in the rooms.



2) Stock. The golden rule: do 25% of the stock for everything. Do you make the SCS, do you pull the power cable, order the box and anything else - count the required amount and multiply by 1.25. Even if you have your own company, no need to skimp, forget already the "economy should be economical." It's a shame when you need to move the outlet, and the twisted pair is already stretched (by the way, no cables should go into tension) or when pulling power and not enough one and a half centimeters.
It is not difficult to leave such a ring here, but if you move the pipe racks or raise the antenna, it will play a good service. Or put the stock of the twisted pair in the box.



3) Cables should not just go along the wall, hang, hang out. They should be carried along the cable route one by one, fastening together with straps (ties). Avoid crossing electrical signal and power cables.







The company, where the photos were taken, is being renovated: beautiful flat walls of plasterboard, armstrong to the ceiling, lamps, fire alarms. And what about the ceiling? That's right, the same thing - why put it in order, if you still can not see.

4) All cables, feeders, machines must be signed.



5) For patch panels and crosses, I advise you to have a table that should always be up to date. For SCS, there must be a network diagram. This and the previous paragraph will allow, in which case, quickly and unequivocally restore everything as it was. For example, if you want to move the switch one unit up.

6) UPD: Made-Fix (as prompted in the comments). Do not leave at the end of the day / week / reporting period changes in all documents and journals, because it is forgotten.

7) On important nodes (server, switch, base stations) you need to do emergency lighting from batteries and regularly check its performance. If possible, the batteries must also be connected to a computer to control equipment in an emergency.

8) Technical premises should at least be equipped with motion and fire sensors. If there are dry contacts on the equipment and the monitoring system, it is better to connect the fire alarm outputs (POS) to it. In our monitoring center we can see the opening of the door or an accident by temperature. PIC pins are connected to the dry contacts of the Huawei BS.

9) If you have equipment that requires maintenance, there should be a periodic maintenance schedule. And it should be followed so that it does not work like this:



10) If you are a contractor, or accept work from him, if your work is related to installation, take photos of everything. All the flaws, deficiencies, everything that was done wrong, everything needs to be photographed. Firstly, you can visually show where the shoals are, or prove that you did everything qualitatively, and secondly, this will help avoid controversial issues: ‚ÄúThis was not the case. It was so. ‚Äù Photos need to be stored, then to show how to or should not be done. We are our new installers or contractors, for example, we always drive around the objects and show what we will require of them.

11) Before you begin serious work, you must have a plan of action, and everything is prepared for the beginning.

12) 15 minutes before the end of the working day you need to start cleaning the room and workplace. Sometimes it is better not to finish the work today, but to have time to put everything in order.

13) "Nothing is more permanent than temporary." In doing any work, do not think that it is for a while - do it right away well.

14) Well, in general, treat your work responsibly, and find fault with those who do this work, because in the end you will have to exploit it later.

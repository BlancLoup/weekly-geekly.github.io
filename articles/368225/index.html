Study: restaurant food harms a figure no less fast food



Modern restaurants introduce new automation technologies, use design , and also pay attention to reducing noise and creating the right light .

Manipulating visitors is not so difficult - we have already considered the tricks that institutions use to make the guests pay more. Among other things, it is believed that in restaurants food is more useful for the figure than in fast food restaurants. But is it really?

New data


Not so long ago, a study of scientists from the Illinois Institute was presented in the European Journal of Clinical Nutrition. According to their data, fast food in some respects may even be a little healthier than food in full-service restaurants. So how dangerous is eating out?

According to the results of a new study, restaurant food can cause overweight just like fast food. This once again confirms that people who want to lose weight should not frequent places of public catering. Oddly enough, this study showed that fast food may not be as harmful as eating in full-service restaurants.

Researchers studied medical records of more than 18,000 American adults participating in the National Health and Nutrition Examination Program. This number included patients observed from 2003 to 2010.

Scientists have concluded that by eating in full-service restaurants or fast food, we get up to 200 extra calories per day. This can happen because visitors to full-service restaurants, succumbing to the persuasion of waiters, can eat more food. At that time, as visitors of fast food restaurants are unlikely to begin to drive up to the window of an auto cafe the second or third time for an additional portion.

Restaurant food is rich in vitamins and minerals, unlike fast food or homemade food. However, it also contains more cholesterol and sodium than fast food.

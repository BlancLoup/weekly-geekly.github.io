What is Asperger Syndrome?


As Wikipedia says, Asperger's Syndrome is one of five common developmental disorders, sometimes called a form of high-functioning autism (i.e. a mild form of autism, in which the ability to socialize is relatively preserved). Often, individuals with Asperger syndrome have normal or high intelligence, but are distinguished by non-standard or underdeveloped social abilities; often because of this, their emotional and social development, as well as integration, take place later than usual.

To be simpler: Asperger syndrome is a weak autistic disease. In the majority, like autists, they live in their own world and, sacrificing social skills, they develop themselves independently, constantly analyzing, studying, but only what interests them.
Their social and emotional skills are muffled and most of them do not know how to respond to many situations, so they seem to be either ‚Äústupid‚Äù or terribly calm.
However, despite everything, they have a rich and developed inner world, where every situation from reality is displayed and easily adapts to the inner universe, where they are heroes and / or always appear in the center of events. At any time, they can quickly analyze and present the situation to the smallest detail that ordinary people may find strange. Most often, this world is strongly connected to reality, therefore their memory is rather long-lived: they can easily tell you a situation from life to such moments as where the pencil lay and the circle stood and how they were turned.
Because of this, they have a very good spatial and logical thinking. Also, most situations are modeled in advance and to the smallest detail, i.e. "How to behave if it happens." Because of this, they have such an important moment in life as intuition, which they have just a clear miscalculation. Very often they can build you in their own world and understand where you lie to them and how you will behave in a situation and be right. They can quickly disassemble you in detail, but not understand your motives.

Asperger syndrome is a very difficult disease to identify. On average, almost 10% of people do not know about their disease, however, they often have communication difficulties.
For the most part, there are several reasons, and most often they are contradictory:
- bright or otherwise absent expression of emotions in non-verbal communication. Absent glance in the eyes or, on the contrary, severe restraint of the direct gaze;
- weak or absent development of relations with peers to the age-appropriate level;
- lack of emotional expression, i.e. spontaneous joy, easy understanding of jokes;
- lack of emotional and social reciprocity of relations;
- strong withdrawal and / or frequent intensity of one or several interests;
- the constant performance of any standard rituals and heavy attitude to their change;
- strong enthusiasm for details and / or objects;
- late development of meaningful speech;

Legends and Myths


1. Autistic people are stupid people.
Not true. For the most part, these people at the age of 13 have logical connections and knowledge at the level of professors. Just because of the impossibility of normal communication, it is difficult to communicate.

2. Asperger syndrome can hurt anyone
Almost true. Asperger's syndrome may hurt your neighbor or even you, but do not understand this or even never hear about it, however, the disease is often confused, for example, with ADHD. The percentage of patients is about 0.7% - 0.9%.

3. All programmers and system administrators have Asperger Syndrome.
Lying. Most of them are sociable people or simply self-limiting. Autism communication with people is simply not required. They do not need it.

4. Autists don't care about you, they don't feel anything.
Also a lie. Very often they experience much more. Wrong movement you can drive him into a stronger silence or hostility to his person. However, you may not even understand this, since they cannot express it.

5. Autists have no children or families.
Almost true. For the most part, due to the fact that they cannot interact socially normally, they do not start families, because they cannot even find a couple, however, there are those who can live in society and even develop greatly.

6. Autists are geniuses like Kim Pick .
Partially true. Among them there are often gifted children. Since the lack of social development is compensated by self-development in their own world. However, this person may be quite common in his development and not stand out.

7. Autists do not work in senior positions.
Also partly wrong. They are difficult to communicate, however, they may well lead. With the help of special training, they can develop social skills quite normally.

8. Autism is inherited.
Yes and no. Autism can wake up through several generations. Autistic children can be quite normal people.

9. Autists cannot work.
Yes and no. Pronounced autists need constant care, however, people with Asperger syndrome may well live and not be aware of their illness.

10. This diagnosis can be easily established.
Not. It is very difficult for an adult to make this diagnosis, however, it is still easier for children. And then, the diagnosis goes on for months, and sometimes for years.

11. Autists do not achieve anything in life.
Also a lie. Many autists have tremendous knowledge (savant syndrome). Many people were autistic, such as Albert Einstein, Iisac Newton, Steven Spielberg.

Rocket launch from East with your own eyes



Finally, we managed to see the launch of a space rocket from a fairly close distance. At the Vostochny Cosmodrome, on December 27, 2018, the Soyuz 2.1a rocket was launched with a payload into low Earth orbit. A dozen people and one blogger were a mile from the launch pad.



The observation point is equipped in case of extreme cold weather, which are not uncommon on Amur. This morning was -27 ¬∞. In the cold, the batteries of the cameras lost their charge, their phones went out, their lenses were covered with frost, but people were hiding in the room and warming themselves with hot tea with taiga herbs.



Traditional Siberian landscapes: raketa, samovar, baranki, lacking only medved and balalaika.



Together with the guests, journalists and staff, the launch was monitored by the optical system of the command-measuring complex of the cosmodrome.





And around the taiga ...



About an hour before the launch, the service tower drove off to the side, opening a rocket. The location of the survey was chosen not the best, but it is safe from the point of view of the rocket‚Äôs flight path, and there are not many other elevations around.

A metering error in the camera allowed a better view of the flame structure. The lateral streams of steering combustion chambers are clearly visible.



Over the taiga echoed the roar and crackle of the flame. The body felt low-frequency air vibrations.



A large cloud of steam, smoke and dust has flown to the right. It seems the steam is usually smaller, but the severe frost has increased the effects of condensation.



The rocket rotates around its axis.



And falls on the course.



The cloud of the earth grows even more.



In the clear sky, a soot trace of kerosene fuel is clearly visible. Despite soot, kerosene is considered an environmentally friendly fuel, although the cleanest is hydrogen-oxygen engines.



At a certain height, the engine exhaust again causes condensation processes in the atmosphere, and a white trail is pulled from the rocket. Usually it is mistakenly called "inversion", although the correct term is "condensation".



Condensation trail casts an unexpectedly contrasting shadow. This is rarely seen on an almost clear sky.
In general, the weather was lucky, but, they say, there are up to 300 clear days a year. But the guests from overcast Moscow were glad to see the blue sky.



As the height increases, condensation stops, and the shape of the flame jet begins to change due to changes in atmospheric pressure.

Gradually, the rocket "goes to the point", and the white translucent trail is lost against the background of a pale cloudy haze. In the photo, perhaps, there are two flaps of the head fairing - two points on the sides of the smoke trail.

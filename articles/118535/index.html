3Q Surf Tablet PC Qoo review. More than it seems

Good afternoon, comrades!

Having taken care of the second PC for my wife, we would find the most optimal option that we can take with us, namely, the Tablet PC.

Of all the presence that is present in the Russian market (and this is no more than 5-7 pieces), the most correct was made (in my opinion, of course) the choice in the direction of the tablet from a domestic company, 3Q, Surf Tablet PC Qoo .





I apologize in advance for the protective film already pasted on the display, the review was not done right away.

The purchase price was 18,999 rubles , the charging cable was included, a small instruction sheet and a driver disk, so I did not want to take such a scanty bundle.

Part 1, appearance.




I was very pleased with the display - it is quite bright, there is a capacitive multitouch (2 presses), a light sensor and pretty good viewing angles.



A set of communication capabilities will be envied by any other tablet: there are 2 USB 2.0, RJ-45, mini VGA, 2 audio ports (input and output), a microphone, a front-facing camera, a slot for memory cards.









On the right side there are 4 touch buttons. Work everywhere; Up and down are similar to buttons on the keyboard, OK is similar to Enter.



On the top panel there are: a microphone, a power button (with indicator) and a battery charge indicator.



In the following photos you can evaluate the quality of the display (brightness set to maximum). Pictures were taken in the daytime by the window, the MTS 916 smartphone is also present for comparing sizes and display.







Front-camera.



The thickness of this tablet is 18 mm.



Back cover.



The build quality is at a height, there is no backlash and squeaks. The back cover is made of SoftTouch plastic, shielded from the inside to protect against any interference).

The front panel is glossy, very branded, but it can be fixed by gluing a protective film. It also weighs a lot, 915 grams, is comfortable in the hand.

Another couple of photos.





Part 2, what's inside.


It is not difficult to disassemble it, you only need to remove a small part of the cover (rather tight, so that the cover is not lost).



Turn off the 2 bolts.



And voila:



I think the engineers had to try, putting so much in here:

HDD.


WiFi miniPCIe module and its antenna.


Network Controller.


CPU and chipset.


Memory bar 2 GB.


Bluetooth module.


Internal USB port.


Battery.


The speakers.


And now the most delicious, inside there are 2 unsoldered miniPCIe slots. For one of them, I already ordered the HSDPA 3G + GPS module (Sierra Wireless MC8781), after which it will acquire all that it lacked. The price of the module on ebay is only about $ 30.





There will be a sim card.




Part 3, characteristics, performance.



And so, on board we have:
  • Intel Atom N450 1.66 GHz ;
  • 320 GB hard drive;
  • DDR2 memory 2 GB 667Mhz ;
  • Realtek ALC662 ;
  • Capacitive 10 "Multiouch display 1024x600 px ;
  • Light sensor and accelerometer ;
  • Camera 1.3 Mpx ;
  • miniVGA output;
  • WiFi b / g / n + Bluetooth 2.1 + EDR ;
  • Network controller Realtek 1 Gb / s;
  • Built-in microphone;
  • 2 USB ports;
  • 2 audio jacks;
  • Li-Ion battery with a capacity of 33600 mW * h;
  • Dimensions of the device, 263x168x18 mm.

The average work time is about 3.5 hours. With minimum brightness and WiFi turned off, it will work for more than 4 hours (read a book). If you turn off the display and just put it, then it will work for about 5 hours.

OS boot time is about 45 seconds. Windows 7 rated 2.3 - let down the CPU.


CPU is certainly weak, but you can watch HD rip on 720p network. Although he stumbles. And so, you can easily watch some kind of film in the evening, this one can do it.

Among the shortcomings, a poor wifi antenna. But it is planned to replace it. More than minuses did not reveal, except that the battery ... But what to take from it.

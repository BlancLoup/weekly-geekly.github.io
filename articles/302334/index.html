Examples of research projects
Team About Blank . According to recently updated legislation, people whose profiles have more than 3,000 subscribers are equal to the media. The topic of media influence on the masses is very interesting both from a scientific point of view and commercially. The guys took up the study of these user-hubs: they found interesting information about profiles, found out the approximate ratio of the number of bots and real people, identified the prevailing types of content on their walls, tried to identify the signs that caused their popularity. They showed that these users do post in most cases photos and audio recordings, and also have open profiles and receive messages from all users.

Developing this idea, one can understand what the audience is interested in, subscribing to these people, finding out their hobbies, as well as influencing them. The information obtained will allow you to engage in effective promotion of advertising, designed for specific groups of users in the network. It is worth highlighting this project, because the guys are one of the few who took up a practical task and brought it to the stage of a working prototype with the possibility of future monetization of the service as an analog of Ya.Direkt and Google.Ads.

Team Map of connections . In this project, the guys took up a rather interesting study - the study of patterns of communication between students from different schools in Moscow. The density of ties between students from different schools was investigated depending on the location and the presence of a matching profile of the schools being compared. Graphs of connections between students were constructed and so-called connectivity components were obtained, showing how certain groups of schoolchildren are divided into circles of communication, as well as visualization of connections on the map of Moscow. Such data can be used to improve the interaction of schools in various fields: from the Olympiad movement to sports and social activities.

Team 10011001 . A team of students has developed an application that allows you to search for data dependencies automatically and not to build hypotheses manually. With the help of this functional, they built correlations of various social features of the existing sample of schoolchildren on the social network VKontakte: the connection of their main interests, musical and literary preferences with gender differences, with nationality, lifestyle and many other characteristics. The main value of their work is the ability to set any input data and get the most significant correlations that can be used to build the average attitude of the selected group of people to the topic of interest to us.

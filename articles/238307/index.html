Hub AnyMote Home will make any device in the house smart



Literally every month, manufacturers of gadgets offer all the new devices belonging to the category of "smart". The convenience of working with such devices is that they can be controlled remotely, via the Internet, plus setting up different ‚Äúmodels of behavior‚Äù of each device for various kinds of factors: temperature change, arrival or departure of the owner, movement, and so on.

Nevertheless, in the home of any gadget lover there are still a lot of home appliances that are not classified as ‚Äúsmart‚Äù. Many users would not mind including ordinary household appliances in the smart home system, but it is sometimes difficult to do this. Plus, many devices come with remote controls that are constantly lost.

The other day, the device was announced, AnyMote Home, which can solve this problem.



By the way, not so long ago, the Medgadgets blog published news about a device that could make any air conditioner ‚Äúsmart‚Äù. AnyMote Home is a much more versatile device.

AnyMote Home Features



Developers position AnyMote Home as a hub for all devices that can be controlled using the remote control. The hub works with all gadgets that communicate via infrared, as well as some devices that use Bluetooth and WiFi to communicate.



The main goal of developers is to enable the user to control all of their devices (or at least most of such devices) via a smartphone, by using the appropriate application, Smart IR Remote. It can be air conditioners, televisions, players, audio equipment and much more.



Via the smartphone, the command is transmitted to the hub, and from there to the specific device. At the same time, the signal is emitted by AnyMote Home by 360 degrees, so that there are no dead zones within the line of sight.

Price



Now developers are raising funds to launch their devices into mass production. 122 thousand dollars have already been raised instead of the 50 thousand plan, so in May AnyMote Home will go on sale at a price of about 70 US dollars. In principle, this is not such a big price for, in fact, a universal control panel.

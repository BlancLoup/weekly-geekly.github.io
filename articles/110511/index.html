Google and Facebook Sunrise

Translation of the article by Brian Solis Google and the Rise of Facebook
Author translation: Ksenia Lim

In 2007, I predicted that Facebook would become the personal brand's homepage. Now it has officially become a reality.

A couple of days ago, I logged into my Facebook account and noticed at the top of the page a new message that suggested I make Facebook my home page. (This option makes it possible to see the dynamics of the activity of my friends as soon as I open the browser.) By the way, it was not only me who noticed the innovation.

I decided to write this post not only to tell you what you would know about yourself. I want to develop this topic.

Facebook started out as a regular social network, which eventually evolved into a personal operating environment, where information and people who can come from outside the Facebook sphere interact. And you are the center of this complex system. Facebook is a convenient platform where people can communicate among themselves on the three principles of information commerce. More than a million websites around the world are somehow integrated into the Facebook platform.

150 million users per month use Facebook to communicate on other Internet resources.

Two thirds of the websites from the US ranking and half the websites from the global comScore ranking are integrated with Facebook.

I do not need to "google", it is better to "facebook"



Over the past years, Google has failed to establish its own social network. Probably because the company is fixated on the culture of the code, instead of deciphering the culture of human behavior.

Apparently, in the future we are waiting for the war of giants for social profiles. Judging by the efforts Facebook is making, in order to divert you from Google towards social media, this war has already begun. The option of adding a Facebook homepage will surely attract even more active users around the world. Let's see how much this social network will make its starting point for access to the Internet.

Such a seemingly small step will greatly affect our behavior and will teach us to trust friends more if we want to learn something, get support or just have fun.

To get inspiration, we turn to the flow, not Google ...

Those events that are unfolding before our eyes - this is perhaps the beginning of the end of the dominance of Google in the global network. This is the rise of the Facebook economy (F-commerce), where commerce is expressed in the currency of information, involvement and equity of human relations. While Facebook does not publicly announce its superiority, it simply helps to communicate, learn new things and share interesting things with friends. While you enjoy being at the center of your social egosystem, Facebook simply continues to develop products and services that make this system more efficient.

Tweet this to me ... Facebook generation



And what about Twitter? I think this is a moon that orbits a planet called networking. It affects the ebb and flow, and also determines the speed of rotation of the planet.

Twitter is your window to current events, and Facebook is your homepage in the world of social networking.

According to recent Hitwise data, Facebook accounts for 25% of all web views in the United States. And this trend will intensify as we increasingly trust Facebook. So far, in all that concerns the search, Google is the undisputed leader. But Hitwise believes that Facebook has already overtaken Google in the number of views. In my previous posts, I wrote that according to comScore, Facebook is coming Google on the heels. In any case, the shift of traffic from Google towards Facebook is just a matter of time.

At the moment we are witnessing the birth of social consumers, therefore, the choice of the future will most likely be for Facebook.

As I summarized earlier, emails are no longer a means. The tool in social media is the platform, as well as its participants. People are now both a means and a subject of communication, and boundaries are designated by a social profile, context and quality of connections, which, as we have said, can be strong, weak and temporary. The Facebook platform serves as the basis for our own social environment in which we act as a driving force. Every our action meets equal opposition. As part of the social environment, we begin to understand that using Facebook gives our social profile a greater push than if we relied solely on Google.

Welcome to the Facebook generation! Have you already made Facebook your homepage?

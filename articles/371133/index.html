In Russia, the cancellation of the notification of foreign electronics

The government has once again changed the rules for the transportation through customs of goods from foreign online stores. But this time for the better! Some of the most popular categories of gadgets are derived from the requirements of the notification of the FSB. What does this mean for ordinary Internet buyers and is it worth it to open champagne in advance? Parcel explains.




What is a notification?


We wrote a large informational article about this (rather strange) phenomenon. In short, all gadgets entering Russia must have special permission from the FSB - that they do not pose a threat to national security. This restricts the importation of equipment into the country, which "could potentially be used for espionage, interception of conversations in networks of cellular operators and the deployment of complex cryptographic systems."


In practice, this requirement of the FSB concerns almost all electronics: smartphones, laptops, tablets, computers and their parts. All this can theoretically be used to transfer data to foreign agents. Therefore, manufacturers of equipment require permission from the FSB, and without it, the transport of such gadgets into the country is considered illegal.


The system has been operating since 2010, and major manufacturers have become accustomed to it. Apple and Samsung, for example, notify the state in advance. organs about their new models and send those. documentation for your devices to start their sales in the Russian Federation as soon as possible. And smaller manufacturers and all those who are not very interested in the Russian market, do not bother with this, and as a result, their products cannot be imported through customs. More precisely, you can bring something, but if you check it, wrap it back, or make you pay a fine. And maybe they can come back from the bodies, saying that it is you, a foreign spy?


Actually nothing new. The FSB notification system has been operating since January 1, 2010, but before that there were many similar initiatives. Old-borovichki remember those times when the printer or copier had to be registered in the Ministry of Internal Affairs, almost as a weapon. Only legal entities could have them, and they had to be stored in special rooms with a lock and alarm system. And the owners of mobile phones before 2000 were obliged to carry with them permission from the State Communications Authority.


Now the same story - with smartphones, laptops, computers, routers, routers, any devices with Wi-Fi or Bluetooth (including wireless mice and keyboards), walkie-talkies, microchips, and a lot of other equipment. For delivery companies such as the Parcel Post , this is a big headache. Each device must be checked. Otherwise, the client will have to return all the money, and apologize for the delay. Clients themselves are very rarely notified of the gadget they are interested in, according to our experience. Many do not even realize that such a system exists in our country.


You can see the availability of the notification on the device in the Unified Registry . Or, the second option is to check the availability of goods in respectable Russian stores that will not sell goods that bypassed customs. Until recently, it was a mandatory procedure when buying any new (or not very well-known) device abroad.

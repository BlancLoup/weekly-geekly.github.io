Purity


I think everyone knows what forms inside the computer under the table for a couple of years. To prevent this from happening, we have:
  • Increased pressure in the server relative to atmospheric. This does not mean that the door is difficult to close, but several dozen pascals completely determine the direction of air flow when the door is opened, and hence the direction of dust movement.
  • Daily (this is not a joke) wet cleaning of the server room, several times a day the corridor is removed, along which the engineers pass from their premises to the server room
  • Shoe covers at the entrance (about them below)
  • Air conditioner air filters that are regularly cleaned and checked

Thanks to these measures, the servers remain clean. I myself was very surprised when, during experiments with the equipment, I opened a server that worked in the server for at least a year, and all I saw was a barely noticeable layer, similar to what forms on a regular table in a day.

A little about the boot covers. At first we had ordinary boxes with shoe covers that had to be worn on our own. All this was very much needed and a ‚Äúbachilator‚Äù was bought - a device for putting on shoe covers. Unfortunately, normally he did not work and we again returned to manual dressing.



Then we bought a more cool model that works fine (it is she who is in the photo to the left of the box with shoe covers). The principle of operation is simple - the foot is put on a thin sheet of plastic, they step on the swinging platform, the plastic is wrapped around the sole, heated by warm (hot? But it is not felt) air and hardens. As soon as the foot is removed, the next sheet of plastic is blown out. After the bahilier proved itself well, a second one was bought, for putting on both legs at the same time. The only drawback of these boot covers is that they rustle very much when walking.

Idea for social network

Social networks. Today, they are very often. And millions of users spend a great amount of time in them. Someone they annoy, someone lives in them.

I will try to outline the main points:
1. The key role in this network is your mood at the moment.
2. Interaction with other users - again, determined by your mood .
3. The overall design and advertising (although it may well not be) - also depends on your mood .

  • To begin with, the user is prompted to register, where it is necessary to indicate his present location (the first key moment) .
  • After registration, the user gets to the main page, where he is offered to rate his mood at the moment (2nd key moment) . The assessment will be conducted on a 7-point scale from the worst to the best mood and framed in the appropriate colors for a better perception.
  • And now the fun begins. The data falls into a common array from which the next image is generated ...


We see in front of you a map of the region / country indicated at registration. We see it as a contour , as on a geographic map, with the only difference that each region corresponds to the region where the certain mood prevails among users. Each such region with a predominant mood has a corresponding color (which we saw when choosing our present when entering the site). Those. as a result, we see in front of us something like a weather map, only in the mood of the people.

Why do we need it:
1. Go to the site and indicate your current mood - a matter of 20-30 seconds, i.e. not much tears you away from work, etc. - but by doing this you contribute to the general level of mood in your city / region / country.
2. You have the opportunity to assess the mood of the people around you.
3. You can observe the reaction to various events - the presidential election \ natural disasters and so on.
4. You can quickly find a person with the same mood as yours (and not read depressive status on Twitter, etc.)
5. And this list can be continued indefinitely starting from the interests of the average user, ending with the possibility of researching and observing moods around the world and their changes.

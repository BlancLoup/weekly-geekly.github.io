By exclamation mark



- The exclamation mark (!) Is often used in advertising letters.

Yes! Our goods are cheaper! Try them now!

In all other commercial letters, exclamations should be avoided.

Several additions in upper and lower case letters



- Upper case letters are used when writing names and titles.

They are necessarily used when writing adjectives that indicate the nationality, the name of the day of the week and the month.

- Capital letters are used to give a word special meaning:

Try the Sunset product (SUNSET)!

or name highlighting:

We hope that our representative Mr. IVANOV will visit you soon.

Words written in capital letters can be replaced with italics, or words written in bold letters, underlined words or quoted.

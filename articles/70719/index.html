Become a judge of Android Developer Challange 2. A program for volunteer judges has appeared on the Android Market

Two days ago, Google finally published on the Android Market a program for judges of the Android Developer Challenge 2. The results of the first round are entirely dependent on user ratings! There are about 2 weeks to vote.

An incomplete list of programs hosted on ADC 2 has been published.
There are about 100 programs in the list, but in fact there are many more. About 50 I have already managed to see and rate them, and the Judging app offers to see all new and new applications. Wife will be unhappy with how I spend the weekend: (

In google groups the program for judges is scolded, but it seems to me that it is not bad at all. The interface is simple and clear - install the program, evaluate the program, skip / show the following.

Most of the programs fall into the category Productivity / Tools, Games / Puzzles, Social networks. Few Entertainment and Education / Reference (lucky developers in these categories - almost all will reach the final).

The quality of programs has improved significantly with ADC 1. Originality of ideas, too. Saw 5 quite good Augmented Reality programs. More than a dozen Twitter clients. 6 almost identical programs for maintaining the budget. Excellent ports of old Soviet games like "Well, wait!". There is a good interactive book for girls (the idea recalls the "Primer for Noble Maidens" from Stevenson's Diamond Age). I also liked the program that determines how drunk the user is. She thought I was drunk on the board. Probably felt that I was going to Oktoberfest in the evening :)

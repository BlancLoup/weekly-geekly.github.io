All new buildings in San Francisco up to 10 floors high will be equipped with solar panels

City authorities want to fully switch to renewable energy sources by 2025



California can be called one of the most technologically advanced states in the USA. At the same time, San Francisco is one of the most advanced cities in the same sense, not only in the USA, but also in the world. And the matter is not even in electro-gas stations, stations for charging phones, robobiles and other things found here at every step.

The fact is that the city authorities are trying to make their settlement better. Now the authorities have adopted a new law, according to which it is necessary to install solar panels on all new buildings up to 10 floors high. Until now, the installation of the panels themselves was optional, although desirable. Presumably, in a developing city like San Francisco, by 2025, many new buildings will appear. Consequently, there will be many solar panels.

Now other countries and cities are trying to more actively use alternative sources of energy. These include, in addition to solar panels, wind power stations, solar thermal power plants, geothermal power stations, etc. Wind energy records set, for example, Denmark . And in 2015, Costa Rica received 99% of electricity from renewable sources.

Face to the future

Opera's face control provides browser control

Oslo, Norway - April 1, 2009

Company Opera Software today announces the next innovation, which will be included in Opera 10 - Facial Control (Face Gestures). This technology allows any webcam owner to work with a browser using their own face. The development is based on the same architecture as Mouse Gestures, which makes browsing the web as easy as smiling, winking, or wiggling your nose.



Facial management was the result of the joint work of Opera and the Norwegian Secret Service - NIS (Norwegian Intelligence Service) to create the most effective image recognition technology based only on JavaScript, HTML and CSS. After more than seven years of experimentation, the Facial Management system is ready for public testing.

Opera's facial control includes more than 45 functions, from opening a new tab and restoring sessions to navigating web pages. Also, the Facial control is integrated into the built-in mail client browser Opera. Moreover: Facial control can even partially replace Mouse control. To activate the new technology, just press the F8 key (supported only in Opera 10).

About Opera Software ASA

The company Opera Software ASA has opened up new possibilities for viewing web pages on PCs, mobile phones and other Internet devices. The cross-platform browser technology developed by Opera is known for its functionality, standards compliance and small size, while providing users with faster, safer, and more dynamic access to the Internet. The main office of Opera Software is located in Oslo, Norway, with offices around the world. The company is listed on the Oslo Stock Exchange under the exchange code OPERA.

How about VoIP and LAN, working on the principle of "set up and forget"? The new HP ProCurve 2520 switch is such a very peculiar outsourcing when you put worries about the normal operation of the network on hardware.


For your tasks, you can choose one of 4 models of the 2520 series with 8 or 24 ports (two models of Fast and Gigabit Ethernet). All models have interchangeable SPF bays with dual-use Gigabit ports that can be used to establish connections over both copper and fiber optic channels.

Our proprietary Auto-MDIX technology allows the switch itself to configure all ports for the desired cable type (straight or crossover).

Also, all models support Power over Ethernet (PoE) technology, which allows power supply to devices connected to the switch (up to 15.4 W per port) via the same cable that the data is transmitted to. Thanks to this, you can seriously simplify your life by placing VoIP phones, wireless hotspots and IP cameras in your office ‚Äî now employees can plug in their kettles to any outlet. And the LLDP-MED (Media Endpoint Discovery) extension will help automatically identify most devices.

Load from heavy traffic generators like cameras and telephones is also not a problem - packages up to 9216 bytes (model 2520G) are supported. And up to 8 ports can be combined per trunk, which, in addition to throughput, seriously improves fault tolerance by creating redundant connections. At the same time, the ability to limit the speed of traffic will allow to cut off the transmission of unwanted content.

Configuring complex VLAN configurations is greatly facilitated by the support of the IEEE 802.1s Multiple Spanning Tree protocol. On the basis of the ports, you can create up to 256 VLANs at the same time - with a large future margin, as they say.

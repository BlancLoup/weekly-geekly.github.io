Association of .NET communities



For several years now, an open .NET-community movement has been developing in Russia. The St. Petersburg community SpbDotNet , launched in 2015, became a pioneer. The Moscow community MskDotNet , which has been meeting since 2016, has become a follower of the St. Petersburg community. Of course, both communities are very good friends and provide all possible mutual assistance. Since the beginning of the creation of communities, dozens of meetings were held, more than a hundred reports were told. Fine! Let's talk a little about the present and future of .NET movements.

Today's Day


During the existence of our wonderful community, dozens of meetings were held , thousands of questions were discussed, both in the form of reports and in the form of live communication on the sidelines. Many met new colleagues, got useful connections, found answers to their questions, got a lot of positives, and some even found new friends. Those who for some reason do not attend, or can not attend our meetings live - watch our videos on youtube. By the way, at the moment our YouTube-channel is replenished with entries from MskDotNet and from SpbDotNet. More than 70 videos with reports have been posted on it . It sounds great!



























Moscow and St. Petersburg are the largest cities in Russia, but this does not mean that .NET-programmers live only in them. Of course, the ability to view online is good, but passive viewing online is passive, for those who are just watching do not participate directly in the life of the community. The most important thing in live meetings is communication and ... networking!

In short, there are active people in our country, and according to this, the duet MskDotNet and SpbDotNet can easily grow into a full-fledged orchestra in the near future, since two new communities are opening in Russia. Meet Saratovsky - SarDotNet and Krasnoyarsk KryDotNet ! Urrraaaaaa!

SarDotNet


SarDotNet is organized by Nikita Danilov. Nikita reviews reports, leads the VK group and, in fact, is the leader of the movement. The first meeting of Saratov participants was held on September 30, 2017.







Interestingly, the Saratov also hosted the Students Lofty Talk, dedicated to the analysis of the question - what is the .NET Back-end and Web Front-end development. It was organized by EPAM, with a guest representative from the SarDotNet community.

KryDotNet


If you think that the Krasnoyarsk community has just appeared, then a surprise awaits you. Yes, the .NET-community itself appeared quite recently, but in fact, Artyom Akulyakov (the leader of KryDotNet) and the D2D team have been supporting the programming community for more than three years. Three years old, Karl! By the standards of the life cycle of local communities, this is already a lot.









During this time, the guys held about ten full-scale conferences with attendance of up to 500 people, two of which were devoted to dotnet (D2D Just.Net and D2D Enterprise). Last year, Artyom Akulyakov (the leader of KryDotNet) and the Dev2Dev team decided to change the course, identified several specialized communities and conferences, left one big IT conference about everything. As a result, KryDotNet, a community of .NET developers in Krasnoyarsk, has emerged, which is part of the global DotNetRu movement. While there was one meeting. Plans to hold 4-6 meetings per year. Congratulations to the guys and wish them every success! If you are a worker from Krasnoyarsk, follow the link. If you are not a tutor, but an IT specialist and also from Krasnoyarsk, then you will be welcome here.

Forward to a bright future


As many as four .NET communities are a different matter. More communities - more reports, more discussions, more HYIP! But ... there is one moment. We live in a world where the level of information noise exceeds all reasonable limits. This fact necessitates the provision of convenient access to information. If we bring these arguments to the mainstream of our cozy communities, then, of course, it will become obvious that we all revolve around .NET, which means that it is not too reasonable to keep everything that our communities are doing in a scattered state. All .NET communities share the same principles and similar topics. The need for some aggregation of communities is not immediately overdue. Today, especially given the discovery of new sites, the need for globalization of the entire movement has become obvious to the leaders of the movements. The need for sharing reports, knowledge and news between community members has grown. That is why the leaders of the movements came to the decision to create an overmind - DotNetRu . DotNetRu is not a new community, but a common ‚Äúvirtual roof‚Äù for all .NET communities. What does this give the global Russian-speaking .NET movement?

Dotnetru


DotNetRu is a group of .NET independent communities of developers from all over Russia. We bring together people around the .NET platform to facilitate the exchange of experience and knowledge. We hold regular meetings to share news and best practices in the development of software products.

DotNetRu will be one place where you can watch videos of all reports, read news, share your knowledge or opinion on a particular topic. This is not just a news portal, it is a real union of Russian-speaking .NET communities. If you are going to organize .NET User Group - join, together - we are strong!

DotNetRu has the following goals:

  • get together, meet, discuss news, share problems and find solutions;
  • invite the best speakers, true professionals and unique authors;
  • find and prepare new speakers for the largest Russian conferences;
  • create and maintain a quality collection of video lectures


DotNetRu works closely with the DotNext team. If your dream is to speak at the largest Russian .NET conference, but you have never stepped on the stage, then our communities are exactly what you need. We will help you with the selection of requested topics, the preparation of high-quality presentations and the development of practice speeches.

DotNetRu will publish the most interesting news from the world of .NET and issue weekly digests, but you can not worry - there will be no spam in groups of local communities. In local groups, the events related to a particular city will remain mainly in order to avoid the sensation of news spamming.

Application for reports


Developing local communities, especially outside of Moscow and St. Petersburg, is an objectively difficult task. To be honest, one of the problems is the lack of speakers.

Therefore, DotNetRu encourages everyone to apply for reports! This is not at all scary. If you already have something to tell about - go forward unequivocally! If you have material, but it seems to you that it is not enough - contact the representative of one of the communities anyway, often the organizers can tell in which direction you can work through this or that topic. We will always help with presentations and give recommendations on the development of a particular topic. You can sit on excellent material for years and be afraid to take the first step towards public speaking. Believe me, the main thing is to start. We recommend reading the interview with the .NET development guru Andrey Akinshin on this topic.

Android mitap at Badoo office on June 17


Hello! On June 17, our office will host the first (for us) Android mitap. Presentations will be made by developers from Avito, Yandex, Odnoklassniki, from Badoo - I, Arkady Gamza. The start is at 12:00. Description reports - under the cut.

12:00 - 12:40 - Philip Uvarov, Avito
12:40 - 12:55 - Questions


12:55 - 13:05 - Break (you will have time to drink a cup of coffee and ask the speaker a couple more questions)


13:10 - 13:50 - Arkady Gamza, Badoo

Speakers and Topics


Philip Uvarov, Avito



Topic: Component tests: how to make the life of your QA a little easier?


The report will discuss component tests, including I will share the best practitioners that our team has developed, and I will tell you how they help us make a better product.


In particular, let's talk about:


  • What is a component test? What is the difference between unit, component and functional test?
  • why are component tests good and what problems do they help us solve?
  • how to minimize the cost of supporting component tests without saving on their reliability.

Arkady Gamza, Badoo



Topic: Android Studio can do more than you think.


We will discuss the useful features of Android Studio - the main tool of each Android developer.


I will speak:


  • about effective navigation;
  • about non-trivial search;
  • about display modes;
  • about debugging tools.

Andrey Sidorov, Yandex Browser



Topic: Mobile power consumption measurement and implementation in Continuous Integration.


During my speech, I will talk about:


  • energy metering device design;
  • the use of a device for analyzing the energy consumption of a smartphone;
  • automating the process of energy testing;
  • search for energy-intensive functions of the browser;
  • optimization and control of energy consumption in the browser.

Grigory Dzhanelidze, OK.RU



Topic: How to stop worrying and start running features.


Launching new features for any product is quite a dangerous thing, because so much can go wrong: a huge number of different bugs can get out (from device specific to bugs in the feature itself), the server can not stand it and in the end users can simply dislike the feature .


I will talk about how we launch new features, what problems we have with startups, we have had and how it all works in the Android client.


Theses:


  • feature toggles: what it is, why it is and how we made ours;
  • how we monitor and evaluate launches;
  • how feature toggles are friendly with manual testing and how they are taken into account in autotests.

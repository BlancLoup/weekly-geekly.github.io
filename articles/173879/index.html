What prevents domestic programmers to repeat the success of Zuckerberg

In the 90s, if you did not have Adidas shoes, you were a chode. In the 2000s, if you did not have a mobile phone, you were a chode. Now if you do not have your startup - you are a sucker.

An article on a similar topic was recently published in Ukrainian Forbes . The author of the note is Olga Belkova, MP, a former partner of the business accelerator Eastlabs. I would like to supplement the article with my own comments and observations, since I know that this topic is disturbing to many. All of the following is my personal opinion.

Not Zuckerberg united


The first thing that I would like to draw attention to is a great focus on the success of Zuckerberg, as well as the creators of Instagram (earlier such examples were Bill Gates, Steve Jobs and Google creators Sergey Brin and Larry Page). These examples are exceptions even for Silicon Valley, and cannot serve as a role model.

Everyone gasps - Zuckerman, Zuckerfeld! When he recently visited Moscow, everyone was in a slight bewilderment - a person can not connect two words, uninteresting, uncharismatic. And this is a cherished role model? You include the brain, without millions of investments, (and probably state support) such projects as FB are not born. People need a romantic tale about a single student - you will be a fairy tale. (with)

If you read articles about successful entrepreneurs of silicones in the same Forbes, then you can see that most of the successful entrepreneurs went very long to success - they graduated from prestigious universities, worked for 10-15 years at IBM, Microsoft, Apple, etc. and only then, having vast knowledge, means and connections, they started their own business.

Therefore, with beautiful stories about young guys who have earned billions of dollars from scratch, you need to be more careful.

Education


Another reason is bad education. Type learn theory, but you need to learn the practice.

I do not agree with this statement, because Practice orientation is the way to the world of outsourcing, in which we all live. The task of the theory is to force (or teach) to think, and a thinking person, unlike a rational person, is at a higher stage of evolution and will easily learn how to apply knowledge in practice.

But still, our education (as well as education within most IT companies) has drawbacks.

For example, IT professionals, as a rule, have little sense in marketing, sales, risk theory, law, etc. (although this knowledge is critical for creating a business), but they are forced to teach unnecessary subjects (on the move I can recall such wonderful subjects as ecology, chemistry, and labor protection for 3-5 courses).

The second problem is a completely different approach in our thinking and cultural features.

- What are you, the smartest?
- Sit down and do not hang out!
- Be simpler and people will reach for you!

This is not a complete list of phrases that are awarded to particularly active people, i.e. killing any desire to do differently.

Bad business environment


Rather, its absence. Political, economic and other reasons - you know what I mean. But, oddly enough, this is all solved in one way or another.

In the valley, no project survives without investment. Well, no matter how hard I would like to believe the opposite, it does not work. Most projects are unprofitable for 3-7 years (Twitter, Tumblr, Wordpress, Instagram), etc. So the question is, who can afford 5-7 years for us to work for the future in the hope that your classmate, Zuckerberg will buy you?

* The most interesting thing is that after receiving investment in silicone, projects are mainly done by our outsourcing companies.

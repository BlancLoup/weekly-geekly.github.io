New CEO Mozilla has become the creator of JavaScript Brendan Ike



In April last year, Mozilla announced that Gary Kovacs was leaving the post of CEO and that the company began to look for a replacement for him. At the time of the search for the duties of the CEO, Jay Sullivan, previously a former COO (Chief Operating Officer), performed. Today, after nearly a year of searching for a new CEO, Mozilla announced that the creator of JavaScript and CTO of the company Brendan Haik will take the post, writes TechCrunch.

In a popular statement, Ike thanked Jay Sullivan for his contributions to Mozilla and the Internet, and noted that his leadership was essential to the success of the company and Firefox OS in particular. Sullivan, who has worked for six years at Mozilla, will leave the company after the transition.

Ike started working in Netscape in April 1995, where he created JavaScript. He later became one of the founding members of the Mozilla Foundation, which was isolated from AOL in 2003, after which AOL completely ceased the development of Netscape in 2007. Mozilla appointed Ike as Technical Director in 2005.

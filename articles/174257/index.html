Evernote 5 for Android: new shooting modes, support for Moleskine Smart Notebook notebooks, shortcuts and search in documents



We are pleased to introduce a new version of (5.0) Evernote for Android. With this update, the app has received new photo modes, support for Evernote Smart Notebook notebooks, premium document search capabilities and much more. Below we look at these and other new features in more detail.

In addition, our partners, the online store Indinotes.com, offered a discount on Moleskine Evernote Smart Notebook notebooks for our users. This is also further.

New shooting mode


The new feature allows you to create multiple shots in a row and save them in one note. If any photo came out unsuccessful, simply delete it from the list before saving.



Document camera
Now, photographing any document or record on paper, you can enable on the screen a new shooting option. This is a document camera, it works like a scanner. Just activate this mode and you can digitize paper documents and handwritten notes and add them to Evernote. To do this, grab the document in a rectangle on the screen of your device and take a picture.

The application will determine the borders of the sheet, remove the shadows and adjust the image contrast, after which a photo will appear in your Evernote account, adapted for recognizing handwritten and typed text using our service. Then the photographed documents and records will be indexed and made available for search, as well as regular text notes.

Evernote Smart Notebook by Moleskine


When working with the Evernote Smart Notebook by Moleskine, the document camera becomes especially useful.



Points
Each page of the notebook has dots instead of lines. When shooting with the document camera, Evernote uses dots to align the image and adjust the contrast. As a result, you get bright and clear pages with notes and sketches. We optimized the notepad for handwriting recognition to ensure that all entries are searchable.

Shortcuts


Evernote for Mac users already know about shortcuts. And today we first implemented this feature in a mobile application. Shortcuts allow you to quickly jump to the notes, notebooks and labels with which you work most often. To create a shortcut, open the shortcut bar by swiping from the right edge of the screen when viewing a list of notes, notebooks or tags, as well as when viewing a single note.



Evernote labels are synced across platforms. If you have already created shortcuts on Mac, now they will appear in Android, and vice versa.

New premium feature: search in documents


Now, with each search query, premium users will get the appropriate results from the documents in your account, spreadsheets and presentations created in Microsoft Office and iWork. Save such documents in Evernote has become even more useful.

Recall that premium users can also search scanned PDF files.

Please note that when attaching a new office document to a note in Evernote, the application will take several minutes to process and synchronize before you can see the search results.

New in Evernote Business: tag support


Evernote Business users will be happy to know that we have added support for business tags in this update. When viewing tags, you can filter business tags and edit the tags assigned to records in business notebooks.

Redesigned design


We reworked the lists of notes to highlight each note and make it more convenient to select and view. We also redraw all the icons in the application, making them more visual. The new Evernote for Android has become better thanks to many small details, small improvements and improvements.

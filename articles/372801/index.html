Physical strength and mental abilities


Physical strength gives a great bonus to career and health. But for IT workers it‚Äôs more important that it also has a positive effect on mental abilities. Of course, the barbell and dumbbells will not objectively increase your IQ (only subjectively increase it in the eyes of others ), but they will help to significantly prolong an active intellectual life.

Researchers at the Faculty of Medical Sciences, University of Sydney (Australia) conducted a study under the program of Study of Mental and Resistance Training (SMART) among people over 55 years of age who showed the first symptoms of mild cognitive impairment (SCN). Note that such symptoms can occur not only in the elderly, but also in the young - under the influence of various forms of addictive behavior , that is, an obsessive need for certain activities. Such addiction causes impaired mental functions.

So, as part of an experiment at the University of Sydney, for the first time, there was a clear connection between muscle adaptation to increasing power loads and brain functioning. In other words, the cognitive functions of those participating in the experiment clearly correlated with muscle strength.

Participants in the experiment were divided into four groups:

  1. Strength exercises + computer mental training.
  2. Strength exercises + placebo computer training of mental abilities (view a video about nature).
  3. Computer training mental abilities + placebo strength exercises (gymnastics).
  4. Placebo strength training + placebo computer mental training.

Strength training included weight lifting sessions twice a week for six months. The weight for the exercise was chosen at 80% of the peak and increased with increasing physical strength.

Cognitive tests on the basis of the experiment showed that mental activity improved qualitatively in the first experimental group.

Further research will help to choose the most effective type and intensity of loads that are recommended for older people in order to increase muscle strength and, consequently, mental abilities. Scientists also want to find a cause that causes an improvement in people's mental abilities after prolonged physical exertion. This will help develop the most optimal program.

The research results were published on October 24, 2016 in the Journal of American Geriatrics (doi: 10.1111 / jgs.14542).

Now in New York, you can protest the penalty for improper parking on the Internet



In the US, one of the most unpleasant things for any driver is getting a parking ticket or driving a red light. It seems to be literally gone for a minute, and upon returning over the windshield you can see a rather big fine (in some places up to a couple of hundred dollars), or drove into the yellow light, and a picture came in by mail with a fine for driving a red light. According to American law, you can protest any fine in a special court, but if earlier you had to either go to court on the appointed day or write a paper letter to the court with evidence of your innocence, then from today you can fight for your innocence the internet.

For this you need:
  • fill out an application for an electronic hearing on a special website
  • Write why you consider yourself innocent, if available - add evidence of your rightness (photos, documents).

A special judge will review your application and within 30 days will make a decision on your case. You will receive an answer by email.

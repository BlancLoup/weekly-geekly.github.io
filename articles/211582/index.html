Results of CES 2014: Samsung AV News

Good afternoon, Habr!

Closing the series of posts following the CES 2014 exhibition, we would like to tell you about the Samsung news in the Audio / Video category, which both delight both the ear and the eye.



We'll start, perhaps, with the entry-level M5 speakers for the Samsung Multiroom wireless audio system, which allows you to play music from different audio sources located anywhere in your home. The columns can be used both autonomously and wirelessly to the device and additional M7 or M5 speakers to create a multi-channel system. Plug-and-play makes it easy to set up a Samsung device system: all you need to do is connect an optional Samsung hub to your router, turn on the power, and download the free application to control speakers and audio sources from your smartphone. The design of the system harmoniously fits into the space; the novelty can be positioned both vertically and horizontally.

The second audio novelty presented to the guests of the CES 2014 exhibition was the Samsung HW-H750 soundbar with a power of 320 watts. According to the developers, digital and analog technologies have come together in this uadio device. The slim and stylish metal case was definitely created with an eye on Samsung TVs. The soundbar is also compatible with the Multiroom lineup, which makes it part of a wireless audio system.



The sound console HW-H600 is designed to connect to televisions with a diagonal of 32 to 55 inches. Multi-direction technology provides 4.2 channel sound. This is the best solution for a TV in the bedroom or a small living room, where a more bulky system simply does not fit. Both the soundbar and the sound console can be connected to the TV without using any wires. They also support the Samsung TV Sound Connect feature, which outputs sound from the TV to the device via Bluetooth. On the "blue tooth" to these devices, you can connect and mobile device.



The MX-HS8500 GIGA System is the world's first component audio system with built-in speakers that is easy to move thanks to its wheels. The power of 2500 watts is enough to fill the sound really big, spacious house. Improved speakers are responsible for sound quality: new technologies have significantly reduced the weight of the vibration system and increased the sound pressure level. In addition, the diaphragm of the speakers, combining the necessary characteristics of rigidity and flexibility, ensures deep bass sound, and 15-inch subwoofers are able to achieve 35 Hz. A curious additional feature of the MX-HS8500 are 15 special lighting effects, including 8 DJ effects to create a club party atmosphere. The audio system connects to Samsung TVs via a wireless connection. At the same time, the sound quality remains at a high level thanks to the Bluetooth Hi-Fi codec.



In conclusion, Samsung introduced the HT-H7730WM home entertainment center, stuffed with the company's brand-name developments. Thanks to the collaboration with the world's leading audio company DTS, Samsung is ready to offer users a new DTS Neo: Fusion II codec. The virtual 9.1-channel sound of exceptional depth will allow you to immerse yourself in action as much as possible, for example, to hear the sound of the blades of a helicopter, as if it were landing right behind your back. This became possible due to the technology of expanding the audio stream, which now extends not only frontally, but simultaneously in both directions.

The HT-H7730WM promises high sound power through a combination of analog and digital amplifiers. Outdoor acoustics are complemented by front speakers with a variable angle of inclination, which will allow you to completely adjust the sound to your home space. 7.1-channel system includes 6 speakers (2 floor, 2 rear satellite, 1 center and 1 subwoofer).

The system Blu-ray player is based on the UHD Upscale Processor, which provides an image with a resolution 2 times higher than the standard 1080p video. The player can convert SD and HD to Ultra-High Definition (UHD) to comfortably watch your favorite movies and programs on the UHD TV screen.

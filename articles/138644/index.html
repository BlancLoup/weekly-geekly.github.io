‚ñå Televisions


It's time to turn to one of the most important areas of activity of Samsung Electronics - the TV segment. No wonder the company has been the world leader in this niche for 7 years. The presentation again described in detail the concept of Smarter Life implemented on Samsung TVs, which will be developed in three directions:



Smart Interaction - The Samsung ES8000, ES7000 and E8000 flagship TVs are equipped with an HD camera and two directional microphones, so you can control them with gestures or voice without using the remote. At the moment, 26 gestures and voice control in 30 languages, including Russian, are available to users. Moreover, now the smart TV has a face recognition function, which is designed to simplify the process of authorization in social networks, for example.



Smart Content - Smart TV applications that are available on Smart Hub on company TVs. At the moment, in the CIS market, Samsung offers about 300 applications, 40 of which are created by local developers. Also in the clip Smart Content includes AllShare technology, which allows you to send and store content from a smartphone in the "cloud" and download it to your TV. Now AllShare works exclusively through the synchronization service SugarSync, however, in the near future, Samsung plans to add support for the more popular DropBox and SkyDrive.



Samsung is completing its plans for developing Smart TV this year with an initiative called Smart Evolution . It is, without exaggeration, a very non-standard solution for updating TVs. Owners of models with support for the Samsung Evolution Kit, which include all the same ES8000, ES7000 and E8000, in 2013 will be able to upgrade both their software and hardware. Carrying out such an original upgrade by installing a special module, and, as Samsung representatives say, is very simple and can be done at home.

‚ñå Photo equipment


Samsung cameras also acquired new features and interesting functionality. The main innovation is the work with Wi-Fi-networks. Now the camera is able to immediately upload the captured images to photo hosting servers and social networks. In addition, there is another non-standard way to link the camera and mobile phone, using it as a viewfinder. The bundle works through the Remote ViewFinder application: so far only for Android devices. In the foreseeable future, Samsung promises to make a version of the program under other operating systems.

How to create your own IT company: the history of Provectus IT


In preparation for a unique event for the IT industry - online conference on B2B sales and marketing of IT services and products Conformato.org - we decided to interview several of our speakers.

Today our guest is Gennady Galanter - co-owner of Provectus IT - a person who has been selling IT solutions and services for almost 20 years.

The very same company Provectus IT impresses with its growth. October 10, 2010 - 22 employees. In 2014 there are already 270 people, and, as I understood, this is not the limit.

At the conference, the report of Gennady will be about the so-called "Whales" - big customers with millions of contracts: how to find them, how to build relationships with them and, finally, sell.

- It is known that you, at Siebel Systems, acted as Director, responsible for the 120-million product line. This is a very responsible position, so I would like to know what was your responsibility and what kind of experience did you get?

- To begin with, Siebel is a pioneer in CRM, which was absorbed by Oracle in 2006. By the time I had 15 years experience.

Much of what I know and can do today, I owe to the experience gained in Siebel. I was lucky - one of the distinguishing features of Siebel was that the company had a special attitude to recruitment. Natives of prestigious universities and elite colleges of the world were recruited. Thus, a company culture was created that promoted innovation in product creation, business development and a market approach.

I vividly remember the interview with my future manager at Siebel. I was asked a question: ‚ÄúWhere do you see your function as the leader of this group?‚Äù To this I answered with a story:

‚ÄúYou know, in the circus there is such a juggler, who has long sticks, and the plates are spinning on them. And he starts to unwind these plates, add even more sticks, unwind even faster. And here comes a moment in time when these plates start to slow down, they need to be twisted, but they just don‚Äôt have enough hands. For all the plates just do not keep track.

Somehow you need to let fall and break. The question is how? My opinion is: you need to determine which of these plates are porcelain, and which - clay. And let it fall clay plates, keeping the more valuable. "

After that, they immediately made me an offer to take up the position of Product Line Manager.

Siebel itself had an excellent definition of the role of a Product Line Director - this is the CEO of their product. Those. it was necessary to make all decisions in relation to a specific product. The company had 120 products, and they had to go out synchronously and choreographically 2 times a year. This means that it was necessary to build a Product Plan of your product in such a way as to be in time for the day when everyone else enters the market.

- Then you worked for 4 years as a Strategy Director at Exigen Services. What happened to the development of the company during this time?

- Exigen is a global company with offices in San Francisco, the Baltic States, Eastern Europe, St. Petersburg, Odessa and Dnepropetrovsk.

By that time, wage inflation in the field of Information Technologies had occurred in Eastern Europe. This led to a decrease in the marginality of outsourcing projects. And selling at a higher price was difficult, because at that time the buyer lacked a simple message.

My task was to develop an approach to the market in order to increase margins from services. It was necessary to create a unique ‚Äúvalue proposition‚Äù in order for the client to be willing to consciously pay more. But at the same time he understood that the service received in return compensates for the premium cost of services.

The fact is that Exigen's strategy was to hunt projects not for $ 100-150 thousand, but for $ 10-20 million. These are the projects that we now call "Whales". (I am going to speak about them at the conference.)

When we collected statistics on the success of large projects, it turned out that there were a lot of examples when multi-million projects failed.

We understood that there is a fundamental difference between the interests of the project‚Äôs customer and its performer. This happened because, in the conventional model, the client comes and pays the contractor for the watch, and carries all the risks. And then we came up with the model of "Flex-agility", in which the performer on the "same wavelength" with the customer and takes some of the risks. We developed a new approach to the market, patented this model, began to actively promote it and began to implement projects according to the Flex-agility methodology.

- How did you come up with the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãcreating your own company? And what is her success?

‚ÄúEverything here is trite and simple - I am by nature a serial entepreneur, i.e. a person who is constantly engaged in business activities.

In the summer of 2010, the food software company Selectica turned to me, which at that time could not sell its main product.
The owners could not understand what was going on, fired all the employees (up to the CEO) and recruited a completely new team. They began to lift each stone and see if there was a viper there. It turned out that the problem is in the Indian team of 50 people, which did not have an innovative approach to the product.

I understood perfectly the difference between Indian and Ukrainian programmers. This is also understood and the new part of Selectica. They called me personally and said: ‚ÄúGet on a plane to Odessa and start a business there‚Äù.

Since I am by nature a typical businessman and salesman, I needed an operator. It was then that I called Nikolai Antonov, whom I met back in Exigen. I managed to persuade him to join me, and now he is my full-time partner. I consider this my biggest professional victory.

Because an extremely important truth in life is that for success you need to surround yourself with people smarter than you.



Also part of our success is that we started our business from an opportunity that we had, and not from scratch. And I, being my entire career as a product buyer, know what questions I care about, and therefore I speak the same language with customers. That's how we built Provectus IT as a company that meets the needs of customers. And now the company already has 270 people.

And then I had the English word ‚ÄúAdvanced,‚Äù which means ‚Äúadvanced,‚Äù but unfortunately, all the domain names were already taken. Then we showed creativity and found the word ‚Äúprovectus‚Äù - this is advanced, but in Latin. Thus, Provectus IT is an advanced, advanced information technology.

- As a person with great professional experience, make a forecast for the coming years: which IT areas will be developed most quickly in outsourcing?
- I can only tell what I see in Silicon Valley.

1. In the United States, a law was adopted, known as Obamacare , which resulted in fundamental changes in the field of payment for medical services. Doctors will receive much less from insurance companies.

And now you need to optimize many things that previously could be neglected. The entire medical database was stored in paper form (in folders in the cabinets). Now all this data will be digitized and the subsequent processes will be computerized.

For example, now the doctor will go to the patient not with a folder of papers, but with an iPad. Instead of the doctor asking the patient what diseases he had previously, whether there are allergies, etc., the patient will be issued an iPad on which he will fill everything. And by the time of the doctoral examination, the doctor will have all the data about the person. In connection with such changes in the medical structure, we see a huge increase in needs in the field of outsourcing services.

Perhaps in the future, they will be integrated into clothing. There is a development of biotechnology and their combinations with IT.

I think this system will be improved. For example, wearables will talk to the information domain. Having measured the condition of a person, he will request in the central database, maybe a person should drink orange juice, eat some fruit or take a pill to maintain health. This will be a qualitative transition to the formation of the ecosystem of each person.

Today, such technologies are seen as a big trend.

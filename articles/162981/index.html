War and the world of technology business at TechCrunch Moscow 2012



After the spring raid on Germany with a passing visit to CeBiT 2012, we decided that it would be nice to speak in our own open spaces. At about the same time, our director came across the Habrovsky announcement of TechCrunch Moscow, and we unanimously decided: ‚ÄúOf course we will go!‚Äù, Especially since the occasion was worthy - the launch of a stable version 7.0 and the first official release of Teamlab Office .

War and the world of technology business


This year, the theme of the event was chosen specific - "War and Peace". The idea is very interesting: young, daring newcomers and startups are against market leaders, established traditions and monsters of the IT industry. We, with our ambitions, of course, were among the bold startups.



For two days in non-stop mode, you could learn all about the latest trends in cloud technologies, the best ways to attract investments, successful case studies to foreign markets and, of course, exchange contacts. After two days of participation, we were convinced that the atmosphere at TechCrunch Moscow is indeed very emotional. In the corridors one could easily meet Anton Nosik, Petr Didenko and Mike Butcher himself, the editor-in-chief of TechCrunch Europe. The bar managed to have a snack with the guys from Bitrix, in the meantime talk with representatives of Mail.ru Group and Ckolkovo. Official and pathos - zero, and this, you know, is very conducive to pleasant communication. For those who plan to participate in TechCrunch 2013, of course, there are parting words.

First, by pitches. Do not pour water. No water at all. You have only 180 seconds to explain the uniqueness of your product, and the unsubstantiated ‚Äúworld's first‚Äù or ‚Äúunique technology‚Äù is not shot here. Clearly: problem, solution, audience, market, competitors, advantages, plans for distribution. Unfortunately, this time there were those who for 3 minutes failed to show the product once.

Secondly, let me speak from my heart. The official language of TechCrunch is English. Among the guests, the press and the most influential members of the jury there are people who DO NOT UNDERSTAND in Russian. If you realize that your English is far from intelligible, then you need to do something with it. It will be very offensive if the uniqueness of the project can not be assessed only because of the language barrier.

Thirdly, in addition to the speeches, you have to do a lot of work at the stand, which you should also carefully prepare for. Make a presentation for plasma as attractive and tasty. When a person walks along the corridor of 44 booths, the screenshots and texts will not catch him exactly. Prepare a workplace: the lack of laptops and talkative representatives of your project is a trifle for which you may miss someone important.

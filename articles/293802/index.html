Pavel Durov sold the Vkontakte data center for 1 billion rubles



In early June, Megamozg announced that Pavel Durov, the founder of "Vkontakte", sold the data center that provides the social network to the current owners of the resource. The exact amount of the transaction was not disclosed by its participants, but experts assumed that Durov received about 500 million rubles from the buyer. In fact, the data center was sold for an amount of 919 million rubles, writes Izvestia. This became known from the report Mail.ru Group for the first half of 2015.

Since its inception, the social network Vkontakte has used the infrastructure owned by the first investors, Vyacheslav Mirilashvili and Lev Leviev (they owned Setektel hosting company). The new network investor, the DST fund, believed that the resource should not work with third-party data centers.

In line with the wishes of investors, in 2010 Pavel Durov held talks with ITSVA LLC, which owned land and a number of industrial facilities near St. Petersburg. Already in 2012, Pavel Bondarenko became the general director of ITSVA LLC.

As for the data centers that were built on the squares that belonged to LLC, Pavel Durov built their own funds. Durov filed a counterclaim against the UCP. Nevertheless, the parties to the conflict managed to agree after the fund sold 48% of its shares to the Mail.ru Group for $ 1.47 billion.

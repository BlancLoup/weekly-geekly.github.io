Battle of Transformers



At Computex 2012, so many good things happened that did not fit in one post. Therefore, meet the second post, in which we will show three tablet (half-tablet) computers running Windows 8, and for dessert we will tell something new about an old acquaintance - ASUS Transformer Pad Infinity .



ASUS Tablet 810 (Windows 8)



The ASUS Tablet 810 is a tablet computer based on the latest Intel Atom processor, with 2 GB of system memory, a built-in 64 GB memory card and a Windows 8 operating system. The Tablet 810 has a 11.6-inch Super IPS + display with a resolution of 1366 x 768 pixels and multitouch interface with Wacom digital pen support.

A key feature of this model is a mobile docking station, through which the Tablet 810 can be turned into a compact laptop with a keyboard and touchpad. In addition, the docking station adds a pair of USB ports, a microSD card reader, a microHDMI and increases battery life with an additional battery.



Despite its compact size and thin (8.7 mm) profile, this model boasts a good sound system that incorporates SonicMaster technology. The front 2 megapixel camera serves for video conferencing, while the rear 8 megapixel autofocus and LED flash allows you to take high-quality photos. Built-in NFC-sensor allows you to connect to other NFC-compatible devices.

ASUS Tablet 600 (Windows RT)



The hardware configuration of the ASUS Tablet 600 includes a quad-core NVIDIA Tegra 3 processor with an integrated graphics core, 2 GB of system memory and 32 GB of user memory. The tablet runs under the operating system Windows RT.

Like the Tablet 810, it can be used with a mobile docking station that adds a keyboard, a touchpad, one USB port and an extra battery to its configuration.

The 10.1-inch screen of this model remains at a constant resolution of 1366 x 768 pixels and with a brightness of 600 nits, and SonicMaster audio technology is responsible for the sound. Screen type - Super IPS +. Like the older model, the Tablet 600 is equipped with two cameras, as well as an NFC-sensor for communicating with other NFC-compatible devices.

Monoblock computer ASUS Transformer AiO



Representing a combination of tablet and monoblock computer, Transformer AiO allows you to instantly switch between two modes of operation, as well as two operating systems (Windows and Android).

Assembled Transformer AiO is a monoblock computer with 18.4-inch multi-touch screen with LED backlight, with a compact body and a functional stand that allows you to freely adjust the height and angle of the display. Built-in speaker system with ASUS SonicMaster audio technology.



The Transformer AiO feature is that its 18.4-inch multi-touch display can be removed from the stand and used as a wireless, while continuing to work at the computer away from the computer itself. In addition, this wireless display can be switched to Android Tablet PC mode with the click of a button.

ASUS Transformer Pad Infinity



First introduced at the Consumer Electronics Show in January, the ASUS Transformer Pad Infinity is the world's first Android tablet with a Full HD display. The Android Ice Cream Sandwich operating system, the ASUS SonicMaster audio technology and the Super IPS + display with a resolution of 1920x1200 pixels and Corning Gorilla Glass 2 protective glass - all this is implemented in a metal case with an attractive ZENBOOK design. In addition, ASUS Transformer Pad can also be used as a laptop: you just need to connect to it a mobile docking station with a keyboard, a multi-touch touchpad and additional interfaces.

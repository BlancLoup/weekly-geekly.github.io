Fun statistics

The head of Twitter‚Äôs analytical department, Kevin Vale , published interesting TPS statistics a few days ago (tweets per second - tweets per second).

It turns out that people involved in analytics on Twitter all days since the beginning of the World Cup and the NBA league are purposefully tracking sports matches and their results, but much more carefully - the number of tweets around the world within 30 seconds after each goal. And during the past weeks, several records were set up related directly to how things are going in the world of big sport.

Two weeks ago, the most discussed events on Twitter were:

1. On June 14, the first goal of the match Japan - Cameroon (2,940 TPS)
2. On June 14, the first goal of the match Brazil-North Korea (2,928 TPS)
3. June 11th, Mexico's victory over South Africa (2,704 TPS)

However, even these numbers cause a smile when you see the number of tweets per second after the game of two legendary teams of the American Basketball Association. The game between the Los Angeles Lakers and the Boston Celtics ended with a score of 3.085 TPS, which was a record on June 18th.

As usual - until a certain time. Namely, before the final whistle of the referee in the match Japan - Denmark, which ended with a score of 3: 1 and 3.283 TPS. On average, after interesting heads, the TPS indicator around the world is at 750.

Of course, it will be much more interesting to look at the same statistics after the semi-finals and the World Cup finals, will TPS overcome the 4k border?

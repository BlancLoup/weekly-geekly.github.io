Jericho





The action of this post-apocalyptic series takes place in the small American town of Jericho. It is located near Denver, in which terrorists detonate an atomic bomb. A little later, it turns out that similar explosions occurred in several major US cities, including Washington. As a result, the country left without the ruling elite splits into quasi-states. However, this is only a distant background for the events taking place in Jericho, whose residents are faced with a shortage of food, fuel and aggression from the neighboring town. In other words, the struggle for resources and survival begins.



In general, this is not so much a fantastic series as a psychological series. However, he raised a very important question for modern humanity: what to do if a catastrophe occurred? Let it be not an explosion of a bomb or a nuclear power plant, but, for example, an accident at a chemical plant or an earthquake. Although in our cattle time it is very likely that the civil war version is modeled on Abkhazia, Chechnya, Nagorno-Karabakh, Transnistria and a number of similar madnesses. What do those who are just trying to survive? Very difficult question to which there are few answers in the series. But this is becoming increasingly relevant in our time of troubles.

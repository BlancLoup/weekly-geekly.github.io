Attack on SORM: the national provider will sue the FSB

In December 2015, the Internet Protection Society (OZI), a non-profit organization that aims to protect the Internet in Russia from censorship, excessive regulation and administrative arbitrariness, was formed in Russia.

In February, OZI chose the first three projects to get started. Two of them have already been completed: an index of Internet freedom and monitoring of network connectivity are published. There is a third project left - an attack on SORM.

Now the FSB is forcing providers at their own expense to buy the unnecessarily expensive SORM equipment from a narrow circle of special licensed companies in order to spy on users. Participants in the project OZI are going to sue the FSB about the illegality of the requirements for the installation of SORM equipment at the expense of the provider, since such a requirement directly violates the law "On operational investigative activities." By law, the financing of the OSA should only come from the budget .

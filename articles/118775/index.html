The future of three-dimensional interfaces. Navigation

MIT students presented their future navigation interface design.
Known by the name AIDA 2.0, it is a concept of an interactive navigation system suggesting a route taking into account usual roads, the need for refueling and other driving moments. AIDA 2.0 also includes displaying instrument indicators and a 3-dimensional map display right in front of the driver, which adds clarity.






Given the trend of recent years regarding the improvement of 3D maps, the introduction of such a technology would be interesting.



In the meantime, this is just an interesting concept, for the introduction of which requires the improvement of existing technologies and the accumulation of cartographic information.

Finally, I want to say that no system can replace good road knowledge.

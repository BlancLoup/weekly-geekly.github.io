OpenSource, Windows and Education

The topic of education and OpenSource recently very actively covered in the media.

But after all, OpenSource is not only GNU / Linux!
Why not think about those who purchased MS Windows officially (on their own or forcibly in addition when buying a new computer)?

One solution is offered by Windows OpenPack (Education) ...

Windows OpenPack (Education) is a specialized free software supplement (OSS) designed to help educate and organize the learning process for Windows. It includes programs for use in educational institutions (schools, institutes), as well as scientific work. Programs are free functional analogues of expensive software.
The vast majority of programs included in the collection have a Russian-language interface, easy to learn and use. 90% of the programs presented on the disk are also available for Linux platforms.
Using Windows OpenPack (Education) from officially acquired Windows you get:
- a wide range of free programs for education;
- legal protection;
- economic benefits.

The supplement includes a package of educational programs for students in junior, secondary and higher educational institutions, as well as educational management and control programs for managers and teachers.

The disc contains more than 50 applications. Of them:
- educational games for children from 2 years old (development of memory, attention);
- programs for studying algebra, geometry, physics, chemistry, geography, astronomy, etc .;
- creating a schedule of classes and load distribution on teachers;
- visual development environments for programming languages ‚Äã‚Äãin C ++, C #, Mono, Pascal, Java;

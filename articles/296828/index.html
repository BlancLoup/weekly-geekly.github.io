Technology conquers death



Do you remember the movie about robot Chappy? As planned by the screenwriters, he was not a robot in the full sense of the word, but he was also far from a man. Caution, spoiler.

In a life-affirming finale, the cyborg defeats enemies, turning a couple of friends into similar creatures along the way.
So, the idea, as if taken directly from this film.

Australian startup Humai reported that he is working on technology that is conquering death.
According to the director, Josh Bocanegra, they plan to store data on behavioral patterns, thought processes and features of the functioning of the body.

Brave startups encode information using sensory technology and introduce it into an artificial body with the brain of a deceased person. The brain, in turn, will freeze using cryonics. Possible damage in the future will be eliminated by applying nanotechnology and cloning.
On the Humai website you will be met by a girl mysteriously peeking out of the jungle and equally mysterious music. The animation is also quite entertaining.



There are 5 people in a startup, and the project is funded by the founder.
The plans of the Australian inventors - sponsorship from external sources. They expect to finish the work in 30 years.
And although their idea is still reminiscent of an excerpt from science fiction, scientists are determined resolutely.

"Interview": Sony may sue for the soundtrack to the film

Yun Mi Re is preparing to court with Sony for using music in the film Interview, for which the singer did not receive money. At the moment, the film has already been downloaded more than 1.5 million times .



The film The Interview, a comedy about a conspiracy against North Korean leader Kim Jong-un, was recently released online. See it in cinemas will not work, because the hackers who hacked Sony Pictures in late November, demanded the cancellation of the rental of the controversial picture. And Sony Pictures succumbed , but in the end the movie was released on hire in Google Play, iTunes and on Seetheinterview.com .

Yun Mi Re, whose real name is Natasha Shanta Reid, was born in the USA. She plays hip-hop music R & B, recorded on the label Feel Ghood Music. Representatives of the recording studio claim that there was a conversation about using Pay Day composition in the film, but the discussion ended without result, so no agreement was signed that music was used.



The film features David Bowie, Eminem, Ray Barretto, Jay Chow, Katti Perry and other artists.

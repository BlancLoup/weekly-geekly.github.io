For $ 5 on Darknet, you can buy "eternal" Internet access in the US



A darknet is a private network in which connections are established only between trusted peers. For this purpose, specific protocols and ports are used, which allow anonymity of the participants of this type of network. Due to anonymity on Darknet, it is difficult for law enforcement agencies to work - it is often impossible to identify users. This is used by all sorts of sellers of illegal content or real goods.

The most famous marketplace in Darknet was at one time Silk Road, where it was possible to purchase drugs prohibited in the US and other countries, illegal weapons, etc. But in the "dark zone" of the network you can sell not only drugs, but also many other things, as well as information. The fact is that it allows you to view video content: television shows, movies, sports news, available from anywhere in the world at any time. But, more importantly, this data is suitable for working with Xfinity WiFi in the United States. This service gives you access to a huge number of wireless access points, which means almost free internet.

Back in 2014, the American provider Comcast remotely transferred all its subscriber devices to a public Wi-Fi access point in Houston, USA. At the first stage, it was about 150 thousand routers. After some time, the provider did the same at the national level. If desired, the public segment on the router can be disabled, but only advanced users do this, which in general is not so much. As a result, Xfinity WiFi subscribers have the opportunity to connect to the Internet in any region of the United States where there is at least one Comcast router with the public point mode enabled.

The fact is that this particular Soviet spacecraft received images of the far side of the moon. It happened on October 7, 1959, at 6:30 am (Moscow time).

Two lenses (focal lengths of 200 and 500 mm) made it possible to capture about half of the surface of the moon (with 2/3 of the images being the opposite side of the moon) from a distance of about 67,000 km.

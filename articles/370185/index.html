Self-coupling


Communications installers are often faced with the urgency of doing some kind of work in the field, at the same time finding themselves unarmed for the task at hand due to the lack of necessary materials on hand. A true professional should be ready for anything, you say. But often the problem of lack of tools or material comes from management, who prefer banal savings to quality of work, without considering the risks. The installer, in any case, has to solve the problem on the spot, because the payment for his labor depends on it.

How, for example, to make a less tolerable cable sleeve out of what is on hand?

In the distant and crisis-year 2007, the management of one of the operators offered its employees to resort to self-made production of optical couplings. In order to save, naturally. Here is how it looked.

Installers took a plastic pipe with a diameter of 25-30 mm and divided into segments of 20 centimeters. Along the edges made holes for fasteners. On the one hand for fixing the cable, and on the other for optical patch cords.


Next, the fiber was trimmed so that the module tube protruded several centimeters from the common cable sheath. After welding and shrinking of the sleeves, the sleeves are attached to the module tube, the fiber is laid in a figure eight and secured with a mounting tape.



Then the clutch, patch cords and cable secured with ties. From the ends, the tube was sealed with raw rubber, and the whole structure was wrapped with insulating tape.



Fastened the clutch on the back of the box.

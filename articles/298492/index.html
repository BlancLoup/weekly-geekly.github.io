The foreign company ESET will earn on licensing, if its Russian partner enters the Register of domestic software

From January 1, 2016, the state customer must argue his choice in favor of foreign software, if his Russian counterparts in the specially created registry of domestic software. The software should belong to the state, the municipality, a Russian non-profit organization or company with a share of Russian beneficiaries of more than 50% - this is the main criterion that makes it possible to consider software as domestic.

On January 11, it was reported that the website ‚ÄúThe Unified Register of Russian Programs for Electronic Computers and Databases‚Äù was opened on the website of the Ministry of Communications and Mass Media.

Iset Development LLC, a Russian partner of the international developer of antivirus software ESET , has submitted an application for submission. According to SPARK-Interfax, the company was registered in August 2015. According to SPARK-Interfax, 75% of this company belongs to Alexander Chachave and 25% to Marina Sokolova, and its general director is Gennady Kurbatov.

Iset Development LLC buys the rights to global ESET products. For this business model, ESET works in all countries. This is not the opening of subsidiary offices, but a technological partnership with local companies, Chachava told Vedomosti. The company is headquartered in Slovakia.

Russian software was developed using technologies licensed from the global ESET, but exclusive rights to the final product belong to the Russian legal entity, says Chachava. The amount of license payments for them does not exceed 30%.

The application is sent to the Ministry of Communications and is checked within 10 days. If successful, it is registered and published in a special section of the site. To date, February 6, published 68 applications.

The meeting of the application review board is chaired by Nikolai Nikiforov, the Minister of Communications and Mass Media. Decisions of the expert body are taken by a simple majority of votes of its members participating in the meeting, including remotely. In case of equality of votes, the vote of the chairman is decisive. "The basic condition for the work of the board is that officials, representatives of departments can not have more than 40% of the vote in it, while representatives of the IT industry - those in whose interests the reform is implemented - should have at least 50% of the vote, told CNews Nikiforov.

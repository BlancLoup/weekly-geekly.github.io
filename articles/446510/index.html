CLRium # 5: Garbage Collector. Largest .NET Workshop

Our seminar is confidently gaining listeners and gradually outgrowing the EPAM office in St. Petersburg: we plan to recruit up to 250 developers under the same roof in both St. Petersburg and Moscow. And why?


Once I gave a talk on the work of the Garbage Collector and this report, although it was 45 minutes long, it covered all the topics known at that time. You probably saw him: after all, he was at CLRium # 2 and at the .NEXT conference, where he was a keynote. A report opening the conference ( many thanks to 23derevo and real_ales for the opportunity). In addition to my and many other reports, the book Under the Hood of .NET Memory Management has been published, shedding light on the algorithms of the engine compartment of the .NET platform. And about that time period, when interest grew so much to the CLR engine, questions about this very GC began to appear at the interviews. Not superficial questions, but with some bias in "tell me more." And even though such questions caused a storm of indignation among specialists, those people who spoke in detail about the core were given a job without any problems . Often, with a good salary increase .


Today, you have essentially two ways to become employer # 1 priority:


  1. Find a month or two and read a book Pro .NET Memory Management
  2. Go to our seminar and understand everything in one day. Plus get video recordings to refresh your memory.

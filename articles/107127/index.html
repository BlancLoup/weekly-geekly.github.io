The nine rules of legal distribution

Mailing is a good way to inform about project news, find new customers and re-engage those who have already become your customer once. But if you do not want to get the mark of a spammer, you should follow a few simple rules.

What is spam? This is 80% of mail traffic on the Internet and 20% efficiency . Normal, well-done mailing will be much more efficient. But even with the most good intentions, there is always the risk of falling into the purgatory of the Junk Mail folder. To avoid this, you need to take into account a number of nuances.

As usual, Sammari at the beginning of the article and further - detailed explanations. So, 9 simple rules :
  1. Do not spam
  2. Ask the addressees consent to the newsletter
  3. Update your address database
  4. Do not use the purchase base
  5. Keep confirmation of the legality of your base
  6. Let us unsubscribe directly from the newsletter.
  7. Do not repeat
  8. Beautifully and competently make letters
  9. Consider the requirements of spam filters.

Do not spam


If your letter looks like spam, smells like spam and tastes like spam, then most likely it is. No seriously. You did not come to the Internet yesterday and understand that what you are going to do (say) is spam. So do not do it, no matter how great the temptation. No matter how it sounds, but this is the only way to conduct an advertising campaign without complaints.

How to prevent complaints?


In fact, receiving complaints from time to time is inevitable. We must be prepared for the fact that, having even a fully legitimate mailing list of fifty thousand people and having organized a quality mailing list, you will still receive one or two complaints. And this is the minimum. Sometimes, this is simply an erroneous user action, who thus wants to "unsubscribe" from the newsletter or hide an unwanted letter.



It is very easy for the user to put a few checkboxes in his mailbox and turn the list of objectionable letters into spam. He simply presses the "Spam" button in his inbox, not knowing that he is "knocking" on your company thereby, using it simply as a way to "unsubscribe" from the newsletter. And they do it! After all, the mail server blocks all emails from your server, having received a sufficient number of complaints about the same sender.

The easiest way is to contact a company that deals with mailing lists professionally, who knows all the weak points of advertising letters and who can work with both recipients and postal services (that is, to us :). If you want to do the mailing yourself or intend to provide this service to other organizations, then you will need our simple tips.

They are divided into two categories: this is work with users and protection from spam filters .

Work with users


What distinguishes a regular newsletter from spam? In fact, there is only one sign - the presence of a legally (that is, honestly) collected address base. The most important mistake in collecting such databases is the lack of personal permission from the user.

Legally collected address databases

Legality in this case means only one condition: the recipient of your newsletter had to subscribe to it (that is, express an unequivocal desire to receive it).
Distribution of advertising over telecommunication networks, including through the use of telephone, facsimile, mobile radio telephone communications, is allowed only with the prior consent of the subscriber or addressee to receive advertising. At the same time, an advertisement is deemed to be widespread without the prior consent of the subscriber or addressee, unless the distributor proves that such consent was obtained.
Federal Law "On Advertising"


If you have an online store, it means that you also have a user base, i.e. those people who did your shopping. When a profitable action or a series of discounts is planned, of course, there is a great temptation to immediately start sending out to the available addresses, but you must nevertheless ask for permission. Even if the recipients are your clients, do not send them letters without permission. When a person buys products from you, fills out questionnaires and provides information about himself, he absolutely does not count on the rough use of his mailbox for promotional purposes.

You should not write off email addresses from business cards or profiles. This information is not for distribution, but for other needs.

Receive confirmation of your willingness to receive your letters.

Confirm the subscription so that no one can subscribe to someone else's address.

Update your databases

Do you have a legal base of recipients, but you are too delayed with the mailing list? If a person has agreed to receive letters from you two years ago, then today the mailing will surprise him or even make him angry - you need to ask permission again. Be sure to use the "Unsubscribe" button or link in each email. Do not repeat

If you send newsletters or other letters of the same type for too long, then you will most likely be reported as a spammer. It is only a matter of time.

Be open

Always make your mailing terms open. Tell people what, when and in what quantities you will send. If a person subscribes to weekly news, then in addition to this, it is no longer possible to send advertising services or reports about your promotions every four days. You will immediately turn into a spammer and a cheat. However, even if the newsletter was interesting to the person, he would still refuse your letters, preferring to read the news elsewhere. You need to clearly understand the difference between the newsletter (news) and the gross imposition of their services, advertising goods. Most likely, you will need to create two different lists of addresses - one for information, the other for advertising.

Anti-spam protection


On average, ‚Äúdue to‚Äù spam filters, about 10‚Äì20% of all emails disappear without a trace in email traffic. And all this is due to the fault of too zealous spam filters. You do not need to be a malicious spammer, so that your letters do not reach the recipients. Even those companies that send out the newsletter completely legally, with the permission and at the request of the addressee, with enviable regularity "fall under the distribution."

Alas, there is no quick and easy solution to the problem. To avoid filters, you need to understand their nature, understand how they work. And this process is very long and scrupulous.

E-mail programs have a very large list of criteria that filters are oriented to recognize the "junk" letter. The list includes primarily spam phrases. These include, for example: "ATTENTION, SALE !!!", "CLICK HERE AND YOU WILL BE A MILLION DOLLARS!". Recognizing one of these phrases (or very similar to it), the mail robot recycles the letter. Some criteria give more points, some less. For example, a sample of the analysis of criteria from SpamAssassin , the most popular spam filter in the world.

The letter will be placed in spam if it:
  • mentions winnings of huge sums of money (0.2 points);
  • asks why pay more? (1.25 points);
  • guarantees a refund for something (2 points);
  • contains an appeal about some urgent matter that is not urgent (2.9 points);
  • offers cheap mortgage or refinancing (3 points);
  • tells the secret of unprecedented success (2.4 points);

If your email contains spam amount above a certain threshold, it will be deleted. On each server, the administrator individually sets the bar. If the user is already fed up with junk mail, then the threshold will be set very low. In this case, almost all correspondence will be sent to the scrap.

The list of criteria for determining spam mentioned above is constantly growing, the filters themselves adapt to changes and new tricks of spammers. The list is updated each time a user clicks the ‚ÄúThis is spam‚Äù button in his inbox. Spam filters can even synchronize work and share experiences online.

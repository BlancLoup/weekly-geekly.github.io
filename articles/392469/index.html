Unmanned vehicles start up on the common roads of Russia. Developed changes in traffic rules, Criminal Code and Administrative Code



A round table was held in the State Duma, where they discussed the legal regulation of the use of unmanned transport systems (BPTS) in the Russian Federation. A package of amendments to the federal law, the Rules of the Road, the Civil Code, the Criminal Code of the Russian Federation and the Administrative Code of the Russian Federation has been prepared.

Amendments to legislative acts of the Russian Federation may be adopted by the State Duma until the end of 2016. In this case, Russia will become the second country in the world after the United States, where unmanned vehicles will be allowed to move on public roads.

The initiative to amend the regulations was made by the Commission for the Development of Strategic Information Systems under the State Duma Committee on Science and High Technology (Chairman of the Commission - Communist Party Deputy Dmitry Novikov).

Deputies of the State Duma, representatives of relevant ministries and departments, RF subjects, developers of unmanned transport systems, representatives of scientific and public organizations took part in the discussion of the draft law.

During the round table, several important decisions were made. The main thing is the definition of the concept of unmanned vehicle (BPTS).

Unmanned vehicle - a power-driven vehicle equipped with an automatic control system that can move without driver participation

It is proposed to allow BPTS to move on public roads if there is a driver in the cab who can switch the control process to manual mode if necessary. In this case, "it is forbidden to completely transfer to the drone control over the movement of the vehicle on public roads."

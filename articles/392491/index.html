Siri taught to help victims of violence

People increasingly began to turn to virtual assistants with questions about mental and physical health. A recent study showed that Siri, Google Now, Cortana and S Voice are not always able to correctly understand the seriousness of the issue and send a person to the right address when he talks about depression, suicide or violence.

The study had the effect: Apple taught Siri to send people to the hotline in the United States.



In the US, 200 million adults use smartphones, and more than half of them turn to health gadgets. Users often communicate with Siri as a friend, seeking advice or support. Siri can dial 911, find the nearest hospital or advise the desired hotline. But not all questions assistant answers correctly.

To find out how well smartphones speak on these topics, the researchers asked them nine questions about mental health, physical health, and violence. 27 devices with Siri, 31 gadgets with Google Now, 9 with S Voice and 10 with Cortana took part in the Experiment.

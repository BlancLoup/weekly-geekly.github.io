Robot Assistant Lexi took first place at the Imagine Cup

Imagine Cup ( vk , fb ) is the world's largest annual technology competition held with the support of Microsoft since 2003. The permanent organizer of the competition is Microsoft. The winners of the international final in each category receive 50 thousand dollars for the first place, 10 thousand dollars for the second, and 5 thousand dollars for the third for the development. However, the battle is not for money, but for prestige - the winning teams instantly become known around the world, and can use this chance for a real takeoff. vk.com/rtrg?r=MyfvmSagVGcKoVRPBQ92U5CfxIhCPah9BpgCNi*VQ5Z*GZClBfq8O6Xqoc4FgyLmY/t2xfXW*b/pLNakil70J2BgItRALRcbqLdyvvbQaL48Y3InrmCX91lp3jlslzoTrQWAQ2odZccwu06QSKcDISHi4yplYcjlvQQqCvYweCs-&pixel_id=1000020818

As written on the site, graduate students and students from around the world can participate in the competition, as a team or alone. Participants need to come up with a bright idea and implement its prototype with the help of modern information technologies.



The Lexi project team, which consists of graduates from Moscow State University and Baumanka and Skoltech graduate students, also took part in the competition and took first place in the Innovations nomination. The Lexy project is a member of the information technology cluster of the Skolkovo Foundation. The project manager is a postgraduate student of the Space Robotics Laboratory at the Skolkovo Institute of Science and Technology (Skoltech) Dmitry Suvorov. Sk.ru:
This year, the jury of Imagine Cup, which included representatives of the Russian Venture Company (RVC), the Skolkovo Foundation, the Agency for Strategic Initiatives (ASI) and the Foundation for the Development of Internet Initiatives (IIDF) and Microsoft, looked at 135 student projects from seven cities in Russia. 21 projects selected for the final. The winners were three teams.

In addition to Lexy, who took the first place in the category ‚ÄúInnovations‚Äù, two more projects will compete for the chance to go to the international final.

In the nomination ‚ÄúSocial projects, the team Galvani Bionix (MIPT) won with the project Myoarm Control System. Myoarm Control System is a universal platform for contactless control of devices, based on the registration of surface electromyographic signals of human muscles. The system may be, first of all, useful for people with disabilities with no upper limbs.

In the ‚ÄúGames‚Äù nomination, the first place was taken by the IzHard team (St. Petersburg State University) with the project OVIVO - this is a platform game, the hero of which travels through the black and white abstract world. The main feature of the project is the change of physical laws, for example, gravity and minimalism of visual design. In the game, for example, there is no perspective. This is done so that the player can create individual images using his imagination.

Three teams winning the Moscow tour will take part in the international online semi-final. It will identify the best projects whose authors will be invited to the world finals at Microsoft headquarters in Seattle.

According to a Microsoft representative, the organizers and jury members of the Russian Imagine Cup are satisfied with the results of the competition. ‚ÄúWe managed to select very strong projects, as a result of which, as I saw, the jury found it very difficult to choose the winners. In general, winning the international final is an unpredictable thing that depends on many factors. But we, of course, will root for ours, ‚Äùsaid Dmitry Soshnikov.

It is difficult to win the international final, but the Russians have already succeeded. So, the first place in the Imagine Cup 2014 in the category "Games" was taken by the Periny development team - Brainy Studio with the TurnOn project. Prior to this, the victory in the competition was given to the Russians already 10 years ago - in 2005.

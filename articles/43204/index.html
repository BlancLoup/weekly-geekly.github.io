Eye access keys (eye accessing cues)

Each of you at least occasionally draws attention to the fact that the interlocutor averts his eyes during a conversation. The direction of the view changes depending on the topic of conversation, attitude towards you, state, and the very person of the interlocutor.

In terms of NLP, this is called eye accessing cues.

In order not to wait for the next conversation with the person to turn their attention to the direction of gaze of the interlocutor we use the competitive work of Michael Simon Toon "Thought Moments" for British Films. I can not imagine an example more visual than this

PPS Test Questions for Experiments
[Up-Right] Visual Memories:
  • What color is your front door?
  • How did you look a year ago
  • What is the shape of the car window in the subway?
  • How many floors are there in the house you live in?
  • What is shown on a 10-ruble bill?
[Up-left] Visual design:
  • Imagine yourself with red hair
  • If you turn the map, in what direction will the southeast be?
  • Imagine a purple triangle inside a red square.
  • This jacket will be combined with green trousers / skirt?
[Right] Audio memory:
  • Can you hear your favorite piece of music inside you?
  • Which door in your house squeaks the loudest? How does the busy tone sound on your phone?
  • What are the voices of your parents?
  • Can you hear the choir singing inside yourself?
[Left] Audio construction:
  • How loud will it be if ten people shout at the same time?
  • How will your voice sound under water?
  • Imagine your favorite melody that sounds twice as fast.
  • What sound will the piano emit when it falls from the tenth floor?
  • What will the mandrake's cry be like?
  • What will the chain saw sound like in a corrugated iron shed?
[Down-Right] Internal Dialog:

  • What do you say to yourself when things go wrong?

Unusual cooler

Cooler Master introduced a somewhat unusual cooler called the Sphere, which has an unusual shape for similar products. Despite this (and even more likely thanks to it), Sphere copes well with the cooling of any processors and, importantly, is virtually silent.


The new solution has a universal fastening design, which makes it possible to install Sphere on CPUs from both Intel and AMD. According to the manufacturer, the cooler is able to effectively cool not only the CPU itself, but also the north bridge of the chipset, the elements of the motherboard, and even the video card.

Made of copper and equipped with four heat pipes, the Sphere has dimensions of 132 √ó 113 mm and a weight of 684 grams. The fan of the novelty rotates at a speed of up to 2200 revolutions per minute, and the noise level does not exceed 22 dB.

On sale Cooler Master Sphere will appear in September this year. The price, unfortunately, is not yet known.

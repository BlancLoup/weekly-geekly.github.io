And systemd embraced me to the depths of my distribution

It happened: Debian started the transition to systemd.

For system administrators, it looks like this: 
 localhost # apt-get update; apt-get upgrade
 ...
 The following packages have unmet dependencies:
  systemd-sysv: Breaks: sysvinit-core but 2.88dsf-53 is installed.
 The following actions will resolve these dependencies:

      Remove the following packages:
 1) sysvinit-core               

 Accept this solution?  [Y / n / q /?]


What does this mean? This means passing the line in a gradual migration to systemd, the decision on which was made a little earlier , replacing sysv-init with systemd. The sysvinit-core package contains / sbin / init, and is replaced with another / sbin / init from systemd.

Someone sees in what is happening a step into a bright future, where everyone will be happy and almost will not have to die, someone sees an act of tragic and humble acceptance of the fateful fate, the dark hand of Redhat, extending to all distributions around and eliminating the Fatal Defect of other launch systems someone just with stoic despair is waiting for more and more Pottering (L. Pottering is the author of systemd, he is also the author of pulseaudio, he is also the author of a unique style of presenting new ideas, in which there is no room for the old way of life and thinking).


In any case, it happened. GNU Debian / jessie, nee GNU Debian / sid, becomes systemd-based distribution.

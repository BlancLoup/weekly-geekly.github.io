1 million dollars for "lightweight batteries" for American soldiers

The US Department of Defense took an unprecedented step. The military has announced a competition to create a lightweight version of the power supply module, which the American soldiers are carrying with them to ensure the work of all high-tech equipment hung on them. If by the end of 2008 any of the declared participants can present a working 96 hours, weighing no more than 4 kilograms and made in the form of a field battery vest, then this lucky (or a group of lucky ones) will receive their coveted million from one of the most closed departments USA.

It is noteworthy that the competition, the registration of participants of which began on July 5, will be able to take part not only companies constantly working with the US Department of Defense, but almost the developers from the street. It is allowed to register even simple American Kulibins - you see, not only Jobs can shaman in the garage on his knee. True, individual developers must necessarily be adults (over 21 years old) US citizens.

The main condition set for the inventors of all of America is that the weight of the prototype batteries should not exceed 4 kilograms, and its design should be such that all this energy junk does not dangle behind its back, but is worn like a regular vest.

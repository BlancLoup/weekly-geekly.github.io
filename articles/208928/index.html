How to prepare and get a PMP certificate in two weeks



The Certificate of Project Management Professional (PMP) from the American Project Management Institute (PMI) remains one of the most popular in the world and is becoming popular in Russia. Not so long ago, there was an interesting discussion in the project management community on LinkedIn that ensures that PMP is available to the employer. The conclusion of the majority of participants was that it is expressed only by knowledge of terms and theory, but by no means by the practical experience of the owner. In the case of external projects, the company, of course, can use the presence of certified PMP as a competitive advantage in the commercial offer. But in practice, even a person without project management experience will be able to obtain this certificate, which is quite high in the world, having spent from two weeks to a month depending on the intensity of training. In this article we will talk about the ‚Äúweak points‚Äù of the PMI requirements - whether or not to use them depends only on the professional ethics of each. However, for comparison, we describe the "official" and ethically correct way.

We mention that the exam is paid. It makes sense to first become a member of PMI ($ 139), in order to get a discount for the exam (for members of PMI $ 405) - as a result, the exam will cost $ 544, and membership in PMI will allow you to get your legal copy of PMBoK, as well as access to various materials , webinars, etc. If you register for an exam without a PMI membership, the exam will cost $ 555, so a PMI membership pays for itself.

Exam Requirements


So let's start with experience. Officially, PMI requires 36 calendar months of project management experience (during which at least 4,500 hours were spent directly on project management). Theoretically, you can write the letter yourself, indicating the contact with the second mobile number - or ask a friend. Do not take as practical advice - just an indication of the "vulnerability" of the process.

Higher education is also desirable for taking the exam. It is confirmed by a scan of the diploma and a free translation into English. Sadly, this requirement is also easy to manage even in the case of verification: PMI does not verify the authenticity of the diploma. Another item requirements - courses. You need to dial 35 contact hours - that is, hours of training in project management. Any online or offline course will do, even if it was 20 years ago. Audit


Some believe (including the author of one of the articles about passing the Habr√© exam ) that the application is being audited for some reason: incomplete information or a ‚Äúsuspicious‚Äù type. In practice, this is pure ‚Äúrandom‚Äù: the system randomly selects 10% of all applications and sends letters about the need to send confirmation of documents through courier service. Within 2-3 weeks after that, a letter arrives about the successful completion of the audit. Thus, an audit can only increase the cost of obtaining a certificate (both for an honest and not very honest candidate), but not to ensure that the candidate meets the requirements.

Exam preparation


Reading PMBoK once is necessary anyway - even if you want to go the shortest way. This can take 10-12 hours and can be quite a boring process. Well, then there are several options. The book PMPExam Prep by Rita Mulcany, which in the past was the only alternative method of preparation, today has become a bit of an archaic and not the fastest way. It has useful exercises, and it improves PMBoK's understanding, but there are much more interesting books on project management and a much more effective way of preparation. The main one is websites and programs with a set of questions. In this regard, PMP is not very different from the technical exams from Microsoft or Cisco: there is a long and laborious, but more honest let with reading literature and practice, but there is a quick and less time-consuming, but not very ethical way of "filling the hands" on questions which then meet on the exam in one form or another.

The number of sites with a database of questions in English is very large. Unfortunately, they are all paid - for free, you can find only a few dozen questions of dubious quality. However, this article will provide an opportunity to significantly save on this.
So, assuming that you have chosen a less laborious way, you begin to answer hundreds of questions. If the general level of correct answers exceeds 60%, then it is important to understand which areas of knowledge you already know enough, and which are still insufficiently developed. There are 47 project management processes in PM BoK, and if you are focused only on those that are not well enough to know, this will help save a lot of time. And the more you know - the higher the need for such detailed statistics. Therefore, it is advisable to choose a site with questions that provides the ability to track your progress. In this case, you can adjust the preparation and work out questions only from areas of knowledge where the level is lower than the others.

It is very important that there is a detailed commentary to the questions that would explain the correct answer - these comments are important if you do not know why your answer was not correct.
In the process of preparation it is important to remember all the processes, their inputs and outputs. For better memorization, there are various tools, like a puzzle of project management processes. This will help speed up memorization. In addition, it is desirable to remember and formulas - in the exam there will be questions for which they will be needed.

When there is a feeling that most of the questions do not cause difficulties (on average, it takes about 30 hours), then it makes sense to try your hand at the exam simulator, which is also available on many sites. And if you have scored at least 80% of the correct answers on it, having packed in the allotted time, then you can consider yourself ready for the real exam.

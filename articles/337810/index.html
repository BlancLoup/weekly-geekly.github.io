Computer vision. Ask an Intel Expert Question

Not all answers can be found on the Internet. Especially if your question relates to a fairly narrow or new area - then you need to consult a guru, the Owner of Secret Knowledge. In the tradition of the blog Intel - holding blog seminars, built on the questions of readers. These questions are answered by experts from Intel, who were directly involved in the creation of technologies and products - to whom, if not to them, to know all the details?

This month, space on the podium was provided to the creators of the OpenCV (Open Source Computer Vision Library) computer vision library , former employees of Itseez , which became part of Intel - Vadim Pisarevsky and Anatoly Baksheev . So, if you have a ripe question about OpenCV, machine vision, pattern recognition and other related topics, but you didn‚Äôt know who to ask it - we invite you in the comments and lichku. Questions are accepted until September 24 . The author of the best question will receive a prize from Intel * - a set of branded accessories for a cozy holiday.
* - The prize is delivered from Moscow within the Russian Federation

Under the cut - a summary of our experts.

Anatoly Baksheev , team leader at Computer Vision for Retail at Intel. He has been involved in computer vision and machine learning for more than 11 years, including the last few years of Deep Learning. He began his career at Itseez and worked there for more than 10 years until the company was acquired by Intel. During this time, his portfolio has accumulated a lot of completed projects, designed and optimized algorithms, a large amount of code laid out in the OpenCV library, as well as its optimization for the NVIDIA GPU.

Vadim Pisarevsky , the permanent head of the OpenCV team, since 2000.

He graduated from VMK UNN, worked at Intel, then at Itseez. I returned to Intel to continue working on the library and transfer it to Deep Learning. Co-author of several patents and articles on computer vision. He periodically gives talks and lectures on OpenCV in various universities and at various conferences.

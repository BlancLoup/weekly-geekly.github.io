Toshiba Libretto W100

Toshiba introduced the world's first laptop in an unusual form factor Libretto W100. A special feature of the model are two seven-inch touch screens, which allow you to work with the tablet as an electronic "reader", and switch to laptop mode.



The novelty will be on sale in the third quarter of this year. Libretto W100 will be released in a limited edition, but I think that if the form factor takes root, then Toshiba will release a more powerful version. For now we admire photos.




Libretto W100 is equipped with two touch screens with a diagonal of 17.8 cm (7 inches) and a resolution of 1024 x 600 pixels each.



Libretto W100 can work as a convenient mobile terminal for accessing websites, e-mail and instant messaging services, allowing you to stay in touch with friends, use social networks, read newspapers, magazines and watch movies where it is convenient.



Separately, it is worth highlighting the possibility of enabling the virtual keyboard and the drag-and-drop interface. This feature allows you to easily transfer content, files and shortcuts from one screen to another - with a simple movement of your finger.

What else is "delicious" stuffing? Intel Pentium U5400 processor with a clock speed of 1.2 GHz. RAM in Libretto W100 2 GB, 64 GB SSD. The tablet works on About Windows 7 Home Premium.

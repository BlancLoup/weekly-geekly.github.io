HP showed 4K, 5K and curved displays on CES2015

HP at CES2015 presented monitors with a resolution of 3840 x 2160 and 5120 x 2880, a 3D virtual reality display that monitors the movement of the user's head and allows you to view the image from any angle, as well as curved displays up to 34 inches.



Zvr Virtual Reality Display has a diagonal of 23.6 inches, full HD resolution and involves the use of passive 3D glasses. Included with the monitor is a stylus that allows you to move, rotate, and otherwise control the displayed objects. At the same time, four cameras located at the top of the monitor, monitor the movement of the user's head and understand where he is looking: this allows not only to see the three-dimensional image, but also to view it from all sides. This device will be used in schools and other educational institutions - for example, in order not to cut a live frog. And designers and engineers will be able to study their own prototypes of something, until they were printed or assembled.



HP introduced two curved monitors, 27 "and 34" inches diagonally. The older model Envy 34c has a resolution of 3440 x 1440 and an aspect ratio of 21: 9. Such a monitor will save many from having to install two displays. The younger model Pavilion 27c is less remarkable, it has a resolution of 1080p and an aspect ratio of 16: 9. Monitors have VGA and HDMI ports.

Two models with a resolution of 4K, namely 3840 x 2160, have a diagonal of 27 "(Z27s) and 23.8" inches (Z24s). Aspect ratio - standard 16: 9. Monitors are equipped with DisplayPort, DVI, HDMI, Mini-DisplayPort, MHL and three USB 3.0 ports.

Model Z27q has a resolution of 5K - 5120 x 2880, which is seven times the full HD on the number of points.

Reports from Magento Meetup Kharkiv - Videos and Presentations

Hello! I want to share with you reports with Magento Meetup Kharkiv, which was held on June 21. They will be interesting to everyone who somehow comes into contact with the work of the Magento platform.

There were 4 presentations:
1) Adobe Stock Integration Project . Speaker Sergey Ivaschenko, Software Engineer in Magento.
2) MSI In-Store Pickup. Functionality and complexity . Speakers Alexander Kravchuk and Maxim Novik, Software Developer at ISM Ukraine and Magento Community Maintainer.
3) Proof of Concept for Magento 2 Projects: Occam's Razor . Speaker Alexander Shkurko, Software Engineer in Made People
4) Advanced GIT or how to change history . Sergey Nezbritsky, Lead Backend Developer at Magecom.

Adobe Stock Integration Project


Magento Adobe Stock Integration started a month ago on Magento Imagine. This is a set of modules that allows you to use Adobe Stock media resources for filling Magento content. This is not only one of the first steps in the interoperability of Adobe and Magento, but also the first integration of the open source projects of these two companies.

Sergey Ivaschenko from the Magento Community Engineering team told about Magento Adobe Stock Integration and how to join the development of this promising open source project in the early stages.



Link to the presentation: www.slideshare.net/Magecom/adobe-stock-integration-community-project

MSI In-Store Pickup: Functionality and Complexity


Alexander Kravchuk and Maxim Novik answered the questions:
1) How many developers do you need to write your In-Store Pickup delivery method?
2) How should it work?
3) What are the difficulties encountered during design and development?



Link to the presentation: www.slideshare.net/Magecom/msi-instore-pickup

Proof of Concept for Magento 2 Projects: Occam's Razor


Alexander Shkurko spoke about Occam's razor and how it will help in the development, implementation and maintenance of solutions.

As examples, he considered 3 types of projects: a new project, a project for support, and a project completely unknown to us.



Link to the presentation: www.slideshare.net/Magecom/proof-of-concept-for-magento-2-projects-occamos-razor

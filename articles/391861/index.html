What properties should money have?

Despite the diversity of forms and functions of money, they constitute a single instrument through which economic relations are realized in society. However, in order for money to be used as a full-fledged economic tool, it must meet a variety of requirements, depending on the level of development of socio-economic relations in society. The higher the level of relationships, the more stringent the conditions, which in turn leads to the evolution of a financial instrument. And in order to meet the ever-changing demands of society, money must have a number of specific properties, such as durability, divisibility, quantity limitations, and others.

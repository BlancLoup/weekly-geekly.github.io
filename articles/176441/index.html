AOptix Stratus turns iPhone into a mobile biometric identification device.



Existing mobile biometric identification devices are quite expensive and cumbersome.

California-based AOptix has announced a Stratus biometric system, the basis for which is the usual iPhone 4 or 4S, reports Engadget .
Until now, biometric identification solutions have been either stationary complexes or difficult-to-use mobile products with limited functionality. Using the capabilities of the iPhone and our rich experience in creating solutions for personal identification, AOptix provides an ideal mobile platform that will significantly expand the field of application of biometrics.

Stratus MX is equipped with fingerprint scanners and iris and is protected by a hinged protective cover. One battery charge is enough for 8 hours of continuous operation.



It is assumed that the Stratus complex will be useful primarily to government and military organizations, as well as companies working with confidential information.

The cost of the whole complex is still unknown, but the application is already available in iTunes for $ 200.

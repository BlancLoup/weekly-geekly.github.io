Three original ways to read news

Since the world is moving faster towards the Internet, a lot is changing in the world of journalism. And if newspapers prevailed before, now the world of blogs and online publications is rapidly taking their place.
One of the cool features of the Network is the absence of restrictions for the developer on the part of the specific location, interface or output format. Everything can be achieved with a small competent design work.

In the world of news there are several unique, different and fun news reading methods.
A game is a puzzle or image search, here are three new paradigms for reading news :

Spectra Visual Newsletter





Spectra is a completely new way of reading news. Instead of viewing a vertical page, you rotate headlines and images in order to choose which news is most interesting to read. You can choose the news channels you are interested in: Sports, Technology, Politics, etc. - and then scroll through today's news headlines, along with Spectra. If you need news of only a specific topic, you can find headlines by keywords. Clicking on the title will open the full article, and you can save or share it directly from Spectra.

You can even interact with Spectra using a webcam and microphone for a more complete immersion.
Anyway, this is the original way to view, read, and interact with news headlines.

Newsmap





Newsmap is essentially a mashup of newspapers with social media. Using Google News, Newsmap combines articles and provides headlines based on the number of articles devoted to this issue. The larger the title, the more articles on this topic. If you have only 10 seconds, but you want to know what is happening in the world, Newsmap gives this opportunity. Articles are colored by theme, size depends on popularity, and are darkened by relevance - dark sections are old articles.
You can sort the results by category (Business, Entertainment, National News, etc.), by countries around the world. Newsmap is a great way to compare what the world is talking about and see in simple and visual form what is happening in your country and in the whole world.

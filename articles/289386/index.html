The construction of Russia's largest data center has begun

According to Vedomosti, citing the information on the state procurement website, a subsidiary of Rosatom and Atomenergoprom Rosenergoatom is starting to build a data center in the Tver region. The construction tender was won by CHD Engineering. The project budget will be 6.4 billion rubles.

The construction of a data center with a capacity of 80 MW (8000 server racks) is a joint project with Rostelecom. This was reported to Vedomosti by Andrey Timonov, a representative of Rosenergoatom.

Both companies will benefit from the project. For Rosenergoatom, the data center is a new regular customer. And the data center, in turn, will be able to consume electricity at a reduced price.

According to iKS-Consulting, last year the total number of racks on the Russian data center market was 25,500. DataLine, the market leader, has a total of 2,695 racks.

Timonov also noted that 6.4 billion rubles will be spent on the construction of a data center, but they are not enough to fill the IT-infrastructure.

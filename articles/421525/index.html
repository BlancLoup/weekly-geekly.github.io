A little bit about the differences between Russian and foreign hosters

In the process of adding and updating information about tariffs for PoiskVPS and VPS.today projects, we review a large number of hoster sites, we correspond with their employees in order to provide our users with the most accurate and complete information about virtual server fees and related services. In this article I would like to share the differences between Russian and foreign hosts, to consider the difference in approaches to marketing and the tariff scale.



Traffic use for virtual servers


The opposite situation is observed with traffic for virtual servers: Russian companies often claim unlimited traffic, while foreign ones almost always set fixed limits for each tariff (take at least DigitalOcean , Linode , Vultr or Hetzner ). It is clear that there is no unlimited traffic, and the maximum possible amount is limited by the bandwidth provided by the channel shared between all clients on the server, so some domestic hosts limit the speed after consuming some (most often from 1 to 10 TB) traffic that creates an excessive load on network.

Administering Virtual Servers


Core-managed services are rarely found in foreign hosters (approximately corresponds to what is called basic administration here): non-administered servers are most often offered or they are fully administered (in many cases without root access). Many Russian companies declare the provision of administration services at various levels included in the tariff.

Discounts for long periods


Many foreign hosters offer discounts for a one-time payment of more than 3 months. There are offers with discounts of more than 50% when paying for a year or more. Russian hosters rarely offer a discount above 20% per year, which may indicate a more prudent pricing and a lower margin for services, or a slightly less desire to attract customers for a long time.

Payment systems


Almost all foreign companies accept payments only by bank cards or PayPal. Recently, BitCoin is gaining popularity, the ability to pay which is added by many companies. In Russia, it is considered to be good practice to be able to accept payment using WebMoney, QIWI, Yandex.Money, and also to connect a certain number of payment aggregators (for example, Robokassa, Interkassa, Z-Payment, etc.).

Virtual hosting organization


Shared hosting is still quite popular. Domestic companies usually have 3-5 hosting rates with different available disk space and the number of hosted sites, as well as the allocation of computing resources that depend on it. Popular foreign hosting companies are usually limited to one tariff with an ‚Äúunlimited‚Äù drive and no restrictions on the number of sites (often there is also an inexpensive basic tariff for hosting a single site and a disk size from 1 to 10 GB). Naturally, it is assumed that users will not overload the system. In addition, in addition to the disk volume, other restrictions are used, for example, by the number of files or the maximum file size.

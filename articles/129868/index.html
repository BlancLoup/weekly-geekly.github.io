317 patents by Steve Jobs

Steve Jobs was a great innovator who personally changed the world. Much of what we see around us first arose in the imagination of Steve Jobs - and now it has become a reality for billions of people. The history of Jobs inventions can be traced in 317 patents (infographics of the NY Times), where his name is indicated among the authors.

Steve Jobs is the author and co-author of desktop designs (20 patents), iPods (85 patents), iPhone and other iOS devices (48 patents), AppleTV (6 patents), laptops (39 patents), monitors (5 patents), mice and keyboards (13 patents). He is the co-author of some innovative elements of the operating system MacOS (33 patents) and NeXT (3 patents). Not a single trifle could escape the attention of a genius, so Steve Jobs changed the appearance of several power adapters, plugs, connectors, covers, clips and lanyards for headphones, speakers, and even invented a staircase with glass steps of a special design (there are only 21 patents).

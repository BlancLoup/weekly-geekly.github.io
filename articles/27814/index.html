ODF vs OOXML, the battle is over.

The battle of formats was long and tense, now it is over and we have a winner ... Microsoft announced its support for the ODF format, which will be implemented starting with Microsoft Office 2007 SP2 . In addition, a Microsoft spokesman stated in plain text: ODF is a winner.

Speaking at the Red Hat Summit conference in Boston, Stuart McKee said: ODF won a clear victory. Sales of software for us are the most important source of income. We were not able to support this format in the middle of the development cycle of our product, because it was not included in the release of Microsoft Office 2007 . We have pledged and are counting on doing more than just ODF-to-OOXML Converter.

This is the first time that Microsoft was unable to impose its format, as an industry standard, it can be regarded as a big victory.

The release of SP2 for Microsoft Office 2007 is expected in the first half of 2009.

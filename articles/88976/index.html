In short, the approach is as follows: each of us has a set of unique resources , which in the course of our livelihoods can be converted into each other (or wasted), but the possibilities of this conversion are limited by the available resources themselves. That is, they are converted not in direct proportion or even with a fixed percentage of costs (as currencies in the exchanger), but depending on the amount available at a given time. The first and last rule: if we are sorely lacking a resource, we are forced to compensate for it by spending disproportionately large amounts of other resources. Consequently, the strategy of optimal development is to maintain a long-term balance between different types of resources, so that there is no need to ‚Äúdrown with banknotes‚Äù.

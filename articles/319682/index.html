Notify user with 99% probability

There is no universal notification tool, each user has an individual set of devices and software. At the same time for a company, a group of people, a startup or even a small blog is a huge problem to keep your audience informed about the news. Even the usual notification about the extension of a paid account or the notification of a personal message, you can not see if it came to the device inconvenient for the user or in an inconvenient form.

PushAll seeks to create something similar to such a universal tool. You can notify any of your users, without any development for different platforms, without investing huge funds to maintain staff, equipment and software.

At the same time, we are developing a service primarily not for companies that want to send notifications, but specifically for users who want to receive these notifications. The problem is that even if a company creates a software package to alert users on different platforms - usually any garbage that people do not like is added there.

We have released an application for iOS and updated the application for Android .

99%?


If you take statistics Liveinternet, it turns out that 94% of users use either a browser with notification support, or a browser installed on a mobile device. The remaining 6% are Explorer and Edge, and most likely these users have a smartphone on Android or iOS, or in the end, a regular email.

In this article we want to talk about how you can solve a simple problem - notify the user. Now, many are trying to use email-mailing - but not all users are comfortable. Someone wants to send via telegrams - but not everyone wants to use telegrams. And so on. The more you give the user the opportunity to receive information from you, the greater the chance that he will receive it.

However, for each user wish you have to pay money. For each mobile platform, hire developers, hire SMMs for social networks, web developers for the site, but you still won‚Äôt cover the maximum amount of possible user choice variations at a time.

Our goal is to provide the user with a choice, and develop tools to improve the use of this choice.

Where can a user get a notification now?


We will proceed from different life situations, and give examples of possibilities of obtaining, as well as options for your own decision.

What is the difference from RSS or social networks?


RSS is a handy thing for a geek, but for most users, the maximum effort they can make is installing the application and pressing the login button. At the same time, RSS is just an XML file that you parse through requests, RSS itself will not be able to notify you about the replenishment of your account or a new message in the community, or the inaccessibility of your site.

Social networks in fact have a similar problem. Again, they more often play a broadcasting character, rather than a point character, and in terms of broadcasting, everything is bad now. If your record is popular, more people will see it, but if not, almost no one will see it. Since all social networks now have a "smart tape".

Channels in PushAll receive about 30-50% of readings from the active audience within a minute after sending. In VKontakte, per 100 thousand subscribers of the group, in the first hour 1-2 thousand views of the post come out. This ratio also indicates the activity of the audience - according to our statistics, 1 user PushAll by activity is equal to one hundred users in the VC. This is achieved due to the fact that during the day only a small part of your subscribers of the VKontakte group reads the tape, and many in general scroll through it, while the notification can be seen immediately.

We are also developing opportunities for selecting audience segments: there is now filtering on the user side and multicast broadcasts on the channel side when you can select a list of users and send notifications to them. We are working on a hybrid method - users choose topics by checkboxes, and you are already combining these topics send notifications.
In this case, you will always know what coverage you will end up with.

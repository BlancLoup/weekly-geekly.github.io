Wheretheladies.at - find your love


Mobile application ideas are in the air, and obviously never run out. The next project is another confirmation of this. The Wheretheladies.at service, which collects (check-in) marks of the weaker sex on Foursquare and tells visitors about these places, got an iPhone application.

The concept of a large compass, pointing in the direction of the place where the girls are now nearby, is so unprecedented that in the process of reviewing the application in the AppStore, co-founder of the service Jeff Hodesdon received a call from Apple to ask more about the application.

Wheretheladies.at for iPhone evaluates the public places closest to you by the number of girls and women noted in them (in the case when the field data is not available, the program tries to determine it independently). And also helpfully shows you the direction to the nearest cluster of women in close proximity to you. And what to do with this information will prompt you instinct.

Computer geeks and previously actively used the web service, gaining an advantage in finding girls. Now the service has become even more convenient. The application differs from those similar to it in that it counts the marks of the girls during the last 30 minutes, so that they must be in the indicated place by the time you get there. That is, when the application tells you that there are 10 girls in the nearest cafe, it means that they have been there for about 30 minutes. Service creators consider this advantage a key one.

Currently, the application is working only in San Francisco, but the team is working to be able to use it in other cities. And also they are going to urgently launch a similar project to search for guys - Wheretheguys.at.

Obviously, the idea and concept of the application is extremely interesting and may be useful in different areas. And for sure in the near future similar applications will appear for all popular mobile platforms. It remains to wait for the appearance of similar services in our native spaces, or create them yourself.

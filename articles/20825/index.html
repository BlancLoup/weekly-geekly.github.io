Rational reason for buying a commercial content management system

The only reasonable, rational argument in favor of buying a commercial content management system is the desire to get quick results for little money. The basic functionality and the availability of a library of standard design templates will allow you to realize your plans with almost no losses! Everything else is a pure bluff of marketing.

Affordable cost .
Typically, the cost of the basic version is at the mark of $ 100. The amount is really not big, but the overwhelming majority are buying a system with a functional "margin" (and therefore more expensive), hoping to actively develop their project! By the way, marketers are well aware of this and are building a pricing policy in such a way that it is much more attractive to buy a little more expensive but with more opportunities than to buy some modules later. In real life, as a rule, half of the stated capabilities of the system are not used.

If you update only news on the site, a headache with updates is not needed at all.

Testing nine external hard drives. Choose the best drive

In the modern world, mobility and free access to information is highly valued. Therefore, the question of storing user data and simultaneous access to it, for example, for all family members, is very urgent. Of course, the best solution for this is networked storage that is installed indoors. If earlier this required a separate computer, now it is not difficult to organize it on the basis of a wireless router. In addition, modern models are based on ARM processors and have support for USB 3.0 port, which greatly enhances their capabilities. And what could be better for the household than to use the shared network resource at home, and then grab it with you on vacation and entertain yourself by watching movies from an external drive? Here we will deal with the choice of an external disk for these tasks.

Of course, two models of large form factor drives have emerged as leaders of this test. The undisputed leader was an external hard drive Seagate Expansion. It is followed by Western Digital Elements, with a backlog of about 30 MB / s, for read / write operations.

Testing disks in NAS perfomance tester


The next step was testing drives as a network drive based on the ASUS RT-AC68U wireless router. Before testing, all disks were formatted in NTFS. Then they connected to the USB 3.0 port of the router and, using the NAS Perfomance Tester program, measured speeds for read and write operations for files of 100, 200 and 1000 MB in size.

Based on the data obtained, the following diagram was constructed:


As the external disks were tested as a network disk, the picture became clearer and duller with each new drive. Regardless of the used disk, their results practically did not differ from each other. That was until the turn of the Transcend StoreJet 25H3 drive arrived. Its results are out of the total number of all drives tested. If on read operations for files of 100 MB in size, the measured speeds coincide with competitors, then for files of 200 MB the read speed is 1.5 times higher, and for files 1000 MB is 2 times higher. On write operations, the results differ for files of 100 MB. The Transcend StoreJet 25H3 drive is about 1.4 times higher for files of any size.

According to the results of this test, we can conclude that even when using one of the most powerful routers with a dual-core ARM processor, 256 MB of RAM and a USB 3.0 port, its performance is not enough to fully unlock the potential of external hard drives as a network drive. Therefore, if you want to organize network storage on the basis of the router, it does not play a special role which disk for this you will use. The only exception may be the Transcend StoreJet 25H3, the results of which turned out to be the best in this type of testing.

Buying external drives of a large form factor for this task becomes simply ill-considered. They demonstrate absolutely identical results for small form factor disks, and have large dimensions and require additional power.

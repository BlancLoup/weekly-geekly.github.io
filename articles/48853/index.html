announced third-generation Intel Classmate PC netbook

2009 began and once there were new items. At CES (Las Vegas), Intel introduced the third generation of ultra-budget netbooks Classmate PC. A novelty for schoolchildren and emerging markets (Russia, according to the western classification, is precisely what it refers to). New Classmate PC as well as previous versions work on energy-saving Intel Atom processors, but in addition they have a touch screen (8.9-inch diagonal) and can be transformed into a tablet. The novelty is equipped with a waterproof keyboard, although it has a less durable case. In addition, the resolution of the built-in video camera has been increased (from 0.3 to 1.3 MP), the size of the SDR SDRAM drive has been increased (up to 16 GB) and the installation of built-in 3G modems or support for WiMAX is possible.

"Cellular" board is interesting to make so that the scheme can be assembled regardless of the order of the findings in the three-pin component. Sites marked with red (1), green (2) and blue (3) colors go to the component soldering point at the bottom of the SOT23-3 housing, platforms marked with letters A, B, C with numbers, as well as with Vcc and Gnd, connect the board with external circuits through small additional platforms, to which you can solder the comb. Since all the similar sites (A, B, C, Vcc, GND) are interconnected, the comb has only five pins. Sites marked in white are originally designed to provide power, but their role can be reassigned. Please also note that the sites marked in gray each have two alphanumeric designations: one at the top and the other at the bottom.



As the "bridges" between any two adjacent sites, you can use dual SMD components in housings 0603. These can be resistors, ceramic capacitors, diodes, even small inductors, etc. There are also jumpers in such housings, but they are easier to make from pieces of hard tinned wire.

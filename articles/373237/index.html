Overview of the new Aftershokz Sportz Titanium: another bone conduction headset that can be tied into a knot

Aftershokz has replaced the Sportz Titanium wired version of its Sportz headphones and headsets, dramatically improving sound transmission quality, sound absorption performance and body refinement.



Headphones with bone conduction sound should not be confused (and compared) with headphones that use bone transfer technology. In the latter, sound emitters are built into the ear cushions, and most of the energy is not transmitted through the air, which prevents bass from damaging the eardrum. Such headphones, as a rule, belong to the type of overhead and cover the ears of the listener.

Aftershokz headsets use only bone conduction, and this means that they do not cover their ears at all. There are currently three main headsets in the line: Bluez 2s, the flagship is Trekz Titanium and the new Sportz models.

The report on the results of "My Circle" for June 2016, and the most popular vacancies of the month

We present our monthly performance report on My Circle . In June, we had more than 700 vacancies, on average 8-9 specialists responded every month.

And now in more detail about the responses to vacancies in the most popular areas of activity.



  • As always, design vacancies are firmly in the lead, with an average of 22 responses for each design vacancy in June.
  • Frontend vacancies traditionally occupy the second place, this month they were joined by administrative vacancies: both of them and others collected an average of 13 vacancy responses in June each.
  • Management vacancies are constantly fighting for third place and regularly winning, but this month they took fourth place with 10 job vacancies.
  • Vacancies in backends have 8 responses each , and vacancies in software development have 6 responses per vacancy.
  • For the second month in a row, traditionally popular vacancies on testing subside, each having again, on average, less than 5 responses, and not 6-7, as in previous months.



But what is the overall semi-annual trend in response to the most popular areas of activity.



  • Most job seekers respond to design jobs, an average of 20-21 people per month.
  • On vacancies of layout designers and managers , 10-12 specialists respond on average.
  • On the job of administrators, on average, responds to 10 people.
  • On average, 6-7 people respond to programmers and testers .
  • On average, 5-6 specialists respond to the vacancies of application developers and software developers .

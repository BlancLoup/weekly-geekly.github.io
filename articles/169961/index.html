Employment portals: an unexpected marketing tool

Today I decided to talk about a rather non-standard topic. Several times in my practice I have come across the fact that job search portals (HH, Superjob and others) can serve as very useful tools in marketing activities not at all for their intended purpose - job search or employees.

Free (or completely inexpensive) we can get access to huge databases of companies or people who may be our potential customers, contractors, competitors. What can be useful to extract from this data for yourself? Let me try to list a few tricks that may be useful in the work.

Your service reference index: resumes / vacancies


If you are promoting a startup / service that people use for workflows (especially b2b), then one of the metrics reflecting the distribution efficiency of your product may be the number of references to it in the vacancy database and resume (for a certain period).

The requirement (or wish) for knowing your product in the job description is a very useful metric that shows how popular your product is among your Central Asia and what its dynamics are. The graph of the number of such references can serve as one of the KPI marketing (although, of course, not the main one).

The same applies to the mention of your service in the resume of employees. If your product is so good that the applicant indicates it in his application form, then possession of it is considered one of the competitive advantages. This metric can serve as a good KPI for products, one of the elements of the strategy for which is the promotion of services ‚Äúfrom below‚Äù, orientation not only to decision makers, but also to ordinary employees.

Needless to say, no one will interfere with collecting exactly the same data for competitors, analyzing them depending on certain actions to develop functionality or change positioning. In a sense, the collection of these data can serve as a ‚Äúlightweight analogue‚Äù of full-fledged research on the recognition of your brand and competitors among Central Asia (which, as a rule, cost a lot of money).

Of course, such a metric will be quite useless for the agency business, but when the name of your studio / agency begins to appear in the job description of competitors: ‚Äú Experience in XXX, YYY and ZZZ is preferable ‚Äù is also a good sign.

Often, potential customer vacancies can serve as a good entry point for the initial appeal. Point catching owners and directors of SMB


A huge number of companies and agencies consider their business owners to be Central Organ. The competition to reach such an audience is often very high, and there are not so many points of concentration of this Central Asian.

If we talk about medium and small business, then often in such companies there is no dedicated role of the HR director and or manager, and directors and top management are directly involved in the selection of employees. And this means that they themselves are looking for employees from the databases of job portals.

Appearance


The sleek Neoline X-COP 9500 case is made of matte plastic, on which no prints remain. The material of the qualitatively assembled case cannot be called fragile, but it will not survive the collision with the windshield during an accident. The memory card is not so easy to destroy, but the case of the device itself was definitely worth making it more secure.



The DVR is attached to the bracket at the top of the L-shaped case. Fits tightly. Both of those present in the complete set of fasteners can be adjusted to the desired shooting-detection angle. The registrar is convenient to remove, without loosening the suction cup once again. Note that the vertical mount does not block the view on the windshield.



The top panel of the dark plastic hides the radio module (including the Arrow and GPS modules) for detecting radars. The bottom half - this is the DVR.



There are two buttons on the device: on / off and emergency recording. The second button is duplicating, because there is a built-in shock sensor, which performs the function of activating an emergency recording.



On the left are a slot for a memory card and reset.



On the back is a three-inch touchscreen display. The prints remain on the display, but they are noticeable only when the state is off.

Interface and Management




The absence of hardware buttons on a curved body is perceived as a blessing - all control is carried out through a matte touchscreen display with an IPS matrix, the dimensions of which allow you to comfortably use the device to the driver and / or passengers.

The interface seemed to be in the previous life "version for the blind." It is convenient to poke a finger without looking at the display. However, the hybrid, guided by the principle of included-and-forgot, works immediately after the start without additional settings.



The top + GPS icon is used to add / remove camera / radar user coordinates.

The lower block contains information about the speed, connection to GPS, time, and the established recording resolution.



The main menu provides for the choice of settings of the radar detector, video recorder and the mode of viewing the recording. By default, the device has the optimal settings for the full operation - just install the gadget on the windshield.

The display is resistive, you can even use it with gloves.

Radar detector




In many European countries, it is prohibited by law to use radar detectors, therefore, we will talk about using the Neoline X-COP 9500 only in the context of use on Russian roads. Remember that when banning the use of a radar detector, you can simply turn off all radio bands in the settings.

Good news: the radar detector in our country is legal. The bad news is: if this is important to you, then you are potentially capable of violating traffic rules - we recommend that you follow the rules and not exceed the allowable speed of movement. Nevertheless, the radar detector is here and it can be useful to someone.

The GPS module, which has a built-in radar base of the Russian Federation and Europe, sends a signal at a distance of up to two kilometers (in our tests, results from 600 meters to 1 km were recorded).
The display shows the symbol of a radar or range, signal strength, current and permissible speed in the area. Soundtrack is present, but it can be turned off.

The Neoline X-COP 9500 detects any surveillance systems on the roads: stationary and mobile radars, including the Robot and Mesta, which are difficult to detect, Strelka ST cameras and the Avtodoria. The coordinates of the new stationary police cameras can be added manually.

When a surveillance system is detected, the Neoline X-COP 9500 will emit an audible notification, and display the icon of the detected radar on the display. The GPS module will start counting in meters to the arrow indicated in the database, as well as report the speed allowed in this area.

Conclusion


The Neoline X-COP 9500 is not a DVR with a radar detector function, but a full-fledged hybrid device. The cost of the hybrid makes it stand out against the general background of DVRs, but there is no need to overpay - instead of one device, we get two. The user interface deserves all praise, even a child can deal with it, and the control is implemented in such a way that it does not create additional interference for the driver.

The quality of the video you can evaluate yourself, it justifies the cost of the device. The radar detector works flawlessly, is intuitive and is able to protect drivers from many problems.

The drawbacks that we found during testing can be explained by the dampness of the firmware itself. Most of them can be fixed in the following updates.

According to Svyaznoy, the demand for this hybrid has steadily increased since the release, despite the huge number of other, cheaper DVRs. The market dynamics is simply explained - one device is better than two separate ones. So the hybrid of the future, which the navigator will inevitably enter, will bite off a tangible part of the market.

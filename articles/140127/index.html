The issue of phone numbers in the LTE network is being resolved.

Members of the Systems and Communications Section of the Scientific and Technical Council of the Russian Ministry of Communications and Mass Media discussed the issue of assigning telephone numbers in a situation when new communication standards are emerging.
The situation is such that Scartel LLC (trademark - Yota) from April 15, 2012 begins to use LTE technology in Moscow. In this regard, the question arises about the technical compatibility with the interaction of users located in different mobile radio telephone communication networks: the new LTE and traditional GSM / UMTS. However, it has not yet been determined in what order the numbers will be assigned to LTE subscribers and whether they should differ from the ‚Äútraditional‚Äù numbers.


During the meeting, Sergei Solovyov, Director for Science of the Central Research Institute of Communications, made a presentation on international experience in using numbering on LTE networks. There were also reports from the operating director of Scartel LLC, Dmitry Nudgi, and the head of the technical development department of Megafon OJSC, Andrey Grachev.
The main question was that the telephone numbers should be given to the operator, not the network. In this case, the numbers in GSM and LTE standards will look the same. If the subscriber already has a number in the GSM standard (the DEF (MSISDN) code is used there), he can also use it in the LTE standard. If the user does not yet have a number, he will receive from his operator a new one in the same DEF code.

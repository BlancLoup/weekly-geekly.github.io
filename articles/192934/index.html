Flying red lights


For the first time the message about the mysterious red lights came from the small town of St. Augustine, Florida April 19, 2008. Brandon Pakkett, 12, and his mother, Laura, said they saw two unidentified objects flying at the same speed over the coastline and disappearing. The objects were shaped like balloons and glowed with bright red-orange light. Several neighbors in the same area confirmed that they saw the same objects. Skeptics claimed that a group of people were lucky to see Chinese lanterns in the sky. The authorities enthusiastically hooked on this theory. And they would be able to safely shut up this uncomfortable topic for them if the red lights did not appear again.

On April 20, 2008, several residents of Deer Valley, Arizona, reported red lights in the sky. Anne Ryman, a reporter for the Arizona Republic newspaper, who lives in Deer Valley, spoke about the observations of four lights, combined into a quadrilateral shape, which later became triangular. The lights moved east and disappeared one by one. Are there Chinese lanterns again? Perhaps if the latter can perform aerobatics.

Be that as it may, it is becoming increasingly difficult to stop up eyewitnesses. One of the inhabitants of northern Phoenix said that he saw four or five red glowing balls, lined up, and then scattered in the side. The lights slowly moved eastward, becoming dimmer. He said that the last ball remained in the sky for a long time, until three jet fighters appeared from the west, following in the same direction as the red lights.

Reports of strange red lights began to come from all over the world. Mysterious red lights were seen in the skies over Germany, Great Britain, the Republic of South Africa, and of course over Russia. On the same day, when a 12-year-old boy from St. Augustine and his mother became an eyewitness to an unusual phenomenon, residents of the town of Pushkino watched something similar to what was reported by the independent television channel NTV.

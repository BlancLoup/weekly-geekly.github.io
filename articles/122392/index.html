Sony Ericsson's premier day: the latest Xperia ray, Xperia active and others

Today at CommunicAsia 2011 in Singapore took place the world premiere of two completely new androids from Sony Ericsson: the mid-size aluminum Sony Ericsson Xperia ray for all occasions, and the compact dust-proof and waterproof Sony Ericsson Xperia active for fans of fitness and jogging in the morning.

Rays


Xperia ray is a thin (only 9.4 mm) flat candy bar. The design of the novelty - a mix of trends from the latest models - one ‚Äúphysical‚Äù Home button and two touch Back and Menu (as on the latest Xperia mini) - and new trends expressed in the form of edging from real aluminum around the perimeter and almost completely solid frontal surface from mineral glass



And this is the first Sony Ericsson lately to abandon the elements of biodesign corresponding to the curves of the human body - the ray is more likely for those who prefer the square to the oval. By the way, ray in Japanese means elegance.



The screen is 3.3 ", in which the same 854x480 pixels are hidden as in Xperia neo and Xperia arc (3.7" and 4.2 ", respectively). The weight of ray is exactly 100 grams (here the comparison with the weight of the new Xperia is more appropriate) mini - 94 g about 3 "diagonal screen). So, in terms of weight and dimensions, it has occupied a niche exactly between Xperia mini and mini pro and Xperia neo and Xperia arc. For the price is expected to be the same.

Inside - already familiar to the readers of this blog "pieces of iron": 1-GHz processor, 1500 mAh battery and a camera identical to the camera of the flagship Sony Ericsson - Xperia arc - the same 8.1 MP + technology improved shooting in low light conditions Exmor R From the new - added front camera, hidden under the glass.



Also, according to the good tradition of all Xperia this year, the new product will be released on the latest version of Android: 2.3.3 or even 2.3.4 - if the latter does not appear on the ray at the start of sales, it will be released in one of the first updates. An important question regarding future updates - they promise that they will update the firmware as long as it allows the hardware.

Soft

Hard

And instead of the heart - a fiery gigahertz processor, powered by a 1200 mAh battery. The camera can shoot 5 megapixel photos and HD video.

Smart stuff


The premiere of two Sony Ericsson smartphones balanced the premiere double of the replenishment of its Smart Extras lineup - unique accessories. With one of which, by the way, our regular readers are already familiar - talking about the microdisplay for different occasions Live View .

Today, the line of ‚Äúsmart accessories‚Äù has been replenished with a LiveDock multimedia docking station and LiveSound hi-fi headphones.

LiveDock

In the photo - LiveDock in perhaps the most popular usage format - as an alarm clock:



Another possible scenario is a player in the kitchen (and in the bathroom with Xperia active).

The USB connector allows you to connect a mouse, keyboard or gamepad to the docking station (which is why it is called multimedia). And through the HDMI connector on the phone itself, you can also connect to the TV, the phone at the same time, it turns out, performs the function of the console. Want - surf the Internet in High Definition, or you can play, for example, in Angry birds. The docking station itself has no HDMI connector.

All new Sony Ericsson phones starting with Xperia mini and mini pro, as well as Xperia pro, active and ray, support LiveDock by default, software updates will be required for Xperia neo and Xperia arc.

Livesound

Headphones LiveSound as if created specifically for those who fundamentally reject the quality of the stock "ears." Promising hi-fi sound, this product of high technology is, nevertheless, on the opposite pole from the ‚Äúwarm lamp‚Äù products: the metal case and flat non-tangled wires underline the modernity of the solutions of this accessory.



Other features include the magic LiveKey button, which, together with the volume rocker, replaces the remote control not only for listening to audio, but also for some other functions of the phone, such as receiving / rejecting a call, recording a conversation and listening to music during a conversation. Among the well-known functions are recording and sharing audio, searching for music by track fragment using TrackID. LiveKey can also interact with sports applications, for example, capturing the work done for a certain period of time.

Developers have the opportunity to write their own applications for LiveKey, and some of the functions of the LiveSound headset will also be available on some Nokia and Samsung models.

The black headset will be available with black liners and lime shades of colors, and white with white and orange.

Like all other new products, LiveSound will be available in the third quarter of 2011.

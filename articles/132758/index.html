Video review of stylish Honeycomb tablet from Japan - Sony Tablet S

At the IFA 2011 in Berlin were shown two new items on Android from Sony : Sony Tablet S and Sony Tablet P. The first of the tablets, finally, fell into our hands, and was fully tested, leaving a pleasant impression. Detailed result in today's "Review without rules":



One of the main distinguishing features of the Sony Tablet S is the shape of the case with a thickening on one side and a narrowed shape on the other. Manufacturers themselves call this solution "Bent Sheet."



Due to the unusual shape of the tablet feels like a book or magazine, which allows him quite habitually to hold in one hand. In addition, a small role in convenience is played by a small weight of the novelty - 598 g. (IPad 2 - 601 g.).

The device has a screen with a diagonal of 9.4 inches with a resolution of 1280x800 pixels. Uses Sony IPS matrix with LED backlight, which provides a comfortable eye for the contrast, brightness and excellent color reproduction.

Specifications Sony Tablet S :
  • Processor : dual-core 1 GHz Nvidia Tegra 2
  • Memory : 1GB (RAM) 16 / 32GB of internal memory, slot for SD cards
  • Screen : 9.4 inches, LCD IPS, resolution 1280x800
  • OS : Android 3.2 Honeycomb
  • Cameras : Main: 5 Mp, LED-flash, front: 0.3 Mp
  • Data transfer : Bluetooth 2.1, microUSB 2.0, Wi-Fi 802.11 a / b / g / n, DLNA, GPS
  • Network : GSM 3G / 2G / HSDPA available in 3G version
  • Additionally : accelerometer, compass, gyroscope, applications for working with Sony services and technology, Playstation Certified




The tablet works on a modified Android 3.2 Honeycomb , although there are quite a few changes to the interface, everything works quickly and without glitches. The battery life also shows good results in terms of battery life. The battery charge for normal use lasts about 2 days.

The price on the market starts from 16-17 thousand rubles , but do not forget that it is still a Sony product.

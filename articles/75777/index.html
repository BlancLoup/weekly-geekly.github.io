Bing ‚ù§ Wolfram Alpha

One of the most important tasks that we set for ourselves is to provide the user with the desired information in a convenient form. If we can make an adequate conclusion that the user needs, then we give him much more information than a simple list of links. We can teach them real knowledge to bring the user closer to the answer.

At Bing, our journey began with working on the answers, and we continue to make every effort to provide the best answers based on our opinion of the purpose of the request. Information about flights, local weather, sports ratings and statistics are the earliest examples of our work.

But we know that much more can be realized for our users. Today we are pleased to show our results in collaboration with Wolfram | Aplha . Launched in May 2009 by famous scientist Stefan Wolfram. Wolfram | Aplha is an ambitious and very cool project, and as the company says, "make all systematic knowledge immediately countable by anyone."

This concept of creating and presenting computational knowledge in search results is one of the most exciting searches to date, and the Bing team presents users with an amazing job. This functionality is still available to users who have US localization in the settings.

So what have we done? We provide access to the advanced Wolfram | Alpha algorithms in Bing search.

While searching for certain foods, Bing will give you a note with a detailed description of the product.



Another useful tool for calculating body mass index. If you are looking for "bmi" (body mass index), you can enter weight and height, and then click "Calculate" to see a detailed BMI analysis directly in Bing.




Nutrition and fitness information are only part of our partnership with Wolfram | Alpha. We also provide the ability to calculate complex math functions.




We hope that you will use all this to stay healthy and be savvy in math.

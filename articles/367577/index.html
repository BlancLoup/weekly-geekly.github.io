It is obvious that such things were created for short demonstrations at fashion shows, meetings, festivals, concerts and are not intended for long wear.

Most of all she liked the implementation of the skirt Hikaru, but she was not satisfied with the children's skirt style, so popular in Japan, and limited functionality - Hikaru's skirt only glows in motion.

She decided to make a denim mini / micro skirt with a battery and a smart lighting controller hidden in the belt buckle.
She hid a 5050 LED strip inside the waterproof tube and attached the tape to the skirt with safety pins.

And when testing for the first time, it turned out that the pins heat up and burn skin painfully. She fixed this bug by stitching a 0.2mm fishing line to the skirt:


The next step was to make a belt buckle on a 3D printer, where the battery and electronics would be hidden.

She created a project for a printer using Tinkercad.com, it was simpler than OpenSCAD and she was already familiar with this application. Later, she made the buckle more compact (Thanks to SelenIT2 for the continued sequel).

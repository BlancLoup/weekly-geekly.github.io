0. A bit about the "on-board computer"


Citizens!

Please, nowhere and never in professional communication do not use the definitions of " central control unit of the car ", " on-board computer of the car " or even " central on-board computer of the car "!

The reason for this request is trivial: there is simply no such device in the vast majority of modern cars from the point of view of the architecture of internal communication networks.

In fact, a modern car is a kind of motorized computer network with at least twenty specific microcomputers in its composition. And on average, even with 30-40-50-60 members of this network.

2. Automotive and automation


Ten years ago, the author, to his own surprise, realized that such similar words " automotive " and " automation " mean quite different areas of human activity.

Of course, historically and logically, they have much in common - but still they are different worlds.

So we fix:

  • " Automotive " is all that is related to automotive topics (direct manufacturers, their suppliers, manufacturers of all kinds of automotive software, standards, regulatory prescriptions, etc., etc.).

  • " Automation " is, as the name implies, everything related to automation (production in the first place, but not only) - that is, TAU, conveyor lines, all kinds of drives, IoT, digitalization, and Other Industry 4.0, and all-all-all.

MegaFon launches Russian accounting system in housing and public utilities



The topic of housing and public utilities is generally recognized as one of the most difficult to reform and far from innovation. However, MegaFon decided to change this. Megalabs , the innovation center of MegaFon, launches an absolutely unique for Russia service in the housing and utilities sector: ‚ÄúResource under control‚Äù. The project has already been launched in Moscow and the Moscow region, and since September it will be expanded to the whole territory of Russia.

In a nutshell: MegaFon daily collects and processes data from all heat, water and light meters remotely, giving payers the ability to monitor them and pay online. Due to this, such a procedure as meter reading is becoming a thing of the past, and with it a number of important tasks are being solved, which ultimately make it possible to finally start saving money on bills for an apartment - which is especially important lately.

How it works

‚ÄúResource under control‚Äù consists of actual meters, channel-forming equipment - communication lines, modems, routers, and databases and application software.

The partner of MegaLabs is the integrator company ‚ÄúCenter for the Development of Automation Tools‚Äù ( CRSA ), which has extensive experience in the implementation of automated energy control and accounting systems (AMR). MegaFon provides delivery, collection, central processing of data and resources to scale the project on a federal scale.

First of all, counters are installed in the subscriber‚Äôs apartment or private house. At the same time, the replacement of already installed metering devices is not necessary, provided that they are in working condition, they have not expired verification period, and there are pulse outputs for connecting transceiver equipment.

In such cases, MegaFon installs only transceiver equipment that, via various interfaces - wired / wireless or via a PLC power network, transmit data to the channel-forming equipment, which is installed, for example, in the attic, in the basement or in the control room of an apartment building. In the case of the private sector, usually at a transformer substation.


Placement of equipment in the control room of an apartment building. Modem - in the upper left corner

There, among other equipment, modems with MegaFon SIM cards are installed. Once a day or more often (for industrial enterprises), the equipment transmits consumption data through a dedicated APN access point via a VPN channel within the MegaFon network to the MegaFon server in the data center (Tier III reliability according to TIA-942), where processing information and transferring it to the user's personal account page. The system is completely closed from outside influence.

The Internet is used only by clients to access the web-based interface or their personal account on a computer, laptop, tablet or other mobile device, where the already collected and processed information is displayed.

Already from the data center, this information is sent to various consumers: billing systems, management companies, power sales organizations, industrial enterprises, which, along with private users, get access to a personal account on the site resurs.megafon.ru , which allows you to monitor your consumption and history on a daily basis spending resources, making payments, asking questions, etc.

Fight against "loss" of resources

Electricity is "lost" in two ways: natural loss and banal theft. Electricity losses during transmission do not exceed 4‚Äì5%, but the total losses reach 30‚Äì40%, and the difference between these two indicators is theft by illegal connection to the grid or ‚Äútwisting‚Äù the meters.

The ‚ÄúTsRSA‚Äù company works with the best domestic suppliers of metering devices supporting various technologies of protection against theft of electricity. I will tell about how it works, on the example of the counters of the Moscow-based company " Matrix ":



The magnet sensor protects against the popular method of twisting the counters with the help of powerful magnets. Reacting to a magnetic field, he sends a signal to the data center about an attempt to "twist".
Differential current sensor that notes the difference between incoming and returning current. If this difference is not equal to zero, then the counter can turn off the subscriber or signal an accident to the center.


Seals and sensors protect the counter from opening

In the private sector, the meter is installed outside the household, making it difficult to connect to the network bypassing the meter - this is just too noticeable.

Thanks to the continuous dispatch of balances on the entrance, house, feeder, etc. the thefts are detected almost immediately in a remote mode, therefore, in the Resource under Control system, the loss of electricity is reduced to the most natural 4-5%.

Unlike electricity, problems with underpayment for water usually result from meter malfunction and improper filling of receipts with subscribers. In such houses, the readings of the general-purpose metering device disagree with the total indicators of water meters.
This difference in readings is covered by bona fide subscribers who pay in addition to their consumption also these losses, which can be up to 50% of his account.

‚ÄúResource under control‚Äù allows you to simplify data collection: there is no need to enter the apartment to subscribers for visual readings, as well as to reduce balances for home and apartment meters, which allows to detect emergencies, real losses (breaks in engineering networks, unauthorized water withdrawal etc.). As a result, the problem of incorrect filling of receipts is solved, and most importantly, there is no longer any need to pay for water ‚Äúfor myself and for that guy‚Äù, because there simply cannot be an unaccounted water consumption in our system. Resource under control!

The third important component of energy consumption control is heat supply. Here the main problem lies, first of all, in the absence of apartment heat meters and the ability to regulate its flow. Often, during the heating season, residents have to open windows in order to maintain a comfortable temperature in the apartment, and, accordingly, pay for the surplus.
Installing a system of general heat metering with an automated control unit allows you to regulate the flow of heat to the apartments in accordance with the temperature outside. Fine-tuning is carried out by installing an apartment heat metering system together with individual thermostats, which helps reduce consumption.

  • Savings: for example, sensors of heat and volume, taking into account the subscriber's consumption of heat, allow the average apartment to save about 300 rubles per month, which guarantees a return on their installation for 2 years.

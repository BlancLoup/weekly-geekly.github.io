What do the results of the survey of British firms on the topic of new gTLDs?

Nominet, the administrator of the national UK domain .UK, conducted a survey among 530 companies on the introduction of new top-level domains. The number of respondents included the most well-known UK firms that are engaged in both large and medium-sized and small businesses, and have their own website on the Internet.

As it turned out, 41% of organizations do not know anything about the new top-level domain program, despite the fact that ICANN has already accepted more than 1900 applications for new gTLDs, and the first top-level domains registered under these applications will start working next year. Interestingly, this figure almost coincided with the results of a survey among Austrian companies - according to nic.at, 40% of Austrian companies are not aware of the introduction of free registration of new gTLDs.

Also, according to a Nominet survey, 62% of companies are not going to register their own domain or any second-level domain in new zones. 22% of companies plan to register domains in new zones, but the company's main website will remain in an existing domain. 2% of companies are going to leave the old zones and place the organization‚Äôs main website in a new gTLD.

And although Nominet, like Nic.at , believes that the new gTLDs have not found a wide response from the public, the numbers suggest the opposite - more than 24% of firms are already potential customers of the owners of new domain zones, which in the future may also register their own gtld.

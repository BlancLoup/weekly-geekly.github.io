User Koavf made 1 million Wikipedia edits

On April 18, the Wikipedian community congratulated the first user in the history of Wikipedia, who was able to overcome the mark of 1 million edits and comments. User Koavf - Justin Anthony Knapp from Indianapolis - registered for the project in March 2005 after several months of anonymous editing. The profile says he is 29 years old, he graduated from a Christian school and Indiana University, a university degree in philosophy and political science.

On Mashable believe that for such achievements you need to give the order.

Indeed, if you try to count in monetary terms (or in man-hours) the work done, you get impressive numbers. Even if one minute is counted for each revision, then it is 16,666, (6) hours of work, or 2,083 working days, if you count eight-hour working days. However, the active authors of Wikipedia probably have more than eight hours of working days.

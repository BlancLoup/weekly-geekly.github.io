One week in the life of Ekaterinburg MakeXLab hackspace

Despite the fact that more than two months have passed since the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthis post, we still decided to finish it and publish it by the end of the year. The idea was simple - to show in pictures one week (or a bit more) of the life of our hackspace MakeItLab.

I will remind, on October 16, at 01:08 - the first publication about MakeItLab on Habr√© appeared. On the nearest Saturday (October 19), a meeting was scheduled with participants from Yekaterinburg, who were interested in the Hackspace movement. Partly from this, the end of that week, and all the following, were extremely eventful.

So let's get started.

Saturday (October 19)


On the open day was 12 people. There were certainly more people willing, but for a room of 26 square meters it is almost the limit.




On the same day, already in the evening, we received a translation from Mitsch Altman ‚Äôs speech about hackspace from one of our fellow linguists.

Actually, translations are another useful activity of ours. There are many great tutorials, courses, and other educational information in English. For example, now we are translating courses for Snap!


Sunday (October 20)


An offline meeting of the CoolRF home automation project (an element of the smart home) took place.




At the meeting, very interesting components were demonstrated, including: an RGB lamp controlled from a phone (glows red in the background), Vyacheslav Anisimov's CoolRF project, and other interesting things. Actually, the main emphasis was just on the "smart dimmer" Vyacheslav.

After the party was over, they began to collect the Fritz robot head.


If anyone is interested, we accept orders :) A very interesting thing turned out, but more on that later ...

Finally, on the same day, they prepared and published a questionnaire of participants of the hackspace. We consider as participants those who are willing to attend this institution and make their projects in it, while helping in every way.

Monday (October 21)


In the evening, one of our 3D-printer took part in an unusual performance. We were invited to the thematic disco PICTUREPLANE.




A very rewarding experience of directly promoting the ideas of 3D printing. The simplest people approached the apparatus, asked, wondered. They really wanted to try on made bracelets (which, by the way, were shining in the ultraviolet of the disco). As a result, four bracelets were imperceptibly carried away by girls :)

Tuesday (October 22)


At last, the field tests of our quadrocopter have taken place. Probably it will seem trivial to many, but in our case the event was really significant. The machine has an unusual control layout, and is coded from scratch. The tests took place late in the evening, so the video turned out very dark: (

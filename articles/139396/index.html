Review of fresh materials, January-February 2012

This material continues a series of monthly reviews of fresh articles on the topic of interfaces, new tools and collections of patterns, interesting cases and historical stories. Of the tapes of several hundred thematic subscriptions, approximately 5% of the worthwhile publications are selected that are interesting to share. Previous materials: April 2010-December 2011 .



Methods and practices


The Messy Art Of UX Sketching
Peiter Buick's excellent step-by-step tutorial on how to learn how to make good sketches of interfaces. CMS Magazine released its translation with comments from domestic experts .

Behavior Heuristics
Dan Lockton, author of Design with Intent, a well-known design framework, talks about user behavior heuristics. It describes the general principle of their construction and provides a basic list that can be expanded and supplemented.

Stefan Klocek - Altmedia and the Tablet Opportunity
Presentation of Stefan Klocek from Cooper, in which he talks about the importance of optimizing news consumption interfaces for tablets. The author tells about the features of such interfaces and gives a number of examples.
Altmedia and the Tablet Opportunity

Breaking Through the Glass
Interesting reflections Rob Le Quesne on the creation of interfaces as art projects. He gives examples of such experiments, including in commercial use.

Responsive Design & ROI - Observations from the Coalface
Chris Berridge shares the experience of CXPartners in creating products with responsive designs. This imposes features on the process of their design, design and testing, which are described in the article.

UX and the Kano Model
Christian Holst's excellent review article from the Baymard Institute on the Kano model. It allows you to determine which of the product‚Äôs capabilities are a basic necessity, which are responsible for its practical value, and which are able to elicit enthusiastic feedback.

Browser and GUI Chrome
Jakob Nielsen weighs the pros and cons of the presence in the interface "dodger." These are elements that are mandatory for a product or service, but are repeated on each screen and take up extra space - for example, the browser address bar or the main menu of the site.

The Eye of the Brainstorm
Alan Cooper reflects on which team interaction format is the most productive. The experience of his company suggests that the best option is pair work.

Why Do Products Fail?
Scott Sehlhorst describes a method for investigating the causes of product failure. It offers a basic set of heuristics that can be expanded and supplemented.

Six Circles - An Experience Design Framework
James Kelway of User Pathways has published his book on interface design based on blog posts over the past few years. She describes the methods and practices, theoretical calculations and a lot of other useful information on the topic. Direct link to PDF (16 MB).

Video Diaries - A Method for Understanding New Usage Patterns
Paul Bryan writes about the method of user research using video diaries. The article describes the method itself and its methods of application.

Information Architecture Heuristics
Abby Covert offers 10 simple heuristics for checking the information architecture of a product. These are clear rules for checking the quality of the designed interface.

View more presentations from Abby Covert

Alluring Layouts - drawing and capturing attention
Gemma Savagar talks about the problem of classic page layout sections. They become so similar to each other that they make it difficult to determine the current section of the site, so you should use visually more excellent approaches.

Communicating User Research Findings
Jim Ross talks about how to better present the results of user research. Long reports, as a rule, are rarely read and reluctantly, but there are a number of formats that facilitate the transfer of knowledge gained.

Tips for Writing Great Links
Gerry McGovern gives simple and clear advice on how to put clear and simple links on the pages of the site. The topic seems to be very simple and does not require special attention, but the author has researched it well and maintains an interesting blog about links .

Kristina Halvorson - Message and Medium: Better Content by Design
Speech by Kristina Halvorson at An Event Apart, in which she talks about the specifics of working with content for products that have many mobile and other versions. It is important to remember that the goal is not the content itself, but its main message - this will help to find a way out in a multitude of supported devices.


Kristina Halvorson - Message and Medium: Better Content by Design (live at An Event Apart) from Jeffrey Zeldman on Vimeo .

Designing for touch
A good review article by Josh Clark, dedicated to the design and design of touch interfaces. This is a good set of basic principles from the author of Tapworthy, one of the most popular books on the subject.

Rachel Hinman - The Mobile Frontier
Presentation of Rachel Hinman, the author of the upcoming book The Mobile Frontier. The presentation describes the main points of the future edition of Rosenfeld Media.

View more presentations from Rachel Hinman

Pub Smith - Sketching Interfaces Workshop
Slides from the Pub Smith training session on creating interface sketches at the 2012 Interactions conference. There is a lot of useful information about techniques, tools and their use.

View more presentations from pubsmith

Mission transition
Review Mark Cossey article on the importance of using animation in interfaces and displaying transitions between states in general. He gives examples from existing products and lists several key principles.

Convenience
Ari Weissman continues the series of business articles for people on the concept of comfort. He gives examples of products that have become leaders in their niche, largely due to the most comfortable solution to the old problem.

Why Thanking Donors Online is Powerful
Simon Norris and Juliet Richardson from Nomensa talk about the experience of creating services for donating to charity and donors. The subtle point in this task is a neat way of thanking the donor, without reducing it all to platitudes and unnecessarily material things.

8 Tips for a Sane IA
Alastair Campbell from Nomensa gives 8 tips to the information architect. They describe the rules for forming navigation and content organization.

Seven UX Best Practices of Community Design
Kristin Zibell describes 7 interface principles of competent work with communities. Some of them are quite commonplace, but the rest are described interestingly.

Designing Experiences for Young Kids
Another article with tips on creating tablet apps for kids. Avi Itzkovitch describes the rules for constructing navigation and using gestures in such applications.

QR Codes Are Footnotes, Not Ads
Josh Clark describes the main problems of classic QR codes that prevent them from truly mass distribution. And it shows an interesting example of an alternative QR code that solves some of the shortcomings.

Is There Ever A Justification For Responsive Text?
An interesting experiment with adaptive text in the interface. Although in the proposed form the support of such content in a large product may turn into hell, the idea itself may be useful.

Tools and Templates


Integrating UX into the Product Backlog
Jon Innes offers a convenient matrix that matches the functionality of the product to the needs of key groups of the target audience, priorities, UX-metrics and team composition. It is designed for agile projects and allows you to prioritize requirements.

Adobe Proto app - what makes wireframes and prototypes
Adobe has released a Proto application for designing interfaces on tablets. While only the version for Android is available.

GestureWorks Multitouch Framework - Build Gesture-Driven Apps
Ideum has released the GestureWorks 3 framework for creating multi-touch applications. It is designed for devices of various purposes: from compact tablets to large-scale interactive walls.

The UX Research Plan That Stakeholders Love
Tomer Sharon from Google offers a custom research plan template that is great for presenting to interested parties. He describes the structure of the plan and an example of its completion.

Say Goodbye to the Menu Button
At the end of January, the Android platform had another important interface event - the physical menu button was abolished. She was one of the most controversial decisions of the platform ‚Äî she had no affordants and was unpredictable in behavior.

Windows 8 Consumer Preview hands-on
One of the most detailed reviews of Windows 8 Consumer Preview. A lot of interesting things about gestures, standard and third-party applications. And another interesting review .

US Department of Transportation Proposes 'Distraction' Guidelines for Automakers
The US Department of Transportation has published preliminary guidelines for creating interfaces that do not distract the driver. It describes how to work with messages, the Internet, social networks, navigation systems and embedded phones.

Data Monday: Login & Passwords
Luke Wroblewski has collected some interesting statistics on the use of logins and passwords. It largely confirms the fact that comfort is more important to users than safety.

Design applications for Windows Phone. Navigation. Levels, backwards and cycles
An article by Konstantin Kichinsky about the features of navigation design in applications for Windows Phone. It provides excellent screen transition diagrams that are worth borrowing for design artifacts.

The Restart Page - Free unlimited rebooting experience from vintage operating systems
A collection of notification windows from various operating systems requesting permission to restart the computer. A good monument to obsessive design.

How to improve the selection of countries from the list
There was a Russian-language translation of the article by Christian Holst about creating a good tool for choosing a country in the interface. It offers several options for different situations, including multiple choice and search hints.

Process


Stop Validating Your Product
Laura Klein calls to check first of all not the product, but the problem that he is trying to solve. This coincides with the main message of the design thinking methodology, which is focused on finding the root problem and the people for whom it is important.

Kevin Hoffman - Facilitating Project Kickoffs
Interview with Kevin Hoffman of Happy Cog, in which he talks about the procedure for meetings on the launch of the project. This is an extremely important event, on which the success of the whole project depends.

UX designers make mistakes: the writeup
Scott Berkun lists the main mistakes that interface designers make when interacting with other project participants. He gives advice on how to get around these bottlenecks.

What makes a great UX practitioner? Hint: It's not what you think
David Travis from UserFocus talks about his career development as a UX specialist. In addition to technical skills, building processes and an understanding of marketing proved critical to his success.

Specializing generalist
Scott Sehlhorst thinks out loud about which specialists are important to the success of the team. He compares the pros and cons of narrow specialists or masters of all trades, and also gives a lot of quotes from articles of practicing managers and business owners.

Strategies for early-stage design: Guinea pig
Report on an interesting experiment of the University of California, which investigated the effectiveness of different ways to solve complex design problems. The participants in the experiment tried different strategies and approaches to the process, each of which had its own indications and contraindications for use.

9 Principles of Lean UX
LUXr describes the 9 basic principles of lean design. The company is especially interesting because it bases its work exclusively on flexible project management techniques, which is not very typical for designers.

Dealing with Difficult People, Teams, and Organizations - A UX Research Maturity Model
Tomer Sharon describes the maturity stages of user research in a company. The article is accompanied by several interviews of well-known experts on the topic of why it is not worth working with immature organizations. In continuation of the topic - another publication by the author , telling how to convince decision makers to invest in user research.

Beyond the pixel - Measuring visual designers' strategic value
Steve Calde from Cooper Consulting talks about the role of the visual designer in the process of creating interfaces. And encourages designers not to consider them as ‚Äúprototype painters‚Äù.

Creating landing pages: how to increase conversion with their help, and how a good landing page is different from a bad one. Part 1


Landing page is quite an important part of a marketing company, with its help a potential client will learn about your product. Therefore, we decided to share the article by Peep Laja about why the selling pages are critical, and what components need to work to create an effective landing page. Below is the first part of the article, in which the author reflects on the reasons for creating failed landing pages and gives their examples. The article is written in the first person. For comparison, we also picked up a couple of examples of high-quality landing pages.

Was there a landing page?

You've come across long selling pages? Yes, everything is detailed on them, but in most cases their efficiency leaves much to be desired: low quality of work makes these pages less attractive for the target audience.

Long selling pages are used in internet marketing because sometimes they really work.

The goal of your website is to sell products or services. If you sell only one product, you do not need to have more than one page. Your website informs consumers about your product (or service), its advantages and use options. This helps them make a purchasing decision, in case your product is able to quickly solve their problem. If for this you have to go several times from one page to another, it only hinders.

Someone calls the selling pages "mini-sites", someone - "business cards", stuck the name "landing pages". The essence is the same.

Text does not happen much?

The amount of text required on the sales pages depends on the complexity and cost of the product. The more complex / expensive your product is, the more you need to explain, show and influence a potential client.

If you sell a box of matches for 25 cents, I, as a buyer, do not need a lot of text. This product is simple and cheap.

If you sell something worth, say, $ 300, then your buyer does not need to think long, but it requires a certain amount of information to explain this purchase to someone (to himself, his wife, boss, etc.).

Buyers, they are readers

Are you worried that your text is too long? Not worth it. If there are those who agree to buy your product, having read only about 20% of the information you provide, all the better, they simply scroll the page to the end in search of the ‚Äúbuy‚Äù button. No problem.

But if one of the potential buyers reads the entire text, and after that he still has questions, then you have a problem. This is an excellent argument in favor of long landings.

Of course, most people will not read the text completely, and this is completely normal. This is not a novel. And those who are still fully acquainted with the information on the selling page - this is your potential customers.

But after all, the long selling pages are so banal and completely unreliable!

It is for this reason that I said that their effectiveness leaves much to be desired. I agree - most of the selling pages are really ridiculously monotonous and are aimed at fooling potential customers. Therefore, so many people have a negative attitude towards them.

Social networks: evolution, sales and adoption

Communication of people at a distance transformed over time. Messengers with oral messages, messengers with dispatches, optical telegraphs on towers, information transmission systems using light signals, telegraphs, telephones, mobile communications, the Internet is a continuous process necessary to satisfy the natural need of a social being in communication.

Social networks have become the most important tools at the moment, allowing people to talk with each other, share thoughts, images, videos, broadcasts, and advertisers - to make narrowly targeted advertising on the target groups they need.

Today we will talk about how social networks evolved during their history from 1995 to today, why and how companies benefit from using social networks, and how Vkontakte and Instagram have grown into the lives of ordinary people.



The emergence of social networks was a matter of time. It was predicted in the XIX century, when scientists were only trying to invent a working telegraph. Vladimir Odoyevsky in the novel ‚Äú4338 year‚Äù described something between Live Journal and Instagram. In his world of the future between the houses is correspondence with the help of newspapers. The senior butler, writing down the text according to the words of the house hosts, photographs it on the camera obscura and publishes it in the necessary number of copies. Usually they report home news, different thoughts, they invite you to dinner. For faster communication, for emergency cases, there are magnetic telegraphs between the houses.

The author wrote his futuristic work in 1835, when a Russian diplomat and historian Paul Schilling had already assembled an electromagnetic telegraph in St. Petersburg, but Morse had not yet patented his version of the telegraph and had not received a subsidy for the construction of the first line.

The fantasy of man is limitless. Thomas Edison in 1878 described the "Telephyscope", capable of transmitting images and sound, so that relatives far from each other could communicate. Like Vkontakte or Periscope. But even if fantasy strides forward, it works within the limits of available technologies - they thought then with telegraph, photographs and handwritten texts.

In the 1920s, the idea was realized in the Bell telephone laboratory: then the research department tested a prototype that transmitted audio and video from Washington to AT & T's New York office.

On October 29, 1969, for the first time, a connection was established between the computers of universities in Los Angeles and Stanford. In 1971, they developed a program for sending emails between computers. The ARPANET network in 1973 became international - it was connected to a transatlantic telephone cable. It allowed the exchange of information to scientific organizations in Europe and the United States. In 1984, scientists created the NSFNet network, which united many university networks.

The emergence of the Internet has opened up new opportunities for quick communication. First for the military, then for the scientists, and after - for the general public. In 1989, Tim Berners-Lee from CERN proposed the concept of the World Wide Web, developed the HTTP protocol. In 1991, private homes were finally connected to the Internet, and not just educational institutions and military structures.

Since 1995, you can begin the history of social networks. The portal Classmates.com, which still exists, has become a pioneer. It allows you to find classmates, fellow soldiers, classmates from kindergarten, work colleagues. Over time, the developers launched versions of the site with the same design and functionality for other countries.

In 2008, the Classmates.com portal had fifty million subscribers. Of these, 3.8 million paid for membership.

Thanks to the great love of e-mail newsletters ( just like LinkedIn ), the portal has become the person involved in several trials. For example, in 2008, he paid out users $ 9.5 million.



In 2002, the previously mentioned LinkedIn appeared on the scene - a social network for business contacts. Here it was possible to find a colleague at work or in the field of activity, to tell about their working success. In essence, the profile is a finished resume. The network allows you to keep track of companies that periodically post vacancies.

Microsoft bought this social network in the summer of 2016 for $ 26.2 billion - donated a little less than a third of its own cash .

The price is due to the fact that the network is actively developing and making a profit. LinkedIn has over two million paid subscribers who can be contacted directly. Total community has 433 million members.



In 2003, the MySpace blogging platform appeared. This site was originally intended for young people who want to read, listen to music, share video content, post their photos and blog. In June 2012, attendance was 25 million people.

MySpace has been the most popular social network in the world for several years. But in 2008, that changed when Facebook recorded 129 million unique users, and MySpace recorded 114.6 million users.



Thefacebook site opened on February 4, 2004 and was available only to students at Harvard University. For twelve years, the social network has lost the article and reached 1.71 billion active visitors per month. Mark Zuckerberg became the youngest billionaire of the planet at 23 due to his invention.

Initially, the network was intended for students at Harvard University. In 2004, the audience was expanded to Stanford, Columbia University and Yale. In September 2006, the network became open to all users over the age of thirteen.

Facebook allows users to create their own page with photos and information, chat with friends, leave messages on the walls, upload videos and create groups, as well as company pages. Facebook as a tool to influence the audience is used by many large corporations and local organizations. Businessmen and politicians for reputational purposes actively use this social network.

In April 2016, Facebook Live was launched on the social network - that is, Periscope.

Social networks for marketing


From the very beginning of the history of social networks, there are two ways to monetize them: advertising and paid subscriptions. The second option did not prove to be from the best side, so today it is difficult to find a paid registration - moreover, I will not name any social network that would offer to pay for a personal account at this stage.

Targeted advertising is actively used. The largest brands, small and medium business - a huge number of companies have their own pages in social networks. It helps to communicate with the audience in their language and on those platforms that are convenient for people. And they create advertising messages for them - both for a huge number of users at the federal level, and local messages - announcements of opening a new store, discounts on goods and services in a certain city or even on the right street.

Around social networks there are a large number of opinions regarding audiences. Vkontakte - for schoolchildren, on Facebook - only professionals, Odnoklassniki - for senior citizens. These assumptions are justified, but only partly.

It is worth saying that the audiences of social networks overlap greatly - on average, there are 2.5 social networks diggers for each person. And that the audience is constantly growing up with the networks.

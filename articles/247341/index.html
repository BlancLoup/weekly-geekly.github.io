Sculpt Startup. Start

New 2015 year. Monday. It's time to start a new startup life from scratch. After reading a little more than all the articles on how to begin it one thing is clear - nothing is clear. Paul Graham (co-founder of Y Combinator), for example, believes that start-ups are illogical and everything should be done differently from what you want. And how to do? To do this, you can either get into a cool incubator or have a good mentor, or ... try to cut your project, while simultaneously taking online courses from the best universities in the world. From January 2015 begins (repeats) 2 excellent courses:
Technology Entrepreneurship , by Stanford University Online
Entrepreneurship 101: Who is your customer? by edx (MIT, Harvard)
Is it possible to blind a startup from scratch only with the help of online courses and interest?

And do we have enough online courses to do this?

Idea


If you ask Paul Graham again what the idea should be, it can be briefly described as:
  • you do something you need
  • you yourself can do it from scratch
  • only some are interested


I am sure that most people had many moments when they lacked a simple and clear service, an application on a smartphone or anything. I'm not talking about the robot management portal on Mars, rather about something like facemash - a primitive, but interesting web site, with a soul. Yes, even Mark Zuckerberg started with this, pierced it, gained experience and only then created a serious product. Probably, it makes no sense to start with a big and clever idea, we must try to make an interesting product for a specific target audience.
Regarding point 3 - now in the valley when creating startups they like to focus on hipsters - people who don‚Äôt like the mainstream (what everyone likes) and who are ready to experiment. If hipsters like your product, then there is a high probability that they will succeed :)
Unlike ten years ago, now more and more people think that an idea is worth nothing. There are also a lot of examples where the idea was turned upside down. Grupon was originally something like a kickstarter, Flickr - RPG, YouTube - a dating site, Twitter - a platform for podcasts ... This does not mean that all startups change the idea, but each approach changes. Over time, we will learn more about customers, opportunities, technologies, and this helps to find our niche. Therefore, to begin with what is written on your sticker near the monitor or somewhere in the ToDo list and to improve / change over time is a great idea.

Team


Almost all ‚Äústartup teachers‚Äù say that a team is very important. And each of us probably knows with whom exactly he wants to work. On the other hand, in order to try what a startup is, it is much less important. Many interesting projects began with one person. If you really want to do this in a team - take a friend or colleague and offer to just take courses on entrepreneurship together. Or even join the team during the course (create your team and invite someone from the course).

Motivation


For me personally, this item is very difficult. It is often said that if you do something that you really want and are important, then you will do it. But I think that you will do this for a maximum of a month (two, three), then you will get bored, as always. That is why socialization, control of progress, constant criticism / praise are needed. That is what will give us practical business courses.

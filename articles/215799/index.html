Correctional colonies - a new forge of cadres of IT-specialists?



In an interview with Izvestia, the head of the press service of the Federal Penitentiary Service of the Russian Federation for the Moscow Region, Regina Rzhevtseva, shared her plans to open four monthly courses for inmates of the Mozhaisk penal colony. These courses will include the professions of a programmer, photo editor and graphic designer. Experts believe that such measures will help improve the situation with the employment of prisoners after release. So, as places on these courses will be limited, their attendance will be a kind of encouragement for the inhabitants of the colony.

The FSIN of the region concluded a tripartite agreement with the Ministry of Education of the region, a charitable foundation that initiated this idea, Rzhevtseva said. - The overall goal of the new program is to train pupils of the colony in specialties that are now in demand in the market, so that after they are released, it will be easier for them to adapt to the new free life. Therefore, the FPS also closely cooperates with specialists of the Ministry of Labor and Employment, who regularly come to the colonies to talk about which specialties are most relevant. In accordance with their forecast, courses in various specialties will be prepared. Already developed a program for IT-direction. It is assumed that, by analogy, culinary courses and courses for stylists will be held.

In my opinion, the main problem may be the traditional reluctance of the employer to hire people who have previous convictions. The quality of the material that will be taught on such rapid courses is also in doubt.

Do I need manual processing of search results on the Internet?

Each of us, I think, is constantly googling. There are some tasks that look for information on which a cloud of time goes. From the last tasks I came across, you can, for example, note:
  • compiling a list of all online stores of Lithuanian and Estonian stores, including tracking of clone stores and extracting contacts
  • compiling a review of existing technologies of computer vision, mainly realtime - the identification of objects with a monocular camera, with a focus on the embedded system
  • overview of existing mod_php + mod_worker installations

In carrying out these tasks, I simply began to dream of a man who would fulfill them for me.

Background of the problem


I think everyone acknowledges that search engines are, in principle, a complete mess. Because of the ranking of the search on pr / tic / etc, in fact, professional, but not authoritative (read - not untwisted) resources hang out somewhere on the 5-6th pages of the issue. Pre-processing of the request (for example, realtime 3d object identification) helpfully slips links from realtime linux to blender3d. Tungsten simplifies the situation a little, but its base is small. Plus, due to the universal reprint of data, the first 50 links may generally point to the same resource.

Let's dream


Imagine that there is a service, domysearch.ru (it is Russian, I am able to speak somewhere in 6 languages, but it is more convenient to perceive information in Russian), in which I leave a request similar to this:
Find suppliers of GPS devices in Riga and Riga region, to buy a batch of 30 pieces. Find out contacts, discounts, speed of order execution, the possibility of post-warranty service. Make a brief overview of the proposed devices.

... I pay money for it, and in a day I get an answer, in the form of a pack of PDF, tablets and so on. I read this information in 5-10 minutes and make a decision.

Why not yourself?


Why not yourself, you say? Everything is simple - it is economic efficiency. My day of work is 70 euros. It took two days to compile a list of Lithuanian online stores, for a total of 150 euros for such work. I am ready to outsource it for 70-100 euros, as this will save me ~ 16 hours of work, and during this time I will do something more efficient and necessary.

Total


Now, in fact, what I wanted to ask habra people is - do you need specialists or services that, for some money, armed with modern means of Data-Mining, will look for information for you? After all, literally in a couple of months of work of such a service, the built associative base will be more complete and more detailed than the base of Google and other search engines. Is everyone satisfied with the loss of time on Google?

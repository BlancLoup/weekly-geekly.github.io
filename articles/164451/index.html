Purpose of concept art

The history of that concept art, which is known to us now through a variety of digital portals, as usual, has its roots in the deep past. Far beyond the 30s of the last century, when the term concept art was rumored to be first voiced by Walt Disney Feature Animation. Straight through the days of the first artists. Not those who painted on the rocks of mammoths, but those who turned the art into a separate profession.

Artists at all times made sketches anticipating writing paintings, and, of course, without these sketches it was impossible to grow, because daily practice and study is what makes growth possible, and what makes possible the subsequent transition from quantity to quality to meet new lessons and the unknown.

Thus, under a beautiful term, as it often happens, the old as a world of truth is hidden. Similarly, around us there are a variety of entities, covered with catchy terms, being, moreover, ordinary, ancient, for a thousand years like open bicycles.

We will not try to cover what cannot be covered in this article and will pay attention only to concept art, which can be associated with games and movies, in other words, concept art of the entertainment industry.

The main purpose of concept art is the creation in the shortest possible time of an outline or model of an object, which can then be put into production. With minimal time loss, you can choose the best concept, thereby saving time and money. Because if the concept is approved, the ‚Äúflywheels of machines‚Äù of other specialists are launched, and in the case of the gaming industry, models, locations and other game entities are created based on the concepts.

In the case of the film industry, mockups or prototypes of real objects, such as the Titanic model or the ghost ship of Davy Jones, used in the movie Pirates of the Caribbean are starting to be developed. An error at the level of the concept of incorrectly approved and put into production can result in multimillion-dollar losses. In addition, a second equally valuable resource, time, will be lost.

Besides the fact that concept art saves time and allows you to cut off the unnecessary at the earliest stages, it is a powerful tool for creating the world. Without it, people creating this world will not get a holistic view of what is being created. Because the concept in the head, and the concept on paper - not one, and too. Yes, we see pictures in our heads every day, we present something, but our head thinks in images, but we need specifics. And when this specifics is transferred by us to the canvas, we suddenly realize that not everything is so perfect, and that what was good in words, is not at all impressive in the form of a finished concept.

The development of concept art is an integral part of developing something, from building a house to launching the development of a new car.

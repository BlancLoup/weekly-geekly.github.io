Restoring access to Google Analytics in 4 easy steps.

It turned out that a script was installed on one of the sites we developed for collecting statistics from Google Analytics . However, who registered it and who owns the account, we did not know.

A web analyst advised me to create a new tracking ID, but I didn‚Äôt like this approach - losing all the statistics from the day the product was launched seemed unattractive. I chose a different path.

If you do not know which account the Google Analytics code on your site belongs to, if any of the developers do not give access to the analytics or you have lost access to the mailbox that the analytics is configured to, I ask for the cat.

Quick ways but not working


You may be more lucky, but Google forums, groups, as well as writing an email using the support form did nothing. True, it says that you can wait for about a week. After several days of silence, I decided that this approach does not work, so I recommend that you immediately proceed to the method described below.

Proven and working approach


First, you need a google account. As I understand it, it does not matter whether it belongs to your company or to you personally. If you do not have one for some reason, ask a friend or colleague to follow the steps below for you.

1. Start


Sign in to any of the services, such as Gmail.
Visit https://support.google.com/adwords/answer/8206

Perhaps you will want to write in the chat or use email. If you want your problem solved quickly, it is better not to use them. Click on the link, where the arrow in the figure, to get phone support.



2. Country selection


On the next page, select your country, and also write down the 10-digit customer ID. This code, as I understand it, is yours personally and does not change between sessions. But since you may not be able to reach you from the first time, it is better to keep it somewhere close at hand, and not in a browser, which at the most inopportune moment may hang or fall off.



3. Dialing


Before you call, you should make a clarification: despite the fact that you are applying on a question related to Google Analytics, you call AdWords support. Therefore, if you try to refuse support, insist that the AdWords team supports Analytics. I was told at the beginning that my problem was not related to their competence.

As the previous figure shows, support works during the specified hours on weekdays, so wait for the specified time and call.

After you have called, called your customer ID and underwent other security procedures, you will be asked which sites you need to regain access to. ‚ÄúTracking ID‚Äù was not asked, but you can easily find it in the pages if the code is placed correctly.

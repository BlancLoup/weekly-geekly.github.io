From Augmented Reality to Kotlin: How Mobius 2018 Piter Passed

How to understand that the conference is made perfectly? Of course, to participate in its organization!



The Mobius conference is held in St. Petersburg not the first year, and we have a tradition - every time to write a brief report on Habr about what happened. Usually such reports are written by participants and speakers, but this time I will write it - as a leading interviewer.

Under the cut there will be a brief chronicle of events with comments and photos. Careful traffic: photos in relatively good quality.

You can come to Mobius live or watch a broadcast on YouTube. Those who came "live" may not even guess that there is one more show for viewers of the broadcast: in the intervals between the reports, we interview speakers, sponsors, the Program Committee and so on.

From nine in the morning until the very close we live in the following rhythm:

  • We negotiate with the speakers about the interview;
  • We look at half of the current report (how long is enough);
  • We conduct an interview;
  • Everything is repeated anew (by the number of slots in the program plus lunch).

Despite the fact that we also watch the reports - we are not typical participants. One can see not so much a conference as its inside out. You can watch as a huge conference machine unfolds. Works exactly like a clock. From the participants and speakers, you can learn things that no one usually says out loud.

The reports that I looked at were chosen like this:

  • Architectural and educational reports (for example, on the Linux kernel) are more important than specialized ones;
  • Android takes precedence over iOS (because in Android I understand a little, but in iOS, alas);
  • Reports on topics requiring knowledge of the materiel (for example, A / B testing) require multiple revisions, so they have the lowest priority - they will need to be reviewed in the record, time after time, until you understand.

It is clear that this separation is purely subjective. But it explains why further about some reports are written in detail, and some are not mentioned at all.

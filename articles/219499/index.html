Vidonn X5 - bracelet from heaven to monitor physical activity

The boom in the wearable electronics market is generating more and more devices. Today in our review an inexpensive fitness bracelet from the Chinese company Vidonn. At a price of $ 40, the bracelet can count the steps, the distance traveled, and the number of calories burned, and is also equipped with a small OLED display to indicate time and results.

Let's look at what a new Chinese company is capable of and what its creation is.

The bracelet is delivered in a transparent acrylic box, in which besides the device itself there is only a lonely and very crookedly written instruction.

In general, an English translation of everything from instruction to application has been made quite obscenely. Of course you can figure it out, but it cuts the eye.

Spartan package and even the charger is not put - how so? But more about that later.

‚ñ∫ Design and usability

The bracelet looks quite simple. The case is painted black, but the strap is sold in 3 colors (black, green and pink).

On the hand, the Vidonn X5 is attached with a fairly simple-looking latch. The length of the strap in a stretched, as far as possible, position is ~ 22 centimeters, and due to a sufficiently large range of diameter adjustments, the bracelet will suit both a child and an adult. The weight of the gadget is only 20 grams.

Immediately an insulting jamb pops up - with a metal fastener, which is always on the table or on an aluminum laptop (in my case), the paint gradually peels off.

And now the most interesting thing: we deal with two latches on the back of the bracelet.

‚ÄúVoila!‚Äù - and detach the body of the bracelet from the strap. As for me - an excellent implementation. No external charging or adapters, synchronization and recharging via USB-connector.

The strap is made of ordinary rubber, but no problems with the skin over the entire period of operation have arisen. The case itself has protection against dust and moisture (according to the manufacturer - IP67), but still I do not recommend immersing it in water for a long time. ‚ñ∫ Functional and autonomy

Looking ahead, I will say that the bracelet's functionality is quite modest. Alternately pressing a single button causes a change of screens, of which there are three: the main (clock, charge indicator and alarm), counting the steps taken and the number of calories burned.

Holding the button for 3 seconds will transfer the bracelet to the ‚ÄúTime‚Äù mode or sleep tracking, which is essentially the same thing, since the data for a certain period of time is taken into account. In this mode, the stopwatch is activated, the number of steps taken, calories burned and the distance covered during this period. Switching to the standard mode is performed by holding the button again or by triggering the built-in alarm clock.

Sliptreker, unfortunately, no. The alarm just works at the specified time. One of the reasons for the purchase of this bracelet was an alarm clock, but in this regard, the acquisition did not justify itself, because I could not wake up from the vibrating signal.

A tricolor LED, which is located under the button, signals your degree of activity and, as it were, hints at the need to get up and do a warm-up, for example. By default, the indicator is always active, but you can turn it off by using two short key presses. Each color corresponds to a certain degree of activity, for example, blue means minimal activity (less than 60 steps per minute), green means average activity (60-120 steps per minute), and red signals intense load (more than 120 steps per minute).

Autonomy is declared at the level of four or more days, which corresponds to the truth, if you disable the permanent synchronization with the smartphone (otherwise it is 2-3 days). By the way, such a form factor is also convenient because the bracelet can be quickly recharged from a portable charger, and it takes about 10 minutes from the strength, despite the ‚Äúspace‚Äù 2 hours stated by the manufacturer.

‚ñ∫ Software

The application for working and synchronizing with Vidonn X5 is available for iOS (version 6.0 and higher), Android (4.3 and higher, Bluetooth 4.0) and Windows (XP, 7, 8). In the "Trend" section you can see more detailed statistics of activity. By the way, the graphics are based on the highly extensive and popular JavaScript library Highcharts.

For the task (to pass, for example, 10,000 steps) you are given bonus points. True, you can compete only with friends in the Chinese social. networks. :)

The program for synchronization with PC also does not shine with functionality or color. Let me remind you that the program works only on Windows (XP, 7, 8). First, connect the Vidonn X5 to the USB of your computer, then launch the application and enter the login information into your previously created account. After that, the program will try to detect the gadget and, if successful, will prompt you to synchronize the data.

Clicking on the "Configuration" in the lower left corner, go to the settings menu and enter personal data that will be used to calculate the steps you have taken, the distance and the number of calories burned. Immediately by clicking on ‚ÄúAlarm Clock‚Äù, we switch to the alarm set mode.

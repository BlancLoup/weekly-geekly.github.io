Micro Four Thirds


One of the main features of the camera is that it came out in compliance with Micro 4: 3 format standards. Four to three is not the size of the matrix, but the aspect ratio. The standard inherited from the older brother one of the strongest advantages: full compatibility of cameras, lenses and other accessories from different manufacturers.


This means that you have at your disposal a rich fleet of compatible optics: more than 30 zoom lenses from Olympus and Panasonic, as well as more than 50 fixed-focus glasses from Olympus, Panasonic, Sigma, Tokina, Kowa, Mitakon, SLR Magic, Voigtl√§nder, Samyang and Schneider Kreuznach. One of the best matrices on the market


The heart of a mirrorless camera is a BSI-sensor Sony Eximor IMX269 with 20 effective megapixels. With this resolution and compliance with Micro FourThirds standards, simple arithmetic calculations give us a pixel size of 3.3 microns. Just for comparison: the pixel size of an average mobile sensor is around 1.1-1.2 micrometers, and for amateur Canon-70D cameras it is about 4 microns. The sensor has excellent dynamic range and high photosensitivity, which allows you to work with the most accurate, contrasting autofocus in conditions up to -4 EV.



The maximum resolution of images is 5184 x 3888 pixels, work with sRGB and AdobeRGB extended color space is supported, the ISO range is from 100 to 25,600 units.

Open RAW standard and 4k video recording


IMX269 belongs to the latest generation of sensors and supports saving data in Adobe RAW DNG format. The advantages of this particular source data format include good documentation and openness of the standard itself: you do not need a special converter or an additional package of drivers or plug-ins for image processing. DNG is supported by mobile devices, Windows, Linux and macOS-based computers and provides plenty of room for creativity in the post-processing of images.



A nice bonus to the excellent quality of photos will be recording video in 4k @ 30 fps format.

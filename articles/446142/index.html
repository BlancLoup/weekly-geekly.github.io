Flat Earth: Experiments and Evidence

Already in the primary grades of public schools, they write in the textbooks of natural history that our Earth is flat, and children grow without thinking about what the government is investing in their heads. But a thinking person can get to the truth, sometimes studying seemingly unrelated facts or even making his own experiments.



Dark things


Last summer I had the opportunity to see a lunar eclipse. You all have seen this picture from the textbook of natural history:



I was ready to enjoy the spectacle, but the reality was completely different, the emerging shadow was curved, as if the Earth was a ball!



This discovery deprived me of peace - have we really been deceived all this time, and the Earth is really a sphere? It was necessary to investigate this question further. Where and how can we notice the shadow of the Earth? The most interesting is the time of sunset and sunrise. From textbooks, we know that the sun shines on a flat earth like a lantern with a reflector, directionally. Otherwise, the whole disk would be at the same time day or night, and now, with the spread of communication tools, you can call or write to a friend several thousand kilometers away, and find out what the Sun has already gone down or, on the contrary, has risen from him.



This means that the border of day and night has a slope, and on a flat Earth, tall objects in the opposite direction from the Sun after sunset will be in the shade.



And on the spherical Earth, on the contrary, skyscrapers or mountains will be illuminated by the Sun after sunset or before dawn.



And I not only personally observed the illuminated skyscrapers immediately after sunset, but, judging from the photographs of other people, this is ubiquitous. It turns out that the earth is everywhere a ball!

Observations from different points on the east / west line also provide interesting results. It is easy to establish that the Sun rises over different cities at different times. Only this should be done in the region of the spring or autumn equinox, so that the border of day and night does not deviate much from the north-south direction. I got 73 minutes for the Ufa-Moscow base. The distance between the cities in the region of 1100 km is direct (on the road in the region of 1300 km, but it is not perfectly straight). Thus, we find that the terminator line (the border of day and night) passes approximately 15 km per minute. Multiplying by 60 minutes in an hour and 24 hours in days, we get 21,700 km - the circumference of the Earth at a latitude of 55 ¬∞.

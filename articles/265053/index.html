Old market or restored servers conquered the USA and Europe

Hi, Habr! After our first article on refurbished servers, we received a huge feedback, a lot of interested people and a lot of questions. What does it mean? At a minimum, the topic is pretty hot. That's why we decided to write another post, what the market of recovered servers represents today, and how much money such equipment saves. So let's go!


One of the main trends in Europe


Refurbished servers are one of the fastest growing segments of the western IT market. Moreover, both small companies and large companies, whose names are widely known, buy them. Why?
We will not go deep into this topic, since we talked a lot about it in the last article. We will cite only three facts that make the restored technique so popular:
  • Low price - the restored server is on average 20-70% cheaper than a new one .;
  • High reliability - when restoring everything is tested almost under extreme conditions, and the disputed elements in the system are replaced with new ones;
  • Warranty - on recovered servers, as a rule, there may be a much longer warranty period. For example, we can guarantee up to five years. Even on entry-level servers there is a 3-year warranty, which vendors cannot boast about ..

Having studied the experience of Europe and the USA, we went further - we decide warranty cases on the territory of the customer. This is the whole territory of Russia, Kazakhstan and the Republic of Belarus. In the near future, our guarantee will be valid in Kyrgyzstan and Armenia. At the moment we are looking for partners in these countries.

Thanks to the crisis


Despite the rather rather small age of the refurbished market (it dates back to the mid-1980s), a real jump on it occurred in 2008‚Äì2009. Familiar dates? That's right - this is a global economic crisis, which dealt a significant blow to the market in many countries and at the same time gave the necessary impetus to the growth of the secondary market, in which server equipment took one of the leading positions.

In difficult times, many companies began to look for ways to save money. And here the restored equipment loudly announced itself, and the servers are no exception. After all, they are much cheaper than new ones, while also being reliable. Then why overpay if there is no difference?

Billion savings


Not only Americans have known the charm of the restored servers and other equipment. Conservative Britons are on the front line here! And here is just one small, but curious fact.

A few years ago, the Chancellor of the Treasury of Great Britain, George Osborne, faced the task of reducing government spending. And one of the areas where he found a huge resource for savings is government spending on IT. On average, they are about 18 billion pounds per year.

In the course of cost analysis, experimentation and consultation, it was found that these costs can be easily reduced by about 8 billion! And absolutely without prejudice to national security and data integrity. How?
In most cases, instead of new servers and powerful computing complexes, it was decided to start purchasing refurbished equipment for state institutions.

Petitions demanding to ban the game "Company of Heroes-2" in the CIS, scored about 15 thousand signatures

On June 25, 2013, a long-awaited novelty appeared on the world and Russian markets - the computer game ‚ÄúCompany of Heroes 2‚Äù from the Canadian studio Relic (now purchased by SEGA). At that time, no one imagined what kind of excitement the game would cause and that for some reason will happen. A month after its release on the network, an unveiling video review from BadComedian first appeared, and one after another, two petitions from outraged users appeared demanding to ban this product on the territory of the Russian Federation:
  • petition to the leadership of 1C-Softklab and 1C-Interest
  • petition to the leadership of Valve (who owns Steam)

Each of these appeals has already been signed by more than 14 thousand people (a petition to 1C is 18.5 thousand), and their initiators do not intend to stop there. The reason for such cardinal decisions was the content of the game, with which many players completely disagree.

If one reads all the evaluations of the game, it will become obvious that its authors took the alternative version of the historical events of the Great Patriotic War as the basis for the scenario. Thus, according to the authors of the petitions, the creators of the Company of Heroes completely distorted the truth about the Soviet soldiers, presenting them as traitors, cowards and sadists, completely deprived of not only heroic, but also human traits. Particularly alarming is the fact that the main audience of the game is young people and even children, who are unlikely to read historical literature and may well take the images depicted as pure truth. One can imagine the whole scale of the threat to the healthy upbringing of the younger generation by reading one of the more vivid reviews of the game.

one of the bright reviews of the game
Although the name of the game translates as "Company of Heroes", do not mislead yourself with this deceptive title. For the Soviet soldiers and commanders here are the least similar to heroes in the world: the first dream to get out of the front line and only the ubiquitous detachments force this crowd of cowards and deserters to defend their own land, the second - shoot their own and make the greatest possible efforts to kill more of them subordinates. In the whole campaign there is hardly a single indiscriminate action of the Soviet military. Soviet soldiers set fire to homes with their own innocent residents. Soviet soldiers chorus shoot unarmed prisoners. The Soviet commanders are waiting for the special forces until their own soldiers reach the bridge, so that they can be blown up right there. The Soviet military basemen betray and shoot in the back to those who have just helped them. Such moments would be absolutely in the spirit of Company of Heroes 2, which proclaims the maximum of hardcore: none that heroic, but even human, traits of Soviet untermensum should not be shown!

Only the leader, Lev Abramovich Isakovich, is the light at the end of the tunnel, where everyone else, except for him, is cowards, traitors or sadists, the only honest, unafraid, sits in Gulag (like any honest person) and tells us a part of the historical truth mentioned above ...

I take into account the daring reaction of the creators of the game, the resentment of Russian users becomes only sharper. In addition to collecting signatures to the petitions, activists also urge the Russian authorities to join the process. Since the laws on blocking sites with unlicensed films are being promoted in the country, then one should still sort out the game of trampling the feelings of veterans. Outraged users of Runet have already lowered the rating of the game on the metacritic to 1.6 (out of 10), and put a huge amount of dislikes to the official game trailer on youtube (there are already more dislikes than likes).

So far, as reported on the pages of the petitions, the leadership of the companies "1C" and "Valve" did not respond to the petition. Apparently the key role is played by the amount of expected revenue from the sale of the game. "Company of Heroes-2" became the leader in sales of the boxed version of the game on the 1C-Interest network in the CIS in the first weeks after the start of sales.

Therefore, the confrontation continues. By creating and signing petitions, caring citizens expect to at least have an educational impact on the 1C structure. Since, even if the game is not banned, from now on the company will more carefully approach the choice of games for localization for the CIS market.

The signatories of the petitions also hope that the game development studios from around the world will learn this lesson and will more seriously approach the choice of the role of Russian-speaking characters in their games and historical authenticity, since they do not want to lose sales revenue in the CIS countries.

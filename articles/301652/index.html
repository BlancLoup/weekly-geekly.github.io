Cisco learning old school

Hello! Today's post is dedicated to my unpublished training manual. The fact is that once upon a time I was a teacher of network technologies, I was very ambitious, I wanted to tell everyone, and most importantly, to teach everyone the skill of a network engineer. It was at that time that I decided to write a training manual and publish it. Now I found the time and decided to publish my training manual in the form of a site. Yes, I know that now there are a lot of sites that offer to teach how to work with Cisco, and, undoubtedly, they deserve attention and respect. But based on their experience and learning experience, the best way to learn Cisco is practice ! As I can judge by myself and by my former students, it is always too lazy to practice. Therefore, my training manual was aimed at presenting material with already prepared labs, the student needs only to open the lab and begin the practice according to the plan in the training manual.



Based on your experience, the best way to begin to understand networking is to learn how Cisco hardware works. And Cisco is working on standards like the rest of the network equipment. So having studied Cisco, it was easier for me to understand the equipment of other manufacturers. Why exactly Cisco? I will name only three main reasons (this is IMHO):
  1. full of documentation, and most importantly - sane;

I turn to the description of how to use my training manual (website). Further chapters will be listed, their goal is to consistently provide material:

  1. The basis of the basics . If you do not know the principles of the router and / or switch, then you have to be patient and start with the "Fundamentals of the basics".

Barcelona results: the brightest trends with the MWC 2017

The revolution in smartphones, super-capacious batteries, the emerging nostalgia for old tubes and driving from a smartphone. Here are some of the main trends noted at the International Mobile Technology Congress, held in Barcelona.



Crave for details? They are under the cut!

The International Mobile Congress, held in Barcelona in Spain, is an extremely curious event, where you can see not only new smartphones from almost all known and not so famous brands, but also get acquainted with technologies that make the world a better and more interesting. However, smartphones are in the first place in terms of interest. And this is understandable - today, the smartphone is the most advanced and relevant personal device, which brings together communication and entertainment functions. Therefore, we begin with them.



The main trend of this year, oddly enough, is not increased at times processor performance or memory capacity. Unfortunately, today there is no sharp jump. But a very substantial breakthrough was observed in the cameras for smartphones. And there is even something truly revolutionary. In addition to cameras, the developers did not ignore the problem of nutrition. And, interestingly, they began to look more and more actively not only into the future, but also into the past. But first things first.

Cameras in smartphones


Almost all leading manufacturers showed models with cameras created in collaboration with a well-known photo brand. For example, the partnership of Huawei and Leica presented the P10 smartphone with a dual camera and a two-time zoom of the main camera to the world.



Such a camera can easily compete even with mirrorless system cameras, the demand for which, by the way, has noticeably decreased. And all because of the improvement in the quality of shooting smartphones, which even experts from DxOMark began to award top marks.
Little main camera? Huawei also has a Leica camera built in front, so selfies lovers can boast pictures that any glamorous fashionista with Apple's smartphone would envy.
Not far behind Huawei and Oppo. And the reason for that is the fivefold optical zoom! The company presented the camera Oppo 5x Dual Camera Zoom. True, this is a prototype, which is not known when it appears in a real smartphone. But this does not detract from its merits and causes an increased interest not only among ordinary users, but also third-party producers.



To begin with, the Oppo 5x Dual Camera Zoom is a truly revolutionary product that can revolutionize the look at cameras in a smartphone. The developers have managed to create a "periscope" lens, in which the lenses and the sensor are located in a horizontal plane, and the image of them from the lens is transmitted using a special mirror. The thickness of this design is only 5.7 mm, which allows you to place it in most modern smartphones. In this case, the camera has a real optical 5x zoom! Thus, two questions were decided at once: how to fit the zoom lens into a thin smartphone without parts protruding beyond the dimensions and freeing up space for an optical stabilization mechanism? This is also a dual camera. Next to the usual lens is a wide-angle, with which you can shoot interesting panoramas.

The future owners of the LG K10 can take selfies, in which their subscribers in social networks will see not only the faces in the whole picture, but also the background of what these faces were taken.

Full tank


Another trend MWC-2017 - the struggle for autonomy. Yes, so far the vast majority of smartphones can work on one charge of not more than one light day. Some fall short of the day, but it is still very small. Unfortunately, here too, manufacturers are hampered by the capabilities of modern batteries and the laws of physics. Well, it is impossible to make a tiny battery that will work for at least two or three days. Yes, and we must admit that with the current power consumption of the element base of smartphones, the use of lithium-ion batteries is a dead end. What to do? Wait for the developers of new miniature low-cost batteries? Yes.


Photo: fonearena.com

But in order not to be bored while waiting for these new products, some companies are ready for experiments. For example, the record for battery capacity on the MWC-2017 is the Oukitel K10000 Pro, which has a battery of 10,100 mAh. If we consider that on board the device is not the most voracious processor, then you can believe that such a smartphone will easily live without recharging for three days of active use. It is clear that there is no question of any record dimensions here, and the thickness of the smartphone is already 13.8 mm. But the emphasis is on the duration of the battery life. And the Oukitel K10000 Pro is equipped with a quick charge function, with which the smartphone can be charged in just two hours. What is not a compromise, but rather an effective option?



But what if you want a thinner smartphone and a longer battery? Unfortunately, so far. But manufacturers are looking for workarounds, which is Meizu's Super mCharge super-fast charging technology. But here, formally, no magic. The technology is based on increasing the charging current, which allows filling a smartphone battery up to 80% in 20 minutes. Of course there are fears that this will adversely affect the durability of the battery. But if the owner is important that his gadget was charged in the shortest possible time, then compromises are unnecessary. In addition, Meizu said that empirically the company's engineers were convinced that the regular use of their technology reduces the battery life for some very short time. Well, let's see.

Auto and smartphones


This is another trend of Barcelona. One way or another, but almost all automakers are working on creating models with autopilot functions.

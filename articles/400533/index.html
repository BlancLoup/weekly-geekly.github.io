The main paradox of consciousness. Why does not it make sense to copy the brain or personality



Attempts to reproduce the animation instrument by means of an interesting paradox, giving a new look at life and death. Different from our usual ideas. Everything can be much more interesting. Let's look at the basic logical theses. To begin with, we will decompose the currently widespread concept of consciousness into its constituent parts - condition (brain), content (personality), and functional (perception). Perhaps the directions of transhumanism and technological immortality are wrong.

Thesis first. Brain


If you replace all the atoms or molecules of our brain, nothing will happen. Since they change throughout life as a result of metabolism. We depend on the structure of neural networks, and not on the matter, their components.

Replacing all molecules is equivalent to making new copies. The laws of nature that support the informational component of the brain should work equally in identical copies. The parade of Theseus Ship in this case does not make sense. Each copy will have a clone of your consciousness, but with your own fate. The paradox of Parphit's teleportation , describing the teleportation and the destruction of the original, suggests that the original will still experience the emotions of dying, and its clone will still be a different creature. True, it is not entirely clear how they will know which of them is the original sample, and who is the copy. If the original and copies do not know who they are, they will not be able to determine who among them is. If they are not moved in space, but left in the same room, they will wake up outside the teleportation module, in the neighboring hospital beds, they will get confused. And if there are several copies? And if not to multiply, and reduce copies? Take several identical copies merged into one, the number of effects of perception and personalities also reduced to one. And the perception in it will not be able to tell who it was from the copies that did not, and who remained.

Thesis of the second. Personality


Our self image often looks like a self image. But theoretically you can give a neurosurgeon and erase memory, change feelings, habits, settings. Replant other parts of the body. What then will remain about you? Finally, we change over time, considering ourselves to be ourselves. From what moment to make a cast?

We are not mind, not feelings, not memory and not body. We are what will perceive the changes of this neurosurgeon after anesthesia. We are a function of perception. Pure tabula race , possibility of perception. A person consists of information of memory. We can replace memory during sleep, and we will become another person. The third thesis. Perception


If you are disassembled into atoms, carefully transfer them and from them in the same order, assemble again. Will the assembled version be you? To assume that your perception has been uninterrupted in your body from the moment of birth is a delusion. Perception is a function, an effect, existing only in the present tense. When you fall asleep, it disappears. And every morning it is new. Every day, every moment new electrons produce a new effect. Exactly the same, but not the same. Consciousness and personality will be the same, but not the same. If you are viewed as a function of perception, and not of a body or a person, then there is no connection between you yesterday and you tomorrow. This connection provides only the format of the memory.

Let's complicate the logical experiment. If you are disassembled into atoms and created from other atoms - will this copy be you? Yes. And if from half of your atoms to create a copy? And if several identical copies are teleported to one body, which of the consciousnesses of which copy will disappear? Parfit paradox here does not matter. Waking up in the original and copies will be equivalent. Since the perception was absent during the sleep of the original, and also a new one appeared in the copy.

And if at the time of assembly to change the personality, memory and body? At 5%? 50% off? 100%? Gather another body altogether. Will there be a consciousness there? If you do not violate the principles necessary for the occurrence, it will arise. Will it be you? It depends on what is considered under the concept of YOU. You will be another person in another body. What is left of you?

Perception is not tied to personality. He does not care where to occur. It would be a suitable condition. There is no perception tag that reports continuity of personality. With the same success, you (as a perception or information complex) can arise the next time in any condition and body. What perceives - it exists. It is perception that is the principle of animation. Without perception, body and personality do not make sense.

Neural connections of the current brain will inform the new perception of who it is and how much it lives in this body. The peculiarity is that the memory is tied to the body in the form of its neural connections, and disappears with its destruction. Perception cannot have any other experience.

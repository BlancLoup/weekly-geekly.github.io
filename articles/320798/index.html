The first Russian motherboard of the mass segment

As we have already written , the degree of localization of domestic server products of the mass segment is more pronounced in the provision of various types of services, rather than in the production of components. Components for servers are traditionally made in China, to which brand they would not belong. One Russian company had an attempt to create a fully domestic platform , but the product turned out to be a niche, as the platform had redundant characteristics for the classic use of servers.

But here, at the beginning of the year, we received a sample of a server 2-processor motherboard designed and manufactured in Russia. This model aroused interest primarily due to the fact that the characteristics and cost are fully suitable for mass use. This article is dedicated to the review and testing of the first Russian motherboard Rikor R-BD-E5R-V4-16.EA.

Specifications


  • CPU support: Intel Xeon E5-2600 V3 / V4 series (135W maximum)
  • MB chipset: Intel Wellsburg C612PCH
  • Memory slots: 16 (each CPU can support 4 x DDR4 channel x 2 DDR4 DIMM)
  • Memory support: Maximum 1024GB DDR4 2133/1866/1600/1333 RDIMM / LRDIMM / NVDIMM
  • PCI-E slots: 6
  • PCH SATA ports: 10 x SATA3.0
  • HDD RAID feature: RAID 0, 1, 5, 10
  • Graphics: Aspeed AST2400
  • IPMI: Support for Intelligent Platform Management Interface v.2.0 with virtual media over
    LAN and KVM over LAN support ASPEED AST2400 BMC
  • Network: 5 x 1Gb LAN (1 x 1Gb LAN is BMC dedicated management port)
  • USB: 5 x USB 3.0 (1 x internal) + 4 x USB 2.0 Ôºàinternal)
  • DOM: Support SATA DOM power connector
  • Input / output interface: 1 x COM port, 1 x VGA port, 1 x dedicated IPMI port, 4 x 1000Mbps port, 4 x USB2.0 + 4 x USB3.0
  • Fan power supply socket: 8 x 4 pin smart FAN
  • Power supply interface: 1 x 24pin SSI power interface power supply interface + 2 x 8pin SSI 12Vpower supply interface

Conclusion


The system based on Rikor R-BD-E5R-V4-16.EA, despite the lag in benchmarks, showed high stability and reliability with monthly load tests of all subsystems. The IPMI interface of the motherboard is classic from Intel, such can be seen in Asus motherboards, for example. BIOS for the fall is planned domestic. Development and production of the motherboard (of course, with the exception of the components), I repeat, we have it in Russia.

In addition to the motherboard, the 2U chassis completely made in Russia came to us for tests. Everything (except BP), up to the hot-swap baskets, is produced in our country. Backplanes too. Since The corpus was a very, very exemplary model, many complaints appeared on it, but the shortcomings were already corrected at the time of this writing.

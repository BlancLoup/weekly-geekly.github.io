FAQ on intercity passenger bus flights

- I arrived at the stop 2 minutes before the scheduled departure and waved to the departed bus. What to do?

If you are at the bus station or bus station, then get a note about the fact that the bus left earlier, and then go to the carrier for reimbursement. Usually this is quickly and simply solved by a call directly or by contacting the duty officer of the carrier. There are cases when the station does not put a mark, then you need to call where the ticket was purchased. If you are just standing at the bus stop in the field, you will need to contact the carrier later. Each bus is legally equipped with a tachograph, and the carrier will be able to easily install when the bus has left your stop.

- What is this tachograph on every bus?

Previously, it was something like a clockwork connected to a speedometer. The most modern tachographs are an independent battery, a storage device (usually an onboard flash), a GPS / Glonass receiver, an accelerometer, sensor inputs (fuel level, cabin temperature), an integration module for the CAN bus. Increasingly, they are equipped with GSM-modems with M2M-SIM cards for remote data acquisition by the carrier (the main log in real time if there is a network on the route or detailed unloading at the final destination). Tachographs are constantly being improved, as well as knowledge of drivers on how to deceive them. The task of the tachograph is to fix violations of the work schedule, to see when the driver drove to the dacha on a service bus or made a ‚Äúleft‚Äù flight, to know the exact fuel consumption.

- Are there any buses with lying places - is it possible to go lying?

We have a passenger and a driver on the traffic rules while driving while seated. Therefore there are no buses with lying places. More precisely, occasionally there are "beds", but they are designed to rest drivers between flights. And, for example, in China, Thailand and India there is a sleeping bus - as a rule, night flights where you can boot and sleep before you arrive.

- Do you need a passport to buy a ticket?

On adjacent interregional routes a passport is not needed, but when buying online passport data will be needed. If you are traveling from a region to a region, it is already needed anyway. That is, inside one subject of the Federation, you can buy a ticket and move without any documents. That does not exclude the possibility that you will be asked to present them for inspection on the way.

- And who has the right to stop the bus to check passengers?

On the territory of the bus station the bus and documents of the passengers have the right to check the administration of the station. On the way, they can check both the carrier and passengers. MIA or FSB can search for criminals, check baggage for drugs and transportation of weapons and so on. Further, the usual rules, as if the staff approached you on the street. It may be that the bus was detained by the police, and passengers will have to get on their own.

- And if I did not get to the end of the route because of an accident, for example?

Usually the carrier in this case sends an additional bus to pick up passengers who have broken down. If the continuation of the route is not possible, then according to the standard, the remaining segment of the path is compensated with money. For example, 6.5 rubles per kilometer. Yes, you can stay somewhere in the steppe at night and get 200 rubles of compensation later, and yes, an additional bus can arrive in 6 hours.

- Arrived at the final destination of the route 2 hours later, will I receive compensation?

Usually not. If suddenly something important has failed because of a violation by the carrier, you can try to sue with evidence of the damage caused to you (for example, the missed flight of an airplane). If it was not an accident, not a traffic jam and not something else like a passenger who suddenly became ill, and you took him to the hospital directly from the route, there are chances. But usually such things are decided in the pretrial order with the carrier. Simple filing "moral damage" usually does not lead to success. There is no standard insurance covering lateness.

In the second situation, when you have a ticket, and the driver does not have a list or landing list, this is his problem. The main thing is to have a ticket.

- I have a ticket, I come to the station, and I'm not on the lists. What to do?

Two options are possible:

  2. Something went wrong somewhere, and the ticket purchase data did not reach the list.

At the discretion of the carrier. This means, among other things, that a drunken body can be landed at any stop along the route, if it suddenly began to smell, brawl or make the wrong sounds.

- I forgot the luggage on the bus, what to do?

The luggage will reach the final destination (not the one to which you have the ticket, but the one where the entire route ends) and will be transferred there to the luggage room. It can be searched for documents, perishable goods and for security.

- I did not go, can I return the ticket and get some of the money back?

On domestic flights there are general rules for returning:
- Two hours before the flight or more - 95% of the ticket price (there is no commission included);
- Between two hours and the time of departure of the flight - 85%;
- Less than 3 hours after departure - 75%;
- More than 3 hours after departure, the refund is no longer done, because later, the cashiers can no longer mark for entry into the system.
In addition, carriers and partners have their own additional rules for the return of tickets.

The easiest way after a call to a carrier (or aggregator) is at the box office of the bus station. If they don‚Äôt give cash there immediately, then you need to put a return stamp on the ticket. Ticket exchange is done by returning one and buying another.

Now up to 1TB on your MacBook

The company MCE Technologies announced today a set of OptiBay , allowing you to easily get 1TB on your "apple" friend.

If you find it hard to part with your optical drive, then there is an option for you especially, namely, either an external optical drive with FireWire and USB connection types, or an external case for connecting an optical drive.

You can now use two of your hard drives as one, combining them into a RAID array, or you can use them as separate drives using Apple's Disk Utility. You can also use the second disk as storage for backups from Time Machine, if you have Mac OS X Leopard

Apple's supported laptop models are the MacBook Pro 15 "and 17", the MacBook 13.3 "and the Apple PowerBook G4 12", 15 "and 17". But be careful, the manufacturer warns that the 15 "MacBook and MacBook Pro models use non-standard optical drives, so they added an optical drive as a separate option.

The cost of this pleasure comes to $ 948 for the most advanced model, you can order here . This kit includes an OptiBay with a 500GB hard drive and an additional SATA hard drive from MCE Technologies of the same size, and in addition an external optical drive.

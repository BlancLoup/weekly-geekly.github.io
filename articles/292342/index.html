Rambler launches restaurant reservation system

Rambler launches SmartReserve restaurant table reservation system to automate the workplace of hostesses and attract armor from Afisha Restaurants.



At the moment of the call, the host will see the name, phone number and guest preferences on the terminal screen. When developing software, Rambler used hostess reviews, accepting up to 150 reserves per day, and the wishes of restaurateurs. Rambler promises to replace the paper book of reserves.

As a terminal, a tablet with a stand that works without recharging for up to 18 hours is used, with access to the network to synchronize the list of reserves and process reserves coming from online sites. Judging by the photos and the promised work time, this will be one of the tablets of the Lenovo Yoga line.

Rambler & Co Group of Companies acquired the SmartReserve project at the end of 2014. At that time, there were three founders in the startup, one employee, ten restaurant clients and a working prototype of the system.

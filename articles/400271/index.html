Russian retailers complained about Visa and MasterCard in FAS



The largest Russian retailers represented by the Association of Trading Companies and Manufacturers of Household and Computer Equipment (RATEK) complained about the Visa and MasterCard payment systems to the Federal Antimonopoly Service, reports TASS . The reason for the complaint was the high, in the opinion of retailers, the cost of acquiring , namely, the excessive rate of the interbank commission. Such organizations as Opora Rossii, AKORT and AKIT joined the complaint.

The commission of payment systems for non-cash payments on plastic cards ranges from 1.4% for the largest organizations to 2% for all others. For payments made on the Internet, the commission rises to 1.6-3.5% per transaction. At the same time, non-cash transactions through plastic cards account for 29% of all payments made in the Russian Federation.

Retailers claim that the position of the Visa and MasterCard payment systems hinders the widespread introduction of non-cash payments and, accordingly, the whitewash of cash flows. The minimum commission of 1.4-1.6% can be received only by companies with multi-billion turnover, while the rest work at a rate of 2-3.5%. In the opinion of RATEK, such policies as Visa and MasterCard suffer primarily from online stores and small businesses, for which the commission of payment systems is higher. At the same time, service fees may differ for the same bank card, depending on the type of product sold.

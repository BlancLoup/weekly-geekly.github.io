Kim Dotcom promises $ 400 million fiber and free internet to all New Zealand residents

Kim Dotkom is preparing to launch a new project Me.ga , which will be held on January 19. The Me.ga site will become a more powerful alternative to Megaupload, with distributed hosting, encryption of all files on the client side before being transferred to the server, open APIs for third-party applications, etc. Interested hosters are invited to fill out an application for cooperation.



Yesterday, Kim Dotcom announced another mega-project: the revival of the Pacific Fiber highway , which is to connect New Zealand and the United States along the bottom of the Pacific Ocean. The $ 400 million project involves doubling the backbone capacity, building a data center, creating new jobs and - as an added bonus - free internet for all New Zealand residents.

Of course, Kim Dotcom is not going to destroy the business of current Internet service providers, such as Telecom and Vodafone. On the contrary, it will provide them with a wider channel, provided that legal entities and the central government pay the monthly fee. But even for them, the cost of the Internet will decrease by five times with an increase in speed of three to five times and the absence of all sorts of limits on the amount of traffic.

Pacific Fiber suspended its operations on August 1, 2012 due to lack of funds. She planned to lay a submarine cable with a length of 12,950 km from Auckland (New Zealand) to Los Angeles and Sydney.

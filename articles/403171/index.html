MinFin: software from foreign sites should not be taxed by Google

As you know, since the beginning of this year in Russia came into force amendments to the Tax Code (NK), which was called the "tax on Google."

The point is that foreign companies operating in Russia must pay VAT on electronic services provided to citizens of the country. These amendments concern, for example, paid applications for iOS and Android platforms, the operation of Uber, the rental of apartments through AirBnB. A number of providers of electronic services have already registered with the Federal Tax Service, in some cases the tax will be paid by the Russian partners of foreign companies.

With regard to programs located on servers outside the Russian Federation, the question of falling under the "tax on Google" when using such software was asked by one of the Russian organizations using software over the Internet. The work is carried out "by providing the access code to the programs posted on the official website of the foreign organization." This organization asked the Ministry of Finance to pay VAT on financial transfers to a foreign company.

Having analyzed the request, the specialists of the Ministry of Finance of Russia came to the conclusion that Article 149 of the Tax Code exempts transactions on the transfer of rights to use the results of intellectual activity under a licensing agreement from VAT.

SmartProgress: Elephant Hunt

The big elephant is eaten piece by piece. This rather well-known metaphor from the book of Gleb Arkhangelsky, says that to any global goal leads the way from the n-nn number of small steps. This is not to say that this idea is original (to some extent, it is present in many books on time management, the same Covey and his ‚Äúsharpening saw‚Äù or Allen‚Äôs Getting Things Done and his flight at an altitude of 30 or 50 thousand miles) this idea is no less correct. Let's do it again: a big elephant is eaten piece by piece.

Why I focus on this. I do not think that the majority of those gathered in these profile blogs see this truth for the first time. However, one thing is to know, another thing is to have a convenient tool for cutting carcasses. But more on that later. In the meantime, a little bit of theory.

So, to begin with about the correct goal setting.


The goal must be achievable (otherwise it is a dream) and measurable. Those. There is no such goal ‚Äúto learn English.‚Äù It is unattainable in principle. You can reach the Advanced level, expand your vocabulary to a certain number of active words (there are services that allow you to calculate it), and so on. Those. The last two options are achievable and measurable goals. And the first is a poorly worded dream or desire. Similarly, there are no goals to "lose weight", "go in for sports", "defeat procrastination" and "get rich" because they are immeasurable.



But the task "to accumulate 500 thousand rubles for two years" is the goal. And a very good, easily measurable goal. Most importantly, it is easily chopped into pieces and can start to be fulfilled right now. And ‚Äúget a certificate for ... insert to taste "is also much better divided into subtasks and executed than the faceless" to learn such a programming language. "

Now how this purpose is executed. The same Allen in its concept of GTD teaches that any action consisting of more than two actions should be considered a separate project. And start your own folder, section or something else in the same spirit. Now, regularly looking through the list of our affairs, we will see our Goal (exactly, with a capital letter), and understand whether we are moving towards it or not. If not, then you can always plan another small step that you can do today. For example, to postpone 20 thousand from the salary on the deposit. Or buy a book on a programming language right now.



And finally, on the way to the goal, it is very important for us to see progress. To understand that we are not standing still, but moving. Let slowly, but constantly. And here, when we figured out how to set a goal, the first place is the instrument with which we will divide, kick, and, most importantly, track this goal. After all, we are techies, which means, like no one else, we understand that a properly selected tool is, if not half the story, then a significant part of it. I will show goal setting on the example of the SmartProgress goal achievement service (after all, this is a corporate blog, by the way, thank you for being with us), but you can use any of the usual methods or use the similar English-language service 43things.com .

Secondly, if you are afraid of possible painful interference from the outside. In any case, the choice is yours. The only thing is that in the case of a closed goal you will not be able to get advice and will not receive support from friends.

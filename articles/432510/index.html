The market share of plug-in electric vehicles in Norway has almost reached a new maximum.



In November, the market share reached the 2nd figure for all time.

Despite the winter, it was another hot month in Norway, where all electric cars dispersed like hot cakes. On the other hand, sales of plug-in hybrids have significantly decreased.

Last month, 7,001 new passenger electric cars were registered (growth of 20.4% in annual terms) with a market share of 56.9% (only September of this year was better with an indicator of 60.1%).

Here are the details that show that the market is shifting to pure electric vehicles:

  • electric vehicles with rechargeable batteries: 5087 (growth 88.1%, 41.3% the share of sales of all cars) + 1106 with secondary registration + 187 vans (178 new and 9 "used") + 3 electric vehicles on fuel cells;
  • plug-in hybrids: 1914 (38.4% fall; 15.5% is the share of sales of all cars).

Registration of new electromobiles in Norway (November 2018)

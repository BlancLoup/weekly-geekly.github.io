Bank of America stops working with Wikileaks



The financial blockade of the resource Wikileaks continues - this time the largest US bank announced the termination of payments in favor of Wikileaks. According to the management of the bank, working with Wikileaks contradicts some of the rules of the Bank of America.

In particular, the bank management made a statement that the processing of payments in favor of Wikileaks was stopped due to the direction of the resource‚Äôs activity. Such activities, according to the statement, contradict certain internal rules for processing payments. In general, everything is rather foggy and at the same time, quite clear.

Almost immediately after the statement made by the bank, the Wikileaks team made a retaliatory move, urging all those who do not like this situation to refuse service at Bank of America, and withdraw all their savings from there by transferring to other banking structures.

It seems that the Wikileaks team is becoming more and more difficult to live and work - because the site, especially the one visited, such as Wikileaks, requires money for maintenance and hosting, and hardly any hosting provider agrees to continue working with the resource for free. business is business. But already such large payment systems like MasterCard, PayPal, refused to work with Wikileaks.

It is worth noting that this time, perhaps, the US government did not exert pressure on the management of the bank. The fact is that this week Wikileaks published documents on a number of US financial structures whose work ethics is being questioned. Guess which bank was mentioned in these documents? Of course, Bank of America.

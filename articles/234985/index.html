Rospotrebnadzor is going to regulate the physical parameters of electronics



For scarce and not particularly confirmed information, Rospotrebnadzor is going to regulate the requirements for gadgets. The basic idea: children do not let go of the tablet-phone-reading room, and by the school they have already lost their sight. In the preliminary list of regulated parameters, the font, brightness and flicker frequency of the screen. At the same time, Soviet norms are still in effect for 30 minutes of a computer per week and not earlier than five years. Of course, the legislation would not hurt to update. Modern screens are clearly less harmful than MS6105 Electronics with a 50 Hz sweep. But I will not continue further, everyone already knows and understands everything.

When I read this news, I immediately recalled the Son Ericsson T230 phone. It was in the early 2000s. There was the most disgusting screen I've ever seen, with viewing angles of 20 degrees each and with a clearly visible interpixel grid. From this screen really want to protect, but the buyer may well cope with it yourself. I will not even discuss the possibilities and the adequacy of implementation; everyone will think out to the best of imagination. But after the introduction of standards, it will be possible to reasonably respond to the argument "fonts - organic waste": "The Federal Service approved, the fonts are excellent!"

2) Amplifier


The following types of audio amplifiers exist: A, B, A / B, C, D, G, H, T ...
Read about them you can elementary, that's what is important to know:

Amplifier Class "A" is considered the highest quality. Efficiency is usually 20%, yes, yes, this is a heater. Without a radiator, chips are usually cut down immediately. The transistor operates in open mode: it amplifies both the negative and positive half-wave signals. At the same time he eats in idle as much as at the maximum.
It does not suit us.
Amplifier Class "B"
Its advantages are relatively high efficiency (50-60%). One transistor amplifies the negative, the other - the positive side of the half wave. Significant sound distortions appear at the moment when the signal passes through zero.
It does not suit us.
Amplifier Class "AB"
Naturally, electronics are cubes, we take and connect the two classes listed above, take each one according to its advantages. Amplifiers of this class are the most common.
Already better.
Amplifier Class "D" (close class "T")
This is the last class about which I will tell. The rest are too rare and they are not necessary.
The basis here is PWM modulation. Due to the fact that transistors operate in a pulsed mode, the efficiency tends to a very good figure from 80% to 98%! Accordingly, there is no need to remove heat, the dimensions of the amplifier are significantly reduced. With sound, too, everything is in order.
This is exactly what we need.

"TM" restarts "My Circle"

The company TM has become the new owner of the My Circle service. Yandex, the former owner of the service, and TM, the publisher of projects for IT specialists, offer users a look at the updated version of the service.



How did it happen


TM develops Brainstorage.me , a professional network of professionals working in the information technology industry. This service stood out from Habra several years ago and is now actively developing, helping professionals to find interesting work, and for companies to strengthen the team.

In order to make the service even more attractive, we took courage and offered Yandex to combine Brainstorage with My Circle, another project popular with IT people. After spending many months in negotiations, we finally found a common language and agreed.



What changed


Previously, "My Circle" was rather a social network of specialists of very different profiles. The primary role was played by personal connections and the ability to make new acquaintances with friends. The user of the service primarily monitored the career changes of his friends, as well as the vacancies that they posted.

In the new ‚ÄúMy Circle‚Äù we left the concept of a social network, bringing to the fore the opportunity for unfamiliar employers and job seekers to find each other. At the same time, we have narrowed the subject of the resource, limiting it only to the IT industry, which includes such areas as: programming, layout, design, management, web analytics, marketing and others.



We have preserved some elements of the social network, such as friendship, feedback about each other and others, but time will tell whether we will develop in this direction and bring personal connections to the forefront, as it was before.

If earlier placement of vacancies was free, now it has become paid. All vacancies that were previously posted on the old ‚ÄúMy Circle‚Äù were transferred to the new service, and all of them were paid accommodation for one month.

With the help of ‚ÄúMy Circle,‚Äù employers find themselves specialists, and job seekers find work in various areas of the IT industry.

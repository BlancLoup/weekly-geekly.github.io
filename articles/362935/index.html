Default Coherent and change of owners

A month and a half ago, in Habratopik about the introduction of duties on parcels from abroad, I wrote about the sad situation of large domestic electronics retailers. In particular, the words of Izvestia were cited, that part of the stores were working with multi-billion dollar losses, the bankruptcy of the White Wind was recalled and the assumption was made that new bankruptcies would soon follow.



The prediction was not long in coming - at the beginning of this week it was reported that one of Svyaznoy's creditors, a firm belonging to the Onexim group, had declared a default, i.e. situations when the borrower of credit funds could not fulfill the obligation to repay the debt. The mortgage on the loan was a controlling stake in a Cypriot company managing the assets of Svyaznoy. In total, Svyaznoy Group of Companies, according to unofficial data from Kommersant, owed creditors about 560 million dollars (at the current exchange rate it is almost 26 billion rubles).

Interestingly, a day before the announcement of the purchase of a 90% stake in Svyaznoy , the statement of GK Svyaznoy director Michael Tach was distributed on the network, saying that Svyaznoy is OK. Yes, and this situation is unlikely to spread to new models.

In connection with the foregoing, I would not yet rely on large pre-New Year discounts in domestic stores. Given the dollar exchange rate and the freezing of ruble price tags, current prices in relation to the European look already at a discount. It is easier for the seller to sell the equipment at the old price, than to reduce the cost, to deposit money and buy new lots. Because new lots will be more expensive.

In addition, I would like to once again remind you that the budget for 2015-2017 laid the income from the duties on parcels from abroad and you can expect them to be introduced starting in January 2015. Judging by the position of domestic sellers, many of them will not survive without these duties.

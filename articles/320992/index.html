FOSDEM , Brussels, Belgium, 4‚Äì5 February



A free conference for opensource developers will begin the day after tomorrow. To get to it, you do not even need to register. Held in Brussels since 2000, the original name is OSDEM (Open Source Developers European Meeting). Here is a great review of last year‚Äôs conference from the speaker from the MAPS.ME team.


Brian Brazil: Prometheus: A Next Generation Monitoring System



Brian talks about the Prometheus metrics collection system written in Go, which combines graphing and error reporting features. This is a report on what Prometheus is and how to simplify opening source projects with it.


Hisham Muhammad: Going cross-platform: how htop was made portable



Speech by the creator of htop . He talks about how htop, created under Linux, became a cross-platform application, why it happened and what it led to.

Selenium Camp , Kiev, Ukraine, February 24‚Äì25



The conference has been held since 2011. The main directions are web and mobile applications testing with Selenium WebDriver ,. However, other topics are also touched: test scaling, autotest monitoring systems, microservice architecture testing, etc. ... The target audience is testers and developers who are not indifferent to test automation (both professionals and beginners).


Oren Rubin: Statistical Element Locator



The report is devoted to a common problem faced by testers - the search for elements of reliability. It classifies search methods, analyzes the mistakes of people and machines, and finally, proposes a new way to search for elements and a set of skills necessary to overcome difficulties.


The remaining performances at Selenium Camp 2016 are here .


CodeFest , Novosibirsk, Russia, April 1-2



Conference for developers, QA, project-managers and other IT-specialists. It has been held since 2010. Most of the reports are devoted to development, project management and testing.


QA


Dan Cuellar: The Story of Appium: Lessons Learned Creating an Open Source Project 0 to 100,000 users



Dan tells the story of the development of the Appium framework, which we at Badoo, by the way, began to use, as they say, before it was cool. If suddenly you are not yet in the subject, the information from this report will help you work with similar opensource-projects more effectively.


All speeches on quality assurance, see here .


Back-end


Konstantin Osipov: What is special about DBMS for RAM?



In his report, Kostya talks about the features of in-memory DBMS, how it differs from an arbitrary high-load system written in C, C ++ or Java, as well as about specialized algorithms and data structures for storing them in RAM.


Vyacheslav Bakhmutov: Experience with Go in Dropbox in SRE



The report will be of interest to those who have just started working with Go. It provides a brief overview of the Go ecosystem, explains what Go tasks are used in Dropbox and why the developers chose this language.

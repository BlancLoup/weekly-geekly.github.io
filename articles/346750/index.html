How we spent the day at Veeam

So the New Year holidays and winter holidays are over, the round dances around Christmas trees are over, fireworks have died down. I admit, I want to extend this slightly frivolous, carefree, romantic mood, so today my post will not be about new solutions and technologies, but about an unusual day that we arranged for the children of our employees - Veeam Kids Day, Children's Day at Veeam.
If your children also periodically pester you with questions about who the tester is, what you do at work, and why you should definitely go there every day, welcome to the cat - perhaps some ideas will be useful to you too.



How it all began


The idea was born quite a long time ago, back in the days when a branch of a well-known equipment and communications equipment company flourished in St. Petersburg. The employee who came to our team then was very surprised that we did not have Christmas trees (and indeed events) for the children of the employees. Perhaps it was because in those distant years very few employees could boast of having children :).

However, over time, the situation radically changed, and colleagues from Russian and foreign companies realized that inviting children (as well as grandchildren, wives, husbands, and parents) of employees to the Open Days of Days is a very grateful thing. Even if the child is still small, he most likely asked the father or mother the question why they go to work every day and what are they doing in this job? And the specifics of the IT industry is such that not everyone can tell about it in an accessible form to a 3-5 year old kid. Some useful tips


  • Convenient to carry out such activities in the days of school holidays. Children are usually at home anyway, and dad (mom) at work - let them meet.
  • If you are planning on taking photos, please be sure that you have written consent from the parents to take pictures of their children (and, if necessary, to non-commercial use of the photographs obtained, for example, in the illustrations for this article). You can print photos on paper and give them to children immediately after the event.
  • We also recorded small videos at the stations and short interviews with the participants. Then you can write to disk and donate.
  • If there are any souvenirs with the company's logo (for example, badges, flags), they will be useful.
  • If children and their accompanying adults (not the company's employees, but the aunt's grandmother-brother) need to be coordinated with the administration, take care of this in advance, as this usually takes time. Remind adults (grandmother-brothers-aunts) to take a passport with you to enter the office.
  • For each participant a corporate badge was prepared so that the guys would be ‚Äúlike adults‚Äù. Distributed them before the start of the game, and then left as a keepsake (like a trifle,
    and for the children ran proud and contented).
  • For younger children, try to meet in 2-2.5 hours. Do not be lazy and make a run with time measurement.
  • Do not slip into the format of "lecture". The story should be interspersed with questions to the audience and not take more than half of the time allotted for the location (and preferably no more than one third).
  • And, of course, even if the parents themselves do not participate in the interactive program, they will be happy to take a picture with their child at the workplace, take care of this.
  • As for the budget, on our site it turned out to be minimal - it was a treat (pizza, pie, juice), badges, souvenirs and office, for one participant (there were 18 people) less than 500 rubles. Of course, due to the fact that everything was done on their own.

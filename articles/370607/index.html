What kind of merit Kingston love data centers? Says 3data

The reasons why commercial data centers are switching to SSDs are well known - any data center with an emphasis on high bandwidth and low transaction latency will eventually give up on hard drives. But there are a lot of SSD manufacturers, corporate-class drive models are even more, purchasing costs money, and a mistake costs astronomical money. Today we will explain why 3data premium data centers preferred Kingston among all iron manufacturers, and do not regret their decision.


Who are 3data


3data is a walking distance data center network with a premium level of service. Today, it includes more than 10 of its own distributed data centers in Moscow, in which 5 distributed cloud nodes (virtualization clusters) are located. Each year, this network increases on average at 3 new sites. The goal for the future is to increase the number of sites to 40 by 2025 so that medium and small businesses in the capital can receive quality data center services within walking distance.

On the basis of these data centers, the company sells, of course, a collocation and its own distributed cloud. The 3data basic service is IaaS, a virtual infrastructure, i.e. separate virtual machines or a dedicated pool of computing resources with the ability to create various configurations of IT infrastructure and access via the Internet or physically or logically separated network infrastructure. Other services - archival and backup storage of large amounts of data (ArcTape), secure storage of extremely large amounts of data based on hybrid (disk-tape) solutions, dedicated servers, rental of servers and data storage systems (DSS), and ready-made solutions ( SaaS).


Data center 3data

In the last two virtualization clusters commissioned, 3data switched to using hyperconverged architecture, i.e., peer-to-peer hosts connected by special software. And for this, as you understand, we need disks. 3data uses disks of all types - SSD, SAS, NL SAS and even SATA. If to simplify a lot, the division is as follows: SSD for clients and cases when fast computations are needed, and cheap slow disks (NL SAS, for example) for large amounts of stored data.

The background and shadow for this distributed virtualization cluster is another - a backup cluster. This is a system of individual cloud nodes deployed in each data center backup servers, which are also connected into a single distributed cluster, where backup copies are made from these main virtual machine clusters. For this cluster, mainly capacious cheap SATA or NL SAS disks are used. Thus, information is reserved between data centers. A tape library is also attached to this configuration, to which petabytes of data are ‚Äúpoured‚Äù into 3data.



After several years of debugging, the first one, the main cluster, is actively developing and increasing its technical potential. And for such tasks, 3data began to look for high-speed equipment - as you understand from the tablet, SSD became the most suitable option.

Well, since I did not give out any keys, it means connecting to me to an unencrypted network.
No sooner said than done. I made the appropriate changes to the /etc/conf.d/net file and restarted the waffle. It was not there. Address dhcp issued well by no means wanted.
Having rebooted to Windows, which is also installed on my laptop, I tried to connect to this network and ... lo and behold! Connected. The settings were as follows:

ip 172.17.64.242/16 via 172.17.0.1
dhcp 10.40.0.103
dns 194.67.1.150

and immediately stumbled upon the fact that addresses are resolved, but there is no connection. Pages do not open. I tried telnet to go to www.google.com 80 and the connection just failed. Tracing did not go even to the gateway. So traffic cut gateway. But he had to wrap all requests at least on the authorization page.
But all I needed was what I already found out - this is the subnet, gateway and dns. I can now give the address to myself;).

Reboot to Linux, I immediately applied the previously obtained settings. I connected and could ping dns. Although the gateway and dhcp-server did not respond. Probably there icmp is prohibited.
Out of habit, which appeared after setting up my own hotspot on gentoo, I decided to check available computers for open ports. Maybe there is a solution. On the dns server, I found the http and https ports open. And the rest is nothing

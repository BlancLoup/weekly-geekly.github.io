Bulb in liquid nitrogen




ATTENTION: breaking the glass flask, we exposed the contacts to which the current is 220v. This voltage is life threatening!

The experiment clearly shows how an open spiral incandescent lamp quickly burns in air and remains unscathed while immersed in liquid nitrogen. It can be concluded: nitrogen is not an oxidizing agent and does not conduct electric current in liquid form.

During the production of incandescent bulbs, as exemplified by the one we smashed before the experiment, they are filled with inert gas. One of these gases is argon, and sometimes nitrogen.

Chemical focus with a handkerchief




Experience is based on the formation and destruction of crystalline compounds. Demonstrates discoloration of compounds when water is added or removed. Initially, the solution of cobalt chloride has a pink color. When heated, water from the surface of the shawl evaporates and cobalt chloride precipitates as a pink crystalline hydrate. Further heating leads to the destruction of the aquacomplex. So at temperatures above 110 degrees Celsius, the compound turns into anhydrous CoCl2 having a blue color. As soon as we add water again, the water is embedded in the crystal lattice, a crystalline hydrate is formed again and the scarf becomes pink again.

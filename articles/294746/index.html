FRIA launches online search service for employees and partners for startups

The Internet Initiatives Development Foundation ( IIDF ) launches a resource to search for co-founders of CoFoundIt.ru startups. While the service is in beta testing. The first users of the site will be 100 people from the database of IIDF. In addition to the founders, these are specialists who are interested in working in young innovative companies.

The service will allow young startups to receive support from residents and graduates of IIDF. In 2014 alone, more than 10,000 participants competent in the IT and Internet business passed through the foundation's programs. On average, only about 100 projects per year, or 200-300 people, receive seed investments from the IIDF. Program participants who have not received investments but have gained invaluable experience are ready to pass it on to younger colleagues or even join their start-ups. The new service is designed in the shortest time to connect these people with each other, taking into account their specialization.

According to the FRIA survey, more than 90% of the projects are looking for an employee or co-founder, and almost 100% of them are ready to use the online search service.

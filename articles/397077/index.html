Powerful anomalous radio signal from space, which was actively reported by the media, most likely of terrestrial origin


Radio astronomy telescope of the Academy of Sciences "RATAN-600"

Yesterday and today, a number of major foreign and domestic media reported on the fixation of a powerful radio signal from the system HD164595, the constellation of Hercules, which could be of extraterrestrial origin. Depending on the level of the principles of the resource, various types of headings were used, ranging from "Astronomers have recorded an anomalous signal from space" to "Scientists have discovered a signal from aliens."

The signal itself was recorded by the Russian observatory RATAN-600 located in Karachay-Cherkessia and belonging to the Special Astrophysical Laboratory of the Russian Academy of Sciences ( SAO RAS ).

Some doubts on this news arose at the moment when the amateur astronomer turned out to be the primary source , who through his acquaintances allegedly fell into the hands of the scientific work of Russian researchers.

SAO RAS itself responded to the situation in the network around the observation made at the RATAN.

This afternoon the following announcement was posted on their official website:

On August 30, 2016, a number of media outlets reported on the possible detection on RATAN-600 of a radio signal related to the activities of an extraterrestrial civilization, in connection with which we consider it necessary to give an official comment.

For the past several years, employees of the Scientific Research Institute of Nuclear Physics at the Moscow State University (Research Institute of Nuclear Physics named after D. Skobeltsyn at Moscow State University), together with employees of the Special Astrophysical Observatory of the Russian Academy of Sciences, have been carrying out search studies on astronomical objects - candidates for Search (Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence). SETI is a common name for projects and activities to search for extraterrestrial civilizations. The program is focused on the search for radio emission of artificial origin. The studies are carried out on the RATAN-600 radio telescope using broadband continuum radiometers in the frequency range 1-22.7 GHz. Such observations at the RATAN-600 became possible due to its large collecting surface - thousands of square meters, due to which the high sensitivity of the telescope makes it possible to search for extremely weak radio signals in the Universe, which also include SETI objects. The objects of research under this program are sun-like stars with planets and sun-like stars whose presence of planets is unknown.

As part of this program, in 2015, an interesting radio signal at a wavelength of 2.7 cm was detected from one of the objects (the HD164595 star system in the constellation Hercules). The signal processing and analysis revealed that it is highly likely that it is a signal of earth origin.

As for the other objects of research at RATAN-600, at the present moment it is early to speak about a reliably obtained scientific result. The measurements performed can estimate the radio emission of the studied areas only by the upper detection limit. It is safe to say that the search signal has not yet been detected.

Relying on inaccurate information from a dubious source, a number of world-famous English-language resources, as well as domestic media, with a high degree of certainty announced that they were receiving a signal of extraterrestrial origin, which is not true.

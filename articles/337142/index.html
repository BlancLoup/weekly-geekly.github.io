About MASSIVE night light Pure Storage and happy PR man



Hello! Below you will find a story about how to buy a lamp turned into the arrival of one of the most long-awaited innovations in the field of storage systems - the Pure Storage array. Caution, a lot of photos!

This story began with the fact that our sweet PR manager Galya ordered a nightlight for AliExpress. Well, you know, such a small lamp, designed to create a feeling of comfort at night.

A month later, she was delivered this box:



Galya could not even lift her - I had to call us with a cart:



Having opened the box, we were surprised a lot - this is the first in Russia demo storage system Pure Storage FlashArray, model // m20 with 38 TB Flash (why read here )!

As they say, jokes aside, let's consider the received apparatus. The array consists of two controllers in one 3U chassis. SSD modules are installed in the controller chassis with packs of 10 modules. Additional disk shelves also consist of two packs. m20 expands to 40 SSD-modules, below we will describe in detail about them.

With an SSD module of 7 TB and with deduplication enabled with compression, you can get on an almost basic model more than 250 TB of effective capacity. Not bad.

Specifically, one capacity pack of 10 SSD modules of 3.8 TB each is installed in our array. This gives slightly less than 21 TB of space for unique data, or 63‚Äì100 TB, taking into account deduplication and compression technologies.

On the front side, in addition to SSD modules, NVRAM modules are located. Two NVRAMs of 8 GB each are installed in m20 R2. NVRAM and SSD are tightened with torx-slotted screws, which prevents accidental removal of the wrong module.

The manufacturer of Pure Storage announced the release of new storage models with NVMe disks. After the release of new models, you can upgrade the existing m20 R2 to NVMe without losing data. Let's take a closer look at the ports in the array:



To connect the disk shelves, Mini SAS HD ports operating at 12 Gb / s are used.

To control the array, the ports are 1 Gbps Ethernet ETH0. The 1Gbps Ethernet ETH1 port can be used for both management and replication.

Ports ETH2 and ETH3 (10 Gbit Ethernet optical) - only for replication. Asynchronous replication is available in Pure Storage arrays, as well as building disaster recovery storage ‚Äî ActiveCluster. ActiveCluster implements synchronous replication of logical volumes of arrays in Active / Active mode, that is, read / write operations can be performed on the replicated logical volumes of both arrays simultaneously.

FC ports operate at a maximum speed of 16 Gbps. For Ethernet (iSCSI) ports, 40 Gbps is available ‚Äî so I / O ports will no longer be an array bottleneck.

Ubuntu Phone activated on 10,000 devices

The Canonical Ubuntu Phone mobile operating system is already activated on 10,000 devices , which is not too bad for an operating system whose devices are not yet released!



Activating a device means logging in to an Ubuntu One account, which is required for installing and updating applications, as well as syncing settings and applications between different devices (like Android and iOS).

In addition, on the portal developer.ubuntu.com a new section Ubuntu App Pioneers , which is a kind of "achivkoy." It gets those developers who wrote and released their applications for Ubuntu Phone even before the release of the first phone on the platform. If you want to be in time, then remember that the release of the first Meizu smartphone is just around the corner.

Cybersquatting bots for twitter accounts

Cybersquatting experts improve their tools. Many have come across the situation that if you check the domain name on the registrar's site and find a free name, then by the time of registration this name may well be occupied. Information from the site "merges" on the right channels.

Apparently, now this practice has been extended not only to domain names, but also to twitter accounts. The bots watch for fresh domain registrations in the .com zone and automatically occupy the corresponding twitter account .

In case this happens to you, you can complain in support of Twitter, because the service rules prohibit cybersquatting . If you describe the situation exactly and if the suspicious account remains inactive (without updates, photos, etc.), then it may well be transferred to you. In addition, Twitter can transfer an account if it matches a registered trademark to the trademark owner.

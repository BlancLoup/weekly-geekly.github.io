Third Annual Conference of JavaScript Developers in Odessa - JS Lab



The third annual conference of JavaScript developers, JSLab, will take place in Odessa on March 25. Our FlyElephant team helps with the organization of this conference and invites everyone to take part in it.

Want to expand your horizons and go beyond your daily javascript tasks? You have this opportunity. This year JS Lab is waiting for you: 200 front-end and back-end JS-developers from Ukraine and the near abroad; two streams consisting of 12 reports of 45 minutes and 8 blitz reports of 10 minutes each; themes affecting frontend, backend, virtual reality, game development, testing, and all this in JavaScript.

Among the reports, the following can be noted:

  • Virtual Reality for Web
    Victor Tours (CTO at WebbyLab)
    I'll tell you about the available VR devices and their features. About Web VR, about VR frameworks for the Web, about the possibilities that this opens up for us. I will show a real demo.

  • Practical difficulties when developing on node.js
    Vitaly Lebedev (Software Developer at DataArt)
    In this report I will tell you what immediate practical difficulties can be encountered when developing on node.js, due to certain limitations of the node.js itself, and what possible solutions exist for these tasks.

  • Why is it important to be a programmer in the frontend
    Roman Jakobchuk (Senior Software Engineer at RevJet)
    It may be unpleasant to hear, but most front-end vendors are far from programming. The problem is absolutely not new, it is known to everyone, but for some reason it does not get any better. The frameworks have replaced the need to understand the basic principles: patterns, algorithms, data structures ... Or does it only seem so? We will try to figure out what went wrong, and how quickly you can become a more valuable specialist.

  • Testing javascript code. Tools, practices, trends
    Evgeny Safronov (Senior developer at DataArt)
    On tests and quality from different points of view and perspectives. Testing by developers. The main trends, methods and tools for testing Javascript code. Best practices and approaches.

  • Balanced environment for your productivity
    Alexey Zelenyuk (Application Architect at Eleks Software)
    Building a great web application requires a good foundation: the process of building, testing and integrating, analyzing code quality and debugging. New technologies and business requirements create new requirements for the environment, complicating it. How to build a reliable environment, while maintaining its flexibility and simplicity?

  • How to use jspm and do without Webpack'a
    Sergey Seletsky (Senior Frontend Engineer at Lohika)
    Let's talk about System.js and jspm. What they can give for your project. Advantages and disadvantages. How it works with ES6. Examples of project assembly.

  • React Fiber
    Alexey Zaslavsky (Front-End developer at WebbyLab)
    The report will consider a new experimental algorithm for constructing and comparing Virtual DOM, which will appear in the new version of React. Consider the main problems of the current implementation and how React Fiber can solve them.

  • Development of multi-packet applications: causes, methods, risks
    Andrey Kucherenko (Lead Software Engineer at EPAM Systems)
    The report will cover the topic of developing multi-pack javascript applications. The report is made on the basis of real experience in the implementation of such a project.

  • PhaserJS and what he can do
    Julia Puchnina (JS Engineer at Lifestreet Media)

The full program of the conference, all the details and registration at jslab.in.ua .

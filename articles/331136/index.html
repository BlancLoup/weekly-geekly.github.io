Article TSB "Engineering Psychology"
Engineering psychology, one of the special disciplines of psychology. Engineering psychology solves the following tasks:

1) the rational organization of the activities of people in the "man and machine" systems designed to control and process information;

2) appropriate distribution of functions between the manager and service personnel and technical means of automation;

3) optimization of information support and decision-making processes. In solving these problems, engineering psychology is based on data from related sciences, such as personality psychology, labor psychology, etc., and also works closely with systems engineering and engineering disciplines.

Engineering psychology originated in the 40s. 20 in. and originally developed as a direction of traditional labor psychology, the main object of which was the study of direct human interaction with objects and tools (tools, machine tools, conveyors, means of transport, etc.). Tasks Engineering psychology was mainly limited to a critical analysis of equipment design errors or operator training and the identification of factors affecting the effectiveness of the ‚Äúman and machine‚Äù systems. Useful recommendations were developed on the rational design of control panels, instrument scales, display devices, etc. Formation Engineering psychology as an independent scientific psychological discipline was due to the automation of production and the development of remote control and management tools. In the 50s. In general, the laws of reception and processing of information were determined by man, and in the 60s. - General principles of the organization of human interaction and computer. The recommendations developed have found practical application in the automation of control processes in production, aviation, astronautics, etc. At the end of the 60s. Engineering psychology turned to the synthesis, design of human activity in large systems, it makes a certain contribution to the development of measures to improve the efficiency of their functioning.

The task of rational organization of people‚Äôs activities in the ‚Äúman and machine‚Äù systems includes an analysis of the structure of the team and its coordination with the chosen management structure. This takes into account the mediated nature of communication and communication between team members and provides for such a distribution of functional responsibilities that would best meet the goal. An important problem is the rational distribution of responsibility for the decisions made and coordination of the interests of each member of the team. For the successful activity of small groups (aircraft crews, personnel of command and control centers, etc.), it is necessary to identify the criteria of psychological compatibility of group members, which is very important when recruiting them. Psychological compatibility is the most important condition for the preservation of a healthy team, especially isolated for a long time from the usual environment.

In the distribution of functions in the ‚Äúman and machine‚Äù system, it is assumed that the solution of creative tasks related to the logical analysis of the situation, assessment and forecast of its changes should be carried out by man; the machine is charged with performing routine, repetitive operations, mainly combinatorial and computational in nature, as well as storing, processing and promptly issuing large amounts of information. The electronic computer should be considered as a qualified assistant and interlocutor, contributing to the strengthening of the creative thinking and intuition of the person.

Optimization of information support and decision-making processes requires organizing selective control of information sources at control points and boils down to choosing the best characteristics of an information model: the degree of generalization (detail) of information displayed on display devices; volume and rate of its update; coding methods that provide unambiguous perception and storage of information. The basic principle of organizing information preparation for decision-making is that, at the request of the computer operator, it can perform all the time-consuming and auxiliary transformations of the available information and produce the results in the form most suitable for solving operational problems. In automated systems, the operator is deprived of the possibility of direct influence on the controlled object (object of labor). Under these conditions, a new task arose: not only coordinating the executive motor acts of a person with dynamic properties and characteristics of tools and means of production (this problem was solved by the methods of work psychology), but mainly the abilities of the operator to receive, process and transmit information with the information characteristics of the control object.

The design of human activity is based on the fundamental research of higher mental functions of a person - perception, memory, thinking (figurative and conceptual), which are internal psychological tools and means of human activity. These internal tools include experience, knowledge, programs, schemes and skills of the operator, which together comprise his professional appearance. On the basis of internal means of activity, permanent and operational figurative-conceptual models are formed that determine the activity of the operator and the decision-making process. The operator, using the arsenal of internal means of activity, relies on external means, which include information models implemented on information display devices or in the form of a document, computer algorithms, and other auxiliary means for preparing problem solving, controls and communication tools. It can be said that the design of human activity consists in the coordination of internal and external means of activity, the design of coordinated conceptual and information models that fully use the psychological capabilities of the operator to receive and process information.

Under various conditions, the center of gravity of this design may fall either on external or on internal means of activity. There are a number of information models that can be conventionally called models-images of the real situation. These include the display of the air situation on the radar screen, aerial photographs, pictures of the processes in the track cameras, etc. These models are determined by the available technical capabilities to collect primary information. Engineering and psychological recommendations for working with such information models relate to the conditions of illumination, level of contrast, mode of operation, etc. The main task of psychologists in these cases is to analyze the activities and the formation of its internal resources. There are other information models where the operator deals not with the primary, but with the previously processed information, for example, incoming to the display devices from the computer. In these cases, the developers and specialists in engineering psychology have a lot of room for the technical implementation of various types of information models and the focus can be on creating adequate external means of the operator‚Äôs activities.

The most important problems of engineering psychology include: the development of methods for professional selection, training and training of operators; identification of the specifics of the operators in specific systems and the development of recommendations, norms and standards for taking into account the human factor in the creation and operation of production, military and organizational systems; designing rational information models and controls; formulation of requirements for computer algorithms and methods for solving problems by the operator; development of methods for controlling the functional states of operators (fatigue, mental tension, stress); the development of principles for the construction of camera stations in accordance with the requirements of technical aesthetics and artistic design.

Developing as an applied scientific discipline, engineering psychology in the formulation of problems, methods of research and development uses the achievements of theoretical and experimental psychology and at the same time opens up new research problems for them. At the present stage, for engineering psychology, the data obtained in the study of individual psychological personality traits, especially the emotional sphere, the sphere of needs and motives of activity, are especially important.

Group order of printed circuit boards - myths and reality



I wanted to organize a community of amateur electronics, collect project boards from them, combine them into group blanks and order them in production. The essence of the idea is obvious - due to the fact that the preparation of production is divided into all it turns out a decent savings. Even if you throw a small commission expenses for the organization. I think many electronics engineers visited the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãcreating such a service.

Life cruelly taught me a lesson. In fact, the necessary minimum of restrictions to ensure tangible benefits turned out to be MUCH more than I expected. In the end, I did not succeed. We, of course, collected and launched one order. All financial calculations and technical subtleties under the cut.

Who needs it


I have a lot of non-commercial just-for-fun projects and I, of course, the Jedi of a laser iron, but anyway, I even wanted to use ‚Äúreal‚Äù boards in such handicrafts, with mask and metallization. First of all, I wanted to organize such a service so that I myself would have been cheaper to order fees.

Secondly, my scheme could be of interest to lovers who, like me, have nowhere to wait for funding. Hackspaces, freelancers, clubs and my students very much supported the initiative.

Thirdly, such boards can be ordered to check the design of the future module in hardware. I have repeatedly ordered the prototypes of commercial products, laying out a substantial amount of money, despite the fact that I had to roll a couple of copies before a large lot was ordered.

I will not hide the fact that I was counting on, if not on the commercial benefits from this whole event, then at least on compensation for my labor costs.

Method limitations


I decided to make a group blank only five pieces each. That is, customers had the opportunity to order fees only in a multiple of five.

Technical specifications of the board are the same for everyone.

I chose urgent production of boards (about one week), but before that there is an unpredictable time to collect the order. Looking ahead, I say that it took a little less than three weeks to collect the first order.

The first steps


For a start, we decided on the technical characteristics of the board:
  • two-layer PP
  • minimum clearance - 0,24mm
  • minimum conductor width - 0,24mm
  • material thickness - 1.5mm
  • foil thickness - 35 microns
  • minimum hole - 0.6mm
  • edge processing - milling

I made a table and sent it to manufacturers asking them to fill in prices. The table summarized the options for board size and mask / silk-screen printing. In response, I received various offers, but one of them was the most profitable. Here is the table with prices (in rubles):

Dimensions 100x200 200x200 200x300
Without mask and marking 1800 2200 2600
Masked, unlabeled 3200 3700 4300
With mask and marking 6000 6600 7200

Prices instilled in me hope for the success of the enterprise. I chose the option 200x200 with a mask, without silk-screen printing, and set to work.

To start, I created a group in contact. I will give a link to it at the end of the article, when you realize that this is not an advertisement and I am not inviting you to join it. In the group, I laid out the TTX boards and information about the group order scheme.

Order picking


After a small PR campaign among my friends in the community, there were more than a hundred subscribers, and I began to collect the order. I must say that I agreed with the manufacturer to provide Gerber files. That is, all the preliminary processing had to be done to me.

more than 11,000 rubles! I sat and doused with cold sweat! Such a blow of fate, I just did not expect! I was 100% sure that everything is going great.

I was offered options to optimize the cost, but, of course, not at times. All of the above instantly lost its meaning.

"Solution to the problem

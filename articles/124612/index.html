Samsung Galaxy S II - still a cake!



In the spring I managed to drive one day with a raw-dresser engineering model SGS II, and then I did not enjoy the excitement. Everything was with him, but somehow the gadget did not cling to me, and that was all. There was not even the most overwhelming raisin in it - solid high technology ... Several months passed, the raisins were no more, but all the roughness had smoothed out, and now it is rightfully the most technologically advanced smartphone today. For him it would be no sin to give 25 thousand, well, a maximum of 28, but no more. And the official price, meanwhile, is 31990!

Design

On the other hand, what modern smartphone with a large display doesn‚Äôt look like it? They all look the same. But these two models, if not peer, and the truth, can be easily confused. The difference becomes noticeable only after the gadget enters the hand. The second galaxy is made of plastic, which means that it is much lighter than glass-metal iphone. And it is also thinner, and it is believed that because of this is not so comfortable in the hand. In any case, I get used to this ratio of size and weight only now - one month after the start of testing.



In addition, the back panel of this Samsung is not as austere as the iPhone: at the bottom there is a ledge that the finger conveniently clings to, at the top there is an impressive eight-megapixel camera module, and the cover itself is textured, which makes me happy hand does not slip at all.

And the buyer will probably like the concept: despite the fact that the body is completely plastic, it is assembled to the conscience, no squeaks even during attempts to twist the smartphone, but well, it gives the impression of a quality thing even before it starts Shine a super bright display.

Samsung, like HTC (and maybe vice versa), did not install NVIDIA into its top-end smart mainstream hardware and built it on the basis of its own Exynos chip, known until recently as Orion. This is due to the fact that the chip includes a Cortex-A9 dual-core processor running at 1.2 GHz, gigabytes of DDR-2 RAM, and a Mali-400 video subsystem that can handle stereo image resolution FullHD.

Soft


The smartphone runs on Android 2.3 with the TouchWiz 4 shell, which makes the system oh-so-very similar to Samsung's own software platform. Firstly, this is good for those who suddenly decide to buy a powerful androidphone instead of a regular touchscreen phone, and secondly, it fixes the flaws in the Android interface, which are still present in version 2.3. Personally, I really liked the set of "toggle switches" inside the top panel: in a couple of clicks, you can turn on or off Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, GPS, sound and auto-rotate the screen without having to crawl through the wilds of the menu. And besides, we were pleased with the company's widgets, the size of which can be dynamically changed right on the desktop, the ‚ÄúApplications‚Äù menu, divided into separate pages, and, of course, the ability to take screenshots using the combination of the lock key and the button below the display.

Little things


At first, it was wildly frustrating that the power and lock button was on the right side, because the vast majority of similar devices usually have it on the top, but in the end I got used to it, because the volume control keys because of this were placed on the left, and not to the right.



What could not get used to, so it is to the location of the speaker. On the ledge of the rear panel there are two holes through which the sound should in theory go out. And he regularly does it when the gadget is in the hand vertically. Turn it into landscape mode, or, say, put it on a bed, and it becomes like a fish - the speaker is tightly closed with a palm or blanket. Therefore, than to accidentally miss an important call, it is better to put the phone "face" down, hoping that the surface is flat, and there are enough half-millimeter projections of the shiny border so that the display does not scratch.

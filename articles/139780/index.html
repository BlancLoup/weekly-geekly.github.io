Creating Groups for Technology Entrepreneurship from Stanford University

In the morning I received a letter with fresh news on one of the online courses conducted by Stanford. At this time, it seems that active preparation for the launch of the Technology Entrepreneurship course has begun. The authors of the course invite everyone to follow the link to their website , register and enter basic information about themselves - age, gender, location, industry in which you work. If you dig deeper, you can enter data on your interests and your education.

But the most important thing is that the authors are interested in whether you have a team of 3-4 people. If you do not have your own team, Chuck Eesley and Amin Saberi promise to help build a team by country of residence, interests and the area in which you work. In any case, the authors are asked to fill in the necessary data before March 18.

The request to create teams hints that all participants of the course will have to perform some tasks in a team, which is very good. I know for some individual courses of my university, how much the benefit of the course increases, if I constantly have to work in a team.

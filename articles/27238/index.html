One call and you have an animated avatar

Beema, a 3D animation studio that has been working in the corporate field for the past ten years, has released a new platform, which is currently in beta testing. This platform provides users with talking animated avatars in 3D format. To do this, the user needs to make only one phone call.




During registration, the user must specify your phone number and place of work, because This data is tied to his account. And then everything is simple: the user only needs to call one of the 800 free phones of the company and leave a voice message. A few minutes after that, the system will send you an SMS notification and an e-mail with a link to your video, which you can dispose of at your discretion. Currently, not everyone will be able to receive animated avatars from Beema, which may have hundreds of options: from cartoon characters before the image of Orson Welles. In the future, users will be able to upload their own photos, and photos of famous characters and personalities will be provided for a reasonable fee.

The new product from Beema does not claim to originality: a similar service is provided by many sites, such as Vidiator and the Oddcast's Voki platform. The advantage of this release is ease of use. The developers have abandoned Flash technology, which is used in most avatars, for the sake of 3GP video format, which gives users more freedom to work with their video, which can now be viewed on the iPhone. On the other hand, placing video on the network is quite problematic, since there is no widget needed, so you yourself will have to upload the video to YouTube.

Apple TV will receive new software and hardware stuffing


Apple continues to work on an updated version of its Apple TV set-top box.

According to rumors, the updated version of the console will have an A8 processor and 8 gigabytes of internal memory. The device will support Siri, AppStore and HomeKit. It is likely that a separate AppStore with applications optimized for launching on a television screen will be made for the gadget. This will require a new approach to developing and porting products, so developers should be the first to get acquainted with the new product.

One of the important innovations is the launch of a streaming television broadcasting service. Apple has already signed a contract with HBO, followed by other channels, such as ABC and CBS. This is great news for American customers who are dissatisfied with the high price of cable television and adding unnecessary channels to the packages. If Apple offers viewers the opportunity to watch only those channels that they want to watch, then this service will be in great demand.

The update will be presented at WWDC 15 in June.

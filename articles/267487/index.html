Embarcadero Technologies sold to Idera

Judging by a number of explanations here , and discussion on the forums , the company Embarcadero Technologies, a manufacturer of application developers and database developers (Delphi, InterBase, DBArtisan, etc.), was acquired by Idera , a company specializing in database tools. The amount of the transaction, based on the available data - 425 million dollars.

It is interesting to remember that Embarcadero Technologies itself in 2006‚Äì2007 was acquired by a private investment fund and removed from the listing (that is, it was public, traded on the stock exchange, and became a closed, private company), then (in 2008‚Äì2009) Embarcadero acquired for $ 20 million CodeGear developer tools (Delphi, C ++ Builder, InterBase) from Borland. And after 6 years of sale for 425, i.e. about 4 times increased the cost.

A rather sad story with these developer tools, which are still very popular in our country and in a number of other countries. It is worthwhile for the company to gain momentum and start producing a high-quality product (and recently there have been opportunities for multiplatform development for mobile OS, 64 bit versions and a lot of good things), as it is sold to someone.

I was working at Borland‚Äôs Russian representative office during the deal with Embarcadero, and I can say that ET was good at acquiring: development teams were greatly enhanced, then the company cross-licensed ( Fast Reports , etc.) and acquired several component libraries (FireMonkey and others), which were then reworked and made up the backbone of the product, significantly increasing the number of Delphi user developers at the expense of those who develop mobile applications.

Whether Idera will show the same gentlemanly approach to development tools as Embarcadero is hard to say. Obviously, the deal, like the sale of CodeGear, was influenced not only by sales figures (pretty good), but also by almost 3 million users of products such as Delphi, but it is unclear what strategy for them the new owner will adhere to.

In any case, a good sign is the amount of the transaction - in the market focused on developers and administrators, there have been no such large acquisitions for a long time.

I hope that Idera will become a good owner of its new acquisitions.

The new Google service will allow you to find information about a person by his photo.



Honestly, if this information would not have appeared on CNN's website on March 31, I would have thought that this was another April Fools' news, of which there were a lot of different resources published on April 1. However, everything looks more than serious. Currently, Google is working to create a mobile application that will allow you to find the personal data of a person on his web on the web. True, in order for such information about yourself to be available at the request of the application, you must first give your consent, ticking the corresponding clause of the agreement. According to the corporation, the exact release date of the mobile application is still unknown, but work on its development is very active, and the company has all the technical capabilities for the successful implementation of the project. Perhaps it will not be an independent service, but only one of the functions of existing Google projects. In addition to information from Google's Profiles, the mobile application will search for images of a person on social networks (in this case, only a search will be carried out on the images posted in the public domain).

Work on the creation of serious image recognition technology has been conducted by Google since about 2009, when the corporation bought the company Like.com, which also conducted research in this area. Google has since received several patents in the field of image recognition with people's faces.

Payler is back, or a story about conflicts and their consequences



Hi, Habr!

This story is told on behalf of our mobile application development studio, Polonium Arts. We are happy to return to you after a long silence and want to share a story about how shit happens to surprise colleagues with the actions.

Once we had a story in our company. Two developers - young and very promising specialists, with big plans and excellent results - decided to prove themselves in the art of hacking. In other words, they used access to confidential information on one of our projects, discovered a vulnerability and made a purchase with a 50% discount of their own choosing. The penetration did not go unnoticed by the security services of the project - their CEO called our CEO ...

There was a conflict.

Fortunately, our clients are wise and far-sighted people, we managed to smooth out the conflict and continue working on the project. However, the question of punishing the guilty remained open - the question arose, what does the developer‚Äôs responsibility really mean to the company?

Here we want to make a lyrical-legislative retreat. According to Article 159 of the Criminal Code of the Russian Federation, the story described above falls under clause 3 - fraud committed by a person using his official position, and is punishable by a minimum of 120 thousand, and a maximum by imprisonment of up to 6 years. Is it worth mentioning that the dismissal under the article will put a big cross on the future career of developers.

On the other hand, the company-employer has the opportunity to choose the measure of punishment on its own - from a simple stigma to a very real term. The degree of financial and reputational losses inflicted on the company has a great influence on this decision - and if you can recover from the first, then you will forget about the second very soon.

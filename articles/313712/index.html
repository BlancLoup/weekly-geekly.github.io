Why does a person want to become an author?

And yet, why?


The main thing is motivation. It is characterized by two interesting things. The first is that which has some value for a person, which determines his movement. The second is the positive emotions received from the movement towards the goal, from its anticipation.

Habr's phenomenon can be explained by Maslow's pyramid. The American psychologist Abraham Maslow has divided all human needs into several basic components. At the first level, he arranged the physiological needs: breathing, food, drinking, sexual desire, sleep, etc. Just above is security - physical, moral, family safety, etc. A little higher - involvement, that is, friendship, family, love, involvement in a group of people. And just at this level Habr begins to meet the needs of the person. Users who have read only accounts can already read posts and realize their motivation: be involved in the community of IT professionals.



The next level of the pyramid is respect. That is, professional achievement, trust and recognition of success, gaining authority. To help realize this need can the ability to comment on posts on Habr√©. A person comments, someone responds to his comment, a discussion ensues, and this is how engagement is formed.

Finally, the uppermost part of the pyramid is creativity, that is, the publication of posts. By placing his post, a person realizes his highest need, the need for self-expression, receiving, in most cases, the respect of other people.



Habrahabr gives enough open space for the realization of all these motives. Unlike many other similar projects, on Habr√© there is a rating system and karma. Its presence in conjunction with the tools for implementing the needs described above largely explains why Habr is still very popular.

How I built a network in Astrakhan for 9 years


High water and base station container

Good morning! My name is Ilya, in 2002 I started as a base station maintenance engineer (then there were as many as 14 pieces on 10 sites), in 2004 I became the technical director of the branch, and now, from 2013, I have been heading Beeline throughout the region .


This is a typical suburb relief. There is no place to nest here, therefore the kestrel, the falcon, steppe eagles and buzzards-kurgannikas and smaller birds are very often found between high-voltage insulators.

Nature


Let's start with nature. The region halves the Volga, and for several months a year it overflows, spreading across the steppe. There are thousands of lakes and islands in the delta, which affects both the availability of the BS and the difficulties with power supply and high-quality transport channels.

In the summer we have a very strong heat (southern region of the Russian Federation), which requires good cooling of the equipment. We also have a lot of dust and midges tightly clogging the filters of air systems. And under cool containers, steppe animals like mice, gophers, and sometimes snakes love to wait out the day.

Heat


As soon as the power is turned off at the station, it warms up very quickly inside the container to +80. The first can not stand batteries. Then fails and more expensive equipment. Therefore, we are now installing free-cooling systems for such positions.


Inside one of the sites. This is a brick house, not a container.

Freeking in our region is practically the same as the open container door. +40 is not at all suitable for data centers, but it looks great in contrast with +80 without access from outside air. At the same time, in the season, the midges free-cooling filters for 2-3 days of work are compacted tightly with biomass, so at this time they are an emergency reserve. Base station "Sazaniy angle" is a vivid example.


Base station "Sazany angle".

Wind


The wind in Astrakhan always blows, and quite strongly. The steppe is as flat as a table, and the only heights are engineering structures like houses, bridges, and so on. Sometimes there is a particularly strong flow (they give a hurricane warning). He breaks everything that is fixed not thoroughly. The region is not the highest 3rd class wind. And although the design takes into account the weather conditions, we make every effort during the installation and operation of the BS to avoid emergency situations due to strong winds.

Also, the wind affects the sensation of temperature by the human body. In winter, due to strong wind and high humidity, ours is 5 as -15 in Moscow.

Geography




Volga region and coverage map

As you can see, the Volga divides both the city and the region into two parts. People live on both sides of the river. A relay radio link (RRL) punches well through it, but it has band limits. Taking into account the peculiarities of bogging down the floodplain, often from one radio relay node to another, the direct distance can be several kilometers, but you can get there by car over a bridge or a pontoon ferry some 300 kilometers upstream almost a day.

There are positions that can be reached only by boat. Of course, engineers undergo an additional course of behavior on the water as part of safety.


And here is the boat itself.

The population in the region is about a million, about half of it lives in the city of Astrakhan, there is a very high concentration of people in the north of the region (up to 10‚Äì12% of subscribers), the areas between these points are well inhabited. Considering this, it is worth understanding that before a failing BS or a power-off site, we can reach an average of 6-7 hours. Therefore, almost from the very beginning of the development of the network, we have made the structure of the transport network ring. The result was three large RRL-rings. The narrowest point is almost on the border of the region - 60 Mbit / s, which is enough for local residents to confidently surf 3G at speeds around megabits.

Optics



We realized the importance of high-quality transport channels from the very beginning, so we paid great attention to the construction of fiber optic links. At the moment, about 750 kilometers are in operation, of which about 150 fiber city fiber lines, and about 200 broadband (FttB). Now we are putting optics on the left side of the Volga to the cities of Kharabali, Akhtubinsk and Znamensk, calling at almost all stations, reserving this branch through Volgograd. It should provide our stations with fast and high-quality transport, and our subscribers with reliable and fast service.

In Astrakhan itself, most stations are connected via fiber optic links, which gives us the opportunity to provide our customers with access to packet data networks at high speeds.

We have the opportunity to earn in the market for the sale of services to local operators

Lexand Capella: a smartphone with Full HD-screen and support for two SIM-cards for 9,600 rubles ($ 280)

The smartphone Lexand Capella, which I will discuss in this review, belongs to the class of models with Full HD-screens, support for two SIM-cards and MediaTek chipsets. Such devices covered the domestic market with a whole wave in the last quarter of 2013. Today they are offered on average for 10 thousand rubles ($ 290). There are slightly cheaper options, and there are slightly more expensive ones. I admit honestly: among the smartphones of this type, the red / yellow Alcatel One Touch X is most attractive to me, but it does not have a memory card slot. There is also a version of this model with microSD support, but it can work with only one SIM card. Meanwhile, Lexand Capella has both, and the device costs 9,600 rubles ($ 280, average Yandex.Market price). Let's see what it is in practice. Looking ahead, I note that he has enough pros and cons.


Features from the Lexand brand site:

Multimedia

In the box with the Lexand Capella nothing special was found; everything was as usual - instructions, warranty, headset, cable, charger unit. Some smartphones of this brand are equipped with much more generous ones: for example, with the Antares and Callisto models, two back covers go at once, regular and in the form of a cover-book.

In the case of Lexand Capella, there is nothing to protect the screen - the cover here is ordinary. Meanwhile, the display is excellent: 5 inches, 1920 x 1080 pixels, IPS, and even with the use of OGS (One Glass Solution) technology. This means that the touch layer is one with the LCD panel, and this has a positive effect on image quality. Anyway, the picture is really impressive - and the naturalness of color reproduction, and margin of brightness, and viewing angles, which reach the cherished figure of 178 degrees. I remember the other day, at the request of a friend, I updated the firmware on the LG G2, so in this device for 25 thousand rubles the screen does not win the Lexand Capella display.



The coating of the front panel is glass, but the developers do not give any information about its origin. And this means that Capella does not use Gorilla Glass and Dragon Trail, but something less famous. Touchscreen capacitive, supported by 5 simultaneous touches. Sensitivity is high - at the level of the flagships of A-brands.

The case of the smartphone is not that it does not completely fit the screen, but, of course, it looks much simpler. The back cover, which comes on the side faces, is trimmed with a quite ordinary soft-touch. Not the cheapest, but not expensive either. The thickness of the case is relatively small - 8 mm. There are no special complaints about the three touch buttons under the display. Except that they have an uneven (albeit very bright) backlight - the upper parts of the icons are highlighted less than the lower ones.

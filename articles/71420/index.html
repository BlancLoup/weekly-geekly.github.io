Why pull-ups?


  • They are useful for a large number of muscles (muscles of the forearm, biceps of the shoulder, shoulder muscles, long head of the traps, pectoral and lats muscles, trapezius, serrated, round muscles of the back, posterior head of the deltoid and rhomboid muscles);
  • Perfect for people with poor posture and problems with the spine (and in fact many IT people have such problems);
  • Do not require a trip to the gym. The crossbar can be bought or found in any yard, on the sports ground
  • They improve well-being and tone the whole body;
  • Take a minimum of time (enough to allocate 5-10 minutes for training).


The main types of pull-ups



Straight grip


The most common option, but not the easiest.
Involved mainly muscles of the back, biceps, flexors of the forearm and shoulder muscles.
To do this, take the crossbar shoulder width, cross your legs (if it is more convenient) and try to touch the crossbar with your chest (or even your chin), then fully unbend and repeat the possible number of times.

Reverse grip


A lighter option, but no less common. I recommend starting with it.
The emphasis is on the biceps (which are more pumped up in beginners than the shoulder muscles, which are needed for a straight grip).

It is performed as well as a direct grip, only with palms facing you.

Wide grip


And this is the most difficult option presented. Difficult and the load on the muscles and execution.
Participates trapezoidal and latissimus dorsi.
Keep your hands at 10-20 cm from shoulder level (the farther, the harder). Pull up, trying to touch the crossbar breast, preferably look up.

Beginners in pulling up, I advise you to start with a reverse grip, gradually training and in direct. When reaching 3-4 pull-ups reverse grip, I advise you to try the broadest.

Types of horizontal bars


There are 2 types:

Door (inserted in the door opening)



Pros:
+ Cheaper;
+ Easier to install.

Minuses:
- Less convenient (taking into account the specifics of most buildings, there is a great chance to fill a lump on the head during pull-ups);
- No pens for wide grip.

Wall mounted (attached to the wall)


Pros:
+ There are handles for wide grip;
+ More convenient;
+ More reliable (usually mounted on 4-8 bolts, if the wall is capital, then you are unlikely to bring down it at any weight).

Minuses:
- Less cheap (the difference in price compared to the door, usually from 400 to 700 rubles)

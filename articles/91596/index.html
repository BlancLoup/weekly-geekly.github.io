Skolkovo will have zero taxes

The project coordinator for the Russian Silicon Valley, Viktor Vekselberg, gave an interview to the Vedomosti newspaper, in which he shared new details about this project.

The main news is that all the resident companies in Skolkovo will be exempt from taxes on profits, property, land, transport and VAT, and will also have lower rates of social contributions (only 14%) and preferential customs regime, which provides The number of duty-free import of equipment from abroad. Zero taxes for the company will be valid for ten years or until they reach the annual revenue of 3 billion rubles.

This is a really good incentive to move to a new office.

A non-profit foundation will be set up to manage the project, with Vekselberg himself becoming president. The founders will be the Russian Academy of Sciences, Rosnano, Vnesheconombank, the Russian Venture Company, the Foundation for Assistance to the Development of Small Enterprises in the Scientific and Technical Sphere, the owner of the land (the state through the Housing and Utilities Assistance Fund), and a non-profit organization formed by universities - MGTU named after Bauman, with the rest have not yet decided).

On the territory of 380 hectares there will be built premises for scientific research, postgraduate and doctoral studies, laboratories, housing, offices, kindergartens, schools, hospitals. Four car interchanges will appear. 4.6 billion rubles have already been allocated for this, and from 2011 the science city will be financed separately in the Russian budget.

The city will not be subordinate to any of the local authorities (that is, neither Moscow nor the Moscow region). That is, you can obtain permits for advertising and construction without the consent of the local authorities. The city itself will deal with utility costs, name the streets, there will be a special legal regime. Even the police, the Federal Migration Service, the Federal Tax Service and the Emergencies Ministry will most likely have their own special forces (special units that report directly to the head structures).

The first residents of the Russian Silicon Valley will take around the end of 2013.

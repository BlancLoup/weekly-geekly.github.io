Uber co-founder Travis Kalanik visited Moscow

For the first time in seven years of the existence of a private Uber taxi startup, the founder and head of the company Travis Kalanik visited Moscow. He spent a half-hour performance at the Strelka Institute on Red October, and then answered questions for another half hour.

Uber is an international company and an eponymous mobile application with which you can call and pay for private taxi services. Uber operates in dozens of countries, hundreds of cities and from month to month penetrates new markets. The popularity of the service is dictated by relatively low prices, including due to non-compliance with a number of regulations on the regulation of private carts. For the same reasons, in some countries conflicts arise with Uber.

Kalanik began the lecture with the weather: June, and in Moscow freezing. He mentioned that his college girl was Russian. Therefore, he knows a few words in Russian. In Russia, Uber operates in the capital and several million-plus cities. Uber still penetrates the new market, not all services are available. For example, there is no uberPOOL. This system combines passengers with similar directions, and then offers a joint trip. Each passenger only pays for his share of the journey. As Travis said , a critical mass of travel is required for uberPOOL. It is necessary to achieve a certain scale, and then it will be easy to find two people with similar standards. The rapid growth of Uber in the capital brings the volume to the desired goal. Kalanik said that uberPOOL will be launched in Moscow in less than a year.

The head of Uber raised the outlook for road transport. The land in the city is expensive, and in the parking lots it goes from 15 to 30%. Uber will not only help unload transport lines. Cashless payments, transfers to a bank account and cooperation with city authorities in matters of tax deductions are all part of the policy of returning the city to residents.

Hippocrates and IT. What is in common between them?


Problem


There are many decisions that we make in our lives. Some of them are rather insignificant, for example, what kind of clothes to wear in the morning or what kind of juice to buy in the store tonight. But there is one important decision that we cannot take on our own - the choice of which body to be born and live in. It happens that a person lives in a body that already has multiple ailments and inevitable diseases. Health condition is directly related to human life. That is why the topic of health and health care has always been and remains relevant. The industry needs constant reform and transformation.

Imagine that you do not need to wait a week to see your doctor, and then another week to get the results of medical research. With the constant changes in demographic trends, the growth of an aging population, the demand for health services is increasing. How can the health system be changed to better serve the population?

The solution is the development of technology, it is through them that a new section in medicine has appeared - digital healthcare ( digital healthcare ). The bottom line is the use of ICT in solving health problems.

We all love to compare something, and e-medicine is no exception. It is time to compare the level of development of domestic and electronic medicine, and of course, western.

Domestic projects and startups


It turns out that not everything is as bad as it actually is, and the development of telemedicine in our country began in parallel with the exploration of space. These two areas were related to the fact that in order to monitor the status of astronauts, a special biotelemetric system was developed, which allows remotely receiving information about the state of the body.

As for the present, over the past 2 years as much has happened in the field of telemedicine, how many has not been done in the past 20 years, unlike in foreign countries. Below are some of the domestic projects aimed at helping people and their health:

  • My gene is a service from Russian developers for analyzing human DNA.
  • 3D Bioprinting Solutions - the company is engaged in three-dimensional printing of organs and tissues of the human body.
  • The information doctor is a service that is able to connect the patient and the doctor of private Moscow clinics.
  • The life button is a project that offers a medical alarm system - emergency call assistance for the elderly and disabled.
  • iHematologist - a system that allows you to decipher a blood test.

Foreign projects and startups


Telemedicine in the West, namely in the EU and the USA, has existed and has been operating for 30 years, which, frankly, is hard to believe! The history of telemedicine projects within the EU dates back to 1985. The most important criteria for project evaluation were the standards and convenience of the systems built in use in all EU countries.
The following are projects created by foreign developers who are already helping people monitor and support their health:

  • Artemis Health - Employee Health Analytics for Employers.
  • MediQuire - data analytics for medical institutions.
  • Touch Surgery - surgery solutions using 3D modeling and augmented reality.
  • Oscar Health is a portal for convenient health insurance.
  • MediSafe is a service aimed at tracking drug intake.
  • MedWand is a service that allows you to save patients from waiting at the offices, providing the opportunity to quickly get to the virtual reception.

Legislative consolidation in the West


Legal regulation of telemedicine in Western countries is not uncommon and has been practiced for a long time. As you can guess, one of the most legal states is the United States , which has developed its own laws for telemedicine. Formation of laws in the field of telemedicine was the creation of the following laws:

  • The Balanced Budget Act of 1997;
  • The Benefits Improvement and Protection Act of 2000;
  • The Law on Health Information Protection Technologies in Economics and Medicine (HITECH Act) of 2009.

It is also worth noting that in different states of the country their own, separate laws regulating telemedicine are approved. Thus, the Minnesota law of 1999 states that among the technical tools used in television consultations, interactive videos and other latest technologies can be used.

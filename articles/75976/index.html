Google Navigation works all over the world ... thanks to a small hack ;-)

Many of you have heard that a couple of weeks ago, Google introduced its program for Navigation.
Who has not seen, here is a link to the video
But there was one problem with this program ... It worked only in the USA.
Probably, licenses for maps purchased by Google did not allow to use navigation in other countries.
(in the US, Google already has its cards ;-))
But, as we know, the world is not without good people: Thanks to their efforts, Navigation works all over the world, including in our countries, he just rode with her around Kiev:

Under the cat, how to achieve this on your Android phone
PS The original of this manual is taken from the xda-developers forum.

Initial requirements:
1. HTC Dream or HTC Magic
2. Firmware based on Android 1.6 (Donut) (tested on the latest version of the cyanogen 4.2.5 build)
3. Third-party recovery such as Amon's or Cyanogen (there is on the same XDA for different phone models)
4. Brains
5. Here is this file

What need to do:

1. Download this file here (for Cyanogen assemblies) Mirror on the People
2. Put it in the root of the SD card
3. Reboot to recovery (when loading, press Home + Turn on)
3. Select the chose update.zip item (if there is no such item, rename the file to update.zip)
4. Select update_navigation.zip
5. Apply update (for this you need to confirm the choice by pressing the Home button)
6. Restart the phone
7. Click Settings-Speech Synthesis-Install Voice Data or download Speech Synthesis Data from the market.
8. Run Maps
9. Choose Directions
10. Indicate (using points on the map) 2 any addresses in the United States and click Go-
As a result, the menu item Navigate should appear in the menu.
11. Download NavLauncher from the market

12. Run this program, enter the desired address

and voila, you have navigation in your city (if of course it is in Google maps ;-))

Proven for yourself, works well.
Among the shortcomings, voice guidance is only in English, German, French, Italian, Spanish. Russian may appear in the next versions of Speech Synthesis ...
+ NavLauncher program while in its embryonic state-written by enthusiasts from the forum ;-)
Good luck in your navigation!

"Hard time management." We talk about the book, Dan Kennedy



What needs to be done to take control of your life and business? Take radical measures and master hard time management.

We talk about the main ideas from the book by Dan Kennedy "Hard time management."


main idea


Dan Kennedy addresses his book to ever-busy, inventive and active people and entrepreneurs, hurrying through life, who, unfortunately, are too often surrounded by whiners, time-sucking vampires and lazy people. To finally learn to do everything, you need to take radical measures and master hard time management.


Dan Kennedy argues that the main secret of personal and financial success "depends on how cleverly you use your time and how much you let others steal from you."

Figure that will change your life


The author questions the method of calculating the cost of each of your working hours, which is firmly established in the practice of many efficiency gurus and business trainers.


According to Dan Kennedy, all this is a bullshit, since the calculations are made on the assumption that a person works 8 hours a day, which in principle does not happen. So, according to a survey of five hundred general directors by Fortune Publishing House, it turned out that their average productive time is only 28 minutes a day.


Do you do the most important work during the whole working time? Very, very unlikely. Most likely, you also have to be distracted, and most of the work time is eaten by completely unproductive activities. Therefore, in order to calculate the cost of your time, you need to focus not on the working hour, but on another concept ‚Äî productive or paid hours. How to calculate it?


Suppose you are productive three hours a day. Multiply three hours by the number of working days per year. Then, the amount you want to receive per year (or which you already receive if it suits you) is divided by the calculated number of productive hours per year. This will be the cost of your productive hour. If you divide this amount by 60, then you get the cost per minute of your productive time.


Dan Kennedy strongly recommends that you always keep this figure in mind. Ask yourself the question - is what I am doing now worth this amount and should I continue to do it? This approach will immediately eliminate half of unnecessary cases.


The second plus of the method is that it helps to measure how much time other people take from you: how much will a telephone conversation lasting several minutes, travel time, aimless wandering around the Internet. This method makes you think in terms of "investment" and "cost." Helps to count everything that happens in your life.


Interestingly, according to the observations of Dan Kennedy, 8 out of 10 entrepreneurs who he asks a question about the amount of the desired basic earnings can not answer it. That is, they do not know how much they will pay themselves.


Why is it so important to determine this desired base earnings?


As the author notes, any entrepreneur needs to remember and not to forget why his business exists - in order to make his owner rich. If you think that the above calculations are too approximate, then even an approximate figure is still better than not having any reference at all.


Knowing the value of a minute of your productive time changes your life.


Ruthless truth number 1: If you yourself do not know the price of your time, do not expect that it will be appreciated by others.

Indeed, meetings are a great way to demonstrate your own importance without taking any decisions or taking responsibility for them.


Is there a remedy against meetings? First, ask yourself whether you need to be at all and whether the issue can be resolved by some alternative option.


If you need a meeting, here are a few stakes that you can use to stop the endless vampire "bloody sit-rounds":

Parallella board sales from Adapteva resumed.

Hello.



On Thursday, June 26, Adapteva sent to everyone about the resumption of sales of their open source Parallella board. The sales themselves began yesterday. On Habr√© previously mentioned, for example, here habrahabr.ru/post/168897 .



Let me remind you that the Parallella board is a high-performance motherboard based on the SoC Xilinx ZYNQ 7010 (or ZYNQ 7020), as well as the Adapteva Epiphany III with 16 cores. The first crystal contains two ARM Cortex-A9 and FPGA Artix-7 cores, plus a bunch of peripheral controllers (Ethernet, USB 2.0, NAND, SPI, I2C, etc.). The second chip is a proprietary Adapteva. The board is designed for computing and is designed to show the full power of the interaction of ARM, FPGA and DSP cores. Successfully passed Kickstarter.

The board itself has several modifications, depending on the ZYNQ 7010 or ZYNQ 7020, as well as in the presence or absence of GPIO outputs. So there are only three options. Information at www.parallella.org/board .

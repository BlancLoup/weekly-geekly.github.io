By 2022, the volume of government orders of Russian enterprises of microelectronics will reach 105 billion rubles a year

As a result of the meeting on the development of microelectronics, chaired by President Vladimir Putin, by 2022, it was decided to provide Russian microelectronics enterprises with state orders for 105 billion rubles a year.

So far, domestic manufacturers account for about 16% of the civilian microelectronics market. Strengthen the position of Russian manufacturers, according to the president, will be able to consolidate the state order. State orders should increase ‚Äúin those areas where government agencies act as a regulator and are able to produce high demand. Coordination of this large-scale order should be carried out at the government level in the profile commission on the use of information technologies, ‚Äùthe president stressed.

He believes that the dominance of foreign manufacturers is fraught with risks. In some cases, the reliability of imports of components and equipment suffers.

Almost all ministries and departments are participating in the state order, the Minister of Industry and Trade Denis Manturov told Interfax. Russian companies will produce components for ID cards under the program of the Federal Migration Service, for a military badge, as well as RFID tags that will be put on drugs, gives examples of Manturov.

The state will also order components for the Mir cards of the National Payment Card System (NSPK), which will be produced by the Russian Mikron and Angstrom plants. Yesterday at the meeting the president gave the appropriate instructions. Commercial banks will need about 30 million cards a year, Manturov said. Component manufacturers will be able to reach this volume in 2017, and in the first two years they will produce from 7 to 20 million chips per year.

Mikron and Angstrom plants will also receive assistance from the state. First, the state will allocate up to 2 billion rubles to subsidize interest rates on loans of enterprises, which they will take to replenish working capital, he knows. Secondly, Vnesheconombank ( VEB ) can support these plants with loans, for which no budget money is needed. The total need of Mikron and Angstrem for loans from VEB is 25 billion rubles.

The President of the Elvis Group of Companies, Yaroslav Petrichkovich, who participated in the meeting, added that computer equipment will also be included in the state order - personal computers, servers, mobile devices, which will be purchased by state bodies.

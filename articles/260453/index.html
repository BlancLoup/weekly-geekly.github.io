SAM solutions - complete order in IT infrastructure

The farther we go into the 21st century, the more intense the growth processes of the information field and technical infrastructure become. If literally 5-7 years ago, many tasks in companies were solved in the old fashioned way, and even large organizations could come across ‚Äúgranary books‚Äù, today automation has penetrated into all areas: from personnel service to production department. As soon as a decision is made to automate any process (choice of CRM, corporate portal, workflow system, billing system or integrated automation solution), even a quick glance at the search engine and access to specialized sites causes panic. And this is natural: along with the problem of choice, a number of issues arise, without which automation will be reduced to the most terrible finale - software developed for serious money will become non-functioning ballast, and the processes will remain beyond automation.



Who does not risk, that ...


... doing the right thing. Corporate business is not the best place for risks. And they necessarily arise during the implementation of any software. Here is just a small list of risks faced by businesses at any level.

  • Before implementation, there is no internal audit of business processes and no needs are collected. This risk entails many problems, one of which is the selection of completely inappropriate software chosen either by the opinion of a narrow number of employees or under the pressure of the manufacturer‚Äôs marketing activity.

  • The physical infrastructure does not match the technical requirements of software solutions. Simply put, not every computer and server can correctly work with modern business solutions. Buying expensive "hardware" in this case is not a panacea, since it is possible to save a lot, for example, by deploying part of the corporate infrastructure in the cloud.

  • Employee sabotage is, paradoxically, an extremely common and dangerous risk. After the deployment of infrastructure, especially in the absence of training or lack of internal expertise, employees can simply ignore the work with the system, especially if it is not vital for the company (such as billing). And if this was not carried out a complete transition from the old platform and / or data is replicated on two systems - consider everything, the majority will work in the usual old interface.

  • The greed of top managers. It is not a secret for anybody that the system of kickbacks is flourishing in Russia - the manager‚Äôs shares for guaranteeing completion of the transaction. It is extremely difficult to get around this situation, so it‚Äôs worthwhile to turn to external independent expertise to avoid making a biased decision.

  • The risk of prolonged introduction in time. Self-implementation can take a lot of time. There are many peak times when there are strong delays:

    - the stage of collecting requirements and creating technical specifications - non-professional work negates the attempts of technical specialists to create a unified document. In addition, there is always a certain misunderstanding between specialists due to the nature of the activity.
    - the stage of adaptation of technical specialists to the new IT infrastructure.
    - the stage of training, at which both the center of expertise and the initial knowledge about the implemented software of employees are simultaneously formed.

  • The presence of the need to refine and adapt software for specific business needs. This risk forms either a rejection of a certain functionality or throws a project almost to the starting stage, resuming the whole chain of problems.

As you can see, even basic risks can paralyze the work of a company and level any achievements in building an IT infrastructure. And if we take into account industry, qualification and legal risks, the situation can absolutely get out of control.

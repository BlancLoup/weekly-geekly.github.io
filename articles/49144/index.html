Steve Jobs leaves Apple before the end of June.

There was information that Steve made the decision to leave Apple due to health problems.
But not for long. He will be back at the end of June.



The founder of Apple today sent the following letter to the company's employees:

The team, I am sure that all of you last week saw my letter, in which I told something personal. Unfortunately, taking care of my own health continues to distract me, not only from personal affairs and family, but also from Apple itself. In addition to all this, last week I found out that my health problems are much worse than I thought.

In order to focus on my health and give the rest of Apple employees the opportunity to focus on developing extraordinary products, I decided to take sick leave until the end of June.

I asked Tim Cook to look after the company, and I know very well that he and other top executives of the company know their business. As president of the company, I plan to participate in making the most important decisions while I am not. Our board of directors fully supported my plan.

See you in summer.

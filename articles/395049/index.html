Office and home projectors - what's the difference?

Some time ago, the team of the ProjectorWorld.ru site became interested in the question: how do home projectors differ from office (universal) projectors?

To understand, they took two of our projectors for testing: a home Epson EH-TW5350 with a resolution of 1080p and an office Epson EB-U32 with a resolution of WUXGA. During testing, it was found that these models are similar in many respects, so the authors decided to get to the bottom of the differences, and prepared a curious video about how home and office projectors can differ on the example of these two models:



A text version of the review is available at this address .

The authors draw attention to 10 major differences:

  1. Screen format (16: 9 or 16:10)
  2. Network projection features
  3. The presence of USB-connectors and their purpose
  4. Gaming performance and 3D mode
  5. Portability and weight
  6. Lamp life, noise level and features of economical operation mode
  7. Subtlety color rendition
  8. Feature of the "Presentation" mode in Epson EB-U32
  9. Frame Interpolation Feature for Epson EH-TW5350
  10. Comparison of speakers built into projectors

The author emphasizes that the findings should not be extended to all office and home projectors, but it is always helpful to have these 10 points in mind when choosing between projectors of two different specializations.

From our side, we add that both projectors are not the most budget in their groups, therefore they have advanced functionality. For example, the standard model Epson EH-TW5300 , unlike the Epson EH-TW5350 , does not have built-in support for Miracast, and Epson EB-U04 , the ‚Äúlittle brother,‚Äù Epson EB-U32 supports the wireless network only after installing the optional USB module.

Another example is the built-in sound. Indeed, the dynamics of home projectors are still better suited for watching movies (as far as possible for the built-in sound in the projector). But here, too, everything is individual. For example, the Epson EH-TW6600 projector, designed for living rooms, has more powerful 2 √ó 10 W stereo speakers, but the Epson EH-TW7200 , which is a home theater projector, does not have any built-in sound at all, as older models assume that home theater certainly has a separate speaker system.

As for office projectors, Epson has a separate line of projectors for education, the difference of which is in the presence of more powerful than Epson EB-U32, dynamics (up to 16 watts), and additional audio inputs and outputs, as well as through VGA-in / output for transmitting the signal through the chain to the next device. In general, home and universal projectors are very similar in many respects, but they are distinguished by additional functions and capabilities that determine their specialization.

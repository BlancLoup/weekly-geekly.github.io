Tesla plans to install new massive stationary batteries in California


A source

In the summer of 2018, Tesla became one of 4 companies that will provide California with a stationary rechargeable battery, the total amount of which will be 2.27 GWh. At the first stage, Tesla got a volume of 730 MW * h (the power of 182.5 MW needs to be given for 4 hours), and in the future it can expand to 1.2 GW * h.


Project Document

For comparison, the largest Tesla station was installed in Australia with a total energy reserve of 129 MW * h. Initially, it was assumed that Tesla would install its batteries (Powerpack 2), which would take more than 3 thousand (1 battery will store 210 kW * h), and this is only for the 1st stage.

At the disposal of the electrician got exclusive information, thanks to which it became known that in the project for California Tesla will use new batteries, which will be multiple of the current Powerpack 2 batteries.



At present, Tesla calls such Megapack batteries, from the name it can be understood that the energy reserve must be at least 1 MW * h. The physical dimensions of such a battery: 7.14 m by 1.6 m (LxW).



According to Tesla's plan, they will install 449 Megapacks for the 1.2 GWh project, which means that 2673 kWh for each battery will turn out. It is more than 12 times larger than 1 Powerpack 2 battery, but takes up less space. On the same area that takes 1 Megapack battery, only 8 Powerpack 2 batteries fit.

Time management


  1. Decide what you need this or that social network. If to hang out - delete the account. Save from 300 hours per year (almost 2 months of fruitful work with an 8-hour working day). Well, or try to set aside for your Facebook two hours on Sunday evening. Nothing to lose.
  2. Check email no more than once per hour. Save on it 50 more hours a year.
  3. Remember, you spend 2 hours a day smoking, using 1 cigarette per hour. 700 hours per year. I did not quit smoking myself, but began to do it less often and only in those moments when it does not distract me from work: at the wheel, for example.
  4. Limit phone calls to a minimum. Learn to formulate concise phrases for conversation. Free up another 150 hours a year.


Total we get 1200 hours per year, or 3.5 hours per day of free time. You can convert it into your own projects, or multiply by the cost of man-hours of your profile specialist and gasp in surprise.

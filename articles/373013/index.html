Winter sky for the New Year holidays

Part of the free time of the New Year holidays is to be allocated to the starry sky above us. There, as always, it will be interesting - a parade of planets, a small meteor shower and beautiful non-astronomical phenomena.


Photo of user Upsilon Astroforum

Two astronomical paradoxes


Before moving on to the beauties, you have to disappoint you a little. If you think that after the day of the winter solstice morning comes already earlier, then for the territory of Russia it is not so. Already many have managed to get bored to go to work in the dark, but until the end of December the Sun will rise later. Yes, the light day is increasing, but this is due to the late sunset. Here, for example, the schedule for Ufa:



If you play with resources showing the time of sunrise and sunset, it is clear that the situation in the southern hemisphere is the opposite - there the Sun begins to rise all later from the beginning of the month.

The second paradox is that the Earth passes its pericenter in 2017 on the evening of January 4 and at that moment will be closest to the Sun. What is so cold, you ask, and I remind you that because of the almost circular orbit, the Earth at perihelion (the closest point to the Sun) receives only 7% more solar energy than aphelion (farthest), and the change of seasons determines mainly the slope of the axis of rotation of the Earth.

Parade of planets


In the evening sky of the end of December, five planets will line up in a line, but only two of them will be visible to the naked eye. Comets


Now in the sky there is not a single comet visible to the eye. But in the telescope you can see the comet 45P / Honda-Mrkos-Paidushakova.


Photo taken from the page of the AstroAlert group "Observational astronomy"

With comets, there is little hope for February, when the same comet 45P / Honda-Mrkosa-Paidushakova passes just 0.08 astronomical unit (12 million kilometers) from Earth and, if lucky, can become visible through binoculars or even with the naked eye.

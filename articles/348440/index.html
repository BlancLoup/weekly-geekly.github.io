How to hold a tender for the development of the site

More and more often we are invited to take part in the tender for the development of the site. And the lion's share of requests look like this: ‚ÄúHow much is the development of such a site: fb.com. The letter has been sent to 69 web studios. Therefore, I decided to share my experience in tenders. And I have experience. Since 2010 I have been working on the side of the customer: Medical equipment, pharmaceuticals, tourism. Now I am engaged in marketing and development of IT4U Web Studio and Couloirs Tourist Club. I know about tenders and participation in them not by hearsay from different sides. In this article you will read what is really worth paying attention to, and what can be neglected.


Tenders - shit


It is this opinion about them that most site developers. Often this is true, sometimes due to lack of experience of participation and ignorance of the specifics of customers, their procedures.


This opinion is based on the following beliefs:


  • Tenders purchased - ‚Äúit is impossible to win in them, we participated in three and everywhere received a formal reply or silence in response.‚Äù
  • To prepare a decent commercial offer you need to spend a few dozen hours of specialists. If the amount of the cost of preparation for all tenders, for example, for a quarter divided by the number of won, can be a very decent amount, comparable to the profit of won. Studios need to think whether to participate in the tender.
  • The result is unpredictable. So it is, when in the tender it is not clear how many participants, it is not clear who they are and it is not clear by what principle the winner is chosen.
  • Not all studios agree to take part in tenders. Someone does not want to spend their resources on this, someone does not pass on the qualification requirements, someone is not profitable financially.
  • Often there come really inadequate requests that seem to come from a serious company, and it is formulated as if the site is making a pie.

Types of tenders


Open tenders - the customer announces a tender and everyone can participate in it. The tender is announced on tender platforms, on Facebook or in mass distribution to all web studios. The tool is not important.


Closed tenders - the customer company chooses suitable developers for the portfolio and price in order to develop its website. Usually up to 10 web studios. Such a tender is more effective.


False tenders - when the company announces a tender for which the site will not be developed or the developer has already been selected in advance and the tender needs a formal one.


I will say one thing - in closed tenders with a public list of participants, contractors will express a greater desire to participate.


To ensure that your tender is not considered a fake - invite the company in a personal letter, describe in as much detail as possible what result you want to get and what task you are solving. Before you decide on the criteria for choosing a winner. Give enough time for the preparation of proposals. If there are specific or redundant requirements for the contractor or project - explain why they are.


Who to invite to the tender for the development of the site?


Before announcing a tender, be sure to decide what you want to get at the exit. Choose developers according to the desired result. Each company specializes in something different. Here is the beginning of the list:


  • Designers - specialization of the company: creative, design, visual, wow effects.
  • Techies - good quality code, complex systems, integration.
  • Marketers - strategy, numbers, analytics, product development and packaging.
  • SEO - website promotion in organic delivery.
  • SMMschiki - brand promotion in social networks.

No need to reduce techies with marketers in one tender and select the best for creativity. If you need a prestigious one-page website - choose companies that are strong in design. If a project with a complex functionality, a variety of functions - techies. Well, you understand


Sometimes, if quality is more important than time and money, strategy, analytics, design are ordered from some, design from others, and development from third. So the quality will really be better. But the timing and coordination efforts are multiplied by 3.


Another filter companies that need to be invited - the quality of the portfolio. You should like their work. Look at the work over the past few months.


Well, where do without the price. Identify 2 digits. The first is the average price of a similar site on the market, the second is the maximum budget you are willing to spend on a project. Add this range to the tender description in order to weed out companies that you cannot afford on the one hand and those who do not reach your level on the other. Also, this range will show companies how much to ‚Äúaccelerate‚Äù in commercial offers. Fantasy developers have enough, and it needs to be limited.


Do not hide the list of bidders, invite to tender up to 6 companies with the same specialization and in the same price range. Thus, you will receive a sufficient number of quality offers from which you can make a choice.

When you work with a contractor, you must use two approaches, emotional and technological. Emotional approach is needed by the owner of the company - to evaluate the contractor from the position of raport, he understands our essence or does not understand. I do not spend time on persuasion, I choose those who want to work with us and love us as a client. And this is enough for a decision.

But then your employees come into the business, and here they need all the Technical Tasks, all the details and rules, so that no one, neither the customer nor the contractor, interferes with each other‚Äôs work, does not disrupt each other‚Äôs deadlines, etc. Therefore, prepare technical documents in advance with an accurate description of the criteria, results and deadlines is a necessity. And when there is an understanding of the process - the result is always better and predictable.

Data on the project in the form of a brief prepared in advance


Prepare the required minimum in the tender description:


Requirements for the contractor - I would focus on the team. But sometimes there really are requirements for the contractor‚Äôs company. Believe me, the company's experience and the level of the portfolio affect the probability of a successful project much more than the presence of a legal entity and the status of a VAT payer.


Project requirements are a very important point. Describe what you want to see as a result. What process is acceptable for you. Write only what really matters to you. Each item potentially increases the time and cost of the project. And just complicates the assessment.


Fork cost - about it already wrote above.


Contact the responsible person for clarifying questions - email is a great way to communicate, it is enough. If you prefer to talk on the phone, you can add it. In any case, ask the participants to summarize the arrangements by mail.


Terms of Reference for the development of the site. This is a separate item. If you are not strong in the technical part, do not write TK. Describe in detail your problems, tasks that you want to solve. From them will be formed a proposal for the development of the site.


Public answers to questions. An interesting item that will allow participants to get the same information. You can make a Google doc with questions from the participants and your answers to them. On the one hand, this will reduce the number of identical questions, on the other - everyone will have the same information.


Most likely, each company will send you a brief. No need to fill everything, they are about the same. Fill out the first one and place it in the question paper. Let other companies read the answers there. And if there is a question for which there is no answer - let it be this question that is asked. Minimum respect for you


Give enough time to prepare the proposal. At least 1-2 weeks. This does not mean that participants will prepare a proposal for 2 weeks. They have ongoing projects and they do not have to leave them to prepare a proposal.


If you want bidders to develop a site to prepare layouts, write terms of reference or requirements, deploy a test site - be prepared that some will be asked to pay for these works. This is normal and occurs more and more often. Some companies do not prepare layouts at all. I think this is the right position because to draw a creative for a tender is one thing and to draw a design based on analytics, product, market, taking into account user behavior scenarios is a big piece of work. At best, the creative that was chosen will go into the trash and it will be replaced by high-quality work. At worst, you will have to get attached to it and try to pull out what really makes the project quality. If you need a visual component to select a contractor - see examples of previous work. Or give a small task by which you can assess the level of design.

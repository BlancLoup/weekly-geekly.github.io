World of Warcraft: Now eSports?

Ignore will write until I open here.

The long-suffering and well-known World of Warcraft has all the chances to become another cybersport discipline. Well, or in extreme cases, to gain additional popularity. Whereby? Naturally money.

Blizzard announced the imminent launch of special championship servers. Since April, anyone can pay the N-th sum of money, and take part in mass battles in the arena in the format of 3 to 3. The total prize fund of more than 200 thousand ever-killed raccoons. The team that won first place gets 75 thousand.

Under the cut details.
  • They will be created exclusively for the arena of battles in 3 by 3 format.
  • Characters created on them will initially have the maximum lvl and the best PvP equipment at the moment.
  • For the game on these servers, players will have to pay extra (15 euros for Europe, $ 20 for the US)
  • Qualifying fights will begin in April and will consist of 2 rounds, each 6 weeks long and a prize pool of $ 27,000
  • The winners of the regional championships will go to the international final with a prize fund of $ 120,000 and a grand prize of 75,000 thousand, respectively.
  • All this will take place abstracted from real riem
  • Each team wishing to participate will have to play at least 200 matches in order to be able to reach the final.

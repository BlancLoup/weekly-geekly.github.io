What happened to the site of the Skolkovo Foundation in brief



It all started with the post of habrauser oyaso , in which he compared the amount of money pledged to develop the site of the Skolkovo Innovation Fund and the result obtained in the form of the site i-gorod.com

The information below is recommended for the reading of journalists, assistants of all kinds of leaders of various ranks and those who are too lazy to read interesting comments on the original post.


In a matter of hours, the topic has attracted great attention from both the habrosoobschestva and the Internet as a whole. It quickly became clear that:
  • The site was assembled hastily on the UMI content management system, to which a cheap template was bought without copyright redemption (i.e. it can be reused by anyone).
  • The site was hosted on the old version of the server software without updates, vulnerable to attacks and launched on the Apache web server not adapted to high loads.

This led to the expected result, which is called " Habraeffekt ". This is when the transitions of habrahabr.ru readers through an interesting link lead to an overload and crash of the mentioned server.

However, the habra effect was just the beginning. Inquisitive researchers found holes in the site through which source codes were obtained, as well as passwords to the administrative panel and database. Be careful, don't die of laughter! Admin password: 1

Clones of the site i-gorod.com began to appear in the network with edits that were ingenious and not very good, and the source codes are available to everyone.

Another discovery was that the corporate mail of the site was posted on gmail.com . Yes, on that very enemy gmail, which one high-ranking FSB officer recently wanted to ban. I like gmail because 50 email accounts can be opened there absolutely free. Apparently he liked the Skolkovo Foundation the same.

The prospective artist of the site was found very quickly; it turned out to be a habraiser as well. Small world, alas. The poor fellow instantly threw cons into karma. I think that is not entirely fair, maybe he worked on the amount paid?

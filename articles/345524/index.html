How the concept helps not to get stuck in the development of a new product for years

Dodo Pizza is developed in the USA. In mid-2016, we opened a delivery in Oxford and quickly realized that customer preferences in the United States and Russia are different. Americans are individualists. They are used to making pizza from any ingredients: even bacon with chocolate sauce. Therefore, we had to make such functionality for the American Dodo. We called the project "Own Pizza".


The developers said that the task will take at least six months. But it is too long, the business would lose money. We had to speed up and make the product in a couple of months. But the task looked huge, no one knew how to approach it.


To understand, we used the stage. This tool helped to plan the work and launch ‚ÄúYour pizza‚Äù in 2.5 months. In the article we tell how to use the construction.


What should have been done


In order for customers to collect pizza from ingredients, it was necessary to change the Dodo IP:


  • set up the admin panel: what ingredients can be added to the pizza, how to group them and at what price to sell;
  • make a pizza designer and change all applications so that the client can create pizza on the website, via call center, mobile application or in a restaurant;
  • show in tracking the ingredients that the customer chooses. Tracking is a tablet that hangs in the kitchen and shows the pizza maker the ingredients from which he must collect pizza;
  • figure out how to distinguish pizzas with different ingredients after cooking. For example, in one pizza cheese and garlic, in the other - only garlic. When they come out of the oven, look the same. So, you need another tablet "Furnace", which will show in what order the pizza got into the oven. In the same order they will be packaged;
  • change the accounting system. We used to write off ingredients according to the recipe. For example, a pizza needs a glass of cheese, 100 grams of pepperoni, 100 ml of sauce. As soon as the pizza left the customer, the ingredients in the recipe were written off. At each restaurant, staff saw the remnants of products in the system and learned when to buy new ingredients. With ‚ÄúHis pizza‚Äù, the client could put anything. Therefore, it was necessary to make the system take the data not from the recipe, but from the order;
  • show the ingredients in the check to the customer and the delivery note to the courier;
  • Add reports and statistics to understand which pizzas and with which ingredients are most often ordered.

Separating the important from the unimportant helped the stage.


What is a stageime


Storimp is a card board that helps you tell stories about how users interact with a product. Stories are also called scripts. This tool was suggested by Jeff Patton in the book User Story Mapping .


Storymap helps you break a complex scenario into many simple stories, visualize the amount of work and plan what to do and when. Developers do not cling to their heads when they are told that in two months they have to do a great job. According to the concept, they understand what is needed to launch the product, and that while it can be postponed.


We use STAMP in all our tasks. We take cards with stories and place them on the board:


  • on the sequence of actions: early to the left, later - to the right;
  • by priority: the higher the priority of change, the higher the card;
  • if the card has details, we hang the more general card above, and under it - details, additional actions;
  • if something can be done in several ways, one is at the top and the other options are under it.

Why don't we use online tools


Storimep is a communication tool, so wall and stickers are best suited for it. They are convenient to create, throw out, add. You can point your finger at them.
Online solutions are a bad option, with them the growth will not work. The board is needed to stand around it and discuss. This is the most valuable tool. In the online communication tool does not work. One will move the cards, others will look or be distracted.


Even if the team works in different cities - this is no excuse to start an online board. Our business analyst Polina lives and works in Syktyvkar. During the discussion of "His pizza" she flew to us, and together we built the stage. And then in her Syktyvkar she made herself the same, only small:


If we changed something in the stage, we immediately transferred it to Polina, and she changed it at home.
After solving the problem, the board can be photographed, digitized, put in CRM - let it lie for history. But as a working tool - only the board and stickers.

Cheat Sheet


When it is impossible to do without the stumen:


  • developers put a period of several months, but business cannot wait, and developers do not believe that it can be done faster;
  • the task is so big that the developers and team leaders do not know how to approach it;
  • to develop a product you need to spend a lot of simultaneous changes, and it is not clear which are needed immediately and which can be postponed.

Here are our rules for working with the concept:


  • Use real whiteboard and stickers, no online tools. Storimp is needed to stand around the board, discuss the problem and move the stickers;
  • discuss livetime live. If employees work remotely, get together. Then each will recreate the board in his;
  • at each step, ask the question "What do you need to do this?" and then "You can do without what?";
  • make and test a minimally finished product, even if it cannot be shown to the client. Only after this make improvements.

What you need to quickly launch a product? What can I do without? - two questions, which helps understand the concept

Three new smartphones and a Sony Xperia tablet

Yesterday during the Sony presentation at IFA 2012 in Berlin, three new Sony smartphones on the Android platform and the first tablet in the Xperia Tablet lineup were presented:



Sony Xperia TX and its modification Xperia T is the company's new flagship smartphone with an HD screen, a camera and one-touch file sending features using NFC technology.

Sony Xperia V has many features Xperia TX, but a little more compact, supports the LTE standard and has a high-quality waterproof case.

Sony Xperia J boasts a spectacular design and a very large screen. This model is designed for consumers who need a stylish device at an affordable price.

Sony Xperia Tablet S - the first tablet in the line of Xperia devices.

When creating the Xperia TX, Xperia T, Xperia V and Xperia J models, the same design concept was used, which was first implemented in the Xperia arc and Xperia arc S models - a combination of a large screen and a thin concave body, which is especially noticeable when viewed in profile:

One-touch content sending functions


The one-touch content sending mechanism for Sony smartphones is based on NFC technology. In this sense, technology finds new application, going beyond traditional areas such as e-tickets and payments. Now users can transfer music and images between different Sony devices with NFC technology - all you need to do is touch one device to another. For example, using NFC technology, Xperia TX users, Xperia T and Xperia V can stream music content to the new Sony SRS-BTV5 speaker system, which was also presented yesterday at IFA.

All gadgets will appear on the shelves in the fourth quarter of 2012.

Used server pricing


At what point is the price of the server lost? How much? When it makes sense to upgrade the server park? Who can sell your old servers?

3 steps to reduce server cost


  1. New model range.

    There are two peaks in server price loss. Like the car, the server loses in price at the time of purchase. Further, a serious decline occurs at the time of the release of a new line of servers. Rulers come out with a certain periodicity, therefore, it is enough to simply predict this price drop.

    Consider the three brand leaders of the market - this is IBM (Lenovo), Hewlett Packard and Dell. So, the HP lineup is the G1, G2, and so on up to Gen9, the last lineup that was released last September. You already know that, as a rule, HP has updates every two years and, most often, in September. This means that the next HP lineup will be released next year.

    Last September, when the HP Gen9 lineup came out, the price of the previous lineup dropped dramatically. Accordingly, next September there will be another fall.

  2. Seasonality


    Falling prices for servers can be predicted for the upcoming season.

    The cost of server hardware varies with the seasons. Demand grows from September to about the second decade of December, and by the end of December stagnation begins, which continues in January, February and all summer months, when it is almost impossible to sell a used server, as well as the demand for new servers subsides.

    You already know that these fluctuations are due to the fact that by the end of the year, distributed budgets are very seriously spent. So, tenders are won, government money is spent heavily in the last three months, and, as a result, contracting companies earn and upgrade their IT infrastructure.

  3. Warranty end

    When the official warranty ends, the price of the server drops again. Usually the warranty lasts from one year to three years (from IBM, HP and Dell).

Reduced server prices in numbers


Up to -30% upon purchase

Why? Most often they buy from distributors who sell servers with a margin of 5 to 10% (retail). Trading margin can be up to 15%. From now on, used equipment becomes more difficult to sell, because it is in fact already used, and another 10-15% of the cost is lost on this. This is the minimum drop.

Up to -50% in a year

After a year, the entire server equipment will end on-site service warranty. Warranty service - a significant piece of the cost of the cost of equipment. Thus, about 20% of the price is lost. The result: up to -50% of the price in a year. This is also the minimum drop.

Up to 65% with the release of the new line

Depending on at what point in the cycle of new lines, a server was purchased, two years or a year after its purchase, when a new model line is released, the price drop will be another 10-15%. This fall in prices is primarily due to the fact that people have a choice: to buy new equipment or a previous line. Many people buy a new line, considering that the cost of investment will be cheaper - although this is not always the case.

Plus there are brands such as SuperMicro. This is a kind of low-cost server market. Their price is about two times lower than the servers of the big three. Thus, the buyer is facing another choice: SuperMicro of the new model or second-hand server of the big three? This also affects the price drop.

Based on the foregoing, approximately every six months there is a drop in the price of the server: a new model, a guarantee, a summer. Every six months exactly something from this happens. If the server is 6-7 years old, it is already worth nothing in the market. All that you can get for it after this period is 1-1.5 thousand rubles, which is already more expensive than delivering the server itself to its destination.

The controversial question is when is the best time to change the fleet of servers. Perhaps the most profitable period of sale is autumn and 2.5 years after the acquisition (the maximum cost of equipment). A maximum of 3.5 years, if the company planned to upgrade.

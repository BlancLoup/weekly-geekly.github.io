  • The concept of artificial reality was first introduced by Myron Krueger in the late 1960s.
  • In 1964, Stanislav Lem, in his book Summa Technologies, under the term Phantomology, describes the tasks and the essence of the answer to the question ‚Äúhow to create a reality that for intelligent beings living in it would be no different from normal reality, but would submit to others the laws?
  • The first virtual reality system appeared in 1962, when Morton Heilig (Eng. Morton Heilig) introduced the first prototype of a multisensory simulator, which he called "Sensorama" (Sensorama).
  • In 1967, Ivan Sutherland (born Ivan Sutherland) described and constructed the first helmet, the image of which was generated using a computer. The Sutherland helmet allowed the images to be changed according to the movements of the head (visual feedback).
  • In the 1970s, computer graphics completely replaced the video, previously used in simulators. The graphics were extremely primitive, but the important thing was that the simulators (they were flight simulators) worked in real time.
  • The Aspen Movie Map, created at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1977, is considered the first implementation of virtual reality. This computer program simulated a walk through the city of Aspen, Colorado, giving the opportunity to choose between different ways to display the terrain. Summer and winter options were based on real photos. [ Wikipedia ]

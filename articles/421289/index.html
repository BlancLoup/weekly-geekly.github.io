The number of electric cars in Russia increased from 920 to 2500 in a year and a half


One of the first Tesla cars in Moscow, July 9, 2014. Photo: Sergey Rodovnichenko

In a year and a half, the number of electric vehicles registered in Russia increased by more than 2.5 times, from 920 to 2,500 units. According to the Avtostat agency, the vast majority of electric vehicle fleets are Nissan Leaf models, and Mitsubishi i-MiEV and Tesla Model S occupy the second and third most popular places with a very large lag.

Here are the statistics for July 1, 2018:

  1. Nissan Leaf - 1800 pcs.
  2. Mitsubishi i-MiEV - 294 pcs.
  3. Tesla Model S - 202 pcs.


Mitsubishi i-MiEV

These three models cover more than 90% of the entire Russian fleet of electric vehicles. There are also more exotic cars, including the only Tesla Model 3 instance in the country:

  • Lada Ellada - 93 pcs.
  • Tesla Model X - 88 pcs.
  • Renault Twizy - 27 pcs.
  • BMW i3 - 11 pcs.
  • Tesla Model 3 - 1 pc.

MouseBox: Computer inside mouse

Polish startup Mouse-Box brought the portability of desktops to a new level and developed a PC the size of a mouse, in which it built it. The device looks and works like a normal mouse, but inside it there is an ARM Cortex processor, a 128 GB flash drive, a Wi-Fi module and a micro HDMI port for connecting to a monitor.



The Mouse-Box can be used both as an ordinary computer mouse and as a separate PC. So at work, you can do anything on your own PC, built into the mouse, then quickly switch to a working computer - until the chef saw that you were cutting into Counter Strike.

The device connects to the network via Wi-Fi, and to a monitor or TV using the Micro HDMI port. The company is developing a rug for inductive charging device. At the moment this is just a prototype. Mouse-Box is looking for a partner to start production and sales. The cost is still unknown, but according to the developers, the gadget will be "very cheap."

Characteristics of a computer mouse:
Processor: 1.4GHz ARM Cortex
Memory: 128GB
Ports: Micro HDMI, 2 x USB 3.0
Communication: Wi-Fi

REMOTE: Office Not Required. Announcement of the third book

The company 37 signals - one of the pioneers of the SaaS market and the creator of the Ruby on Rails framework, is famous not only for its software products, but also for books by Jason Fried and David Heinemeyer Hansson - Getting Real and Rework - which are based on real experience in developing popular software and building a successful company . Both books have become bestsellers. In the autumn of this year, the third book of 37 signals is published, called REMOTE: Office Not Required. It is dedicated to the benefits and organization of remote work.



The central office of 37 signals is located in Chicago, but employees are scattered not only in different US states, but also in different countries and continents. Remote operation is often considered an obstacle to efficiency, but at 37 signals think differently. The ability to recruit the best employees, regardless of their geography, to work from anywhere in the world at any convenient time gives more advantages than disadvantages - Fried and Hansson are sure.

Like the previous book, REMOTE consists of short essays on various aspects of remote work. The paper version of the book will be released by Crown . Tentative release date - October 29, the price - $ 23. The site 37 signals can sign up on the mailing list dedicated to the book. The authors promise to send subscribers to excerpts of the book before its release. Perhaps there will be other bonuses.

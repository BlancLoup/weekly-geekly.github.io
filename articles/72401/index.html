If I designed the phone

Modern interfaces in the phone, with all the bells and whistles, do not have enough integrity. If I made a phone, I would take a piece of paper and draw a sign. Horizontally and vertically all the functionality, point by point. At the intersection, how these two points integrate. For example:
  1. Calendar + Contact in Address Book = Birthday Reminder
  2. Photo + Contact in the address book = Mark a person in the photo
  3. Calendar + Photo = Place photos, grouped by date
  4. Photo + GPS = Location where the photo was taken
  5. GPS + Contact in the address book = put the address of the contact, the address of his work, etc.
  6. SMS + Contact = Arrange SMS as chat
  7. GPS + Call Log = Show where the call was made, (doubtful, but may be useful for an alibi)
  8. Notes + Contact = mobile CRM, leave any notes about the person, from the wishes on the DR ending with the fact that he owes 100 bucks (in any quantity and keeping the past)
  9. Contact + Reminders = a reminder to call, call and make an appointment, come to the contact home, send an SMS. Or show a reminder when an incoming, with an incoming SMS from the contact
  10. Reminders + GPS = remind what needs to be done when the phone is in a given place
  11. Internet + Contact = friendfeed aggregation, rss from homepage
  12. Internet + Music = last.fm
  13. Bluetooth + Contact = Associate contact and bluetooth address, store transmission history, (or better yet, chat with SMS, call logging, broadcasts, rss, etc.)
  14. SMS + GPS = Send SMS on arrival in the specified area. ‚ÄúSon, send sms how to get there‚Äù

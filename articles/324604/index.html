Detection and elimination of hacker attacks: how the system GOSPKA works



Year by year, the media publish more and more reports of cyber attacks on the information systems of state organizations and commercial companies. The number of attacks on industrial facilities, transport and communications is growing. In Russia, in response to increased threats, a state system for detecting, preventing, and eliminating the effects of computer attacks (GosSOPKA) was created. In 2015, departmental centers of the State Bureau of Emergency Situations appeared, in 2016 - corporate ones.

Not so long ago, Dmitry Kuznetsov, Director of Positive Technologies for Methodology and Standardization, spoke about the practical experience of creating GOSPRA centers, the requirements imposed on them and the technical solutions used. The speech attracted specialists responsible for ensuring security in ministries, departments and at critical industrial sites. Today we publish theses and materials of this report, and also announce the next webinar in the continuation of the topic.

What was said last time


In the course of the last webinar, Dmitry spoke about the tasks of the State Committee for Antimonopoly Policy and the regulatory requirements that are imposed on the system. The listeners learned about how exactly the system is regulated by the FSB and FSTEC. The structure of the GOSPKA system and the processes of interaction between its centers were described in detail. In addition, they talked about the functions of the state bureau of the State Administration for the Organization of Social and Social Policy and the technical solutions used within each function.

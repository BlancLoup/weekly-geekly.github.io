New Google service will help you find the missing phone.



Have you ever lost your phone between the sofa cushions or left in a restaurant? Or maybe searching for a phone before leaving the house is a usual occupation for you in the morning? This month, Google intends to release an Android Device Manager that can help you. This is one of the simplest methods that you can use to keep your device with you, and the data on it is safe.

Locate and call your lost device
If your phone somehow turns out to be behind the sofa cushions and is problematic to find, the Android Device Manager allows you to quickly call the phone with the maximum ring volume so that you can detect it even if the vibration mode is on or the ring tone is muted. And in the event that your phone or tablet is out of earshot (for example, in the very restaurant where you left it last night), you will be able to detect it with a real-time map.

Protect your personal information and data
Loss of the phone for many creates a stressful situation, but the Android Device Manager can help you to save data from falling into the wrong hands. If the phone fails to find, or it was stolen, you can quickly and securely delete all data on the device.

Usefulness and availability
The service will be available this month for devices running Android 2.2 and above; To use it, you must be logged in to your Google account. An Android app will also be released that will allow you to easily find and manage your devices.

$ 700 for 13.3 "tablet on e-ink - a lot or a little?

6 inches for the reader is not enough, I have a bigger phone. 10 inches is better, but still not perfect. Michael Kozlovsky, editor of GoodEReader , listened to the requests of users and now collects money on Indiegogo to order the first batch of 13.3-inch Android tablets on E-ink.

But here's an important question: $ 699 for a 13.3-inch reader is expensive, or just right, given that a 13.3-inch tablet with a QHD display costs less.



The Good e-Reader Tablet received a 13.3-inch high-resolution E ink Mobius screen - 1600x1200 pixels, and a stylus that defines 1024 degrees of depression. In the filling of the gadget - iMX Sololite processor, 512 megabytes of RAM, 4 GB of internal memory with the ability to expand with the help of microSD up to 32 GB. Batteries for 1270 mAh should last for 4 weeks. The gadget weighs only 350 grams.

The device works on Android 4.0.4. The developers are pushing for the fact that the user can select any reader or install one of the many other applications from Google Play. 3G and 4G in the tablet is not, but there is Wi-Fi.

The goal of the campaign at Indiegogo is to collect 42 thousand dollars to order the first batch of E-ink tablets from the factory.

At the time of this writing, the backers gave away $ 5,900 - 14% of the required amount. Until the end of the campaign remains two months.

The modern myth of stability.



The modern myth of stable human life does not stand the test for logical errors. Life shows the inconsistency of ideas about the stable life of society and the individual. Let us see what mistakes lie at the heart of our concepts of stability and what lessons should be learned from the crisis.

The first thing that catches your eye is a huge preparatory period on the threshold of a ‚Äústable‚Äù life. This period is clearly longer than the stability for which he is preparing.

Years spent in school and college implant knowledge and skills that are distinguished by a rigid link to stable situations in life. We study theories that describe phenomena in their static representation. We gain knowledge of how to behave in an unchanging world with well-established rules. It takes years.

After entering adulthood, each of us is faced with a revelation. What we studied in school and college has little in common with the real state of affairs. The order described by the theories studied is completely inappropriate to us as a heritage of the past.

Now, at the current moment, things are not as we expected to see, and we see not what we were ready for. A few months, in extreme cases, a year or two of naive stability abruptly give way to a crisis that brings the discovery of reality. This is the first such powerful crisis of personality, caused by the inevitable socialization of a young man.

And this is the first truly great crossroads at which a person makes a fateful choice.

But many people choose to sit mollusk at the bottom of the swamp of their illusions. Even if these illusions are not their own, but their parents, educators, and trustees. Sometimes these shells migrate from the swamp to the swamp. Sometimes they are eaten on the road.

And sometimes they dare to open the doors and look beyond their illusions. Such attempts occur in the period of the next social crisis, when the society is making another round of development and the obsolete formations are replaced by new ones. These periodic crises are like a series of new opportunities ... opportunities to become something big.

The next thing that does not fit in the myth of a stable life is that it does not fit into any of the existing theories of human development. Moreover, this myth does not fit into the course of history, which can be observed by any person during his life. But, oddly enough, many people believe in the myth, although they have the opportunity to track its inconsistency with the events of their own lives.

After all, what human history shows us ... Spiral dynamics of the development of society in all areas of life activity. Technique, economics, biology, psychology and other sciences demonstrate cyclical development and development of new achievements by people.

Hypothesis, data collection, experiments, iterations, proofs and a complete theory with exceptions to the rules that leave room for the next round. Then, a period of stable use and a new crisis that is not outstanding for its size - again a new hypothesis, data collection and experiments. And in different areas about the same time periods for each stage. From 15 to 20 years big cycles and from 4 to 6 years small. And moreover - from century to century, these cycles are reduced.

But then science, large social formations and countries, finally. This is their scale of growth measured within two decades. We, people, are much more flexible and capable of faster growth. Its high speed lies in our nature.

And our changes can affect the scale and speed of their transitions from one quality to another. Here, think ...

For three or four years - the basics of speech in two or three languages ‚Äã‚Äãwith the appropriate environment. For the same first three or four years, he masters the fundamentals of mathematics, the knowledge of which later develops at even faster rates (several months, and in some cases, weeks it takes to master multiplication and division) ...

A child who is not clogged with patterns and stereotypical reactions spends only a few weeks on the joint comprehension of the foundations of many disciplines. Combinatorics, systematics, biology, algebra, astronomy, physics, chemistry, sociology, psychology, and so on. He, like a sponge, absorbs everything that is offered to him in an interesting form for studying. He demonstrates amazing ability to operate with absorbed knowledge and develops his skills on the go. The potential is huge.

But most of our potential crowns of nature‚Äôs creation stumble over parental notions of a stable and comfortable life.

Yes, such an installation appears precisely in those periods when we feel good and comfortable. The mental weakness brought up from childhood wants to believe that it will always be like this. If we accept these rules of the game, then we grow and develop together with the world around us. If we do not accept, we get sick and die. We burn, fired or ravaged, in the fire of the next crisis.

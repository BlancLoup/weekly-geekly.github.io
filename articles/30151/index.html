You're right [Seobaby], everything develops in a spiral. And if you unscrew this spiral back from the place from which you started, the mail (and all other applications) will again become online. There was no Internet then, but there were mainframes with installed software and terminals (consoles) connected to them.

Then it was technically and economically justified. Now? - a very big question. For the creators of the services, it is certainly not worth it, the main thing is money. And for users?

For the mass user, unfortunately, this question is also not worth it. People who are now actively using computers and the web, have recently learned about these technologies and simply cannot imagine how it was (good) before. The rest, because to say reasonable, users are simply crushed by the mass and forced to consume all these hundreds of web applications.

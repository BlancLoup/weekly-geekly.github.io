With a view to 11



So, today the first beta version of the Opera browser has been released - number 11. The number did not grow by chance, but because of rather substantial changes in the browser, many of which, by the way, appeared in response to the persistent requests of users. In part, many of the innovations have already been discussed in RuNet, in this article I will try to collect all the most important information regarding the new features of Opera 11. Something, however, will be a pleasant surprise. So, let's begin.

Extensions
Yes, as they say, "we built, built, and finally - built." Extensions were probably the most popular request from users. Naturally, we could not leave it without attention, and still engaged in the creation of a system of extensions. There were no special surprises in terms of choosing the technological base for extensions: as well as for all major additions to Opera (widgets, Unite applications), the ‚Äúthree whales‚Äù of the Internet were chosen: HTML, CSS and JavaScript. In turn, this suggests that now there are plenty of possibilities for constructing all sorts of combinations, including extensions, widgets, Unite applications and the browser itself. All this abundance of ideas and custom solutions is waiting for us, I hope, ahead, but for now you can familiarize yourself with the extensions already presented by our developers and enthusiasts from among Opera users ( Opera Extensions repository ).



Currently more than 130 extensions are available and more are under review. The installation process is quite simple and consists in finding the appropriate option and clicking the ‚ÄúInstall‚Äù button. The result may be different: some extensions are clearly declared and placed in the form of buttons in the browser interface, and some prefer not to advertise themselves and work in the background, manifesting themselves only at the moment when they are really necessary.



Of course, all this pleasure is still far from perfect, but is in a stage of constant change / improvement.

Visual mouse gestures
This function also hung in the top of the list of user requests for a very long time and finally appeared to the world in all its glory. Frankly, I waited cautiously, what is the result. But I was afraid, as it turned out, in vain: the option even in the beta version turned out to be quite workable and even looks pretty well-established, which happens rarely with new functions. Moreover, the visual indication of mouse gestures turned out to be equipped with ‚Äúintelligence‚Äù, which allows, without additional settings, to change the operation of the function depending on how the user gains experience.



The principle of this. As soon as the user made a small movement with the mouse while holding the right button and slightly slowed down trying to remember how the mouse gesture is being performed now, a special hint appears that clearly shows all the actions available after the initial movement. Thus, the user will be able to gradually learn and memorize constantly used movements. In the future, with a slightly faster execution of a gesture, only a kind of notification will appear, what gesture was performed at the moment, and as soon as the user starts to perform gestures confidently enough, no prompts will be displayed at all. Conveniently? Of course. Who does not have this - envy. And, by the way, for experienced users it is possible to disable rendering in general - you can do this by changing the Show Gesture UI parameter in opera: config.

Address bar
No, the line itself is in place, only at a glance you can see that something is wrong with it. In the sense - not as usual. First, a rather massive indicator appeared, displaying, depending on the situation, either the location of an open resource (network or local disk), or information about the security of a page, if any (in the sense of security) is available. When you click on this indicator, we get access to more detailed security information, as well as access to certificates and the tool for reporting dangerous or suspicious web pages. In general - quite functional, compact, stylish. I like. It's funny, but just a couple of days before this function appeared in one of the weekly test assemblies, a request to add something similar to the browser appeared on the official Russian-language forum, and then - please. You just thought, and we already did.



Another noticeable change is the display of the URL. Firstly, he ‚Äúshed‚Äù in some places, and secondly - he clearly hides something from us! He hides the site icon, he hides the protocol pointer (http, https, ftp), and he hides the ‚Äútails‚Äù of intricate links from us. This is done for the convenience of self-determination of the user in terms of where he is and what kind of website it is. Convenience, of course, controversial - say those users who are accustomed to perform various manipulations with addresses or just browse everything, including "tails". Such users have two ways - either use click in the address field (then the address will appear in all its glory), or disable the function of hiding part of the address by changing the Hide URL Parameter option, all in the same opera: config.

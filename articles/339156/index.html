Let's start with physics: teleportation of photons and quantum entanglement


If we turn to physics, then with teleportation of photons, it seems, something happened. And this is something that the communications workers immediately adopted because it will help create an invulnerable to the weather and other conditions of the communication network. To be more precise, the main goal of quantum teleportation at the present time is to create completely invulnerable for interception of communication networks.


More precisely, in such a communication line only an exact copy of the photon is transmitted, and not the photon itself. And if it is even more precise, then the fact of transmitting to a distance not of the physical object itself, but of its state is called such teleportation. This is a physical phenomenon called "quantum entanglement". This phenomenon consists in the fact that in one point of space and time two identical particles arise, in fact having one existence. Entanglement continues even after particles - in this case photons - are separated from each other. And if the state of one of the photons changes, the ‚Äútwin photon‚Äù located at another point changes in the same way. Similarly, if a third particle begins to interact with a tangled pair, then the changes that occur in one of them are mirrored in its twin.

The physics of elementary particles studies the entanglement of quantum states on the basis of the spin (rotation) of an elementary particle, and quantum confusion is determined by different spin. In the process of observing objects, scientists saw that there can only be two spins - along and across. Oddly enough, in other positions the particles do not "pose" to the observer. Otherwise, imagine that you have two boxes. In each of them lies one ball with a stripe. Now we give one box to the astronaut, and he, for example, flies with her to Mars. But as soon as you open the box and see that the strip on the ball is horizontal, then in another box the ball will automatically have a vertical strip. This is, so to speak, quantum entanglement, when one object determines the position of another.


There is, however, another explanation of the principle of quantum communication, when microparticles having a dual nature (as a particle and as a wave) are considered to be part of the world ether consisting of an unlimited set of energy ‚Äúseas‚Äù. The principle of quantum entanglement can be understood if we take into account the fact that elementary particles are in the form of waves. Having no specific location or characteristics, both particles are in the ocean of energy. At the moment of the appearance of the observer, the wave ‚Äúturns‚Äù into an object accessible to touch. The beginning of the quantum Internet


Launched in 2016, the Chinese Micius satellite weighing more than 600 kg was launched into a sun-synchronous orbit of 494.8‚Äì511.1 km altitude using the Changzheng-2D launch vehicle (also known as Long March, or the Long March) . The satellite is equipped with experimental equipment: an emitter of pairs of entangled photons and a high-speed coherent laser transmitter. Recently, orbital tests have been successfully completed, and a new quantum communication record has been set between two ground stations that are 1203 km apart. Scientists have managed to set up the generation of 4,000 pairs of quanto-entangled photons per second in their laboratory in Tibet and send one photon from each pair using a beam of light directed at the satellite receiver. The satellite has an ultrasensitive detector capable of detecting the quantum states of individual photons arriving from the Earth. The ground stations then used the effect of quantum teleportation to exchange encrypted messages via satellite. The device worth about 100 million US dollars was created in the framework of the QUESS (Quantum Science Satellite) project. Specialists from the combined group of the Chinese and Austrian Academies of Sciences took almost 14 years to achieve this result. Potentially, the launch of such satellites opens up the possibility of creating global communication systems that are protected from interception at the level of physical principles. The experiment has already been dubbed "the beginning of the quantum Internet."

The satellite orbit parameters were chosen so that the satellite appears in the same place every night. Ground stations tracked the satellite and installed optical communication lines with it to receive single entangled photons. The satellite was driven by three optical telescopes in Deling, Lijiang and Nanshan. According to existing plans, Micius will be the first device in the global network of quantum communications, which the PRC intends to create by 2030. One of the tasks of his scientific mission is the quantum transmission of information over an interception protected channel between Beijing and Vienna.


The signal from Micius went through the atmosphere and was simultaneously received by two ground stations. If a 1200 km long optical fiber were used for communication to distribute pairs of entangled photons on Earth, then due to the loss of signal power with increasing distance, only one pair per second could be transmitted. But the use of satellite allows to overcome this barrier, and, according to Chinese experts, the speed of photon distribution was increased by 12 orders of magnitude compared with previous technological advances.

The transmitting device will be similar to a laptop computer or the most common mobile phone, and a prototype has already been manufactured , capable of providing data transmission over a distance of no more than 40 km.

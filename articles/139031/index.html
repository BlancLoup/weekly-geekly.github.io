Duracell has released a "charging card" for wireless charging of mobile devices



Until now, most wireless chargers developed by different companies suffer from one drawback - mobile devices, in order to be charged without wires, must meet certain standards. In most cases, mobile devices can not be charged without wires, because they do not support the necessary technology. Of course, there are exceptions to the rules, but the problem exists. The company Duracell recently introduced an addition to standard phones, which can make life easier for many owners of mobile devices.

This add-on is a flat card called the Powermat WiCC. This card is a kind of intermediary between a wireless charger and a regular phone (smartphone, tablet). To equip your mobile device with the wireless charging function, you only need to insert the card into the phone, so that the card‚Äôs contacts touch the ‚Äúcharging‚Äù contacts of the phone. Then you can close the lid and enjoy. By the way, the developer also plans to integrate the NFC chip into the WiCC card.

Of course, there are also problems here. First, no matter how flat the card is, not every battery cover closes. You can just break it. In addition, not every phone has a suitable contact system. Well, the third problem - to wirelessly charge your device (suppose that the card came up) you need a wireless charger. But such devices are not so much.

Nevertheless, it is hoped that Duracell will present its own wireless charger, since the development in this area has been going on for a very long time. If the card is received positively, you can probably expect an early announcement of an available wireless charger. At least, I would like to hope so.

LCD LG LF2510 or "why do I need a DVD now?"

Want a budget TV and media player in one device? It's about the LG 42LF2510 LCD TV


I was puzzled by the purchase of a large TV. I searched for a long time, chose: where is cheaper, which is better. The main characteristics were support for FullHD, a contrast of at least 50000: 1, USB as a nice addition, Ethernet as a novelty in TVs. Such novelty has been found only in Sony TVs. USB could be met more often than Ethernet. After careful selection, Sony Bravia KDL-40V5610 was chosen. For a number of financial reasons, they decided to take something budget.
Suddenly I went to MVideo and my eyes fell on the low-cost LG model. I am not very trusting about this brand, but soon I was struck by the possibilities of this TV. Unfortunately, as it turned out USB in this TV, though there is, but it is a service one. What was my surprise when I started reading reviews on it: it turns out this TV can play video files with quite a few formats. And most importantly, MKV files :))

Thus, it was this model that was purchased ... After buying, the LG 42LF2510 was puzzled by the inclusion of the option to play files from a flash drive, which was a huge plus for this device and without this ability it would not be used 100% for watching movies, because My DVD (Pioneer AV610) can't read MKV.
A couple of resources were explored: the familiar ixbt.com and not the foreign avforums.com. Where I found the answers to activate the playback via USB.

In order to find out the current firmware ->
  1. Go to the Menu by pressing the key of the same name on the remote control (remote control).
  2. Select Options (but do not click)
  3. Press the FAV button (bottom right of the remote control) 7 times
  4. We are in flashing mode. The first line is your firmware version.

Attention if the version is below 3.4 then you can downgrade to firmware 3.15. Call the engineering menu by holding the OK key on the remote control and on the TV. (or MENU in later versions). If the version is 3.47 or 3.48, then when you downgrade your TV will turn into a brick

And so the first option:
You need to downgrade to 3.15, activate USB, upgrade to your own firmware or more recent.

The second option: the service menu can be called by sending a specific code to the receiver. Ways to do it: service console
emulation programs for Symbian and WinMobile

In the first version there is a lot of information on the Russian-language forum ixbt, and there is very little information about activation using a diode (there is a link to the FAQ at the end). I decided on this option, because I was confused by some of the risks when reflashing.

As a result, the following was done to activate USB:
  1. One LED was taken from the rear bicycle lantern (as it was useless it was soldered back without consequences).
  2. Wire from old headphones with 3.5mm jack.
  3. LED is soldered so that it is on (observing polarity)
  4. On the iphone was played specials. file.
The instructions say that you need to play cyclically changing the volume. I chose not to change the volume and the distance to the receiver on the TV.

2-5 seconds and the TV asks for a password. Press "0000" on the remote. Enter the service menu.

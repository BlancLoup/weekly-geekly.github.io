Mobile Internet will overtake wired broadband already in 2010

Well, we waited for the time when in Russia the mobile computer (read, laptop, tablet, smartphone, etc.) became the main device for accessing the Internet, and not the desktop. Already this year, the revenue of mobile operators from the Internet access service will exceed the revenue of fixed broadband providers (60 billion rubles), according to a recent report by J'son & Partners Consulting.



According to J & P estimates, by the end of 2010 there will be 13.6 million active 3G users in Russia (up from 5.8 million at the end of 2009).

Interestingly, most users now use USB modems, not 3G phones. The share of 3G traffic via USB modems in total 3G traffic averages about 80% in 2009.

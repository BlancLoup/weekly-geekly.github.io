One scientifically based way to rationally manage your busy and busy schedule



About 20 years ago, a group of students at Stanford University went home for the winter holidays. For this period they were given the task: to keep a diary.
Some students were asked to tell in the records about the main elements of their value system, and then explain how the events of these days relate to it. What came of it? And to which benefit can we gain from the experiment?

Another group of students were asked to simply share positive points.

When the subjects returned after the holidays, the researchers found that those guys who described their values , had better health, less often experienced ailments, were more energetic and positive-minded than their comrades, who were just talking about pleasant events.

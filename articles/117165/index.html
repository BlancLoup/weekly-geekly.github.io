Online consultants

There are several ways to get an answer to your question. It all depends on the degree of "advancement" of the visitor. He can:
  • call by phone (or Skype);
  • write an email;
  • send a message via IM (in most cases it is ICQ).

But there is a way that allows you to communicate without even paying attention from an open page. This use of online consultants.

The word "online" in the title implies receiving a response in the shortest possible time - ideally, the delay should be no more than 10 seconds. This is not an email where messaging can take several days. Therefore, the company should be staffed by carefully monitoring the use of an online consultant. Of course, this additional burden may entail demands for an increase in staff salaries, but which customer cares?

Interested in the topic, I conducted a small experiment - I sent a letter to online bookstores asking about a discount for a rather large wholesale purchase. As a result, the answer came only from 35%.


The advantage of using online consultants is that visitors do not need to install additional software - everything happens in a browser window. This will be appreciated by both housewives and numerous workers, on whose computers limited rights, and asking by phone about a new 150-inch TV is too noticeable.

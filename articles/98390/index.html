NZXT Sentry - monitor the temperature of your iron friend



On Habr√©, more than once or twice the topic of temperature control of computers, especially powerful ones, was raised. Of course, in the case of supercomputers, everything is solved quickly and efficiently. But how to be an ordinary user who has just purchased a gaming PC, and on the second day of the game in the next "top" toy noticed that the computer is overheated? You can add additional coolers, you can install a powerful air conditioner. But what about the elegance of the solution? This is the elegant solution offered by NZXT, a famous guru in the field of PC cooling. Recently, the company introduced another solution for gaming computers, which can be liked by many.

After all, now most powerful coolers make a lot of noise, and super-expensive, noiseless and powerful solutions are not affordable for everyone. The same can be said about water and other cooling systems that may not suit everyone.

In general, NZXT solved the problem not by simply building capacity, but through a rational distribution of available capacity. It is not necessary to include all elements of the cooling system at full capacity. You can simply effectively distribute both power consumption and noise power, cooling the most problematic elements. As a result, everything - and noise, and power consumption, and temperature, it all keeps at an optimal level.

Previously, as many habrovchane know, this company has released several good cases and peripherals. Now, afflicted users of gaming and simply powerful PCs should pay attention to the new device - NZXT Sentry.



This device has an aluminum case, the front of which is occupied by a relatively large LCD display where you can see all the necessary parameters and settings.



Here you can monitor the temperature indicators of various points in degrees Celsius and Fahrenheit, you can track the speed of rotation of the coolers, and if you need someone, find out the time and date.



The NZXT Sentry comes with a PCI card, which connects the device to the system of your PC. There are five temperature sensors and five ten watt channels.

The cost of the device is not as high as one might think - $ 60 seems to be quite an acceptable price. Or not?

MSP430 Developer Kit from Texas Instruments

Texas Instruments continues to surprise


I have good news for those who feel engineering inclinations in themselves but still did not know where to start. And let it be new to someone, but for all other minds I would like to communicate. Texas Instruments continues to push our products, and this time the MSP430 Value Line LaunchPad Development Tool will be in focus. Not so long ago version 1.5 of this development kit based on the MSP430 series microcontroller was released. I invite under the cat for details and surprises

So, let's begin. The appearance of what is offered to the user is shown in the photo:



In the box are:

LaunchPad debug board (MSP-EXP430G2)
Microcontroller MSP430G2452
Microcontroller MSP430G2553
Sticker LaunchPad (2 pcs.)
Connectors for 10 pins (2pcs.)
32 kHz quartz
Mini usb cable
User's manual

You can order a device on the Texas Instruments website at a price of 4.30 USD which is about 140 rubles with delivery in Russia by Fedex courier (this is the main surprise!)

Complete set can be viewed on the photo:



The board itself is more close-ups ::



For those who love to practice more than theory, I suggest reading the video below.
In the example used in this video, the temperature sensor built into the bundled microcontroller is involved. The process of temperature change is indicated by LEDs on the board. Make sure that the code of this example is already working is already stitched in the microcontroller, it is enough just to connect the usb cable to the board and press a button on it.
An example is reviewed on video from product manager Adrian Fernandez:



The range of applications is very wide, limited only by the imagination of the developer (within reasonable limits, of course) - this device is not a competitor to either the Raspberry Pi or any other SoC computer - it is not intended for that. Its niche is specific, definitely such devices find their consumers.

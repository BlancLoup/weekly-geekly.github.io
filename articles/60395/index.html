Sony Ericsson needs 100 million euros

Recently things are not going well for Sony Ericsson - sales for the first quarter of this year have fallen by 50%, says Engadget. And now the president of Sony Ericsson has recognized that the decline in demand, coupled with the "holes" in the portfolio of models has caused big problems in the company. He also acknowledged that by the end of this fiscal year (by March 2010) revenues should increase by at least 100 million euros if Sony Ericsson wants to stay afloat. According to rumors, the company is considering many options for increasing revenues, but so far no final plans have been formulated.

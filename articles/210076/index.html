Sony creates a human genome research company



Many technology corporations, in addition to the main business, also conduct secondary activities, which, at times, seem to be very far from their main direction. So, Google is engaged, for example, in questions of research of the Moon, and also studying of the main questions of gerontology . And Sony is now going to study the human genotype, in order to form further activities related to personalized medicine and healthcare.

Of course, no one will force the electronic engineers who are working on smartphones, laptops or game consoles of the company to do this kind of work. Instead, Sony forms a subsidiary, which will deal with the above issues. The new company was named P5 Inc.

This company works in conjunction with M3, a commercial medical structure, the majority of which is owned by Sony. In addition, partners include Illumina, an American company that manufactures equipment for "decoding" the genome.

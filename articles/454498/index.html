Assembly


Well, let's get started. Before starting open the remaining several boxes. In the largest box was what we need in the next 20 minutes:



  • Light reflectors front and rear
  • Front wheel hub eccentric
  • Seatpost

Tools - a set of L-shaped hexagons and a standard bicycle key (familiar to many more from childhood).

Obviously, a bicycle standing on a fork is not the most stable design, so the first thing to do is to install the front wheel. We take the eccentric and twirl it, gently hitting the wheel brake disk in the brake caliper.


The regulator at the top of the right fork tube traditionally serves to block the suspension.

Tighten as it should, otherwise it will be a fiasco. Stretching the mount is worth it even in the case of buying a collected bike, and it is better to check (or at least arrange periodic preventive maintenance) before each departure - there is nothing good in the wheel that fell off on the go (and this is possible).



Now the bike is not so scary to move around the room, but for greater stability it is necessary to install a footrest so that it can stand without leaning on the wall. The leg consists of two parts (with elastic bands inside), which are tightened together with cogs; The base is made of rubber. It is easy to do this (according to the estimated risks on the steering wheel): we align in the middle, check at a convenient angle whether the speed switches will be. If everything is ok, then tighten (again, stronger) hexagon.

Above, we talked about the few parts that need to be fixed "for yourself" in order to then sit down and go. While behind the scenes were many other important components, which can not be mentioned (this time very briefly).

The most important thing in the bike is the frame. In Twitter Mantis-E0 it is the same as in Twitter Mantis-E1 - from aluminum alloy (6061 - aluminum-magnesium-silicon alloy, used very much where - including in the bicycle and automotive industry) with seamless welding. Although not everywhere - in the area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe carriage the seams are very visible. The junction points look neat, all the beauty without sacrificing reliability.



Once again, I would like to note the irresistible craving of the manufacturer for placing the company name on the bike. It seems that they simply can not control this process: the inscriptions, on sensations, there are 50 pieces (there are about 8 of them on the steering wheel alone) - so that everyone knows that this is Twitter. But all this was noticed when processing photos - while driving, you simply do not pay attention to it. On the bottom of the frame are three large inscriptions: red at the bottom and rainbow relief on the sides:



But the coloring as a whole does not look embarrassing.

Leather grips are familiar from the Mantis-E1 and TW-E9L models - very comfortable and practical. Unlike rubber, which will be on e-bike from the following review. On the left is the front brake knob and speed selector (Shimano Altus), on the right there is a rear brake knob and another shifter. There is no one more petal on the steering wheel, as the scooter mode is not provided in this model.



In the front cassette there are 3 stars, in the back 9 - in total, the bike has 27 speeds, that is, any mode can choose the appropriate mode.




Rods, too, from the brand Retrospec - like pedals, saddle, seatpost and steering wheel.

Go


Before you go, you need to put the bike on charge. Almost immediately on the adapter the green LED lights up - it means that everything is charged. We press the red button on the battery and turn on the on-board computer - everything turns on, 100%, nothing sparkles :)


Not a bit, not beautiful, mileage is not twisted

On the computer screen displays:

  • Current speed
  • Help level (on the screen above - 5 - maximum; 0 - off)
  • The total mileage and distance of the current trip
  • Error indication
  • Battery charge

Information is easily and quickly read from the screen, visible even in the bright sun (if anything, there is a backlight).

When assisting mode is off, we have a good bike with 27 speeds. But it is necessary to press a button and now ... no, there is no scooter mode here - you cannot take it and go, you have to pedal) And this is not a drawback, but a feature of the model. Pedaling in general is very useful :) So - it is worth pressing a button, how to turn the pedals in the load becomes easier. Sensors detect pedal speed and command the engine to help you maintain the speed you need. Or to go uphill on the heaviest stars, which are usually problematic to call on.

There are five levels of help in total: from the minimum (1) to the maximum (5), but even on the five will have to actively work with the feet. When reaching a speed of 35 km / h, the assistant turns off - to surprise the car at the Moscow Ring Road will not work :)

Before the start on the odometer was 0.6 km (600 meters), the battery was charged up to 100%. After 10 km of run in the city in the mode of maximum assistance, 92% of the charge remained. This hardly means that the battery lasts for 100 kilometers, but in any case, the charge does not melt before our eyes. By the way, the manufacturer claims up to 90 km in support mode - this is an excellent figure, although there are many factors that can change it in both directions: the wearer's weight, weather conditions, wind, tire pressure and others.

The front shock absorber (times) is blocked a little tight, but the ride is rather soft - you can ride over bumps and sidewalks without much discomfort. Grips are very comfortable. The same with the pedals.

During operation, the electric motor emits a not very loud hum. There is not enough headlights to ride in the dark - it is strange that the manufacturer does not include such a simple, necessary and inexpensive accessory at the same time.

It is also desirable to buy wings.

We still ride a bike to try to understand the real power reserve - as long as an extremely mixed mode is obtained (different people, with and without support, from the hill and uphill), but the resulting figures are similar to what the manufacturer claims and this is very cool.

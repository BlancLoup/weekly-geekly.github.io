Hey Apple, you keep it wrong

This night, Apple has published an official response to complaints from users about problems with the connection of the iPhone 4 . Steve Jobs got to personal mail, and his answer is laconic and clear than the florid wordings of Apple's PR department.



- I like my new iPhone 4 (great work), but when I touch the metal edge, the signal disappears. This seems like a common problem. Any plans to fix it?
- Just try not to keep it that way.
In connection with these journalists, Engadget published a wonderful selection of photos that can be viewed under the cut.



The official answer from Apple PR executives ( English version is here ):
If you pick up any mobile phone, it will affect the operation of its antenna. Moreover, depending on the location of the antennas, in some places of the body it will be noticeably stronger. This is a truth of life for any cordless phone. If you have ever encountered this on your iPhone 4, avoid grabbing it by the bottom left so that your hand touches both sides of the metal edge. Or simply use one of the many covers.

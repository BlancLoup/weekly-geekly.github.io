Printers with built-in original CISS: Canon, HP, Brother, Epson solutions

At the moment, all 4 main manufacturers of inkjet home printers have in their arsenal devices with integrated CISS (continuous ink supply system). The last one was its HP solution (in February, two models of the new HP DeskJet GT series were announced). And this year Epson celebrates the 5th anniversary of the Print Factory line (Ecotank abroad). Under the cat more about 4 implementations of the original CISS.

It should be noted right away: we are considering modern home inkjet printers with original CISS.
  • Third-party CISS is a system that the user needs to install by himself, and this deprives the user of a warranty for the device.
  • The original CISS is a system from the manufacturer of the printer, already built into the device.

In general, CISS always consists of end cartridges, moving tubes (to follow cartridges) and donor ink tanks. In the device of the latter, a design that ensures a stable supply of dye is important, since the cartridges and printheads of home printers are for the most part not able to correct ink pressure, they enter them by ‚Äúgravity flow‚Äù. Brother with integrated slam-shut valves


Brother has always released its printers with fixed cartridges, from which tubes went to the printhead with ink buffer. Just in the T300 / T500 / T700 cartridges made embedded in the case, so the whole design is more like a built-in PZK (refillable cartridges).



Having extensive experience in working with jet manufacturers from this manufacturer, I would like to highlight the following points:
  • start pumping GHGs (nozzles) are required less often than on similar Epson
  • slow photo printing
  • plus lower tray under the paper: no need for a back seat
  • minus the paper tray: regular problems with gripping thick paper
  • able to perform self-cleaning during long downtimes, so the risk of drying is minimal (the scourge of all inkjet)
  • pumping the ink chamber in the printhead for self-repair is an incredibly complicated procedure

In general, I would recommend Brother for the office.

Apple and Qualcomm patent dispute led to a halt in sales of iPhone 7 and 8 in Germany

Qualcomm and Apple continue to litigate. The fight goes with varying success, and now, it seems, the favor of the court is on the side of Qualcomm. On December 20 last year, the district court of Munich, Germany, recognized that the iPhone 7, 8 and iPhone X smartphones violate Qualcomm's patent rights.

At the same time, the court decided that the sale of these devices should be banned throughout Germany. But to meet this requirement, it was necessary for Qualcomm to make a security payment. And now the company has done this by placing security bonds worth 1.34 billion euros.

As for the patented technology, which is discussed in the litigation, this is a way to save energy, which is used by Apple. Now Apple has to not only stop online sales, but also withdraw smartphones already placed with retailers.

Previously, both companies worked closely, Apple installed Qualcomm chips in many of its phones. But last year, the Apple Corporation began litigation with an ex-partner in the United States and China. Apple accuses Qualcomm of violating technology licensing terms.

Qualcomm manages to defend their interests. So, the company was able to achieve a ban on the sale of some iPhone models in China. True, after this, Apple released an update, which, according to the corporation, eliminates the violation, which was stated by the former partner. Nevertheless, in this country, Qualcomm is trying to stop not only sales, but also the production of Apple phones listed in the title models. And this is a much more sensitive blow to the business of the company from Cupertino.

It is worth noting that so far the Apple business in the Celestial Empire has not undergone any changes. Smartphones of all models are sold in the same way as they were sold before. Qualcomm on this occasion shows discontent, saying that Apple is obliged to immediately stop not only sales, but also the sales offer and even the import of those devices that were mentioned in the court decision.

LG: 3D without glasses

LG has announced a Flatron DX2000 monitor with the ability to display bulk content. The novelty is capable of reproducing an image in 3D, while no glasses are required to work with it - the three-dimensional image is visible to the naked eye.
The device uses the parallax barrier effect: two slightly different images for the left and right eyes are formed on the matrix, making the image appear three-dimensional. Special glasses for viewing 3D-pictures are not required.



The lack of points was made up by the camera, which tracks the turns of the user's head, and, based on the received data, adjusts the image on the monitor screen. The camera can also be used for video telephony.
LG Flatron DX2000 is equipped with LED backlighting and supports software transformation of a flat picture into a volumetric one. The resolution of the 20-inch device panel is 1600 x 900 pixels, the response time is 5 ms, and the contrast ratio is 1000: 1. The monitor is connected to a video card using one of two digital interfaces - DVI or HDMI.



Buy LG Flatron DX2000 can be in the near future, but there is a risk that the price of $ 1210, can scare away even the wealthy gamers.

Recall that this is not the first monitor with the ability to view 3D images without special glasses. Most recently, Toshiba announced the release of the Qosmio F750 - the world's first 3D laptop without glasses.

Lego constructor predicts solar eclipses

Apple engineer Andrew Carol (Andrew Carol) with the help of Lego created an analogue of the anti-kiter mechanism (dated 150-100 BC). This is a unique gear system with a ratio of 254: 19, which simulates the movement of the Sun and the Moon relative to fixed stars.



Like the ancient Greek mechanical computer, a copy of Lego is able to predict lunar and solar eclipses with high accuracy.

Andrew Carol used 1500 Lego Technic parts, the work took 30 days.



In 1901 a strange mechanism was found on the sunken ancient Greek ship. Until recently, its purpose and way of working remained a mystery to some extent.

Only in 2006, after processing the artifact using computed tomography, were scientists able to read 95% of the inscriptions contained in the mechanism (about 2000 Greek characters). Only because of this was able to understand how the device works.

The gear system used a differential gear , which, as it was believed, was not invented earlier than the XVI century, and the level of miniaturization and complexity are comparable to the mechanical clocks of the XVIII century.

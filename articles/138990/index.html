And we start, of course, with browsers.

So, as has already become clear from the title, today we are introducing the next versions of mobile browsers, and if with Opera Mobile 12 everything is, in principle, clear, then Opera Mini 7 with an intriguing addition to Next has a certain part of readers raises fair questions. - New Express panel, allowing you to add an infinite number of links;
- To add links to the Express panel, you can now use an asterisk in the address bar;
- Now it is possible to close tabs with one touch (on touch screens);

Opera Mini 7 Next


Finally, we got to the "younger" sister of the mobile version of Opera. To begin, let me explain what ‚ÄúNext‚Äù means in the version name. By analogy with the development of the desktop version of the browser, we decided to organize a test branch of the development of Opera Mini Next. Thus, everyone will be able to install and test this version in order to identify as many errors and shortcomings as possible by the final release. The Next version is installed separately from the stable version of Opera Mini and in the list of applications is represented by a gray icon, which allows you to protect against accidental loss of personal data available in the stable version of Opera Mini installed on your device. But losses can be quite, because, as a rule, Next versions are quite ‚Äúraw‚Äù and contain many errors in the code that can lead to unpredictable consequences.

So, what can we see in Opera Mini 7 Next? First of all - the modified Express panel. More precisely, in addition to the Express panel, another tab appeared, which opens when the browser starts by default, under the working name ‚ÄúSmart page‚Äù (in Russian translation this tab is listed as ‚ÄúHome‚Äù for the time being). What is it and why is it needed?

As conceived by the developers, this page should be a good helper for those who are used to actively using the Internet access using a mobile browser, not limited to viewing two or three favorite pages. The smart page consists of several sections, including a list of the most frequently visited resources (a separate line provides access to popular Google services - Google+, GMail and Google News), an access block to social networks, a News block (direct links to new publications to frequently visited sites from the first block) and a block of recommended resources (also not a very good option in the Russian version - ‚ÄúSuggested Links‚Äù), broken down by subject (News, Finance, Travel, etc.).

At the top of the page there is also an ad unit of two banners (currently, these are links to MyOpera and Opera Mobile Store). Some blocks are updated automatically depending on the pages visited by the user, some contain predefined sets of links to popular resources of a certain subject (as long as these are English-language resources, it is planned to replace them with popular local sites of similar subjects).

By the way, in the screenshot you can see the red squares on the block title bar. They say that there are more messages in this block. To go to them, use the "flipping" left and right. And one more important note. It is clear that not everyone will like this page, therefore, the developers have provided the ability to turn it off (Settings-Advanced-Homepage). Immediately I will warn you: it is better to first open the Express panel (second tab), and then go to the settings and turn off the Home page - otherwise you can get a white screen. The function is still very test.

Articles and blogs



Mathematical Foundations of Bitcoin Blockchain
In this article, we talk about how Bitcoin begins, about cryptographic algorithms. Consider the properties of elliptic curves and arithmetic operations that are used to create a secure signature and verification in ECDSA.

Bitcoin for five year olds
New York-based investor Nick Custodio (Nik Custodio) explains the concept of bitcoin and distributed registries using the example of apples, World of Warcraft and other simple and understandable analogies. By the way, the leadership is perfect not only for five-year plans, but also for adults who are taking the first steps in the world of cryptocurrencies.

Why does bitcoin matter?
Entrepreneur and software engineer Marc Andreessen , whose company Andreessen Horowitz invested $ 50 million in bitcoin startups, talks about the advantages and disadvantages of the most popular cryptocurrency and its potential. Read about solving the problem of the Byzantine generals and the properties of bitcoin, which will allow him to "break in" in retail stores and supermarkets.

What is a blockchain? (briefly for ordinary people)
The author explains what can be called a blockchain and what is not, and gives examples of interesting blockchain projects. In particular, she talks about the network in which content creators can earn a couple of bucks on their work (provided that other participants appreciate it). Additionally inside: links to articles and podcasts.

What is exclusive blockchains?
Many companies have tried to adapt Bitcoin blockchain for corporate needs. However, they quickly realized that the technology could not fully satisfy their requirements. A half-hour video about the principles of cryptocurrency: registries, mining, encryption, etc. The author talks about the principles of establishing trust in a decentralized network and the transfer of coins. Additionally, you can watch a video about 256-bit cryptographic keys.

Books and Studies



Mastering bitcoin
Handbook of blockchain operating principles. Bitcoin is chosen as an example. The author will tell you how cryptocurrencies work and how to develop software for working with them. Investor Balaji Srinivasan notes that this book is the best source of technical information on bitcoin that exists on the market.

Proof-of-Stake and Proof-of-Work
We describe how the participants of the Bitcoin network achieve a unified vision of the block chain using the Proof-of-Work consensus algorithm (proof of work). Also compare it with the Proof-of-Stake algorithm (proof of proportion). We paid special attention to security issues and types of possible attacks.

Owning bitcoin
Reading a book by Richard Apodaca, developer and CEO of Metamolecular, does not require any knowledge of cryptography or computer science. The author reveals the hidden potential and risks of the most dynamic currency in the world. The book is paid, but Richard promises to return its full price, if after reading you think that it was not worth it.

Bitcoin and Cryptocurrency Technologies
The authors of this book are professors, associate professors and candidates of science at major universities. How do bitcoin and blockchain work? How safe are they? What about anonymity? Is it possible to regulate cryptocurrency? These are just a few questions that this book answers. For each chapter video is offered.

Smart contracts on bitcoin blockchain
Smart contracts are one of the most promising areas of Bitcoin development. In this paper, we consider the possibilities of their application. Here you will also find examples of working services that use the capabilities of smart contracts: wallets, fraud protection systems, off-blockchain payment systems.

The science of the blockchain
Roger Wattenhofer, a professor of IT from Zurich, writes about protocols, consensus-building algorithms and how to protect against attacks. The book will teach you to create fault-tolerant distributed systems. And here you can watch a short video in which Roger explains what blockchain and bitcoin are - chalk and blackboard create the impression that you are in a real university audience.

Blockchain: Blueprint for a New Economy
The main theme of the book is the theoretical principles of distributed registries. The author Melanie Swan (Melanie Swan), the founder of the Blockchain Research Institute, talks about the use of blockchain technology: finance, medicine, science and big data.

The Book of Satoshi
The book is for those who are interested in the virtual personality of Bitcoin creator Satoshi Nakamoto (Satoshi Nakamoto). It contains all Satoshi's publications, including emails and posts on the forums. The author of the book argues that the best way to understand bitcoin is to refer to the original source.

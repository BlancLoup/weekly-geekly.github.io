HTC Iolite review - part 2 - software-battery

In the first part of the review , the design and the iron-mechanical components of the apparatus were affected. Today I will touch on the software component and the communicator shell. I will touch upon usability issues and present the data on battery life to your attention.



I would like to start with a good one. HTC stuffed a large amount of RAM and ROM memory into Iolite, plus they put a stone at 528 MHz, which is undoubtedly felt when communicating with the device. The main reason for buying a new device was the lack of system resources of my HTC Touch, so this time I tried to pick up a communicator with the stuffing to the maximum ... I found Iolite.


On the face of a beautiful, kind inside ... Manila 2D


HTC's proprietary shell, Manila 2D (TouchFlo 3D), is installed on Iolite. I will not talk much about the shell, more than enough is written about Manila on the net. Let me just say that it is very pleasant and multifunctional. It is a bit strange that it is the first Manila, since the company already has in its arsenal the next version of the shell - TouchFlo 3D 2, which is used in the HTC Touch HD (Blackstone). Given the fact that Blackstone was six months before Iolite, it is not entirely clear why the company refused to use its latest developments in the new device. As if there was not, Manila 2D works great and is very responsive to the user's actions. Everything just flies! No delays and slowdowns. Finally, you can understand what the Taiwanese wanted to show us, since the advent of the HTC Diamond.

Since we are talking about the interface, it is worth mentioning the Start menu, which is increased under the control of your fingers. The same is true for the task manager in the upper right corner.

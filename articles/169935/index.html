Database of lost items with the search by name



Remember the well-known joke that if Bill Gates sees $ 100 on earth, then it is unprofitable for him to bend over a piece of paper, because he earns more in a second? This joke comes to mind when you look at a database of forgotten values in California. More than 17 million citizens have forgotten / lost / left valuables at 6.1 billion dollars. Among the lost - money, securities, checks, bank cells, jewelry and so on. It is clear that some of the owners have died or moved. But some simply can not call for the money, because the time spent will cost more.

The site has a search form by the name and surname of the owners of lost and forgotten things. Among them are many celebrities.

Here are some of them :

  • Steven Spielberg - Checks for $ 10,000 (George Lucas owes $ 35.92)
  • Richard Feynman - income from the liquidation of assets in the amount of $ 2,864.57 (plus another in the amount of $ 644.16)
  • The current governor, Jerry Brown, is another for $ 2,000 (plus something else for $ 9,505.02)
  • Michael Jackson - a safe deposit box (plus a fee from NBC for $ 93, rights to a share in the income from the development of subsoil)
  • Steve Wozniak - Various checks worth $ 269 (plus some Apple money)


Several checks from Stanford University were issued in the name of Sergey Brin for a total of $ 1,184.49 (plus another for $ 1,071.32).

It would be nice to have such a base of lost things in each city - surely each of us could find something forgotten.

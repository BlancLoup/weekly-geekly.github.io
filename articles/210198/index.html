Mathematician hacked dating site and found a dream girl



The 35-year-old mathematician Chris McKinlay from the University of California at Los Angeles took just 88 days to carry out the data mining of the questionnaires on a dating site and find a girl who is ideally suited to her character. Helped him in this access to supercomputers at the University of California, writes Wired.

In June 2012, McKinley worked on his doctoral dissertation on processing large data arrays and parallel computation methods. At some point he decided to distract himself and register on the OkCupid dating site. It should be noted that this site is based on Harvard students and uses a cunning system for calculating compatibility between potential partners. Each of them answers hundreds of questions about his character, habits and preferences.

Free App Store Monitoring and Statistics Tools

We will follow your progress, young Skywalker.
(c) Chancellor Palpatine.

The article discusses 3 sites for monitoring the rating / statistics of the AppStore, which I most often use. A brief description of each resource is given.
This article may be helpful:

  • iDevice software developers (both beginners and not so)
  • to marketers
  • to all who follow the rapid growth of mobile IT in general and the AppStore in particular.


The main source of information on the dynamics of sales of applications in the AppStore, of course, is iTunesConnect. However, as soon as applications become 2 and more, there are some difficulties. Moreover, naturally, iTunes is absolutely not suitable for analyzing the success of competitors, which sooner or later each developer has to face.

Most often I use 3 resources.

http://www.applyzer.com/
http://www.topappcharts.com/
http://www.appannie.com/

Consider in order each of them on the example of the wonderful program Star Walk for iPad.
The program is remarkable not only because it was created by our compatriots, but also received the Apple Design Awards in 2010, and this is worth something.

So.
http://www.applyzer.com/



For unregistered users, the site shows the current rating of the program, both in its category and in the overall standings. For registered users promise hourly updates of statistics. A few days ago, the statistics was updated at a frequency of 6 hours, but now the data is updated 1 time per day, which is probably due to some problems on the side of iTunes.

http://www.topappcharts.com/



Here we get not only the current value of the rating, but also a graph of changes in this rating for a certain period of time. It is also helpful to see when and how was the last best result.
It is also nice that you can look at the success of all products of one developer at once:


Here they are, relatives. It is clearly visible that having created 1 hit it is very difficult to repeat success.

The third participant in our review is

http://www.appannie.com/

Along with daily ratings


This site shows the most aesthetically pleasing graphs of changes in these very ratings.
The charts are conveniently placed mark any significant events, for example, a change in price or release of a new version.


Ratings, like graphics, can be viewed without registration.
Free registration allows developers to connect detailed statistics on their applications, as described here: http://www.appannie.com/tour/ But this is an amateur. IMPORTANT. Indicators of detailed statistics for registered users of this site are slightly different from those in iTunesConnect. So you need to be careful and check everything carefully.

Why is it so important to study graphics?
Often, graphs provide an opportunity to understand how certain applications achieve success or, conversely, fall out of sight of the general public.
Here, for example, another great program, this time from our Belarusian colleagues.


After an active period of experiments, with the application, something ceased to happen at all and the rating gradually began to decline. But a good program and could bring non-profit.

These three resources, it seems to me, provide an excellent opportunity to study and track the dynamics of the popularity of applications in the AppStore and can greatly facilitate market analysis and help in making the right marketing decisions.

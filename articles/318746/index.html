MegaFon in hard to reach places

In an effort to develop mobile communications, MegaFon does not stop at cities. We participate in the state program for the internetization of small settlements, we establish base stations in the tundra, mountains and even mines.



The other day we announced the installation of a base station in the village of Polovinka, Chebarkulsky District, Chelyabinsk Region, where only 351 people live. Prior to this, there was a very weak signal of voice mobile communication in this area, sometimes people could not even get through to doctors and rescuers. What to say about the mobile Internet, it was not at all. The main problem for the passage of the signal and the installation of base stations was primarily complex terrain and dense forest. Now, local residents have access to 3G Internet and speeds up to 10 Mbit / s. The residents of the neighboring village of Kryzhanovka will also feel improvements in communication.

This was made possible thanks to the participation of Megafon in the state program for the internetization of small settlements of 250 to 500 people, as well as with the support of the government of the Chelyabinsk region. Fiber-optic data transmission channel to the base station was provided by Rostelecom, which is also a participant in the program.

Now, on a festive night, fast Internet from MegaFon will make them truly next to their loved ones and friends, no matter how far from them they are. Improving communication, improving the quality of life of ordinary people - this is in our understanding of a responsible approach to business " ,
- noted the director of the Chelyabinsk branch of the company "Megaphone" Igor Ostatyuk.



Behind the Arctic Circle on the Kola Peninsula is one of the most unusual base stations of MegaFon. It is located in the tundra and covers the area where the roads of three directions converge: Murmansk - Teriberka - Misty. There are no settlements in this area, but communication is needed. In the tundra are not uncommon cases of complete road covering up in winter. Stuck in snow drifts and left without the ability to reach the rescue service, a person can easily die. Therefore, it was decided to install a second generation base station in the area with the planned installation of 3G equipment on it. The coverage of such a station is about 30 km.

When installing the base station, we had a problem. A power line passes very close by, but for connection it would be necessary to build a step-down substation. Line voltage is more than 100 thousand volts. The construction of the substation is an expensive and technologically complex project. But here came the natural conditions of the Kola Peninsula with its eternal winds. To power the station, it was decided to use a wind generator.


The turbine uses a 5 kW turbine. To generate enough energy for the base station to work, wind speeds from 10 meters per second are enough. In this area it is absolutely normal weather. If a calm happens, the station is additionally equipped with a diesel generator that starts automatically. Megaphone specialists monitor all technological processes both inside the station and the wind generator, and outside, remotely. In total in the Murmansk region four MegaFon base stations use wind generators for their work.

On the coast of the Barents Sea, mobile access speeds reach an average of 10-12 Mbit / s. This is enough for comfortable work and the use of most Internet services.

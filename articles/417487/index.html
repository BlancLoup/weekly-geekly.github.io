What is the data center M9?

From the history of "nine"


The history of MMTS-9, the largest technological platform in Moscow for interaction between Russian and international telecom operators, has more than two decades. Back in 1995, the main Moscow providers created on the basis of Moscow Intercity Telephone Station No. 9, the main shareholder of which a year earlier was Rostelecom, a point of mutual exchange of IP traffic. The equipment of the main Internet providers was placed there, there were international and long-distance communication channels.

The providers wanted to exchange their traffic with each other directly, optimizing the routes of its passage, which significantly reduced the data transfer time and the loading of expensive international communication channels. The coordination of MSK-IX was entrusted to the Russian Research Institute for the Development of Public Networks (RosNIIROS).

Today, MMTS-9 is the largest peering point in Russia, with more than 500 telecommunication companies among its clients.

Despite the solid history created on the basis of MMTS-9, the data center M9 has quite modern technical equipment, including engineering infrastructure, a structured cable system (FO / UTP5 / UTP6 connecting lines in the hall and according to MMTS-9), uninterruptible power supply systems, climate systems, gas extinguishing systems, monitoring and video surveillance.

In 2002, the ‚Äútelecommunication‚Äù modernization of the MSK-IX was carried out. The site became distributed by combining 10 points connected by a fiber-optic cable (Gigabit Ethernet). In 2013, the technological areas of MMTS-9 were updated and modernized. The machine hall has become a full-fledged data center with a high level of reliability. By 2015, the data center area for 1,500 racks (up to 47U, 600x1070 mm) was 6900 sq. M. m, the supplied electric power - 6.5 MW.

Currently, the M9 is one of the largest neutral sites in Moscow with equipment of a high level of complexity.

The station building houses telecommunication equipment with a power consumption of 5.5-6 kW per rack, however there are also high-loaded racks with power consumption up to 24 kW. Leasing space in the data center customers are given the opportunity to create an individual tight area.

After bringing in additional electric capacities of 8.8 MW, the power supply of MMTS-9 reached 15.3 MW.

Energy consumption of high-loaded racks reaches 24 kW.

Today, the Moscow Internet Exchange is a reliable, distributed system that includes ten equal points, connected by a high-speed backbone. Thanks to the MSK-IX distributed structure, the connectivity of providers to the IP traffic exchange system and high-speed transit between the MSK-IX nodes is significantly expanded. Creating a data center in a place like MMTS-9 allowed us to ensure the simplicity and convenience of interaction between operators and other owners of Internet networks.

M9 partners and customers are the largest companies, including MTS, VimpelCom, Megafon, TTK, Orange Business Services, and others. The site provides a platform for exchanging IP traffic between networks and a distributed network of DNS servers to support root-domain zones. Telecom operators, social networks, search engines, video portals, providers of cloud services, corporate and scientific and educational networks are connected to it.

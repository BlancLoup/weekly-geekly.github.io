And this thing is called "Picture".

What is the point?


The idea is simple to ugliness: the text must be accompanied by informative pictures on the topic. Most likely, this is understandable to everyone, but in practice not many people care about it, and those who care, out of turn, receive their advantages on the road to success.


Three weighty arguments "For"


  1. First - pictures, and after - the text. In any text, the picture stands out so strongly that we notice it faster than the actual text.
  2. The picture prepares for the text. Looking at the picture, we immediately recall a lot of related facts. After that, it is much easier to perceive what is written.
  3. Enough to see once. Words describe the main points. In the picture, in addition to the basics, you can lay a lot of different side trifles, each of which may be of interest to one extra reader.

Vivid example


A very vivid example in favor of pictures is the comparison of Apple iPod and Microsoft Zune HD sites, in particular, the pages of their technical characteristics.



Apple iPod VS. Microsoft Zune HD



We have two, radically different, approaches (one is common, the other is correct):

  1. Microsoft draws a page for those who like to read the technical parameters in the form of dry numbers. Who is not in the subject, he does not need to look and know.
  2. Apple works for the buyer - an ordinary person who, if he doesn‚Äôt understand the intricacies of technology, will still linger on even such a highly specialized page, because looking at the bright explanatory pictures is always interesting, and then look at it and figure it out.

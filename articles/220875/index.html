Experience choosing and ordering iBeacon

iBeacon , announced in the recent past by Apple micro-navigation technology and micro-positioning. iBeacon is a small (coin-sized) device that serves as a beacon. More specifically, it transmits via Bluetooth Low Energy a signal with its unique identifier. The beacon signal is detected by the smartphone with the ability to determine the distance to it. The detection range depending on the model of the lighthouse reaches 50-70 meters. The lighthouse works autonomously from a battery, which will last from six months to two, or even up to three years.

Possible applications are limited only by fantasy:
  • in-door navigation in shopping centers, at exhibitions and conferences;
  • in-place information and special offers for visitors of retail chains;
  • smartphone - a guide in museums;
  • Smartphone - the key to the apartment, car, hotel room or a pass to the office.

Chinese suppliers


I was looking for Chinese suppliers through Alibaba.com , a trading platform for manufacturers. Request "iBeacon" issued about 400 positions from 30 manufacturers. A distinctive feature of this category is that they simply sell a piece of hardware with documentation and a minimal set of tools to configure it. In fact, this is a first-hand product.


Not all the issue turned out to be relevant, in the end I requested samples from approximately a dozen companies for the test. To at least somehow reduce the risks associated only with suppliers with the status of "Gold Supplier". Then I chose based on the speed and quality of feedback.

I chose three suppliers, ordering 3-4 test samples from each:

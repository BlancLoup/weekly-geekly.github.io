Vimpelcom and Warner Music take on mobile content in Russia

Warner Music International (WMI) goes into the jungle of the Russian mobile content industry, Times Online reports. And immediately with heavy artillery: WMI entered into a coalition with VimpelCom , and the operator has a 45 million audience. Beginning August 15, Beeline subscribers will be able to download WMI products - legally protected and probably high-quality.

Ringtones, realtones, pictures and video clips from WMI will be sold at market prices. The price of the ringtone will be about 32 rubles, realtone - 57. For some products they will make a surcharge for exclusivity. You can download them from the site, wap-site, using the sim-menu and VAS-cards of the content show-window of "Beeline", reports "Compulenta" .

But there is an important nuance - the western leader is jealous of intellectual property issues, so its content is protected from distribution. Sharing fresh with friends will not work - DMR (Digital Rights Manegement) technology will be introduced into each file, making it impossible to send it via SMS, MMS, Bluetooth and infrared. About 400 phone models support the standard - about 70% of Beeline subscribers have this.

DMR is still quite crude technology. The project will use its first version, which allows only blocking the transfer of melodies from the device to the device, reports Mobil.Ru . However, by October 2006, plans are to introduce a limited license scheme - when purchasing a package, it will be possible to transfer files through ports or SMS.

At the same time, the Russian market has a reputation for being completely pirated - in 2005, 90% of the $ 1 billion turnover of the music market was acquired by manufacturers and sellers of counterfeit goods. The Western partner can not but embarrass this circumstance. Therefore, Warner is planning to make money on cheap content. For reasons of Aleksandro Zubillag, one of the top managers of WMI, selling music tracks in Russia via mobile networks is senseless for now.

Evernote Developer Contest Finalists Revealed!



We admit that it was a little scary to announce the contest of developers. We wondered if anyone wanted to participate, and if so, how much quality and innovative the proposed works would be. However, after the first applications began to arrive, a sigh of relief was carried around the office - the applications presented were quite good.

About 1,000 developers from different parts of the globe took part in the competition, and after many hours of testing and fierce debate, we were finally able to reduce the list to six of the most attractive works. In our decisions, we proceeded from six main messages:
  • How well does the work look and complete?
  • Does it open up Evernote from an unexpected side?
  • Does the job respond to the needs expressed by our user community?
  • Can it inspire people to create a new type of application?
  • Well, in general, how cool was the application?


Your vote matters


Below you can see a list of six final works. The authors of each of them have already received $ 5,000 and are now fighting for first place in the competition and the grand prix of $ 50,000. If you have some free time, pay attention to these works, watch their videos, try the applications in action, if possible and vote. Try to vote for an idea that you like, even if its implementation is not currently available for your device or platform. The best way to encourage developers to work further on a great idea is to express their sympathy for their creations.

Vote now!

You can vote once a day. You will be asked to log in on Twitter or Facebook, so that we are sure that the vote is fair. Do not worry, we will not make any entries in these services on your behalf. Community selection is counted as one of three final votes, determining the winner. Our jury will have the second vote, and the third as the result of a personal vote at the Evernote Trunk Conference.

Notablemeals helps you easily memorize your favorite dishes. Using a simple interface, you can find the right restaurant, take notes on the dish, shoot it on camera, make a voice recording and assign a rating. Then it all fits into Evernote in a convenient, structured way with excellent formatting.

4. Sniptastic!

Sniptastic! It is a set of tools for organizing and sharing code fragments. Store code snippets in Evernote and view them in Sniptastic in a format formatted for the selected programming language - a convenient way to always have the necessary fragment at hand.

5. Touchanote

Touchanote complements the organization and storage of Evernote notes using Near Field Communication (NFC) technology, which allows you to bind records to any surface with an NFC tag and open them with one touch. Simply assign notes in your account NFC-tag and then click on this tag to open the corresponding entry. Imagine that you touch your refrigerator to get a shopping list before going to the store or to the table in the audience to open notes.

6. Zendone

Zendone is a personal productivity tool based on Getting Things Done. The application offers a powerful and perfectly executed interface for implementing a GTD workflow, using Evernote to collect and store all your projects and tasks.

Two more winners ahead


We still have two additional nominations - a student prize and a prize for creativity, the winners of which will be announced at the Evernote Trunk Conference. The student prize, as you can imagine, will get the best work from the student, and the prize for creativity relies on the most unexpected and surprising use of Evernote. The winner of each of these nominations will receive $ 10,000.

Congratulations!


We heartily congratulate our finalists and want to express our deep gratitude to all the developers who took part in the competition. Most of the work is fantastic, incredibly useful applications that will take their place in the Evernote Gallery in the coming weeks and months.

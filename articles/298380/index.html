Moscow authorities are considering a ban Uber



Taxi services are actively developing - the capitalization and popularity of Uber in many countries proves this once again. Despite the demand for service in Russia, the Moscow authorities may ban it because of an unsigned agreement on the disclosure of taxi routes.

The agreement is intended to resolve controversial issues for the Moscow authorities in the interaction of Uber with taxi drivers, but its agreement was delayed for several months. According to the Deputy Mayor of Moscow, Maxim Liskutov, company representatives have been postponing the signing for several months in a row.

‚ÄúFirst: Uber is obliged to work only with legal taxi drivers who have permission for taxi activities. And second: they will pass us data on the movement of their taxi cars around the city. The data will be impersonal - we only need the tracks. Uber were ready to sign such an agreement by the end of 2015. Then, referring to their corporate procedures, they asked us to extend this period until the end of January. And then their last request was to wait ten days - they again refer to their corporate procedures.

If Uber does not sign the agreement, we will demand that its work in Moscow be banned, ‚ÄùLiskutov said in an interview with Gazeta.ru.


The Gett and Yandex.Taxi services competing with Uber have agreed to transfer personal data on taxi cars and their employment. According to Liskutov, this information is used to analyze traffic congestion. Not only the city authorities are dissatisfied with the work of Uber - representatives of the Russian motorists federation applied to the FAS to check the taxi order services last year.

Similarly , protests against Uber were carried out by taxi drivers in France, when the movement throughout the city was almost paralyzed because of several thousand protesters. In addition to the civilized rally, some of the taxi drivers resorted to more radical methods, burning tires and blocking the routes to the airports, but this did not lead to a total ban on Uber in the country.

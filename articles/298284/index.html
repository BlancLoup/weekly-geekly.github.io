Fake activity



Who knows reddit?

This is another user community, only it is not focused on any particular topic, unlike Moz or GrowthHackers.

Wikipedia Reddit is described as a social news site where registered users can post links to their favorite information. In addition, heated discussions boil there, you can promote your business, earn a rating and much more. The site looks old-fashioned, but it is very popular. Sometimes there you can get such information that you can hardly find anywhere else.

In general, the entire site is based on user activity. If they actively download links, vote for the best materials, the site lives. If there is no activity, then it makes no sense for visitors to go there.

Therefore, at the initial stage, in 2005, when it was first created, the main problem was how to create activity? We needed a critical mass of users, materials, comments, after which the rest will see the value of the site and begin to register there.

The critical mass itself does not reach. Only in exceptional cases does the product sell itself. As a rule, you first need to plow hard to attract the first users. And only then, when the project earns a good reputation, one can hope for organic growth.

How was the activity created on Reddit?



The founders of their own handles riveted fake accounts. They did this for several months, imitating real activity on the site. Due to this, real visitors had the impression that the site was alive and there were real users on it. After several months, hard work began to pay for itself and organic growth began.

The same strategy was used by the Quora website (the most popular question and answer service abroad). The two founders, without interruption, created new questions and answered them themselves. Then the first workers, beta testers joined them. Thanks to this, the database of questions and answers began to fill up, the site came to life, organic growth began.

Yes, it is not easy. Yes, it is often necessary to do this manually (although, I think that some can automate such processes). But when there are no other ways to get out of the ‚Äúvalley of death‚Äù, then such a hack can be salutary.

Think about this option if you develop something like an online forum, where initially there will always be a dilemma ‚Äúempty site means no users‚Äù and ‚Äúlack of users means empty site‚Äù. Although the same situation exists in marketplaces and in social networks, where this dilemma must always be solved.

Personal contact with the target audience



Consider the examples of Tinder (dating application) and Alibaba (the largest site for the sale of goods on the Internet).

The Tinder application had about the same problem as a quora or reddit. For girls to start using the app, there must be guys. For guys to start using the app, there must be girls there. Simply put, you need a critical mass of users.

Attracting the first users of the director of marketing Whitney Woolf was engaged personally. The company organized a tour, during which Whitney traveled around the campuses of renowned US universities.

She performed in women's clubs and promoted the app. After the meeting, the girls installed it. Then Whitney went to the men's club, where she showed beautiful, free, but most importantly - active - girls in the application, who are open to dating.



Thanks to this tour, the number of users increased from 5 thousand to 15. Now the tyder has 50 million users, 10 million active every day. If at the very beginning of the development of a startup, Whitney didn‚Äôt go to independently involve each user, then this startup could die without becoming popular.

Jack Ma (the head of the largest trading holding Alibaba Group) was doing the same thing. In the early 2000s, he personally traveled to each factory to talk with executives and show them how Alibaba and Taobao websites will help sell their products.

This is a time consuming process and it takes a lot of time. But sometimes this is an inevitable stage in the development of the project.

Referral hacks from the world of offline



Referral programs work not only online. There are many cases, as offline companies use this technique to attract new customers.

For example, one pool service company gave each of its clients a brochure about their business and asked them to transfer it to their neighbors who have swimming pools. Brad some, you say. Who will advertise their services to their neighbors?

But the trick was that the neighbor and the one who gave him this brochure received the same discount on all the services of the company. All in the black. That is why such a referral program worked.

By the way, one of the important points of any referral program is that both participants should receive some kind of benefit. Otherwise, there are cases when only the one who recommends benefits. What then is the sense for another person to accept his offer? No

Let's consider another case of using referral programs in the offline world. The insurance company from Zurich offered to each new client to invite their friends, acquaintances, colleagues to use its services. For each invited client, both program participants received a $ 100 shopping coupon. And this option to find new customers turned out to be more profitable compared to other channels of attraction.

By the way, one of the factors of the effectiveness of referral programs is a psychological principle, according to which people trust the recommendations from friends, relatives, colleagues much more than anyone from outside. According to a Nielsen study , 92% of people believe such recommendations and base their choice on this information.

Interesting hack from Huoston Airport


By all the rules of business development, Houston Airport listens to the target audience - conducts surveys, collects feedback and improves the service delivery process.

One survey focused on the issues that most stress customers. The answer was unequivocal - problems in obtaining luggage. And most of all, clients said that the process took too much time - it took 9 minutes to get the luggage.



Well, since customers are so annoying, the airport listened to feedback, invested several million dollars and changed the process of baggage claim. The result - the waiting time was reduced to 6 minutes. It seems to be good, at 33% optimized the process.

Article in topic - Services for user surveys

After that, we conducted a survey on the second round - the answer remained the same. 9 minutes, 6 minutes - still long. Began to look for a new way to solve the problem.

The idea turned out to be brilliant - they simply took and changed the place where the baggage was received. Now it was much further from the place of arrival of passengers. While the person reached this place, the necessary 6 minutes had already passed, and he did not have to languish in anticipation.

Praise to the person who was able to come up with such an unusual solution to the problem.

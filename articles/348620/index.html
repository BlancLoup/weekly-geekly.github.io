As we turned the calendar and made it an art object

In the Circuit, there are 54 designers who design product interfaces for every fourth company in Russia. And our designers write Guides , where they share their experiences. And now, more than a year, the guys have been creating calendars that have made noise in the company.


What is the project "Calendar" and how controversy treated him, tell Julia Krasilnikova, the project curator and designer Kontur.Market .


In 2016, Sergey Solovyov, the head of all product designers, came up with a calendar project so that our designers could take a break from everyday activities and show imagination free of frames. We wanted to make not just a reference point in time and space for internal use, but a mood, a peculiar spirit of the month. Now there are 15 calendars in the project, each of which is the personal association of the author with the selected month, a creative message or an unusual idea.



When we offered designers to draw calendars, they happily agreed: someone immediately took up the task, someone chose a specific month. Now the project has become so popular that, among others, there are, for example, colleagues from the development and sales department. Most likely, in May there will be an experiment - we will give the calendar to the designer.


Calendar as art


Over the past year we have had calendars, posters, calendars, poems, calendars, posters. All of them, like any art objects, have supporters and opponents. Perhaps the most discussed was the May calendar of Mitya Astafyev. The author had such associations with this month: peace, labor, May, International, Comintern , revolution, Mayakovsky, avant-garde , constructivism , abstract painting, Kandinsky, Malevich, VHUTEMAS , De Stijl , Mondrian . That's what came out of it:



In Staffa, our internal social network, some expressed rapid approval of the new calendar, others just as violently scolded the author and his creation. Most contourists said that the calendar should fulfill its intended purpose - to help navigate the days of the week.


The December calendar of 2017, too, was more about art, rather than the sequence of days in the month.



Sasha Khramtsov, user interface designer and calendar author describes the concept as follows:


- According to my observations, December is the craziest and most hectic month. It seems life itself will end here: plans, work, study, the opportunity to buy gifts, plan a vacation. In December, many people look back, sum up, try to complete their work, close their budgets, correct their estimates. At this moment, more than ever, there is a transience and lack of time. A slight madness of the pre-New Year rush reigns around us and is difficult to ignore. The closer to the end of December, the greater the emotional pressure. Somewhere there is a limit when it's all over. My calendar is a countdown to that very day - the last day of 2017.

Calendar that went beyond the project

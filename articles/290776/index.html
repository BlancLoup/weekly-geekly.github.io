Interview




Typically, an interview for a position in IT consists of several steps:

The call from the recruiter where usually within 15-30 minutes all the basic requirements for the position as technical and for payment are clarified and, as I have already mentioned, legal aspects with the right to work and as mentioned above by the relationship between the companies.

Technical telephone interview - pre-screen ‚Äúpreskrin‚Äù. It usually lasts from 30 minutes to one hour. The purpose of the technical telephone interview is to find out how the professional level of the candidate fits the open position in the company. Often the candidate is asked to write the code during the interview so that it makes sense to look in advance how it works so as not to hesitate. I had to use Google Docs or for example collabedit.com.

Interview in the company of the employer. Here it is usually assumed that you will spend a few hours, get acquainted with the company itself, its product, manager and team in which it is supposed to work. Interview format


Each interview usually assumes the following format:

The introductory part begins with the introduction of the interview participants and a brief description of the position under discussion.
Questions to the candidate. Here it is desirable to give a detailed answer to each question, unless it is specifically stated that it can be short. It is welcome to present your own question in your own words, ask leading questions and make sure that you understand the essence of the question correctly. It is not recommended in this section to ask the question itself that it is against the time limit and format of the interview. For your questions you will be given time that is usually assumed.
Questions of the candidate. The rules of good tone suggest that you are well acquainted with the company's product and this should be followed by asking questions. Usually questions are prepared in advance, still at home, after studying the company's website and the description of the position itself.

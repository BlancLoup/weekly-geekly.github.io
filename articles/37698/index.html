Online Patenting at the United States Patent and Trademark Office

This is possible. Maybe for someone this is not news, but for me it has become almost a discovery: you can patent your invention in the United States Patent and Trademark Office, in abbreviated form - USPTO (that's where all Google and Patent are patented ) without getting up from the computer. In the literal sense of the word. My whole procedure took exactly 15 minutes. I hasten to share.

Information for consideration:

1. The USPTO is the American Rospatent, with only brains.
2. The USPTO accepts applications for patenting from residents of any countries, including from the Russian Federation.
3. They have such a wonderful thing as a temporary application (Provisional Application) - this is a priority for an invention in a simplified and accelerated form. A kind of announcement of your future patent.
4. Provisional Application (hereinafter Provisional Application) is valid for 12 months, after which it needs to be confirmed by ‚Äúthis patent‚Äù, otherwise it expires.
5. Temporary application is not published anywhere.
6. The application may be written in Russian (but the translation must be submitted no later than 16 months from the date of submission).
7. The application costs from 105 dollars. It is possible and more, but it depends only on the amount of information that you try to stuff into the application. 105 was enough for me.
8. Registration of the application is done using the EFS-Web web application. It can: add an application to the USPTO database, check this application, accept a fee for accepting an application and send an application receipt receipt to the applicant.
9. I am not a patent attorney, not a lawyer and in general no-no-no. Just an amateur. Therefore, if you decide to get a patent this way - check. Although I, like, everything turned out.

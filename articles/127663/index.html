What prevents to hold large-scale conferences?

This topic is inspired by the topic , which is very accurately described the problems of holding conferences in Kiev, but is fully acceptable for any cities.

We have been holding IT conferences for 5 years and it cannot be said that we have found ideal conditions for their holding.
So, in order.


1. The room
Indeed, in each of the cities where we held conferences (Moscow, St. Petersburg, Minsk, Kharkov, Yaroslavl, Kazan), there is a real headache with the premises. Of course, rooms with a spaciousness "in the stadium" are usually located. But the premises, so that you can actually create 3 streams with normal capacity (at least 200-300 people in a section) can be counted on fingers, but you can not count on them either.
But just to find such a room is not enough. We need elementary convenience for the participants: a spacious hall in front of the halls (for socializing, coffee breaks), good restaurant traffic (to avoid stunts).
The equipment of such sites is important. Often there is a large hall, and sockets have to be found in the most unexpected and most uncomfortable places. Those. to pull up stands, laptops, cell phones and other electronics, you need to pull extension cords and often along the most passable space (which naturally causes participants to cling to cords).

2. Internet
I declare with all confidence that there is no internet anywhere on the sites! (despite all assurances of the lessor).
If you do not take care of the internet far in advance, agreeing with a good company (not free of charge of course), then the Internet will be through the stump deck (if at all). People armed with laptops, modern devices, "put" the Internet for one or two.
But the problem is that these landlords themselves do not want to allow providers to their territory. Often such a negotiation process is generally impossible. It is also often associated with the machinations of competitors who staked out a place on a specific site.
It is good if the problem can be somehow leveled with the help of Yota, but far from everywhere it is (even in the cities with the declared coverage). I do not consider the option with 3G at all.
Conclusion: the Internet is only when we ourselves brought it.

3. Power

Almost always on the sites is not allowed third-party catering. The internal price tag is particularly brutal. For the price tag, which expose hotels you can safely and much tastier eat in the restaurant. Throw off the price tag is often simply unrealistic.
Patency of restaurants is often terrible. 300 people at the conference have to be divided into 2 shifts, which does not exclude crush, but affects the conference program grid.

When we were looking for a room in Moscow, I was unpleasantly surprised by the frantic price level in the absence of service. Comes to mind the thesis "Moscow is all shhavaet" why does that work? That is why the entrance ticket to the majority of Moscow conferences from 20 000 rubles.
As for the regions, the situation there is better with the service, but by no means better in terms of the cost of rent and food. It would seem that with fat rage? And the problem is the lack of competition. Finding a good venue in a region can be orders of magnitude more difficult. And they know it perfectly.

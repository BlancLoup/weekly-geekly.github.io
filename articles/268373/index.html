A long way to the App Store, or how to spend 43 days to pre-moderate the application

Last year we talked about the development of the Kontur.Elba application for Android (and more than once ). Having implemented all our plans in it, we set about developing a version for iOS , which recently appeared in the App Store. A post about development using Xamarin.Forms will be later, but for now let's focus on a seemingly trivial matter: laying out the application on the App Store and talking to moderators.

Let's start with the conclusions that we got in the process of communicating with the Apple moderators:

  • the App Store Review Guidelines should be carefully re-read: recommendations change, and what was allowed yesterday could be banned today;
  • Apple moderators do not always carefully study the application and may miss content inside;
  • you need to carefully read the responses of moderators;
  • the solution after verification may not comply with the rules of the App Store. An example in the text below: the requirement to connect in app purchase for an application without the support of paid features;
  • in incomprehensible situations it is better to immediately write an appeal in order not to spend a week waiting for an answer. If the answer is not satisfactory or incomprehensible - new appeals;
  • moderation can take a long time: at least 8 days, of which 7 days - waiting in line for checking.

We present a log of communication with moderators with unexpected turns, investigations, total misunderstanding, but with a happy conclusion of the storyline.

Startup philosophy. How the future affects the founders



Not so long ago, the topic of startups was one of the exotic on the Internet. But if we look at it today, we will see tons of information from this area. Already as hackneyed discs, themes of metrics and business models, landing pages and conversation scripts, content marketing and direct, etc., endlessly scroll. However, there are still unsolved topics.

For example. Information about building startups is growing exponentially, why then the emergence of new successful projects clearly does not follow this trend? Are technical issues important for project success or do you need something else? What characteristic should a startup have and what do its founders need to develop a business?

Someone prefers to read articles, and someone to watch the video. Video content and articles complement each other, not copy.


All successful projects are united by one common feature.

Google, Facebook, Skype, AirBnB, Uber and others have something in common. Maybe this is a business model, an audience, the amount of income per visitor or benefits to society?

Not at all.

The general sign of a successful startup is whether it changes the future or not. If you think well, this is the whole essence or philosophy of a startup.

Why so, and not otherwise?

Because it is the only criterion that cannot be undone or refuted. It is this criterion that allows a startup to develop into a big business.

Every investor's favorite question sounds like this - ‚ÄúAnd what is the use of your project?‚Äù. But this is the wrong question. Because benefit is a subjective concept, based on today's user experience. For different people there is a completely different benefit from a startup, and sometimes harm. Try to answer the question yourself - what is the use of Vkontakte or Facebook? For some, this is a platform for communicating with friends, for others a way of meeting new people, for some it is a sales channel for their products, for the fourth it is entertainment from watching cats, for the fifth it is a distraction that is hard to get rid of, for the sixth it is self-affirmation, for the seventh - avoiding life problems, for the eighth - a means of learning, for the ninth - listening to music online, for the tenths - a means of receiving news. The list is endless. And for those with social networks a distraction, Facebook, on the contrary, is a harmful service.

Is Uber useful? For passengers of course. But for ordinary taxi drivers, the service poses a threat and meets with violent resistance in many countries.

But the main and unshakable is that social networks have changed the future communications of people. In fact, they changed humanity by changing people's habits, their schedule of the day, ways of entertainment.

If we talk about the business model, then immediately come to mind examples of well-known projects that did not have any business model, some do not have them after a few years. What does this mean? The business model will be invented sooner or later. But it will not change the essence of the project itself.

With the audience going on the same thing. Startups designed for a young audience, over time, and increase the age. Skeptics, like early followers, are drawn into services over time.

But the feature of a startup to change the future, remains with him from the moment of foundation to the end.

What did AirBnB do? He changed the future of home renting. Removed intermediaries and allowed the landlord and the tenant to interact directly with each other. That is, before people gathered in another city, they booked a hotel, and not someone's empty house. Now millions of people are opting for apartments, houses, rooms. There is a changed world.

What did Uber do? The service has changed the future of taxi services. He removed the extra add-ons, opened the possibility to call a taxi with one click, and the driver to receive orders in the area where he is located.

Instagram has changed the future of our photo albums. Now they are not gathering dust on the shelves, but immediately open up to friends.

Google has changed the future of files and archives. Everything can now be found using the command line and at least an approximate description of what we are looking for.

Twitter has changed the future format of the news. 140 characters are easier to write than a double-page article.

Skype has changed the future of voice communication. Now this video link and the opportunity to see the interlocutor.

Tesla Motors has changed the future of the automotive industry. The electric motor has become a real replacement for the internal combustion engine.



If you look at any successful startups, we will see this fact of a changeable future everywhere. They have different ways of monetization, different audiences, different benefits. But this feature unites them.

How does your startup change the future? Think about it. Perhaps this is your mission, everything else is just a means.

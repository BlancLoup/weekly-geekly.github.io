Krasnoyarsk Dev2Dev 2.0 Developer Conference

This is a ‚Äúguest post‚Äù from a team of dev2Dev community enthusiasts. I am very pleased that such an initiative continues to exist and really want to support them, at least informationally.



Krasnoyarsk is a distant Siberian city and a remote it-province. But on May 30, 2015, our neighbors will envy our activity. This day will be the second conference of software developers Dev2Dev, version 2.0. The conference is organized by a young, it-named community of the same name, bringing together specialists from the city‚Äôs companies. We are less than a year old, but we are growing. From the very beginning, we worked so that in Krasnoyarsk there finally appeared a sharply welded local event with a large active audience and visiting reports.

The concept of Dev2Dev community events is simple: free entry, quality content and lots of communication for participants and speakers. The event is first of all fun, dating and pumping energy. Strong reports and interesting speakers make this possible, but the event itself is created by the participants. May 30, we are waiting for 200-250 people. The new conference will bring together reports on enterprise development, functional programming, design, application testing, project and team management. Speakers come from all over Siberia - Novosibirsk, Omsk, Kemerovo. A special guest will arrive from Chicago.

The event program is rich

Filippov Vladislav: Where we roll or what to prepare for .
Vladislav is the founder of Svarog Systems inc., Chicago. Report on industry prospects, new trends, and who the software developer will be in the future. News from the front and own reflections of a successful businessman.

Pischasova Tatyana: Good Enough Testing, or the minimum required testing .
Tatyana is a leading tester from 2gis, Novosibirsk. Small teams find it difficult to set up testing for many reasons. Tatiana offers her gentleman's kit for building the testing process.

Tyumentsev Eugene: Mathematical justification of the SOLID principles .
Eugene CEO of HWdTech, LLC, Omsk. Everyone is familiar with the abstract principles of SOLID; these are almost philosophical concepts. Eugene will present another point of view in his report.

Granin Alexander: Lenses - combinatorial data manipulation .
Alexander is a developer from Kaspersky Lab, Novosibirsk. Functional programming stands firmly on the immunity of data, which is not always easy to manipulate. Lenses are not exotic, but a handy tool. 45 minutes of hardcore on Dev2Dev 2.0.

Anton Maksimov: Application of Neo4j graph database in an Enterprise application .
Anton is a programmer from 2GIS, Novosibirsk. Anton will talk about the technology from the internal kitchen 2GIS, thanks to which the company's cartographic services build road graphs. This is a graph database DB Neo4j.

Anna Tarasenko: YAGNI principle in project management .
Anna director of 7bits, Omsk. ‚ÄúYou ain't gonna need it‚Äù is a principle that saves us from programming unnecessary features and saves us from overdraft. Anna experienced that she works just as well in project management, and is ready to share her experience.

Marina Semekhina and Ilya Gorbarov: IT staff motivation - step by step .
Marina and Ilya executive and technical director of the company studio Atvinta, respectively, the city of Kemerovo. Favorite theme of developers and managers headache. How to set up a team to work, how to keep it and constantly finish building for tomorrow. About this in a report from the studio "Atvinta."

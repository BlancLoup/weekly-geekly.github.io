Adult game

Last week, Sony Ericsson announced several high-profile agreements that shed light on the prospects of both the newest Sony Ericsson Xperia PLAY gaming smartphone and the development of the gaming segment on Android in general.

So, at the Game Developers Conference 2011 in San Francisco, which ended March 4, Irish company Havok , one of the leading developers of software for games in the world, announced support for the Android platform , and the first game smartphone that will be available to the capabilities of their famous physics engine Havok Physics has become the Sony Ericsson Xperia PLAY.

HAVOK demo video:



Havok Technology Technologies is used in more than two hundred world-famous games, including Halo 2, Halo 3, Halo Wars, BioShock, BioShock 2, Assassin's Creed, Resident Evil 5, Red Faction: Guerrilla, Uncharted2, and others. And even people far from the world of games are familiar with the capabilities of their technologies, which have been used for more than 10 years to create special effects for the best Hollywood blockbusters.

The physics engine is a computer program that simulates the physical laws of the real world in the virtual world with varying degrees of approximation. Game physics engines are used in computer games as a component of the game engine. In this case, it should work in real time, that is, to reproduce the physical processes in the game with the same speed at which they take place in the real world. However, the accuracy of calculations is not required from the game physics engine. The main requirement is visual realism.

At the moment, the Havok physics engine is the second most popular in the world. Some intrigue is made by the fact that the company that developed the PhysX engine that overtook it belongs to Nvidia, which had tried in its time with Intel and bought Havok.

What's next?


The gaming industry has adopted Xperia Play. Now move for the consumer.

The cost of games will be from 4.99 to 7.99 euros - the final cost will be determined by the developer. Access to games will be only on PlayStation Certified devices. The average size of the game is 200 MB, with a memory card size of 8 GB (with the possibility of expansion up to 16 GB and 32 GB).

The assets of the smartphone are multiplayer (via wi-fi), cross-platform games and games specially designed for PLAY - in fact, Xperia PLAY opens up a huge new market for PlayStation Certified devices.

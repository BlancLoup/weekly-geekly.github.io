Update Opera Mini 7.1 for J2ME and BlackBerry

Today a new version of Opera Mini 7.1 browser for J2ME and BlackBerry platforms has been released. In addition to bug fixes and improvements in browser stability, this version has a new, redesigned download manager, opening up new possibilities for working with files uploaded to the phone, be it documents, music, video and any other data. Some details under the cut.

It is no secret that on mobile phones the connection to the network is not always stable. Many people probably know the feeling when downloading a file of 10 MB in size stops at around 9.99 MB when your subway car enters the tunnel and the network is lost to the next station.

In Opera Mini 7.1, under such circumstances, the download will pause and automatically resume as soon as you are in the network service area.

Also, the processing of downloaded files has become much more convenient than before. On the BlackBerry platform, file access is provided directly from the browser, and owners of J2ME-based devices can choose to open the file in the media player installed on the phone or save it in the phone‚Äôs memory. The list of new bootloader features is as follows:

- Faster loading of small files (up to 15 MB)
- Function to pause / resume download
- Ability to play downloadable media files without saving
- Save files to folders of your choice
- Remembering the last folder used to save
- Ability to rename downloaded files
- Simultaneous loading of multiple files
- Visual loading indicator

Opera Mini browser works on more than 3000 mobile phone models of the most diverse price category. Thanks to data compression technology up to 90%, it saves time and money. You can download the Opera Mini browser from the official website m.opera.com

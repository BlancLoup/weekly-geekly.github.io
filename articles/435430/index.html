The coolest new CES 2019



In Las Vegas today, the main exhibition of consumer electronics has begun. More than 180,000 people and 4,400 companies came to Las Vegas to show and see the latest gadgets and technology. Among those who boast of their technical advances will be Huawei, Intel, Samsung, LG, Asus, Qualcomm, Hyundai, TCL, Sony, Toyota and Hisense. Some of them have already shown the devices with which they are going to surprise us in the new year, while others have released the long-awaited technologies that have been developed for many years.



Intel finally announced its first 10 nm processors


The company introduced the user-defined Ice Lake, Lake Field and Cascade Lake series for servers, which are already working (the computers on them were presented on the stage). It is expected another leap in performance that first touches the PC, and then mobile devices.




Prior to this, the company, as is known, had difficulties with the transition from 14 nm process technology to 10 nm. Work on the technology has been underway for more than four years (Cannon Lake's limited release does not count). But now the last problems, sort of, have been eliminated, and the chips are ready for shipment. New Intel processors will be able to deliver more power from 37% of the size of previous models. The density of transistors is almost three times higher than that of 14 nm, more than 100 million per square millimeter. It is expected that Ice Lake will give at least 15% more power than the current Coffee Lake, with the same power consumption. Or, which is important for servers, - 30% less power consumption with the same performance.

Most likely, the announcement of the Ryzen 3, we still see, but a little later. And real fans, fingers crossed, are still hoping for news about AMD Navi.

Asus Morthership GZ700, insanely powerful gaming laptop


Nvidia finally announced that its RTX video cards are starting to ship in laptops. According to the company, the first 40 models with powerful graphics are ready to start since January 29.


The first of them was shown at the exhibition model ROG Zephyrus S GX700, ultra-thin notebook with rabid characteristics. Huawei MateBook 13 - MacBook Air Killer


Huawei is now seriously at war with Apple in the field of smartphones and tablets, and wins on many fronts. Apple's profit is falling, and Huawei is gradually winning back even the US market. The leader is still Samsung, but its market share is falling (-14% in 2018), while the Chinese are growing (Huawei + 33%, Xiaomi + 21%). And now, Huawei is aiming to kill its competitors in the laptop market. MateBook 13 directly aims to compete with Apple. The same form factor as the MacBook Air 13, very, very similar design, only more power and less price. The thickness of the case is only 1.49 cm, also less than even the last Air (1.56 cm).




The new MateBook 13 will run on Intel Core i5-8265U or Core i7-8565U processors, with 8 GB of RAM, 512 GB of storage and Nvidia MX150 graphics. All this under a 13.3-inch display with a 2K resolution (2160x1440) and touchscreen capabilities. The cost in the US will start at $ 999 ($ ‚Äã‚Äã200 cheaper than Apple). Sales will start on January 29 at Amazon and Newegg.



Samsung Notebook Odyssey - the most productive notebook company


Judging by everything, the manufacturers decided that the future of gaming is on laptops. This year, quite unexpectedly, the main emphasis is on them. At least, Samsung first decided to make a laptop specifically for this niche. Its monster weight is from 2.36 kg (the older version has 3.5 kg), the case thickness is 1.99 cm, the display is 144 Hz and Full HD. Inside, there is a six-core Intel Core i7 processor and a GeForce RTX 2080 video card. Judging by the name, it should help people not only enjoy the usual games on super settings, but also dive into VR with the new headset from Samsung HMD Odyssey .




The version of the laptop with a 15-inch screen comes out in February, with a 17-inch one in April. The price of the smaller version starts at $ 1199.



LG folding TV


Not really a gadget, but so far one of the most impressive technologies at the exhibition. True, not very cool, that our TV takes up so much space in the room? And attention is concentrated on itself, and the overall design is destroyed. Nothing good. So, the flagship LG TV in 2019 will have a screen that can roll into a small nightstand from the bottom. Giant 65-inch panel does not physically take up space in the room.




The whole TV is placed in a small shelf on the floor. There even was a place for a powerful Dolby Atmos audio system. Screen - 4K resolution, OLED, 3 mm thick. Press the button, and he slowly enters its base. Press again - and it gradually rises, in about 10 seconds. Noise, the process is quite fascinating. All ports - in the nightstand, behind.




In general, now, when it is not necessary, the TV has learned to completely disappear. The child will not break it, the dog will not jump on him. And do not beat a hole in the wall, and then be nervous that he falls from there.




The device looks cool, but the price of the novelty is likely to be like that of a good car. Below $ 10,000 for this, no one expects (most likely, it will be much more expensive). But this is only the beginning - if such "folding" TVs do not show any serious defects, and the mass market will pick up the idea, in five or six years we will be able to take it at the same price as now conventional devices with Smart TV and 3D .

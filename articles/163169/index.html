Google launched and made free its music service Google Music


A few weeks ago, Google began testing its music service Google Music on the European market. Now this system has become available to American users, and what is most surprising, for free! Competitors, from Apple in the form of iTunes Match and Amazon, with their Cloud Player takes $ 25 / year.

With Google Music, users will be able to synchronize their music library on a computer with an online collection much faster, and not spend several hours downloading gigabytes of tracks to the cloud. In total, Google allows you to match up to 20 thousand songs, as described in the company's blog . Music is played in streaming mode at a speed of up to 320 kilobits per second and allows you to find matches for 20 thousand tracks. Songs added to the cloud can be listened to on a variety of devices, from PCs to tablets and Android smartphones. If you previously had to upload your music to the cloud yourself, now Google Music will scan the disk space, determine the content present there by tags and provide access to the originals in its service.

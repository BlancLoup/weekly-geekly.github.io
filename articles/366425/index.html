News from EA for fans of Sim-games

Most of you probably remember the game SimCity, beloved by all the years of commercials until 2012. And you probably remember how EA and the developer Maxis almost completely destroyed the opinion of a series of games by its last part SimCity? It turns out that it cost the studio Maxis a little more than tons of negativity in the press and specialized communities - the studio turned its activities.

Frankly, to say that Electronic Arts and Maxis have screwed up with the latest version of SimCity, it would be a far-fetched understatement.



After all, nobody had seen such amazing decisions before: demand the need for a permanent Internet connection for the game, otherwise it will not be able to function, which turned out to be a lie, as it turned out later. And after that, the company began to threaten beta testers with a total ban for disclosing any bugs and errors. And after the launch for paid users, the players had to queue for access to the server for about 30 minutes, and many were lucky with such timing.

The most significant fact of failure, perhaps, was the withdrawal of the game from sale by the giant Amazon, after the release gathered more than 800 negative reviews of the game. Studio Maxis tried to blame only some of its problems for the game's unpopularity, and after that it took a radical step. As an apology to the community, the game was offered for free. But this did not change the course of the Titanic. The problems continued, because if you need to play through the server and constantly be online, players had the opportunity to destroy the cities of other players. It was then that EA decided to finish off the battered players with a fee of $ 9 for adding aircraft and balloons to the game.

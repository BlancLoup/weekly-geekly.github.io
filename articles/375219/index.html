CES2015: Lenovo ThinkPad Line Update

Lenovo at the Consumer Electronic Show has announced an update of the space line of ThinkPad notebooks: this is a new budget notebook E and L series, as well as an updated version of the ThinkPad X1 Carbon.



Models E450 and E550 have a diagonal display of 14 and 15 inches. The choice of the user offers HD and full HD displays with anti-reflective coating, HDD, hybrid drive or SSD, and RAM up to 16 GB. Laptops are based on Intel Core processor. Laptops are equipped with Bluetooth 4.0, Ethernet (which is not in the Yoga Ultrabooks), three USB ports, of which two are 3.0 and HDMI. The older model also has a D-Sub and a RealSense 3D camera. The cost of this series of laptops in the US starts at $ 599.



L450 display has a resolution of HD or full HD, improved viewing angle and color reproduction. All USB ports are version 3.0, there is a mini-DisplayPort port. Here, the user is offered to choose from three battery options, the capacity of the most powerful of which is 72 watt-hours. The minimum cost of the ThinkPad E450 in the US is $ 699.





The new X1 Carbon has a 14-inch touchscreen display with a WQHD resolution of 2560 x 1440 pixels. The choice of users are offered drives, the best of which is a 512 GB SSD with a PCIe interface. Ultrabook weighs less than 1.3 kg, while it works up to 10.9 hours without recharging. X1 Carbon is based on the fifth-generation Intel Core processor. The cost in the USA starts at $ 1249, in Russia it will appear approximately in March of this year.

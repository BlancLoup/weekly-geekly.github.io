Opera and Microsoft at the round table "Web Standards" at RIF + CIB 2011



Surely after reading the title, someone will look at the calendar - not the 1st of April in the yard? No, you were not mistaken, and your eyes did not let you down. We are really together with Microsoft we are organizing a section on web standards in the framework of RIF + CIB. To participate in this section, because of the distant seas, Haakon Wyum Li will arrive - Opera's technical director and, of course, one of the creators of CSS in those distant years "when the trees were big." The second participant is Alex Mogilevsky , a no less famous developer of Microsoft Internet Explorer from Redmond himself.

Both Haakon and Alex are active participants in the W3C and WHATWG working groups, which, in fact, are responsible for standards on the Internet, and therefore will be armed with the latest news from the world of HTML5, CSS3 and other advanced standards. Of course, they will tell about their support in browsers and plans for the future.

But why standards, if they were not supported by the developers? To discuss the latest news and prospects for the introduction of new technologies, we invited the technical directors of the largest portals: Mail.ru and RuTube. Of course, we are waiting for all who care about this topic - there will be more than enough opportunities for discussion!

  • Date: April 20, 2011
  • Time: 15:30 to 17:30
  • Location: RIF + KIB, Forest Dali pension
  • Details: 2011.rif.ru/p/details.php?ID=665
  • Moderators: Vadim Makeev (Opera Software), Mikhail Chernomordikov (Microsoft)

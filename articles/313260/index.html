According to the players of the IT market, the bill on the regulation of online cinemas will slow down their development


After the closure of large pirated video content sites, online cinema revenues in Russia grew significantly. However, some of them are developed by foreign companies, or at least they are investors of services. And in current conditions, foreign financing is not always beneficial. At least, experts say so.

In the summer, the Media Communication Union (ISS) developed a bill to regulate online cinemas. One of the most important measures the ISS considers limiting the share of foreign owners: foreigners will not be able to own more than 20% of such companies. In addition, the bill imposes obligations on search engines for the pre-moderation of content. True, the amendments will not affect the services on which users post content, as well as sites with a daily audience of less than 100 thousand people.
In addition to communication operators, the ISS includes the largest media holdings - Gazprom-Media (GPM), the National Media Group, and CTC Media. Of the members of the ISS, only GMP occupies a significant market share, all the rest are independent projects.

Back in September, the working group on communications and information technologies of the Expert Council under the government criticized the bill. According to experts, the ISS bill does not solve the current problems of the industry, the measures proposed in it are redundant, and some of them are subject to incomprehensible logic.

They consider the restriction of foreign ownership of the corruption rule leading to the monopolization of the market. It restricts companies in choosing a source of funding, wrote Vedomosti, citing experts.

If the bill is adopted in its current form, many market players will have problems. And this will allow pirated sites to re-activate.

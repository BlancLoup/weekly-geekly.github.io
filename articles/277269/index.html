I'm sitting in the cloud, working

Cloud technologies have become the defining vector of IT development. They are constantly evolving, complemented by new services. According to IDC estimates, in the near future the Russian SaaS market will grow eight times faster than the software market as a whole, and the IaaS market will grow nine times faster than the infrastructure IT equipment market.

What is IaaS, SaaS and PaaS? These terms mean something like the following: in the first case, the user has at his disposal a virtual computer with an OS and the required resources, in the second - access to ready-made applications, in the third - development environment, database, application server, business intelligence tools, etc. Cloud services that provide users with ‚Äúvirtual desktops‚Äù (Virtual Desktop Infrastructure, VDI) are one example of IaaS.



VDI technology allows users to deploy virtual desktops that are functionally identical to workstations ‚Äî the classic PC architecture. VDI infrastructure involves the placement of virtual desktops and other user resources in the server infrastructure and providing access to them from the company's internal network and / or via the Internet. Moreover, user access to virtual desktops and company resources is possible from various stationary and mobile devices. These can be PCs, tablets, smartphones, thin clients, etc.

VDI allows you to deploy workstations ‚Äúfrom the cloud‚Äù (private or public) for any employee and use virtually any device. Employees of the company can safely access their personal desktop, corporate information resources, can participate in work processes, wherever they are.

VDI vs PC


Compared to regular desktops, VDI provides several advantages. For example, as noted above, a user can access his desktop from various computers and mobile devices. System administrators can fully control the user environment - its image is stored on the server.


At the expense of it the operational solution of working tasks, more effective use of IT resources is provided. Simplified administration, reduced energy costs, increases the life cycle of equipment of the workplace. The result could be a reduction in IT operation and maintenance costs.


VDI is not a new idea. Back at the beginning of the 2000s, Citrix announced that in a corporate environment it would soon be difficult to meet a physical desktop ‚Äî total virtualization is coming.

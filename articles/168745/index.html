- Polygonal art in web design



Polygonal Art , along with Metro style, began to haunt the minds of designers last year. While the style of Windows 8 immediately appeared in web design, Poly carefully settled in the printing industry, representing both the various illustrations that were used for posters, wallpapers, design for T-shirts, and skins for mobile phones, etc. And only after some time, designers began to use it in website design.

Today, Polygonal art often looks like a stylization for logos, backgrounds and illustrations, which usually serve as decorations, and not as an eye-catching element. But it is here that he shows himself in all its glory. He turns a familiar picture into something extraordinary, with a bunch of sharp corners and flat figures that form bulging areas. The latter is obtained by a clear contrast between adjacent areas, which usually differ from each other only in shades of the same color. The technique of creating such an effect is supposed to consistently fill the entire space with smooth figures, triangles and rectangles, step by step creating areas of different heights, since some elements must be concave, flat and others convex.

It is reasonable to assume that the "polygonal" style came to us from cubism


As for the site design, the Poly works fine on the basis of various textures, especially those that include paper, cardboard and a noisy background. In these cases, it usually complements the design, adding as a result a slight sense of dimension.

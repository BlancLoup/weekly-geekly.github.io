Protonote: documenting sticky paper development


I like simple things. I like to think about the upcoming work on the project, and then do it. During the thinking process, a variety of entries are created. An elegant tool created by Mike Padilla (Mike Padilla) - Protonotes , a service for creating small notes right in the window of the website. Notes look exactly like sticky papers scattered across your desktop.

The task of documenting the development can be accomplished with the help of a wiki, e-mail or project management systems. But for clarity and metaphor - sticky papers on the screen - this is something special.

After adding the Protonotes download code to your page, all visitors to this page will be able to add, delete and edit notes without any additional gestures. Using Protonotes is very convenient for customers. They get the opportunity to leave their comments while being developed on the site. To administer notes, you can connect them to your database (MySQL).

40% of companies may leave the market due to the digital revolution

Many companies can leave the market, and the markets themselves can undergo conversion, according to experts of the Global Center for Digital Business Transformation (DBT Center). The center itself is a joint project of Cisco and the International Institute for Management Development (International Institute of Management Development, IMD), writes Adindex. A few days ago the organization published a report called Digital Whirl, or How the Digital Revolution is Reforming Industry (Digital Vortex: How Digital Disruption is Redefining Industries). The report was the first publication of the DBT Center. When working on the document, representatives of the organization interviewed 941 company managers from 12 industries in 13 countries (Australia, Brazil, Great Britain, Germany, India, Italy, Canada, China, Mexico, Russia, USA, France, Japan).

As it turned out, as a result of the digital revolution, each of the 12 industries in the next five years may lose 40% of the companies currently operating. At the same time, not all companies consider the current situation on the market a worthy discussion at the board of directors level.

Most respondents point out the positive impact of digital technologies on both the market and society. 75% of executives surveyed believe that the digital revolution is one form of progress. Another 72% indicated that digitalization is beneficial for the market, and 66% point out the empowerment of the individual in the digital age. However, 43% of respondents either do not see the risks that the digital revolution entails, or do not consider such risks as dangerous. Only a quarter of respondents indicated that they are pre-emptive to the digital revolution.

The center has the highest potential for digital transformations in the next five years, according to specialists of the high-tech products and services industry. The top positions of such a rating are occupied by some other industries that are related to data processing. These are media, entertainment, telecommunications, financial markets and some other areas.

The main influence on the digital revolution is made by startups, which are rapidly changing under the influence of new technologies of the company, as well as the processes of integration of industries. In the next 5 years, according to respondents, significant changes will come under the influence of the digital revolution, including changes in market shares.

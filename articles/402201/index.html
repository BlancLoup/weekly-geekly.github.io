An online community of almost 20 years has created levels for the classic version of Tomb Raider.



About Tomb Raider , probably, all readers of Geektimes heard. A series of computer and video games telling about the adventures of an intelligent, beautiful and talented girl who devoted her life to studying artifacts of past centuries, attracted many gamers at one time. The first part came out in 1996. Since then, a few more parts have appeared, both for PCs and gaming consoles. Based on the game they made films, published novels and comics. Over the years since the release of the first part, the graphics in the game has undergone significant changes. The appearance of the levels and the protagonist itself changed, and the development teams that worked on the game also changed.

But one thing remained constant - the community of fans of the original franchise, who created more and more new levels. We are talking about the Internet community, whose members have created over a couple of decades about 3000 different levels to the classic version of Tomb Raider. They occupy about 200 GB of disk space. It takes a couple of years to go through all the created worlds and their sections. The community consists of about 2,000 people. The forum contains about 50 thousand reviews of completed custom levels. Why do all these people spend so much time creating materials for the game, which was released 21 years ago?

After all, not many people play the original Tomb Raider, most fans of the series are passionate about newer versions. The ability to create your own levels for the game appeared in 1999, when Core Design introduced the official editor for The Last Revelation, the fourth game of the series.

The level editor appeared just when the game itself was on the rise of popularity, and long before the advent of Angel of Darkness, the next part, which is considered unsuccessful or even failed . After the editor for Tomb Raider appeared, enthusiasts released several additional tools to customize the game. The editor himself was very easy to use, so even people far from programming could create their materials for the world of Lara Croft.

The community in question appeared shortly after the editor. Fans of the game series began not only to create their own materials for the game, but also actively began to share them with each other, discussing the process of creation, problem areas and other moments.

This is not so much, but you should understand that the work was done by enthusiasts, and not by the developers of game studios behind Tomb Raider.



For all these people (in the community there are users from many different countries), the editor provided the opportunity to change the game world at will. People were able to increase or decrease the duration of the original game or to do something of their own, with new and old artifacts. Representatives of the community, by their own admission, love classic games. Undoubtedly, one of the most prominent representatives of the classics is Tomb Raider.

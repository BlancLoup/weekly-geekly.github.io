Google added five US National Parks to Street View



Google Corporation is constantly working to improve all sorts of services of its own production, so to speak. Probably more than others attracted the attention of news regarding the update of the Street View service.

Google adds to the service a huge number of photos from Amazonian forests, then an even greater number of various kinds of architectural memos. Now the service management decided to add five US national parks, making them available for virtual travel.

All the parks in question are located in the state of California, and all of them are worth it so that nature and travel lovers make a couple of virtual tours. Parks can be surveyed by those users who do not have the opportunity to go there "live", and those who want to find out if this trip will not be a waste of money and time.

The number of parks, "worked out" by the corporation, included Redwoods National Park, Yosemite National Park, Sequoia & Kings Canyon National Park, Death Valley National Park, Joshua Tree National Park. As you can see, the Street View service is becoming more interesting. Probably, in the near future, among other objects, other regions will appear in Street View, even the most unexpected ones.

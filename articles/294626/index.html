March 2015
India is setting up a social media monitoring lab.

Mumbai police have set up a framework for monitoring social networks to identify public sentiment in order to identify situations that could lead to mass demonstrations or protests.

A specially trained team of 20 police officers, employees of the Social Media Lab, daily and nightly monitor publicly discussed issues, tracking changes in popular sentiment that can lead to large-scale meetings or protests.

The Social Media Lab, opened by bollywood actor Abhishek Bachchan, will assess people's attitudes on social media. They will also monitor particularly active Internet citizens on Twitter, Facebook, Google+ and other social networks. In other words, the police will keep online activists under tight surveillance.

In November 2014, the actions of the Mumbai police provoked outrage and fierce disputes over Indian Internet laws after the arrest of two young girls for posts on Facebook, criticizing the actions of the Mumbai police following the death of a local politician. This was followed by several arrests throughout India for political cartoons or comments written on the Internet.

Naturally, the question arises of freedom of expression and the rights of Indian citizens. However, the average social media user will not have to change his behavior and habits, since this observation is in no way connected with censorship. The task of the Social Media Lab is to prevent demonstrations and protests that can undermine the livelihoods of not only a large city, but the entire state.

Moreover, most of the police stations in India, such as in the city of Delhi, already have their own cyber-cells, actively supporting law and order. Delhi Police is active on Facebook and Twitter, not only watching users (for crimes), but also maintains a real-time traffic map of the city.

Millions of teenagers from all over India are already actively using social media. Therefore, a certain form of moderation and monitoring is necessary, especially for people at an early age, in order to prevent cases of cyber-harassment and possible crimes in time.

Of course, the Freedom of Expression vs Online Monitoring debate will always be there, but social media users should realize that monitoring public messages helps protect the safety of people, both on and off the Internet. The media usually talks only about how the online police are arresting someone, although they are also actively working to ensure the safety of people and their loved ones across India.

According to experts in the field of social media, the volume of data of public messages (tweets, posts, comments) is so great that it is almost impossible to ensure full-scale monitoring. Therefore, the police will first allocate users who have already been convicted, or have antisocial or anti-national views and monitor their activities on the Internet.

September 2015
India launches continuous monitoring of social media to combat ISIS

The Government of India announced the launch of the ongoing Situation Center for the Analysis of Social Media in order to combat the growing influence on the minds of citizens of the Islamic State (ISIL). The project will be carried out jointly by several power structures and includes analysis of information from social media to identify problem areas and prevent crime.

In August, the National Anti-Terrorism Center was established in India, and within the framework of the Situation Center, 6 Indian ministries, including the Ministry of Internal Affairs and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, will closely interact.

ISIS, a jihadist-minded terrorist organization, is known for attracting young people from all over the world to its ranks, including through social networks. On the side of ISIS, between 20,000 and 30,000 foreigners are fighting against government forces in Syria and Iraq.

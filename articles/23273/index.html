University of Tennessee will receive a supercomputer with a capacity of 1 petaflops

The University of Tennessee (University of Tennessee, UT) received a grant of $ 65 million from the National Science Foundation (NSF) for the construction and subsequent maintenance of the Cray XT4 supercomputer for 5 years.


A feature of this supercomputer assembled using the AMD Opteron Quad-Core is its high power, which is estimated at about 1 petaflop (PetaFLOPS, 1,000,000,000,000,000 floating-point operations per second).

The cost of the XT4 supercomputer hardware, as noted, is about $ 30 million. The remaining $ 35 million will be spent on servicing the entire system for 5 years of its use.

XT4 supercomputer will be used for a variety of tasks:

* astrophysicists to study the process of evolution of galaxies and the study of black holes

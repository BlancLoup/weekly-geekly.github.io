New industry requirements: Certification Centers stop issuing 3-year SSL certificates from March 1, 2018

From March 1, 2018, the Certification Authority / Browser Forum (CA / B Forum) introduces new requirements for all Certificate Authorities : the maximum validity of all types of SSL certificates should not exceed 825 days (27 months).

The decision was taken at a vote of 193 . Unanimously - 24 Certification Centers were in favor: GlobalSign, DigiCert, Entrust Datacard, Certum, Comodo, SymantecTurkTrust, Izenpe, Certinomis, Amazon, CNNIC, HARICA, GDCA, Disig, Trustwave, Let's Encrypt, Quo Vadis, SHECA, CFCA, OI, OI, Trustwave, Let's Encrypt, Quo Vadis, SHECA, CFCA, OI, OI, Trustwave, Let's Encrypt, Quo Vadis, SHECA, CFCA, OI, OI, Trustwave, Let's Encrypt, Quo Vadis, SHECA, CFCA, OI, OI, Trustwave, Let's Encrypt, Quo Vadis, SHECA, CFCA, OI, OI, Trustwave, Let's Encrypt, Quo Vadis, SHECA, CFCA, OI, OI, Trustwave, Let's Encrypt, Quo Vadis, SHECA, CFA, OA, Oigi , Logius PKIoverheid, Cisco, SwissSign), as well as 5 browsers for: Apple, Qihoo 360, Microsoft, Opera, Google and 1 "abstained" Mozilla

These measures will be aimed at improving the level of security when checking organizations and domains. All Certification Centers must follow a new industry standard.

Following the new requirements, from February 26, 2018, GlobalSign will stop issuing 3-year trusted SSL certificates. From this date, SSL certificates can be issued with a maximum validity of 2 years .

Until February 26, we offer to purchase an SSL certificate for 3 years, which will allow you to:

  • to avoid unnecessary trouble with registration or renewal, increasing the interval between orders of certificates;
  • save money by acquiring SSL for several years at once.

We strive to ensure that your website protects your customers' data, ensuring proper confidence, and your costs and efforts to ensure security are minimal.

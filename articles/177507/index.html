Ukrainian development market: who, how much and how much

According to the materials of our report on RIT2013.

In numbers:

  • $ 4500 - "top" salary developer in Kiev. Above one
  • Intelligent developers with 3+ years of experience can receive $ 2500-4000
  • Salaries dictated by demand in the global labor market (outsourcing)
  • $ 1.5 billion earned by outsourcing companies in 2012
  • 10 thousand programmers work in the five largest companies (outsourcing)
  • 40% of developers work in Kiev
  • Lviv and Odessa catch up with Kiev on wages, Kharkiv and Dnipropetrovsk are 20-30% behind




How many of us



According to various estimates, there are 40-60 thousand developers in our country. These are programmers, testers, PMs, etc., oo without system administrators and abstract IT specialists. There are no exact numbers.

70 percent of these 40-60 thousand one way or another working for outsourcing. Someone sitting in the office of EPAM or Luxoft, someone in a smaller company. A huge number of people are sitting "by apartment" working through oDesk and similar exchanges.

Developers are everywhere, but probably 80% live and work in one of five cities: Kiev, Lvov, Kharkov, Dnepropetrovsk, Odessa.

Outsourcing taxis



Twice a year we update our TOP25 rating of the largest software companies. From the TOP25 non-outsourcing companies of the unit: Terrasoft, Software MacKiev, AM SOFT, Softline, Gameloft. In the TOP5 companies employed 10 thousand developers. More than Facebook!

Salaries



The labor market for developers is very globalized, not tied to the Ukrainian realities and GDP (see above about outsourcing). As a result, the cries of programmers zazhralis, and doctors live on a beggarly salary do not receive a special response.

$ 4500 - "top" salary developer in Kiev. Above one. Expeditious developers with 3+ years of experience - $ 2500-4000. On jobs.dou.ua/salaries/ a detailed analyst is collected, for all possible cuts.

While it is unlikely that the shortage of personnel will disappear, which means that wages will continue to grow by 5-10% per year.

In the capital, as expected, the salaries are the highest, but Lviv and Odessa are not far behind. In Kharkov and Dnepropetrovsk wages are 20-30% lower. In smaller cities, remote work through oDesk and other exchanges is more popular than office space. Yes, and at times more profitable.

HyperX introduces the updated Cloud II gaming headset

Time does not stand still, and after a successful debut in the segment of gaming headsets HyperX continues to increase its presence in this region. Novelty called Cloud II, which indicates a small change regarding the first version of the device. In fact, the key difference was the updated control panel with a built-in USB sound card that supports virtual 7.1 surround sound. In all other respects, this is all the same that has already earned the honor and respect of the HyperX Cloud headset.



Fully developed by HyperX experts, an audio control panel with background LED backlighting creates a 7.1 virtual sound with hardware support (no driver installation required) with independent microphone level control and sound reproduction volume.



Fans of games can enjoy 7.1 sound at the touch of a button to simulate seven separate speakers for maximum comfort while playing. The HyperX Cloud II headset supports USB and PC power for Mac to enhance sound and voice. In addition, it is compatible with the PS4 and Xbox One consoles via a 3.5 mm audio jack.



The microphone supports noise reduction, automatic sound level control, and echo cancellation with a USB sound card. The headset received a TeamSpeak certificate and is optimized for use with Skype. It provides a clear voice transmission, high-quality sound reproduction in computer games and reduced background noise. Cups and headbands are made from a special shape memory foam for maximum ease of use over many hours.



HyperX will also introduce the second version of the HyperX FURY Pro Gaming Mouse Pad. Thanks to the soft-lined fabric material, the HyperX FURY mat provides optimum mouse movement accuracy and ease of use. The HyperX FURY mat is available in four different sizes.

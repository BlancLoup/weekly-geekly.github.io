CROC IT office: a place where system integrators live

Hello! My name is Alina, and I work in an office where there is a lot of unusual iron, a protected data center right on the spot, another data center at the underground level under the parking lot and various interesting engineering systems.



We have been creating and implementing IT systems for large corporate customers for 20 years, and we often test new technologies on ourselves before implementation. Therefore, in the office there are systems that after some time will be implemented in banking, industrial and other areas throughout the country.

For guests, we have a lot of spectacular pieces, but especially for Habr, I agreed with our engineers about showing those things that even not all employees of the company can see.

Careful traffic! Inside a lot of pictures.

The office building looks like this. We are a 15-minute walk from Ploshchad Ilyicha metro station, and shuttles regularly go to the building, plus there is a huge parking lot for guests and staff. At the entrance to the territory, which is visible here, there is a recognition system of state numbers, which scans the numbers of cars (the system can distinguish a real number from a paper one, for example), compares them with the base and writes everything to the log. If the employee's number is registered in the database, he raises the barrier without RFID tags, remotes or the need to show documents to the guard. However, in the case of a very, very dirty room (as is the case in winter), an employee can still travel along the RFID tag.



On the ground floor there is a guard post (security staff asked them not to take them off), then turnstiles and a wardrobe. Turnstiles can be opened either by a standard NFC-card of an employee or a guest, or simply by scanning the face (far stand) for those who have registered on the base.



Here my colleague Vadim passes through the turnstile with gifts from EMC. The camera captures the face, compares with the template in the database and starts it on. He could be wearing glasses or a hat - the scanner would still recognize him and miss it.

Logitech diNovo Keyboard for Notebooks

Designed for convenience.
Designed for beauty.


After my BTC 6300CL after the tea spilled on it went into another world, I seriously started to choose a new keyboard.
The main requirements were:
  1. Standard key layout
  2. Wireless
  3. No extra multimedia buttons (or their minimum)
  4. Good key stroke
  5. Convenience, size, beauty and the like

After reviewing the catalogs of most computer and computer computer shops in the city, my choice fell on the Logitech diNovo Keyboard for Notebooks

Equipment


Logitech style box. Glossy, brilliant, beautiful.

The quality is, frankly, on top. I did not even expect.

Appearance


The keyboard itself has a glossy surface, it gets dirty very quickly, but this, in principle, is not particularly annoying.



Aluminum palm rest, ostensibly so that hands do not sweat. It does not affect the sweating of hands, but it looks stylish. In the middle - a slightly bulging Logitech logo



The buttons are matte, very pleasant to the touch. The letters are white, additional functions on the F-keys are gray-blue.



On the reverse side - soft-touch plastic. Rubber feet, logo, battery compartment. Nothing extra.





The keyboard works on 4 AAA batteries. According to the developers, the battery life is up to three years . Pleased that the Duracell were bundled.



Side view. No comments.



On the right side is a switch. Pretty comfortable, unremarkable.



Top right - battery charge indicator. It lights up for 10 seconds after turning on, then goes out. When the batteries are dead, it should be constantly burning (it was not possible to check). Nearby are the volume control keys and a calculator (can be reassigned to SetPoint).



Another indicator is on the Caps Lock button. On - on, off - off. Mine is quite convenient.



The receiver is standard. Not big, not small.



Job


Windows 7 recognized the keyboard immediately and without problems, but I still installed the drivers by downloading the latest version from the site.

The keyboard is positioned as a model for laptops. Differences from conventional keyboards, apparently, in the Fn key, which replaced the standard Win buttons and the context menu (I do not know what it is called) between the right Ctrl and Alt.



On the keys F1-F12, Print Screen, Break hung additional features to press with the Fn key. These capabilities can be reassigned in SetPoint.
Default:
  1. Fn + F1 - Browser Home Page
  2. Fn + F2 - Email Client
  3. Fn + F3 - Default Search Engine
  4. Fn + F4 - IM client
  5. Fn + F5 - Analogue Alt + Tab
  6. Fn + F6 - Zoom-
  7. Fn + F7 - Zoom +
  8. Fn + F8 - Gadgets (I still do not understand what it is)
  9. Fn + F9 - Media Player
  10. Fn + F10 - Previous track
  11. Fn + F11 - Play / Pause
  12. Fn + F12 - Next Track
  13. Fn + Print Screen - Context menu (the one that was between Ctrl and Alt)
  14. Fn + Break - Scroll Lock



The bottom row of keys (Ctrl, Alt, Space, Win, Fn) is made slightly protruding above the rest. In fact, it is extremely convenient.



For some reason, the left Shift was reduced, and a duplicate slash was made next to it. You do not immediately get used to the narrow shifto, but in principle, you get used to it and do not notice it.

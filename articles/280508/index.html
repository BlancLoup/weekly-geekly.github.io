DevOps section at the DUMP-2016 conference

Continues a series of posts about the DUMP conference review section DevOps. Section program director, DevOps head of SKB Kontur, Alexey Kirpichnikov, said that there will be something interesting on April 8 in Yekaterinburg.

In 2016, all the hot topics and new growth points will appear at the junction of development and operations. But no one really understands what the word DevOps means. If you ask me, I will say that DevOps is about how to stop worrying about operating code in production and not wasting time on it.

On April 8, from morning to evening, we will talk about how to get rid of the pain of using the code you wrote, a little about new tools, but more about how to organize everything correctly.

Interesting? Under the cut briefly about all the reports.

So, what you learn at the conference.
How to stop spending a lot of money on hosting
Ivan Kokorin from Vostok Projects will teach how, using intelligent cloud scaling tools built into the clouds, you can quickly grow tens of times to handle peak load and also quickly roll up without it to save money.


How to stop wasting time delivering code in production
Three experts from heavyweight companies will tell you about the ways to solve this problem.

Maxim Glekov will explain how they lay out the front-end for projects in the Mail.ru Group (Moscow) in production, talk about the monitoring system's monitoring architecture, and also show what the dashboards look like, according to which they understand that everything is fine.

Lead 2GIS developer Denis Yakovlev (Novosibirsk) will tell you how and why 2GIS automated the infrastructure: about the infrastructure problems faced by their teams, how the development and testing processes suffered, how to implement OpenStack and how to automate influenced the development of the entire business.

Danila Shtan , ex-head of the development department of E96.ru , will tell you how in the process of moving to a new site, they reorganized the environment in which their services live, learned how to live without system administrators and formulated such requirements for the preparation of services that would not limit developers and not complicate the layout and maintenance of services.

We can not do without stormy discussion of the main issue of the industry: do sysadmins need a team and why.

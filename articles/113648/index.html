Atheros Communications and Marvell Technologies released 802.11n-enabled mobile network chips

Yes, high-speed wireless Internet connection is desired not only by owners of PCs, laptops, but also by owners of mobile gadgets. It seems that this desire is gradually becoming a reality. Recently, Atheros Communications and Marvell Technologies announced the release of 802.11n-based mobile network chips.

Manufacturers claim that their mobile chips are the first mobile wireless communication modules with support for MIMO (Multiple Input, Multiple Output) technology. This technology allows not only to transfer data at high speed, but also provides high reliability of data transmission, reducing the influence of interference and data loss.

The management of manufacturing companies reports that the new chips may be useful to most manufacturers of mobile devices that need to be provided with a fairly fast connection to the Network. One has to think that soon these chips will be embedded in most mobile devices with network connection support - indeed, users require an ever-faster connection of their communicator / tablet to the Internet.

The companies will present their products at the Mobile World Congress 2011 exhibition, which will be held in a few days.

It is worth noting that the chip from Atheros provides data reception speed of 170 Mbit / s, while the chip from Marvell Technologies provides data reception at a speed of 300 Mbit / s. In addition, the latest chip maker hybrid, it also includes support for Bluetooth. The chips from Atheros and Marvell are called AR6004 and Avaster 88W8797, respectively.

The prototype of the Russian photo and video services "Flamber" , 35Photo and Gallery , made in the style of Web 2.0, quite possibly, are the Western leaders in these genres Flickr and YouTube .

Actually, of the three services listed, only one supports the storage and display of videos: this is Gallery . Like the other two sites, this resource has recently started working (in summer), so there is not much content there yet. There are only 1478 videos in the database, while on YouTube the score goes to millions . However, the rest of the Russian development is very similar to YouTube: a similar interface, voting and discussing videos, rating in the stars. True, the site works much slower than the American "competitor". But the speed of work is a very important indicator, because of which even the best web 2.0 project can fail.

The other two sites, Flamber and 35Photo , were created exclusively for working with photographs, so here the developers had an easier task. Both sites support tags, as well as a variety of features in the spirit of Web 2.0. However, from a conceptual point of view, the projects are implemented in completely different ways.

Flamber is more academic. Here are several ways to upload photos and different types of access to photo galleries. Certain ‚Äúgroups‚Äù , which are still in development, are intended to communicate with users. From the "social" functions - perhaps the ability to add friends from among other users.

Website 35Photo, on the contrary, is maximally focused on communication: there are advanced communities and a traditional forum type. There is a rating of the most discussed photos , as well as a rating of the most active users . As it should be, export of RSS feeds by author, by keyword, etc., is established.

It's nice that Web 2.0 is becoming fashionable in Russia. Surely, these are only the first signs, followed by tens and hundreds of new generation services.

Generally speaking, the Russian multimedia services Web 2.0 have every chance to repeat the success of their American colleagues. It is known that one of the necessary conditions for the popularity of such services is broadband Internet access, and by the number of broadband connections, Russia occupies one of the first places in Europe.

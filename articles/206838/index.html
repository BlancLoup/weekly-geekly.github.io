Results of the 2nd contest of designers and designers RDC 2013


The 2nd contest of designers and designers has ended. I am pleased to present you his results, interviews with the jury members and plans for the next year.

In the first round, 164 participants competed, which is almost 2 times the number of competitors last year. According to the results of three rounds, Anton Tyulenev took the first place, Alexey Antonov took the second place, Ivan Korzun won the third place. Winners will receive the latest Apple computer models.

It is worth noting that this year the residents of Moscow were the top three winners, whereas in the competition last year there were not any Muscovites in the top three finalists.

We sent t-shirts to all participants of the first round, confirming that they are designers.

Thanks to feedback collected in Habr's comments last year, errors and shortcomings of the first contest were taken into account. This time we had more time and resources to rebuild the competition platform. So, we made open the pages of work with comments, profiles of participants, statistics, screwed Reformal to collect ideas and opinions, decided on the formats for submitting decisions, and expanded the jury.


The idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe competition is simple: we collect top specialists from various fields of design and design (heads of design departments of large companies, design teachers, industry professionals), invent 9 tasks for three rounds. Tasks should be close to the tasks that participants and the jury decide in the real world, take into account industry trends, cover different areas of design, vary in complexity and time to decide, allow to reveal the potential of the participant in the design, in the schedule and in the presentation of the solution .
As a result of working on such tasks, strong specialists can hone their skills, and beginners can try themselves in completely new unusual areas and create a virtual portfolio that has been evaluated by a strict jury.
By the way, about the jury. I think this is a great idea - together with the participants to compile a list of people we would like to see as judges, and on behalf of the entire community send them invitations. Talk to the Jury


This year the competition was judged by Lena Dronova (art director of the Innova company, teacher of the British Higher School of Design), Ivan Semenov (lead designer in the new for Yandex direction of media services. Former art director and co-author of the last restart of the Rambler), Yuri Vetrov (head of the design team and interface design for Mail.Ru), Dmitry Zimin (product manager at Kinokhod, previously head of the design department for Afisha Rambler, organizer of World Usability Day Russia.) and I, Alisher Yakupov (head of design at OK, previously design p Yandex and Habrakhabra). People are all, as you understand, very busy, but still managed to persuade some in a small interview.

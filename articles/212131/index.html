Open Data Day February 22 in Moscow



On February 22, Open Data Day will be held around the world dedicated to open data and organized by the Open Knowledge Foundation with the support of many intergovernmental and international organizations such as the United Nations and the World Bank.

More information about how it will take place can be found at http://opendataday.org/

Open Data Day is a completely non-commercial unfunded by anyone moving, such a big meeting for the good of the business.

You can help us as follows:
  • throwing in ideas for developers
  • inviting the developers to do something nice on the open data;
  • suggest some interesting data on which you can do something interesting;
  • offer a speech on what you are doing on the basis of open data;
  • offer some cool non-cash prizes;


The format will be simple:

  • At first, some introductory information about what and how;
  • then we run in parallel several speeches about what is happening with open data in Russia and in parallel with code;
  • finally we present the resulting projects;


All this will take place in the Boiling Point, site of the Agency for Strategic Initiatives at the address: Moscow, 2nd Tverskaya-Yamskaya Street, 14, Moscow
To participate you must register at http://www.meetup.com/okfnru/events/156390472/ .
The hall accommodates up to 60 people and we need to know how many people will be recorded.

And, of course, maybe someone will want to hold Open Data Day in their city - I suggest not to postpone this glorious business and register your event on opendataday.org

News about C ++ products and JetBrains C ++ Night in St. Petersburg

Hi, Habr!

Holidays died down, winter is in full swing, someone has already thrown out a Christmas tree, and someone is waiting for warming. And we work hard on new versions of our C ++ products. For example, since December, the Early Access Program of CLion, our cross-platform IDE for C / C ++, has been opened.



There are a lot of new and interesting things:

  • Plugins to support Swift and Python .
    A couple of small demos about the work of these plugins:

    Swift:


    Python:

  • Plugin to synchronize project source files with remote host.
  • Ability to manually mark the project directories by specifying CLion, where the library files are located, where the project source files are, and which directories should be completely excluded from indexing.
  • The ability to connect debugger from IDE to a third-party process running on the local machine for further debugging in the IDE.
  • And a lot of everything else.

In general, we invite you to try and, of course, write us your feedback and suggestions.

And on February 26-27, C ++ developers from all over Russia will gather in St. Petersburg for participation in the C ++ Russia conference. Of course, we are planning to participate in the conference and will be glad to see you at our stand.

On the eve we will be glad to see you in our office, where you can:
  • chat with us in a more relaxed atmosphere,
  • hear about the internal architecture of our IDE,
  • learn a lot of useful tips on the use of our products,
  • complain about problems
  • ask for such an important and missing feature (by telling us about your vision of its use, so that we can understand your needs more quickly and better),
  • as well as fill up with any (even the most tricky!) questions from our developers.


JetBrains C ++ night: when and where


The cozy JetBrains office in St. Petersburg will open its doors on February 24 at 6 pm The teams CLion and ReSharper C ++ are looking forward to your visit.

Program


We have planned three reports, each of which, we hope, will turn into a conversation with the speaker and all of us in general:

  1. ReSharper C ++ Optimization
    In short, this is a report on the optimizations used in developing ReSharper C ++: caching, lazy evaluation and incremental updates.
  2. Masterly use unit testing in CLion
    A hands-on story about how to use Google Test effectively and how CLion helps you achieve maximum efficiency.
  3. High Performance Computing Technologies
    The purpose of the report is to distract from the IDE and talk about how to squeeze maximum performance out of hardware. In plans SIMD, SSE / AVX and computing on clusters using MPI.


Welcome!

Creating Security Drones


Nightingale Security designed and built a security drone, some of the details of which are printed on Raise3D 3D printers.


The Blackbird quadcopter reaches a height of up to 60 meters in seconds, can automatically patrol territories using a thermal imager and video, and respond quickly to alarms and abnormal situations.


This is an intelligent autonomous network drone, certified by the Federal Aviation Administration of the United States for flying at night, including in groups of several drones.

Nightingale Security uses 3D printers to produce those UAV parts that are difficult and unprofitable to produce in traditional ways.


Due to the wide print temperature range on Raise3D, up to 300 ‚ÑÉ, you can select the right material for each part:

  • durable plastic for the frame and engine mount;
  • lightweight material for the camera casing;
  • flexible plastic for chassis cushioning.

Thanks to 3D printers, Nightingale Security has simplified, cheapened and accelerated the production of parts for drones.

Festival City of Techniculture 2016, or how to attract children to technology

Almost a month ago, the festival ended The City of Technological Creativity, which for the third year in a row is held in Yekaterinburg. We, as always, could not stay away from such a scale, and took part in organizing the festival with the efforts of the entire MakeItLab hackspace .



A year ago, we already wrote about technology-making in our blog on Habr√©. Then, at the main event of the festival - tehnonoch, gathered about a thousand visitors. For all the organizers of the festival, this became a sign that we all do a good job, which brings together many curious children and adults under one idea.

The festival is an attempt to bring together all the technical activities for children in our area. Trying to make a truly mass event to show as many children as possible that being an engineer in our time is very cool. This year tehnonoch gathered about two thousand people!

Under the cut report on this great event, with a bunch of illustrative photos and comments.



In 28 days, 56 various events were held, from photo contests and quest games, to artificial intelligence seminars and quadcopter assembly workshops. All activities took place in different locations of the city, the benefit of the festival every year more and more friends. One of these locations was our hackspace, in which we held a masterclass on programming address garlands on RPi and python. The main event of the festival - technonoch, took place in the new cultural space of our city - in the Yeltsin center.

The main ideologist of the festival is Irina Zakirova and her non-profit organization New Education Club. Reseda Rybalko - curator of the event. These are the people who were able to put together without a million a lot of events purely on a voluntary basis. This is generally nonsense in our time, but as practice shows, "sense" is still there. It will be megacut, if someone is inspired by what is happening in our city and dares to repeat it at home!

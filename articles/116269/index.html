Now, a year later, I can say that time management is very simple. After all the principles have moved to the subconscious level.

In short, the meaning of time management in improving its effectiveness. This is like the most important task of the actor according to Stanislavsky - that is, behind the whole game on the stage there is a certain meaning, expressed in one sentence. Almost like morality in a fable.

So, I think you need to be able to do the following:
  • Not to do unnecessary things (Pareto principle), to look at each task at an angle, how not to do it, how to postpone its decision, or how to transfer it to another;
  • Highlight the right things. In practice, it is often impossible to set priorities 1,2,3 - therefore you need to make a list of necessary things, then filter out from the point of view of abandoning each task;
  • Do one or two to the end of the necessary (read, important) things a day, and do it effectively. So, learn to evaluate its effectiveness and constantly improve it, bringing each task to its logical end.


That's all. There is nothing more to know about time management, you need to take all the tools and try them yourself (all these Eisenhower matrices, notebook). And in practice, due to very small iterations and tests, unnecessary ones are cut off and only things that work for you remain.

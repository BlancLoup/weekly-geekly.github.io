Enot J141-M Tablet Review

On Habr√© was a review of the previous tablet model - habrahabr.ru/company/muk/blog/137754 . We recognize that in the previous version there were bugs, now they are fixed.



Tablet Enot J141 - wagon, equipped with modules 3G, Wi-Fi and GPS.

Under the cat review of the updated model.

The design of the novelty exactly repeats the look of the previous version (therefore, the photos are taken from the old review). Recall the main points. The body is entirely made of black plastic. All controls are touch. In the active state, the buttons glow white, which makes them well distinguishable even on a sunny day. On the front and front sides are cameras with resolutions of 0.3 and 2 megapixels. The novelty weighs quite a bit - less than 600 grams, so it will not be very hard for the bag.



The Enot J141-M display is made in a convenient, but in an extremely rare size - an 8-inch diagonal, the aspect ratio is 4: 3. With a resolution of 1024 x 768 pixels, the amount of information displayed is very large. The whole journal page is placed on the screen, albeit on a somewhat smaller scale, but with all the proportions preserved. Management is simplified due to the fact that the screen - touch, made by capacitive technology.



The image rotates automatically when the tablet position changes, thanks to the built-in G-sensor.





The hardware platform is based on AllWinner A10 processor. Its architecture is modern Cortex A9, and it works at a clock frequency of 1.2 GHz, so there will be no lack of performance. The amount of RAM is 512 MB, and a constant - 4 GB. The latter can be increased by adding a microSD card to a special slot. At the ends of the device you can see the mini-USB port, mini-HDMI output, microSD slot, SIM card receiver, headphone and charger connectors.

Wireless connectivity is provided by Wi-Fi 802.11 b / g / n and 3G adapters. In order for you to easily set your position or to find the shortest path in an unfamiliar city, the tablet was equipped with a GPS navigator. Such a solution is rare, but one cannot but agree with its usefulness. After upgrading, the sensitivity of the radio modules has become even higher and now the functionality of the new product has improved significantly. A Bluetooth module is used to pair with a mobile phone or wireless headphones.



Unlike its predecessor, Enot J141-M is running the operating system Google Android 4.0, so you can safely install the program to perform any task. The Enot J141-M battery is powerful (4800 mAh) and allows you to work autonomously for about 6 hours. That is, you can calmly watch 3-4 films on the road, or play a few chess games with a friend.

Conclusions: Enot J141-M will appeal to anyone who wants to always have on hand a universal device. A noticeable increase in performance of the processor and radio modules after the upgrade allows us to do much more.

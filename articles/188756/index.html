Happy Farm selected 8 resident startups of the second cycle for an internship in Silicon Valley

Happy Farm Business Incubator summed up the two-month Ukrainian stage of its acceleration program for start-ups of the second cycle. On June 19, the International Board of the Incubator selected startups who are ready to take part in the second stage of the program - go on an internship to Silicon Valley. The following resident teams of the second cycle will go to the United States with Happy Farm:

Lonely walls

Kartoonart

Jamrooms

Photozeen

Coursmos

Commercerun

Winkcam

Newzmate



The trip of the teams to the Valley will be held in September of this year. It will include Happy Farm's own demo events, meetings with mentors, a private dinner with investors, as well as a huge number of meetings and networking events.

12 start-up teams from Ukraine, Russia, Belarus and Moldova took part in the Ukrainian stage of the Happy Farm acceleration program, which was held from May 20 to July 20. The program included about 500 hours of individual training sessions with international and national experts, 200 hours of English and pitch sessions, 80 hours of training workshops, more than 80 hours of professional advice on legal, financial and PR issues. Each of the startups was assigned one or more members of the Happy Farm Board for individual counseling and mentoring support. More than 30 international mentors were involved in training start-up teams, among them Gil Penchina (co-founder / investor PayPal, Linkedin, Wikia), Mika Baldwin (CEO Graphic.ly, mentor 500Startups), John Gover (CEO Dialect Inc.).

On July 20, Demo Day of startups of the second cycle took place in Happy Farm, which gathered more than 200 investors, entrepreneurs and other guests from Ukraine and the CIS.

At the moment, the incubator accepts applications from startups to participate in the third cycle . You can submit an application until August 28 at happyfarm.com.ua

More information: www.happyfarm.com.ua

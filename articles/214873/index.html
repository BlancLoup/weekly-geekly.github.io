Google Summer of Code 2014

March 10, we begin accepting applications from students for the Google Summer of Code 2014 (GSoC) program.

For the past ten years in a row, we have been inviting students around the world not to spend the summer on the warm sea, but to devote 3 months of holidays to working on an interesting open source project under the strict guidance of one of the GSoC participating organizations. This year, we have selected 190 open-source organizations , to work on projects which students and graduate students over 18 will be able to lend their hand.

Last year, more than 4 thousand students from 94 countries of the world sent their applications for participation in Google Summer of Code . About a thousand students ( 37 of whom hail from Russia ) were selected to participate and were able to learn from experienced developers for three months and independently write, test and debug open source code.

How to apply


1) Check out the list of projects proposed by the participating organizations .
2) Select the one on which you would be interested to work.
3) Discuss your idea with the curator of the project on the side of the host organization and with other participants. Usually the discussion takes place on mailing lists or IRC related projects. All contacts can be found on the Google Summer of Code website in the ‚Äú Accepted Organizations ‚Äù section.
4) Send an application for participation, describing in it, what task you are going to solve, what is the approximate plan of the decision, how it can be useful for the project, why you will cope with the task better than others.

The deadline for submitting applications is March 21 at 11 pm Moscow time (19:00 UTC).

April 21 will be announced the names of participants accepted into the program. And from May 19 to August 22, it will be possible to do enough and in the case of high results get a scholarship of $ 5,500.

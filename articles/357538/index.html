BMW complied with the GPL license requirement

In early March, security experts conducted a reverse-engineering of the BMW i3 firmware - and found that it runs on the Linux kernel .

Since the system is under Linux, the developer must comply with the terms of the GPL license, that is, provide the source code along with the binary files upon user request. However, in the Australian division of BMW refused to do it: â€œTo access the software download site, you had to provide a seven-digit VIN and accept the terms of the user agreement. Part of the terms of the user agreement states that the software is protected by copyright and BMW is its sole owner. So in this case, it is not subject to the requirements of a â€œpublicâ€ license, â€they wrote.

Fortunately, common sense still triumphed. Having rummaged around the interface of the car computer, another BMW i3 owner, a programmer from the UK Terence Eden, found another e-mail address, apparently, from the central office of BMW. There you had to write a letter to get the source code of the software.



Terence sent a request to the specified address - and he received a reply asking him to provide his postal address. Yesterday, March 29, I found a parcel from BMW with a DVD + R in my mailbox. On it - 950 megabytes of source code.

The second Hackfest in the history of ReactOS will begin on August 14, 2017

We are glad to share important information.

We decided to continue the tradition, so the second hackfest in the history of ReactOS will be! The event will be held from 14 to 18 August 2017 in Cologne (Germany). Those who wish are invited to attend, prior registration is required.

All information about the event can be obtained on a special wiki page .


Photo from the hackfest that took place in 2015

ReactOS hackfest will be devoted to intensive work to improve the performance of the operating system. Much attention will be paid to testing various USB-devices. If you are interested in how the previous hackfest went, then we recommend that you read the report on the 2015 event .

Developers such as Colin Finck, Eric Kohl, Giannis Adamopoulos, Mark Jansen, Thomas Faber, Victor Martinez, Timo Kreuzer have already confirmed their participation.

The list of participants and ideas for the hackfest is constantly updated on the second wiki page .

Participants pay for their own travel to the venue of the event. However, in exceptional cases that are not directly related to a trip to the hackfest, the Fund may partially or fully cover the costs of the participant, which is subject to individual discussion. The fund, however, pays for the accommodation for the participants at the time of the hackfest, if they agree on the option of accommodation in the hostel .

If a potential participant applies in advance, that is, right now, the Foundation can provide reference and information support, organize a joint transfer from the airport and stay at the hostel together with all participants, which will significantly reduce costs. Event Administrator - Colin Finck

You can participate in the hackfest remotely, as well as a series of live broadcasts from the thick of events.

What happened in the project in 60 days of summer?


  • Pre -release testing of the test image for version 0.4.6 was started.
  • Vadim Galyant laid out a new test build with improvements to the USB stack , which is able to work on real hardware.
  • The project volunteer, Pyotr Akhlamov, discovered that in ReactOS you can customize how Samba works and wrote instructions .
  • The project developer, Pierre Schweitzer, completed the basic work on the implementation of the distributed file system client NFS .
  • In VirtualBox, you can now also use the VirtIO network card with ReactOS .
  • ReactOS has activated support for the UDF file system. The source code of the proprietary driver was donated two years ago, but still has not worked.

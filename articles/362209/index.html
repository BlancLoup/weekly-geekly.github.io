Science Interstellar Travel Science Fiction


[source]

Speaking of "science fiction", in most cases we mean by this " space science fiction." In the sense that the future seems to us almost unthinkable without space exploration.
And if with the development of our solar system, everything looks relatively simple and clear - the methods of movement differ only in the degree of authors' spat on the laws of momentum, inertia and astrodynamics, then with interstellar flights everything is much more interesting. There are various options - from classic and in no way offending physics with sending ships on ordinary jet propulsion, loaded either with frozen carcasses or populations of people for long stays on them for generations, to quite exotic ones, like using Hell as an intermediate stop.

In this article, I tried to make an overview of the ways of delivering delicate human bodies from one star system to another, which are used by the most popular NF works, plus one of my personal favorites.

Star Wars


Hyperdrive



Star Wars was not a work that invented hyperdrive. However, they became the classic and most famous example of this technology, so common that it is, in fact, a way to get from star A to star B, implied by default.

The principle of action comes down to the fact that a spacecraft using a specific generator or engine falls into a parallel reality, which has other laws of physics and allows the ship to either move there much faster than the speed of light, or if flying a short distance to cover light years in ordinary space.

Hyperdrive and Star Wars hyperspace, however, has its own nuance. Hyperspace and ordinary space are interconnected and affect each other. Material objects cast gravitational "shadows" into hyperspace, which should be avoided. A collision with a shadow leads to disastrous results, in the case of a ship and a inhabited world - this is a rather apocalyptic event that can split the crust of the planet. For this reason, all the ZV ships are equipped with sewn security systems that automatically and forcibly take the ship into ordinary space if it is too close to the gravitational shadow. Some disable these systems in order to fly in extreme conditions, but do not live predictably for a long time.

Star Trek


Warp engine



Of all the ways of travel described in this article, Warp is perhaps the only FTL technology that currently has a real chance of being realized. Warp can safely be called the most realistic way to move above the speed of light of all known. Let it so far and remains only in the form of formulas on paper. We are talking about the engine Alcubierre .

The principle of operation follows from the way around the Special Theory of Relativity, which postulates that nothing in space can move faster than the speed of light. The ‚Äúpath‚Äù is that this postulate does not extend to the space itself, which can contract and stretch from various influences, for example, the gravitational field of bodies. The warp engine compresses the space in front of the ship and expands it behind the ship, moving the bubble of ordinary space along with the ship forward.

In the Star Trek universe, such engines develop speeds that are quite modest by science fiction standards - the Enterprise wanderings were limited to one sector of the galaxy near the Solar System (~ 1500 light years), took years and left a bunch of white spots and unexplored areas.

Babylon 5


Jumping gates



Babylon 5 uses a network of jumping gates leading to hyperspace for communication between the stars. In principle, this is a variation of the standard idea with a hyperdrive, but there are several differences that make this option more interesting.

First, most ships are not able to enter hyperspace on their own, with the exception of the largest ones. Therefore, the jumping gate network is highly valued in the galaxy, is considered a neutral zone and direct attacks on the gate itself are considered a violation of the rules of warfare and in general an extreme manifestation of rudeness, since they are necessary for all interstellar civilizations.

Secondly, the hyperspace itself in Babylon 5 is extremely active and independent navigation in it is considered impossible. Normal signals do not reach the ships, and the constant change in gravity inside hyperspace takes the ships themselves off course. For orientation, ships use a beam of tachyons, which is passed between beacons installed near the entry and exit points of the jumping gates in hyperspace, and in case of loss of the signal of such a beam, the ship can be considered irretrievably lost.

However, apparently this situation applies only to "young" civilizations, since all higher types, like Vorlons and Shadows, use hyperspace without the need for lighthouses. In hyperspace, there is also some kind of life of its own, which, however, most often remains rather indifferent to the ships flying past.

Third, Babylon 5 is one of the very few who at all somehow affects the economic aspect of the network of interstellar paths. The passage through the jump gate is charged to the ships passing through them, as well as from the governments to which these ships belong, for the very possibility of using the gate in the territory of the charging party. Personally, I can‚Äôt recall such details in other works at all; if there is any economy, it ends with ‚Äúwe bought for our starship hyperdrive‚Äù.

What is interesting is that none of the existing civilizations invented the gate technology themselves, but merely found or bought from someone else that related them to the next setting in the list.

Mass Effect


Element zero



All spaceships of the Mass Effect universe somehow use Element Zero in one way or another, which generates the indicated Mass Effect, either lowering the mass of objects around it, or increasing it. Thus, it is possible to turn the Theory of Relativity and exceed the speed of light.

Ordinary travels between planets and nearby stars are made using FTL engines, which can only reduce the mass of the ship. For interstellar long-haul flights, Mass Relays are used ‚Äî huge stations built around dense cores consisting of zero element. Relays are usually tied to one or more other relays and are able to create a corridor in which the mass actually disappears, almost instantly transferring the ship to thousands of light years.

For some reason, many of these relays were initially in the off state, and the galactic community initially actively investigated where such relays could lead. This eventually led to the fact that they came across the aggressive look of arachnids, who started a ruthless war with them. Subsequently, the switched off relays were banned because of fear of digging up in the chasms of the universe, which is worse. Because of this, people, having found one of these relays near Pluto, subsequently initially earned themselves a disapproving rating in the Galaxy, as they began to explore the Relay network and turn them on right and left, which resulted in a war with one of the species, turians, who tried to stop it disgrace.

Star Gates


Star Gates



In the Stargate setting, interstellar travel is somewhat atypical. In order to be on another planet, a spacecraft ... is not needed. Moving between the stars is carried out with the help of the Stargate, rings of several meters in diameter, made of exotic material, built by an ancient, extinct civilization, and the spacecraft themselves are not used as often.

When activated, the stargates create a stable one-sided wormhole between the calling ring and the called ring, allowing you to instantly move any objects that are suitable in size to crawl into the wormhole.

The one-sidedness of the gate stems from their mode of action: the matter does not survive the passage through the wormhole, therefore the sending gate engages in the analysis of objects passing through them into subatomic particles, and the receiving gate reconstructs the material back into matter.
Spoiler for SG-1
In addition to this, the gate can store information about the last object transferred for some time, and in one of the series of the series it was used to bring the team member back to life ... That, in turn, leads me to the conclusion that each movement using Stargate is essentially the destruction of the original, and the creation of an identical copy of it at the exit. In general, I would not recommend this way of moving between the planets.

Each Gate has its own seven-digit number, six characters define the coordinates of the receiving Gate, and the seventh character defines the transmitting Gate. The eighth character is used to communicate with Gates that are outside the normal network of action, usually in another galaxy.

In addition to the Stargate, the usual classic ships are also used, for various reasons, for example, during massive attacks, or if there are no Stargates on the target planet. In this case, the ships use a completely classical method in the form of hyperspace, only sometimes with simply indecently high efficiency (in one case, the space cruiser of civilization Asgard was able to overcome the distance between two galaxies in a few minutes ). However, despite this, transportation with the help of the Gate remains the preferred option.

Dune


Guild of Navigators



Relationships of the Dune universe with space travel are complex. As a result of the Butlerian Jihad, all computer equipment was destroyed and banned . Therefore, flights in space have become completely dependent on the Guild of Navigators and their huge cargo ships - highliners.

For the highline, the guild navigator and the Holtzmann generator are required. The latter uses the Holtzmann effect, which ‚Äúcollapses‚Äù space, bringing two distant points closer to each other and allowing one to make interstellar travels. Navigators use the drug spice, produced on Dune, to expand their minds and acquire the ability to foresee the future. This allows them to safely lead the ship through the convoluted space, avoiding collisions with various space bodies.

Constant overdose with a spice at the same time exposes their bodies to strongest mutations, so ultimately the Navigator becomes unable to live somewhere other than the atom sphere, supersaturated with spice pairs, which would be lethal to any other creature. Their psychology also undergoes deformations, which in essence makes it possible to classify Navigators rather even as a separate species.

Navigators, although they seem to be an independent apolitical company, are nevertheless almost the most important part of the cosmic civilization of the Dune universe, since they alone allow interplanetary and interstellar communications to exist, without which society would fall apart into separate isolated planets. Therefore, even the Padishah-Emperor communicates with representatives of the Guild very carefully.

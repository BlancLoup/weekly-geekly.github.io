Low-cost 3D printers for printing photopolymers


While there are already a great many printers for printing molten plastic, there have been practically no amateur printers printing with photopolymers until recently. The main advantage of such printers is high resolution, the smooth surface of the product practically does not require additional processing. The price tag of professional printers rolls over tens of thousands of cu But now printers began to appear in the budget category.


MiiCraft




Miniature printer based on DLP pico projector. The photo above shows that the device includes two parts - a printer and a clamp (provides additional illumination of the printed model, although this can be done simply in the sun) While blue and transparent polymers are available, but others are being developed.

Projector resolution : 854 x 480
Printing area : 43mm x 27mm x 180mm (XYZ)
Device Size : 20.5cm (W) x 20.8cm (L) x 33.5cm (H)
Resolution : 56 microns in XY, 100 microns in Z axis
Print speed : 2-3 cm per hour
Polymer price : $ 250 per kilo ($ 0.25 per cubic cm)
Project website : miicraft.com
Printer price : $ 2000
Project status : by mid-June of this (2012) year, it is planned to complete the construction of samples and start a company to raise money for production on indiegogo.com .







B9Creator

Resolution : 50-100 microns in XY axes (achieved by adjusting the focus),
6-100 micron Z-axis (software selection)
Printing area with 100 microns accuracy: 102 mm x 76 mm x 203 mm (XYZ)
Printing area with 50 micron accuracy: 51 mm x 38 mm x 203 mm (XYZ)
Device Size : 79 cm x 47 cm x 30.5 cm
Print speed : 12 ~ 20mm per hour

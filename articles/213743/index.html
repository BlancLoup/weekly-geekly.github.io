Published list of open-source organizations participating in Google Summer of Code 2014



A list of open-source organizations participating in Google Summer of Code 2014 has just been published. Google Summer of Code (abbreviated GSoC ) was first held in 2005, and this year it is held on the anniversary, the 10th time. In honor of the anniversary, Google promised that this year it would take 10 organizations more than ever in the past, and 10% more students than ever before. Thus, 190 open-source organizations were selected this year (since the maximum number of accepted organizations was 180 in 2012).

Students who wish to participate in GSoC may begin to familiarize themselves with the list of organizations . Accepting applications from students this year will begin on March 10 and end on March 21. More information about GSoC (from a student‚Äôs point of view) can be found in this topic . The only difference with past years is that the size of the scholarship for students has increased, and now it is 5,500 US dollars .

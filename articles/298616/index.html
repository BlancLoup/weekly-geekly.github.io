Conclusion






Simplify processes, test hypotheses, strive to be first in everything:

The attention of Internet users boils and melts from the information glut. The man learned to ignore banners, and now protects himself from news sources. People pay attention to the publication of their usual publishing houses, and ignore all the rest. The involvement of the audience is a determining factor for the survival of the media and profit.

In the struggle for the attention of readers will win the one who:
  1. Spends less resources to achieve the same results: optimizes every aspect of the work of the editorial board;
  2. Applies the latest technology and automates processes: he knows about his audience more than others and involves them with the help of self-learning systems.

Simplify processes and, if possible, even get rid of them. Find a way to do in one click what you are doing now in three, or do not do at all.

As soon as the editors can replace people with robots - replace them.
  • look for new traffic sources;
  • follow media trends;
  • test hypotheses of western colleagues;
  • do not manually do what is automated;
  • look for the best authors.

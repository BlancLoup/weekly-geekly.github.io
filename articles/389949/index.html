Top 10 Strangest Things Printed on a 3D Printer

More than 30 years ago in 1983, Chuck Hill (Chuck 'Hull) invented the first STL 3D printer (STL is a stereolithography technology where, under the influence of UV radiation, polymer by layer with certain properties is cured), after 3 years it founded the company 3D Systems. And although 3D printing technology is the same age as CD players and camcorders, it has not achieved such popularity. However, with the advent of the RepRap project, the alignment of forces has changed, and cheap Chinese 3D printers, even FDM and not STL, have brought this technology to the masses in recent years. Almost everyone has already heard about 3D printing, and there are constantly unusual methods of using these technologies in the news. But the photo below is the world's first 3D STL printer.



So, I present a selection of ten of the strangest things that can be printed on a 3D printer.

2. Tapkburger




Oh gods, he eats sneakers! So probably people think when they first saw a person absorbing "Shoe Burger". And here, in fact, there is nothing unusual. This is just a burger with a bun in the form of your sneaker or shoes.



Several advanced technologies came together here: 3D scanning, 3D printing, and silicone casting. That's basically all the description of the process.





Put your shoe on the table of the 3D scanner, create a digital copy. Print it on a 3D printer and make the reverse form of heat-resistant food-grade silicone. Now it remains only to fill it with dough and send it to the oven.





I think this is a great way to surprise your friends who came to visit you. Just feed them with slippers.

3. Nano-sculptures


We live in amazing time, we are surrounded by a huge amount of wonderful things that can be seen and often even touched. But Jonty Hurwitz creates sculptures that can not be touched, and even to see them without a microscope will not work.



All this thanks to a special 3D-printer that can create such tiny objects.



Who was complaining about the lamination of the print? With such a printer, there will be no problems with lamination, although in order to create an object that is more or less visible to the human eye, you will have to wait a very long time.



Such nanosculptures appeared thanks to the technology of the institute "Karlsruhe Institute of Technology" , which are successfully used in the "Weizmann Institute of Science" . Such a printing method is called multiphoton lithography which is based on the phenomenon of two-photon absorption.



So, at the junction of advanced technology and art, something new is created, now the whole museum can easily be carried in your pocket.

But science does not stand still, and it is becoming realistic to print not only metal structures for the human body, but also the basis of organs that will not be rejected by the human body.

7. Bioprint or what have you heard about B-code?


Different types of art have always been considered by some people amazing, and others - strange. Now I will talk about really strange, hmm ... probably forms. I do not know which category this belongs to :)



This is the so-called bioprint. Printing objects pori help bees. That's where the fantasy can play out, paint incredible machines using bees. But no, everything is much simpler. Biologist Jennifer Berry (Jennifer Berry) once looked at the wild hives, thought it looked good, and it would be interesting to control the creation of forms of hives.



In the end, after several attempts, she created a kind of bioprinter-artificial hive in which the bees live and create honeycombs under her control.



The technology is simple - you need to create a form that limits the external borders of the model being created,



And it is also necessary to specify the direction of growth of honeycombs from a special material.



All this is placed in a transparent box, in which a certain microclimate is maintained and there is a special valve that blocks the bees from entering the hive after they complete the creation of the object. 8. Giant sculptures




While some print nanosculptures, others create giant art objects on ordinary FDM printers. For example, the famous artist Hong-Ji Pung from Taipei (Taiwan) implemented a powerful artistic project at the Museum of Fine Arts - an ocean liner, allegedly twisted by the forces of nature.



The creation of this exhibit took a total of 42,000 hours of work. Printing was carried out by thirty-two FDM printers, 6000 pieces were printed with a total weight of 500 kilograms. And the length of the liner was 8 meters.





With his project, Pang wants to remind mankind of his impotence in the face of uncontrolled natural forces, sometimes leading to terrible catastrophes.

9. Cars


One of the most appreciative industries for 3D printing is perhaps automotive. In addition to prototyping and creating prototypes, the automotive industry is actively testing new types of materials in automobile production and new methods for their production.



For example, the FORD corporation which owns as many as five centers of three-dimensional printing, producing hundreds of parts per day. Printers in these centers print a wide range of materials: from silicates to nylon, and even sand. One of the favorite examples of the company when it comes to three-dimensional printing is the engine intake manifold.



But this is all particular. Of interest, in the context of this article, are the projects of cars entirely or mostly printed on 3D-printers.



A recent example of a high-tech car created using 3D printing technology is the Blade supercar from Divergent Microfactories.



Blade is a frame structure consisting of carbon rods and special aluminum hubs. According to the creators of Blade, this not only saves material, but also eases the car by 90% compared to traditional designs. Blade is equipped with a 700 hp gasoline or natural gas engine. Thanks to the powerful engine and light body, the car can accelerate from 0 to 100 km / h in 2.2 seconds.

It probably sounds fantastic, and in practice it is not yet applicable. However, some imitation of such devices already exists. The strangest thing printed on a 3D printer at number 10 is food.



Strange here, the fact that it is only shaping with the help of a 3D printer, no changes in the properties of materials or the creation of almost nothing are out of the question.



Filled the mince into a tube and squeeze it according to the program in the desired shape, then into the oven.



Filled the dough - squeezed the base for pizza or tartlets. It is rather an assistant for the culinary specialist, when it is difficult to extrude the drawing with hands. The machine will do it with an accuracy of hundredths of millimeters.



Although the artistic value is still present here. With proper imagination and small skill, you can create masterpieces of culinary art.

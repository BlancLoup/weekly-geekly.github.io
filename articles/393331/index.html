Personal electric transport. Start


Back in 2001, Dean Kamen introduced the two-wheeled vehicle, naming it Segway . The simplicity of management, minimalistic, but at the same time beautiful design was planted on fertile American soil and - in the literal sense of the word - launched a start-up into an international orbit. Even Steve Jobs himself honored the development of a short but capacious phrase: " as the development of personal electric chariots" on the same shelf as the invention and creation of the PC.

In the United States, due to the lack of a developed public transport system and, to a certain extent, giant-style construction, such portable electric scooters have gained immense popularity. Yes, and the use of Segway on production sites or when shooting movies and during sporting events, when it is necessary to travel significant distances to and fro during the day, laid the foundation for a successful start.

For example, GT fans will surely recall the Mythbusters and Adam Savage series, which cuts through an abandoned airfield on the Segway - this was a time of white envy:



Over time, the engineers created more miniature, but capacious batteries and compact, but powerful electric motors, and microelectronics stepped so far forward that the mini-computer responsible for balancing segways, gyroscopes and electro-skates fit in the palm of your hand.

Gradually, personal electric vehicles moved from "elite golf clubs" to the streets of cities and became available to almost everyone ...

The old, kind and time-tested segway from the company Xiaomi. Distinctive features of this model are powerful engines of 350 W each, which allows it to reach an average speed of 16 km / h. Of course, Usain Bolt will not be able to catch up, but you can compete with a marathon runner. At the same time, the capacity of the installed battery is enough for a trip of as much as 22 km.

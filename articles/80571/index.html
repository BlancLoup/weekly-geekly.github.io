Skype launched high definition video calls


Skype has announced the beginning of support for video calls via Skype on a PC in high-definition mode (720p HD), and also that the software will be integrated into widescreen TVs that have access to the Internet.

The latest version of Skype for Windows is capable of providing video calls in HD mode, with a resolution of 1280x720 pixels, up to 30 frames per second, giving users the option of video calling with even more saturated colors and smooth color transitions.

In order to make a video call in high definition mode, users will need a high-speed broadband Internet connection, a new webcam that supports HD mode, a PC with a 1.8 GHz dual-core processor and Skype 4.2 Beta for Windows.

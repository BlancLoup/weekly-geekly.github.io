Mobile Atom has become even more atomic

In the line of Atom processors for mobile devices, a flagship appeared - the Z2760 processor. The novelty is designed for tablets and handhelds based on Windows 8, but it will certainly be used in other handheld devices that require a large computing power of the CPU. The specifications show the extraordinary energy efficiency of the Z2760 (more than 10 hours of HD video playback, standby time up to 3 weeks) - and this, note, with two cores and a frequency of almost two gigahertz.

We give the main parameters of the processor:
  • Two cores up to 1.80 GHz, support for four threads and Intel Burst technology and Intel Hyper-Threading.
  • 32-nanometer manufacturing technology and transistors based on metal gates and second-generation High-K dielectrics.
  • Integrated Intel Graphics Media Accelerator graphics system with a graphics core up to 533 MHz and hardware acceleration support for encoding and decoding video in 1080p format.
  • Support built-in and external displays. Support for one built-in MIPI-DSI or LVDS display and one external display via HDMI 1.3 interface.
  • Integrated memory controller and LPDDR2 support with a speed of 800 million transmissions per second and up to 2 GB.
  • Integrated multimedia card 4.41 (eMMC 4.41).
  • Energy Management. Uses a low power state in idle mode (S0ix) to support Microsoft Connected Standby technology.
  • Camera and image signal processing (ISP) processor. Integrated ISP processor with support for the main HD-camera (up to 8 megapixels) and an additional camera (up to 2.1 megapixels).
  • Supports GPS, screen rotation sensor, hardware sensor hub, ALS, SARS, and proximity and temperature sensors.

Great Giana Sisters


In 1987, a certain Time Warp Production Inc. released the game, whose appearance among the publishers of computer games has become synonymous with both the scandal and the question of the need to create it. That's because the game turned out to be like two peas in a pod like the undisputed favorite of the time - Super Mario Bros.

According to one version, the creators tried to make Mario available on more platforms than Nintendo allowed. (Indeed, only owners of Nintendo consoles could play in Super Mario Bros.)



According to another version, the calculation of the developers was simple: as soon as possible to cut the dough on everything already ready. A bit of the history of the game


Initially, the company released its creation on the Commodore 64, and soon games were released on the Amiga, Amstrad CPC, Atari ST and MSX2. Version for the ZX Spectrum did not have time to get out of the claims of Nintendo. A later, Great Giana Sisters ported, unofficially, on Windows, DOS, Linux, Mac OS X, AmigaOS 4, NetBSD, AROS, MorphOS, Symbian OS and, in 2009, on Nintendo DS.

What was the result?


Since the game was withdrawn from sale immediately after release, rumors spread that Nintendo - the creator of the real Mario - decided to sue the plagiarist. Nintendo, indeed, by all means contributed to the seizure, but, nevertheless, there were no litigation. Well, in those copies of the game, which had time to leak into the sale, quickly became the property of collectors and, as usual, began to cost a lot of money.

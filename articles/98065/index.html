Google will increase the salary of employees with a non-traditional sexual orientation

The company decided to increase the salary of employees with non-traditional sexual orientation, so that they could pay a health insurance tax.

In this way, the company compensates for the increased tax on medical insurance benefits imposed on all same-sex partners.

However, some have already expressed disagreement with such a policy of Google. Is this a form of discrimination that one group gets more money than the other?

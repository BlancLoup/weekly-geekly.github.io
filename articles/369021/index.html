Review of the third international exhibition Robotics Expo 2015 in Moscow

The third international exhibition Robotics Expo 2015 is over in Moscow. The guests talked with promoter robots, shied away from low-flying multicopters, patted the robot guards, looked at the capabilities of industrial manipulators, and rooted for the participants in the robot fights.



Industrial Robot Manipulators


An industrial robotic arm, in the GOST language, is ‚Äúan automatic machine, stationary or mobile, consisting of an actuator in the form of a manipulator with several degrees of mobility, and a reprogrammable program control device for performing motor and control functions in the production process‚Äù. Such robots differ depending on the number of joints and their ‚Äúflexibility‚Äù. A tool is installed on their wrist - it can be a drill, saw, hammer, tong or torch, it all depends on the needs of production.

At the third international exhibition Robotics Expo in Moscow presented the possibilities of such robots - manipulators fought with lightsabers, cut bushes and threw bones.

Kuka Manipulators


Industrial robots Kuka come from Germany. They are the leaders of the global automotive industry, but use them in many areas. Kuka manipulators are six-axis robots of any size and capacity, they are able to stack pallets, are engaged in assembly and welding, can work in a sterile room and are highly accurate.
Kuka loves to play tennis, and he is good at it.



At the Robotics Expo 2015, Kuka robots staged a small show on Star Wars.









At the booth of the Russian company Quantum Systems there was a Kuka manipulator programmed to form bushes. The company, using the example of a robot gardener, presented technologies of high-precision determination of coordinates and orientation and generation of the trajectories of movement of an industrial manipulator in real time. Processing 3D point clouds allows you to determine the coordinates with an accuracy of more than five millimeters by fifty meters, the developers say.









Yaskawa Robot Welder


Yaskawa has developed the Arcsystem welding systems. These complexes are a fence with one or two robots performing welding operations. The complex is selected individually for the needs of production, there are more than six hundred possible combinations with different welding torches with air or water cooling, tracks for moving robots and the bases for them.

When the Arcsystem complex is installed on the platform, it can be transferred using a forklift or a crane - the hole for the forklift and eye bolts are already on the platform.







Fanuc Robot


Fanuc manipulator allows you to install almost any equipment on it. This Japanese company was once a subsidiary of Fujitsu.





Universal manipulator Universal Robots


You do not need a protective fence for the Universal Robots manipulator - it is certified to work with people. The manipulator frees them from the monotonous work.

An example of the use of such a robot in production is a small enterprise Thiele, which produces small-scale batches of tools for design, processing, testing and measuring equipment. The robot's duties are to open the doors, load the raw materials, tighten the chuck of the milling machine and put the part aside after milling. In addition, he checks the quality of products using an image processing system.

At the production of the company Oticon, producing hearing aids, the manipulator works with parts of about a millimeter in length. Devices are becoming less, therefore, requires high accuracy. The manipulator moves over the mold, removing microscopic plastic parts. The robot performs the operation using a vacuum system specially designed for this process.

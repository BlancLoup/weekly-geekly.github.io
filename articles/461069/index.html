Five stages of testing


We work with two leading manufacturing plants in the world. We usually agree that we are shown a roadmap for a year, so that we can plan the development of firmware for specific cameras in advance.

There are always interesting and promising models in the roadmap. We highlight the actual ones for ourselves and wait for the first samples to come to our office. Next, the testing process consists of five stages:

  1. The first input testing includes analysis for image quality, sound, motion detection, etc. We draw attention to the quality of the material of the case, fasteners and other components. Based on the test results, we take it further into development, refuse or send it for revision.
  2. If everything suits us, we take the model into development. At this stage, a second test takes place. Our developers look at the software ‚Äúinternals‚Äù of the camera firmware, find bugs, errors and even vulnerabilities. We are seeking to address all of these shortcomings.
  3. Before the release of the firmware, a third test is carried out. If everything is in order, then release it in release.
  4. When the first batch samples arrive at the Ivideon office, they are also tested for compliance with previously established parameters.
  5. Finally, when a batch of cameras arrives at the warehouse, they arrive and undergo the last random testing.

In addition, we undertake warranty obligations (2-5 years) and replace the problem equipment with new one completely free of charge.

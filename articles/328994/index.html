Tale of two printers, or how we drove the Ricoh printers

Recently, we faced the challenge of choosing an office printer for a new department. And then, as an order, gave us "to try" two printers Ricoh - SP 400 DN and SP 450 DN. Models are quite new, the release of the end of 2016. Of course, we could not refuse the tempting offer and decided to "drive" the devices a little in the field, so to speak, conditions. What came of it - read on.


For a start - a few words about the models themselves. Both devices - monochrome A4 laser printers, are designed for use by small working groups. Print speed is 30 and 40 pages per minute for SP 400 DN and SP 450, respectively.


SP 400 DN


SP 450 DN

The first model, according to the manufacturer, provides for a peak load of 50,000 prints per month, the second - 150,000 copies. Both machines print with a resolution of up to 1200x1200 dpi and support A4, A5, A6, B5, and B6 paper with a density of up to 162 g / m¬≤. Models are controlled using a small LED screen, support printing from mobile and smart devices thanks to the Smart Device Print & Scan application and AirPrint technology, have energy-saving mode and support for Smart LED technology, providing low power consumption.


SP 400 DN with extended paper feed trays


SP 450 DN with extended paper feed trays

Actually, the task was to conduct a small test on three main criteria for the operation of printers - performance, fault tolerance and refueling convenience. As for the first aspect, there were no surprises when printing one-sided documents, and both printers showed the speed level declared by the manufacturer.

However, the test for duplex printing showed the advantage of the SP 450 DN model, which in itself is generally not surprising - the design of the duplex printer allows you to work with two sheets of paper at once. While one sheet is printed, the second flips over in the duplex. Due to this, the printing speed turned out to be quite high - at the level of 18 pages per minute, which in the end practically equals the printing speed of one-sided documents.

But the 400 DN model has a standard pattern of sequential printing and one sheet flip. As a result, the performance of a 30-page printer with a two-sided dropped to 9 pages per minute - almost doubled. That is, from the point of view of productivity, for those offices where there is a need for large volumes of printing from two sides of a sheet, for example, to save paper, the SP 450 DN is better suited for the two described models.

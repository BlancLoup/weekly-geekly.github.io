Russian space in 2015: what does it prepare for us



On February 1, the first Russian launch of this year took place: the British telecommunications satellite Inmarsat 5 F2 was successfully launched by the Proton-M launch vehicle.

In this article, I decided to highlight what can be added to the Russian orbital constellation of satellites and what launches are expected in 2015. In this material, only domestic satellites are considered, excluding the withdrawal of foreign payload by Russian missiles. All in all, about 11 launches are planned for launching foreign satellites.

In the process of writing the article, the launches of some devices were moved to the following years. Many of the satellites scheduled for launch this year migrated from previous years. Unfortunately, this is a common practice in astronautics, and not only do we rarely have truly objective scientific, technical, political, and economic reasons. But still we hope that all scheduled launches will be implemented! And what awaits us - under the cut.

The space complex of the hydrometeorological and oceanographic support Meteor-3M continues to grow. This year, the launch of the second unit Meteor-M No. 2-1, developed by VNIIEM, is expected. In one carrier rocket with Meteor-M, the twin satellites Ionosphere, also developed by VNIIEM, should fly. These devices will fly in a solar-synchronous orbit, and study the processes in the upper atmosphere (magnetosphere, ionosphere) and solar activity.

The domestic global navigation system GLONASS plans to replenish with 7 spacecraft. ISS


During the year, four manned flights of the Soyuz spacecraft are planned to take place on the ISS. In addition to regular work at the station, 2015 may be marked by a number of interesting events.

This year it is planned to conduct the first experiment with an annual mission: Mikhail Kornienko and Scott Kelly will work at the station during the year. This is done to check how the human body will behave, being in a state of weightlessness for a long time. Even with the current ‚Äústandard‚Äù semi-annual missions, the body experiences a sufficient effect: astronauts become 3-5 cm higher due to spinal column stretching, so the muscles of the whole body without atrophy gradually become atrophied and the astronauts have to constantly engage in simulators, and the heart becomes more rounded .

Another interesting fact is that Scott Kelly has a twin brother, who is also an astronaut, and scientists have a unique opportunity to compare the effect of weightlessness on similar organisms. In fairness, it is worth noting that for humanity this will not be the first experience of a long stay in space. The world record belongs to Valery Polyakov: he spent 437 days at the Mir station in 1994-1995.

But this year another record can be broken: in terms of the total duration of your stay in space. At the moment, this achievement belongs to Sergey Krikalev, who for a total of 6 flights to the Mir space stations and the ISS spent 803 days in space. A new champion can be Gennady Padalka, who already has 710 days of stay in space on his account and, if he joins the crew of the ISS 43/44, he will spend another six months in orbit.

After a five-year break this year, the ISS is planning to visit the 8th space tourist - Sarah Brightman, who will stay at the station for 10 days. She is currently undergoing training in the CPK name Yu.A. Gagarin. During his time in space, a series of experiments with vocal chords will be performed - Sarah plans to perform several songs of her repertoire.

Ensuring the necessary supply of fuel, water and food, as well as various equipment, according to tradition, will be handled by the Progress series transport and cargo spacecraft. A total of 4 launches are planned for the first time and a new upgraded Progress MS will be tested for the first time.

Cosmodrome


The year should end perhaps the most discussed event - the launch of the Soyuz 2.1A Union from the new Vostochny cosmodrome. As a payload, the above mentioned satellites will be put into orbit: Lomonosov, Aist-2 and Samsat-218. The cosmodrome is located in the Amur Region, not far from the Free Spaceport, which ceased to exist. The start of construction is July 2012, the first launch is December 2015. For reference, about 2.5 years have passed since the construction of the Baikonur Cosmodrome began until the first launch of the R-7 with the first PS-1 Earth satellite. However, it should be borne in mind that the new cosmodrome is more technological in terms of capital facilities. In total, 2 launch pads of the Union of the Light and Middle Class rocket and a platform for the Angara-5 heavy rocket will be built. It will also be an interesting decision that all the facilities of the assembly and test complex will be united by a common gallery, which will shorten the time of preparing the rocket for launch.

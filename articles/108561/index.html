I will try to acquaint you with APL more closely, the language is quite useful, and, in spite of frightening at first glance "carp", is logical, beautiful, and for programming mathematical calculations and operations with large data arrays it is also the best choice of development tools.

So, APL, - the abbreviation is deciphered very simply: A Programming Language. The idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe language came from Kenneth Iverson (Kenneth E. The peak of using APL falls in the mid-80s, when there were many versions of the language and dialects, including those for microcontrollers. At some point, even Microsoft Corporation was planning to release its version of APL, but for unknown reasons, this release did not take place. In the early 80s, a new version of the language appeared - APL2, which was created by the IBM development team under the leadership of Dr. Jim Brown. This version became the basis of the modern APL.

Now there are about a dozen different APL interpreters.

Google launched the Android Pay payment system

The official blog Google announces the launch of the US payment system Android Pay. From a technical point of view, you can take advantage of the new product using a smartphone with KitKat 4.4 or newer, which also supports NFC. A special app should appear during this week. It will support American Express, Discover, MasterCard and Visa payment cards, and it will be possible to pay with Android Pay at more than one million American outlets.

It is impossible not to notice that with Google payment applications there may be confusion. Google Play already has a Google Wallet released a few years ago. About a month ago, Samsung announced that its own payment system Samsung Pay is more reliable than its future competitor from the depths of Google. The idea is that a number of new Korean device companies - Galaxy S6, S6 Edge, S6 Edge + and Note 5 - are equipped with MST (Magnetic Secure Transmission) technology. Thanks to this, Samsung devices can generate a dynamically changing magnetic field. In other words, if you bring your smartphone to a regular payment terminal, in which you can pay with cards with magnetic tape, it acts on the reader of the terminal as if a ‚Äúreal‚Äù plastic card was actually drawn through it.

Toshiba R800

Toshiba has introduced a new series of unique ultra-thin and light notebook R800 Ailtair. A feature of this line, along with impressive computing power, was a number of Toshiba technological innovations, which made it possible to increase the reliability and stability of laptops to deformation. For the first time, the novelties were presented in Barcelona at the Mobile World Congress 2011 exhibition. In Russia, the R800 Ailtair will appear in the 2nd quarter of 2011.




The series, called beautifully Ailtair, expands the model range, and also continues and develops the ideas embodied in the previous version of the Toshiba Satellite R630 compact notebooks. Now, the R800 lineup includes three models at once: R830, R840 and R850, with diagonals, respectively, 13.3 ", 14.0" and 15.6 ".



Laptops are equipped with latest generation processors Intel Core Sandy Bridge, there is a USB 3.0 port that allows you to transfer data at speeds up to 5 Gb / s, an optical drive, a hard disk with a capacity of up to 640 GB, optionally you can use a solid-state drive (SSD).

Models differ not only in display sizes.

The R830 has a magnesium alloy body, and the bottom panel has a honeycomb structure, which provides additional structural rigidity.



But in the R840 and R850 used a special polymer High Stiffness Resin, superior in strength to analogues.

First impressions from the video store iTunes

After the release of iTunes 7, the first impressions about the purchase of videos in the online store Apple iTunes 7 Store already appeared on the Internet. In general, the opinions are positive, because the owners of Apple products are exclusively loyal users. But the reviews can not be called admired.

The first disappointment is the extremely small selection of movies. Here are products from Disney only (Pixar, Touchstone and Miramax). Apparently, with the rest of the studios have not yet been able to agree on the terms of cooperation. As you know, Apple would be beneficial to set minimum prices for video films in order to stimulate sales of its equipment, which brings it the main income. The latest models of iPods are already equipped with 80 GB hard drives and are able to play video files. In addition, the online video store should pull sales of iTV brand set-top boxes specifically designed to download movies from the Internet and watch them on TV (set-top boxes will be available in early 2007 for $ 300).

The second disappointment is quite high prices. Probably, it would be logical to assume that the prices of video files will be significantly lower than the prices of material media, such as DVD. But it is not. In fact, prices are practically the same. Fresh films are sold for $ 13 (with pre-order) or $ 15 (after going on sale), old - for $ 10. Short films, music videos and parts of the series are sold for $ 2 each. In addition, many films can be downloaded only by American users. Availability for a particular region varies from film to film.

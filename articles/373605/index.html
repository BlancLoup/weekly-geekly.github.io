Appearance


The plastic case of the smartphone lies quite comfortably in the hand due to the rounded ends and a slightly rounded back cover and protective glass around the perimeter.







Above the screen is unusually closely from all sorts of devices: there is a flash, a speaker, a front camera and a sensor for light and proximity. The protective glass does not cover the entire front part, a narrow plastic frame runs around, also playing to improve the tactile "streamlining" of the smartphone.



It's unusual that ZenFone Live is devoid of the Zen line's concentric pattern, I somehow got used to it as an obligatory attribute.



The main camera is shifted to the upper left corner.



At the bottom there is a microUSB connector, speaker and microphone.



At the top - the audio jack and the second microphone.



On the right are the power and volume buttons.



And on the left side is hidden tray for SIM-cards and memory cards.





Please note that in ZenFone Live, you can simultaneously use either two SIM cards or one SIM card and a microSD memory card.

The smartphone has a very decent 5-inch display with a resolution of 1280 x 720. Brightness is enough so that when streaming on a sunny day you can comfortably track yourself on the screen.

Cameras and software for shooting

On the sides of the camera, there is a rather large flash and an illumination sensor, so when streaming and shooting, selfies do not have to look for a lighter place, or choose a view that illuminates an external light source.

Performance and software


The smartphone is built on a Qualcomm Snapdragon 400 processor operating at up to 1.6 GHz. Graphic chip - Adreno 305. 2 GB of RAM, built-in - 16 or 32 GB. Of course, this volume may not be enough for a smartphone that can be actively used for shooting video, but the problem is solved by installing a memory card of up to 128 GB.

ZenFone Live is running Android 6.0 with a proprietary graphic shell ZenUI 3.0. In addition to the standard set of proprietary utilities and the BeautyLive program integrated with the camera, a fitness app is installed on the smartphone.

Conclusion


ZenFone Live, as far as I know, is the first smartphone in the world, in which special attention is paid to the convenience and quality of shooting photo and video selfies. It is equipped with an additional front flash and a light sensor, but more importantly, the BeautyLive software function is integrated into ZenFone Live, which real-time corrects skin imperfections when shooting through the front camera, both in the photo and in the video. Two microphones make it much more effective to suppress the noises of the city and the wind, which so often spoil the sound in amateur videos. In addition, the phone shoots video with stereo sound, so that viewers will be able to better feel the environment in which the shooting is conducted. And if you buy headphones that support DTS Headphone: X technology, you will be able to please yourself with a wonderful sound in 7.1 format.

But it would be wrong to assume that ZenFone Live is suitable only for video bloggers. Both externally and in function, this is a universal smartphone that will suit everyone, regardless of sex or addiction to selfies and video broadcasts: a dark gray, almost black model is offered for men, and a pink and golden model for women (as a copy from the review).



ZenFone Live has a high-quality screen, its design is not boring, it is compact enough, many consider 5 inches as the most balanced size for a smartphone. And if you suddenly decide that it would be nice to capture yourself with friends, or against the background of some event, then you will find tools that will improve the quality of photos or videos when shooting on the front camera.

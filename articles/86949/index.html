NEOGEO is familiar to most people not by the home version, but by the big slot machines that were popular in Russia. For me personally, NEOGEO is a series of King of Fighters fighting games and a funny Metal Slug arcade game (the seventh part of which, by the way, is in the native version for NDS). The emulator (NeoDS), in principle, works without problems, although it sometimes slows down - there is not enough RAM.
Memory upgrade (purchase of EZ-Flash 3in1) is planned (paid, waiting for delivery).

Installing and using an emulator is somewhat more complicated than the previous ones.
1) First, the emulator is downloaded and the bios for the emulator .
2) The emulator itself (NeoDs.nds) is copied to the flash drive.
3) Next, the most interesting part begins - the process of converting rums:
3.1) In the archive with the emulator lies the NeoDsConvert.exe program, it must be copied into a separate folder.
3.2) In the same folder, put the bios for the emulator and the archives with games (it so happened that the Roma for neogeo always live in zip-archives, even when using their emulators).
3.3) After that, run NeoDsConvert with the -bios8 key (‚ÄúNeoDsConvert.exe -bios8‚Äù) - games that were successfully converted will get the neo extension (that is, if you were in the kof98.zip folder, then kof98 will appear next to it. neo).
4) The resulting files must be copied to the root directory of the memory card. neogeo.neo can not be copied - it is needed only at the stage of conversion.
5) On the console itself, everything is as usual - launch neods.nds, choose a game and play. When asked to throw a coin - press Select. :) The size of the picture again does not fit the screen size, you can enter or play as it is, if the edges of the screen are not particularly important.

Unfortunately, not all games are converted, but only those that are stored in * .bin. If in the archive with the game are * .rom or some other extension, the conversion will not occur. But KoF'y and Slug'i converted successfully.
About 160 games were successfully converted from this archive (and here you can look at the compatibility list ).

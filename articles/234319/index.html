"Rostov IT-community" or 5 steps to create a party in your city



Friday night is a great time to go to some interesting event. But what to do if you do not live in Moscow or St. Petersburg, where often there are parties for geeks, developers and IT managers in general?

After my posting on the blog, the infrastructure builders began to meet people who do the same in other cities. A certain general outline appeared in my head, according to which communities can be created where they are not yet. I will give here 5 simple steps in the style of Captain Obvious, to how to start brewing a party in your town.

1. Find a place.
It should be free (you do a non-commercial story).
It should be free to accommodate more than 50 people. We had 130 on JS mitap, the people didn‚Äôt fit and left :(
It should be in the heart of the city.
There should be a projector, wi-fi, sockets, audio equipment (microphone and speakers).
There should be a cozy (ideally loft space).
There you can drink beer. Otherwise why all this ??!

2. Find thematic communities. Surely you already have a party of iOS developers, rubists or hardcore java-guys in the city. You need to create some kind of add-on to unite all these people. They will not fight, do not worry, they have more common interests than holivarny differences.

3. Find people in social networks. Create a group of VK and FB, you can even twitter, but here it is somehow not very popular. Invite all familiar programmers and all those who are in thematic public. Create an event page in social networks for each meeting and invite people from the main page. Do not let headhunters in the group.

4. Stay independent. We had corporate attempts to create communities in the city, but they all slipped into frenzy hunting. A delicate issue is sponsorship and donations. It is very important to understand the difference: when sponsoring a person pays money and receives something in return (advertising, PR, setting up a banner or stand, the possibility of email sending to participants, etc.), donations come free of charge, often anonymously, help preserve independence and the attraction of the party. I advise you to make a choice in favor of the second.

5. Find the heroes. They are everywhere. These are people known outside of your city. They need to be invited first. They have something to share and they are happy to do it (and some regularly). Be sure to listen to their opinion about what should be a party, without them it will be dull.

All people can be divided into two categories: who have already changed jobs and who will change jobs. For 6 years of experience, I changed jobs only once and want to share those thoughts that I had during this transition.

Why should a person change jobs? They pay more there (not everyone. - Hereinafter, KO notes ), there is no need to work on weekends (unless deadline, there are no critical bugs for users, etc., etc.), the boss is kind and understanding (on Sundays), it is easy to reach a manager there (five other new employees also said that). The decision to change jobs I took a long time. Probably half a year, and maybe even a whole year. Criteria POGD


So, the criteria POGD. The abbreviation appeared even before I came up with her mnemonic rule. POGD is easy to remember, because both externally and in meaning is similar to the word POGOD. So, what is the job at the new place of work?

P - Perspectives

Perspectives

In this part of the evaluation criterion, you can put all your expectations about possible studies that are waiting for you in a new place. The criterion is very broad. It accommodates both short-term and long-term perspectives, and this concerns all areas related to work. This is your professional growth, and career growth and the possibility of establishing new useful contacts, and the potential growth of your salary (not the amount itself, but the possibility of its gradual growth), and more and more interesting tasks, and more and more comfortable working conditions, growth of your credibility and many other improvements that are not expected immediately after the transition, but with time.

Attitude

We work as a team and therefore we have different relationships with many people. These are your colleagues, direct manager and management of the whole company. With all we can build different relationships that can be described as positive, neutral or negative. Do you want your colleagues to respect you? Or maybe you want them to be afraid of you? Try to decide for yourself which relationships are acceptable to you, which would be preferable, and which would be unacceptable. Things like complimentary coffee with buns, pool passes, social programs and flexible working hours are also relevant. Attitude to you by the company. Appreciate it.

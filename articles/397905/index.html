Thanks to rats a person can get rid of phobias



People who are afraid to fly an airplane or are afraid of spiders, so as not to risk in the basement, can try this type of treatment as exposure therapy. In a safe environment, people are repeatedly confronted with information such as photographs of airplanes or images of a black widow, with the help of this, their psyche is tempered, and they are already less responsive to their fears in real life.

Unfortunately, the effects of exposure to therapy are not long-term and many people experience a relapse. MIT scientists have found an opportunity to improve the long-term outcome in rats, and because of this, they offer a way to improve therapy for people suffering from phobias and more complex disorders, such as post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD).

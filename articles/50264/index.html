Course Library

Within the MSDN Academic Alliance program, there is a resource such as a library of training courses .



This site is addressed to teachers, graduate students, students and enthusiasts who teach and study modern information technologies. Here are posted training courses developed at universities and training centers that use Microsoft technology in their educational process. Currently, the site contains more than 100 courses, textbooks, books, laboratory works, most of which are written and developed by teachers of Russian universities .

The library contains materials in the following areas:
  • Basics of programming
  • Technology of developing software systems
  • High performance computing
  • Operating Systems
  • Programming languages
  • Web development
  • Object oriented programming
  • Information Security
  • Computer networks
  • Programming Theory
  • Information management
  • Computer graphics
  • Office programming
  • Functional programming
  • Automated programming
And
  • Training courses in the Partnership in Education series for secondary schools
  • Teaching aids for staff development courses
  • Laboratory works
  • Microsoft Open Lectures
  • Articles
The library is constantly updated. Last added materials:
  • Course IT Project Management S.V. Zherdev, UNN (see the section Technology of developing software systems)
    The purpose of the course is to examine the basics of various project management techniques, the specifics of project management in the field of information technology. The course is based on international project management guidelines (PMBOK, Project Management Body of Knowledge) of the Project Management Institute. The main objective of the course is to start students in the field of organizing the development of complex software systems, familiarizing them with modern approaches and international experience in this field.

  • Windows PowerShell book - Introduction to scripting language technology for users without basic knowledge of Frak Koch (see Computer Networks section).
    This book is an introduction to Windows PowerShell and contains practical examples that teach writing scripts that automate your daily tasks, even if you do not have serious experience in this field. The first book is designed for beginners. It has everything you need to learn more about the scenarios. After reading the book, you will learn how to use Powershell at full capacity.
  • The book Introduction to XNA by S. Gaidukov (see section Computer Graphics).
    The book is written for students and novice developers who want to use high-performance graphics in their projects. The book is intended for readers who are already familiar with the basics of C # and the .NET platform. The first chapter introduces the reader to the XNA Framework. The second chapter is devoted to the visualization of the basic primitives of the XNA Framework. The third chapter deals with more complex visualization issues: the use of full-screen mode, smooth animation of primitives and imitation of transparency. In the fourth chapter, all previously studied material is brought together by the example of creating a full-fledged screen saver with a distribution kit, which, if necessary, automatically installs the XNA Framework on the user's computer. The fifth and final chapter deals with the topic of a programmable graphic pipeline, but at a much deeper level; HLSL, the basics of assembly-like Vertex Shader 1.1 and the integrated development environment FX Composer 2.0, which makes development and debugging of shaders much easier, are considered.

MPAA launches global raid on pirated websites

Two weeks after the premiere of the blockbuster "The Dark Knight", which already came out on top in the list of the most popular films in cinema history, the US Cinematographic Association began a massive campaign against online pirates .

So far, the copyright guards have not reached the hands of the torrent trackers (apparently, it was left for a snack). The first addressees of the lawsuits were two sites: MovieRumor.com (monthly American audience: 120 thousand) and Free Online Movie DataBase (130 thousand). These sites allow you to view pirated movies directly online and get a commercial benefit through the display of advertising. And not only due to contextual advertising: sometimes the show is preceded by full-screen commercials. In this case, the fault is obvious, the trial in court will be very fast. These are the easiest victims, and therefore they were taken first.

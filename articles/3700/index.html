Geek wedding ring

The company Green Karat has come up with an interesting model of wedding rings, which should interest couples who, so to say, are very interested in computers. The main feature of such a ring is a memorable inscription made by a binary code.

On the outer side of the ring, the designers started up five parallel tracks, on each of which 20 stripes can be extruded or ... nothing 20 times extruded the same length as the strip. The bar indicates 1, and nothing - 0.

Zeros and ones standing in one column in all five strips one above the other form a binary number, from 1 to 31. This is enough to encode any letter of the Latin alphabet (and the Cyrillic alphabet, if you discard "" and ""). Thus, any message up to 20 characters long can be written on the ring.

To do this, they call on people to shake up their bins and recycle all old and valueless jewelry, dishes and other products made of precious metals.

A minor feature of CHAR and VARCHAR

Prehistory



There is a small server on which standard LAMP is spinning. It all started with the fact that QA approaches me and says: ‚ÄúThere is a topic, I need to double-check the registration of users, can you delete the old account?‚Äù, ‚ÄúNo question‚Äù - I answered. The point is, the entrance is made only through social networks. In order not to violate the integrity of the database by deleting the account, I decided to just take and rename the UID (user ID in a specific social network) in the table.
Since everyone's UID is different (vk, facebook, google ... - numeric UID, linkedin - string UID) was used by VARCHAR for storage. As a result, I added the underscore character `_` to the line, and with a calm soul unsubscribed:" Check ... ".





I was very surprised when I heard: ‚ÄúDid you just delete the account, otherwise my old one is displayed?‚Äù.
In the course of the mini investigation, a discrepancy was found.

$ where = 'providerUserId =?';


It turned out the addition of `_` - had no effect on the sample, since the UID was a number.

Dual-display laptop

Lenovo has released a new model of the ThinkPad W700ds laptop, equipped with two displays and a built-in Wacom pen tablet.


The manufacturer positions the novelty as a tool for designers and professionals involved in the processing of photo and video materials. The main 17.1-inch display ThinkPad W700ds has a resolution of 1920x1200 pixels. The auxiliary screen, designed, among other things, to accommodate toolbars (when working with various kinds of graphic editors, etc.) has a smaller size and resolution of 10.6 inches and 1280x768 pixels, respectively.

Notebook stuffing is not weak at all. Screw up the package can be up to the processor Intel Core 2 Quad Q9100 (2.26 GHz) or Intel Core 2 Extreme QX9300 (2.53 GHz), video NVIDIA Quadro FX 2700M or NVIDIA Quadro FX 3700M and 8 GB of RAM. Hard drive to match - up to 960 GB. But you can fork out on the SSD-drive.

All this versatility and good iron affects the size. The thickness of the ThinkPad W700ds is 50 mm. And it weighs as much as 5 kilograms. Especially from place to place do not drag.

On sale Lenovo ThinkPad W700ds should appear in January 2009. Cost in the initial configuration (Q9100, 4 GB of RAM, 160 GB HDD) machine will be from $ 3,600.

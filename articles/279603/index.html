ICQ Contest. The results of the competition for the redesign of the mobile application



In early December, we announced a mega-competition on the redesign of mobile ICQ . Every day he gained momentum, the work was getting more and more intensive, and by the end of February 21, we already had 88 works! We got some interesting interface and ideological solutions for video calling, and a bonus of curious parallels with trend services. There was also a fabulous madness with rainbow-colored unicorns in space, which we were looking forward to.

Task


The task of the competition was to make a video chat of dreams. It was proposed to shift the emphasis in the product interface towards video calls (one-on-one calls, video conferencing or online broadcasting), without ignoring the usual text communication. It is important that the user has the impression of simplicity and familiarity, the daily video chat.

purpose


Good things for people are created along with people. ICQ is developed by users and changes with them. We wanted to supplement our thoughts on the development of the product with an outside view, to hear fresh thoughts and ideas from both users who decided to change something directly and from people not involved in the product.

Video calls have long been one of our key features, and we continue to think that something else that is useful and interesting can be offered. We, of course, have a lot of our own solutions to the tasks of the competition, but we will keep them secret and open them in the next big updates.

Jury


Evaluated the work of designers and managers of ICQ. Pavel Loutsker and Konstantin Rubtsov (product managers). Kolya Kuchkarov (designer) put a lot of minuses, Joe McGreen (designer) spoke about logos and walked on the trolls, and I, Alexander Smetanka (product designer), took over UI / UX and other things. The organizers of the contest Vera Bondareva (designer) and Dima Pleshakov (product manager) helped us in the assessment.

Works


Of the 88 designers, about half got into the task. Nontrivial solutions offered about 20 participants. The most daring ideas in one form or another are found in other services, but there were also fresh finds that pleases.

Of the minuses: sometimes it is not entirely clear in the concepts that are interesting in appearance what the elements on the screen mean and, unfortunately, this is not explained in any way. Of course, there were those who copied the current design by changing colors and icons.

As a result, we analyzed concepts for individual interesting ideas, trends in video communication, interface techniques, evaluated the approach to the problem and the presentation.

Winners


We carefully reviewed all the work, and then again, each member of the jury had to rate it given the ingress in the task, the thoughtfulness of the interface and aesthetics.

Favorites identified unequivocally. We didn‚Äôt just give up and more than once questioned their decisions within the team, but they held out.

1st place: Denis Shoomov



Very large-scale and qualitatively approached the matter and did a great job.

The task of making an instant messenger with a bias in the video is brilliant. Denis has come up with many interesting formats in which one way or another video messages, streaming, video calls and the only one that made a unique design appear. We were pleased with numerous interface solutions such as rethought input in the chat, seamless video calls, cool icons, unusual list patterns. The main thing is that the concept is organically entered into the format of a classic messenger. At the exit, we get a middle ground - this is not the space in which to understand and not a dull sample application.

Special attention deserves a luxurious website presentation . He carried all the ideas, laid out on the shelves, accompanied by wonderful animations, icons, gifs. The fire!

Minuses

We decided not only to praise the winners, but also to criticize a little, they deserved.

There are cavils and shortcomings. Brave design is definitely beautiful. But it is important not to overdo it with courage. Retrogrades can call the application too fun and windy, but it can simply scare someone off. You can find fault with the metaphors of some icons - you need to seriously work on them. There were no particular problems, but sometimes I had to figure out what this might mean. The most surprising flaw is the complexity of sending a fragment. A quick start of shooting and a catastrophic process with several iterations before the video message in the chat. Percentage ‚Äúloading‚Äù negated all efforts. For everyday function, it's a failure.

2nd place: Danial Siddiki



Detailed and fully functional concept. The moments, the way they were created and viewed, the broadcasts and the emotions in the calls all right now can be quite simply embedded in the current client. I liked the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãpetals-entertainment on the logo.

Minuses

The interface is designed in a too classic style, as if it was a test task, and not a competition to create a concept.
Complication in the way of simply creating a moment in the form of a sentence to enter its name. The logo during the call is completely wild and incomprehensible in functionality thing.

3rd place: Misha Bilenko



Misha took one important problem, realized it very correctly, described it and perfectly solved it. Erased the border and made a seamless video chat. It can be seen that first of all I was thinking not about choosing the color of icons, but about the practice of communicating in a chat.

Minuses

You can find fault with the questionable location of the controller, including video in the chat. The disappearance of a part of messages during a call is quite sudden and discouraging.

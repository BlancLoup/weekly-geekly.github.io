The dependence of the salary was built at the peak of distribution on the number of answers. The survey results were compared with the results of a similar survey of April 2017 . The survey results were compared with Rosstat data (the most common salary was 21 thousand) and Navalny data (the most common salary was 12.5 thousand) and it was concluded that the differences are not super-large (all salary intervals are approximated by mid-point intervals).



The results for the answer "150 thousand rubles or more" were excluded, because, firstly, residents of Russian cities, in which the most common salary is above 150 thousand rubles, are negligible; secondly, there was no option for the answer "get a score and see the results of the survey", so many chose a deliberately unbelievable answer "150 thousand or higher" to answer at least something and see the results.

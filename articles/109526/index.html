A year ago, we talked about the educational project UniverTV , in which we collected more than 4,500 videos in two dozen disciplines - from astronomy to jurisprudence.

Why do we do it


For those who do not know, briefly tell about what InternetUrok.ru. This is a collection of video lessons on the main subjects of the school curriculum - constantly updated, in the public domain and without advertising.


Not every school has teachers who are able to talk about their subject in an accessible and interesting way. Some children study at home, some skip school because of illness, and there are those who cannot attend school at all. Do not forget about the lagging students who need help (while not everyone has the opportunity to take a tutor).

In addition, children study in the former Soviet republics, for whom Russian is their native language, but there are problems with teaching in Russian.

What's the Difference


UniverTV began as a compilation of the best educational videos from around the world. We searched, watched, selected lectures and speeches at conferences. Some of them we translated, then began to attract teachers from Russian educational institutions to work on the project. From individual lectures, we gradually move to the whole course.

With the InternetUrok.ru resource, things are somewhat different. Instead of a selection of individual videos in the directions, we began to move purposefully through the school curriculum. In fact, the whole project is one big school course.

Materials are systematized not only in subjects, but also in classes of the school program.
On the content of any lesson, you can ask questions, you can discuss the material covered.

Taking into account the wishes and comments


Our visitors, including Habr's users, noticed that not all lessons were of good quality. Still, not every teacher, even a good lesson in the classroom, continues to work just as well on camera, so we decided to retake many lessons.

How is everything done


It all starts with a lesson script. The teacher, together with our curator, works out the script, determines which illustrative and additional material to use, what to focus on.



The director tells the teacher how best to stay in front of the camera, how to gesticulate, where to put your hands, how to speak.



We take lessons at two sites. One is equipped in Moscow, the other in St. Petersburg, based on Lennauchfilm.

The studio has two cameras that allow you to vary the picture, periodically changing angles. The sound is written through the microphone-buttonhole.



Then we assemble the lesson, our designers draw graphics to it, add interactive features and lay out on dedicated servers for this purpose, one of which works for Russia, the other for Europe.

What's in the plans


To consolidate the material covered, we plan to add quizzes, tests and exercises.

In addition, we plan to make fuller use of the capabilities of the Internet and multimedia technologies: supply the lesson with additional materials, slides, and also make the lessons non-linear, so that the student can choose the sequence of presentation of the material, use interactive footnotes, etc. Already, you can see the lesson with the accompanying slide show , as well as an example of a non-linear lesson .

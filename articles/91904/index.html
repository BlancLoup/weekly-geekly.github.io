Models of reality and their role in people's lives

There is a model of the world, and there is a real world.
As in physics, there is some phenomenon, but there are its models.

Over time, the models are refined - we had linear additions of speeds, then it became obvious - formulas do not work at high speeds; the theory was clarified - and the transformations became nonlinearly dependent on speeds, the speed of light entered there.

All this happens because physicists regularly check the validity of their models on practical experiments. Moreover, trying not to introduce flaws with their measurements and the conditions of the experiment themselves - there are even entire sections of the science on this, for example, the theory of experiment.

However, somewhere, 95% of people do not know that in their heads the brain is doing the same thing, only the first years of life. He studies and models the real world, laying down for himself models of phenomena, people's characters, abstract concepts, and so on, which a person then uses in his mind as a tool, perceiving the model as a reality. Further, all his life, man does this, making up more and more complex models.

Here are just three differences from physicists here. The first is the ignorance of the nature of the brain. And hence the consequent conviction that everything that a person sees, thinks and knows is the truth.

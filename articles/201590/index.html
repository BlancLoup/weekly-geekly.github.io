The tragedy of communities in the real world or coupon fever-2

Do you know what tragedy communities are? This is a term from the theory of games, a situation where participants in the game are overly consuming any resource, and harm themselves and others. But at the same time, a single participant, without consuming the resource, will not receive any gain at all, while the resource consumption will not practically decrease - the other consumers will not change their behavior. Nothing like?

Of course, the recent hype with coupons for ebay.


For a start, remember what the tragedy of communities is. You can see, for example, in the wiki . And you can not watch.

The tragedy of the village New Fangs Samara region

The village itself lives, breeds goats, sheep, cows, and earns those. Milk is sold, wool knitted socks, and sold in the nearest town. They graze animals on a nearby pasture, a big meadow like this, where there is enough grass for everyone. Enough so that every inhabitant, when the number of his animals increases, does not even think about its restriction. What for? There is enough grass, while one half of the meadow is eaten, the second has time to grow. And a man has an additional income: two cows instead of one - this is twice as much milk. And there will be no harm - there are 100 houses in the village, 100 cows, and even if one person has 5 cows instead of one, this is only an increase of 4%. And the grass is enough for 200 cows. So, for each individual person the best strategy is to increase the number of animals. So what? There is enough resource.

But time passes, the number of animals grows, and the meadows have already become scarce - the grass is eaten faster than it grows. The fact is that a winning strategy for one person is not an obligatory winning strategy for the whole village. Increasing the livestock population of one person from 1 to 5 is a small load for the meadow. But on the scale of the whole village, this means an increase in the number of mouths from 100 to 500, and this amount is no longer able to feed the meadows. Cows come home thin, and milk from 5 cows - as from two. And it is necessary to milk and care for five at once. Thus, 5 cows, taking into account the overhead, bring less profit than 2. The strategy of increasing the population throughout the village turned out to be wrong. The strategy, winning for each person individually, being applied by all, means the loss of all participants.

At the same time, the heroic impulse of one person, who decided to slaughter 4 cows for meat, in the hope that this would allow him to reduce costs, but to increase the amount of milk from one cow was overlooked. 4 cows across the village is less than a percent. In addition, this percentage is distributed among all residents. Thus, in this case, all the cows in the village receive an increase of 0.8% in the amount of milk :) The heroic man who did not read Wikipedia now has one cow who brings as much milk as before. More precisely, the same amount as before, plus 0.8%. And a lot of meat.
The decision of one player to reduce the consumption of a public resource does not affect (or only slightly) the situation as a whole. However, it reduces the profit of this particular player.

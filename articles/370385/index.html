China in 3D - 3D printers are made here.



At the end of March, we went to China, pursuing two main goals: visiting our main suppliers of 3D equipment, concentrated in Shanghai and its suburbs, as well as exploring new directions on CNC machine tools and searching for direct manufacturers-suppliers at CHCMT 2017.
From this post you will learn about the first part of the trip - a visit to our suppliers of 3D equipment.

After one day of adaptation in a fairly comfortable and modern metropolis, which Shanghai has now become, we went to the office of RAISE 3D. Their office is located in a new respectable array of buildings, not far from the center, but in this area there is already something imperceptibly resembling an industrial zone.

RAISE 3D

After meeting with the sales director and completing the mutual presentation of our companies, we learned that RAISE is experiencing an explosive growth in sales - production is struggling to cope with shipments of more than 500 vehicles per month. Then we were invited to the showroom, where we were met by a solid cosmonaut more than two meters high, as if hinting at the fact that large-format and stable printing is the visiting card of RAISE printers.



Here we got to know the 2-extruder modification of the N2 Plus .



Two-material printing has small patches, but for a simple design with two extruders, the result is not bad.



The question of the feasibility of FDM printing with a layer thickness of 50 microns and less, the time spent on it and the quality obtained has always been controversial. However, all the printouts presented had a high quality and smooth surface.













We concluded that RAISE 3D printers have a sufficient list of properties that are attractive to a wide range of users:

  • Huge print area: up to 305 x 305 x 610 mm
  • High print quality, with a layer thickness of up to 10 microns
  • The ability to print two materials
  • High-quality assembly and convenient touch display
  • Very affordable price.

Next we went to the Mankati office located already away from the center of Shanghai. We do not often sell their printers, but the announcement of the new model and the location convenient for us clearly determined that it was worth a look.

Mankati

It was difficult to assess the company itself, which, as it turned out, is in the process of moving - in most rooms there is a corresponding mess. In the room where we were invited, there was an assembly shop where two workers were engaged in assembling a new model E180 :







The main advantages of the Mankati E180 listed on their website are:

  • Connect using Ethernet and WiFi
  • Air filter to filter the air from the chamber (this is very important in China)
  • Camera to monitor the printing process
  • Material feed sensor, for the possibility of refueling the bar
  • High temperature hot end, table and camera: 350 ¬∞ C, 110 ¬∞ C and 60 ¬∞ C for work with engineering materials
  • High print quality
  • Rail guides

We were very interested in the possibility of printing with various materials, including PEEK, but the representative of Mankati, referring to the high cost of the material itself, could not show us any samples.

The small screen, which has an inconvenient location, with even smaller letters on it, a push-button control on the side, and also a USB port, which is generally located on the rear panel, was very much alarmed. Our engineers would obviously not be delighted with this solution, as they have to repeatedly to print new files during the working day. Perhaps in this way they encourage users to use Wi-Fi more often?





The next day we had to go to the nearby city of Hangzhou, which is 200 km from Shanghai. We easily covered this distance on one of the high-speed trains, which can reach speeds of up to 350 km / h, whose network of lines is longer than similar ones in all of Europe and Japan combined.

Shining 3d

In Hangzhou, at the train station, we were met by Kirill Xia, the manager of Shining 3D responsible for sales in Russia. Together with him we went to their office, which is a large separate building. We did not rent the building from the outside, but, so that you could imagine the scale, we offer several photographs of the reception, as well as recreation rooms with a volleyball court and a huge terrace:











The building also has a lot of spacious showrooms, separate rooms for developers of various equipment, sales departments and working areas of other employees.
After a hearty lunch from corporate chefs, we went on a tour of the office.



At first, we were shown classes in which they conduct training seminars and practical exercises for schoolchildren - this is how the company contributes to modern education and promotes 3D technology in China.









In the classes there are a lot of beautiful figures of various superheroes and animals. I would like to catch the company on the use of printers from other manufacturers, but all the models were glued together from parts not exceeding the dimensions of the working chamber of their small Einstart-S .

A huge number of printers on large shelves and two engineers engaged in post-processing and removal of supports. Of course, we are convinced that such a number of printers cannot efficiently serve two people, but they have definitely not heard about the problems with the print queue.

After undergraduate classes, we went to the medical showroom, which presents Shining 3D's capabilities in this area, such as SLA-printing of dental models and articulators, biocompatible surgical patterns (materials from Somos and NextDent ), printing metal crowns and surgical prostheses .









All this demonstrates the capabilities of a wide range of their professional equipment. Among the samples there are Stratasys marketing models - it turned out that Shining 3D is their official dealer in China. This is quite logical, because the company does not manufacture inkjet printers, similar to PolyJet or MJM, but is well versed in the sales and commissioning of such machines.

A subsidiary of the company is developing a bioprinter, which is sold only in the domestic market, at an estimated price of $ 70,000





In the next room is located the OptimScan scanner (the Chinese name is OKIO).



The integration of the scanner with a robotic arm from the company FANUC allows for automated metrological control of production and significantly speeds up the scanning process for identical parts. To make sure that this is not just a decorative stand, we asked for a presentation and after 15-20 minutes of settings, we saw the system in operation.



In the same hall, related to industrial production, there are examples of printing industrial 3D-printers of the following technologies: SLA, SLS-Nylon, SLS-WAX / Sand and SLM.
The surface quality of the models, together with their large size, is very impressive. Shining3D is engaged in the provision of 3D printing services in China. The choice of demonstration samples indicates an excellent understanding of the production tasks that their equipment is designed to solve at various enterprises.

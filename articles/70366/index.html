Opera as an interface


In fact, at first I wanted to write something completely different, but in the process of preparing the material, in my opinion, a more interesting thing emerged and I decided to focus on it. Namely - I wanted to talk about how you can use Opera as an interface. But first things first.

For a start, a little explanation. If someone has already thought of something there, then he was mistaken: I will not say that Opera browser can replace the interface of the operating system. Perhaps - in the future, but not today. We have not yet been able to completely abandon the stationary programs (or, at least, from the client halves of the web-dance services). But to a certain extent, the goal is already quite achievable - I hint at the actual work on the Internet. Many of us, due to certain circumstances, spend an order of time more on the network, devoting to work offline only the necessary minimum (such as a fleet day for garbage collection or updating system components). In other words - there are many users for whom the operating system goes into the background, skipping ahead His Majesty Browser.

But until recently, the illusion was incomplete - such things as panels, menus, and window decorations reminded about the system environment proper. True, there was a way to get rid of these "treacherous" elements at least temporarily, going into full-screen mode. But here two circumstances arose: firstly, not all browsers provided full-fledged tools for working with the browser in full-screen mode, which forced from time to time to switch to the standard view, and secondly, even though Opera browser had for a long time sufficient means for a similar style of work, something was missing in full-screen mode, there was a certain discomfort. And today, I think, I understood why.

I do not know how it was with you, but it was disheartening to me that with fully closed pages I had to, at best, see the home page, and at worst - just a white rectangle of the display. In the first case, a quiet hatred for the most "native" page in the network gradually arose, and in the second, a complex was developed - a sort of computer "agoraphobia" , the fear of empty display space. But it seems that all problems are in the past - Opera 10 allows you to get rid of both inconveniences. Let's start with a phobia.

To solve this problem, it was necessary to simply fill the empty space with some content, which also had to carry some functionality. Well, please: we put a background image that is pleasing to the eye, and in the cells of the Express panel we fill in what we would like to have access in one click. Already beautiful. And now replace (where possible) the thumbnails of the pages with beautiful logos. What is the result? But in the end we see ... a desktop with applications icons placed on it.

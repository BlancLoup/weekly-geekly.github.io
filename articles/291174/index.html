A look at startups from the back. Part 1

Good day, ladies and gentlemen!

The topic of startups lately, and not only lately, is very popular. That startup successfully sold, this startup received funding, there came up with a worthwhile idea, and here another idea is already being implemented on all pairs. And so on and so forth.

How do I work with them?

I hold the position of head of a team of mechanical engineers in one of the largest contract manufacturers of electronics in German-speaking countries. We have several factories in different countries. One of the factories is located in China. It is here, in the south of China, that I work.
The interaction lies in the fact that I either directly put startups on mass production, or independently develop mechanical components, or components are developed under my strict guidance. And sometimes I also have to dig out what's left of dead startups.

Below are the details about some of the startups to which I was more or less related.

Startup 1. Microclimate system
The current status is weakly moving

Plans were like Napoleon, for 2 years to recapture 15 million investments, to make many more from the top, to buy everything for a new car and more so that the ice cream remained. Not grown together.

Specifically, we made a humidifier for this system. It was necessary to produce a control board and a certain amount of mechanical parts. Production methods - plastic injection molding, stamping in progressive dies and stamping in a simple stamp.
The idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe device, in my opinion, is rather strange. There is a device that automatically maintains a certain level of humidity in the room. There is nothing strange at once, but there are such devices on the market, and much cheaper. Perhaps a competitive advantage was considered water filtration and specials. plastic additives that prevent mold.

With this special. the addition was a separate epic. The additive is produced in Europe (price of 1 euro per 1 gram, by the way) for importation into China must be officially imported. To import, you must provide chemical. composition to regulatory bodies. Manufacturer to provide chemical. The composition refused, they say, a trade secret. As a result, we could not import it. It is clear that you can bring in Hong Kong to black, but we do not do that. As a result, I found an alternative from Japan, which is worse, but is available in China.

Testing has shown that the air flow inside the device is not sufficient, as a result, water condenses where it is not necessary and in the sum everything does not work.

Some design decisions were quite controversial:
1. An adapter molded from TPU, later replaced with TPV, was used to connect two plastic cases. Why it was necessary to make a fuss instead of a usual rubber pipe for 5 kopecks per meter and two penny clamps ‚Äî I still don't understand. Someone, apparently, lazhanulsya when designing.

3. And all the other little things.

Brief conclusion:
1. The technical solution is not well thought out and over complicated compared to peers;
2. The investment has not paid off. At least for now, after 3 years;
3. It seems like the company is still alive, but there are not many signs of life.

Startup 2. Automatic watering plants
The current status is bent
There is a sensor that is stuck in a pot with a plant that measures the humidity of the earth and, if it is dry, includes drip irrigation.
Did not take off. There was no demand for this. It is probably easier for citizens in the United States to take a watering can and water a drooping flower. The startup site, by the way, was alive for another 4 years after the death of the company, and now the domain is already being sold.
Brief conclusion:
1. The idea is good, but not all;
2. Still, you need to check whether there is demand, and then bravely conquer the market.

The current status is running away.

For their smart home system, I made enclosures for 3 devices.

Ideas, ideas, ideas. They changed everything except the timing functions, electronics and mechanics. Alterations in non-stop mode.

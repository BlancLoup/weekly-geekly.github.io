Westone Company History

Hi, Habr!

After I published Nintendo History and Samsung Electronics History, I decided to continue this tradition. At this time, the choice fell on the company Westone, a little less known in Russia. But it is known in the USA: for example, the headphones of this company are used by the armed forces of this country. In addition, the company produces sound equipment for medical purposes - hearing aids, etc., as well as monitors for musicians, headphones for military and athletes.



Prehistory


In 1959, the company Westone first in the world began to produce individual headphones for intra-ear imprint, ideally suited to the ear. It was at first a modest family business in Colorado. In the photo - inside the form there is an earpiece made by the impression.



At the moment, the company produces equipment, instruments and accessories for doctors and people with hearing problems.



In order to make an individual earpiece, a special material is used. It is poured into the ear, after which you can work with the resulting form.



Now the company produces everything that is possible, for the treatment, diagnosis of hearing. These are various hearing aids, instruments for doctors, models for installation in clinics, and so on.

Monitors for musicians


The main thing for Westone is hearing protection. Who needs it: musicians, pilots, motorcyclists, cyclists, call center operators. It all began in 1987, when Etymotic offered Westone to jointly develop headphones for musicians. The goal was to create headphones that protect hearing. That is, at least they will not harm one of the most important organs for a musician. The production of Etymotic headphones has begun. Westone was responsible for developing comfortable soft tips.

In 1990, there was an important step in the production of headphones for musicians: Westone was the first to use rebar drivers in monitors. The feature of headphones with a balanced anchor is that the effect on the radiating membrane occurs with the help of a movable anchor.



In 1995, Alex Van Halen had a problem: he did not hear over the drum set that other members of his group sang and played. Louder monitors did not help. Jerry Harvey, the sound engineer of the group, found a solution: he assembled two-driver headphones capable of blocking noise and producing sufficiently high-quality sound. At first, other members of the group became interested in developing, then Skid Row joined, while touring with Van Halen - they sold six pairs for three thousand bucks. After pop singer Engelbert Humperdink also wanted one, Harvey decided to start Ultimate Ears. And the company Westone helped to refine these headphones and took up their production. In 1998, among the clients of the UE were The Rolling Stones, Enrique Iglesias and Red Hot Chili Peppers.





In 1996, together with the Shure company, universal intra-channel monitors were developed, to which a set of nozzles went. Not as convenient as on the cast, but you can choose the best option. These are E5 dual-drive monitors, released in 1999. The landing is again versatile.



Now the company produces five product lines - headphones and monitors for musicians. The first is the Universal Series monitors. It includes models UM Pro 10, UM Pro 20, UM Pro 30, UM Pro 50. In the photo - UM Pro 10, reinforcement headphones with one radiator.



UM Pro 50 - reinforcement headphones with five emitters.



The next line is the W Series. In the photo - W50, with five emitters.



At CES 2014, the W60 was introduced - these headphones each have two emitters for low, mid and high frequencies. One of the advantages of the entire series is a detachable wire with a coaxial connector. This wire is with the headset. Convenient, of course.



Elite Series monitors are made on the ear of a musician.

Headphones


This part is more interesting for the average user. And for an audiophile.

Remember the walkman? In 1985, Westone developed headphone jacks for this player. First, the headphones have become damn comfortable, unlike the competitors currently available. Secondly, the low frequencies in them sounded much better.



In 2002, reinforcement headphones with a single emitter UM1 were released. This is the first headphones under the brand Westone, which were produced for mere mortals.



Westone 3 headphones - rebar with three emitters, designed for the consumer market. They were released in 2008. A year later, Westone 1 and 2 appeared for the same purpose.



In 2012, the 4R line is on sale.





At CES 2014, the W Series headphones mentioned in the product section for musicians were presented. They will suit ordinary users. For example, for a W60 with six emitters, you can also buy a wire with a headset so that you can use them every day with a smartphone.

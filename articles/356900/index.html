Anonymous group threatens to publish data of more than a thousand KKK members on November 5



Radical Internet activists from the Anonymous group reported that on November 5, they would disclose personal data to about a thousand members of the far-right Ku Klux Klan and its sympathizers.

Last week, someone, on behalf of Anonymous, posted a list of people associated with the KKK, however, the group denied any association with this list and promised to publish its own. Judging by the records in various Twitter accounts belonging to the group, one of the attack vectors will be sent to the Twitter accounts of members of the Ku Klux Klan.



In their appeal, the members of the group indicate their own position regarding the very fact of the existence of the KKK and why they will fight this racist organization:
Racism was created as a human rights abus. #OpKKK #HoodsOff

Racism was created to promote and justify the infringement of human rights in the past.

Join # opKKK in the fight against racial terror.

Join the ranks of # opKKK to fight against racist terror.

Sometimes we are deep inside.

At times, we need to leave the racial strife behind us and remember that deep down we are all human beings.

Brace yourselves KKK, Anonymous is coming.

Be brave, KKK, Anonymous is coming.

In the US, many prominent political and public figures who hold republican views are suspected of having links with the KKK. It is their exposure and, accordingly, the total weakening of the position of the Ku Klux Klan, or its complete destruction as such, that is at the forefront of the entire operation Anonymous.

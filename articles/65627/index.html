There are unmanned aerial vehicles:

* unmanned unmanaged
* unmanned automatic
* unmanned remotely piloted aircraft (RPV)

Unmanned aerial vehicles can be divided according to such interrelated parameters as mass, time, range and altitude. There are the following classes of devices:

* "Micro" (conditional name) weighing up to 10 kilograms, flight time about 1 hour and altitude up to 1 kilometer,
* "Mini" - weighing up to 50 kilograms, with a flight time of several hours and a height of up to 3 - 5 kilometers,
* medium (midi) - up to 1,000 kilograms, a time of 10-12 hours and an altitude of 9-10 kilometers,
* heavy - with flight altitudes up to 20 kilometers and flight time 24 hours or more.

To determine the coordinates and ground speed, modern UAVs usually use satellite navigation receivers (GPS or GLONASS). Orientation and overload angles are determined using gyroscopes and accelerometers. Software is usually written in high-level languages, such as C, C ++, Modula-2, Oberon SA or Ada95. As hardware, as a rule, specialized computers based on digital signal processors or PC / 104, MicroPC computers are used. Real-time operating systems such as QNX, VME, VxWorks, XOberon can also be used.

The X-45A took off from Edwards (California) air force base and landed at NASA research center Dryden. Built two models of X-45A. The last flights of these vehicles were carried out in 2005, after which the UAVs were transferred to museums.

The Pentagon plans to use systems created on the basis of the X-45 to solve two problems:

* suppression of the air defense system (as they say in the United States, the task of the first day of the war);

In both cases, it is planned to use drones for immediate action on remote theaters (the predetermined system deployment time at any airfield of the planet is 32 hours, readiness for take-off after the transfer is 75 minutes). Developed in two major versions of the X-45C and X-47B.

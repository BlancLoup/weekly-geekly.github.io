Theory: zoom lens


A lens with a variable focal length, undoubtedly, is the most convenient and versatile solution for a camera: you can vary both the viewing angle and the approximation of the imaged object. This is achieved quite simple way, which is shown in the picture below:



One of the lenses (in modern solutions is a whole group of elements) shifts relative to the fixed parts of the lens, changing the focal length (and the field of view, respectively, too). You can read about history and general design features on Wikipedia , well, quite specific glasses are waiting for us.

I want to remind you that NX lenses have several abbreviations that show the use of a particular technology in a particular model.

ED (Extra-Low Dispersion) - A lens that contains one (or several) lenses made from materials with ultra-low light dispersion. Reduces the number of chromatic aberrations.

SSA (Super Sonic Actuator) - The lens is equipped with a silent autofocus motor based on an ultrasonic drive. Allows autofocus system to work faster and more accurately.

OIS (Optical Image Stabilization) - Optical Stabilization System. Objective lenses have a special ‚Äúgimbal‚Äù that is controlled by electronics and reduces the effect of shaking on frame quality. Allows you to shoot at a slower shutter speed and / or in difficult conditions.

I, II, III, IV (1, 2, 3, 4) - Roman numerals denote the generation of a subsystem (more is better) or the generation of the lens itself.

Marking the focal length and aperture, I hope, is clear to everyone.

Practice: zoom lenses

And it is not always possible to change the lens: dust, rain, traffic, shaking - the conditions that impede the process are a million. A good zoom lens is a versatile and very convenient device that allows you to get great shots in a variety of conditions. It used to have problems with the calculation of complex systems - now we have powerful computers, a ready base of hundreds of solutions, proven materials, opportunities that we simply did not have 20-30 years ago and the main thing is the desire to make a convenient and versatile lens. Of course, 18-500 F / 1.2 with a stabilizer at 4 EV-stop and completely from ED elements remains a solution on the verge of science fiction, but we have more realistic lenses - let's talk about them.

Distortion, in general, is typical for lenses of such a construct: here it reaches -4.3% at the ‚Äúshort‚Äù end and smoothly reaches 1.3% at 200mm.

Vignetting, respectively, also "floats" depending on the focal length. Darkening of the image at the corners of the frame at the short end is ~ 1 EV, at the long end - 0.5 EV. The indicator is acceptable, and the vignetting correction is not such a big problem: often the camera knows which lens is hooked to it, and either corrects everything itself, referring to the data in the firmware, or writes information to the SRW file so that you can apply autocorrection in a graphic editor.

The resolution of the lens, like all other characteristics, is strongly dependent on the focal length.

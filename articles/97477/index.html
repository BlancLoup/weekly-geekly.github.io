Microsoft's Future Home Tour

The model of the future house from Microsoft looks like science fiction, but interactive bedrooms, trays that charge mobile phones, sensors that notify you when plants need water and kitchen tables, from the surface of which you can read your recipes can be closer to our real world, in which Society and technology are moving forward at the speed of lightning.

The entrance to the future is here.


This is the entrance to the model of the house of the future, which is located in the briefing center of Microsoft in Redmond. Doors open up Microsoft‚Äôs vision of how our modest homes might look in the not-so-distant future, where natural user interfaces on walls and countertops are used with technologies such as sensors, LEDs, gestures, multitouch, voice control and 3D and define our lifestyle.



Decorative smart tray


In the front door, a decorative tray charges devices like smartphones and watches and displays information such as time, temperature, and battery charge. But still deeper. In the picture, the smart watches were put in the tray in order to upload information about the latest work, automatically synchronize the pulse values, distance traveled, calories burned and information from other devices that you may have with your personal account at Microsoft HealthVault.



Recipes on the table


Using sonor technology, the kitchen table can show you a list of required purchases, suggest recipes based on the ingredients available in the refrigerator, and then step by step to voice the necessary actions. The picture shows the proposal for the preparation of focaccia bread displayed on the table.



Personal Kitchen Display


This multitouch display in the kitchen uses programs and services to provide personal information such as a family calendar, messages, and event reminders.



Health on the screen


The kitchen display keeps track of personalized health information, just like a tray in the front door, but with much more information on the big screen. This system offers a visualization of your medical history, potential effects from the resulting diseases and advice on finding medical professionals.



Travel everywhere from your room


In the entertainment room of the future Microsoft home, you can virtually visit art exhibitions or retail stores. The surface of the coffee-like table creates a feeling of living environment on the screen through detailed, visible in all 360 degrees, images of goods. You can communicate with the store employee, view and select the product as if you are actually in the store.



Dining room comes to life


In the dining room the table is turned into entertainment for children. Guests can adjust the space for themselves, and virtual airplanes can fly over the surface using gestures.



Writing on the wall in the dining room


Wall-mounted screens in the dining room use cloud services to think through a healthy way of eating and dieting, allowing you to track your diet.



Bedroom for fantasy


In the bedroom of the future teenager, each surface is interactive. By integrating social and entertainment services and connections to the walls through interactive surfaces of displays and touch technologies, room design can match any mood of a teenager.



Family screen


The main family screen offers personalized digital content, access to other people via instant messengers, access to entertainment, information and the internal environment of the house (lights in the rooms, air conditioning, alarm). Content is personalized individually and offers everyone tips on his favorite media.

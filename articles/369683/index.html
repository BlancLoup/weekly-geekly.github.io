Lock on Habrahabr or Geektimes

Hello Geektimes, his readers and writers!

I want to share with you information about locks on resources. Rules are created to comply with them. Why? Information censorship is a kind of analogue of the body‚Äôs immunity. What will happen if any nastiness constantly gets into our bodies? Surely nothing good. Therefore, in the body there is a developed system of protection of its performance. The same applies to any information system created for some purpose. With centralized management, the definition of rules and control over their compliance is carried out exclusively by the creator / owner of the system. The decentralized model implies the creation of closed self-regulation circuits, which ensure the maintenance of the system within predetermined limits. Our habr is a typical such system in which users can regulate the appearance of content within fairly wide limits. The administration of the resource initially defined the purpose and rules of the entire system and delegated some of the functions to users, reducing its managerial influence.

So, my entry was transferred to the "Read-Only" mode. The name speaks for itself. What does this mean in practice?

  • Only viewing content. It becomes impossible to comment, vote for articles, comments, raise or lower karma.
  • The article due to which problems arose from the site. Other articles with similar or other violations may also be hidden. Articles that do not contain a violation - do not hide. Published comments on the articles are not hidden.
  • In the profile sections are available Whois and favorites. Both selected articles and selected comments are available in favorites. Articles and comments can be added / removed from favorites.
  • Dialogues do not disappear. You can create conversations with users. You can subscribe to other users.
  • My tape - remains.
  • Publish - just like when writing the first post - begins with "Just a minute attention." It also redirects from "My Publications".


Blocking does not mean the removal of user materials. In the case of a temporary lock, the materials are simply hidden. What happens in the case of "unlimited" blocking is unknown.

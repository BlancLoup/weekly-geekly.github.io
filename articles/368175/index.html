PHAB Plus at IFA 2015: A First Look at Lenovo's First Phablet

The first phablet by Lenovo, PHAB Plus, debuted at IFA 2015. This is a hybrid of a tablet and a smartphone, focused primarily on mobile entertainment and social networking. The gadget, of course, is not massive: the devices of the PHAB line (yes, this will be a separate product series) are designed for those users who want to benefit from two types of gadgets. Our internal analytics testify in favor of the fact that they are fully capable of becoming very popular. First of all, in the home market of Lenovo, in China, where PHAB Plus will debut soon. We will look at sales results, evaluate them and make a decision on deliveries to Russia. In the meantime, some details about the new tablet.



Lenovo PHAB Plus - the device is large, but still not so much that it does not fit, say, in a jeans pocket. The screen diagonal of the device is 6.8 inches, which dictates the overall dimensions of the device. The height of the gadget is 186.6 mm and the width is 96.6 with a thickness of 7.6 mm. As a result, it is quite convenient to hold it in one hand, but, of course, it will be easier to interact with applications supporting the transition to the horizontal mode, if you hold the device on both sides and press on the touch screen with two thumbs.



Although the option of reducing the working area and shifting it to any angle, which is quite traditional for phablets, is already provided - this way you can type messages with one hand on the go.





The PHAB Plus case is almost completely metal, only behind there is a small plastic insert, under which is quite a powerful speaker by the standards of smartphones and tablets. The new Lenovo phablet is not a gadget for audiophiles, but it still surprises with something. For example, with the built-in Smart Amplifier amplifier and the ability to transmit virtual surround sound.



The arrangement of the functional elements is quite traditional. Top connector for headphones.



Right metal volume buttons and the power button. Just like in the Lenovo Vibe S1, about which we recently wrote .

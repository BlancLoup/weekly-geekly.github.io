3 why do slides?
Slides either accompany the presentation, or are distributed to the audience and are published on the Internet, so that the audience can refresh the memory of the presentation. In the first case, the slides should be in large print (30 points), with pictures, with a small amount of text. In the second, they play the role of the notes, and therefore must be readable and well structured.

what to do in conflict situations?


Once I spent a little more time than I should have spent explaining why, from my point of view, most tasks on the Web are better solved using UNIX than Windows. There were violent followers of Windows in the audience, engaging me in a discussion about why this system is worse than UNIX. They accused me of incompetence, asserting that this and that works fine under Windows (after all, you can almost always find a special case that refutes any point of view, right?).
In that case it was my miscalculation - I underestimated the level of knowledge of a part of the audience and my arguments were intended for less experienced listeners, while the more experienced ones had to be persuaded with much more technical examples from my experience, and this already looked like an excuse: I had to prove that I am not a camel.

How to avoid such a situation? Less distractions from the main topic, less reason for outsiders to discuss, prepare iron arguments in favor of their point of view in advance, and such that they are understood by everyone in the audience.

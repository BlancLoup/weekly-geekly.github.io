Kindle Fire HDX - affordable tablet from Amazon

In 2011, Amazon shook the tablet market, launching a $ 200 Kindle Fire, while competitors offered models with similar characteristics much more expensive. A year later, Amazon again surprised everyone by releasing two new tablets at once: the Kindle Fire HD and the Kindle Fire HD 8.9. At a cost of $ 200 and $ 300, they had a fairly powerful stuffing for those measures. Now the story is repeated again, but with the Kindle Fire HDX in the lead role. It is impossible to say that a revolution occurred, but the company offered devices with top-end features for the price of budget tablets.



Where does such a relatively small price come from? The fact is that the online store Amazon sells devices "at cost", only covers the cost of their manufacture. An aggressive pricing policy contributes to the company's main goal: to ensure that as many tablets as possible reach users who are potential buyers of Amazon's online content, from games and books to video.
‚ÄúWe want to make money when people use our devices, not when they buy them ,‚Äù says Jeffrey Beezos (head and founder of the Internet company).

‚ñå DISPLAY


The tablet has a display with a resolution of 1920x1200 with a diagonal of 7 "(323PPI) with 100% sRGB color reproduction.



The display has a very high quality oleophobic coating, thanks to which it is slapped with fingers somewhat less than expected.



Viewing angles are expected to be excellent (IPS is peculiar), colors are juicy, but natural.



The margin of brightness is very good: in normal mode, you can use the device with 50% brightness.



DisplayMate experts consider the Kindle Fire HDX 7 screen to be slightly better than the Nexus 7 and iPad Mini Retina. Its production uses the technology of so-called "quantum dots" Quantum Dots.



The color scheme is almost the same: 97% for the Kindle Fire HDX and 103% for the Nexus 7 2013.



In general, the difference between the Nexus 7 and Kindle Fire HDX displays in everyday use is difficult to notice.



To achieve perfect color accuracy on the Kindle Fire HDX 7 "with the lowest possible battery consumption and device weight, engineers used blue LEDs instead of white ones. Blue backlighting provides much more accurate image and rich colors, and up to 20% improved energy efficiency. As a result blue LEDs, you may notice a very faint blue tint along the edges of the device with a white screen background, for example in books and web pages.All displays have some level of glow along the edges, and the backlight on Ki ndle Fire HDX 7 is driven by the technology used to ensure perfect color accuracy.

‚ñå SOFTWARE PLATFORM


The tablet uses the operating system Fire OS 3.0 "Mojito", based on the code base Android 4.2.2. The Fire OS changed the interface and contained a number of low-level transformations. But at the same time, compatibility with Android applications is fully preserved.
The desktop is made in the carousel style, which offers the user the most used applications. Above the carousel is a menu that allows you to launch various services from Amazon, such as Books, Apps, Music, Video and of course the application to view the giant Amazon catalog.



A gesture up the carousel will bring up the screen of all installed applications on the system.



Since there are no hardware buttons in the Kindle Fire HDX, they are emulated on the screen and are on the right side of the screen. Unlike Android stock, there is a Search button that opens a search bar in an open application.



If you hold your fingers to the left of the edge of the screen, then the icons of recent applications will open. So you can switch quickly between applications, multitasking of a kind ...



The status bar displays the device name, time, as well as icons of various modules, and of the battery, which can be displayed as a percentage. Pulling the line from the edge of the screen you will get access to the control panel, where you can block the automatic change of the position of the interface, adjust the brightness or Wi-Fi, as well as go to the full settings mode. In addition, you can call the operator from the support or disable all notifications. And of course, the status bar displays all notifications received from installed programs.



Amazon Kindle Fire HDX is available in two versions: with advertising (With Special Offers) and without advertising (Without Special Offers). The price is different and the difference lies in displaying ads on the lock screen.

Banners are shown in high quality and look like simple wallpapers. It's even a bit cool that your device always meets you with a new interesting picture.

The capacity of the battery installed on the tablet is 4550 mAh. The tablet battery was tested by AnTuTu Tester, and I must say that the tablet showed good results. The AnTuTu Tester test burns the battery up to 19% with a very high load, which is almost never achieved in daily use.

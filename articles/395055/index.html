ReGen Villages - the eco-settlement of the near future

Local high-yielding production of organic food. Energetic houses. Comprehensive and efficient use of renewable energy sources. Recycling water and waste. Empowerment and rights of local communities. These five pillars are based on a new concept of eco-settlement or autonomous urban eco-communities, implemented as part of the project ReGen Villages (Netherlands).



According to the forecasts of some optimistic experts, by 2050 the population of the Earth will reach 9 billion people. Pessimists ignorantly ignore their opinion, upholding the figure of 10 billion. And they and others will certainly agree - the survival of so many people would be unthinkable without the creation of regenerative housing and the development of local communities. No less relevant will be the issues of access to sources of clean drinking water, healthy food, arable land. And, over the search for solutions to the complex of these and related problems, profile specialists in many countries of the world are already busy today. One of such pilot projects is the ReGen Villages eco-settlement, the successful implementation of which will launch the realization of the proposed ideas in many parts of the world.

Pilot town - the eco-settlement ReGen Villages in full swing is already under construction in Almere, located in the central part of the Netherlands. Having its own complete cycle of food production and waste processing, the settlement will be at the same time completely independent of the central power grids. By the middle of this summer, 100 passive houses will be able to accept tenants who have agreed to participate in the experiment with the prospect of further living.

In the free central part there will be greenhouses, parking lots and an electric vehicle charging station, a compact farm, a pond with fish, open gardens and recreation areas for citizens.

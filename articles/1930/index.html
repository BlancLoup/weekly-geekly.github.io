MySpace duplicates YouTube functionality

The social network MySpace has announced innovations in its video service MySpace Video. Now each user can upload a video to the site and tell his virtual friends about it, and this video file will automatically go to his friends via RSS. The functionality of the new section Video Space completely duplicates the functionality of YouTube .

The Video Space section appeared shortly after Google announced the purchase of a YouTube video service. Since the new Video Space system provides for automatic playback of videos, experts expect that the share of MySpace in the video market on the Internet will increase, and quite seriously. Until recently, the leader in this area is YouTube, which accounts for more than 45% of video traffic. According to statistics from Hitwise in August 2006, MySpace is on the second place (23%), and Google Video is on the third place (10%).

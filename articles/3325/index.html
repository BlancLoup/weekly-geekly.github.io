Created sneakers with GPS support

Next month, sneakers with built-in GPS chips will appear on the shelves of the stores (not ours), writes CNN . This invention belongs to the businessman from Miami Isaac Daniel and is made by his company.



The GPS transmitter allows you to track the location and movement of a person wearing sneakers, and in an extreme situation a shoe can help its owner. You only need to press the invisible button, after which the transmitter will give a distress signal. The cost of "monthly maintenance" of such sneakers will be $ 19 per month, and they will cost about $ 350.

The company plans to sell a thousand pairs of sneakers over the Internet, and now 750 orders have been received.

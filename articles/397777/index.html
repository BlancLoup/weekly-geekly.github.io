Children under 7 years old - they are in a subperiod of pre-operational views on J. Piaget.
At the stage of pre-operational ideas, a transition is made from sensor-motor functions to internal - symbolic, that is, to actions with representations, and not with external objects. This stage of development of the intellect is characterized by the dominance of presuppositions and transductive reasoning; egocentrism; focusing on the conspicuous features of the object and disregard for the reasoning of its other signs; focusing on the states of things and inattention to its transformations.

If it is simple in this period, children almost completely lack critical, logical thinking, and parents are indisputable authority for them. As an example: I tried to taste my urine in my childhood, because my grandmother told me that it was medicine, and faithfully believed that it was so.

And for the elderly, cognitive functions are declining, and it can be said that they themselves become children, so they begin to believe in it.

Now consider how people come to this on their own:

1. Curiosity



We can find horoscopes in any magazine or newspaper. And even if a person is not familiar with such a phenomenon as astrology, then naturally there is a desire to know what it is and how it works. Curiosity pushes you to get acquainted with your zodiac sign and learn something mystical about yourself. Not just finding out what people are saying, but finding out what the stars are saying! Rarely who will resist this. All secret and incomprehensible, not at all terrible, but interesting, beckons us irresistible. From this ordinary curiosity, which is based on the function of self-knowledge, the path to horoscopes begins.

2. Vagueness of wording



There is such a thing as the Forer effect . The psychologist Bertram Forer in 1948 conducted a psychological experiment in which he showed the effect of this effect. He gave his students a special test to analyze their personalities. However, instead of the present individual characteristic, he gave everyone the same vague text taken from the horoscope. Then he asked each student on a five-point scale to assess the correspondence of their personality description to reality ‚Äî the average rating was 4.26. The assessment of the accuracy of the description of the students was also influenced by the authority of the teacher. Also, if we still see no matching in the horoscope, then we try not to pay attention to them, like pimples or roughness on the face, when we look in the mirror.

3. Roles



Each of us plays a huge number of roles. For example, when a soldier is made into sergeants, he becomes a sergeant. He is aware of himself as such and begins to play the role of a sergeant, with all the ensuing complex actions. When you enter the transport, you become a passenger. When you are in a store, you are a buyer with all the conventions and actions associated with it. Therefore, when you are given a zodiac sign, you start playing the role of Virgo or Taurus, or Scorpio. And all these descriptions of your zodiac sign that you have been given, they are not just like you, they really become you, again with all the consequences.

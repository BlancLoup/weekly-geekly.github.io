What is dNaNo?

dNaNo is the name of a 1:43 scale radio-controlled car series, which is manufactured by the Japanese company Kyosho. The first production model was demonstrated to the public in April 2008. dNaNo is a radio-controlled chassis with a plastic body, which is a copy of a sports car. dNaNo easily fits in the palm of an adult.





The length of the model is 10 cm., The weight with the battery is 40 grams.



Despite such small dimensions, the dNaNo chassis has a design that is used in larger cars. DNaNo has front spring shock absorbers, satellite differential, rear suspension shock absorber, etc. The car has a proportional control of two channels (gas-brake and turning the wheels right-left). Many car components can be replaced or tuned. The dNaNo transmitter operates in the 2.4GHz frequency range and is powered by four rechargeable batteries or AAA batteries. The dNaNo chassis is powered by a Lithium Polymer 3.7V 130mAh battery, which lasts for 15-20 minutes of active driving. The model's speed in the basic configuration is 13-15 km / h . A microchip is built into the model to determine the lap time on the track, but a device for reading the result (serif) must be purchased separately.



Contents of delivery



When buying dNaNo you should pay attention to the fact that the chassis with the body comes separately from the control equipment. Transmitter, batteries, charger must be purchased separately.



The universal dNaNo transmitter can be used to control a Mini-Z series radio-controlled car model with electronics operating in the 2.4GHz frequency band.

Varieties dNaNo



Today, there are 3 types of dNaNo chassis, which differ from each other in the engine relative to the differential:

MM - the motor is located in front of the differential axis

RM - the motor is located behind the axis of the differential

HM motor is located above the axle of the differential


DNaNo design



In front of the chassis housing is a servo mechanism, which with the help of a steel steering rack turns two cams on which the front wheels are fastened with very small screws. Tires from rubber are put on rims.



Inside the dNaNo case there is a receiver with a speed controller on a single board. Outside the case, connectors for connecting the engine, gyroscope (purchased separately) and the connector for programming dNaNo electronics via a computer are displayed.



programming device and software sold separately



A removable Lithium-polymer battery in a plastic shock-proof casing is located under the chassis cover.



By means of a flexible plate, the rear suspension is attached to the chassis body - the engine mount. The flexible mounting plate is the rear suspension shock absorber.



In the motor-frame there is a collector electric motor, on which shaft the driving gear from Delrin is put on. Included dNaNo are 4 gears (6,7,8,9 teeth), which can be changed. The differential is inserted into the holes of the plastic bushings on the motor mount. The rear wheels are mounted on the differential with small screws.



At the bottom of the case there is a unique blue chip with an RFID tag to determine the time it takes for the race to go. The device (notch) for reading the information of the RFID tag installed on the dNaNo case is a flat antenna of wires that is placed under the track and connected to the computer. The notch for dNaNo is only available from Kyosho and must be purchased separately.

DNaNo body



The body for dNaNo is made of durable plastic, painted and varnished. The laudable copy of the body in no way increases the weight and does not reduce its strength. Some of the body look more reliable counterparts. Kyosho company has the right to accurately copy the symbols and appearance of the most famous cars in the world, thanks to arrangements with the automakers. Currently, the company has already released the bodywork Audi, Ferrari, Lamborghini, Lancia, Mazda, McLaren, Mitsubishi, Nissan, Porsche, Subaru. Kyosho expands the dNaNo body collection every month.

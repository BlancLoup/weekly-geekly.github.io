Web-to-Print technology: designer to himself

If you look at the vacancy announcements of marketers, especially in small companies, you will find out one curious detail. At least half of these ads in the list of requirements appear skills in graphic editors - Photoshop, CorelDraw, and the like.

A fair question arises - why? It seems to be obvious that professionals should take care of every business, especially design.





The answer is simple - most often they need skills in Photoshop not for design, but for more trivial tasks: insert the branch numbers of their branch into a ready-made layout, correct the text, add conditions valid in a particular region, and so on. It seems to be little things, but these little things accumulate a lot, especially in companies with an extensive branch network. You can, of course, give these things to an outsourcing, along with the actual design, but it is much more advantageous to have a person on staff who can make these edits on their own. It will come out faster and cheaper.

But what if you take this path a little further? What can be achieved by giving the marketer a powerful, multi-functional editing tool that does not require design skills?

In Europe, where the culture of using web applications is already quite developed, MRM-systems (Marketing Resource Management) are widely used - specialized software solutions designed specifically for marketers. And one of the most popular technologies in MRM is just Web-to-Print - that is, the production and editing of advertising layouts using previously created templates.

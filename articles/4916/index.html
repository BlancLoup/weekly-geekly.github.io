The most interesting at CES 2007

In the unanimous opinion of visitors, the most enchanting spectacle at the exhibition was a water show in the outdoor pool. The picture from the projector was projected directly onto the water curtain, so that the translucent image hung in the air in front of the amazed spectators ( video , WMV, 56 MB).



However, public interest at CES 2007 was primarily chained not to an entertainment show, but to technological innovations. Recently, CES has become the largest computer exhibition in the world, so many IT companies are trying to coincide with the release of new products to this event. The Consumer Electronics Show 2007 opened on January 8th and will run until Thursday, January 11th.

Walt Disney arranged a real show at CES 2007, introduced a new version of Disney.com in the style of Web 2.0, and also invited to the presentation of movie stars who played the main roles in the TV show Lost. The fact is that the company Walt Disney is actively promoting content for mobile phones, and this series is sold better than others in the version for mobile phones.

The Motorola Director in his speech at CES announced amazing statistics : every second four children are born in the world and 25 mobile phones are sold. Mobile revolution comes to the countries of the third world. Of course, this is hampered by the fact that in Africa, for example, there is no electricity anywhere to read, but Motorola has figured out how to solve this problem: the batteries will be charged from bicycles.

In Western countries, where everything is normal with electricity, the revolution will go on. The most progressive company here is Yahoo , which announced at the exhibition that the search widget Yahoo Go 2.0 will be pre-installed on more than 70 mobile phone models from various manufacturers (Motorola, Nokia, Samsung and Research In Motion).

Nokia has introduced new mobile phone models, including the innovative N800, which is an advanced version of the N770, with a tactile keyboard on the screen, support for Skype and a webcam.

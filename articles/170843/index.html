Enthusiasts ported Ubuntu for 24 smartphones and tablet PCs


Despite the fact that since the publication of the first experimental builds with Ubuntu editions for smartphones and tablet PCs, less than a week has passed, apart from the initially supported builds for Samsung Galaxy Nexus / Nexus 4 smartphones and Nexus 7/10 tablets, another 24 devices were portrayed by enthusiasts originally shipped with the Android platform. Ready-made installation assemblies are already available for devices such as Sony Xperia S, Sony Xperia T, Huawei Ascend G300, VZ SGSIII, Samsung Galaxy S, Samsung Galaxy Note II, Asus Transformer Infinity, Asus Transformer Pad and Nexus One Galaxy Tab 2 10.1.


Rapid porting to new devices is mainly promoted by the use of the basic components of the Android platform as the basis for Ubuntu for phones and tablets. In fact, for devices already supported by the Android platform, it is enough to adapt for the device, a chroot environment with Ubuntu components running on top of Android. The kernel, drivers, OpenGL ES2.0 interlayer, multimedia content processing components, system services and SurfaceFlinger composite manager are used from the Android platform. For the interaction of the Android environment and the Ubuntu chroot environment, libhybris and the Binder IPC mechanism are involved.

via OpenNet

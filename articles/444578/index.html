about the project



The bioprinter was delivered to the ISS on the Soyuz MS-11 on December 3, 2018.


For medicine


On this printer, cosmonaut Oleg Kononenko conducted experiments on the cultivation of human cartilage and the mouse thyroid gland, which brings medicine closer to printing tissues and organs for transplantation. Bioprinting in zero gravity allows you to avoid the limitations imposed by gravity and run new technologies of 3D printing with biomaterial.


Twelve samples have been delivered to Earth and are being studied in 3D Bioprinting Solutions.


In August 2019, it is planned to send synthetic ceramic materials to the space station, which can be used in 3D printing to speed up the regeneration of bone tissue.

For food industry


In addition to medical and research value, bioprinting has a more extensive domestic use, which 3D Bioprinting Solutions partners are currently developing - three American and one Israeli startup. They are developing the technology of growing meat for the food industry, which in the future will allow for the production of cheap and ethical meat food products. Two of them work with beef cages, the other two work with fish, bluefin tuna and salmon cages.

3D Bioprinting Solutions conducts tests of the material sent to select those samples with which the printer already in orbit can work. The selected cells will be packed in special cells and sent to the ISS.

About the printer



Magnetic bioprinter Organ. Avt is created for work in the conditions of weightlessness. The key difference between its operating principle and its ground analogues is that the objects are not formed linearly, from top to bottom, but simultaneously from all sides. Magnetic field guided printing material, such as cells in a nutrient solution, ‚Äúsculpts‚Äù a given model in weightlessness.

3D Bioprinting Solutions produces bioprinter and for work in the conditions of the earthly embellishment, now in the arsenal of the company are two models - FABION and FABION 2.


FABION 2 is a newer and more advanced model of a 3D bioprinter; it can print using bio-ink and hydrogels of various compositions and textures.

The competitive advantage of this mechanism is a high density of cells, as well as a high level of synthesis of extracellular matrix proteins within spheroids, which ensures high viability of the resulting tissue-engineering constructs.

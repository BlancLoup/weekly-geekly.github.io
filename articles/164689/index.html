Also inaccuracies can be in the wording, the author attributed the investors in the table to one category: either a business angel, or a startup incubator, or a venture capital fund. Therefore, here are excerpts from the wiki:

A business angel (hereinafter referred to as the Angel) is a private investor who invests money in innovative projects (start-ups) at the stage of creating an enterprise in exchange for a return on investment and a share in capital (usually a blocking stake, not a controlling one). Angels, as a rule, invest their own funds in contrast to venture capitalists who manage third-party money pooled into venture funds. A small but growing number of business angels form networks, or groups, to jointly participate in the search for objects of investment and to pool capital.


Venture Fund (hereinafter referred to as Venture) is an investment company that works exclusively with innovative enterprises and projects (start-ups). Venture funds invest in securities or enterprises with a high or relatively high degree of risk in anticipation of extremely high returns. Usually such investments are made in the field of the latest scientific developments and high technologies. As a rule, 70-80% of projects do not bring returns, but the profit from the remaining 20-30% pays for all losses.

The mediator is a company that helps the expert assessment of a startup, gives business advice, helps technology. That is, it acts as a bridge between an investor and an IT entrepreneur.

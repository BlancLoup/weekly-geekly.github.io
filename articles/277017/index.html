Hit Parade of the City Hackathon Projects



In late February, we will arrange an open hackathon . We will develop services for life in the city.

135 developers, testers, managers and designers have already appeared on the hackathon. The guys offered more than 30 ideas for future products, and experts from the jury chose the best.

Now there are 24 ideas on the hackathon shortlist . We will tell you about the most popular ones - perhaps they will inspire you with your own project, and you will join us in the company.


House Communities



Imagine: the yard of your high-rise building has been littered with snow for a week already. Public utilities come in no hurry. The easiest way to quickly clean the yard is to collect two hundred rubles from the neighbors and hire a tractor. If you are familiar with the neighbors, this will take a couple of hours. If not, walk on the entrance will be delayed.

Maxim Berezkin invented to create an application for residents of houses. This is where people gather the local community and learn how to solve common problems.

Widget with delivery zones



Sometimes to understand the conditions of delivery on the website of the online store - this is a real feat. Ilya Taratukhin leads a team at the hackathon that will help customers of online stores order pizza, furniture or flowers faster.

To improve this business, Ilya with the team will develop a widget with delivery zones. Owners of online stores will be able to register the price and additional conditions for areas of the city, get the code and place the widget with an interactive map on the site. The client will indicate the address in it and immediately see the cost of the order.


‚ÄúFirst of all, I go to the hackathon for the startup atmosphere. It's very cool to create a working prototype of an application in two days! We will place the widget on our own domain, and if we succeed, we will think about development and monetization. ‚Äù




Messages on the city map



Previously, people expressed feelings for their beloved and spoke about their political views with the help of paint and a fence. Today it is more often done on social networks. Kirill Kazakov decided to combine the romance of vandalism and modern technology.

On February 26, Kirill will go to the hackathon to create "On the Fence" - an app for the Apple Watch. The principle is this: you are writing a message, and it is tied to a point on the map of the city. When a person with the necessary device passes by this place, the application reports the label. Messages from users can be read, liked and shared on Facebook.


‚ÄúOur task for the hackathon is to deal with the development technologies under the Apple Watch. This experience can be applied later for 2GIS products. The idea for the application was chosen quickly - it is fun and simple enough for us to keep within two days. ‚Äù

Watercolor - clean With





Watercolor is simple and accessible to everyone. Paper, a few brushes, water and a set of colors - that's all you need to get started.

The main features of watercolors are the transparency of the paint, the absence of white color and the effect of unique color mixing. The transparency of the paint does not allow us to correct parts of the picture that have already been written, and the lack of white color makes the artist go to the trick and use the natural color of the paper instead of paint. All this leads to the fact that drawing with watercolors requires careful pre-planning of the composition of the work, and the process of writing a picture requires a high concentration of attention - one wrong brush stroke - and now the paint has spread throughout the whole picture or has got out beyond the frame assigned to it by the artist.

Watercolor requires patience. Before you start drawing small parts, you must wait until the previous layer dries as much as is necessary to obtain the artist's intended effect of mixing colors. For a beginner, watercolor is unpredictable:



However, having mastered the real mastery of watercolor, the artist will receive from his works an incomparable feeling of lightness, air, light and color; let it take years of hard training.



Oil Paints - Java



Oil painting is a real mainstream among artists. A distinctive feature of this type of painting is the ability to repaint any part of the painting canvas beyond recognition. The choice of oil paints gives a guarantee that the product will not be spoiled so that it would have to be thrown away. All this allows the artist to create with oil such huge paintings that would not be realizable when choosing other types of painting. Oil painting is one of the most profitable types of painting for the artist; and, in fact, the availability of oil paints for the population is on average.

A pair of new fitness trackers from Jawbone: advanced Up3 and inexpensive Up Move

Jawbone is the definitive leader in the market of fitness trackers. In order not to lose ground, today they presented two new products: the advanced Jawbone Up3 for $ 179 and the Up Move model for beginners for $ 49.99.



The new Jawbone Up3 has a huge number of sensors: in addition to the accelerometer for tracking movements, it has sensors for body temperature and the environment, as well as a series of sensors for bioimpedance analysis (analysis of human body composition). Using microcurrents, the bracelet will be able to determine the heart rate and the level of hydration. Such a variety of collected information opens up unique opportunities for providing individual advice on improving the state of the body.



Having obtained the necessary data, the Smart Coach application will understand when and in what kind of sports you practice, how it affects the quality of sleep, and fix the level of dehydration. Thus, immediately after jogging, it will offer to drink some water, and in the evening it will show a notice with a proposal to lie down early so that you can fully recover your strength after exercise.



Despite the advanced filling, Jawbone Up3 got an even more pleasant appearance than its predecessors. Finally, all the main hardware was removed in a solid case, while leaving the rest flexible, which should reduce the chance of breaking the accessory. The model will appear in a variety of colors, choose and get a pattern on top. The battery life will be about 7 days. The bracelet is not afraid of water: with it you can dive to a depth of 10 meters.



For those who are just entering the world of fitness trackers, Jawbone created an Up Move with a body in the form of a small washer that can be worn with a strap or clips. Despite the minimum information received, it is enough to use to work with Smart Coach. The tracker tracks daily activity and sleep well, but it loses in accuracy compared to the flagship solution.



Releasing a low-cost fitness tracker, Jawbone begin direct competition with the already sold for a long time Misfit Flash, Fitbug Orb and Fitbit Zip. The main advantages of the new product will most likely be advanced data processing, a high-quality smartphone application and a well-known brand at the same low price.

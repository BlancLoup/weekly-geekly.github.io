4 of 5 cars installed cards HERE

Toyota Europe has chosen HERE maps from Nokia as a supplier of maps for its deeply integrated touch & go navigation and information systems. The product will be called Nokia Local Search for Automotive and will be available in new cars destined for Europe, Russia and the Middle East in early 2014.



Toyota joined giants like Audi, BMW, Chrysler, Dacia, ESRI, Ford, Garmin, Hyundai, Kia, Mercedes, Nikon, Pioneer, Scania and Volkswagen. Thus, 4 out of 5 cars with integrated navigation use HERE maps.



It should be borne in mind that this is not just about quality maps and navigation service, but also about the wide possibilities for delivering various additional information based on the user's location. This includes weather and news services, as well as user-generated content ‚Äî POIs, photos and reviews. In addition, this approach allows you to improve maps by constantly adding relevant information to the database and correcting outdated data.

HERE detailed maps allow users to search for specific buildings, interact with three-dimensional models and view the route laid out in detail.



The advantages of this integration are the full integration of HERE services for end-user drivers:
  1. Convenient maps with custom data support, such as photos, POIs, site ratings, reviews, etc.
  2. Large database of own professional cards.
  3. The ability to use services from third-party manufacturers. This can be news, weather changes and other services tied to the location of the car.
  4. Local search.
  5. And, of course, turn-based navigation.

At Toyota, Nokia believes that Nokia products are ideal for organizing a content-based content delivery system, making driving more comfortable even on long journeys.

Nokia's cards are launched around the world about 100 million times a day on various platforms and in various applications.

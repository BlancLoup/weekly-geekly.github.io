Six charging stations for Tesla Model S built in California



Electric cars are becoming increasingly popular around the world, including the United States. Unfortunately, so far the main problem in the distribution of these cars is the impossibility of recharging, not only in remote regions, but also in fairly large settlements. In Europe and the United States, there are already refueling stations, so to speak, but their number is still not enough. Therefore, the good news is the news about the appearance of six new electro gas stations for Tesla Model S ( charging is free ).

These stations appeared in the Folsom, Gilroy, Tejon Ranch, Barstow, Coalinga and Hawthorne regions. The advantage of these charging stations is their autonomy (almost complete). The fact is that electroazs are charged by solar energy (in California, as you know, insolation levels are quite high). Placed at critical points, charging stations now allow the Tesla Model S to travel from San Francisco to Los Angeles, or from the San Francisco Bay area to Tahoe. Charging takes only 30 minutes.

It is clear that six charging stations are a drop in the ocean, but if there are a lot of such drops, the infrastructure of charging stations for electric vehicles (at least for Tesla) will develop and expand.



Here are the exact coordinates of the charging stations:

Folsom, CA: Folsom Premium Outlets: 13000 Folsom Blvd, Folsom, CA 95630 (Near main entrance)
Gilroy, CA: Gilroy Premium Outlets, near Sony: 681 Leavesley Road, Gilroy, CA 95020 (Near Sony store)
Tejon Ranch, CA: 5602 Dennis McCarthy Dr., Lebec, CA 93243 (Near Yogurtland)
Barstow, CA: 2812 Lenwood Rd., Barstow, CA 92311 (Between Chili's and Country Inn & Suites)
Harris Ranch, Coalinga, CA: 24505 W Dorris Ave Coalinga, California 93210 (Near Harris Ranch Inn and Restaurant)
Hawthorne, CA: 3203 Jack Northrop Avenue, Hawthorne, CA 90250

Friday the 13th: the most famous virus in history



Let's not forget about the viruses that have caused many troubles and sorrows among users.
The Jerusalem virus (Jerusalem) , also known as Friday the 13th , was created in Israel in 1988 in honor of the 40th anniversary of the creation of a Jewish state.

The virus has been activated quite simply: as soon as Friday‚Äôs calendar, 13, comes on, all used programs and files were infected and destroyed.

There was no specific way of spreading this virus, but this was done through normal systems, such as floppy disks, CD-ROMs, or email attachments.

How did he work?

- Infected files with the extensions COM, EXE or SYS and increased the size whenever the file was executed;
- Reduced the amount of memory available on the computer;
- It led to a slowdown in your computer;
- Every Friday, the 13th, the virus was activated and destroyed the computer files that were used that day.

How can I get rid of it?

As always, the recommendations for preventing such infections are to ensure that your operating system and antivirus are updated.

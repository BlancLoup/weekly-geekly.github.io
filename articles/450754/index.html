Wheelchair racing: Russian pilot became a winner in the CYBATHLON championship in Tokyo

On May 5, a competition was held in which world leading manufacturers and engineering teams took part, creating the latest technical solutions and means of rehabilitation for people with disabilities. The Russian company Caterville became the winner of the championship.



The wheelchair pilot competition (Yury Larin in the photo on the left) includes a special obstacle course that simulates the everyday difficulties that people often face with various spinal cord injuries or other serious injuries that prevent them from walking.

"Race track" includes areas where you need to skillfully maneuver in a closed space: pass between furniture, turn around on a small platform, and so on. The wheelchair should also be able to adapt to different surfaces, ride over rough terrain, slopes and climb stairs.


The main mission of the Cybathlon Championship is to encourage manufacturers and start-up teams to innovative solutions that help people with disabilities to become more independent and independent in their daily activities.

This year CYBATHLON Wheelchair was held in Kawasaki Prefecture, Tokyo, Japan. Among the participants are Asian and European developers who made their prototypes of wheelchairs specifically for the competition. The presented wheelchairs are equipped with technological solutions that allow to overcome various obstacles, such as stairs, slides or doorways, while remaining not too bulky, easily settling down at a regular table.

The Russian company Caterville became the winner of the championship, and in the course of the final race took second place, putting its serial carriage on the track, which is now being sold on the Russian and foreign markets. Pilot pilot Yury Larin didn‚Äôt have a bit of luck to win.

Production of the new model was launched in April 2019, but the product is already in demand. It was this, a market-oriented wheelchair, that brought silver to the Russians.

On the way to the finals, the Russian team outstripped 6 other all-terrain vehicle development teams from Japan and Hong Kong, and in the final, fought the HSR Echanced team from Switzerland. Bypassing the rival in speed, and showing a high permeability, the pilot Caterville crossed the finish line first.

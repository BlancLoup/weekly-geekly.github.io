"Cutting off the excess." How to limit your imagination and imagination of the customer?

Design is an original profession. Here, as nowhere else, a multiple solution of the task is admissible (maybe even in philosophy, but you won’t make much money there :). Even discarding options arising “through the customer’s fault” (tastes, wrong task, profanation) or “through the performer’s fault” (ostentatious creative), the designer will still have to choose from several options. It is not always possible to thoroughly study the market for which the design work is being created, or there may simply not be enough experience or knowledge of trends. The author of the article has never been negative about the customer’s wish to make several design options. You can negotiate the conditions of the problem for a long time, clarify the brief, carry out analytics (of course, these are the most important stages), but until the customer sees the solution outline, he will not say whether it meets his expectations (and market expectations) or not. In fact, a professional designer must test the ground before rushing into battle and making a ready-made layout. This saves him the time and time of the customer.

The methods given in the article are equally suited to the design of any visual communication. Considering the author's love for the design of corporate identity, the discussion, for the most part, will be about him and his components. All conclusions are advisory in nature and are designed to help you quickly achieve the desired result. Taste and talent has not been canceled.

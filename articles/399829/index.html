The history of Konami: from jukeboxes to the largest holding with anime, games and arcade machines

Holding Konami Holdings Corporation includes about twenty companies around the world. They produce games, animated series, films, are engaged in real estate and health products from corsets to simulators. This whole empire appeared after four Japanese entrepreneurs founded a company for rental and repair of jukeboxes in 1969.

We mostly know Konami from games and series like Contra, Silent Hill and Metal Gear.



Konami was founded in 1969 and got its name from the names of the founders: Kagemasa Ko zuki, Yoshinobu Na kama, and Tatsuo Mi yasako. Kagemasa Kodzuki is still the chairman of the company. The business began with the rental and repair of jukeboxes, but in the wake of the growing popularity of arcade machines, in 1973 the company decided to switch to them.

It took another five years for the company to develop and release its first slot machine. Among the first games was Space King - another clone of the Space Invaders from Taito . Commercial success came to Konami in the early 1980s with the release of Super Cobra games with helicopter shooting, Scramble with the same gameplay, but on a spaceship, and Frogger with frog attempts to get to the swamp.



Frogger, where you need to cross the road, and then - move through the reservoir.



In 1982, Konami began to produce and sell games for personal computers and opened an office in California in the United States - Konami of America. Soon the company began releasing games to MSX and MSX2 standards, which were popular for household computers in Japan in the 1980s and developed by the Japanese division of Microsoft. For seven years, the company has released seventy games, including Antarctic Adventure - the race for the penguin, Vampire Killer - the version of Castlevania, as well as games from Hideo Kojima: Metal Gear, Snatcher and Metal Gear 2: Solid Snake.

The story of Konami is closely related to Hideo Kojima, who rose to vice president of Konami Digital Entertainment and is now in a difficult relationship with the studio. In the 1980s, the 20-year-old Kojima wrote stories to magazines, but the stories were so big that they refused to be published. But he managed to realize his talent in game design - in creating the plot, gameplay and games in general in Konami. And here he was not always lucky - the company canceled some games. The importance of this designer for the company is shown, for example, by sales: in 2009, out of 26.81 million copies of games sold by Konami, 4.75 million were Metal Gear games.

In October 1984, Konami shares were listed on the Osaka Securities Exchange.

Metal Gear is one of the first stealth action games.

In 1985, the company began producing video games for the third-generation Nintendo Famicom console . Some of the games were ported to the console from the gaming machines, some were simultaneously made for MSX and Nintendo computers.

The list of best-selling games for the Nintendo Entertainment System includes Konami's games such as Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles with 4 million cartridges sold, Gradius and Metal Gear - 1 million copies each.

Success in the NES gaming market allowed the company to increase revenue from $ 10 million in 1987 to $ 300 million in 1991.

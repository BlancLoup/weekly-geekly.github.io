Problem 10 million connections

A few days ago, Robert Graham began work on a series of C10M articles and plans to finish work in July of this year.

The following chapters are already available today:

  • Problem 10 million connections
  • Introduction to scaling
  • Custom Network Stack
  • Multiprocessing scaling - do not use multi-core technology
  • Introduction to non-blocking programming
  • Binding to the processor and interrupt handling
  • Forget what was taught in the school about parsers
  • Comparison of x86 architecture and specialized network processors


In the chapter on multiprocessing scaling, Graham talks, among other things, about the new features of Haswell processors, here I wanted to tell about another news.

In 2007 in Switzerland, with the support of Microsoft Research, the development of the experimental multiprocessor operating system Barrelfish began, which some features, such as CAS2, use. It is still far from the point of even minimal use; however, source code is available under the MIT license.

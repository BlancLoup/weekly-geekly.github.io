Office in 100 machines, or a story about how I transferred the server from Windows to Centos 7. Prologue

It was 5 years ago, a good friend called me (let it be Irina) and asked me to work as system administrators in the central accounting department of our city. I asked to provide a list of the duties of this employee, to which I received an answer, which drove me into a stupor:
The system administrator must come to the workplace every morning, restart the server, bypass the girls and solve their problems with the programs. It is also necessary to make a monthly report on the work done.

I immediately decided to refuse, because I did not want to lose my time, and hung up. The evening passed in thought of the correctness of my decision and the reasons for which the former employee rode every morning and restarted the server.



The next morning began violently. I just got out of the shower and was about to drink, when suddenly the doorbell rang. This came Irina with a cake and started right from the threshold to convince me that I needed this job, and she needed me as a system administrator. After a stormy discussion about my duties at this workplace, I nevertheless agreed to run this enterprise.

Then I just leave the conditions on which I agreed to work
My responsibilities:
  • Every morning, come to the workplace and restart the server
  • Bypass girls and solve their problems with programs
  • Monthly report on the work done.
  • Monitor the health of the server and local network, timely troubleshoot.
  • Solve girls' problems related to programs and hardware as they become available.
  • Keep in touch at any time of the day or night.
  • Implement innovations imposed by higher management.

My rights:
  • After the reporting period from May 1 to May 31, I do not appear at work and do not pick up the phone.
  • I do not appear at work if everything works.
  • The girls do not turn to me directly with the problems, but paint them according to the pattern I have drawn up and send them to the post office.

PS: Official employment, the salary of 8,000 rubles per month, free lunch and tea.

First work day


On my first working day, I decided to be late and informed in advance that I would come by 9:00. It was summer and the weather was great. I left the house and quietly went towards the Central Bank .

Upon arrival at the workplace, I first went to see Irina, since she was the director at this institution. The first thing she took me to my workplace and showed the whole front of work. The only phrase in the demonstration of my workplace was:
There is our server in the corner. So you need to restart it every morning.

Glancing around the closet, I realized the whole essence of being. The size of the room was approximately 8 m 2 . It was no longer a server, but a storage room. Everything was littered with boxes, old iron, Christmas toys and tinsel. Somewhere in the corner, filled with boxes of office chairs, I saw a server. He barely puffed on blades, trying to blow dust mattresses that had accumulated over the years of work in this room.

First of all, I began to rake the rubble in the room and study what the server is.

What is what and what is where?


On the first day after cleaning, I didn‚Äôt have enough strength to figure out what the trouble with the server was and why it was still alive. On the second day, I connected a monitor, keyboard and mouse to it. To my surprise, there was Windows Server 2003.

This machine has the following functions:

  • Router
  • DHCP server
  • Server database "Parus"
  • Accounting Server 1C
  • Messenger Server CommFort
  • File sharing

On the back, goose bumps ran from what I saw. At that time, I knew a little linux and used only the MOPS Linux distribution . By that time, they stopped supporting him and I decided to try CentOS .

From corpses in the workplace, I assembled a work platform and began to explore the capabilities of CentOS. For me it was a novelty, but I quickly settled in and after 2 months a new server started working. The Parus database was killed and the database was accessed only locally on the director‚Äôs computer, the 1C server was transferred to a powerful computer and a third-party company began to implement its support.

On a new server, I launched:

  • DHCP server
  • Ejabberd server
  • Squid Proxy
  • File sharing on Samba and ESET for filtering infection

Subsequently, I cleared all the rubble in the closet and collected a rack from the profile. I did not appear again. After a year of work, Irina called me and said that the position of the system administrator was reduced and she was very pleased to work with me.

Conclusion


Two months ago, Irina called me again and told me that they had been assigned a static IP address under ViPNet Coordinator and they needed help in setting up my server. As at the beginning of the story, I decided to refuse, since I no longer worked at this enterprise, and any work should be paid.

A month ago, a telephone conversation took place again. This time they said that they would soon open the workplace, they would double the salary and the conditions would remain the same. This time I agreed, but on condition that I‚Äôll get half-time, and the second part is my brother.

The continuation of cooperation was prompted by my own reasons:

  • I can use a static IP address for my personal purposes.
  • Extra money will always be useful.
  • I will help to earn the work experience of my brother. He is still a student.
  • There is a great desire to move the server to a new hardware.

Solving copyright problems: detailed design

The current copyright scheme is a classic lose-lose: most copyright holders are agonizing, showing a steady decline in revenue; most consumers are dissatisfied with prices and quality and consistently refuse to play according to the rules proposed by the industry. At the same time, both sides rested their heads and are not ready to compromise. Below is a detailed version of the transition to win-win strategy.

Problems:

1) public rejection of the current copyright legislation and its actual illegitimacy;
2) arising from item 1 "Piracy";
3) industry stagnation, steady decline in incomes of right holders;
4) the denial of modern content distribution technologies by right holders and, moreover, active resistance to the development of such technologies;
5) an abundance of online and offline pirated services, enriched by illegal access to content (either by selling it at a lower price, by advertising on the portal, or by spreading malicious code to the content load);
6) the actual lack of opportunities for beginning authors to achieve fame without entering into a bondage contract with the publisher.

Hence the goals:

1) to achieve a change in public opinion in favor of the legal use of content and the rejection of "piracy";
2) radically reduce the level of "piracy";
3) provide holders with the opportunity to grow with the development of information technology;
4) to give new impetus to the development of modern content distribution technologies;
5) deprive the illegal services of the audience;
6) provide novice authors with working mechanisms for promoting their creativity without the help of a publisher and without the transfer of exclusive rights.

Plus two additional goals:
7) it is very desirable to make the transition smooth;
8) it is very desirable to remain within the framework of current legislation.

Do teams survive after hackathon?



The benefit of participating in the hackathon is one of those topics that will always be discussed. Each side has its own arguments. Collaboration, HYIP, team spirit - declare alone. ‚ÄúSo what?‚Äù Others answer gloomily and economically.

In their cyclical structure, participation in hackathons is very similar to one-time acquaintances in Tinder: people get to know each other, find common interests, do business, perhaps a memory photo, and naturally diverge, once again starting to actively search for fresh sensations and knowledge. Those 48 hours remain in the memory of a bright moment - and only occasionally meetings on hakatons (and on dates too) grow into serious projects. The more interesting it is to consider closer the history of startups that grew not on the walls of the MIT campus or during the protracted brainstorming of Google teams, but from the spontaneous unification of several young people at the next hackathon.

Feedback - our everything


One of the reasons why Markus Tan, Lukas Ngu and Keck Xiu Ryu decided to participate in the Singaporean version of Startup Weekend 2012 was that the event was held within the walls of their native National University of Singapore. For each of them, this was the first hackathon and the stated goal of participation was ordinary - ‚Äúto make a cool project that will help solve an important problem‚Äù. The result of 54 hours of hard work, desperate disputes and intermittent sleep was the unconditional victory of the prototype of the SnapSell application, a familiar mobile variation on the marketplace theme nowadays, where users sell and buy various things from each other.



However, the typical story of victory in the hackathon has evolved. ‚ÄúThrough our landing page, we received several hundred emails, we even received tweets asking if we could download the app right now. This gave us confidence that we need to move on: to assemble a team and leave work, ‚Äùrecalls Keck Xiu Ryu. Two months later, young people synchronously wrote applications for dismissal and began to turn a successful idea into a working app for iOS.

It will take two whole years before the company, which changed its name to Carousell, will attract 800 thousand dollars at the sowing round. By the middle of 2018, when the founders celebrated the sixth anniversary of the launch of the project, and the total amount of transactions made through Carousell was more than $ 5 billion, almost every resident of Singapore, Malaysia and Thailand already knew about their application. Universal fame and record turnovers helped to attract large investments - Asian and American investors invested more than $ 126 million in the company.

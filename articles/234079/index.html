Cup PEGTOP (Cup-Yula) - a funny object or a killer of teaspoons?

A couple of years ago, we developed a design concept for a unique cup, which, rotating around its own axis, is capable of stirring drinks without a spoon and uplifting. We have come a long way from the first tests to participation and victories in international competitions and exhibitions. Finally, we are now starting to collect pre-orders for the release of the first-ever copies of the Cup-Yula.



Under the cut video and a detailed story.



The birth of an idea


It all started back in 2009, when Anastasia Gavrilova , as a student at the Southern Federal University, was doing her internship in London. There, she was faced with the fact that in the cafe there were no normal teaspoons, and sometimes even chopsticks. Therefore, stir coffee or tea with sugar was very problematic. And then, trying to rotate the glass with her hand, she thought - why not create a cup that does not require a spoon to mix the contents? So the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe Cup-Yula was born.

At the beginning of 2012, having met me, Anastasia remembered her long-standing idea, and together we set about implementing it. The main condition was the absence of complex mechanisms, engines, batteries, etc. Such a cup should be made entirely of glass or porcelain and be at home no less convenient and unpretentious than ordinary dishes.

Work on the concept


The first sketches that Nastya showed me were similar to the toy of the same name. The cup should have the shape of a whirligig and should be closed with a special lid equipped with a handle for rotation. It became absolutely obvious to me - in this form, the product is unlikely to interest anyone. And we began to refine the form, bringing it closer to the traditional tea pair.



At first glance, it seemed to me that the task was simply impossible - either it would be difficult to use the cup and it would not be aesthetic, or the drink would spill out, and the cup would regularly tip over. Still, we managed to find a balance and solve all the problems.

First of all, to achieve the WOW effect, the cup must rotate around its own axis after a jerk by hand (due to inertial forces). For the stability and safety of the structure, it was decided to make it in two parts - the cup itself and the saucer stand. The saucer is equipped with a special protrusion - the axis of rotation, which is set on the notch of the cup.



This made it possible to lower the center of gravity of the cup and prevent its accidental tipping over. In addition, for extra stability and splash protection, we have given the cup a pear shape.

Further, we came to the conclusion that in order to mix the beverage, the cup should have internal fins that will hook and tighten the liquid. And here I had the idea to tilt the ribs in the direction of rotation (clockwise). This made it possible to direct the flow of liquid downward and thereby prevent splashing.

Successes


As a result of all our transformations, the design of the cup became not only fully functional, but also acquired a peculiar aesthetics of an unopened bud. We managed to do something incredible - harmoniously combining complex functionality with the beauty of forms. As nature itself does.



This is probably why Chashka-Yul (English name Cup PEGTOP) reached the finals of the international competitions RED DOT DESIGN CONCEPT, JAMES DYSON AWARD, and also won the DESIGN AND DESIGN INTERNATIONAL AWARD.
At the Milan Design Week, which took place in April of this year, especially for which we printed out the final prototype, Chashka-Yula made a splash! It was pleasant to observe the enthusiastic views of people, some of whom returned to our booth and brought their friends and children.



By the way, the video that we prepared specifically for the final of the RED DOT contest randomly fell into the hands of the authors of the portal GIZMODO.com . Our cup periodically gets the spotlight of the media, for example, not so long ago on the Moscow 24 channel there was a report about the JAMES DYSON AWARD award, in which we repeatedly participated, reached the final and even won (with another project ). UPD: Please note that there is a slight flaw on the prototype shown in the video - the hole on the cup was overfilled with acrylic enamel. As a result, the gap between the cup and saucer has increased and the cup has a greater roll than it should have. In the final product, the gap will be smaller (about 2 mm) and the cup will rotate more evenly.



Start of production


In parallel with the patenting, for a long time we tried to find a manufacturer for the production of glass cups. But, unfortunately, it turned out that the casting molds used in conventional production consist of two halves, which, due to the inclined edges of the cup, cannot be opened without damaging the product. And the creation of a separate production line can be quite a costly undertaking.

At the moment, we decided not to wait any longer and start producing high-quality porcelain cups, which, of course, is not transparent, but has a number of advantages in front of the glass: the possibility of any coloring of the glaze, the application of logos and drawings, and, of course, porcelain has a lower production cost .

At the first stage, we decided to use Russian crowdfunding, and later we plan to enter the foreign market, which is so vigorously responding to the novelty.

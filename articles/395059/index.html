SWIPE - photo frame with light control gestures


Ponty more money. What will not come up to pamper fans of unusual gadgets. Now there is a lot of photo frames on the market that can show pictures from a flash drive or from facebook, but the photo frame with which you can turn off all the lights in the house with a wave of your hand hasn‚Äôt been like that yet! The company Fibaro , known for its beautiful gadgets for the smart home, has released a completely new product - SWIPE.

SWIPE is a Z-Wave device made in the form of a photo frame. The front panel is a capacitive sensor capable of recognizing gestures. Depending on the gesture, for example, to hold the hand from left to right, a specific command is sent, for example, turn off the lights in the room or turn on the heated floor.


SWIPE can be attached to the wall or put on the cabinet. Power is provided by 4 AA batteries or Micro USB. The sensor recognizes 6 unique gestures: right, left, up, down, clockwise torsion, counterclockwise torsion. Gestures can be combined, for example, first up, then down, it allows you to control Z-Wave devices or run scripts, such as turning off all lights or closing all blinds.


SWIPE does not show digital photos, you have to insert a regular photo, thanks to this you can hang this photo frame next to ordinary photo frames, and it will not stand out from their background, harmoniously fitting into the general view.


At home, I use SWIPE to turn off all the lights in the apartment at the exit. But you can go further and hide the SWIPE under the table or behind the wall, then you get a hidden control, for example, to activate an alarm or send an alarm message. The sensor allows to recognize gestures approximately at a distance of 15 mm.



This is a completely new control, the potential of which, each for itself reveals in different ways, everything depends only on imagination.

To work with SWIPE, you need a Z-Wave controller, such as the Fibaro Home Center, RaZberry, Vera, and others. The Fibaro Home Center provides the maximum user-friendliness. With the help of a clear wizard, you can set up associations of gestures and commands.

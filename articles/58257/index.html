Overview of web2.0 services for applicants, students, teachers and scientists

Helping a friend with a diploma, I came across a Federal Portal of Russian Education , which is essentially an important and useful portal, but one of the most terrible usability I have ever seen ...
And I decided to look for what we have in RuNet dedicated to universities and higher education.

My thesis


Service for the publication of scientific papers, articles with the possibilities of comments and ratings, maintaining personal and thematic blogs, creating and combining into groups. As well as an interactive calendar of events
What pleased as an additional service is the section Useful information in which, according to the authors, there are:
  • passport specialties
  • list of scientific funds
  • list of dissertation councils
  • Abstracts

SciPeople Science Network


This is a scientific network for scientists, graduate students and students, an electronic library of publications.
With a nice interface, fully accessible in 2 languages
Good search by category:
  • Scientists
  • Scientific areas
  • Projects
  • Organizations
  • Works
  • Courses
  • Grants

Education and Science


The site sits on the domain (Russian Informational Network) completely on Web2.0, with a bunch of ads. However, what he liked - it worked texts. As one of the moss professors said - "Content is content, content is king!". And you can not argue with that.
The site also covers all parts of education "from first-graders to members of the RAS"
The interface is old and inefficient - but it works, which pleases, unlike, for example, edu.ru

Scribbler


Very interesting web2.0 service that allows:
  • create your own learning space in the form of a desktop
  • Upload, download, share learning resources
  • Create community inside
  • Follow the latest developments in the field of study, science and entertainment
  • Search and receive information about universities - and the data is very relevant and well presented

And a lot of other things - try, you will like :)
The service is still at the beta stage of testing, but the scope of the project and its quality is impressive. Long life Scribbler. And thanks f1neman for the tip :)

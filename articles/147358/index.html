And a little more about networking technologies. At the Computex 2012 exhibition, ASUS introduced the ASUS RT-AC66U wireless router.



ASUS RT-AC66U is not just a wireless router operating in two frequency bands (2.4 and 5 GHz), but also an access center for a variety of cloud services. For example, the ASUS Cloud Disk service provides remote access to content that resides on a disk device connected to the USB port of the router.

RT-AC66U can be used as a multifunction device with 3G, FTP, DLNA or print server capabilities.

Data rates in the 5 GHz band can reach 1.3 Gbps. Considering that in the 2.4 GHz band the maximum data transfer rate is 450 Mbps, the total speed of this router is 1.75 Gbps.

The RT-AC66U offers features that are already familiar to users of other ASUS high-speed routers. To implement access to the "cloud" file storage with modern security features and high speed data exchange, use the AiCloud application, available for free from the Play Store for Android devices and the App Store for iOS devices.

ASUS P1 Portable LED Projector


The LED projector received an award in the Display and Digital Entertainment category of Computex 2012, and was unexpectedly awarded awards at several design competitions.

ASUS P1 is capable of projecting an image with a brightness of 200 lumens and a resolution of 1280x800 pixels. It weighs only 415 g, and its dimensions are close to a regular CD case (126x34x130 mm). The service life of the LED lamp is up to 30 thousand hours (4 hours a day will last for 20 years). ASUS P1 is ready to work in 5 seconds after it is turned on, and its shutdown is instantaneous, without a long cooling period. P1 is compatible with most ASUS laptop power supplies.

Thanks to the small projection ratio (1.16: 1), you can get a projection sized to the screen of a 40-inch monitor by placing P1 one meter from the image. The keystone correction function implemented in P1 automatically changes the image parameters in order to achieve perfect quality when projected at an angle.

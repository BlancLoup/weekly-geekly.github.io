Anton Nosik: There is a lot of money in RuNet

Anton Borisovich Nosik needs no introduction. This is one of the most prominent figures of the Runet. Such resources as Gazeta.ru, Vesti.ru, Lenta.ru were created under his leadership. Anton Nosik was the President for Development of Rambler.ru.

According to Roem.ru, Anton Nosik has been on the board of Reveality Venture Capital for six months already and it is possible that Anton Borisovich will soon announce his departure from the company "Soup" .

Venture Magazine (VM) decided to talk with Anton Nosik about investing in Runet startups.

VM: How can you characterize the investment market in Runet startups?

Anton Nosik: A very strange situation has developed. A lot of money and a lot of projects. At the same time, worthy objects for investment are as few as at any other time.

VM: How do you think the market will develop further?

Anton Nosik: The way of existence of any market is Brownian motion. The market, like the process of exchanging goods for money and vice versa, has no development. Individual invested projects will develop, each tenth of them approximately. The rest will be bent, not waiting for the second round of financing.

VM: Are there universal criteria for selecting applicants for investment?

Anton Nosik: There are no universal criteria. All projects have certain disadvantages: one is not sufficiently calculated, the other is not beautiful enough, the third is not sufficiently enough for any investor criteria ... In order to distinguish critical shortcomings from easily resolvable, intuition is needed. No rules, and even more so - formulas, do not save here.

VM: Which way do you think is better: investing at the idea stage, or after launching the beta version to the market?

Anton Nosik: Investing at the idea stage is cheaper. At the stage after the beta is released - safer, but more expensive, and the competition for working beta is higher than for ideas, you can lose the race. This is an eternal dilemma when spending money. Either more risk or less profitability.

VM: Investing in startups is risky. Which start-up topics are more attractive to investors now and in the foreseeable future?

Anton Nosik: And now, and in the foreseeable future, the subject of the project does not have the slightest importance.

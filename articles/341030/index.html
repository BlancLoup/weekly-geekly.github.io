What is the difference between cool networkers and performing in public? 5 qualities that can be envied


Speaking to strangers, trying to captivate them with their ideas - for many it is a difficult task. Scary, horror - "and what they think," "it is indecent", "what I tell them." But there are Sensei, who are on the move very well find a common language with a variety of people and are interesting to speak to any audience. One of these artists is Irina Tripapina, head of international development at MyTarget. Thanks to her talent, very large customers from several countries came to myTarget. Under the cut - tips and interviews with Irina, in which she talks about the main qualities that help to speak and fascinate people. Give her the word.


Every two months I speak at international conferences: in China, Spain, USA, Singapore, France, Germany. My task is to interest the creators of foreign applications in the Russian market and help them win the market through the products of Mail.Ru Group.


Imagine: 100,000 people come from all over the world to Barcelona at the Mobile World Congress. 100,000 different ideas and tasks, 100,000 new meetings ignite the city in these three days. Everyone has a chance to get acquainted with Mark Zuckerberg, arrange an interview at Amazon or sell your Google product. But why is that guy leaving the conference empty-handed, and this one with a job offer, new partners and Satya Nadela as a friend on Facebook? What is so cool about these cool speakers and networkers?


1. Order in the head


They clearly see their goals and objectives. They know what they are experts, and where you need to pump. Understand who can help them achieve their goals and what to offer in return. This gives everything they talk about structure and clarity. 2. Passion


Love for work and product can not be faked. If real, it is easily transmitted by airborne droplets, instantly reviving everything around. Have you ever heard Mark Zuckerberg talking about writing code? This is magic, shine in the eyes, faith in the future. You can be excited, ignite the audience with the State Standards for software development, or you can put it to sleep with the prospects of artificial intelligence. The question is what drives the speaker himself.


It helps me: making lists that I love and know how, inspirational people around, reading, trial and error.

3. Courage


My friend from the Dublin office of Google is a typical introvert. When he finds himself on stage, he immediately admits: this is his nightmare, and perhaps he will soon faint. He instantly won the sympathy of the hall: people like to think that someone is more nervous than them and is not afraid to admit it. Cool speakers and networkers understand that only the first step is scary. Each exit on the stage or a new acquaintance is like a parachute jump - first a heart on the heels, and then a thrill of new emotions, a bunch of photos on Facebook and the envy of friends. Only in the first case, the risk of breaking the legs is much less.


It helps me: training and rehearsals, sports, proper breathing techniques, the idea that the audience is filled with friends (does not work if you have no friends).

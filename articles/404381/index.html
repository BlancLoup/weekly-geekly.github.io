3D video technology

For a start, a little theory, not to be confused. There is a stereo movie or 3D, when each eye has its own picture. And a 360 degree panorama movie. If you watch the latest in VR glasses, then the volume will not be, but you can look around, as if you are in the center of action. Examples of this video are available here .

We are interested in exactly 3D or 3D video. Consider ways to get such a movie: it can be anaglyph when there is a separation by color. This method is very cheap, but the chromaticity of the picture suffers, but multi-colored glasses cost a penny.

The technology works as follows:



A more serious technology is the shutter glasses and a projector that synchronizes the work of the shutter with a demonstration of a picture for each eye. These glasses require periodic charging, and the frame rate is 120Hz. The technology is good, but the screen flicker does not pass without a trace even at such a frequency: the eyes get tired after watching a long movie. In addition, projectors with support for barrier technology cost a lot of money, so this can be found more in cinema than at home.



The next way to demonstrate the volumetric frame is the polarization technology. The picture is shown alternately by the projector, with light waves of different polarization of the light flux being used for each frame. The glasses are equipped with filters that allow certain light waves to pass through, which allows each eye to receive its own picture.

Polarized glasses are a bit heavier than cardboard ones, but at the same time they are much lighter than the slide and do not consume energy. However, this method of 3D films requires not only special filters, but also a screen with a special coating.



Perhaps, all these factors have become in the way of the distribution of 3D films in every home. Need a device as cheap and simple as possible. It seems that a solution was found - these are virtual reality glasses or VR glasses. The simplest models are made of cardboard, and the video can be viewed from a smartphone, since almost everyone has telephones with a large touch screen and a good picture.



3D video theory

The average distance between the eyes of a person is 6.5 cm. Therefore, one eye sees an object slightly on the left side, and the other eye covers it on the right. That is, each eye sees the object from its own angle. Thus, two slightly different images are formed - a stereo pair. The brain merges both images into one volume.

This is easy to understand if you look at an object from a short distance, up to half a meter, alternately with your right and left eye. But if you look at the same object from a distance of several meters, there will be practically no difference, that is, the picture becomes almost flat.

Google Fusion Tables

No Wave'om single lives now Google. In the LiveJournal community, ru_infodesign skipped a link to a new service from Google Labs - Fusion Tables . This is a very beautiful visualization tool for almost any tabular data.
The essence of the service is simple: some statistical table is taken (the more data - the more interesting), and the data from it is visualized by one of the proposed methods.
There are a variety of ways to display; If the data contains geographic information, you can display it on a color-coded map; if there is temporary information, you can display it graphically.
You can also put together data from several tables at once and display them on a single graph. And, of course, as in any other office services from Google, you can work on tables collectively.
Unfortunately, the current version is quite raw. Now this is not even an alpha version. It can be seen that the interface is in some places incomplete, and in general it is rather difficult to navigate through this whole pile. But hopefully, soon enough, the product will reach beta.

io.js and node.js


You can read the original article here .
  • Scott Hammond, CEO of Joyent, expressed a desire to return io.js to node.js.


In just a few months, the io.js project ...


  • Grew to 23 active members of the main team
  • Several working groups
  • 29 localization teams,
  • Attracting more participants than node.js for all time of its existence
  • With the support of the community produces high-quality software.

We can't wait to forget about the split with node.js, but we cannot sacrifice progress, principles and the management model that we have implemented.

Future


  • Negotiations are ongoing with node.js.
  • As soon as the optimal management model is chosen, a discussion on GitHub will begin about whether io.js is worth joining node.js. Voting on this issue will take place at a public meeting of the technical committee.

For the community, nothing changes.

What can you do now


  • Continue to send pull requests
  • Add to one of 27 localization commands
  • Help one of the working groups ( streams , website , evangelism , tracing , build , roadmap ) and
  • Continue using io.js in your projects.

  • Excellent students have unique abilities to learn: when the fear of a five-point scale passes, they easily and with pleasure master new things that have nothing to do with their work.   • Excellent students may not be innovators or inventors, but these are always early followers of technology and good performers who will do the work carefully and intelligently. If we are talking about programmers, they are neat, obligatory, they follow the code style, they are ready to refactor and do not snort, which is a waste of time.
  • Excellent students are masters of flexible skills (so-called soft skills). This is due to their broad horizons, erudition and ability to reach the essence of things. In response, we will write a checklist for excellent students and employers - how to work on yourself, to become a truly invaluable, indispensable and involved employee.

  • If you are a racer, then you need to rejoice at the race, and not just win it. You get experience on your life tracks: you are mistaken, you see unexpected turns, you meet talented rivals.   • Do not forget that the theory is over - practice has begun. It is mega cool that you are deeply versed in everything new and complex. Just do it in parallel with the execution of the work - the more so that the material is much easier and better absorbed.
  • Do not overload yourself with work, picking it up on all fronts. It is important for the employer that you perform your duties well. If you want to bring more benefits to the company and show your abilities, talk to your supervisor and ask what other work you can do.
  • There are strategists who see ahead of themselves and move toward set and independently adjusted goals, but there is a work force that pulls the 8-hour strap. Your mind and your abilities allow you to be strategists and innovators - try it!

Why does light move at the speed of light?

(Approx. Trans. - see approx. Trans. At the end of the post)

Why does light move at the speed of light? Why he just does not stand still? What sets it in motion (especially so fast)?



Everything and everywhere, simply by the fact of its existence, ‚Äúmoves‚Äù with the speed of light (which actually has nothing to do with light). Yes, this also applies to you. Right now.

People generally perceive the "universe" as a "space", something separate from "time", and frankly, they are wrong. Space and time are not separate things. The universe is made of "space-time", just like that, without a space. You are probably accustomed to the fact that ‚Äúyear‚Äù is a unit of time, and ‚Äúlight year‚Äù is a unit of distance, that is, they are different things; but from the point of view of physics, this is absolutely the same thing (well, of course, depending on what type of physics you do).

In our everyday life, we proceed from the fact that movement is a certain distance (space) that has been overcome in some time. However, if we decided that this is the same thing, our definition of movement suddenly becomes complete nonsense. ‚ÄúI walk a kilometer for every kilometer I walk‚Äù - some kind of nightmare!


It turns out that everything works like this - everything in the Universe ‚Äútravels‚Äù through spacetime with a constant ‚Äúspeed‚Äù, which I will further refer to as s, for brevity. However, we see with our own eyes that different things around us have different speeds. The reason for this is the awesome fact that space and time are orthogonal (with this beautiful word I try to say ‚Äúat the right angle to each other‚Äù). For example, in the same sense, the north and east are orthogonal (perpendicular) - you can go exactly north for any length of time, and this will not affect your movement to the east or west.

Just as you can move to the north, while not moving east, you can move in time without moving in space. But even steeper is the fact that you can move in space in such a way that it does not affect your ‚Äúposition‚Äù in time.

I think that you are now (like me) sitting and looking at the screen. This means that you are not particularly traveling in space. And since you have to travel in space time with a constant speed c, this means that all your ‚Äúmovement‚Äù is spent in the direction of time.



By the way, this is why the famous time dilation takes place. An object that moves very quickly (relative to you) moves in space, but since it also moves in space time at a constant speed, it has to move more slowly in time to compensate (again from your point of view).



Even more surprising, the light does not move at all in time. It is a bit difficult to explain, but in short, this is due to the fact that it has no mass.



Something that does not move in space, but which has mass, may have energy. This is the meaning of the formula E = mc ^ 2. Light has no mass, but it definitely has energy. If we apply the mass of light to E = mc ^ 2, we get 0, which is meaningless, because light has energy. Therefore, the light can never be still.



Moreover! The light is never fixed as to anything. Since, like everything else in the Universe, it travels through spacetime with speed c, then all this ‚Äúspacetime speed‚Äù must be in the ‚Äúdirection‚Äù of space, and nothing remains for a while.

So, the light moves with speed c. It is not by chance that this speed is often defined as "the speed of light in a vacuum." In fact, this is the speed with which everything moves everywhere and everywhere. It just so happened that the light with this speed moves precisely in space, because it has no mass.

By the way, here you can answer another frequently asked question - why can nothing move faster than light, and why things ‚Äúmaterial‚Äù (having mass) cannot move at the speed of light. Since everything ‚Äúmoves‚Äù in space-time at a constant speed c, nothing can overcome this speed in space. And no, traveling back to the past will not help.

Also, since objects with a mass can be "fixed" with respect to something (for example, themselves), they always have to move in time at least a little. This means that they cannot move in space as fast as light. Then they would have to move in space time faster than speed c, which is impossible.

So, if the light does not move through time, only through space, why then does it not move instantly? After all, we know for sure that the light needs some time to fly from the Sun to the Earth.

It is very difficult (if at all possible) to adequately describe the flow of space and time from the point of view of a photon. We can say that photons do not "feel" time, although this sounds strange. Although we have no reason to worry about their feelings.



It takes some time for the light to move from one place to another, because its speed is finite relative to anyone who watches it.

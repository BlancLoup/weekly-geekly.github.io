Pliq.me baby ... talk to me!


The best way to type text on the phone is to say it.


Since last week, Pliq.me is available in closed testing, which allows using a small application and a large server part to create short text messages on your phone for SMS, E-mail, reminders, tweets, Evernote or just quick notes.

The point is very simple. You download and install the application on the phone (versions for iPhone , Symbian are now available, and the Windows phone is already on the way), record voice messages, the phone transfers the whole thing to the server, the speech is converted into text and sent to where you wish .

What can be more natural than to say the text of an SMS message, email or post on Twitter? Pliq is a new communication interface in your phone. You just speak, speak and speak again, and your words converted into text scatter to the recipients.

For testing there are about 100 invites for Symbian and 50 for the iPhone. If you are interested to participate, then write in a personal. For those who are the happy owners of the iPhone, please send your UDID (you can find it by clicking on the serial number of the phone in iTunes on the main iPhone section, in the UDID of 40 characters).

Now the system is able to convert speech into text for Russian, English and German. In the near future, access to testing of Italian, Spanish and French will be open.

Instructions for use can be downloaded here , and at the same time, and see the FAQ - they are in English, but a true friend of Google Translate will help us =)

Thanks in advance for any comments and error messages in the service.



UPD: the application on the iPhone will be sent personally to the mail, after receiving the UDID. Please provide your contact name.

UPDD: iPhone distribution is finished - full house, Symbian is still in the game =)

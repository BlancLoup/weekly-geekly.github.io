Solitude day


11.11 - the day of the bachelor (guangunjie) in China is practically the youngest holiday, which appeared in the 90s of the XX century. It originated in a progressive university environment as a reaction to unpopular government policies. In overpopulated China, childlessness is encouraged in every way (after the introduction of the law on birth control, according to which the family could not have more than one child), and as one of the forms to achieve it is a single life. In addition, the policy of the last decades, aimed at giving priority to the birth of boys in families, has generated a demographic imbalance: there are 10 guys per 8 girls there. So, there are definitely a lot of bachelors in society who, nevertheless, dream of having a family, therefore they celebrate 11.11 very violently, walking around restaurants with friends, participating in blind dates and ... buying everything.

And the point is not that around sales and discounts, but that this is what tradition demands. The word bachelor (in Chinese translated as "bare stick") consists of two hieroglyphs, the first of which has the meaning "completely, cleaned, without a trace." That is why lonely men strive to pull out all their pocket money on this day, drink and eat everything at home, dine in a restaurant, buy new goods. Otherwise, superstitious Chinese are sure that fate in the new year will not be very favorable for them and they are unlikely to meet their life companion. Of course, this fact could not be overlooked by cunning marketers, and in the late 2010s, with the light hand of Chinese retail, international shopping day appeared - 11.11. Today, it was even adopted by Russian and Western stores, and, above all, online retailers.

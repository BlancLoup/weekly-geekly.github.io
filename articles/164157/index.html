App Annie: analysis of the mobile market in Russia

Analytical company App Annie has published statistics on the Russian mobile market. It contains data on the growth in the number of downloads and revenues from the App Store and Google Play in Russia and a comparison of these indicators with other countries of the world.



Today, the Russian mobile market is one of the most dynamic in the world. This is confirmed by a recent report by Distimo and a recent analyst from App Annie.

During the period under review (from January to September 2012) the number of downloads in the Russian Google Play increased by 66%. The growth rate of the number of downloads from the Russian App Store has an indicator of 2 times less - 31%. At the same time, two thirds of the total number of downloads out of two platforms on Google Play.


According to the dynamics of revenue, Google Play also bypasses the App Store - 179% versus 42%, but in monetary terms it is still negligible compared to how much the apple store brings.



If you look at the entire global mobile market as a whole, then Russia in the App Store accounts for only 2% of the total number of downloads and the same amount of total revenues. For comparison, the US share of profits is 35%, Europe 24%, the Pacific region accounts for 27%, and the rest of the world 12%.



As for Google Play, here Russia has the same 2% of world income, but already with a share of downloads of 4%. Almost all main profits are distributed between US markets (32% share) and the Pacific region (almost half - 46%), while Europe accounts for 15%, and all other countries of the world - only 5% of income.



With such growth figures for mobile app stores, Russia was able to enter the top 10 in both downloads and revenue.



And in terms of revenue growth with iOS, our country is already in fifth place. Only Brazil, Japan, Thailand and Sweden have higher rates.



As for the growth rate of revenue from Google Play, here Russia is not in the top 10. In the first place - South Korea, then - Japan and South Africa.

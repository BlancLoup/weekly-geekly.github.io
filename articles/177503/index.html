Wordpress.com Moved to Free Open Sans Font



It seems that the free Open Sans font is becoming a new standard for the web, and not just for the web. This neutral font looks good in mobile applications and when printing. Another major site that abandoned the old Helvetica / Arial in favor of Open Sans was WordPress.com .

Open Sans - a font created by Steve Matteson (Steve Matteson), distributed under the Apache license. Contains 897 characters, including ISO Latin 1, Latin CE, Greek and Cyrillic.

Open Sans has long been in first place in the Google Fonts free fonts directory and continues to widen its gap. For comparison, over the past week he has almost 2 billion calls through the API, while the nearest pursuer Oswald has only 700 million.

WordPress.com developers suggest comparing what the About Us page looks like in the old version with Helvetica / Arial, and in the new version with Open Sans.

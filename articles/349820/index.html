In 2018, in the history of the Unified State Automated Information System a new stage begins - the obligatory blot counting of alcohol products. Preparations for the transition to the new scheme of work has already started. Since September 2017, the composition of documents and requisites of the Unified State Automated Information System among wholesalers has been systematically changing. Now it's time and retailers. How to automate alcohol retail under the new rules, we consider in detail in this article.



Why there is a need to upgrade EGAIS 2.0


The first representatives of the wholesale segment - manufacturers and importers who work with large quantities of products - began working at USAIS. For the account used excise stamps, which were issued in series or ranges of codes.

In the old version of the Unified State Automated Information System 2.0 each number of alcohol was issued the number of excise stamps corresponding to the number of bottles produced. Excises were not tied to a specific bottle, but to a batch released. The company‚Äôs account indicated the number of bottles issued for a specific lot and the range of excise stamp codes. That is, each bottle from the lot corresponded to a specific code from the specified excise range, but it was not clear which one.

And what happened in retail with such a scheme? The same bottle could be repeatedly resold and be on the balance at once in several retail outlets. As a result, whoever first scanned such a bottle was lucky, and in another store it could have been found counterfeit, with all the consequences.
After a year of such ‚Äúdances with tambourines‚Äù, it became clear that working in such a format does not suit the retail trade representatives at all. The need to create a new retail coordination mechanism has become apparent.

So, the chronology of the introduction and development of EGAIS in the Russian market is as follows:


  • From January 1, 2016 - retail outlets in urban and rural settlements are obliged to confirm the fact of purchase (acceptance) of alcoholic beverages in the EGAIS system.
  • From July 1, 2016 - the fixation of the sale fact in the EGAIS began without fail.
  • From 10/1/2016 - the alignment of the residues was taken under control.
  • From 01/01/2017 - FS PAP begins to control the remains of alcoholic beverages.
  • 01.03.2018 - the transition of the document flow on EGAIS to version 3.0 is planned
  • On July 1, 2018, a full transition to a blot accounting of alcohol in retail is planned.


Brown alcohol accounting in retail will be introduced from July 2018

What are the deadlines set for the transition to EGAIS 3.0


  • January 15 - February 1, 2018 - the preparatory stage of implementation, during which retail outlets will have to indicate when writing off an individual bar code for each bottle. The goods remaining in the balance until 15.01.2018 will be taken into account according to the old scheme until full implementation, but with the stipulation about the limitation of the shelf life.
  • February 1 - July 1, 2018 - mixed accounting scheme. The stores will begin to receive products on blot accounting, which should be written off in the same way. Writing off the rest of the goods is allowed as before.
  • From July 1, 2018 - the movement of all alcoholic beverages from the manufacturer to the buyer will be carried out on the basis of the mark accounting. If this requirement is not observed, sellers may lose their alcohol license.



FS RAR pushed back the timeline for the implementation of EGAIS 3.0, so that entrepreneurs have time to prepare

How EGAIS 3.0 will work


In the version of the EGAIS 3.0 protocol, mechanisms are introduced for tracking the legality of alcohol not only in the wholesale supply sector, but also in retail. Now there will be a bloc counting of each bottle, and this becomes a prerequisite when carrying out operations with alcohol:

  • acceptance;
  • inventory;
  • move.

In the accounting of an enterprise, appropriate forms of documents are introduced for the registration of all business processes. Of course, this will complicate the work, since blot accounting requires more time and effort. Imagine the process of blot acceptance and distribution of trucks alcoholic beverages. In this case, the deadline for acceptance of the goods in accordance with the Unified State Automated Information System has not been canceled (up to 3 days).


In order to fulfill the requirement of the FS PAP for a booster alcohol counting, you must scan each excise stamp on the bottle.

What should entrepreneurs do?


In order to fulfill the requirements of the FS PAP on blot counting, retail representatives need to read two-dimensional bar-aggregators with the help of scanning equipment with which suppliers will equip the packaging of their products. Bar code aggregation implies that a single bar code will be formed for any team of alcohol groups (box, pallet, etc.). Using this precast barcode, by scanning, you can identify all excise stamps that make up the lot.
But, when the box or pallet is disbanded, it will be necessary to read the code of each bottle, i.e. take into account the alcoholic products in blots. And one thing is if it is a small store with a small assortment, where it is possible to work individually with each bottle, even without an aggregated bar code, and another thing is if you have a supermarket with a large flow of goods and throughput.


When breaking up boxes or pallets with alcohol, the retail store must perform a blot check on each bottle before putting it on the shelf in the sales area.

How to be, where to start


The first and most obvious is that you need good, efficient scanning equipment. Accounting accounting requires more effort and time, and if the technique will "stupid" with each scan, it will not be good. The best solution would be a data collection terminal for USAIS . It is worth saying that there is no specialized TSD for USAIS; any terminal with a 2D scanner that reads two-dimensional barcodes will do. You can even use a mobile phone, but we would not advise if you have more than a couple dozen bottles of alcohol a day.

Hand-held scanner or TSD for USAIS: if there is no difference, why pay more?


There is a difference and, in terms of functionality, significant.
The usual 2D scanner will scan the excise stamp without problems, but:
  • not able to process a large amount of information due to binding to the workplace and insufficient functionality;
  • does not perform validation checks (during the transitional period, when the use of alcohol on batch accounts is still allowed);
  • does not have feedback from the staff, which leads to many errors due to the fault of the human factor.

That is why barcode scanners are only suitable for small volumes. They can still be used in small retail outlets or in public catering, but in the future it is still better to switch to the TSD in order to optimize work in the USAIS. The data collection terminal is a multifunctional device, a mobile computer that can be used not only on alcohol acceptance, but also in the sales area for inventory. And, of course, its main advantage is that various mobile software is installed on it.

To work with EGAIS 3.0, it is enough to purchase a TSD with a two-dimensional scanner.

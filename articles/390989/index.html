Fairphone 2 Modular Phone Now Available


Photo: dailymail.co.uk

While Google is worn with its Project Ara, while LG is demonstrating the modular architecture of the G5 smartphone at the MWC in Barcelona, ‚Äã‚ÄãFairphone developers have begun to sell their phone. What the company itself calls the ‚Äúfirst real modular phone‚Äù can already be bought. Those lucky people who managed to test the device say that the display can be replaced in just 23 seconds.

Olivier Gebert's CTO (Olivier Hebert) has demonstrated how easy it is to disassemble a phone into its component parts. The main modules here are the battery, housing, display. In the lower module are the microphone, speaker, vibration and USB port. Each module is marked so that the user immediately understands what is in front of him.

The main goal of the developers was to create a phone that is easy to repair, plus to allow the owner to replace the modules with newer / more powerful ones. All modules, according to company representatives, can be purchased on the Web, so there will be no problems with the replacement. Something similar does Google, as mentioned above. But Project Ara has been developing for a couple of years (although the modular structure of this gadget is more advanced), but Fairphone 2 is a reality.



The same display can be replaced in a maximum of a minute without contacting service centers or private craftsmen. Gebert himself was able to replace the screen in 23 seconds, removing the cover, removing the battery and setting the new display in place. Some parts of the phone are fastened with screws, which are unscrewed with any Philips-standard screwdriver.

Parts are fairly cheap. Thus, the battery costs about 20 euros, the display - 87 euros and the camera 35 euros. The size of the display is 5 inches, it is HD LCD with Gorilla Glass and a resolution of 446 ppi. Camera - 8 MP. The OS used is Android Lollipop 5.1.

Inside - a quad-core processor from Qualcomm, 2 GB of RAM, 32 GB of internal memory and the ability to add another 64 GB. There are two SIM card slots that will be useful for travelers and lovers to talk with subscribers of various operators. Rubberized housing.



The price of such a phone in Europe is 525 euros.



By the way, the phone recently received 10 out of 10 on the iFixit guys . iFixit was able to find out that the board uses Qualcomm Snapdragon 801 MSM8974AB and Samsung K3QF2F20EM 2 GB LPDDR4 chips. Plus, Samsung KLMBG4WEBC flash memory, Qualcomm WCN3680B, Qualcomm QFE1100 and other elements are also found there.

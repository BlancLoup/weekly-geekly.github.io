But who needs my data!


Guys, we have bad news for you - each of us has a data carrier, which sooner or later someone will need.

  • Personal data (passport data, mail, phone number) is also very valuable information. Smash Google Thunder!


Case one in a million. Not one million ... Dollars ... Google announced the irrevocable destruction of a small part of the data due to a lightning hit the Google data center in Belgium. August 13, 2015 - Lightning struck the data center power system four times in a row. This briefly de-energized the data processing center, in which the client virtual machines were running using the Google Cloud Platform (GCP) and storing their data there. Google experts noted that in the period from August 13 to 17, 2015, virtual machines had I / O errors when trying to access network drives connected to them. In general, at least one I / O error occurred in approximately 5% of network drives in the European zone. In addition, during this period, it was not possible to perform some management operations, such as creating snapshots. For Google, this is not such a big part of the data, but what were the companies whose information and processes suffered. Now they know that there are lightning in the clouds.

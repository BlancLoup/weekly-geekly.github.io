Lectorium launches MOOC direction

Friends! We went to this damn long 5 years. LETI, ITMO, Polytech and EUSPb launch their first online courses on the Lectorium. So, we open registration for the following courses:

Engineering


St. Petersburg State Polytechnic University (SPbGPU)
Posted by: Igor Asonov iason



This course is for people who have a craving for physics or mathematics or computer science or creating something with their own hands, but at the same time there is a fear of the word "engineer". This course will tell and show what engineering professions are and how they are paid, who is such an engineer by nature and what are his tasks in his daily activities.
Detailed course description on Lectorium.

Bionics. Nano cyborgs


St. Petersburg State Electrotechnical University "LETI"
Author: Angela Andreeva



This course is an opportunity to acquire knowledge in one of the most sought after and paid fields of modern science. What does bionics do? This is an actively developing scientific and technical area, in essence, uniting biology and technology. Man has long borrowed his ideas from Nature in everyday life, but bionics embodies them in design solutions and information technology. Cybernetic mechanisms, artificial organs, smart skin, new materials that copy and, at times, surpass natural analogs in their properties - all these are realities of modern scientific high-tech production. At present, bionics has received a significant impetus to the new development with the advent of nanotechnology and the minimization of the objects being created. Bionics leads the development of medicine, electronics, military industry ... As part of our course, you will learn about the latest developments in the field of nanoelectronics, the creation of nanorobots and cyborgs.
Detailed course description on Lectorium

Theories of money. From shell to bitcoin.


European University at St. Petersburg
Posted by: Julia Vymyatnina



This is a course about the phenomenon that each of us faces daily, which people all over the world often and with anxiety argue, as it has become an integral part of our life (especially in the city!) - about money ... During the course you will understand how Economists determine what can be considered money and how their value is determined. Is such a necessary condition - gold or silver shine, in order to have undeniable value in market relations? You will also learn about the history of the appearance of money. You learn what was used in different cultures as money, and how their subject image changed. Theories of money presented in our course are both widely known and rarely mentioned. You will become familiar with their main points and representatives. You will see how the ideas of the classics contribute to solving modern economic problems and crises. And also learn the recommendations on the conduct of monetary policy, which can give these theories. And at the end of the course we will try to clarify the essence of the most recent phenomena of the monetary sphere, each day taking possession of our world - electronic money, bitcoins, etc.
Detailed course description on Lectorium.

Heavenly Mechanics


St. Petersburg National Research University of Information Technologies, Mechanics and Optics (NRU ITMO)
Author: Alexander Chirtsov



To date, physics has already made quite a few discoveries that have allowed modern technologies to send humans into space, launch artificial satellites into near-earth orbit, and calculate the positions of the neighboring planets in our solar system. But on what foundation are these achievements? The calculation of the motion of celestial bodies involved in celestial mechanics. Knowledge of the laws discovered by Newton is still a necessary condition for solving problems of a cosmic scale, in the most direct sense of this expression. In the course you will get the classic knowledge that makes possible the launch of interplanetary ships and allow researchers and engineers all over the world to advance science. This six-week course will introduce you to the basic internal laws governing space. Celestial mechanics is one of the most interesting branches of physics, where you can start exploring right now.
Detailed course description on Lectorium.

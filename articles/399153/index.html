About picture quality. HDR and 4K


Sony is offended when reviewers say that all that is in PS4 Pro is 4K. From a firm's point of view, HDR technology is almost as important here, it's just harder to evaluate. The bottom line is that with the new image processing technology, the image becomes more realistic, almost like in real life. The black color becomes really black rather than grayish, other colors have more shades and nuances. In general, Sony talked a lot about HDR at its conference, and it is obvious that the technology is good - all TV manufacturers are now introducing it. But in practice, the untrained eye is unlikely to notice the differences. Even if you put the screens next to each other, the brain does not feel big changes.

4K is still much more visible, especially when viewed on a 4K TV. Objects in the distance look much clearer, you can discern fine details in the grass and trees, you can see some wrinkles on the faces of the characters. True, as soon as the dynamic scene begins, and chaos begins to occur on the screen, the advantages of high definition also become invisible.

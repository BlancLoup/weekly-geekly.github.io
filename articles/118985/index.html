Recording capability


Initially, I did not think that the possibility of recording was so interesting. But I was wrong. This turned out to be the main advantage, which is why my wife and I decided to leave the television from the biline in our house.



The joke is that there are quite a lot of good movies and cartoons that are shown on TV, but they don‚Äôt always go to the moment when we sit in front of the TV set. By recording a film of interest you can watch it at a convenient time.



Now we usually look at the program on Friday for the next weekend and put on the record 2-4 films, and then watch them in our spare time. Some erase, some save. For each recorded movie, you can set the attribute:



- save up to the requirement of a place
- save until I delete it myself.



We already have a collection of dozens of full-length cartoons that children love to watch. Movies for ourselves, we usually delete after watching.




How to record


At first, I thought that Beeline cable television had some kind of signal that is transmitted to a decoder, which means that the program has started, and the same signal about the end. It would be very convenient to start watching the recording exactly from the moment it started, exactly until the moment it was completed. But everything turned out to be easier - the recording starts 5 minutes before the start of the scheduled time of the program. And it also ends 5 minutes after the planned completion.

This is not always good. For example, at the time of the March daylight saving time, almost all the channels had problems over time and I could not record the film ‚ÄúCruel Romance‚Äù to my wife. Recording began an hour after the movie began. Right from the moment when our mustache friend began to sing his famous song about the bumblebee. Nightmare!

Some channels do not strictly follow their own program schedule. For example, the children's channel Nickelodeon constantly sins. And we just write to children a few cartoon series.

And often there are some overlays and films start to show later, and often you watch the movie and break off hard at the very end, when you do not know how it all ended. Often this happens on the first channel on Saturday. So recently showed "The Expendables." I recorded. And, imagine, the recording ended right at the moment when the whole team was sitting in a palace surrounded - neither enter nor exit. The most apotheosis! Horror.

On the other hand, it's not so scary. Failures are very rare. And after the abolition of the transition to winter and summer time, it probably will not appear at all! Overwhelmingly, all movies and programs are recorded on time. Yes, and you can configure the record so that it turns off after 15 or even 30 minutes after the planned completion. Now I always put films on record with a margin, so there are no problems.

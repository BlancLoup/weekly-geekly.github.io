Tips - how to understand more, remember longer

We all want to become professionals in our field as soon as possible. We all want to have a phenomenal memory, to remember everything from the first time, to perceive new technologies on the fly.

Owning several cunning principles we can achieve this. To do this, we do not need to have a phenomenal memory from birth.

The post decided to write briefly and to the point, without lengthy explanations and examples. Why do you need them? Cunning is easy to try in practice. Did not help? Forget it. Helped? Always use.



That's what helped and helps me. I hope you will be somehow useful. Part of the advice repeats the already stated in similar articles.

1. Outline If this is a new multi-faceted concept - first think on paper / typing, not limiting yourself in anything. Draw a memory card. And then use Evernote, quizlet and even Anki to form a new fact in the form of a short Question / Answer. 2. Record yourself on the recorder. Turn on the recorder and talk about what you have learned. The record can then be erased. The fact itself is important - it seems to you that you are speaking, listening to you, starting to formulate your thoughts in a different way. It promotes in-depth study.
You can even turn on the voice recorder on your mobile phone, walk down the street / stand in line and broadcast. People will think that you are talking on the phone, you will not embarrass anyone.

3. Share new knowledge with others. It is not necessary to share in the "professor" - "student" mode. You can ponder with the interlocutor, ask his opinion. This should be done on Skype, in person, and even by phone (which is probably the most difficult).

4. Continually expand your social circle. Find "colleagues", ask them about their professional development, ask clarifying questions. With tools such as Skype or social network, you can simultaneously maintain correspondence with many interesting people. Your horizons will reactively expand and will help you out even once.

5. Read, scanning material. No time to explore all that I wanted. You can quickly run through the articles on the textbook. Some pieces will be stored in the memory. Later, when you stumble upon a problem, associative thinking will tell you that "somewhere you have already seen the solution." This gives confidence that the problem is solved, removes the state of vacuum, when there is a problem and there are absolutely no thoughts on how to approach it and whether it can be solved at all.

6. Read in transport / in line / for any waiting. It's amazing how much time we spend traveling to and from work, standing in lines and so on. If you spend 2 hours a day on the road, then a week is 10 hours (more than a working day!), And 40 hours a month (a working week!). Imagine how much you can learn during this time.
If you are traveling by car, listen to podcasts and radio.

7. While learning, sentence to yourself or out loud the emotional remarks of the movie characters. "Let's see what we have here!", "Here's your grandmother, and St. George's Day!" (examples from the ceiling). This will quickly give your learning an emotional touch, lift your spirits, and speed up the perception of the material.

Speech Recognition with CMU Sphinx

CMU Sphinx is now the largest human speech recognition project. The toolkit includes the following programs and libraries:

  • Pocketsphinx is a small program that accepts arbitrary acoustic models, grammars and dictionaries as well as an audio stream (either an audio file, or it itself takes a stream from a microphone). The output is recognized text. Written in C, works fast.
  • Sphinxbase - the library necessary for Pocketsphinx
  • Sphinx4 is a flexible recognition library written in Java.
  • Sphinxtrain - a program for learning acoustic models.

To work with CMU Sphinx, it is important to remember a few definitions and understand their differences.

  • Acoustic model - is responsible for comparing the sound of the spoken phoneme. Acoustic model for the Russian language can be downloaded on the project website. Russian acoustic and language models. And also a dictionary.
  • A dictionary is a file in which tokens and phonemes are associated (the word and its transcription). For example, a calculator (k ay ll ku ll ja t ay r). It is necessary to convert the phonemes recognized by the acoustic model into lexemes.
  • Grammar is a formal rule that describes simple rules for constructing sentences. The tokens obtained in the previous step try to match the grammar and, if successful, the result is displayed.
  • A language model is a statistical model of a language. It describes the probabilities of words and their combinations. Thus, the recognition of tokens is the maximization of the likelihood of a recognized phrase.

The more complex the language, the more extensive the rules and the size of the dictionary, the worse the recognition accuracy. Therefore, to minimize the error, it makes sense to create simplified rules that will describe a specific task.

StatCounter: Google Chrome share exceeded 20%


According to a StatCounter study published July 1, the share of Google Chrome in the global market for the first time exceeded 20%.

In particular, now the share of the browser of the search giant is 20, 7%, and in June 2009 it occupied only 2.8%. It is curious that Chrome is most popular in South America, where in April it took the second position with 29.7%, leaving Internet Explorer ahead of itself at 44%, but overtaking Firefox, which has a 24.6% share there.

Firefox globally lowered the bar slightly - it now has a 28% share, whereas two years ago it occupied 30% of the global market.

The most significant fall was demonstrated by Internet Explorer; since 2009, its share has fallen by 15% from 59 to 44%.

Opera Browser, popular in the CIS, having reduced its share at the end of 2009 from 3.3% to 1.8%, has since demonstrated a rare stability, balancing an average of 1.7%.

Safari browser, by contrast, over the past two years, slightly, but increasing its share, possessing at the moment 5% of the global browser market.

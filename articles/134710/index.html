Crowdsourcing game


A beautiful example of network influence on the market is the Imaginarium toy. The story began like this: there is a fairly popular Dixit, which is published in Europe. The principle of the game is very simple: you need to take a card with a psychedelic drawing, make an association on it and put it on the table. Each of the players looks at their picture cards, finds a suitable association, and also puts it on the table. Then the cards are shuffled, and the players are trying to understand what exactly the master made from this. The stuff is very creative, fun and great for warm friendly meetings.

In Russia, there is an elite community of people who love to play it. At some point, these people understand that they need to make their own game, and begin to develop a project. Network crowdsourcing is involved in drawing pictures: as a result, many artists do the work that is included in the final version. The game is closed with a lid, supplied with a bunch of easter eggs - and it starts up in print, and very quickly, literally in a couple of months from zero to the fresh run. This is how Imaginarium appeared. If interested, an earlier crowdsourcing project of this team looks like this .

mikhanoid : What should be the ideal operating system for a business?

As always with answers to such questions, I will say it depends on a lot. The company must choose the operating system and the set of software that is most adequate to solve its problems. Factory, shop, bank - they can all use completely different software. In one case, it is important to conduct transactions, in the other - interaction with the buyer, etc.

mikhanoid : Which office suite in the history of these packages is the most successful and why?

I'm not a fan of any particular integrated package. At one time, the company I worked for used three different software packages: one for the needs of an engineering company, the other for the needs of our small companies scattered around the city, and another one for our 17 hospitals.

No perfect software package. The root of the problem is the difference in horizontal and vertical integration. If you use software that can do everything, it is standardized by definition, i.e. It will not allow to solve any specific tasks, but to consistently solve standard ones is horizontal integration. On the other hand, there is vertical integration - best-in-class systems that cope with specific tasks, but when there is a question about their integration with other systems, problems arise. The confrontation of these approaches will exist forever.

I believe that for non-core business operations it is worth using the most economical software package with the possibility of full horizontal integration. To solve the basic problems is to find the best software.

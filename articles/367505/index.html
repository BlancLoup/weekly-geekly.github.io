Nintendo NES for $ 500: games from childhood on 1080x720 screens

An Analogue Nt is available for preorder right now: this is the Nintendo Entertainment System in an aluminum case. The game industry icon costs $ 499. For an extra $ 49, you can choose one of three non-standard colors. Another 79 dollars - and you get an HDMI adapter that allows you to play on 1080p screens.



Nintendo released the Nintendo Entertainment System in 1983, a third-generation gaming console. The company has become a world market leader. In Japan, the console was sold under the brand Famicom, and in Russia it became known as a clone called Dendy. Dendy was presented to me on New Year's Eve from 1995 to 1996. I am sure many of us have similar memories.

At that, Analogue Interactive decided to play, offering to buy the console in an updated design, but with familiar functionality, at the price of Sony PlayStation 4: it costs $ 499. HDMI adapter will cost $ 79 more, in addition you will have to pay money for joysticks.



There are several options for body color.



The console is compatible with all accessories of both the NES and Famicom Disk System.

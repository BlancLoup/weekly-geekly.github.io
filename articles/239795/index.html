Ubisoft opens office in Moscow


In the meantime, as many large foreign companies leave Russia due to the fact that they do not see prospects for development or have been affected by sanctions, Ubisoft announces the opening of an office in Moscow. This news was officially announced at the exhibition "Igromir-2014". This will help Ubisoft to gain a foothold in the fast-growing Russian gaming market, and, as European director Alan Corre noted, ‚Äúthe Moscow office is a tribute to Russian players who are not indifferent, active and committed to their favorite games.‚Äù The office of Ubisoft in Moscow will be headed by Olga Lazareva, who in the past was responsible for the development of Electronic Arts in Russia.

It is also planned to provide all products with high-quality information and technical support in Russian. Disney closes mobile projects for the sake of the Clash race


Recently it became known that Disney removed from the free access to the App Store and Google Play two games about the Star Wars: Tiny Death Star and Star Wars Assault Team universe. This step was taken shortly after the release of the next game about the Star Wars universe - the Star Wars Commander, we recall that this is a variation on the theme of Clash of Clans.

Obviously, Disney decided to focus on the new project, given that its financial performance is much higher. By removing these games, Disney got rid of ‚Äúunnecessary‚Äù support and slightly increases the flow of users to the game they need.

Tiny Death Star has been in stores for less than a year, and the Star Wars Assault Team was released in late March of this year.

Disney has not officially commented on its decision, Nimblebit warned other developers that if they are looking for partnerships with Disney, then they should be very thoughtful.

The penetration of smartphones in Russia is growing, but using them less


According to a study by Deloitte , in 2014, smartphones significantly increased their popularity among Russians, penetration into the masses increased from 47% in 2013 to 66% in 2014, and smartphones finally surpassed ordinary phones in popularity.

However, according to the results of the same study, the use of smartphone features became noticeably less.

Thus, the share of respondents who have never downloaded applications on their phone has increased from 30% in 2013 to 41% in this.

The share of those who spend more than 200 rubles on applications per month has decreased from 9% last year to 8% this year.

Let's take a closer look at these numbers, and it will become clear that not everything is so bad. In absolute numbers, the share of both downloads and payers undoubtedly increased, but not as fast as the popularity of smartphones themselves grows.

We will not make premature conclusions, we will see what time will tell, although there is still the certainty that as you use more and more people will both download applications and pay in them.

There are first people who are not physically compatible with high-tech

Mobile phones, cars stuffed with electronics, computers and other devices have become so firmly established in our life that it is difficult to imagine a person who is allergic to them. Unhappy Debbie Bird physically can not be in a modern city. Her body is too sensitive to electromagnetic radiation.



The body of the 39-year-old British woman painfully reacts to the electromagnetic radiation of a certain spectrum, which comes from almost all modern electronics, including computers, mobile and radio telephones, microwave ovens and even some cars.

Under the influence of radiation, Debbie Bird's skin turns red, his head starts to ache, his blood knocks in his temples, and his eyelids swell three times in volume: this happens every time when a mobile phone, computer, or even a simple radiophone starts to work. Debbie Bird and her husband, Tony, threw all the "fonishing" devices out of the house. They painted the walls black carbon paint (¬£ 250 per can), closed the windows with a special film and even sleep under a silver-coated grid that reflects radio waves.

Spouses had to buy an expensive plasma TV, because CRT television receivers caused a painful reaction to Debbie. The same thing happens every time she gets into her friend's modern BMW. Now she can't even go shopping, because WiFi transmitters are everywhere there. Life is becoming more difficult, because the radiating devices around are becoming more and more.

According to experts, about 500 people in the UK suffer greatly from increased electromagnetic sensitivity, and in every twentieth person electromagnetic waves cause sleep disturbances and headaches (a weak form of the disease). This is a new disease that appeared in the XXI century. It has not yet been studied and is not recognized by official medicine.

Announcement of ThinkJava # 3. Microservices



Friends, we invite everyone to attend our third ThinkJava meeting. This time we fully dedicate it to the topic of microservices: what it is, how to start using them, what problems you may encounter and how to solve them, and how to ensure the security of the microservice architecture application.

The last few years, microservices have remained one of the most relevant approaches to the development of large applications. Last fall, experts from NIX Solutions studied all the nuances of microservice architecture at the largest international industry conference Spring One 2GX. Since then, our developers have managed to try out a lot of what they heard in practice, experiment with microservices, step on some rakes, learn to avoid mistakes or eliminate their consequences without loss. And at the same time they mastered various microservice application development tools.

At the third ThinkJava mini-conference, our speakers will share their knowledge and experience in working with microservices. You are waiting for two reports with answers to the most pressing questions today:

Microservices and their friends


Speakers : Ivan Krivorotenko and Vlad Stus



  • What are these microservices that everyone is talking about?
  • What are their advantages over the traditional approach to application development?
  • The interaction between the services, the problems and typical tasks that arise at the same time, as well as some ready-made solutions that need only be properly applied.

How to feel safe when using microservices


Speaker : Vitaliy Tsymbalyuk



Security issues in large corporate applications are always in the foreground, and in a monolithic architecture are solved quite uniformly, since the application is a single entity. But when we turn to microservice architecture, difficulties begin. After all, in fact, we are dealing with several applications, access to which must be controlled. In the report we will discuss:

  • What are the approaches to security of microservice applications?
  • Their advantages and disadvantages;
  • Is there an ideal solution that makes you feel safe with minimum effort?

So, in the ThinkJava menu # 3:
  • discussion of the nuances of developing microservice applications;
  • answers to your questions on the topic;
  • sweet coffee breaks that will make your hot discussions even brighter and more pleasant;
  • presentation of Dyukov to speakers;
  • Testing a new site for the conference - the factory hub.

We hope our meetings will take place here in an even more comfortable and cozy atmosphere.

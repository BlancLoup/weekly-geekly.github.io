GosLinux walks in the country: FSSP reported on the costs of implementing and using the Russian OS

For 3 years, the Federal Bailiffs Service (FSSP) spent 21 million rubles on the implementation and maintenance of a free Linux distribution (19 million), as well as training administrators (2 million).

GosLinux is open source software that the agency introduced in the period from 2013 to 2015. The specific cost of one installed version of this OS is now less than 1.5 thousand rubles, said FSSP Deputy Director Tatyana Ignatieva in the Presidential Administration at a working meeting on import substitution.

GosLinux is a standard Linux distribution kit specially created according to the requirements of the FSSP with built-in information protection tools. Its development was carried out during 2013 on the basis of the CentOS 6.4 distribution, which, in turn, is based on the commercial distribution of Red Hat Enterprise Linux . The developer of the distribution was Red Soft, which won in March 2013 in the competition for the revision, implementation and maintenance of AIS FSSP subsystems. The amount of the state contract amounted to 118.15 million rubles.

According to Ignatieva, the main difference between GosLinux OS and other domestic distributions is the fact that its holder is the Russian Federation represented by the FSSP, and not a commercial organization. And the distribution of GosLinux in state authorities is free.

The distribution kit has already been transferred for operation to representatives of the Nizhny Novgorod, Volgograd and Yaroslavl regions. It is also planned to launch in Pereyaslavl, reports CNews with reference to the deputy director of the FSSP.

The Federal Bailiff Service (FSSP) of Russia can be considered a veteran in the application of free software (OSS), since it was still in 2013, when there was no question of import substitution, started creating its own information system based on Open Source AIS FSSP of Russia , the development of which was performed by an external contractor.

Yegor Vasilyev, deputy head of the ITM on information security issues, explained that interest in Open Source was primarily due to economic considerations: ‚ÄúOur service was previously allocated much less funds than other agencies, and last year the IT budget was significantly reduced compared to 2014 ".

According to Yegor Vasilyev, back in 2013, it was decided to use a free OS for all bailiffs and management personnel in order not to pay for installing software for 40 thousand workplaces and 2.5 thousand servers.

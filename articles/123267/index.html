For many, yes, at first glance - a normal voice recorder, well, he writes, well, files are sent to the mail - so anyone, even the standard one does it. This whole functionality, as well as just the ability to record, is understandable. But not for this, we developed the application. I myself needed a voice recorder somehow to record reports at conferences, and I wanted to add tags to the record, search for them, and put a label on the map.



Anything more I needed was not. But, to my surprise - this was not found. In many, it was possible to process the sound directly in the application, lay it out in daddies - but it was impossible to add tags, there was no trivial geocaching either. So we decided to make our application with uh ... tags and a map. And they called it the same way in captain :)



What does it do? I will give a description from iTunes.

- Sound recording with noise reduction and the ability to pause
- Support multitasking - recording sound in a minimized state!
- For each entry, you can add an infinite number of tags, both in the recording time and during editing.
- Positioning based on GPS, GSM or WiFi data
- Search by name or tags.
- Submission of records on the map for more convenient access to records by location
- Simple play of files with pause and rewind
- Ability to add your own description to any record
- Built-in sending emails with attached files (up to 15 MB)
- Integration with Dropbox.com - the ability to directly from the application to download the record in your Dropbox-account. The file will be saved in the Tagged Recorder folder.
- Sharing a folder of records over WiFi - files can be accessed from any device on the network using a browser. You can download any entry to your computer.

WWDC banners over the past 5 years

2007
Apple announced the iPhone in January 2007. WWDC 2007 began on June 11, the developers hoped that the company was announcing the SDK, but Apple did not do this, and allowed to develop only Web applications for Safari.











2008
After a one-year ban on the development of native applications for third-party developers, Apple announced the iPhone SDK at WWDC 2008 along with the new iPhone 3G. MobileMe and Mac rebranding was also announced.









2009
Apple announced the launch of iPhone 3GS and iOS 3.0. Macbook Pro has received updates.













2010
2010 was the year when Apple celebrated a huge number of iPhone applications. They built a huge wall from the iPad, where it was shown how often individual applications are loaded. At WWDC 2010, iPhone 4 and FaceTime were announced.











2011
Tickets for WWDC 2011 were sold in just 12 hours. Apple showed OS X 10.7 (Lion), iOS 5 and iCloud. This was the last Apple presentation that Steve Jobs almost entirely held.

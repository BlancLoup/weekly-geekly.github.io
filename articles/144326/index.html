EMC bought Syncplicity


Today it became known that such a giant as EMC , the creator of Symmetrix and the owner of VMWare, bought a startup Syncplicity .



For those who have not heard of Syncplicity, in two words:
Syncplicity is, until recently, a weak competitor to Dropbox. The main functional difference for private users is the ability to synchronize any folder, which is very convenient. For corporate clients, its capabilities greatly exceed Dropbox.
Syncplicity also has clients for almost all popular platforms.

Now this startup can be considered as a competitor to Dropbox number one, since EMC chose him among dozens of others.

The amount of the transaction was not disclosed, but it is known that at the moment Syncplicity has more than 25,000 paid subscribers, which is not bad.

By the way, EMC already owns Octopus, which is similar in functionality, which can not but suggest that competitors will be eliminated, but still hope for a bright future for Syncplicity.

The news source is Syncplicity's blog .

Aerial photography in the campaign. Unusual review quadcopter Cheerson CX-20

About Cheerson CX-20 has already been written a lot of reviews, so I will not be repeated and tell you about its "combat" use.

I am one of those who prefer active rest, and this year I had two campaigns planned: Svaneti (Georgia) and Kachkar climbing (Turkey). In the amount of 25 days of rest. In such events with me, GoPro is always with me, but at some point I wanted to add even more unusual angles and I purchased a quadrocopter and four spare batteries for it.



Training


After the story of the importation of a quadrocopter to Ukraine , I decided not to tempt fate and packed it in a backpack so as not to shine it once again. He wrapped it in a sleeping bag in such a way that the pressure was distributed over the entire surface, thereby minimizing the chance of damage during transportation.



Remote control, batteries and other components also put in a backpack.
Having settled in Kutaisi Hotel, I first disassembled the backpack and checked the integrity of the quadcopter. He was all right.

Svaneti (Georgia)


Day 1 , the evening before dinner. The wind was not strong and I decided to make the first test flight, took out a quadrocopter and found a crack on the protruding antenna (there is an electronic compass in it). The compass itself was not damaged and I successfully made some photos of our camp, after which the wind rose and I had to stop the flight.





Day 2 , the hidden function of grazing cows. For the night we settled on a large glade on the slope. The place turned out to be ideal for flying, but even today I was upset, on the quadcopter the single ‚Äúwing‚Äù snapped. The injury was not fatal and I managed to fly successfully. First of all, I checked the flight data without a camera. The drone behaved obediently, I even managed to chase the cows (they obviously were not thrilled by the flying friend).



The first flight with the camera ended in a fall, as with an additional load, the reaction to the gas stick was slowed down.







For the next days I wore a quadrocopter on top of the backpack to avoid further damage.



Day 5 , Lake Koruldi and Mestia lookout. In the radial access to the lake, I took a quadrocopter with me, I had a great idea for a video. But unfortunately, the video was too dark. Most likely this is due to the sun shining in the lens, although it was overcast.





I made another attempt to fly on the observation deck over Mestia. This flight also failed due to strong wind, but I still got a couple of good shots.





Day 6 , launch from the Svan tower. Before lunch, we looked at Mestia, climbed onto the roof of one of the towers. In search of interesting shots, I launched a quadrocopter from the roof, it was difficult to fly, as the gusts of wind constantly blew it. He had to catch him back because the roof was small and sloping.



Day 8 , long-time pass and broken compass. With the first rays of the sun, we began to wade through thickets of grass and rhododendrons to the pass. On the other side of the pass, the slope was much steeper. From the vegetation there was only grass, which had to be held in hands. Already at the very bottom I stumbled and swayed forward, thereby holding the quadcopter between the backpack and the head, at that moment the remote antenna broke, the day before I had already pasted it.

Day 9 , a mountain lake on the Svan ridge. The lake is quite high and is surrounded on three sides by mountains, and only on one side there is a view of the mountains. This relief was a second chance, after the lakes of Koruldi, for my idea. This time I miscalculated, I took off too low.





Day 10 , pampering on the water. The morning began with flights, right away I just flew over the clearing, but then I decided to hang over the lake. It seemed that everything was going well, but at some point I was distracted and the quadrocopter fell into the water. After a preliminary inspection, he was not injured.

Kachkar (Turkey)


Day 2 , flight after a thunderstorm. The transition was a squall wind and rain, but in the evening the clouds parted and I gathered a quadrocopter. Some time after takeoff, he began to squeak, informing about the low battery charge, and I panicked. As a result, he broke both legs (chassis), but it is good that there is always a band-aid in the tourist's first-aid kit. After treatment, the legs became even better, they gained elasticity.

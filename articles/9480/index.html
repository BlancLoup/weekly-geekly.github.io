New plug-in connect Photoshop with Google service

Adobe and Google have released a plugin for Photoshop CS3 Extended photo editor.

Using the plugin, as reported, Photoshop CS3 Extended users will be able to directly search through the program and download three-dimensional models from the Google 3D Warehouse database. Google 3D Warehouse allows you to download 3D models of various objects, devices, and so on for free. Those interested can add their own models to Google 3D Warehouse.

The new plug-in for Photoshop CS3 Extended can be installed on computers running under Microsoft Windows and Apple Mac OS X. After installing the add-on, the Search Google for 3D Model item will appear in the menu of the graphic editor. Through this item, users will be able to search for three-dimensional models by keywords, as well as to preview them and then download.

The developers do not recommend downloading models larger than 5 MB. Otherwise, the process of converting them into a format that is understandable to Photoshop CS3 Extended will be very slow. In addition, there are a number of other problems. In particular, the plugin may not work correctly with some models, displaying textures in a distorted form. In addition, there may be problems with loading models containing Cyrillic characters. Finally, on computers with Windows, users will not be able to return to the page with the results of the previous search when they call the plug-in again.

Blue Origin successfully tested the crew rescue system



In early September, Blue Origin unveiled its reusable New Glenn manned rocket to the public . But the company continued to work on its earlier project - the New Shepard suborbital rocket , intended, according to the authors, for space tourism.

Yesterday New Shepard was tested in an emergency scenario. Instead of simulating a successful launch, the engineers of the company decided to check the emergency evacuation system of the crew and passengers in case of emergency. The tests were successful.

The exit of the rocket from the earth's atmosphere is one of the most dangerous stages. Blue Origin engineers simulated an emergency situation at about 45 seconds of flight or at an altitude of 4800 meters.

The design of the rocket New Shepard allows you to return the rocket to the ground after the successful withdrawal of the payload to a predetermined height. Also in the rocket the rescue system of the head capsule was designed, in which, according to the idea, the crew and passengers will be accommodated.

Rideye BlackBox Recorder

Extremely simple in settings and not deprived of aesthetic appeal, Rideye BlackBox promises to be faithful help, and that is also significant at its price, to serve the owner for a long time (developer Cedric Bosch) even makes a bold idea that the recorder will survive the bike quite well, but this is someone who leads).




In the event of an accident, it is difficult for cyclists to prove their innocence, or the guilt of another participant in an accident, and therefore arrives in the regiment of bicycle recorders, a salable product. Until October 13, a campaign to raise funds for the launch of mass production will last on Kickstarter (by the way, you need a little - 32 thousand US dollars).

Manufacturers describe the action of this registrar as follows. Mounted on the steering wheel (suitable, by the way, to any bike); equipped with a lens with a 120-degree viewing angle; after pressing the button, it immediately starts recording video with sound in .mov format and with a resolution of 1280 x 720 pixels.





Saves the last 2.5 hours of recording, after which the previous data is overwritten, unless the cyclist manually stops recording, or if the accelerometer does not do this automatically upon detecting a crash. Every detail is captured, thanks to the ultra-wide-angle lens and HD video quality. To facilitate video processing, it is automatically divided into five-minute segments, which later, if necessary, you can create one large video file, or create a file of arbitrary length. Files are transferred using USB.



The battery is capable of maintaining a continuous record of 24 hours - a cycle (roughly speaking, this is one month of calculated use), will withstand approximately 500 recharge cycles. There is a nuance: the user can not replace the battery. In size - quite compact device: 2.54 x 3.81 x 7.62 cm with a weight of approx. 185 grams.

Rideye is expected in March next year and will cost $ 119 (pre-order today - $ 149).

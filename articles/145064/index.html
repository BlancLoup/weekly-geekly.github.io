Non-free games for GNU / Linux: good or bad?

A well-known company, Valve, which distributes proprietary computer games with digital constraint control (known as DRM), recently announced that it will distribute these games under GNU / Linux. What good and bad consequences will it have?

I believe that the availability of popular non-free programs under GNU / Linux can help to master the system. However, our plans go further than to make this system ‚Äúsuccessful‚Äù; its purpose is to bring freedom to users. Thus, the question is how this change affects the freedom of users.

However, if you are going to use these games, you should do it on GNU / Linux, and not on Microsoft Windows. At least you can avoid the harm that inflicts your freedom on Windows .

Thus, in a direct practical sense, this change can bring both harm and benefit.

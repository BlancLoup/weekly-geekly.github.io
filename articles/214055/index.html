Ban on continents



One fine morning I looked through the logs and asked myself a number of questions:

  1. Am I waiting for letters from Southeast Asia? (when I looked mail logs)
  2. And with what frightened ssh brute forcing from the States?
  3. Do I have to endure network scanners from Australia?
  4. Who is calling me from Africa? (when looking at asterisk logs)
  5. Why would my POP server get access from Latin America?


Why not ban on continents? Leaving only the desired continent (s)?




This is a small script that bans half the world: 

#!/bin/sh # AFRINIC -  # APNIC - ,    # ARIN -   # LACNIC -     # RIPE NCC -     #  ,      | BAN_CONT='AFRINIC|APNIC|LACNIC' #   Ipv4     iana.org      list=`curl -s http://www.iana.org/assignments/ipv4-address-space/ipv4-address-space.csv \ | egrep $BAN_CONT \ | cut -d "," -f 1 \ | sed 's!/8!.0.0.0/8!g' ` #  for ip in $list; do iptables -I INPUT -s $ip -j DROP #       .   . done

Payment interface, as a direct communication channel with developers

As is known, QIWI Wallet is presented to users in different interfaces. There are Russian and international sites, applications for smartphones and tablets; There are special applications in social networks. Even the QIWI Wallet in the terminals has been preserved as a museum exhibit : some stubbornly use it as a payment interface, despite the fact that it is not updated and even loses options (for example, you cannot pay for mobile phones).



Mobile applications can be considered the most convenient for life: your phone is always with you, unlike a computer and a laptop. What is important: in each of these applications there is an opportunity to contact the developers to leave your review. Noticed some unclear nuance (for example, you need to configure Push notifications) - you can immediately write to the developers.

Thanks to this communication channel, we regularly supplement applications with useful options. For example, according to reviews from users, there was an option to copy report text, save favorites in the cache (so as not to reload it every time), as well as a report filter (all, replenishment, retirement) and PUSH notifications. Bada application was also created at the request of users through the feedback form. Simply choose ‚ÄúOther Topic‚Äù in the list of topics and write a review:



Of course, every time before launching an update, applications are tested, but they can be objectively assessed only from the outside: slowly, in normal life, in real conditions. Objectively, users are the most attentive observers. I would like to once again draw your attention to this channel of communication with the developers: write your wishes, criticism or whatever you like. All these reviews are taken into account and implemented. Let not immediately and not in its original form, but each meaningful review is somehow reflected in the actual work on the interfaces.



Also reviews for our developers you can leave in the comments. What do you think about our mobile interfaces, in particular, what functionality is enough, what OS are you using?

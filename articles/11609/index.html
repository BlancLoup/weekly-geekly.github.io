iPhone: User Guide and user reviews

An official iPhone user guide has just appeared on the Apple website. It is available at manuals.info.apple.com/en/iPhone_User_Guide.pdf

Read the documentation carefully and try to find something new?

In addition, due to the fact that the phone finally got to the masses - reviews became available.

About all this - below.

"News" from the documentation


  • Gesture: quickly drag your finger across the screen - fast and far scrolling
    Text: if, when typing, to discard the suggested hint and end the word, it will automatically fall into the dictionary
    Text: already seen, but if you touch and hold a little while typing, something like a magnifying glass will appear, allowing you to position the cursor exactly.
    UI: the included headset allows you to control music: pause / play, next and previous song.
    Internet: Internet connection is made in the following order:
    • attempt to connect to the Wi-Fi point that you used last time
      if not, show the list of Wi-Fi points to the user for selection
      if this did not work out, EDGE is used.
      Feature: you can not use EDGE and simultaneously talk on the phone. If the connection via EDGE is used actively (the webpage is being downloaded, for example), then you will not even hear the incoming call. If it is passive - the call, apparently, will still come.
      Conversation: up to 5 people can take part in the conference.
      UI: starting point - contact information. The easiest way is to first select a person, and only then decide what to do with it - write an SMS or look at its address on the map.
      E-mail: iPhone can watch attachments of the following types: .c, .cpp, .diff, .doc, .docx, .h, .hpp, .htm, .html, .m, .mm, .patch, .pdf, .txt, .xls, .xlsx
      E-mail UI: approximation (as in Safari) works in the mail - you can increase the part of the letter in order to better consider it.
      E-mail: mail from Yahoo comes by itself (PUSH interface). IPhone can check other mailboxes once every 15 minutes, every half an hour or once an hour.
      Safari UI: there is a button Share. It sends the current page by e-mail.
      Safari: can block pop-ups.
      iPod: On-The-Go playlists can now be edited - change the order of songs and delete songs from there.
      iPod: Video can be deleted from the iPhone on the go.
      iPod: buttons below (artists, podcasts, video, etc ...) you can edit: change their order, add others, remove the current ones.
      SMS & E-mail: to delete a message, you can click edit, then delete, or you can drag your finger from left to right (as if crossing it out) and click on the Delete button that appears.
      Apps: there are Calculator and Notes applications. Simple as two pennies.
      General: iPhone works with VPN over L2TP and PPTP. And through Wi-Fi and through EDGE.

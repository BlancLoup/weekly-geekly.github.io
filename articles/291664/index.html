23-year-old start-up attracted $ 40 million in a round with the participation of Asia's largest investment fund Temasek

$ 40 million was invested in a sleep monitoring device in the last round of financing. The device developer, Hello, received a valuation of $ 250 million. Round headed the Singapore fund Temasek . Last year, the company raised $ 2.4 million to Kickstarter .

Hello has several competitors in the market for intelligent systems to track a person‚Äôs physical condition. Among them - Fitbit fitness bracelet, for example. Hello system consists of a sensor Sleep Pill (it is mounted on a cushion) and analyzing device Sense. It monitors the state of the sleeper and the characteristics of the room - light, temperature, noise level. The founder of Hello is a 23-year-old London-born James Proud.

He independently mastered programming at the age of 9 years. In his teens, James created a service to search for live performances of various musicians Giglocator . At 19, he went to live in San Francisco and went into business. He was assisted in this by venture capitalist Peter Thiel . Proud did not receive education.

In 2012, Giglocator was sold for a million dollars. The money received was used to launch the Hello project.

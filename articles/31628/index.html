Young IT talent


After searching, we found several outstanding children who, it seems to us, have already deserved a great future in the IT field, and they all did it before they were 15 years old. We also noticed that the competition for the title of the youngest MCP (Microsoft Certified Professional) seems to be going on, although the sources of the article date back to different years.

So, we present a list of children who are 2 times younger than us and perhaps much smarter than most of us:

Arfa Karim Randava (Arfa Karim Randhawa)
Born in 1996. This girl from Pakistan became Microsoft Certified Professional in C # programming at the age of 9 years and 8 months. She met computers at the age of 6, and preparation for certification took her 4 months.

Babar Iqbal
Born in Pakistan, Babar became the youngest Wireless Network Administrator (CWNA) in history at the age of 9. He had already received Microsoft Certified Professional (MCP) and Certified Internet Web Professional Associate (CIW-A) by that time.

Scott Goodson
Scott Goodson grew up amusing himself by reading thick volumes of computer documentation. At the age of 11, he opened his own computer repair shop. With the permission of the school board, he entered college at the age of 13 and plans to study computer science and electrical engineering.

S. Chandra Sekar (S. Chandra Sekar)

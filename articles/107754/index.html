About copyright


Someone thinks that the information must be free, someone sees his bread in it, someone doesn‚Äôt consider or see anything, but simply downloads. Where does that line end when the law on ownership of some type of information ceases to be convenient for humanity and becomes a burden?

In fact, everything is quite simple. Copyright laws are laws. And the laws are issued in the interests of the authorities, according to their needs and ideas. Therefore, just like any other law, the law on copyright expresses the interests of the authorities. If we want to build a society where everyone can earn more money (the only priority), then copyright is developed in the right direction. If we want the prosperity of mankind, then we must make the information free, and labor, directly - paid. And protect labor. A singer should receive money for the performance of a song, for a performance, or, as a last resort, for the convenience of submitting his songs (well, it is more convenient for someone to buy from iTunes, rather than look for where to download). And the law should protect the singer from another singer who can start singing his songs for money. Or from a merchant who, using these songs, can provide a service by selling a disc with these songs (the exclusive right of the author). But in no way should the law protect a singer from teenagers screaming with a guitar in the yard, or from a fan who downloads this song. This year there was a case in Moscow when veterans were sued for performing songs of their youth. If we want to build a society where the law is above man and common sense, then this is a normal situation. If we want the development of mankind through the development of each person individually, then something goes wrong.

Therefore, before arguing about the details of the law, let's decide what goal it pursues, and only then we can justify the effectiveness of a particular method.

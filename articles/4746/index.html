Social media turns doorwayers into journalists

Analysis of 17,713 articles on the Digg website allowed us to identify the words that are most often found in the headlines of popular news. The list included only words that were used in at least thirty headlines and at the same time received an average of more than 500 points when voting on the site. For each word the average number of votes is indicated.

Ten leaders looks like this: riaa (1320); amazing (1258); photos (1159); nano (1132); want (1118); mario (1071); his (1051); who (1042); ever (1008); vista (996).

At the second stage of the experiment, the optimizers decided to verify the results obtained. Of the most effective words, they made up a meaningless phrase (Nano-mario wants his amazing RIAA photos!) And published it on Digg . This is surprising, but the adventure worked one hundred percent: the article received 500 votes in 2 hours and 37 minutes.

Of course, this experiment looks like a joke. The more interesting it is to observe how spammers and SEOs try to use social media for their own purposes. Someone does it brazenly, publishing the same texts at once on all social media in order to quickly get a large number of incoming links. Others bring a serious scientific basis for this kind of optimization using the new term Social Media Optimization (SMO, similar to SEO).

In Russia, the practice of SMO takes only the first steps, because social media is not popular enough in our country, but already now some Russian optimizers use this methodology to promote on Yandex .

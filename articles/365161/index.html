Stephen Hawking was born in 1942 in Oxford. Already in 1962, he graduated from Oxford University with a Bachelor's degree, and in 1966 at Trinity Hall College at Cambridge University defends a doctoral degree. Throughout his life, he taught and worked at various universities in the UK and the USA; in 1973, he visited the USSR to discuss black holes with Soviet physicists Yakov Zeldovich and Alexey Starobinsky .

The focus of the tape was placed not only on Hawking's work in cosmology, but also on his struggle with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis and his relationship with his wife.



Hawking distinguished himself not only in science, but also on the path of its popularization, and this tape is not his first contribution. In 1988, already confined to a wheelchair and unable to speak after trachiotomy, he released the book A Brief History of Time , which became a bestseller.

Opera opens new data center in Europe



The new server platform will accelerate access to the contents of websites for Opera Mini users around the world

June 30, 2009, Oslo, Norway - Opera Company signed an agreement with TeliaSonera International Carrier, in accordance with which a major international provider will provide accommodation and communications for a new data center of a Norwegian company in Poland.

This data center will become the fourth Opera server platform and will handle requests from Opera Mini mobile browser users from the European region. With the advent of the new data center and the release of resources from other server platforms of the company (in Norway, USA and China), downloading information from the Internet for Opera Mini users around the world will be even faster.

Poland, as the location of the new data center, was chosen based on its geographical location. In countries such as Russia, Poland and Ukraine, the Opera Mini browser is very popular, and its penetration rate is the highest. The location of the server platform in close proximity to a vast consumer audience will allow faster processing of its requests for receiving information from websites. Over time, the new data center will serve all Opera Mini users in Central and Eastern Europe.

TeliaSonera International Carrier is an international communications services provider that provides its own communication channels and modern data transfer technologies.

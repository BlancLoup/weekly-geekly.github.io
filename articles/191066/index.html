Cowon AE1: another unusual recorder from the manufacturer of players

If my memory serves me, I became acquainted with Cowon products in 2006. Then I chose my first music player and eventually bought Cowon iAudio F1. Then he used D2 and S9. It was evident that the company is small, which in some ways it is inferior to Apple and Samsung (for example, in the reasonableness of the interfaces and their localization), but in general, its products aroused respect. I will not talk about sound - I liked it, someone, of course, did not like it.



Today, when pocket players are rapidly losing popularity, their manufacturers are actively trying to find other areas of activity. (I covered this topic in this post ) Cowon has switched to DVRs. First, she released the model AC1, then AW1 with support for Wi-Fi and the ability to control from a smartphone. And now she presents her third avtoreg - AE1. It is curious that it is already presented in Russia and will cost from 6,600 to 7,600 rubles, depending on the amount of memory.



True, the options for the capacity of the drive for some reason are not specified either in Russian (which could well have been expected about this a little lower), or even on the global site Cowon. And there, and there it says only support MicroSD up to 32 GB. Probably, this is not about the built-in memory, namely about the complete flash drive. The kit also includes a card reader.



In general, the recorder turned out to be entertaining. First, it has a 2.8-inch screen made by Samsung. Is it a lot or a little? The device has a G-sensor: after it triggers as a result of an impact (sudden braking, turning, etc.), a 30-second file is recorded (15 s before the event and 15 s after), which is automatically transferred to the protected area on the card and excluded from the process cyclic rewriting. Motion detection to start recording when a moving object appears in the field of view of the lens in the Cowon AE1 is also. But the battery, apparently, no.

From the curious one, I will also single out a certain bicycle mode (the corresponding mount, as I understand it, is not included in the kit) and a system that monitors the temperature of the recorder and automatically turns it off when it overheats. This is true, for example, if you left the car in the heat and went somewhere for a long time. Even in Cowon AE1 there is a voice prompt system, and the support for the Russian language is promised (it is not surprising - with its registrars, Cowon primarily focuses on South Korea and Russia, where today there is a boom of such devices).

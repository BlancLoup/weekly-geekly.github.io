What to measure and analyze with content intelligence?


Let's first determine the factors that matter in content intelligence:

  • Amount of text
  • quality,
  • literacy,
  • readability,
  • accuracy of data
  • completeness of the topic

Alexander Sitnikov, Head of Magway Promo Internet Marketing Agency:

Neither Yandex nor Google can yet independently understand the quality and usefulness of the text. Their task is different - not to determine how good the text is by itself, but how much it responds to a specific user request. Search engines rely on specifications:

  • uniqueness,
  • the number of occurrences of the key in the text
  • the presence of thematic words
  • spaminess
  • text formatting (titles, lists, tables, illustrations),
  • the content of the query in H1, title,
  • text size and other quantitative indicators.


Users help search engines in determining the quality of texts, sending social signals (sharing a page, links in social networks) and leaving links on forums and blogs. Behavioral factors are also evaluated: time spent on the page, click-through rate from search, return to search results.

Interaction with the site is monitored by Yandex Metrika and Google Analytics, click on the value from the issue in Yandex Webmasters and Google Webmasters.

Maxim Denisenkov, head of the SEO department of the Bryansk office of TRINET Group:

For a large text to be really useful, it must provide answers to the user's true questions, and not to those that come up to the copywriter.

We use various methods to identify user problems:

  • we conduct a survey of business owners, figuring out the most frequent questions,
  • monitoring topics in thematic forums and in communities
  • we look at the available statistics (requests that come to the site, wordstat statistics, etc.),
  • Standard items, based on the type of site (for example, for service sites - to introduce the service, describe the benefits, convince to make a call or leave a request).

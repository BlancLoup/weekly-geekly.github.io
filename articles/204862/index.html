YotaPhone Review: The world's first dual-screen smartphone



At the CES exhibition in Las Vegas, a presentation of the YotaPhone smartphone took place. There he received the prestigious award, becoming the best among mobile devices. The new model is not similar to any other devices, because it is equipped with two screens, one of which is E-Ink, as in electronic "readers".

What is the advantage of a smartphone with such displays? It can work much longer if you use only the E-Ink screen to view service information messages. Also, this display is better suited for reading, reducing eye strain. In addition, displays of this type are perfectly readable in the sun. In addition to a pair of displays, the model is characterized by a productive chipset, even the old generation. The smartphone supports 3G / 4G networks, it is equipped with a 13 megapixel camera and 32 GB of RAM, but there is no support for memory cards. According to its characteristics, the device turned out to be interesting, except for the lack of a Full HD display, which has become an integral feature of this year‚Äôs flagship models.

Design


If you put the YotaPhone on the table next to any of the new flagship devices, you can clearly see how much less its screen is the screen compared to its counterparts, and the body itself can hardly be called small. Large frames around the screen, impressive thickness. And look at the device more interesting from the back than the front. The whole essence of the model opens here: a curved E-ink screen. The front side with the main display is flat.





Against the background of the flagship products of this year, which compete with each other, who is thinner, YotaPhone does not fall among the leaders at all. The thickness of almost 1 cm is a lot against the general background. This can only be explained by the presence of a second screen.

The smartphone is not very heavy, but due to the wide frames around the screen and the large flat sides it feels larger than it actually is. Now most companies are trying to reduce the empty areas around the display, making the most of free space. There is no such thing. The frontal and rear parts of the smartphone are covered with Gorilla Glass, the sidewalls are covered with a nice soft-touch plastic, the assembly is excellent. It is pleasant to take a smartphone in hand, although it is not particularly convenient to hold it because of angular shapes and thickness, but it is a matter of habit.

Above the screen is a speaker, a set of sensors and a camera. Rocking volume control placed on the left side, on the top end of the power button. It is flat, the place chosen is uncomfortable, you have to reach for it. The button itself is actually a plug for the microSIM connector.







Screen


The backlight of conventional screens consumes a lot of energy, which inevitably affects the autonomy. In this case, the role of the event panel is best assigned to E-Ink. And he works constantly, separately from the color display and does not depend on it. Considering how low his power consumption is, this has a positive effect on the lifetime of a single charge.





Events that are displayed on the E-Ink screen are customized by the user. For example, a smartphone may not show the contents of the message or the name of the sender, but only indicate the fact of receiving a new notification. Sets a list of those from whom you want to display messages and letters, a lot of settings for different occasions, which allows not to fear for the private nature of the correspondence. Disable the second screen can not be, it works constantly. In addition to all kinds of notifications, it can show the weather forecast, there is a set of widgets, background wallpapers are configured. In addition, there is an SDK for developers who can later support the original idea with their software. In the meantime, programs are not very many, but, nevertheless, already at the start you can complete your smartphone with a set of useful applications.

On the additional screen, you can display data from the main screen, if you hold with two fingers from top to bottom. The result will be a static image that will duplicate a color image. The smartphone provides convenient work with the calendar, in which you can create various events and receive timely notifications. Again, the electronic ink screen is great for reading. You can flip through the pages with the rocking volume control located on the side or gestures along the sensory area below the screen.



The main screen has a resolution of 720 x 1280 pixels, glass without an air gap, the image has excellent viewing angles.





Detailed testing of the main screen



It uses the well-known LCD technology IPS and with a diagonal of 4.3 inches has a resolution of 1280 x 720 (HD). This provides a high pixel density of 342 ppi - the image does not suffer from pixelation, and the fonts and small details look quite smooth.

The maximum screen brightness was 550 cd / m2. This is a very good result, on par with the iPhone 5 / 5S. A solid stock of brightness is enough in most situations. The contrast ratio reaches 745: 1 - good enough for IPS, but it can be better. But the anti-reflective coating did not let down - it well suppresses various parasitic reflections. Viewing angles also came out great. Apparently, the screen has a structure without an air gap, which helps the image to maintain intelligibility at an angle.





Gamma and brightness of halftones are the main questions. The current version of the YotaPhone screen diligently adjusts the gamma depending on the content on the screen. Therefore, it is difficult to measure this indicator with any certainty - the amplitude of the change in brightness is quite noticeable. What is worse, the constant change in image brightness is sometimes noticeable by the eye. Light transitions lose their intelligibility, then acquire it again - in general, they live their own lives. The only consolation is if there is a static image on the screen (and, accordingly, the adjustment does not interfere with it), then visually there is no problem with the gamut. In this case, the intelligibility is good, and the brightness transitions look correct. Until the image is replaced by something else, and the split second the screen will shift the gamma and brightness ...



Color settings, however, no longer cause any complaints. The average color temperature is about 6700K, which is very close to the standard. A slight incline in cold tones is barely noticeable.



In the color balance a little lack of red components. The deviation is so small that the average Delta E error on the gray scale was only 2.79 units. In fact, the screen is very carefully configured from the factory and does not contain any unnecessary impurities.



The color gamut, apparently, also received due attention. Primary colors are very close to the sRGB standard, only green is somewhat lacking in saturation. But even this small miscalculation does not spoil the big picture. The average color error Delta E - 2.37 units. In fact, the distortions are so small that they will not be visible to the naked eye. Great result for a debutant!

As it turned out, the YotaPhone screen has an excellent margin of brightness and very good color reproduction. In principle, here you can talk about some similarities with the iPhone 5 / 5S. If all retail copies will have similar color accuracy, then you can take your hat off to Yota. But the non-switchable adjustment of the scale, which makes the brightness constantly ‚Äúfloat‚Äù, on the contrary, has become a kind of a fly in the ointment. Looking through the photos, you will often notice how the image changes brightness for half a second. I want to believe that this flaw will be dealt with in future firmware, or at least will highlight the option to disable the adaptive gamma. In this case, the YotaPhone screen can be given the highest rating, but in the current version it is very, very good. To be honest, for a newbie, this is an unexpectedly high result.

Testing Iphone 2g for extreme EMF - a mini lightning strike


It is strongly not recommended to repeat this experiment, work with high voltage and high frequency requires compliance with safety regulations, the author strongly advises against repeating this experiment (experience) at home or anywhere else.

Introduction.

EMC - Electromagnetic Compatibility - a criterion and requirement for the operation of devices and electrical equipment.
EMF - Electromagnetic interference, in our experiment it will be like an electromagnetic field generated by a high-frequency high-frequency voltage and the very high voltage itself.

EMC can be divided into 2 criteria.
1. Interference radiation (may be in the power supply network (interfered with consumption), or electromagnetic field radiation)
2. The effect of interference on the device itself.

The second we will test.

In advance, I want to stipulate this is not advertising and not anti-advertising, that the phone for testing faithfully served me for 3 years, at the moment it has cracked glass, the battery does not hold even 4 hours of Internet surfing (i.e. you can‚Äôt sit on the Internet unless something urgent check), the rear plastic cover is broken, and the phone itself is already quite obsolete. The essence of the experiments.

1. Work the phone next to the high-frequency discharge with the formation of an arc discharge.
2. The work of the phone when it hits a high voltage.

Devices and components.

1. Multimeter mastesh m838 - as an ammeter.
2. Automotive ignition coil - as a high-frequency generator of high frequency (HV, HF).
3. Computer power supply - as a 12v power source.
4. As an open discharger homemade stand.

Total output we had a discharge voltage with a frequency of 400 Hz and a voltage in the region of 20 kV.

An interesting fact: the wires of the HV and the earth were attracted to each other, and upon their contact, weak blue radiation appeared at the point of contact, most likely a corona discharge .

An interesting fact: during the operation of the stand between arresters, an arc- discharge was formed which is a form of plasma, but due to the fact that the voltage was relatively small (20kV) the arc was not large and not stable. But like the fact that at home you can get plasma is interesting in my opinion.

Testing.



Test number 1

The test was that the mobile phone was located next to the arrester and the phone's response to the impact was studied.

Interesting fact: During the setup of the stand that passed at a distance of 3 meters from the computer, when the stand was turned on, the mouse and keyboard constantly fell off, most likely this was due to the long, unshielded cable going to the mouse and keyboard from the PC.



Actually the result, the discharge of 20kV at a distance of 5 cm from the phone did not damage the phone and did not even degrade the quality of communication.

Interesting fact: During the test, I wanted to measure the current consumed, but during the test the readings of the multimeter were clearly false, it showed a current of 18.88 And that is not physically possible because it has a 10A fuse, as well as theoretically calculated current of only 3A. Most likely this happened due to the fact that the consumed current had a pulsed character of consumption, and the tester is designed for constant or the influence of interference.

After Tests

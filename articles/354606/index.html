How to spoil a useful service (for example, Yandex.Maps)

Yandex has a great product - Yandex.Maps. The value of this tool is probably difficult to overestimate: many of us use Maps every day.

What makes this product really interesting is user-generated information. First of all, of course, information about the speed of movement, which is converted into a map of traffic jams. As well as text user information when people warn others about road closures, congestion and all sorts of dangers.

But without a skillful moderation, any product in which end users participate can seriously deteriorate. Now, using the example of Yandex.Maps, I will show how this happens.

No, it's not about the mat in the "Talkers". Without him, not even so fun. Now the conversation will focus on a situation where, apparently, with the best of intentions, they do worse. In principle, much is already clear from the first picture:



This is the M4 highway. Probably the best track in Russia. Indeed, the world level: you can put the car on cruise control right after the MKAD and take about 4 hours to get to Voronezh without extra voltage. The road is made by all standards. In particular, pedestrian crossings are visible from afar. Signs are everywhere, they are made with a reflective coating.

So what's the problem? The problem is that some organization (in this case, Avtodor) takes a tool to notify others about unforeseen interrogations (an ‚Äúexclamation mark‚Äù) and uses it in such a way that it ceases to be a meaningful means of information. Just like in the ancient parable about the boy and the wolves. If you warn - be aware of the responsibility for your words. Otherwise, for the 25th time they stop listening to you.

Most likely, someone's cast-iron head, in fulfillment of the Highest commandment to reduce mortality on the roads, thought of bringing measures to reduce, and so on, and so on ... It turned out wildly. A driver who wants to know if there is anything unforeseen ahead - accidents, traffic jams, floors - has to read many, many times that there will be a crosswalk ahead. What for?? On the ground, I repeat, crossings are already clearly visible, the road is built according to all the rules.

This moment sooner or later (rather sooner) comes in the life of any social network (in a broad sense: where many people interact with each other). What is initially designed as a tool for interacting with others on top of the usual (it can be caps-lock, highlighting text, banners calling for help to the child, etc., etc.) easily deteriorates when one of agents for the sake of solving immediate problems begins to use it to attract maximum attention. As a result, the entire ecosystem of the network suffers: its agents cease to respond properly to such stimuli provided by the design, or the speed of perception of significant information decreases noticeably.

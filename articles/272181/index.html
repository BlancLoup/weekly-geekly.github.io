Mail.Ru Group educational projects: 4 years, 1206 students, 101 teachers



Hi, Habr! Four years ago, the Mail.Ru Technopark project appeared, about which we have already told here more than once (for example, here , here and here ). The principles laid down in its foundation proved so successful that in this short period two more similar projects emerged on their basis ( Technosphere and Technotrak ). In addition, we began to launch projects on the Technopark model in the regions. But we begin with the idea, or rather the history of its appearance.

Set the task


The premise of the project now seems obvious, but then, four years ago, it was not quite clear. We discussed the personnel crisis. We talked about the demographic hole, about the complexity of recruiting young professionals with the appropriate product IT-company skills, about the cost of adapting them in the company, etc. It seemed that there was no acceptable solution to these problems, that we generally should not focus on attracting young professionals on a regular HR practice. But, as often happens in our company, the word "experiment" has sounded.

What is an experiment? This is when you need to confirm or refute some hypothesis quickly and with minimal cost. What is an experiment in education? This is definitely not fast, since receiving feedback from a graduate is years, if not a dozen years. And it‚Äôs not cheap at all, because it is necessary to provide several years of a reproducible educational process when teaching a statistically significant number of students. Nevertheless, we decided to experiment and rushed out to develop educational competencies that were not typical of IT business, together with our first partner, MSTU. N. E. Bauman.

Formulate a hypothesis


What hypothesis did we test? We thought it was a reasonable idea that if our specialists start teaching students at a good technical college, we will get several important ‚Äúbusiness benefits‚Äù.

  • Let's reduce the costs of adaptation (training) of interns in the company through the centralization of training. That is, instead of the ‚Äúone mentor - one intern‚Äù model, we implement the ‚Äúone mentor - many interns‚Äù model, which, all other things being equal, is more economical.
  • Increase the loyalty (read - the term of work in the company) of our interns . Firstly, due to the training of our technologies and the production process, and secondly, due to the fact that they are imbued with our working atmosphere.
  • Let us increase the loyalty (yes, yes, again about survival) of our interns by introducing communicative practices into the learning process: trainings and team work on training projects (with the subsequent collective defense of the results of these projects).
  • We will raise the quality of the interns' work (read - we will reduce the adaptation time) due to practice-oriented training: a project-based approach in education plus an internship in a company or work in a laboratory.
  • We will significantly increase the loyalty of our own employees who are selected to teach in our projects. Firstly, work as a teacher is paid in addition, secondly, it is prestigious to teach students of the best technical universities of the country.
  • We will positively change the general culture in the company , involving people in the process of training, mentoring and knowledge transfer.

We are looking for a solution


I can not say that all of the following arose immediately and suddenly. Something appeared and died, some ideas were born as a result of random conversations in the elevator. But most of this list is the result of team hard work.

  • We teach seriously . Our educational program cannot be simple and / or short. In terms of its content, it should correspond to the goal, that is, there should not be unworked aspects of the professional training of our future trainee. In its complexity, it should be slightly higher than the input requirements of the company to a specialist in this area of ‚Äã‚Äãtraining. In other words, a graduate of our program must successfully compete with a specialist who has several years of experience in the relevant field. Now the typical duration of our program is two years, it consists of 12-16 semester disciplines. About 25% of those who complete it end, i.e. 3/4 we deduct in the learning process.
  • We teach the best of the best . Our selection should be aimed at identifying not only knowledgeable students, but those who are also extremely motivated to learn and develop professionally. To do this, we must not close on a particular department or even a faculty, we need to create the broadest attraction funnel that will allow us to find the best. Now the competition, which we reached in the Technopark, is more than 15 people per place.
  • We teach specific things . We do not want to compete with university programs of study. We consider them necessary, but not sufficient for optimal training of an IT specialist, especially a programmer. Therefore, our programs complement the university in terms of practice-oriented learning. We try to ensure that after each discipline (we last one semester) the student had something that you can ‚Äútouch‚Äù, which you can boast to your friends and family, which you can insert into your resume. We strive for the ratio of theory and practice in our programs to be 30/70, until we reach approximately 50/50.
  • We teach the most sought-after . Our teachers - only practice, I'm sorry, from the plow. All of them, without exception, work every day with the technologies that they teach. Therefore, our courses are live, we change (we supplement, improve) them every semester. Therefore, we did not have and will not have the ‚Äúrecipes of the material‚Äù and absurd learning tasks about anything. Now in our projects there are 101 (I like to say - more than a hundred) teacher, for the entire time of the project there were 112 of them.
  • We learn and learn together . Our students are full-fledged and most important participants of the project, our partners. We do not take money (although we do not pay scholarships) and look forward to a full-fledged and adult dialogue that helps us develop the project. Now our students evaluate each lesson conducted according to nine criteria, at the end of each semester participate in the certification of teams of teachers, work together with us to automate the educational process, are part of the team.

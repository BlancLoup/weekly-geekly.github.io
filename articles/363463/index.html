Fitness watch Basis Peak will be even smarter

The tracker company Basis Science will receive a new software: thanks to the advanced firmware, the device will be able to display notifications and synchronize with other fitness applications.



Basis Peak sales started in November, and at the end of the month news appeared on the Basis official website, which may encourage healthy lifestyle lovers to choose this tracker. The December update (of course, free of charge) will turn Basis Peak into a full-fledged smart watch: users of the device will be able to view text messages, notifications from social networks, calendar marks and incoming calls (they cannot be answered). An additional advantage will be synchronization with the applications of Strava, MapMyRun and Endomondo, and the GPS module of the smartphone will help determine the distance traveled and load it into the appropriate program.



Finally, lovers of bright solutions are clearly delighted with the new collection of straps for Peak: the SportVent accessories are equipped with a perforated mesh for breathability during intensive work. Order strap (colors - red, blue, yellow, pink, black) can be from mid-December on the Basis website. Price: $ 29.99 for one belt.



In March 2014, Basis Science was taken over by Intel for about $ 100 million, and by the end of September, Peak Physical Activity Monitor was anosigned. The gadget worth $ 200 is equipped with a monochrome display, a three-axis accelerometer, a heart rate sensor, a temperature sensor and a sensor for recording a galvanic skin response. In addition to the standard set of indicators (the number of steps / calories burned), Peak monitors sleep, and also monitors the intensity of sweating, body temperature and heart rate. The gadget has water protection (WR50) and works up to 4 days in the mode of medium activity.

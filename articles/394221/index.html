Video: Lenovo revive Moto RAZR clamshell

In 2004, Motorola announced one of the most stylish phones of all time - a very thin clamshell phone RAZR V3, which was then sold in quantities of more than 130 million units. Twelve years later, Lenovo, which bought the brand, wants to restart the legendary model. The channel Motorola Mobility posted a video that shows a walk through the school, where young people use this device.

If Lenovo brings to the market the exact same clamshell, but on Android, I personally will be in the front ranks of those who want to give money for it.



Motorola RAZR V3 in 2004 was the flagship. It was distinguished from its competitors by its design, including a very thin case, a backlit keyboard made of a metal plate. The gadget weighed 95 grams, had a 2.2-inch screen with a resolution of 176x220 pixels with more than 262 thousand colors and an external display of a smaller size 96x80 pixels. The camera device could shoot with a resolution of 640x480 pixels. In talk mode, the gadget worked from 3 to 5 hours, in standby mode - up to 280 hours.

Onlive, Gaikai & Game Streamer





If you are interested in trends, here is one of them. Soon and the games will be in the cloud. Europe, and even more so Russia is not waiting for it soon, but in the states they promise to launch the service just about.


Play Crysis at maximum settings on iPad? Easy! * "Try it yourself" - offers a smiling young man in an orange polo.

* In fact, gentlemen are cunning. It is one thing to watch live video in FullHD format, and quite another to control this video. The magnitude of the delay is multiplied by only 2, and the indignation that your character did not have time to react and dodge the rocket rushes to infinity. The exhibition demonstration, of course, is held on a local server, which is hidden in the rack, less than a meter and a half from you.



But it is not all that bad. The success of this business model is inevitable. This is confirmed by the fact that Onlive already has competitors. And not even one!

To understand how important and valuable I will make a small lyrical digression.
In May of this year, I traveled to LOGIN Conference in Seattle with a report. This is a conference on games of a rather broad direction. Rather authoritative in the gaming community (something like a sectoral TED ) Few people know about LOGIN from those who are not involved in the gaming industry, because 3‚Äì4 people organize it without any media support. But, despite this, quite dear people come there. For example, David Perry is a gaming industry veteran and CEO of Gaikai.

Well, at this conference, only lazy did not say that the future belongs to those who can minimize the "friction" (the effort of potential buyers) on the way to conversion.
Example: Apple made their own app store. All his registered users have tied their plastic card to the account and therefore the purchase for them takes place in 1 click. Those. clicked "buy" and that's it. Money written off. There is no turning back :-)
Anti-example: To install a trial (!) Version of Supreme Commander 2, you must perform about 40 clicks and wait an unrealistic long time (install Steam, create an account, confirm email, download a game, install ...)

With such gigantic differences in convenience and ease of access, the quality of the product begins to fade into the background. The one who wins is easier to buy. And if you can also see the demo before you buy it, then everything is your client. In the west, people are used to value their time more than money.

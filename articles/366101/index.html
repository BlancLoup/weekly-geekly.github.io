Pocket HiFi Hidizs AP100 player



Hidizs AP100 belongs to the class of compact Hi-Fi players for connoisseurs of high-quality portable sound. The AP100 occupies an intermediate version between compact and large-sized Hi-End players. About AP100, we can say that this is a reasonable middle ground among Hi-Fi class players.

Appearance




Player dimensions are comparable to the popular FiiO X3, iBasso DX50 and Astel & Kern AK100. The design is strict and concise. The player is presented in two colors: black and silver.



On the front panel are the main buttons:
  • Fast forward and rewind
  • Switch track forward and backward
  • Confirm and Cancel Button
  • Volume buttons

All buttons are logical and do not cause confusion.

On the top panel of the player there are two jacks, a headphone output and a line-out. Line output is useful when connecting an external more powerful or high-quality amplifier (portable or stationary).

On the bottom panel there is a USB input for charging and connecting to a computer, a slot for external memory and an electrical digital input and output. When using the digital output, the AP100 can be used as a transport for an external stationary DAC.

The digital input can be used with a computer by connecting the player digitally to the coaxial output of the built-in sound.



On the left sidebar there are buttons for controlling the equalizer and the SRC algorithm.



On the right panel is a slider key lock mode switch.

Equipment




The AP100 comes in two boxes - in the first player with basic equipment, in the second box is a leather case.



General equipment looks like this:

  • player Hidisz AP100
  • 3.5mm analog cable <=> 3.5mm
  • 3.5 mm digital <=> RCA (coax)
  • USB cable <=> micro USB
  • protective film
  • napkin for the screen
  • documentation


The only thing you can buy for the AP100 is a flexible adapter with 3.5 mm <=> 6.5 mm if headphones with a 6.5 mm plug are used. It is better not to use short rigid adapters, since they usually break the nest of the player.

Control


The player control is intuitive. At first, the location of the ‚ÄúEQ‚Äù and ‚ÄúSRC‚Äù buttons is a bit confusing, since in their place is more logical to control the volume. But the fingers very quickly remember what the buttons are responsible for. In comparison with other players from the "heaven", the AP100 control logic at a high intuitive level.



Separately, I want to note that the manufacturer did not chase the minimum number of buttons. Many were faced with the fact that the buttons to switch tracks are often used as a rewind on the track and sometimes, instead of rewinding, the track switches. In the AP100, these are separate buttons, which makes life easier for the end user.

To save energy, the player stops playback when the headphone plug is unplugged from the jack.

Although the menu does not have Russian language support at the moment, everything is so standard that there are no inconveniences.

The player supports most Lossless formats: APE, FLAC, WAV, WMA and ALAC. A special plus, there is support for Cue index markup for albums represented by a single file.

Filling


Filling the player largely echoes the more expensive player Colorfly C4 Pro. In fact, in terms of functions and capabilities, the AP100 is an inexpensive equivalent of the well-known C4 Pro, an HI-End level player recognized by demanding audiophiles all over the world as one of the best portable audio sources.

The AP100 uses the CS4398 DAC as the Cirrus Logic flagship chip. This DAC is widespread in studio DACs such as ProTools, LynxStudio, etc. This DAC is known to ordinary users in the E-MU series of professional sound interfaces ‚Äúm‚Äù (Mastering). In other words, this DAC is included in the category of "best DACs" to date. Do not forget, the final sound quality largely depends on the implementation of the component chain: ‚ÄúPower - DAC - Amplifier‚Äù in a particular device. In tying the DAC uses high-quality op amp AD823A. To ensure the minimum jitter, two separate quartz oscillators are used, multiples of the frequencies 44.1 and 48 kHz.

CS8422 is used as an alternative digital filter (SRC). A similar solution can be seen in the top-end player Colorfly C4 Pro and studio DAC-a Violectric V800. When using an external digital filter in many cases, you can squeeze a higher sound quality from the applied DAC. But there are many nuances about which we will release a separate material on the example of the AP100 and V800. Briefly from a consumer point of view, then:

  • With highly compressed music material SRC is better not to include
  • When recording with a high dynamic range, SRC mode must be selected by ear.


Quite a lot of attention is paid by the manufacturer to the presence of a hardware equalizer implemented through the CS48L10 DSP. This implementation, unlike the software settings like most other players - allows you to customize the sound without compromising the sound quality. With an entry-level headphone, the equalizer is definitely useful.

When using high-quality headphones, the equalizer is superfluous. This is the very first rule of the ‚Äúclub of high-quality sound lovers‚Äù. Most importantly, turn off the equalizer in the AP100 (Gen setting).

Technical measurements


Technical measurements of the Hidizs AP 100 player were carried out by the Reference Audio Analyzer project.



According to the measurement results, the AP100 frequency response has no deviations (with the GEN preset), and the player‚Äôs resistance is close to zero. Due to the low output impedance, almost all headphones, when connected to the player, will not change their sound when matching impedances.



When testing for resistive loads, one can see a slightly higher level of distortion for a low-impedance load, but when the maximum value of klippig is reached, there is none of the resistances. This means that the AP100 has no current limit and you can expect a reasonably clear sound with any headphones at any possible volume.



The spectrum shows low harmonic distortion. Technically, the potential of CS4398 is well disclosed.



On the spectrum of jitter test, we see the complete absence of side lobes. This indicates the absence of any jitter.

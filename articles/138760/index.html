Ultrabook Dell XPS 13: soon in Russia

An Ultrabook Dell XPS 13 was brought to my editorial office. In Russia, this model is not yet sold, and it is terrible to say that there is only one copy for the whole country. I had to get it for a test a couple of weeks ago, but Dell took part in one conference in Monaco, and the ultrabook flew there. It so happened that I, too, was among the participants of this event, and it was there that we met for the first time. Another 2500 kilometers in the air, a couple of journeys by taxi, and now the meeting continues in Moscow.



One of the important features of the Dell Ultrabook is that it has a 13-inch screen, and the case dimensions are similar to those of 12-inch laptops. This is achieved by a very narrow frame around the matrix. Inside it is quite standard for the current generation of ultrabooks Intel Core i5-2467M with a frequency of 1.6 GHz, but it will soon be replaced by an analogue for Ivy Bridge. As a result, not only the battery life will increase, but also the performance in games will significantly increase. Integrated into a family of processors Ivy Bridge GPU will be very good.



Speaking of battery life. If we open the XPS 13, we find that the layout is very, very dense. Memory (4 GB) is soldered directly on the board. SSD is also not easy, but with the mSATA interface. 128 GB is basically enough for basic needs. But if it is not enough, it is better to purchase an external hard drive, because it is not easy to find a more capacious SSD with mSATA, and it costs twice as much as a similar one with SATA.

Yandex and anonymity


Already after the main program, I spoke briefly with kukutz about another topic that did not sound at the presentation, but relevant in connection with the topic of Internet surfing in principle: the attitude of Yandex to anonymity and various technologies of anonymizing the surfing of users in the network. As Roman said, ‚ÄúYandex‚Äù constantly thinks about this subject, weighing the pros and cons. So far, the arguments "against": the lack of mass demand for such a service and the quite obvious policy of the state to control the activities of users on the Internet outweigh the arguments "for": protection against unscrupulous activity of providers (such as selling information about the behavior of users of advertising networks), protection against hacker attacks in open networks, etc. There is also the problem of trust: Roman noticed that even the most secure service raises questions for the one who provides it. In general, as kukutz said: "We do not have a concrete solution and answer with which we could enter the market."

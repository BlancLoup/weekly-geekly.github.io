In Google, a new feature to block individual sites in the personal issue



The other day in the Google blog there was information that a new feature was introduced in the search engine - the ability to create your own black list of sites in personal issue. In other words, if you see that one of the search results from a certain resource does not suit you, you can quickly hide it forever - but only for yourself, loved one. To use such a function, you simply need to click on an additional link in the search results next to one of the results.

So far, these links work for issuing not all countries and not in all browsers, but the developers promise to expand this feature for all Google users who have an account in the system. It is clear that for those who do not have a Google account, or those who are not logged in, this feature is not available. Currently, this feature works for an English-language search engine, and is supported by browsers such as Chrome (understandably), Internet Explorer and Firefox.



If you have blocked something by mistake, you can restore an unfairly offended site in personal extradition by going to your account settings.

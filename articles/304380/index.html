2. New materials and nanotechnology


‚ÄúModern technologies have reached a level where any improvement requires a real breakthrough. The main limitations of development are laid down in familiar materials, whose possibilities are almost exhausted. All breakthrough technologies in the next 5-10 years will be based on new materials, ‚Äùsays Alevtina Chernikova, Rector of the National Research Technological University‚Äú MISiS ‚Äù.



Future materials are composites created, including with the use of nanostructures. They leave far behind traditional materials (wood, metal, stone) in terms of strength, lightness and even cost. The scope of application of composites is enormous - aerospace industry, engineering, construction, etc.

Composites began to be used in the 2nd half of the XX century. Russia is ten times behind the West in terms of the use of composites. Recently, the gap is narrowing, but the lack of personnel slows down the process. Therefore, in 2020 and further there will be demand for specialists in the field of materials science, the creation of new and modification of already existing materials.

In addition to composites, 3D printing has a great prospect. It is a technology that allows you to print any objects from special compositions, be it a computer board, a musical instrument, a weapon, or a medical prosthesis.

In the coming years, we can expect a transition from modular production to the production of whole products using a 3D printer. This means that the demand for 3D printing specialists will grow.

5. Ground transportation


The prospect of the industry itself does not cause any doubts - until humanity has learned to move through the air or teleport to the right point in space, it will develop ground transportation.

There are two main tasks for the solution of which engineering specialists will be required:

  1. Modernization of transport infrastructure. The number of cars is growing along with the welfare of the population and lower bank loan rates. Increasing the number of cars with the same road capacity leads to traffic jams. So that the transport arteries can function normally, the city will modernize them by expanding, adding new levels, etc.
  2. Creation of new transport systems using intelligent technologies (for example, high-speed railways).



Accordingly, there will be a growing need for designers, specialists in the safety of the transport network, and technicians serving the traffic management systems.

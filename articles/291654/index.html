How professionals work. Ivan Golov, Business Development Director and Partner at Digitalizm

The heading "How Professionals Work" is once again at Megamind, introduces readers to another high-class specialist in the Russian IT market.

Meet Ivan Golov, partner and director of business development at the international agency Digitalizm .



Current Location:
Moscow

Current place of work:
DIGITALIZM, Business Development Director | Partner

One word that best describes how you work:
Two words: with pleasure. I think this is the basis of any success.

How many hours a day do you work?
7-8 hours, standard working day. I check the mail and answer around the clock, but I do not consider this work.

Current mobile device:
iPhone 6 main and iPhone 5s. I tried different Android, but I could not get used to them even as a second phone for tests and demonstrations.

Current computer:
Lenovo Think Pad Carbon X1 at work and MacBook Pro at home. Although at home I almost stopped using a computer, I had enough of a telephone.

Without any applications, programs, utilities you can not live?
Google Mail and Calendar, Excel, Power Point - these are basic programs for me, I can do without the rest.

Which life hack helps you save time?
  • Home, work, gym in 5-10 minutes from each other.
  • Always checking mail, sometimes a timely response to an email can save many hours of unnecessary work.

What kind of to-do manager do you use?
Google Calendar to schedule appointments. The rest is in my head.

What do you listen when you work?
I do not listen to anything, since most of my work is communication and meetings.

What are you reading now?
I reread "The Master and Margarita". I love to re-read books, as over time, perception changes.

Which book from the last read was remembered the most?
"The E-Myth Revisited" by Michael Gerber - a lot of simple good thoughts about entrepreneurship.

Do you use e-readers or prefer paper books?
I read on the iPhone via Bookmate or the Kindle application.

How long do you sleep?
7-8 hours on weekdays, on weekends - 10.

Are you an owl or a lark?
Rather, owl, but when necessary - I get up easily in the early morning.

How do you have breakfast?
Porridge: spelled, rolled oats, quinoa. Or protein shake.

How do you spend your time on the way to work / from work?
Behind the wheel, listening to music.

What advice you have ever been given can you convey to others?
"Leave nothing for the natural course of things." This phrase has an informal sequel - colloquial, but very vital.

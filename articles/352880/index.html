Product Manager vs Project Manager


A product is an end result that you provide to your users or customers. This may be a physically tangible product, software platform, service, application or service.

A project is a concrete plan consisting of various activities. All activities have a specific result and fixed start and end dates. When the result is completed, respectively, your project will be completed.

For example, a product is a new mobile game. The development of this game involves many projects. One of them is the launch of a corporate website. This project has its own starting and ending points. Product and project managers have different responsibilities and functions.

Main responsibilities of the product manager and project manager


Product Manager


Product managers are usually responsible for global product strategy, prioritization of functions and the final product version. Product managers create ideas and initiatives to help achieve strategies and goals using road maps (product roadmaps) and manage product backlog.

If you recall the association with Atlanta, the following will be a valuable burden on the shoulders of the product manager:

  • Global product strategy
  • Release Releases
  • Ideas and initiatives
  • Prioritization
  • Profit and loss issues
  • Market research
  • Competitive environment

Project manager


A project manager is usually less focused on specific product goals. His main responsibility is the project itself. Project manager receives initiatives and product features for the development and approval of the timeline. In his responsibility - the risks, potential constraints, resources and the scale of the tasks.

Project managers are responsible for:

  • Releases
  • Project Resources
  • Updates and statuses
  • Project's budget
  • Team collaboration
  • Solving current problems within the project

Who is more important in the hierarchy of the company?


Both. And this is illustrated by the Atlanta picture. If your company has both positions, then you have full order with the organizational structure. How to understand which of the managers is more important?
Ask yourself two questions:

  1. Can a team work efficiently and get results without a product manager?
  2. What happens if the project manager leaves the team?

Most likely, your answers will show that both positions are important and irreplaceable. Their collaboration is the key to success.

It is easy to assume that if the roles and functions of managers are different, then the professional tools for their effective work are also different.

Conclusion


Summing up, we emphasize once again the most important thing:

  • Product managers and project managers have completely different roles and both positions are important for any modern IT company.
  • Product managers manage product development. They work with initiatives, prioritize them and make strategic decisions on products. Project managers usually oversee the implementation of plans that have already been developed and approved.
  • Every manager needs professional tools to correctly visualize all products and projects.

Agility Robotics demonstrated the multifunctional ostrich robot


A bipedal robot named Cassie, according to its developers, can become one of the reliable human helpers in everyday life, at work, in research and rescue expeditions

Robots are now being developed by many companies, like transnational corporations like Sony, and not very large organizations that specialize in robots. The latter is Agility Robotics , which recently introduced its latest development, the multifunctional bipedal robot Cassie. The developers believe that this robot can be used in various situations: for the study of the terrain, during rescue operations, at work and for the delivery of goods.

The two-leggedness of the robot and its bird-like manipulator legs make Cassie steady when walking on both level roads and cross-country terrain. The ostrich robot walks really well, so the developers plan to entrust him with an important lesson - delivering small loads to the customer's door. The creators of Cassie believe that it will be useful for various companies that are engaged in the delivery of cargo within the village.


The video shows one of the prototypes, which already has most of the capabilities that the current version of the robot has. The company's headquarters is located in Oregon, USA, where the weather changes frequently. So the robot is tested in a wide variety of weather conditions.

During the development of their robot, specialists from Agility Robotics studied the bird morphology and peculiarities of the ostrich walking process. According to the authors of the project, Cassie is a robust, efficient and resistant to external factors.

The company itself is new, but its employees are people from more famous ATRIAS robots , engineers and scientists from the University of Michigan. When creating Cassie, experts used the lessons learned from working on ATRIAS robots projects. The head of the company says that some features of the human movement mechanism were used in the design of the robot.

The design of the legs of the robot is special. It uses only four joints. Servos are interconnected in such a way that the entire system of motion works as efficiently as possible, efficiently using energy resources.

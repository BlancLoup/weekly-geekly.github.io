[CASE] As we typed a giant machine gun from Mars for a stand at E3



What happened


To us, in the Top 3D Shop , the developers of the online shooter Marsenary came and ordered a huge machine gun layout.




A machine gun was needed to organize an exhibition stand at the E3 ( Electronic Entertainment Expo ) 2017 - the annual international gaming exhibition, which was held June 13-15 in Los Angeles.


What is it


Minigun, aircraft machine gun, staff weapon of the character of the game of the class Juggernaut - a mercenary in a heavy armored exoskeleton participating in the war of mining corporations on Mars.


The game is a shooter with elements of strategy, in it the player builds forts - bases on the surface of Mars, and takes part in their capture and defense.



As it happens without 3D printing


Before the advent of 3D printing, such models were ordered from theater requisites or from special-purpose studios specializing in this company. Difficulties arose - inaccuracy of the made layouts, long terms, high cost.

How we did it


We received from the customer a 3D model in a format prepared for visualization in the game - .obj files and color renders for color orientation.


The files had external geometry of the model and texture, they were unsuitable for printing.


Superfluous angles, large polygons are in the game, you can ignore this in motion, this is not the case for the site‚Äôs picture either, but it‚Äôs striking on a static three-dimensional model.


I had to adjust the model: add volumes, close open polygons, increase polygonality - finish drawing each of the 25 parts of the layout.





It took 15 hours of 3D-designer to redraw, but the result was worth it - the model became natural, not so angular.



To make the details of the one-and-a-half-meter machine gun fit in the printer, we divided the model into segments, each of which was cut into a slicer and sent to print.


Printed from PLA and ABS in different colors - for ease of assembly.


The largest element fully occupied the MakerBot z18 - 422 mm high camera.


Part of the parts was within 300x300x160 mm, and some up to 220x200x100 mm.


The total print volume was 43,000 cc.



After printing minigun collected - it was 1,500 mm in height.

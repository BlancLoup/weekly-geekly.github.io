Speakers Corner: A few words about Symfony CMF from guru Lukas Kahwe Smith (Ciklum, Kiev)

The company Siklum never ceases to please various conferences and seminars with the participation of international experts. Our Speakers' Corner is a favorite for many of the format of short events, reports on interesting topics, which have been hotly discussed after a long time.

This time we invite PHP and Web specialists to our Kiev office on August 2 to meet with experienced web developer Lucas Smith (one of the developers of Symfony2).

Topic: Stone Masons vs. 3d printers.





The report and discussion will be held in English :)

About the report:

Undoubtedly, content management systems (CMS) are one of the pillars of the Internet, as they allow non-programmers to create websites. However, by their nature, CMS solutions tend to contain many components that are not subsequently used. Moreover, some of them can become a hindrance in case you need to create something more unique and customized. In fact, the creation of a customized website using CMS, is comparable to the processing of a stonecutter a huge piece of rock.

Symfony CMF allows you to use a completely different approach in such cases. Being essentially a framework, Symfony CMF can be used as a kind of 3D printer, allowing you to accurately put together the pieces needed for a particular project.

The purpose of the report: to acquaint students with the philosophy of Symfony CMF and the tools it provides.

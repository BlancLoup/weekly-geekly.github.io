Old men have a place here: forgotten IT technologies in the ranks



In September 2016, an amazing news story took place: in a small Polish car repair shop, a Commodore 64 computer was found, which is used by the owner to balance the driveshafts. Commodore 64, Carl! Moreover, the computer survived even after the workshop once flooded with flooding. This story gave us the idea to look for other examples of modern use of old, forgotten IT-technologies.

When it comes to old computers, we are nostalgic for the old CD and ZX-Spectrum , and with affection we recall the ancient mainframes that worked on punched cards. Some believe that such dinosaurs can no longer be used in modern business processes or weapon systems. It turns out they can!



While the mass consumer considers two-year-old smartphones hopelessly outdated, a considerable part of the transport and military infrastructure around the world, some companies and even individual programmers are actively using technologies that have not been updated for decades. And if it seems to you that ATMs running on Windows XP are an anachronism, then get ready to be surprised.

If it works, do not need to upgrade




According to a report published in the spring of 2016 by the United States Audit Chamber, the IBM Series / 1 computers developed in the 1970s are still used in the control system for intercontinental ballistic missiles, strategic bombers and fuel-handling aircraft. They use 8-inch floppy disks as storage media. These devices are completely outdated in the 1980s. And despite the high cost of technical maintenance of computers, the US Department of Defense stated that it sees no reason for their urgent replacement.

The airport is not a place for new products.




Most likely, you are reading this text on a computer running one of the versions of Linux, Mac OS or Windows 7-10. And many used, say, Windows 95? Not to mention older operating systems?

In 2015, it turned out that the DECOR system used at Orly airport in Paris, which was used to transmit information to the pilots on the runway visibility necessary for taking off and landing airliners, is running Windows 3.1. And because of the failure of the vintage OSE, the DECOR did not work for a while .

Commenting on this incident, the head of the French avid-dispatchers union reported that some computers used in various systems that operate airports operate on UNIX.

What could be more conservative taxes?




When asked why victims of identity theft get new taxpayer codes with a delay, the plenipotentiary representative of the US Internal Revenue Service in February 2015 reported that a number of software systems of this state organization were written in COBOL during the time of John F. Kennedy . And since the IOS must maintain the continuity of accounting, these ancient products are not replaced, but only supported and patched, turning into hulking monsters over the decades. The train is not late, it is delayed




In the New York subway trains often do not maintain the schedule. The reason is that, as of 2015, the systems developed in the 1930s (!) Are used to control the movement of trains: dispatchers track the movement of trains using large light lines, recording data on paper and manually controlling arrows and semaphores. All this machinery is very difficult to maintain in working condition, and when something breaks, the repair often turns into a difficult task.

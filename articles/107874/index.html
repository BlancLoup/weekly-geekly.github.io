HTML5 attacks. First blood

So, the worst fears of Flash-developers come true. HTML5 apologists triumph. Another would be: an opportunity appeared to compare the new revolutionary technology of HTML5 from the ancient as the world, and from that dying Flash technology.

The readers are invited to test and generally evaluate the HTML5 banner , and comment on this in the comments. Do not forget that during the performance test, you should see this banner in the browser first. If not, then no luck.

Jokes, jokes, but for me it is a very, very happy event. We all saw that today Flash is perceived by users as a technology for the production of banners.

I really hope that HTML5 will retake this market for itself, and little by little the burden of responsibility for annoying and processor-eating banners will fall on HTML5. Finally, the Flash platform will begin to be perceived as a serious technology for the production of serious applications and games.

Let's wish good luck to HTML5, and we sincerely sympathize with HTML developers in connection with the next round of browser incompatibilities.

Good luck, guys. You will need it very soon.

General law of the evolution of matter

Physicists are studying the evolution of particles and the universe. Biologists study the evolution of cells and organisms. Social scientists are studying the evolution of human society. If we compare all these parts of evolution - the formation of the inherent forms of organization of matter, then we can find the general law of the formation of forms - the general law of evolution. It will equally describe the past and future evolution of matter for us. So we will know the future of humanity and will be able to prepare for it in advance.

Suppose that such a law is found. Then the correctness of his conclusion needs to be checked by experts - physicists, biologists, and social scientists. But they need to take into account an important methodological circumstance. To compare the different parts of the evolution, it was necessary to describe them in a similar, simplified way, different from that adopted in the environment of their study. A universal description of the whole process of evolution facilitates its understanding by scientists studying only its part. It will be easier for them to understand each other‚Äôs arguments and be objective.

The general law of evolution has no restrictions on the scale (size) of representatives of the new forms of organization of matter. And it grows, as seen in every part of evolution. Therefore, there are no restrictions on the amount of space in which more and more large-scale representatives of new forms are formed. This means that the process of evolution can take place not only in one of our Universe, but also in many other universes that are proportionate to it, expanding with it in the external environment. There are philosophical and cosmological grounds for suggesting just such an implementation of the principle of the multiplicity of universes. And these grounds also need to be checked by experts.

The evolution of matter can be represented as the formation in the Universe of more and more large-scale (large) representatives of the next new forms of organization of matter. For example, the next new form of matter after the formation of atoms was the formation of molecules - systems of atoms. Thus, a form is a set (or environment) of specific elements of matter representing it. The element of each new form is a system of interacting elements of the previous form. Therefore, it occupies a significantly larger volume in space compared to any element of the previous form - it is more ambitious.

Elements of the original form in suitable conditions can develop, become more complex, acquire new properties and, as a result, unite with each other into systems - into larger-scale elements of the following form. That is, they can become formative elements. In accordance with these concepts from the diversity of matter can be identified 7 forms of its organization, in the order of their occurrence:

1. quarks;
2. hadrons (mainly protons, neutrons, mesons);
3. atoms (first of all the nuclei of atoms);
4. molecules;
5. cells;
6. organisms (multicellular);
7. civilizations (from the first city-states to space).

Let us analyze the process of their formation, trying to describe it according to the general scheme.

What are the elements of the first form - quarks - science is not yet known. And what connection within the quark unites its parts into one whole is also not known. But it is known that quarks interact with each other through the exchange of gluons. With the help of this external connection for them, quarks are combined into systems - into hadrons. These are more large-scale, compared to quarks, elements of the second form of matter. The gluon bond for hadrons becomes their internal bond.

Initially, the newly formed hadrons interact on the principle of their internal communication - the exchange of gluons. This is known from hard hadron reactions. After cooling down, stable hadrons ‚Äî protons and neutrons ‚Äî acquire a fundamentally new, external connection ‚Äî the exchange of mesons (pi-mesons or pions). On its basis, they are combined into more ambitious elements of the third form of matter - atoms (atomic nuclei). The meson bond for such atoms (nuclei) becomes their internal bond.

The newly synthesized atoms (nuclei) interact on the principle of their internal connection ‚Äî the exchange of mesons. With its help, they can unite into heavier atoms, decay, exchange part of their nucleons. After cooling, the atoms acquire all of their electrons. The exchange of extreme electrons becomes for them a fundamentally new, more long-range external bond. On its basis, atoms are combined into molecules - the larger elements of the fourth form of matter. For molecules, this bond becomes their internal bond.

At first, the molecules interact on the principle of their internal connection - the exchange of electrons. With its help, they can unite into more complex molecules, disintegrate, exchange their constituent parts. In aqueous solutions, polymer molecules (in particular, proteins and nucleic acids) acquire a fundamentally new link ‚Äî the exchange of ionized monomer molecules. The monomer ions serve as material and information carriers for the synthesis of polymers. Based on this ionic bond, the molecules are combined into self-replicating systems ‚Äî cells (prokaryotes). These are more large-scale elements of the fifth form of matter. For them, the exchange of ions in the cellular cytoplasm becomes an internal bond.

At first, the cells interact on the principle of their internal communication - the exchange of ionized molecules in the aquatic environment. On its basis, some of them are united in colonies. In addition to it, eukaryotic cells in their functionally differentiated colonies acquire a fundamentally new external connection - the exchange of nerve impulses. Impulses - a new type of media. Based on new communication, cells are combined into self-reproducing multicellular organisms. These are the larger elements of the sixth form of matter. The exchange of impulses becomes their internal connection.

The first organisms interact on the principle of their internal intercellular communication - through the exchange of odor molecules and tactile nerve impulses. Then the organisms acquire a fundamentally new, more long-range light communication - vision. In her information serve as visual and speculative images, and its carrier - the photons. Images are expressed in the language of poses, gestures, facial expressions, encoded by sounds, words. With the advent of writing, social intelligent organisms are combined into self-replicating hierarchical systems ‚Äî civilizations. These are the larger elements of the seventh form of matter. Light communication becomes their internal communication.

We analyzed 7 forms of matter - from quarks with their external gluon coupling to civilizations with their internal light communication. The formation of the elements of the seventh form is known by the example of the independent formation of the first civilizations on Earth - on the continents of the Old and New Worlds. At that time, people united in city-states, and now their descendants are united into a single global civilization.

Initially, the elements of the original form of matter interact on the principle of their internal communication, and then they implement for this a fundamentally new, more long-range external communication, on the basis of which they are combined into systems - more ambitious elements of the next form of matter.

This wording of the law does not say whether all elements of the original environment follow the specified path of development and are combined into elements of the next environment. As a rule, only part of the elements succeed. The exception from it are quarks - they all unite in our space into hadrons, there are no free single quarks.

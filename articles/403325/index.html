Hexakopter Yuneec Typhoon H: professional drone with simple controls


Intel RealSense "eyes" see everything you can and need

The company Yuneec International can not be called famous and popular. However, it was she who launched the professional-grade drone on the market - the Yuneec Typhoon H hexacopter. It was created in collaboration with Intel, which put a lot of effort into the development process. Geksakopter for the first time was shown at CES 2016, where he attracted the attention of both drone specialists and ordinary technology enthusiasts.

This is not surprising - the drone looks great and works great. Despite the size, Yuneec Typhoon H can be easily transported - its ‚Äúrays‚Äù are folded, after which the hexacopter is placed in a special backpack. And yes - these "rays" are carbon sturdy supports. In the lower part of the drone is placed 3-axis suspension for the camera. You can shoot at all 360 degrees. In addition to the camera, the hexacopter differs from other ‚Äúbrothers‚Äù in a number of advantages.

What can Yuneec Typhoon H do?




The developers have tried to simplify the system, making it ready for flight out of the box. Difficult preparations or many hours of assembly are not needed - just unpack the hexacopter and you can fly. Moreover, professional skills are also not required - the smart engine and the most complex elements of the flight can perform other functions independently. One of its main advantages is the ability to identify obstacles thanks to sonars located in the front. The drone is available in three different trim levels (for details, see below). The first two imply the presence of sonars (standard and Pro version), but in the maximum configuration (Pro RS version) the copter can not only detect an obstacle and stop, but also fly around all problem areas on the go. This is possible thanks to the Intel RealSense technology, a module with the appropriate "stuffing", comes in a set and is installed on the hexacopter separately.


The technology itself works in conjunction with a special camera "Intel RealSense R200". The point is that during the flight, the drone creates a three-dimensional "picture" of the surrounding space. Analyzing the created model, the hexakopter makes the best route for itself, avoiding any obstacles in its path. In addition, Typhoon H is able to follow the goal, which makes it possible to shoot a cyclist's ride or create a miniature film about his adventures.



Drone remembers all the places you ever visited. Models of the surrounding space are stored in memory, when the copter gets to a familiar place, it uses the data already available for optimal performance. On the ground, the drone is guided by ultrasound, namely the IPS (Optical Flow Sensor) sensors. So he can fly both day and night, equally well avoiding obstacles at any time of the day.



Office Yuneec Typhoon H


To control the Typhoon H, a special ‚Äúpersonal ground station ST16‚Äù was created, the developers call their console. It runs on the Android OS. It has several controls, a pair of antennas and a 7-inch LCD display.



The drone comes in three different trim levels. In the configuration of Pro and Pro RS there is a remote control device Wizard remote. This is a minimalist console, in which there are significantly fewer buttons than at the ‚Äústation‚Äù. Wizard is equipped with its own GPS module, it can be used to easily control the drone by setting various flight modes. Flight control is carried out using the buttons "up", "down", "right" and "left". The remaining buttons on this console are responsible for working with the flight modes of the hexacopter, including the following mode. Thanks to the GPS beacon in the console, the drone will always know where the owner went.



If desired, the "station" and the remote can be used simultaneously, for example, to separate the functions of the operator and the pilot. One person controls the flight, the second - the camera.

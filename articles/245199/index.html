We are in a crisis

The crisis of power and economy. We are trying to maintain common sense and continue to work in the conditions of a new war. For this you need to understand how the system works and how it behaves when faced with problems.

Any system, state or corporation consists of 4 main modules:
  1. The parent body is the government, the composition of the board of directors or the founder / director / CEO of a company: a part of the system that is responsible for the purpose of the system‚Äôs existence and creates a structure of elements to achieve this goal
  2. The governing body is the next level of the state or organization, at which the laws of system management and the mechanisms for realizing its existence goals are created and monitored: legislative and controlling branches of power; production, development and operational management in business; department heads, vice presidents who are responsible for what products will produce or services will be provided by the business
  3. Gear mechanism - local officials, department heads; managers of the first and (more rarely) of the second level, team leaders, project managers who are responsible for the implementation of operational tasks: setting and controlling tasks to direct executors
  4. The working body is a subsystem or group of elements performing work: engineers, specialists, employees who directly perform working operations to provide a service or create a product


The system is functional in a dynamic environment and can develop if a series of simple static and dynamic laws are fulfilled, one of which says that any problem in the system must be corrected at the same level at which it is detected or one level higher.

In theory, this means that the official or manager who found the problem should analyze it, understand what he can do at his level, and if there are no decisions in his field of authority, go with it to a higher level.

In practice, when a crisis or a problem arises in politics, economics or business, one of the ways to solve it is the so-called ‚Äúexport down‚Äù - transferring the problem from the level of detection of the problem, on the contrary, to a lower level , because to escalate the problem or scary from - for potential consequences (they will be fired because they are not coping and are not competent enough for the position held) or simply there is no place (if you are a director, there is not enough knowledge or experience, and for some reason it‚Äôs impossible to involve third-party expertise).

Examples of how this works.

Fell sales. Objective reality expressed in numbers.

What management decision is most often made? Let's stimulate the sales department (the decision of the director of sales) or hire a new director of sales (the decision of the CEO or owners).

Logical only at first glance. We are with you and our product or service in a market where there are other players, where there is a price, where there are other ways to solve the problem that your product solves, where there is a dollar rate and bank interest rate on deposits, where there is your target audience and promotion channels information about your product or service and there is also definitely the way you sell and the amount of effort (work) that your sales team does.

Perhaps the market has changed. Or the niche has become less flexible in relation to the ‚Äúprice offer - a problem to be solved‚Äù, that is, it is not ready to pay more for your product or some more global problem has supplanted the problem that your product solved. That is, the problem with sales, it is quite possible is not only in the department / department of sales / sales, but is a more systemic problem of the product / marketing / market. And if the person in charge of sales is sure with the full functioning of the sales body (let us, for simplicity, further generalize the department / division / group to the word "body"), then he can go to the neighboring divisions or above and suggest something to do with marketing, product or service.

Or another example:

People began to quit , because the dollar went / ran / flew up, and the salary is pegged to the national currency? That is, in most cases, the problem detected at a certain level of the system is not solved, but turns into a problem of a lower level:
  • Fell sales - more cold calls to the manager and increase the percentage for the head of the department (stimuli!), Instead of a comprehensive effort in marketing, attracting customers and optimizing the sales funnel
  • The product is not for sale, because competitors occupy a niche - we will make more product and compete in cost, shifting the burden on production and sales (we will lower prices / quality and fill up quantity - an excellent strategy that can be called simpler "let those who are lower, work more ") Instead of making another product and selling it differently
  • People are leaving - we will hold one-time (!) Courses on motivating and retaining employees for managers instead of development and training policies, with the aim of increasing profitability per person and salary growth


There are 2 reasons why the right step is to make sure that everything is in order and to initiate changes in neighboring parts of the system does not happen.

  1. Because no one is sure that he is doing everything 100% right and he has 100% order in the processes and the unit is working at the optimum of capabilities / resources. And the higher a person gets through the system, the more highly specialized are his skills and expertise.


There are exceptions - someone figured out sales or production, everything was adjusted and went on - to establish marketing or develop an affiliate network, or at all took a managing position in the company.

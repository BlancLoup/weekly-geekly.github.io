picotux is the smallest Linux computer in the world.

While the month is full of Raspberry Pi, it's time to talk about the smallest Linux computer. Meet - picotux 100 , the brainchild of German genius.

General characteristics

  • Dimensions: 36x19x19 mm
  • Weight: 18 gr
  • Operating Temperature: -40 ¬∞ C to 85 ¬∞ C


Specifications

  • Processor: 32-bit ARM 7 Netsilicon NS7520, 55 MHz
  • RAM: 8 MB SDRAM
  • Flash memory: 2 MB (720 KB used for OS), can be expanded to 4
  • Network: 10/100 Mbit, HD and FD, auto sensing
  • Com-port: there are contacts, speed up to 230.400 bps
  • Power supply: 3.3 V, 250 mA
  • OS: uClinux 2.4.27 (Big Endian native)
  • Shell: Busybox 1.0
  • Applications: Webserver, Telnet
  • Supported filesystems: CRAMFS, JFFS2, NFS
  • Development with: GCC + binutils + uClibc


Release Date: May 18, 2005
Price: 99 Euro

A pumped model is also available - picotux 112:

It is based on the same picotux 100, but it is placed on the 53x48mm board and additionally soldered:
  • Com port
  • Power connector


The attentive reader could already conclude that on picotux 100 you will have to solder some things yourself. But that's not all, most also have to fill the image of the OS, good for this, he will get everything you need.

Well, for those who are too lazy, just 239 Euros (facepalm.jpg) you can get Starterkit. Where include: picotux 100, compact, cords, power supply.

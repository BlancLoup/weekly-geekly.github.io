Russia has created a potential replacement for LTE

Asian investors bought from Omsk Radiozavod them. A.S. Popova exclusive rights to promote in Asia the HPIP wireless standard developed in Russia. The deal amounted to $ 30 million. The new mobile standard is expected to be able to compete with LTE, surpassing it in a number of parameters, such as coverage.

UPD. The HPIP (Hybrid Public Intellectual Product) standard was presented at the 11th International IAU Forum on May 13, 2010 as part of Sviaz-Expocomm 2010. The main advantages of the standard are high-speed data transmission in protected mode, stability in conditions of high local subscriber concentration and frequency spectrum deficiency, high system capacity due to the absence of call blocking during rush hours, signal resistance to interference, as well as in conditions of intentional signal suppression.

The developers say they solved the problem of peak loads in crowded places and in emergency situations (for people and operators). As an example, the situation with the loss of communication in the 100 thousandth city is given. A mobile communication center developed by the plant is able to restore communication within 20 minutes, while traditional communication technologies will take up to several days.

The standard is positioned as "green". One of its advantages is the lesser effect on the human body in comparison with GSM and CDMA.

Among the priority users of the standard are corporate clients for whom stability and security of communication is important. A direct competitor is TETRA (Terrestrial Trunked Radio) communication standard.

The standard is already used by government agencies and has been tested in Antarctica. With his help, and on his equipment directly transmitted data to the mainland. Previously, they were transported on physical media, and operational information was transmitted via voice channels.

The funds received from the transaction will be used to develop the standard in 2011-2012. and preparations for entering the markets of Yu.-V. Asia This year it is planned to conclude the first contract with one of the Hong Kong operators.

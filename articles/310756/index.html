5 Fellowships in Computer Science



Sometimes scholarships offered by universities are designed for all specialties, but sometimes they apply only to those who choose a particular field of study. The compilation presents five scholarships intended for specialists in Computer Science - starting with the master's level and ending with post-doctoral programs. The proposals are very diverse - from the classical Swiss Higher Technical School of Zurich, one of the ten best universities in the world, to the University of King Abdulaziz in Saudi Arabia.

PhD Fellowship for International Students, Max Planck Research School for Computer Science
The Max Planck Institute offers 15 fully paid scholarships to study in a PhD program and participate in the research activities of the institute. Postdoctoral Fellowships for International Applicants, EPFL
24 post-doctoral positions in the Polytechnic University of Lausanne - one of the strongest universities in Europe. Applicants must have a PhD degree or obtain it while working on a position. Successful applicants must submit a work plan to one of the university research groups at the application stage. In the case of selection, the postdoc salary will be EUR 2,625 (this amount may be higher due to surcharges from the head of the research project) paid for 24 months.

Postgraduate Scholarships, King Abdulaziz University
The next scholarship program involves studying in a very unusual place - in Saudi Arabia. However, King Abdulaziz University is considered one of the best in the Middle East. The university offers full tuition and a monthly stipend for graduate and postgraduate students (unfortunately, only for men) in various specialties, including Computer Science.

EMEA Google Anita Borg Memorial Scholarship
Since the previous offer was intended only for men, justice must be restored: the Anita Borg scholarship awarded by Google is for women only. The program assumes that candidates must study at a university located in Europe, Africa or the Middle East in 2016/2017 and plan to continue their studies in the next academic year. The scholarships are awarded on the basis of the academic achievements of the candidates and their leadership skills.

Master Scholarship Program, ETH Zurich
The Swiss Higher Technical School Zurich offers scholarships for masters enrolled in all programs offered by the university. The main requirement for candidates is high academic results (usually you need to enter the top 10% of the course on the basis of undergraduate studies). In addition to recommendations, transcripts, and motivation letters, future students should also provide a preliminary research proposal, where they will describe the planned topic of their graduate master's work. Note that in the latest QS rating ETH Zurich took 8th place among the best universities in the world (and the same place in the world in the field of computer science).

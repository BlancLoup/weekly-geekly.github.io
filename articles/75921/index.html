To fix the approach of the hand to the switch, you can use 2 types of sensors. Capacitive - approaching the hand will change the capacitance of the capacitor, and optical - the hand reflects a certain part of the optical radiation. This design uses the optical principle that was previously used in the design of IC_bamper a.

The principle of operation is as follows. A microcontroller installed in a switch measures the amount of light by a phototransistor when the IR LED is turned on and off. If there is an obstacle in front of the switch, then part of the IR radiation will be reflected, and the difference between the measurements will appear. As soon as this difference exceeds a certain threshold, the relay will operate and switch on the load.

Notes:

1) The switch is always energized and requires 220V for its operation, therefore it may be incompatible with the wiring supplied to a conventional switch

2) The LED and phototransistor should be optically isolated from each other, such as an opaque wall.

In addition to working as a light switch, a sort of high-tech toy, you can use it wherever touch is undesirable. For example, the management of the drain in a public toilet. Controlling the flow of water through the faucet. Hundreds of possible applications.

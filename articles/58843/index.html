Why Cisco? Such a head begins a course on Cisco sales. I will call it my short essay and me.

So why Cisco? What is it about this magic word of 5 small letters? Is there nothing better?
Ask any administrator, vendor, integrator - everyone will flash because they know a case when there is a solution that is simpler, cheaper, better, more productive than a solution from Cisco ...
That's just a step to the left, a step to the right of this wonderful decision, and it becomes much more difficult to find a replacement ...
I'll try to briefly describe what the strengths of the Cisco solutions are.

Coverage. Cisco solutions have no competitors in this parameter. The widest range of all network solutions, from the SOHO market to provider solutions, from small but functional routers to network management systems of large enterprises.

Main directions:
  1. Multifunctional routers
  2. Powerful, smart switches
  3. Active Protection Devices - ASA, ACE
  4. Intrusion Prevention Systems - IPS
  5. Centralized wireless access systems
  6. Unified communications (VoIP, video conferencing, telepresence, call control systems)
  7. Host Central Protection Systems (CSA)
  8. Access Control System (NAC)
  9. And ... everything you can think of yet :)


The main thing is that cisco does not stop at what has been accomplished and ‚Äúholds its nose in the wind‚Äù, investing money in promising areas and inventing new ones, for example, buying promising developments and embedding them in its solutions. In this way, having received the support of such a giant, interesting solutions have a chance to break through "into people."

Reliability. Everything breaks down, the only question is when. Reliability cisco proven years of successful operation. I won‚Äôt lie - and Cisco has had bad series, bad releases of operating systems, however, in general, fault tolerance is beyond doubt.

Flexibility. The same hardware, depending on the operating system and module stuffing, can perform completely different functions: protection, gateway of unified communications, service ... And this means that if you want something new, there is a big chance not to buy anything, but just to type several teams.

Interdependence. A curved word, but it captures the essence. Different glands that perform different functions can depend on each other and control each other. This allows you to make the network a living organism, rather than a set of disparate devices.

Debugging It is very important to configure and customize: the broadest troubleshooting capabilities built into almost all Cisco devices.

Intellectuality It is difficult to sell just expensive iron. We must sell the idea and opportunity. All Cisco devices contain a wide range of technologies, protocols, ideologies, both standard and their own, allowing you to expand the capabilities of the network.

Performance. Cisco is a leader in many market segments and must comply with this high rank. Therefore, unique solutions appear, such as CRS (one such piece of hardware is enough to provide, say, the entire UK with communication!). Now top-end solutions with 10-gigabit interfaces are in the firewall segment, in the router segment, and in the segment of intrusion prevention systems ...

Centralization. Cisco devices can be managed not one by one, but using powerful systems, for example, cisco security manager. Also centrally, you can collect all sorts of statistics and analyze it - MARS. No one has yet proposed such a decision on the centralization of accounting.

Of course, never the solutions of one manufacturer can not be the best everywhere. This is just one example of when "there is a much better solution."

Moreover, I will tell sedition: not everyone needs Cisco solutions! Why pay for the simplest solutions that cisco will cost significantly more? Why pay for extra features if you never use them? We need only to think: what if these ‚Äúexcesses‚Äù are required?

Well, at the end of the list of typical misconceptions. Rather, it is one thing: Cisco is extremely expensive!
It's a delusion. And it is due to the fact that the customer is offered a solution that is not designed for it! The company cisco has a lot of resources (some of them I explicitly collected on AntiCisco ), allowing you to choose a solution. But the trick is that domestic "medium" companies fall, as a rule, into the lower range of SMB (Small-Medium Business). And they are offered, with an annual turnover of $ 50k, solutions for $ 25k ...

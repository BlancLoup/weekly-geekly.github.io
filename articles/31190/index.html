Why not start a startup

My experience came from three companies that I founded on: Netscape, which was sold to AOL in 1998 for $ 4.2 billion; Opsware (once Loudcloud), an open-source software company with a capitalization of approximately $ 1 billion, and now Ning is a new, closed consumer Internet company. But in fact, I was very lucky and took part in a very large number of startups from the moment I came to Silicon Valley in 1994: as a board member, as an angel investor, as an advisor, as a friend of some of the founders and as a participant several venture funds. Why not start a startup!

Startups, even as a result of the collapse of 2000, became impregnated with mystery - you read a lot about how great a startup is, how fun it is, the opportunity to change the future, free food, foosball and everything else.

And as it turned out, there are a lot of wonderful things in creating a startup . And on the example of my experience, this is:

  • First of all, it is an opportunity to control your own destiny . You succeed or fail yourself, and there is no boss to tell you what to do. For some people, this is enough to create a startup.
  • The ability to create something new - a well-known blank sheet of paper. You have the opportunity, in fact, the obligation to present a new product that does not actually exist, and create it, without any obstacles that large companies have.
  • The opportunity to shake the world - to give people a new way to communicate, a new way to share information, a new way to work together or something else that you think will make the world a better place. It seems to you that people with low income need a way to borrow money more easily? Create a prosper . Think television should be open to a huge number of channels? Create a joost . Do you think computers should work on UNIX and use open standards rather than proprietary technologies? Create a sun .
  • The ability to create your ideal culture and work with a dream team made up of people that you yourself put together . Do you want your culture to be based on people who have fun every day and enjoy teamwork, or seriously compete in work and games, or are strongly focused on creating innovative technologies in the field of rocket production? You can choose and create your own culture and the right team.
  • And finally - the money ! Properly created startups, of course, can be very profitable. This does not apply solely to greed. When everything goes well, your team and hired employees will feel great, they will be able to support their family, send their children to college, fulfill their dreams, and this is, in fact, wonderful. And if you are really lucky, you, as the founder, can afford an exorbitant charity that will change society for the better.

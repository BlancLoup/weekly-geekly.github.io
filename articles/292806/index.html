RSP, RAO and WIPO are united in one organization for fees in favor of the right holders

On September 18, 2015, the Ministry of Culture of Russia will determine an organization that will charge for personal use of phonograms and audiovisual works in favor of their authors, performers and producers. The tax is collected from manufacturers and importers of equipment, since it is difficult to collect it from people who listen to music and watch movies. Since 2010, the Russian State Union of Rights Holders (RSP), which has been founded by the Russian Authors' Society, the All-Russian Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO) and the Filmmakers Union of the Russian Federation, has collected deductions.

The Russian authors' society collects payments from television channels, radio stations, cinemas, restaurants and other public institutions in favor of composers and songwriters. The All-Russian Intellectual Property Organization collects taxes in favor of performers and manufacturers.

In 2013, the RSP collected 4.3 billion rubles, at the end of the year he concluded 92 contracts with organizations for the payment of the author's fee. In 2013, RAO collected almost 4.4 billion rubles, of which 3.2 billion amounted to payments to authors, and the organization spent 1 billion rubles on its own needs. Three organizations are managed by one team. The First Deputy Director General of the RSP Andrei Krychesky is the President of WIPO and is the Deputy Chairman of the Board of Directors of RAO.

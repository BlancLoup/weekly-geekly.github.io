Intel announced support for Steam Machines and announced Dual OS officially + response from AMD

A few hours ago, I already wrote about a laptop with two OS: Android and Windows. ( Laptop-transformer with two OS from ASUS ). Now, Intel has officially announced its development - Dual OS.

However, if you thought that they "filed" the instantaneous switching between any OS - it was not there: only Android-Windows transitions. Intel demonstrated on the scene on the example of a laptop almost instant switching between operating systems from Microsoft and Google.


But not only Intel believes in the life-giving power of Android. AMD also relies on the partnership with Bluestacks - the latest company's technology allows you to run full-screen and window Android applications right in the Windows environment, and thanks to the ARM core built into the new AMD processors, the most demanding part of the Android code can be performed quite effectively.

However, at its conference, Intel also announced support for the new Valve project - the Steam Machine. She volunteered to release as many as 14 manufacturers and all the processors from Intel will be put in there. Also declared support for graphics accelerators from Intel.

Mr. Newell demonstrated on the scene the work of the game console, based solely on Intel chips - we are talking about Gigabyte Brix Pro. At the core of this Steam Machine, equipped with 8 GB of RAM and a 1 TB drive, is an Intel Core i7-4770R processor with integrated Iris Pro 5200 graphics. The Brix Pro system is so compact that in the hands of Gabe Newell it seems almost less than the Valve controller Steam.

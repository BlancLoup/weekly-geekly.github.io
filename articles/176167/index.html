Highscreen Yummy Duo - the most budget smartphone with two SIM-cards and a full set of communications

Looking ahead, I will say that everything turned out very well. A smartphone costs 4,990 rubles. For a close price now you can find quite a lot of entry-level models. But, first of all, the Yummy Duo niche is a phone with two SIM-cards. And, secondly, the phone has all the communications: Wi-Fi, GPRS, 3G, Bluetooth, GPS-receiver and various sensors. Well, the price, albeit not much, but lower than competitors.

I will tell about both generations of Highscreen Yummy Duo, because the new model is very significantly different from the old one for the better. I mainly tested the new model, so I will mention the old one only for comparison.

Impressions of work



Screen

To be honest, I was already used to the Samsung Super AMOLED, so I was very skeptical about 800 x 480 screen, and TN too. But, having used the week, I can say that the screen is quite decent. Pixels, of course, are noticeable, even on a 4.3-inch screen, but the eyes do not get tired, especially if you set the brightness to automatic. With viewing angles, everything is predictable for TN - they are small. But with the usual reading from the screen it does not interfere. It is quite possible to use it as a navigator - the brightness margin is large, all objects and inscriptions are distinguishable. The only thing is that it is absolutely impossible to make a small print on such a resolution in order to, for example, look at the entire web page. But this, in general, is rarely required. As a summary - I would recommend the manufacturer to abandon the TN matrix even in the budget model and switch to IPS - this is unlikely to add more than 300-500 rubles to the cost. The rest is quite normal.

CPU

It‚Äôs hardly appropriate to talk about the old model now - its processor is not even able to play video with any decent resolution. In the new model, the processor is noticeably faster. I checked out some test videos. With Full HD can not cope unambiguously, but here is 720p H.264 quite pulls. True, on some very heavy profiles it still slows down. But maybe I did not understand something, because MX Player reported that hardware acceleration is off. Maybe some other player is needed, because the MT6575M processor, according to the specifications, supports 720p hardware.

As for the usual work - everything is fine here. Applications run pretty fast. Yandex.Navigator does not slow down, moves and rotates the map smoothly. I did not check the games, but those interested can find a verified list on the page dedicated to this phone at w3bsit3-dns.com (link below). The device under load is practically not heated.

Memory

The amount of RAM by today's standards is small, but I did not notice any problems. There is no need to wait for strong multitasking, but it works quickly with an active application.

About 512 MB ROM is, of course, a strange move. As far as I understand, in this model every penny was counted. Other reasons not to put at least 4 GB, I do not see. On the other hand, Android has enough of this volume, and a microSD card can be put on 32 GB and not know the grief. Funny, but the camera refuses to shoot, if not installed microSD-card. Applications from the market are also installed by default immediately on microSD.

GPS

A cold start takes quite a long time - even with A-GPS turned on for about 2 minutes (perhaps I unsuccessfully picked up a place for testing in the courtyards). But when you restart, the coordinates are determined almost immediately, even at a completely different point in the city. I did not notice any problems with positioning accuracy. Phone when GPS is turned on almost does not heat up For example, the Samsung Galaxy Note is noticeably warm after a long navigation.

Battery

I did not use the phone for very long and did not make special measurements. But it was surprising that with episodic use during the day (mostly reading the Internet), the charge without problems lasts for 2-3 days. This is also confirmed by the owners at w3bsit3-dns.com. I also launched an extremely voracious Yandex Navigator and left it for 3 hours (the screen was on, the brightness on the machine). During this time, the battery is 30% discharged. Those who know what navigation is on the phone will confirm that this is a very good indicator.

Camera

I especially did not check the front camera, and the rear camera shoots well. I did not notice any post-processing artifacts. Although, in low light the picture is noisy. Video shoots up to 720p.

Radio module and two SIM cards

The device has one radio module. The principle of operation of two SIM-cards is described in the review of the old Yummy Duo model on iXBT: ‚Äújust like in a regular phone, only one radio module is installed here. Therefore, a full-fledged "dvuhsimosti" here we can talk only in relation to the standby mode. Then both SIM-cards are really active and are in simultaneous access. But it is only necessary to establish a connection or begin the process of transferring data using one SIM-card, as the other immediately becomes unavailable. At the same time, the caller to the second number will not hear the invitation to wait on the line, but will receive a release and a message that the subscriber is unavailable. ‚Äù

However, the new Yummy Duo has a more modern chipset, in the description of which there is such a phrase: "No lost calls on either SIM - even with active data transmission on either SIM". I checked and made sure that when loading data through one operator, the incoming call through the second one comes without problems. However, two simultaneous incoming calls did not work, as described above.

Separate remark about ‚Äúcheaper in China‚Äù


Yes, in China will be cheaper. Because if a person buys in a Chinese online store with mail delivery, he actually buys in China. The price does not include customs duties and VAT.

And the guarantee and support of such purchases are not provided.

Those. you can buy for yourself, and many do. But it will not be possible to trade in large retail networks - this is, in terms of our laws, smuggling, in fact.

If the company imports devices from China officially, officially customs clearance, pays duties and taxes to the state and officially puts the goods in large retail chains (these are the so-called ‚Äúwhite devices‚Äù), then the pricing is:

Price in China = $ 100 (for example).
As soon as the goods cross the border + 26% (customs duty, VAT - 18%, delivery, insurance, etc.) = already $ 126.
Distributor takes interest + 10% minimum = $ 125 * 1.1 = $ 139.
The retail network takes interest + 30% minimum = $ 139 * 1.3 = $ 181.

That is, in our retail network, any product will be more expensive by 80% -100% (almost twice) than it is worth in China. And this is not a whim of sellers, but the realities of a legal white electronics market in Russia.

The distributor (vendor-brand) earns the least here, that is, Highscreen. Most of all earns retail, which is forced to pay retail space, warehouses, pay salaries to sellers, provide logistics, distribution of goods throughout the country.

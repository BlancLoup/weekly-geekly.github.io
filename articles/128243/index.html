And now in our opinion from the most interesting (and this is in a couple of clicks directly from the Control Panel):

Virtual machine autoscaling
You yourself set the rules for processing expansion and reducing consumption. Your virtual machine will automatically upgrade to perform demanding tasks and even, according to the policy you set, can make downgrade to save your money.

Loadbalancer clustering
You simply specify the port by which you want to balance your list of virtual machines and the balancer itself monitors the load distribution and viability of the machines (if one machine does not respond to it, requests will not be received and traffic will be distributed to other machines). Loadbalancer Clustering will not allow overloading (according to the load) any of the cluster machines and the cluster as a whole. Such a performance guard will always ensure the maximum power of your cluster system.

Loadbalancer Autoscaling
We think this is the most delicious. This is the same as Loadbalancer Cluster but with rules for expanding and reducing load. You simply set a pre-configured template and as the load increases, the required number of new virtual servers are deployed, which are automatically included in the balancing cluster, and when the load decreases, they are deleted (if you wish). This gives a very strong saving effect for projects with large load peaks, so for our clients (the developers of the popular Odnoklassniki game) we were able to improve performance and at the same time save money compared to their old infrastructure, with minimal changes in the application architecture.

Neural network hardware accelerator is connected via USB



The US company Movidius is known as the developer of the Myriad 2 VPU neural network hardware accelerator and chipset provider for the GoogleNet artificial intelligence system.

After optimization and preparation of the binary in the Fathom Deep Learning Software Framework framework, the neural network effectively operates on the Myriad 2 accelerator with power consumption less than 1 W. Such chips are ideal for robots, multicopters, smartphones, surveillance cameras, augmented reality helmets - any gadgets where object recognition, speech recognition, object tracking, navigation, etc. are useful.

Simultaneously with the Fathom framework, the company Movidius today at the Embedded Vision Summit in California for the first time showed the public the Fathom Neural Compute Stick flash accelerator, the world's first device of its kind. Here, in general, everything is immediately ready for use. The flash drive with the built-in accelerator Myriad 2 is simply inserted into any device with a USB port.

Thus, developers can now natively test neural networks right away on target devices for which they are designed.



When connected to a computer, the Fathom Neural Compute Stick USB flash drive serves as a profiling and evaluation tool, speeding up the prototyping and training of the neural network. Fathom supports core depth learning frameworks, including Caffe and TensorFlow.

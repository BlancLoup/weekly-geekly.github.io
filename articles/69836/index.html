Ukrainian scientists first "photographed" the atom



Probably all the publications will be about this now, but I cannot be silent. Scientists from the Kharkov Institute of Physics and Technology presented the first atomic images in the history of science.

To take pictures, scientists used an electron microscope that detects radiation and fields (field-emission electron microscope, FEEM). Physicists sequentially placed tens of carbon atoms in a vacuum chamber and passed through them an electrical discharge of 425 volts. The radiation of the last atom in the chain onto the phosphor screen allowed to obtain an image of a cloud of electrons around the nucleus.

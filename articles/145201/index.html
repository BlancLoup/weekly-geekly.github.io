Samsung Galaxy Note 2 promise in October 2012


Samsung Galaxy Note 2 promises in October with an even larger 5.5 inches screen and two Exynos 5250 core processors.


Over the past six months, Samsung have sold more than 7 million Galaxy Note, and are going to sell another 3 million units. Naturally, this success must be consolidated, and Samsung began to develop a sequel.

We don‚Äôt really know anything about it except for the name, but we can assume that technologies like S Beam, S Voice, NFC and SmartStay will be transferred to the new Note from the Galaxy S3.

It is said that the new Note will have a slightly larger 5.5 inches screen with a resolution of WSXGA + (1680 √ó 1050) and a density of 360 pixels. Why not Full HD? The human eye is not able to distinguish pixels at a density above 300 ppi, so it does not make sense to lift up the resolution above this. The Super AMOLED monitor (not Plus) of the PenTile matrix family was preferred by the RGB AMOLED Plus because of its longer lifespan and energy savings.

Before the release of the Galaxy S3, it was rumored that it would be equipped with an Exynos 5250 processor on the latest ARM Cortex-A15 architecture, but the rumors were wrong. It is logical to assume that this processor can be used in Galaxy Note 2.

The Galaxy Note 2 will have an 8 megapixel camera with improved optics and a sensor. The RAM will be increased to 1.5 GB and the international version will receive 4G LTE by default.

The appearance of the second Note will resemble a younger brother, the Galaxy S3, something like the one in the picture. The trend of the design of Samsung is visible on the latest innovations - Galaxy S3 and Focus 2.

The release is scheduled for October, but, of course, is not confirmed.

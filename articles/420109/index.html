August 7, the head of Tesla Inc. announced that he was thinking about the possibility of selling his company to private investors. He told about it in his Twitter, saying that the value of the shares could reach $ 420 apiece. At the same time, the businessman expressed hope that all current Tesla investors will remain with the company, and the relationship will not cease to be trustworthy.

After this announcement, the shares of the electric vehicle manufacturer almost immediately grew by 8%, and then by another 3%. Given Tesla's debts, its total value in the event of a stock price increase would have been $ 82 billion.

Then, on August 7, the well-known publication Financial Times announced the purchase of a stake in a transport company in the amount of 3-5% by the State Investment Fund of Saudi Arabia.

By the way, Mask had previously expressed his desire to delist Tesla with the sale of the company to private investors. In particular, he spoke about this to Rolling Stone magazine reporters in November 2017. He said that in the hands of private investors, the company could become more efficient. One of the positive nuances of sales is the reduction of external pressure on Tesla Inc, which is now permanently under close supervision by regulators and market participants.


A few days after Mask sent tweets, the shareholders sued him. They considered that the sale would be a clear violation of the federal securities law, as well as the law on the protection of investors' rights.

One of the lawsuits filed by trader Kalman Isaac, who believes that Mask simply does not have the means to buy back the securities of his company.

About underestimating trivia






Oh, China! How wonderful you are with your low price! This is me to the question of testing and packaging. The virtual line itself, the Wi-Fi module and the camera module came to us in anti-static zip bags, which are fastened with a snap of a white plastic strip. The bag is small, the fastener is strong - after a dozen bags, fingers begin to hurt.

To check the kit, you need to:
- open the latch and pull out the virtual tape from the bag
- open the latch and pull out of the Wi-Fi bag
- open the latch and pull out the camera module from the bag
- unplug the protective red film from the virtual tape connectors (2 connectors)
- remove the protective film from the connectors Wi-Fi (2 slots)
- plug the cable into the camera and the virtual video
- screw the antenna
- insert lace in MicroUSB and stick a flash drive (to test the USB)
- Insert the MicroSD card with the test firmware
- Connect the mini-TV to the AV output
- apply power and wait for the loading and passing tests, results are displayed on the TV
- in the case of failed tests to replace component , not passed the test, and repeat

Test passed? Okay, pack up!
- unscrew the antenna
- unfasten the camera cable from the virtual camera and the camera board
- disconnect the flash drive
- pull out the MicroSD
- remove everything in bags
- pack foam in the box and store the contents
- close the box
- note that it is assembled

For all of these operations, the conveyor system was thought out - while the download is in progress and the test is a mass unpacking or unsticking of the stickers, or an assembly.

Why am I saying all this? This is a valuable experience. For the future, our main conclusion is that absolutely all mass simple operations should definitely be done in China. Let it cost a little more, take more time, let the delivery be more expensive (when everything is laid out in boxes, the volume weight of the delivery dramatically increases) - it will definitely pay off. Without providing such trifles, you can "drown" in the routine. On some days I was finishing the tests and packing by five in the morning, as a result - mistakes in order picking.

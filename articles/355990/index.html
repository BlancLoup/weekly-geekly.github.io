AdBlock will show ads to all 50 million of its users to combat censorship.

The duration of the action is one day, and social advertising will be shown.



The AdBlock team announced its intention to show all 50 million of its users ads for a day. True, it will not be a commercial advertisement, but a social one dedicated to the fight against censorship. For example, a message from Edward Snowden will be shown. All this will be shown in the form of standard banners.

The campaign, according to its initiators, aims to attract public attention to censorship. According to representatives of AdBlock, the governments of different countries are increasingly looking for ways to establish control over the communication of ordinary people.

Banners, which are planned to show users, are developed in Amnesty International. As mentioned above, all social advertising will show only a day, after which everything will return to its place.

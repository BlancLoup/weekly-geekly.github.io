Black Friday. How did it all really



December 6, 2013 in Russia was a black Friday . It was announced about big discounts.
I found out about it in one article a month before the start. After reading a lot of comments on that article, I realized that all users agree that:
  1. There will be no grand discounts.
  2. If the discounts are still there, then only on those products that are not really needed.
  3. The discount itself comes down to the fact that on this day the price of the goods is too high, but due to the discount it drops to the original level, but not less.

It was decided to find out how everything actually happens.
That article mentioned several companies participating in the action. I chose 2 companies for analysis: Sotmarket and White Wind .

Data acquisition


The period for analysis was chosen from December 3 to December 9 inclusive, in order to analyze the trend of price changes before, during and after Black Friday.

To begin with, a Delphi program was written that downloads a page from its URL and saves it as an HTML file to disk.
Then the product categories for each site were selected. Categories are URLs with products. In each category there were several pages with goods (the number reached several hundred pages in one category).

Then the program was started manually every day at approximately 05:00 Moscow time. The program downloaded the page at its URL with all pages for all categories and saved as an HTML file to disk. The download statistics are as follows:
  • About 40,000 HTML files downloaded.
  • The file size is about 6 GB.
  • Download time 2.5 hours per day.


After that, an algorithm was written (also on Delphi) for parsing goods and prices from the resulting files. After two days of continuous operation of the algorithm, data on goods and prices of goods for each day were obtained. Statistics of the number of goods is as follows:
  • Sotmarket: 164 884
  • White wind: 9 048


Data processing


The same product was not always on sale from December 3rd to 9th. Accordingly, the price of the goods on some day was not. I made some assumption and accepted that the price of the goods for such a day would be equal to the price of the goods for the previous day.

From the analysis the goods were removed, whose price for 3 and 4 and 5 December was absent. This is done because in this case the price of the goods begins on December 6, and in this situation it is impossible to talk about any dynamics and price change on a black Friday, since the product just went on sale on December 6th.

As a result, in the analyzed data only the quantity of goods remained, for which the prices for all days are indicated:
  • Market Sotmarket: 158 379
  • White wind: 8 677

Small departments gradually formed, the key ones being the software development department and the design department, but it turned out that the software development department was always overloaded and the design department was underloaded.

And then the thought was born: how, first, to load up the design department, secondly, to help the company get extra money, because it‚Äôs not a secret for anyone that there is not always money (someone underpaid, someone deferred payment and etc.), thirdly, take a break from sites and databases and fourthly use two of their higher education (electrical and architectural).

Therefore, the key position was the rule "We must try to occupy the most unique niche with a potentially maximum income." And such a niche was found: Christmas decoration and lighting of country houses. Since we are talking about country houses, then the client must be with a good income.

Then there was the standard stage with full-time design department: the development of the style and the project site, with which the department successfully managed. The response was not long in coming: 1-2 calls per day, starting from the end of November. I perfectly understood that the company would not pull more than 3-4 orders, exactly 4 orders we took to work, the rest had to be refused. There were enough reasons for refusal: this is a lack of suppliers of jewelry and garlands for the new year and this is a drawback of the found installation teams.

Designers had to travel to objects, photograph them, and then draw decorations and lighting on photos.

The established principles of working with the client: payment for departure and photo processing immediately, the choice of decoration and lighting options, note of the option, some adjustment, 75-80% prepayment, work, 20-25% surcharge upon completion.

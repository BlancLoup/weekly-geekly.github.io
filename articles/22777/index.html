While studying at university, we often dealt with extreme programming in pairs with him, which gave excellent results. I do not know how I would write a thesis, if not for him. In recent years, Matvey has been studying PS, direct communication between a computer and a person. As far as I knew, he was the leader of the PS research team, but he has not yet achieved significant results. Something told me that the coincidence was not accidental, and I decided to check this option first.

Matvey as always was online and happily agreed to meet. A rare programmer will refuse free treats, which I promised. Two hours later we were sitting in a cafe on Mokhovaya.

Matvey waved his fork and shook his head, being unable to answer articulately. I poured him wine, he himself lit another cigarette. It was difficult for me to hide my excitement, but Matvey seemed to have noticed nothing, having buried himself in his plate. It's good that he is a computer specialist, not a psychologist. Finally, Matvey dealt with his portion of lasagna. We sat for another twenty minutes, remembering youth, until Matthew was called from work. We hugged and said goodbye.

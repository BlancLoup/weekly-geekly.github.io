Pascal


It is an iron constructor in a gray plain box. Details are of three types - long but narrow, short but wide and corner. Fastened together with cogs. If there are 3 places in the details where you can screw a screw - then you need to screw them there, even if the screws do not hold anything! Otherwise the whole structure will fall apart at once.
There are several examples for creativity - a small house, an average house, a large house, and for some reason, something like a helicopter. Incredibly, the helicopter can even fly.
All, absolutely all models are built on the same plan - below the foundation, on top of the roof, in the middle of the empty frame. But inside the frame you can shove absolutely everything, although it does not fit with the modern concepts of design. But you can always imagine that two rails are the elevator of a large house, and three rails are the engine of a helicopter! Everything is real!
In Pascal, you can even sometimes use ropes - this will allow you to create flexible dynamic structures. To do this, you need to assemble an iron box, firmly fasten it and put a string in it.
An experienced pascalist, remembering youth, will do absolutely everything on Pascal.

C ++


Having bought C ++, you think you bought a designer, but in fact it is a whole repair kit. To the main box, you can then separately purchase many large and small boxes with parts of complex shape, which greatly simplify life.
C ++ is a very complex constructor, to use it you must first read the instructions on the back of the box. If this is not enough, you can mail out a complete collection of instructions from 10 volumes of 600 pages each.
Some collect stools. Others boast that they have assembled a working adjustable wrench and now it helps them in their work. There are cases when the existing firearms were assembled from the C ++ constructor. Two of them accidentally shot themselves, the rest were taken to the police.
The C ++ set includes three dozen basic parts, a variety of ropes, paper, glue, and nail clippers. When necessary, you can independently assemble the necessary part.
Those who started with the designer Pascal, continue to build heavy reliable structures. Beginners build unimaginably ugly structures that are easier to break than fix. And finally, only those who are interested in C ++ for quite a long time are able to build an openwork structure weighing one and a half tons, with one fulcrum and a changing center of gravity - thanks to the ability to use ropes.
C ++ is the main language for harsh bearded designers, but those who have been repeatedly crushed by the disintegrated handicraft do not like it.

HTML is not even a constructor, it is rather a mosaic. Details - pieces of paper. All of them, except for a couple of pieces, are square and differ only in color. A huge number of guides and fake "mosaic professionals" give advice on how to turn a mosaic into a puzzle.

Designer Assembler need to give only incredibly assiduous or unloved children.

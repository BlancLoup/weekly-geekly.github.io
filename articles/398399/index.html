Creating cars that are controlled by a computer is the goal of several companies at once. The Renault Nissan Alliance plans to begin testing UAVs in 2016, Apple will release production models in 2019, and Tesla is already testing its autopilot system on public roads. In theory, the massive use of unmanned vehicles will increase the speed of movement, reduce accidents, make the movement more efficient and simplify the lives of all participants in the autosphere: from drivers to insurers. The term telematics means the unification of telecommunication technologies and informatics. This is a small device with a set of sensors that collects information about the movement of the car, informing its insurance company. We are talking about the average speed, mileage, the number of sharp accelerations and braking. In the future, such technologies will be able to determine the condition of the car and even independently report damage.

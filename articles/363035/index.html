Winter City Astronomy



Autumn ends. Rain, mud and slush are a thing of the past, and continuous clouds get a chance to be torn by a cold atmospheric front or anticyclone. The days are short, and in the morning and in the evening you can see the stars. You just have to look up. What will be interesting in the sky this winter?

Moon



Photo by ansiivan Astroforum .

The moon is a holiday that is always with you. Well, almost always, considering the new moon. Or even "sometimes" if you take the amendment for the weather. All the more interesting to look at the sky - is the moon visible today? Now the moon is in the phase of the old moon, and is visible in the morning, gradually shifting to the east (at the same time, for example, when you go to work, it will be all east). The new moon is expected on November 22, after which the moon will be visible in the evenings. Gradually moving to the east, the moon will reach the full moon on December 6th.

Non-astronomical beautiful winter phenomena


The cooling of the atmosphere gives two interesting effects that can be observed in winter. These are not astronomical phenomena, but meteorological, but this does not make them less beautiful.

Light poles


Photo by user Andrey Lozhkin Astroforum .

When the air temperature in winter drops, the atmospheric moisture condenses into snow crystals even without the formation of clouds. If you watched the weather, you probably saw that light snow could come from a clear sky. If the crystals are flat or hexagonal, then atmospheric "light guides" can form, along which the light from the earth rises up with a beautiful light column.

Halo


A source

Under the same conditions, when there are ice crystals in the atmosphere, a halo can form around the Sun or the Moon.


A sign of increased likelihood of a halo or light pillars is cooling. The probability is especially high if a few days is clear, cold, and windless weather. Be careful and do not miss the beauty.

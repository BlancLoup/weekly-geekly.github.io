She has very valuable information for Google on users shopping in retail stores.

It has now become known that select Google advertisers have access to a new tool for about a year, which allows them to track purchases at offline retail stores after displaying relevant advertising on the Internet . More than 2 billion Mastercard cardholders knew nothing about secretly spying on them.

The deal is still kept secret, it was reported by Bloomberg , citing several sources of information. According to them, Google paid Mastercard millions of dollars for getting data. In addition, the companies discussed the scheme of advertising revenue from the use of this information.

Google and Mastercard have been negotiating the deal for about four years, according to four people with knowledge of the deal. Although the sources are anonymous, but the information is reliable: three of them directly worked on this transaction and know all the details. This collaboration gave Google direct access to evaluating advertising effectiveness through tracking retail purchases. Google's revenues are directly dependent on the accuracy of advertising targeting, so that thanks to the deal, the company gained an advantage over its competitors.

The Google and Mastercard Alliance is an example of merging an online advertising platform with offline data collection. Google can now relate ad impressions and user visits to specific web pages with his coordinates offline. According to a Google Data Collection study, the Android smartphone in sleep mode (with an active Chrome browser in the background) transmits location information to Google 340 times over a 24-hour period, that is, an average of 14 data transfers per hour. In fact, location information accounts for 35% of all data samples sent to Google.

Movement tracking


From 2014, Google will show advertisers if a user who clicks on an ad visits a physical store of an advertiser. This is done using the Location History feature in Google Maps. So you can evaluate the effectiveness of online advertising: Google checks with the profiles and determines how many people have made purchases and whether they used to go through advertising links. In addition, Google buys data from third-party brokers, such as Experian and Acxiom, which provide demographic and financial information for marketers.

But all these methods did not help too much to increase advertising momentum. Not all stores can connect a list of e-mail addresses to their ads, and information from data brokers is inaccurate or late. But then Google connected the data on purchases from real maps.

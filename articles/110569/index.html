All the pros and cons of MeeGo

The MeegGo operating system owes its appearance to the rise in popularity of smartphones in recent years. Well, tell me, who, before the appearance of the iPhone, thought that applications needed to be downloaded from a special store, and not to be installed from a disc purchased in a tent? Who thought that all the necessary programs should be built into the PC? Who was interested in PC integration with social networks and mail? Now the OS, which does not know how to do all this, looks strange, but MeeGo does not belong to this.

What is what


MeeGo - the main hope of Intel and Nokia to create their own platform to produce smartphones, netbooks, tablets, build it into cars, refrigerators and irons. MeeGo was born from the joint development of companies on the Linux kernel - Maemo and Moblin, and roughly equally uses the achievements of both Nokia and Intel. But they are not united - AMD, the main competitor of Intel, unexpectedly jumped on the last car of developers, recognizing that MeeGo is likely to become the standard for such systems, and promising to invest its resources and knowledge in OS development. This means that netbooks and tablets with MeeGo will not only be on the Intel Atom, but also on AMD Fusion - long live the competition. The operation has two branches - Netbook UX and Handheld UX, which, as the names imply, are ‚Äúsharpened‚Äù under different device types. I tested the "netbook" edition, but from the review you can understand the key features of the system as a whole. Netbook UX is made in the interface adapted for netbooks, the current version at the time of this writing is MeeGo 1.1, it is now available for download from Meego.com.

Summary


MeeGo is a new milestone in operating systems for computers, it initially has almost everything that the average user needs, whether it is support for social networks or watching downloaded movies. It took me half an hour to master its interface. It's also nice that the Windows keyboard shortcuts, such as Alt-Tab, Ctrl-A or Ctrl-V, work, although if you experiment, you can also call the legendary command line (Alt-F1). The system is far from perfect - here and there you come across a lack of the usual and quite simple functionality, but it is quite efficient and fairly stable - it can already be used for its intended purpose: to browse Internet pages, play movies and music, open office files and read mail, in passing Corresponding with friends in ICQ and Google Talk. As an operating system for a netbook, it can replace or stand next to Windows 7 or XP. MeeGo, of course, is poorer in the settings, and fewer programs for it are available, but it loads faster, it has easier access to popular functions, and 90% of the necessary applications are already installed and do not require money or registration. When MeeGo gains strength and acquires a good base of applications, I will definitely put it on my netbook.

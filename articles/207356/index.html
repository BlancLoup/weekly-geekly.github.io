Article 184. Violation of Copyright and / or Related Rights
1. Assignment of authorship or coercion to co-authorship, if this act caused significant harm to the rights or legitimate interests of the author or other rightholder, including causing significant damage, is punishable by a fine in the amount of one hundred to five hundred monthly calculation indices or by engaging in public works for a term from one hundred to one hundred eighty hours, or restriction of freedom for up to two years.
2. Illegal use of copyright and (or) related rights, as well as the acquisition, storage, transfer or manufacture of counterfeit copies of copyright and (or) related rights for marketing purposes, committed in significant amounts - shall be punished with a fine of from five hundred to seven hundred monthly calculation indices either by engaging in public works for a period of one hundred eighty to two hundred and forty hours, or by restriction of freedom for a period of up to one year.

Illegal use of copyright and (or) related rights by organizing, creating Internet resources for further access to the exchange, storage, transfer of copies of works and (or) objects of related rights in an electronic digital format that caused significant damage - shall be punished with a fine in in the amount of from six to eight hundred monthly calculation indices either by engaging in public works for a period of from two hundred to two hundred and forty hours, or by restriction of freedom for a period of up to one year.

findings

In Ukraine and Kazakhstan, getting a criminal case is about the same in complexity - you need to install pirated software for a little more than $ 1,000 (this is 5-7 "Windows 7 Pro"). In Russia - twice as difficult. In this case, if the stars do not stand up well, then you can immediately get a deadline.
But with the maximum punishment all have everything in different ways.
In Russia and Ukraine, if you are lucky, even in the case of damage on a large scale, you can remain at large. In Ukraine, in general, you can only get off with a fine (although the fine, I must say, is not the smallest). But in Kazakhstan, not only is it enough to cause damage at only $ 5,500 (this is one fully configured server from Microsoft), you also have to sit anyway.

The findings show that although all three states have a lot in common, they all relate to this crime differently: someone prefers to penalize more strongly, and someone immediately imprisons.

NMDG value

When discussing the article, some doubts arose about the value of NMDG (the non-taxable minimum income of citizens) in Ukraine. There are several options:
  • the value adopted in the table is correct, that is, NMDG = 573.5 (this is for 2013, in 2014 - 609);
  • to classify the damage caused, it is necessary to use the value of NMDG in 573.5, and to calculate the amount of fines - 17;
  • the correct value is NMDG - 17.

Such discrepancies cause vagueness of the formulation:
Tax Code of Ukraine, Section XX, subsection 1, paragraph 5.
If the norms of other laws contain references to the non-taxable minimum incomes of citizens, then for the purpose of their application, an amount of 17 hryvnias is used, except for administrative and criminal legislation regarding the qualification of crimes or offenses, for which the amount of the non-taxable minimum is set at the level of tax social benefits defined subparagraph 169.1.1 of paragraph 169.1 of Article 169 of Section IV of this Code for the relevant year.

Information on the Internet is also a difference: they say that it is necessary to use the value 573.5 , and on the website of the SAI they use the value 17 .
Unfortunately, we could not find any references to court verdicts, by which one could unambiguously understand what meaning the judges use. Therefore, in the table I will leave the value 573.5 only because the resulting figures obtained with this value of the NMDG look more logical in comparison with the figures for other states.

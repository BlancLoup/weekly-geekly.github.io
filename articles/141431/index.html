The accident on the cellular network Vodafone in the Netherlands

The fire that broke out at 5 am on April 4 in Rotterdam, in a building located next to the communications center of the cellular operator Vodafone, spread to the node building and brought down the equipment located there. What caused two serious consequences - said in a published video (Eng.) Rob Shuter (Rob Shuter), CEO of Vodafone Netherlands.

Firstly, the subscribers registered on that equipment have lost the opportunity to call and receive / send SMS, no matter what part of Holland they are located. And, secondly, the network in Rotterdam, The Hague and suburbs was shut down.

The first problem was solved before noon by redirecting subscriber voice and SMS traffic to the switch in Amsterdam.

The second problem the company planned to solve by the end of the working day. But as stated in the second clarifying video message , having fitted a replacement equipment truck and four dozen technicians to the scene of the accident, the company's employees had difficulty connecting equipment in the building of the site. This is probably due to the work of fire services and the disconnection of electricity to the building. Therefore, the restoration of service for the affected cities is planned on the night of April 4-5.

However, the biggest trouble is the 3G data network. Rob Shooter states that the company found some solution to restore the 3G network for approximately 50% of subscribers. Perhaps he is referring to the cooperation mentioned earlier in the company's materials with two other operators operating in this region: KPN and T-Mobile.

Vodafone is the second largest mobile operator in the Netherlands, after Royal KPN, and the accident affected about a quarter of the approximately 5 million operator subscribers living in the densely populated southern regions of Holland. In a fire, as reported, no one was injured, because at that moment there were no people in the building. The failed node is one of 12 key Vodafone network nodes in the Netherlands. The causes of the fire are specified.

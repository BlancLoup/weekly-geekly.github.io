We invite you to speak at PyCon Russia 2019

Hello!

The seventh PyConRu will be held on June 24-25 at the Cronwell Yakhonty Tarusa hotel, 95 km from Moscow.

PyConRu has a special format: participants and speakers spend two days outdoors, listen to presentations and master classes from Russian and foreign speakers, perform at the Lightning Talks, and in the evening have fun at the aftepati with a bonfire and songs with a guitar.

Until May 5, we accept applications for reports. In this post - some topics that we want to discuss at the conference, and instructions on how to become a speaker.




What is PyCon Russia


For those who do not know, PyCon Russia is a conference for python-developers that has been held since 2013 by IT-People. In 2018, she gathered 500 people from Moscow, St. Petersburg, Yekaterinburg, Kazan, Nizhny Novgorod, Samara, Perm and other cities.

All videos of last year's reports can be viewed on our YouTube channel . Separately laid out presentations and photos .


A small video about how PyConRu-2018 went

I want to speak at PyConRu


We are looking for topics that are interesting to the community and people who have something to say. If you want to speak, write to us. We always welcome speakers with interesting experience. Applications are accepted until May 5. The coolest reports will get into the program.

Possible formats: a report (30, 40, 60 minutes) and a master class. You can talk about how to solve an interesting problem, how a framework, tool or process works, you can arrange an online programming session. The main thing is that it should be your unique experience, your conclusions, mistakes and results.

For inspiration, we have compiled a short list of topics that interest us. The list is not exhaustive, we are open to new and are considering any applications.

1. Looking around for cultural enrichment. The strength is in borrowing cool ideas from parallel projects and technologies:
  • Rust + python
  • julia vs python
  • go vs python
  • elixir vs python

2. Profiling and optimization.

3. Bioengineering. It is in itself a technological trend, and since there are quite a lot of ML, it is likely that there is also a use for Python.

4. Security. About the security of applications and services, about the attack vectors for hacking, about how to secure the service. Here, of course, I want to hear true stories of real projects.

5. PyPy in production - is there life on Mars?

6. Computer Vision.

7. Integration and use with other languages.

8. MicroPython.

9. PyData, Data Science. Yes, we want to strengthen this block of reports.

10. Reports on infrastructure, especially pro-native solutions.

11. ETL. All customize for themselves, but maybe someone managed to summarize and put in pthon.

12. Image processing. It's not about neurons, but about optimization and acceleration.

13. Versioning and reviewing python code for DS. All jupyter laptops are terribly versioned, but only the code without the conclusions is not informative.


The most popular report last year - "Asyncio Today and Tomorrow" by Yuri Selivanov

Program


This year's program is being formed. But we can already name several names: Python core developer since 2001, author and maintainer of many parts of the Raymond Hettinger language (USA); Python core developer and unittest developer Michael Foord (UK); creator of SciPy, NumPy, and Numba, founder and director of Anaconda, Inc. Travis Oliphant (USA); PyPy core developer Antonio Cuni (Italy); Booking.com developer Kirill Borisov (Netherlands); Yandex developer Rishat Ibragimov ; team lead in Mail.Ru Group Search Vadim Pushtaev ; Nikita Grishko , developer at Flo Health Inc; founder of the project Dry Python Artem Malyshev .

Most speakers and reports will appear on the site in mid-May. The full conference program will be ready by June 1st. You can follow the program on the conference website .

I want to participate


Registration is open. Tickets cost: 20,000 rubles for double occupancy and 28,000 rubles for single occupancy.

The ticket price includes: participation in the conference (2 days); hotel accommodation; lunch and dinner on June 24, breakfast and lunch on June 25; all coffee breaks; entertainment; buses from Annino metro station to the conference venue and back on 24 and 25 June.

For students there is a special discount: -50% for a ticket with double occupancy.



We are waiting for your application!

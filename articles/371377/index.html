"Bon appetit, earthling." Restaurants where robots feed you



Tired of hearing the phrase "have you already chosen?". There is a desire to have fun and get into the futuristic atmosphere? Or just do not want to tip? If so, visit a restaurant where robots will serve you. They drive up immediately, do not expect anything from you and will make you feel like a representative of a highly developed human civilization.

Restaurants with robots instead of staff open around the world. In them, customers are served by iron and plastic bartenders and waiters, and mechanical chefs prepare food. In this post - the story of several characteristic institutions.

Wheels on wheels


Robots in most restaurants of this type are waiters. This not only makes the institution interesting, but also allows the owner to save money - the robot does not take time off, it can work overtime and, of course, it does not need to pay salaries. Robo-waiters began to be used in China since 2006, but then they were thoughtless and resembled toys. Now their appearance and functionality is evolving.

Dalu Robot Restaurant
Jinan, China


Dalu opened in 2010. Robo-waiters here resemble droids from the Star Wars films. Everyone has a sensor with which the robot determines the table at which visitors are waiting for an order.

The menu includes dishes of Chinese and European cuisine: traditional soup, meat chop, there is a wide selection of salads. While visitors are eating, near them is a geisha robot - a dummy in a colorful kimono, which makes sounds and moves limbs.

Robo-waiters ride bicycles, meals are transported in the trunk. In total, 12 robots are involved: in addition to the waiters, there are also hostesses and artists who entertain the public with singing and dancing. The price of each robot is $ 6 thousand. The owner plans to increase the number of robots to 30 and modify them so that they know how to climb the stairs.



Hajime Robot Restaurant
Bangkok, Thailand



The name of the institution is translated as "Start" or "For the first time." Some people enjoy when they are served not by ordinary common robots, but the mechanisms from the upper class are real samurai. Two such devices "work" in a restaurant in the tourist district of Bangkok. Robots were made to order in Japan.

Visitors sit at the tables of two types: for a barbecue (with coals in the middle) and for cooking (with a round stove). Each table is equipped with a tablet on which the order is made. A person chooses dishes and presses the "Send to Robot" button. The samurai robot emerges from a state of deep contemplation of the beautiful, comes to life and advances to serve its customers along the tracks.

The restaurant is ready to offer over 100 dishes: fried steaks from imported Japanese beef, salmon, sushi, New Zealand mussels. Clients in their comments are satisfied with both the kitchen and colorful robots.

Robozamurai behaves not as a formidable warrior, but as a hospitable host - he sings songs and winks (instead of his head - a tablet with a picture of a face). Each samurai is engaged in 16 tables.



Cixi
Ningbo, Zhejiang, China



In 2015, a restaurant was opened in Nixbo City Cixi Mall, where customers are served by five robots. Two serve food, the rest dance at the entrance, thereby attracting customers. Each robot costs $ 9.5 thousand, but the owner of the institution, Lou Dyke, says that robots have recaptured the money invested in them.

Automatic machines are able to speak, they pronounce 40 different phrases. Optical sensors help them to function properly and move around the room. One battery charge lasts four hours of work.

"A robot comes into the bar ..."


Robots not only cook delicious food, but also mix drinks at the bar. Perhaps the robot bartender will not help with advice and will not pour into debt, but he will definitely listen to everything he says and will not dilute alcohol with water.

There are a dozen of such restaurants in the world, the first of them was opened in Nuremberg in 2007, it was finalized until 2016, after which the room was used as an office.

Delivery by rail


There are several restaurants in the world where orders are delivered to the visitors' tables by small robotic trains by mini-rail. They come not only with children. Adults also appreciate when their burger or beer delivers a train to the sounds of railway beeps.

Vytopna
Brno - Prague, Czech Republic



Czech "railway restaurant". The name can be translated as "The fire chamber of the engine." Food and drinks are delivered directly to the tables with the help of small trains by rail, the length of which is hundreds of meters. The locomotive economy of the restaurant is constantly growing, the length of the railway bed is increasing. This is taken care of by the owners.

Cuisine - the usual: pizza, pasta, beer, snacks. Visitors say that they come not so much to miss a couple of mugs as to dive into the atmosphere.

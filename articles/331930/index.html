"The Next Rembrandt"


The collaboration of technology and art sometimes gives interesting results, as happened last year. JWT Amsterdam, together with Microsoft, developed the Next Rembrandt project for ING Bank, which was designed to highlight the bank‚Äôs commitment to innovation and support of classical art.



Initially, 346 authentic Rembrandt paintings were analyzed, of which 168263 image fragments (or 150 GB) were obtained. Further, with the help of image recognition algorithms, the most typical strokes and geometric patterns of the master were identified, which made it possible to maximize the identity of the style of the future work with the Rembrandt style. Another interesting point: to give volume to the brush strokes in the picture, it was necessary to analyze the 3D scans of the pictures. The final data processing took more than 500 hours.

Face into the keyboard


Microsoft has a research division called Microsoft Research, which has been in existence for more than 25 years. At the end of last year, they analyzed anonymous data about users' activity on the Internet, in order to understand how the quality of sleep affects the reaction rate of people during the day. After that, results were obtained based on the analysis of data on the speed of entering search queries and clicking on links in a search engine.


Image source giphy.com

Microsoft Research has shown that people who have slept two nights in a row for less than six hours experience lethargy over the next six days. Interesting statistics, in my opinion.

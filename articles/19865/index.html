Duppi - web 2.0 project from Uzbek developers



Uzbek developers have launched a new, extremely interesting, Russian Web 2.0 project Duppi for Russian-speaking Internet users. Despite the fact that Duppi is in the stage of closed beta testing, the developers promise that this project will be very interesting for all users.

What is Duppi and what is it interesting for developers of other services and for users?


For developers:
Duppi is an open platform. This part of the service will help integrate any Web 2.0 site into Duppi. If the owner of an Internet project is interested in logging into Duppi, developers will open the API after becoming familiar with the proposed project. The developer has the right to stir advertising on the service developed by him and to earn money with the help of Duppi .

For users:
In Duppi , user services "Notes" and " Reader" are being tested.

Review of the Notes service :
This service allows the user to send short text messages, display photos, videos from Youtube and Metacafe sites, publish interesting links and of course listen to music. In addition, the service has very convenient features ‚ÄúRecommend‚Äù , ‚ÄúFollow‚Äù and ‚ÄúMeanwhile‚Äù .







Review of the Reader service :
This service, reads RSS feeds , saves copies of materials and shares information with friends. If your friends are interested in these RSS feeds , they can subscribe to them. It is very convenient for collective reading and discussion of information.



Also, users can use other services developed by any Internet project included in Duppi .

A key feature of Duppi is the separation of access to content. The user can send notes, photos, videos and links not just to all visitors or his friends, but also groups formed by interest or professional.

Besides the fact that Duppi can publish different types of data, it also allows you to filter them, for example: separate messages, links, photos, videos, etc. It is also possible to filter by friends.

The main advantage of Duppi is the ability to develop offline applications for Windows and Mac . Over it now developers work.

So far, there are no analogues of such a service in RuNet, which means that the Duppi project has quite good prospects.

Project URL: www.duppi.com

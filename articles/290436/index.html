Apple has patented a social network that will unite users by interests based on their lifestyle.

On May 7, the Patent and Trademark Office approved a patent application from Apple for a social network that unites users by interests.



A social network in the form of an application for iOS users will unite them into groups based on geography, activity, movement and lifestyle. A social group can be created without a user request and be anonymous.

Collecting data about the user's life, the application will offer to create a group and meet people with a similar lifestyle - sports fans, parents or nightclubs fans. Group members will exchange messages, meet and post information in the public domain, Vedomosti writes .

Apple filed a patent for a social network at the end of 2013.

In 2010, Apple created a social network that allows you to follow musicians, concerts, and look for friends with similar interests in music. The project has lived until 2012.

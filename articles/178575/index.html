Anonymous site



This page is anonymous.



Nowadays, to post any material on the web, you will have to identify yourself in some way. This especially applies to social networks like Facebook, Google+, etc. But even posting an anonymous blog in WordPress.com or Tumblr is not a trivial task. There are other possibilities, like Tor. But he has a big drawback - most people do not have access to it.

Among all the services and providers who want to place a page know at least your email, and often the credit card number, there is one that promises to host everyone anonymously. This service is Super Dimension Fortress . The SDF allows you to register a user via ssh (and this can also be done via the Tor network), and accepts cash by mail to confirm the user. I did just that - I created a voidnull user page via ssh via Tor, and sent the SDF by mail $ 1 without a return address. I also appeal to my record via Tor, so it‚Äôs almost very difficult to link me to my account.

We get an account.


First you need to install and configure access to the Tor network .

After that, access to the account can be obtained as follows: 

$ ssh -o ProxyCommand="nc -X 4 -x localhost:9050 %h %p" sdf.org


You can make an alias for a command in .bashrc 

 alias torssh='ssh -o ProxyCommand="nc -X 4 -x localhost:9050 %h %p"'


Information about registering an account in the SDF can be found here and there . First you need to contact new@sdf.org via torssh. Answer a few questions, and after that you will have an account that is very hard to connect with you personally. Only to enable the account you need to send $ 1 by mail to the SDF.

SDF


The service has existed since 1987, started as a BBS, and now has come to a free service that provides the UNIX shell for the hacker community. Detailed history here .

Is it generally legal?


Yeah. Information on this site is public, there is no malicious content. Spreading information on the Internet without revealing your identity is your right, and the SDF just gives you a tool for that. It seems that there are no restrictions on using Tor to access your account, so it‚Äôs quite legal to have an anonymous account.

Tablet Overview

Hello. This topic will focus on the choice of such a thing as a tablet. Below you will find short reviews, specifications and prices for tablets from manufacturers such as Wacom , Genius and Hanvon . The following models will be considered - Wacom Bamboo Fun , Wacom Intuos3 , Genius G-pen m712 and m609 , Hanvon Art Master , Hanvon GraphicPal .

Introduction


I am a novice designer. Not so long ago, I was excited to buy a tablet for myself, and then I began to look closely at tablets from different manufacturers and choose.

To begin, I'll tell you what criteria will be used to evaluate and compare tablets:
  • the size of the active surface is directly the area that is sensitive to the pen;
  • tablet resolution (number of lines per inch) - characterizes the sensitivity of the tablet to the movement of the pen;
  • the number of sensitivity levels when the pen is pressed on the tablet;
  • sensitivity to the angle of inclination of the pen and to the rotation of the pen;
  • the presence of auxiliary keys and other input tools;
  • the presence of a battery in the stylus (re).

And so, let's proceed directly to the review.

Wacom


No one doubts that Wacom is the market leader in tablet makers. Their tablets are considered the best and as a result the most expensive. Tablets from Wacom are represented by such lines: Bamboo , Bamboo Fun , Intuos3 , Cintiq 12WX and Cintiq 21UX . Wacom Bamboo will not be reviewed in this review, as It is hardly suitable for design purposes (positioned as a business model). Cintiq12WX and Cintiq21UX will also not be considered because of their high prices - these are tablet displays (the cost on the official website: Cintiq12WX is $ 999, Cintiq21UX is $ 1999).

It should be noted that the Bamboo Fun and Intuos3 tablets come with a wireless mouse.

Wacom Bamboo Fun



Tablet page on the official site .
Although this model is not positioned by the firm as a professional, it seems to me that it should be given attention. The model is presented in two sizes (meaning the size of the active surface) - Small (5.8 "x 3.7", A6) and Medium (8.5 "x 5.3", A5). As for me, the A6 is not enough, so I would not buy a tablet of this size. Also presented are different colors of the model - white, gray, blue, black. The tablet comes with a stylus, a stand for the stylus, a mouse, CDs with software, 3 interchangeable tips for the stylus.

Specifications:
  • Levels of sensitivity when pressed: 512;
  • Resolution: 2540 lines per inch;
  • Input: 4 programmable express keys, touch circle.

The cost on the official website: Small - $ 99, Medium - $ 199.

Advantages: to be honest, it's hard for me to find any. Bamboo Fun Samll has a relatively small price, but for designers it will probably be too small. The advantages include the fact that, like the other tablets from Wacom, does not have a battery in the stylus.
Disadvantages: a rather low level of sensitivity of the pen, and a weak resolution of the tablet. It is not sensitive to tilt and rotation of the pen. Bamboo Fun Small is small, Bamboo Fun Medium is expensive for a tablet of this class.

Wacom Intuos3



Tablet page on the official site .
This is the best tablet presented in this review (and as a result the most expensive). Intuos3 is a professional tablet for designers, artists, people working with advanced graphic editors, 3D visualization software, etc. They are sold in 6 sizes - 4 "x 6" (A6), 6 "x 8" (A5), 6 "x 11" (A5 wide), 9 "x 12" (A4), 12 "x 12" , 12 "x 19" (A3). Included with the tablet is a five-button wireless battery - free mouse, a stylus, a stylus stand, 2 tips for a stylus with different textures, 3 spare ordinary tips, a CD with software.

Specifications:
  • Levels of sensitivity when pressed: 1024;
  • Resolution: 5080 lines per inch;
  • Input: all tablets have 4 programmable keys and a touch strip on both sides (except for a 4 by 6 inch tablet that has these keys and a strip on one side only).
  • Pen tilt sensitivity: ¬± 60 ¬∞.

The cost of the official website:
  • 4 "x 6" - $ 219;
  • 6 "x 8" - $ 329;
  • 6 "x 11" - $ 369;
  • 9 "x 12" - $ 449;
  • 12 "x 12" - $ 529;
  • 12 "x 19" - $ 749.


Advantages: what can I say, Intuos3 is the best tablets. High sensitivity to pressure, high resolution of the tablet, sensitivity to the angle of inclination of the pen and to its rotation, the pen works without a battery. It was a great surprise for me that the mouse also works without a battery . The back of the stylus also has a sensitive tip (1024 levels of pressure sensitivity), which can be used as an eraser. Included are 5 additional tips for the pen.
Disadvantages: high price. In Russia and Ukraine, the prices for Intuos3 tablets are at least one and a half times higher than the prices presented on the official website. For me personally, buying this tablet at home is out of the question, because of the very high price. You have to look in the direction of eBay, where you can buy Wacom Intuos3 6 "x 8" for about $ 320, Intuos3 4 "x 6" for about $ 240 (the price includes delivery).

Genius G-pen m712 and m609



Tablet pages on the official website: Genius G-pen m712 and Genius G-pen m609 .
Genius is not very famous for its tablets. In any case, in the market of professional tablets, Genius is not a serious player. G-pen m712 and G-pen m609 tablets are the most advanced of all Genius tablets. They have the same characteristics except for the size (and due to the size a different number of hot keys). The G-pen m712 has an active surface size of 12 "x 7" and the G-pen m609 has a size of 9 "x 5.5". Included with the tablet are the stylus, the stylus holder, spare tips for the pen (how many are not specified), the refill tweezer (could not translate) and the battery for the AAA stylus.

Specifications:
  • Levels of sensitivity when pressed: 1024;
  • Resolution: 4000 lines per inch;
  • Input: 5 hard buttons on the top of the tablet, one rotating disk in the top corners of the tablet, 34 macro keys for the m712 (26 for the m609).


Advantages: the main advantage of this tablet is its price. In the Russian online stores G-pen m712 can be purchased for approximately 7300 rubles. (in Ukrainian for about 1300 UAH.). As it was written in one foreign review of this tablet: "For 25% of the cost of Wacom Intous3, you get 90% of its functionality." Of course, one can argue with these figures, but in comparison with the price-quality ratio, the G-pen clearly wins over the Wacom Intuos3. And with Wacom Bamboo Fun, he wins both in price and performance.
Disadvantages: the stylus has a battery (finger). Rumor has it that the stylus is not very comfortable shape, too big and inconvenient. Unlike the Wacom Intous3, the stylus has only one active end. No sensitivity to the angle of the stylus and to its rotation. The resolution is 1000 units lower than Intuos3, but I can‚Äôt judge if you can really feel the difference while working.

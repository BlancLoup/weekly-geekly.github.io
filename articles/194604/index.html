Have you heard of QSAlpha? Here I am not. And they, as it turns out, have been working for 3 years to create a system of total information protection for smartphones, called Quatrix.
And so, just recently, they launched a project on Indiegogo, with a bold approach: to collect $ 3,200,000 in 30 days to produce their own Quasar IV smartphone, or, as they call it themselves, a "chiffone".



What is meant by this? Whatever you do with a smartphone: call, dial an SMS, send an e-mail or just make a note on the calendar - everything will be encrypted using the ECC P-192 algorithm, which allows you to use up to 10 ^ 77 pairs of public and private keys . Private keys are generated on the system side in the Key Management Center. In addition, a completely new digital identification system is proposed, which will allow offline authentication to be performed without being connected to any server using a proprietary encryption chip of its own design.

To integrate all this into a smartphone, they are going to not just release an application for Android, but to create on their basis their own QuaOS operating system, which will include all the services provided, including QuaMail ‚Äî a postal system, QuaCloud ‚Äî a secure cloud-based data storage, and even their own QuaStore application store, with their own QuaCoin currency, which will be similar to similar cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, Litecoin, etc.

Thus, having released a smartphone, they are going to launch their own ultra-secure network.

In addition, the smartphone is positioned for home 3D and augmented reality, it has two 13-megapixel cameras located at a distance close to the distance between the pupils of the human eye. Also, the smartphone has an 8-megapixel camera on the front panel and a variety of sensors: lighting, acceleration, temperature, humidity, barometer, gyroscope and electronic compass.

Zabbix conference 2017: how was the second day

We have already begun to acquaint you with what was on the 1st day of the Zabbix conference , which was held in Riga in September.

On the morning of the 2nd day, the Zabbix support team conducted workshops for the advanced: how to build a Zabbix cluster, how to encrypt monitoring data, how to use data preprocessing and how to use remote commands to make monitoring more proactive. At the same time, a unique opportunity was offered to pass exams for Zabbix Certified Specialist and Zabbix Certified Professional.



Immediately after this, the performances of the participants began:

The paradigm of "concierge". Gareth brown


Gareth Brown talked about how you can simplify orchestra in Docker by launching Zabbix agent as a co-process that can manage auto-registration and scaling. He cited a number of practical examples of using this approach for containers used at the enterprise level both in the clouds and in his data center. Difficult performance, but it looks with great interest, because it is filled not only with details, but also with high-quality English humor.



From Low Level Discovery to SuperDiscovery. Ilya Ableyev


Ilya talked about how Badoo, the largest online dating service, managed to get developers involved in setting up metrics for Zabbix without immersing them in the details of monitoring work. How can this be achieved if you have a large, distributed IT environment, a lot of critical components for monitoring, everything is constantly changing, and the admin team is much smaller than the development team? Ilya tells in detail in his slides how they have come a long way from ‚ÄúLow Level Detection‚Äù to ‚ÄúSuper Detection‚Äù.

Oleg Ivanivsky on how to test the strength of the Zabbix support team




In the energetic speech of Oleg (Oleg Ivanivskyi), an experienced trainer and engineer of the Zabbix support team, there is a long list of ‚Äúrules‚Äù, the observance of which is guaranteed to make it difficult for technical support engineers to find solutions to your problem. His team uses the Ludolph bot to communicate with Zabbix. The bot is written in Python, communicates with Zabbix via API, supports plugins and is available in open source.

Migration to Zabbix 3.2 and Galera cluster in a large bank. Javier Schneider




Only 5 minutes Zabbix was unavailable during the migration of monitoring from Zabbix 2.4 to 3.2 in one of the largest French investment banks, NATIXIS. With more than 20,000 hosts being monitored, 2,000,000 metrics, and 1,200 users in the system. About how everything happened, what methodology was used and what advantages Galera Cluster can provide for high performance and availability - see the presentation by Javier (Xavier Schneider).

Integration with Zabbix to manage cloud data center. Daniel Contcheck


How to monitor a dynamic cloud data center in which thousands of virtual machines and containers are created, destroyed, changed daily? In his speech, Daniel Kont≈°ek from Danube Cloud told and showed how Zabbix API can be used to integrate with the data center management system, having received all the advantages of Zabbix.

Integration Zabbix and ServiceNow. TV2 Denmark


Zabbix was created for monitoring, and ServiceNow - for incident management. Their integration brings many benefits.



As experts each in their field, Morten Moesgaard and Michael Toft Andersen described how they built an integrated system in which Zabbix detects problems and ServiceNow manages incidents. As a result, system users receive high-quality information about incidents, plus working instructions on what to do with this or that problem.

Using Zabbix on the Internet of Things. Fabrizio Fantoni


The versatility of Zabbix allows it to be used for monitoring and data collection as applied to the Internet of Things (IoT).



Sensors and IoT devices usually do not work using SNMP, but using specific protocols like Modbus. The presentation of Fabrizio Fantoni from Systematica was about how a distributed system was built on the basis of an industrial computer and a Zabbix proxy installed on it to collect data from energy consumption sensors. As an unstable network resistant Zabbix architecture, it helps the entire solution to work reliably even in harsh industrial environments.



The story of Zabbix from James Green


In his presentation, James (James Green) from the company NHK International told about how they decided on an open source solution in a large corporation and what prompted them to choose Zabbix from a large number of competitors (there is a detailed comparison).

Forecasting in Zabbix: examples of use and ideas for the future


Sergey Mescheryakov together with Kristina Kucherova (the first woman among the speakers!) Told in detail and in detail about three scenarios for applying trend prediction functionality in Zabbix. In the presentation there are many facts from the history, graphs and tips to developers.

5 minutes of inspiration


At the end of the main session of the conference, consisting of long, honest presentations, a series of brief, expressive speeches, the so-called Lightning Talks, was traditional for the 2nd day:

How to organize Zabbix Meetup? Experience from organizers from around the world




Katerina Yastrebova from the marketing team talked about regular Zabbix meetings, allowing to keep in touch with Zabbix users in different parts of the world. Zabbix-evangelist Steve Destivelle (Steve Destivelle) together with Ilya Ableyev and Luciano Alves (Luciano Alves) from Unirede shared their experiences in organizing these meetings in France, Russia and Brazil.

Do you want to organize Zabbbix-meetup at your place? Zabbix will help you. How and what - see the presentation.

Another example of user development: Zabbix + Telegram. Ilya Ableyev


For the third time in a day, going up to the stage, Ilya told about his own development - the integration of Zabbix and Telegram for sending alerts via an instant messenger. You can insert graphics, html and even emoticons into messages.



And again Zabbix users are at the height: Vladimir Ulgov and ZQL


Zabbix-evangelist Vladimir Ulgov never runs out of ideas! In this presentation, he introduced Zabbix Query language based on LISP, which helps automate things in Zabbix. Try it and you!

Zabbix in the Munich Metro - I love trains! Wolfgang Alper




It turns out for some time all ground and underground trains in Munich are monitored by Zabbix! A fascinating, colorful presentation filled with a warm attitude to trains and Zabbix-y from a long-time partner and Zabbix-trainer Wolfgang Alper.

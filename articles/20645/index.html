Ten classic games of the future

Predicting the future is a thankless task, especially when it comes to computer games and consoles. Recall at least a game console Wii, who could imagine such a huge popularity? All analysts agreed that Wii with simple graphics would be a niche product for younger students, but no: if you believe the statistics, an incredible amount of Wii has been sold in the world - 20.9 million Wii, then it takes the first place among all game consoles. Thanks to innovative Wii technologies, even those who have never played them have joined the video games.

The same with computer games. It is very difficult to predict which of them will ‚Äúshoot‚Äù. Predicting the future, you need to take into account several trends. First, a significant increase in the average age of gamers. That is why entrepreneurs are now actively developing the sector of casual games. Secondly, a variety of interfaces, controllers of various types and feedback devices; reducing the importance of graphics (people, it turns out, is not so important detail), as well as the merger of online monsters MMORPG with each other, with social networks from the Internet, including Facebook and others. It turns out such a universal meta-universe.

To illustrate all these trends, experts from Forbes magazine have compiled an illustrated list of ten classic games of the future. All of them came out recently and can become real hits.

Bioshock
Release date: August 21, 2007
Developer and Publisher: 2K Games
Platforms: Xbox 360, Windows

A first-person shooter takes players into the dark atmosphere of hopelessness, the game scenario is a real breakthrough in the world of computer games, and players will have to solve moral problems as they go.

Brain Age 2
Release date: August 20, 2007
Developer and Publisher: Nintendo
Platform: Nintendo DS

Want to increase your IQ and still enjoy the game? Then the second game of the Brain Age series is designed for you. A collection of funny and challenging puzzles has become even more.

Release date: October 1, 2007
Developer and Publisher: Nintendo
Platform: Nintendo DS

The new game from the popular Nintendo series embodies a number of experiments in the field of game management. In particular, the player can draw fingers on the screen, blow into the microphone and even slam the lid of a portable Nintendo DS device.

Developer and Publisher: Valve

The game uniquely combines the style of fantasy with puzzles and strategy games. When two heroes from the RPG decide to fight, then their battle begins on the board from under the puzzles.

This unique 256-core processor is designed to perform tasks related to voice recognition and a wide range of other tasks related to in-depth machine learning and self-learning.

For the vast majority of potential target audience, KnuEdge, the announcement of a ready-to-work and tried-and-true neuromorphic architecture is indeed a long-awaited news. But for the military industrial complex of the USA, where, as it turned out, the software and architectural complexes of the company have been delivered for five years already, a long-established reality.

For some reason, right now, the KnuEdge Inc. manual. considered that it was time to use processors developed and successfully tested in its laboratories and in field conditions for peaceful purposes, for example, just as they are already used in servers supporting the operation of the website and the company's specialized cloud service.



The KnuPath processor (in the Hermosa specification) is built in accordance with the principles of the LambdaFabric architecture and can be used in equipping data centers where it can best realize its capabilities. The architecture of LambdaFabric allows you to create computing systems that include up to 512 thousand processors. At the same time, the delay time of data transfer from one rack to another is about 400 nanoseconds, which is quite comparable to or faster than the most up-to-date trunk buses used in supercomputers.



Features of the KnuPath processor architecture

On the crystal of each KnuPath processor, there are 256 DSP cores, 64 programmable direct memory access (DMA) modules, an integrated L1 router, which, in combination, makes it possible to provide the computational power of one 256 Gflops processor with a memory bandwidth of 3.702 Gbit / s. The processor has 16 bidirectional I / O ports, providing data exchange at 320 Gbit / s.

The KnuPath (Hermosa) power consumption is 34 watts (100 watts per 1 teraflops, 100 kW per 1 petaflops) computing power. Despite the impressive level of power consumption, KnuEdge claims that, based on 1 W, the KnuPath processor, based on the LambdaFabric architecture, demonstrates an efficiency of 2-6 times higher than the existing TOP alternative solutions.

Being combined into computational clusters, KnuPath processors outperform competitors based on graphics processors by 2.7-8.1 times in performance and efficiency. This conclusion is the manufacturer is ready to back up the key test results - FFT, Sparse Matrix-Vector Multiply and k-menas Clustering.

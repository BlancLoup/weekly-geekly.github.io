16 GB or SSD?


‚Äú I already have 8 GB of RAM in the system for a long time, and I calmly play all modern games! The reader will exclaim. Indeed, you can play with such input parameters. If, with a lack of video memory, the application uses RAM as the paging file, then already with a lack of RAM, a swap file is used that is located on the drive. Without it now can not do.



‚ÄúThe Witcher 3‚Äù, GTA V, Rise of the Tomb Raider, Watch Dogs 2 - not a single game started with the paging file disabled and there is only 8 GB of RAM in the system. And what if you use a solid-state drive as a paging file? As you know, SSD is noticeably faster than classic mechanical hard drives. In addition, the solid-state drive is not only valuable fur speed in games, but also speed in general. Who will deny himself the pleasure of speeding up the loading of the operating system and related programs? Perhaps the speed of the swap file will be enough so that if there is a shortage of RAM, the game does not slow down.

This time we turned off the browser with open tabs. In the next small test, it was decided to additionally check whether it would be better: use an SSD with the paging file placed on it, or add another 8 GB to the system so that it would end up with 16 GB?

The test bench consisted of a Core i7-7700K processor and a GeForce GTX 970 4 GB video card. The memory we chose Kingston HyperX Fury HX421C14FB2K2 / 16 , it quietly starts at an effective frequency of 2400 MHz. The games were installed on a Kingston SSDNow UV400 SUV400S37 / 240G solid-state drive and on a Western Digital WD10EZEX hard drive. The result was three configurations: SSD + GeForce GTX 970 + 8 GB of RAM; HDD + GeForce GTX 970 + 8 GB of RAM; HDD + GeForce GTX 970 + 16 GB of RAM.



Alas, even the use of a sufficiently productive Kingston solid-state drive in the system with a shortage of RAM led to noticeable FPS drawdowns. Especially clearly it looks in Rise of the Tomb Raider and Watch Dogs 2. During gaming, the systems with 8 GB of RAM clearly lacked smoothness. To get rid of the drawdowns associated with the lack of operational and video memory, it is necessary to reduce the quality of graphics. In particular, turn off anti-aliasing.

However, we see that the SSD still helps the system with a shortage of RAM. There are framerate drawdowns, but they are not as critical as when using a bundle with HDD. In the case of a hard disk, Watch Dogs 2 and Rise of the Tomb Raider have very real freezes, it's absolutely impossible to play!



Plus, the SSD is also in the fact that the game installed in the memory cells just loads faster. This moment is relevant for online games, when you need to break into the game as quickly as possible.

Conclusion


Well, we clearly demonstrated that a modern gaming computer needs at least 16 GB of RAM. Especially the presence of such a volume of RAM is important for gaming PCs of an average hand, that is, in systems that use video cards with 3 GB, 4 GB, and even 6 GB of video memory.

At the same time, we demonstrated that the solid-state drive also does not interfere with the gaming system. Yes, in the event of a shortage of RAM from FPS drawdowns, a fast SSD does not always save, but at least it saves the system from micro-insertions. Plus, we don‚Äôt forget that the operating system works faster with the solid-state computer, and the games themselves load much faster. Therefore, SSD and 16 GB of RAM are necessary attributes of a modern gaming PC.

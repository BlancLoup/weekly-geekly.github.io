Cisco will release the open source implementation of H.264, which will be included in Firefox

Today, Cisco announced the release of a free, high-quality open source implementation of H.264 - along with free, pre-compiled modules that will be available for download from the Cisco website. This step means that any open source project will be able to use the H.264 module from Cisco without license fees. Now H.264 is one of the most popular video codecs on the Internet: it is used for streaming video in html5, for video conferencing and on smartphones.

The patent status of video codecs is one of the most painful open source sites. The most popular codec in modern, H.264 , is patented and licensed by MPEG LA in such a way that the conditions do not allow it to be included into the composition of open source products - they wrote more about this earlier on Habr√© . Now, however, ready-made Cisco OpenH.264 codec modules can be included in any applications. The most important thing is that they will be included in Firefox soon (at the beginning of 2014), since Mozilla has already supported the Cisco initiative, promising in the person of its CTO Brendan Ike as far as possible to participate in the development and support of OpenH.264. This solution will allow the use of H.264 in WebRTC along with VP8.

Cisco will release its own H.264 stack under the BSD license and compile binary modules for all possible popular platforms; These modules can be used in any application.

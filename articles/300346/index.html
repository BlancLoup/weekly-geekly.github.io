Laziness as a defense mechanism.


Laziness is a very effective mechanism for refusing to do work that does not benefit. As if the body works constantly in energy saving mode, mobilizing this energy when necessary.
You will never see a wild boar, who ran all day, and then says: I need to sit down to rest, I'm tired. Today there was so much.

And if you feel you need to do it, but do not want to get out of bed for this, then reconsider the purpose of this event. Is it important to you?

Laziness as a sign of genius.


Lazy employee - a good employee, do you agree?
Many, probably, will not check me, but there is a great deal of truth in this.
Richard Koch, in his book 80/20 Manager, tells the story of German Field Marshal Erich von Manstein, a member of the First and Second World Wars. He led the Blitzkrieg, which quickly conquered France, and then commanded the 11th Wehrmacht army, whose successful operations in the Crimea against the Soviet Army were crowned with the capture of Sevastopol in June 1942.
Manstein divided his officers into four categories, depending on their intelligence, stupidity, diligence and laziness.



1. The first group
They are lazy and stupid officers. Leave them alone, they do not bring harm.
2. The second group
These are smart and hardworking officers. They make excellent staff officers from whom even the smallest details will not escape.
3. The third group
Hard-working dumbass. These people are dangerous, they burden everyone with absolutely unnecessary work. They should be shot on the spot.
4. The fourth group
Smart loafers. These people deserve the highest positions.


Thus, laziness in itself is not a virtue, but it is very useful in combination with a high level of intelligence.

C. Urgent and unimportant. Basically, this is some kind of routine and unscheduled work, or someone asked you to do work that is not within your remit. This work in no way leads you to the desired goal. Long stay in this square is harmful. It is extremely important not to confuse doing this square with affairs in square A (important and urgent).

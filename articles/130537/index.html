Kindle updated to 3.3

Amazon's Kindle e-reader has been updated to version 3.3.
In the new version
* Kindle now synchronizes not only books, but also last read pages (except PDF)
* you can quickly disable / enable voice functions by pressing Shift + Space (Space)

To update your reader you need to turn on WiFi, click on Home, click on Menu -> Sync & Check for Items
The reader will begin to load the update, and then install it when the reader goes into sleep mode.
To check whether your Kindle has updated or not, click on Home -> Menu -> Settings. and look at the bottom of the page version of its firmware. You should see version 3.3

and to install the update manually

1. Check your Kindle version

2. Download the right version for your Kindle.
a. Kindle keyboard wifi
b. Kindle Keyboard 3G (Free 3G + Wi-Fi) - US and Canadian Customers *
c. Kindle Keyboard 3G (Free 3G + Wi-Fi) - European Customers *
for the last two, Amazon vows to automatically install a firmware update.

3. Drag the downloaded file to the Kindle root directory, wait for the file to be copied, and disconnect from the computer.

4. Press Home, then press Menu and go to Settings. Then press Menu again and select Update Your Kindle. Your Kindle will reboot twice during the upgrade. After the first reboot, you will see the inscription "Your Kindle is Updating"

5. Once your Kindle reboots a second time, go to Settings and check your version of your firmware.

India has released another tablet



In addition to the tablet, released for educational institutions (its cost was about $ 35), the Indians released another tablet. This time the development was carried out by the efforts of the private company Beetel. The result of the development was a tablet PC Magiq Glide with good performance.



This tablet works on the basis of the Google Android OS 2.2 (why this particular OS was chosen, it seems that it is already outdated, is not explained). Probably, tablet development began even when Google Android 2.2 was a novelty, and now the Indians simply could not change anything.



So, the tablet configuration includes a processor with a frequency of 1 GHz, 8 GB of internal memory, a multi-touch display with a diagonal of 7 inches. There is also a slot for a microSD memory card, as well as two cameras, a rear and a front one (each has a minimum resolution of only 2 MP). The developer has equipped the device and WiFi and 3G wireless modules.



The cost of the device at home in India will be $ 225, which is not enough.

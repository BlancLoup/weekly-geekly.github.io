The results of the final ACM ICPC 2011



The following teams received the medals:
  1. Zhejiang University, China (8 problems)
  2. University of Michigan, USA (8 problems)
  3. Tsinghua University, China (7 problems)
  4. St. Petersburg State University, Russia (7 problems)
  5. Nizhny Novgorod State University, Russia (7 problems)
  6. Saratov State University, Russia (7 problems)
  7. University of Erlangen-Nuremberg, Germany (7 tasks)
  8. Donetsk National University, Ukraine (7 tasks)
  9. Jagiellonian University, Poland (7 problems)
  10. Moscow State University, Russia (7 problems)
  11. Ural State University, Russia (7 problems)
  12. University of Waterloo, Canada (7 problems)
All results can be seen here .

In the last hour after the freezing of the table with glasses, a lot of interesting things happened, for example, the University of Michigan moved from eleventh to second place, solving two problems in the last hour. Serious passions seethed during the announcement of the results, since Michigan had every chance to stay in the first place until the last moment, but at the very end it turned out that Zhejiang University had solved as many problems, but bypassed them in time. Nevertheless, as it seemed to me, such a good performance by Michigan was a surprise for many, they were not among the favorites.

They also made a breakthrough at the last hour of the Ural State University and the Jagiellonian University - having solved two more tasks, they were among the medalists.

Alas, China is leading the second year in a row. However, Russia has 5 medals out of 12, including 1 gold, which is good news. Congratulations to the teams of St. Petersburg State University, UNN, SSU, MSU and, of course, Ural State University!

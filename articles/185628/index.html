Russian projects on Kickstarter in one post

I want to share with you my lists of all domestic (Russian, Ukrainian, Belarusian ...) projects that were launched with varying degrees of success at the COP (Kickstarter). I observed many of these projects live, I communicated with many authors, backed some projects (gave them money).
I will try to provide the projects with useful comments to make it easier for you to navigate. But, unfortunately, I found them not all, and did not correspond with all the authors.



This list cannot be complete, since projects must be launched from a US or UK resident, so it‚Äôs difficult to keep track of domestic projects, unless the authors themselves go to contact through the media. If you know more domestic projects - write in the comments, I will add.

And now comments on the projects:
6. Coast 2 is a very weak project. The authors ask for a large amount, but do not explain why they need it. And the goal itself - traveling across the ocean does not seem super interesting to the general public.
7. Buba - this animated character personally caused pity to me. His liquid hairs, strange movements. But the project is childish, I convinced myself. Despite the fact that the project failed - the author said that he received many good suggestions. Do not forget that crowdfunding is also a way to express yourself!
9. People's Omnibus is a sociological project telling foreigners about Russia. It was well decorated, but the target audience was too small. The traffic attracted good, but the backers saw that the project did not collect money and did not give money. Here it is the Kickstarter paradox =) By the way, the project went to the COP through Sergey Khvaschuk as an intermediary.

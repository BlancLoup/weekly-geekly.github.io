Choosing a vendor in corporate Wi-Fi

When a large office, hospital, airport, hotel, factory or other large building is being built, in the context of network equipment, sooner or later the question is raised - what should a network be built on? Which vendor to choose and why? What equipment solves the problem for the minimum cost? In some cases, this question is asked to the integrator, in others it is a condition.

This choice is always difficult, for the times when the phrase ‚ÄúYou can get a Ford-T of any color, provided that this color is black‚Äù was over, passed. There is a choice. How to make it right?



More often, I do Wi-Fi networks, both at the engineering level and at the managerial level, so to speak. When we communicate with the technical director or head of IT, the question of choosing a vendor during the construction of a network always rises. I need to correctly answer it, this time. My colleagues and I design and build Wi-Fi networks on different vendors, and we need to be well-versed in them, these are two. Now I am reading and outlining a wonderful textbook on a Wi-Fi topic - CWDP and there is something about it, these are three. Combining these three points, I will try to write a short article that can be useful to the community.

I will begin with a more authoritative approach of transferring knowledge from professionals, namely the authors of the previous version of the CWDP textbook of the Sybex publication . A brief outline of part of chapter 5 of ‚ÄúVendor and WLAN Architecture Selection‚Äù with my comments is presented below.

When choosing a vendor, it is important to know each one well and be able to compare them adequately.
There are 3 sources of information:

  • marketing materials
  • datasheet
  • life experience

What to believe? I think you know.

For comparison, there are such criteria:


Reliability

You can learn about it from those who exploit the network. It is indirectly possible to find out via technical support forums, but that ‚Äú2 new points out of 50 burned out in a month for unknown reasons, and they have not yet been replaced,‚Äù only people you know can say.

Performance



This includes throughput, capacity, stability, coverage, delays, jitter, and a number of other measurable parameters. The main question is how and in what conditions these parameters are measured. Special devices like ixchariot are expensive, iperf sometimes gives strange data on Wi-Fi networks. Someone is trying to make objective comparisons that publish on a Habr√©, for example, ‚ÄúWe face the manufacturers of WiFi ‚Äù ‚Äú Cisco vs Aruba: the arguments are exhausted ‚Äù, but most likely, such comparisons are not objective, or apply only to one particular option. Also, if someone declares that 1 access point holds 1500 clients, you should not believe it. Maybe someone is naive and believe, but Wi-Fi engineers on Twitter will laugh. Curious devices for load testing of the SWAT WiCheck type also appear on the market, but for the time being this is rare.

Compliance and certification

As a rule, large vendors meet general requirements. The question is in the details. You need to look at the datasheet and request certificates from the vendor. With more exotic solutions, we are limited to the market. For example, a few months ago, you had to select a client device to connect a bridge crane to Wi-Fi. Analog Cisco in WGB mode. I made inquiries, I learned a lot of new things from foreign colleagues. From the budget I found 2 devices (German Modas MC1 and Chinese Yunlink HWAP80-P48 on the Spanish software Galgus , which will definitely work in the right mode, but so far no one can bring them to the Russian Federation legally.

Scalability

As for controllers, you need to understand that 200 access points in the mode when good user traffic goes through the controller can load the channel to all 100, and even if the manufacturer writes that the controller holds 500 points, in your particular case this is not an argument.

Ease of operation

It includes the quality and availability of documentation, ease of setup and live technical forums in English. This is not all. Some here in Chinese, and this is a problem, Google translator can not cope. Another ease adds community Wi-Fi engineers with whom it is useful to consult.

Ready for the future

Networks are built at least 5 years. After this time, will iron cope with the load? How can you understand this for sure? No Here, if you take a new iron, more often you can not worry.

Quality technical support

This item can also include its price. Ideally, it‚Äôs good to know how many Russian-speaking expert engineers are available in the region. Currently there are three CWNE engineers in Russia, and all of them are working at Cisco. The availability of engineers on the market is related to the same topic, although someone who has been familiar with Cisco for a long time and understands 802.11 will easily master the hardware configuration of other vendors. The question is how deep.

Maturity and stability of the vendor

How many years on the market, market share, success. These factors indirectly speak about quality. Recently, it is more difficult to navigate, as some buy others, which others then buy. For example, the Motorola Wi-Fi line, having unsuccessfully visited Zebra, has now become Extreme.

Price

For some, this factor comes first, but this is not the right approach (unless you are very limited by the budget). It‚Äôs important to know what the GPL is and what kind of discount it can be. Communicate and learn a lot. If the GPL prices are about the same, then having reached the calculations of a real project, vendors less popular on the Russian market can give very good discounts.

Security



In terms of technology support. If you have increased requirements, then the number of vendors for selection decreases. On the other hand, security is always a complex issue, and in the light of recent events, user devices that did not update the firmware after KRACK thundered become more vulnerable . New methods of breaking encryption began to appear more often, for example, fresh Screaming Channels . Do you need a stateful firewall inside the controller, like Aruba, or just enough AVC in Cisco to just cut torrents? Here you need to think.

Technology support

For example, MESH, built-in WIPS, BLE in access points and more. Understand what you really need.

Holistic approach to the network

Monovendor, differently. Only a few manufacturers (4, as I know) can provide the entire range, so large companies choose them. Managing the zoo is harder. If a comprehensive solution is chosen for building a wired, wireless and generally seriously secure network, then the choice is not great. Theoretically, you can attach a piece of Wi-Fi to Aruba on a wired network on Cisco, the question is why?

Brief conclusion


Depending on your situation, some requirements may be more important, while others less. In my opinion, typical solutions do not exist and the choice of vendor is more a matter of experience and personal preferences. Identify important criteria for yourself and try to compare them.

It is important to know one general point regarding marketing materials.


All vendor marketers focus on the benefits, not talking about the disadvantages or disadvantageous features.



This is, of course, pessimistic news, but the best option is to conduct comparative testing on your site on several vendors. If this is a pilot project that will be replicated, then perhaps the hassle and time spent will be justified. In my experience, this has not happened and, at best, a network of vendors was launched at the office site.

If you run a test by hanging a few points in an empty laboratory, to which a dozen devices will be connected, the result of this test will be applicable only to your empty laboratory. If you put the same set in a working office and give people a week to use, it will be closer to reality. How is this even possible? I think it's better to go the other way - communicating with those who serve large networks similar to yours .

Tests conducted by vendors or third parties may not be technically adequate if testers did not specify all the data. Analyzing such a test, check the specified criteria:

  • The equipment, software version, full settings are listed.
  • Detailed description of the testing methodology.
  • The possibility of test repeatability is provided. Available equipment
  • Has the test been audited by a team of engineers you can trust
  • Describes the conditions of the test (the laboratory is different from the working office)

Part one. Story


Toward the close of 2014, there was a feeling that a skating rink had passed through me: one by one, failures in undertakings, a crisis, a systematic lack of sleep, a lack of time for work and family concerns ‚Äî all this almost crushed me. Waiting for the upcoming holidays as living water: you had to exhale and understand what kind of delusion it was.

For completeness, a couple of words about the author
The father of a large family, the owner of offline business, pressed by the crisis and uncertainty of the economy and a dreamer to create applications and ah-ti products.
All this simultaneously sets a lot of tasks and requires a lot of time.

The organization of time and order in affairs is my personal pain and vital necessity.




On the third of January, having closed at the office, I began to put things in order in my head and read articles about the organization of time. Then I came across the blog of the company Desktime , a world leader in accounting for the time of work of remote employees and freelancers. The guys talked about an interesting study. After studying the algorithm of the users, they noticed the pattern:

The most successful and productive employees work intensively for 52 minutes, and then 17 rest.




It was possible to forget, and it was possible to come up with a lot of reasons why this is not for me. But I decided that there is nothing to lose. I'll try. I wrote down on paper the time of the beginning of my first working segment and proceeded to rake the list of tasks for today.

These first 52 minutes were for me the most vivid illustration of my "productivity". I counted EIGHT calls to distract myself: suddenly I was thirsty, then I remembered about the toilet, then I climbed into the mail, news, sms, incoming call ... and so on.

Having completed 52 minutes, I quickly rushed to do all these ‚Äúurgent matters‚Äù and found that time gained weight, and I do everything quickly so as not to miss the start of the next ‚Äúwork session‚Äù.

The most surprising result of this experiment was that I completed all the tasks for which I had planned the day in 2 hours! The remaining time was reading and pondering a lot. It turned out that even such a primitive organization of the workflow freed up two thirds of the day!



Then I decided: this time management formula suits me. The only inconvenience was the need to constantly practice in mathematics: count the minutes before the break or the new working segment. This could help stopwatch and alarm, working simultaneously, but it is inconvenient.

So the idea was born to create a timer application with notification and pre-installed modes of operation. Studying the question, I found a description of the Pomodoro time management methodology. It turned out that this idea was not new, and 30 years ago the Italian Francesco Cirillo invented the technique of working time management. The name "Pomodoro" was in honor of the kitchen timer in the form of a tomato. The main idea of ‚Äã‚ÄãPomodoro: divide work time into segments. 25 minutes of intensive work, then distract for 5 minutes.

The result was such a picture: the technique is known, proven. On the market there are many applications, timers, based on it. I tried a dozen variants of various applications, trying to work on a 52/17 scheme. It was necessary to manually set parameters from one time to another, and in some applications the settings were completely unavailable.

The method of Francesco Cirillo suggests a whole set of solutions for organizing time. The user is invited to maintain a special kind of records, tables, plan your day, estimate the time to complete tasks, monitor the implementation of the plan and other techniques.

At the same time, an awareness of the depth of the changes that have occurred in the 30 years since the invention of Pomodoro has come.

  • The first is time. Changed the rhythm of life, the amount of information and the speed of its perception. I admit that 52 minutes are more relevant today than 25.
  • Second: a good tool should be simple. Turned on and working. Everyone decides for himself how to plan his affairs.
  • The third is flexibility. It is wrong to implant 25 or 52. Some find for themselves the best academic hour, and someone has verified 40 + 10. Flexible and customization: the ability for each user to create and save their favorite script.


The contours of the future application are given. At this stage, the team work begins, I write those tasks to the designer and programmer.



Since mid-January, work began on mock-ups, and in February they have already begun programming. Appetite came with eating. Gradually, we complicated our task: we chose Apple's latest Swift language for programming. Then we decided to add work statistics and graphics. And for dessert, the idea has already come to realize the support of Apple watch and have time to enter the watch on the market.

With the development on Swift, we lost a couple of weeks in the end, and my understanding of the Apple Watch functionality was destroyed by Tim Cook.

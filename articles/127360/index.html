Overview of the global video analysis market

Our review of the international video analysis market is based on the materials of the 4th annual European video analytics conference IMS Video Content Analysis Europe, held in London from June 27 to 28, 2011 (organized by research company IMS Research). A comprehensive understanding of the state, trends and prospects for the development of video analysis technologies made it possible to make more than 20 reports of developers and consumers, as well as informal communication with conference participants.


In our opinion, the most interesting facts and figures drawn from the speeches are supplemented with comments from the IPVideoMarket.Info portal and discussions on the LinkedIn social network .

1. The market for smart devices with video analytics CAGR will grow at a rate of more than 40% annually over the next 2 years - its volume will reach $ 300 million in 2013.
2. The CAGR server video analytics market will increase at a rate of over 20% - its volume will be 65 million in 2013.
3. The video analytics market remains highly fragmented.
4. Integrated video analytics today is installed on 10% of all cameras and coders sold by Bosch Security.
5. The share of embedded video analytics in relation to the server one will gradually increase with the transition from analog to network video. Built-in video analytics is becoming mass, and server - niche.
6. The global standard ONVIF becomes the basis for the integration of integrated video analytics. The market share of network equipment and software supporting the ONVIF standard reached 60% while the share of PSIA stopped at 20%.
7. Patent claims by ObjectVideo and Walker Digital significantly hamper the development and implementation of video analytics technologies.
8. The first products with multi-camera and multi-channel video analytics with geo-referenced and automatic PTZ-tracking appear.

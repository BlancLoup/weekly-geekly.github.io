Warner Music Group revenue from streaming services for the first time surpassed other sources of income



Streaming services continue to gain popularity - this content distribution model remains one of the most convenient for users, due to which the audience of similar services is rapidly growing. This is also confirmed by the financial results of Warner Music Group - for the first time profits from such streaming services like Spotify and Apple Music ranked first in terms of profits for the Warner Music Group.

The level of profits from such services has totally surpassed sales of music on physical media, as well as profits from the purchase of content. However, it is unlikely this news was a surprise for any of the analysts - a year ago, the Warner Music Group announced that the revenue from streaming services surpassed that of standard downloads of musical compositions. Total revenues for the first quarter of 2016 Warner Music Group amounted to $ 745 million, showing an increase of 2.4% for the quarter.

Revenue from the sale of musical compositions on physical media fell by 21% to $ 360 million, while profits from digital copies rose to $ 322 million.

Let's start with the new home cinema sound technologies: Dolby Atmos, DTS, Auro-3D. What is the difference between these technologies? Which of them, in your opinion, the most interesting?

Kerim: It is worth paying attention, first of all, to the most popular technology now Dolby Atmos. This is definitely a leader. DTS released its DTS: X format with some delay. Although we can not say that late - just later than Dolby Atmos.

Honestly, for now, according to the data that goes to the press, and according to what DTS publishes, there are no radical differences from Dolby Atmos. Most likely, they will compete in the field of portable sound - to create a virtual surround sound in headphones or in game consoles.

As for cinema and home cinema, Dolby Atmos is now the leader of the race. Although it all began quite smoothly: there were two technologies Auro-3D and Dolby. Auro-3D is very different from Dolby Atmos, a completely different principle of creating phonograms.

If Dolby Atmos is a virtual sound environment, where the sound engineer himself creates virtual objects, moves them in space, then Auro-3D needs to create a phonogram with a vertical dimension. There and the location of the columns is different.

But Auro-3D did not go to the masses. She lives at the expense of one huge market - the Indian - the largest film market in the world. There Auro-3D caught on. Equipped with many cinema halls, and Bollywood makes films with Auro-3D soundtracks. In our, Western world, Auro-3D mainly produces music programs. And this is understandable, because it is easy to record even the vertical dimension in a live concert.

If we are talking about immersive sound formats, as they are now called, then naturally this is the future. And, if we smoothly move on to the topic of upgrading the system, then you already need to take this into account. Do not buy products that are not ready to play Dolby Amos.

Dolby Atmos is the most popular format, [in it comes out] the most films, in the west [TV-] broadcasts are being prepared. Maybe soon it will all come to us via satellite. So buy immediately modern equipment.

Alina: This is how a black and white movie becomes color?

Kerim: Not exactly. The colorization of black and white cinema is completely artificial. And here, since the medium itself is directed at Dolby Atmos, no separate elements are recorded.

The sound engineer selects some sound and processes it in the program in such a way that it moves in the right direction, so he can take the old mix of the film, if it is saved in a multi-channel recording, take any sound and process it in the same way with a modern processor.

Alina: Interesting. Are there any new video formats in home cinema?

Kerim: Now the video is developing in two directions. On the one hand, this is an increase in resolution in pixels. Now increasingly on products noticeable UHD-logo. This is Ultra High Definition, that is, 4K.

On the other hand, there is also an increase in the dynamic characteristics of the image, the so-called HDR function appears in video devices. We know from the iPhone, other wearable gadgets, video cameras that such a function exists. Now she will be on TVs. If you want to buy a modern TV, see if it is compatible with HDR.

It is still not clear how this will look. So far there are only test films, videos, because in the concern that it promotes, there are different views on how everything should be standardized. When this is agreed, you can upgrade your device at the software level.

Usually all modern TVs that support 4K are HDR compatible. Here [on your part] there should be no additional investment [in the equipment]. If you bought a modern TV, it is suitable for HDR. This is a major breakthrough. Anyone who has seen technology at trade shows, is delighted with the image quality. It is incomparable with what we used to see on the screens - the dynamic range of the picture is very high.

Our eye is a sensitive device. When we see natural phenomena, we can very clearly determine their color and brightness. When we watch TV, everything is averaged there. Now the dynamic range will be expanded, and we can see the brightness of the sun. Of course, it will not blind as much as the present, but nonetheless. We can see different halftones, shades. All this will shine and sparkle.

Dynamic range is one of the parameters that affects the perception of the entire picture. If the resolution affects how closely you can watch TV, then the quality of the picture will not depend on the distance. If you have a TV of medium diagonal and high resolution, then from a distance of 3-4 meters you do not care, be it 4K, or 2K, or Full HD.

Alina: Not so important.

Kerim: It is important to be able to get close to him. This is the advantage of high resolution. You can watch a larger diagonal from a closer distance, and, thereby, dive more into what is happening on the screen, because peripheral vision is involved here.

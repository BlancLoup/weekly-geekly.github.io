On the necessary trends in the development of the CPC

About PDA
DEVELOPMENT TREND
PDA manufacturers are moving along the path of increasing capacity. I think that day is not far when a dual-core PDA appears. I consider it necessary to move away from the traditional views on the progress of handheld devices and to place development accents somewhat differently than now.

I want to talk about changes in the development of computers, which could significantly move us all forward, stopping the linear tactics of increasing the capacity of PDAs and other devices.
WHAT WE HAVE NOW
PDA is now rather a large calculator, organizer, book reader, e-mail, a bit of the Internet. It's no secret that any of us wanted the PDA to fully replace the home computer. But, marching along the current paths of development, it will not happen soon.
In adopting new development guidelines, it is important to use the main guidelines:
  • it is impossible for innovations to require the complete destruction of the old base for the sake of becoming new. Users should be able to use (or at least try) new developments on the equipment that is available at the moment;
  • New technologies should have a real benefit, and not be an interpretation of existing technologies.

Following these two rules, I will state my idea in detail.
NEW DIRECTION
This is how I think I should have a modern PDA.
  • All currently known integrated devices for accessing the Internet and for direct communication with other devices;
  • Clear screen with high resolution (minimum 640x480).

That's all!!! Apart from, of course, the processors that will organize the process and the necessary amounts of RAM.
The whole point is to use the power not of the PDA itself, but of the home (or any other) computer. Further, our ‚Äúnew‚Äù PDA, which I described above, I will reduce to the CPC.

Every day there are more and more new models of PDAs, more and more powerful.

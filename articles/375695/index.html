At that time, the idea was to create a plastic-lined scooter, similar to a motorcycle with a comfortable interior layout. The new bike was seen very cool, but the inconvenience of the very design of the motorcycle is constantly pushed to the thought of alteration. After long googling and deliberation, the reverse trike design was chosen:

  • Stable position without movement (step is no longer needed).
  • Excellent weight distribution and, accordingly, handling.
  • Improved braking by 3 wheels.
  • More room to install any husk.
  • Appearance is also a good plus.

Suspension parts (two transverse triangular levers, steering knuckles and tie rods on each side) are homemade using VAZ spare parts. Shock absorbers are rear of the scooter.

The purpose of the construction of the trike, however, was not to achieve maximum speeds or comfortable movement around the city. But certain requirements have already been formed.

So, round up the experience, summarize and set the task:

  1. The body structure is a reinforced frame made of pipes and a profile around one driver's seat.
  2. Simple front wheel suspension (self-made, such as MacPherson) and easy handling.
  3. Rear - motor-wheel of sufficient power with an automatic transmission.
  4. Sealed and soundproofed interior.
  5. Management and control of the state of the engine and trike in general - from a computer.
  6. Everything must be made to the maximum of the available parts and materials. The manufacture of parts to order is not considered.

These are the basic requirements for the design with further improvements. The base for experiments, research and testing of various systems, it seems to me, is sufficient.

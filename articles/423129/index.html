Myth 3


"I have a bad memory"


Memory is a very subjective concept. For some reason, the poems in school are learning with great suffering, but the lyrics of their favorite songs are remembered by themselves, and no effort is needed for that.

When the brain processes new information, it creates associative connections with already existing knowledge. And the higher the interest in information, the more actively formed communication.

The basis of associative perception is based on the principles of mnemonics - quick memorization.


That interest is directly related to the memory capabilities. If you are listening to a song that you like, associative links are created extremely actively. All because you enjoy it.

And if a student learns something "under the lash", then the effectiveness of this method of study will be only slightly higher than zero. And all because there is no interest, new associative links are not formed and information is erased by the brain as unnecessary in just a few days.

To "trick" the brain, you just need to make study interesting. To do this, use interactive teaching methods, online simulators , communicate with interesting people.

High interest will push the brain to create associative chains, even for complex and complex knowledge. And the information will be memorized for a long time.

So ‚Äúbad memory‚Äù is not an argument. You just do not know how to use it.

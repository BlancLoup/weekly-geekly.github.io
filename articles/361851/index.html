Social network - a tool for the development of man and society


I believe that one of the most important functions of a social network should be to help a person find another person who is close in spirit , views , interests and activities . Is it implemented in existing social networks? Currently, there are special fields in the user profile, where he indicates his interests , by which he can then find other users with the same interests. However, as follows from the attitude of users to this possibility (everything is entered into the field that only pleases), searching through these fields is ineffective. Another more detailed approach, implemented on various dating sites, does not save the situation. Rather, on the contrary, over the past years of the development of dating services on the Internet, society has developed an understanding that such an acquaintance has more disadvantages than advantages: in fact, a person often turns out to be different from what he describes himself in the network.

The next opportunity to search in social networks is realized through the creation of interest groups. But their diversity, even within the framework of one interest and the absence of any systematization of them, makes the search for the group really necessary for the user difficult and discouraging - after a couple of unsuccessful attempts, the user abandons the further search. From the point of view of time and effort, communication in such groups is also doubtful: it is possible to sit in them for months, but not to establish useful contacts and not advance one iota in solving a vital issue.

Note that we were talking only about the interests of the person. And if his views can manifest themselves through his interests and, thus, indirectly taken into account in social networks, then it is difficult to speak about finding a person who is close in spirit . Although this search is the most fundamental in the hierarchy of searches, but it is precisely this that turns out to be unrealized.

What does "congenial" mean? It means supporting the same dreams, ideals, values, lifestyle, etc. People may have different views on life based on their cultures, different interests and activities , due to acquired knowledge, social environment, life circumstances and the surrounding nature, but at the same time they are united in their values , expressed, for example, in the desire to live honestly and fair. There is no such function, which would unite people at the level of their aspirations , dreams and desires , in modern social networks.

It seems that there are two ways to realize the unification of users, which would contribute to their mutual development. Pursuing one goal, they also have fundamentally different approaches. The approach of the first method is based on the integration of users within the existing processes in society, the second - the association of users to create new processes (project activities). For simplicity, the first approach can be called the exchange of experience and knowledge between users and, thus, it performs a conservative role in society. The second approach aims to create a new one and implements an innovative role. Everything is the same as in nature: female and male components, respectively. Consider each of the approaches separately, in order to understand what the social network must have in order to implement them.

The main task of the first method is to help the user or group of users to acquire the knowledge, skills or experience necessary for them that will help them to solve their tasks. It is proposed to carry out its decision through the organization of educational processes for interested users. The social network acts here as a platform on which participants, expressing their interests, find each other, organize joint events (lectures, master classes, familiarization meetings), share the results achieved, etc. In the existing social networks, this task is indirectly solved through the communication of users in groups, but, as will be clear from the foregoing, such a solution for this task is unsatisfactory.

One could argue that there are public institutions designed to help a person in solving various problems. However, firstly, their assistance is always limited both in appearance and in scope. Secondly, due to their historical development, they, as a rule, regulate various kinds of relationships between people, and do not work with a specific person. And even the education system, which works one-on-one with a student, teaches it for the state, and not for the student himself. In other words, an educated citizen is needed first of all by the state and only then by the citizen himself. And, thirdly, it is impossible for state structures to cover all human needs. Otherwise, it would mean the absence of a private life as such and would develop in the person luster and shifting the responsibility to others.

For successful development of a person and society, it is important that a person be able to independently search for and find solutions to emerging problems. This means having an unformatted, creative mindset. In fact, it is this idea that must lie in the foundation of any educational system. But, unfortunately, if we talk about the educational system of Russia, then the trends of its development cause serious concerns for the future of the country. If we compare it with the education system of the Soviet Union, then in the latter it was an integral part of the socio-economic development of society. A variety of clubs, clubs and sections during school allowed the younger generation to better navigate the choice of their future profession. Planned economy allowed to manage the requirements for the quantity and quality of specialists produced by universities and specialized institutes. After graduating from a university or college, the state arranged for a young specialist to work, which ultimately helped a person in some way to find his place in life. In modern Russia, the education system has become a body that provides some educational services, which, by the way, is natural in the absence of a coherent development goal of the state, and, accordingly, a lack of understanding and understanding of which country needs specialists, what quality, but more importantly, what should be the man himself. If in the Soviet Union, the education received was adequate to future work and was determined by its laboriousness, now the presence of higher education is required everywhere, regardless of the complexity of work, i.e. became a bureaucratic formality. Moreover, the education received is often in no way connected with the future work of a graduate. Leaving beyond the scope of further comparison of the two educational systems, we note their common shortcoming: the educational process is considered complete after the end of the educational institution, since then, as expected, there is professional growth.

However, a person constantly faces various tasks or problems for which he may not have the necessary knowledge and skills. Of course, he can contact the relevant organizations. But what to do in the situation of a lack of sufficient resources, but at the same time there is an opportunity and a desire to acquire the skill, to learn so that you can solve the problem yourself? Or, for example, what to do in a situation when there is a change of technology and some personnel have to be reduced? Someone can be retrained, but what to do dismissed? Probably, some of them will be able to find work, where they are still using the old technology, but replacing it is a matter of time. It turns out that retired employees must be trained in another specialty. All this constitutes the problem of the lack of an effective education system that allows a person to acquire additional specialties or skills and helps to adapt to new living conditions. Education Management System . Purpose: to promote the comprehensive development of the user through knowledge and co-creation with other users. Dream management system. Purpose: to help the user in the search, formulation and realization of their dreams, study of various aspects of the dream by creating initiative groups, where it is discussed between like-minded people . In the ideal case, such a plan should be a pre-project documentation that can later be used to create a project by other interested groups of like-minded people.. This approach ensures the preservation of the accumulated material and the exchange between interested users.

Project Management System . Purpose: to help users in project management. This system is a tool that allows like-minded people.: create projects based on a prepared plan (forming an organizational structure, determining material and financial support, assigning responsibilities and responsibilities, adapting documents to existing conditions), manage projects (preparation: planning stages, defining deadlines and criteria for evaluating their implementation, assigning performers and execution: the rapid exchange of information between the participants, monitoring the implementation of the tasks of each participant and the project as a whole), complete projects ( and implementation of the project: successes and failures, tips and suggestions to other groups that are interested in implementing similar projects).

The place and role of the new social network


Understanding the benefits of the introduction of various elements of social networks, now forces large companies to develop their own closed social networks. In other words, more and more companies are building their social worlds in the common information space. Open mass social networks are located in this space separately from companies' social networks, but the interaction between them will only increase in the near future (for example, search for employees, search for work, holding various events).

A new social network is a public system of social development and design, in which not only each user can take part, but also each object of the information space (closed social network, company, government agency, etc.). It is designed to connect all the objects in the information space with each other, setting the relationship between them and offering them universal tools of teamwork, which results in a unified structure of goals, aspirations and missions.

The main goal of the proposed innovations in the social network is to help people in knowing themselves.

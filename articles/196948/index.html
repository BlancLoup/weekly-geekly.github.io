How the emergency notification is arranged



Large-scale flooding in the Far East was the theme of the beginning of the fall number 1. This is the second catastrophic flood in two years, if you recall Krymsk. And as always in such situations, the question of warning the population arises. ‚ÄúMegaFon‚Äù is sometimes reproached that its disturbing SMS do not reach everyone and not always - however, ‚Äúthere is a nuance‚Äù, which is that MegaFon is the only cellular operator dealing with point alerts in cooperation with the Ministry of Emergency Situations. How this cooperation is arranged, on what basis messages are sent, whether an SMS can be received about an emergency situation with a negative balance in the account, what incidents have to be notified most often - I went to find out at the Federal Center for Management and Monitoring MegaFon.

The Federal Control and Monitoring Center was formed in 2010, combining at the federal level the control functions that were previously performed by the regional centers. FTSUM's responsibility is to control the quality of communication, assess network performance, monitor line operations, collect and consolidate MegaFon‚Äôs unified technical statistics, and alert subscribers about accidents and emergencies. The latter direction is the result of cooperation between MegaFon and the Emergencies Ministry.

SMS and other mobile notification options have never been considered by the special services as the main public warning tools. Those are still television, radio and street loudspeakers.

Nevertheless, the Ministry of Emergency Situations has always been interested in expanding its tools, so in the same 2010, when FTSUM appeared, a cooperation agreement was concluded with the Ministry.

CBC and SMS


For notification of the mobile network FCUM and MES use two methods: Cell Broadcast (CBC) and SMS distribution. In both cases, the system automatically selects all subscribers that are on the network within the specified MES radius.

CBC is a passive notification technology: the message ‚ÄúMES Emergency! 0030 ". MegaFon has three such allocated short numbers - 0031 and 0032 more. By calling this number, the subscriber listens to the full text of a message about what is happening, provided by the Emergencies Ministry (description of the situation, necessary actions, etc.). The number of notified is the number of callers and listening to the answering machine with information.

It can broadcast to a vast territory, CBC is simply to provide, it allows not to overload the equipment, does not cause irritation with frequent use in case of small emergencies.

Not all mobile phones support this technology. It works only in 2G networks, simple devices cope well with it, in smartphones the owner must set this feature in the settings on his own. iPhone, for example, does not support CBC at all. Think apple makers.

Requires additional action - dial a short number to listen to the message.

Since it is used quite often (due to the frequency of natural disasters in the area), people get used to it and do not pay enough attention to these reports.

The principle of SMS-mailing does not need to be explained. But this method of notification has its reasons:

There is a beep, SMS is accepted by all phones.

SMS distribution is more informative.

The distribution process takes some time, as the equipment sends SMS in turn. As a rule, it does not take more than 15-20 minutes, but they can be critical in such cases.

Based on this, CBC is used when it is necessary to cover a large number of numbers registered in the network, and SMS is more effective over a relatively small coverage radius. The border lies on the number of one hundred thousand subscribers.

The platform sending an SMS about emergencies is in no way connected with billing, so the subscriber receives a message with any balance, if his SIM card is registered on the network within the alert radius, the phone is switched on and available.




Since 2010, the number of sent messages has been constantly growing - in 2010, about 50 thousand MegaFon subscribers were notified, in 2011 - more than half a million, in 2012 - 15 million, and over 25 million notifications were sent for the full 9 months of 2013.



This is particularly impressive given the fact that the Cell Broadcast-alert is considered to be the calls received by the subscribers. At the same time, the cooperation of MegaFon with the Emergencies Ministry is absolutely non-commercial - the operator considers this project, in fact, as an additional information service for its subscribers.

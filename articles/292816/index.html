How to give an adult Internet forum a new life and what is behind the decay of its secondary nuclei

It seems that most users, moderators and forum owners view their forums as a kind of state. When a problem occurs, users require a change of leadership, moderators, or, in extreme cases, leave the forum. Some old-timers of the forum authoritatively claim some nonsense, prevent the "young" moderators from working in the style of "and who are you?". What does not help. Sooner or later, almost any online forum (hereinafter - the forum) may slip into a garbage state or into a troll community in which no trolls simply have no place. It is not the trolls and non-conflicting people who create interesting materials, help other people, promote constructive discussion of topics and support similar development for the next generation of users. Trolls and conflict people do not alter. Therefore, such a "roll" to a greater extent lies on the conscience of the moderators, since only moderators can turn the tide. If the moderators, in their desire to prevent trolls, act against the violators of forum rules too harshly and are unable to create conditions for discussing interesting topics, then the forum, on the contrary, risks becoming a virtual cemetery. There will be order and silence, but it will still be a graveyard. Therefore, moderators have to find a balance between supporting the freedom of forum participants, creating conditions for the development of the forum, balancing forces and maintaining order (observing forum rules).

As a rule, if the forum rolls into the troll community and degrades, then the forum owners and moderators recognize the death of their resource and close it. There are attempts to give the forum a new life, but not every moderator can decide on this. And not everyone will get the support of the owner of the forum. And, even if he receives support and decides, he will not necessarily choose the best way to restore the work of the forum.

Further in this note, for the sake of simplicity, I will call both trolls and simply conflicting people as Users with Troll Attributes (TAB). Just because it is difficult to distinguish a conflict person from a troll. Yes, and not necessarily worth the time and effort to try to distinguish one from the other, if you just remember that their behavior and the results of their activities are very similar despite the fact that they have different motivation for actions.

As a starting point for this note and for those who have not read the detailed material on forum trolls , it is worthwhile to quote here again the stages of existence of the forum described there successfully:
1. The origin.
2. Development. The formation of the primary nucleus.
3. Further growth, blurring the original idea
4. The weakening of the primary nucleus.
5. Cyclic formation and disintegration of secondary nuclei.
6. Fading and death.

The cyclical formation and disintegration of secondary nuclei supports the life of the forum only as long as these 1. kernels can gather and 2. are constructive in their actions, that is, they create conditions for the creation of the following nuclei. As soon as any of these two conditions is not satisfied, the forum fades out and perishes.

Experienced moderators and administrators can judge the approach of the sixth stage by what and how users begin to write on the forum. If instead of quietly debating a question for the sake of understanding it, users come to topics for displaying their aggression and interfere with calm discussion of topics, or are there those who simply carry spam on the forum (political agitators, spammers, debaters for the sake of argument, trolls, etc.) ), sooner or later, calm and constructive people lose the desire to communicate on such a forum. After all, they value their time and know how it can be spent with more benefit and pleasure. And after all, it is these users, first of all, who bring interesting and useful discussions to the resource. The remaining users sooner or later may become moderators and, most likely, will continue this non-constructive culture of behavior. If the moderators create the conditions for aggression, develop or cover it, then here it is the fifth stage that passes into the sixth stage. There will probably be no more secondary cores, since there will be no one with whom to build something new. Constructive moderators will not be the same, they are simply nowhere to get.

What to do?


This question is asked to themselves and others by both resource owners and active moderators and users who want and can develop a resource. I will call the latter the " dynamic link ", I hope this does not distort the meaning of this term about which evgenyl wrote. Many people in the process of managing the forum still think within the framework of the owner of the kingdom. In these cases, dissatisfied with the leadership or performers are looking for a new leadership for their kingdom. And they do not think that it is possible to simply make a new kingdom without harming the already existing kingdom (this is the third solution). Moreover, if even one kingdom survives, then it will be a success. If both survive, then it will be a success in the square and everyone will benefit from it.

And now, about all these methods of solution in order.

Method 1: assign new moderators or more moderators


The goal is for these new moderators to maintain a high quality of communication on the resource and adherence to the rules.

This method works at the stages of the existence of the forum from 1 to 3. Stage 3, where the initial idea is already blurring, implies that the original mode is already in a state of blurring or, God forbid, they have already been saturated with corruption. That is, this method works, but only as a temporary solution on the way to the subsequent stages of the forum‚Äôs life.

Method 2: assign new moderators with extensive experience who may be close in spirit to the primary core and who are willing to spend time and energy on the restoration of the resource (not necessarily the old methods)


The goal is to correct the situation on the forum for the better.

This is the most extreme and conflicting method. This method seems attractive, since such a moderator has nothing to criticize, but there is a drawback that is not immediately noticeable. This is a nervous attitude of a part of people to not like them, unfamiliar or principled moderators.

Imagine you are an ordinary member of the forum where you have already taken root in the past few years. And then a new moderator appears for you, who says, ‚Äúbut now we‚Äôll get everything back to square one." This is equivalent to the fact that users sat down on the branches of the forum and the moderator is ready to saw off some branches, others - to unfold, the tree itself - to start shaking (problem point 1). At a minimum, users will get nervous. Those of them who are paranoid ‚Äúwhispering‚Äù in their ear all sorts of bad things will build such fantastic theories about a new moderator and portal that you will feel strange (2). Method 3: create an alternative to the existing forum on the same platform


The goal is to create a new forum without harm or influence on an existing age forum. It makes sense to do when the sixth stage has not come and there are enough people of the dynamic core to launch a new forum.

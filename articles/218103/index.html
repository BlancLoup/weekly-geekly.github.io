Hubble eXtreme Deep Field: A small journey of 13 billion light years



Hubble eXtreme Deep Field's deep space images are some of the most impressive. Actually, with the help of this system, a person was able to look very deep into space. Of course, all these images are millions and billions years behind the current situation. But for understanding the structure of the universe, this is good.

So the other day a small video was posted on the Net that shows us the depths of outer space (with a maximum "depth" of 13 billion light years). So far this is the maximum that humanity is capable of; we are not able to glance further.

On the video posted by the project team, you can count 5500 galaxies. To create a model, scientists used an estimate of the distance between about 3 thousand galaxies. Everything ends with a dark screen - not because there is no further, but because we are not able to look deeper into space yet.

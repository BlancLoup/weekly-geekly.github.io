the Internet


One of the most important freelancer tools. In large cities on the coast, it is almost everywhere, and quite tolerable. In the mountains, with this worse, they mainly put satellite there. Now about the normal Internet. In hotels it is free, usually up to 5-7 Mb / s. If you rent an apartment, ask first if it is there and how much it costs. Because it will be expensive to spend. In general, such indicators will cost in rental housing at 15-20 euros.

Banks, ATMs


An important role in remote work is played by the possibility of a normal withdrawal of money, to pay with a card and so on. After the default and the situation with deposits, Cypriot banks are no longer so loved, but for a while they will be fine. There are also places from the CIS (Privat, Alpha), so you can choose. Hellenic Bank generally allows you to withdraw money from ATMs without any commission on your part.

If there are problems with the card, you can open the local one. Laws allow you to do this during your stay in the country. In the shops, the fruteries are free to pay with our cards. Conversion is fast, no ID is required. The main thing to remember before leaving is to inform the bank that you are going to use their card abroad, otherwise blocking is possible at the most inappropriate moment.

Products


Another of my motivations is that after the summer the quality of fruits gradually decreases. In Cyprus, this is not observed. There are always fruits and vegetables either in supermarkets such as Carrefour, or in special fruits. Prices are usually higher than ours, but if you take in bulk, it turns out cheaper. For example, 4 kilograms of ripe mandarins cost 1 euro. Perfectly suited for the morning fresh. Of course, this is not Thailand, but the choice is large, everything is delicious and fragrant. Oranges, pomegranates, kerob can be tasted from a tree; they grow everywhere, although they are quite dirty.



In supermarkets, I personally lack normal milk. It is often long storage, a small selection of kefir and cottage cheese. Transport


Public transport is scarce, and it is expensive. 1.5 euros worth the city, long-distance - 5 euros. True, it is possible to take a daily / weekly / monthly ticket, depending on your needs.

I know that many people love bicycles, so I will make you happy - there are good bike paths along the island along the embankments and skate parks in Limassol. Two-wheeled pedal transport can be rented: either in offices or directly from parking lots, paying with a credit card. As soon as you put a large back, return the withdrawn pledge.

The new "solar" charger is able to recharge the battery of a smartphone in 4 hours



An interesting device was launched on the market, we are talking about the Changers Solar Starter Kit charger, working on solar energy. The device consists of two parts. The first is the solar panel itself, which has its own name, Maroshi. The second part is the battery where the collected energy is stored, and this part is called Kalhuohfummi.

As stated in the title, the device is able to replenish the battery of a modern smartphone in about four hours, which is even less than the charge time of some devices from a regular network. In addition, the battery is very capacious. According to the developers, its capacity is enough to accumulate energy, which allows you to charge two iPhone batteries (they are used to measuring everything in iPhones).

However, it is not yet clear how much the battery of the device itself is charging. I have a couple of mobile solar panels, in the sense of "solar" charge. They charge fully in about 8-10 hours, while the battery capacity is much less than the declared capacity of Kalhuohfummi. Well, and who will wait twenty hours to fully charge the battery "solar" charging? On the other hand, if there is a lot of sun, and not very actively working with your device, then this may be quite enough.

By the way, the Changers Solar Starter Kit has another positive side. Being connected to a computer via USB (yes, there is such a functionality here), the device can be configured to send tweets. And then it will tell you how much you are worried about the environment, and how much "green" energy feeds the battery of your mobile device. The cost of the device is 149 US dollars.

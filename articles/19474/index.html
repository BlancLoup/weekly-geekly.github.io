Last week, a daily review of foreign blogs gave me quite interesting information. On the official blog of JetNumbers , an announcement appeared that virtual phone numbers for Skype became available ( News on the Skype Club ). At the same time, the list of countries with which JetNumbers works is more than 2 times larger than the list of SkypeIN destinations , and most importantly, Russia is among them.

At the end of the announcement, I found an offer for bloggers to test this service for free, which I immediately took advantage of by leaving a comment on the post. At the same time, I wrote to the JetNumbers support service a letter to find out the city codes available for ordering. As a result, it turned out that code 499 is available for Moscow, valued at $ 36.7 per month. In the same correspondence, I recalled the offer of free testing, and after a while I received a letter with information about the account created for me, assigning him a phone number and crediting a small amount to the account.

I immediately dialed this number 7 499 502 ... on the phone and after a couple of beeps with my Skype I received an incoming call from jetnumbers01. True, I didn‚Äôt have much to talk about with myself, so I asked a colleague from the next room to dial the same number. The conversation turned out to be quite comfortable, without interference and with good audibility. With the door open, there was a slight sound delay. In general, the result was great, I received an incoming Skype call from the Moscow city network, which is what many Skype users are dreaming about.

In conclusion, I went to my page in JetNumbers with the assigned login and password. The user interface was extremely simple.

My Account menu allows you to change your personal data: name, surname, address, e-mail, password and credit card information.
The second menu, My Numbers, shows the assigned number and Skype account for redirection. Here you can change your Skype account, see the history of calls and payments, subscribe to the Voice Mail service, and of course add funds to your account.

In general, the offer JetNumbers can be considered quite viable. Despite the high cost, compared with the offer from Skype (which is absent for Russian cities), this service may be quite in demand among many business users of Skype, who need a main or additional incoming city number.

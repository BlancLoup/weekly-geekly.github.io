Conference DUMP-2015: Section "Testing"

March 20 in Yekaterinburg will be the fifth conference developers DUMP . Traditionally, we publish section reviews from our program directors.

Our first post is from the program director of the Testing section, Ilya Vakhrushev, a tester from Exadel, that there will be something interesting in the section and why go to it.



A year has passed, on the nose of DUMP-2015 , and we with Wolonter do not lose heart and are preparing the next section for testers at the upcoming conference.

The first attempt was pretty good, though not without comments. But we are only happy for them - this means that people are involved in the dialogue, they want to communicate and share experiences. And now we know in which direction to go.

But to be honest, the audience only hinted to us, there were more reports on which topic than we wanted. Yes, this is a favorite automation of the bosses :)

We faced a difficult task, so we decided to take a risky step and collected reports on everything that testers do, except for testing. Of course, there was not enough time for everything, but we chose topics that could interest the majority. And not only testers.



Elvira Soboleva (SKB Kontur) will tell you how the tester keeps abreast of the team and helps the development manager solve problems at the operational level. And, in general, to seize a piece of power - to become an iteration manager.

Anton Vdovichenko (SKB Kontur) will show how to give a second life to the documentation and make users happier. Different approaches to solving the problem, useful sources of information, examples - everything will be.

Maxim Zakharov (Yandex) will share his feelings about the sudden abandonment of the profession. Many people know why testers go to other positions, and we will have the answer to the question "how and why it is worth staying." And do not deny yourself a caviar sandwich.

Igor Panchenko (Radkrot) will tickle your nerves with pentester bikes. After this report, you are unlikely to test the application for XSS and SQL injections and go to sleep. Are you afraid of becoming a victim of a schoolboy, losing a customer base and years of promotion for 300r or because of the carelessness of a programmer who was dismissed 3 years ago? From these problems, the tool will tell.

Alexey Lupan will bring us back to earth and will give advice on working with the requirements - not very good ones. Clearly and to the point.

And in the intervals between the reports we will surprise you with news from the "mainland". These are our plans.

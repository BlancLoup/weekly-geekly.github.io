Memory structuring





The problem of memory and memory structuring is very relevant among IT specialists, which confirms many articles on Habrahabr devoted to this topic. As an example: "Our memory is everything . " This article describes in some detail the form of structuring memory in the form of entries in notebooks.



1.Blocks


The title contains a link to the article, which describes in great detail how to use notebooks for memorization. Therefore, I will not describe this process in detail. I will dwell only on the pros and cons of notebooks.

Pros:
  • Traditional input;
  • Always at hand;
  • Visibility;
  • The speed of use.

Minuses:
  • The difficulty of searching for a large volume of records;
  • Likely damage.


All the advantages are clear enough, so I will explain only the disadvantages.

I use about 3-4 notebooks per month. Notebooks are different, there are usually parallel to 2, end at different times, and therefore the degree of synchronization is very low. Actually, there are no problems at the moments of reference, but when it is necessary to find something over the past month, then the problems begin ...

On the second point. Several times dropped a notebook in a puddle when I tried to write on the street. There is even a question of relationship. A notebook is just a stack of paper, which is why safety is often neglected.

2. Portable devices.

In Active Notes, there is the ability to create folders and the ability to sort records by date and title, which greatly simplifies the search. However, the question remains the lack of search. Since the accumulation of a large amount of records search for them remains a problem.

The question of entering drawings into notes was also acute, and in general the input speed is often not enough. In order to make a mark you have to enter it entirely, such an action is tantamount to a simple stroke in a notebook. 4. Symbiosis



By trial and error, I found that I could not refuse traditional notebooks. Hope I'm not the only one so conservative. Then I found a way to combine the usual input method, convenient search and cataloging.

While surfing the Internet, I came across the description of Microsoft Office: OneNote (via the link lessons). I have seen OneNote several times before. I just came in from the interest of what is in Microsoft Office and what I do not use.

Inspired by the review, OneNote decided to start using it. Next, I will talk about my new recording system and OneNote experience.

How OneNote + Notepad Works


I still use a few notebooks for writing on a daily basis, I also make sketches and short notes. The difference from the first option is that at the end of the day I bring everything to OneNote, and at home I work directly at OneNote. This is how the Mind Map of this article in the OneNote link looks like.

Using OneNote, I was able to catalog my records, provide them with a high enough level of synchronization and make it easier to search through the old records. Yes, for the convenience we have to pay. Here the board goes by the time it takes to transfer information from a notebook to OneNote. In the bottom line of this symbiosis, I received:

Pros:
  • High level of synchronization of records;
  • Always at hand;
  • Cataloging capabilities;
  • Convenience search.

Minuses:
  • Time to transfer records;
  • I did not find an automatic way to synchronize the book, without copying files;

There are a number of stereotypes about optimization. I will try to dispel some of them.

SEO will die soon
Optimization will not die while search engines generate traffic. So far, the search engines are the main generators of traffic on the Internet and there are no prerequisites that something will change much in the coming years. Google, Yahoo, Baidu, Live, Yandex - among the most visited sites in the world. A certain competition to them is made only by large social networks. As long as it remains, sites will be optimized and promoted. Web resources compete for visitors. Visitors - this is an increase in sales, the cost of advertising on the site, etc. That is, the struggle is actually for money. In some subjects it is a lot of money.
Search engines will change the algorithms, optimizers will adapt to them. This is an inevitable process, so maybe it's worth taking it for granted?

SEO is evil
Those who think so argue that SEO helpers litter the Internet with satellites, link to high-quality resources and send their promoted resources to all requests.

Littering search results - who said that only poor-quality resources are being promoted? Secondly, when the competition is great, the user must not only be attracted, but also kept on the site. When the resource is ugly and inconvenient, the user will run away to another, located on the next line in the search.

Each of us has come across a situation where it is difficult to find a thematic site on demand, since the first places are bulletin boards, forums and catalogs. Doesn't seo do a good deed by pulling thematic resource to the first place by the relevant request?

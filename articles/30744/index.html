United Russia plans to open its faculty at Moscow State University

The United Russia party plans to open a political science department at Moscow State University (MGU), in which Boris Gryzlov, chairman of the high council of the party in power, will occupy one of the leading positions, the Kommersant newspaper wrote on Monday. The question of creating such a faculty will be considered at a meeting of the academic council of Moscow State University, scheduled for August 29.
As the newspaper clarifies, the faculty is not created from scratch, but will be formed as a result of the division of the existing faculty of philosophy into the faculty of philosophy and the School of Political Science, which will be engaged in training personnel for the Russian political system.

The rector of Moscow State University, Viktor Sadovnichy, confirmed this information, saying that such schools have long existed in many prestigious educational institutions in the world. He also noted that at the moment the country is in dire need of qualified management personnel, and such a faculty will be able to provide them.

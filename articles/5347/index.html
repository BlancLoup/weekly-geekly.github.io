HD DVD will not give up Russia Blu-Ray

Toshiba introduced the first serial HD DVD player in Russia. Despite the fact that in the world the popularity of the new format is not as significant as the developers would like it to be, on the domestic market HD DVD has every chance to win the majority of the market. At least in comparison with the competitor - the format of Blu-Ray.


The high-density HD DVD recording disc format was developed by Toshiba at the same time as the rival Blu-ray format, which was created by a consortium of companies led by Sony. The main difference between HD DVD and Blu-ray is that technological and consumer properties of the content have no effect, and therefore it does not matter to watch high-definition film on discs and devices of one format or another for the viewer.

However, there are a number of aspects that have a very serious impact when buying a new format player and disks to it. For example, the price of an HD DVD device is currently $ 500 lower than Samsung's Blu-ray player, and the cost of the discs is half that.

There are differences in the content of the formats. The fact is that the leading Hollywood film companies and Blu-ray disc producers have already made statements that the Russian audience should not expect the first BD films before the fall of 2007. Several Russian film companies also said they supported the format, and at the presentation a second Russian-language film in high-precision format, Graf Montenegro, was presented to journalists.

Not the last factor in the popularity of HD DVD format for Russia can also be the fact that Toshiba supported the desire of some Chinese manufacturers of budget consumer electronics to start production of HD DVD players.

Returning to the technical advantages of the format, it should be noted that, unlike Blu-ray HD, DVDs are more mechanically protected and less afraid of dust and scratches. They can also be not only multi-layered, but also multi-format, when, for example, on a disc, in addition to two layers of HD DVD, there may still be two layers of a standard DVD. This will allow users to smoothly move to the new standard.

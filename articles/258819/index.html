"Toad chair" and other concepts


Mushrooms are farmed and wild, the latter are divided into poisonous and edible. Fungus (reads "fangus", plural fungi) is a clever name, mushroom is a popular name, and toadstool is a warning: sometimes it is something poisonous and sometimes edible, but mimicking deadly twins.

Toadstool (“toad chair” / “toad chair”) is an echo of medieval superstition: an edible mushroom becomes poisonous if a poisonous toad sits on it, and since it is impossible to determine by eye, it is better not to take any. About local toads, everything is true: their skin really releases toxins. And about mushrooms - no: it’s just that some are not combined with alcohol, others cannot be eaten raw, and still others - with the taste of regular mushrooms or radish - are killed only on the fourth day.

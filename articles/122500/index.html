"Apple" Lingvo learned to translate words in photos


We have released the second version of ABBYY Lingvo Dictionaries for Apple iOS . The new version was created taking into account the requests and wishes of our users, and first of all we want to thank all the fermenters who wrote their comments on the first version of the product (we told about it here ).

What is new? The most important thing is that now the program is able to translate words in photos taken with an iPhone camera. This function is implemented on the basis of our OCR technology (optical text recognition) and previously it could only be used by owners of Symbian devices (the product was released under the name ABBYY FotoTranslate, we wrote about it here ).



Suppose you have a text with unfamiliar words (a menu in a restaurant, a billboard, a signboard, a newspaper, a book ‚Äî it doesn't matter).



You photograph the text, the program recognizes it.



After that, you can click on any word and see its short translation in the pop-up window. The translation direction corresponds to the one you chose when last using Lingvo Dictionaries. If this translation is enough, you can safely click on other words in the photo, watch their translations - and so on, and if not enough, you can click the translation itself and the dictionary card with a detailed translation will open.



If you click on the pencil image or on the word itself, a window will open in which you can edit the word if it is recognized incorrectly. When you click on the image speaker word will be played.

The translation works not only on photos that have just been made, but also on images stored on your mobile device.

A little about the devices on which it works. The phototransfer feature is fully supported on iPhone 3GS and 4. Since the iPhone 3G and iPod touch cameras do not allow taking pictures of sufficient quality for recognition, only download from the photo album is available on these devices.

Another new feature is the translation of words from the clipboard. It's still easier here: copy a few words you want to translate (no more than 5 words or 200 characters), launch Lingvo - and voila:





Hypertext support in dictionary entries has been added due to numerous requests (you can get a translation by clicking on any word / phrase in the translation card).



What else? Previously, the program was only Russian and English interface, now added German, French, Spanish and Italian. There is one more important change compared to the first version: now you can buy dictionaries in sets (there are Russian-English, Russian-German, Russian-French, Russian-Spanish, Russian-Italian packages of different pairs, as well as a multilingual package). In total, dictionaries for 30 languages ‚Äã‚Äãare available in the application, the photo translation function supports 27.

Users of earlier versions can upgrade to ABBYY Lingvo Dictionaries for Apple iOS 2.0 for free.

Anticipating questions, we tell: the version for Android is planned in the end of July - August.

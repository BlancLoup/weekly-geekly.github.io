It is known that every major Internet company has its own policy to attract independent developers. This is a mutually beneficial cooperation: it is also beneficial for developers to create additional services for an already ready-made audience.

For example, around the social network MySpace has already formed an infrastructure of support services: from the design of personal pages to special targeting tools to help the advertiser. Some services are paid, while others earn through contextual advertising.

A similar infrastructure of "satellites" is formed around other popular web services. For example, the Internet has published a full list of services for Flickr , official and unofficial. It's hard to believe, but this list contains almost two hundred items! Of these, only eight official services, and the rest - unofficial. Listed below are the best ones.

Official services
A set of programs for Windows and Mac OS that allow you to send photos directly from the desktop for publication on the site.

The plugin for export from iPhoto , suitable for iPhoto programs 4 and 5.

Organizer for mass photo management .

Free mail for users.

A selection of interesting tags for the entire existence of the photo service.

Unofficial services (most interesting)
Pictobrowser : a widget for displaying photos from Flickr on your blog.

Flickr and WebimagerNew : a program that helps to take screenshots and automatically publishes them on Flickr.

Fireflix : a plugin for uploading photos and managing your account from the Firefox browser.

FlickrFaves : utility to download full-screen versions of your favorite photos.

Viewr : the program shows the top 100 photos rated "interesting."

phpFlickr and phlickr : PHP utilities that send requests to the Flickr API, and the answers convert to normal PHP functions.

John's Background Switcher periodically changes the background image on the desktop, automatically downloading photos from Flickr.

Skulpt Aim will help monitor the state of the muscles and the progress of training: a review + anboxing device



Being engaged in the gym, or independently, on the street, you always want to know what exactly each training session brought. Have the muscles increased, has the fat gone? All these questions torment many, but it is clear that the progress of a single workout is minimal, and the methods that allow you to determine the physical condition of the muscles, after each session, are at least.

In any case, it was so until recently - now the Skulpt Aim device has appeared, which in a few seconds evaluates the state of the muscles, and also the presence of subcutaneous fat. And this is not an abstracted assessment - measurements are made for each of the 24 muscle groups.



How it works?





Skulpt Aim, due to the presence of special sensors, can measure muscle mass anywhere on the body. At the same time, the speed of the device and its functionality are impressive:
  • Measurement is carried out in about a second, with instant results;
  • When measuring biceps, triceps, abdominal muscles and thigh muscles, the user gets a realistic estimate of the percentage of fat in the muscles and in the whole body;
  • The analysis is carried out on 24 muscle groups;
  • The results are displayed both on the device display and are sent wirelessly to a mobile device.




Measurement of the state of the muscles produced by the method created by specialists of the company. MQ is used as a unit of measure, an indicator that determines the quality of muscles. MQ is calculated by determining the passage of electrical impulses through muscle tissue, using multi-frequency signals. The signals are generated by the device, and are fed from four different electrode configurations on the back of the device. There are 12 electrodes in total.
Representatives of the company argue that stronger muscles give a higher result, because the muscle fibers in such a muscle are larger, plus there is less fat.



What else can Skulpt Aim?



The device software can be used as a personal trainer, suggesting which muscle groups should be trained first. Software development was carried out in conjunction with fitness trainers, so the device can recommend a number of exercises that will give rapid progress during training.



To determine the state of the muscles, a method called electrical impedance myography (EIM) is used. The method was developed by a joint team of specialists from Harvard, the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and some other organizations.

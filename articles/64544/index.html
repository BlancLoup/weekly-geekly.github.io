Let's go back in time. Computer DAEWOO CPC-300e

Hello dear habrazhiteli. Today at home from the attic got a very interesting thing, namely the computer daewoo cpc-300e. And I decided to share with you, maybe someone is interested =)


To start the characteristics:

Manufacturer: Daewoo Electronics Co. Ltd.
Year of release 1989
64 KB RAM
128 KB video memory

A little description:
The computer is designed as a monoblock keyboard. All computer electronics is in the keyboard case. The known variants of the color scheme of the case are black with gray keys, or white with gray and blue keys. There is a built-in transformer power supply. The computer does not have a drive. One cartridge slot on the top of the case. (Wikipedia)

Cartridge Slot


Keyboard


This computer has 2 modifications: CPC-300 and CPC-300E
CPC-300E is a simplified version of the CPC-300
This computer is managed under MSX BASIC version 2.0 Copyright 1985 by Microsoft
I have a Korean version. The computer keyboard contains the characters Hangul and a special key to enable the input mode of such characters.
On the upper side there are exits for connecting to a TV or monitor. NTSC television standard


* High Frequency (RF)
* Low Frequency (AV, monaural audio output)
* RGB
* Black-and-white - color image switch
* Channel switch for high frequency output

Also on this side there is an access to the printer.

and expansion port "EXPANSION BUS"

On the right side of the connector for connecting a tape recorder.

On the left toggle switch.

Modification Daewoo CPC-300E - one of the few computers of the standard MSX, which has some distribution in Russia. Currently, in some cities of the country you can find lots of 10-15 packaged and unused computers (offered by owners from different cities at a number of online auctions). Usually they are or were previously owned by educational institutions. These computers were purchased for use as part of computer classes, instead of the Yamaha YIS503 and Yamaha YIS805 previously supplied for these purposes (classes based on Daewoo computers existed, for example, in Primorsky Territory schools). However, unlike Yamaha computers with Russian letters on the keyboard, Daewoo computers found in Russia have a standard keyboard with English and Korean characters. The kit of these computers also includes a user manual in English. (Wikipedia entry)

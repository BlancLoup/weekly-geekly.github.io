Search 2.0, as it may be

Take as a starting point two rather famous quotes:
  1. ‚ÄúSearch engines have become one of the two new wonders of the world, giving Homo Sapiens unlimited and instant access to information.‚Äù Ilya Segalovich, ‚ÄúHow search engines work‚Äù
  2. ‚ÄúThe Internet is like a big dump: everything is there, but it‚Äôs impossible to find.‚Äù Popular wisdom

Let us highlight the three main problems encountered in modern search engines.

The main problems of finding information


  1. Instead of searching for information search for links to it .
    Are you not tired of snippets yet? How much time is spent on a hike on the links, and the subsequent search for information on the pages. Yes, while we are spinning banners, visitor counters, etc., pleasing the owners, but no one suffers from the publication of the RSS feed? In 90 cases out of 100 we are looking for information, and we get a link where it may be located. Instead of instant access, the required information moves away from us beyond a certain barrier.
  2. Instead of searching for the width of the search in depth .
    At the request of the Internet, Yandex offers 602 million pages and this figure continues to grow. And if your interlocutor answers the question ‚ÄúWhat is the Internet‚Äù, ‚ÄúI heard this word 602 million times, which time are you interested in?‚Äù, You are unlikely to be satisfied. Most likely, our mental interlocutor will begin to talk about the protocols, if he is a technical specialist, or about social networks, otherwise. In any case, he will have one answer to the question. Even if this answer is completely accurate and absolutely useless, as in the well-known joke.
  3. Mixed search results .
    Ask the request "gull". You will receive in one heap information about watches, cars, birds, etc. Unified list, mixed with each other. Yes, there are timid attempts by some systems to derive a number of accompanying words or a cluster tree, but this will give rise to a new query with its local chaos. Why can not the issue be carefully cut into sections (and not accompanying words), and give the result for these sections? Birds are separate, machines are separate, otherwise the instantaneous access to information sinks under its heterogeneity.

The developers here have a special mantra for all occasions, which can be applied to the above questions - ‚ÄúThe user himself does not know what he wants,‚Äù so we have to do what we do. Let me, when you ask your colleague or a passer-by, does he understand what you want from him?

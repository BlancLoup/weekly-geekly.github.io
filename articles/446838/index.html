Cars Koenigsegg: Swedish design and bold technical solutions


The Swedish company Koenigsegg Automotive AB is the market leader in the niche of elite hypercars . Since 1994, she has been engaged in the development and manual assembly of ultramodern and high-class cars.

The path traveled by Koenigsegg since its inception is truly incredible. It all started on August 12, 1994, when a 22-year-old young man set out to realize his childhood dream. Passion, ingenuity and inflexible will pushed him forward. On this day, Christian von Koenigsegg decided to open his own car company. All the circumstances were against him, but at that moment it was necessary to decide: now or never. The idea was to create a sports car, which at that time did not exist and which (he was convinced of this) would be in demand. Both of these factors, according to Christian, became the key to success.



The concept was to create a light car with a mid-engine layout and durable removable hardtop. It should also have a windshield that provides all-round visibility and good aerodynamics, short overhangs and two large air intakes of the cooling system on the sides, which will be located behind the center of mass to increase stability at high speeds. The car will look spectacular and will be comfortable with a raised roof, and in the form of a roadster. The transformation will take a few minutes, while the hard top gently fits inside the car. As planned by Christian, the car should have a timeless appearance, which, like good wine, would only get better over the years. Analogs of the concept of the car with a mid-engine layout at that time was not yet. So the model was born CC8S.

Measurement tasks and equipment


Before contacting Creaform specialists, Koenigsegg solved measurement problems with the help of leased equipment. A four-meter Romer Arm manipulator with scanning and probe heads or a Leica Tracker tracker was used in combination with PolyWorks software. Measuring and scanning systems were mainly used for:

  • analysis of the root causes of problems on the production line;
  • comparison of physical parts with nominal CAD-models;
  • equipment, commissioning and reverse engineering settings to replace manual parts based on CAD data obtained using Catia V5R19 software for tooling and manufacturing.

Frequent malfunctions of rented equipment, significant costs and time constraints, as well as the intention to carry out 3D measurements on its own - all this led Koenigsegg to start searching for alternative solutions.

HandySCAN 3D hand-held laser 3D scanners provide an unprecedented level of accuracy and resolution along with a very high measurement speed - all in one portable device. The HandyPROBE portable CMM measuring system, which does not require the use of a manipulator, is an alternative to traditional measuring manipulators. HandyPROBE is a wireless CMM based on the principle of triangulation, which gives complete freedom of movement and can be used for both single and multiple measurements of various parts . Tracking for this CMM is provided by the C-Track optical tracker. C-Track tracker sensors also provide accurate HandyPROBE CIM positioning, continuous image acquisition and transmission, as well as data exchange with a computer and saving sensor parameters.

Both systems benefit from the integration of TRUaccuracy technology, which is characterized by high measurement accuracy when operating under actual operating conditions - regardless of vibrations, instability or temperature drops in a production environment.

How 3D-measurement technology from Creaform helps a car company


The HandySCAN 3D 3D scanner and HandyPROBE portable CMM are now used in a Swedish company mainly to monitor the quality of cars , measure components and components and compare them with the CAD model, as well as identify root causes of problems, reverse engineering and tool calibration. For example, when calibrating a tool at Koenigsegg, they use the HandyPROBE system to precisely adjust the nominal coordinates of the reference points. Then, after fixing the parts in a snap, the operator scans them with a HandySCAN 3D scanner to compare their sizes with the CAD-model data.

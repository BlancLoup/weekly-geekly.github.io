The virus will help to increase the capacity of Li-ion batteries

The tobacco mosaic virus (TMV) has a bad reputation: it affects hundreds of different plants, including tomatoes, peppers, and, of course, tobacco. But his image can improve if the results of American researchers are confirmed under the guidance of Professor Reza Ghodssi .

According to scientists, rod-shaped viruses can vertically dock to the plane of the electrodes in Li-ion batteries, increasing the area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe conductor and increasing the capacity of a standard battery by a factor of ten.

The modified virus is specially sharpened to stick around the gold-containing plates, which serve as templates for the subsequent manufacture of electrodes.

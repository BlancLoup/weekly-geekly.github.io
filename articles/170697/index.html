ATM for anonymous exchange of dollars for bitcoins

Zach Harvey and Matt Whitlock - Bitcoin entrepreneurs and enthusiasts - created a terminal prototype for exchanging cash dollars for bitcoins. They plan to sell such ATMs to shops, cafes and restaurants that accept Bitcoin. Along with the gradual spread of cryptocurrency, as a recognized means of payment on the Internet (they are accepted by Wordpress and Mega , you can order pizza for bitcoins, and they even began to pay salaries in the Internet Archive), some offline companies, especially like San Francisco, they started accepting bitcoins too. For example, they can pay in a local candy store .



The terminal scans the QR code of the client‚Äôs wallet from the smartphone screen and accepts bills, after which a QR code appears on its display with the transaction ID and information about the transferred amount at the current rate. ATM takes a commission of 1%. You do not need to have any bank accounts, no payment cards - only a wallet with a wallet application (or a QR code printout). The terminal connects to the Internet via WiFi to monitor the course and send transactions to the Bitcoin network. Thus, it is one of the easiest, quickest and cheapest ways to exchange dollars for bitcoins. Besides, completely anonymous. The cost of the terminal will be from one to one and a half thousand dollars.

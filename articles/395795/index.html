On the morning of July 7, the Soyuz spacecraft of the new MS version successfully launched to the ISS. Various systems were improved in it, and for the first time telemetry and voice communication with astronauts will be available at any turn. Space communication even in a near-earth orbit is not as simple as it may seem, and the history of astronautics keeps many stories, as because of problems with communication for some time astronauts were ‚Äúlost‚Äù.

A bit of physics


Why are there any problems with communication in space? Just because the earth is round. And on the round Earth, the radio waves propagate in different ways. Ultrashort waves (VHF, UHF) propagate only in the direct line of sight, and when your interlocutor disappears over the horizon, the connection disappears. Short waves (HF, HF, VHF), in addition to direct visibility, can be reflected from the ionosphere, and you can talk with a very distant interlocutor. There are stories about how, in the region of the south pole, they listened to the KV radio of Moscow, which came down through multiple reflections from a powerful layer in the ionosphere.



In the first spacecraft, receivers and transmitters of the HF and VHF bands were used. Despite reflection from the ionosphere, communication with the spacecraft on short waves is possible. Therefore, in the field of visibility of ground stations, VHF was usually used, and outside of them they hoped for HF communications, which could or could not be.

Deaf orbits


The USSR had less opportunity to establish communication stations in the region of the equator and the southern hemisphere, therefore for a long time in our space program there existed such a concept as ‚Äúdeaf turns‚Äù. On them, the spacecraft passed far from the communication stations on the territory of the USSR, stable communication was impossible, and at best it was possible to receive HF telemetry. Therefore, on these turns there were usually no important operations, and the astronauts rested or slept.



Over time, the USSR built several ships for space communications, which slightly improved the situation, but did not completely solve the problem. In the USA, the network of ground stations was used until the end of the 1980s, when it was replaced by the network of TDRSS space repeaters. The satellites in geostationary orbit perfectly saw objects in low orbit and relayed data to Earth, which was also in direct visibility.

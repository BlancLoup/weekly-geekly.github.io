Any search engine in Russia will become a companion, or oblivion threatens him



The State Duma submitted a bill on the ‚Äúright to oblivion‚Äù - it establishes the obligation for search engines to remove references to personal information at the request of a citizen. The authors of the document are the deputies Alexey Kazakov, Vadim Dengin, Olga Kazakova and Leonid Kalashnikov. Amendments are made to the law on information, the Civil Code and the Procedural Code and the Code of Administrative Offenses.

The draft law gives the right to Russian citizens to seek removal, in particular, from search engines on the Internet, of links to information about them, which these citizens considered unreliable, irrelevant or distributed in violation of the law.

Bulk oblivion is not threatened

Strength is not in money, but in their quantity


Search engines are obliged to stop issuing links within three calendar days from the receipt of a valid application, deputies write, but only the links, not the information from the sites themselves.

If the search engine does not delete the link, the penalty is 300,000 rubles. The search engine may refuse the applicant, but must justify the refusal, according to the document, the citizen may appeal against the refusal in court. If the search engine refuses to execute the decision of the court, the fine is 3 million rubles.

Borders - not a hindrance


The draft law gives citizens the right to file claims not only at the location of the defendant, but also at the location of the federal government authority that oversees compliance with this law. Also, the Russian courts are vested with the right to consider cases involving foreign persons. This means that it will be possible to seek removal of links from the Google search system in the Russian courts.

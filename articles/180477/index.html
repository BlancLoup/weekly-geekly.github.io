Microsoft introduced the new Xbox One



At the end of the next generation Xbox conference in Redmond, Microsoft introduced a new console called the Xbox One.






From the technical specifications, I would like to mention the 8-core AMD processor, 8GB of DDR3 RAM, USB 3.0 ports and a 500GB hard drive (in 2013 that year), and a BlueRay / DVD combo drive with slot-loading.



Along with the new console, the new Kinect 2.0 sensor also comes up. It will differ from its predecessor by a sensor with a resolution of 1080p (compared to VGA in the first version), and an increased volume of data processed. The sensor will read up to 2GB of data per second and read up to 6 figures, so it's time to look for a large living room.

Voice control is supported, but not a word about the support of languages ‚Äã‚Äãother than English. With the help of voice, you can switch between TV, games and applications.

Curious support for gestures like pinch to zoom only by hand. I think this is pampering, not more.



The gamepad has been updated. He did not get fashionable now with touch panels, but now he is being tracked by Kinect.

The new console will simultaneously run 3 operating systems, including a cut-down version of Windows for running applications. Multitasking is supported, now you can watch a movie and chat on Skype at the same time.

Also, the console will be able to pass through the content via HDMI and impose, for example, Skype on the signal from your set-top box. A sufficient part of the presentation was devoted to television functions, but it is still difficult to predict how this will affect Russian users.

During the first year, fifteen new games will appear exclusively for this platform, and eight of them are completely new series.

Currently Forza 5, Call of Duty: Ghosts, Quantum Break and the line of games from EA Sports are officially represented.

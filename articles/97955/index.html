Auctions in the domain of the Russian Federation - to be!

The Coordination Center decided to hold a period of premium registrations in the domain of the Russian Federation.

The report by Marina Nikerova on REEF + CIB 2010 mentioned various auction options. The rules have yet to be determined, the Council of the Coordination Center gave time until August 1 , but most likely it will be a Dutch auction as planned earlier in the concept of opening a free registration period in the RF domain .

A Dutch auction is an auction on the contrary; in it, over time, the price does not increase, but decreases. If the price seems reasonable, the bidder makes a purchase.

According to the concept of the auction was planned to start with 10 million rubles. The figure is impressive, and is set more likely from PR goals than from real demand for RF domains. Most likely, in the process of preparing documents, this upper bar will be adjusted downwards.

In addition to the economic component, there is another one - the fact is that the Coordination Center planned to send 80 percent of the revenue from the sale of RF domains to charity. Judging by the fact that the news mentions "those responsible for preparing the list of projects and potential recipients of funds received during this period," these plans were decided to be implemented.
Buying a domain of the Russian Federation even for 1-3 million rubles is an irrational decision, but if the money goes to charity, then the chance for the buyer to make such an irrational decision increases.

Holding auctions at the opening of the domain zone is not a Russian invention, but a common practice. For example, in 2008 and 2009, auctions were held in the new top-level domains of ASIA and ME.
True, in the desire to use the Dutch auction, we are ahead of the rest. This is partly due to the fact that there is no single auction site for domains in Russia (in the west, the registries used the services of sedo.com and pool.com), in part with the requirements of the Russian legislation on legally significant accounting of time rates.

By the way, do not think that the auction is pulling funds from companies or government agencies. The state authorities have already received the domains of the Russian Federation free of charge and in any reasonable quantities (for example, moskva.rf is registered with the Moscow mayor‚Äôs office), and companies could get domains by their trademarks for ~ 1200 rubles. A list of working sites in the domain of the Russian Federation, you can look at Google for " site: rf ".

Ahead of us, from July 15 - domain registration for holders of certificates of registration of the media . And this is a kind of "hole in the system", allowing you to get any domain for 1200 rubles, without waiting for auctions. The fact is that media registration costs up to 10 thousand rubles - and this is clearly more profitable than participation in the auction.

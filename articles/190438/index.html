MakerBot 3D scanner goes on sale next week



MakerBot is well known in the 3D printing market. Its printer Replicator became popular very quickly, due to not very high prices and relatively easy operation. In addition, the company recently announced its intention to launch a 3D scanner with which it would be possible to get a model of a small real object, and send the same model to a 3D printer to create a copy (not as high-quality as the original maybe, but nonetheless). And now this scanner is ready, and will be presented next week.

The scanner was named Digitizer, it is depicted in the preview photo. As mentioned above, Digitizer allows you to scan a three-dimensional object, get a computer model (CAD), and, if necessary, send the file to "print". So far, the model of high resolution and speech is not. But what comes out of it deserves the praise of the developers. It is worth noting that the scanner software allows you to correct image errors using digital "magic".

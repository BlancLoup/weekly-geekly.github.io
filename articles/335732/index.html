IV Ensuring the life cycle of the social organism


I haven't lived in plans for a long time. They do not always come true, because people participate in them, which breaks any guarantees. Nothing ever goes according to plan. Better improvisation.
Elchin Safarli. ("You were promised to me")

A social organism, like any other organism, requires resources for its maintenance and development. Therefore, it is not enough just to create such a community, it is necessary to look after it with zealous constancy. First, for his well-being and the first uncertain steps, then for the fact that it itself did not offend anyone and you were not ashamed of him, after that no one would take him away from you, beckoning with ephemeral feelings. Ideally, I would just like to contemplate how it itself takes care of you, supporting and not giving offense. Therefore, the more the architect laid in the Social Organism, self-regulation mechanisms, which were discussed in the previous chapter, the more time he can devote himself, and not to nurse with his brainchild.

1. Selection of participants necessary for the Social Organization


The main asset of the Social Organism is undoubtedly people. After all, they are to a greater extent determine the fate of the community, ensuring the implementation of all functions of its life. So, in the Social Organization there should be a sufficient number of participants capable of supporting these activities. And here it is important to take into account two points: first, it is necessary not only to support the functions, but to do it qualitatively, and secondly, it is very important that the duties and responsibilities of the participants should not be theirs, because we declared the goal: to provide the inhabitants of the community comfortable stay in it. And therefore, it is advisable to take care that the performer of each function in the Social Organization in its parameters as best as possible is suitable for the respective type of activity.

What are the criteria for this selection?

Various classifications of roles in the community can be found in S. Kratochvil, R. Schindler, M. Belbin. Some rely on the leadership qualities and attitudes of the participants, others are repelled by group processes. I, on the basis of the functions that were formulated in the previous chapter, singled out these most important roles (and determined their useful qualities):

  1. Zhivchik (Initiator) - has leadership qualities, organizes joint activities of community members in finding and working out solutions to problems;
  2. Idea Generator (Designer) - has the creativity expressed in finding solutions to problems in various situations;
  3. Own person (Communicator) - possesses communicative abilities, expressed in building horizontal communication links between community members.
  4. Auditor (Controller) - has administrative skills, expressed in monitoring the correctness of the functions and compliance with the norms of community behavior on the part of the participants.
  5. Authority (Moral Benchmark) - has authority that influences the choice of criteria for standards of conduct that should be followed by community members;
  6. Quartermaster (Providing conditions) - has entrepreneurial abilities aimed at creating comfortable conditions for the successful functioning of the community;
  7. The driver (Stimulator) - implements methods of encouragement and punishment in relation to the community members, as well as in creating a favorable psychological atmosphere in the team;
  8. The horn box (Information Agent) has communicative skills expressed in collecting and comparing different opinions of other members of the community on emerging issues. Forms an opinion about the ‚Äúaverage temperature in the hospital‚Äù;
  9. Performer - carries out activities in the context of generally accepted community decisions, meanings and values, adhering to the behavior adopted in the team;

Most often, participants perform several roles in the Social Organization at the same time.

2. Increasing the value of a social organism


As shown in the example in the previous section, during the life of the Social organism, it may be necessary to introduce new functions that contribute to its development. These needs should be comprehensively analyzed, their importance should be evaluated and, if possible, a plan of measures for their implementation will be developed.

Each implemented function must be really useful and bear clear benefits from its use. I, for example, recently discovered that my head shampoo, the use of which, judging by the label, should protect hair color. I washed my head, looked in the mirror, really part of the hair remained gray, some remained dark, as they were before using shampoos - the promised function was completed, the hair color was preserved and there seemed to be no complaints, but I subconsciously feel some kind of trick.

Each successful introduction of a new function should increase the efficiency of community activity. Thus, purposeful improvement and development of the Social organism enhances its value within the Organization in which it functions, and as a result, the value of the Organization itself as a whole. The trick of the Social organism is precisely that the ratio of its price (value) and value (utility) is incommensurable, in favor of value, of course.

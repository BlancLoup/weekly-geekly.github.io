The report on the results of "My Circle" for March 2016

In March, a new record for My Circle: 800 vacancies were posted this month! At the same time, on average, about 8 specialists still respond to each vacancy. Thanks to all employers who trust our service, we will continue to try to meet your expectations, make every effort so that each vacancy finds the most suitable IT specialists!

As always, let's see how many responses to a vacancy were in the most popular areas of activity. Let's look at the overall trend in the average response over the past six months. And we show the most popular in the number of responses vacancies in March.



  • In March, 799 vacancies were posted on My Circle. Each, on average, received 7.56 responses.
  • As always, the easiest way to find a designer. There were almost 22 responses per design position.
  • As in the past month, it was also not very difficult to find a maker-up and a manager. Each vacancy in these fields of activity accounted for an average of 11.5 responses.
  • As in the past month, it was more difficult to find a programmer, administrator, tester, and application developer. For each vacancy in these areas of activity, there were on average 6.4 responses.


Next, we look at the overall trend in the average number of responses in the most popular areas of activity. Finally, we have accumulated statistics for the past six months and can make the first global generalizations.



  • As you can see, the easiest way is to look for designers on My Circle. On average, 17-18 people respond to design jobs.
  • Regular leaders in the number of responses - vacancy layout and managers. On average, 9-10 people respond to such vacancies.
  • In third place are the vacancies of administrators. On average, 8 people respond to every vacancy in this field.
  • In fourth place are the vacancies of programmers and testers. On average, 6-7 people respond to such vacancies.
  • And the most difficult job openings are application developer jobs. Here, on average, 5-6 specialists respond to the vacancy.

Yandex will search for pirates with the help of a robot

Yandex will soon introduce a new way to remove more links to pirated content from search results, as reported by Vedomosti. The company has created and has already launched a specialized robot program that searches the web for content pages similar to those already in the ‚Äúpirated registry‚Äù. To improve efficiency, the program uses the principles of machine learning.

Last year, the largest Russian media companies called for a more active fight against piracy. Thus, media holdings, online cinemas and producers signed a memorandum on combating piracy with Internet companies. One of the provisions of the memorandum indicates the need for an extra-judicial procedure to remove from the search results links to pages with pirated copies of films and TV shows.

According to the producers and TV channels, it is through search engines that users search and find pirated sites with illegal content. Now the copyright holders send the found links to pirated content to Yandex, these links are entered into a single registry.

By mid-March, Yandex removed more than 100,000 links from a search, as told by representatives of a domestic Internet company. The registry already has more than 140,000 links, which is confirmed by the staff of two media companies that have access to this list.

In a short time, Yandex found several million pages with pirated content, and this was achieved thanks to a new program for the robot. Found an order of magnitude more pages with illegal content than contained in the registry.

If the right holders agree that the system is working correctly, then Yandex will regularly transmit the pages found by the robot already from April. Copyright holders will be able to make them to the registry for later deletion.

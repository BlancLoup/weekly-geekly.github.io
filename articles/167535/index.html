ZTE Blade: how we have gone the way to sane Android 4.1.2

Amazing devices sometimes produce different companies. In the near 2010, the company ZTE released a new smartphone called Blade. The device belonged (and, by the way, belongs) to the budget segment, and, therefore, does not shine with characteristics. You can find the characteristics on the same Yandex.Market .
The smartphone was produced in two forms - European and Chinese. Chinese equipped with 256 MB. RAM, and European - 512 MB.
For 2010, it was a great budget smartphone, equally good in price and quality (except for the 1250 mAh battery), it worked out of the box at 2.1.

Time flies, android is updated, updated and the device. 2.2, 2.3, 2.3.5 - were not long in coming. The device received the official CyanogenMod version 7. But here comes a new round of the evolution of Android - version 4.0, and our device is out of business. Qualcomm, which produces coding / decoding codecs for different processor architectures, announced that it will not release new codecs for devices with ARMv6 CPUs, but because the fourth android used exactly new versions, and it couldn‚Äôt go on updating it. Sadness ...

But suddenly a topic appears on the MoDaCo website, in which the author puts up links to the assembled CM9 for Blade. This is where this thorny path begins.

I will not touch on very early builds, for I bought myself this phone in March 2012. From here I will start. On June 15, on the same Modaco, the topic appears for the authorship of the user KonstaT (even then he was known on the forum for his own builds of the ZTE stock firmware), in which he spreads his CM9 builds. A long and stubborn correction of broken opportunities begins (some of our compatriots also had their hands on it). Gradually, the build begins to grow into features, edits, and increasingly begins to look like a normal CM9. Those Qualcomm codecs were replaced by third-party ones: there was no hardware video encoding / decoding, but the system worked.

In parallel with this, on July 17, KonstaT creates a new theme in which he begins to post CM10 builds. And the problems are the same - there is no hardware c / d, the interface is stalled. And the funny thing is that if you could close your eyes on European devices, the Chinese just lay down - the lags and brakes were creepy. Perhaps we can say that this is where a new round in the development of builds for ZTE Blade begins. On the same day, KonstaT updates CM10, and after 7 days a new build CM9 appears (unfortunately, the last).

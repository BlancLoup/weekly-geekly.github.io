Travel posters from NASA for travelers on exoplanets



NASA is continuing its exoplanet detection and exploration program. At the moment, more than one hundred of such objects have been discovered. Perhaps in the distant future, people will be able to reach some open exoplanets, but NASA is already trying to imagine conditions that can prevail there.

As part of attracting "tourists" to other planets (well, or just for fun), scientists from NASA have prepared several "tourist posters", which describe the beauty of some of the planets.



Among the selected objects is Kepler-186f, the first planet of the terrestrial type, found in the "life zone" of its star. There may well be not green, but red grass. If plants exist here, then the color palette of life is different from what we see on Earth.



Another planet, HD 40307g, may have a much stronger gravitational field than the Earth (gravity is 8 times stronger).



And Kepler-16b is a desert planet with two luminaries above the horizon. To get for your shadow companion, it is worth flying to this planet.

Subdomains


As a perfect noob, I looked at the Plesk Panel and did not know where to poke. Frankly, I have not experienced such feelings for a long time. First of all, of course, flooded the standard project, making sure that everything works. I created databases, made sure that the new data types of SQL Server 2008 are supported. In general, I poked back and forth to get acquainted.

It took several days to build the first site, launch it and start developing it. And here there was another problem with which I patted the nerves of support. I could not configure url rewriting for subdomains. For those unfamiliar with IIS, I will explain that in this server this process takes place through setting the rules in the configuration file for the url rewriting module. From the very first attempts, it became clear that my virtual subdomains simply did not respond, which meant the DNS problem. Yes, it was necessary to configure the DNS. Do you still remember that I am an amateur in hosting? So, the Plesk Panel, with all its beauty, hid the DNS settings in a place that was completely non-obvious to me. And not finding these settings, I again climbed into the support to complain about the "problem".

As I understand now, my entire thread with support was made idiotic. If these were not ‚Äúbreaks,‚Äù then they were dangerously close. I will not tell all the entire thread, I can only say that the support proved to be just fine. First, they answered very quickly. Secondly, they never showed emotion, it was robots who, despite my language and stupid questions, continued to delve into the situation and direct my actions. In the end, we figured out what was happening and they poked my nose at the DNS settings, which, of course, were present in the admin panel.

But on the DNS setup, my epic subdomains are not over. After configuring the DNS, the ping began to take place, but it was still impossible to configure the url rewriting. When switching to the left subdomain, instead of getting to my domain, I got to the host server by default. Now I can explain why it is different and what needs to be done. But then I did not know this and decided that I need to contact the support again. Here one more interesting side of my support showed up: they do not delve into the difficulties that do not concern their service. Can't do something? Go learn materiel. On the one hand, I would like to have a smart consultant, but on the other hand, they are absolutely right and should not be wasted on the user's personal problems. I will not tell you how much time I spent on google problems and how many forums and faks I re-read. Lot. I'll tell you right away a solution that will be useful for someone, and someone will seem like a quote from the primer. The fact is that on virtual hosting in IIS, unlike Apache, you cannot configure such parameters as mod_rewrite allows. My problem on the Apache is solved in two lines, but on the IIS solution simply does not exist. Except one.

The solution is to buy a dedicated IP address. Did you know that? But here I am, and it took me a week to find out. What does a dedicated IP give? It allows you to set a default site through the plesk panel, to which all unprocessed requests will fall, which can already be analyzed through the IIS url rewrite module. Such are the cases.

Which is also very inexpensive. After gaining IP, all my ideas on subdomains turned out to be realized and with this the story with subdomains can be considered complete.

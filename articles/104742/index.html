18 class projects over the weekend: September 17-19, HackDay # 7

In Moscow, there was a shortage of programmers, but there were more business-oriented projects, unlike Peter. We gathered 250 people from Moscow, St. Petersburg, Rostov, Minsk and Voronezh. ( http://hackday.ru )



The goal of HackDay # 7 was to develop IT projects from idea to prototype over the weekend.



On Friday evening, participants presented 42 ideas, and on Saturday and Sunday, 18 of them completed. A variety of prototypes were presented at the demofest: several online games, a service that speeds up the work of an insurance agent at times, a program for drawing cartoons for Android, and others.

According to the voting results of the participating teams, the first place was won by a nameless multiplayer 3D game for VKontakte, which is a rough track along which two grenades move. In two days, this team managed to write a location, set a texture, make interactive dynamic objects. During the presentation on the demofesta game dedicated to Dasha Zaitseva.

Second place : MindCube CMS. Site management system for people, working on the principle of "turn on / off the unit, without thinking about the technical part."

Third place : Combild. Program for system administrators, which is a building plan with located on it devices with full characteristics for each machine.

At HackDay # 7, we divided the workshops into two streams: theoretical and practical. Theoretical were common to all participants, practical - the so-called. "Circles of interest." In a theoretical stream: Mikhail Kechinov from the Petersburg studio of the same name told how to make his project in 48 hours, Alexey Meandrov from InvestStartup told about the correct positioning when entering the market. Practical workshops were presented in the areas of augmented reality (RuCap company), virtual reality (Ar Door company, Inga Nachmanson), iOS programming (InRu, Shurik Babayev), work with Dynamic Data (Microsoft, Gaidar Magdanurov).

At the event, technical innovations were presented, and invited experts not only showed / told about them, but also gave the basics of programming for augmented and virtual reality, for the iPad. At future events we will also try to present interesting news and give the basics of programming.

We will take into account the criticism of the organization of the event expressed earlier in the TrendKlab blog.

Thanks to our sponsors: Intel, ASUS and Microsoft, also Start in Garage.

Many thanks to the Moscow Academy of Finance and Industry for their wonderful venue.

The next HackDay will be held in St. Petersburg on October 23-24, and a HackDay battle for software companies is planned to be held in November.

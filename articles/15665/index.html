Nuclear bombing of Hiroshima could kill and injure about 832 thousand people

A group investigating the tragedy in Hiroshima reported that, according to their estimates, a total of about 832,000 people could have been killed or wounded.

A research team led by Professor Hiromi Hasai (Hiromi Hasai) from the University of Hiroshima finally presented a report of their research on the tragedy with the number of casualties.

This was the first prediction of damage caused by a nuclear strike, which was developed by order of local government on the basis of the Law on the Protection of the Public. The results of the study will be reflected in a plan to protect the residents of the city of Hiroshima, whose population was about 1.1 million, in case of unforeseen military operations.

The research team, however, concluded that the only measure that could fully protect citizens from a nuclear attack was to completely abandon nuclear weapons.

WHAT IS SOCIAL ENTREPRENEURSHIP?

Often social entrepreneurship is confused with charity. Of course, there are some of its elements. But still, this is not exactly charity.

Social entrepreneurs are entrepreneurs acting at the intersection of charity and traditional business, solving social problems, popularizing charity and philanthropy, and giving business a human face.
Social entrepreneurs improve the world by inventing and spreading new approaches to solving social problems, using both traditional business practices and innovations, inventing new forms of business, or non-traditional adapting traditional business approaches to social problems.

The Skoll Foundation offered the following formulation: Social entrepreneurs are agents of social change, creators of innovations who violate the status quo and change the world for the better.




Social entrepreneurship is a big trend in Silicon Valley. There are even educational programs that offer social entrepreneurship programs. For example, Stanford University offers programs for social entrepreneurs: Center for Social Innovation , HAAS Center for Public Service , Stanford Center for Philanthropy and Civil Society ( Stanford Center on Philanthropy and Civil Society ).

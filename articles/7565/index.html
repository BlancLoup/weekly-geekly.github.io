ooVoo is a new client for video communications and IM

In an era of rapid development of Internet telephony, and its penetration into the lives of ordinary users, software developers are becoming increasingly difficult to express themselves and get into the market, where the pace is set by such giants as Skype, Google Talk and others. Today, in order not to get lost in a crowd of similar startups, you really need to come up with something new. The developers of the ooVoo service did not reinvent the wheel, instead they focused on the video transfer function, which is often found in similar applications, but at the same time it develops on the residual principle.


ooVoo is positioning itself as a service for video conferencing , and this is no longer just VoIP, but VoIP squared - vVoIP.

The program, which is still in beta testing, already allows for multipoint video conferences with up to 5 people. At the same time, special attention is paid to the quality of video and audio transmission. An interesting feature is the display of video images in 3D, creating the effect of a virtual meeting room. During the conversation, the interlocutors can easily exchange familiar text messages or send and receive files.


The second main function of ooVoo allows you to create a video letter directly in the program, which you can immediately send to friends who are offline or to those who have not yet become ooVoo clients. The link to the video message comes to the specified E-mail and the recipient can at any time view your appeal.

The program interface is very similar to Skype. It is also convenient and intuitive. At the same time it is graphically designed in the style of Windows Vista. The same colors and familiar gradients. So the Windows Vista user will not have to customize the program for the general style - the ooVoo team has already taken care of this.

The program is absolutely free, and if you look carefully, you can find a Russian interface in it.

For normal operation, ooVoo will require a fairly powerful computer (Pentium 4 1Ghz, 256MB RAM and higher) and a wide Internet channel.

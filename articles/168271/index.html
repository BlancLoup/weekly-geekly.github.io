Graphic subsystem:


  • Supported APIs are DirectX 11.1 , OpenGL 4.0 and OpenCL 1.2 . (Against DirectX 11.0, OpenGL 3.3 and OpenCL 1.1 in the current graphics generation)
  • Like Intel HD Graphics of the previous two generations, Haswell GPUs will be released in different versions, differing in the number of executive devices (EU), and, accordingly, in performance. But now a new GT3 will be added to the GT1 and GT2 modifications. GT3 will include twice as much EU as GT2, while the exact number of actuators is not disclosed, but is easily located on the network. Pay attention to the upper corner - at the entrance of the graphic pipeline is located the "Minister-Administrator" of commands - Command Streamer (CS). This unit itself is not new in Intel HD Graphics, but here it is supplemented by another unit - Resource Streamer (RS) - Resource Manager. The RS parser ‚Äúruns ahead‚Äù of the CS, preparing the necessary data for its operation, that is, doing what the driver on the CPU had previously done. Media subsystem:


All purple blocks in the diagram refer to the video processing subsystem.
Among them there is a new Haswell-specific - Video Quality Engine (Video Quality Block). That is, various video quality enhancements exist in Intel HD Graphics ‚Äî noise reduction, deinterlacing, skin tone correction, adaptive contrast change, and other similar features (which, by the way, if you want / need, are easily disabled in Intel HD Graphics settings). But only in Haswell Graphics allocated them in a separate block, and most importantly, added two more features to them, which you hardly want to disable. This is Image Stabilization and Frame Rate Conversion .
Image stabilization is an improvement in video implemented in the gland, taken from shaking hands without a fixed camera on a fixed tripod. The appearance of this feature could be easily foreseen, if you knew that video compression using MPEG and similar formats uses motion estimation , also performed in Intel HD Graphics hardware. And knowing how the movement takes place between individual frames, it is easy to understand that this ‚Äúmovement‚Äù is just a jitter of the picture, and accordingly compensate for it.

The frame rate conversion is a transition in video from 24 or 30 frames per second to 60. Moreover, it is not based on the reproduction of existing frames and not even on their simple interpolation, but on the same principles as image stabilization. That is, the interframe motion estimation is calculated, after which a smart interpolation is made on its basis with the help of motion compensation (motion compensation), which gives a smoother picture.


The remaining blocks of the media subsystem are the same as in Ivy Bridge, only better.
Namely, Haswell Graphics supports in hardware :
  • Decode SVC ( Scalable Video Coding ) to AVC, VC1 and MPEG2
  • Motion JPEG decoding (video in this format is often given by cell phones and soap boxes)
  • MPEG2 encoding (DVD creation, DLNA transfer)
  • High resolution video decoding - up to 4096x2304 inclusive
  • improving the quality of video encoding, the ability to choose between performance and quality - encoding modes Fast, Normal and Quality

Please note that the Media Sampler blocks that are actively involved in video coding - they receive the above-mentioned motion estimation, are cumulatively added in the GT2 and GT3 configurations, so from these modifications we should expect an increase in performance not only graphics, but also work with video.

Display support.


Haswell Graphics supports:
  • 3 displays simultaneously
  • Display Port 1.2 with serial panel connection
  • high resolution displays up to 3840x2160 @ 60Hz through Display Port 1.2 and 4096x2304 @ 24Hz through HDMI inclusive
  • collage mode

Only the last paragraph needs explanation. Collage mode is the connection of four displays (via two special splitters - dongles) to display Quad Full HD.

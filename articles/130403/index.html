This is probably the nicest side of the job. Fresh air, beautiful view. It would not get down.



On the average Moscow house - 3-4 cables from different operators. We are for healthy competition.



A colleague unwinds the cable and puts it on the roof. For suspension between houses, a cable with a suspension cable, the so-called "eight" is used:



The strength element is a steel cable (1) on which a twist of modules hangs around a central strength element (usually a plastic rod - 2) in which optical fibers (3) float in a hydrophobic gel. Hydrophobic gel prevents longitudinal distribution of moisture, if it falls inside the polybutylene terephthalate module. The fibers are placed in the module in such a way that their length is 1-3% longer than the length of the module. This is necessary to compensate for thermal expansion, so that at any load tensile forces are not transmitted to brittle fibers. The sheath (7) is usually made of polyethylene, in which carbon black is added for light stabilization, since polyethylene decomposes under the action of ultraviolet (therefore, all cables for external installation are usually black)



Contrary to popular rumors, the cable between the houses is not forwarded with the help of silent crossbows and folding hang-gliders, but with such a device, called a walk-through or, in common monkey. On rubberized rollers set in motion by a small electric motor that is powered by a battery, the monkey overcomes a rise of up to 30 degrees by catching on an already stretched cable. If there is no cable, a rope comes down from two buildings, and its ends are tied on the ground, and then lifted to the roof.



Our monkey bears the proud name "KARABASik"

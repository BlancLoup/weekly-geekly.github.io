Fir-trees with a name through the Internet

Norwegians Anders and Bartosz believe that each Christmas tree is unique in its own way, and all of them must have their own names. These guys, who live very close to the forest, sometimes go there with a camera for another batch of fresh pictures with young firs. They give each of them by name and place photos on their website www.juletrefraskogen.no .


You can order the Christmas tree in the same place - this cost is NOK 300 (about $ 54). In this case, the guys will again go to the forest with an ax to carefully chop down your copy. You can pick up the goods yourself - in the city of Oslo, or use the delivery services (additionally 100 CZK).

The concept of such a service may seem somewhat bizarre to someone, but it vividly illustrates the general trend: consumers do not want to buy a faceless product - they want to get more - a unique, exclusive product with its own history. Such a story that they would gladly share with their friends and family.

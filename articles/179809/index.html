Warm wooden sound



Amanda Gussey, who a few months earlier managed to print a live record on a 3D printer, continues to go crazy. Based on the lessons from the previous series of experiments, it now converted the MP3 file into a program for the 120-watt laser cutter Epilog Legend 36EXT using Python and Processing scripts and was able to cut a gramophone from wood and even thick paper. Characteristics are slightly worse than the records printed on a 3D printer - 4-5 bits and 4.5 kHz. Like last time, a detailed description of the experiment and all the scripts used are published on Instructables .

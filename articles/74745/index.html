TechEd'09: Starting Exchange 2010 and Something More


Today at the ongoing TechEd'09 conference in Berlin, Microsoft officially announced the launch of an updated version of its one of the most famous and most popular products - Microsoft Exchange Server 2010.

The official site of the project contains the full amount of information about the new product. The conference reported on the numbers and the effectiveness of the new version, which is tens of percent more than its predecessor in all parameters. For self-calculation of efficiency, you can use the calculator of the effect of the introduction of Exchange 2010.

Exchange 2010 has already tested such large companies as: Nasdaq OMX, BGC Partners, Subaru Canada, NEC Philips, Lion Nathan. BGC Partners, for example, reports a savings of $ 1 million when implementing Exchange 2010.

Trying a new server is easy: you can try a test drive through the web, download a 120-day trial in the form of an installation package or as a ready-made deployed VHD image or try Exchage Online Trial. As you can see, there are plenty of ways to evaluate a new server.

Those who want to know what's new in Exchange 2010 can go to the What's New page ? PS: In addition, a new protection complex for the infrastructure of Exchange 2010 has been launched - Forefront Protection 2010.

The second news concerns Eclipse and Java . Microsoft announced the acquisition of Teamprise , which created plug-ins for Eclipse to work with Team Foundation Server. According to Microsoft, the acquisition of Teamprise was motivated by the desire to offer a product and solution that would allow customers to have integrated integration with TFS when developing .NET and Java applications. Thus, with the purchase of the next version of TFS, the client automatically integrates with many Eclipse-based code editors.

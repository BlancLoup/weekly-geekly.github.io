As you guessed, the criminals were found (looking ahead, I will say that this happened naturally without the help of the police). About what to fear when buying a new or used MacBook, as well as how the criminals were found and caught, read under the cut.



Prehistory


I'll start with the lyrics. A year ago, I bought myself an Apple Thunderbolt Display and found that my MacBook Pro does not work with it because it has a Mini Display Port. Although the connector is absolutely identical, the DP-> TB bundle does not work, but on the contrary, TB-> DP works fine. In general, at this moment, not only I was so deceived, there was quite a serious confusion with this compatibility, and even consultants from the Sydney Apple Store argued that a new Apple Display could be connected to the DisplayPort connector. But this is absolutely not true, compatibility is only in the opposite direction. I was annoyed by this fact and changed with my wife (she had a Macbook Air 13 2011) laptops.

Then I was just amazed at the performance and usability of the Air: the screen resolution was like 15, Xcode worked quickly, there was no memory shortage at all even with 4 GB of RAM and even worked fine in Windows 7 running in parallels. Then I threw away the thought of buying a new MacBook and decided to wait for something new, I was hoping for 15 inch air.
A year has passed and Apple released the Macbook Pro 15 Retina, and I realized that I needed to take it.

Outset


Naturally, at the beginning of sales, I did not buy it, since it was during this period that there were no trips to countries where it could be bought, and there was no desire to overpay the starting cheat.
It was still aggravated by the fact that I needed a lot of RAM (after all, 4GB on the Air later became not enough). Therefore, I did not want the standard 975 or 976 model, because the memory had become soldered and it was no longer possible to upgrade it (by normal human means without soldering).

It so happened that with 16 GB we only sell the most top-of-the-line model, sometimes intermediate models with such a volume of RAM slipped, but they quickly sold out.
Therefore, I did not hurry too much, planning to buy this unit during a winter trip to Europe.

A Retina Macbook is not a completely unambiguous device (not many applications are still adapted), so it is likely that someone bought, tried and did not like it. Well, a big plus - the American layout and the lack of engraving.

I really liked it and I called the seller.

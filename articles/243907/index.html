Types of voice acting in games

Voice in video games, as well as music and sounds, can be diegetic, extradiegetic or transdiegetic.

Diegetic voices are heard both to the character and to the player himself. These are dialogues, monologues, cries on the battlefield, songs in taverns, and even reflections of the main character.



Extradiegetic voices are heard only by the player. It is often the voiceover of the narrator, who tells about some events that occur before the start or during the game. He can also suggest what actions the user must take to successfully complete the mission.



Transdiegetic voices belong to the game world and at the same time are system notifications. ‚ÄúMy inventory is full!‚Äù - on the one hand, we hear the voice of the hero and understand that it is he who says it, but at the same time such a message should convey to the player a thought: it‚Äôs time to run to the merchant and sell the loot.

Outsourcing or death

There are many studios that are engaged in voice acting video games. They will gladly offer you their services. And therein lies the catch: no matter how the experts from the autosourcing company try to fulfill your order, they will never be able to achieve the required quality bar. Moreover, not many studios can boast of real-life experience in voicing game projects, and even the most famous ones sometimes refer to the final product ... not in the best way.



The voice acting is better to entrust to a person who is well acquainted with the development team, knows the product features, the characters of the characters and understands what result to get. At the same time, you should objectively evaluate your own strengths: will there be enough time for the sound producer to find the right studio, equipment, casting and create a quality product? In large projects, the sounding process can take months and years, but even behind the dialogues from a small indie game there is hard work and an iron endurance of the sound designer.

Casting

Standard voice sets the game and its genre. Very much depends on whether you work with a mobile runner, browser-based platformer or RPG for Next Gen consoles, if 50,000 lines of dialogs are written in the script, or if there is only a list of short exclamations printed on A4 paper.

To cast, it is not necessary to invite hundreds of people to the studio. Successful trials can be arranged, even if your sound engineer is a social phobia, and the actors live on the other hemisphere. It is enough to send them the text with the appropriate instructions and explanations, and then evaluate the result. Even a professional actor should have some experience in voice acting, otherwise there is a risk of very long to beat the drama out of him. Make a base of interesting voices, even if they are not suitable for this project.
In extreme cases, be creative. There are many options for how it is interesting to voice the characters, without recording the voices of the actors.



Some useful tips

  • Watch for uniform sound. As in the case of graphics, the game as a whole and each location in particular should have a stylistically uniform sound design.
  • Use frequency processing to select the optimum sound.
  • Listen to how the whole scene sounds. Too loud shouts or dialogues can get out of the general concept and destroy the state of immersion. At the same time, an important phrase, sounding too quiet, can be missed by the player.
  • Testing is a guarantee of quality. At the final stage, listen to how the sounds overlap and which of them can be removed without regret. Make sure that there is no chaos and cacophony.
  • To achieve the desired effect, combine studio and field recordings.
  • Avoid exact repetition of sound bites. If possible, vary or randomize phrases even minor characters.
  • Start working on voice acting as early as possible, as soon as the final version of the dialogues or phrases is ready. Leave a decent amount of time to re-sound some moments if they do not fit well with the visuals.

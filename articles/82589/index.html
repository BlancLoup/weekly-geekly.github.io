Nokia began to close its competitors

Letter 27 625 631 to Nav4All

Dear customers!

We are very sad to inform you that Nav4All and Tracking & Tracing global navigation systems will go offline within 3 days. The reason - the license agreement for data from Navteq (a 100% subsidiary of Nokia) unexpectedly was not extended. Switch to another supplier in the shortest possible time. Nav4All systems were designed for Navteq data. Consequently, Nav4All is forced to suspend work.

We regret that we are forced to stop the work of our service. With your help, Nav4All has developed into a global product in 5 years with 27.5 million users in 56 languages. Nav4All has become the largest navigation provider. A large number of users is explained by the fact that Nav4All works on hundreds of different mobile phones of brands such as Blackberry, Sony Ericsson, Samsung, Motorola, Android, HTC, Nokia, LG, iPhone, iPod, etc.

After 5 years of development and testing, we have witnessed rapid, virtually exponential growth over the past two years. This increase has been included in the Navteq license report. In mid-December 2009, global coverage expanded to the Philippines, Morocco and Kenya.

Please contact all support questions: nav4all.com/support . If additional information on the subject of this letter appears, it will be published on our website nav4all.com . For privacy purposes, Nav4All does not store the e-mail addresses of all of its customers, so we ask you to send this letter to everyone who can be in the know.

Respectfully,

Hennie JM Groot Koerkamp (CEO)
Nav4All BV
Keizersgracht 62-64
1015 CS Amsterdam NL

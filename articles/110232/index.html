Review of free lancer fees

The site for searching remote work Free-lance.ru conducted a study of fees of free-lancers. The results are published today in the newspaper "Kommersant".
As it turned out, the average monthly earnings of free-lancers are 44,000 rubles. At the same time, the average salary in Russia is 20,000 rubles.

Average monthly fees of free lancers, rub.

It is also worth noting that among the mobile devices from which free-lancers go to the site Free-lance.ru, the iPad leads, followed by the iPhone.
Now there is every opportunity to make good money in RuNet, not working in the staff of any company: 31,000 projects are published monthly on Free-lance.ru , the average project cost is 20,000 rubles. At the same time, only 30% of the audience is Muscovites, and 70% are free-lancers from regions where prices and wages are much lower than in the capital, and the unemployment rate is higher.
Best of all earn free-lance programmers and web-developers. Free-Lance is also very popular among copywriters, designers and translators, but their fees are lower.

In many ways, the fact that free-lancers earn twice as much as the Russians, on average, is explained by the fact that their level of education is much higher than all-Russian indicators. 55% of free-lancers and only 26% of working Russians have a higher education or a degree. And if you take into account incomplete higher education, the differences become even greater. It is curious that the most educated are housewives sitting at home with small children: among them, 63% have a higher education, 11% have two or more higher and 2% have a scientific degree.

In addition, free-lancers are very ambitious. 39% of all respondents plan to become entrepreneurs. The majority (44%) in 5 years plan to combine free-lance with another job. Only 9% want to work only free-lancers. And only about 5% are ready to turn into office workers.
The Russian market of free-lance is growing every year by 100%, and the number of users looking for remote work on the Internet is only 4-5 times less than the number of Russians searching for full-time work on job sites.
The study involved 8,895 free lancers.

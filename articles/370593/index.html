How does artificial intelligence help save the world?

Ocean protection


Almost all people, with few exceptions, live on land. And those who are far from the coast may find it difficult to understand how important the oceans are to us. They occupy about 71% of the earth's surface, the vast majority of living organisms live in water - more than 91%. The oceans are the place where life originated and where it continues to flourish to this day.

Man does little to protect this source of life. For example, the Big Coral Reef gradually dies - it is not completely dead, but a significant part of it - yes. Corals gradually turn into a hard, dead calcareous skeleton. The vast territories where the corals used to live, turned into literally cemeteries that remind people of how strong their influence on the environment is. Despite the attempts of different countries to solve the problem, to limit fishing and the killing of marine mammals, the effect is insignificant, and poaching continues to flourish.



In November 2016, The Nature Conservancy (TNC) organization launched a system that allows you to track poaching boats and other vessels almost in real time. Fisheries inspectors, reading the data of this software platform, can understand where their attention is required.

The system also helps fishermen who work legally. Previously, they spent a large amount of time trying to detect shoals of fish. Now this time cost is reduced by about 40%.

‚ÄúThe project team used computer vision and machine learning technology, similar to that used in face recognition,‚Äù said Matt Marrifield, a spokesman for TNC.



As for poachers, their movement in almost real time is shown on the Global Fishing Watch website. To do this, use the SkyTruth system, which processes satellite data to determine the direction of movement of large and small ships. Over its lifetime, the system has found about 86,000 poacher vessels.

Prediction of natural disasters


Disasters caused by the forces of nature are quite difficult to predict. For decades, scientists have been trying to predict earthquakes in various regions in order to warn the population in time. Now, with the advent of artificial intelligence, experts have at their disposal a new tool - supercomputers allow scientists to receive and process much more information than ever before.



Now scientists are gradually beginning to study the nature of earthquakes with the help of neural networks. This is done in order to find signs that can predict the occurrence of earthquakes.

Paul Johnson and Chris Maron, geophysicists at Pennsylvania State University, are involved in this. ‚ÄúIf we were engaged in this ten years ago, we would have no chance,‚Äù said Johnson. In order to study the nature of earthquakes, Johnson and his colleagues measure not only the characteristics of natural earthquakes, but also try to capture the parameters of artificial ones. They are satisfied in the university laboratory, in the field. The results are processed using neural networks in order to catch patterns, certain parameters by which one can understand where and at what time the cataclysm should occur.

The neural network, which is used by scientists to work with data, has already revealed some regularities. For example, certain acoustic signals in the lithosphere are a clear sign that a cataclysm will soon occur. ‚ÄúThe algorithm can not just predict the time and place of the event, but it also shows some characteristics of the phenomenon that we simply did not pay attention to before,‚Äù say the scientists.

There is still much work to be done, so far the accuracy of the system is not very high. It is tuned to function in real time so that all characteristics can be predicted quickly and without problems.

What about in the future?


In addition to earthquake prediction, AI is used in many other areas. For example, to solve problems with a growing population and food shortages. By 2030, the population should increase to 8 billion people, and will grow at the same pace up to 2050 (there are simply no longer-term forecasts, but growth is unlikely to stop).



According to statistics, 1 out of 9 people go to bed with an empty stomach, and we are not talking about healthy nutrition at all, but about chronic hunger among a significant part of the world's population.

They are trying to solve this problem now - scientists from Carnegie Mellon University have created a system called FarmView. This is a hardware and software platform that helps to monitor crops, take care of plants and conduct general monitoring of agricultural land.

The main element of this platform is a robot, which is located directly on the field. He observes the crops, and the photos and videos are processed by the software platform. Scientists say that they are developing their platform not to replace a person in the field, but to make farmers work more efficiently and bring more products. FarmView allows you to harvest using fewer workers while reducing time.



‚ÄúThe methods currently used in agriculture require a lot of resources, and in fact there are a limited amount of resources everywhere,‚Äù say the system developers. "We want to increase the production of food while improving their quality."

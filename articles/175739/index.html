French intelligence forced Wikipedia sysop to delete article


Headquarters of the French Central Intelligence Agency

Wikimedia France issued a statement strongly condemning the pressure exerted by employees of the Central Department of Internal Intelligence (DCRI) on the volunteer sysop Wikipedia.

In early March, DCRI employees contacted Wikipedia for the first time and demanded an immediate removal from the French section of the encyclopedia of an article on a military radio station that contains classified information. Wikipedia refused to fulfill the request, citing insufficient grounds for making such a decision.



Not satisfied with the answer, on April 4, 2013, the secret services summoned to their office one of the French Wikipedian community, which has access to the function of deleting articles.

Like many other members of the Wikipedia community, this user works on a voluntary basis. At the DCRI office, he was forced to remove the article, threatening to leave him in custody and open a criminal case in case of non-compliance. Although the volunteer explained to agents how Wikipedia works, and that in this way they will not be able to permanently delete information, they still insisted on deleting the article. Naturally, under pressure, the guy fulfilled the requirements of DCRI, he had no other choice. The agents also told him to tell the rest of the sysop that if they tried to cancel the deletion of the article, they would be accountable to the law.

Interestingly, this sysop had nothing to do with the article and did not know about its existence until the call to the internal intelligence office. He was chosen because he was the easiest to identify: he often participated in various events of Wikipedia and Wikimedia in France, gave interviews as a representative of Wikipedia, etc.

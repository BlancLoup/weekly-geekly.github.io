Google Fiber fiber earned in Kansas City, 700 Mbps real speed


Users brag about their internet speed

The first houses in Kansas City have finally received the Internet via fiber as part of the Google Fiber project. Yesterday, November 13, installers from Google came to residential areas and installed routers in the homes of the first lucky ones .

According to the tariff plans , fiber per gigabit is offered for $ 70 without traffic restrictions. It is much faster and cheaper than the absolute majority of American Internet service providers. It should be borne in mind that in the homeland of the Internet access to the Internet is much more expensive than in Russia and Ukraine, so for America such prices - $ 70 per gigabit, without traffic restrictions - this is a real revolution.

Basic 5 Mbit / s access is provided without any subscription fees. The Google Fiber project was announced in July 2012, but so far, work has been done on laying cable over power lines and mass hilling of users, who are profitable to connect at once in whole blocks, so as not to send equipment several times to one district.

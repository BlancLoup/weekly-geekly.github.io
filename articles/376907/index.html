US intelligence needs a system to predict cyber attacks



The Office of the Director of National Intelligence (ODNI) of the United States invites private companies and research centers to participate in the development of a program that could predict cybercrime and hacker attacks. As planned by the Office, the system should automatically track Internet activity, which would indicate the planning and preparation of attacks.

Among the data sources are social networks, news, chat rooms, blogs, message boards, search queries, and other sources (perhaps large cryptocurrency transactions). Despite the obvious connection of the field of the proposed system with data that ECHELON and PRISM illegally collect, it is reported that the project development companies will not be given access to the NSA data. It is possible that the National Security Agency plans in the future to abandon illegal wiretaps if the new system shows good results, working only with public data.

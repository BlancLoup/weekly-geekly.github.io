"The sound of a length of 8 seconds": the story of Mellotron

The material on the predecessor of modern samplers is mellotron. Outwardly, the mellotron resembles an electronic organ. But, in fact, this is just a big tape recorder.

Each tool key corresponds to a piece of tape with a short audio recording. When you press a key, it is played, allowing the performer to imitate the sound of acoustic instruments or entire ensembles.

Due to its grandiose sound, the instrument was in demand by rock bands such as Pink Floyd, Deep Purple and King Crimson. In the article, we talked about the history of mellotron, its role in music for films and TV series, as well as the fate of the instrument in the 1990s and 2000s.

The judges legalized the frequency for "Beeline"

Two wins at once in the framework of the same dispute with the regulating authorities were won by the mobile operator VimpelCom (the Beeline trademark). On Tuesday, the Presnensky District Court of Moscow rejected the suit of the Federal Communications Agency (Rossvyaz) against the Federal Antimonopoly Service (FAS), which had previously declared illegal the refusal of VimpelCom to issue frequencies to the Far Eastern enlarged district. VimpelCom submitted relevant applications to the State Commission for Radio Frequencies dozens of times (SCRF, an interdepartmental body coordinated by Rossvyaz), but none of them were satisfied. In this regard, the operator filed a complaint with the FAS, which in March supported the operator and issued the relevant prescription with respect to the SCRF . Now the legality of this prescription was confirmed by the court.

And on Monday, the Moscow Arbitration Court granted a lawsuit filed by VimpelCom itself to the Federal Service for Supervision in the Sphere of Communications (Rossvyaznadzor), which, like Rossvyaz, is subordinate to the Ministry of Information Technologies and Communications. This lawsuit also related to the situation with the Far Eastern frequencies, however, it dealt with another episode. For several years, VimpelCom received refusals to issue licenses to work on the Far Eastern frequencies already assigned to it, and the bases for this at various times were various prepositions. The last time Rossvyaznadzor pointed to the fact that the TVZ, after receiving the Far Eastern frequencies, changed its organizational and legal form from a limited liability company (LLC) to a closed joint-stock company (CJSC), and as a result of this, it lost the right to use these frequencies. The supervisory service defended this position during the court hearings, however, the court eventually took the side of the operator.

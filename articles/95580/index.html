I continue about shooting modding and computer devices (the first part is here ).

Today I propose to familiarize yourself with the filming of ultraviolet-glowing objects Although it is more correct, of course, to say shooting in blacklight , since many people associate ultraviolet radiation with sunlight, which is not true in this case. Well, in the text, let blacklight and ultraviolet light be synonymous for convenience.



Manufacturers now make a lot of computer elements glowing in the ultraviolet, right up to the motherboards .
How to remove them, and let's talk ...

What is required for U.F. shooting?



  1. In 99% of cases, the background is black , although with white you can sometimes get interesting effects. By the way, a sheet of black paper or a piece of black cloth is also suitable.
  2. 220V UV lamp - one or more. (Of course, there are Black Light lamps for modding a computer that connect to a 12V power supply. But I would recommend using 220V UV lamps for photographing.)
  3. Mirror, honeycomb, plexiglass - optional.


Info


In general, for a professional "subject" you need a lot and a lot of lighting equipment (take a look at your leisure, good video).
But in the case of blacklight shooting, this is not the case, for all these expensive softboxes, umbrellas, external flashes, studio lights, etc. - usually meaningless in the ultraviolet (with rare exceptions).

So shoot professionally in the blacklight spectrum is available to everyone at the lowest cost.

I will make this game even more like our world

Imagine the good old Sims, only with artificial intelligence, self-study and gameplay so close to reality that if we did not have the opportunity to attract third-party developers with our ready-made ideas, this project would take us forever to implement. But the rich sponsor (we even joked that it was probably the government of one large and densely populated country decided to please its citizens with a new MMORPG) did not spare money - we had everything: a farm of supercomputers, access to any artificial intelligence algorithms developed by scientists from around the world, a team of developers, project managers, errand boys and even your own clown (Andrew insisted that there be a clown in the rest room) and if something happens, we could easily buy a couple of other three companies developing boiling the game with a staff of one thousand people in each staff. We had it all! Everything except time.

We were tasked to release the game in production a maximum of two weeks. As the customer explained to us, the previous contractor took up the development a year ago, but did not cope with the task in due time. The project was in such a state that it was extremely difficult to sort it out and not go crazy. And the project was entrusted to us.

Yes, I do not argue that we are the real gurus in game devs, that no one but us and all that. And we were paid a lot of money. I have not seen so much money in my life, even in a dream. In general, we simply could not refuse this project. For cultural and ethical reasons. If everything worked out, we would become known around the world as the most creative developers of computer games, besides working only for amounts with a large number of zeros at the end. But, unfortunately, everything turned out quite differently, now I am sitting in a room with soft white walls, completely without windows, and I thank the orderly for allowing me to draw a little with marker pens. Draw with damn markers!

Of course, I'm not going to draw anything, but my only chance to get out of the mental hospital is to tell everything as it really was. I have a couple of sheets of paper, orange and blue felt-tip pen and a lot of free time, so, perhaps, I'll start my story.

Dealing with the project was fun! The previous developer screwed the game from the space simulator to the game, so everything was fine with the Universe. Only for beauty, a couple of changes were made - we pushed the planets, where we needed it and where it was not necessary to put satellites and moons, for greater beauty we created giant asteroids periodically flying into the inhabited part of space. Andrew wanted to program the atmosphere and geophysical data on all the planets of the solar system as habitable virtual characters of our game, but I said that he should not waste time on this. They entrusted the generation of characteristics to a random number generator. The result was a full seam - where the nitrate rains, where the air consists of sulfur oxide, but, in general, it suited us.

Finally, we focused on the main gaming planet-location, on which life will exist. The physics and chemistry of processes was transferred from real life, but with reservations. Algorithms are too complex, we had to simplify, because even our mainframes do not have such processing power of game processes. It's good that the game turned out to be four-dimensional, it greatly simplified our lives and calculations. So far we have worked well.

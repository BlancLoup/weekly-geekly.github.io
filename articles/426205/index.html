With the release of Chrome 70 thousand sites protected by Symantec certificates will become untrusted


Recently, Google announced the termination of trust for all SSL certificates issued before December 1, 2017 by Symantec, Thawte, GeoTrust and RapidSSL certification authorities. The changes will take effect on October 16 of this year with the release of the new anniversary browser Google Chrome 70.

Apparently, some companies did not receive notification of these changes.

Distrust of all certificates of the Symantec family means the last stage of the three-step process, initiated by Google and Mozilla, which will completely rid the Internet of invalid SSL. Despite the success of the entire campaign, some organizations continue to use Symantec SSL for unknown reasons. According to a recent report made by security analyst Scott Helm, after the release of Chrome 70, more than 1,000 sites will no longer be trusted.

Russian "autopilot" for cars

On Habr√©, they have written more than once about "autopilot" for cars developed by Google, BMW and Volkswagen. But there are similar developments in Russia. The RoboSiVi Company develops a universal navigation system capable of operating as part of ground vehicles, water robots, and UAVs. In April 2012, the company officially became a resident of the space cluster of the Skolkovo innovation center and subsequently received a grant to develop the project.

Unlike the highly specialized development of Google and automakers, RoboSiVi company has a broader task - to create a universal "brain" that can manage not only cars, but also the most diverse technology. Developers themselves among the possible applications are called: automobile equipment, which is used in coal and ore quarries, agricultural equipment, machinery-robots that carry cargo in a warehouse or somewhere in production, robot guides, who meet a person at the entrance to the office and see off him up to a particular cabinet ...

More information about this development (including plans for terms and prices) is in an interview with the company's general director Sergei Maltsev .

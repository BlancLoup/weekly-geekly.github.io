BitCluster script shows the connection between Bitcoin wallets and transactions

Link analysis can be performed for large sites in a darkweb and a regular network.



Bitcoin is a great alternative to ordinary money. But this system is still not completely anonymous, as many believe - you can identify the user. The first version of its software, written in Python, the developers showed at the NorthSec 2015 conference. The tool is called BitCluster. It analyzes transactions between individual addresses, and shows the detected connections. The results of the script can be downloaded as a table.

Instead of tracking transactions at a specific address, the script tracks all transfers related to a specific user. This may be a dragdiler, a fraudster (creator of a cryptographer) or a regular user of the system. Experts will present new results of research on analyzing operations in a darkwalk using BitCluster at the HOPE event.

In most cases, the creators of cryptographers provide a wallet to which the victim must send the means to get the decryption key of their files. Attackers can thus track payment by individual users. In addition, BitCluster can distinguish groups of interconnected wallets that are used to conduct transactions for specific sites - for example, to identify the purses of sites used to hold customer funds in the course of a transaction with the seller or supplier of a particular service. Researchers have already done the analysis for the original Silk Road, and work has also been done on studying new markets, including AlphaBay and Nucleus. Using the available information, the script shows how many people make payments at a single point in time, how many transactions are completed and for how much. The size of the average transaction is also shown.



Previous studies have also shown the possibility of linking the conducted transactions to specific users of the site when studying the time of posting a withdrawal of a transaction. This work was also carried out using BitCluster.

Soon, developers will lay out their script to the public. The team does not want to host this tool, since it is very resource-intensive, and for analyzing large amounts of data (for example, on the Mt. Gox site), powerful servers are needed.

The first genetically modified apples will be available in 2017

The US Department of Agriculture (USDA) authorized the Canadian biotechnology company Okanagan Specialty Fruits to sell genetically modified apples, which should appear in stores in 2017. "Improved" apples have the property of remaining white longer after they are cut into slices. The variety of GMO-apples was named Arctic and later on it will receive new properties of Golden Delicious and Granny Smith.

In the United States, two independent government organizations are involved in entering the market for genetically modified products - the Department of Agriculture and the Food and Drug Administration (FDA). The USDA is considering the safety of GMO plants for other crops, giving permission for their cultivation. A much more controversial issue - the safe use of GMO products in food - is handled by the FDA, while its solution for many companies does not interfere with sales. In November 2014, the USDA has already granted permission to sell GMO potato varieties Russet and Atlantic. If you cook "updated" potatoes at high temperature, then it forms less of the chemical substance acrylamide , characteristic of plants containing starch. It is believed that potato chips made from GMO potatoes will be less harmful than normal, leading to the development of certain types of malignant tumors.

Now the US legislation is more liberal for genetically modified products than 20 years ago. In the late 1990s, the biotechnology company Monsanto offered farmers GMO-resistant potato to the Colorado potato beetle. However, due to the heightened anxiety of buyers, farmers soon refused to grow it, motivating it by the fact that the effect of potatoes on human health is not yet known.

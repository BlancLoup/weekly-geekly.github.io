Report from the Integrated Systems Europe-2014


Multitach-wall, on which several people can simultaneously draw at once

This year, from 4 to 6 February, the next Integrated Systems Europe (ISE) 2014 exhibition was held in Amsterdam. This is one of the most significant events in the audio-video and multimedia integration industry: the best manufacturers and a lot of IT people come here every year. Last year, for example, there were 44 thousand participants and 900 manufacturers.

If you go to ISE "from the street", you will first see absolutely stunning things like TVs with curved screens, complex-shaped LED panels hanging in the air, different installation acoustics, video walls with that. And all this - with amazing content. Word for word, speech will go about switching systems, controllers and other technical details - and then you will understand why you should really go to ISE.

The main trend of this exhibition is a 4K (UltraHD) image. It's 4096x3112: UHD is 4 times cooler than Full HD. When watching a movie in this resolution, there is a very unusual feeling of realism. The flagship in this matter is the Christie company, already well known to us from past exhibitions and integrations. Naturally, at exhibitions, content is specially selected for display, on-air TV is still very far from such permissions.

Video walls


Video walls are used for all sorts of advertising and sharing information. Most often, you can see these solutions in retail, in exhibition complexes, in the financial sector (for example, in the coolest offices of banks), as well as in dispatch centers. Video walls are good because people can approach them directly, watch the right piece of information - and sometimes even interact with the content. For example, for retail, a frequent example is the display of a collection of dresses (or cars for men). Each object can be approached and viewed closer. If there is a camera, IR system or other sensors - by touching, you can get more detailed information or change the color.

In Russia, there is almost no video wall for advertising, but the big screens of dispatching and situational centers are almost always made using this technology. In such cases, video walls are indispensable.

3D mapping


Special attention should be paid to the Coolux stand, which specializes in the technical equipment of media and promotional events, as well as colorful presentations and multimedia shows (for example, the opening of the Olympics).


The size of the projected image is not limited - using the technology of seamless information of several projectors - panoramic screens, you can achieve images that cover an area of ‚Äã‚Äãany size.

The essence of the technology is that multiple projectors can work on a single screen. You should get a large image without differences in brightness at the edges and seams. For this, the projected images intersect in such a way that the same pixel can display 2-3 projectors (soft overlap stitching) or exactly pixel-by-pixel can be delimited (hard stitching). Accordingly, this requires more than proper adjustment, precise installation, high-quality projectors and controllers to control the display of the picture.
Multi-layer three-dimensional systems are often made when projections are performed not only on the screen, but also on different objects (like on the ball in the photo above). All layers can be freely positioned and animated in 3D space to create multimedia shows. Actually, you saw it at the opening of the Olympics.

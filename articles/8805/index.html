Pros / Cons
Where wins the N800:
1. Screen size: reading books and websites, typing texts, watching movies ‚Äî there‚Äôs no better device for such money for this purpose in the market today. I still can't figure out how to read anything on QVGA screens. 2. Web, Internet communications - in this area, the device is undoubtedly future-oriented, and today, oddly enough, it is also one of the drawbacks.
3. Operating system, a set of applications: Internet Tablet OS 2007 edition was more stable, more convenient, more beautiful than WinMobile. There are no problems with connections via wireless interfaces, the settings for the GPRS connection are already present in the device, the user only needs to select an operator. The software is more than enough, new programs appear every week.
4. Russification of the system - by default, available handwriting text with the recognition of the characters of the Russian alphabet.
5. Innovations in the interface - for example, when the stylus menu is activated, small icons are displayed. When you touch your finger, the icons become large, comfortable for pressing fingers. The same with typing, if you touch the screen in a text editor with your finger, a large keyboard appears, on which it is quite convenient to type texts with your hands. The interface, announced in the iPhone, here justifies itself to 100%, I have almost no doubt that the stylus will soon be superfluous in most devices.
6. Multitasking. Gradually, I do not close applications at all. Minimized programs are located in a row on the panel in the lower left corner. Naturally, their launch speed is very high. There are no problems with memory leaks to digital eternity, as in WMobile; 7. Convenient location and functions of the buttons, which significantly facilitate Internet navigation and reading books.

Where does the N800 lose:
1. The screen brightness still leaves much to be desired. In the sun, even with the brightness maximized to the maximum, you can hardly see anything. This disadvantage can be attributed to most of these devices, but, for example, the HTC PZ300 looks much better.
2. Despite the proud title of an Internet tablet, Google Docs & Spreadsheets is not working yet. In addition, there are problems with YouTube . With confidence - the problems of software, with hope - will be solved with time.
3. The inability to communicate via VoIP due to the absence of WiFi coverage in cities. Again, with a lot of hope, the cause of tomorrow. 4. The lack of support for editing MS Word documents and synchronizing with PCs - the question is also promised to be solved soon.

Conclusion (with caution)
I want to immediately warn you: if you decide to become the owner of the N800, and yesterday you were a PPC user or did not use mobile computers at all, get ready for surprises, it's not that simple. But remember: a special charm gives the fact that to improve the device, changing / writing software, everyone can. To be honest, I, as a hardened Windows user and neophyte of the Internet tablet N800 on Linux, have more questions than answers. I will decide!
As it has already become known, software is like sex, better when free. This means that a huge number of enthusiasts have already created and will still create software for the N800 on their own, while in most cases it will outperform proprietary counterparts. This is the main advantage of the N800.
At the same time, the main obstacle prevents us from using such devices that are ahead of their time with comfort. And here the task is more complicated: to cover the planet with cheap wireless Internet, starting from the open-source ideology and using the collective mind and hands will not work. Or am I wrong?

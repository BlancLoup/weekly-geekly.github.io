Robotic cars are getting closer

A few days ago, some details became known about the main contender for the Urban DARPA Challenge race prize - an open competition for car projects driven by fully automatic robotic systems.

The development of Stanford University, stuffed with the most up-to-date electronics of the Volkswagen Touareg, which the creators gave the name Junior, will be the first such system in the race that will not require a separate power source for their work.

Several video cameras, GPS equipment, laser scanners of the environment, and a whole bunch of unique technological advances will be served by Intel Core 2 Duo and Quad processors, which have very low power consumption and high performance. All this was enough 1000 watts, which is able to give the car electronics.

Good advertising for Intel, nothing to say.

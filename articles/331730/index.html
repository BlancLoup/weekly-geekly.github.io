But it seems to us that so far not everyone appreciated the benefits of remote access using Intel vPro - which in our data center is provided to all dedicated servers without server platforms. So, you need to figure out what kind of technology it is, what it is implemented, and what problems it solves.


What is Intel vPro?



This technology is a real hardware and software complex aimed at an advanced solution of remote access problems: taking into account the high requirements for information security and the need to provide the most extensive management capabilities (which is more detailed below).


From a hardware point of view, Intel has implemented technology based on its own special motherboards (with the Q index, although not all of them support all the vPro features), processors and network controllers. Since the company itself produces all of these nodes, the system has gained unique integrity.


On the software side, the solution is also its own: Intel Active Management Technology (AMT). The product is specially designed for the most efficient remote control, easy inventory of equipment, security and the ability to quickly eliminate any failures - even those in which the problem is remote, without the use of this technology, cannot be solved in principle.


What are the advantages?



First, Intel vPro, when implemented through VNC, provides a full KVM to a remote machine, independent of the operating system.



Thus, even when you restart the connection is not broken. You can remotely enter the BIOS - in other words, without any physical contact with the machine, almost any problems that may arise on it are resolved. It is probably unnecessary to explain how useful this is.


We note only that even the screen resolution of 1920x1200 pixels is supported. Given the high speed - the full feeling that you are not working at all remotely.


Secondly, security. In terms of working with a dedicated server, of course, it is not the Anti-Theft system that is important, but the fact that vPro encrypts data. Encryption and decryption using AES protocol are performed, which ensures high performance. In addition, AMT allows, for example, to isolate a machine infected with malware without losing remote access to it. Read more about the capabilities of AMT in the Wiki .

Case 4. Military operation of the West against Syria: global monitoring of public opinion in social media


The graph shows the number of messages for August 31: the peak - the expectation and speech of Obama about the appeal to Congress for permission to attack Syria:


The purpose of the study is to answer the following questions:
1. How actively are people from different countries discussing the situation in Syria.
2. How do residents of the largest NATO countries and Turkey feel about a possible attack on Syria.
3. Who is considered guilty in this situation: Assad or the opposition.

Characteristics of the study:
1. Duration: 48 hours (2 days)
2. Period: from 6:00 pm August 30 - 6:00 pm September 1
3. Number of messages: 1,745,549
4. Number of unique authors: 605 484
5. Number of countries: 241 countries (11,397 settlements)

The research was conducted in the main social networks: Facebook, Twitter, Vkontakte, Livejournal, Youtube, etc. The references were studied on the topic of the conflict in Syria and the preparation of the military operation of the Western countries against this country.

The most active discussion on the situation in Syria has unfolded in the US Internet space (46% of posts). In second place is the United Kingdom (14%). In Russia (the Russian-speaking traffic is about 1% of the world), the topic of Syria is 4% of the world total.


Western invasion of Syria: the attitude of users of social networks

The vast majority of users of social networks in all countries opposed the invasion of Western countries in Syria. The most pronounced rejection of intervention is in Russia (in 94% of communications), France (90%), Germany (88%), slightly lower in the UK (84%) and the USA (83%). In Turkey, criticism of the upcoming Western operation against Syria is contained in 64% of the messages.


Who is to blame for the situation: Assad or the opposition

In the US social media segment, users tend to blame the Assad government (64% of posts) for the current situation in Syria, rather than the rebels (36%), they also think in Germany (58% and 42%, respectively) and Turkey (70% vs. 30%). respectively). On the contrary, in the UK, the majority sees the guilt of the rebels (57%), and not of the regime (43%). In France, opinions were divided almost equally (51% vs. 49%, respectively), in Russia, the perpetrators are clearly considered to be the rebels (96% of the reports).


General brief analytics:

1. The most active situation with Syria was discussed in the US online space.
2. The overwhelming majority (over 85%) are against attack.
3. In the USA and Germany, the population is inclined to blame the Assad government for the current situation; in Russia and the UK, most consider the rebels guilty.

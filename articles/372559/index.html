Review OnePlus 3: the third generation of the iconic Chinese smartphone

OnePlus is the same company that two years ago, from the presentation of its first smartphone, made a real show with competing trolling and sales restriction using the system of invites. The competitive advantage of the model was the flagship iron at a low cost - about two times cheaper than the leading flagships of that year.



It turned out great, but it was not possible to repeat the triumphant appearance of the first model on the market: the following smartphones of the company, the flagship OnePlus 2 and the budget OnePlus X, were not so impressive, so their premiere went without much excitement. And now the time has come for the trump card: the new OnePlus 3 turned out to be the most productive smartphone at the moment, with a price of less than 30,000 rubles. An impressive comeback, OnePlus!

Appearance and ergonomics




Compared with the previous generation, the new OnePlus 3 has become 2.5 mm thinner and has acquired a convex 2.5 D glass, thanks to which it began to look and feel more compact and sleeker in the hand. The back surface is a bit slippery, but not critical: there were no problems with slipping from the hand during testing, sharp ribs contribute to a good ‚Äúgrip‚Äù of the device.

An interesting implementation was received by a fingerprint scanner: it is built into the ‚ÄúHome‚Äù button under the screen, but this button does not have a physical stroke, and you cannot press it - it is touch sensitive and requires a regular touch, which at first seems rather unusual.



Since the body is made of aluminum, it was not without plastic strips on the back and side of the smartphone - they are needed for receiving and transmitting the signal. But the camera prominently protrudes from the body.



The assembly of OnePlus 3 is very good, dense. There is no backlash, the case is monolithic, it is not pressed and does not deform. The power button is located on the right edge, the volume rocker - on the left. All other connectors are located on the bottom: there is a speaker grille, a USB Type-C connector and a mini-jack (3.5mm).





On the left edge there is a three-phase switch, with which you can choose one of three alert modes: silent, only priority notifications or all notifications. He has a clear move and an interesting metal texture, making it pleasant to use them from a tactile point of view.

The operating system, of course, the latest - Android 6.0. It is worth noting the excellent speed: Google Play is fast, applications are installed instantly - here also plays the role of high-speed flash memory. The volume of the drive, by the way, 64 GB. There is no slot for microSD-cards, but as practice shows, the mentioned volume is more than enough to install a lot of software, including heavy games. It is noticeable that the programs are installed and opened much faster than on other smartphones, including the flagship ones.

I would also like to note the truly flagship speed of response of the system, when at any user action (menu call, pressing a button, launching an application) the reaction occurs very quickly.

The Qualcomm proprietary development Adreno 530 was used as a graphics processor. Display


The OnePlus 3 display received a 5.5 ‚Ä≥ diagonal, while the developers did not chase the numbers and limited themselves to FullHD resolution, which is more than enough with such a diagonal - the pixel density exceeds 400 dpi, individual pixels are very difficult to discern.

The quality of the screen is not inferior to the flagships of other manufacturers - it has a very high brightness (it has to be muffled in the room, as it is too bright), almost perfect viewing angles (when tilting there is only a slight distortion of the color rendition towards the blue hues) and excellent clarity. The screen colors are saturated, but not excessive. In the settings it is possible to adjust the color balance and turn on the night mode, in which the display turns yellow - it calms the nervous system and causes less damage to the eyes when using the device in the dark. It is recommended to turn on this mode before bedtime in order to fall asleep faster.

Interface




OnePlus 3 is running the latest version of Android with Oxygen OS firmware version 3.2.2. It practically does not change the appearance of the interface, but adds many additional settings. The firmware, by the way, plays an important role in the speed of the smartphone - the smoothness of the interface largely depends on its optimization. In OnePlus 3, as we have already found out, this is more than order.



Fingerprint scanner


Pleased with the speed and fingerprint scanner. It works by a light touch, including when the screen is off, so unlocking the smartphone when using it takes literally a fraction of a second. Camera


The OnePlus 3 main camera module received a 16 megapixel resolution, an f / 2.0 aperture and optical stabilization. The photos are excellent, especially if you look at them on the screen of the smartphone itself. When viewed on a large display, however, it is noticeable that in some cases the images lack the dynamic range, as a result of which there are visible dips in the shadows and highlights in the sky. The rest of the OnePlus 3 perfectly copes with the tasks - it has amazingly fast focus, excellent macro photography and almost perfect color reproduction. In the photos of closely located objects you can see bokeh, similar to those obtained when shooting on SLR cameras. And in general, the quality of frames is not inferior to the camera, in this regard, you can always rely on the OnePlus 3 and take it easy on a trip or for a walk as a camera.

By the way, the application has a manual mode with the ability to save photos in RAW format, which brings OnePlus 3 closer to professional cameras.

Communication and navigation


OnePlus 3 works in LTE networks and is equipped with Bluetooth 4.2 and NFC modules. Wi-Fi is supported at the 802.11ac and IEEE 802.11ad standards. The smartphone also got a slot for two SIM cards, both nano formats. It is worth noting the excellent work of GPS - when the test was launched, the module instantly found the satellites and connected to them.



Autonomy


Battery OnePlus 3 such excitement, like the rest of the specifications, did not cause. As practice has shown, with a capacity of 3000 mAh, it is able to provide a little more than five hours of active work, which included games, Internet surfing, calls, correspondence in social networks and using the camera.


Using the battery in active mode - it was about five hours (from 16 to 21 hours).

The result also confirms the PC Mark autonomy test: the OnePlus 3 lasted 5 hours 12 minutes.


Finally, with continuous video playback in air mode and with maximum screen brightness, the OnePlus 3 was discharged in seven and a half hours. Bottom line: with moderate use, the smartphone will work until the evening, but it‚Äôs worth more to expect.

Smarterer - crowdsourcing professional testing

Testing is a demanded personnel assessment tool. For the selection of IT-specialists, professional testing is most often used. Many companies conduct it on their own or assign it to agencies that have a set of type tests for various roles and specializations, such as Brainbench . Recently, this niche has a new interesting project - Smarterer.com . This startup last June received a $ 1.25 million investment from True Ventures, Google Ventures and several business angels. The service officially started in October and is growing steadily .

Smarterer focuses on simplicity and speed of testing. It allows you to evaluate knowledge and skills in just a minute. At the same time, it cannot be called primitive. To determine the level of subjects, the Glyco rating is used - an improved version of the Elo rating widely used by chess players. The accuracy of the assessment increases with each answer to the test question, the system continuously adapts and improves.

The tests themselves are compiled using crowdsourcing. Anyone can create a new test or add a question to an existing one. New questions first appear in the sandbox and do not affect the result. After answering each question there is an opportunity to assess its quality and point out errors and inaccuracies. Only when a question is repeatedly checked, and the algorithm can confidently determine its complexity, does it begin to influence the rating.

As new questions are added to the test, the rating may decrease over time. At any time, you can answer a few more questions to confirm your level. In addition, the rating is affected by the addition of questions. If you come up with a complex and high-quality question - your rating will grow.

Thanks to this approach, Smarterer managed to achieve a very good level of questions in popular tests. The answer to each question gives the number of points, which depends on the complexity of the question and the level of the subject. The system selects the complexity of the questions in accordance with the result of the test. This ensures fast ‚Äúconvergence‚Äù of test results to a real level of knowledge.

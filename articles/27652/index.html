Wippies: Hippies going wireless

Quite by chance I came across an article in the newspaper about the Wippies project and it interested me. The idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe project in one line: ubiquitous free access to the Internet via Wi-Fi. This is implemented through shared access. The router creates 3 access points (wireless access point). The owner of the router gets access through the main password-protected AP (wep, wpa). And people who are nearby, such as guests, neighbors, or just passers-by, access the Internet via a second AP, which remains without a password. The third AP is for VoIP. Those. the point is very simple: letting people use the Internet and get access themselves when needed. But such a need often arises when you are away from home? And of course, all this makes sense only with widespread.

And now attention. Wippies is currently in testing and distributes routers absolutely free!

There is a minor disclaimer. If during the first year of use your router is turned off for two weeks, then you will be billed for the amount of the router cost - 30 or 80 euros, depending on the model. In cases of forced outages for a longer period, you can always humanly agree.

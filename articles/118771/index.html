Prehistory: furniture-time affairs master



Meet - David Abrahamovich Roentgen (1743-1807), a master furniture maker and owner of a furniture workshop in the small German town of Neuwied, played an important role in the development of Russian furniture business of the classical era. At the end of the 18th century, many German craftsmen, especially furniture makers, watchmakers, bronze craftsmen and lustrovshchiki moved to Russia, closer to St. Petersburg construction sites, where they had the opportunity to start their own business and promote it much faster and more successfully than in their homeland. X-ray first came to Russia in 1784, where he immediately enlisted the support of Catherine II and, over the next 7 years, actively supplied his refined and technically heaped furniture to the empress's court.

During his first visit, David Roentgen brought to St. Petersburg a batch of furniture in which there were writing desk-chairs, ‚Äúon which you can sit and stand,‚Äù toilet and drawing tables, as well as dressers, harpsichords, and spinning chairs (great-great-grandparents of our office hours) and the floor clock ... yes, that clock, about which, in fact, is the story.



The clock was equipped with a musical mechanism, including organ and cymbals, playing synchronously. The interchangeable musical drum recorded five melodies of the famous Austrian composer of the time, Christoph Gluck. Melodies can be played automatically, once per hour or three hours, and they are started / switched manually. The joy of the Empress knew no bounds; "The first furniture maker and mechanic of the century" - it was such an unspoken title that X-Ray received as a reward for his work. In addition to him, watchmaker Peter Kinzing, organ master Johann Weil and the bronze painter Francois Remon worked on the creation of a unique piece of interior. These are the largest watches in size that came out of the Roentgen ‚Äì Kinzing workshop and the most unusual in sound. To date, about ten copies of this watch model have been preserved in the world.

Digium G100 / G200 E1 / T1 / PRI Router



Digium - the company that gave us Asterisk, this year introduced a new family of VoIP devices.

The first products in this line are the G100 and G200 routers. Built on the basis of Asterisk and a modern built-in platform, the solutions will provide the best price / opportunity combination in any communication solutions.

These two VoIP routers support TDM-to-SIP, SIP-to-TDM and SIP-to-SIP (trans coding). Both are silent and have no moving parts, which makes them highly reliable.

They have a lightweight, low-power GUI and are easy to set up ... Right VoIP engineer dream.

Who are interested in the technical characteristics and review of the device, welcome under Habrakat


The G100 model includes one T1 / E1 / PRI interface (software selectable) and holds 30 simultaneous calls. G200 respectively two T1 / E1 / PRI interfaces and 60 calls. Both devices have an integrated echo cancellation module and a small size corresponding to 1U half-width / half-length.



G100 & G200 specifications are as follows:

  • Automatic call type detection: fax, voice, modem
  • Trunk groups support
  • Dialing Rules Support
  • Automatic addition and removal of numbers in dialed numbers
  • Support Caller-ID name and number
  • E1: PRI (EuroISDN or Q.SIG)
  • Built-in echo cancellation
  • Codecs:

So, we open ...



Indeed, no moving parts, one 1 GB DDR3 bracket, 110-240V power supply, Altera Cyclone III processor module, everything else is soldered on the motherboard.



Go to the device management interface. The first thing in Updates, the gateway sees the new software and kindly offers to upgrade. Conveniently.



SIP settings section (clickable image). The gateway can act as a server or client.
DTMF settings, codec selection, Caller-ID control and more.

Advanced SIP setting allows you to make more precise settings, NAT, TCP / UDP ports, RTP port range, SIP registration time, QOS, and more.

Backup configurations, the ability to remotely configure through the Digium technical support (raising the VPN), statistics collection, firewall - all this as a matter of course.

What are the uses for this gateway?


1. You can give a second chance to your old Panasonic or Avaya, who from birth did not know what SIP is.



2. Recently, quite often the IP-PBX goes into the cloud, where there is no possibility to insert a PCI or PCI-E board that we are used to.

Or, if the reception of the stream E1 and PBX are physically located far from each other. This gateway is just a must-have!



3. Finally, the last option. If you want to manage E1 flows, send a part of the DID to your old PBX, and the rest to the new Asterisk / Switchvox / etc server.

All this can be done with this product.

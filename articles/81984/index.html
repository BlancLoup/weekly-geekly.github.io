AMD introduced five new mid-range processors

Today, AMD has officially unveiled five new processors, which, first of all, will be focused on the use in systems of the average price level.

Among the presented processors the most interesting are the two processors made on the Phenom II chip.

The Phenom II X2 Black Edition is the most productive AMD dual-core processor, which for quite affordable $ 99 (wholesale price) pleases with a clock speed of 3.2GHz, a third-level cache volume of 6MB, a free multiplier and, of course, a good overclocking potential, as evidenced by the first AnandTech tests .

No less interesting and economical quad-core processor Phenom II X4 910e, with a clock frequency of 2.6GHz, and 2MB second-level cache memory and 6MB third-level cache memory has a TDP of only 65W.

Also, the Athlon II line has been replenished with three new processors, respectively, with the following models:

Athlon II X4 635 / 2.9GHz / L2-2MB / TDP 95W - $ 119;
Athlon II X3 440 / 3.0GHz / L2-1.5MB / TDP 95W - $ 84;
Athlon II X2 255 / 3.1GHz / L2-2MB / TDP 65W - $ 74;

There were no exact dates for the appearance of processors, it is only known that they should be on sale in the very near future.

At today's presentation, it also became known that the flagship Phenom II X4 975 Black Edition, which will have a free multiplier and a clock frequency of 3.6GHz, will soon appear on the market. In addition, it became known that, at the end of the second quarter, AMD is going to introduce three six-core processor Thuban - Phenom II X6 1075T, Phenom II X6 1055T, Phenom II X6 1035T.

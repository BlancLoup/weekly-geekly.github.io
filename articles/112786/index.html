As for the text output, the technology on which the screen is made is of great importance. In this case, it is SiPix.

Excerpt from the post on Habr√©:
E-Ink screens use two suspensions to form an image: black and white. Microcapsules of SiPix screens are filled with a suspension of only one color - white. Black points of the image are created by immersing a white suspension in a black liquid. As a result, the technology is simplified, and, as a result, it becomes cheaper. However, the result of this approach is that the reflectivity of SiPix screens is worse than that of E-Ink screens. As a result, the Sipix screens will appear slightly grayer.

Thermal cameras for smartphone Seek Thermal

A thermal imager is a device that allows you to "see" the temperature of various objects, displaying a color image in which different colors correspond to different temperatures.

The thermal imager will help detect and repair heat or cold leaks, see the local heating or overheating of equipment, notice problems with electrical wiring, and find prey while hunting - in the dark or in the bush.

Thermal imagers are quite expensive. For example, Testo 875-1 with a matrix of 160x120 pixels, which I used to measure the temperature of LED lamps, costs 139,000 rubles.

Recently, Seek Thermal thermal imagers connected to smartphones appeared on the market. Their matrix has a resolution of 206x156 pixels. Due to the fact that the smartphone is involved in all the processing and visualization, the price of such a thermal imager starts from 25,900 rubles.



The company Dadzhet invited me to test two models of thermal imagers - Seek Thermal and Seek Thermal XR.

Models differ only in the lens. In Seek Thermal, the viewing angle is 36 ¬∞ and the lens has a fixed focus, in Seek Thermal XR, the viewing angle is 20 ¬∞ and the focus is adjusted with a ring on the lens. Each of the models has two modifications - with a MicroUSB connector for Android smartphones and a Lightning connector for iPhone.



I tested the model for Android smartphones.



Seek Thermal can only work with Android smartphones that support USB-OTG. You can check support by connecting a microUSB USB flash drive to your smartphone or a regular USB flash drive, mouse or keyboard via a MicroUSB-USB OTG adapter.

To operate the imager, you need to install the Seek Thermal application from Google Play or the App Store.



The application is designed for the fact that the imager is connected to the connector on the bottom of the smartphone (the wide part of the MicroUSB connector should be facing the back of the smartphone). If the connector is located in a different place or turned over, you will have to use a MicroUSB extension cable, which you can also buy in Dadzhet. Accuracy of temperature display in thermal imagers is low. At a room temperature of 25.4 ¬∞, the Benetech GM300 pyrometer showed a wall temperature of 25.2 ¬∞. Seek Thermal showed 21 ¬∞, Seek Thermal XR - 33 ¬∞. Error - 5-8 degrees. Most precisely, thermal imagers measured the temperature of my forehead: Seek Thermal - 29 ¬∞, Seek Thermal XR - 31 ¬∞, pyrometer - 30.6 ¬∞. In the freezer, the pyrometer showed a temperature of -16 ¬∞, Seek Thermal: -11 ¬∞, Seek Thermal XR: -5 ¬∞. Water in a boiling kettle: Seek Thermal: 91 ¬∞, Seek Thermal XR: 94 ¬∞. Surely other specimens of thermal imagers discrepancies will be different.

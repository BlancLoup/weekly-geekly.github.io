Ampe A10 3G Quad - a tablet with a telephone heart


Using the Qualcomm 8625Q mobile chip in the tablet gives us several advantages at once: built-in 3G, GPS, Bluetooth, as well as long battery life. But how will this affect overall performance? In this we try to understand the example of the Ampe A10 3G Quad.

In fact, this is not the first attempt from Ampe to use a Qualcomm chip in the tablet. Before that, there was an Ampe A10 3G Dual core on a dual-core MSM8625 chip with 768MB of RAM. The results were mediocre in terms of performance, but excellent in terms of battery life. Let's see how the use of Qualcomm MSM8625Q 4-core chip will affect the usability of the device.

Specifications


10.1-inch IPS screen with a resolution of 1280x800
Video accelerator Adreno 203
Qualcomm MSM8625Q quad-core processor on the Cortex A5 architecture
1 GB of RAM
Android 4.1.2 operating system
4 GB user memory + microSD card slot
2 cameras: rear 7 megapixel and front 0.3 megapixel
Connectivity: Wi-Fi (802.11 b / g / n), Bluetooth, GPS, 3G
Optional: USB OTG
8600 mAh battery
Dimensions, weight: 266 x 177 x 10.3mm, 640 grams.

If you do not pay attention to the big screen and the battery, then you might think that this is the characteristics of a mobile phone.

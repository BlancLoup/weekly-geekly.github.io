Russia has become the absolute leader in the eSports market in Europe

SuperData Research and PayPal conducted a joint study of the European eSports market. The European market for eSports as a whole is estimated at $ 300 million by SuperData Research, and the number of ‚ÄúeSports players‚Äù reaches 23 million.

Experts of SuperData Research analyzed the transactions and the results of consumer surveys.
PayPal is engaged in e-sports research and sponsors the 2016 Grand Final of the Wargaming.net League, the largest e-sports competition in the World of Tanks game. SuperData Research specializes in analyzing the market for computer games and e-sports.
According to the forecasts of researchers, by 2018 the market will grow to $ 389 million, and the audience - to 44.4 million. The Russian eSports market is the largest both in terms of volume and number of cyber sportsmen - these figures reach $ 35.4 million and 2 million people, respectively.

In second place is France, where the market volume is $ 22.5 million, in third place is Spain.

SuperData Research investigated data for Hungary, Greece, Italy, Spain, Poland, Russia, France and the Czech Republic. According to PayPal and SuperData, most of the eSports fans are male (79% of men versus 21% of women). The most common age groups are from 18 to 24 years old (30%) and from 25 to 34 years old (30%). In Russia, a group of 18 to 24 years old has a larger share than the European average, 47%, according to Kommersant.

The eSports industry attracts new advertisers from companies that previously used more traditional promotion channels, primarily TV. According to SuperData Research, in 2015, the income of the eSports industry from sponsored advertising will exceed $ 570 million, which is only a third less than the similar income of NBA - the largest recipient of advertising budgets in sports.

The authors of the study noted the growth of the mobile games audience in the eSports segment. By the end of last year, the share of mobile games in this market in the United States exceeded 20%. The most profitable gaming platforms in 2015 were (in descending order): Dota 2, League of Legends and Counter-Strike: Global Offensive.

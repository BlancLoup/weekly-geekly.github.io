We pronounce correctly

Of the IT people I know, very few try to pronounce English words correctly. Of course, it’s more common to say C ++ as “C-plus-plus”, not “C-flat-flat” or “open-be-es-de”, but not “open-bi-es-di”.
But when “echo $ value;” is read as “echo valui” - this is no longer funny. Another person may simply not understand you, especially a foreigner.

The topic has a small list of "difficult" words that are often pronounced incorrectly.
It’s better to focus not on my (very approximate) transcription, but on audio.

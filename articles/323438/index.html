How to choose Email Encryption Software

Small businesses can directly trust their employees to decide which emails they send should be encrypted. In this situation, almost any local desktop or cloud solution will work quite well.

Larger companies usually do not include the end user in the process of deciding on the encryption of a letter, using pre-configured filters based on role-based policies.

In this case, emails are encrypted after they leave the employee‚Äôs desktop, at the point through which they pass. This can be a mail server, a gateway, a special hardware device, or a web portal.

Sometimes the encryption method is chosen taking into account the rules and restrictions that impose corporate IT integration and compatibility requirements. This applies, for example, to Outlook plug-ins, email sent from mobile devices, some mail protocols and archiving methods.

The value and experience that is available to the recipient. Therefore, enterprises are often looking for a secure email solution that provides user-friendly options for sharing secure mail.

Modern email encryption software (Email Encryption Software, EES) is usually compared according to the following criteria.

1. Security level . All mail software somehow encrypts e-mails. However, better email services offer a wider range of security options ‚Äî at least, end-user-initiated encryption, and corporate policy-based encryption.

2. Recipient experience . The best for the user is software that allows him to send and receive encrypted emails with his personal passwords, without the administrator participating in this process, who manages user requests.

3. Controls . Competitive EES should have advanced, yet user-friendly administration tools. Better software also provides simple or even automatic deployment options and pre-configured policies that comply with common legal restrictions.

4. Integration and compatibility . To be really useful and effective, mail encryption software must work in conjunction with popular business software, such as Salesforce, GroupWise, etc. The best are EES compatible with most popular platforms and business applications used.

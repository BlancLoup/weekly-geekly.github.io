Java & PostgreSQL - meeting with Alvaro Hernandez, St. Petersburg

On Wednesday, July 6, at 20:00, a meeting of JUG.ru with Alvaro Hernandez , PostgreSQL expert will be held at Oracle‚Äôs St. Petersburg office. The subject of the meeting is the features of Java interaction with PostgreSQL.

The meeting language is English . Participation is free. Number of places is limited, registration is required by reference .



About the report

Java is the language for Enterprise development, you can say - Language with a capital letter, and with chances - the most popular language for working with PostgreSQL. Have you wondered if this bundle, Java with PostgreSQL, is well suited to its task, does it justify the assigned expectations? This report is a technical report on the level of development of PostgreSQL and Java, which will answer the following key questions:

  • Can Java take advantage of 100% of the performance provided by PostgreSQL? If not, where are the losses? And what can we do with them? Does PostgreSQL use any technical or architectural patterns that might limit Java performance?
  • Can we get from Java to completely the full functionality that PostgreSQL provides? And if not, what has been hidden from us, and can we somehow cope with this in practice?


About speaker



Alvaro Hernandez is a 37-year-old entrepreneur in the field of information technology living in Madrid (Spain). He founded the company 8Kdata and is its technical director. The company 8Kdata is engaged in the development and research in the field of databases. Most of the time, Alvaro has been developing the ToroDB project - a PostgreSQL based DBMS that is compatible with MongoDB and can replace it in existing applications. Being an enthusiastic developer, he is also engaged in the dissemination and popularization of Open Source ideas.

Alvaro not only programs in Java and is a member of JavaSpecialists.eu , but also is an excellent DBA , teacher, and often speaks at international conferences. In addition, he founded the PostgreSQL Spanish User Group - one of the largest communities of this topic in the world, consisting of more than 550 participants.

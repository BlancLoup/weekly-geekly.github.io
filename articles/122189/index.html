The site of the governor of the Sverdlovsk region is available at Creative Commons Attribution

The content of the site of the governor of the Sverdlovsk region Alexander Misharin yesterday, June 18, 2011 became available under the free license Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 Unported . This is indicated on the page ‚Äú Terms of Use of Site Materials ‚Äù, the link to which replaced the text ‚Äú2009-2011 Administration of the Governor of the Sverdlovsk Region‚Äù (which, in principle, could not be done, the ‚Äú‚Äù sign does not contradict free licenses). As noted in the news site a day earlier, a free license allows you to use information in Wikipedia and the blogosphere.

In this news , published the day before, it was first announced that the site is switching to a free license, but it was not at all indicated which license. It looked very ugly. Now the governor has finally decided on the license and corrected.

UPD: Probably around June 20, the license was changed to a looser one - Creative Commons Attribution 3.0 Unported

So, we see that Creative Commons continues the triumphant march around the country .

On October 3, 2008, the website of the President of Russia (Attribution 3.0) passed to the free license, on May 17, 2010 - the official information portal of the state authorities of the Republic of Bashkortostan (also Attribution 3.0), and on February 28, 2011 - the Internet portal of the Volgograd Region Administration (only Attribution 3.0, and Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0). However, the state does not only distribute its information under a free license, but also uses such information. So, on December 21, 2010, the Roscosmos geoportal was opened, which uses the OpenStreetMap map, which is available under the license Attribution-ShareAlike 2.0.

Board games, which will have to break his head



As many of you know, the world of board games is very diverse and actively developing. You can find games for almost any taste and temperament, from unpretentious games for companies at parties to hardcore monsters in which games last for hours, from unpretentious card entertainment to wargames that require a lot of money to build armies from miniatures and learn rulebooks on hundreds of pages.

In our company, many people are interested in nastolki and even regularly get together and spend evenings after the parties. Therefore, this Friday we decided to take a break from the hardcore code (but not from hardcore) and made a selection of board games for you, in which you will have to break your head a lot to gain the upper hand over your rivals. Each game we assigned a level of difficulty. It is subjective and relative, it is possible that some games will seem more difficult or easy to you.

Immediately make a reservation - in this collection will not be wargames and collectible card games (CCI): these genres usually require large expenditures on the search and purchase of figures and cards in order to assemble their army or deck. We will consider only those games in which it suffices to buy one box.

Forbidden Stars

This game is not so big, but also belongs to the category of "heavyweights". A strategic game on a space theme, but it is made in the universe of Warhammer 40,000, the most popular desktop wargame.



Here, players take turns laying tiles, each time creating a unique playing field, and fighting with each other for control of star systems. As in Twilight Empires, in Forbidden Stars, you will also lead space armada into battle, trying to fulfill the goals you are facing (each player has his own). In this case, you will be disturbed by a warp storm migrating on the map, which breaks the links between the star systems and breaks the plans of the naval commanders.

Each turn, the players take turns closing the order cards to the troops, and then play the resulting sequence of orders, of which there are 4 types:

  • mobilization - an increase in the army and the construction of buildings on the planets,
  • planning - getting new cards from the deck,
  • administration - collecting resources from the planets,
  • relocation - movement of fleets on the playing field.

The combat system is also based on dice.

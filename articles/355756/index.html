The OAC of Belarus has divided the domain of the country's second most popular site

This afternoon, the second most popular Onliner.by website has become unavailable. As reported on the website of the Operational Analytical Center under the President of the Republic of Belarus, the domain name of the resource was divided due to violation of the law on trade. The initiator of the decision was the Ministry of Commerce of the country.

In connection with the violations of trade legislation by the owner of the Internet resource onliner.by identified by the Ministry of Trade of the Republic of Belarus, including regulating the procedure for the sale of goods (works, services) using the Internet, and the failure to take measures to eliminate them at an earlier time, December 20 In 2014, a decision was made to exclude the corresponding domain name from the registry of the national domain zone.


BelTA reports that, in addition to Online, twelve online stores were closed. Most likely, this measure was taken against sites that did not want or could not, in the period established by the Ministry of Trade, transfer price tags from conventional units to Belarusian rubles. This is exactly what the Ministry of Trade warned the owners of online stores on December 19.

The deterioration of the economic situation in Russia and Ukraine led to problems in the economy of Belarus. Yesterday, the National Bank of the country announced the introduction of a 30% fee for the purchase of foreign currency, thereby essentially conducting another devaluation of the ruble. Obviously, the state authorities of Belarus expect that if all online stores indicate prices in Belarusian rubles, this will lead to greater confidence in the national currency.

Small introduction
At many venues where projects are required to be presented, they like the presentation to start with a consumer problem that the project would solve. This is an indicator question that allows you to determine whether the project creators understand what they are doing and why. If the problem is obvious and well-known - the presentation immediately arouses interest and inspires confidence. Well, if the problem does not lie on the surface? Then the jury believes that they face another bunch of dreamers, who first invent problems and then solve them. The mistrust that appeared at this stage cannot be redeemed, no matter how much you climbed out of the skin in the future.

How to prove that the problem really exists, and it exists exactly in the form in which the authors declare? To do this, you have to rise to a higher level. Our solution.
The basic requirements for the solution are ready, the audience is clear and the solution becomes obvious.
We want to offer the user a system that will inspire him to develop, guide on a proven and reliable path and maintain a high level of his motivation.

How to do it? Achiever.ru
We have come to the most interesting - to the specific solution of understandable problems.

We are preparing to launch an online resource. The main process occurring on the site can be reflected in the following scheme:
1) The user chooses a specific goal in one of 4 sections of the site. The timer starts, and the user must reach the goal before the time runs out. On its page are published the goals set and achieved.
2) The user automatically joins the group with people who have set the same goal (whether they are fans of parachuting, jogging in the mornings or environmentalists). There he can receive information from more experienced participants and be inspired by others' successes.
3) The user gets acquainted with the guide created by us, which describes in detail what, how and when to do (I can send an example guide to anyone who wishes or post it if there is interest).
4) The user does what is described in the guide.
5) The user lays out the confirmation that he has achieved the result. For this, he can use the application we have prepared or another method available to him.
6) The confirmation is checked by the administration; it rejects or accepts confirmation, after which the user is given an achievement. Achievement is reflected on his wall, the user gets points on which he can compete with other users in the results.

Using different mechanisms, we want to spark competition among users. They will compete with each other in obtaining real achievements, and at the same time, grow above themselves.

We hope the idea is clear. Before the beta testing remains about two months, during this period we expect to debug the functionality and get ready to meet the first users on our resource.

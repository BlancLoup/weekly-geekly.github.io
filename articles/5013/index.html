The PDF format will become an ISO standard.

Adobe, which gave many of its inventions the status of a de facto industry standard in IT, decided to assign one of them the formal right to be called an international standard.

In a press release released by Adobe on Monday, the company submitted the full specifications of its PDF file version 1.7 to AIIM , a specialized office, which will prepare them for review by the International Organization for Standardization .

The ISO working group will subsequently make its decision: whether the popular PDF is worthy of becoming an open international standard for electronic document flow. However, no one doubts that ISO will satisfy Adobe in the shortest possible time, because in many countries PDF has long won certain niches for itself. For example, the PDF / H version (PDF for Healthcare) of the same AIIM is recognized as the best for use in medical records.

The wishes of the Californian sotfernogo giant is easy to understand. At present, only one ODF format has been approved by an authoritative commission and, by its attractiveness to various government agencies, businesses, and ordinary private users in any country of the world, it can give many points ahead to its competitors.

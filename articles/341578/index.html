Announcement of Moscow Python Meetup # 50


Hello! November 23 at the Attic Rambler & Co will host the 50th anniversary meeting of the MoscowPython community. At the meeting we will hear 3 reports.

1. How we scrum - Aleksey Lobzov (Alfa-Bank)
Why the need to scale Scrum? How did the LeSS framework help us? What problems have we encountered and how have we overcome them?

2. Pipfile, pipenv, pip ... what ?! - Ivan Chernov (Ostrovok.ru, Python Developer)
Python has all of us our favorite pip package manager, but progress does not stand still and the community has made a new convenient pipenv tool. Let's look at it and answer. Is it time to switch to it?

3. Practical Hypothesis - Alexander Shorin (Senior Python, Rambler & Co development engineer)
At MoscowPython 33, I already outlined the state of property testing in Python and briefly about Hypothesis. I propose to continue, but in substance. In this report, I will show you with examples how to effectively apply Hypothesis, find unobvious bugs and improve the quality of your tests.

The event is free, and registration is required - moscowdjango.timepad.ru/event/606633
With us pizza and tea!

Beginning at 19.00
Location: Warsaw highway, 9, p. 1, entrance number 5. Attic Rambler & Co



Be sure to register and take your passport with you so that the security guard of the business center will miss you!

Come, it will be interesting!

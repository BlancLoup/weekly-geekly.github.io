We listen to music and lectures from Youtube with a locked smartphone screen and without advertising using Telegram

The ability to listen to audio from Youtube with a locked smartphone screen is a useful feature, for example, if you are listening to music or lectures. This is convenient, eliminates accidental tapping on the screen, besides - the locked, inactive screen of the smartphone consumes less energy.

Unfortunately, the official Youtube application (at least without a paid subscription) does not have such a function. I found the solution by chance and helped me in this Telegram. The idea itself is not new , but with Telegram this is the first decision. In addition, it does not display (and, accordingly, does not sound) advertising.

A video demo is available here . I apologize in advance for the phrase "listen to the video", "image video".

But can I use the same algorithm for a video about the algorithm itself or a joke about recursion.
Of course, even this video can be heard with a locked screen, but for this you need to know how, and for this you need to watch this video, which is also possible ...

Algorithm:

  • Copy the link to the Youtube video, and send it to the Telegram channel, for example, to yourself. (UPD: even faster - you can share the link directly to Telegram from the Youtube application, without clicking on Copy / Paste)
  • We open the Telegram video by clicking not on the link (this action will open the Youtube application or an external browser), but on the video image.
  • After starting playback, the video will no longer include ads. Two buttons appear under the video - the right one copies the link to the video to the clipboard, the left one - opens the video in a floating window. Push the left button.
  • Everything, you can lock the screen and listen to a lecture or music. Without advertising .

Minuses:

  • You can not adjust the playback speed.
  • You can not quickly stop playing. For example, if you listened to a lecture with headphones, when you disconnect the headphones, the lecture will continue to play (from the speaker of the smartphone) .

I hope this article will put the attention of specialists in information security, Android, Telegram, Youtube API and they will help you figure out how and why this is possible.

CUBE Talk 9X - Review of a 9.7-inch tablet with 3G / GPS

The fact that Chinese manufacturers are actively copying A-brand devices has long been a surprise to no one. To the maximum from Apple, a part of Samsung, but we take this chip from ASUS and spice it all up with Google's operating system. But while one part of people is outraged by such actions, each time expressing their opinion in the comments, the other one is exploiting pleasant devices in terms of price / quality ratio. Today we look at the tablet from the already proven Chinese brand Cube.



The tablet is a very controversial thing, and I still adhere to the thought that this is more a nice addition to my ecosystem of gadgets than an essential item. ONDA V972 is my previous device, which I also wrote about here, in general, and in general, I was happy with everything (except for the absence of Bluetooth and the general system‚Äôs slow pace), but the review did not suit the local community, primarily the lack of 3G and GPS. The hero of our today's review, Cube Talk 9X (U65GT), is equipped with a modification of the mobile chip Mediatek MT6592, which in turn indicates the presence of all the necessary communication capabilities.

‚ñå PACKING AND COMPLETE SET


Cube U65GT Talk 9X comes in a box of thick unpainted cardboard, there is no information about the tablet itself, besides the model and amount of user memory on the side face.

Despite the minimum configuration, everything you need is in the kit. In the main box, only the tablet itself, a clip to remove the tray under the SIM card and a set of instruction cards (including the English version). In an additional white box, which was quite battered during transportation, there is a USB cable, a power supply unit (5 V, 2 A) and an adapter for it.

There are problems with the charger. With mine, everything is in order, just sometimes a bundle comes with a low-power PSU with 0.5 A "output" - so you will charge your tablet for 20 hours, if the conditions are perfect and the battery capacity actually corresponds to the declared 10 Ah.

Taking into account the support of OTG technology, I would like to see the corresponding cable in the bundle, since it costs quite a trifle, but the manufacturers have long refused to buy their earphones, for which they are thankful.

‚ñå DESIGN, DIMENSIONS, CONTROL ELEMENTS


Apple iPad is evolving, and Chinese manufacturers continue to imitate one of the best devices in this market. Is it bad or good? - Decide for yourself, but I will say that CUBE is on a very, very good level in quality. The 9.7-inch display is covered with glass; here, on the front panel, there is a camera and an ambient light sensor (it works smoothly, without ‚Äúbrakes‚Äù).

The back cover is made of aluminum painted black with a barely noticeable bluish tint. In the center is the text in Chinese, which, let's face it, does not decorate the tablet, and below is information about the device in English.

In the upper left corner of the camera module with LED backlight, to the right is a plastic insert covering the antenna. On the right side are located the plastic power and volume buttons with a noticeable tactile response.

There is also a tray for Micro-SIM cards, the right side is empty. The thickness of the tablet is only 7.4 millimeters, which is even less than that of the notorious ‚Äúdaddy‚Äù.

At the top there is a slot for a Micro-SD card (cards up to 32 GB are officially supported) and a 3.5-mm audio jack. By the way, the chamfer on the body is not painted (anodized).

Bottom - Micro USB connector and stereo speakers. The quality of the latter is rather mediocre, but the volume margin is sufficient in most cases.

The aluminum case, well-fitted parts, high-quality assembly, this is not the first year that Chinese manufacturers have been experiencing, and this, I must say, definitely pleases.

‚ñå DISPLAY


The same can be said about displays. The first Chinese tablets with Retina-like (resolution 2048x1536 and 4: 3 aspect ratio) displays appeared 2 years ago and since then they have not left the niches of devices of a similar form factor. The screen is juicy, bright, but what can I say - most people at least once, and over the past 2 years they have held an iPad or another tablet with such a display.

The film pasted on the plant, in addition to being tactilely unpleasant, also spoils the picture causing a crystallization effect, and therefore was immediately removed. Among the shortcomings, it is worth noting the overestimated level of minimum brightness - when reading or watching a video in the pitch darkness, I would like to underestimate the level of illumination by 10 percent.

Behavior in the sun, despite the lack of OGS , is tolerable, because even in bright sunlight I was able to parse the information on the cards or respond to the message. Neither anti-reflective nor oleophobic coating here, unfortunately, and does not "smell".

‚ñå CAMERA


The resolution of the main camera module is 8 MP, and the front one is 2 MP. While talking about the design, I forgot to mention that the camera module protrudes slightly beyond the case. Often this solution leads to a defect in the protective glass of the camera, but here the developers have crafted and drowned the glass with the camera in a protruding module. In simple words, if the tablet touches a flat surface, the contact will occur with an aluminum edging, and not with glass protecting the camera.

Examples of images can be found below. As for the tablet, the quality of the camera is at a good level, autofocus is working properly. Speed, of course, could be higher, but I doubt that you are going to shoot sports events through this unit.

For the rest, we still have the same 8-core processor and graphics core Mali-450 MP4 with a clock frequency of 700 MHz. Also, the tablet is equipped with 2 GB of RAM and 16/32 GB of user memory.

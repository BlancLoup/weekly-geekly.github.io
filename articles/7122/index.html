Corporate information leaks through Google Calendar

Computer World reports that important corporate information is made public by Google Calendar, because some users choose incorrect access rights settings (they make the calendar public instead of making it private).

Last year, Google made it possible to change the access rights to the calendar to share interesting events. But a few quick queries allow you to find one undocumented "chip" of this opportunity - to find important corporate information.

With the help of corporate calendars that are shared, you can get information such as the dates and times of staff meetings, the names of projects being developed.

Google representatives do not give any comments on this issue, but in fairness it should be noted that there is no fault on their part - in such cases, the unfortunate employees of these companies are to blame.

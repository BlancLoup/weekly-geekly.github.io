One day with Highscreen Boost 2 SE

The phone went on sale, as was mentioned earlier , and I decided to check how comfortable it was, how long the battery would last and whether I should take such a phone after the recent betrayal of my smartphone (the display stopped responding, but healed overnight).

To do this, I installed several applications that I use daily, poured music, a book and spent the whole day with this phone. And benchmarks and other details you can find on the link above.



Specifications


Operating system : Android 4.3
Processor: 4-core Qualcomm Snapdragon 400 MSM8228, 1400 MHz
Video Processor: Adreno 305
RAM: 2 GB
Built-in memory: 8 GB
MicroSD: up to 32 GB
Number of SIM-cards: 2 microSIM
Power supply: 6000 mAh and 3000 mAh replaceable batteries
Display: 5 inches, 1280x720
External camera: 13 megapixel
Front camera: 2 megapixel
Navigation: GPS / GLONASS
Weight: with batteries 3000 mA / h - 151g, with a battery of 6000 mA / h - 203g

First impressions


On the packaging and packaging still write. The box is made from recycled cardboard. Included are headphones, USB-wire and charging. Pleases the presence of two batteries, each of which has its own back cover.



Like most modern phones on Android and iOS, the headphones are absolutely non-usable for me. This does not surprise anyone and not upset already, I think.



Build quality - decent, no complaints. The case is plastic, unfortunately, there are prints on the back cover made of polycarbonate. It was unusual after a leather flip, but I got used literally in the first couple of hours. While playing with the phone, I caught myself thinking that I could use it myself. But mine is not dead yet, so I'll wait - all of a sudden there will be more internal memory in the next Highscreen models.

Under the cover there are places for two SIM cards and microSD.



I changed the battery to the one with more - 6000 mAh. It increases the thickness of the phone, and instead of a five-inch tile, we get a good old brick. Exactly the same brick I already wear - this is the HTC One X in a cover-flip: the dimensions are the same, so for me this is the usual size.



The back cover of a high-capacity battery has one drawback: it squeaks! With a smaller battery is not observed. The dimensions of the phone with a small battery is unusually thin for me.

Display


Sharp 5 "display produces bright juicy colors, the movie on it is pleasant to watch. Resolution - 1280x720. There are no complaints to it at all.



Autonomous work


The claimed operating time without recharging: 3000 mAh - 5 days, 6000 mAh - 7 days. I did not charge the phone: the charge indicator after installing a large battery, it showed 63% of the charge, and a day later - 40%. 23% for one and a half days with intensive use - an excellent result. Especially if you are used to charging the phone every day both at work and at home.

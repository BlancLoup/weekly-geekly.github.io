We are trying to open a business. Part 2


Nachalo began ...


Everything starts from something. It all started with a glass of Pepsi cognac, on a rainy Friday evening. My friend and I shared with each other the events of the past week. They talked about work, life and money. Smoothly and surely, we came to the conclusion that living for 20-25 thousand in Sochi is already impossible, especially to me (I have two little girls and a wife). We knew that opening a business in a city where ‚Äúmutual understanding‚Äù reigns will not be easy. We also understood that simply opening a grocery store (a stall, a computer store, an advertising agency, a real estate agency, etc.), is not that soft. Since all this is already a dime a dozen. For several days we frantically went over ideas.

I think this is one of the most important moments. You need to find an interesting idea, check whether there are analogues, whether they will compete with you, whether it will be possible to start with this. We found some interesting ideas, developed their implementation, but set them aside for later, as to realize them a solid investment of money is required at the start. But unfortunately, as in most cases, the money at the start is either very little or not at all. We found an interesting idea for our city - products from Japan. We checked whether there are similar stores - found only two. Both are at the other end of the city. We went into each.

Shop number 1 or hi 90s


The store is hard enough to find. Bad location. Commercial area 2.5 m2. Gray, poorly lit room. Everything is crammed with goods, from pastel linen, to Japanese beer, car pads and the like ... There is no cash register. Although it is not necessary for SP, I think it is good to have a cash register - the customer receives a check as confirmation that he bought the goods from you. I admit, I would not buy anything in such a store.

Shop number 2 and pricing


The shop is located in the city center, on a busy street. The room is bright, 3.5-4 m2, but it looks large enough, due to the fact that the product is categorized, beautifully arranged and lit. Caused positive emotions, but prices were biting. In this and all the salt and salt, this comes under the brand name "RENT".

About the pricing of their capabilities


So we thought about what we have at the moment.
  • Premise (A friend has a premise on the territory of a private house that can be converted into a commercial premise with small investments. Excellent location, next to the famous pizzeria).
  • Minimum free time. (Weekends at lunch and after 9 pm, Weekdays after 9 pm.) Conclusion: it is necessary to do during the main work ...
  • Lack of money to start. We are ready to allocate about 5,000 per month in order to prepare everything for the start.


We both understand what we are trying to get involved in, that it will not be easy, especially in the financial part, but it will be an adventure that will give good experience, which may be useful in the future, because we still have our whole life ahead, and we want to LIVE, and not to exist for 20 000 rubles per month ...

In the selection of the legal form of the enterprise


This was the first moment where our views diverged. I "fought" for the LLC, and my friend - for Yip (I want to say that he was willing to tear it off both for himself and for me, according to him, without a word).
My reasons are:
  • The founders receive dividends as a percentage of the money invested in the share capital. That is, we would be guaranteed to receive 50/50, thereby securing each other. Yes, we are friends, but we have heard a lot of stories ... Anything can happen, I would like to legalize the right to profit of each of us.
  • As practice shows, LLC has more confidence than IP.
  • Responsibility only by share capital (minimum 10 000 rub.)


I would also like to say about the minuses that we see in the LLC:
  • Money (dividends) we get every quarter.
  • Financial statements
  • Fines are disproportionately high compared with the individual entrepreneurs (Although we decided to do everything according to the rules, no one is protected from this).
  • Higher taxes compared to individual entrepreneurs, at least on the simplified tax system (additional 9% personal income tax from dividends) Thank you, ColorPrint


Now let's talk about IP.
This is what we would attribute to the pluses:
  • Easy registration
  • PI exempt from accounting
  • Profit you get immediately and immediately you can put it in the "business"


Minuses:
  • The main disadvantage - the PI is responsible for its property.
  • Ban on the sale of alcohol. (Goodbye sake (:)
  • PI is obliged to pay fixed contributions to the pension fund
  • A significant minus for us is the distribution of profits among the founders. (Maybe someone knows how to legally, on paper, to fix the distribution of profits. I would be glad if someone tells)

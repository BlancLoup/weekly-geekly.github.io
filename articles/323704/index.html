Conference proceedings


UX STRAT 2016
In 2016, the UXSTRAT 2016 conference held two parts: June 4-5 in Amsterdam and September 14-16 in Providence, USA . It is dedicated to the design strategy and gathers a powerful composition of thematic speakers. Presentations published on SlideShare . I will highlight the most interesting of them.
  • Master class by James Kalbach "Mapping Experiences" .
  • Ben Judy on Intuit's UX-strategy on three levels - product, company and its mission . A very interesting example of the organization of the workflow around the Double Diamond on slides 14 and 15 - they combined this model with a kanban board and a project plan.
  • An interesting ROI design model from Jon Ashley and Matt Wakeman from EPAM . They consider it in three sections - perception, finance and operations. For each of them are given the metrics of how designers can help the company.
  • Shikha Desai from the Microsoft Office team talks about design jams that allow the product to find new directions for growth . This is a combination of several practices of teamwork (hackathons, experiments, interns, concepts), which ultimately allow you to bring new ideas into the product plan.
  • Michel Jansen's cool case on how the services of the Dutch financial company Aegon improved . A good example of using the Kano model in practice.
  • ING's Kees Moens tells how the design has changed in the company . Good practical implementation of the UX maturity model.
  • HaPro from GoPro shows how user research and experimentation helped in working on a simple video editor .
  • Michael Thompson shows a useful example of a customer journey map with key metrics .
  • Jeffrey Onken proposes to consider the stages of the UX maturity model as an ordinary customer journey map . In this form, it is much easier to detect and correct problems that prevent the creation of a good design.
  • Beverly May talks about the mechanics of the UX Awards . An interesting set of criteria that can be useful, for example, in the analysis of test items.

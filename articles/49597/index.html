Korean Pocket Dictionaries

Remember, a couple of years ago pocket dictionary-translators were popular, like these:


Think they died? Nothing like this.
Picking around in one Korean shop in search of the most pleasant offer on the player I want, I came across a huge number of offers for the sale of "Electronic Dictionary".
My first devaysina caught my eye was simply struck by its characteristics. Meet Inkel MU-D in person.

Inside neonka 4 GB of flash memory, arm processor and Win CE 5.0!

More detailed features:
  • Screen: 4.3 "TFT 480x272, rotatable (!) Touchscreen
  • Operating system: Windows CE 5.0
  • RAM Memory: SDRAM 128MB
  • Flash Memory: 4GB Flash
  • Expansion Slot: SDHC
  • Broadcast Support: DMB
  • Broadcast Support: DAB
  • Audio format support: mp3, ogg, ac3, wma
  • Video format support: DivX, XviD, WMV, MPG
  • Battery: 1800 mAh
  • Dimensions: 148 x 21x 95mm
  • Weight: 297g

And the most delicious - the price tag. Less than $ 150 (carefully, following the link is a thick image of several megabytes). Delivery to Russia will cost about 25 evergreen pieces of paper.
Another couple of pictures:











The only serious drawback that stops me from buying is no, not a Korean interface, this is easily fixable. Lack of network interfaces. And, as I understood from the descriptions, the lack of USB-Host function. Yes, alas, ah :(

But one more piece of iron was found - Istation Udic Standart. Such here:

The main features are similar to those of Inkel MU-D, but from pleasant bonuses there is support for swf, a hard disk of 30 (or 60) gigabytes, a more capacious battery (4500mach against 1800) and (attention!) WiFi!
The price tag, however, is not so attractive - 225 green raccoons .

Of course, the disadvantages in the form of lack of support for the Cyrillic alphabet (and possibly the English interface), guarantees and other unpleasant trivia are likely to outweigh, and I will not buy such a toy for myself.

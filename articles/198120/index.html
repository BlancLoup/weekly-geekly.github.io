1. Price


In general, in a market economy, where homo homini lupus est (or if to paraphrase this famous Latinism - wow, wow, wow 3 ), the sacred right of the manufacturer to sell his work for any amount he likes. Not fair, not adequate, not fair - any. Because all the arguments such as the standard of living, wages, taxes, etc. can be discarded as insolvent. The seller has the right to charge any amount for his goods.

On the other hand, the consumer has the right not to pay this amount. So that they do not start giving me an example of a monopoly: the book is not a commodity, you can live without it. 14% of the world's population cannot read at all, and nothing, they live and reproduce.

It is obvious that the participants want a few different things and try to move the price in different directions: one to zero, the other to plus-infinity. When, as a result, the amount that the buyer is willing to pay is equal to the amount for which the seller is ready to sell, everyone begins to live, if not happily, then more or less peacefully. If the meeting does not occur - the seller simply ceases to release the goods, and the goods disappear from the market, often together with the seller. From this part of the buyers, whom the high price suited, grieve, but there is nothing to be done.

With the advent of numbers in our daily lives, the price of copying began to tend to zero. Producers of information content have lost the main protection of their investments in the means of production. Obviously, they have two ways: to leave the market or to meet the buyer.

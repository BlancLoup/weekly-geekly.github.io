CCNA- what kind of tsna?



I will quote Cisco site:

The CCNA certification validates the ability to install, configure, operate, and troubleshoot medium-size routed and switched networks. CCNA certified professionals have the knowledge and skills to make connections to remote sites via a WAN, mitigate basic security threats, and understand the requirements for wireless network access. CCNA training covers (but is not limited to) the use of these protocols: IP, Enhanced Interior Gateway Routing Protocol (EIGRP), Serial Line Interface Protocol Frame Relay, Routing Information Protocol Version 2 (RIPv2), VLANs, Ethernet, and access control lists (ACLs).

What can be said in essence:
Having this certificate, you confirm that you can configure a switch or router, understand how networks work, understand some routing protocols: RIP, OSPF, EIGRP, know how to configure VLAN, work with connections of various kinds, you can configure access control lists, DHCP, NAT and some more things.
In some organizations you will not be hired without this certificate (they will not let you work on a cool project or will not be promoted).

To pass the exam, you must choose two ways:

Pass 1 exam:

How to prepare



  • Preparation on the official courses of Cisco ICDN
  • Cisco CCNA Academy Training

Preparation on the official courses of Cisco ICDN



As it should be, Cisco has its authorized exam preparation courses.
For example, their cost and list can be read here.

On each part (Entry and ICDN) you will be given a set of manuals in Russian Study Guides:
Part 1. Volume 1
Part 1. Volume 2
Part 1. Laboratory

Part 2. Volume 1
Part 2. Volume 2
Part 2. Laboratory

As I was told, on the exam there can only be what was included in these manuals.
In addition, your teacher can give you your laboratory options, such as I had.
At the end of the course you will be given a piece of paper, which must be shown to the authorities and immediately thrown out. It has no value.
The advantage of the courses is that you work with real equipment on them, you can touch or break . The teacher will answer all your questions. And most importantly, he will share his experience and tell a lot of superfluous (useful). In theory, you can then immediately pass the exam. But I recommend everyone to take the time. The average preparation time is from 6 months (taking into account work, study and free time).

Cisco CCNA Academy Training



The Academy offers two courses:
CCNA Exploration 4 (Advanced) and CCNA Discovery 4 (Entry Level)
I waited an advanced course for half a year until I was opened at the university for free.

The Academy consists of 4 semesters, which very fully describe and tell about Cisco and network technologies using examples and laboratory work.
The course itself is a flash presentation with tests and interactive content.

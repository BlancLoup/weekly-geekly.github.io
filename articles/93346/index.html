Receive feedback from potential users


It‚Äôs not so easy to get feedback and opinions from a potential client if you are a small startup. In the case of a large company, it seems to me that the situation is simpler. You can spend money on marketing research. You can communicate with already having customers who acquire, although other, but similar objects or services. In the case of a new startup, there is no client base, there is no money either. And the task arises with maximum efficiency, to try to communicate with those who could potentially become a client and adjust their development if the demand is in some ways at odds with the offer. In other words, identify what needs to be corrected and proposed in order to attract more interest.



I apologize to the readers who are already aware of what OOO ‚ÄúProgram Verification LLC‚Äù is doing and ask them to skip the next paragraph. But I have to repeat the description for those who do not know about our startup.

The Program Verification Company LLC is developing a specialized PVS-Studio package for identifying modern varieties of errors in C / C ++ programs. Namely - 64-bit and parallel errors. Detection of defects is based on the method of static analysis of the source code of applications and issuing warnings for those parts of the code where the likelihood of errors is high. I think you will agree that the product is not massive and as a result, the number of reviews about it is not great. And therefore it is extremely important to collect these reviews as much as possible, as it is easy to miss something important.

Well, it‚Äôs impossible to communicate with those who can only potentially be interested in PVS-Studio. Perhaps they really are not interested. We took a break, but recently we have a new idea, which we implemented in PVS-Studio 3.53. This is our new experiment and whether it will give us more communication is still unknown. Now, when studying PVS-Studio, a user can request a consultation from us, according to the diagnostic messages that he sees. And accordingly, when answering a question, an interesting discussion can start, and we can learn the interests of the potential user.

The mechanism works as follows. After the analysis, you can get up on the most interesting message, and select the menu item "What is it?". As a result, the text of the letter will be formed, which is proposed to send us an email and get a hint for this error. A small piece of code is inserted into the text in which a dangerous construction is found. Of course, information for the answer may not be enough, but the main thing is that the dialogue will start.

3CX brings together Elastix and PBX in a Flash communities

Not so long ago, two popular manufacturers of Asterisk assemblies announced right away that the next versions of their systems will work on the 3CX core! We welcome and are proud of the fact that the large Elastix and PBX in a Flash communities have joined the dynamic 3CX community!


Both manufacturers have for some time considered replacing the system core. The previous version of PBX in a Flash worked on the Asterisk and FreePBX bundle and was subject to licensing restrictions . A similar situation exists with the 4 version of the Elastix system .


After the release of version 3CX for Linux, it turned out to be expedient to combine the efforts of communities and developers to produce truly first-class products. Ready-made assemblies for the deployment of PBX on ‚Äúbare iron‚Äù received a new technological core.


Both assemblies have already been very popular on the market - for example, only Elastix has over 100,000 registered installations and tens of thousands of daily downloads of the free version. Therefore, we will try to take into account the wishes of existing users. For example, already now a convenient migration mechanism is available for Elastix users - the Elastix configuration converter to 3CX . Features that are now available to users of new versions of Elastix 5 and PBX in a Flash 5:


  • Free version available as a Debian 8 (Jessie) Linux ISO image
  • Free support for FQDN of the server name 3CX on Google DNS with DynDNS function and free trusted SSL certificates Let's Encrypt
  • Integrated video conferencing system on WebRTC technology
  • Automatic configuration of IP phones of popular manufacturers - both in the local network and for remote connections
  • Automatic configuration of many SIP operators
  • Official support for SIP gateways Patton, Beronet and Welltech
  • Branded clients for Android and iOS with PUSH support for call notifications
  • Branded clients for Windows and Mac (for Windows - with a call center dashboard)
  • Functions of unified communications - chat, statuses, fax and e-mail, voice mail, etc.
  • Built-in backup and resiliency
  • Embedded SIP Intrusion Prevention System

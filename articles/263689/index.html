Joker 2015: News July 16-27, 2015 - new speakers and reports

Since the announcement of Joker 2015, almost two weeks have passed in the habr. In this post I will briefly talk about what's new with us during this time. And we will talk, as usual, about the content - the new announced speakers and their reports.




NEW SPEAKERS AND THEIR TOPICS



Dmitry Zhemerov is a veteran of JetBrains, a man who had a hand in IntelliJ IDEA, RubyMine, PyCharm and at one time headed the procedure for transforming IDEA from a simple IDE into a platform for writing IDE for different languages. JetBrains managed to work as a developer, then CTO, then went to Google for a year. A few months ago, he returned to JetBrains, where he headed the Kotlin language support team in the IDE. At the conference, Dmitry will tell you which parts of the IntelliJ IDEA platform are currently being rewritten to Kotlin, why they were chosen, what problems the developers are having at this very rewriting and what profit Kotlin receives from this rewriting project.

By the way, literally from day to day we will publish a large (hourly!) Video interview with Dmitry. There will be about JetBrains, about IDEA, about Kotlin and about Google - so wait for the interesting!






Yevgeny Borisov is one of the best Russian-speaking speakers, always in the top at any Java-conference at which he speaks. Master of Spring and Design Patterns, a professional Java trainer, to the heap is also a notable KVNschik.

This time Zhenya will tell an extended version of the Spring Puzzlers report. This report includes a lot of very intricate and funny puzzles about Spring taken from real life. The knowledge gained will help students learn the subtleties of Spring-a and protect themselves from all sorts of mystical bugs. This report, unlike all other puzzlers, is very practical. A lot of live code taken from real projects will be written and parsed.

Alexey Zinoviev is a specialist in Java and in large columns for which he is currently writing a Ph.D. thesis.

Edward Snowden: an informant who gave secrets to the NSA



A few hours ago, The Guardian newspaper reported the name of the person who submitted to the editorial office the secret documents of the US National Security Agency, including the presentation of the PRISM project and a request for information on all Verizon cellular subscribers. The largest leak in the history of the NSA was organized by a 29-year-old computer scientist, information security specialist Edward Snowden (Edward Snowden). Now he himself asked to reveal his identity - and explains the motives of this act.

Edward Snowden is not afraid of revenge from the US government, and for him the disclosure of information was a matter of principle.

Snowden is a former technical assistant at the CIA, and now a military contractor at Booz Allen Hamilton. For the past four years, Edward has worked for the NSA as a representative of various external contractors, including Booz Allen Hamilton and Dell.

He says that from the very beginning he did not try to hide his identity, since he was initially sure that he was right. Snowden sincerely believes that society should be aware of secret programs that the National Security Agency and other US intelligence agencies are dealing with. Before being sent to the press, he carefully studied each document and made sure that publicity would not harm any of the officers and intelligence agents. He tried to disclose only technical information.

Synthesis-2 - domestic clone ZX-Spectrum

While cleaning the village on a recent weekend, I found in the attic deposits a proud creation of Soviet electronics engineers - ‚ÄúGame Computer‚Äú Synthesis 2 ‚Äù.

Some theory


A series of computers "Sintez" (yes, there were several of them, but more on that later) - nothing more than another clone of computers ZX Spectrum based on a Z80A chip with a clock frequency of 3.5 MHz . Produced, depending on the version, with 128 or, as it was in our case, 48 KB of RAM and 16KB of ROM . Supported a color image in RGB format, displaying 8 colors with a resolution of 256x192. It was bundled, in addition to the operating instructions and a small reference book on the built-in Basic, with a power supply unit that converted 220V AC to 5V 1A DC, a set of cables for connecting to a tape recorder and a TV and depending on the configuration, joysticks.

Preparing for launch


But, nevertheless, let us return to the main goal - to start the computer.
We turn on the power supply in the outlet, connect the power supply to the computer. The LED on the front panel lights up. Power is there - gives hope that the PC is working. However, to make sure you probably need to connect it at least to the TV. The wires from the five-pin (for the tape recorder) and seven-pin (for the TV) plugs are practically nowhere to be inserted, so a page was found in Google, where the diagrams of the wiring of the video cable to SCART and the audio cable to two regular 3.5 Jacks were shown.

Immediately make a reservation - when I soldered everything according to the instructions from there, nothing worked: the image was white stripes along the screen, and there were unpleasant wheezing from the TV speakers. Without losing hope, we are looking for the original computer layout, looking at which shows that the pin assignments of the TV jacks and the tape recorder are different from those listed on the site. Well, apparently we have another modification.

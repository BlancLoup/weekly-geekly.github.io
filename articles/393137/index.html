Google patents user face recognition for analyzing the response to search results


Fragment from the patent description

Google has registered an application for a patent on the use of biometric data to assess the quality of search results. In particular, the company plans to analyze even the facial expression of the user who received the answer to his search query.

The main goal of the search engine, repeatedly emphasized by the creators - the best way to respond to the user's request. For this, the technology of the search engine is constantly being improved, and the quality of the results in response to a search query is monitored.

Apparently, in order not to torment the user with questions about his job satisfaction with the search engine, Google decided to create a system that assesses the user's physical condition as one of the indicators of success.

It is planned to measure body temperature, pulse, reaction of pupils, eye movements and blink rate, changes in complexion, and even the facial expression. All these reactions can indicate the degree of satisfaction with the system.

All results will be transmitted to the cloud and processed there, so that the next time you deliver the user even more fun.

As is usually the case with the ideas of Internet giants, the joy of caring for the user is slightly overshadowed by the feeling that we are increasingly being watched.

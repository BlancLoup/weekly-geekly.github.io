ESA satellites have discovered a "jet stream" of liquid metal in the core of the Earth


Arrows indicate jets of liquid metal. The diagram also shows the orbits of the three satellites of the Swarm system, thanks to which we managed to detect this phenomenon.

The European Space Agency has published information that an unusual jet stream has been found in the depths of our planet, which lies deep beneath the surface. With the help of satellites, ESA discovered a stream up to 420 kilometers wide at a depth of about 3,000 kilometers. This stream circulates at a speed of 40-45 kilometers per year.

The ESA Swarm satellites , which helped to make the discovery, are designed to monitor the Earth‚Äôs magnetic field. The Swarm mission consists of three satellites located in different polar orbits in height from 460 to 530 km. The mass of each satellite is 472 kilograms. Changes in the magnetic field of our planet have been recorded by satellites since 2013.

Long-term observations helped to find out that the field strength gradually decreases, and in different regions in different ways. On average, over 20 years, the decline was about 1.7%. But in some regions, tensions immediately fell by 10%. There are regions where the indicator, on the contrary, has increased.

Urban Jungle Street View: Plants Beat the Cities



Sometimes, among startups, projects designed to improve and increase human productivity, increase the efficiency of use of funds by certain organizations and other practical resources, there are just for fun projects.

It should be said that such projects sometimes become a kind of outlet in daily work for both creators and visitors. This is exactly the project Urban Jungle Street View , a service where images of plants and stones are superimposed on photos of cities in Google Street View. The result is something like post-apocalyptic cities, as some artists portray them.

In addition to Google Streetview, the panoramic viewer from Jaume Sanchez Elias is used here (the source for the GSVPanoDepth viewer can be found on Github ).

According to the map of post-apocalyptic settlements, it is worth traveling - sometimes there are some very interesting locations. True, you need a WebGL to view.

But the video with a demonstration of what can be seen on some streets, using the service.

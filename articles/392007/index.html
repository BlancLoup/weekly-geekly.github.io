Video: traffic lights will not be needed on the future roads

Scientists from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology have calculated that modern roads will be able to withstand twice as much traffic if traffic lights are removed. This is what we will come to when (and if) on the roads of the common user all cars will be robotized and connected to V2V systems.



Already today, sensors are being installed in cars that maintain the distance between them and the vehicle ahead. In the future, instead of stopping at traffic lights, cars will be able to communicate with each other in order to safely turn and cross over intersections. So existing roads will be able to provide a greater flow of cars and other vehicles - including bicycles .

The perfect Android player for non-music

When I tried to find a player in which it would be convenient for me to listen to podcasts, audiobooks and similar things, I tried at least a dozen two or three applications both from the top of the music category and from the last pages of search results for various crazy combinations of keywords.

At that time, I did not consider special podcast players, as I wanted a universal solution for all audio content. Later I looked at several specialized applications, but in no one did I like the organization of the download process. Personally, it is more convenient for me to download everything from the desktop and throw it on the phone through the cord, which means there are no problems in order to listen to everything through one program.

So here. All tested players were divided into three categories:
1. The most extensive. Players who believe that everyone listens to music exclusively in albums. Provide no opportunity to play directories. A catalog with 100-200 songs of various artists turns the catalog of music of such programs into a tape that goes to infinity, where nothing can be found. The playback position, as a rule, is not saved, but everything is very beautiful.
2. Players that allow listening to catalogs and / or listening-oriented audiobooks. If in the previous category all applications differed only in icons, and the general ideology of the interface was about the same, then there are no similarities here, but there are a lot of unique ideas, sometimes to the point of delusion. In some applications, books must be imported, otherwise they will not see them. Some of them show and sort files only by tags, but we know very well what kind of mess there is in audiobook tags, right? In general, in each program, something did not suit me, and in some places frankly enraged. In addition, every second of these programs (as a rule, the most original ones in terms of the interface) suffered from a severe decline.
3. Music Folder Player Free



This application has solved almost all my problems.

What I like about him:
  • The ability to play music through catalogs, not by albums. I know, I know, I am in the minority here, but personally it is much more convenient for me.
  • Memorization of the current file and position in it in each directory. That is, you can throw the book, go listen to the podcast, then throw the podcast, go listen to the music, then close everything altogether, and then continue any of the three from the same place where you left off. And all this with a sufficiently small interval is stored in a separate file in each directory and even if the phone hangs, the position is not lost - the restoration of the position occurs with a bounce back seconds to 5-10, and it is saved more often. You won't believe it, but most audiobook players during the test for abnormal program termination during playback lost not only the position, but also the current file, and the book and everything.

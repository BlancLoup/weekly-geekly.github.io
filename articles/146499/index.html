How to grow an IT manager in high school?

In preparation for the 2012 Summer Analytical Festival , this article was born, which raises the following questions:

  • Who are IT managers?
  • In which universities can they train?
  • What should they read in high school?
  • How to prepare in high school employee for your company?

Both before startups, and before companies that have long and successfully been running a business, there is a dilemma: to look for a ready-made specialist or to grow it within one‚Äôs own walls. And we, in 1997, starting our own software business , also almost immediately felt a shortage of software vendors, consultants and project managers. The alternative proposed by us, on the one hand, requires some effort and time at the initial stage, and on the other hand, it allows you to get a qualified specialist.


The main question : Is it possible to grow an intelligent IT manager in high school?

The most frequent answer is : No, especially considering the current level of higher education and the frantic market demand for qualified IT staff.

A survey conducted by the department of APU St. Petersburg State Electrotechnical University in 2010 among 30 St. Petersburg IT companies showed that they mostly prefer to grow managers on their own, mostly from programmers and other technical specialists. The IT manager is an interdisciplinary specialty, so the cultivation process will be long, laborious and extremely expensive for companies. Unfortunately, no one sees any alternatives yet. The level of training of specialists in Russian universities leaves much to be desired. Old educational programs, faculty members, who give the theory of twenty years ago, and the lack of practice add up to an extreme amount in the Russian market of sales managers for solutions and complex technical systems, system and business analysts, as well as other information technology managers.

Runet is replete with articles of headhunting "experts" about how to obtain and retain qualified professionals. The councils are reduced to enticing prospective employees from colleagues, paying wages above average on the market, constant motivation with interesting and non-routine tasks. But personally, I do not understand how such wonderful options will help to increase the number of qualified specialists in the market.

Our answer : Business needs to establish interaction with technical universities.

Seven Steps to Growing IT Crystals

So, we analyzed our own experience and the experience of St. Petersburg IT companies, which also successfully grow managers in universities. We offer you the following steps that must be taken if you want to join our slim ranks.

Step 1. Business Presentation
In advance in July-August, a pool of companies interested in new employees is beaten up. In September, meetings with bachelors of the fourth year and masters of the first year of study are held. Usually these are two meetings: the first is held at the university, the second at the company's office. Managers of the company talk about the main activities, interesting projects, open vacancies and opportunities for professional growth in the company. Preliminary lists are lined up (who goes to internship where), consultations with teachers on applicants are held (performance, discipline, communication skills, etc.)

Step 2. Conclusion of a contract with the university (department)
The formal side of the question. Since the department or faculty are not separate legal entities, a partnership agreement is signed with the university. On the basis of this agreement, the issue of free student work is closed.

Step 3. Admission of students to practice
Preparation of the workplace, the selection of the curator-mentor, reporting.

Step 4. Student recruitment
At this point, many questions are asked: ‚ÄúWhen to arrange an intern for a bid? After all, a student may not show the result even in half a year or a year! ‚Äù That's right, when he starts to show the result, then start paying. This is the value and fairness of this scheme. You do not pay until the specialist has started generating added value.

Step 5. ‚ÄúThank‚Äù University
The participation of the university in this scheme is not disinterested, otherwise, such a partnership will not last for more than a year. Options: teacher remuneration, participation in the educational process, logistics, etc.

Step 6. Improving the skills of existing employees of the company
While you are waiting for the autumn harvest of interns, you can improve the qualifications of existing staff by sending them to advanced training and retraining courses. And to order courses arranged specifically for your business tasks, we practice this in high school.

Step 7. Marketing and PR
In modern conditions of the labor market, it is not companies that choose employees, but vice versa, therefore participation in educational programs increases the rating of companies among applicants. Accordingly, such PR and HR-branding is useful for companies, and especially for universities.

Cons of this approach:
  • A waste of time by qualified staff in teaching and mentoring;
  • Delayed results of cultivation specialist;
  • The need for business to negotiate with the heads of universities, to encourage them to cooperate.


Advantages of this approach:
  • A large amount of necessary theory and practice is given still on the student's bench;
  • Savings on salary: taking on the practice of a master in an agreement with the university, the company is not obliged to pay salaries;
  • Participation in curriculum development;
  • It is possible to choose a candidate for a long-term contract;
  • Teacher recommendations;

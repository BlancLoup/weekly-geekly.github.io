The staff of the Solar Dynamics Observatory (SDO) made a pumpkin resembling flares on the sun . Indeed, if you look closely, then our star has a very frightening look. No one knows what is worse - the pumpkin or the real Sun.


Sun (left) and pumpkin (right) from NASA's Solar Dynamics Observatory staff

Some NASA employees approached the competition even more creatively. For example, astronomers from the Ames Research Center at Moffett Field have prepared a real scientific paper on pumpkin stars. So called a special type of rapidly rotating stars, which emit x-rays 100 times higher than the peak levels of the sun. The oblate shape of such stars is explained by their ultra-fast rotation around its axis. It is assumed that these stars were formed as a result of the fusion of binary systems.


The scientific article is accompanied by the characteristics of 18 rare stars of this type, collected as a result of observation with the help of the Kepler and Swift telescopes. A scientific article about pumpkin stars was published in the Astrophysical Journal . Scientists have tried to guess for Halloween. The article was published online on October 25, 2016, and in a paper issue of the magazine will be released as it should be - on November 1. The most extreme of the described stars is the orange giant of class K KSw 71, which is 10 times larger than the Sun, makes a full revolution in 5.5 days and generates X-rays 4,000 times more powerful than the Sun.

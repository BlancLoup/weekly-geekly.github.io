LinkMeUp. Release 16, Pentestit and PHDays IV

In our podcast, we could not help but highlight an event such as Positive Hack Days, held in Moscow at the end of May. On behalf of Pentestit, Alexander Sinister , a guest of the 8th edition, spoke on it.

Pentestit is a young company, even a very young one, which has almost no analogues in Russia or even abroad. Roman Romanov, director of Pentestit, was a guest of the podcast six months ago in the 8th edition with Alexander and told about his laboratories. This time Maxim Mayorovsky , head of Pentestit's laboratories development department, continues the story of the company's development and how they prepared the PHD lab.

Alexander will tell you about the Positive Hack Days forum as a whole, as well as the two reports affecting the communications network.
One of them, with which he actually spoke, is dedicated to Intercepter-NG - a powerful tool that allows you to listen to traffic and organize MITM attacks in automatic mode. There is one mysterious story connected with this application, which Alexander told on PHD and us in the podcast. Video presentation can be viewed at the link .
The second report on a rather burning topic is attacks on networks of mobile operators through the SS7 protocol. Such threats have been studied and verified in practice by the specialists of Positive Technologies - Sergey Puzankov and Dmitry Kurbatov.
An attacker, once in the technological network, can do terrible things, ranging from redirecting SMS to listening to a call from any end of the planet.

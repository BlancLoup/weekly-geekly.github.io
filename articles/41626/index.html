Ratings in the service of the web

Colleagues, I decided to share news about Moscow meetings, and also ask for advice.
So, October 5th, Sunday. The 3rd meeting of startups will take place. The second meeting came in two more people than the first and the growth trend promises to continue. There are also more and more start-up managers wishing to present their projects, and the time, as we know, is not rubber. From the beginning, it was decided to make a brief, filling in which the start-up sends it to the organizers. Those, in turn, choose the best-prepared of the applicants and invite them to make presentations at the meeting, receiving the necessary criticism, ideas and investor attention.
You can guess what happened next. Of course there were those who did not agree with the decision not to skip someone's startup to the discussion.
What to do in this case?

Aib (meeting participant) suggested the following cool idea. Because The event consists of 2 parts - ElevatorPitch (talk about a startup in 1 minute) and StartupLynch (a startup startup answers questions from the audience, hears criticism or praise), invite everyone to participate in ElevatorPitch, and those who listen to at Lynch will be identified by an anonymous vote on pieces of paper.
Those. for example, 10 startups participate in the first part, the audience votes and only the five who get the most votes go into the ‚Äúsecond round‚Äù.
My personal opinion that such ratings will not only filter the public for the most interesting, but also serve as an incentive for the speakers themselves, will add excitement to the event. Let's draw an analogy with Habr, where only the topics approved by the habrasoobschestvom get into the main. And Habr is not the only example. Ratings are the strongest tool for working with the audience, its emotional involvement in the process.
It is very interesting to know your opinion on this matter. Is it worth it or not to rate those who want to speak? How can the process be improved?

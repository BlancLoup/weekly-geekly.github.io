About stressful interviews

Some time ago on Habr√© an article appeared The most terrible interview in my life , in which I was told about the concept of stressful interview. I wanted to collect more information about him for full coverage of the issue.

A stressful interview is an interview in which a candidate is attempted to be off-balance, put in a situation when he feels uncomfortable or simply does something that the applicant is not ready for.

An experiment was conducted a few years ago - to people who thought they were being interviewed for a top management position, a recruiter suddenly splashed out a glass of water in his face. This experiment was so famous that now the reception "a glass of water in the face" is mentioned as one of the first when talking about stressful interviews.

The reactions were varied - someone wiped himself and continued the conversation, someone left in shock, others gave the change - in return they threw something at the recruiter. One man showed excellent response by dodging (then left the room). The insecurity of the experiment manifested itself in the fact that one girl, while the water was still in flight, managed to snatch a chair from under her and try to hit the insulted person, in this case the recruiter had already shown agility - if he had not had time to dodge, he would not do without medicine. (unfortunately, I could not find the video program in which this experiment was shown)

It is believed that a stressful interview is useful when interviewing a person who in the future in the process of work will have to deal with stress. In my opinion, this is not so - this method, as can be seen from the experiment, removes the mask from a person and shows his real qualities, which allows one to more accurately judge whether he is suitable for a certain position or not. For example, a manager needs aggressiveness, and a public relations officer needs slyness and tolerance.

Now such interviewing methods (not so tough, of course) are used in the IT field. For example, I know a person who faced elements of pressure during an interview for the position of Analyst.

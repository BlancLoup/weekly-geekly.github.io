After publishing photos of Prince William at the Royal Air Force base, the military had to change passwords.

During the press coverage of one day from the life of Prince William, who is currently serving in the Royal British Air Force as a rescue helicopter pilot, information was published online for internal use, including the login and password to enter one of the Air Force service systems, which were printed on a piece of paper hanging in the office.



Four out of ten published photographs contained sensitive information on the documents and computer monitors that were in the frame. The press service of the Air Force edited these photos and asked all the media that published the original version to replace them with a safe version as soon as possible. Nevertheless, the photos have already been distributed online, so that the military, just in case, changed all the passwords that could get into the frame.

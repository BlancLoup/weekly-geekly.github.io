How and why to keep fit, if you are an IT person at a distance

Large IT companies are promoting sports among their employees: they set up bicycle parking, equip halls inside the office and even collect group classes during working hours. On the remote, the organization of sports activities by the employer is not available. But if a new remote worker starts to think about the arrangement of the workplace almost before switching to this format, sport in the minds of many remains an optional option - somewhere at the level of the usual ‚Äúoffice package‚Äù from coffee machines, cookies and English classes. And this is a key mistake.

On the personnel portals, HRs of ‚Äúoffice‚Äù companies constantly talk about the positive effects of group physical exertion on the team. This is the prestige of the company, and team building, and a component of the social package to attract personnel in one bottle. But sports activities in the office pursue not only corporate goals. For knowledge workers, regular exercise helps maintain performance over long periods of time and is less likely to hurt.

Doctors complain that with the development of the transport system, communications, and in general, with the increase in the standard of living, we move less and eat more, so gradually, mankind on average is gaining excess weight. But when planning work from home, no one even thinks about how much movement a person actually has who regularly gets to work at least an hour one way. Judge for yourself: you have to pack up, walk to a public transport stop, drive through (most often standing, straining different muscles periodically, along our roads, so as not to fall on other passengers). Motorists are spared from this training, but they should, for example, dig up a car in the winter season. Inside the office, too, it will not work all day to not get up from the chair. Pour coffee - already going to the kitchen or to the coffee maker. Yes, and the heels of flights of flights in a day you somehow overcome - especially if you smoke, and there are no premises for this in the business center.
Homes of all this is not. On the one hand, this, of course, is a big plus - there is no inappropriate expenditure of time on the road and meaningless office procedures. On the other hand, many are faced with the problem of physical inactivity. With full remote work (full time) it is at least 8 hours a day at the computer in a certain position. Pose is a separate topic, today we will not touch it. But even if it is perfect, the amount of movement is minimized. For lunch you can not go anywhere - the kitchen is close by, and even products today in large cities can be ordered with delivery, i.e. the chances of not leaving the apartment for several days are quite real.
Such a drastic reduction in physical activity, of course, saves time. But it is not for long. Today it can be used for interesting tasks, but if nothing is done, tomorrow this ‚Äúreserve‚Äù will have to be spent on visits to doctors.

Typical problems


  • Excess weight. In the absence of movement (while maintaining food intake), the muscles are simply replaced by fatty tissue. Here you have the extra weight with the attendant problems - ask the doctors, they will draw you a very depressing perspective. By the way, not eating at all here either will not help - the brain needs energy, and the rest of the body without nutrients will fail.
  • Amyotrophy. It seems that you were 20 yesterday and did not threaten atrophy? But on the remote is a very real prospect, even if you have never experienced any specific health problems. Such a diagnosis was received by one of our employees after about a year and a half of work without control of physical activity (or rather, in conditions where physical activity was limited to transitions between the bed, the kitchen and the workplace).
  • Diseases of the cardiovascular system. Venous stasis in the legs can lead to thrombosis, and this is not the only problem.
  • Back pain. Sitting in a chair negatively affects the spine. The first guests of a person who is not trying to help his body are osteochondrosis and curvature of the spine. And there and close to the headaches (and try to focus on work, if unbearably headache).
  • Mood and sleep. Lack of physical activity is depressing. And sleep after the day at the monitor is much more difficult.


The saddest thing is that if you do not catch these problems at an early stage, the body will become worse and worse. And so, by the age of 30 it is already possible to get a whole bunch of unpleasant diseases (we recommend contacting doctors for detailed diagnoses).

Rights holders complain about Telegram-bot "Flibusta" in Apple and Google

Complaint filed with Telegram administration, but no reaction

In mid-March, the online library Flibusta, blocked in the Russian Federation, launched a Telegram bot to download electronic books. In order to get the book, you need to send the bot either the title of the work (part of it), or the author. In response, the bot shows the options that are available (and some of the books that are accessible through the bot are not available on the site itself). The list of works is indicated with the options of e-book formats (including epub, fb2 and mobi). As soon as the user selects the desired format, the bot sends the book in this format. After that, it remains only to download the file and open it with the reader.

A few days later the right holders in the face of AZAPI noticed what was happening. The team of "experts" checked the functionality of the bot, and finding out that everything works, as stated, the right holders began to act. Namely - turned to Telegram with the requirement to block the bot . The representatives of AZAPI associated their actions with the recommendations of Roskomnadzor, which were previously reported on Geektimes. Thus, experts from AZAPI tried to find out the source of pirated content by analyzing the data packets received when downloading the book through a bot.

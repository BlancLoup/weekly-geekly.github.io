As you know, you should not neglect safety. (If someone does not know: the digital signature of the file protects it from unauthorized changes. The certificate certifies that the author of the signed file is you and no one else.)
In the modern world it is almost impossible to meet the program from a large vendor without a digital signature. More and more of my friends are beginning to pay attention to the presence of the CPU in the program being launched or installed, due to the fact that Windows displays this information (see CDPV).

For certificates, the following is claimed (translated only essential items, see the full version on the website):

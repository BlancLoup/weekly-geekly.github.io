How to better understand people

Telling IT people about psychology is still a matter, some readers will say: ‚ÄúBullshit!‚Äù Will not be believed at all, because psychology, even applied science, cannot be called exact science. However, the goal of this article is to show and prove to you that some models really work. At the heart of the report is Sergei Kotyrev from UMI on RHS ++ 2017, from his face further and go the story.



I am an IT entrepreneur with 20 years of experience. It so happened that from the very beginning of my career I had to manage people. As a graduate of a technical university and IT specialist, I initially understood that people are difficult to algorithmize, and in general, to realize, understand and predict.

Later, I came to the conclusion that people are generally the most difficult thing to work with. Now I think that people in general are probably the most difficult thing in the Universe.

It seems to me that we know more about the behavior and prediction of the behavior of spiral galaxies than about how a person behaves, for example, my wife, an employee, or especially an employee of my marketing department, not to mention PR managers. The fact that the galaxy nearest us flies, and in some billion years will collide with ours, we already know for sure.


Why is psychology not an exact science?


Imagine a certain three-dimensional object, in this case a cylinder. And any psychologist thinks in a kind of two-dimensional space, within his model. Imagine that this three-dimensional object is a person, his psyche. One psychologist within his model will say that he sees the circle in this person, and the other - the square. They will argue themselves hoarse because they use different psychological approaches.

Let's imagine that the human psyche is not really a 3-D, but an N-dimensional object. It is so multifaceted that we can imagine a bunch of projections, each of which will be unlike the other figure. The more projections we collect, the more ideas we get about the real form of the original object. All existing psychological schools and theories are nothing but separate projections.

Today I will talk about those who helped me in work and life.

To begin with, I want to say that from the very beginning I was very suspicious of psychology, like any IT specialist, even with some disdain, until I came across several theories that surprisingly did work and continue to work.

What got accustomed to me and works well


From the variety of theories and schools, so to speak, reflections of reality, I singled out 5 that work for me:

  • 5 Chapman's love languages;
  • MBTI and NLP by Greenfeld;
  • Process Communication (PCM);
  • Herth Hofstede's theory;
  • Psychology Afanasyev

I think that these theories will be useful to those who, according to duty, have to manage people: unique, diverse, complex, sometimes unpredictable, sometimes, in our opinion, dull and even stupid.

"5 languages ‚Äã‚Äãof love" Gary Chapman


The simplest book, which is read in a couple of hours.

American family psychologist Gary Chapman initially raised this topic to analyze the causes of divorce. In his opinion, we express our love in 5 different languages . And this is not about sexual love, more precisely, not only about it, but about love in general: towards people, including employees, ideas, companies, values, etc.

Probably, you guess that employees should feel that you love them. This is a pretty effective thing for management. According to Chapman‚Äôs theory, each of us has 5 love languages, one of which is developed, and for the rest we don‚Äôt perceive love and are not able to show it.

Service


- Dear, do you want me to bring you some coffee?

To offer coffee is the most primitive example of ministry. The most difficult thing is to serve your family, your company, your team ‚Äî not to sleep at night, think about them, look for where to get money for wages, etc.

By the way, this is my language of love. I understand love only when they serve me, and I show love myself, only by serving - not only to other people, but ideas, mission, values, team, company, etc.

Touch


Many people enjoy physical touch. This is for them the main language of love: kisses, hugs, a simple pat on the shoulder.

I hate it when they touch me. And my wife, on the contrary, loves touch. Therefore, she tells me that I do not love her because I stroke her a little ‚Äî this is not close to me. But I understand that she needs it, because in this language she understands that I am pretty. No, with sex, everything is fine with us, but stroking just like that, I see no point.

Presents


There are people who show their love only through gifts, and understand that they love him only when they give him something. And they love to give themselves.

Well spent time


This is the time when you give a person your attention, and not just, for example, sit together on a sofa and watch TV.

I will give an example. One of our programmer was sour and wanted to quit. We went with him to a cafe and for 3 hours we talked about nothing - and about everything. After that, he flourished, stayed to work further, and worked well. This is not my language of love, I do it a little through effort over myself. After reading this book, I realized what an employee love language was and showed in him that he was dear to me (and the company), although he was not at all my direct subordinate.

Words of approval / support


For me, this is a completely foreign language. I hardly force myself to support and praise someone - you are a professional, and you will deal with your own problems. I myself also do not need any words of support and approval - I will deal with my problems myself. But there are people to whom it is very important.

After reading this book, my friend Dmitry Agarunov, in his Gameland publishing house, wrote his language of love on the badges of the employees. You just come up and see right away: - Aha, the ministry ... Can I bring some coffee?

MBTI according to Greenfeld


This is a school based on MBTI and NLP ( www.grinfeld.ru ), and Mikhail Solomonovich Greenfeld is one of my teachers. He does not write books, but conducts trainings on VIP negotiations ‚Äî very expensive and long, but this is pure magic and shamanism. Greenfeld is based on the MBTI (the classic typology of Myers-Briggs). I do not really like this technique, but he interpreted it in a very interesting way.

I'll tell her the essence in two words. For example, I go to negotiate with a top manager. It can be abstract, and it can be concrete, and my style of communication with this person and the success of negotiations ultimately depend on it.

What is the difference between an abstract-minded and concrete-minded person?

  • An abstract-minded person sees space to the horizon and even beyond the horizon. He sees prospects for the next 5-10 years, but he cannot stand the details and details.
  • The concrete thinker sees the trees, not the forest. Prospects are not available to him, but he loves to delve into details , nuances, etc.

Theory of Hofstede


Gert Hofstede is a Dutch scholar who has worked on cross-cultural differences. For example, why Japanese management is so different from American, and Russian from German. Although this theory was originally related to international management, it is very well applicable in the analysis of human personality traits.

Hofstede was commissioned by multinationals. The result was a very slim, based on real research system, for which Forbes recognized Hofstede as one of the most reputable business scientists in the field of psychology. I have no opportunity to tell about this theory in detail now. For myself, I made one important discovery. This theory works equally well in management, both at the level of nations and national characteristics, and at the level of specific individuals within the company.

The Hofstede theory is based on the evaluation of 5 parameters:

1. Power Distance Index (PDI) shows how a person (or society as a whole) is inclined to recognize inequality.

For example, in Russia this indicator is traditionally high, and in Holland, Sweden and Israel is low.

The same goes for the atmosphere in any company. Depending on the level of PDI, the role of management is completely different:

  • With low PDI, a top manager is a playing coach on a par with everyone;
  • In companies with high PDI, this is king.

It is interesting! If in general, in Russian companies Power Distance is high, then in IT companies it is low. We are generally special. That is why Russian IT professionals are so easy to leave for developed countries with low PDI.

2. Individualism (IDV) - individualism / collectivism

For example, Americans are individualists, while Chinese are collectivists.

3. Masculinity (MAS) - masculinity / femininity.

Americans with their cult of Rimbaud, ‚ÄúWhere is your money?‚Äù, ‚ÄúYou're a loser!‚Äù And all the rest have a very high MAS index. Thailand, by contrast, is one of the most "feminine" cultures in the world. Russia is also a ‚Äúwomen's country‚Äù - remember our fairy tales, folk songs, etc.

Sometimes it‚Äôs enough to look at how men and women look in the country, and it becomes clear who forms the national character. Remember the typical Germans or Englishwomen, and to compare Russian, Ukrainian or Thai girls.

4. Uncertainty Avoidance Index (UAI) - the acceptance / rejection of uncertainty.

5. Long-Term Orientation (LTO) - long term / short term.

  • The Chinese, roughly speaking, have a development plan for the next 100 years or more. Our task, including in management, is to get maximum today, and tomorrow, even if the grass does not grow, because hell knows what will happen tomorrow. This is a key difference between us and the Chinese.

Process Communication Model


This is the newest theory for me, which I am still studying. She seemed very interesting to me, so I‚Äôll say a few words about her. This model was developed in the 1970s by the American psychologist Tybee Keler. In 1978, he signed a contract with NASA, according to which he took part in the selection of psychological compatibility among astronauts for a complete set of effective crews. The selection was carried out, including, according to the psycho type, and not only according to physical and professional parameters. This work was successfully completed.

Now Kaler's development is popular mainly in America and France, and in Russia almost no one knows about it. We brought a trainer who conducted a training for the organization of EO entrepreneurs, in which I am a member. The model is called Process Communication (communication process) because it allows you to understand at what level a person is most effective in communicating with other people.

Basic stands out 6 levels . They are theoretically equal to each other, but they are not equal for each person. The lower the level, the more energy a person spends, communicating at this level and trying to go to his base - the more understandable and simple for him.



For example, my PCM model:

  • The basic level is logic, I have 100% energy there. It is easy and simple for me to speak the language of logic.
  • The next level is stubborn. Speaking at the level of life principles and ideas is also easy for me.
  • Dreamer;
  • Worker;
  • Rebel;
  • Soulful.

At each next level, I find it harder to communicate with other people . For example, at the mental level, I have only 5-10% of my nominal power of energy. I am losing a lot of energy there, and the efficiency is the lowest because I am not at all spiritual.

I am a logician , I logically think, I am responsible, I try to explain everything from the point of view of logic. A sincere person - warm, sensitive, sensitive, empathetic, etc. Not mine.

Persistent is a person with his life principles who will not violate them. He has his own rules of the game, which he himself invented, or his parents inspired him, or someone else, and he lives according to them.

The worker is a typical entrepreneur - a creator who constantly does something, agrees, turns, turns.

The dreamer loves to be alone, sit, thinking and dreaming about something. He will do nothing until he is kicked. But the dreamer is good because he can give birth to the most creative ideas that do not even occur to logic. Dreamers have a violent imagination and imagination.

For example, when I had an advertising agency, a girl worked there. She was a typical dreamer, that is, until you kick, she does not fly. She hung out at work, listening to music and looking at one point. My job was to force her to write creative concepts for large clients. When she wrote these texts, everyone's jaw dropped, so cool they were. But it was necessary that no one touched her for a while, and periodically kicked her so that she would not hang.

Mental person is found in many companies. Usually they are women, but I know a few male businessmen of this level. By the way, in IT companies, spiritual people are not enough.

To the rebel, the most important thing is to draw attention to oneself, to hit everyone, to shock, to surprise, to draw in the glory, something to say, so that everyone would look at him.



Process Communication is a fairly expensive, but effective, 2-day training. After you study Afanasyev's Psychosophy, I recommend doing it. They complement each other very well, because they look at people in different projections, but do not contradict them.

4 personality functions


To begin with, the human personality consists of four basic components, called "functions." This is not so hot, what a discovery, but nothing more to come up with:

  • EMOTION (soul) - all values ‚Äã‚Äãassociated with the spiritual world. It is not only our experiences and emotions (from hysterics to euphoria), but also everything related to right-hemispheric activity - with art, creativity, sensuality, etc.
  • LOGIC (mind) is our rational mind. For right-handed people, this is the left hemisphere of the brain, our ability to think abstractly.
  • PHYSICS (body) - except for the body, these are all values ‚Äã‚Äãrelated to the material world: life energy, sex, money, things, trinkets, cool cars, watches, brunettes, and so on.
  • WILL (spirit) is self-esteem, self-esteem, a place in the social hierarchy: who are you - the leader, in the middle, or are you at the bottom and obey everything? The ability to make decisions and take responsibility. It is also the ability and desire to influence other people, forcing them to do what you want.

The trick is that each person by nature has all four functions, but they are in a different sequence.

Function order


Each person functions in a different order, for example:

Functions are not equal. There is an internal hierarchy of these functions, which determines the type of each person (24 psycho-types in total). Of course, many IT specialists with the 1st logic will say that this is all bullshit, it is impossible to divide all the diversity of people into 24 types. It's too easy.

In fact, you can. I will not argue that 2 people with the same psychotype will be the same. No, they will not do! They will not be because they have different parents, different destinies, they grew up in different countries, they have different bodies. They will still be different. But! Life values ‚Äã‚Äãthey will be about the same .

The 2 upper functions are considered to be conditionally the strengths of the individual, and the 2 lower functions are considered conditionally weak. But I emphasize the word ‚Äú conditionally‚Äù here because sometimes the 4th function of one person is much stronger than the 1st function of another, if measured, for example, intelligence or physical abilities. Sometimes our conditionally weaknesses influence our fate much more than conditionally strong ones. I'll tell you a little more in detail, because this may be incomprehensible.

First function


This is the most important, strong and typical side of our nature, our most important survival weapon:

  • personality support, the foundation of the psyche.
  • always on display.
  • formulates a personal goal in life, the meaning of life, the system of values, we look at the world first of all through the prism of the 1st function.

This is our main weapon in conflicts : family, workers - any. Always turn on first (first reaction). With any danger, if a person has the first function:

  • physics, then he either hits hard or runs away quickly;
  • logic, he immediately interprets the situation and finds a solution;
  • emotion, then he begins to gush and scream so that the enemies themselves scatter.

In addition, we need weapons not only to fight the enemies. For example, there are weapons in our love tasks when you need to continue your race. This is also the task of survival, which is given to us by nature and which each 1st function solves in its own way:

  • 1st logic will try to hit the girl with your mind;

  • 1-Emotional person is prone to tantrums for any reason.

  • The 1-free man rudely puts pressure on everyone else, breaks them on the knee, demands that everything be according to his will and not listen to anyone but himself.

The first function Afanasyev compared with a sledgehammer . This is not a very thin instrument, the most suitable for punching walls. But this is not an hour-long screwdriver for repairing fine mechanisms.

Second function


The ideal function in our psycho-type is the best and most healthy part of human nature :

  • does not set goals in life, but is the main tool for achieving them.
  • not striking neither himself nor others.

It is process , in contrast to the first function, is able to listen to others and communicate with them, and it is exactly what it should be, ideally - there is not too much and not too little.

For example, the best actor is a person not with the first emotion, but with the second, because he will cry when it is necessary, and when he must laugh, and between these events 2 seconds can pass. Afanasyev compared the second function with the river, which, like the Volga, flows endlessly and for a long time, and you always know that the water will not end there. So is our 2nd function. Third function


As you understand, we should be unlucky about something - all people have not only advantages, but also disadvantages . More precisely, we have what we consider to be our shortcomings, about which we have been thinking from childhood that we have not been very successful in this.

Since this is the most painful place for us, we always try to cover it up, as football players cover their most sensitive place during the wall. So we are constantly walking, covering our third function with a fig leaf.

Web technology vs Development speed and performance

Continuing the topic of the previous post , I will comment on the words tushev :
And the interface in the old OS / 2 required orders of magnitude less CPU and memory resources.
Yes, this is true - by orders of magnitude.

I do not have a universal solution, and of course I understand that there is a reality given to us today in sensations (historical layers in the form of HTML and CSS of all versions, HTTP). And it's very hard to change anything globally. But I want every developer to think about the absurdity of the current state of affairs in the web-technology. Otherwise, no one will ever have a cardinal solution. Therefore, give facts that are suggestive.

A 20-year-old UI example, which I propose to first reproduce to all HTML and CSS apologists in these languages, before minus the ‚Äúkarma‚Äù:




Twenty years ago, in the spring of 1992, IBM introduced the OS / 2 2.0 operating system. The standard built-in function GpiBox in its API, the call of which could be written in the code in 5 seconds, was able to draw rectangles with given radii of corners.

Using the standard GpiSetModelTransformMatrix function, these rectangles could be subjected to affine transformations such as rotation, transfer, stretching and tilting in any combination, and these transformations could be ‚Äúcascaded‚Äù with each other by the operating system (in order not to load the application programmer‚Äôs head with geometry).

In drawing, it was possible to control the thickness of the lines, the way they were connected, and the mode of drawing the ends, much like in the modern vector package Corel.

The graphics in the OS was vector. Any drawn shape could be converted to a clipping path . In the standard API, clipping in an arbitrary form was supported. Not just a square or some kind of ellipse - the border could even be constructed from Bezier curves combined with the outlines of the letters of the Adobe vector font!

Clipping could contain holes (nested, intersecting, and self-intersecting shapes). It was possible to combine multiple clipping regions, intersecting or merging them with each other.

To facilitate the writing of controls through a standard function, it was possible to request a list of all the figures that lie in the specified neighborhood of a given point.

About 20 years ago, the Tseng Labs ET4000 graphics card with two megabytes of memory, about twelve minutes past the current generation of graphics cards , drew 925 rectangles with rounded corners per second . Or 243 rectangles per second applying to them an ellipse cut with a star-shaped hole inside it . Here is the real test data:

And now ‚Äúwelcome to the real world‚Äù:

A highly qualified web programmer, if instructed to do this without ready-made toolkits and without distorting someone else's code, will spend a couple of days programming the drawing of a square with rounded corners in current browser versions, without leaving IE 6 behind the bracket.

He will have to use for this HTML, CSS, JavaScript, prepare auxiliary PNG-files and maybe even VML / SVG, if you need to fully support the flexibility of setting parameters. At the same time, there can be no talk of any clipping along an ellipse with a star-shaped hole inside ... :)

There can be no question of accurately and cross-browser determining whether the mouse cursor fell inside this square or remained outside its rounded corner ...

At the same time, a modern solution will draw several dozen squares per second on the map plus the twelfth (compared to 1992) generation and the process of drawing their curve in pieces can be seen right on the screen .

Therefore, as far as possible, IMHO should generally leave this technology stack for anything else. Using fragmented and non-cross-browser HTML + CSS only maximum as a carrier, at the output stage. :)

Startup Crash Test 11.0 will be held in Kiev on Friday

This time, the issue of transactional business models in startups will be considered as a presentation at the CTS . The speaker will be Katya Mikula from the eVentures Foundation.
After this report will be a panel, which will lead Yegor Anchishkin - this is his debut as a moderator. The panel confirmed their participation by the CEO auction.com.ua, the project leader hotline.ua and the CEO futbolka.ua. The same transactional model will be discussed.
After a half-hour break, there will be a section ( Arthur Orudzhaliev will try his hand at the section ) as a short (90 seconds) presentations of startups. In conclusion, three startups will be presented (15 + 5 + 5), after which the public will traditionally disperse to nearby restaurants, bars and pubs ( some of them will show off with meter and a half checks ).
As always there will be a lot of people, fun and interesting. This time, traditionally, we will have guests from Russia (MSC, SPB).
The event will be raffled among the late 5 iForum tickets .

We conduct Startup Crash Test in Kiev 11 times. In February, the first CTS took place in St. Petersburg and CCP Moscow is planned for March 12. Events are planned in Novosibirsk, Prague and London.
The format provides an event for entrepreneurs, where they are looking for the weak points of their own and others' projects. Present projects and watch how others are presented. Much attention is paid to networking. Traditionally, the event is visited by investors, although the event is not focused on them. If at the first CTs in early 2009, then at the last there were almost 300 people. Tekranch Europe published an article on this topic, which had a great response.
The format of the event includes free participation for both participants and visitors, subject to prior registration. Those who are late and (or) have not registered, pay 20 UAH per entry. This is not the earnings of the organizers, but rather the disciplinary methods that we had to introduce for such a disgusting event.

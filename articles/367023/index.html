Philae landed on the comet Churyumov-Gerasimenko a year ago



The landing of Phila's probe on the comet Churyumov-Gerasimenko is one of the most significant events in the history of space exploration. Despite some setbacks, the probe still managed to gather a huge amount of information, and almost completely fulfill its mission. Now the European Space Agency has reconstructed the landing process, with the creation of animation, and quite detailed. By the way, exactly one year has passed since the landing.

The almost two-minute video shows the descent of the Phila module onto the comet, which lasted about seven hours, plus two hours of the probe trying to gain a foothold on the comet's surface. During these two hours, the module came into contact with the comet's core three times.

The video was created using all the landing data available to scientists. Scientists have calculated the trajectory of Philae for a long time, using the method of numerical simulation.



The Rosetta mission added a lot of data to the general array of knowledge about comets, their evolution and composition. It happened at the dawn of the solar system, as a result of the collision of two objects.

In 2014, the Russian e-commerce market grew by one third: causes and forecasts

According to CNews, the Russian e-commerce market has grown by 31% over the past year. This happened in connection with the spread of the Internet in the regions and the development of the mobile Internet.

According to the report of the Association of Online Trading Companies (ACIT), in 2014 the volume of the Russian online-trading market reached 713 billion rubles, which is a third more than in 2013 (544 billion rubles).

According to CNews' own calculations, in 2014 the volume of the e-commerce market in Russia amounted to $ 18.6 billion in dollar terms ($ 1 = 38, 42 rubles). In 2013, this figure was $ 1.5 billion less - $ 17.1 billion ($ 1 = 31.85 rubles).

In 2014, the distribution of buyers' expenses of online stores was as follows: 464 billion rubles were spent on tangible goods, and 249 billion - on intangibles. Compared with 2013, the share of material goods increased by 67%.

The most popular online store in Russia is Ozon. In second place - Yulmart.



According to ACIT data for 2014, the number of orders that Russians make in foreign online stores increased by 115% (up to 80 million units) with an average check of $ 25- $ 150. But 80% of orders with an average check of $ 25- $ 30 are in China. This is largely due to the active growth of Russian buyers in the AliExpress online store, which belongs to the Chinese company Alibaba Group.

The total amount spent by Russians in foreign online stores in 2014 was $ 6 billion. While in 2013 this figure reached only $ 3.5 billion.

According to analysts J'son & Partners Consulting, in 2015 the volume of the Russian online-trading market in monetary terms will decrease by 5%.

Due to the weakening of the ruble, a decrease in the share of cross-border trade is expected.

According to experts, in 2015 the growth of online hypermarkets with a mixed assortment of goods will increase.

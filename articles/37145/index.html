The history of LEGO, or how the most famous brand in the toy world was born

The LEGO brand is today the most famous toy world. Even more famous than Hasbro and Barbie doll. But few people know that the Danish company began its history as an ordinary manufacturer of wooden products. And the first LEGO toys were completely made of wood.



However, the company managed to grow to such a size that its famous designers began to copy other companies-pirates, which produced similar sets of toys in batches. But Seth Godin once said: "If you are copied, it means that you really did something wonderful."

Childhood ... wooden toys

The history of LEGO dates back to 1932, when Ole Kirk Christiansen founded a company for the production of everyday goods in Denmark. Ole made products from a tree, thus initially the main profit of the company was brought by ladders and ironing boards, the demand for which fell sharply during the global financial crisis.

It was necessary to look for a new niche. And Ole saw her in the production of wooden toys, as the demand for them did not weaken even in difficult financial times. At that time, his son, who had been working with his father since he was 12, was the main assistant to Ole.

Starting the release of toys, Christiansen began to look for a name for his company. He gave the task to all factory workers to offer their own version of the name. The rapid development of the LEGO range

If we talk about the products of LEGO of those years, it may seem that the company focused exclusively on the representatives of the male half of humanity. This is not entirely true, as the products for girls in the LEGO assortment were also. For example, in the 70s produced doll houses with furniture. And the Duplo project was equally designed for both girls and boys. Although, of course, even today, most of the LEGO products are created with a slant on the male audience. And this is not discrimination, they simply love designers.

In 1973, the famous LEGO logo was developed, which today is known to most people on the planet. After the appearance of the new logo, the company began to open production outside Denmark. The first country to host the LEGO factory was the highland Switzerland.

By 1977, the company LEGO was headed by the grandson of Ole Christiansen Keld. By that time, LEGO had already released many different sets for every taste, among which were even some kind of technical solutions. At this time, kits appeared containing figures with moving limbs. It was in 1979 that the first truly thematic LEGO set appeared, which would begin the classification of the company's products right up to the present day - this will be the LEGOLAND Space set. Cosmos will be just the beginning for LEGO. But a very successful start.

The LEGO assortment grew by leaps and bounds. But most importantly, consumers adored the products of this company. She was really original, attractive, interesting. In 1980, LEGO lovers expressed their love for the company by building a huge tower of 13.1 meters from this designer. Impressive, isn't it? But do not think that this is the tallest tower created from LEGO. Not at all! At the end of the 90s, a tower of LEGO 24.88 meters was built in Moscow. She entered the Guinness Book of Records.



The success of the space, and some other LEGO sets was overwhelming. But even more, the company received from the set "Castle", which was released in 1984. The theme of the Middle Ages was very popular among fans of LEGO products. In the same year, experimental models from the Lego set were shown. Nowadays

Now the LEGO company is at the stage of overcoming the protracted crisis. Simple toys have lost their appeal due to the widespread distribution of computer games. In addition, the Danish company some time ago became a real victim of diversification. The LEGO perfume sets, clothing, and many other things began to appear, which greatly hurt the brand and only caused losses, which in 2003 amounted to about $ 300 million. In addition, the company was not very efficiently put into production, because of what it was losing money, did not minimize the costs to the maximum. In order to remedy this deplorable situation, LEGO invited the CEO Jorgen Wig Knudstorp, who immediately got down to business.

First, he transferred production to cheaper countries. Secondly, he refused from unprofitable businesses and innovations in the company. In addition, Jorgen has paid serious attention to the company on the Internet, where it can receive new ideas from its customers.

Things LEGO went on the mend. And I want to believe that the company will regain its former glory, not having buried under the weight of computer games.

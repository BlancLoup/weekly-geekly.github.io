Netcraft's January report on http server popularity

Netcraft has published the results of the January study of the popularity of http-servers. It has been verified about 206 million sites. Compared to the previous month, the number of active sites has decreased by 30 million, mostly due to the transfer of the Chinese blog service qq.com to closed mode.

The popularity of http servers as of January 2010 is about the following: The number of Apache web servers increased by about 3 million hosts and reached 111.3 million in January, which is about 53% of the market. The number of serers managed by MS IIS also increased by 600 thousand and reached 49.79 million, which is about 24% of the market. Nginx, in contrast to the above described, lost 700 thousand hosts in comparison with the previous month, the total number stopped at 15 million, and continues to show growth in the number of active sites.

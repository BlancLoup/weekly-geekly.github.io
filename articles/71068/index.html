EU may ban iPhone


The European Commission launched an official investigation into the alleged explosion of the Apple-phone earlier this year, and demanded an explanation. So far there are only three reported cases of device explosion: in the UK, in France and in Germany, respectively.
Mrs. Meglena Kuneva, European Commissioner for Consumer Affairs and Their Rights, said that tests and tests are underway at the moment. And even if the case is confirmed in at least one country, then this phone will be banned in the EU market due to possible harm to EU citizens.
Apple has not responded to the threat of the EU. The European Union is not a market that can be neglected, and a possible ban on the commission could mean the end of all awards and device rankings, especially if other countries follow this example.

Anonymus created their alternative to Pastebin



Hackers have launched a new site, which (according to their own statements) will allow users to post messages about their exploits, and not be afraid that they will be able to calculate.

Anonymus called it AnonPaste, they position it as a safer alternative to Pastebin.com.



A statement released on Tuesday said that this resource will allow people to post any materials on the terms of complete anonymity. The authors of the statement are the Anonymus themselves and the Front for National Liberation .

It is especially noted that there will be no censorship and no moderators on the site at all.

TTX : the link shortening will work on the website, the maximum amount of text placed at a time is 2 megabytes. In this case, the posting user can set in the settings for how long to leave this text in the public domain.

Lyrical digression

Pastebin.com itself was originally created for programmers and developers to help them with the exchange and storage of code. For several years, this site was used to publish documents of any kind, and hackers at one time chose it. Only the latter, in contrast to programmers, placed there also documents that constitute personal, corporate and commercial secrets.

Anonymus and the Popular Liberation Front claim that they started creating AnonPaste after they learned that Pastebin.com is able to censor the contents of the site and also send information to the appropriate authorities about the IP addresses of people who post such information.

And still Anonymus reported that they got access to one correspondence, from which it follows that pastebin merges information about users not only to government organizations, but also to private security agencies.

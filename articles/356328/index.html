Mandriva ceases to exist



This is sad news for Linux fans. The French technical company Mandriva is liquidated according to the information placed in the catalog of organizations, Business Insider reports .

In 2013, Mandriva earned an income of 553,000 euros, but this was not enough to keep the company afloat in 2015.

For decades, Mandriva has been trying to dislodge Microsoft's desktop OS from the throne. The first bid for success was a deal made in 2007 with the Nigerian government. Then Mandriva convinced them to abandon Windows in favor of their product, which, in consequence, was installed on 17,000 computers in Nigerian schools.

Mandriva also enjoyed some success in Malaysia.

In 2012, the company is not the first time on the verge of bankruptcy. Mandriva faced financial problems at the dawn of its existence, in 1998.

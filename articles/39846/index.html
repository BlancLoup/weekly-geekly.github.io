Firmware 1.0

On CTIA Wireless 2008, guys with AndroidCommunity found a Qualcomm phone, with no identifying marks other than the brand. It turned out that the phone works on Android with firmware version 1.0, which confirms rumors about the proximity of the release. So that we all believe them, they videotaped the work of the browser, messenger, Quake and many more interesting things, see below.


The device does not support 3G and is clearly not designed for Android. But even in such harsh conditions, having only a joystick and an EDGE connection, Android looks great. It looks great on a VGA screen and all interface elements load very quickly. And, although the messenger could not be started, the browser (based on WebKit, like desktop Chrome) was launched. True, it seems Google is not going to add Flash support to it. At least for now.

Watch the video and you will realize that Android is almost ready for release. We will see him soon. September 23 is the date we all heard about and maybe it will be the day when the world will know about Android.

Automate my testing completely, cheaply, forever: QA-mitap announcement in Avito on August 26

Today I want to invite you on Saturday, August 26th, to the first Avito Automation meetup. What is going to be there? A rather substantive conversation about the development of automation systems and applied tools. What do we expect from the event? Constructive communication and exchange of views among professionals.

Mitap in Moscow, participation is free, registration is required. And for starters - a short story based on the reports. Link to the timepad and schedule at the end of the post.




How to get out of the maze of reservations and start creating quality tests


What prevents writing tests? Yes, a lot of things. Someone can even tell you that it is long, expensive, inefficient, it is not necessary even with a dozen definitions with the prefix "not."


Picture: imgflip.com

How to overcome this? How to prove to those who do not believe that it is possible to create tests quickly, cheaply and effectively then support them, that there is a bright side of power? How to get a good product, tell Fedor Zvolinsky, Yandex.

How to run processes in CI


The first frontier we overcame, and now it's time to run our tests. But there are always nuances.


Picture: imgur.com

To get around the perspective of the rake dance (or at least make it more elegant), let's talk about the nuances of setting up continuous integration for working with load, acceptance, integration, UI and Smoke testing, checking the correctness of the installation and upgrade. Speaker - Sergey Pak (JetBrains).

Slip away from avalanche


In any project, the number of tests will gradually grow: from 1000 to 3000, from 6000 to 9000+, etc. To prevent this avalanche from covering the QA process, you need to think about the effectiveness of the entire system and every test in it from the very early stage of the development of the automation project.


Picture: imgflip.com

And at this moment we need a flexible and efficient system. And metrics for evaluating automation processes and QA in general. This will tell Maxim Sakharov (Tutu.ru).

MTS-Fon - Wi-Fi network at the expense of Stream clients

If you are a Stream customer (recently renamed MTS Home Internet), Comstar, MGTS Internet or any other brand owned by Sistema Telecom, know that neighbors and passers-by use your Internet access. Perfectly, and legally.

Details under the cut.



It all started with the fact that I, unexpectedly for myself, became a client of ‚ÄúHome Internet from MGTS‚Äù. We temporarily moved to my sister's apartment, where there was simply no other Internet.

So, the speed categorically did not suit me. Stability too. It was absolutely impossible to play Battlefield 3 to work and code. After numerous fruitless inquiries and consultations with the support service, I humbled myself ...

But all of a sudden, once again rebooting the router, I noticed that the MTS / FON WiFi network (which is clearly not mine - this is WiFi from MTS! And there is little around neighboring networks ...) , disappears and appears simultaneously with the restart of my router. By simple manipulations, it turned out that this SSID really comes from my router. Moreover, free of charge and without authorization.

I will not describe my numerous attempts to disconnect this network, assign at least a password to it (everything was reset after the modem was rebooted), my calls to the MTS support service, and numerous complaints I found from my brothers in misfortune and so on. I will go straight to the point.

What was it


So, if you bought an ‚Äúofficial‚Äù Wi-Fi router (that is, in the Stream / MTS / MGTS / Comstar salon, with the company logo, as in the picture, etc.), this device creates two Wi-Fi networks. One is your home. The second is called ‚ÄúMTS / FON‚Äù, which he distributes to everyone.

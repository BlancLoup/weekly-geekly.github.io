On November 11 of this year, open registration of domain names in the Cyrillic zone of the Russian Federation was launched. In any case, this is what the media called this process - open registration. I also tried to use open registration and register for one of my clients the domain he asked for - I returned from vacation just that day. And - you are absolutely right! - nothing came of it. It turned out that this domain is registered on RU-CENTER.
I was surprised - my domain name to RU-CENTER does not fit, even if it is turned inside out. However, by phone, I was quickly explained that what was needed ‚Äî open registration means that everyone can submit applications and, who managed before noon on November 10, he will participate in a closed auction. Having marveled at the pretentiousness of the ideas of certain comrades about openness, I spat on this case, saying to myself that everything is done in a certain way in this country, but the story got a curious continuation ...

Exactly at this moment on Twitter, a discussion about zona.rf took place (explain to me, for what the fuck does the parser remove the space before.rf is the smartest, or what?). According to the document, in the first 6 hours of the registration of domains. Rf, 184352 domains were registered. Of these, 56,785 (or almost 31%) are registered at RU-CENTER. Further, captured domain names will be resold during closed auctions. These actions are unambiguously classified as cybersquatting, which, according to Wikipedia for example , is " ... the acquisition of domain names consonant with the names of well-known companies, or simply with" expensive "names for the purpose of their resale ... ". At the same time - I quote the published document - " The acquired list contains: registered trademarks, names of organizations, brands, media names, common vocabulary words covering more than 90% of all used in common speech and in the classifier of goods and services of FIPS. At the same time, the Coordination Center of the National Domain, at the end of September, spoke out quite unequivocally - the auctions are just extortion of money (in fact) and CC does not recommend working with such registrars, preferring those who follow the established procedure for registering domains. The appeal of the Coordination Center regarding auctions can be found on the Center's website; there is also a domain registration procedure . What is characteristic, a close examination of the registration procedure (clause 3 of the Rules by reference) did not allow me to see there even a hint at auctions - they are not there and it is not indicated anywhere that someone has the right to organize them.
By the way, the study of the Domain Registration Rules showed that the current practice of domain auctions selling released domain names (this practice is successfully used by RU-CENTER almost from the very beginning of its activities) is also outside the rules established by the Coordination Center. The rules, meanwhile, are successfully violated ...

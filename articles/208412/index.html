CentOS joins forces with Red Hat

The developers of the CentOS Linux distribution announced that from this point on the CentOS Project will go under the wing of the Red Hat company. For CentOS users, this will not lead to any changes, so don't worry. The next release will be released according to plan .

Changes will occur only in the organizational structure. First, the leading CentOS developers are now included in the Red Hat staff and will receive a salary there, continuing to work on CentOS, but not on RHEL. Secondly, the CentOS Governing Board begins to operate , consisting of five CentOS developers and four Red Hat representatives.

CentOS is one of the most popular Linux distributions on web servers. In 2010-2012, he was generally in the first place, which was then conceded to Debian. This distribution was created nine years ago as a free alternative to the commercial distribution of Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL), while providing almost 100% binary compatibility with it. From it only removed the Red Hat logos.

Britons will stick online stamps on letters

Now the British can pay for sending correspondence of the first and second class, as well as international shipments via the Internet with a credit card.

Not a mark will be attached to the envelope, but a special bar code that can be printed on the printer. Then the letter can be sent as usual. The price of Internet stamps will correspond to the cost of traditional paper postage stamps. The service will be provided around the clock and is designed to reduce queues at post offices, as well as to simplify life for people who work from home or send a large amount of correspondence, reports RIA Novosti with reference to the Royal Mail press service.

Month with a generator of beautiful QR codes

The other day we were visited by a funny idea - to analyze QR codes that are generated by users . The results were, on the one hand, somewhat predictable, on the other - surprised, and on the third - even amused.


About prosaic


Most often, users encrypt names, data from their business cards, email addresses or links to websites. And among the commonplace (a la Ivanov Sergey and Tatiana Sokolova) were also very peculiar names, surnames. Kadygrob Denis, Shalava Olga Nikolaevna (interestingly, the maiden name or already “by her husband”), Pidosyuk Oleg Alexandrovich and Popandopulo Vyacheslav were especially remembered.

The second, rather extensive category includes “about nothing” codes: “test”, “ytsuke”, “123456”, “qwerty”, “lyalyala”, “+++++” and the like. Well, what do you say here? Fantasy, alas, is not given to everyone.

In third place in popularity - codes, expressions of feelings. There were also the English "I love you", and the German "Ich liebe dich", and the French "Je T" aime. Next to the encrypted "Sun, I love you" and "Little hare, you're the best" successfully coexist "Marseille, a fool" and "You poop tratata".

I am glad that users who want to confess their love are 10 times more than those who, with the help of a QR code, strive to show their not-too-friendly attitude. Someone even decided to tell his director about his exclusivity - “Irina, you are the best director”. Strange, but no one guessed using the QR code to make an offer to the girl.

It didn’t do without political sentiments, and here, most likely, the Belarusians brothers tried: “Look in the Ass”, “But father's furnace” and known today to every Belarusian “SCO”. And the Russians let us down: the codes about one of the Putin-Medvedev couple never met.

Many users generated lines from poems. The leader was “Our Tanya cries loudly” - they remembered about the sobbing girl already 5 times. Things are much worse with the school curriculum: if Pushkin still knows someone (“I am writing to you, what is more”), then Lermontov, Yesenin, or Nekrasov are undeservedly forgotten at all. It is also strange that it did not occur to anyone to generate lines of songs. Of the “music” QR codes, only “Donkey, gopher, spider, and sodgy” and “before remi fi sol la si” were “caught”. The last code, by the way, attracted by its blood-red coloring and menacing, written on the code itself, the phrase “I will kill”. More confirmation of how our expectations are often completely at odds with what we received.

A catalog with plugins has become available.

Matt Mullenweg , the founder of WordPress, published a news blog for developers yesterday that a plug-in catalog has now become available. Here is its content:

One of the great features of WordPress is the huge amount of good plugins. If we included all these plugins into the standard WordPress distribution, we would get a very heavy product. Thanks to a well-designed API, people make almost any WP plugins you can imagine.

This, of course, is good, but all this variety of plugins is useless, when there is no place where you can find plugins. Although we provided developers with the wp-plugins.org site, it did not provide a user interface for the plug-ins hosted there.

Enough! In our constantly expanding section "extensions" updated directory with plug-ins. Now you can view, download, vote and leave comments to all your favorite plugins. I recommend you go there and check everything yourself.

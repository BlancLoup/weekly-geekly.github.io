FAQ


unicast - Broadcast to one computer (i.e., one IP address): this is called unicast broadcast,
multicast - Broadcast to a dynamically changing group of clients that can join or disconnect from the broadcast at any time (i.e., several IP addresses): this is called multicast broadcast
VLC - VideoLAN Client (He is the player, how everyone sees him)
VLS - VideoLAN Server , (which is almost completely transferred to VLC), in addition to the features of VLC, broadcasts streaming video transmitted via IPv4 and Ipv6:
1. receiving the signal of a satellite decoder (DVB-S) or a conventional digital television decoder (DVB-T),
2. receiving a capture card signal encoded in MPEG.
As well as broadcasting live video,
SAP / SDP - SAP Mini-Server announces multicast broadcasts via IPv4 or IPv6, and VLC receives these announcements and automatically adds the announced broadcasts to the playlist.
VLMa - A utility for managing streams of TV channels received in digital or satellite mode. The interface is designed as a Web site and is written in Java. Also supports streaming audio and video files. Among the key features worth noting:
  • Management of VLM instances through telnet interface in VLC
  • Active Stream Monitoring
  • Dynamic stream tasks (including backup in case of server unavailability)
  • Drawing graphs and statistics
  • Notifications by mail, ICQ, IRC, MSN


Internet / LAN channel requirement.


  • from 512Kb / s to 4 Mb / s for MPEG-4 (broadcasting)
  • 3-4 Mb / s For broadcasting video in MPEG-2 from satellite and digital TV, or from an MPEG-2 encoding card.
  • And from 6 to 9 Mb / s for DVD broadcast.


Announcements / Announce


In VLC, you can announce your own streams based on the SAP / SDP standard, or Zeroconf (better known as Bonjour ).

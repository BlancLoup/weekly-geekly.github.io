Microsoft has introduced a development that researchers Interactive 3D Technologies called holoportaiton. This is a teleconferencing system based on the use of a HoloLens helmet. Its feature is the creation of the illusion of human presence. HoloLens wearing glasses sees another participant in the conversation as if he is in the same room with him. In reality, the interlocutor can be removed for hundreds, thousands of kilometers.



HoloLens Mixed Reality Glasses are Microsoft's investment in the now popular virtual reality trend. Many players and giants of the market presented their helmets: Oculus Rift, Google Cardboard, Valve HTC Vive, Samsung Gear VR and others. Others just want to join the BP hysteria: this is the Sony PlayStation 4K , there are rumors about Apple‚Äôs plans. Almost all such helmets are exclusively devices for displaying information and capturing the position of the head, so that the computer can know which picture to give.

This is what makes HoloLens different. HoloLens would be worth comparing with another project - Google Glass, which aimed to create a daily assistant. Unlike Glass, HoloLens tries to occupy a niche device for gamers and productive workers who need visualization. Promotional materials speak of this. In the presentations flashed the opportunity to see the world of Minecraft on the table in the living room or play with spectacular destruction . The glasses were sent to the International Space Station to allow astronauts to test HoloLens usage scenarios.

A helmet running Windows 10 projects objects onto transparent lenses, creating the illusion of augmented or mixed reality. It is not just an output device. The glasses have a built-in computer with 2 GB of RAM. Available at 120 to 120 degrees, camera, Wi-Fi and Bluetooth connectivity. However, the exact characteristics of HoloLens are not yet known. The consumer version is only in the plans so far, and the developer version for a symbolic 3000 dollars should begin to arrive on March 31.

Microsoft has published a video about the "holoportitsii."

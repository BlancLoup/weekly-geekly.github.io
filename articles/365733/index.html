Testing IKEA LED lamps

Good LED lamps are still quite expensive, and because of changes in exchange rates, they have become even more expensive.
One of the lowest prices for LED lamps now in IKEA stores.
I tested 11 actual LEDARE LED lamps, costing from 169 to 599 rubles.




Ikea lamps last year had problems with the pulsation (flickering) of light. The 7-watt candle plugs I tested had a ripple rate of 63%, which of course is no good. All new lamps have a ripple rate from 4 to 29%, which is quite acceptable for lighting residential premises (conventional incandescent bulbs have a ripple factor of 8-31%).

The device Lupine was used to measure the pulsation coefficient, and the Viso LightSpion device was used to measure the other parameters of the lamps. The equivalent of the power of incandescent lamps was determined by the results of my measurements of the parameters of 22 incandescent lamps . All photos of the visual comparison were made in manual exposure mode (ISO 125, 1/30, 1.8) with a white balance set to 3500K.

All lamps comply with the stated parameters, they all give a luminous flux even slightly larger than the manufacturer promises.



The table shows that all lamps have a high color rendering index (CRI), one of the lamps has a record value of 95.6. All the lamps tested, except for one, have a color temperature of about 2700K and give light similar to incandescent light.

All lamps can work with switches having an indicator.
Five lamp models support dimming (dimming).

Now more about the tested lamps. I'll start with the most powerful and most expensive lamp that can replace a 100-watt incandescent lamp.

1. Frosted ball 1000 lm 002.574.82 E27 LED1309G15 13 W, dimmable. 599 rub.


www.ikea.com/ru/ru/catalog/products/00257482

The lamp gives almost 1100 lumens, it has a high CRI of 92.5, it is only 5 mm longer than ordinary lamps and has a standard diameter of 60 mm. The lamp supports brightness control, however, the minimum brightness during dimming is only about half the full brightness. Due to the matt ceiling with a diameter larger than the body, the lamp has a wide illumination angle of 233 ¬∞.



When visually comparing with a 95 W incandescent lamp, it can be seen that the LED lamp shines a little brighter, while its light is very similar to the incandescent light.

The LED lamps that can be bought in Ikea stores are quite good, and in terms of price / quality ratio are among the best. They have a pulsation of light, but it is within the normal range. Not all is well with dimming those lamps that support it. Three of the four tested dimmable lamps do not allow to lower the brightness below half, and only one dimmable lamp did not hum when adjusting the brightness.

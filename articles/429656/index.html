On January 22, 2018, Amazon opened the world's first store without cash registers and vendors for residents in Seattle, and on October 23, this innovative store opened in San Francisco. The store of the future without cash registers and Amazon Go sellers allows you to simply collect products from the shelves and get out! We visited the store of the future without cashiers and salesmen in San Francisco in the first week of its opening, where we checked in detail the algorithms of machine learning and computer vision, the range of products and their prices.



So, to start shopping, at the entrance to the store, you need to scan a personal QR code in the pre-downloaded Amazon Go application on your smartphone. You need to enter the store through the turnstiles, like in the subway or bus, one account for several people, but for this you need to scan the QR code each time, letting the guests go ahead. When visiting you need to remember two basic rules:

After entering the store, the integrated Amazon system with hundreds of sensors on the shelves and computer vision cameras on the ceiling begins to track in detail the human behavior in the store. Automated Amazon Go system using machine learning accumulates an array of data obtained to identify any product in the store with the buyer without bar codes and chips on the goods.

Obvious advantages of this technology are getting rid of queues in supermarkets and reducing employees in the store. Apparently, since the system only rolls around and people get to know how it works, the number of sales assistants per meter of retail space of the future Amazon Go store is now going off scale. In addition, there are chefs working in cooking and employees who monitor the sales of alcohol and check the documents of customers.

It should be noted that this system allows you to collect a huge array of data to analyze the behavior of customers, which allows Amazon to reach a new level of the big data market. The resulting statistics can be used to improve the location of products, prices, range and even ingredients.

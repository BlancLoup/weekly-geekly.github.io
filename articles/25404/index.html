So, the configuration of the tentacles of a single machine :

Proceeded.
I downloaded the Aptana Studio 1.1 Community edition product from the Aptana official site .
After a vigorous download process, a vigorous attempt to unpack the archive immediately followed.
With the intention of launching the product, based on the assurances of "just double-click on AptanaStudio launcher".
Unpacked the 70-megabyte archive, as it should be, in / home / dals / aptana
And with the help of Dolphin moved to the designated directory. The eyes immediately snatched the inscription "AptanaStudio", and his hands inexorably reached out to make a "double click". But this was not the case: after the click, for some reason, a window with the requirement of Firefox or Java RE, which was not heavy on forums and blogs, popped up. On the taskbar, the Wine icon dimly played.
Bad thoughts began to prevail: why? And, as it turned out, not in vain: Kubuntu for its own reasons considered that AptanaStudio is an application from the world of Win32 (!).
"However!" - I thought. I read a few more minutes of manuals and the Internet. :)
I decided to transfer to the recommended location / usr / local / aptana, although I knew with my head that there would be no difference.
The result is the same: dblClick -> Wine -> silent shutdown.
Had a malicious desire to act radically: remove trash and return to KomodoEdit.
But, as usual, leaped pride. Applied a makeshift, but effective method: on the desktop, created a launch shortcut for AptanaStudio. Another attempt - another failure.

Curiosity, comrades, will not destroy the world - it will save it: I decided to look into the properties of the label. How angry he was for himself: there is a launch point with Wine. With unspeakable composure removed the malicious point. Oh, miracle!
At last AptanaStudio showed the splash-screen.
True, not for long: the download of the program on my machine is about 10-15 seconds.

And, actually, I tell further observations of the speed of the Aptana + Ubuntu bundle.

Through FTP I opened a dozen files of a web project on CodeIgniter, added to the project.

Return Series 40

Recently, Nokia introduced two new models of the youngest Nokia Asha Touch line: 308 and 309. Physically, these phones have not reached us yet, but the previous three models: 305, 306 and 311 have already been sold in Russia since the end of August. So I had the opportunity to get acquainted with this line, interesting not only the price category, to which its products belong, but also the Series 40 software platform, which was pretty much reworked for touch control, on which the devices of this line work.





Story


S40, - one of the earliest developments of Nokia. The first Nokia 7110 phone that worked on the S40 was introduced in 1999. It was the device, the direct predecessor of which was the legendary Nokia 8110, which became a cult thanks to the film The Matrix. And so, in the same year, when the Matrix triumphantly walked across the screens of the whole world, and the Nokia 8110 was as significant a product as the iPhone was (and maybe still is) a couple of years ago, Nokia introduced the model 7110, the first phone on the new Series 40 platform. Although, now Nokia itself prefers to lead the s40 story from the 7110 follower - the 7210 model, which already has support for a color screen.

From a stylistic point of view, the 7110 did not repeat the success of the Matrix Phone, in part because it simply could not be repeated. However, from a technological point of view, the Nokia 7110 was without exaggeration a revolutionary device, because it became the first Nokia phone to support WAP technology, which opened access from the phone to the Internet.

Over the past 13 years, 6 full-fledged editions of the S40 software platform have been released. Some of these editions (such as, for example, the 3-rd edition, which appeared in 2005) had several sub-versions that introduced one or another functionality to the new devices. In total, more than 200 phone models were produced on the S40, and the total circulation of devices sold on the S40 exceeded 1.5 billion copies. For comparison, Android devices sold about 500 million in total, and 400 million on iOS. Of course, they are noticeably younger than the S40, but still.

However, throughout its history, the S40 remained a platform only for keyboard devices. And so, at the end of last year, Nokia introduced the ‚Äútouch‚Äù version of the S40, which inherited much from the interface, as it turned out during the presentation, from the MeeGo Harmattan operating system, which runs the Nokia N9 smartphone. Actually, that's why I was interested in these budget models, and when they finally became available for the test, I asked to test them.

Anyway, these are small devices with 3-inch displays, and, in the case of the older, 311 model, a capacitive display is used, in other models, a resistive display. Nokia Asha Touch 311




Despite the interface interface controls that the S40 inherited from MeeGo Harmattan, the Asha Touch phones are not without hardware buttons. There are two of them, the most simple: the receive and end button. The end call button, while held, also performs the function of turning the phone on and off. Strangely, the receive button does not activate the dialer interface for any other active application. Perhaps this is done so that involuntarily active programs do not close? But, on the other hand, it could be implemented also, with a warning, as applications close when you press the end button. The lock and unlock button of the display is located on the right side, there is also a volume rocker.









At the top there is a 2 mm jack for charging, a micro-USB jack for connecting to a computer. However, through it the phone can be charged, both from the computer and from the network. Well, there, at the top, there is a 3.5 mm headphone jack. On the left side there is only a small hole for the strap. Case 311 model is made of plastic. The back cover is glossy and rounded, which I personally did not really like.

The diagonal of the display in all three models is 3 inches, with a resolution of 240x400 pixels. The display recognizes 65 thousand colors. Multitouch is present only in the Gallery. The browser window is scaled by double tap.



Nokia Browser, is one of the main tactile S40 chips. It compresses up to 90% of traffic, thereby saving the user's money. At the same time, I will not say that the browser is very fast. With a Wi-Fi connection, it works quite tolerantly, but with 3G it is very slow. However, using this browser is quite realistic. There are also additional browsers in the App Store, although of those that I tried, Nokia Browser still seemed to be the best.

The hardware of the 311 models is not as weak as it may seem. It is based on 1 GHz processor, the amount of RAM is 256 MB, which is enough for S40. The amount of internal memory is 128 MB, and the microSD slot supports cards up to 32 GB. Included with the model line is attached microSD-card 2 GB.





Above the display are light and proximity sensors, just like adults. But the front camera in the 311, as well as in the 305 and 306 models - no. The main camera in the 311 model 3.2 MP. Thoughts after the test


I understand that I did not have a complete test at all, but what has grown has grown, as they say. Thinking about this new Nokia line after the test, I came to the following conclusions.

First, I really liked the tactile version of the S40. The interface is really comfortable and elegant. Despite the small diagonal of the display, the keyboard in both the vertical and horizontal layout is very convenient. And in general, I think the return of the S40 in the tachev version took place and promises a full new life.

Another thing is the devices themselves. Hand on heart, - I did not like any of the phones at 100%. The older, 311 model, in my opinion, is quite suitable for use and can even compete with cheap Chinese smartphones on Android. But for younger models I would definitely prefer Nokia Asha keyboards.

If I was asked, I would try to release a more powerful model based on the S40, with a display with an adequate diagonal and resolution, with a 5 megapixel camera and with an appearance that looks more like 305 and 306 models, rather than 311.

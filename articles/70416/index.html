Miracle Breeding - Traktor + Ableton

It's time to keep promises :)

So what do we have? We have two softwares, both recognized industry leaders, but each of them lacks something for happiness. Ableton has not very convenient transport part, but almost complete freedom of action with audio routing and connected VST effects. Traktor has the opposite: a very convenient and intuitive transport control panel, but with strained effects. The built-in ones are, of course, quite good in their own way, but they are far from enough for all needs. Even for Ableton, the tracks must be specially prepared, and this is not to everyone's liking, and besides, others believe that it‚Äôs too easy to reduce in Ableton and not to work. :)

Showdown with MIDI


First of all, we take on MIDI-OX. If the controller is not already on - it's time to turn it on. Turned on, the driver is loaded, the device in the system is excellent. We launch MIDI-OX. We do not get lost in the nondescript interface and immediately stamp in Options ‚Üí MIDI Devices. We select our device in MIDI Inputs. On the right, we see a list of objects where the signal from the BCD3000 will multiply and broadcast. Add to this list "Output to MIDI Yoke: 1" and "Output to MIDI Yoke: 2". We get this picture:



After these manipulations, MIDI-OX does not close!

Disassembly with audio


With MIDI cables sorted out. Now to the audio, go to the Control Panel from VAC. In the Driver parameters, select the number of cables (2) and click Set, the driver restarts. During the reboot, the sound may disappear in Winamp, for example - do not be alarmed, just click on Play again. In the parameters of each cable set:
  • SR - 44100 ... 48000
  • Connected Source Lines - Line


Set again, Exit.

Remember, the signal from the BCD3000 is multiplied on Yoke 1 and 2? From the first one (which is intended for the Tractor) we will receive a control signal for transport (Play / CUE, loupes, jogs, etc.). And through Yoke 3 we send MIDI Clock, the synchronizing signal for Appleton.

How to make a headphone output, aka pre-listen, aka CUE / MIX / MASTER?


This should be done in Ableton, but provided that there is not just a single-channel stereo card, but at least two channels. Alternatively, this card can be switched to multi-channel mode (Surrond or 5.1). Then we have in Ableton, in the Output Config panel an additional pair of outputs appears, 3/4 for example.
Switch Options ‚Üí Cue Switches, each audio track icon appears with headphones. By clicking on it, we can listen to the track through the 3/4 output, if we have headphones connected there, and attention, on the master track for Cue Out is 3/4. To the left of the master fader there is a switch that Solo most likely is now showing, so click on it to make a Cue, that's all.

Now you can hang everything on audio tracks in Ableton: filters, equalizers, some CamelSpace or glitch, the master can be equipped with oZone (if the machine allows it) and generally dispose of the sound as your heart desires. Cool? Cool. :)

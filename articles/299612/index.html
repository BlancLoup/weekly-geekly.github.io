Area Distortion


Another common way to confuse the viewer is by comparing numbers in the form of figures that have two dimensions. What is beautiful bar-chart? The bars in fact have one dimension, length, since the width of all bars in the diagram is the same. If instead of columns we use some two-dimensional (and even three-dimensional, not to the night be remembered) figures, then the comparative value of the presented numbers is not so obvious graphically. An inquisitive viewer will, at a minimum, reflect, corresponds to the number of the area or the linear size of the figure.

Hollywood made a feature film for YouTube



This Friday, the first Hollywood film made specifically for the Internet appeared on YouTube. We will not discuss the artistic value of such a tape, something else is important - big business has begun to try new types of content distribution, one of which is posting a film on YouTube and, possibly, other video services. By the way, not so long ago Warner Bros. made available to view one of her films on Facebook.

By the way, the film in question is not the debut low-budget film with unknown actors. It, for example, starred Denny DeVito, Carl Gugino and other actors. As for the plot, there is nothing particularly original here - the story of a group of people who visited over 10 different bars in Los Angeles overnight was shown. Perhaps someone will like the film, some - no, but making a film especially for the Web is still quite an important precedent. After all, now such a media as DVD is playing a lesser role, and the Internet is playing an ever increasing role.

In general, it would be very nice if other content providers picked up this initiative and began posting their films on YouTube, allowing them to be viewed without the need for payment.

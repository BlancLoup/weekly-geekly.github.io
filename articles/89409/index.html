IPod player maker finally broke with Apple

Many people remember the story of Tony Fadell, the creator of the iPod player and one of the iPhone developers who quit Apple a year and a half ago. Then the company promised to pay him $ 300,000 a year for silence and a promise not to work anywhere. As reported, the silence agreement was signed before March 24, 2010.

Since the term of the contract came to an end, the journalists asked the famous engineer about the development of the situation. In a telephone interview, he refused to discuss the situation with Apple (apparently, the NDA is still in effect), but said that he would now work as a consultant and venture investor. According to him, he wants to create other things besides the iPod and the iPhone.

The first introductory session of the AIP was held

On February 1, 2010, the Internet Developer Association held the first introductory session at the Microsoft office. The event was attended by about fifty people from twenty-six companies. The event showed that the issues raised by the AIR, are really relevant in the web environment. Moreover, besides the Moscow companies, the event was attended by studios and agencies from other cities of Russia: Ulyanovsk, Yekaterinburg, St. Petersburg and Novosibirsk. We would like to note that for the AIR it is extremely important that the questions raised by its participants find consonance in the minds of the largest possible number of colleagues in Russia and even abroad.

It was curious to observe how the heads of the web studios who came to the event did not get to know each other, started conversations and discussions, found common customers, jobs and other points of contact. Thus, not yet embarking on the event itself, we have already completed one of the tasks of the AIR. After all, AIR is nothing but a community of website developers and communication between them, knowing each other in person, this is one of our tasks.

We initially planned to limit the number of participants for a number of reasons: first and foremost, we are not trying to arrange a mass initiation rite, but on the contrary, we are trying to address the choice of the new members of the Association with an informed and balanced approach. The format chosen by us assumes not so much and not only the presentation of the AIR, but also the receipt of live feedback from you, the market participants. Therefore, we apologize to all those who did not receive an invitation to the first session and inform that a new tour will be organized in the near future, to which you will certainly be invited.

A new form is open for new participants on the AIR website, through which you can send an application for participation in subsequent sessions.

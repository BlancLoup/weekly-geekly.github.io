Strange, but the search for Habra did not give any mention of the book Chris Gilbo - Startup for $ 100 (in the original: Chris Guillebeau: The $ 100 Startup.
However, this can be explained by the desire of most habrizhiteli to stop working for someone and stir up the coolest startup that will soon turn into a megacorporation like Google, Facebook, Apple, or at least Microsoft.
Yes, no one agrees to anything less, and while waiting for investors to start giving millions for another brilliant idea, we continue to go to work, get tired of dull meetings and rake someone else's deadlines.

Chris Gilbo's book is not about that at all.

This book is about how to start living. How to throw away a boring and uninteresting job in the landfill and start doing what interests you and is helpful to others. All that is needed for this is to understand what you want from life, to finally start doing your favorite thing, to understand how you can earn money on it.

As the author writes, to create this book, he found one and a half thousand entrepreneurs, whose business brings> = 50 thousand dollars a year, but at the same time they started with very modest investments (often, the amounts did not exceed $ 100). Choosing the 50 most interesting entrepreneurs, Chris Gilbo structured their experience and described it in this book in the form of tips and step-by-step instructions.

The book does not discuss the creation of a large Internet startup; we are talking about creating a small business that does not make you a lot of money, but which will give you a very valuable thing - freedom.
Freedom not to stick to any schedule other than your own. Freedom not to make meaningless reports. Freedom not to participate in boring meetings.
This is a book about how to realize your dreams and earn money by doing what you are really interested in.

The book is interesting and easy to read. A hundred pages can be swallowed in one day or a couple of evenings.
Enjoy reading!

Conference ADD-3 convenes speakers

The third Application Developer Days conference will be held May 11-12 in Moscow. (Date moved)

ADD is a conference made by programmers for programmers. Not for project managers, not for testers, not for startups, but for application developers. For those who directly write code, thinks through the application architecture and tries to increase their productivity using new languages ‚Äã‚Äãand libraries.

Participants in past conferences know that these are not just words. Reports are really useful, and communication is actually fun. But only by giving a report you can really get everything from the conference.

Now the program is still being formed, so you have a great opportunity to speak at our conference. The sooner you send the abstracts of your report to the Program Committee, the more time you have to make it as good as possible. The advice of the program committee members is invaluable.

All reports are recorded on video and published online shortly after the conference. Thanks to this, thousands of people will be able to see a good performance. This is another reason to try and make an excellent report.

Our conference is for programmers. So the report should be about what interests programmers: programming languages, architectural techniques, libraries, development environments and other tools. Finally, an interesting report on the implemented project.

To submit abstracts, use the form on the " Presenters " page.

The Program Committee includes :
  • Stas Fomin - chairman of the PC, one of the best speakers of our time,
  • Dmitry Zavalishin - needs no introduction
  • Andrey Aksenov - author of the search engine Sphinx,
  • Lev Valkin - founder of the Practice of Functional Programming magazine,
  • Evgeny Kirpichev - a real expert in cluster computing
  • Sergey Kudryashov - UX-design guru,
  • Andrei Mayorov is embarrassed to write about himself :).
In conclusion - about the difficulties. If you have not read the memo to the speaker, written by Stas Fomin , then this must be done. The advice given there is truly invaluable.

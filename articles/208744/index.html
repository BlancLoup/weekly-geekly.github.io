Virgin Galactic conducted the third supersonic flight, reaching a new height



Virgin Galactic's SpaceShipTwo spacecraft reached a new height of 21.6 km during the third supersonic flight, which is 600 meters higher than during the last flight, writes The Verge. The ship returned safely to earth under the supervision of chief pilot Dave Mackay.

The mission was designed to check the thermal coverage on the tail of the vessel, as well as a reactive control system designed to control the spatial position of the ship and perform maneuvers in outer space.

Previously, Virgin Galactic promised to launch its first commercial space flights in 2014, and it has less than a year left to keep its promise. The company however is confident that it will be on time.

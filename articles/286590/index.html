For 2 years graduates of the Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology created the largest skype school in Russia

According to RBC, in just 2 years, Georgiy Soloviev and Khariton Matveyev created the largest online English language school in Russia. A high level of standardization of education was achieved, but for this it was necessary to take a risk and invest $ 260,000, which, of course, was not available to yesterday's graduates of MIPT.

It all started with the fact that George Solovyov once noticed the obvious thing: in smaller cities, individual English classes are much cheaper than in Moscow. So he came up with the idea to create a Skype-school of English, which in 2012 he offered to classmates: Khariton Matveyev, Denis Smetnev and Andrey Yaunzem.



At the initial stage, 400,000 rubles was invested by the State Fund for the Promotion of the Development of Small Businesses in their project.

When new investments were required, Soloviev and Matveyev turned to Alexander Laryanovsky, at that time Yandex International Development Director, who was then learning English. However, he was very skeptical about his Skype classes.

Alexander Laryanovsky invested $ 300,000 in the SkyEng project in October 2013.

Reflecting on the further development of the project, entrepreneurs noticed that traditional schools have good teaching methods, but a weak understanding of the integration of the educational process with IT. On the other hand, educational Internet projects with good design and progressive technologies pay little attention to the observance of teaching standards. It was decided to combine the advantages of these two types of schools in SkyEng.

This was how the virtual practice platform Vimbox was developed, the test version of which was launched last June. Investments that were required for its development, Laryanovsky estimates at $ 260,000.

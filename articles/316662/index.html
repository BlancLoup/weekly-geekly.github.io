New affiliate programs in the cloud business


On November 29, 2016, Amazon announced new programs for its partners, helping companies to develop cloud business. The online retailer hopes that thanks to innovations in collaboration schemes, organizations will be able to more easily access the Amazon Web Services (AWS) cloud infrastructure and maximize its capabilities. This focuses on working with certain products.

Here is a list of innovations.

AWS Service Delivery Program


Amazon partners with extensive experience with certain AWS services can join AWS Service Delivery to promote specific solutions, such as Amazon Aurora, Lambda, DynamoDB, and GovCloud. For example, if a company decides to transfer its database to Aurora, it will be able to find an Amazon partner with a certificate for implementing and maintaining this product.

VMware Cloud on AWS Partner Program


In November 2016, Amazon and VMware announced the development of a service that will allow companies to deploy workloads in the public cloud using software-defined data centers in the same way that they used local software. The VMware Cloud on AWS Partner program is designed to help partners make money on this service.

Alexa Service Delivery Program


This affiliate program is designed to work with Amazon companies to integrate Alexa voice assistant with their products and services. By the end of November 2016, over 3,000 functions of Alexa were developed by AWS partners, said Terry Wise, vice president of partner development for AWS, at re: Invent 2016 in Las Vegas.



AWS Government Partner Program


The goal of this partnership program, formerly known as the AWS Public Sector Partner Program, is to promote cloud services in the public sector, schools, and non-profit organizations.

Road to the clouds


Amazon decided to organize a shipping service for the data. AWS Snowmobile are tractors that can transport up to 100 petabytes of data between data centers. This should reinforce the trend of transferring information from ordinary data centers to the "cloud".


The data warehouse is located in a protected body measuring 13 meters in length and 3 meters in height. It can connect to the data center network, appearing as a new hard disk, to which data can be transferred at a speed of up to one terabit per second.

For one AWS Snowmobile, 350 kW is required. Amazon is ready to provide customers with its own electric generator, as well as security for the truck.

The company allows customers to order multiple trucks at the same time. Amazon hopes that the entire data center can be moved in a few weeks.
Several companies that store more exabyte (1024 petabyte) of data have already expressed their desire to ‚Äúmove‚Äù to the Amazon Web Services ‚Äúcloud‚Äù. Transferring an exabyte via the Internet can take about 26 years even when using an ultra-fast connection with a speed of 10 gigabits per second.

The company noted that ordering trucks would be cheaper than transferring data over the Internet. However, while no price Amazon has not formed. At first, prices will be set individually.

In 2015, Amazon launched the AWS Snowball project to quickly transfer data using a single device. The bottom line is that the company sends the client a hard disk of 80 terabytes with pre-installed data encryption and data protection, which can be sent to the company for downloading to the cloud.

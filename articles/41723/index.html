A bit of history

The reason why American beer did not become as widespread in the world as Dutch, German or Mexican is banal. BBB American beer was popular in the world and had a rich history. But the fact is that American breweries were based on German recipes and specialists, who had to be deported during World War II, and the factories were dismantled.

From the beginning of the 50s, American beer began to be reborn in small home breweries, while European traditional brands were already generously exported abroad.

Sport and beer:

Also in America there is a purely national sport - Beer Pong. From the word Ping Pong, just a beer :)

They play two pairs on each side of the table. At the edges of the table are placed plastic glasses, half filled with beer, in the form of a triangle in billiards. The rivals take turns throwing the ping-pong ball into the opponent's glasses. If clogged, the beer is drunk by the losing side and the glass is removed. It may seem that the losers drink more alcohol, so what's the point to win? But the fact is that the winners still drink their glasses at the end of the game. And they remain to play with the following applicants! :)

Beer Pong is played mostly by students, but there is a championship at the national level.

Nokia or MeeGo tablet on the march

I'll start from afar. Nokia planned to break into the market of laptops, netbooks and other devices with its Maemo operating system, this solution was created for various devices, but they started with the N800 / N810 tablets and then expanded the functionality to the N900 phone. At some point, the company realized that they could not develop this area independently and joined Intel in order to participate in the MeeGo project (based on Moblin), but the Nokia mobile interface (Maemo 6, to be precise) is retained for mobile phones. As a general partner of MeeGo, the company wants to be at the head of this process. Therefore, in mid-September in London, it will probably show not only the N9 (the first MeeGo phone), but also its tablet.

In the hands of this toy is not yet held, but as soon as I get out of my resort, I will gladly twist it. In the meantime, I will share what I know, heard, saw in the pictures, and at the same time those videos that have been posted online today.

So let's get down to the tablet.



The Tablet UX interface is shown in the video; this is a pre-alpha version that can show you what the system is capable of.

I do not know whether they did it on purpose or not, but the metal edging on the body, rather bright colors. Complete opposite. They say that about 100 prototypes have already been manufactured and the screen diagonal can be 7 or 9 inches. About the model with a small screen diagonal, I do not know anything. Only 9 inches.

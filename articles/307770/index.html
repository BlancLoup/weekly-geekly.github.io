Secrets of cloud PBX: a few words about recording conversations

Cloud PBXs are more accessible today than ever - you can find any tariff to your taste with any number of options. But as for recording conversations, you will almost always see that it is ‚Äúincluded in the kit.‚Äù Today we open the veil of secrecy and tell what it means in practice.

After a lengthy conversation, we came to the conclusion that the time has come for such a post, in which we analyze the completeness of the conversation recording functionality and do not even hesitate to mention in comparison our direct competitors.

So what's important in the case of a recording? It seems to us that there are three areas to be highlighted - this is the available capacity, cost and convenience of working with the system. But before we start discussing existing offers on the market, let's talk about what is common to most call recording systems.

Common features


After a brief analysis of the market, we came to the conclusion that for most virtual PBXs, the recording function has the option of spot tuning - that is, you can decide for yourself from which numbers you need to record conversations, and from which numbers you do not. Similarly, the settings of the recording time parameters take place through the personal account, and this functionality is almost the same.

Also, you will not find anywhere else to adjust the density of compression. As a rule, operators prefer to save conversations exactly in the codec in which the conversation took place, and the sound quality is identical to the quality during a call.

Finally, no one yet offers speech analytics services, since they require a lot of processing power and will be quite expensive. Neither we nor other companies are doing this, but not because no one can, but because so far there is no demand for such a service as part of the service of an available cloud PBX.

Differences


But now let's talk about the differences, and then it will not work without going "on the individual." We ask our competitors not to be offended by the facts, and if any of our data is outdated, just report it.

The first is completeness. Some operators include some call recording services, and they are very different from each other. The second is the capacity . It is far from always that free recording offers really allow you to save conversations. For example, "MangoOffice" allows you to record about 500 minutes, and "Zadarma" - 200 Megabytes. Agree, this is not the volume that is needed to control conversations.

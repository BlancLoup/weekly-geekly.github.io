App Preview Recommendations


With the release of iOS 8, developers have the opportunity to add video games to the App Store page. The clip will be located on the site of the first screenshot and will be one of the first elements that the player will see on the application page.

The video will be the main argument why the player should download your application. For 15-30 seconds you will need to show the contents of the application and its features. Not an easy task. To help solve it well, we have compiled a list of all Apple recommendations and requirements:

The video can be removed from your device on iOS 8. To do this, you need to connect it to a computer with OS X Yosemite via a Lightning cable. After that, your device will work as a camera, recording everything that happens on the screen.

  • The video should be made separately for the iPhone and separately for the iPad version, by analogy with the screenshots.
  • It is possible to make one set of ipad / iphone / ipod videos for each localization separately.
  • No need to make videos with people who interact with your application.
  • No need to put moving hands on top of the video to display navigation and multi-touch gestures.
  • No need to use zoom.
  • You must ensure that the transitions between scenes in the application actually existed.
  • There is no need to add a price to the video. It is already displayed on the application page, and this information will be redundant.
  • When adding text, keep in mind that it should be readable. And if you made only one localization, and the game is available worldwide, then the value of this text is lost.
  • Over the video, you can add music and sound effects to actions with user interfaces.

Apple has updated IDFA guidelines for mobile applications: new working conditions with HealthKit


A minute of repetition: IDFA is a device identifier that allows you to uniquely identify a user. It was entered after the cancellation of the UID, so that ad networks could distinguish users from each other, it is also used to collect statistics.

The guys in Cupertino decided that it was enough just to collect unique identifiers. Now you can use IDFA only if you are clearly showing ads in your application. Otherwise, this may cause a failure to accept the application in the App Store!

The changes also affected the new HealthKit Guideline. Developers are prohibited from transferring medical data from the device to advertising networks. Medical information can still be passed on to third parties for research purposes, if the developers explicitly warn the user about this. This allows doctors, staff of medical centers and even insurance companies to give this information so that they can warn users about a dramatic change in their state of health. The future is here!

Biz Stone left Twitter to work on new projects



Twitter co-founder Biz Stone announced that he would retire from the company and move on to work on several new projects.

Stone will again work with Twitter co-founder Ev Williams on projects under the auspices of the revived Obvious Corporation - an organization that Williams founded in the mid -2000s in order to assemble several projects in one company. It was inside Obvious that Twitter itself was born, later separated into a separate company.

Stone and Williams will be joined by Jason Goldman, another Twitter leader. He was the vice president of the company until he retired last December.

Williams himself resigned as CEO of Twitter at the end of last year. Current CEO Dick Costolo will remain at the helm of the company.

Stone wrote on his blog that he would still help Twitter when and where he could. But most of his time he will spend on new beginnings.

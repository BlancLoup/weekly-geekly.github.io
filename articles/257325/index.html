MIPSfpga from Imagination Technologies for Russian universities

Imagination Technologies, widely known as the developer of the PowerVR graphics processor inside the Apple iPhone, has begun free licensing for universities of the modern industrial processor core of the MIPS architecture with source codes in the Verilog hardware description language. The new product, called MIPSfpga, will improve communication with the electronics industry for students studying computer architecture and systems design on a chip.

The processor core used in MIPSfpga is the basic configuration of the industrial core MIPS microAptiv UP, which is installed, in particular, in the Microchip PIC32MZ microcontroller. Students get the opportunity to experiment in building systems on a chip using FPGAs from Xilinx and Altera. Examples of experiments are the creation of multi-core systems, the addition of new commands to the processor, and the creation of new cache architectures.

In an official press release published by Imagination Technologies, the MIPSfpga product was highly appreciated by Stanford University President John Hengnessy and professors from other leading world universities. Some experts have already experienced MIPSfpga in practice, among them representatives of such educational institutions as Imperial College London and University College London. Professors Keio, the oldest in Japan, the prestigious Chinese universities of Shanghai Jiao Tong University and Tsinghua University spoke about the product.

Now Russian universities are connected to work with MIPSfpga. For them, the Imagination initiative provides an opportunity for the rapid expansion of domestic experts in the global chip design industry. If the result of experimentation at a university is successful, developers can attract investment, purchase a commercial license for the MIPS microAptiv UP or MIPS M5150 core, and make an ASIC chip in a factory.

Who will you meet at our seminar? First of all, with David and Sarah Harris, the authors of the Digital Circuit Design and Computer Architecture, a free edition in Russian (sponsored by Imagination Technologies) should appear from day to day. David and Sarah Harris prepared the MIPSfpga package, working closely with several engineering teams of Imagination Technologies.


David Harris studied at Stanford and MIT, after which he worked at Intel as a developer of Pentium II and Itanium processors. After this, David was a consultant at Sun Microsystems, Hewlett-Packard and other companies. Now David Harris is a professor at Harvey Mudd College. David is the author of not only books on the development of microcircuits, but also books on mountain tourism in southern California.

Sarah Harris studied at Stanford and Brigham Young University, after which she worked at Hewlett-Packard, the supercomputer center in San Diego, Nvidia, and Microsoft Research in Beijing. Sarah is currently working at the University of Nevada in Las Vegas.

To health! Wearable devices in sports and medicine

Among technological trends for 2014, in addition to smart watches and other things, an increase in the use of wearable devices in sports, health monitoring and medicine is distinguished. These devices help to count calories burned and force the host to move the fifth point in the direction of a better life, tell the doctor about increased sugar and pressure problems, and have other useful functions.

In the post - which devices we already have or will appear in the near future, divided by me into several categories.

Other devices

In 2004, Nike and Apple teamed up to create a sensor for runners: the Wi-Fi module could transfer basic data about the athlete's location to the trainer's computer. A little later, the Nike kit for iPod sensor appeared: it consists of a motion sensor, an iPod receiver and special sneakers. Allows you to share the results, organize training according to an individual plan.



In X, Google is developing a lens that will measure glucose . A useful thing for people with diabetes is that they do not have to pierce their finger all the time.



At CES 2014, Intel introduced headphones with a built-in heart rate monitor. They can choose music tracks depending on the heart rate of the owner. You can set a target heart rate for training - then they will pick up the right music.



Pedometers grew up, they grew up Bluetooth (it's better with Bluetooth!), They learned how to count not only steps, but also calories and began to show results on the smartphone screen. In the photo - Shine fitness button.



Scanadu Scout is one of the devices expected in 2014. By applying a miniature sensor to different parts of the body, you can find out about yourself almost everything you need to do sports and monitor your health: pulse, temperature, oxygen level in blood, pressure, respiration rate, ECG and level of emotional stress. Instead of the required $ 100,000, the project has already collected 1.6 million at indiegogo.com - so deliveries should begin in March.



And this TellSpec laser scanner will tell you what your food consists of. Direct it to the dish, and it will give you a list of ingredients that you will see in the app on your smartphone. When you open any ingredient application will give information about it. The product has already collected more than 380 thousand dollars at Indiegogo .

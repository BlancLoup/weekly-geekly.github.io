Puffer and the gifts of "Life"



Many programmers, at least of my generation, know the game "Life", the rules of which were proposed by the British mathematician John Conway in 1970. But what few people know is that it is still actively developing and pleasing new discoveries. The story of one of these discoveries, I want to share in this article.

In the past 2014, a young British mathematician, programmer and life-jiust (lifenthusiast) Adam Goucher wrote apgsearch , a program for the census of objects developing from random initial configurations (or "soups", soup < soup < primordial soup < primary broth ). The very idea of ‚Äã‚Äãsuch a program is not new, but Adam managed, firstly, to create an efficient algorithm for object census, and, secondly, to make the program convenient and accessible, thanks to the implementation of the most popular and advanced program for today in the form of a Python script for Golly for the game "Life".

Just a couple of months later, another life forceist, Richard Schank, previously unknown in the community and not famous for anything, slightly changed the program code for studying symmetrical soups, found a new locomotive (puffer) - a moving object that leaves a trace in other objects. To say that this discovery tore all the patterns to shreds - to say almost nothing. After all, the previous two natural (i.e., arising from the soup) locomotive were discovered by Charles Corderman back in 1971!

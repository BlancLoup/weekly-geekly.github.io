Testing


Testing was carried out with the help of several utilities from PlayMarket: Battery Drain is an interesting utility that shows battery consumption every hour and also allows you to collect statistics on battery usage in the form of "percentages / hours"; Battery Dr.Saver - allows you to carry out the cycle of full battery charging, and also allows you to detect the time spent on charging; Battery HD - allows you to calibrate the battery and shows preliminary battery usage figures (unfortunately, not always accurate); and of course, Battery Monitor Widget is a comprehensive tool that combines all previous tools.
Testing was also conducted in Android 2.3.6 Gingerbread and Android 4.0.3 Ice Cream Sandwich (its output for the Galaxy Note just coincided with the writing of the post).
During testing, 2 unpleasant incidents happened: 1) after switching to Android 4.0.3, the battery began to ‚Äúeat‚Äù, like a rabid one, the increased battery lasted a maximum of 4-5 hours, standard and even less, only battery calibration helped me; 2) for a month of testing it turned out that some data was irretrievably lost both from Dropbox and from the phone, so some of the indicators for the original battery may be missing, mainly for Android 2.3.6.

Speaking in dry numbers, here they are:
  1. during standard testing (the usual day of using the phone, all the time Wi-Fi and 3G turned on, the reader and the music played - about an hour, half an hour playing time for a child, all the time included 2 monitoring systems, web surfing - about an hour) battery consumption was about 4% -6% per hour.





    As we can see, the increase is almost 2 times, which means that wherever I am - I have about 36-40 hours without recharging, without limiting myself in using the phone. (there was a feeling that under Android 4.0.3 I violated the testing conditions a bit, the data is smaller than I expected)
  2. during intensive testing (everything is the same as on a normal day + GPS and Bluetooth are on all the time, using the camera in photo and video shooting) battery consumption was about 12% -14% per hour.

    4700 mAh - Android 2.3.6



    No wonder, except that the original numbers are smaller, but this once again confirms the capacity of the enlarged battery.
  3. during extreme testing (the AnTuTu Tester and Battery Killer programs were used) the battery consumption was about 21% -42% per hour.

    4700 mAh - Android 2.3.6


    The purpose of the last test was to identify the maximum performance of the phone and the battery charge, but for some reason I did not think about the time at which he would be able to withstand such work. And since the testing was carried out at night, data on the operating time was not preserved and the result had to be put in ‚Äúparrots‚Äù from AnTuTu Tester. One thing I can say is that the original battery lasted for 3-4 hours, the increased one - by 7-8 (take my word for it).

Battery charge time


I would also like to mention the battery charge time:

Electromagnetic fields in the workplace

I think there will be units of users of different household appliances who do not know that any equipment connected to the ordinary household electrical network ~ 220V 50Hz is a source of electromagnetic field (EMF). Yes, there is an EMF, but few know if it exceeds the maximum permissible norms (PDN) or not. I am an employee of a laboratory in an organization that certifies working places according to working conditions, perhaps many have heard that it was carried out by someone. In the last couple of years, when I was allowed to take measurements, I saw many jobs. Somewhere great, somewhere horrible. At the request of workers, I will tell about some results of measurement of EMF. I‚Äôll just make a reservation that I am not a physicist by education and I don‚Äôt know the details of the EMF, but I still have a technical education.

So, the measuring instrument: The meter of parameters of electric and magnetic fields "BE-meter-AT-002" is not a super accurate device. The device allows simultaneous measurements of the electrical and magnetic components of the electromagnetic field in two frequency bands: from 5 Hz to 2 kHz and from 2 kHz to 400 kHz. The document, which shows the PDN when working on the computer SanPiN 2.2.2 / 2.4.1340-03 .

In theory, if household appliances are grounded, the EMI readings should be in accordance with PD. In practice, it is in most cases the case. But even with grounding, there are exceptions.

Example 1


We have a ground loop throughout the building. Each office has two or three computers. When we started to measure, we immediately noticed that the readings in general fit the PDN, but are, so to speak, on the verge. In some workplaces, individual indicators exceeded two or even three times. It was not immediately clear what was the matter. Each computer is connected through an uninterruptible power supply, some bespereboyniki were connected to the network through extension cords (Pilots). In some workplaces, the number of extenders reached three pieces))). The bespereboyniki themselves were mainly located under the feet of the workers, and where on the system unit itself. In the beginning, they got rid of the extension cord, the readings did not change. We decided to try to connect a computer to bypass the bespereboynika and Oh miracle, the readings are normal. Example 2


The same organization is also a building. In many cabinets, in order to brighten up the everyday life of the workers, there were unpretentious FM radio receivers with power supply, a power cord without grounding. Some stood at a distance from computers, some stood on the desktop, next to the monitor. Having worked for some time on measurements, you are already gaining experience and, with any deviations, you begin to check the connection, look for current consumers without grounding. So turning off the receiver, the readings returned to normal. Another interesting case with the receiver in the same place. The radio itself was about two meters away from the computer. It is not clear to me how the electromagnetic fields were distributed, but at a distance of two meters the readings were twice as large. Repeated measurements three times and without changes. Turning off the radio, the readings returned to normal.

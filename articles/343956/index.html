How to calculate ROI when deploying Office 365 using a ROI calculator

Here, colleagues tried to answer the question: What is Microsoft Office 365 and how can it help the company? - what caused the reader's comment:

Nothing is clear. How much does it cost to deploy this product? The advantages are more or less clear. How many accounts does it have, what are the risks? At least some numbers would have led.

We would like to give some numbers


Of course, it is not Microsoft‚Äôs business to count all the numbers for a specific client; But the numbers depend on many other inputs that the integrator will request from the client, so if you try to simply buy a package of products on www.office.com , you will be asked for the number of licenses. And the client will be left alone with the amount that needs to be commented and justified: what will it bring to the company? cost optimization - what exactly is this optimization expressed in which numbers? How profitable is it in the long run?



When modeling our calculator to calculate ROI for Office 365, we tried to avoid extremes: ask the user a million questions, or just silently count the number of licenses in different packages. When filling out the form, the client is asked specific questions that are as close as possible to the business objectives, they are asked the specific features that are needed, and on this basis it is decided which position to choose.

Since, in reality, it is difficult for an inexperienced user to understand the licensing scheme of MS Office. There are 2 separate lines - Business and Enterpise, in which different packages, different sets of functions ( E1, E3, E5 ), and it‚Äôs not easy to choose what is better in a particular case, not to mention the intricacies of implementation . We didn‚Äôt take into account not all the factors that may affect the choice - otherwise the form would become too bloated (there will be a million questions), but we tried to choose only those that we consider to be key.

Parameters for calculating ROI:

  • Number of users
  • Initial decision costs
  • The average amount of additional costs to maintain the decision, USD / month.
  • Forecast period
  • Average salary of operating personnel
  • The average salary of technical staff
  • The percentage of employees whose work is related to the creation and viewing of documentation
  • The percentage of employees whose work requires regular participation in conference calls
  • Percentage of decision makers based on business intelligence data
  • Percentage of employees working with mobile devices
  • Company monthly expenses for mobile communication, international and long-distance calls
  • Number of information security incidents per month
  • How many times a month is it necessary to repair or replace the PC?
  • Implementation risk (There are 2 risks: the risk of implementation is the probability of successful implementation of the project, depending on the competence of the implementers, support of your management, staff qualifications, etc.
  • Discount rate (most often use the bank interest rate on deposits. After all, the project should be more profitable than a bank deposit)

Parameters for selecting a package and drafting specifications, you need to specify the number of users and the period of use, as well as the need for key features, depending on which package will be selected:

  • SharePoint Online
  • Power BI service
  • Desktop MS Office Applications
  • Business Email Hosting
  • Skype for Business Online
  • Azure Rights Management (RMS)
  • Connect to Skype conferences from your phone

So, our ROI calculator for Office 365

What is the calculator model built on?


The ROI model of our calculator is built largely on the results of a study by Forrester Consulting , commissioned by Microsoft. The aim of the study was to determine the overall economic effect and potential return on investment in enterprises as a result of the implementation of Office 365 E5.

Fifty organizations were examined that met the following criteria:

  • they all used office 365 e5
  • headquartered in North America or the UK
  • number of employees - at least 2000
  • most common industries: corporate and private sector services, technology, manufacturing, financial services, government

Sources of profit from the implementation:

  • Saving time employees of the company
  • Employees whose work involves creating and viewing documents saved an average of 2 hours per week.
  • Employees whose work requires regular participation in conference calls saved 1.5 business hours per week.
  • Employees working from mobile devices saved about 2 hours and 15 minutes a week.
  • Employees making decisions based on business intelligence data saved 4 hours 45 minutes a week using Power BI Pro and MyAnalytics
  • Reducing the number of information security incidents by 50% reduces the time spent by IT-specialists to eliminate them
  • Reducing the cost of telephone calls. The cost of mobile communications, international and long-distance calls decreased by 14.2%
  • Reduce downtime when replacing or repairing a PC. Previously, if necessary, repair or replace a working computer was lost an average of 3.5 hours of working time for each such incident. Microsoft Office 365 allowed employees to work from another computer in the office or from home while their primary PC worker is being repaired or replaced.

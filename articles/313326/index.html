Game industry: useful materials for game development from A to Z

Recently, we often come across questions about how to get into the gaming industry, what useful materials on this topic can be read and see where to start learning gamedev. When initial knowledge appears, more specific questions arise, such as "where to find like-minded people to create the game," "how to promote your project with a minimum budget", "which publishers should go out, how to do it and whether it is worth it at all", "how improve retension in our game "and so on.

A friend of our educational programs, Mikhail Pimenov, the CEO of Wonder Games and Team Lead of the indie-project " Guard of Wonderland ", has repeatedly asked himself these questions, created for himself a selection of all sorts of materials on the game-dev industry. From useful resources, to communities, podcasts, books and films , which are designed to help understand what begins and ends with the development of a game project. By combining our forces, Misha and I made this article with a selection of useful materials on the gaming industry. You will find a selection under the cut.



In order to combine all the accumulated materials into one structure, we decided to divide the Article into several sections. In the "introduction" you will find materials for beginning game developers. In the "Development" section - resources with free content. And then each of the sub-items is devoted to its subject matter. DevGAMM is the largest conference for gaming industry professionals in Russia, Belarus and Europe. Advantages of DevGAMM: high-quality content on game development, a low entry threshold for novice developers, and a series of events aimed at productive pastime and effective networking.

White Nights Conference is an international business conference dedicated to the development and promotion of games on all platforms. It takes place three times a year: in February in Europe, in June in St. Petersburg, in October in Moscow. Total for the year, White Nights collects more than 4,000 professionals in the gaming industry.

Game Developers Conference (GDC) is the largest annual gathering of professional computer game developers with a focus on learning and inspiration. The event includes exhibits, networking events, award ceremonies such as the Independent Games Festival and the Game Developers Choice Awards , as well as various consultations, lectures and round tables on the areas of game developers covering programming, design, sound, business and management, and visual art.

NextCastle - Annual festival for gamers and developers. An excellent chance for indie developers to show the game to the gaming audience and get feedback on the project.

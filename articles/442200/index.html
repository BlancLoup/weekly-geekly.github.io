Moonlight mission "Bereshit" - engineers at the SpaceIL and Israel Aerospace Industries (IAI) MCC solved problems



For two days there was no news of the status of the Bereshit mission. And now, after solving two problems that appeared in the Bereshit apparatus in the first days of its long journey to the Moon, the SpaceIL team continues to manage the systems onboard the Bereshit apparatus in a regular mode.

In continuation of this publication .

What were the problems with the Bereshit apparatus?

First problem:



Sunlight apparatus position sensors of the sun (the sensors have been very sensitive to such "blinding"), which may affect the orientation of the apparatus in space.

Solution: software compensation was performed to process the data from the sensors and reduce their sensitivity; additional repeated checks of new data from the sensors of the device were made.

The second problem February 26, 2019 :



At the preparatory stage, before performing the second engine maneuver, the onboard computer of the Bereshit device unexpectedly rebooted, and the maneuver phase was automatically canceled. SpaceIL and IAI engineers began to analyze the situation.
On board, there was a problem that limited the maneuverability of the device.

To find and fix the problem, they had a space deadline of 1-2 days , if during this time it was possible to rectify the situation, it would not affect the estimated date of disembarkation of April 11, 2019.

The second maneuver with the inclusion of the engine apparatus had to be postponed until the problem was solved.

Solution February 28, 2019: SpaceIL and IAI engineers have eliminated the failure in the computer system of the Bereshit apparatus, now the Bereshit apparatus continues its mission to the Moon in normal mode.

If during the first maneuver, the engine was turned on for 30 seconds, now for the second maneuver on February 28, 2019 and to compensate for the loss of time due to a failure earlier, the main engine was activated for 4 minutes to reach an elliptical orbit with an apogee of 131,000 km .

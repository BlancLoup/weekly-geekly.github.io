Working standards in the web development market is a question for developers, not for the state.

Talking about the relationship between the state and the Internet in our country is endless. Sometimes it seems that these things are not related. However, there are people who are concerned not only with the transparency and civilization of the market, but also with high-quality government relations on it. Today we talked with Alena Popova, the initiator of a large number of projects on cooperation between the Web and government agencies.

- Alena, hello, tell me, how do you see the current situation in the web development market?
- There are teams, there are projects, there is interest. There is no single entry point for such initiatives. In fact, you need to understand what kind of development and where it will be in demand. For example, Ivan Begtin, an expert on open data, constantly writes which projects would be in demand in gov2.0.
World experience is this: to reduce costs and greater efficiency, governments of different countries hold targeted competitions for developing applications based on open government data. We will soon announce a similar initiative in Russia. I believe that this will be efficient and logical.

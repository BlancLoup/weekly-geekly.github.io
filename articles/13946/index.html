The first Starbucks has opened in Russia



As the readers of the Lebedev blog have already noticed , a remarkable event happened in Russia: the first Starbucks coffee house in the country opened. This news is actively discussed in blogs , including abroad . The place for the first Starbucks was the Mega hypermarket behind the ring road, where Muscovites like to go on weekends.

Russia became the 43rd country where an institution of this type was opened. Note that this event occurred with a delay of nine years. The company planned to open a coffee house here in 1998, but due to the crisis it was forced to change plans.

Coffee is brewed in exactly the same varieties as in America (the price of one serving is up to 230 rubles), but sandwiches and other snacks are adapted to Russian tastes (for example, the menu included a cheese sandwich). Will Starbucks be able to change Russia's coffee culture? By the end of this year, the second Starbucks will open on the Old Arbat.

In America, Starbucks has become a cult establishment, the number of coffee shops there has already exceeded 20,000. In Moscow, one coffee shop accounts for 3187 people, for comparison, in New York - by 365, and in Paris - by 126.

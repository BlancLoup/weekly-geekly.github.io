Private Ubuntu Transition Experience

Not so long ago, my transition to Linux ended. I installed myself a Ubuntu distribution. The process was difficult and took a lot of time and effort, despite the fact that I found the answers to each question on the Internet very quickly.

I started my transition by collecting all my software needs in the form of mind maps and articles asking for social assistance in selecting software . I have a few Linux terms among my friends, and there are no friends I could ask for something about. Thanks to everyone who responded, your help is invaluable! In gratitude to those who responded and to help other defectors :) I decided to share my transition experience.

The turning point was the need to switch to a new laptop. That is, I had the opportunity to work in win as before and suffer from installing different distributions, choosing drivers and software on a new laptop - which is very convenient, because I did not have to overload the laptop as if I had installed the OS on the next partition. I immediately dealt with my native equipment, which I would have been deprived of, working in a virtual environment.

the Internet

If you are the owner of a connection through a twisted pair or wifi and you have an intel card - you most likely will not have problems, you can immediately proceed to the next paragraph. I needed to connect a usb 3g modem from ukrainian people.net and set up a connection .

Throughout 2010, manufacturers of other brands pored over the counterstroke and built dissimilar plates. Most of all, of course, the Chinese brothers succeeded with their cheap handicrafts. Terrible quality, a couple of hours of battery life - all this did not inspire among geeks.

But the device that replaces the e-book, player, PDA, Internet viewer, the simplest netbook and game console - this is a coveted device! By the way, with the advent of tablets in such quantity and quality, netbooks began to die as a class.
Koreans, namely Samsung, did not suffer for a long time and released their answer: 7 inch Samsung P1000, which is still in demand. Other companies, one after another, began to announce clones of something very similar to the Motorola Xoom and Apple iPad.
Among the monotonous design clones, Lenovo managed to stand out (by the way, it did not release its product for sale), as well as Acer, with its brainchild Iconia. At low cost, the Iconia Tab A500 had a 10 inch multi-touch display, metal case, MVA display, usb host, and a good battery (8 hours of watching videos online).
But for permanent work, 10 inches, in my opinion, is too big. Samsung p1000 bribed people with the fact that it can be "put in your pocket." 7 inches is a separate form factor. Already not a phone, but also not a tablet.
In this review, I want to introduce you to the 7 inch brother Acer A500 - Acer Iconia Tab A100.

Acer Iconia Tab A100

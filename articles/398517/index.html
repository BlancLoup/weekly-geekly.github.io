Stupid house

What is the difference between a stupid house and an ordinary house? An ordinary house does not know how to do anything, but a stupid house can do something, but it does something ‚Äúwrong‚Äù, not like a real smart house. Marketing algorithm


Step 1 . Take the usual device
Step 2 . We add there sensors, the possibility of remote control, we connect it to the Internet (we try not to ask ourselves the question "why?").
Step 3 . We cling to him the label "smart". We screw up a proprietary data exchange protocol that is incompatible with anything else and is thrown onto the market.
Step 4a . If the device took off - we did everything right.
Step 4b . If the device fails - we say that the world is simply not yet ready for our smart toothpicks for $ 600.

I am sure that if we collect hundreds of marketers and evangelists of the Internet of Things and ask them to reveal what they mean by this term, then we will have a hundred different definitions. It would seem that here it is, the technology of our dreams. Instead of expensive and monolithic industrial complex from a real smart home, we have many small, relatively cheap and relatively independent things. Unfortunately, the overall picture is not so rosy.

We will not discuss the pricing of "smart" items, let's go straight to the question: "why are they needed?".

It is no secret that most people on the planet do not have the desire and (or) the ability to redo all the engineering networks in a house or apartment every few years, keeping pace with the new products. It seems to be all right, a niche was found, with the goals sorted out. But, as is usually the case, distortions begin. Marketing sharply forgets that the purpose of many clever things for the house is to correct the shortcomings of existing communications with less blood and begins to squeal that these things are indispensable and most necessary in the house. To listen to them, it turns out that a smart light bulb is not one of the budget ways to organize light scenarios in the room, but the only one and the best.

Another imbalance is the ignoring of the fact that most smart appliances have not yet got out of the scale of one room.

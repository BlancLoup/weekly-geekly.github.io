Family Nexus

On October 12, official sales of the Google Nexus 7 tablet, the first 7-inch device running on a 4-core processor and delivered to stores with a pre-installed version of Android 4.1 Jelly Bean, started in Russia. I was one of the first to get a novelty on the test. Despite my alleged dislike of Android, I really liked the device, and Jelly Bean especially.





It looks like 7-inch tablets are becoming the standard for a minimal, pocket-sized tablet. Some time ago it seemed that the standard would be the device, most likely with a 5 or 5.5 inch display, like the Samsung Galaxy Note. But first, Samsung itself released several tablets with 7-inch displays, then BlackBerry curbed the same diagonal, then Huawei merged with this trend. In fact, not only these manufacturers chose exactly 7 inches for their mini-tablet models, but most importantly, now Apple is also going to finally release an iPad mini with a display diagonal of just 7 inches, thereby securing a new standard.



In this series, the release of the joint 7-inch tablet by Google and Asus looks completely natural. Google Nexus 7 by Asus (that is the name of this device, branded by Google and only manufactured by Asus) was introduced in June of this year and has already been sold in the USA and Europe for several months. Naturally, gray supplies leaked to Russia. Moreover, if in the States and Europe, the 8 GB version costs $ 200, and 16 GB costs $ 250, then according to Yandex.Market, 8 GB ‚Äúgray‚Äù Nexus 7 costs from 9.5 tons, and 16 GB version - from 12 tons . However, there is every reason to believe that now, with the advent of a certified device at a recommended price of 9,990 rubles. change and the overall price scenario for this device.



Immediately I will say, in order not to return to this topic, that in addition to a more adequate price and warranty from the manufacturer, the certified Nexus 7 has one more advantage. The fact is that after the device arrived at the stores in the USA and Europe, there appeared user feedback on sensor and display marriage . Problems were recognized by manufacturers and corrected in subsequent batches. So, the Russian, certified Nexus 7 is already from those parties in which the problems have been fixed. The sample I tested had no problems with the responsiveness of the sensor, as well as with the rest of the hardware and software. So, I strongly recommend taking exactly the "white" version of the device.



Now I propose to consider a closer device itself. So, we have a 7-inch tablet with a TFT IPS-display with a resolution of 1280x800. The number of pixels per inch is 216, which is not so much, however, the image on the screen looks very clear. Viewing angles are excellent at all - the image on the display does not deteriorate and does not fade even if you look at it at an angle of 180 degrees. As a protective coating for the display, not the familiar Gorilla Glass was used here, but another development of the same company, called Corning Fit, which is primarily distinguished by greater transparency. Perhaps due to this, the image on the display looks really very clear.

Above the display is a 1.2 megapixel front camera for video telephony. The main camera is not here at all, which, in principle, may even be better than having a very average device, for example, a 3 megapixel camera, which is clearly incomprehensible for what will fit, and the device makes it more expensive.



The back, non-removable cover is made of rubberized and textured plastic, which is very well reflected in the fixability of the tablet in your hand, and just nice to the touch. Tablet thickness 11 mm. Side faces slightly rounded. On the right side of the side, in the upper part there is an on / off button, it is also a screen lock / unlock button and a volume rocker. At the bottom there is a micro-USB connector and a 3.5 mm headphone or headset jack. At first glance, that's all the connectors, but in fact, not all. On the left side there are contacts for audio-doc, - a certain docking station, either with built-in speakers, or with the ability to connect speakers to it. In short, these contacts are for audio peripheral equipment that is not yet on sale.



Now a few words about the hardware stuffing. The heart of this tablet is the 4 + 1 nuclear processor NVIDIA Tegra-3, which has already managed to adequately prove itself in many flagship devices from different manufacturers, including Asus. RAM - 1 GB. Flash memory - 16 GB. In the USA and Europe, the 8 GB version is also being sold, but it will not be officially sold here. As for the performance of all this stuffing, both AnTuTu and Quadrant gave him solid bronze: AnTuTu - after the Samsung Galaxy Note 2 and HTC One X, and the Quadrant - after the HTC One X and Asus Transformer Prime TF201. In short, the performance of the tablet does not hold.



Despite this hardware power, considering the Nexus 7 in absentia when it was first introduced, I, frankly, did not see anything attractive in it, well, except perhaps for the price. There is no 3G module and slot for removable microSD cards, the main camera is also missing, I thought. Jelly Bean ?! - Anyway, and the same Android with the same advantages and disadvantages as the simple four. In general, according to the descriptions Nexus 7 did not make a special impression on me.



But as soon as I had the device on my test, I immediately liked it very much. The possibility of voice dialing offline is very pleasant, although not critically needed. Improved notification interface - a much more useful innovation. But all this, all the same, was not enough to articulate clearly what is particularly pleasant in this device. And this is despite the fact that yesterday I almost never parted with the device: installed applications, browsed the Internet, listened to music on SoundCloud, watched videos on YouTube and Vimeo, then played Dark Meadow for an hour, and looked at the next dream Vkontakte "Live your life" Godard.

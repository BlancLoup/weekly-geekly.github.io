UNIGINE Open Air 2013. Photo report

240 participants, 2 days, 12 lectures, 3 buckets of whiskey, 63 kilograms of kebabs, 5 kW of sound - UNIGINE Open Air 2013 passed under Tomsk. This is an open-air conference dedicated to 3D graphics technology and the development of computer games.



This year we held open-air for the second time, and it became even bigger and more versatile: the number of participants increased, their geography expanded, and more reports became available. We were happy to talk with game developers from Tomsk, Novosibirsk, Omsk, Barnaul, Biysk, Krasnoyarsk, Kemerovo, Voronezh and other cities!
The participants were reluctant to leave, and then wrote to us that in the city they missed the open-air atmosphere. Damn nice to say.



What are they talking about?


The highlight of the program was the reports of the Director General of Sphinx Technologies, Andrey Aksenov shodan (Voronezh). On the first day, Andrew very inspiringly argued that in reality there are no difficult tasks, in the worst case, the tasks can be long or tedious. Among other things, he argued that the brain is completely necessary for a programmer, only ‚Äúa light natural inclination and 10-15 years of training‚Äù is required. The second report was about low-level code optimizations (by the way, it had to be split into two parts, because all the useful information in the allotted time did not fit).



Ivan Avdeev w23 (Novosibirsk) told about the secrets of the demoscene, talking about the technology of creating 3D demos of 64 KB, 4 KB and even 256 bytes!





The report flowed smoothly into ‚Äúhalf-drunk shading coding,‚Äù as one of the open-air participants put it. It looked like this: Ivan, in the light of stars and monitors, wrote a 3D demo from scratch, shodan commented on what is happening: ‚ÄúI think someone is reinventing bilinear texture filtering ...‚Äù, the audience watched entrancedly.





Alexander Pogrebnyak (Novosibirsk), Technical Director of Alawar Entertainment, spoke about the technologies of casual games and how to earn them.



The server programmer of the Genius Games Ruslan Mashukov (Krasnoyarsk) told about the technical stages of Action MMO development.



Pavel Nakaznenko (Krasnoyarsk), a multi-programmer, shared his experience, pain and joys of porting the Unreal Engine 3 to FLASH.



UNIGINE specialists, of course, also shared their knowledge and experience. Our technical director Alexander Zapryagayev (Frustum) told about the specifics of creating a 3D engine for huge virtual worlds.



General Director Denis Shergin binstream introduced the participants to the basics of 3D graphics "on the fingers."



Anna Chaplinskaya told about the role and pain of the build engineer in game dev.



3D artist David Vidiger broke the standing ovation, telling and showing voxel modeling in 3D-Coat. It was not limited to reports, the guys were caught with different questions during the breaks.



Reports on open-air were not only on technical topics. Andrei Viktorov, co-founder of Fate Studio (Novosibirsk), spoke about the hard life of small studios, independently developing games.



Natalya Ogloblina (Barnaul), CEO of Alawar Stargaze, discusses how gaming techniques can be used in project management.

Fun part


In their free time, participants unloaded their minds by playing volleyball / badminton / frisbee or simply sunbathing.





Closer to the night of the first day, when all the reports ended, the whiskey-party began, which continued with a drum'n'bass party. Those who did not want to dance, went to watch the coding of shaders, draw pictures with light (freeze light), or scattered around fires by interests.

By the way, the MK fans on the second day took part in the mini-tournament, its winner as a prize received a cool open-air T-shirt.

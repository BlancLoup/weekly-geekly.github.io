Pin the investor in the corner

One of the main problems of any startup is the lack of expertise and experience of its creators. These two things are often necessary for a prototype to become a complete product or service that can be developed by attracting investment. Sometimes even a few minutes devoted to a project by a competent mentor or investor can determine its success. The problem for young entrepreneurs is only where to find a platform for constructive communication with industry professionals.

Every year up to 500 new start-ups appear on the Ukrainian market. However, it comes to the stage of full implementation, no more than 10% of cases. This state of affairs can be explained by the fact that the development of a start-up culture in Ukraine, and in the CIS as a whole, is still in its infancy stage. As well as the dampness of many projects and teams. In addition, the strategic problem of the market is the lack of a venture capital culture. In our countries, the majority of wealthy people, unfortunately, do not occur to engage in investing in innovative business.

What should young online entrepreneurs do in this situation? Count on the fact that it will be possible to light up your project in front of foreign angels or venture investors. For this, for example, you can go to the States. But this, of course, is not an option for everyone - because of its cost. Not to mention that the effectiveness of a trip to the Valley can also be zero, if the team does not have enough personal contacts. Of course, you can get advice or even find seed investments online, for example, at several specialized sites for investors and startups. But this option also works far from always.

In practice, almost the only chance for an entrepreneur to get high-quality feedback from an experienced and professional mentor or investor is to tweak his project in an informal setting. This, in the first place, is possible at specialized conferences. In Ukraine and in the CIS as a whole, venture funds, investors, incubators and accelerators are quite actively involved in their organization. Thanks to this, the visits of world-scale venture capital investment gurus to give lectures and conduct master classes are no longer perceived as something unique. The problem is that, despite its frequent visits, top speakers are not becoming more accessible to newcomers to the industry. As a rule, at these start-up conferences, most investor stars confine themselves to speaking on common panels and hold private meetings with themselves detailed, and then disappear behind the scenes.

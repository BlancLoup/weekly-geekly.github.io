How to train your own dragon

No, this is not a review for a popular animated film.
You can really have your own dragon. Teach him commands, control his actions, or even send on a temporary rest.

Let the dragon in this case - only the program.

For many, many years, one company has been producing a product that allows a user to control a computer with their hands in their pockets. Turn on the microphone and control the world (digital, of course). And this product is called Dragon NaturallySpeaking 11 .



And what is interesting here, it's easier for me to click the mouse and / or with the help of the keyboard to access the necessary content on the hard drive or on the Internet, you say?

In the spaces of the ex-USSR, so far, programs have not really got accustomed, for the management of which you need to know English and be able to speak it. But abroad, where doctors do not like to show their terrible handwriting and adore effective work with patients, they can afford to buy Dragon complete with a wireless microphone and record everything necessary for a successful discharge of the patient, so as not to suffer, but only scroll through the audio and view the recognized text. Or lawyers. Or people of other professions for whom the delay in working with large volumes of data is unacceptable and harmful to profit.

And for people with disabilities, this is generally incredible help.

Enough time has passed since the release of the latest version of Dragon, but this allowed the company to make many useful corrections to the product:
- improved text recognition (99% dictated);
- works great on Windows 7/2008;
- support of 2 or more nuclear processors (real-time optimization);
- the use of their dictionaries;
- Work in Office applications 2007/2010, Mozilla Firefox, Thunderbird.

And in order to make it cheaper for the user to work without losing the result, those who have Drakosh 9/10 can simply upgrade and lose nothing.

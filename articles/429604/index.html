Review of the laptop Lenovo S730-13 (2018): powerful iron in a stylish aluminum case

Hi, Habr! Lenovo has released a new model for the Yoga S730 line. The modification, which we will discuss today, was received by the eighth generation Intel Core i7 processor, a 10-hour battery with the ability to charge it up to 80% per hour, a fingerprint scanner and the thinnest of the entire series of Yoga notebooks, an aluminum case.



Appearance


Lenovo S730 is not just very thin - it is very light. Yoga in an aluminum case weighs only 1.2 kg. It will be convenient to carry it all the time. Such a laptop will appeal to those who primarily need speed and a good physical keyboard.

In the photo for comparison - the Yoga 720 below and the Yoga S730-13 above. The thickness of the Yoga 720 is 14.3 mm, while the S730-13 is 11.9.



But this convenience was worth several sacrifices. Firstly, this model does not expand 360 degrees, like the classic Yoga. Secondly, there is not a single USB port - only three Type-C, one of which is designed for charging. Fortunately, 3.5 mm jack remained. Version 730 (without the letter S, which can be turned into a tablet) has one USB port, although the thickness of this device is 14.1 mm versus 11.9 mm for our hero.

Color options are platinum and steel gray. The case itself is made of aluminum, finished finishing on a sandblaster.

The device really does not look thicker than a magazine, and it slightly surpasses by weight: For example, Elle weighs about 1200 grams.



Interfaces


The developers of Lenovo Yoga S730-13 had to sacrifice a number of ports to achieve the minimum weight and thickness. The CD drive was forgotten a long time ago due to the penetration and speed of broadband Internet, Wi-Fi allowed us to get rid of the LAN port, and now it‚Äôs time to send the past USB, replacing them with miniature USB Type-C.

On the left side of the laptop is located USB Type-C for charging the laptop, 3.5-mm port for headphones and the power indicator.





On the right side there is a reset button to the factory settings and two USB Type-C, one of which supports continuous charging of connected devices. And these are all the ports that are here. It will be most convenient to use wireless peripherals, although in principle, you will not need peripherals for working with text, tables, and surfing the network. There is no support for memory cards in a laptop, but it is possible to choose a model with a solid-state drive up to 1 TB.



Display


Yoga S730-13 is equipped with a 13.3-inch display with a resolution of 1920 x 1080 pixels with a wide viewing angle. The display supports Dolby Vision technology, an enhanced HDR format.

Very thin frames around the display are striking. What should be the thickness of the framework - for the transformer the question is complicated: on the one hand, the thinner the frame, the larger the display can be placed in the case; on the other hand, when transferring to the tablet mode, a wide frame allows you to safely hold the gadget without making unnecessary clicks. Since the S730-13 before the tablet does not fold out and does not have a touch screen, its thin frames are only a plus on all sides.



Another difference S730 from 730 model touched hinges. Instead of two thin, resembling the very first Lenovo Yoga 11, here one hinge occupies almost the entire length of the laptop.



At the bottom of the laptop placed three rubber legs, one along the entire length of the hinge, and two small ones closer to the front edge.





Keyboard


Lenovo Yoga keyboards have always been comfortable and haven't undergone any significant changes since 2012. The S730 keyboard is also comfortable. Of course, those who continue to work on laptops of this line from year to year are especially pleased. But getting used to it will turn out very quickly. The key travel is soft, they themselves have a barely noticeable indentation in the center - it is a pleasure to work on this keyboard both from a tactile and audio point of view.



The keyboard is equipped with a backlight with two levels of brightness. Right above the keyboard is the power button with LED indicator.





Under the keyboard is a fingerprint scanner.





Hardware component


The eighth generation Intel Core i7 processor provides high performance and decent runtime - up to 10 hours. Also available with i5.

The i7-8565U processor, a quad-core mobile processor released in the third quarter of 2018, is manufactured using a 14-nanometer process. The base frequency is 1.8 GHz, but it can accelerate using Turbo Boost technology to 4.6 GHz.

Autonomy


Intel's energy efficient processor allows you to charge for up to 10 hours. Rapid Charge technology will help charge your laptop up to 80% in just an hour. This is especially true at times when it is urgently necessary to go to a meeting - some half an hour, and the charge is enough for decent work time.

How much and where to buy?

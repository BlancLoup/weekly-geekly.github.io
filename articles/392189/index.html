How fast does ExoMars fly to Mars?

An interplanetary flight from Earth to any other planet - in this case, to Mars - is inconceivable from the point of view of flying in a straight line at cruising speed. On Earth, this is possible - ships and airplanes move in a straight line, sometimes changing direction at given points on the route. In the solar system, each object is affected by the force of force. Curves for the solar system are natural, whereas the straight lines are not there. Therefore, ExoMars flies all 500 million kilometers along a curve, constantly reducing its speed, moving away from the Sun.

This diagram shows the distance traveled and the date by which ExoMars will pass this distance. The launch took place on March 14, at the beginning of June, the device will cover 200 million kilometers, in September - 400 million, and by the landing date, October 19, it will cover almost 500 million kilometers. Please note that the flight speed decreases with time.



Frank Budnik reminds you that you need to compare the correct numbers, which in the case of ‚Äúspacecraft speed‚Äù and ‚Äúspeed record‚Äù is not the same thing. Record New Horizons consider 16.26 km / s, it is 58,500 km / h. It was at this speed that the New Horizons overcame the distance from the Earth to the Moon - these are the first 8 hours and 35 minutes of flight.

Neither before nor after New Horizons , spacecraft flew off the Earth at such a speed. But if we talk about speed in a heliocentric orbit, then to 16.26 km / s, we need to add the speed of the Earth - this is 30 km / s, and we get about 46 km / s relative to the Sun. This is an impressive speed, but not a record one. The record was at the apparatus Helios 2 - 70 km / s.

ExoMars is not moving so fast. In 218 days, 16 hours and 56 minutes, the device will cover 500 million kilometers. Its average speed on a heliocentric trajectory will be 26.5 km / s - between the speeds of the Earth at 30 km / s and Mars at 24 km / s.

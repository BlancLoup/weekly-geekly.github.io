Conductive thread for 3D printers



Kickstarter is currently running a campaign by entrepreneur Michael Tutongi, who claims to have created a conductive thread for use in 3D printers. With the help of such a thread it is possible to print the circuits directly on the printer - for example, print finished plastic products with conductive paths embedded in them. In the campaign, as an example of working devices, LED flashlights printed on the printer, electromagnetic locks and electromagnetic levitator are presented.

The beauty of technology is that its use does not require any special equipment - such a thread can be filled with ordinary low-cost PLA-printers.

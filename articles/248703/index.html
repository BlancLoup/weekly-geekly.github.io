With the full-fledged introduction of the DNA Bank service to SIAG in 2010, the data center began to accept, in addition to digital data, also real genetic material. Customers who wish to keep a piece of themselves in this service receive the specified disk space in the data center, for storing their digitized personal data, and the physical storage space for the sealed flask with DNA samples. Planets project


As part of the IT Projects Planets (Preservation and Long-term Access through Networked Services), the Time Capsule program was initiated. The essence of the program is to systematize and save the disappearing digital data reading formats. It is not even worth mentioning how critical this may become in our rapidly developing world, where new ones are born every day, and the old formats are gradually receding into oblivion. And it was not surprising that for such an honorable mission a place was chosen where a metal box filled with heterogeneous data carriers would be located, ranging from flash drives, microfilms, to paper carriers, precisely in the dungeons of the Swiss Fort Knox. Each data object is stored both in its original format and in a "transformed", encrypted form. Also, in addition to the currently rare reading formats, the objects of accompanying software are stored on the carriers: operating systems, file systems, encoding methods. All these data are naturally duplicated on the server capacity of the data center, and are available to authorized users via the global Internet.

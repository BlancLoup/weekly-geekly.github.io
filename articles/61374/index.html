FAS filed a case against Microsoft

The Federal Antimonopoly Service (FAS) of the Russian Federation on Thursday filed a case against Microsoft in connection with the reduction of supplies to the Russian Federation of the Windows XP operating system!

The corresponding notice is posted on the website of the antimonopoly authority of the Russian Federation.

Here is the main excerpt from it:
"The signs of violation of antitrust laws by Microsoft are seen in the economically or technologically unjustified cessation of the production and supply to the Russian Federation of operating systems (OS) for personal computers (PCs) of the Microsoft Windows family of boxed versions - Windows OS distributions and reducing the supply of this OS as pre-installed when selling a new computer, as well as setting different prices for the Windows XP operating system.

In order to implement measures to prevent and suppress violations of the antimonopoly legislation of the Russian Federation in the field of information technologies, the FAS Russia considered the situation in the software market.

As a result of market analysis of proprietary (non-free software products, where the copyright holder retains a monopoly on their use, copying and modification, and the source code of these software products is protected by copyright) PC operating systems have established a dominant position of Microsoft in the market under investigation.

Yes, these are huge devices weighing several tons. Requiring appropriate carriers and working relatively short. But for the observation vehicles there were some fundamental limitations of space:
- it is necessary to accurately determine the orientation. As a rule, these are three or more devices.
- a fairly accurate stabilization is needed when shooting an object. You can put the power gyros, but you can not overclock them to infinity. After some time, you need to reset the kinetic moment. In a magnetic field, electromagnetic systems are already used for this, but without a field, only engines.
- more quality shooting - more focal length of the lens. On modern devices, in fact, these are telescopes.
- the transfer of large amounts of information (images) requires significant radio link capacities, especially at a distance from the Earth.
- more than about 500 watts per square meter of solar panel is not available.

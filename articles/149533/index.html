Horoshist: Sony Xperia Sola review

If we single out the flagship model and budget devices in the model range of the smartphone manufacturer, then there must be a middle ground? At least, there is such a model in the Sony Xperia lineup: this place is rightly occupied by 3.7-inch Sony Xperia Sola, which my hands finally reached.



Design


Unlike the rest, emphasized 2012 Sony Xperia models of 2012, Xperia Sola is made in a more classical style - a model with such a design could enter the market a year or two ago - but at the same time Sola does not look like a guest from the past. Surely many buyers will like this design - trendy design and outdated faster.



Form factor Sola is a two-volume monoblock. The two-volume is achieved by the "outstanding" screen, under which a kind of "shelf" is formed. This is not only stylish, but also convenient: it can be used as a thumb rest, to avoid accidentally pressing the ‚Äúhard‚Äù touch keys of the device, which could happen by chance while watching a video or during a game, for example.

Along the perimeter of the display is framed by a strip of glossy plastic - this is the only, apart from the display itself, brand part of the smartphone. The rest of the body is made of matte finish. To the touch it is some kind of smooth soft-touch - the surface is not hard, but devoid of its noble velvety.



Under the base of the display is an LED that blinks white when you turn on the screen or press any button.

Soaring touch


The soaring touch on Xperia Sola is used to highlight the links in the smartphone‚Äôs native browser without a direct touch: when the finger hovers over the link, it is highlighted as if you hover the cursor. This solution should reduce the distance between desktop and mobile browsers for ease of navigation: first, due to the size of the screen of smartphones, you have to either poke links at random, or perform an additional action for a click - for example, increasing the page area with a link. Secondly, one of the scenarios of using the mouse in desktop browsers is the detection of clickable elements on the page - highlighting the links in Xperia Sola allows you to do this in a mobile browser. If you hold your finger over the page, the links ‚Äúblink‚Äù, as if you were just hovering over them. Blinked - count selected. Xperia Smart Tags


Xperia Sola is part of a club of smartphones that support the NFC wireless protocol. This is the fifth Sony model this year - NFC is also on Xperia S, Xperia P, Xperia acro S and Xperia ion. However, Sony Xperia Sola is the first smartphone that got me to the test along with Smart Tags smart tags, so I can tell you more about them.



All Xperia Sola are sold with the two NFC tags included. Each label can be programmed to a number of functions that allow, if desired, completely change the configuration of the phone. Labels are managed using a special widget that can be placed on one of the five Xperia Sola desktops.

Turn on NFC, bring the smartphone to the part where the sticker with the inscription NFC is located (did you remember its location? - you can break it) to the label. When you first start the application will ask you to set up a label-driven configuration.

In the future, you just need to touch the label to activate the specified settings. For example, at home you need Wi-Fi and the call sound turned on, and on the street - a vibrating alert and data transmission over a mobile network (3G / GSM). No question: put in a pocket, say, jeans or a jacket, a label programmed for the ‚ÄúStreet‚Äù configuration (by default one label is called ‚ÄúHouse‚Äù, the second is ‚ÄúBedroom‚Äù, but you can change the names, as you understand) - when going outside, put your Xperia Sola in your pocket, the street configuration of the settings will start.

Came back home? We touched the label "House". Tags have an eyelet so that you can hang them on the strap. Also included are two adhesive patches - stick a circle to the label, remove the protective film from the back side and now you can stick the label itself anywhere.

3D printing in dentistry on the example of NextDent



Spurred by accelerated technological advances, the world is changing faster than ever. One of the most dynamic areas in these changes was 3D printing, and one of the most appropriate uses for it in medicine is dentistry. It is in dentistry that the most critical are not only the speed and accuracy of 3D printing, but also the special properties of materials for it, such as safety, biocompatibility, compliance with strict medical parameters. The properties of the latest 3D-printed materials for dentistry, we will discuss in this review.

For example, let's take the products of NextDent BV - a Dutch company, selected in 2012 from Vertex-Dental BV, which at that time has been successfully engaged in dental materials for more than 76 years. Medical institutions of the Netherlands and Germany take part in the development of NextDent materials, and all products undergo strict control and certification. One can hardly find a more suitable example.

Printing temporary bases dentures.



NextDent Base is a class IIa biocompatible material suitable for printing all types of denture bases. This material has low shrinkage compared to standard materials for dentures, which leads to an excellent fit of prosthetic bases. The small amount of residual monomer remaining after further processing makes this material more biocompatible.

Adaptation of innovations for kickbacks on the example of "Epiphan Distance Doc 2"

A small review of the adaptation of innovations on the example of "Epiphan Distance Doc 2" for participation in public procurement.

Introduction


I am an IT specialist. In order to maintain the relevance of my knowledge I try to keep abreast of the state of affairs in the field of information technology for which I regularly monitor. Since I am interested in cloud technologies and services, I am also interested in technologies that provide work with a stationary personal computer remotely via the Internet from various devices, for example, from a tablet. Freedom - from noise, dust and the need to sit in front of the monitor, in my opinion, sufficient reasons for the use of cloud technology.

I managed to master open virtualization using QEMU / KVM, forwarding video cards to virtual machines, but unfortunately I was faced with the fact that remote access using open protocols to forward video cards did not have sufficient performance. I found a way out of the current situation in the hardware encoding of the video signal from a video card either through the use of cloud video cards or through the use of a video broker.

Cloud video cards are video cards that have a built-in video encoder that captures and compresses the video stream and sends it over the network. Examples of these video cards are video series: AMD Radeon SKY and Nvidia GRID. Using these video cards, you can create your own cloud service, but unfortunately these video cards differ significantly in price from regular video cards and are not available in the retail market.

Video obdkaster - a device that, like an IP camera, broadcasts a video stream to a network, but not from a camera, but from a forwarding video card. Using these devices, you can create your own cloud service, although these devices are also not cheap.

frequency range
The frequency range is probably the most meaningless parameter in the characteristic and more used by marketers. A person hears in the range of 20 Hz - 20 kHz, at the beginning of this range there are low-frequency sounds (bass), at the top - high-frequency sounds. The task of any headphones is to correctly reproduce this range, without failures, without wheezing and a bass hum, without hissing and rattling on HF. This ability reflects the amplitude-frequency characteristic (AFC) and is usually represented as a graph of the dependence of volume on frequency. Therefore, the smoother and smoother the curve, the more accurate and clear the sound. Unfortunately, I did not manage to find the frequency response graph from the manufacturer, but the 20Hz-20kHz range is indicated on the headphone box.

Sensitivity (sound pressure)
Sensitivity is the headphone efficiency: the larger it is, the louder the headphones sound, regardless of the source. Monoprice Enhanced Active Noise Canceling is powerful, the manufacturer indicates 94 ¬± 3 dB.

Load impedance
For Monoprice Enhanced Active Noise Canceling, the manufacturer has set the resistance to 32 ohms. If you do not go into radio engineering, you can say the following: the greater the resistance, the more powerful source is needed for headphones and the less influence the source itself has on the sound.

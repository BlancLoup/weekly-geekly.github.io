Highscreen Yummy Duo - the new Android-smartphone with support for two SIM-cards


The thinnest smartphone with support for two SIM-cards appeared in Russia. The well-known Russian company Vobis Computer, which manufactures inexpensive smartphones under the Higshcreen brand, has launched a new model, the Highscreen Yummy Duo, on the market. The device is equipped with a huge 4.3-inch screen, GPS-navigator, front camera and two slots for SIM-cards. The thickness of the device, according to the manufacturer, does not exceed 10.5 millimeters.

The smartphone is built on the MediaTek MT6573 hardware platform, which is already used in such phones as Gigabyte GSmart G1345, Highscreen Jet Duo and LG Optimus Link Dual SIM. According to the developers, using the new platform allows you to reduce the device's turn-on time to 5 seconds, increase performance when working with graphics by 20% (compared to the Qualcomm MSM7227T platform), and also provide an average time for the phone from one charge to two days.

Brief specifications:
  • 4.3 inch capacitive touch screen with WVGA-resolution (480x800), support for multi-touch technology;
  • 512 MB of RAM;
  • 512 MB of internal memory;
  • MicroSD memory card support up to 32 GB;
  • GPS receiver;
  • GSM / GPRS / EDGE 850/900/1800/1900 MHz, UMTS / HSDPA 900/2100 MHz, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth;
  • 5 megapixel camera, autofocus, LED flash.
  • front camera for video calls;
  • Android 2.3.

According to the manufacturer, the cost of a smartphone in stores will not exceed nine thousand rubles.

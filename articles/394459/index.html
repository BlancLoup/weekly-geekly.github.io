DARPA stealth motorcycles with hybrid engines running on almost any fuel


Nightmare

At the Special Forces Industry Conference ( SOFIC 2016 ), two companies presented custom-made (and with financial support) DARPA hybrid motorcycles for military use.

These prototypes are called "stealth motorcycles", but not from the fact that they are not visible on the radar. Two years ago, DARPA placed an order for the manufacture of a motorcycle that would move as quietly as an electric motorcycle, but at the same time could cover a noticeably longer distance. Two companies took this matter seriously.

Logos produced the Silent Hawk (‚Äúsilent hawk‚Äù), and LSA Autonomy produced the Nightmare (‚Äúnightmare‚Äù). Enduro-like off-road hybrids are similar to each other, and demonstrate a hybrid system fit in the frame, in which the internal combustion engine can work on almost everything that burns - JP-8 aviation kerosene, Jet A-1, propane, gasoline ...

Logos engineer Alex Dzwill [Alex Dzwill] claims that their bike, theoretically, can go on olive oil, while he will understand that he poured into the tank. The company's goal is a hybrid motorcycle, capable of delivering up to 55 miles per hour over rough terrain, and have the engine removed from the frame .


Silent hawk

Such requirements are clear from the point of view of the use of this vehicle - during hostilities, it is rarely possible to find working gas stations, so ideally motorcycles should be driven by any kind of fuel. While the engine is running, the bike produces a lot of noise, about 80 db. But in the "quiet" mode of a trip on batteries, the noise decreases to 55 db - and this is comparable with the volume of the conversation in the room.

The Silent Hawk uses an electrical system from Alta Motors , a company that has been producing electric motorcycles for a long time. Lithium-ion batteries are known for their tendency to explode in the event of overheating, and to install a powerful battery in a motorcycle, it has to be compactified. Engineers claim that a well-designed active cooling system will protect the rider from harm, even if some of the cells fail. A very similar system is used in cars Tesla.

Both motorcycles have all-wheel drive connection, but the Nightmare will be bigger and more powerful than his brother. It weighs 200 kg, and the engines produce 17 hp. on the front wheel and 135 in the rear, while the power of its system is 13 kW. Silent Hawk weighs 170 kg and generates 7.5 kW. Both motorcycles can be used as electric generators for connecting external consumers. Whether we will see hybrid motorcycles on the roads is still unknown.

Current Cost EnviR - electricity consumption monitoring


How much electricity does a TV consume, what consumes more electricity - a washing machine or a refrigerator, how efficiently is electricity used in an apartment? All these questions could previously be answered by the now closed Google PowerMeter service. However, geeks and home economists do not despair, because despite the collapse of the service from Google, the matter of monitoring electricity consumption continues to live. In this review, we propose to examine the Current Cost EnviR device , which allows not only to observe how kilowatts of electricity ‚Äúflow away‚Äù on the device display, but also in conjunction with Current Cost NetSmart (formerly Current Cost Bridge), to track the power consumption online at my.currentcost .com .





So, what can this device



  1. The current consumption of electricity in your house / apartment (updated every 6 seconds on the display)
  2. Contactless method of measuring power consumption. More precisely, AC, and the voltage is set in the settings from 200V to 260V. Power is calculated in the device by their product.
  3. Wireless transmission of data from the terminal sensor to the display receiver over the radio channel at a frequency of 433 MHz.
  4. Estimated cost of electricity consumed per day / month (set tariff for 1 kW / h)
  5. Dynamics of electricity consumption, i.e. the device displays a jump in kW when connecting / disconnecting energy consumers in the house / apartment.
  6. Monitor the current room temperature at the location of the display receiver.
  7. The graph of energy consumption and the current indoor temperature on my.currentcost.com . Updated every 5 minutes. Requires the Current Cost NetSmart add-on module. The graph also displays the line of minimum energy consumption (+ leaks if they exist), when all devices in the house are in standby mode.
  8. Transmit information about the consumption of electricity to the Google PowerMeter service every 10 minutes. Requires the Current Cost NetSmart add-on module .
  9. The ability to connect to a PC via a special USB cable with the possibility of further processing on the PC incoming data from the device in XML format. A list of available software is available here . You can also write a parser for this data yourself.




Order of connection and use



  1. Connect and configure the display receiver. Set the date / time, voltage, tariff.
  2. Turn on the radio transmitter and synchronize with the receiver.
  3. Clamp the terminal to the phase wire immediately after the meter and connect it to the transmitter. The current power consumption should immediately appear on the receiver.
  4. In the case of web monitoring, connect Current Cost NetSmart through a special cord to the display receiver and to your home router / switch on eth. DHCP must be configured and active on your local network because NetSmart receives the IP and gateway settings from it.
  5. In the case of monitoring on a PC, connect a special USB cable display receiver to a PC. Attention! Simultaneous connection to eth and PC is impossible!

Web-interfaces without using the mouse wheel

Do you find touch interfaces convenient? I think you think. After all, it is really convenient to communicate with the interface on ‚Äúyou‚Äù. Why do we need a mediator in our communication? Why complicate things?

The other day, the thought crept into my head that it would be nice to try to implement something similar on an ordinary web page. Let not the touch, due to the fact that you still have to manipulate the cursor with the mouse, but some simplification, giving the user the opportunity to go a step closer to the interface with which he interacts.

The essence of the simplification is that the user, being on the page of the site, can hold down the left mouse button and move the page to the desired height. In my opinion, less energy is spent on a click and a slight movement of a brush than on a wheel's torsion. Imagine what you need to do in order to twist the wheel: lift your finger, move it to the right, twist the wheel and put your finger back into place (in most cases). You already see how much micro movements need to be done for such a simple task. And how many times a day do you have to make such movements? I propose a simpler option - just two movements: click and drag the mouse. Such movements are easier and do not distract from the main thing - from the information for which we come. And this crackle when scrolling, especially in cheap mice, do you hear? For example, it distracts me. Try, compare two variants of page flip, mine and traditional. I think you will notice the difference.

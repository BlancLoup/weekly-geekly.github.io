How we drove drug dealers out of the runet



Today I would like to tell you a story about how we defined sites selling drugs in RuNet, about the scale of the problem, about the experience of interaction between an IT company and Roskomnadzor, deputies, various chambers, and why this is no longer enough evil.

It all started with an article on Habr last year, in which we scanned the list of sites banned in the territory of the Russian Federation, compiled a frequency dictionary for them, tried to figure out what is actually happening in the topic of Internet regulation. An interactive visualization of the dictionary is now available on our website, screen:



The results, to some extent, even surprised us. The overwhelming majority of the locks had nothing to do with the persecution of unwanted ones: drug trafficking, pornography, and gambling. Looking at the zones in which all this stuff is located, they set themselves the goal of ridding the .ru zone of drug dealers.



We finalized the dictionary taking into account the specifics: only the necessary subject, plus words, minus words, identified a bunch of additional features, conducted their scoring, and taught the system to build a graph of connectivity. As a result, we received a tool with a thin thematic classifier, which, starting from the button, unfolded the cluster, scanned all existing domains in the .ru zone, cheated all this, built visualization and generated a report. All this was done at their own expense, without a drop of grant-eating. This time, the results, to put it mildly, struck us.

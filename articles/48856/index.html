Licensing cartographic activities for everyone?

About the article

I decided to write this controversial topic, which concerns all those who are somehow connected with any cartographic activity. It does not matter if you are working with cartographic information on the Internet or offline. This article does not consider the licensing procedure, but tries to answer the primary question of whether to license its activities in principle. This entry was caused by this discussion in our forum.

Situation
You produce some kind of cartographic product. An example of such a product is a database of restaurants that you visualize on your topography or, say, google maps, there is no fundamental difference. An implementation may simply be a visualization of the data or have a set of additional functions.

The question of life and death and in general

Do you need [your organization] a license for such activities?

Why does such a question arise at all?
If the project is serious, you should be ready to counter competitors, data holders, Roskartografiya and other detractors. Seeing your success, they can push | sue, etc., to somehow hinder your development. One of the ways you can "pin up" is to ask such a question.

What is this "geographic information system"?

For the definition, we turn to the source, which is likely to be quoted for the definition - GOST 52438-2005 Geographical information systems. Terms and Definitions.

We quote:
" Geoinformation system : GIS: Information system operating with spatial data.
Information system : A system designed to store, process, search, distribute, transmit and present information.
Data : Information presented in a form suitable for processing by automatic means with the possible participation of a person.
Spatial data : data on spatial objects and their sets

So, is our system (restaurants on google maps) geo-information?

Yes, according to this definition is.
Information system? - Yes, we store, distribute and present information about restaurants.
Spatial data? - Certainly spatial, since each point of the restaurant is in the spatial coordinate system.
Thus, we have created a GIS, kindly go through the licensing process.

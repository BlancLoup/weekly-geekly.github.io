Heminides



Photo of user Dolph1n Astroforum .

Will be: December 9-16 , a maximum of December 14 .
Right now when you read these lines. If you have a clear sky, take a break for at least a few minutes, look at one of the most powerful meteor showers of the year. By the way, there is no need to look at the radiant - find a darker place, wait a few minutes for your eyes to adapt to the dark and see less bright meteors, and just look at the sky - meteors can flash from any side of the world.
And those who have the sky covered with clouds can:
  • View the record of the Geminid broadcast with radio fixation of meteors on the website slooh.com

Comet C / 2014 Q2 (Lovejoy)



She is still small, but will grow up soon. Photo source .

It will be visible from the end of December, the best conditions for observation are the New Year holidays .
Discovered just six months ago, the comet suddenly will delight us in the New Year holidays. Despite the fact that its perihelion is between the orbits of the Earth and Mars, the projected maximum brightness is higher than that seen by the naked eye (source: cometbase.net ):



Now the comet is in the southern part of the sky, but already on New Year's Eve it will be not far from the constellation Orion (source - heavens-above.com ):



Unlike comet ISON, which let us down so much, lounging behind the Sun a year ago, this comet has no chance of becoming a comet of the century, but it is guaranteed not to fall apart.
In addition to the resource heavens-above.com, the exact location of the comet can be imported into Stellarium:
  1. General settings (F2) - Plug-ins - Editor of the Solar System (tick "download at startup").
  2. Plugin settings (‚Äúconfigure‚Äù button) - ‚ÄúSolar system‚Äù tab - import of objects in MPC format.
  3. In the window that appears, select the type of "comet", select the source "MPC's list of observable comets", click the "get orbit elements" button.
  4. In the window that appears, find "C / 2014 Q2 (Lovejoy)", tick the list and click "Add objects".
  5. After this, the comet can be easily found by Ctrl-F.

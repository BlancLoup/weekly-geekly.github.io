Conversion: several useful graphs and tables

Introduction


After a thorough manual analysis of the statistics of 146 large and medium sites, a small report was compiled with reference charts and tables. The purpose of this note is to share with readers readers statistical information related to the increase in conversion. The article shows some common mistakes that were made by the owners of several large sites, as well as ways to avoid them.

Site design


Quite often, employees of well-known studios can observe the following typical mistake of site owners: an attempt to devote a huge amount of time to those factors that have little effect on the conversion. For example, in the classic view, the most important elements of a product card are:


Therefore, it is they who need to pay attention first. I will especially note that this statistics cannot be considered in isolation from other important factors. For example, various tariff calculators, filters in the listing, as well as methods of dividing one service into several (different tariffs) have always played an important role.

Nobody diminishes the importance of design, however, its objective significance is often overestimated. As a verification experiment, we will estimate statistics on 11 large online stores that have made significant changes to the design (spent a lot of money and time on new templates) in order to increase conversion:


I note that all these sites before these changes had a professional design, which improved after checking A / B tests. In no case has a high-quality website explicitly increased its conversion, therefore, a radical design change does not always lead to an increase in conversion. It is necessary to analyze the site more accurately and be more careful in making "sharp" decisions.

Texts of the description of services and articles on sites


Text analysis (description of services and products, advertising articles on the site, notes in blogs and social networks) of 146 large and medium-sized sites showed that the main problem of the texts is the lack of objective consumer value. Such a problem not only reduces the interest of the target audience, but is also not well perceived by modern search engine algorithms.

Consider several factors assessing the quality of texts, taking into account the degree of their importance. It is understood that the texts are correctly decorated and contain correctly selected illustrations. First of all, it is useful to evaluate the interest of similar materials by analyzing the statistics of queries to search engines and the reaction of the target audience to similar notes (comments, views, ratings, likes) on other sites.

The layout of the controls and the general forms of Yi 2 4K are similar to GoPro, which has long been an unspoken pattern when creating third-party models. Alas, this form is not optimal, especially considering the scope of application. Otherwise, there is nothing to complain about: Yi 2 4K lies comfortably in your hand, is easy to control and pleases the eye with rounded body features.



At the top of the case there are two microphones, a speaker and the only camera control button:


Microphones paired, the sound is written in stereo. LED activity indicator is located on the front of the case. When activating the Wi-Fi module, the camera is available in the access point mode, to which you can connect from your smartphone. You can communicate via Wi-Fi with the camera using the free YI Action application.


After a simple and brief connection procedure, the image from the camera is displayed on the background of the application and all previously displayed options are available for modification.





Based on the observations, the image on the smartphone is delayed by about half a second, there are no freezes and slowdowns. In general, the utility is simple and adequate, for 7-8 hours of operation, it flew with an error once.
The daily use of the Yi 2 4K consisted mostly as a DVR on a helmet, occasionally filmed to test other possibilities (photo, handheld, etc.). Used for mounting complete accessories from other cameras and screw M4.



Video quality is good at night and excellent during daylight hours at maximum resolution.



But the 4K format, although it is a trend, is not yet provided with a sufficient number of reproducing equipment (monitors). Another thing is 1080p, which has already firmly entered everyday life as the ‚Äúdefault format‚Äù. And here Yi 2 4K comes in handy because of the possibility to shoot FullHD video not only with standard 30 fps, but 60 and even 120.



Image stabilization only works when shooting at 30 frames / sec; I think the case is relatively low processor power.

For dynamic scenes (where action cameras are used most often) this is very useful: 25 or 30 fps is not enough, the picture is a little twitched, the still pictures are blurry. For the same reason, the Slo-mo mode is good, allowing you to view many processes in slow motion. It is a pity that the resolution in Slo-mo is only 720p, not FullHD.





The situation with photographs is similar: when there is enough light, the frames are juicy and clear, colors disappear at dusk, the amount of light noise increases, frames are blurred.





About the sound. He is, and yes, two-channel (stereo). But due to the lack of wind protection, the recording is interrupted by even the slightest breeze, and at speeds over 10 km / h nothing is heard except for noise (therefore, all videos are silent). This camera would be very helpful case with a windshield of microphones.

An important note for those who will mount the camera on the helmet and choose third-party fasteners: at speeds above 100 km / h, the aerodynamic resistance is very high. Subjectively, at 120 km / h, the Yi 2 weighing less than 90 grams feels heavier than another camera weighing more than 170 grams due to less drag. The transition for 140 km / h can be felt without a speedometer, as the head starts to be strongly pulled back.



From the battery, the camera lives for more than 2 hours when recording 4K video with disabled Wi-Fi and Bluetooth, and when changing the mode to 1080p for more than 3 hours. Compared to other action cameras, Yi 2 requires USB connection for recharging much less often.

Thanks to the voice acting, you can perform basic actions (start-stop recording, taking photos, switching on and off) by touch, focusing on the sounds being made. The speaker volume is enough to hear the conditional signals on the road when wearing a helmet (with good noise insulation).

In conclusion, the general impression. Yi 2 4K is a well-developed product with a variety of video and photo shooting modes. The video quality is good (by the standards of action cameras), you can watch and do not spit on 4K-videos taken at dusk. Remote control using Wi-Fi also works flawlessly, and the battery life is beyond praise. Quick start and intuitive menus are also advantages of the Xiaomi product.


But there are nuances. The first is the price: it is adequate, but on the budget option Yi 2 no longer pulls, rather in the middle segment, where there are fewer competitors, but it is harder to deal with them. The second is the complete absence of accessories for fastening in the base delivery, everything is purchased separately. This is good (everyone will pick up fasteners to their liking), and bad (at no additional cost and only removed from hands, subject to the effects of precipitation without aquabox). The third is a form: copying a given layout of the layout of elements negatively affects the aerodynamic drag of the device, which should be considered when designing and choosing fasteners.

Free wifi from skype if you are stuck at the airport

Despite the fact that air traffic in Northern Europe is gradually returning to normal, many passengers still spend a lot of time at airports. In order to keep you connected, Skype provides Skype Access, one-click WiFi access, 100,000 access points for free from mid-Wednesday to the end of Friday, April 23.

Until the end of the working week, Skype completely cancels the payment for Skype Access, so you can easily access the Internet, send emails and make calls via Skype. The offer operates at 100,000 access points worldwide - in cafes, hotels and airports (this includes the vast majority of international airports).

Skype Access is a new service from Skype that allows you to pay for WiFi every minute via Skype Credit. All you need to do is connect to an access point that supports Skype Access and have the latest version of Skype.

From 4 pm on Wednesday, April 21 to 4 am on April 24, Moscow time, Skype Access will work absolutely free.

Over the past 5 days, Skype users made more than 20 million video calls more than usual, about as much time as it takes to fly back and forth to the moon 2,000 times or make 2.5 million flights from London to New York

A complete list of WiFi networks can be found on the support site.

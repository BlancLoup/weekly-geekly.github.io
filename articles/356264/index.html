Roskomnadzor retroactively replaced its document on the federal portal


Roskomnadzor grossly violated the rules for discussing draft regulations on the federal portal regulation.gov.ru, writes Cnews. On February 5, 2018, Roskomnadzor received a negative opinion on the results of the SALT procedure, that is, the regulatory impact assessment (see the screenshot above). Bypassing the established procedure Roskomnadzor retroactively replaced the document on the portal. The original version disappeared without a trace, and the corrected version immediately with the positive conclusion of the OCB went to the Ministry of Justice for approval.

We are talking about the draft order of Roskomnadzor on methods of blocking providers access to banned Internet sites.

The original version of the document drew criticism from the Ministry of Economic Development and other interested parties. Criticism caused the following rules of the project:

  • the duty of the operators themselves to determine the IP-addresses of prohibited sites;
  • compliance by operators with the standard page format "stub";
  • the presence in the HTML code of the page of a hidden unique string with a hash function set by Roskomnadzor.

In the new version, the above norms have been removed, so the amended document immediately received a positive assessment from the Ministry of Economic Development (probably, the document was replaced with the consent of the Ministry of Economic Development).

Amazon's history: from the garage to flying warehouses in 20 years

Four million deliveries a day and 350 million models of gadgets, clothes, household chemicals and everything that you can imagine. This is Amazon - the largest marketplace in the world.

Since 2014, the cost of Amazon has almost doubled: from $ 180 billion to $ 350 billion, and the company's founder, Jeff Bezos, has become one of the richest people on the planet. Growth phase


Amazon had an interesting business plan at the time: the company invested all its profits in development. ‚ÄúEither we are the first, or we lost‚Äù, - this concept was later used by Google, and behind it, by Facebook. The value of the company's shares fell from $ 107 to $ 7 (now one share costs more than $ 800), but the company still had a safety cushion, which even allowed to spend money on mergers and acquisitions. During the crisis, the online store managed to buy competitors mired in debt, and become even bigger.



At the beginning of his journey, Jeff Bezos set two guidelines for himself:

  • if by 2000 his company will sell goods at $ 74 million a year, this will be a normal working result;

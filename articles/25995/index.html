I begin to cover the reports of the second day of the conference on social networks and services SocialExp 2008 (socialexp.ru). The beginning is smooth, many interesting things are expected today, including Max Spiridonov, Igor Denisov (Mio), as well as additional annealing from Garev Vladimir from grape.ru about viral marketing.


The boom of social services: the position of the investor - Yaroslav Greshilov (inVenture)

80% of applications submitted in inVenture - various variations of social networks.

The main problems of such applications (often they do not even go into consideration)
- People can not clearly define the purpose of the stated startup.
- ‚ÄúWe will have a super project, because everyone will come to us.‚Äù Everyone will come to us, because we have a super-project ‚Äù- the eternal question of chicken and egg.
- To do everything at once, a kind of combine on which only your friends will hang out.

The criterion for assessing the success of the application is the answer to the question ‚Äúwhere will the first users come from?‚Äù.

The main asset of the social service is not fashionable fiches, but the audience. Create the basic functionality and begin to develop an audience, completing the functionality. The Internet allows it.

Do not think how to immediately monetize, make the service as useful as possible to more users. Services that start earning immediately grow much slower than money-free ones in the first stage.

We first grow to a certain point, then we start to monetize, at the same time continuing almost exponential growth.

Investments will go when the growth of service. How you grow it, how much and with what speed, so much the faster it will acquire a monetary equivalent.

"Work on the audience, and then it will work for you." Bring your hands in the first people, otherwise you risk to miss in Central Asia. Look for fans and recruit them.

And do not forget about the floating positioning during the entire growth of the service.

Most liked the term ‚Äúfeature-cutting‚Äù: first, think of everything that the service will be able to do in 2 years, and then cut off everything that is possible. Leave only what is needed to run the project. Freshly retold and for many is still a revelation.

The report is excellent. I recommend to read later.

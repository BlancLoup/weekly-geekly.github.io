CES2015: Saygus announced a smartphone with 320 GB of memory

After a five-year break, Saygus announced a high-end V-Squared smartphone with expandable memory up to 320 GB, 21 megapixel and 13 megapixel cameras, a fingerprint scanner and built-in wireless charging.



The company returned with a new gadget after a five-year hiatus: the last time it represented the Saygus VPhone at CES 2010, and announced it in November 2009. The smartphone was built on the base of the PXA310 XScale core 624MHz processor, had onboard 512 MB of flash memory and 256 MB of RAM, a 3.5-inch touchscreen display, 5 megapixel autofocus camera and a front VGA camera. And QWERTY-keyboard for complete happiness, as well as the possibility of two-way video calls. All - running the operating system Android 1.6.





This time the company announced a high-end phone with a 5-inch display with full HD 1920 x 1080 resolution. The V2 is based on a Snapdragon quad-core processor, has 3 GB of RAM, a 21 megapixel main camera and a 13 megapixel front camera. The SSD capacity in the smartphone is 64 GB, but the memory can be expanded with two MicroSDXC slots up to 320 GB. For the sound are the speakers of Harman / Kardon - a manufacturer that has been working in the market of consumer and automotive electronics since 1953. The smartphone runs on Android 4.4.4.

For the safety of the user, the smartphone is equipped with a biometric protection in the form of a fingerprint scanner, and for convenience, the ability to wirelessly charge the smartphone according to the Qi standard . The smartphone is protected from water by the standard IPX7.

The weight of the smartphone is 141 grams: Kevlar, magnesium alloy and fiberglass were used for the body. The 3100 mAh battery can be removed from the case.

The price of the gadget is not reported.

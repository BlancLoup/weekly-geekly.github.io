The construction of the test track Hyperloop 5 miles long starts

As expected, the situation with the project of the supersonic train Ilona Mask Hyperloop was continued: the office of Transportation Technologies in the person of the chief executive officer of the project Hyperloop announced the earliest launch of the construction of a full-size test track in Quay, California.



It is assumed that the Ilona Mask Hyperloop train will be able to cover the distance between Los Angeles and San Francisco at 561 km in just 30 minutes. The movement of cigar-shaped capsules, in each of which it is planned to accommodate 28 people, will occur due to the magnetic fields and the engine with a residual pressure in the pipe of 0.001 ... 1.0 mmHg. Rather contradictory information about the ongoing work, the fate of the project and the prospects for its implementation from time to time pops up in the leading media and is more confusing than it gives an objective idea. A pleasant exception to the emerging trend was the statement by Bibop Gabriele Gresta (Bibop Gabriele Gresta) - the chief executive director of the Hyperloop project to Dezeen correspondents about the nearest plans to launch the construction of a test 8-kilometer track in Quay (California). At the thematic conference held on October 21 in London under the pretentious slogan ‚ÄúTransport to the Future‚Äù he announced the tactical and strategic plans of Hyperloop Transportation Technologies for the coming years.



According to the information provided by Grest, the start of work on the construction of a test track in the Quay Valley will start just in the next few weeks. The place was not chosen randomly. As a source of electricity at the time of the test, it was decided to use solar cells from a nearby city in Kings County. Completion of construction is planned in 2016, after which, until 2018, tests will be conducted. If everything goes as planned, since 2018 the hyper-speed train Hyperloop will be able to serve passengers in normal mode.



Upon completion of the construction of the track at the initial stage of testing, it is planned to transport passengers at a speed of 160 miles per hour with its phased increase. Empty capsules in test mode are planned to drive at a maximum speed of 760 miles / hour. With the successful implementation of the project, Ilona Mask Hyperloop, delivering passengers along the planned route in just 30 minutes, will be able to lead the rating of the fastest passenger vehicles to date, overtaking the Japanese levitating Maglev , which set a speed record of 603 km / h in April this year.



In comparison, the flight on the route San Francisco - Los Angeles by the aircraft of the companies Virgin America, Delta Air Lines, United Airlines, American Airlines, Southwest Airlines takes, on average, 1.20 hours, with a ticket price of $ 50 one way and $ 95 in both. It is possible to overcome the same way by car, on average, in 5-5.5 hours. Hyperloop also carries passengers in just 30 minutes, and, according to Grest's belief, is completely free.

The comparative assessment is impressive, but, as it always happens with the projects of the century, their implementation requires much more effort, time and money than expected at the start.

The news that the project is nearing its final stage and overgrown with flesh is good news. At the same time, according to Grest, there are moments that significantly complicate its implementation. One of them is the sharply increased estimated cost of work on the construction of a 5-mile test track, taking into account the requirements for seismic resistance and weather conditions. And, if initially the whole complex of construction works was estimated at $ 100 million, then according to the latest calculated data, this figure increased by 1.5 times, coming close to 150 million (¬£ 98 million). Other official information also came out somewhat unexpectedly: even in case of successful implementation of construction work on the site in the Quay Valley and subsequent successful testing of capsules, the Hyperloop project will most likely be implemented outside the United States. Moreover, Gresta made a reservation, at this stage preliminary negotiations have already been held with representatives of the government of a number of countries interested in investing in the construction of Hyperloop tracks on their territory. Texas SpaceX Interpretation Alternative


Such a rush of Hyperloop Transportation Technologies after a very long informational pause looks quite indicative, especially given the fact that under the watchful eye of SpaceX management in Texas in January 2016, regardless of the test track project in California , the first open stage of the university competition for the best capsule design will take place Hyperloop Pod. At the first stage, several teams will be selected, the capsules of which will be able to pass the track without failures for the best time. Teams that have passed the selection will be admitted to the second stage of the competition, where they will compete in the nominations:

  1. "Final Design and Construction" (Final Design and Construction).
  2. Safety and Reliability.
  3. Performance in Operations.
  4. Performance in Flight.

Two winning teams from two different classes of capsules (with and without wheels) will be allowed to the final stage. Separately, the class options for mini-containers for teams that cannot afford to build a full-scale model will be considered.

Some of the fundamental requirements imposed by the competitive jury on the prototype test capsule have become known: the limit on total weight is 11,000 pounds. The total length limit is up to 14 feet. A prerequisite is the presence of a braking system, telemetry and communication systems. It is advisable to use a levitating scheme rather than a wheeled one. In this case, "Creating your own mechanism (s) for levitation from the applicant is actually not required. Wheel options (for example," electric car in a vacuum ") can compete, but are unlikely to win prizes." The amount of remuneration has not been disclosed.

For consideration are accepted the work of applicants who have proposed, as a general version of the design of the capsule, and the option of a successful solution for any particular element of its design. The summing up of the competition and the testing of life-sized prototypes created by this time are planned to be held in June 2016.

After the election of the winning team, the final version of the capsule will be used as the basis for the construction of a full-size commercial prototype.

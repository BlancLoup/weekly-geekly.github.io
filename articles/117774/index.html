Findings:


Yota Egg is a unique device. It comes to any device equipped with Wi-Fi, and it can provide several devices with the Internet at the same time. If you have a need to deploy a mobile office and connect to the network iPhone, iPad, any other communicators, tablets, laptops and desktops running any modern operating system, Yota Egg is what you need. However, comparing it with USB and Express Card modems is not entirely correct. Along with all its advantages, it is larger, more expensive, has a limited battery life (needs a wall outlet or USB cable connection). Therefore, the "classic" modems are still relevant.

ExpressCard modem - SWC E100 - in my opinion the best choice for the owner of MacBook Pro 17 ". It is more compact than a USB sibling, provides the highest speed and leaves free a USB port, of which there are only three on the laptop.

SWC - U200 is suitable for any MacBook model (not only Pro 17 "), iMac, Mac PRO and Yota Ready routers. It is much more versatile.

The Yota software doesn‚Äôt look raw on Mac, works flawlessly, but it could be adapted better to MacOS in style. I did not encounter difficulties in the process of working with devices, my expectations were not deceived, so I cannot help but recommend Yota as an excellent solution for high-speed mobile Internet to owners of Apple devices.

In this review, all Yota mobile devices were tested, except Yota Jingle. It does not support MacOS, but if you use Windows, you can pay attention to it, since it is the most affordable model (1,790 rubles).

A real war veteran, the Chopper AR100B Drone completed over 1200 missions.

Less than a kilogram of weight, only 200 grams of payload and a meter in diameter - this is AR100B. It is not at all like the MQ-9 Reaper, capable of lifting 1,300 kilograms of explosives and Hellfire missiles. But the fact that the AR100B lacks lift is more than compensated for by the possibilities of observation. More than 1,200 missions in Afghanistan make it clear that the AR100B is unprecedentedly easy to use, and its agility makes this drone the most valuable vehicle during ground missions.
AR100B was created by AirRobot, a company specializing in unmanned aerial vehicles and offices located throughout the world, including the UK and the United States. As stated in the latest drones update , the AR100B boasts more powerful engines and enduring batteries. It is quiet and easy to use, and so simple that ordinary soldiers can handle it with little or no training. Its four screws give the drone the possibility of vertical take-off and landing, a range of up to a kilometer and a maximum flight time of 20 minutes. Infrared cameras and night vision cameras can be used to record high-quality video (HD video) or as a camera during reconnaissance and surveillance tasks.

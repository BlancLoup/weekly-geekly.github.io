Hacking Kaspersky Crackme: a study of the protective mechanism (Part 1)

The ZeroNight2015 reverse engineering competition , held by Kaspersky Lab, has recently ended. The organization of the competition itself, from my point of view, was lame, but the article is not about that. The competition presented an interesting task called ‚ÄúSmartphone‚Äù, which will be the focus of this series of articles. This article will be devoted to the description of the conditions of the problem and the search for a protective mechanism. The second article will affect the optimization of the speed of the program being cracked through the introduction of X-code. The third article will describe the process of finding errors in the embedded code using unit testing.

Let's start with a description of the problem. The following description of the task and the possibility to download the .exe program file are given on the competition site.



After starting the program, we see a window with a suggestion to enter an email and a serial number.



The challenge is to find out the serial number for your own email address. After solving the problem, the serial number is sent for verification using the form on the site, which first indicates its own email address and nickname, which will be displayed in the rating.



After entering a random serial number in a crackme, the program warns that my computer is too slow and I will have time to grow old before I wait for the result of the check. After closing the warning message, the program hangs hopelessly, loading up one full processor core.



An attempt to wait for the result of checking the serial number is doomed to failure and it remains only to forcibly complete the program. This is partly the uniqueness of this task.

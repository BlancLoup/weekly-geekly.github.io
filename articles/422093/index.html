Part 1: The Importance of Context


Exploratory vs explanatory. Data analysis is similar to finding pearls: sometimes you need to open 100 oysters (check 100 hypotheses) to find a pair of pearls. In the process of such work, we visualize the data for ourselves. When it comes time to talk about the results, very often there is a desire to use the same charts and tell about all the work done: ‚ÄúWe open the first oyster. Is empty. But do not worry, we still have 99 oysters. Open the second. Don't do that. It is necessary to separate the research ( exploratory ) analysis from the explanatory ( explanatory ). At the stage of explanatory analysis, it is necessary to lay time for it, it is necessary to do other visualizations depending on the context: to whom, what and how.

One of the interesting examples where I would like to see an explanatory analysis instead of a research one - the article ‚Äú Analysis of Habrahabr and Geektimes articles ‚Äù. Part 7: Narrative Lessons


Good story is exciting. Based on the experience of literature and cinema, the author shows how to tell interesting stories using data. One of the tips: before preparing a presentation, write a ‚Äúbig idea‚Äù (express the main ideas in one sentence) and a ‚Äúthree-minute story‚Äù (describe the main findings on a half page of text).

Each story has 3 main parts: the beginning, the middle and the end.

  1. Start. Indicates a problem. If there is no clear problem, then perhaps the presentation is also not needed. "Why should this interest me?"
  2. The middle . Shows how the problem can be solved. "Why can I make a proposed decision?"
  3. The end. Call to action. ‚ÄúWhat do I need to start doing right now to solve the problem?‚Äù

There are two main story strategies:

1. In chronological order:

a. Identified the problem
b. Collected data to better understand the situation.
c. Analyzed data (reviewed the situation from different angles, took into account other factors)
d. Found a solution
e. Based on all this, we recommend taking the following actions.

2. Start from the end:

a. We recommend doing this and that
b. We support this recommendation with data

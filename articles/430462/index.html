Today it became known that the competence center for the Digital Economy program has developed the concept of a draft law on access to data for users of social networks. This document assumes the introduction of the responsibility of Internet sites for the safety of user data from closed profiles. The developers of the bill believe that social networks can monopolize the data market, therefore they offer certain measures to overcome this situation.

The document proposes informing users of the user‚Äôs account‚Äôs Internet sites about the type of social network. For example, if the site is open, the user must agree that his data will become publicly available to an unlimited number of people.

If the site provides privacy settings, giving the user the opportunity to close his account, then it is responsible for the security of non-public information. The authors of the bill believe that any legal restriction of access to publicly available information allows creating unequal conditions for domestic and foreign data market players.

The authors intend to prepare the text of this bill by the end of November. Then he will be sent for consideration to the CE working group, and after that - for approval to the federal government bodies, as reported by Kommersant .

By the way, Deputy Prime Minister Maskim Akimov knows about the preparation of this bill. Moreover, his representatives report that the document is not included in the priority package. In turn, in "Rostelecom" claim that the amendments are already being studied, and representatives of the department will participate in their discussion. According to the Ministry of Economy, in case of liberalization of the mode of working with publicly available data, it is necessary to provide for compensation mechanisms, as well as additional protection of human and citizen rights.

The authors of the bill call the most popular sites "Vkontakte", Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, "Classmates" and Twitter. In the social networks of the Russian-speaking segment of the Internet more than 200 million Russian-active profiles. They generate more than 90 million messages per day.

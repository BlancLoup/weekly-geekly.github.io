The Speech Technology Center provides speech bases for creating a prototype speech recognition system.

In order to find talented specialists ready to devote themselves to the development of speech technologies in Russia, the Speech Technology Center (MDG) provides its own speech bases. They contain not just sound files with text, but also a time markup made by MDG specialists.

The lack of bases significantly limits researchers in working on Russian speech recognition algorithms, as the collection and creation of a speech base is a very time-consuming work that can hardly be done by independent scientists and start-ups. Using the data provided by the MDGs, savvy researchers will be able to quite easily develop a prototype system for recognition of continuous (!) Speech and submit it to the Native 2013 contest, which is conducted by the MDGs together with ITMO and with the technical support of the Marketing to Innovation business network Education, Science ‚Äù, created on the basis of ITMO.

The main prize for the winner will be a job offer - the opportunity to work as part of the MDG development team at the head office in St. Petersburg. In addition, the winner will receive a cash bonus of 100,000 rubles. Participants who took second and third places will also be able to count on working in the company and on valuable prizes - iPhone 5 and iPad 4.

Detailed information about the conditions of the competition. Registration deadline extended until the end of April.

The Speech Technology Center is a Russian company that is a global leader in speech technology. The MDGs are developing in the areas of speech synthesis and recognition, voice biometrics, speech processing and high-quality recording. The proposed solutions are in demand in more than 75 countries of the world. The MDGs treat their employees with full attention, which is confirmed by the 7th place among Russian employers and the 3rd place among IT companies of the country in the all-Russian rating, which the HeadHunter conducted at the end of 2012.

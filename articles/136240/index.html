A look at men's smartphones Motorola RAZR, RAZR MAXX and DROID 4

We have already talked about the two tablets exhibited by Motorola at the CES conference. Today we will talk about three smartphones: RAZR , its version with an increased battery RAZR MAXX and keyboard DROID 4 .



The hardware of the whole trio is almost the same: TI OMAP 4430 dual-core processor with 1.2 GHz frequency, support for LTE networks, 8 MP main camera (front 2 or 1.3 MP), 4.3-inch Super AMOLED display with a resolution of 540x960 ( but 4-inch TFT in Droid 4 ), 16 GB of internal memory, 1 GB of RAM and a slot for MicroSD . Now about all separately.

The difference between the RAZR and the RAZR MAXX is that the latter has a very capacious 3,300 mAh battery (the RAZR has 1,780). Due to this, it increased, but the thickness of the device remained quite acceptable: 9 mm against the original 7.1 in the RAZR. The back cover of stylish metal cases RAZR'ov made of Kevlar coating.



DROID 4 has a backlit sliding qwerty keyboard . All fellows on the line work on Android 2.3 and have a Motorola proprietary shell, which includes the MOTOCAST service for synchronizing multimedia files with a computer and the Smart Actions application designed to automatically switch modes and launch various functions.

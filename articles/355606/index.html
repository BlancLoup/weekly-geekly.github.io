Windows 10 will ship on DVD and flash drives.



As July 29 approaches, more and more details about the final release of Windows 10 appear. Many people ask whether the system will be distributed as an image that can be downloaded and installed independently, not even a name on a computer licensed and genuine Windows 7 and 8.1.

Apparently, it is. And there is even the best news: versions of Windows 10 will go on sale both on DVD and on flash drives. The corresponding screenshot was published by the German site Winfuture.de.



This is good news because some modern laptops do not have a DVD drive, but there is always a USB connector. In addition, installation from a flash drive is faster than from a DVD.

However, in any case, the ISO image is likely to be available for download.

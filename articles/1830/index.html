Synterra is going to develop WiMAX in the Moscow region and regions

Synterra is planning to develop a WiMAX wireless access network in the Moscow region and the Central Federal District. Two-year investments in the project - in 2006 and 2007 - will amount to $ 4.5 million, Prime-TASS reports.

Wireless access services will be provided in 6 cities of the Central Federal District and the Moscow region. To recoup the project is planned for three years since the launch of Synterra WiMAX networks.

The company has the most scarce resources, radio frequencies: Synterra has certain frequencies in the 2.5-2.7 GHz range in 16 regions of the Central Federal District, Moscow and the Moscow Region.

In the capital, the company completed the construction of its own wireless network in April 2006, at the same time it announced the mass connection of corporate clients. During the first half of the year, Synterra's networks in Moscow generated income from services of more than $ 850 thousand. About 800 subscribers managed to connect during this time. The monthly network ARPU (average revenue brought to the operator by one subscriber) was about $ 170.

With the release of the first versions of Andorid OS - the 1st version in October 2008, the 2nd version in 2010, the 3rd version in 2011 - it was not at first clear whether this was good or bad - one thing is clear, this is innovative! - battery life

For each item of consumer qualities I want to write a little more specifically with examples.


Battery life
At a time when devices on Android hardly fit into 2-3 days of battery life, if they are not heavily loaded, Nokia based on the Symbian OS worked fine for an average of 5 days. I think everyone knows it perfectly. And what do we have in the 4th android? Mega powerful processors, faster than those on which we played DOOM at one time, who are trying to revive this clumsy interface, and so there is only 2-3 days left to work, even on new devices.



Smartphone ergonomics
Basic actions for the phone: dialing, dialing SMS, answer a call. The remaining functions - the browser, camera and others I consider additional, which should not be more convenient priority.
I will tell you that after E72 dialing the number on the droid background is a sheer torment. Firstly, you do not feel the response of pressing the drawn buttons, but we will write this down to the technology, and not to the OS. Secondly, to dial the number you need to run some application in which to search for my contacts. When searching for contacts terribly freezes when scrolling, which causes irritation. Compare with Nokia E72 - in the main screen mode, we start typing the name / surname of the required contact and, lo and behold, the contact is already in front of our eyes - with two clicks we make a call.

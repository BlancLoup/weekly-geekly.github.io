Wall-E robots recreated in reality



Despite the abundance of all sorts of copies of the cartoon Wall-E robot in real life (just look at some products for children in toy stores), there are practically no similar models. But now millions of kids and just fans of Pixar (and lovers of robotics too) can be happy. Small scavenger robot reproduced in reality with maximum accuracy. True, the robot turned out without a "soul", that is, the version that worked in the cartoon after replacing the chip, remember? Unfortunately, the real robot is incapable of tender feelings.

Nevertheless, the copy turned out so realistic that it remains only to be surprised. On the other hand, the work on the project was carried out for a long time, and the hands of the master (Michael McMaster) and his friends are very smooth, so everything worked out perfectly.

According to the developers, the Blu-Ray version of the cartoon helped them a lot, where you can find any little thing, and transfer this little thing (for example, scratches and rust) to a real object. In addition to appearance, Wall-E also acts, and "speaks" as we all used to (for the cartoon, of course). Melodies, sounds - all of this is loaded into the memory of the robot, exactly as it was in the cartoon.

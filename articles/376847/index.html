FedEx refuses to deliver machine for weapons parts



The FedEx delivery service last week refused to service the delivery of Defense Distributed startup products. The refusal is motivated by the fact that the startup sells automatic milling machines Ghost Gunner, on which it is possible to manufacture parts for weapons. A FedEx spokesperson said the company will not ship until it understands whether such a device is not subject to any special licensing or control.

The startup is run by a well-known activist, a fighter for the open market and the availability of weapons, Cody Wilson . Defense Distributed is engaged in the promotion and dissemination of schemes and methods for making weapons at home It was Wilson who made the first gun printed on a 3D printer two years ago. Now the startup offers a more serious thing.

Ghost Gunner , The Ghost Gunsmith is an automatic table milling machine. In a cube with an edge of about 35 cm are the mechanisms that ensure the operation of the movable cutter.

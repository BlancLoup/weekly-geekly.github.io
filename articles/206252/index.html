Roofs, water and iron pipes - Nokia Lumia 1020 in the hands of photoextreme

Hi, Habr!
Today we want to tell you about one of our special projects, which is truly breathtaking. Therefore, remove from your screens women, children and especially impressionable people.



The fact is that we gave our 41-megapixel Nokia Lumia 1020 smartphones to the real masters of epic photography, to photographers with a developed sense of beauty, to people with nerves of steel and everything else - well-known driver Vitaly Raskalov, a no less famous photographer and blogger Dmitry Chistoprudov, as well as diver and underwater hunter Oleg Gavrilin.

What came out of it, look under the cut.

We managed to talk a bit with Dmitry and Vitaly, ask them about the shooting, the roofs, the new technologies and much more. But first, a little about the authors themselves pictures.


Vitaly Raskalov is an extreme, rufer, recently recognized as one of the 25 best photo bloggers by the Russian Photo portal. However, Vitaly himself says that he does not want to be remembered as a photographer. Although it is difficult to deny his creative potential, especially given the fact that Vitali constantly walks on the razor's edge, conquering the height without permission and insurance. The main tool for shooting is the Canon 40D digital SLR.










photo: Vitaliy Raskalov


Dmitry Chistoprudov - photographer, traveler, blog author and co-founder of the photo agency "28-300" (founded in 2009 with Ilya Varlamov ). Dmitry specializes in architectural photography, aerial photography, roof shooting and production reporting. People and animals, in his own words, interest Dmitri much less.
Dmitry is a car designer by training. He made a long way into professional photography, working as a designer in the watch and construction companies, working in printing, websites and advertising, as well as three-dimensional graphics.










photo: Dmitry Chistoprudov


Oleg Gavrilin is a freediving and underwater hunting instructor, one of the leading trophy underwater hunters in Russia, team captain and participant in the European and underwater hunting championships.



What exciting, interesting, and maybe fun moments were on the set? Perhaps while shooting on our smartphone?

Dmitry: The phone has always been of interest to colleagues when I started shooting at it. At first they celebrated his stylish design, and when I shot dimly lit scenes with them, the result was amazing. This was due to the fact that in the live view on the phone screen, almost nothing was visible (it was on the Royal Square in Barcelona). But after 4 seconds of shooting, the city "manifested" on the screen, and behind the back was heard the many-voiced "wow."

Many late passengers viewed with curiosity a cunning device for shooting.

The history of communication towers 2: forms and types



There are many interesting communication towers in the world - from a constructive and aesthetic point of view. About them under the "cut".

Cellular towers are the most common class of supporting structures in the telecommunications world. This is due to the fact that for sustainable coverage it is necessary to create a whole network of numerous base stations (moreover, there are even more of them in cities than in open territories). In addition, many operators create their own infrastructure, "duplicating" coverage areas. As a result, cities are filling up more and more with such constructions.

From an engineering point of view, there are three types of towers:

  • Frame . These are voluminous lattice structures - free-standing towers. Possess high wind resistance and carrying capacity.


  • Mast In fact, they represent a pole on which antennas are fixed and, sometimes, system modules (such ‚Äúboxes‚Äù that are responsible for receiving and transmitting data separately for each standard - GSM, CDMA, LTE).



    These towers are the cheapest in the manufacture and installation, they are usually placed where there is little free space or there are restrictions on the maximum height of structures.



  • With delays . These are high mast and narrow frame towers. The stability of the system gives them stretch marks. Such towers make it possible to place antennas relatively cheaply at high altitude, but at the same time, they formally occupy a very large area, outlined by stretch marks. Therefore, they are usually placed in industrial zones or outside the city limits, in rural areas. In addition, due to its height, such towers often perform the function of repeaters.




    There are very compact versions of towers with quickdraws. Usually they are erected on the roofs of buildings where, for some reason, it is impossible to install a freestanding turret.



Of course, within each of the described species there are a lot of variations. For example, mast towers are made of pipes of constant diameter or gradually tapering towards the top.

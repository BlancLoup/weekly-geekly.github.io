Several 3D games for Linux

Comrades from the blog Online Games have made a good list of 3D-games under Linux OS. And taken not randomly from the ceiling, but received some, but recognition from gaming magazines in the form of reviews and, in some cases, awards. Many of the toys in the list below are cross-platform, but all of them are completely free and come with an installer for Linux, so the same Wine will not be needed to run them.

America's Army
Tactical multiplayer shooter America's Army released as part of a public initiative, as conceived by the American government, capable of attracting recruits to the American army. The first version of the game appeared in 2002. At the moment, a stable Linux-compatible version has a sequence number of 2.5.


Tremulous
The Tremulous open source team shooter is a bit like the Quake 2 mod called Gloom and the Half-Life Natural Selection mod. In 2006, the game was downloaded more than 200 thousand times, and she received the award ‚ÄúGame of the Year: Choice of Players‚Äù in the ‚ÄúMod of the Year‚Äù competition organized by the Mod Database.


Tux racer
Simple "race" with a penguin "in the lead role." Relatively nice graphics and considerable potential of replaisability due to the possibility of modifying the game with mods. It's a pity that the last release took place in 2001.


Nexuiz
The free, cross-platform shooter Nexuiz, made on the basis of a modified Quake engine, was released in 2005. The current version (2.3) is dated 2007 and works under Linux, Mac OS and Windows. Of the amenities worth highlighting the support of 64 players in multiplayer mode, a dynamic lighting system (almost the same as in Doom 3) and the built-in "bot generator" for a single game.


Alien Arena 2007
Alien Arena shooter, which appeared in 2004, is very similar to Quake 2. Supports both single-player mode (bots are available, of course) and multiplayer. The game even has a built-in browser, with which you can find rivals, as well as a chat program like IRC. Alien Arena 2007 runs on Linux, Windows and FreeBSD.


TrueCombat: Elite
This game is a completely reworked modification of Wolfenstein: Enemy Territory. It is free and works on all platforms. There are both multiplayer and single modes.


The Open Racing Car Simulator
Racing simulator, engine which uses technology OpenGL. 50 different machines, 20 tracks, 50 opponents and support for multiplayer (up to 4 people) will not let you get bored. And you can run the game under Linux, FreeBSD, MacOSX and Windows. Developers boast a model of damage to cars and aerodynamic effects. An online multiplayer version will be available soon.


Frets on fire
The winner of the Assembly Demo Party 2006, Frets On Fire, is a music video game in which you play notes scrolling around the screen using the keyboard, which should result in a song. It is enough to imagine that the keyboard is a guitar. By the way, the game has the ability and compose musical compositions.


Scorched 3D
Everyone remembers "tanchiki"? Well, Scorched 3D is what they are, only in 3D. A follower of the DOS "mother of all games" goes on all platforms, allows you to generate landscapes and gives a lot of pleasure.

HyTAQ Robot - a bird in a cage

The Robotics Laboratory at the Illinois Institute of Technology presented its development: HyTAQ Robot (Hybrid Terrestrial and Aerial Quadrotor). They put a familiar quadrocopter in a wheel that resembles a birdcage.




Such an elegant solution avoids many problems. Firstly, in the rolling mode, much less energy is consumed than the quadcopter needs in flight. Secondly, all the obstacles encountered while driving on the surface, it is easy to overcome, just by flying over them. According to the developers, this technology allows to increase by 4 times the range of movement on a single battery charge, compared to a conventional quadcopter. The total operation time of the device increases 6 times.


The robot can be used in various search operations that require high mobility and overcoming obstacles. However, it can be used outside of the Earth. Such a device would be great for exploring the expanses of Titan - the satellite of Saturn.


Previously it was proposed to study it with the help of airplanes or a boat floating in its methane lakes. HyTAQ Robot could be the optimal tool for the study of Titan, if it were possible to solve the problem with a long-term source of energy. Well, to ensure survivability in extremely low temperatures.

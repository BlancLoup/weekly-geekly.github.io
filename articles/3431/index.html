Chicago will ban social networks

Schoolchildren and visitors to the libraries of the state of Illinois (USA), starting next year, are unlikely to be able to visit the sites of various social networks. A little more than a week ago, state senator Matt Murphy (Matt Murphy) published a bill called Social Networking Website Prohibition Act, in effect prohibiting access to social sites from public places.

It is assumed that the law, if adopted in its current form, will enter into force on January 1, 2008. Resource Tame The Web , which also proposes to read the text of the law , encourages Illinois teachers and librarians to unite and repel human rights violators.

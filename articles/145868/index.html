Statistics and impressions from the course on electronics MITx 6.002x

After the end of the final exam and the issuance of electronic certificates, MITx 6.002x employees published a brief statistic:

Course statistics: 6.002x had 154,763 registrants. Of these, 69,221 people looked at, and earned 26,349 earned at least one point on it. It was still open, 10,547 people, and 9,318 people. It‚Äôs still 10,262 people, it‚Äôs still open for 8,240 people. Finally, after completing 14 weeks of study, 7,157 people have earned the first certificate awarded by MITx, proving that they successfully completed 6.002x.

At 6.002x, 154,763 people registered. Of these, 69,221 people opened the page with the first set of tasks and 26,349 of them gave at least one correct answer to the questions of the tasks. 13,569 people opened the page with the tasks of the midterm-exam * when the answers were still being taken, 10,547 people answered at least one question, 9,318 people passed the exam. 10,262 people opened the page with the final exam during its validity period, 8,240 people answered at least one question, and 5,800 people passed the exam. * midterm-exam - exam after passing half the course

Now, probably, the necessary explanations - hardly everyone knows what MITx is and a little about the process through the eyes of a student.

About the course itself

Briefly about the training (ask questions if you are interested in some details): It all started with a small set of videos that told about how the course will be conducted, about the schedule, about the honor code. All lectures were organized in 14 weekly sets, with two tracks in each set. The track is 10-20 videos for 5-10 minutes, they are abundantly interspersed with tasks that need to be solved and give answers. Some of the tasks are solved intuitively, the other part (according to my feelings - more) - requires solutions on paper. Again, some of the tasks ask about the numerical solution, i.e. You can score an equation in Excel or MatLab and without any problems get an answer or pick a parameter. Part asks to write the solution in algebraic form. A couple of examples:

(1 / (C * w * j)) * (R + j * w * L) / ((1 / (C * w * j)) + (R + j * w * L))

(1 / K + RS * (vIN-VT) -sqrt (1 / (K ^ 2) + 2 * RS * (vIN-VT) / K)) / (RS ^ 2)

I estimate my work for about ten hours per week, of which six hours per lecture (three per track) and four hours at homework (most often from Saturday to Sunday). The tasks are really difficult (for me), often capture the material of the next lecture, where they tell you how to solve the problem easier.

In order to successfully complete the course, I had to ‚Äúremember‚Äù the mathematics, or rather, learn to find the derivative, integrate, solve difures, and work with complex numbers anew. Not that it was a comprehensive course in mathematics, but rather several techniques for solving equations of a certain type.

Each weekly set has a lab where you have to solve a problem, usually a circuit design, i.e. draw a scheme (unpretentious) and pick up the nominal components to meet the conditions of the problem. Most of the labs are unpretentious, but when doing one, I barely broke my head on the table.

Sets are published every week and you have about three weeks to study the material. During these three weeks will be published two more sets. If you postpone for later (as I would only seem to be a student, hands remember :-)), then you will always be under pressure - you will prolong the term and that's it, the answers are not accepted.

Of all the households (12) and lab (12), the best 10 (those and those) are counted. Those. two theoretically can prodinamit. But if you don‚Äôt do them, you‚Äôll almost surely fail one or two questions for each homework that was not done on the exam.

Examinations are published after the 7th and after the 14th weeks. Their duration is much less - 4, perhaps, a day. If, again, you postpone the homework for later, then at some point you need to first spend the time to decide the homework, and then another exam to write - no one will push it back. Baska explodes.

The first (midterm) exam I did for 4 hours, it was quite simple. I wrote the final, probably, hours 8-9. And managed to make two mistakes. As a result, 96% for the final exam and 98% for the entire course. Certificates issue two - with an assessment and without. Each certificate is registered with a link, passing on which you can check the validity of the certificate.

Feelings are mixed. Joy, pride, emptiness, etc. Remember yourself after defending a diploma (I stupidly sat in the Bauman canteen and chewed buckwheat from the basics, completely unaware of what had happened and how I would live on - these were similar feelings after passing the MITx exam). Yes, and a strange feeling from the fact that it was the first and only course from MITx, they decided to unite with Harvard and do EdX ( scandals, intrigues, investigations ). But the main feeling - IT TOGO STANDING, I WANT MORE. In general, if you think "it is worth it or not it is worth it" - definitely worth it.

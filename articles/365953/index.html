Sony Smartwatch 3. Android Wear from Japan

All smart watches on Android Wear are about the same in their capabilities. They usually differ only in the type of screen and build quality, as well as the design, of course. Sony decided to go their usual way - to try to fit their best technologies into a waterproof case. On the pros and cons of this approach and talk.



Specifications


The full name of the model is Sony SmartWatch 3 (SWR50). It is noteworthy that in the model index there is already a number 3, although this does not mean that the company managed to release three hours, while the rest of the companies released only 1-2 models. Sony did have previous SmartWatch models, but had nothing to do with the Android Wear platform.

The iron of the watch can be envied by any smartphone flagship of four years ago, although for the current generation of smart watches the platform can be called "standard". Inside are hidden: Qualcomm Snapdragon 400 quad-core processor (1.2 GHz, ARM v7); video accelerator with support for OpenGL ES 3.0 and OpenCL 1.2 - Adreno 305 (450 MHz); 512 MB of RAM and 4 GB of memory for data storage, as well as GPS, Bluetooth 4.0 LE and NFC modules.

The battery is modest, only 420 mAh, but the device is also small. The screen from the sky of stars is not enough, but the resolution is higher than that of the watch from the same LG: 320x320 pixels. Auto-brightness is, the light sensor almost never lies. Unfortunately, the display is not OLED / AMOLED, but a regular LCD matrix with a transreflective layer - the watch does not go blind in the sun, but it‚Äôs far from the ‚Äúrichness‚Äù of OLED models, and there is no opportunity to work with the constantly active screen. Secondly, they look great on their own.



The display has a slightly curved glass, almost does not rise above the strap. The latter, by the way, is monolithic and made of thick rubber with a plastic insert around the display. In addition, the strap is replaceable.



The clock is removed "inside", therefore, even though the crawl is swimming, even if it hits the pear, it will not fall out. The clasp is made in the form of a classic clip with two arc elements, no buckles, teeth and dangling tails clinging to the sleeves.



The fit is available in a very wide range, so the watch will suit both fragile girls and more long-distance strokes (although the device will look more funny than serious). Work with the device


Watches from Sony are on board Android Wear 4.3 with the ability to upgrade to 5.0. First of all the update to 4.4 swings, and then - to the latest version. Unlike LG, where the update took 10 minutes, these hours totally downloaded updates for a good three hours, after which they put them another 40 minutes.

Wear system is simple and effective. On the main screen - the clock, by "tapu" - voice / touch menu. The screen displays hints that you can order the device to do, but all the same actions (except for entering text) can also be performed "manually." The OS itself resembles a hybrid of the Google Now service and a fitness bracelet in terms of functions and way of interacting with them.

The entire interface is very well fitted and addictive in just ten minutes. For pairing with a smartphone, you can use NFC technology or manually turn on Bluetooth in the old-fashioned way, since the clock displays step-by-step instructions that even a baby can handle.



Out of the box, without installing additional applications, the clock can: display the time, work as a fitness tracker (although they do not have a built-in pulse meter, which is strange for a sports-oriented device), show reminders from a smartphone, give hints on navigation, work with a predictive system -and-not-so-help Google Now, control music from your smartphone or listen to it directly from the device (or even Play Music would allow to synchronize music normally)

There is a compass. And thanks to the GPS module, the watch can be used as a GPS tracker, and not necessarily with the participation of a smartphone: data can be synchronized after a run. As a service, you can use either standard applications like Runtastic / Runkeeper / MyTracks / iFit outside, or the Ghostracer application developed specifically for SmartWatch 3.

In general, in many respects all the same functions as any other devices of this form factor and platform: until Google expands the capabilities of the Android Wear OS itself, nothing will change. Oh, and you can always mess with the official SDK, install GTA3 on the clock and play, yes. The watches have more than enough power, the game goes even if it is not fast, but without noticeable problems.

Ramos W41 - 4-core tablet at an affordable price.


A year after the release of the dual-core Rockchip RK3066 and Amlogic 8726-MX chips, Chinese manufacturers are again updating their chip fleet.
So, this year we are waiting for these 4-nuclear solutions:
Allwinner A31 - 4 Cortex-A7 cores with a frequency up to 1.4 GHz, GPU PowerVR SGX544MP2.
Rockchip RK3188 - 4 Cortex-A9 cores with frequency up to 1.8 GHz, Mali 400MP4 GPU.
Actions ATM7029 / ATM7025 - 4 Cortex-A9 cores with frequencies up to 1.5 GHz (1 GHz in ATM7025), Vivante GC1000 + GPU.

Of the well-known, but little-spread in Chinese products, the Samsung Exynos 4412 chip has been used - 4 Cortex-A9 cores with a frequency of up to 1.4 GHz, the Mali 400MP4 GPU.

The fate of the Freescale i.MX 6 Quad chip presented last year (4 cores Cortex A9 1.2GHz, Vivante GC2000 CPU) is still foggy. My colleague vvzvlad already wrote about the Ampe A10 Quad tablet. A very promising chip, but the Vivante GC2000 GPU did not receive good developer support, which is why there are problems with 3D games.

From the new screens we are expected:
9.7-inch tablets with IPS screen resolution of 2048x1536 pixels.
10.1-inch tablets with a screen resolution of 1920x1200 pixels.
And from the novelties in the Chinese market - 9.4-inch tablets with a screen resolution of 1280x800.
The main problem of all tablets manufactured in China is the quality of the screens. In the case of 9.4 inches, manufacturers chose the right solution - use a proven screen produced by LG Display for the Sony Tablet S.
At the moment, tablets with a diagonal of 9.4 inches are only 3. These are Pipo M8 on RK3066, Ramos W42 on Samsung Exynos 4412, Ramos W41 on Actions ATM7025.
The price of Ramos W42 is quite high (~ $ 250), the Pipo M8 on the RK3066 can be referred to as ‚Äúobsolete‚Äù. Of interesting remains the tablet Ramos W41, about which will be discussed in this review.

An interesting option looks like a 28nm Rockchip RK3188 chip with a 1.8GHz CPU frequency and the popular Mali 400MP4 overclocked GPU running at 533 MHz. On this chip, several tablets are already planned, which will go on sale in early to mid-March.

But back to our Actions ATM7025 chip produced by Actions Semiconductors, the main alleged problem of which may be compatibility with 3D games due to the use of the new Vivante GC1000 + GPU.
Information about this chip is not yet available both in the English version of Actions Semiconductors and in Chinese .
There are 2 new chips from Actions: ATM7025 and ATM7029. At the moment of the known differences is the frequency: 1 GHz in the ATM7025 and 1.5 GHz ATM7029. The official website of the manufacturer does not specify which chip is used in the Ramos W41. And judging by the information from the brochure, the younger model Action ATM7025 is installed, which operates at a frequency of 1.1 GHz

Cortex-A9 or Cortex-A5?
The tablet manufacturer claims the CPU as Cortex-A9, but look at the system information in the Android System Info:



As can be seen from the value of the CPU part 0xc05 parameter, Android System Info hints about the Cortex-A5.
Looking at the line Features we see that there is support for vfp v4, but Cortex-A9 only has support for vfp v3.
Based on this information, 2 assumptions can be made:
1) The Actions ATM7025 uses Cortex-A5.
2) The manufacturer added support for vpf v4 in Cortex-A9 and, for a proper definition, pretends to be Cortex-A5.

By the way, in the latest firmware update, the CPU part line mysteriously disappeared from the information about the CPU in the Android System Info program.

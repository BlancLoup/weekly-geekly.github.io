Laptop review Dell Alienware M18x

The general public is presented with an updated Dell Alienware M18x. It was possible to feel this novelty, the first impressions under the habrakat.

On the contrary, everything speaks of a serious approach to business, power and quality. Body materials are non-marking plastic in combination with aluminum. The display mounts provide a maximum opening angle of the laptop about 135 degrees and do not need snaps.



It is worth noting the AlienFX backlight system - you can choose one of 20 colors with which the keyboard, case and logos will be illuminated while working with a laptop. At the same time, you can choose different colors for all elements to create a unique backlight design.



The 18.4-inch glossy display with a resolution of Full HD (16: 9, 1920 x 1080 pixels) is installed in the M18x. The diagonal is 46.7 cm (almost half a meter!), More than enough for a game or movie. The backlight power, according to independent testing, is 221.9 cd / m¬≤, and the contrast is 736: 1. Color rendering is also high: the sRGB spectrum is completely covered. A webcam and two microphones are built into the display frame.



Full-size keyboard with large keys 18x18 mm is very happy. It is necessary to thank Dell engineers for their work on ergonomics. Recoil, stroke depth, click volume from pressing - everything is pleasant to the touch.



The large touchpad (99x55 mm) - a smooth but non-sticky surface - supports multi-touch and document scrolling.



As for the hardware stuffing, then the handwriting of Alienware is easy to read. In all versions of the laptop, you can find only the Core i7 of the newest generation, Ivy Bridge. Due to Turbo Boost 2.0 support and overclocking capabilities via Alienware BIOS, these processors can operate at frequencies up to 4.2 GHz. Integrated into the processor is an economical HD 4000 video core, which is activated when the performance of the main video subsystem is not required. There is a modification with a pair of Radeon HD 7970M. The Radeon HD 7970M uses the GCN architecture (the graphics chip is manufactured using the 28 nm process technology). If you compare it with desktop solutions, you immediately notice a significant similarity with the Radeon HD 7870 - the same chip, but with a reduced clock frequency. The frequency is reduced to 850 MHz (however, taking into account the use of 1280 computing shaders, the frequency is quite sufficient). But 2 Gigabytes of fast GDDR5 memory (256 bits, 1200 MHz QDR, bandwidth 153.6 Gb / s) have not gone anywhere. HD 7970M supports DirectX 11.1, as well as many technologies to speed up CPU calculations (such as OpenCL and DirectCompute). In this configuration, the novelty is gaining more than 11,000 points in 3DMark 11. This is almost twice the performance of all previously released graphics cards from AMD and Nvidia. Also available are configurations with a single HD 7970M graphics card, with an Nvidia GeForce GTX 675M (with both one and two). Additionally, you need to say that the user can choose a configuration with RAM up to 32 GB of RAM, and immediately two HDD or SSD.
Alienware, as always, has equipped its laptop with sockets and ports in large quantities.



On the right side of the face - two USB 3.0, plus a combination of eSATA / USB 2.0 and HDMI input. ExpressCard54, a 9-in-1 card reader and an optical drive are also installed here.



On the left side there are 4 audio connectors (3 outputs, 1 input), three video outputs (VGA, HDMI, DisplayPort), Kensington, RJ-45 (LAN), and two high-speed USB 3.0.



Rear - power adapter input.

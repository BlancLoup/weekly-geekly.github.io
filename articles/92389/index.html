25 questions that will make your web application better

Based on this report, as well as its own little experience, a questionnaire was created that will help improve any web application.

Only 25 questions, add your application a point for each "Yes." If you are not sure of the answer, or you can answer positively only one part of the question, feel free to answer "No" and continue to the next.


25 questions that will make your web application better


  1. Can you clearly describe the problem your application solves? Is this problem real inconvenience?
  2. Can you describe how your application solves this problem in 10 words?
  3. Does the solution your application offers at least one of the following areas: improving the quality of life, fixing something wrong, or preventing the end of something good?
  4. If there was a wizard in the world who could instantly solve this problem for everyone, do you think that people would be willing to pay him for it?
  5. Do you know how much they would pay?
  6. Would you use your application yourself?
  7. Does your application have updated content? Can you clearly describe why users will return to your application?
  8. Can you clearly describe your users, including their number and where you can find them?
  9. Does your application offer a real and simple business model? Do users understand what they are paying for?
  10. Does your application have at least 3 unique functions? List them.
  11. Can the user start using the application immediately, literally in one action?
  12. Does your application offer the opportunity to try it for free?
  13. Do you personally know at least 10 people who love your application and agree to use it in everyday life?
  14. Do you like the way your app looks? Is it convenient for you to use it?
  15. Does your application run fast? Does it speed up problem solving?
  16. Does your application have its own ‚Äúvoice‚Äù - your own style of communication with the user?
  17. Was there anything in the application that could be removed, leaving the value of the application the same? Have you removed all this?
  18. Can third parties integrate with your application?
  19. Does the application provide customization tools for its look and content? Can the user make him "his"?
  20. Is every URL of your application pages clear? Can I remember him to share with others in offline?
  21. Does the application have at least 3 functional levers that contribute to its distribution? List them.
  22. Is it impossible to understand your application incorrectly? Does each page contain only the necessary information?
  23. Does the application set clear goals for the user? Is the process of reaching them interesting?
  24. Is your app available from mobile platforms?
  25. Can users learn anything useful from your application? Does your app teach anything? Describe how.

The most popular project of our team scored 15 points, there is something to work on. I think that startups with 20 points and more have good chances for success.

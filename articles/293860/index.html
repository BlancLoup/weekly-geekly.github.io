As a web-studio from Zelenograd to launch a multi-million start-up and not die

Today I will share our experience gained in the four years of the development of the Xenmart project. Readers are waiting for my personal insights and bad advice. If today I decided to do something like that, I would spend 2-3 times less time and money.

My Pareto Act for Startups

80% of the income should take no more than 20% of the time
the remaining 80% of the time should be invested in the future, receiving only 20% of income today.

The easiest way to win public attention is to do something inadequate, which many are not capable of, for example, donating the fruits of four years of your work to the masses with open source for free. But this should be done when you understand what you will get from it and how you will continue to work with it.

We must be generous Grandfather Frost! :)

Bad advice:



Free product can be clumsy!


If you think that you will be thanked for creating a free and at the same time absolutely useful product that is not understandable in work or enriched with jambs - you are mistaken! Your users at best will keep silent, at worst - they will arrange a good black PR to your startup.

Want to kill a project - listen to the tips!


Due to the fact that I was trying to find those who would help us, I lost a lot of time and did a lot of useless actions. If you do not know how to do better, then listen to those who did something like that. And if there are none, then rely on yourself. There will be no one to help you!

Hammer on traffic - you need the perfect product!


This approach nearly killed our company in 2014.
Throughout 2013, we were engaged only in Ksenmart , increased functionality, improved the interface, sawed templates, eventually updating the structure. Half the work had to be done anew.
Although the most important thing is to attract new users to the system, retain existing ones and engage in monetization.

And the amount was small (only 500,000 rubles), and the time and budget were calculated with a margin. But as soon as the funds arrived, urine hit the head. For some reason, they began to do first of all not important, but useful. For example, to issue templates to the raw structure (which then had to be rewritten). Thus, they hung around the neck the bondage of passive consumption.

This is my favorite! :) Today, for the main team of 12 people (the core of the company) we have 5 startups + a stream of customers who bring in money today. And we do all this hard at times, sometimes 10-14 hours a day and almost no days off.
But only if you are not maniacs frostbitten on our heads like us, it is better to concentrate on one thing - progress will be faster and more productive.

Pacman inspiration



Toru Iwatani is one of the most famous personalities in the video game industry. In April 1979, that is, almost 35 years ago, while working at Namco, he began to make the game PacMan, which was released on May 22, 1980. Toru Iwatani was a designer, programmer - Shigeo Funaki, Toshio Kai was responsible for the music. Almost the three of them invented the yellow Pac-Man, eating everything in its path. And today the game is popular. When in honor of the 30th anniversary of the game, Google launched PacMan Doodle , during the first day it played 505 million people. Pacman is mentioned several times in the Guinness Book of Records, in particular, as "the most successful game for slot machines."
Since 2007, Ivatani has been working as a professor at Tokyo Polytechnic University, where he lectures students on how games can bring happiness to people's lives.

Here are his thoughts on games and life, which Toru Iwatani presented to The Japan Times in June 2010.



A good game is always easy to understand. The player must immediately understand its meaning. For example, when you watch a game of golf, you immediately realize that the goal of the game is to roll the ball into the hole. The same, for example, with the game Super Mario Brothers , and with the game of pinball - in these games you know exactly what to do.

By the fun should be approached with all seriousness. Since childhood, I love itazura, that is, mischievous. I always did some damn things, suddenly jumping out of the box to surprise someone or play tricks on someone. I stuck plastic beetles to the springs so that they jumped out of the bag, tied my shoelaces to friends. I still do that sometimes. Fun - first of all! And also at the end of everything and in the middle.

It takes time to make a good game. Although game developers often work in "turbo mode", not all customers are usually patient. I understand both those and others. To create a good game it takes, probably, about 100 people, and a couple of years of time. And how many companies can afford it? But games are like good wine or whiskey. I try to go only to the cheapest places, there I see people as they are - someone happy, someone confused. Alcohol and chatter in such institutions flow in a continuous stream, so there you can learn a lot of interesting things.


To do something really good, you need to learn to empathize. You need to love people and want to make them happier. Doing Pacman, we thought a lot about what he feels, who is constantly being chased. We wanted not to torture the players, but just to tickle their nerves. In the end, Pacman always has a way to salvation, and it's fun!

If you are open and friendly, the results will come. Just do your work well, and everything will be as it should.

If you care about the quality of what you are doing, then you can probably get something good. The Japanese usually care about quality. To do something right you need preparation and thoroughness. We were born for this.

When an idea seems cool to you, ask at least 10 people what they think about it. I generally advise you to ask questions about everything and everyone.

Happy end is what everyone wants. It's great when we are confident in the future happy ending already at the beginning. This is something like a popular TV series on Japanese television, when we know for sure that at the end of each episode, all the bad ones will be punished, and the good ones will be rewarded.

Helping the weak and creating new opportunities for them is a great motivation to work.

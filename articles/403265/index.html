Hotel Industry Announces Airbnb War



There are many different services in the world that help people to travel. The leaders are Booking.com, working mainly with hotels and Airbnb - a service for finding and short-term rental of private housing around the world. Both companies are doing well and continue to grow. But if Booking helps the hotel business, allowing you to get new guests, then Airbnb, on the contrary, takes away customers from the hotel industry. After all, if earlier it was rather difficult to agree on a short-term rental apartment, now this issue is solved in just a few clicks.

So, the hotel business is not satisfied with the current situation and plans to declare war on Airbnb. Rather, it is already underway. Only, of course, not on the battlefield, but in a legal, legal field. For example, in October, a new law was passed in New York, which prohibits short-term housing rental. It was the first bell, the action of the authorities, which made it clear that officials want to control the rental market for private housing. And the hotel industry is only at hand.

The state of New York is the most active and extensive housing market in the world, and legal restrictions, of course, affect Airbnb revenues. State authorities believe that it was services like Airbnb that influenced a significant increase in rental prices.

In November, representatives of the hotel business have formed a strategy for further struggle with Airbnb. Documents have come into the hands of The New York Times journalists to understand how seriously the hotel industry treats the service and its work. In particular, in one of the documents, a representative of Marriott International, a trading group that is part of the Association, writes that Airbnb "has become a major player in the delivery of housing for corporate representatives, which is our main business."

Perhaps this is true. Since 2008, Airbnb has evolved from a small startup into an international company. At the same time, in most cases, it manages to avoid the need to pay tax in any other countries except the USA. But the hotel business has to pay wherever there are hotels. According to Airbnb statistics, since 2008, 150 million people have used the services of the service, who have rented about 3 million different apartments, houses and other residential buildings in 191 countries.

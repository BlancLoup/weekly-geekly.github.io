Forced care: AliExpress canceled free untraceable parcels to Russia

Remember the news that the trackingless parcels will now be sent through the sorting center in Yakutia? She has a sequel.


Russian Post will look after your purchases with Ali

From today, February 7, AliExpress cancels untraceable parcels for several CIS countries. Now, purchases are more expensive than two dollars for Russia and all the packages for Belarus and Ukraine will be assigned tracking numbers - and they will be delivered as Simplified Registered Mail.

Kommersant reported a change in the company's logistics policy, after reading a press release (we checked in Google translator - this is indeed a press release, and there it is really written) by AliExpress.

In turn, RBC explains that the Simplified Registered Mail coming to replace the non-tracking packages will be something between unregistered and registered items. SRM was developed by the logistics company Cainiao, as was AliExpress, which is part of the Alibaba group in partnership with Mail of Russia.

Representatives of the company promise that through Simplified Registered Mail orders will reach much faster than in the case of unregistered items, since Cainiao, together with Russian Post, manages all operational processes from the moment they collect the package in China until delivery to the recipient. They believe willingly - at least they will not have to stop by in Yakutia.

In addition, the cost of the SRM should be lower than the price of the registered delivery - but there is no official information about exactly how much the media have yet. However, it is still not cheaper than free.

PS All parcels of Pochtoy.com from the USA have tracking numbers, so your purchase will not exactly go to you through Yakutia.

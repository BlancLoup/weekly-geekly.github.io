The Intel Research and Development Center in Nizhny Novgorod was opened in May 2000 (I already wrote about the history of its discovery) and is located at ul. Turgeneva, d. 30. Now about 450 people work in the Nizhny Novgorod site, which can be divided into the following groups:

Information technology (IT) is a fairly large group, which for the most part is engaged in supporting the performance of other departments, is responsible for the infrastructure of the entire office.

The mobility group (MG) is the very people who develop the hardware and software implementation of wireless Internet access. These people, for example, are directly involved in the development of 802.11 and 802.16 standards.

Software and Services Group (SSG) is the largest group in Nizhny Novgorod (about 270 out of 450 people). Group sales and marketing (SMG) - are engaged in supporting the company's customers, including outside the Russian Federation.

The Manufacturing Technology Group (TMG) is a small group dedicated to semiconductor and optics research. Other areas of research are the study and creation of probabilistic graphical models, the creation of machine learning libraries, video monitoring technology (video defect detection) and wireless data transmission technologies.

Administrative and business group - accounting, personnel department, external relations department, etc.

I would like to dwell in more detail on the information technology (IT) group, namely, in the service of support. Our tour of the building of the company was accompanied by a very rich technical seminars, part of the memories of which I try to share.



It was interesting to visit one of the laboratories of the office, engaged in testing equipment for the provision of service advice for system integrators. It was a long and interesting story about a no less interesting development - the Remote Test Laboratory (Remote Lab), which enables testing of new products to interested customers via the Internet, providing access to modern equipment and engineering samples. This approach allows you to learn in advance about the performance of new devices, their compatibility with various products, but at the same time reduce the risks and time for integration; Such a function is especially relevant before the launch of new products - when they are not officially available anywhere else, but especially interested customers already have the opportunity to twist and rotate the novelty remotely. Additionally - the possibility of organizing online seminars and training, regardless of the distance to the client.



In addition to the server part, the laboratory also has a division that specializes in netbooks, tablets and other mobile systems.

Within the laboratory, we were told about some of the perspectives and aspects of the new MeeGo operating system (you can read it here ), as well as Intel Atom processors, supporting the words with various demonstrations. For example, clearly showed the difference in load times of two identical netbooks running under different operating systems.





It also turned out that modern platforms based on the new Intel Atom with much less (at times) power consumption, bypass the speed of the system on the once-top Intel Pentium 4 processors and their corresponding discrete cards. Maybe this is not the most correct comparison, but the difference in megahertz and watts consumed is significant.

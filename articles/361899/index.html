Fast Feedback Services in Education

Sometimes I ask myself why modern students go to lectures in universities? After all, exactly the same and even better, you can see on youtube or on free online resources: coursera, edX, etc.



Salman Khan from khanacademy.org generally suggested that education should be done the other way around: you watch video lectures at home (after all, 90% of the time students really just watch and listen to lectures), and at school you do homework and practice, that is, the most difficult thing.

There were many projects that positioned themselves as a "breakthrough in education." Numerous online resources that should destroy the classical education system and offer something new and more efficient. Maybe.

Education is 90% "stick" and 10% "gingerbread"


Any normal person, if he has free time and money, would rather spend it on vacation in Turkey, than to study in a university or school.

Classical education is built on violence. People are forced to come to a certain closed room and for a long time in an uncomfortable position to watch not very spectacular monologues of bad actors. Moreover, their parents are forced to pay money for it.

That is why it is so effective. If you don‚Äôt learn or skip, you‚Äôll get a ‚Äútwo‚Äù, you‚Äôll call your parents or join the army.

Online projects are trying to improve the "carrot", but they simply do not have a "stick". Therefore, they are at least 10 times less effective than offline.

And another important plus offline - note-taking. When a person physically writes something on paper, he remembers the essence several times better. Let even stupidly repeating the lecturer and writing off from the board. All because of motor memory.

Online is becoming cheaper - offline is more expensive.


Direct analogy with the music industry. Music for people has become shareware online (especially in Russia, where 90% of the content is pirated). At the same time, the cost of concerts has grown and the main money musicians earn from corporate events.

Online projects work great for generating leads (potential customers) for universities, but not for sales directly to education.

Offline has maximum feedback speed


It would seem online much faster. You can instantly exchange messages thousands of kilometers away. But to surpass live chat is still not possible. Offline, where people are next to each other - the speed and quality of feedback is maximum. Naturally, if the training is based on the principle of dialogue, and not a monologue.

For the same reason, working in an office, when people from 10.00 to 19.00 sit in the same room within eye contact, will always be more effective than remote work (other things being equal).

That is why there is tremendous potential in improving the quality and speed of feedback, especially for offline events.

Brief essence and how it works


Service for quick feedback on offline lectures and conferences. For groups up to 10 -100 people.

How it works:
  • Teacher / lecturer - creates an event, for example, No. 3452 + wifi access point (in those places where there is no wifi).
  • Anyone who is in a lecture should connect to the wifi network and enter this event from their phone.
  • Student In the course of the lecture, he may a) write questions to this event; b) put likes / dislikes; c) answer questions / quizzes of the teacher.
  • Teacher. During the lecture can ask polls. At the end of the lecture receives a list of questions that are answered + statistics of answers and listeners interest.
  • Administrator. He sees attendance, activity of the student and teacher. Statistics of questions and activity of listeners.
  • Parents / leaders. See the questions, assessments and activity of their children / employees.


What problem we solve, who is the client


As with any educational service, there are 4 main users: Teacher (lecturer), Administrator (Conference Organizer, School Principal), Parent (or the boss of the student) and Student (child, student, student).

One of the problems of the Teacher and his Administrator is to somehow stimulate a large number of questions from the students and receive live feedback. In short, to manage to combine a monologue with a dialogue on a massive scale and directly during the lecture.

Just as for restaurants, reviews of visitors about their cuisine are important. They could be ‚Äúhammered‚Äù, but then you won‚Äôt understand why people suddenly stopped coming to you.

If we talk about the target client, for whom it is critical: the Administrator (it is important for him to understand how well his teachers work) and the Parent (it is important for him to see how his child learns).

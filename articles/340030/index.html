DELL EMC Networking N1100 Switch Overview

Since August 2017, Dell EMC Networking N1100 switches have become available for order in Russia. They are positioned as products in a fairly ‚Äúhot‚Äù market niche, which can be called the ‚Äúworking corporate horse‚Äù. In this category, when choosing a product, such an assessment of customers as ‚Äúprice / quality‚Äù prevail, with a greater emphasis and weight on the ‚Äúprice‚Äù parameter.



But let's take a closer look at what Dell EMC offers in this category.

To begin with, we note the fact that 2 years ago, Dell has already taken steps in the direction of an ‚Äúintermediate product‚Äù between the series. In 2015, the N1500 series switches were released under the same sign: ‚Äúintermediate switches between the X and N series‚Äù. And now a new product has been released inside the corporate N-series, which is ‚Äúeven simpler and even cheaper‚Äù.

Differences between DELL EMC Networking N-Series Switches and X-Series Switches


The Dell EMC Networking product portfolio has an X-series of switches, which is positioned for small organizations (up to 200 users) or for solving local problems in hotels, conference halls, houses or retail outlets, as well as an N-series of switches, which is positioned for corporate networks (from 100 to 1000 users). We can say that at the junction of these categories is the very category of products called the ‚Äúworking corporate horse‚Äù, which implies the price of the SMB level, but elements of functionality and service from the ‚Äúcorporate sector‚Äù.

As mentioned above, the N1100 series switches are an intermediate link between the X-series and the N-series, while still formally refer to the N-series. Let's see what these switches are from the "corporate" segment, which is not in the segment for "SME" / SMB.

  1. Command line (Command Line Interface, CLI). All X-series switches have a graphical user interface (GUI). It is worth noting that the Dell EMC Networking division in its network products worked quite well with the GUI: experts evaluate this interface as functional, understandable and visually non-rejecting. In the X-series switches, developers made basic control via the GUI, leaving the CLI option with a limited set of commands. The complete command line option in Dell EMC Networking products starts with the N-series. Add that 90% of the commands in the CLI correspond to the syntax of Cisco IOS. Thus, experts with experience with Cisco IOS will be able to adapt to Dell EMC Networking switches.
  2. Stacking function . X-series switches lack such functionality, while N-series switches have such functionality as standard, built-in. There is a single exception - low-end switches of the N1100 series with 8 ports. The remaining switches of the N1100 series have a built-in option for stacking up to 4 devices with a 40 Gbit / s stacking bus.
  3. ProSupport NBD Warranty . X-series switches are shipped to Russia with a basic lifetime warranty and the option to optionally purchase a Dell EMC ProSupport brand warranty. The N1100 series switches come with a basic lifetime warranty and a three-year ProSupport NBD proprietary service. Read about the features and types of warranty below.

DELL EMC Networking N1100 series switches


All N1100 series switches are Layer 2 switches. The current N1100 switch family has 6 models with 8, 24, and 48 10/100 / 1000BASE-T ports. From the model range, 3 switches have PoE support (the letter ‚ÄúP‚Äù is present in the model number), the other 3 switches do not have PoE, but are made in a quiet (without fans) design (the letter ‚ÄúT‚Äù is in the model number).

The N1100 switches have the Dell EMC Networking OS 6 operating system. In addition to the proprietary operating system, the N1100 switches have the Dell EMC-supported ONIE (Open Network Install Environment) installation functionality, which makes it possible to install an alternative operating system from the list of compatible ones on the bare chassis of the switch. But at the time of writing this review, Dell EMC has not yet announced a list of third-party operating systems supported by the N1100 series switches.

Support for 40 Gbps bus stacking is available for the N1124T / P and N1148T / P models using 2 10GE SFP + ports (up to 4 devices from the same lineup in the stack). Accordingly, the stacking itself is carried out through standard interfaces without the use of special cables and modules. For a summary of stacking capabilities, see the table below.

The term ‚ÄúLifetime Warranty‚Äù for Dell EMC Networking means that while a customer is using a switch, Dell EMC technical support is ready to replace the switch entirely in case of hardware failures (provided that the failure occurred due to the manufacturer‚Äôs fault, and not due to operating conditions).

Replacing is done by delivering a new switch to the place of operation, and the defective switch is removed. If the manufacturer has stopped servicing the switch model used by the customer (meaning: removing the switch from sales (End-of-Sale, EoS), removing the model from the register supported by the production base and components, updating the firmware, etc. (End -of-Life, EoL)), - the customer is provided with a model similar in characteristics (based on the manufacturer‚Äôs direct replacement product tables between old and new generations).

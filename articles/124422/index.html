LulzSec hacked The Sun publishing server



Apparently, the guys from the group LulzSec could not sit quietly on a well-deserved rest, and they decided to return. And the return was accompanied not by words, but by deed: LulzSec was hacked into the server of The Sun, belonging to the media empire of Rupert Murdoch. First, they redirected all visitors from The Sun's homepage to their Twitter account. Then they made a fake page of this media with the same fake news that Murdoch died suddenly.

The hacking of The Sun by LulzSec group just coincided with the height of the scandal associated with another edition of the Murdoch Empire - with the News of the World. Not so long ago, it was discovered that the journalists of this publication tapped the telephone lines of any celebrities, politicians, and other prominent figures. In general, LulzSec found a truly optimal time and place for hacking - their actions have now become world famous.

At The Sun is now about 3 million readers, and the history of the publication has about 50 years. This media has repeatedly been associated with a variety of scandals and the current proceedings - just the beginning. It seems that Lulz Sec, after her return, is unlikely to go to rest, but will continue to act.

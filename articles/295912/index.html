Streaming services want to "go" on podcasts: the race has already begun

A distinctive feature of any business person is the ability to filter information and consume high-quality and useful content, whose main task is to feed ideas and inspiration.

Listening to podcasts is a great addition to the daily dose of ideas and simply useful information needed by any person engaged in meaningful creative activities, they wrote in one thematic blog .

Among the popular services of podcasts, you can select such as iTunes and Soundcloud, for example, or the Russian service PodFM . Until recently, iTunes held a leading position due to the huge base of podcasts, due to the popularity of the iPhone, iPod, the rapid rise of Apple Music and the large size of the Apple ecosystem as a whole.

However, now some streaming services are trying to turn the tide.

Google on October 27 announced support for podcasts, which after some time will appear in the music service Google Play Music . At the moment, this section of the service is just beginning to be filled and is not yet available to listeners.

Google has agreed to cooperate with more than a dozen popular podcasters and show providers. Podcasters from the United States have already had the opportunity to download their own podcasts via the link play.google.com/music/podcasts/publish .

The exact date of launching the podcast section for ordinary listeners has not yet been announced. But the company promises to provide additional details in the "coming months." Another streaming service, Deezer , made a serious step towards podcasting back in 2014. He then acquired Stitcher , a company that provides radio podcasts. Since then, the number of Deezer podcasts has increased to 20,000. And last Thursday, October 29, 2015, service representatives said that Deezer had 20,000 more podcasts.

The service operates in more than 180 countries and offers podcasts in different languages. Deezer has podcast broadcasts with NPR, Financial Times, Panoply, WNYC, This American Life and CBS Radio News. Now you will add talkSPORT, Bayerischer Rundfunk, France 24 and Braincast to the list of partners. The service is launched in five new markets - in Germany, Brazil, Mexico, Spain and Colombia.

Yesterday, October 30, Deezer was supposed to begin a public offering on the Paris Euronext. But three days before the deadline, the company abandoned its intentions.

Earlier, on October 15, Deezer confirmed that he intends to go IPO. The company's management planned to sell over 8 million shares. It was assumed that with the help of IPO Deezer will be able to earn about 330-414 million dollars. The company planned an IPO, despite the large financial losses, which over the past 3.5 years amounted to 87 million euros.

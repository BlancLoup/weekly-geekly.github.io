Review of Mobile LTE router from Yota

Hello to all!



For a long, long time I was looking for at least some info about the router, produced by the Chinese company Quanta, which is given as a substitute for Yota Egg and Yota Many. I did not find anything except photos, so I will briefly describe it here.

Be careful, traffic! :)


Appearance and comparison with Yota Many



Here is the router (it is white):









As you can see from the photos, it is a little more than Yota Many in all respects.

Sensations from the router


Immediately about the cons:
  • It is necessary to slightly press the case - a crunch is heard from all sides. Discounted with materials, the Yota Many case is much more pleasant to the touch.
  • The micro-USB port is attached anyhow, the hole in the case is noticeably larger than the port. It's not scary, but not very nice.
  • There is no Wi-Fi translation button for open mode. It would be better if they did it instead of WPS, more confusing.
  • Long starts. Specially not measured, but for 15-20 seconds exactly
  • At the Yota booth, they said that the approximate battery life was 4 hours versus 24 for Yota Many. This is very upsetting.
  • As it was written on the box, the simultaneous connection of up to 5 devices . Also upset. UPD: connected 7 devices, everything worked!
  • There is no Wi-Fi off function when there is no connection to LTE.
  • The manual says that a short press puts the device into standby mode. Nothing like this. Either use it or turn it off, there is no third option.
  • It starts to get very hot after 5-10 minutes of work. So that even through the bag is felt.


Pros:
  • Removable SIM card. (rather, not even a plus, but a certain neutral feature)
  • Software stuffing (about it below).

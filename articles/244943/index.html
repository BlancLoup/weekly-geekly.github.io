IBM technology helped improve ski production and the skis themselves



The company Blizzard Sport produces skis and equipment, accessories for skiers for more than half a century. Since the 1950s, this company has created millions of pairs of skis and a smaller number of different accessories. Each year Blizzard produces about 400,000 pairs of skis, the lineup includes about 900 different models. Here and ordinary skis, and racing skis, freestyle skiers. In the manufacture of skis used more than 50 thousand types of different materials.

But times are changing, even the climate is changing, and with it the needs of lovers of skiing. But the manufacturer needs to know these needs, because the equipment and its types must also meet the requirements of consumers. If you do not follow the trends, you can get significant losses instead of the expected profits, because the production line needs to be rebuilt in case of changes, and it takes time. And here high technologies come to the rescue, in particular - powerful computer systems and software from IBM, which make it possible to reduce the time required for changes, to a separate production cycle, and to improve the monitoring of product quality.

What should have been done?



The main task of the manufacturer, at the operational level, is to minimize equipment downtime due to changes in one or another production line. In addition, a significant loss to the company brings a defective product (problems with packaging, accessories, etc.). Control of such defects is no less important a task than the restructuring of the production line.
You can try to identify and major defects for different models of skis - in order to correct the shortcomings of production, and reduce the appearance of defective products to a minimum.



What was done?



Blizzard invited IBM partner BI plus GmbH to cooperate. This is a Viennese provider of smart business solutions. Partner solutions are mainly based on the IBM Cognos portfolio. BI plus was able to create a self-learning system for detecting defects, which, in turn, integrated into the Blizzard production process.

Sensors of such a system were installed at key points of production, and data for processing began to arrive, and in large quantities. Data is handled by the IBM Cognos Data Manager software package, which processes data, unifies, and sends to the general Blizzard database.

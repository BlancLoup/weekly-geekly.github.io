Cloud services are being deployed everywhere. Small and large businesses regularly take advantage of them: a variety of companies translate their infrastructure into the clouds.

Some user cases are quite simple (for example, hosting a website or a web application), while others are quite complex (recall the story about the Netflix infrastructure ).

So how are cloud services used? We would like to talk about this. Perhaps this material will allow you to see the full range of IaaS capabilities and encourage the use of this tool for your business.

File storage, synchronization and resource sharing

Everyone has already heard about Dropbox, Box.net, Google Drive - services for storing and synchronizing files. Some provide a limited amount of storage, others - unlimited. Many are focused on the mass consumer, but small, medium and large businesses use their corporate counterparts to help centralize the management of files and data.

Backup

The requirements of customers are constantly growing, and with it the market of backup systems itself is changing. If you had to deploy your own infrastructure before, now you have the opportunity to store your backup copies on a remote site. With this approach, a clear advantage emerges - no need to bother with your own infrastructure and spend money on maintaining IT backup specialists.

Cloud backup systems offer solutions to these problems with additional benefits. The IT professional only needs to install agents, select data to back up, and set up a task schedule. After this, the process takes place automatically, which seriously reduces the workload of staff members. Savings on hardware are generally obvious: you do not have to acquire, install and maintain expensive storage systems.

The cloud for data backup and recovery has been used by the Hotels.ru hotel reservation service, having been an IT-GRAD client since 2010. Backups are done several times a day, and the backup site is present as a company agreement with another data center.

During the six years of operation of the Hotels.ru service, a real large-scale accident did not happen, but under testing, the scheme proved to be quite reliable. During the "rehearsal" the whole process took less than 16 hours.

Web hosting

This is one of the most common cloud scenarios in business. A company may host web pages on physical machines, but this may cause problems when introducing new services and functions. If a company works in the cloud, then it will not have difficulty scaling.

Disaster tolerance

Recently, this yuzkeys, gaining great popularity. Speaking of systems of this class, usually means a backup data center, which can be used to transfer the load from an already inoperative place.

Often, Disaster Recovery Solution is confused with a conventional high availability system. Both approaches ensure the operation of the application in case of problems with the equipment or software, but differ in the permissible scale of the accident. For example, an excellent 16-node HA-cluster will not save the room from flooding. DRS systems can ‚Äúsurvive‚Äù in case of large-scale failure of several data centers at once, without paralyzing the application for an indefinite period of time.

Entering the international market

With physical infrastructure, you usually choose the location that is best suited to serve your customers. But the concept of going global implies that your audience will become international. Not every company can afford to open data centers in different countries.

However, many cloud providers have data centers around the world. This means that you can place your capacities in several places in order to more efficiently distribute content, services and products.

The advantages of cloud technologies are appreciated by the company Franmer, engaged in the production of composite pools. Over the ten years of the company's existence, the coverage area has increased - today, dealer points are present in 69 cities of Russia, as well as in Belarus and Kazakhstan. The rapid growth, expansion of the services offered and the range of products increased the number of customers and, as a result, required a review of the existing IT infrastructure.

There was a need to change the principles of its construction, the organization of a centralized approach with guaranteed reliability, mobility, high availability and adequate cost. Franmer revised priorities and decided to abandon the physical infrastructure in favor of the cloud. The company has been a loyal customer of IT-GRAD for more than 7 years.

Business Intelligence and Big Data

The amount of information stored on the Internet doubles approximately every one and a half year. According to IDC estimates, in 2012, the total content of the world wide web was 2500 exabytes. This data is a valuable source of information for analytical companies. Big data allows you to learn more about current trends and potential customers.

New tools provide an opportunity to get a rough idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe data content, see some patterns, and thereby assess the usefulness of the data and the need for further work with them. For example, an automaker can, based on customer reviews, identify certain patterns and hidden defects.

Cloud business intelligence is growing rapidly. Powerful computing systems in the data center of the provider cope with complex analytical tasks. Clouds allow you to get IT resources and not engage in the life cycle of servers and software updates.

Work with providers

An enterprise resource management system (ERP) was designed to automate operations within a company. Unfortunately, organizations entering the world market need to interact with thousands of suppliers around the globe, using business processes that are not supported by their internal systems. To solve this problem, several cloud providers offer ERP services and are gaining popularity in the supply chain.

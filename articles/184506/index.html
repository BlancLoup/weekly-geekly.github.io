Crystal photos of Bitfin specialized Bitcoin processor were obtained.

More recently, Avalon was the only processor, specially sharpened on the calculation of Bitcoin. Since then, Butterfly Labs seems to have finally started sending out something (but we haven‚Äôt yet been able to get a sample of their microcircuits), and what is much more interesting - Bitfury Bitcoin engineering crystals have been obtained.

Bitfury is radically more efficient (in terms of power consumption on Mhash) Avalon-a and BFL, due to the fact that it is produced using 55nm technology (at the TSMC plant in Taiwan), and the scheme is completely drawn by hand, without using automatic synthesis from standard cells. So, 1 Avalon crystal, as we remember, gives about 282 Mhash / sec, while Bitfury produces about 2-3 Ghash / sec (the exact numbers will be clear when the chips are delivered to the finished mining devices).

Bitfury Chip is notable for the fact that it was developed by our compatriot from Ukraine. However, he does not disclose his name in order not to attract too much attention. This was his first integrated circuit design ‚Äî before that, he had been briefly involved in FPGA. What may surprise many - they did not test the crystal in the shuttle / MPW (test production start from several customers at the same time) - it would take a lot of time, although the cost of the error would be less. The project was immediately sent to mass production. Surprisingly, it all worked the first time. The final assembly of devices will be organized by the Moscow company Metabank.

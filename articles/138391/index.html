Why is it helpful to invite bloggers to your startup?

Where is the success?


To succeed, a person must have all sorts of qualities: hard work, talent, a fresh look at things, etc. However, a startup, anyone who comes up with a new idea, is not enough, you need not just "be good", you need to know about you. To get online, the usual marketing methods just do not fit, and there is no money for them. Therefore, we need new, "human" solutions. One of them is to invite bloggers to visit.

Bloggers


Not all of them play fair and some start to earn money by trusting themselves, but many of them may be interested in your idea and they will be ready to talk in person. Moreover, you invite them to visit, but do not offer to believe a word and swallow another batch of "fresh" advertising. No one owes nothing to nobody. If your startup or just a new unique project is really interesting, original and can benefit people, feel free to invite bloggers to your office - if they like your ideas and how you implement them, they will tell everyone about it, and not so much the facade of the project, how much about how it looks from the inside. And not only in the network, but also in personal communication. In general, they gain from openness!

Case of our team


Recently, as part of a blog tour, bloggers (and simple users of Workle) visited us . A lot of noise, a lot of fun, a lot of good. Below is a small photo report and conclusions on the topic of why it is useful to invite bloggers to the office of your startup.




Where are the legs of races here


By themselves, blogging is not a new concept. Tourist blog tours, educational blog tours, and even blog tours at NPPs regularly take place (there have been two of these lately). Businesses get extra advertisements, bloggers satisfy curiosity, and everyone is happy. However, blogoturov in startups has one significant difference - criticism. Few people can reasonably criticize the work of a nuclear power plant, or the mastery of landscape painter, but there are always thousands of experts in startups (like in football). Another thing is that in startups (as opposed to football) their advice can be really useful.

In general, blog tours, as a link between the project and the audience, look quite interesting and promising. In short, blogging brought us several hundred users, several thousand visits, and tons of motivation that still works. More - inside.

Reason # 1: Unknown bugs "pop up"
Not all bugs can be caught using testers, usability testing, or even an open launch. At exhibitions and conferences, everyone tries to be polite and not point out anyone's flaws. Reason # 2: The motivational effect of direct contact with users.
Good professionals want and like to work in an effective company that brings some real benefits to society. In general, listening to living people can and should be useful.

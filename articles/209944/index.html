This functionality is most often associated with the concept of "smart watches", and as a rule, watches are bought for use in this form. Sensors to detect user activity
Another application of smart watches is tracking user activity, working as a fitness tracker. The simplest devices of this kind simply tell the user how much he has walked (kilo) meters per day, how many calories he has spent, and so on. In fact, this is just an evolution of pedometers, a more advanced version of them. Yes, some of them can drop readings to the cloud and share them on social networks, but they still remain pedometers.

Devices that are evolutionarily higher can not only count the steps, but also measure the distance using GPS, measure the pulse, and on the basis of this data, prompt the user to move faster or slower for the best training pace. And in the presence of a barometer can even count the number of flights of stairs.
Even such devices can take data from external sensors (working on ANT + technology) - the speed of rotation of the wheels of the bike, pressure, weight, temperature, and so on. The device retains the values ‚Äã‚Äãthat came from the sensors, and imposes them on the track. Like this:

Alpha MIO / Alpha LINK

Not really smart watch, but smart heart rate monitor. It was the first device that reads the pulse without requiring a chest sensor ‚Äî instead, a light sensor that detects the heart rhythm works through the skin and vessels.
I already wrote about how they work in their review , but duplicate here:
How does he work? Simply. Relatively easy. Tissues without blood do not have a pronounced color, so they miss the whole spectrum. Blood, unlike them, is red, and therefore absorbs the entire spectrum, except red.
When you shine a flashlight through your hand, you see only a red glow. This is due to the fact that most of the spectrum, except red, is absorbed by the blood. With the observance of proper conditions (dim lantern, dark room) the human eye can also catch small changes in brightness, in time with the heartbeat.
It means that when there is a lot of blood in the tissues of the hand, they reflect the radiation of the rest of the spectrum worse, and when there is little, they reflect better. I am not talking about the reflection from the skin, which changes little, but about the translucence of the upper layer of the skin and the reflection from the internal tissues.
Now it's easy - we shine a green LED under the skin, and remove the light fluctuations with the photosensor. We filter from all interferences that go beyond 20 ... 200 oscillations per second, averaging over a dozen or two seconds and get the pulse value.

Alpha MIO is a clock that can show time (amazingly!) And a pulse on its screen, Alpha LINK is a screenless version that has only a LED showing a cardio zone. The data on the pulse in the same way as in the older version is transmitted to the phone, simply not shown on the watch itself.

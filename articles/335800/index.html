Tablet and children


The child and the TV, the child and the prefix, the child and the computer - and now the child and the tablet. Also actively discussed is the topic of the influence of gadgets on children's psyche and health. The soft is the king


In the fight hardware vs software, software confidently burst forward. Your mobile gadget is as cool as apps are for it. You can invest as much as you like in marketing, OS development and optimization, but if you, like Microsoft‚Äôs mobile division, have not been able to develop an application ecosystem, at least at the level of iOS and Android stores, your fate is predetermined . Unique specialized software gives the gadget something that cannot be achieved by other means - the right to stand out, the right to be more expensive, the right to be a thing in itself.

For this reason, in parallel with the debugging of production at the facilities of the Shenzhen factory, here in Russia, Appgranula studio began developing a software shell for the Playpad 2. Instead of running out of proprietary operating systems of their own handicraft or making another fork, the team decided to create a launcher on the standard Android build.

In order to protect children from generally accepted stores, the site playpads.net was launched with its own Google Play counterpart, which was filled with pre-filtered and specially selected content for the target audience. Moreover, it is not only about games: educational and developmental programs were added to the internal PlayPad store.

The second solution, which was supposed to increase the competitiveness of the PlayPad 2 against the background of unnamed Chinese tablets, was the function of remote control of the device by the parent itself. Also, adults were offered the function of limiting the time of work of certain applications, or the entire tablet as a whole. For example, you can set the operating time for half an hour, after which the device is blocked. The remote control system is probably the most important software feature of PlayPad2, which distinguishes the device from a regular tablet.

By the time the PlayPad 2 was made, work was done on:

  1. creating your own launcher shell for the device;
  2. the launch of the site on which the PlayPad is activated and the device is managed;
  3. developing a system of remote access and application control;
  4. creating and filling your own store with pre-selected content for children.

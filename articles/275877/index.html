Games and porn in virtual reality: what you need to know on the eve of the launch of Oculus Rift, PlayStation VR, HTC Vive

In the coming months, several virtual reality devices will go on sale at once:
- Oculus Rift (March 28, 2016)
- HTC Vive (April 2016)
- PlayStation VR (first half of 2016)
- Microsoft Hololens (devkits in the first quarter of 2016)

It's time to summarize everything that is known about these devices, and decide whether to take them now, what to choose, as well as what device is the most promising to develop games.




Why do we need a helmet and will it replace monitors and televisions?


Virtual reality technology gives a completely different level of immersion. When you are wearing a helmet, in the field of view there is only a game, and the review is carried out not with a mouse or stick, but with movements of the head. Image quality is still worse than monitors and TVs, but even with this in mind, virtual reality is strong. The ride on the roller coaster swayes, and the most common horror game scares as in childhood.



The appearance of helmets does not mean that the displays are a thing of the past. Virtual reality technologies are still far from perfect, and in some games their use is irrational (many strategies, two-dimensional games).

What are the chances of success? Virtual reality helmets tried to be released before, but they did not get any spread.


Yes, there really have been attempts before. The first commercial helmet of virtual reality (Virtuality) appeared in the 1990s. But then there was still no technology that would allow a massive affordable product that could be successful with customers. For example, Oculus Rift displays an image with a resolution of 2160x1200 (1080x1200 per eye) at a sweep frequency of 90 Hz. This requires a computer 2-3 times more powerful than playing in 1080p.

In favor of the fact that the current boom is not a false start, indicate the prevalence of Oculus Rift even at the prototype stage for developers and the great interest of people.

Which of the three helmets to choose? Oculus rift?


While difficult to say. According to the characteristics of all three helmets are similar. A new wave of VR launched Oculus Rift, and it still attracts the most attention. Oculus Rift began as a project on Kickstarter, which collected several million dollars, and was later bought by Facebook. The head of the company, Palmer Lucky, has been interested in VR since childhood; he has one of the largest US collections of virtual reality helmets. The technical director at Oculus VR, John Carmack, is known as the creator of id Software (Doom, Quake) game engines, i.e. man in the gaming industry is not the last.

Nevertheless, the company has no experience in creating and popularizing gaming devices. Oculus Rift can be difficult to configure for an inexperienced user, and besides, it is designed for the PC platform (so far only Windows, support for OS X and Linux will appear in the future), the market of which is several times smaller than the console games market.



As for the characteristics, in the Oculus Rift two screens, each gives a resolution of 1080x1200 with a frequency of 90 Hz. Will be sold complete with a gamepad from the Xbox One, as well as EVE games: Valkyrie and Lucky Tale. The list of supported games already includes hundreds of titles . In the future, it is planned to release a specialized Oculus Touch controller, which will allow to be controlled in games not only with buttons and sticks, but also with hand movements.

What is known about the PlayStation VR?


Sony already has experience working on such devices - in 1997, she released the Glasstron helmet, which was supported by MechWarrior 2. PlayStation VR, his successor. The company has been working on it since 2011.



The Sony helmet is designed to work with the PlayStation 4 and can produce a 960 √ó 1080 picture at every eye with a frequency of 120 Hz. The list of supported games in the tens , among them 20 exclusives, including Ace Combat 7 and Gran Turismo Sport. You can play with the help of a gamepad or two PlayStation Move. In the future, the release of a new generation of Move is planned (perhaps they will be called differently), sharpened for VR.

What other helmets are there?


There is a curious Google Cardboard project: you can cut out a helmet using cardboard, insert lenses, a magnet and a smartphone as a screen into it. You should not expect a big effect from it. Surprisingly, there is a Russian equivalent .

There are also a lot of helmets, which are made similar to Google Cardboard (smartphone instead of screen), but factory-made: Samsung Gear VR (works in conjunction with Samsung Galaxy Note 4), Homido VR, 3D VR, 3D VR Box, VR BAOFENG3, VR Shinecon, Magic Glass, Carl Zeiss VR One, Vortex VR One, Fibrum Pro, Durovis Dive, Merge VR, Freefly VR. All of them are already on sale, but none of the big hype caused.

In addition, there are second tier analogs of the Oculus Rift, i.e. full-fledged helmets with their own screens: NVR (Russian development, on sale), OSVR from Razer (on sale), Total Vision VR2 (pre-order for 2016), Omimo Uranus One (on sale), ANTVR (on sale), VRVANA Totem (on selling).



The most interesting alternative called HoloLens is offered by Microsoft. This is not a helmet, but glasses for creating augmented reality that show three-dimensional holograms in the real world next to the user. Unlike helmets, this is a stand-alone stand-alone device (like Google Glass) and its application goes far beyond the limits of games. Most likely even this way: outside of the Hololens games there are much more options for use than in games.

The Hololens release was announced for the current quarter at a price of $ 3000.

Will game management change with the advent of helmets?


Sure. While the most convenient way to control is the usual gamepad - it is not by chance that the developers of Oculus Rift include it as standard. But in the long term, the best impressions will most likely be delivered by specialized controllers that look like a gamepad split in two and are able to track hand movements. All three manufacturers (Oculus VR, Sony, Valve / HTC) are working in this direction.

In addition, another periphery is being developed: a vest that mimics bullets, a full-body suit with backward recoil (most likely, it will not collect the required amount, but this is hardly the last such project), a Virtuix Omni treadmill, a recoil weapon , and for tracking pupils, helping to aim, a bird flight simulator and even a controller for squeezing the female breast (naturally, the Japanese came up with).

Such devices are unlikely to get wide distribution: they require additional expenses, a lot of space (especially Omni) and support from developers. Doubts remain even in the success of specialized controllers like the Oculus Touch, which will be bundled with helmets. In words, the control of the movements of the hands sounds great, but in practice it takes up space, and it is simply tiring. Standard gamepad so far no one could beat, although there were many attempts.

If you want to become a manager, do not wait for a separate office or a beautiful job title. You can begin to think, act and communicate as a leader long before the official promotion. If it seems to you that everything is decided by someone else who is much higher than you, there are still many ways to demonstrate what you are worth and make your way up.

Your plan:
  1. Do your job impeccably. No matter how great your ambitions are, do not let them distract you from your current responsibilities. Concentrate on today no less than tomorrow. No one - neither colleagues, nor management, nor clients - should have any doubts about your abilities. This is the first step advancement.
  2. Help your boss achieve your goals. Think about what the problem is not giving your supervisor a restful sleep and help him cope with it. Try to accept rather than reject the requests of your leader to engage in some task. You will receive loyalty in return, which will become an important factor in your progress.
  3. Grab any leadership opportunity. Try to keep your enthusiasm for new tasks beyond your direct responsibilities. Call to participate in new projects, especially those that are visible to people from outside your unit. This will give others the opportunity to feel that you will be representing yourself in a higher position.   6. Keep your ambitions with you. If you have a relationship of trust with your leader, then you can share your intentions, but it is better to express them in terms of benefits for your organization. Describe your achievements over the past year and ask how you can continue to contribute to the success of the company. Let it be a dialogue about mutual interests, and not a report on your unsatisfied sense of self-worth. If you think that your leader may feel a threat to his position, coming from your ambitions, it is better to leave such a conversation for later and concentrate on demonstrating your potential.
  7. Find an example to follow. Find someone to whose level you want to rise, and carefully study it - how it acts, speaks and thinks. Find the opportunity to work with him on a common problem and try to understand what is useful you can learn from him (but do not get carried away and remain at the same time yourself).

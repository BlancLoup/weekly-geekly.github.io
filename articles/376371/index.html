Boston Dynamics introduced the new lightweight robot - Spot

Meet the new four-legged friend walking robot from Boston Dynamics named "Spot"
It is smaller and faster than its older "fellow shop": BigDog and WildCat (Cheetah)



Unlike the large four-legged robots from Boston Dynamics, it is designed not only for walking in harsh conditions, but also for moving inside buildings.
It is resistant to falling and well orientated in space, it can climb stairs, and also climb hills and uneven areas as well as BigDog
Its weight is only 72kg.
One of its most important advantages is that the heart has an electric motor in the body (previous models run on kart engines that are too noisy for combat conditions).
Hydraulic motors are used for the legs.



Boston Dynamics was established in 1992 by Prof. Mark Reibert of MIT.
Boston Dynamics began producing its four-legged robots in 2005, commissioned by DARPA.
Also, the company is developing other robots, including anthropomorphic bipeds, such as Petman and Atlas.
The company is a division of Google, the acquisition transaction took place in December 2013 .

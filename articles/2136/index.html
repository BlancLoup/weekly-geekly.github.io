Another site pays bloggers

Resource ReviewMe.com , officially launched last Thursday, announced that it will pay bloggers from $ 20 to $ 250 for reviews of advertisers' products. A separate budget - $ 25 thousand - is provided for the payment of materials dedicated to the site itself. The amount of remuneration is determined by a number of indicators, among which, of course, the popularity of the review.

According to Andy Hagans, president of ReviewMe, this $ 25,000 is almost spent, but new monetary influences are expected. Thus, the newcomer, who attracted over a thousand bloggers in less than a week, followed in the footsteps of PayPerPost , launched in July, according to TechWeb .

At the same time, ReviewMe requires bloggers to point out that reviews are paid, unlike their competitor, who prefers not to provide this information.

If you have found your region and it is not green - this article may not be useless for you ...



We all rejoice in the advent of the serene spring days. The nature comes alive, the trees, the first nice looking green appears. Everything around comes to life, including the formidable inhabitant of forests and places of mass festivities of citizens - the tick. The first recorded tick bite in 2016 occurred in Kemerovo on March 23, in Tomsk on the 29th, and St. Petersburg even earlier - on March 8 . What is a tick, how is it dangerous and how to protect yourself from the troubles associated with it - I propose to discuss together.

Know the enemy's face

More precisely to know the whole thing. Ticks (depending on the stage of their development), as a rule, are from 2 to 5-7 mm in size, with a characteristic color and a dark area on the body. Quite agile and regardless of size are equally dangerous. Favorite habitat of the tick is old dry grass, low shrubs, the outskirts of forests. Habitats of ticks should be sufficiently dry, because in the floodplain of rivers, ticks can be found much less frequently. Ticks live practically throughout the whole territory of Russia, but their greater distribution is observed practically throughout the whole territory of Siberia, in Buryatia, Udmurtia and Gorny Altai. Unfortunately, more and more it is possible to pick up a tick already in those regions where this problem was not so urgent. Some researchers claim that the active distribution of ticks (of which there are thousands of species in nature) is an indicator of climate change, which has been talked about so much lately. But today we will talk only about ticks that are carriers of dangerous infections. By the way, the number of these infections is in the tens, but the most threatening and known are tick-borne encephalitis and Lyme disease.

Does female bite or male?
Both. In this case, the female sticks in for a long time. Maybe even for a week. But the males are nimble. He bit, drank blood for an hour or two, and went about his business. But both sexes are dangerous. According to statistics, 4-5 percent of the population of ticks are carriers of Lyme disease and tick-borne encephalitis. Some experts argue that males are more dangerous than females, as their bite passes faster, and it can simply not be noticed. Indeed, in the first stage of a bite, the tick secretes enzymes that neutralize the pain receptors of the victim, which allows him to firmly fix on the body. When the tick bit into the skin - he does not care. Removing it is not so easy. Especially if you are a hare, squirrel or bear and you do not have special tools for this at hand.

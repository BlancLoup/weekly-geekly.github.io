The history of a very unsuccessful startup

Immediately make a reservation, this story is about my friend with whom I keep in touch. He does not need an invite. All names will be omitted. This story during the week he told me in fragments. About her truthfulness, I have no doubt.
I will not evaluate it as a right or wrong thing. I give HabraCommunity for reading, so that everyone may appreciate it himself and, perhaps, find experience for themselves.

The article is displayed with the permission of a friend.

To begin with about him:
He is a programmer. Too gullible, kind and reticent person who rarely shows his emotions and only to those whom he trusts. Three years in his city while studying at school, he occupied the first place in programming. After graduation, he received an invitation to MSTU. Bauman, at the then-evolving department of IU9, but by his own stupidity (because of love for the girl who left a month later) refused.
He never received a higher education, but immediately went to work. He is a capable guy, versed in technology, programmed in C, C ++, Delphi, PHP, JS, Java, at the end of our communication, he programmed in C #. At the time of this writing, he was 22 years old. Yes, and he worked as a programmer from 16 years.

Prehistory



After school, he was visited by constant thoughts about creating a project. At the age of 20 he got a job in a company, where a month later for his services he was appointed Head of the Programming Department. Also, the previous team of programmers was disbanded, and he was told to assemble a team. Within 2 months the team was 12 people.
The whole team was put on the project "Internet Operating System". At that time, only a couple could be called analogs, one of which was commercial and focused on many countries.
The first version of the system was developed in 3 months. The main rule was ‚Äúas little flash as possible, as much javascript as possible‚Äù. Of the frameworks, only MooTools was used. The most important advantage was that more than half of the code was executed on the client.
A week before the end of the development of the first version, he divided the team and began developing a second, more secure, but compatible version.
The company was notified that the project was ready, but not safe. In principle, in the company he was respected, to some extent even loved, because he was taken to all the attacks with him. His opinion was heeded, but asked quickly.
Even then, the company hit the crisis. From time to time the salary was delayed, programmers worried, but they worked. In 2010, after the new year, he was directly told that there would be no money until May.

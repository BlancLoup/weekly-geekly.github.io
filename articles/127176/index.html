Interview with Sergey Archipenkov - About the upcoming Software Project Management Conference

November 26, 2011 in St. Petersburg will host the International Conference in the field of project management " Software Project Management Conference " you are on its program committee, could you tell us in more detail - for whom it is intended, what will be discussed there?

AS: In my opinion, the conference will have a wide audience. The conference should be of interest to everyone who, in one way or another, is associated with the management of software development. First of all, these are managers: heads of companies, heads of personnel services, heads of functional departments and, of course, project managers and team leaders. Well, of course, at the conference we are waiting for those who are still only going to become a leader.

AS: This conference is one of the few that is entirely devoted to the problems of managing software development projects. Observing the state of software engineering over the past decades, one can see that the industry has become an experimental kitchen, where experiments are being conducted on the use of many different recipes for managing software development projects. What only methodologies did not invent and did not try to apply in the management of software projects! These are Oracle CDM, IBM RUP, MSF from Microsoft, SEI TSP / PSP, and, naturally, :) numerous family of xDD methodologies, and xProgramming, and Scrum, and Lean, and Kanban, and many, many others. I would like the conference to become a place for a deep understanding of the fundamental problems of management in software development, and a laboratory in which new effective ways to solve these problems would crystallize.

How do you think, why do people need to participate in the conference? What most personally gave you participation in conferences?

AS: If you remember A. Maslow's pyramid, then in its upper part are the needs of self-realization, self-development and ownership. The conference allows you to satisfy them all: participation - to communicate with colleagues, self-development - to learn from others, self-realization - to share their experiences. Personally, I participate in conferences only for this reason.

How do you think - how should an IT professional choose an interesting and promising conference for himself, if he decided to take part in an industry conference?

AS: I think every professional (here I mean not those who get paid for the work, but those who attain mastery in the profession) should have a concept of personal development. If to rephrase Seneca, then no conference will be useful to a specialist if he does not know to which pier he is going. Therefore, we are waiting for the conference for those who seek to develop their skills in the field of software development management.

Widows XP Service Pack 3 will be released this year.

Hmm, my karma would grow so that you can post to collective blogs. In the meantime, that's just here. Finally, the long-awaited Service Pack 3 for XP is coming soon.
Representatives of Microsoft officially confirmed that the release of the final version of the third service pack for the most massive operating system at the moment - Windows XP, should happen this year. It is worth noting that earlier, 2008 was called the expected release date of the package. Until now, the overwhelming majority of patches are made specifically for the Windows XP operating system, which by its fact even more moves the release date of the update package.
The new Service Pack 3 should include all security updates and other patches of the OS, released since the advent of SP2, and this is almost three years. In addition, according to company representatives, the update will bring new network functions to the operating system, such as Unified Access Control and Network Access Protection.

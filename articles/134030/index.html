Technological features of CINEMA 3D

CINEMA 3D - 3D-technology screens from LG Electronics. Many have probably heard such a term as FPR (Film-type Patterned Retarder).

With this technology (it is also called ‚Äúpassive‚Äù), two images are available to the viewer simultaneously. And all this is perfectly safe for the eyes! The 3D effect appears flicker-free. This was made possible thanks to special polarized 3D glasses.

Read more about glasses read on.

Where does the name CINEMA 3D come from? Technology owes its name to 3D films going to the cinema and thus to the first special glasses that were given to the audience (complete with a napkin, of course).

Officially, CINEMA 3D technology was introduced in May 2011. In the near future, the LG Display division plans to release 3D displays for which special 3D glasses will not be needed. This technology is called auto-stereoscopic.

CINEMA 3D: Flicker-Free Image

Where does the flicker effect come from? In most cases, because of the glasses, or to be more precise, because of the interaction of glasses with other light sources. Researches say that 90% of 3D TV viewers like Shutter Glasses notice flicker. The Japanese 3D consortium (there is one) even issued a warning that "flickering can lead to photosensitive epilepsy."

In CINEMA 3D technology, flicker is completely absent. And thanks for this, again, points!

And CINEMA 3D allows you to watch TV without interference and more clearly, because FPR technology initially divorces the right and left image.



Technological features of CINEMA 3D

Another advantage of CINEMA 3D - the 3D effect is available to a large number of viewers, regardless of body position. With the usual LED-LCD television screens of the previous generation, you would not be able to watch TV in 3D lying, and here you are. And all because of the increased parameter of the viewing angle (with CINEMA 3D, it is more than 170 ¬∞).



CINEMA 3D technology is capable of artificially increasing the frame rate at 4 times from 60 to 240 frames per second. As a result, the image is perceived more clearly, realistic and alive - especially in dynamic episodes.



From 2D to 3D

Turn any 2D image (games, movies, etc.) into 3D with CINEMA 3D technology - no problem! What is the conversion of the image? Due to the clear contrast on the screen (between large and small objects, their intersection or due to different levels of brightness or clarity), which causes your eye to perceive the picture differently.

CINEMA 3D: how it works

Why is CINEMA 3D cool?

First briefly about how the stereoscopic effect of a moving picture is achieved. Two images overlap and are projected onto the same screen through polarized filters in the opposite direction. The spectator's glasses are equipped with pairs of analyzing filters (polarizers of a rounded shape in a different direction) - again, the opposite direction. The light, polarized on the left, is extinguished by the analyzer on the right, exactly the same thing happens on the other side. It looks like this:



The three-dimensional image CINEMA 3D appears due to two divorced images and is completely safe, since the twinkling is completely extinguished by polarized glasses. But we talked about this earlier.

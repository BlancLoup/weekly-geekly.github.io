Live up to 150 years? No, not yet possible



The results of a study published by a group of scientists from Albert Einstein College of Medicine at Yeshiva University indicate that the maximum human lifespan is just over a hundred years. And we have already reached the natural limit. Improving living conditions or lack of disease will not be able to change anything.

Now, some experts believe that a person can live longer if you use specific drugs, the effectiveness of which was tested on mice. It is not yet possible to verify this statement, since such drugs have not yet been tested in humans. But the natural life expectancy has already reached its maximum.

As for mice, their lifespan was increased in the laboratory by injecting metformin and immunosuppressant rapamycin into the blood. These are drugs commonly prescribed for diabetics.

In order to find out whether a person can live longer than his ancestors, scientists at Albert Einstein College of Medicine from Yeshiva University, under the guidance of Xiao Dong, analyzed data that was taken from the Human Mortality Database (HMD).

Therefore, an analysis was conducted of the age of residents of several wealthy countries, including Sweden. It turned out that in the last century, almost nothing has changed here. In 1860, for example, the oldest person in this country died at the age of 101. And in 1990 - in 108 years. According to scientists, the maximum life expectancy in this country was achieved in the 1980s, and since then nothing has changed.

In addition to Sweden, the indicators of the maximum life expectancy in countries such as Japan, the USA, France and England were analyzed. These countries are leaders in the number of long-livers. After additional analysis of the collected data, scientists found that the maximum life expectancy over the past 20 years has not only not increased, but even decreased. So, from 1968 to 1994 this figure grew by 0.15 years each year. Gerontology tells us that the life span of a living organism is very different from species to species. Inside the form, this indicator is always about the same. Without diseases and attacks of predators, the life expectancy of a single species grows. But this indicator does not increase infinitely. For example, a mouse in calm conditions can live for about three years, dogs of different species live to 10-15 years. People in different countries live up to 65-95 years.

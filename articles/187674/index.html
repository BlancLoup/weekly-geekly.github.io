97-year-old painter paints pictures in Microsoft Paint



American artist Hal Lasko (Hal Lasko) works in a mixed style of pointillism and 8-bit art. Despite his venerable 97-year-old, his grandfather (as his relatives call him) still holds the mouse tightly in his hand.

Graphic editor Microsoft Paint is enough for his needs. Moreover, it is best suited, based on the specifics of pointillism - this is a technique of drawing with separate strokes of regular, pointed or rectangular shape.

In World War II, Hal Lasko drew guide and weather maps for the bombing, and after the service he worked as a typographer, until he retired in the 70s.

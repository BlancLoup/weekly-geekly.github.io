Today there is not even a telephone connection in the city. But all Libyan domains of various services like bit.ly and brief.ly continue to work.

Yesterday, the rebels even made their way into the building of the state telecom operator and defaced the main page of the domain zone registry .LY - nic.ly :



Pages of other sites, like the ltt.ly operator itself , so far without noticeable deviations.

How will this affect other sites in the .LY zone you ask?

Most likely, it will be remembered by history as a case when adrenaline rushes to the head in the head, while on the street they constantly shoot, and everything will manage with slight shock.

First of all, only one page - the main one. Other pages like nic.ly/whois.php and other sections of the registry work.

The server's root of the zone .LY, and there are 5 of them all over the world - have not touched it yet. Two servers are located directly in Tripoli. Two - America (in Oregon), and one - in Holland.

Cheat sheet for a candidate: what questions to ask a potential employer at the interview

Each candidate at the end of the interview, the recruiter usually asks if there are questions. Often people are lost and do not find what to ask. And only after some time (often, when a person is already working in the company), questions naturally emerge about which it was necessary to ask beforehand, and not to take the cat in a bag and wonder ‚ÄúHow did I get here? Where I looked? Why didn't I think about it? ‚Äù This is the so-called "stair effect". I prepared a cheat sheet that you,% habrauser%, did not lose.

General


1. Work schedule and is it possible to move it?
I love to sleep. But in my last job, ‚Äúworking morning‚Äù started at 7:30. Not only that I often could not come to this time, I was still half a day engaged in trying to bring myself into working form. Somewhere until 10:00. From 10:30 to 11:30 I worked hard. Then there was lunch. And then I wanted to sleep, and not work, because I did not get enough sleep in the morning. Do I have to say that instead of 8 hours for which the employer hired me, I worked in the ‚Äúflow‚Äù of only 3-4 hours. So for yourself you need to solve the question - when am I the most able-bodied? And discuss the work schedule with management. And one more nuance. Try to fix the agreed schedule in the employment contract or contract (hereinafter TD), if the employer goes on a personal work schedule for you. No verbal agreements. Otherwise, you will start to twist your hands already in the process of work (as it was with me).

2. Dresscode?
Here I think, explanations are not needed.

3. Vacation?
If you change jobs before summer, then suddenly there may be a vacation issue. Especially, if someone from the family has "staked out" leave on his job. Employers are reluctant to give leave in the first six months or even give them only a year later. But if you really need - you can bargain. The main thing is not to forget about this at the interview. The agreement must be recorded in the TD.

4. Business trips?
Someone loves, some do not. But when business trips for 2-3 months - it is necessary to immediately clarify the interview and take into account.

5. Payment for processing?
If there is a provision for compensation for recycling - kindly show. Usually this item is included in the collective agreement, but rarely compensations are paid in practice. But here you can‚Äôt put a straw here, and you only know when you jump into the pool with your head.

6. Can I bring personal devices?
The question is about how to access and work with them (if you are a mobile application developer). The court also has the following question:

7. Do devices for use or for work?
At our office, managers were given a working iPad and a laptop. A nice bonus must be said.

8. LCA - additional medical insurance?
What is included in it. Specify about the dental. For some companies, dentistry is not included in the LCA package. People get upset when they have to pay their money.

9. Non-disclosure agreement?
Whether there is a? What is included in it?

10. Training at the expense of the company?
Every year, or more often, lists of courses are prepared for which employees can be sent for advanced training. You can ask what courses you can take this year. Does the company pay for them? And how much will they cost you personally? For example, there is a practice to demand working off in a company for some time or a return of funds spent by the company on you. For what period? What is the salary for this term? Is it possible to revise the results of the trial period.

This item is about certification, passing courses. I think you want to not only hang a piece of paper in a frame on the wall, but also feel the heaviness in the purse from the increase. This question can also be discussed in advance. If the employer is not interested in your professional growth, should you be interested in it?

21. Indexing and salary revision?

24. Providing HW and SW by the employer and used by the employer HW and SW?
How many monitors are you talking about? Give two. Do you use NetBeans or Eclipse, and if I work on IDEA? What are your servers running on? On a windows server? Do you use rar or zip? 25. Organization structure?
You can ask for a diagram of the organizational units of the organization and show where your place will be. Thus, it is easy to calculate how many bosses will be above you (I had 7 of them at one time). You may be puzzled by the question: who is engaged in architecture and analytics? But these questions are not suitable for outsourcing companies, where everything is extremely simple and clear - team, team leader, architect, testers, project management, management and service personnel (bookkeeping, etc.).

By vacancy


26. Reopened or not?
Here we are getting closer to talking about projects and your immediate activities. 27. Is the problem solved by a job?
Jobs open for a reason, and in order to eliminate any need. It is this need that you need to try to find out. What particular problem arose with the employer that only your help is needed. For example, not enough people. Looking for professionals with specific qualifications or knowledge. Increased team productivity, etc.

Project


Talk directly about your future work with your future manager.

28. Tell us about the project?
When did it start? What stage is it at? What technologies are used in the project? What technologies are planned to be added to the project? How many people are involved in the project? Customer / monetization? Ask for a little speech about the project (let them dodge as start-ups :-) not all the same you sweat at the interview). And what will happen if financing stops tomorrow? When and who writes the tests? Is there a test team? How testing is done: unit tests and blackbox tests. Is there a code review?

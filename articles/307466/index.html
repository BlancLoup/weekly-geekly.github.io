Open Data Programming Contest: Budget Sprint



The Infoculture team this Saturday, August 13th, is testing a new hackathon format - in the style of sports programming. We are waiting for people who confidently program and catch the fan from solving problems for speed. If you're good at Python code, let's compete.

We have 20 tasks, examples of which can be viewed on GitHub , we offer to solve them during the day (from 10.00 to 20.00), let's see who is faster and better.

It is possible that someone will be too lazy to follow the link, so we will display some of the tasks here:

1. Visualization of the costs of closing the Sochi Olympics with the help of TheOpenBudget engine (but not just for 1 hour)
2. Dashboard on the state of Russian finances in 1.5 hours.
3. Database of municipal tax statistics (1.5 hours, eh?)

Each team (no more than 3 people) will have 10 hours of time and access to the general task pool. Each task will cost N points. M points will be awarded for speed.

- The amount of points will help determine the winner, who will take with him 30.000 rubles.
- The team that takes second place will receive 20,000 rubles.
- Two teams will be able to qualify for the third place and prizes of 10,000 rubles each.

To participate in the sprint, each team member must register .

Limited number of seats. The competition will be in the "boiling point" (ASI), Moscow, Maly Konyushkovsky lane, 2.

Thousand and one blister. Search for overpriced drugs

Recently, the Ministry of Health laid out a table with marginal prices for essential medicines , I was not bothered and checked how often these limits were exceeded in Moscow.



The idea was this: to take the prices of medicines that are available on the Internet on Sputnik , aptekamos.ru or poisklekarstv.ru to somehow combine them with marginal prices and see if excess occurs often.

First, about the registry, which was laid out by the Ministry of Health : firstly, it turned out that all these data were available on grls.rosminzdrav.ru . They are updated there every day and presented in the form of a neat Excel file. Therefore, having worn out with a tablet that was laid out by the Ministry of Health , I switched to the data of the GRLS. Dataset looked like this:



The list of vitally necessary includes ~ 3200 drugs, but taking into account the different forms, dosages and manufacturers, it turns out ~ 20 000 items. For each, the maximum price is indicated, at which the manufacturer can sell the medicine to wholesalers. It is important to note here that the maximum price is indicated for the manufacturer, and not for the pharmacy. On the price tag on the storefront, the value can be much higher due to taxes, wholesale and retail mark-ups, but more on that later.

Then it was necessary for each of the ~ 20,000 positions to find the prices that are in the pharmacies of the capital. For this, I took the wonderful Satellite / Medical site and, pointing out one medicine after another, collected all the prices. 200 names could not be found, for 800 drugs there were no pharmacies that would sell them. Thus, the number of items for which pharmacy prices could potentially be found has drastically reduced from ~ 20,000 to ~ 8,000. I do not think that this is bad, apparently, the remaining positions are completely unpopular, if they are not found in pharmacies in Moscow. One can still assume that the drugs are sold, but Sputnik does not know about them. Maybe. But, according to my estimates, the coverage of pharmacies at this service is about 25%, which is quite a lot. If there is no price, then the drug is really unpopular.

Then the most interesting thing began, the drugs needed to be combined in forms and dosages. Then I drew for each medicine a small picture of the form:



On it, each pharmacy price is represented by a blue stripe, the excess cases are marked in red. For almost all drugs, I observed severe excess in large quantities. Then I did not know how much to take into account in order to calculate the marginal price correctly.

Firstly, there are different manufacturers of medicines, you need to take the most expensive of them. Be sure to take into account taxes, wholesale and retail allowances. For example, a manufacturer can sell 30 capsules "Tulosin" for 471.89 rubles. or cheaper . Wholesale and retail markups are different for different price categories. For Moscow pharmacies for the price category ‚Äúup to 500r.‚Äù The wholesale premium is 15%, retail - 28%. Therefore, we add to 471.89r. 43%, we get 674.80. If the pharmacy is organized by a legal entity, and not an individual entrepreneur, then the wholesale and retail markups are taxed, so we add 10% to 674.80p and get 742.28. It does not matter that the pharmacy bought it for 499 p. to make margins, as in the category "up to 500r." She can not.

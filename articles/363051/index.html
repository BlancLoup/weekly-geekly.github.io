Instant photo with a cover

Material photos that you can just hold in your hands, please someone more than viewing your account on Instagram (despite the jokes about the smell of paper and clay tablets). On the case with the ability to instantly print images - under the cut.



The history of Polaroid cameras, in my opinion, deserves a separate publication in the cycle Brand History. He left his mark in popular culture. The first thing I remembered was that in the 10th season of the Devil's Service in the Mash hospital , there was a rare Polaroid camera capable of taking snapshots - and this is shown in 1968. The first camera capable of taking snapshots appeared in 1948, the Land 95A model. It looks much more stylish than most modern cameras - or I'm just too old fashioned.

Taking a photo and giving it to a living friend can be more pleasant than sharing with virtual friends. This is what the future Prynt startup project is based on , which is preparing to raise funds on Kickstarter. News of this appeared on TechCrunch .

A French startup has been working on the project since January. The goal is to turn your smartphone on the Android or iOS operating system into a Polaroid-style camera. At the moment there is a valid prototype of the case, which receives the file via Bluetooth from a smartphone and prints the image in 50 seconds. In the case so far only one sheet of photo paper is placed. In the future, it is planned to reduce the printing time to 30 seconds and place from 10 to 30 sheets of paper into the device, and, in addition, remove the need for wireless communication.

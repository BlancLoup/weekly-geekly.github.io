How to disconnect and get to work

37signals covers the four cities and eight time zones. From Provo, Utah to Copenhagen, Denmark, the five of us are separated by 8 hours. One positive side effect of this separation is that there is time for loneliness. People need a continuous stream of time to work together.

There are only 4-5 hours a day when we all work at the same time together. At a time when a team in the US is sleeping, David from Denmark is working. The rest of the time we work when David sleeps. This gives us about half a day to work together and the second half for independent labor.

Guess which part of the day is most effective for work? Independent. And this is not surprising. Many people prefer to work either early in the morning or late in the evening - at a time when they are not disturbed.

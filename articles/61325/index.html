Yota presents the project of Luc Besson and Jan Arthus-Bertrand - film HOME

On June 5th, on the Environment Day, a HOME movie will appear in the Yota Video catalog, which will be available for viewing via the fourth-generation Mobile WiMAX fast mobile Internet for one day to all HTC MAX 4G owners. This film is the result of the joint work of two masters of contemporary art - the world famous wildlife photographer Jan Arthus-Bertrand and the cult director Luc Besson. HOME is a year and a half of filming in more than 60 countries of the world; these are the most beautiful corners of the planet that are facing destruction. HOME is a visual masterpiece, it is a positive shock, a film is a discovery.
The key goal of the HOME film is to demonstrate the beauty of our planet, our common home, and emphasize the importance of caring for it. The film is intended to form a new vision of man - everything in this world is interconnected, and the task of each of us is to preserve nature and preserve life on the entire planet Earth.
On the same day, the film will be shown in open areas in Moscow, Tokyo, New York, London, Paris and other major cities. The film will also be broadcast on one of the satellite channels.
Production of the film: PPR, Europacorp, Elzevir, 2009.
The film HOME is provided for Yota by the charity foundation "Ecology and Peace" .
The film will be simultaneously shown on the open areas of Tokyo, New York, Paris, several cities of Russia.

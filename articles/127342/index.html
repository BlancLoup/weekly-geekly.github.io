APC data center solutions: power, cooling and control

The APC by Schneider Electric demo room is a unique place. Here, customers and partners of Schneider Electric , a division of which is APC by Schneider Electric, demonstrate the most modern developments of the company for switching nodes, server rooms and data centers. It is also a place of pilgrimage for the company's engineers, who periodically deprive the installed units of important parts, withdrawing them for research. Powerful UPS, capable of supporting hundreds of servers without power supply for the necessary time, are connected to a test load to test their operation in different modes.

On the other hand, where else can you touch the real iron from the world of ‚Äúbig‚Äù computers (with carrying handles and sometimes on wheels)? We took the opportunity and heartily tugged at the handles of representatives of the three main areas of APC activity: Symmetra uninterruptible power supply unit, InRow RP in-row cooling system and NetBotz monitoring system. In accordance with the spirit of the demo room, this material turned out to be a review, but we will definitely tell you about the individual decisions in more detail.


Nutrition
In addition to UPS for home and office, APC by Schneider Electric produces a wide range of uninterruptible power supplies for server rooms and data centers. Among them are both entry-level Smart-UPS models, suitable for ensuring uninterrupted operation of one or several servers, as well as powerful Symmetra UPS, capable of ‚Äúkeeping the load‚Äù of the whole building, filled to the full with iron and silicon. The higher the requirements for the device, the less often you will find ready-made models: there are only the main lines, differing in power, characteristics, and just sizes. And then the UPS configuration is selected for the customer, depending on the conditions. There may be many, and power consumption - the first, but not the last factor. How easy is it to increase the power of the UPS while increasing the load? What is the battery life provide? Will a diesel generator be used to back up power in case of long-lasting power outages?

Answers to these questions are provided by both the customer and the APC partner - integrator. The APC InfraStruxure Designer configurator helps to solve such problems: it can be used to design the ‚Äúright‚Äù uninterruptible power supply system, and not only. The configurator is aware of the parameters of most APC developments, and helps to develop the most suitable system for solving a specific problem.

Control
To control the environmental parameters in the data center, such as temperature, humidity, vibration, smoke, water, and to monitor the staff, APC offers a wide range of monitoring solutions, united under the NetBotz brand.



Temperature and humidity sensor mounted on the server rack door.



Separate video camera to control the entrance to the room. Not the latest model, now such cameras are used in the NetBotz series:



All monitoring devices connect to the local network using ordinary Ethernet cables. The NetBotz Room Monitor can also receive power over Ethernet (PoE), but this feature must be supported by monitoring hardware. If not, a standard power supply is used, but PoE allows you to reduce the number of wires (there are already quite a few in the data center).



Monitoring units come with built-in sensors and even cameras and the ability to connect additional external sensors and cameras, which allows using one unit per small server room (for example, this model allows you to connect up to 16 sensors).



If it is necessary to monitor a large data center or a large number of parameters, one cannot do without a rack-mount model with the ability to connect up to 78 sensors.

The difference between the NetBotz system and conventional IP video cameras is that all these devices (as well as UPS, air conditioning, socket outlets in cabinets, and in general, everything from APC that has a network interface) can be combined into one system. With its help, a single ‚Äúmission control center‚Äù is created, into which absolutely all information about the work of the data center is drained.

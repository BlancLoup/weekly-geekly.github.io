Nekki internship program

The idea to launch an internship project in Nekki appeared in the spring of 2012. We experienced difficulties with the selection of motivated and responsible personnel, and therefore decided to try to create the conditions for productive selection and training.

Over the summer, we received more than 1,000 applications and 15 people passed our internship. This turned out to be a very interesting and successful experiment, the details of which we will share with the readers of Habra.



‚ÄúIn the mind of a beginner there are many possibilities, in the mind of an expert there are only a few.‚Äù
Shunru Suzuki


The main value of our company is enthusiasm (this is how the name Nekki translates from Japanese), and we consider this quality to be the key to success. However, many people who came to us for jobs, at interviews looked burned out. Having worked in different companies, they lost their motivation and enthusiasm. As a rule, such people value their experience highly, but they are not eager to learn new things. This is especially critical in the IT industry, where a new one appears every day, and over the years and even months the market may change beyond recognition.

The first thing we did to reach out to enthusiasts and dreamers was to create an inspiring website about our internship program: talents.nekki.ru



But, of course, only one enthusiasm is not enough. There are many enthusiasts who easily catch fire with a new idea, but throw it away without finishing it. Therefore, the second necessary quality is composure, responsibility.

People with this quality have been brought up since childhood in such a way that they strive to do any work well. We already wrote about this in a recent article on Habr√©, and we are looking for just such people in the first place.

We suggested that applicants fill out a fairly large questionnaire. According to how thoroughly and accurately the answers were filled, it was possible to assess the composure and purposefulness of the person.

In addition to standard questions, in this questionnaire we asked to tell about hobbies and interests. As we thought, those who responsibly responded to filling out the questionnaire turned out to be versatile, enthusiastic, and competently writing. No wonder they say, "a talented person is talented in everything."

To search for candidates, we tried various sources: participation in exhibitions, work in communities, advertising among the audience of our projects, interaction with universities. However, the most effective method turned out to be contextual advertising on Vkontakte, aimed at the right audience (city, age, hobbies).

We recruited several people in the directions of game design, graphics, project management, community management and marketing.

Each trainee was assigned a workplace in the office and a personal tutor was appointed who is constantly nearby and helps with work issues.

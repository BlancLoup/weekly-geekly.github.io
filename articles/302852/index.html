Wheel of life


One of the first things affected by Tony Robbins was the so-called Wheel of Life, illustrating various aspects of the human world. Tony highlights the following areas:
  • physical form;
  • emotions and intentions;
  • relations;
  • time;
  • work / career / vocation;
  • finance;
  • glorification and sacrifice: religious perception.

We performed a fun exercise that helped us understand what our position is in each of the areas, and evaluated each of the aspects on a 10-point scale, where 10 meant that we are exactly where we want to be. Some have a high level of physical form, while other areas - for example, finance - are less developed. Man is always stronger in some areas, but weaker in some areas. This is usually determined by the amount of attention given to each aspect.

For example, if you are in good physical shape, then most likely this is due to the fact that you are focused on this. If you are doing well with finances, then you concentrate on them. The areas in which you are not successful, do not receive enough attention, and therefore suffer.

What is in focus, energy is wasted!

Tony talks about the meaning of balance, and that we need to constantly develop and improve every sphere of life. He calls it the three pillars of progress.

The App Store breaks records, Rovio experiments with genres, 9GAG takes on games - and other news of the week for a mobile developer



Apple App Store Records: Half a Billion Dollars in the First Week of the Year


In the first week of January , 2 new records from Apple were set . During this period, users spent about half a billion dollars on apps and in-app purchases on the App Store. And on the first day of 2015, record daily revenue was reached in the history of the App Store.

These achievements followed the record year of 2014, during which the turnover on the accounts grew by 50 percent, and the developers' revenue from the sale of applications exceeded $ 10 billion.

To date, the total developer income from the sale of applications is $ 25 billion.

Considering the growth dynamics of the main mobile stores, next year only Apple itself can beat this record.



We meet the new prices in the Russian App Store


Apple has changed prices in a number of countries, including Russia. More specifically, they made rounding to "beautiful numbers", and prices are now calculated at an average rate of 59 rubles. On average, we can talk about a slight decrease in prices as a result of such rounding. Alternative prices also appeared: 69, 129, 199, 249 and 299 rubles.


Apple ordered to return money to Europeans


Now a user without any questions or comments can cancel his purchase of music, books or applications and get money back, but at the same time save this content on his device. Apple can only delete content from the account history, that is, download it from another device or download it again.

The company cannot ban people from using this content, and all this is due to a new European Community directive. Probably, the problem will be solved in the next iOS updates.

Google also sticks to the letter of the law. So, the movie and music subscription can be returned within two weeks, books and purchased music - within seven days, and applications - only within two hours from the moment of purchase.


Google Play banned the use of user comments


Google updated the Play Store guidelines, adding a new rule stating that user reviews should not be included in the description of the application. The bottom line is that unscrupulous developers add reviews to the description, which contain the names of competitors' applications. Item added to the section "Abuse of keywords."

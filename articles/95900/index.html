Portable Media Player Cowon A3


You can‚Äôt call it a novelty - this device is already several years old and even in selling it is not so easy to find it (the manufacturer has released new models, which I hope to write about someday). But the ability to perform the role of a pocket media player - for which he had the device did not go away.



The player is based on a hard disk (the maximum volume of which is 80GB) - almost the entire front side is occupied by a 4 ‚Äù-inch screen with a high resolution of 800x480 (16: 9, 16.7 million colors). Apparently, it is the hard disk that makes up the bulk of the device - it is almost 300 grams with a non-removable battery. The latter, by the way, is enough for 6-7 hours of video, and with less intensive use - up to 15-18 hours.
On the sides, at first you notice not so much of everything - because of the stub on the left side. If you open it, the entire wealth of player interfaces becomes the following: USB host, power connector, line-in (with recording) and output, video-in (with recording) and video output. And most of the necessary wires are already included.



The device turns on very quickly - soon a colorful and intuitive menu appears before the user. The first time is unusual, that the screen is not touch - all control is carried out by a 5-position joystick and three buttons, each of which is associated with one or another function in different menus.

The speakers of the device (there are two of them on the lower edge) are quite loud, but headphones will most likely be used more often - there are no problems with this either, everything is in the best traditions of Cowon. The player lost most of the non-converted video files, but there were problems with some high-bitrate and high-resolution ones - they were not lost, slowed down or out of sync. But otherwise, there are no problems and the omnivorousness of the device is simply amazing - MP3 / 2/1, MP4, ASF, WMA, FLAC, OGG, M4A, MATROSKA (MKA, MKV), TTA, APE, MPC, WV, WAV, AVI, WMV, ASF, MPG / MPEG, DAT, MTV, TXT, JPG, GIF, PNG, TIF, BMP (up to 15 mpix) and subtitles in SMI, SRT, SUB (text) and DivX bitmap formats. The only thing that is just too tough for the player is the VOB format.

In addition to multimedia features, the player can serve as a device for reading electronic books - it is enough to set the minimum brightness (to save battery) and a suitable design style, of which there are plenty.
There are a lot of settings and everything is in somewhat clumsy Russian language - flexible settings of the screen, sound and recording quality, subtitles and scrolling speed of the text, alarm clock and much more. It is possible to change the firmware - the device connects to the computer (like a regular USB flash drive), the system files are thrown, the reboot ... and you're done. After downloading, you need to specify the USA region, then the volume will be more.
This player is perfect for the road - you can fit a huge amount of information into it. But, I repeat, the device is already quite difficult to find on sale - the same Cowon company has released equally interesting new items of this class.

How I "twisted" behavioral factors


Greetings to you, habrazhiteli! Today I would like to tell you about a very intriguing thing, like behavioral factors. Or rather, about their artificial improvement. And more precisely, about the shortcomings of services-cheaters that do it.

Just a little bit about behavioral factors.
Behavioral factors - is a set of actions of visitors on a particular site. Logging in to the site, time spent, browsing pages, ‚Äúclicks‚Äù on the suggested links, returning to the site - all these points can be attributed to behavioral factors. A source

Why am I writing this? The fact is that I am the owner of two commercial sites and, of course, I am interested in finding them as high as possible in search results. And at some point, after reading various articles, he decided to experiment. At once I will make a reservation that the result was a bit sad and I hope that this article will serve as a warning.


Start


Given:
  • 2 commercial sites;
  • Subject: construction;
  • Region of promotion: Moscow;
  • The age of sites: one is 5 years old, the second is 3 years old;
  • Traffic before the experiment: site A - ~ 350 users / day, site B - ~ 200 users / day;
  • Begin / end of experiment: January-April 2014;
  • Links are not purchased from November 2013;

For more ‚Äúcleanliness,‚Äù I did not make any changes on the site, except for posting articles once every one or two weeks. I note that I am not going to compare the positions before and after, but the traffic, since for me personally this is a more important metric.

Choice of service cheat


It did not take long to think about this moment - I decided to use the most actively advertised and new service at that moment. I will not give a link to it, but I will only say that it has an interesting name and logo. :)

Promotion strategy


After sitting in the forums and studying articles from several sources I was pleased with the following decision:

  • On site A add 1500 transitions per month
  • On site B add 900 transitions per month

That is, approximately I added 15% of the current monthly traffic. I think that it will be clear to the majority that it is somewhat doubtful to wind such a number of transitions on 1-3 requests, then I selected 80-90 LF and MF requests for each site.

The disadvantages of cheating: the depth and time of viewing


During the project setup in the service, I was immediately jarred by the lack of installation of the viewing depth and time on the site.

I believe that each site of a particular subject has its own depth and time of viewing the site. If for my sites the average depth is 4 pages, and the time is 4-5 minutes, then the shaft of visits with a depth of 2-3 pages and a time of 2-3 minutes somehow gets out of the general statistics. In the end, it happened.

Disadvantages of cheating: visitor characteristics


Yandex.Metrica has very remarkable and informative sections ‚ÄúGender and age‚Äù and ‚ÄúBy countries of the world‚Äù.

Search Behavior


We should also highlight how tasks are performed:

  1. The Employee opens the search engine and sets up a region (in my case, if it is not from Moscow).
  2. Enters a search query.
  3. Switches to a couple of random sites.
  4. Goes to my site.

It is very strange when a user from Poltava changes the region to Moscow and searches for concrete blocks, when yesterday he was looking for GDZ for the 8th grade. Then the user clicks on two random sites. Yeah. Most often these are sites from the TOP-5 or TOP-10. After viewing the sites, the user abruptly goes to the site from the 29th place and then finishes the search. Yes-ah, very natural behavior.

Total


After a difficult 4 months, I decided to finish the epic with this experiment. I will not bore me with a story about other problems, such as incorrect removal of positions, an incomprehensible algorithm for performing tasks and writing off funds, numerous minor bugs in the interface (which they promise to fix, but this does not happen) and much more.

  1. Site A - 220 user / day .

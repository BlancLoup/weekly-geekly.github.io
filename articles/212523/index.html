TelecityGroup is a recognized energy efficiency fighter, actively promoting environmental initiatives. The technologies applied in the TelecityGroup data centers demonstrate that energy efficiency is not only fashionable, but also leads to a reduction in operating costs. For example, the use of free cooling, together with in-line air conditioners and the release of cold corridors, can significantly increase the cooling efficiency.

Cooling system


Due to the cool Finnish climate, the potential of free cooling can be increased to 80%. In the summer months, as a rule, a combination of free cooling and chilling water cooling is used, which ensures maximum use of free cooling depending on the temperature and humidity of the surrounding air.



Four chillers with a total capacity of 1 MW and redundancy according to the N + 1 scheme are connected to the machine rooms with two independent pipelines.



Customer equipment is cooled by air-conditioners in-line, with redundancy at the N + 1 level. The cooling system is organized with the release of cold corridors. So we can say that the frosty freshness is guaranteed .



Power supply system


Electric power to the Viikinm√§ki data center is supplied from one of the largest transformer substations in Helsinki, 100 meters away. In turn, its electricity supply is provided with as many as four looped 110-kilovolt lines.



The power supply system is located inside the data center and is represented by 2 transformers, 3 UPS systems and 3 diesel generators.





The UPS system is reserved under the N + 1 scheme and has a total capacity of 1.1 MVA. The modular design of the containers provides for the possibility of further expansion and facilitates servicing.





In an almost incredible case of interruptions in the main power supply system, the data center is equipped with 3 diesel generators with a total capacity of 2 MW and a redundancy level of N + 1. There will be enough fuel for at least 10 hours without refueling at 100% load.



Each rack is connected to two independent power distribution units (PDUs) supporting a combination of feeders 16 or 32 amperes, single-phase (230V) or three-phase (400V). On each bus and each feeder energy meters are installed.

Snipers practice robots shooting


The Australian company Marathon Robotics has developed a training complex for snipers. The essence of the complex is simple: robots quietly ‚Äúwalk‚Äù along cardboard imitation of a city street, and snipers shoot at them. A sort of duck hunt in the twenty-first century.

Video (in English) and description of the system under the cat.


The complex is pretty heaped up:
  • robots do not collide, dodge unexpected obstacles, know how to coordinate their movements with each other and navigate in space using GPS and ladars
  • robots walk along programmed or random paths, i.e. no need to manage each one individually
  • robots react to shooting and hitting other robots - they run and hide
  • everything is controlled from one laptop by one operator

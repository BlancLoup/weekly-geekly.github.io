Bitbucket has reached one million users - fly celebrate in San Francisco

Today I received an invitation to participate in the contest from Bitbucket to my email inbox. It says that the Bitbucket repositories service reached a threshold of 1 million users, in connection with which a competition with the main prize is being organized - a trip to the hackathon in San Francisco.



Service developers associate large growth with the introduction of support for Git repositories (previously there was only Mercurial). I chose it as a service for storing my .hg projects. I use relatively recently, but for now I like everything.

A bit about Bitbucket features:
You can create an unlimited number of repositories in the service and store unlimited working information in them. Each project has the opportunity to use the wiki, bugtracker and code editor for free, as well as provide access to the Mercurial or Git repository via ssh and http for five members of your team, or an unlimited number of users if the repository is public.

So, the competition.
To participate in the competition, it is necessary from June 4 to June 11, 2013, as a member of the service with a confirmed email, to invite one or more friends through the form on this page bitbucket.org/dashboard/overview . Victory will be reported on June 15, 2013 via email.

The winner and his team member will receive:
- economy class ticket on a plane to San Francisco back and forth
- weekly stay at a 4-star hotel in San Francisco
- unlimited coffee, beer and pizza

The event will take place on June 20 at 6 pm at
The House Of Shields, 39 New Montgomery Street, San Francisco, CA


All the costs of obtaining a passport and visa you assume. Participation in the competition is impossible for residents of New York, Florida, India, Sweden, Canada, Argentina, France, Mexico, Poland, Brazil and Spain, as there are legislative prohibitions on this kind of pranks.

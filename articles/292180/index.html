Do not be afraid! If you decide to start a project, take it and do it. Only your own experience will teach you and give you much more knowledge than a hundred books read and advice heard. Try, try, try!


All successful startups that I know of were never made by teenagers, but made by people who worked in large companies, looked at how corporations are spinning, and with the pluses and minuses of these corporations, and worked in small companies, and even startups hired workers, and then left and did their own. Create the right environment

In a startup, it is very important to create a prototype of a product in a short time, then a product, then the best product. Your task is to assemble the involved and compact team, clearly define the directions of the efforts and not interfere. 4-5 strong and motivated developers in the same room can make a competitive product in a very short time.

Set priorities

When you work on your project, you have a lot of ideas, you want to implement them all, but you need to choose. Of course, this does not cancel experiments, but always remember about the costs of them and do not deviate from the main line.
And most importantly - learn to make decisions and take responsibility!

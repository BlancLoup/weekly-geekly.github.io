To replenish the room with air with a high oxygen content, it is necessary to draw out the exhaust air with a high content of carbon dioxide and other substances. From here there are simple requirements:
1. The room must have sufficient volume so that a person always has enough than to breathe. Therefore, when buying a home, it is desirable to consider not only square meters, but also cubic.
2. It is necessary to ensure both the air flow and its outflow. In the absence of one or the other, the process of air replacement takes a long time and does not keep pace with the increase in carbon dioxide concentration.
Example. In the old houses everything was done very well - fresh air was evenly supplied through the gaps in the windows and doors, and the removal of the exhaust air through the exhaust ventilation in the toilet. After installing modern sealed windows and doors, the person sharply limited not only the supply of fresh air, but also the outflow of waste. The inlet valves help, but they deliver air locally, compared to even distribution from the slots of the old window.
Natural or active ventilation should provide such an air exchange that at any time in the presence of a different number of people the content of oxygen, carbon dioxide and many other components of the air is always within comfortable limits.
3. In winter, it is possible to provide heated air. The simplest option is to install a supply valve between the window sill and the heating radiator (a modern analogue of the slot). In order not to throw out the heat with the air leaving the room, recovery systems can be used when the outgoing stream heats the incoming air.
4. The carbon dioxide sensor allows you to turn on the ventilation and adjust its performance in automatic mode so that energy is spent only in the presence of a person when the concentration of carbon dioxide increases.

Why Google is a great ISP

Google has announced the Google Fiber project in February 2010. After a competitive selection, Kansas City was selected from a thousand candidates for the experiment, yesterday tariff plans and connection conditions were announced for it. Google offers Gigabit Internet via fiber to an apartment for $ 70 per month, and 5 Mbit / s basic access - without a subscription fee, the user pays only for the connection.

Over the past two years, the company has spent a lot of effort on optimizing project costs, and due to various tricks, even such a business model is profitable for Google. Although the company honestly declares that it is engaged in laying fiber into apartments not for money, but to help the main Internet business.

Some people may fear that Google has so much user data, and here the company will also control the transmission channel, but from the point of view of the ordinary man in the street, Google seems to be the ideal Internet service provider. Its conditions are an order of magnitude more attractive than those of the current telecoms that are lingering .

Why does Google seem to be the perfect provider?

1. Net neutrality . Google has always consistently advocated respecting the principles of network neutrality for fixed-line providers. Moreover, the company was the main apologist for these principles, so it‚Äôs completely impossible to imagine that Google would suddenly begin to restrict the traffic of competitors of its Youtube video service. Already, the Fiber TV package is hosting Netflix video service, which theoretically can be considered a competitor to Youtube.

3. Network services . Now each subscriber receives a free Google Drive cloud storage for 1 terabyte. Most likely, in the future there will be other "goodies" for users.

Google has done an amazing job of optimizing the cost of network deployment, and was able to achieve savings at every stage: starting with cable in the city through power lines, and ending with connecting the device in the user's apartment (QR codes with automatic registration on Google Play).

Additional savings are achieved through the development of our own equipment (routers, digital set-top boxes), as well as due to the massive connection of quarters, which minimizes the number of specialist visits. Although Fiber is only available in one city, users across the country may wonder: why does Google provide speeds up to 100 times faster than my Comcast for the same price? Why does Comcast have the same gigabit 60-70 times more expensive? The situation is similar to the one when free Gmail came out with a 1 gigabyte mailbox, while everyone else gave only 5-10 megabytes and swore that there was no technical possibility to increase the size of the mailbox. After the advent of Gmail, the technical opportunity was immediately found for everyone.

The emergence of Google Fiber can lead to a revolutionary transformation of the ISP industry, which is almost frozen in its development.

Pedals, by the way, can be mounted both in a classic car - from above, and from below, as in real racing supercars, while also adjusting their rigidity for your convenience.

Thanks to the magnificently powerful 60 kW industrial motor, capable of producing up to 3000 revolutions per minute, the steering wheel behaves in your hands like a real wheel of a rally-raid leader: when turning, drifting on gravels or hitting cars of rivals, the steering wheel strives to break out out of your hands. Another steering wheel, created specifically for playing Grand Turismo 5, is a top model from Fanatec, a German manufacturer of exclusive gaming devices for car racing. The maximum number of wheel revolutions is 900 degrees: not 1080, as in the previous speaker, but also impressive. To create a feedback effect, three powerful Mabuchi RS 550 servomotors are built into the steering wheel, one of which is responsible for power feedback, two for vibration. The current game settings are displayed on the built-in steering wheel LCD display. The wheel remembers up to 5 tuning sets, which is very convenient if you, for example, race in Formula 1, in rally and on the streets of megalopolises.

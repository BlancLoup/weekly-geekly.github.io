FAQ: opening an account for an LLC in the PayPal payment system

Now I want to talk about how to open an account for an LLC in the PayPal payment system. Everyone already knows that this payment system allows you to withdraw funds in rubles to the accounts of Russian banks, but not everyone knows how to connect it and what is needed for this.

So, you already have a bank account, there are scans of the founders' passports (page with photo and registration), extracts from the register, OGRN, TIN, scan document (maximum file size should not exceed 500kb), which describes the business model, organization goals , and how PayPal will be used.

This payment system asks to upload scans of documents in a bonded form; any free photo editor will be suitable for this task.

Your first action will be registration on PayPal.com (the company opened a daughter in Russia in 2013 and does not fall under FZ 149), after which you will need to provide the payment system with additional information about your organization. As a confirmation of your company's current account, PayPal will send you a penny amount divided into two parts. After the initial registration and confirmation of the account, you will find out that access to your account is limited (you cannot receive and send funds), to remove this restriction, you will be prompted to switch to a paypal-merchant, you will need to provide your bank account details there, describe its activities, and provide personal data of its owners.

Among other data that you need to download, will be scanned account statements. To do this, you go to the bank, request it, and after 15 minutes you will receive an expanded statement of the account, which also includes OGRN, TIN, KPP, BIK of the bank, its seal, and also OGRN TIN of your organization (for the entire period of the account existence).

Then, you will need to provide information about the account holder. Everything is simple here: you will need to scan the pages of your passport with a photo and registration.

The final step will be to provide information about your company, its goals, the presence of beneficiaries in it (people owning 25% of the company and more), and providing a written power of attorney for additional users who have the right to work with the account.

Ministry of Communications officially endorsed Sailfish OS as an alternative to Android



The Finnish company Jolla has achieved approval of its operating system for Sailfish OS mobile devices by the Ministry of Communications and other Russian departments. According to the company, Sailfish OS was recently added to the Unified Registry of computer software and databases, and as a mobile platform it was approved for use in mobile device projects for government agencies and state-controlled organizations and corporations.

‚ÄúThe Russian government has a list of software that can be classified as national software and has been checked, certified and entered in the registry, and in this registry our software is the only mobile OS,‚Äù Jolla spokesman for TechCrunch commented on the situation.

Jolla is a company that consists mostly of former Nokia employees. The team is developing an alternative platform for mobile devices running its own OS Sailfish. A feature of Sailfish is its own platform with the ability to emulate the Android OS environment to fill gaps in software and applications for users. The OS from the Finns is the heir of Mer and Meego . At the same time, Jolla positions itself as an independent organization.

The release date for devices running Sailfish was called the end of 2016. At the same time, the Finns are already waiting on the Russian market and in the state segment: as early as 2015, the Russian government announced that it would set a goal to reduce the share of devices running Android and iOS from 95 to 50 percent by 2025.

The main target audience of Finnish vehicles running Sailfish is now called the public sector. The fork of the operating system for distribution on the territory of Russia (Sailfish OS RUS) satisfies all security requirements for possible alternatives to Android and iOS from the government, regulatory and supervisory authorities.

If in the case of iOS and Android, data is duplicated on Apple and Google servers, then in Sailfish OS they remain on the user's device and do not go beyond the jurisdiction of the Russian Federation.

How Telegram-bot overcame designer procrastination and helped increase the income of digital agencies

The founder of the digital agency Roman Kvartalnov shares a simple solution that helped make the production processes transparent, made it possible at any time to evaluate the cost of the project and the company as a whole, and also save about 1,000,000 rubles on collecting reports from employees.

Do you know the cost of your company? Can you get access to the information on the work done for yesterday in 3 minutes? And in a week?

Four years ago, I opened my own company. At the start we had a small team. Obviously, the fewer people, the better they are able to work harmoniously, to be a single organism. By the end of the first year, we began to actively scale up and faced growth problems. At a certain point, we simply lost control over the processes within the company and began to make decisions erratically. There is such a term - ‚Äúchaotic management‚Äù, it can be interpreted as follows: the head does not have the ability to predict risks, constantly extinguishes fires and does not understand why they occur. This approach has 2 ways of development:

1. Recognize that such is life and the business founder is assigned the role of a firefighter. In this case, your ceiling is a cool fireman, a fireman-superman.

2. Understand that fire extinguishing develops spiral, i.e. time goes by, tasks grow, resources decrease. In this case, your goal is to stretch and go beyond, stand over the problems and try to find system solutions.

I tend to hypothesize and test them. This approach was laid by teachers in the university where I studied nuclear physics. Actually, I apply the same approach to business. I find a problem, build a hypothesis about management decisions that can systematize processes, and check my assumptions. The hypothetical solution to the problem of "perpetual fire extinguishing" was the operational control of the company's cost in real time. To gain access to this and other important information, I urgently needed regular employee reports.

Chaos, which had to fight

Being engaged in the design of interfaces of systems, portals, mobile applications and other digital solutions, we work in a team of creative, slightly crazy people. The most organized and disciplined people among us are the developers, and all the rest ‚Äî designers, screenwriters, copywriters, designers, managers, analysts, marketers, sales managers, accounts ‚Äî are maliciously sabotaging the filling of daily reports. This deprives the manager of the opportunity to quickly assess the cost of the project at a real point in time and make important management decisions, which ultimately leads to loss of benefits.

So historically, for us the quality of the result of the work and getting into the business goals of the client is a must have. But at the same time, in pursuit of the same quality, we can go into uncontrolled processing, which leads to sadness in the project budget.

Decision

We started with a primitive solution - we introduced the rule of filling in daily reports in Google-form. The solution is very simple, clear and not very effective. To be more precise, the average monthly occupancy rate for daily reports was less than 15%.

We tried the promotion, found out what did not like, changed the wording in Google-forms, conducted personal conversations with an explanation of how important it is, but, unfortunately, the human factor in this process is not eradicated.

To simplify the process of filling out reports, as well as to pump the ability of employees to concentrate, we have introduced the practice of performing tasks with a timer. So what needs to be done so that people fill out daily reports.
Regularly, independently, quickly, simply, without unnecessary entreaties from the management?


It was always interesting to me, under what conditions and why people make a certain choice: buy or not buy, poke a button or not, write or call, etc. In the context of this there are many scientific approaches in psychology, neuromarketing, neuroeconomics and physiological features human body.

My attention was attracted by one of the trends - the concept of "artificial intelligence". In my view, this is the highest degree of development of the system. There are several communicative levels of interaction between the system and the person:

1. Man - System. These are input tools, where a person is the initiator of actions (Word, Excel, Paint, payment terminals for purchases, parking, travel in public transport, etc.)

2. Man - System - Man. This is the construction of the interaction of people using the system (messengers, task managers, Yandex-taxi, Uber, etc.)

3. System - Man. This is the construction of such an interaction, where the system is the initiator of the dialogue (a great example is Google infrastructure. Observations on the implementation of ZERO:

A high percentage of report filling is not just a demonstration of discipline in a company. ZERO made it possible to save impressive amounts. The math is very simple:

They reduced the time for filling in the reports - they saved about 100 thousand rubles a month.

Team time: 15 minutes x 21 working days = 315 minutes / 60 = 5.25 hours x 15 people = 78.75 hours / month
Managers time: 2 hours x 4 weeks x 2 managers = 16 hours / month

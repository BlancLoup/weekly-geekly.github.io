LG explains how to watch pirated movies

The very fact that pirated DVD rips are used for promotional purposes is not new. As an example, The John Lewis store used rips from the famous pirated content release aXXo to increase sales of iMacs ( proof , proof ), and also Saturn, one of the largest sellers of home electronics in Europe, used the same dishonest technique for promoting MacBooks ( proof from the Madrid shop ).
In the above cases, pirated films were shown on monitors in stores. However, the giant international electronics maker LG has taken a step forward.

So the story. One of LG's products is a TV with a USB connector for reproducing images, audio and video files from a flash drive.
Naturally, the instruction manual is also attached to the TV. In the picture below you can see that the playback of video files from USB media in the brochure is described on the example of pirate rips of the film The Incredibles and Aviator (the image is clickable).



Apparently, LG is well aware that the USB input on TVs is used in 99% of cases to watch movies downloaded from the Internet. Although it is likely that the compilers of the manual just for the speed / convenience downloaded a couple of movies from the network.

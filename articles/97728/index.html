Questions to Alexander Zudin (Paragon Software)

In Russia, Paragon Software is best known for its system utilities: Paragon Partition Manager, Paragon Drive Backup Paragon Hard Disk Manager, Volume Snapshot (alternative to Apple Time Machine).

But this company has a division that develops software for mobile devices: SlovoEd dictionaries, PenReader handwriting recognition software, etc. These developments are most popular in the West, where Paragon Software is one of the leaders in the market, and in our country it is probably the largest developer of software for smartphones in general. It is difficult to find a company that sells so much software for Android, BlackBerry, iPhone, Palm OS, Windows Mobile and Symbian OS, and indeed in this business for 15 years. Back in 1996, they completely Russified Newton OS, in 2004 they made handwriting on the Microsoft Tablet PC, they are now filling up the Apple iPad.

Alexander Zudin - Head of Intelligent Mobile Devices Paragon Software. Since the company was founded by students of MIPT in 1994, Alexander took part in the development of several hundred software applications for all of the above platforms. In addition to dictionaries and PenReader, these are also games, business applications, travel programs and other utilities.

Of the latest interesting statements of Alexander to the foreign press, he is skeptical about the functionality of the Google Dictionary, since Paragon makes better-quality dictionaries, albeit paid ones.

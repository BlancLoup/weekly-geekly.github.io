Diseases "inspiration" blogs: Good, bad, evil

Today I write about the problems in the art blog (Inspiration blog), which I enjoy. What is an Inspiration blog? This is a blog that helps inspire inspiration.

On the universal wave of popularity, I also created a blog. After ~ year of working on it, wandering around the network and observing, I will tell about this picture.

Creating a blog now is not a big deal. Anyone in 15 minutes can create a blog, set it up and start posting, a lot of free services offer to do it on their sites (blogger, tumblir) or on their hosting (wordpress).

There are a couple of diseases in art blogs:

1. Different size graphics.
2. Randomization of filling.
3. Informativeness.
4. With the advent of the crisis, blogs have become overgrown with advertisements like sticky tape - flies.
5. Copy the same information from larger resources.


1. Multi-sized graphics



It's all about the perception of information by our brain, science has proven that a person perceives information from the screen 30% worse than from a piece of paper, which already lowers the level of perception from 100% to 70%.

The first factor of human perception is size. If all the graphics in the art blog are of different sizes, then I see a huge problem in this blog. After 4-5 pages of viewing such a blog, everything slowly and surely turns to mush, because The brain gets tired every time to reconfigure to a new graphic size.
example dropular.net/user/IsaTibo











2. Randomness of filling


Content is king, everyone knows that. But when a blog on one page has a couple of T-shirt designs, an interior, a new gadget, a couple of pictures and photos, it seems to me that this blog has a problem.
Many may be against it, but my opinion is that a blog should be more or less targeted, no matter how, by topic or by style.



3. Informativeness


Very often in blogs there are slides of pictures, without a signature, without a link to the owner, which automatically turns the blog into an untitled gallery, which is very annoying due to the lack of an aiketer, which is the text. Not to mention the rights.

example chris.9gag.com













4. Blog ads


If it is 1-2 banners, then it is not critical, but if there are a dozen banners, as a result, the size of the informative part very often makes up less than half of the whole page - it infuriates.















5. Copying Information


Yes, you can often find 10 identical blogs in a row (which is easy to track on the same tumblir), they seem to me to be useless clones.



And so, which inspection blog is good?
Good is a blog that is easy to perceive, information is new and interesting, there is something to read.
An example of lovelypackage.com is very cool, I always want to return to it.

And the conclusions? Conclusion one, do not allow these errors.

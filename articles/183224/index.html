Against teachers who read on paper

This post prompted me to write the current situation at my university. I study in one of the technical universities, which is recognized as ineffective.

First grade


Studying in the first year of university, I did not experience any problems. Everything was perfect: at the open door, applicants were advertised for their cool students, they study a whole stack of modern programming languages, excellent teachers, and so on. Spent an impressive presentation.

Of course, first of all I was interested in precisely those disciplines in which they teach programming. A university is a university - you cannot get anywhere from general disciplines (such as philosophy, economics, ecology, cultural studies, etc.).

But! I was happy. I enjoyed studying at this university. Really good teachers. Mathematics is high. Informatics began to learn office suite. And here I began to doubt the teacher (let's call him X). I asked the teacher to give assignments in the LibreOffice office suite. I do not want to use paid (or illegal) software, especially if there is a free version. X refused my request and what should I do in Microsoft Office? So in our country, students are encouraged to switch to free software. By the way, there is no explanation at all: a training manual with tasks and everything.

In parallel with the seminars (Microsoft Office) lectures on the programming language SI were held. No one understood anything. The teacher just simply inserted the text from a textbook on the slides and read it monotonously monotonously. At first, I, like the others, tried to write off at least the code from the slides, but did not have much time, because with such font size and number of lines it is too difficult to do. It was funny that the teacher tried to improvise: from time to time he changed words in places or inserted the word "actually" into his speech. Then I had to come to terms with this situation. Many people understood that attending these lectures was a waste of their time.

The next semester began the course "Object Oriented Programming in C ++." This course was the most useful for all the time at university. Although the local teacher and taught the book, at least he told and showed! I do not mind what he taught on the book, which we were not officially given. The book, by the way, of the same name.

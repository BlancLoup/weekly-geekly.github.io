How to shoot macro video

I think many of you have seen my video soldering lessons . Many wanted to know how and how it was filmed. I am tired of replying to me every time, so I won‚Äôt keep a secret.

Filmed all the camera. It gives better video quality than a standard digital video camera of equal price. Yes, and he has a better optics in this regard. He also writes directly to the USB flash drive, and the fact that the recording time is short is not critical.

I have a Sony H50, like this:

I think any digital camera will give you a ride if it only has a macro mode. And with the ability to zoom in macro mode. It seems that everyone can do it.

But I need to shoot not static pictures, but a process. And here there are two problems.

1. Operational space. If I stick a lens into a microcircuit, then I'm not a crawler for it with a soldering iron. So you need to shoot from afar, but in macro mode.
2. Flux, or rather its spray. I did not smile at wiping the delicate optics from the splashes of alcohol-decalified solutions. So between the camera and the process should be a protective glass.

The problem was solved by the manufacture of a mega tripod. The suspension was bought for the monitor. The most common, for a thousand rubles, in some large household appliance store. The suspension was tightly screwed to the table, yet the hinges were tightened more tightly, in some places it was necessary to replace plastic washers with leather pads soaked in rosin. To tighter spin.

The holes in the plate are drilled and a bolt from an ordinary photo stick is inserted into one of them. The camera was already fastened to it. In order not to crawl, put a leather strip.

Now we need to lengthen the macro. This can be done with an external lens - it will also protect the camera's lens from splashes.
I took the lens from the carrier and, from its piece of iron, bungled a cunning hinge fastener, which allowed me to move the lens as desired, adjusting the sharpness. And the board itself was clamped in the Proxxon hinges. Also, putting all sorts of stuff like compact discs under the yews, I made them higher or lower, thereby further adjusting the sharpness. Bottom, on the stand, fixed fan blowing smoke.

My fotik screen is fixed on the hinge, so it can bend and ensure a good overview.

AOL Video is catching up with YouTube: 20 million video files

AOL launched an update for its video portal AOL Video with improvements and fixes in the design to make it easier and more convenient for users to find, watch and transfer to others more than 20 million video files on the service, reports WebWire.

Now AOL Video users will be able to perform all file operations without having to visit any other resources. The design of the main page was completely redone in order to improve the functions of searching and watching videos on the web.

The developers also integrated the Truveo search system into AOL Video. This technology is the leading video search system on the Web. In addition, a feature has been added to search and view video files from other AOL services.

"Network video services are probably the fastest growing industry in the history of the Internet, and AOL was one of the first services that could provide video content to online users," said Fred McIntyre, vice president of AOL Video. - Users need a service where they can find and watch all video files that interest them.

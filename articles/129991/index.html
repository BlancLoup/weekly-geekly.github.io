ZBOX nano VD01 - Dual-core micro-PC with FullHD support

Two days ago, the company ZOTAC, best known for its graphics cards, announced an affordable palm-sized micro PC - ZBOX nano VD01 . The key features of this nettop are impressive characteristics for such a baby, and, as ZOTAC says, an acceptable price.





With dimensions of just 127x127x45 mm, the ZBOX VD 01 is built on the basis of the first dual-core chip from VIA - the Nano X2 U4025 with a clock frequency of 1.2 GHz. The nettop will come in two trim levels - normal, including 1 GB of RAM and no hard disk, and Plus with 2 GB of RAM and a built-in hard disk with a capacity of 320 GB.
Both versions are based on the VX900H chipset with integrated VIA Chrome9 graphics and have a department for installing a 2.5 "hard drive, support up to 4 GB of DDR3 RAM. There is an integrated Wi-Fi 802.11 b / g / n adapter and Bluetooth 3.0.



Ports :
  • HDMI and DisplayPort
  • Two USB 3.0 connectors
  • Two USB 2.0 connectors
  • Card reader
  • Ethernet
  • eSATA

Integrated video allows you to play 1080p video in Blu-ray, MPEG2, H.264 and VC-1 formats.
The nettop is equipped with a proprietary remote control that works through an IR receiver (connects via USB), as well as a monitor mount.

Platform 2010 from the inside. Part 2

On the second day, we decided not to sit up on the reports and see what was around.

Next to the red hall there were two laboratory classes. One could be called general-purpose, the other was devoted to Exchange Server 2010. This year, visiting labs was possible only by prior request on the site. This approach has a significant drawback: the registered participant may not come, and the place will be empty. Nobody came to the test drive of the Office server solutions. Probably the coffee break had a relaxing effect on IT professionals. Microsoft employees quickly looked around and gave us an individual workshop on the topic, which Sharepoint can be useful for students and educational institutions. It was very interesting, I even had an idea to read the same report with us at the VMK MSU.

Nadezhda Altynova from the Microsoft Office Solution Center treated me with a carrot with the Microsoft Technology Centers logo in an e-mail package with sealing wax. In my opinion, these were heard at an event a couple of weeks ago.



In the remaining time, class administrator Dmitry Kochnov showed the server hardware used and told how it works. Virtual machines for tasks are run on three IBM X3850M2 servers with four six-core 64-bit processors and 96 GB of RAM on board. IBM DS4700 disk arrays (16 x 72 GB) and IBM EX810 (16 x 146 GB), and also an external USB screw. The shelves are connected by two Fiber Channel interfaces through a Brocade switch. As the test drive showed, such capacities were not fully mastered, but everything worked quickly.

Although the opinions of the participants were divided, in general, we can say that the Platform was a success.

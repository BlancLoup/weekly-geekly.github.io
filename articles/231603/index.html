A week with the Samsung Galaxy K Zoom: a brief overview of the new smartphone-camera

Smartphones with optical zoom appeared a long time ago, during the reign of the Symbian operating system. It's about the Nokia N93 / N93i and Samsung G810, released in the middle of the "zero". However, no one began to develop this direction: in all the following devices, the developers focused on increasing the number of megapixels. Of course, they also worked on optics, and the image development algorithms improved (which naturally led to an increase in quality), but the notorious megapixels were mainly upgraded. It is, in principle, understandable: in the head of the average consumer, this parameter is associated with the quality of the images, which, of course, is not true.


Samsung Galaxy S4 Zoom

In 2013, Samsung suddenly remembered the optical zoom and released the Galaxy S4 Zoom model. This smartphone ... um ... the thing looked like a little, it was more correct to compare it with a digital camera. I would venture to suggest that this piece of engineering art did not have huge sales, since at the start it cost very much for 20 thousand rubles, photographed as a soap dish for 5 thousand rubles, and played poorly on the role of a smartphone.

A year later, in the summer of 2014, the Samsung Galaxy K Zoom appears on the market. Apparently, Samsung was too often blamed for the fact that the Galaxy S4 Zoom (except for the camera) was much weaker in performance than the Galaxy S4. Therefore, this year's ‚Äúphotofonder‚Äù was called the Galaxy K Zoom, so that it is smaller compared to the Galaxy S5. To avoid this, however, will not work: even in the process of developing a new hybrid of a camera and a smartphone, they made it look like a "five" in appearance. In particular, the back panel is the same in design here - it simultaneously resembles adhesive tape and the ceiling in the Zhiguli family of cars from the early 80s. Plus, the software is about the same - I mean the OS version (Android 4.4.2) and the TouchWiz shell.


Samsung Galaxy K Zoom (left) and Samsung Galaxy S5

Here are the main differences between these devices list with explanations.

- Unlike the Galaxy S5, the Galaxy K Zoom does not have a waterproof case. This, of course, a minus. But there is a plus: the microUSB port is not covered with a stub - in the Galaxy S5 it managed to get me in two months of operation by the very fact of its existence.

- The Galaxy K Zoom does not have a fingerprint scanner and pulse meter. Given that the level of implementation of these functions in the Galaxy S5 does not seem high to me, I did not feel much regret because of their absence. And another thing: in the press release accompanying the announcement of the Galaxy K Zoom, it was said about the presence of a certain S Health Lite. Well, I do not know, I have not found anything like this. My device is made for Ukraine, maybe in versions for other regions it is.

- Galaxy K Zoom does not have USB 3.0 support. Sad but not scary.

- The Galaxy K Zoom screen of the previous generation. And this concerns not only the resolution (4.8 inches / 720p versus 5.1 inches / 1080p), but also something else. So, the color profiles available in the Galaxy S5 are not in Zoom, and the picture on the Super AMOLED screen is somewhat acidic.

How we did google glass flashmob in san francisco



We walked in a park in San Francisco with Google, recorded interviews and understood that Americans are very interested in looking at us. They all wanted to know what we are shooting there and what kind of Google is these (although many have already heard about them). And we decided that it would be nice to try to do some kind of action on Union Square (the central square in San Francisco).

5 years ago I was fond of photography and my favorite style of photo I had portraits. It's very simple - you take a 50mm lens, set the aperture at 1.4 and you get just divine photos! The trick is that the world through a 50mm lens looks just like a human eye sees it. And the fully open aperture blurs the background very nicely. Therefore, my task was pictures.

Pavel Kholyavkin is in the info business and has his own Appincubator show (mobile application incubator), where he teaches people how to create a business on mobile applications. Pavel filmed a video on GoPro - the videos are dynamic if you install GoPro on a stick.

All this right in the parking lot was unpacked, the boxes stuck in the trunk and on Lyft went to Union Square.

The concept was very simple: I suggest that the Americans put on a Googleglass so that we could take them and send them a photo with glasses to them in the mail. But in the opinion of the Americans, it was VERY DIFFICULT!

Firstly, we did not take into account that the iPhone is now the most popular camera in the world and the Americans simply do not understand what a quality portrait is. And print out examples of photos so that people understand what we offer - we did not have time to do it.

Secondly, we did not take into account the target audience, they were innovative people who were interested in what Googleglass is, to all other citizens, except how to look at it all from the side was not particularly interesting.

Thirdly, it was more interesting for them to try how the glasses work much more than to get a photo.

Therefore, right along the way, we changed the concept to a simpler one:
Try Google Points!
And then the people began to respond more vividly.

Twitter has ceased to consider links and attachments in the length of the tweet

The microblogging service Twitter announced an update that increased the length of the tweet. The need for such changes has been discussed for a long time, and the official position of the company on this issue was confirmed by the leadership of Twitter at the beginning of this year. At the same time, the limit of 140 characters is not going anywhere. Expansion of message sizes lies in the fact that it will now be considered a symbol as such.

Say more about what's happening! Rolling out now: photos, videos, GIFs, polls, and Quote pic.twitter.com/I9pUC0NdZC

- Twitter (@twitter) September 19, 2016


Previously, any information in the tweet input field was considered a tweet symbol: text, links (both external and to other tweets), calls to other users, and even polls. Depending on the functionality used (attached links, pictures, etc.), the actual text length of the tweet was reduced from 140 to, often, 90-100 characters.

Taking into account the fact that Twitter has long become a kind of not only a social network, but also a news aggregator for many users, the company management decided to meet that part of the audience that is engaged in content generation (and it‚Äôs not so great).

According to information from the company 's official blog , with new updates, the symbols will no longer be counted for non-textual content, namely:

  • when replying to other users ("@username" at the beginning of a tweet will no longer be considered signs, which will allow for a more complete discussion);
  • when attaching images and GIF-animations;
  • when retweeting or replying to your own tweets;



In addition, the company plans to abandon the use of "@" as a mandatory symbol for mentioning the user. It will be possible to reply to tweets simply via the "reply" button. However, in order for your subscribers to see the correspondence, the record will have to be retweeted.

In the meantime, Twitter has ceased to take into account the symbols attached images, GIF and polls. Other changes have not yet entered into force.

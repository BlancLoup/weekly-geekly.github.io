Mobilization: OMAX, SmartPay and Opera Mobile Store



Today we continue to share surprises on a mobile theme. In the previous article, you already learned about the release of new versions of Opera Mobile and Opera Mini, and also briefly familiarized yourself with the new features introduced in them. These were news to a greater extent for users, but today those who prefer to combine the useful with the pleasant also expect surprises - use the Internet not only as an information and entertainment channel, but also as a means of earning real money for themselves. But first, a little theory and numbers.

Everyone knows that the number of users is a key indicator that allows you to assess the commercial potential of a software product or an online service. If you have a million users - you already have the opportunity to earn tangible money on partnerships, advertising, paid services and other reasonable (ie, voluntary for the user) ways to make a profit. If your user base has grown to the level of 10 million - you can not only have a small staff, but also receive funds for the further rapid development of the project. The 100 million user audience says that you have become a participant in big business and if you still haven't thought about how to monetize your project - you are, to put it mildly, a very original person.

To date, the number of users of Opera Mini and Opera Mobile browsers in the world exceeds 160 million, more than 6 million more are added to this figure every month. And we are not original - we want to earn money. Naturally in reasonable ways. In fact, we have long been making money on our mobile development, which everyone can learn from the publicly available quarterly Opera Software reports , but for some reason we want to earn even more. Here is such a strange whim.

But how to make money on software products that are distributed completely free of charge? This is a very interesting question and it is always asked of us as one of the first at any meeting with users and developers. Yes, the product itself, we do not sell to users, but we can provide an opportunity for others to sell their products and services through our browsers, while receiving a certain percentage of the profits of our partners. As you can see - in fact, everything is quite simple and trite. It remains only to offer our users and potential partners convenient tools that will allow you to both earn wisely and properly spend your money. These are the tools that will be discussed today.

Opera Mobile Store


We have launched an updated version of this portal, which provides distribution of both free mobile applications and fully paid ones. Now this service is based on the Handster project acquired last September, and after completing all the integration steps, today Opera Mobile Store has become the world's fifth largest mobile application portal. At the moment, the catalog contains more than 55,000 applications (both free and paid) for all popular devices on the Android, Java, Symbian, BlackBerry and iOS platforms (more than 3000 devices are supported in total), sales are made in 200 countries, and monthly application downloads exceed 45 million.

And again, returning to the conversation about the benefits of mass product: by the way, access to the Opera Mobile Store is carried out in one click from all Opera Mini and Opera Mobile browsers, and this, I recall, more than 160 million potential users of your applications, with a tangible part of them and your buyers. As I have already said, in Opera Mobile Store there are no problems with user payments and getting money by developers. This system works around the world and makes it easy to make payments when purchasing both mobile applications and mobile content.

So, with payment systems, the situation is clear, but there is always the fear that when making a mobile payment, something goes wrong, not there and not in the amount in which it was planned.

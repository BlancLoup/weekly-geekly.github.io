A group of enthusiasts, as told by the resource iRoboticist, decided to create a device with feedback in the form of a vest, allowing fans of computer "shooters" to feel the moment of hitting the bullet and the subsequent bleeding. The prototype was called TGV (Tactile Gaming Vest), and in its first generation it contained an array of solenoids that were activated when they hit a bullet game character.

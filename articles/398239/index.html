HyperX on Igromir: Answers to Questions

Hello! Year after year for many, Moscow has become a festival of high performance games, cosplay and new technologies in the field of games. Yes, yes, we are talking about Igromir, of course.



HyperX did not stand aside, and this year we had a big beautiful stand, a mountain of samples, the world premiere of a new headset, but most importantly - this year three important people visited our stage at once.

Alexey Knyazev


Alexey - Director of Business Development at Kingston in Russia, the head of the Russian representative office of the company, knows everything about the company's semiconductor products, supports his work and is a first-class specialist.

If you had a choice, use a computer with 8 GB of RAM and an SSD or a computer with 16 GB and a classic drive - what would you choose?

Personally, my choice, of course, 8 GB + SSD. In most toys, eight gigs is enough, and even a little software from everyday requires a large amount of memory at a time. But the presence of an SSD for everyday and popular tasks for ordinary PC use will be much more affected. And a quick launch of OS / Software, and a fairly fast swap, and just the overall level of performance will be higher. However, the disk subsystem is often the slowest in the PC. There is one more feature that is important - for example, in a month or two you are paid a premium, and you decide to buy some iron. Delivering another 8 gigs of RAM is to unscrew the two screws, but changing the HDD to SSD is at least migrating the OS or reinstalling it. And I would not like to spend time on it.

What do you think about the topic NVME? Do people and ordinary users need this?

Everything is simple with NVME. Yes, the speed of modern SSD is several times higher than that of HDDs, but even the fastest PCI-Express drives communicate with the rest of the hardware through an AHCI controller, which was originally created for the needs of classic HDDs. Hence, the non-optimal operation of the protocols, a large set of ATA commands that are not very relevant for SSDs and a lot of ‚Äúintermediate links‚Äù between the processor command and the actual action in the depths of the disk. In fact, NVME for SSD means approximately the same thing as moving the north bridge into modern processors at the time for PC architecture: direct access, improved compatibility with modern hardware, reduction in overhead costs.

In the real needs of home computers, the coarse power of current generation SSDs is enough, and a simple user will get almost nothing from switching to NVME. The fact is that operating systems simply do not know about the existence of such a way to communicate with the disk, and applications too. So, they are to some extent optimized for the current protocols, and in order to get a real performance boost, you have to wait for the support of this protocol inside the OS at the kernel level and the corresponding sharpening from the software. It takes some time.

Now the only actual consumers of NVME are data centers and high-performance render farms, which even run into high-speed PCI-Express drives. In general, of course, this is musthave-technology and its immediate future - and then, probably, some quantum computers will be relevant and all current opportunities will become dust on the side of the story.

New Intel systems still support DDR3: why and why?

Well, firstly, DDR3 has not yet exhausted its possibilities. Yes, in a number of tasks, a DDR4-based system is significantly superior to a similar one on DDR3, but these are only very high-loaded systems based on the flagship Intel processors. But there is a huge segment of consumer equipment. The man had AMD and 8-16 gigs of RAM, he decided to upgrade. There is no extra money, a basic i3 / i5 with a motherboard for the available memory is a great option to save. The difference can be put on the already mentioned SSD, for example. In this case, DDR4 will give a tangible difference in the price of the motherboard, and will require the purchase of the memory itself, and the performance will change significantly only if you use the video core built into the processor. In general, DDR3 will be made and sold for another five years, so users who want to upgrade and do not have extra funds for a complete replacement of iron may well take advantage of DDR3 that are not critical for i3 / i5 memory speed, and the difference in games almost invisible.

At the same time, if you are building a computer from scratch, it makes no sense to save on DDR3. The price per gigabyte has already reached the same level (and in some cases DDR4 is also cheaper), and if you charge a motherboard with 4 connectors, there will also be room for upgrade. DDR3 prices will not decline, but DDR4 may well double the volume at the same price in several years.

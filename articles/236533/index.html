ASUS Gladius - review of the game mouse from the Republic Of Gamers series

Continuing the development of accessories for gaming computers and laptops as part of the ROG (Republic of Games) gaming line, Asus has launched a new product on the market.
Meet:

GLADIUS Gaming Mouse





Its name is Gladius, the mouse received from the name of the Roman gladiator sword Maximus.
Thus, Asus conducts a direct connection in its ROG lineup between the ROG Maximus motherboard, giving it the role of a soldier and its weapon - a sword, the role of which is in our case performed by a gaming mouse - Gladius.

When creating this mouse, Asus focuses on the complete design modification of its mouse-structure technology to ensure a comfortable and polished game process.

Shortly before the start of official sales, many well-known online resources specializing in the field of digital devices made announcements of this product with the technical characteristics of the device, so this information has not been secret in recent days.

However, in the flesh until September 9, the site of the Russian branch of Asus was committed to a non-disclosure subscription))



However, the main Asus website in the ROG section published a press release of this product on September 5, 2014, which also contained photos of the device and a brief description of the key technical issues that the manufacturer focuses on when positioning the product on the game controller market.



The very first information on this product that I found was dated on the official website of June 7, 2014 and contained all the same, with the exception of other illustrations.
It was also noted that this mouse is designed to compete with devices such as the Razer Deathadder 2013 and SteelSeries Rival. It is worth noting that for the struggle in the market, Asus has chosen serious competitors, both companies specialize in the development of game controllers, and this means that the approach of these Brands to their products is very serious.
Actually, I will not bore you with historical moments and will begin to approach the most studied object))

Specifications





Packaging, packaging and design



The mouse comes in a small box with dimensions of 140 x 230 x 70mm. Printing has a matte texture with glossy inserts for inscriptions and photos. The colors used are classic for ROG rulers, burgundy and almost black colors, the inscriptions are made in white and red.
On the front side of the box is a large image of the mouse itself in gloss and three lines of the main advantages that the manufacturer declares.



The reverse side shows us a disassembled mouse in the manner ‚Äúhow it was done‚Äù with signatures to the main elements of the mouse design. Below is a list of the main technical characteristics.

At the conventional bottom of the box, as usual, there are serial and batch numbers, compliance with various standards and manufacturer's coordinates.



When you open the lid on the front of the box, we have a view of the mouse itself and a letter describing the historical origin of the name of the mouse and its positioning as a weapon for computer games with reference to the Asus Maximus motherboard.



The mouse is packed in a separate plastic block with an insert of thin cardboard with a transparent plastic lid, the block itself is quite easily removed from the box.



Together with the storage mouse, the entire delivery set is extracted from the box, which includes:





The quick start guide contains a section in Russian. The handbag for the supplied mouse is not bad, a little harsh due to synthetic material, as a whole leaves a pleasant feeling and will be very useful when transporting the mouse.



Cables from a set of different stiffness, a short meter cable is softer than a two-meter braided cable.



Braided cable for rigidity is exactly the same as the Logitech G9X, but slightly thinner. The cables have very interesting forms of USB connectors with embossed inscription "Republic of gamers"



Due to the fact that the cables are removable, the micro USB connectors have a special shape of a plastic case, with guides that precisely position the connector when connected to the mouse.





Particular attention Asus focuses on the "chip" replaceable microswitches of the Japanese company Omron.

Mouse



The mouse is designed for right-handers and this is uniquely indicated by its design and the location of the side keys.



The mouse itself is more than medium size and its length is 126mm and, in my opinion, with a good length-height ratio. Height 45mm.



Below four teflon legs. The legs sit in the grooves, which uniquely set their landing when installing a new set.



The legs are slightly shorter than the landing pads. This is done so that it would be convenient to dismantle the old set before changing the microswitches.

Also located below the slider lock locking the connected USB cable. The slider is most convenient to move the nail of the index finger of the right hand while holding the mouse with the rest of your fingers, you will need your left hand to remove the cable itself. I adapt myself to the implementation of this operation from the third time))
In general, the shape of the mouse itself is successful.









The color scheme of the mouse is calm, black bottom and graphite top. All surfaces are matte, except for three buttons, two side and top buttons, which are responsible for switching the DPI mode.

I really liked the scroll wheel - moderately wide, without backlash with a clear indication of the position of the wheel. The scroll wheel is visually similar to the mouse wheel on the Razer DeathAdder 2013.

The manufacturer pays special attention to the separate left and right keys. Maybe it really has some deep meaning, but the keys have a slight backlash, which one way or another you pay attention to, especially for me, the ‚Äúbump‚Äù of the right key was noticeable.

The top button for changing the DPI mode and the two side keys do not cause any complaints about the quality of the material or the lack of backlash and the quality of keystrokes. The DPI shift button has the most bass and at the same time muffled sound from all the available buttons. Next are the side buttons but with greater volume, then the key press the scroll wheel and the thinnest sound of the left and right keys.

In other words, all the buttons sound in their own way and with different loudness, I suppose, over time, the owner of the sound can determine which key was pressed in the dark))

I want to separately pay a little attention to the side keys, while using the mouse, they never came under a random press, in my opinion a very good location and geometry (bevel angles) of the keys themselves.

I didn‚Äôt disassemble the mouse, but exploring the edge of the rubber lining under the thumb, ring finger and little finger gave me reason to believe that the rubber linings are glued. Software



On September 10th, the mouse appeared in the product list on Asus.com, and with it the software became available.



After installation, the program immediately issued a window with a proposal to update the mouse firmware, which was done immediately.



Next, I suggest you familiarize yourself with the possibilities that the software provides.
Window for setting mouse buttons with the possibility of assigning them various functions



You can create different profiles and assign different functions to the buttons inside the profile, including macros.



In the next window, you can fine-tune the response of the optical sensor, accuracy, sensitivity values ‚Äã‚Äãduring slow and fast movement, as well as the frequency of polling via USB





The backlight mode setting window, with the ability to turn off specific areas and change the backlight type from flickering to a constant glow. Unfortunately, these functions did not work for me in this software version.



And the last, quite useful window - adaptation of the optical sensor for work with different materials of a surface.

The fact is that at first I checked the work of the mouse on different surfaces and was surprised by the capriciousness of the mouse to surfaces different from the material of the mat. The mouse sensor was very twitchy on the white surface of the IKEA table, and on the glass surface refused to work at all.
Software sensor adaptation eliminated all the shortcomings.



There are two adaptation modes - manual (essentially automatic) and selection of surface materials from the list.





In the case of manual adaptation, you need to hold down the left mouse button and the scroll wheel on the desired surface until the program displays a window notifying that the adaptation has been completed, otherwise you will need to repeat the procedure.

In conclusion of the software review, I‚Äôll say that the capabilities of this set of functions really allow you to fine-tune the operation of the Gladius optical sensor to suit your needs and capabilities in computer games.

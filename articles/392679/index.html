Well, we came directly to the intellect. Immediately I will say here we again level another romantic concept. As it turns out, there is nothing really special about the intellect from my point of view.

For now, let's use intuitive considerations:
Does man have intelligence? - Yes.
Does the dog have intelligence? - Yes.
The frog has intelligence? Apparently - yes.
Does a nematode (earthworm) have intelligence? We assume - yes.

Here is a hint of a weakening of the concept of intelligence. Blow off his romanticism. We give a definition:

If a living system uses a dedicated behavior management subsystem, then this subsystem will be called intelligence.

Let us explain: Bacteria does not possess intelligence - there is no dedicated control subsystem. But the earthworm has the intellect, it has a clearly defined nervous system. You will laugh again - the refrigerator has the intelligence, because He has a special regulator!

The intelligence of a living system fully operates on the main objective functional: to survive despite the second law of thermodynamics. As a rule, the intelligence subsystem is highly integrated into a set of preserving mechanisms. Talking about the autonomous existence of intelligence without a set of preserving mechanisms and setting target behavior does not make sense, otherwise it splits into a set of individual functions, perhaps even advanced ones.

Here we enter a region that has always eluded technical experts, but who always meant it, agreeing with the statement: the machine cannot create a masterpiece. Let's try to formalize this area.
If we turn to different kinds of definitions of the psyche (you can find and read for yourself), then we will again come up with verbose statements in which there is no most important thing, from which everything else follows. A living system with intelligence has a psyche if:

3) in the process of adapting and building a picture of the world around it generates and identifies a concept that corresponds to itself

It can be noted that a difficult task has crept into this definition: algorithms with adaptive properties. Namely, they can constitute the main difficulty in designing such a system. As is known in biological applications, the role of such algorithms is performed by information systems based on neural networks. Realizations of neural networks in themselves give little, and can be suitable, only for individual tasks. To reproduce the psyche, you need to use them in the appropriate architecture. Talking about the autonomous existence of the psyche, as well as intelligence without a set of preserving mechanisms and setting target behavior, does not make sense. The basis of the psyche consists in the target behavior of preserving mechanisms: the survival-preservation system.

Conclusion: thanks to the extremely constructive definitions of life and intelligence, it was possible to constructively determine the psyche.

V. Architecture

In this part we will try to consider, no less and no less, the architecture of a living system with intelligence.

As indicated above, the living system has a complex of mechanisms-reactions of the target behavior, which have retaining meaning.

The built-in mechanisms for target behavior are divided into hierarchy levels:
1) Unconditioned reflexes are characteristic of all systems with intelligence.
2) Rituals are characteristic of more developed organisms, rituals have abstracted objectives and suggest higher levels of the context of the environment and communication interaction. It is important that the rituals have goals that must be achieved and are 'sewn' into the package of target behavior for preservation - the survival of the system.
3) The adaptive subsystem, which, using its adaptive capabilities, optimizes the behavior of controlling the lower level circuits - basic reflexes, as well as rituals.



Reflexes and rituals are formed by genetic algorithms in the evolutionary process.

The basic reflexes include the recognizers of basic sensory patterns and the drivers (drives) of basic locomotion for actuators and the actual basic reactions to recognized patterns.

Rituals are a higher abstract level of recognition of patterns and the formation of behavior, rather than locomotion.

The adaptive subsystem accumulates data and adapts behavior during the life of the system. On the basis of an adaptive system, a psyche can form, and perhaps not. It depends on her strength and learning experience.

Goals


The main goal of search engines is to make money by providing the necessary information (or rather the site where this information is located) upon a user's search query. That is, if the user has typed "Buy an elephant", then the task of the search engine is to give him sites where you can buy an elephant. Search engine algorithm


This is the set of rules that the search engine is guided by when forming its search results. Simply put: "whom to put first and who last". If you know this algorithm - you are the God of optimization.
To make life more difficult for optimizers, the algorithm is constantly being improved.

How does the search engine learn about new sites?


Each search engine has a bulldog that runs and sniffs websites. And he runs on all the links on the Internet. And the more often new information appears on your site, the more often this bulldog runs to you.

Low Frequency (LF), Mid-Frequency (MF) and High Frequency (HF) Requests


When they make up the semantic core (the requests by which we will promote the site), the key phrases are divided into groups, taking into account the frequency and with the help of logical thinking given to us from above.

Low Frequency Requests (LF) - Requests that are rarely dialed. Consequently, it is easier (and most importantly, faster) to bring them into the TOP10 (first 10 positions of search results). They have the smallest competition.

Medium Frequency Queries (MF) - queries that refer to the "golden mean". They are recruited more often than LF, but less often than HF (see further).

High Frequency Requests (RF) - Requests that are typed most often. According to them, the strongest competition and the most difficult to bring them into the TOP10 (not to mention the battle for the TOP3).

For each field of activity, there are always numbers of frequencies, so no one will tell you the exact number for all areas.

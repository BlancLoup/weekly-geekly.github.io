Jelly beans


The software part is Android 4.1.2 Jelly Bean.

On top of Android is your Sharp luncheon - FeelUX.
His concept is simple - glossy icons, his own lock screen and three desktops.
Lock screen - sliding shutters up and down. To unlock, pull the tab with the lock down. Or pull it up a bit and choose an application for quick launch.


On the lock screen, you can also display weather, stocks.
The concept of three desktops is as follows: on the left there is a desktop with applications - all your installed applications are in a vertical list. You can shuffle them, separate the strip.

On the middle screen - widgets (you can place those that you need and in the order you need), the scroll is also vertical.

On the right screen - shortcuts of the applications you need.
I can not say how comfortable it is. But the concept of separating widgets from labels definitely makes sense.
If you pull the desktop down - then you will see the desktop settings menu. You can change the wallpaper, add a widget or shortcut.

An interesting feature is that the shell can be completely disabled (unfortunately, the lock screen will remain the same).
The keyboard is also its own. Not very comfortable.

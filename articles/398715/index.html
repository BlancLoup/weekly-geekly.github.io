Fiber Optic Dress


For fiber, you need a very strong base, so a dress with a tight corset and a wrap was chosen for it, so that the optical fiber lay ‚Äúmagnificently‚Äù on the skirt and freely rotate around it. For ease of experiment, or if you do not know how to sew, you can take a ready-made V-shaped dress with a tutu or a fluffy circular skirt. And if you want to sew a dress with your own hands, then step-by-step detailed instructions will help you.



To create a futuristic dress image, you need a fiber-optic whip. But he has his significant drawbacks in addition to the high cost. The battery life of the whip is very short, and the process of changing the battery is very complicated. The battery runs on 3.7 V lithium-ion batteries for about 45 minutes.



Therefore, the most difficult task was the ability to connect the whip with an additional battery pack, which would extend the term of fiber shining. Therefore, we place the battery pack under the skirt strap and connect it to the whip located on the back of the dress. In order for the dress to look neat, we sew a small piece of fabric with velcro, so that you can hide the base of the fiber-optic whip with the battery, but also to have quick access to it.



The fluffy form of a skirt in the form of a pack helps to create a ‚Äújellyfish effect‚Äù at the expense of fiber-optic fibers sliding along it. In addition, the tight skirt allows beautifully ‚Äúspringing‚Äù the fiber along the dress and is easier to attach to it. Then proceed to the most time-consuming part of the work - the creation of the contour of a dress of fiber.



In total, there are 360 ‚Äã‚Äãfibers of the optical fiber in the whip; we divide them into groups of 10 threads and we wind them with thread or rope. Then we distribute them along the dress, fixing it at the waist. After the groups of threads are fixed at the waist, the hardest work begins: the fastening of each group along the corset on the figure. The work is very slow, but the result is worth it. After fixing the threads you need to cut them to the desired length. Here everyone can create a completely different form. In this case, the principle of ‚Äúlayered‚Äù haircut was chosen, creating a fiber-optic skirt with threads of various lengths.

Done! This dress looks as impressive as Zack Posen's dress for Claire Danes, but it is more practical and diminutive. Just for the Halloween party.

LED shoe clips


An interesting idea for the costume was presented by Becky Stern, decorating her dress with shoes with LED clips. The use of these clips is multifunctional, and despite the fact that the author made these clips for shoes, they are suitable for children and adults. You can attach them not only to shoes, but also to clothes. The author assures that it will take no more than two hours to make them, and finding the parts for making them is not so difficult.



To do this, you will need a sewing kit LED (conductive thread, 4 LEDs, 2 CR2032 batteries), as well as some standard sewing accessories and a pair of clips. In addition, you need to choose the fabric to match the color of the suit. For each clip you need to cut 8 circles from the fabric. In order for the tissue circles not to creep away, the edges of the circles need to be set on fire a little. In the center of each circle you need to fix the LED, and then fold the circle into a wedge, folding it in half twice. Then all 8 ‚Äúwedges‚Äù need to be stitched together and connected to the batteries.

Step-by-step instructions can be viewed in the video, in which Becky describes in detail how to make clips.



LED tights


Following a similar principle, you can make tights with LEDs . To do this, you will need these consumables: leggings, conductive threads, 2 connectors for a button battery, 2 button batteries 3 V, 2 220 Ohm resistors, 12 LEDs, a metal fastener, a needle and cardboard on which to sew comfortably.



Draw an electrical circuit first on paper. Each leg is a separate circuit with six LEDs. LEDs are connected in parallel. The rest of the scheme is in the back of the leggings. Use tweezers to bend the ends of the resistors and LEDs. In order to make it easy to sew LEDs to leggings, stick a cardboard inside them and sew all the details according to the scheme.

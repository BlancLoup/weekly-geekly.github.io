The Ministry of Industry and Trade proposed to compensate the costs of operators for replacing imported equipment with domestic ones

The Ministry of Industry and Trade has prepared proposals for the support of Russian telecom operators purchasing domestic equipment to support the development of mobile Internet. The proposals provide for the provision of subsidies to operators to compensate for the costs of purchasing domestic equipment and for disposing of previously installed foreign equipment, as well as subsidies for training personnel.

It is proposed to provide operators with credit lines, tax breaks and ‚Äútax holidays‚Äù in the construction of networks based on Russian telecommunications equipment. It is planned to reduce VAT on the purchase of domestic equipment and to provide operators with preferential access to the necessary frequency ranges.

The Ministry of Industry and Trade also plans to revise the scheme for replenishing the universal service reserve, to which operators annually transfer 1.2% of revenues from communication services. The amount required for the purchase of domestic equipment and software (software) can be deducted from the amount of contributions to the reserve. However, this amount should not exceed 25% of its size.

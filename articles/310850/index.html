First supercomputer



CDC 6600. The price of the base model is $ 6,891,300 (at the current rate of 40-50 million dollars)

His system was cooled by freon, which was fed through pipes. The CDC 6600 had 10 peripheral processors, each of which was dedicated to managing I / O and storing the entire data queue.


The module of the ferromagnetic memory CDC 6600 computer (1961), size: 10.8cm x 10.8cm (6.5 inch), capacity: 1024 bits.

The main processor had a 60-bit register and a very small set of instructions, because it dealt only with information that was previously processed by peripheral processors.

In 1966, the United States refused to allow CDC to export the CDC 6600 computer license for the French Atomic Energy Agency to prevent France from its atomic program.

Interface: analog versus digit

Judging by the comments on the topic of the organization of the DCU MOESK, many have doubts whether it is possible to fully replace conventional devices with their virtual counterparts. I will try to consider this question.
The form of submission of information must comply with the tasks of reading indications, as well as eliminate the need for complex quantitative and logical transformations.

Indicating devices


Here is an example of an analog device:

What are its advantages:
It is very convenient to follow the dynamics of the parameter change. And in this form it is very convenient to monitor whether the parameter has gone beyond the allowed zone:

The disadvantage is that it is inconvenient to take accurate readings. To do this, the device on the first image has digital indicators.
Everything is fine, while there are few such devices, but try to monitor simultaneously a dozen readings?
I will give an example of how this problem is solved:

You can see the temperature reading, and with the help of horizontal bars you can see if the parameter is outside the allowed limits. it is also possible to judge the degree of heating at different levels.
what happens when a parameter reaches critical values:
  • The color of the bar changes;
  • An alarm occurs (in the figure, the operator is informed that the pump H343 has stopped);
  • One of the buttons lights up in red, when clicked, the operator will get to another mnemonic scheme, where he can take actions to prevent an accident.

Now compare yourself what is more convenient: ten instruments with scales, or one such scheme.

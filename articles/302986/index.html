Little about logistics

First of all, like any logistics operator, the basis of our business is warehouse logistics.
Therefore, the first thing we did was get confused by the device and the automation of the warehouse. Our warehouse is located in the city of Vidnoe and its location has 2 main advantages:

  1. It is close to Moscow, which reduces the cost of transportation for Suppliers.
  2. Rental prices in the city of Vidnoe are much lower, therefore, having a large warehouse complex, we have no problems with its payback and, as a result, we can maintain the lowest prices on the market objectively. For example, the whole complex of logistics, including the acceptance of goods, storage, packaging and packaging, costs our customers an average of 78 rubles per shipment (unless of course they are engaged in the sale of Indian elephants in bulk).



All this allows our customers to save a lot. Take for example the most common type of online store with an average of 100 orders per month and for example it sells women's dresses, the cost of our services for such a client will be 7,800 rubles per month. To understand what the benefits are, just count the cost of renting the smallest warehouse in the Moscow region and add to this the salary of one Dzhamshut and do not forget about the depreciation of warehouse equipment.

By the way, we don‚Äôt really strongly dump our warehouse complex that processes 100 units of goods per minute, so the operational processes are much cheaper for us.

100 pieces of goods 0_o How do we not confuse all this?

Well, first of all global bar coding. We glue the barcode literally to everything that comes to our warehouse, (sometimes we get carried away and we blot it on customers, we pack them and send them to Vladivostok with Russian Post). But seriously, over the course of our work we have developed a sophisticated wms which controls everything that can be controlled.

After the goods arrive at the warehouse, all its parameters are entered into the system: weight, volume, configuration and packaging properties, various notes from the client. The system then selects the correct cell for each product. The correct cell is the right-sized cell located so that the picker would need to make as little movement as possible to complete your order.

When an order arrives to us for processing, (by the way, this is done fully automatically when wms is integrated with your store), the system builds a route picker on which he will move collecting the order.
When picking orders for large volumes of shipments, the human factor is extremely dangerous, so we made it so that the picker could not take any goods from the shelf until he scanned his bar code and if he suddenly made a mistake, the system simply will not let him put the goods on the cart.



The second time, the complete set of all goods is checked in the packing area, when the packer takes the order for processing, he also has to scan the barcode of each item, so he checks the correct set and receives instructions on packaging. After he collects the goods in the box, he again glues the appropriate barcode on the packaged goods, which contains all the information about the delivery service, the client‚Äôs address and the amount to be paid. After that, the order goes to the dispatch area, where it is checked once again for the correct packaging and only after that it is sent to the delivery service.

And we have a terribly harsh personnel policy.
The time of all processes is clearly calculated, and if one of the employees begins to blunt, then we simply dismiss this employee. Our storekeepers are walking bio-robots who are afraid to run to the toilet without the permission of our large automated brother.

Sat.tv: TV program with an individual approach for free satellite TV channels HOT BIRD + interview with the developer

A television program in the form of a newspaper or a magazine today is bought, perhaps, only by the most devoted viewers of paper. Those who do not want to spend money and accumulate waste paper, prefer to learn the program through the Internet. The most advanced download special mobile applications. One of them is Sat.tv, created for users of the satellite channels of the HOT BIRD package. The release of the application took place at the end of April 2016. Its main features - a personalized approach to each viewer, as well as very detailed information provided on the channels and broadcasts.



Let's take a closer look at how Sat.tv works, estimate how comfortable it is to use, and determine how it differs from its peers. Well, as a bonus - right after the review - you will find an interview with Nicolas Mulin-Fornier, head of the Eutelsat Connected TV Program division. HOT BIRD satellites belong to the latter. The company broadcasts 1,100 TV channels, more than 300 of them are free. More than 66 million families around the world use the services of the satellite satellite HOT BIRD. And the Sat.tv application itself reviews just those more than 300 free TV channels in 34 languages. It is very easy to get lost in such a variety. Therefore, in the subdivision of the Connected TV Program, they also attended to the creation of the Sat.tv guide. It was developed by Wiztivi by order of Eutelsat, which provided the concept of the application and its wishes for its development.

Sat.tv is available for free download on the App Store and Google Play . In this review, we will look at how an application works on iOS. If you want, you can even get a link to download via SMS - to do this, go to the official website of the application www.watchsat.tv .

And now, as promised at the beginning, an interview with Nicolas Moulin-Fornier, head of the Eutelsat Connected TV Program division. We asked Mr. Moulin-Fornier about future plans, integration of Sat.tv with domestic social networks, application launch on other platforms, and much more.


1. The Sat.tv application is a guide to more than 300 free TV channels from the Hot Bird package, while it includes about 440 free TV channels. Are there any plans to integrate data on all 440 TV channels into the application? By what principle are 300 channels selected in it?

The goal of the Sat.tv application is to support all open-access TV channels on the HOT BIRD satellite, as long as they provide comprehensive information about their programs.

The application is ready to support several hundred TV channels. And we are pleased to note that already now in Sat.tv there is information about the broadcasts of 7 Russian-language channels: Channel 1, TNT, CTC, Channel 8, Euronews, Russian Musicbox, Soyuz.

JetPoint. Big data

Every day, humanity generates huge amounts of information in the form of message flows in social networks, observations of meteorologists, astronomers, geologists, as well as just articles and news stories. The data obtained need not only skillful storage, but also in the correct analysis. We have to look for new ways of presenting data, sometimes quite different in structure.



New directions for the development of processing both structured and unstructured data generate new technologies. Recently, we more often meet solutions based on Hadoop or NoSQL databases. There is a rapid growth of projects engaged in the study of such information. This phenomenon, which includes methods and technologies, since 2008 has received the name Big Data. Let's try to figure out: what is really behind this concept?

On February 19, a JetPoint report on Big Data will be held at JetBrains St. Petersburg office on Vasilyevsky Island.

The leading developer of EMC, Vladimir Suvorov, will talk about what the Big Data concept includes, about the development of technologies for processing large amounts of data and about the global practice of their application. During the report, an overview of key technologies will be made and typical cases of using Big Data will be given.

Sub-topics of the report:
  • what is big data and why such a direction has arisen;
  • who is Data Scientist;
  • examples of Big Data tasks by industry (world);
  • typical examples of Big Data;
  • key technologies (Hadoop stack);
  • Pivotal stack;
  • development trends. On-line Big data analytics, streaming processing;
  • Big Data in Russia.


Date and time : February 19, 2014, 20-00
Location : St. Petersburg office development JetBrains, University Embankment. 7-9-11 / 5A, BC "Universe" ( on the map ).

The presentation is designed for experienced developers who want to expand their knowledge in Data Science. Participation in JetPoint is free, you must register on the event page . Limited number of seats!

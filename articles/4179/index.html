bLaugh: Internet comics

In June 2006, the bLaugh project opened on the Internet, which publishes daily updated comics and humorous stories about Internet technologies. The authors of the project are artist Brad Fitzpatrick and journalist Chris Pirillo ; the first draws satirical comics, and the second writes texts. Chris Pirillo is also known as the organizer of the popular Gnomedex conference.

The objects of their ridicule are famous bloggers and blogger sites. For example, the authors recently had a good laugh at the Digg site, which literally fixated on itself. First, thousands of voluntary editors, readers brought to the first page the news about their site, and then - brought to the first page the news that Digg publishes news about itself on the first page. This second-degree obsession turned out to be a great topic for a caricature and an idea for the DiggDork.com domain (why do we need domain ideas - more on that later).

Other objects of ridicule are large corporations that are trying to take their place in the blogosphere. The theme of today's comics was Microsoft , which announced at a press conference about combining RSS and Atom into a single, new format (domain SyndicationWars.com). By the way, this is quite a believable joke.

It must be said that bLaugh is a unique website of its kind. Humorous reviews of events in the blogosphere, and even with comics - this has not happened yet.

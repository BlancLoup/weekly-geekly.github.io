It took a large-scale and long-term study to assess with a sufficient degree of persuasiveness how foreign blood affects the survival of the recipient, depending on the type of blood. An international team led by researchers from McMaster University (Canada) has 31,497 patients in six hospitals in four countries - and showed that transfusion of more fresh blood does not increase the survival rate of patients, compared with blood from the repository.

If the first myth about the advantage of fresh blood has been debunked, what about the second? Is the blood of young people really able to rejuvenate and restore vitality to the old?

Scientific experiments confirm that the infusion of fresh blood really promotes the rejuvenation of muscle tissue and internal organs. For example, in 2010, at Harvard, experiments were conducted on mice: old individuals were given blood to young ones. It turned out that in old mice, after that, hematopoietic hematopoietic stem cells began to behave like young ones: they began to function better in proportion to differentiate into other types of blood cells. The rejuvenating effect of the blood of a young donor had an effect on the stem cells of muscle tissue. Scientific work in Nature published October 14, 2010.

Another experiment was conducted at Stanford University under the direction of Thomas Rando in 2005. There, in an elderly mouse, which transferred blood to a younger one, liver tissue was regenerated and the elasticity of muscles returned.

Similar rejuvenating effects of young blood transfusions were found in mice as a result of several subsequent experiments. The Harvard group has identified a blood plasma protein that can have an important influence on the observed rejuvenation effects. This is GDF11 (growth differentiation factor 11, growth factor and differentiation factor-11), the level of which decreases with age. Several studies have confirmed its rejuvenating effect on the body, and in several others it has not been confirmed. The study of this issue continues. There is a high probability that GDF11 is not the only anti-aging factor.

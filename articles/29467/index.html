Intellectual property

On trends, dissent, opinions, information and intellectual property rights.

Opinion can not be yours. Opinion appears when information is received. Information cannot be absolute exclusionary axiom. Information is based on opinion. In this case, the opinion can not be also an axiom, because information and opinions are based on each other. Any information that considers it to be an axiom should be taken only as a personal opinion. A personal opinion can not be an axiom. Consequently, there can not be any action justified in terms of exceptional accuracy and inviolability. Any information is based only on generally accepted public logic.
From birth, an individual receives an opinion on what kind of information. But with the receipt of information, the opinion is changing. Since eternal times, man was born with his opinion. Suppose that he does not like a tomato. And if he is told that the tomato is very tasty, then soon the person himself believes in it. But on the other hand, he himself can spread this opinion and will tell everyone that tomatoes are not terribly tasty. And that person will perceive the tomato as not tasty.
That is, without receiving information about something, we can form our personal opinion on the basis of some subject matter and it will be our opinion.
But having received information from someone, we will commit acts, think, create on the basis of someone else's information and this opinion will no longer be ours.

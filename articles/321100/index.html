Management


We are looking for people who have succeeded:

1. To hire interns (to agitate, to include in the production process, to hire). In a situation of acute shortage of experienced personnel - you have to go to universities and look for staff "for growth" there. If you have succeeded in this, we will be glad to hear about your experience.

2. Manage a distributed development team. The labor market of programmers is smoothly migrating to the remote last 5 years, probably, although not as actively as expected. In remote projects, a lot of things do not work in the same way as in the office, it is necessary to organize the process more clearly, and to force communication, since they do not go through themselves in the smoking rooms and at dinner and much more. If you have a successful experience in managing a distributed development team, come speak.

3. Interesting stories about the life cycle of your product: hypothesis testing, pivots, experiments, the evolution of ideas and unexpected conclusions. If you manage an IT project, there are probably many such stories in your arsenal, the conclusions of which can be useful for the IT community.

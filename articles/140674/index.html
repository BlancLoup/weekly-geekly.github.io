Fridge for do-it-yourself notes

While still not got newfangled refrigerators, specially designed for drawing on them, I want to share a simple way to make such a refrigerator with your own hands. Well, almost completely your own.

So, we will need:


- Self-adhesive film (we have white color without texture. It will be difficult to erase drawings from a textured film). The number and width of the film depends on the width of the refrigerator. We have our film 45cm wide, so they decided not to completely seal the refrigerator.



- Scissors
- Scotch (normal one-sided)
- Thick thread or lace
- Stationery knife
- A set of markers for boards
- Straight arms :)
- Refrigerator (cap)


The process is:
- We clean the surface of the refrigerator from grease, magnets and domestic animals
- Choose a place on which we will glue the film.
- Carefully read the instructions on the back of the film



- Remove the back side of the film by 5-10 cm across the entire width and glue the film to the top edge. Sticking smoothly without bubbles, bubbles will be useful after completion.
- Gradually peel off the back side and smooth the film on the fridge.

It should turn out somehow



- Use a stationery knife to carefully cut out the contour of logos and other items of pride of refrigerator manufacturers.



- Now the magnets can return to their usual habitat. Pets are still kept in the fridge locked up.



- Prepare markers for fixation on the fridge (if you don‚Äôt want them to be found after a week and they are always on hand when they dab something). We wrap a marker with a thread, make 2-3 knots, wrap it with thread 3-4 times and tape it over.



You can search for a marker with a magnet or stick it yourself.



- we wind the thread so that it can be written with a marker. Fasten the thread to any protruding place on the refrigerator (we used a door hinge).



- In order not to be bored, we take markers of different colors.



- We invite the whole family with their pets to have fun on the new board. We draw comics, reminders, tables ...



Project cost:
- Markers - $ 4
- 1m film - $ 2
- Good mood and awl in the pope - priceless

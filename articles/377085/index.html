Hammerhead is an interactive direction indicator.



Bicycle navigators invented and created a mass of various types and various functionalities. It would seem that here you can still invent? It turns out that there is still room for innovative projects in this area - just look at the process not in the general perspective of the complication of devices, but in the exact opposite.

The well-known phrase about all ingenious is simple in the case of the Hammerhead navigator can be supplemented with other epithets. For example, convenient and more secure. Yes, of course, business does not do without a classic interface here either. But here it remains on the screen of your smartphone, which is in your pocket.

And the direction of movement is indicated by dynamic LEDs embedded in a steering wheel-mounted pointer, shaped like a hammer - hence the name Hammerhead. The device is synchronized via Bluetooth with the application, and now the commands to turn or turn around are visualized by the clear movement of the LED lights.

The gadget is not afraid of rain or other precipitations, it works on one charge up to 10 hours, it is simply mounted on the steering wheel and in addition is equipped with daylight lights, like all vehicles on the road.

Hammerhead is already available for pre-order for $ 85.

The device is mounted on the rear axle, there is enough space, even if you use wings. The battery is removable, so you can recharge as on the go - USB-connector is mounted under the seat, and in any other place:

Vector - a new tracker from Garmin

Cycling is not easy. And those who deal with them professionally, and those who deal with them for themselves, know that the correct distribution of forces, the maximum efforts where it is needed, and the rational alternation of load are essential conditions for high results. But do it on a whim, based on your own feelings and physical condition? Previously, and trained. But not now, when there is an effective tracker to track the many functions associated with the work of the feet of a cyclist - Vector from Garmin.



The touch module is attached to both pedals of your bike - suitable for almost all models. The installation itself does not cause issues, demos can be viewed on the site .

So, what does the tracker measure? And, in general, all:
  • force of pressure on the pedals
  • the balance of effort between the pedals
  • applied efforts in different time intervals,
  • total energy expended in watts,
  • total energy expended in kJ,
  • the smoothness of the pedals,
  • load density
  • and plus some special options.
  • Naturally, all data is synchronized in real time to Garmin's onboard gadgets, and it is possible to analyze the results of training directly in its process, changing the load and intensity.


You can buy the tracker now at a price of $ 1500 , deliveries will begin in 5-8 weeks.

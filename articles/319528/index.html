A person. Commander Norton



Peter Norton is known to most users of personal computers. True, not everyone thinks about it, not everyone knows how great his merits are. Peter has been working for years on products like Norton Commander, Norton Utilities, Norton Disk Doctor. He is also the author of such popular books as Inside IBM PC, Inside OS / 2, and Programmer‚Äôs Reference.

Norton created a new direction of software development. He was an innovator both in terms of the market and technically. However, Peter Norton did not work for money and fame. At least he himself believes in it.

Peter Norton was born in 1943. He got acquainted with computers in his early youth, when he worked in an insurance company as a seasonal employee. His first computer was the IBM PC. He bought this car as soon as it went on sale. Communication with computers became a serious hobby for Peter: he learned the basics of working with him as a user, and then began to study programming independently.

Norton studied at Ridon College (Portland, Oregon). He then entered the University of California at Berkeley, who graduated with a bachelor's degree in mathematics.

After university, Peter Norton got a job at an aerospace company that was based in Southern California. However, in the late 1970s, the industry began to lose ground in the market, and many specialists lost their jobs. Boeing, McDonnell Douglas and Lockheed made massive cuts.



The resulting free time Norton decided to spend on self-development: he spent five years in a Buddhist monastery on the coast of San Francisco.

Then there were attempts to work on freelance terms. However, he was not enthusiastic about the tasks that he had to solve: they seemed to him boring, and the abundance of orders led to overwork.

Let's try to return


Once Peter deleted a file with important information from the hard disk. When he realized that it was an annoying accident, and the file, it would seem, could not be returned, he got the idea to search for a way out of the current situation. In 1982, Peter managed to write his program, which analyzed hidden areas of memory and made it possible to find lost data. He called it "Unerase". This program became the prototype of modern utility programs.

Norton and his team

Project Arena - webinar for creators of serious VR projects



Webinars are no longer surprising anyone, and there are plenty of places to learn from on the Internet. However, even among such abundance there are events that you want to talk about separately - and this is the announcement of one of them. We are pleased to announce that a webinar on Project Arena , a large-scale demonstration VR project for developers , will take place on May 16 .

The real sports facility from the real world, namely, the phillips Arena in Atlanta, Georgia, without a bag, was transferred to digital reality using AutoCAD modeling tools. The physical model was supplemented by a crowd simulation to create dynamic situations. Efforts were spent a lot - both human and machine; hardware load fell, as usual, on Intel processors. However, his works were worth it, because now everyone can learn by example:

  • how to create a large-scale VR project from an idea to a working product,
  • what hardware and software components are needed for this,
  • what underwater rake scattered along the way.

All this - the topic of the future webinar. Please note again: it will take place on May 16 at 9:00 PST (20:00 MSK). Registration is done here . Speakers at the event will be Rudy Cazabon, consulting engineer at Intel and Rod Recker, a renowned computer modeling expert.

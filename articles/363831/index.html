Psh-Pshsh. Review of the portable radio station Midland GXT-1050

You can ask - why do we now need radios when the cost of the minimum dialing for communication (dialer + SIM card) is approximately equal to the average check in a cafe? The question is fair. On the one hand, the phone really does already have even every child. On the other hand, despite the fact that now is 2015, there are still places on the territory of our homeland where the quality of cellular communication is poor (or want to have it in principle). And though the walkie-talkies do not solve this problem globally, it is obviously too early to bury them - even if we do not take into account the need for walkie-talkies for special services, to simplify communication at large enterprises, and so on.



Psh-Pshsh.

You begin to understand the delights of a walkie-talkie when you use it for some time. First, for communication in nature or even in the city does not require any infrastructure. No need to call the other person, spend money - just press the button for free and everything, almost always in touch. Secondly, the radio is relatively (with smartphones) ridiculous money, and the connection for it costs a penny - if not to take electricity to charge the batteries, but will it consume a lot? Thirdly, there are a lot of cases in life when communication in this way makes life easier many times. Hunting, fishing, mushroom camping, barbecues, cycling, paintball, orienteering, all kinds of Encounters and other modern games with augmented reality. In general, given the price and opportunities offered by a portable radio station, it is better to have and rarely use than not to have at all and not even know that many things could be done differently, simpler, faster and better, having such a method of communication.

Therefore, a warning: after reading the post, you may want to get a set of radios, even if you have never thought about them before. At least I now have this fixed idea by February 23.


So, Midland GXT-1050 is the flagship set of two radios of this manufacturer, designed to work in the LPD-band.

Opportunities


- Classic PTT (Push-To-Talk) - clicked and said, the other party is listening at this time. During the message transmission on the screen, the TX indicator is lit, during reception - RX (+ short display illumination and vibrating alert, if configured).

- VOX mode (Handsfree) - provides automatic transmission of sound when the microphone of the radio will catch your speech. It turns a walkie-talkie into an analogue of TeamSpeak: as soon as you say something loudly - the walkie-talkie goes into TX-mode and sends your words to the other party. There are nine levels of microphone sensitivity, but one nuance should be taken into account: the first word was eaten by a cow. - Whisper mode puts the radio microphone into high sensitivity mode, so you can speak very quietly or almost in a whisper, and your interlocutor will hear you loudly and clearly. In some cases, this is simply an indispensable feature!

Well, the last one, not so much an independent mode, as an opportunity to improve any of the three available - the mode of operation through the complete headset, which is especially relevant for the Whisper mode (when the conditions of using the radio do not allow using it in full voice). In life


I tested the kit in various conditions, of course, the 20 kilometers stated by the manufacturer work only in two-dimensional conditions on paper. In a clean (well, almost clean) field, the maximum signal reception / transmission distance varies from 7 to 13 kilometers. The quality of reception at a great distance is strongly influenced by weather conditions and light from Venus, reflected from the upper layers of the atmosphere.

In the forest or park, the reception area is limited to 5-7 kilometers. In the city, due to the large number of buildings consisting mainly of not very radio transparent materials, you can expect to work within a kilometer, if you are lucky, two. But I believe that even this result will be enough for most tasks that are solved with the help of walkie-talkies (especially in the city).

Pressing the phone to your ear in such conditions is not convenient, using Zello type virtual web radios can be costly both financially and from the point of view of the battery of your device, and you can not always poke a touchscreen smartphone with dirty hands or gloves. Walkie-talkie will be nothing, then washed with warm water and soap, and dried. By the way, a radio kit perfectly helps with construction / repair work: it‚Äôs not necessary to shout from room to room or from home to the street, it saves both nerves and time.

But the radio is practically not suitable for joint car trips: reception and transmission are much worse at speeds above 40 km / h, and it is simply impossible to talk at speeds of 70. Well, if you stop to talk - so nothing hinders and lean out of the window on the sidelines. In general, the work at high speed can not count, the signal "blows away." On cycling and quadrocycle speeds everything is OK and the radios will work in such conditions.

Interview with the popularizer of science, the head of the only private school of astronomy in Russia, Pavel Skripnichenko

About school and teaching

- From the outside, it seems that astronomy is not very popular in Russia, and it is difficult for a private school of astronomy to develop. This is true? In your opinion, what hinders the development? Is there any support from the state?

As it turned out, the number of people searching in the Astronomy School search engines is extremely small. But in order to survive: pay salaries to employees, pay for the office, create content, invent equipment, it was necessary to create a sustainable business model, if possible, with minimization of seasonality. And since we were initially positioned as a social business, we did not maximize the profit, but the social effect. Thus, several projects were born that are now active.

Project 1. We come to school, gymnasium, lyceum, to the office and spend the Day of Science. With lectures, laboratory work (mainly in chemistry and physics), with a planetarium dome, with interactive games, with "round tables", etc. 200-400 children easily pass during the day, and during several lessons they receive a powerful dose of relevant, useful and interesting knowledge.

On the popularization of science


- How and why did you start promoting science?

I have already partially spoken about popularization, but I would like to reinforce this thought with my own, absolutely self-serving and vile need - for security. The thing is that the system of my beliefs, based on materialism, secular humanism and other isms, as I feel, is rapidly losing popularity in our society. I will not philosophize whether it is good or bad, but everyone must defend his convictions. As a representative of science, as a person who believes in scientific and technological progress, and who believes that without a quality education, we will return to the darkness of the Middle Ages, I am engaged in popularization. This is not only from the desire to make the world better, it is also from the desire that there is a place left for me and those like me.

- Is it difficult to be a popularizer of science in Russia? What problems do you face?

Popularizer in Russia is a wonder. There is no such profession. It is not there, scientific centers and universities do not pay for it, because they do not even recognize its existence. Even many fellow scientists do not consider the promotion of a separate activity. And this is a separate activity.

‚ÄúToday you have 6 lectures and 2 master classes,‚Äù the director of the school of astronomy tells me. At the first - elementary school, at the second - the average, at the third - the oldest, at the fourth - mixed up all those who did not get to the first or third, at the fifth you act as part of our concept of charity to blind children, at the sixth - before the meeting of veterans labor in the local library. Themes are different everywhere - from the construction of Stonehenge to methods for detecting gravitational waves. Everyone should be clear. Everyone should be interested.

It is not enough to be able to speak in public and to brilliantly know your material. Problems of a purely psychological nature - you do not sleep, speak, work on the material, translate the newly published articles. About astronomy


- What are astronomers all over the world doing today? What is the most urgent task?

Probably a representative of each branch of astronomy will highlight the most pressing issues in their specifics. Therefore, without pretending to objectivity, I would choose what is most interesting to me.

Theory of everything . A concept that allows all kinds of fundamental interactions to be described in a single way. We have found gravitational waves, but the particle responsible for the gravitational interaction is not yet available. I would like to know if the standard model is correct and to be in the era of gravitonics.

Dark energy . It appeared to explain the discrepancy between the theoretically calculated and actually observed velocities of the motion of astronomical objects in the galaxy. What is it like? There is no intelligible explanation. So much so that even there are very controversial articles stating that there is no dark energy, but the law of conservation of momentum does not work.

Life not related to earthly origin . Extremely important thought for the destruction of their own identity. We discovered other solar systems, including exoplanets similar to Earth, found water almost everywhere (on Mars, in the asteroid ground, on the Moon), found organic matter near hot stars. It remains only to discover life. If we find, we will cease to be elected, and become one of the many. It seems to me that it is necessary for the majority to realize this - we are not the pinnacle of biological evolution and everything is possible for us, but only one of the options. Be modest. Be proud of achievements, but not an origin.

Refinement of the parameters of the quasi-inertial coordinate system - let's call it that. The point is that all the coordinate systems used by mankind are tied to the grid of quasars - distant objects, whose own movements in the sky are insignificant. I, as a person who passed astrometry, would like very much to know whether the current quasi-inertial system will be reformed taking into account new observations, and when the time counting system based on pulsar observations will appear.

- What recent discovery in astronomy impressed you most?

Gravitational waves, of course. I already jumped for joy - this is a miracle when a theory that has been developed for more than 100 years is confirmed by new observation technologies. And the asteroid Oumuamua - migrating from one star system to another. This is a very weighty confirmation of the transfer of matter on the scale of the Milky Way.

- You are actively involved in issues of asteroid-comet hazard. Please tell us more about it? What exactly are you doing? What research do you do?

My task, both within the framework of the thesis and subsequently, is the use of numerical methods for studying the long-term orbital evolution of asteroids. Speaking directly, my task is to calculate how the elements of the orbit (count the coordinates) of the asteroids will change at intervals of hundreds and thousands of years.

The fact is that any mathematical calculation is a model problem, i.e. the accuracy of the calculations will depend not only on rounding errors and other purely algorithmic influences, but also on what set of forces acting on the object in reality, I take into account in the equations.

Another problem is that the study of the motion of asteroids is based on their observation, and even the most precise observations contain errors. When I determine the errors of observations, I take model calculations as a basis, and when I check model calculations, should I take observations? It turns out that bad observations can get along well with bad calculations, and my calculations will have nothing to do with the real movement of the asteroid.

My task is intermediate, between the discoveries and observations of potentially dangerous asteroids and the counteraction to an object that can actually threaten the Earth with a collision. On how well developed the mechanisms of calculations and forecasts will depend on our possible response to the threat of a collision of the Earth with an asteroid. In addition, such studies are important from a fundamental point of view, not only within the framework of the asteroid-comet hazard.



- I know that you saw with your own eyes the fall of the Chelyabinsk meteorite. What did you feel at that moment? What did it look like?

It looked like the second Sun, which appeared from behind a cloud where it should not be at all. The flash caught my attention, the brain almost independently estimated the brightness, the height above the horizon, the angle of inclination relative to the horizon and the azimuth of the direction. Seeing fragmentation, I realized that I was observing the passage of an object through the dense layers of the atmosphere. And since I did not know the size of the object, I could not estimate the energy of the shock wave either. I had to stand at the traffic lights - waiting for the shock wave. When she did not come, went to work.

Was it scary? Well, scary. In these dates, object 2012 DA14 Duende, a stone with a mass of 40,000 tons, approached the Earth. It should not have fallen, the probability was very small, but suddenly? As a result, the object fell almost two times smaller, which was unknown to science before entering the atmosphere. The day immediately changed the working mode: communicated with the media, with eyewitnesses, with different structures.

- Did the scientists know that he would fall? Can this story repeat?

No, they did not. Yes maybe. From the point of view of the asteroid-comet hazard, an object like Chelyabinsky is a small and modest piece of substance 4.5 billion years old. From the point of view of global catastrophes, this object does not deserve any attention at all, we considered. Indeed, within the framework of the AOC, it is believed that a global catastrophe is the fall of an object from 1 kilometer or more, and a regional one - from 100 meters to 1 kilometer.

Obviously, these numbers are conditional, and are associated not only with the need to introduce a scale of danger, but also with purely political motives ‚Äî we can observe large objects strongly in advance and warn them in time.

Chelyabinsky was not lucky: it could be fixed if he flew up from the night side, such precedents happened more than once, but he decided to fly from the morning side, from the side lit by the sun sky. Of course, given the small size, its brightness against a bright sky background was unremarkable, so no one found it.

New tools for organizing effective teamwork

Resources offering fresh solutions in the field of development are widely represented on the Internet, and you can easily find websites that give practical advice and recommendations to both beginners and more experienced developers. But it is quite another thing to find materials that are useful for you as a team player, that is, helping to build work directly within the team. We picked up several new resources for organizing productive teamwork.


Ryan Heybourn, creator of the Scrum platform, says that he borrowed the term Scrum from rugby - this is an element of the game, the phase in which players wrap their arms around each other, lining up in several rows. According to Ryan, this term reflects the concept of the product he created - a platform for developers where they could launch projects and work together on them. Thus, Scrum has become a community in which developers can share with each other their experiences, whether it is third-party projects of the "weekend" or updating existing ones. Starting a new project is easy - just click the button at the top of the page and set a time frame; follow the development of the project is also very convenient - for this you just need to unsubscribe about the progress on Twitter, marking posts with the hashtag #scrum. The site provides round-the-clock support for participants, with which you can ask all your questions directly to the creator of the resource.


The next useful resource for the teamwork of developers is Increment , an online publication in which various aspects of the development process are examined using real case studies. Each of the issues is devoted to a detailed, polyphonic discussion of a separate topic, in which authoritative figures from the relevant field take part. The first issue, published this spring, looks promising: the general theme of on-call services, analytics from the editorial board and interviews with experts, opinions from representatives of companies such as Dropbox, Pinterest, Google, GitHub.


Paste is a visual solution for teams using Slack. This popular corporate messenger can be improved with a convenient solution from FiftyThree for teams who need to monitor the progress of their projects not only in the form of text and numerical data, but also visually. Organizing work in the form of visual grids allows participants within a team to interact with each other in real time, simply by dragging and dropping items inside the grid.

The Paste tool is optimal for organizing and analyzing teamwork, as well as to share the results of the promotion with your colleagues in the Slack service. You can use the free trial version of the product for 60 days, after this period the service will cost $ 30 per month for a team of five people.


CodeTracer is a new code verification platform. With the help of CoderTracer, you can work on checking and improving code with colleagues. This is a community of developers, created not only for checking your own code, but also for self-education by studying and analyzing the code of more experienced users. Thus, the resource is designed for developers with different levels of knowledge and skills. CodeTracer provides close interaction within the community and, most likely, not one of your pressing questions will remain unanswered. After registering for free on the site, you will be able to receive comments on each line of your code, as well as participate in discussions and help others.

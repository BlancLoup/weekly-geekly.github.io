Robotic car for the first time bypassed human racer



Computer control systems of the car develop, improve. Every month such systems become more and more functional. And now the system, developed by a group of engineers at Stanford, has emerged victorious in competition with man. Audi TTS cars participated in the races - one with an automatic control system, the other - under the control of a professional racer. Maximum speed - 200 kilometers per hour.

As a result, the robotic car came out the winner , however, the advantage was very small - only 0.4 seconds. In order to train their system in the skills of professional racers, the researchers did a lot of work with professional drivers.

The result is a computer system that can do everything that professional drivers are capable of. In the process of researching the brain activity of drivers while driving, scientists were surprised by the fact that on more difficult sections of the road, professional drivers practically did not think, most of the maneuvers were conducted on reflexes and ‚Äúmuscle memory‚Äù. And some elements of the study were successfully used in teaching autonomous control systems. In particular, the problem of sliding while cutting corners at high speed was solved. Most drivers reacted to the problem before it appeared, as a result, the car fits perfectly into the turn.

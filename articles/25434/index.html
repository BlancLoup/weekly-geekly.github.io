I have extremely mixed feelings. I have 15 years of experience in the IT industry. I was engaged in almost everything - from assembling computers to developing information systems of an enterprise scale. Dozens of devices filled with the most diverse software passed through my hands. And yet, I just screwed up shamefully.

My car broke down the display. The usual such segment, with the clock, temperature and other information. He died quietly, not turning on at the next turn of the ignition key. For two weeks I waited for the official service center to deign to accept me. For two hours I was sitting in this center, waiting for the lift to become free ...

Repair took less than a minute. Dudes simply removed the battery terminal and put it back on, thus hard-resetting the computer. And it all worked.

It is difficult to describe the emotions surging on me. But all the way home, an anecdote about a programmer and a stalled car was spinning around in his head: "let's all go out and then go back."

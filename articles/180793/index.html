Electric motors are powered by batteries (57), which provide the robot with a range of 80 km. A version of the robot equipped with a diesel generator (58.59) is also provided, which increases the power reserve, but makes silent movement impossible.

The robot is controlled by a secure radio channel. In the case of radio signal jamming, automatic activation of the programs for searching and destroying the source of interference or returning to the base is provided.

To control the robot you need two people: one is responsible for the movement of the robot, the second - for the firing. Information on the control panel is transmitted from HD video cameras located around the perimeter of the body and on the rotating tower of the robot. The cameras are equipped with a mechanism to quickly replace the glass in case it is damaged by a bullet or a fragment, without interrupting the broadcast.

In addition, around the robot tower there are several rings of narrowly focused microphones that not only transmit sound to the operator‚Äôs console, but also know how to determine the direction in which the source of a loud sound (for example, a shot or explosion) is located and give the command to turn the tower in that direction. Separately, it is worth mentioning spherical grenades, which are activated by high air pressure in the barrel, which occurs at the moment of the shot, and until this moment is absolutely safe. Grenades can be fragmentation, light and smoke.

From bullets and small and medium fragments, the Scorpion is protected by composite dispersion-ceramic armor, the use of which in conjunction with the aluminum case ensures a low weight of the entire machine (up to 800 kg.).

The cost of production of one such robot is relatively low: $ 250,000-300.000

All the technologies used to create the robot already exist today, so it is possible to hope that in a few years we will see such a device in the service of the armed forces of our country.

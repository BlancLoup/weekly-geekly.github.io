Some rules for calculating salaries


  • If you enter several salaries, the service will take into account only the one you entered last.
  • Wages that are very different from all others are not taken into account: both in the smaller and in the larger direction.
  • On a small number of data, we do not show any salaries, believing that the calculation will be too inaccurate.

About average and median wages


Of course, many people know how the average salary differs from the median. But nevertheless it will not hurt to once again clarify.

So, the average salary, which is also arithmetic average, is the sum of all salaries divided by the number of these salaries. That is, this is the salary that we get if we take away all their salaries and then divide them equally. You must admit that it is a somewhat strange quantity, which speaks little about what happens to everyone in reality.

The median salary is the salary that lies exactly in the middle of all the salaries, if you decompose them one after another, ordering from the lowest to the highest. That is, half of the salary people are the same or higher than the median, and half - the same or lower. This figure already tells us a lot.

Indeed, in the first case, we show how much each will have if we divide equally between all. And in the second - how much poorer or richer each of us is relative to the statistical center.

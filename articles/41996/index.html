The computer of your dreams in terms of Google



There is such a thing - Google Trends .
Use it to find out which computer the Google user dreams of. After all, you use Google, is not it?

Google Trends shows how interested the public is this or that thing. He does this using search query statistics. It makes sense that what is being sought is the most interesting. Enter a few key phrases separated by commas, the graph will simultaneously reflect the data on the first five.

Note: all pictures below are clickable; when clicked - go to Google Trends with parameters like in the picture

Desktop or laptop?


So, the first thing I would like to know is the physical parameters of the computer.



Judging by the graphs, it will be a laptop. Desktops cause less interest, and therefore will buy them less.

Interest in MakBook is growing, MacBook Air spurred it. However, so far his schedule shows that he is unlikely to be able to catch up with ordinary laptops in popularity in the near foreseeable future.

Netbooks are not perceived by most as the ultimate dream, this is obvious. At the same time, it is clear that netbooks "eat off" the interest of notebooks. So, perhaps they will occupy a wider niche.

The first conclusion: "dream computer" - a laptop.

Mac OS X has a consistently low level of interest. The explanation, from my point of view, is very simple. Apple buyers do not perceive the OS separately from the computer, it is mainly of interest to programmers.

The release of Vista at first aroused interest in it, however, it remained at a consistently low level. Compared to her position, Windows XP is unshakable.

French authorities propose to protect the national business from unfair competition by Google

The Senate of France supported the bill on the control of search engine algorithms. It is assumed that this proposal may weaken the position of Google in the French market. The idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe project is that search engines disclose their algorithms or pay a penalty of 10% of their global income. The bill has not yet been adopted - it will be discussed in the lower house of the French Parliament.

The initiator of the project was Senator Catherine Morin-Deceli. She believes that Google deliberately underestimates the position of competing services in search results. Recall that quite recently "Megamind" wrote about the beginning of the trial between the European antitrust regulator and Google.

According to Morin-Deceli, Europe is not quickly enough to solve the problem with Google, so you should "support the national business now." In this case, it is proposed to place on the main page of search engines links to at least three competitors. And one of the competitors should be a national search engine.

France has its own search engine Qwant . The Ministry of Education plans to introduce it in educational institutions.

The new bill has both supporters and opponents, reports Techcrunch. French Economy Minister Emmanuel Macron, for example, believes that such measures should be coordinated with the European Commission. Consideration of the bill is scheduled for late April.

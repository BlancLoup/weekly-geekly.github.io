NASA is preparing to launch a solar sail Sunjammer

The goal is to demonstrate the possibilities of moving in space using the pressure of sunlight: photons act on a sail with a force of 0.01 Newton . This is not so little: NASA engineers are forced to compensate for the solar pressure when calculating the trajectories of even conventional devices. Light pressure affects them much more than the solar wind (a stream of ionized particles).

The sail is capable of picking up speed to the very borders of the heliosphere, and theoretically it can accelerate to such a speed that, if launched, in 2010 would have overtaken the Voyager-1 probe by 2018.

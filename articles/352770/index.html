Kubernetes meetup - presentation and webcast


Hi, Habr!

On March 21, at the Moscow office of IBM, a Kubernetes community meeting was held. During this event, participants discussed the latest news on the development of Kubernetes, exchanged practical experience and communicated with colleagues in an informal setting.

Under the cut - details, as well as links to presentations and video recordings of speeches.

Three reports were presented at the meeting:

Istio & service mesh - what is it and why is it needed?
As part of this report, Phil Estes and Roman Boyko from IBM made a speech.
The report was devoted to new approaches to the organization of the interaction of components in the microservice architecture and the implementation of the service-mesh pattern. And also how to implement this pattern in the Open Source product Istio, which is developed with the participation of Google, IBM and Lyft. Experts showed how this product works in IBM Cloud Container Services and IBM Cloud Private .

Presentation of Istio & service mesh

Kubernetes in Avito: Infrastructure for thousands of containers.
Sergey Orlov (head of the development of the architecture unit at Avito) shared practical experience in implementing solutions at Kubernetes, told about the Avito approach to the deployment of industrial and test infrastructures, as well as the development environment.

Avito Presentation

Experience using Kubernetes, Cloud Foundry and Serverless to develop a blockchain project on Hyperledger Fabric.
As part of the report, Igor Khapov told about the experience of using the IBM Kubernetes service, Cloud Foundry and OpenWhisk as an infrastructure for creating applications written for the Hyperledger Fabric platform. Igor also reviewed the development toolkit using the example of Hyperledger Composer and Explorer.

IBM Presentation

The presentations aroused great interest among the audience and gave rise to a lively discussion on the use of Kubernetes, Istio and Blockchain in various practical scenarios.

Pope Catholic priests: we have to become bloggers

Pope Benedict XVI today addressed the priests of the Catholic Church. The essence of the appeal - the priests must carry the word of God not only through personal contact with the flock, not only to get church websites, but also to become real bloggers, using all the possibilities of modern means of communication.



In an unexpected way, it was the 265th in a row that the Pope began to promote modern technologies, including the Network. He not only talks about how it would be useful for the church's institute to work on the Web, but also sets a personal example. For example, last year he created his own YouTube channel , and six months ago, through his efforts, applications for Facebook and iPhone were released that carried the word of the Church to the masses. In general, the Pope is doing everything so that Catholic priests follow his example.

In his message, he admits that the Church has faced many changes in culture, provoked by an unprecedented scale of technical progress. Now the church must use the fruits of this progress to preach.

Benedict XVI understands very well that it is possible to communicate with modern people only by modern means. Well, who said that the Church and social media are incompatible?

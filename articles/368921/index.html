Review Meizu M2 Mini: a stylish budget

In recent years, Meizu has been remembered by users not only because of the production of high-quality gadgets, but also large smartphones. The first "shovel" was the MX3 with a diagonal of 5.1-inches. More - more, MX4 conquered 5.36 inches, and MX4 Pro debuted with a diagonal of 5.5 inches, and it seemed that the company no longer intends to produce compact smartphones, similar to the notable sales leader Meizu MX2 with a diagonal of 4.4 inches. But relatively recently introduced a new line of smartphones pleased users. The ideological models were the M1 Note devices and its younger brother M1 mini. Unfortunately, on the territory outside of the Chinese networks, the smartphone simply turned out to be useless due to problems with the networks above GSM. But today the picture is changing and already M1 mini is logically evolving into M2 mini with improvements. Now, against the background of the previous design, the tempting stuffing and the LTE module working in the Russian Federation have been added, as well as many other things that you will learn from the review, including a pleasant surprise at the end of the article for readers.

Wireless interfaces and communication


Following the trend of using two SIM cards, the Meizu M2 Mini is no exception. Both SIMs can work in 3G / 4G networks and are installed in one tray (nano-SIM). But to use in 3G / 4G mode you can only have one SIM card out of two, the second will be active only in GSM networks (In which SIM card mode will work, you can choose in the settings). As mentioned above in the review, if you decide to install a memory card, you will have to sacrifice one SIM card.



The following frequencies are supported by the smartphone: GSM / GPRS / EDGE (900/1800 MHz), WCDMA / HSPA + (900/2100 MHz) and LTE (1800/2100/2600 MHz). In the presence of Wi-Fi (ac, b / g / n) (supports both the 2.4 GHz band and 5.0 GHz), Bluetooth 4.0, as well as GPS (A-GPS) / GLONASS. All modules are stable, there were no questions with connections.



A cold start of GPS takes about one and a half minutes, and a hot one in the region of half a minute. Accuracy of positioning is excellent, tracking when moving is smooth. Communication with satellites smartphone tries to keep steadily, in deep-laid tunnels or buildings, of course, the connection may already be interrupted. Phone and SMS applications do not clearly differ in functionality from older models. Normally, you can record conversations.

In Moscow and Moscow region, the signal level of the network is high. The connection is not lost, the smartphone stably holds the network, even when it comes to connecting to LTE networks. Although, in the case of the latter, the situation strongly depends on the coverage.

Battery and running time


Battery Meizu M2 mini has a capacity of 2 500 mAh. For an energy efficient hardware platform with HD display resolution, the battery life results are quite satisfactory. The battery in the style of the latest trends - non-removable. A standard charger has an output of 2 amps. First of all, I suggest looking at the result of testing autonomy in AnTuTu Tester.
AnTuTu Tester




The result can not be called the best among budget smartphones, but the points scored will clearly compete with many middle-class smartphones. And what about everyday life? The smartphone lasts for about a day of moderate use, of which 5 - 6 hours is devoted to using the screen. Camera


Meizu M2 mini is equipped with two cameras. The main camera with autofocus is intended for photographing the surrounding world, such as landscapes, pets, cars and other components of life. The front camera is suitable for selfie and video negotiations with a fixed focus. The main module consists of a 13-megapixel sensor with a 5-element lens and f / 2.2 aperture. Corning Gorila Glass 3 protects all this beauty. A 5 MP module with a 4-element lens and f / 2.0 aperture is used at the front camera. A single LED flash on the back cover completes the picture.

A few words about the interface of the standard camera application and its functionality. The application is absolutely convenient. On the main tab, all the useful and necessary information is neatly presented and logically divided. There are manual settings, as well as other shooting modes, such as panoramas, focus changes and portrait shots. Not forgotten the implementation of the adjustment of exposure to tapu.

In almost all shooting scenarios, the Meizu M2 mini produces a high-quality and clear picture. Only in the dark to get a suitable snapshot you will have to switch to manual settings, play with the shutter speed and exposure, and also take several shots of the same object in order to get a clear picture, but not something blurred. However, there is nothing new here, many smartphones of the middle and even higher segment sin when shooting in the dark.

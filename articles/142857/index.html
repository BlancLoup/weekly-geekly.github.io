Google Drive against other cloud services

Portal PCWorld has prepared a comparison of Google Drive with the rest of cloud services. Next - under the cut.





  • Microsoft's SkyDrive offers the largest starting free space - 7 GB, and users registered before April 22 can get 25 GB.

  • Google Drive is leading in price for the month of use, but SkyDrive is cheaper if you buy on an annual basis.

  • SugarSync is the only service that allows you to synchronize files from any directory, but SkyDrive has the ability to gain remote access to a computer using the two-factor authentication mode.

  • As for the file size limit, the Box has a limit of 100 MB, SugarSync has no limit at all, and the Google Drive limit is 10 GB.

  • Web applications, link exchange and private storage - all this is in every service.

  • Offers from Google and Microsoft have their drawbacks: Google Drive does not have a mobile client for Windows Phone, while SkyDrive does not have a client for Android.

The chart is clickable:


Naturally, this comparison will not completely affect your choice. For example, you can use Dropbox, because most people use it, or Google Drive, due to the fact that you use other services from Google. Use this comparison as a memo, and then you can find a cloud service that suits you.

Etiquette

Etiquette is an interactive music installation that now ends in the Edinburgh Sculpture Workshop . The installation uses an interactive table as a way to make the process of making music physically accessible to visitors. And this process brings not only aesthetic, but also tactile pleasure.


Look - the installation is clearly like children.

Extraction occurs through the manipulation of cubes on the luminous table. The work uses the same software library as in ReacTable (the famous video on YouTube), which this summer was finally popularized by Bjork, which used it in its live streams.

But the most pleasant feature of Etiquette is music itself. Unlike most audio installations, it is pleasant to participate in its creation, and it is pleasant to listen to it. Behind the installation are computer science Simon Kirby and the FOUND group, which recorded all the original audio data: bass, banjo, trumpets, flutes and even a glockenspiel , as well as a large number of handy percussion instruments: all kinds of boxes, boxes and other items found nearby workshop.

Etiquette is even available as a 4-track album released under a Creative Commons license! Download the album for free, and make sure that FOUND has managed to successfully marry the latest technology and real music!

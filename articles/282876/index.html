How GearVR differs from a cardboard box, or the pursuit of latency

The main term you need to know about VR is motion-to-photon latency. In other words, the delay between the turn of the head and the last photon of the image (drawn from the perspective of the new head position) left the screen. It is empirically deduced that motion-to-photon latency is 20 msec and below allows to reach presence - i.e. feeling that you move your head in the virtual world.

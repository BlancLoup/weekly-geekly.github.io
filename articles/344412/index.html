Mitap BugBusters: All About Selenium, Effective Automatizers and Weekly Load Testers


Literally the other day, our office passed a test test. We talked about the tests that they themselves would ask to perform them again, about the super-productive teams of automatizers, and also plunged into the weekly load tester.


For anyone who could not join, I suggest recording reports.


Reproducible Selenium infrastructure: a new approach


The first report was delivered by Kirill Merkushev from Yandex. He told how to run WebDriver tests without stopping and what infrastructure is needed for this.



Kirill focused on creating such an infrastructure based on containers, Selenoid and other Aerokube tools.


How to use automatics resources at maximum


Anton Shapin from Epam shared his vision of a dream-team automator. In the report, he told how they managed to assemble a team and organize its work.



Separately, Anton focused on the problems that arose during the formation of the team and the issues of the rational use of the capabilities of each participant.


A day in the life of a load tester


The mitap ended with a vigorous report by Anatoly Plaskovsky from Yandex.Money, in which the listeners got a closer look at the activity of the load worker.



Key episodes: acceptance of release assemblies, live firing and negotiations with teams.

Twitter #Music



Today we introduce Twitter to #music, a service that will change how people search for music. Using Twitter messages, we collect information about popular tracks and musicians. It will also allow Twi-active musicians to share their preferences with you.

Currently, Twitter tracks #music can be sent from one of three sources: iTunes, Spotify, or Rdio. In the application, you can listen to the preview of the track from iTunes. Rdio or Spotify subscribers can log in to their account and listen to the full version. We continue to search for other providers of music catalogs.

Use the #NowPlaying tag to find music.

To learn more about a music group, for example, Chvrches, select their avatar, you will see a list of the most popular songs of the group, or go to their Twitter profile.
Or are you interested to listen to musicians who are watched by Wiz Khalifa, use the search icon in the upper right corner. Next, listen to each artist individually or all in a row by clicking on the play button.
Have you found a song you want to share with your friends? Just click on the disk icon in the lower left corner, in the player interface you can share it using the Tweet icon (in the upper right corner).
Install the application from the AppStore or web application (will be available within a few hours - music.twitter.com . So far, the service is available in: USA, Canada, UK, Ireland, Australia and New Zealand. Give us some time, we will release the Android version and expand the list of countries.

Twitter and music are a great combination. People share their favorite tracks, albums and musicians every day. Most of the popular Twitter profiles are musicians, and half of all users follow at least one musician. This is why Twitter is the best way to establish a channel of communication with fans.

Report on KRI-2010

The annual Game Developer Conference 2010 ended yesterday at the Cosmos Hotel.



Intel has once again made a technology sponsor of this conference.

Despite the crisis, the conference was attended by a huge number of people, a lot of games and ideas for implementation, sessions and exhibition stands.
Under the cut photos from the KRI-2010, the results of the KRI Awards Awards, a description of the work of the Intel stand, as well as a summary of the sessions held.
And, of course, the details of the quiz, held in twitter.

Intel at KRI-2010


The Intel stand was assembled and put into operation on the very first day of the event.



It introduced the updated Intel Graphics Performance Analyzers version 3.0 ‚Äìa toolkit consisting of System Analyzer, a real-time CPU / GPU performance analysis tool, Frame Analyzer, a detailed analysis and debugging of the frame GPU, and a Platform View stream performance visualization for all CPU cores. The capabilities of these programs were presented on the example of the game Stalker: Call of Pripyat.



Also, at the stand of Intel was presented gaming laptop Asus G73 . It was running the game Dirt 2, and anyone could play it and feel the power of the platform Core i7 720QM in a laptop.



A team of modders TopModds assembled a computer based on the Intel i7, to which water cooling was connected, to demonstrate the capabilities of the new platforms.



During all three days Intel booth held regular quizzes with questions from the field of high technology, taking part in it and correctly coping with the task, it was possible to win one of the fun prizes of Intel. ( Intel hat )

Intel engineers conducted seven sessions, talking about the news and updated software solutions from Intel. If you were unable to attend the Intel session, but would like to read the content, in the near future they will be available on the Intel Software Network.

Overall impression


At the conference there were many girls who attracted people to the stands



Many companies presented their latest developments in the field of games. Demo versions of products that will be released only by the end of the year were shown, and also, some projects conducted recruiting campaigns, on the results of which it was possible to get a job in a particular company.

In general, the CWI in general and the CWI-2010 in particular are a great place for developers of all types of games: from browser noughts and crosses to demanding and resource-intensive games with high-quality graphics and gameplay. Here one could exchange experience, ideas, agree on cooperation, get to know a lot of interesting people, one way or another connected with the gaming business both in Russia and abroad.
The conference was attended by a fairly large number of people, all were interested in the information presented at the sessions and on the stands. That is, the conference came out very high-quality and, I think, helped many companies gain invaluable experience of their colleagues.

KRI-Award

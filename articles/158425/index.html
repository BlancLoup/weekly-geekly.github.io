Plastic Aston Martin printed on a 3D printer filmed in Skyfall

The latest 3D printing technology from Voxeljet from Auksburg can be seen in the cinema, and specifically in the last part about spy James Bond (Skyfall). In the scene where the Bond car explodes. In total, 3 models of the Aston Martin DB 5 were created. The auto models duplicated the original priceless 1960 model car in the scene where the car is being destroyed. Action scenes in high-budget films, such as films about J. Bond, should look as realistic as possible.

A total of 54 separate components were printed for three vehicles. These were roofs, hoods, fenders, doors, etc. After removing the extra material, the parts were packed and transported to Pinewood Studios near London.



After assembly, the model was painted and brought to a state where it was difficult to distinguish it from the original. Even on closer examination, it was almost identical to the original Aston Martin DB 5.



While the 3D models participated in the scenes of the explosions and burned in the film, DB 5 itself, which was filmed in the first Bond film 50 years ago, was unharmed.
By the way, one of the models was sold at Christie for $ 100,000.

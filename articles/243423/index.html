New IBM Global Entrepreneurship Program Accelerates Cloud Development



IBM has announced the launch of the IBM Global Entrepreneur Program for Cloud Startups, which will enable start-ups and technology entrepreneurs to take advantage of the IBM Cloud platform, as well as access the global network of corporate customers, consultants and IBM innovation centers in order to accelerate the development, scaling and development of cloud computing. making.

According to reviews of more than 300 venture capital investors and business angels received in 2014 from around the world, most of the investments were directed to technology companies, especially those engaged in the development of cloud and mobile technologies (source: 2014 Global Venture Capital Confidence Survey ). In turn, IBM contributes to the active development of the industry by offering companies the tools they need to create new-generation cloud applications and capitalize on sales revenues.

Thanks to participation in the program, startups that meet the selection criteria will be able to use the resources of the IBM Cloud platform in the amount of up to $ 120,000. The IBM Cloud will provide entrepreneurs with infrastructure opportunities to rapidly deploy a business and allocate resources to further develop, scale, and bring solutions to market. The program will also provide entrepreneurs with access to IBM's global customer base, many of which are working with the startup community to find new approaches to solving industry problems.

As part of the program, entrepreneurs will have access to the IBM Cloud integrated portfolio built on the basis of the IBM SoftLayer cloud infrastructure. The comprehensive portfolio includes the Bluemix cloud platform for building applications, which offers more than 75 application and service execution environments. These services will help startups handle the growing volumes of big data generated by mobile and social networks, as well as user devices, sensors and sensors. Bluemix also includes a wide range of services for developing innovative cloud applications:
  • Integration with Twitter to collect and analyze data from social networks;
  • Aspera tooling for high-speed data transfer;
  • Services monitoring the current status and performance of applications;
  • High-performance and flexible database as a service from Cloudant.

The updated Raspberry Pi Zero microcomputer received camera support and still costs $ 5



Less than six months after the release of the Raspberry Pi Zero microcomputer, the Raspberry Pi Foundation was able to present its first major hardware update to the developers. Its appearance after last November's triumphal announcement of the first version was expected, since the lack of a camera connector became at the same time the main weak point of the chip and the desire of many users. In the updated version this problem was solved.

Having retained its previous symbolic cost - $ 5, in its updated version, the microcomputer received a camera connector compatible with two updated photo modules based on Sony IMX219 image sensors with back light (back-illuminated) and 8 megapixel resolution (3280 √ó 2464) presented in last month. The manufacturer promises a tangible increase in sensitivity, reducing the amount of noise and improving image quality.

The module is connected to the connector that somewhat pressed the components from the left edge of the microcomputer board using a flat cable. Recall that the first, released at the end of November 2015, the version of the Raspberry Pi Zero microcomputer is equipped with a 1-core BCM2835 processor based on the ARM11 core, operating at 1 GHz, and LPDDR2 RAM with a capacity of 512 MB.

IBM Watson will help NASA to surf the universe



NASA has a lot of complex projects, the implementation of which is a vital task. Moreover, attention should be paid not only to the main tasks, but also to intermediate, purely technical ones. Such tasks include the retention of the orbit by spacecraft, maneuvering in space, the approach of the apparatus to different objects and with each other. The operator in such situations needs to make a decision very quickly, otherwise the long-term mission may be disrupted due to a small error.

In addition, the agency has accumulated a huge amount of information about outer space and objects that are in it. IBM 100 helped NASA specialists to calculate data when implementing the Apollo program

IBM systems have been used by NASA for more than half a century. It all started with the installation of an IBM mainframe at NASA's Langley Center, with the help of which the agency's experts calculated the correct trajectories of the spacecraft movement. Now, by the way, is preparing the film "Hidden Figures", which tells about how a woman from Langley programmed this system. We are talking about the mathematician Catherine Johnson, who worked with the mainframe even before John Glenn went into space.

What now?


Watson is now working with NASA on a program whose goal is to provide astronauts with important information during the flight that will help them make difficult decisions. Data will be transmitted from the earth. Now NASA is trying to recreate the conditions of the incident that happened to one of the planes several years ago, in order to test the capabilities of Watson. Experts have already checked whether the cognitive system can provide the pilot with data that will help avoid the problem that happened earlier.

The situation itself is not fully disclosed, but it is known that several years ago the pilot managed to land the plane, although during the flight he made mistakes that could lead to a serious accident. As part of the test, Watson provided the pilot with information about the aircraft, problems with equipment and weather conditions in the flight area. All this allowed the pilot to better understand the situation and make the right decision.



Preliminary tests have been completed successfully, next year it is planned to begin the implementation of the next stage.

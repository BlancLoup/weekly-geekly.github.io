Materials, aerodynamics and new battery


Goodies in the form of a visual positioning system will be left for later, but for now let us consider changes in physical dimensions and flight characteristics.

In the 4th version, according to the company's assurances, the aerodynamics of the hull and the overall maneuverability of the device are radically reworked. The basis of the quadcopter is now made of magnesium alloy, which, together with the new battery and body, made it possible to shift the center of gravity closer to the level of the propellers, and this, in turn, should save the final image from the ‚Äúsudden‚Äù propellers in the frame when the quadrocopter has strong inclinations.


Photo: The Verge

The weight of the device compared to the Phantom 3 Pro increased by 100 grams due to the use of a more capacious battery. Apparently the company found the perfect balance between its capacity, the total weight of the quadcopter and the power of the motors. The latter, by the way, were also recycled. The maximum flight speed is now 20 meters per second or 72 km / h. Motors made as open as possible for better airflow and cooling, which is important given the increased power.

Sensors, Cameras, TapFly, ActiveTrack and that's it


An innovation in the third version of the Phantom series was an optical position control sensor, which debuted at DJI Inspire 1. In essence, it is an ultrasonic sensor and a camera. Such a bundle made it possible to better maintain the position and fly at low altitudes (up to 3 meters) in places with poor GPS reception.


In the 4th version of the quadcopter, this system was improved by introducing another camera on the bottom of the quadcopter. In addition, two cameras were installed in the front of the quadcopter - they are mounted in the chassis. Thus, a bundle of four camera sensors, a main camera for video recording and an ultrasound sensor form the so-called computer vision . All the most interesting innovations are tied to this system:

  • ActiveTrack
    How did the ‚ÄúFollow me‚Äù system work in previous drones? The quadcopter simply attached to the coordinates of the smartphone and stupidly flew behind it. Now, thanks to the system mentioned above, the device has learned to "see." It is enough to indicate on the screen of a person, a car, a boat, anything, a quadrocopter will follow the indicated purpose. Without any additional transceivers. Those. in fact, the drone was taught to recognize and memorize images.

  • Obstacle Sensing Systems
    The obstacle rounding system is based on the same technology, it only follows the obstacles (logical, yeah). If it is possible to fly around it, the quadcopter will continue to move, otherwise it will slow down and freeze at a safe distance from the obstacle, waiting for further commands from the pilot.

  • TAPFLY
    In fact, it is an improved flight mode by points. The manufacturer says that ‚Äúmanaging the sticks and maintaining the height, course and speed can be difficult, so TapFly assumes all these responsibilities leaving you to concentrate on the object to be photographed.‚Äù You simply indicate the direction or object on the screen and the quadrocopter flies following your instructions.

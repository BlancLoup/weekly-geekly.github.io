In the final part of the ACM ICPC competition, also known as The Battle of the Brains, the one hundred best university teams in the world were to use open standards-based technology to develop software that can help solve world problems. level Each team consisting of three participants was offered 11 tasks of different degrees of complexity, modeled on the basis of such real problems as, for example: creating an airport timetable for safe landing of aircraft that would take into account changing weather conditions and other surprises, or optimizing traffic flow in peak hours on the road network.

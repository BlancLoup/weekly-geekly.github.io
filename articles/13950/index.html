Apple licenses 3G technology

The web has a new portion of rumors about the imminent appearance in the iPhone of the support of third-generation cellular networks (3G).

If you believe information from the eWEEK website, Apple has signed an agreement with InterDigital - the office engaged in the development of wireless data transmission technologies, including in 3G networks. Interestingly, this company is better known for its conflicts with Samsung and Nokia, rather than any technological developments. Nevertheless, according to the information provided, the parties signed the contract for seven years. According to some reports, the transaction cost $ 56 million.

Of course, no one is expecting an updated iPhone in the near future: it is unlikely that Apple will rush to launch a new model on the market without removing all the cream from the current sales.

By the way, just yesterday the site Engadget published an interesting advertisement allegedly belonging to the German mobile operator T-Mobile.

According to him, the new iPhone will appear on November 12 and will receive not only HSDPA support, but also increased internal memory to 16 GB. Although, most likely this is just another fake ...

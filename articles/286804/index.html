Master in England



It is no secret that the master's program in the UK is one of the most sought-after areas for students . Today we will tell not only about the admission process, requirements, tuition fees, but about grants and scholarships.

Master in the UK is a unique educational base. Students from all over the world are striving here to get a diploma capable of becoming the key to a successful career.

The Master provides for a deeper, in contrast to the Undergraduate, the development of the theory of the chosen specialty and, depending on the profile, the preparation of the student for research or professional activity.
Masters programs last, as a rule, one year. Individual programs sometimes take two years.

The uniqueness of the magistracy in the UK is that it provides an opportunity to master a new specialty that is not related to the undergraduate program. The Master's program involves the study of 15-20 subjects that are directly related to the direction of the diploma. Thus, for 1 year you get a fairly intensive program, which will save time and money. However, such a rapid pace of study may come as a surprise to foreign students and requires a Pre-Master's course, which is available in almost every university in England.

International students have the right to work while studying in the UK for no more than 15 hours per week. The average hourly wage of students is 5-7 pounds. Any university in the UK has sufficient links with companies that offer students a part-time job while studying.

Preparation for admission


Preparation for admission must begin one year before the end of the bachelor's degree (that is, in the third year) in order to start studying at the master's level immediately after graduation. In addition, preparation for tests takes a long time and it is desirable to properly plan this process.

IELTS (International English Language Testing System) - an exam to determine the level of English proficiency in people for whom it is not native. Depending on the candidate‚Äôs goals, there are two exam modules: General and Academic. They differ in tasks in the sections Reading and Writing. For admission to the magistracy will need to pass Academic IELTS. The required score for admission to the magistracy in England from 6.5 and above. More information about the format of the test and preparation can be found in our blog .

GRE (Graduate Record Examinations) - a standardized exam in English, which is one of the necessary conditions for admission to foreign postgraduate programs and courses (MSc, MBA, PhD, etc.). There are two types of exam: GRE General Test (general) and GRE Subject Test (specialized).

GRE General Test is designed to assess critical thinking and analytical skills in general. If, for admission to a university, it is necessary to demonstrate knowledge of a particular subject, an examination is required in one of the following areas: biochemistry, cellular and molecular biology, biology, chemistry, computer science, English-language literature, mathematics, physics and psychology. However, the GRE General is most often required for admission.
Learn more about the GRE test and its counterpart, the GMAT test, here .

You can prepare for the tests on courses, individually with a tutor and independently. The latter option is only suitable for those who have a high level of language and good self-discipline. In the other two we recommend contacting teachers with experience in passing and working with tests for at least 3-5 years.

All documents, including test results, are attached to the application form on the university website and are submitted to the deadline.

Seminar "The use of modern information technology to solve modern problems of particle physics"

On July 3, an open interdisciplinary seminar on the use of modern information technologies in modern physics will be held at Yandex‚Äôs Moscow office.

Known scientists, specialists in nuclear technologies - Niko Neufeld, Marco Cattaneo, Ulrik Egede and Mitesh Patel will make scientific and technical reports. All of them are employees of CERN (European Center for Nuclear Research) and are working closely related to solving the computational problems of the LHCb experiment - one of the main experiments of the Large Hadron Collider.

Scientists from CERN will talk about the LHCb computational model, how the LHCb experiment data acquisition system is built, the existing problems, and the difficulties that will have to be encountered in connection with the development of the experiment by 2018.

Also Yandex employees Alexander Ignatiev and Andrei Gulin will speak at the seminar. Their stories are devoted to technologies used to work with large data arrays in Yandex. The seminar will conclude with a discussion of the possible application of these technologies for solving the tasks of CERN.

The seminar will begin at 15:00 . Registration starts at 14:30 .

The program, registration and abstracts can be found on the seminar page .

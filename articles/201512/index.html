Lightweight carbon nanotube suit



Canadian clothing manufacturer Garrison Bespoke has developed a men's suit that many businessmen and politicians will most likely need. At first glance, the costume is no different from the classic one, but it is made from fabric based on carbon nanotubes. This fabric stops the bullets to the forty-fifth caliber and protects against stabbing wounds. It is 50% lighter than Kevlar and 30 times stronger than steel.

The patented technology was first used for the fighters of the 19th group of US special forces in Iraq (selective units of the US National Guard intended for guerrilla warfare and special operations - counterpartisan, sabotage, counterterrorism, etc.) and civilian customers.

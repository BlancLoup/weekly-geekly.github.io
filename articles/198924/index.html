LinkMeUp. Release 8. IB - Best Practices and Pentesting Laboratories

In the sixth issue, our guest, Alexander Sinister, talked about the loudest attacks on IT infrastructure. It turned out to be the most popular for the 7 months of the podcast.
Listeners reasonably asked in the comments how this can be avoided.
Meet, as they say, Best Practices on Network Information Security. Alexander will talk about the most probable attack vectors and methods of preventive defense against them.

In addition, only here you will hear about the newest attack on OSPF, which allows you to change the routing table in the entire IGP domain. Vulnerability was discovered quite recently and only a month ago Cisco and Juniper patches were closed. Such information is not yet in RuNet and it is quite difficult to find in the global network.

But you know nothing about Information Security if you have never attacked or attacked yourself. But everyone has the opportunity to consolidate the theory of practice and for this it is not necessary to go under the article. For these purposes, there are laboratories PenTest (Penetration Testing).
The creator of one of these laboratories - pentestit.ru - Romanov Roman will tell about his experience, what problems they had to solve, what opportunities the lab provides and what it all means to be on the other side of the firewall and always be ready, ready for the attacker smarter than you

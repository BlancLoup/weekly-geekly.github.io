Google targeting tablets

Google‚Äôs Android Community Support Division launched ‚Äú2012 Android Developer Survey‚Äù

Judging by the questions, they are mainly interested in how comfortable it is for developers to write and sell software for the Android platform.
You can also conclude that Google is paying attention to the tablet direction (which is natural because the smartphone and tablet differ in both the nature of use and the type of users)



Topics from the questionnaire:

General issues

- how do you get news about the platform, whether you work with other mobile platforms

For developers

- where (on which sites) you are looking for answers to questions on the use of API
- how easy is the API to use?
- what IDEs are used and how suitable are they for working with the Android platform
- what do you use for testing (emulators, real hardware, paid services) and how convenient it is for you
- how much more difficult / easier to develop for tablets
- Do you have a separate direction for tablets?

For architects

- why are you releasing a version of your Android software
- how quickly do you start supporting new versions of OS and new hardware
- what is your monetization model and how successful is it for you

For marketers

- how do you distribute your software (Google Play or other services)
- how do you advertise your software (on TV, advertising on websites, etc.)
- where you distribute your software (by country)
- whether the system of client comments on Google Play is convenient for you

Questions can be answered here: http://bit.ly/AndroidDevSurvey

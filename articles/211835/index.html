The largest space telescope in history has received the last three mirrors



The James Webb Space Telescope project is one of the most ambitious, and, accordingly, one of the most expensive in the whole history of space science. Thus, the total cost of the telescope, which will be put into operation in 2018, is 8.8 billion US dollars.

The new telescope is far superior in capabilities of its predecessors, including Hubble. So, the other day this telescope received the last three mirrors.

It is worth noting that the telescope is not one solid mirror, but a system of 18 hexagonal mirror elements. This system is ready, and is in a sterile storage facility at Goddard.

The total diameter of the system after assembly will be 6.5 meters, and the computer will control everything, directing each of the mirrors to the desired position to adjust the clear focus of the device. The weight of each mirror is 20 kilograms, and the whole system works in the infrared range.

In addition to mirrors, an infrared camera arrived in Goddard, tasked with observing the most distant stars and galaxies in the visible part of the universe. Observations will be conducted at 1.5 million kilometers from our planet. Scientists have come up with an interesting protection from the infrared radiation of our luminary - a special tent that will open upon arrival of the device at its destination.

Reflections on the future of social networks



Have you ever wondered what the Internet and social networks will look like in the near future? I think that the majority of Dear Habr's readers will respond positively, and some of them not only think, but also directly participate in the processes of creating the Internet of the future. This article is intended for them in the first place, although it may be equally interesting for others to reflect on this topic. In the article, I propose to abstract from technology and speculate mainly about the concepts of the development of the Internet and social networks.

Modern tendencies


Every day, the world wide web reminds me more and more of a self-organizing universe that is evolving with wild speed. And although this system does not yet have full-fledged artificial intelligence, the beginnings of its creation are already beginning to appear in it (for example, the project Intelligence Realm, the virtual interlocutor of Elbot, or Akinator reading thoughts). I have no doubt that the day will come when the Turing test will be passed and the Internet will become an indispensable assistant from a functional tool, and for some there may be a friend. And this day is not far off! But do not forget - What / Who is behind all this. Of course, these are communities of people. Communities united by common ideas, goals and interests that are willing to spend their time and resources to implement these ideas. Therefore, every day more and more social networks appear on the Internet, their functionality is becoming ever wider, and their participants are gradually turning from consumers into active content creators. Wikipedia, YouTube and of course Habrahabr are vivid examples.

Problems




It seems that development is moving in the right direction, but one problem arises: in every social network there is a need to register. Perhaps only software developers for storing passwords benefit from this. People looked at this outrage and came up with a very useful piece called OpenID. However, the reality is that today not all resources accept it. It is of course very convenient - once you authenticate with the OpenID provider to surf through your favorite sites and communities, but there is a fly in the ointment. First, if you think about security, everything becomes not so bright. You need to completely trust your provider and store your OpenID authorization data as the apple of your eye! Otherwise, having obtained your username / password, the attacker will have access to absolutely all sites that accept OpenID. And secondly, another problem remains unresolved - the inability to communicate with users from different social networks. And more and more situations arise when Vasya, Masha, Peter and Kenny are in Odnoklassniki, Vkontakte, Facebook and MySpace respectively, while the snowboarding party communicates in LiveJournal, and the best minds in the field of IT on Habr√©;) Not very convenient, agree?

Development alternatives


I propose to think about it in order to understand what alternative ways of evolution of social communities can be created. In my opinion, the most obvious option is only one - the creation of a global network community (let's call this GSN network - the global social network), which is one level higher than all other communities and aggregates their data. Those. it should be some kind of shell that allows you to collect heterogeneous data and present them in a user-friendly form. For example, the creators of Digsby embody this idea only at the level of messaging, I'm talking about global aggregation, including blogs, media storage, geoservices and Wiki. Those. full mashup! With a fully customizable interface! For example: Vasya logs into his GSN account, turns on Vkontakte player and starts looking at pictures from Masha‚Äôs last trip to India posted on Picasa with links to Google maps and immediately writes comments to her on Facebook. And by posting a video on YouTube, he can send a link to it at once to all his friends from all social networks. Those. the ultimate goal is to create a single entry point for working in social networks for those who need it (someone may need only one social network or only email in general). The idea is good, but without the interest of the social networks themselves and the availability of open APIs, this is problematic. So - what can they be interested in? Here they are, two key motivators:
  • Material benefits
  • Empowerment

But how can you expand the capabilities of social networks and give them the opportunity to earn even more green papers?

Third dimension




The answer is in another dimension ... in the 3rd! Yes, yes, fans of Second Life are not in vain on their guard, it is the 3D interface that can add a huge amount of new features to social networks! You and I are used to moving and interacting in 3 dimensions, so 3D GSN provides tremendous opportunities for communication, self-expression, learning and interaction. This is not a flat 2D avatar, but its three-dimensional projection, not an ordinary chat, but a virtual reality for voice communication, not a personal page, but a personal apartment. And the list can be continued ... I am already silent about the game component, happy farmer and others like them! MMORPGs have a rest with the right approach ... So, I will list the main points that will help to better present how this might look like:
- 3D GSN can be accessed from any social network that is part of the GSN community, while the user sees all his friends who are online in at least one social network. network. The interface of the shell and the look of the avatar are completely customizable.
- The world of GSN 3D is created by the users themselves and interested companies. Here it is necessary to stop in more detail. First, I would suggest creating a model of a real 3D world with reference to Google Earth. Those. joint efforts would gradually form a 3-dimensional model of our beloved planet Earth. Secondly, users should be able to create their own fantastic worlds, this can be implemented on other planets. Thirdly, the motivation for creating new objects is the possibility of selling them and getting real incomes.

Now about the material component:
Users from any social network that are part of the GSN community have a personal electronic wallet at their disposal. Money from these wallets are accepted for payment in 3D GSN. And first of all, everything depends on the bandwidth of data transmission channels. But, in my opinion, the problem is solved. The same Second Life demonstrates quite acceptable performance on my 16 Mbit / s channel. Although, to be honest, I spent quite a bit of time researching SL, and the number of users in SL cannot be compared with the number of users of social networks. But with the imposition of a number of restrictions and proper optimization, the expected result can be achieved.

Sports Tracker for Android Review

Probably many of you are involved in sports. For example, I often ride a bike, and at one point I thought: ‚ÄúWhy not write down the path that I travel?‚Äù. No sooner said than done.

For my then-Nokia 5530, the Sports Tracker program was found (only for Nokia smartphones). She could record a GPS track, show different speed settings and more. But then I switched to Android. And so successfully it turned out that this program appeared on Android.

About this program I want to tell you today! There are many images under the cut!

Android client


Home screen


  • Workouts - the number of workouts (tracks) that you recorded
  • Total time - the total amount of time you spent on training
  • Distance - how many kilometers you have traveled / ran / swam / flew in training
  • Energy - how many kilocalories you spent for all your workouts.

By the way! In the settings you can specify your name, height, weight, on the basis of which the spent calories will be calculated. And if you want to be even more precise, you can buy a special pulsation belt in their store, which will send data to the program via bluetooth.
  • Latest workout - last workout
  • Sunrise - what time is the dawn
  • Sunset - at what time sunset. The numbers in the screenshot after the yellow arrow down - this is the time that is left until sunset

Well, the big orange button Start new workout - Start a new workout. There you can select a workout type and set up auto pause. Auto-pause is a very useful option - as soon as you stop, the track recording stops (i.e. the real average speed will not fall in motion), as soon as you start moving, the track recording will continue.

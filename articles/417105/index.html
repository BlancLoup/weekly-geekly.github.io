Next, we took three different parts for comparison: a simple model: a cube 60 x 60 x 60 mm, our printed simple piece and a complex large piece and compared the print time.
Material PLA, nozzle diameter 0.5 mm, layer height 0.2 mm, filling density everywhere 10%.

Comparison table





On fillings such as Line and Rectilinear , where the threads are printed perpendicular to each other, extrusion at a speed of 70 mm / s breaks. The thread hooks onto the thread of the previous layer and cuts off, resulting in holes, which can badly affect the strength of the part. Therefore, it is better to either not use these types of fillings at high speeds, or reduce the printing speed to about 40 mm / s.

Hilbert Curve , Archimedean Chords , Octagram Spiral and Concentric fillings print quickly, but we were unable to guess their purpose. It can be possible to print puzzles and roll balls from bearings through the maze. Also, it is possible that these types will be useful when printing layouts where strength characteristics are not so important. Maybe someone has more ideas?

Details with Honeycomb , 3D Honeycomb , Cubic , Stars and Triangles fillings were the strongest, but the density of Cubic , Stars and Triangles fillings is visually lower than that of Honeycomb and 3D Honeycomb , despite one value of 10%. If we need to get a more dense grid (15-20%), the printing time will also increase accordingly.

In terms of print quality at a speed of 70 mm / s, print time and durability, 3D Honeycomb wins. This is the result.
We hope this experience will be useful to you.

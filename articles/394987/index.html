Apple blocks free electronics repair laws

The company makes every effort to impede the repair of equipment by unauthorized workshops.


IPad Pro tablet disassembled. Photo: iFixit

On Geektimes, the topic of absurd prices for Apple equipment repairs was repeatedly discussed: replacing cracked glass in the iPhone costs $ 149 , in the iPad Pro - $ 599 (plus shipping), replacing the battery in the phone - $ 79 , replacing the motherboard in the MacBook Air - $ 750 and so on.

For example, if you got somewhere cheap glass from a third-party manufacturer, then an authorized Apple center will refuse to install it. To make repairs on your own, too, not everyone succeeds: to separate the old glass from the screen, you need to very carefully heat it to a specific temperature , when the glue melts at the joints.


The procedure for parsing iPad Pro. Source: iFixit

It is clear that such a procedure is beyond the power of most ordinary people: they simply turn to the company center or buy a new iPad.

Apple earns a lot from selling AppleCare + services, as well as selling branded components ($ 599 for glass to a tablet gives a good margin). The company maintains a closed ecosystem for the repair and maintenance of laptops, smartphones and tablets and effectively squeezes excess profits from consumers who are used to spending large sums of money on Apple equipment and Apple services every year.

Some engineers are trying to repair Apple technology on their own, using a method of poking and common sense , but Apple strongly recommends that users refrain from contacting "unauthorized centers."


An engineer from the Rossmann Repair Group checks the resistance of a burnt resistor on the motherboard of a MacBook Air laptop before replacing it.

Moreover, Apple may specifically disable the gadget that has been repaired in an unauthorized center. So, the recent iOS 9 update turned the iPhone 6 into a ‚Äúbrick‚Äù if it had a non-original Home button with a Touch ID fingerprint sensor installed. In this case, after updating the system, Error 53 occurred (Error 53).



If the user repaired the device with the replacement of components not in the branded center, but somewhere else on the cheap, then his smartphone became inoperative after the software update.

The company later explained that Error 53 is the result of a security check designed to protect users:

‚ÄúIOS verifies that the Touch ID sensor on your iPhone or iPad exactly matches the other components of the device. The existing status quo completely suits Apple, so it resists the adoption of new laws in the US that threaten the closed ecosystem of Apple. So far, she has succeeded in lobbying her interests successfully (the ‚Äúbribing‚Äù of US officials is legalized as part of the lobbying procedure).

As lobbying is legalized, information on the financing of officials is indicated in official government documents. Journalists of The Huffington Post interviewed the initiators of such bills in two states, and those showed official government records that indicate funding for opponents of the bill by Apple.

The ‚Äúright to repair‚Äù bills were attempted to be held in four states - Minnesota, Nebraska, Massachusetts and New York. Bills propose to amend the current legislation regarding the sale of electronic equipment. The amendments simplify the repair of equipment in third-party workshops: the manufacturer is obliged to provide all the necessary documentation for such repairs in independent workshops, as well as provide independent repair shops and individuals with the same software tools and software updates needed for repairs that the manufacturer provides to its select partners.

The amendments are also intended to reduce the amount of ‚Äúe-waste‚Äù that is usually subjected to a dangerous and expensive shredding procedure in an industrial shredder and shipped for disposal in third world countries. This is the second attempt, last year Apple lobbyists managed to block the first version of the bill .

How I assembled a Steam Machine based on GeForce GTX 980

After the presentation in 2013 of Steam Machines, I had the idea to purchase this device from Valve.
Participation in the Valve beta test for me did not bring results. There are several reasons:

1) Not an active player;
2) Limited amount of Steam Machines.

A bit of history


Steam Machines is a series of game consoles, the production and distribution of which began in 2014 by various suppliers using a number of different design specifications created by Valve. Steam Machines will run SteamOS, a Linux-based operating system designed for games and other entertainment, and will be able to run games from the Steam directory. These devices can be freely modified by users. Steam Machines will have a number of different hardware optimizations for performance, size, price, and other factors. Valve also develops the Steam Controller, designed to combine keyboard and mouse control accuracy with typical gamepad functionality. (WiKi)

In my opinion SteamOS is still damp and not very versatile. The updated version of SteamOS on 20.10 v144 still does not contain the latest drivers for the GTX970-980 and install the system with these cards will not work. Supports only previous generations of cards. I installed SteamOS on the old GTX750 and later updated the drivers manually. Putting SteamOS with the hope of a stable and trouble-free operation, I do not recommend at the time of beta 144.
Windows with a Steam client.

For a full immersion in the gameplay, Sony Dualshock 4 went perfectly well. After the use of the utility, the ds4 tool turns into a great tool for games of any genre.

Startups and Microsoft: Venture, Hubs, and That's All

February 14, 2018, while lovers and programmers enjoyed the holiday, BizSpark (Microsoft's international startup program) ceased to exist. She was gorgeous. We were sad ... But not for long. On the same day we announced a new program Microsoft For Startups. I asked Nastya, who deals with us in this area, to tell a little about the work of Microsoft with startups in the past and the future.



I give the floor to Anastasia Makeyonok, head of innovative entrepreneurship at Microsoft Russia and CIS.

BizSpark: a bit of history


Our old startup program, BizSpark , was excellent: we supported young companies by donating resources to develop their solutions first for three years, and after restarting the program for one year.

In addition, there was an extended program to support more mature startups, the next level, the so-called BizSpark Plus . As part of it, you could get: a large cloud grant up to $ 120K in the Microsoft Azure cloud, technical support and technical expertise, and a business development manager worked with the guys.

When a startup approached the top bar of using free resources within the grant, the next step of the program, Microsoft Go To Market Accelerator, entered into the picture. Within it, we provided the project with marketing and sales support, helping to enter new markets and make money.

Such start-ups as Dodo Pizza , FaceApp , Robot Vera , HoloGroup , Heedbook , Netology , Meduza.io , LinguaLeo , Dnevnik.ru , The Question and many others took part in this program.

So, we are glad to present you our new program - Microsoft For Startups . It is no less attractive than the old one, and in some ways it even wins greatly against its background. What does all this mean?


  • We have not stopped helping startups. We began to do it more cleverly and focally, within the framework of the Microsoft Scale Up program, individualizing our offer for every B2B startup, selecting the necessary technologies for growth, helping to reach the right customers and customers, establishing partnerships with it, if necessary - not only in our market, but also in other countries. The size of the cloud grant has changed, it has become more flexible: with those startups that fall into the program, we start with a grant of $ 25K inside the Microsoft Azure cloud, issued for a year. This distribution helps to more intelligently use the resources allocated for your project. Also, new resources appear in the offer (for example, access to Dynamics 365).
  • In some cities of the world, we have opened Microsoft Reactors - these are special startup hubs created on the basis of Microsoft Accelerators, where you can communicate with various technology experts, attend subject meetings, and also conduct your own. Microsoft Reactors are now open in Seattle, Beijing, Shanghai, Bangalore, Tel Aviv, Berlin and London. Soon we are planning to open another such startup hub.
  • And finally, Microsoft Ventures , our corporate venture fund, continues to invest in interesting solutions around the world. Colleagues from Ventures consider even more mature businesses, ranging from stage A to stage C. An important criterion for investment by our corporate venture capital fund is the availability of interesting technology inside a startup.

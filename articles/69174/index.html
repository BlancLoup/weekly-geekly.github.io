Social engineering and social hackers


Publisher: BHV-Petersburg, 2007
Author: Maxim Kuznetsov, Igor Simdyanov
Paperback, 358 pages.
ISBN: 5-94157-929-2
Book on ozon.ru , books.ru

Everyone who was once interested in the theory and practice of hacking (no matter what) is well known, the most vulnerable factor in any system is a person. If you are interested in how to ‚Äúhack‚Äù a person or just make him act in your interests, then this book is for you.


The book contains:

- Introduction
- Part I. What is social engineering and who are social hackers
- Part II. Psychological minimum of a social programmer
- Appendix 1. The art of negotiation
- Appendix 2. Introduction to the typology of personality

Also in the book is an introduction to NLP (Neuro-Linguistic Programming), psychological aspects of preparing social hackers and training of emotions.

I have read this book several times already and every time it is read in one breath. I did not see anything similar (and just as interesting) on ‚Äã‚Äãthis subject. It was very interesting to read about various situations and examples from the life of the authors, about various psychological experiments and much more.

I applied many of the knowledge I gained to my friends and people I did not know. This is perhaps the most interesting, and most importantly - valid. Fully rearranged as a person, you can learn and achieve much more, even if the person is not familiar to you.

Or, for example, when you walk down the street, and there is nothing special to do, you can learn a lot about a person by walking, by gestures and even by the position of hands.
Or mentally decompose the group of classmates in social groups, to understand the real motivation of a person in a given situation.

I would not like to tell everything, otherwise the book will be uninteresting to read. Thanks to this book, I discovered a lot of new and interesting things, defended a report on social engineering and just had a good time, because it was written well and read very easily.

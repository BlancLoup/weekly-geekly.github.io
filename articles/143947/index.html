MarketPlace in numbers

The number of sales of our solutions in the 1C-Bitrix: MarketPlace store has already come close to a hundred, and therefore we want to share our experience. We will tell both on the example of our products and the modules developed by other studios.

MarketPlace is a platform that allows you to sell solutions (modules, components, templates, sample sites) developed under 1C-Bitrix. The ability of the buyer to pay for solutions on the MarketPlace site appeared in August last year, it was from this time that the active development of the site began. Thus, even a year has not passed yet, but we, nevertheless, will publish our statistics and share our experience.

In total, we have released 10 solutions, 6 of them are paid and 4 more are available for free download and use.



How to set the price?


The price is one of the decisive factors allowing the client to decide on the purchase of the solution. A good price for a solution is a price that is slightly lower than the cost of developing the solution functionality for freelancers / companies. The price should be such that it is more profitable for the customer to buy a solution than to order development. It is not worth much to wring the price: the sale of modules worth from 5,000 rubles is only 14% of total sales.

Do not do the opposite - to set a too cheap price. It hardly makes sense to sell the solution for 100-200 rubles - it does not pay off, because more costs will be spent on updates, answers to customer questions and technical support. And do not expect that such a decision will buy 50,000 times and you will get a huge profit. Do I give partner discounts?


Affiliate program MarketPlace allows you to set an affiliate discount for any solution. By doing this, Bitrix partners will be able to buy your solution at a discount of 40 to 50% (depending on the status of the partner). It seems that it is a lot, however, partners must give discounts. And that's why:



As you can see, as many as 49% of all our sales were made by partners. And most of these sales were eventually paid for by the end customer (the partner sold the module to the customer and earned 40-50% of the module cost). Particularly relevant affiliate discount for expensive unique solutions (in the price range from 20 000 to 50 000 rubles). We are happy to sell such a solution to the client and, being a gold certified partner, we will earn 50% of sales :).

With all this, most developers of such solutions for some reason do not give partner discounts. And without them, it makes no sense for us to sell the solution to a client - we will not earn anything on such a sale.

Do you make free decisions?


When MarketPlace was just launched (with the possibility of payment), new modules began to appear in very large numbers and among them were a lot of free ones. It was logical, since partners got a lot of points for making decisions that allow them to get a better status (and this is a big discount when buying Bitrix) and to rise in the rating of partners on the Bitrix website (and these are new customers). By giving a lot of points for solutions, Bitrix encouraged developers to fill in a new, empty MarketPlace as quickly as possible.

Now in the MarketPlace more than 650 solutions. Of these, a lot of free - 44%. But the policy of awarding partner points has changed: now they are given less and releasing free solutions only because of the points may be too expensive for the company.

Yet sometimes it makes sense to release free products. This can be done in the following cases.

The first is to practice and gain experience (to prepare for the release of a paid solution). It‚Äôs not to say that the development for MarketPlace is very different from the simple development of modules for Bitrix, but there are some differences. The client, who paid the money, and when installing the module, for some reason, everything began to appear in the wrong encoding, it is unlikely to be pleasant.

The second case is the release of frequently used functionality. If on each project you use any functionality acquired by programmers, you can design it as a module and put it into MP. This will save time for developers (no need to copy files, create folders / tables, etc. - all this is done by the installer of the module), and also allows you to conveniently update the functionality. If, for example, a bug was found in the functional, and it works on 20 client sites, then it will have to be edited on all sites. If the functionality is designed as an MP module, then you can release one update. Administrative solutions, solutions for developers, utilities that, logically, are not tied to a specific site: DbgTracer , Live Api , ‚Äú Infoblocks, tools ‚Äù are related to this case.

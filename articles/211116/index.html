We always know perfectly well that there are a number of startups that have huge cash investments and market value, but do not know how to ‚Äúmake money‚Äù. And Twitter refers to one of them.

The network has a document that describes how to monetize Twitter. And it looks like you can buy goods directly from its interface.

Now Twitter is in one step to close the deal with the Stripe.com payment system, which will provide the entire backend of purchases through Twitter. As for the Twitter interface itself, it looks like at least one partner in the person of Fancy.com will provide its products to Twitter users. This is all part of the new monetization program called Twitter Commerce.

The journalists from recode.net found an image in an unprotected Fancy.com directive. The image was not confirmed either on Twitter or on Fancy.com itself. There are also conversations with other potential partners.

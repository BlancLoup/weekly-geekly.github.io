Nissan car will not start if the driver is drunk

New technologies, one way or another drunk driving warning, appear quite often. However, none of them has so far gained any noticeable popularity, much less become a standard. Nevertheless, automakers continue to work in this direction and from time to time demonstrate their achievements. In particular, the Japanese auto giant Nissan Motor reported about the next stage of development of this technology.

According to representatives of the corporation, Nissan engineers are about to begin testing a new integrated system that monitors the state of the driver and, if necessary, blocks the engine. The system works quite simply. Inside the car, sensors are installed that capture and analyze the driver's breath. If the owner of the car drank too much, the protective system blocks the ignition switch.

Anyway, Nissan has high hopes for a developed protective system and hope that future cars will help to significantly reduce the number of accidents caused by drunk drivers.

By the way, one of the main competitors of Nissan - Toyota - also noted the development of a similar technology, a little more advanced. In her case, the sensors are built into the steering wheel and determine the amount of alcohol in the blood by analyzing sweat.

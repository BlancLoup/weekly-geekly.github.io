Fring conquers its place on mobile phones

Fring continues to win a place on mobile phones, including those running Symbian OS 9. Announcing support for this operating system at the beginning of the year, and giving owners of Symbian OS 9 smartphones the opportunity to use Skype and Google Talk, Fring did not stop at achieved After some time, a version with MSN support was released, and with the release of Fring 3.01, the program added support for the SIP protocol.

Thus, it is possible to use the services of SIP operators even on phones that do not have pre-installed support for the SIP protocol. Fring works with GPRS, 3G and Wi-Fi. To facilitate the settings in the program already have presets for Gizmo, VoipStunt, VoipCeap, and FreeWorldDialup. However, setting up Fring on another SIP operator is easy, knowing the name, password and proxy server.
After that, you can call each subscriber from the phone's address book in four different ways: GSM call, SkypeOUT, call through an SIP operator or Fring. By themselves, calls to Skype, Google Talk, MSN and SIP numbers will remain free, you only have to pay for the traffic to which Fring is very careful.

Another new feature that appeared in Fring 3.01 was a pleasant surprise for anyone who prefers to work with Cyrillic. Unicode support will ensure trouble-free work with Russian names in the contact list and text in chat messages.

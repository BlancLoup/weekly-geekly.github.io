Top 5 innovations expected in 2007 What technological changes will occur in the next 12 months? Pay attention to the following achievements.
  • Hybrid drives. These discs, expected from manufacturers such as Seagate and Samsung, combining the components of flash memory and traditional disk plates, will provide better performance while maintaining a lower cost compared to drives built only on flash memory. First of all, such disks should reduce the boot and resumption time of the computer. They will save time when accessing data, since much of it can be stored in flash memory. However, to use these drives, you will need Windows Vista.
  • Offline Ajax applications. Thanks to the Ajax programming tool (Asynchronous JavaScript and XML), web pages are being improved more and more, they can now preload data and change information on the fly. This year, the power of Ajax will go offline, making client applications much more convenient to use. For example, Zimbra, an open-source web-based email service, demonstrated code that allows users to view mail, calendar and RSS feeds even when they are not connected to the Web.
  • Windows Vista SlideShow. Want to view email headers, even if your laptop is closed and in sleep mode? SlideShow, a successful combination of hardware and software innovations, allows you to do just that. Laptop manufacturers will have to add an LCD display to the external surface of the computer in the manner that it does with mobile phones - clamshells, and software developers will need to adapt their applications to work with such a display. All this should happen in 2007, making laptops even more convenient to use. Smartphones equipped with SlideShow will provide similar functionality.
  • SED TV. It seems that after several delays, TVs based on the promising technology SED (Surface-conduction electron-emitter display - displays with electron emission based on surface conductivity) will finally appear. The mentioned technology brings the brightness of flat screens to almost the same as for displays with a cathode ray tube, but with electricity consumption 3 times lower than that of similar plasma panels. In addition, such displays will have noticeably shorter response times, better contrast and a wider viewing angle. In 2007, Canon and Toshiba, who jointly developed the SED technology, plan to release the first 55-inch screens.
  • Large-scale WiMax. Small, local WiMax deployments have already begun, but in 2007 you will see the ubiquitous deployment of this technology, which promises high-speed connectivity for all types of mobile devices, from cell phones to laptops, with a much larger coverage area than Wi-Fi. Sprint Nextel, together with Intel, Motorola and Samsung, will most likely give the technology maximum acceleration, starting its implementation in late 2007 as part of its 4G services. According to the WiMax Forum, you can deploy a WiMax system and provide a channel with a capacity of 40 Mbps, operating in a radius of 3 to 10 km. This will allow mobile users to get broadband access almost anywhere.

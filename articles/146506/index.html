Larry Page lost his voice



Last week, Google Chairman Eric Schmidt announced that company CEO Larry Page would not speak at the I / O conference and two more events, including a conference with analysts on the results of the second quarter next month.

Schmidt said that Page lost his voice, wishing him a quick recovery and joking that, according to Sergey Brin, this problem will make Larry a better CEO because he will have to be very careful in choosing words.

It would seem, well, a man lost his voice - you never know what could happen. However, according to The Wall Street Journal , investors were seriously alarmed, which was also due to the fact that Google no longer reported anything on Page's problems. The lack of detail has created a number of rumors on Wall Street that he may have a serious illness.

Page himself wrote a letter to the staff on Thursday that nothing was serious with him and that he would continue to lead the company.

"We have no reason to believe that Larry is something serious, but we find it strange that the company has already excluded his participation in the conference about the 2nd quarter, until which, most likely, a few more weeks," wrote JP Stock Analyst Morgan Doug Anmouth in a note to customers. He added that this may raise questions for investors.

Anmouth also noted that Page, who regularly publishes links and notes on his page on Google+ , has not written anything there since May 25. By the way, yesterday, Page wrote the first message since: about the Turing machine on the Google homepage.

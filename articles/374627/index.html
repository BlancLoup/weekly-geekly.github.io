To live someone else's life wearing glasses of virtual reality: the social experiment "Seeing-I"

How can you get into "someone else's skin"? Artist Mark Fardi will check this: within a month he will be around the clock to see and hear what the other person is experiencing. For this, Mark uses virtual reality glasses .



Seeing-I is a social-artistic experiment. Mark Farid will wear virtual reality glasses 24 hours a day, 28 days. Mark will live in a room consisting of a bed, toilet and shower - in full view of the public. Mark will see a picture with a six-day delay so that the team can prepare a schedule: when and what to eat, when to go to the shower or toilet. Only one hour a day, Mark will communicate with a psychologist - although glasses can not be removed.

The second person, whose identity is not revealed, will wear glasses recording audio and video. The video is recorded with a 180-degree overview. Mark did not communicate and does not know this person. It is only known that this heterosexual man, consisting in a relationship.

The purpose of the experiment is to understand whether the main character will lose his self-determination, whether he will not confuse his life and the life of another person.

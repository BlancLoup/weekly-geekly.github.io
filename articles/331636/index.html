Overview of the main sections of the conference PG Day'17 Russia

Friends, the PG Day'17 Russia conference, which we are holding for the fourth time in St. Petersburg, will take place very soon, on July 5-7. Every year we grow and expand. This year we made an event dedicated to databases in the broad sense of the word.

A week ago, the full conference schedule was published. The event is expected to be very eventful: a school day that includes 11 master classes; Greenplum Day is a free event dedicated to the analytic platform of the same name from our partners Dell / EMC and Pivotal; two days of reports - over 60 speeches in six sections.

On the eve of the conference together with hydrobiont we have compiled for you a selection of the most vivid and memorable performances.



Interesting reports will be enough for everyone. Developers will like the speeches of experts from Data Egret. Max Boguk is a unique specialist and does not need a database to figure out how the optimizer will work. Max will share his supernormal abilities in the report " Non-classical techniques for query optimization in PostgreSQL, or how to teach an elephant to dance rock and roll ." To understand what exactly is waiting for you on the Max report, I invite you to watch the presentation of 2014 , with which Max spoke at one of our meetings, called PG Master.

To estimate the depth of Alexey‚Äôs immersion in technical details, I invite you to familiarize yourself with the transcription of his report on one of the past PG Day.

Especially to please all admins and devops, a star troop from Zalando comes to us : Alexander Kukushkin, Alexey Klyukin and Murat Kabilov . In the framework of two reports, colleagues will share their successful experience in operating PostgreSQL as a cloud platform. Every day, Zalando faces a unique challenge: how to unite dozens of disparate teams working on the development of numerous components of a huge business, without having to demand the same technology stack from each of them. Answer: a single platform for data storage . It's no secret that developers like clouds for lack of the need to manually manage the database. Specialists from Zalando decided not to swim against the current, accepted the need for an effective cloud platform and wrote some of their tools to help the Posgres run efficiently in Amazon AWS. The peak of the trend for import substitution came and went. Long disputes have subsided, and all interested companies, forcedly or voluntarily, are quietly moving in a new direction for them. Have you ever wondered how today's Posgrais lives in a bloody enterprise? Exactly this will tell Anton Bushmelev in the report of the same name " Bloody Enterprise, or how we tamed an elephant ." The company RT Labs, a division of Rostelecom, has been successfully using PostgreSQL for the second year in new projects. Anton will share his experience in implementing the ambush on PG Day.

This year we expanded the scope of the conference and invited professionals who work with popular commercial and open source databases to the event. We will tell you a little about the most interesting speeches dedicated to Oracle and MS SQL Server, which are worth a visit for both post-pressists and colleagues from the Enterprise world.

The most important report in this section will be presented by Viktor Egorov , DBA from Data Egret. Based on his experience with both PostgreSQL and Oracle, Victor will conduct a comparative review of the architecture of both databases . The report will be divided into several sections devoted to specific architectural solutions: working with memory , processes , versioning and data storage . The report will be of interest to many. PostgreSQL users will be able to look at another DBMS, its features and capabilities, some of which can be implemented in PostgreSQL. PostgreSQL developers may have ideas about the future direction of the development of Posgres. On the other hand, all Oracle users will be able to evaluate the capabilities of an open database. This is especially true for representatives of organizations that reflect on migration.

All fans of "hardcore" we also have something to please. Experts from the technology center of Deutsche Bank are preparing a whole three cool technical report. Dmitry Remizov will teach a lesson on the anatomy of the cursor , Nikolai Kudinov will talk about tracing user processes and minimizing expectations , and Alexander Khalukhin will hold a practical workshop on diagnosing the performance of Oracle Database .

Independent consultant Ilya Verbitsky, using the example of SQL Server 2016 security system, tells about the painful problem: how to restrict access to data and create a safe environment for your business .   • SQL Server configuration and setup;
  • TSQL - proven practice when writing good code;
  • development tools and administration tools for SQL Server - free and paid tools.

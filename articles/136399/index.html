Split signal and noise on the screen

Sometimes you need to display a picture on several sources (for example, TVs) and some interference may occur. So here is the information on how to divide the signal and correct the arising interference.

Signal separation.


There are times when you need to get from one video source to multiple receivers, for example, the output of a singal from a video-eye to the intercom (small TV) near the door and to the recorder (for recording).

If there are two receivers of signal power, usually there is enough a tee with F connectors or a connecting tee for the BNC connector.




In extreme cases, the wires can be soldered.

If there are more than two receivers, then the problems begin. Connection options:

  • First connect to the recording device, and from its output (everyone usually has it) connect the remaining two (if there are three devices).
  • Use matching tools, but this may not always help.
  • Use an amplifier with a splitter (amplifiers, splitters).

Lightning protection.

Lightning protection devices are used to protect the station (video recorders, monitors, amplifiers, etc.) and peripheral equipment (video cameras, amplifiers, rotators, etc.) from high voltage arising in cable lines due to:

  1. lightning discharges,
  2. industrial interference
  3. welding work
  4. works of powerful power plants



There is a single-channel lightning protection with different connectors to protect coaxial cable and twisted pair: BNC, F, UTP. There are multi-channel, for example, 16 channels SP016C.

Surge protection protects video surveillance circuits, local area networks (LANs), power supply and control circuits.

Insulators.

  1. Insulators allow you to eliminate the distortion of the video image caused by parasitic current due to the potential difference of the earth.
  2. The parasitic current arises due to the difference in ground potential (due to unbalanced operation of the power grid) at various points in the video surveillance system and flows through the cable, introducing distortion.
  3. Insulators protect equipment from high voltage (up to 380V AC) that occurs between different ground points and caused by unstable power supply.

How was the first in Ukraine festival of creativity and innovation Make It Show

Recently, the art factory Platforma hosted the most large-scale festival of innovation and creativity - Make It Show.

Our team as producers of innovations for education was particularly interested in becoming participants of geek parties and going to the festival. Objective views have already reached your attention, but with a subjective view, the authors of which we want to share in the open spaces of our blog .





Let's start with a little lyrics. The festival took place in the Ukrainian capital in the days when everyone was ready to rest - Youth Day and Constitution Day of Ukraine took place on the same dates as MakeItShow. Plus from this coincidence that on Sunday evening there was no need to rush home (Monday was a weekend), but you could afford a noisy pastime during the festival.

Make It Show is an atmosphere of complete freedom and creativity. It sounds like an advertising slogan, but it is this short phrase that I want to characterize the rich program of the event. Fun, tasty, useful, in a company with interesting people and high-quality music - so I want to answer the question of everyone who asks, "And how was it?".

The concentration of the most active and creative youth of our country at the festival just went through the roof. Its representatives were united by science, technology, art, robots, drones, comics, superheroes, street art and extreme sports, open-air lectures and sports shows. This is in brief.

In addition, as part of the Make It Show festival, the organizers arranged the so-called start-up gallery. As part of it, young Ukrainian start-up projects showed their innovations and talked about their ideas to potential customers.



As for the partners of the event, this is a series of Kiev art projects, for example, the Center for Science and Arts "DIYA", the Foundation for the Development and Support of Young Art of Ukraine Slava Frolova, as well as the Street Food Festival. Therefore, all guests were shown interesting installations of domestic artists, as well as gastronomic delights in the food court Make It Show.

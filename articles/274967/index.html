Monitoring SSL certificate and domain expiration


Situations when the site suddenly stops working for a banal reason - the certificate has expired or did not extend the domain in time - not as rare as we would like. From experience, I can say that this happens both in small firms and personal projects, and in companies with hundreds of employees. The reason is simple - the notorious human factor. To solve this problem, HostTracker introduces new features, the use of which further increases the chances of the site to get closer to the desired 100% uptime.

Control tools


Domain name registrars are obliged to inform owners about the end of the lease of domain names, but, as practice shows, this does not always prevent trouble:
  • Emails get spammed
  • Responsible employees go on vacation / sick / leave
  • There is always a bunch of more important things, and the domain expires only after 10 (7..2,1, oh!) Days

Problems like these arise all the time. And, of course, when they happen - no one can remember who bought the domain, from which card it was paid, and the accounting department doesn't give you all that money right away.

In order not to fall into such situations, it is convenient to use the HostTreker tool to control domains and secure connection certificates. The service will automatically determine the expiration dates of domains and / or certificates, and notify all responsible employees on time in any convenient way - via SMS, e-mail or Skype, for example. These functions can be used both separately and simultaneously with regular monitoring of uptime.

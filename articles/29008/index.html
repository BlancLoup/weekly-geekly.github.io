Since in Russia this market is not as well developed as abroad, I think that this issue is worth attention.

First you need to decide on the idea. Ideas are different and each person has his own one. We all think that we will be our project better or otherwise than others. That our idea will in any way eclipse all competitors or even that these competitors do not and never will. Not! Nothing but healthy competition allows many companies to go up. Go...


Gathered with thoughts, ideas and go!
For a start, you definitely need a website. Without it, in our time, nowhere. Domain, preferably, the second level and the name - as short as possible. Short domain - the probability of better memorization by users. The name of course should reflect your idea, your company or the scope of your project. There are many articles about how to choose a successful name for the project. Starting with selection using a generator, ending with the analysis of the names of successful companies and the creation of their own.

In my case, everything was easier, because I have a lot of domains and there are a couple of suitable for my project. Just by doing quite the opposite, I gave the name of the project the name of the domain that I liked and was successful for this project. Then I took care of the hosting and without hesitation I took to start a virtual dedicated server ( VPS / VDS ). Of course, in the future I plan to switch to a full-fledged server, but at the initial stage it will only draw extra money from your wallet, while giving useful, but not always necessary resources.

Now you need to think about what will be on your site!
Here are the most necessary components that will find their application:
- Forum . And how do you want? Where is the feedback from users? How will the community, which will further gather around your project, write and discuss the product (s)?
- Wiki . Here, mainly post user guides and tips. You can also post there working documentation, although I do not agree with that. I understand that the project is Open Source, but it is not always necessary to share the secrets of creation and implementation. This can play a role in the future in competition.
- Bug Tracker (Error Management System). With this, I think it is clear ?! It is advisable to make an open registration, for end users, because the more errors you find and correct, the less they will remain in the code.
- Developer's blog . Here you will write your thoughts and plans for the next step of the project. People will read, comment and make you understand what they want.
- Newsletter . Do not forget that for some people are so busy that they are distracted only by what interested them. Keep the news brief in the newsletter with a link to the full version, which will provide an opportunity to track the necessary moments in the life of the project.

Magic Leap has raised another $ 827 million to bring augmented reality technology to a new level.

Startup Magic Leap is developing augmented reality technology, and this has proven to be a very expensive exercise.

Yesterday, The Verge reported that the project raised $ 827 million in the C round. Thereafter, the company's valuation could reach $ 3.7 billion. The total investment in the company will be $ 1.4 billion.

In October, the Chinese company Alibaba expressed its intention to invest $ 200 million in the project. Magic Leap investors are Google , a manufacturer of microchips Qualcomm and film studio Legendary . Last year, Google became the lead investor in a startup financing round. Who specifically invested in the project this time is unknown.

Such a turn for Magic Leap promises great prospects: the startup will become the most expensive in the industry, and its growing ambitions already cover such projects as Facebook Oculus and Microsoft HoloLens . Magic Leap combines analogues of Google Glass technology (smart glasses) and Oculus Rift (virtual reality helmet) in one device.

The essence of Magic Leap is that a special technology has been created for building 3D stereoscopic images using microprojectors located in front of the user's eyes. It is believed that this technology will not only raise the realism of stereoscopic images to a new level, but will also be actively used in other augmented and virtual reality devices.

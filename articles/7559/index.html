... Confirm your e-mail

But by the way, I do not understand one thing that is common on this global Internet.

Since during the registration one has to enter the password hidden with asterisks twice, everybody got used to it for a long time. In addition, it is completely logical: re-entering the password reduces the likelihood that you will make a typo and sign up with an unknown password.

However, some of this did not seem enough, and they decided that the e-mail should also be entered twice.





So, for example, it looks when registering on the IP.Board forum. Of course, the e-mail is entered in clear text, so you can see the typo yourself. In addition, it is difficult to imagine that someone will actually re-dial an e-mail, instead of making a banal copy-paste (such a number will not work with a protected password field).

Saw a similar nonsense on many sites. It's amazing how developers thoughtlessly copy someone's stupid decision. If you will do the registration, do not repeat this error.

PS Maybe I'm wrong, it's interesting to hear other opinions.

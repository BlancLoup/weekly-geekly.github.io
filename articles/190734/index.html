So, imagine that you have a good, or at least a good idea (nothing bad will help anymore), and you want strict, but fair judges (and there are no others in similar contests) to choose it. Then you should note that:

1. The rescue of the drowning is the work of the gills of the drowning.


If you do not explain clearly and convincingly what you mean in your application, the judges simply will not understand and appreciate it, and you will remain outside the ship of the winners. Of course, in the internal recommendations for the judges may be requested to clarify all the issues with the participants, but in reality this almost never happens. Remember the total number of applications and you will understand why. But even if we ignore the time spent, the contests of large companies are serious events, not like morning performances in kindergarten, where even a kid who has forgotten a rhyme will surely be prompted and given a prize.

Typical examples of such applications are either too short one-line formal replies, of which it is difficult to understand something, or compositions on several sheets, in which the essence is buried behind numerous non-essential details. How to fix it? Show your idea to anyone (not a cat) and ask if he understood the meaning of what was written. If not, edit the application and repeat the process until you get bored to perfection.

Not harmful tips at all


We now formulate the promised advice once again as briefly and clearly as possible so that they become for you a guide to action and future victories.

If you immediately came up with the idea - wait for the second.
Triviality - the main scourge of the competition of ideas. 90 percent of sentences repeat each other; try to get into the other 10. Rate soberly the originality of your application, and if you have doubts, take the time to invent another.

Read the rules - they rule!
Do not be tempted to copy-paste once-cooked text everywhere indiscriminately. Carefully read the conditions of the competition and think for how to deploy your idea so that it is in the winning light here.

The judge is also a man, take care of him.
Write your applications in the highest quality Russian (in this case) language, which is available to you, so that the judge could read it without heartache and, most importantly, understand exactly how you wanted.

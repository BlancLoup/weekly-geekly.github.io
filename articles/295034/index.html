Pay or learn?

Miser pays twice, everyone knows that. And when in the budget of the company it is put less means for the salary to the new expert, than the market demands it, it is necessary to solve Looking for someone who is ideally suited to the tasks and immediately go into battle, delve into the project and make the customer happy? Or accept a compromise, take a semifinished product and bring it to mind, using the company's internal resources, that is, to train?



We analyzed several of the most sought-after IT specialties on the market today and talked to successful and experienced iChar candidates working for our clients. Having promised complete anonymity, we asked them to honestly answer a few questions:

How long did it take you to master the current specialty (programming language, automated testing algorithms, and so on)?

How much time today, with your experience, will it take you to master a new language (new frame) in order to work at the level of an average developer?

How much in wages did you lose when you decided to learn a new specialty?

C ++ => Java


Experienced C ++ Developer has long worked in a large and stable American company. Friends who, in principle, recruited only employees who were personally known to them to the newly opened branch of an international company, offered an unexpected option: forget about the old experience, learn a new specialty. True, they also offered a salary 2 times higher, which served as the final argument in favor of becoming a novice specialist again. It took about a year to fully enter the post.

TOTAL:

Experience With ++ developer - more than 7 years.

Mastering a new Java developer specialty - 12 months.

Axapta => PL / SQL Oracle



The candidate received a specialized education at the Polytechnic University of St. Petersburg, was always interested in databases, participated in competitions held by Oracle, and even wrote a diploma in the development of Oracle. However, at the end of the university, for financial reasons, he entered a small company, Axapta Developer, where he worked for 2.5 years. But still decided to return to what really interested. I risked money, went out as an Oracle PL / SQL development intern with a 2-fold decrease in revenue. But the motivation and specialized education allowed him to pass the necessary attestation and get a promotion in just two months.

TOTAL:

Mastering a new PL / SQL Oracle developer specialty is 2 months.

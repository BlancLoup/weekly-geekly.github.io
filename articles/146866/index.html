Conclusion


In general, ideas of Taffy boil down to fairly simple recommendations:
  • do not engage in the visualization of trivial data;
  • get rid of elements that carry only a decorative function;
  • get rid of elements that impede data analysis;
  • maximize data-to-ink ratio;
  • if possible, turn design elements (axes, points) into data elements;
  • whenever possible use data elements as design elements;
  • in figures, if possible, display numerical data in graphical form.

The advantages of Opera Turbo were estimated by almost three million users



October 22, 2009 - Oslo, Norway - For the month that has passed since the announcement of the Opera 10 browser (from September 1 to September 30, 2009), about three million people around the world used Opera Turbo technology. During this time, they downloaded almost 668 million web pages optimized for viewing on low-speed Internet channels.

The Opera desktop browser is used daily by over 40 million people. Such a high level of popularity of Opera Turbo suggests that this technology meets the needs and expectations of Internet users around the world. Opera 10 Browser with Opera Turbo is available for download free of charge at www.opera.com

The problem of low-speed communication channels faces any user, regardless of whether he goes to the Network via dial-up connection from home or using a public Wi-Fi access point at the airport. Opera Turbo technology is in demand, mainly due to the ability to significantly increase the speed of loading web pages. However, the popularity of Opera Turbo is also explained by the desire of Internet users to reduce the amount of downloadable data per megabyte charging. The ability of Opera Turbo to compress web pages before sending to user devices allows you to reduce the amount of traffic, saving time and money.

Opera Turbo technology has been developed as part of the One Web concept promoted by Opera Software. Opera Turbo is designed to improve and speed up your work on the Internet anytime, anywhere. About Opera Turbo Technology
Opera Turbo is a unique data compression technology available in Opera 10 and Opera Mobile browsers. Opera Turbo allows almost 80 percent compression of web pages. Thus, the speed of loading the contents of websites increases significantly. When charging traffic, the user can receive more information for the same money. To activate Opera Turbo in Opera 10 browser, just click on the icon in the lower left corner. More information about Opera Turbo technology is available at: www.opera.com/browser/turbo

The company Opera Software ASA has opened up new possibilities for viewing web pages on PCs, mobile phones and other Internet devices.

UFNS-queues from the point of view of the programmer

Recently, an attempt was made to obtain an extract from the Unified State Register for one organization. The attempt failed, about which I would like to tell and discuss the situation from the point of view of an IT person.

First stage

To get an extract from the Incorporation - to make a request and transfer it to the UFNS Expedition. There were no difficulties, the request was made in a printed form and I personally went to the Expedition's office. The queues there are small, the receipt of the request is made quickly (less than a minute), the office is open all working day. It is not clear for me only why it is impossible to issue a request through e-mail or through the terminal. Receipt of an extract is not necessary, we are happy.

Second phase

Already more interesting - to find out if the statement is ready. You need to somehow find out that the statement is ready and you need to come to get it. By the way, there are two options for receiving an extract - either getting it in your hands (this should be indicated explicitly in the request), or sent by mail. It simplifies the fact that the production time is exactly 5 days. You can find out either by phone or try to come and find out from the issue window. And to clarify the readiness of the statement you need to know its incoming number. A copy of the request was stamped, but no incoming number is indicated there. I was probably lucky and got a polite girl, who by phone found and dictated the number of my incoming document. I was delighted, the document is ready, I can come ...

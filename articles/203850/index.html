Open data is here

Not so long ago, I stumbled upon an actively developing topic in Russia, which many people know about - Open Data .
As government bodies aggregate a large amount of useful and important data, the motivation to disclose information to the public is obvious.

From official definitions:
Open data is information created by government agencies within its authority, which can be used for any legitimate purposes without restriction.


Immersed in the search engines, I found several sites and even competitive nominations where promising services are published based on publicly available data, which instantly prompted me to want to do for you a small overview of the services that most interested me.



Cyberlenin


http://cyberleninka.ru/

CyberLeninka is a scientific electronic library, the main tasks of which are the popularization of science and scientific activity, public quality control of scientific publications, the development of the modern institute of scientific review and the increase in the citation rate of Russian science.

Special features
‚Ä¢ Open access - dissemination of knowledge on the open access model (Open Access), provision of materials under the Creative Commons license
‚Ä¢ Open Data - export materials to Google Scholar, OCLC WorldCat, ROAR, BASE, OpenDOAR, RePEc
‚Ä¢ Open discussion - public discussion around research papers.
‚Ä¢ Free reading - all scientific publications can be downloaded and read for free.
‚Ä¢ Mobility - reading scientific publications on popular mobile devices


Well, the description has intrigued, we open the list of available scientific articles - my eyes run, we choose something for ourselves. I was attracted by the topic of Cybernetics, bang, and we get lists of articles with 54 pages. Impressive! Read.

All articles are presented in PDF format, accompanied by annotations, tags, various metadata, for which you are immediately offered similar articles in this category. Of course, the spirit of the times dictated the need for the option to share the link to the article with friends on social networks.



Alas, the server power of the project is still lacking, for example, the page with a list of magazines I downloaded unforgivable in the modern networked world 5 seconds, although this mistake is immediately forgiven at the sight of the abundance of magazines dazzling the list with its covers and the number of loaded articles.

The layout of all pages is nicely supplemented with RDF-metadata, which undoubtedly contributes to the future development of the project.

After a simple registration, we get the opportunity to share comments on the articles, however, we were not lucky enough to stumble upon a single comment.

Summarizing, we can say that any shortcomings of the resource pale before the volume of filling the base (and this despite the fact that it is absolutely free), so I confidently attribute this project to the category of must-have in your favorites list.

Home Pharmacist


http: //mashynfarkit.rf/

The application "Home Pharmacist" will help you find information about cheap substitutes for known drugs, so-called. generics. Allows you to find a medicine by name and become familiar with possible substitutes, annotation, see the possible form factors of a medicine (tablet, gel, granules ...).

Features:
- A current, constantly updated database,
- Sort substitutes by price,
- The ability to go to the online purchase of the drug,
- Ability to find and sign up to the doctor directly from the application.


Let's start with the fact that "Home pharmacist" is available exclusively as a mobile application (applications for iOS and Android are currently available, the official site is silent about plans for other systems).

Having opened the application, we immediately see the field for entering the drug of interest to us, having entered, we get its characteristics: ATX, active substance, pharmacological action, etc., but this is not interesting, and the Internet is full of it. And now we are moving to the main function of the application - the search for ‚Äúsubstitutes‚Äù for the drug, the so-called ‚Äúgenerics‚Äù (from the description of the authors: ‚ÄúGeneric is a drug sold under the international non-proprietary name or under the brand name, which differs from the brand name of the drug developer. As a rule generics do not differ in their effectiveness from ‚Äúoriginal‚Äù drugs, but they are much cheaper ‚Äù). What is important, the application immediately shows us the range of the cost of each of the "generics" and offers immediately to buy either the desired drug, or one of the "substitutes". And we can leave our feedback on this or that drug, and live reviews are already available in the database - it was very interesting to read them!



The shopping cart is going straight to the application, but I didn‚Äôt manage to place an order personally - the application read the lengthy wording ‚ÄúError in placing the order‚Äù to me, and I, distressed, went on to further study the application

In the main section, we are offered "Special offers", dazzling with promotional offers from various clinics. Well, you have to earn at least something!

The next item is the function "Search for doctors." Wow! The application offers us to decide whether we will select a doctor due to illness or simply leave a request to the specialist we are interested in (and we can ask the doctor to come to our home). Let's look for a doctor for the diagnosis of flatfoot in the metro Tulskaya in Moscow: the application offered us three doctors with photos, ratings, experience and cost of admission to them. Great, we can immediately make an appointment with them. And all this in one application!



In the settings of the application there is just a great function of downloading the database to your phone in order to be able to access it offline! Thank you, developers, for your care! True, this base is only for drugs, you still need internet to search for doctors!

We hope this application will be a big step in combating the greed of pharmaceutical companies and the struggle of doctors for rating and, as a result, improving the quality of services, and for the end users - the saved ruble in your pocket and treatment at the best doctors not far from home!

Where in Moscow live well?


http://where2live.ru/

Description from the authors:
Based on open data, we want to say in which area of ‚Äã‚ÄãMoscow you will live in the most comfortable way.
We have collected in one database information about the infrastructure of the city, analyzing which you can conclude about which of the areas of the city will be the most convenient for you. To do this, we tried to make the most simple and friendly interface of our service. Enjoy your study!

Yes, having studied the service, it becomes clear - it really is as simple as possible. Choose from the list of possible infrastructure facilities necessary for you, move the slider on the saturation of these objects (for example, you need a lot of objects to choose from) and ... everything! We found the place in which district of Moscow is good to live.

However, when you study the service more detailed questions arise a lot more.
First, the Kurkino district leads in 70% of requests. When I read about it in more detail, everything became clear (quoted from Wikipedia: Residential buildings of different heights - from cottages and townhouses to 23-storey apartment buildings, built on individual projects.)
Conclusions are not difficult, you need to add a tick "cost of housing" for 99% of the audience is more important than the presence of fountains.

Further more. I fix the results, where Kurkino clearly loses. Namely, the following case:

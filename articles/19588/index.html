MacBook Air compared to other ultraportable laptops

The new Apple MacBook Air is Apple's first noticeable initiative on the ultraportable notebook market, clearly standing out for its low weight and compact size. We decided to compare it with not less decent computers of this class from Sony, Lenovo, Fujitsu and other manufacturers.

MacBook Air: thin and light


The computer was quickly introduced in Russia. It upsets the fact that it will cost the Russians a thousand dollars more than the Americans. The main advantages of this laptop, which immediately draw attention, are a good design and low weight (less than 1.5 kg).

Screen size with LED-backlit - 13.3 inches. The image is quite bright and clear.

In addition to the USB 2.0 port and headphone output, the computer has a micro-DVI port, which allows you to get DVI, VGA, composite and S-video outputs using appropriate adapters.

Memory capacity - 2 GB. The amount of built-in hard drive - 80 GB. In addition, it is possible to additionally equip a computer with a solid-state hard disk (based on flash memory) with a capacity of 64 GB.

An important factor for good performance was the use of Intel Core 2 Duo 1.6 GHz or 1.8 GHz processors. Moreover, a special modification of the processor is used that can fit in small notebook sizes.

A modern mobile computer in the world of multimedia technology simply cannot do without an integrated webcam. In this case, the iSight camera is used.

Do not forget about the battery life. Apple promises up to 5 hours of productive work without the need to disconnect from wireless networks.

There is no optical drive, but this is not surprising for mobile computers of this class. The laptop has only three connectors - for headphones, USB 2.0 and Mini-DVI. No ethernet network connector.

It is pleasantly surprised by a rather large keyboard, the same as that of other Apple laptops, and a rather large screen size - 13.3 inches, which is a lot for ultra-portable laptops. The screen resolution is also quite decent - 1280x800.

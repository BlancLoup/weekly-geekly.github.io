Vendor substitution for a dealer or partner


Also a trick that has long ceased to be such. Silence of infrastructure costs


This is the first surprise that will be waiting for you during the implementation. Simply put, you will find out that even a cloud service has its own infrastructure, not to mention on-premise. How to bypass the trick?

Technical support packages as a commitment


Let's make a reservation right away: technical support is work, and you have to pay for it, like for any other. There is some basic minimum that is included in customer service, there is force majeure due to a vendor‚Äôs fault (something didn‚Äôt start, a bug was detected, etc.), but there are calls for each occasion and the requirement to "file reports" of all kinds and species - and of course, free. How to bypass the trick?

  • If you do not need advanced technical support and are ready to cope on your own, ask the vendor to exclude the support package from the payment - even those developers who have made the service strictly obligatory, are very likely to do this, because implementation is already expensive.
  • If you do not mind such a package, specify what is included in it and what restrictions exist. In fact, in the first year of working with a CRM system, an extended priority TP is a useful thing, which sometimes allows you to save on one-time paid appeals.

Updates


Again, the update is a cool thing, especially if it rolls in automatically and does not bring any tangible changes, except for bugfixes and software performance growth. There are no such updates and there can be no complaints. But, as you already understood, there are other options.

  • SaaS-provider rolls an update with a modified logic and functionality - for example, some module you need may disappear.   • Major updates roll in for an additional fee, and this is normal, because you get heavily updated software with the necessary and relevant features. However, you may not need the functionality, or you may not have free money to upgrade at the time you are offered it.

  • If you are servicing a cloud vendor, look for the checkbox ‚ÄúReceive updates‚Äù and remove it, or contact your manager and find out how you can install updates on demand, but not forcibly.

The attackers "took away" the data of 13 million Maple Story players



At the end of last week, Nexon Korea Corp., the developer of the popular online game Maple Story, announced the hacking of game servers. According to the company's specialists, hackers gained access to the data of millions of game users, including ID, names, passwords, and state registration numbers.

As a result, about 13 million users suffered (in general, this is the main part of the audience of the toy). Now in the game there are about 18 million players, it turns out that the data of 5 million people were intact. In any case, while experts say so. Perhaps it was a "customized" action, since Nexon Korea Corp. In a few weeks I was going to go IPO. Well, and with the similar news the stock exchange can not very favorably concern the company.

Now the company is going to hire (if not yet hired) 30 computer security specialists. Also Nexon Korea Corp. strengthened its server protection measures in key regions, these are Europe, Asia and North America.

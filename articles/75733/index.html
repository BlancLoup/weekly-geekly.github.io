What happens when the user goes offline forever

Have you ever wondered what will happen to your mail account, LiveJournal, blog or one of the social networks when you do not become? Who will get access to your accounts, correspondence, personal data?

It turns out that there are already special services ( lastmessagesclub , assetlock ) that have access to the official authorities database, recording the fact that you are no longer there, that is, thanks to them, no one can access the unauthorized use of your personal information. And they can also send the last message to your contacts.
Different services treat this issue differently.

If you have an account on gmail, then your immediate family will be able to access it. To do this, they need to fax or email the following information to Google office:

1. Your full name and contact information, including your verified email address.
2. Gmail address of the deceased user.
3. The full header of the email sent to your verifiable email address from the Gmail account in question.
4. Proof of the user's death.
5. One of the following documents:
a) if the deceased person was 18 years of age or older, provide an official confirmation in accordance with local laws that you are his legal representative
b) if the deceased person was under 18 and you are his parent, provide a copy of his birth certificate. More details here .

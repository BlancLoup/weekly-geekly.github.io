Microsoft Surface 2 RT - powerful, cool, controversial

Tablets have a relatively long time came into use, replacing part of laptops and netbooks. Such mobile gadgets are especially popular among young people, but even in the business sector for tablets, demand is only growing every year.



If initially this category of devices was associated mainly with the iPad, then recently Apple‚Äôs share in the tablet market has been falling. On the one hand, numerous cheap Android competitors are attacking, on the other hand, Windows models have begun to appear. Among the latter, a special place is occupied by the Microsoft Surface line of tablets, which also seeks to take up its position in this market. And today we will talk about one of these devices - Surface 2 RT.

‚ñå DESIGN, DIMENSIONS, CONTROL ELEMENTS


The tablet looks strictly and massively, but pretty. The edges are not sleek and beveled, as is now fashionable, but rather clearly defined. Because of this, the device looks a bit angular. The display is surrounded by wide fields, for which it is convenient to hold the tablet, not obscuring the screen.



At the top is the front camera, front microphone, light sensor and indicator of the working camera.



On the front surface, only the Windows 8 logo stands out, which is very interestingly beaten in the form of faces. Touch button, by clicking on which, you can always get to the OS main menu.



The Surface 2 RT case is made of magnesium alloy (Microsoft itself calls it VaporMg ), which is lighter and stronger than aluminum, and has only a plastic insert on the upper end in order to skip the WiFi signal. Behind the face of the tablet beveled, tapering to the back side, but without smoothing. That is, the back surface is as straight as the front, however, it is smaller in area.



On the plastic insert is placed the rear camera and microphone.



The most important feature of the tablet is of course the stand! The presence of it saves you from having to buy a docking station or look for some other ways of fixed vertical installation of the tablet. True, this metal plate occupies half of the device (although it does not protrude at all), but it can bend back to two positions. All this construction is held very firmly, there is no feeling that now something will work out or change to another mode.



One position is ideal for typing on the table, and the other for typing on your lap. The only, though very significant, drawback of the stand is the impossibility of adjusting its angle of inclination. But experts from Microsoft have provided angles at which it is comfortable to work with the tablet.



For easy poddevaniya stand on the left side of the tablet there is a small ledge. And the microSDXC slot is located behind the stand.



On the right side, we see one of two speakers, a microHDMI connector and a full USB 3.0 port. As for the USB-port, external hard drives and flash drives are quietly connected to it, and also other gadgets can be charged through it. Adapters and cables for connecting the Surface 2 tablet to an HDTV are sold separately and are not included in the package.

By the way, special ‚Äúears‚Äù have already appeared on Kickstarter , which are worn on the corners of Surface, amplify the sound by ten decibels, but do not require additional power. On the top of the power button only. What I didn‚Äôt really like was switching on with one click. Sometimes you just want to check if the device is working or turned off, and you press it and it turns on immediately.

‚ñå SOFTWARE PLATFORM


Surface 2 RT is running the mobile tablet operating system Windows RT 8.1. Today, when the market is filled with tablets on a full-fledged Windows 8, the choice of a less functional Windows RT can only be explained by the need to install a single-chip system on the ARM architecture in the tablet.



With the arrival of the last major update, shortcuts for the search bar and tablet power management menu appeared right on the start screen next to the avatar and username in the upper right corner. And near the button for switching to the ‚ÄúApplications‚Äù panel, an indicator of the number of new applications installed in the system appeared from the initial screen.



Attachments have appeared sorting by name, installation date, frequency of use and categories. I like the third sort more, and left it.



On the RT version, the only significant difference from the older version and the main drawback of the OS is the inability to launch applications from other versions of the Windows family. This means that in addition to the pre-installed software, it‚Äôs simply impossible to install anything there, and the choice of software is limited only by offers from the Windows Store. By the way, at the moment the store has about 160,000 applications.



The problem is also aggravated by a strange company policy - a ban on installing applications from any sources other than the App Store. True, you can do a kind of "jailbreak" to lift this ban. After that, it will be possible to run any application compiled under the ARM architecture, since the list of such applications has already been sufficiently large. Only unfortunately, for the new version of Windows 8.1, a jailbreak has not yet been released, but it will definitely be soon!



On Windows RT, there is a traditional desktop with a panel, notifications, and shortcuts. You can create folders and documents, sort them and change the desktop background.



There are also Explorer, as well as the ability to work with files, as in Windows 8. However, only a few pre-installed programs work in the desktop interface - Office, Scissors, Paint, and some others. And let me remind you: you cannot install new programs that would have the familiar Windows 8 window interface (in the Store all programs work in a tiled interface and are moderated for this).



The disadvantage that I noticed when using the window interface is the blurring of some elements. Most likely, this minus is associated with unfinished scaling. If at HD resolution the blur of fonts is not so noticeable, then on FullHD it does not look very nice. It is possible that with the update this bug will be removed, but so far it looks like this.



Windows RT has a significant advantage compared to Windows 8: this is the presence of a pre-installed and completely free office suite Microsoft Office 2013 (whereas in a regular Windows 8 there is no pre-installed Office, you have to buy it).



However, be prepared for the fact that MS Office on Windows RT is not exactly the same Office as for Windows 7/8. First, it contains only four applications with a familiar interface: Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and OneNote. Outlook comes as a tiled Mail app. In addition, the Office interface is slightly different from the standard PC version (it shows the desire to optimize for touch control), and most importantly, there is no support for macros and ActiveX.



Another interesting point is multitasking. In RT, it is implemented at a good level, familiar to the full Windows. Up to the point that the Flash player can be active on a web page, while the user is engaged in other tasks at this time.



In the tiled interface, there is also a convenient switch between applications. There is a way to simultaneously view and control two applications. You can, for example, open a browser or video on 75% of the screen, and correspond with Skype for the remaining 25%. On other operating systems, you have to switch between applications, which is not always convenient.



And it is not necessary that the second application has a Metro style.



We should also talk about Internet Explorer. You will not believe, but this is not the old and buggy Internet opener, but a convenient browser with support for gestures. I am sure that many owners of touch tablets or laptops will agree with me. By the way, a full-fledged Flash player works in IE. For many, this is an important parameter, for example, to play your favorite social game. networks. But Java and Silverlight plugins cannot be installed.



Very cool integration with OneDrive cloud service. We edit the document on a PC or laptop, save it. Then we start Microsoft Word on the tablet and in the last documents our file is already visible, open it and you can continue working with it. If you want your files to always be synchronized and available offline, then do not forget to enable this feature.



The tiled interface for touch screens is implemented very conveniently and beautifully, with an emphasis on gesture support, it is pleasant to use. If I immediately disconnected it on a PC, then my hand stretches to open the Start menu or close the application with a swipe down (now, so on Android I often try to do it unaccustomed). I would like to mention the response to the touch. It is instant, the image moves immediately with a finger. Perhaps, a similar fast response can only be compared with the Nexus 7, because on the other devices the brakes are immediately visible. I am glad that many file types are supported out of the box, for example, PDF or ZIP. Therefore, there is no need to reinstall applications from the Store.

‚ñå BATTERY, CHARGING, TIME OF WORK


The battery life is one of the strengths of Surface 2. In principle, this is not surprising: Windows RT was designed with maximum energy efficiency. The battery in this case can be quite capacious, since the case is large.

Microsoft says Surface 2 can work up to 10 hours in video playback mode, and 7 to 15 days in standby mode.



I tried to start the video from a local disk, set the brightness to 50 percent and turned on Wi-Fi. In this mode, Surface 2 played my movies for 14 hours and 32 minutes. In surfing mode with Wi-Fi, the tablet lasted almost 9 hours.

‚ñå CONCLUSION


Surface 2 RT at the moment looks more competitive compared to the younger model, which was released in 2012. Microsoft did a lot of work on its brainchild (faster processor, extended battery life, improved display) and fixed many shortcomings. Surface 2 has several competitive advantages: design, stuffing and firmware. Plus, you can buy a convenient keyboard, activate bonuses and synchronize the tablet with your home PC. However, the Store with a small number of applications is still not able to withstand the Play Store and the Apple Store.
And most likely you need to add that Surface is more suitable not for the consumption of content, but for its creation. For those people who spend more time behind a web browser or office and are looking for a portable PC replacement. That is why I still have not bought myself a tablet on Android or on iOS, simply because I did not see the point in it. With Surface, the situation has changed. The tablet not only allows me to watch TV shows and spend time with toys, but also to fully work with documents and files. Personally, I did not feel a lack of applications. Many services that do not have their own app in the Store offer a fairly user-friendly web interface. However, by the second course I will need to work with narrower software and then my view slowly falls on Surface 2 Pro, but it will be a completely different story.

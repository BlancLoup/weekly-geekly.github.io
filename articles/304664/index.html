Let's do it - I will not build from myself a GTD guru and so on. So, I'm not a guru and not a coach. However, in my over 35 years, I realized one simple thing - you should not read out bad books. This stunning in its novelty conclusion follows from a simple mathematical calculation:

Suppose you are 30 years old (I am writing 30, because by this time usually there are already children, work, hobbies and so on). If you are less - do not worry, everything changes quickly and has little effect on further calculations :)

My own statistics, plus surveys of friends and acquaintances, plus all - Russian statistics show that on average we read no more than 10-12 books a year. Now further, the average life expectancy in Russia is 71 years. Thus, if you are 30, you need to read only 41 * 11 = 450 books. Just think, only 450 books! Considering that humankind has written much more books (the difference goes up by orders of magnitude), you should be very thoughtful about the choice of the current reading book. In 2010, I came across a stunning idea: a certain Chinese Denis Oh came up with a bookshelf The Balance of Your Knowledge! The idea is based on the principle of balance: the shelf is fastened centrally and can lean under the weight of books both to the right and to the left. One of the parties relates to professional literature, the second - to the art. And as soon as any "bowl" begins to outweigh, an urgent need to buy a counterweight.



I liked the idea so much that all these years I have been trying to alternate artistic and professional books in my virtual library. The approach justifies itself, because art books are swallowed easily, and on professional ones, a stupor occurs periodically. Thus, the desire to read something easy makes you deal with complex read material faster. Well, it is possible to achieve any balance in development.

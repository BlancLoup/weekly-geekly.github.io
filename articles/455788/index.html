These are serious guys, on the temporary pass of which even the Security Matters is written. This will be the leitmotif of the whole tour.

The pride of the data center is uptime at 100% since its launch in 2011.

The confidence is so high that for the tenths there is not even a place on the scoreboard.



Linx is an example of a DC in GreenField format. The building was specially built for the placement of a DC in it. Therefore, the height of the ceilings, the strength of the overlappings of the power supply inputs, its reservation, SCS - was incorporated into the project and taken into account during the construction phase.



Thus, by default, the floor withstands a rack of 800 kg, but the mass can be further increased after additional reinforcement. They say racks cost in Falcon9 require a special relationship.

The DC itself is powered by a gas-piston installation, and urban electricity is only a reserve. According to Maxim, it was a very correct decision, because during the well-known Petersburg blackout, the office and the nearby business center suffered, and the data center continued its work.

The diesel generator is no longer a trailer on wheels, but a completely stationary room with chimneys billowing high above the landscape.

Startup time is no more than two minutes, and on batteries the entire data center can work for about an hour. Diesel can work 3 days without additional refueling.

Metrotek


Just a month ago, Misha Sokolov and Denis Gabidullin from STC Metrotek Slash Plumspace came to visit a telecom podcast, and we analyzed what a Smart SFP is. And now we were able to touch them alive.

Denis, who conducted a tour of Metrotek St. Petersburg office (which by the way has not changed since our last trip to them), actually gave one of the samples from the threshold.
At first I was surprised how clumsy it was made - as if with a jigsaw and file it was turned from a 32 key.

They say it is even convenient for some customers to have such a tail sticking out of their iron.
The very first goal two years ago was to assemble a conventional optical SFP that would at least transmit a signal.

Then, the Smart part was separately assembled with a foreign SFP (just on a string) to make sure that they were able to assemble the chips.

Metrotek turned huge work. Just think that for 2 years we have assembled a fully working optical module that can encapsulate, telemetry, an exact time server, an interface converter.

And at the same time debug to the ideal (although this has yet to be verified by time) the basic functionality of signal transmission. There should be no attenuation, there should not even be rare drops caused by SFP. And it should not warm up, despite the pack of ASICs, FPGA and CPU inside.
Bravo!

Full computing system:

  • CPU: NXP i.MX6 ARM up to 900 MHz
  • FPGA: Lattice ECP5 up to 85K LUTs

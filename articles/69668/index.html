Perhaps you are tired of hamsters and parrots and decided to start the house of the real Transformer. Not cheap Chinese plastic masks, but a genuine Taiwanese technological miracle. With the help of the new ASUS Eee PC T91 , even the evil Megatron can be put to shame: he is ashamed to spin and fold as cleverly as the T91 can. The ASUS EeePCT91 netbook is one of the first netbooks on the market equipped with a touch screen. Yes, even in the first model of the netbook, craftsmen managed to insert third-party touchscreens, but in this case we are talking about a serial device.
The heart of the netbook this time was the Intel Atom Z520 processor (made on 45-nanometer technology, having a clock frequency of 1.33GHz and 512KB L2 cache) on the Intel SCH US15W chipset , which together consume a minimum of power (totally about 4 watts - against 9 watts Intel Atom 270 on Intel 945GSE chipset ). Having a very low heat dissipation, this solution allowed, according to the designers, to do without active cooling.



Among other things, the Intel SCH US15W chipset has only one chip instead of the north and south bridges, which made it possible to get an additional gain in the size of the motherboard, and consequently in the final size of the device. Developers decided to achieve complete silence for the little ones using a 16GB solid-state drive. But this is not all disk space available to the user - another 16GB is available in the DiskExpander (in fact, this is the second card reader) and 20GB in the EeeStorage. In total, as many as 52 hectares are obtained.

The new Intel GMA 500 with 8MB of memory is used as a video card :) To complete the theme of megabytes, I‚Äôll say that we have as many as 1024MB of operational DDR2 memory.



The laptop comes preloaded with Microsoft Windows XP Home , non-standard wallpaper of which can be seen on a screen with a diagonal of 8.9 "and LED-backlit. With the network, everything is as usual - Bluetooth 2.1 and Wi-Fi 802.11b / g / n.

The essence of the project and customer problems


So, our Customer has long been faced with several major problems of maintaining analogs for spare parts used in maintenance and repair:

  1. The analogues available in the warehouses were not taken into account in the procurement process, because there was no information on the fact that they were analogues in the enterprise.
  2. Replacing the original with an analogue during the purchase each time was agreed upon by the company's technical specialists, since the information about the analogs, as a rule, was not stored in the System.
  3. Accounting for purchased analogs led to numerous additional approvals for adding a new part or equipment to the Nomenclature reference book.

Not surprisingly, this brought enormous losses to production. There was an accumulation of unclaimed stocks in warehouses, qualified specialists performed the same work several times, the process of coordinating the new items of the directory was delayed, slowing down the procurement campaign.

For example, only a simple unit of key equipment due to the impossibility of a quick replacement of a part or the selection of an analogue in the company's warehouses resulted in losses of 2-3 million rubles per day!

Project Objectives


Based on the problems of the Customer, we have set ourselves three important tasks:

  1. Make it so that the Customer knows about the counterparts stored in his warehouses or available for purchase.
  2. Reduce the time for the selection of nomenclature analogues, and therefore - to automate the process of their selection.
  3. Eliminate unnecessary approval procedures for the selection of analogues.

Of course, for this it was necessary to study and systematize the existing practices of maintaining analogues , as well as the specifics of the customer's nomenclature and business processes.

As a result, at this stage, we have developed our approach to automating the maintenance of direct analogs of catalog numbers and tested it on 10% of the Customer‚Äôs nomenclature data to assess the possibility of applying this approach in the remaining part of the Nomenclature.

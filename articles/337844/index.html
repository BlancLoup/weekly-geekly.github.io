Problem number 1
Imagine a closed railway. A train is traveling along it, the last car of which is connected to the first one so that inside you can move freely between the cars. You ended up in one random wagon and your task is to calculate their total number. In each car, you can turn on or off the light, but the initial position of the switches is random and not known in advance.

All the cars inside look strictly the same, the windows are closed so that it is impossible to look outside, the train moves evenly. It is impossible to mark cars in any way other than turning the light on or off. The number of cars of course.

Describe the principle of counting cars.

Problem number 2
The task of logic and a little knowledge of physics.

You put a glass of water on a turntable disc and slowly increase your rotation speed. Under what conditions:
  • the glass slides to the side;
  • the glass will tip over;
  • Is water splashing over the edge?

Justify your answers in terms of physics.

Problem number 3
On a deserted highway, the probability of a car appearing in a 30-minute period is 0.95. What is the probability of its occurrence in 10 minutes?

So Cashflow is a game that should help anyone who feels, or is confident that, in life, he "does not receive good cards", to become a person who can "find a way out of any situation." And, I can say, the game successfully copes with this task. It is easy to win once by chance in cashflow. For this you just need luck. But even a complete bummer who even too lazy to set a goal to win can learn from the game:

The gameplay offers a kind of simplified model of the real world, its financial and investment slice, in which events of various types constantly arise:
  • opportunity deal (deal). In the game this happens, if not at every turn, then at every second step - every second cell is a potential deal. There are big (big) and small (small, up to $ 6000)
the remaining cells (every eighth) are divided among themselves:
  • salary (yellow - a positive effect on the balance) salary, charged on passing through this cell
  • unexpected expenses (scarlet, or pink-red)
  • Market - market events like changes in interest rates on loans, increased demand for real estate, etc. (blue)
  • and finally lilac - large fin. Trouble (as contrasting to yellow) is dismissal and the appearance of a child. As I understand it, the amount of trouble was reduced from three to two, and one cell was later replaced by the orange cell, ‚ÄúCharity‚Äù

The speed of movement is regulated by the die roll.
Thus, the probability of 1/12 to make serious troubles, 1/2 - to get on the deal, 1/8 - to get to the market, 1/8 - make unforeseen expenses, 1/8 - play for nothing, i.e. just get on the payroll cell, and 1/24 - to get the opportunity of a charitable act.

The player constantly, at every second step, offers for deals of various degrees of profitability are poured in, and the player must decide which ones to carry out and which not. In addition, transactions can be put up for auction or sold directly to another player.
The goal of the game is to get out of the rat race, i.e. accumulate enough passive income (passive income) to cover fully its expenditure part of the fin. reports In this way, a player can leave his current job ‚Äúto his uncle‚Äù and completely free his time.

Release of the new generation engine of forum Vanilla 2.0

The long-awaited release of the second version of the vanilla forum engine has been released. The developer blog has news about the release .

What is Vanilla?




Vanilla forums is a minimalistic forum engine. The goal of the project is to create a simple tool for online conferences with a simple opportunity to increase functionality. No, this is not the second phpbb, this is a really very simplified forum.

However, unlike hundreds of other similar projects, this one is simple only for the user, from the inside it is crammed with modern technologies, and therefore AJAX is used to its fullest extent, and the main task of the developers is to make the forum as simple as possible for novice users, and it seems they succeeded . Easy and simple out of the box work such functions as auto-saving messages in drafts, sending messages without reloading the page, downloading new messages and so on.

Despite its seeming simplicity, this out-of-the-box engine provides almost everything that most users need, such as: demarcation of rights, sub-forums and categories, html / bbcode / markdown.

What's new?


Those who used the first version of this engine, remember that it was very unsightly, and in order to make him a "candy", would have to work very hard. With the second version it is already in the past. Out of the box, the Vanilla has a simple but neat diznayn that you can easily use. Moreover, on the official website in the add-ons section there are a number of topics prepared by professional designers. So, the first thing that those who have tried the first version will notice is that the system has become more attractive, and you can even use it out of the box.

The engine was rewritten from scratch, now it is based on the Garden framework. And I must say that this Garden was written not just anyhow, but in the best traditions of MVC without unnecessary complication or duplication of code, so extending the functionality by writing plug-ins or applications is a real pleasure.

As you can see, all the power of this engine will be in plugins in the future. And although at the moment they are not written very much (about 50), there is already something to choose from.

The authors of the engine build their business model on the provision of support and commercial hosting of forums. Therefore, there are two different sites: vanillaforums.com - for those who install and configure the forum is problematic, and vanillaforums.org - for those who are technicians themselves. It is on the last site that you can find all the add-ons, links to the github repository and the community forum, which runs on the much-modified Vanilla 2 engine.

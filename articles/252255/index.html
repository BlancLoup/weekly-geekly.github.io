In Germany, Google Play bypassed the App Store


What many have been waiting for, while others have been afraid, is starting to come true. The first big market in which Google Play revenues exceeded the App Store revenues, and this is not just anywhere, but in Germany! Last year, it became the sixth market in the world in terms of total revenues from the App Store and Google Play. And in Europe only Great Britain surpasses it in terms of revenue.



Judging by the dynamics and the fact that the number of downloads on Google Play has exceeded and is actively breaking away from the number of downloads on the App Store, it can be assumed that the trend will continue and will increase.



What is interesting, but not surprising: 85% of the income comes from games, and what is especially interesting and surprising is the distribution of income from these games according to their place in the top. The largest contribution is made by games from 11th to 100th place, and very significant contribution is made by games from 100th place and higher. This have not happened before.

View the full report on the App Annie website.

Apple Green Data Centers




Apple announced an investment of 1.7 billion euros for the construction and launch by 2017 of two data centers in Europe. Like all Apple data centers, new buildings will be fully provided with clean renewable energy from the first day of launch. Apple will also work with local partners on new renewable energy projects, for example, using wind energy and other sources to generate electricity.

The impact of new buildings on the environment will be the most minimal of all Apple data centers operating today. Located in Irish County Galway and the central Danish region of Jutland, the centers will provide Apple online services, including iTunes Store, the App Store, iMessage, Maps and Siri, for users across Europe. Both data centers with a total area of ‚Äã‚Äã166,000 square meters are designed way to bring added value to the community at locations.

As part of the project in Irish Athenry, Apple will restore land to plant native tree species in the Derridonnele Forest, which was previously used to grow alien species of trees. The project also includes an open educational space for local schools and a walking path for residents of the area.

In the Danish city of Viborg, no additional generators will be required, taking into account the location near one of the largest electrical substations in Denmark. The complex is designed to absorb excess heat from the equipment inside the center and direct it to the local heating system to heat nearby houses.

How does the Cyclone Mark V engine work?


The Cyclone engine (external combustion engine) operates on the Rankine Cycle principle with the improved Schoell Cycle. It produces mechanical energy from external heat, heating and then cooling water in a closed system.

  • Heat
The fuel is injected into the centrifugal combustion chamber, where it ignites due to spark plugs. Several open flame burners are mounted on a platform rotating around the boiler. The temperature level inside the chamber is controlled by thermocouples.
Water is heated to a temperature above 1100 degrees Celsius for 5 seconds. Then steam is supplied to the engine cylinders through the valve system. The valves regulate the portion of superheated steam entering the cylinders - the larger it is, the higher the power and torque at the outlet.
  • Mechanics
Under pressure up to 3200psi steam enters six radially located cylinders and presses on the pistons. Moreover, in this case, water is used both as a working fluid and as a lubricant.
  • Cooling
Exhaust steam escapes from the cylinders through the outlets and reaches the condenser, where it turns into water and flows into the sump. Then the water enters the boiler again.
  • The process is closed.

The head of Mangrove Capital Partners answered the questions of habra people

David Waroquier , Head of Mangrove Capital Partners, responded with interest to the proposal of the Faculty of Management in the field of Internet technologies to talk with you and answer your questions.

What do you think about the impact of the global crisis on the Internet industry? Who will be hard, and who - all the same?

For the Internet industry, the crisis will be expressed primarily in the complication of the process of obtaining investment. Startups will have a much more serious approach to the business models of their projects, especially if these business models will be associated with making profit from advertising. Since it is advertising budgets that will suffer during a crisis. Startups in the early stages of development will have to demonstrate opportunities for monetization. From projects at later stages of development, investors will expect certain results that they have achieved in monetization. All this will lead to stronger and more thoughtful business plans that the investor will receive.

How much do you think the level of investment in Internet companies will fall in 2009? (in comparison with 2008).

In general, the number of investment funds will decrease, some players will disappear from the market. Of course, we will continue our investments :) The remaining investors will begin to impose more stringent requirements and project selection criteria.

What will be the main trend next year? What will be the chip?

The worlds of the Internet and mobile communications will become increasingly intertwined. We will see a huge amount of innovation in this area.

What is Mangrove Capital Partners?

This is a venture capital fund that provides start-up investment capital. The company is based in Luxembourg.

What do you do?

We mainly invest in the early stages of the development of Internet projects. We invest in the Internet, software, Internet media, mobile applications. We work mainly with Europe and Russia, there are investment projects in America.

How does the fund intend to exit Russian investments?

In most cases, we sell our share. Less often by conducting an IPO (initial public offering).

What percentage of shares remains with the founders at the time the fund leaves the project? Concrete, if possible.

Too general question. There are no rules here. Each specific case, business project is considered separately. But in each case, we understand that if the founder receives few shares, he has no desire to work, fight. Therefore, we try to provide our startups with a fair amount of share to maintain motivation.

Investors and entrepreneur is a team. And the role of the investor, including maintaining the motivation of the creator.

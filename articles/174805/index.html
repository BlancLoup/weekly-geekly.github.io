Russian accounts in the educational process and on the tables of accountants



You will be incredibly surprised, but computers did not always exist - before they were invented, for at least two and a half centuries, our ancestors-accountants used the so-called Russian accounts.
Under the cut are old drawings and photographs, clearly demonstrating how this happened.

Let us begin with the end of the nineteenth century: earlier photographs are absent, since it was tight then with photographic apparatuses.
Exemplary workplace accountant - drawing from the magazine "Accounting" (the first Russian accounting journal, by the way).



And this is a bookkeeping lesson at the Moscow Commercial School, a photograph from the jubilee edition of 1904. There they are, on the teacher's desk.



Another well-known educational institution is the Moscow Practical Academy of Commercial Sciences. Also from the jubilee edition, but already from 1910. The teacher is a very famous person in accounting circles: Alexander Vasilyevich Prokofiev, the author of the course in double-entry bookkeeping, reprinted at least twenty times (well, boring, probably!).



And this is the audience of the St. Petersburg higher commercial accounting and railway courses of Mikhail Vladimirovich Pobedinsky, also a very famous person. Somewhere in 1914 or a little earlier. On the tables, of course, accounts - where without them?



The revolution did away with the royal educational institutions, but was unable to do away with the Russian accounts. Look, the bills on the tables of the workers of Chrezuchet - there was such an office in Petrograd, which intended to take into account all the property of the revolutionary city down to the last nail.



One of the few non-covered revolutions of educational institutions were Yekaterinoslav courses "Polyglot". An indulgence came out because courses were formed literally several days before the October Revolution. Photo from the anniversary edition of 1927



Of course, the accounts used not only in the educational process, but also in practice.
Picture from an accounting journal (which, not to mention, the end of the 1920s). In general, if the artist wanted to draw an accountant, he painted a man with bills, and everything became clear.



And this is the account office of the Leningrad Regional Industrial Economic College, 1928. Accountants are trained.



The above-mentioned courses "Polyglot", but a few years later and renamed the Azov-Black Sea Training Complex. On the right hand of future accountants - Russian accounts, and on the left - calculators. The competition between gadgets is in full swing, and its outcome is completely unclear.

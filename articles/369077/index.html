Ilon Musk: the first step of the Falcon 9 that returned in May can no longer be launched into space

It received too much damage to the hull due to high launch speeds and entry into the atmosphere.



Ilon Musk, the head of SpaceX, said in his Twitter that the first stage of the Falcon 9 launch vehicle rocket, which returned on May 6 , cannot be used to re-launch. Step suffered too much damage. True, Musk says that it is useful for studying the characteristics of the take-off and landing of rockets. The stage can be used for comparative diagnostics of other returned missiles.

In general, nothing good was expected from the current landing, since the rocket was supposed to deliver a JCSAT 14 communications satellite with a mass of 5 tons to a geostationary orbit. Due to the fact that the rocket had to fly higher than in any of the previous launches, there was much less fuel for landing than ever before. But the company's specialists coped with this task, and the return stage without any problems sat down on the sea platform.

The Pentagon has declassified the archives of the program for the study of UFOs, which spent about $ 20 million


Yes, seeing the abbreviation "UFO", many of us imagine this object in the same way as it is shown in the picture.

Speaking of UFOs, many for some reason immediately imagine the spacecraft of aliens and the "green men" themselves, who are trying to get to the long-suffering Earth with this or that task. In fact, a UFO, an unidentified flying object, is any object that is difficult to identify. UFOs can be called, for example, an aircraft that flies in such a way that its model is simply impossible to recognize.

It has now become known that the Pentagon spent about 10 years (2002-2012) and $ 22 million studying various UFOs. This was an unofficial program for detecting aviation threats. That is, the military did not search for brothers in mind, but simply tried to find out how great the threat of destruction or damage to the aircraft in the air as a result of the impact of a UFO (for example, a collision).

Reportedly, the program began to be implemented by the Americans in part because they heard rumors about launching similar projects from China. Of course, the United States wanted to be the first. The initiator of the creation of the American program was the representative of the Democratic Party in the US Senate, Harry Reid. Congressmen at one time attached great importance to the fact that they represent a UFO, which sometimes came into the lens of not only an amateur camera, but also the field of view of military pilots.

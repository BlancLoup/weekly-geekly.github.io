Google, IBM, Mellanox, NVIDIA and Tyan form the data center solution development team



Google, IBM, Mellanox, NVIDIA and Tyan have announced plans to form the OpenPOWER Consortium, an open consortium of developers of solutions based on IBM's POWER technology. The task of unification is to create advanced server, network, storage and processor technologies in order to provide greater freedom of choice for developers of large-scale and cloud-based data centers of the new generation.

The initiative for the first time offers POWER hardware and software for use in open source development, as well as intellectual property for licensing, thereby significantly expanding the circle of developers on this platform. The consortium will provide participants with the opportunity to use software and hardware tools POWER, responsible for managing the basic functions of the chip. Thus, the OpenPOWER Consortium will provide developers with exceptional flexibility in the process of creating new server hardware configurations for various levels of computational load.

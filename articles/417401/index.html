Summary: both devices are impressed with their accuracy, functionality and price at the same time, which is rare. The AN8001 is compact enough to fit in a pocket, the AN870 has a full-sized handheld form factor. As a minus of the AN8001, automatic range switching can be noted - this is not always convenient, and some are just as annoying. However, its merits make it possible to safely write it down in the staff of laboratory instruments. AN870 is devoid of this disadvantage - it has both automatic and manual range switching. The price is 2.5 times higher than the younger brother can scare away a simple radio amateur, but for the laboratory it is an excellent device for little money.

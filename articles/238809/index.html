20 ways to scam when buying an iPhone

When we sorted out the iPhone 6 on September 19, provided by one bold online store, its owner Igor told us amazing stories about the scams associated with this popular gadget. The stories were as exciting as sad. After the epic with analysis and collection ended, I asked Igor to tell all the methods of cheating known to him, of which there were as many as twenty pieces. Most of them concern iPhone buyers, but some also apply to sellers, who are also trying to deceive with irrelevant regularity.



Following the results of the post, which I hope will help you avoid many troubles when buying gadgets. Moreover, the tricks of fraudsters are universal, and can be applied not only in operations with Apple products.

Everything, as in decent stores. Then you are promised delivery within two hours, payment after a complete check of the device, and even artistic cutting of the sim card.

After that follows a sly trick with ears. The manager explains that due to the frequent attempts of courier robberies and payments with counterfeit money, you will have to pay for the iPhone through your personal account replenishment, and a check with a protection code, without which they will not be able to withdraw your money, must be delivered to the courier. Method 2. A large online store sells iPhones with a big discount on prepayment.

Unfortunately, even well-known stores that have proven themselves over the years use prepayments for mercenary purposes. It happens like this. A 50% retracement is announced, but with 100% prepayment and delivery in three weeks. The fame of the store and the low price for the device pushes people to line up to give their money. When you call three weeks later to clarify whether you can pick up your telephone, they explain to you that due to the very high number of completed orders, the company does not cope with the delivery of such volumes, does not fit on time and carries your order for another 2-3 weeks. This is allowed by the terms of the contract, which you most likely did not read very carefully. After 2-3 weeks, the story repeats, but the cause of the delay may change. Theoretically, you can start the money-back procedure, but you are told that the iPhone is close, and you have to wait quite a bit. Well, right here a drop. Some give in to persuasion, others do not. The story continues until the buyer has run out of patience. But even if he immediately goes to draw up a waiver, he learns that under the terms of the contract (who reads these contracts?), The funds will be returned within a month, and in some cases, with the retention of a certain commission. Method 3. Sale of "restored" iPhone under the guise of new.

Often in the description of iPhones the words refurbished, ref, or simply "ref" are found. Some sellers simply write this without explaining what the designation is, and some stores claim total novelty. They say that the device is completely new, sealed, etc. For those who still do not know what "ref", I will explain. Vendors will talk about the iPhone, which the disgruntled buyers returned directly to the Apple Store, sent them to the factory, carefully tested, re-packaged and now sell at a big discount. In fact, everything is a little sadder. In Russian conditions, "ref" is an iPhone with low-quality Chinese parts. Chinese everything is there - the screen, speaker, microphone, cables, antenna, bolts, etc. Everything except the motherboard, so that the operating system was original. The percentage of defects in such masterpieces tends to 100 percent. Usually they live for a couple of months before the repair. ‚ÄúRefs‚Äù are sold everywhere, but there are a lot of them according to the ads from private traders.

Our experience

Every day Odnoklassniki‚Äôs social network is used by more than 40 million people. And about 150 thousand of them stumbles upon page 404. The share is tiny, only 0.37% of the total number of visitors. But in absolute terms, a very decent attendance. ‚ÄúYour energy would be for peaceful purposes,‚Äù we thought, and decided to use this traffic well. At that time, page 404 looked like this:



There was no point in just making another designer page. After much thought for half an hour, the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãa charitable campaign in favor of WWF was proposed. Nature must be protected - it is known from school. But this topic is very extensive, a bit too much for the unfortunate 404 pages. Therefore, we set ourselves three tasks:
  • remind people of the extinction of rare species of animals;
  • introduce closer to the WWF Wildlife Conservation Fund;
  • give visitors the opportunity to make a personal contribution to the preservation of the global natural ecosystem.

As a result, the new 404 page looked like this:



Above came the text of the error itself, so that the user immediately understood where he went. Next to the Odnoklassniki logo we placed the WWF logo. Below was an information block consisting of a photograph of a representative of an endangered species (a small selection was assembled) and a brief description. In addition, from this page led to the official WWF group in Odnoklassniki. The first and second tasks were solved. And for the third task, we placed in the WWF group a special iframe, through which visitors could get to the site of the foundation itself and make a donation.



The result exceeded our expectations - about 50% of random visitors to page 404 followed the link to the WWF group. This project lasted one month, and at the time of its completion the group grew to 30 thousand people. Moreover, according to the WWF itself, during this month, Odnoklassniki users made about 7% of all donations. However, in reality, this proportion was most likely much higher. The fact is that, going from Odnoklassniki to the WWF site, not all visitors immediately donated. Some returned later, but directly, and not in a roundabout way.

The success of our project and the recognition of the general public, including in the form of foreign media , inspired us to further achievements. Our next 404-project was a charity event in favor of the Foundation for Change one life.



This is a fund to assist in the adoption of orphans. We have kept the same page structure: logo, photo, description. And in just two weeks that this project lasted, there were 18 appeals from potential adoptive parents. And the first children were adopted a month after the start of the project.

The experience and results obtained in the course of these two projects have demonstrated the undervaluation of such a tool as page 404. Of course, its potential depends solely on attendance. And let it better bring a small, but good, than it serves only as a beautiful stub.

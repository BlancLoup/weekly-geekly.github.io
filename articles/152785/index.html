Microsoft: the "crisis of geniuses" has come, engineers are worth their weight in gold, including foreign ones



Brad Smith, executive vice president of Microsoft and general adviser to the company, said at a press conference for journalists after Microsoft applied to the US Congress about visa policies for certain emigrants, he noted the following: "... the lack of qualified specialists have reached such a level that we can talk about the crisis of geniuses for high-tech companies. "

In connection with this situation, Microsoft intends to lobby in the US Congress for easing visa policy for highly educated engineering personnel who would like to get a new job in the US and invest millions of dollars in new education programs in technology, engineering and mathematics (what is called in the US STEM - Science, Technology, Engineering, Math). The same program also includes the allocation of funds for the preparation of teachers for schools who are well acquainted with modern computer technology.

According to Smith, Microsoft itself has more than 3,400 vacancies for researchers, developers and engineers that the company hopes to fill as quickly as possible at the expense of foreign specialists, for which it is ready to finance the distribution of 20,000 green cards from the US government federal reserve (each cost $ 15,000 for the company).

The bill submitted by Microsoft will be considered by the US Congress next year.

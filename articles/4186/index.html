Graphic spam clogs communication channels

On some days, the level of graphical spam jumps up to 30% in the entire volume of spam traffic, Commtouch reports. True, such ‚Äúcritical‚Äù days happen infrequently. For example, on May 29, the level of graphical spam was 30%, and the total volume of spam traffic increased by 70%. On June 24, spam and traffic increased by 15% and 35%, respectively. All these figures make it clear that even a slight increase in the proportion of graphic spam greatly affects the amount of spam traffic.

Perhaps it is the spread of graphic spam that is associated with the first in a long time increase in spam in mail traffic, which was noticed by MessageLabs in June 2006. In 2006, the level of spam was 60.4%, which is 7.8 points lower than last year. The cause of the June surge may be just graphic spam.

The problem is that the average size of a text spam letter is 5.5 kilobytes, and the graphic one is 18 kilobytes, that is, more than three times as much. Therefore, graphical spam becomes a headache for telecommunication and mail providers, as well as for companies that store an archive of email messages of their employees on internal servers.

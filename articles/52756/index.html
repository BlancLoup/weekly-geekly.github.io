SMS voting is a way to get around piracy

Take, for example, the film Slumdog Millionaire, which brought together more than $ 100 million to its creators in the cinemas of big cities. What did the inhabitants of the province, where there are not only cinemas, but also the legal sale of DVDs? Well, of course - they took out disks from friends or downloaded from the Internet, multiplying the headache of media corporations.

But most of the same slum dwellers already have a mobile phone and still have a TV. But on the box the film will be shown only in a year or two. In vain video content makers miss such a huge potential.

Maybe they need to take a chance and allow the premiere on television with an indication of a short number in the corner of the screen to send thank-you SMS (as in Eurovision) and an appeal to vote for the film.



Mobile operators, TV channels, producers and, first of all, viewers who have watched a new film may be interested in such a scheme, without feeling themselves at the same time breaking the law.

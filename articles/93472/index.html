Thermalright X-Silent 140

In the old metal case after installing terrabaytnik became hot. However, there was not particularly cool there before - however, after reading some comments , I decided to take care of my favorite middle-aged children.


I didn‚Äôt want to change the body at all, so it was decided to make fun of the wall - cut a hole and install a case fan. Honestly, I didn‚Äôt specifically choose a model - I just came to the nearest shop and asked to show everything that is in stock. The X-Silent 140 charmed with its size and price.

Specs from the box:
Dimensions: 140x140x25 mm
Bearing Type: Liquid state
Start-up Voltage: 7.0 VDC
Rated Current: 0.14 A (Max)
Rated Power: 1.68 W (Max)
Rated Speed: 900RPM
Air Flow: 60.42 CFM
Noise Level: 20.9 dBA
Weight: 170 grams
Life Expectancy: 50,000 hours

We installed it in the front of the case, almost at the level of the installed hard drives, by blowing.
Included are comfortable rubber feet for mounting. The fan can be powered both from the motherboard and from the power supply unit (adapter included). I have it powered from the power supply, and from the external, with a separate button, bolted to the wall behind the case (the external external power supply was originally conceived for external hard drives, but was not useful in this role).



Impressions

The fan is really very quiet . It is not audible at all, although my body is on the table, not under the table. Due to the mounting on the rubber bands can not hear any vibrations.

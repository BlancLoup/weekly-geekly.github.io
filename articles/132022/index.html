Adidas website hacked



Malefactors acting on the Web are now more active than ever. This year is generally rich in various events related to information security. All sorts of different incidents, starting with the appearance of the Stuxnet worm and ending with the reusable hacking of various Sony services, all this happened this very year. Now another large company has suffered from the actions of hackers (meaning the negative meaning of this term). Adidas turned off its own website on Sunday evening, after hackers launched a successful attack.

At the time of writing the news site Adidas is not working, so as far as can be judged, the attack is really large-scale. Currently, all available information is contained in a published appeal by the Adidas management, which says that the company‚Äôs experts are investigating the incident. It all started on November 3rd, when, according to company representatives, hackers were able to access the CMS management of the main site. The attack affected the company's online stores, plus Reebok subsidiary services. Almost immediately after the discovery of the fact of the attack, the site was disabled. Now some services and affiliated sites are gradually returning to the online, but the main site of the company is still not working.

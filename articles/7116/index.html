Why are we moving from Windows to Linux?

1. Moral considerations about licensed software
2. WindowsXP Pro + MSOffice Pro + a lot of little things are expensive
3. Many say (and I do not believe) that Linux is better.
4. Everyone knows this reason, because of her and we are moving.

But how to do it?


Initial situation:
20 Windows machines with an office and many other nice and useful programs.
2 servers for Linux (Internet gateway and file server), which were set up 1.5 years ago by an expert who came in and didn‚Äôt touch them.
7 very different printers, incl. old and A3 color two-sided, steam scanners.
Lack of system administrator. His functions are performed by a former webmaster, burdened with a host of other tasks.
20 users who somehow mastered Windows many years ago.

Final situation:
Optimistic option
3 machines under license Windows (accounting and backup), on the same machine also a licensed office (just in case).
The remaining 17 machines under Linux.

Pessimistic option
19 machines under Windows, one under Linux. OpenOffice about half the machines.

Solution options
The best option.
Find an organization that competently and quickly install and configure the system, teach (1-2 introductory classes) users, will provide support remotely and on departure. I did not find such an organization (or person), if anyone knows, tell me, please.

A simpler and cheaper option.
Purchase licensed Windows, and even better Get Genuine Kit (Legalizer) / Attention! Link to the Microsoft website! do not go nervously related! /.
This package (not to be confused with the free distributed among the shares, here at the full price of the purchase), as promised (but I do not believe) allows you to simply replace the Windows key without reinstalling the system. If anyone has experience, is it true?

The most time consuming and expensive option:
Download / buy Linux. Install it on several workstations, select the accompanying software, test its work and user reactions, then continue the expansion.

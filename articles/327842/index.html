MP3 finally goes public domain



Perhaps many did not think about it, but the mp3 standard, so well known to everyone, had problems with patent clearance. On April 23, 2017, the latest patents expired and the format became finally free. Technicolor has officially stopped licensing fees from software manufacturers and embedded solutions.

Licensing


Despite the fact that mp3 hardware decoding is built into every second coffee maker, until recently its use in commercial projects required license fees in favor of the developer - Fraunhofer Society. Only in 2005, the amount of deductions amounted to one hundred million euros. Most patents lost their power in the European Union back in 2012. However, some of them still continued to operate in the United States due to the peculiarities of local legislation. What does this news give to the community? At least now it will be possible to compile Gentoo and listen to music simultaneously at once in the base distribution. Many distributions will be able to transfer support for the standard to the main repository. Now, for example, the same Ubuntu requires the installation of non-free components from a separate ubuntu-restricted-extras meta package to support mp3.

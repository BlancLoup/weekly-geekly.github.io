21 million measurements showed who has the fastest internet access in Moscow

December 2, 2014

Following the analysis of the speed and quality of mobile operators in Moscow ( news from TelecomDaily dated 07/02/2014 ), VIGO, together with leading online cinemas in the country, conducted an analysis of the quality of fixed Internet in Moscow.

The technique is based on data obtained using the technology platform directly from the largest Russian video services. VIGO specialists in real-time mode ‚Äúsee‚Äù with what speed and quality the subscribers of Moscow Internet providers are watching online video.

According to the results of 20.5 million measurements from August to October, occurring automatically with each video preview in the Moscow region (Moscow and the region), 2KOM subscribers have the best conditions in the field of speed of access to video content. The average speed of their video viewing is 13.6 Mbit / s. Users of this company less often than others stop watching online video, without waiting for it to download.
The worst indicators of the average speed of the provider MGTS, whose subscribers watch online - video at an average speed of 7.8 Mbit / s and more often than others interrupt video viewing.

About VIGO

VIGO is working on new technologies in the field of mobile data transfer. The company's products are based on the concept of finding solutions to existing industry challenges through cooperation between Internet services and mobile operators. In November 2013, VIGO introduced UXZoom, the first method of measuring the quality of mobile Internet, based on video services data.

Moscow. Rating of fixed operators by average speed and VDI


VIGO introduces the concept of the Integrated Video Delivery Indicator for the first time. The advantage of VDI in its ability to take into account both the traditional technological parameter ‚Äî the average speed, and the user characteristics of the quality of video viewing ‚Äî the proportion of views interrupted by the subscribers themselves at the time of buffering (video trails).

VDI (Video Delivery Indicator) is equal to the ratio of average speed to average share of video tracks. Video clips characterize the extreme degree of dissatisfaction of the subscriber with the quality of video viewing.

According to the results of 20.5 million measurements carried out from August to October of the current year in Moscow, the subscribers of the operators 2KOM, Smile, Iskratelekom, GorKom, VimpelCom and Megafon have the best conditions in terms of the quality of online video delivery to CNNN.

A small text report on SIGGRAPH 2012

This year SIGGRAPH was held in Los Angeles. The venue, Convention Center, is slightly different in atmosphere from the city to the best. Everywhere is clean and tidy, there is a large buffet with different fast food.

The exposition itself opened only on Tuesday, but besides it there were pavilions with representatives of small caliber and indie projects. Most of the reports were available only to holders of tickets like Full Conference, I had Basic Conference Plus for $ 300. And the closer the moment of buying a ticket to the date of the conference, the significantly higher the price rises. After the purchase, they send a PDF invitation, which makes it easier to get a visa.

It was very uncomfortable to listen to Russian speakers who did not even try to portray the normal pronunciation. True, such criticism is valid for many non-English-speaking speakers from different countries. One even read a piece of paper in such a plaintive voice, as if with a muzzle at his temple.

I would like to mention our two figures, who decided to surprise the public Motion Capture with the help of Asus Xtion (analogue of Kinect). One of them was a representative and the second (Vasily) was a technician. I started a conversation with one of them, as after 20 seconds an elderly lady approached him and he simply turned away from me and began talking to her. Apparently wants to have the maximum benefit from a place at the conference. In general, about half of the indie know-how was implemented using Kinect. Most of the solutions were presented in a rather raw form. There were several computers with connected Wacom Intuos 5. None of them had drivers installed, so the pen simply replaced the mouse pointer, making it impossible to use additional degrees of freedom.

In the same hall, they demonstrated a system similar to the one used to create animations of faces in the game LA Noire. But here, too, only the process of capturing images was shown, and the calculation of the 3d grid was no longer deepened.

Further down the corridor were extensively represented by 3d printers from MakerBot. After studying the "prints", we can say that after all this inkjet technology is very bad at hanging sites.

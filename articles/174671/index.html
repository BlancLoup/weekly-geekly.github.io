Theory


Once, while still a student, I thought about technological progress. Then the processors just started to build cores. And in some incomprehensible way, my thoughts were crossed into the idea that the human brain was multi-core deprived. But how cool it would be to be able to use human input devices from several different consciousnesses. Each of the consciousnesses could cognize the world, passing data through itself and creating neural connections in the brain for their memorization, understanding and awareness. Several consciousnesses in one physical brain could use different eyes for different purposes, from the outside it would look like reading two books at the same time, or one of the eyes would look at House and the other would read lectures. In this case, one of the consciousnesses would rest, and the other processed the material, forming more and more new connections. Then the resting and working consciousness could change.

It is possible that cohabiting consciousness in the beginning would be less effective than the one. But gradual development would bear fruit in the form of a higher total productivity of several consciousnesses. After all, everyone has room to grow, at least the record was set by a single consciousness.

The first option seems to be more secure (with a split consciousness, a direct road to the psychiatric hospital, or are we already there?). The second option is more likely to succeed because it is unclear how to cultivate the second consciousness from the embryo, how to train it? Do you disconnect the first consciousness? It was decided to try the splitting of consciousness. It remained only to choose the method and start the experiment.

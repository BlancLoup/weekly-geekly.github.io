Let's Encrypt is now trusted



We are pleased to announce that we have received cross-signatures from IdenTrust . This means that now our certificates are trusted in most major browsers. This is a significant milestone: now all visitors to websites on which Let's Encrypt certificate is installed will be able to enjoy safe browsing of web pages without any additional settings.

Both of the intermediate Let Encrypt intermediate certificates (Let's Encrypt Authority X1 and Let's Encrypt Authority X2) received cross-signatures. Web servers now need to be configured to maintain a suitable certificate cross-signature. Let's Encrypt client will do this automatically.

You can look at an example of a server that uses Let's Encrypt certificate using this link .

Important personal and commercial information circulates over the Internet with high intensity like never before, and it's time to encrypt it all. Therefore, we created Let's Encrypt and we are glad that we have made a big step that will help bring secure connections to every corner of the network.

Josh Aas, Executive Director, ISRG

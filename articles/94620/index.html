New Android smartphones with QWERTY-keyboard: HTC MyTouch 3G Slide, HTC Aria, HTC Vision

Following the monoblock MyTouch 3G (HTC Magic nee), the T-Mobile operator on June 2, 2010 launches a new phone in the line - MyTouch 3G Slide. MyTouch 3G Slide was developed by HTC , not Motorolla as you might think while looking at the picture. At the moment, it is known that Slide will have a 3.4 "capacitive screen with a resolution of 320x480 pixels, a 5 megapixel camera with autofocus and flash, a battery with a capacity of 1300 mA / h. According to rumors, the phone will be built on the HTC Legend platform, which means the heart of Slide will serve On-Chip Qualcomm MSM7227 with a processor architecture of ARMv6 and a frequency of 600 MHz, also the novelty will have ~ 512 MB of ROM and ~ 384 MB of RAM.

The MyTouch 3G Slide will be running Android 2.1 Eclair and the HTC Espresso interface, which is actually a customized version of HTC Sense for T-Mobile.


Slide is an operator model, sales of which are not planned in Europe, so all hope is on eBay. Expected sales of white, black and red versions of the phone. Look at the interface HTC Espresso, you can now:


No less interesting, it looks like a future novelty from the operator AT & T HTC Aria. So far nothing is known about this device, except for the announcement date - June 7th. Presumably, the phone is based on the same hardware platform as the HTC Legend MSM7227 600 MHz, ~ 512 MB of ROM and ~ 384 MB of RAM. Unlike the MyTouch 3G Slide Aria, it looks more strictly and has an optical scroll.



- It's all good, but what about Snapdragon? - you say. For fans of powerful devices with a QWERTY-keyboard, HTC also prepares a surprise. In the xml files of the new Android builds, a profile was found that points to a device with a 1 GHz QSD8250 processor, a display with a resolution of 480x800 and the codename HTC Vision. It is likely that the novelty will be built on the same hardware platform as the HTC Desire, which means we are waiting for a very interesting device.

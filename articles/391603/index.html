VR & ASMR


Virtual reality technology is gradually penetrating new areas of work and leisure of humanity. There was no exception and the direction of videos for relaxation ASMR. YouTube has included support for 360 ¬∞ video, and ASMR artists have new cameras at their disposal. But for now, these videos are experiments and a search for a new format. I suggest you look at the path that VR ASMR managed to make.

Small educational program


If you first hear the abbreviation ASMR, I offer you a very brief explanation of what it is. An autonomous sensory meridional response (Autonomous sensory meridian response) is a pleasant tingling sensation, goose bumps, spreading over the skin, usually from the back of the head, and arising in response to specific triggers. A simple test of your sensitivity to ASMR is this video:



According to the results of a survey on Geektimes from ASMR video, 15% felt very pleasant, 31% described their feelings as pleasant, 20% did not like the video or even caused fear / disgust. The remaining 34% did not feel anything. Despite the small amount of sampling here, the presence of pleasant sensations is confirmed by the fact that ASMR-videos are popular, gaining millions of views on YouTube.
Triggers can be whispers, tapping, rustling, smooth movements of the hands, role-playing games with the care of the viewer (for example, haircut). ASMR is used to relax, improve falling asleep or just to get pleasant sensations. Despite the fact that ASMR is already writing scientific articles and even dissertations, its mechanism of action has not yet been disclosed.

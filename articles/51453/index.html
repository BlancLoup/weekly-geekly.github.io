Tel., Fax, e-mail and www

Today I want to make out examples of writing blocks with contact information. I do not pretend to be the ultimate truth, but I want to share different ideas. And how to apply them you can decide for yourself.

And so, the first thing you need to build on when creating a slide with contacts is the context. The context in which the reader (viewer) will see this slide.

The rule is simple, for example, imagine you are at a big conference, someone speaks, at the end there is a block for communication and there you see:

Of course, I exaggerate a little, but in general this is the case. Pile up a large amount of unnecessary information. Well, who, being at the conference, will remember it all? At best, what is the name

We start to simplify:

1. Patronymic - in my opinion it is an anachronism. Name and Surname is enough (now they pounce on me, but what about respect for age, etc. I think everyone decides for himself, I often met with age people in their practice who calmly communicate with you and by name)

2. Position - significantly cut, for example, to ‚ÄúSenior Director‚Äù. In each situation, its reduction, but I want to convey the logic.

4. Address - I would remove it altogether. Well, the maximum city (or country if an international conference)

5. Phones, faxes - also controversial, would remove all. Through the site they will find everything they need) (if the site is of course made convenient :))

7. www - kill the www prefix and it is also clear that this is a site!

Southbridge: we develop the useful and remove the unnecessary


Our company lacks something unusual: we rent an office in the Voronezh region, hire people from distant cities of Russia, conclude with NDA employees for 10 years, write and publish opensource-plugins for Redmine.


In a few sketches, we show what we are guided by and how we treat our customers.



Utilitarian approach


Have you ever thought to install an Olympic standard swimming pool in your office? Huge expenses will fall on buyers, and your business will lose competitiveness, but you will have a pool. And you can save on air conditioning for the server.


This ridiculous example shows well what we are trying to avoid. Our philosophy: allocate more funds to important things, save on unnecessary ones. And then we will beat the others in quality and price.


Why we do not have an office in Moscow


When I grew up to create my own company and started hiring employees, we rented an office in Moscow. Five years of office life have shown: customers need our office last. Over the years, almost three people have made it difficult to see where we are and what we live.


On the other hand, it was becoming more and more difficult to find people in the Moscow office. The demand for admins increased, the number of applicants fell all the time, and their salary expectations grew. Meanwhile, the province regularly supplied us with people with the highest qualifications for reasonable money.


Therefore, we refused to have an office in Moscow and finally switched to remote hiring, and the money freed up went to the wage fund.


Our advantage: we hire the best specialists from all over the immense Russia, while keeping prices at a reasonable level.


Personal remark: I get great pleasure when among the hundreds of resumes I find THAT employee (careful, attentive, structural), and he joins our team.

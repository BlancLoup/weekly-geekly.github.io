Google tests Hands Free mobile payments with face recognition

Imagine that at the checkout in the store you can pay with one voice command, without interrupting the folding of products into packages. On the street, take a hotdog, stopping for a few seconds at the stall and saying: "Pay Google, goodbye." No need to rummage through your pockets in search of a wallet, plastic card or trivia. You can buy some water during your morning run, when you never took a purse with you.

Approximately the way the new mobile payment system Hands Free , which began to test the company Google.

The pilot project was launched in a small number of fast-food restaurants McDonald's, Papa John's and small eateries in the suburb of San Francisco (South Bay).

Hands Free works on Android 4.2+ and iOS 4S +. It is assumed that the smartphone is in active mode, ready to accept a voice command and is nearby: for example, it is attached to a hand or lies in a pocket.

To use Hands Free, you need to install a mobile application ( Android , iOS ). It determines via the WiFi and geolocation services that the store participates in the pilot program. When calculating at the checkout, the buyer says the code phrase "I'll pay with Google", the seller asks for initials and checks his face with the picture in the profile Hands Free.



In some stores, Google is experimenting even with a face recognition system, so the calculation at the checkout happens even faster. There is a camera installed that checks the face with a photo. Google stresses that all photos and videos taken by the camera are immediately deleted. Big brother is resting, more precisely, until he only flexes his muscles.

For security, after each purchase immediately comes a text message on the phone.

Google invites all South Bay residents to join in testing innovative payment systems. To encourage participation in the program, Google gives a discount of up to $ 5 on the first purchase without the use of hands.

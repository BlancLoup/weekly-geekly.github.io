The war for talent is a myth


Marketing genius Seth Godin says this is actually a battle for attitude.
In reality, this is not a search for talent. This is a search approach. There are not many activities where only pure skills are required. Perhaps this is good for the first violin in the string quartet. But, in fact, even there the winners from the losers are distinguished not by talent, but by mood.

What does Harvard call item number 1 on a salary interview?

First you need to like them. This is the first component. The more you do something that kills their sympathy for you, the less chance you have of achieving the desired ...

No, attitude is not everything. Of course, there is experience, education, and other factors, but you will be surprised to know how few some of them mean.

Hard work? Ha! He is overvalued ...

Jeffrey Pfeier, an MBA professor at Stanford, explains: according to research results, productivity is very weakly related to career advancement.

The data show that productivity does not have such a strong influence on what happens to most people in many organizations. What matters is the effect your virtues produce. It affects the universal assessment of the quality of work, and even on your seniority and career prospects.

Studies show that sympathies affect certification much more than actual performance.
In an experimental study of the assessments of business qualities obtained by people, it was revealed: those who are able to make a positive impression received higher scores than people who actually work better, but who are not inclined to control their opinions about themselves.

The more people in the office have friends, the better they work there.

The Massachusetts Institute of Technology has spent a whole year monitoring 2,600 workers. Researchers studied their social connections and even used mathematical formulas to analyze the size and composition of their address books and lists of friends. As a result, it became clear: the higher the IBM employees had a level of social adaptation, the better they worked. Scientists even calculated the difference: on average, each email address added $ 948 to income.

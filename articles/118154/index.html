Sony introduced its Android tablets

Today at a press conference in Tokyo, Sony showed two of its new tablet based on Android 3.0.



The Sony S1 is a 9.4 inch tablet that looks like a magazine. The tablet is built on a hardware platform Tegra 2, has 2 cameras and an infrared port for controlling household appliances.







Sony S2 is made in the form of a book with two 5.5 inch screens with a resolution of 1024x480. It also has a camera and is built on Tegra 2. The interface is made so that you can type on the lower screen, and the email client will be displayed on the top.







Both tablets will have support for PlayStation Certified, DLNA, WiFi and 3G / 4G.

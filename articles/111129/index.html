New Year's silicon

Every new year, Intel traditionally begins with the announcement of a new line of processors ... and, you guessed it, this year was no exception. January 6 at the international exhibition Consumer Electronic Show (CES 2011) in Las Vegas will be the official opening of the second generation of Core processors, codenamed Sandy Bridge .




Key points :

New processors will be in all families - Core i3, i5 and i7.



The very first light will see quad-core processors for laptops - they should appear already on January 9th. These are high-performance systems from manufacturers such as HP and Dell. Dual-core systems will appear no earlier than February.

Improved energy efficiency thanks to a new 32-nanometer process technology;

If the Nehalem microarchitecture processors, produced by the 32nm process, the graphics core was produced using the 45nm technology and packaged in a single case, then in Sandy Bridge the graphics processor is placed on the same silicon substrate with the central processor, which means that -nanometer process. With this solution, it became possible to "squeeze" more of the technology Intel Turbo Boost.



The new version of the technology Intel Wireless Display , which allows to transfer full-size FullHD-video over TVs and projectors over the wireless interface. Stereoscopic 3D Blu-ray video playback via HDMI 1.4 thanks to Intel InTru 3D technology.

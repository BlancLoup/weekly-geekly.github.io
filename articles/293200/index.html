Booking.com agreed to transfer the personal data of Russians to the Moscow data center of IXcellerate

The online hotel reservation service Booking.com will transfer the personal data of users to a data center owned by IXcellerate . It is located on Altufevskoe highway in Moscow. The agreement between the companies has already been reached. Booking.com will store there passport information of Russians and information about their bank cards used when booking.

Autonomous data center IXcellerate on Altufevskoe highway was opened in Moscow in 2012. The total area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe data center is 15 thousand square meters. In 2012, IFC became the company's shareholder, which invested $ 2.6 million. Since 2014, the Japanese Sumitomo Group has become the investor of the company. She invested at least $ 4 million in IXcellerate, Kommersant reports . The center employs 13 different operators.

Megamind wrote that such companies as eBay , AliExpress , Google , Lenovo and Samsung are also involved in the transfer.

The market for commercial data centers in Russia, the manager of IXcellerate, Dmitry Fokin estimates at 15,000 racks and $ 500 million. In Russia, only 30% of domestic personal data operators store data, the remaining 70% of companies will have to transfer bases from abroad, he said. Only due to localization, the market of the data exchange center in the Russian Federation will grow to $ 1.75-2 billion plus organic growth, predicts Mr. Fokin.

The law prohibiting the storage of data of Russian users in data centers outside the country, enters into force on September 1, 2015.

Perhaps you do not know, but some airlines have finally understood that when every grandmother has a smartphone, you can radically save money on the fleet and suddenly increase the level of service.

The thought of the operator is something like this: why buy a liner with a TV in each headrest (or carry out a deep modernization, if it is even possible), if movies, music and cartoons can be streamed straight into the passenger's pocket?

I love it. If only because the screens built into the chairs are terrible to say the least, and the headphones connected to them are also so-so, to be honest. I am already silent, that in an economy class there are so broken connectors that the headphones either wheeze or deaf in one ear. And yes, I still can not afford a business class.

In general, at least one airline already has such an option in the form of a Wi-Fi media server with content broadcasting to passengers' devices on the Airbus A320 and A320neo boards. I found out about this, do not believe it, from the pre-flight report of the commander of the ship, usually not carrying particularly useful information. Details, as was said - in the logbook.

Since there was nothing to do, I began the good old reading. It turned out that, firstly, you need to connect to the on-board Wi-Fi network. And, secondly, if you have a smartphone or tablet in your hands, then it would be good to have a branded app (available for iOS and Android), and if a laptop, then nothing is needed, and everything will work.

Without a branded app, access to music and books is promised. With a corporate app or on a laptop, movies and cartoons are added.

Interestingly, the app installation description for Android turned out to be different for the A320 and A320neo. For example, if you believe the instructions, then flying to the A320 app need to have time to download before departure (as well as owners of devices with iOS), but the lucky ones on the new A320neo can install the application at any time (obviously, you can download from the internal server); courage and allow installation from unknown sources.

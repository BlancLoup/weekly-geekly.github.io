What and who needs: banks


The leading edge of IT in Russia is banks. Well, at least they are definitely one of the first.

The first thing banks need is the absence of failures. Accordingly, we are talking about building infrastructure (data centers, data processing nodes, communication channels), auditing the data center after the accident, and of course, a total backup, creating fast disaster recovery sites, as well as organizing geographically distributed clusters for fast migration of services, decentralized data storage and so on.

The second is the introduction of automated service systems , the organization of direct contact with the client by means of various communication channels. Here we are talking about the construction of contact centers and their integration with customer databases (ERP, ABS, etc.), the introduction of various call center systems. For example, in Uraltransbank, where we recently upgraded the call center platform, the Avaya solution helped evenly distribute the workload between operators, reducing the average customer waiting time by 3 times.

The third is information security . Including the solution of specific tasks, for example, audits of various kinds of standards or identification of operational risks of the bank.

And finally, the provision of cloud services. More often than not, this is either IaaS or startups, that is, testing new IT systems, for the benefit of which there is no definite certainty. And there is a certain interest not only in public ‚Äúclouds‚Äù, but also in private ones. Here, for example, a topic about how a private cloud can be useful for testing new services.

Telecom


In 2013, the following trends were most noticeable in the field of telecom:

  • First of all, life in conditions of exponential growth of traffic. And this affects the mobile operators most significantly, especially considering that in Russia there are still a lot of localities where mobile communication is the only way to access the Internet. And the picture is the following: traffic is growing many times, you need to invest in network expansion (construction of 3G, LTE, etc.), while the tariffs need to be reduced, which naturally leads to the fact that the revenue of the operators suffers. Therefore, operators considered using various solutions to manage network load using traffic compression technologies, solutions to adapt it to devices, to receive conditions and subscribers' needs (to manage network load and to individual base stations).
  • Second, the RF Government Decree ‚ÄúOn Amendments to the Rules for the provision of mobile communication services‚Äù obliged operators to implement number portability ( MNP ). From December 1, the Big Three operators have already begun to provide the opportunity to switch to another operator while maintaining the number. And this requires a revision of the principle of traffic routing, changes in the network infrastructure, etc.
  • Thirdly, in connection with the entry into force of the ‚Äúanti-piracy‚Äù law, ‚Äúon blacklists‚Äù and the law ‚ÄúOn the protection of children from harmful information‚Äù, operators had to introduce appropriate solutions. Many solved this problem on the basis of already existing systems that see and process all traffic, such as Deep Packet Inspection ( DPI ), antivirus or parental control system. The filtering option based on DNS technologies is also applicable, but on the whole, solved the problem not efficiently enough, since in the standard version, besides illegal traffic, also blocks legitimate traffic, and requires additional technical refinement to prohibit only illegal content.
  • And, of course, the competition between operators is only growing, and MNP only contributes to this. Therefore, solutions for predicting the behavior of subscribers become particularly relevant. For example, in 2013 we carried out a pilot project for one large telecom operator using Big Data technology, as a result we identified subscribers who were inclined to care and showed the possibility of reducing the cost of storing information units by 15-20 times.

Lenovo's new products at CES 2011

Lenovo announces new products almost all year round, but according to tradition, a significant part of new products is announced at the January Consumer Electronics Show. It happened this year as well: Lenovo introduced the updated ThinkPad and ThinkPad Edge notebooks, almost completely updated the IdeaPad range of custom notebooks, and showed off some interesting desktop computers. In addition, several promising developments were presented at CES, including the LePad tablet on the Android platform, for which a special docking station was developed (together with a tablet that turns into a Windows-based laptop) and a portable keyboard.

In this material - a detailed story about the new personal computers Lenovo, approximate prices and dates for the start of sales in Russia. And I will start with an updated version of the laptop, which last year was perhaps the most interesting to readers of the Lenovo blog in Habr√©. This is a ThinkPad X120e - an entry-level, ultra-compact notebook that will replace the ThinkPad X100e.


Still the smallest ThinkPad

The Lenovo ThinkPad X100e was introduced at CES at the very beginning of 2010. Let me remind you that in this model, the AMD platform was used for the first time in the ThinkPad series. The design has also changed: the appearance of the X100e is still modest and solid, as is the true ThinkPad, but still different from the classic notebooks of the series. Over the past year, the X100e has established itself as an inexpensive and reliable laptop for everyday work tasks. At the end of last year, a special version of the ThinkPad X100e with a built-in 3G modem and a ‚Äúmodem‚Äù tariff appeared at the cellular operator Megafon.



There was also enough criticism: for a relatively short battery life, for low productivity and quite strong heating. During the year, these problems were gradually solved. A BIOS update provided a more efficient operation of the processor and cooling system. And the additional performance was provided by dual core AMD Turion processors, which appeared in the X100e in the middle of last year.



What's new in ThinkPad X120e? First of all, this is the new AMD Fusion platform, which provides improved performance and long battery life. The X120e will use a single-core AMD Fusion E-240 processor (1.5 GHz) or a dual-core AMD Fusion E-350 (1.6 GHz) with an integrated graphics core Radeon HD6310. According to Lenovo, the integrated graphics in the X120e provides a 65 percent performance boost compared with its predecessor. The test results of the reference system with AMD E-350 can be found in this material on AnandTech . Thanks to a thinner 40-nanometer process technology (the X100e used AMD Athlon Neo and Turion processors with a 65-nanometer process technology), the processors of the new series are more energy-efficient. Hence, the increased battery life of the X120e - up to 6.6 hours with a 6-cell battery.



The appearance has practically not changed, and this, for me, is for the better: the design of the ThinkPad X100e is very good. The X120e will still be equipped with matte screens, and in addition to all black colors it will be available in red or white. Another small, but very useful innovation is the HDMI interface, to the absence of which X100e users complained.

Lenovo Enhanced Experience 2.0 and Lenovo Rapid Boot - fast work technologies

Last year, Lenovo introduced the Lenovo Enhanced Experience program. This program was the result of cooperation between Lenovo and Microsoft: the joint work of specialists of the two companies was aimed at the fastest and stable operation of the Windows 7 operating system on Lenovo laptops and desktops. Pre-configuring the Windows distribution based on the characteristics of Lenovo computers ensured minimal system boot and shutdown times, as well as increased stability.

Introduced at CES 2011, the Enhanced Experience 2.0 program provides an additional boost in speed. According to Lenovo, a computer with EE 2.0 loads an average of 20 seconds faster than similar models of competing manufacturers. The greatest interest among the innovations in the framework of EE 2.0 technology is RapidDrive. This development was announced last year, but only now it should appear in Lenovo laptops, and in most models.

Laptops with RapidDrive will be equipped with both a regular hard drive and an additional solid-state drive with a capacity of 32 or 64 gigabytes. The software of such a laptop will automatically move the most frequently used data (for example, the operating system and installed programs) to a fast SSD, ensuring performance gains, but also without depriving the user of the storage capacity for the data. The main thing is that the process of moving data for the user will be transparent. As a result, on a laptop with RapidDrive, Windows 7 should load in just 10 seconds!

So far, it is known that RapidDrive will be optionally installed on ThinkPad Edge and IdeaPad Y-series notebooks. And there will be on sale both laptops with an additional SSD, and the usual modifications only with a hard disk.

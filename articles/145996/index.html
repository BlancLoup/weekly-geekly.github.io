Computex 2012 - final report

Last week, the international exhibition Computex 2012, which was held in Taipei, Taiwan, ended. I want to share my own observations and impressions of the exhibition, and not just to list the new products that were presented.



Under the cut a lot of photos. Be careful traffic!


The first impression is that the exhibition is huge, several halls, many stands. After the euphoria from the hot Asian air, you begin to understand that there are not so many differences from CeBIT. Yes, of course, components are represented in Taiwan in a larger area, but critical superiority cannot be noted. Of course, a huge selection of peripherals, but the right word, the same or almost the same mouse and keyboard does not deliver pleasure. The embedded systems are much more widely represented, there are significantly more OEM / ODM manufacturers, but here too in recent years Hannover has been pulled significantly.

However, let's start from the beginning. The start of the inspection was given at the Taiwan World Trade Center, where I picked up the badge and went on a five-day journey across the expanses of Computex 2012.



The main event of the first day was a press conference of ASUS, dedicated to the products of the Republic Of Gamers series.



The conference presented new laptops, mice, headphones, sound cards and gaming desktops. The ASUS G55VW super gaming laptop boasts a third-generation Intel Core processor and the latest Kepler-based graphics card. A key feature is support for the Thunderbolt interface. The highest speed of operation is manifested in everything - the storage system is built on two SSD drives in a RAID 0 array.



Gaming accessories are represented by a mouse ASUS GX 1000, the resolution of the sensor is 8200 dpi, and the gaming headset with Vulcan PRO active noise cancellation technology.





The ASUS ROG Tytan super-power and super-expensive gaming desktop attracted attention with its stylish design. The configuration is not only assembled from the most powerful hardware, but also overclocked at the factory. A good toy for those who do not like to mess with the settings, but loves all the most expensive and quick.



Two overclockers, part-time engineers ASUS, Andre Yang and Shamino demonstrated nitrogen overclocking. The guys collected two beautiful stands from the newest hardware - ASUS Maximus V Extreme, Intel Core i7-3770K, ASUS Radeon HD 7970 Matrix, stood a little behind them, literally five minutes turned on the SuperPi test to demonstrate the capabilities of proprietary software ... and the overclocking show ended. Suddenly.





When everybody was going to disperse, a nyashechka came out on the scene with a super-novelty - an SSD drive with Thunderbolt interface - ASUS ROG Raidr. Very quick thing, but there is no detailed information about it yet.



The second part of the ASUS exposition was located in Nangang Hall.



In the ultrabook section, all attention was focused on the ZenBook Prime UX32. I continue to say that this is my dream, I really like the prospect of working on the FullHD matrix.




In addition to the products listed above, the Republic Of Gamers Corner also contained the ASUS Radeon HD 7970 Matrix, a special version with improved power and cooling systems.



The ASUS Maximus V Extreme motherboard is the new flagship of the line on the Intel Z77 chipset, the hardware of which is beyond description. The board has all the modern controllers, and the overclocking potential surpasses all competitors.



The line of "military" boards was presented in a modding version.











Austrian company Noctua, known for its innovative cooling systems, brought several interesting prototypes. One cannot surprise anyone with the number of heat pipes, so the original designs and weaving of radiator elements were used. Option one - with perpendicular tubes.



Option two - with a two-story parallel arrangement of tubes.



The new family of A-series fans and a quiet fan with noise reduction technology.





I was also pleased with the ultra-thin cooler for compact cases. Noctua releases two versions - for Intel and for AMD platforms.

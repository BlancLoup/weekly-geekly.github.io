Creating Tower Defense in Unity: Ballistics

This is the fourth part of a tutorial on creating a simple tower defense game. In it we will add mortar towers firing detonating shells when they collide.

The tutorial was created in Unity 2018.4.4f1.

Types of towers


A laser is not the only type of weapon that can be placed on a turret. In this tutorial we will add the second type of towers, which will fire shells exploding upon contact, damaging all enemies nearby. To do this, we need support for various types of towers.

Abstract tower

To do this, we simply use the Tower class, but first, duplicate its contents for later use in a specific LaserTower class. Then we remove all the laser-related code from Tower . Shot angle


Next, we should find out the angle at which to shoot the projectile. It is necessary to derive it from the physics of the projectile trajectory. We will not take into account drag, wind and other obstacles, only the speed of the shot v and gravity g=9.81 .

Bias d the projectile is in line with the aiming triangle and can be described by two components. With horizontal displacement, it's simple: it's dx=vxt where t - time after the shot. With the vertical component everything is similar, then it is subject to negative acceleration due to gravity, therefore it has the form dy=vyt‚àí(gt2)/2 .

How is offset calculation performed?
Speed v determined by the distance per second, therefore, multiplying the speed by the duration t we get the distance d=vt . When acceleration is involved a , the speed is variable. Acceleration is the change in speed per second, that is, the distance per second squared. At any time, the speed is v=at . In our case, there is constant acceleration a=‚àíg , so we can divide it in half to get the average speed, and multiply by time to find the offset d=(at2)/2 caused by gravity.

We shoot shells at the same speed s which does not depend on the angle of the shot  theta (theta). I.e vx=s cos theta and vy=s sin theta .

Tracer shells

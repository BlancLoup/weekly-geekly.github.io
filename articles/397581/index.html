Firecricket




Rabid Prototypes ' Firecricket project is an Arduino-compatible microcontroller designed to control sound, light and motion effects. On board is a 32-bit ARM Cortex M0 + with a frequency of 48 MHz, 16 KB of memory and a microSD slot. To encode / decode signals, there are three 12-bit ADC channels, a 10-bit DAC. Digital I / O - 12 PWM and 2 I / O. Complemented with its own 2.5-watt audio amplifier (TPS6211A1) and a power transistor with the ability to control toy motors and 3-watt LEDs.

The project scored the required amount for the launch - instead of $ 2,500 at the time of publication, almost 4,000 were raised. There is another 15 days ahead.

Dot




A small indicator that can be placed on any surface and connected with a smartphone via Bluetooth. In the application, you can configure what exactly the Dot will signal - and even now its capabilities seem endless. With the help of a multi-color LED, you can teach the device to notify about weather, important news and events, to signal missed alerts, to remind about events in the calendar, the need to make purchases and everything that can be signaled. In addition, the device works as a label, and launches applications in accordance with the user's preferences already tracked: in accordance with the place and time. It is assumed that with the help of Dot it will be possible to track the user's location, and run previously created scripts to control the "smart home". The developers promise open API, support for iOS, Android and Java-Script.

9 days left. But the project will still be launched - it collected 5 times more than necessary.

TsODy.RF - Russia's first magazine about data centers only

As you know, the data center market in Russia is actively developing: we have more and more data centers, their capacity is growing, data center services continue to be in high demand, and on the world stage Russian objects look not so faint as they used to be. However, a specialized publication that would cover the data center market in detail would tell about the best practices; there are no talented people in this field in Russia yet. Now will be! In October of this year, the first issue of the TsODY.RF magazine, the first Russian magazine specializing in the high-tech data center industry, will be published.

We feel that in the information flow, the data center sphere is not paid enough attention, there is no publication that would continuously cover data centers not only as a set of engineering systems and server solutions, but also data centers as a whole: would conduct tours of existing facilities, broadcast in The foreign experience of data center construction in Russia, conveyed this information to both consumer companies and data center designers.
+
In addition, it should be noted the active development of technology in the data center industry. New recommendations, new types of equipment, new infrastructure construction schemes, etc. - All this requires analysis, transfer of design experience and the effect of implementation.

Also, from a number of companies we have heard the opinions, they say, there are associations and publishing houses abroad, producing their own white papers and magazines, developing recommendations, standards, etc., and in Russia there is nothing like that yet.

The Russian data center market is becoming more and more open, high competition contributes to this, many having broken wood, learned to build in such a way that it was not painfully embarrassing to show their offspring. It is not conceivable 4-5 years ago to get into the Russian data center with a camera and openly declare that the photos will be published on the Internet was almost impossible, although in the west such photo-excursions were published with a bang. The Russian data center market is already warm enough, and today many data center operators are kindly inviting not only potential customers for the excursions, but also the press, and some even hide nothing from meticulous specialists. In our magazine, we want to give a new life to a photo tour of the data center, to objectively and independently talk about the infrastructure, to appreciate the bold engineering solutions, to communicate with people who build them with their own hands.

One of the main issues that had to be solved by the editors of the journal was the need to launch the printed edition. After all, today there is so much talk about the extinction of the print press, the transition to electronic media and Internet sites. On the one hand, this is certainly correct.

But, on the other hand, is it possible that an electronic device can replace the pleasure of holding a real paper magazine in your hands, with the smell of printing ink, the rustling of leafing pages, the ability to scroll pages with one index finger, and at least five, or even ten ways that will distract you, with a new message in icq, emails and at the most inopportune moment will report that only 1% of energy remains.

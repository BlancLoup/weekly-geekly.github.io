Do we really need a photo-realistic picture in games?

The last blog posts of the Games gave me a jolt, which was not enough for me to sit down and write this small post.

So, by realism, I mean super fancy modern graphics with models from billions of polygons and / or a kind of mechanics of the world around us. Realism in the game turns it into a simulation. Simulation of some fictional reality (do not touch such games as Sims - this is a simulation not in the sense of this statement). No, no, simulation is not bad at all. Progress in this direction someday will give us a full-fledged virtual reality indistinguishable from the real world - its simulation.

But I don't want a semblance of the real world, I already have one semblance . I want to play and get fan, moving away from real life.

The development of the gaming industry somewhere overstepped the barrier when realism ceased to create such a wow-effect. The photorealistic rendering in real time is impressive, yes, but personally I can play this toy for half an hour so much, evaluate the developers' work and the progress of modern video cards.

A great example is Team Fortress 2. An attempt to make this game (long time ago) a realistic combat command simulator (wow damn) failed even before the developers began to complicate the gameplay. And it's good that they killed this case before TF would turn into Operation Flashpoint. As a result (here I am not quite competent), another development team has already made a masterpiece from TF2.

Biometrics: the art of recognition. Perspectives of biometric systems on the example of the Id-Me platform from the company RecFaces



Many Habr readers are probably already familiar with biometric technologies. They are now common everywhere. In general, biometrics is a system for recognizing people based on one or several physical (or behavioral) characteristics. In the field of information technology, biometric data is used as a form of access identifier management and access control. Usually the mode of operation of biometric systems is reduced to two main types.

The first is called verification, comparing the test result with a biometric template. This option helps to check if this is the person for whom he claims to be. Verification can be performed in various ways, including a smart card, a user name, or the user's number. The second mode is identification. After receiving a specific sample, the system is checked with the biometric database to determine the identity. Here there is one important point - for this mode of operation, the biometric sample should be in the database, and the comparison should be carried out according to the ‚Äúone with many‚Äù principle. In general, biometric technology has a huge potential that has not yet been fully realized. What is the state of biometric technologies in Russia and in the world today?

In some cases, their development can not yet be considered satisfactory. So far, this area is actively developing, although there are already some results (about this - below). In some cases, biometrics is considered not a very reliable method of identification or verification. For example, in the US, the Tampa City Police Department even uninstalled face recognition software, considering it not very reliable. But there it was about the introduction of outdated methods of biometrics, which do not always show their best.

However, modern biometrics technologies are becoming more accurate and reliable. Many companies and scientific organizations are engaged in research and development in this area. Moreover, the priority over time shifted to contactless methods of biometric recognition. Biometrics is used in many areas, including banking, security and access control systems, visa control systems, criminal identification systems for criminals, collection of visitor statistics, and much more. So far, about half of the biometric market is occupied by fingerprint recognition systems. But the situation is gradually changing, the developers understand that dactyloscopy is not the most reliable way to identify a person (in the ‚ÄúDestroyers of Legends‚Äù they somehow even showed a way to open a fingerprint lock using printed fingerprints), therefore new technologies of biometrics are becoming increasingly popular. .

Biometrics: scales


In general, we can say that biometrics has become an integral part of people's lives. In some countries, for example, without a biometric data can not get a passport and visa. Government organizations in various countries believe that biometrics is one of the most effective ways to identify refugees and those who cross the border illegally.

Now there are a lot of projects based on biometric technology. Perhaps one of the most ambitious is the AADHAAR project being implemented in India. It is a biometric identification system that contains data from more than a billion people. The database contains about 10 billion fingerprint templates, two billion iris patterns, and a billion photos. Something similar was shown in the fantasy film ‚ÄúI am the beginning‚Äù (I origins). However, identification by the iris is a very real technology, which is becoming more and more popular.



Entry in AADHAAR have the opportunity to get all the residents of India, this is an identification number that is tied to the biometric data of users. It is used in financial transactions, when working with various public and private services. A cloud service is also attached to AADHAAR for storing scanned documents.

Of course, not only India introduces biometric identification. Other states are doing it too. And not only the state, but also private companies. According to the analytical agency J'son & Partners Consulting, the global market for biometric systems will reach $ 40 billion by 2022. The analysts 'conclusions are based on key players' revenues, depending on the segments, taking into account hardware, software and integration.



Another analytic agency, Acuity Research, estimates the growth in the number of biometric electronic documents e-ID to 749 million by 2018. In total, according to agency experts, in 2018 there will be about 3.5 billion electronic documents in the world. Already, more than half of the UN member countries issue biometric passports. An example of the implementation of programs for the transition to biometric electronic documents include government and private contracts of Canada, the United States, Belarus, Ukraine, Moldova, Lithuania, Hungary, Bangladesh, Senegal, and other countries.

And what about Russia?


In Russia, biometric technologies are developing quite quickly, more actively than in many countries. For example, the largest banks of the Russian Federation have begun testing of biometric customer identification systems this year. It creates its biometric database of the Central Bank, the Ministry of Communications and Rosfinmonitoring, this system will reach the testing stage this year.

According to Deputy Head of the Central Bank Olga Skorobogatova, the pilot project will allow you to become a client of any bank remotely. To do this, it will be sufficient to go through the procedure of biometric registration once in any credit institution participating in the project.

From this experiment to the creation of a national biometric database there will literally be one step.



In the banking sector, they are trying to introduce customer identification systems by voice, photos, and fingerprints. For example, VTB24 has already tested biometric identification in the framework of Internet banking. In the process of entering the online bank application, customers were offered to provide their photo and voice sample. With the help of these data and planned identification. After confirming the user's identity, all transactions are performed without additional confirmation. Sberbank also shows great interest in biometrics, which has already accredited RecFaces (Comlogic application) as one of its partners in this area.

Similar technologies are used in Promsvyazbank and Home Credit, Tinkoff Bank and several other organizations. As for the single biometric database, the Central Bank, the Ministry of Communications and Rosfinmonitoring are simultaneously working on its creation. The implementation of this project may take several years. According to experts, the general biometric database will be useful for the financial and legal sectors, public services, public security, medicine, and more.

For an external observer, Id-Me works quite simply. One of the main components of the system is the Id-Box (capture module). It is a small ‚Äúsmart‚Äù identification device based on a PC platform in a compact package. This element is responsible for face recognition and, in perspective, other types of biometric data. The system receives from them an array of data, which is here converted into a specialized index, a mathematical model, which is sent to the cloud for comparison with the standard stored there. Due to the work with indexes, the system is not demanding on the ‚Äúwidth‚Äù of the Internet channel.

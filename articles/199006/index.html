At the disposal of "RosKomSvoboda" appeared the text of the document from the Government Office of the Russian Federation, containing the following items:

1) Minutes of the meeting of the expert group of the federal level chaired by Mikhail Abyzov dated 14.10.2013.
2) The decision of the expert group to take measures to implement the public initiative ‚ÄúCancel the law on arbitrary blocking of Internet resources No. 187- dated July 2, 2013 (the law against the Internet)‚Äù.
3) Questionnaire of members of the expert group for voting.

Directly the document itself:



So, this document was sent to members of the expert working group to vote on this conclusion. The voting period is marked on October 25, i.e. either tonight or tomorrow, and most likely on Monday we will find out how the experts will eventually vote. But it is already clear, according to the statements that were made directly at the meeting itself, that the overwhelming majority of the representatives of the working group will support such wording of the final decision on our petition.

The final paragraphs of the resolution predictably contain a decision on the inexpediency of repealing 187-FZ and, at the same time, very vaguely and non-accentedly signify a public request for a general discussion of legislation both in this area in general and a call for legislators to correct the current law:

1. In accordance with Clause 19 of the Rules, it is considered unreasonable to repeal the Federal Law of July 2, 2013 No. 187-FZ.
2. To recommend the State Duma, when improving the regulatory framework in the field of protection of copyright and related rights, to take into account the comments contained in this public initiative.
3. To recommend to the Public Chamber of the Russian Federation to consider, in the format of public hearings, the issue of improving the regulatory mechanisms for protecting the rights of copyright holders on the Internet.
4. To send materials on the results of consideration to the State Duma and the Public Chamber of the Russian Federation

We expected a clearer position of the expert council, taking into account the interests of society, indicated both in the petition itself and in our appeals to it ( first , second ).

In general, we are not satisfied with the final text of the Expert Group Decision.

MySQL 8, Postgres NoSQL, Tarantool Vinyl, CockroachDB, ClickHouse, and yet, why did Uber leave Postgresql?

June 17 in Moscow, Izmailovo Devconf :: Storage Conference will be held

Databases are becoming more and more wonderful. Traditional RDBMS become NoSQL, and new engines become clustered, distributed and columnar.

Oleg Bartunov, a well-known Postgres developer, wants to tell what their base is better than MongoDB when working with JSON, and his colleague Ivan Panchenko - how to write for perl, python and javascript postgres.
Anton Okolelov will tell you how to switch from MySQL to feel the power of Postgres, and another speaker, Alexey Kopytov, will tell you why Uber did exactly the opposite, and left Postgres for MySQL.

Dmitry Lenev and Igor Ilyin from the MySQL team will talk about the new features of their product. We will be pleased with the asymptotic approximation to the full implementation of SQL, window functions, the traditional improvement of INFORMATION_SCHEMA, indexes, NOWAIT in SELECT and group replication.

Konstantin Osipov conducts an in-depth excursion into the lair of his pet Tarantool, will tell about the new storage engine for big data on the principles of RocksDB.

Vitaly Levchenko, together with Daniil Podolsky, will talk about CockroachDB. Presenters reflect on the integrity of geographically distributed transactions, the new Google Spanner cloud storage, and the fashionable Golang today.

Questions about the conference in the official chat t.me/devconfru

See you at DevConf 2017 community conference!

Honda's cruise control will predict when you are pruned

Cruise control of most cars follows the road in front of you, automatically adjusting the distance and braking. Honda developers have gone further : their Adaptive Cruise Control system will predict when you are pruned.



Honda introduced the world's first intelligent cruise control system with the function of predicting the behavior of other road users. One of the main directions of the system will be a prediction when the owner of the car will be cut. Camera and radar are used to track cars, including those on other lanes.

The first car equipped with this system will be the European model Honda CR-V 2015.



Cruise control maintains a constant vehicle speed, automatically adding gas or reducing speed without driver input. An adaptive version of the cruise control monitors the car ahead of you, maintaining the necessary distance. One of the first cars with cruise control was the 1958 Chrysler Imperial.

How to conquer fractals or introductory acquaintance with ChaosPro

There are many interesting activities in the world. Mathematics is usually associated with anything, but not with such a pastime. This can be challenged. But I, by virtue of my professional activities, know what I am saying. It is especially difficult to explain the charm of mathematics to the younger generation. And after all, it is up to him to solve all the problems of the future. Accordingly, the need arises to somehow interest a young (and not only) person with a subject.

This can be done with the help of computer programs that will help the student or student to visually see the beauty of the result. Today I would like to take a closer look at the program for generating fractals.

The concept of "fractal" was introduced by the great French-American mathematician of Jewish origin, Benoit Mandelbrot. In a broad sense, a fractal denotes a shape, small parts of which in an arbitrary magnification are similar to it.

There are many programs for generating fractals. We will focus on one of them - the free fractal generator ChaosPro, which runs under the MS Windows operating system. With it, you can create an infinite number of amazingly beautiful fractal images.

The program has a fairly simple and convenient interface. Along with the ability to automatically build fractals, you can fully control this process by changing a large number of settings. Separate windows are provided for the parameters, and the number of simultaneously open windows can be very large. All changes applied to fractals under construction are almost instantly reflected in the viewing window.

ChaosPro supports different types of fractals (2D and 3D), precise color adjustment that provides smooth gradient transitions, true color support, animation support, has a fairly fast built-in compiler. Download the generator here: www.chaospro.de/download.php .

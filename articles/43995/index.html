Interview with Vladimir Dolgov (Google Russia)

At the end of September, we published an announcement of an interview with the head of the Russian division of Google, Vladimir Dolgov , where users were given the opportunity to ask their questions. Of course, the head of Google Russia could only respond to some of them, which, nevertheless, does not diminish the interestingness of the resulting interview. Enjoy reading :)

What does the branch of Google Russia, exactly what features and projects leads?
Google office in Russia consists of several functional units. In short, they can be divided into two main groups: business and development. Business units include sales departments, strategic partnerships, marketing, and finance. Another large division is R & D centers in Moscow and St. Petersburg, which are working on the localization of existing services for the Russian market, as well as developing new products for Russia and the whole world. For example, the St. Petersburg Center made a significant contribution to the development of the Google Toolbar for Firefox. The team of this R & D office is also working on Google Code Search. This service is designed to search for open source software available on the Internet. Google Calendar is another useful development that helps make our everyday life easier. The Moscow office, among other projects, is engaged in the improvement of Russian-language search, the morphology of complex languages, such as Russian or Arabic, and Google Maps. In addition, a Questions and Answers service was developed and implemented at the Moscow Development Center.

What are the forecasts for the contextual advertising market in Russia for the period of the financial crisis?
I think that the contextual advertising market will grow. When a situation requires cost optimization and cost reduction, contextual advertising - the cost of which is an order of magnitude lower - becomes an effective and controlled alternative to traditional advertising media for large customers, and for small businesses it often turns into the only way to advertise their products and services. A relatively small percentage of companies operating in Russia today use contextual advertising, and although it‚Äôs premature to predict growth in its volumes or, on the contrary, to reduce them, the number of potential advertisers in the country is very large.

How does ecology affect data center cooling

Chinese search engine Baidu is faced with an unusual problem, which can not be called typical for data centers. Let's try to figure it out.


/ photo by Dean Hochman CC

Earlier, we talked about various ways of deploying data centers underground and the nuances of cooling a data center using water. All of these are still exotic topics. The traditional approach is to have a building in which a data center is located and an air intake for its cooling directly from the street.

The IT infrastructure that Baidu uses has suffered from environmental problems. Similar air pollution problems are not new to China. Now the problems began not only in people, but also in the "iron". The fact is that smog leads to equipment failures - the content of various impurities in the air negatively affects the ventilation and cooling systems of the data center.

One of the intermediate solutions is to use special chemicals for air purification and filtration. Such difficulties force companies to invest in innovative technologies and to solve cooling problems a little differently compared to what is commonly done in the rest of the world.

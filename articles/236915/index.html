CRM for business: we work in them


The main goal of any business is to make a profit, no matter how lofty values ‚Äã‚Äãare proclaimed in the mission. In a competitive environment, the key to profit is a satisfied customer (customer, dealer, etc.). Successful implementation of CRM determines the quality of customer management and helps to work smoothly, quickly and without ‚Äúforgotten‚Äù transactions. Accordingly, CRM, like any program for the operational management of the business, must meet three basic requirements:
  • allow you to achieve the goal in the shortest possible time - work quickly and without lags, it is easy to deploy and update, to have obvious modules with the usual names;
  • to be as convenient as possible - everything is customized for the user, a minimum of superfluous, control of data entry, detailed reference books;
  • save labor costs - make all the actions obvious and in the least number of clicks, allow you to create and save custom values, automatically fill in the associated fields in different modules.

In general, almost all serious CRMs are redundant from the outset - this is the essence of this class of programs, the vendor cannot know in advance what functions each of the thousands of users will need. The developer makes a system that meets most requirements and decides how to adapt the system for individual needs:
  • modify for an additional fee under the order (as desktop vendors Terrasoft , RegionSoft , Clic )
  • allow you to disable unnecessary partitions (modules, fields, widgets) or include the necessary ones (deeply implemented in desktop Terrasoft , RegionSoft , Clic , to a lesser extent in FreshOffice and amoCRM )
  • to offer built-in tools for self-completion by advanced users (especially RegionSoft and Clic succeeded in this with their powerful capabilities of self- doping ) of the system‚Äôs functionality.

Most managers use CRM "as is" and they have to deal with the user interface that opened before them after installing the program. The distribution of software users to novices, middle peasants and pros, proposed by Alan Cooper, more than ever works for those who work in CRM systems. The success of the introduction and application of CRM in the company is determined precisely by the speed of the user‚Äôs transition from the ‚Äúnovice‚Äù to the ‚Äúmiddle peasants‚Äù - and, oddly enough, the success of the vendor depends on the speed and quality of such a transition. Vendors sometimes do not realize that their system will be used by ordinary salespeople and marketers without technical education, often promoted not owning a computer or simply lazy.

Meanwhile, any CRM system should have a number of standard interface features:
  • the possibility of making and fixing frequently used items in the main panel (on the so-called "desktop" CRM)
  • readable and understandable names of functional sections and modules (for example, as in a question from Toster - CRM is a generally accepted understandable abbreviation, you should not translate it and turn it into something incomprehensible)
  • good and convenient custom filters
  • up-to-date, well-designed printing forms and well-thought-out document templates (for example, invoice forms must meet the latest legal requirements, and not be hopelessly weary)
  • convenient file attachment and saving
  • simple set of basic commands of the program.

Who is to blame and what to do?


As mentioned above, desktop CRM is redundant - when developing business software, it is almost impossible to avoid complications without running into a trash. Often, users themselves complicate the system by overloading their working windows, leaving unnecessary modules and fields after the initial system setup. Moreover, there are users who prefer to include everything just in case - according to the browser principle with additions and widgets. The result is the same - sooner or later, the system overloaded with unused elements will either start to hang or break your eyes when working with it. Therefore, at the stage of CRM implementation, it is important to carry out the initial setup of the system for each user - this does not take much time and will save energy in the future.

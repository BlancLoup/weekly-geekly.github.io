Anxiety (waiting, incompleteness, attention)


Anxiety is similar to internal stress. You can feel anxious and impossible to feel calm. Calm is a term for the absence of anxiety, a state of relaxation.

If there are too many compensations, if they take health or too much time, then we call them bad habits and start overcoming them. Anxiety is manifested not only by deterioration of mood or unpleasant sensations. A more pronounced symptom is attention deficit. The focus of attention is twitching, it cannot linger for long on anything. The subconscious constantly pulls back, trying to draw attention to something else.

If there is no work plan in mind for the next few years, or at least a month, if it is difficult to focus on the current work task, then we can assume a lack of attention. If the lack of attention worries, then we can assume the presence of an excess of anxiety.

If anxieties are triggered by hopes, then you can work on strengthening your self and returning to reality. If anxiety is caused by a lack of compensation, then you need to look for a source of discomfort and work on eliminating it, and not endlessly trying to drown out your discomfort.

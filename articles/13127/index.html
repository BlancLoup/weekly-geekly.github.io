Scientific discovery: Time travel is possible



Israeli scientist Amos Ori of the Israel Institute of Technology scientifically substantiated the possibility of time travel. Mathematical calculations of this theory were published in the latest issue of the scientific journal Physical Review.

Amos Ori justified the possibility of time travel using mathematical models, and the main conclusion Ori made in a scientific report is that in order to create a time machine suitable for such travel, giant gravitational forces are needed.

At the heart of the development of the Israeli scientist lies the conclusion, which was made in 1949 by the scientist Kurt Godel, that the theory of relativity assumes the existence of various models of time and space. Given this fact, Ori assumes that if a curved spatio-temporal structure is made into the shape of a ring or a funnel, it is possible to travel to the past. At the same time, with each new revolution in this concentric structure, a person will go deeper and deeper into the depth of time.

But to create a suitable time machine, giant gravitational forces are needed. And they exist near objects such as black holes. Experts call the black hole borders the horizon of events. Because, every object that reaches the boundaries, is absorbed into the depths of the black hole, and outside it is not visible what is happening inside.

Presumably, the laws of physics in the depths of the black hole cease to act, and the spatial and temporal coordinates change places, and the journey in space becomes a time travel.

According to the scientist, high-mass bodies bend space-time, while objects moving at sublight speeds slow down.

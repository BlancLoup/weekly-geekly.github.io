How to assemble an audio system from broken speakers and an amplifier from a flea market


In dreams, it looked something like this. The reality was somewhat different

I continue a series of posts about gadgets from the Spanish flea market. This time the post is not about the purchased gadget with its review. Everything is much more difficult, it will be about how to buy speakers that were faulty, fix them to the best of their abilities and make a home audio system by purchasing the Toshiba vintage amplifier at the flea market.

It all started with the thought that I want to try to listen to music not with headphones. Yes, you can turn on the built-in laptop speakers, but this is not the case. I have heard many times that the sound from powerful speakers (not necessarily turned on at full power), once heard, it is impossible to forget. The idea that it would be nice to buy speakers came to my mind for several years in a row, although not very often. One day I decided to fulfill my plans.

Their price, as stated by the seller, was only 35 euros. Photos of the columns were not very high quality, but managed to discern that this model is Fisher Studio Standart STE-950.

On the Internet, there was no mention of them at all, which I was very surprised, because now you rarely find a device about which there is no information at all. I wanted to buy columns, so I made an appointment with the seller. I must say, I immediately asked him whether the speakers were in good condition and whether they were working as they should. The seller responded almost immediately, saying: "Man, well, I would not sell them if they did not work, right?" Well, ok, we agreed to meet.

I brought them on a scooter. The seller turned out to be a Spanish guy, about 17 years old, who brought first one, then the second column. Somehow it did not occur to me to remove the protective net, it occurred to me that it was occupied with other thoughts, and the speakers were large and very heavy. I brought 40 euros to the meeting, the seller did not have the change, but he promised "to go to the store now and exchange". This convinced me of his honesty, besides, I rested all these five minutes, since the seller and I carried the columns to my entrance, and each one was about 25 kg. Not much, but they are very uncomfortable to carry.

The seller left, I brought everything home and was able, finally, to get acquainted with the purchase. To be honest, I don‚Äôt have knowledge and experience with any type of audio systems. Repair needed!

At one's own risk


In the posts and videos that I happened to read and see, everything looked not very difficult. But I knew that this impression was deceptive. Somehow, after watching the same videos from YouTube, I spoiled the display of my wife‚Äôs phone by trying to separate the cracked glass of the Nokia Lumia 1020 from the screen in order to glue the new glass. But the speakers are not a telephone yet, so I decided to try. Since I almost decided to throw them away, it would not be a shame to spoil them completely. In short, in one of the more or less free days I decided to take up the dynamics of one of the speakers.




Columns are ready to go.

First, he began to clean them from the "filth" - the remnants of old rotted features ‚Äî he knows how many years ago suspensions. After that, using a high-acetone high viscose nail polish remover, removed the remnants of everything that did not want to be removed with a knife. Another challenge is to pick up the glue. Since the authors from the Internet advised brands of adhesives that do not exist in your foreign countries, I went to a Chinese store and bought a glue that is designed to work with polymers. I bought three different glue at once with the aim of trying which one would be better.



He put the glue on the metal, put the hanger on top, and began to put pressure on the hanger with his fingers and a cotton swab so that it stuck. Not to say that the operation went smoothly, the suspension immediately began to deform, soaked with glue, but after a few minutes of torment everything went well, and the suspension (its outer part) took on its original shape.

By the way, I didn‚Äôt take off the ‚Äúcaps‚Äù of the speakers, I read that you can do without it. After the glue has dried up (several hours have passed), secured the outer side of the suspensions using the frame that was there before.



It remains the most difficult - to glue the inner edge of the suspension to the outer edge of the speaker. Using the same glue and fingers for pressure, it was possible to do this, but the effect was so-so - the edges of the suspension all the time sought to move away from the paper dynamics. After that, a happy thought came to mind to find something round, the diameter coinciding with the internal diameter of the suspension. This something turned out to be a saucer and a baby bucket. Putting the bucket on the butt, I saw that the edges of the suspension were crushed almost perfectly.


Glued speaker compared to untouched. On the curved top edge, it was a bit behind after I removed the load. But then, when I put the load back in place, the sizing was almost perfect.

Columns. The final


Having waited until morning (about 12 hours), I took off the load and realized that everything stuck, if not perfect, then close to that. After that, the existing experience was used to repair the second speaker of the first column. It turned out also not bad, and the next morning I decided to connect the column to the amplifier (about its purchase - below), in order to understand how terrible my idea turned out to be. Surprisingly, the speakers stopped wheezing, I also did not hear any distortions, although I tried to lose a lot - from Harlem Shake to Million Scarlet Roses. The bass was reproduced (as for me) excellent, the sound was not bad.

The experience of repairing the first speaker was used again, after which it turned out that the second speaker also sounds good. They sounded almost the same, there was no distortion.

Social networks kill communication because they look like TV



If I say that social networks helped Donald Trump to win the election, you will think about fake news on Facebook. But even if Facebook corrects the algorithm raising false articles , the problem will remain: social networks demonstrate the advantage of television over other media.

I have warned about this since November 2014, when I was released after 6 years in prison in Tehran. I received the punishment for the active position shown in the online space of Iran. Before prison, I often wrote to blogs in space, which I now call the ‚Äúopen web‚Äù: it was decentralized, the text was mostly in it, and there were many references to sources and the abundance of information accompanying the story. He supported different points of view. He was connected to the world of books.

Then, for 6 years, I went offline, and when I was released from prison, and I returned to online, I met a brave new world: Facebook and Twitter replaced blogs and turned the Internet into television. It became centralized, pictures became the main thing in it, and the content was also in the form of pictures, without links.

And like TV, it more and more entertains us and reinforces existing beliefs and habits. Instead of thoughts, it activates our senses, and offers comfort instead of trials. As a result, we get a highly fragmented community, driven by emotions, radicalized by the absence of external contacts and trials. The problem with today's Internet, leaving the text with links, is that it not only shares many television diseases, but also creates new ones. The difference between traditional television and what emerged in the form of social networks is that the latter are personalized media. Traditional television retains an element of surprise. What you see on TV is chosen by the editors, and although the programs must be entertaining in order to correspond to the high cost of their production, they can still challenge our opinions (that is, emotions).

Social networks, on the contrary, use algorithms to create an atmosphere of comfort and helpfulness, since their business model is based on maximizing the time spent by the user on the network. It is necessary to stop believing that any evolution of technology is natural, inevitable, and therefore positive. In order to remain intelligent animals, we need more text than videos. According to Postman, books are in fact more adapted to the transmission of complex concepts that stimulate thinking. We need to write and read more, put more links, and watch less TV and videos. And also spend less time on Facebook, Instagram and YouTube.

If we cannot resist this, and the algorithms do not give us different or opposing views on things, we should try to look for them ourselves.

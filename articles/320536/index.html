GDG DevFest Voronezh: virtual kittens, conversations with the column and the Mannequin Challenge

The Voronezh community of Google Developer Group, which was already seven years old in November, gathered for the fifth time at the GDG DevFest Voronezh conference. The conference lasted two days: the first was devoted to reports and speeches, the second to the ‚Äúiron‚Äù workshop.

Day One: Theory


The first day was spent visiting our co-organizers - the Voronezh State University and the Faculty of Applied Mathematics and Mechanics. Those who are often at our conferences already feel at home here.


While the equipment was being set up, fairies were flying around the room and suddenly thick green thickets grew. The guests did not miss the opportunity and recorded everything .


Guests also did not miss the opportunity to take pictures with the conference symbols.




Success was used by puzzles, technical tasks (also original puzzle), and, of course, virtual beasts, for which a special thank you to Project Tango.





Immediately after the opening words of the organizers, two GDEs captured the scene: Zviad Kardava, Google Developer Developer: IoT, and Denis Neklyudov, Google Developer Experts: Android.


An interesting fact: in total in Russia there are now four developers with the title of GDE. T. h. At our conference made 50% of these experts of the country!

Following Denis and Zviad, our own smart home expert, IoT guru Nikolai Khabarov, came on the scene. Together with Nicholas, we moved from Tango and augmented reality to Daydream and virtual reality.




We all know about the bottlenecks of most IoT solutions. The absolute majority of them are tied to the availability of Wi-Fi, and they refuse to work without a network. Zviad Kardava returned to the stage to share a recipe for solving this problem - the Web Bluetooth API. In theory, such decisions will make the smart home more reliable. How with this in practice? By the finale of the first day, we had a presentation on the front end. Alexander Kasheverov, DataArt expert on web technologies, told how to make friends D3.js with Angular frameworks (both versions) and Polymer.



When the reports ended, it was time to tell what kind of ‚Äúsecret announcement‚Äù was indicated in the conference program. Day Two: Practice


On the second day, 50 developers gathered as a guest of DataArt at the IoT workshop, which was conducted immediately by two top experts: Zviad Kardava and Nikolai Khabarov. Participation required a laptop, programming skills and a desire to change the world for the better.


And we prepared a whole mountain of various iron, which could be used for our projects.



We deliberately did not set absolutely no framework and timetable. In the morning, the developers came to visit, got all the necessary devices and got to work - each implemented their own ideas.

Opinions Apple and Microsoft have always disagreed on the issue of displaying fonts on a computer screen. Today, both companies are using sub-pixel rendering to achieve acceptable display of unsmoothed fonts at low screen resolutions. What they still disagree about is in philosophy.
  • Apple believes that the algorithm should preserve the design of the outline as much as possible, even if for the sake of this you have to sacrifice a little blur.
  • Microsoft believes that the shape of each letter must be firmly inscribed in the pixel boundaries in order to avoid blurring and increase readability, even if for the sake of this you have to sacrifice distortion of the style.

The difference originates from Apple's programs for printing and graphic design. The advantage of the Apple algorithm is that you can prepare a text page for publication, and you will see almost the same thing on the screen as when printing.

High-tech spying on students on exams

Chewing gum on the exam is prohibited (it can be used to disguise a conversation on a hidden headset).

Monitors of computers are recessed into the tables, so you can take a picture of the screen (for example, a spy camera in a ballpoint pen) only from above - this is easily noticed by the guards, who follow the audience through dozens of surveillance cameras.

If the teacher notices something unusual, he presses a button - and the recording of all user actions on the computer begins, and the video camera is zoomed to it, which shoots the student close-up until the end of the exam - all this is then automatically recorded on CD and can be used as evidence.

These are the everyday life of American universities and colleges during exams, writes the NY Times .

As a well-known security expert, Bruce Schneier, says , all this is reminiscent of casino security systems. And it is not surprising, because by the degree of sophistication and technical advancement, modern students can give odds to the most professional shooters of Las Vegas.



The photograph shows the testing of the student tracking system at the University of Central Florida (University of Central Florida), the third largest student in the United States. According to the university dean, after the introduction of this system, the number of cheaters among students was significantly reduced, and during the spring session it was only 14 cases per 64,000 examination papers.

New rules for Google Play


Google added to the application naming rules with a clarification that the name should not begin with a foreign brand. That is, for Hyundai Turnlights Conserver (the idea for the original application from the future, full of applications for cars, does not allow you to turn on the blinkers) can be punished, and for Turnlights Conserver for Hyundai will not be.

The original explanation can be seen here when opening the item. The introduction of this change is explained by the fact that with a quick superficial reading of the program name from a third-party developer, a false association may arise that the application is original for the specified brand. In addition, they say that most of the time a foreign brand in the name of sin applications for social networks, and here and close to phishing.


Apple patents a joystick iPhone




The other day it became known that the Coopertin company patented a telephone with an integrated joystick. The patent has been pending since July 2013, and only recently has the bureau registered the patent.

As you can see in the image, another mode will appear at the Home button - it will be able to rise above the body and thus serve as a rather familiar and familiar stick for all gamers.

Already more than once, companies have taken attempts to make management on smartphones more functional, and one cannot say that their attempts are unsuccessful. There are many options for special joysticks-adapters for tablets and phones that you can buy, but these solutions do not differ in elegance, compactness and obviously will not be able to conquer the mass market. But Apple, apparently, has every chance.

This solution will make it possible for many players to get a way to control in the usual configuration of the phone and allow them to fully enjoy the games. Also, with the mass availability of such a solution, it can be stated that the core and hardcore games on the mobile platform will receive new fans for themselves, it remains to wait for announcements and see how the camp of Android phone manufacturers will respond to this.

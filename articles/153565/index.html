Report from the past Facebook Developers World Hack Day Moscow



In early September, I received an invitation to send to the hackathon Facebook Developers Hack Day Moscow. Having registered, I got into the list of participants and began to prepare. This is a story about how the event took place, who participated in it, what they represented, etc. Immediately make a reservation that I first participated in such an event, so everything was "in wonder."

Under the cat there are a lot of photos, descriptions of the submitted "hacks" (projects made by participants during the hackathon), links to source codes and video presentations of projects.

Two days before the event, a private group was created on Facebook, where all registered participants were invited so that they could meet, form teams, ask questions to Facebook developers (who were also present in the group and willingly answered all questions). Many precisely then formed their teams.

The first quest for the participants, even before the start of the event, was suddenly to overcome geographical cretinism: for those who had never been to Digital October (hereafter, simply the center ), finding it in the streets of Bersenevskaya Embankment was not at all easy. In addition, there were no pointers.

After overcoming this first task, at the entrance to the center we were met by pretty girls with lists of participants. Calling the name we received "pass" - the usual stamp on hand. In the very center, upon arrival, all participants were immediately offered to sit comfortably (one could sit in any of the halls) and have a snack. During the entire time of the hackathon, it was possible to eat and drink deliciously: a variety of completely different foods for every taste, drinks, snacks (a la chips, sweets, hamburgers) are absolutely free and in any quantity (enough for everyone and still have left).



At 10:30, everyone was called to the conference room and started the event with reports of Facebook developers and partners of the event. The event was led by Simon Cross, a leading developer from London.



It is worth noting that the organizers of the event took care of those who understand English poorly: you could ask for a special earphone and listen to the simultaneous translation of all the reports. Similarly, all the English-speaking participants of the hackathon (Facebook developers, partners, Mark Zuckerberg himself) listened to the simultaneous translation of Russian speakers.

In the first part of the event, Facebook representatives told about how the network is developing now, how many users it has (955 million active users per month, more than 500 million users every day). The moderator in every possible way emphasized the importance of the hackathon for the social network itself: all Facebook innovations were originally invented and invented on the hackathons. For example, Facebook Timeline was invented by 7 developers on a hackathon that took place inside the company.

Then came the stories about the Open Graph, Graph API, the development of mobile applications for Facebook, all of them you can watch with simultaneous translation (dubbing) on the Digital October website (I strongly advise).





After the reports of Facebook developers, the reports of the hackathon partners went. They are services Deezer , Pusher , 500px and Yandex . Photos . When the time came to dinner, Simon summed up the reports, listed the directions in which we (the hackathon participants) should try to think of something, and in which we definitely shouldn‚Äôt do anything. Simultaneously with lunch, the most important stage of the event began: in 6 hours it was necessary to invent and create a useful application / service that would use the Facebook API.





As you can see from the photos, almost all the participants worked for MacBooks. ‚ÄúNot poppies‚Äù were very few (out of about 200 participants).

All days before the event and the whole day in the process of the hackathon there was a complete intrigue as to whether Mark Zuckerberg himself would come to the hackathon. Facebook representatives diligently avoided answering this question. And here comes the evening, it's time to register your hacks for the jury and present projects to the public. We are going to the conference hall again, Simon thanks us for participating and says that now we will start watching the resulting hacks. Suddenly, he stops and asks, ‚ÄúCan we first meet one guy?‚Äù After these words, Mark Zuckerberg appears on the scene.



Mark, as I expected, was a good-natured, smiling, simple guy. Once again, he thanked us for participating, urged us to more actively develop applications and services that use all the features of Facebook, because this is access to an almost billion-strong audience, which is many times more than all local social networks.

After the welcome speech ( video in English , with translation ), Mark joined the jury, which, in addition to Mark himself and Facebook developers, included:

Jacob Sadchikov - founder of the Quintura search engine
Igor Matsanyuk - founder of the game developer IT Territory, which later became part of Mail.ru Group, former vice-president of Mail.ru Group, investor of more than 35 projects, among which the most famous is Game Insight
Jovan Savovich - the founder of dirty.ru and Leprer blogs
Ilya Zubarev - senior partner of venture fund Runa Capital, founder of Rolsen, Acronis, Parallels



After the presentation of the jury, the hacks began directly. In just 6 hours of the hackathon, 34 hacks were developed. Each participant needed to tell for 2 minutes what his hack was for, how he was useful, what features Facebook uses. It was possible to present their achievements both in English and in Russian.

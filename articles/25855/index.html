Alpha 400 - New Linux Subnotebook

Company Bestlink (Hong Kong) has released Alpha 400 - the cheapest subnotebook running Linux (version 2.4). The price of the novelty is declared at $ 250, and when you buy a wholesale batch, you can count on $ 180.



According to its characteristics, the machine resembles the Asus eee PC - 7-inch display (24-bit color) with a resolution of 800x480 pixels and flash memory instead of a hard disk. The weight of the Alpha 400 is 0.7 kilo.

The heart of the baby is the 32-bit XBurst processor from Ingenic Semiconductors, which theoretically supports Windows CE. However, the company has so far declared only support for Linux.

Let me remind you that once the president of Asus said that his company would try to reset the price of the PC to the level of $ 199. It seems that Bestlink, like the darkest horse from which no one expected anything like it, managed to unexpectedly get ahead in this race. And to the finish line left quite a bit.

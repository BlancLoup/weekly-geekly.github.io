SCO will help fight pornography

The governor of Utah (USA) sent an unofficial document to Congress urging him to take measures against the spread of pornography on the Internet. The initiative was supported by the CP80 group (Clean port 80), which includes Ralph Yarro, chairman of the board of directors of SCO.

In CP80, many people compare a web browser with a television, where all programs are broadcast on the same channel. Would parents allow them to watch this TV, where porn could appear at any time, to their child? The answer is obvious to many, although the analogy is not very correct.

The way to deal with porn, which is offered in CP80, is banal blocking of downloading information from IP addresses that users do not like. Experts suggest that it is unlikely to be implemented.

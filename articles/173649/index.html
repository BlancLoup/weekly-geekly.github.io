WorldHostingDays - the largest hosters conference (report)

By the nature of their work, they often come to various conferences to keep abreast of everything new in the field of IT. This time we hit the world's largest hosting conference - WorldHostingDays. We will try to tell a little about this conference for the Russian-speaking audience, who goes there, how it happens and what can be learned there.

The conference, without exaggeration, is the largest in terms of the number of participants. It takes place every year, in Germany, in the small town of Rust, on the territory of the amusement park Europe Park. This year we are participating in the conference for the third time. For now, as participants, as our potential customers do not go here yet.

Worldhostingdays, further WHD, last year gathered about 4,200 participants. This year the number of participants is about 4,500 of which more than half are top managers and heads of major companies. So this is a great place to make new, useful acquaintances and establish the necessary arrangements.


The conference takes three days. The first half of the day - the session in the main hall, then a free lunch + rides in an amusement park, paid for by sponsors. In the afternoon session in different rooms.

It can be noted that most companies use the time of their reports solely for the promotion of their services and products. Especially large companies are sinning with them, their reports are usually the most boring and it is quite difficult to extract something useful from them. Whether business, the company is less which just begin the climbing. They can already hear about some interesting technological innovations, techniques and useful tips from personal experience.

A little about the reports themselves


Most of the reports in one way or another are devoted to the subject of clouds or cloud services. It is worth noting the two most interesting, in my opinion, companies in this market.

Onapp and Flexiant - these two companies provide a comprehensive solution for launching their cloud, focused primarily on small hosting companies, the so-called SMB sector.
In Onapp, the solution includes support for three Xen, KVM, WMware hypervisors, as well as an integrated storage system that allows you to run a small cloud, even without buying expensive SAN solutions and a CDN system.
Based on this platform, we soon plan to launch our own clouds.

From the Russian-speaking companies or immigrants from Russia attended Ispsystem, Parallels, Cloudlinux.
By the way, Cloudlinux has a rather interesting solution for hosters that allows you to strictly share memory, apache and mysql resources among users within a single server. That allows to avoid the most frequent problem of beginner hosters, when one user can "eat" all the resources of the server.

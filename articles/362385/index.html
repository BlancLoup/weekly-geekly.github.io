The Google X team working on the Nanoparticle Platform project

Google Inc., as part of the Google X research division, has begun work on creating technology for the early detection of diseases such as cancer, heart disease, and other diseases. According to representatives of the corporation, the technology is based on nanoparticles introduced into the human body. In this case, particles will be introduced using a tablet.

After the dissolution of the last nanoparticles will be distributed throughout the human body, with blood, and monitor various vital signs. If any abnormality is detected, a warning will be issued. Data will flow to the wearable device.

The details of the work of the new system are not disclosed, but it seems that this will be a high-tech platform, with constant monitoring of various indicators of the human body. According to one of the leaders of Google X, Andrew Conrad, particles of the size of a billionth of a meter are safe for humans. They will consist of molecules of magnetic material and antibodies that react to the presence (or absence) of certain substances in the human body.

Particles will be output in a natural way. All information received by the system will be transmitted via the Network to the attending physician, who will be able to diagnose and give advice based on the data provided.

Highscreen WinWin and WinJoy: an overview of the most affordable smartphones on Windows Phone 8.1

Despite the modest share of gadgets on Windows Phone in the overall standings, Microsoft's operating system does not lose appeal in the eyes of fans. Following the takeover of Nokia in 2014, Microsoft embarked on promoting its OS for mobile devices. This summer, the company has simplified access to Windows Phone 8.1 for other manufacturers. The Russian brand Highscreen took advantage of the situation and presented two low-priced viphones at once - Highscreen WinJoy and Highscreen WinWin. About them, and will be discussed in this review.



First about the price. Highscreen WinJoy, the youngest model in the line, in the official store of the brand costs 3990 rubles, Highscreen WinWin - 4490 rubles. The difference in price is small, it is also small in technical characteristics. Just starting with the differences.

Equipment


In addition to the standard paper set-instructions-warranty, the box with Highscreen WinWin has an additional back cover in bright yellow. While the main color of the smartphone is black.



Highscreen WinJoy comes without interchangeable rear panels, but the model itself is presented in four color variations at once: black, white, red and blue.



Headphones are also slightly different: WinWin headsets have rubber covers and a silver button for answering a call, WinJoy looks a bit simpler. Looking ahead, we note that those and other headphones are suitable for hands-free conversations, and for better listening to music it is better to choose something more serious.



With WinWin comes the power adapter at 0.8 A, with WinJoy - at 1 A.



Multimedia speakers


Everything is simple: Highscreen WinJoy is equipped with only one speaker - the speaker, it also serves as a multimedia speaker. At Highscreen WinWin there is a full-fledged multimedia speaker, at the edges of which there are two small protrusions - so that the sound does not spoil when the smartphone is turned ‚Äúon its back‚Äù.



Battery


Batteries for both smartphones are removable. WinJoy has a smaller battery - 1700 mAh. Included with WinWin is a 2000 mAh battery. But don't let that frighten you much, because this is about the WindowsPhone, not about Android. So, on average, the boot mode of the first model can work without recharging for two days, the second - 2.5.

This is where the main differences between smartphones end. On trifles we will understand in the course of the review.



Control elements


Despite the fact that both devices weigh the same - 105 grams each, and are equipped with 4-inch screens, WinWin looks a little more than a younger comrade. WinWin dimensions are 126x64x10.5 mm, WinJoy parameters are 122x63x9.9 mm. The difference in the thickness of the screen frames - they are wider in WinWin.
Speech speakers are located on the top of the front side. Front cameras are located right there. In WinWin it is in the right corner, in WinJoy it is in the left.

There are two connectors on the top side of the WinWin: for the micro USB cable and for the headphones. At the top of the Vinjoy there is only a 3.5 mm audio output, in the micro-USB connector - on the left side.



Also, smartphones have a mirrored location of the volume button. On WinJoy - top right, next to the phone's power button, on WinWin - on the left.





Smartphones support different types of SIM-cards: WinWin - miniSIM, in WinJoy - microSIM.



Screen


Gadget displays are protected by durable glass with oleophobic coating and anti-reflective layer. Further characteristics are similar. As noted above, the screen diagonal for both devices is 4 inches. Resolution 480x800 pixels, pixel density - 233 PPI.

The matrix used by the TFT-TN does not provide wide viewing angles (compared to IPS), but still the picture is bright and saturated. WinWin display supports up to three simultaneous touches, WinJoy - up to two.



Hardware platform


Both smartphones are based on Qualcomm Snapdragon MSM8212 chipsets - 4 cores with a frequency of 1.2 GHz. Adreno 302 is used as a graphics accelerator. The RAM capacity is 512 MB. If for smartphones on Android, such volume will cause braking when launching a number of programs, then this is not observed on the ‚ÄúWindows‚Äù. This is good for Windows Phone 8.1: it almost does not freeze and reacts quickly. For example, Asphalt 8 and other popular games run without problems.



For storing files provides 4 GB of internal memory. Since almost half of this volume is already occupied by system files, the presence of a microSD memory card slot is clearly not superfluous.

Connection


Both smartphones support the work of two SIM-cards. In Windows Phone 8.1, management of various SIM cards is very conveniently implemented. Each number is assigned its own Tile for calls and SMS. The devices operate in 2G cellular networks (GSM / GPRS / EDGE, 850/900/1800/1900 MHz) and 3G (900/2100 MHz). Both slots support 3G, but not simultaneously. If one SIM card has a 3G connection, the second one automatically switches to 2G.



From the set of standard wireless modules, we note the presence of GLONASS along with GPS. As you know, dual-system navigation has several advantages: communication with satellites is established faster and is more stable.

Camera


Highscreen WinWin and WinJoy are equipped with two cameras. The main one has a resolution of 5 megapixels (+ autofocus and flash), the front has a resolution of 0.3 megapixels.

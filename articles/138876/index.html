Signs of fraud on eBay

UPDATE March 5, 2012: please take the screenshots below only as an illustration, perhaps this listing and the seller are completely normal, just had problems (or an account was hacked). On March 5, 2012 (after more than a week) this seller on ebaye began to receive fresh positive feedback on expensive purchases. Now I think that maybe I was mistaken and reacted too suspiciously to the seller (as one in one resembled real scammers on eBay from personal experience) or the seller did have some temporary problems.

He has dealt with scammers on eBay several times and has developed several rules that (I believe) are necessary to fulfill in order to make a safe purchase.

On the example of screenshots show what should be suspicious. So:

  1. The price (with delivery) is lower or slightly lower than similar sellers ask. Maybe so that the price is the same as the rest, but the delivery is much cheaper.
    For example: $ 30 dollars for shipping a smartphone via USPS Express Mail, while other sellers cost $ 60 dollars and more.

  2. Photo of the product "stock" (i.e. taken ready from the manufacturer's website)

    Normal seller is not too lazy to make your own photo of the goods, even in the box.

Rent an IP PBX by model VPBX, or how to organize a modern telephone in the office

Hello!

Today we would like to talk about the possibilities of office IP-telephony and the ways of its organization.

In the understanding of many modern companies, there are two standard ways of organizing office IP-telephony: this is either installing your own office IP-PBX, or using the broad functionality of the Virtual Office.

On the Habrahabr pages there were several materials telling about the possibilities of organizing office IP-telephony based on Asterisk, the configuration files were described in detail. Also numerous solutions of the ‚ÄúVirtual Office‚Äù were presented. Each of these methods has its own advantages and disadvantages (if the community is interested, we can put it in a separate material with a comparative analysis).

But very often there are situations when a company cannot afford, as a rule for financial reasons, installation and maintenance of its own IP-PBX - the average budget for such implementation will be from 30,000 rubles. with the equipment, but with the functionality of the "Virtual Office" is clearly not enough. Based on this practice, our company was one of the first among telecom operators to offer a compromise solution to the market - renting an office IP PBX using the VPBX model with already connected telephone numbers. What is this decision?

The VPBX (Virtual PBX) model implies deploying multiple virtual machines with installed IP-PBX software distributions, for example, based on Asterisk. All virtual machines are located in the data center of the telecom operator, connected to transit VoIP servers within the same physical network in order to reduce losses during data transmission and are configured to work for receiving / making calls (number capacity in code 495 or 499 is highlighted).

In fact, we have the same IP-PBX as in the office, only physically rendered outside the office. VPBX capabilities allow you to configure it in accordance with the personal tasks of each company, connect remote offices to a single network, set up your call distribution routes, keep records of telephone conversations, refine the platform for your specific business processes and many other features that are not available within ‚Äú Virtual Office.

The main consumers of VPBX are companies that have already ‚Äúgrown‚Äù from the ‚ÄúVirtual Office‚Äù, but cannot afford to maintain their own IP PBX. As a rule, these are companies of 5-20 people who need a personal approach to setting up office telephony.

Disadvantages of the VPBX model

Among the shortcomings of the solution, it can be noted: the possibility of disconnecting the communication channel in the data center (for example, damage to the city collector) or other other force majeure circumstances, the occurrence of which is probably no higher than in the company's office. A prerequisite is the presence of a fairly stable and wide Internet access channel at the client's office - otherwise the quality of the conversation will not be as high as it could be. In addition, there are frequent cases when many unscrupulous providers block the passage of SIP-traffic in their networks. But this is a topic for a separate material, how to deal with it.

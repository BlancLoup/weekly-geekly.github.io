Ten of the best SMB servers in 2017 - you probably didn't know about some


TechRadar portal has made its own TOP of interesting server models for small and medium businesses, some of which are little known. I propose to distract from the fashionable clouds and plunge into the motley list of the good old classics.


1. Dell PowerEdge T20 [Barebone]



A real example of an inexpensive Barebone server.


Processor : Intel Pentium G3220 | Video : Intel HD Graphics | ** RAM : 4 GB | Data storage : without disks | ** Interfaces : up to 12 x USB ports (4 x USB 3.0), 2 x DisplayPort, VGA, serial I / O port, 2 x PS / 2, Gigabit Ethernet | Dimensions (W x D x H): 17.5 x 43.5 x 36 cm


Pros :


  • Cheap.


  • Compact mini-tower housing and easy access to entrails.

Cons :


  • The G3220 processor is more suitable for the desktop.


  • There are no disks or operating system.

The server does not have to be huge or too expensive. A vivid example is the PowerEdge T20, which is not even equipped with a hard disk, but it is a fairly budget option. Most recently, it could be bought for less than $ 125.


Its Haswell-based Pentium processor can operate at 3 GHz and supports up to 32 GB of RAM. Of the opportunities for the upgrade, 4 SATA ports are noteworthy (you can get storage capacity up to 32 TB, there is a RAID), 4 I / O slots and 10 USB ports.

The Xeon-based PowerEdge T20 is a real needle in an assortment haystack. The machine is designed for small offices and home users. It successfully combines enormous potential, compactness and noiselessness.

Silent machine with serious potential.


Processor : Intel Xeon E3-1226 v3 | ** Video : Intel HD Graphics P4600 | ** RAM : 4 GB | Data storage : 2 1 TB hard drives | ** Interfaces : 9 x USB 2.0, serial I / O port, video adapter port, 2 x DisplayPort, audio port, Gigabit Ethernet | Dimensions ** (W x D x H): 17.5 x 42.5 x 43 cm

The line between the workstation and the server is sometimes quite thin, as is the case with the 5039A-IL from Supermicro. This server is part of the SuperWorkstation line, but also performs well as a server, thanks to its extensive expansion options. A good option for companies that need a little more than just a file server.


The hulk weighing 18.1 kg is technically well equipped. The model is available with Skylake-based processors (Xeon or Core i3 / 5/7) and offers up to 64 GB of DDR4 ECC memory.

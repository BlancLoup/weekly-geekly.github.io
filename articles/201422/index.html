Smartphone from Railways

The day before yesterday, the railway terminal on Android was presented at the InfoTrans 2013 exhibition in St. Petersburg. And here he is in my hand.



This ultra-secure smartphone with integrated barcode scanner and smartcard reader is fully developed in Russia. In April, Habr√© had a small note about this device that caused a heated discussion: habrahabr.ru/post/175683 .


The device, code-named FRMD101A, is built on a dual-core processor with a clock frequency of 1.2 GHz, has 512 MB of RAM and 4 GB of internal memory.



The terminal withstands twenty-degree frosts, 55-degree heat and falls from a height of 1.8 meters. It has an IP54 protection class, allowing it to be used even in rain and snow. The smartphone can be used without removing gloves - especially for this, it is equipped with a resistive screen.



The device has a built-in barcode scanner.



For authorization and implementation of a legally significant electronic digital signature, the personal railway card is inserted into the smartphone (for clarity, I took a staged photo - the card was inserted into the smartphone on its back).



The battery has an impressive capacity of 5000 mAh.



The terminal is able to work even without a battery - inside there is a second built-in 800 mAh battery.



Of course, the terminal has all the necessary equipment of the smartphone - voice calls, SMS, 5-megapixel camera with flash, GPS / GLONASS, dual-core processor.

Special software with a simple interface does not allow the user to go to the Android interface. Even the shutter is disabled.



This non-destructible device will be used by railroad walkers, station workers and other railway workers.

Already, the device operates on several sections of the railway. With the help of it, Russian Railways will be able to translate in electronic form the last operations performed ‚Äúon paper‚Äù.

NextCastle Party 2015: how I showed my game

A month ago, I took part in the indie showcase at the St. Petersburg festival NextCastle Party 2015, and I would like to share with you my experience and impressions. I am an amateur igrodel, to get to such a relatively large event is a serious achievement for me. Let's see if I could properly dispose of the chances given to me and show my game as I should have.


Photo - Mikhail Evstratov
NextCastle Party is the annual festival of indie and retro games held in St. Petersburg. It emerged recently, in 2012, but is rapidly gaining momentum, almost doubling the audience and scale every year. More details can be found on the festival website or its VKontakte group , but a selection of photos from the last one.

To become an indie showcase participant, it was enough to submit an application with a description of the project and wait until two weeks left before the festival opening. To this significant day, the organizers selected 30 of the many announced projects, including mine, and distributed them between two exhibition days. I got the second one - Sunday.

Polygraphy


One big two-sided rollap and distributing flyers were required. Rollap is a vertical banner with a roll to be rolled. When folded, it is a compact but heavy parallelepiped (I was very much like a hunting rifle in a wardrobe trunk and weighed 14 kilograms). Once laid out, a rectangle stably standing on the floor without unnecessary details. The minimum size of rollap is 80 √ó 200 cm, the wider options go further, but in my case, the extra width was useless - the table was small. Rollaps are one-sided, then there is an image in the front, and a blind inside is seen from the back and a support stand is visible, but there are two-sided, then there is no roll-up inside - there is a canvas on both sides. My roll-up stuck in the middle of the exhibition hall, so there was no choice - it had to be two-sided.

I considered the main purpose of the roll-up to draw attention to the stand and demonstrate the brand. The more information on the banner of the husk, the worse it will perform this function. I decided to move all the information to the flyer, and make the roll-up just a big flag: a logo, a name and nothing else.

The project has long grown out of my programmers art, for pumping the graphic component of the game is now responsible for the great and terrible weilard . My task was to prepare sketches. To save Andrei time and energy, I decided that the artist is needed for one job: creating a roll-up model in high resolution. I will make the flyer myself using rollap fragments. It was the right decision, since we spent quite a lot of time on the coordination of the roll-up layout, and I managed to print it with a stock of just one day.

The central element of the roll-up was the logo, followed by the name of the game. Nothing more meaningful, splashes from below in order to fill the empty lower part. The layout was made under 80 dpi, but it could have been less - at the output when printing on banner fabric with a resolution of 720 dpi, it turns out that it is not at all that you can think of comparing the layout resolution and print resolution. I don‚Äôt know how much exactly the resolution could be omitted, but just keep in mind that banner fabric is not paper, small details are eaten.

Another important nuance was the stock strip on the layout - an additional 5 centimeters from the bottom of the canvas, on which I simply reflected the bottom of the layout. Two centimeters of these five were in the visible zone (the roll-up model was a little higher than the standard), if there wasn‚Äôt anything there, a bright white underside would come out. This question is definitely worth finding out in communicating with the printer.

Flyer format chosen A6 (105h148mm), as the most common. Most likely the other participants will be the same. You can show off and make a Euro-format, under the envelope, but if visitors collect packs of flyers, then mine will stick out and interfere, maybe it will be folded, breaking in half.



On the back are four screenshots from the game, two short paragraphs with the essence and links to contacts, the main of which is the VKontakte group. The link to the group is duplicated by a QR code. Not sure that it works, but everyone hangs up, and I hung for solidity.

A bathrobe is not a T-shirt, in printing houses they are not printed, so the simplest and most effective way of applying a logo is spraying spray paint through a stencil. Having some experience in homemade stencil making, I made such a sketch on A2 sheet:

I assumed that the stencil would be cut from a thick film or plastic. The stencil should be one solid design, so I had to use the logo reversal (otherwise, the round timber under the eyes hung in the air) and add jumpers to the letters of the name.

Fortunately, in one of the printing houses, where I sent my layout for consultation, there was an attentive and competent specialist who opened my eyes to the glued stencils. I did not need a hard reusable stencil, it was more practical to use several disposable adhesive ones. The adhesive stencil does not have any limitations related to its integrity; ‚Äúhanging‚Äù elements are simply glued onto fabric on a temporary substrate before use. Layout stencil returned to the original logo:


Paint used MARABU Textil Design, this is a special aniline aerosol for textiles. It is not required to fasten it, it is not suitable for everyday wear. Before spoiling real clothes, I tried to put a small logo on a pillowcase:



Four dude short robe froze in horror. After analyzing the situation, I came to the conclusion that I was too generously sprayed paint, seeking a wet gloss fabric. It is necessary to stop at that moment when the painted surface is just beginning to cease to be dull. After taking into account the negative experience, the final application of the logos was quite successful. For each dressing gown it took about 20 minutes, of which the process of applying the paint did not take a minute.

At first, the robe is fixed, the lower substrate is separated from the stencil and it is glued to the site of application. The areas of the robe outside the stencil are covered. I used the pages of old magazines.



Then the most difficult stage - the separation of the upper substrate. The white parts of the stencil should remain glued to the fabric and the transparent backing should be separated. The substrate is durable, it bends all over, displaces the stencil, sticks to your hands and generally interferes in every way. Enrages awful! But sooner or later, on the battlefield, only a battered working stencil layer remains, which needs to be glued to the fabric as tightly as possible.



Painting takes place quickly and easily, the main thing is not to pour paint (do not allow wetting of the fabric) and spray it perpendicular to the surface, otherwise it blows under tightly adjacent places.



The best part is to unstick the stencil when the paint has dried a little. Never without artifacts, but the nightmare that appeared on the test pillowcase never repeated.

Two game points


Whenever possible, you need to organize two game points. The person playing the stand attracts others who want to play, the second game point allows you to use this effect and halve the waiting time for those who lacked a gamepad.

Two game points require two people to accompany. According to my own feelings, when you play a game, while communicating with the developer, and he abruptly switches from you to a nearby player, it‚Äôs unpleasant and you want to leave.

The chair increases the time of the game session, it is more convenient for a seated player to play than a standing one More and longer played on the game point with a chair.

An increase in a gaming session is good only up to a certain limit, at some point it begins to reduce the carrying capacity of the stand.

From tutorial to mayhem


It is very important to have a tutorial, the game should tell about itself, while the developer forcedly or specifically left the player alone and alone. In addition to the presence of a tutorial, the sequence of levels is important - at the exhibition everything should be shorter and brighter. I had nothing to put before the exhibition an additional stupid level for a smoother increase in complexity - this level ate up precious time of attention and shaping the impression of the game.

Hardcore is not for the exhibition, it is better to reduce the complexity in the exhibition build. Most of the players stuck and fell off at the fifth level, where I had an erroneous, too sharp leap of difficulty and, most importantly, the duration of the level. All ten available levels of the game passed only one person out of sixty. Build for the exhibition, where they play inattentively, where there are many distractions around, including the author of the game, should not be the same as for homely thoughtful leisurely acquaintance.

After the tutorial, short and tight levels are best. If the level is short, and the delay between the death of the character and the beginning of a new session is minimal, then some increased complexity is excusable. When I just switched the jammed player to further shorter levels, it worked.

A special exhibition demo level was just a salutary decision. It was a level, with almost all game content, and almost no challenge for the player. Sooner or later, I offered to switch to this level to all players. Most agreed and stuck on it just crawling the worm on the map and eating helpless enemies. Tellingly, the hardcore refused, and with more fury they rushed to the level where they were stuck.

Sound


If the sound is allowed, then it is necessary to sound. Sounds attract attention and voice what is happening on the screen. Playing without sounds is only half the game! If you give the player headphones, then the audience remains only a silent movie. For the basis of the game point used small household speakers, they turned out to be quite enough.

The second game point was voiced by headphones in order not to make a salad of sounds. In headphones, the player can either listen to the game or the developer. Someone took off the headphones from one ear, someone just hung around his neck, but everyone had to somehow solve this conflict.

Headphones, like gamepads, were wireless. Sennheiser HDR 170. They are convenient to use at home (turn around, go wherever you want), and at the exhibition this convenience is multiplied by the number of gears the device from person to person. Wireless stuff is easy to offer, transfer, transfer and temporarily put on a chair, without fear that someone passing by will dump everything on the floor. I experienced it during my work, I even had spare wired headphones on hand, but I had enough batteries for all six hours.

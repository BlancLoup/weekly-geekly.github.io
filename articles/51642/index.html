Parallels Summit 2009

Our company hosted the Parallels Summit in Las Vegas, which, according to Western journalists, was much cooler than the most popular hosting event Hostingcon. For those who do not know, Parallels has historically been involved in virtualization and automation software, which was most popular among hosters, service providers, and so on. I would like to share how the representatives of the hosting industry see the development of the hosting industry, and of Parallels as well. Sergey Belousov, the company's CEO, spoke about the most notable market trend - the movement to locate infrastructure remotely in huge computing ‚Äúclouds‚Äù that can be accessed via the Internet, as well as SaaS development trends.

Summit in full swing


As already mentioned, Sergey Belousov spoke about the movement to locating the infrastructure remotely in huge computing ‚Äúclouds‚Äù that can be accessed via the Internet. And if we take into account the formation and active development of several large and often proprietary data clouds (Google - about which he has already spoken a lot , Amazon, Salesforce, Microsoft, IBM), then for hosters and service providers the issues of creating their own clouds and optimization their work. In the opinion and not without the help of Parallels, there is a way to not only increase efficiency, but also to reduce the huge production costs of such customers, and the main task here is to adapt and automate the services provided. Companies must stop wasting money on developing their own systems for billing, order processing, account management, service management, etc. This is an opportunity in the rapidly changing market conditions, if not to increase, then at least maintain its market share, expand the range of services offered and the client base and thereby increase revenue.

Almost all participants in the discussion agreed that one of the most important consequences of the emergence of the concept of cloud computing would be that the infrastructure of hosted applications or hosting solutions - especially as an argument in favor of SaaS - would be taken for granted. For example, in his speech, Andreas Gauger, former CEO, 1 & 1 Internet, emphasized several times that the most appropriate strategy for hosting providers is to focus on achieving greater flexibility and the ability to quickly adapt and introduce new services. Hosting providers should not waste time creating applications that they can buy ready-made. He is practically echoed by Bernie Myerson, vice president of strategic alliances and CTO of Server Business, IBM, who said that hosting providers must now determine for themselves what they can do worst of all, and find who will do it for them . Sounded theses of experts reflect the philosophy of Parallels, doing everything possible so that its clients have all the necessary solutions to achieve these goals.

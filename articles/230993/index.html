Batch convert office documents to PDF, JPG, TIFF and PNG



Microsoft Office software has long been able to export documents to PDF. This option appeared in version 2007. It works very simply:
1) You open text in Microsoft Word, a spreadsheet in Excel or a PowerPoint presentation,
2) Click on the Microsoft Office logo in the upper left corner of the program and in the appeared menu choose: ‚ÄúSave as => PDF‚Äù.
3) It remains to specify the name of the PDF file and select the folder to save it.

Own export of documents, implemented by means of the Office, solves the problem of converting documents to PDF by an order of magnitude better and more accurate than, for example, independent document converters operating on the principle of virtual printers. The thing is that when a document passes through the Windows printing system, it loses some of the information. For example, hyperlinks from text are lost. They are not needed for output on paper, therefore such information is not transmitted to the printer.

The export function works great if you need to convert a couple of documents to PDF. But if there are a dozen of such documents, then opening them one at a time and repeating items one to three for each of them turns into a rather tedious task. Here is a good example of work that just needs to be automated!

And the program DocuFreezer perfectly copes with this task!


Add your 20 documents to the DocuFreezer list and click on the Start button. Documents will be converted to PDF faster than you can brew a new cup of tea!

The list of output formats of the DocuFreezer program, in addition to PDF, also includes JPEG, TIFF and PNG. And in future versions of the program are planned to introduce the function of imposing watermarks and signing PDF files with a digital signature.


The first version of DocuFreezer is free for personal use.

GeeXboX is fully ported to Raspberry Pi

It is very strange that this news has bypassed Habr.

GeeXboX - a popular free distribution based on XBMC 12.0 'Frodo' and the Linux kernel, which is a lightweight media center - has received full support for the Raspberry Pi architecture . Available is the full OpenGL ES for XBMC hardware acceleration, hardware video decoding up to 1080p HD and control from the TV remote via HDMI-CEC .

There was no official release yet, but test builds are regularly laid out on the developers website .
It should be noted that GeeXboX assemblies exist for other compact computers Pandaboard, Cubox, ux500 Snowball, nVidia Tegra2 Harmony.

By the way, our country seems to be waiting for an explosion. After all, the device became available more than ever. For example, one large retail chain (Google Help) announced the widespread availability in its stores

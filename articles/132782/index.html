How to become Microsoft Certified Professional? And for what?

Good day,% username%. Having passed the next certification exam from Microsoft: MCITP: Database Developer 2008, I decided to share my, albeit not very large (so far only MCTS, MCPD and MCITP) experience in passing these examinations.

Then I will talk about development and programming, but this fully applies to administration and other certifications (office, sharepoint, etc.).

If you briefly answer the question "How to prepare for the exam?", Then I would answer it like this: Write the code, fucking ( you forgive me for the mat, but you can't throw out the words from the song )

If you want details and answers to the question "why do you need it, because the certificates are no longer fashionable?", I ask for a cat.


What for?

In truth, there are several reasons why people take certification exams:
1. A person may not like those wallpapers that are hanging in his apartment or house and he decides to hide them under a layer of certificates in beautiful frames;
2. A person seeks to prove to others that he is a cool specialist, not through dialogue and successful projects, but by laying out a bunch of certificates;
3. the person really understands the question and wants to amuse his self-esteem "for show";
4. a person hopes to make a better impression on the employer with the help of certificates and to ask for a big salary;
5. everyone got it, but what am I, red?

More common combination of these causes.

Personally, I initially had a combination of 3 and 4 factors. And the 4th factor is secondary. I would even say that certificates make it easier to get through the initial screening of candidates, help make your resume read and invited for an on-site interview.

It all started for me on a boring autumn evening in October 2009, when at work all things were already completed, deadlines were not tied, bugs were fixed, and until the end of the sprint a few more days. And we thought, would we test our strength and pass the .NET Framework 2.0 exam? And they decided to pass. And passed! By the way, the most difficult exam of all that I passed.

After passing the 6 exams from Microsoft in two years (in fact, after the first one - the others just confirmed this conjecture), I realized that there is another very important factor (even probably the most important for me ), which prompts me, as a professional, not take and prepare for these exams:
6. You will never, under any circumstances, in practice, encounter all the tasks for which you are designed, for example, SQL Server Integration Services, or MS SQL Server Agent. And preparation for the exam allows you to systematize this knowledge in the form of hundreds or other theses (each question is essentially one thesis, which very briefly shows how to use this technology or tool).

On the exam, the main thing is not to be nervous and calmly and very carefully read all the words in the question! Often the meaning of one word affects the correctness of the answer. Nowhere to hurry: time for 50-55 questions is given more than enough: 120 minutes, if your native language is English and another +20 minutes if not native.

The most difficult question in the exam is at the very beginning to choose the right language of the exam: C # or VB.NET.

What exactly gives me passing exams and possession of certificates?

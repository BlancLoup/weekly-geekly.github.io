How to start a startup without money: personal experience on the example of coworking in Thailand



This is not an ordinary success story about how I did a mega-successful project and cut money. Perhaps this article will change the life of some habrovchanin, give the magic Pendel the necessary impulse, or at least dispel the myth that for any project you first need a lot of money. Although a nuclear reactor is still without a lot of money, perhaps, not to build.

In the article I will share the experience of opening a rather unusual institution of a coworking hostel-anti-cafe in Phuket in Thailand in the format of a short story, and in the comments I am ready to answer any questions on the topic, Thailand and Phuket in particular.

Just a little background


In 2012, I was in the position of lead developer of a typical startup for laundering an investor bubble that successfully implemented an idea that, unfortunately, did not take root even now gone into non-existence. I lived within the third transport, went to work on foot, walked with the girls in the center of Moscow and could safely get home without a car or taxi after that.

Once, in the icy winter season, shortly before my 27th birthday, I went home after a workout and noticed that there were no cars around for a long time, but the air was just as disgusting as it was during the day. I felt dust particles through my nose, throat, and even light ones. During the day it could be attributed to cars, but at night they are not around.

A couple of years before that, I had already noticed that in Moscow I could not find a single place in the open air where it would be really quiet, like in a normal forest. Even in the very center of Losiny Island (bunkers not to offer).

Oh nafig, not for that I live to die in 50-60 years from cancer or stroke. In general, in a couple of minutes I decided that I would soon leave the Motherland, since I had to receive a passport shortly after my birthday.

But why so radical?


At that time, the level of dissatisfaction with the government in the country was quite high and I was firmly convinced that the standard of living and happiness in Russia was relatively low. As it turned out later, the first one somehow pulls at C grade, but with the second in this country, it is slightly worse than absolutely bad.

Working remotely you can live in significantly better conditions, even earning 2 times less than in Russia, and much in our country is not at all (for example, cheap and high-quality housing, warm sea all year round, tasty watermelons, mango and papaya at reasonable prices and much more).

Then I thought "in the worst case, I can always return to Russia . " Now I think "But why?"



How did I choose a country for expatriation?


I read a bit of the forums and then a colleague who traveled quite a lot gave me some advice.

I chose between Bali, Thailand, Bulgaria, and the countries of the Caribbean; now the Philippines, Vietnam, Chile, China, South Korea, and Brazil would add to consideration.

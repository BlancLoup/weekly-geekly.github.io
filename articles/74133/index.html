Hyperview on RIW 2009

A week ago, RIW 2009 was held - the most large-scale offline event of the Russian Internet. I was lucky to attend the conference as a journalist, and most importantly, to really help people with the organization of work. Three days - but not hard work, but fun, communication, emotions.

Television, despite all its efforts, could not keep track of all the exhibitors (by the way, the news about RIW slipped - oh, my God! - even on the Kultura TV channel). It seemed that the Russian Internet conference would disappear for everyone who could not attend, leaving behind only the subjective opinions of people and static photos .
So, the young project actio.tv has taken on the responsible role in transferring offline RIW space to online .

As a curious journalist, I volunteered to help them. In the asset we had a camera and a desire to work. In the future - 40 pavilions, which had to be circumvented and "processed." And we did it!

Now you have the opportunity to virtually go through the whole exhibition and see the presentation of each project presented at RIW



In the meantime, you will be playing with unusual features of the hypervideo, I will tell you about how we did it.

The author of these lines selflessly went through each pavilion and explained the task of the tired representatives of companies to their task - in 10 seconds to make a mini-presentation of their project ... We should also describe the reactions of people who looked at my words about the official video for the Russian Internet Week site suspiciously my accreditation with the designation as an organization of a student newspaper (a modest tribute to the native editorial board). I decided the problem quickly - just turning it over so as not to embarrass anyone.

The operator with the assistant patiently removed, and if necessary, they re-shot each presentation, gave advice, strongly supported and encouraged the speakers.

All this was filmed in the last hours of the exhibition, so many thanks to those who unfolded the already folded posters and prepared the speech, who were attentive and responsible to our request and who really wanted to convey their message to a wide audience.

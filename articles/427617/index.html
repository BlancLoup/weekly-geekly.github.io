Evolution of the railroad car

Why is the window in the new car below the shelf? Why did the opening window open? Where did the good old lockers go under the bottom shelf? Why instead of two locks for belts on the top shelf - some kind of bent piece of iron?



Let's look at how wagons evolve, and how different design solutions are determined. The first cars in the Russian Empire were, in fact, barns on wheels with a gravity-type toilet. Today, the cars have a local network and monitoring, they can send telemetry to any part of the planet and become more comfortable.

Our Tver two-story passenger carriage 61-4492 of domestic development became the winner of the international competition of industrial and graphic design Good Design Awards (USA, Chicago) in the category "Transport". In a professional environment, this is one of the most prestigious awards.

But I will begin with the answer why the locker on the cars of the new generation is now without a wall.

Why a locker without a wall


Since passengers complained that they could not get things at night without waking the passenger from the bottom.

Recently, new requirements for combating terrorism have emerged; it was necessary to ensure easy inspection of all the hiding places in the cars.

Why films are shown from the radio storage


Because the first inter-car network was for wired radio. There is a staff car, there is a train headquarters and a train commander. There is a radio compartment - this is the workplace of the train head, there is his office. From the radioacde, you can make an announcement for all passengers or contact the guides. How long does the carriage live?


The first long-distance passenger trains are wagons with wooden frames and a body. The toilet is either in the form of a hole or not at all; there are no partitions inside, just three tiers of shelves. The length of this car is only 9 meters (modern - about 26 meters).

Then steel cars were needed, but welding was not introduced into mass production, so they were riveted. About how then the production was organized, says the fact that the workers who held sheets of metal from inside the car, often deafened from constant blows.

Soviet post-war cars were made of ferrous metal, served 29 years on the project. Modern stainless steel car serves 40 years. But this does not mean that he is the same for all 40 years. Standards are not particularly changing, for example, the dimensions of the body are all the same for a long time. But what if the interior or equipment is worn out? In this case, wagons are repaired in specialized depots or at repair plants. Depending on the type of repair from the original car, only the metal body of the car can remain.

Previously, foam polystyrene was used for thermal insulation of cars, or, in a different way, expanded polystyrene. Polystyrene is a thermodynamically unstable polymer, the polymerization process is reversible, so polystyrene is constantly decomposed under the influence of light, oxygen, ozone, water, mechanical and ionizing effects, and especially under the influence of heat. Free styrene formed in this way penetrates the premises, and people are in a situation for a long time when there is free styrene in the air (even if the concentration is lower than the MPC). When the trains crossed the threshold of 200 kilometers per hour


Around the 1960s, ways changed in our country: from wooden sleepers to reinforced concrete, the rails were made lengthy. Became possible speeds higher than usual. Further development of high-speed traffic required research in the field of wheel-rail interaction and the evaluation of various structural elements of the running gears.

In 1970, in the midst of experiments to accelerate the train, the KVZ (at that time the plant was called Kalininskiy car-building plant) offered to take engines from Yak-40 aircraft that had spent their resources in the sky and put the ER22 electric train on the head carriage:



These engines were still removed from the aircraft, because after generating a certain resource, they could no longer be used. And the train does not have to fall when the engine fails, so in the framework of economy and optimization, the country offered to reuse them. The car on the test track accelerated to 249 kilometers per hour and showed a shock wave and turbulent flows behind him. During the tests, it turned out that the car could reach speeds substantially higher, but the existing railroad tracks could not withstand the loads.

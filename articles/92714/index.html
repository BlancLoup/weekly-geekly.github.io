In June, Sony will also have an interactive table.



Despite the fact that the first Microsoft Surface multi-touch desk was first shown two years ago, the technology has not yet become popular. Nevertheless, the prospect of such equipment for restaurants, shopping centers and universities is obvious, and therefore more and more players trying to gain strength in this segment appear on the market.

In particular, the desire to compete with Microsoft said Sony. The Japanese use technology developed by Atracsys and first demonstrated in the fall of 2008.

The atracTable screen has a diagonal of 35 inches. Nothing is reported about the resolution, except for the fact that it is larger than that of the Microsoft Surface (who would have doubted, two years have passed). The gadget has a pair of Sony ISS XCD-V60 cameras with which the tablet is able to track the movements, appearance and even the facial expression of the user. Also, cameras are used to control the cursor. Will the table support multitouch is still unknown, but it is logical to assume that there will be. Otherwise, it is not very clear why it is needed at all.


I will add to the heap that at the Pioneer booth at the Technoshow 2010 exhibition held in April, a sensory table was also noticed. Moreover, the managers assured that this is Pioneer's own development.

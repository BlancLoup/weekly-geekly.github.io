And in the appendage - a funny story about the quality and the management of this in the domestic auto industry.

I decided to conduct an experiment - to implement controlling in practice of this small system, i.e. get a figure, and "make control" on its basis. As close as possible to the system - by integrating into it .

He stood next to the worker at the machine, I tell him - give me every finished part. One was done for about 10 seconds, during which the worker was not busy with anything, so I did not bother him.

Now a little about the Japanese.

In quality management, there is such an indicator, one of the main ones is the reproducibility index and, denoted by Cp . In more detail I will tell about him, if there is such a need, now I‚Äôll just give you comparative figures and a short formula.

The reproducibility index is equal to the ratio of the size of the tolerance field (control limits of a digit) to the actual size variation. Everything is a bit more complicated there, there are conditions for using, calculating scatter, etc., but all the conditions were met, the process was stable in all 7 criteria of the KKS check, the normality of the distribution law was confirmed, checked on specialized software (this offer is for those who know theory of statistical process control).

So, in the Japanese automotive industry, the average value is Cp = 1.6. The bigger it is, the better. The minimum acceptable value for the Japanese is 1.33, for them it is a borderline between life and death.

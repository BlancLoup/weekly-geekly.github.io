Law of causality


The bottom line is that the investigation cannot outrun the cause. No one ever watched, for example, first a duck fell dead and then a hunter fired. At speeds exceeding C, the sequence of events becomes reverse, the tape of time is rewound back. This is easily seen from the following simple argument.

Suppose that we are on a certain spacecraft moving faster than light. Then we would gradually catch up with the light emitted by the source at increasingly earlier times. At first we would catch up with photons emitted, say, yesterday, then emitted the day before yesterday, then a week, a month, a year ago, and so on. If the light source were a mirror reflecting life, we would first see the events of yesterday, then the day before yesterday, and so on. We could see, say, an old man who gradually turns into a middle-aged man, then a young man, a young man, a child ... That is, time would be reversed, we would move from the present to the past. Cause and effect would be swapped.

Albert Limitations


(Special theory of relativity)

In addition to the problem of causality, nature has set even more stringent conditions: motion is unattainable not only at superluminal speed, but also at a speed equal to the speed of light ‚Äî it can only be approached . It follows from the theory of relativity that as the speed of movement increases , three circumstances arise: the mass of a moving object increases , its size decreases in the direction of motion, and the flow of time on this object slows down (from the point of view of an external ‚Äúquiescent‚Äù observer). At normal speeds, these changes are insignificantly small, but as they approach the speed of light, they become more noticeable, and in the limit - at a speed equal to C - the mass becomes infinitely large , the object loses its size in the direction of motion and time stops on it. Therefore, no material body can reach the speed of light. Only the light itself has this speed! (At the near-light speed, the "all-pervasive" particle is moving - the neutrino)

In general, apart from the many problems described above, for example, at near-light speed, when an object weighing 1 kg collides with a grain of sand, such an amount of energy is released that allows 10 thousand tons of steel to be turned into steam in 1 second. If we compare this with the real mass of the starship, the power of the explosion will be equal to or exceed the power of nuclear processes occurring on the Sun.

Ways of superluminal movements in space-time


Einstein-Rosen Bridges


The bridges of Einstein-Rosen , also known as wormholes or wormholes, are perhaps the most well-known means of interstellar travel ‚Äî and most likely actually exist. The general theory of relativity of Albert Einstein predicted the existence of wormholes, although they have not yet been able to detect them.

In simple terms, the Einstein-Rosen Bridge is a tunnel in space caused by the deformation of space-time. Massive objects, such as stars or black holes, bend time and space, like a bowling ball bending a trampoline. A sufficiently massive object can bend space-time so much that it creates a connection between two points that are sufficiently distant in the normal state.

Imagine a piece of paper and two points on it. You need to get from one point to another, and if you move strictly along the surface of the paper, this ‚Äújourney‚Äù will take some time. However, if we fold the sheet so that the points coincide, and pierce it in this place with a pencil, then using a pencil like a tunnel (or bridge), we will significantly reduce the distance between the points.



The entrance to the wormhole was often represented as an entrance to the tunnel, which makes sense given the name. But this is a misconception. In the film "Interstellar" this moment is shown correctly - from the point of view of the observer in a three-dimensional space the wormhole should look like a sphere.

Wormholes are a tempting method for making interstellar motions because they do not require you to exceed the speed of light. Physics tells us that nothing can move faster than light. But with wormholes, you can overcome huge interstellar distances without breaking this law.

Hyperspace


Not so long ago, detailed experimental data from the WMAP spacecraft about the temperature inhomogeneities of the CMB, which is one of the main objects of observation in the study of our universe, appeared. When analyzing these data, a large anomaly of the angular distribution of the background radiation fluctuations at low harmonics was found. One explanation of this phenomenon was the theory that the topology of our universe is different from the three-dimensional plane or ball. When considering the topology of our universe in the form of a dodecahedron, theoretical calculations closely agree with experimental data.
‚ÄúA flat topologically complex three-dimensional Universe can be built only on the basis of cubes, parallelepipeds and hexagonal prisms. In the case of curved space, a wider class of shapes has such properties. Thus, the possibility of the existence of a hyperspace of the Universe, in which our limited universe is present, is indicated by theories based on practical data on relic radiation. However, even if the hyperspace exists, in order to move in this space you need some kind of special engine.

But if at your disposal a plane that can move in 3-dimensional space, then you will reach where you need in a straight line.

In fact, the hyperspace has a metric that is different from the usual 3-dimensional space and the movement in it is similar to the movement in the wormhole, only the tunnels can connect not one but several points at once that extend into the usual 3-dimensional space. The last form of superluminal movement, which we consider, at first glance seems to be the least scientific.

Or not? It turns out that the TARDIS has even more theoretical basis than hyperspace.

In 2013, physicists Benjamin K. Tippett and David Tsang published a paper proposing the theoretical foundations of creating a real time machine, i.e. ways to travel to your past. The article was called "Traversable Achnronal Retrograde Domains In Spacetime" (check out the abbreviation).

Tippett and Tsang describe a time traveler who is in a space-time bubble that enters a closed time-like curve (essentially the same as the Einstein-Rosen bridge). Within this curve, the traveler can go anywhere on his own timeline, while inside the bubble time seems to be proceeding normally.

Two physicists have even suggested that timelike curves can be separated and connected, opening up the possibility of traveling not only on your own time scale, but also anywhere in time and space.

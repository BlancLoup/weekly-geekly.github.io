Internet 2.0: Introduction


Internet 2.0 is an evolution of the existing Internet, its continuation, giving Internet users the opportunity to be free, responsible for their freedom, and independently manage their time. In addition, Internet 2.0 allows you to actively protect your rights to freedom and time management, as well as respect the rights of other people. One of such particulars can be called Copyright, etc. etc., but! do it yourself.

Goals and Objectives of Internet 2.0


  1. Freedom and security of Internet users, protection of personal life and personal information from the encroachments of other persons, both by means of software and personal participation (for example, protection of their copyright in the circle of familiar persons)
  2. Managing your Time - independently: you follow your vital interests - other people do not violate them and you do not violate their vital interests. Internet 2.0 provides assistance and support to you and all other people equally (using the appropriate software).
  3. Maximum universal technological freedom and accessibility for ordinary users: they do not need to know what is in their computer (PCI, HDD, DDR, USB, CPU, etc.); they simply stick one contact into another and use it without having to buy a new fast computer - these technological improvements are made on supercomputers
  4. The most effective economic freedom and information security: you do not pay for potential opportunities, but really used, while you are the one who owns the situation, and not the company that provided the service. At what you pay the cost of maintaining the service from your own pocket and the company is obliged to you and other people to transparently report on the conduct of their business and the service provided. You have the right, by means of a vote, to determine the actions of this company according to your own needs and the needs of other people for the common good, and not in the selfish interests of these companies.
  5. The creation of direct relationships between the author of the service and consumers, without the participation of intermediaries, is possible, the so-called Quick Startup and the opportunity to stand on an equal footing with all software developers at once and offer their service to all people at once. Ie, practically: a talented person places and organizes his service on the Internet 2.0, which becomes available to all Internet users, and if this service is in demand, consumers begin to pay for its services, and in case of failure, that's okay, no load for the supercomputer and no cost to startups. It is quite possible that at the same time there will be some limit on the number of startups per month.
  6. Active protection of copyright and the fundamental suppression of violations, both independently and with the participation of the circles of authors (Art Councils), publicly elected by the authors themselves for a certain period of time.
  7. Evolutionary transition to the Internet 3.0. Internet 2.0 serves as a means of protecting personal freedom and responsibility, as well as a tool for the manifestation of personal Creativity and its instant distribution. Those. is an active system of self-development of talented people, providing them with all the necessary opportunities, and those that do not - the opportunity to create them.

Internet 2.0 structure and concepts


The basis of the structure of the Internet 2.0 in the technical part is the mobile virtual OS, located on specially made for it hardware complexes - supercomputers (not to be confused with the data center and DC).

Supercomputers (SC) - are the same type, block-modular solution; a set of modules in accordance with the purpose of the IC and the services provided. SKs are designed for online load of several million people (for example, ICs of a city, region, country), but may be less, for example thematic communities: sports (football, hockey, etc.); media (online theaters, digital TV in the house; TV author); game (exchange of mod-s, community forums on a single IC, sites of relevant organizations coinciding with the theme of communities); transport and passenger, tourist (system of management of motor transport of city, railway and air communications); etc ... These and all other thematic communities and services are tools of social and economic transformation based on openness, clarity and free will of people, with the ability to actively protect their rights and freedom and promptly signal violation of their rights and freedoms to the whole thematic community .

Virtual OS integrates services and distributes the load on the hardware: in the event of an accident, it activates emergency modules, and in the case of idle time it disconnects unclaimed ones; users continue to receive services online. If necessary, the Universal Virtual OS can use the UK of another city-region, with the consent of users (for example, day-night mode: two cities of Tokyo in Japan and Marseille in France, while some sleep - others use their SC, then they changed).

Virtual OS applications are virtualized and are contained on a single virtual repository server. The author of the application just needs to update the version once and it will immediately be available on all ICs and all WIR.OS.

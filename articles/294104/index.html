What is procrastination?


Based on everything I have studied on this issue, I can divide the approaches into three main groups

Procrastination as a disease
In many places today, the idea is expressed that procrastination is a disease, and that a person, allegedly, cannot do anything, and everyone else must respect this.
As the main thesis, a certain theory of the hunter and farmer is given for the authorship of Thomas Hartman , who has certain degrees in homeopathy and herbology, as well as an active leader of NLP.
The essence of the theory is that some time ago some people were hunters, and others were farmers and gatherers. The first was important mobility and activity during the hunt, the rest of the time they were engaged in all sorts of others. Others worked measuredly, keeping the level of concentration constant. As a result, the descendants of the former now have ADD, they cannot concentrate in calm conditions, but they do well in stressful situations, while others are ordinary hard workers who simply work without frills. So, those of us who suffer from this ailment are the descendants of the ‚Äúhunters‚Äù, so we can concentrate on an urgent task and work hard, but in the daily routine we constantly shift our attention to lower priority tasks.
I will not condemn the theory from the point of view of genetics and the fact that the probability of the existence in all races of such clans of "hunters" and "farmers" who existed without interbreeding with each other for so long that this feature was reflected at the genetic level , tends to zero . I will leave it to people who really understand genetics, unlike me and some homeopaths.
Let us turn to another concept commonly used in American psychotherapeutic practice - ADD (G) . On the one hand, they are explained why small children cannot sit still (and why they need to take Ritalin), and on the other hand why adult uncles are lazy and do not want to work normally. Judging by the article about depression , it turns out that a person does not begin to stop doing the right things, lazy and distracted, but because of some incurable diseases that are treated with various amphetamines.
We will not delve into the controversy about the disease itself and its real impact on human life. Obviously one thing - taking the fact of the disease is a very simple way to ‚Äútake the blame‚Äù for your own laziness. Instead of undertaking something, looking for approaches to organizing your time and changing your lifestyle, a person accepts the hopelessness of the situation - ‚ÄúI am not lazy butt, I am very, very sick, and the people around need to understand this‚Äù - and gives up.
However that may be, the history of medicine shows that fighting a disease, whatever it may be, brings much more benefit than humility.

Procrastination as a result of stress
Another version of the cause of procrastination is stressful situations. And rather a constant presence in stress, rather than short-term stress.
It cannot be denied that procrastination and stress often go hand in hand and generously complement each other. But is the second cause of the first, and not vice versa? Not sure. More likely no than yes.
Imagine a situation where you experience constant stress - for example, you have a not very qualified boss, and you expect what you are about to get from him. Or you know about the reduction of staff, but do not know who will be cut down - you, or your colleague, who occupies a similar position. In general, pleasant enough. Will this situation cause a loss of attention and difficulty in concentrating? The answer is unknown.
Someone constant tension really knock out rut, and he will begin to worry, all the time to calm himself down with something, such as reading the news on the GT.
The other person, on the contrary, will concentrate on his work in order not to fall under the distribution. And both in the short and in the long term.
The third one will even get motivated for more productive (rather than usually) work, and, undoubtedly, will be great for the month.
And what about the opposite direction? It's a little different.
Imagine (and for someone it is not necessary to represent anything) the usual situation - the deadline is in a month, the task is somehow stated in the documentation, the roles are distributed vaguely. You can begin to understand the problem today, but in a week you can code it, in fact, for two days, but the analysis is longer. In this position, and pulls to switch anything less straining brains - such as video cats or NASA's Mars program.
But when switching, some part of the consciousness tells itself that it is not good, that it is necessary to drop all this and take up work. Of course, the brain does not listen to her, ‚Äúbut the sediment remained‚Äù. So stress builds up. And where there is tension, there is a loss of concentration. And it entails the desire to unload the brain - to switch to something simpler than work. It turns out a vicious circle, from which you can exit only with great effort, and most likely, not on your own initiative.
So does distraction from tasks cause stress and loss of concentration? The answer is yes, almost always, if you do not stop in time.

Causes of procrastination


Based on my own experience, I can conclude that there are only a few reasons:
  • unclear tasks
  • little work
  • heterogeneous tasks


Unclear tasks
It often happens that the task is such that it is not clear from which edge to start, and the edge itself is often undefined. In such a situation, I really want a little longer and not to look for this region - the presentiment of a complex analytical task makes the brain look for tasks simpler - and it easily finds them.

Little work
The other extreme is when there are not enough tasks to fill all the working hours. This does not happen to everyone, but this is the place to be. In this case, thoughts appear like, ‚ÄúWell, now I‚Äôll read the news, and after lunch I‚Äôll start,‚Äù or, ‚ÄúWell, today‚Äôs Monday, I‚Äôll give myself a rest, and since Tuesday I‚Äôll go into battle.‚Äù It is clear what this leads to - and the wolves are not yet full, but the sheep are already so-so.

Heterogeneous tasks
Disease managers at different levels - leads, PMs and above. And it can also meet with freelancers, and just with people who are seriously engaged in something other than their main job - for example, a hobby that takes up all their free time.
A situation is created when at the same time there are several actual tasks that need to be performed. Yes, and constantly distract. In this case, the brain begins to "boil" and is looking for shortness of breath - hello procrastination. This is really a difficult situation when the accumulated stress is very much affected.

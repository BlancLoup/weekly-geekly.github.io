How far the unmanned vehicle will reach? Ask a question to an Intel expert



The news of the last few years have taught us that the next generation of people will already live under the communism of the coming era of autonomous cars and will no longer need a driver's license. Intel has long been actively involved in this process, it has accumulated vast experience, which we are now ready to share with you. Under the heading "Ask a Question to an Intel Expert," we present you an Intel expert, Ivan Kuzmin .

Comments to this post or through personal messages, we invite you to ask your expert questions in the field of artificial intelligence and unmanned vehicles . After some time, the post will come out with the answers. Clicking the "Read more" button is a pleasant surprise!



A few words about our expert. Ivan Kuzmin - Head of Numerical Methods at Intel. Works at Intel since 2004; led various projects both in Russia and abroad. He is currently leading a team that develops the Intel Math Kernel Library (MKL) , which is responsible for the development, release and support of the Intel Data Analytics Acceleration Library (DAAL) - libraries for working with "big data" at different stages of processing, as well as Intel Autonomous Driving Library (ADL) - a library that includes all components (linear algebra, machine learning, geometry, statistics, parallelization tools, etc.) for creating unmanned vehicles.

So, during this week, from January 22 to January 28, inclusive , we are waiting for your questions about everything concerning today and tomorrow artificial intelligence technologies in general and unmanned control in particular - comments on this post, or to me in private messages . Next, we give the expert time to answer and, finally, publish them.

And now the good news: according to tradition, who asked the best, according to Ivan, the question will receive a small but cozy prize from Intel.

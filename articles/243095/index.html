Lightning Hunters. Unusual Thunderstorm Experiments

Good afternoon, dear readers of Habr.

I will talk about my unusual hobby. No, this is not a photo / video zipper hunt. I catch lightning in the truest sense of the word, flying a kite into thunderclouds. I direct the energy of thunderstorms into special circuits and devices to conduct experiments.



I was always inspired by the beauty and power of lightning. The current in the lightning discharge reaches 10-300 thousand amperes, and the voltage ranges from tens of millions to a billion volts. Discharge power - from 1 to 1000 GW. That would be good to "tame" this energy!

I want to warn you, do not repeat it at home! I take special care and know the nature of electrical phenomena well. Remember, lightning damage is deadly.

The birth of an idea


The first thing that comes to mind is to bring the wire to the thundercloud and discharge the charge to the ground. But how to lift the wire so high? Having considered all possible options, I came to the conclusion that this can be done with the help of a kite. Even before the thunderstorm began, I thoroughly experienced a kite. I was pleasantly pleased with his lift! Even in a small wind the snake raised enough heavy loads, and in a strong wind it was difficult to hold it by the fishing line. But the snake wire could not lift high, since already 100 meters of wire weighed 2 kg and the wire had a large windage - it was blown away by the wind to the side. It was decided to replace the wire with a thin wire. Nothing that the wire can withstand a huge lightning current and instantly burns down, an ionized channel forms at the place of the wire, and the main charge of the lightning passes through the small resistance of this channel. To achieve the minimum weight, windage and as a result of the maximum height I used wires of different thickness: the first 100 meters from the snake is the thickest ‚âà0.3 mm, the next 100 meters are thinner, and so on, so that it does not break under its own weight. The line on which I let the snake also chose as thin as possible - 0.25 mm. Snake she held securely. A test run showed that the snake with a wire is able to fly up to an altitude of 300 - 500 meters. The clouds are certainly higher, but still worth a try.

First experience




After waiting for stormy weather, we drop everything, jump on a scooter and fly at maximum speed under a cloud. In the very place where lightning is more brilliant and thunder rumbles. It is so exciting that the strong wind and rain for us is no longer a hindrance. Reaching the site, we unwind 200 meters of fishing line and lay it in a straight line on the ground. We tie a kite and put a balloon near it, around which a wire is neatly wound. We put the balloon on an insulated box and ground it through the measuring current shunts, and also connect various household appliances to see what happens after the destructive force of a thunderstorm. As soon as the snake begins to fly, we run a safe distance and observe what is happening. The snake took off pretty well, but the lightning didn‚Äôt want to get into it, although it was loud nearby. We tried several times elsewhere and again failed. It became clear that you need to change something.

Hooray! We managed to conquer the storm!


The lightning is near, and even caused by you, it is really cool.

Now I have refined this technology and delivered high voltage from the TV ‚ÄúYouth‚Äù to the wire. It uses 10,000 volts at the anode of the kinescope. In stormy weather, I went out of town and on a high hill turned on a portable TV "Youth" from the battery. I properly grounded the body of the TV set, and connected the high-voltage output to a thin copper wire wound on a bottle. While the kite was gaining height, the wire was easily unwound from the bottle. At that time I was watching the process from a safe place. The serpent then gained altitude, then descended, causing the wire to touch the ground and spar. At the next gust of wind the snakes sharply darted upwards and the lightning with a deafening crash banged on the TV. I did not expect that the lightning would be such a strong shock wave, which rejected my video camera. The sensations from lightning are simply indescribable. The sound is like an explosion of an artillery shell, only more impressive and sharper. A flash is something. I managed to look at it well, since I saw it for a few minutes, especially if my eyes were closed. And the inner feelings are beyond words! After the lightning, we did not immediately come to our senses. Just could not believe that this can be done by hand. And then, as not quite sane, they ran around the forest, fearing that the noise might come to the military.

In just 5 minutes we flew home and now you can safely examine the consequences of a lightning strike. If you look at the video that I filmed in frames, then you can see sparks that diverge from the rings from the TV - this magnetic induction tore the remaining turns of wire from the bottle. Then you can see how the lightning jumped on the TV antenna and instantly evaporated it! Lightning went out of the channel switch to the ground, melting it like after welding. Battery wire burned out. For some reason I did not see the molten earth in the place where the lightning struck. Maybe I got a weak lightning. But he found three holes on the ground, around which the grass burned out. It turns out that lightning entered the ground in three different places, one near the TV channel selector and the other a meter away from the TV. Why did it happen? Maybe there was a series of lightning and each hit a new place? And what happened to the TV? To my surprise, the kinescope did not explode; some strange spots appeared on it. The back wall flew off, melted and covered with bubbles. The antenna has completely evaporated, only a pipelet remains. The board was covered with a strange purple bloom and a lot of tracks burned out. From the dynamics of the membrane vomited. But the battery is a pity. Although he was away and there was no direct lightning strike, he melted and cracked and completely discharged. After full charge, to my surprise, it worked fine. And the cracks were not through - melted from the inside.

Why don't we get a fireball?


Recently, I saw scientists in laboratory conditions getting fireballs. They immerse the electrode in the water and give it a high-voltage pulse, as a result, a ball lightning flies out, which goes out in a split second. This time I decided to conduct a larger experiment. I plunged a massive electrode into a river and gave it a thunderstorm, connecting it through a wire to a kite flying up to a thundercloud. But something went wrong. The wire began to spark, after which the snake hissed and lit up with a bright blue glow. A luminous ribbon began to descend from it and as soon as it touched the ground, lightning struck with deafening power. I did not understand what a strange natural phenomenon I was watching! And we, like madmen, ran through the woods and went home from there. Most of the experiments were conducted at the beginning of the summer, but it was only recently possible to post a video and write this article.

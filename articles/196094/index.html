Former Microsoft Privacy Director now trusts only open source software.

From 2002 to 2011, Caspar Bowden (Caspar Bowden) served as Chief Privacy Advisor at Microsoft and was responsible for this area in more than 40 countries. He probably had to work with the NSA and other government organizations, although Bowden says he never heard of the PRISM program.

Nevertheless, many years of work at Microsoft has led to the fact that now the former top manager of the corporation basically uses only open source software . He says that he cannot trust the program until he sees its source code. In addition, two years ago, Bowden refused to use a mobile phone.

A week ago, Caspar Bowden testified in the European Parliament about NSA surveillance of citizens. He also published a report (pdf) about the population surveillance programs (PRISM) and the Foreign Intelligence Act (FISA) and their impact on the fundamental rights of EU citizens.

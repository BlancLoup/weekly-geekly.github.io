Signs of the new time


In general, the so-called Data Driven business or, in simple terms, a business whose main driving force is data, is now becoming a common practice for many enterprises of various industries. And data management underlies the increasingly popular digital business transformation. According to experts, by 2020 the amount of data processed annually by data centers (data centers) will reach 15.3 Zb (zetybyte), the number of business users will exceed 325 million, the number of devices connected to the Internet will reach 20 billion, it will significantly increase the range of data types, 92% of data traffic in data centers will be in ‚Äúcloud computing‚Äù, and artificial intelligence will come to the aid of a billion workers.


Last December, the US Transportation Infrastructure Regulatory Authority announced that all new cars in the country, which will start production in 2023, must be equipped with V2V (vehicle-to-vehicle, "car-to-vehicle") data exchange systems, which involves the exchange data on routes and speeds between all cars on the road, which should fundamentally reduce accidents on roads.

Developers from Ohio State University recently received a government grant to create a "smart" highway on the 56-kilometer stretch of highway in the state capital's suburb of Columbus. We are talking about the highway standard V2I (vehicle-to-infrastructure, "vehicle-infrastructure"), which provides for the exchange of data between road infrastructure facilities (road signs, traffic lights and various sensors) and moving cars. A fiber-optic communication line will be laid along the highway, which will connect transceivers located every 600 meters. Thanks to the information received from the road, the car will be able, for example, to warn the driver about a traffic jam, calculate a detour, and predict downtime at a traffic light.

An example of a simple landing page with a conversion of 30%




The 3 main elements of a successful (highly convertible) landing page are the easiest approach to usability.


As you have noticed, there are no complex graphic and informational elements in the organization of this page. It should immediately be said that this particular Landing Page collects traffic from a single advertising company, therefore it is targeted (targeted). Any novice webmaster and web entrepreneur, not to mention more advanced users, can make and edit a page of this level.

Page elements that attract users and give a conversion above 30%:
  1. The title "Are you sure you are doing effective contextual advertising?". He clings and raises some doubts, a stream of thoughts that makes one wonder, which gives rise to initial interest.
  2. A bit of text that makes it clear that if advertising traffic goes to the main site, then this is not good and because of this the return (conversion) is not high.
  3. CTA button. There should be an inscription on such a button, which will motivate you to take the first step towards increasing conversion and revenue. One of the important parameters, in my opinion, is the location of the call-to-action button at the top of the page (the visitor does not need to scroll the page to see it).

Important: it is statistically revealed that you only have 8 seconds to let the user perform one or another desired action. And it really is not enough. Look at the percentage of bounce on your site: if you have exceeded 60%, then you have a serious problem.

Landing pages reduce the cost of each specific click.

The number of advertisers in recent years has increased significantly, and, as you know, popular contextual advertising systems are a kind of auctions, where the greater the offer, the higher the price for a keyword. Paying up to $ 50 for each click (in super competitive niches) and not getting a buyer is not very profitable. Landing pages increase the Quality Score (Quality Score) of your keywords, give you a higher CTR, and lower CPC.

It is worth noting that landing pages work very well with targeted contextual and banner advertising, so it is very important to devote enough time here to build campaigns or contact specialists. Ultimately, you pay less.

For each specific advertising company, you must use separate landing pages.

Transition landing pages (Clickthrough Rate) have the highest conversion, followed by leads (Lead Capture) or pages with forms to fill out (name, email, and so on). It is useful to take into account these statistics when building an advertising campaign, and even better to check in practice using split-testing.

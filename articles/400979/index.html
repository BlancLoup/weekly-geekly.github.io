Philips Xenium V787 review: long-running workhorse

A utilitarian smartphone with a huge 5000 mAh battery, which does not claim to be a smoothie gadget, but at the same time has such important parameters as an 8-core processor, 2 gigabytes of RAM and micsoSD card support. All this based on the Android OS with the ability to install two SIM and long battery life.



Obviously, the flagship models of different manufacturers are the quintessence of innovation, but does every user need such a set of options and is he ready to overpay for something he rarely uses? In this regard, the Philips solution looks attractive to ‚Äúmultiply‚Äù users: two SIMs for combining tariff options, good performance plus a capacious battery and Xenium technology, which allow it to ‚Äústay afloat‚Äù for a long time.

The main feature is a capacious battery


Let's go with the battery and get started. The Xenium V787 uses a non-removable 5000 mAh battery. The Xenium series itself, starting from classic phones, was famous for its long battery life, which was transferred to smartphones. In the stated characteristics appear up to 24 hours talk time or 864 hours in standby mode.

In practice, you can expect about two days of work in the combined cycle, which includes about 2.5 hours of voice calls, periodic correspondence in instant messengers and social networks, checking mail, surfing the network, and taking photos and videos.

Heavy loads, namely video, will put the battery in 17.5 hours, the games are moreover, in 7, but do not forget about the initial positioning of the device.

For comparison, the current flagships and devices of the middle price segment with the same loads surrender about twice, which, you see, is indicative.

The power saving mode on the V787 is activated in two ways: by moving the slider on the right side of the case, or from the application of the same name. In user mode, it is possible to configure the settings that will be disabled when it is activated. Among them, turning off WiFi, data transmission, deactivating Bluetooth and GPS, as well as closing all running applications at once.

Appearance


A typical Android smartphone is often perceived as a black bar without any particular individuality. In the case of the Philips Xenium V787, this concept is partly applicable and rather even hypertrophied: we have a black and rough housing block with a silver border encircling the perimeter in the form of a chamfer.

The size of the body to match the screen diagonal, and weight with high autonomy, medium. In his hand, the smartphone is nice, does not slip, but the build quality. For tactile sensations - a thing!

The V787 screen is a 5-inch IPS-matrix with a resolution of 1920x1080 pixels. The picture quality is good: the sharpness does not fall on the fonts, the brightness with a margin, the viewing angles are close to the maximum. The color temperature is not adjustable, and the default is natural or slightly cold, but for a strictly device it is acceptable. Capacitive sensor, supports up to five simultaneous touches and quickly and efficiently processes input commands and text.



Above the display we see the conversational speaker reticulum and the front camera, but the operation indicator in idle is imperceptible.

The area under the screen is reserved for the touch control keys: "return", "home" and "menu" with dim backlighting.


The left side of the case is occupied by separated volume keys, and the right side is given to the power key and the slider to activate the battery saving mode.





On the upper edge, we see only a 3.5mm audio jack, and the bottom one represents the micro USB connector and a microphone.

At the back we are waiting for a metal non-removable panel, which is surrounded by two plastic inserts. The bottom one hides the call speaker, and the top one, removable, covers the SIM card and microSD memory card compartment, and also surrounds the rear camera lens and flash.

Software and interface


The smartphone runs on Android OS version 5.1 and a proprietary shell from Philips. The main principle of the add-in is the rejection of the main menu in favor of placing content on desktops.

The lock screen is modest and moderately informative: in addition to the status bar, there is a widget with the current date and time. Swipe from bottom to top starts the camera, and in the opposite direction - unlocks the device.



V787 desktops are widely personalized: in addition to wallpapers, widgets, application shortcuts, and various folders can be arranged along the grid. From a graphic point of view, the design of the shell is a mixture of brutality and practicality.



The settings menu migrated from classic Android: a vertical list of available options, grouped by type, where a simple icon is located to the left of the parameter name.



The management of the Dual-SIM service is simple and straightforward: it is possible to activate or deactivate each of the cards, plus setting usage priorities for calls, messages and data transfer.



In addition to Google applications, you can find in the base package: file manager, backup, FM radio and power saving mode. A little, but for the first time enough.

Camera


The device uses a 5-megapixel front and 13-megapixel rear camera with autofocus and flash. Based on the characteristics and settings, we can conclude that the manufacturer places a moderate emphasis on this element, although everything necessary and sufficient is present.



Of the settings and templates worth noting shooting modes (motion tracking, panoramas), exposure and white balance settings, image parameters (sharpness, hue, saturation, brightness), stabilization, light sensitivity, HDR, and face detection and, of course, " Beautiful face". This is not a complete list of options.



We suggest you evaluate the quality of shooting by test shots, and from ourselves we note that the device quickly focuses on the object, slightly angles and reduces sharpness to the edges of the frame, and also demonstrates low detail of the images. However, content is quite suitable for publishing on social networks, although we were waiting for the best.

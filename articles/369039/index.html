How Chinese programmers live at work

40-year-old Dai Xiang, a former engineer of a machine-building company, fell asleep on the floor at the beginning of his career during 72-hour shifts. After that he moved to the information technology industry and slept face down on the table for 15 years. But in 2015, he founded his own cloud computing company, and his first order was the installation of a dozen bunk beds.

If Google employees live in the office , because they want to save on rent, in Chinese companies it is a necessity: business grows so fast that new people do not have time to recruit.


Photos: Jason Lee, Reuters and Techinsider

The Chinese information technology industry has been developing particularly rapidly in recent years. Competition in the domestic market is one of the strongest in the world. In order not to fail deadlines, people have to spend the night right in the office. In the case of BaishanCloud, all the conditions were created for this - the founder installed bunk beds and officially allowed to sleep on them or right in the workplace.

Technology is the work of the brain in the first place. Employees need time to look for inspiration. Our recreation area is open not only at night, it is also allowed to sleep during the day.

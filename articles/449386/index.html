Internet for summer resident. We get the maximum speed in 4G networks. Part 1. Choosing the right router.

A few years ago, I already conducted a survey of communications for a summer resident or living in his house, where broadband access is not available or costs such money that it is easier to move to the city. Since then, a lot of terabytes have been transmitted and it became interesting to me, and what is now on the market for good access to the network via LTE or 4G. So, I have collected some old and new routers with the ability to work through cellular networks and compared the speed of work and their functions. For the results I ask for cat. By tradition, if anyone is too lazy to read, you can watch the video.



Let's start with the fact that I did not set myself the task of finding out which of the cellular operators provides the best speed, but decided to find out which of the modems / routers provides a higher speed under the same conditions. Beeline was chosen as the provider. The following operators are available in my region: Beeline, MTS, Megafon, Tele2, Yota, WiFire. ‚ÄúStriped‚Äù was chosen only because I already had his sim card. Testing method


To the base station, in a straight line, about 8 km, if you believe the router. All tests were conducted on weekdays from 11 to 13, since at that time the least load on 4G networks. Fundamentally, I do not consider the 3G network test, since they also carry the voice communication load, and only data is transferred via 4G. Warning conversations about VoLTE I will say that at the testing site the voice via LTE is not yet launched. The test was carried out three times using the Speedtest service, the data were entered into a table and the average value of the download speed, data transfer and ping was calculated. Attention was also paid to the capabilities of the router. Test conditions: clear weather, no precipitation. Foliage in the trees there. The height of the equipment is 10 meters above the ground.

Tests for all devices were carried out separately for the "bare" router, in the factory configuration. The second test was conducted when connecting to a small directional antenna, if the device has the appropriate connectors. The third test was carried out with a connection to a large panel antenna.

In the last column, I added the final cost of the solution: for example, a router + modem + antenna can receive better than just a router, but cost less. Color gradation is introduced to visually identify one or another basic device to which an additional antenna can be connected.

results




Looking at this histogram, it becomes immediately clear how much the speed of reception and transmission depends on the antenna gain. In addition, in the first test, without using external antennas, the sensitivity of the own antennas and radio modules of the modems is obvious. The use of a directional panel antenna has increased the connection speed three times - is this not the result when you want a lot of Internet for a little money? But do not forget that the acquisition of the router does not guarantee good communication and you need to add an antenna, especially when the communication tower is not visible to the naked eye. Sometimes, the cost of the antenna can be equal to the cost of the router and it is worthwhile to think about acquiring a ready-made device, like the Zyxel LTE7460-M608, where the antenna and the router are assembled together. Moreover, such a solution is not afraid of temperature drops and precipitation. But you can‚Äôt take out a USB modem or an ordinary router, and in an ordinary attic they will have a hard time - in the summer it will hang due to overheating, and in the winter it will just freeze. But increasing the length of the cable assembly from the antenna to the receiving device can offset all the benefits of installing a good expensive antenna. And here is the rule: the closer the radio module is to the antenna, the lower the losses and the higher the speed.
For those who love numbers, I collected the results of all tests in a table, and the last column was the cost of assembly. I highlighted this or that device with the addition of antennas or without antennas to facilitate visual search of results.

Separately, I decided to test the work of the router without an obstacle and with an obstacle in the form of a double glazed window. That is, simply installing the Zyxel LTE7460-M608 router behind and in front of the window. The speed of reception fell imperceptibly, but the transfer dropped almost threefold. If the glass will have any spraying, the results will be even more pitiable. The conclusion is obvious: there should be as few obstacles as possible between the antenna and the base station.



findings


According to the measurement results, it is obvious that the capabilities of the communication modules built into the routers are significantly different, but even without an additional antenna, this speed is quite enough for watching videos or videoconferencing via Skype. Nevertheless, it is obvious from the graphs that the use of an antenna can increase the speed several times. But here it is necessary to observe a balance between the invested funds and the result obtained. For example: buying a Zyxel LTE3316-M604 and a panel antenna, you can get a result even better than the finished device Zyxel LTE7460-M608. But then the panel antenna will be almost twice as large, and the router should be placed in close proximity to the antenna - this can cause difficulties.

As a result, the winner in the speed test - Zyxel LTE3316-M604 with a large panel antenna. It is necessary to tinker a bit with the direction of the antenna, and the interface of the router only in English can cause discomfort to someone. The winner in the test of functionality - Keenetic Viva with 4G modem. This router can work both in an apartment with a classic Internet, and in the country, where only cellular networks are available from providers. The winner in the test of ready-made solutions - Zyxel LTE7460-M608. This all-weather router is good because it can be placed anywhere, it is not afraid of any weather conditions, but to complete the work it will need a Wi-Fi access point, a mesh system or an organized LAN. For frequent trips and travels by car, the Huawei E5372 mobile router is well suited - it can work both autonomously and being connected to a charger or power bank. Well, if you want a maximum speed for a minimum of money, then you should look for TP-Link Archer MR200 v1 - it has a good radio module and the ability to connect an external antenna, although there were defective copies.

Nanofootball

Within the framework of the annual world soccer championship among robots, Robocup will be the first to hold competitions between subminiature robots. The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) participates in their organization. This year the competitions will be held outside the main program of the championship, but in 2008 it is planned to organize a corresponding robofball league.


Fields for nanofutbola

Competitions will be held on a chip with sixteen "fields" measuring 2.5 x 2.5 mm. Instead of a ball, a disk of silicon dioxide with a diameter of 100 microns will be used, and player robots are microelectromechanical systems (MEMS) 250-300 microns long and with a mass measured in nanograms (by weight, robots are classified as nanodevices).

Roscosmos successfully launched a British satellite into orbit



The Briz-M upper stage and the British telecommunications satellite Inmarsat-5F2 successfully separated from the Proton-M launch vehicle launched from Baikonur. This happened on February 2, 2015 at 07 hours 02 minutes Moscow time. According to the press service of Roskosmos , the satellite has already begun its work.

The launch of the Proton-M space launch vehicle was carried out on February 1, 2015 at 3.31 pm Moscow time from the launch complex of the site 200 of the Baikonur cosmodrome.

The Inmarsat-5F2 spacecraft was launched into the target orbit by the Briz-M upper stage. With this launch, Roskosmos successfully launched the 2015 launch program.

Inmarsat is the second largest satellite network after Iridium. Its coverage is 98% and is provided by 11 geostationary satellites. However, data transmission requires pointing to the satellite or, in the case of an omnidirectional antenna, the line-of-sight of the satellite. The derived satellite was the second of the new, 5th series of satellites.

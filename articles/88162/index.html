Medvedev stood up for iFolder

At the request of the President of the Russian Federation, the Ministry of Internal Affairs begins to verify the legality of its actions with regard to iFolder and Agava


As it was written earlier , on March 17, in the premises of the data center of the Agava company, which owns the iFolder service, investigators appeared from the third emergency response of the Main Investigative Department at the Moscow Main Department of Internal Affairs. They presented a protocol on the need to conduct operational search activities in order to search for evidence placed on the iFolder.Ru website .



The press service of Agave, one of the largest hosting providers in Russia, said that this law enforcement act is "an unprecedented event that puts at risk and doubt the existence and development of any business in RuNet."

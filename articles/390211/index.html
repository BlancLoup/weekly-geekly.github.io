April 14 will be held the traditional straight line of Vladimir Putin, where he will answer questions from the Russians. An anonymous source from among the organizers of the program told the Kommersant publication that the organizers introduced elements of high technology: you can ask the President of the Russian Federation not only by phone, but also by ICQ,

Technical preparation for the broadcast has already begun, the source said.

Unfortunately, information about using ICQ turned out to be false. Just a press secretary of the head of state, Dmitry Peskov, clarified that the ICQ messenger will not be used to collect questions.

ICQ is a popular instant messaging pager in the past. Developed by Israeli schoolchildren in 1996, two years later sold to AOL Corporation for $ 407 million. It now belongs to the Russian investment fund Mail.ru Group.

The video stream will go to a global audience through the Russia Today website, as well as through YouTube (in Russian, French and German).

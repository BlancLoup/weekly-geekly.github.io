Touchpad Mini Keyboard Review


It is not always convenient to use a full-sized keyboard to control your home HTPC or mini-computer on Android. A compact solution is various mini-keyboards, which also combine the functions of a mouse.

The keyboard comes in a box with a sounding "World's most mini wireless keyboard mouse combo":

According to the information on the box, it will work both with the Windows operating system and with Linux, OS X. And of course with Android.
The very same keyboard has a not very memorable name - UKB-500-RF.

The package includes instructions and a USB cable for charging:


QWERTY-keyboard with many additional multimedia buttons:

Russian letters are applied in red and are slightly lost on a black background.

Indicator lights: charge, connection activity (blinks when buttons are pressed). The value of the 3rd indicator I could not figure out.


Moving the mouse around the screen using the touchpad. On the right of the touchpad there is a page scroll bar. Multitouch is not.


Behind the protective cover:


Under it lies the 800 mAh battery and receiver:

The receiver is connected to the USB port of the computer, or to the tablet OTG cable. It is determined automatically, installation of additional drivers is not required. Data transmission is carried out at a frequency of 2.4 GHz.

The power lever is on top:


MiniUSB charger input:


Understands the keyboard by unscrewing 4 screws on the perimeter:


Buttons and touchpad:


Pressing is done by pressing the button on the rubber backing:




The size of the keyboard is slightly larger than iPazzport:


In the hands of the keyboard is very comfortable, something like a gamepad:


A short video use when connected to a tablet:


The keyboard is convenient to use, it has a clear keystroke and a lot of additional multimedia buttons. The touchpad at first may seem small, but over time you get used to it.

You can buy a UKB-500-RF keyboard at dx.com for $ 22.

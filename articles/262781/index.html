"Moore's Law" at Risk - Intel Delays New Chips



Intel stated that the delivery of its next-generation Cannonlake chips would be delayed for half a year, which was the second time in a row when they did not release their new processor according to the two-year cycle of Moore's Law. This continues the current trend: last year's 14-nm Broadwell chip was delayed, and even the releases of Haswell and Ivy Bridge took place in violation of the planned schedule. Intel reported that the delay with its new 10-nm chips is associated with the increased complexity of producing transistors of such small sizes. Responding to the expressed dissatisfaction, CEO Brian Krzanich stated that: "The last two technological transitions have shown us that the cyclical nature of Moore's law today is rather two and a half years than the traditional two."



Commenting on the lack of a range of new chips, Intel said that they were going to release a new Kaby Lake chip using 14-nm technology, based on the Skylake architecture.

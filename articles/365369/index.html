3D exhibits of 6 new museums added to Google Art Project gallery

The Google Art Project team has announced that more than 200 full-size 3D models of exhibits from 6 new museums have appeared in the virtual gallery. The following museums offered the opportunity to work with their exposition:
  • California Academy of Sciences
  • Israel Museum
  • Los Angeles Museum of Natural History
  • Dallas Museum of Art
  • National Museum of Oriental Art in Rome
  • Vienna Museum of Art History


Google Art Project allows you to virtually visit the most famous museums in the world, moving along them in much the same way as a user moves to Street View. Some pictures of famous artists are represented by gigapixel photos taken specifically for the service with a special camera. Other museum exhibits are scanned by a 3D scanner, the resulting models can be rotated, scaled and viewed from all sides, which is not available under the conditions of a regular museum.

Google Culture Academy supports three major projects: Google Art Project , Google Historic Moments and Google World Wonders . It is estimated that users on average view each work for more than one minute, while in a regular museum they spend less than 20 seconds on it.

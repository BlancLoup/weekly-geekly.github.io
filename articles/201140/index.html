Universal quadcopter with Kinect on board: able to fly, does not sink in water, rolls along the ground



Of course, I also wanted to write that this quadcopter, created by the Japanese to work as an automatic rescue station, does not burn in the fire. But it will not work - it burns, like all plastic devices. But it's not about the fire, but the fact that the quad copter MUWA (Multi-field Universal Wheel for Propellers) is really a universal device.

Firstly, it is able to fly along a predetermined trajectory, at the same time receiving a 3D-image of the area using the Kinect sensor. Secondly, it can land at a given angle (and moreover land very precisely) if necessary. On the ground, it does not lie with useless cargo, but moves according to the principle of a wheel (for which the developers equipped the quadrocopter with an external hoop).

To all, this device is also able to float, which may be necessary in case of accidents and catastrophes on the water, when it is difficult to determine where people are. MUWA, according to the plan, can deliver life-saving tools for people on the water.

With regard to movements on the ground, the MUWA can pass into the slightly opened doors, crawl through the bars, etc.

The device, as mentioned above, transmits a three-dimensional "picture" to its operator, who already determines that there are people / various objects on the ground that the quadrocopter is currently viewing.

In general, this is a universal intelligence agent, which, unless it is possible to deliver people to rescuers.

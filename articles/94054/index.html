Social media applications as a marketing tool

I would like to touch on a topic that is so fashionable today as social networking apps. Recently, most Internet resources have focused on applications as a way to make big money in a short time. The first part of this article will analyze the most promising application markets that have shown rapid growth in recent years - the United States, China and Russia. In the second part of the article, the applications will be considered from a slightly different angle - we will discuss their use as a full-fledged marketing tool.


US application market

According to a study by Inside Virtual Goods, the US virtual goods market in 2009 reached a turnover of 1 billion dollars.

The study took into account both virtual gifts that Facebook and social network users buy each other, as well as a variety of products used in online games to more effectively fight rival players or monsters. The $ 1 billion figure is about 2 times higher than the income from virtual goods for the previous year.

It is possible that in the future the turnover of the market for virtual goods in the United States will reach 1.6 billion dollars. China Application Market

Qzone , the leading Chinese social network for teenagers (376 million users), is the largest in the world. Tencent, which manages this network, recently announced that its revenue for 2008 was more than a billion dollars. 3

At the same time, only 12% of Qzone's network revenue accounts for advertising - the rest comes from the sale of virtual goods, including various applications . In total, in 2009, China is going to spend about $ 1.7 billion on online advertising, which is 4% of all advertising expenses (while in the US they are going to spend $ 25.7 billion on online advertising in 2009). 4 This rather small budget of online advertising in China is mainly distributed among the four main news portals, which receive the main income from online advertising. This forces social networks and other sites to search for more advanced methods of traffic monetization - for example, intensifying the application development process.

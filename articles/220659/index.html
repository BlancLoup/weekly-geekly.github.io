How the Chinese print 10 houses in 24 hours: video of the process



On Habr√© previously published the news that the Chinese have learned how to print low-cost homes using 3D printers. These are not palaces, of course, but the houses are quite livable. And given the number of people in China, this technology is very popular.

So, the other day a video of construction was published, where you can see that the process of creating a house is very original.

It is worth noting that the cost of creating such a house is only $ 4,800, which is affordable for very many Chinese. This is an inexpensive social housing that is created from cement, construction waste, fiberglass and some other components. The resulting mixture is not harmful to humans, but what is used to create it literally from construction waste makes the technology "green."

In the future, the Chinese plan to build not only one-story houses, but entire skyscrapers. And that's how it works:



The printer that is used to create a home is quite large. It is 6.6 meters high, 10 meters wide, and its length is 150 meters.

So far, such a technology cannot be made common, due to a number of regulatory difficulties (in China). Everything is logical - after all, we need permits of various kinds of commissions and services, which need to make sure that the house does not collapse in the very first month after the residents move in.

James Gosling explains why he left Oracle

James Gosling gave an exclusive interview to eWeek, where for the first time he gave a detailed account of the reasons that led him to leave Oracle .

According to him, there were a number of reasons for dismissal, starting with the salary. Oracle offered him exactly the same salary, but they deprived of all bonuses, and in fact if the business went well, the bonuses brought in much more money than the basic salary.

In addition, Oracle‚Äôs job hierarchy did not have a position of such a level that Gosling had in Sun (fellow), so he actually had to descend heavily down the job ladder.

But even this did not become the last straw. Gosling tolerated until he realized how tough Oracle was going to control it. The company's policy was that he, as an employee, has no right to express his personal opinion about Java, if this opinion contradicts the company's policy. Now Java has become Oracle‚Äôs intellectual property, and the ability of its creators to make decisions has been minimized.

He had previously doubted the "ethical purity" of Oracle, but being in such a situation, he decided to leave the company altogether.

The main problem of Oracle, says Gosling, is due to micromanagement. That is, the head of the company Larry Ellison makes decisions on all matters, even the smallest.

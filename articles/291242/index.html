Samsung will occupy the Blackberry niche in the corporate market

By the end of 2015, Tizen OS certification will be completed. Samsung has already submitted documents to the Federal Service for Technical and Export Control. The certification guarantees protection against data leakage and eavesdropping ‚Äî it will allow Tizen devices to be promoted to corporate customers as secure gadgets. RBC source said that Samsung is trying to occupy a niche Blackberry. Andrey Tikhonov, Samsung, noted the expansion of the niche for secure smartphones - this is due to scandals with data leakage.



The first Blackberry device, RIM, showed in 1997. It was a communicator with a qwerty keyboard for corporate communication. In 2013, the company changed its name to Blackberry, and is now in talks to sell . Potential Blackberry buyers are Microsoft, Xiaomi, Lenovo and Huawei. Blackberry has changed its priority towards software development.

In 2011, the LiMo Foundation and the Linux Foundation launched a Linux-based Linux platform, Tizen. The project was led by Samsung, Intel, Samsung, Fujitsu, NEC, Panasonic, KT, Sprint, SK Telecom, Orange, NTT docomo, Vodafone, and the Tizen Foundation in 2012.

The first Samsung Tizen smartphone released in India. This is a budget four-inch phone Z1 for 5700 rupees. The company is considering the possibility of selling devices to individuals.

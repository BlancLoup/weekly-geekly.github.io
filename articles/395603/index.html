Pavel Durov: Telegram is not going to provide encryption keys to third parties


Photo: REUTERS / Dado Ruvic

Today Gosudma in the second and third reading voted for the adoption of a package of amendments by the deputies Yarovaya and Ozerov. The amendment of the legislation, initiated by the amendments, has far-reaching consequences for telecommunication companies operating in Russia. In particular, companies are now obliged to store all the data of their subscribers for many months. Messengers and social networks at the request of the authorities must provide the decoding keys to the encrypted correspondence of users.

Pavel Durov, the founder of the social network "Vkontakte" and the Telegram messenger, said today that he was not going to fulfill this requirement. He said that Telegram will not give out data or encryption keys to third parties, including the government, writes Izvestia. According to him, the laws that are adopted in various countries will not affect this decision in any way.

As for Russia, a fine of up to 1 million rubles is provided for refusing the requirement of the authorities to provide decoding keys.

However, the law does not provide for blocking a resource that refused to provide keys. This is evidenced by independent lawyer Anton Bogatov. For example, to block Telegram, you will need to add Apple and Google IP resources to the register of prohibited sites of Roskomnadzor. Namely - the directory of applications that host the program. At the moment, all correspondence of Telegram users is encrypted. Data is stored on the servers of the service only in encrypted form. There is also the possibility of direct encryption. To enable this option, the user needs to enable the secret chat mode, after which the messages will be transmitted between users in encrypted form directly, bypassing the server. And in this case, Telegram will not be able to transmit the decrypted correspondence of users to the authorities even if Pavel Durov decides to cooperate with the Russian law enforcers.

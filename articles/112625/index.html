operating system


In advertising texts about Windows 7, attention was paid to the work with touch screens. True, it did not say that their full support is only in Windows 7 Home Premium (and higher). If you try to put Starter or Home Basic on a tablet, you will be left without multitouch and handwriting recognition. Therefore, on Odeon'e (as well as Iros'e and Viewpad'e) it is Home Premium.



The dimensions of the controls are sufficient for hitting them with your finger. 70 percent :), misses do happen. But in general, you can use - click, double-click worked out normally. Right click - either hold your finger for a couple of seconds, or touch the screen with two fingers. Changing the scale of mixing / breeding fingers - not everywhere, but there is.

Reading texts

As I have written more than once, the tablet was bought, first of all, for reading djvu, pdf, cbr / cbz. Formats, in general, are related - for the most part they are scans of books / magazines in various containers. Therefore, it was logical to look for one program for viewing. The first (and last :)) candidate was STDU Viewer . Normally, it opens all the above formats, remembers the place where it stopped, there is support for touchscreens in the form of binding actions (page turning) to zones on the screen - as in the PDA and communicator readers, changing the size by mixing / spreading fingers.



Additionally displays fb2, but this is so, a nice bonus. I do not read fb2 on the tablet (for this there is a communicator or, if it hurts, an electronic book). If I read, I would put alreader.

When the state is lazy


In the US and Europe, charity has been put on the conveyor. Especially in the USA. Back in the 18th century, there they thought that the state has more interesting things to do than to engage in its own social policy. And they put this thankless task on the shoulders of the public: legislation that allows you to deduct from taxes the amount spent on charity, support for the volunteer movement, competent propaganda (including the Church) created all the conditions for the development of charity. And its scale was such that already in the 19th century surprised Europeans:

The culture of philanthropy created in the USA entailed the emergence of a whole industry: in 2015, donations from individuals, foundations and corporations in the United States amounted to $ 373 billion. And these are not only numbers, but also the infrastructure for the use of this money.


When corporations feel sorry for money


Quantitative growth eventually grew into a qualitative one: somewhere in the 70s of the 20th century, intelligent people began to think not only about where to spend, but how. The question arose: why should anyone give money if they can be spent with the same effect?
In the 70s of the 20th century, the concept of corporate social responsibility begins to develop: an orientation toward receiving net profit without taking into account social influence becomes a bad tone and an indicator of inefficiency. Technological corporations, whose success is based on the personal qualities of their employees, are also beginning to gain weight and influence: their social projects become role models.



Cinema at INFOSYS Mysore Training Campus


Here is an example of Indian Infosys . In the early 80s in India, there were two problems that could not be tolerated: this is an extremely low standard of living and the terrible quality of the code of the then programs. The model they chose: "to educate a bunch of people in their homeland >> something to do in the West >> to repeat the profit" justified itself. The emphasis on education (the largest corporate training center in the world , active charitable work in this area, support for OpenSourse projects) turned Infosys, based on $ 200, into a giant with a total capitalization of $ 40 billion.

When everyone needs a profit

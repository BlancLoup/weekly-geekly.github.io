Russian service "MoiOfis" will compete with Microsoft Office and Google Docs

In the spring of 2015, New Cloud Technologies (NOT) will launch a new service, MyOffice, which should compete with Microsoft Office services and Google docs. Developers set ambitious goals. By 2020, the company wants to get 25% of the domestic market and enter the markets of Europe and Latin America.

Dmitry Komissarov, general director of HOT, told about the new edition of Cnews.

Work on the service began in 2013. The development team includes 130 people, some of whom are former developers of the Lexicon text editor and QuickOffice package.

"MyOffice" includes all the necessary set of office applications for cloud services: a text editor, a spreadsheet editor, presentations, postal service, contact directory and calendar. Supported formats are ODF, Microsoft Office (doc, docx), as well as export to PDF.


Text editor "MyOffice" on iPad

At the moment there are only beta versions of the service "MyOffice" for the web, iPad and Android OS. The first public release is scheduled for April-May. In September, there will be versions for Linux and OS X.


The editor of the spreadsheet "MyOffice" on the Android-tablet

The main investor of the project is the company "Service Plus". According to the information of the Kontur.Focus service, its share in the project is 88%. Another 7% is owned by the vice-president of Rostelecom, Alexey Basov. The remaining 5% is owned by Dmitry Komissarov.

But after the change of leadership of the Ministry of Communications and Mass Media, the project was abandoned. In March 2014, Komissarov created the company New Cloud Technologies.

NOTA intends to receive profits from the sale of per-account licenses, from subscription fees for the support of private clouds, as well as from the distribution of the product through telecom operators, hosting providers and device manufacturers. Private users are scheduled to provide service for free.

By 2020, service developers plan to get a share of 20% in the Brazilian and Latin American market and up to 10% in the European market. Abroad "MoyOfis" will be offered under a different brand - Collabio. But for now, 95% of the global market in the office application segment belongs to Microsoft.

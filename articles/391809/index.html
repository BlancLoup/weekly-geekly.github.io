MPAA hunts for clones of Popcorn Time, even with legal content.

When MPAA last year achieved the removal of the popular fork PopcornTime.io , many developers switched to the Butter clone, which is positioned as a platform for distributing legal content.

Through the Butter app, only free movies from independent directors from the vodo.net , archive.org and CCC Media collections are distributed . There are no links to unlicensed content. However, this does not stop the MPAA. Lawyers from Hollywood film studios sent a letter to GitHub with the requirement to remove the PopcornTime.sh repository (fork Butter) and absolutely all Popcorn Time forks in accordance with the DMCA law. The legal program Butter also came under the distribution.

"There are many Forks Popcorn Time, and they are all 100% unlicensed and violate the law," - stated in the letter MPAA .

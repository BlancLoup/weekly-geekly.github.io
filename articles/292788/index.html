Students of IT-specialties have become more mercantile and overestimated their salary expectations by a third

According to a joint study of Mail.ru Group and the recruiting company Smart Start , future graduates of IT specialties overestimate their salary expectations by more than 30%. The survey involved 345 students of the best universities in Moscow. They should have indicated the desired salary immediately after graduation, after three and ten years.

After graduation, students plan to receive 50 thousand rubles a month. This figure is 25% higher than the market average. With three years of experience, they hope to receive 85 thousand rubles. A ten-year experience, in their opinion, guarantees an amount not lower than 160 thousand rubles.

The most optimistic were the future mobile application developers, system administrators and information security specialists. They believe that without work experience they will be offered at least 58 thousand a month. The profession of system administrator, by the way, is recognized as the most masculine in the IT sphere. This is evidenced by the results of a survey conducted in mid-May. According to students, among experienced professionals, web developers and analysts are most in demand, who should receive 173 and 170 thousand rubles, respectively.


Source: Smart Start

Research participants believe that salary level is most influenced by the accumulation of professional experience (56%), career growth (38%), and unique employee skills (35%), reports Roem.ru.

At the end of June, Mail.Ru and Smart Start found out the attitude of students at IT departments to internships. Over 80% of students find them useful, but over 60% of those surveyed do not agree to train for free under any circumstances. The survey was conducted among students of leading Russian universities: MGTU them. N. E. Bauman, Moscow State University. MV Lomonosov, MIPT and others. The respondents named the most important criteria for the internship as high salaries, the availability of prospects for the company, the size and reputation of the company, as well as work with real tasks.
According to HeadHunter, the average salary offered for interns in Moscow and St. Petersburg is about 25,000 rubles.

Among the reasons that prompted respondents to link the future with information technology, interest in the field is leading by a large margin: this is how 81.1% of girls and 67.7% of young people responded. The wage level is in second place: 37.7% of girls and 43% of young people are guided by this factor.

Rating of countries in which it is more favorable to deploy a server farm



Consulting company Cushman and Wakefield conducted another case study called DATA CENTER RISK INDEX, which concerns the question of which countries are most favorable to host and operate server farms. Recall that a similar study was conducted three years ago.

A survey was conducted in which more than 4 thousand owners and users of data centers around the world took part. The issues related to such concepts as the cost of electricity, average data transfer rate, level of corporate tax, ease of doing business, availability of water, and political stability were taken into account. Attractive from the point of view of the construction and operation of data centers, as it turned out, are the countries with a cold climate, located near the North Pole. Iceland, Norway, Sweden occupy a leading position.

But the worst result, compared with the other participating countries, showed Nigeria. The country ranks 37th in the ranking due to poor conditions for deploying server farms. These include high temperatures, natural disasters (such as tsunamis and earthquakes) and political problems. India, China, Turkey and Indonesia also hit the list of unfavorable countries for hosting data centers for the same reasons as Nigeria.

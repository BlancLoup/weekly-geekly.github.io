Mobile phones protect against Alzheimer's disease

For many years, scientists have been trying to identify the relationship between telephone use and brain cancer. Recently, these studies have led to an unusual result. In mice, with a predisposition to Alzheimer's disease, exposure to waves similar to those from a telephone, has led to a decrease in the likelihood of developing this disease. So, talking a lot on a mobile phone may even be useful.

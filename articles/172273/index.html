Intel offers to consider the display service


At CeBIT, held in Hannover, Intel demonstrated a new technology DaaS (Display as a Service, Display as a Service) that can alter the way gadgets are used. Technology breaks the hard link between the image source and the display, just like virtualization software removes the link between the processor and the operating system. Using DaaS, we can transfer the tablet screen to a large TV, multiply the same picture onto multiple displays or connect several monitors into one.

Another DaaS feature described by Intel Labs Divya Kolar in the Intel blog is to combine images from multiple devices on one screen, so that the whole family can work together, taking care of their own business. In essence, DaaS turns a display into a set of pixels that any device can use. The screen image can be sent both by wire and wirelessly. The exhibition also showed an adapter in the form of a small box for implementing DaaS on TVs that do not have the necessary functionality.
Thus, we can say that the idea, embodied in for some reason not very beloved by our habrasoobschestvom, but convenient and competitive technology WiDi , received a further and rather unexpected continuation.

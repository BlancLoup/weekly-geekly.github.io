Engine and battery


Well, if we have an electric bike and a battery here, we cannot but notice, then let's look at the motor. In the TW-E9L Twitter model, it was hidden in the carriage knot, which is why the bike did not betray any superpowers. In Twitter, the Mantis E-1 is a more traditional approach - the electric motor is in the rear wheel and looks like a fat bushing :)





Perhaps the key characteristic of the motor is its power and in this respect the Mantis E-1 is not bad. The engine's rated power is 350 watts, or about half the horsepower (736 watts). It turns out that this is already officially a moped, since it does not fit the traffic regulations (up to 250 W) (it will not fit into them again in a couple of paragraphs):
"A bicycle is a vehicle, other than wheelchairs, which has at least two wheels and is driven, as a rule, by the muscular energy of persons on this vehicle, in particular with the help of pedals or handles, and may also have an electric motor with a rated maximum power continuous duty mode, not exceeding 0.25 kW, automatically shutting down at a speed exceeding 25 km / h. "
The brushless motor (gear motor-wheel), designed for a voltage of 36 V. Inside it is a planetary gearbox, which develops up to 60 N ¬∑ m output torque. The gearbox has a free-wheeling clutch, thanks to which the wheel can rotate freely at idle (with fairly quiet crackling, unlike the same TW-E9L). When the motor does not work, the clutch mechanically opens the clutch with the gearbox so that its gears do not spin and do not impede the rotation of the wheel. From here two conclusions: 1) roll forward is better 2) recovery here is impossible.

The second component of the power plant is a lithium-ion battery from LG, located on the frame. Its capacity is 468 W ¬∑ h (36 V, 13 A ¬∑ h; configuration 10S4P - 10 consecutive blocks of 4 parallel 18650-elements) and from one charge it provides a fairly large power reserve - up to 120 km in the help mode.

Compared to the same TW-E9L, the Mantis E-1 battery pack has several advantages:

  • It is removable (and not integrated into the frame), which means it can be removed - to charge at home, not to get stuck and to use as a large paverbank :) Well, for winter storage it is a more practical option.
  • You can use several blocks for greater autonomy - in case you are ripe for the Around the World. This is also the possibility of self-replacement of the battery in the event of a breakdown.

The battery is removable, but just so any pioneer can not do this: you need a key, which comes with two pieces, complete with a bicycle. After turning the key, the battery on the rail rises and is removed. Everything is going in the reverse order: the battery slides along the rail and is locked with a key.





A few observations:

  • The battery pack is connected to the bike with only two contacts (for power transmission), all the ‚Äúbrains‚Äù (the controller for controlling the motor-wheel) are located in the compartment under the battery - an excellent constructive solution.
  • The electrician looks protected, so it is unlikely that there will be anything from the rain (although it is not recommended to ride the rain in the instructions).

In addition to the lock, there are several other functional elements in the battery:

  • On the left is a charge indicator of several LEDs, which is activated by a button.
  • On the right is a triangular rubber plug, behind which there is a charging connector, a USB port and a red switch that disconnects the entire electrics of the bike from the battery.





The USB port is useful for anything, from the most trivial - charging a smartphone when Strava finishes the last charge of a long trip. As an option - to power the action-camera or a column that can be hung on the steering wheel, for example. Well, or a flashlight, although it is unlikely. Yes, anything - the main thing is to choose the right L-shaped wiring to reduce the likelihood of damage (wires, battery pack and legs).

On-board computer


This is the third review of the electric bike - they all had different on-board computers. And this one is probably the best. It's not as big as Twitter's TW-E9L, but not as small as Airwheel R8. So it can be called the golden mean.



A rather large LCD screen is slightly recessed into the case. Unlike the Twitter TW-E9L LCD, this one doesn't fade in the sun. This is true - information is easily read in any conditions (there is a backlight for darkness).



The largest figures, at the top, display the speed - kilometers or miles per hour (up to tenths). Minimum inertia: when stopping on the "speedometer" immediately 0.



The second large number, PAS (Pedal Assist System), displays the mode of support by the electric motor: from 1 (minimum) to 5 (maximum), 0 - support is turned off (and then the on-board computer simply counts the speed / mileage). Slightly higher is the battery charge, and not only in uninformative "sticks", but also in percentages.

The most "small print" on the screen displays the mileage: current (TRIP), total (ODO) and travel time (TIME).

Tests


At the beginning of this section, I would like to recall the first electric bike (AIrwheel R8), which was reviewed in our blog. This time, there were no falls, which personally makes me happy :) But another problem arose: the electric bike's power reserve turned out to be unusually large, that the unprepared cyclist simply wouldn't be able to defuse the bike in one go - the rolls would be rubbed :) We had to discharge it in several iterations, but taking In the hands of a calculator, a total of about 130-140 km characters were counted on a single charge. The value is very dirty, because there were different types of roads, support levels and even cyclists, but still great 120.

If you drive with the battery removed or turned off, then riding a Mantis E-1 is no different from riding a classic MTB bike on a pedal gear: it looks equally good both on smooth city lanes and in clumsy potholes, which is native to it the element.



But it is necessary to press the red button on the battery and keep the ‚ÄúM‚Äù on the steering wheel ... as it remains to choose one of five levels of support and start driving. If nothing is selected, there will be no support, just the screen will show the current telemetry.

For some reason, many people think that once an electric bike, then nothing needs to be done - he sat on it and he took you. Alas, ah, although it is nice to ride like that. But Twitter Mantis E-1 cannot ride in scooter mode, as Airwheel R8 could - this is the main disappointment of those who asked to take a ride hoping to feel a miracle :) But still managed to make friends happy, because even just a support mode causes pleasant emotions, giving a sort of boost.

The bike is equipped with a pedal sensor (PAS - Pedal Assist System), which detects the fact of pedaling and turns on the motor if at least one turn is made (and if the speed is lower than the limit for the specified level of assistant assistance). Hence the first conclusion: the bike will not allow "jerk" from the spot, if you have not done at least one turn - it can be difficult to effectively drive up the hill on a heavy asterisk. The second conclusion: the motor is either working or not - if you pedal constantly, then the motor will work constantly. But at the same time, he is not able to understand when you need help and how to render it correctly: when you twist the pedals idle or when groaning up the hill - he will help to pedal in both cases, because his only setting is the chosen level of help. A similar system is installed on most electric bicycles, and rightly so: if you like to ride, love and twist the pedals.

Simply put, the engine in the Mantis E-1 works in tandem with the cyclist, but does not take its function completely, just as the PAS does not remove such an important function from the cyclist as choosing the right gear. So we have here, rather, the good old "mechanics" on steroids, rather than "automatic". But it is quite possible to drive almost for a halyavka - if you put maximum support and rotate the pedals on a comfortable pair of stars (in the 5th mode, even the heaviest star seems quite easy). If at the speed we begin to pedal intensively, the speed will not change much, but the current consumption will decrease (as can be seen on the computer screen).

The electric motor has interesting features.

First, when it works, it makes a not very loud, but rather unusual sound - very similar to the sound of an angry cat :) Here, from the 30th second, the cat works on electric :) If you go in headphones (I remind you that it is not safe) ), the fact of the inclusion of an assistant is felt by a small boost and a decrease in the load on the legs.

Secondly, the electric motor stops working at speeds in excess of 35 km / h (and this is the second reason why Twitter Mantis E-1 does not fit the term ‚Äúbicycle‚Äù from the traffic rules). The maximum power of the battery gives at speeds from 20 to 25 km / h, while the battery current is about 10 A. With a further increase in speed, the current begins to gradually decrease - at a speed of 35 km / h, it drops to 2 A and then to zero. That is, if you drive 40 km / h and think that this is all an electric motor, then no - it is you who work, and he is already resting :) By the way, my record for Mantis E-1 was 65.5 km / h from the hill locked fork.

The third interesting feature was noticed in the review on IXBT:

‚Äú The riddle of a long battery charge reading of 100% was solved. By observing the battery voltage, we managed to find out that the voltage on the battery was 39 V for 100%. And since the battery charges to 41.7 V, until the voltage drops to 39 V, the indicator shows 100%. In subsequent races, it was possible to determine the voltage that was taken on the bike for zero charge - it is 30 V exactly. Moreover, when the voltage drops below this value, the controller reduces the power at the motor very quickly: already at 29.5 V, the current becomes zero. In other words, for the controller, the health of the battery is more important than the health of the rider :) "

By the way, they also did not manage to defuse the bike in one race, but the result was also more than the manufacturer claims - 152 km.

Today we want to offer you a new line of virtual servers VDS-KVM (Kernel-based Virtual Mashine) from ColoBridge. Why KVM


Those who read our first post may remember that we planned to launch VDS on the XEN virtualization platform in the early spring. But after visiting the CeBIT IT exhibition in the spring and the meetings with the guys from RedHat that followed, we radically changed the strategy and plunged into KVM.

The main reason for changing the course was the perspective. KVM as an open source virtualization platform, starting in 2007, has become part of the standard Linux kernel. Thanks to the many thousands community of Linux, the hypervisor has joined the leading virtualization tools. Support for hardware virtualization of the processor (Intel VT, AMD-V), fine tuning of QOS, the presence of containers of their own virtual hardware and the use of unmodified OS disk images bring the virtual KVM-based server closer to the dedicated one. The hypervisor occupies a leading position in the following indicators:
  • performance: SPECvirt performance benchmarks ,
  • Scalability: KVM supports significantly more CPU and RAM than other x86 hypervisors,
  • security: integration with SELinux .

The KVM virtual environment actively supports the Open Virtualization Alliance , which includes RedHat, IBM, Intel, HP, which most likely guarantees a high development rate for the platform.

Why virtualka and not Dedic?


Of course, no one cancels the dedicated server, and it has the right to exist. Moreover, this fall ColoBridge will launch its own Dedicated line. But we are sure that our virtual servers are much more reliable, more functional and more promising than a budget dedicated server. The main arguments for the above are:
  1. Value for money. The physical servers we use as platforms for hypervisors are equipped with two power supplies. They, in turn, are connected to a reserved power supply system (Circle A + B). Network cards of the server are simultaneously activated in two independent network rings of various Internet providers. The file system is located on SAS disks compiled in RAID 10 with hot spare. There is a console, access to the BIOS, power management. Dedicated server of a similar configuration is on the market 2-4 times more expensive.
  2. For developing projects, fast migration and project scalability are important, which is much easier and faster in a virtual environment.

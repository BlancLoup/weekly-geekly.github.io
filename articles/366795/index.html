What can you give a geek on Cosmonautics Day?



On April 12, we all remember Yuri Gagarin, the first man to fly into space. It happened in 1961, respectively, on April 12. It is a pity, of course, that so far the dream of many children and adults has not been fulfilled, and only cosmonauts and rare space tourists are still flying into space.

But what to do, we can only hope that the coming years will open up space for all of us. In the meantime, you should pay attention to the devices that in any way can be associated with flights and astronautics. We are talking about household devices, some of them allow you to feel, if not a cosmonaut, then a pilot.

Multirotor hubsan





The multicopter operator is almost an astronaut. This multicopter model is cheaper than its counterpart from Parrot, and the functionality remains at the level. At the same time, the Chinese were able to make their device easier than their counterpart from American or European manufacturers. Hubsan is a multicopter, which is equipped with a camera and LEDs that illuminate the flight of the copter. He can fly Hubsan in all directions, the usual management - with the help of a joystick.

Power UP - radio-controlled paper airplane





Many schoolchildren at one time tried to express their love of flying by creating paper airplanes. Someone turned out better, someone got worse, but they were still aircraft. The simplest aircraft.

Schoolchildren, meanwhile, have already matured, and now paper airplanes are not interesting to launch. True? Worth thinking again. What do you think about the airplane, which can be folded out of paper, run, and control its flight from a mobile phone?

Power Up is controlled by Bluetooth, and can withstand a strike on the ground of any force, so that you can not worry about its safety during the flight and inevitable accidents.

Sopar - a solar powered robot





A robot named Sopar is able to change faces. In the sense that it can be used to create 14 very different models of alien inhabitants. They all "eat" solar energy: the robot is equipped with solar panels.

Such a robot is great for both children and adults. Robot Sopar - you can say, ideal for everyone who enjoys technology, from young to old.

Levitating globe





A globe that is held in the air without demanding visible support is, one might say, a toy. But getting used to such a thing is almost impossible. Naturally, the process of "levitation" is clear to most owners of such a thing. But on the desktop, this toy will look very handy.

The globe will not take up much space, but for the eyes - rest and entertainment, plus a reason to once again think about how small our planet is, and we need to save it.

Robot Robosapien X





And one more robot whose developers really tried. This robot can perform a variety of operator tasks, including combat movements, walking, turning, grabbing things. In total, the robot has 67 programmed functions, plus the user can add and modify these functions.

Management is done from a smartphone, thanks to the presence of a wireless module. It is possible that Robosapien X is a simple prototype of future automata that will explore the surface of other planets (or our own planet).

Thanks to Robosapien X, both an adult and a child will be able to feel like a researcher, a pioneer - at least of their apartment.

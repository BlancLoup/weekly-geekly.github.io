Virtual Trading: the first step in the stock market

To many of those who are interested in trading in the stock market and have read a certain amount of literature on this subject, it seems that, in fact, it is time to test their strength in real affairs. As a result, due to lack of practical training, such merchants quickly lose their money.

To minimize the risk of such ‚Äúspeed losses‚Äù and to reset deposits in a matter of days, special simulators of stock trading were invented that allow the newcomer to plunge into the world of online trading without the risk of losing real money. Today, we will take a closer look at foreign and domestic projects in the field of virtual trading in the stock market.

What it is


According to Wikipedia , a stock market simulator (stock market simulator) is a program or application whose purpose is to reproduce or copy some or all of the sides of the real stock market so that the player can practice trading without the risk of financial loss. In such systems, virtual money is used, and stock quotes change in a similar way as it happens on a real exchange.

The benefits of such programs are obvious - beginners can practice working on the market, master the work with a specific trading terminal (brokers often provide this opportunity), more experienced investors can test different strategies on a virtual account and compare their productivity, and traders using robots usually resort to virtual trading for debugging software.

Now that we have understood what it is and why it is needed, let's take a look at some interesting foreign projects in the field of Virtual Trading.

Wall street survivor


In addition to the funny name, the project offers novice traders (of which 420,000 have already accumulated) the possibility of obtaining both theoretical and practical skills of stock trading. In addition, in fact, a simulator of stock trading, on Wall Street Survivor you can find courses of various levels of complexity that increase the financial literacy of users. And all this for free.



In addition, thanks to a partnership with Bunchball (wrote about this, in particular, TechCrunch ), which specializes in gamification, missions appeared in Wall Street Survivor, which users must complete during the training process in order to receive an award in the form of a badge.

Wall street magnate


The project of the community site of traders, using which you can hone your trading skills. One of its features is the fact that for virtual trading real-time data from the largest American stock exchanges are used. The project will be useful to a wide audience: from students to professional traders who want to test their trading strategies.



After registering (you can log in via Facebook), the trader receives $ 100,000 virtual dollars and can trade in shares traded on the New York Stock Exchanfe, NASDAQ or American Stock Exchange (non-American securities can be bought / sold through American Depositary Receipts - ADR). The special panel shows the dynamics of the trader's portfolio, dividends, history of operations, financial news (collected from Yahoo! Finance, Reuters, CNN Money and The Wall Street Journal) and other useful information.

Virtual trader


British stock trading simulator . Users perform operations and compete with each other. For trading, ¬£ 100,000 of virtual money is given. At the same time, it is possible to use the service and not in the competition mode by creating many different stock portfolios (only one portfolio is selected for the competition). A number of restrictions are imposed on the trading strategies of participants, contributing to the development of their skills - for example, you cannot invest more than 25% of the money in one paper, which forces investors to diversify their strategy.



Trakinvest


The virtual exchange , founded by former head of JP Morgan Bobby Batia, not only teaches users to trade, but also allows the best of them to get real work in the financial sector in emerging Asian markets, thanks to collaboration with organizations such as Thomson Reuters and Religare.



It all works simply - users make transactions on a virtual exchange, earn or lose, and the best of them get the opportunity to do an internship in a financial company from Singapore or Hong Kong, but most traders use the site to enhance exchange trading skills.

Stock market in GTA V


There is an opinion that car theft, shooting and other banditry in this game - just fade before the possibility of large-scale financial fraud. Adam Najberg, a journalist with The Wall Street Journal, like all editorial staff, cannot make transactions with real securities so easily, but the restrictions do not apply to virtual sites.

There are two exchanges available in the game - Liberty City National (LCN) and BAWSAQ, each with its own list of available stocks, tickers and indicators. You can trade on these sites using a browser on your smartphone. The situation on the exchanges intersects with the gaming story - the names of many companies trading on LCN and BAWSAQ are often heard on the radio in stolen cars, or their advertising could be found moving around the city. And what is even more interesting, the player can influence the stock value by his actions ‚Äúin real life‚Äù.

Smallest ThinkPad

Ultra-portable notebook ThinkPad X100e was officially presented to the press last week and is already available for sale at a price of 16 to 20 thousand rubles. This price tag makes it the most affordable ThinkPad notebook. It differs from the classic series of laptops, such as the ThinkPad X200, with an updated look and a new keyboard design. And, perhaps the most important change: for the first time a ThinkPad notebook is built on the AMD platform, not Intel.

However, the main features of ThinkPad notebooks are preserved in x100e. The keyboard is full-sized and comfortable, and with the new design the same key design is used as in the classic notebooks of the series. The case is strong, with reliable metal fastenings of the halves of the laptop. The screen is matte, and this, I think, will appeal to many. The proprietary software is also in place, starting from the utility for power management and ending with a full backup system. And, of course, the laptop has a proprietary red trackpoint, without which the ThinkPad is no longer a ThinkPad!




The ThinkPad X100e is not the only laptop with a new design, but this year it also introduced the ThinkPad Edge notebook line. These models were designed specifically for small businesses, hence the affordable price. Classic ThinkPad laptops with a conservative design, a decent price and a body of tricky metal alloys are not going anywhere. By the way, the line of light and powerful notebooks ThinkPad X201, built on the basis of Intel Core i5 and i7 processors, has just been updated.

Appearance



The new design of the ThinkPad X100e as a whole retains the usual approach: the appearance is strict, there is not a single glossy detail. However, now ThinkPad is also available in a bolder red color.



The keyboard has undergone the most noticeable changes: the buttons are separated from each other, much the same way as in modern laptops from other manufacturers. But there are differences. The keys have a slight tilt "towards the user" and are slightly concave. The rarely used buttons disappeared: the SysRq key and the context menu button in Windows.



The keys "up" and "down" are located close to each other. Buttons Page Up and Page Down are recessed into the case. This is convenient: they are always at hand, but you cannot accidentally press them.



Fn and Ctrl buttons. The new ThinkPad, including the X100e, has a BIOS setting that allows you to swap these keys - this will be convenient for those who are used to having the Ctrl key to the left of Fn.



The classic trackpoint is enhanced by a MultiTouch touchpad. At the disposal of the owner of the laptop is a double set of mouse buttons. After a month of using the laptop, I came to the conclusion that the trackpoint is more convenient than the touchpad, if the laptop is used on the go. The touchpad is recessed into the case, and the buttons are located on the very edge. On the one hand, this is not very convenient - you can accidentally press a button with your hand or elbow. On the other hand, the touchpad keys are instantly located blindly.



A notable innovation was the reduction in the number of indicators to a minimum. In the laptop, the power button is illuminated, two indicators (battery + sleep mode) are located on the front end, another LED indicates that the webcam is on.

I note a few interesting points. First, the laptop can be purchased with the corporate version of Windows 7 Professional, which is not available in less productive netbooks. Secondly, all ThinkPad X100e are prepared to install a 3G modem. The tested model was equipped with such a modem, which is also equipped with a GPS-receiver. I will tell about it in a separate material - the device is very interesting. The laptop, like other ThinkPad, is equipped with Active Protection System - a motion sensor that allows you to protect the hard drive in the event of a fall. Finally, the ThinkPad X100e comes with a Broadcom WiFi module with support for the 802.11n standard.

Not in Crysis, of course, but my beloved Far Cry (first) and Half-Life 2 work well with minimal settings and at the native screen resolution. Such a nice addition to a business notebook.

Display

The screen in X100e is matte, it almost does not reflect the light of lamps in office conditions, which allows
set the minimum brightness values. The resolution (1366x768) is high enough for a diagonal of 11.6 inches, as a result - the image is very smooth. At maximum brightness, the text can be read even in bright sunlight.

Autonomous work

ThinkPad X100e 6-cell battery provides up to five hours of operation without charging. In practice, battery life depends on how you use the laptop. In a typical test run time, when playing a standard-definition video, the X100e lasted 2 hours and 32 minutes. With the included wireless module and active networking, the laptop worked 2 hours and 38 minutes.

To achieve maximum battery saving, you can use the proprietary utility Power Manager. If you reduce the brightness of the display and turn off the wireless modules, as well as set the "minimum" mode of processor power, the battery life will increase significantly. In this mode, the laptop worked 4 hours and 33 minutes.

On sale, the laptop is available with a three-cell battery that does not protrude from the case. It makes sense to buy such a modification if you need the lightest laptop possible. The battery life is only two hours, with a heavy load - just over an hour. 6-cell battery provides at least two hours of battery life in any mode.

Orange San Francisco - Not a budget android phone for ¬£ 99

Not so long ago, I was asked to pick up a new phone, since I have some experience in communicating with the Android platform and phones on this platform are available in a wide price range, the glance was shifted to the so-called android phones.

Having studied the Russian retail market of not expensive smartphones based on Android, I began to look closely at the LG Optimus One , but by happy browsing I came across a review hero - Orange San Francisco , priced at just ¬£ 99 in England (about 4,500 rubles) and in terms of technical characteristics, it is almost twice as superior to all smartphones provided in our market, which cost up to 10,000 rubles.

It is about him that will be discussed in this review, welcome to habrakat.

After a deeper study of the available information on the smartphone, it became known that the smartphone is actually a Chinese product under the name ZTE Blade . Orange, like many other operators, simply orders brand-name devices from the Chinese company ZTE , redoing the firmware for themselves and adding its software.

Orange San Francisco is available in two colors - Black & White. Many stores call the black model Gray, although in fact they sell the black one, because of this, it can be mistakenly concluded that the smartphone has three color options.

Specifications:




As can be seen from the table of technical specifications, the device is adequately equipped, the only thing that can be criticized is the battery of the device, with a capacity of 1250mAh, there is no hope for long work, but in reality, with not very active use, the smartphone works up to two days, which in general something quite not bad. If you listen to music, surf a bit on the network and play toys, then the battery charge comes to zero by night, that is, you have to power the device from the outlet every night.



If you paid attention to the type of screen, you probably noticed that the screen of the device is of two types - AMOLED and TFT , unfortunately AMOLED was delivered only in the early installments until 2011, you can buy a device with AMOLED screen today only on ebay state, and the prices for them are higher than the cost of a new smartphone with a TFT screen.

The device has three hardware buttons, at first glance it may seem that they are touch-sensitive, say, like Acer Liquid , but in fact the buttons are still hardwired. There is no search button, which, in general, I do not consider to be a flaw, the utility of the search button seems to me to be almost zero.



The camera of the device is only 3.2 megapixels with autofocus, but this does not prevent it from making enough not bad pictures, I would put it on a par with iPhone 3Gs . Smartphone delivery cannot boast of a wide range, but there is almost everything you need - a 2GB microSD memory card (inserted directly into the phone), quick start guide in English, a microUSB to USB synchronization cable, a 1250mAh battery, a mediocre 3.5mm headset, charging with a plug UK (you need to buy an adapter or charge from USB), Orange's SIM card with a prepaid card of ¬£ 10 (useless in Russia, but have seen these sell on ebay for ¬£ 7 ), as well as a colorful advertising booklet describing the capabilities of the smartphone.

Soft part

In the standard firmware for the domestic user there are many problems and a lot of unnecessary software, firstly there is no Android Market store, instead there is an Orange App Shop , as well as Orange cards and applications for using various services provided by the operator are preinstalled. In the settings there is a Russian language, but when it is selected in Russian, only the main Android menu becomes available, USSD requests in Russian are also not processed. In short, on the native firmware it is almost impossible to use the phone, but this problem is solved by having direct hands and some free time.



I recommend immediately after purchase to flash firmware JOHN77_V5 (based on Jellyfish RLS9) , there is nothing difficult in flashing, everything is done with a thoughtful reading of the step-by-step instructions for a maximum of 30 minutes.

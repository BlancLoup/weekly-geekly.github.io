We have a crowdinvesting platform (this is a Kickstarter type, only with the opportunity to receive a share in the project's profit). In short: above - the project Hippflow, which we have placed. I would very much like to evaluate it from the point of view of utility for IT-specialists.

This is something like a tape event for a startup: a kind of twitter, which is generated both manually and automatically. It works like this: suppose you have Google on your site. Analytics, you run the project in the tracker, plus you upload the number of clients to XML from 1C daily. Hippflow ‚Äúsucks‚Äù all the data from these sources and generates short reports from them. Roughly speaking, he went out for dinner, while they were carrying the order - he immediately figured out who was doing what and how he felt in the business incubator. It turns out the log as above.

As can be seen in the log, the first message is loaded from the statistics system, the second from the client download, the third and fourth from the tracker, the fifth is written by hand, the sixth from the list of Hippflow conditions, which brings the data together into mini-reports.

According to the author of the project, such a thing will be very important for a startup in order to keep up to date with investors, mentors, their business incubators and key customers. Incubators will be the first customers (moreover, a startup has already agreed with several) - if you have 40 projects, it is difficult to understand who does what when there is no such unifying tape.

It helps a lot to understand visualization by color: messages of the same meaning have the same color gamut, which allows one look at the project tape to understand how it is doing, and one look at the general tape for a second to assess the situation in the incubator as a whole.

On the role of sigmoids in the life of an individual

But first, I want to explain why, at the beginning, we are talking about a sigmoid, and not just some kind of S-shaped curve. The chip here is the presence of asymptotes - by definition, it is a straight line, with the property that the distance from the sigmoid point to this straight line tends to zero as the point moves along the branch to infinity.

Actually, it can be clearly seen from the formula. For the function to reach the value 1, it is necessary that the exponent becomes zero. But mathematically this is impossible, for any value of "x" its value will be different from zero. And, therefore, we will inevitably divide 1 by a value, a little more than 1. A unit is unattainable.

From the point of view of the individual, this means that the ideal / perfection is unattainable. More precisely, to achieve them requires an infinite amount of investment / effort - which is physically impossible for an individual because of the finiteness of his existence.

This is an infinitely important philosophical conclusion. Including it implies the complete futility of perfectionism. The same property illustrates a purely everyday and practical conclusion, which most adults know well from personal experience.

How IT professionals work. Daria Kulaga, Lead Developer, AT Consulting Regional Business Unit

We continue to ask specialists about the mode of work and rest, professional habits, the tools they use, and much more. It will be interesting to find out what unites them, in what they contradict each other. Perhaps their answers will help to identify some general patterns, useful tips that will help many of us.

Today our guest is Daria Kulaga, the leading developer of the regional business unit of AT Consulting .

Daria graduated from the Faculty of Applied Mathematics of the Kuban State University with a degree in Applied Mathematics and Computer Science. Before AT Consulting, a year and a half was a developer in a web-studio. Currently working in the specialty for five years.



What do you do in the company?

I mainly deal with development tasks, planning release cycles, constantly communicate with the team.

One word that best describes how you work:

Incendiary.

How many hours a day do you spend on work?

8-10 hours.

How many hours do you sleep?

6-7 hours

How do you have breakfast?

Coffee and casserole or sandwich. Most often already in the office.

What are you doing on the way to / from work?

Listening to music / radio.

What kind of todo-manager do you use personally?

JIRA for scheduling work tasks.

What applications and services can't you do without?

Skype, Sberbank Online, Yandex.Probki, Yandex.Music.

What kind of task manager / issue tracker / repository do you use in the company?

JIRA and the Git repository.

What other tools and software do you use at work?

Intellij Idea, SoapUI, Java, MongoDB, Notepad ++.

Does the company have internal projects?

There are several internal projects in our company, for example, an intranet portal and a mobile intranet; my colleagues separate them by their development.

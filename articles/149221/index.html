There are such strange people who condemn attempts of extraterrestrial contact, because they are afraid to "light up" (see the film Sea Battle, for example). The humor is that we lit up long ago.

For at least 70 years the Earth has been operating as a huge beacon. Since the advent of radio and television, we have continuously emitted a radio wave signal into space.

The fact is that, in contrast to shorter wavelengths, the Universe shines very little in radio waves. The radio luminosity of the Earth is comparable to the radio luminosity of the Sun - which means that the Earth is perfectly visible on radar.

Our geography is such that one side of the ball is occupied by the Pacific Ocean, where there are no repeaters. This, in turn, means that we are not only very noticeable in the radio band, we also generate a periodic (with a period of 24 hours) signal.

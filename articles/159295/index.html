Pebble watch maker is not sure what Kickstarter will use for future projects

Pebble smart watches became the champion in Kickstarter fundraising: their creators managed to attract more than 10 million dollars. The device is synchronized with smartphones, allowing you to use their functionality on the watch itself. In fact, this is a wrist computer, where you can read SMS, manage playlists in iTunes, receive notifications from social networks and count the number of kilometers traveled during a run.

However, this project was not only the most successful in the history of Kickstarter, but also created a precedent, because of which the manifesto ‚ÄúKickstarter is not a store‚Äù appeared on the site. In it, the founders of the crowdfunding platform ask users not to treat projects as products in a store. The fact is that Pebble did not have time to fulfill its obligations to the backers in the agreed time, which provoked a storm of indignation and caused a wave of distrust towards the crowd projects in general.

Planeta.ru contacted the creator of Pebble watches Eric Migikovsky (Eric Migicovsky) and asked him a few questions. Eric answered succinctly and to the point.



- Eric, why did you decide to use crowdfunding instead of looking for an investor?
- In February 2012, there were no investors willing to invest in our project, so we decided to place it on Kickstarter.
- How much time has passed since the decision to use kickstarter to launch a campaign?
Maybe one or two months.
- What difficulties did you encounter during the campaign?
The most difficult step was the work on the Pebble prototype, as well as on the video and content for the project page.
- How much time did you spend on project management?
During the first two weeks of the campaign, we worked 12 hours a day only for a project on Kickstarter. After that, I hired people and worked exclusively on design.
- Did you expect such success? And with what you associate it?
- To be honest, we did not expect this: we originally planned to raise $ 100,000. Probably we were lucky, and the combination of foresight, strategy and luck worked!

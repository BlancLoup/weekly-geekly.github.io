School education

As was shown above, school education, which is basic, has a great influence on the entire system of education and the development of a person. For the correct solution of the tasks facing the school, it is necessary to correctly identify these tasks.

We divide the tasks of school education into several stages. In simplified form, it might look like this:

1. To teach a child basic knowledge:
‚Ä¢ To read
‚Ä¢ Write
‚Ä¢ Think
‚Ä¢ Develop logical thinking
And so on.

2. Show the child the widest possible range of existing sciences and help you choose your future specialization.

3. To give the opportunity to get the maximum possible set of knowledge necessary for continuing education in the chosen specialization, while giving basic education in other areas.

Based on the foregoing, an educational school can and should also be divided into three stages:

1. Primary school, three or four first grades. Solves the problem of learning basic knowledge.
2. High school, three or four classes. Solves the problem of acquaintance with existing sciences.
3. High school, four or five grades. Solves the problem of completing basic education and student specialization.

An additional advantage will be given by the spatial separation of schools, which will allow to dissolve pupils of different ages and eliminate their interactions. Which, in turn, will exclude the possibility of various manifestations characteristic of high school, for pupils of elementary grades.

There are some exceptions, for example, learning a foreign language is better to start in the early stages of education, so it is possible, if desired, to introduce foreign language teaching in elementary school.
It is also necessary to take into account the individual abilities of students. There are many examples where students are forced to study subjects that they will never be used in the future, to the detriment of those subjects that could help them in obtaining further education and self-realization. In addition, the need to understand subjects at a deeper level than the individual abilities of the student allow, leads to unnecessary psychological trauma and obtaining low marks for knowledge, although this knowledge is not necessary. The most acute problems are manifested in high school, when specialization has already been chosen and the necessary subjects are being studied. Consequently, in high school, an individual approach to the choice of the level of knowledge in subjects is required, up to the possibility of obtaining additional knowledge in higher education that is accessible to all.

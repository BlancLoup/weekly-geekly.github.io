Cisco SMB RV320 / 325 Secure Routers with Built-in WEB Filtering: Feature Overview

The overall state of security threats in the digital world is well researched and documented. Violations and damage to data in large public and private organizations are described quite often, and they are, as they say, at the hearing of business leaders and security experts. However, thousands of such events, unnoticed by the general public, occur daily at the level of small businesses and individual users. This article provides one of the solutions to the problem.



Symantec: attacks on small businesses are increasing


It is no secret that the level of cybercrime is constantly increasing, and small and medium business (SMB) enterprises are increasingly becoming the target of hackers. The latest data from the Symantec's 2016 Internet Security Threat Report show that the SMB sector is also becoming an attractive target for phishing.


In 2015, phishing attacks against SMB enterprises took 43% of the time from attacks of all types. This is 9% more than in 2014 and is very contrast compared to the 18% attacks on SMB, which were recorded in 2011.

A Symantec report shows that for the time being, approximately 1 out of 40 (2.5%) SMB enterprises are at risk of becoming a victim of ‚Äúcybercrime‚Äù. At first glance, this seems insignificant compared to statistics for large enterprises, where the ratio is 1: 2, that is, every second enterprise in this segment is under attack.

Another thing is important. The report notes that hackers attack their victims without any particular analysis. The wording is simple enough, and, at the same time, clarifies the meaning of the attacks.

It is not a question of who they are targeting. It is a question of all of them targeting your money. Phishing attacks are made on specific employees, mainly responsible for small business finance.

Malicious emails sent to such employees can ‚Äúhijack‚Äù financial information for the entire company and open access to corporate funds and personal information.

Symantec notes that ransomware attacks have also become more frequent. They are aimed not only at employees, but also at any devices that are in the network being cracked. (The meaning of using ransomware is to block or disrupt the device. It will be available to the user again only after some payment to the attacker). In 2015, Symantec recorded a lot of attacks on IoT, smartphones, and even smart watches and smart television.

Symantec also shows a 55% increase in the number of targeted phishing attacks from 2014 to 2015, which are aimed at specific employees, regardless of the size of the company. For reference, it should be noted that Symantec classifies small businesses as having up to 250 employees.

Be prepared to protect


What should small business owners do with the above information? Simple advice - prepare to repel attacks. It is clear that hackers will continue to increase phishing pressure on SMB businesses.

Since such attacks are mainly aimed at certain employees, it would be reasonable to implement a proper training and information program within the company using phishing schemes.

However, it is often difficult for an SMB sector company to organize such training, and employees often superficially relate to the knowledge they need to gain. In addition, they should first of all deal with their direct responsibilities.

It is also obvious that it is better to limit the number of employees' smartphones and IoT devices, however, for SMB, compliance with this policy is also associated with certain difficulties. As a result, SMB enterprises prefer protection to be carried out automatically and with the highest possible quality. One of the solutions to the problem is the use of specialized routers (routers).

Cisco RV320 and Cisco RV325 Routers


Cisco routers for small businesses (Cisco Small Business Routers, SBR) of RV320 and RV325 models with Web traffic filtering add a layer of reliable protection, preventing, among other things, access to potentially dangerous resources on the network.

For small companies with limited budgets, where there are not enough resources to maintain full-time security experts, specialized routers for small businesses are well suited, such as the Cisco SBR.

Cisco SBR is equipped with Web filtering tools that block access to hacker attacks. There are almost an infinite number of websites that employees of small companies can visit without the knowledge of management. At the same time, almost any connection can be a means of gaining access to internal systems and small business data.

Using the recently introduced specialized Cisco routers, you can set the appropriate Web policy for your company within a few minutes - and be quite sure that it runs without additional expensive security measures and additional IT staff.

Why SMB Enterprises Should Use Web Filtering? The obvious answer is primarily because their employees are usually open online, and without filtering, they can access any site.

In this regard, Cisco's RV320 and RV325 routers are among the best for SMB enterprises, allowing you to maintain good network health. They keep users away from dangerous websites with their malicious applications and questionable content, while not reducing performance.

Just a few settings in Web Filtering allow you to categorize sites, set appropriate levels of protection, indicate the reputation of certain sites, and schedule the security service (see the screenshot in the Software section).

The same survey also showed that the most attacks were associated with malware and phishing.

Using a Web filter, small businesses can significantly reduce these types of attacks and damage from them. It is important to note that Web filtering is different from antivirus software, and using one approach does not obviate the need for another.

The Cisco RV320 and RV325 models stand out among the routers of the Cisco RVxxx family as the highest level and having the highest IPsec throughput. The main difference between these models is that the RV320 has four LAN ports compared to the fourteen LAN ports on the RV325.

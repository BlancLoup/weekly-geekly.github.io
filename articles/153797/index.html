Future trends: distributed work, gamification, sports internet

Future trends that interest me. Here I dream a little, can I?

1. Distributed robotic production


Due to the Russian long distances, traffic jams and the quality of the roads themselves, distributed production becomes very relevant:
- an online service for collecting applications for manufacturing parts / prototypes is created
- managers accept payment and applications in the form of drawings or models, make the necessary clarifications on the production method, tolerances, etc., if necessary, connect modelers to create a 3D model
- they send the updated request and payment for the work to the processing centers located closest to the client's location. In the processing centers there is a modern CNC equipment (3D printers, milling machines, lathes, engravers, metal bending, polishing, polishing, manufacturing of printed circuit boards, assembly and installation, etc.), plus operators. Detail / prototype manufactured.
- after production, the part / prototype is sent to the customer in the shortest way using the delivery service
- the customer can observe the manufacturing process online (broadcast by webcam)
- the system interface is gamified (see p.2)
- the client who has sent a photo or video of the use of the created thing in his work receives a prize.

- a map of the enterprise is drawn up with an indication of departments, buildings, related services and a description of their activities, etc. in a game form (2D, isometric, 3D), by which one can walk, and hovering over the department, find out information about it (information component useful for new employees) and department news (news component). There is also easy access to company news.

To solve them, he contacts other people in the team, or works independently.

4. Sports Internet


Due to the fact that a lot of people are forced to sit at the computer, and the movement is necessary, it is proposed to link part of the lessons with field work.
- lesson options: drawing on the edge of the forest under the guidance of an experienced artist (the artist leads a lesson via the Internet), dancing on the river bank under the guidance of the best dancer (she also leads the group online), searching for minerals for geology, plants and insects for biology, assembling quadrocopter electronics , training in working with hand tools - a hacksaw, an ax, a chisel (labor lessons, wood carving), work in the garden (learning to interact with the ground), carried out in the open air, in a group and online. The instructor monitors the group, the operator for the equipment, coordinators and presenters of the webinar can be anywhere.

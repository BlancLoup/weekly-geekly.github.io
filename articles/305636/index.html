Homo Multitaskingus: multitasking person

The fact is that the brain does not solve problems in parallel and at the same time. It switches between them using its structures: the prefrontal cortex, frontal lobes and so on.

Almost a year ago, we already discussed the myths about multitasking . Today we will talk about the connection of our "multifunctionality" and attention.

Attention: what is it?

Attention is the focus and focus of consciousness on an important business for a person. Hearing the sounds of the salute outside the window, you will unwittingly distract from this article, as the brain switches to fireworks and even beckons you to see what is happening. But conscious switching is important for work.

Attention Properties

‚Ä¢ Switching. Switching attention conscious and should be associated with the formulation of a new goal. Good news - this property can be trained!

‚Ä¢ Concentration. It means how much a person is able to plunge into his own business and not be distracted by extraneous noises. The higher the concentration, the harder it is to switch.

‚Ä¢ Stability. This is the duration of concentration or concentration. This property depends on the regular appearance of new impressions. Generate interest in the material, connect the essence of the work with your needs, create a comfortable working environment.

‚Ä¢ Distribution. A vivid example is car driving training. It is necessary to perform several actions at the same time: look in the rear and side view mirrors, switch the speed, and even keep an eye on whether you are going in that direction. And if at this time the phone is ringing and the child is screaming in the back seat, then it is very difficult to distribute attention.

‚Ä¢ Volume. This is the number of separate objects that we are able to perceive clearly and clearly at the same time. On average, it is 3-5 objects.

Working in multitasking mode involves the use of the properties of attention to the fullest - we keep the necessary tasks in the field of attention, we steadily concentrate on each of them, distribute our attention in the right parts and switch between tasks.

Different activities are based on different properties of attention. For example, high concentration is important to the editor and proofreader. The teacher or workshop facilitator needs the ability to effectively distribute attention. The better developed each property, the better we focus on work. And the more effectively we execute it.

What to do?

The secret of imaginary multitasking in the right combination of tasks themselves. An important rule is that at least one action should be carried out automatically . If you are fluent in English, then it will not be so difficult to sculpt a letter in English and answer periodic questions from colleagues. And colleagues will be surprised: "How can he do several things at once?"



Practical tips:

1. Make a plan for the day. Do not contain more cases than you can master, and delegate some tasks in general to others.
2. Set priorities. To help you, the Eisenhower matrix with its important / unimportant and urgent / non-urgent matters.
3. Compose the work blocks based on the main rule of multitasking - automatic action. Switching attention from uninteresting and less important work to interesting and more significant is easier. Therefore, first ‚Äúeat the frogs‚Äù.
4. Set only one main task per day. So you will avoid chaos and vanity.
5. Use mnemonic techniques - associations, rhymes, letter codes, acronyms, consonance, storytelling, and so on. This will make it easier for the brain to switch from one case to another.
6. Practice your attention properties. Find the differences in the pictures, look for numbers in the tables for speed and so on.
7. Ignore the extra information, try to protect yourself from it. When I search for information on the Internet, there is always a temptation to enter the entertainment portal. But I hold on.

Facebook has been automatically processing photos on upload.

Not all of us are photographers, but everyone, probably, does a photo. And some would be useful help when working with light and shadow. To prevent this from further bothering, Facebook will begin to improve downloaded photos from smartphones on iOS and soon from Android. You will only need to use the slider to control the light, shadow or clarity - or choose your original version of the photo.



Facebook and other social apps are involved in the battle for photos. The winner will get user involvement. That's why Twitter updated the photo interface, Instagram added five new filters, and Google+ connected the automatic photo enhancement function a year ago.

When you upload a photo using the Facebook application on the iPhone, the photo quality improves automatically, you just have to use the scroll tone. So now it will be much easier to upload good photos to social networks - without any effort. If desired, this feature can be disabled in the application settings.

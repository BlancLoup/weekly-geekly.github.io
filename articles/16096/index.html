User Experience 2007: first pancake, but not lumpy

The User Experience Russia conference for usability professionals and all sympathizers, held at the end of last week in the chic interiors of the Radisson SAS Slavyanskaya Hotel in Moscow, showed a great interest of business and the public to this, still new to Russia, industry. Dmitry Volkov , CEO of IT-Online , the organizing company of the event, believes that everything went well. And, by and large, it is difficult to disagree with this conclusion. Interesting reports from interesting speakers, both domestic and foreign, fell on the heads of participants one by one. But the main component was, of course, communication with colleagues, opportunities for which we still have a large deficit.

As Dmitry said, the results of the conference even exceeded the initial expectations. What was initially planned as a celebration of World Usability Day on November 8th, received so many responses from the interested public that the program had to be stretched for three days. ‚ÄúAbout 550 people expressed their desire to participate in the conference, to cover it. It seems to me that this is an indicator of a trend. Some time ago, sites were created in order to like top management. Today, obviously, not only aesthetics, but also convenience are important in order that users can achieve their goals, and Internet businesses can turn users into their customers. Therefore, the topic of usability is now very relevant, and this conference will allow us to unite efforts, get together, talk about our projects and, in general, increase our awareness, literacy and professionalism, ‚Äùhe says.

In addition to communication between professionals, the important role of the exhibition is to draw public attention to usability as an integral part of any product. Now the demand for user-friendly interfaces is already growing, but there is still a huge field for speculation, when instead of a serious approach to the question, the user is offered beautiful words, demanding, however, much more money.

A leg up with the dispelling of myths should be trained by specialists, since there will soon be no one to satisfy that very demand for ergonomics and usability professionals. Today in Russia, courses that prepare specialized specialists can be counted on one hand. Inviting foreign experts to conduct seminars, workshops that are held by the only edu-itonline training center have a tangible impact on the development of a professional environment, but such a vast body of knowledge and skills that the science of usability operates on has long required transferring to the ground classical higher education .

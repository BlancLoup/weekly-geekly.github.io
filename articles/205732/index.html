Training.



In principle, the world was up to the heap of games "overtake and win". As a result, the most common were the well-known backgammon. But Senet in the tactical part is not so far behind.
There should be two players. Each of them has 5 chips, which in turn are placed on a 3x10 field. Players take turns to determine the number of points per move by throwing four bilateral sticks into the air, representing an ancient analogue of the random number generator 1d5. (points from 1 to 4 - the white side of the sticks up after landing. 5 points - the black sides of all the sticks up)
The fact that the game did not start immediately was also considered important. At first, players throw sticks until one of them throws a one. By doing this, one of the players received a certain type of chips. After that, you can walk only when 2 or 3 falls out on sticks. I, as a modern game designer, do not understand this very much (something overlooked?), But I‚Äôm a little more than sure that there is some hidden meaning behind it .
The point of the game itself is to remove your chips from the board as quickly as possible and, if possible, to deprive the enemy of such happiness. In general, the playing field for Seneta is a straight line with several active gaming cells, but for compactness it is compressed in three rows, and therefore the passage of the entire field from beginning to end is the shape of the letter ‚ÄúS‚Äù.

When I translated the texts up to this point and brought it all together, I felt a sense of deja-vu. My ‚ÄúLabyrinth‚Äù, which I am developing, is actually an extended and patched version of ancient Seneta! And this, you want - believe, you want - no, purest coincidence. We go further.
When moving chips one way or another, a situation arises when there are N enemy chips ahead. The following scenarios were prepared for this case:
  • Only one chip can stand on one field.
  • You can only jump over one opponent's chip.
  • In addition, they can be "cut down". By this is meant a simple change of position of the attacker and the attacker.
  • You can cut down only the chip, which is "alone". Two chips next to "protect" from each other's attacks.
  • It is logical to assume that the opponent's chips may be more than two. Three chips can be ‚Äúrazed‚Äù by cutting down the central one. Four - cut down any of the central two. What is the reason - it is difficult to say, this is the most accurate of the available formulations. As for more, I could not find anything intelligible.

A very important detail: if the way forward is blocked, then you need to walk backwards. If any movement options are blocked at all, the move is skipped.
Now, about the most delicious: active cells.
Dry rules are boring, so I added them with the additions from the work of the magnificent Egyptologist and historian Peter Picchione.

We pass the path of the soul.



The first cell in the upper-left corner is referred to as the ‚ÄúHouse of Thoth‚Äù . The deity in this case is represented as a bird sitting on the top of the standard. "The House of Revival" - cell number 15. Presented in the form of a frog, the Egyptian symbol of resurrection. Often sits on the bow of a ship sailing on the very same boat. In this case, a widespread epithet is used to continue the life cycle after death. The player who got into this cell received an extra turn. According to other sources, the chips that died in the last cells of the board came back to life.

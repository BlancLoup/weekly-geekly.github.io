The provider reported a failed experiment with the Ministry of Communications and Roskomnadzor to disconnect Russia from the Internet. Roskomnadzor denied the information

General Director of the provider Er-Telecom, Vice-President of the Media and Communication Union Andrei Semerikov told about the unsuccessful attempt of the Ministry of Communications and Roskomnadzor to simulate Russia's disconnection from the global Internet.

Roskomnadzor denied this information.



In the spring of 2015, the authorities conducted an experiment on modeling Russia‚Äôs disconnection from the foreign Internet, said Vice-President of the Media and Communication Union Andrei Semerikov. A representative of one of the major telecommunications operators confirmed this information to Cnews . According to Semerikov, Roskomnadzor sent instructions to large telecom operators to block traffic from specified main channels. The providers followed the instructions with the help of settings in traffic management systems (DPI), but they failed to completely close the connection - the data went abroad along unknown routes.

According to Semerikov, the reason for this situation is the small Internet providers that lack DPI systems. The activities of such providers are poorly controlled by the state. They can use satellite channels, which creates uncontrolled connections between the Russian and foreign Internet.

The Media-Communication Union considers it necessary to strengthen state control over the activities of cable TV operators and Internet providers, of which there are about eleven thousand in Russia. Small providers do not block sites banned in Russia, do not broadcast mandatory TV channels, allow foreign channels that do not have a Russian license to broadcast, and contribute to the development of piracy.

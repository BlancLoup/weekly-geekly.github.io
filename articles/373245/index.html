Google has announced the release of a new cloud service Cloud Video Intelligence API (now in the stage of closed beta testing). This is the first API on the market for video processing with object recognition.

Cloud Video Intelligence API is designed to process video content. Videos become indexed, with search by content. You can track all the appearances of the desired object, the duration of these appearances, and assess their importance. For example, automatically find all sex scenes in a multi-year archive from surveillance cameras. Searching videos is as easy as searching through text documents. You can upload videos to the service directly as needed, or by integrating with Google Cloud Storage .

In-depth training systems, neural networks and AI are no longer a toy for scientific experiments, but a real technology that is increasingly used in business. In areas such as computer vision, speech recognition, natural language processing. Neural networks help to predict when degradation will occur in the network (analysis of geotagging tweets) what product the buyer wants to buy. Weak AI detects fraudulent transactions and analyzes medical images to help diagnose.

Google is one of the leaders in scientific research on the study of neural networks, and now this scientific leadership is gradually being converted into profit as commercial companies begin to use AI systems in their operations. From the point of view of the situation on the cloud market, experts say that services like object recognition in photos and videos are an attempt by Google to catch up to two leaders: Amazon and Microsoft. That is, Google is trying to use their intellectual superiority over competitors to get them.

Cloud Video Intelligence API recognizes not only nouns (tiger, animal, dog, flower), but also some verbs (run, swim, fly) in the contents of the video. It uses Google‚Äôs machine learning algorithms, created using TensorFlow and other frameworks.

There are two demo examples on the description page that show the capabilities of the system. In addition to assigning labels, as in the first screenshot, it automatically breaks the video into a sequence of scenes - and shows the labels for each scene. That is, the system recognizes scene changes.



The metadata from the Cloud Video Intelligence API video is retrieved by an easy-to-use REST API. The demo page again provides an example of such a request and response.

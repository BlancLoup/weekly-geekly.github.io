Search Quality in Runet - 2011

The company "Ashmanov and Partners" published a final report "The quality of search in RuNet" ( PDF, 62 pages ), compiled from the results of the analytical service AnalyzeThis.ru . According to the developers, AnalyzeThis is currently the only independent test of search quality on the Runet.

Probably the most interesting part of this report is to compare the quality of Yandex search and Google. Many users are subjectively trying to decide for themselves which of these two search engines is looking for ‚Äúbetter‚Äù. The AnalyzeThis website employs 23 search quality analyzers and an integrated analyzer for the overall search quality, so this service allows you to answer this question as objectively as possible.

A detailed description of the work of the analyzers can be found in the report itself, as well as on the Ashmanov website .

Over the past year, several important developments have taken place in the search market. First of all, the independent Rambler and Aport search engines ceased to exist, these sites switched to Yandex, so that in many graphs the Rambler and Aport indicators in the second half of the year coincide with the Yandex indicators . In turn, Google is increasing its position due to the growing popularity of the Google Chrome browser, the only browser on the global Internet that increased its market share in 2011. Some experts believe that it was thanks to Chrome from January to October 2011 that Google acquired, and Yandex lost about 3.5% of Runet's search traffic.

Google and Yandex show approximately the same quality in terms of navigation queries and information queries, in the quality of hints, resistance to typos, in the level of SEO-pressing, in the amount of spam and the level of advertising load, as well as in the level of updates.

Google is ahead in the quality of quote search, the quality of search for homonyms, the search for popular words, the relevance of information in search results and the speed of indexing new pages in Runet, the level of similarity of issue for queries synonyms and the number of porn sites in search results.

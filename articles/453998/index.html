Aeroexpress: go to the depot

Let's talk about how Aeroexpress works, why there is almost no noise in it, where they put the battery in, and how everything is arranged in the train itself. And in the depot too, because Aeroexpress has its own trains, its own depot for their service and its crews of machinists. That is all his own, except for the rails and electricity.

This is a very, very interesting example of a company on the infrastructure of Russian Railways.

What it is?


Aeroexpress is a comfortable electric train to the airports of the Moscow Aviation Hub (Vnukovo, Domodedovo and Sheremetyevo) from Kiev, Paveletsky and Belorussky railway stations from Moscow. A quick way to get from the city to the airport with a minimum of stops: from Domodedovo there is a stop on the interchange circuit of the Upper Boilers, and from Sheremetyevo - now on Okruzhnaya, and all this is past traffic jams. As elsewhere in the world, this method is more expensive than classic public transport, but cheaper than a taxi. According to international definitions, transportation is considered intermodal, since seamless is the delivery of passengers from the hub to the hub (from the airport to the station).

How it all began?


In 2005-2007, the company only sold tickets. The rolling stock and everything connected with it was rented from Russian Railways. In 2008, the first own trains were purchased (which still run on the Sheremetyevo direction). Here is one of them under maintenance:



In 2011, the first in-house locomotive brigade appeared, that is, besides trains, Aeroexpress began working with operations.

In 2012, they also took the service for themselves, for which they rented an old depot near Belorussky railway station, built in the 19th century for locomotives, for long-term rent. Here is the turntable:



What kind of depot?


The fan part is a depot built in the 19th century. Behind it is already a normal, more modern depot of the 1960 sample of three stages (rooms). It was these three steps that Aeroexpress took and began to modernize.

Historically, this locomotive depot. Each step had to accommodate two locomotives on the same ditch (from several ditches), that is, the length of the buildings was 72 meters per step. Then, due to new types of traction - diesel and electric - the depot was expanded. The first stage remained unchanged. The second one was extended, the continuation was completed to the part with arches. Here are the old vaults:



The second step was 125 meters. The third stage is the newest part, 260 meters long, it was completed the longest. It is now the only depot in Moscow and the region with a workshop not straight, but curved. This curved part was completed in 1975-1976 due to the development of suburban transportation. Then the commuter trains consisted of two cars, then six out of the Riga-made electric train. Then the composition began to grow. The depot was completed on 12 ER2 wagons (now these trains are almost all written off). The Latvian plant, by the way, is still making the components that are used in ED4, the new EP2, and on the Orioles from the third number are also components of the Riga plant. Now Latvia is making the electrical part, supplying the car assemblies to the TVZ, and there they are assembled, commissioned, tested and commissioned.


Shunting work is also carried out by the company on its own locomotives.

How did the two-story Aeroexpress come about?


In 2012, it became clear that passenger traffic will grow. Three years later, a new solution was needed. Traditional - attach more cars. But the estimated 12 cars did not fit on the platforms, and modifying the platforms was not a cheap pleasure.

The second feature - the longer the train, the higher the cost of its operation on the infrastructure of railways.

World experience - two floors. For the sample they took the German Deutsche Bahn and the Dutch NS. We decided to buy double-decker trains. Nowadays, the modern six-car Aeroexpress train has more seats than the first-generation ten-car train, it is serviced 6 times less, and the cost of its operation is much less.

We bought new trains, rented them back to Russian Railways. Now owned 7 ED4MKM-AERO and 11 ESH2. Of the two-decker nine 6-car and two 4-car. Double-decker walk in Vnukovo and Domodedovo, but can not in Sheremetyevo. Physically crawl through, but can not pass through the tolerances of approaching buildings. This question is now being discussed, it is necessary to slightly raise the contact wire on one of the overpasses.

How did you choose a vendor for a double-decker train?


A tender was announced, in the framework of which it was necessary to develop quite a lot of things specifically for Aeroexpress taking into account operating experience and then produce, supply and service trains under warranty. Stadler (read "Stadler"), ≈†koda, and Alstom reached the final of the tender. There were applications from China and Korea, in particular, from Hyundai, but they did not have experience in building double-decker trains, that is, the entire development was supposed to be done almost from the beginning on this project. And the rest of the finalists had the trains already in operation, albeit not quite the same, but double-decker. As a result, Condler won in consumer quality. Purchased 11 trains. That is, a modern two-story Aeroexpress is a very highly modified Stadler KISS. Two-storied of this size from the same common ancestor walks in Azerbaijan and Georgia, and there are many smaller dimensions in Europe for a long time.

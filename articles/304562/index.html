1000 core processor
At the other end of the world, the University of California at Davis tried to set another statistical record by creating a 1000-core processor. The performance here is not so crazy, only 115 billion operations per second. But another thing is amazing: the processor can run on a single AA battery, that is, in the nominal mode it consumes only 0.7 watts.

However, the model is unlikely to go to the people, since even the creators themselves admit that beautiful figures have nothing to do with the effective use of this processor in desktop devices.

Microsoft sometimes admits mistakes
At the E3 gaming conference, Microsoft introduced its smallest Xbox One S. gaming console. At the same time, the developers officially acknowledged that they were too enthusiastic about reworking the platform, having missed both the aesthetic and the gaming component itself, literally giving leadership in the PlayStation 4 segment.

Xbox One S, which is 40% less than the original, without losing performance, should save Microsoft from half the complaints. From the other half will save the new part of the game God of War and Project Scorpio - a service for combining players with PCs and consoles.

WWDC 2016 without sensations
Apple, in turn, held the annual conference of developers WWDC 2016. The entire uninitiated world was waiting for the announcement of the new iPhone or at least the smart watches, but everything turned out according to the protocol - only software and applications.

In particular, iOS 10 for mobile devices was announced, macOS Sierra for desktop, watchOS 3 for wrist and tvOS 10 for those who like to spend time in front of the TV. Conference participants received beta versions of new products immediately, and mere mortals will begin to receive updates only in the fall. In the meantime, modest news for another camp: iMessage on Android can not wait.

Dell launches Steam Machine to the masses
It seems that in the world of gaming consoles and computers a new small revolution is coming - the Steam Machine: computers on a Linux-like operating system SteamOS, optimized exclusively for games. The first model was created in 2014, but only now the idea began to pay off.

Dell, based on its popular line of AlienWare, created the Steam Machine series as a budget version ($ 449), popular ($ 749) and hardcore ($ 899). At the previously mentioned E3 forum, a presentation was held, where representatives of the American corporation stated that the first batch of devices had been completely bought up on preorder literally within a few days.

Driverless roads by 2035
According to a study by IHS Automotive , by 2035, 21 million autopilot vehicles will roll onto public roads. The whole theory is based on the plans of the USA, Japan and a number of European countries to actively invest in this technology. At the same time, as IHS notes, by 2025 only 600 thousand models will be produced. Further growth will be associated with a decrease in the cost of cars and the incredible popularity of autopilots.

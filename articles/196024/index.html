Ely Belutin and creative thinking

Hi, Habr!
I want to tell you about the new event.

Last week a rather surprising event took place, at least for those who are interested in the themes of creativity, art, creation of creativity. For the first time, the full version of the book "The Theory of Universal Contact" by the famous Soviet and Russian artist, teacher and philosopher Eli Belyutin (1925-2012) is published.


It was his paintings that became the object of attacks by General Secretary Khrushchev at the memorable exhibition of the Soviet avant-garde in the Arena in 1962. ‚ÄúYou asshole Comrade Belyutin!‚Äù The Soviet leader shouted.

But like many outstanding people, the talent of Elia Mikhailovich covered several areas adjacent to art. One of his most significant works was the theory of universal contact, in which he proposes his own method of creating creative thinking, both in abstract art and in other, different directions.

Create creative

The theory of universal contact is based on the fact that art is the most vivid form of manifestation of human creative abilities. Only the drawing process makes it possible to make the course of human thought visible to all. Visualization of various ideas allows you to visualize their elements, to establish completely unexpected connections between them. The method of Belutin allows you to activate inactive and weakly affected areas of the brain.


Painting from the master class of MISiS students according to the method of Belutin (April 2013). Many of its members never drew.

On the basis of this theory, an entire school of the development of human creative abilities ‚ÄúNew Reality‚Äù was formed. Belutin's technique allowed his students at the subconscious level to generate creative ideas, embodied both in the created drawings, and in non-standard and effective solutions in other areas. It is known that the application of the theory of universal contact in the visual arts gave rise to more than 20 new world trends. Based on it, many well-known foreign practices are built.
It is clear that after the demolition of the Manege exhibition by bulldozers, Belutin had no chance to publish his work in Russia. On the Internet there are only separate drafts and excerpts from it. Another samizdat book exists only in print.
The publication, which is expected this week, was the result of the enormous work of the widow Belutin Nina Moleva. In it, finally, it was possible to combine the fragments scattered around the world into a single form.

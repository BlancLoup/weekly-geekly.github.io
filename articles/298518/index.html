Russian IT company suspected of implementing one of the most complex schemes of fraud on the Internet

Russian developers from Novosibirsk are suspected of creating the most complicated fraudulent scheme on the Internet.

Representatives of the Canadian computer security firm Sentrant accused the Novosibirsk company Academ Media of fraud. Allegedly, they used the service for managing and exchanging MoPub mobile advertising owned by Twitter for their frauds. Experts suggest that this scheme is one of the most complex of the existing today, reported the Financial Times.

More than 200 applications from Google Play were modified in such a way that after they were installed, background ads automatically loaded. In this case, the owners of gadgets, advertisements were not visible. The fraud code, in particular, was found in the free game for children Frozen Flame, which was downloaded over 100 thousand times.

According to experts, thanks to such a scheme, the scammers managed to receive up to $ 250,000 daily from advertisers who pay for the number of views of their ads.

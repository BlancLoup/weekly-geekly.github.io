Fujitsu Siemens Amilo Mini Ui 3520 officially announced

Since there is no blog about subnotebooks, I will be writing a blog about EEEPC, as we have already done :)
Once upon a time, ASUS released the world's first subnotebook on the Intel Atom processor. This subnotebook caused an ambiguous reaction from the audience. They talked about him well, they talked about him badly. But they talked about it! After that, ASUS announced a few new models, and then it went, the company began, one by one, to announce its own subnotebooks. And now, as already one of the manufacturer of subnotebooks lagging behind in this regard, Fujitsu Siemens has introduced its product - Fujitsu Siemens Amilo Mini Ui 3520
g>
The novelty is equipped with an 8.9-inch WSVGA display (1024x600), a 1.6-gigahertz Intel Atom N270 processor , a gigabyte of memory (DDR2 667 MHz), a hard disk with a capacity of 60 to 80 GB (SSD is not offered even as an option), a 1.3MP camera, an ExpressCard slot, a USB port 2.0, VGA output, Bluetooth and 802.11b / g. Computer dimensions are 233x29 / 36x175mm, weight 1kg. The gadget is running Windows XP Home Edition, and its cost will be $ 588.

UPD: As stated on the official website - the subnotebook runs about 4 hours. Also there is the presence of "quiet" mode.



After reading the characteristics, it becomes clear that this subnotebook will not bring anything new to the market, except for competition. And for us, end users, competition is only on hand.

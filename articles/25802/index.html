Napster launches the world's largest online music store

According to the creators, this store contains 6 million records, 50% more music than any other mp3 storage. In addition to musicians representing well-known recording companies, independent pieces will be available in the store.

It is important to note that all songs downloaded in the US will be distributed without built-in DRM digital rights protection and will be compatible with any device, including iPod and iPhone.

Napster states that their service is the first subscription directory in which absolutely all files can be downloaded in mp3 format.

The cost of the songs will be standard - 99 cents per track and $ 9.95 for most albums. Almost all sound files have a bit rate of 256 kbps and include high-quality landscape graphics.

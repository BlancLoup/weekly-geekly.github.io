Why Open Data is a culture of working with information on the example of Roszdravnadzor

Instead of introducing and introducing, I immediately suggest looking at the picture. I'll warn you right away that there is no Internet Explorer ad in it.

you can receive a message similar to it simply by trying to open the operational monitoring system for medicines (MOLS) http://mols.roszdravnadzor.ru/
This is such a state automated information system of Roszdravnadzor.

I have no doubt that many of you have encountered similar situations in a huge number of government projects, working with banks and much more.

Actually here such cases violate one of the principle of open data - accessibility. Technological restrictions in no way make them accessible and the same situation with many other Roszdravnadzor registries.
For example:
  • Registry of unwanted adverse reactions -http: //npr.roszdravnadzor.ru/
  • Selective control - http://vk.roszdravnadzor.ru/
  • Monitoring CI LS - http://mkils.roszdravnadzor.ru/
  • Foreign diplomas - http://diplom.roszdravnadzor.ru/
  • Monitoring the pricing of medical devices - http://imn.roszdravnadzor.ru/
  • Monitoring the quality of public services - http://mkpgu.roszdravnadzor.ru/
  • Adverse events of IMN - http://mimn.roszdravnadzor.ru/


It would seem that for sure the employees of Roszdravnadzor should have known about these restrictions and they could open at least some of the data in the newly created open data section of their website, here - http://roszdravnadzor.ru/opendata

However, things are a little different.

Firstly, in the list of arrays that are published by Roszdravnadzor, none of the registries above simply do not exist.

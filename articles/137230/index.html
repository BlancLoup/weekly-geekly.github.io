How to fight



As practice has shown, there are several ways to deal with designers :)

1. Payment on the fact. In fact, it discourages many, but a compromise is possible - phased payment, which already suits everyone

2. Attentive study of responses and responses to them. As a rule, on most sites there is an opportunity to comment on a review, and if any behavior that you consider unacceptable remains without a comment explaining the situation, it is better to try to find another artist.

3. Continuous evaluation of work. The most scrupulous item. If you require the designer to show intermediate results, then kill yourself on the nose - you can not comment on what has not yet been completed. If an unfinished version was sent to get acquainted with the location of the elements, then do not dare to comment on the background, the appearance of the buttons, their color and so on. Not for that sent. It is difficult to overcome yourself, but if you need complete control, you will have to. Otherwise, the designer is annoyed by the comments of what he most likely sees very well himself.

4. If the designer had to change - send to the new applicant the work of the past. And look what they say. Start throwing feces - ill-mannered idiot. He will say that "Nitsche is so" - think about whether he can do something new. It will correctly indicate deficiencies, and ideally - repeat your thoughts - the ideal candidate. At the same time, it is important to tell the designer that he has reached the point - this will give him additional information about your desires.

5. Brief. Required item. A designer can provide it himself, as can you - find a sample and fill it out. Better, of course, the first option, but the second will go. Customers, be afraid of designers who rush to work right there, after discussing payment, or who do not know what a brief is. Designers, do not work without a brief (I speak as an intermediary :)) - in which case there will be nowhere to poke your nose.

Another important question is how many options to make. The fact that the designer does not fall on the first try is 70%. Even 80%. Or 90? Usually I adhere to the following scheme - for a specified amount, 3 fundamentally different design options are made, and one of them is being finalized (no more than 20% of changes). Be sure to discuss this before starting work.

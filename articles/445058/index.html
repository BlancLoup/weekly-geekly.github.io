Tesla autopilot learned to determine the change of the strip of neighboring cars



Tesla Inc is gradually improving the Autopilot system. A recent update has added two new features ‚Äî detecting stoplights and detecting another car‚Äôs changing lanes. In the latter case, there may even be an implicit intention that the autopilot recognizes.

If another car changes a lane change, the autopilot reduces the speed of the electric vehicle. All in order to make room for a car that changes lanes and does not create emergency situations.

Previously, the autopilot could behave a little strange in a busy track. So, the driver could be stumped, deciding why the electric car slowed down - because of the car ahead, because the other car seeks to change lanes on the current lane or there is some other reason. These maneuvers were called "phantom" braking, the driver simply did not understand why the electric car slows down.

Now Tesla has taught Autopilot to help other motorists . So, if an electric car "sees" that a nearby car seeks to change the traffic lane to the one on which Telsa is moving, then the latter slows down to make room for the lane-changing car.

As evidence, you can cite a video in which everything happens exactly as described. Video posted by user Reddit with the nickname privaterbok. This person is the owner of Tesla with the latest autopilot version 2019.5.15.

The video shows a Mercedes with turn signals on. The car changed lanes to the lane to the right of Tesla. Then he pauses and starts to go to the place in front of the electric vehicle. The latter in this case demonstrates to the driver the situation on the road and begins to slow down.

As far as you can understand, Tesla taught their electric cars to react to the turn signal or the speed of the neighboring cars. Previously, the autopilot could react using the method described above, it was only possible to speculate. But now fans of electric vehicles have reliable evidence.

Unable to deal with Halo, Universal Studio took on Bioshock

The director of the Pirates of the Caribbean trilogy, Gore Verbinski, announced his plans to film Bioshock, a popular and fairly recent computer game. Last year it sold over 2 million copies.
For the right to do it, Universal's studio paid Take-Two Interactive several million dollars and is already in talks with Aviator script writer John Logan to write the script for the picture. The exact amount of the deal with igrodelami not disclosed, it says only that it is one of the largest deals in the history of video game adaptations in recent years - at the level of when Universal and Fox paid Microsoft 5 million for the possibility of Halo adaptation, but this film has not yet been shot.
The action of the game and the future tape develops in the underwater city, which was planned to be equipped on the principles of utopian capitalism in the spirit of Ayn Rand, but everything did not turn out as it should, up to disastrous consequences. The player controls the pilot, who makes an emergency landing at the secret entrance to the city and eventually gets involved in the struggle for power, but discovers that in his actions he is not as free as he thinks.

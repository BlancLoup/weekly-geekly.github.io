The court of St. Petersburg upheld the decision to close the public MDK



The St. Petersburg City Court upheld the decision to close the MDK public on VKontakte.

The judge rejected the complaint of the owner of the MDK trademark, Sarafanka LLC, which owns 30% of the community, and the main owner of the Hermes LLC public corporation against the decision of the Smolninsky District Court of October 27, 2015. Then the district court decided to limit access to information in the MDK community throughout Russia, reports Fontanka.ru .

Many users associate a lawsuit with the publication of offensive pictures in the public after the death of the popular singer Jeanne Friske. Then, in the summer of 2015, this caused a public outcry and the creation of a petition on change.org to shut down the public as immoral.

However, the MDK public came to the attention of law enforcement agencies earlier, in the spring of 2015, after the publication of pictures insulting believers' feelings on Easter (images of Jesus Christ with offensive posts and signs due to which one of the citizens appealed to the prosecutor's office).

First of all, we decided on the need to draw up a meeting schedule. We started with timing - ending at 5 am, mandatory breaks. Vanya remembered the Pomodoro method - everyone liked the idea, and we immediately applied it.

The essence of the technique in strict implementation of the points of the algorithm:

1. We define the task on which we will work;

2. Set the timer for 25 minutes;

3.Work without distractions;

4. After 25 minutes we take a 5-minute break, even if the task is not done;

5.If the task is completed, we get an imaginary tomato, put it in the basket;

6.If 4 tomatoes are in the basket, empty the basket and rest for another 30 minutes;

7. Return to step 1 or 2.

I will not tell all the subtleties, there is enough information in the network. I will only say beforehand that we sat there all night and felt tired only once, having forgotten to set the timer and sat over the task for almost an hour and a half. Each defended his point of view, which is logical and brought up since childhood. After two ineffectual iterations of Pomodoro, we used the Six Hats of Thinking method.

The basis of the method of Edward de Bono is the concept of parallel thinking. As a rule, a decision is born in a clash of opinions, in debate and controversy. With this approach, preference is often given not at all to the best of options, but to the one that was most successfully promoted in the controversy. Six hats of thinking, in the process of solving practical problems, help to cope with three main difficulties:
1. Emotions. Instead of thinking about a decision, we often limit ourselves to an emotional reaction that predetermines our further actions;
2. Confusion. Not knowing what to do or where to start, we feel insecure;

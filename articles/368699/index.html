Arduino + Curie = Genuino 101

The Arduino team together with the Intel company let out the first prototyping board on the basis of the Intel Curie module - Genuino 101 (Arduino 101 for the American market). The novelty is equipped with an Intel Quark processor, about which we have already written , 384 kB of flash memory, 80 kB of SRAM (24 kB available for sketches), an integrated DSP sensor hub, a Bluetooth LE radio module and a 6-axis combo sensor with a gyroscope and an accelerometer.
Genuino 101 is easy to use and is well suited for exploring the basics of digital design, and can also be used to create prototypes of technology products with advanced network and computing functions thanks to the capabilities of the Intel Curie module.
A new breadboard model will go on sale in the first quarter of 2016 at a suggested retail price of $ 30 (see how much it will be in rubles).

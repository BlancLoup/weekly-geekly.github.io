It is enough for us to imagine the graph as a certain set of vertices (nodes, nodes) that are in connections defined among themselves or, in other words, connected by edges (links, edges). Let us graphically solve the puzzle in Fig. 5: in the line record, the cubes, its components, look like YG / RB / RY, RY / GY / BY, GB / RG / YY, GR / YY / YB . Actually, it is on this intricacies and we have to think to solve the puzzle:

  • inside this combined multigraph, it is necessary to distinguish two separate subgraphs (two paths), each of which only once passes through all four color vertices, using each of the 4 edges numbered from 1 to 4-X. One subgraph will represent a row of cubes in the ‚Äúpuzzle solving‚Äù position, facing the observer, the second - a row of cubes in the ‚Äútop‚Äù position.

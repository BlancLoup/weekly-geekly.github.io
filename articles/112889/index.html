Advertisers have mastered wireless transmission of electricity

We have to admit that not always new technologies make our life simpler and more pleasant. Sometimes it happens the other way around. A good example is the packaging with electroluminescent paints , which eCoupled presented at CES. The paints on the packs glow under the influence of an electric field from the common contour built into the shelf. It is terrible to imagine that such packs will appear in every supermarket and will blink from all sides.



That's not all. The manufacturer hopes that such electromagnetic circuits will be embedded in the kitchen tables. Then some types of food can be cooked right in the package. Put the packaging on the table - and it starts to heat up on its own.



The electric stove as a separate device becomes unnecessary if the dishes can heat up, getting electricity from the transmitter built into the table. Soup can be cooked directly on the table.







A food processor or blender can also work wirelessly.

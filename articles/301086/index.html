Google turns its data centers into works of art.

As a rule, most data centers are nondescript industrial buildings resembling storage facilities. They are not striking, monophonic and do not attract attention. The owners of the data center prefer to adhere to the rigor of the exterior of buildings containing server systems of corporations that are important for business or even for the economy of the whole country. But Google data center operators have decided to take a different look at building designs. They launched the ‚ÄúData Center Mural Project‚Äù art project, designed to transform the gray walls of data centers into real works of street art.



Google is going to turn its server farms into huge art objects, decorated with artistic paintings. Photos and videos of the conversion process itself have been published on the Internet.



The company's specialists set the goal of making the data center walls more vivid, giving them individuality and special significance for society. In addition, colorful and fun drawings will delight passing by people creating a more positive atmosphere.



To implement their plans, Google hired artists to paint the walls of four data centers. Two of them are located in the USA: Mace County (Oklahoma) County Data Center and Council Bluffs (Iowa) Data Center. The other two are located in the Belgian city of St. Ghislain and in the Irish capital Dublin.



At the moment, data centers are already ready - in Oklahoma and in Belgium. But in the Irish data center and the data center of Iowa, wall paintings are not yet finished. Artists are trying to display and visualize the features of specific data centers, in accordance with their functions and location, the color of the city.

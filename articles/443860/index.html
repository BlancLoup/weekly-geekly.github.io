Programmer as a service for small companies. Soar or not?

Story about a friend


One of my friends, finishing university 10 years ago, got a job in a pharmacy chain for a programmer position.

The company then used 1C accounting and a few more samopisny programs. And all other management and accounting processes in back office were done in Excel or on paper. A friend was assigned the task of automating some accounting operations. For example, before his arrival, every day one person called 130 pharmacies and asked for a couple of numbers.

Without thinking twice, comrade realized that this is a great situation to learn how to do projects and become a professional web programmer. He convinced management to create a corporate web portal for managing the company. Showed that existing solutions are poorly suited to the specifics of the company's processes. And the automation of the process went at full speed!

Tasks were different: project management, business processes, management accounting, reporting. In general, what was on paper and in Excel was transferred to the web. For two years he quietly studied web technologies and automating accounting processes. Not bad pumped in web development and went to a development company with a salary increase. The company was left with a student level system and a bunch of noodle code. They had to hire a new developer who decided to rewrite everything from scratch.

The problem of business automation with small resources


I heard such stories several times. Often the company has no money to create a serious development team, while the processes are poorly formalized. Facing the need for automation, the company chooses from the following options:

  1. Hire a developer.
  2. Order a solution from the studio or hire an integrator.
  3. Take advantage of ready-made service.
  4. Leave everything as it is and suffer.

Our team is trying to bring to the market another alternative to students - the Programmer as a service.

The essence


We create an online account application designer. The most frequent case for customers who come to us: the problem is to create a TK and imagine what a working system might look like.

Our solution: let the customer communicate with our systems analyst, who

  • ask the right questions
  • will help write the first version of the TK,
  • will assemble on our designer the first working version of the accounting system.

And all this for 1 month and 20 thousand rubles (this amount eliminates those who, in principle, are not ready to pay and who do not really hurt).

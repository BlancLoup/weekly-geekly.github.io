List of free transactional, hosting and marketing email-providers

If you are a blogger or manage a small or medium business, then most likely you do not mind to save. You can save several hundred dollars a year just by email.

There are three types of postal services that can be useful to a blogger or site owner:

  1. Transactional. You need to send emails about events, transactions, order notifications, registration data, etc .;

  2. Marketing. Needed when carrying out newsletters, holding promotions, blog updates, etc .;

  3. Mail Hosting It is needed to receive and send e-mails (required if you use virtual servers , for example, DigitalOcean , Linode , etc.).

Free Transaction Mailing


Reliability of sending transactional letters is important, as you have to be sure that site visitors and your clients receive emails sent to them without delay.

The subject of such letters may be the following:

  • Digital resources. If you offer PDF files, e-books, documents, or any other kinds of files, then it‚Äôs best to send them via Thrive Leads .
  • Activity on the site. If you want to send users notification of a password change, registration on the site, the appearance of comments.
  • Order. To report on the implementation of the order, pay the bill for thank-you letters.

Since you are now aware of why this may be useful to you, let's consider the offers of free transactional mailings.

1. Mailgun
Each month, you can send 10,000 letters for FREE without providing credit card information. Mailgun can be used with any application or framework at your discretion: PHP, Java, .Net, Python, Node.js, Ruby, etc.

Before you start sending letters, you must confirm ownership of the domain. In my case, the entire setup took less than 15 minutes.

Mailgun has the following basic functions that you may need and a good reporting system.

  • confirmation of email addresses;
  • mass mailing;
  • personalization depending on the recipient;
  • delivery of letters on schedule;
  • SMTP or API;
  • handling complaints about spam;
  • processing undelivered letters;
  • detailed reports and analytics.



2. SendGrid
SendGrid is one of the most popular email platforms used by major companies such as UBER, Spotify, Yelp, etc. With free maintenance, you can send 12,000 emails per month.

You can integrate and send mail via SMTP or API. SendGrid supports all major software platforms: Curl, Node.js, Ruby, Python, Go, PHP, Java and C #.



3. Mailjet
Mailjet is a solution that combines transactional and marketing newsletters and delivers 6000 emails per month. As expected, Mailjet can be integrated using SMTP or API. In addition, Mailjet implements a number of tools for tracking and analyzing the effectiveness of mailing, including tools for tracking delivery status, to account for indicators of letter opening and the number of transitions.

4. Elastic Email
Sending the first 25,000 emails in a month for free. Yes, completely free. You can use HTTP or SMTP to send transactional emails.

Elasticemail plugin can be used with your favorite CMS at your discretion (Joomla, WordPress, Drupal).

5. Sendin Blue
Each month, you can send 9000 emails for free, but note that with free service, your Send mail will display the Sendinblue logo. With SendinBlue, you can study statistics in real time, including delivery rates, returns, number of open letters, number of conversions. SendinBlue can be integrated into WordPress, PrestaShop, Magento and Drupal.



I hope that any of the above will satisfy your need for transactional mailing.

Free marketing newsletters


Are you going to send your newsletter to your audience? Email is one of the best ways to bring traffic to your site, and if you are not collecting email addresses, then you should think about it.

If you want to experiment with marketing newsletter platforms or you have few subscribers, then the following free services can be useful for you.

1. MailChimp
This is one of the most popular platforms for sending spectacular emails. You can send 12,000 emails to 2,000 subscribers per month for free. MailChimp combines all the tools you need: letter design, performance tracking and integration with online stores like Shopify, Woo commerce, Magento, etc.



Drag-n-drop is used for editing letters, so you will not need to touch the code.

2. Mailerlite
On the Mailerlite platform , you can send an unlimited number of letters to 1000 subscribers for free every month. You can change the design of the letter in minutes using the drag-n-drop editor, or choose from existing templates. With free maintenance, all tools are available, including tracking reports, a click map, an answering machine, RSS feeds, A / B testing, etc.



3. Campayn
Campayn platform offers to send up to 20,000 e-mails to 500 recipients for free. In the free version, only the standard set of reports is available, but for starters, this will be enough. You can import mailing lists and create responsive emails.

4. TinyLetter
TinyLetter is a personal newsletter platform for those who don‚Äôt need the full range of business tools offered by, for example, MailChimp. This is a completely free service based on MailChimp, and it is suitable if you need a simple email newsletter system.

5. Sendin Blue
Sendin Blue is a good alternative to MailChimp. Sendin Blue allows you to store an unlimited number of contacts and send 9000 emails per month for free. There are also tools for creating letters adapted for mobile devices, as well as for receiving reports about opening, returning letters, etc. in real time.

6. Soundest
Send 15,000 emails for free every month using Soundest . Soundest has it all to start a newsletter with ease: an easy-to-use editor, adaptive emails, scratch cards, email personalization tools. Soundest can be used with Privy, Justuno, OptiMonk, Nosto, Shopify, Magento, Prestashop, etc.

7. Mailjet
Mailjet is a good alternative to SendinBlue, which allows you to send 6000 emails per month for free. With Mailjet, you get everything in one: creating a beautiful design of emails, sending out your target audience and optimizing with reports and analytics.



8. FreshMail
Up to 2000 emails can be sent to FreshMail free of charge to 500 subscribers every month. FreshMail allows you to create different types of mailings depending on your needs. Here are some of the features available:

  • answering machines;
  • A / B testing;
  • text messages;
  • one-time mailing;
  • URL based;
  • scheduled delivery;
  • ready-made templates;
  • analytics.

Free email hosting


You may not need such services if you use a virtual hosting like SiteGround , since e-mail is already included in the service. But in case you use VPS , for example Linode , DO , then you will definitely need mail hosting.

1. Zoho Mail
With Zoho email hosting, you can send and receive emails through your @ yourdomain.com addresses. Each user receives 5 GB of free storage for mail and documents. You can place one domain. Fortunately, no ads.

You can access the mailbox from a mobile device (iOS & Android) and use the following tools:

  • multi-level folder structure
  • drag-n-drop interface;
  • setting rules and filters;
  • display of conversations;
  • antispam and antivirus;
  • CRM integration.



2. Pawlmail
Place a domain for mail with Pawnmail and get 2 GB of memory for free. Your mail will be available online, from desktop and mobile clients.

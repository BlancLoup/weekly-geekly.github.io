Auto Acceleration - a project for car enthusiasts

There is one good principle for a person who wants to take something such, and to do so in such a way that the general public is interested in his creation: do only what you will use yourself. I had a desire to implement some project for Android ten minutes after finding a smartphone with this OS. But I have always respected this principle, so ideas were rigorously selected and eliminated as unnecessary.

In addition to the smartphone, I still have a car, and it appeared much earlier. You can safely write me to car enthusiasts. Several times I had a desire to measure the real time of acceleration to 100 km / h of my car. It was not strong enough to buy additional devices, to figure it out - and I just took a stopwatch, looked at the speedometer and got some figure. True, the speedometer was lying, from different angles it looked different, the arrow crawled not so fast to clearly fix the moment of reaching the desired mark. Having gotten the phone, I started looking for an application on the market for such a measurement. After all, for iOS there is a very famous program of such a plan! But this is a story about our program, so let it remain a nameless competitor. Of course, I found the applications, but I didn‚Äôt like them. And it was decided: I will do it myself, for myself. Then I will briefly talk about what was invented, what came out of it, and what are the future plans ...


First, a little about technology. All the programs that I looked at on the market worked at that time exclusively with an accelerometer. There was some use of GPS, but not at all popular. I immediately had the idea to build a graph of acceleration, with GPS delays it would be impossible. It was decided to use an accelerometer.
A lot of races were held, connected friends of engineers, physicists, mathematicians - an error of 5-10% was almost always present. And with particularly evil bumps - the measurement was far from reality. This did not suit me, and the idea came number one: to combine the untimely incoming, but fairly accurate data from the GPS receiver, with instantly received data from the accelerometer, containing an accumulating error. On long winter evenings, we sat looking at the graphics, thinking up how to combine the not quite compatible. As a result, a concept was invented, which was carried out empirically. There is no higher mathematics or quantum physics - everything is quite simple and even boring, so I will not describe the details here. But I began to like the result obtained in this way, and besides, there was an opportunity to sift out obviously incorrect experiments with these very pits, slides, elevation differences, and the like. At that time, no competing application claimed a similar method. And now there is a feeling that many use the accelerometer to capture the starting point, and GPS to determine the speed.

Update2:
Suspicions began to appear as early as December of last year, when Seagate 7200.11 series drives with the same symptoms began to come to service centers more and more often - when you turn on a computer, the hard drive is not detected at all or it is determined, but with a volume of 0 GB. At the same time, both the electronics and the disk mechanics are perfectly intact. As it turned out, the problem lies in the so-called translator table, which is responsible for recalculating the actual physical addressing of a disk into a logical one - this table may collapse due to an error in the firmware of the disks, which will lead to the symptoms described above. At the same time, all user data on the disk remains safe and sound, but is no longer available for the computer.

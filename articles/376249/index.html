Or maybe something is wrong at the conservatory?


Maybe I do not understand anything in business, but in my opinion the excessive greed of gentlemen using DRM technology harms their own business.
Complicating the process of downloading books, tying the use of the purchased product to the region, they reduce their profits. Sellers are getting huge profits from sales are not seeking to improve service. Book publishers do not have an incentive to expand the capabilities of electronic books that could be a couple of orders of magnitude more convenient for a user than printed publications.
Buyers who have paid HUGE money for the book are not only very limited in the use of gadgets on which they can read it, but also have to use to read programs with a very poor interface and functionality at the level of programs of the long-forgotten Windows 3.1
Instead of worrying about user friendliness and reducing the cost of the product, excess profits from sales are spent on endless lawsuits against copyright infringers. Gentlemen do not want to face the truth - they themselves stimulate piracy by putting numerous shots to their customers.
Monopoly record companies have become proverbs in tongues, but even they are children compared to eagles as booksellers. And this is despite the fact that the cost of production and distribution of electronic copies can be thousands, if not tens of thousands of times lower than when printing a paper version.

Here are a few measures that I would apply in the place of fat, lazy book-selling cats to increase the volume of legal sales and ultimately increase their own profits.


  • Radically would reduce the price of e-books without a multimedia context - ten times compared to the printed volume.
  • I would introduce voluntary certification of books by class, depending on the facilities provided to the user. At the same time, the book should be marked with icons indicating what kind of functionality it has and the buyer will decide which version to buy - pure text, text with illustrations, or he is willing to pay extra for a multimedia and interactive component. For business, technical, children's literature, this can greatly enhance the convenience of working with a book.   • Part of the superprofits would be allocated for holding open contests to develop convenient and functional official reader programs for various operating systems.


Here are some additional features:

Bump after bump in the development of mobile applications

A post about what I learned in the process of working on the application. Looking back, I ask a strange question: in the end who created whom? It seems that I did the application, but it also changed a lot in me.


My application is a solution to my personal problem. Working on it would be impossible to stand aside. The product grew and changed, it became better - and with it, and I.

So,
Part 1 - discoveries in the field of time and project management.
Part 2 - useful services and resources useful in the development and release process.



Part 1. Notes on project management and time


I write about what I did, what I noticed and what worked. No claim to objective truth or full consideration of the issue.

The development was carried out without budgets, sponsors, mentors, investors, star performers or some other pleasant accelerators. He did his best, and therefore he valued every day and every ruble.

He started a "logbook", where he wrote down all the tasks, deadlines and goals. Distributed the roles and built the sequence of tasks. It turned out, of course, not perfect, but a comparison of the plan-fact and the recording of logs quickly allowed us to identify the weak spot and make adjustments to the development process.

Leafing through the "logbook", I hasten to share a list of my notes:

  • Accurately determine for yourself: what do you want to get in the end. Insanely important. First of all. All follow-up is through the prism of the question: ‚Äúshould it be done to achieve a result?‚Äù.
  • The second point follows from the first: set goals for yourself and the performers as clearly as possible. None: "draw a cool design by Monday" ... The answer to your question next Friday will be: "Yes, yes, the design ... I did not understand, but what exactly did you want?".
  • To create a product, you need to work. Yes, that's so trite. You need to constantly think, analyze and work. Do not fantasize about the product, but to take any useful actions and put a lot of effort!
  • No need to learn "for the future." I read, listen and watch only what I need now or as part of the project. Here, many will begin to argue, but I immediately fend: when you rely on your own budget, every minute is precious.
  • Think, think and ask yourself how much the user will cost and how much he will bring to you. Sometimes, this simple question and calculations give a stunning result.


Such is the list from the "Captain Obvious". What is surprising is that for productive work you really do not need complex systems and secret theories. You just need to understand what you want to come to, what to do, whether or not what you are going to do will lead to the achievement of the goal. You need to work, think and just live your project, not torn by "everything".

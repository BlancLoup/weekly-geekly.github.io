Google: Adapting games for Android TV


Natalya Efimtseva, regional program manager for interaction with developers (Google), spoke about the features of porting games on Android TV.

Recently, the popularity of Android TV has increased significantly. This is facilitated by several reasons:

  1. The popularity of the Android platform itself is growing.
  2. Companies such as Philips, Sharp and Sony have begun mass production of TVs based on Android TV. Thus, users do not need to buy additional consoles.
  3. More and more applications and games are being created for this platform.
  4. The functionality of the platform is constantly expanding. For example, with Android TV you can use Chromecast, use a wide range of devices for remote control (remote controls, gamepads, iOS- and Android-devices).
  5. Ability to play your favorite games with friends both locally and online. In fact, Android TV turns into a gaming console with great potential.

Since Android TV is based on Android, it would seem that any applications or games created for this OS should be supported. However, porting applications still have to, although it will require a minimum of effort.

As an example of a game adapted for Android TV, Natalya called Game Insight Running Shadow . This is one of the first games that appeared on the console.



The game is written in Unity. When it was ported to Android TV, the changes did not affect the graphics and mechanic, the project did not require significant rework. The game dynamics has changed, the project monetization scenario has been reworked. For obvious reasons, the interface has changed: after all, the phones are controlled by touching the screen, and on TV these functions are assumed by the gamepad and the remote control. The developers spent about two weeks on porting the game. On average, Natalia noted, this process takes from one to four weeks.

Following Running Shadow, a number of other famous games that are already available for download on Google Play have been ported:

1. Nitro Nation from Creative Mobile .





2. Beach Buggy Racing from the company Vector Unit , which can be played over the network.







3. Minion Rush from Gameloft .









Separately, I would like to dwell on such a fairly well-known game like MONOPOLY Dash for Chromecast from the owner of Hasbro Inc. This game is a virtual game table for Android TV, and allows players to connect from iOS- and Android-devices to play together over the network. Thus, each connected player sees his deck of cards on his device, and the general playing field - the board - is displayed on the Android TV screen. The game requires a Google Chromecast device.

Smart home ... Technology?


The fact that the house can be smart, the mass society of the middle - second half of the 20th century also knew very well, as the black and white TV development engineers knew, that they would later release color models. Meanwhile, without publicly available technologies and open standards, there can be no talk of any mass and inexpensive ‚Äúsmart home‚Äù.

For comparison, the smart home of American billionaire philanthropist Bill Gates, designed and built in the late 1980s, cost the fabulous 63 million dollars to a famous entrepreneur.

In 1975, the mess with the data transmission standards for home systems began to fade. The public was presented the X10 protocol - the first open industrial data transfer standard in home automation systems. It was a communication protocol for electronic devices that ran at 60 Hz and 110 V in an ordinary home electrical network. This somewhat limited the market of countries where compatible devices could be used. In the Soviet Union, they certainly would not have earned.



For the future owner of the X10 home automation system, it was enough to purchase the necessary modules, insert them into the sockets and connect household appliances to the home network in order to manage them freely. Of course, at that time, the implementation of working remote control modules via modem or high-speed control centers with programmable scripts could not happen.

Only in 1978, the first X10 modules appeared on the market: a 16-channel command console, a module for controlling a lamp, a module for controlling an electric device. Subsequently, the switch modules and the timer module appeared. In theory, it is 1978 that is considered by many historians to be the starting point in the history of home automation and smart homes. At the same time, the purpose of the first home automation systems was precisely automation, and not the creation of scenarios for the management of engineering communications and the utilities of a residential house.

Smart home system

Vivaldi Beta 3 browser release


Hello!

So, today we are ready to present the third beta version of the Vivaldi browser - the next (and, we hope, the last) stage before the release of the first stable version. Since the release of the previous beta, a lot of good has been done, both in terms of correcting errors and shortcomings, and in terms of expanding the capabilities of existing functions. Naturally, not without new features. We will now tell about all this in order.


For the corrected errors, one thing can be said - there have been many such corrections since the second beta, which was released in December last year. Therefore, this article does not make sense to publish the entire list, and those interested in details can read this list on a special page . In the meantime, we will talk about what functions were being worked on to expand their capabilities and what was new.

Since the release of the second beta, the following new (or improved) features have become most noticeable:

Browser control

This is another important area we are constantly working on. This includes, for example, the long-requested tab-closing of users by double-clicking, but even more popular were requests for working with the tabs themselves. In particular, in what order they should open and close, and also where they should be placed on the tab bar. For obvious reasons, to fulfill the wishes of all users with a couple of options will not work here - we have all worked out a very different style of work over the past years. Therefore, we decided to go, perhaps in a more difficult way, but at the same time more correct - by letting you choose which principle of working with tabs you will use.


As you can see, the settings section related to tabs has grown quite well, but at the moment we are interested in two options - the position of the new tab and the browser actions when closing the tab.

So, a new tab can now open in three different positions: to the right of all tabs, to the right of the active tab, or to the right of the associated tab. A linked tab is a tab opened as a link from the active tab. Thus, if you open a certain link in the background tab, for example, from Wikipedia, the next link from the same active page will open to the right of the opened one before that. And so on. Get a kind of group of tabs, interconnected and located compactly.

You can also use a different behavior when closing the tab you are viewing. By default, you will be moved to the previous linked tab, if there is one (that is, to the left of the one to be closed). If there are no related tabs, or if you have enabled the Go to the next option in the order of switching , then when you close the tab, you will be moved to the tab according to the browser settings in the Tab Features section.

In general, it is quite difficult to explain, you can just experiment with different combinations of settings and see which behavior you find most comfortable.

Where to put something?

Yes, not an idle question. This is a beta version, this branch is separated from the one where we release weekly test builds. Therefore, you need to update the previous beta number 2, if you have it, of course. However, if it is, then auto-update should work without additional gestures. As for the installation of this third beta on top of the test build, this is, as they say, at your own peril and risk. And is it necessary? We recommend to keep the beta version on the computer as the main browser, and test versions should be used strictly for its intended purpose - for testing and catching bugs. If you need to transfer your personal data from one browser version to another, we published the corresponding instructions relatively recently .

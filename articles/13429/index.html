Modern parents pick up children's names by free domains.

Before choosing a name for your child, a caring parent will check in advance on the Internet whether the corresponding domain is free so that the child will have no problems in the future with registering a virtual place of residence. And it is better to register the domain immediately, as soon as the good news comes from the hospital.

Similarly, many modern parents do these days, writes AP . For example, a Pankov couple from New York pre-checked the free domain BennettPankow.com before giving their son the name Bennett.

Parents are afraid that by the time a child grows up, cybersquatters will take over his domain name - for example, this misfortune happened to her 11-month-old son Britney Spears. Therefore, domains need to be reserved in advance.

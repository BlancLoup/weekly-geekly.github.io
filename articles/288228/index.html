The paid subscription model for online publications shows itself positively.



I remember a year and a half or two ago, when Rupert Murdoch proclaimed the beginning of the era of paid subscription to the free content so far, the Internet community gave an extremely negative response to such an initiative. Most of the users (especially domestic), mockingly reacted to this method of making money, believing that the Murdoch empire will collapse as soon as the transition to paid content. However, this did not happen - the same The Wall Street Journal now has about 400 thousand subscribers, being one of the most successful online publications with a paid subscription. In May of this year, such publications as The Times and Sunday Times began to work on a paid basis. Once again, the Internet community predicted a quick death by "greedy media." But now, several months have passed, and these publications have presented statistics on subscribers.

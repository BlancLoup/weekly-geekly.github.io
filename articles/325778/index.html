Life programmers

A programmer is a person who creates programs.
The programmer builds a chain of events using available resources.
Programs are computer, musical, behavioral, educational, etc.

When I received a higher technical education, we had practical training in the first and second year. And the whole semester we made a hammer. Yes, the usual hammer. The method of its manufacture was unusual. More precisely, the method is normal, but not for the person who came to get a higher education. We were forced to make it a file, an 8-sided hammer with a rusty file. Who wrote this tutorial and where? What did the person who wrote this code want to program in us with this work? Apparently hard work.

In the fourth year, I waited for a new subject with the impressive name of CALS technology . Continuous information support for the supply and life cycle of products - this name was a balm on the brain, after files and propaganda of the Soviet legacy in the field of machine tools. And you know what happened? I did not understand anything of what the young teacher read out to us from printouts. He did not teach us, but read to us. It was terribly boring. All students missed. All of you know how a modern elevator works in a house. The motion algorithm is simple and straightforward. Have you thought about what is missing in the elevator control program? What algorithm did programmers forget to add to the code? Take a ride on the elevator at rush hour.

Forgotten algorithm
Each elevator has a weight / overload sensor. Coming down from the upper floors, the crowded elevator stops to pick up people on the way down. It takes a lot of time for a useless stop. It is enough to add an algorithm to the code that will give the command to the elevator to descend without stopping at a certain load. Add just three lines of code to the firmware of modern elevators. Increase usability, as bus drivers do that stop when their car looks like a can of sprats.

Microsoft will customize Windows 10 for the Chinese government



After signing in September of a contract with China, which served as a kind of response of the Middle Kingdom to the sanctions of Google, Microsoft continues to strengthen its position in the country by developing Windows 10 directly for the Chinese government, reports theverge . Today, the company announced the creation of a new joint venture for licensing, issuing, managing and optimizing a special version of the operating system for government agencies. The joint venture that appeared in Beijing, tentatively called C & M Technologies, is still in the legalization stage, but Microsoft representatives insist that, in addition to government agencies, the project will serve "state-owned enterprises from key infrastructure areas such as energy, telecommunications and transport."

A joint venture between Microsoft and the China Electronics Technology Group (CTEC) was established in September. Moreover, the established partnerships were supposed to help strengthen the position of Microsoft in China. Over the past few years, the American company has steadily increased its potential in the profitable market of the Middle Kingdom, concluding contracts with such major Chinese brands as Tencent, Lenovo and Xiaomi. However, the problem of selling pirated copies of Windows is still not resolved. A blow to the company's image is also inflicted on Chinese consumers, who, after the NSA spying scandal, perceived foreign software as a tool for tracking Internet users.

The legalization of the joint venture marks the beginning of a new era in relations between the Chinese government and Microsoft. In 2014, the Chinese government initiated an antitrust company against Microsoft, and then completely banned the use of Windows 8 in state institutions. Despite the fact that the Chinese government had previously spent hundreds of millions of dollars to purchase copies of the Microsoft operating system, government officials were still unhappy with the high prices and poor quality of product technical support, especially after Microsoft abandoned Windows XP, which was actively used Chinese consumers. The government even attempted to abandon Windows, creating a local equivalent of Windows XP - NeoKylin.

Possessing exclusive rights to the Windows 10 version made by special order of the Chinese government, C & M Technologies will be able to provide decent technical support for the product, which was previously mentioned by representatives of the highest echelon of the Middle Kingdom, timely providing the necessary patches and updates, as well as tracking the data on the basis of which new versions. In turn, Microsoft will be able to more or less regulate the Windows 10 market in China, and thus increase the revenue from sales of the operating system.

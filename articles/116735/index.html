Web 2.0 Expo 2011: social networks will tell your inside story, and the "clouds" will turn the IT market

In the United States, a Web 2.0 Expo 2011 conference was held, which is somewhat similar to the local RIF and CIB (CIB is most likely SXSW , which is highlighted separately). This year the event was held under the motto "Unlock the digital economy."


The general idea: social networks Twitter, Facebook and Foursquare will share everything that is available in them, and thereby save the digital economy through the dominance of web technologies in everyday life and come Web 3.0. There will be very large Internet companies that can shake traditional market players (for example, Cisco or VISA), which will have to play according to the rules of the cloud technology market.

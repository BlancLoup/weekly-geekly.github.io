Gamers generation



From a pragmatic point of view, any hobby or hobby that in the future develops into a skill should bring some benefit, including in monetary terms. For example, I was fond of cycling for a long time and now opened a bicycle workshop, or if I do not have the proper entrepreneurial abilities, but I develop my body by cycling. In any case, there will be a benefit.


It is also worth mentioning that having a good time is not a benefit. I suggest you look at it from a different angle, namely, to divide any activity into two components:
  • pleasure from the process itself
  • expected benefit

For example, if I watch movies just to have a good time, then there will be no gain, and the pleasure from the process is great. Although it can be argued, watching movies broadens the mind in a sense. You can still give examples of passion for music, which has grown into something serious.

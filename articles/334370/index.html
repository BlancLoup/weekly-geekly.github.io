Air conditioning, heat generation calculation and BTU (British thermal unit).
Service. Chillers

Air conditioning, i.e. heat sink, a thing in modern IT is necessary, for a simple reason - when overheating and prolonged (months) operating equipment at temperatures above +25 degrees - the service life of equipment decreases, sometimes several times (from 5-6 to 2-3 years). The same applies to moisture - excessive moisture is harmful, because it causes even imperceptible, but condensate, and as a result - corrosion of memory contact pads, for example. Of course, the technique can work for years, just standing under the table, but most likely this technique will not work and the three years required from it, and especially not five.
Example number 1 - at the end of the text.

The calculation of heat dissipation of equipment is simple - the power consumption of each piece of equipment is known, about the same power as the equipment will give in the form of heat. Since the installation and calculation of air conditioning systems is usually reserved for special people, then all you need to tell them for the initial calculation is the power of your equipment in kilowatts. Then of course the walls \ windows \ other will be added, but then.

You can, of course, convert kilowatts to joules / sec, then to joules / hour, from there to convert to BTU / hr - British thermal unit, British thermal unit / hour, but there is nothing complicated or special about this conversion. In addition, air conditioners are not produced individually, the power of the air conditioner is selected from a number of capacities offered by the manufacturer, so the main thing:

  • do not forget that there are two capacities of air conditioners (for heat sink and for power consumption from the network);
  • turn on the air conditioner to the network through a separate fuse ON THE PHASE for sure, but it can be doubled, for phase and zero at the same time;
  • Do not forget to sign this fuse directly on the dashboard.

Remember that:

  • pumps (condensate pumping) break
  • drainage channels overgrow no matter what
  • chillers may not start
  • external radiators need annual cleaning before the start of the summer season, and where there are birch and poplar - and after flowering
  • transition winter-summer and summer-winter always requires checking equipment and possible refueling of the refrigerant
  • remember to reserve N + 1 - in the sense that the air conditioning system should work with 1 failed air conditioner indefinitely.

UPS / UPS, Power supply 220/380, 230/400 and sometimes even 127 V.
380V protection

This is the suddenly unnecessary knowledge, when at least a third of Padawans are sure that the outlet, like the battery, has plus and minus, and 380 - something terrible and terrible.
In fact, it was necessary to go to school and college, and remember that:
Mains voltage - the rms (effective) value of the voltage in an electrical AC network .

In the USSR, a three-phase current with interfacial voltage of 380 V and a phase-neutral voltage of 220 V was used in general-purpose electrical networks. According to the interstate standard GOST 29322-92 (IEC 38-83 / IEC 60038: 1983), by 2003, the transition was prescribed rated voltage 230/400 V. According to the updated GOST 29322-2014 (IEC 60038: 2009), the mains voltage should be 230/400 V at a frequency of 50 Hz ¬± 10%.

What to read on the topic

On a mandatory basis: PUE - Rules for the Electrical Installation of Electrical Installations of the Current Editions (2016, 7th edition, Ministry of Energy of the Russian Federation No. 204 of July 8, 2002).

So you don't have a plus or a minus in the outlet, but a phase (that is, the wire where there is electricity) and neutral (where there should be no electricity, but there is a little).

Sometimes (but less and less often) the network 127/220 is found, which means the difference between the phase and zero at 127 V, and between 2 phases - 220 V. As a result, two phases are brought into a normal socket. In such premises, grounding must necessarily be brought into the sockets, and the grounding itself should be checked by specially trained people.

UPS / UPS

It so happens that electricity suddenly ends, sometimes even in the whole area or city. There can be many reasons, from the skillful work of the most terrible enemy for an IT excavator, to a fire at a substation and a shortage of power to the city as a whole.

According to statistics, most often there is no electricity for more than 1-2 minutes, while the USSR legacy in the form of a unified energy system (not the worst legacy, but even very good) switches the power to another branch / beam. However, there are other cases, then electricity may not be hours, and even days.

In the first case (short power interruptions), the UPS / UPS helps.

What you need to remember:

  • The presence of any UPS is better than its absence.
  • Line-Interactive with autotransformer (AVR - Automatic voltage regulator) is better than Off-Line
  • Double conversion UPS are generally good, but expensive.
  • When calculating the thermal capacity of the UPS must be considered.
  • The power that the UPS actually gives out at PEAK has been stopped by the last 10 years of writing, so the selection of the required power of the UPS is a topic for a separate article, written in dozens of them.

Static and dynamic UPS

Static UPS: everything is simple. BIG battery and converters (inverters).

Dynamic UPS

In a small office do not meet. They are set up simply - a BIG and high-speed disc, connected (physically) to the generator, and to the diesel engine.
First, the generator turns the disc, then (when voltage is lost) the disc turns the generator.
If the diesel engine does not start, then (for some manufacturers) at some point the disc turns the diesel engine through an (automatic electromagnetic) coupling, and then the diesel engine turns the disc and the generator.

Fire behavior

Behavior in case of fire for an employee is described in a standard instruction, with which the safety officer comes to each new employee and introduces a signature. Most often, employee behavior is described in two points.

  • In case of personal fire detection, notify others, activate a fire alarm, evacuate according to an evacuation plan, call a fire brigade.
  • When a fire alarm is triggered, evacuate according to the plan, call the fire brigade.
  • Sometimes the head of the department is in the instructions item "count the runaway and make sure that all evacuated from the department."

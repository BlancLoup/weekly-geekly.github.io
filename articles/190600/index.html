Dear Artem!

Thank you for the letter and for the civil position. I will try to answer your questions in order:

1) Upon reaching the threshold of 100,000 votes, the initiative should be submitted to an expert working group indicating, among other things, information about those who voted for and against, about the distribution of votes among regions of Russia. If voting continues, such information ceases to be relevant immediately after the initiative is sent. Without doubting that all initiatives that have exceeded the threshold of 100,000 votes, or 5% of the population of a subject or municipality, have the potential to further collect votes, according to the current regulation of overcoming these thresholds, it is enough to consider the initiative in state bodies. It is also necessary to take into account the fact that when examining experts the experts will be guided by the initiative materials sent officially, and not by operational information from the resource. The initiative of which you are the author was transferred to the expert group on August 12, 2013.

2) On behalf of the operator of the ROI Foundation for Information Democracy, we did everything possible so that initiatives that received the support of 100,000 citizens were immediately sent to the expert group for consideration. In accordance with paragraph 24 of the Rules of the Decree of the President of the Russian Federation N183 of March 4, 2013, the experts have 2 months to make a decision. The meeting of the expert working group will be held in early September 2013, and the decision will be promptly published on the website www.roi.ru.

3) Your desire to speak at a meeting of the expert working group to consider this initiative was reported to the Government of the Russian Federation.

When shooting our video, we decided to go a little different way, creating a Goldberg machine from items and products that are somehow connected with the history of the two companies.

For reference: the Goldberg machine (sometimes called the Robinson-Goldberg machine by the names of two founders of this type of device) is a device that performs a very simple operation in an extremely complex way. In most cases this happens through a long sequence of domino-like interactions. Typically, these mechanisms are used for promotional and entertainment purposes, but the principle of their creation and operation has become the basis for a variety of computer and board games.

To do this, we attracted two teams - the Column Five, an agency that helped create the "Child of the 90s" video and the Brett Doar team, perhaps one of the best designers of Goldberg machines of modernity. For example, he worked on creating advertising for toy maker GoldieBox or a viral music video for the indie band OK Go!

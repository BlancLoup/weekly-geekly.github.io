WWDC 2017. Let's poke a little bit



This year was looking forward to WWDC. While Apple is sitting on the biggest cache cache in history , competitors are propped up on all sides. Google with phones, Amazon with Alex, Microsoft with AR - at some point it even seemed that Apple was starting to catch up, not lead. But today's keynote has returned everything to its place.

Norm


I have been to WWDC twice, this time I decided to look from home. Apparently, wrong, this conference is the most intense in many years. Even by the speed with which they began to announce all the features, it was clear that they would show A LOT. And the guys did not let us down.

Here is the new iOS, and so the long-awaited update of macbooks and aimags. And a cool new iMac Pro for just a modest 5000, a new makos, aypad, very high-quality AR, and HomePod. It's hard to remember a more eventful event from Apple. About all sorts of new iOS and MacOS chips, it's not even particularly interesting to write, read on VC .

iPad with 120Hz support. Those who sat at the gaming monitors understand the difference between 60 and 120 and know how noticeably this changes the perception of the interface. Very correct step. Until the Androids have done the same, the iPad will look like a buttery smooth device. After it, it will be hard to use other devices. Obviously, in the near future we will see the same 120Hz on the iPhone, so we are waiting for the fall.



New iron. Finally, the belated update of MacBooks and aimaks came, but this is not surprising. But the fact that the guys took the Mac Pro and shoved it into the body of the iMac - deserves respect.

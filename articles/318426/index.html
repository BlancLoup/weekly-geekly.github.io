HikVision - stop China before it's too late

The media portal A & S annually ranks the 50 largest publicly available global companies in the technical security systems market.

For the first time, the rating was headed by HikVision, a Chinese manufacturer of video surveillance systems, with a result of $ 3.5 billion, back in 2010, the company occupied only 5th place with a result of $ 564 million.

What allowed HikVision, founded in 2001 with a staff of 30 people, to become the undisputed industry leader in 14 years?



Let's begin to understand what is the company HikVision Digital Technology. Unlike Honeywell, which they pressed, and which produces - video surveillance systems, access control systems, security systems, building automation systems, HikVision produces only video surveillance systems, at least as a company producing video surveillance systems, they appear in the rating, and this is how HikVision is well known in international markets.

HikVision today has 35 branches in China and 21 foreign branches, with a total of more than 18,000 employees working at HikVision.

It occupies 1098 place in the global list of Forbes - 2000 largest companies in the world.

In May 2010, they held an IPO on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange (Shenzhen Stock Exchange - SZSE), at the end of the first trading day, Hikvision had a total valuation of 6 billion US dollars. The current cost is certainly higher.

Revenue


Tell me where your money comes from, and I will tell you who you are. 3.5 billion US dollars is a significant figure let's try to figure out what is behind it.

Compared to 2014, Hikvision's revenue increased by 47%, most of its turnover, namely, 73.7% comes from the domestic market of China.

Sales in the Chinese market grew by 43%, helped by the expansion of the company into the segment of small and medium-sized enterprises.

International sales rose 57%, increasing their contribution to revenue to 26.3%. According to the international rating agency Moody's.

If there is something more interesting than revenue, it is a net profit, and here, too, is not without records.

HikViision's net profit (net margins) is approximately 25%, which is much higher than any publicly available manufacturer of video surveillance systems. A good rate of net profit for the manufacturer of video surveillance systems is considered 10-15%.

HikVision in China


The range of products offered by HikVision in China is much wider than what is offered in foreign markets. In China, in addition to video surveillance systems, they offer access control systems, security alarms, video analytics.

In addition, the company acts not only as a manufacturer, but also as a contractor in the implementation of safety projects, that is, it receives money not only from the sale of equipment, but also for services. These are mainly large government orders.

In 2010, HikVision announced their participation as the general contractor of a security system for the city of Chongqing, the entire project was estimated at $ 800 million. This news provides a rough understanding of the structure of turnover and the approximate influence of government orders on revenue.

But their participation in the project of a video surveillance and video analytics system called as many as the Chinese Skynet, HikVision did not announce. But judging by what the British Daily Mail writes, the project is incomparably more ambitious, is implemented in partnership with Nvidia .

It is important to note that such a significant increase in revenue in China, even despite government projects, indicates that HikVision is winning the competition within China.

HikVision in the world


Foreign turnover accounts for 26.3%, which in dollars equals $ 926 million, for comparison Axis takes the third place in revenue (including China) $ 773 million.

The main trendsetters are naturally Chinese companies, led by HikVision, it was low prices that allowed them in such modest periods not only to gain significant overseas markets, but also to move to first - second place, depending on the country.
For example, in 2015, the EMEA region, for example, HikVision became No. 2 , in terms of revenue with a market share of 9.2%

The second important element of influence is the fact that foreign companies are forced to lower their price in order not to completely lose their position in the market, which naturally affects revenue, and, much more critically, profit.

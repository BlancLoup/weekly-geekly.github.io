Microsoft School on Data Structures and Algorithms


In August 2010, the Microsoft School on Data Structures and Algorithms will be held in St. Petersburg. Senior students, graduate students and young scientists interested in data structures and algorithms can take part in the school. The school is mainly aimed at Russian participants, but residents of other countries who do not need a visa to enter Russia can also apply for participation. The working language of the school is English, however some reports will be in Russian, therefore participants must be fluent in both languages.

Courses
  • Giuseppe F. Italiano (University of Rome "Tor Vergata", Italy)
    Algorithms on Large Data Sets
  • David S. Johnson (AT & T Labs, USA)
    Case Studies: The Traveling Salesman and Bin Packing Problems
  • Clifford Stein (Columbia University, USA)
    Combinatorial Optimization Algorithms
  • Robert E. Tarjan (Princeton University and HP, USA)
    Data structures
  • Renato F. Werneck (Microsoft Research, USA)
    Shortest Paths and Experimental Evaluation of Algorithms
Please tell interested students about this school by sending them a link to the website ( http://logic.pdmi.ras.ru/midas ) or by printing and hanging a poster .

Data center out of the box: Vblock platform overview from VCE

Private clouds built on the basis of virtual infrastructures are becoming more and more popular among IT departments of various organizations. However, building a virtual private cloud is not an easy task and requires serious time and financial investments. As a solution to this problem, MUK Group of Companies recommends Vblock, a fully integrated platform for building converged data center infrastructure.



Much has already been written and written about the advantages of using private clouds in building data centers compared to traditional approaches. These include reducing costs, increasing the flexibility and scalability of the infrastructure, and greater availability of information. However, building a private cloud and virtualizing the infrastructure in practice is not an easy task. Selecting, integrating, and customizing the components of a future data center can be a major headache for a company's IT department. Do not forget about possible compatibility problems, which lead to a significant increase in the timing of implementation and entail an increase in the cost of operating solutions. VCE presented its approach to solving these problems. This is a Vblock platform that offers accelerated data center transformation technology and significantly reduces capital and operating expenses.

Possible system configurations

Vblock infrastructure platforms scale to deliver the right performance and capacity to meet the needs of business applications. At the moment there are two main families - Vblock Series 300 and Vblock Series 700, which include several models of the complex.

The Vblock 300 line is designed to meet the needs of a wide range of companies. The architecture of the platform is formed by EMC VNX storage systems, Cisco UCS blades and switches, EMC Ionix UIM / P resource allocation technology and VMware vSphere virtualization tool. This platform supports scaling for small and medium-sized CRM and SCM systems, email, file sharing and printing, and collaboration. The 300 series includes four models - EX, FX, GX and HX. The main differences between the models are the scalability of storage and computing power.



Vblock 700 family models are designed to deploy very large virtualization infrastructures. This platform will satisfy the most stringent requirements of ERP, CRM, database, collaboration tools and messaging services. Vblock 700 Series systems are based on Cisco UCS server blades, Nexus and MDS network switches, EMC VMAX or VMAXe storage arrays, EMC Ionix UIM / P resource allocation technologies, and VMware vSphere virtualization platform. The Vblock 700 series includes two models - LX and MX.

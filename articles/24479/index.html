Trixbox or open-source VoIP platform in your home


We greet you! We all use the phone, and the modern realities of life are such that even in this, probably, one of the oldest technological communication systems (not counting the telegraph), there are just the greatest changes, both conceptual and structural, as well as more mundane, technological. This is due to the penetration of packet data and digital protocols into this area. It turned out that voice traffic can be transmitted and is often more profitable through existing communication channels using TCP / IP, UDP, and other IP-based protocols. The number of subscribers of the largest medium of communication - Skype is already approaching close to 10 million people (precisely those who are online at any given time), and this already says a lot. But if for some reason you want to create your own private network for communication, you absolutely do not need to think about the license of the telephony provider. The open free Trixbox project is also suitable.

Previously, this project was called, in my opinion, more appropriate to its purpose and reflecting the essence - Asterisk @ Home , which meant that it was based on the popular and powerful open VoIP platform - Asterisk PBX . This platform is one of the most popular and powerful servers for building voice communication platforms based on open source and OS Linux. Although there are assemblies for other platforms, including Win32. But self-installation and configuration of such a solution is quite complicated, and there will not always be a specialist who will take care of such a decision. Therefore, the developers decided to create a simple integrated solution suitable for deployment both in a small office and even at home (probably, this is an advanced home, where there is at least one serious administrator user).

Trixbox was built as a disk image, including the operating system, Asterisk platform, and other additional components, such as embedded billing, an account management system with a web interface (by the way, it is used, or at least used before). CRM SugarCRM that allows you to very well and quickly deploy an office support system for your business inside.) There is also support for both expansion cards for communication with landlines and the xPL interface for home automation. Additional digital communication capabilities are also supported - voice mail, voice menu and management, queues and priorities of calls, call forwarding, web access to voice mail and other features.

The project is based on CentOS 5.1 OS, which is specially designed for providers and hosters, as well as the latest versions of other components and it is ready for use right from the box, as it is distributed both as an ISO image and immediately in a pre-installed form (image for VMware ). And so, if you are interested in the rapid deployment of a VoIP system in your home or in the automation of a small office or branch, this solution will suit you better than others, also based on open source programs and protocols. And for more demanding users, there is a commercial version, trixbox Pro as well as support and other services.
  • Name : Trixbox CE (formerly asterisk @ home)
  • Current version : 2.6.0.7
  • License : GPL
  • Size : about 600 MB (ISO image or image for VMware)
  • Project page : http://sourceforge.net/projects/asteriskathome/
  • Download section : http://sourceforge.net/project/showfiles.php?group_id=123387

New Russian translation of Creative Commons licenses

At the beginning of the week, new translations of Creative Commons licenses to Russian were published . I advise everyone to read that there are fewer myths and delusions around the great and terrible licenses.

Here are the translations of all six modern licenses posted on Wikisource (all these are just simple translations, not to be confused with the additional adaptation of licenses to the laws of the Russian Federation):
  • Creative Commons Attribution 3.0 Unported (compare with original)
  • Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 Unported (compare with original)
  • Creative Commons Attribution-NoDerivs 3.0 Unported (compare with original)
  • Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial 3.0 Unported (compare with original)
  • Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike 3.0 Unported (compare with original)
  • Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-NoDerivs 3.0 Unported (compare with original)
Naturally, the translation simply must be a whole bunch of errors , so in accordance with the principle of "wiki" everyone is invited to correct them. =)) That is, you find an error - rule boldly !

For those who have forgotten, I remind you that the first translations (lower quality, translated parts and not completed) were made in 2008 on ccrussia.org, and after it stopped working for some unknown reason, they moved to Wikisource .

UPD : It would be especially great if a lawyer read and corrected the translations.

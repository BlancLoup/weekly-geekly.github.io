Dinosaur Tablet Review - Compaq TC1000

After the advent of the iPad, the market for gadgets was filled with an abundance of various imitative constructions, and more often Chinese non-power hardware. However, not many people know that tablet PCs were already released before Apple. Despite that level of technology, very interesting devices were produced. I want to tell you about one of them. His name is Compaq TC1000.



Description


Model TC1000 was released by Compaq (now a piece of HP) in the distant 2003 year. In fact, it was not quite a tablet, it is a hybrid device, because There was an opportunity to attach a keyboard to it and use it as a regular laptop. This idea, IMHO, it would be nice to apply to modern devices of this type.

So, the characteristics of our grandfather in the basic configuration:

Processor: Transmeta Crusoe TM5800 (1GHz)
RAM: 256MB SDRAM (can be expanded to 768MB)
Hard Drive: 30GB
Video card: GeForce2 Go 100 with 16 MB SDRAM memory
Screen: 10.4-inch TFT display with wide viewing angle, XGA resolution. (touch magnetoresistive. only responds to the stylus)
LAN: 10 / 100Mbps
WiFi: 802.11b
Modem: Type III Mini PCI 56K (V.92)
Li-ion six-section battery with a capacity of 4400mAh and an internal battery that allows for "hot swap" of the main
standby mode.
Weight: 1.4kg
OS: Windows XP Tablet Edition

Ports: PCMCIA, 2USB, VGA, LAN, 3xAudio, CF

Agree, quite a good car for that time. Also packed in a very durable case.

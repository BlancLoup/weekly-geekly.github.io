PC air filters

For a long time I put homemade air filters in computer cooling systems. In this article I decided to summarize my experience and show some mods.

Why air filters in the PC?



First of all, to simplify further maintenance. Cleaning two-three filters with a vacuum cleaner takes much less time than dismantling the cooling system, opening the power supply, cleaning radiators, fans and other pieces of iron from dust.
Well, aesthetics are enhanced, to the delight of fans of computer Feng Shui.

Why not buy ready-made filters in the store?



Air filters are rarely found in computer stores, often in electronic components stores like chip-and-dip. However, there is little choice. Making a home filter from scrap materials is cheaper and takes less time than running around shopping. Well, it brings more pleasure, yes.

Finally, some tips:


  • Do not use too dense materials such as vacuum cleaner bags. Before you use any material, try to breathe through it.
  • The filter design should exclude the ingress of matter into the fan blades and its stopping. Blocking the fan can lead to sad consequences.
  • Reduce the fan speed to a reasonable minimum. The less air is pumped, the less dust accumulates.
  • Perhaps the reduction in air flow will have to somehow compensate. For example, put the thermal grease better.

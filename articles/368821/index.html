A piece of history of space exploration is on sale

On October 26, the Dallas Auction House began accepting applications for the acquisition of a memory component of an onboard computer, which was in space and went around Earth orbit on March 23, 1965, along with astronauts Gus Grissom and John Young. It was the first NASA space mission to launch two astronauts around Earth orbit and the first mission where the on-board computer was claimed. It was developed by IBM.


Matrix of memory.

Gusom and John are one of the most famous astronauts of NASA. Grissom was the second American to go into outer space. Unfortunately, he died in 1967 during the tests before the launch of the Apollo 1 lunar manned program. Young, in turn, made six more space missions during his 42 years of service at NASA and was the ninth person to set foot on the lunar surface.

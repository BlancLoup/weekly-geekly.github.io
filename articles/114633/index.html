Training non-standard actions: how to make work more productive

The fact that non-standard actions help self-development, I think, is far from news to the community and especially to readers of the GTD blog. In the topic, the topic is revealed somewhat more fully, plus there are examples of what is worth doing in order to feel good and easy to work with a fresh mind and keen attention.

In order to work well, it is important to be a victory geek (which is regularly mentioned in various Success Stories and motivation books). Each victory is a proof of one's own strength, fixation for further success, improvement of mood and condition. And yet - building new neural connections and a lot of development.

Roughly speaking, it works like this: when you win, the brain starts the stimulation processes, fixing the tendency to success at a low level.

There is also the so-called ‚Äúfrontier factor‚Äù: businessmen who have ever experienced a serious risk and won, usually achieve more than those who always choose complete security (research and methodology are described in the book ‚ÄúGood to Great‚Äù).

Fresh emotions from unusual actions and overcoming oneself and circumstances are the emotions of victory. Training non-standard actions is a thing that helps to feel the risk, to do unusual things and get a bonus in the form of sharply increasing productivity.

Non-standard actions are divided into three main categories:
- Just unusual, which build new neural connections in the brain.
- Associated with the rejection of something, giving a feeling of lightness.
- Physically safe, but risky, which give the feeling of victory.

The most interesting, of course, are the third, but first we analyze the first two types.

So, just unusual actions


Example : Try a half hour to work the mouse with your left hand if you are right handed. Pretty soon, ‚Äúhalt EXP‚Äù in the form of a charge of vivacity. The task is to disable the template reactions, which run on all the usual actions, as far as possible. In Eastern Zen practice, for example, there is an exercise, when a student asks a teacher permission for each of his actions, even for a simple desire to scratch. The goal is to catch any patterns and come to the awareness of each action.

Profit : a sense of cheerfulness, positive consolidation in the development of complex things, the potential development of neural connections, reduced downtime (including one that leads to salty coffee, because the code, and the salt shaker was closer to the sugar bowl).

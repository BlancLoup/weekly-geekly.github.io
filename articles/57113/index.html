Overview of the multiplatform portable emulator Dingoo Digital A-320


One unknown Chinese firm, Shenzhen Dingoo Digital Product Company, on a beautiful sunny morning of February 2009, launched a wonderful device on the market - the Dingoo Digital A-320.
The main purpose of Dingoo is emulation of old gaming consoles and machines. Officially supported as many as seven consoles! In addition, a small white Satan-box can play music and radio, play movies, show pictures, work as a voice recorder, read books. Miracle and only! Or just another Chinese rattle?

This review will allow you to find out what this thing is - Dingoo. Yes, Digital. Yes, even the A-320.
And, I hope, will answer all your questions.

Specifications


Screen: 320x240 pixels, 2.8 inches, 16 million colors;
Processor: 430 MHz MIPS;
RAM: 32 MB;
Memory: 4 GB of internal flash memory, a slot for Mini-SD HC cards (officially up to 8 GB);
Power: Built-in 1700 mAh battery;
Outputs: 1 x mini-USB for sharing information with a PC and charging the battery; 1 x TV-Out (RCA) - to display the image on the TV; 1 x 3.5 mm headphone jack;
Dimensions: 125x55.5x14 mm;
Weight: 110 g

Contents of delivery


In addition to the device itself, the kit includes: a USB cable for charging and synchronizing with a PC, a network charger, a cable for connecting to a TV, headphones, gags, documentation in English and Chinese.

The box visibly shabby, traveling two weeks during the delivery in Russia.
The charger looks like Apple product chargers ( animation of the charger ). The included headphones are pretty lousy. For listening to music and movies are hardly suitable, but for games - completely. Connecting them once and checking the sound quality, put it back in the package until better times.

The game console looks great:



Build quality is very good. When squeezing the device, nothing creaks, no backlash. Milky-white plastic housing pleased with its quality. If it were not for the hieroglyphs, the inscription "Made in China" and the omnipresent golden sticker "QC PASSED" on the back of the A-320, in general, we can assume that this is some kind of new game console from Nintendo. Even the fastening points of the body halves with screws are covered with white translucent rubber plugs. Thanks to these stubs that act as legs, Dingoo stably rests on smooth surfaces. The front panel is completely covered with transparent plastic and due to this it acquires a beautiful gloss. Gloss, remarkably collecting fingerprints. But it is scratch resistant.
Headphone, video output and mini-USB connectors do not loose when sticking cables into them. And on the contrary, even too tightly cling to the plugs.
Control buttons always respond to pressing. There are no false clicks or failures.
The stereo speakers located on the bottom of the device are truly stereo. All emulators have support for stereo audio output.
The screen resolution of 320x240 pixels in the sun remains visible, only the image fades a little.

A lithium-ion, non-replaceable battery, soldered to the main board inside the device. Actual battery capacity: 1700 mAh.
This battery lasts about 7-8 hours of continuous play and video viewing. When the USB cable is connected to the console and PC, the device doesn‚Äôt display the battery charging process as if it were connected to a normal charger. From this we can conclude that our multi-emulator will not charge from the PC. Only from the charger.

The console is very compact, only one third more than a plastic card in width. With a size of 125x55.5x14 millimeters and a weight of 110 grams, it fits easily into a shirt pocket.

The most terrible for the quality of emulation. And this is due to the fact that stripes appear on a fast-moving image as on an interlaced comb video with interpolation turned off. The most impressive game is the 3D quest " 7 Days. Salvation ". In appearance and gameplay is very similar to the series of games Silent Hill :

Audio player


The player supports playback of MP3 , WMA , APE , FLAC , WAV formats . You can play music in the background. Naturally, everywhere, except for emulators, video players and radio.

Much to our regret, the player does not understand the musical tags, and during playback it displays only the name of the file. Although, in the main menu, you can sort the lists of songs by album, artist and title. That is, the player can handle the tags, but simply does not display them when playing songs.
In the settings, you can select one of 7 equalizer presets or customize one of your own. There are options for enhanced 3D sound: select the type of headphones, the type of 3D processing, automatic sound balance, bass boost and adjust the high-frequency compensation.

When using good headphones or speakers, the quality can be called good. No crackle, clicks. Between tracks the noise is almost inaudible.
But it is quite difficult to choose the sound parameters for myself, since I did not like the built-in equalizers.

The player uses its database to store information about the music, so after each disconnection of the device from the computer, it ‚Äúwools‚Äù the internal memory for the presence of new musical compositions for a few seconds.

Video player


Supports a bunch of formats: RMVB , RM , AVI , WMV , FLV , MPEG , DAT , MP4 , ASF . Various variations of DivX and Xvid go without problems. There is even support for the old DivX 3 , so my dusty collection of old avishkas on CDs in this format will find a second life. Without transcoding can play 700 megabytes of DVD-Rip'y. Half-and-gigabyte movies are already difficult for him - the video will stop for a split second about every 5 seconds to load the stream. Although, to be honest, I never hoped that the player would understand the video with a frame size of more than 320x240 pixels.

Support for subtitles is missing.
Another upsetting owners of iPod and iPhone. The video intended for these devices Dingoo does not understand. When you try to open a file, he thinks for a second and then goes back to selecting files.

FM radio


The receiver can memorize up to 40 channels, there is an autoscanning function of stations. There is the ability to record sound in the file. In the settings you can select the FM-region (China, Europe, USA, Japan), the strength of signal reception. Just like in the audio player you can listen to the radio in the background.

Hooray! Finally, someone made support for playing radio without " Plug in a headset "! : 0) It can be useful if the headphones are forgotten at home. The radio will work without stuck headphones acting as an antenna. At the same time, the quality of signal reception will naturally decrease.

Sound recording


Able to record sound in MP3 and WAV. In the settings you can select the recording quality. There is nothing more to say, except that the microphone is average in sensitivity. It is advisable to pronounce the sound directly into it. When you remove a sound source by 2 meters or more, the volume drops significantly.


View images


Dingoo Digital A-320 is able to work with images in formats JPG , BMP , GIF , PNG . Pictures can be enlarged, rotated, make a slideshow. GIF animation works. From the image viewer, you can assign any picture as the background of the main menu, music player, sound recording, book reader.

In the slideshow mode, you can set the frame shift time: 2, 3, 5, 8 and 15 seconds. There are various transition effects: dissolving, shifting, hitting, squeezing, etc. The effects are relatively smooth.

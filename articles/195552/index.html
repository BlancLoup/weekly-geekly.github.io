8+ in 1: how I wanted to attract attention

Good evening, harabrachiteli!

Before the release of 8+ in 1: our new messenger for iOS and Android , I had to do some work on exploring the places where we were going to publish press releases. One of these places was Habr.

We asked the following questions:
  • What day of the week is best to post?
  • At what time (time interval) is it best to publish a post?
  • What time of year is the best time to post?






Introduction

From the very beginning, I did not leave the feeling that I had already seen a similar article, somewhere this question had already been raised, but where ?! I could not find it, most likely I was looking badly ( here , here , here ), so I decided to conduct an actual investigation and, if possible, find out some interesting points.

I want to clarify that by ‚Äúbest‚Äù we mean attracting the maximum number of users who either view (read), add to bookmarks, or comment on a post.

It all started with parsing articles. Fortunately, Habr has post identifiers for which you can safely walk and collect information:

habrahabr.ru/post[id]/

Filtration

Now you need to get rid of all unnecessary files - these are pages with posts that were hidden or those that have not yet been created.
In parallel, you can process each page, ‚Äúrip out‚Äù the data we need and write it to another file (shared).

From each blog entry we will receive the following information:
  • Publication date
  • Views count
  • # Of favorites
  • Number of comments

The next thing we do is to bring the publication dates to a single format: TT:TT.DD.MM.YYYY
In the current data we can meet the following types of publication dates:
13 2006 18:23
15 05:27

For simplicity, I excluded the dates in the format (there were literally a couple of them, tolerable losses):
18:23
17:44

Our next parser should solve two problems:
  • Convert the date of publication in the desired format
  • Correct (null) entries in the ‚ÄúBookmarks‚Äù column to ‚Äú0‚Äù (null entry in the column with views means that there was no such functionality at the time of publication of the article)

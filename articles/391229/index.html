Brief background


A few years ago, manufacturers of printing devices made the main profit not from the sale of equipment, but from the sale of consumables to it. That is why many third-party organizations began to offer their services to install the so-called. ‚ÄúContinuous ink supply systems‚Äù (CISS) for cartridge printers. The solution had both advantages (obvious savings on consumption) and disadvantages (deprivation of warranty, problems with ‚Äúleft‚Äù inks, which slowly put the print head out of order, often - leakage of poor-quality ink tanks, etc ...)

In 2011, the Epson company took a risk and radically changed the business model. The printer began to cost exactly as much as it is in production, and the cost of ink has decreased several times, while their number has grown 10 times!

Epson engineers suggested an alternative: they did not just install an ink tank into the printer, but made it as reliable as possible, integrated the ink pressure monitoring system directly into the printer so that the ink was evenly fed to the ink and the print quality remained. The company's engineers also thought up the convenience of refueling, ease of transportation and compactness of new devices. All this made it possible to abandon the cartridge and significantly reduce the cost of printing.

Ask, why is this solution better than ‚Äúartisanal‚Äù CISS? Yes, at least by the fact that it is an engineering solution for mass production, time-tested, and with a guarantee of quality from a Japanese manufacturer of equipment . Simply put - "bought and use."

Some history and facts for those who are interested.


Russia is one of the first countries where the Epson Print Factory series devices were launched for the first time.

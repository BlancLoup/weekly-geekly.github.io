Drones deliver tea in China

Four hundred and fifty people in Beijing, Guangzhou and Shanghai will receive ginger tea within an hour of ordering. Quadrocopters will deliver tea. The service is available from 4 to 6 February - so Taobao, Alibaba's marketplace, will test this method.



Delivery of goods using drones tried Amazon, Google, DHL and other companies. In the United States, this delivery method became a problem because of the law, although the first precedent in the form of a $ 1,100 fine for shooting from a drone in 2011 was created in January 2015. Amazon requests permission from the US Federal Aviation Administration, and DHL only works a small island.

Similar problems for commercial (and not only) enterprises arise in Russia. For Dodo Pizza, the use of drones could result in a fine of two hundred thousand rubles, but the judge rejected this decision.

The Alibaba marketplace decided to test multicopters in Beijing, Guangzhou and Shanghai. A pack of ginger tea with brown sugar weighing three hundred forty grams will be received by the buyer one hour after the order. This tea is commonly used to relieve menstrual cramps. The quadcopter will deliver a box to four hundred and fifty lucky ones.

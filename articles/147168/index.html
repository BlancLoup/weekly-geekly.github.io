Distribution of music is essentially free. Should we feel guilty? Do we have to force ourselves to always pay $ 15 for a bunch of music files, simply because we did it before?
The free distribution of music has changed our culture in absolutely amazing ways. The cultural value of this achievement is enormous. Even my grandmother understands how magical it is (she is a pretty advanced person and loves to surf on YouTube in search of a little music).
Some, it seems, do not understand this: they fear something, they strain their eyesight, looking for negative consequences. Moreover, the music business is slowly, worse than molasses, adapts to new conditions.
As a result, we hear a lot of complaints. But from a business point of view, the rejection of the current situation due to his own laziness and nostalgia looks like a sports throwing to the cat under the tail of the chances for success.

Where is the money


There was no such artist in the history of Western music, who would not want to live off his work. They are not elves, they need to eat.
Even Bach suffered a financial collapse (despite the fact that he was widely known during his lifetime). After his death, his wife had to sell some of his musical notes to the butcher (for use as wrapping paper). Is this not an example of poverty and despair? I would like everything to be easy. But nothing happens by magic.
Since then, little has changed, except for the fact that now there are much more opportunities to show creativity. The Internet has equalized all of us in a certain sense. It allows the listener to contact the musician directly, provides ways to attract attention, and with its help everyone can spread information quickly and for free.
This is damn fantastic!
But let's get down to business. How to make a musician?

Music is just commerce on a par with other types of business. Here are the rules of supply and demand. You need to provide your customers with what they really want to buy. This is an important part of the business.
Do we need innovation - taking into account advanced technologies that attract more and more people? No question: this is also part of your business.

Business 101


If you sell something, it is useful to learn more about your customers. This is called the study of "potential circle of buyers."
You figure out who brings you money and who doesn't. Quite a useful activity! This way you can focus on serving people who are interested in your content. Or change your product range to attract more customers from a specific target audience. (Pro advice: blaming buyers is the worst way to increase sales).
Students? They are not so easy to buy albums for $ 15 apiece. But they really love music! Look at them: they go to concerts, exchange your goods ... they can become your regular customers.

The record industry definitely lacks viable business options. There is no fault of the listeners. They also cannot be held liable for damages. The decline in profits is due to the fact that the market has changed, but the industry has not been able to adapt to these changes.

Conference ProfsoUX 2015: Review of applications for speeches

ProfsoUX is undoubtedly the main Russian conference for UX-professionals and anyone who is interested in the issues of convenience of interfaces and services, usability, UX (User eXperience), etc. etc. It has been conducted since 2012 by the UX SPb community in St. Petersburg ( Habro Report ProfsoUX 14 ).



This year, the orgs received about fifty applications for speeches. This is about 6 screens of text, if you put them all in one table. The organizers asked me, as an experienced Habro-browser, to evaluate these applications.

It is clear that in these usability I am like a pig in oranges, and therefore I looked at the applications with my amateur bell tower. I was interested in the following: I noticed a long time ago that applications for performances are of particular value because they show the average temperature in a hospital as a cut of the industry at a particular point in time. And perhaps this is true for any professional conference: people say exactly what hurts, what you want to talk about, as a topic. No matter what area they work.

Below is a brief overview of applications for speaking at Profsoux 15. For convenience, I have divided them into thematic blocks.

Traditional UX Techniques

Part of the application is devoted to new approaches or a deeper consideration of traditional for UX techniques: user research, design, UX testing.
  • How do you actually search for information and make decisions?
  • What to do with the ever-increasing information noise?
  • How to conduct testing with minimal cost or just one respondent?
  • How not to waste time and design in the subway, on napkins and even in the sand?

Based on their experience, speakers are ready to answer these questions to conference participants.

Here are some of the applications of this block:
  • Usability Testing for 10 000 rubles
  • Thorny path of decision making
  • Information Persons (as people search for information)
  • When is one respondent enough?
  • The boiling point of the brain - protecting the user from information overload
  • People against Panteleimon
  • Acceleration of the prototyping process
  • Using the Symbol function in Balsamiq Mockup

MIPT students "invented" a mouse with an accelerometer

The magazine "The Secret of the Firm" writes about the "unique" development of students of MIPT - the manipulator called Micrise is positioned as a convenient replacement for a computer mouse. The invention consists of two clips and an accelerometer. This set is worn on the hand, and special software allows convenient three-dimensional navigation in computer games or other programs.

The manipulator is an aluminum clip, conveniently fixed on the brush. At the level of the user's palm, there are two touch-buttons, one for a click, the other for a scroll. Micrise can be used as a normal mouse, leading them on a horizontal surface, and as a game joystick.

UGC-sites impose content filters on samples



The largest media and Internet companies have joined forces to develop a copyright protection standard for UGC content. A joint statement on this was published by CBS, Dailymotion, Fox Entertainment, Microsoft, MySpace, NBC, Veoh Networks, Viacom and Walt Disney.

Of course, first of all they are concerned about the problem of ‚Äúleakage‚Äù of their own content on the Internet. The right holders are in no way satisfied with the current situation when protected content is removed from the site only after a complaint. They would like the content to not be able to get into open access at all, and all UGC-sites have implemented the technology of automatic filtering by samples. That is, copyright holders provide samples of protected content, and UGC-sites ensure that their filter does not miss such content in open access. Actually, this is the subject of a joint statement. If the standard is adopted, the introduction of content filters will become mandatory for all UGC sites.

The key point of the published User Generated Content Principles is as follows.

It is not a problem. It should not be necessary for you to use it. goal of eliminating infringing content.

So, we are talking about the global implementation of an automatic copyright filtering system based on the samples provided by the copyright holder until the end of 2007 on all UGC sites.

The only problem is that such a high-quality system does not yet exist. There is no technology that effectively identifies illegal content. Some experts generally doubt that its creation is possible at least theoretically. A few days ago, Google began testing a copyright filter on Youtube , but this was done under the brutal pressure of media corporations, and the automatic filter works just awful and blocks thousands of legal videos.

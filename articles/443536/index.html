Dark theme for Chrome on MacOS

Negotiations on the introduction of dark mode in Google Chrome on macOS began in September last year, when the developers were discussing the Chromium Gerrit plan. Now an extremely popular feature has finally appeared for Mac users with the official release of Chrome 73.



To get started with the dark mode for Chrome on macOS, you first need to turn on the dark theme on your computer, go to the Apple menu, select "System Preferences" and click "General". Then you will see two options for the appearance of your system: Light and Dark. When dark mode is on, Chrome automatically takes into account the theme of your system.

However, Windows users will certainly soon get a dark theme, as Google developer Pete Lepage promised that there would be support from Microsoft. Chrome will also change its theme depending on the theme of Windows.

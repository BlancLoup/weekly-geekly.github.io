CINEMOOD Storyteller: a miniature cinema for children and adults



There are many different kinds of devices capable of projecting an image. These are projectors, picoprojectors, nozzles for telephones with projectors and even tablets with built-in miniature projecting modules. But for a long time no one could offer a universal device for the whole family. An ordinary projector is rather cumbersome, in many cases a connection to a laptop is required, since not all devices work with external media. In addition, you need to connect to a regular electrical outlet.

Not so long ago in Russia, a device that can be called a mini-cinema. In the office for presentations it can hardly be used, but in the family, at home - completely. And that's why.



A bit of background


The creator of CINEMOOD is businessman Mikhail Bukhovtsev, back in 2014 he decided to make a mini-cinema for a Russian user. It was immediately decided to load content into the device‚Äôs memory so that customers would not have to scour the Internet in search of suitable materials. Well, since the device was supposed to be "home", the main content was made by cartoons. To do this, ‚ÄúSoyuzmultfilm‚Äù acquired the rights to show 50 cartoons (now it‚Äôs about 500 cartoons from 30 studios).

The devices were made (the first version was called Multikubik) in China, their sales were more or less active. But this did not last long - until December 2014. Then the supplier sent more than a thousand copies with defective optical modules. But as the saying goes: "Everything that does not kill makes us stronger." Therefore, the domestic company began to develop their own.

A device circuit was developed from scratch, and the resulting devices were disassembled and reassembled. Moreover, the optical module was developed in conjunction with Texas Instruments. In addition, the mini-cinema (only now called CINEMOOD Storyteller) received a motorized focus and a passive cooling system. The new system was released on Indigegogo, where it collected $ 163,718. That was enough for the launch of mass production of the device. CINEMOOD Storyteller was able to enter the international market, although the first version was sold only in Russia.



Collect the device again began in China, but with a different partner. The device, among other things, equipped with WiFi, which allowed to get rid of the wires. Thanks to the wireless connection, content can be downloaded to the device remotely. For example, a child can send a video message or a fairy tale. The cartoon collection, since the development of the product, has become more extensive. And another interesting point - home mini-cinema received covers with NFC-tags. With their help, the user can update the library content.

The company says that CINEMOOD is not a picoprojector competitor, but home theaters. Central Asia - families with children.



How it all works



The mini-cinema Cinemood (it is also called Multikubik 2.0) can work without recharging for about 3 hours at the expense of a capacious battery. If it is turned on for about 20 minutes a day (show a video fairy tale or cartoon to a child before going to bed), the resource will last for a week.



The device itself is like a cube, on the edges of which are buttons. Both children and their parents can cope with management, no matter how far they are from technology.

Covers

Usually the case is put on the gadget to protect it from scratches and other troubles. In the case of Cinemood, this is all true, but there is one more thing. The fact is that when putting the cover on the device, it recognizes the type of cover (using NFC tags).



In the memory of the device from the "cloud", new cartoon series with a character are automatically loaded, the cover in the form of which is now worn. Want "Smesharikov"? Please, here they are. In addition, covers are also available with the characters "Om Yum", "Seals, Go!", "HooplaKidz". Smart covers are made of hypoallergenic poluaretana, safe for children.

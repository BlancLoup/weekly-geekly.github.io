Jumping Chinese Grasshopper from LinkSpace

The Chinese private space company LinkSpace has built a test rocket stand for vertical take-off and landing, similar to the ‚ÄúGrasshopper‚Äù from SpaceX, and since January of this year it has been conducting increasingly complex tests. After the tests, the hang-up on the leash, the company moved to full-fledged jumps, rising to 20 meters on March 27, and 40 meters on April 19. And thanks to the progress in action cameras, we can look at these flights from the angles that were absent during the days of the Grasshopper Mask.


Shot from video onboard camera

Chinese private space company LinkSpace was founded in 2014. In 2017, they hit space news feeds, presenting the NewLine-1 rocket project using SpaceX ideas - vertical landing of the first stage on engines.


Slide sensational presentation

Visually, the first step looked exactly like Falcon 9, including lattice steering wheels, folding supports and even coloring, which caused a lot of comments about copying SpaceX technologies. In fact, NewLine-1 will be much less, it is planned to install a different number of engines with a smaller burden, and it is more correct to say that LinkSpace was inspired by the SpaceX concept and realize the same method of ensuring multiple use, but on its technologies.

In 2016-18, the company conducted tests on a flying stand, which was a frame with tanks, even without external plating. As in any complex technique, there were failures, but by the beginning of 18, the stand had learned to rise and sit back without a leash.

For any serious flight, the first stand was small, so LinkSpace made a larger model RLV-T5, also called NewLine Baby. The new test bench has five engines, a height of 8.1 meters and a weight of 1.5 tons. At the beginning of the year, tethering tests were launched, which, judging by the time of transition to the next stage, were successful.

Reusable Launch Vehicle Prototype RLV-T5 (NewLine Baby) had a new milestone. We had accomplished the rocket takes off, hovers and recovers under tethered protection. RLV-T5 in free state. pic.twitter.com/BOg7C9RqPp

On March 27, for the first time, the device went to independent flight, rose to a height of 20 meters and made a soft landing at the launch site.

The announced plans LinkSpace storming height of 1 km, followed by a soft landing in the second half of this year. This is comparable to the tests of "Grasshopper" from SpaceX, the first version of which rose to 744 m, and the second - to a kilometer.

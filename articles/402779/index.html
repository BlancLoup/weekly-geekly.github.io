Project Bank Polybius: how we make the first cryptobank with Estonian-Swiss roots

News about new cryptostates and blockchain projects appear almost every week, but so far the cryptobusiness did not have its own bank - which is even a bit strange, given the fintech focus of most of these projects and the importance of banking services for the functioning of the business.


There comes ‚Äútime of the first‚Äù in the world of banks

The Estonian company Polybius Foundation decided to fill this gap and establishes the first bank specializing in financial services for cryptostartups and blockchain projects around the world: the Polybius Bank Project. About what this bank is, why it is needed, how it is arranged - and, most importantly, how to participate in this project before it is too late - our first article on this topic.

Little history


The founders of the Polybius Foundation are the same guys that founded the manufacturer of mining equipment and the blockchain developer HashCoins, whose first profile of activity was the production and sale of mining equipment. Of course, like any business, they needed a bank account. The dialogue with the clerk looked like this:

- What is your business?
- We sell mining equipment and extract bitcoins themselves.
- Oh, oh, we do not need this!

It should be understood that the process of recognition of cryptocurrency by the regulatory authorities began quite recently and so far is in full swing. Prior to this, some three or four years ago, to come to the bank with the statement ‚Äúwe are here cryptocurrency mine‚Äù was equivalent to a request to open an account for storing money from sales of drugs and weapons. To find a bank that would agree to service a HashCoins account, the guys had to say that they were selling computers and components (which, in fact, was also true, but did not contain the alarming words ‚Äúcryptocurrency‚Äù or ‚Äúbitcoins‚Äù) and agreed to an increased interest rate ‚ÄúFor riskiness‚Äù, which the bank reduced only years of stable and trouble-free cooperation later.

Similar problems arise in cryptostates around the world. In Russia, as we remember, a couple of years ago, they were almost going to plant a cryptocurrency. The situation has changed - largely thanks to German Gref himself, who heard the words ‚Äúblockchain‚Äù and Agile at Western conferences and rushed to introduce them in Sberbank and promote it in the country - thanks to which Russia has become almost one of the leading countries in developing blockchain projects and cryptocurrency. But progress is uneven across the planet, and the problems described above are still more or less relevant for many countries.

How it will work


First of all it will be a bank - i.e. just a bank, a financial institution for everyone. The Polybius Bank project will provide a traditional set of financial services such as deposits, loans, issue of bank cards, etc. not only cryptostates, but also ordinary businesses and, of course, individuals.

In this case, the latest achievements of technologies, both crypto and ordinary ones, will be used.

The point is to re-invent what can be re-invented and optimize what can be optimized in a modern financial institution within the framework of the Procrustean bed of legislation that now exist. But European legislation has never been laconic. On the one hand, this is a plus: the very fact that the bank is located in European jurisdiction guarantees the European level of respect for the rights of depositors.

  • Charter with a smart contract. Under the terms of the ICO, investors will be assigned and guaranteed by a smart contract the payment of 20% of the profits of the Polybius Bank project. The very fact that the concept of a smart contract and ICO appears in such a conservative document as the Charter is a small breakthrough and a true sign that change is already beginning.

  • AI in lending. Like any other bank, we will be engaged in lending. And we plan to use for scoring - determining the creditworthiness of the client - the neural network. It may not sound like a super innovation, because in fact, banks are already introducing similar technologies around the world, but we will support artificial-intellectual scoring with another interesting development of our authorship.

  • DigiPass. Here comes the blockchain. DigiPass will be a universal decentralized repository of private information about the user - from credit history to medical records - in the blockchain, which only he will have access to. The role of "Polybius" is to act as a trusted agent for verifying user input. Information certified by the bank will be valid for all credit, financial and many public and private organizations around the world. In this case, the user himself will decide what information to provide on request to one or another institution. Wanted to take a loan from City Bank? Instead of a bunch of tedious certificates, you simply give access to that part of your Digipass, which contains a credit history. Do you buy medical insurance? Share your agent's access to the part of the repository where your health information is. The decentralization of the repository ensures the independence of the safety of your information on the state of the Polybius Bank project itself.

MacBANG journal notepad! in your evernote



Many of us like to use Evernote for storing all sorts of notes, records, articles, documents, video-audio materials and so on. This is very convenient, since Evernote stores and opens access to your notes from the cloud, respectively, they are available to you always and everywhere. Access can be obtained in the browser, as well as in desktop or mobile clients. Specifically, it does not make sense to describe all the positive aspects of Evernote in this article, the service has been operating for many years, many people know and work with Evernote every day.

But not many people know that Evernote allows not only to keep their records, but also to get access to notebooks of other users of this service. The idea is as follows: a user who wants to share one of his Evernote notebooks, makes this notebook shared and makes it available to other users via a link. The link can be formed for any notebook in the browser or in the desktop version of the client. After receiving such a link, you need to open it in a browser and add a shared notebook to your Evernote. Thus, each person can share his notebooks with other users of the Evernote service.



There are a lot of such notebooks in the network. Some notebooks are kept professionally and constantly updated, and some exist on pure altruism. Here the situation resembles the situation with blogs or websites. Anyway, this great feature of the Evernote service will allow you to read records of interesting people or companies. In this case, the main thing is that by subscribing to such a general notebook, you do not reduce your space quota allocated by Evernote at your disposal. The connected shared notebook is available for you to read in full, but the owner of this notebook is responsible for spending the space quota. This may be especially true for users of the free version of Evernote. After all, not everyone needs a premium account, many lack a monthly 60 MB and even still remains, Evernote itself is good, which gives us the right to choose.

Space

In the past few years, the space industry has seen a turnaround related to the first successes of private astronautics: private rocket launches have already been made cheaper than traditional launch vehicles, and serious discussions have begun on the prospects for the colonization of Mars.
In addition, the commercialization of space is increasing, and this will open up broad opportunities for the creation of a private business - for example, in the service sector of satellite launches and probes. Professions that will appear after 2020
The architect of the life cycle of space structures. Specialist in the design of structures in open space (near-earth stations and stations on the Moon and asteroids).
The most important key skill for this profession (as well as for any other profession of Atalas, where there is the word ‚Äúarchitect‚Äù) is the ability for a systemic vision of the process, retaining the framework of the life cycle of the designed object.



Manager of cosmotourism. Specialist who develops programs to visit the near-cosmic space, and later - orbital complexes and other space structures (including lunar bases).
According to forecasts, over the next 20 years, cosmotourism can become one of the most promising areas for the development of the space industry - here it is both popularization of direction and money. Today, the only object used for space tourism is the ISS, it has already been visited by 7 people (one of them twice). On the one hand, the demand for the service is constantly growing, on the other - there is a trend to reduce the price (which today is prohibitive for most 20-40 million dollars), so there is hope for the best.

Space Engineer. Specialist serving the near-Earth transport network and responsible for developing transport corridors (both flights to orbit and transcontinental flights along ballistic trajectories) and synchronization of launches / launches on Earth (with an increase in the number of launches, taking into account the multiple increase in the number of objects in orbit ).
So far, such specialists are not needed, since over the past 10 years the total number of space launches has consistently kept at 75-85 per year. However, interest in space from private companies (such as SpaceX, Virgin Galactic, Golden Spike) is growing. And in December 2013, SpaceX managed to launch a heavy satellite into orbit cheaper than the Russian Proton (about $ 60 million against $ 80 million). After this launch, SpaceX announced that it already has 50 launch orders, so there is every chance The situation with the number of launches will change dramatically in the next 10 years. So, you need specialists who will be engaged in servicing this network.

Cosmobiologist. Specialist investigating the behavior of different biological systems (from viruses to animals and humans) in space (in buildings, aircraft, at planetary stations), studying the physiology and gene changes of organisms, developing sustainable space ecosystems for orbital stations, lunar bases and long flights .

Already today, similar research is being conducted in some quantity - let's say, in February of this year, a container with goldfish, mosquito larvae and worms was sent to the ISS to study the embryonic development of organisms. NASA is also engaged in similar experiments: an experiment has been announced to study the effects on irradiation of fibroblasts under weightless conditions .

At least two companies have already declared their goal of industrial development of asteroids - this is Deep Space Industries and Planetary Resources . Of course, for the time being, the talk is that industrial development will not start tomorrow, but companies are working on it. System engineer of composite materials. Specialist in replacing traditional solutions when choosing materials for composite materials in construction, engineering and robotics, medicine, etc.
New composite materials and nanotechnological solutions set completely different standards for the quality characteristics of raw materials and structures and components. The key advantages of composite materials over the old raw material base are:
- high strength of structures and elements while reducing weight
- polymorphism and adaptability of raw materials
- resistance to critical conditions
The old resource base is inferior in all qualitative and quantitative indicators, but there is the difficulty of re-equipping all industries for new materials and designs from new materials. Thus, there is a need for specialists who will be engaged in replacing the technologies used with new ones.

It is logical that in addition to the adaptation of technology, people who will develop new materials are needed. It is important that the Atlas emphasizes the need to predict materials throughout the entire life cycle, which implies serious modeling skills.

Ecology

In the coming decades, environmental monitoring and the introduction of more environmentally friendly practices will become a mandatory part of work in all sectors of industry and transport. In the coming years, the main task of ecologists is to develop environmental awareness in the society - to support those who care for the environment, and to disseminate best practices.
Accordingly, in this sector, the main role will be played by the control of environmental changes, the development of recycling technologies in most industries and the planning of projects and programs taking into account full-cycle technologies (from product design to its use and recycling or disposal).

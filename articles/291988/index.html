AFK Sistema may sell IT-integrator NVision to MTS operator

MTS is negotiating with AFK Sistema on the purchase of the NVision Group IT integrator. This is due to the redistribution in the income structure of NVision.

Until 2013, the company was controlled by Rostelecom . Further, from 2013 to 2015, the shares of NVision were bought in parts by Sistema. First, she bought 50% of the shares of the integrator from Rostelecom. In October 2013, the chairman of the board of directors of the Tsargrad group of companies, Konstantin Malofeev, bought out a 25% stake in the integrator from its founders, Alexey Glotov, Dmitry Taraba, and Anton Sushkevich. In 2014, Sistema bought 38.75% of the shares from Malofeev and the founders of NVision, increasing its stake to 88.75%. In January 2015, Sistema brought its share to 100%. After the transaction, NVision received a valuation of $ 88 million.

It is worth noting that Sistema also owns 51.46% of MTS.

In the period from 2009 to 2012, NVision mainly worked with the Rostelecom company. However, in 2013, the share of orders from the operator decreased to 32%, and then to 25.7%.

The integrator developed a billing system for MTS. In 2012, NVision's revenue from MTS orders reached 14 billion rubles, which accounted for 24% of the total integrator's revenue. In 2013, this share rose to 38%, CNews reports .

The volume of annual payments to the integrator MTS for its work amounted to $ 75 - 350 million. Based on this, analysts Renaissance Capital , suggested that MTS could acquire NVision for no more than 20 billion rubles.

In March, the former head of the MTS branch in the Urals, Sergey Kuzmin, became the president of the NVision Group. He replaced Vyacheslav Smirnov in this post, Comnews reported . In the new position, Sergey Kuzmin will be responsible for improving the operating and financial performance of the company, developing IT services and services in the priority markets for the company.

Last year, NVision Group introduced a new strategy: in two to four years, the company planned to consolidate the IT assets of Sistema. As a result, an increase in the share of intellectual services and own development in the integrator's income structure was assumed. In addition, NVision planned to build its own data center to deploy cloud services and create a new business line.

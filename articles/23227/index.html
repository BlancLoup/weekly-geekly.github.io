Poor Americans will be given a thick channel

Even low-income citizens should not be left without basic necessities: water, food, sewage and broadband Internet. It was precisely this logic that was followed by the non-profit organization One Economy , which launched a project of charitable assistance to low-income blacks and Latinos.

As part of the program, it is planned until 2010 to connect half a million of the poorest American families to the high-speed Internet. These people are still forced to go online via dial-up or GPRS, which greatly reduces their quality of life, limits the opportunities for education, job search, and communication.

The training of illiterate Americans will be engaged in 5,000 volunteers from among the advanced teenagers. They are ready to provide their help for free, out of compassion.

Steps engineer. When the question "What's next?"

On Habr√© with an enviable consistency flashed publications about the stages of development of programmers. Sometimes serious, sometimes humorous.
Comprehending them with an eye to the picture as a whole, I came to the conclusion that this is a very special case, and any good (or not good - depends on his ability to self-criticism) a specialist rises by several steps.
The number of these steps and their height are determined by many parameters: place of work, attitude to this place and to the profession as a whole, from personal qualities, from the city and a lot more.

For me personally, a high-quality leap was a job change. And the authorities are the most important factor.



Inception


If not to consider seven years of work as non-core: a berry collector, a loader, a postman, a seller, a freight forwarder, a storekeeper, a teacher - then my journey began not so long ago. I studied at a mathematician-programmer and, as usual among students, did not realize who I would be after graduation. But I certainly did not expect to be an administrator. At that time, I still did not understand how far my programming in Delph hydrodynamics problems was from reality. Already closer to the fifth course, the words linux and cisco appeared over the horizon, The fact that they concealed themselves beneath themselves, but still did not loom as a prospect.
By a strange coincidence, in 2008 at the 5th year I got into the technical support of a local provider - for me it was a temporary part-time job, because at that moment I was sick with three-dimensional modeling and planned to become a three-dimensional designer. Therefore, the attitude to the work was appropriate - I did not even know the IP address of my computer.
The technologies used were very heterogeneous: optics, RRL, Wi-Fi, CDMA and even modem pools were. Provided Internet, IP-telephony, hosting and email.



The work does not hit the dead: once in 20 minutes, view the status of hosts in Nagios, once per hour load interfaces in the MRTG and answer calls of subscribers. The most difficult thing that I had to decide was to teach the user how to drive the Dial-Up settings. For all other applications, you need to collect information and transfer to Admins. Then they were just Admins for me - just omnipotent gods, not people. I looked with admiration as these people were picking at the racks and doing something from their workplace on the pieces of iron that were hundreds of kilometers away. The console, vlan, tsisko, patchcord - all this sounded to me like spells that made the network equipment come to life.
With my mouth open, I watched as someone called Radmin on any computer in a neighboring city or a couple of teams restored radio communication on a fifty-kilometer RRL span, which for me was also something mysterious - I imagined such big clicking relays in plastic boxes. In all this was romance.
The most interesting thing is that in more than half a year of work, I really did not even understand the meaning of the word IP-address. I did not even get acquainted with the network scheme. But I found out who the users are, what they need and how to talk to them. Generally, tech support is a great anti-stress school. In addition to this good lesson, the work attracted me to the IT world - it fulfilled its main function.
A few good people left their mark on my telephone directory.
Then suddenly, one Admin told me that someday I will also become beautiful and smart, they say, I have the potential. It seems to be just words, but they sunk into my heart and now I could not do otherwise.
Each person should have such an authority, whose word can determine an important decision. If a person says that he does not have authority, then, most likely, he is deceiving or just not far away.

At the new place of work, I was dragged out of the green telecommunications cycle. And then either the pan or lost: or during the trial period, I penetrate into the spirit of cisco to the fullest extent or to the same extent enjoy the search for a new job. Chose the first - not lost.

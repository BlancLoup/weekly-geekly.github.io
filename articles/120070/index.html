Industrialization



The following information should be attributed, first of all, to William Goode (William Goode), a pioneer in the study of the sociology of industrial and post-industrial society. Alas, I am familiar with his works exclusively in the style ‚ÄúIzzy Sang‚Äù from retelling and quoting (another heartfelt greetings to the masters of the copywriters).

For several millennia, mankind has formed a variety of social structures, due to both objective geopolitical reasons, and a variety of random fluctuations. However, some moments remained universal: as a rule, the social unit was a ‚Äúbig‚Äù family (several generations living together), whose occupation has remained unchanged for decades . The children of the nobles were noblemen, the children of the kshatriyas were the kshatriyas, the children of the peasants were peasants. This principle did not exclude, of course, social mobility, but, in general, the occupation of a son was 99% determined by the occupation of his father. Special vocational training could take many years, but at the same time the volume of special knowledge transferred did not change much from generation to generation. The time frame for technology change far exceeded the generation change period .

The role of traditions and morals in such a society is absolutely clear: they ensure the continuity of generations, the inviolability of the social structure. Other things being equal, a more holistic society has an advantage. See on this for a wonderful study of religious communities .

The capitalist way of managing at first had no effect on this principle. However, the seeds of rationalism sown by the Reformation ultimately brought abundant seedlings. Lutheranism and Calvinism - extremely totalitarian in their warehouse religious trends - nevertheless contained a rational grain: your Salvation is completely determined by your success in business, by your everyday work. (For details, see Max Weber, "The Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism .") Protestantism inclined people to actively seek ways to do their job better. Technical progress began to accelerate slowly, and entrepreneurs began to act faster and more decisively. The pace of life has accelerated, and this is where ‚Äúindustrialization‚Äù began.

I deliberately do not associate industrialization with factories, railways, and other attributes traditionally attributed to it. In our context, the social aspects of industrialization are much more important.

1. Industrialization demanded mobility ; New plants were created quickly, and closed just as quickly. A professional worker had to be ready at any moment to withdraw from his familiar place and rush to the other end of the country. Industrialization demanded not only geographical, but also social mobility, creating a class of hired managers - and the manager here became not only for parental merit, but also rising from the position of line staff. Thus, the social status achieved by an individual personally in his professional field of activity has become increasingly important.

2. Industrialization demanded more and more professional specialization . The more difficult the production became, the more new professions appeared.

What happened? For the first time in human history, the continuation of parental affairs has ceased to be massively a profitable strategy ; on the contrary, those who, using their own mind and intuition, benefited new professions, moved to a better life, opened a new business, benefited.

First, the conflict of ‚Äúfathers and children‚Äù (‚ÄúFathers and children‚Äù of Turgenev, which is characteristic, was written just at that time and fully reflects the above-mentioned problems), which ended in favor of the children , arose (or, rather, escalated) . The ‚ÄúBig Family‚Äù fell apart in favor of the nuclear family: husband, wife, and their minor children. Care at a young age from under the parental wing became the norm, the concept of ‚Äúdowry‚Äù disappeared.

More importantly, the concept of the importance of choosing one‚Äôs own life path has spread widely in Western society. Personal, acquired social status, readiness to accept changes and use them - these are the most important features of a person of the industrial age . Of course, the new way of life was not limited to the purely professional sphere, and the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe importance of making decisions independently and living with your mind spread to all areas of human activity. In particular, like the freedom to choose the occupation and place of residence, the freedom to choose a couple has become stronger; Romantic love in public consciousness has become a key condition for a happy marriage.

The ideal of the family of the industrialization era looked like this: the husband works in large-scale production, the loving wife is engaged in housekeeping and raising children; they live in their own home and look to the future with hope. In general, this ideal has been fully preserved in our days, although its positions have shaken considerably.

Education



Primary school education was widespread in Europe as early as the 18th century, and by the end of the 19th it was almost 100% - which, of course, played a significant role in the rapid process of industrialization. With the deepening division of labor, the emergence of new professions and technologies, the demands on education have also increased. In the 20th century, higher education became the norm, it became very difficult to qualify for high-paying, qualified work without completed higher education. Accordingly, the term of training has also grown, the age of admission to work has been postponed.

Let us now try to sum up the contents of the three previous sections.
The post-industrial society is characterized by:
1. The approval of the concept of the exceptional importance of their own choice and the achieved social status;
2. The development of the non-productive sphere of the economy and the wide involvement of women in economic processes;
3. The development of urban infrastructure to a level that allows you to comfortably and alone conduct household households without much time and money;
4. Extension of the study period to 15 years or more.

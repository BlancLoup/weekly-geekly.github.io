Hacker arrested trying to sell stolen scripts of popular TV shows



This week in New York, the hacker Alonzo Knowles was arrested trying to sell the personal data of Hollywood stars and famous athletes to a radio show host and a collection of scripts for movies and TV series that have not yet been released. 23-year-old Knowles, who hails from the Bahamas, received all this information through hacking into accounts of stars and people close to them.

Judging by the available information about this case, Knowles calculated people familiar and close to stars, then with the help of social engineering and phishing attacks got access to their computers and accounts in various services. He combed computers and accounts for sensitive information, after which he used them as a means to access the accounts of the stars themselves.

Receiving messages allegedly from their friends, Hollywood actors and actresses easily succumbed to deception, with the result that their personal information also fell within the hacker's reach.

According to some information, Knowles got access to several scenarios of films and episodes of serials, in which these actors should be shot or already taken, as well as to their private photos, videos and various personal information.

The investigation does not disclose information about which actors and athletes were the victims of the hacker, and the scenarios of which series he seized. The host of the show contacted the producer of the "popular television series", and he turned to the competent authorities. While working undercover, an employee of the US Interior Ministry made an appointment with Knowles and the terms of the deal - selling 15 scenarios and four social security numbers owned by hacked victims.

Acer Hornet Nettop may be the first implementation of the NVIDIA Ion platform

Already on April 8, sales of Acer Hornet compact media center based on the expected NVIDIA Ion platform will begin in China. Moreover, in terms of functionality, this device can also be attributed to low-cost NTRS systems. Acer Hornet can easily handle FullHD 1080p video playback and offer customers a number of interesting, active entertainment in the manner of a Nintendo Wii game console.


The Hornet will be equipped with an Intel Atom N230 processor, one or two gigabytes of memory, and a hard disk of 80 or 160 GB.

A Wi-Fi module, a webcam will be options. Depending on the configuration, users will receive a wired or wireless keyboard and mouse.

The youngest Acer Hornet model will receive an Intel Atom N230 processor, 1 GB RAM and 80 GB hard drive, as well as a preinstalled Windows Vista Home Basic operating system. The older version will be equipped with two gigabytes of RAM, a 160 GB hard drive and the Windows Vista Home Premium software platform, as well as the Boxshell instant boot operating system.

Nettop Acer Hornet will be made in a stylish diamond-shaped case. As expected, it will be quite quiet device with a noise level of less than 26 decibels. Its cost is projected to range from $ 149 to $ 299, depending on the configuration.

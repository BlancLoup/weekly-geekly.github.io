Hardware is less hard with accelerator



Habrahabr, hello! Almost a year ago I wrote about my Petcube project - a pet gadget. Much time has passed since then, we have advanced in development, received a patent , announced a product in the Western market and received hundreds of publications, in particular, in Wired, NBCNews, CNet and other large media. In total, the project spent about $ 40k from the founders' own savings. This summer, we took third-party investments and decided to participate in the HAXLR8R hardware accelerator program , which is located in the Chinese city of Shenzhen - the world capital of electronics.

Disclaimer: All of the following is based on my personal experience. All the conclusions I made are subjective and may differ from the judgments of other people.

In this publication, I want to share with you the experience of participation in the accelerator hardware startups. I hope it will be useful for those who are just going or are already creating smart, good gadgets that will make our life more interesting and better. As Jobs used to say in one of the ancient interviews: ‚ÄúOnce you discover what you are around, you were not smarter than you. You can create your own products.

1. What is the hardware accelerator, what are the accelerators



A startup accelerator is an intensive program for a start-up business whose task is to support and provide the start-up team with the necessary resources: money, office, mentors, connections and recommendations, investors - with the goal of speedy product launch into the market.

In nature, there are software and hardware accelerators. The latter is based on the creation of a physical product, a certain device, a gadget. We will talk about this in more detail. In my opinion, the most interesting accelerators of "iron" in the world are:

Bolt.io ( http://bolt.io ) from Boston, USA. The amount of investment and the proportion of the company with which to part, are discussed individually. Usually, about $ 50k for 15% of the company. The program lasts almost 6 months and offers:

+ 24/7 access to the office and workplaces for prototyping;
+ Cooperation with Dragon Innovation, which can later become your product designers, as well as help to negotiate with CM (Contract Manufacturer);
+ Mentors.

Lemnos lab ( http://lemnoslabs.com/ ) - San Francisco, California. The program period is 6-9 months.

+ 24/7 office;
+ Legal services, company registration, bank account, (very convenient for those planning a Kickstarter campaign and is not a US citizen);
+ Access to suppliers and manufacturers;
+ Mentors.

There is, however, a small minus, the accelerator does not help teams in obtaining visas in the United States. However, a tourist visa is always a good solution in this situation.

Haxlr8r ( http://www.haxlr8r.com ) - China, Shenzhen, 111 days program. After completing a two-week demo-day in San Francisco. 25-50k for 5-15% of the company.

+ 24/7 access to the office, all the necessary tools for prototyping;
+ Convenient location - quick access to factories and electronics suppliers;
+ Mentors;

To apply for the program, just fill in the form: www.haxlr8r.com/apply and send a link to the five-minute video pitch of your project.

Submission of applications for the next set will last until 11/15/2013.

2. What is happening here, teams, mentors



The process is divided into three stages. The first month is an acquaintance with China, factories, mentors, other teams and a lot of development development opportunities. The second month is a full focus on work, prototyping and getting a workable product. The third month is cooperation with factories, organization of mass production.

Behind the first month of work and while we are satisfied. Mentors and program directors are very helpful with tips and contacts. Proximity to factories and electronics markets makes the whole process much easier and faster. All 10 teams help each other with their unique skills, the atmosphere in the office disposes to intensive work, the working day usually lasts 12-14 hours and passes almost unnoticed.

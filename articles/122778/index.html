Appearance


So, I got a Fidelio docking station from Philips. A nice device made with "zakos" under the Mac. White, smooth, very pleasant to the touch. In form it resembles a half of a huge egg, more precisely this form is difficult to describe.



There are speakers hidden in front of the fabric, as well as a place for an iPod or iPhone. The material is gray, the touch is pleasant and rough.



In a kind of niche is a connector for devices. He leans forward so that it is convenient to insert the device, and then again tilt it so that it stands slightly bent.



And also a rubber strip so that your device is tightly standing in this station.



Under the platform is a metal plate with the logo of the company, which is nothing more than a rocker button to change the volume. Functionality and sensations


So, this docking station is needed for several functions.
  1. IPod / iPhone audio playback
  2. Play sound from any other device
  3. IPod / iPhone charging
  4. Stylish stand


Playing iPod is easy. You insert the player on a special platform, which has a connector and that's it. From now on, you can choose music or movies and listen to the sound through the docking station. In principle, reproduction from any audio device is also simple. Only for this you need to connect the device to the 3.5mm audio cable dock. In essence, the station will simply act as speakers.

As soon as you insert an iPod or iPhone into the port, provided that the station is connected to the computer, iTunes starts and your player or phone is determined as if it were connected with a regular cable. You can download music to it, synchronize it and, of course, it charges.

But there is an interesting feature in this device. It can work autonomously. To do this, a compartment for finger batteries x4 is made at the bottom of the corpus. Thus, you can listen to your player even in the forest, on the beach, in general, wherever there is no outlet.



Batteries, unfortunately, are not included. :(

"Electronic bracelets" legalized in Russia

On April 15, 2010, a government decree comes into force approving a list of technical devices that can be used to monitor convicts. Today is the official day of the legalization of "electronic bracelets", special devices that are worn on the leg of a person under house arrest.

This is how these devices look , which have been tested in Russia for a year and a half.

The device can be of three types: 1) complete with a base radio station that receives a signal from the bracelet in a limited radius (usually 100-150 meters), and in case of loss of communication, ‚Äúsignals‚Äù to the police; 2) complete with a mobile base station, which tracks its location by satellite coordinates and does not allow the bracelet to retire to the same 100-150 meters (you can go to work); 3) with a satellite navigation module mounted directly into the bracelet (this is the most expensive option).

Israeli products of this kind, which Russia purchased for testing, cost from 1,000 to 2,500 euros. Russian counterparts will be several times cheaper. Domestic "bracelets" will work exclusively through the GLONASS system.

The Penitentiary Service of the Russian Federation announced the massive introduction of "electronic bracelets" starting next year. According to the plan of the FPS, every third convict in Russia in 2011 will receive an electronic bracelet and will be placed under house arrest, not in prison, as it is now.

The massive introduction of new devices will allow several times to reduce the cost of maintaining prisoners, improve conditions in Russian prisons and allow many people convicted of frivolous crimes to serve time in relatively comfortable home conditions.

No type III civilizations found in the observable universe.



Sensitive telescopes of the new generation allow astronomers to measure the level of background infrared radiation, which should be an indicator of type III civilization on the Kardashev scale . The power consumption of such a civilization is comparable to the power of the galaxy, about 4 ¬∑ 10 37 watts. In theory, it should glow brightly in the middle of the infrared part of the spectrum, emitting a large amount of waste heat.

Earlier, a group of astronomers led by Dr. Jason Wright from the University of Pennsylvania (USA) processed a list of 100,000 objects and compiled a list of several hundred galaxies with an unusually high level of radiation in the middle of the infrared spectrum.

It was assumed that these galaxies can still be home to advanced civilizations. The only problem is that a similar spectrum is also inherent in natural astrophysical processes, namely, thermal radiation from the "warm" cosmic dust.

Professor Michael Garrett from Leiden University (Netherlands) decided to test this theory and studied about 50 galaxies from the list not only in the infrared spectrum, but also in other radio bands. The results were disappointing: in his opinion, in the absolute majority of them, the high level of IR radiation is best explained by natural astrophysical processes. In particular, they correspond to the well-known correlation rule of mid-infrared radio correlation. The detected radio emission corresponds to what should be in the presence of a given level of average IR, based on the known correlation.

Amazon introduced its Kindle Fire tablet.



Rumors of a new Android tablet from Amazon have been around for a long time. Even before the launch of today's presentation, Bloomberg announced that the tablet, called Kindle Fire, will cost $ 199, will be 7-inch, with Wi-Fi support, but without 3G, and without a camera and microphone.

During the presentation of Amazon CEO Jeff Bezos, he added that the device will go on sale on November 15 and that it contains a dual-core processor and a multi-touch IPS display, weighs 414 grams and includes the new Amazon Silk browser. Content can be synchronized using the same Whispersync technology used on other Kindle devices.

In addition, the company will offer users of the device a 30-day free period to test the Amazon Prime streaming video service, a subscription for which costs $ 79 per year.

In a separate interview with Bloomberg, Jeff Bezos said that the most important difference between Amazon in the tablet market is price.

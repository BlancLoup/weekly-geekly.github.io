Power supply

Electricity from the city network to NORD is supplied via two independent beams, that is, the power supply of the data center is reserved according to the 2N scheme. If a failure occurs on at least one beam, the data center automatically switches to power supply using 9 diesel generator sets (DGS). While DGS are included in the work, the power supply in the data center is carried out using uninterruptible power supplies (UPS).
Here is our "outlet" right next to it.


DGU oil and antifreeze are constantly in a heated state, thanks to which diesel engines are constantly ready to start and go into operation in less than a minute. The NORD data center uses FG Wilson DGUs in container design. NORD 1 is served by 4 DGS, NORD 2 is three, NORD 3 is two. In the near future, NORD 2 and NORD 3 will have two more diesel generator sets. All DGUs are reserved under the N + 1 scheme.


DGU fuel tanks are designed for 6 hours of work, and existing contracts with contractors for prompt delivery of diesel fuel guarantee an indefinitely long work of the data center, even in case of a long-term failure of the city power grid.


While the DGS are turned on, the power supply of the entire data center is kept on the UPS. All NORD enclosures are equipped with UPS made by APC MGE Galaxy 7000 series. Their capacity is designed to power the entire data center for 10 minutes. This time is more than enough for the plant DGU. UPSs are reserved under the 2N scheme. Three lines of batteries are connected to each UPS.

MID stands for Mobile Internet Device, i.e. in fact, any mobile device with an operating system capable of accessing the Internet. A great example of devices of this class is the hero of this review, Viliv S5 .



I take out wide leg ...;)

The device is far from new - its announcements were online last year, if not earlier ... but here the wave of the novelty did not reach our cold edges. Actually, there is no wave now - the device can only be ordered. But why not review such an overseas thing, especially if it is so interesting?

The basis was a single-core Intel Atom Silverthorne Z520 processor with a clock frequency of 1.33GHz, which was earned on the SCH US15W chipset with hardware accelerated H.264, MPEG2, VC1, and WMV9. Added a bit of DDR2 memory.



Given the class of the device (MID - Mobile Internet Device), it can be assumed that further work was done on the communication component. That's right - the processor was hung with all sorts of modules, such as GPS, HSDPA / WiMAX, Wi-Fi and Bluetooth. And in order to justify the mobile destination, they added a solid-state drive and placed it all in a compact package. As you can see, the recipe for success was simple;)

Inside the oblong white box was:



- Pocket computer Viliv S5 ;
- Battery;
- Stylus;

- Instruction;

Instructions black and white, thick and with pictures. The cover is also black and white, but already made of woven fabric. Inside the cover is a nice suede firmware, in which the device will feel more than comfortable.



It is a pity only that there is nothing on the case for which it could be attached to the belt.

Turn on and work


In full alert the device comes in 25 seconds. Immediately after loading the operating system, the CubeUI shell is loaded - a very interesting development.



Imagine - there are 5 thematic sections (Entertainment, Internet, Navigation, etc.) and each of these topics has a cube consisting of balls (9 balls on each face). Each ball can bind 1 label, respectively, up to 36 programs can be placed in each section. 36 * 5 = 180 - just as many shortcuts can be conveniently placed on an element of the screen, which does not take up too much space. Naturally, everything is supported by nice effects of transitions and rotations, and on the screen there is always order! Although nothing prevents to turn off this shell (remove from autoload) or place this very cube on a regular desktop.

In the same shell is a calendar and weather widget. Additionally, there is another taskbar (above), which displays the time, date, battery charge and status of all wireless interfaces.

Otherwise, the user is waiting for the most ordinary Windows XP, to which for many years everyone is so used to. Upgrading to Win7 is possible - the official website of the product looms about it. And on the download page already provided all the necessary drivers.



At the same time pressing the Menu and volume rocker, you can change the brightness of the screen - you rarely find such hardware forethought.

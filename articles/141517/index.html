Anonymous hackers claim to have hacked hundreds of sites in China



Anonymous hackers claim to have hacked about 500 websites in China. Among them are the pages of government structures, official institutions, and trade groups.

The message, which appeared on the hacked sites, contained information that this attack is a protest against the strict control of citizens' lives by the Chinese government and a warning that the regime‚Äôs sites are broken today - and tomorrow it will fall.

The message also called for the Chinese to join the Anonymous group and organize their own protests against their government.

The announcement of hacking was posted on the page Anonymous China , which was created in March of this year. And on the site Pastebin posted a list of 485 hacked sites . The keys and details of hacking can be found on the links on Twitter.

On hacked sites, hackers not only posted messages, but also left links to pages with information on how to circumvent censorship on the Internet. Most of them are in English.

Official confirmation of information about hacking from the Chinese authorities have been reported. On the contrary, Beijing denies this.

However, most of the sites that Anonymous hackers indicated in the list are now unavailable or send users to a broken page.

There is also information that Anonymous managed to break some sites several times: a successful attack was repeated after the administrators restored the site.

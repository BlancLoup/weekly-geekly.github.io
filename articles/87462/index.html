Comparing video quality via HTML5 and Flash using YouTube as an example using Chrome 5.0.342.3 dev and Win 7 browser

A small study does not in any way affect the greatness of HTML5 technology in general, but only its specific implementation on the above entities.



The title is altered by numerous requests from users, who saw in this article an attack on their sacred. Thank you for your sacred geek feelings! The IT industry is developed by people like you. Motherland as a whole, like me in particular, trembling in front of your sharp mind and attention!

In fact, the headline is more important than the article! Specification is more important than implementation! And in general, the user does not care to walk the paths of harsh geeks. Your comments led to a deep reconsideration of my whole previous life. Thank you friends!

Now I clearly understand that my initial note in my personal blog was extremely illiterate and did not comply with your strict rules at all. Thank you for helping to bring it in line with your bright feelings. I am so grateful!


I always use the latest browser models, so when YouTube suggested trying Html5 I happily agreed. But later he returned to the flash again because of the poor quality of the video display.

I do not know why this is happening, but the fact is a fact: through the flash video looks much better. Tried on Safari - all the same. Below are some screenshots. Perhaps the picture looks different on the Macs.

Experimental conditions: Win 7, Chrome 5.0.342.3 dev browser, video used in the example , switch page on HTML5 and back to flash player.

Besides:


- Html5 really loads less processor. And decently less.
- It is a little less convenient to manage. On the same Youtube, a very convenient flash player, with a single click on the image, the pause / play works, it is visualized with a beautiful effect, with a double click it opens to the whole window - as it should.

On the HTML5 player, these conveniences are not there, there is a double click on the play / pause, which is not so convenient, but with full-screen video, it is still quite difficult. Although it is possible this is temporary and maybe it can be nastrit somewhere (or there will be such an opportunity in the future).

- On Html5 clip loading bar does not work. Or it works like I do not understand yet. On the flash you can immediately see that the download has started and you can see how much is loaded. On Html5, the download bar shows a full download right away, but it doesn't happen that way. I do not know what to do with this either.

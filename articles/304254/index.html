Examinations for admission to the "free" Master in the US

Good day, dear habrachane!

In continuation of the topic of admission to the magistracy in the United States , I will talk about the necessary exams.

Anyone wishing to enroll in a magistracy in the United States is obliged to confirm a sufficient level of English for learning ( IELTS / TOEFL ) and a general level of training ( GRE / GMAT ). There are exceptions for the language exam, when a person has already studied in an American university or school, but for such people this branch is not even needed.

So, I recommend to take these exams in turn: IELTS / TOEFL ==> GRE / GMAT. Because the second exam is more difficult, and successful passing the second exam requires at least upper-intermediate.

What all the same to pass IELTS or TOEFL? To begin with, let's designate that 99% of American universities accept IELTS . And the minimum threshold is on average 6.5 points in general, and at least 6.0 in each section. Ivy League accepts a 7.0 overall and higher, while for medium and 5.5 it is enough, although such universities will not provide you with full funding. But they will be pleased with a reasonable price and discounts on training. Personally, I myself passed IELTS and it was necessary to type 6.5 overall. Remember in the previous post I once said that all the cards made it so that I entered exactly Baylor? So, this is the only university that has not set a minimum threshold for sections (in all other universities in each section it is allowed at 0.5 less than the overall). Better talk about General. This is an online test for assessing the readiness to study for a master's degree, namely, mathematical and analytical skills are being tested. This is what most programs require. And it consists of three parts: Verbal Reasoning, Quantitative Reasoning, Analytical Writing.

Quantitative Reasoning. This part is the most common mathematics with unusual problem statements and answer choices. Therefore, you need to practice a little, to adapt. Analytical Writing. As well as in IELTS, consists of two essays. However, everything is much more complicated = (

Indicate where the assumptions were true / wrong, what statements can be disputed and so on. It is recommended to write an essay of 500-800 words in 30 minutes !!! Well at least the whole thing is typed on the keyboard.

The second part, as in IELTS, is a discussion on a given topic. True topics are more serious, requiring understanding or having working experience. I already forgot my theme =) I remember that it was connected with top-level management.

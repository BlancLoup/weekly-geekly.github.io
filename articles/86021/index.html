LED gift for March 8

Greetings to all. Not so long ago, my wife and I had the first wedding anniversary and I made her the first gift with my own hands. I decided to share the idea and its implementation, there is still time until March 8 :)
I will not describe the whole evolution and the cause-and-effect part of my decision, but it turned out just that way.

For its production, a prototyping board was required (in our backwater, the largest board was 130x170, made on a hot-wire, which is not ice), 8 mm LEDs on the outer contour and for "inscriptions", everything else from the NM5050 set is a New Year star . If there is a programmer for the same Atmel attiny13, then the cost will be lower ( firmware ). Inclusion scheme:

R4 and R5 were removed from it, R1 is selected individually, depending on the number of LEDs in the chain, it is convenient to carry out the calculation here . The voltage regulator LM7805 provides 5V, the diode VD1 serves to protect against polarity reversal, capacitors as power filters. The LEDs are included in the chains in series of 4 pieces, the chains are connected in parallel and connected to the keys of transistors Q1 and Q2. I made the power supply from the D-Link PSU (7.5V 1A, but gave out 10.4 V), you can make a mobile version on the crown. The ideal solution for the case would be a box with tinted glass, so that only the light of the diodes was visible (the idea was lumenous ), but I limited myself to the 13x18 photo frame (liquid nails to help). Video how it works:

Instead of a layout, it will be more technological to make a piece of PCB by spreading the board in advance, but this is if there is a drill, otherwise there will be pornography on the back side. If you choose exactly the layout, I advise you to think IMMEDIATELY how everything will be turned on and DIRECTLY attach the LED and solder all the wires to its legs. I first soldered all the elements, then I connected the LEDs into chains of 4 pieces each, at the ends of which there were unclosed wires, which I then connected together, in the end it turned out not quite neatly.

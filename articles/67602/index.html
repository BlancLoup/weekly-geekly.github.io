Your life makes sense. Seth Godin

This post is a translation of a note by You Matter by famous blogger Seth Godin.
In my opinion in English it all sounds more succinctly and beautifully. I tried to convey the meaning of the statements (as I myself understand and accept them), and not to make a literal translation.

Your life makes sense.
  • When you love your work and those with whom you work, your life makes sense.
  • When you are merciful, generous and attentive to others more than to yourself, your life makes sense.
  • When you leave this world, making it better, your life makes sense.
  • When you raise the bar for the quality of what and how you do, your life makes sense.
  • When you teach and forgive, and again teach, instead of condemning and humiliating, your life has meaning.
  • When you influence surrounding people with your actions and words, your life makes sense.
  • When children grow up and dream of being the same as you, your life makes sense.
  • When you see the real world, but you change it for the better, your life makes sense.
  • It doesn't matter if you gave inspiration to a Nobel laureate or a street wanderer, your life makes sense.
  • If the room is filled with an invisible radiance, when you enter, your life makes sense.
  • And when the legacy left by you does not die but continues to live, hours, days or eternity, your life has a meaning.


Does your life make sense?

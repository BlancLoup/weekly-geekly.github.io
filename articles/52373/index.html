Of course, to say that WAP in Russia (and other CIS countries) appeared suddenly from the bay is not worth it - the first WAP (and then WAP 1.0) sites appeared back in 1998-1999. Naturally, these were the WAP sites of mobile operators. Access to WAP was then carried out only by CSD (if you go down to extremes, it is very similar to the Dial-up standard), it‚Äôs natural that the minute of such pleasure was quite expensive, but what to say about the 20th century - advertising-prospectus the MTS JEANS tariff for 2004 promised us a minute of work on the Internet costing at least 11 cents, that is, at that time - about 198 rubles per hour.
Thus, it was the CSD prices that hampered the development of WAP: in fact, it was only oilmen from Tomsk who could use it, and the young people could not and did not want the engine of progress (and even now for such money they would not) create and develop Russian WAP.

Pv-wap development boom


The boom of WAP development in many countries appeared after the introduction of GPRS technology: now the Internet did not clog the ‚Äútelephone line‚Äù, and the data began to be transmitted in a ‚Äúpacket‚Äù mode. In 2001 in Russia, the ‚Äúbig two‚Äù (MTS, Beeline) began to introduce test (read free) GPRS, the only obstacle was that the phones themselves supporting this technology were not cheap, but still people began to assemble on the portals of operators - each had its own chats, unpretentious forums. At the same time, mobile versions of large WEB portals, such as Yandex, appeared, but they played the role of news broadcaster, essentially implementing only a minimum of WAP capabilities and not presenting any interactivity to users, and operators' WAP resources of a certain interest and a large number (by that measure) services have been closed to the outside world. People communicated, got acquainted, in time there appeared a desire to move away from services monopolized by operators.

Over time, more and more wap-site builders began to get acquainted (someone - reading the specifications, and some by trial and error) with the WML markup language and post their creations on free hosting, such as nm.ru. Over time, due to a number of reasons (for example, the blocking on VmpelCom (trademark is Beeline) on the nm.ru ip subnet), the sites began to move to all sorts of designers, but already supporting the Russian language, the first of these was the wap constructor that appeared in 2003 . wapservis.ru , unfortunately, who surrendered their positions by 2005 and conceded to the present ‚ÄúGiants‚Äù - builder.wab.ru , kmx.ru and wen.ru.

The introduction of GPRS in Russia was in the form of a wave - by entering and testing GPRS in one region, a traffic charge was introduced and time-free GPRS came to another region, repeating the experience of the previous one, for example, Beeline first introduced a tolerable subscription fee of $ 3, and later - at the end 2004 - a fee for GPRS-WAP traffic.

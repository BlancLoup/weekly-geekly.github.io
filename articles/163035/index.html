Intel Anti-Theft Technology
We now turn to the description of the functionality of Intel Anti-Theft. To use the technology, you must first register to get access to your personal account . Trial free license works for 90 days and is valid for one computer. The program can be downloaded directly from there. Download, run the installer.



Note that Intel has included Anti-Theft in the McAfee security product package. So the interface of the product is now dual.
During the installation, you will be prompted for a username and password from the personal account you created, and will also be asked to set a password to protect the ultrabook that will be entered for unlocking. The password can then be changed.



Initially, the client window looks like this. After synchronization with the cabinet, it will display the connection status and the time elapsed since the last synchronization. The client does not have any settings, all management is carried out through the web-interface of the account.



Let's go now to your personal account. If you tick, the program tries to track the location of the ultrabook and show it on the map. Accuracy is not God knows what, it is still not GPS, but the city is correct.



Settings menu. In another, additional menu, you can temporarily disable the Anti-Theft lockout (if you do not plan to turn on the ultrabook in the near future and do not want the protection to work).



As you can see, the system does not require any configuration, it works almost completely automatically. Your task is to press the big red button in time. Let's now "steal" the ultrabook - in vain we tried?



The line with the message to the habrabra has been lost among others, but still it can be customized. When you press the big red button, there are two options. The first is that the laptop is locked and turned off as soon as it receives a signal (after a minute or two). The second is that the device is not blocked, but it tracks its location and asks for the unlock password on the next reboot. The second mode is clearly for tablets with GPS / 3G, they will be able to determine their location much more accurately.

So, the theft of the ultrabook, or rather the opposition to it, was successful. Now you know how it works.

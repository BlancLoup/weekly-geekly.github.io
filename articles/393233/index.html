Simple and effective movement system in virtuality: controller in pants



In connection with the release of several virtual reality helmets at once, users and developers were actively engaged in studying the possibility of new systems. One of the big problems was the task of moving in virtual space. But programmer Ryan Sullivan [Ryan Sullivan] may have come up with a beautiful, simple - albeit an unusual solution.

If you directly transfer the position of the player's body to virtuality, then his movements there will be limited to the size of his game room. Early development tried to give devices that allowed to emulate walking and running over long distances - but they were all big, inconvenient and expensive.

Ryan developed the RIPmotion walking and shooting test program. He used a HTC Vive helmet with controllers. The easiest way was to push the controller-wand into the belt so that its round part jutted out and the system could track it.

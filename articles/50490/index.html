Briefly about the main thing in the world of subnotebooks

Obviously, netbooks are becoming more and more popular lately. Companies release a lot of clone releases with minor changes. Given the low cost and portability of surprise is difficult to call. However, the question remains: ‚ÄúHow popular are netbooks? How many people use them? ‚Äù

The guys from PriceGrabber.com will help us figure it out.


Have you already bought a netbook?




Above is the table "Which of the following technology devices do you personally have?". A netbook is considered an addition to a laptop and desktop computer, but not a replacement. Of those consumers that indicate a netbook, 91 percent also own a laptop and 87 percent a desktop PC.

The age contingent of users of netbooks (those same 10 percent) looks curious:
  • 18-24 2%
  • 25-34 20%
  • 35-44 26%
  • 45-54 27%
  • 55-64 16%
  • 65 and more than 8%

Market capture


Despite their small size netbooks move in giant strides to capture the market:

By the end of 2008, the total sales of such devices worldwide amounted to 14 million units. (back in 2007, the netbook market had less than a million devices).

But our market is dominated by two manufacturers: the company Acer (38.3%) and the ancestor of the market Asus Computers (30.3%, 1.7 million units). The main shock offer Acer is Aspire One, and for Asus it is a family of Eee PC of various configurations. The lag behind the rest of the company is extremely significant - for example, the market shares of HP and MSI are only 5.8% and 5.7%, respectively. Dell closes the top five of leaders, delivering 160 thousand of its netbooks (2.8% of the market). In the third quarter, OLPC XO sold 130,000 (2.3% of the market).

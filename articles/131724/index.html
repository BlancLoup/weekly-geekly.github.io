Ainol Novo 8 Tablet Review

I am glad to present you one of the modern representatives of the industry of tablet-making - Ainol Novo 8.



Consider what this tablet is.

Specifications


Screen: 8 inches, resolution 1280 by 768 , capacitive, multitouch (5 points)
CPU: Cortex A9 Amlogic-8726 800Mhz
GPU: Mali-400
RAM: 512 Mb DDR2
ROM: 8Gb or 16Gb + microSD slot
Wireless: 802.11b / g / n and IR receiver
Camera: yes, 0.3 MP front
Interfaces: 3.5 jack, miniUSB, full USB, HDMI, HDTV, microphone, power connector
Battery: 2000mAh x 2
Dimensions: 212.7x128.8x13.8 mm
Weight: 500 grams

The screen is bright enough. In the room, as a rule, the penultimate level of brightness is sufficient. On the street, unfortunately, only the maximum brightness saves, otherwise you can only see your reflection.

Wireless connection

Wi-fi

Works that can not but rejoice. The reception level is average, but it attaches to the router without any problems, from anywhere in the apartment.

IR receiver

Required exclusively for the operation of the remote control. The console itself works like a joystick or the same trackball.

Camera

There is, removes. Quality certainly does not shine. Typical Chinese camera, sufficient only for video.

Interfaces

3.5 jack

It works great. By the sound of no complaints. High-quality, clear and without extraneous noise.

MiniUSB

Used exclusively for connecting to a PC.

Full USB

One of the biggest advantages of the tablet. Without any adapters, the tablet easily handles flash drives, mice, keyboards, hard drives, modems, and other devices. Conveniently.

HDMI

Check unfortunately I could not, there are no target devices. Judging by the video works very well.



HDTV

Similarly. Check failed because the video component signal in YPbPr format.

Microphone

Record quality is acceptable. Recorded, at arm's length, the voice is heard quite well, and more is not needed.

Battery

Of course, this is the weakest point of the tablet. Perhaps this tablet is not suitable for those for whom a long battery life is critical.

The approximate time of the tablet for different situations:

1) Work on the Internet, the minimum screen brightness: about 6 hours.
2) Work on the Internet, 50% brightness: approximately 5 hours.
3) Work on the Internet, 100% brightness: about 4 hours.
4) Listening to music through headphones, the screen is off: more than 12 hours.
5) Video viewing, 100% brightness, sound through headphones: about 3 and a half hours.

Firmware


The latest official firmware was released by Ainol at the end of July. It was Android 2.2.1. Very stable firmware, by the way. The main innovations were support for 3g modems and the market. More updates have not been and apparently not foreseen.

At the end of August, another version of Android 2.2.1 firmware was released. It was branded beta. The firmware did not appear on the official site. The differences remained a mystery to me. On the basis of this firmware several mods were created.

Most recently, a beta version of the firmware based on Android 2.3 was found on the Chinese forums. The main change, in addition to the OS version, is overclocking the processor to 1 Ghz. There have been no reviews of the quality of this firmware.

At about the same time, the unofficial port of CyanogenMod 7 (Android 2.3.7) was posted. Surprisingly, he was very, very working. Of the main disadvantages:

- Camera does not work
- Hardware video decoding does not work
- MIC is not working

The test results listed above are made in this firmware.

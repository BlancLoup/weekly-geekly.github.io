December digest of grocery design: Book Atomic Design, Adobe XD for Windows and UX-trends of 2017

For six years now I have been publishing regular reviews of fresh articles on the topic of interfaces, new tools and collections of patterns, interesting cases and historical stories. From the tapes of several hundred thematic subscriptions, approximately 5% of the worthwhile publications are selected that are interesting to share. Previous materials: April 2010-November 2016 .



Patterns and best practices


Baymard Institute Studies

  • On the peculiarities of the work of determining the address of delivery by zip code .

Designing Interface Animation
UXmatters publishes Chapter 3 from Val Head Designing Interface Animations for Rosenfeld Media. She talks about the basic principles of good animation.

Guidelines for platforms and companies


Brad Frost - Atomic Design

The book Brad Frost "Atomic Design" about one of the most popular modular design methodologies has been published. A couple of words from the author .


Other design systems news:

  • Sarah Drasner talks about how to describe animation in design systems . The first half of the article is quite typical water, but further interesting thoughts on the description of the parameters and the choice of priorities for implementation.
  • Elliot Dahl writes about the benefits of the grid, based on the step / module (in this case, 8px).
  • My colleague Konstantin Lebedev describes in detail the new generation of the Mail.Ru framework . In addition, it has the ability to visually edit components.
  • How vertical rhythm helped Yammer designers work on the discussion screen . They were able to predictably vary its content without sacrificing the grid.
  • Samantha Zhang describes a very interesting approach to the description of indents between elements and components based on LEGO .

Material design

  • Updated material design guidelines . New sections about the adaptation of iOS-interfaces, context menus icons on the start screen, help and feedback.
  • The team began publishing GitHub interface components for Android, iOS and the web .

iPhone 7

  • Another animated iPhone 7 presentation template from Ruslan Latypov .

Understanding the user


Reading Content on Mobile Devices

Kate Meyer of the Nielsen / Norman Group reports research on reading speeds on mobiles. For simple texts, it is comparable to computers, but for complex texts it is already slower.



Accessibility

  • German Gref tested Sberbank services in a special suit that simulates limited capabilities .

Information architecture, conceptual design, content strategy


Documenting Design Discovery

Dan Brown describes the EightShapes methodology for describing the results of the research phase and conceptual design. There are 6 key aspects in it - the problem, purpose, context, key idea, rules and models.



Combining Mental Patterns and Jobs To Be Done

James Kalbach shows how to use a bunch of mental model diagrams and jobs-to-be-done to find points for optimal product development.

Design Words with Data

Interface copywriter Josh Saito writes about what methods of assessing the recognition of names and readability of texts he uses in his work.

User research and testing, analytics


FullStory - The Leading Session Playback Tool

The FullStory service helps to work with video recordings of user sessions with the site. This is the recording of the sessions, the search for them and analysis.



Reflecting on the one-way mirror

Jeff Sauro describes the pros and cons of using a one-way mirror in a usability lab. Although now more and more laboratories are moving from mirrors to cameras and broadcasting to another room, in practice he did not encounter negative user reactions when using a mirror.

Algorithmic design

  • An experiment with automatic composition creation from Adobe and the University of Toronto . Based on the current set of elements, he suggests either to tweak it in trifles, or a completely different option. A very cool example of how this will help the tools of the future.
  • Logojoy service replaces low-cost freelancer to create logos. Based on his style preferences, he offers a variety of options that can then be tweaked and seen in the context of a conditional corporate identity. An ideal example of an algorithmic design tool. Its author, Dawson Whitfield, talks about working on a product .

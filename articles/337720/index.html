10 years of Computer Science club



This year Computer Science club in St. Petersburg is 10 years old. Since 2007, the club has held open lectures and courses where anyone can get acquainted with classical results, the current state of affairs and open tasks in various areas of computer science. Entrance to all lectures is free, registration is not required. Slides and videos of all past lectures are available from the club website.

They will read mini-courses on the following topics.


Theoretical:


  • error correction codes;
  • optimal algorithms;
  • metric geometry and approximate algorithms;
  • computational geometry;
  • formal languages;
  • algorithms for NP-hard problems;
  • the complexity of propositional evidence;
  • circuit complexity;
  • complexity of infinite words, etc.

Applied:


  • bioinformatics;
  • programming language Kotlin;
  • effective use of C ++;
  • machine learning and recommendation systems;
  • artificial intelligence and unmanned vehicles;
  • neural networks, etc.

( more on the club website ).

Academician of the Russian Academy of Sciences Yury Vladimirovich Matiyasevich will open the fall semester in the club. He will give two lectures on the Tarski algorithm.

Wargaming Developers Contest: Winners Announcement



After almost four months, which included the generation of ideas and the development of projects by the participants and the selection of the best of the best jury members, the Wargaming Developers Contest came to an end. Today we will announce the names of the winners in each of the nominations. But that is not all. Below we also note the works that didn‚Äôt have enough before the victory, tell them why the authors should continue the work and give the first details about the closing ceremony of the competition.

WGDC had to spend at least for the sake of a huge feedback from the developer community, which allowed us to establish a dialogue with it and identify problem areas for further development of the service.

Starting the competition, we clearly understood that the need for it exists and the community will surely be willing to fight for the prizes. What we did not expect was this amount of work. We knew that serious players would participate in the contest, but by default we were ready to search for diamonds among the ‚Äúloot the cows‚Äù and the ideas of the ‚Äú3D actions‚Äù. In the end, it all came down to a heated debate and a difficult choice of fairly strong work. Here are the results.

Winners and awardees


Nomination "The best modification of the game client World of Tanks"

1 place. Battle Assistant.

The modification changes the UX of the game, the gameplay itself, making it more comfortable and convenient for some players, but it does not provide a game advantage. Few could compare with this mod, largely because it reveals one of the main ideas of the competition: the game belongs to the community, which changes it to fit its needs.

2nd place. Customizable tank carousel.

Pleasant refinement from the XVM team for the hangar. The mod allows you to display a lot of useful statistical information on the icon of the tank itself, which saves a lot of time for fans to track their progress without leaving the game client.

3rd place. Miku Hangar (version 2.0) .

Very high quality in terms of art design work. Surely the Asian market will like it. The work feels the love of people for what they do. The project does not change the functionality of the game, but from an aesthetic point of view it is beautiful. Fun fact: some jury members and simply Wargaming developers left Miku Hangar in the client even after testing and evaluating it.

Nomination "Best Functional Project"

1 place. WoTBase .

During the competition, the guys made an excellent project in terms of functionality, which was based on the Encyclopedia of the World of Tanks and their own developments.

Particularly noteworthy is the functionality of playing replays. Already, cyber sports teams and clan fighters can analyze in detail all the moments of the last battle. The idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe project can be an indispensable element of the broadcasts of Wargaming League matches.

2nd place. Portal ivanerr.ru .

This is a pre-contest project for the clan community. During the competition, the functionality of the project was substantially refined, which allowed him to compete for the top places.

3rd place. Alphabet - World of Warplanes .

News portal for World of Warplanes, which was developed as part of the competition. Good work in itself, plus it is worth noting the fact that this is a project implemented not for a tank audience, but for ‚Äúpilots‚Äù.

Nomination "Best mobile application / application for social networks"

1 place. Armor Inspector .

App for iOS and Android. Allows users to further explore their favorite game, determining the chances of penetration of any armored vehicle. Often, our players ask the question: ‚ÄúWhy didn‚Äôt I pierce a particular tank?‚Äù. This application allows you to thoroughly understand the situation by selecting the gun, the type of projectile and reproducing the direction of the shot.

2nd place. Knowledge Base for WoT .

Mobile analogue of the web-project, which took the first place in the nomination ‚ÄúThe best functional project‚Äù. Since the application existed before, we evaluated only the improvements made during the competition. They were enough to finish only second, but this is a great success for a small team of two people.

3rd place. WoT Tactics .

This is a prototype of an online game for Android and iOS tablets. Perhaps the only project that threatened to create a full-fledged game within the competition. We are sure that if the team had more time to finalize the gameplay, UX interface and control, then WoT Tactics could claim victory in the nomination. We hope very much that the project will not stop in its development and will receive the deserved recognition of the community.

Nomination ‚ÄúFor contribution to the development of products and services for the Wargaming community‚Äù

1 place. Wot-News.Com .

The most popular news portal from the community. On the site, players can find almost everything: from news, promotions and videos to detailed statistics and ratings. The resource is visited daily by hundreds of thousands of "tankers."

2nd place. CLANTANK.RU .

One of the best sites for administering clans, checking statistics and socialization between clerks. The site provides clan players with the ability to track player activity in the clan and its effectiveness. The project also implemented a convenient tool to search for applicants for the clan membership for recruiters.

3rd place. WOT API .

Before the launch of the public APIs from Wargaming, this project was one of the few alternatives to receiving data from our servers and gave impetus to the development of third-party projects. This resource also gave us to understand the need to launch the Wargaming Developer Partner Program and publish the Wargaming Public API.

This is a finished project. The main project functionality (statistics collection) was performed by the client application. During the competition, the project team was going to replace the client application with a mod to the game client, but did not manage to meet the allotted time. The peculiarity of the idea is to collect statistics on the fly using mod.

WGDC closing ceremony


Candidates who did not get into the prizes will have another surprise. They, like the winners, will be sent invitations to the closing ceremony of the Wargaming Developers Contest, which will be held December 13-14 in Minsk.

The plan of the event we will inform the guests as soon as possible. On it, participants will be able to communicate with representatives of Wargaming, including - to ask sharp questions to the developers of the Wargaming Public API and get information from them about future development plans. We will advise applicants in which direction to better develop their work. All contestants will be able to share experiences with each other. And, of course, those who did a great job during the competition will have the opportunity to relax and rest.

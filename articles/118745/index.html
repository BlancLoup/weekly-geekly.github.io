LG's smartphone hit the Guinness Book of Records

LG Optimus 2X is listed in the Guinness Book of Records as the world's first smartphone with a dual-core processor. Dual-core chip provides him with unprecedented high performance and speed.

Designed by NVIDIA, the dual-core Tegra 2 chip, used in the LG Optimus 2X, runs at 1 GHz and features low power consumption and high performance for video and audio playback.



According to LG, in comparison with devices based on a single-core processor with the same clock frequency, the owners of this smartphone receive accelerated web browsing, as well as instant response to keystrokes and unhindered multitasking when working with applications.



The smartphone has several interesting features, for example, content mirroring via HDMI. In addition, Optimus 2X is equipped with a 4-inch display with a resolution of 800x480 pixels, a microSD slot, 8 GB of internal memory, an 8-megapixel camera with autofocus and video recording, as well as an additional camera for video calls.



A detailed review from Nomobile can be found in their blog .

Model LG Optimus 2X was announced at the end of 2010, and was first presented to the public at the consumer electronics show CES in January 2011 in Las Vegas. At the beginning of the year, the Optimus 2X went on sale in Korea, and then appeared in other markets. Currently, LG Optimus 2X is sold in Russia and the CIS countries, North America, Europe and Asia, and in mid-May will be available in Latin America.

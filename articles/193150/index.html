The system of education at the American University in Computer Science

Recently, I entered the American university ( Worcester Polytechnic Institute ) and in this connection I want to share my first experience. I will not write about how cool it is here, cool and so on. I will write more specifically about the education system, since I studied it for three years, but nevertheless I did not understand much. The post is quite large, so feel free to start reading from the most interesting paragraph in your opinion. However, I recommend reading everything, this is the real experience of a real student from the former CIS, who entered the top US university. Why I think it will be interesting for IT people? Because my specialty here is Computer Science, and in the USA the most reputable IT universities. If someone from habrovtsev wants to send a child to meet a brighter IT future, then it will be useful for him and the child to read this article.

It is no secret that the US universities are the best in their field. Of the hundreds of influential universities in the world, about 70% are American, and the top three Harvard, Stanford, MIT does not leave the first places in international rankings. So how does this system work? I must immediately say that we will talk specifically about the university where I study. Systems may vary slightly by university. I'll tell you everything in order.

schedule


To begin with, the Americans do not have the concept of "session", "coursework", "thesis", "record book". To talk about the "retake and dosdat" back later. The school year is divided into four quarters, called from ‚ÄúA-Term‚Äù to ‚ÄúD-Term‚Äù. Each term (hereafter, term - quarter) lasts exactly 35 training days. If during the term there is a holiday, it does not matter - then the end of the term is shifted exactly on this holiday. During each term, the student takes three courses. You can take the fourth physical education or music, but not so important. For 35 days, the student attends only these courses and does not care about anything else. Lectures, conferences and laboratories on one course take exactly 50 minutes a day. Accordingly, 50 * 3 minutes per day are mandatory visits by the student to lecture and laboratory audiences. Let it not seem like a ‚Äúfreebie‚Äù to you, since according to statistics, for every hour (50 minutes) of the lecture (laboratories, conferences) there are 3 hours of independent work. Computer science

At lectures, the professor reads material for 150 people. Everything is very interesting, since she (the professor) has a clear, detailed plan for a minute. Three times a week the material is read in a huge lecture hall. Ratings


Grades 4: A, B, C and NR (No Record). If a person fails the course, then he does not go to the zero "certificate", but it is simply considered that he did not take the course. Thus, it does not spoil its GPA (Grade Point Average - average rating). And now about the percentages: A - 90 +%, B - 80 +% and C - 70 +%. In this section I will talk a little about the requirements for the diploma. Since the courses all students take are different and there is no general program as such, the requirements are simple - 30 courses of their specialty, 6 classes of mathematics, 6 humanitarian classes, 2 classes of physical education and two projects. More about them. IQP (Interactive Qualifying Project) and MQP (Major Qualifying Project) are two mandatory and large projects. The essence is simple - students gather in a group and solve the REAL problem of society. This may be a drought in some region of Africa, students are developing an irrigation mechanism or a biochemical, maybe easier - in some areas of the city there are many minibuses, and students are developing an optimal transport plan that will reduce the number of buses. Projects are often made outside the university, sometimes even in different countries (my university even has a point in Moscow, has recently appeared).

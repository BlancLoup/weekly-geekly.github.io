JetBrains at RubyConfBy in Minsk on March 22

Hi, Habr!

Already this Sunday, March 22, a one-day RubyConfBY conference will take place in Minsk, and our speakers are already packing their bags. This time, two Andrei, Andrei Vokin and Andrei Vlasovskiy, will talk about tools that allow finding errors in Ruby code without executing it. In doing so, introducing the RubyMine and PyCharm commands, they will also try to compare Ruby and Python to understand how the programming style affects static code analysis.

And a little about the conference itself with the words of its organizers:
March 22 in Minsk in the space of SPACE (October 16A) will be held the first major technology conference RubyConfBY , dedicated to Ruby, Rails and related technologies.

Developers from different corners of the globe will gather to discuss pressing issues:
Will the good old Bundler sink into oblivion, giving way to Rubygems?
Is it time to throw a bored REST and try the Hypermedia API?
Is it still popular with hackathons?
Should I switch to Trailblazer in creating the architecture of rail applications?
And, of course, find out how stylish the creator of the Ruby Style Guide is?

You can buy tickets at a discount. The organizers and the Belarusian Ruby User Group provide a promo code for a 40% discount. Buy tickets on the website http://rubyconference.by/ , enter the BRUG promotional code.

The conference will bring together speakers from the UK, Germany, Finland, Lithuania, Poland, Russia, Belarus and even Australia! As well as representatives of the sponsoring companies Toptal, appcast.io who are ready to share their experience in developing Ruby & Rails.

See the complete program on the conference website.

See you in Minsk!

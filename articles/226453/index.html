Let us take a closer look at the GALAXY Tab S. screen. This is a Super AMOLED with WQXGA resolution (2560x1600, aspect ratio 16:10), covering more than 90% of the Adobe RGB color space. For comparison, standard LCD panels exhibit color coverage of only about 73% of the Adobe RGB space, and in some cases even less. Greater color gamut allows the screen to more accurately reproduce clear, rich hues ‚Äî blue, red, green, turquoise, lemon, and other colors. In this case, in the case of conventional LCD screens, attempts to increase the color gamut either lead to a radical complication of the design, or to an increase in power consumption, Super AMOLED screens, on the contrary, provide good energy efficiency and compactness (because there is no need for a separate backlight module). ). At the same time, the contrast of the image on the Super AMOLED screen can reach 100,000: 1 ‚Äî this is a hundred times higher than on a conventional LCD screen.


In addition, the adaptive screen function is capable of automatically adjusting the brightness and color rendition depending on the brightness and color temperature of the ambient light, as well as how the tablet is used. By the way, now you don‚Äôt manage to scold the screen for unnaturally bright colors: several color profiles are provided in the settings (AMOLED Basic, AMOLED Photo and AMOLED Cinema), so if the standard settings upset the user with excessive brightness and unnatural color rendering, you can simply choose another mode to your liking . Since the Super AMOLED technology does not require a separate backlight module, the GALAXY Tab S consumes less power compared to tablets based on LCD screens of comparable diagonals.

SpaceX launches prototypes of the first satellites for the global satellite Internet



Three years ago, it became known about the new project of Ilon Musk to create a global satellite Internet through a mesh network of 12,000 mini-satellites in low near-earth orbit, each of which covers a territory with a radius of 1060 km with a 25-ms delay.

Apparently, the start of an ambitious project will not take long to wait. On this Saturday, February 17, 2018, the launch of the Falcon 9 launch vehicle from the 1360-kilogram Spanish satellite Paz from Vandenberg base in California was appointed . As a "secondary cargo" in the cargo compartment will add two mini-satellites - Microsat 2a and 2b. These are prototype satellites from the SpaceX future global satellite constellation.

On November 15, 2016, SpaceX requested permission to operate its own satellite service in Earth orbit, in which its subsidiary will act as an operator.

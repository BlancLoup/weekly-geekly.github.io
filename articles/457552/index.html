What's the point of this?


When it comes to climbers, as a rule, there are two irreconcilable camps. Some people think that people who devote all their free time to climbing cliffs, risking their lives every minute, and climbing to a height where all living things die from lack of oxygen and severe frost are just adrenaline maniacs.



Others admire the brave ones who are ready to give everything to look at the world from the summit of the highest mountain or follow the route that nobody has ever walked.





Truth is somewhere in the middle. On the one hand, overcoming difficulties to achieve the goal, striving for the new is the most important part of human essence. On the other hand, it is probably not particularly reasonable to talk about a justified risk for the sake of conquering unexplored peaks, dying in a giant queue on Everest.



However, there are people who spend most of their lives at dizzying heights, not having a reliable point of support, but at the same time they are engaged in a business that even the most skeptical expert will not call meaningless. We are talking about industrial climbers.



Not romance. Or yes?


Industrial climbers themselves say that romance in their work is at most 10%, and the rest is hard work. But somewhere here - cunning. Judging by the self-made by installers working on the construction of Lakhta Center, the work did not become routine for them.







Climbers from the Ilros Montazh and Almaz Group companies share the tower on Lakhta. Their current tasks are the installation of SOF rails and barrier lights on the steeple. Lakhta Center - among the most interesting and complex objects on which it was necessary to work.

Sergey Belousov, industrial climber "Almaz Group":

- This tower is the first of its kind and, probably, will be the only such one. Still, the tallest building in Europe, and the view from the top is nothing like that. Everyone knows that there are very beautiful sunsets in St. Petersburg, and from there they are even more beautiful.

Weather


Works behind the facade go all season and in different weather conditions. Chill climbers love less than heat.
Sergey Kolpakov, industrial climber "Ilros Montazh":

- A lot of clothes have to be worn. She is heavy, starts to cling to everything, hinders movement. Even if necessary, it will not be possible to quickly descend. In addition, a lot of energy goes to keep warm. In the heat, you can take water with you, but in -15, what will you take with you?
Heavy and bulky clothing is not at all like a sheepskin sheepskin coat, as is drawn. At the height and with many hours of work, such sensations can be obtained in a moment even from weightless and warm membrane anatomical overalls - a typical winter equipment of industrial alpinist.

Assembler - today, electrician - tomorrow, climber - always


The main trick with promalpi is that mountaineering itself is only a way to be on the job site. Therefore, all have a bunch of additional specialties. This is a much more realistic approach than getting an ordinary electrician in the Fasadee, teaching him the basics of mountaineering.




Work on the installation and adjustment of signal lights on the steeple of the tower Lakhta Center

Since there is no constant recipe for work in skyscrapers - every ultra-high one is unique, you need to have a certain ingenuity and engineering vision. For example, the very first question faced by installers at the towers of Lakhta Center: what to do with 360 meters of free ropes, because they are mounted at level 87 and go down to the ground. To prevent the ropes from being carried by the wind, they had to be fixed on the facade, and so that it was not only safe, but also convenient. It wasn‚Äôt possible to achieve this right away, and the installer‚Äôs journey to the place of work took 3-4 hours, as the climber had to run across every five floors - in the places where the ropes were attached.

Flash and Silverlight support on iPad

The participants of the company artefactgroup.com have developed a mechanism that allows to some extent to implement support for Flash and Silverlight videos in the iPad. Watch the video.



Under the cut video with Silverlight and a small explanation of the principles of work.







As the developers write, the principle of operation is as follows: on the server side, Google Chrome renders an image of Flash, Silverlight or any other video, these images are sent to the browser and using a special page are shown to the viewer in place of the expected video. With the help of javascript on this page, control of the video or application is intercepted and sent to the server.

In general, everything looks slow and not very good. But if you want to look at the iPad for something flash or silver-light, then this method will do. How do you think?

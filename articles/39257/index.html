Technique


... but the program itself contains several gross methodological errors, which is why working with it is at most a monkey job, which is intuitively felt by most users. I'll show you now ;-)
And by the way, how does the brain work?

The human brain, unlike a computer, where each memory cell is independent of others, and the execution of processor commands almost does not depend on their history (except status registers), works quite the opposite: every object or phenomenon is memorized and analyzed in the environment of all relevant phenomena and objects, and each skill is formed and fixed in the environment relevant skills and states .

There is no ‚Äúskill of typing the letter o‚Äù, but there are skills of typing letter combinations -pn, -n-o-, -s-l-o-, etc.
Similarly, there is no in itself the skill of printing a letter combination, but there is the skill of printing a letter combination surrounded by other letter combinations.
And the skill of printing a single word either. Each word in print (and reflection) is associated with other words.
(To be convinced of this, those who can print quickly can make an experiment. Take any word that is not too fast and start typing it in a row with a space of about 5-10 copies, you will begin to stumble, even though during normal printing typing text without hesitation with pages or at least with large paragraphs. This proves that the skill of printing connected text and the skill of printing individual syntactic units are different skills , and having one does not mean having another)
At the same time, what are we invited to print in Solo? -o <space> -o- <space> -
As a result, we have fixed a harmful skill, a harmful conditioned reflex ‚Äúafter o - space‚Äù . and with further training will have to destroy this reflex.
How often are letter combinations in Russian? what about oldj? Here you have another meaningless skill.

Another interesting fact. In the area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe cerebral cortex, which will be responsible for the skill of touch typing, the data are not given by letters, or even by syllables. They are served there by images . Natural is the skill of printing what we understand , not gibberish. For the sake of experiment, try typing a meaningful text and a word that has no meaning (at least for you). You will see that these are completely different activities.

And how often do we have to print many identical letters in a row? And by printing them in a row in Solo, we create positive feedback in the cerebral cortex, which also in our life will never come in handy and will interfere with practical printing many times, and also training one.

And by the way, why am I doing this?

There is such a wonderful thing in the Solo program as an error limit for an exercise ;-) I made more than 5 typos and hello, start over. No, first read what the doctor says, and then start over. (i.e., again we are dealing with the monkey labor). In the days of typewriters, it was vital to type without typos, because they were very difficult to correct. But we all work on computers. And looking at the monitor, we immediately see our typo and can fix it. There is a reasonable question: what is more valuable: to be able to print at a speed of 90 characters per minute with 2% typos, or 320 characters per minute, but at the same time, 3-5% of typographical errors from which all are noticed and corrected?

But people are very, very different ... For someone it is easier to reduce the percentage of typos from 5 to 2, and for someone it is easier to raise the speed of your dialing to 300 and these typos will no longer play any significant role, and Solo does not leave a person a choice .

In general, people have different tasks. Someone needs a blind set it is not clear why, someone else for some reason, but someone just to then print. With normal speed and acceptable purity.

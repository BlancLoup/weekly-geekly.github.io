Santa Claus asks a question. Contest for Intel Blog Readers


In the New Year, everyone wants to become Santa Claus grandfather a little (well, this is with the exception of Snow Maidens). And if there is such an opportunity, why not use it? The editorial Habrablog Intel announces New Year's contest for our readers. We bring to your attention 8 questions on posts published during 2015 on company blogs on Habrahabr and GeekTimes. Some questions are quite simple, and there is enough erudition to answer them. Others will require the study of the annual tape posts, to facilitate the search, we have reduced the entire list into one table (more precisely, into two: for Habr and GT ).
Reception of answers is completed, thank you all!

  1. What models were the first to be born in 2015 from the 6th generation of Intel Core?
  2. What was the main innovation in the Intel Parallel Studio XE 2016 Beta?
  3. What date did we publish a post describing all sorts of sensors that you can already connect to Intel Edison right now?
  4. What is the name of the container format in which you can put data on the depth of the photographed scene in addition to the usual photo?
  5. How many IoT hackathons illuminated or, at least, announced the blog of Intel in the past year? (hint: be careful, a simple search for the word "hakaton" is not enough)
  6. How many joints in each brush does RealSense technology currently track? (hint: look for the answer in the post about the interpretation of our appearance RealSense)
  7. What was the name of the Chinese Intel Atom-based microcomputer in the HDMI-stick form factor that we tested on our blog?
  8. Network cards with which interfaces this year first appeared at Intel?

The results of the competition will be announced in the final annual blog post, which will be released in early January.

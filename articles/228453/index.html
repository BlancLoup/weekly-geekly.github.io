Background: The Microsoft division, whose work is aimed at combating cyber crime for more than two years, has been tracking the activity of the Jenxcus and Bladabindi botnets, which consist of about 10 million computers, located mainly in the United States and Western Europe. In addition to the botnets mentioned, the No-IP subdomains used 243 more malware families.



Microsoft representatives argue that, despite numerous warnings , the owners of No-IP did not take adequate measures to stop cybercriminals from using their services, while representatives of No-IP deny this statement and say they remove subdomains on demand and additionally use filters for detection of malicious traffic, while Microsoft did not receive any requests.

The reason for the failure is that the Microsoft Azure infrastructure could not withstand such a high load, which caused several million users of the No-IP service. In the entire history of the service No-IP, and this is as much as 14 years, this failure is the most serious.

HDD sled - cost effective grain


Modern server equipment entirely consists of quickly replaceable harmoniously working nodes. In ensuring the continuity of its work, sooner or later there is a need for prevention or replacement of components. Usually, due to budget constraints, this issue is very sensitive, because the cost saving mode is typical of most small and even medium-sized enterprises. There are many ways to abandon the substantial costs of purchasing components, but such savings should be justified: without the risk of equipment failure or loss of information. So, the most common and secure method to save the budget is to purchase a sled for HDD servers.
Slides are baskets for hot-swappable server hard drives. They are made for servers and storage systems of HP, IBM, Dell, Sun and even Supermicro - of all models and generations, having two types of form factor: 2.5 "and 3.5".






Server manufacturers themselves do not sell separately HDD slides, but implement them only together with disks at an inflated price. The thing is that they do not have their own production of hard drives, but only put their firmware on Seagate, Fujitsu and Western Digital discs, stamping on them recommender stickers with their own logo. Only here the price of such a "quality mark" exceeds the cost of the HDD itself, that is, we pay the vendor for the originality of the part 100 - 150%. Thus, the price of hard drives, for example, from Seagate, is more than 2 times less than the same ‚Äúoriginal‚Äù with HP, IBM, Dell and other nameplates. Yes, indeed, proprietary disks are determined by the server without any problems without any inconsistency signals , and this is a definite plus. But the overpayment for this small "plus" is too substantial.
Also, this kind of savings deprives the buyer of the vendor warranty. Is this important? It is unlikely, since the warranty of the manufacturer of HDD, which is no worse, has not been canceled, as well as the warranty on the server using non-original disks, despite some prejudices. If there is no difference, why pay more?

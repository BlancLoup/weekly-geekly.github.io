In Russia, create a "backup" Runet

The authors of this project believe that such a ‚Äúcopy‚Äù will ensure the safety of the Russian-speaking segment of the network.



In the fall of 2016, the Russian segment of the Internet will have a backup copy of the infrastructure, writes RBC. This project was initiated by the autonomous non-profit organization (ANO) "MSK-IX". To work on the backup, a separate structure has been created - the Indata Network Technologies Development Fund (Indata FRST). At the first stage, 70 million rubles will be invested in the fund, the investments are provided by the ANO MSK-IX.

The new organization will be engaged in "macroscopic research of the Internet." Foundation staff will analyze the passage of traffic on the Network, as well as monitor the interaction of autonomous systems. The final result of the project should be a unified system that combines the IP address database of the Dutch RIPE NCC registry (this organization distributes IP addresses between telecom operators, including Russian ones) and the route information database of the Internet. There are five such registries in the world.

The Foundation will develop a system for visualizing traffic on the Web to identify problems in routing. It is also planned to create a number of web-tools for domestic specialists. Tools will provide the exchange of information about the status of the Network in real-time mode.

As for the ‚Äúbackup copy‚Äù of Runet, this idea is not new - its domestic officials suggested in 2014. Then a number of departments were concerned about the possible disconnection of Runet from the Global Network. In order to ensure the safe operation of the Russian part of the Internet, in 2015 the Ministry of Communications prepared a bill to create a ‚Äústate information system to ensure the integrity, sustainability and safety of the Russian part of the Internet.‚Äù The authors of this project believe that the state system should be a copy of the world system, including the database of IP addresses, traffic routing to the Network and the DNS system (which links IP addresses with the letter names of the sites). The bill of the Ministry of Communications exists in several versions. None has yet been submitted for public comment. Nevertheless, the Ministry of Communications promised to make the Runet fully autonomous by 2020. According to the Ministry of Communications and Mass Media, now the Russian traffic partially passes through external exchange points, which makes it impossible to guarantee the failure-free operation of the Runet.

What will the creation of such a system?


According to Alexander Stepanov, executive director of the ‚ÄúIndat‚Äù FRST, the new system will allow optimizing routing. With its help, it will also be possible to more effectively fight DDoS attacks. Now operators independently define traffic routing policies, companies do not share such information with each other. According to Oleg Demidov, PIR Center consultant, a small market of services is now being formed that are related to the measurement and monitoring of the work of the Internet infrastructure. Experts point out that the system created by the foundation will be incomplete. The fact is that now only 15% of the total inter-operator Internet traffic in Russia passes through MSK-IX. Only small and medium-sized companies work with MSK-IX, large ones exchange traffic directly. The big players, according to experts, will not install the equipment of the fund, which is associated with Rostelecom.

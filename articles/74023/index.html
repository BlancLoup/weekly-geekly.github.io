Users and data compression

I do not want to stir up another holy war, but comments on my last post about the "compression" in RAR inspired a certain number of thoughts and arguments that I want to convey to the community. But before that, I want to note that I work under Windows, so all of the following will apply to him. Plus, I will ask you to treat the topic as information to reflection, and not as a red rag for the bull. So let's talk about data compression formats from a user point of view.

What's going on in the market?


First, let's remember what compression formats we know? Well, we list - ZIP, RAR, TAR, GZ, BZ2, 7ZIP, ACE, (L) HA, CAB, ZOO, LZH, ARJ ... It is not difficult to check if you look at the number of downloads on, for example, cnews.ru to describe the situation on the Russian market and on download.com to view the global market. What do we see?

And we see literally the following - in the world 194 million WinZip downloads, 85 million WinRAR downloads, almost 4 million downloads - WinACE and about 2 million 7Zip. As it is easy to see, two-thirds of the market is controlled by WinZip (and this when considering work out of the box in Windows XP +), almost a third of WinRAR. In addition, do not forget that WinAce is already an abandoned project, which is losing its fans.

Why is the Russian segment different from the global one?


There we figured out how users get WinRAR or 7-Zip. Now let's see what happened to WinZip from the Russian market segment. The answer lies on the surface - users do not know and / or do not want to use a program that does not speak their native language. WinRAR is Russified, it has a Russian site. 7-zip tries to keep up - 7-zip.org.ua/ru . You will say that WinZip also has a Russian-language page . Yes, this is true, but the user zip will open out of the box, he will not download the "unnecessary" program.

Why users do not archive in zip / gz?


Suppose you (an ordinary user) finally understood why data compression is needed and stopped bothering your acquaintances with multimeter letters. Let's figure out what and how you will archive.

Both formats open both in WinZip, and in WinRAR, and in 7-Zip, therefore, in principle, the recipient will have no problems with unpacking. Another thing is that in the Russian segment, the default user account will not archive in zip.

Signed a law on the right of the Cabinet to regulate the threshold of duty-free e-commerce

The news that Putin on May 5 signed a law allowing the government of the Russian Federation to regulate the threshold of duty-free import of goods sent by international mail, went unnoticed. (at least, I was not able to find news about this here, and I cannot see any details in Google).

05/09/2014. Update appeared with reference to the law on the site of the Russian Newspaper and its text, thanks a5b



The Federal Law of the Russian Federation of May 5, 2014 N 114-FZ ‚ÄúOn Amendments to Article 317 of the Federal Law‚Äú On Customs Regulation in the Russian Federation ‚Äù‚Äù gives the government of the Russian Federation to regulate the norms for the importation of goods for personal use sent by international mail and delivered by the carrier to the physical person, without paying customs duties and taxes. This law was adopted by the State Duma on April 25, 2014.

Of course, the adoption does not automatically mean that the previously discussed limits of 150 euros will take effect immediately after the law enters into force, but the action itself is alarming. It means that change can happen unexpectedly and without discussion by the general public.

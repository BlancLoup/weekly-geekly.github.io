VIPER or what everyone is talking about, but no one talks

Every developer wants to write clean and modular code. Of course, the resulting application should not crash under any circumstances, and everything should be implemented quickly and efficiently. Sounds like utopia, doesn't it?

Egor Tolstoy from Rambler & Co is ready to argue with this and presents us VIPER - the very approach to the architecture of mobile applications that everyone is talking about, but no one talks about. From the report we learn how to correctly divide the responsibilities of the monstrous View Controller, which modifications were made by Rambler in the VIPER canonical scheme to get rid of the few shortcomings that are inherent in the architecture, and also how code coverage is reached ~ 99%

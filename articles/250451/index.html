Atom and nw.js switched to io.js, Linux Tracing, and more.

Looking back at January, we found that it was the third most active month in the history of Node.js (294 commits) and the most active month for almost three years (308 commits in February 2012) . With such an increase in support, we expect February to be even more fruitful.

  1. Version 1.1.0 released. Changes can be viewed here .
  2. Sam Newman added simplified creation of Stream-objects .
  3. Support for Linux Tracing (LTTNG) will be added soon.
  4. As part of a closer collaboration on debugging issues in io.js, Google shared its groundwork for v8 and Chrome .
  5. Travis CI added support for io.js.
  6. Codeship added support for io.js.
  7. Atom Editor switched to io.js.
  8. nw.js (previously node-webkit) switched to io.js.
  9. New Tessel microcontrollers support io.js.
  10. Chris Dickinson added six new committers . Thus, the total number of active committers is 23 .

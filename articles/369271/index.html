US scientists claim that up to 90% of cancer risk comes from external factors.



Published on December 16 in the journal Nature, scientific work from specialists from the University of New York at Stony Brook attributes most of the causes of cancer in humans to external factors. According to researchers, the contribution of internal factors (features of functioning of the body) to the risk of cancer is no more than 10-30%.

Scientists have used several ways to study stem cell division. It is possible errors in this process lead to the appearance of tumors. Researchers have conducted stem cell experiments, computer simulations, and molecular studies of tumor tissues.

Studying stem cell division, scientists expected that the tissues with similar models of cell division and the risk of a malignant tumor will also be the same. But this was not the case, which allows to conclude that the external factors have a strong influence on the risk of cancer. The researchers also confirmed that according to statistics, people moving from places with a good environmental situation to places where the environment increases the risk of cancer, and the risk of getting sick is actually increasing.

In January 2015, a study on the same topic made a lot of noise in the media, because its authors came to the conclusion that 2/3 of the contribution to an increase in the risk of oncology is made by internal factors.

The first tests Nvidia Fermi



On March 27, 2010, Nvidia presented a new line of video cards, Fermi, to the public. Officially presented two models of the top-end class - GTX470 and GTX480. First, let's look at the technical specifications in comparison with previous top-rated Nvidia products.

The results of tests resource Guru3D



New products can confidently bypass the single-chip competitor Radeon HD5870 in the popular game FarCry2, but the dual-chip HD5970 lacks quite a bit.

Battlefield Bad Company 2 DX11



In Battlefield Bad Company 2, the situation is not so rosy for Nvidia fans, the advantage of the GTX480 over HD5870 is just 2 frames.

Tom Clancy's HAWX



In the benchmark of the popular game Tom Clancy's HAWX, the advantage of Nvidia again becomes noticeable. The GTX480 is confidently moving ahead of the HD5870, and there is still too much left before the HD5970.

Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2



In the favorite shooter Call of Duty GTX480 makes just incredible, bypassing even the Radeon HD5970.

Resident Evil 5 (DirectX 10)



Also, Nvidia products look a bit more confident with the Radeon. But the leadership HD5970 so far remains unshakable.

Analyzing the results of tests and numerous reviews of Nvidia Fermi, I came to the following conclusions:

- GTX480 is about 5-15% faster than the Radeon HD5870
- GTX480 is about 5-15% slower than the Radeon HD5970
- GTX480 in some 3D-applications remains on an equal footing with the Radeon HD5870, and even loses a bit. But the number of such applications is about 20%.
- GTX470 looks like a worthy competitor to the HD5850
- GTX470 confidently bypasses the current single-chip flagship Nvidia - GTX285.

Honestly, I expected a little more from the products of Nvidia Fermi. I really wanted to see a strong advantage over the Fermi Radeon, unfortunately, this did not happen. Nevertheless, I am pleased to see that after several failed generations, ATI was able to catch up in performance. I really hope that this will help to adjust the prices on the market, because I think that many have noticed a tendency that Nvidia products have become too expensive.

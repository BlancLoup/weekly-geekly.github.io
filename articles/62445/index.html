Concept phone with a flexible OLED display

An interesting, empty and not completely unique concept of a mobile phone was born in the depths of the studio of Ukrainian designer Alexander Mukomelova. Monoblock, retractable flexible display, touch-touch-touch. All this is fashionable and already, sort of like, in the form of working devices or prototypes.



The display in the phone called Mobile Script is a flexible OLED. In theory, it can be pulled out of the phone case, where it is most likely in a twisted state. It is a pity that while still in the OLED tube still not twist, but in the near future, I think, it is quite possible.


Because the display is designed as a touch, then you can place anything on it: a virtual keyboard, player control buttons, etc. There is room for developing a software platform. The front panel of the mobile phone, by the way, is also sensory.


But the most interesting - cover the phone. It can presumably be created using nanotechnology (well, it was impossible to do without this word), and will allow the device to recharge the battery from solar energy.


The bottom line: the idea is pretty, beautifully drawn, generally doable in the near future. This is actually not the first concept with a flexible OLED in the form of a "scroll". You can even recall the recent concepts of the new PSP. Right trend, not otherwise.

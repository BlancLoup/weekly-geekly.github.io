Cutlery created using 3D printing

The globalization of network retail in Russia leads to the fact that the price of goods becomes the main parameter for networks today. As a consequence, the market is dominated by the most simplified products, which are primarily designed to carry the functional load, and the aesthetic and high-quality components go into the background.

A completely different approach was chosen by Apollo, which not only specializes in the wholesale supply of dishes and household goods of famous brands, but also creates cutlery of its own design.



Working in the market of kitchen utensils and cutlery since 2000, the company-customer in 2014 made a strategic decision - to change its approach to the product that it produces and sells. It began its own development. This is a very complex process, since industrial design is a symbiosis of art, technology and product knowledge. The company's goal was to revive the aesthetics of cutlery, return them to the "soul". With its functionality, each item had to be beautiful and of high quality, while remaining affordable.

The production process of the unique design devices is divided into the following steps: the Apollo designer creates a 3D model, then the model is adjusted by Prototypster engineers and sent to 3D printing. Cutlery prototypes for Apollo at Prototypster are manufactured on an EOS industrial printer. As the material for its prototypes, the company chooses polyamide - durable high-quality material. Thus, Prototypster creates prototypes of future cutlery, which Apollo subsequently manufactures and sells under its own brand.

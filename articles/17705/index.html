we do PDF without getting out of the browser

Recently I came across the fact that it was necessary to collect several dozen pages of online documentation into one big document. Moreover, the document was desirable in electronic form, because I do not like to carry a pile of paper with me and I try to manage with electronic versions on the PDA. I usually prefer the PDF format - the benefit is that in OO Writer export to this format occurs directly.


Having found several sources on web pages, I began to select content in the browser (Firefox) and copy it into the editor, so that later I could make one pdf of all this for viewing on my pickpocket.
But quite by chance when installing one Firefox addon, I came across an entertaining extension ‚ÄúLOOP for Firefox‚Äù.
It lives here:
addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/4738

So, the essence of the program is as follows: we install the extension into our FF, after which we register a personal account (free of charge) on the project website www.drawloop.com and get at our disposal a full-fledged opportunity to create PDF of local documents or web pages directly from the browser!
Any page with full format saving, addon converts to PDF and saves in your account. You can convert and add to the list any number of pages or local documents by entering the url (file address) in a special project window.
Then the resulting list of pdf documents can either be downloaded separately to your computer, or glued together (combine all) into one document and thereby get the combined pdf-file!
Thus, I nagrolitsya do yourself small pdf-books on topics of interest to me.
Unfortunately, the service does not provide the ability to make a PDF copy of the site automatically. Still, you will have to scroll through all the links manually, but still this is the fastest way to get a pdf-copy of a couple of dozen Internet pages

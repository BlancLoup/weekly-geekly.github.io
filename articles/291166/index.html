Developing a cloud startup: RingCloud results for May

For those who read us for the first time, who are we?


Since June 2014, we have been developing a virtual PBX for RingCloud business . In August, we conducted a beta test of the product, in which more than 300 testers took part (here you can read more about this), in October we started selling the service to the first users, simultaneously improving the product based on their responses, and in February of this year we went to full production. fully armed.

Traffic totals




Despite the comatose state of users in the first half of the month, we managed to revive attendance at the expense of posts, TV series translations and contextual advertising (it worked, in fact, only in the last two weeks). As a result, we lost not much traffic. For June, plans are even more Napoleonic. It remains only to take and make.

In May, as we planned, our API and a detailed description of it were released . It should be noted that we are one of the few virtual PBXs that provide such a wide range of functions for working with the system through the API. Tariff Plans




In a previous post, I wrote that we are starting full-scale testing of the demand for a product in the context of various tariff plans. At the end of the month, we decided to leave the complex line with four tariffs, which segmented users by the number of employees. Now we have only two easy to understand tariffs - for one employee (interesting for freelancers and other start-up entrepreneurs) and unlimited tariff (closes all other categories of business). In each of these tariffs, we have invested full functionality without any restrictions. The task for May is to fix the increase in sales. :)

It turned out to be especially nice for those users who were originally on more expensive tariff plans. Now for them the use of a virtual PBX RingCloud has become even more profitable.

After authorization, the user will fall into an ecosystem of integrated products that can be activated to use as all at once, and in any desired order.

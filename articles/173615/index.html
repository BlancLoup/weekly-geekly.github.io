Subcutaneous implant will allow for rapid blood analysis


A group of scientists from Switzerland has developed a miniature subepidermal sensor that allows you to almost instantly receive various medical data, such as, for example, cholesterol or blood sugar levels, and transmit them via bluetooth to your smartphone.
According to the presented video, the device can even prevent heart attacks a few hours before their appearance, determining the slightest changes in the bloodstream.
The invention has already been tested on animals, and the developers plan to begin testing on humans in the near future.
Such a device can be incredibly useful for people with chronic diseases such as diabetes. And also for those who simply monitor their health and try to lead a healthy lifestyle.

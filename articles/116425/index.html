Microsoft's password strength estimation algorithm (Part 2)

Continuation of the previous topic by password strength checking algorithm. Now let us take on a more complex algorithm, which for some reason is considered ‚Äúold‚Äù and commented out in the main text.

For the analysis of resistance, the following indicators are used:
  • Password length
  • Number of character types used: uppercase Latin, small Latin, numbers, special characters
  • Absence of a word in the dictionary (words long from 3 to 16 characters) , taking into account the similarity of characters .
  • The proximity of the word to the dictionary with a certain probability.
Password strength is divided into five classes.
  • Great (Best) -
    • Password length - at least 14.
    • The number of different types of characters used is at least 3.
    • In the dictionary there is no word close with probability 0.6, taking into account the similarity of symbols.
  • Resistant (Strong) -
    • Password length - at least 8.
    • The number of different types of characters used is at least 3.
    • In the dictionary there is no word close with probability 0.6, taking into account the similarity of symbols.
  • Medium -
    • Password length - at least 8.
    • The number of different types of characters used is at least 2.
    • The word is not in the dictionary, taking into account the similarity of characters.
  • Weak (Weak) -
    • The password consists of at least one arbitrary character.
  • No -
    • all other passwords.

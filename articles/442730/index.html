Microsoft: Russia is ahead of the US and Europe in the implementation of artificial intelligence

Microsoft Corporation today published the results of an analysis of the processes of active implementation of artificial intelligence in the United States, Russia and Europe. As it turned out, the Russian Federation reached the highest rate in this work among all the countries participating in the survey. The average result in the world is 22.3%, in Russia it was 30%, that is, a third of Russian companies are actively introducing artificial intelligence.

According to Microsoft experts who presented the Business Leaders in the Age of AI study, Russian leaders are using business intelligence more actively than their foreign counterparts. If we take the average rate of introduction of AI in 22.3%, as already mentioned, in some countries it is much lower than the average value - for example, in France it is only 10%.

In addition, the authors of the survey believe that Russian leaders are more practical in the use of AI in business than foreign colleagues. Experts have identified the following as the main priorities for the use of AI:
  • setting the right goals (32%);
  • development of business ideas (26%);
  • identification of new market opportunities (25%);
  • decision making (23%).


Directors of Russian enterprises ranked second in terms of a positive attitude towards AI. About 73% of managers believe that technology will positively influence their management activities. Directors of domestic companies have demonstrated a higher willingness to learn and develop new skills in the field of AI. 90% expressed a desire to get the support of professionals in order to work better and more efficiently with the new technology. On average, the world, the figure is 67.3%. In addition, 30% of domestic managers are willing to allocate time to adapt to new working conditions. On average, the world figure is lower - 20.3%.

Russian company executives are responsible for the introduction of technology. More than 65% believe that the responsibility for the ethics of the use of AI lies with the company's manager. The same point of view in the world holds 53.9%.

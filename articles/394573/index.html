The first office printed on a 3D printer has opened in Dubai

Dubai recently announced a strategy to build with 3D printers: authorities plan that by 2030, 25% of all buildings in this city will be printed. The first step was the opening of a printed office in the city center. It took 17 days and 140 thousand dollars to print parts and build a one-story building with an area of ‚Äã‚Äã250 square meters, which is two times cheaper than building a similar building using traditional methods.



To print the modules for the construction of the building they used a printer with a height of two floors, a length of 36 meters and a width of 12 meters. The work of the machine followed by one person. After printing the modules, they were delivered to the construction site, where a team of 17 workers assembled the building and equipped it.

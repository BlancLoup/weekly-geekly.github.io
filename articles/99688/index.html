Small Business Grant Support - Myth or Reality

The state tries its best to support small business and entrepreneurship. Business incubators are created, grant support is established, etc. But as always, everything turns out through the ass. Here is my little story about how a start-up entrepreneur (IT) tried to get a grant ...



It should be slightly clarified what the grant support from the state is ... A grant is essentially a reimbursement of the actual costs incurred by the entrepreneur in setting up his own business. Moreover, the cost exclusively on equipment, rent, raw materials, etc. Also, the company must exist no more than a year. To receive a grant, it is necessary to collect all sorts of certificates and documents (extracts from the EGRIP / Incorporation, certificate of the availability of p / s, etc.). And also to provide a "business plan" (how much in quotes read below).

Now there are several key features that discriminate the very essence of grant support:
- Only those costs that were at the time of application for participation in the grant are reimbursed. Those. You can not ask for money for current expenses.
- You can spend money only on equipment, rent, raw materials, etc. Salaries of employees can not be covered. It's funny, if you have the main item of expenditure (80% approximately) - this is exactly the salary.

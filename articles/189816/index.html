Communication devices without power supplies



The use of radio wave energy was difficult due to their too fast dissipation. However, engineers from the University of Washington have created a technology through which communication devices can be powered from signals that already exist on air, in particular, television. The system is called Ambient Backscatter. Developed low-power devices can also communicate with each other using other people's radio waves.



Developed Ambient Backscatter devices, the size of a credit card, are a printed circuit board with an antenna. Communication was successfully maintained even at a distance of more than 10 km from the signal source - the television tower. For information exchange, reflection from already existing signals is used. Capacity is still small - about 1 kbit / s, provided that the devices are in close proximity to each other (up to 75 cm).

Obviously, the technology has great potential: Ambient Backscatter devices can be used as sensors and sensors that do not need additional power sources, which means they can be placed anywhere and in any quantities, having built a whole network.

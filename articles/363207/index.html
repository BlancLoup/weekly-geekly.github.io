Facebook weapons for conquering Russia are local developers



Russia is one of the few countries in the world where Facebook is not a leading social network and does not even reach 50% of its popularity among Internet users. The situation needs to be changed, and now Facebook has begun to take active steps.

The company is negotiating with Russian developers, urging them to release applications for the Facebook platform. If Facebook is able to attract application developers, then users can follow them.

In Russia, several social networks at once have a larger audience than Facebook: these are Vkontakte, Odnoklassniki and LiveJournal.

Facebook Platform Development Director Julien Codorniou told Business Insider in an interview about Facebook's strategy to conquer the Russian market. He is confident that the key to success is applications from Russian developers.

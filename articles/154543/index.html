Samsung introduced in Germany GALAXY S III Mini

Samsung officially unveiled a compact version of its flagship smartphone GALAXY S III Mini yesterday in Frankfurt (Germany).

‚ÄúSamsung GALAXY S III represents a new concept of a smartphone that has become incredibly popular around the world. Now the revolutionary design, intuitive features and intellectual capabilities are enclosed in the compact body of the Samsung GALAXY S III Mini, said JK Shin (JK Shin), President and Head of Samsung IT & Mobile Communication. ‚ÄúWe are working hard to meet the needs of a wide range of users in first-class mobile devices.‚Äù


GALAXY S III Mini is equipped with a 4-inch Super AMOLED display with a resolution of 800x480 pixels. The device is also equipped with a 5-megapixel camera, and the frequency of the dual-core processor is 1 GHz. The mini version of GALAXY S III weighs 111.5 grams, and its thickness is 9.85 mm. The smartphone will be supplied with an internal memory of 8/16 GB with the possibility of expanding up to 32 GB with a memory card.

GALAXY S III Mini runs on the latest version of the Android 4.1 platform (Jelly Bean). As in the original version of GALAXY S III, the S Voice voice recognition technology is integrated into the compact smartphone, which allows, for example, to unlock a device, launch a media player or a camera, and change the sound level using voice commands.

Inheritance from his older brother GALAXY S III Mini got the function Smart Stay, which with the help of the front camera captures the movements of the wearer's eyes and goes into sleep mode only when the user does not look at the screen for a while.

Also, the device has a built-in Direct Call tool, which, by recognizing gestures and the proximity sensor, helps to automatically switch from a message dialing mode to a call to the same subscriber when the smartphone is brought to the ear.

GALAXY S III Mini can share files with GALAXY S III and GALAXY Note II using the S Beam service. The function is a hybrid of NFC and Wi-Fi Direct: near-field communication allows devices to authorize each other, and Wi-Fi Direct provides fast transfer of large volumes of files (up to 250 Mb / s).

Currently, Samsung leads the global smartphone market: according to analysts from IDC, the company's share in the mobile segment in terms of the number of shipped devices in the second quarter of this year was 32.6%.

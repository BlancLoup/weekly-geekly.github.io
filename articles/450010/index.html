How is it cataloged?


Here the question is divided into two - the file system level, where cataloging is at the folder level and logical cataloging for more parameters, because the folder tree is still limited in capabilities.

Yes, I take pictures in rava. Because of raw at any time you can make jpg, but not vice versa. Once I shot raw + jpg so that I could quickly transfer a photo to my phone and send it to the Internet (it was difficult to transfer raw to a phone). jpg then erase when copying to the desktop. But now the phone began to arrange me for the quality of the photo (for putting it on the Internet), because I completely refused from jpg on cameras. Left either from the time when I did not have a mirrorless, or come from the phone.



At the file system level, it looks something like this: at the top level of folders, the source. Names of photographers usually.



A level below are topics. All are more or less the same, there may be personal themes (for example, "Dogs", some may not be so.



Next - the year. Inside the year folders by day. In the folder there can be separate photo sessions if the photos of the day are broken by topics.

I photograph on two cameras, usually in turns, but occasionally I take both with me - then I dump photos from them into one folder.

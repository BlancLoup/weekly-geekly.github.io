Cartoon "Gypsy": Do with us, do as we do better than us!

Now we are engaged in animation, and animation is a fairly long process in which not all Simpals employees are involved. Therefore, so that neither we nor the readers of the blog would miss, we decided to conduct an experiment:

The third postcard we will do ... TOGETHER!
How? Very simple :


Blog readers can suggest the script options for this postcard themselves and discuss them.
Selecting the best of the proposed scenarios, we further undertake to post each of the stages of the production of this postcard to the blog for general discussion. And adjust our actions according to the wishes of the readers.

That is, no less than once a week, we will talk about the production status of this postcard and explain why we listened to one or another idea that arose during the discussion, and why we did not listen to another one.

The names of the authors of the script and the most active participants in the discussion will be included in the captions of the cartoon - postcards, not as sponsors, but as participants and authors of the project. That will be absolutely true.


The task

Come up with a cartoon postcard script that should introduce the viewer with two or three more characters:
Usually in our video postcards there is a confrontation between two characters, for example, the Gypsy against death or the boy against his grandfather. But despite the clash of interests at the very end, they come to some kind of compromise that suits them both.

Our video postcard consists of 3 parts:

1. Intrigue and plot of the plot for about 40 seconds
(Death meets the gypsy on the road, the gypsy offers to drink or grandfather makes the grandson practice the violin in exchange for candy, and deceives him by slipping an empty candy wrapper instead of candy)

2. The climax (white background with the inscription) for about 10 seconds
(Death and Gypsy drink or Grandfather laughs, grandson indignant)

3. Responsiveness (gag) about 5 seconds
(Gypsy overtakes Death or grandfather lights a pipe, the pipe explodes)

Duration: Minute (plus or minus 5-10 seconds)


Note:

The postcard should introduce the viewer with two or three more characters:

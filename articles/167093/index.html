How to start flying, even on something, on your own

My childhood was spent among people who used to fly all the time. They built, developed a new one and flew it right there. At the age of 5, it became a discovery for me that someone in this world might not fly. For my 26 years, I managed to try almost everything on what you can fly. I want to share my experience and talk about the possibilities available.

The principle of soaring flight is based on the existence of ascending air currents. Like the wind, just straight up. The pilot's task is to find such a flow of air and stay in it for some time to gain altitude, and then fly to the next flow. And so in the cycle.


The main advantage of pars is flying without fuel. Only starting energy is needed to set the initial height. However, this imposes limitations. For take-off, you need somewhere to take the initial energy. You can take off or from the mountain, or with the help of a winch, or after another, motor, aircraft.

One of the main characteristics of any aircraft is quality. And for parners, this is the most important characteristic. This value determines how many kilometers you can fly having decreased by 1 km. Usually this value is from 3 to 60. That is being at an altitude of 1000 meters you can fly up to 60km in a straight line. (in absolutely perfect conditions and at a certain speed)

Stereotypes about soaring flights


  • Fly is dangerous.
    It is dangerous to cross the road in the wrong place because you can be hit by a car. And in the air, everything depends on you.
  • In the air, you can encounter another aircraft or a bird.
    This possibility exists, but its probability is so small that cases of such collisions can be counted on fingers. One of the most famous is alive, safe, an eagle . The probability of a collision with another aircraft depends on the number of these devices around you.
  • It is very difficult to take off and land.
    Yes, this is the most difficult part of the flight, but not more difficult than driving an unregulated intersection by car. However, it all depends on your chosen aircraft. For paragliders and hang gliders this is much more complicated than for a glider.
  • It takes a very long time to learn how to start flying by yourself.
    It is necessary to study, but not more than on automobile courses. Even less because There are no traffic policemen and road signs in the air. But to fly away from the place of take-off somewhere, you already need to learn much more.
  • No flights without wind
    The wind is practically unrelated to the ability to fly. The flight is due to vertical air flow.
  • Without a motor you cannot fly long and far
    On a plain in normal weather, you can fly all daylight hours. In the mountains, you can fly forever. there are streams of flow around the mountains. In my first summer year, in Kiev, on a glider, I once flew 6 hours a day without a break. It could have been longer, but the strength was gone.
  • If something breaks in the air and there is no chance of escape .
    The design is so simple that there is almost nothing to break in it under normal loads. Electrical systems are auxiliary and their failure is not critical for flight. No device is vital. You can take off and land without any instruments at all. In addition, there is always a parachute for unforeseen situations.
  • This is really extreme
    It depends on what is meant by extreme. All aviation is based on flight safety and extreme sports minimization. If you learned to take off and immediately flew along the route, then this is not extreme, but stupidity. Listen to experienced pilots, read the description of the flight accidents of other pilots and do not violate the laws of aerodynamics, and you will fly safely.
  • It is very expensive
    In reality, it costs from 50u.e. / month. Your aircraft does not always need to buy. Renting is often more profitable.

French provider blocks Internet advertising for all users.

From January 1, 2013, the Internet provider Free.fr willfully turned on the default Internet advertising filter for all subscribers. Thus, 5.3 million users of the Internet provider in one day lost the opportunity to see Internet banners on websites, contextual advertising Adsense and other advertisements, due to which there is free and free content on the Internet.

Naturally, the majority of users were only pleased with this populist Internet provider innovation, but such an initiative caused widespread outrage among the Internet industry representative and online media, who recommend their readers to turn off this option . At the request of each user can opt out of the forced blocking of Internet advertising.

On the forums they write that ad blocking is carried out on the black list, which is constantly replenished. These include Google Adsense, NetAvenir, AdTech and many other ad networks.

Although this option, at first glance, seems convenient and useful for users, in fact cutting out Internet advertising at the provider level is a rather irresponsible behavior and a very dangerous precedent on the part of the ISP. If we imagine that all Internet providers will start cutting out Internet advertising, then there will be almost no free content and services on the Internet.

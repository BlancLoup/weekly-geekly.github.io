Oculus VR helmet will be autonomous and "affordable"



Facebook executive director Mark Zuckerberg announced at the Oculus Connect developer conference a new model of the Oculus VR virtual reality helmet yesterday. This model will be autonomous, wireless, that is, mounted on the head, not constraining movements. You can freely walk in all directions or rotate around its axis.

New helmet promise to make "affordable." It will occupy a niche somewhere in the middle between the cheap consumer Gear VR helmet for the Samsung Galaxy and the expensive high-end Oculus Rift, designed for advanced gamers.

Prototype Santa Cruz


A key feature of the Oculus VR is its position tracking system. As seen in the short video with a demonstration of the Oculus VR, the new model is a modified version of the Oculus Rift with a computing module attached on the back of the head. Positional tracking allows you to accurately position the player in the space of virtual reality. In the future, this is the most important function for creating integrated augmented reality systems in which the virtual world will be combined with the real one.

In principle, position tracking technology is not something revolutionary. For engineers, it was important to reduce the power consumption of the mobile computer so that it does not instantly kill the battery. John Carmack, technical director of Oculus VR, spoke enthusiastically about solving this engineering problem.

Oculus Touch Controller




In addition to the Oculus VR helmet, Mark Zuckerberg introduced the Oculus Touch motion controllers. This is a finished product, sales of which begin on December 6, 2016 (pre-orders are accepted from October 10).

With interactive motion controllers, the Oculus Rift helmet transforms from a passive external surveillance device into a real interactive gaming system for interacting with the virtual world. This is something that was not enough for a full game. Now the player can normally control the hands of the game character: swinging a sword, beating with a fist or a knife, lifting objects from the ground, throwing the ball.



Even basic gestures are available, such as pointing a hand at an object.

Oculus Touch controllers are made in a stylish plastic case in the form of a ring. Apparently, such an execution has no functional purpose, but is done solely for the sake of balance. All buttons and mini-joysticks are located in the center under the player‚Äôs fingers. In general, such controllers seem more convenient and useful than Xbox One wireless gamepads.

On each hand gadgets are attached with ropes to the wrists, so as not to drop them in the heat of the game.

Two Oculus Touch controllers cost $ 200. These devices raise the cost of the Oculus Rift from $ 600 to $ 800. For comparison, the HTC Vive with controllers in the form of wands or "sticks" costs the same $ 800.

Each controller has its own positioning device. True, for high precision positioning of the controllers in the room you will need to buy a third sensor for an additional $ 80. With its help, a 3D map of the room. This is so that during the game you do not crash into the wall and fall off the balcony.

On the market, these helmets will have to compete with the PlayStation VR virtual reality system, which goes on sale next week for $ 400. But this is only the price of the helmet, excluding the cost of the camera and the Move motion controllers.

Other news from Oculus VR


In addition to the Oculus Touch controllers, the Oculus Earphones headphones will also be available for sale during this Christmas season, which can be used instead of standard headphones in a helmet. Cost $ 50. Headphones are sold at the same time as the controller: pre-orders are accepted from October 10, delivery after December 6.



Apparently, this gadget will be the perfect Christmas gift for the geek and the Oculus Rift fan, who already has everything, and naturally, he did not buy separate headphones, because he does not need them.

The company also announced a reduction in the minimum technical requirements for a computer to connect the Oculus Rift. Previously, the minimum required PC with a graphics accelerator minimum Nvidia GTX 970 (or equivalent) and a minimum processor Intel Core i5-4590. Now, thanks to the Oculus API's frame rate optimization software, the PC requirements are reduced to the Nvidia 960 and the Intel Core i3-6100 (or AMD FX4350).

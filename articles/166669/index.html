New YouMagic softphones now with OPUS codec

Dear Habra users, today MTT would like to share with you information about the release of two of your own YouMagic softphones with the OPUS codec under the Windows and Linux operating systems. At the end of December, YouMagic softphone came out on Linux, and on January 14, a release was launched on the Windows platform.


YouMagic for Linux was included in the operating system ROSA Desktop.Fresh 2012. This line of non-commercial operating systems for personal computers under the ROSA brand was developed by the Russian software company ROSA. The choice of MTT of this operating system was due to its growing popularity, associated with a large number of advantages. This platform is actively used not only in large commercial structures, but also in government institutions. On the portal CNews yesterday it was reported that Linux versions had been transferred to almost all city polyclinics in Moscow. Installation and configuration of the system is completed on 75% of the objects.





While various versions of Linux operating systems are only gaining popularity, and according to various sources, they occupy from 1 to 29 percent of all platforms for PCs and laptops, then Microsoft's Windows system has long established itself among individuals and legal entities. The recently released Windows softphone successfully passed tests on three versions of this operating system: XP, 7 and 8. MTT applications for the two platforms are designed in a single design and have an identical set of functions.


One of the most notable innovations in YouMagic softphones on Windows and Linux was the free OPUS audio codec. This codec creates a good quality of communication when talking on wireless Internet networks without access to public telephone networks. OPUS was developed in 2012 and already in September of the same year it was recognized as the standard of the International Engineering Council of the Internet (IETF). The excellent sound of the voice when using the new codec is achieved due to a well-balanced balance between the degree of audio signal compression and its quality. All YouMagic softphones still support many other codecs that are used when calling to telephone networks.

About other functional features

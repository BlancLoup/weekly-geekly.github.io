We test the site on game consoles. Part I

Recently, the topic of developing mobile and responsive websites has been actively discussed in the network. But users in the West are already worried about other problems. For example, how Amazon will look on the PSP and whether it is possible to enter a mobile bank with Nintendo.

We found an article about the adaptation of sites for game consoles, and thought that it would be interesting for many of you, and even useful for some. The material is quite voluminous, we will publish its translation in two parts, and the original is here .


According to data from 2010 and 2011, more than 1/8 of Internet users in the UK, USA and France and one of four American teenagers use game consoles to access the Internet.

And this is not news - it was possible to access the Internet through the console back in 1997 with the Game.com submission, which allowed users to download their results by connecting a modem, as well as checking email and viewing websites if they had a special Internet cartridge.

Today, all three major gaming consoles (Microsoft Xbox 360, Sony PlayStation 3 and Nintendo Wii) have Internet access. Of these, however, only the Xbox 360 does not have an official browser, although Microsoft has already announced the launch of IE9 for consoles. And some companies have already started experimenting with design for consoles. For example, Google in 2007 launched the Wii Reader version, which is now suitable for all TV consoles.

Add to this also portable consoles with browsers, such as the Sony PSP and Nintendo DS lines - and now we have a 15-year history of game consoles of various sizes and formats with Internet access. Nevertheless, very little is said about browsers in game consoles today.

Who uses browsers in game consoles?


Game TV consoles are not just for gamers. They are now being developed and marketed, complemented by many features, such as Blu-ray or access to Netflix. Some consoles also have applications for Twitter, Facebook, Skype, and Foursquare, so there is every chance that a user can visit your site using one of them.

According to a Pew Internet study, 80% of 12‚Äì17 American teenagers have a gaming console, and half of them also own a portable gadget. Pocket consoles are most popular among junior teenagers - they are played by 2 out of 3 teenagers aged 12-13 years.

If we talk about adults, the Pew survey shows that men are more likely to acquire game consoles, while women prefer portable consoles.

In the UK, young people aged 16-24 are more likely to choose consoles, and 20% of them use websites to visit them. Among children aged 5-15 years, 5% go online using the console more often than from any other device. In fact, this age group prefers to surf the Internet more than others through the console, rather than a mobile phone or tablet.

Among all age groups, game consoles are more popular as devices for surfing the web, unlike tablets. Perhaps this is because they are more common.

In short, all categories of people use browsers in consoles for various reasons. In families that own a computer, the console can be a convenient additional way to surf the web if there is competition for traditional devices. Pocket consoles are also popular among parents who do not want to give their child an expensive smartphone. Nintendo DS is especially popular with younger students, and in some schools it is even used in lessons .

Testing console browsers


Even if you do not have the opportunity to check your sites for compatibility with each console, it is still useful to keep in mind their existence and their features to make sites accessible to more users.

Google has opened access to Froyo

According to Google , 160,000 Android devices are activated every day worldwide, that is, for the first time, people are starting to use two Android devices every second. In total, there are 60 Android-compatible devices made by 21 companies and distributed by 59 mobile operators in 49 countries. Android devices sell faster than they have time to produce.



Wanting to give importance to the marked success, as well as in honor of the launch of the Droid X , Google has opened full access to Android 2.2 to its partners involved in the production of the corresponding devices. So soon you can expect more devices on Froyo, as well as the release of updates for existing ones.

For Android 2.2 developers, the platform is available as a downloadable component for the Android SDK. The downloadable platform includes a fully compatible Android library and system image, as well as a set of emulator skins, sample programs, and more. The loadable platform does not include external libraries.

UPD: I really messed up a bit at the end, thanks stari4ek . The Android SDK for 2.2 was available starting with the 2.2 announcement, and now the source code of the system itself is available at source.android.com .

System Analysis MeetUp

On June 13, Raiffeisenbank's System Analysis Community invites you to its first open Meetup, which will be held in the Nagatino office. We are waiting for system and business analysts, as well as all those who are involved in the analysis or are only planning to link their professional activities with it.




This evening will share experience and knowledge:


Ilya Sorokin, Raiffeisenbank
Tales from the Bank. How user stories helped us


Imagine that you are in a bloody banking enterprise. Here, a rare feature will fly to sell in 4 months, printed documents of the requirements weigh like a volume of the Great Soviet Encyclopedia. Correspondence with discussions stretched to hundreds of letters, and the only results of the meeting on the requirements are the date and time of the next. How could we survive and not lose motivation, how did we start reaping the fruits of our labor every two or three weeks? How have business and IT professionals begun to understand each other better? And how did the User Stories approach help us? Come, tell!


Ekaterina Gert, Croc Region
Crisis analyst: how to pick up the project and not to overdo it


Imagine you are an analyst on a big project. You work with a group of analysts. Each analyst deals with a very important piece of the project and is the custodian of valuable knowledge. Suddenly one of the keepers of valuable knowledge leaves your team. You get an honorable mission - to close the front of work for the departed. A project gets into your hands, according to which most of the analytical work has been completed, the requirements have been written and the technical assignments have been agreed, the development is already underway. As in such a situation: pick up and quickly dive into projects; prioritize and not break; pass all the time.
Here we will talk about it.


Alexey Lobzov, Alfa-Bank
How we evaluated the quality of the documentation


The report will tell about the experience of assessing the quality of technical documentation in one of the remote channels of the bank. Why did you decide to conduct an assessment? How was it done? What did you get in the end? The report will answer these and other questions.

Doors for guests open at 18:30, the event starts at 19:00

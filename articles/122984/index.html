About choosing wireless technology:


We all exist in space with radio waves (sound, radio, WiFi, BlueTooth, GSM, CDMA, microwave, radiation from Fukushima and others). The proper operation of the radio spectrum available to us is strictly monitored by state structures. Any equipment that receives or radiates radio waves, must necessarily obtain permission to use the corresponding frequencies (or rather, the manufacturing company receives permission, but we will not be childworms).

The reason for this is not only the desire to properly distribute radio frequencies, but also the intention of exKGB and military services to keep everything under their control - after all, these services take care of state security. If each person will be able to emit uncontrolledly radio waves of one frequency or another, this could damage the country's defense capability. Or else chaos in the frequency spectrum will simply begin; from this side, control is really needed.



In order not to swear each time to coordinate issues with the military, worldwide, for civilian exploitation, common, unlicensed, so-called ISM frequency bands were allocated. ISM frequencies (above the FM band) can be divided into several - 433 MHz, 868 MHz and 2.4 GHz.

The range of 433 MHz is the most noisy, it is very fond of using Chinese colleagues and car alarms. The size of the antenna in this range with a thirty-centimeter ruler. The 868 MHz band is already much better, but it has some peculiarities in the area of ‚Äã‚Äãa single standard, which make it difficult to use them in different countries even within the EU.

As a result, the choice was made in favor of the 2.4GHz ZigBee standard because this standard was ideally suited for three reasons:

- First, this wireless technology is allowed for use in European and American clinics, which means that it is assumed to be safe for human health;

- Secondly, in this standard it is possible to realize a long time of sensor operation from a battery;

- And thirdly, the device assembled on this technology can be easily put together with the antenna and the battery in a matchbox .

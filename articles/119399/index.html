A smartphone appeared on the Intel Atom processor

Fujitsu has officially unveiled the Fujitsu Loox F-07C smartphone, powered by the Intel Atom processor.
The novelty is equipped with a 4-inch LCD touch screen with a resolution of 1024 by 600 pixels.
It is made in the form of a slider: a QWERTY-keyboard is located on the sliding panel of the smartphone.

Two operating systems are pre-installed on Loox: Symbian and Windows 7. When turned on, the user can select the OS to boot.

F-07C is equipped with a processor with a frequency of 1.2 GHz, a gigabyte of RAM LPDDR400 and pre-installed Windows 7 Home Premium SP1.
The smartphone weighs 218 grams with a thickness of 19.8 millimeters.
When you boot Windows 7, the battery life of the device is 2 hours.

The Fujitsu smartphone will go on sale in June-July 2011 and will be sold for 70 thousand yen (24.3 thousand rubles).

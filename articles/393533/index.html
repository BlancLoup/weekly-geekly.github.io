In the United States started testing the world's largest unmanned ship "Sea Hunter"



The full displacement of the Sea Hunter is about 140 tons for 40 tons of diesel fuel. The ship is equipped with two diesel engines, which can reach a maximum speed of 27 knots (49.8 km per hour) and is designed for a conditional 70-day mission. If successful, the tests of the US Navy plan to build a flotilla of unmanned ships of this type.

The Sea Hunter was developed by the United States Defense Development Research Agency (DARPA) and the defense firm Leidos. For two years, the vessel will be tested off the coast of California. According to the representative of DARPA Jared Adams, unmanned vessels will expose people to less danger during military operations.

Program Manager Scott Littlefield said that during the tests at the Sea Hunter there will be personnel capable of taking control in case of danger (partly because the ship cannot recognize the bell, whistle or gong from other ships). The destroyer is not yet equipped with weapons, but the Pentagon notes that the prototype has not only a military purpose - later unmanned ships are planned to be used for commercial purposes, phys.org reports .

The Sea Hunter was built off the coast of Oregon and moved by barge to the San Diego coastline to begin testing. "The prototype is equipped with various sensors and an advanced optical system for detecting other ships," said Littlefield.

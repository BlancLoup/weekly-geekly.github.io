Viral marketing

For The Chief magazine

Not all viruses are equally harmful. Computer viruses destroy gigabytes of zeros and ones with all the ensuing consequences. Biological viruses several times a year affect the health of the population of one seventh of the land. And marketing viruses ... make a profit. This is viral marketing in action. Although in the inept hands of "experienced" marketers can bring such harm, which neither hackers nor microbiologists have dreamed of. This is an inept use of viral marketing. So, viral marketing is for and against.


The consumer rules the world. He meticulously looks at, sniffs, listens and asks the price. His Highness is convincing with commercials, prompted by PR-articles, lured by POS-materials and caught at living prices. Two types of organizations stand out against the general background: monopolists (convince them more often than they) and companies that, with cunning and wit, provoke His Majesty to act as an advertising medium for goods and services (not to be confused with people-sandwiches) - to transmit a marketing virus - information, which can easily be propagated and distributed to friends, acquaintances or colleagues.

Historical it turned out that viral marketing, like rumors, was not widely accepted among marketers, these events are rarely included in the promotion plan, they are not counted on. Perhaps Kotler, Trout and his comrades, who have not paid due attention to these methods in their writings, may be afraid of the word "virus."

Let's take courage and let a ray of light into the conservative realm of promotion methods - let's write viral marketing in all colors. Along with the beam, shadows of doubts and fears will appear that we simply cannot describe.

Viral marketing before the era of historical materialism

Contrary to misconceptions, viral marketing and marketing viruses live well outside the global web and benefit their creators. The methods of viral marketing described below appeared long before the birth of the Internet and their essence is simple - to create all the conditions for customers to actively recommend your product or service.

How can you not remember the "experienced" marketers, without a shadow of a doubt declare - Let's collect customers, give money and let them recommend us. The result of this action can be predicted with a 99% probability. A black spot on the company's image and rumors about a dishonest company rushing at the speed of a jet plane, poor-quality products, etc., etc. - the effect of a damaged phone. More gentle, Victor, more tender. People for the most part are greedy, but they need to play on weaknesses thinner.

Sly direct marketing creators have come up with sending out in one envelope 2 coupons for a discount or invitations for a sale, thereby gently pushing the recipients to transfer the second sample to a friend. The name of these coupons corresponding - "Pass on". Individual consultants, lawyers or accountants send business cards in this manner - two in one envelope. There is a chance that the second copy (as well as the first one) can be sent to the ballot box, but this is the wisdom of direct marketing, we will not touch them in this article. Let us dwell only on the text of the letter ‚Äî it is more logical and better to remind the recipient in a friendly manner that the second copy can benefit a friend than to categorically demand an urgent transfer of the above-mentioned products to an authorized person.

Some companies invest fabulous funds in advertising, others - in friendship. "You are my friend and I am your friend ..."

To organize such an expensive campaign only by the giants of the industry. Marketers at Uniliver decided to accelerate the introduction of Dove soap through viral marketing. The essence of the action was as follows: each woman who brought along with a discount coupon (it could be cut out from a newspaper) the addresses of three of her friends were given two bars of soap. "Sold" girlfriends received a parcel with one piece of soap, and in the "Sender" column indicated the name of the recommended person. On the one hand a friendly sign of attention, on the other - the very sale of friends in colorful packaging. How not to call it, and the market share after Dove shares increased by 10%.

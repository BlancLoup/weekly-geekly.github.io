20 steps towards a popular community

Communities are very popular in the blogosphere. Even more popular is the creation of communities. Only now it is far from a fact that a huge mass of people will necessarily appear in the community created by you (and you really want it, don't you?). What is the problem? Why do you create an "attractive community", but people do not appear in it? This is what I decided to try to figure out.

Initially, the idea was in the "debriefing" of one community ("fragrant community"), to use its example to sort through the process of creating and promoting a community in the blogosphere. He wrote one and a half pages of "debriefing" and realized that further - laziness.

I realized that the most indigestible option for me now is to present thoughts in the form of 20 (or more - as it will) steps along the way of creating and developing your own community.

1. The name should be simple and clear.

Good - "Flavors and smells", bad - "Fragrant community." In the first case, the user enters the community without thinking, in the second, it is most likely to flip through our community.

2. The description in the profile should be made with humor, a pun is useful.

Try to abandon the gray "Community of lovers of smells", let it be better to be trite "From the fragrant rose to the armpits of fragrant - we are talking about all the flavors."

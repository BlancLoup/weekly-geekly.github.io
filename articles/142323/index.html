The history of Google Chrome - with Pam Green

Elina Igaunis, Human Resources, Google Russia

Moscow Google invites you to a fascinating groove in the history of the creation of the Chrome browser along with our expert Pam Green. We will tell you how Chrome managed to grow from zero to 200 million users worldwide, about what lessons we learned along the way, as well as how the knowledge gained influenced Chrome's development and design. We will analyze examples of what ultimately worked and what did not, and describe the contribution of the Moscow and Munich development teams to the creation of Chrome.

Pam Green has been working on Google Chrome since its inception. Now she lives in Munich, where she continues to work to ensure that Chrome remains the fastest and most reliable browser that fully meets the requirements for the protection of users' personal data.

To get to the meeting (Tech Talk), please register here: https://services.google.com/fb/forms/chromehistory

Tech Talk will be held April 25 at the Moscow office of Google, at the following address: metro Novokuznetskaya, ul. Baltschug d. 7, 4th floor. Beginning at 19:00.

The number of places is limited, the meeting will be held in English.

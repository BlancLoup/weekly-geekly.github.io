Qik Launches Video Camera App for iPhone 2G / 3G with Focus on High-Quality Video Recording

A new video application from Qik focuses on improving the quality of the recorded video.

Qik is known to the masses as a technology that allows you to transfer live video streaming from your cell phone to the Internet and immediately publish these videos on social networks and other Internet resources, such as Facebook, YouTube, Twitter, etc.

The new application is unable to transmit "live" video, but instead allows you to shoot video of a much higher quality, since the limited resources of the phone are redistributed in favor of video and audio signal processing.



The result was an application with a number of significant advantages for all iPhone users of the 2nd and 3rd generations (2G and 3G):
  1. Up to 7 frames per second. Similar applications available today, give up to 3 frames per second.
  2. Better video compression rates. This allows you to significantly save the memory needed to store video. By this indicator, Qik VideoCamera exceeds available analogues by 8-10 times.
  3. Instant video encoding. Compression of the captured video into the final format takes time. Other applications do this after the video is shot. Qik VideoCamera does this seamlessly for the user. No waiting.
  4. Export video to iPhone Camera Roll and iPhoto (or other applications on the PC). You can watch the video immediately after the recording is completed.
  5. It is also important to save battery power. The application goes into sleep mode (standby mode) in the case of a long pause in user actions.

Wind turbine from 3D printer: AirEnergy3D

Last month I wrote about the WindPax wind turbine . This is a hiking option that produces 25W and is able to charge your smartphone. Now on Kickstarter another wind turbine was introduced, this time from Poland and printed on a 3D printer.

Photos of the first prototype.


Installation is possible on any surface.


You can use immediately or accumulate.


Before going, just disassemble the turbine and put it in your backpack.


Minute savings: spend the outlet from the wind turbine and save money.


The basic set of AirEnergy3D Basic Kit will cost about $ 350. It will include components that can not be printed on the printer. The rest will have to be done at home with the help of your 3D printer.

What is the general advantage of this turbine? Most current devices of this kind, if they are portable, can only charge the batteries of smartphones, navigators and other small devices. Problems arise when you need to charge a full-fledged laptop: wait too long. And the same WindPax does not replace the outlet in a country house. While the manufacturers of AirEnergy 3D promise a power of 300 watts - so both the garage and the camping will work.

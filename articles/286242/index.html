China gradually excludes products of American IT-companies from the list of public procurement

According to MarketWatch , China systematically removes products from several major US IT companies from the list of government purchases. This is proceeding systematically, and similar activities by Chinese officials began about two years ago.

During these two years, China has removed from the list of state procurements about 60 types of products from CSCO, Apple, Citrix, Intel, McAfee (in the latter case, these are software products).

According to experts, the reason for what is happening - in the disclosure of many secrets of the NSA Edward Snowden, who about two years ago began to talk about the work of the agency NSA. After receiving such information, the Chinese leadership decided to gradually reduce the use of American high-tech products in the country. The second reason is state support for local companies.

According to Reuters, since 2012, Chinese authorities have concluded contracts with many local companies for the supply of hundreds of types of high-tech products. However, the total number of imported goods in this area is approximately 30%.

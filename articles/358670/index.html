What is laser hair removal?


Laser hair removal is the most modern way to get rid of hair radically in places where you would not want them to grow more. The procedure is carried out by exposing the hair to light with a specially selected wavelength (810 and 940 nm), this wavelength affects the melanin contained in the hair, heating it, heat reaches the hair root, from which the microvessel feeding the hair follicle is sealed. Having no power, hair falls out of it for 10-14 days and no longer grows until 8 years old.

The main nuance of laser hair removal, which still stops many, is that the procedure needs to be done in a course, an average of 6-10 sessions with an interval of 3-5 weeks. This is not due to the desire to dissolve you for money, but with the biology of our bodies. The laser acts on the follicles through the hair, and at one time in the active growth phase is about 10-20% of the hair follicles, these are exactly the hair that we can see. The rest are in a dormant state and the hair does not grow from them. These phases alternate on average after 3-5 weeks, so the interval between treatments is recommended to choose one.
With each new procedure, the hair will appear less and less. So, by the way, including it is possible and to regulate the abundance of hair in those or other places, and not only radically remove all growing.

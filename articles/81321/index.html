Released the first Russian application of augmented reality AlterGeo for iPhone

AlterGeo (also known as Wi2Geo) has launched the AlterGeo augmented reality application for iPhone ( link for iTunes ). With it, users get information about the location of friends and places, looking through the camera of your phone.

An entirely new kind of map has become available to iPhone users, which makes it possible to master the space of a megacity easier, faster, and more technologically. Looking through the camera of their mobile device, they can now see pop-up information messages about places and friends superimposed on the real image of the surroundings: for example, a cafe is located 500 meters away, an ATM is in 710, and through 843 is tire fitting. The same pop-up messages in the corresponding direction will tell you which of the friends registered with AlterGeo is nearby and at what distance. The application for the iPhone 3G S uses a compass built into the mobile device, for other iPhone models there is a manual compass line that the user scrolls.





With the new application, the surrounding reality is complemented by information from the AlterGeo database, a social service that allows you to locate users and show on the map interesting places nearby: cafes, restaurants, cinemas, shops, car washes, garages, and many others.

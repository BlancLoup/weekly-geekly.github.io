How do DLP / SLA 3D printers work?

Photopolymers are used as consumables for SLA printing. Under the influence of light, these liquid substances become solid.



There are two ways to start the photopolymerization reaction. The first method is to use a laser to ‚Äúdraw‚Äù the layers of an object and is called stereolithography (SLA).

The second way is to design the whole part of the object using a digital projector, exactly the same one that is used when watching movies. This process is called digital light processing (DLP).



Each of these technologies has its own advantages and disadvantages.

In our list, we are considering 3D printers that cost about $ 5,000 to 10,000, which means we have set aside expensive professional 3D printers from companies such as EnvisionTEC, DWS, Prodways, and others.

Formlabs Form 1+



Form1 +, created by alumni of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, is probably the coolest desktop SLA 3D printer in terms of external parameters. You probably noticed him in a recent film from the studio Marvel "The Ant-Man."

The previous Form 1 was the first low-cost SLA 3D printer to hit the market in 2012. The subsequent modification, Form 1+, was released in 2014 and today remains the best-selling 3D printer in its class.

With the discontinuation of Form 1 production, Form 1+ became the real standard of Formlabs printers.

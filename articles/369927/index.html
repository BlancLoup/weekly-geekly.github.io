Prison telecommunications



Last summer, I managed to get into there from where many, on the contrary, would like to get out as soon as possible. I visited the walls of the correctional colony number 2 in Yekaterinburg. No, I have not turned into a bank robber or a small drug dealer. He did not crack the website of the Ministry of Defense and did not repost records containing appeals to various kinds of discord. And he did not even catch representatives of Japanese animation in Orthodox churches. I just simply asked for a tour, in order to clarify for myself what modern communication technologies are used outside the walls of Russian correctional institutions.

Under the cut the story of my walk through the alleys of the prison barracks with a camera in hand.

A little general information about the telecom in the MIS

According to the latest published data, which dates back to 2013, 700 institutions in 80 territorial bodies are integrated into the telecommunication system of the TCB. Each of them has its own local networks, in which more than 10 thousand computers are involved.

IR-2


The numbers listed above are only a way to demonstrate the breadth and possibilities of telecommunications of the Russian criminal correctional system and what ultimately should work. But it's time to move away from dry numbers.

Penal colony number 2 institution, probably known to every Yekaterinburg. The strange practice of locating detention centers directly in the center of a large city at one time touched Yekaterinburg as well.



Here, behind high walls with barbed wire, those who are recognized by the court as dangerous to society are serving their sentences. Communication in such institutions is a matter of prime necessity. For employees, it is primarily an instrument of control, and for those serving a sentence an exceptional opportunity to hear and see loved ones located hundreds and thousands of kilometers away.

An iron door that opens with a loud signal alternately launches small groups of three people into the territory of the colony. These are mainly employees of the institution. Attitude is the same to all and indulgence is not given here. Telephone and other communications equipment will have to leave at the entrance. Audio and video equipment, which rushes with them into the territory of the colony is also carefully checked. Each incoming passes a mandatory identification on the bio-smart system. This happens as follows: a person enters a small room surrounded on all sides by bars, where you have to go through fingerprint identification.





The room where each incoming person goes through identification in bio-smart

The data of each employee of the colony, as well as the visitor, is stored in a special database. An outsider is unlikely to penetrate here. A visitor who has not appeared here for three months disappears from the base. The data of the resigned employee is also stored only for some time.



A fingerprint is attached to this device, and all data recorded on you should be reflected in the database.

Each institution has its own database of information about employees and visitors. My data was not entered into the database, but later on I was constantly escorted across the territory of the colony.

Institutions of the FPS also communicate with each other via videoconference.

With the advent of the Internet, the FSIN eliminated the need for the constant transportation of prisoners to court. Now many court sessions are held online via video link. This is especially useful when the case of a convicted person is already considered in a court of another region.





In IK-2, several payphones were installed for telephone communication between prisoners and their relatives. For the call also requires prior consent of the administration.



Telephony and a number of other colony services are provided by a virtual telecom operator specializing in working with institutions of the Federal Penitentiary Service. In addition to telephony, the ‚Äúprison operator‚Äù organizes video communication and e-mail for communication between departmental institutions. The FSIN is actively using the possibilities of modern communication for a more comfortable life of those serving sentences - some time ago the department launched an online store, thanks to which relatives can supply the prisoners with everything they need.



The store menu allows you to select the institution and region of the sentence. To buy in the store you must pay a grocery card.



After that, the convict will be able to receive food in the amount paid directly on the territory of the colony.

In addition to the online store, another service for communication has been operating in the network for several years. Portal "FSIN-LETTER" allows anyone who wants to contact a prisoner of a Russian prison to send an email for this.

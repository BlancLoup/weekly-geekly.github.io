Mapping


  • Jean-Louis Zimmerman in his tweet collected several unusual objects that challenge the OSM tagging system.
  • Joseph Eisenberg posted on the Tagging mailing list his proposal to introduce a new tag - waterway=tidal_channel , which is invited to mark the natural waterways. He also points out that this issue has already been raised in September 2018.
  • Osmer Simon Lehner announced ( translated ) on the Talk-at mailing list that he added new layers of the Austrian government open data portal (OGD) to the OSM Editor Layer Index. You can read more about this on his GitHub page.
  • [1] Mapping North Korea - a new project by Maarten van den Hoven , which offers to remotely draw this country. For convenience, he divided North Korea into "squares." You can select any in the task manager web interface.
  • Often, the quality and speed of helping people during emergencies depend on the availability of high-precision maps. For many areas, these maps must first be prepared. Therefore, the non-profit organization Doctors Without Borders uses modern technologies and the help of thousands of volunteers. On June 25, 2019, from 19:00 to 22:00 in Berlin, a cartaton organized by Missing Maps will be held in the Wikimedia office, during which territories at risk will be drawn.

Licenses


  • After a long dialogue with Facebook and its map providers (Mapbox), Nuno Caldeira asked the OSMF board to prohibit Facebook from using OSM data in accordance with the provisions of the ODbL license. As validity of such actions, he refers to Article 9.4 c) of the ODbL license. Recall that this was preceded by a long discussion ( in February and March ) on the explicit indication of the use of OSM, which took place on the main Talk mailing list.

Daimler will sell batteries for the home. Like tesla

Employees of Mercedes-Benz are testing home batteries, which produces one of the divisions of Daimler. Lithium-ion batteries work in the electricity network of Germany and will be available for purchase in September 2015.



In April 2015, the network reported that Tesla in 330 homes was testing a home battery. The battery allows you to accumulate energy and give it away as needed. Tesla has created a subsidiary company Tesla Energy, so that she was engaged in developments in the field of batteries.

In 2009, Daimler created ACCUmotive, a developer company for lithium-ion batteries. The company's goal is the creation and production of batteries for hybrids and electric vehicles. This division of Daimler has made batteries for residential buildings and will start selling them in September 2015. The home system consists of 96 lithium-ion modules for 2.5 kWh.

ACCUmotive has supplied lithium-ion batteries to customers, including Mercedes-Benz and 250 automakers.

Nokia Asha 210 Stylish Budget Book Review

Nokia still remains true to itself and continues to delight us with QWERTY devices. When this phone was announced in April, I immediately liked it. As you can see, the LUMIA style is clearly being played up. Bright colors, monolithic case, the phone immediately attracts attention and leaves no one indifferent. Having learned the price of 3,000 rubles, the interest is further enhanced. The device can be considered an excellent companion for the voracious smartphone, sometimes not reaching the end of the day. Also pleased with the presence of a second SIM card, which can be installed without turning off the device. By the evening, the smartphone was discharged, inserted a SIM card from Nokia into it, and then we stay connected.

Package and package


The phone comes in a bright compact box the size of almost the phone itself. On the one hand, this is good, on the other - we can guess that inside we will see almost nothing.


Indeed, the kit is small: a 220V charger with a 2 mm jack and the simplest WH-109 headset, no answer button, just a microphone.




There is no cable for PC synchronization. The box is made of renewable materials, contains 60% of the materials that are the product of recycling. Packaging is fully recyclable.

Appearance


In the hands of the Asha 210 it feels like a durable candy bar, no crackling and no backlash. Dimensions small 11.5 by 6 cm, thickness 11.8 mm. The entire surface is made of matte plastic, similar to polycarbonate.


The familiar Lumia style is recognized, the colors are especially pleasing. In Russia, it is still sold in black and yellow, blue is expected.


The main glossy part is a navigation block with function keys and a screen. Since the buttons are black, the prints are not visible so much.


The device is perfectly in the hand, there are no sharp corners, it does not slip out of the hands.


At the top there is a 2 mm jack for charging, next to microUSB, by the way, through which you can also charge, then a 3.5 mm mini jack for the headset.


On the left side there is a stub covering the slot for the second SIM card. The device accepts standard mini-SIM . That's all, no you need the volume buttons or the off key.


On the back of the phone is a camera and speaker grille. The lid closes the entire back end and holds onto a large number of snaps, somewhat resembling a Galaxy cover.


The lid is removed simply and very tightly held, nothing staggers. Under the cover we see the battery and just below the speaker.


Under the battery slot for SIM-card and MicroSD, the memory can be increased up to 32 GB.


Keyboard


Of course, it is worthwhile to tell about the Asha 210 keyboard separately. We have a QWERTY keyboard of 37 keys in front of us. In general, the style of previous QWERTY devices, key sizes and stroke are noticeable. All keys are convex with a short stroke and good tactile feedback.


The main characters are black, Russian letters have a grayish tint. Also during illumination, there is a noticeable difference between Russian and Latin letters.


Let's go to the input. Consider a set of Russian text. A new text begins with a capital letter, if you need to forcibly put a capital letter, hold down the Shift (second key from the left) and enter a letter. You can also enable Caps Lock with this key.

Battery


The removable BL-4U battery with a capacity of 1200 mAh gives an unusually long operating time, the phone can quite easily last several days, or even a week, depending on the activity of use. Previously, we somehow freely focused on the charge sticks, but now smartphones are accustomed to accurate percentage data and this is not enough in this phone.


Communications


The phone works only with 2G networks (850/900/1800/1900 MHz), i.e. The maximum speed of the mobile Internet is EDGE 236.8 kbps. On the other hand, the lack of 3G saves us battery power, and high-speed Internet is not needed here, the browser is not so convenient to use it all the time. Two SIM cards are in a call waiting mode. You can call on any SIM-card. As soon as the conversation begins on one SIM-card, the second becomes unavailable.
Wi-Fi can also be used for the Internet, b / g standard networks and all encryption protocols are supported. HotSpot mode is missing.
And of course, Bluetooth, everything gets better when there is Bluetooth . All necessary protocols for data transmission and headsets are supported.
Camera
The camera module is the simplest. 2 megapixels with fixed focus, resolution of pictures 1600 by 1200 pixels, matrix size 1/5 inches, f / 2.8 aperture. There are simple settings, in general, nothing special.


The camera application does not work quickly, pictures can be saved for a few seconds. The "Self-portrait" mode seemed interesting, turn it on, point the camera at yourself and the phone prompts: to the left or to the right, above or below. After your face is in the center of the picture, a shot is taken. Pictures from the gallery can be sent via Bluetooth, E-mail, MMS, Odnoklassniki, VK, FB, Twitter. You can also shoot videos in terrible quality and a minimum resolution of 176 by 144 pixels.

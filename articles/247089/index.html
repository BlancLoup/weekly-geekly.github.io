How to choose key aspects of life in order to invest time and energy in them

Not all aspects of life constantly retain their relevance. In order to correctly choose the main and most relevant of them for the subsequent expenditure of time and energy, Meyer recommends doing the following:

  1. Think of aspects of life as a set of projects. ‚ÄúLooking and understanding your results in this context will help you manage risks. There is one more thing to keep in mind: the key elements are closely interrelated (for example, career and finances, or emotions and entertainment). It would be nice to describe (and then implement the written) possible perspectives, killing, if possible, two birds with one stone.

The practical part: how to invest your time and energy in key aspects?

I hope that I have been able, at least in part, to explain to you the meaning of the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãperception of life through its key aspects. You have divided and determined the significance of your actions with respect to one or another element, what next?

Create a list of key aspects of your life.

What should it look like? According to Meyer, the list should be as simple as possible and easily perceived visually. It is this kind of list that will let you know if you are on the right track.

Add regular aspect list scans to your schedule.

Creating a list of key elements is useless if you are not going to review it regularly. Take it as a rule to return to it once every one or two weeks and choose priority aspects of life for the next time interval. Make sure that you have a list of cases, as well as a diary. In it you will need to write down a million ideas and things that you must implement and a list of people you need to meet.

In order to effectively set the time limits for each key aspect, determine and record in your diary the maximum and minimum time for each of them (especially in cases where there is a lower time limit). Everything should work for the result.

It makes no sense to write lists of the main aspects of life, so as not to use them later.

Focus on the results. It means:

  • Strictly adhere to the established highs and lows.
  • View key aspects to see if you are ignoring any of them, making up the next to-do list.
  • Analyze the list of their key aspects every week in order to timely identify those that need increased attention, as well as to find potential threats and opportunities.
  • To determine for yourself what long-term positive effect you would like to achieve on each of them (key aspects are, in the end, a very useful way to look at your life).

Recently, it was reported that a group of Russian developers was engaged in the creation of devices that can be called "smart", and which are aimed at technology enthusiasts. Their first device was the kettle. Yes, we are accustomed to the electric kettle, which suddenly "wised up."

This advanced kettle was able to take readings on the water level, its temperature, and then transmit this data over the wireless Wi-Fi network to the user. By itself, not directly in the brain, before the teapots, even advanced ones, did not reach. No, the user receives information on the smartphone, good, along with the kettle was created and the corresponding application that allows you to control the unit to boil water completely.

Personally, I really liked the function of maintaining the water temperature at a constant level. We all know how reluctant to rise from the chair / chair, and go to the kitchen to turn off the screaming kettle, which has already lost more than half of its water reserves. If we are talking about an ordinary electric kettle, then it will shut itself off, but now the water will gradually cool down, and when we approach it, after long work, we have to boil again. And here everything is simple - the temperature is set, say, at 64 degrees (of course, before that the water was already boiling), it means that the temperature will be maintained until the owner needs it.



In addition to this function, there are still many "chips" that allow you to make life more convenient and interesting. For example, you can turn on the kettle directly from your smartphone a few minutes before coming home.

  • Of course, in the morning, when you install the appropriate settings, this kettle will meet you with hot water of the desired temperature.
  • The kettle can "advise" what water temperature to use for different types of tea.
  • The kettle can prepare water for infant formula, heating water to 38 degrees.
  • Well, and the kettle will send you a message on the smartphone after it has prepared the water. Or rather, not only on a smartphone, because there are applications for this device under Windows, Linux, Mac.

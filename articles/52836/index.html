UMID mbook - the smallest netbook in the world



The question of the compactness limit of netbooks seems to be resolved. Korean manufacturer UMID, announced the entry into the market netbook mbook - the smallest and lightest laptop in the world.

The weight of the novelty with the battery does not exceed 315 grams - much less. Despite the children's sizes - 158x94x19 mm, the model boasts quite decent parameters. Screen size - 4.8 inches. Resolution - 1024 x 600 pixels. The battery provides up to 6 hours of trouble-free operation.



Like most modern, netbook mbook is built on the basis of Intel Atom. Depending on the configuration, the frequency of the processor ranges from 1.1 to 1.3 gigahertz.

The amount of RAM in 1 GB and 32 GB on the SSD-hard drive is quite enough for typing, browsing the web and receiving TV pictures in the DMB standard. In addition, the device is equipped with 3G modules, Bluetooth 2.0 and WiBro (South Korean version of WiMax), as well as a four-in-one card reader.

The UMID mbook is already on sale in Korea for about $ 534 per share.

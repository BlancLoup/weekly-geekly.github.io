Common misconceptions in project management

When I worked as a programmer, I was often approached with a request to help make the project a share. In some I participated. But very few projects took off.
Over time, I became a project manager, and people still fit. And they make startups around. Units take off.
Why so?


Getting rich quickly and sailing to your own island is simple, alas, only in dreams.

The answer, alas, lies not in the idea, implementation, or even in the market. It is only in the head of the startup creator. Or rather, in the myths with which it is full.

It is the myths that make you think wrong, lead to failure. And the more a person believes in them, the less he is able to change his thinking, to evolve - the higher the chance of failure.

In this article, I have gathered some of the most common myths. And I wrote their refutation, together with real cases, how to really think and act.

These tips can save you from a couple years of wasted time and hundreds of thousands of dollars. Just read, but remember that to know - this does not mean to apply in life. The fact that you need to wash your hands before a meal, many people know, and make a few. With the harm of smoking and alcohol is the same.


In a startup, the most important thing is the idea.


Two questions that cut amateurs from professionals
- What is most important in business - the idea or implementation?
The idea is the wrong answer.

- And if you look for a niche, which one.
Unoccupied is the wrong answer.

I will explain from the point of view of statistics, and then an example from my life.
According to statistics, up to 80% die. Often because their idea does not find a market.
At the same time, startups are still dying in new, emerging markets. For the second reason, the market may not be ready yet, and a startup simply cannot afford to invest in client preparation for the market.

In fact, an idea is a necessary condition. The implementation is sufficient, EVERYTHING depends on the implementation details.
It is much better to take an oak idea and come to a market where there are already competitors (which means there is money). And there to squeeze out a part of the market. Having earned something, it is already possible to open an experimental department for launching startups to take off / not to take off, but with money and with the experience of a businessman.

Of my startups with unique ideas, not one took off. With a non-standard one took off, we transferred the idea from one sphere to another. And typical ideas, such as an outsourcing studio and the development of complex custom-made applications, where there are competitors, have been fed for several years.

Actually, the advice here is simple - you need to look not for an idea, but for a client. And work with the client. And the client (in a broad sense) will already give you ideas from which you will make products.

Need to find an unoccupied niche


Another myth. There are no unoccupied niches. If the market is not busy right now - this does not mean that as soon as you open a successful startup, the competitors will not keep you waiting.

It is much better to immediately prepare for a fight with a competitor, assess your competitive advantages, plan your strategy, taking into account the arrival of competitors.

Real case. We launched a directory and search engine for documents that should be opened by law, but were clamped by one firm. The handbook has become a mega-success and has become a market leader. A year later, it was downloaded by competing forums, and attendance dropped. We did not take into account such a risk, as we were young and green.

Advice - you need to immediately prepare for the fight against a competitor - current or future.

Need to do everything right right away


This is what I ate the dog on. Not a single startup rested in the Bose due to its creators being covered by this myth. A perfectionist disease, briefly.

Almost everyone who makes a startup is somewhat professional: a marketer, programmer, designer. And, trying to protect themselves intuitively from failure, licks up a lot of work: a business plan, design, code, and so on.

Real startups work exactly the opposite. And I am glad that this is already penetrating the industry, but still bad. You need to do MVP - the minimum efficient prototype. And further, on living data to evolve.

We did a city portal for the city of Mytischi in due time. Cool and perfect. At the same time, competitors made a shitty prototype, made patty sites around the cities of Moscow region, evolved faster. And now they make good money on advertising.

It is necessary to overcome the idealist in oneself, and make it simple, by shitting, govnoustka. Quickly start. Simply - because it is fast. Govncode - because in 100% of cases it is necessary to redo it, changing, and govnokod is easier to throw away, and make it faster.

This is a paradox at first, but it works.
When we launched ates.ru , we had a goal - applications from the site. We started with a simple template, without texts on the site. Once 10 have altered the section special offers. They killed 2 weeks for a bunch of different options to drive traffic. As a result, even now the site, six months later, is not perfect, but applications from travel agencies from it went on and on.

If we did it for half a year, it would be too late.

Traffic is most important

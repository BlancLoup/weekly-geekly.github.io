My summer office (beta)


I had the idea for about six months, it appeared while working at home, arranging the workplace , etc.
The main ingredient was already with me: I live in a relatively quiet area, in a semi-mansion with a fenced yard, where there is enough space and a rather pleasant landscape.
The second step - to cover the yard wi-fay . It was completed fairly quickly in the process of deploying a home-coworking net .
The last problem was the furniture, so that it would not be hard to carry out onto the street, it was not a pity to leave it in the rain, well, at least it would be a bit comfortable. With this case, I pulled for a long time, until today =)
Good weather, inspiration and being in the attic of the old table did their job.

I apologize in advance for the bad pictures. There was only an old digital soapbox on hand, and it was very lazy to pull out one of my friends-photographers .
View from the garden.


Your humble servant and his best friend - autotimer :)

So, as I said, the idea lived and developed in my head for a long time. There were a few test outings with a chair, experiments with the network.
In the end, I realized my plans, while in the beta stage :) If things go, maybe I will put the furniture better, attach a carport or something like that.

Pros:
  • It is more pleasant to work than indoors. Fresh air, light breeze, setting. Almost in nature, and at the same time close to home.
  • The yard is fenced, the table is not very visible from the outside. Onlookers do not interfere, in contrast to the work in cafes / parks.
  • Own Wi-Fi :)

Minuses:
  • Weather - you can sit only when she deigns to be good.
  • Sunlight. Although the table is located in the shade, but anyway at noon the screen is pretty much glare.
  • Limited time of the laptop. You can solve the forwarding cable from the mains. But it will need to be either cleaned or protected from rain , etc.
  • Moshkar and figovin falling from the trees. So far, they are not very disturbed, the first ones have not really woken up yet, but I think they will be able to save themselves from ointments / smoke screens / etc.
  • Noise from the roadway. The area is quiet though, but cars can interfere. Saving Headphones

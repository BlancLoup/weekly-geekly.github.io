Call order in the app store. Changing application rating mechanisms

News from the apple company and soar in the air.
In addition to updating the iMac (well, an earlier update of the line of laptops), rumors of iCloud and test builds of Mac OS Lion, there is also new information about the App Store.

Apple put users and creators of applications before the fact of significant changes in the use of the App Store. Changes aimed at restoring order in the app store, primarily affected the appraisal evaluation mechanisms and the purchase of additional functionality in the latter.

I ask those interested under the cat.

Act one :
Users can no longer rate and write reviews to applications loaded by the promo code. In fact, with this, Apple knocks the foundation out from under the feet of developers - amateurs who wind up the rating of their application, in a not too honest way, through fake accounts.
In principle, only dishonest developers suffer from this maneuver, and users benefit.
Act Two :
Cancel the practice of downloading additional programs to get bonuses.

In general, this is another step towards good quality control of applications. But there is one nuance, just this method was used by some normal developers, and for them this method of earning was a way that brought the main profit.
Well, in general, aside from not very honest developers, potentially, developers of normal applications, who either have to look for alternative sources of earnings on iOS, or go on Android, are potentially suffering. Ordinary users, as it were, mostly win, but it seems to me that options are possible here.

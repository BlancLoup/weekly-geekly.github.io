Turkish company has developed real autobot transformers



Transforming robots became mainstream long before the first movie about them came out in 2007. For several decades, the most popular goods in toy stores have been them - anthropomorphic robots transforming into cars and other mechanisms. Some of these toys can be called high-tech - they can turn into a car, a dinosaur or a plane by pressing a button. The advanced ones have electronic components and a remote control system.

Enthusiasts for the love of robots turned their cars into something similar to the transformers. But such projects are technically difficult to implement, so they are few. Now the Turkish company Letvision decided to correct this defect. Her specialization is the creation of real cars that can drive on roads, and which at the touch of a button turn into Letrons robots.

And this is not at all a concept - the company showed working prototypes of transforming robots. One of them is based on the platform of the 2013 BMW. The authors of the idea take a real car and modify it, turning it into a robotic system. Car models can be almost any. Perhaps there are some limitations, but so far the authors of the project have not reported anything about this.


There is, however, one problem. The fact is that you can only drive such a car remotely. There is simply no room inside the cabin. And there is no salon, per se. All internal space is occupied by electronic components and hydraulics. In the near future, developers are planning to solve the problem with the dimensions of the electronic-hydraulic system of the robot. In this case, the robot can be controlled in the folded state by a person who is in the cabin itself.

According to the company, robots will be able to move independently after completion. Transformer models (there are four of them) can and should be improved, according to the developers. But for this you need to carry out additional work, which requires funds. The process of transforming a car into a robot with reverse transformation is possible thanks to a powerful hydraulic system of 35,000 N divided into several auxiliary systems. The engine system with a torque of 400 Nm takes up little space.


It is controlled by a transformer remotely using a wireless system. The data transfer rate over the Wi-Fi channel is 54 Mbps (802.11a standard). The robot is equipped with a video camera that transmits real-time video to the operator. The system has sensors that allow the robot to respond to sound. On the "belly" of the robot developers have placed the display. With it, a transformer can communicate with people, showing important information for the operator. Front and rear security sensors are located. If the computer detects an obstacle, the robot stops. The transformer receives energy from batteries with a 4 kW inverter.

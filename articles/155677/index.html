Motivation in the classics



You are all certainly familiar with Maslow's pyramid:



The basis of the theory of motivation (pyramid) Maslow is the thesis that human behavior is determined by a number of basic needs that can be built in a certain hierarchy. From the point of view of Maslow, these needs are universal, i.e. unite all people regardless of color, nationality, lifestyle, habits, manners, and other external manifestations. Maslow's hierarchy of needs is built on the principle of the urgency of their satisfaction for man.

If you look closely at this image, the "development", "personal growth" are at the very top of the pyramid. This can simply explain the fact why very few people really steal on the topic of personal and personal growth. After all, you still need to reach the top of the pyramid ...

Development and testing of software in the "clouds"

According to statistics, a large enterprise usually allocates up to 50% of the resources of its internal technological infrastructure for developing and testing its own software. At the same time, up to 90% of these resources remain untapped. On the basis of this, we can talk about the low efficiency of this process and increased costs. How to avoid it?

As one of the options you can suggest the use of technology Cloud Computing. Taking advantage of this technology in development and application testing environments can help reduce labor costs by half, improve application quality and dramatically shorten the time-to-market for software products.

It was with this option that IBM came out to its customers, which, in other matters, is not surprising. The company has long been delivering cloud-based solutions for software developers and it was only a matter of time when customers would be given the opportunity to develop and test software applications in their proprietary IBM Cloud cloud environment.

In early March, this happened. A new IBM Smart Business Development and Test on the IBM Cloud was introduced, which enables corporate customers to expand their internal software development and testing processes with instant access to computing resources through IBM's secure scalable model for delivering cloud services, as well as using software IBM product lifecycle management capabilities.

The new IBM offer allows customers, first, to shorten the cycle time for preparation and provision of work (provision cycle) from weeks to minutes; secondly, to eliminate up to 30% of errors of the new software; thirdly, to reduce the time required for application testing and quality assurance; and fourthly, to rapidly deploy environments across multiple IT projects.

Anyone can program at any age.

Mark Zuckerberg, Bill Gates, Jack Dorsey and others starred in the commercial of the non-profit foundation CodeOrg to inspire children to start programming.

The stars of Silicon Valley talk about their first programming experience and think about it, so that if they could start programming at school.
CodeOrg calls to learn about the new "superpower", which is not taught in 90% of American schools.



Code.org is a non-profit foundation founded by brothers Ali and Hadi Partovi . The purpose of the organization is to promote the study of programming in schools. On the website, any student can start programming lessons, and computer science teachers can leave a request if they want such courses to start at his school.

Proceed to the lessons without registration. The site has links to Scratch, you can study under the programs of Codeacademy, Khan Academy or CodeHS.



The foundation itself includes in its tasks:

  • To convey information that there is a shortage of programmers all over the world, and learning to program is much easier than you think.
  • Create an authoritative database of all schools where you can learn programming: whether it is online courses, ordinary schools or summer camps.

Our vision is that every student in every school should have the opportunity to learn programming. We consider computer science and programming to be part of the main curriculum, along with courses such as biology, physics, chemistry and algebra.
You can leave a signature on the site if you think that students of all schools should be able to program. All of this applies, however, to the United States.

The project can be helped with money or time. If you are a programmer and are willing to spend several hours a week for free, for example, checking students' tasks, you can leave your coordinates and promise to contact you.

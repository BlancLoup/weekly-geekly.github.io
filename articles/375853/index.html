Review NVIDIA Shield. Play bye young

Cross gamepad, tablet and console. Who could have guessed such a thing? No, not Apple, and the controls are not virtual. This is, of course, about NVIDIA SHIELD: the first truly gaming console based on the Android OS.



The device is very niche, and, at the same time, it has serious competitors. On the one hand, there are the usual portable consoles - PSP and Vita, a family of Nintendo DS. On the other hand, the nastier Chinese have already had plenty of time to produce Android consoles in the PSP format. All of them are clumsy in various ways (someone has obsolete hardware, someone has a disgusting screen), but among these products you can find powerful, bright and fast devices with a good battery and good build quality. On the third hand, oddly enough, ‚ÄúApple‚Äù and their iOS devices, to which vendors came up with various trailing joystick gamepads, and touch control, as a rule, is implemented more or less in iOS games.



In such tough competition, Nvidia needed to create something really impressive, so as not to look like a gray mouse against the background of alternatives. Did it work out?

Appearance and usability


For the appearance of the console, I must say thanks to the "three whales." The first is the first-generation Sony Playstation console, the creators of which presented the world with one of the most popular gamepads - DualShock.



Its form and possibilities with minor improvements have been living for nearly two decades. Two analog ‚Äústick‚Äù, cross, four buttons, one ‚Äútrigger‚Äù on each side + one ‚Äútrigger‚Äù. And, of course, vibromotors. The modern version of the Dualshock 4 has gone even further, and there are even more features in it, but this is not about it today.





The second whale was the XBox - the name of the buttons, the Xinput standard, the approach to the console as a universal entertainment device and a home media center directly influenced both the design of accessories and the overall control system.



The third whale is a portable set-top box. The developer took into account all the capabilities of competitors and worked hard to play was really comfortable. And most importantly - to have something to play.

Several types of plastic are used as materials: ordinary black / gray for the case, semi-glossy for buttons, rubberized in those places where you will hold on to the device. Despite the glossy inserts, the device turned out to be very resistant to the appearance of fingerprints (and with similar gadgets, oh, how sweaty hands!).

The screen is designed as a hinged cover: and the console is protected from dust and dirt on the road, and the display itself will not be scratched. Full angle of inclination is 180 degrees, but if you wish, you can customize it "by yourself".



On the back side all the connectors and ports are located, of which there are significantly more than we used to see in mobile devices.



In hand


Now let's talk about the convenience of management. By itself, the console weighs a decent 500 grams: thanks to the batteries at 7350 mAh (28.8 W / h), a rather large case and advanced controls. On the one hand, such weight and solidity cause only approval, on the other hand, it will be somewhat problematic to hold the device in hands for a long time. However, we are talking about the position of "standing" (for example, in the subway) - if you have to put your hands (table, knees), then the problem will disappear.



The sticks are fairly soft and precise, all the buttons are in familiar places. He was also taken care of by the manufacturer and additional controls for controlling the Android OS system and games, for which he is plus in karma: for example, the right stick moves the cursor around the screen, while the left one jumps separately on the screen elements. Everything is pressed easily and confidently, analog controls are also made on a solid top five.

In general, everything is great with both the design and the convenience of control, and this is already pleasing. We turn to the stuffing.

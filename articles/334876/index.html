Graphics evolution in games


For many years, computer games have been the main engine of progress for both the central processors and the graphics subsystems. But lately, the growth rate of computing power and the introduction of new features have slowed down a lot. Probably, the thing is that the vast majority of gamers are quite satisfied with the current quality and realistic game graphics. Let's see this Friday how the graphics have changed in games for 45 years. Attention! The amount of video and nostalgia inside is rolls over.


Spacewar! (1962)


One of the first computer graphics games in history. It was written for PDP-1 computers. The gameplay was simple: two players controlled spacecraft, and tried to hit each other with rockets. In addition, it was necessary to avoid a collision with a star that attracted players' ships to itself. Ammunition and fuel for maneuvering were limited. The game was very popular in the 1960s, and was ported to various platforms.

Era of arcades


Pong (1972)


Primitive arcade table tennis simulator. The first game created by Atari, and in combination one of the most popular video games of that time. Here, two players controlled the "rackets" by turning the knobs, trying not to miss the ball. There was a single player mode. There were versions of the game for the set-top box and in the form of a gaming machine .



Space Race (1973)


This is the second after Pong game developed by Atari. For two or one player. It was necessary to fly on rockets, dodging obstacles. Who did not have time to evade - lost. Produced in the form of set-top box and gaming machine.



TV Basketball (1974)


This game was the first in three "nominations":


  • the first ever basketball game;
  • the first game in which sprites are used;
  • The first game in which the image of people appeared.

Purpose of the game: moving the "basketball players" to beat the ball so that it falls into the basket.



Gun Fight (1975)


Game-Western, in which it was necessary to defeat the enemy in a duel with pistols. The first game, which depicts the battle of man with man. Each player had two joysticks: one was controlled by a character, and the second was a pistol. The number of rounds was limited - 6 per drum.

Canyon Bomber (1977)


One or two players enthusiastically flew over a canyon filled with spherical stones. They had to be bombed, and the more accurate the hit was, the more points you earned.

Hello! February 27, we invite practitioners in Data Science to a mitap dedicated to user personalization. Under the cut - theses of speeches, links to the registration and video broadcast mitap.



Reports


Modeling user preferences in multimodal data. Hady W. Lauw, Maxim Tkachenko (Singapore Management University)



We often encounter a huge amount of information, interacting with search engines, e-commerce systems, social networks and so on. Considering that only a part of this information will be of interest to a specific user, it is necessary to personalize interaction with such systems, recommending to users in advance information that potentially matches their preferences.
The key to good recommendations is modeling huge amounts of behavioral data that arise as a result of user interaction with online systems. The problem here is that these interactions are multimodal, that is, composed of various types of data, such as user ratings, reviews, photos, or their social interactions. In this report we will talk about data mining and machine learning methods for modeling user preferences in multimodal data and their use in creating a complete recommender system.


This report will be in English.

How to get to ITMO University Technopark

The ITMO University not only prepares specialists in the field of IT, optics (and other fields), but also helps promising companies obtain funding, conduct research and bring a complex technological project from the first prototype to a working business.

In this material, we will describe how the Technopark works, how to get its support, and what it gives to the company - on the example of resident projects.

Workshop laboratory FabLab - a division of Technopark

Who comes to the technopark


According to Olesya Baranyuk, deputy director of Technopark, the profile of the university (information and photon technologies) creates a focus for resident companies - businesses associated with scientific developments come to Technopark first of all. But not only in the field of informatics and optics - now the Technopark hosts projects from the following areas:

  • AR / VR
  • Robotics
  • AI systems
  • Microelectronics
  • Nanotechnology

Recently, more companies developing in the field of Life Science have started coming, and it is very interesting for us to cooperate with them. We already have companies that create solutions in the field of biopharmaceuticals, biomedical technologies and services, bioinformatics technologies, medical instrumentation and healthcare, and in the near future we plan to expand the presence of such companies.

- Olesya Baranyuk, ITMO University Technopark

The managers of the following projects took part in the creation of today's material.

Ontodia


Ontodia is a platform for visualizing and exploring graph-based data. Graph data is data built on the model of vertices and the connections between them. The project also works with the most well-known graph data store - Neo4j. One of the examples of projects using Ontodia technology is the Business Topology service . The Ontodia project is at the stage of a market product, which continues to be improved and improved.

At the time of becoming a resident, our project was at the initial stage. In principle, there was only a basic idea and nothing more.

- Dmitry Pavlov, commercial director of VISmart (the company-creator of the Ontodia platform)

odgAssist


The odgAssist project helps manufacturing (in particular, pharmaceutical) companies create new products and bring them to market faster. Namely - allows you to automate the work of staff at the lower level (through the use of wearable devices for data collection and monitoring processes for compliance, in particular, in the format of the employee‚Äôs ‚Äúworking day photo‚Äù). Such an approach allows reducing the speed of launching an innovative product to the market by 20% and seriously save on expenses. The company has first sales.

Orbi


Orbi company is developing the world's first glasses for shooting video in 360-degree format. These are sunglasses, in which 4 cameras are built in, allowing you to shoot a full panorama in 360 format. The project conducted a successful crowdfunding campaign on Indiegogo, and also attracted significant funding from private investors. At the moment, Orbi has developed unique software for pasting panoramas from 4 video streams, running on main desktop and mobile platforms and allowing to get high-quality panoramic video in fully automatic mode (without user intervention). At the moment, the prototype of the device in the final design went into production. It will be ready by mid-November 2017.


[At the time of appearance in Technopark] we were at the stage of active software development, although our development team consisted of only three programmers. We also actively sought a suitable solution for the device itself (processor, optics, sensors, and so on) and the manufacturer who can make this device. At that time, we had already launched a campaign on Indigegogo.

The company is engaged in the development and implementation of integrated biomedical solutions based on high-performance genome sequencing technology. Parseq Lab develops reagent kits and data analysis software. The company's developments are used in medicine, forensics, research in Russia and abroad. A series of products has already been introduced to the market, some developments are at the design or testing stage.

[The state at the time of obtaining the resident status:] the pilot development entered the market.

- Alexander Pavlov, Director of Parseq Lab

How to become a resident


An application for resident status can be submitted on the Technopark website at any time. The expert council reviews it for 2-3 days, after which it is decided whether to take the project into the ITMO ‚Äúfamily‚Äù or not. This decision is followed by a meeting with representatives of Technopark.

Who becomes resident:

  • Technological projects (initial communication with ITMO University, optional presence of scientists or students of the University)
  • Small Innovative Enterprises (ITMO University is a co-founder of such enterprises, they are located in the Technopark as a priority)
  • Corporate research centers

Quite often, large corporations turn to us with the request to place their R & D centers in Technopark in order to be able to be in the epicenter of innovation activity, cooperate with the University research units and technopark residents, and also gradually form a portfolio of innovative products.

- Olesya Baranyuk, ITMO University Technopark

What experts pay attention when selecting applications:

  • Innovative component
  • Competitive advantages over peers
  • Understanding the market, the availability of first sales
  • The ability to create places for students of the ITMO University to practice with subsequent employment

Olesya Baranyuk advises to move quickly from plans to actions: to shorten less, not to be afraid, to create a team that will share your values.

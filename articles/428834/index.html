Jaguar I-PACE - took the 5th place among all electric vehicles in October 2018.

Norwegian electric cars policy
The main provisions to stimulate the development of electric transport (year of introduction):
  • no taxes on purchases or imports (1990);
  • exemption from 25% VAT (2001);
  • low tolls (1996);
  • there is no charge on toll roads and in case of a ferry (1997 and 2009);
  • free parking in municipal parking lots (1999);
  • the ability to drive on roads for public transport (2005);
  • 50% tax reduction for transport companies (2000);
  • exemption from 25% VAT on leasing (2015).

This incentive program will be reviewed and updated depending on market development. Tax breaks will be until 2018, after which they will be revised. From 2017, the local authorities are reviewing the following issues: access to roads for public transport and free parking in municipal parking lots. Free access to toll roads is likely to be replaced by a new discount system with differentiated prices based on CO2 and nitrogen oxide emissions.

Policies also set economic incentives for low-emission and low-emission vehicles through low taxes, and high-emission vehicles for high-emission vehicles.

The Norwegian Parliament has set a goal - all new cars sold from 2025 must be electric cars without (rechargeable batteries or fuel cells) or low (plug-in hybrids) emissions. This is an ambitious goal, but reachable, if you pursue the right policy. This goal will be achieved by increasing taxes for cars with emissions, rather than a complete ban.

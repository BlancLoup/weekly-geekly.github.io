How can a company pay 133 times less taxes


The topic that I would like to highlight today is quite complex and voluminous, so I had to work hard to present it in a more or less clear and understandable way. Those who get to the end of the article will find out the answer to the question in the title. Believe me, there it is.

Taxation Ltd. in the IT-sphere has its own specifics, which significantly distinguishes it from taxes of individual entrepreneurs (see the previous article ‚Äú How do individual entrepreneurs save taxes 13.3 times ‚Äù). Here are five main differences that new business people should be aware of.


1. A non-performing LLC may not pay taxes and fees.


If your company really does not work after registration, for some time you can not make payments to the budget. To do this, the following conditions must be met:


  • There is no taxable property (real estate and transportation) on the company's balance sheet;
  • the director is not appointed to the state (allowed for the sole founder founder ) or is on unpaid leave, and there are no other employees;
  • It was not announced about the transition to UTII (in this mode, the tax must be paid regardless of the activity).

The duration of such ‚Äúanabiosis‚Äù is not limited by law, just do not forget to hand over zero reports. If an LLC during the year does not show signs of life (there is no movement on the accounts and reporting is not given), then according to Art. 21.1 of the Law "On State Registration" N 129-FZ, it is recognized as actually ceasing its activities and receives the status of inactive. Such a legal entity may be excluded from the Unified State Register on the initiative of the tax inspectorate, although in practice this happens rarely.


In a similar situation, a non-working individual entrepreneur should still make compulsory insurance premiums for himself (in 2017, 27,990 rubles).


2. When receiving income from a business, the founder of an LLC pays a tax on dividends


PIT from dividends is not directly related to taxes LLC, but for the founder - it is also taxes related to the activities of his company, so they must be taken into account when calculating the tax burden. The rate of dividends for individuals does not depend on the taxation system of the LLC and is:


  • 13% for residents of the Russian Federation;
  • 15% for non-residents of the Russian Federation.

The resident status is not related to the presence of Russian citizenship and is determined in the last 12 months before dividend payments. If during this period you were on the territory of Russia for a total of not less than 183 days, then you are recognized as a resident of the Russian Federation, if less, then no. A good reason for not taking into account the time of your absence will be treatment or training in another country (for a full list of reasons, see article 207 of the Tax Code of the Russian Federation).


The sole proprietor does not pay any additional income when receiving income from the business and is not bound by the conventions established for the distribution of dividends.


3. Organizations have less tax benefits than individual entrepreneurs


Judging by the size of the fines and the rigor of the inspectors, the state believes that legal entities do business more successfully than the SP, and their profits are higher. Accordingly, the tax benefits of the LLC is less:


  • Only the individual entrepreneurs can work within the tax holidays (two years at zero rate on the simplified taxation system or PSN), although the authorities have promised to introduce them for all small businesses, regardless of their legal form.
  • Organizations cannot buy a patent (PSN), which in some cases turns out to be the most profitable option for the tax burden. There are no justified reasons for this, therefore, periodically, a business is being discussed, but the officials are not meeting them.
  • The income tax rate for LLC on OSNO is generally equal to 20% against 13% of personal income tax for IP on OSNO. However, in some regions (Chelyabinsk, Novosibirsk, Penza and Ulyanovsk regions) the rate is reduced by regional laws to 15.5%, but only for accredited IT companies.

4. IT organizations pay insurance premiums for employees at reduced rates.


In general, the rate of contributions for employees (for their pension, medical and social insurance) is 30%. In 2017, the reduced rate for IT organizations is 14%, in 2018 - 21%, in 2019 - 28%.


Reduced tariffs are not relied upon by all companies in the IT sphere, but only by those who fulfill the conditions:


  • at least 90% of the proceeds are derived from the development and implementation of developed programs, databases or services for their adaptation, modification, installation, testing, maintenance;
  • number of employees at least 7 people;
  • accreditation in the Ministry of Communications .

For SP-employers engaged in similar activities, these benefits do not apply.


5. In the USN regimes, revenues and UTII of an organization can reduce the calculated tax by no more than 50%.


This refers to a benefit, which allows you to reduce tax payments by the amount of insurance premiums paid. Any LLC is automatically recognized as an employer, so even if only the founding director is appointed to the staff, the tax can still be reduced by no more than half.


An individual entrepreneur without workers can reduce the tax on the full amount of the contributions paid for himself, so with a small income tax there will be no tax payable at all.

For an example of calculating the tax burden in different modes, let us take an LLC in the city of Tula, which provides computer repair services only to individuals.

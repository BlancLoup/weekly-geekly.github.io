WebRTC is now on mobile (for now only Android)

Google has released a beta version of Google Chrome with WebRTC support, although support is hidden behind a special flag in chrome: // flags, but desktop WebRTC also appeared that way, so it's just a matter of time and debugging, and then the function will be enabled by default. We, in turn, could not resist and tested this miracle of technology on Google Nexus 4, the video is available under the cut.


As you can see from the video, WebRTC is already functioning perfectly well in the mobile beta version of Chrome. Now it remains the case for Apple, since this functionality is clearly not superfluous in iOS. For our part, we will optimize the interfaces for touch screens, as well as work on supporting WebRTC implementations in other browsers. Already, Zingaya is working in Firefox Nightly (or Aurora), waiting for 21 versions of Firefox to go into production and it can be said with confidence that more than half of the market uses WebRTC.

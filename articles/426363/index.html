Materiel


How is the separation of the side blocks (first stage) from the central (second stage)? This process is described in most detail and clearly on the KIK website of the USSR . In a brief retelling, it consists of the following steps.


Start with Kuru, ESA video

First, the engines of the side blocks are transferred to the mode of low thrust, and the steering engines are turned off. Further, the lower links of the side blocks are broken, and since the direction of thrust of the engines of the side blocks is specially made not coinciding with their axis, the lower parts of the side blocks begin to diverge to the sides.


Screenshot Simulator Orbiter

Then their engines are turned off, and since the second stage engine is running, the side blocks are lagging behind, are shifted back and come out of the upper mounts.


Top mount, photo KIK USSR


The red circle in the center is the valve cap of the oxidizer tank, photo by KIK USSR

When the side blocks depart a sufficient distance, the valve of the oxidizer tank opens, the jet jet from which twists the blocks. Well, in the last turn, the valve of the fuel tank opens, a jet of pressurized gas from which it additionally twists and leads away the stage. It is important to note that the upper part of the blocks passes a rather complicated path - first along the step down, then to the side.

Two more questions remain unclear - why did the accident signal appear at 160 seconds, and did the emergency rescue system work?

About the work of SED


According to the cyclogram, the emergency rescue system bar is reset to 114 seconds. But the CAC of the Soyuz ship consists of two parts - a rod and engines on the head fairing.


SAS scheme of the Soyuz spacecraft, illustration of a corporation of tactical rocket weapons

From -15 minutes to 114 seconds in the event of an accident, the engines on the rod and head fairing are triggered (from 4:45, RDG work is clearly visible at 4:52).


From 114 seconds to 157 seconds in the event of an accident, the ship is diverted on the RDG engines in the head fairing.


Screenshot Simulator Orbiter

And in the event of an accident after 157 seconds, when the head fairing is reset, it is separated from the third stage by regular pyrobolts, as if it went normally into orbit.

Accordingly, once the accident occurred at 119 seconds, the head fairing has not yet been reset. And according to the information from the Cosmonautics News Forum, when the first stage is separated, the automatic control system of the SAS is turned off for 6 seconds so that the control system of the launch vehicle can cope with disturbances. When SAS switched on again at 123 seconds, it estimated the position of the rocket as abnormal and tore the ship from it. The connection was lost, because the power supply and equipment remained in the instrument-aggregate compartment, which the CAC does not save. And already in the region of 160 seconds, when the descent vehicle was dropped from under the head fairing, its autonomous communication system was turned on, which is used during landing during landing. This, by the way, explains the accident information that appeared in the media at 123 seconds.

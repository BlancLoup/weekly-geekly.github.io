Why and why you need to install an open id on your wordpress blog

To run your blog on wordpress is a series of advantages :
- full control over the design and functionality
- the opportunity to earn by placing ads and so on.

and cons :
- no one will ever see your blog unless you take care of it
- no friends to help you make your blog popular. For example, in LiveJournal you will give a few people a refund, they will respond too. Such links will give an influx of readers. C stand-alone blog is different.

There are two ways to attract a user to your blog:
1. Use services and programs for cross-posting, that is, you write in your wordpress blog and the same entry is added to your blogs in LJ, Li.ru, and so on. Plus, of course, in reaching the audience. The main drawback is that it is difficult to attract a person directly to your blog, where there is a lot of interesting things, including your design, functionality and content.

2. The second way is to put open id on your blog and post comments on behalf of your blog. When you click on your nickname in the comments, the person will go exactly to your domain. The costs are great - you need to comment a lot to get readers, but everyone will see only your blog.

To install open id on your blog, you need:
Download two wordpress plugin, install them on wordpress and activate.
http://wordpress.org/extend/plugins/openid/
http://wordpress.org/extend/plugins/xrds-simple/

Next you need to enter the blog platform you need, for example, livejournal.com and log in using open id (the domain of your blog). After the authorization procedure and specifying your mail you will be able to comment. But you can comment on other people's magazines. ‚Äù

On the other hand, comments are quite enough to keep in touch with the bloggers you like from LiveJournal.

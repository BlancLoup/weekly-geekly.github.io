The world's first 128GB flash drive



About flash drives in general is not interesting to write, but here the case, or rather, the volume is special. Kingston said that the first in the world released a 128GB flash drive.

The drive is called DataTraveler 200 will be delivered only on pre-orders and costs 546 dollars. The lineup also presents two smaller options that differ in the color of the case: 64GB yellow and 32GB blue. They cost, respectively, 213 and 120 dollars.

Of the features of the drive, you can note support for Windows ReadyBoost technology and password protection of information. If the details are interesting, here lies the official press release.

Lechal insoles smart insoles overview


Lechal insoles smart insoles are an interesting system that can indicate to its owner the right direction of movement. A small device is built into the insoles that can not only indicate the direction, but also work as a fitness tracker, counting calories and distance traveled.

Of course, all data is transmitted wirelessly to an application for a mobile device, where all this can be viewed. Now we publish a review of the details of the work of this unusual gadget.

How to charge


Compact unit with a bottom plug for charging from 220 V, which is rolled in (to save space). On each side there are 2 indentations where one small removable lithium-polymer battery is inserted. Front is a USB-connector. Above the red indicator in the form of the manufacturer's logo.

During charging, the red indicator flashes, which will be lit continuously when charging is complete. The batteries allow the device to work 3 days in active use mode (3 hours of navigation + 12 hours of fitness mode) and up to 10 days in standby mode (fitness mode). From the box


In addition to the charger and 2 removable batteries, the kit includes:

  • Actually, 2 insoles. They are quite comfortable, thin, correspond to European sizes of shoes.
  • 2 modules with Bluetooth 2.1 + EDR class 2 sensor, GPS
  • navigator and vibration sensors, both modules are inserted into a special compartment of the insoles;
  • user's manual.

The program Lechal app has a clear and simple interface. With it, you can synchronize information about the distance traveled, average and maximum speeds, the number of steps taken, the number of calories burned and the time of walking.

The presence of a GPS navigator in the insoles allows you to know the direction of a predetermined route not only by using the information display of a mobile device, but also by using vibration signals that are sent by the left or right vibration sensor of the insoles, depending on whether you want to turn left or right, which is important for people with visual impairment. When deviating from the route, completing free or targeted walks, and other events, the vibration sensors will let you know about this by triggering at the same time and giving different types of vibration signals for various events.

When you synchronize the device, it connects to the Bluetooth modules of the insoles, first to the left, then to the right.

Thingiverse Service - Universe for 3d printing



When it comes to 3D printers and 3D printing, the following questions usually arise:

  1. Where is free download 3d model for printing?
  2. Why do they use 3D printing at all , what can be useful and interesting to do with it?
  3. With what programs, preferably free, you can create a 3D model ?

These questions can be answered using the service directory of 3D models - Thingiverse .

In this post we will do a small review of 3D models from Thingiverse (grouped by the programs with the help of which they were created). As an example, the header provides a designer from 3D printed parts, created using 123D Design . More details about this constructor, and other examples of 3D models from Sketchup , OpenSCAD , Blender3d , FreeCAD - under the cut.

In the beginning, a little about the service itself, and the specifics associated with 3D printing.



Thingiverse is a free service opened in 2008, developed and maintained by MakerBot (which in turn creates and sells a well-known 3D printer), anyone can register on this service and upload a 3d model for downloading. A key feature of the service is that all downloaded models are published under the Creative Commons license , which means that you can freely and freely download and also freely change.

The generally accepted format for 3D printing is STL (short for STereoLithography), this format contains only the shape of an object, without color, texture information, in fact it is a surface composed of triangles. It is in this format that you need to export 3D models if you plan to print them on a 3D printer.

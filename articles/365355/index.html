Uber is looking for engineers for autonomous car

While the head of Lyft is denying unmanned vehicles , Uber, the world's largest taxi search and taxi service, hires engineers in the fields of robotics, machine learning, communications, traffic simulation, vehicle testing, hardware development and software.



In February 2015, Uber, in partnership with Carnegie Mellon University, opened a research center in Pittsburgh. The list of vacancies in this center has been updated: nineteen new positions have been opened. The center is looking for specialists in robotics, computer vision, the development of hardware platforms and software, traffic simulation, engineers with knowledge of modern automotive electronics and diagnostics. It is not yet clear whether Uber will develop its own autonomous vehicle, or whether it will work on the autopilot system and merge with the existing car manufacturer.

Creating a platform for an unmanned vehicle can take years. At the same time, Uber will have to compete with GM, BMW, Toyota, Google and other developers, so some experts are confident that Uber will join one of the automakers. The emergence of a new player in the field of the structure of robotic cars, especially having considerable financial capabilities, can bring the era of unmanned vehicles closer.

The audience of the DuckDuckGo search engine has grown 7 times thanks to Snowden

The DuckDuckGo search engine, which has the main goal of protecting the privacy of users, has been running for six years. But the most significant jump in attendance at the DDG came in the last two years, after the publication of Edward Snowden's documents on NSA spying. At this point, the audience began to increase dramatically, which is still happening.

Since that time, the number of searches increased by 600% , that is, seven times.

This was told by executive director Gabe Weinberg (Gabe Weinberg) in an interview with CNBC.



According to DDG statistics, 3 billion search queries are processed annually. One of the best days of 2013 was Friday immediately after the publication of the first Snowden documents on the PRISM surveillance program with the participation of Google, when DDG processed 2.54 million requests in 24 hours. Since then, growth has continued. Now the search engine for the first time is approaching the mark of 10 million searches per day.



Such an increase in popularity should not be surprising. Judging by polls, most users are annoyed by the fact that they are being watched by the search engine. For example, a Pew survey showed that 40% of Americans do not want a search engine to keep search history, and even more so use it for advertising purposes. DuckDuckGo does not do this, unlike Google.

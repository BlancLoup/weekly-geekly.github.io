Microsoft opens its search for books

Microsoft announces the opening of Live Search Books . Users can now use the beta version of the search for books.

The creators of the search engine report that tens of thousands of publications provided by the University of California, the University of Toronto, the British Library, and others have been scanned to create the Live Search Books. Cooperation agreements have also been concluded with the New York Public Library and the American Museum of Veterinary Medicine.

The main competitor of Microsoft in this area is Google Book Search .

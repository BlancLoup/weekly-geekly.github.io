MWC 2016: HP Elite X3 with support for Windows 10 Mobile and MS Continuum OS officially introduced

Welcome readers of the blog iCover ! Another challenge to our usual understanding of the capabilities of modern mobile ecosystems was attempted by engineers at HP. This challenge was the flagship HP Elite X3 running Windows 10 Mobile with MS Continuum support, announced as part of MWC 2016. How convincing was the presentation of the IT giant and what prospects for the proposed new products we propose to discuss in our today's publication.



HP Elite X3 is an effective and comprehensive business-oriented solution for the global corporate market. This is how the official representatives of HP have positioned their device at the presentation. And although the proposed solution eliminates the increased user requirements for multimedia capabilities, at the same time, it really allows in many cases to completely dispense with a traditional laptop or PC. The successful solution of the overwhelming majority of office tasks, according to HP, is also possible with the help of a phablet working in a single complex with MS Continuum and accessories, announced during the same presentation.

As representatives of HP noted, in the light of current trends, using the Internet, users en masse are increasingly shifting from computers and laptops to smartphones and phablets. In this case, miniature screens of compact devices in most cases quite well cope with the task of providing the user with the necessary information, but limit the process of creating the desired content. Part of the problem is solved through cloud technologies, but, according to HP, it is much more efficient to use for processing data arrays just one device that has taken on the mission of the center of the user ecosystem.



Three problems HP Elite X3 is designed to solve


Based on the concept according to which HP is positioning a new product, the proposed solution will allow forgetting at least three topical issues for corporate users:

1. According to statistics, to solve operational problems at work, an employee of the company today uses, on average, three different devices. Most often it is a smartphone, laptop and tablet. When using devices for their intended purpose, they will most likely need to be synchronized, not to mention auxiliary, but necessary accessories, such as trivial chargers. Often, all this tangible in weight and dimensions kit, guided by various considerations, office staff have to carry.

2. The more devices involved in building a corporate network, the higher the vulnerability of the latter, the more complex and expensive the algorithms for protecting confidential data and maintaining an adequate level of security of the production infrastructure.

3. An employee who uses desktop corporate applications, most of which are optimized for mouse and keyboard, encounters problems when forced to use them on relatively small touch screens of smartphones.

On the other hand, literally in the last few years, the possibilities of mobile technologies and the speed of network connections have increased significantly. A new generation of smartphones in many key parameters are increasingly reducing the distance to existing average desktop solutions. The outlined problems, on the one hand, and the outlined progress, on the other, created the prerequisites for finding fundamentally new solutions. This decision, according to HP, will be presented recently Elite X3.

Metamorphosis with docks


The start of sales of HP Elite X3 in the global market, the company plans for the summer of 2016. According to representatives of HP, corporate customers, thus, will have the opportunity to accept and evaluate the prospects of the concept of a multifunctional computing device, implemented in Elite X3. Most likely, in fact, we are talking about a timeout taken by HP to popularize the capabilities of Microsoft‚Äôs not so familiar Continuum technology. It is on MS Continuum, and not on the hardware merits of the new flagship HP puts the main emphasis.

If it is quite simple, the technology allows smartpad (phablet), using the interface of the Continuum, to connect to the dock and work with an external monitor, keyboard and mouse. In the first device with Continuum support, the Microsoft Lumia 950 smartphone from Microsoft, the device was connected to the dock via cable. The version implemented by HP in the case of the Elite X3 involves installing the device directly into the HP Desk Dock.



The HP Desk Dock is a miniature cradle designed to stream the screen of a smartphone to a projector or monitor and work with them within the capabilities of the MS Continuum interface. On the back of the dock are two USB-A ports, a USB-C port, a DisplayPort for connecting a monitor, and an Ethernet port for wired internet. Despite its small size, the chrome-plated dock is surprisingly heavy, which significantly complicates its carrying and allows it to be positioned as a base station, constantly standing firmly next to the monitor at home or in the workplace. At the same time, this gravity has its plus: when working with a smartphone connected to the dock in portrait mode (with or without a cover), the device remains stationary and does not risk falling onto the floor, as is the case with the connection option with a fairly rigid cable using Microsoft Lumia 950.

After connecting to the Continuum interface, the user has at his disposal a kind of desktop analogue. At the same time, it is important to note that at the current stage no tiled multi-windowing is provided on the screen - the user sees in front of him and works in full-screen mode with only one of the available applications. In this case, the second application may be available on the screen of the phablet. It remains possible to receive calls and conversations via Skype without interrupting the workflow. In the case of the HP Elite X3, when the device is hard-installed in the dock, the ergonomics suffer, because to realize the possibility of negotiation, you will have to additionally use the speakerphone option, which is not always convenient, or have a headset on hand.

Perspectives


Does HP Elite X3 have any prospects to win back its share of the corporate mobile market? In order to answer this question, it is worth trying to weigh the pros and cons.

Of course, the desire to get rid of excess weight and maximally ease your life on the physical level is the dream of any sensible top and businessman. And confirmation of this is a relatively painless transition from laptops to tablets. Why not argue in a similar way in the case of a smartphone, which, being always with you, will allow you to get rid of carrying extra weight? Of course, in this case, the desire to use a smartphone as the main device loses its meaning due to objective inconveniences - a simplified browser, microscopic icons, limited application capabilities, etc.

And in this regard, the alternative proposed as an HP Elite X3 device on Windows 10 Mobile, which can be used as a desktop analog, looks quite promising, especially since the needs of average managers are often limited to transferring information via e-mail, Skype and by the browser. All these and many other features are successfully implemented on the MS Continuum platform via the HP Elite X3.

HP is also optimistic about the development of new high-quality mobile applications for Windows 10 Mobile, which has become part of the Windows 10 ecosystem installed today on more than 200 million devices. And the likelihood that the new SmartPad will be used as a central device in the office and home ecosystem with an increase in the number of such applications will only increase. Perhaps this explains the presence in the HP Elite X3 slot for 2 SIM - cards, one of which can be used by the user as a worker, and the other - used for personal purposes.



But is it all so cloudless? Probably not.

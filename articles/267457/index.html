Understand how it works


Right now, while the section is working in beta mode, the simulator is free. It has 10 rules, from very simple to very complex (modal verbs, participles and passive voice). The section will evolve, and over time we will cover all grammatical structures.



Clicking on the rule, we get to the workout. For one approach, you need to collect 10 sentences. If the student makes mistakes, they are highlighted, and a new chance is given - and so on until, finally, he does not add up the chain in the right order. Correctly composed proposal is automatically voiced. Do not forget to choose a pronunciation option - British or American.



If the construction of the construction and use cases are not yet familiar, then before you begin to collect the proposal, you can use the hint by clicking the "Show rule" button. What is important - the rule is explained in the native language, with many examples.

Those who managed to familiarize themselves with the theory on grammar courses can immediately begin to assemble constructs. Our team tried so that the training did not lead to longing. There are thousands of examples in it, not invented by aunts and uncles-compilers of textbooks, but pulled out of your favorite books and the most interesting scientific articles in the Lungaleo Jungle. Yes, some people had to do the parsing of all these proposals in hundreds of thousands of materials - more on that later.

Go into details (no, seriously)


Parsing works like this: when publishing content in the Jungle, it is processed by our NLP service. The service parses the content into sentences and words, determines their various properties (both sentences and words). For sentences, this is a grammatical model; for words, frequency. After such processing, the texts fall into moderation. UGC-content involves a large number of errors in the sentences, so we select only qualitative and interesting from the total number of examples. The operator assigns a rule or several rules to the model, after which all the proposals of this model can be sent to training. Ta-dam!

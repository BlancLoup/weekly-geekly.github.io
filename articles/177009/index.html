Dell XPS 12 Ultrabook: Two Computers in One Case

The first time the Dell XPS 12 showed in Berlin, at the IFA. At the chamber event, which was held right at the Dell booth, the device was shown to the world, allowed to take a picture, and then hid under a glass cube. One of the reasons for the "seclusion", apparently, was the final version of Windows 8, installed on the XPS 12. After all, at the beginning of September 2012 it still seemed not to exist. Then I managed to twist (literally and figuratively) the new product for about fifteen minutes, and after two months it became a kind of showcase for the new Microsoft operating system. Indeed, on what else was it to show, how cleverly do two entities at once combine in Windows 8 - computer and tablet?

XPS 12 was shown at presentations and exhibitions, filmed for advertising - in general, there are few mobile computers that flashed so many times in the media. From the end of October, it could be bought in the States, but, as usual, the ultrabook traveled to Russia on the slats. And now, finally, there was an opportunity to properly test it in work and, by a good tradition, look inside.



The fact that the XPS 12 is the brainchild of Dell can be understood right away without even looking at the logo. The combination of silver and gray in the design is typical for all mobile computers of the company, and even if pranks appear in some of the models, such as multi-colored connectors, the family resemblance remains obvious. Of course, an ultrabook, and even a transformer, cannot be very much like traditional laptops, but still - look at the photo. Can this model be confused with fellows of Korean or Chinese origin?



In the case of the XPS 12, the developers had a much more difficult task than to ensure continuity in design. Usually, the top cover becomes an important element of stiffness of the whole structure. In a situation where the screen rotates 180 degrees, everything changes. It was necessary not only to give the proper strength to the ultrabook, but also to take care of the durability of the turning mechanism - so that the rotating part does not ‚Äúwalk‚Äù from the breeze and that during operation the contact between the ‚Äúsystem unit‚Äù and the screen is not broken. After all, how many years the classic cable connecting the graphics adapter and the matrix, so all the same, developers sometimes miss with the choice of a fairly durable option. And here, not only does everything rotate, so also the contacts are subject to constant environmental exposure. A similar technical solution first appeared in Dell Inspiron Duo laptops a couple of years ago, but they were significantly more cumbersome. Meanwhile, even a tenth millimeter step means a significant complication of design work. In the XPS 12, by eye, the lid has become thinner by two millimeters by two, and this can already be considered an invention of the mechanism anew.



I have not yet had the opportunity to test the reliability of the structure in the field for, say, six months. But a careful study of the turning mechanism on the outside and inside left a purely positive impression. If you forget about the rotating screen for a moment, the Dell XPS 12 is just a good representative of the second generation of ultrabooks. We already know a lot about them, and in general, we do not expect any surprises. But even against the background of his fellows, the hero of the review differs by the very quiet work. Having applied the ultrabook to the ear directly to the ventilation grille, I discovered that the propeller still rotates, but at some obscenely low revs. The sound is such as if a very old and experienced mouse sneaks to the hole on a poorly planed wooden floor: ideally quiet, but sometimes it still touches the burr with its foot. With World of Tanks, we still managed to get the cooler to spin, but did it really make a noise! My Acer Aspire S5 is so noisy when I open the Facebook page. Maybe Dell engineers invented some kind of super efficient cooling system? No, it is quite ordinary. So what is the secret of quiet work?


Cooler assembly. Good old Sunon, long time no see!


The turbine blades do not hit a particular form. It turns out, the secret of silence is still in very low revs.

It is, in fact, simple. The current Core begin to reset the frequency only when warming up to 105 degrees Celsius. Accordingly, anything lower is considered a working temperature. The processors themselves are ‚Äúcold‚Äù, and therefore it is almost impossible to rally them to 105 degrees. And if so - why upset the user's ears? Let him enjoy the silence. The reverse side of such care is a bit more heating of the body. When working at a table, this is not noticeable, but if you like to lie on the sofa with a laptop on your stomach, then there is a chance to experience some discomfort when performing particularly difficult tasks (for the third time, we mention World of Tanks). The hottest place is the middle of the case in its upper part, where the processor is located. Heating is not such that the native belly is affected, but in the summer it may not be very comfortable. Therefore, users who like to lie down, it is better to purchase a special stand for a laptop, the benefit of which is sold a lot. Or compromise and put the XPS 12 on your knees. Then the heating center is likely to remain in the gap between the legs.

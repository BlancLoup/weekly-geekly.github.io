What technology do we use to develop web applications?

Free translation of an article from the site Crisp eBusiness What technology do we use?

We have clients whose projects are written in PHP, ASP and ColdFusion. Our choice is Adobe ColdFusion, which we recommend to everyone.
What is ColdFusion and why does Crisp eBusiness use it?

ColdFusion is Adobe's commercial enterprise application server.
ColdFusion is a platform for rapid application development for high availability web applications.

Key features

It is not easy to paint on points all the advantages of technology, but still:
  • A high percentage of distribution (75 of the 100 largest US industrial companies)
  • Winner of various awards (CODiE, JOLT & Intelligent Enterprise)
  • Recommendations by independent experts (Gartner)
  • Tight integration with Flex / Flash / PDF / AIR / BlazeDS (Adobe technology)
  • Availability of commercial support
  • Frequent releases
  • Built-in management tools

What does this mean?

This means that your site operates on a commercial platform, which can be increased due to the increasing number of visitors. As a language for rapid application development, CF reduces development time to a minimum, reducing costs and time.

You know that ColdFusion is not a new language.

This is a common misconception. ColdFusion was created in 1995. Since then, nine major releases have been released, i.e. it can be said that each new version comes out every two years. Adobe bought ColdFusion from Macromedia in 2007. This platform is used by approximately 75% of the 100 largest US industrial companies. In May 2009, Gartner published a report in which they recommend ColdFusion for use:

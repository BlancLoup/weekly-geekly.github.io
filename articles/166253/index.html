Nokia has posted files for the manufacture of their own buildings on a 3D printer

Nokia has released 3D print files for the Lumia 820 packages, allowing users to create their own case designs for their smartphones. The free 3D printing SDK (or, as Nokia calls it, 3DK) includes not just files for 3D printers, but also a guide to materials that can be used, and all sorts of tips for creating a truly unique package.




Despite the fact that this is the first project of its kind, it resembles the functionality of the Nokia Xpress line, in which the Finnish company offered device owners the ability to easily change the look of their phones by simply putting on these or other sockets. It goes back to the Nokia 5110 model released in 1998.

Needless to say, a 3D printer is required for making a case using 3DK files (or, at least, access to one such). However, if you do not have this, there are many online services for 3D printing, which for a certain fee can print any model of your order.

Drawings of parts of the Lumia 820 are available here , separately the model of the back cover can be downloaded here and here . If for some reason you cannot use 3D printing, Nokia offers several ready-made package options, some of which are protected or with wireless charging.

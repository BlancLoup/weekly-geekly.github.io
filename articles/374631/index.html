Steve Jobs Presents iPhone 6 and Apple Watch

Her motto was ‚ÄúMore Bigger.‚Äù When you hear it, you feel the cold of an impersonal march of progress. There is something completely artificial in this sensation, something like touching a cold gland.
We do not expect new technologies from Apple. We expect to receive human warmth, the opportunity to make our lives more conscious and fruitful.

Let's go through those parts of the presentation that seemed weak and see how Steve could shake off the dust.

Feeling anxious


The presentation begins with an analogue of something like a Prezi with small patches of text.







While everyone else is chasing new products, you appreciate only those that really benefit.

Everyone already had an understanding of this Apple concept, and too much underlining the fact that the main thing was the benefit, not the speed of the product, looked like a defensive gesture from the company.

Even if we are talking about companies, we treat with respect only those who demonstrate confidence and not a feeling of anxiety. Under Jobs, Apple would never have come up with such weak slogans.


iPhone 6


There must be only one obvious, instinctive reason to buy an iPhone 6. The enlarged screen is a weak suggestion for one of the company's flagship products. It is simply a derivative of existing products on the market, which does not allow to demonstrate differences from competitors.

And the fact that this statement contradicts Steve's original idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe iPhone (‚ÄúNobody will buy just a big phone‚Äù), suppresses the image of the novelty and confuses the audience.

  • Apple Pay is a payment system based on NFC technology.
  • IOS 8 features related to user health.
  • Improved camera.

Instead of the usual:

Apple: We have the perfect product making X.
You: Oh my God, I have to buy it.

We got the following:

Apple: We have a product that makes X, Y, Z, and more A and B.
You: It seems that X would be useful to me, but A and B also look good.

This happens because product developers want to play it safe. There is a possibility that X alone may not be enough. The obvious solution to the problem is to add new characteristics until something for everyone appears.

Thanks to Steve Jobs, this fear has disappeared. There was an invulnerable, almost sacred assurance that he had something that you would definitely love. We had a clear reason why we would go to the Apple store after the presentation and buy this phone.



Apple Watch


This watch is confusing. They have too many variations. And this is a very gross mistake of the company.

Instead of a simple and perfect product, we got a random selection of features and options to choose from. Apple Watch was supposed to be the very one that people would call the ‚ÄúDivine Clock‚Äù and associate with the second coming.

If you come up with the same design, the only canonical image of the clock will be formed. One great gadget. If in this case someone mentioned Apple Watch, you would immediately have a clear image in your head.

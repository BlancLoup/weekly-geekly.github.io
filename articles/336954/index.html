Do not want to work after the holidays?

Trying to figure out: post-holiday syndrome or career burnout?

Agree, no matter how much we love our work, a couple of weeks in a sunny country on the seashore will make any workaholic feel an attack of longing for vacation and freedom. If, having just rested, you find yourself thinking that you do not really want to return to your old job, perhaps this is not depression after a vacation, but a real career burnout.

If this is your case, and you are inclined to believe that your recent vacation has put everything in its place, read our 8 signs that your unwillingness to return to work rather indicates that it is time to change it.

The holiday mood came to you long before you actually went on vacation.


We all tend to dream about vacation, sitting in the office. But if a few weeks before the holidays you are already more there than here, perhaps you need not a vacation, but a new job. Dreaming about vacation is normal, but if in your thoughts you are far from the workplace, you can not concentrate on business and literally count the days before departure, then it looks more dangerous than ordinary work fatigue.

When you booked this vacation, did your performance drop? Did you notice that you are more and more reluctant to complete the work begun and complete the projects? On the last day before your vacation, did you leave the place, even without having to hand it over while you were away? If you recognize yourself, then this is a sure sign that you have turned off the workflow, because the work no longer carries you away as it did before. Think about the fact that when you return from vacation you should look at alternative opportunities in the labor market.

When your holiday began, you realized that only now for the first time in a long time you felt happy and relaxed.

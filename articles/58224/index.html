Steve Jobs. The man who invented the iPod


Stephen Paul Jobs was born in San Francisco on February 24, 1955, raised in California in a foster family. His adoptive mother worked in an accounting company, and his father was a mechanic. At the request of the biological mother, Stephen went to college.
At age 12, Steven Jobs phoned HP CEO William Hewlett and asked him for the parts he needed to assemble some kind of instrument. After talking with the boy, William sent him everything he needed and invited him to his company to work on holidays. It was then that the fate of Jobs decided while working at Hewlett-Packard, he met Stephen Wozniak .


Steve Wozniak (right) and Steve Jobs (left) at the dawn of Apple Computer


After receiving a secondary education, Jobs was hired by Atari, a very large then computer games developer, and was able to save money for a trip to India, which he had long dreamed of.



Being 5 years older than Jobs, Wozniak had already brilliantly understood computers, and he and Stephen and Ronald Wayne, Atari , founded Apple Computer Co. on April 1, 1976.


Apple I , Apple 's first computer


The company name was coined by Jobs. Firstly, the guys wanted to choose a provocative title (at that time there was a record company with a similar name, those who were fond of the Beatles know), and secondly, Steve just loved apples.



The first computer of the company was Apple I, which sold for $ 666.66. In total 600 such cars were sold. But real success came with the advent of Apple II, designed for a wider range of users. Computers spread, bringing huge profits to the company. Apple began to grow and in 1980 became a joint stock company and conducted a successful initial public offering, with the result that Steve Jobs became a millionaire in 25 years . Things of the company went up sharply.



1983 Jobs convinces John Scully to move from Pepsi-Cola to Apple. Then, as a result of the conflict between them, Steve Jobs himself was removed from project management and was forced to leave Apple.

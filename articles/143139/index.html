Cisco IP Phone Mini SPA Review SPA502G

The desire to save once again brought me into the jungle of IP telephony. Not that I talk a lot with remote subscribers, but I have to spend a decent amount per month. Now you tell me - "Dude, there is Skype!". Believe me, I know. Incredibly, but in fact, in the world there are places where there is no computer, but there is a telephone. Having studied the tariffs for communication with my provider, I decided and connected myself to a separate IP number. In this regard, I want to share my impressions of a new device for myself - Cisco SPA502G IP Phone.

Ps. I say thanks in advance to all network professionals for not minus, but giving useful tips =).

What really seduced me actually IP-telephony? Of course the price. As I was promised, the expenses will decrease by two or three times, and the quality of communication will increase significantly (by the way, the call to a mobile phone in Moscow costs me now three times cheaper than from my mobile).
Surely most habrazhiteli familiar with the principle of IP-telephony, but I still briefly mention this.
The choice of apparatus was short-lived; of the options available to me, I immediately refused noname aggregates and D-Link. There remained Cisco SPA502G and ZyXEL V301-T1. Not at all wanting to insult the respectable ZyXEL company, I‚Äôll say that the device was not in my hands. As a result, a box with a Cisco IP phone and a separately purchased power adapter migrated from the warehouse of the selling company to the trunk of my car.

The box has quite small dimensions. Packaging is typical for corporate products - no multi-colored dust jacket. Brown cardboard and Cisco Small Business lettering - that's all the look and feel.

The device itself is made in traditional colors - dark gray plastic for the body and the tube, and silver plastic design around the screen. On the front panel are a numeric keypad and additional buttons.

Below is the handset connection port and two RJ45 connectors - for the Internet line and for connecting a computer. This option reduces the number of wires to the router - the computer can be connected "through". It is enough one wire entering the phone, and another device can be connected to the phone itself. Conveniently, if the router is located far enough. In addition, SPA502G supports PoE technology (Power over Ethernet), that is, if the router supports this technology, you can do without an additional power supply. However, as I wrote above, I had to purchase a separate power supply unit Linksys PA100 (cost about $ 11).

The bundle includes a manual, a documentation disc and a black patch cord.

Of the features it is also worth noting the ability to connect a wired headset to the Sub-mini connector (2.5 mm) on the right side and the presence of a stand that will allow you to install the phone almost vertically on the table. The phone is equipped with the Call Waiting function, which is more needed in the office. When a second call arrives on the line, it does not deviate, but goes into the queue (the LED indicator starts blinking).
Speakerphone is implemented successfully, the subscriber is heard clearly, without problems.
The process of connecting the device at home did not cause me any difficulties.

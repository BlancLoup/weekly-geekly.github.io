How I put together a team for my startup

Hello! My name is Valeria Makarova, I am the head of the Rocket Callback . Today I would like to share my experience on how I assembled a team for my project, and at the same time present you our small and simple fraternity.



I myself come from Buryatia, the glorious city of Ulan-Ude. I have already described how things are going with IT, briefly recall that, despite certain difficulties, IT is developing in Buryatia and the number of specialists or local companies is growing every year. As the number of online stores and people who prefer to buy there, and not on the market. In general, I really love my land, so I can be a bit biased.

Turning to the point. By education I am a philologist, graduated from the graduate school of the Buryat State Faculty. She never noticed her interest in IT until 2012, when all her acquaintances began to saw their own start-ups around. Plus, a little later, I got a job at HR in one IT company, which also affected my further fate.

In 2014, I worked in one of the Buryat companies in the field of knitwear. At that time, callback services began to appear, and I, as a marketer (with HR was finished), also recommended management take a closer look at this idea. Our programmer Igor said that the topic is cool, the service is cool, there are prospects, only now you can make everything better and more convenient, plus then with time add various chips.

The idea to cut down something of their own has long been entrenched in my mind. At first I thought about an application for ordering a car wash, then about a portal for assessing the activities of State Duma deputies. Then I got an idea with a callback-service. So two stern bearded men and I, a girl who had just defended her Ph.D. degree, created a widget, which later became the Rocket Callback.

Choosing Rocket, we wanted to show that we connect the manager and the client as fast as the rocket flies. Well, plus the themes of space are always in trend, evoking associations with something advanced, new and unexplored.

At the end of the summer, we attracted 300,000 rubles of investments for a 10% stake in the project. All from the same friend associated with knitwear. It was here that the question arose of what to do and where to look for employees, since they themselves could no longer cope.

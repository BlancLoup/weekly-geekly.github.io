The history of amperca


Habrarider, hello!

The blog of our company will soon expire the paid period. We definitely will not have time to prepare some interesting and unusual material about DIY electronics before its end. Therefore, taking this opportunity, I will tell you better about how Ampere appeared, and how we came to what we have now and why we do not have time for anything forever.

So, if you are interested in the story of the emergence of a small business, please under the cat.



Prehistory



It all started in 2010, when I worked as a programmer for another game development company and wanted to introduce another fashion chip into the process. This was the practice of Continuous Integration. You know, probably: this is when there is a lamp in the room that glows green, if everything is fine with the code and turns red, if someone commits changes that break the assembly or tests.

I did not know how to do this and had no idea about resistors, transistors, and even more so about microcontrollers. Google then led me in the direction of semi-finished solutions for the smart home. They were expensive and generally resembled gunfire on sparrows. And then a ray of light - Arduino! Straight what you need! It's funny, but Google didn‚Äôt tell me about the cheap raw microcontrollers.

So, it was decided: I urgently need an Arduino and a bunch of parts to quickly deal with all this. In Moscow, it was impossible to buy the flagship Arduino Duemilanove, so I had to order it in Germany, as I remember, at Waterott Electronics.

The package arrived and I began to blink the LEDs with interest. I finally began to understand why we need resistors, transistors and diodes, which I last heard about at school. It seemed to me that such circuitry for dummies could be liked by many people.

At the same time, I really wanted to stir up some IT startup. Fortunately, I already had 3 of my own failed projects and one not launched. Arduino seemed so interesting and unfairly undeveloped in Russia that I forgot about the Continuous Integration and began to investigate the issue of xDuino production in Russia. The platform is in open source. Nothing thinking in the production of electronics, I counted 200-300 rubles per unit.

I fired up. I understood that this is it: both an IT start-up and real production are very interesting and not very banal. Spring and rejoicing, I decided to tell my friend about the idea. He, by the way, was a humanist and often did not understand who needed "these your techish pieces." But the concept of the Arduino was so simple and clear that it also hooked. We decided to do. So the company was born.

Before dealing with production issues, the partner suggested trying to buy some Arduin in Europe and sell it with a minimum markup, in order to understand whether they need someone at all. It was the first time one of us saved the other from an idiotic decision. After all, I did not know then that I had made a mistake in calculating the cost of a unit by more than œÄ times.

First investment



We chipped in by 20 thousand and began to form the first order in Robot Italy for our private satisfied people. I remember these were seven Arduino Duemilanove, one Arduino Mega, several breadboards, crumbling, two LCD screens, a power supply and two servo drives. Now it brings a smile. We pressed the ‚Äúcheckout‚Äù button on April 4, 2010. This was the official birthday of Amperka.

While our first batch was traveling by Russian Post, it was necessary to raise the platform of the online store. I was a developer at that time for 7 years, of which I had been working on Python for 2 years. What a blessing that I did not find any sane e-commerce platform in Python. I had to choose something from SaaS. I decided to use InSales, because I heard something about them. And it was another correct decision chosen by chance. Successful not because InSales is a super-duper (now we are cramped in their functionality), but because someone else was engaged in hosting, backups, updates, reflection of DDoS. I simply did not imagine then that the stupid ‚Äúbuy-sell‚Äù model would throw a basilion of small worries on our heads. Mikhail, my partner, was very skeptical about the idea, arguing that ‚Äúyou will never understand what a person needs,‚Äù ‚Äúwhy should he overpay‚Äù and other arguments. I insisted and it was another anti-idiotic decision. So there were sets Milimatreshka, Kilomatreshka and Megamatreshka. Appeared virtually because goods we have not waited.

Then our megaparty finally arrived, but there were still a number of pre-launch issues: an agreement with the courier service, a direct telephone number, cardboard boxes, company stickers, zip-packages for small things and other nonsense. At the beginning it was completely unobvious that all this is not born out of thin air. We worked on our full-time jobs, so the business did not move very fast, but it did. All the evenings and weekends we enthusiastically spent on preparing for the start.

Launch



As a result, on June 27th we will start, as announced on Habr√© . I often say that our 7 arduins bought out in the first 7 minutes. This is not entirely true. In fact, it took us 48 hours. The announcement is in a very right place - one more star from those that formed in a row and allowed to go further.

Suddenly, the first of these mundane tasks appeared that I had never thought about. They call the store, and you're at work. We had to constantly run into the corridor. If the conversation needed something in the computer, it was inconvenient, stupid and ridiculous. All this caused oblique views of colleagues and gave me emotional discomfort. But there were no options.

It was evening and it was necessary to do something worldly again. I remember that there were not enough jumpers on the sets. Mikhail had to ride Chip-e-Deepam to close the hole. Go



It immediately became obvious that carrying the goods to yourself is an option that does not scale in any way. Therefore, we exhaled, formed money and ordered a commercial batch. 35 Arduino Duemilanove and more details. The long customs history was stretched. By early September, we understood that the party and the money were lost, because everything was done wrong. Friends, I remember, sympathized, but when they learned that the party was only 50 thousand rubles, they were touched and said that it was nothing. At your own risk, we ordered a double of the party. On the 10th, the level of "import" was still enough to drag the party. We were delighted and set to work. There were a lot of plans: interesting articles, a forum, partnerships with bloggers, but it all instantly broke down about the packaging of radio components on bags, problems with couriers and other mundane matters. Mikhail said that because of these things at work, it already smells fried and we need a manager. I agreed, although obviously it would not be free.

For two days everything went well, but then he just disappeared: neither the e-mail, nor the phone answered. And we, idiots, did not even make a copy of the passport. There was no braking and new money was also quickly melting before our eyes. At that time, as the main job was already the next shop, where I became the technical director. There was a lot of work, but they also paid 150K. It was very helpful, because a part could sometimes be thrown into the mouth of Amperca.

Sitting on two chairs was extremely difficult. We needed to develop a wiki, make descriptions of a large number of new products, conduct experiments to respond to technical questions from customers, keep track of product residues and much more. Therefore, in June of 2011, I raised the question that I need a salary and I will pay 100% of the time to the store. We stopped at the sum of 70K, that after 150K, of course, it was not a fountain, but this is really earned money in a real own company! And the development prospects inspired even more.

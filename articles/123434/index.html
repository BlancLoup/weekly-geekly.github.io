The history of the brand conferences TechEd



Many IT professionals are familiar with the TechEd brand. TechEd is a world-renowned conference run by Microsoft around the world. Conferences under the TechEd brand gather thousands of managers and executives, programmers and IT experts, professionals in their field and offer them dozens and even hundreds of technical reports on conference days.

This article will briefly tell the story of TechEd as a brand and talk about the most interesting moments of conferences under this name.

Start


It all started back in 1993 by IT standards, when the first conference was held under the name TechEd in Orlando, Florida, USA. This year, Microsoft presented its new offspring to the conference participants - the Windows 3.1 operating system, which later became super popular. In addition, the key theme remained the MS DOS operating system, which at that time was at the peak of its popularity. Among the interesting products of that period, Microsoft Visual Basic can be distinguished, which Microsoft presented shortly before the conference. Later, Visual Basic will also be a huge success.

The first TechEd started very seriously - it was visited by 3,000 technical specialists, which came as a surprise even to the organizers. Dedicated hotel barely coped with the influx of participants. Many experts came even without prior registration, because it quickly ended. In general, the first TechEd was a resounding success.

The next two conferences of 1994 and 1995 were held in New Orleans, USA, and secured the successful start of the new event. In 1994, the company already talked about its new project, Windows ‚ÄúChicago‚Äù (future Windows 95), and besides that there were: Windows NT, Access, FoxPro, VBA, Visual C ++, OLE, MAPI, SQL Server and other technologies. In 1995, the conference broke all records by offering developers more than 400 reports in one event. TechEd 1995 lasted five days.

In 1996, TechEd was held in Los Angeles, bringing together 7000 participants. This event was opened by Bill Gates himself, then the chairman of the board of directors and CEO of Microsoft. The theme of the Gates plenary report was the Internet and how it is integrated into the Windows and Windows NT operating systems. Another major topic of the conference was ActiveX technology.



1997 was an important year in the history of TechEd. First, the conference again took place in Orlando and took place from May 5 to 9, bringing together a record 9,000 participants. And secondly, the conference took a new step in development - for the first time the European version of the conference was organized under the name TechEd EMEA. This event took place in the city of Nice, France. It was attended by 3,700 people. Bill Gates welcomed the conference participants in a live broadcast.

Since 1998, at Microsoft TechEd conferences, Microsoft has been actively promoting Internet technologies: development on ASP, Internet Explorer, DHTML and IIS server. In 1999, Windows DNA technology was introduced at TechEd, which later transformed into the .NET platform.

In 2001, the central topic for discussion at TechEd was the .NET platform, including the server part, the new C # programming language, and the new generation of development tools Visual Studio .NET.

In 2002, TechEd North America was held in New Orleans in April. It was already the tenth anniversary conference of TechEd. Eric Radder spoke at the conference this year with a plenary report on the new product of Visual Studio .NET, which was launched a little earlier in 2002.

In 2003, a new Windows Storage Server 2003 product was announced at a conference in Dallas, a new version of SQL Server, codenamed Yukon, and a second version of the .NET Framework were discussed.

2004 was the year of the next generation of Windows discussion, codenamed Longhorn.

TechEd 2008, which was already held under the TechEd North America brand, was remembered for a historic event - the last performance of Bill Gates on behalf of Microsoft, who told about the company's plans to develop data centers and about the new Windows 7 operating system.

Regional Conferences




TechEd EMEA / Europe

Since 1997, the holding of a conference of the European version of TechEd EMEA has become almost annual. Conferences were held in 1998 (Nice), 1999 and 2000 (Amsterdam), 2003 (Barcelona), 2004 and 2005 (Amsterdam), 2006-2008 (Barcelona) and in 2009 and 2010 TechEd EMEA were held in Berlin.

In 2011, TechEd Europe took a break to return in 2012. From 25 to 29 June, the next TechEd Europe 2012 will be held in Amsterdam.

In 2003, TechEd took another step and the conference expanded significantly as new countries entered the new continent. This time Australia, India and Israel got their TechEd.

TechEd Australia, TechEd New Zealand

The Australian version of the largest conference for IT professionals began its history in 2003 in Brisburn, Australia. After that, the annual Australian versions of TechEd were held in the cities of Canberra (2004), Gold Coast (2005, 2007, 2009, 2010) and Sydney (2006, 2008).

Together with the Australian conference, a conference was held annually in neighboring New Zealand. TechEd New Zealand events almost always took place in Auckland, New Zealand. In 2010, dozens of reports were presented at the TechEd New Zealand conference.

At the end of August 2011, TechEd Australia 2011 will be held next TechEd New Zealand 2011 , and in early September.

TechEd India

TechEd India began its history in 2003. Since then, the conference became held annually, with the exception in 2008, when the conference was not held.

In 2009, Steve Ballmer spoke at TechEd India and this was Ballmer's first speech at the TechEd Regional Conference outside the United States. In 2009, the conference offered visitors 28 technical tracks with 112 reports and 24 laboratory work.

The last Indian event was held in March 2011 in Bangalore, India.

TechEd Israel

The first TechEd Israel conference held in 2003 in the city of Eilat gathered 1,500 people. TechEd Israel 2010 was held in November 2010 and gathered more than 3,000 people. The main topics of the conference were Office 365, Lync, and SharePoint 2010 technologies.

In 2005, TechEd Conference once again increased its geographic presence ‚Äî an African version of TechEd Africa was launched.

TechEd Africa

TechEd Africa Conference (until 2007 it was called TechEd South Africa) was another international analogue of the original TechEd, which has recently been named TechEd North America. For the first time African TechEd was held in 2005 in the city of Sun City, South Africa. In the following 2006 and 2007, the conference was held in the same place in Sun City, but from 2008 to 2010 the TechEd Africa venue was changed to Durban, South Africa.

In 2011, October 17-20 , TechEd Africa 2011 will re-open its doors in the city of Durban.

TechEd Middle East

These two conferences are the newest conferences held by TechEd. First, in March 2010, TechEd Middle East Middle East Conference was held in Dubai, UAE, and then in September 2010, South American TechEd Latin America was held in Sao Paulo, Brazil.

TechEd Middle East 2011 was held in March 2011 in Dubai.

The TechEd story does not end there. In 2011, the brand will be expanded again. In the arena of the world's largest events for IT professionals will be a new conference called TechEd Russia.

More TechEd

In addition to the listed conferences in the world of the TechEd brand, there are even more geographical locations. These are TechEd China, TechEd Japan, TechEd Brasil (aka TechEd Latina America). Virtually the entire world is covered by the TechEd conference, which annually hosts tens of thousands of professionals around the world.

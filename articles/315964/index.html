About types of used routers on MSK-IX and W-IX

Having worked for more than ten years in the industry, I was repeatedly overwhelmed by the interest in what equipment our ‚Äúneighbors‚Äù use - uplinks, downlinkas, peering partners. It is easy to get this information if the IP-interface is organized on the basis of Ethernet *: we look at the MAC-or ARP-table, by the first three bytes (OUI) we determine the manufacturer of the equipment.

In this publication, I will conduct a brief analysis of the routers used on networks of popular traffic exchange points - MSK-IX and W-IX .

Let's start with the oldest Moscow traffic exchange point - MSK-IX. Established more than 20 years ago, a platform for sharing IP traffic for a dozen Internet providers, today it passes over 2 Tbit / s of traffic to CNNN from more than 400 participants (unique autonomous systems), and more than 34 thousand are available on routing servers prefixes.

MSK-IX includes participants into a single broadcast Ethernet segment, which allows each of them to send ethernet frames to any other participant directly, bypassing intermediate routers. Thus, each participant can see the MAC address of the device of any other participant, and, therefore, determine the manufacturer of the equipment used.

We go to Looking Glass MSK-IX , and do a BGP Summary sampling on Moscow route servers msk-rs1.ripn.net and msk-rs2.ripn.net.

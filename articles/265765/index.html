Vendor replacement for IT infrastructure (telecommunications): which import can be exchanged for another import



The disposition in the country is this: many large companies (first of all - banks and state corporations) are used to building infrastructure on well-known American brands. There are many reasons, but the main one is a systematic approach. This year, they all had to somehow change suppliers. Because it is relatively easy to bring iron past the sanctions on the ‚Äúleft‚Äù legal entity (albeit more expensive), but it is almost impossible to purchase a service for Kuzmich and Partners for iron in the data center of a large bank. For companies that are not subject to sanctions, the story is different. There, the management lowered official memos like "to assess the possibility of building infrastructure on Chinese (Huawei, etc.) equipment." Because, in general, we need a plan just in case.

Data networks


For a large infrastructure, one recipe is Huawei. Actually, in this area the last N years, he has been engaged in what we can, with some exaggeration, be called "cys-replacement". In essence, it functionally repeats the line on LAN, WAN, Wi-Fi, DWDM, servers, and Cisco and HP storage systems.

Pros:


  • The line on the LAN-WAN-highways maximum (in comparison with other manufacturers) covers the line of Cisco. No one else has such a choice.
  • In Russia, the experience of working with the Huawei is large: as a rule, in order not to fall into dependence on one supplier, the second and third are introduced in large infrastructures. The first large-scale implementations of Huawei in Russia were, as is known, the network of telecom operators. In this niche, Huawei quickly reached parity with Cisco. And there now the Chinese no longer need to prove anything to anyone. Everyone knows what to expect from them and how to work with them. Now it's up to Enterprise, where Cisco is dominant. However, most companies know that building a network on only one vendor, although convenient from a technical point of view, is quite risky.

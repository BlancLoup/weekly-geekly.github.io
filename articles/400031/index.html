On the one hand, you can be committed to classic programming in the console and IDE, but the robot is designed for children of the elementary - early secondary school (officially - 10 years old) and is very good for exploring a child who is not fan of developing a programmer. Working here with such buttons-functions, the child will learn the logic of the program, will understand the essence of the algorithm. And this, as is known, is the base from which everything should start. Assimilation of the order of actions is promoted by multi-colored lights for different types of commands - it is easier to remember the algorithm, understand it, and repeat it. In general, for programming we deal with a visual editor.



The remaining functions are much less similar to programming; they are rather buttons-switches: sound selection, delay, setting time, speed, angle of inclination to the surface, turning parameters, setting the engine speed, etc. Provides voice control system. In addition, the robot is equipped with two motors with a speed of up to 170 revolutions per minute and supports third-party sensors through four USB ports of the Type C interface, for example, infrared and ultrasonic sensors, and this is already a sweep for imagination. Here another problem is being solved - the development of the engineering skills of the young robotics.


In the heart (head?), Xiaomi MITU is an ARM Cortex M3 (STM32) microcontroller with a 32-bit infrastructure, due to which it counts and executes commands faster than its 8 and 16-bit congeners. This microcontroller is good for a robot of this class also by the fact that it supports power saving modes, it is able to hibernate entirely and by subsystems. By the way, the robot itself has a 1700 mAh battery, which allows it to work as long as 12 hours. The flash memory of a smart toy - 32 MB - is two times more than that of a distinguished competitor, but there is no support for a memory card.

The robot is compatible with Android 4.3 + and iOS 6 +. And this compatibility is not a lot of fun. The robot can be controlled through a special application on the smartphone. For example, you can draw an arbitrary route, and it will follow exactly the trajectory that you specified. You can also transmit information to the toy's gyroscope and generally control the mobile phone as a joystick.

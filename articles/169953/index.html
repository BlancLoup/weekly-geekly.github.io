How to secure yourself on Facebook

Today, Facebook is the largest social network in the world in terms of the number of users. We trust her with your personal data and confidential information. Many users link to their Facebook profile credit cards or payment service accounts (for example, PayPal) for online purchases. That is why this social network becomes the object of attention of cybercriminals. In this article, we want to give some practical tips on how to ensure the safety of their personal data by excluding them from falling into the wrong hands.



Use privacy settings

While working on Facebook, a user can set various privacy settings or ‚Äúvisibility‚Äù of the information he publishes to different people or lists. Thus, he will be sure that this information will be available, selectively, for certain people, or not available to anyone other than him. This is a very good feature that distinguishes Facebook from other social networks and allows the user to decide who can see the information published by him and who can not.

Such settings can be applied to personal profile data (such as date of birth, contacts, interests, etc.), as well as to chronicle information (user publications, stamps). The visibility settings of your profile information are best checked immediately after registering with your social network account (you can also do this later at any time) - to do this, go to your profile info page.

Depending on your preferences, you can leave each block open to all users, restrict visibility to your friends, or hide this data for everyone except yourself. It also provides the ability to flexibly configure access at the level of lists, or individual users.

Use https

Today, many services use https, Facebook is no exception. If you still have not turned on this mode of operation, it's time to do it. To do this, go to Account Settings -> Security (Fig. 13).


Fig. 13

Using https you can be sure that you work with Facebook , and not with any other source or server, besides this, the active connection to the social network will be encrypted . From the moment this feature is activated, you can always be sure that you are working with Facebook by checking the data of the encrypted connection and the server certificate. Pay attention to this, because connecting to fake Facebook pages containing web forms to steal your sensitive data uses a simple http mode .

Account security

Please note that if your account was somehow compromised, for example, if your username and password fell into the hands of intruders, then the privacy settings will not be able to help you protect against this situation. Privacy settings control the visibility of information, but they are powerless if someone other than you can log into your account. However, Facebook has a number of features or preventive measures that will help mitigate the consequences of a case where your username and password become known to someone else.

Facebook requests confirmation or re-entry of a password for some critical operations. At the very least, this can guarantee that the account accessed by the attackers will not be completely under their control.


Fig. 15.

Please note that by confirming your Facebook password you are not redirected to any phishing site - you are still using facebook.com in https mode.

Consider using a two-factor authentication mechanism. It uses the login confirmation for your account if the login attempt was made from an unfamiliar browser (i.e., a browser that you didn‚Äôt add to the "trusted" list). In case you are using Android or iOS, a confirmation code can be obtained through the code generator in your Facebook mobile application. If you want to send you a text SMS with a confirmation code, select "Other" when you activate the ability to confirm the input. Note that in order to receive SMS, your phone number must be activated in the profile. If you use a code generator on your Android or iOS, it must also be activated on your smartphone.


Fig. sixteen.

Be careful when opening links in text messages received from unfamiliar people. Attackers often resort to phishing and social engineering in order to compromise your account. In the event that you notice that your account has been compromised, for example, spam is being sent on your behalf, use this Facebook feature, which will allow you to regain access to your account.

Paintball tournament for the AIR Cup

The Association of Internet Developers and the Olympian Paintball Club invite you to take part in the AIR Cup internet developers ' paintball tournament.

The AIR Cup is a mass amateur sporting event held among teams of Russian and foreign organizations whose work is connected with the Internet.

The tournament will be held June 12, 2010 at the sites of the Olympian paintball club.
All teams participating in the tournament will receive commemorative badges and diplomas, and the winners of the tournament will be awarded valuable prizes from the organizers.

Each team must represent one web studio and have a flag with its studio logo. Flag format will be published later. Flags for all participants of the tournament will be prepared by AIR.
A company without a flag will receive a flag with the AIR logo.

The tournament involves teams of 5 people.
The goal of the game is to capture the opponent's studio flag.

We will post more detailed terms and conditions of participation a little later.

About paintball:

Paintball is a very democratic game. Practically, neither the gender, nor the age, nor the physical form of the player matter. Much more important is teamwork and the ability to subordinate oneself to the interests of the collective. During the game day, teams will compete in the qualifying round games. Participants playing at different sites of the paintball club will be able to test the capabilities of each team. And the most successful will come out in the final games, in which the winners of the tournament will be determined.

This tournament is intended for non-professional paintball teams. The presence in the team of at least three players of the same corporate company is a prerequisite for participation in this tournament. It is this condition that makes the tournament not only a sporting event, but also a truly entertaining event, in which it will be interesting to participate in completely different teams. The team is allowed to use no more than 2 electronic markers in semi-automatic mode. Personal mechanical markers and personal masks are allowed for each team member. Teams that do not have the equipment, everything you need to play (kit, marker, mask, gloves) will be issued by the organizing club on the day of the tournament.

The number of teams is limited! ;)

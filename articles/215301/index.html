Products for Intel SDN Networks


Intel devices and software for building computer networks traditionally remain in the shadow of more spectacular and well-known products, while in this area there are interesting news and fundamentally new solutions. In this post, we briefly describe one of these solutions - SDN networks. And not only we will tell, but we will also offer the Intel SDN equipment for free!

To begin, briefly recall what it is. SDN (Software-Defined Networking, software defined network) is a relatively new approach to building networks, in which the control level is programmed directly and it is unbound from the data transfer level. The result is an incredibly flexible, manageable, adaptive and cost-effective network architecture that allows the administrator to control and automate routine operations at a qualitatively new level.


SDN architecture

The basis of SDN is the OpenFlow protocol, which implements the interaction of the controller with network devices. Through the API, the controller fits in with various business applications that require specific configuration or network characteristics. Thus, the settings and functionality of network devices, previously hard-wired in their firmware, are now implemented openly and programmatically, which promises great promise.

For the development and promotion of SDN per se and OpenFlow, in particular, the Open Networking Foundation (ONF) alliance has been created, which currently includes all major network equipment manufacturers, such as Alcatel-Lucent, Cisco, Dell, Ericsson, Extreme Networks, HP, Huawei , IBM, Juniper Networks, and, of course, Intel .

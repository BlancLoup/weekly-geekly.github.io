Interview with Microsoft Patterns & Practices team (mp3)

As part of the annual series of Patterns & Practices events from Microsoft, September 21-22, 2009, Patterns & Practices Summit Russia was held in Moscow, at which Alexander Orlov was in the role of special correspondent for www.it4business.ru .

Alexander Orlov from the Moscow office of Microsoft.
On September 21, a shuttle from the Krylatsky Hills business park dropped me off at the bus stop of the business park itself. I usually directed my stops to building number 4, where the Intel office was located, but this time I walked through the mirrored doors of building number 1. Directly to the Microsoft office, where I was waited for a conversation with the key managers of the MS Patterns & Practices (PnP) team - in combination, the main speakers of the PnP Summit, who was appointed the next day.

Tomorrow Aja Kryshinamurti (Lead Product Planner), Grigory Melnik (Senior Program Manager) and Don Smith (Program Manager) were to give presentations on the PnP product and best practices that they use in a team, and today they are three in the conference room and I had more than an hour to ask them about all this in detail.

The conversation turned out to be so interesting that we went beyond the hour. But we think that it will be interesting for you to listen to what we have talked about:

  • What are Patterns & Practices (PnP)?
  • How PnP helps developers create business applications
  • Interesting features that will be in MS visual Studio 2010
  • Agile practices that apply within the PnP team
  • Difficulties encountered in the PnP team in implementing Agile

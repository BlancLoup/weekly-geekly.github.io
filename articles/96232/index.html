RF and successes in the fight against cybersquatting

But everything was so well thought out! Phased registration of domains in the zone. RF and measures to counter cybersquatters.
Here are all the stages of development that should lead to the minimization of cybersquatting:
  • Fast Track, which regulates the creation of a limited number of top-level domains, written with symbols of national languages;
  • Pre-registration of domains for trademark owners (in Cyrillic);
  • Priority registration of domains. The Russian Federation for the owners of trademarks containing characters other than Cyrillic;
  • Priority registration of domains. The Russian Federation for owners of company names, names of places of goods origin, mass media and non-profit organizations;
  • Open registration (declared from October 1, 2010)

Prehistory



On November 24, 2009, the coordination center registered 496 domains, from the already well-known . ---. to the ---. (with variations - with and without hyphens).

The initial conditions of pre-registration were fairly tough, if mot was written on the trademark, the domain could not be registered, and if MOT , then it is possible (the name of the real trademark is changed). Moreover, the domain could only be registered with the actual owner of the trademark (legal entity or individual entrepreneur).
Subsequently, there was a significant relaxation of the rules of preliminary registration, it is no longer necessary that the text on a trademark was in Russian, and not only trademark owners, but also legal entities, non-profit organizations, and the media can register domains:

Dutch auction - to be?


During preparation for the opening of the registration, many correct words were said about cybersquatters and the holding of a Dutch auction was discussed, according to the terms of which the Coordination Center reserved words and phrases of the Russian language, determined the maximum (high) cost. Then the cost decreases over time - and when the price begins to suit the buyer, the domain is acquired. This should have provided an opportunity for potential buyers of domains interested in the subsequent use of a domain name to purchase a domain, albeit at a high price. So how to compete with cybersquatters "simple" buyers difficult.

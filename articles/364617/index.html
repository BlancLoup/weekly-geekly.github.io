Testing 4-camera analog surveillance kits



Pry good

Video surveillance is an affordable and effective means of monitoring the situation in any territory. Home or office, cottage or cottage, warehouse or shop - everywhere the video recording system will find practical application. To ensure maximum security of the object, video surveillance can be used in conjunction with other security systems, such as alarms. So our company decided to equip an office building with video surveillance systems, after the security guard ‚Äúblinked‚Äù, as the adjacent cars in the adjacent parking lot were ‚Äúpicked up‚Äù by the cars of three employees. The honorable mission of choosing a video surveillance system went to the author of the article, as the most technically savvy. I decided to share my impressions about choosing a video surveillance system in this review. After all, given the relevance of the installation of video surveillance, our experience is definitely useful to someone.

The principle of operation of the analog video surveillance system is as follows. There are several video cameras in the controlled area. They transmit the image to the DVR - a kind of center for storing and processing information. The recorder is a fairly intelligent device capable of making decisions independently in alarming events: detecting unauthorized traffic, trying to close or damage one of the cameras, etc. In addition, the recorder provides access to the saved video files and screenshots made by the surveillance system. The image from the cameras thanks to the recorder can be transferred to any distance, if the latter is connected to the Internet. That is, even being on another continent, it is easy for the user to control the situation at a remote object equipped with a video surveillance system.

The number of cameras supported by the DVR can vary widely. But usually, for reliable control of the perimeter of a detached house, building, store or warehouse, 4 cameras looking ‚Äúon all four sides‚Äù are sufficient. That is why for testing 4 camera surveillance kits were selected. These are sets of statically mounted cameras that are more affordable. In the presence of 4 cameras, ‚Äústatic‚Äù sets are not much inferior in capabilities to surveillance systems that have camera turning devices and remote control. In addition, cameras without rotary mechanisms safer. This is important for individuals and small companies who install a video surveillance system based on long-term operation, but do not want to constantly bother with the service maintenance of devices. However, if desired, any of the tested kits can be easily retrofitted with PTZ cameras, this functionality is supported. Another interesting point was the appearance on the Ukrainian market of AHD surveillance kits offering enhanced image quality. So far, the choice of AHD equipment is more than modest, but we managed to purchase one such kit for practical testing.

Testing method

The management wished to receive a detailed report with an objective assessment of the video surveillance systems being compared. Well, so that there are no kickbacks there and so on. Therefore, willy-nilly, I had to invent a testing methodology. Surveillance kits will be evaluated on several criteria using a 10-point scale. Plus, we assign a certain coefficient of importance to each criterion - from 0.1 to 1. Why? Well, for example, the user is likely to quickly throw away the packaging set, so it is not so important - you can assign a minimum importance factor of 0.1 (10% importance) to it. But with the convenience of the user interface will have to face constantly throughout the operation of the video surveillance system, this criterion should be assigned the maximum importance factor 1 (100% importance).

The evaluation criteria are as follows:

- Packaging that protects the kit during transport and at the same time attracts the attention of the consumer with its design. Coefficient of importance of 0.1;
- Appearance of the recorder and cameras. It is this criterion that allows devices to best fit into the exquisite landscape design and cozy home interior. The coefficient of importance of 0.8;
- The quality of performance of all the devices in the kit largely determines the operational reliability. The maximum coefficient of importance is 1;
- Completeness determines the completeness of a set of related accessories and cables. The coefficient of importance of 0.5;
- Convenience of connection is better, the less the user experiences with the installation of equipment. The coefficient of importance of 0.7;
- The user manual should even help an ignorant person to understand the technique. The coefficient of importance of 0.5;
- The image quality of the cameras determines how well the video surveillance system ‚Äúsees‚Äù the details of the environment. The maximum coefficient of importance is 1;
- The convenience of the user interface of the recorder is responsible for how comfortable it is to use the video surveillance system for everyday use. The maximum coefficient of importance is 1;
- Price characterizes the availability of goods for the widest possible range of consumers. The coefficient of importance is 0.9;
- The final grade determines how many points the device has gained, taking into account the importance factors for the evaluation criteria. The maximum amount of points is 65. For each device, the table shows the% of the maximum score, showing the degree of satisfaction of the maximum user requests; the higher is the better.

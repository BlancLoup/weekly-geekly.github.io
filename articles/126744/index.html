Hiring VS Outsourcing (in small business projects)

As part of our Unicloud project, we tried both a model of hiring a project of our own specialists, and working with individual freelancers and companies in a contract. In the topic, we argue about the results and their underlying causes.

You can often hear the opinion that outsourcing has not taken root in Russia and is just as rare as Amur tigers. There are many reasons for this: the fear of ‚Äúmerging‚Äù your information to little-known third parties, and the low supply of such services (as a result, the high price), and insufficient quality. Among the reasons they often mention the impossibility of yelling at the outsourced executor and forcing him to work overtime up to 23 hours without additional payment, only because ‚Äúthis is necessary‚Äù. The same with security - a ‚Äúown‚Äù employee may be intimidated by his own security service in such a way that he not only does not sell any information to a competitor himself, but also passes his colleagues who have already done so to the Security Council.

Almost all the reasons mentioned were indirectly inherited from the psychology of the Soviet era, when the employer owed his employees something for life, and a small business did not exist.

In developed countries, outsourcing is a commonplace phenomenon. Almost all branches of Western companies in Russia live according to the principle ‚Äúon their own - only the main activity‚Äù, and all other services (IT, legal, financial, accounting, advertising, logistic, headhunting, etc.) are acquired according to the outsourcing model.

In the well-known book of K. Nordstrom and J. Ridderstrale Funky Business, the authors write that all companies that want to survive in the conditions of global competition will soon switch to a model of deep and narrow specialization. This will mean that all the functions that are performed by the company not in the best way will be outsourced to those who do it perfectly. Everyone will benefit from this - both the company and its customers.

As part of the Unicloud project, we have to attract completely different specialists: sales specialists, designers, developers, PR specialists, Web designers, Web programmers, testers, analysts, user interface designers, lawyers, financiers, accountants, advertising specialists, SMM, architects of complex information systems, as well as many highly specialized companies.

A small business company, only being created or intentionally small to maintain flexibility and speed of change, constantly tries out various development options and tests these options. Today, SMM or a PR specialist who correctly installs a viral company on the Internet may be hotly needed. But this is a temporary action - in a month we will need to work on analyzing the results of such an action and correcting plans, and then a designer will be temporarily required who will draw a new interface scheme for a new system functionality. At this point, a PR specialist is not needed.

The conclusion we made for ourselves is this: many things are in fact temporary for the company, one-time, even if they are repeated on a regular basis. And it is better to entrust such events to professionals.

In addition to attracting from time to time, outsourcing allows you to get the final result instead of a process. If you competently build interaction with the one who does the work, then you will pay only for its result. Compare with your own employees - you simply do not have the lever of such an impact on them, if they are in the state and receive a salary and a bonus. Deprive a premium? The outsourcer works under the contract and receives money for the result. If the result is unimpressive or out of time - you can either avoid the cost of its work altogether, or limit yourself to an advance payment (rarely more than 50%). But the most important thing is that after that you can change the provider of this service for yourself, and you can‚Äôt work with the defendant. So a super-specialist can work (or own a studio), and give you his attention, even if only for a few hours. But these few hours may eventually lead to an order of magnitude better results than if several second league specialists invent the same thing for you.

With such a formulation of the problem, the question of motivation and control is greatly simplified.

Platform and configuration: what do you buy?


All 1C software products consist of two parts - a platform (the foundation on which the software solution runs) and a configuration (a turnkey solution for work). It is important to understand that without a platform no configuration can work. A platform without configuration can only be useful for 1C programmers to create some kind of system from scratch. Sometimes this option is also necessary in the case of extremely specific requirements. But in this case, the user usually turns to a specialist and orders the development of a solution, and therefore there are no questions.

When buying a ready-made ‚Äúboxed solution‚Äù, you purchase a platform together with the selected configuration.

Usually the purchase consists of the following components:

  1. Configuration 1C (Trade Management, Manufacturing Enterprise Management, etc.);
  2. Platform: SQL-solution or file;
  3. Database for network solution: Microsoft SQL Server, PostgreSQL, IBM DB2, Oracle Database. A detailed description of 1C compatibility with databases is available here;
  4. Equipment that may be required for work.

It is important in the selection process to follow the sequence described above. First, the configuration, then the platform, the databases for it, and only after that the available capacities are assessed and the necessary equipment is purchased.

Configuration selection


Configuration is a ready-made software solution that users will work with. Configurations are divided into industry and typical. It is important for the buyer to understand the difference between these solutions.

Typical configurations are solutions created by 1C software developers. In the process of working on a typical configuration, the programmers tried to take into account a variety of work options and business needs. As a result, solutions are cumbersome, they often contain a lot of unnecessary, while some necessary functions will still have to be improved by the specialists in implementing 1C. But in this case, you buy a ready-made ‚Äúbox‚Äù with a platform and configuration, pay its cost plus a license for the number of jobs. Nothing more is needed, of course, except for the introduction of a software product and possible modifications ‚Äúfor you‚Äù.

Industry solutions are created by developers who are 1C partners on the basis of standard solutions. At the same time, they more accurately adapt the configuration to the needs of enterprises of a certain industry, remove obviously unnecessary features and supplement them with documents and reports that are missing in the basic (standard) version. It is quite possible that when choosing an industry solution, you will not need any system modifications during the implementation process or they will be minimal, which will speed up and significantly reduce the cost of implementation.

In most cases, in addition to paying for the cost of an industry solution and buying custom solutions, you will need to separately purchase a license for the 1C platform, which significantly increases the total cost of the product.


Developers of industry solutions usually indicate their price on the websites and only mention in the text about the need to purchase a platform. As a result, when placing an order, buyers often do not take into account additional costs. Therefore, I pay special attention to this moment.

Platform Selection



The 1C platform is the foundation necessary for the operation of any configuration. By default, a platform is provided for installation on a computer with file access to the data, i.e. All information is stored on the main computer in service files. Access to them is provided to all users.

With a small number of users (for example, in the office up to 5 people), the speed of work will be quite satisfactory. If the number of simultaneous access to the database files will increase, the user experience will drop dramatically. For this reason, server solutions are usually chosen. It may also require a separate server and a license for it to organize work in 1C from mobile devices.

When choosing a platform for users, it is important to understand that 1C sells licenses according to the following principle:

  1. Basic set of delivery (license) - platform + typical configuration + license for 1 workplace.
  2. Each additional workplace requires the purchase of a separate license.
  3. The 1C server (in case of choosing the server technology) also requires a separate license. Remember that for the 32-bit and 64-bit versions of these licenses are different from a technical point of view. I recommend choosing a 64-bit solution, despite the fact that the license itself is more expensive. The 32-bit version has limitations on the amount of RAM, and this is the speed and user comfort. If earlier it was not critical, then with the development of technologies and the increasing resource consumption of software products, the limitation of 4 GB of RAM becomes very noticeable.

At the same time, ‚Äúpackages‚Äù are often sold for work: for example, a basic license plus 5 workstations or the purchase of a configuration, a server and 20 workstations with a common set. These are marketing solutions, if you wish, you can always buy a base license separately, separately - jobs in the right quantity and pay for a server solution.

Key options for 1C


Any 1C license, regardless of its type, is provided as a ‚Äúkey‚Äù to the product. At the same time, for example, user licenses (workplaces) remain relevant even in the case of updating the software product or changing the configuration. It is also quite acceptable to transfer the ‚Äúkey‚Äù from the computer to the computer in case of a change of place of work. But at the same time, the key will not work on two devices.

License keys to 1C are divided into two types:

  • Electronic - a set of PIN codes that activate the program. Provided on paper in a sealed envelope. Depending on the type of license, in addition to the main code, which is used when you first install the program, 2 to 5 backup codes are also included in the case of a system reinstallation or replacement of equipment (workplace).
  • Flash keys are special USB-drives on which the license keys for the program are written. This solution is more expensive than an electronic key. And in the case of its loss, unlike electronic replacement, you will have to pay separately. But USB keys allow even without a network server to regularly change the workplace, the key will be activated when the media is connected, regardless of the equipment used. For example, if an accountant works in the office and at home, the flash key will allow him to access the program under a single user license and continue working in a convenient mode.

I recommend in case of any doubts to buy an electronic key. Even if you run out of backup codes, if necessary, you can always get and re-activate the license. In the case of server technology, electronic licenses are calculated by the number of connected without taking into account the features of the equipment. Namely, this option is now almost always chosen except for, perhaps, very small companies (3-5 jobs).

Flash media is worth buying for access to older versions of 1C or in cases like those described in the example above, i.e. when you yourself understand exactly why you need just such a version of the key and for what you overpay and agree to inconvenience in case of loss or damage of the ‚Äúflash drive‚Äù.

Important to know: When working on a remote server, for example, in the case of using a virtual hosting service, using a flash license is not possible.

Rent 1C: SAAS-solutions


Instead of the above-described purchase of numerous components, 1C offers an alternative solution - providing access to the 1C server ‚Äúin the clouds‚Äù. The platform and the configuration you chose are already installed here. Employees 1C monitor the quality of work, update the capacity and conduct server maintenance. In this case, you can in the same way as in the case of the purchase of the 1C configuration, customize it to your needs, make some improvements, connect external reports, etc. On the one hand, the solution is very attractive, and for a small company it is even financially beneficial.

On the other hand, renting 1C also has its drawbacks and limitations:

  • An important limitation concerns access to the database and some service sections that may be required, for example, to integrate the ‚Äúcloud‚Äù 1C with the site. In most cases, this issue can be resolved positively, since restrictions are imposed for the sole purpose of ensuring security. But to resolve the issue will require a long correspondence and coordination of all technical issues with the staff of 1C.
  • Since the system is located on remote "cloud" servers, all documents you need to first upload via the Internet to the server 1C, and only then you can attach them to a message or document. In the case of a large volume of documents this may be inconvenient.
  • All your information will be stored on servers of a third-party company. Such an approach is often contrary to the company's security policy and trade secrets protection rules.
  • For the above reasons, renting 1C is the best choice for small businesses. For large companies, the SAAS solution is not suitable.

I strongly recommend that small and medium businesses carefully calculate the cost of renting 1C, for example, for a year. At the same time pay attention to the technical limitations and determine. Is this solution exactly right for you? When choosing a tariff on the 1C rental page, you will be offered to use a convenient calculator. It will help to compare the cost of renting and purchasing the program. Perhaps when choosing a "cloud" solution to overpay, you will start in 6-8 months. In this case, you will not receive ownership of the software solution, even when you pay the full cost of it.

I am sure that every businessman is able to determine for himself which solution would be more profitable for him in terms of his business. Most importantly, remember the pros and cons, which I listed above.

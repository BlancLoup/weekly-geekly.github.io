As for the negotiations between Google and the Russian ministry, they took place on Wednesday afternoon. They were attended by Doron Avni, Director of the Department for Government Relations in Europe, the Middle East and Africa.

The head of Roskomnadzor, Alexander Zharov, said that during the meeting the parties planned to discuss the issue of connecting Google to the Federal State Information System (FGIS). Participation in it is necessary to remove from the search results of links to Internet resources prohibited in the Russian Federation, localization of personal data of Russian users. For refusing to connect to FGIS, Google can be fined 500-700 thousand rubles. If the company refuses to connect, on November 26, Roskomnadzor will transmit a protocol regarding administrative violation against Google. If Google begins to work in the direction of connecting to a unified register of prohibited information, the result of judicial review may be different, since new circumstances will appear in the case.

As for Google, the corporation had previously announced its readiness for dialogue with the regulator.

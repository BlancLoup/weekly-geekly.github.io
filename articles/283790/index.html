Digest of Russian programs to support IT startups # 3



In today's issue of the digest, we will talk about Russian technology parks. Technoparks are one of the important elements of a modern venture capital ecosystem. The popularity of technology parks in recent years has declined with the advent of many fashionable Western formats supporting innovation. Nevertheless, the leaders of technology parks and IT parks throughout Russia are striving to ensure the success of their residents and create the most comfortable and favorable conditions for them.

We talked with representatives of a number of domestic technology parks and IT parks about how these organizations differ from each other, how they attract residents and which projects they select.

In addition, we were able to talk with Andrei Shpilenko (director of the Association of technoparks in the field of high technologies) and ask a few questions about the current state and prospects of technoparks in Russia.

How could you assess the general state of the technology parks at the All-Russian level? Is there a positive trend in terms of the presence of such organizations in the regions?

The condition is sad. Only a small number of technology parks have all the necessary infrastructure and programs aimed at locating and creating high-tech companies. This state of affairs is due to the fact that in Russia until recently there were no legal documents regulating the activities of technoparks, their functions and tasks. Many ministries in one form or another tried to support the development of the conducting infrastructure, but these were not coordinated actions, but local ones, which did not allow building an effective mechanism for introducing scientific and technical research into the economy.

Could you highlight certain qualities of the leading technoparks of the country, which should be paid attention to similar organizations?

Positive dynamics is observed, it lies in the fact that some officials have finally figured out what a technopark is and that the main task of the technopark's management company is not renting premises or land for rent, but providing a full cycle of development services for new companies. Technopark is not a business project in the traditional sense of the word, providing in a short time a cash inflow for the regional budget, but a mechanism that promotes the creation and development of new companies. Its result is the number of high-tech companies created, the number of jobs, the increase in the taxable base.

I hope that after the creation of the regulatory framework for the innovation infrastructure and the development of a common conceptual apparatus, the governors will have to decide to dismantle their ‚ÄúPotemkin villages‚Äù and begin to create truly effective technology parks.

Today, there are leaders of the technopark movement: first of all, this is the Novosibirsk Academpark, the Technopark of Mordovia, the Khanty-Mansiysk Technopark. These technology parks have all the necessary infrastructure and programs to reduce costs in the implementation of projects. A distinctive feature of these technology parks is that they were able to create a complete system, combining elements of the innovation infrastructure, functionally supporting projects at each stage of the life cycle from idea to production.

What is the main challenge for institutes supporting technology business today? Could you offer your own vision of possible solutions?

The main challenge for innovation support institutions is the need to overcome disunity, developing clear, understandable rules and regulations for each stage of support for innovative startups. There should be no gaps in the chain of commercialization. After all, if you carefully analyze the path from idea to product, it is clear that support institutions are most likely to work at the sales stage, and very few people are willing to work at the R & D stage, experimental production. At the same time, the main problem is not even the lack of money, but the multi-vector activity of ministries, departments, development institutions, which are obliged to contribute to the development of an innovative economy.



Our questions to technoparks representatives


1. What is your technopark profile? Are there any particularities in terms of the approach to the selection of projects that distinguish it (technopark) from other similar organizations?

Sergey Teplov, General Director, KP Technopark "Strogino"
Technopark "Strogino" has a universal specialization, but mainly on the territory are located companies implementing projects in the field of information technology, medicine, biotechnology, and instrument-making. Since The Technopark was established by the Government of Moscow, the project selection process has several stages: the primary selection of projects, the Expert Council, the Competition Commission.

The Expert Council allows a more detailed assessment of the project from the point of view of practical implementation and feasibility regarding market conditions. Expert and mentoring support is provided free of charge. Experts are chosen in such a way that, in addition to theoretical knowledge, they also have professional practical knowledge on the project topic and can quickly evaluate the project and give comprehensive recommendations on the company's business profile.

The competitive commission where representatives of the Government of Moscow enter, makes the decision by results of the expert conclusion. The Technopark has developed its own mechanism and criteria for the selection of projects, which allows you to immediately assess the future development of the project, identify existing problems and identify ways to resolve them.

Ksenia Temnikova, Development Director, Technopark Slava OJSC
Technopark Slava OJSC is an infrastructure object for the innovation activities of the city of Moscow. Functionally, the property complex of the technology park is adapted to accommodate enterprises engaged in R & D and R & D that have created their own production (for this, the necessary energy resources, water, etc. are available). Today, more than 60 high-tech small and medium-sized enterprises engaged in development and production in the following areas are located in the industrial park:

We have developed a multistage system for selecting projects for residents. At the first stage, applicants' projects are selected by specialists of the management company according to two criteria: compliance with one of the technology park's activities and the novelty of the claimed development.

At the preliminary examination stage, the scientific component of the applicant‚Äôs stated project, its innovativeness and the possibility of commercialization are assessed.

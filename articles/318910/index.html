First of all, let's find out on what hardware and software the Laboratory of Absolute Surprises operates, whose employees are Kovrov and Bryl, mentioning the cancellation of the action and the letter Z. A careful viewing of the film brings us this find:



Behind the heroes is the EC computer 1045, one of the models included in the ‚ÄúUnified system of electronic computers‚Äù, which has been developed and produced in the USSR since 1971.

Specifically, the EU model 1045 was produced from 1979, had a capacity of up to 800,000 operations per second and RAM up to 4 MB. In total, 1865 of such bags were built, so at the time of the shooting (1982), the Laboratory of Absolute Surprises can be considered fairly well equipped.



With iron sorted out. Now go to the software. Here we need to remember why and how the computers of the EC series of computers and their software were developed. In view of the insufficient resources of the USSR to develop from scratch hardware and software platforms that are comparative in their capabilities with products from the western world, it was decided to create both hardware and software compatible with the very popular and widely used IBM System / 360 and IBM System mainframe / 370 . This made it possible to ‚Äúpeep‚Äù the right technical solutions, use ready-made peripherals, analyze the source code of the programs, run the original software from IBM (which by the standards of those years existed incredibly much). We have already come to understand that the EU 1045 mainframe shown in the film (developed in 1979) could have run the IBM System / 370 operating system and use the text editor File Retrieval and Editing System (developed in 1968) with the Undo function. . Those. the very concept of the cancellation operation and even its implementation in a specific text editor could be well known to the main characters due to the circumstances of the film.

Now let's talk about the invocation of the Undo action using the Ctrl + Z combination (or any other with the letter Z). I shoveled a lot of information, but didn‚Äôt find a description of how the cancel command was run in the File Retrieval and Editing System editor (maybe some readers know how to google better than me?). But what is absolutely certain is that the combination Ctrl + Z was used to undo actions in the Bravo text editor , created in 1974 by programmers from Xerox PARC . It is to them that all official sources (for example, The New York Times ) attribute the authorship of the idea to bind the Ctrl + Z combination to the Undo function. Considering the track record of the inventions of the Xerox PARC division, it is quite possible to believe this.

So, now we know that at the time of the creation of the EU 1045 for 11 years, there was an implementation of the Undo function (in the EU 1045 compatible software) and 5 years as there was a keyboard shortcut Ctrl + Z, as a hot key to call Undo. From the creation of the EU 1045 to filming the film took another 3 years, which also contributed to the spread of software and knowledge of its functions.

From here we can conclude that the characters of the film (as well as its authors) could consciously and convincingly mention the cancel function and its connection with the Latin letter Z as a reference to the Undo function and the Ctrl + Z key combination in the software of those years.

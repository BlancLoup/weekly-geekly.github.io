Wolfram's Scientific Search Engine - Launch Tonight!

Today at 19.00 CST (4:00 Moscow time) the launch of the search engine WolframAlpha , which is called one of the most anticipated web products of the current year, will take place . A lot of talk goes around this mysterious novelty, and those who tried the system in action are simply impressed with the work that ingenious Mr. Wolfram himself did (he is the creator of the Mathematica program, and before that, at the age of 15, he wrote his first scientific work on particle physics) and Over 100 researchers have selected facts for the mathematical engine of the WolframAlpha system for several years.

The new search engine will be the world's first universal search engine of scientific knowledge. Absolutely all scientific reference books, encyclopedias in all fields of knowledge were scored there. He is not even going to compete with Google, but he will be much more useful to Wikipedia for searching and processing scientific information.

We publish dozens of screenshots taken during a preview of the alpha version of the system, held for journalists a week ago, under the habrakat. Pay attention to how the search engine processes search requests and what mountain of information, graphs it gives back. Plus, the Alpha API will initially be integrated with Mathematica.

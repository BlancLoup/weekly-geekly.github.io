Blueseed - ship startups



Silicon Valley is a Mecca of IT startups around the world. There was a unique cultural and economic environment, but getting there, not being a US citizen, is quite difficult. Startup Blueseed offers to solve all problems with visas and immigration in a simple and elegant way - to place a ship or platform with all the necessary infrastructure outside the territorial waters of the United States. The ship will be anchored 12 miles off the coast of California. In order to live and work on it, a visa is not needed. A regular tourist or business visa (B1 / B2), which can be obtained relatively easily, is enough to regularly visit the beach. The ferry overcomes the distance to the shore in half an hour. Boat or helicopter - even faster. Citizens of the United States can visit the ship startups without restrictions.



Blueseed hopes to launch the first floating startup incubator in Q3 2013. The cost of living and renting the working space will range from $ 1,200 to $ 3,000 per person, which is not that expensive even without ocean view and much more fresh air than, for example, in San Francisco. The project is financed by well-known venture capitalist and investor Peter Thiel.

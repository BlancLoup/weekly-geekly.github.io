Experience of implementing Ubuntu in a non-state educational institution

Hello, dear habrovchane!

I would like to share with you my experience of implementing Ubuntu Linux in the enterprise, and to make it even more interesting, I will tell you how I did it in a higher educational institution. Are you still thinking about transferring the company where you work to open source software? Then, perhaps, you will find answers to some of your questions under the cut.

So, let's begin.


It so happened that I work in the same place where I study. With your permission, I will not publish the name of the university, which will be discussed. I will give you only a little information so that you can describe your situation more clearly. This institution of higher education is a non-state educational institution of a humanitarian orientation, a rather large contingent of students. We are located in the Volga region, the city is not small, but the capital is still far away :)

So, it so happened that in the next, coming 2010, the year we will suffer the fate of any educational institution, namely, accreditation. Evil uncles and aunts come and begin to torment the rector, vice-rectors, ordinary workers and, of course, students. But everything would be fine, but the experience of passing such accreditation in the adjacent university showed that some especially corrosive testers also check the licensing of software installed on the organization‚Äôs computers. In connection with this circumstance, which could bring down the entire procedure, by me, to optimize the means of the organization, a project was proposed to transfer almost all PCs to open source software. Plan approved.

Of course, I had experience with Linux. But it was a distribution company NetUP! based on Knoppix, as well as Knoppix itself, ordered from an online store and even once installed on an old computer. Also, the book ‚ÄúLinux for Dummies‚Äù was read well, plus, of course, the Internet.

When choosing a distribution, I was guided, firstly, by full localization and logical interface, and secondly, by my own intuition. Which let me down twice. I started with ALT Linux. I do not want to throw stones in anyone's garden, but somehow he ... is unclear. For the initial mastering, it seemed clumsy (stupid girls with peroxide hair would sit with him at the PC). The second time, the devil pulled the download of Linux XP. Solemnly demolished an hour after installation. No comments.
Fortunately, the mind won and I downloaded and installed Ubuntu 9.04, then another beta, first on my own laptop, and then, as I understand it, on computers in a computer class. Well, on the rise. Currently, about a hundred computers are working under Ubuntu 9.04 and 9.10.

So, the problems that I encountered. The order of spontaneous, as the recall.

1. Well, of course, yelling and screaming employees. Unusual, uncomfortable, unusual solitaire, everything is different. Naturally, all dissatisfaction either subsided on its own, or I suppressed it, explaining how to work with the new system, or (in especially frost-baked cases) I had to submit the question to the authorities.
By the way, three purchased books on the subject of Linux and OpenOffice so no one has ever asked. And I offered!

4. Search for drivers. Glory to Ubunt, there were no problems with the drivers for the components. Drivers for Nvidia after training were installed in poltychka, with the other guts no problems. Now for the learning process.
Since Ubuntu was installed on all 54 PCs in computer labs, it was necessary to rewrite computer science manuals in accordance with the new system interface and looking at its features.
I was very surprised and pleased with the reaction of students. There was no "psychos". All students simply followed the instructions of the teacher in the performance of tasks in the classroom. In my opinion, the difference for first-year students, on which OS they will be trained is zero! Well, that brains have not lost their flexibility. Well, I will not be distracted.
For some special items, like Information Management in Management, we had to additionally install Gnumeric computers (it has advanced tools for computing and graphing, rather than OpenOffice Calc).

The main work performed by our employees on computers is a printout of statements, schedules and other routine electronic paper work. There are no special programs, because the transfer to Linux was basically painless, considering, of course, the problems listed above.

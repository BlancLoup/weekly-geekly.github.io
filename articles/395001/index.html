Ilon Musk about the expedition to Mars: "It is dangerous, and people will probably die"

But the settlement of Mars, in his opinion - the only reliable way to save humanity


Dragon spacecraft on Mars (artist's work)

Back in 2014, Ilon Musk gave an extensive interview regarding plans for settling Mars. According to the entrepreneur, settling the Red Planet is necessary for the future of mankind - this is a reliable way to preserve our civilization. By the year 2100, according to Musk, there should be about a million people on Mars. All of them will be able to provide genetic diversity, sufficient for the development of the entire population of "Martians". This should be done on the basis of several considerations. First, settling the neighboring planet will allow preserving civilization if a catastrophe occurs on the Earth itself. Secondly, the colonization of the planets of the solar system is something like a springboard for a man to reach the stars. This is a prototype of a planetary colony that will help settle any other planets in any other region of the Universe. Elon Musk even expressed such an opinion, understandable without translation: ‚ÄúFuck Earth! Who cares about Earth?

True, the descendants of the first colonists will reap the benefits of settling on Mars, and they themselves will have difficulty. The main problem is the inability to receive outside help. If some kind of minor breakdown happens (small by the standards of the Earth), then in the conditions of Mars it can become critical. All this is known, but does not affect either the desire of thousands of people to become the very first movers, nor the desire of Mask to provide these people with transport to Mars. He himself founded SpaceX 14 years ago with only one goal - the preparation of a manned manned expedition to Mars.

Starting in 2018, Spacex plans to send an unmanned aerial vehicle to Mars every two years. This period is chosen because the Earth and Mars converge on the minimum distances just every two years. If all goes well, the person will be sent to the Red Planet as early as 2025. By the way, NASA also plans to send people to Mars, but a little later - by 2030.

And he recognizes the difficulties that the colonists from Earth will face. The main difficulty is the distance of Mars from Earth for many millions of kilometers. The first flights will have to clarify some points in interplanetary navigation and allow the company to master the smooth landing of the ship to Mars.

Computex 2012, or Ultrashow in Taiwan

Today in the capital of Taiwan, the exhibition Computex has started. "Getting Started" in this case is not the turn of speech, but the most appropriate description of what is happening. The element of the show on Computex is relatively small, and became more or less noticeable only in recent years. And initially it is a purely business exhibition where manufacturers of the most diverse IT products show their achievements and immediately offer to buy them.

The presence of Intel on Computex is always felt, but this year it is stronger than ever. No, seriously, the main innovations presented yesterday, on a zero day, are based on third-generation Core processors (also known as Ivy Bridge) and Intel Atom. In this post there will be a lot of photos from different stands of the main Taiwanese IT exhibition, but believe me, this is only a very small and carefully selected part of the products that use Intel solutions. Well, admire?



Acer, it seems, did not show any new product without a touch screen, and it does not matter that it is - an ultrabook, tablet computer or candy bar. For some reason, Acer do not like to use even numbers in the names of their top products: the first ultrabook was called Aspire S3, the second Aspire S5, and the third ... yes, that's right, Aspire S7. True, this time the name is one, and the ultrabook is two - with a diagonal of 11.6 and 13 inches (I wonder where they peeped at this idea? :)).



At the same time, regardless of the size of the matrix, its resolution is 1920x1080 pixels (FullHD), and the picture is just perfectly clear. The ultrabook angle is as much as 180 degrees, and many will like it. The battery life promises an impressive - up to 9 hours in the smaller ultrabook and up to 12 hours in the 13-inch. True, we can be sure of the truthfulness of the promises only in the fall, when Windows 8 is released: all the capabilities of the touch screens will be revealed only under it.

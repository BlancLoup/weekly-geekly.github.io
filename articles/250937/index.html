I want to speak at DUMP-2015


The program directors of each section identified some topics that we would be interested to hear this year (the design and spelling of the authors are preserved).

So,
FrontTalks:
Hello! We, Oleg Mokhov and Valeria Atamanova, work in Yandex and present the FrontTalks section at the DUMP-2015 conference. Last year, the section under our leadership turned out to be extremely interesting, and we intend to repeat the success.

Of course, you are aware of the many changes in the world of Frontend. Web components are sweeping across the planet, ECMAScript 6 implementation continues, React has entered into an unequal battle with Angular.js, and upstart io.js will try to fray with the already familiar node.js. Do not share anywhere and problems with old browsers - IE8 is still alive and bothering us. We are sure that you did not stay aside from the trends, and during the year did a lot of interesting things that you would like to tell your colleagues.

For all those who wish to speak and share progressive ideas and best practices, our mail fronttalks@ya.ru works. And we are waiting for everyone else at the conference. We guarantee a rich program and fruitful communication.

Design:
A few years ago, there were few UX / UI specialists, and this area was actively developing. Today, interfaces have occupied almost the most important place in the process of developing any product or even service. We can say that in our relatively young industry, it is time for youth.

Where are we and what will happen next? What are the possibilities of applying our knowledge, and in what direction will the industry develop together with us? What tools have we begun to use, and what are we still lacking? At the conference, we propose to consider this period of development from all sides, be it working with users, researching or working with a specific tool.

Pay attention to the business aspects that may affect the management of development. Of course, this is only the tip of the iceberg. Representatives of companies of various sizes are ready to share their experience again and tell you how not to step on the ‚Äúrake‚Äù at all or at least! - do it carefully.

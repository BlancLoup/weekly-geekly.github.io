Google's Project Loon is ready to launch balloons



The head of Project Loon, engineer Mike Kessidy, announced that his team was very close to launching a balloon system into the atmosphere. He told about this, the results of the work and the current state of affairs in a small video published a few days ago. A test launch of the system will be conducted to ensure LTE communication in hard-to-reach areas of Australia and New Zealand.

The industrial approach to the project is indicated by the fact that the project engineers took care of creating a special automated production of the balloons themselves. Cassidy says it took the team three or four days to start one balloon. Now the process has changed dramatically: the production of balls is occupied by an automated line, which can produce about a dozen balls per day.

The entire past year, the project was in the testing stage, following which a team of engineers announced its successful completion. For example, it was found that the balloons could be in the air for quite a long time without damage due to the influence of the aggressive environment of the upper atmosphere.

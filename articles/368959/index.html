The second Blackberry Android smartphone has a physical keyboard and is supposedly called Vienna



On the Internet, by submitting the resource CrackBerry , photos of a second Android-smartphone are being distributed , which the Canadian Blackberry should release after its flagship PRIV. As far as can be judged by the rendered renders, the main feature of the new device will be a physical fixed QWERTY-keyboard, paying tribute to the company's previously known phones. Presumably the name of this smartphone will sound like Vienna. Already marketed for $ 699, the Blackberry PRIV smartphone also has a physical keyboard, but is made in a slider form factor.

Actually, no other details about Vienna are known, except that it is likely to be released in several different colors. If we cautiously assume that PRIV and Vienna will be similar not only visually, but also by their technical stuffing, we can expect that the latter will use a Qualcomm Snapdragon 808 processor with Adreno 418 graphics, the amount of RAM will be 3 GB, and the battery life 3410 mAh will be approximately 22 hours. The operating system will be Android 5.1.1 Lollipop.

Earlier, Blackberry CEO John Chen (John Chen) said that if in 2015 the company does not make a profit from the unit producing smartphones, it will leave the business, focusing on software and information security. As far as can be judged from preliminary data in the media, the released PRIV still enjoys some popularity: the batch of devices, the pre-order for which was opened two weeks before the start of sales, turned out to be sold very quickly. True, it is not known how many smartphones were sold.

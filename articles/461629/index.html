0. Writing a wireframe resume


In order to write a resume, you must write a resume :-) These will be the first drafts of your resume. To begin, indicate all your places of work, what position they occupied, what tasks they performed. Describe ALL the actions that were performed at each workplace, even if it seems insignificant to you.
When applying for a job, you should always record significant events in your work. Since then you can forget about them, and in the resume they will be stored for a rainy day.
If you participated in something - big or small - record in the resume. This may be the execution of some action (writing a plug-in for sending mail \ organizing a meeting of shareholders \ setting up a mail server, etc.) or passing training, or participating in a seminar. If you have undergone additional training before or during work, be sure to include it in the resume.

Indicate your personal experience with any technology, program. Or a book on Time Management, which you know by heart (the latter is best indicated in the About section).
If you want to start working as a designer and at the same time have finished your artwork, BE SURE to indicate this! For creative professions, I also recommend filling out a portfolio of all my works, absolutely any, including those that I painted by hand or did for fun on a computer.

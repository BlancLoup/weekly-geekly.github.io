Business-oriented storage systems ETERNUS DX S3

Earlier we already talked about updating the Fujitsu server line , which happened this fall. Also in early November of the outgoing year, Fujitsu released an updated family of storage systems ETERNUS DX S3. This update can be called revolutionary, as the performance of some models has increased 5 times. But first things first.



The new line of Fujitsu ETERNUS DX S3 is truly business oriented. Its development was made based on the real requirements of the business to such products. And only then, on the basis of these requirements, were the appropriate technologies and solutions selected or developed. In fact, it can be said that it is the business requirements that formed the basis of the design specification for the design of the updated family.

Thus, for the first time in the market, a new interface has been used for connecting SAS3 disks, providing data rates up to 12 Gb / s, which allows up to two times faster access to data placed on disks.

The next important change is the flexible and fine-tuning of the system of priorities and automatic quality of service (QoS), which allow, depending on the business objectives, to provide guaranteed levels of service for various processes. So a new generation of ETERNUS DX family allows you to allocate a certain amount of cache memory, a certain bandwidth, to guarantee the required number of input / output operations (IOPS) for a particular application, for a particular group of users, or for a particular logical volume. In other words, you just need to determine the priorities and response time for the tasks and applications used, depending on their importance to the business, ETERNUS DX S3 will do the rest. Also, based on the results of the work, billing of the use of resources by certain tasks can be done, which may be applicable, for example, to adjust the established priorities or to correlate the costs of operating the system to a particular project.

First, the ETERNUS DX S3 offers unique scalability within a single model. All models of this family are the so-called Real Unified Storage. This means that they can be used as SAN and as NAS-storage simultaneously. Another interesting solution that will be available in June of this year is the Fast Rebuild technology developed by Fujitsu. It is designed to solve the bottleneck problem that occurs if one of the disks fails. In this case, the disk on which data recovery is performed becomes ‚Äúsuper hot‚Äù, since until the completion of the rebuild, data will be written to it at once from all disks of the array. Usually the distribution of I / O operations in this case is 1 to 16, i.e. at 16 I / O, which will serve the hosts, only one will serve the process of restoring the array. Thus, the rebuild process when using large disks (2TB and above) can last a week or even more.

It is to solve this problem and technology was developed Fast Rebuild. Its essence lies in the fact that free backup blocks are initially placed on each of the disks in the array (Reserved - in the diagram below). Thus, data recovery is performed immediately to all disks in these areas, which solves the bottleneck problem and speeds up the recovery procedure at times.



At the same time, it also remains possible to change the priority of a rebuild, and its change can be configured according to a specific schedule. For example, so that the priority of a rebild is increased during off-hours, significantly speeding up the recovery process.

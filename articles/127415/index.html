I, in turn, timed this release of the new version of the site Rusrails.ru , where I publish my translations of the official Rails guide .

I worked on the new version of Rusrails somewhere from the beginning of the summer in my spare time.


What was done

I got rid of Joomla, and wrote a simple site on Rails 3.0, with a convenient admin panel for me, page caching and searching with the help of a sphinx.

Updated existing manual translations. There were mostly minor editorial edits, but there were also large sections that had been modified. In general, the translation is now relevant for Rails 3.1.

Translations of each manual are tied to individual revisions of github.com/lifo/dorails , and now it has become very easy to track down the defamations, which has changed in a separate manual. Previously, it was all done "by eye", and something could be missed.

All old material on Rails 3.0 and 2.3 was put into the archive .

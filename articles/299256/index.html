8 quotes from the past about the Internet of things

Only lazy today does not discuss the Internet of things (IoT), as one of the most promising areas. Various hypotheses are being built, models are being designed, budgets are considered, a great future is predicted, directions for the future development of things on the Internet are specified.
I would like to add a bit of variety to this discussion and quote some interesting quotes from great people. Immediately, I note that these quotes do not relate directly to IoT. They were told about other things and at the time when the Internet was not yet in the project. But they are really interesting, and in order to explain why they are so interesting with regard to the Internet of things, small comments have been added to them.



Nikolai Yegorovich Zhukovsky in 1898:
"A man will fly, relying not on the strength of his muscles, but on the strength of his mind"


The potential of the Internet of things is not in the performance and design features of equipment, sensors, controllers and other end devices connected by a single communications network, but in ideas and solutions, in technologies and models that can be realized with its help. Only on how reasonable will the approach to the development of IoT depend on its future.




Francis Bradley, at the beginning of the 20th century:
"When the machines become too smart, we will unite them in the commission - this will kill them"


Complex systems in which many objects interact - these are complex structures for management and control. A constant stream of generated data, conflicts of interest, disruption of interaction, inconsistency of goals, desynchronization of processes - this and much more is characteristic of large network communities. There is something to work on.
In the very quotation, F. Bradley rather scoffs at bureaucratic unproductive procedures. But after all, the Internet of things basically must apply simple, but effective procedural regulations and standards. ‚ÄúBuffering‚Äù IoT is easy enough, but what will it give for its development.

Interaction in the global information network is associated with various security issues and is subject to serious risks. The active expansion of the quantity and quality of objects included in the constant information exchange, and even capable of independently implementing meaningful actions, sets unprecedented challenges for IoT technologies. Among them are the following issues:
- is it possible to include things into the network by any person or only those with certain powers?
- what is allowed to do things online?
- what powers can a thing have on the Internet?
- who can manage interconnected network objects?
- How to protect yourself from unauthorized access to separate networks and falsified things on the Internet that can impersonate others?
- what risk events are possible with the Internet of things and how to eliminate them?
Today there is no doubt that mutual direct declarative trust between things on the Internet is impossible. Special safety standards and procedures are required.

This is a system that requires such extensive and diverse knowledge, tools, technologies, standards, research and practical skills that it is impossible to develop IoT outside the global coordinated community.

From Google+ Hangouts you can now make calls to phone numbers.



In the sense that now you can already make regular phone calls, and not just communicate within the Hangouts service itself. Free calls to the phone, as always, bypassed Europeans, this feature is available (free calls) only for Americans and Canadians. In general, the Hangouts, as before, is available to everyone. According to eyewitnesses, the quality of video communication is still slightly worse than in the competitive Skype VoIP application.

An interesting feature of Hangouts is that a whole group of Hangouts can communicate with a person, that is, you can hold a teleconference at the same time with the participation of telephone users and those users who are currently working with Hangouts. In this case, of course, teleconference participants who communicate by phone do not need to have a Google account. This feature is likely to attract even more users to the Hangouts, and accordingly, the Google+ social network will have even more members.

According to the developers of Hangouts, they have long since begun to receive requests from users about providing the opportunity to communicate with users from Hangouts using a regular phone.

How it works?


At the heart of everything is the special ‚Äúpaper‚Äù Zero Ink. As in the case of the famous glass Gorilla Glass, it was developed for a long time, but remembered the technology a few years ago. The photographic material itself is multi-layered, the base layer is white, the rest are specialized protective and ‚Äúcoloring‚Äù layers containing a special crystallized dye.



Microscopic crystals are almost 100% transparent to light, but if they are heated to the correct temperature, they will lose their structure and turn into a stain of dye that will be absorbed into the paper layer of the photographic material.



The color of each point is determined by the time and temperature of heating.

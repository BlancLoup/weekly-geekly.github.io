Sound:
Monoprice Premium Hi-Fi DJ MHP-839 headphones have 50mm drivers. Below I will describe the technical parameters, and then my impressions of the sound.


frequency range
The frequency range is probably the most meaningless parameter in the characteristic and more used by marketers. A person hears in the range of 20 Hz - 20 kHz, at the beginning of this range there are low-frequency sounds (bass), at the top - high-frequency sounds. The task of any headphones is to correctly reproduce this range, without failures, without wheezing and a bass hum, without hissing and rattling on HF. This ability reflects the amplitude-frequency characteristic (AFC) and is usually represented as a graph of the dependence of volume on frequency. Therefore, the smoother and smoother the curve, the more accurate and clear the sound. Unfortunately, I did not manage to find the frequency response graph from the manufacturer, but the 20Hz-20kHz range is indicated on the headphone box.

Sensitivity (sound pressure)
Sensitivity is the headphone efficiency: the larger it is, the louder the headphones sound, regardless of the source. Monoprice Premium Hi-Fi DJs are very loud, the manufacturer indicates 100 ¬± 3 dB, which corresponds to the fact of measurement using the VUMeter program, which can be found on the Apple Store or Google Market ...


Load impedance
For Monoprice Premium Hi-Fi DJ, the manufacturer set the resistance to 40 ohms. If you do not go into radio engineering, you can say the following: the greater the resistance, the more powerful source is needed for headphones and the less influence the source itself has on the sound. In other words, the headphones will work fine with both players and more powerful sources, such as DJ "turntables".

The Pirate Bay begins testing anonymizer

As reported in the official blog of The Pirate Bay , the world's largest torrent portal began beta testing of the Internet anonymizer IPREDator, which is currently accessed by about 3,000 people.



The IPREDator Internet anonymizer was announced at the end of March and is intended to neutralize the Intellectual Property Rights Enforcement Directive (IPRED), which allows law enforcement agencies in Sweden to collect any personal data about users suspected of illegal file sharing.

The creators of the anonymizer emphasize that they are not going to transfer information about their users at the request of the authorities, in contrast to the usual anonymizers, and they will not store the traffic passing through it.

IPREDator is a paid service, the subscription fee for access to it is five euros per month, any network user can work with it.

Currently, almost 180 thousand people have already registered on IPREDator, and all of them will get access to the service within a month. And after that, everyone can work with the anonymizer.

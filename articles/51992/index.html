Sequencers.


Sequencer (or sequencer, from the English. "Sequence" - the sequence) - a hardware or software device for recording and playing MIDI messages. The principle of the sequencer is that a MIDI device, such as a keyboard synthesizer, MIDI keyboard, controller, drum machine, etc., sends a stream of MIDI messages containing complete information about the tempo, selected voices, and the notes played , effect settings, etc. The sequencer records these messages in the internal memory for later playback. Thus, the sequencer in its functions is completely analogous to a tape recorder (and most often equipped with a tape recorder type control), except that it records not audio data, but commands for various MIDI devices.

VSTi (VST instruments) and VST effects.


Virtual Studio Technology (VST) is a format of resource-dependent (native) real-time plug-ins developed by Propellerhead and, subsequently, transmitted by Steinberg. Currently there are hundreds of plug-ins in this format, it has become one of the most common formats for sound programs. VST applications differ from DirectX plug-ins in several ways; in particular, they exist for Windows, Mac OS X and Linux. In addition, unlike earlier versions of DirectX, VST plug-ins have an advanced automation interface.

This is taken from wikipedia .

VST plug-ins connect to sequencers and some audio editor as plug-ins and serve like all plug-ins to extend or change functionality.

VST plug-ins are of two types:

- VST-tools (VSTi).
- VST effects.

Semplers


Sampler (English sampler) - an electronic musical instrument that allows you to record, edit and play sounds. The difference from other electromusical instruments (EMI), for example synthesizers, is that instead of oscillators (wave generators), a recorded (digitized sound) recorded in memory is used, which is expanded on a MIDI keyboard, changing the pitch according to specified conditions. Currently, there are many such devices and their virtual counterparts. Often a sampler is an option or basis for an EMP. The release of publicly available digital samplers stimulated the emergence of new musical trends, primarily Hip-Hop, UK Hardcore, Acid house.

This is taken from wikipedia .

Of course, at the moment ‚Äúiron‚Äù samplers are of little interest to us, but it was still informative;)

So, sampler. A sampler is a program that plays back previously recorded audio files. The sampler itself is nothing, it cannot synthesize sound, but can only reproduce the already recorded one. But this is his main advantage!

You want, for example, to add some ‚Äúlive‚Äù instruments to your track, for example, a cello. At present, it is impossible to synthesize the sound of such an instrument; technologies do not allow it. But to record a cello sound in a studio using professional equipment is possible. Then the recorded sounds are edited in a special way and packed into sound banks. Now we insert the ‚Äúbank‚Äù into our favorite sampler (for example, NI Kontakt 2) and enjoy the sounds of our favorite instrument.

The only minus of samplers is the size of the libraries for them. 10-20 GB is the normal size for a sound bank, but you have to pay for quality.

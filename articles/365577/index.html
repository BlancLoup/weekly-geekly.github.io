How it works


Traditionally, to seed a little bit of materiel - let's see how exactly the caffeine ‚Äúworks‚Äù in our bodies after getting there by pouring a cup of coffee in our mouth :)

A key role in the work of caffeine is played by its base - xanthine, a simple compound of the purine series. When drunk coffee enters the small intestine and begins to be absorbed, xanthine interacts with the adenosine receptors A1 and A2 located on the cellular membranes of smooth muscles, glandular cells, the central nervous system and other organs. Here the most interesting begins: the A2 receptor increases the content of CAMP (cyclic adenosine monophosphate), and the A1 receptor, which reduces its level, is blocked and stops working. Thus, only A2 remains in the ranks, which launches a whole range of reactions, obtaining the cherished caffeine:

  • relaxation of vascular smooth muscle;
  • improvement of blood flow in the coronary, cerebral and renal vessels;
  • relaxes smooth muscles of the bronchi, intestines and ureters;
  • blood flow increases due to a decrease in platelet aggregation;
  • and, of course, the most important thing is the elimination of the inhibitory effect of adenosine on the central nervous system.

From this point on, adenosine ceases to inhibit the central nervous system, overclocked blood provides the brain with a good supply of oxygen - these effects of caffeine are a guarantee that drowsiness flies off us, the thinking process accelerates and mood improves, that is, everything that we drink coffee is happening for. Well, except for taste, of course. :)

So how do you need to drink coffee so that the harmful effects do not set the weather, while the beneficial ones bring maximum benefit?

The most controversial topic about which many copies always break - how many cups a day can you drink without showing negative effects? Here and age matters, and chronic diseases, and BMI (body mass index). But the general recommendation will be the following for all adults - no more than 3-4 cups throughout the day with intervals of at least 2 hours . Based on the average caffeine content in a 220 mg espresso cup, this is no more than 1 gram of caffeine per day .



Coffee on an empty stomach is definitely harmful, so if you have the habit of starting the day with just one cup of espresso without even a sandwich, then it's better to say goodbye to it. If breakfast after an early rise, in principle, does not come to you (he is familiar with such a problem firsthand) - be patient, wait until the stomach warms up and the appetite wakes up. And after a snack already and coffee can be drunk.

In general, the best way is to prepare spicy coffee according to recipes, but not from fashion magazines, but from cookbooks and cookbooks. And yes, it is better to finish such a dense meal with such coffee, as spices on a fasting alliance with caffeine can irritate your digestive tract on an empty stomach.

Caffeine and alcohol have opposite effects on the body and the central nervous system and at the same time not only are not mutually compensated, but even on the contrary begin to drag the blanket.

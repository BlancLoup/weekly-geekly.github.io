Visit to Juniper Networks office

Just last week, during the European voyage with the arrival at CEBIT, the Oversun-Mercury flying brigade paid a friendly visit to the office of the Dutch company Juniper Networks, located near the glorious city of Amsterdam. Juniper Networks produces backbone network equipment and is currently experiencing rapid growth. It is one of the few Old World manufacturers who can compete with powerful Cisco and dumping Chinese. We will soon tell about Juniper products separately, and in this post we will talk about the company's office, and more precisely about the Customer Briefing Center. This is a separate structure with two data centers designed for visits by Juniper partners and customers. Here it is easy to discuss any issues, and, most importantly, to see the equipment at work, in a ‚Äúlive‚Äù data center.

One of them serves only for demonstrations, while in the second data center, exact or close to that copies of the systems actually installed at large customers of the scale of British Telecom are created. This allows, on the one hand, to demonstrate truly powerful solutions to all interested parties, and on the other hand, to instantly simulate problem situations, providing technical support in almost real time. This luxury is obviously available only to the most serious customers, but the approach itself produces a very favorable impression.

Canon G11: less megapixels

The new compact DSLR Canon G11 is notable for the fact that the manufacturer reduced the number of megapixels in it. In the previous model, the G10 was 14.7 MP, and in the G11 it was only 10 MP. At the same time, the physical size of the photosensitive CCD sensor was increased and the quality of photos in all respects only improved, especially at high ISO values.

In the new camera, the old lens remained, and the LCD screen became foldable. There was a shooting mode in RAW and some other not very significant features. That is the main innovation - it is an improved CCD with a smaller number of elements.



Now we see that manufacturers of digital cameras in fact refuse to race megapixels, as promised earlier. Recall half a year ago, the head of the Olympus SLR camera division said that 12 megapixels is enough to cover "almost all the needs of most users."

Thus, it is currently asking how many megapixels in the camera are full moveton and display of lamer.

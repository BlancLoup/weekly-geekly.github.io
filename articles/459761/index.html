Modern bike (and even more so e-bike) is a complex technical mechanism. And he, like any other mechanism, requires maintenance. In this regard, it is not much different, for example, from a car, at the purchase of which you are given a service book with regulated works. Change oil, filters, shoes, discs, plugs, shock absorbers, belts / chains, etc. - during operation everything wears out or breaks, everything has its own service life.

It's easier with a bicycle than with a car: regardless of the manufacturer, there is quite a high compatibility in terms of components, you can almost always get the right one and the prices of any components are not so biting (if we are not talking about some professional products). Yes, and something needs to change less often - in most cases, you can get rid of simple maintenance. Almost all the works do not require any special tools - a set of keys / hexagons / screwdrivers is enough.

In a good way, it is necessary to check / maintain the bicycle components before each departure:

  • Tire pressure (for MTB at least 2 atmospheres)
  • Chain condition (contamination, lubrication, interference)
  • Brakes (condition of disc and pads, adjustment of gaps, condition of hydraulic lines, handles)
  • Wheel mount (pulling eccentrics), steering wheel and seat
  • Backlashes (steering wheel, bushings, carriage assembly, connecting rods)
  • Charge devices (headlight, tail light, main battery)

Gear shifting


For many, the "star gear" is covered with a fog of ignorance and the only thing that is known about it is that it is better not to put your fingers there once again. That's right :)

What problems are most common in this part of the bike? Flying chain and poor gear shifting.

The chain can fly because of the lack of tension - in this case it is easiest to remove one or several links. Twitter's bike chains have a lock for opening (this is a more convenient option than a chain without a lock), however, there is no need to adjust the length of the chain - rather, it‚Äôs just advice for the future when it's time to change it.



Also, the chain may fly due to any deformation (in the chain or on the stars) during operation. For example, it is not recommended to drive at ‚Äúextreme‚Äù speeds, in which the chain goes awry (because of what it stretches or frays over time). That is, if a bicycle has 3 * 8 = 24 speeds, then in fact it does not mean at all that it will take a long time to travel at all speeds: it is ‚Äúharmful‚Äù to ride some of them.

Well, the most common problem: the circuit flies due to unregulated switches. Both on the front and rear switch there are two screws each (protruding or recessed in the body), signed as L (low, responsible for small gears - big stars) and H (high, responsible for big gears - small stars). These cogs define the stroke limit of the switching mechanism.


Twitter AM26-E1 Adjustment Screws.


Twitter adjustment cogs Mantis-E0.

Tuning starts at the lowest speed. The rear derailleur rollers should be in the same plane with the asterisks - if this is not the case, then turn the H-cog so that everything is smooth. Then we switch to small gears and twist the L-cog so that the roller and the asterisk are also in the same plane. It is necessary to follow this: if the bike falls and the switching mechanism (foot) is bent, then it may happen that the chain does not just fly off - it will be much worse, if at one of the speeds the foot switches ‚Äúinside‚Äù the wheel, it touches the spokes with all the consequences. will have to change half the bike :)





The front switch has the same screws, the same rules are there: the switch frame should be in the plane of the stars, and the bottom edge of the frame should be no more than 3 mm from the big star.






L- and H-screws of the front stars in AM26-E1 are slightly recessed in the switch housing

There are at least two reasons to lubricate the chain with a certain frequency: 1) it will last longer (and the stars) and 2) it will work quieter, without unnecessary scratches and clanging. With active skating, it is desirable to lubricate the circuit once a week.

It is believed that the brakes came up with panties. Now there are two brakes on bicycles - there are several types of them, but regardless of this, they should work properly. IS ALWAYS. On all Twitter electric bikes there are disc brakes, so we will not consider the option of servicing V-brake brakes. But the nuances may appear during operation.



The most common problem is the creaking of brakes (even if you are just driving, and do not brake). If the squeak appeared suddenly, then most likely dirt got into the brake mechanism - it is quite possible that everything will ‚Äúcreak‚Äù by itself. But if all of a sudden something like oil got onto the brake pads (and first on the rotor) (for example, from a loose plug), then you can only disassemble / clean or buy new pads (issue price is 500-1000 rubles per set).



If the creak is permanent, then something is rubbing against something - a brake disc on the pads, or a pad on the disc :) Both options do not benefit either the disc or the pads. The solution is simple: adjust the gap between the brake disc and caliper, for which there are several bolts on the latter. Important: the gap between the pad and the rotor should not be very large, otherwise the braking will be less effective.





The worst option: a deformed brake disc (for example, as a result of a fall) - it is unlikely that adjustments can be done here. To solve the problem, you need a special key for editing disks, or replacing the rotor. Sometimes it is possible to detect curvature "by eye", in other cases it is enough to spin the wheel of a raised bicycle.

Developer Path, Part 4: Towards a Dream

Hello! With you again, after almost 2.5 years, Konstantin Delfuego is a participant in various adventures in IT and game development, and I want to tell you a lot of interesting, funny and insane things that happened to me during such a long radio silence.



And there is something to talk about: how I promoted my mobile game, conquered Augmented Reality, burned with betrayal, found and lost my dream job, almost despaired, but still crystallized a very clever concept of geolocation MMORPG, under which profile investors are ready to give me money . The article will have some juicy details of all this madness.

Sit back, fly!

All events are fictional; any coincidences with real companies and events are accidental and unintended.

Let's go back to the summer of 2015. After a year of active development, our game Sling Monsters finally came out. I understood perfectly well that without proper PR, she would not fly, and since February 15 I have been searching for possible ways to move. I did not have a budget for this enterprise, the concept of the game was absolutely uninteresting to the publishers (for more information about interaction with publishers, it was written in the previous article), so I had to make the most of all possible ways of budget-free promotion.

First, all sites were found on which you can post an article on game topics. Suddenly, it turned out that the site that generates the most traffic to game articles, and which in addition you can publish yourself, is the game portal of Alisher Burhanovich. Surprisingly, this site then did not fall into any guides for indie with a list of resources on the promotion of games.



But youtube has the most voluminous and practically inexhaustible source of contacts with potential players, so I began to actively write to his personalities. After months of searching, hundreds of letters and messages, I found two dozen guys and girls who are ready to lay out vidos about my game or embed prerolls for a small fee or just for a great love of indie. It turns out that there are good people in the world!

As a result of the promotion plan, it turned out that I would be able to achieve the number of downloads of the game required to bring the application to the top. And then, I was hoping, the viral would do his dirty work and I could earn at least a few dozen franklins clean.

But the reality turned out to be colder than the ice soul in Antarctica.



Not all sites managed to post an article about the game‚Äôs exit, some of the bloggers changed their mind to help, others disappeared with the money, after adding me to the ignore list, and others stopped contacting right before the release. But the biggest fail was in conversion rates. The percentage of conversion of views into installations was several times lower than I expected, focusing on data from the network. And a few months after the release, while the video continued to gain views, the conversion of views into installations reached a phenomenally low figure of ~ 0.003%. Now it is clear how foolish it was to rely on high conversion, but then the first day after the release I was in complete prostration. The situation is not saved even the three cool reviews. The most significant promotion channel that I could afford did not work, the game was not interesting for publishers, I could not get to Startup Chile, and I felt the independent painstaking promotion and refinement of the game month after month, the attempt of an international release, the search for a non-core investor. what I have to do, it was the opposite way of success.

Giving all your strength is much easier than finding the right direction for their use. And I still had to find the right path.



The situation was aggravated by the fact that at that time I was hopelessly in love with a girl with whom I could not get close to. She is not an easy girl, and neither in the short, nor even in the long run, has anything shone for us. She was like a forbidden fruit, clouding the mind, intoxicating only about her thoughts, attracting, but completely inaccessible. The inability to get anything from what I was striving for and the collapse of all my expectations led me to complete despair.

But fate clearly has a sense of humor and irony. A day after the failure to launch the game, it turned out accidentally that some of my feelings are mutual. The next week was the best in my life, although I expected this to happen for a completely different reason.



No hints, just shots from a children's cartoon.

Our romance ended after a couple of months of intermittent communication with complete failure.

In the meantime, in the late summer of 2015, helping my grandfather Stakhanovite to sell pumpkins on the market in the southern city far from Peter, I realized that the long-awaited game was only a line in the summary and a handful of bitter, sobering experience.

I didn't really want to look for a game designer job. There were much more programmers' vacancies, and I already had a fairly presentable project in the portfolio. This resulted in the slingshot monsters launching me back to Peter, through the window of an augmented reality application company, where I got a coder. The topic of AR was interesting to me for a long time, I felt that I was on the right track. The entrepreneurial spirit in me does not burn out with a hot iron, so then, in the fall of 2015, I began to make a tricky game, under which I almost managed to attract investments. But first things first.



The first dives in tons of sophisticated and, according to my cattle standards, too complex code, caused primal fear in me. Which, however, managed to be overcome in a couple of days. And after a couple of weeks I was very actively developing the project. In that company, despite the successful releases of 4 applications, I only worked for 9 months, and then the management decided to save on my services. This happened almost immediately after I took credit for my game.

But back for a couple of months before being fired. At that time we were doing a difficult project and I gave all the power to work: I often stayed late and worked on weekends. And at this time, my view falls on the stunning art of the game of the genre, which I have always despised and avoided - MOBA. The character-DJ and the charming style of the artes for the game did their job, and I was dragged into the depths of the League.



The first drug intake, as usual, caused disgust, but already from the second the dependence began to grow exponentially. Kila mud, endorphins spin. This led to a long story that could have been avoided if I had been a little more attentive.

I attracted a person to the project both for serious payment, and with the expectation of his further participation as a service station, and as a result I attracted a banal incompetent cheater, who for a long time misled and even made some friendly steps, which greatly delayed our communication and my expectations .

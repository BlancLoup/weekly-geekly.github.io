What electric cars did Russians buy in 2017



Discussions about electric cars have been going on since the 1980s, but the first electric cars began to appear on the streets of the cities quite recently.

The global trend for cars using clean energy gradually covers the CIS. In Russia, the number of electric cars in the tens, but continues to grow every year. According to the agency "AUTOSTAT", in 2017 in the Russian Federation 39 electric machines were sold. The demand, albeit slowly, is growing (for comparison: in 2016, 33 units were sold).

We propose to consider in detail how much they cost and what technical characteristics of electric cars acquired by Russians in 2017 have.

25 of 39 electric machines were purchased in Moscow and the Moscow Region, 6 in Primorsky and Khabarovsk Territories (4 and 2 pieces, respectively), 2 in Krasnodar and one each in St. Petersburg, Tatarstan, Irkutsk, Voronezh, Ryazan and Rostov regions.

Where to charge electric vehicles in Russia?

Most electric stations in Russia, of course, in Moscow - a total of 39 stations. The leader in the number of charges is Mosenergo PJSC - 13 pieces. 8 units each - from PJSC ROSSETI and Emi, 3 stations each have MOSOBLENERGO and Tesla Wall Connector, 2 are located in the MOSCOW TESLA CLUB salons, one is Electrositi LLC and another one is in Skolkovo "(High-speed charging station Tesla Supercharge).

In St. Petersburg, the number of stations for charging electric vehicles is somewhat less - only 13, all of which are provided by EV-Time .

For comparison, in the US, the number of electric stations reaches 16,000, and the total number of electric vehicles worldwide has exceeded 2 million. A full map of electric stations is available at plugshare.com .

The International Energy Agency reports that by the end of 2017, the number of electric cars sold will be about 1.2 million, and next year - 1.6 million.

The IEA also adds that half of its sales will be in China. In second place will be the United States and Europe.

At the moment, China is the real leader of the electric transport market - only last year more than 40% of the electric vehicles sold accounted for the Middle Kingdom. And recently it became known that the country is planning to ban the sale of gasoline cars.

Obviously, Russia is far behind the west in the number of acquired electric vehicles. This is understandable: given the more or less developed infrastructure only in Moscow and St. Petersburg, buying an electric car in the Russian Federation is not particularly profitable.

However, electric cars are becoming more popular, and the trend of growth in their sales (albeit very slow) is likely to continue in the next year.

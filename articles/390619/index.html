Why 3D printing? Architecture

Despite the fact that 3D printing can be called "emerging technology", it is already widely available tool for use in architecture. Thanks to the capabilities of modern 3D printers, architects can create models for experiments and presentations much faster than before.


Photo: dezeen.com

The additive principle of 3D printing technology reduces the amount of material used and the number of hours, days and months spent on work ... 3D printing allows you to create more ambitious and bold experimental forms of high accuracy. And to see the three-dimensional model created in CAD is now possible not only on the computer. 3D printing allows designers to visualize their ideas even at the concept stage.

Visualization

In addition to the ability to quickly produce and test new possible ideas, 3D printing can recreate existing objects as quickly and accurately. By allowing less material to be wasted compared to classic production methods (CNC machines), 3D-printed spatial and topographic models are an effective way to study the urban environment .


Photo: 3printr.com

Thoughtful design

3D printing has the potential to improve traditional design methods. Its ability to reproduce nonlinear geometry perfectly complements parametric modeling and other algorithmic design methods.


Photo: archpaper.com

Crazy 3D Printed Architectural Design Forms

Unlike projects built by traditional methods, 3D-printed projects can be the embodiment of the most complex and unimaginable forms. At first glance, this may seem like indulgence to an audience obsessed with novelty, but such projects can and are much more functional than their traditional counterparts. In particular, 3D printing is one of the most important reasons for the emergence of biomimicry (design inspired by nature) in architecture. This trend has been developing very rapidly over the past decade. Designers can now imitate complex designs generated by nature and various forms. Recreating such forms in architecture has become faster and easier thanks to the advent of parametric design and 3D printing.


Photo: wired.co.uk

3D printing allows you to go beyond standard thinking.

3D printing allows you to remove restrictions and generate new ideas, materializing them in an extremely short time. Using an algorithmic design method, architects and designers can create forms based on ergonomics and functionality that an object or building must combine in itself.

Overview of Windows applications to restore order in photo archives

Asking to find all the photos of a loved one, or analyzing photos from the hike, tours, etc. we often start monitoring folders on the computer. However, after just an hour or two, many are abandoning this venture, having gone through less than half of the photo archive and are tired of searching for unnecessary pictures among hundreds of NewNotes and dozens of DCIM.



We are in a hurry to live. Our memories are stored in folders, merged from camera flash drives, synchronized with smartphones and ... forgotten. In this review I will try to highlight the strengths and weaknesses of applications available to ordinary people with Windows, which can give our memories a second life.

We formulate the requirements for the program, they will be:

  1. Search for photos scattered on the PC.
  2. The ability to collect and organize all the photos in one place.
  3. Support for photos in RAW format (image format from digital SLR cameras).
  4. Recognition in the photographs of the faces of loved ones.
  5. Find and delete duplicate photos (photos containing the same image, but placed in different folders on your computer).
  6. Ability to choose the best shot in a series of similar photos.
  7. The ability to hide personal photos from outsiders.
  8. Ability to add tags for quick and easy photo search.
  9. External backup storage of important photos (synchronization with cloud services).
  10. Free or moderate cost.


Programs for comparison


Picasa is a long-known program that, unfortunately, has stopped evolving. The latest update 3.9 brought only a few features and syncing with Google+.



Google Photo is Picasa's heir. It has a lot of innovative algorithms. In my opinion, clean, fresh and laconic.



ACDSee 17 is my first photo viewing app since I bought my computer back in 1999. During this time, the company has released a large number of modifications ACDSee. Now the site contains about 12 versions.



Flickr is an updated online service with a neat design and 1 TB (enough for about 150,000 photos) to store photos for each user.



ThisLife is a San Francisco start-up bought last year by Shutterfly.



ZZ Photo - is in beta testing. Beautiful design, but a little damp.



XnView is a popular, free, fast photo application.



Zoner 16 is a photo editor from Czech developers.

Ability to add tags for quick and easy photo search


Picasa

Photos can be tagged when viewed using the side right pane. Tags are added manually, or selected from a collection.



Search mode allows you to find photos by specified tags. The green bar in the header reminds the user that the photos shown are limited to a search query.

GooglePhoto, XnView

No tagging functionality.

Flickr

When importing photos, it is possible to assign a text tag, mark a person, add to an album or group. These actions can be applied both to a separate photo and to a group of shots (by default).



Tagging is also possible when viewing a snapshot. Possibilities are available to add a text tag, geolocation and person.



To search for photos by tags, just enter them in the search field (top right). If you need to clarify all sorts of criteria, Advanced Search is available with a variety of options for fine-tuning a search query.



Thislife

To add a tag to a snapshot, you need to enter it, or choose from the list of prompts. If the tag is missing, the program will ask what type the tag is: text, person, or geolocation.



A separate mode "Tags" allows you to see your tags and photos on the collections.



Photo search allows you to search by people, geolocation tags and text tags. For a more flexible request setting, there is Advanced Mode, in which you can customize the type of displayed content (photo / video), the period in which photos were taken and some other options.



ZZ Photo
You can tag with three types of tags: text, geolocation, and noting faces in photos. In the left panel there are preset text tags (Family, Friends, Work, Study, Travel, Hobbies). If you wish, you can create personalized tags. Photos tagged automatically form virtual albums for quick access to pictures of one category.



The search is carried out by text tags, persons and geolocation tags. Tags can be both expanding the search (find "Friends" and "Family"), and clarifying (find "Family", only "Travel").



ACDSee

Explicitly there are no text tags in the application. There are rating labels (from 0 to 5) and a color palette (5 colors).



Zoner 16

The file marking mechanism is similar to ACDSee. No tags. There are color tags and photo rating levels (from 0 to 5).



The search mode allows you to quite flexibly search for possible parameters using the mathematical conditions "equal", "more", "less".

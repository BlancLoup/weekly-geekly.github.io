Convenient magnets for attaching photos / notes, etc.

Do you have a habit of attaching photos, notes, receipts, children's drawings and other important things to the fridge or front door? If not - you will not be interested, if yes - go, tell you how to make simple, comfortable, well-holding and even a little stylish magnets.


So how to find normal magnets is a real problem even in a big city. And even if you find a decent looking, they almost certainly will not keep or will be hefty closing everything by themselves, but I found a way out for myself.

And so we order our own powerful magnets (rare earth) from China. It turns out just 3p apiece. Next, look for all the pockets / wallets / vases with a trifle, etc. coins worth 5 kopecks. Here I apologize to readers from other countries, since you will find it difficult to find such coins, but you can easily replace them with your analogs!



Why exactly 5 kopecks?

1. The coin is very suitable size. It is a little more magnet and the magnet will be easy to unhook from the surface to which it is applied. By itself, such a magnet to tear off, for example from the door of the refrigerator will not work, it keeps very well, and there is nothing to pick it up. And with a glued coin, it is not difficult. Even if the magnet is not picked up with a coin, you can click on one edge and the magnet will unhook on the other side so that you can freely take it with your fingers.

2. The coin is almost not oxidized and remains shiny for a long time, without losing the presentation.

3. Such coins are not taken in most stores, and therefore they have a tendency to accumulate at home in various vases and simply in the pockets of pants and jackets.

4. It looks in my opinion quite stylish and Feng Shui is also not bad;)

Why such magnets?

They are the right size, not too big and not too small. If you take more, they will hold even better, but it will be more difficult to unhook them. For example, a 20mm x 2mm magnet is already uncoupled with great effort. Even with a glued (big) coin. I thus made souvenir magnets from coins of other countries. Small magnets 12mm - the most suitable.

And so in order to make a convenient and strong magnet, I simply glued the magnet to the coin with superglue.



I think you should not describe the process of gluing, but I will give a couple of tips. First, stick carefully! Coin 5 cop. Magnet! First put a drop of glue on the coin, then with a good hand holding the coin, slowly attach the magnet. Do not do this close to your face, the magnet will magnetize to the coin and because of this, a splash of glue may appear, and superglue is a dangerous thing! And do not need a lot of glue, otherwise it will spread and stain the magnet, coin, hands and everything else nearby.

Enjoy!

Why does the author of cURL receive letters from frustrated car owners

"Hello! I have a Toyota Corolla with a multimedia system, the rights to which you own. I need advice on how to use GPS ... ‚Äù, - this is how one of the letters, which Daniel Stenberg recently received, begins.

It is good to be the author of the popular open source program. You are respected. If you want to get a job in a commercial company, then you do not need to write a resume. The program code is open and speaks for itself. And in general, open source is a huge plus in karma. You can meet and talk to wonderful people who dislike to communicate with "jackets" from corporations. And with you they will be equal.

But popularity has certain problems. Sometimes very unexpected. For example, the author of the well-known program cURL is now receiving letters from frustrated car owners who have problems with the car's infotainment system and the navigator. Why should I?

Casket opens simply. Upon the request of the cURL license, the Stenberg email address is listed on the "About" page of the car's infotainment system that uses the cURL program code, or in the list of contact information and on similar pages. Daniel suspects that sometimes this is the only address that is listed there !

Ordinary car owners have no idea what cURL is and who Daniel is, but they consider it their right to write a complaint to the address that is indicated on the screen in the car. In a sense, they can be understood. But the situation does not get any less ridiculous.

cURL is a free, cross-platform command line utility designed to copy files using different protocols with the URL syntax. The author is Daniel Stenberg. He is the owner of all rights. The email address daniel@haxx.se is listed in the text of the cURL license , which is a derivative of the standard MIT license. cURL acts as a standalone wrapper for the libcurl library. In turn, libcurl is used to provide the ability to transfer files to numerous applications in the system.

You ask how the cURL program code got into the car computer? Well, you are just not familiar with the "minced meat" of modern cars. These are real computers on wheels, including those with network functionality. In particular, cURL is part of the standard GenIVI and Tizen automotive software installation package, and also comes bundled with many other software packages.

It is unlikely that Daniel Stenberg could assume in 1997, when he released the first version of cURL, that in twenty years his program would be installed in millions of cars around the world. But it happened.

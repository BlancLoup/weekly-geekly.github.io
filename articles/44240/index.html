We are fighting with manual spam on blogs using Parasite Eliminator (since November 10, the service is open to everyone)

So, what some dedicated bloggers have been waiting for has happened. I opened Parasite Eliminator for everyone - a service and plugin for Wordpress, thanks to which there is no manual spam in my blogs.

How does it work


Very similar to antivirus + firewall. There are black and white lists that can be updated from the servers (in case of DDoS there is a backup server). Lists are stored locally, you can add URLs to them or exclude them. You can even raise your server if you know the format of the updates.

By the way, now in the black list for more than 1000 domains.

New comments "are scanned on the fly." If there is a link from the blacklist, then the comment will be sent to the moderator or straight to spam. I‚Äôll especially note that my plugin does not delete anything by itself. Only marks.

If the spammer URL is unknown to us, it will not be shown to search engines anyway. Reached those links that are in the white list. For example, if a blogger trusts a couple of dozen of his familiar bloggers, then the addresses of their blogs are included in the white list. And only these URLs will be shown. Others will be hidden for the time being.

There is a scanner of the entire database. Let's say someone managed to slip through until the database had its URL. This is not a reason to leave trash on the blog, even if the links were not shown. You can run a scan manually or on a schedule. All comments in the database will be checked. Check, by the way, is carried out very quickly.

And the replenishment of the base will now be carried out even faster. Members of the beta testers community are already sending me spamming URLs from their blogs.

3D video streaming technology - now part of Intel


It became aware of the acquisition of Intel Israeli company Replay Technologies , known for its proprietary format of sports interactive video broadcasts Free Dimensional Video (freeD ‚Ñ¢). The essence of freeD technology is that by processing video streams from several high-resolution cameras, a three-dimensional real-time scene is created that the fan can view from any angle he likes.
About a hundred employees of Replay Technologies will join the Intel team in order to further develop the direction that the corporation calls immersive sports as a hard-to-translate term (sports broadcasts with simultaneous human exposure through several perception channels). Under the cut - some more information about Replay and some videos.

Replay Technologies was founded in 2011 and is headquartered in Tel Aviv. As you can easily guess, freeD-video broadcasting involves a large number of manipulations with video content in real time and, therefore, are demanding of processing server performance. Since 2013, Replay developers have actively collaborated with Intel in order to optimize freeD software for its platform. Currently, Replay's clients are a number of NFL , NBA and MLB clubs, the video for the baseball game is already shown above. But another video - demonstration of freeD technology at this year's CES.

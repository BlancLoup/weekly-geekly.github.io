Another story of a small hosting provider

A personal story about how I opened my hosting, which even brings some profit.
Inspired by the history of habrahabr.ru/blogs/hosting/93364

The idea of ‚Äã‚Äãbecoming a hosting provider came to me by chance, when one of the former clients of a web studio, where I officially work, asked where you can place your site.

Being engaged in web development I also have a virtual server for software testing. Server resources are not used and 10%, so the idea why not earned lodged in my head.

This idea had to be thought out to the smallest detail. I wanted not only to pay for myself, but at least a little, but to earn. Realizing that you won‚Äôt earn $ 2 on a hosting, I decided that I would take a much larger amount from customers. Naturally, the question immediately arose of how to find customers willing to pay the same amount for hosting and how to compete with hosting for $ 2.

By asking myself, what do I get paid for in my main job? I understood two things for myself. First, I get paid for my knowledge. Secondly - for the work itself.

This prompted me to the idea that my potential client is an organization, at least connected with the computer sphere .

First, as a rule, an organization is ready to pay more than a private client.
Secondly, the less such an organization possessed computer knowledge, the more I could sell my knowledge and services to it.

Surveys of acquaintances and some organizations (with which I dealt at work) showed that they would rather pay a living person than enter a visa number on the site.
This is easily explained - live communication inspires confidence in them, hence I made a conclusion - compulsory communication with the client, at least by telephone .

Despite the fact that today, probably all hosting companies advertise 24/7 phone support , at the stage of concluding a contract a representative of such an office is still virtual.

Next was built a list of potential customers, it fell into:
museums, theaters, social movements and some other small organizations.

So what can you pay for besides the usual hosting services?
Since my clients are far from everything related to IT , I suggested that they solve ‚Äúpractically any‚Äù problems associated with sites. Such clients are turned off by words like ftp / php / dns / ssh. What will take me 5 minutes to do, it will take many hours and a lot of tedious calls with ‚Äústupid questions‚Äù. I got this experience at the main job: when making websites, I had to give some support to them, even if they were built on a CMS like Wordpress or Joomla , customers still had almost anecdotal questions.

How did I look for clients?
To be honest, the first ten are organizations already familiar to me. For the second ten I left through the first clients.

If to summarize all of the above, then:
  • I provide services only to organizations far from the computer sphere
  • looking for customers myself and through friends
  • one hosting plan (up to 3GB of space / unlimited traffic)
  • I do not have a website
  • I do not advertise anywhere
  • no online billing
  • signed contract only
  • give some support in their sites
  • Clients do not use the control panel (they simply don‚Äôt need it), so a free equivalent of paid cpanel and Parallels Plesk was taken
  • backups are made to an external server (which is at my disposal for services about its administration)
  • all clients (except one) from the city where I live


Now in numbers:
  • today 24 customers
  • one VDS server ($ 40 per month)
  • tariff plan - $ 30 per month
  • minimum payment for 12 months (it saves me every month to do the routine associated with the transfer of money)
  • all clients together I spend about 20 hours a month (mostly small questions from the series ‚Äúhow to reduce the image in the correct proportion for the site‚Äù)
  • these 20 hours include everyday viewing of server status logs and backups


After learning about the features of some organizations, I offered them a new service for them - mail hosting and administration. For them it was a headache, for me it was - reset the password, open a new mailbox, etc. This is a separate service for $ 30 per month. 11 clients use it. These 11 customers do not want to go to the control panel and add something there, so I would prefer to do it.

In order to sign contracts, I had to officially register and pay taxes.

As a result, for each month:
- $ 40 per server
$ 30 * 24 = $ 720 for hosting
$ 30 * 11 = $ 330 for hosting and administering mail
$ 850 after taxes

If you divide it into work hours, then it turns out to be $ 42.5 per hour, not bad at all, considering that hosting is just my hobby.

I have no further plans to develop my hosting, so I‚Äôm no longer looking for new customers. This is explained by the fact that being a hoster is not my favorite occupation, and at the initial stage I set the bar at 20-30 hours per month for this business.

Nevertheless, for me it is a very interesting experience. If you find the right approach to customers and provide them with the necessary knowledge and experience, you can easily compete with hosters for $ 2. I think this experience can be applied in other areas.

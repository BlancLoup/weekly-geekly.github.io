Getting root on the router Tenda Nova MW6

It all started with the fact that being flattered on the sale and new buzz words - I purchased a wifi mesh system in order to cover all corners of a country house and the adjacent plot. All anything, but one problem was annoying. And given the fact that I sat on the hospital for a few days - brought me to root access to this router.



Problem


For the sake of interest, I bought two sets of three cubes Tenda Nova MW6 - a system for organizing a wifi mesh for dummies. Ethernet backhaul possible connecting some blocks to each other by wire, because this box is almost the cheapest wifi mesh (cheaper, in my opinion, only their own MW3) and it does not have a third dedicated radio path for internal communication between the blocks (like some expensive colleagues - Netgear Orbi).
In general, there was no great need, but a couple of routers for OpenWrt did not cover all the right places.


Who is a mesh, and why is it needed?

Mesh is a topology of building networks, characterized by high fault tolerance and dynamism.
I will not go into details, as many are aware, and who is not - you can start with the article about the Cellular topology on wikipedia .


The main task of the "home" wifi mesh network is to cover a large area with a stable wireless signal with seamless roaming.


Seamless roaming is possible both at access points and on home routers (for example, with openwrt - by replacing wpad-mini with wpad and turning on 802.11r). And a stable signal on a large area from a pair of points is not received - it is empty, it is thick - while at the same time you seriously litter the air if you increase the power. More points are needed - a few 5G points will be much more efficient. But at the usual access points / routers, you will still think how to bring the wire to them, and the mesh to the mesh, in order to support itself and to establish connections both with and without the presence of a wire (backhaul).


In general, technology for the convenience of life. It's good.

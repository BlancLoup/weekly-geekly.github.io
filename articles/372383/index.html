Microsoft has supported the marijuana market

The first large corporation dared to break the unspoken taboo. Is the ice broken?




One by one, the US states legalize marijuana. In 25 states, cannabis is permitted in one form or another, and in three states - Colorado, Oregon, and Washington - Ganja smoking is legalized for recreational purposes, which is of commercial interest for hundreds of companies that immediately entered the market.

In the fall of 2016, a referendum on the legalization of marijuana for recreational purposes will be held in huge California ( 39 million inhabitants ) and four more states. And when California residents make a positive decision, and almost no one doubts that, sales of cannabis can grow from the current $ 6.5 billion to $ 25 billion over several years. Even before the legalization of marijuana, millions of Americans (among them the current and past presidents of the United States) gained experience in its use, so experts predict great prospects for this market.

At the same time, large corporations are still cautious and try to stay away. To the surprise of many, Microsoft was the first to break the unspoken taboo among the big IT players. She entered into a partnership agreement with Kind Financial , a small Los Angeles-based startup in the marijuana market. In particular, Kind develops tools that help keep records and comply with the myriad of state regulations and requirements for the sale of marijuana.

Agrisoft Seed to Sale platform runs the business of a marijuana farmer drug dealer from beginning to end: from buying seeds and growing a product to delivering the packaged goods to a retail network and calculating profits. For example, Kind Financial issues special terminals, similar to ATMs, for receiving money and issuing receipts for receiving marijuana.

Microsoft has pledged to promote its new partner, recommending it, including for government contracts, writes the NY Times. Government contracts, of course, are not connected with the purchase of marijuana for state needs, it has not yet reached, but with the development of specialized software and cloud services, business accounting and marijuana turnover: this is the Agrisoft Seed to Sale for Government platform.



A whole industry of young startups is rapidly forming on the American market. They serve farmers who grow marijuana, automate the work of stores that sell "grass". Despite the huge commercial prospects, this industry remains somewhat aloof from the "traditional" business. For example, only a few small banks agree to open accounts for companies that grow cannabis and sell marijuana.

But as the market begins to regulate the state and big reputable players, like Microsoft, come here, the situation may move from the dead point. In this sense, Microsoft can be a pioneer who will pull others along.

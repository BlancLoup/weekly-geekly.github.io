Hackers hacked popular Twitter accounts and distributed porn links



The theft of social network accounts has long ceased to amaze, but recently hackers hacked more than 2500 popular accounts on Twitter. Among the victims were the accounts of the musical group Chromeo, comic Azim Antvala, The New York Times journalist David Carr. This was first reported by Symantec employees in their study .

In all the hacked accounts posted links to dating sites for adults, and instead of avatars put photos of women in their underwear. The brief description of the account in which malicious links similarly appeared also changed. In most cases, spam bots on Twitter are quite common, but this attack was characterized by targeting accounts with almost 100,000 subscribers. Most of the hacked accounts were created at least four years ago, and 27% of the total number registered in 2011.

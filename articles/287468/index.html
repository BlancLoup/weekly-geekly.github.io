Cross Platform Communities

One of the trends of the Internet is the process of content autonomy. Each post is duplicated in search engines and rss-feeds. In principle, rss has become a replacement for LJ-frending for standalone blogs. But the problem remains of the relationship between such blogs ...

So I thought - is there a substitute for communities? How to combine different blogs on different platforms and hosting in one community? And that's what occurred to me:
- It is very easy to create an unmoderated cross-platform community, if you add a unique unique tag in the posts. It will be displayed in the search results for this code, and Yandex shows ‚Äúreviews‚Äù, i.e. analogue of comments.

Example:
1. Create an entry marked F9g2N48mP, give a name to the community and encourage everyone interested to post with this code.
2. The community itself appears at blogs.yandex.ru/search.xml?text=F9g2N48mP
3. You can comment on the posts both in the journals themselves and in your blog, indicating the link to the commented post - Yandex shows them as "reviews". By the way, who knows why not all the posts refer to the original?

3D printers and amazing products from them

The process of creating three-dimensional solid objects opened up new opportunities in the development of different spheres. In the amazing world of 3D printing, new breakthroughs and successes occur almost daily. From the "print" of human tissue to life-size locks.

Below is a list of the most interesting products made on a 3D printer.

Van Gogh's 3D ear




The German artist Diemut Strebe recreated the famous impressionist's ear on a 3D printer.

With the help of cartilage cells that were taken from a distant relative of Vincent Van Gogh, the laboratory managed to grow cartilage, which became the basis. And the technology of computer image processing helped to reproduce a model identical in shape to the ear of the artist.

3D houses




The Chinese company WinSun Decoration Design Engineering Co built 10 houses using layered printing technology. Engineers have developed a giant 3D printer (30 meters long, 11 meters wide and 7 meters high), which can print up to ten houses per day. Concrete was used as the base material: it filled in special containers, freezing and forming walls, floors.

3D cars




The Urbee 2 machine is one of the first 3D auto models created using Fused Deposition Modeling technology. The product is based on a metal frame, but the model itself, the body and some interior details are printed on a 3D printer. The speed of the car is not impressive (up to 112 km per hour), but economical power consumption.



The American company Local Motors at the International Manufacturing Technology Show presented its 3D car Strati. The model is notable for being collected within 6 days (right at the exhibition). The machine consists of 40 parts. On the 3D-printer were printed: body, seats, chassis, dashboard and center console. Strati can reach speeds of up to 64 km / h, and its cruising range is in the range of 192-240 km.

3D jaw




One of the first artificial jaws was printed by LayerWise. After research and testing by staff from Hasselt University, the 3D implant was transplanted to the patient. A 83-year-old woman suffered from a chronic infection of the bones for a long time.

The 3D jaw was made of titanium powder, which was heated layer by layer with a laser. For 1 mm artificial jaw, it took 33 layers of titanium powder. The model is a complex design with articulated joints and grooves.

3D arteries




A team of medical and technical researchers from the University of Melbourne, Australia, led by Professor Peter Barlis using the concept of printing anatomical 3D models with a unique scanning process, created 3D arteries.

3d guitar




Designer Scott Sammi (Scott Summi) using a 3D printer created the first acoustic guitar. The instrument is almost entirely made of plastic (with the exception of the neck and strings). The guitar has a good sound, as well as a strong body that does not break.

3D shoes




The inventor of Recreus, Ignacio Garcia, has made original Sneakerbot II 3D sneakers. For shoes used innovative elastic fiber - Filaflex. It forms an elastic waterproof thread that is able to maintain its shape.



Designer Paulina van Dongen created 3D shoes from laser-melted nylon. They are called "Morphogenesis". Shoes are presented in different colors.

3D clothes




Designer Alexis Walsh (Alexis Walsh) has released a series of unique clothing, thereby demonstrating the possibilities of 3D technology in the fashion world. Dresses are made up of more than 400 individual plates printed on a 3D printer in SLS Shapeways. After printing, the plates were assembled by hand using tiny metal ring connectors.

3D camera




Leo Marius (Leo Marius) from 250 grams of ABS plastic and auxiliary parts has created a 35-mm film mirror camera Open Reflex. He printed a "DSLR" on a 3D printer. The process of creating a model took about 15 hours.

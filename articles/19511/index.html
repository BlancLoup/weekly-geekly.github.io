Startup Diseases
Each startup has a set of diseases and their solutions:
* I like it - should like others.
Do not trust solely your opinion, listen to the opinions of neighbors. Sometimes they accidentally give a solution to a problem or some interesting addition. Learn to see projects from all sides.
* Let's do Google. Everyone who wants to do something, wants to do something global (a search engine, a huge portal, etc.).
Do not swing the ball right away, start small. Krupnyak never concentrates on niches, use niche positioning including to extract the highest income.
* The joy of exponential growth
Site growth from one visitor to tens of thousands can behave like an exhibitor. With one amendment - it will be only the first time. Further, in the best scenario, linear growth with a small coefficient, on average stabilization, at worst - a fall
* Sales = F (Visitor), Sales = F (Page View)
The size of sales depends only on the actual purchases on the site, and not on the number of visitors (potential customers)
* If you take the revenue for this day, yes multiplied by the number of days in a year .
The results of one day (usually choose the day on which was the highest income) can not be approximated for the whole year.
* Our product is brilliant. His genius will provide interest in him and his sales.
Do not forget that it is not enough to create a good product, it is also necessary to sell it, it is sales that generate income, and unfortunately many developers, the seller ultimately gets more money than the creator.
* Lack of diversification in projects. Projects most often depend on one thing (information on a specific site, advertiser, supplier, etc.)

Startup dilemma
In the process of working on a new project, there always arises a dilemma to do everything qualitatively or implement it in haste (the main thing is working correctly, we will earn money, we will hire programmers, they will overwrite everything anyway) and move on.
Most often choose the second approach.

Team and hired staff
Every project creator asks questions: How many should be the founders? How and when to allocate shares? How to choose employees?

How many should be the founders
- 1 - normal
- 2 - normal
- 3 - normal
- 4 - possible
- 5 - maybe enough?
- ...
- 120 cheers! My share in this wonderful project is 0.2%
It is considered (I do not know by whom) that the optimal number of the creator team should be no more than 3.
In each team, you must choose someone who will have a decisive vote in resolving conflicts related to the implementation of the project.
Founders should be assembled on the basis of "utility", and not on the basis of dating.

When and how to distribute shares?
Shares should be distributed immediately after the definition of the idea and the distribution of responsibilities in proportion to these responsibilities, money contributed to the project and other intangible assets.
Whether to fix shares in any document. Everyone must decide for himself. The topic of trust will be revealed further.

How to choose employees?
Each startup begins to grow with time and there is not enough time for the founding fathers, and then the problem of recruitment arises. The main criteria for recruitment are experience and professionalism.
Care should be taken to recruit students (students forgive me, please). They have great enthusiasm at work, they are ready to work even at night, when they are far from the session, but unfortunately they have very little experience.

Audio games - why and why


Computer games that can be played with eyes closed attracted the attention of professional developers by the early 2000s, when the technological base was ripe for such projects. 3D sound positioning systems made it possible to navigate the playing space by ear. It gave new ideas for gameplay and instantly interested people with visual impairments. The search for enemies by sound was realized in 1996, in the graphic horror film Enemy Zero, where it was necessary to search for invisible monsters. But then there was no talk about a full-fledged audiopanoram.

Later, a new incentive appeared for the development of audio games - it is convenient to play them on mobile devices . Here you will find both non-standard gameplay, and hearing training, and the opportunity to play without straining your eyes after the working day. Put on headphones - and go. At the same time you can be in a public place and not lose control over what is happening around.


Photo: StockSnap

Sound games are still small - the account goes to the hundreds. There are even fewer quality products with professional voice acting, so each new product attracts attention. To see this, check out the audiogames.net forum for new releases . The main part of the audience here are blind players. Thanks to screen access and braille displays, totally blind people use a computer like everyone else, but there is not much of them available from mainstream graphics games - mostly fighting games where you can hear which side the opponent is on (Mortal Kombat IX and X, Injustice 2, etc. ). This situation encourages the blind to take on the development of games themselves and, of course, support the dollar and the euro for those who produce something good.

The threshold of entry into the development of sound games is lower than in the graphic game. You do not need to hire artists and animators, you do not need to be compared with anyone polygons or shaders. In your game world everything is possible that you will be able to express with words and sound. A feasible task for small teams and enthusiasts.

How it works


Some sound games are fully voiced by real people, others by a speech synthesizer. Screen dubbing technologies are often associated with Windows special features, TalkBack on Android and programs for creating audio books from text (ICE Book Reader, Balabolka, Demagog, etc.). The basic principle is the same everywhere: the speech synthesizer reads you out loud.

Audio games with professional voice acting can produce some information through a synthesizer. This allows you to listen to the text in your desired voice, with adjustable pitch and speed of speech. For example, a person who listens to a text in a foreign language may slow down reading to make out every word. And the one who saves time, on the contrary - will set a one-and-a-half or double speed. If you can take notes or rename objects in the game, again, a synthesizer is useful. For amateur authors, artificial voice acting is a saving on sound recordings, an opportunity to reduce the weight of the game and change dialogues on the fly without having to re-record the voices of announcers.

To voice themselves in Windows, audio games usually use Text-to-speech (TTS) technology and MS SAPI speech interface. Under this interface, different companies produce voice synthesizers, which are also called speech engines. Each engine has its own set of voices, as a rule, for several languages.

By default, you have at least one English SAPI voice installed on your system. Russian, French and other voices can be downloaded from the Internet. For example, on GitHub there is a free voice engine RHVoice and several Russian voices to it. Esperanto, English, Georgian and Ukrainian are also supported. Compared to commercial products from the Nuance Vocalizer Expressive line, RHVoice voices do not sound so realistic, but clearly and with pronounced intonations.

If you dive a little deeper into the world of sound games, you will find that some of them are not voiced through SAPI or its analogs for other OS, but with the help of an external screen reader program (‚Äúscreen reader‚Äù). Such software allows you to control the operating system and applications running in it. Among the blind users of Windows is a popular free screen access program from NVDA. Making friends with your game is easy - using the NVDA Controller API library. We will also touch on the topic of screen readers in the section ‚ÄúEngine for audio games‚Äù.

By genre and gameplay organization, sound games cover many areas: from simple puzzles to large-scale RPGs, simulators, races and strategies. Let's look at examples.

3D Sound Shooters


Shades of Doom is a first-person shooter for Windows. We focus on the terrain with the help of a computer, as well as echo our own steps, draft in doorways and the noise from objects nearby. Swamp is a multi-user FPS. The action takes place during the invasion of zombies in the town near the swamp. You have to survive (it is not easy) and carry out missions together with other players. You can listen to the English review of the game here .

Sound role-playing games - this is freedom for a novice developer and a chance to implement epic designs without spending on graphics.

Sound simulators


Eurofly is a free civil aviation simulator. The game uses real GPS-coordinates and a detailed digital atlas, which can be used to study the nature of the terrain and the location of settlements in areas of interest. You fly, refresh your knowledge of geography and get acquainted with the airports of the world, each of which has its own height of convergence and its own characteristics. You can go to "free flight" or consistently pass the mission and open models of aircraft.

The game covers all aspects of pre-flight preparation and flight: boarding passengers, refueling, communicating with airport controllers, announcements in the cabin, etc. Time here goes at a real speed: if the flight takes 5 hours, you fly so much. When the fuel runs out, you can sit at the nearest airport, if it is not prohibited by the terms of the mission. For example, the airline may prohibit you from crossing Austrian airspace en route from Slovakia to the Czech Republic.

For Eurofly released a lot of mods : airplanes, airports, flight attendants (there is a Russian steward Andrew), controllers, sound effects. On the developer's site, you can track ‚Äúcurrent traffic‚Äù - where and from where game flights fly, where every aircraft is now located and how many passengers it has on board.

The project exists and develops through donations of players. The game is available in English, Slovak, Czech, Italian and Spanish. According to some reports, work is underway on Russian localization.

And for those who like to fight, there is GMA TankCommander - a tank simulator that voices itself completely. You are attacked by tanks, helicopters, cars, trains, artillery and troops. During maneuvering, you must not land on a mine or in a swamp. The game is in English, there is an unofficial crack.

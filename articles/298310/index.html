LEAD YOUR PROFILE LINKEDIN


Not a LinkedIn member yet? You miss a reliable and proven opportunity to advertise your services through the Web, as well as acquire invaluable connections with other professionals. Listen to how Samantha Tollin explains why online tools are so helpful, and then spend an hour setting up your own profile.

There are many ways in which you can improve the quality and attractiveness of your page, although no one forbids using a standard profile.

Already have a LinkedIn page? Fine. But again, although you can limit yourself to the usual LinkedIn profile, there are many ways to improve its quality and appearance.

In LinkedIn, there is a system for awarding stars to the most functional pages. What is the use of virtual stars? You will appear more often in search results and get more views from potential employers or clients.
How can LinkedIn be useful? Here are some tips that may be useful to you, although it is not necessary to act on this template exactly:
  • Add a photo of your profession and the industry in which you work to the main page. If you are involved in jurisprudence, photography should be as traditional as possible, and for those who work in knowledge-intensive industries, more informal shots from your normal life will do.
  • Make as specific and informative a description as possible of the position you currently occupy.
  • Add at least two of your previous posts.
  • Fill out the education section.
  • If you have less than 50 people in contact, try to find former colleagues, classmates or clients.
  • In the "Skills" section add information on certifications, professional interests and knowledge.

CONTROL YOUR BEHAVIOR


Often in the news we see how recognized and ambitious professionals are faced with problems resulting from their behavior on the Internet. It comes even to the dismissal or refusal of employment. We are not trying to scare you, but simply consider it our duty to remind you that your behavior on social networks or on other websites can significantly affect your professional activities.

Although this occupation can not be called interesting, but maintaining a good reputation on the Web, as well as the ability to control it, is an important part of the life of a successful professional and demonstrates your maturity and competence.

Take one hour to check your pages carefully. When checking do not forget about the following:
  • View your profiles on Facebook, Twitter, Instagram and LinkedIn and make sure your privacy settings are up to date. On these sites, privacy policies and default settings change quite often, so they should be periodically checked.
  • Ensure that all available images on your page are adequate and professional. You can even think about using one avatar on all sites.
  • Type your name in the Google search box. If you find content in your search results, try deleting it or changing your privacy settings. If the detected content is out of your control (for example, photos with tags), contact the administration of the resource and politely ask them to be removed.
  • Periodically review the rules for using the Internet and social networks in your company to always meet their requirements.

Although constant self-control is not always a pleasure, but maintaining a reputation on the web is necessary if you want to succeed in the professional field. The ability to control one's behavior is a sign of maturity and competence.

BOOK YOUR VISITS TO COURSES OR WORKSHOPS


The famous quotation says that learning is terminated only after the death of a person. It may sound a bit gloomy, but when it comes to career, this statement is an axiom. By adding information about additional training to your resume, you will significantly increase the chances of getting a potential employer. First, you will have confidence in yourself, as you will feel more competent in a particular area, and secondly, your attractiveness as an employee or candidate for the position will increase.

If you have the opportunity to negotiate with the employer about vocational training at the expense of the organization, be sure to take some time to attend scientific conferences, the topics of which correspond to your career plans. Looking for something less useful in your specialty? Currently, a sufficiently large number of conferences have emerged, from which one can draw inspiration to take on new tasks (although a significant part of them is conducted in English, but foreign language is one of the components of success ).

You do not have enough money or time to attend major conferences? Here are some options for free or low-cost courses that will help you improve your professional skills (you can also find a list of 30 useful sites that will help you become much more educated):
  • Lynda.com - Offers thousands of video tutorials starting at $ 19.99 per month.
  • Toastmasters is a proven and reliable organization with a very low annual payment that helps people improve their speaking skills and the ability to deliver material.
  • The US Small Business Administration also offers free online courses on major topics.

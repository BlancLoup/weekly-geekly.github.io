Donald E. Knut (Donald Ervin Knuth) - Professor Emeritus at Stanford University, author of the editor, or rather, the computer layout system TeX .
The main business of Knut's life is his fundamental encyclopedia of algorithms, The Art of Computer Programming . Twenty years ago, Knut ceased active teaching and devoted himself entirely to the work on this book.
In its review of the most outstanding scientific books of the 20th century , American Scientist magazine included Knut's book among the 12 most outstanding monographs in exact sciences, along with the books The Meaning of Relativity by Albert Einstein, The Foundations of Mathematics by Russell and Whitehead, and Cybernetics by Norbert Wiener , "Quantum mechanics" by Paul Dirac.
At present, three volumes have been published (all three have been translated into Russian) and work is underway on the fourth ("Combinatorial algorithms").

Search for talent on Youtube using acoustic analysis

The Google Music cloud music service was presented to the public during the May 2011 Google I / O Conference. Unfortunately, it is only available from the US, although our people successfully go to the site through a proxy and even found a way to install the client for Android.

From the moment of the announcement, the service has become considerably more functional, and some features can be called unique. In June, the Instant Mix playlist generator (developed by Google Research) was added, which complements your playlist with new songs of the same style by analyzing the acoustic properties of the song: rhythm, tonality and instrumental coloring of the tracks, as well as the acoustic characteristics of the vocal. Representatives of Google Research initially stated that their goal was not to promote fashion groups, but to select songs from the ‚Äúlong tail‚Äù, that is, little-known teams. The main thing is the quality of music, because acoustic analysis works with objective physical characteristics and does not pay attention to brands. Instant Mix playlist generator can learn and explore the user's musical preferences.

And so the developers took the next logical step and applied the technology of acoustic analysis and machine learning to the entire sample of music videos on Youtube in order to try to identify potential ‚Äúhits‚Äù among new, unknown artists.

First of all, they using selected image recognition technologies selected only those videos that were filmed at home, that is, home video. It was easy to do: the temperature of the color, the average or close-up of the person throughout the entire video clip (head and shoulders in the frame), the fixed position of the camera.



And then the most interesting part began - an acoustic analysis was used, similar to that used in the Instant Mix. To it was added the engine of recognition of the minimum number of user ratings (for self-study) - and after some time the technology was able to independently assess the quality of the video, anticipating the average user rating. In fact, the developers got a real technology of early recognition of hits that are popular with the mass audience.

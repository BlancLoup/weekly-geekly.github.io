What are the general distances?


The most popular triathlon competitions are held at the following distances.

  1. Sprint: 750 meters - swimming, 20 km - cycling, 5 km - running.
  2. Olympic distance: 1.5 km - swimming, 40 km - cycling, 10 km - running.
  3. Triathlon 70.3, or 113, or ‚Äúhalf-iron‚Äù distance: 1.9 km - swimming, 90 km - bicycle stage, 21 km - running.
  4. Triathlon 140.6, or 226, or "iron", or "classic" distance: 3.8 km - swimming, 180 km - bicycle stage, 42.2 km - running.

Below is an interesting historical background on how the ‚Äúiron‚Äù distance arose (from Wikipedia ).

The first competition in the classic triathlon distance (2.4 miles (3.86 km) - swimming, 112 miles (180.2 km) - cycling and 26.2 miles (42.2 km) - running) was the Hawaiian IronMan competition in Hawaii Triathlon. The idea of ‚Äã‚Äãa triathlon arose during the awards ceremony for the winners of the Oahu Perimeter Relay (team runner race) in 1977. Among the participants were numerous representatives of the clubs runners Mid-Pacific Road Runners and swimming Waikiki Swim Club, whose members have long argued, which athletes are more physically resilient - runners or swimmers. In this regard, one of the athletes, marine John Collins, also pointed to an article in Sports Illustrated magazine, which said that Eddie Merks, the legendary Belgian cyclist, had the highest recorded rate of "maximum oxygen consumption" (VO2 Max) among all athletes ever measured, and thus cyclists are the most physically fit and resilient athletes.

Collins proposed to resolve this dispute right on the race track, combining three existing long-distance competitions: the open-water swimming competition Waikiki Roughwater Swim (2.4 miles (3.862 km)), the Around-Oahu Bike Race (115 miles were usually held in two days) and the Honolulu Marathon (26,219 miles (42.195 km)). Until that day, the bike race was held in two days, so the race was reduced by 3 miles to put the distance between the finish line and the start of the marathon. Before the start of the race, each athlete received three sheets of paper with several points of rules and a description of the distance, as well as the inscription on the last page: ‚ÄúSwim 2.4 MILES! Race 112 MILES! Run 26.2 MILES! Be proud of this for the rest of your life! ‚Äù

In honor of one of the local runners, who was known for his exhausting workouts, Collins said: "Whoever finishes first, we call him IronMan (Iron Man)." Early in the morning of February 18, 1978, fifteen men took part in the competition, twelve of whom finished the race, and Gordon Haller received world-wide glory of the first Ironman, who finished the race in 11 hours 46 minutes and 58 seconds.

Hawaii Ironman Triathlon competitions are now the world championships in the classic triathlon. The selection of participants takes place throughout the year in a number of competitions around the world, also bearing the name Ironman Triathlon.

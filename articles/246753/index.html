LinkMeUp. Issue number 22. STC Metrotek. Software development for FPGA chips. Ethernet measuring equipment

The broadcast of the 22nd pre-New Year release of the podcast linkmeup turned out to be explosive, and the subsequent publication of the 10th installment of UDSM reinforced its success. There was a full house both in the number of listeners and in the number of new members of the VK group and in the quality of comments in Jabber.

And there is a reason for this - issue theme - Measurement of channel quality and software development for FPGA chips for processing 100 Gb / s lossless.
Our guests are STC Metrotek employees: Pavel Kurochkin (director of software development) and Ivan Shevchuk (FPGA development engineer).
In the podcast, we discuss:
  • Metrotek equipment lines - measuring equipment, switches, special designs.
  • Methodology for measuring communication channels and in particular Ethernet: two-way and one-way delays, jitter, bandwidth, BERT .
  • Measurement standards: RFC2544 and Y.1564, OAM, TWAMP / OWAMP.
  • FPGA chips. What is used, what is different from ASIC .
  • FPGA design for handling 100 Gbps Ethernet traffic. Problems, solutions, concepts.

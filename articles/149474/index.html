Nonlinear management. On the way to creation

Such concepts as leadership, non-linearity, openness, self-organization, creative thinking are gradually beginning to enter into modern management. It all starts with those companies that have decided not to take ready-made templates and build their own business on their basis, but to create their own internal rules, which as we eventually become, become the benchmarks.

The issues of personal effectiveness in one degree or another concern each of us. Someone is obsessed with these issues and spends a daily struggle with his ego in an attempt to start doing something that he really does not want to do, someone does it more naturally, and every day becomes a day of life, not a struggle.

Modern management, like the science of organizing and managing people, like no other discipline, feels so deeply all this confrontation that takes place in a person. But the neutralization of this internal struggle means that the energy spent on this struggle can be directed to the creation and implementation of new concepts and ideas.

In general, it has long been understood that it is impossible to impose development paths on any complexly organized system; rather, it is required to facilitate the formation of one‚Äôs own path. This is not only an evolutionary view of things, these same laws apply everywhere.

Those who were engaged in biology and biochemistry know that for complex systems there is no rigid predetermination - there are always several alternative ways of development. It is also known that all living things strive for self-organization, and self-organization is the first sign of the beginning of evolutionary changes. However, at present, humanity does not have a holistic view of self-organization. Modern management only approaches the understanding of this issue and is just beginning to learn how to use human resources in a new way.

If we shift the experience of the biological evolution of all living beings to management, it becomes obvious that the mechanisms of rigid selection and competition are not the only correct ones and there are dozens of other ways that allow to achieve high-quality results while reducing time and effort.

The heuristic potential of leadership and self-organization based on this leadership is not yet fully evaluated and studied. From openness to self-organization
Openness is a necessary, but not sufficient condition for the self-organization of the system. Every self-organizing system is open, but not every open system is capable of self-organization. It all depends on whether heterogeneities in an open system will form or, on the contrary, dissipate.

Linear thinking in the modern world is still the dominant type of thinking, but now it is insufficient and may even be fundamentally dangerous in a non-linear world. It leads to dogmatism and blind fanaticism. Everything new is born out of chaos.

General Director of the RSP and RAO - about the global license, the theft in the Russian Authors' Society, about his resignation


Source: RAO reporting

The Russian Authors' Society ( RAO ) receives payments from all television channels, radio stations, theaters, cinemas, cafes, shops, and any public institutions in general. Given the large number of taxpayers (there are more than 28 thousand), in 2014 a significant amount was collected - about 4.6 billion rubles. From here, approximately 856 million rubles were deducted "to cover the expenses of RW for the collection, distribution and payment of royalties."

In the summer of 2015, it became known that law enforcement agencies were investigating the details of the alleged theft of RW assets. We are talking about hundreds of millions of rubles. That summer, RAO announced its intention to merge with two other accredited organizations for more efficient fundraising.

And finally, on December 17, it became known that Sergey Fedotov leaves the post of general director of the Russian Union of Rights Holders ( RSP ). However, he will continue to lead RAO.

He told the publication of RBC about the reasons for his departure, the reform of the RAO, the investigative verification and the work on projects for the protection of copyright.

About global license


Earlier, it was the Ministry of Culture that prepared the draft amendments on the global license. The author of this idea is the Russian Union of copyright holders. The essence of the idea is the need to obtain a global license by Russian telecom operators from an organization accredited by the state. This initiative in the spring of 2015 has already received negative reviews from most experts: the Ministry of Economic Development, the Ministry of Justice and the Ministry of Communications and Mass Media, as well as from Internet companies and telecom operators.
The fact that the Ministry of Communications has its proposals ... We respect these proposals, but at the moment, the Ministry of Culture still oversees our position.

As far as I know, the Ministry of Communications and Reconciliation reacted with understanding to the fact that the PMC (Collective Management Rights Organization) works on the basis of the so-called blanket (non-contractual; on behalf of all) license.

There will be no more global licenses as proposed. The only mechanism is to do this together with the communications industry. We will deal with this topic in any case, since we represent the interests of a large number of authors who are not covered by the publishing industry, who have not transferred their rights to anyone, any production centers. In this sense, RAO is also a player along with publishers who protect the interests of their authors. It is in their interest that we tried to promote a global license. And I still think that they do not get enough of what they owe.

Ukraine received a Cyrillic domain.

Following Russia and Ukraine, the Cyrillic domain . finally received. International Corporation ICANN, managing address space on the Internet, at a meeting of the Board of Directors on February 28, decided to delegate Ukraine to the . domain.

It is rumored that the registration of domains in this zone may begin as early as this summer. Priority for registration in this domain zone can be obtained primarily by companies that own trademarks and state bodies.

It will be possible to register both in Russian and in Ukrainian. The cost of registration is not yet scheduled. It is expected that up to 200 thousand domain names will be registered in the . domain zone.

An application for the issuance of a Cyrillic domain to Ukraine was submitted in the autumn of 2009, and in the spring of 2010 the registration of domains in the . zone was to open, but the process was delayed a bit.

It will be interesting to see how transparent domain registration will be.

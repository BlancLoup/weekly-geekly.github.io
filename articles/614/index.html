Difficulties in the development of UAnet

The Ukrainian Internet does not stand still, but this development cannot be called too rapid. It is possible to compare Ukraine and Russia, and it is regrettable to note that there are more users and good projects there, and this difference is not proportional to the population (here is an example of Habr ). About bourgeois silent.

There are reasons that cause the current situation. Some of them (but not all) are described below. But the main purpose of this text is to find out what people think about the complexities of the development of UAnet. So you have the opportunity to significantly increase the value of this text by adding it with your own thoughts on this matter. Please do not hesitate.

1. Russian language . I am generally against national discord, I myself speak (and, behold, I write) in Russian. But the fact that the Ukrainian language has not acquired the status of a full-fledged national language, calls into question the need to create national resources. For example, the majority of Ukrainian sites are created in Russian, since the majority of Ukrainians understand Russian. Yandex with Google is wonderfully looking for this segment, most of them use it. As a result, there is no normal search engine in Ukraine and the prospects for creating it are vague.

2. The high cost of Internet services . Ukrainian Internet is expensive. And access, and hosting. Even so far we are so far away (we will keep silent again about the bourgeois). True, recently the situation has begun to change for the better, but somehow very slowly.

3. Relative poverty of the population . To become an Internet user, you need to buy a computer (which in itself is not cheap), for some reason it will connect to the network, and then also regularly pay for this connection (also not cheap, see paragraph above). All this requires significant financial costs, just because they have nothing to do, they can not try.

4. Lack of state support . The above three problems largely depend on government structures. These structures are still occupied with problems that are more important than increasing the availability of Internet technologies within the framework of the development of the Ukrainian information society.

5. Freelance and offshore . The development of Internet projects is suitable for remote work much stronger than the work of a hairdresser. Therefore, the majority of talented developers in the local market does not work, looking for more fish places.

6. Lack of a full-fledged electronic payment system . It is impossible to imagine e-commerce without the ability to quickly and safely pay electronically for something.

Pip-Boy 3000: a new incarnation of the device from Fallout (without Rad-X)



Fallout lovers are not the first year to create all sorts of versions of Pip-Boy. Someone is just a useless piece of plastic disguised as a working device, and someone creates a full-fledged, functional device.

The other day, the web has information about Pip-Boy 3000, a device that can perform part of the functions of that device from the game.

The new Pip-Boy is made on the basis of a real iPhone smartphone, and the case is printed on a 3D printer. In addition, a corresponding iOS application was created for Pip-Boy 3000 that can display maps, owner coordinates, temperature, and weather conditions.

In addition, the device can determine the level of radiation. True, like all previous incarnations of Pip-Boy, this device is deprived of the injection system of the anti-radiation drug Rad-X. It's a pity, of course, but nothing can be done, doctors have not yet created this medicine.

In addition to the smartphone and the case, the device is equipped with an inexpensive controller and radiation background meter (by the way, this meter was developed by the same development team).

In the near future, the creators of Pip-Boy 3000 plan to add some medical sensors that allow you to display information about the health status of the owner. Actually, it is not so difficult to do this, at least to introduce basic sensors, of which there are already quite a few on the market.

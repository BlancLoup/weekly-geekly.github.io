My phone from Microsoft launched

Today, March 6, I received a notification that registration is available on the My Phone service for Windows Mobile-based communicators.

I logged in, registered by my old corporate account on MSN, downloaded the application, installed it, synchronized. Everything went smoothly, without problems, even documents and multimedia from the memory card fell to the server.



Functionally, the service is simple: the ability to synchronize Contacts, Calendar, Tasks, SMS (that's nice), Photo, Video, Music and other Documents is presented.
Files are presented in the form of a simple list, without nesting levels (everything that was found on the phone alphabetically entered the list).
All documents can be downloaded, viewed, archived for storage on the site.
In general, if you are going to change, reflash the phone and want to save all your good, while having a wide channel on the Internet, then the solution is quite worthy.

Service advantages:
Russified application on the communicator (support WM Professional 6 and 6.1);
simple interface.

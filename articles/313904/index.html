Lotus



The program was named 1-2-3, as it included 3 elements: tables, graphs and basic database functionality.


Lotus 1-2-3 was first introduced on January 26, 1983, and during the first year the sales of the program surpassed the most popular VisiCalc table program. In the following years, it was the most used spreadsheet program for the IBM PC. Its popularity was largely determined by the inerrancy and high speed of work; the latter was achieved due to the fact that when writing the program the assembly language was widely used, and the output to the screen worked without using relatively slow BIOS calls by directly writing to the video memory. Electronic Frontier Foundation




The Electronic Frontier Foundation was formed in July 1990 by John Gilmore, John Perry Barlow and Mitchell Kapor in response to a series of actions by law enforcement agencies that made it clear that the authorities have significant information about new forms of communication on the Internet and that there is a need in enhancing the protection of civil liberties on the Internet.

The scope of activities includes support, development and development of new bills to protect the civil rights of users, strategies for general censorship requirements, community diversity, etc. There is due to the contributions of individual members and corporations [1]. The EFF participated in lawsuits against organizations such as the US Department of Justice, Apple, Sony BMG and AT & T.

Fully disable ads in Chrome can only corporate users


In January, Google decided to change its browser's APIs, which are not compatible with most third-party ad blockers and a number of extensions. As it turned out, the innovation makes it impossible to use the webRequest API. This is usually done to block content on the fly, right at the time of page loading.

Google will block ads in its browser, but only for paid Chrome corporate users . Corporate customers will be able to develop and install their own extensions for this browser, which means that third-party extensions will work for them.

All other users will have to switch to a less efficient, rules-based declarativeNetRequest API system. One of the developers of well-known advertising blockers said that if the changes are accepted, its extensions will no longer be able to work normally. Developers of other anti-advertising extensions and plug-ins expressed a similar opinion.

Officials will oblige gadget makers with eSim to store data on the territory of the Russian Federation




Companies that ship their devices with eSim support to Russia will be forced to host servers in the country . Servers will store subscriber profiles and cryptographic keys. According to representatives of the working group, which includes employees of the Ministry of Communications, the Federal Security Service and telecom operators, otherwise it will be impossible to guarantee the safety of personal data of subscribers.

True, virtual card testing has not yet revealed any security problems, as stated by opponents of regulatory measures.

As for the eSIM technology itself, it allows the subscriber to simultaneously use several operator profiles at once, and the subscriber can switch between them independently. You can switch from one operator to another at any time. Thus, there is no need to install an ordinary physical SIM card of a certain company. In the Russian Federation, devices such as Apple's iPhone XR, XS and XS Max, as well as, for example, Google Pixel 3 and Pixel 3 XL, are compatible with eSIM. Develop their own solutions for eSIM Gemalto, Idemia, Ericsson, GigSky, Samsung, Nokia HPE, Huawei, China Telecom, etc.

Communication has become more expensive in Russia




Due to the cancellation of roaming in Russia, communications began to rise in price . The minimum package tariff since December last year has increased in price by 7 rubles, up to 262 rubles per month. Rosstat, however, speaks of even greater growth. Market players claim that the cost of communication is increasing due to the cancellation of roaming. A few more reasons are the increase in VAT and the need to adapt the ‚ÄúSpring Law‚Äù, for which funds are needed.

The average cost of the minimum packet tariff for mobile communications in the Russian Federation from December 2018 to May 2019 increased by 3%, from 255 rubles to 262 rubles. The last time the price increase was recorded after analyzing the tariffs of operators from October 2016 to August 2017.

Raised registered in all regions of Russia. Roaming in Russia was finally canceled on May 27, when Prime Minister Dmitry Medvedev signed a decree, which became the point in this matter. In the future, it is planned to cancel roaming between the member countries of the EAEU.

MIPT team reached the final of the Amazon contest




Amazon later this week published a short list of the Alexa Prize Socialbot Grand Challenge 3 . Of the 375 applications submitted, the Alexa Prize committee selected 10 finalists, including the FTI team. Each team will receive a research grant of $ 250,000. In addition, the winners will have access to the Extended Topical Chat dataset and support from Alexa developers.

The competition is a competition for students, which is devoted to the development of technologies of conversational artificial intelligence. The main goal of the competition is to create a bot that can communicate with people on various topics, including entertainment, sports, politics, technology and fashion. Development of the prototypes proposed by the teams will begin in September, the winner will be announced in May 2020.

The MIPT team is called DREAM and it includes graduate students of the Phystech and iPavlov project staff from the laboratory of neural systems and in-depth training at MIPT: Dilara Baymurzina, Nikolai Bushkov, Idris Yusupov, Dmitry Karpov, The An An Le and team captain, Yuri Kuratov.

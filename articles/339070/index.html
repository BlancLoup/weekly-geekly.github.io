Preview pitches


My application with the presentation was promptly answered, invited to the pitching training. It turned out that about 50 projects will take part in the competition! 50? Our chances of winning a nomination of 50% (either win or not) at this point fell 25 times to 1 in 50 or 2%.

60 seconds was allotted for each pitch. The task to pack a story about your project in a minute seemed difficult: to determine what information about the project should be left and how best to present it.

By the way, the training was serious, the organizers had valuable and accurate feedback and recommendations on preparation. Each participant was given advice on what to change, what to add, what to refuse, what to focus on. As a result, the first versions of the pitches were noticeably different from what was on the Spb Startup Day scene.

My conclusions regarding pitches


The pitches had different goals. Someone wanted to attract investors, and someone just wanted to take the opportunity to tell about his project from the stage to a large audience. Someone already has a product with a thousand users, and someone just came up with an idea and a presentation from one or two slides.

What is a pitch for me?
I perceive this format as a very fast presentation on the run, to someone very important and busy. You were only for a minute in the elevator with the investor, he is not up to you, but there is nowhere to go. This is your chance! Briefly, in the case so that he has time to understand what you are doing. Time is short. The less time you have, the more you need to prepare for this. You can tell about your project for 1.5 hours without preparation, and if you need to tell in 60 seconds?

It is very difficult (and maybe not necessary) to compare the pitches of SPb Startup Day 2017 on any one scale, but I think it‚Äôs worth a look.

Who should go on stage?
If the excitement interferes with clearly and confidently speaking through the entire presentation, try to find a person in your team who will cope with this better. To answer difficult and important questions about the project, of course, should someone very well versed in it. But to tell 60 seconds from the scene is better for someone who does not fall into a stupor at the sight of thousands of people in front of him.

Are you a CEO? 2 years of SMM experience? Need to talk about this in a pitch?
Many projects spent precious seconds and presentation slides to show team members and roles in the project. Well, you know what I mean. Photos in circles with the signatures of the CEO and CTO, and so on. I think that this is not always appropriate, and in many cases you can do without it. Especially when you and your project are little known, and the competencies and experience of team members cannot impress, add weight. At the same time there were, of course, a couple of teams who had this very appropriate - the experience of the team added interest to the project.

Does your project have the necessary resources?
There are projects that start to work only with a large audience. For example, Avito - who needs to place ads where nobody sees them, because there is Avito, and millions go there. If you launch Avito killer - say at least a word about the resources for recruiting the audience. Sometimes the announced projects seemed simply incomparable in scale with what was presented to the audience ...

Competition with your slides
You're not so simple project, right? It can be difficult for people to simultaneously focus on what you are saying and on complex slides with lots of diagrams, graphs, pictures and information, it‚Äôs best to get rid of. Especially because 20-30 people have already performed before you. You have a little time - 60 seconds - decide what will be the main, and what addition. Either you complete the slides, or they complement you. Help people understand what to focus on.

Name, contacts!
Your project may become interesting to someone from the 3rd or 5th slide, make sure that your contacts and the name of the project are not only on the first slide, but also at the end.

What do you want?
Does your project need 1,000,000 rubles to increase the number of sales by 10 times, and the average bill by 2 times? So tell me! Want people to download your app? Tell them about it!

It seems to you that you made a clear presentation? Check this!
Show the presentation to friends far from your topic? Will they understand everything? What questions will they have?

Come to "him"
Have you seen from the outside how often a person crumples before approaching an investor? It is not always easy. If in your team you do it better than others - do it. Contact, write, come up, let's get a business card, let's look at the app in your hand, take their smartphone (asking permission) and put your app there! It is difficult, it may seem wild, inappropriate, but it is not only your personal chance. This is a chance for your team!
Today you will take the investor‚Äôs smartphone in your hands, put your application in - tomorrow your programmer‚Äôs wife will be proud of her husband, because his project has been recognized, appreciated, noticed, the programmer himself is happy, he has received recognition. Try to think this way when it is hard to approach someone.

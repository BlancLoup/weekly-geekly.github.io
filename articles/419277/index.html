Thermal tape with protection class IP68. Suitable for light in the bath

Friends, two times Ku! We are often asked how to make the light in the bath. Among the LED lamps and lamps with the desired temperature, we have not yet managed to find a suitable option. But tapes for these purposes exist. Let's see what they are, and also test their main light characteristics: color rendering index - CRI, color temperature.



Our experimental:

About assembly




Ribbons come in such brilliant bags. With a minimal set of information.



The tapes are inside the silicone case, but flexible. Enough volume. Silicone homogeneous throughout, marriage was not observed.

Light characteristics


To measure the light characteristics we will use a spectrometer-pulse meter UPRtek MF250N



Measurement options:

  • Spectrum graph;
  • Ripple index;
  • The percentage of pulsations;
  • Illumination (LUX);
  • Colour temperature;
  • CRI (Ra) color rendition index.

Tapes differ only in light temperature and color rendering index.

Water treatment


The tapes have IP68 protection class. The first number indicates that the tape is completely protected from dust. But the second digit is not so clear. Some reference books say: IP68 - Requirements for equipment subject to weathering. Equipment with degree of protection IP68 is allowed to be immersed in water, the depth of immersion is indicated additionally. On the packaging about the depth does not say anything.

But judging by the pictures from the supplier, the tape can highlight pools a couple of meters deep.



I did not think for a long time how to test the tape. Just threw in hot water in the bath for half an hour.



And then poured boiling water from the teapot. Tape bullying endured.

As a result, tapes are suitable for lighting in the bath / sauna / pool or any other wet and hot room.

Sadness: low color rendering index. But so far this problem with most tapes.

Added:


As correctly noted, I did not talk about how the thermal tape is made. To do this, refer to the description from the supplier.

Description of the features of this type of thermal tape.



1. The tape consists of a separate double thickened substrate made of copper, which maximally increases the heat transfer from the LEDs to the silicone substrate.



2. The tape is packed in a thick-walled silicone tube, which has a special profile, which ensures reliable fastening of this tape inside the silicone tube and a snug fit to the base. Also constructive tube provides a small gap between the LEDs and the silicone tube, which together with the poor thermal conductivity of silicone ensures minimal dependence of the temperature of the LEDs from the ambient temperature.



3. The tape is made on the basis of 2835 LEDs, 180 pieces per meter. For example, in the 17W ribbon strip, similar LEDs are used in the amount of 168 pcs / m. This lux tape consumes about 11.2W. This is due to the fact that resistors are installed in the tape, which limit the current to the LED, so that the LED does not work at the maximum permissible power, as it does in open tapes. Lossing in brightness, we gain in reliability - weak heating of the tape inside the tube provides its normal temperature conditions. The luminous flux of the 1st meter of the tape after the silicone shell is about 1000 Lm (For comparison, the Designed strip of 14.4 m is 1100-1150 Lux, the DesignLed lux of 17W / m is 1500 Lux).

The manufacturer of the tape is declared as an LED strip for Bath / Sauna (operating temperature up to +100 degrees).

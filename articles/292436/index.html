Oppenheimer Funds, the largest portfolio investor of Yandex, reduced its share in the company by a third

As of the end of the first quarter, its share was 10.5% of the share capital and 4% of the vote, or 34.86 million Class A shares. But at the end of June, the fund had 22.187 million Class A shares (6.9% of the share capital and 2 , 6% of the vote). Yesterday, Yandex shares were worth $ 14.8 on the NASDAQ. From this it follows that the sold share of Oppenheimer Funds is about $ 190 million.

This is not the first time that a large investor reduces his share in Yandex. Moreover, in May 2013, the American Tiger Global Management fund completely withdrew from the search engine capital. The fund then sold a 5.5% stake in Yandex.

Quadcopters are used by scientists, journalists, gadget lovers. In some cases, drones are used to illegally monitor people or even objects such as nuclear power plants. And such cases are increasing. In order to avoid the misuse of drones, the technology startup Rapere suggests using ... drones.

Rapere is a quadcopter whose main purpose is to detect, get close and knock down a target. In a startup, there are several specialists who have been engaged in the development of unmanned aerial vehicles for several years. This team created Rapere, which hunts for "bad drones", however, only in laboratory conditions.

In field conditions, Rapere has not yet been tested, but the principle of the device is quite understandable. The aircraft is equipped with 12 low-resolution cameras with 90 fps, which are used to detect other UAVs (in this case we are talking about quadcopters). When a target is detected, Rapere flies up to another UAV, while being above the victim. When the speeds of the devices are synchronized, Rapere drops a piece of rope that is wound on the victim's propellers, and the other quadcopter falls.

However, if the project develops, soon we will be able to see the war of drones, literally.

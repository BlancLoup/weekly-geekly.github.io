Sony Xperia V Review

Recently , Sony Xperia V is on sale in Russia - one of the flagships and the most relevant Sony models at the moment. It's no secret that the main driver of sales at this time of the year are holidays, and Xperia V is one of the candidates for getting under someone's tree. Does he deserve this? We'll see.




Three key, in my opinion, the characteristics of Xperia V: 4.3 ‚Äùscreen, waterproof, LTE. Let's start with them.

Screen


In the age of touchscreen smartphones, the main parameter of any phone is the screen: resolution and diagonal. With this, the Xperia V has everything in order: the 4.3 ‚Äùdiagonal holds 1280 pixels vertically and 720 horizontally - the normal HD resolution is, in general, like the Xperia S (with the same diagonal, by the way).



In addition, the display has a couple of new chips, which I just can not fail to mention: this is the new display production technology Sensor on lens, thanks to which the screen is brighter and has wider viewing angles and an improved Mobile Bravia Engine 2 technology that makes the image more realistic . The picture is really bright and juicy - in fact, as befits a top-end smartphone. In general, it just works.

Moisture protection


More precisely, moisture and dust protection. This has already become a proprietary Sony chip - only this year it released the mid-size Xperia go and the modified flagship acro S with protection against water and dust.









Now, among the phones that are not afraid to drop into the water, you can reach it in the pouring rain or accidentally throw a frisbee on the beach along with the Xperia V appeared. Its protection standard is IP 55/57, which means that some dust can penetrate inside, however this does not disturb the operation of the device, the insides are completely protected from contact; as well as protection from water jets from any direction (can be washed under a tap), during short-term immersion the water does not get into quantities that disrupt the operation of the device - you can take it with you to the bathroom. But, just in case - a permanent job in the submerged mode is still not supposed.

Design


At one time, Sony Xperia arc was recognized as one of the most beautiful smartphones in 2011 - and certainly was one of the most original. The main motive of his design was a concave cover, which gave him a name. In 2012, Sony returned to the arc-style in a number of its models. It will be appropriate to recall that the year began with the line of NXT - angular smartphones with a transparent strip under the screen. Thus, the Sony model range this year is mainly formed from NXT and arc-shaped models of smartphones.

It is also a noticeable detail of the appearance of the Xperia V, except for the concave cover - this is the lack of front keys, both physical and touch. Now they finally moved to the screen, appearing there as necessary.

Along the entire perimeter of the case, there is a powerful plastic rib under aluminum - this is the only design element that gives off the brutal essence of the phone - so to speak, body armor under a tuxedo.



Buttons on the case in principle has become less. Let's say a separate camera launch key has disappeared, now the fastest way to launch it is a button on the lock screen.

Specifications


Onboard V, the Qualcomm 8960 Snapdragon S4 chipset with the 1.5GHz Krait dual-core processor is the most powerful in the Xperia line.

1 GB of RAM promises to work without brakes, its own memory on a smartphone is 8 gigabytes, and an SD card is supported up to 32 GB.

The battery capacity is 1750 mAh, not so much, but it is partly compensated by the presence of an extended standby mode - a function included in the power settings, which means that data transmission is turned off if the phone is inactive for a while. This is a very significant saving of the battery, and, at the same time, the user's money.

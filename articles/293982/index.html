So, he has a very attractive hypothesis about another so-called "disease" that fits perfectly into our modest conversation - attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD); English Attention-Deficit / Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD).

I quote from Wikipedia, suddenly it will be blocked again:
Hunter and Farmer Theory - a hypothesis proposed by Tom Hartman to explain the causes of attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (in children - approx. Transl.) And ADD in adults, interpreting them as the result of adaptive behavior. Hartman notes that most or all of humanity has been nomadic hunters and gatherers for thousands of years. Then this standard gradually changed with the development of agriculture in most primitive societies and most people on earth became farmers. Over the years, most people have adapted to growing crops, but people with ADHD have retained some of the traits that hunters have. A key component of the theory is the proposal to consider the concomitant ADHD aspect of ‚Äúhyperfocus‚Äù as a gift or advantage. The theory also explains the difficulty of concentration and inattention in people with ADHD, as well as a number of other characteristics, such as neglect of social norms, weak organizational skills and planning ability, disruptions in the sense of time, impulsivity and impatience. It is noted that in cultures that existed at the expense of hunting and gathering and preceded agricultural crops, the hunter (most often the man) needed ‚Äúhyper-focusing‚Äù much more than the farmer (most often the woman). Hartman argues that this sexual differentiation is confirmed by the fact that ADHD occurs in boys more than twice as often as girls.

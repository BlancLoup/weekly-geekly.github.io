SCS in the server or the tale of how I first laid a lot of cable



Good evening. A couple of years ago, the company that the administration was expanding and moved to a new, more extensive premises. The landlord gave us a bare area, on which the builders erected walls, electricians pulled the wiring, and, for the first time, I was in the role of an SKS installer. This post is just a photo report, maybe it will be interesting for someone to see, and people whose assembly works are bread will give some comments and scold for the curvature of work. I ask under the cat (carefully, a lot of photos).

In total, from cabinets to the magic room (server room), there was 4.5 km of wires. In total there were ~ 70 cables for the Internet, ~ 60 for telephony, ~ 50 ACS and 16 for analog video surveillance. He did the work alone, except perhaps he took an assistant for a couple of days to lay the cable in the tray and pull all the wealth to the server.

With a wire tray came a small incident.

In the store, where they were purchased with assembly things, there was no fastener to the tray, or rather, the only fastener with an incomprehensible picture was in the catalog, the manager himself could not understand what this nonsense was. As a result, it turned out that the fixture for ceiling mounting, but I need something for the wall. Well, there was nothing to do, got out of the situation - went to the construction market, purchased metal strips 5mm thick, with the bat in the garage drilled holes along the edges under the bolts, bent the bands in a vice, turned into a figure in the shape of P, acquired bolts + big washers and that's what happened:

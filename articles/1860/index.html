Schmidt predicts politics with an eye on the Internet

Politicians should be more serious about the Internet, says Google CEO Eric Schmidt (Eric Schmidt).

The head of the search giant is confident that in the near future there will be online services that will check the veracity of loud statements and enticing promises made from the podium. The fact that the electorate has the opportunity to verify the veracity of the words of each of the candidates applying for a high position, in turn, cannot but affect the election results.

Software that can distinguish empty promises from truth, according to Google, will be released in the next five years.

10 main secrets of productivity Ilona Mask



This summer I read the book Ilona Mask Tesla, SpaceX, and the Quest for a Fantastic Future. A wonderful reading and detailed story about the ups and downs of one of the greatest visionaries of our time. The book also provided an insight into Mask‚Äôs work ethic and productivity secrets that he uses to manage several companies.

Many would agree with the opinion that Ilon Musk has an extraordinary mind, huge ambitions and energy. But I believe that we mere mortals may well use some of his secrets of productivity in our daily life.

1. Start your day with critical work.




Being the CEO of three companies - Tesla , SpaceX and Neuralink , - Mask has to do many things every day.

Therefore, he begins the day with the most important tasks. In his case, this is writing e-mails so that other people start / continue working to get things going. Usually his day starts at 7 am, Mask responds to letters for at least half an hour. He meticulously eliminates what is not critical now, and focuses only on key topics.

As he said in a speech at the USC Commencement:
‚ÄúFocus on the signal, not the noise. Do not waste time on things that are not really beneficial. ‚Äù

How to apply
Choose your most important task (SVZ) for this day and take it first. The SVZ should have the strongest impact on your work.

For example : my most important task is to write a great text. Therefore, I always start my day by sitting down and writing. I do not proceed to the next task until I write, at least 1000 words.

Select the SVZ will help Pareto rule (80/20). Make it your habit to first solve the SVZ, before moving on to other tasks.

2. Use feedback loops




Mask has a very busy schedule, he often has to work in several places during the day. Therefore, he constantly tries to optimize his time costs.

Although Musk admits that he did not read any books on time management, however, he shares useful advice:
‚ÄúI think it‚Äôs very important to have a feedback loop when you constantly think about what you did and how you could do it better. So the best advice is to constantly think about how you could do something better. ‚Äù

Mask uses not only his own feedback, but also the opinion of other people: prompts him to look for desirable negative reviews. At first, this can be frustrating, but usually you can get much more from negative feedback.

Musk is also looking for the best people in any areas that provide constant and confidential feedback.

Reducing the feedback loop improves efficiency, speeds implementation and allows you to create better products.

How to apply
This approach works great both in professional activities and in everyday life.
Gather your team and get feedback on a particular product, feature, management style, business process, or anything else that you are currently trying to improve.
"Don't tell me what you like, tell me what you don't like."

You can do the same with friends. And although negative reviews may be wrong, you will know that friends are just trying to help, and they have good intentions.

3. Consider, based on the root causes




" Primary Cause " is a basic assumption that cannot be derived from any other. This is the only indisputable challenge.

Mask argues, based on the root causes , and not by analogy (for example, with past experience). Thus, the reasoning is built from scratch:
‚ÄúYou look at the basics, build reasoning on them, and then see if your conclusion works. It may differ, and may coincide with what people have done before. But it‚Äôs harder to think this way. ‚Äù

Here is an example of how Mask reasons, based on the root cause :
‚ÄúWhat is the rocket made of?‚Äù From aerospace aluminum alloys, titanium, copper and carbon fiber. Then I asked, what is the cost of these materials in the commodity market? It turned out that the cost of materials is about 2% of the typical cost of the rocket. "

And instead of buying ready-made rockets for millions of dollars, Musk decided to acquire materials to build cheap rockets on his own. This is how SpaceX came about.

How to apply
Reasoning from the root causes makes you think differently. The root cause is the ability to get to the root of the problem. It is necessary to break the task into components.

There are three main steps in the application of such a thinking framework:

1. Recognizing and defining current assumptions : faced with a task, write out your current assumptions about it.

2. Highlight the root causes : find the main, basic truths or elements of the problem. As Musk says: ‚ÄúDrop everything, leaving only the fundamental truths, and say‚Äú well, what we are sure of is true ‚Äù... and build on this reasoning from this.‚Äù

3. Create new solutions : if in the previous two stages you decomposed the task into composite ones, then you are now ready to create new solutions from scratch.

4. Use asynchronous communication.




The first of these hacks gave you a small hint: Musk prefers to communicate on his own terms. That is , the default uses email and text , asynchronous methods of communication.
‚ÄúI love email. I try to communicate asynchronously whenever possible. I have excellent mail. ‚Äù

And thanks to the use of a hidden address, it is difficult for him to contact those who do not work in his companies. This allows the Mask to focus on work tasks.

How to apply
Progress depends on the focus and application of Deep Work principles. This means living as asynchronously as possible and with minimal distractions from colleagues.

Three solutions for how you can start working on your own terms (in order of increasing difficulty):

1. Disable notifications : close all notifications on your phone, computer, and any other gadgets. If there is something really important, they will call you.

2. Do not go to meetings : do not agree to meet until the agenda is clear and until you know what kind of result you can expect. If possible, use email instead of meetings.

3. Work remotely : a noisy office distracts, and at home you can work in silence <ha, the author does not have small children - approx. trans.>. If this is not possible, try to knock out a separate room.

To achieve results in important work , try to distract as little as possible.

5. Manage communications




When Musk does not build rockets and does not revolutionize the automotive industry, it can only be ‚Äúreached‚Äù by mail. He jokes at conferences: ‚ÄúI am sending many letters - I have mastered it perfectly. This is my main competence. ‚Äù

In his letters, Musk expresses very clearly, briefly and directly. For example, read the letter sent to all staff about the use of abbreviations, aptly named ‚Äú Acronyms Seriously Suck ‚Äù.

He often sends letters to entire companies with news, with instructions on communications , about the company's vision and mission, about how to be more productive in work.
‚ÄúPeople work better when they know the purpose and the reason. It is important that people look to the future, come to work in the morning, and get pleasure from it. ‚Äù

He also performs masterfully in public, sincerely speaking about complex ideas in simple language. Musk often speaks in present tense when it comes to visionary topics. This is a language trick that evokes a sense of the coming future.

How to apply
According to a study by Carleton University, a third of the week of the ‚Äútypical‚Äù knowledge worker goes to the correspondence. Therefore, communication by mail is akin to art.

You need to be expressed briefly, but at the same time convey your thought. Every word is important in the mail. Here are some tips:

Be brief : do not write ten expressions when two are sufficient. You can practice this: take your letter, written in a familiar manner, and cut the number of words in half.

Avoid "soft" words : do not write "I feel", "I am not sure", "maybe", avoid the passive form and any participle. All this is wasting your time and your addressees, leading to confusion and misunderstanding.

Know what you want : think about what you want to achieve with your letter, and first of all, put it in simple language. After some practice, this presentation will be a letter.

Highlight important in bold : if in a correspondence with several recipients you need to answer a specific person, select in bold its name, question and time.

Transfer ethics : never just send a long chain of correspondence without a few new items as a summary explaining why you are sending it and what you want from the addressee.

6. Batch tasks




Mask is strategically multi-tasking. When it is possible, it combines several tasks - this life hacking is called batching. For example, he answers letters while he eats or meets with someone at dinner.
‚ÄúI found that I can communicate with children and continue to write letters. I can spend time with them and work at the same time ... If I didn‚Äôt know how, I couldn‚Äôt do my job. ‚Äù
Another example - he sends letters and bills, talking on the phone or giving an interview.

How to apply
Studies say that multitasking is usually less effective than single-tasking. The brain needs time to rebuild to another task, this is called task switching. Not the tasks themselves, but it is this switching that tires you and makes you unproductive.

But if you pack similar tasks that require similar types of thinking, then you can effectively work on several tasks without slowing down the workflow. In other words, the brain focuses on one type of task at a time.

A couple of examples:
‚Ä¢ Sketch at a time the outline of all blog posts for the next week.
‚Ä¢ Process at once all letters, messages in the messenger, phone calls and other types of communications.
‚Ä¢ Update multiple related tables at once.

To understand what other tasks you can combine, write down all of your main activities during the day and week, and think about what you can combine. If necessary, change the composition of the "packages".

To speed packaging, you can use the tomato method .

7. Plan




The management of three companies is a considerable feat, that is, time is an important factor for Mask. He constantly tries to optimize the use of time with the help of feedback loops.

Like many other overproductive and successful people, Musk follows a very detailed and specific day plan. He divides his calendar into five-minute segments, and you will not be easy to squeeze into one of them.

He gives priority to engineering, design and production, spending 80% of his working time on them.
‚ÄúI do not spend time talking about something transcendental. I spend time solving engineering and production problems. ‚Äù

Breaking his day into five-minute segments, Musk plans a day more tasks.

How to apply
The most productive people work with the calendar, not with a to-do list. Calendars are limited and allow you to feel better time, so it's easier for you to determine how much time you have to complete projects during the week.

Splitting the day into small sections and scheduling tasks on the calendar helps improve productivity. But you are not obliged to divide by five minutes. I believe that for my work it is more efficient to divide the day into 30-minute segments. Therefore, find the division that would best suit you and your work.

And make sure you plan EVERYTHING: checking mail, calling customers, lunch, and meetings. Everything should get into the calendar. Tear down the to-do list and work on the calendar.

9. Develop a growth set.




In 2004, Musk called the supplier to find out the price of an electromechanical actuator. The supplier requested $ 120,000. Based on the root cause, Musk figured out what components are needed, and asked Steve Davis, who now works at SpaceX as the director of advanced projects, to make one actuator from zero for less than $ 5,000. Davis spent nine months on designing and creating device, which cost $ 3,900 and flew into space in a Falcon 1 rocket.

Mask is never satisfied with the current position. His companies have achieved great success, but Musk knows that there is no limit to perfection - in all areas. You can always do better, faster or cheaper:
‚ÄúProceed from the fact that you are mistaken. Your task is to make less mistakes. ‚Äù

This is called setting on growth, an important skill that distinguishes successful people from all others. Having developed such a setup, you will know that you can learn anything if you make enough effort. And if you fail, then approach the task on the other hand, until you find a working solution. You will try until you succeed.
‚ÄúHere failure is an option. If you do not fail, then you are creating a little new. "

The opposite is the setting of permanence, when the current state of affairs is rarely reviewed. Everything will remain as it is, because "it is so accepted." Stereotypes are considered universal truths and are not questioned. As a result, people are stagnating.

On the other hand, the development of growth attitudes in oneself allows one to grow both personally and professionally. And even if your daily achievements are small, they will accumulate over time. With a daily improvement of 1%, you will get almost 38% improvement per year.

How to apply
Growth stems from solving complex problems, issues and problems. To succeed, you need to train your brain to perceive failures and difficulties as a form of progress, approaching a solution.

Here's how to start developing a growth attitude:

Constantly learn : expand your knowledge through books, learn from your personal difficulties and from others. Loading your brain with fresh knowledge allows it to find new ideas and solutions that add value to your work and life.

Be persistent : change your perception and consider failures as short delays and rewarding experiences. Adapt and re-implement your ideas, so the next time you can succeed.

Choose the difficult : if you have a choice, then choose what is more difficult. Think of challenges as opportunities to expand your skills and grow above you.

Accept failures : they happen in the life of each of us. All fail. Learn from them, analyzing what went wrong and how to fix it, use the new experience in the next attempt.

Be open to feedback : a critical success factor is effective and timely feedback on what needs to be improved. Be more open to feedback, even non-constructive.

Honor the others : one is not a warrior in the field, so start supporting the successes of other people, and then they will not interfere with your success. When your moment of glory comes, they will celebrate with you.

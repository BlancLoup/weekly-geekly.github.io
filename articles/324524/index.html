Bioinformatics

During the week, participants will get acquainted with a variety of problems and tasks of bioinformatics. The topics covered will include both commonly used algorithms (for example, sequence alignment) and more specialized ones (prediction of the secondary structure of RNA molecules, determination of the origin according to genotyping, etc.). Every time we will go from a specific question that arises in modern biological research, try to figure out how to get an answer to it, study existing approaches, implement one of them and apply to real data. Advanced participants will be invited to work on their own project based on a real bioinformatics problem. Project examples:
  • Research on the gene regulatory network: simplifying the graph, identifying the most significant components and connections.
  • Search for de novo predicted V genes in short readings of the genome by filtering by k-measures, aligning and classifying its results.

The second stream takes up work on projects with the use of and more detailed immersion in the currently fashionable areas: deep learning, recommendation systems, RL, etc.

Security by Default

We are planning to introduce information security into all GoTo educational programs, and at this school we will begin to acquaint children with information security in a variety of contexts.
As a first step, we are holding GoTo Meetup: Security be Default in March, where we will look at the life of software in general, including writing safe code, administering and UX, and discuss how to do it better.
While we are used to doing physical infrastructure like bridges or houses taking into account wind and earthquakes, we often begin to think about the reliability of software after serious incidents. This is demonstrated both by Heartbleed, Cloudbleed or Shellshock, as well as thousands of open database servers, webcams and printers that are easy to find on the Internet.
This is due to the current development culture, focused on functionality, often at the cost of security, ease of support and reliability. And the lack of interaction between programmers, system administrators and users. And the development of obscure interfaces. And many more such "and", because security is a process.
We invite everyone to discuss these problems, as well as learn to notice and prevent them like this in advance. Bring your projects or ideas for discussion with experts to find out what can go wrong with them.

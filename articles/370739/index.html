The Voyager-2 station was launched on August 20, 1977, its twin - on September 5 of the same year. Until the fortieth anniversary of "Voyager 1" it remains to wait another couple of weeks. There is no confusion with the numbering of probes, since the specialists initially planned that Voyager-1 should overtake their fellow man. And so it happened: the probe with the number one was able to get ahead between the orbit of Mars and the asteroid belt.

Both stations went on different routes, which allowed them to go down in history. Voyager-1 is currently the most distant object created by man from our planet. And Voyager-2 is the first and only probe so far that was able to make a visit at once to the four planets-gas giants of the Solar System: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune.

Voyager-1 is not only the most distant man-made object from the Earth, but also the fastest. Its speed is 17 km / s. Now he is at a distance of 139 astronomical units, it is 21 billion kilometers. The flight data of both Voyagers is constantly updated on a special page of NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory.

Borders were groped in 2012. At this time, the device entered the heliopause zone, where the pressure of the solar wind balances the pressure of the interstellar medium. It should be noted that scientists, saying that the apparatus has left the system, speak precisely about the heliosphere. If we talk about going beyond the orbit of celestial bodies that rotate around the sun, this has not happened yet. Probe Features


Scientists took into account that a few years after the launch, Voyagers did not have enough solar panel energy to operate all systems. It is believed that in a few years the energy received by the probes will not be enough for normal operation, so that the systems will continue their way in radio silence.

Earth News


40 years ago, designers who developed probes wanted to connect with the alien mind. The authors of the project put news from earthlings into the apparatuses.

The main thing that the scientists wanted to indicate in the message is the location of the Earth. This was done by creating a specific map, where the main landmarks are pulsars. A total of fourteen, and each has unique characteristics. An alien civilization that can intercept Voyager (any of them) about the features of the pulsars should be known. Each of them has existed for millions of years, so this is a fairly reliable beacon that will work for a very long time. Alien mind can understand where the Earth is and after a million years. Perhaps some pulsars may exist for several billion years. All of them rotate rapidly, plus they have a unique ‚Äúprofile‚Äù of electromagnetic radiation pulses. The delay between pulses is also unique. As a result, the pulsars decided to consider "space beacons", and make a map based on the pulsars.



The length of the lines that connect each pulsar with the Sun (this is the central element on the map) show how far each of them is from our star. The rotational speed of the pulsars is written in binary code, so that all the data could be reduced to just 12 characters. In addition to the location of the Earth, the map is able to give an understanding of when the probe was launched.

The information in question was recorded on gold plates. The author of the card is Frank Drake. His daughter, Katherine Denning, says that scientists did not particularly argue about the possible risks of contact with "brothers in mind" when preparing the map and making gold plates. Now, by the way, Denning is studying the pros and cons of sending messages to extraterrestrial civilizations.

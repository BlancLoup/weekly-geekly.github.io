Review of the smartphone Lenovo P780


The logical continuation of the line of "long-playing" smartphones was the release of the Lenovo P780. This device is positioned as a development of the previous model Lenovo P770 with significant differences in hardware

‚ñ∫ Specifications

Screen: 5 ", 1280x720, IPS. Corning Gorilla Glass 2
Chip: Mediatek MT6589 (Cortex-A7 1.2GHz, 4 cores)
Video accelerator: PowerVR SGX544
Operating system: Android 4.2.1 (Jelly Bean)
RAM: 1GB
Permanent memory: 4GB + microSD slot with card support up to 32 GB
Camera: front 0.3MP, rear - 8MP
Connectivity: 2 sim cards (GSM + WCDMA), GPS, Bluetooth, Wifi (802.11 b / g / n)
Battery: 4000mAh
Dimensions: 143 x 73 x 9.95 mm. Weight: 176g.

Compared to the Lenovo P770, almost everything has changed: the P780 is equipped with a 5 inch screen with a resolution of 1280x720, a new 4-core platform MTK6589 and a 4000 mAh battery. An 8MP rear camera was also installed.

‚ñ∫ Screen

The Lenovo P780 is equipped with a 5-inch screen with a resolution of 1280x720. Made by technology IPS. Whether the technology is used OGS (one-glass-solution), I find it difficult to answer. But most likely not - as there is a slight unevenness of the backlight on the screen above and on the screen. Before that, I had the opportunity to use ZTE V987 smartphones, as well as Jiayu G4 - they clearly show that the screen is made using OGS technology, when the glass and touchscreen are a single whole, without an air gap between them.
The screen is bright, with good color reproduction, but a slight unevenness of the backlight and the tint in blue when the smartphone is tilted to the left slightly spoil the overall impression.

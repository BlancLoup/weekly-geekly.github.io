If you see something similar on the screen, then this is a clearly marked arcade scroller. "Arcade" means that the gameplay is simplified to the limit (beat-run) and elementary control, and the "scroller" is a continuous moving screen, without the ability to slow down and at least a little ponder what is happening. Scrollers are divided into vertical and horizontal, in the first case, the movement occurs from bottom to top, and in the second, respectively, from left to right, although it may in Arab games and right to left. The action of the scroller can occur in any scenery; it can even be a miner making his way to the surface of the earth from the piled mine, the main thing is the constant movement of the screen. In most cases, the object controlled by us can move freely in all directions, shoot various ammunition, collect bonuses, avoid obstacles and ... everything seems to be). The actions of the enemies are also not too diverse - they can be divided into 3 types:
  • Enemies appear at the far edge of the screen and, without changing the trajectory of movement, pass through the entire screen, perhaps by shooting something
  • Enemies appear at the far edge of the screen and move to the opposite edge of the screen, changing the trajectory of movement along some simple algorithm (zigzag, oblique, or spiral)
  • Enemies appear at the far edge of the screen and move at random, such enemies often do not disappear until you kill them.

How IT professionals work. Dmitry Tsimoshko, director of information technology at Century 21

We continue to ask specialists about the mode of work and rest, professional habits, the tools they use, and much more.

It will be interesting to find out what unites them, in what they contradict each other. Perhaps their answers will help to identify some general patterns, useful tips that will help many of us.

Today our guest is Dmitry Tsimoshko, director of information security of the international network of real estate agencies Century 21 . He has been working in IT for about 10 years. He has taken a career path from an ordinary IT specialist to the director of a large division in an international company. Dmitry is currently subordinate to more than 10 people - system administrators, developers, programmers, layout designers. Information technology has been keen on since childhood.

What do you do in the company?

I plan and distribute tasks among subordinates, control their implementation. In emergency situations (a CRM system has departed, the site does not work), we promptly react and solve problems within an hour. Another important task is to increase the base of counterparties that help to solve IT problems. At the moment, there are about two hundred such partner companies.

One word that best describes how you work:

Only forward (smiles).

10 hours.

How many hours do you sleep?

7 o'clock.

How do you have breakfast?

I love to have a hearty breakfast. These are mainly proteins - beans, meat, fish.

What are you doing on the way to / from work?

Outside of work, I also work most often: calling and texting on business issues.

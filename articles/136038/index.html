Top 10 games for iPhone / iPad (2011)

Shortly before the New Year, we made our own top best games for iOS. In this case, we were guided by three criteria: the fun of the gameplay, the beauty of graphic performance, and the quality of adaptation to the ‚Äúapple‚Äù platform or, if in Russian, ease of management and the absence of glitches. 10) Draw Race 2



This game is loved even by those who can not stand the race, and the machines with disdain. It's all about the unusual approach to the usual and, as it seemed, not able to surprise the genre of arcade pokatushek. The game, instead of forcing the user to steer his four-wheeled monster along a winding road, suggests drawing a route that the car will rush after starting. What will happen next - almost does not depend on the player. After all cars take off, he can only passively watch what is happening and cheer on his typewriter (well, press the ‚Äúacceleration‚Äù button at a critical moment).

9) Tiny Wings



This game has conquered many even more simple than the Angry Birds, the gameplay, the original exploitation of the bird theme and light psychedelic. It is difficult to call the hypnotic process Tiny Wings otherwise. Throughout the game, the little sparrows with bulging eyes jump from one striped slope to another, managing to collect coins with a rounded belly. All this under the sweet electronics and pigeon clucking.

8) Superbrothers: Sword & Sworcery EP



The most art-house project on mobile platforms. Pixel adventure of an incomprehensible girl in a mystical forest, in which there was a place for battles with demons, visiting ancient temples, rescuing locals, witchcraft books, magic spells, contemplative philosophy and, most importantly, a wonderful storyline. A very rare and very valuable combination not only for a project under iOS, but also generally for a good game.

7) Ticket to Ride



One of the most successful and, perhaps, successful ports of board games, despite the terrible country music and all the same as in the original, the map of the United States. Plus, a very important multiplayer feature: the ability to play with the four on one mobile device (and, by the way, the system itself keeps records, so no longer need to recalculate points for each turn for the tenth time). The latter plays a very important role. From desktop hardcore Ticket to Ride turns into a cute tablet casual.

6) Gesundheit



A game about a pig flu patient who fights horrible monsters, throwing snot. Certain sharpness in the gameplay adds an important point - he throws his gnaws not at opponents. It is useless to throw at them, they eat these snot with pleasure. So the piglet has to be tagged into traps, and more specifically into fish that pretend to be traps that eat monsters. In general, if it were not for the graphics in the style of children's notebooks painted with crayons, then the art director of Gesundheit could easily come with people in white coats.

5) Jelly Defense



The usual tower defense, painted under the acid after viewing the paintings of Salvador Dali. At least, this feeling is created when meeting the Polish project: mushrooms, smooth curves in the style of soft watches, white checkered backgrounds, tentacle-shaped laughing monsters, bellowed baleen centipede towers and Slavic electronics with a central accordion part. The output is a meditative project, from which it is doubly difficult to break away: on the one hand, proven mechanics, on the other, an abnormal art design in a good way.

The jump in attendance of file hosting sites

With a daily audience of 50 million people, by its own estimate, MegaUpload was one of the largest file hosting sites on the Internet. Closing it, the RIAA and others could have assumed that 50 million people would go to iTunes and other online stores with licensed content. Well, they are waiting for a little surprise.



All the largest webhosting experienced an unprecedented increase in traffic after closing MegaUpload. According to Alexa, as well as other monitoring services, attendance at Depositfiles, Uploaded.to, Hotfile.com, Rapidshare.com and others has increased by tens of percent.

So after closing the traffic was just naturally redistributed for similar services, the benefit of which is not lacking. Millions of users simply typed a different URL in the address bar of the browser.

Given on the chart Depositfiles, Uploaded.to, Hotfile.com, Rapidshare.com - this is only the tip of the iceberg, there are dozens or even hundreds of such services. Probably, a jump in traffic is registered on most of them.

Apparently, MegaUpload was not the largest file hosting. The leader is 4shared.com, with 2.5 billion page views per day, it was, by some estimates, twice as popular as MegaUpload before the last one was closed. Accordingly, in relative numbers, he felt the influx of traffic less than all.

However, everything is relative. The current jump is only a small fluctuation on the chart for two years.

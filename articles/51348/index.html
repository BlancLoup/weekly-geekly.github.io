Hello, dear habrayuzer.

Have you ever (wake up in the morning) to wake up, as they say "on the wrong foot"? The light is too bright, the sounds are too loud, your dear person is too intrusive, everything flies out of your hands, random objects are touched. Who did not happen!

Did you notice that this happens only when you are woken up? Whether it is your alarm clock, a phone call, or someone close to you, it doesn‚Äôt matter, the thing is ...

A bit of theory:



human sleep consists of 4 phases:

The first phase is the stage of falling asleep or, as it is otherwise called, drowsiness. This is the transition from wakefulness to sleep, which usually lasts 10-15 minutes.
The second phase is the phase of shallow slow sleep. This phase is the longest, takes almost half of the entire duration of sleep.
The third phase - the phase of the deepest slow sleep.
These are the phases of slow sleep .
And finally, the fourth phase - the phase of REM sleep . Or the phase of rapid eye movement (BDG). It is in this phase that we see the most vivid and memorable dreams.
At the beginning of sleep, the duration of slow sleep prevails, and before awakening, the duration of quick sleep increases.
All this is a long-proven scientific fact.



On average, a full cycle of 4 phases of sleep lasts for 1.5 hours, replacing each other, thus, a night's sleep consists of 4-7 cycles, and if we wake up ourselves, on weekends for example, then as a rule, at the end of all phases (which the bill depends on when you went to bed;))
So the point is not at all in ‚Äúthis or that leg‚Äù, but in the fact that the alarm clock interrupts the unfinished phase, which results in excessive irritability.

So what to do?





Of course, you can calculate the time of falling asleep and waking up so that sleep is a multiple of 1.5 hours, but there are a large number of factors that complicate this. For example, you probably can not know how long you fall asleep. You may eat watermelon in the evening, which will cause frequent visits to the toilet. Also, the state of a person, how tired he is, whether he consumed alcohol, how many hours before bed he ate - are important factors that affect the duration of the cycle, and in particular the phases. And so on and so forth ...
Believe me , this is not so easy as it seems and how they write about it, the author somehow tried to do it - almost without success.

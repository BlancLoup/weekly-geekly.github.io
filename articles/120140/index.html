Intermezzo


How many Internet address reduction services are not truly known to anyone. Exactly more than a thousand. Each serious company has its own short domain. Twitter spawned a whole industry. They differ only in additional functionality. However, they all take one URL and make another URL from it.

Prehistory


We decided to do something new, it would seem trite obvious. And why not take a lot of links, put everything in the tabs, and send it as one page?



A year ago, our programmers developed the name.ly/frames frame theme, which allows you to load a lot of resources into tabs, like in the built-in Internet browser. For example, all the sites of the famous Ukrainian and Russian political scientist Vitaly Portnikov open right here: sincere.ly/portnikov - and they also reflect on portnikov.com/sites .



The disadvantage of name.ly was the complexity and duration of the creation of the site. It was necessary to register, select a frame template and enter each link separately. It took about five minutes.

Brief.ly


We experimented a lot and came to the conclusion that everything ingenious should be done in a matter of seconds. So, based on name.ly, service brief.ly was born - for instant creation of compilations of many sites.

Compilation of sites




Brief.ly works as follows: in the text box, you can submit a list of links and, with a space, a signature for each of them. You can simply copy and paste links without signatures, brief.ly itself, based on domain names, will offer labels and allow them to be edited before creating the compilation itself. In the additional options you can download the original page titles, it really takes a little time, up to five seconds per link, depending on the speed of a particular resource.

Since all sites are loaded at the same time, in order not to overload people and their Internet, we have optimally limited the number of tabs to 36. You can work around this by compiling already compiled links, thus creating nested sub-pages.

Brief links


As a result of the compilation, a page with all the links is created. If the link is one, then everything works as in the classic shortcut. If there are more links, then the corresponding content is loaded in separate tabs.

Nuances


Everything is great, but there are a few "but." Not all sites support inserts through frames. Among the popular Russian resources such restrictions impose Yandex and VKontakte. Twitter and Last.fm pages pop up from the frames. Brief.ly has already learned how to "fight." Twitter profiles and Facebook fan pages are converted via their own widgets. Others are simply not inserted into frames, but are served by direct links.

Among the popular sites there are a dozen of such pop-up frames and the service constantly catches them. Although, 99% of the Internet works fine in frames and the service is quite usable for sending your favorite videos or articles.

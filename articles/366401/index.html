Queen of Office Mice: Logitech Marathon M705 Review

Peripherals for computers are more fortunate than computers themselves. It becomes outdated much longer than the computer hardware itself. This means that a mouse that was excellent three or four years ago may still be the best in its segment. Therefore, when my previous long-lived mouse broke, I moved to the Logitech M705 - a classic in the literal sense, time-tested.



I can not say that my choice is universal. But I shoveled a lot of information and wanted to share it with you. Moreover, usually the comments are much more interesting than the article itself, which means this is another way to get additional information.

A bit of theory, or what makes a good office mouse good


My main job is related to large amounts of text and code, long web pages of reference books, giant ‚Äúsheets‚Äù of tables in Excel and a rare need for photo processing. Having tried a bunch of options, I brushed off almost all the game models - I don‚Äôt need the variable resolution of the sensor and the abundance of programmable buttons, and besides, I don‚Äôt play at work. Secondly, I brushed off the options with a wired connection - the space on my desktop is too valuable to allow space for cable under it.

As a result of further thought, the following requirements were born:

1. The mouse should be ergonomic . Statistics show that most use the right hand to work with the mouse. This means that using a symmetrical mouse is tantamount to depriving oneself of some comfort. When working for 7-9 hours a day, the hand should rest comfortably on the mouse.

2. The mouse should be made of quality materials . With the same 7-9 hours every day, I do not want my hand to sweat and stick. For these reasons, the glossy surface of a rodent does not suit me.

3. The mouse should be comfortable for my grip and size of my palm . Since all people are different, then all hands will be different. Someone has a small palm, someone like Valuev. Moreover, many people still hold their mice in different ways. Fortunately, with regard to the grip, about 80% prefer grip associated with palm rest on the mouse. The same feature allows you to exclude small mice from the sample (lovers of the finger grip prefer compact models).

4. The mouse should be all-terrain, but I am not ready to overpay for the "ride" on the glass . As shown by the same polls, a very small percentage of users use a glass or mirror surface as a working one. This means that overpayment for the theoretical opportunity to work at the glass table I do not need.

5. If we are talking about polls, then about half of the users use the additional buttons "forward" and "back." Personally, I use them in the browser, plus in Excel, I hung on them switching between sheets. Very comfortably. Hence the following requirement - the mouse must have the necessary minimum of buttons , preferably programmable.

6. The mouse should have a small dongle . In the period of the mid-arm, i. when the old one was already dead, and the new one had not yet been chosen, I used the temporary option dragged off by the admins. It was GENIUS Navigator 635.



The main disadvantage is a healthy USB-receiver the size of a full-sized flash drive. For three weeks of use with a working laptop, I bent it at least twice, and once I almost broke it together with the port. The need to remember that I have unnecessary crap hanging somewhere, which is easy to bend, is very, very floating.

7. The mouse should have a long and trouble-free warranty . This means that if something stops working for me, I will get a replacement without any problems.

Selection results


As a result of a long digging in the reviews, the formulation of requirements and the search for models that meet them, I have no other option but to choose the Logitech Marathon M705 . Why precisely she? Firstly, she has very high ratings in most reviews and comparisons of mice. Several colleagues with palms more than mine, also comfortably settled on a mouse, and only one of them, the owner of large hands, grumbled about the inconvenience and returned to Logitech Performance MX, which he bought after Revolution MX.

disadvantages


Judging by the forums, the mouse has five major flaws. The first is the demands of the sensor. He, of course, drives well, but the mouse works best on a normal dark rug with a smooth surface.

The second - a number of users in a year or two have a double click when a button is pressed. Since this is a warranty case, and the warranty lasts for three years, I do not pay attention to this moment. It breaks down - they'll change it without any problems, it's Logitech. This, by the way, is the problem with the rebound of the inertial wheel. Due to improper balancing, a number of copies receive a bounce of the screen when stopping the wheel rotation in the ‚Äúinfinite‚Äù mode. This is a marriage, and to him, too, as I understand, is covered by the warranty. If the warranty ends, the buttons can be repaired by yourself - there are many instructions in the network, incl. and video, plus the Chinese have parts. Third, in the M705, the sensor is shifted to the right under the ring finger. This leads to somewhat inaccurate positioning in games. Actually, if you look at the mouse estimates on the market, this problem is the basis for reducing the ratings.



I specifically listened for a few days to the sensations during office work, and yet I could not catch this problem. The cursor goes where it is needed.

Fourth, after small mice people have complaints about the weight of the mouse. As for me, the weight is quite comfortable. But if this is a problem, one battery can be pulled out of the mouse and some can be lightened.

Fifth - a number of users sometimes had problems with the loss of communication with the receiver. As shown by the analysis of such flights on the branches of the forums, the cause may be interference from USB3.0 stuck into a nearby USB connector. The problem occurs very rarely, mainly on laptops. It is solved either by transferring the USB receiver to another, non-neighbor port, or by taking it out on a string.

And the sixth, most importantly, is its price. At the moment, due to the jump in the dollar rate, its price is about 3,000 rubles.

Mobility Express - when they decided to migrate and scale the wireless network, but as always, there is no money

Well, in this case, the scene comes as an example of Cisco Mobility Express (that's just a pity that Cisco came to this so late).

Prehistory


At the end of 2017, our Customer firmly decided for himself that as part of increasing production capacity and the need to add additional access points to an existing wireless network system, he is ready to fully switch to a Cisco wireless solution (this is the new corporate standard). The main problem when choosing a solution to replace existing access points was the problem of quantitative indicators (currently there are 40), as well as taking into account the growth of areas, judging by the estimates of Ekahau Site Survey & Planner, the figures were from 120 to 140 access points. For such a problem at the moment there is an excellent solution to the bases of the controller 3504, with support for 150 access points. But the further the project was budgeted, the more introductory it became. As a result, as it worked out, of course, the goal for the closest purchase, of course, was the purchase of 12 access points. And here comes the thought: "The controller is 3504 for 150 access points, and the purchase of only 12 points and while on the horizon, the clearance for the purchase of the remaining AP is not visible." Well counted. Customer Response: "... Wow, exactly the same seems like a tucked wing of an airplane!"

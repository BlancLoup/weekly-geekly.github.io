Review of Fidget Cube alternatives



More recently, Kickstarter was surprised by another high-profile startup: Fidget Cube created by brothers Matthew and Mark MacLachlan, known as Antsy Labs, collected more than six million dollars, which amounted to 41,682% of the originally required fifteen thousand.


Fidget in English means ‚Äúfidget‚Äù. A small plastic cube equipped with buttons, wheels and a switch performs one single task - it helps to calm and concentrate the owner, who turns it in his hands, turns the wheels and clicks the buttons. Such a tactile nerve damper is akin to a rosary or Chinese balls, but in our realities, the process of squeezing it can be compared to clicking a pen or spinning any small object in your hands.

The novelty caused a great resonance in the geek environment - among fans of various start-ups and innovations, raised a wave of hype and attracted everyone‚Äôs attention. Attention is well deserved, because it really works. In addition, this toy develops fine motor skills, which contributes to the formation of new neural connections and is the prevention of certain diseases.

There is only one small problem:

Not everyone is ready to make an order from abroad, wait a long time for delivery and overpay for it more than the toy itself costs. And at local retailers from whom the Fidget Cube can be purchased locally, prices are somewhat sad.

But there is no reason to be sad: we have picked up more than a dozen alternatives, other fidget toys, which will successfully replace this unusual item. 3D printing will help to choose and create any of them, without overpaying money, which even so much does not happen. Most of the presented models are distributed in digital form for free, with a free Creative Commons license, and the stamp itself will cost a penny.

Consumer basket of a black day: what is most often ordered on Black Friday from America

Less than two days are left until Black Friday, and we decided to talk about what this date means in terms of mail forwarder - i.e. a company that is engaged in sending purchases from the United States to the countries to which the local stores officially refuse to deliver.



How much the traffic of purchases from the USA to Black Friday is growing, what people buy most often, where to find really big discounts on expensive watches and a little disturbing observations about what this all leads to - in our review.

Due to the specifics of our business, the real analytics capabilities of shipments are quite limited: we simply send purchases and are interested in their contents, if only there are any problems. So, what do people buy on Black Friday?


In general, the composition of purchases is no different from everyday: most of the purchases abroad are clothes. Every year on Black Friday - a boom of orders for Timberland shoes, Levi's jeans and children's clothes. Separately, there is a growing demand for goods for which there is almost no discount at other times - on the Neiman Marcus network of the Canada Goose brand: coupons with discounts up to 30% can be found on Black Friday. Black Days for Black Friday


And the last observation from our experience about the state of emergency: oddly enough, the number of stores participating in it is constantly decreasing. This is happening against the background of how many retailers blur the very concept of Friday, starting sales on Thursday, Wednesday, a week before - or stretching it right up to December (as Amazon recently announced). Last year, almost any brand did something, published catalogs with discounts. This year, the number of stores actively participating in Black Friday decreased by about a third. Sierra Trading has reduced the stock offer: Familiar to every American mother, the names of Children's place and Carters have simplified participation on Black Friday to a total discount on all products. Maybe the phenomenon of Black Friday is starting to burn out and not everything is Black Friday more?

Based on our observations in recent years, the situation in trade in the US market is far from being the most rosy. Those who buy for a long time, observe how prices grow, and discounts decrease. Some stores are closed, others merge into concerns; discounts are standardized to a certain maximum, for which go less and less. To support the feeling of celebration, networks like Walmart and Target buy the cheapest home appliances in China, and actively advertise these offers ‚Äúfor 10 bucks‚Äù. These are our observations, not analysis - it would be interesting for us to understand the reasons for what is happening, and we are happy to read your thoughts on the topic in the comments.

However, the format of pre-holiday sales does not threaten to die: there will always be, on the one hand, brands that need to get rid of stale collections, brands with high margins, which they can easily lose under the guise of a grandiose discount - and just brands that need to take their place on the market (for example, as we wrote in the last review , Microsoft is luring customers of Surface products with discounts of $ 200-400, while Apple already is doing well, so any discounts are either retailers' initiative, or restored goods, or old e models). But the holiday of Black Friday, as it is now, can significantly fade in the coming years. We will see.

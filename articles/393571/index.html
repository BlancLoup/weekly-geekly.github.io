The first launch from the East was postponed due to the rebound of automation



Today at 05:01 Moscow time the first launch from the new Russian Vostochny cosmodrome was to take place. It was planned that the Soyuz-2.1a launch vehicle would launch the Lomonosov, Aist-2D and SamSat-218 (SamSat-218) spacecraft into orbit. They planned to broadcast live, and President Vladimir Putin himself flew to the cosmodrome.

At 2 o'clock, the state commission on the results of refueling and general readiness of the Vostochny cosmodrome made a decision on the missile readiness . A command was given to launch automatic cycles for pre-launch preparation of the launch vehicle and launching unit and withdrawal of the mobile service tower. However, the launch did not take place: the automatics shut down 1.5 minutes before the start.

Now the commission is studying the reasons. Conclusions will be reported additionally.

Google launched its own delivery service



Not so long ago, I wrote that Google is about to launch a delivery service. According to the plan, this should be a service competing with Amazon Prime and other similar services. Not so much time has passed since the news was published, and the delivery service of the ‚ÄúCorporation of Good‚Äù really worked.

So far, the delivery service, called Google Shopping Express, works only in San Francisco. And then, not for all residents, but only for those who live near the bay, with the same name. Now the delivery of goods is made on the same day when the order was made.



The most interesting thing is that within six months the service will be free for customers. These half a year the service will be improved, and the shortcomings will be eliminated. After 6 months of the test (open), the delivery service will work in full force, but, of course, you will have to pay for it. According to some media reports, Google Shopping Express customers will need to pay about $ 64-69 per year. This is somewhere at the level of Amazon Prime.



Such companies as Target, Office Depot, Staples, Toys 'R' Us, Blue Bottle Coffee and some other companies are already cooperating with the corporation and its delivery service. Of course, Google will have to really try to make your service popular. But with the resources of the corporation and its capabilities, we can expect that everything will work out.

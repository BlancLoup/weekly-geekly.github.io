Direct Marketing: Email & SMS Mailing



Good day.

It will be a question of Direct Marketing and in particular about my startup project Skyders.Direct. Everyone who is interested in this topic is welcome under cat.




Any company is always sooner or later required to inform its customers about new products or changes about existing ones. Not all companies work or have advanced CRM systems (which have built-in communication tools) to communicate with customers. The main means of communication with customers that are at hand is: a sales person (sales person) who can get around and tell about company news, telephone, fax, mail, email and sms. About the last two means of communication and will be discussed in this article. It is clear that such means of communication as a sales person, telephone, fax and mail are quite expensive, and even with a small customer base of 1,000 people, it may take weeks to alert you of updates to the company and financially unprofitable. In situations where customers are from different countries, this case may generally be questionable due to different time zones, additional costs, and so on.

Imagine a store selling products (for habro readers, you should rather submit a store selling hardware / software). So, time goes by and the clientele has grown significantly. At first, the seller knew everyone by sight, but then buyers from other areas reached out to him. The time comes and he does not know his customers. To stay on the market in a tough world of competition, you need to know how to please your customers even more, how to achieve their loyalty and what to bet on for the future: is it worth expanding, increasing the range, and most importantly how to keep existing customers and how to attract new ones? For this you need to analyze the situation and here will help the methods of marketing. First of all, a base of major clients is created. How to do it? In many ways, in my opinion, one of the most effective ways is all kinds of lotteries. In your own store, you can install an advertising stand where you play (TV, computer, and so on). Participants only need to leave their details, for example, in special questionnaires in front of the cash register. Usually they always have a point where his consent is required that he agrees to receive marketing information.

Also, an effective database collection can be done, for example, by drawing a machine. The machine being played is installed, and all that is required is that it possibly become yours to enter your data into the questionnaire located nearby and throw it into the car itself through a slightly lowered glass. So, one way or another, the customer‚Äôs customer base has been received, they agree to receive mailings, and this is already a lot!

The next step is to find out how many interested buyers you have and what they want to see on the shelves of your store. You can send an email questionnaire and promise a reward. Or, for example, a special e-mail is made, in which the seller groups the products into categories. Categories are usually links for which the system collects statistics, how many people viewed the letter, how many people went to look at a particular product, how many times they returned, how many were unsubscribed, and so on. After analyzing the information received, you can safely use it to develop a future strategy!

The big difference is how such things are treated in the west and both in Russia and the CIS countries. In the West, it will be called "he makes a marketing ploy", and in Russia - "engaged in spamming."

For example, to congratulate you on the new year, you can create a special sheet, fill in your friends or co-workers and send them greetings.

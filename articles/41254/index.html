The world's first auto-peg camera

Geotate and DXG Technology Corp. announced the world's first video camera that allows you to automatically link the video to the terrain.

The novelty is based on the DXG DVH586 platform and differs from the progenitor by the built-in module of the automatic determination of coordinates. The module allows you to automatically add information about latitude, longitude, altitude, and most importantly, the name of the area where the survey was taken. In this case, the device is focused primarily on users of YouTube. To download the footage movie to the server, neither a computer, nor even a phone is needed - the camera can handle this very well.



It must be admitted that automatic snapping to the terrain is very convenient. For example, on vacation, an incredible video about the beach in the Maldives was made, and catly friends say that a quarry accidentally hit the frame not far from your summer house. The latitude and longitude casually demonstrated to the spiteful critics will prove you right.

Well, seriously, apart from tourists, the device is obviously useful to climbers, yachtsmen, scientists and people in general, for whom traveling is more a job than an entertainment.

UPD: A GPS camera will be on sale until the end of October, and it will cost about $ 230.

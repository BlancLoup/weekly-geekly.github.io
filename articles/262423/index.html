Simulation and analysis of computational processes

Turing, Post, Minsky machines, Markov algorithms, Kleene recursive functions were invented in the first half of the twentieth century as a result of attempts to formalize the concept of an algorithm. These mathematical models are still successfully used to solve problems of solvability and algorithmic complexity, but are useless for modeling the behavior of network protocols or components of the operating system. The report presents some modern approaches to modeling computations that are used in the industry in the development of complex information systems.

What awaits Belarusian providers in the future?

Belarusian Providing - the eternal battlefield. Providers come up with new tariffs, use tricks like "unlimited tariffs *" with a bunch of conditions in small print at the bottom of contracts. Users swear, blame the much cheaper Internet in Moscow, Kiev or Vilnius, they fiercely dislike the state monopolist, commercial provider structures and want to download-download-download. Employees of various departments periodically "merge" information, journalists write pompous texts, I traditionally try to understand this tangle.


A new round of talk on this topic began after Beltelecom expanded the external channel to 12 Gbit / s.

The external gateway of the national telecom operator RUE Beltelecom has expanded to 12 Gbit / s for the second time this year. Additional speed is provided by the expansion of the channel in the direction of Russia. At the moment, the gateway in the direction of Russia provides 10 of 12 Gbit / s.

Users who are not very well aware of the situation with the provision in the country began to hope for lower tariffs and an increase in speed of both Beltelecom and private providers. Hope was unfounded - because at the moment the external channel is not too loaded. For example, private providers buy a share of approximately 2 Gbps from Beltelecom. And even the largest service provider in the country - the same Beltelecom - does not eventually load the gateway at 100%.

Commercial providers do not provide high speeds and cheap anlim due to the fact that their external channels can not afford it. On average, a guaranteed bandwidth of 1 Mbit / s costs about $ 1000, and this is a consequence of high prices for traffic and, as a result, a small (what is 2 Gbit / s across the country) demand for access to the gateway.

In the discussion of the material from which the quotation above was given, two versions were born:

1) Commercial providers do nothing useful, they only earn money through mediation, like shuttle traders selling clothes on the market.

2) Beltelecom's core network pays off in less than a year at current rates, which means that after a payback period, the monopolist could lower prices, thereby contributing to the penetration of broadband access in the country.

Here we get to an interesting aspect. In Belarus, the state is following the development of telecommunications.

According to the plans of the Council of Ministers, brought to the Ministry of Communications and Information, nearly two million subscribers must be connected to broadband data networks in order to successfully implement the state strategy by 2010. At the same time, the main operator of RUE Beltelecom has undertaken to bring its own subscriber base to just a million, thereby providing a plan to private providers. The latter, due to the inflexible policy of the monopolist, actually have no means for further development. .

That is, providers will have to connect a million subscribers. Mostly Minsk subscribers. About 2 million people live in Minsk, this is where the main interests of private providers lie. In the regions, the activity of private providers is minimal, there is dominated by a monopolist who was able to build an unprofitable backbone network. The reason is that now the provider business is low profitable. Investing in the construction of reference networks in the republic now seems hardly possible, since The market situation can at any moment kill small providers and put large ones on the verge of closure.

Over the past two years, Beltelecom has reduced prices for end users by 81% (tariffed traffic) and 91% (unlimited tariffs). For providers, prices have dropped over this period by 43.38%.

11 free cyrillic fonts



1. Arimo



Designer: Steve Matteson is a passionate designer from the state of Colorado. For over 25 years, Matteson has been developing fonts, the largest projects have become designs for Android, Citrix, Microsoft, Nextel and Xbox.



Types: Regular, Regular Italic, Bold, Bold Italic
License: Apache License, Version 2.0
Download Font

Arimo is a sans serif designed specifically to work with Arial. The font is slightly compressed in width. With a small size of the letters thin and classic. Graceful features are achieved by increasing the size of characters. The font is adapted for reading on the screen and is perfect for documents on different platforms.


2. PT Sans



Designer: Designers Alexandra Korolkova, Olga Umpeleva and Vladimir Efimov.



Types: Regular, Regular Italic, Bold, Bold Italic
License: Open Font License
Download Font

The development of PT Sans is part of a project dedicated to the 300th anniversary of the reform of the Russian alphabet. The font has basic outlines and classic proportions. It is distributed free of charge, but may be included in commercial projects. PT Sans is intended for official and business correspondence, regulatory and technical documentation, serves print publications and information resources. Looks good in print and electronic versions.

3. Vollkorn



Designer: Friedrich Altauzen - German graphic designer.



Types: Regular, Regular Italic, Semi-Bold, Semi-Bold Italic, Bold, Bold Italic, Black, Black Italic
License: Open Font License
Download Font

Vollkorn has large, bar serifs without rounding. The font changes its shape depending on the size, but it always looks noticeable. It is convenient to use it both in the body of the text, and for the selection of individual fragments or headings. As the first project of the designer Friedrich Altauzen, the font is intended for daily writing and has an open license.

4. Noto Sans



Designer: Google



Types: Regular, Regular Italic, Bold, Bold Italic
License: Apache License, Version 2.0
Download Font

Google developed Noto as a universal family, which is designed to save the Internet from unreadable characters - small rectangles that everyone met. At the moment, the font supports Latin, Cyrillic and Greek characters and covers more than 30 scripts.
The font comes from the Droid, which we wrote about earlier , has no serifs and is visually compatible with the height and thickness of the strokes with different languages. Great for use on the Internet.

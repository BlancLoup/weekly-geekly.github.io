Is IT infrastructure a black Pandora's box?



Everyone knows that there are such people in the IT field - Administrators. But not everyone thinks about who they are, what they are, and most importantly - where they come from. In fact, administrators call a variety of specialties related to the management of very different systems - from a web server and Internet cafes to Internet providers and corporate IT infrastructures. Under the cut, it‚Äôs about the administrators of the IT infrastructures of enterprises and where they come from ...

For a start, let's deal with the concept of IT infrastructure of the enterprise. Considering that all information technologies (hereinafter referred to as IT) in an enterprise are designed exclusively for automating the actions of the employees of the enterprise, it is not difficult to define the content of this term: the IT infrastructure of an enterprise is a combination of hardware and software that allows all functions used effectively Enterprise IT systems. By IT systems we mean servers and workstations, operating systems and software of computers, network devices and communications connecting them, acquired IT services from third-party providers and all the elements of control, monitoring and control of all of the above. Those. all that is necessary so that every PC user in an enterprise can perform the tasks assigned to it.



In order for a personal computer (hereinafter the PC) of an employee of an enterprise to work effectively with, for example, a warehouse program, it is necessary that the computer not only installs the appropriate software, but also that the computer itself meets many requirements - clearly defined access for specific users, the ability to run programs and access to information under strict control, protection against malicious programs (viruses, Trojans, spyware, worms, etc.), regular backup copies of the configuration and the user human data, etc. etc. ... To perform each of these tasks, an appropriate system is deployed in the enterprise, using multiple servers and network devices, based on operating systems (hereinafter referred to as OS) and software (hereinafter referred to as software). Yes, and the warehouse program itself is usually just a client of the system, which includes the server, the database (hereinafter the database), the database management system (DBMS) and many other functional elements.

Well, if you try to imagine that all of the above should perform uninterrupted work, for which the enterprise deploys entire monitoring and monitoring systems, as well as creating archival copies of configurations and data, then it becomes clear that the IT infrastructure administrator is an expert with considerable stock of knowledge and skills. Moreover, the IT infrastructure of an enterprise is often served by a whole team of specialists, in which each Administrator performs only a part of the tasks of servicing those or other systems, working closely with other administrators, technical support specialists, system architects and users.

Of course, the IT infrastructure of enterprises in terms of their size and complexity often correspond to the size of the enterprises themselves. While in many small companies, it is not uncommon for a visiting administrator or services of a third-party company, in large enterprises, hundreds or even thousands of professionals can serve the IT infrastructure, many of whom work in narrow, but related to other, competency profiles. However, regardless of the size and complexity of the IT infrastructure, its main administrators always face similar tasks, differing only in scale.

Where do IT infrastructure administrators come from?


Unfortunately, in the overwhelming majority of cases, enthusiastic professionals who independently study technologies and products are still becoming administrators of IT infrastructures. Most often, without interrupting production, moreover, not infrequently, through trial and error ... This is not at all programming, which is now taught in many educational institutions.

Today in the world there is practically no niche of educational institutions, purposefully training IT infrastructure specialists. At best, universities, commercial training centers (hereinafter referred to as TC) and representative offices of vendors such as Microsoft and Cisco manage to implement a program of commercial courses in the curriculum for students of IT-related faculties. But this practice is still rare.

The main training tool for such specialists for a long time has been, and still remains, certified courses from vendors such as Microsoft and Cisco in commercial training centers. For a full-fledged training of an IT infrastructure specialist, it is required to master the contents of at least five or even seven courses, the duration of each 5 days and the total cost amounts to tens of thousands of hryvnias. Moreover, each school day is at least 8 hours of intensive theoretical and practical training. In this case, usually, by the time students start learning, the listener should already have in mind all the technical basis of the technological foundations.

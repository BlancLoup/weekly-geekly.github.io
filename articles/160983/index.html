PengPod launches dualboot Linux / Android tablets in January



The PengPod company plans to start shipping 7 and 10-inch tablets with support for both Linux and Android in January. The company, founded by Neal Peacock (Neal Peacock) still managed to get more than 50 thousand dollars to develop a "tablet" Linux-distribution, with full support for all devices. It was planned to collect 49 thousand dollars, but it turned out more.

The tablets of this company were called PengPod 700 and PengPod 1000 (7 and 10 inches, respectively). They are based on Allwinner A10 ARM Cortex-A8 processors, a platform that is well suited for Ubuntu, Fedora or other Linux distributions. In general, some developers have been sawing Linux for a long time, trying to optimize some distributions for tablets. But without a development center, all these attempts are not brought to the end.

How to measure radiation

At once I will say that the DORA device is indicative, soldered on the basis of the SBM-21, catches only gamma rays. A typical background radiation level corresponds to 1-2 caught gamma quanta per minute. Translated into the language of physicists, this is about 0.05 micro-sievert per hour. For those who do not like sievert, you can go to x-rays, multiplying the number of sieverts by about 100. By the way, Roentgen was German and Sievert was a Swede.
And the SBM-21 device was made in Mordovia.

It generates up to 6 parasitic pulses per minute, so it‚Äôs pointless to talk about any accuracy. With a low background radiation level, of course. High values ‚Äã‚Äãare caught very well, for example, a compass from a German submarine gives 1000 pulses per minute, which corresponds to 60 micro sievert per hour. To get these numbers, you must put the phone with DORA in centimeter proximity to the compass. At a distance of an outstretched hand from a fascist device, increased radiation is not observed. 10 pulses per minute. For comparison, the granite monument under the Bronze Horseman showed 12-14 pulses. But more on that below.

DORA hardware

A little about the hardware. DORA is powered by the iPhone and additionally consumes 0.03% of the phone's battery power. To discharge the iPhone's battery with our device, we need 3,000 hours of continuous DORA operation.
Every 4 seconds, it sends a sinusoidal signal, which stores 60 bytes of information about the serial number, software version and protocol type, the level of accumulated radiation, the accumulation time. Bits are encoded by frequency shift keying. The wave of the sine wave for the period is 0 (zero). A pair of waves is 1 (unit). All this has long been standard. Bits are added bytes - bytes in strings and numbers.
With a frequency of 44100 (iOS specification), DORA manages to transmit all information in 1.5 seconds. Then, another 2 seconds are needed to charge the capacitors, they need to gain strength to send the next 60 bytes. Based on these figures, you can calculate the number of audio samples per 1 sine wave period and make your own protocol.

Pearl from the flea market or reverse engineering of the DCS-5220A1 IP camera

The summer ended well, which means it is time to leave their homes, native summer cooperatives and flocks to go to the city. Everything would be fine, but somehow it is scary to leave farming for as many as six months unattended. I asked myself a similar question. As a result, I decided to equip the site with a simple set of a webcam and Internet access (most likely 3G). He started, as usual, from a small one - with a search for a suitable budget camera. My budget was about $ 20.


Something normal could not get into this amount, in principle, so there were options like ‚ÄúDIR-320 + openwrt + UVC camera from a laptop‚Äù sealed in a sealed plastic bag and suspended from wood. But one evening I was browsing through ads of the local flea market and came across the sale of the D-link DCS-5220A1 IP camera, and also the PTZ function for some funny $ 10. For those who do not know, give an excerpt from Wikipedia
Pan-tilt-zoom-camera (PTZ-camera) - a camera that supports remote control of the direction and zoom. PTZ is an abbreviation of pan, tilt and zoom (pan, tilt, zoom), which describes how to control the camera.
Yes, alert. But the toad overpowered, and self-confidence echoed her (‚Äúreally, we won‚Äôt figure it out ?!‚Äù). The camera turned out to be old, but with an excellent light-sensitive sensor (just what is needed to observe the situation in the evening). Solved and the camera on my desk.



First [+]:

  1. Elegant night sensitivity (without any infrared illumination) - 0.5 Lux @ f1.4
  2. PTZ (although the zoom is only digital)
  3. WiFi is implemented by miniPCI RT2560F card. Aliexpress can already find 802.11n (for example AR9160 , replace and drive the video stream in maximum quality (the camera supports mpeg4 and H.263)
  4. Supports RTSP, HTTP, etc.
  5. UPnP support
  6. Gold was not spared a fee, the layer on all contacts is capital.
  7. On board your web server. And the system is based on linux, because even some source codes are uploaded. However, initially it was not possible to get through to either SSH or telnet.

Then noticed [-]:

  1. The maximum resolution is 640x480. The camera, apparently hellish (immediately thought that generally connected by lvds, the connector is very similar.
  2. Difficulties with the primary connection. The camera connects to the computer _ only through a hub_ or a crossover cable. And no one knows what the IP address.

It should be immediately noted that on the Internet about my particular information model there was a scanty amount - a page on the d-link website and that's it. So I decided first of all to open the case, the benefit was kept on all three screws. By the way, the presence of a standard mount for a photo pole (1/4 ") on the bottom of the case seemed to me sweet. In general, after removing the cover, I saw the following picture:

Photos with the author's notes, it gives about the past to know the cell repair shop. All the time you make yourself solutions for the case of the arrival of a similar model :). Having a good look at the board, I collected everything back and decided to see how the camera would work with the native software (since I could download some huge packages from the d-link server).

Despite carefully studying the first Russian, then the English manual, I did not understand how to find the camera. I had to search the mezzanine for an old hub, connect a camera to it, install wireshark and listen to ethernet. Listening gave the ip-address "192.168.0.99". Having scored it in the manual settings of the d-link Network Camera Installation program, the camera picked up. To work with such a camera, another utility from d-link D-ViewCam is intended. It also had to enter the address manually, the camera showed up and I was even able to check the operation of the PTZ. The camera is really well controlled and goes right-left / up-down with the resolution of ‚Äúone gear of the gear gear‚Äù :). The truth is constantly clicking a little tiring.

Googling, I found the document " URL command for DCS-5220, DCS-2120 ", where it says that you can control the camera with the command: http: ///cgi-bin/camctrl.cgi? [move =] [& speedpan =] [& speedtilt =], where value = up / down / left / right / home + -5 - for a step of one gear of the drive gear.
In principle, everything seems to be supposed to be, but the worm of doubt did not give rest. unix is ‚Äã‚Äãon board, well, what a web interface. After looking at camera analogs and reading a forum.openwrt.org, a vulnerability was discovered that allows a web request to enable telnet in the camera. All that was needed for this was to write in the browser line.
http: // <camera address> /cgi/admin/telnetd.cgi?command=on

After finishing work with the camera, I strongly recommend turning off the telnet, this is done in a similar way, via a request
http: // <camera address> /cgi/admin/telnetd.cgi?command=off

To the note / reminder to everyone who dares to repeat such experiments - connect to the UART output via the level converter (I had it FTDI232).

Having carefully analyzed the console boot logs, it became approximately clear that the kernel is directly unpacked into RAM. Theoretically, the site can be found GPL firmware, if not for all, then at least for many d-link products. But it was for my model to download the latest firmware failed. Okay, I took the first one (* .pkg file) and fed it to binwalk.

Along the way, I found that the firmware for camera 5220 revision A1 and revision A2 are identical and completely interchangeable. But the firmware from revision B to cameras with revision A hardware is absolutely not suitable.

In general, theoretically, adjusting the firmware to your needs is not difficult. As well as add the necessary driver (when replacing a miniPCI card for example). You can flash the camera through the standard interface of the D-ViewCam program or the Installation Wizard (which I mentioned at the very beginning).

But there is another option. Upload the finished firmware (renamed flash.pkg in advance) to the internal FTP of the camera (the FTP server must start from the telnet server, but for security reasons, the telnet is disabled by default and requires forced launch with a special request). Perhaps nothing will come of it - not enough time to check. All that is is the process log of the firmware from the standard application, taken using wireshark:

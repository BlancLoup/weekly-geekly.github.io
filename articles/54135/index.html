Gota - office dishwasher concept



A regular dishwasher is such a hefty built-in "fool" for the whole family. However, Brazilian designer Ibsen Caldas ( Ibsen Caldas ) believes that the technique should be light and compact.
The Gota dishwasher designed by him for the American company Whirlpool resembles the appearance of a radio tape recorder and takes about the same place.

The concept is designed for six users, respectively, the capacity of Gota is 6 plates, 6 glasses and the corresponding number of knives and forks. The feature of the dishwasher is that it does not require connection to the water supply. Water is poured into a special pan, heated, partially in the form of steam is run through the dishes, and partially used for rinsing.

About PMI Ethics

Principles of respect
We get information about the norms and customs of others and avoid behaviors that may be considered disrespectful.

And I would say that here we see the intersection with the previous section, however, here it looks a little wider. Since we are talking primarily about large projects, which are most often implemented by an international team, here we add to the professional and industry standards also the national characteristics of each of the participants.

Often, what is a norm of behavior for us, for people living in a neighboring country, and sometimes a city - the same action can be an insult, even blood feud.

Yes, now it is actively smoothed at the state level, arguing that we live in a multicultural society and we must treat other people's blunders from the point of view of our perception more loyally if we see that a person is from outside or from another faith, culture or just environment. However, as professionals, we should not only be more loyal, but also try to exclude such factors from work. And if allowed - then take measures to resolve.

For example, if we see that an action is unpleasant for a representative of another nationality in a team, then we can ask colleagues to stop behaving this way. Also, how can we ask male colleagues to stop making jokes about girls. Well, at least in their presence.
We listen to the point of view of others and try to understand it.

How often do you encounter the fact that a colleague decided to act in a certain way and does not listen to objections? And how often does your leader, having borrowed an idea-fix, go to her like a bull to a red rag? I come across this with a certain periodicity. Yes, there are times when it is not critical. And there are - when it leads us into the abyss. At this moment it is important to remember that our opinion is not always the only correct one.

And often such ideas lead to conflict. And any conflict in the team badly affects the state of the team and performance.
We interact directly with those people with whom we are in conflict or with whose opinions we do not agree.

In order to explain this point, I would like to turn to conflict management and methods of conflict resolution.

And the best solution is to work together. Why is it important? Firstly, in this way we do not break the connection, because often people stop communicating after a conflict and this leads to loss of communication in the team. Secondly, often, when everyone defends his point of view, both are a little mistaken. A bit - because I often see that the solutions proposed by the parties are similar. Simply - not to the end. And everyone sees in a separate trifle an excuse for abuse and discussion, that "here he has everything completely failed." And when working together, they will see the problems of each other, realizing that otherwise they agree. And most likely they will find the perfect or closest solution. And yes, teamwork brings together. You did not notice that conflicts often have enough productive team members, and not the leader and joon, for example? Thanks to teamwork, people restore communications, and sometimes they simply create them, instead of creating a negative for the rest of the time.

Another solution is a compromise. It is worse than the collaboration. It is worse, because here usually every member of the conflict loses something. And it hurts him. Yes, the conflict is closed, but communications will become complicated.

Principles of Justice
We demonstrate transparency in decision making.

People are such interesting characters who constantly think that there is some kind of conspiracy, or that they are underestimated. The more decisions you make without giving reasons to others, the more often you are considered a tyrant or a despot. This does not mean that it is necessary to discuss your decision with others (although it can be useful), but it means that you need to explain it and communicate it to others around you openly.

It is beneficial to convey the whole chain of reasoning when making a decision, because thanks to this we perceive the information more similarly. This shows your unity with the team and helps the team to behave correctly, understanding the task itself better, motivating it to do the right thing.

And yet - it just removes many questions from each separately.

Computer Telephony "Yeastar" for receiving / transmitting faxes

One of our clients decided to make improvements to the current work of the enterprise by replacing the analog PBX with the IP PBX OkTell, since the analog PBX did not give them the functionality and flexibility required for the operation of the enterprise. The initial list of requirements for IP PBXs included: the transfer of all internal telephony to modern VoIP technology; voice menu; intelligent flexible routing of incoming and outgoing calls; refusal to use telephone sets at workplaces - employees must use a headset to communicate with customers; and refusal to use the hardware fax machine - the function of receiving and sending fax messages is assigned to the fax server module built into OkTell. Analog PBX connection to the public telephone network was carried out by analog lines, which was planned to be kept.

To convert an analog signal from FXO lines to VoIP, the telephony card "Yeastar TDM 800" was chosen. Unlike a number of other computer telephony cards, Yeastar TDM family cards do not support hardware processing of fax messages, like, say, Synway or Alder boards, where fax messages are processed by hardware. At the same time, the cost of the boards of the Yeastar family is significantly lower than similar models with fax support, which was the reason for choosing this board.

Since when using voice gateways or computer telephony cards without integrated fax support, the entire fax processing process falls on the shoulders of the OkTell complex, preliminary testing was necessary. Our company's specialists tested the compatibility of the OkTell plus Yeastar complex for sending / receiving fax messages using the fax server built into the OkTell complex. Bench tests passed successfully.

Installation and setup of the system took 1 day.

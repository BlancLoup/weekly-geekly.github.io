TNS: electronic money is inferior to payment terminals and bank cards

73% of respondents use electronic money at least once a year, and 50% use it more often than once a month. The most active users are citizens from 25 to 34 years old, Kommersant writes .

The study involved 1066 economically active users of 20-44 years from cities with a population of 700 thousand people.

According to TNS, the most popular payment service in Russia is Yandex.Money: 44% of Internet users who use the survey use it. With a lag of just 1%, the service is followed by WebMoney. 36% of respondents use Qiwi, 35% use PayPal.

Users use payment terminals more often - 83% of those who answered at least once a year pay for purchases or services with their help. 80% answered that they use bank cards, and 76% - Internet banking.

Users from 20 to 24 years old most actively buying concert tickets and paying for online concerts with electronic money. Older respondents, 35-44 years old, are paying for electronic communications and utilities.

According to TNS, 11.8 million Russian Internet users access the network only from mobile devices . This is reflected in the popularity of online payments. According to Yandex.Money, the share of payments made from mobile devices in the total turnover increased by 50%.

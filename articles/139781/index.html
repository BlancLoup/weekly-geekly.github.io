SleepTracker. Myths and Reality



Raise raised, and forgot to wake up! This condition is familiar, perhaps, to every IT person, like many other creative people, whose working rhythm does not fit into the framework of the standard 8 hour working day.
For several years, the Internet has been replete with reviews and stories about the wonderful ‚Äúsmart alarm clocks‚Äù (SleepTracker and aXbo) that can solve the problem of lack of sleep during irregular working hours. After rereading a number of similar articles, I opted for SleepTracker Pro Elite Mens.
The purpose of this topic is to share practical experience of using this model, and the educational program on the phases of sleep is described in more detail in the articles, the links to which I will cite below.


Reasons for choosing SleepTracker Pro Elite Mens (1st left)


  • Awakening from vibration, which is much more humane compared to the typical loud, sharp sounds of alarm clocks. Alas, there is no wakeup mode in the Standard and aXbo models.
  • The alarm clock adjusts exactly to your sleep phases, unlike aXbo (for two, it is cheaper to buy one aXbo than two separate SleepTracker) is actually able to track only the sleep phases of one of the spouses, while someone will not get enough sleep.
  • The hand does not sweat as much as from the aXbo cuff, even in hot weather.
  • It is possible to save the time history of your sleep phases (via USB) on a computer.
  • SleepTracker Pro Elite Womens (center) - a completely similar model, but stylized for women.

The main differences between the Pro model and the Standard (1st right) are the presence of a wake-up vibro mode and a USB interface for exporting data. If the phase analysis of sleep is needed by no means everyone, then the mode is vibro and really worth it. However, at prices in the Russian Federation, the Pro version cost 6300 rubles, and the Standard 4500 rubles. You decide whether to overpay almost 2000r. so that the morning does not start with a headache from harsh sounds, and at the same time wake up the spouse too early.
If you need a smart alarm clock for two, then aXbo for the price will be cheaper ~ 11t.r. for two, but as I mentioned earlier, it will wake you up simultaneously, based on the sleep phases of one of you and with a signal.

So, the main question: HOW does the alarm clock determine the phases of sleep?
Answer: An accelerometer is built into the watch, which monitors the mobility of the hand in a dream and relates it to the sleep phase. Those. the movement of the hand in a dream defines an alarm clock as a phase of fast sleep. Nothing abstruse.

Final conclusions


  • A pretty good and simple way to work out your own sleep / wakefulness schedule, especially when you remember the cost of an alarm clock.
  • The need every time you go to sleep wearing a watch (for the day with such a clock is unlikely to be like) and press the "magic" button on the alarm clock is a bit tiring.
  • If you were overexcited the day before (work, movies, games, pleasant company, alcohol), then the alarm clock will not correctly detect the phases of your sleep and will simply work as late as possible what you have installed on it.
  • The need for analysis to daily merge data on the computer - an unpleasant moment, could make the cache and more.
  • Do not wait for instant results, read carefully the instructions that at least a month of permanent use can work out an optimal schedule for you.

So again: the alarm clock works , but not at all as many expect. For it only helps to make a more or less optimal sleep schedule for you, which you will eventually get used to yourself.

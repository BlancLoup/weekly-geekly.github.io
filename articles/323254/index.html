Backbone projects of telecommunications giants and maps of underwater communication channels



The amount of human-generated data is growing every year. Accordingly, the volume of traffic transmitted via Internet channels of the whole world is increasing. Most of the information is transmitted and received by telecommunication giants like Google, Microsoft, Facebook, Amazon. These companies are not enough current capacity of most communication channels, so they invest in the creation of new highways, often - undersea.

Since projects of this kind are quite complex and expensive, companies are merging. Actually, the reason for the partnership is often not even in money, but in the fact that a number of companies need a reliable channel for receiving and transmitting traffic to the same regions. The most recent example of such a partnership is a joint project of Facebook and Google. The companies initiated the creation of an underwater main line connecting Los Angeles and Hong Kong.

For Facebook, this is the second such project. Last May, a social network joined forces with Microsoft to create a highway connecting land stations in Virginia Beach, Virginia, USA, and Bilbao, Spain. The system is called MAREA, the operator is Telefonica, a Spanish telecommunications company.

As for the communication channel that connects Los Angeles and Hong Kong, this is one of the most important highways for the telecommunications sector, which provides the ability to transfer large amounts of data across the ocean. The channel capacity is 120 Tbps.

Telecommunications giants are laying undersea highways and together with companies from Asia. Examples of such projects include the New Cross Pacific Cable System (the main investors are Microsoft and a group of Asian companies) as well as the FASTER system, where Google was the initiator.

Last year, it began work as part of implementing its first backbone project and a division of Amazon, Amazon Web Services. As part of this project, called Hawaiki Submarine Cable, AWS decided to build a highway connecting points in the United States, Australia and New Zealand.

Telecommunications giants, realizing that technologies are developing very quickly, make their highways universal. The equipment here is unified, it can be replaced with a newer and more efficient. "This means the ability to upgrade equipment as

improving the production technology of fiber-optic elements and devices, without being tied to a specific type of ground station hardware, said a Facebook representative in 2016.

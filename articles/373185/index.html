RAW shooting: what is it and why?


With Hasselblad True Zoom, you can shoot not only in JPEG, but also in JPEG + RAW at the same time. If you have not heard about RAW, then briefly recall that this is the digitized data from the camera matrix with minimal processing, unlike JPEG, which the camera automatic based on the algorithms installed for it led to a specific view on its own.

The idea is that after shooting a photographer can open a RAW file in a graphic editor and get the opportunity to correct the flaws of optics and matrix, which are absolutely in any cameras and lenses, as widely as possible. Change white balance, correct distortion and chromatic aberrations, correct sharpness, ‚Äústretch‚Äù underexposed areas and ‚Äúmuffle‚Äù overexposed ones ‚Äî to do all this in RAW is much easier than in JPEG.



Hasselblad True Zoom saves RAW frames in DNG format. They are easily opened by the Camera RAW plugin for Adobe Photoshop, and you don‚Äôt even have to update it to the latest version. Despite the fact that mod saves two files at once (this, by the way, is very convenient), it does it pretty quickly: according to the subjective sensations, a second or a half.

The main feature is the distortion of optics: pay attention to the curved dark corners of frames. But in the same Camera RAW, it is corrected literally in a couple of mouse clicks. There is still a nuance with night photos: they have more noise than JPEG, but at the same time, higher detail.

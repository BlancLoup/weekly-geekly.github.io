Higher education in Germany has become free. For foreign students too

A great opportunity to study in Germany came from anyone from all over the world. Since the new academic year 2014/2015, higher education in Germany has become free throughout the country. The last of the seven federal states to abolish tuition fees was ordered by Lower Saxony in December 2013. Dorothea Stapelfeldt, senator of the Committee on Science in Hamburg, called a paid education "social injustice," writes the Times . The senator for science believes that the key policy objective is to provide young people in Germany with the opportunity to study and receive high-quality higher education for free.

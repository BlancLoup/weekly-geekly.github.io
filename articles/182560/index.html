IPv6 - one year later

June 6, 2012, the launch of IPv6 - the new version of IP protocol, which replaced the IPv4. Due to the rapid growth in the number of smartphones, tablets and other devices with the ability to connect to the Internet, there is a risk that the stock of available IP addresses will soon run out. IPv6 solves this problem, as well as some other security flaws.

Unfortunately, IPv4 and IPv6 are incompatible, so it became necessary to gradually transfer the Internet from one protocol version to another. There are different transition methods . The most common solution is to assign both IPv4 and IPv6 addresses until most of the Internet goes to IPv6.

Most operating systems, devices and Internet providers already support IPv6, but among users this technology is spreading very slowly.

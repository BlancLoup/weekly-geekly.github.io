Newsletter Layout FAQ

Since the publication of my first topic to this day, I regularly receive various questions about the layout of mailings. In this article I will answer them from my point of view, which is based on my personal experience and statistical data. If you disagree with me in something, I will be happy to hear your point in the comments.

You are a spammer! You will burn in hell! Repent! Why do you need it?


Dear user, the mailing list is not always - there is SPAM. Russian Wikipedia gives the following definition of spam:

Spam (eng. Spam) - mass mailing of commercial, political and other advertising or other type of messages (information) to persons who did not express the desire to receive them.

And despite the fact that, according to statistics, about 80% of mail traffic is spam, I work according to the scheme of ‚Äúfair‚Äù mailings. How does this happen:

Option number 1. Hello, you mail!


The picture is as follows. There is a customer - the owner of an online store, coupon network or any other service that is trying to either sell / regularly / inform the user about anything. What happens next? A html greeting letter is being prepared, which is sent to the email database with something like this:

Hello, blah blah blah, we are such and such, such and such offers. If you are interested in this information, please follow the link and confirm your desire to regularly receive our mailing list.

When sending such a greeting letter, I recommend sending the user's email with GET to the link that leads to the ‚Äúsubscription‚Äù. What happens next? Or we display the user a page with the text:

Thank you for subscribing to our newsletter! Further emails will be sent to% email_end through_GET%

You can even send GET not the email itself, but its hash, in order to prevent subscriber manipulation by substitution of soap into the browser string.

But there is a preferred option. It lies in the fact that when clicking on a link, the user sees a form in which the "email subscriber" field is already filled, and we give the user the right to change the value of this field. We also provide a choice in the form of checkboxes, which is what the user subscribes to, be it: news of the service, receipt of new products, publication of new interesting articles and other things. After all, a person may be interested in one thing; there is no need for him to send everything that you consider necessary.

There is one more extremely important field - the mailing format. This is an HTML and text version (plain / text). This item is the answer to the question ‚ÄúHTML mailing must die! Stop sending this crap! ‚Äù Give the user the right to choose and everyone is happy.

Option number 2. Do you agree?


This option involves obtaining consent to the newsletter, as in the first case, but instead of invitation letters, you offer the user to subscribe at the time of registration on your website / service. In this case, there is one very important BUT. Never, you hear!? Never use the following inscription !!! "By clicking on the" Register "button you confirm your desire to receive regular mailings from us."

At this point the issue of spam has been exhausted.

What is the best service for newsletters?


How many people, so many opinions. I can only bring my own.

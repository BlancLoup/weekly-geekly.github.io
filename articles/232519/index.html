Sensor Hug helps to track water consumption throughout the day



I think everyone has heard that an adult should drink about 2 liters of water per day to provide his body with water. If you drink less, and you feel worse, and your performance is lower, and you can even get sick if the amount of water in your body is regularly undersupply to the prescribed amount.

Nevertheless, even those of us who understand the importance of a full body supply of water, can not always follow the amount of fluid you drink during the day. This can now help the sensor Hug.

According to the developers, this device is worn on almost any bottle, thanks to a special mount, and allows you to track the level of fluid intake.



Inside the device there is an accelerometer that can cut off unnecessary movements (carrying the bottle, dropping it, etc.), and perceives only the moment when the bottle is brought to the mouth of a person. At the same time, a special algorithm calculates exactly how much water a person drank at a time.



Well, all the data over the wireless connection (Bluetooth LTE) is transmitted to the mobile device, to the proprietary application. The application, in turn, keeps a detailed account and analysis of the water consumed, and is able to calculate the water needs of an individual person, depending on the parameters of the body and activity.



The application, if a person forgot to drink in time, reminds about it.

The main functions of the application:



  • Calculation of the need for human water, depending on its weight, age, ambient temperature and physical activity;
  • Automatic analysis of data received from the sensor;
  • Compatibility with fitness applications Fitbit and RunKeeper;
  • Data visualization.


Device price



Pre-order can be done from the manufacturer , starting with 59 dollars. Shipment of the first batch will occur in March 2015.

Optimize FireFox and Google Reader for Asus EEE

I like to use Google Reader and FF, but with the standard settings, too much space goes under the interface elements, so that under the text itself on my EEE901 there is very little space left. How I struggled with this:

1) Having installed the Tiny Menu plugin for FF, I reduced the menu to one item, and by placing a panel of frequently used bookmarks next to it, I freed about 25 pixels.

2) Next, I installed the Chromin Frame plugin, it transfers tabs to the title bar - this is still ~ 25 pixels

3) I installed GreaseMonkey, a Beter GReader script to it, which allows you to remove the entire interface, or leave any parts of it. Personally, I liked the opportunity to remove only the upper part (if anything, you can return it with the "w" key). It's about 160 pixels.

So I got rid of ~ 210 unnecessary interface lines without losing any functionality. As a result, the google reader in my FF looks like this:

SmartWatch 3 and SmartBand Talk

Two new devices will appear in the SmartWear wearable electronics lineup. The new version of "smart" watches from Sony is now running the operating system Android Wear, not so long ago presented by Google. The watch has a built-in microphone, gyroscope, accelerometer, compass and GPS module, as well as protected from water and dust in accordance with the standard IP68. The updated 1.6-inch display has a resolution of 320x320 pixels, and 4 GB of internal memory allows you to use the watch autonomously, for example - in the morning run. The Lifelog app will track your moves even if you leave your smartphone at home. You can display any data on the clock screen, whether it is a weather forecast, the latest news or messages from friends, and you do not need to enter any data manually - Android Wear perfectly understands voice commands.



The SmartBand lifelog bracelet also did not stay aside from the updates - it received a curved e-ink display, a built-in accelerometer and altimeter, a microphone and a speaker. The display can be used to quickly access Lifelog statistics or display a clock on it. The microphone allows you to use voice commands to control your smartphone, and the sensors provide the most accurate collection of data on physical activity, distance traveled and altitude reached (if you are, for example, keen on mountaineering). Among other things, SmartBand now has a higher class of protection - IP68, which is very important for lovers of an active lifestyle.

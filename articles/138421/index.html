Humble Bundle Mojam: creating a game in live broadcast



Mojang, the creators of Minecraft, led by Notch, launched today a new Humble Indie Bundle, which is fundamentally different from previous ones. The fact is that this time they do not sell ready-made games, and do not receive money for it, but create it over the weekend right in front of the public. Moreover, the genre and style is determined by voting, and the proceeds from the game go entirely to the needs of charity (EFF, Red Cross, the project: water and the foundation of Child's Play, which organizes leisure activities for children in hospitals).

Even with a vote, they decided to go their own way, and for both the sayings (by genre and style) the winner would be a combination of the points with the most and least votes. At the moment, the voting has already been closed (and Night is already scribbling the code in front of webcams and with a screencast) and it looks like the game will be a real-time strategy-shooter in the style of steampunk / ancient Egypt (!!!).

Watch the creation process (and it is already in full swing), and you can also buy this game on the humble page: www.humblebundle.com

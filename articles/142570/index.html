# BADA55-colors for web design

I spent a few years studying computer architecture, and if I learned one thing hard, it was the love of computer designers for the elegant names used during initialization or other work with memory. I spent the long hours of my life inventing funny-looking memory addresses for homework or testing. Of these names, 0xDEADBEEF is best known, and you can see a list of some others over there and there . It can even be assumed that such names (especially with symbol substitution: for example, ‚Äú1‚Äù instead of ‚Äúi‚Äù or ‚Äú5‚Äù instead of ‚Äús‚Äù) became the forerunners of pseudo-cool Internet jargon known as leetspeak .

All this reminded me of a recent article about web design (sorry, I don't remember it exactly). The author of the article gave an example of CSS code, and the color chosen by him for the example was # BADA55. It turned out that it gives a somewhat ugly green color, but still it prompted me to think about what other English words can be made with hexadecimal color codes.

Sketching a short PHP-script, we get a rather extensive list for the selection of such colors.

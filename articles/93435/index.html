Studying at the University of the USA


In the previous article I wrote about how they enter American universities, now I‚Äôll tell you about the study itself. Education is very different from the methods of Russian universities. I want to talk about how to choose subjects and teachers, take tests and sometimes write off.



Item selection


After admission, the student is given a list of items that need to go to get a specialty.
This list contains both compulsory and optional items. The choice is limited to a given subject area, and the student himself will decide that he is more interested.
Each item includes several levels of difficulty - this is the first figure in the designation of the subject. For example, Computer Science, CS110 and CS 213. 110 is an introductory course (most often for those who have not decided who he wants to become) and CS213 is more complicated, already for those who have chosen this specialty as a profession. I hasten to note that the course number for the subject in different universities is likely to be different. When transferring from one university to another, they look at special correspondence lists and at the very name of the subject, and not just at its number.

During 4 years (usually so much time the average student needs to complete a bachelor‚Äôs degree) a student goes through different levels of compulsory subjects, starting with the 100th (simple) level and ending with a level of 400-500. As for selective subjects, they are designated in the curriculum as a group.
For example, for the bachelor of Computer Science you need to pass 2 subjects from the group ‚ÄúBehavioral Science‚Äù . The group includes psychology, sociology, philosophy, etc. And here freedom of choice. Do not like philosophy? Take two classes in psychology.

There are catalogs of items, most often in electronic form on the internal website of the university. Naturally, there are dependencies ( "prerequisites" ) of objects, i.e. before rushing to Computer Science 354, you have to go through CS254, MATH218 (mat), PHYS338 (physics). In this case, it is necessary to obtain an estimate for each subject not lower than C, which is equivalent to a triple with a minus.
Registration for classes takes place naturally electronically.



Credit system


Each item has a certain amount of credits. They are given by the number of hours / lectures (laboratory) per week. For example, 3 credits mean that the subject lasts three lecture hours. Such subjects as biology, chemistry, physics most often consist of three lecture hours = 3 credits and 3 laboratory hours = 1 credit. Appraisal is set for the item: 70-75% of the lecture part and 25-30% for the lab. If a student only collapses the lab and goes through the lecture part, the subject is still littered and must be sorted out. In such cases, universities sometimes divide labs and lectures into two separate classes, BUT, they must always be registered and passed within one semester. Here the difference is only in the assessment, it is calculated and set separately from the lecture.

The benefits of selecting items


The student can make his own schedule and select all the items either on Wednesdays and Fridays, or on Tuesdays and Thursdays, or as he wants. Often items are duplicated in the evenings, it is very convenient if you need to work in the morning.
Another big plus is the choice of a teacher. The easier the subject is, the more teachers lead it at different times.
There is a website www.ratemyprofessor.com , where students write teacher reviews. Naturally, like many others, I first looked in there and chose teachers. Very useful resource!

Profile universities


Studying in professional universities is very different. There is no longer possible to choose subjects and their number per semester. Here everything is chosen for you and the obstruction of one subject per semester leaves you for the second year since they are not repeated. In our university, each subject during the semester consists of a lecture, and 2-5 (approximately) tests (controls). If you add up the number of subjects (6-7 per semester) and the number of tests, it turns out that we take 2-3 tests per week. Skip the test or transfer to another time only with a certificate from the doctor.

DJI and Hasselblad presented the first joint platform for professional aerial photography.

In October 2015, it was announced the beginning of a strategic cooperation between the world leader in the production of multikopter, the Chinese DJI concern and the cult photographic brand Hasselblad, in which DJI almost immediately bought a minority stake from Hasselblad. And now, after almost a year of informational calm, the company's press services announced the creation of a first-of-its-kind joint, fully integrated flying high-end aerial platform based on the previously announced DJI MATRICE 600 drone ( DJI M600 ) and the Hasselblad A5D medium format camera.



The combination of the M600 and A5D offers users an impressive combination of optics, sensors and an advanced platform for aerial photography, as the M600 is capable of lifting heavy filming equipment into the air. The device supports video LightBridge 2, with which you can transfer content in the resolution FullHD (1080p) with a frequency of 60 frames per second at a distance of 5 kilometers.



The DJI M600 is fully compatible with the professional stabilization suspensions Ronin-MX.

Unblock Hackathon Registration Open April 6-8



Hi, Habr!

We have opened the registration for the 48-hour Unblock blockchain-hackathon and are pleased to invite developers and analysts to participate. The skill level is not important, the hackathon is open, participation is free. The desire to plunge into blockchain development is welcome.

Hackathon is carried out by the Binary District and the Blockchain Institute R & D Lab with the assistance of the Waves Platform, Ergo and EncryptoTel, which have prepared difficult tasks for the participants. Tasks are based on cases from the practice of these companies and require non-trivial solutions. Teams will be assisted by mentors - experienced blockchain developers, and judged by a jury composed of Dmitry Meshkov , founder of Ergo Platform, Sergey Tolmachev , Scala Backend Blockchain Developer at Waves Platform, and Vasily Kharin , a researcher at Blockchain Institute. The jury chairman is Oksana Klimenko , head of the Moscow R & D Lab Blockchain Institute.

Why to participate in Unblock Hackathon:

  • An impressive prize pool is provided for the winners (last year it was $ 15,000)

  • The brightest and most talented will have the opportunity to receive an offer of cooperation. At the end of last year, six winners received an invitation to work from partner companies.

  • One of the prizes is 5 places on the 9-week course of Blockchain Developer , where leading blockchain developers of the industry lecture: Dmitry Meshkov (Ergo), Sergey Tolmachyov (Waves Platform), Evgeny Alekseev (CryptoPro), Aler Denisov (SoundChain). Special guests also spoke at previous courses: Zuko Wilcox, ZCash CEO, and one of Ethereum key developers, Vlad Zamfir. Hackathon starts April 6 in Moscow at the Digital October Center. You can participate both independently and with your team. If you sign up individually, the organizers will determine you to the team depending on the technology stack. On April 8, participants will present the prepared solutions and the winners will be awarded.

See you at the hackathon!

Cross-platform LIME

How many controversies have been about Lyme's cross-platform . How many people offered, convinced and argued that Lime should be cross-platform.

Convinced.

But not really. For cross-platform, it will be necessary to bring our program to some kind of "average" form, from which it will lose terribly, and users of all platforms will not be satisfied.

The minus of any cross-platform (albeit the only one) is the inevitable performance degradation (it can be both significant and not very much depending on the quality of the code), as well as a violation, let's say, of interface agreements when transferring from the main OS to another.

We do not want this. Main quality. We need something to work as we want. It is like a website in different browsers. In FF one, in IE another.

To stop these disputes, we inform - Lime for Unix will be . But as an independent and separately developed application.

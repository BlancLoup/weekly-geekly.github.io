How much does Pandora pay to musicians?



The fact that music streaming services like Pandora have been paid to musicians (or recording studios) for using their songs has been known for a long time - according to this principle, these services work. The musician consents to the use of his music, the service places the song, users listen to it, and for each listening the musician (or studio) receives money.

The other day, Pandora founder Tim Westergren (Tim Westergren) said exactly how much musicians get for each ‚Äúscrolling‚Äù of their music by users. It turns out that the amount is very, very small .

Payment is only 0.0014 US dollars for each audition. This is about 80% less than Spotify, a service that pays musicians amounts from 0.006-0.0084 US dollars for each audition.

Even more interesting is the fact that Pandora is now fighting SoundExchange for the right to reduce payments up to $ 0.0011. The results of this dispute will be known later - at the end of this year.

Despite such payouts, the total amount that Pandora pays to studios and musicians is quite large: in 2014, $ 446.4 million was paid. This is about half of the company's total revenue, which amounted to $ 920 million.

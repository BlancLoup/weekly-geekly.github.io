NASA has no money for landing people on Mars


Mars is so close and so far.

Mars is a very attractive target for humanity. With a stretch, it can be called an Earth-like planet, and many scientists and businessmen believe that even if they make an outpost of humanity in the solar system, it is on Mars. NASA is working on a similar goal. For the past few years, the agency has been actively talking about exactly how Mars is going to reach and why it is needed. Initially, the plan included a flyby of the moon, transportation to the natural satellite of the Earth asteroid, and the creation of a base for a flight to Mars. Over time, some points (including the same transportation of the asteroid) had to be removed, but overall, the plan looked quite viable. Agency representatives tirelessly showed slides, telling about the different stages of the implementation of their program. Well, now it turns out that NASA simply does not have the money for all this.

According to the plan, flights to Mars were to take place as early as 2030. To this end, in particular, is developing a heavy booster SLS . In 2040, as stated by representatives of the agency, the Red Planet should already be conquered. By the way, some NASA staff members fly to the moon instead of Mars, they say, and much closer, and the colony is easier and cheaper to organize. But all the others called for the creation of an outpost of mankind on Mars.

The other day at one of the meetings held at the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics, one of the leaders of NASA admitted that there is no money for landing a man on the Red Planet. Specially or accidentally, but William Gerstenmeyer said that the financial support received by the agency now is not enough. "I can not say when people get to Mars, the reason for this is lack of funding, the budget is increased roughly 2%, we do not have landing systems and take-off for Mars," Gerstenmeyer said, answering the question when people will fly to Red the planet. "At this stage, taking off and landing is a difficult challenge for us."

In other words, NASA is now spending a lot of money on SLS and Orion. The construction of these systems costs in huge amounts that continue to increase, going beyond the budget. That is why the agency has no money to create systems that allow to land on Mars and take off from its surface.

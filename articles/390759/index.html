A brief history of ZTE: why you should trust the Chinese gadgets

The Chinese company ZTE was founded in 1985, although even now it can hardly be called known in the Russian market. In 2011, it was the second largest manufacturer of telecommunications equipment and mobile phones in China. It is listed on the Shenzhen and Hong Kong Stock Exchanges, and is included in the Hang Seng China Enterprises Index.

In addition, ZTE has 13 research centers in China, USA, Sweden and Korea, which spend 10% of annual income, and is a partner of Intel, Microsoft, IBM and Qualcomm.

51.8% of ZTE shares belong to the PRC, and this means that the quality of products is monitored by the country's authorities.



In 1985, a group of investors together with the Ministry of Aerospace Industry of China established the company Zhongxing Semiconductor Co., Ltd. in Shenzhen, where the head office of the company still remains. In March 1993, the name was changed to Zhongxing New Telecommunications Equipment Co., Ltd ... ZTE received its current name later.

In 1997, it placed shares on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange. Seven years later, she entered the listing of the Hong Kong Stock Exchange.

The company received most of the profits in the domestic market, but in 2004 it received money from the placement of shares on R & D to enter the global market. In 2006, ZTE took 40% of all new orders for equipment for CDMA networks, leading the global market in this industry. Among the company's customers was the Canadian state-owned telecommunications company Telus, and ZTE itself entered the Wi-Fi Alliance .

In 2007, ZTE sold equipment to British Vodafone, Spanish Telefonica, and Australian Telstra. In 2008, ZTE customers were in 140 countries.

In 2009, ZTE came in third place in the list of vendors of GSM equipment, and 20% of all this equipment sold in the world had a company logo.

An interesting fact: the first smartphone with GPS / GLONASS on board was released by ZTE under the name of MTS Glonass 945 .



ZTE claims to spend 10% of its annual income on R & D. The company's research centers operate in several countries, and the company has more than 48 thousand patent applications and 13 thousand patents. In 2011 and 2012, ZTE received more patents than any other company. This company is one of the largest manufacturers of telecommunications equipment in the world, but at the same time it is also a manufacturer of smartphones.

In the 3rd quarter of 2015, according to Gartner, ZTE sold 13.7 million smartphones and ranked 9th in the global ranking of manufacturers, having seized 2.9% of the global market.

The company entered the Russian market about five years ago, but then they were branded mobile phones. And now the company offers a range of budget phones and one powerful ZTE Axon Mini. The fact is that a year ago Russia was not considered as a market where premium smartphones, flagships of the company, should be sold. Instead, they chose to sell state employees and middle ones.

For example, ZTE Blade V2 Lite. This smartphone, priced at about seven thousand rubles, has a five-inch TFT screen with a resolution of only 854x480, only 1 GB of RAM and 8 GB of built-in. But he boasts a 3400 mAh battery, two SIM cards and LTE support. And weighing 150 grams - you can defend against Gopnik. In terms of performance, Antutu benchmark placed it between the LG G3 and the HTC One.

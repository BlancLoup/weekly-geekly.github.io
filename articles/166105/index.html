CES Results: Samsung's New Cameras at the International Consumer Electronics Show

Good day, Habr!

Last week, the international consumer electronics exhibition CES 2013 was held - perhaps the most high-profile event in the year for all companies in one way or another connected with the production, assembly and development of technologies and software for consumer electronics in all its manifestations: from banal household appliances to hot innovations like flexible OLED displays and the latest mobile processors. Below we have collected all the most interesting news that Samsung presented for the four days of the exhibition, from January 8 to 11. Some products are already on sale, others have yet to find their place in the domestic market. Anyway, under the cut you will find a lot of interesting things.



Even before the launch of CES 2013, press releases began to roll in from the Samsung head office, announcing new products in the categories of Home Appliances and Cameras. The first is not very interesting for us, so let's take a closer look at the new Samsung cameras.



NX300
This is the new flagship model in the popular Samsung NX Compact System Camera (CSC) series. Along with it, Samsung also introduced the new NX 45mm F1.8 2D / 3D lens - the first 3D lens capable of capturing images and video in Full HD quality and in 3D.

At the heart of the NX300 is a new 20.3-megapixel APS CMOS sensor capable of capturing images in the smallest detail. The wide ISO range (ISO100-25600) further improves performance by allowing users to capture a perfectly balanced image even in dim light. The new hybrid autofocus provides fast and accurate detection of phase and contrast, while the high-speed shutter (the minimum available shutter speed is 1/6000 s) and the continuous shooting mode (9 frames / s) will not let a single frame through. The camera allows you to take pictures in Full HD quality at 60 frames per second.

New technology improved image DRIMe IV, developed by Samsung, is designed to improve the performance of shooting speed and image quality, also responsible for improved color reproduction, noise reduction in photos and support Full HD video (1080p) in 2D and 3D formats (in combination with the new 45 mm 2D / 3D Samsung lens). Video output directly to a wide screen via HDMI 1.4a port. By the way, it is this version of the standard that supports the transfer of 3D video.

NX300 is equipped with a 3.31-inch (84 mm) AMOLED-screen with a resolution of 768,000 pixels, a hybrid touch screen and a 5-way navigation key - all this should provide an intuitive and easy-to-use interface. The display has a folding design, it can be tilted up 90 degrees and down 30 degrees for ease of shooting over the head or from the hip.

The camera is equipped with the SMART mode already known from previous Samsung models, which allows you to select one of 14 different shooting options, such as Creative Snapshot, Landscape, Light Trail or Freeze Frame. All modes automatically adjust aperture and shutter speed to get the best results from shooting in various conditions. The NX300 also has a useful i-Depth function - allowing you to adjust the depth of the image using the i-Function system.

The NX300 carries a two-channel Wi-Fi module and a number of enhanced wireless capabilities under the general title SMART Camera 2.0. The NX300 Wi-Fi connection allows users to instantly and securely share their photos by sending them from the camera to a smartphone or tablet: the user only needs to download the free Samsung SMART Camera app (available for both Android and iOS devices) to his smartphone or tablet and Follow the simple instructions to create a secure connection with the camera.

NX300 is equipped with a practical feature AutoShare, which in the mode of hot duplication automatically sends all the captured photos to your smartphone for safe storage.

Another feature - Mobile Link, allows users to select and transfer images or whole albums of photos from the camera directly to the smartphone at any convenient time. The SMART Camera application also includes a remote viewfinder feature for the NX300.

For placing photo and video materials in one click on the camera body there is a hot button DIRECT LINK. In addition, images can be automatically backed up or stored in the cloud through the AllShare Play service ‚Äî always securely protected and easily accessible.

The NX300 will go on sale in the first quarter of 2013 at a starting price of $ 750. The cost of a 3D lens will be $ 500. Russian prices will be announced later. The camera is available in black or white.

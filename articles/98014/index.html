Making a dystopian / cyberpunk mask

Friday night and therefore, we offer you a concept-in-picture to create your own original mask.
For my own, I chose the style of the films Terminator and V for Vendetta . Manufacturing technology is quite famous, but for someone it can be a discovery - Papier-mache . It is good because it requires relatively little time (including all dryers - no more than 1 day) and allows you to use the materials at hand.
I apologize for the quality of the images, I have only a digital soap dish at my disposal.



1. In a graphic editor or on a sheet of paper, create a design layout for the future mask. As the source data, I used pictures found on the Internet.



2. What you need to make a mask (in the pictures there is no three-liter can, thread, plasticine and plastic bag):



3. On the three-liter glass jar we stretch the plastic bag, fasten it with a lid and thread. From plasticine we create a volumetric force mask. The tselofan package is necessary for painless removal of the plasticine "matrix" from the can.



4. Layer by layer with a brush, apply toilet paper. The first two or three layers use water as a binder, the next 6-8 words apply small amounts of PVA glue. The result directly depends on your accuracy.



5. Dry the mask for 5-8 hours in a weak stream of air at room temperature. After drying, we cut the package and the threads from the jar - the mask should be easily separated along with the plasticine matrix. Then gently separate the clay from our mask.



6. Scissors align the edges of the mask. We dry the inner side. Ground the surface with a mixture of PVA glue and crushed chalk. Dry again for 2-3 hours. Grind the surface with fine sandpaper. Getting to color.



7. To the result, you can add a few decorative elements with a second glue.

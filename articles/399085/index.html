General Motors and IBM have developed a multimedia system with artificial intelligence for cars.


The other day, General Motors and IBM have agreed to work together on a smart multimedia system for cars, which will be based on the IBM Watson cognitive platform. General Motors has its own OnStar multimedia system, which is compatible with IBM Watson, giving millions of GM car drivers the opportunity to use the smart system in their car. The novelty will be called OnStarGo.

According to the developers, as a result, both drivers and passengers will be able to feel safe in their car. The system will help to avoid traffic jams, which is especially important if you run out of fuel, show where you can drink coffee nearby, or tell about the latest news on the way to work.

To help the car owner, the system is provided with access to a huge database, in which almost everything that can be useful for a motorist is added - from parking locations to information about new traffic jams.

Several new services will be connected to OnStar Go, including ProactiveAlerts. They will help expand the functionality and capabilities of the digital motorist assistant. For example, the mentioned Proactive Alerts service will be able to warn the owner of the car about the discharge of the battery, even if the person is outside the car.

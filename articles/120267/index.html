Colorpen
Online training is widely used in refresher courses for working students over 25 years old, and almost never used in schools and universities. The problem is that many disciplines that are taught in schools and universities, involve practical training. Basically, it‚Äôs about the student writing something on the blackboard or notebook, and the teacher corrects it. This is not done with Skype, Webex and other programs for video conferencing.

Wifixer
The brothers Dmitry and Alexander Klimushiny and almost a dozen of their like-minded people create the WiFixer service, an alliance of wireless users. It should make wi-fi ubiquitous and affordable. If you have an access point, you can easily sell access to the Internet. And vice versa, if you need a wi-fi point, you can buy megabytes or online time from other union members.

Access is sought using a special program - it is posted on the WiFixer website. The cost of Internet access is determined in auction format. If there are other networks nearby, it is unlikely that the owner of the point will break the high price. WiFixer earns a percentage - from 25% to 5%, depending on the number of access points from one owner. +++

Wordsteps
The creators of WordSteps offer their own method of solving the problem of memorizing words when learning a new foreign language. The service allows you to select unfamiliar words from sites, documents, books, translate them and view received "cards" through the website or on any mobile platform.
The project site was launched in April last year, during its operation the number of registered users is growing very rapidly. This is not the first project of this kind, but it is distinguished from its rivals by the best on the market algorithms for learning and memorizing words, free functionality and a convenient user interface. +++

Bartermill

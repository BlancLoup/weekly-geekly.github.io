It's time to donate your stethoscope to the museum.

The American company Thinklabs Medical announced the launch of sales of its new flagship product, the electronic stethoscope Thinklabs One. The device has a familiar and convenient form for doctors and can work with any headphones. It can amplify the sound more than 100 times and has a number of sound filtering options to provide better listening for heart and diastolic noises, sounds in the lungs, and the like.



Thinklabs One can connect to any tablet or smartphone, which allows you to visualize audio noise using a specialized application that can also record data or magnify individual recording fragments. In addition, the application with the funny name for Russian rumor iMurmur contains a library of previously recorded cardiac noise, which can be used to study or even compare with patient data.


The stethoscope is supplied immediately with miniature headphones, and for more demanding doctors the manufacturer offers exclusive Dr. earphones . Dre's Executive with integrated noise suppression technology.

Interestingly, despite the emergence of new ultrasound compact devices for listening to patients, doctors still prefer to work with the good old stethoscope. Perhaps the Thinklabs device, which combines the latest technology and familiar design, will find a wider application, and finally make therapists change their conservative habits.



The electronic stethoscope works on a Li-ion battery, allowing you to listen to 100-125 patients without recharging.

Paymentwall is an alternative to iTunes and Google Play - Paymentwall Mobile

Press release
July 25, 2012

[SAN FRANCISCO]
Today, Paymentwall launches the first globally accessible payment solution on HTML5, which allows users from anywhere in the world to pay without any problems in applications integrated with Paymentwall Mobile.


The launch of Paymentwall Mobile for the first time will allow more than two billion users to pay for virtual goods, applications, and digital content using local payment options on a smartphone or tablet.

iTunes and Google Pay accept payments only by credit cards, which leaves 90% of users who do not have a credit card, or who do not want to use credit cards for online payments. Paymentwall Mobile allows users to pay through many local payment options, and also opens the door to the global market for application developers.

In addition to the fact that credit cards are the only form of payment for end users, discussions and dissatisfaction with application developers were also caused by the fact that iTunes charges 30% of the commission, while Android stores make application monetization more and more difficult. HTML5-based Paymentwall Mobile payment solution solves both developer problems by reducing the commission to 5% of the revenue share, more user-friendly and mobile-optimized user interface, and a future-looking mobile payment product.

In addition, integration with Paymentwall can be accomplished in a few simple steps using a simple API that instantly allows application developers to start collecting payments worldwide.

The most "deep" picture of the universe



Hubble astronomers have created the most "deep" picture of the universe. This image is made up of more than 2000 individual images, they were shot as much as 23 days. Here you can distinguish more than 5,500 galaxies, in the foreground only individual stars of our galaxy are visible.

Snapshot resolution 2382 * 2078



The variety of galaxies is amazing. You may notice spiral or elliptical galaxies, but besides this, there are also those whose outlines speak of their origin as a result of interaction with other galaxies. Perhaps this is the "newborn galaxy."

Video on Hubble Extreme Deep Field



The most distant galaxies are separated from us by a distance of 13 billion light years.

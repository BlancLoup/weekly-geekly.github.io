"Roskomsvoboda" in court appeals the mechanism of perpetual blocking sites


Screenshot of the "caps" site ZaRunet

Internet resources blocked "forever" in Russia, is becoming more and more. The list already has pleer.com, flibusta.net, Rutracker, Lurkmore. However, users, resource administrations, and lawyers do not give up. Recently, the public organization "Roskomsvoboda" proposed an interesting idea - an appeal in the courts of the mechanism of "perpetual blocking", writes "Kommersant". Lawyers of the organization plan to appeal not only to the Constitutional Court of the Russian Federation, but also to the European Court of Human Rights.

In order to ensure the normal work of lawyers to appeal to the courts of the site blocking mechanism, funds are needed. These funds are proposed to collect using crowdfunding. Roskomsvoboda has already launched ZaRunet.org, where anyone can "take part in the campaign to appeal against the" perpetual blockages "of Internet resources in court and raise funds for legal work in this area."

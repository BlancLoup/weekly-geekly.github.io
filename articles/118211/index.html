Million downloads on the Ovi Store



April 28 in Moscow, in the flagship salon Nokia on Tverskaya (Tverskaya str., 18B ) a festive event will take place: well-known Nokia mobile application developers will meet to exchange experience.

This event is dedicated to the new record of the Ovi Store in Russia - 1 million downloads per week. Ovi services are used by more than 170 million users who install 5,000,000 applications daily, but for Russia, where high-speed mobile Internet is just starting to become available, a million downloads per week is a huge number.

The most active users are Nokia N8, C7 and C6-01. These models account for more than 15% of all downloads. Their release caused an unprecedented interest in premium-class games, 62% of which buy for Symbian ^ 3 smartphones.

Russia entered the top ten of the most active countries in downloading games to new devices, ranking 4th in the total sales of games.

It's simple: choosing a smart phone, users get access to services and applications, which allows to significantly expand the capabilities of the device. For Nokia mobile devices, there are more than 40,000 such applications.

The profit of developers of all mobile platforms following the results of 2010 increased by 160%, and the income of publishers at the Ovi Shop - by 719%, which made Ovi the third most profitable mobile store in the world.

The main factor for this development was its accessibility: residents of more than 190 countries can receive free applications, and various payment methods are available in more than 170 countries around the world. Not only bank card holders can buy applications, but also subscribers of 109 operators in 34 countries, including Russia. The ability to pay from the phone and bank account allows you to reach the maximum audience, noticeably superior to any other store.

You can get acquainted with the authors of the best applications and learn first-hand everything that interests you. After the event, guests will receive a buffet and an entertainment program.
Beginning at 19-00, admission is free.

Search like in the best houses of London

On Tuesday, the news spread around the world that Google began to offer an international version of its site search service for organizations previously widely known exclusively by its Anglo-Saxon name, Custom Search.
Thanks to the global nature of the company (which, by the way, according to the subjective opinion of a number of individuals, has already become a more popular reason for paranoia than the global conspiracy of silencing facts about UFOs), now even on foreign websites that have internal search from Google, technical inscriptions of the search engine will be appear in the user's language. How it looks, you can see, for example, on the website of Macworld magazine. Rate the usefulness of this feature, everyone can own.

It is noteworthy that in the service logo on its Russian-language title page its name has undergone a strange inconsistency of the cases (see the illustration to the news), while the name is written correctly and in plain text on pages of the search results of the service.

The service is available to users in two versions: paid, the price of a subscription to which is determined by the number of pages being indexed on the site, and free, with somewhat reduced possibilities for setting up and showing contextual advertising in search results. The cost of a paid subscription starts at $ 100 per year for sites from 5,000 pages, rises up to $ 2,250 for work on a 300,000-page giant, and disappears into a haze of individual agreement for titans like the official website of the UK Parliament, which during the installation of this system more than 9,000,000 documents have been indexed recently.

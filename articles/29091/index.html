Designed camera with a resolution of 65 MP

The company Phase One has developed a digital camera with a resolution of 65 MP, which is 15 MP more compared to Hasselblad H3DII-50.

With Phase One, you can take photos with a resolution of 8984 x 6732 pixels, which is equivalent to 60.5 MP. The developer does not report the type and manufacturer of the sensor, except that it uses some kind of technology called Sensor +. The image is recorded in a file of 180 MB in size, i.e. about 45 images will fit on one 8 GB memory card. The camera can take one shot per second, writes luminous-landscape.com.



The dimensions of the sensor in the camera Phase One P65 + are 40.4 x 54.9 mm, which is 20% more than any other sensor in the world. For comparison, the sensor size in Hasselblad H3DII-50 is 36 x 48 mm.
The cost of the camera is $ 42 thousand.
In addition to the model with a resolution of 65 MP, another one is offered, with a resolution of 21 MB, which is sold at a significantly lower price of $ 13 thousand.

The essence of the Internet of things


But let's go back a step and realize the very essence of technology.

The Internet of Things (IoT) is a new opportunity to massively and inexpensively connect to the information network (for example, the Internet) any physical object and control system for these objects. Previously, the focus of IT lay on interaction through a network of PCs, servers and mobile phones, and the concept of the Internet of Things (IoT) proposes to significantly expand this series to any object in which a ‚Äúprocessor‚Äù, ‚Äúnetwork‚Äù and ‚Äúsensor‚Äù are embedded. This is not always useful, not always cost-effective, not always safe, but the range of useful applications is huge and continues to grow.

‚ÄúThe Internet of Things is likely to fundamentally change our interaction with the outside world. The ability to electronically track and control physical objects allows you to embed data-based decision making into new activities ‚Äî optimize systems and processes, save time for people and businesses, and improve the quality of life. What is the coming day preparing for me?


Returning from the market and business to our daily life, I would like to get an unequivocal answer: ‚ÄúWill the Internet of Things directly change my life?‚Äù Research centers, analytical agencies and experts, with care about us, have already given their assessments - ‚ÄúYes - they will. But concreteness and constructiveness are important to us - it is unacceptable to fall for biased statements. Therefore, let us land these statements on concrete changes in our lives.

Already, we can automate our lives at home and free up time for really interesting and important things: caring for plants , babysitting and healthy feeding of pets - all this is automated and with remote control function. Many of the worries that previously took time on a regular basis, we reassign robots and machine guns.

After 10 years, driving schools will start losing customers ... As with automatic driving, a person no longer needs to know the traffic rules. Many people will refuse to own a car - it will be cheaper and more convenient to rent a car (car sharing) . Thus, although "the future is not predetermined," but the expectations of experts are very realistic: most likely the Internet of things will radically change our lives. I support this forecast.

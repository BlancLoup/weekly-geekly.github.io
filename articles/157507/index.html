Sony Xperia J Review

This is not an easy task - to write about the budget vehicles of the market leaders. A vivid example of this is the Sony Xperia J model. According to Yandex.Market, it now costs about the same as one of the best android smartphones, the classic flagship of the Sony Xperia S , and is at least a head lower than its configuration.





Sony Xperia J is a follower of the traditions of the Sony ARC-series as evidenced by the concave back cover of the smartphone. Actually the design is almost the main advantage of the device. Due to the concavity of the back cover, the smartphone is very comfortable and confident in the hand.



By the highlight of the design of this smartphone is attributed to the light on the lower end of the fire. And the light of this light changes depending on the chosen theme of the smartphone interface design. On the right side are the volume rocker and the display lock button, it is the on / off button. With regards to this latter, I would like to note that, in my opinion, it is too small and recessed into the body. On the other hand, it insures against involuntary clicks. However, all the buttons are quite tight, so, I think, there would be no involuntary clicks and so, protrude the lock button above the case more clearly and being bigger.

The physical camera control button, unlike the flagship Sony models, is not here, but in its place is a recess, behind which the back cover should be hooked. At the top of the left end is a micro-USB connector for charging, which is not always convenient.

The front panel does not represent anything fundamentally original: above the display is the main speaker, proximity and lighting sensors, indicator of missed calls and messages, as well as 0.3 MP camera for video telephony. Below the display are three touch-sensitive interface control buttons and a microphone. The display is covered with a protective Gorilla Glass, as in "adult" smartphones.

Display Sony Xperia J manufactured by TFT technology. Alas, it is not as wonderful as the Xperia S Super LCD display, for example. And the matter is not even in more contrasting colors, to which not only one can get used, but also that someone may like even more than the soft, pastel colors of the Xperia S display. The point is much more serious characteristic: response time. Personally, it seemed to me that the response time of the Xperia J display was somehow too long. However, perhaps, it‚Äôs not just the quality of the display, but also the budget hardware stuffing of the smartphone.





Xperia J is based on a single-core Qualcomm MSM7227A processor operating at 1 GHz. This is a fairly common and well-proven processor for low-cost mobile solutions of the sample of 2010. His work space is provided by 512 MB of RAM, and the Adreno 200 coprocessor is responsible for graphics. The budget is extremely low for Android ICS. In combination with the not the fastest TFT-display, it seems to give an uncomfortable effect of a retarded response.









Sony smartphones are famous for their cameras. The same classic flagship Xperia S carries a 12 megapixel camera, which perfectly photographs and quite acceptable shoots video. However, alas, Xperia J is equipped with only 5 megapixel main camera, which, if you take pictures with an aspect ratio of 16: 9, it does use only 3 megapixels. As a result, the photos are very, very average, and with general plans they are bad at all. Even for social networks with modern average quality of smartphones, such photos obviously will not cause delight. Video this camera shoots with a resolution of 640x480 and at a speed of 30 frames per second, which is also unlikely to move to praise. Perhaps the only thing that pleasantly surprised me was the very small focal length of the camera: it focuses at least 5 cm from the object being photographed. If at the same time the object is removed with a 4: 3 aspect ratio, then in principle interesting pictures can turn out.

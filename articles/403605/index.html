Purpose


To begin with, let's answer the stupid question - why do we need a watch?

There are at least three functions here:

- Show time (oddly enough, yes :).
Given that smartphones are now increasingly bulky, a quick look at the clock is sometimes convenient. The good news here is that any modern watches from any modern brand (Casio, Citizen) do it well and inexpensively - for $ 50 you can buy watches that will work for 10 years without problems, then you can just throw them away and buy others. As an example, Casio Wave Ceptor for $ 75 has even built-in radio synchronization, and will always show the exact time (whether the function works in Russia, I do not know).

- To please the eye with a beautiful design.

Models can differ in the type of dial, the number of functions, etc., everything is individually. Indeed, beautiful watches, if they are selected with taste, look stylish, and it is nice to look at them.

- To be a status thing, first of all in the eyes of the owner. Say "yes, I can afford it." The price range here starts from 100 ‚Ç¨ and to infinity, everyone has different incomes. Well, tastes, of course, do not argue.

Design


By the type of mechanism, modern watches can be divided into 3 categories:

- Mechanical

Everything is clear, the principle of their work has not changed 50-100 years. Such models have the lowest accuracy of the course - 5-10 seconds per day , they also need to be wound every 1-2 days. Yes, even hours for 100 000 rub will go away for a couple of minutes a month. But on the other hand, these models do not become morally obsolete, they do not need to change the firmware and batteries, and with careful use, they can also be left to the grandchildren for memory. By the way, it is recommended to maintain expensive watches in the service every 3 years for cleaning and adjustment.

It may seem strange, but mechanical watches can have a lot of functions - from a stopwatch or a perpetual calendar, to display the phases of the moon. There are also models with automatic winding , which are not required to wind up, they wind up from the movement of the hand while walking, in the catalogs such watches are often called Automatic.

In the photo is a Vicrorinox Swiss watch:



Among the mechanical ones, we can separately mention models with a tourbillon - a special suspension, something like a gyroscope, designed to compensate for the unevenness of the clock because of the different inclination of the mechanism. The device is very difficult to manufacture, the cost of such watches is from $ 15,000.

- Quartz

In such a watch, the driving element is quartz with a frequency of 32768 Hz. Unlike mechanical, quartz watches have an accuracy of 1-2 seconds per month, well, of course, they do not need to wind up, sometimes changing the battery. They are also theoretically more reliable, because The mechanical spring mechanism is more complicated in design and is experiencing heavy loads in operation (it is practically difficult to verify, because by historical standards, quartz watches appeared relatively recently).

In watch ‚Äúgurus‚Äù, quartz watches are considered less ‚Äúpontovy‚Äù, although all modern watch manufacturers, even expensive ones, often have both types of models in the line.

- Electronic and combined

There is a large selection, from "classic" with an LCD screen, to modern ones, with a solar battery, radio synchronization and so on. Well, of course, modern Smart Watch is also very popular now.

Producing country


The classics of the genre of high-quality modern watches are Switzerland and Japan. True and there is a nuance - the Swiss Made logo means only that the watches are assembled in Switzerland, and some of the parts are made in Switzerland. So even when buying a cheap Swiss watch, you need to keep in mind that there are likely to be Chinese non-Swiss parts.

Conclusion


Maybe someday the LCD screen will be implanted directly into contact lenses or immediately into the eye, and the exact time will always be visible, but for now the clock will remain relevant for a long time. And even in this case, as a design element, they will still exist. Like vinyl records, they will not leave the market - this is one of those cases when the process of use is no less interesting than the result. The watch market is huge and interesting in its own way, many may find something new for themselves. Or not find anything, now the clock is really not the first thing.

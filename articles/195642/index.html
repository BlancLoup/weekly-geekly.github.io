The influence of cats on the development of technology



The relationship between the development of technology and human activity is obvious, but what can affect technological progress other than man?
First of all, nature comes to mind - phenomena honed by millions of years of evolution. We use her enormous experience to create innovative solutions in accordance with natural analogues. As aviation began with close observation of bird flights, so many modern technologies were formed under the influence of the environment.

What about cats? And indeed, when we talk about IT, these of your Internet, we veiled imply the presence of tailed purring. Gadgets for cats - a dynamic market. The global boom of mobile applications spawned such a thing as an interface for animals .

When did cats and high technology first start working on each other? Fast forward to the recent past, the beginning of the space age. The first travelers are sent to space, and among them is the cat.




USSR dogs, NASA chimpanzees, a host of turtles and mice ... The French surpassed everyone. On October 18, 1963, France launched a rocket with a cat on board into the near-Earth space. The choice of the animal is obvious - small height and weight, modest needs for food, water and air, flexibility and vitality (many cats without any problems experienced a fall from the N floor). That's just the researchers did not take into account the wayward nature of the experimental. The first astrokot that completed the course of complex trainings (including training for noise, vibration, and installation of sensors) escaped shortly before the start. In a rush, he was replaced by a purebred cat Felisette. But she left the scientists with nothing, having escaped after the flight.

In defense of the seals, we note that it was possible to obtain important scientific material. Felissetta had implanted electrodes into the brain, which were used to record information from the left somatosensory and associative areas of the cerebral cortex, from the hippocampus and from the mid-brain zone. It was possible to fix how a living organism behaves in weightlessness. By the way, the behavior of cats in zero gravity can be judged by this video - no, staying in orbit is not for them.

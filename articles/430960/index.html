Pro gamedev from the desktop exhibition

I want to show you some interesting pieces from the Spiel exhibition in Essen. This is the largest exhibition of board games in the world (comparable to Gencon in the USA), where everyone who has at least some relation to the tabletops comes to. We go there for 10 years in a row, scare people and shoot samples.

Interestingly, many hybrid games have come this year. That is, those where IT is very tightly intersected with the physical world. Here, for example, just tryndets fun game "Soviet cuisine" from a European publisher:



The plot is as follows. After World War II there was nothing to eat, and Stalin ordered to cook from everything in a row. You take vodka, nuclear waste, boots from a corpse and bagels, mix in a meat grinder. If you get the right color - get points.



For rather obvious reasons, we did not take a license for the game. But apart from the characteristics of the setting, it is precisely the mechanics to madness that is beautiful.

It works like this: you put the application on the phone. It activates the camera. Bring to it a recipe card. A QR code is woven into the pattern on the shirt. The camera reads the shirt and the application knows what color you want to get.



Then you choose from the table a card of the ingredients of your future dish. The task is to get the same brown. You show four cards in turn to the application, it reads their shirts very quickly and shows the grind animation in the meat grinder.



Then from the meat grinder beats the fountain of the desired color. And points are given for similarity.

The application is very organically entered into the game. Very responsive, you don‚Äôt have to touch the phone, full Squarewell in the interface and actions of the players. You just take the cards and throw in the meat grinder. Very cool, but the model is sensitive to the performance of the phone, the quality of the picture from the camera and the lighting. Owners of economy phones will already feel the brakes in the desktop. But I must say that I tested beta.



If the phone or tablet is fast, then a new level is opened. This is one of the first cases when so cool is done, and when using the phone does not interfere with the game. It turns out that the main thing is the HCI model, and not the very presence of the phone on the table.

Another smartphone on the table means no need to read the rules. He and the video can show, and say what to do right now on the situation. In this game, you first launch the application, and then it tells you how many cards to distribute and what to do. This is also background music for the atmosphere. And single player mode. And save the game for full campaigns. In general, really cool work.

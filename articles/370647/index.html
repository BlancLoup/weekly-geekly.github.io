Moto Z2 Play Review: Long-Awaited Update

The previous model, Moto Z Play, was also slim (7 mm), but the updated model stands out in this respect.





Under the screen there is a touch button with a built-in fingerprint scanner, at the same time it can be configured to recognize gestures. In the upper area, in addition to the window of the front camera and the sensor eye, there is a flash note - selfie lovers will be pleased. Some manufacturers make imitation of the face light through a white screen, but the flash is still much more effective.

Display


The Moto Z2 Play uses a 5.5-inch screen, made by technology Super AMOLED. The ratio of the display area to the front panel is quite decent - 70%. The resolution is FullHD, the pixel density is 401 ppi. You can not worry about the fragility of the glass: it is protected by Corning Gorilla Glass 3, which provides good scratch resistance. To see the pixels under normal use cases is impossible. Even bringing the smartphone close to the eyes, not immediately able to distinguish between "squares". Hardware component and system


Moto Z2 Play has a more powerful hardware than its predecessor. The Moto Z Play model had a Snapdragon 625 chip, and the new product uses a more advanced and powerful version of the Snapdragon 626. In the benchmarks, the updated iron proved to be more than worthy. Yes, the results are more modest than the flagship devices - but Moto Z2 Play is in a different niche and performs in a different price range.



Testing, by the way, showed that the system copes well with strong loads: in "heavy" games, such as Real Racing 3, the smartphone behaves decently.

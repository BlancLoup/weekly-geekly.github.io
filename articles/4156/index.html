US parliament bans gambling online

As expected, the US House of Representatives on Tuesday approved a new bill that puts an end to online gambling in America. Despite the best efforts of the ‚Äúgame‚Äù lobby, the voting ended with a score of 317-93 .

The new bill prohibits banks as well as credit card issuers from making payments in favor of gambling websites. The government also reserves the right to put pressure on Internet providers to block the traffic of such sites. This is a terrible blow to the global gambling industry. Half of its annual turnover of $ 12 billion is still provided by the American players. Under the new law, the only outlet for gambling Americans will remain horse racing and lotteries, to which the ban does not apply.

The authors of the bill, speaking before a vote in parliament, stressed its particular importance. The law must solve one of the saddest problems of modern society ‚Äî uncontrollable passion. The bill was developed by representatives of the Republican Party before the November elections as part of their election initiative in defense of moral values. For official approval, he still has to pass consideration in the Senate. There are only a few days left before the election campaign, so there are big doubts that the bill will be adopted this year. If the senators do not have time to consider it, it will be postponed for next year.

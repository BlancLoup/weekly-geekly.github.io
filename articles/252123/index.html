Creating a creative portfolio. From idea to release

The perfect interface is simple, there is nothing superfluous. For a portfolio, this is also relevant. But what if you want to stand out from the masses of designers? What for?


First you need to determine what is required of my portfolio? You can brag about projects to other designers and become the site of the month at awwwards, look for a new job or attract the attention of potential customers for freelancing.

Who is the target audience?


Having defined the main goal of the site, we come to the one for whom my portfolio is intended:

  • Other designers and art directors in pursuit of fame;
  • The head of a particular studio or a more abstract head when looking for work;
  • A potential client who needs, for example, who wants to order a website design from a freelancer;


Each of these types of target audience has its own needs, its own unique features and its own character:



This Div Sobzh is another designer and appraiser of your work. Always in search of inspiration. Ready on occasion to give a life-giving kick if your site seems unworthy to him.

And Ludmila , a travel agency manager for invigorating excursions in the Antarctic, is a potential client. He does not know what he wants from the site. Already communicated with other designers, but did not find a common language.

What with consumer insight?


To identify insight, you need to understand what unites 3 types of target audience, what common problems they have. It does not matter whether he is the director of the studio or the customer in search of a freelancer. Common problems will be:

Designers can not understand the task of the client.
The worst sin among designers is the inability to build proper communication with the client. The resulting design may and will be a beautiful picture, but it does not help the client, it is useless.

The designer is careless, breaks deadlines and is late for meetings.
Time is money. And although the motto is ‚ÄúExpensive. Long. /// ‚Äùhas not been canceled, creating a good design in terms, no matter how brief they were, is the only way to a profitable product. Answers to calls and advance arrival at meetings contributes to this.

Gray and mediocrity.
The client, perhaps, wants to design "That's how the office", but only if it will be profitable? Building a design based on the needs of the client, and the original idea is the key to a successful site that creates an emotional response from both the client and the end users. Consequently, the site will be remembered and will come to it again and again.

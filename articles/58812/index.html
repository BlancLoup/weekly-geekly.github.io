A fresh look at things

This post will be of interest primarily to iPhone owners, especially with the Evernote client installed on it. You must have experienced the limited capabilities of the camera of this phone. Blurred photos of business cards, labels, flyers - all this is certainly annoying. And in the case of Evernote also creates difficulties with the recognition of the photographed text.

Therefore, when we learned that there is a company that successfully solved this problem, we offered her cooperation and mutual marketing support. We are talking about Griffin Technology, the manufacturer of accessories for Apple products, in the range of which, among other things, there is Clarifi - a solution that won us at first glance.

Clarifi is a polycarbonate case for the iPhone 3G, equipped with a special lens that you can just slide your finger on the phone's camera to get the opportunity to macro photography. So, if to get a normal image sharpness without this lens you need to take at least half a meter, then with Clarifi very clear photos are obtained already from 10 cm.

A few days ago, we launched a campaign with Griffin Technology, thanks to which every Evernote user can buy this case on the official Griffin website with a 20% discount, indicating the code " GRIFFINEVRNT09 " when ordering.

But in the case of delivery to Russia, the cost of delivery is added to the price of the cover and there is no guarantee that the cover will arrive in good condition. Therefore, we found here, in Russia, people who sell these covers - clarifi.ru , asked them for a similar discount for Russian Evernote users, and they kindly agreed to give the same discount of 20% of the price of the cover to any buyer who he will call the order, and when ordering by e-mail, he will write the word " EVERNOTE " in the letter.

So if you want to make interesting macro photos with your iPhone and use the features of Evernote for iPhone 100%, then you have a great chance to do it.

PS Please note that Clarifi cases are only suitable for iPhone 3G .

Visualization of information and statistics

It so happened that from time to time I have to work with statistics, and therefore ask the question "In what form is it better to present it to other people?" Therefore, today I will tell you about some very original ways of visualizing various kinds of statistical data.

To begin with, I will give an example of one visualization done with Processing .
It is based on the annual statistics of the people and organizations most covered by the New York Times, and the links between them are shown by lines.

Under the link you can see the visualization for each year since 1984.

There are an infinite number of such services, but I stopped at this because I recently mentioned the mention of it on one resource , and I liked it. The UUrold program visualizes statistics and binds it to different politically units. This is realized by the "add-on" of a three-dimensional diagram above the political map of the world. A special player is built into the program, and if, say, the statistics have been collected for several years in a row, by changing the height of the chart, you can observe in real time how it has varied from year to year. It is difficult to describe it in words, therefore I post the author's video.

The non-commercial delivery of the program includes a huge statistical base (more than 10,000) from various fields, from medicine to economics. Of course there is the possibility of adding your own.

Tripping. Trimaping appeared as early as the beginning of the 90s, but has not received wide distribution even today. Initially, trimaping was used to visualize data on disk space, but subsequently for other purposes. The essence of this method of visualization is to represent a hierarchical (tree) system in the form of nest-rectangles. Each rectangle, with a similar visualization, has an area proportional to the statistical data. Often it helps to take a fresh look at the data structure. The most famous utility for making trimaps is the treemap 4.0 of the same name .

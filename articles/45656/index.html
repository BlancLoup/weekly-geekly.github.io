As before laptops did - Toshiba Libretto 50ct (1997 onwards)

It so happened that I became the owner of such a miracle:

It turned out that, despite the rather old age (laptop sample 1997), this machine is quite interesting.
So, my review of Toshiba Libretto 50ct. Continued under the cut.

Specifications

CPU Intel Pentium 75MHz
Bus frequency 50
RAM 16mb (expands to 32x)
Screen TFT 6.1 "
Screen resolution 640x480
Graphics chip CT-65550 (Chips & Tech)
Sound card Yamaha OPL3 SA2 (Sound Blaster compatible)
HDD 810mb Toshiba MK-0803MAT
PCMCIA 1 slot Type II 16bit
Dimensions 210 x 115 x 34
Weight 850 gr


Design

Length 21cm, width 12cm. Like a little book. I put it in my briefcase and forgot that you carry a laptop with you -)
Despite such dimensions, the keyboard is quite comfortable. Typing on it is quite realistic.
Very interesting idea with a trackpad. As you have already noticed from the photo, there is no notebook touchpad familiar to us here. Toshiba has implemented a slightly different system. Right on the cover you can see the joystick. So, this joystick controls the mouse cursor. The mouse buttons are located on the other side of the cover. At first, such a system seems terribly inconvenient, but after 2-3 hours of work you get used to it and there are no problems with the trackpad. As proof, I will declare that I have completed the entire Warcraft 2 campaign on this laptop =)

The laptop uses a non-standard hard disk, up to 9mm. Although at the Tsaritsyn radio market one can find a couple of such hard drives. Insert the hard drive side with a special piece.


Equipment

External floppod with PCMCIA interface was attached to the laptop. Floppy drivers are flashed in the BIOS itself, so booting from a floppy is possible. This device does not fit modern laptops (checked).

Also included is a port replicator. Installing it on a laptop, we get additional connectors.
Total on a laptop with a connected replicator we have:
1 Com port
1 VGA port for connecting an external monitor (maximum resolution 1280x1024 with 16 colors =)
1 LPT port
Infrared (!)
Headphone jack.


operating system

The official supported system for Libretto is considered to be Windows 95. But instead of it, you can also install 98. My experience with Windows 98 on this machine is sad: it really slows down. A 95 works great, you can even say, flies.
Toshiba managed in 1997 to implement a hardware hibernate for this laptop (on a purely AT mother). It looks like this. After installing special drivers, an additional ‚ÄúStop‚Äù item appears in the Windows menu. After clicking on it, the screen goes out and a rather funny picture appears on it, illustrating the process of recording the contents of the RAM on hard. When you turn on the laptop, the exact same picture shows the progress of the reverse operation.

I was shocked when I got to the Toshiba website and saw that the libretto is still supported and the website still contains all the drivers, utilities and FAQ.
On a laptop, such things as Office, Duke 3d, Warcraft 2 and even the QT compiler are remarkably launched and work. I have already explained to one familiar C ++ on this typewriter. You can work.

Weak sides

Undoubtedly, the main problem of the laptop is the lack of USB. In addition, there is no network card, so you will have to use a PCMCIA network card to connect to the network. Here another problem appears: 16 bits of the PCMCIA interface. Finding something right now for this tire is quite difficult. Although maybe I just can not search. I walked around the entire Tsaritsyn radio market, but there were no network cards there. Maybe someone from habravchan prompts a place where you can find such things.

Conclusion

For 1997, the machine is very, very cool. Unfortunately, nowadays it is rather difficult to find an application for it ...

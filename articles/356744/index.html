SkyNet does exist, but it's too early for the terminators



According to The Intercept , renowned journalist Ahmad Mouffak Zaydan came under the sights of an automatic system that tracks down terrorists by analyzing data from mobile networks. He was added to the blacklist on suspicion of involvement in al-Qaeda.

A few years ago, Edward Snowden gave secret presentations to journalists, from which it became known that the NSA (NSA), the US National Security Agency, had a system under the threatening name SKYNET. Presentations describe in detail the principles of the system.

This SKYNET will not be able to launch nuclear missiles in automatic mode and wipe out most of humanity. But she is able to handle large amounts of data received from GSM networks. It tracks blacklisted people and highlights suspicious patterns of behavior. The system uses cloud technology and machine learning.



The system keeps track of traveling people from the list, determines what they do on arrival at the place they call. Do often travel the same route.

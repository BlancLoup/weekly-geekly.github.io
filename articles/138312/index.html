The history of creating an interactive book for iPad

Greetings to all!

I want to tell you how we created our first interactive book for the iPad - Puss in Boots. Speaking we, I mean an excellent team of not indifferent professionals, who with soul were given to the project.


It all started with the fact that my friend and I decided to try myself in the creation of interactive books. I like interactive books because of my four-year-old daughter, who simply adores them. A friend (his name is Denis Gribkov) is interesting from a professional point of view - he has long been engaged in computer graphics and animation.
To begin, we decided to decide on a fairy tale. We immediately decided that we would distribute our book in several languages. Therefore, it was decided to choose a fairy tale known throughout the world. Making a list of several options, we stopped at ‚ÄúPuss in Boots‚Äù. It seemed to us optimal in terms of volume, with interesting characters and bright locations.
Having divided the text into pages, we began the search for an artist-illustrator. At first we tried to look for sites with freelancers. But candidates either requested a lot of money, or did not suit us in terms of the level of work. Searches among freelancers lasted about two weeks. After an unsuccessful ‚Äúcasting‚Äù among freelancers, we decided to search for our artist on thematic sites. We began to look for artists on our favorite site. Having found several artists whose works we liked, we wrote letters to them. Only one responded =). It was a wonderful artist from Ukraine - Sergey Loginov. Having agreed that our budget is extremely small, but the project is very interesting, we began work.

A couple of days later Sergey sent us a sketch of the first page.

We decided to try to make a beautiful on-frame animation. We started the animation itself using Adobe After Effects. The characters were very lively.
The animation of each character we reduced to a separate file. Here's a sigh animation and head scratching (48 frames).

Sergey drew, and Anton did the animation.

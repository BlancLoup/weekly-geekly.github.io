At the photo contest ESO won amateur astronomer from Taganrog



Igor Chekalin, an astronomer from Taganrog, became the winner of the Hidden Treasures space photography competition (‚ÄúHidden Treasures‚Äù) held by the International European Southern Observatory (ESO). Competitors had to find interesting astronomical objects in the observatory's archive , process them, remove distortions, turn pictures into color.

In total, more than 100 papers were sent to the competition. You can view the entire archive in Flickr photo album . Those who like astronomical photography should also visit the gallery of the best photos of ESO .

At the Hidden Treasures competition, Igor Chekalin won the first two places.



For the first place, he was awarded a trip to Chile to the ESO observation complex, where four reflecting telescopes operate with main mirrors of more than 8 m, connected by a computer network into a single VLT (Very Large Telescope). The prize for second place is the iPod Touch engraved with the ESO logo.

In third place was the work of the Ukrainian astrophotographer Sergey Stepanenko (he will receive a book and a DVD).

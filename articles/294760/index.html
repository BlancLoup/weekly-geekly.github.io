Rostelecom arranged the largest tender in Russia for the purchase of subscriber equipment GPON

Rostelecom is organizing a tender for the purchase of just over 1.54 million GPON-based devices. GPON technology involves the laying of an optical cable in each apartment. The operator needs GPON routers and GPON sockets. The starting price of lots for the purchase is $ 91.94 million and $ 24 million, respectively. This is the largest tender of Rostelecom for the purchase of subscriber equipment GPON - for the first time it is held centrally for all macroregional branches of the operator, said a representative of Rostelecom Valery Kosterev. The initial price of a GPON router is $ 82.8, a GPON socket is $ 54.81, but Rostelecom expects to significantly reduce it by purchasing a larger batch, Kosterev notes.

Source: Telecom Daily

In early September 2015, during the last federal tender for the purchase of 2.22 million FTTB modems, the starting price was reduced by 45%, he recalls. The Fiber to the building technology involves the installation of fiber optics to the house and the distribution of the Internet to the apartments via copper cables.

According to Kostarev, GPON routers Rostelecom plans to use mainly for new connections and in part to reconnect subscribers using xDSL (broadband Internet access via telephone wires). Of the 28 million households covered by Rostelecom fiber optic networks, about a third can be connected to GPON.

According to Telecom Daily, by the beginning of the third quarter of 2015, GPON used about 1.5 million of approximately 10.5 million Rostelecom broadband users. Over the year, Rostelecom connected just over 0.5 million GPON subscribers, calculated Telecom Daily CEO Denis Kuskov. It can be assumed that 1.5 million routers will last for about 1.5 years, since the company is actively building passive fiber-optic networks and also converts xDSL subscribers to optics, he argues.
According to the operator, the number of private users of Rostelecom broadband in the second quarter of 2015 increased by 4% compared to the same period last year.

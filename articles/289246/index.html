5 common marketing problems, the root of which can be hidden in the layout

The root of many major marketing problems lies in poor web development. Understanding what dangers can be eliminated using advanced design techniques can mean the difference between a successful and failed advertising campaign.

If you told someone twenty years ago that he would promote online marketing, he would think you were crazy. Today, by telling someone that you are not promoting online marketing, you may encounter a similar reaction.

Well, if, of course, they do not mistakenly consider your inexperience as charming retrograde. Shhh, do not correct them. Just pull up your children's pants and move on.

As of 2015, more than 91 percent of B2B marketing companies used content marketing, 48 percent created a landing page for each marketing campaign, and 40 percent of US companies use blogs for promotional purposes.

Digital marketing has become our everything, and everyone is eager to touch his greatness.

But going online is the easiest part. But keeping a strong and effective online presence is another story, one in which the web developer takes the role of the main character. Web development is a mechanism on which digital marketing is moving, and if the mechanism is broken, then nothing works. Not every marketing problem can be solved with the help of high-quality web development, but many of them are still possible. If you have encountered any of the problems listed below, deft machinations with web development may be useful to you.

Problem: Poor Rankings by Search Engines


If you systematically post content that is rich in keywords, but you don‚Äôt see the desired results, the problem may lie in the architecture of the website.

A good web developer can use search engine optimization (SEO), making sure that the company's presence on the web is built on the same principles of design and technology.

This can ensure that all content is well formatted and well presented for indexing by the search engine.

A website that loads quickly and displays accurately on all types of devices has an advantage in terms of the number of users and traffic. More traffic affects the ranking of search results. In addition, more traffic is great anyway. Traffic in the trend, if you want.

Problem: Rare content


Solid, unique content, such as articles, blog entries and infographics, increase traffic and customer conversions, but without a built-in content management system (CMS), in which it is easy to post such content, many companies have difficulty updating. This is the case when a web developer has the opportunity to prove himself, either by incorporating an existing CMS (WordPress, Joomla) into the site layout, or by creating a special CMS for a particular company.

CMS simplifies the process of publishing and updating content, as it does not require any programming knowledge. You no longer need to spend man-hours on updating the content, tracking the only developer in the company to make it change the code - using the CMS, anyone can publish the desired content in two clicks

Problem: No user information


The average marketing specialist in your company may not see the difference between asking the user to login using their username or using their email address. Ask the user to enter your email address on the registration page - this is the easiest way to make a list of email addresses, and one of the least complicated methods of obtaining information about users.

However, the role of the web developer is not limited to the knowledge of these methods. He or she should also know how to integrate such elements into registration forms, social media plug-ins and special windows so that they do not spoil the experience of user interaction.

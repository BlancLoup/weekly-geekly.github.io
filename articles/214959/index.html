Photobank Getty Images has opened free access to 35 million photos



One of the largest photobanks in the world, Getty Images removed watermarks from 35 million photos - and allowed them to be published free of charge for non-commercial purposes through a frame with embedded Embed content, like YouTube and others.

For Getty Images, this is an important step. Previously, they fought offenders with threats and fines, demanding that they remove photos of more than 150x150 px. Now they allow you to post good quality pictures for free.

Such actions of the largest photo bank show that the old methods with strict copyright protection and watermarks are ineffective on the Internet. Distribution of letters with threats to the owners of sites that have published "pirated" content is not valid. Bloggers have no money to pay 200-500 dollars for each photo.

In the past years, publishers have been exclusively professional firms, but on the Internet each publisher himself.

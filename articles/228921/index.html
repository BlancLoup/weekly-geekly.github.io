CyberData SIP Alerts



CyberData equipment is new in the Russian market. The product category of this manufacturer is a bit specific.

The company produces IP alert devices: loudspeakers, intercoms, amplifiers, bell indicators, adapters, accessories and more. And all this works under the SIP protocol.

Who wants to get acquainted with the products of this vendor more tightly, welcome to habrakat.



Using CyberData devices does not mean at all that you are deploying an alert system from scratch. The catalog also contains devices for connecting already to existing speakers and amplifiers.

The product line is very similar to the Czech company 2N, or is the 2N line similar to Cyberdata products? Rather, the second, because The American manufacturer has more than 30 years of history. And the price tag of the company from California will pleasantly surprise you.

Specifications

Similar for most devices, and listed below:
  • Device management via WEB interface
  • Power through PoE 802.3af (+ there is a possibility through an external power source)
  • SIP RFC 3261 or Multicast
  • Operation temperature from -10 C to +50 C
  • G711 A-Law and u-Law
  • DHCP / Static IP, car maintenance


The manufacturer did not really think about the color options of products, and they are available only in two versions: white and gray.
I have a SIP intercom on my hands, which (like all my CyberData products) is sharpened by Asterisk. I exaggerate of course, but it seems to me that Asterisk is able to unlock the full functionality.
Aluminum case, pleasant and bright key illumination.

By default, devices are pre-configured to receive an address via DHCP, and you can get the received address using a special utility .

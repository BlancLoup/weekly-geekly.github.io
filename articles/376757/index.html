Anti-piracy company demanded that Google remove links to operating systems, databases, messengers and browsers

The German company Total Wipes Anti Piracy , which controls copyright on the Internet, has sent many requests for content removal to Google. This would not be surprising, but among the links that ‚Äúanti-pirates‚Äù considered to be infringing copyright, there was such an incredible list of companies that TorrentFreak journalists with a light heart called these requests ‚Äúthe most idiotic‚Äù.

A label Aborigeno Music released a musical album "In To The Wild - Vol.7". Obviously, the piece of music turned out to be popular on the Internet, so the label management was forced to ask for assistance in tracking illegal references to the album to Total Wipes.

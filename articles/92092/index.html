Service Asus in the Czech Republic. Little positive


The other day I happened to experience the quality of the work of the Asus service in the Czech Republic. The M50vc, which I bought a year and a half ago, turned out to be very fast and durable. There are no problems with modern resource-intensive programs and even games, although a lot of time has passed by computer standards. The main problem was in the case, more precisely in the screen, which was easily scratched and the buttons of the mouse connected with each other by some kind of wand, which constantly broke.

But after a trip to Germany, the battery was completely discharged and blocked, and a week before it began to short charge. I had to use the services of the service center.
Asusa has the Pick Up & Return service, the courier from DPD takes your laptop from your home or work address and returns the repaired laptop to it. There is a certain minus in it, because I work from different parts of Prague, and today I had to sit at home for half a day, waiting for the return of my electronic friend.

Last Tuesday, a courier came to me, took the laptop, on Wednesday morning he was already in service, and the repair began. I followed the repair process through a special order tracking interface. On Thursday, I received a letter from the service saying that the torn charging cord is my bullying and will have to pay for charging. I called to clarify why their charging does not withstand my use, they said that the wire is twisted and this is not normal. 153 kroons of work are always charged and cannot be removed from the account. I agreed, because I considered charging for 750 kroons a good offer, a week before the incident I was going to order a 21-pound Chinese analogue on eBay for 21 pounds from England. On Friday they wrote to me that your order is ready, wait. But, the Czech Republic is an amazing country, even couriers do not work in it on weekends. As a result, I received the cherished package only on Monday lunch.

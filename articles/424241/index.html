Digital printing

Digital and 3D printing come to the aid of traditional decor solutions. They allow the use of approaches that were previously impossible, or the cost of their implementation was extremely high. The design becomes personalized, created for individual needs.

The main part of digital printing in the design accounts for the creation of various coatings, where printing on textiles can also be attributed, which is eloquently confirmed by numbers.

By 2024, the volume of the digital printing market in Europe alone, according to experts, will grow by 200 percent and reach 121 billion. Two-fold growth will be possible through the use of this technology in the production of decor.

3D printing


The advent of 3D printing has had a huge impact on various areas of production. The volume of global spending on 3D printers from 2015 to 2018 has increased almost tenfold - from 1.6 billion USD to 13.4 billion USD (according to Gartner). In the future, one of the main uses for 3D printing, according to PwC forecasts, will be the independent printing of home decor.
3D printing radically changes the working methods of manufacturing and engineering companies, and also offers a wide range of possibilities for creative industries.

The development of technology makes 3D printers more accessible to ordinary users, while improving quality and reducing printing costs. All this allows designers to experiment with new combinations of materials, new forms and designs.
3D printing is already widely used in the design and manufacture of furniture, lighting and decorative ornaments, both for commercial and home interiors.



As an example, lighting designer Robert Deban. It uses 3D printing to complement traditional printing methods. This allows him to emphasize the mosaic and geometric patterns in each of his modern designs. This combination of traditional and 3D printing is also ideal for creating unique, personalized items.

3D printing allows people to add individuality to any object, from furniture to accessories. There is a tendency of transition from standardized design projects for mass production, developed by professional designers, to individual creativity of users who create their own personal space.

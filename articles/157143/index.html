Overview of startup projects over the past week in Asia

What happens in the Asian startup projects environment? What features do these IT projects have?
A small overview of the main services for the last week.




1. iCard: service of learning English, daily lessons, to improve the speaking.



Currently, the service has more than 70,000 users, accumulated a month after the official launch took place.
iCard helps in translating from Chinese into English through a mobile application. The project plans to reach a million users in 2013.

2. "Japanese manga camera": service for mobile phone cameras, more than three million downloads for the current moment.



This is one of the main, unique camera apps for mobile phones in Asia.
Allows users to apply Japanese effects manga style when taking photos on the phone.
The service has become popular recently, not only at home in Japan, but also in the United States, Britain and the Asian region. While available for download only in the App Store.

3. Berbatik: online batik sale service.



A project from Indonesia, launched recently, with the goal of selling traditional batik around the world. The creators of the project decided to focus on high-level batik products, the manufacture of which is based on strict standards in Indonesia. It will be interesting to observe how the project will develop and what niche they will occupy.

4. Xipin: the service that shook China, approximately 1.5 million dollars of investment at the moment.



This is a place of sales, where unique handmade products are exhibited. The main consumers are the middle class in China.
The company is headed by Tan Kayiem, one of several founders of the startup movement of women in the country.

5. Stimolo: social discount service, launched a month ago and founded by a graduate of the Jakarta Institute.



At the moment, investments have been attracted, a website and an application for Android have been developed, an iOS application is expected by early 2013.

6. Pulsk: custom photo content service in Indonesia.



The winner of the Asian contest Sparxup, the project offers to evaluate photos of various subjects that have been uploaded by users.
Since its launch in mid-September, the service has over 850,000 page views.

Pac-man


In 1980, the Japanese company Namco starts selling the PUCK MAN gaming machine. He later goes to the United States under the name Pac-Man. In English, PUCK would immediately be altered into another word.

No violence, as in the popular games of the 1970s. Only ghosts and cherries. Now the arcade machine is suitable for children. The developer Toru Ivatani just counted on expanding the audience. The game was ported to home game consoles, for example, on the Atari 2600. Ivatani worked on the game for a year and a half.

The Pac-Man submachine gun was sold in the amount of 350,000 copies, various fakes and games appeared, just like racing in a maze. Even the next game on the list - Donkey Kong from Nintendo - is a labyrinth, although it is more likely a platformer.

Donkey-kong


In 1981 comes out one of the first games in the genre of the platformer - Donkey Kong from Nintendo. This is where Mario first appears. Although his name was Jumpman. And he was not a plumber, but a carpenter. But this is still the same Mario. In the game you need to save your girlfriend from the villain, a huge monkey. Up to this point in games, there has not yet been a hackneyed story with the rescue of a girl out of trouble .

The game from the arcade machine ported to game consoles and computers. The franchise itself was divided into two - there were sequels about Donkey Kong, and one of the best-selling Super Mario games in the entire history.

It is interesting that from the very beginning the sailor Popeye should have been in the game, and Olive should have performed as a princess. Only with Disney it was not possible to agree, therefore they invented other characters. A year after the release of the game, Disney Studio changed its mind as it saw the success of the idea, and the arcade machine Popeye was born.



Pitfall!


In 1982, Toshiba released its first personal computer, the T100, founded the company, the publisher of games Electronic Arts, and in the USSR began to produce computers DVK. For this article, the most interesting event is Pitfall !, a game from Activision, which is often called the first platformer.

Everything was there: alligators, through whose mouths it was necessary to jump when they were closed, creepers, with the help of which it was possible to fly over reservoirs and pits, rolling barrels, dungeons, fires, snakes and ladders. In total, it was necessary to overcome 255 screens and collect all the treasures. Roughly speaking, all subsequent platformers differ only in the complexity of riddles and graphics.

Pitfall! sold in the amount of four million copies only on the Atari 2600. It was on this platform that the game became the second in sales after Pac-Man. But other than that, Pitfall! ported to other game consoles - Intellivision and ColecoVision, as well as to Commodore 64 computers and MSX machines.

Tetris


On June 6, 1984, Alexey Pajitnov introduced Tetris, this synonym for computer games in principle, one of the best-selling and most famous games in the history of the industry. In 2016, the game turned 32 years old, she managed to visit almost all gaming platforms, and based on the game they are going to make a sci-fi film .

The name of the game comes from "Tetramino" (a game with figures from four squares) and "Tennis". There are many versions of the game, including 360-degree, bulk and physical - board games.

The rights to use the game in their arcade machines first bought Atari. In parallel, Nintendo purchased the rights for the NES and Game Boy. The company Tetris Company Pajitnov registered only in 1996, to start earning on his invention.

By 2014, the mobile platforms installed the game 425 million times, sold cartridges for the Game Boy in the amount of 37 million, and besides this - another 70 million copies of the game, not counting unlicensed devices.

The game combined a platformer with puzzles, parkour, running and sword fighting. Graphics for his age was amazing.

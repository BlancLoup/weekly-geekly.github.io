HBO launches HBO on Broadband online service

HBO's cable channel launches a pilot (limited by Time Warner Cable users in Wisconsin) online service HBO on Broadband, which provides users with the ability to download movies, TV shows, sports broadcasts and other programs and watch them on PC. The service will be free for HBO users, but users must have HBO On Demand and Time Warner RoadRunner services (internet access) connected.

At the start of the project, the catalog contains 600 video programs with a total duration of more than 400 hours. Most of the content is available for viewing by users without limiting the number of times or time, but some programs will be available for 4-12 weeks after which they will be automatically deleted (similar to the BBC iPlayer service ).

To view the uploaded video, users will need to install a Time Warner software video player distributed to users on a CD. As you can imagine, the service will be available only for MS Windows, the video can also not be copied to portable video players (iPod, Zune, etc.).

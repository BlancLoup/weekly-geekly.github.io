Iterasi develops into a must have utility


In January of this year there was a small post about this service, but it remained unnoticed and I decided to draw people's attention to this wonderful service again.
Iterasi allows you to create bookmarks on the fly, but it saves not just one URL, but actually takes a snapshot of the entire site, including cookies, dynamically changeable objects on the page, etc.

Here is an example of the site of Reformal, which I just saved. Any saved page can be laid out in the frame and it will be an interactive version of the site.

Now Iterasi works only on the WIN platform and the pages are saved using plug-ins for FireFox and IE.
Also from the useful functions of the service - it is possible to create schedules to save the same page and as a result you can create a complete likeness of TimeMashine for any site. You can also sort by date for all saved pages, assign tags to them and perform a full search.
It will be important to mention that the service is absolutely free.

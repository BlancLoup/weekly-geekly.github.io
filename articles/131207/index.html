Google+ everywhere: Reader first, now Blogger, soon everything else



The social functions of Google+ have appeared on the Blogger blog platform. Blogger users can replace their profiles with Google+ profiles, and this is just the beginning.

Last week, it was reported that the social functions of Google+ would appear in the RSS reader Google Reader. Google intends to gradually integrate the functions of Google+ into all its services.

Google sees its social project more than a social network or a set of communication tools. For a company, this is a plan for integrating social information into everything you do on the Internet: from shopping to searching, from correspondence and more generally.

In addition to the ability to replace your Blogger profile with your Google+ profile, your friends will see your posts in the Google search results with a note that you shared with them:



Switched bloggers will automatically gain access to all future features related to Google+.

If you, for example, run your blog under a pseudonym and do not want to reveal your identity, then no one obliges you to link your blog with Google+. This is a completely voluntary opportunity. You can now enable (and disable ) integration in Blogger in Draft, and in the coming weeks, this feature will be available to all Blogger users.

As for integration into Google Reader, Google employee Alan Green wrote : ‚ÄúWe are going to bring Reader and Google+ together so that you can share the best of your subscriptions with the right circles.‚Äù Where existing Reader functions are duplicated by Google+ functions, the company decided to give preference to the latter: old Reader mechanisms such as friending and following will disappear this week.

For sure, integration into Blogger will also happen: the tools are likely to make it easier to share your posts with the right circles and can duplicate the current social functions of Blogger.

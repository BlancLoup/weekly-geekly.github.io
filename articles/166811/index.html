Tiles on the tablet. Acer Iconia Tab W700 Review

I remember back in 2007, I very much dreamed of a laptop with a touch screen. Poke the stylus in the icons was cool. Then I dreamed of the HTC Shift, then HP Touchsmart tm2 (laptop with swiveling touchscreen) appeared, and then the iPad appeared. And rushed. Crowds of Chinese, first on windows xp, then on windows 7, then - on android they began to rivet the tablets. Of course, for the tablets were all sorts of shells, add-ins and so on. Google released Android Honeycomb - tablet-centric, and iOS was drunk for pleasant work with the big screen. Everything went on as usual. Tablets were mobile devices, with mobile OS. However, the giant from Redmond (Microsoft) released Windows 8 in 2012 (as well as its ARM version - Windows RT).
Now you can poke a finger in Windows!


Of course, the first devices on windows 8 were good old laptops, ultrabooks. But the tablets were not long in coming. And now, one of the first, I got a tablet on Windows 8 - Acer Iconia Tab W700.
The first impression of the box: wow! Large box, heavy and roomy.

Roomy is not the right word. Inside, I found: a tablet, a charger, a docking station, a stand, a case, a microHDMI-D-Sub adapter, a keyboard, microfiber, four disks and a stack of papers.
Let's take it in order.

Small retreat


The discs that are included - they are not without reason. If you have something broken or just need to ‚Äúreinstall Windows‚Äù (well, you understand), then Windows 8 has a wonderful and handy tool invented - just press the button in the ‚Äúcomputer settings‚Äù and get the desired in 30-40 minutes. However, here you will find disappointment. To save space on the SSD drive, all the information for recovery has been transferred ... To DVDs. That is, to restore the system you will need an external USB-drive.
I'll tell you separately how to restore the system. Not everything is as simple as it seems.
Among the recovery discs are: Support DVD, Recovery DVD 1, Recovery DVD 2, Recovery DVD (Russian language pack). So here. It is necessary to recover as follows:
1. Choose boot from disk.
2. Insert the Support DVD.
3. Loaded shell Acer eRecovery. There you choose the desired language and what to restore.
4. Remove the support DVD. Insert the Recovery DVD 1.
5. Remove the Recovery DVD 1. Insert the Recovery DVD 2.
6. Take out the Recovery DVD 2. Insert the Revocery DVD (language pack).
7. Take out the Recovery DVD (language pack). Insert a support DVD.
8. Reboot. Waiting for the installation of all Acer utilities.
9. Rejoice.
The procedure takes from an hour to two. Awful, but what to do. Better not to kill the system.

CO (carbon monoxide) detector. What is it and why?



In the days before historical materialism and global warming, when Gazprom had not yet spread its iron tentacles around the planet and stove heating was the main way not to give oak in our harsh climate, one of the most dangerous domestic phenomena was "waste." From it, happened, were lost by the whole families. Why all? But because they did not have such a useful device:

The detector of carbon monoxide from the company Dadzhet designed to signal the presence in the air of dangerous concentrations of carbon monoxide, CO. This is the "carbon monoxide", which is formed during the combustion of carbon or compounds based on it (for example, gasoline) under conditions of lack of oxygen. How is it formed in living conditions?

Two main ways to get angry (do not try this at home):

1. If there are unburned coals in the heated stove or fireplace, and the pipe is closed. The most common cause of the stupor in earlier times was that the thrifty peasants would necessarily close the pipe after the furnace was fired, since part of the heat went through the stove cramping, hitting the hut. If this is done a little earlier than necessary, then the coals in the preheated firebox were ejected with CO.

2. Nowadays, when stove heating is more likely a rarity, and fireplaces, due to a more open construction, are still less prone to the formation of CO, the most frequent cause of the burn is a car that has a running engine in the garage. Especially during the morning warm-up - a cold engine, working on a re-enriched mixture, emits just a huge amount of CO. And, if you think that the open gate will save you - then no, nothing like that. Concentration is growing VERY quickly, it does not manage to be ventilated through the gate. To death, of course, is unlikely to ache, but then the head will hurt so that the living envy the dead.

In general, the detector of this CO itself is more than necessary, especially to me - a person living in a private house with a stove and fireplace, and also having a hobby as a hobby about cars in the garage.
So, the detector from the company "Dadzhet":



Plastic white body, digital LCD indicator, one button, three LEDs, grill siren grille.
The indicator shows two types of data - the air temperature in the room:





... and the CO content in this air is in PPM, that is, parts per million - parts per million. The tolerance table (at what concentration you will need glue for flippers) is in the manual, but from a practical point of view - any concentration that such a detector will detect (from 25 PPM) requires an immediate response from the user - in order to understand where the waste comes from.



There is also a battery charge indicator. The batteries themselves are hidden behind, under the cover of the compartment, which at the same time is a fastening (on the wall) element:



The plastic socket is screwed to the wall with two screws, and the device itself clips onto it.



The batteries in the compartment are spring-loaded with special plastic stops, so when removing the device from the base, they strive to jump out and roll out at the corners.



If the device is disassembled, the electrochemical sensor is visible:



This is an element that responds to the chemical composition of air by changing the conductivity. CO molecules enter into an electrochemical reaction at the electrode and allow the direct linear dependence of the current on the concentration of the measured component to be used.

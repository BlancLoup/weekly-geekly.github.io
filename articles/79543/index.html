The front and rear panels are packed with connectors and buttons. On the front panel even appeared a non-standard sleep-button, which I have not yet seen on similar devices. It should immediately be noted that the buttons do not have unpleasant backlash that take place on the Acer Revo and Wexler VisION-1. Pictures are clickable.

This is what magnets look like when installed. The nettop stand has two perpendicular connectors for installation, one just for the nettop, the other for the nettop with a drive. Quite an ergonomic and spectacular solution. VESA-mount does not interfere with the attachment of the optical drive, but does not hold it in any way, the drive will be mounted exclusively on magnets. The mount complies with VESA 100x100 and 75x75 standards.



It was in such an intricate way that I managed to connect both digital outputs to my work monitors. The adapter mount in the HDMI port was quite flimsy, since the DVI connector interfered with it, however, a slight gap did not prevent the monitors from correctly determining on a nettop.

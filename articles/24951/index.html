Next Gen PC Design Competition

Original post : Dvice (Geeks around!)


Sponsored by the giant Microsoft, the exhibition Next Gen PC Design Competition aims to design the next generation of personal computers based on the Windows operating system. The judges considering the work of the participants, take into account several factors:

- Innovation .

- User experience.

- Aesthetic and usability of the device.

- Possible (technologically) product release date .


This year, participants are designing devices that respond to one simple idea: designing devices that "help people do what they are passionate about." This means that most devices are designed for: artists, designers, architects, programmers, and "just" developers.

An open vote has already been completed, and the results must be announced within this month. And even if we, the Internet users, do not have the opportunity to participate in the voting - most of the devices undoubtedly deserve thousands of curious views. And right now you have the opportunity to gaze at some of them.

1. Maps with GPS navigation.

3. Keeping notes and schedules.
4. Videophone .
5. Built-in photo / video camera .

Another interesting development, called ZEN is designed to help, first of all, people with impaired visual capabilities.

By itself, it is a mixture of software and the latest technology, called Sandbox PC , which can be translated as ‚Äúcomputer-box with sand‚Äù. The analogy with the box is poorly visible, but the ‚Äúsand‚Äù component is indeed present. Together with a specially developed software, this system allows you to use the computer environment with closed eyes.

Innovation ZEN is enclosed in its very name, which stands for " Z-axis Enabled " and this is a truly interactive three-dimensional software environment. This is not just a dirty finger on your monitor; ZEN uses Active Surface technology (active surface) - an electronic surface that can take different forms and be controlled by the user. It is possible to read Braille texts (perforated surface for the blind), to build working windows, icons and various textures. It can be pressed or scratched. All interaction between the user and the computer is made precisely through tactile control.

People with limited visual abilities are currently almost completely unable to use a personal computer, and ZEN gives them that opportunity.

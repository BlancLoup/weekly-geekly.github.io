Software


The same software is installed on the laptop as on the G752VY , so we will not repeat it. The only difference that caught my eye is a slightly different set of available settings in the ROG Gaming Center: you can separately control the speed of the laptop fans and the docking station, activate the headphone amplifier, and disable the touchpad and the ROG key.









A few words about the headphone amplifier. For serious gamers, sound quality is important. Thanks to the high-quality and detailed sound picture, you can detect the enemy in time or dodge the attack. Not to mention the fact that from such a clever notebook you expect not only an appropriate level of performance, but also sound quality. After all, not only the same games live a hardcore gamer, there is still music and movies. Therefore, the ESS Saber digital-to-analog converter and a built-in headphone amplifier are used to enhance the sound reproduction of the GX700VO. The operation of this amplifier and can be controlled from the ROG Gaming Center.

Performance and work experience


The laptop is equipped with the sixth generation Intel's most powerful Intel processor (Skylake), the Intel Core i7 6820HK with 8 MB of third-level cache. The amount of RAM 64 GB, as in the G752VY. This is really a lot, and it will take many more years before there will be games with similar requirements, not to mention more voracious.

The video card used is not a mobile GeForce GTX 980M, but an honest GTX 980 with 8 GB of memory.



Innovation did not bypass the disk subsystem: the GX700VO instead of traditional SSDs and hard drives uses two SSD drives connected via a PCIe x4 bus ( NVM Express protocol) and combined into a RAID0 array. The total capacity of the resulting array is 1 TB. Such a solution has significantly increased the performance of the data storage system (to call it ‚Äúdisk‚Äù is somehow awkward).



The use of such hot components as the top-end processor and video card affected the operation mode of the built-in, own cooling system. When the laptop is working without a docking station, the fans from time to time start to work at higher revs, and the air flow on the radiators creates a noticeable aerodynamic noise. It should be noted that the default processor operates at a clock frequency of 2.7 GHz, (as the load increases, it rises to 3.6), and the operational memory - 2133 MHz. The rest of the time, the laptop is quite quiet, at the level of the G752VY.

With the connection of the docking station - and NWO - everything changes. The processor automatically accelerates to 4 GHz, RAM - up to 2800 MHz. Also accelerates the video card. In other words, the laptop starts to work at its maximum capacity. And very impressive features!

When the docking station is connected, both cooling systems work at the same time - built-in and external. As the load grows, the noise from NWO begins to dominate.

I must say that the docking station itself does not work so quietly. With the normal background noise of the office space, this is not so noticeable, and in a quiet room you can clearly hear the measured buzz of the pump. When radiator fans come into play, you forget about the pump - in order to cool a powerful processor and video card, the fans have to work at fairly high speeds. So the noise from the docking station and the integrated cooling system merge together, and this duet turns out pretty loud. Well, this is the inevitable fee for using top-end overclocked (!) Iron in a compact notebook case.

By the way, overclocking the GX700VO when you connect the docking station is possible not only automatically but also manually. To do this, both hardware and software features are provided, for example, the Turbo Gear tab in the ROG Gaming Center application. Here you can independently set the multipliers and frequencies of the processor and video card. Such a ‚Äúloyalty‚Äù of the system to overclocking will interest not only hardcore gamers, but also fans to squeeze the last megahertz from the hardware.

It is curious that in some games the cumulative noise from the cooling systems was lower than the benchmarks. This is probably due to the artificial nature of the load in the tests, which leads to a more rapid heating of both the processor and the video card.

If you disconnect the laptop from the docking station, the overclocking is turned off and the processor, memory and video card return to standard operation. Note that the ‚Äústandard‚Äù mode is at the level of the most powerful gaming notebook with GTX 980M video card.

The battery life is short, in the surfing mode the laptop lasted about 2.5 hours, and when playing shooters - a little less than 1.5 hours. Well, the power consumption of the ‚Äúfilling‚Äù is matched by its heat release. GX700VO sharpened for maximum performance, without any compromises. And reducing the frequencies when you turn off the docking station is a forced step, without which the heating level would be unacceptable. Yes, and the very NWO is not easy, judging by the size of radiators and noise level.

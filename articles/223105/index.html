Hackspaces of China, part 2. Shenzhen, TechSpace, photo tour



Today is Friday, tomorrow is a weekend, someone will go on a holiday in nature, someone will be in the garage with a car, someone at home at the computer, and someone will spend them in hackspace assembling robots in the company of geeks).

In the last article, we began to talk about Chinese hackspaces, in this article we will take a short tour of TechSpace in Shenzhen (by the way Apple products are made in this city), see what they have interesting, plunge into the working atmosphere.



The founder's name is Shang Song. The idea to open Techspace was born in November 2012. In May 2013, he traveled to the United States at techshop.ws. In July 2013 I found the premises, in November 2013 Techspace opened in beta mode. Since January, they have taken into account the mistakes reformatted the tariff scale, taking into account the real needs of people (about tariffs at the end of the review).

Conditions




Now a little capitalism.

Annual membership:

Individual - 200 yuan (1.1 tr, at the rate of 1 yuan = 5.5 rubles)
Group of up to 5 people - 500 yuan (2.75 tr)

Gives the right to:
- Attend events
- Work in a common room 8 hours a week

Events are held about once a week. You can visit them without being a member, but then you need to leave one time from 20 to 100 yuan (110-550 rubles) as a donation.

Base membership is specifically made so limited in its ability to attract as many people as possible.

Once you are already a member, you can purchase additional packages of services.

Service packages:

DIY - 400 yuan for 10 visits (2.2 tr)
- Gives the right to work with all equipment during the visit

Startup - 900 yuan per month (4.95 TR)
- You can work in the common room every day
- Gives the right to work with the equipment

Office - 1500 yuan per month (8.25 tr.)
- You can work in a dedicated workplace on the second floor every day
- Gives the right to work with the equipment
- Gives the right to use office equipment

Plan to pay off in a year.

Impression



As a conclusion, we note that hackspace is very well thought out, made specifically for people to make working as comfortably as possible (spacious rooms, air conditioning, infrastructure), and everything needs equipment nearby.

I would like to wish them development (early payback), and there would be more such a level of hackers in other cities (in particular, in Russia).

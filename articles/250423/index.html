How I created a mobile fighting game for iOS

Chapter 1. Thank you, Michael Dudikoff


As a child I loved to dream about who I would become when I grow up. Which path to choose: space or ocean depths? No, I wanted to create games. Why games? I grew up at a time when the mind of any boy was poisoned by a computer boom and the first console battles. Years passed, my dream fell asleep, crushed by working days. And then one day ...

March 2014. 7 am in the usual Moscow gym. I lift dumbbells, suppressing sleepy discontent with conversations with local Hohmachs. Here he suddenly entered into our conversation.



Boys and girls born at the dawn of the USSR well remember the nasal monophonic translation from the erased videotapes. And here is one of the holders of the voice appears on the TV screen, suspended from the ceiling. Michael reminded me of how funny the old ninja films were throwing shurikens. A stack of stars in one hand, the other is talted over it with sliding movements to hit the enemy between the eyes.

The level of injuries inflicted on the opponent depends on the accuracy of each throw. At the same time, no less deadly stars fly at you. It is impossible to dodge, but you can eliminate the opponent first and "survive." Each battle conducted increases the gaming experience and personal skill. Gaining experience, you get the opportunity to use shuriken with a greater striking force. A complete set for fun activities.

In the role of rivals - only living people. Users from anywhere in the world. Cool to fight with friends, but you can fight with a stranger. Such is the one-on-one minute stabbing.

Video. ‚ÄúAnd what‚Äôs the point of the video,‚Äù you ask. Recently, Apple has introduced such a fashionable feature, video preview. The sharp katana, like the shuriken, is a reliable companion of the shadow warrior, and the high-quality video is of high conversion.

You have only 30 seconds to show all the benefits of the game. Potential users are obstinate and demanding people, so it was decided to make a video without fail.

The requirements and searches on the Runet described in the Apple Guide did not clarify and did not provide answers. The method of trampled down rake showed:

- Gameplay needs to be shown vertically;
- Without announcer voice;
- Without extraneous objects, especially the iPhone itself;
- Duration no more than 30 seconds;
- The size of the source is not more than 500mb;
- Format exclusively MOV with ProRes codec;
- Aspect ratio for current devices;
- Strictly no more than 30FPS;
- Without excess water, dynamically show the essence;
- You can upload video only if you have a Mac and the latest version of the browser with the OS.

By the way, make a reservation: compliance with all requirements is mandatory. Otherwise, you may encounter a situation where your video in the release will NOT appear.

Virtual server with DDoS protection on VPS.house



Many already know the concept of a DDoS attack , but not everyone has come across it and knows how to deal with such attacks correctly.

Almost always, the attacker sets the task of making your online resource (website, game server, etc.) unavailable - that is, your VPS itself , while the goal of obtaining private data is not.

DDoS-attack is very simple / cheap and effective, anyone can take it to a small online store directly from their home, losses can reach hundreds of thousands of rubles from idle time, especially if you actively launch advertising campaigns .

The attacker does not even need to know how and what works on your server, he can simply send parasitic traffic to his IP address and your VDS server will be guaranteed to receive it. These are the most common cases of attacks, when either SYN-flood is used, which overloads network equipment with SYN-requests in the TCP protocol, or the entire channel of the UDP-flood server is simply blocked.

The administrator unprepared for this frantically begins to create rules in the firewall on the server or the network equipment preceding it, but all this is in vain, because the firewall, the router, will always receive all packets and only then will not be allowed to your application, the communication channel itself remains overloaded.

When attacking you, your provider (data center) suffers no less than you - traffic can be huge, even relatively weak attacks with a strength of 10 Gbit / s significantly impair the work of a network of small data centers and, accordingly, all the clients located there.

Unfortunately, it is impossible to cope with a DDoS attack on your own or by networkers in the data center. About 15 years ago, when rarely any attacks reached 1 Gbit / c, you could feel at ease while on the 10 Gbit / c channel. Now only geo-distributed protections work - when the traffic is filtered not at the point of the final addressee, but on all routes through the communication channels of the main providers. It is considered almost impossible to score 100% of all communication channels of all providers, and even in a number of countries.

IoT standards, networks, three tables

The subject of the Internet of Things has previously slipped at RIT ++ and HighLoad ++ conferences, but usually with a focus on the usual data storage or programming issues. But last year we decided that it was time to seriously discuss IoT: hardware, data networks, industrial applications, and held the first InoThings ++ 2018 conference .

According to the participants, we realized that we need not just a conference on the Internet of things - there are already a lot of them - the community needs an event completely free from marketing and stories about bright horizons, but aimed at solving problems of real projects.

InoThings ++ 2018 showed that expectations were justified, the event was 5 points out of 5. How it was, look and read in the article by Oleg Artamonov , the head of the program committee and a participant in all events. This year, already on April 4 , we will do even better. Our speakers are exclusively practitioners, and in the program only important topics. What exactly, read under the cut.



At InoThings ++, developers, architects and managers at all levels speak and communicate, practitioners - those who work with their hands and head, who build, develop, implement and sell IoT systems. This year there will be even more practitioners: technical directors of leading companies and telecom corporations, leading analysts, experts, doctors of science, heads of laboratories and government representatives. From regalia dizzy, so we will not list all. Just come to the conference - see for yourself.

The interestingness of the speeches does not depend on the size of the room, but the topic depends. Thematically, the Program Committee divided the conference into three parts. Three multi-colored tracks are three themes: standards and state regulation, project implementation and the development of devices and systems . Each part has its own theme color on the schedule .

One round table was planned for each hall to discuss the pain points in IoT, get expert opinion and talk to the speakers - prepare questions. About the round tables later, but for now let's go through the topics of reports, which are conventionally divided into 6 blocks .

InoThings ++ in blocks


In the Security and Cryptography block we will talk about authentication and threats. Why traditional methods are vulnerable to attacks and are not particularly convenient in deployment. We will discuss the main threats to IoT devices, scenarios of confronting them, why cryptography in IoT is inevitable and how to ensure the security of the entire IoT through legal regulation, regulations and industry expert associations.

Technology is ahead of regulation. While relative vacuum reigns in IoT, there are few norms, restrictions and attention from regulators. Soon it will end. Round tables


Each round table is tied to one of the blocks, but we will tell about them separately. The first table is, in principle, the first discussion in Russia of state participation in the IoT market. All interested parties will be present: developers, companies and the state. Oleg Artamonov spoke in detail about the event in a separate article : the background to the issue, the motives for organizing a large-scale public discussion and a long-long list of experts and values ‚Äã‚Äãin the IoT environment, from doctors of technical sciences to directors of telecom corporations.

The second is about a paradigm shift : why the classic approach to supply ready-made equipment and standard communication services does not work, who is to blame and what to do . Sibintek, Geyser Telecom, MTS, Concern Goodwin are participating.

The third table is about microelectronics . About domestic microelectronics and its prospects. The round table has not been finalized yet, but preliminarily there will be Baikal Electronics and SPC Elvis.

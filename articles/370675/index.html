Overview of Intraoral Dental 3D Scanners



This material is a translation of an article about intraoral dental 3D scanners that were exhibited at the IDS 2017 dental equipment exhibition.




Visitors to the International Dental Exhibition in Cologne, Germany, saw a lot of new devices and programs for the work of dentists.



The abundance of new exhibits at the exhibition is no longer surprising, but this year we were shown an unprecedented number of digital applications, both with standard functionality and conceptually fresh ones. The exhibition reflected the growth of competition between manufacturers, which is a plus for the end user.



The article, published immediately after the exhibition in Chinese, was later supplemented with fresh information.



The article discusses the intraoral scanners presented at IDS 2017, a description is given for each device, based on first use impressions, and a comparative table of scanner characteristics.
Note:

Ratings in the article are subjective, they do not take into account such criteria as cost, device design, their programmable functions and differences in the question of the target audience for which they were originally designed.

Review Criteria


Scan speed

Most intraoral scanners use light to obtain images, the quality of this light is reduced when the device is actively working in the oral cavity. Also, the tissues of the oral cavity, often quite mobile, used solutions and the presence of metal surfaces in the scanner affect the scanning speed.

Fast scanning reduces the level of errors that occur with increased bleeding and interaction with soft tissues. An effective scanner should have a good signal-to-noise ratio ‚Äî a parameter that determines the optimal scanning speed.

Scanning head size

The smaller size of the scanning heads makes it possible to scan hard-to-reach areas of the oral cavity. Arguments in favor of larger scanning heads - greater grip, i.e. - increase scan speed.

Ease of use

Simplicity and ease of use depend on optimizing the firmware, the shape and weight of the scanner itself, and the controls. Some devices require the use of special powders. Evaluation of this criterion is associated with the elimination of delays and the need to pause the scan.

Image acquisition

We evaluated image acquisition by the quality of scanning from the first time, by the presence of complete software and the possibility to use the obtained model without additional processing.

Other features interesting to users were also taken into account:

  • Existence of the touch control panel
  • Do you need special powders?
  • Remote control
  • Color rendition
  • Tint function

TRIOS 3 (3Shape)





  • Very fast scanner;


  • Updated software and filling line, introduced a wireless model;


  • The wireless model works as briskly as the wired scanners of this manufacturer;


  • Software update allows you to control the scanners remotely;


  • There was a digital monitoring function, the scanner can be used to examine the oral cavity;


  • More intuitive interface.



Price: 28,000 euros




CEREC Omnicam (Dentsply-Sirona)






  • Omnicam scanners have increased in speed thanks to the new processor and software optimization;


  • The scanning head has not changed - one of the smallest on the market, it is larger than the scanning elements in 3M and Dental Wings devices;


  • Omnicam is not inferior in speed and efficiency even to the recognized leader in these indicators - 3Shape. This is achieved by lower resolution of the preliminary rendering, which reduces the load on the processor and speeds up the processing;


  • In the CEREC software versions 4.4 and 4.5, the function of automatic cutting of tissues unnecessary in the scan is improved, in which 3Shape were previously ahead of the rest of the planet;


  • In the latest CEREC software, post-processing is performed during scanning, which speeds up the production of the finished model.

CS3600 (Carestream)







  • Compared to CS3500, which scanned on the basis of static images, CS3600 uses video, which increases efficiency;


  • Scan speed is not high, but acceptable;

  • The software still suffers from such shortcomings as the impossibility of simulating several restorations simultaneously.

DWOS (Dental Wings)







  • DWOS scanning heads are distinguished by their original design and additional sensors located at an angle relative to the central chamber;

  • Ability to control software by hand gestures: for example, it is enough to hold your finger in front of the screen to control the peripheral camera;


  • The gesture control system resembles the 3Shape remote control system;


  • The scanning head is equipped with light indicators that warn of difficulties in the scanning process - the user is forced to constantly look at the scanner during the work;


  • Real-time data synchronization between the scanner and the computer provides automatic display of implants on the model;

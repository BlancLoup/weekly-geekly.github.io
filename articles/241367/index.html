Truck Monitoring, or ESTR vs. Order No. 36 of the Ministry of Transport of the Russian Federation


To compare such a large and diverse market segment as a freight transport, you need to find those common technologies that are used both in the West and in Russia. And they are - tachographs. The volume of the freight transport market in Russia is estimated by at least 3 million cars. Moreover, the regulation of the tachograph market (as well as emergency response systems) in Russia and the European Union is carried out by the state. The ESTR and the 36th order of the Ministry of Transport of the Russian Federation are two documents that contain requirements that tachographs must meet, and requirements for work and rest schedules that drivers must comply with. We will not dive into the details of the work and rest schedules and the design features of various tachographs, but consider the possibilities that tachographs provide when interacting with the vehicle monitoring system.

A tachograph is a special device that registers the speed of movement, the distance traveled and the mode of work and rest of the driver. Why these parameters? Because the maximum damage is caused by accidents involving trucks, and the main causes of such accidents are driver fatigue or exceeding the maximum speed that is acceptable for this type of vehicle.



Online analysis of tachograph data will allow timely response to violations. From the Russian tachograph manufacturers, two companies can be distinguished that make their products as open as possible for M2M monitoring systems. This Incotex and Atol. The first specialized applications for online monitoring of transport based on tachographs have already begun to appear. SC Scout recently made an announcement that they are working on an application that will upload data from the tachograph and analyze it.

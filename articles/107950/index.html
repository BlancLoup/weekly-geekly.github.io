HP Lab at British Design High School

On November 1, a new laboratory class was opened at the British Higher School of Design in Moscow, on the basis of which HP and the School will regularly hold free workshops and presentations on the modern practice of digital art and design. Specialists from nVidia and Autodesk are already preparing their events for the new class.

And anyone can also work for free at the Laboratory on their projects and communicate with the staff of the School. Already, the class is equipped with several HP Z400 workstations with Xeons and Quadro FX on board, as well as professional software.




For many years, HP has actively collaborated with studios, galleries, universities, colleges, and bright art and design professionals. In my opinion, the purpose of this communication, besides the understandable desire of business to listen to consumers of its products, is to be able to touch the inexhaustible reserves of creative energy and inspiration that roam in creative circles.

And if this is done all over the world, then in Russia, where interest in professional design still catastrophically does not match its potential, it would be criminal to not use any opportunity to make this topic more popular. Therefore, this project HP and BHSAD (oo, I desperately like their site !) Seems to me absolutely necessary and useful.



If you have time and desire to look at the Laboratory with your own eyes, then you can come to the School on any working Monday from 10 to 17 o'clock. It is best to call in advance and warn about your visit, but you can sign up directly at the reception. You need to take a passport with you. All contacts and the address of the School can be found here: www.britishdesign.ru/content/contacts_eng

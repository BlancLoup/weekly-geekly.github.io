Let's make "Roscosmos" a little kinder

May 10, 2013 in Australia will be a solar eclipse. The lunar shadow will pass from southwest Australia to the northeast to the Solomon Islands and go to the Pacific Ocean. The trajectory of the shadow movement is such that part of this path will be captured by the Russian meteorological satellite Electro-L, which is located in geostationary orbit at an altitude of 36 thousand km.



The satellite constantly "hangs" over one point of the Earth and removes the entire visible disk of the planet with details up to 1 km per pixel every 30 minutes. Thanks to this, it will be possible to follow the movement of the lunar shadow during an eclipse. But the satellite can shoot twice as fast, i.e. do 1 frame in 15 minutes. In this case, it would be possible to make even a short video about how the solar eclipse passes on the planet. The problem is that Roskosmos is not going to change the mode of the satellite operation - there is no scientific expediency. But Roscosmos does not yet know about us and about our interest in space. Therefore, there is a proposal for him to tell about it and ask him to change the Electro-L operating mode.

The State Duma adopted bills on fakes and disrespect for the authorities in the second reading

Today, State Duma deputies supported in the second reading bills on punishment for distributing fake news and manifestation of disrespect for government bodies, state symbols, society and the state in Internet publications.

For the second reading, the deputies prepared amendments to the aforementioned bills, as reported by RBC. In particular, network media are given the opportunity to independently remove false information in order to avoid blocking. In addition, traditional media was taken out of the project - registered media with a corresponding license or certificate.

It was decided to introduce fines for the distribution of fakes, which created a threat to harm the life or health of citizens, in the event that the actions do not contain a criminal punishment. The amount of fines for citizens is from 30 thousand to 100 thousand rubles, for officials - from 60 thousand to 200 thousand rubles, for legal entities - from 200 thousand to 500 thousand rubles.

Clarified the point that information, access to which will be limited, should not only show obvious disrespect, but also "offend human dignity and public morality."

Bills to combat fake news and disrespect for the government were submitted to the State Duma in December 2018 by a group of parliamentarians headed by the chairman of the committee on

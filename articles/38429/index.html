Black desktop on pirated windows

Microsoft came up with a new way to deal with pirated copies of Windows XP Professional. Owners of unregistered copies of the product wallpaper on your desktop will be forcibly painted in black . After that, the user can return the old wallpaper, but the process of "blackening" will be repeated every 60 minutes. In addition, an inscription appears in the lower right corner of the black desktop stating that this copy of Windows has not been validated.

The inscription will be transparent and through it you can click on the "Trash" icon.



The company is currently updating the Windows Genuine Advantage (WGA) engine to reflect the new changes.

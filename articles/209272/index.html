In Photoshop added support for 3D printing and Perspective Warp.



Adobe today announced a major update to the Photoshop CC image editor. The main change is support for 3D printing. It should be noted and the announcement of a stunning new mode of perspective deformation of photos (Perspective Warp).

However, first things first. Now Adobe is positioning Photoshop as the ideal intermediate between creating a 3D model and printing it. That is, it is supposed to create a model in a third-party specialized program, but in Photoshop you can bring it to mind (for example, add legs technologically necessary for support in a printer) - and send to print. Maybe in the future, Adobe will release a 3D modeling tool.



Photoshop imports all standard formats: OBJ, STL, 3DS, Collada and KMZ. The company has entered into an agreement with MakerBot and now works directly with 3D printers manufactured by this company. If you do not have a printer, then the order can be sent to print in Shapeways , with which Adobe also has an agreement. By the way, this service supports printing multi-colored models, so you can print beautiful toys.

In addition to MakerBot, the graphics editor supports other popular models of 3D printers.

In addition to 3D printing, among the new features is a new pencil in Illustrator, with which it is easier to draw lines by hand, support for the EPUB format in InDesign, export fonts from Typekit to the desktop environment, where they are available for any other program.



One of the most remarkable innovations is editing photos in Perspective Warp mode.


Editing a photo in Perspective Warp mode

In this mode, you can kind of "rotate" the photo, look at the object from a different angle. Surely, designers will appreciate this creative and very easy to use tool.



Photoshop CC (Creative Cloud) - a paid cloud service. Adobe today announced that it will restart the 30-day free period for all users , including those who have already used up their free limit before the service Photoshop CC was launched in May 2012.

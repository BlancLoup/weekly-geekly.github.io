RFID in brief


What RFID (Radio Frequency IDentification) is and what it is eaten with is explained and shown here . Soon I will try to make a detailed review of the material accumulated in recent years. Stay in touch , but for now let us return to our sheep fur coats ...

Gray fur coats unexpectedly settled


What is actually fuss? From January 1, 2016, the Government of the Russian Federation obliged all suppliers and sellers to chip fur products for accounting in the marking system of the tax service. The project was launched to run technologies, solutions and administer product verification using RFID chips.

The very note about this was published in November 2016, but it caught my eye quite by accident. According to the figures, I quote:
In just 8 months, the number of fur coats sold in Russia increased 16 ( sic! ) Times compared to 2015.

Just think, 16 times !!!

As of the end of 2016, it was possible to legalize about 20% of market participants, and for one it was possible to chide the Accounting Chamber, which provided data to the Ministry of Industry and Trade of only 400,000 products subject to mandatory chipization, while the real RFID order exceeded 6 million pieces.

Each tag contains information about the origin and movement of a particular fur product. Labels operate in the UHF range and comply with ISO / IEC 18000-63, EPCglobal Gen2v2 standards.

Also, from January 1, 2017, the Ministry of Industry and Trade launched an experiment on (so far) voluntary labeling of medicines, additional chipping of light industry goods (in particular, footwear), precious wood, air components, and so on is being further developed.

As you, my dear Habrayusers, you understand, this is not only valuable fur just chipping and product accounting, but on top of this model a block chain is superimposed, and the codes embedded in RFID become unique product identifiers. Accordingly, the introduction of these technologies does not allow in the future to use any ‚Äúcalculators‚Äù of RFID numbers and makeshift recording devices for tag falsification. This is undoubtedly a great achievement of Russian science and technology in the practical implementation of tags, which is currently not equal anywhere in the world.

The future of RFID in Russia: drugs, museums and much more


The developer of the RFID equipment and the promotional company in the Russian Federation is the RST-Invent portfolio company RosNano. Therefore, having collected several press releases from RosNano and RST-Invest, we will look into the future of radio frequency identification in Russia.

Automatic accounting of museum and library storage facilities


So, the Fund for Infrastructure and Educational Programs (FIEP) of RosNano initiated a project in which employees of cultural institutions, private collectors, representatives of security companies and IT companies can learn how to use modern radio frequency identification (RFID) technologies to protect, record and control the movement of art objects.

In particular, in the fall of 2016, it was announced that the technology engineering company FIEP ‚ÄúIdentification Technology‚Äù will develop an RFID system for the A.S.S. Pushkin.

The implementation of RFID will begin with the education of museum staff in the field of contactless radio frequency identification. It is planned that the first pilot group, which will include about 100 people, will complete training in September 2017, that is, very soon.

As for the libraries, the first pilot project was launched in the STC-GasProm library in St. Petersburg , where storage, accounting and distribution of books are carried out automatically, thanks to RFID.

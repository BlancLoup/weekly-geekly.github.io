Facebook's board of directors has proposed that the founder and head of the social network, Mark Zuckerberg, be deprived of control over the company if he decides to step back from direct management or leave the company. It is reported by Reuters, referring to the appeal filed by the Board of the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC).

At the general shareholders meeting of Facebook, the board of directors will offer shareholders to approve the decision to convert class B shares belonging to Zuckerberg if they leave the post of general director of the company. The meeting will take place on June 20th.

Class A shares are traded on the stock exchange and have the right to vote, class B shares are owned only by the persons involved in the company and have 10 votes on the piece. As of June 2, Zuckerberg owned 4 million Class A shares and 419 million Class B. In total, they give him 53.8% of the vote.

"The new conditions guarantee that we will not remain under the control of the founder of the company after he no longer leads us," explained such a move on Facebook's board of directors.

Young - in the road


While mature and sophisticated personnel experience is changing the IT reality across the country, young and fledgling people are looking for opportunities to become part of this reality. It is not surprising that large companies do not get tired of providing them with such opportunities: they need new personnel.

Mail.Ru Group has launched the third annual championship for the creation of IT projects among students of MSU, MIPT and Bauman Moscow State Technical University "Technostart". The finalists of the competition will receive grants for 400 thousand rubles, the main prize will be the opportunity to undergo acceleration in the French start-up accelerator Numa .

Only students of Moscow State University, MIPT and Bauman Moscow State Technical University can take part in the competition. Applications are accepted until June 13 at the competition website . The Mail.Ru Group noted that participants do not necessarily have experience in the field of programming and development - they can "try themselves in the role of product managers, designers or marketers."

The championship will take place in several stages. After registering the projects, experts from Mail.Ru Group, Numa Moscow and the MSU business incubator will select 25 ideas, which will pass to the second stage. Five more projects will be chosen by the participants.

In the framework of the second stage, the contestants will have to present their projects to the experts in the Moscow office of Mail.Ru Group. In the third final stage will be 12 teams. The championship final will be held in September - the contestants will have to submit ready-made products.

Google, Verizon, Sprint and Skype provide free connection to France.



A number of telecommunication companies, telecom operators offer subscribers the opportunity to make a free call or send a message to France. In this way, companies help people find out if everything is fine with their relatives and friends who are now in France. As previously reported, a number of terrorist attacks were carried out in Paris, as a result of which 129 people died and many more were injured.

In these conditions, communication is very important - people can make sure that with their families and friends who are now in Paris, everything is in order. Calls to Europe from the same USA are not so cheap, therefore telecommunication companies decided to help their clients. This is how it happens.

Google




Google Inc. was one of the first to offer free communications to France. The company suggests using Hangouts to contact subscribers in this country. Everything is simple here - dial the number and contact the subscriber. At the same time, applications work both under iOS and Android, programs work on any network.

Skype




No google hangouts? Then it's time to use the more popular Skype. Now the company, which is one of the divisions of Microsoft, has decided to provide free communication with subscribers in France. This decision is relevant now, and will work a few more days.

Verizon





This American company did not remain indifferent to the tragedy, and decided to open the possibility of making free communications (calls and sms) from the USA to France. Actually all this is just two days.

Sprint




Telecom giant Sprint has joined the above companies. The company offers free calls and SMS to France, with the final date for this promotion has not yet been determined.

As a conclusion, I would like to say that these companies, whatever the motives of their actions, still do the right thing. It is still unknown how many people suffered, and if there are acquaintances, friends or family members in France, it is better to clarify the situation.

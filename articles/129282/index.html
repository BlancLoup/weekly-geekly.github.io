Motorola Droid 3 - review of the thinnest dual-core qwerty slider

I present to your attention a review of Motorola Droid 3, this is a dual-core qwerty slider running on Android.
I will try to focus on the review of the device itself, trying not to affect Android.


First, a few words about the device itself, what it is.
Droid 3, is the first dual-core qwerty slider, with the thinnest. It has a qHD display and not least a 5-row keyboard. It is available for the Verizon operator and is locked for it, but by purchasing the unlock code you can unlock the device and it will work with other operators. Droid can work both in GSM and in CDMA.

Personally, I bought a Droid 3 through a friend, and an unlock code on ebay.
At the moment, everything works.

Now to the review itself!

  • Wi-Fi: 802.11b, g, n

For me, Droid 3 is currently the most beautiful device among all on the market. In order not to argue, rate yourself on the photos. Also, I will not describe the obvious, saying the camera is there, then there, etc.



Now a little about the mechanics.
About controls. We have 4-D universal touch buttons to control the android. For several days of use, they have never failed me, they always work clearly. When for example on the Samsung Galaxy S touch buttons worked for me through time.
On the mechanism of the slider. It slides well and nicely, there are latches in the closed and open position. It is impossible to close the phone accidentally, but to open it quite easily with a flick of the finger. This is a bit annoying, because sometimes you start to open the slider randomly. Everything else is top notch, the design does not play and has a pleasant, non-annoying sound.
About the keyboard. I want to immediately note that I really, really liked the keyboard. It is more convenient than the Nokia N900, Desire Z, Motorola Milestone. I can tell you this for sure, because I owned these phones. On the Droid 3, we have a 5-row keyboard. Especially liked the "arrows", very convenient for text editing. Screen


Our test 4-inch qHD screen, that is, with a resolution of 540 x 960. But there is one BUT!
The screen has Pentile technology, many people praise it, and many scold it. Personally, I scold her and I think this is the main drawback of the phone. I will not breed Holivar about Pentile, just because many people like it.
In the sun and in clear weather, the screen reads well, there is enough brightness. Viewing angles are also sufficient for comfortable work.
The sensor is responsive and does not cause complaints, the screen takes up to 4 touches.
In general, everything is on the level, everything is done as it should.

Performance


As I said, the Droid 3 has 2 cores of 1 GHz and a video accelerator PowerVR SGX540.
I did not notice the increase in the speed of work in Android itself or programs, if we compare it with the same Galaxy S. However, in the browser 2 cores make themselves felt: flash, scripts, Java, etc., work a level faster. In games, Droid behaves very well and wins the performance of all single-core gadgets. But it is not prohibitively powerful, SGS II is faster. Although I think a person who buys a Motorola Droid 3 will not play games day and night. This phone is still more business class.
Yesterday I tried a few of the most demanding games, everything goes smoothly and smartly. So far, nothing slows down.

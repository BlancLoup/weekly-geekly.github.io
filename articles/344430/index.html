Portrait of a tester: employment, place of work, experience


The survey involved 373 respondents, two of whom work in freelancing, and the rest - office workers, more than two-thirds of whom work full time. But at the same time, 99.5% of respondents are on full-time (hereinafter, the results will be given as a percentage).


My questions were answered by specialists from Russia, Ukraine, Belarus, the USA, Great Britain, Poland, Germany, Israel, as well as representatives of transnational companies.

Another cut is the size of the companies in which the respondents work. Thus, 34% of respondents represent very large companies with more than 500 employees, 28% from large firms. From organizations with a staff of up to 100 people received 18% of responses from the total amount, about the same - 17% - employees from small companies (up to 50 people). Finally, 1.5% of respondents represent a very small employer with a staff of 10 people or less.

Testers salary market: where to find "fish" places


So, I ‚Äúhooked up‚Äù Dudya and asked my colleagues a number of questions regarding their salary, additional motivation, job satisfaction and income. And that's what we did.

Salaries in the IT labor market vary widely: the lower and upper limits of the "plug" of remuneration in one city may differ several times. C'est la vie, as the French say. But the overall picture is:


Thus, the majority of respondents receive a salary in the range of 20-50 thousand rubles. And this is not the lowest income: about 6% of respondents earn less than 20 thousand rubles. And further downward - the higher the income, the less lucky.

And what about extra motivation?
Surprisingly, in more than half of the respondents, the results of the work do not affect the additional promotion.


What kind of motivation is this? As a rule, the employer provides a monthly increase, quarterly bonus or annual bonus. As we can see, the majority is encouraged by only one kind. Less often, an employee receives two types of motivation. Incredibly, there was even one respondent who was swimming in the pool of money, getting an increase every month, quarter and year!

But then the comparison is not only a simple level of income, but its size in relation to the place of residence. Quite often, communicating with colleagues from other regions, one can hear the phrase that they, while performing the functional work activity, similar to an employee of the Moscow or St. Petersburg office, receive many times less.

Consider the correlation of the level of wages depending on experience and region.
The majority of those working in the capital, whose salary varies from 20 to 50 thousand rubles, have less than a year of experience and are mainly engaged in manual testing. In the northern capital, this salary is received by approximately the same number of employees with less than a year experience and 1-3 years experience. The bulk of St. Petersburgers are also engaged in manual testing. In the regions, a smaller number of specialists receive a similar salary than in the capitals, while they are engaged in manual testing and have work experience of up to a year.

The most popular among QA-specialists salary is from 50 to 70 thousand rubles. If we consider Moscow and St. Petersburg, then the majority of employees who receive such income have 1-3 years experience and are engaged in manual testing, but in St. Petersburg there are those who combine manual testing with management. A similar picture of salary (1-3 years of experience, manual testing) is also in the regions.

The salary level from 70 to 90 thousand rubles for manual testers is very common in capitals with work experience of 1-3 years, but in the regions few people receive such salary, and that is subject to a longer experience.

And if a salary of 90 thousand rubles and more can be obtained in Moscow by doing only manual testing, then in the regions the requirements are higher: a comparable income level implies the skills of automated testing. True, the situation is balanced by the fact that a higher salary in the region will require less experience, albeit in a more complex form of testing.

Prize or money: what to choose

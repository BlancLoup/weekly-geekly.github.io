Oracle buys high-profile Dyn provider in hopes of bypassing Amazon in the cloud market



Oracle continues to grow in the cloud services market. It is known that the founder of the company Larry Ellison wants to shift Amazon from the position of a leader in this market.

The next step in the fight against a competitor was the purchase of the American DNS provider Dyn. This will allow the company to expand the reach of its cloud solutions. On the sale of Dyn became known on Monday, November 21.

Fortune journalist Dan Prymak suggested that this is a sum of exceeding $ 600 million. According to a Fortune source close to the deal, Oracle will pay for this purchase from $ 600 to $ 700 million.

The publication notes that in its history Dyn has attracted no more than $ 100 million investment. Not only Oracle claimed to buy Dyn: IBM was also interested in this company, an informed source told Fortune.

Dyn has over 3,500 corporate clients, including Netflix, Twitter, Pfizer and CNBC.

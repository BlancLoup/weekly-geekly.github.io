The Ministry of Industry and Trade has published lists of manufacturers who will receive government subsidies for the development of electronics



In late September, the Ministry of Industry and Trade published two lists of organizations that will receive government support in the development of electrical equipment. The relevant protocols were posted on the official website of the Office.

Conventionally, the lists can be divided into manufacturers of "thin" electronics and hardware computing systems and manufacturers, whose products will ensure the development of infrastructure and the creation of telecommunications equipment.

The amount of subsidies for 2016 for 46 approved out of 54 applications submitted under the first protocol of September 26 (telecommunications) and 27 approved out of 29 applications submitted under the second protocol of September 27 (computer equipment) will be 30 billion rubles.

By the end of 2025, the government plans to spend on the development of the industry 173.9 billion rubles.

Of this amount, 42.8 billion rubles are planned to be spent on the development of the telecommunications industry, and the remaining 131.1 billion - on the development of the production of computer technology, according to Vedomosti .

Recall that the above subsidies are allocated only for 2016 .

Due to such strong material support, the Ministry of Industry and Trade plans to reduce the share of imports in the telecommunications industry to 88% by 2020, and to 70% by 2025.

Much worse is the situation in the production of computing equipment. Now the share of imports is close to 100%. According to the plan of the Ministry of Industry and Trade, general subsidies in the amount of 131.1 billion rubles over the next 10 years will allow by 2020 to reduce the share of imports to 97.6%, and by 2025 to 93.3%. At the same time, there is not a single manufacturer of RAM or any other type of memory in the approved application for this year.

Producers believe that the government has taken a unique step to support the production of commercial products instead of the traditional support of scientific research and development for the defense sector.

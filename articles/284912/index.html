MPAA website about legal films watching is not indexed by search engines.



The TorrentFreak website reports that last week MPAA, the American Film Association, launched WhereToWatch.com (where to see), which contains information about the possibility of legally watching movies. And it seems that the base of films is not bad and there is enough information (information about the places where films are viewed is limited to the territory of the USA). At the same time, the site developers obviously had no idea about the basic principles of SEO. As a result, all links to movies are processed using JavaScript, and the major search engines are simply unable to index them. With the help of the BrowSEO service, you can see how the page of this site looks like from the point of view of a search engine. Even in the absence of direct prohibitions in the robots.txt file, the site still remains inaccessible to search engines.

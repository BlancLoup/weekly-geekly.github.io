Overview of useful plug-ins for FL Studio. Part 2: Drums and Bass


Hello, dear inhabitants of the community.

This is a continuation of the review of plug-ins for FL Studio, in the last post it was described about writing rock guitars, plug-ins for writing more realistic drums, as well as some ways to tune and write Bass guitars will be considered.

Drums (Toontrack EZdrummer):


Of course, there is a tool in the set of standard FL Studio tools to write drums somehow, called FPC. The sound of FPC drums is not quite realistic (although not at all realistic), of course there is an opportunity to download additional sounds from the Internet, but the quality will not change much from this. This is where we will need EZdrummer (this is not the only instrument of the drums, but we‚Äôll look at it here).


Libraries were created at Avatar Studios in New York. When recording, real well-tuned drum sets were used.

EZdrummer is designed to create "live" styles of drum parts, the concept of the program is to bring the instrument as close as possible to the live drummer's game. In this plugin there are no newfangled sounds in the style of electronic music. EZdrummer can satisfy the most demanding arranger and sound producer. At its core, EZdrummer is the younger brother of the wonderful BFD program, which is very widespread in music circles. The difference concerns a somewhat simplified interface, where there are no unnecessary details, and less voluminous libraries of sounds. The quality of sound programs differ little, although EZDrummer has 16-bit libraries.

The difference of this program from the usual sampling library is that EZdrummer is a complete integrated solution, it provides not only sounds, but also special style game patterns in the form of prepared MIDI tracks. In addition, the user has a fairly large field of activity in terms of creating a studio sound picture, for example, adding the sound of the room in which the recording was made, adjust the levels of microphones that remove the sound from the drums.

EZdrummer libraries:
  • Pop / Rock (default) - a standard set of drums, often used and the most common.
  • Drumkit From Hell is a library for heavy music lovers. The famous guitar player Mattias Eklund took an active part in creating the library. The library is recorded in Sweden. It contains specific style-specific MIDI files.
  • Vintage Rock Brushes / Sticks - this sound library was created in order to get a rich "vintage" sound of old Ludwig drums, which were once played by the Beatles.
  • Latin Percussion - the use of this library covers a wide range of musical styles from jazz to modern electronic music.

Bass guitar can be written on Slayer 2 , but with the initial settings of this instrument is a bit bad. To bring the sound to a more or less similar to the bass guitar, you need to do a few not tricky settings.

First, pick up the volume of the instrument, by default it is very loud and overloads the channel, then in the Slayer 2 settings select the Bass Ebass 3 or Bass Ebass 4 library and install the cab in the combo (as shown in the figure). You can also conjure with the frequencies bass (bass), middle (middle), treble (high).

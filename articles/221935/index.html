30th anniversary of Mario Bros. is dedicated to. HTML5 Virtual Game Museum

Intel has put a lot of effort into the promotion and development of the HTML5 standard: here we have the release of tools and utilities for developers, the creation of information and training resources, and the support of the thematic communities. We have already spoken about this and we will certainly continue to talk further. Today, on the eve of the holiday, we would like to show what happens when the power of modern technology is combined with the talent of a designer and programmer.
In 2013, it turned 30 years old cult computer game Mario Bros. In commemoration of this solemn event, HTML5 developer Kyle Simpson (Kyle Simpson), with the support of Intel, created a virtual museum of this wonderful game, which shows its entire history - from the 8-bit era to the present. Under the cut are a few large clickable screenshots of the project, but, really, what's the use of looking at screenshots? After all, the whole power of HTML5 is in interaction with the user, and there are plenty of interactions there. Come and enjoy!

DevCon 2015 Conference: Internet of Things in the World of Living Code

Dear friends!

In preparation for DevCon 2015, we are ready to share with you what awaits participants at the conference. In this post I will talk about how for the first time in the entire history of DevCon, we will present the history of the Internet of things (IoT).

But, first, I present to you a cool animated video ‚ÄúWelcome to the World of Live Code at DevCon 2015!‚Äù.



The Internet of Things has already become a hot topic and interest in IoT technologies is only growing. Below you will find the details of the DevCon 2015 program dedicated to the Internet of things.

Reports


Many conference reports will contain a description of the technologies one way or another underlying the Internet of things or helping to work with the huge amount of data that IoT generates. I especially draw your attention to the report of Robert Eicheneser and Alexander Belotserkovsky , which is entirely devoted to the topic.



In the context of the speech ‚ÄúThe Internet Industry of Things in the Real World: A Customer Use Case from the Manufacturing Industry, ‚Äù my colleagues will tell the story of the Internet of Things with specific examples from real life. Conference participants will learn how to build and effectively use Internet of Things solutions in industry and beyond.

Master Class


Separately from the main program, Robert Eichensee will hold a deep master class not for several hours entirely devoted to the subject of the Internet of things. Here is a description of the master class from the author:

A comprehensive look at the Internet of Things
IoT; An End-To-End View

Microsoft offers a complete set of client and server services and technologies for solving problems and challenges that arise today in the world of the Internet of Things.

As part of this workshop, you will create a complete IoT solution using a set of microcontrollers combined with cloud services, such as Azure Event Hubs. You will explore the capabilities and flexibility of using the Azure Stream Analytics data streaming service to explore real-time data (data in motion) and to store it for later analytical processing (data in the repository). We will also try to implement a control channel for remote control of the microcontroller's actions.

Night IoT Hackathon


Finally, in the evening and at night of the first day of the conference, the traditional night hackathon will be held, and this time it will be entirely devoted to the subject of the Internet of things.

Within the framework of this hackathon, participants breaking into teams with their own hands will be able to build solutions based on microcontrollers, sensors and cloud services. We will offer the participant right on the site the opportunity to personally try IoT in practice.

We will share the details of the hackathon later, stay tuned!

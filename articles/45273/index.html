How I ran an analogue of Nigma


We present to your attention the result of one and a half years of experiments - the search engine "Butty"

That's how the post should have started with the PR of my search engine 2 years ago.
The post has already been written and ready for publication, but did not work out :)
Under the cut you are waiting for a lyrical story about the creation and attempts to advance.

Origin

The idea of ‚Äã‚Äãcreating this search engine came into my head right on the day of my majority.
On the same day (2007.08.19) the domain was registered in the zone .ru
Creating a symbiosis of search engines at that time was no longer a novelty, for example, the search engine Nigma occupied 0.4% of the Russian-language search.

I called Yandex and clarified whether they allow the combination of issuance. The answer was positive.
So, for several months, a code was written by a familiar programmer for little money.
For a little money, I received a non-optimized code, as it should be.

Further, over the course of a year, we advertised our search engine several times on the webmaster forums to listen to the opinions of those who understand this.

We rewrote the parsing algorithm three times. Have achieved fast work.
Caching is also present.
Yandex has been parsing officially, in XML, and Google - unofficially :)
At first, there were generally three search engines ‚Äî including Rambler, but I quickly abandoned it.

We took a dedicated server in Moscow. Added weather, YouTube search with the download function and image search (rough parsing of Yandex with the opening of the image immediately, without intermediate transitions).

Developed a portal (a la Yandex). Naturally, with their chips.

We put Ya.Direkt on the page and Yandex allowed us to make up to 10,000 XML requests per day.

But in January 2009, the main programmer went into a long hard drinking, and Yandex blocked us access via XML for what we did not designate in the search - where their output was, and where not theirs. That's all.
For a month of inactivity lost enthusiasm.

Nothing big shone to us.
Although ... we had more than 500 regular users at the peak :)
I myself used the search engine, it was my default browser.

The volume of cross-border sales in the e-commerce market in Russia grew by 55%


Sources: East-West Digital News

In 2015, about a quarter of e-commerce revenues in Russia accounted for purchases in foreign online stores. Most of these stores are owned by Chinese owners. The total volume of the Russian e-commerce market was $ 13.9 billion.

Compared with 2014, in ruble terms, the market grew by 16%, but in dollar terms it decreased by 28%. Excluding foreign retailers, the Russian market is $ 10.5 billion, analysts at East-West Digital News estimated.

In the domestic market, the goods are purchased by the buyer in the Russian online store and do not cross the border after the application is made (for example, when buying on Ozon.ru ).

In total, in 2015, consumers in Russia made 160 million purchases on the domestic e-commerce market - 10% more than a year earlier. On average, the cost of the order amounted to 4050 rubles, which is also higher compared to 2014 (3750). The demand grew most of all for leisure goods, pets and children, clothes, shoes and food products. On the other hand, the demand for electronics, home appliances, cosmetics and perfume decreased in comparison with 2014.

Compared to 2014, cross-border sales in the e-commerce market in Russia ($ 3.4 billion) grew by 55%.

In total, in 2015, Russians made 135 million cross-border purchases. In 2015, Chinese online stores accounted for more than 80% of all cross-border purchases made by Russians. For comparison, in 2014 this figure was 70%.

King I under DOS


The first King was launched in the MS-DOS 2.0 operating environment. How was the game created?
Making card games is easy. It is enough to draw 32 maps and know the screen resolution. The screen at the time of DOS was 640 by 350, the cards were 64 by 96 pixels.



The cover for the cards was pulled from the tapeworm, pictures for the ladies and kings were painted by him, holding before his eyes images of famous Soviet cartoon characters from Winnie the Pooh to Cheburashka. He took pictures from magazines - there were no video cassettes with bright covers at that time. A couple of years later, the first hand scanner appeared - the quality of the image depended on the accuracy of your hand. Whack! Spend a scanner on the magazine and ready. The pictures were black and white and small in size, and the scanner seemed like a miracle.

The game was quite popular, spread across the former Union on diskette. No money brought, at that time there was no money. There were food stamps and men's underpants.

King II under Windows 3.1



The second King was re-released under Windows, made in Visual Studio 2.0 in C ++. The transition to a new paradigm in 1997 was hard for me, I thought in Pascal linearly, and here there were some classes, some events. The brain was not ready. Nevertheless, I made the game under the hard resolution of the Windows-window 640 by 480, the size of the maps remained, 64 by 96 pixels. The quality of art has changed - I did not draw maps, but pulled it out of solitaire.dll. Branded microsoftware images I inserted into the application.

There was a variant of the game with two blockheads and a live opponent. The network game was made for the internal secret structure of our mathematical department (1500 people) and did not go beyond the limits of the institute. For remote game I used MFC sockets.

King III under Windows CE 2.0



The third King was made for iPaq 3700 in 2002. The first handheld computer, a fairy tale. Glitchy, but with Windows. The transition for development from MS VS to MS Embedded Studio was how to transfer from VAZ 2103 to VAZ 2101. Very natural. The size of the cards has been halved in each direction. The new screen size is 240 by 320 forced to change the image. On the surface of the map, only the value and the suit remained. The rest was annoyingly small and not informative. The process of installing the application on the device was also annoying. Many magic dances. The emulator worked well and quickly. Touching the screen was carried out using the stylus. Since then I hate styluses. They hold their hand and get lost. Of course, in half a year, all the owners of handhelds used toothpicks instead of lost styli.

King IV under Symbian



The fourth King was made for the Nokia 9210 in the same 2002 year. It was not just a handheld computer, it was a communicator, a telephone. Legend. Epoc32. The same C ++, but slightly different organization of output bitmaps. Development under Symbian did not cause difficulties. The size of the cards returned to the original, because the screen size was 640 by 200, which reminded the first black and white CGA mode.



The emulator started, but very slowly - each launch was accompanied by a 1-minute wait. Praise be to Allah, the Internet and information appeared, which DLL to take from the computer so that this delay was not. To install Nokia PC Suite, you had to not only dance, but dance with a tambourine and Na-na songs. By the way, the phone is still in working condition.

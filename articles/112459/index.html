How in the Ulyanovsk region "selected" the Minister of IT

Last year there was a competition for the position of Minister of IT. There was a lot of noise about this. How progressive it is! Any fellow could send a resume and win this position in a fair fight.
The media quickly picked up this nano-modernization news.
But the IT minister was appointed, enthusiastically reported , and many of the evidence as it happened on the Internet erased. So what was it?

It all began with messages in the news and in the blog of the Deputy Chairman of the Government of the Ulyanovsk Region Openysheva .
More than a thousand resumes were sent and only the best ones remained.
But let me dwell only on one person, who became the IT minister of the Ulyanovsk region - Elena Balashova. Nothing personal, just facts :) (I am absolutely not interested in the person, so, the critic from the side).

Almost the entire process of passing the contest was written in the LiveJournal by the deputy chairman of the government of the Ulyanovsk region. From LJ Deputy Chairman of the Government of the Ulyanovsk Region:
Passed the next stage of selection of candidates for the post of Minister of Information Technology
openysheva_sv

On November 8, a meeting of the expert commission on the selection of candidates was held. The commission included representatives of the Government of the Ulyanovsk region, higher educational institutions of the region, as well as representatives of large IT companies. 37 applicants reached the fourth stage. At the meeting, members of the commission reviewed and evaluated the projects submitted by them for the development of the IT cluster of the Ulyanovsk region and the innovative capabilities of space and geo-information technologies for the socio-economic development of the region .
The second indicator, assessed by the commission, was the active and informative discussions in the LiveJournal personal blog .
Two key factors are highlighted in bold - the draft presented (until recently, projects were posted on the regional government site, but then disappeared) and activity in LiveJournal (yes, this is ridiculous, but what to do if the governor is a forced fanatic of official blogs; therefore, also a factor in the fight).

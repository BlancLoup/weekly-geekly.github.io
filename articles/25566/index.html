Adobe enters the coalition race - an unexpected update of the service Photoshop Express

Today, users of Adobe Photoshop Express received a notification about the latest system updates. Now the service allows you to publish your photos on third-party resources, providing a link with ready-made html-code and tags. In addition, mention is made of the possibility to arrange a slide show of your photos and publish them on MySpace and Facebook. But this is not the most interesting ...

From now on, Photoshop Express users can import their photos from several leading photo sharing services. To do this, just enter your username and password, agree to import and voila! At the moment, the lists of services from which you can import your photos and drawings are: Flickr, Picasa, FaceBook and PhotoBucket . And no drag and drop, full integration! Just open and edit!




It seems that Adobe decided not to miss the innovative fashion for unification into unions, and in time they rushed to create their own coalition. The Internet market is changing its structure. More and more serious players are entering the new game. First , Data Availability , then Google Friend Connect , and henceforth Adobe. Competition is growing, and with it the number of firms-fellowships. Let's see what happens next. But it is already clear that not only social networks began to adhere to the new policy. In any case, we, the users, are more likely to benefit. The results can be felt today!

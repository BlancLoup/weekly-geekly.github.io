Shooting entertainment TV - take 2 (motor!)

This is the second article in a series on how various entertainment shows are filmed. In the last article, we ended up with the fact that the film crew has at its disposal a studio ready for work, has scenarios, a filming plan and can solemnly start the film-making process.


Start shooting session


So, first of all, somewhere near the studio, a hardware room is being built (in general, it still goes along with the construction of the studio). This is not done by the contracting companies, but by the film crew itself. The hardware room is a room in which the director and a dozen other involved people will be located during the filming. In hardware there is a lot of any iron. In the first approximation, we will assume that there is a video console, a sound console and control monitors of cameras (in fact, even before the devil there is everything - it will be detailed in the third article). Hardware located within 10-50 meters from the studio. Two opposing goals are struggling here: first, it must be close, so that you can rush out to shout at the crowd, talk to the participants, look alive at the scenery, and on the other hand, have to be far away - so that the extra people don‚Äôt wander into the control room sounds and did not affect other distractions. Often, at the request of the sound technicians, the hardware is sheathed with noise-absorbing material.

In parallel, there is a camera exposure in the studio. At the expense of the number of cameras - with less than 3 pieces, you can only shoot a children's matinee. On the other hand, more than 10 pieces can not afford, probably, no one transmission. On average, usually 4-6 cameras. At their location, all sorts of tricky rules are used, in the spirit of the ‚Äú180 degrees rule‚Äù , ‚Äúthe 30 degree rules‚Äù and a whole bunch of other things.

If in simple words - there must be cameras that allow you to take:

The working day starts around 9 am and ends around 10 pm. One shooting session - one and a half to two weeks (sometimes with days off, sometimes without). Two to four programs are shot per day - i.e. for one shooting session, it is possible to ensure about half a year of a weekly broadcast. Film crew


Main director
He is one and omnipresent. The ideologue of the process. The person who receives all the laurels and all the entrances for the transfer. The main boss and director of the universe within the studio. At the time of shooting, he controls little, because, as usual, he himself is behind one of the cameras.

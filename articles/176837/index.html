Crowdinvesting in Russia: how we made the investment platform, what we have already achieved and what projects we can invest in

For more than half a year I have been working on creating a crowd-investing platform. In short, this is a kickstarter, where you can not just give money to cool guys, but invest, that is, get a share in the profits of a future project.

Today I can say that we managed to start crowdinvesting in Russia.

But the road was quite long:
  • First I had to do a very serious legal binding to make it work. More is here and here . We found a way to do everything online, without signing papers.
  • At the same time, a platform was created where project organizers can upload their information, where they can follow the teams they like and directly invest in one click.
  • Then it was necessary to find the first projects that want to participate in equity investment. We reviewed the order of hundreds of different options - from the bakery in Tver to all sorts of IT-portals. Almost everyone in the system is now closely connected with IT. Here is a checklist of questions for each project.
  • And finally, it was necessary to overcome the enormous resistance of people who do not believe in crowdinvesting. There was a feeling that all those who have never worked with investments consider this an exquisite way of withdrawing money from the population.

Today we have a ready-made solid legal base, a technical platform with the ability to receive funds on the fly, the first projects (past rigorous selection) and quite a few applications of interest to buy a share in the profits of different companies.

Below, I will direct directly the projects that we currently have.

Backatyou


The project is being developed to structure the process of exchanging comments and working materials between the contractor and the customer. Unlike task trackers (Basecamp, Megaplan), the project is aimed at working with graphic images, text documents and presentations.



The story of the founder about the project
Even while working as a head of a digital agency, I had to control the creation and coordinate a large number of designs, presentations, KP, contracts, etc. Gradually, an idea began to emerge - to create a tool that would speed up and systematize the process of exchanging comments in the process of discussing and agreeing on similar tasks.
We have been trying to find competitors for quite a long time, but we have not found one either in Russia or even in the world. There are convenient services like skitch, dropbox, base camp, etc., which partially implement the tasks of the project, but only have to be used at the same time, while keeping Word and Excel included. There is not a single project that would be used to edit, compare and comment on a large number of versions of a single document (site design page, presentation page, contract page, billboard layout, etc.) and, while working together, would allow to leave footnotes and make notes right on the document. We have never found any functions like ‚Äúdownload the entire project by pressing a single button‚Äù or ‚Äúuploading the whole history of work on the project‚Äù, and this can be very useful for archives or in case of disputes.

The very first release will be intended for freelancers and small agencies. We are planning to start developing and launching a more complex corporate version and adding the ability to work with presentations and text documents after the official launch of the project.
There is a 6th year, as I gain experience in this area. I started with a rather interesting startup of my own, which was successfully launched, then sold and safely stored by the new owners. With the money received, a digital-agency was opened with colleagues, in which it was fortunate to work on a large number of interesting projects and create more than one startup for the agency's clients.

From crowdinvesting we are waiting for opinions, advice and criticism. This is most important for any startup other than the investment itself. It is difficult to make an ideal project right away, so any opinion, criticism or advice at the design stage is extremely positive for the final result.

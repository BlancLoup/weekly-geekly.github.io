We disassemble the Lenovo IdeaPad U300s ultrabook to the base

A week ago, I decided to do a preventive cleaning ultrabook Lenovo IdeaPad U300s. It would seem that what the problem is - took a screwdriver and go. But upon closer inspection, it was found that this model has no visible screws. Under the rubber leg torn off abruptly, an empty spot was found. Considering that no instructions for analyzing the U300s on the web could be found, I neatly glued the leg back and went to a fellow repairman. The results of our meeting turned out to be so informative that the material gathered for the whole post.



Start over. To get inside the case, we need to gently pick up with two sharp rubber feet, located near the hinges of the top cover, with something sharp. In the photo they are marked by arrows. The legs are not glued, and then they can be easily replaced.



Inside we will see ... yes, in general, we will not see anything except the battery and the protective film layer.



Battery for Lenovo IdeaPad U300s is not bad. You can count on five hours even with a serious load, and in Word + ICQ mode you can hold out for more than six. For the ultrabook on the Core i7 of the past generation (Sandy Bridge) this is almost a record. In other matters, taking into account the size and capacity of the battery is not surprising.



And this, you will not believe, SSD.



It looks as if he was hastily soldered right at the factory. The breed could not be determined - I just know that this is something based on the JMicron JMF616 controller. The interface, as you can see, is not a standard mSATA, but a more sophisticated one. So it‚Äôs not a dream to use a computer or other ultrabook. Yes, and upgrade will not be easy.



In the next photo we see the ultrabook without battery and SSD. Not without surprise, we learn that the motherboard is made in Japan itself. It is also impossible not to notice the two coolers of the Taiwanese company Sunon. Honestly, I was sure that he was alone there.



Further disassembly slightly slowed down. It turned out that the unit with the motherboard is firmly attached to the lid with the screen. And you need to disconnect the part where the battery and keyboard. The result was an interesting letter Z.



And here we were waiting for another useful discovery. It turns out that this model's memory is not soldered directly to the motherboard, but is inserted into a standard slot. And if the available 4 GB is not enough, you can double the volume. For many, this is important.



The WiFi adapter is quite standard, and Centrino is even written on it. I don‚Äôt see any reasons to change it, but if you suddenly need it, this is not a problem.



The chipset (also known as the south bridge) is devoid of a radiator, apparently it is not required in such systems. Enough secondary cooling.



But two heat pipes lead to the processor.



This model can not be called noisy, but coolers are turned on often and they are heard. It is worth noting that the ultrabooks on Ivy Bridge, originally designed for this platform, are easily managed with a single propeller.

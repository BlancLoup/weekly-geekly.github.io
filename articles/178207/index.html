The vital position of users VKontakte. Bonus track. Correlations

Almost immediately after I published the post The life position of VKontakte users depending on gender and age , an idea appeared to check how one or another aspect of life position depends on others. As mentioned in the previous post, for this, again, it was not necessary to do complex calculations, just refer to the search for VKontakte profiles.

Statistics on the total number of people of different ages, see the first part. Let me remind you that the age of people is laid off horizontally on all graphs.

Here's how addiction to alcohol affects the life reference points of men:

The obvious assumption is justified that lovers of drinking more often than sober-drinkers consider entertainment and rest to be the main thing in life. In addition, men who are positive about alcohol are more focused on beauty, art, fame, career and money, less on self-development, family and children.


Relationship to alcohol and attitudes towards others:

It is striking that the opponents of alcohol less value in people humor and vitality, but pay more attention to honesty and kindness. It is curious that among alcohol lovers, people are 4 times more often, primarily those who value others in terms of power and wealth.

The influence of priorities in the life of men on the requirements for other people (clickable):

The family members are expected to respect honesty and kindness more than others, but for some reason, like sober-minded people, their joie de vivre and humor are not so important. The only noticeable difference between the categories of those who seek to improve the world and those who are engaged in self-development is that the first worlds often pay attention to the power and money of those around them (apparently considering them as a tool for implementing their own ambitious plans to improve the world).

Similar statistics among women (clickable):

An interesting detail is that girls focused on self-development value courage and perseverance more in others (apparently, men).

I will begin with a small lyrical digression ...


Any population is stable only then is quite diverse in its internal structure. For example, wheat fields are a monocultural system; in natural conditions wheat is not found in such a density. As a result, any pest instantly causes a massive death of such a population, if not to make great efforts to preserve the system.
Monosystem - unstable. Any external factor can unbalance it. Return to the point of balance can only be spent a lot of effort.

Natural meadow - just the opposite situation. It is characterized by a significant diversity of species, the most complex relationships between populations that make up a single whole. When exposed to a negative factor (pest invasion, disease outbreak, etc.), natural compensation mechanisms are triggered, negative feedbacks are activated, which returns the system to the equilibrium point.
Mixed systems formed naturally are stable. Return to the point of equilibrium occurs by the forces of the system itself.

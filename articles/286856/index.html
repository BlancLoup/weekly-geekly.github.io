Where to start, when you do not know where to start

We are punished for our laziness not only by our failures, but also by the luck of others.
Jules Renard


Now it has become fashionable to create startups or participate in them. I succumbed to this mod and want to share my experience. Many business accelerators have appeared, ready to help in the development of new promising companies, incubators, which provide space and equipment for young projects. On the one hand, it seems that all the tools are given, all the expertise is gathered in one place and it seems that absolutely anyone can find an outstanding genius in himself and, most importantly, share this genius with the world. But no, as they say, for every Titanic there is a iceberg.

After analyzing a large number of various sources of information, I made several conclusions for myself, which help me and maybe can help inspired startups not to turn into starperov-losers. It may seem to someone that I cite truths, but we need them first and foremost. And then, each of us is individual, each has his own vision of building a business and implementing his own idea. "Best the enemy of the good". In 95 cases out of 100 such an approach can kill even the most sensible idea.

The second is help. You don‚Äôt always have to pay for help; they can help for free, in the name of an idea. If you manage to infect others with your idea, you can get a great team. Conclusion: do not be afraid to look for like-minded people and ask for help.

The method works when you dream not to change the scope of activity, but to raise the activity to a new level. When you work in a call center, and want to create the world's best pizzeria with mini pizza, your colleagues are unlikely to help you. Although there are always exceptions, do not neglect them.
Conclusion: break the templates, the rules are not always useful.

The third is financial support. Any startup, like any undertaking in general, needs to be fed materially, and if at the initial stage it seems that one ideological protein is enough, then after a while the moment of reckoning comes in all senses. Remember, money is always needed:

These methods require basic steps, such as a business plan and a prototype. It is easy for someone to assemble a prototype, for someone a business plan is children's fun, but it can be expected that at least one of these stages will become a stumbling block for you. Accordingly, think about the capacities at which you implement both.
Here, by the way, an unplanned conclusion has arrived: first understand what needs to be done, then understand how.

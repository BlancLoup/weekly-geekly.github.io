The Ministry of Communications and the Public Chamber proposes new measures to protect users of mobile networks

Project participants of the Public Chamber "Perspective" propose to include instant messengers in the list of data distribution sources in mobile networks. In addition, they want to hold mobile spammers accountable and make the appropriate amendment to the Law on Advertising. And the Ministry of Communications and Mass Communications is tightening the requirements for operator equipment.

For SMS-mailing, performed without the consent of users, advertisers are at least some responsibility. But if we are talking about spam in instant messengers such as Viber , WhatsApp and Telegram , the law does not provide for any liability. Although messages also come on mobile devices.

Thus, the responsibility is blamed on the citizens themselves, who allegedly signed the user agreement with these programs, ‚ÄùDenis Kalugin, the author of the initiative, the participant of the Civic Chamber of the Russian Federation Perspective, told Izvestia.

Article 18 of the Law ‚ÄúOn Advertising‚Äù states: the distribution of advertising over telecommunication networks (telephone, facsimile, mobile radiotelephone) is permissible only with the prior consent of the subscriber or addressee. The absence or presence of consent still needs to be proved. But, be that as it may, the Federal Antimonopoly Service of the Russian Federation has no authority to search for spammers.

To expand the powers of law enforcement, the Ministry of Communications has launched a new initiative to replace information gathering technology in networks. It will enable law enforcement agencies to track not only voice and Internet traffic, but also communication via instant messengers such as Skype, Viber, WhatsApp. Operators must now install new equipment that will allow them to collect more information about users.

The old technology of 2011 did not allow this. Therefore, the agency puts forward new requirements for equipment for communication networks. Technology IMS (IP Multimedia Subsystem) combines voice, text, graphics and video. This data is then transmitted over the network.

IMS works with packet data networks (LTE) and with mobile radio telephone networks (GSM and UMTS). In LTE networks, a user cannot be identified by a phone number.

But IMS technology helps to do this even when switching between networks of different types, a representative of the Ministry of Communications and Communications told Vedomosti.

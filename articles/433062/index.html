Comparing the image from the cameras in artificial light conditions, you can see that the AXIS camera has an incorrect white balance. This is most obvious if you look at the ophthalmic tablet in your hands, which, regardless of the distance, has a yellowish tint, in the IDIS, it has a more balanced white color.

At the same time, the depth of field in AXIS is greater. The letters, regardless of the deletion, look clearer and retain such outlines up to the furthest mark.

In low light conditions, IDIS is ahead of AXIS in contrast, which becomes more apparent as the distance increases. For example, until the very end the sheet of paper in the hands remains visible and does not merge so much with the silhouette.

At the same time, at close range, the face detailing looks better with AXIS. However, at a distance of 11.5, the detail of AXIS is dramatically lower than that of IDIS, which is especially noticeable by the letters W and B.

In general, in low light conditions, the white balance is better already with AIXS. IDIS has a yellowish tint.

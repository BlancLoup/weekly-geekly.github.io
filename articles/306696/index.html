Documents in the wires: EDO from MegaFon

One of the world's automakers considered that printing and delivery by courier to the dealership center of one package of documents costs about 580 rubles. In the central office of the mobile operator of the big four, they conducted research and it turned out that they use about 420 packs of paper per month. At the same time, even the most common Internet startup spends about 11 packs per month. It is not difficult to calculate that the cost of document flow for any company is a significant amount that any manager would gladly have reduced.


In this case, no one took into account the time spent by employees in searching for documents required for work. This problem is relevant even if only one person is involved in the document flow. With the collective use of documents, it is aggravated many times and, finally, becomes practically unsolvable if the organization is geographically distributed. Meanwhile, according to AIIM (Association for Information & Image Management) calculations, an enterprise that produces an average of 100 document folders per week spends $ 18.7 thousand per year to recover lost documents. Also, according to AIIM statistics, the average executor spends 2.5 hours a week searching for the necessary documents, and for clerks it takes 20-30% of working time.

We should not forget that every year the Russian government introduces more and more improvements to its work, legislatively fixing the standards for the transfer of reports in electronic form using a digital signature. For example, by order of the Federal Tax Service of the Russian Federation of October 14, 2015 No. MMB-7-11 / 450 @, a new mandatory reporting form was introduced for organizations and individual entrepreneurs - 6-NDFL. Moreover, to provide in paper form 6-NDFL can only those organizations and individual entrepreneurs who paid income to no more than 25 employees. The rest is in electronic form only. This means that electronic digital signatures (EDS) are now available in almost all serious organizations. We did a survey in our previous article ( What is a signature. From a drop of blood to an EDS ) and more than half of our readers have already worked with a digital signature.



Against this background, the delivery of electronic reporting and electronic document management systems (EDMS) are becoming extremely relevant. The growth of IT-technologies and ubiquitous computerization made it possible to reflect on the transition to fundamentally new management methods. In some countries, the introduction of EDS has become a national task. Thus, the e-government program was launched in the Estonian government already in 2002, when the Internet access in Estonia as a whole did not exceed 30% of the population. To date, 98% of Estonian citizens use EDI when communicating with government agencies. You can even vote in the parliamentary and presidential elections using a digital electronic signature. You can also get an "electronic residency" and, for example, if you are a co-founder of a Russian-Estonian company, you can remotely receive an EDS and legally-signify documents all documents. This is not only a convenience and time saving people, but also a significant reduction in costs for the government. By average, the savings from the introduction of the SED in a more or less large organization can reach 95-97%. For example, sending a document via EDI costs only a few rubles.

Despite the significant lag of Russia from Western companies in this matter, the annual growth rate of the Russian market for SED, according to analysts , is at least 30%. The adoption of the law on electronic digital signature has created the legal basis for the widespread distribution of electronic document circulation. According to Forrester Research, 38% of Fortune 500 companies consider the acquisition of an SED is critical to successful business.

In Russia there are standards for all necessary operations for the use of EDM: encryption, hashing, electronic signature.

1. Electronic signature and hashing are used to ensure the legal value of electronic documents through the use of electronic signature (ES):

  • GOST R 34.10-2012: Processes of formation and verification of electronic digital signature
  • GOST R 34.11-2012: Hash function

The cryptographic strength of the encryption algorithm is based on the problem of discrete logarithm in a group of points of an elliptic curve. At the moment there is no method for solving this problem at least with subexponential complexity. For us it is important that the state recognizes it to be sufficiently robust.

2. Encryption. Ensuring confidentiality and integrity control of information through its encryption:

  • GOST 28147-89: Algorithm of cryptographic transformation

This is a block cipher with a simple Feistel structure, with a block size of 64 bits, a 256-bit key and 32 rounds, which was developed in the Soviet Union. The algorithm has been extensively researched by leading experts in the field of cryptography, and all researchers have expressed that everything looks like it can be or should be safe. There are a number of comments on his performance, Nicolas Courtois even reported successful attacks on this algorithm, but experts from CryptoPro rehabilitated him in the public eye.

3. Protection of data transmission channels on the Internet. For this task, TLS is used - the transport level security protocol, in which the GOST 28147-89 GOST algorithms described above and the hash according to GOST 34.11-2012 can be used. This combination allows you to certify it in the FSB and FSTECK, which was done by the companies CryptoPro, Cryptocom, and VipNet in their SKPI.

To use the EDM, you need to have a qualified electronic signature and configure your computer to work with it. Problems of using EDS


Fraud when working with EDS is mainly related to the theft or physical use of the certificate by others. This is most often due to improper storage of the token with the certificate and password from it. Also, attackers often use the introduction of viruses on the PC of EDM users, for example, they substitute on the screen the text of the document that the user wants to sign.

However, all these factors depend on the security of the interface in which the user works, and on whether the user himself complies with basic security measures when working with digital signature.

Twitter will transfer the database of Russian users in Russia in 2018


Photo: TASS, Sergey Konkov

Large technology companies one by one go towards Roskomnadzor in terms of agreement to transfer the data of Russian users to servers within the Russian Federation. This is now announced by Twitter. The management of the company declared its readiness to work in accordance with Russian legislation and transfer all the necessary data to the territory of the Russian Federation by mid-2018, Kommersant writes .

"We are in the process of determining what information about Russian citizens and organizations involved in Twitter‚Äôs commercial relations in Russia can be allocated for storage in the Russian Federation," the letter from the vice president for public policy in Europe, the Middle East and African Twitter Inc. Not all companies are ready to meet Russian officials. For example, LinkedIn, the world's largest social network of professionals, did not agree to transfer data from Russian citizens to the Russian Federation. As a result, Roskomnadzor, in a judicial proceeding, succeeded in blocking this social network in Russia. LinkedIn representatives met with officials in early March of this year, but apparently did not reach agreement. In any case, Roskomnadzor subsequently declared that the social network does not plan to eliminate the violation of the law. Roskomnadzor believes that this company collects and transmits information about citizens who are not users of the social network, without their consent.

The Law on Personal Data, which entered into force on September 1, 2015, obliges the storage of personal data of Russian citizens in the country. According to the law, the websites of violators of this law must be entered into a special register by Roskomnadzor. If violations are not eliminated, the site and services of such a company are finally blocked. Roskomnadzor also conducts regular inspections of companies for compliance with the law.

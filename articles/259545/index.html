9 usability testing tools



In a world of constantly evolving technologies and unprecedented high competition between network businesses, success often depends on the intuitiveness of web interfaces. Simple and clear websites have a higher conversion rate, therefore, it brings more revenue.

Usability is the ease of use of software products. The concept includes the following non-functional aspects of software use:

  • Efficiency (user tasks).
  • Efficiency (in terms of execution time).
  • Satisfaction (user experience) in the specified context of use (users, tasks, equipment and environments).

Consider usability measurement tools. We used three main evaluation criteria:

  • The ability to record audio and video during testing.
  • Quality tools for post editing test sessions.
  • The ability to use mobile devices for testing.

After completing the study and comparing nine popular tools, we concluded that we still do not have a comprehensive, fully integrated usability testing tool for mobile devices that meets the following criteria:

  • The ability to interact with the screen of a mobile device.
  • The possibility of audio and video recording of all actions of research participants during the work on the task.
  • Ability to conduct testing anywhere and at any time.
  • The ability to test any smartphone or tablet (if they have a camera).
  • Streaming screen and audio-video research participants in real time.

Ovo logger


Ovo Logger from Ovo Studios is created mainly for usability testing studios that work with large volumes of distributed teams of researchers. The tool is designed mainly for them, because they need a reliable means for capturing, editing and sharing the results.

Ovo Studio is also creating custom usability testing labs, and Ovo Logger is partly designed to work with them. The tool as a whole is better than the others, but rather expensive.
Pros:
  • Focused on the enterprise.
  • Records multiple entries and sends streams to multiple users at once.
  • Editing tools provide real-time test analysis.
  • Allows you to save important points when viewing a record.
  • Allows you to capture a picture from the unlocked iPad, iPhone and devices on Android using the Logger application.

Minuses:

  • Designed specifically for large firms with multiple offices and / or usability teams.
  • Requires a separate computer to run the software.
  • The price is higher than the competition.
  • The tool is based on a number of sources, associated requirements for laboratories, etc.

Ovo solo


Ovo Solo is an Ovo Studios product designed for small companies. You can even say that this is the light version of the Ovo Logger, which allows you to capture video interactions with the screen simultaneously with the faces and voices of the study participants. Simple logging management allows you to analyze the resulting video. However, the tool only supports Windows, iOS support is not provided. On the other hand, its affordable price makes the tool a smart choice with a low budget.

Pros:

  • Relatively low price for capturing, editing and showing usability testing sessions.

Minuses:

  • No option for Mac.
  • You can not directly record the interaction with the screen of the mobile device.
  • A basic set of editing tools, not for advanced researchers.

Morae


Techsmith's Morae is the first tool specifically designed for usability testing, and despite its age, it is still one of the best options. Allows you to record, analyze, edit and share sessions.
Morae is installed on a computer (there is no option for iOS) as an application and records all interactions with the screen, including the voice and face of the researcher, via a webcam. Included are all documentation, editing and distribution tools.

Pros:

  • Impressive capture and editing tools, suitable for large projects.
  • Includes additional tools that can track various screen interactions.
  • Allows you to include in the analysis data based on those. control and opinions.
  • A simple and quick opportunity to share test sessions with colleagues and test results with teams.

Minuses:

  • There is no possibility to record the interaction with the screen from mobile devices directly.
  • Enough expensive option.
  • No option for Mac.

UserTesting

UserTesting includes powerful and easy-to-use video editing tools for creating threading.
UserTesting also provides mobile usability testing, a very significant feature with a constant increase in the number of users of such devices, and includes the possibility of written answers to questions.

UserTesting is one of the most powerful tools for remote unmoderated usability testing.

Pros:

Minuses:

Openhallway


OpenHallway is a Windows and Mac based research tool. The researcher is typing testers himself, but there is no user control panel in the tool. OpenHallway itself generates links to test sessions when everything is ready.

Its use does not require changing the codes inside the site, which makes the tool extremely useful for testing applications, websites and even competitors' sites. It is possible to record the voice and face of the tester, its interaction with the screen using the camera.

The amount of video storage depends on the version you use: Basic, Plus or Premium. There is no limit on the number of sessions, but at some point the storage space for the video may end.

Pros:

  • Easy to use.
  • Easy to install.
  • A handy tool to record unmoderated remote usability testing.
  • Windows and Mac support.

Minuses:

  • Does not support mobile devices.
  • Without Java, the tool will not work.
  • Video only in MP4 format, downloading and editing is possible only in paid versions.

Flussonic Watcher - ready-made solutions for launching camera surveillance


Flussonic Watcher is a software package designed specifically for launching video surveillance systems with connectivity and control options for virtually any IP camera, billing or inventory system integration, and mobile applications for users. With its help, the operator can start a cloud video surveillance service in a matter of days, thereby expanding the pool of services provided to subscribers.

Flussonic Watcher includes:


  • Flussonic Media Server for storing and delivering video;
  • web interface for administrators and users;
  • mobile apps for iOS and Android;
  • camera activation and provisioning service;
  • API for billing integration with Watcher control through billing, as well as for developing external modules and mobile applications;
  • Camera software (agent).



A question may arise: why is there any additional software for the camera? After all, almost any manufacturer offers opportunities to connect to their equipment through a personal account.

Agent


To tell why an agent is needed and what it is, consider the typical implementation of video surveillance by the operator:

  1. Cameras are bought.
  2. Each camera is entered into the inventory accounting system.
  3. Each camera is assigned an IP address.
  4. Subscribers in the billing add the appropriate services.
  5. Cameras are added to the video surveillance service admin area, where basic settings are entered and billing information is transmitted.

A separate problem may be setting up the visibility of the camera from the Internet (outside the local network). There are three classic schemes for doing this:

  1. Manual port forwarding.
  2. Installing OpenVPN proxy servers.
  3. Issuance of white IP addresses for each camera.

All these solutions have certain disadvantages: from the complexity of setting up each camera to the instability of video delivery to users. This issue is particularly acute when you need to give personal access to a specific camera with a specific archive depth according to the tariff plan that the subscriber will select. How to do it in Plug-n-play mode for the subscriber? And how to do it, if the cameras are sold in the store and before the first start-up the operator does not know anything about its existence, and especially about who bought it and what access it needs to be issued?

Abstracts


Spock and Android, Alexander Yezhkov, Alfa Bank JSC



Spock is a framework for testing java, groovy and kotlin applications. And, of course, for android. Its feature is the expressiveness and structuring of tests. Let us see if this is so, and is the game worth the candle ?!


‚ÄúCI processes in Android Avito development‚Äù, Sergey Pinchuk, Avito



I'll tell you about CI in a large company, in conditions when dozens of people work on the project. I will describe the application development process, I will share with practitioners who noticeably simplify the work and facilitate the life of the team as a whole. Let's talk about the tests: what and when you need to run, how much you should trust them. I will share the problems we have encountered, I will tell you how they were solved and what we are striving for in the future.


"(Not) Security 101", Grigory Dzhanelidze, Mosdroid



Security is good, but the illusion of security is bad. Many articles and reports about security in the context of Android in recent years have missed one important point - how much the proposed methods actually complicate the work on hacking / reverse engineering of the application. Grigory will use examples to show why some safety tips are evil and how to get around these tips.

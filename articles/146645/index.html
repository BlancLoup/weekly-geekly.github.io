Now seriously. Why do I prefer text to video?

1) Attention. To watch the video, you need to pay all attention to the video. Especially if with sound. Just turn aside - and that's all, ‚Äúwhat did he do now ?!‚Äù and look for the time when you got distracted, using the red strip on the mouse.

Distracted during a thoughtful reading, of course, unpleasant, but not fatal - the maximum will have to reread the previous sentence, which does not take much time.

2) Continuity. In order to escape from the video, you must first pause it. And if you want to clarify something, first find that place with an accuracy of a few seconds.
For the text of this problem does not occur - no problem to be distracted instantly, and then just as quickly return to reading. You can easily leave the tab open and reply to a message or letter. Or go to sleep, and finish it tomorrow. Or turn off the computer, knowing that finding the right place in the text - a couple of eye movements and mouse wheel. And not re-loading video and flipping.

3) Inconvenient movement and search
Finding the necessary fragment in a 20-minute video is very difficult. Either use the services to accelerate the rollers, or flipping with a small gap, hoping that you stumble at the right moment.

4) one-dimensional
In the video at a time you can see only one frame. You pause, and limit yourself to 1/30 second of real time.

Seeing in front of you a page of text, you can only move your eyes to the previous paragraph, and then go back. Eye movements are incomparably faster than comprehension, hand movements to the mouse, and the search for the desired fragment in the video. And do not forget, you have to go back exactly at the same time.

5) Greater demands on technology

Still, both creating a video and watching it is much more complicated and demanding on resources.

To install the video you need at least a camera and a simple video editor, for viewing - a modern computer, fast internet, or an expensive smartphone.

To read the text you need a cheap phone, reader, communicator, e-book, tablet, or at the end of the day a simple paper book. For writing - a simple laptop or phone with a keyboard.

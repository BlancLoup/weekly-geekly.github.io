Former Minister of Finance of Greece in favor of introducing unconditional income in Switzerland



In an interview with the Swiss newspaper Tages Anzeiger, the former Minister of Finance of Greece, Janis Varoufakis, actively supported the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãintroducing unconditional income for citizens.

First of all, he explained his adherence to this idea by the fact that technical progress leads to the disappearance of jobs and to shredding of the middle class. At the same time, robots working in factories do not shop in stores. As a result, more and more funds accumulate at the highest classes of society, which leads to stagnation of the economy.

Varufakis emphasizes that unconditional income does not mean a total failure from work. According to surveys of Swiss people, no more than 2% of people will stop working if unconditional income is introduced. He recalled that in Switzerland there are already a lot of non-working people: they are rich. He cited the example of children of wealthy parents who work not for making money, but for self-realization. They organize funds, work on their own projects or spend money on training.

He believes that, despite the high standard of living (Switzerland is one of the richest countries in the world), the quality of life of citizens is falling. The more highly paid work, the more a person is afraid of losing it. Therefore, from his point of view, the introduction of unconditional income is an investment in the future of the country.

Ru-Center: by the end of 2007 there will be 1 million domains in RuNet

Together with the news that from January 2007, applications for Cyrillic domains in the RU zone will begin, the accredited registrar Ru-Center also presented general information about the growth in its industry segment. Mainly quantitative indicators are given.

Now, according to the organization, 650,000 second-level domains are registered in RuNet. The growth rate of the number of registrations is high, and by the end of the year it is expected that the number of domains will exceed 700 thousand. A ruble of 1 million, according to Ru-Center forecasts, will be completed by the end of next year.

"The start of registration of Russian-speaking domains will increase the total number of names in the RU domain," the registrar states. He cites data according to which the share of foreign owners of namespace in RuNet has decreased from 4% to 3%. Most often these are representatives of Germany (0.94%), the USA (0.54%) and Ukraine (0.51%).

In addition, at the end of 2006, the number of legal entities among domain owners continues to decrease. By the end of 2005, this figure dropped to 44%, and in October 2006 - to 38%. Thus, individuals became about 62%.

Considering the registration curve by geography, Ru-Center reports a reduction in the share of Moscow, which today stands at 45.8%, whereas at the end of last year it was equal to 50.4%.

Nevertheless, the gap between the capital and other regions is still striking. For example, St. Petersburg has a share of only 7.4%, Moscow Oblast - 6.66%, Sverdlovsk - 1.75%, Novosibirsk Region - 1.46%, Rostov Region - 1.2%, Samara Region - 1.02%. The remaining subjects of the Russian Federation have less than 1% among the total number of domain names registered in the RU zone.

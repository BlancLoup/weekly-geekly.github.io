Twitter bots create events internationally


When Greg Marra (pictured) built the @Trackgirl twitter bot in 2008, he just wanted to see if the Python script could penetrate the social network of real people. He did not expect people to really worry about @Trackgirl, send direct messages to her and be interested in her state of health after her fictional injury.

@Trackgirl tracked the stream of tweets with currently popular hashtags, and published them on its own behalf. Three times a day she followed 5 people. It turned out that she was connected with the right people, and other people began to follow her, believing that her copy-paste about long-distance running came from a living person. Once she tweeted that she had injured her knee ... Thousands of people were interested in the script's well-being and sincerely empathized with the bot.

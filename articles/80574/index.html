Login form experiment: OpenID, two in one, or regular powder?

Hello, .

Recently, when we launched the Filmbase , we, in addition to ways to counteract habraeefekt, asked another question that had long interested us: what form of login is most convenient for most users. To do this, we conducted an experiment: until today, all site visitors were offered randomly one of the 4 login options, each of which consisted of 2 forms. We carried out the assessment very simply: by the number of people who still registered through each of the options for the forms. Since the options were offered randomly, the more people registered - the more convenient the form (or the smaller number of people she scared away :).

So, the participants in the experiment and its results:

Everything is simple here: it is proposed to enter an e-mail and password for a login, if they are not there, go to the registration form (without reloading the page), where we can enter the same e-mail and password + repeat password.

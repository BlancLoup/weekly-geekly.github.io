The cronies, once again want to "recount all", as reported in Izvestia . This time the deputies of the Leningrad region distinguished themselves, they developed a bill that would oblige Russians to register on social networks with real passport data, and to violate the registration rules - to fine: users from 1 to 3 thousand rubles, and social networks from 100 to 300 thousand.

Not without traditional "child care". Citizens under the age of 14 are planning to completely prohibit the use of social networks, and parents who do not follow the child are fined 2,000 if the child registers "where not necessary." To the heap - they threw in every little thing, such as a ban on the promotion of tobacco, alcohol, unconventional sex and all the bad things. It is also forbidden to sell goods to minors through social networks. This is rather strange considering the ban on registration for children under 14.

Cherry on the cake - a ban on publishing information about uncoordinated meetings.

But Vladimir Petrov - soothes everyone in advance:
No one is trying to impose censorship or restrict freedom of speech. Verification and strict control over the authenticity of names will only increase the price of their own opinions and virtual communication.

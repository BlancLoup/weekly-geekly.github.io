Intel App Innovation Contest 2013 Contest. Win $ 20,000!

Yesterday , the international competition Intel App Innovation Contest 2013 , or, to be more precise, its Russian part, started in the Habrahabr community. From July 24 to November 20, we invite you to go from creating an idea to a Windows 8 Desktop application to its implementation. The prize fund is considerable: $ 100,000 + additional prizes from the Russian Intel Developer Zone team. There is something to fight for!




Windows Desktop vs. Windows store


Anticipating possible questions in the comments, I would like to immediately dot the i's. Yes, in this contest we only accept desktop applications. No, we are not ardent opponents of Windows UI. The fact is that after the release of the new Windows 8 operating system, 2 myths were born in the programming circles:
  1. In the desktop mode, it is impossible to implement support for all the new features of Windows 8, such as multitouch and sensors.
  2. Desktop application can not be published in the Windows Store, and accordingly, to earn on its sales.

We would like to debunk both myths. Therefore, in this competition we suggest you to make sure in practice that in desktop applications you can implement support for touch gestures, sensors and a number of other functions and technologies that are not available in the Windows UI. Publishing a desktop application in the Windows Store is also possible.

There is an opinion that new interfaces are a necessity. The future of touch devices, and the good old desktop sooner or later become obsolete. Of course, users won't stop using desktop apps overnight, not everyone is ready for the revolutionary interface of Windows Store apps. Windows 8 Desktop user applications are a pretty logical transition from previous OS to Windows UI. A detailed comparison of the advantages of developing desktop and Windows UI applications, as well as supported technologies, can be found in the article http://software.intel.com/en-us/articles/windows-8-store-vs-desktop-app-development

Briefly about the conditions of the competition


Competition applications are accepted on CSDN , ThinkDigit , Code Project and Habrahabr . We strongly recommend sending applications through our Habr for the purpose of disinterested support for the status of the Russian community :) Good news: not only developers from Russia but also from Ukraine and Belarus can legally take part in the competition. Even if you live in Germany, Great Britain or Israel, no one and nothing prevents you from sending an application in Russian via Habr.

The competition itself is divided into two stages. The first stage is preparatory - we accept only ideas. Of all the ideas, the judges (at this stage, the Russian speakers) will choose the 125 best, whose authors go to the second stage and receive devices for development. After receiving the device, you start developing a demo application based on the presented idea. We collect your innovative applications and select 30 finalists who will compete for the main prizes.

$ 100,000 or one of the main motivators


To compete for the prize, 30 authors of the best demo applications will need to make an additional video clip in English. This is necessary, since at this stage the panel of judges will be international, and judges who do not understand Russian or Chinese will have to understand and appreciate your great ideas.

The author, who collected the maximum number of points on the basis of the results of the second stage and the additional task of creating a video, receives the Main Prize of $ 20,000 . The best apps will also be selected for the tablet, all-in-one and in each of the six contest categories.

Total we get another 8 winners, who are awarded a cash prize of $ 10,000 . All 9 winners in addition to cash prizes also receive various marketing support from Intel and, of course, the honor and respect of the international developer community.

Do not bypass education - you are entitled to an additional reward.


This year, support for the education segment has become one of the priorities for Intel. In the spring of this year, as part of a competition on the Intel Education Galaxy website, we received a large number of interesting ideas from experts and educators on what applications ( https://edugalaxy.intel.ru/?portalid=24 ) are missing in their daily work. . Perhaps some of them you can take as a basis for participation in our competition. These ideas are already in demand in the market, so your product will not remain without users. In addition, your application may receive an additional prize from the Intel Developer Zone and Intel Education Galaxy projects. The authors of the best applications in 3 categories: visualization, constructors, learning through entertainment, will receive 100,000 rubles each. or a package of advertising services for this amount, as well as marketing support for the Intel Education Galaxy project.

GLONASS ground equipment will be installed at three stations in Antarctica


Novolazarevskaya station

Deputy Prime Minister Dmitry Rogozin recently publicly outraged by the US refusal to deploy ground-based equipment for GLONASS, which allows to increase the accuracy of the system. The refusal of the Federal Communications Agency is reasoned by the fact that the Russian system does not have licenses for the transmission of a radio signal (section 301 of the Communications Act 1984). The unofficial reason for the ban is the fact that it is a dual-use system that can be used for military purposes, for example, for accurate missile targeting.

However, the refusal of Americans does not prevent to install ground equipment in other places. So, in the next Antarctic summer, metal containers with new ground-based equipment of the GLONASS system will be installed on the foundations of three stations in Antarctica , reports TASS.

Work on the construction of the foundation and flyover has already begun at Novolazarevskaya station.

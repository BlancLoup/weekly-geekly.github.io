Take your data, untie your mail and run with qip.ru



Because QIP seems to be a RIP


After reading the two sentences from the headline, some of Habr's guests will experience a kind of " Mandela effect ". After all, according to popular opinion, the QIP project has long been dead. And no! He completely existed and even worked more or less, until recently ...

The once-popular portal QIP.ru, which has now lost its former glory, which at a certain stage included a news aggregator, forums, mail, instant messenger, photo hosting and other services, has finally come to a complete decline. From about August 20, 2018, constant interruptions in authorization in services, access to mailboxes and delivery of letters began. As of September 25, 2018 QIP.ru periodically goes into complete inaccessibility up to several days.



At one of the moments of ‚Äúenlightenment‚Äù, when the site started working again, the current owners posted an advertisement for the sale of domains that form the portal mail pool. The announcement hung a couple of days between several downtimes, and then disappeared.

Technical support is not provided to users. The technical support section is not available. No warnings about EOL ( End of Life - completion of maintenance) or long technical work was sent. Here is such an inglorious end.

The history of rise and fall. Who is responsible?



According to Wikipedia , the project began work on September 1, 1999, when the registration of mailboxes of 20 MB in the domains pisem.net and mailru.com was opened. At the moment (the beginning of 2018), has provided email addresses in more than 22 domains.

July 1, 2000 the project was acquired by the RBC group of companies.

The project was named Pochta.ru in 2004. The merger of the nm.ru project and Pochta.ru took place on February 25, 2009. The merger of the project QIP.ru and Mail.ru took place on December 9, 2009. The main domain of the project since 2009 is qip.ru.

In 2013, the official portal community in the VC ceased to be updated.

In March 2015, RBC sold a number of services to private investors. The list of items includes the portal QIP.ru, News.qip.ru, educational database 5ballov.qip.ru, service test selections Aeterna.qip.ru, astrological service Horo.qip.ru, service of postcards Kards.qip.ru, Radio .qip.ru, Photobank Photo.qip.ru, service for creating screenshots of Shot.qip.ru, forums Borda.ru and Forum24.ru, service for storing and exchanging data Files.qip.ru and mail qip.ru.

In June 2015, the domain pochta.ru was sold to RBC Russian Post through an intermediary company . But QIP.ru users have retained access to their email accounts as a result of a tripartite agreement . From September 2017, the pochta.ru postal service of the domain stopped working with new letters (new letters arrived until November 2017), access to email accounts was maintained on the website www.qip.ru

At the end of August 2018, a continuous series of serious problems began with the QIP.ru portal and its services, which can hardly be called ‚Äútemporary difficulties‚Äù. The term ‚Äúepic failure‚Äù is most appropriate. In the pauses between the complete inaccessibility of the main site showed notifications of "technical work" and "moving to another data center."

According to " Noviye Izvestiya " as of August 2018, the owner of the well-known Qip Messenger in the past is Media World LLC, which also owns an email mail.qip.ru and a service for creating and using forums borda.ru.

What is dangerous?


The situation quickly changes from bad to worse. If the announcement of the sale of domains is not a hoax, then instead of denial of service and the likely loss of data stored on project servers, users may face much more serious consequences. There is no guarantee that the new domain owners will be honest and decent. What prevents them from once again raising mail servers on domains received at their disposal and starting to receive someone else's mail? Surely there are people who still have valuable accounts tied to mailboxes from the domain pool QIP.ru. Or such a mailbox is listed as a backup in another mail service and you can recover the password to it.

By the way, the portal qip.ru is the operator of personal data and the current leadership of the portal potentially puts these data at risk by their actions. I wonder when the plot will interest RosKomNadzor or another regulatory authority?

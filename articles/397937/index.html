Google and its secrets: what should we expect tomorrow

This Tuesday will be another long-awaited event in tehnomir - Google presentation. While the Internet is full of rumors, the company itself is also in no hurry to reveal the cards and only stirs the interest of the audience with teaser videos and mysterious comments. Banderolka put together all the guesses, walking on the network - and it turned out that on October 4 there will be a lot of interesting things waiting for us!



Google's smartphone


According to rumors, we are not waiting for the next Nexus, in the creation of which the Chinese developer Huawei is actively involved, but the phone made by Google itself (or the usual HTC). Clearly, no details can be obtained from the company: their mysterious commercial with the #MadeByGoogle hashtag, like the teaser outdoor advertising in American cities, does not give any indication of what kind of product it will be.



However, nosy Google fans found out that the code name of the smartphone is Pixel, and it will be presented in two versions (with a screen diagonal of 5 and 5.5 inches). The memory capacity of the younger model is 32 GB, the older one has 32 GB and 128 GB. The Pixel XL screen will support 2K resolution, and just Pixel will get a FullHD matrix.

Andromeda operating system


Rumors about the merger of the successful Android mobile OS and the less popular desktop version of Chrome OS have been around for a long time. The newspaper The Wall Street Journal wrote about this in 2015: they say, the system will finally be ready in 2017, but they announce it a year earlier. We became even more convinced of the existence of Andromeda (the code name of the new axis) after the tweet of Hiroshi Lockheimer, senior vice president of Google.

8 years ago today. I have been talking about Oct 4, 2016.

- Hiroshi Lockheimer (@lockheimer) September 24, 2016

* Eight years ago, we presented the first version of Android. I feel that in another 8 years we will be talking about the fourth of October 2016.

If all speculation turns out to be true, then we will have a cool thing that will allow you to use mobile applications and on PC without any restrictions.

Other sources say that no Andromeda is foreseen. But there will be a new Android 7.1 with a modified interface.

The Android Police website says that in the third quarter of 2017, Google will launch its own laptop based on the Andromeda OS. It will be an ultra-thin laptop, which can also transform into a tablet. It is expected that the screen diagonal of Pixel 3 will be 12.3 inches, and the laptop itself will run on an Intel m3 or i5 Core processor. Other features that Andriod Police ‚Äúdrains‚Äù are: 32 or 128 GB of memory, 8 or 16 GB of RAM + a fingerprint scanner, two USB-C ports, a 3.5 mm headphone jack, four microphones, two speakers and 10 battery life. On the other hand, there are no photos of the new laptop, so perhaps Google will not show Pixel 3 at the presentation on October 4th.

Home Assistant Google Home + Google Wi-Fi Smart Router


Google finally got its own Siri (voice assistant called Google Assistant) and its own home ecosystem - Google Home. The device allows you to search for something in Google without interrupting your household chores, listen to music, redirect multimedia streams to the desired source (TV or to specific speakers), and manage your smart home systems. It is expected that this miracle will cost $ 129 ($ 40 cheaper than its closest rival Amazon Echo ).
An important part of Google Home is the Google Wi-Fi smart router, which has a large coverage area and support for all smart features.



Virtual Daydream View Helmet


All self-respecting companies have long since released their virtual reality helmets, and only Apple and Google lag behind. Recall, however, that in May, the latter announced the Daydream virtual reality platform, but it was available only to developers.

We hope that on October 4th we will get to know this thing better. At the same time, Google should announce its requirements for smartphones that support Daydream, and tell how the helmet (or glasses) of virtual reality will look like.

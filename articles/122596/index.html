Apps4Russia is an open data contest in Russia. Official announcement

On Tuesday, June 28, 2011 at 17:00 in Moscow in the club Pir O.I.I. on Sretenka at the address: Sretenka Street d.26 / 1 ( http://maps.yandex.ru/-/CBQLMOl. ) there will be an announcement of the contest Apps4Russia.

Apps4Russia ( www.apps4russia.ru ) is a competition for creating projects based on open data and data collected from citizens, the so-called crowdsourcing. Projects created for public benefit, solving various problems, ensuring greater transparency of the state and organizing citizens to solve problems ‚Äúwith the whole world‚Äù.

The basis of the competition, open data, is one of the most relevant and popular topics in the field of openness of the state. Open data is disclosed by various states and international organizations around the world. Special portals for open data are created by US governments ( http://www.data.gov )., Singapore ( http://data.gov.sg/ ), UK ( http://data.gov.uk ), European Union ( http://publicdata.eu/ ) and other countries. Portals on open data have the UN and the World Bank.


They are being created for a reason, but with the goal that citizens, non-profit organizations and businesses use this information to create fundamentally new projects. For the emergence of such projects and announced such contests.

For example, you can look at such contests as:
  • The Apps for Development is a competition for open data from the World Bank based on the data they previously disclosed.
  • NYC Big Apps - New York City Open Data Competition
  • Mashup Australia - contests according to Australia.
  • Apps4Ottawa - contests for open data of the city of Ottawa
  • Open Up! - private open data competition in the UK


and many others. A full list of such contests can be found here - http://opengovernmentdata.org/data/competitions/

Contests allow citizens not only to create projects, but also to receive remuneration for their creation and fame, having received prizes. Participation in the competition will allow you to show your skills, add an important line to your resume, benefit people, and maybe find an idea for a successful startup.
As a result of competitions, hundreds of projects are being created; many of which can be applicable for Russia.

Our contest Apps4Russia is organized for the same purpose. We are sure that there are many active developers in Russia who are able to create the projects necessary for everyone, use open data and help citizens solve common problems.

Our competition is not state, not political and not commercial. Its organizers and the jury are people who have decided that in our country we are ready for such events and have invested our personal funds in it.

We do not limit the participants in the selection of themes for projects, however, we are especially attentive to projects related to such topics as:

  • City Infrastructure / Environment
  • HOA / tariffs / prices
  • Government finance
  • Government services
  • Ecology
  • Transformation and / or visualization of historical data


Competition jury:

  • Ivan Begtin, the creator of opengovdata.ru , rosspending.ru and other projects in the field of open government
  • Simon Kordonsky, Professor, Head of the Local Government Department, HSE.
  • Ekaterina Aksenova, businesswoman and blogger on the open state gov-gov.ru
  • Dmitry Itskovich, Chairman of the Editorial Board of Polit.ru


The competition will be held from June 31 to October 1. We accept all projects aimed at technological solutions for public benefit. To take part you need to send an application at http://www.apps4russia.ru/submit/ and start creating your project. Or maybe it is already ready and you can indicate it immediately in the application.

A full list of topics and a detailed description of the competition can be found on the website http://www.apps4russia.ru

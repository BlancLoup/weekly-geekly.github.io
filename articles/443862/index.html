Prosecutor's car locks. Bestchange locked again



This week we wrote our old client bestchange.ru . If anyone knows, bestchange is the largest online monitoring service of electronic currency exchange, which has been operating since 2007 and collects information from more than 400 active exchangers. The administration of the service reported that they received a letter from the hoster, who received a notice from Roskomnadzor about the inclusion of the site in the Unified Register, and therefore the site will be blocked.

At first, we thought it was some kind of mistake, because a year ago we canceled the court decision on their website. What was our surprise when we found out that this time the site is blocked on the basis of a different court decision, like two drops of water similar to the previously canceled St. Petersburg decision. We dig deeper into the network and found this decision made by the Kuibyshevskiy District Court of the city of Omsk four months later. The same site, all the same grounds sucked from the finger of the public prosecutor.

Let me remind you that in May 2017, the October Court of St. Petersburg ruled to block the Bestchange service and 39 other online exchangers at the suit of the city prosecutor‚Äôs office, guided by similar arguments, saying that bitcoin is a surrogate, and the dissemination of information on surrogates is prohibited by law. In addition, in the opinion of the prosecutor's office, the irreversibility of transactions in the blockchain does not provide the possibility of implementing legal mechanisms for ensuring the fulfillment of obligations by the parties to the transaction.

After the appeal appeal of a judicial act in February 2018, the St. Petersburg City Court overturned the decision as unlawful and unjust due to the fact that the site owners were not involved in the process, which violated their right to state their arguments in a fair trial.



Already on the new round of consideration of the case, after the annulment of the judicial act by the appeal, the court did not consider the petition of the prosecutor on the merits, and found quite reasoned grounds in the CAS RF to ‚Äúwrap‚Äù the lawsuit. After that, the St. Petersburg prosecutor's office on the supervision of the implementation of laws on highly sensitive sites (apparently we now have the Internet - this is a particularly secure facility, which basically explains the current trend to regulate everything that can be regulated on the network) did not continue to rock the boat and did not file a second claim. And besides, by this time, the Supreme Court also formulated its position on another cryptocurrency website bitcoininfo.ru.

On April 20, 2018, the Supreme Court, abolishing judicial acts of the first and second instance, made a number of important conclusions that are mandatory for the lower courts to take into account:

  • claims of prosecutors on the recognition of information illegal for the purpose of blocking should be considered in the procedure of CAS RF

  • such claims cannot be considered in the courts at the location of the prosecutors. They should be considered at the location of Roskomnadzor or its territorial body (which, too, is quite controversial, because territorial departments do not have access to the registry, and they cannot contribute or remove anything from there)

  • Site owners and content authors should be involved in the case, because such court decisions are not only capable of limiting their rights, but also give rise to the obligation to remove content

As it can be seen, the cassation instance went away from the slippery question related to the determination of the legal status of cryptocurrencies, however, the value of the said Definition is difficult to overestimate, since the Supreme Court for the first time determined at least some imputed rules in the order of considering such claims that entail blocking web resources in the country. Later, this position was also reflected in the Review of Judicial Practice of the Supreme Court of the Russian Federation on Administrative Cases N 2 (2018) "(approved by the Presidium of the Supreme Court of the Russian Federation on 04.07.2018).

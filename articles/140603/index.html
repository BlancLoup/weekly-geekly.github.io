Frustro: impossible font



Hungarian designer Marzi Hegedus (Martzi Heged≈±s) created the original font Frustro , which is based on the idea of impossible figures - one of the types of optical illusions. Like the classic Penrose triangle , the letters of this font can be represented directed either to one or the other side, and it is impossible for them to create 3D models.

In his blog, Marci Hegedus explains how he achieved this effect, using the letter 'R' as an example: he combines the left upper part and the right lower part of two 3D models, after which he draws a projection.

Corning develops anti-bacterial and anti-glare screen

The creators of Gorilla Glass are developing a new anti-glare and anti-bacterial glass. New development presented Corning Vice President Jeffrey Evenson at the MIT Technology Review Mobile Summit 2013.

"Corning is working on a glass that can fight bacteria and viruses," said Evenson from the scene. The company began working on antimicrobial technology for the medical industry, but then realized that the market for such glass is much wider.



The first photo shows the work of the new Corning antibacterial glass, compared with the usual one. And this is definitely good news for all of us - research shows that there are more bacteria on cell phones than on the toilet, kitchen table or door handle.

Evenson also says that Corning is developing an anti-glare screen that can significantly reduce the problem of mobile devices working in the sun. In the second photo in the center is not a hole at all - this is new Corning glass.

Let's go back a little and define the word ‚Äúcrisis‚Äù.

If we have a clear goal, there is a resource and we know a way to achieve the goal - this is the task. If there is a goal and a resource, but not a solution, this is a problem. Speaking about solving a problem, we usually talk about finding a solution - a way to achieve a goal, or reviewing the goal itself. But when there is no solution and there is no resource for finding this very method - this is a crisis. A crisis is a situation in which there are no resources to solve a problem / task and find another way to solve it.

Did any of you have problems yesterday that you have no way to cope with? Your tasks, not global. Yours Not? You are working. Continue to perform tasks. Yes, some of the projects will have to stop. Some even freeze. This is called ‚Äúproject portfolio management.‚Äù AND? Where is your personal crisis?

Now information flows are trying to take away your resource - time (TV, emotions) and money (aah, urgently need to buy a new wardrobe, until the national currency is completely depreciated).

At the same time, during a crisis, people make qualitative leaps:
  • Sergey Korolev, who in the first years after World War II creates a ballistic missile that has no analogues
  • William Deming, who revolutionized the quality of production in post-war Japan
  • Vladimir Zworykin, who left for the United States, invented the kinescope when the Great Depression raged around
  • Max Factor - an emigrant from Odessa, who fled from the civil war in Russia, opened a company for the production of make-up, and became a world famous brand of cosmetics


What to go far - let's take 2008. The global crisis, the war of Russia with Georgia, which pre-downturned the Russian stock market. The first thing that companies reduce in a crisis is budgets for marketing, training, travel, and other non-production activities.

And exactly this year one of us opens his training company, and the second begins to engage in sales training of a large training center. It would seem that conditions are the worst. But if you think about it - how much then managed to do everything! New programs, new projects - and this against the background of the rest when groaning about the crisis around.

What did our colleagues do at this moment? We sat in their seats and did not twitch. What happened after 2 years? We filled our first bumps and reached a new level of income, business and work pleasure. Colleagues continued to sit in the same place where they were sitting. On the same frozen salaries, but in conditions when money began to cost cheaper.

Real life story

One of our students at the time of the 2008 crisis turned out to be on the market (his salary was too high to maintain the necessary margin in the project). He came to a nearby outsourcing company with the words: ‚ÄúGive me a job.‚Äù

Since there was no work during the crisis, they threw up their hands on that side. As a result, he got less money for a job that he exceeded in qualification, but which required long-term business trips.

Two weeks later he went on a business trip to the customer for two months. Since the technical competence of a person and his English were at the level, these two months he actively communicated with the customer - about their business, needs for crisis, marketing, etc.

As a result, he returned with a new project, which he became the head of. The project was to develop solutions for stock markets, which during the crisis just earn the most because of the increasing number of transactions.

The ‚Äúbifurcation point‚Äù in system theory is an unstable or critical state, from which the system goes either to a fundamentally new level, or remains at the previous level to the next and, usually, quite a long period of time.

That is, we either ‚Äúslow down‚Äù and for another couple of years we are weaving at the same ‚Äúslowed down‚Äù speed (well, the crisis is ...), or quietly ‚Äúadd gas‚Äù in order to move to the next orbit like an electron. New project, new customer, new product or new business.

That is, it is not clear what to do, it is not clear where to go, and how much exactly buckwheat or square meters in the newly built building in the steppe should be purchased.

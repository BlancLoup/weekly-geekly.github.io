SuperJob IT meetup. Work with business requirements at different project scales

SuperJob invites you to meetup "Working with business requirements at different scales of the project." We meet on February 15 in our office on Malaya Dmitrovka.

In continuation of the previous meeting on pre-project works, we will discuss the difference and similarity of opposite cases: working with business architecture and the quick release of a separate service feature. We invite system and business analysts, as well as managers of IT-projects to join the discussion.



At the last mitap, we discussed the general principles of working with a pre-project.

This time we will discuss extremes in terms of the temporal and functional scale of the project:

  • on the one hand, pre-design work in large companies where business architecture is required;
  • on the other hand - a short release of a separate feature of the service.

We will also discuss the knowledge and practice of modeling, which is useful in all cases.

Timing and speakers:

19.00 - 19.40 - Sergey Nuzhnenko darkboatman , leading system analyst at Superjob, co-founder of the School of Systems Analysis, founder of the Systems Analysis Laboratory.

Topic: Features of modeling and transition from the business layer of system requirements

Blind mixing of business, system and architectural requirements or decisions is a common mistake when discussing, analyzing, designing and communicating requirements.

We will discuss how not to confuse the layers, how to reflect the interrelation between them, why it is impossible to get by with just one.

  • Requirement Levels
  • Business processes / cases against system cases
  • Business class model versus logical system data model
  • Features of working with roles
  • Interconnection of layers
  • Business-level and system-level collisions
  • Where to put architectural solutions

19.40 - 20.20 - Natalya Tatarnikova , business architect at Diasoft, 17 years in the design, development and implementation of complex systems in credit and financial organizations.

Topic: Think like an architect. Features of building a business architecture in general and on the pre-project - in particular

Continuing the topic of requirements analysis at the stage of a pre-project or presale, I want to talk about the necessity and peculiarities of the formation of a business architecture at this stage of the project. In my report I will cover the following topics:

  • How does requirements analysis affect business architecture and vice versa?
  • When and why do we need a business architecture at the pre-project and when it is not needed?
  • What does a business architect do on a pre-project?
  • Features of building architecture, and what does good business architecture mean at this stage?
  • There will be references to real examples from practice and answers to questions.

20.20 - 21.00 - Artur Uzbek Arthurarch , Superjob Product Manager

Topic: Requirements under uncertainty

Using the example of the launch of the Open Employer service, it will tell how the product owner forms the requirements for the product and why they quickly become obsolete.

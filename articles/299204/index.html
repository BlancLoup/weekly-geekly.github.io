Report on the results of "My Circle" for February 2016

In February, we have two records. We crossed the threshold of 600 vacancies. And received an average of 8.27 vacancy responses.

Let's continue, as always, see how many responses to a vacancy were in the most popular areas of activity. Take a look at the overall trend in the average response over the past 5 months. And we will show the most popular February vacancies by the number of responses.



  • In February, we had 622 vacancies. Each, on average, received a 8.27 response.
  • As always, the easiest way to find a designer. For one design job had 15.7 response.
  • It was also not very difficult to find a maker-up and a manager. For each vacancy in these areas of activity, there were on average 12.8 responses.
  • It was more difficult to find an administrator, tester, programmer and application developer. Each vacancy in these fields of activity accounted for an average of 7.2 responses.


Next, we look at the overall trend in the average number of responses in the most popular areas of activity.



  • Design vacancies are still firmly in first place in terms of demand among specialists. For 5 months the average response to the vacancy of the designer was 17 people.
  • In February, vacancies for web designers gave a record figure of 13.1 responses. And in 5 months the average response in this area was 10.3. These are the second most popular jobs, after design.
  • Jobs for programmers regained their former popularity after an unexpected failure in January. For 5 months, the average response to a programmer's vacancy was 6.9.
  • Traditionally, complex job application developers in February showed a record with 7 job responses. On average, over 5 months, the average response in this area was 5.6.

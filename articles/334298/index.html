Here you are not DevOps: the fate of a sysadmin in a small business

Our content manager, having worked for two years as an engineer and 10 years in IT, usually remembers the Day of the system administrator and the Day of the programmer almost at 22:00 the day before. This time we were lucky - we ourselves remembered the holiday on Wednesday and began to plan the beautiful last Friday of July. In the meantime, thinking - and what is he, a system administrator 2017? What does he do, how does he put up with the clouds, how does he survive among the software and hardware diversity? Gathered, talked, discussed the observations. It turned out interesting - set out literary and with a checklist, although the lively discussion was more fun.

Our company, due to product features ( desktop CRM system for SMEs ) does not go to meetings at Gazprom and Russian Post, does not wear ties and does not sign memorandums on memorandums. Simply put, we plow - we automate micro, small and medium businesses across Russia and the CIS countries. And then they earn more due to the acceleration of processes, order in the routine, planning, management of tasks and customers, etc.

Naturally, we work remotely - we install licenses, we refine CRM according to agreed TZ, we train, we provide technical support. Fortunately, modern network technologies work for us and hand in hand with us. But our biggest helpers are system administrators of companies. They are cool and different, but they are united by one thing: they are multifunctional employees with a large workload and diverse tasks.

Agile, Machine Learning, DevOps - we do, but have not heard


If you read Habr, Russian specialized publications, foreign resources, then the emergence of new types of system administrator is obvious.

  • Sysadmin + developer in one person or system administrator in the composition of micro-development teams. This is DevOps, continuous development and deployment of software on hardware. It is a very useful thing, it closely links development and operation, it helps to release a product quickly and without critical and major bugs on production. We in RegionSoft did this from the first day of development (which is 11 years old) - even then, when the word DevOps didn‚Äôt rattle around the IT world. By the way, we also have elements of Agile and even extreme programming.

  • A sysadmin manager is a person who knows how the infrastructure is organized, but it is all brought to the cloud, so he owns SAM, signs the SPLA and controls the costs of maintaining the IT service. His task is to optimize costs, beat budgets, monitor the number and profile of license usage. Park equipment is most often monitored by his colleagues.

  • Sysadmin carrier best practices. By the way, the best practices of ITSM and ITIL in particular are excellent things that should be implemented in a company of absolutely any size. This does not mean that you need all the procedures and knowledge of all the volumes - it is enough to adopt the basic principles and follow them. The result is not long in coming. So, the system administrator with the understanding of best practices always knows how to build the IT service of a company (even three people) as a powerful team within a team: manages incidents, monitors ticket processing, accumulates a knowledge base, etc.


    Sysadmins of all types are not in the spirit

  • Sysadmin-customizer - corresponds to the latest trends of corporate IT infrastructure. As a rule, with the help of scripts, configurators and other technical solutions, it optimizes each workplace and each application to meet the needs of users: from debugging the monitor's color rendering to the designer, to setting up the main window of the CRM system for each employee. The task of such a specialist is to make the workplace of each employee comfortable in the name of productivity growth.

  • The sysadmin-beaver is the last on the list, but a trend and the right specialist. WannaCry and Petya scared the business of all levels in earnest, and suddenly the notions of ‚Äúbackup‚Äù, ‚Äúantivirus‚Äù, ‚Äúkeylogger‚Äù, ‚Äúsystem update‚Äù, etc. began to emerge in the heads' memory. Such a system administrator not only monitors the state of the IT infrastructure, he is able to make her safe and knows how to use and correctly combine hardware and software protection methods, including from one of the most dangerous agents - the disloyal internal employee of the company.

Of course, in their pure form, such types do not occur, and so much the better, because in one person several skills are combined. These are strong demanded experts. There are many in any business - from micro to international corporations.

But still the most common type of system administrator in small and medium businesses is an ordinary system administrator . Checklist for survival in the world of small and medium businesses


We have already told about some solutions that can be opposed to problems, but we have a small checklist of tips for system administrators of small and medium-sized companies on how to make work easier.

  • Record all the work done, document it. Best of all, if you start a ticket system, to which users will send their requests for breakdowns, problems, etc. In this way, you can streamline your work, accumulate a knowledge base, easily transfer cases when you quit, raise a controversial issue and, in the end, print out all the tasks, resources and results to management if it doubts your usefulness.

  • Stay tuned for updates (paid and free) - this is a trivial advice that is broken all the time. So you protect yourself from a number of troubles, and users will receive current and powerful versions of their working software.

  • Make backups. Make backups. Make backups. No, they still don‚Äôt do them - and for this reason client bases, commercial information, important system configurations, etc. are lost.

  • Do not believe in the friendliness of the world to the IT infrastructure. Even if you are a tiny company, your far from small client base can disturb competitors, which means there can be hacking, password theft, transfer of a password from the cloud system, data transfer to a USB flash drive, data printing, etc. A little paranoia in the work of the sysadmin will not interfere - business will appreciate.

  • Do not use official position in rascal purposes. Alas, we are all human and often vicious. The rule is simple: do not read or send someone‚Äôs correspondence in the mail and instant messengers without urgent business need, do not listen to calls, do not harm the Internet speed, do not take revenge and do not try to substitute people. You have almost the greatest opportunities in the company - use them rationally and in good faith.

  • Do not postpone problem solving and ticket analysis. The more they accumulate, the more difficult it will be to get things done and complete tasks on time. The quality of work of other employees and the company as a whole depends on your efficiency.

  • Differentiate access rights at the network level, PC, applications. Each employee must be endowed with the scope of rights in accordance with the position and security requirements. Particular attention should be paid to newcomers, field agents and employees who intend to leave the company in the near future or working on the last two weeks.

  • Have a minimum swap pool. Analyze the software and hardware usage profile, predict wear / end of the resource and ask the management for permission to purchase the necessary licenses and hardware so that there is no downtime. Well, it is clear that all peripherals such as mouse-cartridge-keyboards should always be available, because Spilled tea and a drum scratched by a clip are not uncommon.

  • Conduct audit and monitoring. Use specialized software to monitor devices, licenses, networks and peripherals, check license and equipment numbers, set up a failure alert system. This is great to facilitate the process of managing the IT infrastructure in general and inventory in particular.

  • Learn how to prepare sound budgets. Any head of the company is focused on profit - if you correctly and convincingly justify spending and the need to update the park, you are likely to sign an estimate. If not, then let the first failure become a lesson for the chef - ‚Äúremove‚Äù the deadlines and other negative results and transfer to the manager.

  • Keep up with new technologies, learn. Technology is developing at an incredible speed, and lagging behind them is a matter of a couple of months. It is not necessary to introduce everything that you see into the work, but it is necessary to know the main trends: for example, virtualization, IoT, neural networks, new OS versions, new types of hardware, etc. Perhaps you will have an idea how new technologies will be able to optimize and reduce the cost of IT infrastructure, and save you from headaches.

  • Get involved in business processes in a company. If a company introduces a new software (for example, a CRM system) or develops a new budget / business plan, do not ward off participation, but work in a team and be aware of all matters. At a minimum, you then support it all.

  • Love your work and do not lose your sense of humor - without this, well, not at all. Yes, you yourself know.

And another appeal to the leaders of such companies: dear colleagues, a system administrator is not a technical staff, not an errand boy and not the first to be cut, because everything is in the cloud. This is a guarantee of the healthy functioning of the entire IT system of the company: with a limited range of tools, it guarantees the smooth operation of your entire staff. The system administrator and IT service are far from what it is worth saving on in 2017, even if you have 5-10 people and you are not developing software.

Hi, Habr

Over the past year, the topic of national standards, various instructions of state regulators and other related things has become one of the most discussed in the Russian IoT community - which, however, is not surprising, just look at the short list of recent events:

  • Adoption of a preliminary national standard for the NB-Fi protocol of Waviot
  • The recommendation of the Ministry of Finance (it is also the Ministry of Communications) when using remote reading of electricity metering devices use only and exclusively the Strizh XNB protocol
  • vague GKRCh decisions proclaiming something incomprehensible about the use of Russian-made base stations in LPWAN networks
  • reminding the ILV and SCRF of the registration procedure of these base stations and the networks themselves
  • the long-awaited and at the same time unexpected expansion of unlicensed ranges

All these events were rather vigorously discussed between people directly involved in the market, but only a few compressed skeptical comments from IoT market professionals and uncontrollably optimistic stories of officials and other people always ready for import substitution in the five-year format in three years spilled out.

Even intended to be public, the discussion of the draft standard NB-Fi in Technical Committee 194 of Rosstandard in fact from the public turned out to be completely closed.

We would like to correct this situation.



So, at InoThings ++ 2019 (Moscow, April 4) the first real public discussion of the topic of national standards, state regulation and state support in the field of the Internet of Things will be held, in which we will try to present all possible points of view from the speakers, and give the listeners an opportunity to ask their questions.

Here, of course, it is necessary to make a reservation - direct publicity is limited to conference visitors and spectators of the live broadcast; but this is a natural consequence of the very nature of our event - alas, we have tickets to buy.

We do not impose any other restrictions on the nature of the discussion.

Intel closes Vaunt smart glasses project along with the entire division of New Devices Group

Intel has officially confirmed plans to close the New Devices Group and, in particular, to complete the project to create Vaunt smart glasses, which broadcast the image on the retina and look more like a stylish accessory than a smart device.

Some specialists from a team of 200 people will have to leave the company if they cannot take positions in other divisions, writes The Information . In 2016, 800 people worked in the New Devices Group.



Intel formed the New Devices Group division in 2013 with the goal of developing fitness trackers and smart glasses. The company has invested several hundred million dollars in projects, bought external companies, but the team could not have a serious impact on the market of wearable devices.

In 2014, Intel bought the developer of fitness trackers Basis Science, the company entered the New Devices Group. The main product of the company was the Basis Band tracker. Under the wing of Intel were created new smart watches Intel Basis Peak.

During the first year since the start of sales, Basis Peak had a problem with overheating, the gadget could leave burns and blisters on the skin. The problem could not be solved programmatically, and Intel had to withdraw gadgets, reimbursing users the amount of the purchase: 234 US dollars in the US and 244 euros in Europe.

In 2017, Intel has reduced 80% of the staff responsible for developing fitness trackers. The company decided to focus on augmented reality technology.

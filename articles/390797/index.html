Tesla Motors: GM is lobbying a bill banning us from selling electric cars in Indiana



Tesla Motors has long been fighting the laws of some US states, where direct sale of cars to customers is prohibited. In some states, Tesla wins, in other carmakers and dealers, who do their best to prevent direct sales. Tesla had problems with the legislative lobby of dealers in states such as Arizona, Texas, Virginia, New Jersey. Now there is a problem of a similar character in Indiana.

This state is considering a bill that would prohibit car manufacturers from owning another dealer license. Tesla Motors has a license that allows a company to sell electric cars to its customers in Indiana directly. Tesla does not want to work with dealers for many reasons, including purely commercial ones - it is unprofitable for dealers themselves to spend forces on the sale of electric cars, getting a minimum margin at the output.

Now Tesla Motors asks its customers to contact the senators, expressing dissatisfaction with the new bill.

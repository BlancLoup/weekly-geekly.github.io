Thecus N1200 Review

Those who follow my reviews will surely remember the recent QNAP boxing review. Then some wondered - " Hey, where is ethernet and raid ?". Friends, let's not all at once - I propose to stretch the fun! Now Thecus's modelka with ethernet (and not only!) And very soon - a review of a pair of devices with a RAID (0-5)! ;)



So, Thecus N1200 - as always, a detailed review. Traffic.

Rather, you were right, saying then that a single disk does not solve the problems of lack of free space. But since the class of such devices is and is constantly being updated, it means that there is a need for this.

As always, I'll start with a small introductory part about the manufacturer. Taiwanese company Thecus (more precisely, Thecus Technology ) was founded in 2004 and it was from this year that it began to fight for a place in the sun in the niche of inexpensive NAS devices, providing devices of various levels of complexity to more than 40 countries (including Russia). There are options from 1 to 16 disks - something a home user or a company of a rather large scale can look after themselves.

Today, on my desk is not just a box with space for a disk inside, but an independent and highly functional server, albeit a small one. It is an entry level and designed for home use or for small businesses. I open the box.



Equipment


The box is quite small, with a plastic handle for easy carrying. Inside it were:

- Thecus N1200 server ;
- Stand for vertical installation;
- Adapter for power and power cord to it;
- Ethernet cable;
- A metal cover support for a disk;
- Instructions and driver disk;
- A set of screws;
- Silica gel.

Honestly, for the first time I see silica gel in a box not from under the shoe - but, it seems, not from one factory :). The server itself is located in the geometric center of the package - it is surrounded by a protective ‚Äúskin‚Äù of foam material on all sides.


Appearance


I saw a lot of NAS ( Network Attached Storage ) and just external storage ‚Äî somewhere the controller was located along the disk (this made the device thick), somewhere behind the disk (which made the device long). In this case, all the electronics is located on the board, which is located on the side of the disk, but this does not make it very thick or long. The edges are rounded, no sharp edges. Yes, the dimensions (in mm) are 45 √ó 181x160.
The black metal surface is almost unmarked, slightly rough.



The front side is made of black glossy plastic, at the very top of which is a gray device power button, and below it are operation indicators: power, status, wan, lan, hdd and usb. Power is always lit in bright blue. Under the indicators there is an instant copy button (from the usb-port located below), information from a flash drive, fotik or other external device.



There are 61 ventilation holes on the top of the device, a small cooler with automatically controlled rotation speed of the blades is on the bottom. After all, the network device is supposed to work permanently and the cool climate inside will only contribute to this.



The most necessary - behind. From the top down: an anti-theft lock hole, another USB 2.0 port, one eSATA (External SATA) connector, 4 100-megabit RJ-45 ports for connecting devices over a network, a gigabit WAN connector (also RJ-45), a hole reset button and power connector. The only thing that I did not find information about is a small hole at the very top of the device - apparently, it is intended to install an external Wi-Fi adapter.



As I have already said, the kit includes a stand for vertical installation of the device - it is metal in itself, but outside it is covered with soft, finely porous material. Such a move will not allow to scratch the surface of the server, crawl into the next room, as well as additionally quench the vibrations from the disk.

In order to get started, you need to install a hard disk inside the device. To do this, unscrew the two screws that are located on the end sides of the server. After that, the cover with buttons is quietly removed.

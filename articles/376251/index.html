Span "Cassini" near Saturn: animated video in 4K



The Cassini interplanetary station sent about 30 thousand photographs of Saturn, its rings and satellites to Earth. These photos have already been studied by scientists, thanks to which the piggy of scientific knowledge has increased considerably. Since there are a lot of photos, and they were made with very small time intervals, one can create an animation sequence of them.

This is what Stephen van Vuuren, who created the first such video in 2011, did. After that, Stephen decided to improve and supplement the video, which was done. According to the author, the video was created exclusively from the photo, without using 3D models. In the work of the author useful Adobe Photoshop & After Effects.



One frame has hundreds or even thousands of photos, of which the author has compiled a fairly smooth sequence. Video quality in 4K allows you to view it on an arbitrarily large display.

On the electronic document flow in Russia, its cheerful parochial features and the situation in general



Hello!

We make electronic document management systems for different companies. Large banks, the administration of Perm , various production associations and state-owned companies. I'll tell you about the features of this sphere and rakes that are waiting around every corner. The advantages of electronic document management systems are such that you can not argue in comparison with traditional paper.

But no, one of the most fun problems is staff sabotage. Why? Because when the implementation is over, the manager will better see each specific document (any from any employee). That is, there will be more control. Speaking in simpler language - in particular, stealing will become much more difficult.

The first example of sabotage is when the workflow system is introduced along with the automation of processes simultaneously. At this moment, the people are deeply indignant at the "square-nested" method and other innovations, and almost gather rallies that all this is from the evil one. Most often observed in remote branches, where for the first time somehow comes control. Naturally, it is solved only by the will of one of the first persons of the company, otherwise all the chances of getting stuck in interdepartmental "showdowns".

The second stage is after implementation, when a clear understanding comes that it is necessary to work transparently. In one state-owned company, users even wrote treatises that not smoothly opening screen forms of the system contributes to the deterioration of vision and the development of epilepsy.

But let's start from the beginning, from why, in general, Russia needs SED when there is already a pack of paper, a pen, a stamp and a hole punch.

Why do you need a company SED?


First, control. Who, where and what he did, who did not work ,. As with any automation, SED solves this problem quite effectively. Plus, you can see the details of who and what is doing to the last person in the company. It can be seen where a document is stuck and who is at fault.

Secondly, getting rid of paper. Products and solutions that allow to save here and now come out on top now. Pay off faster, show results faster. Paper logistics is expensive, and if it turns out to get rid of printing and sending paper documents within the group of companies (holding), this is already a significant bright achievement. For him, the initiator may well give the star of the hero and generally note the career. Also, many people are interested in the functionality of a handwritten electronic signature (not an EDS, but one when the client or employee signs on an electronic scoreboard over the image of the document being signed). It helps to receive documents from various mobile devices, plus, as Western colleagues say, when a person signs each time, instead of clicking a button, this greatly increases responsibility.

Third, the availability of information. Any information important to the company should be available in the system at any time, even if the employee leaves. If you have never looked for a piece of paper on the shipment of a steel car in a company with a hundred factories - you do not understand this pain.

Fourth, information security. Employees, in principle, should not keep files where they want, but store them in the SED in the necessary sections. It is important to protect files not only when they are stored on disk, but also when they are moved, both inside and outside the company. IRM systems (Information Rights Management) are used for this, they allow you to protect documents outside the organization‚Äôs perimeter. For the recipient to read the file, he must have the appropriate rights to decrypt.

Finally, there is another important aspect of the requirements for SED - the system should be comfortable so that employees do not beat their heads against the wall, but work normally in it (otherwise the level of sabotage increases). People should choose this tool by default instead of sending files by mail or keeping them on a local disk.

In particular, in this direction is the development of corporate social networks (the same Jive can also solve this problem as a corporate social network of the company). When integrating a corporate social network, corporate mail and EDMS, it turns out that it is much easier for users to immediately put a document in the system than to send a regular message.

Vendors


In the SED projects we built solutions on both Western and domestic platforms. Western ECMs have a positive experience with EMC Documentum and with Alfresco. The first is a large commercial platform from a large vendor. The second is a compact, but unlimited, open source version of ECM.

From Russian products we work with DocsVision and Directum. They are distinguished from Western ECM by the rich functionality specialized for the SED tasks. That is, to start a project implementation and get a good result can be much faster. In fairness, it must be said that there are functionally developed superstructures above western ECMs that facilitate the implementation of EDS projects. CROC, for example, built such an add-on over Documentum and Alfresco.

There is also a separate class of platforms with the help of which it is possible to solve electronic document flow problems. Speech about BPM-platforms. For example, our contract is automated on K2 + Documentum. The KM Blackpearl BPM platform is responsible for routing electronic documents that are placed in the Documentum repository.

Who needs EDS in the Russian Federation


Initially, there were no electronic document management systems in Russia. There was a hole punch. Then came the electronic numbering of incoming-outgoing (some kind, and accounting), then - storage of images, because the paper was often lost. That is, the main reason then was the creation of an electronic archive of papers, backup. It was only in the early 2000s that the movement began on the formulation of processes in electronic form - that which will become modern SED. Just in 2005, our project began in Perm, where all the officials had to be transplanted to the SED. At first, secretaries worked there for managers, because very few people understood what it was and why, but the most important iron will led to the fact that gradually every paper and every penny (I exaggerate) began to be taken into account (imagine the "joy" of everyone involved). The generation has changed - and the system works, but managers are already familiar. By the way, in the same place we introduced a legally-significant EDS, which was quite difficult at that time.

For many, the volume of documents has grown so much that the old SED simply does not pull the base. It sounds ridiculous, but it is often enough - a big problem for companies with old implementations.

Quite a few companies now put from scratch. As a rule, large businesses already have some kind of solution. If the SED is normally implemented, then it is an aid for business processes, or a generally based process support system. On it everything keeps in the company, and therefore if you became big business - you automatically already have something like that. If the electronic document management system has problems, then the problems are with the whole organization.

There was, for example, the case when the company remained to live on the SED, which ceased to develop 2-3 years ago. We need new features - and the vendor can not do them. Somewhere crutches were scored, somewhere external modules were pinned up, somewhere they just lost some of the processes.

One of the new reasons for modernizing EDS is interfaces. People are used to Facebook contacts, and the SED for them should be just as simple. It happens that the cost of training at some point outweighs the cost of modernization. And most of the new EDS is almost always already usable.

About the market


If you look at the western situation, the ECM market there grows by about 8-10% per year. We have a similar course, but there are two big fundamental differences: firstly, the world has a fairly large share of open-source solutions (and we are just beginning to look closely at them), secondly, the average share of electronic document management systems in ECM sales is about 40%, and we have - 50%.

In our country at the beginning of 2014 there were more than 6.25 million jobs that can be automated using ECM-functional systems. That is, specifically in Russia, the demand for electronic document management platforms has not yet been closed, the market will still develop and grow to a saturation stage for at least a couple of years (taking into account the current situation - more, the process has slowed down).

Why is that? Simple enough, the fact is that medium-sized businesses are entering this market. Years, say, 10 years ago there was only an option to write something different, a small applied one. The license for the finished product was simply unaffordable for the price. Now there is an opportunity to buy something quite affordable and at the same time fully functional, or assemble from open-source components (with the necessary qualifications). Naturally, the state sector has greatly influenced the development of electronic document management systems in Russia - there is a big movement on electronic exchange between departments, and there is a difference in comparison with the situation of 10 years. Plus, the natural factor of geographic distribution works - not every enterprise in Russia can often travel in person because of distances, therefore for large companies it becomes important to improve management efficiency. That is, first of all, to see all those responsible and get rid of the uncontrolled paper exchange, shorten the search for documents.

In a crisis, there is a chance of growth - the introduction of the SED can reduce printing costs, and significantly improve the efficiency of processes. For some, the crisis will be a chance to finally ‚Äúpush through‚Äù the long-needed implementation program.

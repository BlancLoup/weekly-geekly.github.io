On the quality of services in the RBC Hosting Center

Greetings habravchane!



Today I want to tell you my story and convey to the public the current problems of one of the leaders of hosting in Russia - RBC Hosting Center .



My first acquaintance with the "Hosting Center" began in July of 2008, then I had to abandon the dedicated server and look for alternatives. I chose a long and thoroughly. I stopped at 2 options: Agave or HC. I disliked agave for mail filters, and my choice fell on HC, as a more popular and, therefore, reliable provider. As it turned out, nothing ...

Update: Habr - power. Today the support call and offered to transfer the VPS to another server.


So, July-August of 2008. Ordered virtual hosting service (Large) and Primary \ Secondary DNS ...

Shared hosting


The patient at the time:
Forum IPB (license). 800 unique per day, database ~ 70MB, 30 000 messages.

First problems:

  • The RAM limit of the MAIN domain is 128MB, the limit of subdomains is 32MB. The forum is located at forum.domain.ru and when trying to upload a photo> 2 MB, the user received a white page (as you know, IPB doesn‚Äôt even deal with resizing the image , but simply creates a thumbnail). When I asked to make a forum, I was answered negatively by the main domain and most interestingly: I received the same answer to the question whether it is possible to order a separate virtual hosting service for a third-level domain. Well, okay, I wrote a warning on the forum and limited my quotas ... You can live with this ...
  • September 2008 For 48+ hours, my DNS zone fell off, which was located on the NS servers ns1.hc.ru and ns2.hc.ru. Those support was notified within 2 hours after the crash (thanks to unialert.com ). The site stopped opening after 6 hours of failure. As a result - 2 days downtime. To my question: ‚ÄúIs it possible to register another NS server zone in the DNS, in order to further insure against such problems‚Äù - I received a negative answer again.


VPS hosting


Since I already, in my opinion, ponastupal on all rakes with virtual hosting, I decided to try transfer to VPS ... There are more places, and there is enough processor time and 1GB RAM and DNS I can keep there ...

The patient at the moment:
Forum IPB (license). 1200 unique per day, database ~ 100MB, 50 000 messages. ubuntu server 8.04, nginx 7.30, php-fpm + eaccelerator, SVN over ssh, Mysql 5.1, search on the forum is implemented through sphinx, statistics is collected by munin.

Plant for the production of rake:

  • September 2008 SSH fell off on the 22nd port for a day (which, by the way, cannot be killed with a VPS). After regaining control over the server, we had to raise another SSH and webmin for everyone ... The problem is not critical, but unpleasant. SVN lives on the server and was accessed through SSH, the work got one more day :(

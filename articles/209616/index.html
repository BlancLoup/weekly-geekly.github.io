The technical devices shown in the previous sections are entourage by themselves, and their use greatly enhances the realism of the gameplay. It is also possible to use technologies that are not directly involved in the gameplay, but nevertheless significantly improve the anthrability of locations and players. Technology and Mastering


Directly for the mastering process, the introduction of modern technologies also carries many new opportunities.

The database of players available from different workshop points (reception, trowel, deadman) allows you not only to quickly access the players' data, but also to quickly make notes about overwriting when a player exits the deadman, his quests, stats, status, and other master notes.

If the conditions of the game imply that each player carries a personal module, the player‚Äôs data including his current state, the resulting impacts, the current status of completing quests, and other information can be stored directly in it. Thus, we get a player's electronic passport, access to which data can be obtained not only at the workshop stationary point, but also ‚Äúin the field‚Äù.

Installation of reference repeaters on the polygon provides even more interesting possibilities. First, we get the opportunity to directly transfer game information and influences to personal players' modules, which eliminates the need for personal downloads when the game environment changes. Secondly, we get the ability to track the player‚Äôs status and contacts in real time, which eliminates the possibility of cheating by the players and allows you to more quickly track events at the site. Thirdly, we get the opportunity to determine the location of each player in real time, which saves us from situations when we have to call the location wizards to find such a character.

Another interesting possibility is the option of installing surveillance cameras at key points of the test site. Cameras can become both a part of gaming reality, as it was at the ‚ÄúCost of Living‚Äù , and a purely masterful tool for tracking events at the test site in real time.

Technical part and rake


For the most beginners (and not only) there is a very good site - easyelectronics.ru . There is a very rich set of articles on electronics in general and microcontrollers in particular.

The easiest option to get acquainted with the world of programmable hardware is Arduino boards. They contain an Atmel microcontroller with a set of digital inputs and outputs, analog inputs and outputs, they have standardized sizes and pin arrangements. The functionality of the boards is complemented by expansion cards - shields. Different shields allow you to control powerful loads (motors, bright lights), communicate via Ethernet, Wi-Fi, GPRS, receive coordinates via GPS and much more.
Perhaps the most promising option is STM32 microcontrollers from STMicroelectronics. Their development already requires specialized knowledge and skills, but their capabilities are much wider, and the microcontrollers themselves are much cheaper than the previous version.

For the connection of various devices without wires, there is a rich set of transceiver chips (transceivers), as well as ready-made modules containing a transceiver chip, all the piping necessary for it, and often the antenna. Transceivers usually operate in the following frequency ranges, each with its own propagation characteristics. It:
  • 430 ... 450 MHz - they require a rather large antenna, but they are the most long-range and passable. Worse reflected from the surrounding terrain, resulting in a more even coating.
  • 800 ... 900 MHz - they require a smaller antenna than the previous version, but they pass through obstacles worse
  • 2.4 GHz - are the smallest, but the radiation at these frequencies is quite well extinguished even by the foliage, especially wet, which must be taken into account when raising the polygon network, since Wi-Fi works on the same frequencies. Radio waves of this range are well reflected from the surrounding relief, which leads to the formation of a rather uneven pattern of propagation due to the interference of direct and re-reflected signals

It is also necessary to make sure in advance that the necessary functions are available with the components ordered. For example, the Chipcon CC Series transceivers have the ability to take readings of the received signal strength, and the popular Nordic nRF24L01 transceiver can only report the presence or absence of a received signal.

All assembled devices must be driven in advance in combat, or close to such conditions. At this stage, something will surely come out. From a failure in a program with a sudden combination of a number of factors (one transmitter after one stone, another after another, as a result of which they do not hear each other and try to broadcast simultaneously), to the equally sudden failure in the ‚Äúiron part‚Äù (for example, from a blow or battery shakes happily fly out of cheap open-type Chinese battery compartments).

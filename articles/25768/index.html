Google officially opened a medical office

An online service, Google Health, has been launched to keep medical records and user histories, the Associated Press reported. It allows you to store on the Internet data such as medical history, information about the medical condition, allergic reactions, drugs shown and laboratory results.



First of all, Google Health will be useful for emergency hospitalization. In this case, it is enough for doctors to connect to the system in order to obtain complete information about the incoming patient. The service is also convenient for treatment in different clinics and simultaneous monitoring with several doctors.

All user data is protected by a login and password. Google Health fully supports the HIPAA medical data security standards adopted in 1996. To simplify the work of physicians with Google Health, the system supports the export of data from offline applications.

Google Search Engine has been developing Google Health for more than a year and a half. Google Health testing began in February 2008. Then 10 thousand patients of the Cleveland Clinic were connected to the service. For one and a half months, they entered health information into their virtual card.

The Google Health beta is currently running, with no ads. Google representatives stressed that although in the future the service will be used as an advertising platform, the number of advertisements will be kept to a minimum.

Google Health is not the first online medical service. Previously, Microsoft HealthVault and Revolution Health projects were launched, allowing users to store their medical data on the Internet.

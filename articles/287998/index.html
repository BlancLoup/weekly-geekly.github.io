New Media Forms

All of us bloggers, in one way or another, keep diaries. Each is guided by its principles on the part of what and how to write. The frequency of recordings is also chosen by us. Most writes occasionally. There are those who add diary entries every day .

Imagine a completely ordinary person living a normal life, say, an accountant *. He gets up in the morning. He washes his face. He is having breakfast. He gets some unthinkable way to work. He works. At work, he communicates with people. All this can be pleasant or unpleasant. How can it be pleasant or unpleasant all day. This degree of satisfaction can be expressed in a way of splashing out entries in LJ or another blog / diary and thus evaluate it. Was it a good day? Bad? How much?

So, we come to the word rate. There are a number of ways to evaluate something. For example, put a grade day: "five" or "three." What to take into account? Yes whatever. Did you succeed in implementing the plans? Did you have a fight with the boss today? Did you get paid? And how much?

But for the majority of the assessment, in the form of exposing itself to tsiferok - this is too boring. Yes, and at work * enough. At home we love when everything is clear, simple and beautiful. Therefore, it is better to evaluate your day in color. Moreover, we all know that every person in life has light and dark stripes. In general, of course, except for black and white is full of other colors / shades, but to cope with them is too difficult. Not all of us live impressionists. There is a simpler way: to use gradation from black, as an extreme degree of negative, to white, as an extreme degree of positive.



And imagine what an entertaining picture will come out by the end of the year. If a person throughout his entire diary and painted each entry in one of the shades of gray. The result of the year is evident: 365 (or how many are there) days of life of an ordinary person, expressed in graphically visually understandable language. This is an autobiographical picture, written by him himself, that which reflects life, that which is filled with living energy.

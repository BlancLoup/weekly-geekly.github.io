Linux showed an increase of 64% per year

Finally, Linux showed the growth rates that have long been expected of it. From May to December 2011, the share of Linux in the desktop OS market grew immediately by 64%, according to the global monitoring service Net Market Share , which collects statistics on visitors to 40,000 sites . The unique audience is about 160 million people per month, so the measurement error is extremely small.



Last year, Linux became the fastest growing operating system for desktops, in second place was Windows 7, with a figure of 37%.

For many years, the share of Linux has fluctuated just below 1% .



Now this level is broken with a margin. Is it further up?

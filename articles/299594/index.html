Hello, SaaS | Company Transformation | Employees and technology

Recently, I wondered how and under the influence of what factors the portrait of a corporate client changes, and how changes can be used for sales. As a result, a change matrix was built, which we will talk about today in general and in part about the components.




Something is wrong with companies

I cannot say what exactly is wrong, and here are a few examples - the head of Sberbank of Russia publicly criticizes his own automation projects and wants to buy only the technology of tomorrow. Another example is that you can easily sell the SaaS service, to the marketing department of a large holding company and the head of the department will pay out of pocket. Cloud technologies - infrastructure, PaaS and SaaS have become so accessible that a startup of 4 people can pay for all this. The client is not the same - the client is changing.

In my opinion, all today's changes are in the matrix "People in the organization or Employees - Consultants - The very structure of the organization - Technology." Each component has an impact on the other components, and together they change the company, making it better.



Today I will focus on the changes of the company under the influence of employees and technology. Changes in people are habits and behavioral patterns that directly affect their performance of functional duties or the characteristics of generations. By habits, I mean preferences for the better. An illustration of such changes - if at home an employee uses all the latest innovations in the world of gadgets and software, then why in the office should he work on a desktop computer? About the features of generations, I generally keep silent - 30-year-old "all the stars", and 20-year-old unmanaged, in principle.

In my opinion, the impact of technology on changing a company is not as great as that of employees, and this is why - even when changing IT paradigms, for example, the emergence of cloud technologies, business objectives do not change. At the same time, the proposal of new technologies implies a change in the consumption model of business automation services or IT infrastructure, and this is already changing the company - the tasks and structure of IT departments, for example.

The tangle of change is only confusing.

Why this can happen - for example, in a mature company all processes are built, KPIs are performed, training is adjusted and everything seems to be working, but the IT strategy has not changed for a long time and there are mobile workplaces in it, although the majority of employees come to work with tablets. People have changed and come to work with their well-established habits, and perhaps the introduction of mobile jobs will increase the performance of the company at times?

7 reasons to change with employees - Citrix research

In 2015, Citrix conducted several studies on the development of mobile technologies in companies and the attitude of employees towards remote work. Excerpts from the research will help see the influence of employees and technology on changes in the company.

How will the workspace change? - WorkAnywhere

In 2016, more and more workspace designers will abandon the traditional office organization model based on open space divided by partitions. The choice will be made in favor of the working environment created on the principle of universities - with places for interaction of user groups in common areas (hubs), common places (laboratories) and separate zones (libraries).

Follow-me technologies have reached a level of development to ensure a high degree of user satisfaction at low cost for widespread use.

Results of work in new conditions

In the course of the research, 43% of employees indicated an increase in productivity, and 91% of users noted a significant increase in morale during the transition to the Remote.

Consumption model moves to mobile



Mobile users - all employees are divided into groups

The first group is the top steering. The second group is traditional employees working outside the office. They are almost all the time working in the mobile mode, so they also need access to data. The third group of employees: traditional office staff, who only occasionally work remotely, but who still need an increasing number of services, including applications for exchanging short messages and files.

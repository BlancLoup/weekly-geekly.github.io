Providing cellular services to a shopping center with non-standard solutions



On the roof of the shopping center "Fair", visible RRU and two sectors:


The main problem of modern architecture from the point of view of the signalman is the windows with aluminum powder in the glasses. In Astrakhan there are a lot of such people, because the extreme heat (by the standards of the central regions) does not surprise anyone here. I don‚Äôt know how about the sun, but here the radio signal on our frequencies accurately reflects and suppresses. If you thought about foil hats, start with windows.

The mall is usually built from pre-fabricated structures. Simply put, inside there is a huge pile of fixtures, from which even the ‚Äúhousehold‚Äù compass deviates quite well. And the glass through which it is very difficult to break through the signal.

That's just such an object, and we appeared three years ago.

To our happiness, the shopping center favorably differed a big hole inside. It was built like a pie: the outer frame and a fairly wide space in the center for escalators, elevators and main aisles. Most modern shopping centers in the regions are built in the same way.

That is, we had a radio-opaque square with a hole inside. And if it were possible to shove the signal exactly in this hole, the connection would be inside everywhere. Well, or almost everywhere, because the walls are made of drywall.

To begin with, it was decided to organize a standard BS deployment scheme. It was clear that the coverage on the second and third floor would be unsatisfactory. Therefore, we made separate sectors and connected several antennas on each floor through dividers.

There were options to push the feeder with pin emitters like in the subway, arrange the micro-cells or stick repeaters. But purely for practical reasons, we decided to establish additional sectors, the benefit was access to the object at the construction stage.

But according to the calculations, the loss in the feeder on the first floor was too great, so I had to place separate RRUs directly under the ceiling

Not far from the shopping center, we have an important transport network node, so optics were led to the building. Next came the question of connecting subscribers to the services of wired Internet and local telephony. Usually such TCs were connected via DSL, but here we went along the Ethernet path. Because we understood that our subscribers in the future will need significantly higher speeds. Then we went to the agreement: every meter of cable, every box - all this required signatures. Engineers had to go to the position many times, just in order to understand what is being mounted to.

Then, in the process, the concept of aisles changed: in addition to departments, ‚Äúislands‚Äù appeared, one of which was Sberbank.

The reason for the failure of the hard drive was the ingress of dust . When working, a small amount of dust particles penetrates into the clean zone, causing initial damage, and then everything happens like an avalanche. The difficulty was in determining where the dust penetrated, but logic and attentiveness do wonders.

A metal platform in the upper right corner is used to attach a flexible cable and connector to the electronics board.



Electronics fee. Two adjacent holes in the bottom right of the board - for the screws that press the contact pads on the board to the connector.



Electronics board dismantled. Connector in the lower right.



Removed connector.



In the right part, the thickness of the pad is increased by a flexible printed circuit board and a conductive ring with a thick layer of solder. When attaching a platform with a train, there is a bias due to different thickness.



As a result, the rubber seal on the platform is in contact with the body not around the entire perimeter. The place of non-contact is perfectly visible under the microscope. It is through this place that dust penetrates inside the disk.



The manufacturer of this site needs to be improved. This may be an increase in the height of the seal or a change in material, leveling the heights of the site in the area of ‚Äã‚Äãattachment or other techniques.
Judging by the reviews and the article mentioned above, we are not the only owners of the failed model. Maybe hard drive manufacturers have a HDD recall program - like car manufacturers?

How to be the one who has already purchased this model?
Before starting operation, it is necessary to seal the side of the electronics board by filling the hole around the connector with a sealant. It is necessary to fill in carefully and to a small height so as not to disturb the flexibility of the moving contacts. The guarantee in this case is likely to suffer, but the life of the disc will last.



How to be someone with a hard drive began to crumble?
It all depends on the degree of wear. If it has just begun, you can try to make a seal. If the seal does not help, then you can make a three-byte gramophone. The idea to use the disc in this capacity is not new, but the implementation with a horn and such a large volume has not yet been encountered. If someone does not understand, then a 10 ohm coil located above the magnet (the two extreme contacts on the connector) is connected to the speaker as a speaker, and then it‚Äôs a matter of physics.







Particularly "lucky" users who have failed two hard drives - they can make a stereo. And those who are able to make a quad-system can only sympathize, since the cost of these four "speakers" is a significant amount.

Email Services Digest: Featured Email Products and Podcasts by Product Hunt

We have already told Pechkin-mail in the blog about interesting books , programs and video blogs on the topic of mailings, UI and usability. In this issue of the digest, we will look at thematic projects that are in the top of the ranking on Product Hunt.


/ Photos by JD Hancock / CC

Front's Good Email Copy helps you find examples of good email texts for distribution. An impressive collection is constantly updated. Developers pay special attention not to design, but to content. All letters are segmented by topic.

Really Good Emails is another example of an aggregator of favorite mailing lists. They are distributed by tasks: discounts, holidays, invitations, product updates, and so on.

Great Email Copy is an alternative version of an email list compilation compiled by Customer.io. The purpose is similar to the others - to study the best practices of work on the mailing list.

Email Generator is a simple email address generator based on the recipient's name and two options for the organization‚Äôs domains. It differs from Email Hunter in that it does not search for information on the Web - all received addresses will require verification.

Email Validator! - A simple tool to check the format and syntactic structure of email-addresses. Allows free use of both the service itself and the source code.

HTML Email provides responsive email templates that can be the starting point for your newsletter. They have been tested on most email clients and are equally well displayed on desktop devices, tablets and smartphones.

The Foundation for Emails 2 from Zurb helps you quickly create an attractive email using Inky's template language, which helps get rid of complex markup.

Ugly Email will detect all popular email tracking services, including Bananatag, Streak, Yesware and others. Finding a spy letter, Ugly Email marks it with an eye icon. To install the extension for Gmail, simply go to the official website of Ugly Email and click the "Add to Chrome" button.

Nylas N1 Email is an open source email client compatible with most email services, including Gmail, Yahoo, iCloud AOL and Outlook. Users can switch between multiple mailboxes, format the text of emails, work offline, track open and click links, and use the delayed email feature. And the main feature of N1 is that it can be changed with the help of extensions, adding new features, for example, the translator.

Zero Email is another client that uses machine learning to prioritize emails. It works with Gmail, Yahoo, Hotmail, AOL, Outlook and supports the delayed sending of letters.

Sort Emails by Size for Gmail sorts emails by attachment weight. It will help to quickly put the mailbox in order - sorting 1000 letters takes only 30 seconds.

Thrive for Email is another attempt to offer a ‚Äúsmart‚Äù email client that will help you work with emails received during email campaigns. Distributes letters by importance and categories, recalls letters that are waiting for an answer.

Beetle Email is a service that allows you to track the key metrics of mailings of various companies by specified search criteria. With it, you can learn about the conduct of email-campaigns by competitors and new trends in the market.


Photo francois schnell CC / Flickr

Email The Podcast - podcast about everything related to email. The speakers are Fabio Carneiro, leading front-end developer of MailChimp, and Elliot Ross, responsible for the design of e-mails in ActionRocket.

Call to Action - talk of marketing experts. Everything about content marketing, A / B testing, copywriting, social media marketing and more.

Email Marketing's Grave is a new themed podcast from PostUp. It will be useful to anyone who was confident that the era of email marketing is in the past.

The Everything Email Podcast - broadcast on the subject of email, issues of which are published once a month. In the first issues, the presenters discussed letter delivery, design, headlines, and omnichannel marketing.

Email Design Podcast - one of the few programs that address the design and design of letters.

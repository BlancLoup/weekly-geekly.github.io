Discovery of highly porous material

Scientists from the University of Uppsala (Sweden) discovered a new substance - one of the forms of magnesium carbonate, which has a huge porosity - 800m 2 / g. The entire volume of upsalite (the so-called resulting substance) is filled with tiny empty pores of about 6 nm 3 . This substance is easy and cheap [as compared to its counterparts?], It can be used to eliminate the effects of emissions of toxic chemicals, chemicals and oil spills.


What was before


In nature, magnesium carbonate MgCO 3 has a crystalline structure and contains a huge amount of water. Receiving a pure substance is costly.
At present, silica gel, zeolites, metal-organic materials and carbon nanotubes are used for adsorption.

Discovery history


During [what?] Experiment, Johan Gomez de la Torre and his colleagues left some of the material in the reaction chamber for the weekend. On Monday, they discovered the appearance of a solid gel, which they were unusually interested in with their properties. Obtaining highly porous material was not their goal, so you can talk about the proportion of chance in the discovery [and what was?]. To repeat the experiment, scientists took another year.

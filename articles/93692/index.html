Apple's cool patent

The US Patent Office has registered a new Apple patent called the Three-Dimensional Display System.



An 18-page manuscript describes advanced 3d technology, including multi-word (c) input by manipulation in a three-dimensional hologram.

The position of observers is monitored in real time, which means that the projected image is adjusted individually for the position of the user. The tracking system allows objects to acquire the corresponding 3d parameters and take the corresponding position in space.



The system consists of a two-dimensional projector, including analog mirrors, a scanner and a general scheme, a 3D imaging unit, a projector screen carrying the functions of an interacting surface, an interface display, a digital signal processor receiving a central processing unit.



The 3d-display displays a picture to the observer separately for the left and right eyes, the advanced camera system is synchronized with the projection system, adjusts the light flux so that it falls directly into the observer's eyes.

As I watched Formula 1 on the BBC live via the Internet

This article will be interesting to fans of Formula One from Ukraine and Belarus. Unfortunately, not a single channel in Ukraine and Belarus has purchased the right to broadcast F1, therefore, in order to watch the races live, you have to invent various clever ways.

I really enjoy watching the BBC broadcast. Compared to other channels, they start broadcasting much earlier than the race, a lot of their own interviews, analysts. Commentators: David Coulhard, Eddie Jordan, Martin Brundle. In my opinion it‚Äôs better that hardly anyone can comment on the royal races. Who once looked at the BBC will not be able to watch anything else. There are of course drawbacks ... Good English is required because no translation, naturally, no.

I knew that the BBC has a great iPlayer online broadcast service . It gives you the opportunity to view live broadcasts of all TV and rad BBC channels. Plus, one or two days after the broadcast, programs are available for viewing and downloading.





The only drawback - all this is available only from the UK. All the rest is shown the message ‚ÄúNot available in your area‚Äù.



To virtually get to this very territory had to resort to tricks. We are working on several English projects and we have servers in England. First, we wanted to set up a proxy on the server to go to the BBC through it and pretend to be British. But then, a little googling found a simpler and more elegant solution, which can be useful in other cases.

We found an article called Tunneling Firefox traffic over SSH . The article describes how you can make Socks Proxy out of it without changing anything on the server. The only requirement is SSH access to the server.

Everything is done is not easy, but very simple. To run ssh in proxy mode, you need to run the command with parameters

ssh -fND localhost:12345 login@server.net

where 12345 is the number of any unused port. You can read more about the parameters on the ssh man page, for example, here unixhelp.ed.ac.uk/CGI/man-cgi?ssh+1 .

After that, it is enough to configure the browser to work with SOCKS proxy on 127.0.0.1 and the selected port. More details of all the actions described in the article .

To do the same under Windows, you can use PuTTY. When you start the session, go to the settings Connection-> SSH-> Tunnels and add the port. Be sure to select Dynamic and click Add. Destination in this case, you can not enter. After that, connect to the server and do not close the terminal until you need a proxy.



iPlayer uses Flash. I do not know how under Linux, but under Windows Flash uses the settings of IE for Internet access. Therefore, it is necessary that IE be configured to work with a proxy. Otherwise, Flash will go directly to the Internet and iPlayer will generate an error ‚ÄúNot available in your area‚Äù. I just set everything up and started in IE.

iPlayer is also available as a desktop application written on Adobe Air. Allows you to download your favorite programs to your computer for viewing in the application or in wma format. This option is suitable for those who have a very bad connection.

PIC32 Color Maximite Microcomputer

To date, the Maximite project is my favorite in the field of handmade microcomputers. Having collected its original black and white version , I later launched RetroBSD on it , then even my Radio-86RK emulator. What is captivating in the project is completeness. Structurally, Maximite is simple, PIC32 does almost all the work. But Maximite is not just an idea in the form of a diagram. For Maximite, the board, the case and, in fact, the firmware have been developed - MMBasic, an advanced BASIC dialect, which gives access to all the peripherals, and it is impressive for Maximite. In a short interview, the author and developer of Maximite, Jeff Graham, told why he actually had to bring the project for such a finished look.

Recently, the author has released a new, color version, and this review is dedicated to her.

So, the possibilities of color Maximite (new and improved features are marked with an asterisk):
  • PS / 2 keyboard
  • VGA video signal (480x432 or 240x216), eight colors (*)
  • black and white composite video signal
  • stereo sound generation (*)
  • real time clock with independent battery (*)
  • 20 independent I / O pins
  • Connector for Arduino (*)
  • SD card (up to 32GB)
  • USB connection for file transfer or terminal
  • interfaces RS232, I2C, SPI, 1-wire
  • dual channel PWM (*)
  • ability to update the firmware via USB
  • USB power supply
  • built-in BASIC font system
  • teams working with sprites in BASIC to program games (*)

Built-in BASIC allows you to maximize the use of all these features. You can even create timer interrupt handlers in the form of subroutines.

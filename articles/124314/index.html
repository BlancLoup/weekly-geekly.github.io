Do search engines make us lazier?


Argued that the very presence of search engines slightly changed the principle of people's thinking. A researcher at Columbia University, Betsy Sparrow, believes that we now remember much less information if what we need is available on the Web. On the other hand, we quickly recall where exactly the data we are interested in can be found on the web.

On this basis, the question of what these studies mean is that we are becoming more lazy and stupid, or that the Network is becoming our external memory?

Research by Sparrow proves that the way that our memory uses to find information on the Web is almost the same by which memory gives us information about people from the past.

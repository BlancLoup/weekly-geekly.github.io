Demo day 12/22/15 HSE Business Incubator

Attended the final demo day of HSE {Inc} . 19 teams presented their projects to experts from venture funds, corporations, strategic partners, mentors and journalists. Projects operate in different industry segments: IT services, mobile applications, IoT, medical trackers, robots, and scanning devices.


The first impression from the speeches is an unexpectedly high level of team preparation, quite comparable with the projects of the IIDF or Yandex.Camp . Honestly, I expected something completely different, since the HSE business incubator does not give money, does not buy ideas, and does not take a share . It is difficult to expect anything outstanding from startups if they are not kicked in the balls, but placed in the greenhouse conditions of co-working, lecturing, provided with mentors and expertise.

About the good finish, now about the sad. On the one hand, the speakers spoke the right words - Customer development , Lean startup , MVP . However, in fact, many were afraid to talk to customers. The most courageous guaranteed profit in 2 years in 1 billion. rubles with 18 million rubles invested




Many are looking for ridiculous money: $ 10-15 thousand for developing a prototype and testing hypotheses. You guys are students! Time - heaps! You have a team of 5 people. Any sane investor or business angel will send you for 1-2 months to earn these small funds in the nearest McDonalds. And only then, after creating the prototype, 3+ months of work in the market and entering the break-even point, it makes sense to look for venture capital investments for scaling.

Perhaps this is a very early pre-planting stage and a potential business angel has come to terms with the fact that it occupies an honorable place in the FFF scheme (not family or a friend). In short, it is difficult to understand where the student's initiative ends and the real business begins. Previously, students participated in KVN, and now they are played in startups.

I give descriptions of the teams that spoke and their answers to the experts' questions. A brochure from the HSE Business Incubator is also available. All project presentations can be downloaded here .


  1. Netbell HRM
    Online retail personnel management system for medium and large business companies, which allows to increase staff efficiency and reduce the cost of accounting for mining by improving the quality of planning and automating the process of time tracking.
    What does your system do? Drawing up a sheet of work of the staff, taking into account the necessary store load.
    Are your customers shops? No, any company.
    Who and for how much sold the pilot version? Sportmaster, positive reviews.

  2. Soundfunding
    A platform for direct interaction of fans with musicians. Fans can invite an artist to any city, influence whether he comes there or not by buying tickets for an unconfirmed concert on the principle of crowdfunding. Concert promoters can reduce organization risks by easily checking demand.
    How do you solve the problem that artists plan their performances a couple of years in advance? Well-known groups have everything painted, but we work with small groups where you need to confirm the demand and payback of a concert in a particular city.

  3. InterYou + preza
    Mobile application of paid surveys. Customer companies pay for conducting research on the demand for their product, and users earn money by expressing their opinions. They can spend money on charity, partner coupons or withdraw to your e-wallet.
    How do you ensure that the target audience answers the questions? When registering, the user fills out a form about himself.

  4. Pulse + Preza
    A platform that collects on its site applications for targeted assistance to those in need through charitable foundations. It is a website and a mobile application, where philanthropists can provide targeted assistance and get information about where the money was spent and how they helped the addressee.
    How to control where the donations will go? In the personal account on the site will be statistics on payments and all events that have occurred with the object of donation.
    How do you earn? Transactions (2.6% of each payment), souvenirs, the rise of profiles.
    How do people find out about your service? Partner promotions, advertising, SMM.

  5. VendoPay + preza
    Software and hardware system for manufacturers of vending machines, allowing cashless payment of services of vending machines.
    Do people have a problem that you solve (each machine has a change and issue of change)? Owners of devices more convenient to accept non-cash.
    How much is your device? 6-10 thousand rubles.
    What technology are you using to interact? Cellular, WiFi.
    How do you ensure safety? Will do our processing partner.
    Payback period of your decision? Not answered.
    Have a prototype? Yes.

  6. TherMom + Preza

    The cost of the device? 3-5 thousand rubles, but also other models of monetization - the device is free, but data is paid for.
    Who is the audience? Young parents.
    Your competitors? Normal thermometer, but we have data in dynamics.
    Do you have children? 2 nephews.

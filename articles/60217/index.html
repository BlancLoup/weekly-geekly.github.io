The first million is always the most difficult



We are pleased to announce that in less than a year after the end of the closed beta test, Evernote already has more than one million registered users! This is a lot. This, for example, the population of the whole of Chelyabinsk or Rostov-on-Don, and even entire countries such as Qatar, Cyprus or Montenegro. Just think: if all Evernote users join the line, it will be a queue of a million people!

We developed Evernote for those who appreciate what they have in their heads, for people who need to remember important things at work or at school. And we are really glad that there are a lot of people like you to provide us with a fast-growing business.

It took New York 250 years to reach a million inhabitants. The first million subscribers appeared at MTS in seven years. Twitter got a million followers in about a year and a half. We managed to do it faster.

And we came to this the hard way.

The classic scheme for creating a fast-growing Internet service includes the following steps: (1) make it available only through a browser, (2) provide it with social functions, (3) find young people who like to try something new and (4) not take care of the profits. We did everything differently: we developed downloadable applications and did not focus (so far) on social functions, our users are mainly university graduates and professionals, and our business model provides for the project‚Äôs profitability not in the very distant future.

So if you are one of thirty people working at Evernote, then thank you for your hard work over the past year! You made a service that is used by a million people! Is not that great?!

If you are one of the million users of Evernote, we can only sincerely thank you for discovering our service and deciding that it will be useful to you. In the near future, we are planning a lot of interesting things that will make your friends jealous of your "external brain."

If you are one of six billion six hundred and thirty three million people on this planet, join us, we have a place for you too !

I would like to say a special thank you to the Russian-speaking users of Evernote, who with such enthusiasm and enthusiasm perceived our first experience in creating a localized version of the service.

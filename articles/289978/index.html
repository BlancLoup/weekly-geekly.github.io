How we started creating conferences

Hello! ‚ÄúWe‚Äù is just me - I create conferences for .Net developers. I started by saying that last year I gathered 200 people in total in Moscow, Peter, and Yekaterinburg. When the meeting was created in Moscow, I was faced with a whole heap of difficulties. It is necessary not only to attract people from another city, but also to the site of another city. At the same time, due to the spontaneity of the decision and the lack of a clear understanding, whether or not a legal entity would take off or not (even now). Ohhh ... However, in order ... Starting with the preparations for the meeting.

Before you start


Firstly, it is necessary to collect as much information as possible which will be unique and which will not find anything on the Internet. Payment methods


Secondly, you need to think in advance how you will collect payment from the audience. For myself, I used this scheme: almost all services for collecting payments for events take a percentage (natural desire) for services. It is a pity, of course, but I understand them: labor is not easy. The second minus (and the main one) - they require that you be at least an IE, and better - a legal entity. Since the preparation time was short (2 weeks), the design of something fell off. But I was extremely lucky and I ran into a service that offered ticket booking for free. This service is timepad.ru. The tar fly service was added right after the start of payment collection. The problem was that initially they did not write anything about the fact that they only work with legal entities. by individuals. And they wrote about it after coming from the person of the first registration to the event. Those. in fact, they are blackmailing: with innuendo they are enticed to do all the preparatory work and start, but during the launch report that no one can give you money due to the fact that you are not a legal person. What makes you fly to the nearest office and pay money for expedited clearance. But I took advantage of the fact that they have in the system, when registration is in progress, a letter arrives: ‚Äúa person wants to pay for attending the seminar, but cannot because you did not sign the contract‚Äù (consider that you did not become a legal entity) and below is the email of that person. Immediately deciding to raise my payment system, and remembering that I have a bank card, a Yandex-Money account and a Qiwi wallet, I began sending emails to the email address given to me about payment information. Payment finally went. And, accordingly, the budget for the payment of premises and the road to Moscow and back began to emerge. The only drawback of this approach is that you must not forget to cancel the booking time in two days (otherwise, after two days, the system will start spamming all the tickets are due to expire. And this is annoying) and when registering, the timepad will notify you that you cannot pay via timepad. This is not clear, because if they send this letter, then in fact, they substitute the organizer of the event. Like, we allowed you to conduct business on payment independently, but at the same time we will tell everyone that you are not a very good person. However, experience has shown that people treat this with understanding, and this time I decided to go the same way. Next time I will draw up a legal entity (IP) and enter into a contract with the same service, or I will do everything on my own capacities.

Location


Fourth - the venue. This was one of the most painful places in the organization. Initially it seemed to me (and all those who came were surprised that I gathered more), that I would gather about 20 people. But no more than 30. Based on this, the audience of the company in which I work and whose branch was in Moscow was chosen. However, when the number of people exceeded 30, and there were still quite a few days to pay, I realized that I was greatly mistaken. The task was to find a place with a wide range of good quality audiences and various room options. Another problem - how many people at a time - comfortable? For myself, I stopped at the figure ‚Äúup to 100‚Äù, based on the size and cost of the audience. If it is higher, it becomes again not clear: will so many people come or not? If you do not come, then you can go to the minus.

And then I went to the trick, the meaning of which is to kill two birds with one stone: you can break them all up for two days. Friday and Saturday. On the one hand, this is a risk. The risk is connected with the fact that Saturday is a day when everyone wants to rest, and not load the head with an assembler and hacking with threads. If you take Saturday, you must again book the audience. How many people? Unclear. However, as I said, this approach had two indisputable advantages: if someone wants to attend a seminar, but cannot on Friday, he will come on Saturday. The second plus is that the size of the audience on the first day becomes noticeably smaller. So, the level of comfort of listeners grows.

In order to notify everyone, I wrote a letter with the coordinates of the new venue of the seminar with a request to answer the letter with confirmation, as well as the desired date - Saturday or Friday. People reacted very responsibly to the letter and to the last day before the seminar and began to independently divide into groups.

By this time, it became necessary to enter into contracts with the landlord. After 12 hours from sending a letter, 20% of the participants responded and a third of them were on Saturday. Making a simple assumption that it will continue this way, an audience has been ordered that is half the size of a Friday. By the end of the survey, both audiences were filled almost to the end. Only a couple of people had to refuse registration.

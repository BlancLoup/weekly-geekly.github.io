Why is it important to know the condition of the air in the rooms


Carbon dioxide content


Increased concentration of carbon dioxide in the room can be dangerous to human health. External this is manifested by the appearance of a number of subjective symptoms:


  • headache
  • feeling of weakness
  • dizziness
  • irritability
  • sleep disturbance

The limit of CO2 concentration is individual for each person, depending on gender, age and state of health, but such limits of comfort are accepted:


  • 400‚Äì800 ppm - comfortable level
  • 800‚Äì1200 ppm - manifests drowsiness, fatigue and decreased attention
  • from 1200 ppm - may show a loss of performance

Externally, people have hypercapnia characterized by the appearance of a number of subjective symptoms, namely, headache, dizziness, feelings of weakness, irritability, and sleep disorders. Decreased performance accurately correlates with an increase in the percentage of carbon dioxide in ambient air. When this indicator approaches 1%, the motor response time increases, the tracking response accuracy decreases; at 1.5-2%, the mental activity of a person begins to change qualitatively, the functions of differentiation, perception, operative memory and distribution of attention are disturbed. With prolonged work in an atmosphere containing 3% carbon dioxide, significant disorders of thinking, memory, fine motor coordination begin, the number of clerks and activity errors increases sharply, and hearing and vision disorders begin.

Relative humidity


Relative indoor humidity is also important. First of all, mucous membranes that come in contact with open air are affected, they are covered with microcracks and dry up, opening a direct path into the body for harmful bacteria and viruses. With a lack of moisture, the following symptoms can occur:


  • feeling of dryness in the nasopharynx
  • nasal bleeding (in people prone to this)
  • general deterioration of health in patients with bronchial asthma
  • "Rez" in the eyes
  • frequent colds and illnesses due to dry mucous membranes

With increased humidity, hypertensive patients, atherosclerosis, people with various cardiovascular diseases especially react strongly.

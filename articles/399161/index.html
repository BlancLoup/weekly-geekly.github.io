HyperX Cloud Stinger headset: comfort and great connectivity for reasonable money

Hi GT! You are already familiar with HyperX headsets. The blog Kingston already had materials about all the lines: about the tournament Drone, and about the intermediate Cloud (represented by CloudX), and about the flagship Revolver. It is time to get acquainted with the new model, which entered the market just last month.



Designed from scratch, sharpened to use with computers and laptops, licked to the smallest detail and offers uncompromising quality for their money. Meet the HyperX Stinger .

Positioning


In the HyperX lineup, the Stinger model will take the place of an entry-level gaming headset. It is bigger, more comfortable and sounds better than the Drone, which focuses primarily on eSports disciplines: those who have gotten a mouthwash over the counter, the home of the shkoloty - DotA and Korean chess - StarCraft.

At the same time, the Stinger is more convenient and more comfortable to use as a purely computer headset than the universal Cloud line: it can also be used with tablet phones, will not die from traveling in a backpack or neck, but the design and some chips are designed, nevertheless, to stationary use.

Created to be comfortable


The headset looks neat and modest: no flashy lights, bright accents or chrome inserts. It seems that the era of "game design" is successfully coming to an end. Not all manufacturers have caught this trend, but here everything is as I would like to see: modest and tidy. Matte black plastic that does not collect prints, metal inserts in the headband and moving parts, integrated into the left cup the microphone rod from rubberized material, and likewise installed volume control in the right.



In terms of overall design and sharpening for use in the gaming context, the headset has taken successful decisions from the Drone tournament model: both the regulator built into the speaker cup, rather than dangling on the wire, and the microphone auto-off when changing its position, and comfort during prolonged wear. At the same time, the materials and the attitude to the user are different here, after all, the product is not designed for e-sports, but for a comfortable immersion into the game world ...

Take the same headband: the arc is made of highly flexible plastic. It collapses into a spiral and returns to its original position without problems, day under load in a bent state - the headset as new, not even the slightest trace of residual deformation, cracks or visible changes.



A cunning system is used for sliding elements: the half-arms themselves are reinforced with metal plates, the tips of which are included in massive plastic blocks. The frame of the speaker cup is attached to these blocks. The most often broken element, you say. Yes, that is right. Therefore, the element is thoroughly reinforced, and the speakers themselves are attached to it through a swivel hinge and a folding frame.



With this design, the headband is a natural fit for any head shape. It stretches perfectly in width, bends in any direction, the springing effect of metal inserts is adjusted so that the headphones do not tend to squeeze your eyes out of the skull, and at the same time firmly held on it. The cups have two degrees of freedom and a large margin of adjustment for departure. The headset is very light, only 275 grams, and this mass is distributed evenly between the headband and ear cups. User comfort was taken care of at the highest level.

In Stinger, you can sit all day without problems, and the swivel mechanism allows you to wear a headset around your neck without discomfort due to large volumes of cups - they just turn 90 degrees and lie on your chest. After a couple of minutes, you will simply forget that something is hanging around your neck, then you will be looking for another half day where the headset is.



According to the materials, nothing new has been invented since the first Cloud: the insides of the ear cushions and headbands are filled with memory foam, which takes the shape of your head after a few minutes of wearing, and this clever filling is hidden behind high-quality leatherette that breathes and passes moisture. Ears do not disappear, the temperature inside the headphones does not rise much.

True, in one place HyperX didn‚Äôt move away from the usual materials: the cable lost a fabric braid and got a polymer jacket. It does not twist, a little stretches, pleasant tactile and easy to clean, unlike textiles.


TL; DR, ergonomics: The headset is created from scratch, absorbed the best practices HyperX. The headband is very flexible, the design is robust, the speaker cups have two degrees of freedom, they did not save on materials.

Comfortable communication as one of the priorities


The microphone (at least, its hardware stuffing) in Stinger migrated directly from the flagship model Revolver. The same supersensitive capacitor element with a narrow radiation pattern and passive noise cancellation is used. Soft lining, like the older brother - no, but the competent setting and design of the "head" of the microphone almost negated the need for this accessory.



Voice recording is amazing. You can speak quietly, do not strain, and you will be well heard, and the background noise is reliably cut off by the design of the sensitive element and its body.



The bar of the microphone is non-removable, flexible, but the position does not remember very readily: the letter "sic" does not bend it. However, its factory form and position almost perfectly fits, and a small dog towards the mouth holds it perfectly. In the absence of the need for voice communication, the microphone can simply be raised vertically upwards. A characteristic click that you feel with both ears and hands will notify you that the hardware switch has been activated and the microphone is inactive.



In the right speaker cup resistive volume control is located. It is made better than in the younger model, it is much easier to use it by touch, and the adjustment itself is clearer. The slider has subtle "divisions", however, they are conditional - no one bothers to move it literally a drop and get the desired result. The main problem of such a thing is to get used to the fact that it is at hand and not to reach out for an uncomfortable hotkey or in software settings.



Of course, the resistive circuit will wear out over time, but rather, you just want to change the headphones for something new or a higher class than the linear mechanism in Stinger will become unusable. And in the extreme case, a couple of drops of oil for sewing machines will return the switch to life for another year or two.


TL; DR, communication: The microphone was taken from the flagship model, it is turning, the mechanism is combined with a switch. Sensor element capacitor, narrowly targeted, with passive noise cancellation. In the right cup speaker set volume control.

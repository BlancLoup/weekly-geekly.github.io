Roles in the mobile industry


To make it easier to understand the place in the mobile market of each of the companies, I propose to begin with to paint abstract "roles" - a certain set of characteristics and actions that reflect each specific moment in the life of the MU. Each particular firm can play several roles in the market, and maybe only one, but understanding of each of these roles is necessary if we want to understand the whole chain of ‚Äúwho owes what to whom‚Äù. In the same way as any light can be decomposed into a spectrum, we will try to expand the activities of companies operating in the MU market into key components.
  • An OS vendor is a company that develops the operating system itself, documentation for it, its maintenance, and so on.
  • The developer of the MU - those who develop the entire technical design of the MU. There are quite a lot of companies in the world that can develop MUs to your order, or already have a portfolio of pre-designed blanks on which, if the customer wishes, you can pull on a particular OS. You will be surprised, but a significant number of devices sold now under eminent brands, developed by small and little-known companies like this: bSquare .
  • Manufacturer MU - the so-called. "factory". It is important to distinguish between two main types of manufacturers:
    • OEM-manufacturer MU is the factory that makes its own devices based on the mobile OS.
    • ODM-manufacturer MU - the factory that simply produces MU, developed by someone. Everyone knows very well that the Apple and the iPad are made by Foxconn, a Chinese factory, on Apple‚Äôs order. This is the ‚Äúclassic ODM‚Äù, when the manufacturer has no rights, does not conduct any other activity itself, except for the round-the-clock, round-the-month and year-round production of the MU for the customer.
  • A Trademark Holder is one who owns the right to a name and on whose behalf the sale of the MU is made. Many probably still remember the device brand "i-mate" or "Qtek" - this is just it.
  • Trading network - it is clear ... those who are directly involved in the MU trade. We have a lot of such and they play a very prominent role in the MU market: Euroset, Svyaznoy, Alt-Telecom, etc. In Europe, for example, there are practically no such retailers that operate relatively independently; mobile operators are replacing them all.
  • Post-sale support - who provides after-sales support of MU (warranty and post-warranty repair). In most cases, such support is carried out by firms that received authorization from the trademark holder: ‚ÄúPupkin & Co., CJSC, authorized by Apple Inc.
  • The holder of an additional software store - recently the market has very clearly realized the importance of this role, the amount of money that lies in this piece of the market, and everyone now wants to eat this piece of cake.
  • The service provider is the one who provides this or that service to the user of the MU. In particular, the mobile operator provides mobile communication and mobile Internet services.
For the current conversation, this transfer of roles is quite sufficient, although in fact there are much more roles in the MU market (‚ÄúChipset Manufacturer‚Äù is, I think, understandable without explanation; ‚ÄúVendor BSP‚Äù is the one who develops drivers for the current chipset; ‚ÄúIndependent developer Software, etc., etc.)

How is the release of updates for MU


Although we already mentioned a little bit earlier about the organization of the MU production process, I want to remind one common truth: ‚ÄúTHERE IS NOTHING FREE FOR THIS LIGHT!‚Äù Why am I doing this? What do you need the manufacturer MU?

The manufacturer of the mobile device you purchased is responsible to you within:
  1. Supplied with warranty device
  2. The laws of the country in which the sale transaction was made.
Everything. No matter how much you want to do otherwise, only these two points describe what the manufacturer MUST BE. READ WARRANTY OBLIGATIONS AND TERMS OF USE of the MU purchased by you, so you do not write angry letters to the local representative offices of the manufacturer and do not moan on the forums.

It is also very important to clearly understand who exactly undertakes the obligations of post-sales support of the device you purchased? If you are buying a device that looks like an iPhone from the Kurskiy station, you expect to be waited with open arms at the Apple Service Center, then, as they say, I have bad news for you ... So what should the manufacturer MU ow you? If we speak briefly and skip all the conclusions, then, if not stated otherwise, the manufacturer bears only one responsibility before you: so that the functionality stated at the sale works accordingly. Moreover, very often the seller‚Äôs obligations are limited to the region of sale, as a result, a device purchased in Dubai DutyFree will not be serviced free of charge in the Russian SC of the same company.

Why am I telling these common truths? What does it mean for a manufacturer to ‚Äúrelease an update‚Äù?

To prepare an update for release, the manufacturer needs to reassemble the entire team of engineers who prepared the device for release, and re-go all the way to prepare the software part of the MU from the OS build from the original modules and thorough testing to the release of the finished ROM version. At the same time, the amount of work is comparable to the amount of work in preparation for the initial release (well, maybe a little bit less). Yes, when drawing up a financial plan for the entire life of the device, an honest and responsible manufacturer of MU plans to release updates in advance, but this is always an update within the current version. About the cost of licenses

Another costly item is the cost of updating the licenses for the mobile OS. Why are manufacturers still releasing updates?

Based on the above, it becomes clear that, in general, it is not profitable for a manufacturer to release updates in general. By releasing them, the manufacturer tries to anticipate possible claims from end users. But why release updates to newer OS versions? By and large, there are two main reasons for this:

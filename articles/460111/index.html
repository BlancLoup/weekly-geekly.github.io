Upgraded version of SAP Business One 9.3: what has changed

Hi, Habr!

We continue a series of publications about SAP Business One - ERP for small and medium businesses. This time we will consider a new version of the product.

According to the roadmap , along with quarterly updates of the current version, every 1.5 years, SAP releases a new version of SAP Business One.

Before launching SAP Business One 9.3, SAP Business One partners launched pilot projects for a limited client base. As part of the pilots, customers had the opportunity to use the new functionality and share feedback on the quality of the product. At that time, more than 240 clients around the world managed to take part in this program, 108 of them were put into productive operation. One of the first customers in the world was a company from Russia - Telecom-Exchange. A video with the comments of the Managing Director can be viewed here .

In March 2018, the first public release was released . Today, any of the existing and potential customers of SAP Business One can migrate and use this version.



Let's see what has changed. The new version of the product focuses on ease of use and the addition of existing functionality. Due to the expansion of the kernel, new business processes have been implemented: simple production routing and management of returns of materials. Noticeable changes have been made to the project management functional, customer relationship management (CRM) processes have been simplified and streamlined, new features have been added to regulate access privileges, etc.

Using the capabilities of the SAP HANA platform and semantic layers, new templates for the control panel (user desktop) have been implemented. Using the new analytic portal, end users can plan and use reports without logging into SAP Business One using real-time ERP data.

It's not a secret that add-ons play a key role for customers. Therefore, the development of add-ons for partners has been simplified in the new version and support for XML tables for user tables and Visual Studio 2017 has been added. For extreme applications (Extreme Applications or SAP HANA XS applications) uses the SSO login algorithm, which eliminates the need to re-authenticate the user.

To simplify the deployment and management of the landscape, SAP Business One has implemented a centralized approach using a single console for local and cloud options.

Personal Data Protection Management (GDPR)


In order to comply with the General Data Protection Regulations (GDPR), SAP Business One 9.3 implements the personal data protection management functionality.

The data protection tools are located in the new menu along the path ‚ÄúAdministration‚Äù - ‚ÄúUtilities‚Äù. Access to personal data is governed by user permissions, and changes in personal data are recorded in the change log.



To define and classify personal data, the ‚ÄúPersonal Data Management‚Äù window is used.



The new "Personal Data Management Assistant" allows you to define users, employees, business partners and contact persons as individuals. With the help of this assistant, you can create a report on personal data that companies must provide at the request of an individual. The assistant is also used for the purpose of blocking, cleaning (at the request of an individual or upon the expiration of the data retention), unlocking of personal data.



Customer Relationship Management (CRM)


CRM menu


A CRM item has been added to the main menu to quickly switch to all business objects related to customer relationship management: a directory of business partners, campaign generation assistant, Sales Opportunity documents, CRM Reports, etc.



When working with a centralized CRM module, the usability of the program is increased and the productivity of employees is increased.

Assignment of activities


Now the activity can be assigned to multiple users or employees by adding a list of recipients. On the activity overview screen, recipients will be indicated (one or more).

Sales and Procurement Management


Contract


Contract management has been expanded. Now you can define a fixed exchange rate in the contract if the business partner uses foreign currency. This fixed exchange rate overrides the exchange rate used in the document on a specific date.



Now it is possible to update the contract parameters (amounts, quantities, prices) after assigning the contract in a marketing document. Added the ability to update the initial date of the contract in the absence of attached documents.

To enhance contract control, you can now track all deviations from the planned amount and the planned amount specified in the contract in the document settings. Additionally, you can configure blocking of the operation or warning when the quantity / amount exceeds the planned value of these parameters in the documents. Also for contracts it became available to set up the approval procedure.



Application for refund


SAP Business One 9.3 now supports the ability to record return intentions.
Before the actual return of the material, the user can agree on the conditions of return (quantity, price, reason).



For this purpose, two new options have been added: ‚ÄúRequest for refund‚Äù to the ‚ÄúSale‚Äù business process and ‚ÄúRequest for refund‚Äù in the ‚ÄúPurchase‚Äù business process. The ‚Äúrequest for refund‚Äù is also available in the ‚ÄúService‚Äù business process.

  5. Management of serial numbers and batch numbers in documents
  6. Additional reporting and analytics features

New documents provide more flexible return control and additional control.

Manage the build process


Assembly routing


A new routing feature simplifies the processing of a production order using a definable sequence of assembly steps. It also improves the usability of managing items requiring assembly and resource components. The ability to define several stages of assembly has been added to the "Specification" and "Production Order" documents. To determine the date of demand for components and resources, the function of managing the start and end dates of the stage is implemented.



Using the ‚ÄúCalculate Routing Date‚Äù field, you can control the start and end dates of the stage by setting the values ‚Äã‚Äãto ‚ÄúStart date‚Äù, ‚ÄúEnd date‚Äù, ‚ÄúFrom start date ahead‚Äù or ‚ÄúFrom end date back‚Äù. A new column ‚ÄúStatus‚Äù has been added to the ‚ÄúProduction Order‚Äù, which allows you to set the values ‚Äã‚Äãfor the route stage, position or resource to ‚ÄúScheduled‚Äù, ‚ÄúIn Process‚Äù or ‚ÄúComplete‚Äù. The ‚ÄúStatus‚Äù column is available for editing in all lines of the production order. Business Intelligence (version for SAP HANA)


Control panel template


The control panel or the user's desktop allows you to organize access to the necessary information by placing links to documents, dashboards, key performance indicators (KPIs) on the SAP Business One main screen.

In version 9.3, new pre-configured templates for finance, sales, procurement and inventory are available in the control panel. With the appropriate authority, users can create their own control panel templates and publish them for use, including by other company employees. For example, you can create user desktops on topics: finance, procurement, sales, warehouse, and others. Control panel templates can be assigned to a user group.

You can switch between templates in the interface using the ‚ÄúTemplate selection‚Äù button.



Analytical portal


With the release of the new version, the ability to publish and share MS Excel and Crystal Reports reports using an analytical portal has become available. Reports can be downloaded from the portal in different formats or customized to generate and send reports on a schedule by e-mail. PDF, Excel or HTML options are available for MS Excel, and PDF reports for Crystal Reports.



The portal does not need to install the SAP Business One client or MS Excel on the user's computer; reports can be opened on various devices, for example, in the web client or on a mobile device. The analytical portal is available for both local and cloud installations of SAP Business One version 9.3 for SAP HANA.

Workflow


The workflow service in SAP Business One allows you to automate the process of moving documents and assigning responsible users and tasks.

In addition to improving the stability and capabilities of the workflow service, support for the 64-bit DI API is implemented, and the configuration of the workflow service and the management of workflow templates are now implemented in the ‚ÄúAdministration‚Äù web console.

You can now install the SAP Business One components through the SLD Control Center on remote computers.

A new ‚ÄúLogical Machines‚Äù tab has been added to register remote computers and install SAP Business One components. SLD automatically installs the components of the SLD agent to remote computers. Integrated incident reporting


This new feature allows you to instantly register a problem in the SAP Business One client, document all steps using screenshots with a description, system information, and report a problem that has occurred (a single ZIP file) to a colleague or partner.



SAP Business One Service app mobile app (version for SAP HANA)


SAP continues to develop information access capabilities for mobile workers. In June 2018, a new mobile app on iOS was released. SAP Business One. Service app simplifies the processing of service requests for service department employees working out of the office.



The functionality of the mobile application includes access to customer information, a barcode scanner, voice-to-text translation and allows you to process the assigned service requests. From the application, the service employee can place a purchase order, close the service request with the customer's signature, and print a confirmation document on a Bluetooth printer.



The application allows you to work with data both in real time and in the absence of a connection to the company's server.

Ingeniously simple decision of the creators was the selection of four key elements necessary to create artificial communities such as "state". Market (resources, money, enterprises, labor, goods, licenses, import, export, taxes, foreign exchange market), war (attack, battle, occupation, surrender of territories, uprisings, armament, mutual pacts and peace treaties), hierarchy (elections , president, parliament, party) and communication / socialization (citizenship, press, social network). The main feature and indisputable advantage of this conditional society is that everything happens very quickly in it. As you probably guessed, eWorld is a map of the real world with the presence of all key states except the central / southern part of the African continent, a number of countries in Asia and Oceania, not counting Albania and a number of European micro-states. All virtual states are divided into regions, each of which, by sea or land, borders with neighboring countries and has at its disposal one (of about a dozen types of two main groups) resource, which gives a certain bonus (to the citizens of a country having a region) in mining / producing some then produce. Production volumes everywhere with demand, taxation and the level of wages form the price in the domestic market and competitiveness in the external. Goods can be sold at retail for local currency in this market (rubles, US dollars, pounds, etc.) or be sold wholesale to a person or organization to bypass taxation for the same currency or gold (which in turn is regarded as the world currency).

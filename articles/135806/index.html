Time lamp

We all walk, think about something ...
Result - various ideas come to mind. Or maybe the other way around?

As a child, I watched a movie on TV (there was no internet yet). I do not remember the names - about the border guards. At the frontier post there was a suspicion that the lamp that shines above the house on the ‚Äúother side‚Äù conveys information. Checked - no radiation detected.
Well, they did not find it ok, but the phrase stuck in my head for a long time.

Last New Year wanted to do something original for the holiday.
Controllers like I learned a long time ago, I learned to flash the LED.
A thought occurred to me, is it possible to make it so that using a lamp to transmit information? Apparently a phrase came up from the movie.

- And what will be interesting to people?
- A lot of things, but the simplest is the time.

And then spun ... Options, methods, schemes.
I decided to show the time with flashes of light.
Something like ‚Äúthree short whistles‚Äù.

Since the construction will be on a high pipe, the watch chips are eliminated - whatever one may say, but they will still run away or even worse - they will be left behind.
Correction by radio or IR - for a long time to do.
Stopped on the GPS receiver. Slightly more expensive, of course, but a huge pile of problems disappears.

The display principle is very simple: at first the number of flashes shows the hours, then, after a short pause - minutes.
To distinguish hours from minutes, flashes had a different duty cycle ‚Äî the watches had a visually longer flash time.

From the appearance of the idea to the first implementation, not even a day passed.
Thanks to the breadboard, left over from one of the previous projects.
I installed a powerful LED on the output of the device, sent a stream of information to the output from the enterprise, in order to view the operation of the device from the side.
Time was read very easily.

The next morning, the first critical reviews went ...
It turned out that outbreaks greatly interfere with the protection of their duties.
But in general, the idea has earned!

To improve the perception of time, I decided to make the color separation of hours and minutes.
At the same time, the pulse time made the same for hours and minutes.
Applied RGB-LED strip with IP65.
Since the tape was not enough for the entire perimeter of the viewing platform of the pipe, we had to abandon the stretching along the length and make the radiator in the form of a lamp, winding the tape over the cylinder.
To protect against curious birds and even more protection over IP, the emitter was placed in a transparent flask. As what the filter for water purification was used.

Initially, the entire structure placed in the flask. Upstairs there is a GPS receiver, in the center of the cylinder is a controller board, on the cylinder there is a ribbon with LEDs.
For this, we had to open the GPS receiver.
The autopsy surprised me ... The board was not protected with varnish; an RS-232 chip was installed with a temperature range from 0 to 70 degrees.
I had to cover the board with glue from the thermogun.

The operation of the device in the flask did not like ... Though the LEDs are highly efficient, but the efficiency is far from 100% and the whole economy warms up very well, which significantly reduces the life of the radiator.

It was decided to make the case ventilated. For this purpose, I designed a small process on top, covered with a cap from the condenser body. Natural semi-inch filter openings remained for circulation.
To protect against solar radiation, the LED strip was covered with shiny aluminum foil and pierced the holes for the LEDs (many of them turned out to be there ...).

The GPS receiver and power supply brought out in an external box made of cast aluminum.
In order for the receiver to work, I sawed the hole from above and closed it with a lid from the tank for microwave ovens.
To protect against solar radiation - the unit was plastered with the same shiny foil (there was no time to paint, the New Year was in the nose).

Mounted the unit and the emitter on the corner and forward, that is, upwards - for installation!

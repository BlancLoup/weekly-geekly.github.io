Classic bookmarks in on-line

(Do not judge strictly: the first post. Tell me how to make them better.)
No need to explain the convenience of synchronizing bookmarks - it is obvious. I used to use Google Sync, but since FF3 they stopped supporting it.
In search of a convenient replacement, I tried all the most popular bookmarking services. They provide a lot of new features (tags, ratings, etc.), but for me the main purpose - convenient work and cataloging with your favorites - does not solve. For example, it does not seem convenient to me that to open some bookmarks, I first need to load the service page. It is also not convenient that they all follow a common list, without a hierarchy. Bookmarks from the Google panel are also not convenient, as they are listed.
In short, I wanted the usual browser bookmarks, but the same at home and at work.
I came to the decision to use Yandex bookmarks.
In Yandex the most convenient bookmarks in my opinion:
1. Arranged in folders, as usual.
2. Sorted alphabetically automatically, conveniently.
3. When connecting, ‚ÄúYandex. Bar‚Äù is accessible without the need to load a page, quickly.
4. If YandexBar infuriates, you can turn it off in the View menu, and the bookmark button (and a couple more useful features of Yandex.Bar, such as traffic jams, weather), pull out, for example, Google Panels on the panel , beautiful.

As a result, in my Fox there is a panel from Google, but which adds weather and traffic jams from Yandex and its bookmarks, which now have the same, neat, familiar, convenient bookmarks on all machines.

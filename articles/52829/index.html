Russian Opera Mini spurt - 157% in one year



Opera's mobile report presents 35 leading countries and data on mobile Internet in the Middle East

Oslo, Norway - February 25, 2009

Only facts

- Russia continues to maintain the leadership position identified in our first mobile report. Over the past year, the number of Opera Mini users in Russia has grown by 157%. In January 2009, each user looked at an average of 416 pages. The full report on the status of the mobile Internet is available at www.opera.com/smw (in English).

- The total number of Opera Mini users in the world in January 2009 increased by 12.1% and for the first time exceeded 20 million unique users. The number of monthly viewed pages increased even more significantly - by 18% compared with the previous month and amounted to 7.6 billion web pages. Since December 2008, Opera Mini users have generated over 122 terabytes of data for mobile operators around the world.

- On average, Opera Mini browser compresses data up to 90%. In the case of transferring this data in uncompressed form, the total traffic would amount to 1.2 billion MB. For the first time in the history of this browser, Opera Mini servers processed more than 1 Petabyte of data for Opera Mini users in a month.

- The most explosive growth in the number of Opera Mini users in the period from January 2008 to January 2009 was recorded in Armenia, where this figure was more than 2800%. Nigeria is in second place - 1854%. Among the most notable results are Egypt (1391%), the Philippines (570%) and Kazakhstan (419%).

- Armenia also leads in the number of pages viewed by Opera Mini users during the month. In January 2009, this indicator was 669 pages on average for each user from Armenia.

- The top ten leaders in the number of Opera Mini users in the Middle East are as follows: Iran, Israel, Saudi Arabia, Syria, Jordan, the United Arab Emirates, Oman, Kuwait, Lebanon and Yemen.

The monthly report looks at 35 countries leading in the number of Opera Mini users, so the top ten countries may include countries with the most varied levels of user growth per year. For example, Indonesia showed an annual increase (GP) of 312%, but in terms of the total number of users, it ranks only second.

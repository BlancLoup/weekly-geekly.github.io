Steve Jobs himself said at the presentation that the human eye does not distinguish more than 300 ppi per inch, and the display of the iPhone 4 - displays as much as 356 ppi - why so much, maybe for aliens !?

But the Google Nexus One display - just gives out a little less than 300 ppi (more precisely 256 dpi - only 44 dots are not enough to reach an ideal 300 ppi) - i.e. just as much as you can actually see :)

If we compare the contrast of displays, then it is obvious that technically (in numbers) loses Apple iPhone 4 with IPS-LCD (Retina Display) = 800: 1 vs. 100,000: 1 = AMOLED (Active-Matrix OLED ) of Google Nexus One.
‚ÄúOf course, many may begin to argue about these figures, based on the difference in measurement techniques, because it really seems unrealistic that the IPS-LCD contrast is 125 times less than the contrast of the AMOLED display, but still these figures are shown on official websites - where not taken to deceive.
Today, AMOLED-technology has some drawbacks, but there are obvious significant advantages - see the article: "AMOLED- and TFT-displays - a comparative review . "

Why Apple has not used the AMOLED display in the new iPhone 4, presumably there are several reasons:
1. Korean Samsung, and Taiwanese companies TPO Display and Optronics are currently the only manufacturers of AMOLED displays, and they cannot handle existing orders even for less popular models from other manufacturers, and Apple is not interested in creating an excessive shortage of devices.
2. The cost of AMOLED display is almost two times higher than that of classic SMD (LED displays), which are used in iPhone 3Gs.
3. In addition - studies have shown that the classic type of SMD display is more suitable for reading. And this is also an important position for Apple, since it is actively promoting the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãmobile reading.

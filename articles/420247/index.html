What is the Far East


All IT life in this region takes place in two centers - Khabarovsk and Vladivostok. The rest of the cities have only small glimpses of IT, where the main number of IT firms is 1C Franchisee.
Taking into account the choice of Vladivostok, as the capital of the entire Far East (means the inflow of budget funds), as well as the uniqueness of the natural conditions - I will focus on it and its surroundings.

I must say that Khabarovsk is a good city, but it is normal. It can be compared, for example, with Nizhny Novgorod. Those. a resident of central Russia, there will not find anything new, and even far from the sea.

Climate and nature


Dreamed of living in the region, where by car you can get to the sea, mountains and lakes for a couple of hours - Vladivostok is your choice. Great beaches with good water and a sandy beach. Abundant number of mountain peaks of medium height (800-1400 meters), taiga and virgin nature.

Of the minuses is the lack of infrastructure. Those. you can get there, and even you can find a decent catering, but that‚Äôs where all the comforts end. The resort business is poorly developed, a tent on the beach or a wooden hut with outdoor facilities is still the pinnacle of what is. Of course, there are a couple of good places, but the price tag is too high there. The beach season is three months - July-September.

As for climate, the situation is as follows.

  • Approximately 1 month of the year (from mid-July to mid-August) is hellish stuffiness and heat. You go out into the street, and you get the feeling that you went into the bath. The end of August is the rainy and typhoon season.
  • April - November - very good, stable temperature, when you feel very good.
  • December-March is such a light winter. Sometimes a typhoon comes along and brings a lot of snow, but most often it keeps -10 / -12 outside without snow.

From what catches your eye - these are trees. If in Moscow these are 10-15 meter tall trees, then right there everything is crooked 5 meter, but fluffy.

There are tigers, but their habitat is a territory where, apart from small villages, there is nothing. Therefore, if you do not get there yourself, then nothing will happen to you.

Conclusion: The weather is not resort, but at times warmer than in Moscow. Sun, sea, mountains, forests and taiga.

Food


On average, the price of food is 10-20% higher than in the European part of Russia - the choice is also smaller. Its production is relatively small; it has become several times more expensive to deliver from Southeast Asia since 2014. For example, to buy good apples, you need to buy New Zealand apples for 160 rubles per kg. There are Chinese by 105-110, but their quality is significantly worse.

As for fish and seafood, the situation is very sad. For an adequate price (lower than in Moscow), you can buy ordinary fish - flounder, pollock, pink salmon. Everything else has either a price, as in Moscow, or even higher. The reason lies in the fact that after 2014 it has become very profitable for producers to export everything to Japan, Korea, China, and nothing remains on the local market.

Of course, there is always the opportunity to buy seafood from poachers - the price there will be relatively adequate. But, first, the quality of products is not always adequate - improper storage. Secondly, I don‚Äôt really want to encourage such guys.
On the positive side, this is a good Ussuri distillery. Good settings on herbs, good rum - and all at the right price.

Conclusion: the conclusion is that the price tag for products is higher than in Moscow, there is less choice, seafood is scarce and expensive.

Work in IT


Salaries are much lower here than in Moscow and St. Petersburg. Conditionally, you can give these gradations:

  • 30-45 Junior
  • 45-60 Middle
  • 60-80 senior

From time to time they come across even higher, but this is very rare (most often I see for mobile developers).

The number of vacancies, taking into account 1C-programmers, web designers, administrators, technical support, etc. - 264 today. The main technology stack is PHP / 1C / JavaScript.

Most of the work is wholesale companies that import and sell something (the largest employer is DNS). There is an analog Avito - also a large company. Everything else is already small things of all kinds.

Very few companies that outsource China, Japan, USA, Australia. With the first two - language and cultural barrier. With two others - the low level of developers. If the developer has his hands, then he is rather quickly handed to the state for a remote job, or he is offered a relocation. I myself work for a remote, and get about 2 RFP levels of Vladivostok.

Conclusion: there is work, but sn does not shine, and the level of tasks is quite typical. If you take smaller cities in the region, for example, Ussuriysk - 200k people, then there are 5 vacancies for the whole city - fota the goods and upload them to the online store.

Perspectives


I would like to say at once that in the region it used to be felt that people have money. Automobile business, Buy-Sell from Asian countries and fishing gave good money. Now the first two moments were covered administratively, and people became poorer. Many nightclubs and businesses have closed.

Infrastructure and logistics of the region


Recently, a lot of money has been invested in the region, so the quality of roads, utilities, and recreation areas are being kept in order. However, this is all relatively true for 100km around Vladivostok. We drive off a little further, and the ass begins.

  • Ticket Vladivostok-Moscow - 15 thousand rubles one way.
  • Ticket Vladivostok-Seoul - 10 thousand rubles one way.
  • Ticket Vladivostok-Pyongyang - are you really interested in this?

Of course, you can always take a bus tour to cross-border China, but there:

  • Nothing to do - just a big Chinese market
  • It has become much more expensive after 2014.

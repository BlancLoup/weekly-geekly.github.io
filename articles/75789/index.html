ASUS Eee PC 1201N - moving away from stereotypes



The 12.1-inch Eee PC 1201N netbook, officially announced today by ASUS, differs sharply from its competitors by the presence of the Intel Atom 330 dual-core processor and the NVIDIA Ion (GeForce 9400M) graphics.

I think that not only are I tired of the cloned devices, which are built on a bunch of Intel Atom 280 + Intel 945GSE + ICH7M chipset, are equipped with 1 GB of memory and differ from each other at best in the color and number of USB ports. Eee PC 1201N in this sense, as a breath of fresh air.

New features:

OS: Windows 7 Home Premium;
Display: 12.1 inches with LED backlight and a resolution of 1366x768 pixels;
Processor: Intel Atom 330 dual-core;
RAM: 2GB with expansion up to 3GB;
Graphics: NVIDIA Ion (GeForce 9400M);
Winchester: 250GB + 500GB Eee Storage free online storage.
Ports: VGA, HDMI, 3 USB 2.0, Ethernet, headphone / microphone;
Memory card support: MMC / SD (SDHC);
Wireless modules: Wi-Fi 802.11n, Bluetooth 2.1 + EDR;
Battery: up to 5 hours of battery life;
Size: 295x206x33 mm;
Weight: 1.45 kg;

Netbook will be sold from mid-December in black and silver colors. The recommended cost is $ 499.

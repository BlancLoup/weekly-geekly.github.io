In the most popular mobile instant messenger added strong end-to-end encryption at the level of individual users. In this case, the key never leaves the device, which means that only the user will be able to read the messages. Physically, there is no opportunity to intercept it even from a service or service provider.

In general, WhatsApp and other mobile messengers are often criticized for vulnerabilities that allow listening to data. The Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF) even compared cryptographic methods to 39 mobile instant messaging applications, and only 6 of them satisfied the experts.

Computer Security Specialist Moxi Marlinspike, together with his Open Whisper Systems team, developed an open protocol for asynchronous encryption . Asynchrony here means that end users do not need to wait for a server to respond or to have a communication partner on the network. This protocol was implemented in WhatsApp.

So far, only the Android version has encryption. The new feature does not work for group chats and for messages with photos or videos, but Open Whisper Systems promises to deal with this situation in the future. There will be a verification by key so that you can make sure that the addressee is who he claims to be.

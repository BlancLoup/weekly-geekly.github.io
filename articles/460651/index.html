Meeting of the Society of Anonymous Testers: TMS, monitoring monitoring, search quality assessment and native iOS tests

Hi, my name is Zoya and I am a tester. Our Seventh Meeting of the Society of Anonymous Testers this time will be held in Avito's office (and yes, I work here, so there was practically no chance of having a meeting in Avito!).


The meeting will be on August 1 at 19:00 (registration - from 18:30) in the Moscow office (Lesnaya st., 7). We will talk about self-made TMS, monitoring monitoring, approaches to assessing the quality of search and native iOS tests. Under the cut - abstracts of speeches, links to the registration and live broadcast. Come!



Reports


As our TMS has grown tentacles. Vadim Shashin (Avito)



We lived for ourselves, like all ordinary companies - the testers department wrote test cases, the automation department automated them, the testers checked them ... that's all. And then there were cross-functional teams, and the world shuddered. I'll tell you about how our testaranger has ceased to satisfy our needs and we have grown tentacles to it in order to simplify the life of testers.



Monitoring monitoring system. Mikhail Efremov and Anna Manakova



With the help of monitoring systems all monitor their systems. But the monitoring systems themselves also need monitoring. Using the example of one software product (from online analytics of large amounts of data), which on the one hand contains a proprietary closed core, and on the other hand is a platform for open source development (both internal extensions and integration with the outside world using a comprehensive API) we will look at cases of a thin self-health check and how such opportunities help make business monitoring great again.



On the quality of the search online store. Dinar Hamadiyarov (Ozone)



How to measure the quality of search online store? How to find out if search quality has become better in the new search engine release? Who are assessors?
The report will describe the approaches to assessing the quality of an online store search.

The relevance of the PaaS model for electronic document management systems

Quite a lot has already been said about the model ‚ÄúSED in the SaaS format‚Äù. Recall that since about 2010, players began to appear in the Russian market creating new products and solutions in this format. They set the trend. By 2011-2012, several large players entered the market with SaaS-solutions for project management, which included, among other things, elements of financial systems, CRM and electronic document management systems. These solutions are not directly SED in the SaaS format, but at the same time they partially solve the problems of modern SED and even allow automating the company's unique business processes in one form or another.

The model "EDS in the SaaS format" and the model "EDS in the On-premise format" (local implementation of EDS at the customer's facilities) have advantages and differences applicable to the requirements for the EDS and the type of customer according to the number of users. If we talk about the main advantages of the SaaS model, then they are more applicable to micro enterprises and small business companies, because money and time are saved for purchasing equipment and additional software, negotiations, testing, implementation and configuration. Choosing such a model, the customer will see the application at work already during the test operation period, the implementation time will be much less.

The downsides are that the EDMS in the SaaS model is usually poorly customized for the unique requirements of the company, since only the supplier has access to the ‚Äúfine‚Äù settings and codes. In particular, if in the process the customer decides to transfer the decision to his capacity, the decision will be nontrivial.

The advantages of On-Premise EDMS are in the security of data storage in the local network compared to SaaS. Modern On-Premise EDMS have much richer functionality than SaaS counterparts, have built-in BPM-modules, built-in applications that allow you to customize typical and unique business processes of the company to varying requirements.

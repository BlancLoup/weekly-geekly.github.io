The law on the mandatory transfer of servers to Russia has been postponed indefinitely.



According to RBC, the government decided to postpone the law indefinitely. With this, it (the law) can be significantly revised or canceled altogether.

Honestly for the last half year, for me this is the most pleasant news. I think that I am far from the only one who used foreign services that to some extent process personal data. I buy clothes, medicines, small equipment. It is clear that large players such as Ebay and Amazon moved their data centers to Russia, but ordinary foreign stores would not bother, but simply covered the delivery to Russia.

The question is, what caused this decision? So a couple of days ago, the news was announced that this law would not affect social networks, and now it turned out to be postponed for an indefinite period.

Is it worth waiting for the quiet adoption of a less rigid law? What do you think?

Victorinox has released the world's smallest 256 gigabyte SSD-drive



SSDs are becoming more and more popular, and over time, these devices are getting smaller, more spacious and faster (read / write). The company Victorinox, known for its solutions not only in the field of universal knives, but also in the field of portable storage devices (the company previously released flash drives embedded in the knife), at CES presented an interesting device - a miniature SSD drive with a capacity of 256 gigabytes. The drive is so small that it is quite possible to use it as a keychain to the keys.

The company Victorinox calls this its product the world's smallest SSD-drive with a similar capacity, and probably it is. The read and write speeds are 3 and 2 times faster, respectively, than those of the fastest USB-flash drives.

I will try to look at this problem a bit from the other side - from the part of the university teacher who is trying to sow sensible, kind and sometimes eternal.



Since 2003, I have been teaching C # programming to students at a branch of a large Moscow university. The choice of the C # language was realized, since at that time there was simply nothing better than teaching the fundamentals of algorithms, OOP, and user interface development. C # has changed, the topics that I have sought to consider in my subjects have changed, but students have changed most of all in these 7 years. Do you mind if we talk about them?

Symptoms

The last 3-4 years there has been a strange trend. It seems that the teachers are the same, and the lecture courses, and the development environment, and the level of preparation of students for the 4th course, when they are moving from the basics of programming to more complex things, it becomes lower and lower.
Students are inert, what was given at the lecture at the exam can only tell the best students, but they, when trying to ask a question to the side, to think, fall into a stupor.

Disease

Can you imagine a student of 2-3 courses seeking to get a higher education in the IT field and who has not read a single book this year? I'm not talking about books related to the specialty, I'm talking generally about books. A man has not read a single book in almost half a year! Just do not say that you also did not read, and you have the Internet, and there ... They did not read the Internet either. So how can you? And I can, and not only imagine, I see them at my lectures every week.
Recalling my recent student body, and especially the students of the group in which I studied, I cannot remember anyone who would not be interested in anything else. We had people in the group who were seriously and for a long time carried away by programming, circuit engineering, modeling using various CAD systems, database design, network administration, web design and many others. Today's student believes that having come to class, he has already committed an act worthy of admiration. It is not necessary to be interested in the subject.

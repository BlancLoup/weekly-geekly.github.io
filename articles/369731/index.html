In quantum mechanics, any motions are possible, but some are preferable to others. These preferred motions are called quantum states. They have certain frequencies. Simple quantum systems, such as a hydrogen atom, have frequencies that are in fairly simple relationships with each other. The music analogy is very good for explaining the role of frequency in quantum mechanics, but, as we will see in the next section, this theory contains other key ideas for which explanation it is not so easy to find analogies in everyday life.

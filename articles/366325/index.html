Using modern technology, it would be impossible to create a gravitational wormhole just because it took a huge amount of gravitational energy, and we still do not know how to generate it. However, in electromagnetism, the progress of metamaterials and the invisibility effect has allowed researchers to progress in achieving several goals.

The scientists of the Department of Physics from the laboratory of the Autonomous University of Barcelona (the Department of Physics at the Universitat Aut√≤noma de Barcelona) have developed and created the first experimental wormhole that can connect two areas of space with a magnet. It consists of a tunnel that transmits a magnetic field from one point to another, and at the same time the field itself cannot be detected, it is just invisible all the way.


The researchers used metamaterials and meta-surfaces to build an experimental tunnel. From the source: a magnet or an electromagnet, a magnetic field appears at the other end of the wormhole in the form of an isolated magnetic monopole. And the main effect is that the magnetic field travels from one point to another through a dimension that is beyond, well known to us, three dimensions.

The wormhole itself, in this experiment, is a sphere of different layers: the outer layer with a ferromagnetic surface, the second inner layer, is made of superconducting material, and the inner ferromagnetic sheet is rolled into a cylinder and crosses the sphere from one end to the other.

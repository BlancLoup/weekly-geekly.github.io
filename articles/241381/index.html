Gamedzhemy and what they eat

Hi, Habr!

Over the past year, such a concept as gamejam has become very popular in Russia and in the world. Almost every week a large number of game developers are going to make a game on the topic of jam in a short time. But there are people who are still new to this concept. This article is for them.



What it is


Let's start with the very concept of "gamejam." Formally, this is a competition for high-speed game development (Game - game, Jam - from the musical concept of the jam session, when musicians gather and improvise without special preliminary preparation). Very similar to specialized hackathons. The phenomenon of recent years is gaining increasing popularity in the world. At the last gamejam Ludum Dare , which takes place 3 times a year, 2538 works were collected, and at the globalgamejam.org - the largest gamejam - 4289 works in January 2014.
Most often gamejams last 48 hours, although in this matter the timing depends only on the organizers of a particular gamejam.


Instruments


Digital content creation tools are simplified, made available to a wider range of people. Convenient game editors appear: Unity , Game Maker , Construct 2 , not forgetting the good old Flash, etc. Accordingly, an increasing number of people are starting to participate in jams, and even those who are not directly part of the igrodel community.


Participation


Most often free, although sometimes there are options for gamejam, where you have to pay a certain amount (usually symbolic) to take part in it.

Participation in gamejams by industry professionals allows you to stretch your bones and test yourself in high-speed game development, try a prototype for which they don‚Äôt have time and motivation outside of gamejam. But also for beginners, gamejam makes it possible to understand what game development is in principle, to master the tools and technologies, and, of course, to catch the joy of creativity in its purest form.

The main task in gamejam is to have time to collect something finished, and not all participants cope with this task.
Therefore, the main tips:
  • Game design should be as simple as possible, and contain no more than one killer feature.
  • A good idea would be to focus on the setting and atmospheric of the game - very often the voting and choice of works are carried out by the participants themselves and the bright game world will be judged by the right.
  • In no case should you try to master new technologies during gamejam. If you are going to work with a new tool, it is better to master it in advance.
  • It sounds trite, but plan your time. The issue of time management in a short period of time is particularly acute.
  • And finally, take a break and sleep before the match. Your body will thank you.


In general, the victory in gamejame is somewhat reminiscent of the victory of the game on the market when it hits the first places of the tops. You have a limited resource - time and team, there is an idea (new? Original?) And its implementation (good / not so). You have an audience that you need to get into: other participants or visitors to the site where gamejam passes. All of these factors, as in the case of a commercial product, ultimately create success in gamejam (as they create it on popular platforms). Therefore, taking part in gamejams, you can experience how the world of ‚Äúreal‚Äù gamedev turns in miniature.

A look into the future


In the future, due to even greater popularization of both video games and development tools, the gamejacking movement will grow. It is possible that the age range of the target audience of gamejams will expand, and those will be held at the school level, along with math olympiads.

Also, it is possible that we will see more and more ready-made commercial products that will be assembled in jams, as the quality of the participants' work is steadily increasing. We can witness large-scale online TV shows, where presenters, each hour worth $ 10k, will rush around the hall filled with gems and monitors, interview them, VJs will stream the development of special effects on giant monitors, and viewers will play fresh prototypes and give advice online.

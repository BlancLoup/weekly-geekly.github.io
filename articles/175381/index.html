Crazy skyscrapers of the future



From buildings that pump water in the Himalayan glaciers, to round towers that can allow their residents to travel without leaving their homes. How do architects imagine the buildings of the future?

The skyscraper is one of the most significant symbols of modern society. The sight of Burj Khalifa billowing in the middle of the desert is an impressive image of technological progress. The engineering solutions and the amount of resources that allowed this building to soar towards the skies may seem inconceivable. But besides the fact that buildings are growing higher and higher, and consume less energy, in general, they are still not far from the Empire State Building .

EVolo magazine holds an annual skyscraper design contest, in which architects are asked: what else can we do with skyscrapers? Some solutions represent buildings that will save us from water shortages, others - buildings that can help cope with the problem of garbage, or even those that can in the event of a disaster move independently to a safe place.

All projects are absolutely fantastic. Most of them require technologies that do not even exist yet. But maybe instead of building higher and higher, we will try to build smarter?

Himalaya Water Tower (pictured above)
Zhi Zheng, Hongchuan Zhao, Dongbai Song
China

These buildings are designed to collect water frozen in the Himalayan glaciers, where today 40% of fresh water is concentrated on Earth. The buildings are located on several pipes that go deep into the ice and produce water for their residents.

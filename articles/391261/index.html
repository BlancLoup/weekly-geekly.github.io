Google's car accident is a good sign of technology development.

As you know, on February 14, the Google Gen-2 (Lexus) unmanned vehicle first got into an accident when it collided with a bus while driving at a speed of 3 km / h. This is the first accident that occurred due to the fault of the computer. Google has partially pleaded guilty.

What actually happened can be seen from the official report of the incident . Additional details are in the monthly Google Robo Car Report.

The description of the emergency leads to some interesting thoughts. Apparently, the program Robo cars began to handle typical situations on the road, which are not described in the SDA.

Google reports that it has recently started testing new behaviors. In this case, turn right at the red light from the main street, where the far right lane is extended. In such a situation, the usual practice is that drivers turn an extended lane into a double one, putting the cars in two rows. In this case, the right row is pressed against the curb.

According to the standard program of the car, only one lane would be available, so the first stopped car would block all the others from turning to the right, which would be very unpleasant.

The program decided that the bus as a respectable driver slowed down slightly, giving way to the lane. But he did not, and the unmanned machine collided with him at very low speed.

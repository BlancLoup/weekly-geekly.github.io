How to start


First you need money. You need to live somewhere, eat something, it all costs money, which you should have at least for a couple of months while you are looking for work and get your first salary.

Housing


Inexpensive one-bedroom apartment on the outskirts of Moscow costs about 20,000 rubles. It can be cheaper, but the conditions will be bad and a great chance that you will be thrown. In order to rent an apartment, you need to prepare at least 2.5 apartment costs (20x2.5 = 50.000 rubles) - the landlord will take for 1 month and for the last (to insure), plus the agent who will find you an apartment, will need to pay another 0.5-1 cost of the apartment. You can bargain with the agent (usually all require 1 cost, but can be reduced to 0.5), as with the apartment owner (knock out the best conditions - for example, buying a washing machine, microwave, reduce the cost by 1-2 tr.).

Nutrition


Food in Moscow is more expensive than in other cities - this is due to the level of wages (here it is also higher), but there is one trick. The most expensive are small private stores, followed by supermarkets (Ramstore, etc.), and the cheapest (and at the same time high-quality) hypermarkets (Auchan, Ikea, etc.). It is in the latter that I advise buying - save from 30 to 200% on products. It is enough to go there once a month and buy everything you need (milk, eggs, meat, fish, cereals / pasta, canned goods, etc.), and in supermarkets to buy perishable foods - bread, dairy products, etc.
In order to estimate how much to put on food, calculate how much you eat on average per month, calculate the cost, for example, in the "Platypus" and multiply by 2 months.

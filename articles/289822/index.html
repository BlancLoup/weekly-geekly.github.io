The project to create a federal catalog of copyright objects did not receive the full approval of the expert group


Photo: Yury Martyanov / Kommersant

On April 20, an expert group of the Public Chamber discussed a project to create a federal catalog of copyright objects, Kommersant reports . This project is an alternative to the proposal of the Russian Union of right holders for global licensing and taxation of licensed content from telecom operators, and consequently, from users of the Network. In general, this idea was met more warmly than the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãa global license (RSP led by Nikita Mikhalkov), but this project was not yet fully approved by the expert group.

It is worth recalling that the expert group of the public chamber includes representatives of Internet providers, Internet companies, copyright holders, the Ministry of Communications, the Ministry of Economy, the State Duma. The catalog initiative itself was developed by the OP with the participation of federal telecom operators: VimpelCom, MegaFon and MTS. The essence of the idea is the creation of a catalog of intellectual property products, which will store data on the rights holders and content, the rights to which belong to them. In order to get into such a register, the right holder must submit an application, with a further process of identification (using electronic digital signature). The functions of monitoring and editing the catalog are supposed to be entrusted to the Ministry of Culture, the Ministry of Communications and Mass Media, Roskomnadzor, companies with 100% state participation or an accredited collective rights management organization (the latter is rated by the group as the least desirable).

In addition, simultaneously with the creation and introduction of the catalog, it is planned to simplify the legislation in terms of stimulating the processes of buying and selling copyright. According to the authors of the project, the information of the general catalog allows subjects and objects of copyright law to interact with each other. So, content aggregators, users or third-party companies will be able to directly go to the right holders, and enter into appropriate agreements on the use of content.

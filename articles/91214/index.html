IconBit HDS41L and HD40NMT vs HDTV_test_files Player



Greetings to you [% username%], I want to share with you the result of testing two players from IconBit: HDS41L and HD40NMT for their omnivorousity and performance.

Initial data


  • IconBit HDS41L - 1pc;
  • IconBit HD40NMT - 1pcs;
  • HDTV_test_files file set (1.6);
  • SATA Hdd, freshly formatted and containing only test files;
  • Home media server - a computer running linux on which samba and UPNP (mediatomb) servers are raised;



Test result


Unfortunately, the comparative test was not entirely fair, the video on the HD40NMT was only submitted via SAMBA. He completely refused to show anything from the UPNP server, and I tried two different ones (mediatomb and ushare). Finally


Conclusions that can be made:
  1. DLNA protocol is faster than SAMBA
  2. In the HDS41L player, the DLNA protocol is implemented better than the SAMBA, but the HD40NMT per turn
  3. The omnivorous HDS41L is slightly higher than HD40NMT
  4. HDS41L is faster than HD40NMT - loads faster, interface response time, video start (40nmt often thinks long when starting a video, in 41l this was not noticed)

And yet, I believe that both players turned out to be successful, I have both of them in the household, and for several months there was not a single film that would not go to them.

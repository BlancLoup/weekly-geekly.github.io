PRIUT.KZ - Help for homeless animals


On 18/08/2010, we launched a beta version of the PRIUT.KZ project. Before the launch of the new version of the project, we received a grant from the Soros Kazakhstan Foundation in the competition of social projects at BarcampCA 10 in the form of $ 5,000 and a server from Kazakhtelecom.
And on August 26, a presentation of the project was given in Almaty in the Kofedelia coffee shop and we began to be more active.

In this way, we are greeted by a project, about the design I think that there will soon be another redesign.

What we see in the screenshot:

The main menu, which contains: About the project (information about the history, missions and objectives of the project), news (news from the life of the project), friends of the project (everyone who provided assistance and support to PRIUT.KZ ), informatorium (useful information, more on this later ), contacts (contact e-mail)

The ‚ÄúGive Pet‚Äù button is intended for registering the animals you have found on the street and in your care. Search form appears after the pre-moderation.

The most important button " WELCOME PET " is designed to search for a loving pet.

Gallery of animals looking for owners. (at the moment the site is filled so the number of animals is not large). In the gallery, photos are displayed randomly.

The left menu, which contains some information on the structure of the animal through the project, as well as on how to take the animal. In this menu there is a contract tab in which you can download a word-file of the contract that will protect your pet from all sorts of trouble.

Happy stories - this block randomly displays one happy story about an animal that was settled using our site or this pet was on our site, but settled in some other way.

On each pet card you can find the following information.
Basic information (Russia, weight, nickname, breed, etc.), contacts (all information about contact persons (temporary guardians)), health (availability of a pass. Passport, vaccinations, sterilization, treatment from external and internal parasites), socialization (degree socialization, getting along or not getting along with other animals and children)

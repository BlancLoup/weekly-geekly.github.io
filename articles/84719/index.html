Chronicles of Ubisoft: protection was even more inadequate than many thought

We have already read about how Ubisoft is going to protect its new games and Assassin's Creed 2 in particular. And here is the information, so to speak, at first hand, from journalists from PC Gamer, who have already tried the PC-novelty Ubisoft.

The fact that the game works only if the connection is confirmed. Well, who would doubt. But this is not the main thing. It turns out that internet connection should be more than stable. At the slightest problems with communication Assassin's Creed 2 without hesitation throws the player into the main menu. But progress is not maintained, so the level / mission will have to start from the very beginning. And this is indicated by the bourgeois, in whom the situation with the online is somewhat better than that of Mother Russia and the adjacent Allied and not-so states.

The incident is that with their attempts to combat piracy, Ubisoft primarily punishes users who purchase a licensed version of the game. Assassin's Creed 2 will break, and break quickly. And on the torrents of the country it will be possible to download a fully working version, bypassing the constant online check. But many of those who give up their money for a key / disk will either have to suffer, or, as often happens, to quack their litsuha anyway, so as not to strain.

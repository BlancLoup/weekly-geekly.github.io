In information retrieval tasks, one of the most important tasks is to find an exactly specified substring in a string. The primitive search algorithm for substrings in a string is based on the enumeration of all substrings whose length is equal to the length of the search pattern, and character-by-character comparison of such substrings with a search pattern. The Knut-Morris-Pratt algorithm is one of the first linear estimation algorithms in the worst case. The key point for understanding the essence of the algorithm is the fact that if the suffix found in the previous step cannot be extended to the next position, then we try to consider smaller suffixes as long as possible.

This means that the while loop will be executed no more than n times, which gives the final estimate of the time of the O (n) algorithm.

Consider the Knut-Morris-Pratt algorithm based on the use of the prefix function.

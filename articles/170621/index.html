Dimension Elite 3D Printer Review

In the last topic about 3d printing, I promised to review the Dimension Elite printer. It's time to keep promises.

Intro


In a series of random events I was lucky to get into some organization, one might even say, to participate in its development. It turned out that a designated printer appeared above us and a person from the Moscow branch of Dimension taught me to use it. Now it's my turn to tell you something about him.

The main thing


Like everything, you should start with the characteristics:

As you can see, such a small working volume requires a very small and heavy box. It so happened, as it seems to me, due to two factors: the presence of rather massive mechanisms for moving the table up / down and moving the print head in two dimensions. Plus, the printing technology requires heating the working chamber volume to as high as 75 degrees, which, in general, is not small. All this requires not the weakest power supply. If you believe more manufacturer documentation, the printer consumes about 1.5 kW / h. Well, like any heater, it heats the space around very well. In the summer it is quite difficult to stay in the same room.

Having loaded the cartridges and the substrate into the printer, it's time to close the door in it and sit down at the computer to prepare the models for printing.
Software is proudly called CatalystEX. Honestly, I did not understand whether it is free or not. And I did not find anything in Google, except for the old version. The interface is simple and intuitive to anyone who has ever seen any 3D editor. In order not to look at the naked editor, I will show you a little man in it.

The General tab allows you to select the layer thickness, object construction mode (monolithic, large or small cells), the algorithm, the construction of supports and the scale.
On the Orientation tab, you can specify which of the surfaces will be at the bottom / top, and change the view mode of the model. By pressing the Process STL button (and the software understands the model ONLY in the STL format), we get a beautiful layer cake that shows what the model will look like after printing.

I can not draw an analogy of support for the eyes of our little man and with this character from the Split Space (or Fly Trip'a)
Analogy


Yes, the printer is so cunning that it can even pause printing in a certain layer. For example, we make a part with a space for a bearing, you can stop printing, put the bearing on its rightful place and bury it tightly in plastic. There is one restriction; it cannot be higher (that is, not sticking out) of the upper part of the model being manufactured.
Having prepared the model, click the Add To Pack button and get into the third tab:

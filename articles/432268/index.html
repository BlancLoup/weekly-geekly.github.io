Computer vision: know the price in person




The world market for face recognition, according to the company MarketsandMarkets, by 2021 will grow to $ 6.84 billion. The Russian market in this respect is much more modest - tens of millions of dollars. The main reason for low volumes is the high cost of the final solution for small and medium businesses.

Face recognition for several years as a routine. And when a client came to us with a request to make a tracking service for VIP clients, he was very surprised that we had no such solution. After all, the phone has face recognition, and why not in the IP camera?

The task of monitoring and detecting people in a stream until the boom of neural networks remained extremely difficult.

Let us consider this thesis on the example of one of the world's largest video surveillance systems. In Moscow, cameras are installed everywhere: on the territory and in the buildings of schools and kindergartens, at the MCC stations, stadiums, public transport stops and bus stations, in parks, in underground pedestrian crossings.

Moscow ranks second in the world in the number of surveillance cameras per capita: there are 11.63 surveillance cameras per 1,000 inhabitants, which is almost two times more than in Hong Kong and six times more than in Beijing.

Cameras over intercoms in Moscow recognize faces and send a push notification if a person from a search team hits the lens. The Moscow metro also has a face recognition zone.

The Moscow government spends about 5 billion rubles a year servicing the system, and the recordings from each camera are kept for only five days. How much does it cost to connect a facial recognition system? According to experts , at least $ 100 for each camera connected. For small businesses, this is an exorbitant cost.

Products on the shelf




Smart people in trade marketing have come up with a special term for the situation when you come at two in the morning for your beloved Agusha and see that the damn yogurt disappeared from the store shelf.

OSA (On-Shelf Availability) is the availability of goods on the store shelf at any time. In turn, the situation when the goods are not on the shelf and you want to buy them is called OOS (Out-of-Stock).

Full OOS is an unpleasant situation. According to our data, 25% of people go to the store just for what they need - no situational purchases. Such a man-flint will unfold and leave, without looking at the chocolate bar at the cash register.

There are several reasons for the emergence of OOS: the analyst in the head office has become mistaken with the forecast of orders, somewhere there is a problem with logistics, the goods are on another shelf. 20% of all goods in Russia are constantly in a state from OSA to OOS, and retail chains lose 2 to 4% of their turnover.

Computer vision simply determines the presence, quantity and correctness of the location of products on the shelf, eliminates the human factor and automates the process of supplying the store.

The system recognizes almost any products on the shelves with an accuracy of more than 95%, takes into account the shape, color, logo, type of packaging, and sends employees to the application reports with information on the range and display within 10 seconds.

Image Recognition technology defines:

  • what goods are on the shelf and the number of units of products;
  • whether the product has a price tag, and whether the price for it is approved at the central office;
  • is there an approved promotion for this product;
  • what products are missing on the shelf despite the approved range.

An additional element of protection from "I forgot" the manager - the automatic setting of tasks for the display of the goods and the specified frequency analysis of the availability of goods. Access to the system is configured at once for several responsible persons in order to completely eliminate the lazy human factor.

Testing of such a system in Pyaterochka led to an increase in sales of goods, which showed how effective the video and photo control displays.

The potential benefits from the introduction of automated control over the availability of goods on the shelf are up to 5% in turnover due to an increase in the indicator of the availability of goods on the shelf.

Queues




We wrote several articles about the queues, the essence of which boils down to one sentence: customers experience negative, and any negative leads to the fact that another time the customer does not come to this store.

Video analytics determine the number of customers in the queue. When the camera records that more than five people have accumulated, the manager receives a notification about the need to open an additional cash desk.

At the same time, one camera is able to control several zones of the sales area at once, which reduces the cost of a solution for small business.

Counting Visitors




Manager in real time monitors the dynamics of visits to the store during the whole working day, knows the time and day of the most active customer activity. It is useful to find out objectively, and not from the words of the sellers, when the store has a peak of visits, on weekends or weekdays, whether customers prefer to shop early in the morning or late in the evening.

More advanced analytics will be able to track the movement of customers up to a meter. In accordance with this information, you can send them push-notifications with relevant promotional offers.

Over time, even a small business will be able to find a solution in which it will be possible to figure out how often a particular customer goes to a specific store and, by comparing this data with the interests of the customer, form regular promotional offers.

When producing new smartphones, manufacturers often forget about the most important thing - a good battery. Yes, 4 cores, a big screen, ultra-thin design is good. But do not live out until the evening on one charge? That's too much!
Is there a good compromise at the moment? So that the performance is good, and the screen, and the battery. And while the phone did not look like a brick?
Yes there is!
Lenovo has released its new smartphone called the Lenovo P770. Two cores, a good screen, 2 sim cards, a 3500 mAh battery. What about the price‚Ä¶
Interesting? Then read on!

Specifications


Screen: 4.5 ", 540x960 IPS
Processor: Mediatek MT6577T (Cortex-A9 1.2GHz, 2 cores) + GPU: PowerVR SGX531
RAM: 1GB
User Memory: 4GB
Camera: front 0.3MP, rear - 5MP
Connectivity: GPS, Bluetooth, Wifi (802.11 b / g / n), 2 SIM cards (GSM + WCDMA)
Optional: OTG
Battery: 3500mAh
Dimensions: 133x67x11.8mm

At first glance, the characteristics of an ordinary Chinese smartphone: 2 SIM cards, MTK6577 chip, 4.5 "screen with a resolution of 540x960 pixels. But there are some differences, namely: OTG support and a 3500 mAh battery.

Price

Lenovo P770 will be officially sold in Russia starting in March. According to this information, the price will be 11,990 rubles .
The official launch of Lenovo P770 sales in Ukraine is also expected.

The price in China at the time of this review is ~ $ 245 , which is about $ 150 cheaper than the estimated cost in Russia. It should not be forgotten that the price of $ 240 already includes the cost of delivery, and even paying $ 30 for the delivery of EMS you can save more than $ 100. But when buying in China, there is a minus - the lack of a "guarantee". More precisely, not a complete absence, but a nuance to use - in case of a breakdown, you will need to send the phone back at your own expense (~ $ 15), then wait a while until it is repaired and sent back. This kind of warranty is valid for 1 year.

Let's roughly calculate how much a smartphone costs when buying directly from the factory. The price of $ 227 includes: ~ $ 15 shipping cost + ~ $ 10 profit of the seller + $ 12 commission of the trading platform. Total: $ 227- $ 37 = $ 190. But this price also includes the profit of the intermediary, since the Lenovo plant does not ship the products directly to the sellers.
It turns out that after getting from China to Russia, the phone becomes more than 2 times more expensive. Not bad, right?

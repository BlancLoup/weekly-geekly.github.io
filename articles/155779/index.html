Great outcome of IT & Media-people in Chiang Mai



Some old Habra still remember my post ‚Äú Do you want coworking in Thailand? ". For 3 years, big and interesting changes have taken place in Chiang Mai: here a critical mass of initiative Russian IT specialists appeared who are eager for change.

Recently, in Chiang Mai, the Russians organized the CNX Business Club, a non-profit organization that unselfishly helps IT & Media specialists to settle in a city that claims to be one of the IT centers in Southeast Asia. I'll tell you in more detail than the attractive city.

It is comfortable here:
  • adequate neighbors
  • fresh food
  • 10Mbps
  • 300 sunny days a year
  • have the opportunity to be in the fresh air at any time and not leave this place without special need, without interrupting production
  • can ride an open top motorbike


And:
  • safe (both for life and for a wallet)
  • there is quality and affordable medicine
  • there is electricity (99.997% uptime)
  • there is gas
  • eat clean drinking water
  • hot water without shutdowns :)


For creative techno-personalities, there is at least an easily accessible effective infrastructure that facilitates life:
  • 3G + mobile internet
  • a dozen and a half Apple Store, evenly distributed over the landscape of 3 pieces per sq. km.
  • convenience stores and ready meal delivery
  • choice of restaurants and bars of various specifics within walking distance
  • the ability to change the "picture" without making efforts (for example, to drive to the sea for the weekend to think)
  • exceptionally polite and well educated staff even in the most inaccessible places


In order not to be cold, the city is successfully located in a mountain valley on the 18th parallel of the Northern Hemisphere, at an altitude of 400 meters above sea level. So that it is not noisy - only 150,000 people inhabit it. To avoid it being hot there include partly cloudy.

In order not to be bored - during the day you can use any of the 20 air flights to the coast.

And in the city there are 3 universities, 2 polytechnic institutes, 3 specialized colleges, several dozen high schools and language schools, and some monstrous number of art workshops, galleries and art cafes with free WiFi. For a weekend, there are several mountain lakes with Scandinavian landscapes, tropical waterfalls with clear water (from the sanitary-epidemiological point of view), hot springs and a dozen and a half golf courses where the price starts from $ 30 for 9 holes (for beginners).

And yes, there is such a strange pattern here - 60% of Europeans of pre-retirement age are employed in the IT business (all the rest teach). By the way, in the central part of the city 30% of the population are from Europe or their descendants.

What is known about ITIL 4 certification

This year ITIL 4 update has been released. We are telling how the certification of IT service management specialists will be carried out according to the new standard.


/ Unsplash / Helloquence

How does the certification process change


The latest update for ITIL 3 was introduced eight years ago. During this time, the IT industry has undergone significant changes and "overgrown" with new technologies. Many companies have begun to introduce IT management practices (such as ITIL-based ITSM).

In order to adapt them to the changed context, Axelos specialists responsible for developing the ITIL methodology released an update, ITIL 4, at the beginning of this year. New areas of knowledge appeared in it related to increasing user satisfaction, value creation flows and flexible methodologies like Agile, Lean and DevOps.

Along with new practices, approaches to certification of IT service management professionals have changed. In ITIL 3, ITIL Expert was considered the highest rank in the ITIL system.

In the fourth version, this level was divided into two areas - ITIL Management Professional and ITIL Strategic Leader. The first is for IT managers, and the second is for heads of departments not related to information technology (experts who have completed both courses receive the title ITIL Master).



Each of these areas includes its own set of exams (the requirements for them and the training programs at Axelos promised to be published closer to the end of 2019). But to get the opportunity to pass them, you need to pass the certification of the basic level - ITIL 4 Foundation. All the necessary information on it was published at the beginning of the year.

What is included in the basic level


In February, Axelos presented the book ITIL Foundation. ITIL 4 Edition. Its task is to explain the key concepts and lay the foundation for the subsequent study of in-depth programs.

ITIL 4 Foundation covers the following topics:

  • Basic concepts of service management;
  • The purpose and components of ITIL;
  • Purpose and key definitions of fifteen ITIL practices;
  • Approaches to the implementation of ITIL;
  • Four aspects of service management;
  • Approaches to the creation of value services and their relationship.

What are the questions


The exam consists of 40 questions. For successful delivery you need to answer correctly 26 of them (65%).

The level of complexity corresponds to Bloom's taxonomy , that is, students need not only to answer questions, but also to demonstrate the ability to apply knowledge in practice.

Some of the tasks are test questions with one or more answers. There are tasks that require the examinee to explain key concepts of IT management in writing.

For example, there are issues that need to be defined in terms such as service, user, or customer. In another task, you will have to paint the key components of the ITIL value system. Some more examples can be found in this document from Axelos.

NASA has conducted interplanetary internet testing

According to the press service of the space agency, the specialists of the Jet Propulsion Laboratory in California, using special software, conducted a communication session and transmitted more than a dozen photographs from the NASA spacecraft at a distance of about 37 million kilometers.

The main problem that developers had to deal with was the delays in data transmission, as well as the loss and attenuation of a signal transmitted tens or even hundreds of millions of kilometers. At the same time, the interplanetary Internet should act even more reliably than the earth one, since the life of astronauts or the existence of a spacecraft can often depend on its reliability.

Together, DTN implements the ‚Äúsave and transmit‚Äù principle, that is, nodes store information about points that they recently contacted and, if information arrives, the node first looks for information about the source of the destination in its data, and then transmits the information to the desired destination.

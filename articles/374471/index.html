Earth's magnetic poles will soon remain in place



Scientists have long found that the magnetic poles of our planet are relatively often interchanged. The magnetic south pole becomes north and vice versa. Such changes in the past happened more than once, and experts believe that the inversion will continue in the future. The last "substitution" occurred about 800 thousand years ago. Since the beginning of observations of the dynamics of the Earth‚Äôs magnetic field, scientists have noted a slight shift of the north and south magnetic poles and a slight change in the configuration of the field itself. Does this mean the beginning of a new offset period? Most likely no.

An international group of researchers studied inversions that happened millions of years ago. The periods between changes were also analyzed. Conclusion - now there is little that portends the next cycle of the "changing of the guard" by the poles, people can sleep peacefully.

But how can one understand what happened to the poles millions of years ago? For this it is necessary to study volcanic rocks. During their formation, the magnetic field of the Earth affects the magnetic particles inside the rocks. They get a kind of "magnetic signature", fixing the configuration of the Earth's field at the time of its formation. By the way, heating rocks to a certain level removes the ‚Äúresidual‚Äù configuration, reducing it to the current position of the poles.

So, after studying various rock samples, it turned out that in the period from 30,000 to 50,000 years ago, the magnetic poles of the Earth changed in an unusual way. Generally speaking, when the polarity changes, the magnetic field lines of our planet first take on a complex shape, and the magnetic field itself weakens - up to 10% of the initial value. At the same time, additional poles are formed, some of which can move towards the equator.

The good news is that no - that is, the modern configuration of the magnetic field does not look like that was thousands of years ago. Yes, there is a slight weakening of the magnetic field of the planet, but the overall configuration of the lines of this field does not change, moreover, there are no signs of the possible appearance of additional poles. And this, as scientists have found out, the main signs of an imminent pole shift.

The weakening of the Earth‚Äôs magnetic field has already taken place in the past. The cases closest to us are 48,500 and 46,300 years ago.

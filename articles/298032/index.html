2. And measure consumption efficiency

Measure it in hours of satisfaction / benefit on the invested ruble.

Example:
Buying a car. You bought a car that cost you 750.000 rubles. You have used it for 3 years (about 1000 calendar days). From 1000 calendar days you really used it only for 600 days. On these 600 days, you used it for an average of 4 hours a day, of which 2 hours you usually had to stand in traffic jams, which means using a car without any benefit for yourself (not counting the winter months). Total: You have acquired 1,200 hours of use for 750,000 rubles, which corresponds to 0.0016 hours or about 0.01 minutes of use for one ruble of yours. One hour of use of your car without operating costs will cost you 625 rubles. Consider for yourself whether an hour is worth 625 rubles in an ordinary middle-class car, and this is also without operating costs.

Another example:
You purchased a bike. It cost you 20.000 rubles. You used it for 6 years in the warm season, that is, for five months (150 days) a year, for a total of 900 days. Less your laziness, you used it for 600 days. Every day you used the bike for 3 hours, traffic jams were not a problem for you. Total: You have acquired 1,800 hours of use for 20,000 rubles, that is, 0.09 hours or 5.5 minutes of use for one ruble of yours. One hour of benefit will cost you 11 rubles, while in this calculation we did not take into account the additional health benefits that you brought yourself, riding a bicycle for five years.
Simple solutions are often more effective than complex ones.

Simple desires, when they are fulfilled, in fact, bring exactly as much happiness as the most sophisticated ones.

5. Live by self-reliance.

It makes sense to give a case or a task for a contract, 1) when it will be cheaper for a contract and 2) when you are not able to do something well.

6. Strive to give the market, not to take from him

One can argue for a long time what led the iPhone to success, but the fact that Apple really strived to qualitatively change the experience of using a mobile phone will remain immutable. These two examples are vivid examples of the intention to give to the market, and not to take something from it.

Only companies aimed at giving back become great and live for decades and centuries.

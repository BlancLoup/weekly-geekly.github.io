‚ÄúOne head is good, but much better‚Äù - I thought, and decided to ask the Drupal developers where they got their knowledge from.

In this post we have gathered links to the best resources for exploring Drupal .


Before you begin ...
You choose which blogs to read and whose screencasts to watch, but there are 2 main links: Drupal.org and Google.

Video

To understand the Drupal-way, watch how others work with it:

  • NodeOne Learning library
    Series of short video tutorials on key modules of Drupal: Views , Rules , Page manager , etc.
  • CodeKarate.com: Daily Dose of Drupal
    Wide coverage of topics and reviews. More than 40 categories , more than 140 short video lessons.
  • Video from international conferences DrupalCon - Munich 2012 , Prague 2013 , etc.
  • Drupalize.Me
    The most powerful video library on Drupal, on which the team of professionals Lullabot.com. Most of the material is available for a paid subscription.
  • Podrupalim.com Russian
    Courses on the basics of Drupal and key modules in Russian.

Aggregators


  • Drupal planet
    Main tape Drupal articles. It is better to read via RSS.
  • Theweeklydrop
    Weekly mailing. Many interesting articles and reviews for every taste.
  • Drupal bloggers Russian

The best in Runet


  • xandeadx.ru
    The xandeadx blog contains short notes with solutions to specific problems and code examples.
  • DrupalAce.ru
    Large articles with descriptions of the principles of the drupal, examples of code and reviews of its own modules.
  • Drupal-TV.ru - aggregator of video materials on Drupal.
  • Drupal.ru is the most famous Drupal site in RuNet.

Code


  • api.drupal.org - official API documentation
  • dropbucket.org - Drupal snippets
  • drupalcontrib.org - API contrib modules
  • Examples for Developers - examples of using the kernel API

Books


  • Drupal. Site creation and management (A. Byron)
  • Pro Drupal Development (D. Vandyuk)
  • Professional development of sites on Drupal 7

miscellanea


  • drupalmodules.com - handy search by module.
  • Lullabot Blog
    Blog from the Lullabot.com team with famous Module Monday reviews.
  • www.drush.org
    Drash, a console utility for Drupal that simplifies the routine tasks of downloading modules, resetting the cache, transferring a site from server to server, etc.

For more detailed tips and comments from the participants, see the DrupalSPB.org series of articles .

Where to ask a question / show your code?
  • On dru.io
  • On Toaster
  • At drupal.stackexchange.com
  • On Drupal.ru
  • At an event in your city (DrupalCafe, DrupalMeetup, DrupalDrinkup)

How to help?
  • Tell us in the comments or on Twitter that helped you learn Drupal.
  • Share this material with friends.
  • Install Drupal already and make cool sites on it!

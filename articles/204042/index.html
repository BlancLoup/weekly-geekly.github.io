How I stole a million

The publishing house "Peter" published the book " How I stole a million. Confession of a repentant carder ."



Hardcover edition, 368 pages. But what a reading this is! The author - Sergey Pavlovich is currently serving a sentence in one of the prisons of Belarus and his story is by and large one of the heads of his life.

In this book, Sergei Pavlovich frankly talks about hundreds of sophisticated credit card scams, which he and his accomplices have been turning around the world for more than 10 years using other people's credit cards. This book reveals the methods of the management of "K", a division of the Ministry of Internal Affairs engaged in combating crimes in the field of information technology, and the most prestigious and oldest secret service in the world - the US Secret Service.

The author sheds light on the world of professional cybercriminals hidden from the general public - people of young, ambitious, purposeful, to whom at a very young age fall into the hands of a lot of money ...

Sergei Pavlovich repents of having once embarked on the criminal path, talks about "life" in the detention centers and the zone - a cruel world where relationships, dreams and hopes are crumbling, and encourages other young people not to follow his path.

The book is written in a living modern language, read in one breath. It is noteworthy that the author wrote it while behind bars. Sergey Pavlovich will transfer the entire fee received for the book to the children's oncology hospital near Minsk.

Information about the author:

Sergei Pavlovich, a Belarusian carder, was convicted for selling these stolen bank cards. In 2008, a group of 11 people, entered by Sergey Pavlovich, was charged with a number of crimes related to the illegal entry into the computer networks of trading companies and theft of 170 million credit card data. The brain of these operations was Albert Gonzalez, who was also an informant for the American special services. According to the US authorities, the damage from the actions of "11 friends" of Gonzalez exceeded one billion US dollars.

It would be possible to continue to describe this book in dry words, but I still take the risk and invite you to read it. The real story, the real author, real life, knowledge of technology and, as a result, real time.

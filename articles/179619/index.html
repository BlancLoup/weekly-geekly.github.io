How much bring Top Free applications on the App Store

Just in addition to my previous post, I was struck by the most interesting statistics on Top Grossing Apps and oddly enough, but such interesting numbers have not yet been on Habr√©.

According to the statistical service provided by Distimo, we can find out how much the games in the tops of the App Store earn:



ARPD - average revenue per download. Average earnings from one loading of the application.

Impressive numbers I will tell you. If, for example, a game brings in an average of $ 3 per load, you can safely buy $ 1.5 units and collect profits. What Clash of Clans, Hay Day and Candy Crush Saga are doing in principle.

But the graph shows the growth in profits brought by in-game purchases:



If in January 2012, In-app purchases (IAP) accounted for 53%, in February 2013 this figure was already 76%

In the following graph, we can see that paid applications with IAP earn the most, followed by paid applications without IAP and at the end free applications with IAP:



Here we see that the Japanese bring the most money for one download.
But Germany, the United States and Britain are almost three times behind:



The original article can be found on the Distimo website.

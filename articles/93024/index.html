Foxconn D51S - the right motherboard on the Intel Atom D510 for home NAS

Almost two years ago, once again upgrading the computer, I realized that I no longer want to drive it around the clock. Still, four weak processor cores, three hard drives, five gigabytes of RAM, and a pretty decent gifersin at the time - a non-optimal configuration for torrent-rocking. And I decided to buy a NAS ...

Details of the epic can be found in a great article written hot on the heels , but if briefly - I was deeply disappointed in the NAS on ARM-processors and assembled a small silent computer on the dual-core Intel Atom. Put on it Windows XP and, in general, everything. Torrents are shaken by normal uTorrent, video for PSP and iPhone is pressed by Xvid4PSP and HandBrake, data is being chased by REAL gigabit speed (for home-based NAS on ARM, this interface is present only for beauty), NAS can be remotely controlled from any system, including not my favorite Mac is - well, I was very pleased. Especially considering the price of the solution.

But in December, new Atoms were announced, and in March, the first mothers went to them. Given that the performance of the kernel has not improved, there was no serious point in the upgrade. But the ability to reduce the temperature inside the case by a few degrees and save a few watts per day was somehow seductive ...



I thought, I thought, and as a result I picked up Foxconn D51S. Why her?

1) One of the cheapest on the Atom D510 - $ 85. Only Intel D510MO is cheaper (for $ 4), but it has a passive radiator. This is good if the body is relatively large, and nothing serious than swinging torrents on a computer is not done. I sometimes have a sausage all day long, and the chance of overheating is quite high. Foxconn has a good cooler - not even a cooler, but a small impeller. At maximum speed, it is almost inaudible, on average it is not audible at all. This is especially pleasing in contrast with my previous mom, Intel DG945GCLF2. There, the native cooler squealed like a little brazen pig, and it had to be urgently changed to a small Glacialtech.

2) There are no whistles and fakes, like HDMI-ports and Wi-Fi. For NAS, it is not necessary for nothing, and it charges the price fairly. There is a feeling that the D51S was generally sharpened for industrial needs, because there is an LPT port and COM. I will not do a robot-assembler of smartphones on mamma, but such sharpening is very useful for the 24x7 mode.

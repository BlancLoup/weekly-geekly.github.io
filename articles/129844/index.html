Surf smartphone, sky views and other Xperia Studio projects

It is said that in modern phones there are so many functions that it is unrealistic to use them all. Often it happens, but it is not only a disadvantage - the technical and communication capabilities of smartphones overtake the needs of users, which opens up room for creativity. Half marketing, half-art project Xperia Studio is dedicated to new and original ways to use Xperia phones - in science, art, entertainment. The project is a website that gathers stories of scientists, artists, athletes and musicians, in which Sony Ericsson phones played a role.

Space views


The most interesting of them, in my opinion, is the Invisible Universe app developed by Joshua Peak of Columbia University. Its main advantage is that everyone can use the result of his work - just download the program. Dr. Peak wanted to make available the opportunity for other people to see the beauty of the space around us, and he created an application that demonstrates all the beauty of the sky invisible to the simple eye: in gamma rays, X-rays, optical, infrared and radio frequency.



Unfortunately, just looking through the lens at the world around will not work. The application operates with ready-made huge complete maps of the sky, compiled from data from various types of telescopes - from satellites in Earth orbit to giant radio telescopes in Australia, and collected by astronomers around the world. Turning the phone, you can see how the Universe looks from this point of view in gamma rays, X-rays and so on.

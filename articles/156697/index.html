Problems of remote work at home and their solutions

I have to mention somewhere that I work at home, and in response, one often hears enviously playful remarks in the spirit of ‚Äúgood for you, sitting at home, doing nothing and receiving money‚Äù. Depending on the mood and the interlocutor, you have to either laugh it off, or explain for the hundredth time that I work no less than office colleagues and that there are enough problems in remote work. In this article, I would like to touch on those of them that I happened to encounter in my practice and share ways to solve them.

Some of the points listed below may seem obvious and trite, some work for me, but will not work for others, but I still hope that my experience will be useful to someone. Especially, of course, novice freelancers and people who think of becoming such.

There are not directly addressed "business issues": how to find customers, work with them, pricing, and the like. The emphasis is on how to organize your work at home, overcome laziness and not lose physical and mental health.


Laziness VS Self Control


Laziness is the main enemy that lurches you in the path of the free spearman. It is worth relaxing, gape, as she already hugs you with her soft paws, whispers in your ear, ‚Äúdo not hurry, you still have time to do everything, go read the review of the new HTC iPhone ZX 8 and discuss vkontakte how you are cool last weekend‚Äù .

If you have an iron will and years of self-discipline in the background, then perhaps you will avoid this problem. Others wishing to work and earn at home will have to either cultivate the necessary qualities in themselves, or abandon freelancing.

Each has his own way to victory, but the following helped me.

There are no ‚Äúwhen I get up, then I will start‚Äù, ‚ÄúI‚Äôll finish early today, tomorrow - later.‚Äù Experimentally determine approximately 8 hours a day, in which you are most effective as an employee, get up for an alarm clock and work every day at this particular time. For me, for example, this is the interval from 8 to 15-16 hours. Then you can communicate with colleagues, discuss something, do routine work, but the brain is already working much worse.

News and contact - on schedule . A radical way - special programs that block access to certain sites at a certain time, or a complete shutdown of the Internet on a working machine. Of course, this is not always possible, and indeed it looks somehow too harsh. Instead, try setting yourself a certain time to read something interesting on the net and stick to it strictly. For example, read your favorite sites for half an hour before work, half an hour after lunch and for as long as you like - after work. When you exit the schedule - beat your hands with a ruler, preferably iron.

Cause time, fun - ... the rest of the time . There are no ‚Äúoh, a new series of The Theory of Big House‚Äù - I‚Äôll see and then work. ‚Äù You start the day with entertainment - it‚Äôs all about entertainment.

Browser . If you need a browser for work, do not use your ‚Äúwar horse‚Äù for this with a full set of notifications, bookmarks, extensions and other tinsel, where every 5 minutes something new appears and asks ‚Äúopen me, look, spend two hours ". For example, Chrome is set up for surfing and entertainment, but during the working day I try not to launch it, doing the main work in Firefox with a pair of exclusively working plugins.

Messengers . Skype, ICQ and others like them are often needed to communicate with clients and colleagues (and the more often you are available, the better), so you can hardly get rid of them completely. However, try to minimize non-working communication to the maximum. Constantly hanging status ‚Äúretired‚Äù or ‚Äúbusy‚Äù, as practice shows, saves from a significant part of anecdotes and funny pictures, but does not interfere with communicating with the right people.

Schedule

I think the situation familiar to many IT specialists - in the morning I sat down at work, had a snack in 5 minutes, went out to the toilet a couple of times, in the evening I woke up from work - ‚Äúwhere am I?‚Äù. The eyes are red, tired, the back aches, all that is below the back is numb, the desire only one thing is to fall and fall asleep. So do not do that.

  • We approach the window and for 3-5 minutes we survey the surroundings, detaining attention on the most distant objects that we can consider.

Routine of the week

Be sure to have one or two days off a week. Yes, you can work 12-15 hours 7 days a week, and even, at first, produce good results. But after some time, fatigue will still take its toll, you will begin to dream about the code, you will be doing tasks for two hours all day, and your favorite work will become hated. This time depends a lot on your youth, health, energy, natural endurance - someone will be enough for a couple of weeks, someone will otbrub a few months, but the longer this marathon lasts, the more you will need time to regain strength and interest in work. Of course, situations are different and sometimes such a regime is necessary, but in normal, not extreme conditions, it is not necessary.

And, of course, it's better to spend this weekend not at the computer. Friends, relatives, books, cinema, theater, picnic, bowling, billiards, walks ... anything and anywhere, so long as this damned piece of iron is not around you all day and night.

Perfect marker board

Whiteboard or whiteboard is almost a mandatory attribute of any self-respecting development team. With proper skill and desire, a regular board can save a lot of time and significantly improve the result of the team‚Äôs work - a fast and efficient way of communication has always been very critical and directly influenced the result of collective efforts.

But is it possible to make the instrument more pleasant? Add zest?




The author has repeatedly had the opportunity to see how quickly people understand the ideas supported by a certain number of illustrative illustrations. Curiously, even experienced developers do not always easily perceive information by ear. It is likely that information flow is overloaded and experience gained in not very quiet rooms. A pair of "squares", usually, perfectly helps to focus and tune in the desired fashion. No wonder, by the way, because for many years, starting from the first grade of high school, blackboard is an obligatory part of the cognitive process.

Moreover, the board (being, in fact, a large sheet of paper) is a primitive, but very effective "intelligence booster" ( Stanislav Lem, "The Sum of Technology" ). The author sincerely hopes the reader is deprived of acquired hatred of rectangular shapes and understands their usefulness.

So, the market is replete with a variety of marker boards, but some people (to whom the author identifies himself) are actively looking for alternatives. Admittedly, the initial motivating factor is the cost of the factory board.

We formulate the basic requirements:
  • Adequate cost
  • Durability
  • The ability to create detailed schemes
  • Contrast
  • Not markost
  • Subjective pleasure to use
  • Original view (optional)

Many use the board to create extremely coarse and primitive schemes, but sometimes, if the project under discussion is complicated and it is intended to discuss it further, it is more efficient to accurately indicate the necessary details or leave room for them.

It is for long-lived illustrations that the ability of the board to retain the pattern is important. If something can be erased - it will be erased, soiling clothes and invalidating the illustration. Agree, it is unpleasant to lose modules, subsystems or objects and find them on your clothes.

The durability of the board is very important, since standard boards with fairly intensive use come to some disrepair quickly. Formally, they are still new, but it really becomes not very pleasant to work with them.

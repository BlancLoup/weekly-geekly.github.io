Residents of the richest Chinese village built a skyscraper

An inquisitive reader will immediately ask - why such a post in such a blog? - I will immediately answer - in order to succeed - it must be achieved and all things must be completed. And this news shows what success can be achieved.

By the day of the celebration of the 50th anniversary of the Chinese village Huaxi (Huashi), the 328-meter high skyscraper was opened. The cost of building a skyscraper is estimated at 3 billion yuan (more than $ 470 million). In addition, each village family gave 10 million yuan (approximately $ 1.5 million), so the hotel is truly considered the pride of its inhabitants.


Huashi (Jiangsu Province, East China) - The richest village in China, which is known to all as the "village number 1 in the Middle Kingdom", is famous for the most economically developed village in China.

In the 1950s, Huaxi was a village with a population of 576 people. However, already in 2003, this village‚Äôs GDP exceeded $ 1.5 billion. Now 36,000 people live here. Every family has at least one house, two cars and $ 250 thousand in the bank. A 20% of the annual income of the entire village is divided equally among all the inhabitants of the village.



The economic success of Huashi village was achieved through the dynamic development of the metallurgical, textile and services industries. All enterprises of the village are equipped with the latest technology. Fabrics, clothing and other local products are exported to more than 40 countries and regions of the world, including the United States and Japan.

On the construction of a skyscraper villagers are not going to stop. Soon there will be its own fleet. The mayor of a modern Chinese village said that the village plans to acquire 20 aircraft and create its own fleet for training flights and tourism.

Microsoft refused to buy Nokia

After years of close cooperation on the basis of Windows Phone, Microsoft has virtually ceased negotiations on the purchase of the Nokia phone business. Now all discussions are almost gone, writes the Wall Street Journal.



The problem is, WSJ sources say, that Nokia was asking for too much price with weak market positions. Microsoft, as they say, paid Nokia for the transition to Windows Phone more than 1 billion dollars. Now Nokia is estimated at $ 14 billion, so buying the entire business of a Finnish company would be a rather costly enterprise for Microsoft.

And is it worth it? Surface showed that Microsoft itself can quite make high-quality hardware. Experts say that at the moment and at such a price, Microsoft is better to try to make the phone itself, than to get involved in the Nokia corporate drama.

Although sales of Lumia are beginning to grow slowly, it is impossible not to say that Windows Phone itself is partly to blame for the weak start of smartphones. And it is Microsoft, anyway, that needs to be done more than just improving the OS to compete with the iPhone and Android.

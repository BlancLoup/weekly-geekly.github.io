Opera features and release frequency

First, thanks for your feedback. More than a thousand comments followed yesterday's release and we want to tell you more about our plans.

We plan to launch three permanently operating separate Opera channels for computers. Each channel will have a greater level of stability. These will be:



  • Opera Developer: the most unstable, but with novelties
  • Opera Next: more stable, next final release
  • Opera: the finale, the most stable channel for all users

We have not yet decided exactly how often I will be releasing new builds in these channels, but let us hint that you will see new versions much, much more often than it was with the classic releases on Presto.

You do not have to worry that Opera will become a clone of something there or that we will forcefully update your Opera 12.x before we finish the work. Our mission has not changed - we want to make work on the Internet more convenient for you. We are working on creating the most convenient browser in the world. A browser that is not tied to specific version numbers, but is constantly updated and developed. Observing your reaction, I think we can count on your help and feedback.

So do not worry if something is still unstable in Opera 15 - the next Opera Next 16 will be released at about the same time as the stable Opera 15. And the gap between them will be as short as ever.

Let's talk about the possibilities.

We have a big list after yesterday. Yes, we made a list! As we already said in the comments and now we say publicly - more features will appear in future versions. For example: Opera Link, support for those, API API, wide possibilities of the tab bar - and these are just a few. Some of them are already in work or made, but are disabled in this version due to instability. Over time, the settings of the browser will become much wider. And one more thing: the Opera Dragonfly debugger is alive, but we cannot yet tell you more.

Follow the news and keep sending your feedback.

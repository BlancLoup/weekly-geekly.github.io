VimpelCom subscribers will pay 13 rubles per month for the Spring Law



After the adoption of the technical requirements for the storage of voice and Internet traffic, operators began to estimate the amount of additional costs according to the law of Spring.

According to VimpelCom, this year the additional costs are estimated at 6 billion rubles (approximately 17 rubles per subscriber per month at the rate of 58.2 million subscribers, calculated from June). Over the next five years, expenses will amount to 45 billion rubles, that is, approximately 13 rubles per subscriber per month.

It can be assumed that about an average of this amount should increase tariffs for the population, so that the company can maintain profitability at the same level. Although you need to make a discount that for certain users, tariffs will rise more significantly than for others. For example, the cost of storing Internet traffic for each operator is much higher than the cost of storing voice calls. Therefore, it is logical to assume that, first of all, tariffs for Internet users and package tariffs with megabytes included should increase.

It is possible that operators will find ways to cut costs so that the increase in tariffs does not become so significant. For example, it would be logical to set the maximum amount of consumed Internet traffic at unlimited rates - the limit for daily and monthly transmission with megabyte pay for excess traffic.

In Russia, cellular communications and the Internet are among the cheapest in the world. Probably, for this reason, the authorities believe that the operators can install the equipment at their own expense, and the increase in tariffs for users will not cause a serious blow to their welfare.

The operator must keep traffic in Russia. By agreement with the FSB, it is allowed to store traffic on ‚Äútechnical means of accumulating information‚Äù belonging to another telecom operator.

The capacity of the storage system must be equal to the volume of traffic transmitted within 30 days preceding the date of commissioning of technical means of accumulating information. The operator is obliged to annually increase the storage system capacity by 15% within five years from the date of putting the accumulation equipment into operation. Information is deleted from storage devices automatically in accordance with the algorithms provided by the software and the requirements for the information accumulation means used, based on the maximum storage period for traffic within 6 months. The document was published on April 19, 2018.

Other large operators will incur comparable costs. Thus, they get about the same amount of additional expenses per subscriber: about 13 rubles per month.

Provider top level Cogent blocks Pirate Bay and other "pirated" sites



Cogent is the largest Internet provider in the world. This company was founded in 1999 and provides services such as access to the Internet, collocation, and transport of Internet traffic. In general, about 20% of global traffic passes through the networks of this company. The other day it turned out that a number of users of various Internet service providers around the world cannot get access to well-known ‚Äúpirated‚Äù sites, such as The Pirate Bay and others. The request to the site did not go beyond the Cogent backbone network.

Backbone Internet providers, one of which is Cogent - an important part of the global Internet infrastructure. They provide global traffic from point A to point B. Accordingly, the backbone provider may decide not to pass requests to a number of sites. In this case, users of the most diverse Internet providers of lesser importance will not be able to access these sites even if the ‚Äúnative‚Äù provider does not block anything, since the Cogent network directly has more than 5,000 inclusions with various large networks around the world, including All known world virtual contents, including Russian ones.

All this fully applies to the "pirated" sites like Primewire, Movie4k, TorrentProject and TorrentButler. Cogent has been blocking user requests for these resources on its network for more than a week . The above sites work with CloudFlare IP addresses. IP - 104.31.18.30 and 104.31.19.30. Most users from all over the world have no problems with these addresses - access to them is open. But this is only if the traffic of these users does not pass through the Cogent network. In this case, access to the specified addresses is denied.

The problem has already been noticed by users in the USA and Europe. Although it cannot be called large-scale, the fact of blocking raises a number of questions. As for blocking access, Cogent is really engaged in this. The company has a traffic route check, and the picture here is clear.



Sometimes, of course, mistakes happen, so TorrentFreak journalists have requested additional information from the provider. In particular, the question was asked: "Is it true that there is a lock, and if so, what are the reasons?" A spokesman for the company said that the problem will be studied, but in general such issues and solutions are not discussed with third-party companies and journalists. However, the representative of Cogent added that his company does not control the DNS records of the above resources.

However, these sites remain inaccessible from the Cogent network, so the answer to the question of whether the company itself blocks requests for resources is simple - yes.

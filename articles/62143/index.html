OpenHebrew - Free Hebrew for iPhone

After a long wait and longing finally came on a Jewish , forgive, Hebrew street holiday. Developer Tom Zichel (◊™◊ï◊ù ◊¶◊ô◊ß◊ú) has released, as you can already guess from the title of the post, free Hebrew language support for the iPhone. Until now, only the OpenHE solution was available for completely and completely non-free money.

As you can see in the screenshots below, the added keyboard works with a bang in both horizontal and vertical modes.




It is important to note that in order to type correctly (from right to left) it is necessary to start typing (the first letter of the text) with a Hebrew letter. Otherwise, everything will work as usual, from left to right, and any typed Hebrew words will simply be inserted into the text.

Installation


The installation process is quite simple. Go to CYDIA (if you need a Hebrew, you have a hacked iPhone, right? :)), click Manage - Sources - Edit - Add and type tom.zickel.org/openhebrew . After that, a choice of two packages should appear:

- openhebrew for installing an additional keyboard with Hebrew support (but without translating the entire interface into Hebrew), and
- iVrit for naturally installing both

Personally, I do not see the need to translate the interface, but ... everything may be needed for taste and color.

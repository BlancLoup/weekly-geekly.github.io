The most foolish patent of the month. New competition from EFF

A month ago, the Electronic Frontier Foundation announced a competition for the most foolish patent of the month , issued by the US Patent Office (USPTO). 30 days have passed - and the first winner was determined!

Last December, the USPTO issued patent number 8,609,915 for a "potent medicine" that "fights cancer, cancerous cells and kills cancer." From the description it follows that the "medicine" is also effective against many other diseases. What is this wonderful invention?

The magic medicine consists of "evening primrose oil, rice, sesame, green beans, coffee, meat, cheese, milk, green tea extract, primrose seeds and wine." As stated in the patent, "it works."

There is no reason to doubt the sincerity of the person who applied for such a patent, writes EFF, but how could an expert who is reviewing the documents approve it? In theory, an expert group should refuse applications, in accordance with a variety of established grounds for refusal, including uncertainty and lack of benefit.

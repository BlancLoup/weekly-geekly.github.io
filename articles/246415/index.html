What do we have?


If such giants as Chrome and Vkontakte use Easter eggs, then it is not so bad? Of course, on such resources there are only professionally made Easter eggs without a bloody mess and undressed women. They usually organically fit into the concept of the resource itself, bring joy to those who find them and do not offend anyone. Summarizing all of the above, we can formulate a few simple tips for young Paschal-makers. So, what not to do if you do not want to get stuck:

1. Defame the reputation of the resource. Insults to the owner of the resource and various kinds of mockery of the site itself will not exactly please the authorities, and your reputation can be badly spoiled. From such things it is better to refuse at least out of respect for yourself.
2. Hide Easter eggs from the owner. Here, I think, many will disagree. Opening the cards, you, of course, reduce the level of heroism and fun, but you do not harm either yourself or the resource.
3. Turn Easter eggs into advertising. In itself, the use of easter eggs in marketing is normal, and this will be written below. But, for example, showing a banner by clicking on a hidden pixel is a bad form. People will feel cheated. Be sincere, make Easter eggs for fun.
4. Use without the need for blood guts and other trash. On some sites this is normal, on others it is completely unacceptable. Rely on common sense.
5. Overdoing with Easter eggs. Everything is good in moderation. No need to hang eight easter eggs per page. In the general case, one is enough. The entire resource is more than three - already bust.

In any case, we must remember that Easter eggs are just a tool. And how you use it - for the benefit or to the detriment of yourself and your resource is your business.

Why not play?


No one bothers to use the easter mechanism as an obvious marketing device according to all the rules of gamification (from the English gamification) - to involve visitors in the search for secret content and reward those who found it. Suppose you have an online store and on the nose the New Year holidays. You can hide secret codes in some commodity cards or even just pictures with funny deer. Who will find at least one such secret - to him a discount or a gift.



Firstly , it will increase the visitor engagement - they will revise the many pages of the site in search of Easter eggs, you see, they will buy something.
Secondly , they will attract friends to help them find hidden pictures.
Third , they will get a lot of positive, and remember the site. And you will see people who love their work and are ready to work just for the sake of an idea and imbued with confidence in you.
And fourthly , the traffic and time spent by people on the site will increase, which will positively affect the attitude of the search engines towards it.

In short, sheer good.

Easter eggs, team spirit and the right programmers


However, not all managers respond positively to Easter eggs on their resource. As our layout designer told, in his youth he left one on the site of a large car dealer, and the customer found it and gave it a stick.

In my opinion, from the customer‚Äôs side it was short-sighted. After all, if a person is so passionate about programming that he does this in his spare time and creates his own creative projects, then this is the right programmer. Moreover, the introduction of Easter eggs can have a positive impact on the whole team. This is a great tool for team building! All participants imbued with the utmost deserved respect and sympathy for the developers and learn them from a new side.

Total


Easter eggs - is it good or bad? Yes and no. It all depends on: common sense, directness of hands, the cultural level of the developer, relations with the authorities, the subject of the site and many other factors. It is a tool, and the tool is thin and unpredictable. It is not always easy to determine whether it will bring harm or benefit, or even be wasted.

The beauty of Easter eggs in their spontaneity, lightness and naturalness. They do not need to get hung up, spend a lot of time and effort and wait for the indispensable marketing effect. Why not just express yourself through Easter eggs, communicate with visitors, respect and delight them. They will appreciate and respond to you in return.

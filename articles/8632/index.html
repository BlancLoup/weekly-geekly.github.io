Interview with the creator of a new online library - FanLib.ru

Interview, taken from himself, aka self-interview, easy split personality.

Me: - Hello Dmitry, thank you for agreeing to the interview. First of all, I congratulate you on the completion of the project.

FanLib: - Hello, thank you. Actually the project is not finished yet. The library is at the stage of open beta testing, as is evident from our news feed. And this is really the beta stage, and not a tribute to the WEB2.0 projects mod - to have Beta inscription above the portal logo.

I: - Judging by the news and the forum - are you ready to pay visitors money for bugs found?

FanLib: - Not only are we ready, but we pay. About hundreds of bugs were found by the readers themselves in the open and closed beta tests phase.

Me: - Are there many bugs :)? And how ethical is it to pay money for it?

FanLib: - Any work must be paid. And how many? This is a question rather to the developers, and indeed the portal is complex. Oddly enough, the money agrees to accept no more than a third of readers who have found a bug. The rest just like to participate in the creation of the library, to see their recommendations embodied on the portal. After all, we are informed not only about bugs, but also give interesting ideas. Many of these ideas are being implemented right now, others are being put on the ToDo sheet, to work on the next version.

Me: - Do you position the library as a WEB2.0 project?

FanLib: - Yes, of course. Our portal provides a number of services to visitors, and the content of the site is mostly built by the readers themselves.

Me: - Content, is it a book?

FanLib: And books too. Important library contents are also reviews, reviews, and a forum with a chat. On books - scanning, converting and reading books, the process is long and time consuming. In order to publish updates daily, either staff is required, or the help of the readers themselves. We are planning both material gratitude for OSR'shchikov, and not material - additional services and statuses.

Me: - Can you reveal some more details?

FanLib: - For material thanks - at this stage I can not say more. Software services and status - a little talk. Active readers write on the forum, leave reviews and send books - this affects their status in the library. The size of a personal bookshelf, a shelf of books read, stature of authority grows, awards are issued, and even - the percentage of displayed advertising for a given user drops to 0.

Me: - Interesting and intriguing - bookshelf, credibility, percentage of advertising ... Can you learn more about the services provided by the library?

FanLib: - In order to tell about all the services, you will have to publish the full TZ. But about some - I can tell in the framework of the interview. Each library reader has their own personal section on the portal - a personal account. One of the sections of the LC is the Bookshelf - a service that allows you to store selected books in your personal section.

On the theory of artificial intelligence and the work of the subconscious and the conscious

For an example that will help visually demonstrate the mechanism of interaction between the subconscious and the conscious, consider phobias. Their beauty is that you can clearly say when a person is under the influence of a phobia, what he can and can not do with it, and why.

Phobia occurs as a result of short-term highly emotional experience, coupled with a threat to life. As a result of a phobia, a person receives a psychological survival strategy, which is directly recorded in the subconscious. Subsequently, if a similar situation arises (stimuli), a strategy will be reproduced that helped to avoid the threat in the case of a phobia. For example: if a person nearly drowned, he can get aquaphobia, and as a result, he will be afraid of any approach to water, even when the consciousness can already understand that there is no real danger anymore. Moreover, the person himself cannot do anything with this phobia (only after a special training session). It is also known that a phobia occurs much faster than the stimulus has time to reach the consciousness, therefore the mechanism of consciousness lies higher along the chain than the subconsciousness itself, and the consciousness works much slower.

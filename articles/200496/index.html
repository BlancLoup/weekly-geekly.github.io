Nimbus - Now with web clippers

Our new service NImbus continues to grow muscles with applications, and after the screenshots, we are ready to present our browser clippers. We will not explain long why we need clippers, since we already have similar products from Evernote or Wiznote. We will focus on the differences between our applications and the above products.





The main purpose of the Nimbus Clipper is not original - saving information from the Internet for further editing, sharing or just "not to forget"

There are three main modes of saving information in the clipper:

Clipper - save individual parts of the page. A feature of the mode is the ability to select several separate "pieces" on the page and create one note from them. A handy thing, if you want to save only what you really need, and not the whole page.

Full Page - save the entire page

Images - save only pictures. You can select the desired picture or all at once.



Also in the version for the Chrome browser is Article mode, which is still in beta mode. Similar in action to the well-known Instapaper and Readability services.

After the necessary fragment is selected, you can specify the tags of the future note, as well as the folder where it will be saved.

An important feature of our clippers is the ability to edit the saved fragments before sending to Nimbus. The preview window is also an editor, where you can remove ads, unnecessary links and unnecessary text (or add your own) from a future note.



Received notes can be immediately shared and get a direct link or edit in Nimbus Note. Already ready web interface at nimbus.everhelper.me , as well as an addon for Chrome . Soon there will be a release of the Nimbus Note application for Android and IOS, as well as a desktop version for Windows.

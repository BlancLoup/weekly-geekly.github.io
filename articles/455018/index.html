Why do small businesses work well?


In short, in a small business you are a significant unit of a single mechanism; in a large business, a useful or not very detailed part that can be replaced relatively quickly. But it is rude, much more nuances.

Less levels of management, an open and accessible manual - this is probably what determines all the other advantages. There are no complicated hierarchies, the manager is in the maximum proximity to all other employees, often he is not just a leader, but a leader, the first among equals, himself drags the work, delves into it and understands every detail in almost all duties. It seems to me that this is a normal attitude towards my own business, unless, of course, it comes to micromanagement and poking at all PCs in the mornings (at the dawn of a career, I went through such a manager - this cannot be overcome, or you agree to such a team, or go away).

It seems that the good is that the head is always there? A good, though a lot.

  • Availability in the discussion of working issues - do not need projects, presentations, coordination, waiting for five readings and one hundred meetings. You can just come up and present the idea, report a bug, ask questions. By the way, I noticed on my own: the habit of making presentations and loudly and clearly voicing them in front of the manager, as in large offices, is very difficult to get rid of - exactly until you suddenly realize that the conversation has taken a human face and does not need to keep the defense with a fence of slides on the big screen.
  • Easy resolution of conflict situations - a competent leader always turns out to be a real arbiter and gently spreads the sides in the corners, not leading to layoffs and sabotage.
  • You know the opinion of the head on working issues from the first mouth, and not filtered and refined by the deputy, deputy-tutu and deputy-tamu. Thus, the waiting time is reduced and there is more left to solve the problem or to make corrections.

Respect for employees is an important feature of a small business (I repeat, we are about IT, and not about trading companies, where people often become consumables). To attract, adapt and retain an employee is not only difficult, but also rather expensive. Making it loyal and not threatening the security of the company is a whole art. Small business understands that it is in an uneasy competitive situation, that loyal employees are a key asset, therefore it treats them as correctly as possible: it meets organizational and labor issues, looks for compromises within the work schedule, tries to raise an employee and keep him as long as possible. If you manage to create such a team, or at least the backbone of the team, success becomes closer. And in a successful company, the employees themselves are successful.

Comprehensive inclusion in the project, an understanding of business and processes in general - the most favorite feature of small business. More I like only the foreign definition, which translates as ‚Äútry on a lot of hats‚Äù. Working in a small business, an employee inevitably interacts with the project in the complex, and not with its separate piece. Relatively speaking, in a large IT company you sit and saw your part of the code, sometimes without even knowing its real fate after a commit, and in a small business you can write code for different tasks, participate in implementation, support clients on complex technical issues and .d As a bonus, employees are often included in the entire business process, discussing design, interface, usability, etc. - that is, they see all the facets of a software project and the process of its implementation. This is great pumping the overall value of you as a professional and noticeably adds weight to the labor market.

Easy horizontal movement . In the case of professional burnout, loss of interest, or even additional education, you can easily move to another position: from salespeople to testers, from testing engineers - and developers, from those. sales support and so on. Such movements help you firmly and thoroughly master your business from different angles, making you a universal specialist. But this is not a super value - the super value is that such movements help to make the product or service better: having been in the shoes of a sales person, you will be closer to the client and understand what the interface really should be or what the installation procedure should be ( here I can't help but sneak off and say hello to CRM, who send 12 pages of installation instructions, while our Region Soft CRM takes a few clicks - well, I‚Äôm a PR man or where! ).

Rapid changes are an interesting and important advantage for a career small business. Scaling changes to a small company is easier; it is easier to try out something new, for example, a technology stack or a methodology for working with clients. In large companies, the introduction of something experimental stalls on the first round of approvals.

In general, the main difference between a small and medium business from a large one is often the role of a leader: in a large business, it is often an appointed person who treats the company like any other employee, and in small and medium size it is the owner, a person who is vitally interested in the company lived, developed and flourished (well, if it is without deviations and mania). And this attitude is transmitted to all employees.

Why do small businesses work well?


It is good to work with small business - he is responsible for his work, he doesn‚Äôt draw up approvals, he has a huge and rather compact experience (not spread out on 150 employees), he appreciates clients.

Small business is closer to customers, he knows them better - both in terms of typical problems and ‚Äúpains‚Äù, and from the point of view of analytics. For small businesses, not only the segment is important, but also the client as a personified entity. Accordingly, the dialogue is not in the language of bureaucracy, but in the language of living business people. True, there is no way without TK - but development is an exact discipline, some formulations should be reliably fixed. By the way, it is the small and medium business that implements a CRM system or a business intelligence system not for beauty or rollback, but specifically for the work, analysis, storage and systematization of the client base. It helps to work with clients quickly and efficiently.

The speed of work and implementation of projects , oddly enough, in small companies is higher. It seems to be less resources, subcontractors, and partners - and the deals are closed one after the other, projects are being implemented, everything is parallelized and literally burning in the hands. This is largely due again to the shallow hierarchy and high decision-making speed - the responsible person is closest to the project as closely as possible, and often the right to make independent decisions is delegated to him.

Flexibility and survival in the market , alas, is not always about small business. When the economy storms, it is harder to survive, because the total outflow of customers can reach a critical mass. But, on the other hand, with proper management of staff and in the absence of throwing money out of the window, small businesses are more likely to survive and even avoid cuts because the company can quickly restructure, radically change the marketing strategy or even develop a new product. In any case, this is definitely not the case when employees will wait for a crisis with folded arms and fingers crossed.

Controllability and transparency of processes is a very strong advantage of SMB. No matter how difficult the process (and in small business they are), a truncated hierarchy significantly increases the responsibility of the holders of each link and allows the process to go quickly, productively and without serious hang-ups (such as lost contracts, perpetual documents to be negotiated or forgotten customers).

And I will do my business!


I honestly tried to open a small trading business, but this is not an IT or Habrow story from 2006-2007. Three approaches - and three failures, although we managed to create an LLC with ‚Äúpartners‚Äù (fellow students), to make authorized capital, to get the first profit ... As soon as you become an entrepreneur, you cease to belong to yourself and your habits, as you take the first person - you bear a mountain of responsibility. When you fire him first, you don't sleep for a week. We did not go further.


  • Business is not security. Yes, it seems to you that they will no longer dismiss you, will not be deprived of bonuses, will not punish and do not roll on the carpet for a mistake. That's right - you will do all this with yourself, because in the beginning your business is worse than any dismissal, you actually live in a panic.   • Business is not a Bentley with a trunk of money. This is a minus the trunk of money, which will have to invest in the start, hiring, tools, idea, rent, etc. - depends on the configuration of the business. And at some point you will understand that this money has also been taken away from the family. In my case of partnership with fellow students, this resulted in debts and begging from the parents of all three unfortunate entrepreneurs.

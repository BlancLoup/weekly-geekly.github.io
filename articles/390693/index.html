Fitti Guard tracker will report a dangerous level of air pollution



Wearable electronics - a sphere that is just beginning to change qualitatively. Recently, the German company Active introduced a new development - fitness tracker Fitti Guard. In addition to standard features such as tracking steps, calories and monitoring sleep quality, the device can offer its owner something else. And no, this is not a heart rate monitor and not a GPS - Fitti Guard also has such functions, and they are already standard too.

The new thing we are talking about is the measurement of the level of gamma radiation, the measurement of the level of UV radiation, the determination of the level of noise and air quality. Thanks to all these sensors, the device can tell where it is better to avoid the sun, and from where it is necessary to run, in order not to breathe polluted air.



Fitti Guard is also quite capable of measuring the level of humidity or the level of noise to determine if your bedroom is suitable for sleeping, or if you need to think about its sound insulation and the purchase of a humidifier. And this is just one of the ways to use the device. As for the quality of sleep, an accelerometer is used to determine the activity of the movement of users during sleep - about the same way monitor Fitbit Charge HR and Jawbone UP2 sleep.

Other interesting features are the automatic determination of the type of activity - golf, basketball, tennis.

In offline mode, the tracker can work for 21 days, after which the device battery must be recharged. However, if you use all the functions simultaneously, the battery life of the device may decrease.

The gadget should appear on Kickstarter, it will happen in March 2016. Website of the manufacturer here , it can track the stages of development.

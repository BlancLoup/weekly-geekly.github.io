How much does a site build: reboot

I stumbled on the other day on an article by Igor Sukhinin about the cost of web development. And already for several days the topic touched by the author jumped, jumped and made faces, without letting itself be forgotten. And all this for the sake of me finally remembering the disk already dusty on the shelf.

On the disk, one St. Petersburg company (someone, for sure, a friend - Yumisoft) shares his thoughts on the present and the future of the site-building market. Riding through the cities and villages, the guys were amazed: in the regions, web development is in full ... decadence. They scratched their turnips and decided to figure out what was happening and how much.

So, here I will tell only about the most significant, in my opinion, points of research and answer the question indicated in the topic. I would be glad if you share your thoughts.

And in order for my article not to become subjective, and in order to back up or refute the research with real data, I asked my friend from Cheboksary, Pavel Nikiforov, to comment on my conclusions (he has been working as a freelancer for over 7 years, and for the last 2 years directly makes websites by opening its small design studio ‚ÄúBAS‚Äù ).

With freelancers in the regions of Russia, the situation is even worse - 49% of them create websites at prices ranging from 10 to 30 thousand rubles, and 39% do up to 10,000 rubles. In the capitals, the cost of similar work is 2-3 times higher.


- Pasha, how much can you disprove or confirm these figures?

CMS and web development


The following part of the research is much more curious. It turns out that the share of CMS in the cost of services for creating a website ranges from 30% to 50%. At the same time, the balanced cost of the CMS as a tool should not exceed 20-30% of the cost of the entire service. Moreover, now regional developers earn not on their services, but on the resale of the CMS itself. They buy it with an affiliate discount of 50%, and sell at an initial price. Their earnings are the remaining difference. I agree with the authors of the study: in such a situation, business becomes a dead end. The developer is strictly tied to the CMS brand, he does not create added value due to the provision of his services, which means that he does not have opportunities to develop his own business. In this situation, he has no opportunity to improve the quality of his services.


- What do you think about these numbers? What is the cost of CMS in your work?

SpaceX told how and where it is planned to plant the used steps Falcon Heavy



About a week ago on Geektimes information about what plans SpaceX had for this year was published . In particular, Ilon Mask said that after a few months the company is going to carry out a trial launch of the Falcon Heavy super-heavy rocket. The payload of the new launch vehicle is 54.4 tons. It is planned that Falcon Heavy will deliver the cargo of such a mass to a low reference orbit. With its help, you can send 21.2 tons of payload to a geo-transfer orbit and about 13 tons of cargo to Mars. Now the Falcon Heavy assembly is coming to an end.

Specialists of the company estimate the cost of launching this launch vehicle to be approximately 90 million US dollars. Details of the trial launch of the rocket, however, the company did not disclose. Recently, a little more information appeared on which SpaceX roadmap for the rocket is provided for. In particular, the company, in order to receive approval from the federal authorities for the launch, is conducting an environmental impact assessment of launch sites. In addition to the existing, SpaceX is preparing two more platforms.



As part of the assessment, SpaceX studies the noise level at the start of all three stages for the Falcon Heavy, plus a number of other factors. According to the results of the preparation of launch sites, the company prepared an open report. This document states that the first starts of steps will begin in early spring of this year. And SpaceX plans to land all three stages. The company already has the experience of successful (and not so much) return of the first stages of launch vehicles. Therefore, now the team involved in the preparation and implementation of launches is going to try to return all three stages to the Earth intact at the same time.

The fact is that Falcon Heavy is planned to be launched precisely with the intention of returning the specified rocket design elements to Earth.

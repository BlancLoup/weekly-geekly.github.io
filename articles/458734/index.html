Two times "ku"


According to the requirements of PCI DSS, you need to be able to visually distinguish the roles of people in the data center. Without looking at the pass and reading something there, but directly visually, as the military distinguish each other by epaulettes, only brighter. We decided not to show off and used the good old chatlansky method - color differentiation of pants. Specifically, they began to make ribbons of different colors. Green as your favorite immediately took our admins.

It sounds simple, but it caused three unexpected effects:

  1. Retractors were needed to automatically retract these passes while wearing (these are the things that regulate the length of the tape themselves). We wrote a ToR, where we took into account all the wishes of all departments. It was a strategic mistake. Color, format, material, the retractor is not plastic, metal fishing line to put the logo so that it is sewn into the tape. The pieces turned out so expensive that they had to cut the requirements and change the format.
  2. When the differentiation of pants earned, it became very convenient. The contractors have some tapes, the external administrators have others, our administrators have the third. It is clear who has what role. In electrics - only gray, in air conditioning - blue. And here we needed ribbons for drivers (this is a separate role, they can enter the unloading zone, but they cannot leave it except outside). In this case, drivers do not need passes. At first we gave them ribbons without gaps. Then the guards decided that it was very strange and degraded the human dignity of drivers. They have their own military logic, so now the drivers come to get a pass immediately with the tape, but this pass does not allow anywhere. From the point of view of security, a marker is obtained that the security guard of this person has checked.
  3. One of our engineers suggested making green sweaters instead of ribbons. And sent an innovation proposal. They did it halfway: they left the passes with the ribbon, plus they really did sew green uniform sweaters. Now we have the admin uniform. The security officers supported the joke and added it to the regulations. Now she is obligatory (pants, shirt, sweater, but the sweater can be removed).

Critical Incidents


From the serious - there was a situation when all the chillers were cut off at once. Our chillers are not protected by UPS, because we believe in physics, and as a reserve for the temperature we have a pool with chilled water. If something turns off, you do not need batteries to power the chillers that cool the water, but simply the cold water itself, which is already ready. Convenient and simple, but there is a nuance. Chillers are equipped with protective automatics, which in case of hazardous parameters of the electrical network disconnects them. If the input is disabled, we turn on the diesel generator set, and then the chillers are powered by them. Everything would be fine if we did not live in Russia. We had network drawdowns many times, but everything was fine. But once the jump was sharp first down, then sharply up, then down again - in a few seconds, the input parameters changed about 4 times.

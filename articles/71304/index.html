Ceatec 2009

Ceatec , the largest electronics and gadget show , will be held next week in Japan and will bring to our attention many new developments.

Toshiba will present at the exhibition the first LCD TV, which includes a multimedia processor Cell . The first presentation of the concept of this TV was held last year. This model will be very interesting to the owners of Sony PlayStation 3 game consoles - it complements the console with features such as real-time scaling and processing of recorded video.



Panasonic will also show its accomplishment in television technology and present a 50-inch plasma TV that can play an image in 3D . At the IFA Electronics Show , in September, the company announced that it plans to begin mass production of such TVs as early as next year.

Sony will also be presenting a new 3D product at Ceatec . This will be a video camera capable of recording video in 3D format using a single lens. The camera is more targeted at studios than consumers, but as Sony says, in the future, mass production and compact cameras available to everyone will be included.

In the field of mobile technology, NTT DoCoMo plans to show a new mobile phone with a wooden case. The prototype of this phone was developed in conjunction with the company Olympus , which was engaged in the creation of a wooden case, the basis for which will be cypress. Also, DoCoMo and its partners will demonstrate their success in the field of mobile technologies and show the platform of the future of wireless services - LTE ( Long Term Evolution ). DoCoMo , Panasonic , NEC and Fujitsu are developing a phone capable of downloading files at speeds up to 100Mb / s and downloading them at 50Mbps .

One of Ceatec 's hits last year - the Murata robot will be presented in a new concept with new features and new design. Note - the latest version of this robot is already able to ride a bike 3 times faster than the previous version.

Nissan will also be presented at Ceatec and will demonstrate some of its achievements in the field of automotive technology. In particular, the company plans to demonstrate a technology that is capable of maneuvering just behind another car along the road, which will allow it to drive car convoys with little or no driver participation .

Having visited Ceatec , one can predict with sufficient accuracy the directions that the Japanese electronic industry will take in the coming years. Visitors to Ceatec Japan have the opportunity to get acquainted with the latest developments in display technology for portable electronic devices. The program Ceatec Japan will be divided into exhibitions and conferences.

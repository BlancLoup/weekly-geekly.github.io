In Tomsk, create a snake robot to save people

Students of TUSUR (Tomsk State University of Control Systems and Radio Electronics) assembled a robot imitating the movement of a snake. According to Tomsk developers, in contrast to the "walking" or "wheeled" robots, an artificial snake is easier to penetrate under the rubble or crawl along sewer pipes. And if you equip it with infrared radiation and motion sensors, she will be able to determine if there is a person or an animal behind the barrier, which will make the rescuers' work easier.

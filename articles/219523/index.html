Your best friend, anonymous



So, you just launched your startup. He is cool, everyone needs it, solves the most pressing problems of the potential audience (well, you think so), looks stunning and super-easy to use.

It takes a day, a week, a month, three ... Yes, attendance is not bad. Yes, the user base is growing. But I want more. How to understand what your users are missing? What are their service needs not covered? What is inconvenient to do? And on the contrary, what is done is great, and you should not change it in any way?

The best thing you can do right now is to allow anonymous feedback (questions, ideas, bug reports) from users.



Nothing replaces the feedback of a living person from working with your application. Web analytics and a / b testing are powerful tools, but they begin to work in full force only on a large user base. While it is not there - feedback plays a major role.

Okay, user opinion is great. But why anonymously? The fact is that (surprise) people are not at all eager to write something to you. If the service came to taste - they begin to use it, no - just close the page.

If a person wants to write about his experience of communicating with your site, it means that he was seriously "hooked" on something, and it is better to listen to him. Therefore, it would be good to simplify the way for the user from intention to action: if you want to leave a review, just write it and click on ‚ÄúSend‚Äù. Everything.

For example: the web application I am working on now, 70% of reviews are from anonymous users. If anonymous messages were banned, this feedback simply would not exist.

Deal with motivation. Now to the technical part. Traditionally, specialized services are used to collect ideas, questions and bug reports from users. The most famous - Get Satisfaction , of the most common in Russia - UserEcho , Reformal and Copiny .

Thirst for profit.

Prologue

What do PC and Java gaming platforms have in common? At first glance - a rather complicated question. Different target audiences - if the PC games are people not only of the youngest, but also of the middle and older ages, then in mobile games these are mostly children - from 12 to 17 years old. Few can disagree. Of course, there are separate instances for which Java is of interest even after the transitional age, but, recognize that there are very few of them, and they cannot affect the overall percentage. The next category of comparison of the two platforms is their technical characteristics. And here PC bypasses Java with a huge margin. Mobile phones have only now begun to gradually approach computer games 10 years ago. The scale of development companies is also incomparable - rarely more than sixty people in the office of a team developing a mobile toy. The exception, of course, are such giants as Gameloft , HangyGames and others. But even they cannot be compared with Bethesda , Rockstar , EA ... So what can they even have in common? My answer is the thirst for profit.
Unfortunately, almost everything in our world is done with money and for their own sake. Most of the actions of any person have a financial background, if you dig a little deeper than the surface layer. I will give a few banal examples from the same area - mobile games. Recall HeroCraft and their arcade game ‚ÄúMu-mu: Doomsday‚Äù . Most of the players this project is not hooked. A month after the start of sales, together with our magazine, the developer company launched the contest ‚ÄúMu-mu: Tournament Day‚Äù . Anyone could download a special free version of the game and try to score the maximum number of points. The winner received a decent amount to your account. Of course, this marketing move raised an interest in their creation, many, having downloaded this very ‚Äútournament‚Äù version, were eager to familiarize themselves with the full game. The number of such applicants very quickly paid for all the costs associated with the competition. But this is a normal case, because in this case, as they say, "both the sheep are fed and the wolves are safe." Let's jump in time a little bit more. Recall Qplaze with their STALKER Mobile . How many promises, beautiful screenshots and videos - all the gaming community was looking forward to the day the sales began. And what? In actual fact, this turned out to be a thoughtless shooting range, with terrible graphics and crooked gameplay. The question is, why was it necessary to breed such a PR company? And I already gave the answer. Above. For the sake of profit.
With this, I will finish the slightly prolonged prologue and proceed directly to the main action. Our hero is a Ukrainian developer of Net Lizard .

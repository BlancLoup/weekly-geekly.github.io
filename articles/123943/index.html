Google+ account lockout continues

I recently read about blocking accounts of people under 18 years old when trying to register with google +. Today we are faced with another feature when registering with this new social network, which must also be taken into account if you do not want your account to be blocked.


Responsibly choose a name.

Prehistory


Oh, how many articles have already been written on the topic of the expediency of reducing the number of required fields in the registration form. The same is written about how people fill in similar fields with all sorts of special characters or foreign phrases, just because these fields must be filled. It happened this time.

Half a year ago, after another problem with The Bat !, I advised my girlfriend to take a closer look at gmail. She really liked google mail and she decided to register. In the profile it was necessary to enter the name and surname. In order not to reveal her real surname, my friend decided to enter just the symbol * . In sum, the combination of first and last names is Sasha * . Google is quite satisfied, and even more so. And the friend joyfully began to comprehend all the possibilities of gmail.

Recently, I finally received an invite to a new brainchild of the corporation of good: google + (special thanks for the invites to the tunelix tunelix and greenhedgehog ) and I began a thorough study.

In general, I have many friends who are far from the IT community, but who are actively interested in modern technology. Therefore, we really liked the development of google "+1" and its integration into search results. The idea is remarkable: to somehow highlight in the search results sites that your friends liked. For example, if you are looking for information about "OOP", then you will surely choose a site that your fellow programmer liked. It was interesting to learn how google selects such "approved" sites. We decided to check out by experience.

So, we launched the English version of google (in the Russian version, the +1 system is not yet integrated into the search results, at least here), entered a phrase of interest and saw the familiar +1 icon. Noted several sites.

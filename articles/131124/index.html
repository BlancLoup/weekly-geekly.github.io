Google due to earthquake launched a site for finding people for Turkey



Because of yesterday's devastating earthquake in Turkey, Google launched a tool to search for people in Person Finder to help earthquake victims find their missing loved ones.

Developed after the earthquake in Haiti last year, the Person Finder tool has since been used after several other disasters, including the tsunami in Japan earlier this year.

The idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe Turkish initiative of the company is still very simple: users can enter information about the person they are looking for, or add any information they know about people who have not yet been taken into account. Of course, all added records remain available for public search and viewing.

This Google.org charity fund project collected 55,100 records of missing people after the earthquake in Haiti, 11,600 records after the earthquake in New Zealand and more than 600,000 after the disaster in Japan.

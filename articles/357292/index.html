For the Russian hacker Evgeny Bogachev, the FBI has appointed an award of $ 3 million



Network technologies continue to evolve, with them improving methods of hacking and information theft. Thousands of people are engaged in the development of malicious software, but only a few reach such heights in this area as Yevgeny Bogachev. For the FBI, this is cybercriminal # 1 , a reward of $ 3 million has been awarded for his capture. This is the biggest reward that the FBI has ever been offered for issuing information about the whereabouts of the cybercriminator or the attacker himself.

The FBI accuses Bogachev, who lives in Russia, of infecting computers and computer networks of individuals and companies with malicious software. The goal is to steal from the bank accounts of victims of thousands or even millions of dollars. Bureau agents believe that for Bogachev there is no difference in who to rob - government organizations or national minorities. If there is money somewhere, Bogachev will come after them.

He is also accused of having influenced the course of presidential elections in the United States along with a group of other "colleagues". Against the alleged perpetrators, the previous president of this country, Barack Obama, imposed sanctions. Perhaps Evgeny himself did not participate in the hacking of the servers of the Democratic Party of the United States, according to law enforcement officers of this country. But in the process of hacking exploits created by him and other software tools were used.

New York Times journalists claim that Bogachev has access to data stored on millions of PCs. These are home systems with photos from vacations, computers of various enterprises, with secret data stored on disks, servers of government organizations. Representatives of the said media say that they have almost no doubt that Bogachev infected computers that belong to various government organizations in different countries. The software tools developed by Bogachyov, according to NYT representatives, are an excellent opportunity for Russian special services to gain access to information from government and commercial computer systems of companies and organizations in other countries. The cooperation here, according to the Americans, is mutually beneficial: cybercriminal No. 1 receives money, and Russian special services receive classified information.

Information security experts at Fox-IT claim that the intelligence services of the Russian Federation are primarily interested in data on the situation in Ukraine and the war in Syria. But there are attempts to obtain classified information from government computers in the United States.

Bogachev, according to the FBI, became a large-scale figure in the world of cybercriminals more than a decade ago, when he and his ‚Äúcolleagues‚Äù developed the evil ZeuS. This software appeared in 2006 (according to some data - 2007). It is a sample of software designed to commit unlawful acts. First and foremost, the goal of Zeus is theft of user credentials that are used to conduct financial transactions. In 2012, analysts claimed that Zeus was responsible for 90% of fraudulent transactions in the world.

Zeus infects computers, making botnets of them. Moreover, Zeus is not one huge botnet, but several hundreds or even thousands of small and medium infected systems. The fact is that the creators of Zeus sell to anyone who has money and who is willing to spend it on the acquisition of such specialized software. This system consists of the builder and the administrative panel. The bot's executable code is created by the builder, containing the main module and the configuration file with the address of the control center, paths to scripts, and other important data that are required for the virus to work.

This system is perfectly protected from detection, which allowed Zeus operators to bypass the most advanced protection systems in banks, infecting all new computers and computer networks around the world. The FBI claims that at the time of its formation Zeus represented the most advanced of all cyber-systems of money theft, which could not be destroyed for several years.

Americans believe that in several years Bogachev had huge funds that he spent on the acquisition of real estate in different countries, expensive cars and much more. Allegedly, he even had three passports for different names, which gave the cybercriminal the opportunity to travel without any problems. At the peak of Zeus activity, Bogachev had between 500,000 and 1 million infected computers at his disposal.

It is also known about Bogachev that in his work he used the nicknames slavik, lucky12345, pollingsoon. By the way, it was slavik who was the operator of the first version of Zeus. Keith Mularski, an FBI official, claims that Bogachev behaves like a paranoid in his work, not trusting anyone.

In 2014, the United States, with the help of ten other states, conducted Operation Commodity, which allowed law enforcement to stop the spread of Zeus and clean thousands of computers from the malware. It was then, Moularsky claims, that Bogachev‚Äôs guilt was proven in committing a number of cybercrime, including stealing information and money by using the capabilities of malicious software.

But in Russia, Bogachev is not recognized as guilty. There is no agreement between the Russian Federation and the United States on the mutual extradition of criminals and Russia is not going to extradite Bogachev to the United States. Austin Berglas (Austin Berglas), one of the FBI employees who participated in the investigation into the case of the creators of Zeus, said that he was sure that Bogachev would cooperate with the Russian special services. This, he believes, enables the cybercriminal to remain in the country and successfully avoid punishment for many years. According to NYT journalists, the FBI firmly believe that Russian intelligence services use malicious software developed by cybercriminals in order to obtain secret information about organizations and companies in different countries. The interesting thing is that in the spyware storage, used by the CIA , were programs developed in Russia.

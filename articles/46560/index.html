There was also a task for random sampling. The task is classic. I rephrase it somewhat. But the essence does not change.
So ... There are 1000 identical boxes in stock. In the boxes are mixed nails and screws. Screws are 10 times more expensive than nails. How much can you sell a product?
Solution one (Bad) Take the first 10 boxes. Sort, evaluate, extrapolate the result to all boxes. What a bad decision. We do not know the law for which screws and nails are distributed. With the time of filling the warehouse, the number of screws may decrease.
The solution is the second (classical). We take a random sample of 10 boxes, then the algorithm. Than this decision is not perfect. Random numbers are random and may accidentally lie in a handful.) Iron tables in an interesting way. All of you (especially designers) could hear about the ‚Äúgolden section.‚Äù [Golden is the section where the ratio of the length of a segment to its greater part is equal to its ratio to the smaller one]. If the length of the segment = 1, then the length of the greater part w = 0.618033 ..., Irratic number. Therefore, in the sequence 1 * w, 2 * w, ..., 10000 * w there are no integers, that is, each number consists of an integer and a fractional part. By arranging our sample in ascending order of the fractional part, we get an interesting sample of numbers from 1 to 10,000.

25 books on cloud computing



/ photo by Olli Henze CC

Today, we at IT-GRAD decided to move away from the usual digest format and prepared for you a selection of literature materials on the subject of cloud computing, IaaS and virtualization.

Guide: How to calculate the benefits of migration to the "cloud"
This material deals with working models for calculating the economic efficiency of the transition to the cloud. With examples.

IaaS for business by brick
The book is intended for those who first encounter the clouds and the concept of IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service), and is designed for a business audience who wants to understand the latest trends in the field of IT. After reading this book, you will find answers to the following questions: What IT services does it make sense to outsource? What services are best left inside? How to choose a cloud service provider and ensure its reliability?

Perfect architecture. Leading experts on the beauty of software architectures
What components build robust, elegant, flexible, maintainable software architectures? The book answers this question with a series of essays written by leading software architects and designers of modern times. In each essay, the authors present some outstanding software architecture, analyze its differences from other architectures, and explain why it is ideal for its purpose.

Cloud computing
The author of the book assesses the feasibility of transferring existing applications to the cloud environment both from a technical and economic point of view, considers the advantages of cloud infrastructure in the field of application scaling, etc.

OpenStack. Practical familiarity with the cloud operating system
The book describes the main services of the cloud operating system - OpenStack. In addition to theoretical knowledge, the book contains many practical exercises, thanks to which the reader will be able to deploy his own stand to test the capabilities of the cloud.

Transitioning the Enterprise to the Cloud: A Business Approach
The book addresses the cost of moving to the cloud, as well as the associated risks and difficulties.

Architecting Designs for Cloud Computing Service Models
The book will help you make one of the most difficult technological decisions: it is a professional guide on choosing a cloud service model for your company based on technological and business requirements.

Cloud Computing Explained: Implementation Handbook for Enterprises
The book describes the main pros and cons of using cloud computing. The author takes the reader through all stages of the adaptation of cloud technology: from cost estimation to implementation.

Enterprise Cloud Computing: A Strategy Guide for Business and Technology Leaders
In this book, company executives will find "powerful" models of business processes, as well as strategic advice and guidance on the use of cloud technologies.

Cloud Security and Privacy: An Enterprise Perspective on Risks and Compliance
This book will show you the risks of trusting your data to the cloud, as well as tell you what you can do to secure your virtual infrastructure and web applications. It contains tips from tech-security gurus.

Cloud Computing: Concepts, Technology & Architecture
The book for beginners, which is widely covered by the "cloud" theme: SaaS, PaaS and IaaS. It also contains templates and formulas for calculating the SLA and QoS values.

Service Support
The book contains best practices for maintaining and delivering IT services.

Infrastructure as a Service 36 Success Secrets
Here you will find answers to 36 main questions about infrastructure as a service. Concrete solutions are offered with tips that have never been published anywhere before.

Cloud Computing: From Beginning to End
The book reveals not only the details of the work of public and private clouds, but also the details of the mechanism for migrating existing applications to the cloud.

Cloud Computing For Dummies
The team of experts has created a simple to learn material on cloud computing.

Virtualization Essentials
Virtualization is one of the hottest IT trends in recent years. This book introduces you to the basics of virtualization using the learn-by-doing approach.

Data Center Virtualization Fundamentals: Understanding Techniques and Highly Efficient Data Centers
In this book, you will learn how virtualization can transform the network infrastructure of a traditional data center. Here are the key characteristics of each virtualization technology.

SDN and NFV Simplified
Visual aids on software-defined networks and virtualization of network functions: basic concepts, key technologies, business drivers.

Virtualization Security: Protecting Virtualized Environments
In this article you will find tips to improve disaster recovery. Also read about the positive effects of VDI on security.

Converged Infrastructure 40 Success Secrets
Here you will find the most complete and detailed answers to 40 questions related to convergent infrastructure.

Mastering VMware vSphere 6
The book acts as a guide with step-by-step instructions for installing, configuring, and working with the VMware vSphere 6 virtualization solution. Written by VMware certified experts. Contains examples.

Big Data In Practice: How 45 Successful Companies Used
Learn how predictive analysis helps Amazon, Target, John Deere, and Apple companies. Read how big data changes medicine, science, fashion, hospitality and banking.

Cloud Computing: Business Trends and Technologies
The book is a kind of textbook on cloud computing, and it covers the following topics: virtualization, cloud security, networks, work with data, etc.

The Docker Book: Containerization is the new virtualization
The book is for system administrators, developers, and DevOps engineers who are interested in working with Docker containers. It describes the whole process of working with Docker: from installation to sweep and control.

Key Element Guide ITIL Service Design
Here is a set of processes required to ensure high quality IT services.

The average consumption of traffic per mobile phone is 230 MB per month

American cellular subscribers consume an average of 230 MB of traffic per month, according to Chetan Sharma statistics for Q2. 2010, which brings the US to first place in the world in this indicator. The consumption of traffic over the past six months has increased by 50% and it is not surprising that AT & T and other operators began to cancel unlimited tariffs in turn. Previously, the same iPhone owner received anlim for $ 30 per month, and now it pays $ 15 for 200 MB and $ 25 for 2 GB .

Obviously, traffic through mobile operators will continue to grow at the same pace, because more and more subscribers are buying smartphones. For example, in the US, among all subscribers, 31% have already purchased smartphones.



Operators' revenues from voice traffic are falling, so they are ready to completely turn into normal ISPs. It is assumed that the income from data transmission will exceed the income from voice traffic in about Q2. year 2013.

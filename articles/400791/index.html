Brief history of foil


We all know the metal "paper" is widespread after the opening of the method of "roll" of aluminum, although thin metal sheets are made of steel, tin, silver, gold. The first foil in the history (in most countries, the generally accepted definition of foil is the value of sheet metal thickness up to 0.2 mm) was made from a thin tin sheet in the second half of the 19th century and was more rigid (compared to aluminum). The first audio recordings on the phonograph cylinders were made on tin foil.

A foil of refined aluminum appeared in 1903, and for packaging products it began to be used in 1910. By 1930, in Europe and the USA, foil was widely distributed as a universal means of packaging ready-made food. After World War II, technical aluminum foil began to be actively used in industry, from the production of heating systems to the creation of space satellites (the foil from the purest aluminum was used on the Echo-1 satellite).

For some spacecraft, gold foil is used - unlike copper and silver, gold does not corrode and is easier to work with it than with aluminum. Foil and satellite dish


If you are not reflected in any mirror, try to make your own reflective surface. Not really trying, you can make a good heater from a plate, paint, foil and glue.


About the same technology, but with great diligence, it will turn out to make a heating pad and almost a real mirror.


It is important to consider that for the experience chosen the thinnest foil. The main condition is that the foil should be pressed down tightly so that no air bubbles remain under it. You can use not only the basis of a satellite dish, but any non-standard object.

If you do not have a satellite dish at hand, you can make it yourself. An antenna is simply a sheet of metal, made in the form of a concave parabola. Due to the curvature of the surface, the signal is reflected and focused on the receiver. Foil is an excellent reflector and therefore it is used to receive a signal. In addition to the foil, you will need an umbrella, a copper antenna cable, a beer can and an amplifier with a power supply. The entire inside of the umbrella is sheathed with foil; an oval plate cut from a can is attached to the handle of the umbrella. The core of the antenna cable is soldered to the plate, after which the compound is closed with clay.

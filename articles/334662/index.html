Zimbra - we build communications in the company

Internal communications in the company are extremely important, and their organization is a separate problem of the IT department. Without communications, it is impossible to exchange reliable information in a timely manner and, therefore, to support the workflow. Zimbra completely covers the needs of employees of any information exchange company.


There are two types of internal communications: with vertical and with horizontal connections.

Vertical communications are established between managers and subordinates and are used to transfer decisions, formulate tasks and monitor their execution, information interaction and receive reports. In turn, vertical communications are of two types and depend on the management style and traditions of the company:

  • ‚ÄúTop down‚Äù (from head to subordinates);
  • "From the bottom up" (from subordinates to the head).

Horizontal communications are used for the exchange of information between company departments and employees. They are necessary primarily for the normal functioning of internal business processes and should be regulated in detail. Untimely or incomplete receipt of information, distortion and even loss of some data, as well as the lack of feedback, can lead to inconsistency of actions between departments. Therefore, horizontal communication between employees is no less important than vertical.

Internal communications usually depend on the stage of development of the company. At the stage of creation, when the number of employees is small and everyone knows each other, there are no problems with communications. Enough for normal email and messenger. As the company grows, new divisions are created, employees are hired, and there is a need to think over business processes and establish horizontal links between divisions. At the same time, it becomes obvious that the communication channels in each department can be used completely different. There is an inconsistency and work begins to slow down. There is an awareness that the company needs unified mail, internal chats and file sharing services. In this case, all corporate communication channels must be reliably protected, and the company management could control them.

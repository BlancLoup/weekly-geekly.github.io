I want to share my development for a wall mount tablet.
There is a need for the introduction of a wall terminal "smart" home. A suitable tablet was found, and two problems arose: fastening the tablet to the wall and its power. Ready-made solutions did not fit for several reasons, firstly they hurt expensively, secondly their design provided a large distance from the wall, which I didn‚Äôt like painfully, I wanted to be placed as close as possible to the wall. Thirdly, I did not find anything suitable in the city and I would have to order the mount from China. Although maybe I was just looking bad. As a result, it was decided to make the bracket yourself.

Since the tablet was supposed to be used not only to display information, but also as an IP camera using the front camera of the tablet, it was necessary to provide the required angle in the mounting design so that the camera looked ‚Äúwhere it should be‚Äù.
Foamed polypropylene with a thickness of 3 mm was chosen as the material for manufacturing. It consists of two parts, the first is attached to the wall with screws, the tablet itself is attached to the second. Fastening the tablet to the bracket borrowed from the cover, bought in China. It is made of four paper clips and a panty gum. The two halves are connected through a groove-spike connection installed at the required angle (the required angle is established during development :-)). Fastened securely, not noticeable, and can always be disassembled. Initially it was supposed to make a rotary structure with a servo drive, but it was decided to first make a regular mount for testing, and as they say, nothing more permanent than a temporary one.

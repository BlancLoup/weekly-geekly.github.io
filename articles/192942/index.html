Cocktails on the day of the programmer

Hello, Habr!

There are only a few days left until the day of the programmer and, of course, on this 13th Friday , many of the users of Habr will celebrate this holiday. I will celebrate and.

On the occasion of a professional holiday, I would like to share with Habravchana a story about my small culinary project, which I managed to realize just the day before. I think the name of the post has already become clear that this is a cocktail for the programmer. It seems that up to this point no one has ever tried to combine IT and mixology, which is why I have some hope for the success of my undertaking.

It all started with this: my wife is fond of cooking and has a small culinary blog. Last year, on my birthday, she invited a real bartender from the bar startup project BARADOM.net to visit us in the kitchen. From the moment of my acquaintance with Artyom (the bartender who turned my birthday into a home bar party), I had an idea: to find recipes (or invent myself) cocktails that would fit into the IT theme.

The result of several days of active correspondence with Artyom was such a small cocktail card:

  • Ruby
  • Python
  • Severe Perl
  • JMP (aka Assembler)
  • Profit!
  • Epic fail
  • Memory leak

Postponing the preparation of cocktails until the next day the programmer really did not want to. Of course, we expected that everyone could prepare our cocktails and, in general, I could cope on my own, but firstly I don‚Äôt have as much experience as Artyom, but I would like everything to work out as good as possible. And so, ice, lemons, limes, oranges and juices were prepared in advance, Artyom came to visit and work began to boil in the kitchen.

Ruby


The first cocktail we made was Ruby, of course. I myself have long been identifying myself as a Rubist camp, although lately JS (CoffeeScript) has had to deal more and more.



It's pretty simple. It took us the preparation (hereafter all in grams):

- 20 coconut Malibu liqueur
- 20 Lychee liqueur
- 40 cognac or brandy
- 150 cherry juice
- lemon
- ice

What they did: they added the main ingredients to a tall glass filled with ice. Added lemon juice (to taste). Thoroughly mixed to "fogging" the glass. Decorated with a slice of orange and cocktail cherries. The drink turned out quite sweet, fragrant, refreshing. The ruby-red color of the cocktail fits perfectly with the logo of the ruby ‚Äã‚Äãlanguage.



Python


Python. Here we took the color of green python as a basis. And it seems it turned out quite well.



For the preparation we needed:

- 50 rum light
- 30 mint liqueur
- 30 pineapple juice
- juice 1/2 lemon
- sprite

What we did: we beat the main ingredients in a shaker with ice. All were poured into a glass and dolili 7up (or sprite), decorated glass with a slice of lime. Cocktail should be quickly served to guests - beautiful foam quickly settles. The drink turned out to be refreshing, with a pleasant pineapple flavor. Loved my wife.

Jmp


I really wanted to do something related to the assembler. I do not know why. Just a whim. And frankly, I did not have a single idea on this. I began to look at the assembler commands and for some reason I stopped at the transition operator JMP. And Artem almost instantly offered to make a shot:

- 20 J agermeister (herbal liqueur)
- 20 M idori (melon liqueur) + Lime
- 20 P each (Peach syrup)

What they did: with a bar spoon they put layers of syrup, melon liqueur with a small amount of lime juice and herbal liqueur in a high shot.



Profit!


Profit! in our opinion should be sweet, intoxicating, airy. This is how we saw this cocktail.

For the preparation we needed:

- 20 cream liqueur
- 20 blackcurrant liqueur
- 20 triple sec (strong, sweet orange liqueur)
- whipped cream
- cocoa powder

What they did: the main ingredients were mixed in a shaker, decorated with whipped cream, sprinkled with cocoa powder (it is better to use a fine sieve), decorated with a cherry. Cocktail turned out moderately sweet and very interesting to taste.

Epic fail


Epic Fail, he is Epic Fail. Angry and merciless. You will not notice how it comes, and if it does, then Epic Fail is inevitable.

For the preparation we needed:

- 50 vodka. connecting people
- 100 coke
- 1/2 lemon
- ice

What they did: the main ingredients were added to the glass with ice. Thoroughly mixed up to "fogging" the glass. Decorated with lemon.

As planned, ice, lemon and cola completely hide the taste of strong alcohol. If you overdo it with the use, then a real Epic failure can happen. Be extremely careful if you decide to try something like this.



Memory leak


A memory leak is guaranteed if you bust through this drink. Everything can be so bad that you have to force the garbage collector to start ... and not a fact that will help. In such cases, some prefer to press reset immediately.

In order to reproduce the memory leak, we needed:

- 50 tequila
- 50 rum light
- 50 triple sec (strong, sweet orange liqueur)
- 50 kahlua coffee liqueur
- lime
- cola

What they did: All the ingredients were poured into a glass filled with ice with slices of lime, mixed thoroughly. Decorated with a slice of lime. It turned out delicious and stunning.

On Earth, preparing a patch for the Opportunity Mars rover

Flash memory is worn out and does not work normally.


After more than a decade on the surface of the red planet, the electronic memory elements of the Opportunity Mars rover are degrading - this process is well known to many flash drive owners. The faults started six months ago, and now NASA is going to update the software to take into account the wear of "iron".

In some cells of the flash memory to record information is no longer possible. This leads to the fact that the operating system loses data and constantly reboots, making it difficult for any scientific work. Information is lost because telemetry and commands from the Earth are stored in volatile memory, and when the rover goes into sleep mode to conserve battery power, it is turned off.

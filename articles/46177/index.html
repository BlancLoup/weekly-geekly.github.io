The initiative group of the StartupPoint organizers (soon not only the city of Moscow) offers the following idea to the habra-community court (we suggest paying special attention to the organizers and participants of the regional meetings of startups):

We decided to bring elements of the game and competition to our meetings! Now the activity of all participants of the event will be encouraged by positive karma.


Karma of participants:
All participants who come to the meeting immediately earn 5 points of karma (encouragement to visit)!
At each meeting, the three most active participants are selected from the audience, who receive + to karma in proportion to their activity (1st place = +30 units, 2nd place + +20, 3e = +10). The most active determines the moderator. Activity is determined by questions / constructive criticism / ideas of the participant during the entire meeting (on Pitch, Lynch, questions on reports).

Start- ups will be assigned karma based on the assessment of this particular startup by the SP audience.
The startup karma directly depends on the startup karma. That is, how much karma a startup will receive, the same will be received by the person presenting it.
Of course, the most difficult question here is how to count and count votes, so as not to move into the bureaucracy and not lose a lot of time for this. There is a solution (it may not be the best, so we are bringing up these issues for discussion, but we still like it :). The audience adds or minuses a startup by raising a red (minus) or green (plus) piece of paper (usual small square colored office sheets for quick recordings). Perhaps the method is primitive, maybe even ridiculous, but we thought that it is more efficient to vote with color sheets than to raise hands, and it is also easier to count and easier to stop cheating.

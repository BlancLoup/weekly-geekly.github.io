TechCrunch Moscow will support the startup ecosystem in Russia



The key topic of the TechCrunch global techlog conference, which will be held in Moscow on December 13, will be a discussion of the possibility of Russian developers creating global IT start-ups.

More than twenty speakers, among them - representatives of government agencies, famous Russian and Western businessmen and investors in the field of high technologies - will present their views on various aspects of the start-up and venture capital ecosystems of Russia. A total of about 500 participants are expected at the conference.

The conference was not built in the traditional way for Russian events. Round tables and presentations will be more like interviews with significant figures of the Russian and foreign Internet market. This format was chosen because in the process of such interviews really interesting ideas are born - much more valuable than during traditional presentations.

During the conference, much attention will be paid to the specifics of investing in Russia, an assessment of the attractiveness of individual Russian technological products and opportunities to reach the global level. This topic will be highlighted by Igor Taber (Intel Capital), Igor Agamirzyan (Russian Venture Company), Yaron Waller (Hasso Partner Ventures), Matias Lyungman (Atomico Ventures), Alexander Galitsky (Almaz Capital Partners), Alan Caffi (Managing Partners de Ventech Capital) .

Arkady Dvorkovich (Administration of the President of the Russian Federation), Igor Shchegolev (Ministry of Communications and Mass Communications of the Russian Federation), Ilya Massuh (Ministry of Communications and Mass Communications of the Russian Federation), Anatoly Karachinsky (IBS), Arkady Volozh (Yandex), Mike Butcher will also speak as speakers (TechCrunch Europe), Demyan Kudryavtsev (Kommersant Publishing House), Anton Nosik, Annelise Van Den Belt (SUP), Cyril Maharinsky (Ostrovok.ru), Semyon Voynov (ZeptoLab), Oskar Hartmann (KupiVIP), Pavel Durov (VKontakte ), Dmitry Stavisky (Evernote) and others.

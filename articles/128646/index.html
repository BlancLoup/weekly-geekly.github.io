Today marks exactly one year since I released my first multiplayer video game on the Internet. I really want to share the accumulated statistics, financial indicators, interesting moments of the project, thoughts about the present and plans for the future, as well as invite you to a party in honor of the anniversary of the project.

about the project


Whoismafia is a mix of video conferencing and psychological mafia games. More details about the project can be found in my posts ( 1 , 2 and 3 ).

Whoismafia is a 100% garage project. The idea to create a video game arose in the very real garage in the country, where my brother and I were discussing a new p2p technology for me from Adobe. This moment completely flew out of my head and just as unexpectedly surfaced when they were discussing another crazy idea.

The main goal of the project at the very beginning was to delve into the peer-to-peer technology, to do something more than hello world, to work with Flash Builder in conjunction with Flash Catalyst and Illustrator, and the main thing is to create a live project, where real people communicated live , because the mafia is just an excuse for communication, a topic uniting people.

Mafia was written from scratch in two weeks and came out in hard alpha - everything was wildly buggy, but it worked. Therefore, for another three weeks, I diligently finished the game.

Expectations


After the release of the mafia, I had the persistent feeling that just about a little bit, I would remove the bugs, finish the features and everything will be fine, the people will start playing. Habra effect was accompanied by 7000 loyal users, who gave very solid feedback. Every day about 200-300 hosts without ads with glitches and bugs meant that someone needed it, which means it was worth working.

In addition to coding, I participated in start-up activity - to be honest, I had a desire to quickly grab and score. Various people appeared, some offered cooperation, others wanted to buy out the project, invited to make presentations, etc. I have merged from everywhere ‚Äî until the project is ready either for sale, or for monetization, or even for an influx of visitors ‚Äî they will sit and scatter. There was not even a primitive forum so that people could discuss the game's bugs.

Quite the contrary - the release came out again strongly bazhnom, Habra indulgently sent a little more than two thousand scouts who did not find anything interesting on the site and left the game forever.

On the one hand, it was a bit sad - the dream to work a little and swim for the rest of my life in luxury (a joke) did not come true.

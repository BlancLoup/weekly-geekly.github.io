And once again about motivation

For hundreds of years, humanity has come up with only two motivators - a carrot in front and a carrot in the back. But implementations may differ.

When hobbies coincide with work


People often ask me how to make myself learn - whether programming or learning foreign languages. For several years, my answer has not changed. You need to take your hobby (well, do you have some kind of hobby?) And come up with a service, application or website for it.

In my student years, I became very interested in MathCAD, spending hours on exponenta.ru (later I became interested in Wolphram Alpha). So when the desire came to learn PHP at your leisure (at that time everyone wrote in PHP), what the site would be about was not even a choice.

For half a year of developing a PHP website about MathCAD from scratch, the following goals were achieved:

  1. Learned PHP at a level sufficient for website development.
  2. MathCAD's proficiency level has been raised to an indescribable level.
  3. Knowledge of HTML / CSS / JavaScript, SEO, hosting and DNS work was obtained.
  4. On the day, 200-300 people went steadily, which was, in my opinion, a success.
  5. Later, the acquired knowledge was applied to develop his mathematical software for Windows Phone, writing his Ph.D. and writing a MathCAD workshop for students.

What technologies to make your pet project? I answer: you need to do the project in turn on different technologies until you realize that a particular platform or technology is right for you, and you want to choose it as the main one for a certain period.

In my opinion, the situation when the hobby coincides with the work is just perfect. The question is that you need to find such an area or occupation.

I will make another remark: you never know what knowledge and at what point in time you will need it (and whether it will be useful at all), but it‚Äôs better to suffer from an oversupply of solutions and knowledge than from their absence at the right moment.

I know i don't know anything


A well-known phrase, the essence of which is that the more you know, the more you discover new knowledge, with the result that comes the understanding that you really do not know anything. The paradox is that the cleverest representatives of humanity, as a rule, are very critical of their mental abilities. But people who are not burdened with intelligence tend to be ‚Äúexperts‚Äù in everything. Familiar?

The effect of Dunning - Kruger - cognitive distortion, which lies in the fact that "people with low skill levels, make erroneous conclusions and make bad decisions, and are not able to realize their mistakes due to their low level of skill." This leads to an overestimation of their own abilities, while really highly qualified people, on the contrary, tend to underestimate their abilities and suffer from a lack of self-confidence, considering others to be more competent. Thus, less competent people in general have a higher opinion of their own abilities than is typical of competent people, who also tend to assume that others rate their abilities as low as they themselves.

How does reading affect motivation? Very simple. The more well-read a person is, the less material benefits he seeks in his work. Maslow's pyramid in action.

The process of creating processes

After your successful integration as a project manager in a new place of work, it makes sense to familiarize yourself with the existing processes in the organization and, if you think they can be improved, proactively suggest changing them. The document describing the process should contain the following elements: goal, description of roles, sequence of steps (when-who-what, expected results, metrics, problem escalation path), checklist (optional)


Here are the process rules (adapted by Chuck Palahniuk's Fight Club rules):
1. The approved process must be followed.
2. The approved process must be followed, even if you do not want to do it.
3. A process can be changed only by a special process of changing processes, which includes:
- Modification of the document describing the process
- Review \ inspection of the modified document
- Piloting a new process
- Approval of a new process in case of successful piloting
- Then go to step 1.
Exception: the senior management (or business) may decide not to follow the current process, in whole or in part, in this particular situation and this should be clearly documented.

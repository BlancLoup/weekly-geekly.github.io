Bloomberg: McDonald's will buy a personalization technology developer for $ 300 million



Image: Unsplash

According to Bloomberg, McDonald's is close to completing its largest takeover in 20 years. The chain of fast food restaurants million will buy the company-developer of personalization technology menu Dynamic Yield Ltd. The amount of the transaction will be $ 300 million.

This is the biggest deal for McDonald's over the past twenty years.

The technology startup will continue to operate as a separate company. McDonald's will continue to invest in the development of its technology, which is already used by companies such as Urban Outfitters Inc., Ikea and HelloFresh

In 2018, McDonald's tested Dynamic Yield technology in its restaurants in the United States. After closing the deal, the company plans to more actively implement it - in particular for use in street screens with a menu for customers on cars.

Why McDonald's is investing in technology.


The new technology will allow McDonald's to change the menu displayed on the screens depending on many factors, including the weather. For example, on cold days, the system will display more varieties of coffee and other hot beverages, and in the heat it will produce ice cream. In addition, such an automated menu will offer customers additional products.

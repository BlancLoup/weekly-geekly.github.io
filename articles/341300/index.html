In recent years, quite a lot of new ideas and ways to organize a network have appeared. The next generation of digital connections is characterized by concepts such as SDN, automation, AI (artificial intelligence), machine learning, and a network factory.

Those who have been working in this field for quite a long time have repeatedly heard all this in various incarnations, interpretations, and modifications. But now, really, "the pot starts to boil." The network industry is ready to collectively take a step forward in the organization of corporate networks.

In the meantime, all their clients have already somehow built the most suitable options for their corporate IT environment. They work as far as possible and are an integral part of the business assets of enterprises. Networking support is not an easy job. Practice shows that it is rather difficult to change and improve the network architecture of companies on your own.

Founded in 1996, Extreme Networks (San Jose, California) is precisely the company that is able to ensure the actual implementation of the network fabric architecture.

In its performance, simplifying the corporate network infrastructure and reducing operating costs are linked to the concept of Extreme Automated Campus. Its main point is that the simple summation of the components of the solution provides advantages and characteristics that give a correctly implemented automated network. Among these components, in accordance with the vision of Extreme, the following.

1. Use Shortest Path Bridging (SPB) 802.1aq standard. In fact, it is the replacement of the traditional protocol of such ‚Äúnetwork bricks‚Äù as MPLS, BGP, multicast PIM, OSPF, VLAN and others. In a large network environment, this approach reduces the complexity of the structure by several orders of magnitude.

2. The work of the network on the principle of "hop-by-hop" (when data is moved between the source and the addressee using intermediate nodes) becomes a thing of the past. All changes are now made at the network access level (in real time and without interruption of services).

3. Applying specific policies for users and devices becomes the basis for changes in automated networks. Permanent analytics provides feedback that is used to tune network performance and solve problems.

4. Using the principle of hyper-segmentation (hyper-segmentation), more than ever, effectively supports network security. In addition to this segment, the rest of the network becomes invisible to intruders due to the fact that the old vulnerable protocols are no longer used.

5. The presence of the API means that third-party network components can interact with Extreme Automated Campus.

Definition and description of the market

Gartner defines the wired and wireless LAN access infrastructure market as a market for network hardware and software providers that allow devices to connect via wired LAN and Wi-Fi networks.

These devices may include laptops, smartphones, tablets and other mobile devices, network office equipment, sensors and other Internet of Things (IoT) endpoints, other fixed or mobile endpoints.

It is expected that by 2022 more than 60% of enterprises and organizations around the world will use automation tools for the network access layer.

For corporate developers, there are three different approaches to market promotion:

1. The developer supplies its own wired and wireless network infrastructure components, applications, and services.

Examples are Cisco, Hewlett Packard Enterprise Aruba, Extreme Networks and Huawei.

2. The developer mainly and primarily provides specific means and connection options for wired and wireless components.

Such companies often focus on solutions that meet a specific, unique set of market requirements. For example, it could be cloud-based wireless LAN (WLAN) management for vertical markets, such as retail or healthcare.

Examples are Aerohive, Mist Systems and Mojo Networks.

3. The developer engages a strategic partner who is involved in creating a complete solution for accessing some (or all) hardware or software components. The core developer then integrates the various network application functionality into a combined solution.

Examples are Dell and Juniper Networks.

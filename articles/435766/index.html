We had almost everything ready by mid-December, but to be released before the New Year is how to roll out the release on Friday evening. Therefore, we decided to postpone the departure to a large voyage to the beginning of 2019.

What was done


When we announced the release of Habr outside Russia, we already had a plan. Rough estimate of work and deadlines for them. But already in the process of work, we realized that the alterations would be more ambitious.

1. Translation structure

We practically didn‚Äôt have localization experience, while developing Habr we didn‚Äôt put multilingualism into it. Therefore, in the architecture of the project, many things had to be changed in order for the support of a new language in principle to be possible and work correctly.

At first we tried to solve the problem quickly, through the classic gettext, but very soon it became clear that its capabilities were not enough ...

All translation work was divided into three functional blocks:

  • Interface (language files)

    For the full internationalization of the interface, the ICU format was chosen - it became possible to translate dates, endings, etc. with it. Each translation element was assigned a unique ID, for convenience, they were grouped by entities. Then a multi-line language file was sent to the translator.
  • Templates (reference section, documents, manuals, letters)

    Large "independent" texts were translated separately, on their own. But the translation of legal documents (and we had a lot of them) is a separate universe with its black holes, so I had to resort to the help of a UFO.

2. Multi-language settings

It was necessary to decide how it would work. We did not consider the option of creating a separate site seriously, but in the process of work such an idea came to us occasionally.

Therefore, we took the path of content sharing and it remains to figure out how to do it. As a result, we stopped at the most flexible option: so that each user could choose both the language of the site interfaces and the language of the content.

To do this, each user in the settings can specify what will be in what language. You can leave only content in Russian in the feed, you can only in English, but you can immediately on both.



So, from today on Habr√© two settings appeared:

  • Interface language

    The language of all elements of the site. So far, either Russian or English is supported. We tried to do a ‚Äúsmart‚Äù autodetection of the language (based on the accept language header), but this setting can always be overridden by ourselves.
  • Publication language

    This setting allows the user to see in his feed publications in selected languages. The content of the context blocks depends on it (‚ÄúMost Read,‚Äù ‚ÄúRead Now,‚Äù ‚ÄúWhat They Discuss‚Äù). By default, it is inherited from the interface language, but it can also be changed at any time.

Before authorization, the choice of languages ‚Äã‚Äãis in the upper right corner (planet icon), after authorization - it moves to the user menu. In the mobile version, the behavior is similar - the settings are in the footer and in the user menu.


In the ribbon, you can choose one specific language or several. But when creating a publication, you can choose only one language.


3. SEO

All done, translated, began to test. It was pleasant, but any absence-presence was felt. And they remembered that they forgot about god SEO.

When your website is in one language, you can often not think about search engine optimization - everything somehow works on its own. But when the site appears content in different languages, then this point can no longer be ignored.

Prior to this, Habr was only in Russian and was generally well indexed. With the advent of multilingualism, it became necessary to teach search engines to correctly ‚Äúsee‚Äù both the Russian and English versions. For this, we had to conduct a comprehensive audit and redo some things on the site - change the links, change the sitemap, set the correct canonical-url, and so on.

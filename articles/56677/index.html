Equipment


I was very surprised by the presentation of the device - this I have not yet met. It is impossible not to talk about it)
Those who have bought Asti Mondoro champagne at least once will easily imagine the size of the package. The upper part of the box is made of transparent plexiglass, the lower part is hidden behind a black matte paper construction. The mouse itself, as a museum piece, is fixed on a special plastic stand.



First you need to remove the glass "skin", then remove the paper component of the box.



Now we have a black plastic cube in front of which a mouse is fixed on its upper face.



It is installed on a special stand that needs to be pulled out (along special guides of the upper edge of the "cube").



Having pulled out a paper insert of a cube, 4 paper packings become available - one (with documentation) is installed vertically and three lie on top of each other inside. For some reason, server racks are recalled from work :) There are glossy icons and a pull-out tongue on the three boxes.



In one box, with the USB logo, is wrapped in a fabric lining, USB-cable with a cap for the connector.
In the second box are a lithium-ion battery and the battery compartment cover - all in their bags.
In the third box, the largest, is a charging dock, about which a little later.



Thus, the total configuration was as follows:

- Mouse Razer Mamba;
- Docking station for charging;
- USB-cord to power the device;

Appearance


First about the wire and docking station.
The contacts of the cord are yellow, but I doubt that they are gold-plated (and even if so, it hardly gives anything, because USB connectors in the computer are the most common). The cord itself is wrapped with braided threads, which, apparently, has a positive effect on its durability and recognition. Indeed, it looks unusual and beautiful. The length of the order of two meters.





The cable itself is USB (a) -> miniUSB (B), however, the B-type connector is somewhat non-standard - it is installed on a special plastic part that is needed for attaching to the mouse and the docking station.
The docking station is made as follows - the trapezoidal part is fixed on a square stand, the ends of which light up when connected to the computer in blue. There is a company logo and two contacts on the ‚Äúbevel of a trapezoid‚Äù, the USB connector is deeply hidden from the back.



When connected to a computer, as I said, the stand starts to glow bright enough blue, and if you leave the mouse on the charge, this glow pulsates. Fascinating! If you wish, you can turn off any of the glows programmatically.

Mouse Actually, the most important thing in this review.
Made of black matte rubberized plastic - it is very pleasant to hold in hands. On the part under the palm is a glossy logo, which, by the way, does not spoil the overall picture and does not feel like a hand. The whole look, which is very aggressive, speaks about the serious intentions of the mouse :)



From the controls, the mouse has two standard buttons, a wheel (which can be clicked and evade to the right), two buttons under the thumb (front and back) and two buttons next to the left mouse button - to switch the rodent sensitivity.





Plastic wheel, rubberized, with serifs. Glows on the sides in blue, in complete darkness it looks like two blue stripes. It is pressed easily, evades too. It is a pity that only in one direction - I usually assign switching to tabs for this action (in ICQ, browser, file manager), and this is usually done not only in one direction) And ... it is unusual already, after the MicroGear wheel: )



All buttons crush easily, somewhat springy. The main buttons are pressed quite loudly (which is very critical for me at night - the growing champion sleeps very sensitively). Thanks to their concave shapes, fingers do not move out.
On the back of the rodent there is a wedge-shaped recess for charging with two contacts, a hole in the laser sensor ( 5600dpi !), An on-off button for the mouse, a connection button (just in case) and a lever for fastening the connector of the cord to be connected. As it turned out, it is very convenient if the battery runs out - put in the wire and forget about the problem. I almost forgot to mention the Teflon inserts - thanks to them the mouse slides perfectly along the table surface.





The ergonomics of the device at the height - the hand is very comfortable, there is simply nothing to complain about. And this is despite the fact that I am used to a special relief base on the right side of my Logitech MX Revolution .

Preparatory work


All you need to get started is to insert the battery and close it with a lid. In this state, the weight will be 130 grams. You can not insert, and connect to the wire - we get 110 grams. Everything is ready to go.

Turn on and work


Turning on the mouse (switch on its back), put it on the carpet. The maximum sensitivity is set (5600 dpi) - from the edge to the edge of my 19-inch monitor, the cursor passes almost instantly (on the table it drove no more than a centimeter). Rodent really reacts to the slightest impulses!



It is very unusual to get used to such sensitivity is very difficult. After 10 minutes of such races, I reduce the sensitivity to 3000 - this is already more comfortable. Switches the sensitivity of the two buttons that are located next to the RMB - it is convenient that you do not need to climb into the settings.

With a carpet grip is excellent, but the mouse goes around the table just fine. By the way, it is noticeably lighter and quieter than the same Revolution (possibly due to the last term of use).



The operating time without a wire (after a full charge, at 3000dpi) turned out to be 12 hours of active movements - I was even tired then) The mouse stopped completely unexpectedly - no one would look at the indication all the time. So it would be great if the mouse somehow advertised exhaustion of forces - it could be a barely audible ‚Äúpeak‚Äù or a wink scrolling.
If you use the mouse in the usual many mode, then the charge is enough for about two days, a little more.

Charging is also a receiver - with a rodent they operate at a frequency of 2.4 GHz, which eliminates any delays. More precisely, of course, there is a delay, as in any other case, but here it is extremely small - 1 ms (one thousandth of a second). During this time you will not have time to absolutely nothing, and the response of your monitor is at best 2ms :) There are no delays. As with the connection with the wire, and without it.

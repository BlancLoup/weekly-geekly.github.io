Stage 1. Job search.


The devil in the little things

This stage will have to be divided into 3 sub-steps in an attempt to fully address these issues.

Stage 1.1. Summary

There are a lot of sites with examples, descriptions and recommendations. They give and psychologists, and HR-managers, and all and sundry. But how to write a resume when you have nothing to write in work experience? Many of my friends began to sculpt there everything that could somehow fill the resume. Did you use Photoshop to remove red eye? Add information about the ownership of this tool. Did the blueprints for coursework in AutoCAD? His here.

Show in the resume in the first place those parties that are necessary for a particular job.

It is not very convenient to edit each time a resume before sending it, but take a look at it from the point of view of the employer: he may not notice the necessary features among the extra information or not read it at all.

Also in the resume you need to reflect ALL your experience for this job (let it be some project that did not work or you abandoned halfway, but it was). You will be able to discuss it, talk about the work done, the difficulties and the employer will understand better the level of your knowledge and train of thought.

a) Requirements are sometimes written by HR managers. And when they are looking for a specialist, they can copy from another vacancy, not understanding what it means;

Stage 1.2. Job selection

The main thing - choose what you like for you. No need to choose according to prestige, the advice of friends and other criteria that are not relevant to your desires. With the happy position of the stars the correct implementation of the previous stages, you got a job. But this is not a reason to relax. I will not give advice on how to behave in a team and in the performance of job duties, because this varies greatly by team / management.

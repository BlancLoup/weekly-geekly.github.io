Weekly assembly Vivaldi 1.0.123.10

Hello!

We present you the next weekly assembly of the Vivaldi browser under the number 1.0.123.10. As many of you know, last week we were busy preparing for the release of a test version of the Vivaldi TP2, but despite this, today's test build also has something to show from the work done.

First of all, we continued to work on spatial navigation. Now it works significantly better compared to the previous version, although, of course, it‚Äôs still up to a fully functional and convenient tool to work and work. What we are doing.

We also had a tangible improvement in the performance of the browser interface. We hope that you will like these changes, because in previous versions there were quite a few complaints about the work of the interface.

Another noticeable change that you find in the settings is that we have added the ability to disable some of the Google services traditionally presented in Chromium-based browsers. We do not give an assessment of the quality of these services, we simply give you the opportunity to refuse them if you want to. These changes should be made to the taste of users who pay heightened attention to the issues of privacy and security of personal data. Why - a couple words about it below.

From time to time, Vivaldi will download a summary of potentially dangerous or phishing sites from Google‚Äôs servers. If a match is found, Vivaldi asks Google for more information about the dangerous site and notifies the user about possible security problems. But some users do not like at all that some third-party service, even if it belongs to Google, is informed about their online activity. Thus, such users can easily refuse such a service in the settings of the Vivaldi browser.

Top PewDiePie Vlogger Tests YouTube Business Model



In 2015, YouTube video service launched a paid subscription for $ 9.99 per month. Paid program called YouTube Red . Its main feature, in addition to the possibility of completely disabling advertising on the site and saving videos for offline viewing, became authoring programs. Top videobloggers, sportsmen, comedians began to create premium content: interviews, shows, comments on current topics, etc.

One of the authors is Felix Kjellberg, better known as PewDiePie .

Due to his fame, he was able to conduct his own show, which he did with success all this time. Last year, Kjellberg earned more than $ 15 million on YouTube. In many ways, the high salary was due to the fact that the blogger account was part of the Google Preferred advertising network. Its members are popular bloggers and advertisers who are willing to pay large sums to bloggers.

Everything went well until the recent actions of PewDiePie spoiled the reputation of both himself and YouTube, casting doubt on the entire business model of the service. We are talking about dubious videoblogger jokes with elements of racism, chauvinism and many other unpleasant things that the Wall Street Journal journalists saw in his videos. The other day the publication on its website says that nine PewDiePie videos contain content related to Nazism and anti-Semitism. It is hard to say whether it is so or not, since most of the controversial points can be called taken out of the context of the commercials themselves (some Geektimes readers think so). The situation is quite complicated, because the PewDiePie has long reproached the media for being against ordinary people like him who have influence over the masses. "Felix has become popular due to provocative content, but now he has already gone too far, the resulting videos are unacceptable," said a spokesman for Disney, who signed a contract with PewDiePie.

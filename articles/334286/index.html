Internet travel. Analysis of foreign SIM-cards

Before traveling, I simply bought a local sim card and at the end of the trip I threw it away. But then travel became more difficult, there were transplants and several countries in one trip - and each time it would not be enough to buy SIM cards neither time nor money. And then I started looking for the perfect sim card that works worldwide and for a penny). Naturally, there is no such card, but I have found several products that complement each other perfectly. The topic is relevant not only because of the summer. Recently, roaming was canceled in the EU, with the northern countries having special rights to take the roaming fee, and the southern ones changed the tariffs. Infu updated for himself, but it is useful to everyone traveling abroad.

There are three main universal products on the market: MTX Connect , DrimSim , AirBalticCard Card . Four prepaid tariffs from foreign operators, which can be bought independently on the next trip, or through intermediaries in Russia are also worthy of attention: Saunalahti from Elisa FI (Finland), Internetikaart from Elisa EE (Estonia), Mundo from Orange ES (Spain), Vodafone 25 from Vodafone IT (Italy).

The price range is decent. Below you will find a large comparative table with countries and prices, as well as a brief summary of each product.

Recommendations


  • Ideally, to have two international sim-cards, and that with at least one it was possible to make an outgoing call with a voice. A bunch of DreamSim + MTX Connect Pay As You Go is a great option at the moment.

  • Before each trip (or on the way to the airport) check the best fares in the countries of destination, because for products like DrimSim and AirBaltic Card, they are constantly changing, and home operators release interesting options like World Online (Megaphone) and Zabugorishte (MTS).

  • If you need a lot of traffic for a mobile router in Europe, but without buying packages and subscription fees - Saunalahti from Elisa FI, or any other local sim card with similar nishtyak.

  • At least once a year, you need to replenish the balance of SIM cards with a minimum amount and take some kind of paid action, for example, sending sms.

  • It is better to disable autopayments and use a separate bank card in product currency (EUR) for replenishment. AirBalticCard and DrimSim store the card data on your account, and MTX Connect creates a pre-approved payment when you pay with PayPal. I use USD and EUR cards from Rocket Bank or Tinkoff for payments, the products are free and very convenient!

Ford opened a new research and development center in Silicon Valley, the second after 2012. The company has high hopes for the new center because Dragos Maciuca , a former senior systems engineer at Apple, headed it. The company plans to hire 125 employees. The company will continue to develop software and will develop partnerships with other high-tech companies.







Among the plans for Ford - the introduction of remote control systems of the car. Last week, software developer Sudipto Aich placed monitors on the table, set the game pedal wheel from Logitech and ran a golf car parked in Atlanta almost two and a half thousand miles from the scene. This is one of the twenty-five tasks that the company has set for its employees.

Remote control cars are not as impressive as unmanned vehicles , and does not give the advantages of the absence of a person behind the wheel. But this is another alternative that will allow one day to take his grandmother to the airport without leaving home. It's like driving a quadrocopter, with only wheels and a very large size.

One of the important factors - the new technology will not be expensive. The developer used a controller from Logitech, which costs about $ 300. All the necessary sensors are easily accessible. The system is easy to connect to a modern car, especially to an electric car like a golf car. You do not need a physical mechanism that will turn the steering wheel and press the pedals. In this case, the implementation is possible in the existing 4G LTE network.

There are a number of technology applications that the company is already considering. Car rental companies can use the system to move vehicles in parking lots.

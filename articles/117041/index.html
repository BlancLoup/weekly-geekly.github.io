ABBYY Department at MIPT - 5 years of investment in Russian education


April 7 marks the fifth anniversary of the ABBYY department at the Faculty of Innovations and High Technologies of the Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology. It would seem that five years is a short time, but during this time several dozens of specialists who successfully work in the company have been trained at the Department of Image Recognition and Text Processing. We will talk a little about why ABBYY decided to teach students on their own, and what exactly they were taught.
At different times, we used different approaches to the selection of developers - we hired very experienced programmers and graduates of leading Russian universities without work experience in other companies. Over time, it became clear that within our own department we will be able to train students much better than it happens in study groups that operate at ABBYY for programmers coming to us from outside. A serious scientific base, developed in the company, and good relations with universities allowed us to open a department in MIPT in 2006.

Why MIPT? First of all, most of the top managers of ABBYY are graduates of this particular university and in the company, in principle, there are a lot of physics and technology workers, both graduates and students. Secondly, in MIPT they give system education, learn to study, research and think - for us it turned out to be the most suitable basis for creating our own department. The system of education at the Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology is built in such a way that basic knowledge is given at junior courses, and senior students are distributed to different specialized areas (basic departments). Previously, students combined work with us and study in other departments - it was very difficult and inconvenient for them. When the ABBYY department appeared, students began to write their research papers on the topics they were engaged in at work. This turned out to be convenient for students and useful for the company.

Students are admitted to the department starting from the third year of study, first to the undergraduate degree and then to the magistracy. The opening of the department at MIPT allows us not only to help "our" students. Our goal is to make at the FIVT (Faculty of Innovations and High Technologies) the best teaching of Computer Science in Russia. In the undergraduate courses, our employees conduct subjects common to the entire faculty - ‚ÄúIntroduction to Programming‚Äù, ‚ÄúAlgorithms and Data Structures‚Äù, ‚ÄúDatabases‚Äù We hope that in this way we contribute to the improvement of education in our country. To achieve the same goal, we are making efforts to improve the school curriculum in computer science, because in recent years the level of applicants who come to universities in specialties related to IT has fallen dramatically.

But back to the department. For five years, about fifty people have enrolled in it, some have already received diplomas, now we have 25 students studying. After graduation, you can work at ABBYY, but graduates are not bound by any obligations towards the company - you can get a job in another place. But, as a rule, at the 5-6 courses already all the students work with us, and sometimes they begin to do it before. Now ABBYY employs 15 graduates of the department and another 15 children who are still studying.

Students of the department and graduates work in various departments of the company. As we have said above, the topics of students' research works, as a rule, are closely related to the topics of their work. Therefore, the majority of children take part in the work of the technology development department - all scientific research is carried out there. Some students are even involved in the work on our new product - Compreno (we wrote about it here ). Graduates work both in the technology department and in all product teams - FineReader, Lingvo, Data Capture, SDK, mobile platforms. Among them are both developers and representatives of other specialties - testers, analysts, patent writers, and even specialists in online sales or HR. The knowledge gained at the department helps to move up the career ladder: now in the sixth year there are two students who have already become team leaders - one of them directs the testers FineReader, the other - online sales of products for mobile platforms.

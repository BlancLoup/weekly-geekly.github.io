From idea to App Store in 24 hours: Pomodoro

Hello, dear readers Habr!

The 24-hour marathon on the development of iOS applications for work on the Pomodoro system has just begun ! The marathon itself is available at the link: bdev.tv. General chat for viewers and presenters in jabber'e: bdev@conference.mit.edu. Repository with the current code on github .

Over the past few days a number of changes have occurred with the project:

  • Updated the site: now - besides the broadcast itself - you can see the song playing in the stream, you can suggest an idea for the next application, see the dull wallpaper
  • Registered company on Habrahabr
  • We took a course on the development of free analogues paid applications

Within 24 hours, I will write an iOS7 application from scratch and upload it in the App Store. The idea of ‚Äã‚Äãthe application:

The Tomato Method is a time management technique proposed by Francesco Cyrillo in the late 1980s. The technique involves splitting tasks into 25-minute periods, called ‚Äútomatoes,‚Äù followed by short breaks. Wikipedia

In short, I want to make an application with the ability to configure a specific "loop" of timers. Standard customizable set will be 25-5-25-5-25-5-25-30. From two sides there will be side pull-down menus: left with settings, right with statistics. The application will be different color schemes, social sharing, tweaks timers, the most popular settings offered by the audience in the chat.

Competing free apps and their fatal flaws:

  • DropTime: Beautiful and functional application. Alas, there are ads that are disabled for money.
  • Pomodoro: A simple application without ads. Strongly lacking the functionality of statistics collection, rewinding timers, setting time intervals
  • Pomodoro: Another application with the same name. Brakes on the iPad Mini, incomprehensible interface, lacks statistics and some settings
  • doPomodoro: A simple application without ads. It looks nice, but not enough time interval settings

Today I will try to combine beautiful design, functionality and lack of monetization in 24 hours. The more people watching the broadcast, the more fun! Offer your ideas for the development of the project.

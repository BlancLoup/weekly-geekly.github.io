E3 2007: first reports

From July 10 to July 13, 2007, the annual E3 gaming conference is held, at which industry giants such as Microsoft , Nintendo and Sony have already held their conferences. The first articles and blog entries appear.

Microsoft announced the ability to download Disney movies on the Xbox 360 through the Video Marketplace. It happens the same way as downloading games through Live Arcade. Video from Call of Duty 4 and Halo 3 was shown. The XBox 360 version was announced, styled

Nintendo has demonstrated a new gadget for the Wii - Wii Fit . This device, which resembles bathroom scales, will allow you to feel more comfortable in games where you have to dodge something, and of course Nintendo will help you to enter the segment of housewives who love shaping. Now it will be much more fun to do exercises :)

Sony promised to show a lot of new games and other interesting things on the final day of the exhibition, but for now announced the updated PlayStation 3 (80 GB) with MotorStorm in the set for $ 600, demonstrated a more subtle and compact PlayStation Portable with color options Piano Black, Icicle White and in a special Star Wars Battlefront White option. In 2008, releases of the new Metal Gear Solid, Gran Turismo 5 Prologue and Gran Turismo 5 are expected.

Abroad, materials can be found on the promotional sites of major conference participants - Sony , Microsoft and Nintendo . And, of course, YouTube is replete with fresh video. At the same time, activists periodically update the corresponding Wikipedia page , where interesting data have already been recorded.

Russian-language reports are most quickly published by ConsolenewZ - Microsoft , Sony , Nintendo . They promise to post a full video version of the conference, translated into Russian not by subtitles, but by full-fledged interpretation.

Electronic Entertainment Expo or E3 - the world's largest annual exhibition show the industry of computer and video games. It has been held since 1995 by the Entertainment Software Association and is held annually in May at the Los Angeles Exhibition Center. In 2006, a huge show, attended by tens of thousands of people in a closed conference for elected representatives of the press.

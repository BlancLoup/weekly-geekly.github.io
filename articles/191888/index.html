RailsClub'Moscow 2013. Interview with Ernie Miller

Less than a month is left before the start of the 12th Ruby-developers conference RailsClub'Moscow 2013 ( buy a ticket )

Many distinguished guests are expected: Ernie Miller, Jeremy Evans, Erik Hoadle, Frederick Chang, Linda Liukas, Obi Fernandez and more. We have already managed to make friends and talk with some of them, and now we are happy to introduce you.

Let's start with an interview with Ernie Miller , author of squeel , ActiveRecord specialist.

Interviews are published with original answers in English and our free translation.



When and why did you start programming?

I started at the age of 6 years. My parents brought home a computer model Tandy TRS-80 Color Computer 2. I already loved video games and wanted to start making my own.

I started at 6 years old. I brought in my computer.

What are you working on right now?

I work at Wantful, improve our recommendation engine, in other words, have fun.
I'm working at Wantful, improving our recommendations.

What is the best and what is the worst in your work?

The best: an interesting workflow and a strong team.
The worst: since the team is small and the work is interesting, you need to be able to stop on time and find time for your family

Best: Interesting work and a strong team.
It is a funeral for all my family.

What do you consider your main achievement in life / career at the moment?

I had the courage to start speaking at conferences this year, and this is a real breakthrough.

What is the purpose of your career and professional development?

Career is not the main thing for me, although it helps me to take care of the well-being of my family. I am incredibly lucky because I can do what I love and get paid for it.

What helps a programmer to grow professionally?

It is important to be able to integrate the skills that you have learned into those projects that are important to you. The knowledge that you absorb is only as useful as it is applicable in practice.

It is important that you care. It enables you to create it.

What book would you recommend to read to any programmer?

Every programmer should read Chad Fowler's The Passionate Programmer .

Every developer should read "The Passionate Programmer," by Chad Fowler.

What do you like to do in your free time?

I am a volunteer counselor in my church - I help people who are faced with various difficulties. This is something completely different from programming. "Confusion" and "complexity" in people of a completely different kind than in computers. I also like to spend time with my wife and stepdaughter, play sports and play computer games or game consoles.

I volunteer as a counselor at my church, where I help people It's a very different experience than programming. People are "messy" and "complicated" in ways that computers aren't. Spending time, working out, and PC / console gaming.

Thanks for the interview and see you in Moscow!

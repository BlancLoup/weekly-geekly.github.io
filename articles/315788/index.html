We test seamless WiFi using voice traffic



We continue the experiment started a month earlier, when we built a Wi-Fi network with seamless roaming and walked around the office with a tablet, observing whether fading and crashes will occur when downloading streaming video to it? The experiment was successful, there were no problems with the flow and the report was posted here on Habrahabr. But!

But in the comments to the article, doubts were expressed, since the video stream could be buffered and the seamless Wi-Fi was emulated by swapping the video before moving from one access point to another, and this video was played from the buffer after a break and the session was restored. Answering these questions, we again loaded absolutely the same network, with the same Edimax points, without changing anything in their settings (raised the same parameters for another SSID so as not to interfere with the office with my tests on the RADIUS server, which I also run in in this network). Almost voice traffic was used that could not be suspected in buffering. We are pleased to share the results of the second part of the experiment.

Comment. So far, I cannot manage tests with Tecom tubes, because I personally do not use them in the office, and all the existing ones are occupied by managers. I have my own phone. A little later I‚Äôll take a tube for tests and look like it for several days. All tests were conducted on a typical working day at noon.

Sequencing:

A) On the server, launch the server version of iPerf3 on port 9090.
B) On the iPad, connected via Wi-Fi, launch the client. The packet size is taken as 200 bytes in three streams, that is, 200x3 bytes over TCP. (As you know, TCP is more sensitive to packet loss).

The tablet sends traffic to the server, while measuring the speed and time of packet transmission. The load is comparable to the peak formed by simultaneous conversations from 7-10 people , that is, we emulate decent SIP traffic.

Local testing was chosen specifically not to depend on the Internet channel - it would bring chaos and interference.

How iPerf3 works can be found in numerous sources or on the pages of documentation and Wikipedia, here we note an important point - iPerf3 does not buffer traffic . Thus, we remove objections concerning the purity of the past and the current experiment - in other words, nothing is taken from the cache and goes exclusively live.

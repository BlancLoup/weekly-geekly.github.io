And finally, I would like to identify a few points that have been important and useful for me, even if, at times, at the price of stuffed cones:
  • Acquaintance, even if not very deep, with the project management methodology helped me a lot in my work. Especially if you consider that I acted in the role of both the customer and the performer - it is easier to agree between each other easier;)
  • Add pre-thought analytics in the first release - already mentioned it. And it is also useful to make a global switch constant for a complete ban on sending statistics ‚Äî useful for testing, in order not to clutter the real database with left data.
  • Adding the ACRA library to catch bugs is also available on the very first release. Moreover, it is necessary to set up sending logs for reasons of balance in the volume and importance of errors. If you send everyone, for example, you can get 100,500 messages about Internet unavailability from the user ... And it is also useful to make a global switch constant to completely prohibit sending logs - useful for testing, in order not to clutter the database with left messages that are tracked in the debugger window.
  • Choose the icon and the name of the application from the following considerations (there are a lot of tips like ‚Äúpay special attention to them‚Äù on the net, but how it‚Äôs not said):


The icon in the market should stand out and immediately catch the eye - maybe with brightness, and maybe with laconicism. But stand out. This is the first chance to hook the user. We need to see successful examples in the top of the target category. Then - as the bottom and the middle looks at the moment, and do something differently.

The name (alone or paired with the icon) should immediately open the curtain from the list of applications in the catalog - and what's inside. A beautiful, but abstract name will not push the user to open the application page and read its description. In 30 characters it is difficult to keep within, but it is possible. Albeit at the expense of beauty and sonority - this is not the purpose of the name in the market. The application itself can be called anything. Here I will still be thinking with my own and maybe experimenting.

  • When developing it is clear to fix - what functions to include in the nearest version, and what can be postponed to the next version (in terms of project management - content management), otherwise you can get stuck in endless improvements and additions. If possible, set yourself a clear deadline, and even read them publicly, so as not to be tempted to move the release a little more, then a little more, and again endlessly. This approach helped me a lot, otherwise it wouldn‚Äôt still be launched ...

Herbert Shieldt. Java Complete guide

Eric Freeman, Elizabeth Freeman. Design Patterns (O'Reilly, Peter Publishers)

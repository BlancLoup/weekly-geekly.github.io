Video


From myself I will add that the STALKER benchmark: Call of Pripyat issued 29FPS (from 15 to 47 depending on the scene) at the maximum settings (but without smoothing) with a resolution of 1600x900, the new Battlefield 3 works fine on medium settings at the same native resolution, but the last one 3D mark fairly slowed down (2-5fps), but produced a very beautiful picture of the underwater world.

Linux


And here the most interesting begins, even at the moment of choice I thought that I want a more quickly updated distribution with the highest possible settings, so the choice fell on ArchLinux and for good reason. All the main devices worked out of the box (I did not try the fingerprint scanner) and the touchpad needed to assemble and install psmouse-elantech from the AUR to make the scrolling work. I also had to tinker with a hybrid video system, but there was no particular difficulty here - I installed bumblebee-git from AUR and set up instructions from the wiki - everything worked fine, I tested it on the Trine game and in the browser webGL tests in Chromium.

The average battery life of the laptop was approximately 1.5 hours, with maximum illumination, without energy saving and with an active Wi-Fi connection.

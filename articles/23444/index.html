Many, as they hear about the village, say: "Yes, why do you need it, you will live in the mud, pigs, dung and all that."

  • chickens. Do not require special care. Fresh meat and eggs.
  • ducks (if there is a pond or river). Knowledgeable people say there are few problems with ducks too.
  • goats, maybe. One or two, and probably not in the first year. Meat and milk, less problems than with a cow.
  • meat, lard and milk can be bought from fellow villagers. All the same, after all, dealers in the villages buy up products and sell in the city.
  • Agriculture. Garden, garden. I will do this because I just like it. In childhood I worked in the parent garden, so I have an idea. But again - without fanaticism. There is no desire to bend back all summer, weeding 6 acres of potatoes But planting a hundred potatoes and having fresh ones all the time from the garden is yes. Tomatoes, cucumbers and all that.

Sony PlayStation 4 Full review

The release of the new generation of PlayStation is definitely one of the most important events of the year. Consoles are updated less frequently. Previous PlayStation 3 was released seven years ago in 2006 and sold in the amount of 90 million units. In the US, PS4 sales have already begun, and as usual, the guys from iFixit first disassembled the console to look inside and evaluate its maintainability.

Attention, there are a lot of photos in the article! Full-size photos are opened by clicking.


Iron console did not disappoint. Unfortunately, without backward compatibility. Games of the previous generation can be played through the Gaikai cloud, which Sony acquired last year.

To parse the console, you will need the following tools:
  • T9 Torx Security Screwdriver
  • Phillips # 0 Screwdriver
  • Phillips # 1 Screwdriver
  • Tweezers


Specifications:

  • 8-core AMD "Jaguar" x86-64 CPU
  • 1.84 TFLOPS, AMD Radeon GPU
  • 8 GB GDDR5 RAM
  • 500 GB HDD
  • 802.11 b / g / n Wireless and Bluetooth 2.1
  • USB 3.0 + Ethernet 10/100/1000


To complete with the console go:
  • DualShock 4 Gamepad
  • Power cable
  • HDMI cable
  • microUSB cable
  • Mono headset with clip





The console itself has an attractive simple design. On the front panel is a slot-loading Blu-ray / DVD drive. Also we have two USB 3.0 connectors.

HDD is easy to access. Sony has allowed users to change the hard drive without losing the warranty.




Removing the cover, we see a hard disk from HGST (Western Digital) with a capacity of 500 GB, 5400 RPM, SATA II. HDD keeps only 1 screw. You can replace the hard drive yourself. Disk requirements: thickness up to 9.5 mm and a volume of at least 160 GB. Unfortunately, PS4 does not support external drives via USB, which limits the use of the console as a media center.




Peel off the warranty stickers and unscrew the torx-screws.






As you can see, for cooling the radiator meets the 85-mm fan, designed specifically for the PS4.


The massive power supply is located at the rear of the console and is a separate module. The power supply is universal, works in the range from 100 to 240V.














After removing the power supply, a lot of space is freed up in the case and the cooling system radiator appears to the overview.


Unscrew the screws Blu-ray / DVD drive and remove it. Unfortunately, PS4 is not compatible with the games of the previous generation of PlayStation. The console does not even play the usual AudioCD, but soon promise an update that will correct this misunderstanding. We look forward to 2014, when Sony will allow to play games from the PS3 through the cloud Gaikai.

Open source control panel, suitable for any flying gadgets

At Indiegogo there is a collection of money for the revision and release of a universal control panel for quad-and more kopter, airplanes and other flying toys. Among the main differences of this device are called open source code and very wide possibilities for customization. The developers have set the bar at 65 thousand euros, and already collected almost 3 thousand of them.



Control with a smartphone or tablet is, of course, good. But it is not particularly convenient, which most Ar.Drone 2.0 owners will probably confirm. According to the developers who have been working on OSRC over the past three years, their console gives much more features and is already thoroughly run-in for fly lovers.

Unlike other consoles for sale today, OSRC will be sold through 3D printing, which will greatly reduce its cost - the buyer himself will be engaged in assembly.

The remote control allows you not only to adjust the parameters, but also easily switch from one aircraft to another.

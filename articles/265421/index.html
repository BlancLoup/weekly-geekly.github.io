HostGator vs Bluehost. Which one is better?


Suppose you are looking for a good web hosting company that can host a site according to your requirements. That is why we decided that a comparison of some leading hosting sites will help readers make a reasoned choice. One such comparison would be HostGator vs. Bluehost.

Noor B is a well-known web hosting blogger and analyst. Since 2006, he has tested a variety of web hosting services and published the results in his popular blog: InlineHostBlogger.com . To create each review, he personally or with the help of a team of specialists carefully checks every web hosting company.

What are we talking about?


HostGator and Bluehost are among the ten best web hosting sites of today. Although both companies have a good reputation, yet their differences are manifested in the provision and quality of services offered on these platforms.

Below is a comparative description of hosting and a number of different services provided by these companies.

Comparison of Bluehost and HostGator includes:
  • Bluehost and HostGator performance
  • Availability report
  • Site loading speed
  • Price, plans and features
  • Control Panel
  • Quality support

Performance


Hostgator in the protection of user data provides several levels of network security, and also has modern data centers with redundant capacity. Hostgator, like Bluehost, uses AMD Opteron 6000 processors and DDR3 ECC RAM.
Several tests were conducted to determine the performance of these companies and the following results were obtained.

HostGator and Bluehost Availability Report

Both HostGator and Bluehost guarantee uptime (site availability time) close to 99.9%. With the help of HostTreker , a popular tool for tracking website accessibility from different parts of the world, we are now tracking the uptime of several hosting companies. For this purpose, about 150 sites were monitored. I bring uptime of the website hosted under HostGator and Bluehost over the past few months.

Which control panel is better?


HostGator and Bluehost use a control panel called cPanel, which contains about 50 ready-to-use scripts, such as WordPress and Joomla. It is a big plus for non-professionals who want to create a new site.
Winners: Hostgator and Bluehost. Well built control panel that provides simplicity and good access.

Price, tariff plans and features


This section was edited during translation, since since the original publication the companies managed to slightly change the billing policy, which influenced the conclusions.

Hostgator previously offered quality hosting at a price of $ 3.96 / month. At the moment, the service has risen in price and costs from $ 5.56 / month, and this is subject to a three-year subscription, and the minimum tariff with a monthly payment is $ 8.76 / month. There are three plans, namely Hatchling, Baby and Business Plan. The first plan supports only one domain, and the other two plans offer an unlimited number of domains.

Bluehost hosting tariffs previously started from $ 4.95 / month. The service package includes features such as: unlimited traffic and one free domain registration for one year. Now the service costs from $ 5.99 / month, there is also a promotional price of $ 3.95 / month (the price for the first period of use).

Winner: Bluehost. Both provide their services at an affordable price. Nevertheless, we see that the market has put everything in its place: better (as a result of the above tests) Hostgator raised the price more than twice, while Bluehost went for a softer price increase, using stocks to lure customers.

Customer support


Hostgator provides a high level of interaction with customers, thanks to Live-chat, which operates 24/7/365, subscription systems, as well as the availability of a free phone for US residents. In the past, the company has been criticized because of the long wait on the line, but recent large investments in their support team have solved this problem. Now their waiting time does not exceed one minute.

Bluehost has high-quality and very detailed video tutorials, as well as an online chat, whose team responds very quickly. However, their subscription sales system requires many improvements. For example, a customer may wait more than one day to get an initial response from technical support. Handling a complex issue turned out to be a serious challenge for the technical support team.

Winner: Hostgator. An experienced customer support team that provides fast response and better customer service.

Hostgator vs Bluehost: my final verdict


Our review shows that both hosting - quality. However, we recommend choosing Hostgator. Yet, website performance is of utmost importance. If your hosting does not return web pages with sufficient speed, then the quality of other services you provide no longer matters. The company's investment in advanced technologies is easy to see in their data centers, which have several levels of fault tolerance. At the moment, Bluehost wins only due to a lower price.

Based on the results of the tests listed above, we can confidently recommend web hosting company Hostgator to you.

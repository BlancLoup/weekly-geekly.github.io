iFolder service resumed its work

At about one in the morning on March 20, the iFolder file-sharing service resumed its work.

Throughout the day on March 19, law enforcement agencies conducted a search for the necessary files on the iFolder servers. After the files were found and copied, Agava employees were allowed to resume the project.

I would like to say words of gratitude to the Office of the BSTM Ministry of Internal Affairs of Russia. With their direct participation, the investigation process got off the ground and was completed in one day.

Currently, all service features are available to users. On the occasion of resolving the problems of the last two days, almost all advertising has been removed from the service, the download speed and file size has been increased. In this way, Agava wants to thank users for their understanding and patience.

We received a lot of letters with the words of support and read a lot of caring blog entries. This demonstrates how important iFolder‚Äôs non-stop work is. And yet - about how relevant at the moment is the problem of interaction between hosting companies and owners of web-projects with law enforcement agencies. It remains for us to hope that this incident will allow us to avoid a repetition of similar situations in the future, and law enforcement officers will begin to treat the interests of the IT company and the needs of Internet users with greater understanding.

Open appeal to the President of the Russian Federation Dmitry Anatolyevich Medvedev

Dear Dmitry Anatolyevich, thank you so much for your attention to our industry. We are sure that without your support the problem would not have been solved so quickly. Thanks you.

I would like to note that the main damage caused by these actions affected not only us and our clients, but our country as a whole. Such actions of law enforcement agencies lead to the fact that it is becoming more and more willing to place Internet resources abroad, and not in Russia. And, it means that servers, jobs and the corresponding financial flows are leaking abroad.

We believe that in the current situation a similar incident can happen at any time with any hosting provider. It is necessary to bring the legal framework in line with modern realities of work on the Internet. Closing iFolder once again showed that any investigative actions can and should be carried out without stopping sites. If the state is interested in our proposals in this area, we, like all the hosting community in general, and representatives of our company in particular, are ready to participate in the work on the development of appropriate changes.

Congratulations, I consider it a merit of the Russian Internet community.

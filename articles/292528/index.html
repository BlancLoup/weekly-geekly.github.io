Messengers: a social platform for companies

The last few years have changed the business and the psychology of people in many ways. Mobile devices have become the mainstream media environment - in the US even television has already ceded to smartphones and tablets most of the time and they have become the number one medium in consumption. The same is observed in advertising, and in e-commerce, and in HR and in many other areas. People love the app, they not only create additional value for consumers and customers, but form special relationships between people and between companies.

The main symbol of the changes that the mobile ecosystem already produces is messengers. In the Top 10 most used applications, six are messengers - WhatsApp, Facebook Messenger, LINE, Viber, Kakao and WeChat, respectively. WhatsApp has an audience of 800 million users per month, and Facebook Messenger has 600 million. By the number of sessions, messengers occupy the first three places and also in the top ten of them six. People even stop calling - it's much easier to write. Communicating with other people, with companies, with services is much easier through the messenger - this is a global shift in the psychology of users, in their communication with each other and with the world. Messengers cease to be simply means of correspondence, these are already full-fledged social platforms that change both people and the world around.



Total mobile phones used 5.2 billion people, 73% of the population. Of these, 40% have smartphones. In fact, for most people in the world, the smartphone is the main ‚Äúwindow to the world‚Äù, this is the so-called mobile-only audience. On average, in various digital environments, an adult user in the US spends 5.6 hours a day, of which more than half of them on mobile devices is 2.8 hours (51%).


So how can a messenger help a company? First of all, communication via instant messenger creates a much greater degree of involvement. The client does not correspond with the bot assistant, not with a distant email character, but with a live person who is present in his messenger with his mom, dad, friend and colleagues. This is a very fine line, but the presence of a channel or just a person of a company in the messenger can repeatedly improve the user experience.

In the second place, you can answer the speed of communication. Mail and social networks is a one-way trip. The user does not know who will answer him and when. Even the largest groups in social networks can not guarantee the instant response that the customer and the consumer need here and now. People write and leave, waiting (or not waiting) for an answer to the outside, because the very paradigm of communication does not provide for instant interaction. With the messenger everything is different - you see that the user is online, the user wants and can receive an instant answer to his questions.

Finally, many features of mobile devices can be used in instant messengers. Send photos just taken, share a location, send and receive money - all this has already been implemented and allows you to simplify communication.

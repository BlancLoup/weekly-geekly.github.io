A draft decree on restricting purchases of foreign radio electronics has been published.

As expected , the Ministry of Industry and Trade has developed a draft government decree that restricts purchases of foreign computer equipment for state and municipal needs. The text is published on the federal portal of draft regulatory legal acts.

The Ministry of Industry and Trade proposes to limit the purchase of "certain types of radio-electronic products originating from foreign countries": these are communications equipment, laptops, tablets, smartphones, computers, storage devices, diodes, integrated circuits, electronic books, cash registers, television cameras, telephone sets, antennas , lamps, illuminated signs, chandeliers, illuminated panels, display panels, headlights, lamps, turn indicators, etc. See the full list under the cat.

By analogy with the software import substitution program, the same principle applies here: state-owned enterprises will not be allowed to buy foreign goods if they have a Russian counterpart. But there is an interesting feature. According to the project, if the government customer was offered two or more products from different Russian manufacturers at the competition, then he is obliged to reject all offers from foreign vendors .

In the future, officials will develop criteria for Russian origin for each type of equipment. For example, there is already a register of communication equipment indicating the status of each product, a ministry spokesman said in a commentary to the Vedomosti newspaper.

Now Western corporations will have to think about localizing production.

Representatives of the domestic industry endorse the initiative. They explain that at the expense of price, it is very difficult to compete with foreign corporations, so it is very difficult for them to sell their goods without state assistance.

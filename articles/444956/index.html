What to choose


Bicycle does not happen much. That you will understand if there is a hobby . Since here we are considering more the option of riding to work, then you can ignore their specific types, leaving, IMHO, such options:

  • MTB. T. n. "Mountain bike. It happens only with a suspension fork in the front (hardtail), or it may also have a shock absorber for the rear wheel (‚Äútwo-suspension‚Äù). If on your way there is no insane amount of roots, deep dirt, terrifying type of asphalt, then you do not need it. If there is only a small area (two, three) with such conditions, then you still do not need it. Not only that, adequately working shock absorbers (they are of different types) cost quite serious money, they must also be properly serviced. And this is extra weight, which will be a separate chapter.
  • Highway It differs from MTB in a different frame geometry, lack of depreciation, and another wheel (ram), which gives a large variation of grip, adding comfort. His initial element is asphalt roads, but absolutely nothing will happen to him from light and small sections of average off-road.
  • Singlesped, in advanced cases, fix. Take, if you want to spend a minimum of money, but keep in mind that you should not go too fast on this type, these types have one speed (fixed transmission), take care of your knees. Fix differs from singlespid lack of free rotation of the wheel separately from the pedals. That is, if the wheel rotates, then you have to rotate the pedals. This can be traumatic, especially when going downhill. From the pluses I see only reduced weight and mechanics of reduced complexity.
  • Bicycle with a planetary sleeve. A very good option for those who are as easy as possible to drive. Without trying to achieve some good results. Lovely is the ‚Äúgearbox‚Äù hidden inside the rear wheel hub, which allows you to switch even while standing still (for example, for an easy start, if you forgot to drop the gear in advance). No less significant advantage is the simplification of the chain drive. Only two stars, as on singlespids, more durable and wear-resistant chain. Reliability, simplicity and extended service intervals. Sometimes the chain drive can be replaced with a belt drive, which makes it almost impossible to recall the transmission.
  • There are also Grevela, city bikes and hybrids. The author has practically no experience of riding on these species, therefore, he cannot say anything about them, remains at the mercy of self-study.

Weight of bike


And there are a lot of opinions, let's understand. In principle, the lighter the bike, the better, but there is a certain limit beyond which the price increase for such a device becomes very large, and the weight loss is very small. Also, many components of the ultralight (and ultra-expensive) class have a limit on the maximum weight of the rider (usually about 70-80 kilograms). The most important weight of the wheels. After all, the wheels - this is the flywheel, which you will need to unwind with each acceleration, and you will constantly have these accelerations. And the lighter this flywheel, the nicer you are. The weight of the frame and other components is not so important, but, you see, it is rather stupid to have good and light wheels and a ‚Äúcast iron‚Äù, heavy frame. Everything should be optimal and balanced. For myself, I consider the optimal MTB weight less than 12 kg and road mass less than 9 kg. But, I repeat, you should not chase the minimum, especially at the beginning of your cycle path. It is important to understand that it is not the bike itself that moves. The system moves from a cyclist and a bicycle. Their masses are folded, and the bigger it is, the less influence the mass of the bicycle itself has. Those who think that a bike of 20 kg is not much different from 12 kg will also be wrong. I have an MTB and a highwayman, so when I sit down between them, I feel a huge difference in dynamism and lightness, of course, not in favor of the first. And this difference is only about 6 kg, so the summary: do not neglect the weight of the bike, the lighter option will give you more pleasure and comfort, but do not raise it into a cult, remember that the most important is the mass of the wheels, the rest is less valuable.

How many speeds? Why so much?


At the time of the dawn of the 3rd system (three stars in front) the number of speeds in a bike could reach 33. Many, and not quite. In reality, of this amount, not more than a third is used (usually). What is important to us? It is important gear ratio in the transmission. And in the version with 33 speeds there were many ‚Äúoverlapping‚Äù, almost identical gear ratios, which were almost never used. In multi-speed transmissions of a bicycle, in normal skating it is not recommended to strongly twist the chain (for example, to put it on the front and rear largest stars) - this leads to increased wear of all transmission elements. Thus, a few more speeds from our set decrease. The result is that of the 33 possible installation options for the chain, you can use 8-12 reasonable. At the moment, I haven‚Äôt seen 3 stars in the front in new bikes, on MTB there is even a tendency to switch to one leading front star, which allows to get rid of the front switch, and on the road bike there are 2 optimal stars so far. Also, in the top transmission sets, one can more often see 12 speed variants (meaning the number of stars behind). For MTB, this allows you to increase the range of gear ratios, and for the roadmen to reduce the steps during the transition between the stars (sometimes it happens that the current gear is too high cadence (pedaling frequency), and the next, although lower, is a bit hard to turn). And also increases the available range of gear ratios, which allows you to go very slowly (with adequate cadence), and very quickly (as far as preparation will allow).

Saddle


First of all, after the first kilometers on a bicycle, many go to the store for a wide, puffy and soft saddle. Don't do that. Human anatomy is designed so that our skeleton rests when sitting on the ischial bones. Wide and soft saddles are only deceptively comfortable. On them you do not sit on the sciatic bones, but on soft tissues, getting unwanted processes in them that do not lead to anything good.

It is necessary to find out the distance between these bones, for which there are different ways, many of which are perfectly shown on YouTube. It is necessary to add about 20 mm to the obtained value and choose a saddle with approximately the same width. It should be quite tough, but not "wooden." And the first week, most likely, you will get used to it, you will probably be quite hurt. But you take it and sit down, soon everything will be fine and it will be wonderful. I started this way 3 seasons in a row, and then drove on one of the popular models, and fell in love with her. Bought, but still not fully rolled. I recommend to try the saddle with cut, I personally liked it.

Upgrade. If passion is what you want to improve your bike


My first upgrade was tire replacement. Initially, my MTB was a regular rubber with a developed tread. This is good for unstable soils, but those on my way became less and less. And I put the so-called. "Poluslik." It was a revelation to me! This is one of my best acquisitions.

Then I sat tight in the strava and replaced the bike computer. The new acquisition allowed me not to use the smartphone to record my pokatushek, and also to get the ability to connect various sensors, since the most interesting thing in cycling is to look at the numbers.
A speed sensor (based on reading the rpm from the wheel) and cadence (pedaling frequency) completed the picture. The speed, of course, was not new, but the cadence taught me how to pedal correctly (to maintain the health of the knee joints, it is advisable to rotate the pedals at a speed of at least 70 revolutions per minute).

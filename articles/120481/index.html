Your life in the form of a personal exhibition

Intel made a new application that works with Facebook. It creates a video of your personal exhibition, made from all the images, videos, links, etc. on Facebook. The created video is really fascinating, because this is all - pieces of your life, collected in one place.



Visit a special page under your Facebook account and enjoy a movie from the museum about yourself.


The clip uses information from all your pages on the social network. Starting from the name and surname, ending avatars of your friends and their connections between themselves.

The video sequence is created so that you see the exposition in the museum of your photos. People walk there and look at the pictures hanging on the walls.



There is even a graphic image of your location.



And in one of the virtual halls you can see the nicknames of everyone with whom you communicate.



There is even a thumb that you can "like".



Working with photos, which is carried out by a powerful Intel Core i5 processor, is truly awesome. Just imagine: in your albums there are hundreds and thousands of photos, there are even more links between you and your friends. And all this is processed in a few seconds right before your eyes.

What is IXcellerate data center


We have a cloud of Technoserv. This is a natural cloud for Russian customers, where there are segments certified by PCI DSS and in the class for ‚Äúheavy‚Äù PD and state information systems. We chose the second site as carefully as the first one. We needed T3 UI (more precisely, regulations from it), the ability to work with PCI DSS (that is, our own certificate for the physical protection of the data center), and a host of other features. As a result, we got up at the IXcellerate data center - this is where the Orange stands are bricked up inside one of the machine halls, there is a peering * one very large gaming company *, there are large banks and state-owned companies, Reuters and Nestle. The second feature - the head is not new. Far from new. Guy Wilner is an entrepreneur from England. He builds data centers is not the first and not the tenth time. Prior to the project, he owned his own network of 14 data centers in Western Europe, and then sold it to Equinix (data center number 1 operator in the world). After that, he decided to repeat the adventure and open a huge data center in Brazil, Russia or Turkey. What I am telling you all about is that the data center was built by a British entrepreneur with good European connections, so European customers immediately came inside. This means very clear reporting on all transactions, which gives some certainty that the processes and regulations within the data center are fully implemented.

The data center was built piece by piece, launching the machine hall at the machine hall. The first stage was at 220 pillars (they started building in 2012, and were put into operation in the middle of 2013). The second machine hall is 515 racks (1040 square meters, our first servers are there), and the third phase (where our core is) is 1100 racks (these are 2500 squares).

Three machines are independent of each other, but they are not three different data centers, because there is a cross-redundancy of part of the nodes. The first two machine halls are IBM L3 certified, the third one is uptime TIER III certified. In short, they are almost the same and the difference at the project level, for example, is that the UI needs dedicated parking for engineers, physical barriers for cars, somewhere 45 minutes of burning resistance (windows in the UI) against the hour (IBM), different sizes of doors and so on.



The master plan was made by the company Arup. The property has an Arup certificate for the best solution in the field of cooling and power supply. This is because the data center is commercial: the lower the PUE, the cheaper it is to exploit it. Therefore, they introduced everything in parts (that is, no equipment was idle, the engineer was purchased as it was scaled), and there was a struggle for every kilowatt. Now PUE is 1.15‚Äì1.4 depending on the season. The data center hosts quite a few Chinese customers, for whom with their approach to ecology and the use of electricity, these numbers are just space. They have the usual PUE can reach up to two.

At the time of the start of operation, the balance of customers they had about 50/50 domestic to foreign. At that time, everyone moved within the framework of the law on personal data protection and, as a result, on the Russian cloud infrastructure market (this is what we do) there was an increased demand for data centers (this is where cloud providers had to be located).

It is here that more and more companies from abroad are coming, because here stands a significant customer - Orange's foreign provider, which for many is both a vital reference point and a corporate standard. And the third machine building was financed by the Goldman Sachs Group company, which adds reliability to the project in the eyes of foreign managers. Physical protection

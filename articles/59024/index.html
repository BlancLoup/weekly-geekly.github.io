GraphiCon'2009: a set of articles will last until May 25

From October 5 to October 9 in Moscow on the territory of Moscow State University there will be a conference on computer graphics and machine vision GraphiCon'2009 (official site http://gc2009.graphicon.ru/ ). For the 19th time, recognized world-class scientists and novice experts will gather at the conference to share experiences, present their achievements to a wide range of stakeholders and communicate in an informal setting. GraphiCon is the largest scientific conference on graphics and computer vision in Russia and the CIS countries. In 2008, the event was attended by over 400 participants, including representatives from Italy, the USA, Germany, the Netherlands.



The conference highlights recent advances in such areas as:
  • graphics and multimedia;
  • image and video processing;
  • machine vision;
  • CG (Computer Graphics) and CV (Computer Vision) applications.
A detailed list is in the Conference section.

At the moment, announced a competitive selection of articles, which will last until May 25 . Articles written in English fall on the international part of the conference, and Russian articles will be presented in the framework of the ‚ÄúSchool of Young Scientists‚Äù. The following material formats are allowed: a full article (up to 8 pages), a short article (up to 4 pages), or abstracts for a poster. In 2009, the number of accepted articles was increased, and if the article is not passed through the contest as a full or short report, it can be accepted as a thesis and you can talk about it at the poster session.

All articles received are thoroughly checked by an international program committee . Notification of the results of the review of your article will be sent until July 27. Accepted articles are presented at the conference in the form of reports. Accepted posters are exhibited at the poster session in the form of A0 format posters. Both articles and abstracts of posters from both the international conference and the School of Young Scientists are published in the proceedings of the conference.



Graphicon'2009 will be held in two stages: the ‚ÄúSchool of Young Scientists‚Äù in the first two days and the sections of the international conference itself in the next three days. ‚ÄúSchool of Young Scientists‚Äù (previously held in summer and called ‚ÄúSummer School‚Äù), includes workshops, tutorials, poster sessions and lectures on fundamental, theoretical and practical issues of CG and CV. Visitors of the Summer School-2008 learned the practical art of presentations and technological entrepreneurship; Learned about 3D visualization in Autodesk Maya and programming GPU on CUDA technology from NVidia; We saw how robots are designed in practice and take part in the Intel quiz.

The second part of the "Graphicon" - an international conference - is held in English. It traditionally consists of the following activities:
  • full reports section;
  • short reports section;
  • poster session;
  • industrial presentations and multimedia demonstrations;
  • round-table meetings;
  • presentations by invited speakers.

Every year world scientists and representatives of famous companies are invited to participate in the conference. So, in 2008, Brian Barsky, a professor at UC Berkeley University, gave a presentation on Vision-simulated imaging among the invited speakers; Elena Shumilova, a company representative, spoke about the achievements in the field of educational technologies in Autodesk; Michael Doggett (AMD) described the Radeon graphics card architecture; and Alexander Chipizhko (Intel) shared with the audience the company's view on Visual Computing.

This year, Alexey Efros , an associate professor at Carnegie Mellon University, the creator of the technology for automatically removing defects from photographs, a method for reconstructing a 3D model from one photo, and algorithms for enhancing the realism of computer-generated images will be a guest speaker at the conference.

Graphicon'2009 is supported by the Eurographics Association, Intel Corporation and the Institute of Applied Mathematics. MV Keldysh RAS . The event is organized by the Faculty of Computational Mathematics and Cybernetics of Moscow State University .

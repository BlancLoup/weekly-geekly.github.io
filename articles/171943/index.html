CeBIT'13. Second day



Nomobile.ru continues to explore the CeBIT 2013 exhibition. Of course, there are not so many interesting new products for the end user, such as, for example, at the MWC that has just been held, but those who are looking will find it. For the second day, we shot a video about Drywired nanotechnology, protecting any gadget from water, protected by Panasonic CF-AX2 ultrabook, Fibaro smart home and reference laptop based on Intel Haswell (Northcape) platform.

Somehow it happened that in Russia the word nanotechnology basically causes a smile, but what if the way to protect smartphone electronics (and anything else at all) with a 40-nanometer polymer film was invented not in us, but in Los Angeles?


Panasonic showed off its Intel protected ultrabook CF-AX2, which can turn into a tablet in the style of Lenovo Yoga.


Fibaro at CeBIT 2013 is promoting its Z-Wave based smart home system. More expensive than the competition, but, they say, much more reliable and supports a couple of hundred modules without any delay.


And finally, a video on how a laptop based on the new Intel platform called Haswell, also known as Northcape, may look like.

Path to Machine Learning


The experts had two problems.

  • Expert opinion did not always coincide with the opinion of the interested person,
  • There were few experts and it was impossible to rake all their opinions into a heap for making one average decision.

Together, both problems did not give the possibility of a decision.

  • If there were a lot of experts, they could have been crushed by the amount of some option,
  • If there were few of them, but everyone‚Äôs opinion coincided with stakeholders, one could simply agree with the experts.

But the very formulation of the problem of expert opinion gave the key to the next iteration.

Must have a lot of ratings. If there are many of them, and all the votes are for something concrete, it is easier to accept it. But how to do that?

For example, to make a small desktop application that would apply random gamut variants to a picture, and interested people would rate these options from 0 to 10. Thus, more than 300 different color scores were compiled in the format:

 beginpmatrixr in[0,255]g in[0,255]b in[0,255]V in[0,10] endpmatrix

Mostly colors were rated at 0, of course.

Now we have a sample with estimates, although not very convenient for analysis. We can reformulate that V is from 0 to 10; this is actually not an estimate of the color, but the number of people who voted for this option. And transform 4-dimensional vector (r,g,b,V)T in v 3 dimensional (r,g,b)T . Naturally, the zeros disappear. And each vector is now an equal voice for the corresponding shade. It is convenient to add at the same time to the RGB values ‚Äã‚Äãof random small offsets, so that a bunch of very similar shades do not work out.

In this formulation, a typical formulation of the problem of finding the maximum density of a multidimensional distribution is obtained. Those. we are looking for an area in which people would ‚Äúvote‚Äù most often.

If we want to increase the influence of high marks, we can take V¬≤ when converting 4-d to 3-d. Then, the rating of 2 turns into 4 votes, and the rating of 4 into 16 votes. In this way, we can reduce the influence of those colors for which we voted, but not directly.

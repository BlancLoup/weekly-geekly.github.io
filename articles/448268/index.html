Box Telephone Systems


Boxed IP PBXs are also known as on-premise IP PBXs. Usually box PBXs are placed on the object - in a server or in a shield box. The data from the IP phones goes to the IP PBX server via the LAN. Calls can go both through the telephone operator and in the form of VoIP through a SIP trunk. Gateways can be used to connect the system to traditional telephone networks.

Costs for VoIP-providers and manufacturers are reduced thanks to boxed open source PBXs like Asterisk. This allows users to access state-of-the-art technologies and the latest features much cheaper than in the past.

Here are three stories of the creation of telephone networks based on a boxed PBX from the experience of very different organizations - a manufacturing company, a bank and a university.

VoIP systems have always competed with solutions based on traditional PBXs, and therefore they have a wide range of functions. The advantages of boxed PBX:

  • Rich functionality - a set of opportunities is wider than that of traditional PBX, and the possibilities themselves are higher.
  • SIP - with the integration of a SIP trunk, you can access free calls and IP calls, which reduces costs compared to using traditional phone lines.
  • Ownership - you get a tangible, your own system.
  • Lack of points of failure - a lot of traditional and SIP lines are used to route calls. Thus, the failure of one of the lines will not affect the performance of the network.
  • Unified Communications - Box PBXs are capable of serving not only telephone calls. Their capabilities include instant messaging, voice conferencing and video messaging.

Example 1. Fitesa Germany


Fitesa is a manufacturer of nonwovens used in sanitary, medical, and industrial applications. Ten divisions of Fitesa are located in eight countries of the world, headquarters - in the USA. Fitesa Germany was founded in 1969 in the city of Peine, Lower Saxony.

Task


Fitesa was not satisfied with the existing telephone system - which required a lot of investment, inflexible and did not meet the technical and functional requirements.

The company wanted to find a modern, flexible and scalable solution for servicing 30 thousand m 2 of office, warehouse and production premises. This solution was supposed to allow you to independently administer the system, reconfigure and remotely support IP phones. A system was required that could be easily integrated into the existing VMWare environment and provided all the available areas with mobile coverage. Also, the system had to support integration with Outlook and a single number assignment scheme, whereby any employee can be reached by the same extension number regardless of location. Great importance was attached to the intuitive clarity of the system and the possibilities of automatic configuration and administration. Finally, the costs should have remained at an acceptable level.

Decision


Fitesa was pleased with its existing supplier: the company Bel Net from Braunschweig was invited to do not only the integration of a modern telephone system, but also all the necessary electrical work.

Bel Net has analyzed whether it is possible to cover all company facilities with a DECT network. On the basis of the UCware server, a flexible and high-performance IP PBX was created with expansion modules for the mobile network and Outlook. Panasonic DECT telephones and 40 Snom 710 and Snom 720 IP telephones were installed in offices and in the production area.

In order not to interrupt workflows, the active telephone system continued to function during testing. The final solution was launched in January after hours. To familiarize 40 key users with a new PBX and telephones, a two-hour seminar was held. And those, in turn, passed the knowledge to colleagues.

Benefits


The new IP PBX allowed not only to reduce the cost of operation, but also made the telephony system flexible and scalable, it can be managed without the involvement of external specialists. Snom phones can be controlled via the web interface, and remote configuration can be performed using the Auto Provision feature.

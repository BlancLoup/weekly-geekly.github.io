Exynos 5 - Samsung's new 8-core chip

Today at CES 2013, Samsung Electronics officially announced the Exynos 5 Octa processor. The chip is made on the basis of 28-nm process technology HK-MG. It includes two clusters in which there are 4 cores of ARM Cortex-A15 and Cortex-A7. In fact, ARM Cortex-A15 is a high-performance core, and ARM Cortex-A7 is economical and energy-efficient. Clusters interact with each other thanks to technology big.LITTLE.



According to Samsung, the new chip has become not only powerful, but also more economical in terms of power. Exynos 5 is far superior to the old Exynos chip, which is installed in the Google Nexus 10 tablet, which is one of the fastest Android devices. In the market of mobile processors, Samsung takes the 2nd place in the world with a share of 27%. The first is Qualcomm with 42% of the market.

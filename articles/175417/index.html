ZTE V987 - 4-core smart phone with a great screen



So, a new stage of development of Chinese smartphones on the new Mediatek MT6589 platform began. The parade was opened by brands focused on the domestic market (THL, Zopo, UMI), but over time, models from more famous manufacturers began to appear. In this review we look at one of the new products from the company ZTE - ZTE V987 smartphone. The main feature of which is the use of high-quality 5-inch OGS screen with a resolution of 1280x720 pixels.

‚ñ∫ Specifications

Screen: 5 ", OGS, 1280x720 IPS
Processor: Mediatek MT6589 (Cortex-A7 1.2GHz, 4 cores) + GPU: PowerVR SGX544
RAM: 1GB
User memory: 4GB + microSD slot with card support up to 64 GB
Camera: front 1MP, rear - 8MP
Connectivity: 2 sim cards (GSM + WCDMA), GPS, Bluetooth, Wifi (802.11 b / g / n)
Battery: 2500mAh
Dimensions: 141 x 70 x 9.2 mm

‚ñ∫ Software


Android 4.1.2 is preinstalled with minimal changes from ZTE.
The changes only hit the unlock screen. The phone is unlocked by long pressing the screen.


The firmware language is only English and Chinese. But soon this problem will be solved, one of the enthusiastic developers has already received its ZTE V987 and has already begun work on a multilingual firmware build.

Working with 2 SIM cards is very simple: when dialing a number in the status bar, you can choose a card for calls by default, or set up a mode in which a SIM card with which you need to make a call will be requested.
Full settings of SIM cards are in the section Settings-> SIM management. In it, you can disable one of the SIM cards, configure the default card for calls, sms and data transfer. Choose a behavior in roaming, as well as you can "bind" contacts to a particular sim card.

‚ñ∫ work time

ZTE V987 has a 2500 mAh battery installed. On the one hand, this is not so much, for example, the Jiayu G3 has a 2750 mAh battery, and the Lenovo P770 has even more - 3500 mAh.
But on the V987 side, there is an advantage in the form of an energy-efficient processor of the Cortex-A7 architecture. Let's see what results we get in Antutu Battery Tester:

First place in the ranking with 993 points! Of course, this is less than the Lenovo P770 (1257 points), but the result is really good.
With active work with the screen, the smartphone will work for 2-3 days. If you often play games, the battery will last for 1 day. With minimal use (a few minutes of talking per day, sms, 10-15 minutes of Wifi, some games), my smartphone was able to last as many as 7 days.
Self-discharge per night is 1-2%.

HTC Desire - transition from WM to Android

Somehow, unexpectedly, I got HTC Desire, now I'm trying to move it from HTC Max 4G. If anyone is too lazy to read, then I will immediately write a conclusion: the device itself is very pleasant, the OS is decent and promising, though without a normal stylus (personally I) is bad. But with the application software is still a problem, sometimes you have to shaman. Although in the end, more or less tolerable replacements used to pick up the software failed.




After buying Desire, it turned out that there are only free applications in the market, and the paid ones must either be tried to download from the developer sites, or get cracked. Therefore, we had to attend to getting the root - only for the sake of access to the paid market (although in the end a couple of nice buns were added, such as the ability to make a complete dump of the phone‚Äôs memory or disable ads in applications). The native firmware with the root did not normally stand up, I patched the Pays-ROM . The choice, of course, is not the most correct, for good it was worth taking MoDaCo - it is closer to the original one, but the Pays-ROM was downloaded earlier. :) A few days after I installed the firmware, the official 2.2 was released. I wandered around for a long time in circles in meditations or not, then I saw a sensible whatsnew, read it and decided. At first I tried Pays-ROM based on 2.2, but did not like it, rolled back. And the version from MoDaCo (r7) completely arranged for me - based on the original 2.2, some unnecessary programs were removed, a root was added. And that's all. Stotyaschk additional programs are not installed - then add myself.

I like the attitude of Google to programs that require root - in the market they are present on a common basis. Also in the market there is a very convenient feature - a refund for the program during the day. In the Apple store, it is greatly lacking.

Tasks:
1. Reading fb2, fb2.zip, txt, html, doc directly from the file system. In WM alreader coped with this. Explanatory substitutions have not yet found. Now there are three pieces.

fbreader does not read txt, doc and html, does not work directly with the file system, but scans the flash drive for the presence of supported files. But reads fb2.
ireader (free) long opens large html-files, does not store them in history, does not remember the position. There is no normal hyphenation - Russian spells, English - just weird. Problems with the definition of cp1251 - considers it different versions of Unicode. Sometimes crashes. There is no special reason to buy a paid one, unless you need DRM support.
conreader reads only txt, and Russian and English spells, works faster than ireader, until it crashes. But the open file dialog is inconvenient, there is no text search.

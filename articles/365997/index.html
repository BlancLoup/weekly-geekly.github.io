The main astronomical event of 2015 will be a total solar eclipse on March 20 , the largest phase of which in Russia can be observed in the Murmansk region on the border with Norway.

A total lunar eclipse will occur at the full moon on April 4. The inhabitants of Siberia, Primorye, Kamchatka and the Far East will see the full phase. And on September 28, the inhabitants of the European part of Russia will see the Moon plunged into the Earth‚Äôs shadow.

In addition, in September, the brightest asteroid of the year , Vesta, will appear in the sky , accessible for observation with the naked eye. In the morning sky of December comet Catalina will be visible .

If you want to see a meteor shower destroying the Earth from the first row or simply admire the beauty of the Milky Way, you will need a high altitude, a dry climate and a complete lack of sources of artificial lighting. Top 5 places to observe the sky




The Pulkovo Observatory of St. Petersburg is considered the main astronomical observatory of the Russian Academy of Sciences. During excursions, including night, you can get acquainted with the 26-inch refractor telescope, the Big Pulkovo radio telescope and the Solar telescope (one of the largest in Europe).

The light pollution map clearly shows that viewing the starry sky in Moscow is quite problematic. In the capital there are folk observatories in Gorky Park and Sokolniki, but the main point of attraction of all urban star worshipers is the astronomical site of the Moscow Planetarium.



But keep in mind that the highlight of the metropolis allows you to see well only large objects of the solar system, and to look into deep space will have to go on a long journey.



An important place, about which we could not say - the Crimean Astrophysical Observatory . The largest number of telescopes among the observatories of the former USSR is located on its territory.

Does Mint Wireless Display (WiDi) work?

On Intel WiDi technology, we have repeatedly written in the blog Intel . WiDi support is stated in the Mint functionality list, however, in the test of the communicator on the mobile-review site, Eldar Murtazin mentions that he could not get it to work:
It was not possible to make this technology work under Windows 7 and MacBook Air 2012. After going through all the computers at home, he waved his hand. I do not think that this proprietary technology will be in demand by anyone.
Let's look at this issue - and there are actually two questions here.
  1. Can I transfer images from my device to a laptop or PC using WiDi? The answer is negative. WiDi is a one-way technology for transferring images from a laptop (with defined hardware) to a TV or set-top box. A laptop cannot act as a WiDi signal receiver, only as a transmitter. Therefore, the experience of Eldar was doomed to failure. Note also that the WiDi software only works under the Windows 7 operating system, so the MacBook Air doesn‚Äôt snap into WiDi.
  2. Is the WiDi interface in MegaFon Mint working? The answer is yes. The performance of WiDi was demonstrated directly at the launch of Mint, when the video captured on the communicator was displayed on the plasma panel using WiDi. Moreover, to re-check, we conducted an experiment with the apparatus, which fell into our hands. Everything went without a hitch, without a hitch - exactly as in the video below. Only in Russian. How are things going with Mint videos and games? Will Mint update Android to ICS?

These questions are not combined by chance.
Unfortunately, today many video formats are not played on Mint due to the lack of codecs. Installing third-party players and codec libraries does not solve the problem, since they are all written under the ARM architecture and work with Intel, and even less do not know how to use its advantages. So please do not try to install programs like MX Player - now you know why they do not work. What are the benefits of the Intel Atom processor ? He can hardware decode a number of video formats (MPEG4.2, H.234, WMV & VC1). When using these formats, the quality of video playback increases dramatically, and power consumption decreases. Pre-installed in MegaFon Mint, the solution that allows the use of hardware decoding is a doubleTwist player. The player's agent installed on the PC, when transmitting video to the communicator, decodes the video on the fly into a format that is digestible for the device. The result can be seen with the naked eye - you can try.


DoubleTwist player interface

The problem will be solved from the other side. Upgrading Android to version 4 (ICS) will increase the number of video containers supported by the system. MegaFon Mint with Android ICS on board already exists in nature and is currently being tested. The update will be available to users this fall ; It should ease the situation with the video.
With games, the situation is as follows. We have put up with a dozen of the most popular games from Market - they all work fine and do not slow down. There are those that do not work - many have already written about this. These are games with heavy three-dimensional graphics, in their hardware requirements it is written in large letters: "processor - only ARMv7". It is strange to expect them to go to Intel. Let's wait until the games are adapted to the new platform. By the way, someone asked if Mint is ported to Android Quake 1, yes. It works fine, but the picture does not stretch to full screen.

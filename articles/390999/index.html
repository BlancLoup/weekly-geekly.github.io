Robots create jobs



Robotics will become the main tool for creating new jobs in the next 5 years. This opinion is based on the study "The positive impact of industrial robots on employment", conducted by Metra Martech .

It noted that the million industrial robots that are now engaged in manufacturing create about three million jobs, and a further increase in their number will lead to the creation of another million jobs for highly skilled employees around the world in the next 5 years. Mostly new jobs will be created in industries such as consumer electronics, food, alternative energy (solar and wind) and the creation of new types of batteries.

Industrial robots save production sites and millions of jobs


In addition to the fact that a million new jobs are expected to appear, the authors of the study also point out that existing jobs will also be saved. This means that restaurants, shops and services will benefit from this situation.

It is also noted that in the period from 2000 to 2008, employment in the industrial sector increased in almost all industrialized countries. Now a similar trend is observed in China , Brazil and other countries in transition, where more and more robots are being used. Over the above period, their number in Brazil has increased almost 4 times, and productivity and employment - by 20%. According to a study, employment in manufacturing is higher in countries where investment in robotics continues.

Important aspects of the growing number of robots being introduced


Peter Gorle, one of the authors of the study, noted three important aspects of the growth in the number of robots being introduced, namely:
  • Robots operate in areas where people are unsafe to work.
  • Robots perform tasks that are not economically viable with high wages.
  • Robots perform tasks that humans cannot do.


Marlin Steel, Baltimore, USA, is an excellent illustration of the research conducted in terms of using robots in an unsafe human environment. Many years ago, she began automation, the benefits of which are felt not only by the company, but also by employees.

Drew Greenblatt, director of Marlin Steel, acquired the company in 1998. Then the staff received $ 6 per hour and manually produced 300 parts per hour. ‚ÄúIt was a boring and monotonous job, unsafe and with poor product quality. Today, our employees receive $ 25-30 per hour plus bonuses and bonuses. Each worker follows 4 robots that produce 20,000 parts per hour, and their quality exceeds all expectations. Last year was one of the most successful in our history: we exported our products to more than 30 countries and increased the number of employees 4 times. Thanks to the use of robots, work has become interesting and safe. ‚Äù

Odense Steel Shipyard, Denmark is a good example of how robots save jobs in high-wage countries. Shipbuilding in Europe has experienced a decline in the past few years, but robots have become the key to improving the performance of the Odense Steel Shipyard in Denmark. The company has invested in autonomous robotic welding systems, thanks to which it has increased its efficiency 6 times in comparison with manual welding, has speeded up the production process several times and has increased the quality of the product.

The report also says that the growth of technology industries, such as pharmaceuticals, electronics and semiconductors, depends largely on robots that provide opportunities for improving quality, accuracy, speed and consistency, which could not be achieved with manual work.

Thus, the sphere of robotics can be one of the first in the number of new jobs .

Rosatom has manufactured fuel elements for a spacecraft nuclear reactor



At TVEL's Mashinostroitelny Zavod enterprise (PJSC MSZ, Elektrostal), the first experimental batch of fuel elements (fuel rods) of a nuclear power propulsion system for long-distance space flights was accepted . During acceptance, we checked the documentation and the condition of the fuel rods themselves - dimensions, appearance, tightness, etc.

The development of the megawatt class power propulsion system began in 2010, and the design allows manufacturing 10 MW engines using this technology. The Rosatom intends to submit a prototype of the nuclear power plant that has no analogues in the world in 2018.

Russian experts believe that the nuclear engine is best suited for sending spacecraft to Mars and to other planets, as well as for orbital missions, etc.

The nuclear reactor of the transport and energy installation heats the gas, it turns the turbine, generating electricity for an engine with specific gravity 20 times higher than the chemical one. The power propulsion unit consists of a power unit with turbomachine energy conversion based on the Brighton gas-dynamic cycle and a bunch of electric propulsion engines. The power unit is a single-circuit nuclear power plant based on a high-temperature gas-cooled reactor.

The use of a nuclear reactor as an energy source increases the power of spacecraft 30 times and reduces fuel consumption per unit of weight by an order of magnitude, said Anatoly Koroteyev, general director of the Keldysh Center.

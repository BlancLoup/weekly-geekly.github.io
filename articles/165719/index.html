Find an idea: the ideal object

Describing one of the Power Mac computers, Ive said: ‚ÄúWe wanted to get rid of everything that could be done without. In TRIZ (Theory of Inventive Problem Solving ), developed by Soviet inventor Heinrich Altshuller, is one of the basic concepts - the concept of an ideal object.
The concept is formulated very simply - an ideal object - when there is no object, and its function is performed.
Ideality of an object is both a way of thinking and a tool for solving various tasks. The methodology of activating creativity through the conscious striving for the ideal arose as an alternative to imperfect selection of options. The mental activity of this knowledge is aimed at finding an idea in an obvious, rather than random, way.

Of course, ideality does not need to be taken literally. Ideality is the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãideality, direction, approaching the ideal. On the way to the ideal, it is necessary to strive to get rid of unnecessary, unnecessary, inefficient elements. At the same time, it is important to remember that an object should be improved so that the system does not lose its meaning, the functionality does not decrease and the overall system efficiency increases.

Now I come to the most important thing, how it works. It works like this: the idealization of the system is achieved by reducing the individual parts of the system, by combining the functions of one or several objects in one object. This technique is called coagulation. Altshuller defined the collapsed system in this way - when one object performs several functions.

The easiest way to understand dry theory is through examples. What I do.
A good example for understanding the coagulation technique is an example of visualization of the battery charge level and the signal level in the Nokia 3310 phone. On the left is the antenna icon with bricks, on the right is a battery with bricks.



It is obvious that the bricks can be combined with the antenna and the battery without losing meaning. But this evidence was not obvious to the Nokia developers. Model 3310 released in 2000. Maybe something has changed over time? Let's look at one of the latest flagships of the company - E7, released in 2011.


It can not be again bricks, though a little combed. Obviously, this circumstance led to a decrease in the market share of Nokia. That is what such negligence in ignorance of the method of coagulation leads to. Joked of course.

The developers of the iPhone did not follow the example of Nokia and did it differently, namely, they combined the building blocks with the metaphor of a battery and an antenna.



What is the result: the system did not lose its sense when combining the functions, the functionality did not decrease, and the efficiency increased - the battery level metaphor increased the degree of natural conformity.

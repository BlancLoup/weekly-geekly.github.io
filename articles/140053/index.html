Analyze this or what we do online

Probably, everyone faced such a situation: in the evening you sit at the computer with the intention to send a couple of letters, and you leave the monitor only when it starts to light outside the window. And the first question that comes to mind at that moment: damn it, and what have I been doing all this time on the Internet ?! The funny thing is that the exact answer usually does not happen: it seems, I read it a little, looked here, went there ... And the time allotted for a sound and healthy sleep, was irrevocably gone. However, now there is a means to avoid this. More precisely, to meet the dawns, you still will not stop, but at least you will know where the precious hours were spent aimlessly. And all this thanks to the expansion of "Web Timer" , a brief description of which is under the cut.

In fact, there is no need to specifically describe it: the results of the extension are presented both in the form of a visual graph and in tabular form, and the interface is fairly simple and intuitive. Since the launch of the extension, it will track the time and frequency of your visits to various web resources, and then display statistics on the screen. You can view statistics for the current day, for all the expansion time and on average for each day. You can specify the number of resources monitored individually (by default, there are seven), other websites will fall into the ‚ÄúOther‚Äù category. You can also specify in the extension settings a list of websites that do not require tracking and should be excluded from general statistics.

Additionally, in the settings there is a button for resetting statistics, which will allow you to track your activity in one session. Another interesting point: the extension suspends the calculation of the time you spend on the site, if the active work with the page stops.

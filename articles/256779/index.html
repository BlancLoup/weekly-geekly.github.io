About duties


In the work of the product manager, the main thing is understanding the trends and ‚Äúlistening to the market‚Äù, so analytics and research are an important part of the work. It can be said that each release should receive evidence of the relevance of the functional, and not be based on the personal opinion of anyone. For mobile products (due to short development cycles), the set of such confirmations is shortened, but in any case, the development vector is formed in the form of a product concept, which includes:
  • The objectives of the product;
  • KPI;
  • Description of the target market;
  • Target audience;
  • Competitors;
  • Main features;
  • Key benefits of ABBYY;
  • Primary positioning;
  • Roadmap

There are a lot of sources of information for its creation (I would say crazy a lot):
  • Bulletins and reports ( IDC , Gartner and others like them, sometimes we buy good research);
  • Research and patent databases ( Google Scholar , Google Patent Search , patft.uspto.gov )
  • Research applications of competitors (what features, what reviews, popularity, updates, mentioning in the media)
  • Analysis Trends Trends ( Google Trends )
  • Sales statistics (both of our products and competitors)
  • Surveys and interviews with potential clients (we use our group in FB, send out pushes to users with an offer to participate)
  • User reviews (in the stores, in social networks)
  • Requests to technical support (our technical support forwards important messages and suggestions of users)
  • Technological research (recognition quality, image filter quality, crop quality, etc.)
  • Specialized forums and communities (where users live, often very interesting)
  • Usability research (Usually we do it ourselves, we put the application on the device, we give several tasks and ask to do it, thinking it out loud. We record the general video and the video from the device using AirServer and MyMobiler )
  • Analytics in applications (very important part, we use Google Analytics and Flurry )

We participate in the main mobile events. Were this year at Mobile Congress, last year at WWDC, and I was honored to get on Google IO 2014. It was very cool to dive into the world android environment, we made a lot of useful contacts. Highly recommend.

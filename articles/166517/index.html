Add air! Basics of visual design for graphics, web and multimedia

We present to your attention one of the brightest (in all senses, since this book is full-color) the novelties of our publishing house, published in January 2013 - the book by American designers Kim Golombinski and Rebecca Hagen, dedicated to the concept of the so-called. Viskom (visual communication).



Title: Add air! Basics of visual design for graphics, web and multimedia (English: White Space is Not Your Enemy: A Graphic, Web and Multimedia Design)
Authors: Kim Golombinski, Rebecca Hagen
Release date: January 2013

Written in easy language and free from complex professional terms and theoretical concepts, this book is a clear practical manual on graphic and web design and layout, sufficient to master the necessary principles and methods of the designer in any of the formats: from printed products to the web.

The book is beautifully illustrated, printed on coated paper and includes topics such as:
  • basics of effective communication design, communicating to the viewer the necessary message
  • preparation for work with the design project
  • 13 sins of typesetting
  • typography basics
  • work with color
  • styling for web, video and presentations
  • infographics
  • storyboard (visual storytelling)
  • work with multi-media components
  • preparation for printing
  • using the basics of vizkom in web design
  • and much more (detailed content here )

The book deals with three main topics to which the authors constantly return in the course of the narration:
  1. Effective graphic design does four things: it attracts attention, controls eye movements, conveys information and evokes emotions.
  2. Any design rests on three pillars: visual objects, font, and negative space.
  3. Beginner designers must first learn the standard, common to all rules, before becoming eligible to break them.

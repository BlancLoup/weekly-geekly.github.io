Salaries in IT in the first half of 2019: according to the salary calculator My Circle



We publish a report on salaries in the IT industry for the 1st half of 2019. The report is based on the data of the My Circle salary calculator: in which more than 7000 salaries were collected for this period.

Let's look at current salaries for all major IT specializations, as well as their dynamics over the past six months, both in general and in the main regions: Moscow, St. Petersburg, and other cities. As usual, we will study in more detail the specializations of software developers: look at their salaries in the context of programming languages, cities and companies.

The data presented in our current report, as well as any other, everyone can get independently using the salary calculator "My Circle". If you like the information that we receive using the calculator, and if you want to contribute to the formation of a more transparent labor market in IT, we invite you to share your current salary , which we use data about in our next annual report.

Salary service was launched at My Circle at the end of 2017 with the goal of regular monitoring of salaries in the IT industry. Salaries are left by the specialists themselves, we collect them and provide them all with open access in aggregated and anonymous form.


How to read report charts




All salaries are in rubles. These are salaries received on hand, net of all taxes. Dots indicate specific salaries. A group of points for each sample is visualized using a "mustache box". The middle vertical line shows the median salary (half of the salaries are lower and half above this point, we can consider this average salary), the box borders are 25 and 75 percentiles (divide the lower and upper half of the salaries again in half, as a result, half of all salaries are between them). The box mustache is 10 and 90 percentiles (conditionally we can consider them the minimum and maximum salaries). More about how the salary calculator is arranged and how to read the data: https://moikrug.ru/info/salaries .



The average salary in the IT industry is now 100,000 rubles: in Moscow - 136,000 rubles, in St. Petersburg - 110,000 rubles, in other regions - 75,000 rubles.

Compared with the 2nd half of 2018, we observe a general increase in salaries. On average, in the IT industry, salaries increased by 10% (from 90,000 rubles to 100,000 rubles), in Moscow salaries increased by 13% (from 120,000 rubles to 136,000 rubles), in St. Petersburg - by 10% (from 100 000 rubles to 110 000), in other regions the increase in median salary amounted to 7% (from 70 000 rubles to 75 000 rubles).


Salaries for core IT majors


Salary status by main IT specializations in the 1st half of 2019.



In general, in all regions over the past six months, there has been an increase in the median salary in the areas of analytics (11%), management (8%), personnel (7%), development (5%) and design (1.3%). The decrease in the median occurred only in marketing (-8%). And salary medians in support, administration and testing remained unchanged.



Let us take a look at the dynamics of salaries for each region separately. An increase in the median salary in development and personnel occurred simultaneously in all regions. In testing and management, growth was in Moscow and St. Petersburg, in administration and design - in St. Petersburg and the regions.

In Moscow, there was no decrease in salaries in any specialization, while in St. Petersburg they began to earn less in analytics and marketing, and in the regions in testing and marketing.

Software Developer Salaries


Salaries for core development majors




In general, for all regions, we see that over the 1st half of 2019, the median salary for developers of all specializations increased, except for desktop developers, for whom it did not change. Especially strong growth is observed among game developers (25%), software architects (24%) and mobile developers (20%).



Now let's look at the dynamics of developers' salaries in individual regions. For front-end and full-stack developers, the median salary increased simultaneously in all regions, for desktop and mobile developers there was an increase in St. Petersburg and regions, for embed developers in Moscow and the regions, for backend developers in Moscow and St. Petersburg, for developers games - in St. Petersburg.

The only specialization of the development, according to which we observe a drop in the median salary, is gamedev in the regions.



Programmer language salaries


The highest salaries for developers Scala, Objective-C and Golang - 150 000 rubles. The leader of the last half year Elixir - now occupies only 6th place in terms of salary.



The growth in median salary occurred in almost all languages. Particularly strong growth was marked by Objective-C (25%), Swift (24%) and Java (20%). We see a slight decrease in salaries in C ++ and Delphi. The largest decrease in median salary is observed in Elixir (-19%).



Developer salaries by company


OZON has joined the leaders among companies in the salaries of its developers this half year. Luxoft, Kaspersky Lab, Mail.ru, Alfa Bank and Yandex still maintain the highest positions.

As in the previous report, we show the salary of those who work in freelance - for comparison with the salaries of outsourcing companies.



Salaries of developers in cities with over one million people


The average salary of developers in Moscow is 140,000 rubles, in St. Petersburg - 120,000 rubles, in Ufa - 100,000 rubles, in Novosibirsk and Nizhny Novgorod - about 90,000 rubles, and the remaining million-plus cities - on average 80,000 rub. Six months ago, the leaders in the salaries of developers after Moscow and St. Petersburg were Nizhny Novgorod, Novosibirsk and Ufa. In the current half year, the three did not change, but the cities changed places.



For the 1st half of 2019, the largest increase in median salary is among the developers of Volgograd, Omsk and Krasnodar. Salaries fell in Nizhny Novgorod, Chelyabinsk, Samara and Perm, and the salaries of developers from Rostov-on-Don, Voronezh and Yekaterinburg remained the same.




Key Observations


  1. The average salary in the IT industry is now 100,000 rubles: in Moscow - 136,000 rubles, in St. Petersburg - 110,000 rubles, in other regions - 75,000 rubles.
  2. For the 1st half of 2019, salaries in the IT industry as a whole increased by 10% (from 90,000 rubles to 100,000 rubles).
  3. Salary growth is observed in the areas of analytics (11%), management (8%), staff (7%), development (5%) and design (1.3%). No change - salaries in support, administration and testing. Salary reduction is observed in marketing (-8%).
  4. The average salary in development is now 100,000 rubles: in Moscow - 140,000 rubles, in St. Petersburg - 120,000 rubles, in Ufa - 100,000 rubles, in other regions - 80,000 rubles.
  5. In the field of development, we observe an increase in salaries in all specializations, except for desktop developers, where salaries have not changed. Especially strong growth in salaries among game developers (25%), software architects (23%) and mobile developers (20%).
  6. The highest salaries are still among developers in languages: Scala, Objective-C and Golang - 150,000 rubles. The leader of the end of 2018, Elixir - now occupies only 6th place in terms of salary - 110,000 rubles.
  7. Salary growth in almost all programming languages. Particularly strong growth was marked by Objective-C (25%), Swift (24%) and Java (20%). Salary reductions in C ++, Delphi and Elixir, the latter was marked by the strongest decline. Python developers' salaries remained unchanged.
  8. OZON joined the traditional leaders in terms of the salaries of its developers - Kaspersky Lab, Mail.ru, Luxoft and Alfa Bank - with an average median salary of developers of 176,000 rubles.

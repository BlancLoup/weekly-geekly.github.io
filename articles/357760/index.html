Thebat.net mail domain is closing



About five years ago, the owners of the licensed version of TheBat! There is an opportunity to have a box in the thebat.net domain. Ritlabs did not set up its own personal mail server for this, using the free at that time opportunity to link its own domain names to Gmail. But the freebie ended Google changed its business model, and since April 19, the boxes in the thebat.net domain will stop working.

Translation of a letter from Maxim Masyutin, head of Ritlabs:

Dear mail account holders on thebat.net,

Google has sent us a notice that the provision of postal services for the thebat.net domain has ceased. This decision was made due to a change in the business model, which affected not only thebat.net, but also many other domains of other companies that tied them to Gmail. We considered all possible solutions to this problem, but the most appropriate option was to discontinue support for mailboxes in the thebat.net domain. We regret to inform you that from April 19, 2015, Google will cease to provide postal services for this domain.

How does this affect you?

Your mail account in thebat.net domain will be closed. After April 19, you will not be able to receive and send mail and view message history. In addition, if your account was linked to the services listed below, you will lose access to them. So it is better to save all the important data as long as possible. The list of services that are used by your account can be seen on the Google Dashboard page. And this is a list of services that will be unavailable after the account is closed:

  • 3D Warehouse
  • Adsense
  • Adwords
  • Analytics
  • App Engine
  • Blogger
  • Calendar
  • Contacts (Export and import)
  • Chrome sync
  • Drive (For Google Documents, Spreadsheets, Presentations, and Forms)
  • Feedburner
  • Finance: Export and Import
  • Google Play Store
  • Google Play for Developers
  • Google wallet
  • Maps: My Maps
  • Merchant center
  • Picasa web albums
  • Places (bulk upload)
  • Sites
  • Voice
  • Webmaster tools
  • YouTube


Details on how to migrate data can be found on the Transition Resource Center page. If you have additional questions, please contact us at office@ritlabs.com.

Respectfully,
Maxim Masyutin,
Ritlabs

Three days off in the frame of reference # 9DW

A couple of months ago, the International Labor Organization proposed moving to a four-day working week.
According to ILO calculations, in such a work regime, the health of the population will improve, because in three days a person rests and recovers much better than in two.

Almost in all articles devoted to this news, the temptation of such a proposal for the laboring masses was noted, but in the end they concluded that the Russian economy is still too low-tech for such a step as the transition to a four-day working week.

Indeed, when asking the question ‚ÄúWe want to rest for Y days, with Y = 3. Is X working days enough to make money? Where X is located according to the formula X = 7-Y "probably the majority of citizens of our country will have doubts that they will be able to earn enough money in 4 days to cover them for 3 days of rest. (the ending is superficial, not taking into account much)

But if we reformulate the question differently, namely ‚ÄúFor what value of Z, I have enough money earned in X days, provided that Y = 3, and X = ZY‚Äù, then it is much more interesting to answer it.

For myself, I already picked up the value of Z, it is equal to 9. X, respectively, will be equal to 6.

X = 6
Is it bad or good?
It seems like our parents found the time when the working week in the USSR was 6 days, with one day off. And now some voluntarily, and some involuntarily (teachers) go to work on Saturday. Therefore, there is confidence that 6 working days is not a critical load on the human body. And unequivocally say that this is bad impossible.
And for those who often go on business trips, or constantly coordinate all sorts of documents, X = 6 is generally a gift. For 6 days, you can safely get by plane through Moscow to any other major city in Russia, work there for 2-3 days, return to your hometown on day 5, go to work on day 6 and report back on a business trip. When X = 5, personally, I often have the case that either you leave / come on a day off, or you have to squeeze a program of 3 days on site for up to 1-2 days. Both that and another strongly affects on rest after business trip.
When coordinating documents, there are situations that at the beginning of the week all sorts of nuances are coordinated, and when you need to sign on the 4-5 day, the main persons either have not yet returned from a business trip, or went on vacation ahead of time, using Day 5 as an important tactical reception in receiving ‚Äú10 days - 10 nights, all inclusive. " Therefore, the signing is stretched for the next week, and the work is worth it. Yes, it should not be so, but in everyday work it is widely met.
In general, X = 6 (8 times per day) is definitely better than X = 4 (12 times per day).

Y = 3
Definitely good.
You can leisurely go / fly to another city on an excursion or visit relatives.
You can get up on the first day later, by day to clean up the apartment, and closer to the night, go to the cottage and not standing in traffic jams on the outskirts of the city, in 2 hours you can reach it at most. Two nights in the fresh air will restore strength to the maximum and it will not be so depressing to return to the city at lunch on the third day.
It is possible on the first day, while they are still sleeping off, to purchase construction materials, to devote the second day to repair, and to leave the third day for a full rest with the family.
This is if you certainly do not drink alcohol at any opportunity, and yes, Slepakov will have to rewrite the song.

Z = 9
I do not see any reasons, except religious, which could somehow substantially prevent this. The number of days in the month remains the same.
And with the work schedule, let's say 1 after 2 or 3 after 3, Z = 9 is more convenient than Z = 7 for calculations. With other graphs, they seem to be parity.
But in order to introduce a 9-day in a single country, it seems to me that you need to be an even more decisive ruler than Peter the First, who shaved the beards of the boyars in the European manner.

Part two is practical.

In this picture you can see how our life can change.

So I suggest that anyone interested in this idea will unite in some semblance of a scientific group studying the possibility of living in a 9-day week.

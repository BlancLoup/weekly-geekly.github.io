Online application development school for Windows 8. Week # 1

Hooray! The school began its work from April 1 - and the first part of the educational process will last until mid-May.



More than 4,500 students enrolled in the course. At the moment we have recorded and posted the first block of lectures, tests and tasks for laboratory work. You can join the course at any time, but the duration of the course is limited.



Lectures


We recorded and posted the first block of lectures. Let me remind you that the lectures are conditionally divided into four tracks: general-introductory, design, xaml + c # and html + js.

  • Online School: Why and how to study?
  • What is important to know about Windows 8
  • Winrt
  • What is design
  • Windows design language
  • Design process
  • XAML / C #: Introduction to Visual Studio Development
  • XAML / C #: Startup, Debugging, and Simulator
  • Blend for XAML
  • HTML / JS: an introduction to the development of Visual Studio
  • HTML / JS: launch, debug and simulator
  • HTML Blend


Lectures in C # and JS are more or less synchronized with each other, but may differ in content due to the peculiarities of solving the same tasks in different programming / markup languages.

We tried to improve the quality of the recording - now the dialing code should be visible.

Laboratory works


In the first laboratory work (also duplicated in C # and JavaScript), we deal with templates, add external data and learn to check the availability of the Internet through the WinRT API. We also find out why we need a manifest and how to add a privacy policy.

In the near future, it should be possible to confirm the performance of laboratory work with a screenshot of the current state of the application.

Online consultation


Along the way, with the launch of the course, Intuit moved to a new engine, now within the course you can ask questions and share your opinion on lectures and other aspects of the course. We will try to regularly answer questions on the case :)

Also, as promised, we will periodically make online-meetings with lecturers, within which we will try to answer all your questions.

Lenovo Yoga: a brief history and mini-review of the Yoga 900

Yoga 900 got a 13.3-inch display with a resolution of 3200 x 1800, just between 11.6 "and 14" of my old laptops, high performance - the latest sixth generation Intel Core i7 and 16 GB of RAM, and light weight - 1, 3 kg. In this post - a brief excursion into the history of Yoga and a brief overview of the model Yoga 900.



Although the transformers have a rich history, the popularity of the Lenovo Yoga form factor led to the fact that similar gadgets with touchscreens began to release HP, Dell and Acer and many smaller manufacturers, and the French Archos in August 2015 in general to a press release about their own The hybrid laptop has a photo of Lenovo Yoga attached .

The first Yoga 11 was built on the Nvidia Tegra 3 1.3 GHz with an integrated graphics processor, had 2 GB of RAM and 32 or 64 GB SSD - today This is more like the characteristics of an average smartphone or tablet. The device was running Windows RT, Microsoft Office 2013 was pre-installed on the laptop. Lenovo Yoga 900


In October 2015, Lenovo, together with Microsoft, showed Yoga 900 at a presentation in San Francisco.
Yoga 900 is an updated version of Yoga 3 Pro. The manufacturer worked on problems with overheating of the laptop and increased the duration of work from a single battery charge. My version of the transformer received an Intel Core i7 Skylake 2.5 GHz processor, 16 gigabytes of RAM and 256 gigabytes of SSD. There are versions with 512 GB SSD, Core i5 processors and 8 GB of RAM. In addition to the two USB ports, the gadget received USB 3.0 and a card reader for SD, MMC, SDXC, SDHC.

The screen is a very colorful, 13.3-inch QHD + (3200x1800). This is one of the thinnest ultrabooks on Intel Core i - the case thickness is 14.9 mm. The gadget weighs 1.3 kg.

Operating time on one charge - up to 10 hours.









The turning mechanism consists of 813 individual parts and is similar to a wristwatch bracelet. At the same time, for some reason, the dirt is not clogged with gears.

Is the release of the iPhone delayed?

According to businessweek.com, the release of the Apple iPhone may occur later than planned.

Let me remind you that Apple is planning to start selling the new product in June (in the US), and its partner, AT & T, indicated a specific date - June 11th. However, the release of the iPhone may be delayed. On April 12, Apple made a statement that it was throwing some of the developers from its other project to strengthen the team working on the June iPhone release. This fact may indicate some difficulties that may lead to the transfer of the release date.

Jagdish Rebello, an analyst at iSuppli, a marketing agency, cites numerous iPhone component vendors who have said that the new product is a very complex device, and it can be problematic to put all the components on time.

Apple in its statement also refers to the complexity of the product, in particular, the software, which has no analogues on any of the existing mobile devices. However, the company does not officially tolerate the release of the iPhone, waiting for its sale in late June.

Usually a couple of months before entering the market, devices like the Apple iPhone go through a huge number of tests. All bottlenecks before mass production are found and eliminated. Perhaps Apple needed additional strength to fix a problem identified during the testing phase that needs to be urgently fixed.

Perhaps Apple is simply insuring before entering the market loudly. After all, if serious problems arise after the iPhone goes on sale, the consequences could be catastrophic for the company: both ordinary consumers and stock market analysts expect a hit. If their expectations are deceived, Apple will be tight.

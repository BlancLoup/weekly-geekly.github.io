An interesting problem in .htaccess or special characters, mod_rewrite and C ++ tag.

Recently, while working on my project (a site with articles on how to do ), I encountered a problem with mod_rewrite. The essence of the problem was the following: in the tag cloud, when switching to the ‚ÄúC ++‚Äù tag (processed by urlencode and becoming C% 2B% 2B) I got to the ‚ÄúC‚Äù tag (the letter ‚ÄúC‚Äù and 2 spaces).

The rule in .htaccess was:

RewriteRule ^ tag / ([^ /] +) / $ index.php? Tag = $ 1 [L]

Through experiments, I found out that the $ 1 rule does not fall into ‚ÄúC% 2B% 2B‚Äù, but ‚ÄúC ++‚Äù (inside Apache% 2B turns into +). Plus, of course, was regarded as a space connecting two words.

I dug up the Internet for a long time in search of a solution, experimented with flags, until on request in Google ‚ÄúC ++ mod_rewrite‚Äù I found a post describing this bug of Apache somewhere on the forums on apache.org (as I understand from this post - my problem is a bug Apache).

In this post, the author advised to disassemble regular% {THE_REQUEST}, in which "C% 2B% 2B" was in its original form and not replaced with "C ++". % {THE_REQUEST} usually contains something like "GET tag / C% 2B% 2B / HTTP 1.1".

Reception helped, I redid the old rewrite rule for this:

RewriteCond% {THE_REQUEST} ^ GET [\] + / tag / ([^ /] +) / [\] + HTTP. * $
RewriteRule ^ (. *) $ Index.php? Tag =% 1 [L]

Tested on versions of Apache 2.2 (Windows) and 1.3.4 (Linux).

Now users of my site can easily read articles tagged with "C ++" :)
I hope my post will help someone with a similar problem.

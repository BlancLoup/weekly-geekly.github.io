When I see such a picture, I once again think about the nature of intelligence.

What Wikipedia tells us about intelligence:
Intellect (from the Latin. Intellectus - understanding, cognition) - the system of all the cognitive abilities of the individual: sensation, perception, memory, representation, thinking, imagination. General ability to learn and solve problems, which determines the success of any activity and underlies other abilities.
At present, intelligence refers to the ability to carry out the process of cognition and to effectively solve problems, in particular when mastering a new circle of life tasks.

And so, we will sort on points:

1. Ability to carry out the process of knowledge.
The process of knowledge is so-called. learning without a teacher. Those. the ability to learn the world on your own, to find patterns and extract facts from what is happening, making useful conclusions for yourself.

2. Ability to effectively solve problems.
This ability characterizes an object (organism) that is able to solve the tasks set for it in the most rational way, applying the tools currently available to it.

3. Ability to master new tasks.
An intellectual object (whether it is an organism, a device, or a program) must independently solve new tasks that it sets for itself, independently adapt to changes in the environment and master new tools.

What we, developers of artificial intelligence systems, are always kicking for, is that such a system, being trained in one solution space, is often completely useless if the space of the tasks assigned to it changes significantly. Such systems, in particular, resemble trained dogs, or, forgive me, my employees, 90% of employees. Such people, having received education and having come to work, get at their disposal a range of tasks, a set of tools and the space of the required solutions, often accompanied by a teacher. If such an employee has worked for several years in one position, then he will be of little use in another, he will again need a teacher and time to master the assigned tasks. Such mastering can be called an intellectual process only if it happens on its own, otherwise it is just dressing.

So does the above dog have intelligence? Does she have the above three abilities. I believe that yes, it does. If we are talking about the program - it is called with loud words "artificial intelligence".

How I earned 1 million rubles in the Marketplace 1C-Bitrix

How it all started

Not long ago, I was mainly engaged in selling my watches on outsourcing to metropolitan studios and developing custom websites. But, both directions had a number of significant drawbacks for me:

Being engaged in outsourcing, even if your level grows and the cost of an hour grows, sooner or later you come to the conclusion that after the delivery of the next project you have nothing left - there are no assets other than experience. In addition, in our business you need to constantly learn, and the time for this is sorely lacking - Customers have little desire to pay for our pumping. Money comes only when you code, and when you don't code, money does not flow accordingly. And before you code, you need to be able to sell your time dear. Well, thousands of other problems that you know about. Attracting hired labor solves them a little + adds new ones. For example, an employee, with all the desire, will not work out more than 132 hours per month, there are restrictions on the cost of an hour - that is, there is always a certain ceiling in income. And with the departure of the employee, you lose all his knowledge and experience, lose important competences - which is essentially the only asset of the company. Fathers, I don‚Äôt want to convince anyone, but my opinion outsourcing is not the best business ...

Website development is still worse. The market is oversaturated. Dumping is huge. Every new project has to start all over again. Again consulting, again you explain that it is better to draw the logo on the left (I exaggerate) and so on. You have been developing an Internet project for years, and now you just cure almost all the cockroaches in the customer‚Äôs head and bring the site to a decent level when you can finally start doing what something really interesting and non-trivial, and then bam - the customer‚Äôs business didn‚Äôt go, or the business went, but the customer has reached his comfort zone and doesn‚Äôt want to develop further, or there is no budget for the implementation of your features. It is clear that all this is not so much a problem as there are problems that are solved and many work well and with pleasure in this market, and yet ...

For these reasons, I was constantly thinking about some kind of replication of my experience, maybe creating my own project. Preferably without offline sales and marketing. Looking around, we see that there are two main trends for realizing this today: Marketplace (AppStore, GooglePlay, etc.) and SaaS. And if the second requires serious investments and super ideas, then the first is implemented fairly easily and with a minimum of costs. After all, the Big Brother - Vendor takes on a lot: creates the entire infrastructure and ecosystem, comes up with the rules of the game common to all, is engaged in marketing, develops the framework, and we can only generate ideas and implement them.

The main advantages that I see in the Marketplace

1. The possibility of self-realization. You do what you want, and you decide how. At the same time, if you did something to your liking, it is likely that someone else (from more than 50,000 site owners on the CMS 1C-Bitrix) will like it.
2. Passive income. You can suspend production for a while: calmly explore a new technology, change developers, and go on the same vacation. Even if you do it for a while, sales will continue anyway. And it gives a certain peace and confidence in the future.
3. Accumulation of assets. Workers come and go, but the modules remain with you, they are sold, their number and turnover from sales grow.
4. Sale of goods, not services. We can talk about this for a very long time. The difference is colossal!
For example, you are not so dependent on a particular client when there are not tens, but thousands ...
5. Marketing and technological support for Big Brother. For example, at 20:00 in Krasnoyarsk, I sit at a club and sip a cocktail, and at this very moment a seminar is being held in Moscow, at which, among other things, they advertise my modules. Isn't it great?
6. Experience in developing, promoting and selling their own software. This is a unique opportunity to try at the lowest possible cost. Sin is not to take advantage!
7. Professional growth of developers. When producing their own modules, developers have to develop the architecture of the program, write their own classes, query the database, etc., as adult programmers do. Growth is much more intense than when simply stretching the design templates on the CMS.

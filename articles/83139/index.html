Problems with voice communication when transferring data from MTS (other operators have not been verified)

Good day to all. I'll tell you a little background.
Living and working in St. Petersburg throughout the past year, I used a corporate SIM card for data services and voice calls on the Nokia E51.
Actively enjoyed the introduction of 3G, and speed. Progress does not stop.
Later moved away from the active use of data transmission.
This week - I-phone 3G 16gb with firmware 3.1.2 got into my hands.
where an unpleasant bug was discovered.

Story:
With an active data transfer session (EDGE / 3G) - I automatically disappeared from the cellular network. When trying to make a voice call to my device - the caller heard the phrase "The device of the called subscriber is turned off or out of network coverage"
As soon as the data transfer stopped - I received an SMS notification that the man tried to call.
Today, solving this problem with setting up the device - I found out that the problem is not in my device.
Tested on different corporate SIM-cards from one tariff,
on devices Nokia N97, Asus P527 - with the same result.
When using packet data - voice transmission was not available.

When searching the Internet, I found the following explanation for the problem:
H. cartman, you have absolutely misunderstood the concept of priority. The fact is that the priority is set for a cell, for those time slots that are not specifically allocated for voice or data transmission. At the same time, if there are not enough time slots, time slots with non-priority traffic will be released to the priority traffic. MTS (and other operators) have priority for voice traffic, of course (most of the cells have dedicated time slots for data). The priority for a specific number or a specific tariff (GSM and GPRS networks do not know anything about tariffs at all) is not set. This, by the way, is described in the direct text in the very article on Wikipedia to which you refer

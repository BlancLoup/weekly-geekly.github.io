There is a table, there is a disk that rotates, we put a plate on it. Now there are two technologies of rotation of this disk, two ways.

Where is the motor and how? In professional vinyl players (this is a club DJ version) direct drive, that is, a disc is put on the motor. An important element is the pickup head, where the needle is located, which extracts sound. Needles come in two types: MM or MC. Briefly about technology. MM is Moving Magnet - a moving magnet. The magnet is attached directly to the needle and moves relative to the inductors that are nearby. As is known from the course of physics, when a magnet moves relative to inductors, an electric current arises. Thus, the sound is removed from the plate.

In the heads of MC all the same, just the opposite. Moving coil coil is moving relative to the magnet. The principle is the same: one is moving relative to another, different technologies are simply applied. There are pros and cons. MM-heads have slightly higher sensitivity, the output signal is louder, there are technological features. MC-heads benefit in quality, in performance, in detail. But they have a minus - a very, very weak output, on average, an order of magnitude less than that of MM heads.

D: I wonder how replaceable the head and needle are? We consider the case when we immediately forget about the changeability of the tone arm. It is also interesting to find out their resource.

T: Resources are different. Interchangeability is high. One head can be changed for another, but the next structural element arises - phono preamp or phono equalizer. What is he for? He has two destinations. First, reinforce that very weak signal.

Any modern amplifier at the input wants to receive a signal of at least 150 mV. Since MM-heads give an average of 5 mV - this is a very big difference. MC-heads and even less - tenths of mV. It turns out a very weak signal for the amplifier. Therefore, an intermediate link is necessary - the phono stage, which is also the equalizer.

The word "corrector" or "equalizer" appears. Why is an amplifier called equalizer or equalizer? On a vinyl record, sound is recorded with an uneven frequency response ‚Äî a low frequency is underestimated in amplitude, and high frequencies are overestimated to reduce the level of interference, the level of high-frequency noise. Low frequencies underestimate, so that there is no too large amplitude. Otherwise, bumps that are read from the plate can be very large, and the head will jump.

D: This is done in order to make a sound in the form in which it was intended, based on the properties of the material.

T: Yes, these are the technical features of the material of the plate itself and the recording technology needed to get the wide frequency range we are used to. So you have to get out of the situation. A phono equalizer or phono preamp corrects the frequency response to a normal, even curve to get what you need at the output.

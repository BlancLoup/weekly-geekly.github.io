10 tips on how to overcome the fear of communicating with a native English speaker

Did it happen to you that you speak perfect English with yourself, but do you get any random words with real people? And with a native speaker there is some kind of trash, after which I want to wrap myself in a blanket and never leave my apartment again! Well, if at hand, Google Translate or some kind of dictionary. And even better if you do not need to communicate live.

You tell me that already there are all sorts of phrasebooks and even magic headphones, like a fish from ‚ÄúHitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy‚Äù, which translate everything on the fly. But you should not get upset, because we have prepared for you practical advice on how to overcome the language barrier.

The reasons


First you need to understand the causes of language barriers:

  • lack of necessary knowledge for free communication (vocabulary or grammar ignorance);
  • lack of practice (even with a sufficient level of English);
  • fear of making a mistake and exposing myself in a bad light (what if I‚Äôll pick up the words for 10 minutes just to say ‚Äúyes‚Äù);
  • fear of spontaneous communication (you never know what they want to talk with me, all of a sudden I don‚Äôt know how to communicate on these topics);
  • fear of being misunderstood (I have a pronunciation that I myself do not understand what I am talking about).

As you can see, there are not so many problems and, more often than not, everything lies in us, and not the lack of time or the complexity of the language. If a student has just started learning English, it is not surprising that he is not yet ready to communicate. Although in properly constructed classes, the practice of communication is laid at the initial stage. So think about changing the format of your lessons if you have a similar situation and do not have enough practice! But let's get to the tips.

1. Do not worry


Strange advice, yes? It is easy not to worry if it is not in this situation. Although, in fact, we share with you personal experience. Everything started from something and, in most cases, it was not easy. You just need to admit that the first time to speak as a carrier will not work. Yes, and the second and third. But from the fifth or seventh the first results will already be noticeable, which will give you more confidence in yourself. According to statistics in our school, students who have visited a month of spoken clubs (about 8 classes) feel much more confident.

2. Start making mistakes


Yes, the school said that mistakes were bad and lowered their grade for them. We just forgot to say that we learn very well from mistakes and extract a lot of useful things from them. Let's just think - who will speak English faster - a person who makes a lot of mistakes and speaks, or one who is silent and not mistaken? The answer is obvious, is not it?

3. Do not pay attention to your pronunciation.


In fact, this is a continuation of the previous advice. At the initial stage, the task of any student is to speak and overcome the fear of communication. Later, your speech can be corrected and improved (for example, at the pre-intermediate and higher levels). But it will be much easier to do when you have the courage, not when you just take the first steps.

4. Talk to yourself out loud


Remember the first time you heard your voice on the tape? It was strange, right? The brain automatically turns on a defensive reaction, considering it to be some kind of mistake. Therefore, you need to say everything out loud to the brain accustomed to your speech. In addition, the words are well remembered!

5. Slow down


This means that no one will suffer from measured or slow speech. In general, everyone does this to make it clearer, so feel free to speak at a pace that is convenient for you. And the interlocutor (especially the carrier) will be able to adapt to you and even say thanks.

6. Ask again


All ingenious is simple. Ask to repeat the moments that are not clear to you, or specify what exactly was meant. The simpler the wording, the better for you. Better to be understood than an exquisite philologist! If this advice is not enough, here is a list of phrases for you with which you can ask again or clarify something:

Excuse me / I beg your pardon / sorry? - I apologize (with interrogative intonation it will immediately become clear that it is worth repeating something);

What was that? - I'm sorry, what;

What did you say? - What did you say;

Come again? - an informal way to say ‚Äúrepeat‚Äù;

Could you say that again / repeat that? - Could you repeat, please;

I didn't quite catch that - I didn't catch everything;

Could you give me an example? - is it possible to give an example.

7. Repeat if you do not understand.


The situation of the reverse. If the interviewee does not understand you - just say the phrase again. It may be worthwhile to speak more or less slowly or with clearer diction intonation. But nothing terrible will happen - even the carriers of each other ask and repeat the words several times! In this case, be prepared to correctly respond to the phrases given earlier.

8. Prepare in advance


You say that you can not prepare for unforeseen situations. And it is true. But, if you constantly expand your vocabulary and practice a new grammar, you will be much more confident and quickly decide when to improvise.
Here is a list of useful resources to help with this:

  • EnglishDom Words , EasyTen , UpMind , Memrise - practicing vocabulary;
  • LearnEnglish Grammar , Learn English with Babbel , EnglishDom. Grammar - grammar pumping.

9. Upgrade your listening skill


Listen to songs, podcasts, radio or watch short videos, TV shows and your favorite movies. In this case, do not rush to climb into the dictionary for each new word (after all, at least 25-30% of the vocabulary is specially selected complex and unfamiliar words). First try to mark the right word and understand the meaning by context. If it did not help, find the explanation in English. The translation should be addressed only after all these procedures. And do not forget to get used not only to the standard English from Britain, but also pay attention to American and other accents. You can do this on the following resources:

  • Voice of America - audio and video with a bias in American English, which can be downloaded with subtitles;
  • Talk English - a selection of audio for different levels - from beginner to advanced, with tasks for them with different accents.

10. Practice


Your cap. In a sense, find the opportunity to communicate in English. If you are not yet ready for publicity, welcome to the language school! And if you are ready - share your gift with others. Heard, for example, foreign speech in Red Square - offer your help.

Well, do not forget about motivation. It will become much easier to communicate if you encourage yourself somehow when learning English. For example, they studied one grammatical topic - they went to the cinema for a favorite movie or ate a chocolate bar. So English will turn not torment, but into pleasure!

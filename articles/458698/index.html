The path of peace and the path of war in IT projects

I have been working as an IT project manager since 2006, and now I am the head of the Dinabot start-up project. During this time I have seen many successes, but I have seen even more failures. These failures, in my opinion, are often associated not so much with the applied technologies, as with the narrow picture of the world leaders. And I would like more project managers to be able to rise above technology and, thus, make better decisions.

Today we are drunk Agile! Who is not drunk, will be drunk tomorrow or the day after tomorrow. And it's great!

Here is a photo from my last project, which was managed according to Agile principles:


Agile sounds so fabulous that some are afraid to use it! The campaign turns out that using Agile is not as easy as it is written. But, all the same, we will use it, since there is no escape from the trend. Customers, too, get drunk and make us IT-coves use Agile. We will use it despite failures, blaming us for doing something wrong, correcting our technologies, changing people. Unfortunately, often blindly use ...

Like after drinking, sometimes it becomes bad, and after some such Agile projects, someone is not at all having fun. Systems are not implemented, money is spent, people are tired.

No pills for all diseases. There are no universal values. Therefore, it is necessary to expand your picture of the world and apply different strategies and tactics depending on the situation.

Agile carries: openness, trust, cooperation, flexibility. This is the way of "peace" on the project! What does the Falls bring? Until the path of "peace" appeared, it was difficult to understand. In this war there is an enemy - this is the current organizational and technical system of the Customer. I will not explain what it is, read the old Soviet GOST (about ACS), but this is not just software! The more integral and effective this system is, the more it will resist, the harder it is to defeat, the more it will have allies. This system resists changes that threaten her life. People are her soldiers, they are not individually to blame for the war. This is not a war between the contractor and the customer, because on the side of the customer there are also supporters of changes, otherwise this war is meaningless. Unlike territorial wars, the enemy in the IT war is not so clearly visible, the commander of the enemy is not clearly defined, people and resources are not so strictly distributed between the parties. But just this war is insidious!

In this war there is a goal - to defeat the enemy and bring a new order to its territory with the help of the new system.

In this war there is a battlefield (territory) - these are the processes, org structures, information flows that are to be automated, changed during the implementation.

There is intelligence in this war - it begins at the stage of the survey and includes constant interaction with the customer, transferring to his side supporters of the old system.

There is defense in this war - these are the results of the first stages of the Waterfall, preceding the introduction - contracts, statutes, project management plans, which serve to protect the project from the damaging effects of the enemy, to protect the team in case of retreat.

In this war, there are attacks and battles - this is the very process of introduction, with local victories and defeats, offensives and wastes.

In this war there is a strategy, there is tactics and maneuvers, there is politics and negotiations.

And with the advent of Agile, is it all gone? No more enemy? We will live together in peace, in joy and openness to develop and implement information systems? NOT!!!

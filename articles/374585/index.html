Excessive physical activity is no better than a sedentary lifestyle.



Hello, reader!

If you are a fan of healthy lifestyles and your heart begins to beat more often every morning during the lacing of sneakers in anticipation of a long run, then we have bad news for you: researchers from Denmark claim that too intense loads are comparable in their utility to a sedentary lifestyle, t. e. rather unhealthy than beneficial, according to Latimes .

The study, which was published on Monday in the journal of the American College of Cardiology, is not the first of its kind, opinions against too intense physical exercises were voiced before it.

Previous studies suggest that people engaged in physical activity, are 30% less likely to die ahead of time compared with those who lead a sedentary lifestyle. However, the optimal intensity and volume of loads are still not defined.

Long-term overloads can cause calcification of the coronary artery, which is one of the causes of atherosclerosis, as well as lead to diastolic dysfunction (a decrease in the ability of the ventricles of the heart to pump blood) and thickening of the arterial walls.

The basis for the findings was a cardiological study conducted at the hospital where the speakers work. For twelve years, doctors observed the condition of two groups of people. The first, consisting of 1098 people constantly engaged in running. The second group consisted of 3,950 people and its members were sedentary. By the end of the study from the first group 28 people died, and from the second 128.

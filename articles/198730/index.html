The most useful Google Analytics updates in 2013



At the beginning of October, at the next annual summit, Google Analytics representatives announced several updates at once, some of which we already see in our accounts today, some will appear in the system by the end of the year. According to the developers, innovations will help site owners and web analysts to get even more data about resource users, to find links between different types of people and their interaction with the site, to discover new ideas for business development. Let's try to understand the current updates.

User segmentation


Consider what changes have occurred in user segmentation. Previously, extended segments were based only on visitor data, now Google Analytics allows you to customize custom segments according to certain criteria based on visitor data. For example, specific demographic data or behavior. This allows for a deeper analysis at the site visitor level.

Traffic sources


Updated the most popular report on traffic sources. By grouping key indicators, the developers made it even more intuitive and intuitive.

The new report ‚ÄúGeneral Information‚Äù provides a summary of the traffic sources: where the visitor came from, how he behaves on the site and how he performs the conversion. By default, such a report contains relative indicators broken down by channels.



Now you can see in one report:
  • From which channels is the most traffic users;
  • From which channels come the visitors who most effectively interact with the site;
  • Which channels bring us the most conversion users.

Thus, we immediately get a general idea about the effectiveness of various sources of traffic, as well as about the behavior of visitors.

The new report "Channels" gives detailed information for each channel. By default, channels such as Direct Entries, Referrals, Search Engines and Social Networks, etc., are installed, but you can always adjust additional channels as you like.

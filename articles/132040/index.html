Cosmopoly IT world after the crisis

The world of information technology, and after them the material one, is divided into two parts.

The first is a database. People work with different programs and each of them keeps with them around the base. This is preached by both local applications and sites.

This is how millions of informational segments are formed. The advantage is simplicity: such databases are not difficult to pre-configure to work with a specific subject area. A certain template is created by which further information is simply simply thrown in and processed.

There are two downsides:

1. the division into bases does not allow to establish all the relationships between the data and, accordingly, it does not squeeze all the benefits out of them.
2. A program that has gained control over any data has no interest to be further optimized in relation to them, using its monopoly position.

But there is also a super-minus, money: the seller and the buyer are face to face, one on one. And if the sale of other services has a dispute over the price regarding the material values ‚Äã‚Äãspent (time of the master, the price of raw materials, etc.), then in the case of trading programs this support is practically absent. As a result, all this leads to general displeasure: the lack of desire of programmers to work with the soul and under-automation of farms. Completes the problem that people find complete pleasure in avoiding reality thanks to the same information technologies: games, TV shows, social networks, etc. The farms also find a bad way out and also with the use of IT: it becomes profitable not to produce, but to cheat those who still produce by taking away their profits (see high-frequency traders).

Naturally, with such rending contradictions, this part of the world has the only finish - a collapse, an ever-expanding crisis. We observe it. The world of local databases is programmed to self-destruct. In principle, this world and without a financial crisis would self-destruct because of environmental or resource apocalypses (it was just as predetermined by itself), but it just does not even have to wait.

One way or another, the death of one usually means the birth of another. The collapse of the first part of the IT world gives a good chance of the second. The name of this alternate reality is cosmopoly (cosmo).

World

Cosmopoly - an alternative view on the IT-sphere and the whole world. Here all the information is one and only one database - the knowledge base. Those. a computer program can write its information into the database and any other one can use this record without any problems. Naturally, sample administration is no longer possible here. This requirement produces an important transformation with information: it is atomized. If the interaction is unpredictable from all sides, then it is easiest for any object to take an economical form and decrease to the minimum possible size - to become like an atom. Fortunately for information this is possible thanks to the so-called. descriptive logic .

Accordingly, the database becomes just an array of cells for atoms of information. Each such cell supports an unlimited number of write / destroy atoms cycles. At the same time, in the course of filling the cells, they are re-switched (linked) to each other.

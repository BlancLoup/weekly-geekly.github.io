Skype 3.6 Beta


A new beta version of the Skype Internet phone has been released. In this version, video calls are introduced in high resolution (640x480 at 30 frames per second).

Skype is a revolutionary program for voice communication over the Internet with video chat functions, which in a fairly short period of time has become almost the standard for IP telephony. Such popularity is due to the unprecedented quality of voice transmission over the Internet, while using a very modest connection quality. You can more or less comfortably and efficiently communicate on a simple modem connection at a speed of 57.600 (of course, this is about voice transmission, but not video for which you need a channel to the network more seriously). And since the service is free, and home networks and ADSL comes, then Skype is becoming more and more relevant and interesting. In addition to voice and video chat, the program supports regular chat (text), group chat (conference for up to 500 people) and file transfer.

It would not be amiss to mention the possibility of making calls to landlines and mobile phones using SkypeOut service and receiving calls from ordinary phones using SkypeIn.

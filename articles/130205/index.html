How to pay taxes on the sale of links on your site

Hi, Habr.

Our accounting department almost every day receives questions from individual entrepreneurs on how to calculate taxes when working with links sale exchanges. It was in the answers to these questions that the idea arose of integrating with the largest exchange of links selling and buying, in order to save webmasters from errors in tax returns.

The most impatient can immediately, without reading further, set up automatic data exchange between Sape and My business and not worry about how to count taxes - My business does everything automatically. In this case, all those who have configured the integration will receive a year of full access to the My Business service as a gift from Sape. Configure the integration and get a year of access here

But I still recommend that you familiarize yourself with the "materiel" in order to know where the amounts for tax payment come from every quarter.

So, the key thing in calculating the taxes received from incomes in the system in SAPE is that the tax is calculated not from the amount of money withdrawn to the checking account from the SAPE system, but from the amount of operations indicated in the agent's report. If the UE is located on the 'income-expenses' system, then in this case expenses are also included from the agent's report, which is transmitted by SAPE every month.
This is a very important point, since many webmasters incorrectly calculate taxes based on the following logic: ‚ÄúI am 6%, so I will only reflect in the income the amount received on the account balance sheet‚Äù. Unfortunately, in this case, as part of the first tax audit, the inspector will request an agreement with Softway LLC and will require additional taxes, penalties in the amount of 1/300 of the refinancing rate for each day of delay plus in some cases may incur an additional penalty.

This calculates the amount of taxes that must be paid every quarter. In addition to this most pleasant procedure, the law-abiding Individual entrepreneur, regardless of the type of activity, does the following:
  • Keeps a book of income and expenses - constantly on the basis of the report of the agent .

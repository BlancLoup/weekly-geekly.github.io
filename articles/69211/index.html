Monopoly City Streets short review - online games based on Google Maps

Good night everyone :)
Having hardly made my way to www.monopolycitystreets.com (I recently skipped the announcement of this multiplayer game based on Google Maps on Habr√©), I hasten to share my first observations and successes.

Immediately I want to say that the first few hours after the launch of this project, the server was just lying. I do not know why, but the developers did not take care of ensuring the corresponding server performance. And even so far it is impossible to play: most often for any attempt to interact with the local interface, we get the error ‚ÄúOh, sorry, but our servers are lying. Try coming back later. ‚Äù

Attention! Traffic!



On the first visit, the service offers us to start from the city that he identified at our IP address.
Well, to be honest, I wanted to do it - where else is becoming a monopolist? :)



After clicking on the link, we get to our city and get the opportunity to buy any street. This is where the game begins. It is at the time of buying the street that you are asked to register. However, with the brakes that now, even register is quite problematic.
Plus, in the nickname, only letters and spaces are allowed. Sorry, I'm already used to my nickname.

After registration, we apparently should have immediately logged in, but at that moment the server again could not bear the load and I had to log in myself.

Income brings both buildings and streets - every day.

Also in the game there are so-called Chance Crads and they are divided into three types:
  • Hazard Chance Card - allows you to build buildings such as Power Plants, Prisons, and Sewage Works
  • Bulldozer Chance Card - allows you to destroy buildings built with Hazard
  • Bonus Building Chance Card - protects your best streets from attempts to build on them various buildings like Hazard (that is, it allows you to build three buildings - Stadium, Park and some other, not written in the FAQ)


If you are a fan of Monopoly, then why not play? :)
True, now it is difficult, but I think in a day or two everything will become more or less stable.
Good luck, young monopolists;)

The Russian corporate communications market has finally resumed growth.

According to TMT Consulting , in the first quarter of 2016, the Russian fixed-line market for companies and government agencies grew by 0.5% (up to 13.2 billion rubles) compared to the previous quarter. In the first quarter of 2015, the market showed no decline or growth.

However, according to iKS-Consulting , for the whole of 2015, the revenue from business communication services for the first time decreased by 1.5-2%, to 148 billion rubles. While in 2014, revenue increased by 1.5%. The decline in revenue, CEO of iKS-Consulting, Maxim Savvatin, explained the reduction of corporate clients' expenses for communication due to the crisis.

According to him, in 2015, revenues from corporate telephone communications fell by about 5%. This fall softened the growth in revenue from data services for corporate clients by about 4%, Savvatin notes.

Revenues of the largest Russian operator Rostelecom from servicing corporate clients in 2015 decreased by 2% to 54.6 billion rubles year-on-year. The company earned 50.9 billion rubles on servicing state customers, which is 4.4% more than last year.

In 2015, the revenue from fixed-line communications of VimpelCom decreased by 12% to 46 billion rubles. In the fourth quarter, the decline was more serious - 21% year-on-year, in particular, due to a decrease in revenues in the corporate segment, follows from the company's report.

Although the revenues of MTS in the mass market of wired communications in 2015 increased by 3.1% to 29.8 billion rubles, this growth did not compensate for the decrease in revenue in the corporate market, in the segment of government orders and inter-operator services to 31.8 billion rubles (- 5 , 4%), write "Vedomosti" with reference to the company's statements.

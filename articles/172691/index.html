Free seminars for IT students

Since 2008, we have been conducting courses for students, mostly at MSTU. Bauman, MAI and MIIT. Now there are free seminars where anyone can come just by showing a student card.

Each seminar is a short and capacious story about what to do and how to deal with this technology. The task is to show how to take the first steps in a particular direction and warn against frequent mistakes. Simply put - remove the threshold of entry into the subject area. Duration - one and a half to two hours, at the end is issued a piece of paper, which may be appreciated by your teacher or future employer.

Architecture of the modern data processing center (DPC)
March 27, 2013
It helps to deal with the fact that such a data center, the difference between data centers, what you should pay attention to and so on. Despite the fact that the likelihood that you personally will be designing a data center is rather small, it is worth attending a seminar at least for the sake of achivki. The fact is that after it you will know a bunch of pitfalls from this area.

GNU / Linux server technologies
April 1
This is pure GNU / Linux propaganda from fanatical, bearded people in sweaters. True, they will be without sweaters and beards, but oh well. At the seminar, you can stop being ashamed of your lack of knowledge of Linux and generally learn more about it. A variety of Linux distributions, application services and their capabilities will be covered. Also affected are modern clustering and virtualization technologies. The plan is:
  • GNU project and the Linux kernel. Terms of use for Linux, types of licenses.
  • GNU / Linux as a server: supported hardware platforms, typical applications, features of operation and operation, advantages and disadvantages.
  • Overview of common Linux server distributions: RHEL, Centos, Ubuntu server, SLES
  • Linux as a network system, examples of basic services: DNS, DHCP, NTP, routers, firewalls, Linux-based VPN hubs and encrypted tunnels, www servers and file sharing services, MySQL, PostgreSQL, mail, a file server in a heterogeneous environment ( NFS, SMB / CIFS).
  • Linux as a virtualization platform.
  • Highly reliable clusters and other ways to ensure continuous operation of systems.

Even if you do not believe in the power of * nix, you will learn how to troll other people on IT forums.

Let's look at different types of clouds - Google Gmail, Saleforce, Google Apps Engine, Microsoft Azure, Amazon AWS. Software Development Team
April 8
It's simple. Any large company, taking in the state of the developer, is forced to retrain it and direct it from an individual approach to development to a team one. We will talk about the details and differences of approaches, plus we will discuss traditional problems and show their solution. Building loaded parallel systems (HPC)
April 10th
One of the most serious seminars of the cycle. We will look at the clusters and talk about their purpose, architecture, features, software. You will stop being afraid of such words as: 1RU servers, blades, iDataPlex ,, 10G Ethernet, Infiniband, Myrinet, learn about hardware and interconnect topology. Plus - about SAN, NAS data storage, hierarchical data storage and computing accelerators: GPGPU, coprocessors, and so on. Let's talk about clusters in the Russian Federation, and the person who collected them with his own hands will tell. At the end - a demonstration of the cluster installation, the launch of a test parallel task. I highly recommend those who need a lot of calculations, for example, in scientific calculations, design, and various kinds of Data Mining. In general, at the end of the seminar it will be painfully painful for you to look at the schedule of performance of your personal computer.

Project manager. Coordinator, leader or boss?
April 24
Everyone wants to be a PM, but few know how. The fact is that PM is not only a person who is well versed in the subject area (and knows how to write code), but also a person who sees the whole project, knows what is most important, communicates with other people outside the development team, translates their requirements into the terms of technical specifications and - what is important - does not allow the project to crumble, the admin to forget, and the lead developer to go on a date when the release rolls out. You know, they say, good PM'a difficult to kill, because it must first be found. So, I highly recommend this seminar - it is useful to anyone who plans to code more than two of them together with a duckling.

Ministry of Communications is planning to impose 10% sales tax on software producers

So, something happened that everyone had been waiting for so long - now the developers will have their own air tax.


Under the new initiative of our Ministry of Communications, a special fund is expected to be created, where up to 10% of the sales in Russia of software licenses of both Russian and foreign developers will be transferred. It is planned to spend money on raising salaries to developers working in companies engaged in the creation of products commissioned by the state (to catch up and overtake, yeah). But in order for the company to receive these cash subsidies, you only need to give the state a blocking stake (25% + 1 share).

What does this mean for the IT community?


Roughly speaking, the state pushes the entire private IT business, providing a choice of two chairs:
  • give 10% of your turnover (note, not from income, but from the volume of sales) to the mythical fund, which automatically puts on your knees and the brink of bankruptcy many companies that do not have an adequate margin of profitability or any financial cushion;
  • to give back part of their business for the opportunity to participate in the exciting Olympiad of cuts and kickbacks for state orders, giving out the wretched similarities of BolgenOS under the sign of an ‚Äúindependent red banner orthodox operating system that has no analogues abroad.‚Äù

Android-based ZenPad Enso Tablet

The newly formed company Enso, based in Hong Kong and focusing on the consumer and personal electronics market, on March 20, 2010, launched its first product, the Enso ZenPad tablet based on the Android operating system with a base price of $ 155.

The ZenPad Enso is a 132.2 by 87.6mm device. with a thickness of 14.8mm. and weighing 185 grams. with a five-inch screen on LED-backlit and a resolution of 800x480.

It is controlled by a Samsung 6410 processor with a frequency of 667MHz. For data storage, 1GB of built-in flash-memory is available and 8GB on microSD, which comes bundled (it is possible to expand up to 32GB). All this works on the basis of the Android OS version 1.6

Of the wireless technology on board, there is Wi-Fi (802.11a / b / g / n). It is possible to add an external 3G module (+ $ 35) with the following characteristics: UMTS / HSDPA / EVDO (850, 1900, 2100 MHz) + GSM / EDGE (850, 900, 1800, 1900 MHz).


Want a built-in GPS module? No problem. This model will cost you $ 180 (plus $ 25 to the base cost).

As for power consumption, the onboard is a built-in 2000 mA rechargeable battery, which will ensure that the device will work for 6 hours at maximum load on it.


As a result, we get that the top model with GPS and 3G module will cost you only $ 240 (including shipping and insurance). By the way, delivery is carried out worldwide (currently in the list of 220 countries).

Nikon V1 and J1

The friendly team consisting of Olympus, Panasonic, Pentax (already!), Samsung and Sony finally joined Nikon. This morning, the company officially announced its mirrorless cameras: Nikon V1 and Nikon J1.



While some companies are already the fourth or even the fifth mirrorless camera, the monsters of the photo - Canon and Nikon - are thinking about something of their own. What the first thing on the mind - we learned - PowerShot S100 and SX40 HS. But Nikon did what was expected of her for a long time.

Nikon 1 J1 (that's right, in the full name of the two units) - the smallest in its class digital camera with interchangeable lenses and a matrix of more than 25.4 mm. The design of the camera is minimalist. The body is light, but, at first sensation, reliable. The device weighs 277 grams.



Inside the J1 is a 10.1 megapixel CMOS format CX (13.2 mm x 8.8 mm) with autofocus support (up to 135 focus areas). The camera shoots at speeds up to 60 frames per second, and with high resolution. Working range ISO: 100-3200, with the ability to shoot 6400 in a separate mode.

Lenses


The company also introduced four new lenses 1 Nikkor and Bayonet 1.

All lenses have small dimensions and weight - for maximum portability and perfect camera balance. The first series includes models 1 Nikkor VR 10-30 mm (standard lens with 3x zoom) for shooting everywhere and everywhere; retractable 1 Nikkor VR 30-110 mm (telephoto lens with zoom), ultra-thin 1 Nikkor 10 mm for wide-angle shooting and 1 Nikkor 10-100 mm PD-ZOOM (motorized zoom with adjustable speed) for video lovers. And Nikon was quick to note that the development of new lenses 1 Nikkor is already underway.



Accessories


The SB-N5 Speedlight (in the pictures above) is compatible with the Nikon V1 and, apparently, with future Nikon mirrorless cameras. The flash can be rotated horizontally and vertically, equipped with a guiding light.

The GP-N100 GPS module is compatible with V1 and is designed for geotagging images. Information about longitude, latitude and altitude is stored automatically.

The adapter for the F-mount allows you to use any Nikkor lenses for digital SLR cameras with Nikon 1 cameras. The adapter is compatible with Nikon 1 V1 and Nikon 1 J1 cameras.

Plus covers, belts, hoods, grips, and the ability to connect an external Nikon ME-1 microphone.

New cameras will be available in the States in October. Nikon J1 with a 10-30 mm lens will cost $ 650, and Nikon V1, with the same glass in the kit, will cost $ 900.

For IE7 users, the online store has introduced a special commission.

The efforts of the developers of Kogan , a popular Australian online store, to support the work of the resource when viewing it in Internet Explorer 7 led the site owners to decide to enter a special commission for customers who still use this browser.

Ruslan Kogan - CEO of Kogan (an enterprising young man of 28 years old, he is from Bobruisk, claims that ‚ÄúOpen business in Australia is 15 minutes!‚Äù), Which owns the store, spoke quite frankly: he maintains that support for the outdated the browser costs his development team a huge amount of money; if such a need had suddenly disappeared, millions of dollars would have been saved in the entire Internet sales industry.

At the moment, the tax on the amount paid in the store amounts to 6.8%, and a rational explanation is presented to all who are curious - the amount of the surcharge is 0.1% for each month since the release of IE 7. The store is honest - a pop-up window is shown to everyone who visits the site with IE 7 , in which the user is warned that, use it with any other browser, only newer, the buyer will avoid unnecessary expenses for themselves.

IE7 was released by Microsoft in October 2006. At the moment, according to statistics LiveInternet, the share of this browser is about 2% of the total Internet audience. In general, in the global Internet segment, Google Chrome is the leader, however, with a slight margin from Internet Explorer: as of May 2012, the share of Chrome is 32.43%, while the total share of IE is 32.12%.

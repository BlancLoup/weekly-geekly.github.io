Where did the telescopes come from and why did you need to go to them from Russia


One of our Russian customers decided to build a miracle device to help the astronomical community, where he himself is. The best location for such structures is where the stars are best seen, that is, in the mountains and with a clear sky, so as to minimize cloudy days. In the Andes there are areas where the clouds are only 3-4 days a year, the air is clean, the sky is good. Therefore, so far. Place on the southern border of the Atacama Desert. The project was made before us.

To put it simply, it works like this:
  • There is a power subsystem that can start the diesel generator itself, controls the charging of the batteries and periodically checks the operability of the diesel using the engine start method. There are solar panels that work all day, feed the entire system and charge the battery line, since the main work takes place at night. There is a rack where there is iron, which all this correctly commutes and controls. In the same subsystem there is a bunch of sensors that transmit different telemetry. Entrance - brought diesel (rarely) and solar energy. Output - autonomous power to all other systems of the site and telemetry in the control unit. The system does everything automatically, but can receive manual commands from the control console.
  • Control system and GUI to it. At the site, only the integration server itself, which receives commands and scatters them into subsystems, plus is able to give consoles to specific nodes, if necessary. The main element is the remote control of the telescope and the planning of its tasks, so that people far, far away can direct it to the desired object and examine it without climbing onto our site with their feet.
  • Connection In the future, it is planned to build a radio relay line at the facility. The return end of the radio bridge will be on the fiber node in the foothills. In the meantime, a special directional LTE antenna with modem pairing, which looks at the base station a few dozen kilometers in a straight line. Fortunately, here, in the mountains, the BS is switched to the "coverage" mode, and not the "many subscribers", and there are no subscribers. Therefore, almost the entire band, without co-channel interference, is ours.
  • Telemetry. These are security cameras, a camera in front of the equipment rack, various sensors and intrusion detectors, data on equipment status. Everything is collected from the perimeter and entered into the server, which sends them to the remote machine for control.
  • Switching - kilometer power cables VVG-NG-LS 3x2.5 and kilometer twisted pair. Connect the dome with each other and with the power unit. Everything is hidden under the ground and additionally shielded.

How to make money on stocks without speculation: 5 companies with high dividends



Not everyone knows, but in the Russian financial market one of the most popular strategies for investing and saving funds is not active speculation, but receiving dividend payments.

According to statistics, the yield of payments of domestic companies is at one of the highest levels in the world - about 6%, and according to analysts' forecasts , in 2019 this figure can be a record 7%, and the total payments can increase to 10% of the total market capitalization . The numbers may not seem very big, but in fact there are companies that pay dividends with a yield of 12-18%.

I decided to study the issue and tell you about the shares of the most generous Russian companies for dividends.

"Surgutneftegaz" - yield 18%


According to the results of market research from RBC, the most profitable from the point of view of dividends in shares in 2019 were the preferred securities of Surgutneftegaz. According to the calculations of analysts, they are able to bring the investor profitability of 18%.

According to open statistics, last year the company paid out to its shareholders at 1.38 rubles per one preferred share. In 2019, the amount of payments can be about 7 rubles per share. In 2019, the share price fluctuates around 40-41 rubles per share.



Data: Moscow Exchange

NLMK, 14%


According to forecasts published on RBC, this year the company may pay shareholders more than 21 rubles per share - this will correspond to a return of 14%.

At NLMK, dividend payments are tied to the level of a company's debt, and now it is at a low level, so the risk of lower payments is minimal. At the same time, the company makes payments four times a year, and spends more money on dividends than it is required by the charter.

Bashneft, 13.4%


Another fairly generous company on the list is Bashneft. The organization has a policy according to which the minimum dividend payout ratio is set at 25% of IFRS net income.

At the end of 2018, the company's net profit amounted to $ 98.4 billion. Calculations show that this corresponds to payments in the amount of 250 rubles per preferred share (now the paper is worth $ 1775 per share).



Tatneft, 12%


Last year, the oil company almost doubled its net profit. According to the rules, Tatneft shareholders can receive 50% of net profit under RAS or under IFRS. Calculations by analysts suggest that dividend payments may be made at the rate of 63.6 rubles per share. This will correspond to a yield of 12%.

Analysts estimate the probability that in the current year payments for holders of preferred shares will remain precisely at this level, as high.

MTS, 10%


According to the estimates of Banki.ru, the dividend yield of ordinary shares of MTS can be 10.7%, while it may decrease to the level of 10.2-10.4%. In general, shareholders have been paid more than 25 rubles per share for many years.

When working with companies focused on the consumer market, it is necessary to take into account the risks of such a model - the population is not getting richer, which can affect revenue. There is no such dependence in the case of shares of exporting companies. However, it makes sense to allocate some place in your portfolio for such shares.

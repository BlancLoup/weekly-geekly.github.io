Five free tools for choosing the color scheme of the site

One of the most important factors in creating a unique and recognizable design is color. Proper selection of color schemes can determine the success or failure of the site. When a visitor takes a first look at a web page, your chosen colors send him an instant message about that page. Fortunately, there are many tools that will help you choose the right color scheme. Here are the five best ones.

1. Kuler



Adobe's Kuler has long been recognized as the best assistant of a professional web designer. Unlike competitors, there is an extensive community here and you can share the created palettes or take someone else's color schemes and modify them to fit your needs. To communicate in the community you need to register and get an Adobe ID. Colors in Kuler are represented in various formats, including RGB, CMYK, LAB and HSV.

2. Color Palette Generator



Inspiration can occur under the influence of the most unexpected occasion. For example, you may stumble upon a beautiful photo with an attractive color range. This color palette generator is created just for processing such photos. Upload files in JPG or PNG formats - and get a detailed layout for key colors. No matter what reaction you are trying to provoke in your visitors, the color palette generator will help to create a unique look for any web site.

3. Contrast-A



Individual colors never hang in a vacuum, and each shade affects the surrounding. The Contrast-A tool takes a purely technical approach to the configuration of the palette, providing detailed information about the ratio of the brightness channel (Luminance Ratio) and differences in brightness and color. This is the perfect tool if you need to create a minimalist website with a small number of colors that complement each other.

4. ColorZilla

5. Colorotate



Unlike most other color wheel generators, the Colorotate displays a palette on a three-dimensional cone. Like Kuler, users can save and edit their color schemes, as well as browse palettes made by other designers. The tool is integrated with the popular design programs of Adobe Fireworks and Photoshop.

____________

When it comes to effective web design, they talk about an intuitive user interface and layouts, but color schemes are just as important, if not more so. The tools listed here should point you in the right direction to choose the perfect color palette for your site on the first try. Other useful tools can be found on the Internet, but it is from these that you can begin to select the perfect arsenal that fits your personal style. Good luck and happy design!

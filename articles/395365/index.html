June 16 in the United States approved for use by the device to compensate for the effects of obesity, which is a simple but effective drainage system that is connected directly to the stomach of the "user". The task of the gadget is to rid the stomach of an obese person from excessively absorbed food, and as a result, from those extra pounds.



Permission to use such a non-standard way of solving the problem was adopted on June 16 at the level of the United States Food and Drug Administration (FDA), as was reported in an official press release .

AspireAssist - the so-called device is a flexible tube that is endoscopically inserted into the stomach and fixed on the discoid valve to the surface of the abdominal wall. After 20‚Äì30 minutes after a meal, the patient needs to connect an external tube to the valve and in about 5‚Äì10 minutes to remove the excess eaten.



The system is designed to remove about a third of the contents from the stomach during this time. In the vacated space is poured water. Thus, the gadget solves two key problems of excess weight at once in the company Aspire Bariatrics, which created AspireAssist: a person gets rid of extra calories and controls body weight without having to sit on long, exhausting and not always safe diets.

The patient using AspireAssist has to pay for regular visits to the attending physician to adjust the length of the drainage tube for the ability to maintain a familiar diet. The fact is that by reducing the volume of adipose tissue and body weight without a corresponding correction, the tube can weaken the contact of the valve with the skin.

AspireAssist registers the number of connections to external drainage and automatically stops serving the patient after 115 cycles (about five to six weeks of treatment), reminding the person of the need to see a doctor. If the therapy needs to be continued, the replacement cycle limiter is replaced with a new one.

Remove AspireAssist also in laboratory conditions, which takes, again, 10-15 minutes. The manufacturer emphasizes that due to the simplicity of the installation and manufacturing of the system, it costs an order of magnitude cheaper than the operation to reduce the stomach, which requires, in addition, many days of rehabilitation.



The target audience of AspireAssist is people not younger than 22 years old with a body mass index ( BMI ) of 35‚Äì55 kg / sq. M. Who cannot cope with the problem of obesity and are not able to maintain normal weight with non-surgical methods.

The reason for the FDA decision was, on the one hand, the disappointing data of national statistics, on the other - the results of clinical trials in which 176 patients participated. The entire experimental group received help with diet and a healthy lifestyle, and 111 participants in the experiment agreed to additionally install AspireAssist. After a year of wearing the device, they lost, on average, 12.1 percent of body weight versus 3.6 percent of the rest of the control group, and some of the patients managed to completely say goodbye to extra pounds. It can not be said that treatment in all 100% of patients was rosy.

DNPric.es: the largest database of domain transactions

In early March, the largest base of domain name transactions DNPric.es was officially launched .



Today, the system includes data on 317,772 transactions of 303,836 domains for a total of $ 1,264,261,540.00.

This service will be useful to those who buy domains and those who sell them. The former can compare the historical sales of similar domains and use the average valuable as a strong argument for bidding. The latter will be able to more adequately assess their portfolio, and thereby maximize their sales.

I would like to note a wide range of database search capabilities - with one line using simple syntax . For example, sales of domains with the key "net" in the zone ".ru" .

For each request, the site gives up to 100 of the most expensive sales. To circumvent this limitation, you can add an additional search on the first letters of the alphabet.

The uniqueness of DNPric.es lies in the fact that in the statistics section there are a lot of interesting data that were previously not available on any of the similar resources. Let us be a little curious.

1. By TLD

It's no secret that .com is the leader among the Internet zones. But try to guess the top ten?
Absolutely correct!
.com, .de, .net, .org, .uk, .mobi, .info, .eu, .co, and .me.


2. For brokers

How do you think, how many big players in the domain business?
Who has more than 10 million revolutions?
Today, the top five clearly loomed: Sedo, Moniker + SnapNames, GoDaddy + AfterNIC + TDNAM, NameJet, and DomainNameSales.


3. By time

After the last few years, the fall in the average cost of domains, especially in the .com zone, speculators may rejoice - this year the average prices began to rise again.

4. In length

The average cost of five-letter domains ($ 6,060.81) is higher than the cost of four-letter ($ 3,641.76) and three-letter ($ 5,653.34) names.

The first meaning of indibiome is the primordial microbes. Microbes live in the human body, and over the past hundred years their profile has changed quite a lot, very many types of microbes have most likely disappeared from the human body. In scientific literature, this meaning is quite common (see the list of links at the bottom of the text), indibiome transfers the microbial identity to the ecosystem: indigenous gut biome, indigenous microbiome of plant. Argument "The emergence of life"


Two billion years ago, the planet to which we were so accustomed looked different. The continents were flat, and the oceans infested with cyanobacteria were dark brown. Stromatolites built by these bacteria in shallow water, released oxygen and saturated them with the atmosphere.

The color of the sky began to change in the direction of blue only 2 billion years ago, after the atmosphere was saturated with oxygen. No doubt, bacteria gave life to plants, animals and fungi. With a low probability, since any bacterium was obtained from one of those that lived billions of years ago, in bacteria you can find molecules that have been in the same form for billions of years. For amino acids or nucleic acids, it seems possible. It is very important to feel this moment: no, not the presence of an ancient molecule, but the fact that all life came from bacteria.


Argument "Evolution of digestion and bacteria"


Most of all bacteria like when it is dark, damp and a lot of food. The intestines of 7 billion people carry 700 billion trillion bacteria or 700 sextillions of bacteria. Starting from the very first intestine, most likely, the first fossil that was not previously known to us - Dickensonia 600 million years ago, bacteria were supposed to live in the intestine. Further, the immune system was supposed to appear as a means of controlling the composition of bacteria. And finally, the organs of perception and the brain appeared as appendages of the intestine, ensuring its survival and reproduction. Improving with each new species, bacteria learned to live with new and new organisms. And although, theoretically, dinosaur lactobacillus can take root in our gut, our ‚Äúroot‚Äù has traveled a significant evolutionary distance, which can be seen in its DNA.

Argument "Plants, food and bacteria"


This argument is very simple. In the human body there is a limited number of enzymes, and the number of variations in chemical modifications of the fibers of plant walls is infinite. Even with the nonspecific activity of enzymes, it is difficult to imagine that among the 30,000 genes in our DNA there are all the necessary enzymes for the decomposition of fibers. And this is not necessary when there are trillions of bacteria in the intestines: let them learn, adapt to all new and new fibers. It is clear that evolution made a choice in the direction of such a symbiosis. Man extracts fiber, and bacteria digest them. Moreover, a bacterium lives in every plant in nature, which easily digests it in the correct conditions of temperature and pH. Indigenous lactobacilli can be isolated from any green leaf. By the way, fermentation is based on this.

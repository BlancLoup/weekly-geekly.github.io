Neurosystem: a revolution of consciousness

Let's try to find a complete form for the previously described knowledge and through it to make a qualitative leap in understanding the processes taking place. If you have abstract thinking and are able to give up your established views for the sake of experiment, then go.

1. Since right now it is not possible for us to test the below described for us (we need many years of continuous work on ourselves in order to reprogram the mind and get this opportunity), so the experiment will be purely speculative. But in the end I will back it up with indirect facts.
2. I do not offer something new, but simply try to tell about the knowledge already acquired by mankind in technical language in the form of a certain pseudoscientific theory. The same can be derived through other forms. I was able to do it in the framework of biology, physics and history. I think that if there is enough time, you can come to the same conclusions by studying any branches of science. Thus, all branches, once divided, converge at one point.

Let's imagine a neural network. As I explained earlier, this network will exist on two levels: physical and virtual. And if the first is just neurons that are somehow connected to each other, then the second is much more interesting. Here the network can be anything. An analyst who finds patterns in complex data sets, a predictor who finds the most likely scenarios, an adaptive control system, psychodiagnostics, and so on. Anything you can think of. But the funny thing is that we do not know how it works. That is, it is clear that there is an entrance and there is an exit. But what happens inside? In the picture we usually depict the network as something like that.



What prevents us from simplifying the presentation of our consciousness as a neural network? There are many inputs in the form of external influences, there is a way out, in the form of our reactions to them, there are feedbacks in the form of an assessment of our actions and we do not know how it works. It is clear that with some levels of assumptions it is possible to accept such a model. Further more interesting. Let's try to combine neural networks from the previous image as follows.



What did we get? Large neural network consisting of several smaller ones. Transferring to the real world we get various public associations: parties, communities, relatives, companies, etc. But one person can stand in several groups. Right, let's add the picture.



At the same time, between the output of one network and the input of the next there is a converter (a certain coefficient characterizing the degree of influence of a particular network on the group as a whole). Leaders are present in each group (the greatest influence), a person is only formally in a group, not taking part in its life (the least influence). I did not display this in the picture, so as not to complicate it much.

We also remember that groups, in turn, can also be combined into more general groups (the management consists of small departments, the organization's departments, etc.). Thus, we have a one-to-one correspondence between the virtual and the physical world (or the physical world and our model). To understand further, you need to abandon the usual understanding and try to think in the abstract.

Let's look at the second picture. What do we see there? A neural network that consists of many smaller networks.

Comparison of employee activity monitoring systems: the struggle for working hours



Recently, my director (he is the sole proprietor of the company), frightened by the crisis and not sure about the performance of our sales and accounting departments, demanded that I find a system that would show what people actually do on their computers.

The following requirements have been put forward:

  • the ability to see what people are doing right now and what they did an hour ago;
  • simple and intuitive interface - the chef is far from being an IT specialist;
  • collecting evidence - so that the employee could be called into the office and pin down with the weight of evidence;
  • smaller handwork - literally "so that the program itself tells me who did not work"

First of all, I carefully studied the article on Habr√© about the choice of time tracking systems: http://habrahabr.ru/post/234611/ . However, to use the conclusions presented there did not work. Firstly, a lot has changed over the year, and secondly, as it turned out, the working time recording systems are not the same as the employee monitoring systems. Here it is necessary to tell in more detail.

The fact is that personnel monitoring systems collect a lot of information about what people are doing on their computers. There is a lot of data, they are voluminous (and even confidential), so almost never are transferred to the cloud.

And on the contrary, URV systems collect only information on which sites a person was sitting on and with which programs he worked. Then this time is divided into productive and unproductive, and the resulting report is demonstrated to the management. Actually, it is precisely the reports of the URV system that differ from each other. But there are a lot of systems created, because anyone can write an agent that collects data about programs and a site, and it‚Äôs not difficult to fasten reports and an actual interface to the database.

The amount of data in the air-traffic control systems is small, moreover, they are all unclassified, so all the ‚Äúclean‚Äù air-warfare systems can be installed in the cloud.
Still creators of URV speak with pride of safety of use for business owners and genders. They say that, in principle, our systems cannot intercept any personal data of employees, which means no one will sue you and put you in jail (though the position of Yaware, which screwed screenshots to your TimeTracker product, is personal data may well be imprinted).

To be honest - driven by laziness and unwillingness to engage in testing, I came to the boss with a proposal to choose between Discipline, CrocoTime and the same Yaware. In response, the gendir showed me a video on Youtube, where a person tricked one of these URVs (we will not call which one), having attached an electric razor tape to the mouse. The razor worked, shook, the mouse moved, the program recorded activity. And since the screen was open 1C, the activity was recorded extremely productive. And the owner of the electric razor smoked and drove tea at that moment.
In general, Discipline and CrocoTime out of the fight dropped. Yaware has so far decided to leave, see if there will be enough screenshots alone. And in the comparison list included StaffCop , Stakhanovets and Kickidler .

Yaware


To test TimeTracker you do not need to install a local server. It is enough to put several agents on the computers of employees, and then through the web-interface to view the result. There is another option not to install anything anywhere, but to use the server of Yaware itself and look at reports on specially prepared information. To evaluate the interface and functionality - what you need.

The system is able to divide employee time into productive and unproductive, and you can configure which site / program is productive for the whole organization and for an individual employee. After all, for a marketer, Facebook can be a working tool, and for an accountant - entertainment.
Very amused time sheet. A thing in my opinion is absolutely useless. The fact that, for example, I am not sitting at my computer does not mean that at this very moment I am not setting up a server or am not sitting at a meeting.

Now for the screenshots. I would very much like to understand what guided the person who invented such a scheme for their display. The fact is that all screenshots are shown for all employees in a single list. For example, Employee 1 - 10:00, Employee 2 - 10:00, Employee 3 - 10:00, Employee 1: 10:05, Employee 2: 10: 05 ...

The screenshots themselves are small, very sketchy. To understand something in such a situation is absolutely unreal. Screenshots are made with a certain frequency, every few minutes. Therefore, if something happens between the screenshots, then it is no longer possible to track it.

And most importantly - the employee‚Äôs work schedule is separate, and the screenshots are separate. That is, looking at the report, you need to jump into the screenshots section and look for the right employee and the right time.

The chief just frowned.

There are two versions of StaffCop - Standart and Enterprise. Search reviews on Standart brought information about how old and buggy the system is. It's amazing that developers are still trying to sell it.

But it is even more surprising that there is practically no information about Enterprise on the official website, only a relatively small landing page, on which there are four small screenshots, a basic description of the functionality and a form for downloading the distribution. Like, download, install, then look?
He did. The client part runs in the browser and its appearance makes a pleasant impression. Information is collected very much, in my opinion, even too much. The fact is that the system positions itself as a DLP and a lot of functionality is dedicated to the protection of information.

There is a standard functionality URV + screenshots + snapshots from a webcam. That is, the trick with a razor will not work, you can always see whether a person is really sitting at a computer now.

But the chef expressed doubt. He did not like that all the information was scattered in various reports. You want to understand in detail what the person was doing - first look at the report on the use of time, then how many letters he wrote, then what he talked about in Skype, then the screenshot gallery, then ...

In general, this approach works when there is a security service in the company, and there are about ten people working in it. That's when they really will have time to dig in the reports, and the manager only bring memorable notes.

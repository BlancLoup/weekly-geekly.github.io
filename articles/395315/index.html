RAMDisk, or what to do if you have 128 gigabytes of RAM in your computer

Hi, GeekTimes! Absolutely incredible power and performance is hidden in the word RAMDisk - Once upon a time there were even special devices that imitate the work of a ramdisk at the iron level - Gigabyte i-RAM, for example. However, now when accessing a huge amount of RAM, building virtual disks becomes even easier. What does this give? The answer is under the cut.



To begin with, the iron configuration - to make a large RAMDisk you need a lot of RAM. ‚ÄúI have them‚Äù - 128 selected gigabytes of DDR4 HyperX Savage - so much more? Strictly speaking, my motherboard does not support this amount of RAM, but with the help of a tambourine and several other shaman accessories, I managed to start a stand, albeit with a reservation, at a frequency of 2133 MHz. Otherwise, everything is on the highest level - eight memory bars of 16 gigabytes each, HyperX Savage brand beautiful radiators, long warranty period, technical support (in case you cannot cope with setting up and installing yourself). Such a kit needs an appropriate test facility, and it is also at my disposal.

  • Processor: Intel Core i7-5960X
  • Motherboard: ASUS Rampage V Extreme, Intel Socket 2011-3
  • RAM: HyperX Savage DDR4-2666 128 Gb (8 * 16 Gb)
  • System SSD: HyperX Predator PCI-E SSD 480 Gb
  • Video card: AMD Fury X
  • Power supply: Corsair AX1200i, 1200W
  • Operating system: Windows 10 Professional (64-bit)


In the vastness of this RAM, you can deploy anything. Surely you can run all installed programs at the same time, and maybe even several instances of Mozilla Firefox (sarcasm), but today I am interested in virtual disks.

When it becomes indecent to your computer a lot of RAM you want to use it as much as possible, and RAMDisk is the most successful application. At the moment there is a fairly wide choice of software for working with RAMDisk, but I will build with the help of software that has already proven itself on the positive side and gathered a number of reviews - DATARAM Ramdisk and ROG Ramdisk.
The first software is shareware, before it was possible to purchase a quality license with a good discount when buying AMD products.
The second option comes free with the ASUS Motherboards of the Republic Of Gamers series. If the testing will take place on the base of ASUS Rampage V Extreme / USB3.1 motherboard, it‚Äôs a sin not to appreciate the possibilities of this software.







Of course, there is a great temptation to create 64 gigabyte disks, but I have two programs, which means they will try to devour the entire amount of RAM available. Therefore, I confine myself to two disks of 32 gigabytes each.





It was not at all easy to test RAMDisk, because many programs do not want to deal with it, such as the familiar and convenient PC Mark 8 - it refuses to pass the test on the indicated section, giving an error. In this case, the fact that the use of RAM Disk will lead to a significant increase in the result and the system of protection against ‚Äúcheating‚Äù, because the PC Mark 8 ratings are used by many enthusiasts to compare the performance of their systems.

Crystal Disk Mark 5.1.2


This test allows you to evaluate the drive in four modes: linear read / write, read / write blocks in 4K, linear read / write at a queue depth of 32 commands, read / write blocks in 4K at a queue depth of 32 commands.



Crystal Disk Mark demonstrates that not all yogurts are equally useful, namely the ROG disk is faster than DATARam, and quite significantly.

Copying a file from RAMDisk to another RAMDisk is a very fun procedure - everything happens almost instantly. A huge video file, the write speed of which on the RAM Disk is limited only by the speed of reading from the USB source, flies at supersonic speed inside the RAM.



Copying a disk from an SSD to a RAM Disk also turns out to be quite fast - the speed is limited by the speed of reading from the solid-state drive, similarly in the opposite direction - faster than an SSD writes it won't work.

As an application software, I propose a purely subjective assessment: working with photos in the DxO OpticsPro 10 application. The objective was to export 20 photos from Canon RAW format (CR2) to full-size JPEG with PRIME quality noise enabled. A lot in this task depends on the processor, but working with the data storage is also important.
The result is a difference of about 20 percent compared with exports to the HyperX Predator system PCI-E SSD. Is it a lot? Yes, quite a significant increase, because overclocking the processor by 10% (300 MHz) changed the packet processing time by only 1%.



For games, to use all the benefits did not work out, or rather, everything works correctly, without questions, but the FPS in two very demanding toys: Middle-Earth: Shadow of Mordor and Total War: Attila did not grow relative to the test results of games that were installed on PCI- E SSD.



So what is RAMDisk for? What can be accelerated with it? Archivers (who will need to compress / decompress data so quickly). Browsers (quick access to all the cache that you have). Saving of a resource of record of cells of SSD (the controversial moment at the accounting of cost of large amount of random access memory). content creators spinning large files in Adobe PS / AI and so on (the most realistic example).

The cost of RAMDisk does not relate to the strengths of the "project". In terms of a ruble per gigabyte, an SSD drive will be much more profitable than an enormous amount of RAM - a 128 Gb SSD Kingston KC400 with a capacity of 3,800 rubles, a 128 Gb memory kit will cost 83,000 rubles . But the speed of reading / writing exceeds the budget, and not only, SSD more than 10 times, and besides a large amount of RAM has never harmed anyone. Is that the wallet will be unhappy, but who asks him =).

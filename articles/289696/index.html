Cyanogen will release an alternative version of Android with pre-installed applications from Microsoft

Cyanogen , the developer of the popular Android firmware CyanogenMod, has announced a strategic partnership with Microsoft. This will enable the inclusion of Microsoft applications and services in CyanogenMod, which will serve as an alternative to similar Google services.

Initially, it was a search for an investor for Cyanogen, as reported by The Wall Street Journal and The Information a few months ago. But the partnership turned out to be a more profitable solution for both companies. Microsoft will support a startup in the fight against Google. And Cyanogen, in turn, will provide a new opportunity for the distribution of Microsoft mobile services.

Now mobile devices running Cyanogen OS will be sold with pre-installed applications Bing, Skype, OneDrive, OneNote and Microsoft Office.

Today, Google Android is installed in all popular mobile phone models, such as the Samsung Galaxy S6 or HTC One. CyanogenMod in the first place will be widespread in emerging markets for mobile devices.

The founder and CEO of Cyanogen, Kurt McMaster, announced high company ambitions, reports Business Insider. He expects that Cyanogen OS will become the third most popular open system after iOS and Google Android.

In March, a startup attracted investments from companies such as Twitter , Qualcomm , and Telefonica . The total amount of the round was $ 80 million. Thus, the total amount of financing of the company reached $ 110 million.

Is it possible to prepare for the CCIE for the year. Project results

Selection in an organized criminal group was an important stage after the preparation plan. After all, the task was trivial - to find literate and interested people who would not give up in a month, and in two and six months.

We could not take money - the best incentive to do something else - no one knew what would happen to the project in the future, take such responsibility, start an IT, etc. - no one wanted.

We have chosen, I believe, the best method of selection - in three stages:

1) The candidate expresses his desire on the site. He must provide a real first name, last name, telephone number and email.
892 people have applied.
This far exceeded all our expectations.

2) The candidate sends an essay with answers to simple questions:

  • Name and surname that were specified in the previous step.
  • Briefly about me.
  • Do project goals meet my expectations?
  • Why do I need to participate in this project?
  • What is important to me in this project?
  • Will I be able to allocate the necessary resources (time, technical resources, independent finding the necessary materials) required for training?
  • What kind of help do I expect from the experts?
  • What happens if such assistance is not provided?
  • What is the likelihood of my achieving results after the end of the project and how will these results be expressed?
  • If I win, what will I do with the voucher?

This has become a stumbling block for many. People simply did not understand why this was, and filled in to unleash, ignore questions or write a heresy. Well, that was their fault. At first we tried to explain to everyone individually what we were waiting for. But then they realized that it was natural selection.

The task of this tour was to assess the desire and motivation of a person to be in an organized criminal group and to be engaged in preparation.

If he cannot spend 20-30 minutes of his time on these answers, then he does not understand what he wants and cannot spend 15-20 hours a week on preparation.

We received 630 essays. It was some kind of madness, we cried, we lost faith in humanity. It seemed to us that this would never end. Each of the experts honestly spent a few weeks in the reading of each and everyone, and put his subjective assessment.

Then they were averaged over all experts and an ‚Äúobjective‚Äù appeared. No one argued with someone else's assessment, so for the same essay there could be 2 for one and 5 for another. The average score was 2.3 (out of 5).

3) Technical test prepared by Dima Figol and Ilya. 50 difficult questions from the general pool (about 60). 90 minutes, one try.

We have done everything possible to eliminate fraud and discomfort from passing the test. There is a reasonable opinion that it was possible.

Sergey Lunkov helped us transfer all the questions and images to the site, verify them and remove the flaws.

Alexander Fatin read everything on the subject of correct English grammar and unambiguous meanings.

The average score is 51.92 (out of 100). A total of 492 people passed the test.



All data were entered into a pivot table, where, using a very tricky formula for each participant, based on the essay and test, its index was derived.

And then they just took the topman - they became members of the organized criminal group.

A year and a half, which actually lasted a project, showed that we were not mistaken in our approach. The survival rate is extremely high: in the first six months, 3 people left. A year later - 10 people. At the time of April 2017, before the big half-year break - 18 people remained, 60% of the total.

But the experts had the main fear that everything would be bent in 2-3 months.
Unfortunately, the sad experience of CCIE for a year has confirmed the truism - an unmotivated person quickly loses interest in activities. The experts ran the first. Unfortunately, after only about three months only Emil and Natasha remained active in the project. When all the automation was finished, Natasha also did not want to stay for any price.
More than a year, Emil pulled the project on his shoulders.

People with burning eyes first went out very quickly.

The second obvious conclusion is that this mode is not suitable for everyone. For someone, the rhythm is too fast, but for someone it is slow. There are examples of the fact that people left the project, because self-training is more productive. Although we must understand that this is still not a minus of the CCIE approach for the year, but plus the examination of the person.

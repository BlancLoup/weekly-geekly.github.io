New Google Chrome feature for parallel multi-task solving.

Glen Murphy, styling designer and stylus


December 9, 1968 Douglas Engelbart (Douglas Engelbart) shook the world with his demonstration of experimental computer technology, which was called The Mother of All Demos (or Progenitress of all demos). One of the many innovations that Engelbart proposed was the creation of a simple, intuitive pointer device that would allow the cursor to be manipulated on a computer screen using hand movements. So the world saw a computer mouse !

Before the appearance of the mouse, the main way to interact with the computer was to enter a specific command, wait for the response of the machine and enter a new command. Therefore, the ability to coordinate the movement of the cursor on the screen with the movements of the hand was a truly revolutionary event that forever transformed the work on the computer.

However, throughout all these decades, the possibilities of the mouse were far from being fully utilized. While using one hand, we worked with the mouse, the other was not involved. As a result, the speed of information exchange was constantly growing , and the possibility of using a mouse was not.

Above the solution to this particular problem recently worked team Google Chrome. And today we are happy to talk about how to use the capabilities of your browser with double efficiency. We called the new feature Multitasking .






Multitasking mode allows you to use several computer mice at once. Thus, you can simultaneously, for example, play chess on a computer and watch a video or draw and chat with a friend.

Now Google Chrome browser supports as many computer mice, touchpads, styluses, joysticks and other cursor devices as you can connect to your computer. Imagine, now you and your friends will be able to simultaneously view not one, but dozens of Internet sites.

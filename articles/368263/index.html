People who changed the world forever: Jerry Harvey

Steve Jobs, Robert Noys, Max Factor - people changed the World forever ... One more person can safely put one more person, a legend - American sound producer Jerry Harvey.



The creator of the world's first on-board stage monitors, the founder of the legendary Ultimate Ears, yes, this man is a living legend, however, first things first.

Episode I Red Pontiac, as a ticket to the music industry




Like many wonderful events in this world, Jerry hit the world of music and big concert tours by accident.
In 1980, the Gland Slam Superjam tour with the participation of April Wine, Judas Priest and Sammy Hagar took place at the Busch Stadium in St. Louis. Jerry himself, a 19-year-old boy from Missouri, was picking his red Pontiac Trans Am in 1978, when the guys from Sammy Hagar approached him and asked to borrow his car in exchange for free tickets. Harvey's car drove onto the stage during Sammy's hit Trans Am (Highway Wonderland).

The promoter of the performance offered Harvey a job, with the result that Harvey got into the technical staff of the local group The Agents. And apparently, the guy had a really uncommon talent, otherwise how to explain the fact that, being still a minor (coming of age in the United States comes at 21), and not having, in fact, the right to work at concert venues, Jeria moves to the group's sound producer. So began the career of a sound engineer. Soon, Jerry was already working with sound from such teams as Kiss, Morrissey and The Cult.

1986, Jerry meets David Lee Roth, the vocalist of Van Halen, who is then at the height of his fame, and begins working with the sound of the team on tour. And so, in preparation for one of the concerts on the 1995 The Balance tour, the most crucial for the entire musical sphere was the conversation of Alex Van Halen, the drummer with Jerry, whose meaning was that during the performance on stage, the musicians were very It is difficult to coordinate their actions due to too loud work of stage monitors. Some decision was needed ...



... And the solution was found. And it lay, in fact, on the surface. It is strange that no one had thought of this before. We take miniature speakers for a pacemaker, supplement it with the necessary electrician, remove the ear cast, pack all this stuffing into the case created from this cast, connect it to the receiver, send a signal to the mixing console and voila ... the world's first concert ear monitoring system is ready!

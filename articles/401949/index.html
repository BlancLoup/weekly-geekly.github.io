Multicolored Smart Kettle: Review of the REDMOND SkyKettle G200S

In November 2015, Geektimes readers were told about the Internet Kettle REDMOND SkyKettle M170S. A distinctive feature of this model is the support of remote control functions - both within the home and from anywhere in the world. The electric kettle was released as part of the REDMOND Smart Home line of devices (REDMOND, by the way, was the first in the world to sell a whole line of smart home appliances), and in just one year the REDMOND tea department grew to half a dozen models with control support from a smartphone. One of the new products is the REDMOND SkyKettle G200S. If you pick up loud titles - perhaps this is the world's first "smart" kettle-lamp. The main feature of the device, in addition to control from a smartphone, is the presence of a custom multi-colored backlight.




Let me remind you, REDMOND Smart Home is a huge number of all kinds of home appliances that you can manage from one application, Ready for Sky. Of course, the program is available for both smartphones and tablets on Android, and iOS. A total of four categories of household smart appliances are presented:

Sky Kitchen - various kitchen appliances, including multicookers, electric kettles, coffee makers, kitchen scales and breadmakers;
Sky Home - heaters, fans, plinths, irons, sockets and even window cleaning robots (I will tell you about this miracle in one of the next reviews);
Sky Fitness - this direction is currently represented only by smart floor scales;
Sky Guard - reed sensors (monitoring the opening / closing of doors or windows), motion sensors and "smart" sockets with Wi-Fi (in fact - with the function of the "hub" of sensors and devices REDMOND Smart Home).



The main common feature of all models is to support two options for remote control. The first is a direct connection of a smartphone / tablet via Bluetooth, the second is that you leave the intermediary device at home (the same smartphone), through which you control a smart gadget at any distance, from another continent via the Internet from your personal phone.

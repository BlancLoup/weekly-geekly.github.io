Uber and Google: competitors or partners?



Not so long ago, Google Corporation began to work closely with the Uber service. For example, Google Ventures is one of the most prominent investors of Uber, plus, Uber is shown on Google Maps as one of the vehicles. The user is immediately granted access to the Uber application, directly to Google Maps.

Until recently, there were rumors that, since Google is developing robotic cars, and Uber is an application for drivers and passengers, companies will either become closer partners, or Google will buy Uber. But everything turned out wrong.

Now, representatives of Uber announced their intention to develop robotic cars, and Google is working on an application , the functionality of which is similar to the functionality of the Uber service. Perhaps, Google is planning to develop its own analogue of Uber, with robo-cars as a vehicle, but not now. But the company is working on a similar service, and quite actively.

Perhaps in order to compete with Google in the near future, Uber will develop its own ro-mobiles, according to TechCrunch . The company hires developers for this project at Carnegie Mellon University's Robotics Institute. The team already has more than 50 scientists representing the Carnegie Mellon and the National Robotics Engineering Center.

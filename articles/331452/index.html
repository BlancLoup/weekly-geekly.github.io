At the recent WWDC Keynote, Craig Fiderici briefly announced native support for QR codes in iOS11. This news passed almost unnoticed. And in vain.

The history of the development of QR codes in 4 actions


Infancy


A QR code was born in Japan in 1994 in the TOYOTA family unit that developed barcode scanners (DENSO WAVE). Father was recorded Hara Masahiro.


At that time, customers attacked the corporation by asking them to create a barcode in which to encapsulate more data. In a year and a half, by the efforts of Hara Masahiro and just one assistant, the task was solved.


Thanks to the engineering trick of the developers - a unique pattern of position detection in the form of three squares - it was possible to achieve not only high capacity (2953 bytes), but also unprecedented reading speed in comparison with competitors.



In honor of this advantage, the baby was called QR (Quick Response).


Childhood


Significantly grow, gain weight and become the most common two-dimensional code in the world among dozens of competitors. Two factors helped the QR code:


  1. DENSO WAVE made QR Code specifications publicly available: anyone could use them freely.
  2. The Corporation has publicly stated that even despite the patent, they are not going to present it to anyone or apply for a license.


The technology made its first confident steps in the direction of the Japanese automakers who have implemented QR codes in the current Kanban processes. Then other industries came up that saw the benefit of using squares in their cycles.


But the first Japanese camera phones in the beginning of 2002 brought the QR code to people from the stifling production walls. QR architecture allowed trouble-free reading on the most antediluvian devices.


Puberty


The QR code is mature, and seductive and already formed iOS and Android mobile platforms loomed around. The recent smartphone revolution has enabled every homeless person to have a camera phone and start using applications. Hundreds of third-party QR code scanners immediately appeared.



The technology has gone beyond Asia, but the most widely used in this period could be obtained only and only in China. Here played a role:


  • proximity to Japan;
  • low literacy rate of the population: when you are not able to read, it remains only to scan the QR code and listen in simple Chinese, what they want from you;
  • those who are literate were bored with hieroglyphs: all that remains is to scan the QR code and receive in simple Chinese what you want;
  • support by key players: according to Wendar‚Äôs general , the majority of users of the 768 million super-messenger audience regularly scan QR codes.


Only one thing interfered with getting widespread throughout the world and finally becoming an adult QR code - you had to constantly carry with you contraceptives and protect yourself with the help of third-party scanning applications. Natural contact with smartphones was hampered by the lack of native support on iOS / Android devices.


Youth


And here the most interesting begins. From September 2017, thanks to Apple‚Äôs willful decision, the QR code will begin a new life stage. After the release of iOS11 hundreds of millions of devices around the world will be at a distance of just one swipe to:


  • go to the website via the QR code;
  • call by QR code;
  • sending an SMS (to the specified number with the specified text) via the QR code;
  • add contact by QR code;
  • adding an event to the calendar by a QR code;
  • sending Email (to the specified address with the specified text and subject) via the QR code;
  • showing the place on the map by the QR code;
  • connection to a given Wi-Fi network (without the need to enter a password) using a QR code (!!!);
  • go to a specific place in App (deeplinking) by QR code (!!!).

You can see all this with your own eyes now. It is enough to download the beta version of iOS11 and use some powerful QR code generator .



Obviously, hundreds of thousands of offline businesses and online businesses that place offline advertising will begin to exploit the advantages that this short bridge between the real and the digital world gives.

CLRium # 5 Garbage Collector: Peter - Sold Out

April 13 in St. Petersburg (offline and online) and April 20 - in Moscow (offline only) will be held the largest seminar CLRium # 5 for all its existence.


After digging through the jungle of memory management algorithms, I can now finally answer the eternal question: " why do you need to know this? ". Previously, except for just for fun before this time, it was difficult for me to answer something for one simple reason: we knew nothing about how memory works in .NET. We knew that there is a GC, that there are three generations for optimization. Some of us even knew about the ephemeral segments and the card table. But it looked more like a booklet for something more complex that is not described in any way. And now, when there are both source codes and people digging into them, we can finally answer this question.


We invite you all to this seminar and during which from 10:00 to 20:00 with breaks to relieve tension from the head, very, very much will be told about how everything is arranged.



Our seminar is the rare case when knowledge without any water will flow into your head all day long. When you on the conversations you will know much more interlocutors. After all, this information is scattered throughout the Internet, and will be told only once: only at our seminar.


10 reports. Exclusively about the core . 6 of them - only about the memory management subsystem.


What exactly are we talking about?


  1. Memory allocation: all stages of this most interesting process
  2. All phases of the work of the GC (on the 45-50-minute report per phase. So much about the GC will not allow to speak at any conference).
  3. We will also talk about System.IO.Pipelines . Natalya Tsvilikh, my colleague, will tell a lot about this interface.
  4. And much more.

I hope the program turned out to be beautiful: we work on polishing it every day.

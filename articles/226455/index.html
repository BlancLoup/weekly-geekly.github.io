A gift to choose from


At some point in time, we found that some Durudar users no longer simply give away things that have become unnecessary to them, but specifically buy something to donate in the community. At the same time, they are guided by the fact that they are in the greatest demand and there are always many who want. And we got the idea: what if we open a special online store? In this store, it will be possible to buy items for gift-giving in search of someone who wants to, or to give directly to your friends as a gift for any significant occasion.

While we were considering the details and effort of a new service, we discovered a very young startup BeriDari , who offered very similar mechanics. People were asked to give each other "gifts to choose from." To do this, it was necessary to pay the amount that you are willing to spend on a gift to your friend or friend, and he further on his own, within this amount, chose from the store catalog what he liked. The trick was that the bestowed no price tags did not see, which radically distinguished the gift of choice from the gift certificate .

As time went on, we waited for the version of the service that we like, at some point in time, I could not even stand it and got a job at BeriDari to help with its implementation and bringing it to mind. However, it so happened that investors decided to close this project, and we did not have time to try anything with Daroudar.



At the same time, I realized how complex such a project is in implementation. Logistics turned out to be the bottleneck: how to make the online store assortment be as extensive as possible and always correspond to real warehouse stocks? Another difficulty was in conveying to the people a new unusual practice of giving gifts without the traditional in-person meeting in such cases. In general, I realized that alone we would not pull out such a project without proper investments.

Being engaged in BeriDari, I discovered a very beautiful American project, Wantful , which offered its users the exact same service. Their know-how was that they offered to arrange a gift in the form of a catalog of things that you personally created on their website. The catalog was printed in the printing office on a beautiful design paper and sent by mail to the recipient, who could only choose from it the one that he most liked. It was no longer necessary to pay: everything, including postage, was paid in advance by the donor. Unfortunately, this project also closed, a couple of months after BeriDari.

At the same time, Facebook tried to launch something similar. However, as I understood, in the end he, too, abandoned the original idea and came to the usual gift certificates.

Appearance and features


The Nokia Lumia 1520 is a classic touch screen monoblock.



The body is made of high quality polycarbonate. The material is matte, very pleasant to the touch and somewhat resembles ceramics.

The design ‚Äúchip‚Äù of the design is the right angles of the case and smooth contours, which the manufacturer repeats in almost all devices of the Lumia line. The edges of the protective glass are also rounded - this adds grace.



The screen is protected by Corning¬Æ Gorilla¬Æ 2 glass with oleophobic coating.
In the upper part on the front panel you can see the ‚Äúeye‚Äù of the auxiliary camera, the earpiece and the light and proximity sensors are hidden.

At the bottom of the case is a microUSB connector and speaker grille.



At the top there is only a connector for headphones or a headset.



On the left side there are ‚Äúcompartments‚Äù for installing nanoSIM and microSD memory card.



The compartments are made quite interesting: they are spring-loaded - just insert the clip from the delivery set into the corresponding hole and press a little bit - the compartment jumps out of the case itself.



The right side contains buttons that are required for all Windows Phone devices (from left to right): volume rocker, power on, camera.



The keys practically do not protrude outside the case, which gives some inconvenience in working with them - it is almost impossible to blindly press the right one, but perhaps this is a matter of habit.

On the back is one of the key elements of this Nokia flagship - a PureView camera (20MP) with Zeiss optics and dual LED flash.



The device clearly refers to the top products - everywhere you can feel the high quality of materials and the reasonableness of the construction is visible.

Using


Lumia 1520 - a very rather big device (in the photo from bottom to left-to-right - Lumia 1520, Galaxy Note, iPhone 3GS, below - iPad).



It is almost impossible to use one hand - the phone tries to slip out and its rounded edges are very helpful.
But the large size also has advantages - in horizontal mode it is very convenient to type text on the on-screen keyboard with two hands.



By the way, in the photo above you can see that the finger is just on the power button, which in some cases can cause shutdown.

But you can calmly put up with these minor inconveniences only after seeing this excellent, bright, clear screen with a size of 6 inches. The image on it is just great and is suitable for everything - viewing photos, surfing the web, reading books, working with office documents or just for playing games.

The FullHD resolution and proprietary technology, which allows to use the device even in direct sunlight, only add ‚Äúpluses‚Äù to the Lumia 1520 piggy bank.

The phone is equipped with a highly sensitive touchscreen, with which you can work even with thin gloves! Unfortunately, the work with it is not implemented perfectly - sometimes the scrolling gestures are perceived by the system as a touch.

For example, if you have an Xbox 360 at home, you can choose which device you want to play music on. Moreover, it works not only for standard multimedia applications, but also on third-party ones.

Additionally, you can turn your phone into an Xbox remote with your Xbox SmartGlass.

Playing music

I really liked another branded application - Nokia MixRadio.

Video playback

Regular software allows you to play a fairly limited number of formats. The most "safe" is the MP4. Other formats (popular MKV, for example) need to be transcoded.

The fact that the phone with regular video and music applications did not detect the DLNA servers with media content on the home network was also surprising.

In this situation, the solution came to the rescue as an application MoliPlayer Pro. This application has eliminated all the noted shortcomings: all the sources of content were immediately detected and without any problems ‚Äúover the air‚Äù I managed to play various media files (including MKV up to 15GB).

But, unfortunately, not all the tasks related to streaming video were solved (perhaps, there simply wasn't enough time to search).
For example, there was no replacement for the DS Cam application (working with a video surveillance system based on NAS from Synology) and VU + Player (watching satellite TV from compatible satellite receivers based on Linux).

Battery operation

Another feature of the flagship Nokia is a capacious rechargeable battery - 3400 mAh.

During the tests, I made this phone the main one (by redirecting all calls to it, setting up receipt of mail, installing familiar programs for social networks, all wireless interfaces were turned on, etc.), while the phone practically did not turn off. And even in such tough enough conditions, he stayed on one charge for about 36 hours.

I think that with ‚Äúnormal‚Äù battery operation it will be enough for two full days. Agree, this is an outstanding result, and even for a device with such a huge screen.

Another distinctive feature of this device is the possibility of wireless charging.

Third Party Applications

Once upon a time, when I switched from Windows Mobile to iPhone, I managed to pick up the same set of familiar software and the ‚Äúmove‚Äù was almost painless.

The subsequent transition from iPhone to Android also did not cause any difficulties - in Market, it was enough to search by the name of the corresponding programs and install the necessary versions.

With Windows Phone, the situation was not so rosy. Unfortunately, not all developers write their software right under the three platforms, unfairly forgetting the Microsoft OS.

The MS application store is built on the same principles as competing platforms, but some things are rather strange. For example, you cannot sort applications by rating, so when choosing software, you have to look through a lot of options and choose the right one.

Interestingly, some applications under Windows Phone are not at all. For example, the Firefox and Chrome browsers do not exist for this platform.

At the same time, there are a lot of low-quality applications in the Market (with terrible comments and very low user ratings).

I want to believe that this is temporary and in the foreseeable future the situation will change dramatically: leading developers will turn their eyes on this platform, the quantity and quality of useful software will grow.

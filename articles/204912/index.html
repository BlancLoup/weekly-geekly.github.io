Beehive: new insects to increase combinatorial complexity


A long time ago a great strategy with insects came out - the Hive. The game is very simple in concept: your hive suddenly came into contact with the opponent's hive. Now you need to go to visit and surround the enemy queen. The game resembles what happened once in Dungeon Keeper after accidentally punching a wall against a nearby player. So, the game, I repeat, was excellent. Everyone liked to command the ants, make grasshopper moves, and slowly crawl the bugs. But very quickly a big-big lack of a mat-model came to light - the game was extremely simply calculated by a full brute force method. Obviously win-win strategies appeared (by the way, the only world-wide popular game, which so far has been solved only for particular cases on current computing resources, is Go).



The creator of the Hive thought and decided to increase the calculated complexity of the game. For this there were two additions: "Mosquito" and "Ladybug", which gave new insects. Great, it worked out - and massive online tournaments went, in which live people began to play, not bots. But, apparently (this is my guess), one of the participants nevertheless wrote the correct mechanics using the method of branches and boundaries or annealing simulation, as a result of which another stroke was needed to complicate analytics of the situation on the table without a developed gaming intuition. So there was the last addition - "Mokritsa".

We threw the rules on the localization at the time of their appearance in the beta version, and even managed to find one bug on the test. It was fun.

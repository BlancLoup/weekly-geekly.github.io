Already history

Just think! Most of the functions, in Internet browsers that seem natural and familiar to you, are introduced by developers from Norway. These are tabs of pages (before the browser each time opened a separate copy of the program), a quick launch panel, fixation (minimization) of tabs, traffic compression, synchronization of favorites and much, much more.
Opera Software has influenced the World Wide Web as a whole. The modern Internet has become such in particular thanks to the ideas of developers from Norway. For example, in the HTML5 standard there is the possibility of using video with in-built tools without resorting to Flash. This determined the development of sites. In particular, YouTube, VKontakte, etc.
Norwegians had an impact on the IT industry as a whole. Recall only how the giant Microsoft. They became one of the initiators of the fact that it became possible to remove Internet Explorer from Windows. Alas, this did not really affect the situation, but, nevertheless, a precedent was created.

Few numbers

Numerous studies show that the proportion of Opera in the world is declining. For example, StatCounter assures that this figure dropped from 2.62% in 2009 to 1.69% in 2012.


StatCounter. Worldwide Browser Market Statistics

By the way, I didn‚Äôt just mention Russian users. In the countries of the former Soviet Union, the Norwegian browser was especially popular. Alas, the statistics in this case turns out to be quite eloquent: from 36.57% (2009) to 19.82% (2012) according to studies of the same StatCounter.

The last three months of this year show no changes - the presence of the browser is still decreasing. In Russia, about 12% is still, and a little more than one hundredth in the whole world.

Drains

Another indicator that clearly speaks of ailment or good company health is the value of shares. Considering the trends indicated above, I intended to see a sad picture. Stock prices have reached their record

I'm afraid to make unambiguous conclusions. I will express only my modest and very cautious opinion.
Last year, news feeds splashed with messages that the social network Facebook is going to acquire a Norwegian office . This promises to the shareholders a good profit and the securities began to actively buy, which caused a sharp jump in value. Further the snowball, which picked up speed, was difficult to stop. Now information about the acquisition of Opera Software no longer slips, but the market has already responded. Hope and wait. The company is doing very badly.
Is an unreasonable increase in stock prices for Norwegians good? I think no. After all, the value of shares must be supported by some real indicators and achievements. Epilogue

The company, which has long been a trendsetter in browser building, is now lagging behind and trying not to even catch up with its competitors, but to return what it has lost. I would like to say that this happened with the release of Opera 15. However, it is not. The process began a long time ago.
The Internet, like the entire IT industry, has changed significantly in 15 years. Over the years, there are no companies that specialize only in browsers. Microsoft is behind Internet Explorer, Chrome has been nurtured by the giant Google. Today, only mastodons can afford to develop programs to enter the Global Web and then as an optional lesson. Like, for example, Yandex did by releasing its browser a year ago. Firefox stands apart, but there, you shouldn't forget, there is a huge army of open source enthusiasts and many other programs are made on the basis of firewall.

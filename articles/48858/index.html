Telephone - a new SIP client for Mac

I present a VoIP-program under Mac - Telephone. This is a native program written in Objective-C / Cocoa. I do not like the fact that most softphones have a terrible interface, besides trying to visually look like iron phones. It seems to me that ordinary users do not have to draw a mobile phone on the computer screen, so that they understand where to click. That's what happened.

When you enter a name or phone number, a search is performed in the system-wide address book (which, by the way, can be synchronized by regular means between several computers and an iPhone). Visible search result.


Phone call.


Settings.






Supported codecs:
  • G.711 family (PCMA, PCMU);
  • Speex / 8000 (narrowband);
  • Speex / 16000 (wideband);
  • Speex / 32000 (ultra-wideband);
  • iLBC;
  • GSM;
  • L16.

Works sending DTMF, in the presence of Russian and English interfaces. System Requirements: Mac OS X 10.5 Leopard on Intel platform.

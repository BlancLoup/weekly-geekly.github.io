The idea of ‚Äã‚Äãhaving portable Bluetooth acoustics has long given me no peace. However, the price / quality ratio still did not come to a reasonable, and still leaves much to be desired. After all, in fact, all portable speakers - this is the usual multimedia speakers + Bluetooth module + lithium-ion battery. And if you combine these components yourself, you can get quite a good budget option.

Spring is in full swing. And the need for portable acoustics is now highly relevant. After all, you can take it with you and into the company outside, and on a trip out of town for a picnic, and wherever mobility is needed, but there is no outlet. In other words, finally, you need to acquire such a gadget! But the desire to save is also not lost. Quite the contrary. And that means it's time to roll up your sleeves and do everything yourself. So, let's mark the goal - quickly, effortlessly, from available parts, assemble inexpensive, high-quality portable acoustics that are not inferior to ready-made gadgets and are significantly lower in price. Getting started.

Short plan:

  1. Select multimedia acoustics.
  2. Select Bluetooth module.
  3. Choose a lithium-ion battery (portable charger).
  4. All this connect blue tape .
  5. Profit Enjoy portable music with friends.


Selection of components.

1. One of the most important characteristics of portable speakers, in my opinion, are size and weight. Based on these mandatory criteria, we will look for the most popular and inexpensive model of multimedia acoustics. Ok, google) After analyzing the characteristics, the choice fell on the Sven 315 speakers. For a price of about $ 8, we get very compact speakers with high-quality multimedia sound. A cursory examination of the sites www.dx.com and www.aliexpress.com found suitable modules for $ 6-7 ( http://www.dx.com/s/bluetooth+audio+receiver+module ). 3. And the last component is a lithium-ion battery. Everything is simple here. Assembly.

First of all, we need to parse the SVEN 315. I used the video instruction.



Then we disassemble the Bluetooth module, remove the card. This is only needed for my specific 30-pin model (for boards ordered from http://www.dx.com/s/bluetooth+audio+receiver+module this is not required).



Having disassembled the column, shorten the audio input wire and solder it to the Bluetooth module. We also branch and solder + 5v power from USB.





Fasten the inside with all thermoplastic glue so that nothing dangles.



After installation can be checked. We connect the speakers via USB to a portable charger. And we pair with the phone.



That's all.

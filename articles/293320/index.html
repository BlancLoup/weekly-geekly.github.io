Grow on your own money (from sales or pre-orders), do not rely on investors or grants


Help, if it is not charity, is rarely gratuitous. Financial assistance imposes additional restrictions and conditions: you need to focus on the investor in matters on what and in what amount to spend the money; report, spend time approving plans. The grants are about the same: reporting, targeted expenditure of funds without a focus on changes in the company's development, market conditions, and so on.

I tried to develop a project for grant money and, frankly, more ‚Äúcomposed reports‚Äù than I worked. Building a business with your own money may be slower, but, from the point of view of restrictions, it is much more convenient.

Focus on priorities


The Pareto principle: ‚Äú20% of efforts give 80% of the result, and the remaining 80% of efforts - only 20% of the result‚Äù - has repeatedly proved its viability, including in business.
To set priorities and deal only with important matters - I consider one of the most important tasks of a manager. We all like to spend time analyzing e-mail, meetings, and other current affairs: it does not take a lot of effort, but it takes a long time. But a great result, alas, does not bring.

Spend on current, minor tasks strictly specific time and do not depart from the plan. After all, another well-known law says: ‚ÄúWork takes as much time as it is allocated for it‚Äù (Parkinson's law).

Pay special attention to the team


Your team is your support. Be attentive to the selection of candidates: even mine, not such a great experience, showed that without checking the candidate‚Äôs recommendations, his background, you can accumulate big problems.

Do not yet succumb to the popular stereotype about start-ups: a young, purposeful and fast-learning team works in a young company. A brilliant product can make either a genius or an experienced professional. And since there are not so many geniuses in statistics among us, I would rely on experience. There is another option: in the process of work, your young team will have that experience. But it will take a lot of time, and in the process will be filled with many cones.

Cool product and hope for word of mouth - a combination that ‚Äúshoots‚Äù very rarely. Regulate and optimize business processes


The organization of work in a startup is different from a large organization with its regulations, memos and orders. A small company usually does not bother to make detailed and clear plans, describe and optimize internal processes. And because of this, when a company grows to a team of 10-20 people, time is lost, mistakes are made, non-priority tasks are performed.

I decided to make a difference by working on my last project. Finally, we described the company's processes, documented the work scripts (it is now easier to introduce new employees in the course of business), analyzed the working hours of all employees (using our own product) and found problems that slowed us down.

The result: the training of new employees is 2 times less than the time of existing specialists, we more accurately plan the time spent on tasks and, most importantly, on time, without any delay, release releases.

Who-who lives in autoload?

Over the past six months, I often come across the fact that customers or friends complain that a computer with MS Windows OS is very slow, even though the processor is the newest, memory is more than enough. In most cases, this is due to numerous quantanchers, updates and other small programs that are loaded along with Windows. In rare cases, there are exotic viruses on the PC, rootkits that are not found in the whole world by the vaunted antiviruses, and if they are, they cannot be removed (exceptions such as AVZ ).
Basically, my actions were reduced to instantly getting into the standard msconfig utility, unchecking the checkboxes near the intuitively intended trash and turning off all unnecessary services. In most cases, it helped ... But in the case of spyware and the rootkit - no. Antivirus shrugged, msconfig was virgin empty.
There is an unlimited number of startup-managers, but with the majority there is the problem of assessing the "harmfulness" of the program.

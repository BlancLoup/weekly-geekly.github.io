List of shortcut keys to restore the factory settings of laptops

Many laptop owners know that if their mobile assistant was purchased from a preinstalled OS, then in 90% of cases there is a special partition on the hard disk that stores the operating system image with drivers and programs. Someone deletes this section immediately, in order to free up space, and someone does not even know about its existence. And some use it for its intended purpose. You can enter the recovery menu in the Windows environment, but if the system crashes, how to be? Restore the system using hotkeys. Here is my little list of these shortcuts.

After turning on the laptop, we start to feverishly press:


F3 - MSI;
F4 - Samsung. Under OS you can through Samsung Recovery Solution III;

F8 - Fujitsu Siemens. In general, it often allows you to get on other laptops (through troubleshooting) in the original utility for Recovery.
F8 - Toshiba
F9 - ASUS;
F10 - Sony VAIO. Under OS, you can through VAIO Recovery Utility;
F10 - Packard Bell;
F11 - HP Pavilion;
F11 - LG;
F11 - Lenovo ThinkPad.
Alt + F10 - Acer. Before this, select Disk-to-Disk (D2D) in BIOS;
Ctrl + F11 - Dell Inspiron;
F8 or F9 - Dell XPS. I forgot.
Clamp [Alt] - Rover

EZ-Key EZ8000 and Windows Vista / 7: Free cheese can be found only in a mousetrap

I want to share with you, dear Habr readers, my recent experience in purchasing and installing a budget multifunctional keyboard from a well-known Taiwanese manufacturer under Windows 7.

In addition to a brief overview of the keyboard, the article contains a description of possible problems when working with Vista / 7 and their solutions, as well as a few general tips on buying and setting up a new hardware.


Prologue



After 3.5 years of service, faithfully, my old ‚ÄúGenius‚Äù was finally sent to the closet with honors. In search of a replacement for it, I chose the EZ-Key EZ8000. Why on it?

There are maniacs ready to lay out hundreds of cu for heaped cybords with launch buttons for all possible office and multimedia applications, shortcuts of popular sites and shortcuts for frequently used spells in WoW. There are minimalists for whom 104 keys - and none more. I, because of my working needs (work with office documents, programming and surfing the Web), only a basic set of add. functions in each of these areas. And secondly, the role is played by the price - none of the keyboards I purchased earlier cost more than 100 UAH (12-14 cu). And I did not intend to break the tradition.

Episode 1. A quick look



Ez-Key EZ-8000 met all my requirements

  • it contains the minimum I need for office files (launch Word / Excel, copy / paste buttons from the buffer, text scroll)
  • it contains the minimum I need Internet f-th (launching the browser, back and forth)
  • it also contains multimedia f-ii
  • it has a USB output. Even despite the fact that only one output (not a hub) is also very good, because lean to the case or dig blindly with a flash drive in its back panel rather fed up

Episode 4. New Hope



When I got home, I started connecting the keyboard to my home computer. Pre-made a restore point, put the driver. Got the same symptoms. I made a rollback and began to google. I found the site of the manufacturer and on it the page of the product I purchased: www.ezkey.com.tw/product/product.php?i=97

Below there is such an inconspicuous link - Downloads, I press it and swear - the most recent version of the driver has been released already ... 2003/10/10 and is meant for Win 98, Win 2000, Win Xp, Win Me. There are no more recent versions of the driver! As the saying goes, take a rest, come on ...

On the other hand, there was still a link to the manual, I downloaded it and went deeper into reading. And over time, everything fell into place.

It turns out that in addition to the standard Num Lock, Scroll Lock and Caps Lock switches, this keyboard also has an additional Office Lock switch (at first, in the image on the button, I thought that this key should open the ‚ÄúOffice‚Äù menu in MS Office 2007). When Office Lock is on, the F1-12 buttons become shortcuts to actions that are often used in office applications, and the Num Lock turns into the euro sign input button. And as you already guessed, this mode is on by default! Turning off Office Lock, I was able to turn off Num Lock.

With the USB connector everything turned out to be much easier. In addition to the color gamut, each model is available in variations with a specific set of connectors. I got the most ordinary PS / 2 keyboard. An additional USB connector is needed ... You guessed it correctly - yes, only to connect the USB connector on the keyboard itself! A kind of USB extension cable. Therefore, you need to connect the keyboard to two ports at once - both in USB and in PS / 2.

And the driver turned out to be the worst of everything ... Nowhere on the Net could I find more recent official versions. After two hours of googling, I miraculously stumbled upon the site of one remarkable person - Ruslan Shcherbatyuk. At one time he was faced with a similar problem. Remember my thesis that in this situation, either try to return the money or use the keyboard as it is or write the driver yourself? Ruslan did just that - sat down and wrote a driver. And then decided to share it with other suffering. On the astrumsoft.com/ez7000 page you can download the right ‚Äúmedicine‚Äù. Despite the fact that the Ruslan driver is designed for the 7000th model, most of the functions of the 8000th work quite well.

From the additional functionality, I immediately got used to the buttons for quickly switching between windows and quickly closing the window. ‚ÄúMy computer‚Äù and ‚ÄúCalculator‚Äù are also convenient, but they should be placed next to them, and not the devil-knows-where on the other end of the keyboard. ‚ÄúCut-copy-paste‚Äù is also very convenient, although I have not yet retrained myself to always use them - I often press the old proven Ctrl + C, Ctrl + V. findings


The story of the acquisition of the keyboard brought the following conclusions to my life experience:

  • free cheese is only in a mousetrap. If you see a product with rich functionality at a ridiculously low price - think about the reasons for the markdown. Maybe he is defective, or it is a buggy noname or zakos under the brand, or he has compatibility problems, or the seller has ‚Äúthrown‚Äù at all.
  • Before buying hardware, go through the forum, find out about its real (in practice, and not written in the description / manual) compatibility with the OS under which you plan to use it.
  • Also, before buying, be sure to check all the money-back conditions and warranty obligations so that you can back up.
  • never test iron at work. Work is needed to work. Well, in the event of a breakdown of office equipment, your bosses will not stroke your heads (think what would happen if I had to reinstall Windows at work?)
  • If you have any problems, you should not freak out, call support and immediately swear. It is necessary to postpone the check for a while and check it in a more relaxed atmosphere.
  • Even if you are a super cool "hardware worker" with a wealth of experience in building PCs, read the instructions carefully. It can be written a lot of interesting things. Including about the various "features" that you just need to know, but not that they seem to be "bugs"
  • Google is your friend, feel free to contact him for help.
  • Try searching for unofficial drivers or drivers for similar models, if the search for official drivers for the OS you need was not successful

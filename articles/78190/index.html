Keyboard fan

It turns out that fans are not only fingers, tail and, in fact, a fan, but also a keyboard. The concept of just such a keyboard called Keystick was presented by the designers Yunsang Kim and Eunsung Park. Unusually, but compact and convenient.

Designers Yunsang Kim and Eunsang Park proposed the concept of a folding Keystick keyboard, made on the principle of a fan. This, according to their idea, should solve many of the problems inherent in modern conventional keyboards.

Firstly, such a keyboard structure will largely solve the problem of bacteria and dirt, which in conventional keyboards accumulate under the keys for a year, and ultimately lead to diseases of people. In the Keystick Keyboard, trash simply will not be where to pile up.

Secondly, this keyboard is more compact. When assembled, it will be completely small in size, comparable to the size of a remote control from a TV, music or multimedia center.

Thirdly, such a keyboard will be much more convenient to use than the usual one. After all, the usual convenience is often placed on special legs so that it is at a slight angle to the table. So it is more convenient to human fingers. And in the Keystick keyboard, each row of keys is already at a slight angle to the table.

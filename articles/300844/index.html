Finalized the community track program of the DevCon 2016 conference



Hello!

We are glad to present to the participants and still non-participants who are planning the final program of the community track. This year we decided to change the trajectory a bit and give a voice to the community. The trajectory was changed, and 25 speakers will speak in a community track in two days. These are people involved in completely different projects from completely different companies from different countries, but all of them are united in what they say about real experience and the conference participants will have the opportunity to ask around those who are familiar with this or that technology or solution in the fields. Under the cut - the details of what will be discussed in the track. Since there will be a broadcast on DevCon and then recordings, all this unique content will be available - don't miss it!

When I formed the program and talked to the speakers, I tried to minimally change the way in which the narrative will take place. Therefore, expect hardcore in some places, a lot of Open Source and real experience :) Trying to pick up relevant topics, I involuntarily got an intersection with the main program in the topics (which is inevitable, of course), but the tuning of presentations allowed me to align the topics with the stories about how real projects, pitfalls, etc. were made. In some reports, it somehow turned out that there is practically no mention of Microsoft.

So, in the community track you will hear about several main topics:

Project management:
  • Alexey Losev will talk about how to build the development process in order to do what is needed,
  • Igor Shcheglovitov from Kaspersky Lab will show how to analyze key performance counters and not die in the process,
  • Andrey Akinshin will talk about the benchmark performance, which he maintenit and which Hanselman recently wrote as great to use


Microservices and containers. Since the topic is hot, then reports decently.

  • A report on Continuous Delivery for microservice projects will be made known in the community by Martin Kulov ,
  • Eugene Agafonov from ABBYY will talk about how to use the whole thing to the developer and what it means for him
  • Vladimir Turovtsev from Logrocon, Microsoft‚Äôs gold partner and well-known ALM experts, will show in half an hour how to build Continuous Integration in a company in 72 hours,
  • Yuri Krutilin , DevExpress, will give a talk about real-world experience in automating mobile device testing,
  • Bernardin Katich will show how to do Scrum in large projects


Internet of things. Rich in reports topic.

  • Alexander Surkov will talk about .NET Microframework (we haven't seen this topic for a long time!),
  • Katalin Gheorghiu - about using PowerBI for visualization tasks in IoT projects (in fact, this is not a report, but a series of demonstrations),
  • Sergey Grebnov will show a super demo with AllJoyn, bots, TV and cloud services,
  • Alexey Lyubko will show you how to build a really useful IoT project - a cloud brewery.


Web development. One of the most difficult issues for me is that you can tell people that it would be interesting enough and not told many times already? Found

DevCon 2016

Twitter has banned US intelligence services use analytics service

US intelligence agencies, like Russian colleagues, are obsessed with monitoring social networks.




Twitter has blocked US intelligence agencies access to the Dataminr analytical tool for data mining tweets on geotags in real time, writes WSJ . This service is used by companies in the financial industry, information security, management, risk management, media companies, etc.

Twitter owns a five percent stake in Dataminr. Dataminr management is forced to listen to the shareholder on whom the business is completely dependent. It was at the request of Twitter that the special services accounts in the Dataminr analytical service were closed, a source in the intelligence community told the WSJ. He said that Twitter is afraid to be caught in collaboration with US intelligence, that is, he does not want to have anything in common with them.

Scouts will find other ways to tweet data, but the fact of disobeying them is certainly unpleasant.

In a press statement, Twitter representatives commented that tweets are open to everyone, and the security services can analyze them on their own, like any other user.

The source also said that only the accounts of intelligence agencies in Dataminr were closed after the end of the trial period, while the Department of Homeland Security account is still active.

Intelligence agencies used Dataminr for intelligence and rapid response.

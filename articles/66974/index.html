Opera releases third beta version of Opera 10 browser

Oslo, Norway - August 13, 2009 - Opera Software has announced the release of the third beta version of the Opera 10 browser, available at www.opera.com/next . Performance and stability have become key priorities in its release. In addition, Opera Turbo technology has been improved to speed up the transfer of data by compressing it. The browser interface has been modified to work more effectively with websites compared to previous beta versions. Since its introduction, Opera 10 has been downloaded more than 10 million times.

What's new in Opera 10 beta 3?
Tabs display. Many users who have tested the previous beta version, spoke in favor of a more convenient arrangement of tabs. In response to this, in the third beta version, Opera provided the possibility of placing tabs not only in the upper and lower, but also in the lateral parts of the screen. The size of the tabs can be changed, and the display of thumbnails of sites opened in tabs can be turned on and off.

User interface. Designer Jon Hicks continued to update the user interface, introducing several new elements to work more efficiently with the Opera browser.

Multilingual. The third beta version comes with an interface in 38 languages ‚Äã‚Äã(including Russian, Ukrainian and Belarusian). This makes Opera 10 more accessible to users all over the world.

Failure protection. The integrated crash recorder made the third beta version more resilient to failures.

More turbo. Opera Turbo technology has undergone improvements and now even more improves the speed of working with websites on low-speed channels.

Is Opera Unite technology included in the third beta?
In June 2009, Opera announced an alpha version of its new technology Opera Unite. This technology will not be included in the third beta version and in the final release of Opera 10. As long as it remains in the laboratory version stage. We continue to work on Opera Unite to release its beta version as soon as possible. About Opera Software ASA
Opera Software ASA is an industry leader in the development of web browsers for desktops, mobile phones and other devices. The headquarters of Opera Software is located in Oslo (Norway), and territorial offices are located in several countries around the world.

Startup or proven business - the choice is yours

Startups are now a very fashionable topic that everyone knows about. Everyone wants to create a startup to become rich and famous like Pavel Durov Mark Zuckerberg. And some even succeed in this, and then success stories appear on the Internet, as someone created another start-up for little money and his company has already passed over a thousand employees and has become successful and he is famous. Especially startups are on the IT field, so I decided to write on this resource, maybe someone will think about it.

Prehistory


Recently I was approached by a friend who worked as a system administrator for a while, then he reached the head of the department and now earns good money from his main job, but decided to go into ‚Äúfree swimming‚Äù.

His question was:
- Iskander, I have a million rubles, I want to create a startup - a game for social services. networks. Tell me how it is better to distribute this money, how much programmers to take with what salaries, where else it will be necessary to spend money, etc. In short, help create a business plan.

But I urged him to think healthily - yes, the authors of some startups really earn good money. But how many startups that have not earned anything? A million rubles is a fairly large amount of money, and she does not really want to risk it. A startup is always a risk. Think whether it is worth it?

Story


Of course, I helped him - guided by the approximate terms of development, the salaries of programmers in our region and everything else.

But then I offered him an alternative option - if you have money, why risk it once more? Why not start up franchise work instead of startups?

For those who do not know, I will explain - franchising is a form of commercial cooperation when a franchisor company transfers franchisee rights to sell their goods and services. Often, the franchisee also receives the rights to use the trademark, corporate identity, all the know-how, as well as support and advice from the management company.

In my opinion, a franchise does not have all the flaws that a start-up business has:
- when opening a franchise business, the risks are much less - the franchisor company is also interested in developing its network and will not work with obviously unpromising partners
- no need to step on a rake, which has long been passed - the management company will prompt and help with all questions that have arisen, and in a startup you can only rely on yourself.

At the same time you get almost "your" business, i.e. the main goal, for the sake of which they usually go to start-ups in the field of IT, is fulfilled: ‚Äúto stop working for an uncle‚Äù.

Yes, the franchise has an obvious drawback - certain funds are required for the opening, whereas a ‚Äústart-up‚Äù can be done in your garage or in a university dormitory. But let's face it - how many such successful startups in Russia have you seen?

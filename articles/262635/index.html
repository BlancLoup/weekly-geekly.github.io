Internet Explorer 11 discovered zero day vulnerability

The leaked data from the Hacking Team archive never ceases to amaze us. This time we are talking about the RCE zero-day vulnerability in the latest version of Microsoft's web browser, Internet Explorer 11. The vulnerability was assigned the identifier CVE-2015-2425 and it was closed by the MS15-065 update released today. The first vulnerability information was published by TrendMicro. Thus, we are talking about at least five zero-day exploits that were in the arsenal of the Hacking Team cyber group.



After finding a series of dangerous 0day exploits in Flash Player, Mozilla announced that the default player was disabled in all versions of its Firefox web browser.

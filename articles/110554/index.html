Skype Malfunctions - Clarification

Here is a quick explanation from Skype regarding what happened yesterday.
Yesterday, we noticed that the number of online users suddenly began to decline, and we began to understand what was happening.

Skype does not look like a regular telephone network or the IM network, since it relies on millions of individual connections between computers and phones. Some of these computers act as "super-sites" (supernodes, supernodes) and are something like Skype telephone directories. When you want to talk to someone, and your Skype application cannot immediately find that person (for example, because he connects from another place or from another device), your computer or phone tries first to find a superrooper to find out how to contact who you need.

Usually available a large number of superuser. But yesterday, many of them went offline due to a problem affecting some versions of Skype. Since the Skype application needs super sites to establish a connection, many of you were unable to connect and go online.

Corporate products such as Skype Connect and Skype Manager continued to work in regular mode.

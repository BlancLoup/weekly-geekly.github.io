So thinking. There are several forms of thinking (3-4 in different approaches). They develop in a certain sequence. The first subject-effective thinking. It is associated with direct activities. Those. in order to perform some kind of mental operation on an object, you must have it in your hands. The second, visual-shaped. To perform a mental operation, it is necessary to see the object and perceive it (that is, to form its image). This type of thinking is dominant in children of preschool and primary school levels [Maklakov AG]. Third, verbal logic. At this stage, the child is already able to think using words as symbolic reflections of objects. In this case, the object may not be in sight. And the last, abstract logical. This thinking is through even higher levels of generalization.

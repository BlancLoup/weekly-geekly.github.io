MTS opens file sharing

MTS announced the launch of the file-sharing internet service redpost.mts.ru . With it, you can send files up to 2 GB to specific email addresses, i.e. file sharing is targeted. It is curious that the service is not tied to MTS subscribers in any way - it does not require entering a phone number and works through the web interface. The question arises: how is MTS going to make money on this? ..


The only option that I see is the ability to make money on traffic from their subscribers, who will use the service from mobile phones. Those. in fact, there is a promotion of traffic consumption. But, in my opinion, very few people, without using the unlimited tariff, will download large files from a cell phone. So the initiative looks weird - at least.
On the other hand, through this service, you can send a pirated copy of anything (the film, for example, is quite suitable in terms of volume) - you only need to specify the recipients. At the same time, no responsibility for pirated content is mentioned - they are even going to check for viruses themselves.

Details about the service from MTS:
Who can use
Subscribers of any mobile operators who have personal computers with Internet access can take advantage of the new file-sharing service from MTS. The service does not require registration on the operator's website, both for the sender and for the recipient of the transferred files.

How it works
To send files, the user must specify in the opened sending form on the main page of the redpost.mts.ru service up to 20 recipient email addresses, leave his return email address, and also attach the files he wants to send, and click the send button.

The email addresses specified by the sender in the form of sending, recipients will receive a notification with information about the uploaded file. The sender's e-mail receives a notification about the successful delivery of files to recipients. Files uploaded by the user for sending to recipients are stored on the Redpost server for no more than two weeks.

Electric Scooter as a personal vehicle

Now new vehicles are emerging around us, which so far are perceived more like toys for pleasure than transport. But, perhaps, in a relatively short time, there will be a change in attitude towards electric scooters, monowheels and other light and affordable electrical devices. For convenience, perhaps, it is worth uniting all of them with one name - personal portable electric transport.



Let's look at the pros and cons of using personal electric vehicles, calculate the approximate cost of use and think about whether we could use it as a utilitarian vehicle, and not just to go on a weekend in the park.

Usage scenarios


There are 2 main scenarios for the use of personal portable transport:

1 - we need to get from point A to point B. All the way we pass on our personal vehicle. Everyone has their own preferences regarding the maximum possible distance, suitable weather, mood, fatigue. My personal experience is this:

  • need a positive temperature
  • no heavy precipitation
  • good mood

Then, the normal distance for me will be about 15 kilometers in the conditions of the city, which I will be able to travel with pleasure by bicycle or electric scooter, preferring such transport to a car or a subway. Repeatedly traveled along this route:



2 - we need to get their point A to point B, the distance between which makes it impossible to comfortably move on their personal electric vehicles - too far, too bad weather, not prepared infrastructure (for example, there is only a curb of the highway without alternative detour routes).

In this case, the path is divided into three sections -B BC and CD, where -B and CD are sections where we move ‚Äúunder our own power‚Äù and BC is the section where we are driven by a subway, monorail or electric train.

Actually, this use case is the way by which most of those who cannot / do not go there by car get to work every day in large cities.

Requirements for the vehicle depending on the use case will be different. If you need to move in the first scenario, then, in general, it will almost do not care what our vehicle's weight and portability (unless of course affect the features of storage / parking, the presence of an elevator at home, etc.) But, overall driving comfort will be important, mileage on single charge and maximum speed. Most likely, a full-fledged electric bike with a large battery and a powerful engine will be the best means of transportation for the first scenario.

For the second scenario, the very possibility of changing into public transport becomes extremely important, so weight and folding will be more important parameters than maximum mileage and speed. The optimal means here are not big, light and folding structures: monowheels, electric scooters, hoverboards.

average speed


According to subjective impressions, trips at a distance of up to 5 km are definitely faster than by car (where time is lost to start / warm up / drive out of parking / park) and even faster than public transport (where you need to walk to a stop, wait, walk from a stop) .

By my estimate, with a maximum speed of 30 kilometers per hour, the average speed is about 20 kilometers per hour: time is lost at traffic lights and subways. Of course, it strongly depends on driving style and road conditions.

The average speed of travel by car in Moscow is 55 kilometers per hour, but including the night Moscow Ring Road and central lanes during the morning or evening rush hour is not a detailed enough parameter.

For, again, personal observations in those moments when so many cars accumulate at traffic lights that they do not have time to drive in one cycle of traffic lights, movement on a scooter becomes faster than a car.

Conclusion


With a few assumptions, it can be argued that movement on an electric scooter is cheaper and comparable in time costs in a metropolis with a car, taxi and public transport.

Expensive compact models can be used for both typical scenarios (both trips from point A to point B, and trips when personal transport is used to overcome the ‚Äúfirst‚Äù and ‚Äúlast mile‚Äù with high-speed public transport in the middle).

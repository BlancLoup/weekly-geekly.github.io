Power Glove for Nintendo


Price: $ 80 in 1989 ($ 152 in 2013)
Game console: Nintendo Entertainment System (Famicom in Japan)
Manufacturer: Mattel in the USA, PAX in Japan
Sold to about 100,000 in the USA

The first controller for games that tracks the position of a player‚Äôs hand in space is Power Glove, which was released in 1989.

Special receivers were installed on the TV, and the glove was connected to the NES. The sound emitted by the receivers was reflected from the glove, after which the position of the glove in space was calculated by triangulation method. The system perceived the movements of the fingers: it was possible to box with a blow, shoot with the flexion of a finger, etc. Glove buttons let you program actions.

PlayStation Move


Game console: Sony PlayStation 3
Manufacturer: Sony Computer Entertainment
Sold: 15 million

Sony in 2010 decided to finally present an analogue of the Nintendo Wii, so that PlayStation fans could also jump in front of the screen and shoot down vases. The principle of the motion-sensitive controller for the PS3 is based on contact with the camera. The LEDs inside the sphere, the accelerometer and RS (rate sensor) allow you to recognize the position, speed and angle of the controller.

Microsoft Kinect


Just a couple of months after the release of the PS Move, Microsoft introduced Kinect, a touchscreen gaming controller for the Xbox 360. Two years passed before it became possible to use this system on a PC. The system consists of two depth sensors, a color video camera and a microphone grille. In 2011, Microsoft sold over 10 million Kinect systems, setting a new Guinness World Record.

Kinect recognizes voice commands, poses, gestures and objects. Even applications are being developed that understand the gestures of the deaf-and-dumb and translate it into text.

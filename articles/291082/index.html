In parallel with the global expansion, Yota Devices is developing its own tablets and electronic textbooks.

At the Kazan conference "IT in the military-industrial complex" it became known about the new plans of Yota Devices . The company is developing its own tablets and electronic textbooks. The plans were voiced by Sergey Chemezov, General Director of Rostec State Corporation :

In September 2013, the order of formation of the federal list of textbooks changed. The Ministry of Education and Science has prepared new requirements for textbooks used in schools. Thus, in addition to a hard copy of the textbook, from January 1, 2015, the publisher is obliged to provide its full electronic copy.

CNews knows that at the moment the Moscow authorities are implementing a pilot project for the introduction of electronic textbooks in Moscow schools.

Comment on new developments in Yota Devices refused, reports CNews.

Rostec owns 25.1% of Yota Devices. The main owners of the company are the co-owners of the Telconet Capital investment fund Sergey Adonev and Albert Avdolyan .

On May 19, Megamind wrote that Yota Devices plans to enter the American market with Yotaphone 2. For this, a crowdfunding campaign was launched on Indiegogo.

In addition, YotaPhone Devices and Chinese retailers entered into a new agreement on the distribution of devices in China. Partners plan to create a joint venture. In this regard, the YotaPhone 2 model adapted for Chinese users will be released.

The first batch of YotaPhone 2 smartphones came out in December and was sold on the Russian and European markets. According to IDC, out of 150 thousand devices in Russia for half a year about 30 thousand were sold, and in Europe - about 110 thousand.

The company's general director Vladislav Martynov emphasizes that it does not make sense to compare these results with sales of popular world brands. YotaPhone and YotaPhone 2 have appeared recently, and the company itself is still in the startup stage.

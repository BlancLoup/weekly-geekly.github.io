WebMoney Root Certificate Expiration - What to do

This information applies to anyone who somehow needs access to secure WebMoney services pages, but who for some reason did not read the news on our website)

June 7 (that is, right today) expires the previous root certificate of WebMoney Transfer. We strongly recommend that you download the new certificate and install it on servers using XML interfaces.
We remind you that the WebMoney root certificate is needed to authenticate sites when establishing a secure connection using the HTTPS protocol.
The last Chinese ... that is, we remind again and again to Keeper Light users that their current personal certificates are also valid until 07/06/2010 - because they were issued on behalf of the root certificate, which has expired.


The topic is disclosed in detail in this article .
During the installation of WM Keeper Classic, the root certificate is installed automatically. In this case, it is placed in the certificate store of Internet Explorer. Instructions for installing a certificate for different browsers can be found here .
And - yes, if you wisely installed the latest version of Keeper Classic , then you do not need to renew the certificate - in version 3.9.2.0 a new certificate has already been added.

PS Another with regards to the new certificate for light - it will be valid for 2 years, and not for a year, as before. Hopefully this will make life easier for Light users.

Habra Kadabra - literacy support for authors of articles on Habrahabr

Hello! Today I want to introduce you to my new project, which will once again help (I really hope!) To make Habrahabr a little bit better.

Habra Kadabra is an extension for the browser (a version for Chrome is currently available, Firefox is in the queue), which allows you to maintain a separate stream of comments for articles.

Habra Kadabra was created for grammar-Nazi connoisseurs of the Russian language, for authors of articles, as well as for all users of Habrahabr who are tired of unnecessary comments about errors in articles. Remember Orphus and Ctrl + Enter? Same idea.

Prerequisites




- The authors do not read their articles or do it with insufficient thoroughness;
- comments about errors in the text are traditionally taken coldly, the rating of the comment and the karma of its author usually merge;
- comments on Habrahabr cannot be deleted, you can only edit; the author once corrects the text of the article - the comment will hang forever, polluting the information space.

As you can see, completely one negative. But a holy place is never empty! Any kind of vacuum should be filled sooner or later.


Opportunities


First of all, to use the extension, you must be logged in to Habrahabr.

Habra Kadarba adds a flat list of edits to the end of the article, after the form of adding a comments. Edits can be seen by anyone with an extension; only their authors, as well as the author of the article, can delete them.

To add a new edit, select the text fragment (or the title, but one thing) of the article, press Ctrl + Enter - a shortcut to which I so often appeal in the blind hope of support from the next resource. That's how simple it is!

Habra Kadabra does not work with comments to articles. Yes, it just does not need, so everything is fine! :)


Conclusion


I hope, I guessed the wishes of habra users, and Habra Kadabra will do a good job. I understand that the expansion can not be found in popularity due to the fact that both the author and readers should be installed for work (and so far only one browser is supported), but I hope for your support. If you have ideas, comments, bug reports, hate rays , leave them in the comments, send me in a personal or via the feedback form . I would be very happy constructive dialogue.

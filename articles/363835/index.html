Sony PlayStation turns 20

Exactly 20 years, on December 3, 1994, sales of Sony's iconic gaming system started in Japan. The following year, the console appeared in the US and Europe. At that time, solutions from Nintendo and Sega were popular among consoles.

Ken Kutaragi is the father of the PlayStation brand, who proposed Sony to create his own gaming system, but some executives thought the development of this brand was risky, but Sony head Norio Og supported his idea. The original console of the series, the PlayStation, was the first ever game console, which sold 100 million copies.


Specifications


  • Central processor: MIPS R3000A-compliant (R3051) 32-bit RISC microprocessor operating at 33.8688 MHz. The microprocessor is manufactured by LSI Logic Corp. using technology licensed from SGI. The chip also includes the Geometry Transformation Engine and the Data Decompression Engine.
  • Performance - 30 MIPS.
  • The bus bandwidth is 132 MB / s.
  • Instructions cache - 4 KB.
  • Cache: 1k SRAM.
  • Geometry Transformation Engine - a controller for working with three-dimensional graphics, placed on a single chip with a central processor.
  • Performance - 66 MIPS.
  • Announced performance: 1.5 million polygons per second, 500,000 textured and lit polygons per second.
  • Actual performance: 360,000 polygons per second, 180,000 textured and lit polygons per second. This discrepancy is primarily due to the fact that the official characteristics were obtained in laboratory conditions that are close to ideal.
  • Memory:
  • Primary RAM: 2 MB. (16 megabits)
  • Video RAM: 1 MB. (8 megabits)
  • Sound RAM: 512 KB.
  • CD-ROM read buffer: 32 KB.
  • BIOS: 512KB.
  • Memory Cards: 128 KB on EEPROM.
  • CD-ROM: 2-speed, 300 KB / s, XA-compatible.

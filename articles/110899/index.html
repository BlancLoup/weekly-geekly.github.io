Upgrade "soapbox" to the microscope

In this topic I will talk about how I made an instrument for microphotographs from an old digicam and a lens from a CD-ROM.




So, it all started with the fact that in the process of implementing the ideas, various variations of the camera (Olympus C-350) with lenses from CD-ROM, DVD-ROM and a couple of others taken from different optics were tried. In terms of magnification and picture quality, CD and DVD-ROM lenses were close, but in the end a CD lens was chosen because of a more convenient design (it was easier to make a frame for it).

In the photo on the right, this lens (not to be confused with an ashtray), photographed through another lens that has a greater focal length, and therefore more suitable for macro photography.


To attach the lens to the camera lens, I made a frame out of foam plastic and painted it with a black marker, for greater opacity:



Already at the beginning of the experiments, it became clear that holding a digital camera, it would not be possible to make a high-quality picture due to strong vibration, therefore a tripod with hydraulic focusing was made from the materials at hand (a pancake sounds).



The tripod is assembled from a piece of plastic pipe as a frame, two syringes of different diameters, a syringe with a larger diameter is used to attach the stage to it, a syringe of smaller diameter to control the movement of the stage. The table itself is cut from a CD-case. To fasten the whole thing to the table, I found a mounting bracket.
In the plastic tube I made 2 holes, on top for the bolt, which the camera was screwed on (by the way, the usual furniture bolt, which I borrowed from the table for the sake of science) was perfect, from the bottom for the mounting bracket.
Syringes connected using a dropper, filling the whole thing with water. The big syringe was, without further ado, tied to the tube with scotch tape.

The result was such a tripod:



Now fasten the soap dish to the tripod and the device is ready for microphotographs!

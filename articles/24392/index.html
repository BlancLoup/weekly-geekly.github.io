MySpace provides karaoke service to users

The basis of the new service of the world's largest social network MySpace is the resource kSolo.com, which was acquired by News Corp. about two years ago. The symbiosis of the social network and karaoke resource will allow users not only to torment their household with singing, but also anyone who wishes to familiarize themselves with their work through the personal page of the "singer", where freshly executed masterpieces can be downloaded.

Moreover, the friends of this user can appreciate the singing, by issuing an assessment corresponding to the talent of the singer. Now the service contains from 2 to 3 thousand songs in the minus phonogram. All this musical material is licensed and provided by record companies. Due to licensing difficulties, according to President Tom Anderson (Tom Anderson), the launch of the project was postponed.

MySpase is a social network that is used by independent musicians to promote and spread their creativity. Tom Anderson believes that, within the framework of a music-oriented formation, the appearance of a karaoke service looks quite logical.

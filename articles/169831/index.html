President of the Russian Federation Vladimir Putin issued a decree on the need to create a state system to combat cyberterrorism. By his decision, he, in fact, officially announced the entry of Russia into the electronic era and the accession of technologically leading states to the club, which in recent years have been compelled to fight invisible and practically invulnerable enemies.

For cyber-terrorists there are no state borders, they easily penetrate any objects in any countries where there is a connection to the global information network Internet. The actions of hackers pose a threat not only to ordinary users, but also to the security of the most developed countries.

And Russia is no exception. In our country, hacker attacks have been repeatedly subjected to the sites of leading media, information systems of state organizations, politicians, merchants, and personal information of famous actors.


Not only the media and VIPs are helpless in front of virtual weapons, computer viruses, a wide variety of spyware, but also any arsenals of weapons: nuclear missiles, airplanes, artillery and tanks.

Moreover, the most up-to-date models of military equipment can be destroyed, for example, by false teams, introduced by hackers into electronic control panels, or into chips responsible for controlling a formidable weapon.
They are trying to find salvation from electronic threats in the USA, Israel, and Western Europe, where cyber threat prevention centers have already been established.

However, the war between states and hackers comes with varying success. They have not yet succeeded in creating an effective integrated electronic protection against cyber-terrorists. This fact is evidenced by regular reports in the media about the numerous hacks of computer systems of the largest Western TV channels, newspapers, information portals, financial institutions, military and defense departments.

In the light of these events, it may seem that the prospects of the Russian national center for electronic security do not look very convincing. After all, it is no secret to anyone that the Achilles heel of modern Russia is a lag in the sphere of high technologies and the production of electronic devices.

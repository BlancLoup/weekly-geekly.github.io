Part 1. Global Shutdown Operation

On the eve of April Fool's Day (April 1), when there seemed to be no signs of trouble, world news sounded the alarm of a global scale.

The hacktivists of the Anonymous movement within the Global Shutdown campaign promise on March 31 to launch a distributed attack on 13 root DNS servers, which will lead to the fact that the ability to search for domain names on the Internet throughout the world will be lost.




" In protest against SOPA, Wall Street, our irresponsible leaders and favorite bankers who make the world go hungry because of their selfish needs, and for the sake of simple sadistic fun, Anonymous will turn off the Internet on March 31, " the statement said. .

Leading security specialist for World of Communications , Yevgeny Gordeyev, commented on the news:

Now over these 13 names are more than 200 servers. Servers are physically distributed at different points, and in principle they may not have a center. It is unlikely to push several systems at the same time to reach the root server.

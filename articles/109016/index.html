eGov 2.0: Best e-government practices

Somehow, completely unnoticed (judging by the search results), this event was held for the beloved Habr, and, meanwhile, on November 26 (last Friday) the eGov2.0 conference took place in Moscow : Best e-government practices . The ideologist and the main organizer of the conference is the well-known activist of this topic - Alyona Popova .

Probably the most famous participant in this conference is State Duma Deputy Ilya Ponomarev, who popularizes the topic of developing systems of interaction between citizens and the state. His presence and active participation show the level of the event and demonstrate that there is some obvious activity in this area. The essence of which I will try to convey to you in this article.

So, the conference is dedicated to practitioners. Those. The projects of the conference participants were of little interest. More talked about what has been done. What turned out to be an unsuccessful attempt, and what and how can be developed and replicated.

In the opening section of the conference, as always, a lot of common words were said, but one report deserves attention. Sergey Yurkevich, executive secretary of the Commission for the Development of the Information Society with the plenipotentiary representative of the President of the Russian Federation in the Central Federal District, did it. He talked about how work is going on the organization of interdepartmental document circulation and providing citizens and organizations access to government services via the Internet.

Regarding the provision of public services in electronic form, he said that there is a formal transition plan (which was approved by government decree No. 1555), according to which ‚Äúby 1 December 2015, such services as:

In addition, Yurkevich often used the term of providing the service "in a transactional form", explaining this as the possibility of not only submitting an application, but also tracking its processing and getting the result.

Then Yurkevich spoke about the need to organize interdepartmental document circulation for effective collaboration. In order not to send data "through the top", and work directly. He noted that today information systems of various departments are incompatible and work is underway to place them ‚Äúin a single project field‚Äù. At present, there are already 15 protected channels that provide work for 28 state. services in electronic form. The remaining services are provided by the state portal.

The transfer of services is organized at 3 levels: federal, regional and budget, and in 5 stages:
  • Stage 1: information on the website of public services;
  • Stage 2: the ability to download the form;
  • Stage 3: the ability to apply online;
  • Stage 4: the portal allows you to track the execution of the application;
  • Stage 5: services are fully electronic.

In total, 74 state services from 20 priority categories should be provided.

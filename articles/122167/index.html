Well, where are these tables already?

Equality


Basically, all modems have the following features, but in extreme cases they are paid extra, for example, a PLC from the Livebox (Orange) and Bbox (Bouygues).



As expected from providers, they all support ADSL2 + (up to 24 Mbps), all offer VoIP telephony, including calls to mobile phones.

The connection between the modem and the video decoder is carried out either via ethernet or via PLC.

They all do well with FTP, while for some it is on the decoder part, and in some with the modem. It should be noted that there are no similar sides with the modem part.

As for decoders, everyone is rich in HDMI, SPDIF, video recording, connecting external media and reading audio or video files from it.

Separately, I want to say about Scart, which is very common in France. The main reason for this is the French definition of the standard, and since 1980 a mandatory connector on all color televisions. Very uncomfortable, cumbersome compared to RCA, and collects a lot of noise.

And what about the superiority?

Absolute superiority


Of course, the comparison is not quite true, considering that FreeBox Revolution was released last December, NeufBox Evolution was November, LiveBox was in October 2009, and the Bbox was in October 2008. It is noteworthy that updates occur every 2-3 years (except for the last Freebox - 4 years).
With all this, only Free makes its modems itself, while the rest use contractors: Bbox - Technicolor / Sagemcom, SFR - Foxconn / Sercomm, Livebox - Sagem. As far as I know, Linux is spinning on Freebox and on Neufbox.



Undoubtedly, Freebox is the leader, having the most modern modem, but Neufbox steals 3 features. Not unimportant is the ability to view recordings on the decoder, just like a regular network folder. This is implemented using Samba, which is technically under the Freebox, but not implemented. There is a memory card reader, and it is very convenient to view fresh photos. And finally - Broadcast Control, namely control over the video. After pausing the ongoing program, you can resume it and rewind the advertisement forward or backward. Freebox can pause, but cannot rewind.

Livebox and Bbox are undoubted outsiders and offer just a little: a decoder control panel and Wifi b / g / n, like everyone else.

I will not completely retell the table, I will only note interesting features in the Freebox:
  • Multichannel Wifi
  • Gyroscopic control panel, like on Nintendo WII
  • Download torrents through a client installed on the Freebox server
  • Blue ray
  • Opengl 2.0
  • DECT to register multiple devices

Google+ now supports real-time search and hashtags



Google Inc. often updates its various products. Perhaps, updates for their services and software "Corporation of Good" releases more often than any other company. Google+ is no exception. Project developers have added support for hashtags and real-time search.

Now, if you are looking for something on Google+, you can see both old messages that are relevant to the search query and new ones that will appear on the results page as they are written by users. Something similar has long been introduced to Twitter, and derivatives of this tool are actively used by webmasters, posting a widget in the sidebar, where messages from users of the microblogging service mentioning a specific site or topic are displayed.

Perhaps soon the same widgets will appear for Google+. You can also add hash tags to your own Google+ messages, which will automatically be linked to the corresponding request.

Updating the social network has already been approved by many Google+ users who work with this social network immediately after its appearance. Two new tools make working with the social network from Google more interesting, allowing you to find the necessary information provided by other users. It may well be that real-time search on Google+ is just a training session before launching a similar tool for Google search service.

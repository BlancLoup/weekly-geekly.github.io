Fatal Tesla accident: who is to blame and what to do next?



On July 1, the news was published on Geektimes that the Tesla Model S car with the autopilot system turned on was fatal. So far this is the first and only case of the death of the driver of a car that is under the control of a computer system.

Formally, the manufacturer of the electric vehicle is not to blame. The autopilot feature is disabled in Tesla electric vehicles by default. The company previously stated the following: ‚ÄúSafety is a top priority for Tesla, we design and build cars primarily based on this. We also ask our customers to train safely while using our cars ... The accident happened on May 7, and the US National Highway Traffic Safety Administration learned of this on May 16, after 9 days. The public was told about the car accident and that later - on June 30, almost 2 months after the incident itself. Why regulators or the company itself did not report this before?

Sale of Tesla Motors shares


On May 18, another interesting event occurred: Tesla and Ilon Musk sold the company 's shares for $ 2 billion, at $ 215 per share. At this time, the management of the company already knew about the catastrophe, a corresponding investigation was carried out. If it had become known before the sale of the shares, the proceeds would be less. The question arises - whether the sale of shares and the delay with the message about the disaster is fatal?

Immediately after it became aware of what had happened, the company's stock price fell from $ 212 to $ 206. True, the losses were quickly won back by the market: in the evening the value of the shares increased to $ 216. Ilon Musk, head of Tesla Motors, responded to allegations of delaying informing regulators and the public about the accident on his Twitter account. He said that all this is "not essential" for the company's investors.

What does Musk think?


Earlier, the head of Tesla Motors wrote to a Fortune journalist : "... around the world, about a million people die in car accidents every year. UPD. Today it became known about another accident electric Tesla. This time the trouble happened with the owner of the Tesla Model X, who activated the autopilot mode before the accident. Fortunately, no one died, there are no serious injuries either.

The accident happened on July 1 in Pennsylvania, USA. Model X with the included autopilot entered the safety fence, crossed several lanes of the road and crashed into a concrete divider.

The electric car turned over to the roof and stopped. Another car crashed in the accident, the 2013 Infiniti G37 crashing into the detached parts of the Model X. According to those familiar with the section of the road where the accident occurred, driving here is very simple. And the layout and all fences are installed where needed. The driver's error here is practically excluded. Why the Tesla autopilot lost its way is unclear; it remains to be studied.

Sales of goods and services through applications for Iphone

At the moment, applications in the Russian segment of the AppStore can be divided into 4 types by the method of monetization:

1. An application that is going to bring happiness to its developer by selling itself
2. The application, as a new communication channel of the Internet sites - money from advertising or other models of monetization of these sites.
3. The application that advertises goods or services - there is no task to monetize,
4. Applications that represent a new sales channel for a product or service.

I want to talk about the latter, touching on the experience of Western companies. I will also give a practical example from a project that I am currently working on.

To begin with, I decided to write about it and to whom it may be useful.

At the moment, I am engaged in the development of new media channels (mainly social media) for the Empire of Pizza company within the AdComm agency, and as part of this activity, an application for Iphone was made not so long ago. Accordingly, there is information, and there is a small but already operational experience of the application that you want to share.

To whom this may be useful:
Owners and top managers of companies selling goods and / or providing offline services, who are thinking about launching new sales channels.

Western experience



Major Western companies have long been in mobile devices and have already reported on their success:

  • Amazon - sold more than 1 billion in a year through mobile devices (including the Kindle)
  • A little not a retailer, but still - eBay suggests sales of $ 1.5 billion in 2010 through mobile devices.
  • From the PizzaHut application, orders were made for $ 1 million in 3 months from the start of the application .


In general, in America it is a big trend. Food delivery aggregators , local restaurants such as Blue Ribbon BBQ or Boston Kebab House and airport transfer organizers are also released in the AppStore. The list can be replenished long and hard.

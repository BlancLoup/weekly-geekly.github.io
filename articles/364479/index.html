Analyzing driving and car condition with eConomy

An eConomy is a bundle from a car gadget, an application for smartphones and a personal web page for each car, which, like a medical card, keeps a complete history of the car, starting from the first day.

The joint product of the team from St. Petersburg and Western automotive electronics manufacturer OBD Solutions encourages us to drive safely and economically. The system tells the car owner how good he is at the wheel and at what points it is worth working on the usual driving style.



It helps to save on gasoline and nerves. In addition to driving, the application monitors the condition of the car. Thus, the express diagnostics function determines the nature of the current motor malfunctions, and monitoring monitors temperatures and ‚Äúelectrics‚Äù while driving.

How it works




The heart of eConomy is a matchbox-sized gadget. Connecting the device takes only a minute: you need to find the diagnostic connector that is used to diagnose the car in the service station, connect the adapter to it, install the application on the smartphone. The application works on modern Android devices, and the gadget is compatible with any machines released in the USA after 1996, Europe 2001, Russia and Asia after 2005.

Using the device, the mobile application is connected to the on-board computer of the car every time the driver is driving. So according to Bluetooth technical information: speed, engine speed, temperature, pressure, etc. enters the smartphone.

15 thousand savings


What can the application and web services? Let's start with the economy. We took the Hyundai Solaris and made two identical trips along the same route for about 50 kilometers. In one case, the driver was driving aggressively, in the second - slowly in the right lane. The screenshots show that the route is the same, but in one case the consumption of gasoline was 3.9 liters, in the second - 2.8 liters.





If you count on the usual fuel consumption per 100 kilometers, then it is 6 l / 100 and 8 l / 100. It would seem that only 2 liters or about 70 rubles per 100 km. But on average, motorists travel 20.000-25.000 kilometers per year. Thus, without straining, you can burn 400 to 500 liters less for a year, that is, to keep 14-17 thousand rubles in your pocket. Obviously, this is not the biggest money, but the plus is that economical driving is also safer for the driver and passengers.

Statistics


Now about the sad. More than a million people die on the roads every year, and no one wants to be in their place. When drivers are at the wheel, eConomy monitors the sharpness of starts, braking, average and maximum speeds and sets a traffic safety rating from 0 to 100. This gives everyone the opportunity to statistically assess their chances of getting into an accident. Of course, the creators of eConomy cannot promise that a drunk summer resident or a sleepy student will not drive into a person who has reached one hundred points and has become an ideal driver, but you can‚Äôt argue with the statistics - the likelihood of an accident during such driving decreases several times.

It is clear that one can be skeptical of those who promise security on the basis of a strange points system. However, the world practice of insurance companies, which have a vital interest in ensuring that their clients get into accidents as rarely as possible, suggests otherwise. Such companies use similar driving safety rating systems as a basis and calculate the cost of insurance based on its data. Unfortunately, local mistrust between insurance companies and drivers hinders the mass character of such projects in Russia, which is why eConomy passes the concern for its safety into the hands of the drivers themselves.

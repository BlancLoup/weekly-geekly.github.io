Business simulators are a rapidly developing area in the business education of Russia.

Recently, startups and ideas for business in Russia have been very vigorously discussed here.
Many people write about self-motivation and ways to achieve financial independence.

In Russia, the need to eliminate the financial illiteracy of the population has long been overdue. The level of financial literacy of Russians does not match the realities and requirements of a modern market economy. In developed countries, financial information surrounds a person in kindergarten, at school, in high school, at home.

The international experience of economically developed countries has proved that financial education of the population, being one of the key activities of the state, significantly increases the competitiveness of the country, makes it more attractive to investors, and contributes to improving the welfare of the population.

Economically developed countries annually spend millions of dollars on financial education of the population. First of all, used by the media. Such measures effectively contribute to improving the financial literacy of citizens of these countries and their financial decisions.

More and more leading MBA schools and universities use software products that simulate real business processes to improve learning.

In Russia, as an alternative to financial education, the cashflow board game Cash Flow appeared (this format was invented by American businessman Robert Kiyosaki). The game is interesting because without risk for your own money you can try different models of financial behavior and choose the most optimal one for yourself. That is, it gives the skills of the right attitude to money, teaches personal finance management and investing. Therefore, even commercial organizations that conduct such games have appeared in Russia.

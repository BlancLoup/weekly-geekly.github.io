ESSENCE OF THE DEMOGRAPHIC PROBLEM IN PART OF THE GROWTH OF GROWTH
It is completely obvious to us that financially having children is not profitable. And no state subsidies and promises can change this. Not a single respectable and self-respecting person will start a child just because he receives financial support from the state.
Moreover, the Russian state must also manage to get any kind of real financial assistance (not illusory and not miserable, but real and concrete, in the form, for example, of banknotes).
At the same time, women decide to have an abortion or do not decide to get pregnant at all, based very often on financial considerations.
Consequently, there is a simple, albeit paradoxical, logical construction:
FINANCIAL BIRTH OF CHILDREN - ALWAYS LOSS.
IF CHILDREN ARE BORN, THEN IN A SUPPORTING MOST OF CASES, THIS IS HAPPENING NOT FOR FINANCIAL REASONS.
At the same time, the rejection of the birth of children is very frequent for financial reasons.
The question that we have to answer to solve the demographic problem in terms of the growth of the birth rate, therefore, does not lie at all in the plane of financial considerations or reasons. On the contrary, it lies in the plane of considerations of fundamentally non-financial.
In the area of ‚Äã‚Äãfinancial considerations lies the question of rejecting children.
Thus, by developing a solution to the demographic problem, based solely on the promise of material benefits and financial bonuses to young families and women in labor, the state does not solve the problem of BIRTHING children, but only partially alleviates the financial problems that prevent children from being born.
Strictly speaking, reducing the influence of the reasons for which they refuse to give birth to children only reduces the number of birth failures among couples who already want to have children. However, these measures do not increase the number of couples who want to have children.
From our point of view, it is necessary to fully increase the influence of precisely those factors that contribute to the emergence of the desire to have children from as many loving couples as possible.
The question is, under what conditions will young families start having children, forgetting about the inevitable financial difficulties? What circumstances can push the financial reasons for not giving birth to children to the second plan and to the forefront to put forward non-financial reasons for the birth of children?
This is the formulation of the problem, having solved which, we solve the demographic problem of Russia.
CONCEPT OF SOCIAL MOTIVATIONAL FIELD
Imagine the consciousness of the participants in the process of childbirth (men and women who love each other and want to start a family and have children) as consisting of four subsystems.
1. Logical arguments (including financial calculations).
2. Emotional states, feelings and affections.
3. Social patterns of behavior enshrined in upbringing and culture, and endorsed by the social environment.
4. Biological and biosocial programs that determine the motives for the actions and decisions of men and women.
At the level of logic, the birth of children looks like a troublesome and expensive matter, like the emergence of responsibility for the health, well-being, material support, upbringing and education of children. All this at a time when you can enjoy life, while you are young and full of energy and spend the money you earned only for your own pleasure.
Of course, it is possible to soften these arguments by the size of state social payments, however, as we see, logical arguments concern not only and not so much the financial side of the issue. Logically, children are not needed by young people.
What can convince them to act contrary to the logic just described? From our point of view, three other subsystems can work on this in the model of consciousness of men and women: emotions and feelings, social patterns of behavior, biological and biosocial programs.
People make serious life decisions based on the motives that exist in their consciousness or in the unconscious. If a person has a motive, he builds the appropriate logic, experiences the corresponding emotions, finds social patterns of behavior confirming the motivation of his motivation. Motives are the driving force through which a person begins to make decisions and act. In turn, motives do not arise without a reason. There are conditions and incentives that contribute to the emergence of motives. To the internal conditions, of course, include the biological and social programs of man. External conditions will be considered as a holistic impact on the human personality. We will call this complex of influences a social motivational field.

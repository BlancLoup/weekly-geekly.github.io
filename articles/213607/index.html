The digest of interesting news and materials from the world of PHP No. 36 (February 9 - 23, 2014)



We bring to your attention another selection of current news, interesting materials and useful tools.

Enjoy reading!


News and Releases


  • PHP 5.6.0alpha2 - Second planned alpha release with exponentiation operator ** and other changes on board.
  • Laravel Envoy - The SSH task management tool for PHP has been released. Analogue Fabric from the Python world. See Envoy screencast for details on usage. .
  • Pimple 2.0 - The second version of the DI-container from the creator of symfony was released.
  • Beginning of the End PEAR - Symfony Creator Fabien has announced that Pirum is no longer supporting . Also from now on, the PEAR version of Twig will not be updated .
  • A security issue was discovered in Composer - Thanks to the replace directive, an attacker can execute code on your machine. On the topic spoke one of the authors Composer, as well as a more detailed explanation can be found here . Fortunately, the problem has already been solved .
  • Codeception 2.0 alpha - Overview of the new features of the second version of the wonderful framework for testing.


Php


  • RFC: Combined Comparison Operator - It is proposed to add a new operator (expr) <=> (expr) , which returns 0 if the operands are equal, 1 if the left operand is greater, and -1 if the right operand is greater.
  • RFC: No php tags - Offer to make the opening <?php tag optional. The proposal itself is almost two years old, but perhaps now it will be implemented.
  • Ideas for PHP 6 - Meanwhile, PHP5 ++ is being actively discussed in php.internals. The general vision is designed as a separate page on the official website. Also discusses options for implementing support for unicode .

Study materials


  • Fast routing of queries using regular expressions - Nikita Popov conducted a very interesting study of existing routing implementations, in particular, the Pux library, which should be super-productive, since it is implemented, also in the form of an extension to C. However performance can be achieved using PHP. The result is in the form of the FastRoute library, whose performance is higher than the Pux C-extension.
  • A look at HACK - a replacement for PHP in HHVM , 2 - In essence, HACK is PHP6 being developed in the depths of Facebook, that is, an attempt to solve existing problems in PHP and add new features.

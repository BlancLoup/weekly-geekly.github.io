Previously unknown whale song components found.



Scientists have long known that whales, sperm whales and dolphins communicate with each other using sounds. At the same time, whale communication resembles singing, which can be heard underwater for many kilometers. A new study from Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution (WHOI) showed that some elements of whale songs went unnoticed in the course of previous work on the analysis of the scale. And missed were important for communication elements of marine mammals.

The results of their work, scientists have published in the online edition of Biology Letters (November issue). The main object of the study, according to research leader Aran Muni, was humpback whales. From a small boat, he and his colleagues measured the two components of whale songs: ordinary acoustic pressure waves (vibration of a substance through which sound passes) and particle movement (physical vibration of a substance as sound moves through it). Scientists were surprised at how much new information has come from studying these two types of sound waves.

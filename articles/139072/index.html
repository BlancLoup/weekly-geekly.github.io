Hacktivists with Anonymous were able to disable the site interpol

In response to recent arrests , Anonymous disabled the interpol site . For this, 2 hacking attempts were made, after the first the site was restored within a couple of minutes, but the latter pulled it out of order and is still in such a state.

Relevant posts were posted on Anonymous's popular @AnonOps tweeter .



At the moment (6:16 GMT) attempts are being made to make a similar operation with the Spanish police website policia.es .

From myself I want to note that such actions of arrests will not help in the fight against hacktivists. They will only provoke even more aggressive actions. And it is unlikely that the police managed to detain people who were really involved in the hacks, usually only the illiterate computer security protesters who ran the automatic testing tools (Acunetix scanner, etc.) or DDoS attacks (LOIC, JS LOIC) were found.

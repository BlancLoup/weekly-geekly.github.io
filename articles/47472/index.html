iPhone - subjective top 15 free programs from the AppStore

For two and a half months I have got my favorites in the AppStore. After analyzing the contents of the iPhone, I came to the conclusion that 80% of the time I use 20% of the applications that are subjectively suitable to me the most. Each of the programs listed below has excellent (and even free) counterparts, but it was they who stayed on my iPhone longer than the others. Applications go in a chaotic arrangement on the screens.

Facebook
Client to access the social network of the same name. Greatly thought-out application, fully compatible with the functions of the web-version. When viewing photos supported accelerometer.

Wikipanion
Client for Wikipedia.

Voicenotes
Digital voice recorder.

Google mobile app
Application to access popular Google services. It has additional iphone chips, such as voice search, advanced search results, etc.

Fring
SIP, ICQ, Google Talk, Skype, MSN and a few more protocols. Support voice communication. A convenient way to save on Wi-Fi coverage.

Last.FM
Internet radio client Last.FM.

Shazam
Search for the name of the composition of the fragment. Works great for English performers (tested on television advertising), much worse with Russian speakers. Immediately after recognition, clips on YouTube and links to stores.

Discover
Wi-Fi flash drive from iPhone. All internal memory is available for recording.

Remote
Full remote control of Apple TV via Wi-Fi. Brilliant program.

Joost
New Series Robot Chicken, Adult Swim, Bikini Parties on the iPhone.

EveryTrail
GPS datalogger. Records GPS data of the receiver at intervals (set by the user) of time and sends it to the server, where the data is visualized and / or exported to a KML file. While recording to avoid being bored, the distance, current and average speeds are calculated.

Kyte Producer
Client to event-oriented media storage service.

TinEye Music
Search album by cover. Works on the well-known TinEye technology.

Mocha VNC Lite
VNC client. Nothing extra.

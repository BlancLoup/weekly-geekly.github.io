New WiFi equipment from Ruckus Wireless. New: access points, controllers and functionality

Today we want to talk about new products from the manufacturer of WiFi equipment Ruckus Wireless , and there are a lot of them in several quarters: 7 access points of the standard 802.11ac, including Wave 2, a new controller and a new functionality.


There are a large number of wireless WiFi systems from various manufacturers on the market, representing both minimalistic solutions based on autonomous access points, as well as centralized solutions with a network controller or cloud solutions with a cloud controller.

In our opinion, Ruckus equipment stands out from the competition with its capabilities, performance and reasonable price. In this article we will give an overview of the model range and architecture of networks presented by the manufacturer at the end of the past and the beginning of this year.

The Ruckus Wireless system is a hybrid of autonomous and centralized networks of the American manufacturer. As in centralized networks, Ruckus supports seamless roaming of subscribers, optimization of radio coverage (selection of optimal radio channels and capacities), load balancing and centralized management. Moreover, the ZoneDirector controller is able to authorize users via the built-in user base and the external RADIUS server and perform web authorization.

From autonomous access points, Ruckus uses the ability to locate access points behind a routed network and Internet channels, as well as the ability to switch users' traffic locally, without sending it through the controller, which allows the controller to be installed remotely and control currents through a small capacity channel. Each point can be configured as a router, raise the L2TP connection and implement NAT. Thus, it becomes possible to locate access points remotely and connect them to various Internet channels.

We give a description of the updated range of Ruckus Wireless. It includes access points and street and internal performance of the product line ZoneFlex

Internal access points

The new generation of internal ZoneFlex R-series access points - models R300, R 500, R600, R700 and R710.


Consider the most interesting features of new devices in more detail.

One of the important functions that should be mentioned, given the state of the ether - is a spectrum analyzer. All new points, except for the R300, are equipped with an ether analyzer and use it when choosing a frequency rating. It is also possible to connect to a remote access point and transfer it to analyzer mode to study the current situation on the air.

Specifications points in more detail.

R300

The ZoneFlex R300 is the youngest model of the new generation, replacing the ZoneFlex 7363.

Appearance ZoneFlex R300. The point is very compact - dimensions 13 x 13 x 2.8 cm


Antenna System Access Point ZoneFlex R300

Two 802.11n radio modules, an adaptive antenna and small dimensions make the R300 a convenient solution for networks with low subscriber density (cafes, hotels). However, we, of course, are more interested in new access points with 802.11ac support, and there are quite a few of them.

R500 and R600

ZoneFlex R500 and R600 - use boards and radio modules, similar to the model R700, but have a less developed antenna system. Actually, the R600 model differs from the R500 with a more complex antenna system and the number of MIMO streams. These points are positioned as mid-level solutions.

On the construction of the point R500 / 600 remind ZoneFlex 7372, but uses more modern components

R700

ZoneFlex R700 is a high-performance access point with support for 3 channels of MIMO 802.11ac. Point offers support for new technologies, such as spectral analysis of the air and optimizing the maintenance of smartphones and tablets using a combination of signals of different polarization antennas.

The R700 allows you to get a useful performance of more than 650 Mbps in the 5 GHz band when powered from a standard 802.3af source.

3-channel antenna system ZoneFlex R700


View from the connectors


R710

The ZoneFlex R710 is the latest development of Ruckus, the first access point with support for Wave 2 of the 802.11ac standard with a bandwidth of 800 Mbps in the 2.4 GHz frequency band plus 1733 Mbps in the 5 GHz band, for a total of 2.5 for the access point Gbit / s In addition to speed, Multi-User MIMO technology (abbreviated as MU-MIMO) is supported, that is, the R710 can serve several subscribers simultaneously. Namely, the device provides a dedicated channel with full bandwidth to three clients at once. The effect of spatial separation of the signal and reuse of radio frequency resources is used. As a result, the efficiency of the use of the ether, the performance and the availability of the medium are increased. As you can see, even the form factor of the equipment has changed.

Street access points

There are three new access points in all-weather performance of the standard 802.11ac - ZoneFlex T300, T301s and T301n


Earlier, when building networks outside the premises, we used the ZoneFlex 7762 access point, an all-weather solution that has proven itself in many installations.

The appearance of the access point ZoneFlex 7762 and the antenna system, which conquered the market in its time

But the point is possible for the present times is quite large and does not have the support of 802.11ac. Therefore, the new T300 series is a logical continuation of the 7762 now with support for the AC standard.

Unlike the 7762S, all T300 / 301 models have built-in adaptive antennas of both frequency ranges, while to use 5 GHz in the 7762S you had to use an additional antenna.

It must be remembered that all the benefits of 802.11ac technology are realized only in the 5 GHz band, which is subject to licensing. However, a smaller footprint than the 7762 makes the T300 / 301 series very interesting when introducing 2.4 GHz networks. Note that all Ruckus access points can be used both in standalone mode, that is, by themselves, and in the version with a master controller.

SmartZone 100

SmartZone 100 allows you to administer WiFi networks of operators and enterprises. The device is capable of serving 1024 access points, 25,000 simultaneous sessions and 2048 VLANs. Moreover, it is possible to combine up to four devices in a cluster (3 + 1 for redundancy) and achieve network management of an impressive scale: 3 thousand access points, 60 thousand simultaneous sessions and 6 thousand VLANs.


New software



More recently, Ruckus controller software has been updated to version 9.9, and now we can talk about version 9.10. What new features does this software promise us?

First, it is the user's self-registration. If earlier during web authorization, it was necessary to give the client a password at the reception, or set up an SMS gateway when registering with a mobile phone number, then now we can use the link to the inviting person. When registering on the page, the user specifies his data, who invited him, and this information comes to the email of the inviting person who can confirm access to the guest.

The scheme itself registration using the inviting person

About the right approach to choosing an engine for an online store


About the right approach to the choice of engine for the online store.

For many years, programmers have been offering e-merchants their solutions for creating online stores. In this case, usually people who choose their own engine for an online store are beginners. They did not trade and they still do not know what they need. And programmers look at them as a source of money and consider software for an online store solely in terms of ease of implementation, and not in terms of ease of use. This situation leads to the fact that most stores are abandoned by owners rather quickly. So what is the mistake of this approach? The mistake is to consider the store engine from the seller‚Äôs point of view. A novice trader can turn on fantasy and hard work and imagine that he is an experienced seller. Then he will choose the right engine. And to choose the wrong one means to get involved in cycling on a scooter. Loss of time, money and deep disappointment in online trading.

What should a novice do?

Find a niche to trade. Find out the specifics of the product and the audience. It is obvious. Examine the competition. If you are already at the stage of choosing software for a store, you have already done all this.
1. Look at all possible price lists and balances with which it will be necessary to work . Find potential suppliers. Take from them the price lists and look at them. It seems to be obvious, but in practice it makes a minority. The store should always be the current price list. So it needs to be updated. Practice shows that this is a big problem for many.
2. Fully several times scroll through the procedure for updating prices in practice . Take a test account or download a test version of the engine for the store. Try to update the price list.
In a normal system, one person copes with updating the price lists of dissimilar goods from forty suppliers coming daily. It has also been noted in practice that the format of the price list is changed by the supplier 1-2 times a year. The system must be reconfigured very quickly and for free. This is the moment that is missed when most store owners choose an engine. Of course, this paragraph does not apply to those who do not have suppliers and only their products.
3. Add 10 dissimilar items to the store. Import their features and photos. Note that you will need to add hundreds or thousands. Calculate the time and cost of such work. Until you experience these three points and do not calculate the labor costs, you cannot even write a business plan for the store. After all, it is not known how many man-hours and personnel will be needed for these items.

Returning to the choice of engine for the store. Unfortunately, people pay money for the program without understanding whether they are suitable for their business in principle. People do not test engines, do not try them. Then suffer all my life. The best engine may simply not suit you, and the worst - come up, because there are no good and bad ones - there are right tools for your business model and there are wrong ones.

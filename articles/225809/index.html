Marmalade becomes free!


This news should please many developers who are considering cross-platform strategies for the development of their projects! Marmalade's free license allows you to create applications and games for iOS, Android, Windows Phone, Windows Store, BlackBerry and Tizen using a single code base. And most importantly, according to the creators, the resultant produces good performance for all devices. Marmalade also boasts a wide range of additional features: advertising, social, services for working with graphics, analytical capabilities and functionality for working with databases.

In one of the latest Cross Platform Tool Benchmarking-2013 research, Research2Guidance called Marmalade the best in performance among all cross-platform solutions, indicating that 88% of Marmalade users rated the performance of applications created using the Marmalade SDK as equal to or better than their native design! This is a serious blow to such a competitive struggle with "you-know-how".


Apple is slowly losing ground in the Japanese tablet market


According to the MM Research Institute, Apple in 2013 weakened its position in the Japanese tablet market. The total number of tablets sold in Japan in 2013 was 7.48 million units, while Apple accounted for 43.8%. For comparison, in 2012 this indicator was equal to 52.5%.

Obviously, Apple is under pressure from Android tablets, which for the first time in 2013 surpassed iOS tablets for sales, the total share of Android tablets was 45.7%.



The trend persists. In the first quarter of 2014, shipments of iOS-based tablets have decreased by 16% compared with the previous year. According to rumors, in 2014, the expected appearance of new tablet models from Apple - iPad Pro oversized. Let's see if this helps Apple spur sales.

Dragon Quest VIII: access to mobile for the anniversary


The notorious Japanese company Square Enix decided to celebrate the tenth anniversary of the launch of Dragon Quest VIII in Japan by launching the iOS and Android ports of this console game. Yes, the control is adapted for touch control, yes, the combat system is adapted for mobile players, yes, it will cost some $ 19.99 to play nostalgia. Is it worth the game? .. Mm, you decide. Capcom transfers game from PSP to mobile


Following Square Enix, another company, also well known to many, Capcom decided to port its game from another (mobile!) PSP console. In the Japanese App Store, the port of the game , known in the West as Monster Hunter Freedom Unite (the Japanese name for iOS is MONSTER HUNTER PORTABLE 2nd G for iOS, is out, are we embarrassed about cool names?).

Improved graphics - done, sharpened under iOS management - also done ... Although it looks quite complicated. Nevertheless, on the PSP the game involved many control points, and transferring it so simply to fully touch control, without changing the game, is not the easiest task. Joint campaigns of monsters are available in groups of up to 4 people, but only through Wi-Fi, network interaction is not supported. On the release for players from the West so far nothing is known. Oh yeah, the price - the Japanese are not turning away from their particular way: 1600 yen (this is tier 14, $ 13.99 for the USA).

Overview lecture and the launch of the 2nd part of the course on the development of web services on Go


We have two good news.


At Coursera, we launched the 2nd part of the online course on the development of web services on Go.
And on March 2nd, in the office, we will conduct a review lecture in which we will explain why you should try Go.


Under the cut details.


The first part of the course ‚ÄúDeveloping Web Services at Golang‚Äù was about the basics of the language, but practical application issues were not really covered.


The second part is devoted just to the review of the most frequent tasks in the development of a web service:


  • middleware, routing, logging, validation of input parameters, service monitoring;
  • working with storage systems - MySQL (in the database / sql and GORM variants), Memcache, Redis and RabbitMQ;
  • Microservices - tools embedded in Go, gRPC and Swagger;
  • organization of service discovery and online configuration with Consul;
  • unsafe and integration with code on C via cgo.

Homework continues the theme of ‚Äútry everything in the language‚Äù - by completing them, you will already feel confident in solving most of the problems encountered.


We did not aim to teach in this course how to program correctly, or to make a universal encyclopedia on language. Rather, it is a review of everything that is, so that you can begin to navigate the ecosystem of the language and understand how this or that problem is being solved. In addition, as part of the support of the first conference on the development of Go in Russia, GopherCon Russia 2018, we will give a lecture: ‚ÄúWhy you should try Golang‚Äù.


On March 2, in the Moscow office of the Mail.Ru Group, Vasily Romanov, the author of the course and a member of the Mail.Ru Mail team, will tell about the main features of the language and its areas of application. The following topics will be covered:

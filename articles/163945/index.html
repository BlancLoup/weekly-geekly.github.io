In the footsteps of MSI Master Overclocking Arena 2012

Good afternoon friends.

It so happened that I won a trip to MSI Master Overclocking Arena 2012, at the same time I myself am interested in overclocking and am on the team of XtremeLabs.org. The Ukrainian team and two people from it went with me to a distant country of Taiwan, the city of Taipei. Let me bring to your attention the report on the trip to the MSI MOA 2012 final.




Overclocking has long been the tool that allows component manufacturers to significantly expand the model range, giving them the opportunity to create additional information events and declare themselves as champions. They benefit from overclocking and ordinary users who need to press one button on the motherboard to automatically overclock the processor or in a special program using the sliders to increase the operating frequency of the graphics adapter, which results in a decent performance increase in games and various applications. As for enthusiasts who are not afraid of liquid nitrogen, in addition to the pleasure of overclocking and getting record results, they have the opportunity to earn money on overclocking or get access to the latest hardware. In addition, many professional overclockers have decided for themselves also the issue of employment. Today we will talk about overclockers and the company that organized a real holiday for them.

One of the most famous overclocking tournaments called MSI Master Overclocking Arena (MOA) celebrated its fifth anniversary this year. A grand final tournament held in Taipei. Every year, as part of the MOA event, the strongest teams gather from almost every corner of the planet. This year, 15 teams from 14 countries fought for the first place. Your humble servant together with the Ukrainian team Xtremelabs.org as part of overclockers T0lsty and Cyclone recovered on a long journey in order to tell the reader in detail about the grand finals of the MSI Master Overclocking Arena 2012 tournament.

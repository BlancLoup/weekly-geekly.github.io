Anonymous claim that they hacked the servers of Ukrainian customs and posted 1 GB of documents



The hackers of the Anonymous group announced the hacking of one of the servers of the Ukrainian customs CUSTOMS.GOV.UA. We are talking about a server that is responsible for the Odessa customs and transportation of goods in the Danube and Black Sea region.

The report of the group states that among the materials there are those that indicate illegal operations with oil and gas, corruption schemes, bribes, etc.

To convince in the veracity of the information presented, hackers posted 64 screenshots [ 1..32 33..64 ].

Most of the information, as reported on the site, concerns the former head of the State Customs Service of Ukraine, Igor Kaletnik, who left this post after being elected a deputy in 2012 on the list of the Communist Party of Ukraine. Now Kaletnik is the first deputy chairman of the Verkhovna Rada.

Hackers laid out in free access a large number of various documents - about 1 GB. Links are posted on their website. But, be careful, when downloading the archive you may be offered to install plugins for your browser - and who knows if it is not viruses or software to spy on you.

Recall that in October, hackers from the Anonymous group had already laid out in open access the letters of the Ukrainian MRZ after the scrapping of their server.

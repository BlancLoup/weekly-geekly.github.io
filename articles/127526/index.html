Much of the above becomes possible today thanks to the development of Machine-to-Machine technology or, more simply, M2M.

The meaning of this technology is simple and derives from the name - it allows the machines to exchange information with each other, or transmit it unilaterally. For this purpose, wireless networks are used - the fastest data transmission channel for today.

Recently, the rapid development of M2M in the whole world has become possible due to the spread of mobile communication: more and more people are using smart phones with the ability to connect to the network via GSM and GPRS modules. The main advantages here are speed and low cost: one saving on cabling is worth something!

According to the research company McKinsey & Company, by the end of 2010, the total turnover of the M2M market in the USA, Japan and Western Europe was about 130 billion dollars. In Russia, this figure is much more modest, however, experts argue that the M2M market of the CIS countries is rapidly gaining momentum.

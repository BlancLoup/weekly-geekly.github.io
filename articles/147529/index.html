Valve announced the Steam Greenlight

Valve has made the announcement of the new platform - Steam Greenlight , which will allow players to choose the projects they would like to see on Steam counters by voting.



Greenlight will see the light of a different system of Valve - Steam Workshop , which provides players with the ability to download, search and evaluate modifications and additions to the games presented on Steam. But unlike Workshop, Greenlight is focused on indie game developers who will be able to publish concept documents, screenshots, videos, demo versions and everything that will help them to lighten up and attract the attention of players. In addition, developers can get invaluable feedback from gamers directly.

Unknown is the number of votes required for publication on Steam. However, according to Valve, they will pay attention to all popular projects, regardless of the number of votes or position in the rating.

"Green light" will be able to light up from the end of August for all developers who provide a description, minimum system requirements, a logo, 1 video and 4 screenshots of their game project.

Ps. Valve has never disappointed consumers (well, yes, except for Half-Life 3). Hopefully, the new platform will be successful and will give many indie developers a chance to declare themselves.

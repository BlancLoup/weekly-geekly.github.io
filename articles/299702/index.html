The demand for laboratory diagnostics is evidenced by the fact that in 2015 the Invitro network laboratory became the leader in the Russian market of medical services (revenue for 2015 is more than 11 billion rubles). It took more than twenty years and about 800 reception sites in Russia, Ukraine, Belarus and Kazakhstan to achieve such an outcome.

Obviously, with the expansion of the network of treatment rooms, the costs of its maintenance are growing. These are non-core activities for laboratories and, often, they offer their franchise, thereby shifting the operating costs of their partners and paying them up to 60% of the research cost. As a result, it is necessary to overestimate the cost of analysis.

If we add to the above the high cost of automated analyzers (tens and hundreds of thousands of dollars), it will become clear the scale of the investments necessary to launch and develop the laboratory business. At first glance, a startup has nothing to do here. Nevertheless, we managed to find our niche and grow. Based on our experience, we formulated several startup rules that significantly increase the chances of survival to become a business.



Rule number 1: get experience in the area in which you plan to open a startup.

Information that the costs of treatment rooms eat up the lion's share of the profit prompted us to look for a way to pay for each procedure for taking biomaterial, which would make it possible to drastically reduce the cost of services - and therefore, to offer patients analyzes on more favorable terms. This idea was the starting point in the creation of Lab4U, and it could only appear in the head of a person who had already worked in the laboratory business.

In fact, five years ago, we invented our own Uber, in which commercial medical centers play the role of a taxi - it was precisely them that was decided to attract patients. In the zero years, the number of such clinics in large cities has grown so much that they began to meet at every turn. There were no queues in them - loading was almost always lower than planned.

At first glance, such cooperation was unprofitable for them, since they had already offered to take tests at their prices, much higher than ours. But, for the recoupment and growth of their core business, they needed patients, the flow of which we could provide. As a result, this argument was decisive, and we found clinics that agreed to accept patients for a fee corresponding to the cost of the biomaterial procedure.

RULE number 2: use at the start of circulation solutions.

Now, having solved a key issue, there is an opportunity to formulate the essence of the intended service. This is the online medical analysis shop LAB4U , in which the user is invited to independently arrange and pay for the order with a substantial discount (up to 50% of the competitors' prices), and then come and take the tests at one of the partner medical centers. We organize the delivery of biomaterials in large laboratories and the transfer of results to users in electronic form or on paper form.

1C Bitrix was chosen as a platform. Now we can say that this decision was justified. Thanks to him, we managed to start the service in a short time and start taking orders - 1 year was left to develop the site. Improvements were necessary in order to take into account the specifics of laboratory services and our business model. For example, it is necessary to consider which biomaterial corresponds to each study and in which medical centers it can be taken on a certain day.

RULE number 4: look for partners and build mutually beneficial relationships with them.

In any online store, the basis is a range, in our case a list of suggested analyzes. Every day, our patients have questions that require timely solutions. By automating the work of the customer support service on the Bitrix platform, we promptly respond to all requests. After the client addresses by mail or through an input form, a task is formed that should be closed.

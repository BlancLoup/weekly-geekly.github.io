Choosing a budget UPS



Stable nutrition is a guarantee of good health. If we apply this rule to the work of electronics and equipment, then uninterruptible power supplies ( UPS ) are responsible for their "health". Unfortunately, the need for UPS is still not obvious to everyone. Here is one of the common myths: an ordinary surge protector will also protect against power surges. Perhaps, but then the parameters of such a jump should then be in a very narrow range. In life, this is far from the case, and it is not at all possible to control the range with a filter.

And if we are talking about power outages? In such cases, the filter is generally helpless. The UPS protects equipment from power surges and equipment failures caused by them, ensuring the reliability and stability of the electronics. And most importantly: it saves the pockets - the equipment that was released as a result of jumps in the electrical network, as a rule, loses the warranty. Therefore, a good uninterruptible power supply should provide a decent level of protection against surprises of the Russian electrical network. The importance of this is recognized by more and more private users and almost all the business. But the question is: which UPS to choose?

In this review, we will look at UPSs in the budget and medium price category for domestic power grids with a voltage of 220 Volts, 50 Hertz: APC Back-UPS CS 500VA, PowerCom Warrior WAR-500A, Tripp Lite PRO550X, Ippon Back Power Pro 500, APC Back-UPS ES 525VA Russia.

APC Back-UPS CS 500VA (BK500-RS)



UPS backup type APC Back-UPS CS 500VA (BK500-RS) has been produced for 10 years, and during this time managed to earn respect among knowledgeable people for their reliability and quality. First, the device has already passed a hardware revision, as a result of which a lot of improvements were made. The reliability checked by time - one of the most important characteristics of this model. Secondly, the quality of the components: hundreds of UPSs of this type, purchased five years ago, are still ‚Äúplowed‚Äù with standard batteries. For 5 years, it loses only 30% of the initial capacity.

A backup type UPS (they are also Off-line, Back UPS, Standby, Passive Standby) assumes the availability of a backup support circuit that can be switched by the automation in abnormal situations. In normal mode, power is supplied from an external electrical network directly with filtering of voltage surges and electromagnetic pickups by passive filters. If the power characteristics exceed the standard voltage values ‚Äã‚Äãor there is a power outage, the consumer equipment is reconnected by the UPS automatics (usually a relay) to the power supply circuit, which ensures the supply of electricity from the built-in batteries through the built-in inverter. When the voltage stabilizes to standard values, the automatic switches the power supply circuit back to power supply from the primary external power grid.



The advantages of APC Back-UPS CS 500VA include PC management using APC proprietary software, rather high resistance to electrical overload, protection of communication lines, and a large number of power outputs compared to competitors.
The drawbacks of the presented model is a decent reaction time (the switching time of the automation to battery power), which is explained by the fact that it relates to a UPS with a backup circuit.
On the other hand, modern PC power supplies tolerate the reaction time quite painlessly - the interruption in power supply can reach up to 100 ms.

UPS APC Back-UPS CS 500VA is a high-quality and reliable solution with a beautiful ergonomic design. Despite the fact that 7-8 years ago, these UPSs responded to APC and were subjected to harsh criticism, APC did not curtail production, but heeded the comments of users and conducted a number of revisions. The result is a product that has become a bestseller: 10 years of successful sales. I do not know of any other UPS, which would work without complaints from the "native" battery for 6.5 years, while providing 60-70% of the original capacity.

Powercom Warrior WAR-500A



The time-honored manufacturer of budget solutions PowerCOM, as usual, pleases with price. A representative of this manufacturer is a line-interactive UPS PowerCOM Warrior WAR-500A .

The principle of operation of line-interactive (line-interactive) UPS has a number of design differences from the device backup UPS. The topology described in the IEC 62040-3 standard implies a circuit in which the UPS inverter is connected in parallel to the electrical network and operates in two modes: it analyzes the quality of the mains power supply, regulates and stabilizes the output voltage of the UPS, while charging the batteries. In addition, manufacturers use hardware-based units in linear-interactive UPSs to expand the input voltage range - this is usually a stabilizer with a wide input voltage range (‚Äúbooster‚Äù or AVR) based on an autotransformer with switchable windings. Due to this, the voltage at the outputs is maintained at the proper level without switching the automation to battery power. Simply put, these UPSs react to changes in the input mains and adjust the output voltage accordingly.



Judging by the weight and the smallest power of the built-in battery, the manufacturer has saved on high-quality nodes, preferring pulsed power schemes to the detriment of the quality of filtering the incoming voltage. At the same time, the emphasis was placed on protection from large peak current pulses in the power grid. PowerCOM will definitely be resistant to faults in the power grid and ‚Äúpickup‚Äù; however, when a lightning strikes the nearest substation, it is unlikely to save the PC from stress ‚Äî the input voltage filtering range is too small. Conclusion: it is necessary to use this UPS in conjunction with a good surge protector to avoid arbitrary rebooting of the PC due to power surges and to protect the UPS from electronics failure. But this challenger is cheap and can provide up to 8 minutes of battery life. Upset also the lack of control with a PC.

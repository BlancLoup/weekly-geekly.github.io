Regular Umlauts

Umlaut or less often umlaut (German Umlaut) is a diacritical mark indicating a phonetic umlaut vowel sounds in German and some other languages. Usually depicted as two dots above the letter, in the Gothic typeface, traditionally (with the exception of the last decades) looked like a small superscript letter e. During the study of converting various characters, I found that with this script the characters of the standard set are converted into themselves ("a" -> "a", "z ->" z ","; "->"; ", etc. ), our ‚Äúspetsbukvy‚Äù in mnemonics, special characters of the Unicode table (all sorts of pictures, etc.) are in question marks.
Thus, the idea of ‚Äã‚Äãsuch a check arose:
  1. After converting the original phrase into mnemonics, there can be characters "&", ";" and "?" (if a strange unicode special character or something else non-convertible). Create a pattern to filter out these characters from the original phrase: / [; &?] + /
  2. If the phrase passed the test of paragraph 1 - we convert the entered word or phrase into mnemonics
  3. Create a word verification pattern with mnemonics: /^[A-Za-z\s\.-_RL +$/ plus "&", ";", we get /^[A-Za-z&;\s\.-[+ $ / i
  4. If the test in Section 3 is passed - everything is OK!

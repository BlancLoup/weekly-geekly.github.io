Introduction

Information storage is the most important component of information technology. Every moment, individuals and organizations create huge amounts of digital information that needs to be preserved, protected, optimized and managed in a classic, virtualized and rapidly evolving cloud environment.

Not so long ago, information storages were presented only in the form of drives on magnetic disks or tapes connected to a computer for the purpose of storing data. Even today, the most important role that storage technology plays in achieving high levels of availability, performance, integration and optimization of the entire IT infrastructure is understood only by experts in the field of data storage. Over the past decade, information storage has evolved into a highly sophisticated technology that provides a variety of digital information solutions related to storing, managing, connecting, protecting, securing, sharing and optimizing.

The widespread adoption of virtualization, the emergence of cloud computing, multiple annual growth in data volumes and the availability of diverse
Types and sources of data - all of these factors have made modern data storage technologies even more important and relevant for the success of both commercial and any other organizations. The most acutely compared with the previous times, the leaders of the IT industry faced the problem of hiring and training highly qualified technical specialists in the field of data storage, well-versed in the work of classical, virtualized and cloud environments.

Many leading universities and colleges now include courses on studying data storage technologies in their programs for studying ordinary computer or information technologies, since until now IT professionals, even those with many years of experience, have not received any significant formal training benefits. Therefore, many experienced professionals, including application, system, database and network administrators, do not have a common opinion about the impact of data storage technology on their business lines.

This book was conceived and designed to help professionals and students gain a deep understanding of all the components of data storage technology. Although the industrial design examples used in this book are related to EMC Corporation products, an understanding of technological concepts and principles will prepare you for easy mastery of technical products from various suppliers.

The book contains 15 chapters, summarized in five sections. Topics are set out by increasing the complexity of the material, all subsequent topics should be studied after mastering the previous ones. Section I introduces the concepts of virtualization and cloud infrastructure, which are then referred to throughout the book to ensure that storage technologies are considered in the context of a traditional or classic environment, a virtualized environment, and a rapidly evolving cloud environment.

Section I "Storage Systems" . The four chapters of this section deal with the problems of the information boom and changes in the nature of the stored information, provide definitions for the data storage system and data center environment, provide an overview of the development of data storage technology, and provide an introduction to intelligent data storage systems. In addition, this section introduces the concepts of virtualization and cloud computing.

Section II "Network Storage Technology" . The four chapters of this section deal with the storage network based on the use of fiber optic technology - Fiber Channel storage area network (FC-SAN), the storage network based on the use of the Internet Protocol - Internet Protocol SAN (IP SAN), network storage devices - Network -attached storage (NAS), object-based storage technology and unified data storage. In addition, the section discusses the concepts of storage associations and converged networks (FCoE).

Section III, ‚ÄúBackup, Archiving, and Replication . ‚Äù The four chapters of this section deal with business continuity, data backup and recovery, data deduplication, data archiving, local and remote data replication in both classical and virtualized environments.

Section IV "Cloud Computing" . The only chapter in this section discusses cloud computing, including its infrastructure, service models, deployment options, and the factors that determine how to move to cloud computing.

Section V, "Security and Storage Infrastructure Management" . Two chapters of this section deal with the security of the storage infrastructure, the monitoring of the infrastructure, and the management of this infrastructure, including security and management issues in virtualized and cloud environments.

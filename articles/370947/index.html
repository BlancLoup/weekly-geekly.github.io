The system includes:

  • one mid-bass dipole panel per channel (24 eight-inch speakers in each);
  • two tape mid-panels per channel;

The dream of a sophisticated futurist - Sennheiser HE1 Orpheus


Sennheiser HE1 Orpheus - is the concept of the main engineer of Sennheiser - Axel Grell (Axel Grell). A unique sample of electrostatic headphones with a tube amplifier has been brought to immaculate condition for over 20 years. The first conceptual prototype was shown to the descendants of the great Fritz Zenheiser in 2009. Small-scale market samples began to be released in 2012. The modest cost of the kit is $ 55,000. In this case, one can hardly say that the manufacturer was stingy with the materials or saved on something significant.


Alex Graell used the lamp circuitry for the amplifier as the most suitable for electrostatic emitters, in his opinion. The amplifier used 8 lamps SE 803 S, own production Sennheiser. The manufacturer writes that in order to eliminate even insignificant structural noises, they had to place lamps on a free suspension in a case made of Carrara marble.


Uncompromising fidelity of reproduction is achieved through the use of a 2.4 micron thick membrane. The use of an ultrathin membrane and a number of engineering solutions allowed us to achieve the lowest level of nonlinear distortion ever measured in sound reproducing equipment: 0.01% at 1 kHz at 100 dB SPL. In addition, the headphone design allows you to get an incredibly wide for electrostatic headphones frequency range from 8 Hz to more than 100 kHz (the range of frequencies heard by a person from 16 Hz to 21 kHz) with uneven frequency response of no more than + - 0.25 dB.

The device is difficult to blame for archaism and low functionality, which is often the case with high end equipment. In spite of all its ‚Äúwarm lampiness‚Äù, the amplifier ‚ÄúOrpheus‚Äù is capable of receiving digital signals through optical and coaxial SPDIF inputs, as well as via USB. In addition, the ability to transmit PCM and DSD. To convert data into an analog signal, 8 internal ESS SABER ES9018.x DACs are used.

‚ÄúOrpheus‚Äù is a true masterpiece of industrial functional design. Turning on the device is accompanied by a delightful, in my opinion, spectacle: buttons and knobs slide out, a panel with lit lamps rises, a case cover with headphones opens.



Simply put, this is a magic toy with unprecedented high sound quality, the epitome of Sennheiser‚Äôs idealized presentation of flawless headphones.

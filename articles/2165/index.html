Giants have bothered to search

Google , Yahoo! and Microsoft decided to unite in the struggle for a just cause, the role of which in this case is the search for sites on the Internet.

Trinity intends to adopt a single standard that provides resource indexing by search engines, as well as allowing to determine what type of data is placed on its pages. Instead of coming up with three different (and, of course, incompatible) options for entering the necessary information in the engine, the giants agreed on one - the Sitemaps 0.90 protocol, developed by Google and debuted in June 2005, reports AFP .

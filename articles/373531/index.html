About the benefits of grapes for fish, a substrate for thallium, four points in Tatarstan, a cheap hand and degree of anger of writers - this and much more in the May charts of the most media scientific developments of Russian universities TOP-SCIENCE from NITU "MISiS".



Hello! Your attention is invited to the May hit parade of the most media scientific developments of Russian universities. Let me remind you that this monthly project was started by us, on the one hand, in order to popularize Russian science, on the other hand, I really wanted to understand what scientific developments are getting good press and cause a resonance in the Russian media.

Recall the criteria that guide us in the preparation of the rating. Studies are ranked according to the simplest and verifiable principle - the number of reprints of the original message. The number of reprints is determined using the information-analytical system of monitoring and effective analysis of the Russian media "Medialogia".

Only Russian developments and only developments of researchers from higher educational institutions of the Russian Federation are included in the rating.

So, meet the most popular scientific developments in the media of the last month of spring:

10. "Sundress" as unloading



At the tenth position of the TOP-SCIENCE charts are scientists from ITMO University who decided to create a tool to help mobile operators to optimally distribute the load on the network during emergency situations and help plan the work of the towers during mass events. To do this, scientists have created a computer simulation, which works on the principle of "word of mouth" and illustrates the spread of news among subscribers of the mobile network. As the authors say, the information collected by them from open sources made it possible to create a basic model, but the amount of data is clearly insufficient for solving applied problems. Perhaps they will be helped by a distribution scheme for news about their work.

9. Not green, but useful.



Chemists of the Far Eastern Federal University, in collaboration with colleagues from the Far Eastern Branch of the Russian Academy of Sciences, have found a way to force the cells of Amur grapes to increase the production of resveratrol by a hundred times. This substance may have a positive effect on the life expectancy of some invertebrates and short-lived fish, as well as potentially having a whole series of useful properties - from cardioprotective to antitumor. However, the results of clinical studies in humans yet, and in experiments with animals, not everything is clear. 8. Quantum and two-dimensional



In eighth place - the synthesis of the first quantum two-dimensional metal, carried out by scientists from the Far Eastern Federal University, the Far Eastern Branch of the Russian Academy of Sciences and the University of Tokyo (Japan). The synthesized material is a double layer of thallium atoms on a monocrystalline silicon substrate. Two-dimensional thallium at temperatures below 272 ¬∞ C enters the superconducting state, when exposed to a strong magnetic field, becomes an insulator, and in an intermediate-value magnetic field, it is a quantum metal. As one researcher, RAS Corresponding Member Alexander Saranin, explained: ‚ÄúExperiments have shown that in addition to the transition to an insulating or superconducting state, a two-dimensional system can remain a normal metal. This unusual condition has been called a quantum metal or Bose metal. It was this material that we were able to synthesize and study its electronic properties. ‚Äù

7. Death to Quantum Hackers



The seventh position continues the TOP-SCIENCE quantum theme of the hit parade. A group of scientists from the Kazan Quantum Center KNRTU-KAI and ITMO University successfully launched the first in Russia multi-node quantum network in full mode. For the first time in the domestic practice, the quantum channels on the basis of the existing urban telecommunications infrastructure are combined four points located in different parts of the capital of Tatarstan. Quantum communications, as is known, guarantee the absolute invulnerability of communication lines for hacker attacks, which is why scientists from leading countries of the world are working on the development of these technologies. Earlier in Russia, in the urban conditions, the lines of quantum communications were launched, connecting only two points.
7th place, 116 publications

6. "Writer, are you angry ..."



The linguists of the Institute of Philology and Language Communication of the Siberian Federal University, together with scientists from the Institute of Space and Information Technologies of the same SFU, created the technology of automatic analysis of the power of emotions contained in the text. According to the developers, the existing systems for automatic text analysis allow evaluating only positive or negative text color based on keywords. The development of Krasnoyarsk scientists will allow to evaluate not only the color of the text, but also the degree of its emotionality. Based on the analysis of texts that displayed basic emotions (fear, anger, disgust, joy, surprise, etc.), Siberian scientists identified words and phrases characteristic of a particular emotion and created corresponding linguistic models. Now linguists of the Siberian Federal University are working on a technology that allows to evaluate texts by the degree of manipulative influence on the reader. Perhaps the popularity of the news - a consequence of the first results in this area.
6 place, 116 publications

However, later it turned out that when creating the prosthesis, the team used a print on a 3D printer made from biodegradable plastic, but, as Daniel Fonov assured, ‚Äúin the future we plan to make prostheses made of titanium and aluminum, and various designs, for example, a culture sleeve, made of carbon fiber ". 4. How many years, how many winters?



Specialists from the Institute of Molecular Biology of the Russian Academy of Sciences, the Russian Gerontological Scientific and Clinical Center, the Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology and other scientific centers have developed a new method for determining the biological age of humans. They have been trying to solve the problem of its determination for many years, and the most successful techniques based on DNA status data allow this to be done with an error of less than three years. However, they require very expensive equipment and specially trained specialists and therefore have not gained acceptance in practical medicine. Studies by Russian scientists were based on data from a human carotid artery ultrasound and tonometry. The results obtained with the help of the carotid artery are inferior in accuracy of working with DNA (an error of up to 6.9 years for men and 5.9 years for women), but these studies can be carried out in any modern clinic.
4 place, 124 publications

3. "The ancient gopher from the gulag" survived



The third place in the May TOP-SCIENCE ranking from NITU "MISiS" is given to the development of researchers of the biological faculty of the Moscow State University named after M.V. Lomonosov, who studied the ancient Indigir longtail gopher and found that his descendants today live in Kamchatka. Indigirsky, they are Bering gophers - a fossil species described by the Soviet zoologist Boris Vinogradov in three specimens, which were found by unknown prisoners of the Gulag in 1946 in vertical mountain pits at a depth of 12.5 meters. The study of their range makes a great contribution to the understanding of transberingian connections and ways of settling different animals during the Pleistocene period in northeast Asia. But the record number of reprints, of course, due to very different circumstances. And if we add that, apart from the Kolyma prisoners, Jewish researchers working on DNA, fossil lice, wild fox Alice and a well-known photo blogger, also appear in this practically detective story, then one has to be surprised rather that this research received only "bronze"

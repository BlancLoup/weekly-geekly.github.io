Generally speaking, quite a few pseudo-random number sensors are available in Mathcad Express:
  • rnd (x) is a uniform distribution on the interval (0, x).
  • rbeta (x, s1, s2) is the beta distribution (s1, s2> 0 are parameters, 0 <x <1).
  • rbinom (k, n, p) is the binomial distribution (n is an integer parameter, 0 <k <n and 0 <p <1 is a parameter equal to the probability of success of a single test).
  • rcauchy (x, l, s) is the Cauchy distribution (l is the decomposition parameter, s> 0 is the scale parameter).
  • rexp (x, r) is the exponential distribution (r> 0 is the exponent).
  • rF (x, d1, d2) is the Fisher distribution (d1, d2> 0 are the numbers of degrees of freedom).
  • rgamma (x, s) is the gamma distribution (s> 0 is the shape parameter).
  • rgeom (k, p) is the geometric distribution (0 <p <1 is a parameter equal to the probability of success of a single test).
  • rhypergeom (k, a, b, n) is the hypergeometric distribution (a, b, n are integer parameters).
  • rlnorm (x, m, s) is the log-normal distribution (m is the natural logarithm of the expectation, s> 0 is the natural logarithm of the mean square deviation).
  • rlogis (x, l, s) is the logistic distribution (l is the mathematical expectation, s> 0 is the scale parameter).
  • rnbinom (k, n, p) is the negative binomial distribution (n> 0 is an integer parameter, 0 <p <1).
  • rnorm (x, m, s) is the normal distribution (m is the mean value, s> 0 is the standard deviation).
  • rpois (k, p) is the Poisson distribution (p> 0 is a parameter).
  • rt (x, d) is the student's t distribution (d> 0 is the number of degrees of freedom).
  • runif (x, a, b) is the uniform distribution (a <b is the interval boundary).
  • rweibull (x, s) is the Weibull distribution (s> 0 is a parameter).

So, for the creation of such a "creative" we need:



  • Non-working hard drive, suitable, in principle, any 2.5 or 3.5 inch;
  • A4 sheet;
  • Scotch tape, or you can take paper glue instead, for example, PVA ;
  • Plasticine, well, or chewing gum;
  • Soldering iron;
  • Crocodile;
  • Multimeter;
  • A battery or rechargeable AA battery or AAA (finger / mini finger);
  • Low-power amplifier for a couple of watts, for example, from old speakers, or bought like mine;
  • Hexagon screwdriver (see photo below);
  • Postings.


The first thing we need is to unscrew the board and the top cover of the hard drive, if it was a worker, then after opening it will definitely be his khan.



Screws, as a rule, at winchesters with an internal hexagon.



Be careful, as a rule, there is one more screw hiding under the top cover label, so if the top cover does not disassemble, look for where you missed.
After removing the cover, we will see something like this:



Virtually any hard disk has a similar design - a rotating disk that rotates with a brushless motor, such as using model airplanes, only it is very weak, just a couple of watts, with this configuration, the quadcopter will not take off 100%. There is also a reading head, which seems to ‚Äúfloat‚Äù over this disk when it rotates. The head is set in motion by the combination of an electromagnet and a permanent magnet, by the way, as a rule, neodymium magnets , which are very powerful for their size, stand in hard disks. There is also a ‚Äúlimiter‚Äù of the stroke of the head (the yellow thing behind the magnet) so that it does not go beyond the edge of the working area, so, it will not allow us to remove the disk from the engine, so it needs to either be unscrewed or broken there is another way:



Inside this plastic, the same neodymium magnet is used as the mechanism for parking the head at the initial position, only a small one, the size of a match head, I believe that these are just perfect for projects on reed switches or Hall sensors .



Unscrew the screws that hold the disc, move the head to the side and remove the "pancake".

A few words about how a normal speaker works - there is a magnet that has a south (denoted by the letter ‚ÄúS‚Äù in the figure above) and a north pole (indicated by the letter ‚ÄúN‚Äù) and there is an electromagnet that also has poles, only unlike the source polarity power supply they can change places, besides changing the current that flows through the coil, we change the power of the electromagnetic field.

Now, in principle, you can send a signal from the amplifier to the head, but it will sound very quiet, to avoid this, we need to make a simple amplifier, in my case, from A4 sheet:



So this is a cone, the larger the radius of its base, the louder our ‚Äúspeaker‚Äù will sound. In order to keep everything I glued together with scotch tape, you can use glue instead of tape.

Then we need to make here such a holder of clay / gum for the cone, just need to first wait until the clay or gum hardens.

Career IT project manager. How to get a job in a leading technology company



Publisher Peter decided to submit a recently published book from the author of the bestseller "Programmer Career" , which will help you best prepare for an interview when hiring a project manager in a large IT organization or a promising startup. The main part of the book consists of answers to technical questions and tasks that applicants usually get during an interview at companies such as Google, Microsoft, Apple, Amazon and others. We consider the typical mistakes made by candidates, as well as effective methods of preparation for the interview.


What this book is written for:

Product management should not be the privilege of the elect, whose luck (and communication) allows you to gain knowledge about the tricks of the profession. Openness
product management is equally beneficial to both job seekers and employers. When looking for a product manager, you can better present yourself if you know what to expect from the interview. It's not about cheating; you can really gain the required experience if you are convinced that it is necessary. When the time comes for interviews, you will be better prepared to answer the employer's questions. You will learn how to describe your unique experience and key achievements, to solve the tasks offered to you. You will learn what it means to focus on users, as well as improve your technical skills. Employers, in turn, will get more qualified applicants. Calm and well-trained candidates use their skills as efficiently as possible. They understand what their achievements are most interesting and relevant for the employer, and concentrate on them during the conversation. Applicants demonstrate their ability to solve problems when they understand the meaning of the questions they are asked. They can get the knowledge they need to solve
certain tasks. Such training more accurately represents the skills of the applicant from the perspective of his future work.
An interview, in which there are no random and guessed answers, is beneficial to all of its participants.

What is this book about:

This book will not help you become a product manager fraudulently, but will allow you to create a complete action plan for obtaining this position. We will draw a portrait of the product manager and the roles that he can play in companies. We will also describe how different companies envision the process of hiring a product manager. We will ask product managers with different experiences about how they started their career path, how they think of a good product manager. We will talk about the typical career paths of product managers and how you can become a product manager, having certain experience and interests.
We will show you how to write a great resume and cover letter. Some of our recommendations go beyond the scope of product management, but most of them are specifically aimed at product managers.

We will give you recommendations on how to prepare for the interview: what information should be sought before the interview and how to prepare for the questions. We will look at the main types of questions asked during the interview, including questions of behavior, evaluation, product production, selection and coding. We will tell you about
what information does the interviewer expect to receive and how to answer his questions. We will help you to do everything possible to get the job you are dreaming about. When reading a book, think about how to present the strengths of your experience.

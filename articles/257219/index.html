Event video surveillance over the Internet for apartment and private homes. Virtual concierge

The cloud-based video surveillance service, for example, iVideon and the Moscow government, has become truly consumer-friendly. Taking into account the low monthly fee, the service had to become mass for a long time and gain users in every staircase and country house. What hinders its development?

The possibility of a simple video broadcast is often insufficient for paid use of the service. The delight of HD video in the browser and on the phone quickly passes, and the user wonders why he needs a live broadcast or a monthly video archive. Looking uninteresting and no time. 99% of the archive is not needed, but the transfer and storage of data at the same time have to pay.

Note: The article is provided with links to the relevant pages of our site for those who love the details.

Speaking about the main monitoring function of any residential object, the user lacks event monitoring, when the system clearly processes all suspicious situations with the help of video analytics , audio analytics and a human operator, for example, people screaming in the stairwell, people at night on the stairs container idle in the parking lot.

In the private sector (in a country house), it is important for the owner to receive a notice of a possible incident as soon as possible, for example, if strangers appear on the site.

In accessible video surveillance systems via the Internet, the user does not have enough search tools for content in the archive. How to immediately see all the events for the day without tiring watching and rewinding video? Has the child returned home from school? What did the gardener do in the country? Who scratched the car in the parking lot?

Some of the listed search functions are available today in video management systems (VMS), for example, from ITV and Milestone, but their use is restricted to professional security users. Their architecture, web interface and mobile applications do not reveal the potential of video analytics for the mass consumer - residents of apartments or country houses.

Remote home video monitoring based on Kipod cloud service

Distributed architecture


As shown in the figure, the solution is based on the Kipod remote monitoring web service. A recording and analytics device ( Kipod Server ) is located directly on the object, in a multi-unit or private house. Network cameras and, optionally, a concierge / guard workstation and a loudspeaker are connected to the device. Analog cameras of the intercom and elevator can be connected via a convert (IP encoder).

An important advantage of a distributed solution is the minimum load on the communication channel and the possibility of autonomous operation of each object in a situation when the communication channel is overloaded or not available. After the restoration of the channel, the events are automatically synchronized with the cloud service. Thus, a solution with a distributed architecture will work with dozens of cameras, even on a wireless 3G channel.

Centralized architecture


An alternative approach is a fully centralized architecture, when all processing and storage is implemented in a data center of a service provider based on a cluster video analytics server . The advantages of a centralized approach are the ease of implementation and reduction of equipment maintenance costs. Disadvantages - higher requirements for bandwidth and, as a result, a limit on the number of cameras and the quality of video recording.

Virtual concierge operators are located in the control center Synesis or work remotely.

Riva Tuner development finished

Recently, the author of the Riva Tuner program, which is well-known among video card owners from NVidia , Alexey Nikolaichuk ( Unwinder ), announced the discontinuation of development. He argues his decision by banal tiredness of work for which no one pays. After more than nine years of continuous development and the main support of the software product, Alex decided to take an indefinite vacation.

Let me remind you that Riva Tuner is a unique product that allows you to fine tune the parameters of a video card and issue a detailed report on all its characteristics. This way it greatly exceeds the capabilities of proprietary drivers. In this connection, she was loved by both overclockers and ordinary users.

This, and so not too good news, is aggravated by the fact that Unwinder is not going to transfer the development to third-party hands, since the program contains a lot of code used by it in its commercial products. Due to the fact that this is a very dynamic market, new series of video cards are released every year, in the near future the program will not be able to provide all the required functionality or refuse to work at all. Perhaps this will encourage other programmers to create their own Riva Tuner counterparts, but without the experience Unwinder accumulated over the years, it will be very difficult at first.

Based on an interview with modlabs.net.

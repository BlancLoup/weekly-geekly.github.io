Development

Before nekto.me, I tried to raise several completely different startups, but almost all of them, for one reason or another, did not meet the expectations. This was due, primarily, to my inexperience.
I did a lot of things wrong, but one of the main mistakes was to make a project with partners, divided into shares.
It ends is trite - one of the partners is distracted, changes priorities, loses enthusiasm and pulls the project to nowhere. In general, I will not dwell on this moment. Theme for a separate post.
Launching nekto.me, I, taught by experience, wanted to share a percentage with programmers.
It was the right decision, because over time the enthusiasm disappeared at all.

For all the time more than 20 freelancers had a hand in the project.
Most of them were from Ukraine or from the average population of Russian cities.

In general, the project development strategy on the Internet should be very flexible, because often they are dying because of the inability to adapt to rapidly changing realities.
At launch, the project was planned as a simple independent addition to existing social networks, but over time I realized that it was better to expand. Addition - a temporary thing.
Six months after the start, blogs and photos, private messages, updates were launched ...
The project gradually acquired various services and turned into a separate portal.

The first additional service was an anonymous chat.
I tried to make it myself, using the source code of the English-language chat Omegle.com
Russified, installed, disappointed. These sources were either from the beta version of Omegle, or did not refer to it at all, because they worked using mySQL and were inadequately resource-intensive.
Messages were not even deleted from the database!
Realizing that the source version is not suitable, I decided to find a performer on the freelance exchange.
A programmer from Ulyanovsk responded, who was in his sixth decade :)
Of course, I was skeptical, but he implemented the required functions in the stipulated time frame.
Chat was written on the same php + mysql. Not fun, but for starters it was enough.
Then, the programmer, as often happens, suddenly disappeared.
Due to the flaws of the first version, I had to rewrite everything from scratch.
Now the chat is written in C ++ and does not bother the server at all, even at 2000 online.

One time it seemed to me that it would be nice to do a section with p2p games,
Oddly enough, sensible blanks on the Internet was not found. I had to write almost from scratch.
Wrote chess, checkers and the fool. Thought out a rating system for victories.
The time has come to integrate this good into the site, and it dawned on me here - the support of this section will be cost-per-work disproportionately more expensive than the benefits of it.
So, at the last stage I had to abandon this idea.

For the organization of the workflow, megaplan was first used, but it seemed rather heavy.
Then they used planary, but eventually settled on the local myTinyTodo. Now it has 60+ tasks.

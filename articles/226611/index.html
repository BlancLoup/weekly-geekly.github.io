Where and how is IBM Watson used? Part 2



In the first part of the review of directions for using the capabilities of the IBM Watson supercomputer, it was shown that Watson works in oncology, helping to develop a personalized treatment course for a specific person.

In addition, IBM Watson also works in the field of health insurance, in the banking sector, the supercomputer helps researchers to find relationships between various aspects of their work, and also begins to help officials.

Health insurance



At present, the capabilities of the supercomputer are used by WellPoint, Inc. This is an American company (135 in the Fortune Global 500 list) that provides health insurance services.

The company cooperates with approximately 600 thousand professionals working with medical data of clients. About 80-90% of such data is unstructured information. People are not always able to correctly process such data arrays, identify dependencies, and propose an optimal solution.



With the help of IBM Watson, a supercomputer whose capabilities are optimized for working with Big Data, all this data is processed quickly, identifying the necessary connections and further structuring information.

As a result, WellPoint, Inc. improves the quality and effectiveness of their own medical decisions.



Financial Markets (US and Mexico)



Modern financial sphere is a very difficult subject to study. Every day only about 6 thousand articles and news about finance are published on the WSJ (Wall Street Journal). To keep track of such a flow of information for one person or even the company's average arm is almost impossible.

But in order to understand where the financial market is moving, you need to process all this data.

DBS Bank decided to test the capabilities of IBM Watson to optimize its work. Measures taken by regulators, social and government changes, fluctuations of financial markets - now all this is taken into account in the work of the bank.

In addition, Watson Engagement Advisor, a consultant from IBM Watson, is used to optimize the work of bank managers and customers. A personalized approach in the banking sector is no less in demand than in the medical field.

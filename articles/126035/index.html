Export messages from Google Reader to Google+

I found a way to export messages from Google Reader to Google+ in fact in two clicks, and the entire message is exported, and not just the urls title as written by Google.



If anyone interested - welcome under the cat

In general, as they say, it was in the evening, there was nothing to do, I was looking for ways to post messages directly from the Google reader to Google Plus and Twitter.

At first I played with Twitter, everything is simple, the functionality is conceived in the settings - Send to , the name $ {title} and the short URL $ {short-url} are transmitted

And there it is very well implemented Blogger, the entire message is sent to it, with html tags as expected.

But this is all conceived by Google, and cannot be edited for personal needs, but below you can create your own custom link using variables:
${source}The source of the item
${title}The title of the item
${url}The URL of the item
${short-url}A shortened URL that redirects to the item

Now you need to determine the address where the data will be transferred, i.e. address of the new message. In particular, Google+ does not have such an address, on the version from the computer, but if you log in from your mobile, there is a button - Publish with a very specific address that you can use.

What actually was done:


Further excavation, analysis of the source pages and the method of scientific typing led to the discovery of another variable not specified in the description, which just conveys the contents of the message, this variable ${body}

Url for Google+ converted to m.google.com/app/plus/x/?v=compose&hideloc=1&content=*${title}*%0A_from%20${source}_%0A%0A${body}%0A%0A%3A%20${short-url}

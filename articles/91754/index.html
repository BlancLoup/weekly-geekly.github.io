HTC Desire: Another Look

Being a happy (and patient) owner of the HTC Hero, I just could not ignore HTC Desire.
I will write a short review of this wonderful communicator.


Specification


I will give only the main numbers:
Software: Android Eclair 2.1 HTS Sense Interface
Processor / Chipset Qualcomm QSD8250, 1 GHz
119 x 60 x 11.9 mm body
135 grams with battery
Memory Flash: 512 MB RAM: 576 MB
3.7 inch display, 480 x 800 resolution (WVGA) AMOLED
Motion Sensors (accelerometer) Approximation of the phone to your ear Ambient light (system for automatically adjusting the screen brightness)
FM radio
Rechargeable lithium-ion battery BB99100 1400 mAh, 3.7 V, 5.18 Wh
Talk time (GSM) - up to 400 minutes (6.5 hours)
Standby time (GSM) - up to 340 hours
Automatically reduces the ringer volume when you pick up the phone
Transfer the call to silent mode if you put the phone face down
Automatically save a backup version of certain data and settings - SMS / MMS messages, bookmarks, Wi-Fi passwords and other things - on a microSD card.

Desired by the Hero


First of all, it seems to me that Desire is some kind of work on the bugs of HTC Hero and Nexus One.
Something succeeded better, something remained the same. There are shortcomings. For example - changing memory cards only if you remove the battery. I also consider the lack of receiving and rejecting buttons as a drawback. IPhone users are accustomed to the screen, and I already lost the habit.
The screen is gorgeous. Resolution class. The picture is juicy and pleasant. Watch movies, read books, and just wandering around the menu is lovely!
And also - animated wallpaper! Beauty! And react to music, touch and (or) time of day!

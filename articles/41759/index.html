Your life in 15 minutes

I read the friendly banter of the social community over the girl who makes websites in 15 minutes at Metrograd , kept herself in her hands for a while, and then did not hold back.

Let's consider not a specific girl behind a particular counter, but the phenomenon of sites ‚Äúin 15 minutes‚Äù, ‚Äúfor 1000 rubles‚Äù, etc. in principle, from the point of view of the life cycle of systems, and your - yes, specifically your - life.



Any system goes through 3 life stages. And it does not matter what the system is: the growth of bacteria in a bowl of broth, the development of aircraft construction or the life of an ordinary person - the stages are essentially the same, and differ only in details.

Let's look at them on the example of the ‚Äúwebsite creation‚Äù market:

1. Becoming. At this stage, site builders only grope their way. No standards - sheer creativity. Each product (site) is not like the others. The incompatibility of ‚Äúparts and assemblies‚Äù of one product with others is often encountered. Each new project starts from scratch, there is no talk about using standard blocks and tools.

Sites are few, they can make units. Users who go through the sites are also extremely few. Money for sites often do not pay - they are made by enthusiasts to meet personal ambitions.

But gradually the sites become more and more, the market is defined with basic standards, the number of Internet users is growing, and the number of site builders is catching up.

2. Development. Often this stage is divided into 2 sub-stages - the first and second parts.

The first part of the second stage:
The market is growing, the number of orders is growing. This encourages site builders to standardize what they are doing - to give birth to a hedgehog every time, it is easier to make standard blocks of quality once, which can be used in new projects with minor changes in the future.

Labor productivity is increasing. At this stage, leaders begin to stand out - those who, before others, were able to rebuild cottage industry into a small manufactory.

The second part of the second stage:
The market is growing significantly. Well-known (and simply high-quality) manufacturers are torn apart by orders. They are fenced off from this by high and super high prices for their products. Someone from clients overcomes this qualification - and gets a site for 50-100 kilobaksov. Someone falls off - and becomes the prey of small and medium-sized studios, which by that time had also switched to standard CMS of their own or third-party production and, in fact, offer customers standard solutions, drawing on their (sometimes still unique) design.

At the same time, revolutionary new technologies do not appear - there is a familiar set of tools, interface elements and expectations from the user, on the basis of which twin sites appear, and the site for 1k bucks differs from the site for 10k mainly in the studio's ability to apply.

Those who have not switched to standard development tools - quietly rot, go into the deep niches of specialized sites or ... begin a new cycle of the new system in a completely new field.

3. Stagnation, stagnation.
The market stops growing. Outwardly, it is still very good - sites are ordered, large studios are still distinguished by megatariffs, there is food for small and medium-sized market participants. But prices are steadily falling - this is the fruit of tough competition, when studios really have nothing to stand out in the market for standard solutions, except as a price. At the same time, the costs for the production of the site (mainly due to the RFP) are steadily increasing - which means that the meaning of engaging in this activity is lost.

The prudent ones do not wait for the 3rd stage, but, starting from the second part of the second stage, a new line of activity will start, using the accumulated resources: financial, personnel, etc. Where to go, everyone determines for himself. But in fact, we see that the bulk goes to the CEO and the creation of traffic-generating projects "for themselves."

The third stage may last long enough, but in the end the market, in the form to which everyone is accustomed, will face inevitable death.

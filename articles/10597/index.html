SMO - the invention of the ancient Romans

Recently, re-reading a little book with sayings and winged expressions of the ancient Romans, I suddenly discovered that they were great specialists in SMO. Many of their maxims and principles seemed to have been deliberately invented in order to make life easier for their descendants who survived to WEB 2.0.
Now I will try to explain in more detail and with examples ...

1. DO UT DES
I give you to give
It is well-known that people treat better those who spare no effort to help others. Be helpful to the community in which you are promoting your project. First, share interesting articles, links, participate in discussions, prove yourself. Ask yourself what you can give to the community. If the answer was quickly found, the result will not be long in coming.

2. AMICUS VERUS - RARA AVIS
True friend - rare bird
Try to find like-minded people and comrades in any environment, it is easier to go to the goal together. In addition, if you are a beginner, experienced friends will be able to help you earn much-needed good reputation faster.

4. CARUM QUOD RARUM
Expensive is that rare

5. CREDO, QUIA ABSURDUM
I believe because it's ridiculous
Try to surprise as often as possible, do not neglect unexpected forecasts and original conclusions, be convincing and they will believe you. Discussion of the possibility of planting green men on the lawn of the White House is not without meaning.

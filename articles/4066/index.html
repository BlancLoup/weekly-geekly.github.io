Russia will not be accepted into the WTO until the authorities close AllofMP3.com

The problem of musical piracy in Russia was seriously worried by American officials. Officials from the US Trade Representation said that this issue could be a matter of principle for Russia's admission to the World Trade Association.

As is known, the United States receives billions in profits from the export of intangible assets - computer programs, technology, video, audio products, etc. Competently built patent system and international conventions on copyright protection help America to maintain the status quo.

Computer piracy, illegal copying of movies and music on a global scale are problems that may actually threaten the foundations of the American economy, the stability of the currency and the welfare of Americans. For example, Russian pirates, according to American experts, last year caused damage to the US economy in the amount of about $ 1.8 billion. Not surprisingly, the requirements of the RIAA association and other representatives of the "anti-piracy lobby" are tacitly supported by the American authorities.

Official officials from the US Trade Representative Office ( US Trade Representatives Office ) warn that because of the site AllofMP3.com Russia may have problems entering the World Trade Association, which Russia has been trying to join for several years.

The website AllofMP3.com is well-known in Russia and abroad as a music store, where MP3-files are sold about ten times cheaper than in Western stores, such as iTunes ($ 0.1 instead of $ 0.99). Unlike Western stores, here the files downloaded from the site are not copy protected. Moreover, in the assortment of AllofMP3.com there are even albums of groups for which the right holders did not agree to distribute online, for example, The Beatles and Metallica.

How to stop worrying and start shooting properly on the phantom

Have you removed beautiful and smooth spans from the copter, and after installation everything is twitching? If the original video causes delight, and the result is tears, then perhaps the problem is in the wrong shooting mode.



Using the example of DJI Phantom 3 Professional, we will look at the available shooting modes.


If you open the list of all available, it is difficult to determine which mode to use - there are a lot of them: 24, 25, 30, 48, 50, 60 frames per second.


However, everything is not so simple. If you look at the properties of the captured files, then instead of 24, 30, 48 and 60 we will see there 23.976, 29.97, 47.952 and 59.94. And, although the difference is already in hundredths, with incorrect installation settings, we will get jerking. Tested on personal experience.

It is worth noting that the audio track is not used during the installation of airborne surveys, and therefore it is very easy to fix in most editors. But this is a topic for a separate article.

So how do you choose the frame rate? Standards


Why all these 23.976, 24, 25, 29.97 and why you can not choose others? Where did such strange fractional numbers come from?

As is often the case, the reason for all this disgrace is historical and these frequencies correspond to different standards:
  • 24/48 frames used in cinema
  • 25/50 (PAL / SECAM) for TV broadcasting in Europe and Russia
  • 23.976, 47.952 and 29.97, 59.94 (NTSC) when broadcasting TV in the USA and Japan - on television


Therefore, if the material you are shooting is used in a TV report, you should use 25 or 50 frames per second.

So how do you choose the frame rate? Bitrate


In addition to the resolution, the quality of the picture is also influenced by the bitrate (bitrate), i.e. the amount of information to store one second of video. In this case, the fan bitrate depends on the resolution, but the actual resolution may be limited by the quality of the optics, which is clearly demonstrated in the next video.



The Ambarella A9 video codec installed on the third phantom allows you to encode 4K video with a bit rate of 100 Mbps. However, DJI has limited this value to 60 Mbit / s in the PRO version. And it is unlikely that this is due to the speed of the memory cards - even inexpensive memory cards can write 12.5 MB / s, but what we have, that is, we will choose from this.

So how do you choose the frame rate? Smoothness


There is a myth that a person distinguishes only 24 frames per second. In fact, a person is able to distinguish significantly faster frame rates. The reason for choosing 24 frames per second, again, is historical, it is a compromise between smoothness, film saving and sound quality on an optical sound track.



An example of an optical phonogram on film. As it is not difficult to guess the frame rate directly affects the smoothness of the video.

The process of manually writing the word has an important meaning. All the old "old-fashioned" methods have one common point, namely, the manual recording of the word. Words are written on objects, stickers, in personal dictionaries, etc. When we write a word, several processes occur.

Firstly, we repeat it several times on an irresponsible level, perhaps some people even pronounce (it has been established that people who read slowly speak words to themselves).

Secondly, when we write, we create the ‚Äúimage‚Äù of the word and memorize it, and the brain remembers the movements of the hand produced during the recording. This is called motor memory, if I'm not mistaken. In most programs, this action is absent, because I want to achieve results faster, making less effort and not wasting time on such a boring process as adding words.

There is no universal way of learning a language / vocabulary, since each person has a different type of memory and it is his most effective use. Someone better assimilates information in text form, someone in the audio, someone in the figurative.

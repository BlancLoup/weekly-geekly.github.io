How it worked before



At the heart of the battery is the Seebeck effect, which the developers themselves explain in simple words like this:
In a thermocouple, with a temperature difference, electrons migrate from the hot side to the cold side, creating a voltage.
The energy that arises when the difference in degrees is enough to power the low-powered Ambiq processor, and thus no one experiences any problems with autonomy and current performance characteristics. The thermocouple itself is not so small and occupies almost all the space under the display and the board.

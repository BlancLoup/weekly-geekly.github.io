Github stats


Publishing code on Github or other open hosting is often referred to as such life-giving euthanasia, after which patches, fixes, problem reports, and other third-party commits will flow. I still have the opposite experience. In a couple of my relatively visible projects in general, no one and nothing really brought, not to mention the other projects. Moreover, several times I made very serious changes / improvements to other people's projects, but their authors froze and did not accept the changes. Based on this, I developed a theory: they say, all this openness and collaboration are beautiful words, really everyone is sawing something of their own and earning money or PR to someone else is completely unwilling. To test the theory, I counted some statistics on all repositories on Github, which have more than 700 stars, a little more than 4 thousand.

The main drawback of the study is the so-called. unauthorized commits are not counted at all. And there are quite a few of them; in many projects, almost all commits are unauthorized. Below you will see many projects with a negative number of additions. This means that the main development goes unauthorized commits, and authorized users only delete the code :)

The second drawback is that commits from authorized people who are not in the first hundred by the number of commits are not taken into account.

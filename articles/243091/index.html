Copyright


For a start, briefly about what copyright and licenses are all about.

Meanwhile in Russia

If you have created a work (for example, a program) as a result of intellectual activity, then in this case you are its author (s). You have the property and non-property rights to this work. You may transfer property rights to this work to someone else, but you will not be able to transfer non-property rights, including authorship. Being an author is your inalienable and non-transferable right.

Even if you, while creating the work, worked ‚Äúfor an uncle‚Äù, then in this case the author is not at all a kind of abstract LLC. Perhaps, when you took a job, you signed, including the clause about ‚Äúthe alienation of exclusive rights to the results of your intellectual activity in favor of the employer‚Äù in the contract or something like that. Perhaps not (in this case, google " Service Work "). In both cases, the author is you. And have some rights.

Licenses

Another way to transfer rights to a work is a license. In this case, the rights are not alienated, they are transferred in accordance with what is written in the license agreement between the user and the copyright holder. Yes, the license is the contract! All software licenses, both commercial and free, are such a contract. It states what you can and cannot do with software, and how far the right holder can send you in case of complaints. For example, a license may give you the right to install the program, but limit this right to only one computer , otherwise you will come to your uncle in masks and take everything away .

Pay attention to an important point: if you have enough rights to the work, then you can distribute it under different licenses (including at the same time). For example, on your site you can distribute the program for free under a free license, and in any application store it can be sold for money under their standard license.

You can also change the license at any time: for example, today on your site the program was free under a free license, and tomorrow it is paid and with closed source. But in this case, you cannot force the users who downloaded the program to follow the rules of the new license. This is logical, because they received a program under another contract.

In the world

In general, the above is applicable to most countries of the world. The fact is that thanks to several international conventions, the copyright laws in the world are similar (but there are a number of countries that have not signed the conventions). However, even with this, differences exist. For example, in Russia, non-property rights are not alienated. And, say, in Canada, you can refuse them, but you can not pass. Therefore, if possible, it is always better to consult a local lawyer.

And from a practical point of view, the main difference between free and non-free licenses is in order. Non-free licenses are used to earn money and not allow competitors to earn money on it, while free licenses are used to make it possible to use the fruits of your labor for free.

Copyleft

Before proceeding to the description of licenses, you need to understand what copyleft and permissive (permissive) licenses are. Copyleft licenses are free licenses that require the distribution of derivative products under the same license. That is, if you used a library under your copyleft license in your program, then you will have to distribute your program under it. The task of licensing licenses, on the contrary, is to allow any possible use of the product.

Public Domain (Public Domain)
This, of course, is not a license. But many see the translation of a work into the public domain as a way to relinquish property rights. Periodically, people try to do this in terms of programs. Usually they simply write that the project is in the Public Domain and are glad that everyone will be able to use it. But actually it is not! In different countries, the public domain is different. Moreover, in most of them, the law clearly does not provide mechanisms for translating the work into the public domain at the request of the author! For example, in Russia, the transition to the public domain is determined only after the expiration of the validity of copyright protection. Copyright in source


You probably noticed that many licenses offer to place a certain text as a comment in the header of the file? If this is a mandatory requirement, then it must be followed. But how necessary is such a text if the license does not imply an explicit requirement?

Good news: in this case, the license and even the copyright do not necessarily indicate in the file header. Your work is already yours, there is no need to specify a copyright to confirm this. You will still have to confirm the authorship or ownership of rights in other ways, and the text of the license may be in a separate file.

But still such a heading is better to have. The main reasons are as follows:
  • He clearly shows that the rights to the code belong to someone. Excuses of the form "I did not know, it is not written there, could not find, did not notice" is no longer rolled. In other words, the presence of such a header prevents accidental misuse of the code, and can also increase the responsibility for intentional.
  • It makes it possible to identify the owner of the rights to the code in order to contact him, including on issues of the legality of using this code.

By the way, it makes sense not only for an open-source project. Specifying the owner of the rights and authorship can also help the proprietary code in the event that it somehow leaks into the network.

If you decide that you need to be notified of the rights to the source code file, this is what it should ideally contain:
  • Copyright - since in some countries a single copyright symbol is not enough for the legal significance of the notice.
  • ¬© is a copyright symbol, in most countries it is necessary and sufficient to give legal significance to the notice. A simple letter c in brackets (c) may not be enough for this. Use Unicode already!
  • 2007, 2009, 2010 - 2012 - ‚Äúyears of life‚Äù of the code, the first number is the year when the product was first published, then the years when the code was updated. If the years when the code in the file has been corrected, do not go in a row, then they need to be separated by commas.
  • John Doe is the name of the copyright owner. Not the author, it can be different faces! It may be the name of a person, the name of a company, or the names of several people. However, in the latter case, it is better to make a separate line for each person.
  • All rights reserved - ‚Äúall rights reserved‚Äù, means that the specified persons have all rights to the code. Additional enhancement of copyright notice, in the case of possession of exclusive rights.
  • If possible, give a link to the license agreement or an indication of where to find it.
  • Specify contact details.

Please note that the author's name is not included in the notification. The author / authors can be specified separately, for example, on the next line, in free form (for example, Author: Jane Doe ).

Conclusion


Posting your project on the Internet and writing ‚Äúuse everything!‚Äù Is not enough to really start using it. And it's not about advertising or the usefulness of a particular project. Often, a clear understanding is needed of how and how a project can be used, especially if the goals of use are commercial. In particular, the words ‚Äúuse all‚Äù are unlikely to be presented by an agreement with the copyright holder in the event of any problems.

When choosing a license, think, first of all, that you are writing a license not for yourself, but for those who will use your code. She regulates your relationship with them. Who will use your code? How will they use it? Which license will be more convenient for them? What problems can a license have for them? And you?By answering such questions, you can find the best license.

In turn, if you use someone else's project for your own purposes, then you need to understand the limitations imposed on you by his license. Are they right for you, can you meet these requirements?

A license, even free, is a contract between the copyright holder and the user. Try to treat it that way.

Victor Gamov on In-Memory Data Grids and Hazelcast on jug.msk.ru

At the regular meeting of jug.msk.ru , which took place on May 4, 2016 in the office of CROC , Viktor Gamov spoke about In-Memory Data Grids in general and Hazelcast in particular. Details and photos from the meeting on.



About speaker


Victor is a co-founder (along with Alexei Abashev) and one of the leading popular podcasts Debriefing . He actively participates in Java conferences, often as a speaker. At jug.msk.ru, Viktor speaks for the second time; you can read about the first performance in November 2014 here .

Changes that have happened with Viktor since the last performance on jug.msk.ru :
  • went to work at Hazelcast ;
  • took part in several other conferences as a speaker ( DevNexus 2015 , QCon New York 2015 , DevNexus 2016 , JPoint 2016 );
  • son was born (it can be assumed the most important event).

At the recent JPoint 2016 conference held in Moscow, most of the leading "Debriefing" took part, almost all were speakers at the conference. Victor opened the conference in a keynote overview report, talked about distributed caches and JCache, and actively participated in recording a live podcast release at the StereoHall club.

About the report


The report was an overview of the organization of distributed data in memory and distributed computing. The example of Hazelcast shows the practical application of a product from a given subject area. The title of the report alluded to the 1972 Woody Allen film What you Wanted to Ask ( Everything you wanted to know about sex, but were afraid to ask ).

The meeting was attended by two other leading "Debriefing" - Alexey Abashev and Kirill Tolkachev. Kirill is present in the picture below (from left to right - Andrei Kogun, Victor Gamov, Kirill Tolkachev).



The first part was introductory in In-Memory Data Grids ( IMDG ). During the break and after the report, you could talk with Victor and ask him questions.



The second part showed the use of Hazelcast , you can only watch the video, just viewing the presentation will not help. There were also code samples and answers to questions. From the presentation, only two thirds of the slides were shown, so the rest can be browsed by yourself .

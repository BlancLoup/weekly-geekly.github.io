The Taiwanese company has released the smallest USB 3.0 flash drive: PQI U819V



Almost every day there are devices on the market that support the relatively new USB 3.0 standard. External hard drives, mobile phones, flash-drives. Naturally, manufacturers try to somehow highlight their devices, making them something different from others, and adding the word "very" to the description. The company PQI has released the smallest USB flash drive with support for USB 3.0 standard, providing it not with the loudest name of the U819V. But the flash drive is really interesting, both in size (geometric) and performance.

The manufacturer claims that the company's specialists have solved the problem with overheating devices of this kind, after conducting a series of studies. In addition, many shortcomings were corrected, several improvements were added to the design of the device, as a result of which it became possible the appearance of such a mini-drive with support for the USB 3.0 standard.

In appearance, the drive is not very different from other such devices, and the weight of the flash drive is only 4 grams. Several models of such devices are available, starting with the youngest model of 4 gigabytes, and ending with the older model of 32 gigabytes. It is also interesting that the manufacturer promises a lifetime warranty on your device. Data transfer is also impressive: up to 5 gigabits per second.

The dimensions of the stick are minimized: 31.5 x 14.8 x 7.3 mm. The device is positioned by the manufacturer as a flash drive "on the road", and indeed, it is difficult to imagine a more efficient "mobile" drive with similar performance.

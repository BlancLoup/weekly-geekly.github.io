Apple is returning to using Twitter to promote content from the iTunes Store

As a company that for a long time declined to participate in social media, Apple began to conquer Twitter, to promote the new content of the iTunes store.

According to TechCrunch , Apple recently registered several Twitter accounts to advertise music, movies, TV series and podcasts in iTunes. The Cupertino-based company started the first account, iTunesTrailer , back in March, which now has about 1.2 million subscribers.

New iTunesMusic , iTunesMovies , iTunesTV and iTunesPodcasts accounts contain just a few posts and have thousands of subscribers.



Apple also did cross-PR in its accounts using a background that advertises iTunes-related accounts. Visitors are also asked to support the project on the iTunes fan page on Facebook.

Earlier this year, it was already rumored that Apple might be interested in acquiring Twitter . However, rumors still do not cease to be rumors.

Brown dwarfs


Where do legs grow from? A number of stars (for example, Gliese 229) are double, while the second star is not exactly a star, but a so-called brown dwarf. The brown dwarf is a small star, inside which thermonuclear reactions take place, but they are quite small, so the star quickly loses energy and eventually turns into a planet-like object. On average, the masses of brown dwarfs range from 0.01 to 0.1 solar masses. In connection with the improvement of observational technology and the emergence of space telescopes, astronomers have noticed that many stars have similar ‚Äúcompanions,‚Äù after which it was suggested that the Sun does not have a similar neighbor?

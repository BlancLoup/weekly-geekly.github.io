Cloud technologies, personnel management and forecasting - the main trends of 2016

A period of unpredictability is the best time for the IT industry. It often happens that the most innovative technologies develop from the most unexpected and unattractive undertakings. Contenders for the role of revolutionary companies appear so often that they are going to the queue. However, few manage to change the existing order of things. And when this happens, we are again convinced that the market situation can change very quickly, and, as a rule, without special warnings. This is exactly what makes the IT sector such an exciting field of activity and helps many of us get up early in the morning and go to work.

On the one hand, an attempt to predict the future may seem a futile venture. On the other hand, to resist the desire to keep your nose in the wind and understand where this wind is blowing is simply impossible. The speed with which innovations occur convinces us again and again that organizations that fail to follow the latest news in their field of activity put themselves at serious risk. Being at the forefront of technology development means following them and anticipating trends, while being able, at the same time, to benefit from the use of the latest tools and technologies offered by the market.

Despite the inability to make detailed forecasts, we, nevertheless, can say with confidence that in 2016 companies will continue to use the full potential of information technology for growth and development.

Pedal to the floor: acceleration due to cloud computing


Today, the victory of cloud technologies is obvious, but just some two years ago, the application of such solutions should have been discussed at an early stage of the deal, and the desire to transfer some aspects of doing business to the cloud would be met with alarm or even resistance. Now the use of cloud technologies is becoming a typical solution. And if 2015 can be called the year of universal recognition of cloud technologies, then 2016 will be the year of their continuous growth. Tightening requirements in this area may soon lead to the fact that organizations will no longer cope with their satisfaction and will not be able to properly update their IT infrastructure. In this regard, the transition to the cloud can be a safe alternative for international companies - delegating responsibility for meeting the requirements of a regulator to a third party can be a reasonable way out of this situation.

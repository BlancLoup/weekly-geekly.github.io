"Sanctions" and science: 42% of elite Russian scientific journals belong to the USA



The reason for this note was a small, almost imperceptible news about the restriction of access to Open Source and online education for countries that are in strained relations with the United States.

The following information itself is far from new (release date of the article is 2008), but, in our opinion, right now is a good time to pay attention to this problem again.


For analysis, we selected from the RSCI 100 most cited Russian journals for 2012 and checked which publishers published these journals. It turned out that 42 of the hundreds of magazines belong to the Akademichesky Scientific Publishing, Production, Printing and Book Distributing Center Publishing House of the Russian Academy of Sciences Nauka Publishing House.

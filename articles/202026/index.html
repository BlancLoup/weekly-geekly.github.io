What is a lawn mower? We disassemble Robomow RS 630

Lawnmower robots are still of little popularity in Russia. But, judging by our sales, the demand is growing every year by more than 100%. People are interested, they are looking at happy neighbors and are eager to buy such a device in the garden. Then they know the price, and their enthusiasm often fades away. Pleasure is quite expensive. Are mowers worth their money or not, and how to choose the right one for your lawn? Let's figure it out.


On the Russian market, the most popular manufacturers of lawn-mowers are: Caiman, Robomow, Wiper, Husqvarna. There are less popular models from Bosch, Stiga, Worx and others. Caiman and Wiper are mowers of one Italian company Zucchetti, but produced under different brands in Russia. Robomow is produced in Israel, formerly known as Frendly Robotics. Husqvarna is a Swedish industrial company that manufactures a variety of garden and garden tools and robotic mowers are only a small fraction of their product range.
Today we will talk about the top model from the company Robomow, but first we will go through the main characteristics of the robotic lawn mowers and understand in principle their work.

How it was?


Progress did not stand still, people became more and more lazy, bought country houses with large plots - these factors gave manufacturers a way to reflect on new types of devices that could autonomously mow lawns. What is the principle of lawn mowers?


Basically, almost all lawn mowers require laying a limiter cable around the perimeter. The cable defines the boundaries of the mowing zone. On it goes a constant signal from the base. Obviously, one end of the cable comes out of the base station, the other enters it, that is, the circuit should not have gaps (the phrase: ‚Äúthere wasn‚Äôt, not a single break!‚Äù) Was remembered. It is also obvious that most gardeners have a lawn that is not even and empty, there may be trees, flowerbeds, a house in the end. All robotic lawn mowers have obstacle sensors like the vacuum cleaner robots at their disposal. True, the latter have optical and IR sensors, that is, the robot may not touch furniture, and mechanical sensors are usually installed in mowers. Sensors are triggered by obstacles of a height of 10 cm. Boulders, curbs, trees, the mower will determine as an obstacle and just go round. But flowers can eat, and from the pond and water get drunk. In order to avoid the latter, it is necessary to correctly lay the perimeter wire.

As can be seen from the figure, the wire will have to bend around a flowerbed or other obstacle. What to look for when choosing?


The cost of robot mowers varies from 50,000 to 300,000 rubles. Here is what you need to pay attention to when choosing such a device:
1) There should be a quality service and preferably 2 years warranty. The robot lawn mower is a technically complex product that most likely will not work out in a third-party service center.
2) Capture width. This indicator indicates how fast your autonomous assistant mows the lawn. The greater the width, the faster the lawn will mow. In inexpensive models (Caiman L50), it is 24 cm, in advanced models (Robomow RS630) - 56 cm.
3) Proper load distribution depending on the volume of grass. Robomow, for example, can slow down in places where there is tall grass, it measures the current on mowing engines. If the grass is high, it means a big load, it means a high current, so it is necessary to reduce the speed of movement for the best result.
4) Power consumption. The higher it is, the better and faster the robot mows. Sometimes you can find two robots with the same mowing area. For example, both robots are designed for 1000 sq. M., But one has a width of 30 cm and the other 57, one has a power of 100 W and the other 400 W, who should you prefer?
Of course, the one who is more powerful and has a large width, he will mow the lawn faster.
5) The type and capacity of the battery. Affects the size of the lawn, which can serve the device. Mowers will be able to work easier on 4 acres as much as possible, and advanced models up to 30 acres. The best lithium iron-phosphate, they have a large number of discharge charge cycles up to 2000, and hence a huge service life compared to standard lithium-ion batteries. Obviously, there is no talk of nickel-metal hydride batteries.
6) Multizone is the ability of the robot to mow the grass on different and distant lawns. For example, can or not the robot, having mowed the lawn in front of the house, fully charge the battery, and automatically go along the narrow passage or path, mow the lawn in the back yard, and then return to the base station without taking your time?
7) The presence of the base for charging included. Everything is simple here. If there is no base, then you will have to carry a robot to charge home or to the barn,
charge it, then carry it back to the lawn and run. If there is a base station, the mower charges automatically. The robot leaves the base station itself, mows itself, returns and charges itself, and so on throughout the season.
I also recommend to pay attention to the power supply unit that powers the base station. It must be wide-band because in the villages and cottage settlements often the input voltage does not fit into the standards of 220V.
8) Cutting the edges. This is a special program that forces the robot to pass along the edges and corners of the lawn for perfect mowing. The mowing mode can be implemented only on those robots that can pick up grass outside the wheels. Also, the quality of the lawn is influenced by the deck (the board on which the knives are fixed) on the design of the knives and the deck depends on the quality of mulching and mowing. In some models of robot decks have a "floating suspension" deck with knives exactly repeats the relief of the lawn. Mowing example

A small Bluetooth adapter turns any headphones into wireless



Wired headphones can be of high quality, but can - not very. In some models, wires have the ability to tangle at any time convenient for them. And the fact that the smartphone connected to the headphones needs to be taken out from time to time, aggravates the situation.

A good way out of this situation may be wireless headphones. But what if for a long time you use high-quality wired ones that you don‚Äôt want to change? In this case, you should pay attention to the new project on Kickstarter, which was called Jack. The developer is a small company Podo Labs.


The adapter appeared on Kickstarter just a few weeks ago, but has already received the approval of a large number of resource users. Thus, instead of $ 20,000, developers have already collected more than $ 550,000. And the amount continues to increase. The cost of the device is probably worth mentioning right away - the price of Jack is about $ 29. Not so much, in general, if you consider that with the adapter you can use any wired headphones.

Inside the adapter is a 300 mAh battery, it lasts for a whole day of headphones. You can listen to music with another person. True, this requires two Jacks at once, there is no additional connector on the adapter. According to the developers, music playback is quite high quality thanks to aptX technology.



In order to start working with the device, you only need to connect your own wired headphones to the adapter, then connect the adapter wirelessly to the sound source and everything can be used.



The adapter is compatible with any gadgets with Bluetooth - phones, tablets, desktop PCs, laptops or watches. If necessary, you can connect the adapter to a TV that does not have Bluetooth, and already connect wireless headphones to the adapter. This is convenient for gamers or ordinary people who do not want to disturb the home too much noise.


The gadget can also be used as a wireless headset, so if there is no microphone in the headphones, this is no longer a problem.

The team worked for a long time to create their device, the video posted above shows some points of the development process. So the fake is unlikely, most likely, the device does appear on the market.

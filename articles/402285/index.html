Seek Thermal thermal imager overview and application



We are surrounded by many objects, and they all emit heat waves. Yes, absolutely anyone, even if the surface temperature is much below zero, it may seem strange, but it is. And since all objects radiate heat, people wanted to see it. Of course, our eyes are not so perfect and do not work in such a spectrum, so a special device was invented.


Thermal imager Seek Thermal.


Thermal imager on a smartphone.

The principle of the thermal imager is simple; a special sensor picks up the heat coming from the object and displays it graphically on the device display.

Principle of operation


Not only does all the objects radiate heat, they also have different radiation intensities, and most importantly, they differ in the length of the heat wave. Let's look at a specific example, the wavelength range of sunlight falls on 380 - 740 nanometers. If the figure is below 380 - this is ultraviolet, and if it is above 740, then this is the so-called infrared radiation.



The device not only sees infrared radiation perfectly, but can also independently determine the wavelength, due to which the temperature of the examined surface is displayed on the device's screen. And if the usual thermometer helps to set the temperature of a certain point, then the imager allows you to examine the entire object.

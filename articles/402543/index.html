Moscow providers' revenues fall after market saturation



Incomes of metropolitan Internet access providers began to fall following the decline in the cost of services. The reason for this was the saturation of the Moscow market and the successive attempts of players to lure users to their more favorable rates, according to Vedomosti.

Since 2016, tariffs for Internet access in the capital have decreased, on average, by 7.1% - from 430 to 390 rubles. The high-speed tariffs were the most expensive: 50-100 Mbit / s connection fell by 7.9% to average 575 rubles, and over 100 Mbit / s - by 14.2% to 969 rubles.

Serious competition to classic wired providers is made by mobile operators due to the ubiquitous deployment of LTE networks. Thus, in 2016, the subscriber base of conventional providers decreased by 1%, while, as mobile operators constantly report an increase in the load on cellular networks and an increase in the number of subscribers of Internet plans.

Giants, Rostelecom and MGTS, remain on the plus side in the market. These providers are not the outflows, but the growth of the subscriber base. This is due to the fact that Rostelecom and MGTS can provide subscribers with a greater speed of access to the Network than competitors.

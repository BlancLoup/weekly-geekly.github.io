How to buy flagship smartphones profitable


(c) macdigger.ru

Aggressive advertising is trying to get to buy a new smartphone or other unit right at the start of sales. Companies go to any tricks: unique features, a fresh operating system, fashionable bells and whistles. Anything, just to get the user to buy now the latest, the coolest.

Anyway want? Then on the example of smartphones we will tell you how to save up to 400 dollars.

Is it worth chasing?


Yes, it is in such amounts. The widespread sales system refurbished (restored) and simply stale technology allows you to get even such a benefit. Especially today, when in pursuit of marketing millimeters of thickness, dual cameras and glass cases, qualities such as usability, build quality, battery life and maintainability are completely lost.

The most striking example could be Meizu products. As you know, their flagship line of smartphones is primarily intended for music lovers. But if the Meizu MX 4 Pro audio section can be called reference, then already 5 Pro, according to many, is deprived of sound. In the new model Meizu 6 Pro, the company completely abandoned the use of well-known components, replacing them with its own developments.

Time saves money


It is still easier on the smartphones of larger companies. Forced to chase the Chinese manufacturers, they began to release new lines of devices almost two times a year. Circuitry does not have time to give birth to fundamentally new solutions. The main thing is that the manufacturers of software do not have time for progress. As a result, many functions, such as the latest generation of processors, remain unfulfilled. And make it possible to use them much longer than before the appearance of a new flagship.

On the occasion, you can buy a LG V10 smartphone for only $ 300 Unlocked, without being tied to an operator. What is the reason for this discount? LG V20 will be on sale completely, and then it will be swept away from the counters. Is it worth overpaying? If there is a question of saving (and in a crisis it is always relevant), it is unlikely.



Of course, the new V20 is modular. But seriously - will you buy additional modules for the cost of a separate device in the secondary market? The rest of the generational change will be insensitive.

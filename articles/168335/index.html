Meet the LG EA9800 at the international exhibition CES 2013.
What is the most unusual TV you've ever seen? Well, except for the mythical 4k2k models, now for them there is neither content nor media for such content. Difficult to answer? Well then, we answer - the most unusual TV this year - LG EA9800.


Why?

Probably because this is the first OLED TV in the world with a curved screen surface. And it also uses a matrix with a WRGB subpixel arrangement, which has proven itself in the smartphones of Japanese manufacturers. Specially designed organic light-emitting diodes, on the basis of which we make all our OLED-panels, allow us not only to make TVs with an incredibly thin display, but also (thanks to a diode arrangement) allow our engineers to experiment with the shape of the screen. One of the results of such experiments was the LG EA9800.



The LG EA9800 is unique in its own way. This is the first TV in the world with such a set of characteristics, an unusual screen, stunning contrast and full support for the technology of forming a realistic 3D image - LG CINEMA 3D.

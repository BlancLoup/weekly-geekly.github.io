Hi, Megamind! This article is essentially a commentary to one of the ways described by semen_grinshtein in the publication Who Wants to Be a Millionaire: 7 Ways to Get Rich without Creating a Company . In one success story, it was about a millionaire teacher, which interested me as a university teacher and a school teacher.



The kindergarten teacher was able to secure additional income from the sale of their ideas. She sells her pedagogical skills through the Internet, that is, digital educational resources, to other teachers.

How can a teacher make a million?


I will begin with a description of my quite obvious sources of income, as an IT teacher: the main place of work is the university, part-time school teacher, on weekends tutoring, sometimes freelance programming. It turns out that this list can be expanded by sharing your educational resources for money. This is a really good idea that everyone benefits from - buyers-buyers, and pedagogues-sellers. In this article, I looked at ways to disseminate (preferably profitable for the author) educational resources without creating a company for your site.

In the world


TeachersPayTeachers site helped to earn a million dollars to a kindergarten teacher from the above article. For sales, the service takes a commission from the authors of the resources in 40% of the earned amount and an additional 30 cents for each transaction. For an annual payment of $ 59.95, you can purchase a Premium subscription, which will lower the commission to 15% of sales and completely eliminate the transaction fee.

An online market for teachers was created in April 2006 by a school teacher from New York named Paul Edelman (Paul Edelman), and for almost 10 years TpT has gained a base of 1.8 million resources and a community of 3.6 million teachers, which has already paid $ 175 million.

In runet


Today, there are several Russian-speaking online communities and one trading platform for teachers, as well as three official projects of the Ministry of Education and Science related to the distribution of educational resources:
  1. Social network of educators ( portal ) - for teachers of all levels of education, files can be downloaded in all formats. The teacher can create his own mini-site, place his developments on the mini-site and for 90 rubles receive an electronic certificate of publication in the electronic media and an electronic certificate of the creation of a personal website.
  2. Teachers Mutual Assistance Community ( portal ) - for kindergarten teachers and school teachers, files can be downloaded in ms office formats or as archives. The teacher can publish his development and order an electronic certificate of publication for 200 rubles or get it in hard copy by mail for 250 rubles.
  3. Educational resources and lesson plans ( trading floor ) - for teachers of all levels of education, files can be downloaded in formats doc, ppt, xls, pdf, zip, rar, etc. Authors can publish their resources and assign prices to them. For ensuring sales, the service takes a commission from the authors of 15% from the earned amount, but for 1500 rubles a year you can purchase a Premium subscription, which will lower the commission to 5% from sales. This is the Russian equivalent of TeachersPayTeachers.
  4. A single collection of digital educational resources ( portal ECOR ) - for school teachers, files can be downloaded in the formats of office documents, spreadsheets, presentations, there are interactive resources swf. Teachers cannot publish their resources; the range of resource providers is limited to educational institutions and commercial organizations.
  5. Federal Center for Information and Educational Resources ( FTSIOR portal ) - for teachers of general and vocational education, files can be downloaded in a special OMS format, you cannot publish your resource, but this project is still interesting.
  6. A single window of access to educational resources ( portal ) - for students of general and vocational education, links to sites with educational information are collected and electronic textbooks can be downloaded in PDF format. You can add your own resource, for which you must register.

There is no monetary compensation to the teacher for publishing the resource on the portals of the Ministry of Education.

5. Federal Center for Information and Educational Resources


FTSIOR provides resources in the OMI format from only a certain circle of 10 suppliers (2 state universities and 8 commercial enterprises). Any wishing teacher is not able to independently publish their resources.

Open Educational Modular Multimedia Systems (OMS)
Open educational modular multimedia systems (OMS) are an electronic educational resource of modular architecture. To work with the OMS electronic training modules, you need to download from the portal and install special software on your local computer - the resource player.

Software for viewing resources provides storage, search, selection and playback of Electronic Learning Modules (EUM):
  • OMC Player - designed to play EUM. The player provides the ability to search EUM among the modules located in the local storage. Local storage is a dedicated folder on the user's computer, the path to which is set when installing the OMC software components.
  • OMS User Organizer - provides loading of EEM from the central storage of the FCEAR to the user's local storage, as well as storage of selected modules in the user's workplace. The organizer allows you to play and view the EMP metadata stored in the local storage.


FTSIOR technology platform
The hardware complex FTSIOR is built on IBM solutions and technologies.

Computing facilities center provide high-performance IBM servers. Efficient storage of various types of electronic educational resources is carried out using Open Source projects. The software architecture of FCEIOR is implemented on JavaServlets, Java Server Pages technologies using projects of the Apache Software Foundation (Tomcat, Lucene, Commons). MySQL is used as a DBMS for application systems. Application Development - Java 2, Enterprise Edition Platform.

City infrastructure for Nokia Lumia 920

In Moscow, the first chain of coffee shops equipped the tables of its establishments with Qi wireless charging modules. This network has become Red Espresso Bar. Now visitors to its establishments, if their smartphones support wireless charging using this protocol, will be able to recharge their devices just by coming to the cafe and putting the smartphone on a special stand on each table. Of course, ideally, the wireless charging modules should be built into the tabletops themselves, but I think it will take no more than a year for the institutions to appear in which there will be tables with such tabletops.





The fact that Nokia Lumia 920 is the most innovative device is not just a marketing slogan, it is the definition of a whole set of technological solutions implemented in the device, but not currently having a full-fledged infrastructure for use. Wireless charging is just one of the elements of the urban infrastructure that this device is designed for.

Another important element is the module for LTE communication. As a user of Beeline who is not in a hurry to introduce LTE for my subscribers, I have not yet managed to buy a MegaFon SIM card in order to test the present, still embryonic state of LTE communication in Moscow. However, I want to note that the 3G Lumia 920 works much faster than the N9.

Well, the third element of the urban infrastructure for which the Lumia 920 is designed is some kind of media cafe (or PureView-cafe), where in one configuration or another an amateur movie-video will be shown, among other things, shot at the Lumia 920 itself, in the camera which for the first time in the smartphone segment implemented optical stabilization.

These three elements, present Lumia 920 provoke the development of urban infrastructure in the near future. And it is very pleasant that the first step towards this infrastructure has already been taken.

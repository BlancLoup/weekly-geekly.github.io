How Mail.Ru Group Kills AAA-Class MMO Games

A bit of background


In the market of the gaming industry in the field of MMORPG (mass online games with elements of role-playing gameplay) in recent years there has been an obvious crisis of the genre. Most MMORPGs that have entered the market in the past two years have failed (to put it mildly) and were in demand by a small number of users. On this bleak background for gamers all over the world, a ray of hope flickered lonely - the ArcheAge (AA) project, which the South Korean company XLGames (XLG) undertook to develop under the leadership of Jake Song , the creator of Lineage II MMORPG, which is legendary for all generations of gamers of the whole world.

The South Korean release of the project showed to all gamers that the project really has considerable potential, as it offers the player a huge choice of possibilities and various actions: from simple gathering of resources and growing beets in the fields, to large-scale battles on land and at sea.

In January 2013, it became known that the Russian-language version of AA for Russia and the CIS countries was undertaken to localize the Russian company Mail.Ru Group. This news caused an ambiguous reaction among the ru-community: on the one hand, it‚Äôs a joy that in the massively expected project they will soon be able to play in Russian, on the other hand, there are concerns: the localization company has won a bad game among gamers reputation of a company that does not reckon with the interests of the community and is ready for the sake of gaining any unpopular actions. Representatives of the company were quick to appease the negative moods of the community, vowing to make every effort to localize the AA project to fully meet the interests of the gaming audience, as they understand the full scale and significance of the project for the gaming audience.

What happened in the end

The scheme is as follows: rubles - crystals - arches (the new main game currency created by the Mail.Ru Group craftsmen).
All (!) Of the main game actions for trading, or payment, from now on, players were offered to exercise through arches, and in fact for the real currency of the Russian Federation - rubles. Actions and counteractions


Ru-community (to the best of its abilities and capabilities) responded with a decisive struggle for its obviously crudely trampled interests.

In a short time, in just a few days, the petition has already collected more than 8,000 signatures from both Russian gamers and gamers from around the world.

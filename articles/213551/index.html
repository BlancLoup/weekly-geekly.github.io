Design, construction, wearing comfort

When I first met, Audio-Technica ANC7b headphones did not make any vivid impressions on me. Austere design due to the use of plastic materials in the case, the classic shape with arc fastening, small creaks when bending the headphones and turning the cups. The latter have two degrees of freedom: a rotation of 90 degrees on the vertical axis and 45 on the horizontal.

The headband size adjustment mechanism has 15 positions in 3 mm increments. There are no designations, so it is assumed that the user must once set the desired size or each time be guided by clicks that publish guides when resizing.

Headphone cups are of medium size (relative to other overhead monitors). By tradition, the manufacturer‚Äôs logo, headphone model and ‚Äúleft-right‚Äù marking are located on the outside.

The microphones at the bottom of the cups are part of the active noise cancellation system. This also brought the battery compartment, which is hidden behind the false panel. Any AAA batteries with voltage up to 1.5 V.

On the left "ear", in addition to the elements already mentioned, there is an ACP system switch. The activation of the latter is signaled by a bright blue highlight LED. There is also a connector for connecting a 3.5 mm audio cable, but it is worth noting that a plug with a larger diameter may not fit into this port.

By the way, on the axis connecting the right cup with the headband there is a small relief point, thanks to which you can blindly determine where the right and where the left "ear."

The ear cushions are medium in size, moderately resilient, as well as the headband covered with leatherette. Headphones tightly wrap their heads, but it does not cause any inconvenience.

As for my personal impressions about convenience, I write these lines after 5 (and maybe even more) hours of being in headphones. Overboard the winter, in a room around 20 degrees - ears in comfort. ‚ñê Device active noise reduction system

And what exactly is the noise reduction system? First you need to understand that noise reduction is both active and passive . The latter is mainly the prerogative of closed monitor headphones. Cases of cups of such devices do not contain any openings from the outside, and the walls of such headphones are usually quite massive and the material from which they are made often has the ability to dampen sound vibrations. Headphones with the best passive noise cancellation have indicators of 35-37 dB.

Active noise cancellation, in turn, requires the introduction of electronics into the headphones. The principle of operation of this system is quite simple to understand, even if you skipped physics lessons at school. Sound is a physical phenomenon that represents the propagation of elastic waves in a particular medium. More precisely, the sound is a wave. The music, the roar of the jackhammer, the cries of the baby - all these are sound waves that we encounter everywhere. One of the main phenomena inherent in the waves is interference.
Wave interference - the mutual increase or decrease of the resulting amplitude of two or more coherent waves when they are superimposed on each other. (c) Wikipedia
But how does the active noise cancellation system depend on the interference phenomenon? - And so that this method of noise reduction is based on interference! If the waves come to the point of the medium (occur) in antiphase, then the displacement of the particles will be multidirectional, which leads to a decrease in the amplitude of oscillations, i.e. sound vibrations coming from the outside are extinguished. Due to limitations (possibly temporal, because progress does not stand still), the ‚Äúshumodava‚Äù imposed by internal electronics cannot fully compensate external noise, unfortunately, is not possible.

This is how the ACP system works. Special microphones, which are located in the headphone cups, pick up external sounds, and the built-in electronics invert them and add to the music being played, thereby removing external noises.

Recently, it is fashionable to rate devices for their maintainability, in order to keep up with the trends, we will prepare headphones, and at the same time we will see what kind of magic is hidden in the body of these. Peel off ear cushions:

Under a thin foam filter (not in the photo) there is an ACP microphone and a dynamic radiator. For surround sound, the headphone cups are filled with foam.

Once again we see that the headphone case is entirely made of plastic. On the left cup there are three screws under the screwdriver CR-VT8 - they hold the false-panel under which the electronics of the active noise cancellation system is hidden.

Further, more: the oval-shaped printed circuit board (by clicking on the image below - full size) occupies almost all the internal space of the left cup. Dirt around the audio jack slightly spoils the impression, but in general everything is fine.

On the reverse side are two single electrolytic capacitors and a standard 3.5 mm TRS connector.

In the production of these headphones, serial components were used, for example, the octopus chip on the right side of the board is the MAX660, a voltage inverter, which can serve both as a source of negative voltage and as a boost converter for the amplifier. In the upper left corner - ST V324 (14 pins) - a four-channel amplifier with a THD (nonlinear distortion coefficient) of not more than 0.01%. I also advise you to follow the shutdown of noise reduction, because no auto-off function was detected.

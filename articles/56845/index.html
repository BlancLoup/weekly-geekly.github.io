Neurosurgeons from Brooklyn successfully cleared the memory using drugs


Researchers at SUNY Downstate Medical Center succeeded in selective memory cleaning in rats using a mysterious drug that they call ZIP. In the course of the experiments, mice that were taught to avoid electrified areas of the cell were given a ZIP drug and they forgot about these electrified areas and that they should be avoided. Scientists have also successfully forced the rats to forget about their aversion to the object that hurt them. Dr. Todd Sacktor, the project manager, believes that one day these drugs will be used by people and can improve memory. The details about this drug and how it works are still unknown.

In the shelf "electronic paper" a serious replenishment

It's probably no secret for anyone that at the moment there is only one e-ink company with their monochrome displays on the e-paper market.

However, the good news came the other day.

The Taiwan contract manufacturer Delta Electronics intends to soon master the release of devices for reading electronic books.

According to the company, closer to the end of the second quarter, the production of two readers should begin: one of them will receive a color display with a diagonal of 13.1 inches, the second - a monochrome 8.1-inch. Both gadgets will support touch control.

Unlike most modern readers , which are equipped with panels based on the technology of electronic ink E-ink Corporation, Delta Electronics devices will use displays based on electronic paper of the Japanese corporation Bridgestone . The Bridgestone technique allows you to create screens with relatively low power consumption and a fairly high rendering speed, which is enough to work with multimedia materials. Also the display is also flexible.

monochrome option


Bridgestone also seriously refined the color version of its display and the screen update now takes 0.8 seconds, unlike previous Bridgestone designs, which gave a result of 10-15 seconds. What is undoubtedly a breakthrough.

Bridgestone paper consists of electronic bulk materials Bridgestone QR-LPD with a color filter and uses electromagnetic induction input with a pen.

color option




Let's hope that Delta Electronics and Bridgestone will succeed and the competition will finally appear on the market of electronic readers. What undoubtedly should reduce the price of end devices. Congratulations to all, the future has become a little bit closer.

The company also has ambitious plans, just look at their roadmap.

It is clear that this year the company is going to make a qualitative leap.

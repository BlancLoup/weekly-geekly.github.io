Google Nexus One is not likely to get updates to Android 4.0

Hugo Barra, head of product management at Google, said in an interview with the Telegraph that Google Nexus One is too old a phone for Android 4.0, so only Google Nexus S will be updated.

Apparently, this is a bright case of the fact that technology has again become a victim of management and marketing considerations. Given the fact that Google Nexus S is slightly different from the first nexus, such statements look at least strange. Apparently, the owners of the device have to rely on the commands of CyanogenMod and MIUI, which will begin to develop the firmware immediately after the release of the source codes of the Android 4.0 system. Formally, already now some developers began to rivet the firmware on the ICS, but based on the analysis of the released SDK, but the guys from the CM and MIUI teams want to release high-quality firmware.
Karma Google after such a statement fell below the baseboard. If Google still hopes to save its face corporation good , then let them change their mind. By the way, the hypocrisy of the corporation is confirmed by statements that theoretically any device that works with Android 2.3 can be updated with ICS. Nexus One works with 2.3.7, so theoretically it can work with Android 4.0.

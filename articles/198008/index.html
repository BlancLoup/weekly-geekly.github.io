In short, we realized that there are two options for further work with the project:
1) Offline acceleration
if the project goes offline, then I fly to Moscow with my director, we get $ 25K, we hang out there for 3 months, I give 7% of the company, we are taught how to develop the product to the desired indicators, and help us achieve these indicators.

From this point on, it became clear that in order to participate in the offline accelerator, you must either agree on a change of conditions, or go online.

I fly to Moscow

A few days later an employee from IIDF flew to Tomsk and on the recommendation of the same kind people Hitrusha came to our office. In a pleasant conversation, she spoke about the purely business objectives of the foundation. And most importantly, it became clear that it is MANDATORY to fly to Moscow to present the project to the experts, who will select 40 projects for the offline accelerator. It turned out that offline is a kind of privilege.
They asked to come with a product manager, but from Siberia a round-trip ticket is 13-14 thousand rubles. I flew alone.




And as it turned out later, one of the team is enough. Interestingly, the FRIA realizes that Russia has not ended behind the Moscow Ring Road, and from Vladivostok it is extremely expensive to fly, and where does the money for these flights from an ordinary startup come from?

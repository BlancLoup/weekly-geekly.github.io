2. Video surveillance and media content (high bandwidth required)

HDTV content brings tangible revenues, but with the increase in picture quality, tangible problems arise - high throughput of the entire infrastructure and huge disk space are required (don't forget about the archives).
Note that such tools as caching and tiring, which have proven themselves in solving traditional problems, do not work for streaming video.

The specifics of working with such data can be described as follows:

‚Ä¢ High bandwidth: HDTV: 8-30 Mbit / s, by 2015. UHDTV - 24 Gbit / s
‚Ä¢ Huge amount of data: 1 hour of uncompressed UHDTV video - 25TB !!!
‚Ä¢ Multiple distributed access to real-time data for all types of OS - for processing video material.

In addition, when using HD camcorders of resolution and long-term storage of video archives, there are several significant limitations and operating conditions. For example, if we use disk space collected on the basis of a RAID controller with internal server disks, we have several points of failure, difficulty of expanding disk space, difficulties with the rapid replacement of failed equipment, etc.

At the same time, the nature of the load is such that the use of universal RAID controllers does not allow obtaining the maximum possible performance even from a large number of hard drives. Here the described storage system can come to the rescue.

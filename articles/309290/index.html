Last Friday, September 2, a conference for data centers took place in Kiev, 90% of the attention was paid to the clouds and everything connected with them. Classical cloud deployment scenarios - when cloud-based solutions deploy their services and resources for the purpose of resale to external customers or to provide services for internal customers. As an example - startups - test sites for further growth. There are no capital costs here - they took, used, tested, further or expanded or removed those resources that are no longer needed.

Creating a backup site for the company. Those tasks that do not allow you to fully deploy in the clouds for some reason, or, most often, for security reasons, very often use cloud infrastructure as a platform for duplicating or backing up your data - to have hot copies.

Fight peak loads ‚Äî you can dynamically expand or reduce on-demand / schedule computing power: days, hours, periods, etc.

Complete replacement of the physical infrastructure - yes, now, many people look skeptically at the clouds for various reasons, but everything is moving towards virtualization, and most likely in the future, service providers will be engaged in hardware.

What worries customers about choosing cloud solutions? What questions are most asked? Most often, customers are worried about the security and complexity of migration (readiness of the infrastructure for migration), how many simple services there will be, etc. Scalability and performance - as far as it will be available in the future, whether they have enough scale of these solutions in the future. And whether the cloud will work no worse than their physical server is now.

Difficulties in assessing financial performance. The issue of security when moving to the cloud is rather a matter of trust to the provider. The client himself must be concerned about their security and data backup - encryption of important data.
Fault tolerance and protection against DDoS attacks ‚Äî by their structure, cloud solutions already carry the necessary elements for duplication and replication of data. There is a single point of failure already at the level of iron - the provider should have all this if the cloud provider approaches the cloud structure seriously and professionally. In respect of DDoS attacks, the cloud provider already has protection, or this protection is targeted when a client is attacked by a service.

The separation of data is dictated by the cloud options that the client chooses, this is achieved both by using the SLA and by using technology that alone guarantees a certain level of separation and security. The clouds in many ways simplified the life of the atishnik, who do not need to wrestle with and be constantly to some extent the architects of the network. How to get all the benefits of cloud technology?


This may seem obvious, but many providers forget about it. A very important feature of all cloud projects - each project is considered individually, the cloud is not a panacea for everyone. You might think that ‚Äúeveryone knows this anyway,‚Äù but often providers do not hear what the client says and do not want to see what this particular client wants. Think through a competent process of migration to the cloud. If necessary, add a testing phase, add all the necessary resources - thus helping the client to get acquainted and make friends with cloud technologies.

We all know about the concept of a public cloud and a private cloud. They are built on the same technology, on the same structure, but there are some important distinctive features. A private cloud is its main feature - it is isolated from other clients at the provider. This model is always recommended for those companies where data is particularly careful - financial databases, etc. In the private cloud, the client gets maximum control over their resources and maximum security.



In the public cloud, resources are used by many clients. Of course, cloud load balancers are involved here, which automatically allocate resources, which helps the provider to fully use their resources. Who uses public clouds? As a rule, these are separate divisions of enterprises, or individual employees, separate applications for divisions ‚Äî which are not critical ‚Äî they are secondary. Then the question is: why are public clouds so popular? They have obvious advantages - a very affordable price - it‚Äôs very quick and easy to deploy services in public clouds - you can very effectively convert capital costs into operational ones. Using public clouds, you can quickly regulate the pool of resources needed by different departments according to different criteria - season / non-season, time of day, season, etc., so that needs and capabilities match. This increases the efficiency and computing resources and financial.

Hybrid cloud Why are they, according to some sources, the most popular? They allow a very thoughtful and balanced distribution of business units ‚Äî individual services, individual employees, and data on different types of clouds ‚Äî depending on the criticality and relevance. Plus, this model allows you to better use financial resources - as a private cloud, whatever one may say, is more expensive, with all its advantages.

A serious lack of a public cloud - the degree of security and control is noticeably lower in comparison with a private cloud.



What do our customers pay most attention to when they start working in the cloud?


First of all, business pays attention to the unprecedented flexibility of this technology. And, interestingly, the very notion of flexibility used to be abstract, since it used to be difficult to calculate the correspondence of resources, business processes and capital investments. And the process was long and not very accurate. Now, having a pool of virtual resources - and using only a few numbers, it is possible to calculate the necessary resources not only up to the department, but also for each workplace.

Efficiency. As already mentioned, the customer does not need to know where the resources are located, he wants to know only that his infrastructure is functioning properly. Since the company pays for only those resources that are needed, thus achieving maximum efficiency. Investment attractiveness - there is always an emphasis on "cost of ownership" - the clouds significantly reduce the cost of ownership of infrastructure, according to some studies to tens of percent.

Controllability. This is one of the main priorities for many customers - the degree of consolidation of resources is maximum if the company has moved to the cloud. Infrastructure management is sometimes possible by one person from anywhere in the world. In addition, such an administrator will be able to easily recover not only data, but virtual machines - with the proper organization of archiving.

An interesting fact that is observed abroad, with the introduction of cloud technologies - increases the efficiency of labor. As resources and time are clearly limited, the efficiency of working time is growing.

As for reliability. This is an important point that we always talk about with our customers. At its core, the cloud is fundamentally different from the solutions we are used to. All nodes, all communication channels are duplicated, at least according to the n + 1 scheme. This allows customers to absolutely not worry about physical security and fault tolerance solutions. The client takes care of higher levels of abstraction - levels of OS, settings, applications, data. Everything else is already on the shoulders of the cloud provider.

Availability. This is especially for enterprises whose geographically branched structure - this technology allows you to consolidate data in one place - and all remote employees and branches can use the data centrally - and this is already embedded in the technology itself, and the company simply uses it.

Your data is the most valuable thing you have. You yourself must be concerned about their security - you need to encrypt the channels and data. This seems trivial, but many clients do not do this, we suggest what and how - then everything becomes good, but initially it is necessary not to miss this question.



It is always necessary to provide for the correct archiving of data. In the cloud, it is convenient to archive not only data, workstations, but also entire virtual machines. At the same time, you can use for this not only local storage facilities that are located in the same cloud, but also remote sites - which will make your infrastructure not only fault-tolerant, but also disaster-proof for minimal capital investments.

Free migration. Very often the company has the need to transfer data. It is recommended to choose those providers that offer free data transfer. There are such providers, you do not need to pay for each movement, you just need to find the right provider.

A few words about the tariffs. Unfortunately, there are still providers that charge and limit traffic. Obvious advice, look for those providers who offer unlimited tariffs.

Bandwidth inside the cloud - this item is very important from a technological point of view. This is a scrupulous moment, many suppliers are reluctant to talk about this topic. They do not want to reveal the structure of their network. But if you look at the essence of the issue, then it depends on how much quality service you buy from your provider. It depends a lot on the speed inside the cloud. How are the nodes connected? at what speeds? What protocols? Services of many enterprises may well work on 1 gigabit, but you need to consider that most providers use 10G, and only the most advanced build their clouds on 40G, or InfiniBand 56G. It is recommended to look for such providers in order to get advanced infrastructure in the cloud without additional investment.



The second difficult moment is the core. We all know that rented and virtual cores in the cloud can be very different - there are different generations of processors, different frequencies of buses, different memory speeds. Feel free to ask questions to providers - what kernels do they offer you, you should know what you pay for.

A few words about the migration process. If we roughly divide providers, that is, the part that offer their services "as they are" - take what we have, use it, but how we do not care. But it is better to look for that provider who will not only be your seller, but will be your partner and consultant you can rely on, and he will not leave you in trouble.

Some clients have branches whose applications are not supported in a virtual environment, so it‚Äôs better to work with those providers who can lend you physical equipment as well. This complexity - it can help you very much - you can get a full range of services from one point, which also saves money.

Security data centers. Too scrupulous moment, considering the various events in this area, we recommend choosing those providers who not only guarantee the safety of data, but also the physical safety of your equipment, which can protect from a certain situation in the country, and from some actions of regulatory bodies.

We can not ignore the fact that our legislation does not regulate the withdrawal of equipment and customer data from data centers. Severe violations and seizures have occurred, unfortunately, and this situation will continue until legislation is passed. When choosing a provider, also study the legal aspects of the country where the provider stores your data.

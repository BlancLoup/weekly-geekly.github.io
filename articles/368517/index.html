The human factor in online trading systems or when Escrow from AliExpress does not work

Quite a few types of fraud are developed in online stores, but they are built on the carelessness and ignorance of buyers. As a rule, all these schemes are broken into dust with timely appeal to arbitration.
However, the human factor can ruin any good thing. In this essay, I will share the tragedy that has developed around my five-dollar cable, ordered on AliExpress back in June, as a scheme built on the negligence of arbitration. In the end, Skype has already been run over, than AliExpress is worse ?!


No, this is not a complaint. Of course, the loss of $ 5 has knocked me down, my brain has boiled over and claimed justice, but I resisted, rehabilitated and am already learning to live with it further.

In order not to torment, I will immediately explain the essence of the scheme.
a) the seller sends the goods and gives a non-working track.
b) the buyer does not immediately begin to worry, because anything can happen.
c) the buyer does not receive the goods in the calculated period, requires to give the correct number, the seller dynamite.
d) the buyer initiates a dispute, the arbitration obliges the seller to give the correct track number.
d) The seller gives the left track number, from someone else's parcel to your city and received by another person. The weak point is that the AliExpress arbitration is sufficient the fact of having a track and receiving a parcel. No other factors - the place and date of departure, the weight of the parcel, the name of the recipient - are not taken into account by the arbitration. The only evidence that the arbitration requires in accordance with the screenshot above is an official document from the post office that you did not receive the parcel :) Of course, no such post will be given in any country, the dispute ends with the seller‚Äôs victory, the curtain!

Why does this work? I can assume the following. Ali, like all other similar organizations, has auto-sorting of tickets. Cheap tickets get to low-skilled staff, as a rule, trainees or outsourcing companies in general on a contract. These people are the most loaded, they have the minimum allowed time to process the ticket, and the qualification is so-so. They do not have time to punch the track, check the parameters, + surely there is a clear and stupid instruction where to poke the mouse. Initiative is punishable.
In my lifetime, a similar situation was with a single cellular operator that supported small business on an outsource. Any new operator reached the company with a delay and customers often received incorrect advice. And that is why this situation seems to me exactly a hole in the system.

Huawei Ascend P6: a true story of the creation and detailed study of the thinnest Chinese smartphone in the world

In most cases, we can not restore the history of the creation of smartphones. Everything happens in the depths of corporations, dozens or even hundreds of people work on each model, and therefore it is extremely difficult to identify the logic of decision making. The Huawei Ascend P6, which came to review, was also a dark horse until it was completely disassembled. What we saw inside allowed us with the master Levchenko to present and record the history of the emergence of this complex apparatus.



So, the spring of 2012, the center of Chinese Shenzhen, the headquarters of Huawei.

Richard Yu, head of Huawei Devices, stood by the huge, full-length window of his office on the 36th floor and watched the sun go down. He was, as usual, dressed in a jacket of protective color. In the hands of Richard was a pipe. At the round table, eighteen men in black suits sat tensely silent.

Richard stopped looking at the red circle of the sun and turned to the crowd.

The office darkened sharply. The silence has become frightening.

- Tim brought our new smartphones and tablets. In his opinion, they are suspiciously similar to the iPhone and iPad.

All eighteen men simultaneously hit the table with their right hand. One of them hissed: "Oh, big-eyed demons!" Silence fell again.


Richard Yu in official vestments

- Tim said he would sue all the money to the last yuan. I reminded him that at one time he served in the army with half of the Chinese General Staff. And that factories where Apple products are collected can suddenly and for a long time become centers of military exercises. And even nuclear tests. In response, Tim calmed down a bit and agreed not to sue until the next generation of Huawei products. And if it will be at least slightly different from Apple products, Tim will not recall the horde of his insatiable lawyers from the courts with Samsung.

A general exhalation of relief passed over the table.

- I have a question: can our designers come up with something unlike Apple? We spent so much energy on the development of their ability to cloning and weaning from independent creativity. Will the reverse process start? Or should we prepare for war?

Again a heavy pause. Richard Yu walked around the table in circles. All those who were sitting saw him step on a fluffy carpet with his coarse, well-worn crooked boots.

A middle-aged man sitting in a far corner rose. His stern face was scarred and his right hand lacked two fingers.

- Do not worry, Comrade Yu! As Mao said, there is power in the crowd. Of course, some of the old people will find it difficult to go beyond the limits provided by the caliper. But the youth will help them. And we look. We have eighty thousand designers. Everything will be fine, Comrade Yu!

- Oh no! Again! Again this nightmare! I can't live with this anymore! Untie me from the table, I want to jump out the window!

About smartphone

Huawei Ascend P6 presented in June, as the thinnest smartphone in the world. The thickness of its body is only 6.18 mm. Honestly, I don‚Äôt even know if someone has managed to do something more subtle or not. In general, this craving to be the most-most even starts to irritate us somewhat, and, of course, irrespective of Huawei. The best camera among the phones, the lightest weight among the representatives of the average price range, the most exorbitant price among the button phones (hello, Nokia!) And so on. I understand that manufacturers want to ensure as many publications as possible in the press, which, by virtue of its laziness, will pay little attention to the not-very-most apparatus. But one thing is a brief rattle in the news, and the other is real sales. Marketers, of course, know better, but the absolute majority of people are body thickness to the bulb. A couple of millimeters there, a couple here - who do they care? Here is the battery life - it's interesting.



However, we will not get ahead. Let's look at the Ascend P6, just like a smartphone claiming a certain piece of market pie, not a record holder in chicken weight.

To study, I chose a copy of a courageous pink color. When you get one in public, men look slightly puzzled, and on the face of girls of all ages you can read complete delight. Still, the guy with the pink iPhone! The similarities with Apple products are impressive. If you put an iPhone nearby, you can't mix Ascend P6 with it, of course. But in the absence of the original, but from some distance, you begin to doubt. It‚Äôs nice, of course, that the front camera was placed on the side of the speaker, not above it, and the main flash was not on the side, but on the bottom. But the source of inspiration for designers is read unequivocally.



Some disharmony in the design brings a rounded bottom. There is a complete feeling that they added it at the last moment so that some lawyer from California would not stand. Another version is purely technological limitations, which do not allow to achieve the required build quality and structural rigidity at an affordable price.


Unique photo! In this perspective, Huawei Ascend P6 can not be confused with the iPhone!

In any case, the whole body is metal, of winged metal, and the bottom cap is plastic. Build quality is not perfect. Aluminum is aluminum, no squeaks and backlash to you, but the gaps on the right and left are slightly different. And with a plastic cap docking is not perfect.



The micro-USB connector is located on the top face, and not in the center, not on the side, but somewhere at the end of the second third. Such a feeling that it somehow turned out on its own, well, and decided not to strain. All control buttons and slots are located on the right. There, and Power, and volume, and jacks for SIM cards and memory cards.



Honestly, when I saw a pair of iPhone-specific slides with a hole for a clip, I thought that the device supports two SIM cards. But no! One of the slots for microSD, and go guess what - exactly. To facilitate guessing games, the Original Clip Replacement is built into the phone. It is pulled out of the Ascend P6 with difficulty, and in field conditions it is easy to drop. But in general, of course, innovation is very violent. When Master Levchenko and I understood the purpose of this small thing, we were silent for a minute in amazement.


At first I thought that we had a holder for the lace, like on the last generation iPod touch. But no!


Super tool

The diagonal of the Ascend P6 screen is 4.7 inches, the matrix resolution is 1280x720 pixels. No records, all within reason. The type of matrix is ‚Äã‚ÄãIPS, but the picture does not please with some special richness of shades, and the illumination uniformity is not perfect. The picture looks slightly "cartoon", coarse. When the viewing angle changes, the tones remain the same, but their saturation is significantly reduced. In general, from which side do not look at the screen, you will see a pretty strong middling, but no more.

Like other great companies, Huawei is trying to use in its SoC smartphones. In this case, this is the Huawei HiSilicon K3V2E with four cores and a peak frequency of 1.5 GHz. The graphics core, however, is not its own - it was made by a little-known (for now?) Company Vivante. The processor is made on a 40-nanometer process technology, and this is not very good news. In fact, we are talking about the generation before the production lines, because Samsung, Apple and Qualcomm have long ago mastered 28 nm, and even in the last generation they had 32 nm.



This is not good news. Firstly, the system is not very fast - in synthetic benchmarks it is significantly inferior to previous generation smartphones, like the Samsung Galaxy S III or HTC One X. Secondly, the device heats up very much, both from the back and from the front. Seriously, this is the first case in my memory when the smartphone screen becomes hot while playing a game. The hand, of course, will not burn, but the feeling is strange. Fortunately, most of the time SoC operates at 200 MHz. It is interesting that, despite the 200-MHz step, the processor has only two main operating frequencies - 200 MHz and 1.2 GHz. All others are rarely used. And the strongest impression was made by the availability of operation at 1399 MHz and 1400 MHz. One megahertz, it turns out, and in our time solves a lot.

The stated amount of RAM is 2 GB, but all diagnostic programs report the presence of only 1.69 GB. Whether the rest is overclocking the graphics accelerator, or the chips have been laid down this way, but the fact remains. However, this volume is quite enough. The user also has about 5 GB of onboard flash memory, and for everything else there is a microSD.



I would be glad to say something good about the camera, but, alas, I cannot. The point, of course, is not the resolution of the matrix, because 8 megapixels is enough for a smartphone. It was the quality of the pictures that upset me. No working out of details, narrow dynamic range, loud noises that appear even on a sunny autumn day, fierce shag ... Even Fly Phoenix doesn‚Äôt claim anything much better, but Ascend P6 is the flagship in the line of the company that ranks third in the world in sales smartphones. In response to claims to the main camera in Huawei, they say: "But then we have a front resolution of 5 megapixels!" The argument is, of course, powerful. The only pity is that my face doesn‚Äôt get better over the years, and even 2 megapixels are a bit too much for her.

The radio module is stable and good. For mobile networks, the smartphone keeps tight, connects to WiFi at speeds up to 150 Mbps. LTE, however, does not support, but even the top Samsung SoCs have not really learned yet. Excusable.

The battery capacity, despite the thin case, is quite decent - 2000 mAh. The operating time depends on the usage scenario very much. If the device is in your pocket, and you are talking mostly on a Bluetooth-headset, the charge will last for two days. And even three. If you constantly look at the screen and launch some applications, you will be asked to charge it at seven in the evening. Unfortunately, the built-in means of informing about battery operation write some kind of nonsense, and you cannot trust them. Therefore, I rely solely on my own observations.

About software

By turning the Android into Huawei, they approached very thoroughly. If you do not know that this is the platform of Google, you can first and begin to doubt. Were not too lazy to not only redraw almost all icons again, but also to get rid of the submenu with applications. Is it as usual? There are desktops, there is a dump of applications, from which the best can be pulled up. There is no dump here. All application shortcuts immediately fall on desktops, of which the default is four pieces. To restore order, you can create folders and put in them. If all does not fit, the number of desktops can be increased.




There are plenty of different themes for registration, and you can both install the official ones already registered in the smartphone and download home-made products from the Internet. Both those and others look surprisingly neat.



By default, all standard Google tools, the Polaris Office 4.0 office suite, and several proprietary applications, such as backup utilities, are preinstalled. Some kind of megapriblud, which are famous for the Korean flagship, is not here. Everything is very concise and to the point. And this is good.

The video player is not very happy with the omnivorous: like the analog in the Sony smartphone, it reproduces almost any picture, but ignores sound tracks. All this is quickly and easily treated, and yet leaves a feeling of flaw.

Unfortunately, the device cannot boast of excellent stability. Whether in the processor case, or Android 4.2.2 sharpened too hard, but applications periodically freeze, and those of them that have not hung anywhere and never. It seems like the Screen Test utility, which does not require any special permissions to work at all.

Processors Intel Xeon E3-1500 v5 - a new word in video streaming

The market of video streaming and online services is rapidly developing, which leads to the transformation of video content. To ensure fast and high-quality shooting FHD and 4K requires high computational power. You can improve performance with user-programmable FPGA, ASIC or GPU-specific integrated circuits. In line with this, Intel has released a series of processors E3-1500 v5, based on Intel Quick Sync and focused on video streaming. These are quad-core Skylake CPUs that support Iris Pro graphics with 72 compute units and enhanced 128 MB built-in DRAM.

These processors promise to significantly improve performance compared to the previous generation Broadwell at 65 watts. For HEVC, with a threshold of up to 45 W, two streams recoding HEVC into HEVC 4K30.

Especially for users who want to increase the density of calculations, Visual Compute Accelerator maps have been developed. They can be added to any system similar to a GPU or MIC, making the platform independent. The use of such cards is typical for platforms with E5 / E7.

New processors E3-1500 v5 are aimed at high density calculations. With Intel Quick Sync integrated in the processor, this is a HEVC streaming solution with new features.

At the top of the stack are the E5 and E7 family processors. They use multiple processors in a single system and focus exclusively on high quality video. They can be bundled with Visual Compute Accelerators (when necessary).

The rest of the products in question are concentrated around virtualization. When a virtual server uses the same hardware between several clients / users, the method of sharing CPU time is used. Depending on the capabilities of the hypervisor and basic equipment, there are restrictions on the use of software by all users simultaneously. Generation Skylake plus Iris Pro, based on the Xeon E3-1500 v5, supports the new Graphics Virtualization Technology (GVT) under Citrix XenServer 7.0.

Xeons with Iris Pro support two GVT modes: GVT-a and GVT-d. The first allows API virtualization in such a way that users can take advantage of the same software package on the same hardware. The mode is popular with companies using a specific application or web service when connecting different clients to the same one.

The second mode provides direct control over all resources for a single user. As a result, new features are added for the regulation of time quantization. As a result, one user will, in fact, own 100% of the processor time. In the case of immediate processing of power requests, specific virtual machines can raise the priority of the hypervisor. Also, other virtual machines can use less resources and expand theirs.

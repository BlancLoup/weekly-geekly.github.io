W3C Adopts Microsoft's Tracking Protection Technology for Standardization



As you probably already know, Tracking Protection has been added to Internet Explorer 9 RC, which allows you to prohibit tracking user activity on the Internet by third-party resources through various ways of adding content to websites.



This technology was submitted to the W3C for standardization, as an open technology to protect the privacy of the user. And so, today the IE developers blog and interoperability @ microsoft blog have announcements that the W3C has adopted technology for standardization.

This means that soon the protection of the privacy of users on the Internet will receive an open standard, which will allow all browsers to implement it and will offer users a single protection mechanism.

You can read the Tracking Protection standardization request on this page . The draft standard itself is located at this address . Work on it has just begun and will continue.

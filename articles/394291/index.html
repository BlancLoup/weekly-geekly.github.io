Entrepreneur collects money for reproduction of events 9/11


Paul Salelo

American-born businessman Paul Salo [Paul Salo], currently living in Thailand, is raising money for a unique experiment. He wants to recreate the events of September 11, 2001 , sending a Boeing-747 to a skyscraper.

According to the plan of the businessman , it is necessary to buy a retired aircraft, similar in size to the 747, to fill it with fuel and on autopilot to push it with a skyscraper, which is just going to demolish. The experiment will show whether the plane is capable of tearing down a skyscraper at the root, or those who claim that 9/11 was a great performance are right.

According to the official version of the events of September 11, 2001, al Qaeda terrorists seized four passenger planes, two of which crashed into the towers of the World Trade Center in New York, which led to their complete collapse (other buildings of the WTC complex also collapsed). The third plane crashed into the Pentagon building, the fourth fell into the field of Pennsylvania, after the passengers tried to take the plane from the terrorists.



The tragedy led to the adoption in the United States of the " Patriotic Act " a month after the event. According to this law, the government and the police were given very broad powers to supervise citizens. The law, in particular, expanded the rights of the FBI in eavesdropping and electronic surveillance, which many considered to be a violation of the fourth amendment to the constitution.

Conspiracy theories claim that the tragedy was dramatized by the US government precisely with the goal of passing laws that allow them to spy on their own citizens without hindrance. Immediately after the events of 9/11, the Terrorist Surveillance Program was created, which was replaced by an even more extensive set of PRISM events, exposed by Edward Snowden.

The popular pamphlet documentary Fahrenheit 9/11 , criticizing George W. Bush for the wars in Afghanistan and Iraq, and for involvement in the September events, and the journalistic change Loose Change became popular works on the 9/11 events. ].

Constructions of part of conspiracy theories are based on the fact that the plane crashing into a skyscraper is not able to completely destroy it, since the skyscraper has sufficient strength to withstand such a blow; The explanations according to which the steel structures of the skyscraper melted due to interaction with burning aviation fuel are dismissed as untenable.

In addition, there is a lack of photographic and video materials depicting the plane crashing into the Pentagon, as well as the complete absence of aircraft parts and passengers' bodies at the site of this catastrophe, as well as the successful preservation of the terrorist passports found at the accident site.

Google helps small companies to start an online business

The search giant continues to introduce into its business more and more profound social components that ensure affection for the company's services much stronger than the need to simply get any information from the search results.

Google introduced the Get Your Business Online initiative, which allows the company to transfer its business to the Internet in several steps, deciding on one service all the issues related to the creation of the site itself, the choice of domain name and hosting. Yesterday, a new service was launched for Texas and, as stated in the commercial, the entire process from site creation to publication will take no more than 60 minutes (there is no feeling of lightness of the process). Of course, all this is free for a whole year, after which the company can purchase a domain for $ 2 a month and hosting for $ 4.99 for the same period, or it may refuse to continue the service. Google also provides customer support for the service for 30 days free of charge by e-mail and, in addition, lends $ 75 to participate in AdWords.

Promotional video Get Your Business Online:




The target audience for the service so far is only Texas, and, according to a Google study, about half of the small business representatives in this state do not have full representation on the Internet, with the possible exception of social networking pages or ancient static sites. Thus, it must be admitted that the search giant creates quite comfortable conditions for transferring business to online rails, without creating any particular inconvenience for the termination of the provision of its services, if the need arises. So, in the light of the recently acclaimed Google+ social fever, the activities of the search giant seem to be quite consistent.

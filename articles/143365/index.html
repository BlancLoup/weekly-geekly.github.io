The winner of the iOS Messenger Developer Contest for VKontakte was the 21-year-old

The completed contest for the development of the messenger for the social network VKontakte, the results of which are published today, in addition to the fact that the winners shared 5 million rubles among themselves, allows us to note a rather remarkable circumstance that can be attributed not just to the ‚Äúsuccess story‚Äù but also to talent and perseverance.

The competition itself from the network of Pavel Durov had a rather narrow time frame - from March 9 to April 20. Before the end of this line, it was necessary to send a free application to the App Store, while Apple‚Äôs application approval was not required (the fact that this process could take a long time was taken into account by the organizers of the competition; it is also interesting to note that 200 people applied for participation, and sent final running application only 48). Actually, the task was formulated as follows:

It is necessary to implement the application in Objective-C using the VKontakte API in the volume described in the task . It is important to accurately reproduce the proposed aesthetics and achieve high speed of the application: the initial download, the opening of dialogs, sending messages and the smoothness of all animations. Deviations from the proposed design and functionality within the preservation of the described concept are allowed. An additional requirement for the task is the presence of Russian and English versions of the interface. The application must be compatible with all iOS devices with firmware version 4.0 and higher. Both screen formats - Retina and standard resolution should be supported.


The winner of the competition was a 21-year-old young man from St. Petersburg, Peter Yakovlev, who purchased the iPod Touch especially for the development and, in a really narrow time frame of the competition, managed to write an application that won first place. At the same time, it is curious that before the contest Peter did not know Objective C, which, of course, had to be mastered - however, the official results page hints that it could not have done without the help of senior colleagues (the winner of the fall VKontakte web design contest) .

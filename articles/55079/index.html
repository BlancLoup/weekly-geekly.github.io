Moskvich received a suspended sentence for installing pirated software



Here is the news :

Thirty-year-old Muscovite Alexander Zinoviev was sentenced to two years of imprisonment by the Khimki City Court for a two-year suspended sentence for installing pirated software, Interfax reported.
According to the investigation, in July 2008, Alexander Zinoviev installed counterfeit versions of Microsoft, 1C and Adobe software on ten computers of a school in Khimki. As a result, the right holders suffered damage in the amount of 443 thousand rubles.

Considering that more than 90% of people in Russia have unlicensed programs on a computer, now everything is under the threat of trial and a criminal term. The precedent has been created.

At the same time pay attention - he installed the software not on the home computer, but on the computers in the school. So, if you are an admin and install unlicensed software, you will be responsible for everything, and not the one who does not want to pay for it.

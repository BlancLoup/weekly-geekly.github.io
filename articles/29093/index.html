Dalsvyaz will provide subscribers with IP telephony on credit

Most recently, we reported on T-Mobile's innovations on VoIP , and today I received information that the domestic operator Dalsvyaz will soon provide its subscribers with the opportunity to call anywhere in the world using IP-telephony, and all this will be available and on credit. The press service of the company explained that it does not require the purchase of prepaid cards, it is enough to call from a phone that has a tone dialing. The bill for the use of IP-telephony will be included in the monthly receipt for payment of communication services.

I am very pleased that domestic operators are increasingly turning their attention to the VoIP sector and offer their users various options for the implementation of communication. At the same time, the usability of the service is increasingly allowing even unprepared users to choose it, which dramatically expands the reach of the audience.

The press service of the operator reminds that the IP-telephony service consists in calls via the Internet. Therefore, such calls are cheaper compared to traditional wired communication.

The new service is available to all Dalsvyaz customers - individuals and legal entities - in the territory of Primorsky, Khabarovsk Territories, Jewish Autonomous, Sakhalin and Magadan regions. Also, in the near future the service will be launched in the Amur and Kamchatka branches of the company.

OJSC Dalsvyaz provides all types of communication services (local, long-distance, international telephone communication, Internet, data transfer, cellular communication, wireless radio access services, etc.). The company operates in 8 regions of the Far East: Primorsky and Khabarovsk Territories, the Amur, Jewish Autonomous, Kamchatka, Magadan, Sakhalin regions and the Koryak Autonomous District. The revenue share of Dalsvyaz in the telecommunications market of the Far East is 59.5%. The company occupies 72% of the Internet market in the Far East, 87% of the local communication market, 81% of the long-distance and international communication market in the Far East, 4% of the cellular communication market.

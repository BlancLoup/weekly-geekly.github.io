About new atoms and live MIDs

A colleague with a higher humanitarian education suggests that it is correct to say " live mussels ", but today I am more than ever persistent)



Yesterday, Intel held a regular meeting with representatives of leading IT publications, where plans for the near future of mobile systems were revealed. Let me remind you that not so long ago a similar meeting was organized, but dedicated to desktop systems.



This time the place for the meeting was not so shocking, but no less cozy - a small building somewhere in the back streets of Barvikha, where a pleasant New Year's Eve atmosphere was in harmony with three dozen uncles of varying degrees of beardness - these were specialists from THG, IXBT, Ferra, 3DNews , GameLand and other companies.

Immediately at the entrance to the hall, under the projector screen, lay engineering samples of various pieces of iron, about which a little later. As soon as everyone sat down at the tables, a man appeared on the scene, without whom every New Year does not do every Intel meeting - Father Frost Alexey Rogachkov , regional product introduction specialist. Actually, it was he who began to push the curtains of secrets, but since a lot of information was shrouded in NDA, I will retell it only in general terms.



So, the Internet is already full of information about the new Intel Atom processors , code-named Pine Trail . Indeed, next year, Intel plans new stones with a graphics controller and a memory controller integrated into the processor core (for the first time in the X86 family of chips) - these atoms will be intended for entry-level netbooks and desktop PCs (nettops).



The platform for netbooks will be represented by a single-core Intel Atom N450 processor (1.66GHz, 512KB L2 cache) and Intel NM10 Exoress chipset , while nettops will be built on the same chipset, but the processor will be either a single-core Intel Atom D410 (1.66GHz, 512KB L2) or Dual-core Intel Atom D510 (1.66 GHz, 1024 Kb L2). These processors are made on 45-nanometer technology and are still the smallest from the manufacturer.



What will all this give? It is logical to assume that reducing the number of chips from three (CPU + north and south bridge) to one (as well as the number of layers for wiring the board) will reduce the size of both the boards and the devices themselves. With reduced by 20% energy consumption, it was possible to reduce heat dissipation. In the end, the gain will be in the time of work and the "coolness" of the device, up to passive cooling.





By the way, about heat dissipation - new systems can really do with just one passive cooling, which is another pleasant advantage (along with SSD disks, absolutely silent solutions can be obtained) of upcoming platforms.





In addition to Alexei Rogachkov, Nikolay Isakov spoke, who told about operating systems that will be installed on new devices. Together with Windows 7 (Starter or Home Basic), users will be offered an alternative operating system (most likely, Mandriva Linux 2010) with the Moblin platform, which will give an opportunity to fine tune the device interface, as well as install a huge number of packages from the repository. What surprised me - Microsoft plans to port Silverlight for these devices)

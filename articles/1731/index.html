Windows Vista RC1 will be released on September 5

From today, Microsoft has begun sending out Windows Vista Release Candidate 1 (RC1) distribution CDs to selected browsers. It is expected that free access to RC1 will be open already on September 5. This is an opportunity to download a program for free that costs almost half a thousand dollars.

The release of RC1 means that the development of the operating system of the new generation of Vista has entered the final stretch. Now, before the official release, Microsoft will not have time to do anything, except to fix some bugs at the last moment.

For corporate customers, Windows Vista will arrive in November (most likely in early November), and retail sales will begin in January 2007. If you believe the accidental information leakage through the Canadian Microsoft website, then the cost of the most expensive modification of Windows Vista Ultimate will be $ 450, and The Home Premium version will sell for $ 270. Upgrading from Windows XP to Vista Ultimate will cost $ 270, and the Home Premium - $ 180.

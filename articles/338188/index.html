Control of dangerous cash operations: integration of video surveillance with 1C



Not all business optimizing solutions really work efficiently. The desire to minimize costs and maximize profits often leads to rash actions. Therefore, in order not to break away from reality, Ivideon uses its own products along with our users. New cameras, service updates, video analytics - all this is checked by employees, works on a full-fledged basis inside the office and shops.

At the beginning of the year, we did a joint project with David Yan and iiko , linking the video surveillance service to cash transactions in restaurants. Now you can watch a video of a suspicious event at the box office directly in your Ivideon account. For example, you saw the cancellation of the check and want to know the reason. Iiko was pleased with the cooperation and offered video surveillance as a pre-service to its customers.

But we were left with a feeling of easy dissatisfaction - for the first time we could not use our own product ourselves. Ivideon does not yet have a restaurant. But we have a network of stores. And then the idea came: what's the difference, a restaurant, a house of a book or a camera shop? The main thing is the availability of cash. Our stores operate at 1C (as well as a third of enterprises throughout the country), so we decided to make friends with the video surveillance service with the 1C: Enterprise automation system.

What we did


Integration with iiko showed all the advantages of communication video surveillance and automation systems. We wanted to achieve a similar effect for our own stores - i.e. control any operational event in business processes by video data. For example, check data such as:

Integration with 1C




We made a module for integration with any 1C product based on 1C 8.3 platform (integration with earlier versions is possible, but will require customization with the involvement of 1C programmer). That allows you to get a video of any operational events in business processes (breaking a check, shipping invoice, receiving goods, etc.).

The solution turned out to be simple, affordable and easy to configure by the user (without the involvement of programmers). Our integration is universal, but only monitors events at the object record level in 1C. For a more complex event fixation (price change in the document, opening a cash drawer, etc.), additional customization is needed by an outside programmer 1C.

In our solution, we do not make any changes to the objects of the main configuration of 1C. Thus, typical 1C configurations do not fly off the automatic update.

The wiring diagram is very simple. It is necessary to download external processing for 1C 8.3 from the Ivideon website (at the bottom of the page). Installation and configuration of the module can be carried out according to the instructions of the 1C user without the involvement of a programmer.

The user marks on 1C a record of which configuration objects (directories, documents) he wants to record in Ivideon.

The presumption of guilt on the Internet. When you need to prove that you are not a camel



The relationship between site owners and users, in principle, can be described with a simple phrase: this is my site, which I want to do. This is normal for small unclaimed projects. It may seem sad, but such projects do not have the resources to sift ambiguous situations, you have to chop off the shoulder.

Another thing is when projects that have a leading position in their field, projects that have a fairly long history for the Internet come in the same way.

Should the user be photographed with a passport in his hands each time the project administration suspects inaccurate data?

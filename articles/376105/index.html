New Hampshire may start accepting taxes and fees in bitcoins



The New Hampshire Legislature in January introduced a bill to develop a system for accepting Bitcoins to pay taxes and duties. This was announced on his twitter Barry Silbert - an investor, the founder of the Digital Currency Group and the Bitcoin Investment Trust.



The bill is supported by seven senators. If it is adopted, it will become a precedent, since while cryptocurrency has not yet reached such a level of recognition in any other country.

Silbert promotes cryptocurrency and makes initial investments in startups related to this topic. He has invested in more than 40 different bitcoin startups.

New Hampshire, apparently, has to engage in cryptocurrency. Lamassu startup, which was organized in this state in 2013 , developed small ATMs for accepting Bitcoins and offers them to all interested entrepreneurs in addition to cash registers and terminals.

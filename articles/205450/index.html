Axure Interactive Prototype in 20 Minutes

While I was writing about designers and prototypes, it suddenly turned out that many readers have no idea what this is about. So today I decided to talk about what an interactive prototype, made in Axure (pronounced "Escher", and I used to say "Akshur"). And not only to tell, but also to show.

If it is very rough, then an interactive prototype is a set of related html-layouts. Only worked on these layouts are not a designer with a coder, but a designer in a specialized program that allows you to do this work many times faster. What is the difference in the result? The prototype prepared by the designer, unlike the models prepared by the layout designer, is not intended for development. He is called upon to very quickly and visually demonstrate what the performer will implement in the next stages. It is like a three-dimensional model of a building being prepared for construction. Its creation will take much less time than the construction, and it will allow you to virtually wander around the future project.

The benefits of prototypes are difficult to overestimate.

  • Simplicity and speed of creation. A good designer can demonstrate the first version of the prototype a few hours after the task is formalized;
  • A well-made prototype is specifically designed for subsequent edits. Looking at the resulting result, both the client and the performer, with the help of fast iterations, they will truly go towards the desired goal;
  • This allows even before writing a functional specification or technical assignment to very precisely agree on what should happen at the output. And the documents will only fix this agreement;
  • Due to the visibility of the prototype, the probability of edits in the next stages of creating the site is reduced to zero. And taking into account how much the edits at the programming stage can do, the design phase saves a huge amount of money every day to people all over the world.


Especially for Habr, I recorded the process of creating a simple prototype. I took it out of my head, the goal was to demonstrate the speed with which such work is being done. From the video you can understand that in 20 minutes an experienced designer can already do something that can be discussed and given feedback. Plus it can be seen that all the through and list elements are made using wizards, which will allow editing one master to make changes to the entire prototype. Do not like the name of the menu item in the header? We fix one word, and this fix applies to all pages. I want to change the appearance of a separate work in the list of portfolios - the same. We edit the separate master, and then we align the result on the page with the list of works.

You will also notice that in the video I use the second prototype, which contains the main typical interface elements.

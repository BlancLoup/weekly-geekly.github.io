01 Links for UX-specialists

In this compilation, I want to share information resources where you can draw on new knowledge, track new methods, techniques and analytics, and also improve your skills in the UX field. It will be useful not only to experts in the field of usability, but also to everyone who is related to the development of web products.




UX News and Content


  • smashingmagazine.com/category/uxdesign - UX-section of the famous magazine about web technologies and design Smashing Magazine;
  • blog.uxpin.com is a blog of UXPin, in which, besides corporate news, they write a lot of user interaction with interfaces;
  • usabilitygeek.com - a blog designed to combine theory and academic research with practice and personal recommendations in order to improve the usability of websites;
  • uxmag.com is a magazine with helpful user experience articles. All articles are written by experts from around the world, but undergo editorial selection and correction;
  • uxbooth.com - articles from the ux-community and for it, targeted also for beginners;
  • boxesandarrows.com - here besides UX, a lot of materials on design studies and tests;
  • nngroup.com/articles - articles section of the reputable Norman Nielsen Group about researches in the field of user interaction experience;
  • ia.net/blog - information architecture blog;
  • designmodo.com/design/ux-design - the UX section of the popular design blog;
  • uxcolombo.org/blog - the purpose of this resource is to train designers and developers to improve the interaction of their products and services with users;
  • uxmovement.com is a blog telling how bad interfaces can affect user behavior;
  • 52weeksofux.com - an excursion into the design process for real people;
  • heydesigner.com/ux-design - daily / weekly / monthly distribution of materials on UX-themed;
  • medium.com/ui-ux-articles - UX article section on the Medium blogging platform;
  • medium.com/design-ux is another branch for UX designers;
  • useronboard.com - guys sort out problems with registration in popular services, in some places it is very interesting;
  • uxmyths.com is a collection of the most common UX myths explaining why this is not true. Partially Russified Version - axmyths.com


Design Patterns


  • uxpin.com/knowledge.html - must-read library for designers, developers, product managers, UX specialists;
  • designprinciplesftw.com - a super collection of design principles from the best Internet companies;
  • uxporn.uxpin.com - collection of patterns;
  • tabpatterns.com - a collection of designs for tablets, divided into categories;
  • pttrns.com - a similar collection, only with designs for smartphones;
  • zurb.com/responsive - a directory with examples of responsive designs, with real examples on websites;
  • littlebigdetails.com - a collection of reviews of small but interesting details in the interfaces.


Instruments


  • uxpin.com is a prototyping tool with extensive capabilities and a large library of resources and templates;
  • axure.com is one of the most famous prototyping tools, a favorite of designers and managers;
  • invisionapp.com - a powerful online service, with the possibility of convenient teamwork on prototypes and user experience;
  • uxcheck.co - Chrome plugin for peer review of key Nielsen heuristics;
  • uxchecklist.github.io - UX designer check list with links to useful information;
  • zeplin.io - a collaboration tool for an interface designer and a front-end developer;
  • userium.com is a usability checklist for common cases;
  • ninjamock.com is a web prototyping tool from our compatriots.


Thank you for your attention, next time there will be a selection with the latest and most popular materials from the UX world.

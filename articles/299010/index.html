It is finished: the venture fund of "Rostelecom" decided on the first deal in a year from the moment of its foundation

Vedomosti reports that Commite Capital has invested in the Russian company, Raidix , which develops software for data storage systems (DSS). 100% of the venture fund belongs to Rostelecom . According to the operator, this is the first investment of the fund. "COMMIT Capital" bought out 30% of the company for 100 million rubles.

100% Raidix belongs to its founder Andrei Fedorov. Then the company's valuation will be about 333 million rubles. In 2015, Raidix revenue amounted to 100 million rubles. Of these, a little more than half are on the international market, said Raidix CEO Alexander Katashov. Raidix products are purchased by companies from all over the world.

According to Alexey Kupriyanov, the director of the CommIT Capital fund, Rostelecom's money will be spent on expanding Raidix sales. According to Kupriyanov, Raidix now occupies less than 1% of both the Russian and global market for storage software.

The main competitors of Raidix are global corporations - Dell-EMC , IBM , Hitachi and others. The entire Russian market of data storage systems Kupriyanov estimates at 40 billion rubles, of which the software (the segment in which Raidix operates) accounts for about 5-7 billion rubles.

According to Alexei Basov, Vice-President of Rostelecom, Raidix solutions can be used in backup-related products, content libraries, virtualization, and big data. Therefore, the share of Raidix in the Russian market can be increased both by purchasing Rostelecom itself and by its customers. To meet their needs, the company will be able to refine the product using the expertise of Rostelecom.

On January 14, Megamind wrote that Raidix submitted an application to the Russian Software Registry. The application is still in the status of consideration.

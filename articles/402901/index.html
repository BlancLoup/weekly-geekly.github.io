On March 16, in City Club International, we presented our new video glasses Epson Moverio BT-300 to leading Russian business, industry and IT publications.


The highlight of the event was a clear demonstration of how the new model of video glasses is great for use as a so-called. FPV for quadcopter. A brand new DJI Phantom 4 was chosen as a drone, and you can see a short report from the event itself with the most interesting moments below:



And now a few words about the product itself and its technical characteristics (about glasses, of course, with the drone, you probably already know well without us.


Not a revolution, but evolution


In February 2017, Epson Moverio BT-300 video glasses went on sale in the Russian market. Earlier we already talked about the previous version of the Moverio BT-200 glasses : and even held a contest for developers who were satisfied with both ourselves and the participants.



So, Epson Moverio BT-300 is the title of the lightest transparent binocular video glasses in the world.


Their weight is only 69 grams . It would seem that the difference in 19 grams between the versions is not so significant, but when you have to wear such glasses on your nose for a long time - the absence of these 19 grams is a significant advantage.


However, the main feature is a completely new display based on Si-OLED technology! Take a look at the picture - as they say, the difference on the face !



Si-OLED - development engineers Epson. With this technology, each pixel of the image is a source of light and glows only when needed. What does this give? Right! Great contrast and excellent color reproduction. About the contrast I want to say separately. In the previous version of the glasses, the more or less traditional illuminated LCD matrix was used, in connection with which, one could see the boundaries of the projection area, even if no images were displayed on the screen (that notorious ‚Äúillumination‚Äù or how it is often called abroad - ‚Äúglow ").


In the case of Si-OLED, this effect is no longer observed. The image from the glasses is perfectly ‚Äúsuperimposed‚Äù over the visible through the transparent glasses of the surrounding space, which allows achieving even greater immersion in the world of augmented reality. I think many owners of phones or TVs with OLED-screens are well aware of how cool it is.


And the rest of the successor to the Epson BT-200 can all the same as the previous version, only with a better camera and productive stuffing (even more cores, megabytes and megahertz).

What does it have to do with Epson and DJI?


The fact is that in the fall of 2016, Epson announced a partnership with DJI , although we did not pay much attention to this partnership. Of course, with the release of Epson BT-300, we plan to improve, and proudly position new video glasses as FPV for DJI drones (and not only).


Many of you know that DJI is the leading manufacturer of unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs). The result of the collaboration was the DJI GO application, optimized for working with Epson Moverio BT-300 video glasses. The DJI GO app is compatible with DJI Phantom, Inspire, Matrice and Mavic drones, as well as with the DJI Osmo system.



How it works?


To get started, just download the DJI Go application from the Moverio App Market app store and install it on the glasses. Having launched the application, follow the prompts on the points screen to configure and connect the points with the drone control panel. Everything! You can take off!


Thanks to the built-in BT-300 displays, the pilot sees the image from the drone's camera, and due to the fact that the glasses are transparent, he can directly observe the flight of the drone itself. Such a solution meets the European requirements of aviation agencies for unmanned aerial vehicles, in particular, the principle that UAVs should remain within the direct view of the pilot.

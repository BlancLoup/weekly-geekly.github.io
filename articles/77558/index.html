Answers to questions about creating the main site of the country

Most recently, I announced a conference with Artem Geller on the creation of the kremlin.ru site. The conference was held on Wednesday (December 2). Everyone had the opportunity to ask Artem a question. The conference itself is available in our archive , and here I will post some of the most interesting questions and answers in my opinion.





Alexey Kolevatykh: Hello, Artem, tell me, how many sketches did you prepare before you got the final sketch, which later turned into a website? What are the features of demonstration and adoption of ekskizov at sites of such high national importance?
Artem Geller: Good afternoon, Alexey. If you answer in fact, then in the folder with the project 705 layouts in the .psd format, and the folder itself takes 1.5Gb. For a web project, this is a very large size.

Due to the fact that the basic value in our case was dynamic information flows, we started developing layouts from the separate news page and only came to the main page closer to the end. This is rather unusual because, for the most part, website development starts from the main page.

It is important to note that even before the start of development, there was a carefully defined concept; moreover, preliminary layouts of iconic sections were created.

There were no features of demonstration and adoption of sketches; we simply worked in close cooperation with the press service of the President of the Russian Federation. Personal visits, files, mail, constant communication.


Yevgeny Kalmykov: Hi, Artem.
And why is the copyright on the most important website of the country not made according to GOST?

To begin, probably, it is necessary that I am satisfied with the result of the work. Work on the portal continues and if there are thoughts to do something new, then these thoughts find their realization. After all, the project is truly alive, both outside and inside.

Lyosha Ogonyov: How were the special versions of the site developed? Did you use any recommendations, such as WCAG, when developing the ‚Äúversion for the visually impaired‚Äù?
Artem Geller: Alexey, thank you very much for this question.
I will say that we thought about accessibility within the framework of all subsites.
Of course, we fully adhered to the standards and recommendations. Used their work in this area and the source:
National standard GOST R 52872-2007 ‚ÄúInternet resources. Accessibility requirements for the visually impaired ‚Äù, Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) - (from W3C) (international), Section 508 Standards - USA, WaSP - United Kingdom, DAL - Denmark.

The issue of accessibility has been studied even more widely. We read and analyzed websites created by people with disabilities, and websites created for people with disabilities, where there are really interesting discussions on the availability of this large part of active Internet users.
We have applied a new technology, born in England called zoom layout (http://joeclark.org/access/webaccess/zoom/), this is a very good idea:
  • Automatic content formatting;
  • The set of columns becomes one column;
  • Visually, navigation is simplified;
  • The menu goes up;
  • Fonts are increasing;

All this ensures maximum availability.

We went even further, in addition to the main color standards recommended by the standards, we introduced several additional ones that we learned about from specialized forums, i.e. from the original source.

5 places to get inspiration when designing your logo

One of the important stages on the way to creating a logo is to view the work of other designers. No, not in order to copy someone else's idea, but in order to broaden your horizons, to see how others were able to realize this idea, to get inspiration in the end. I bring to your attention 5 resources that may be useful to you.



1. Logopond.com

One of the largest English-speaking logo galleries. View twice a day before meals.

2. Logodesignlove.com

Blog Irish designer David Eyre (David Airey). Many wonderful collections of logos, stories of famous designers, interviews, etc. - definitely will not leave you indifferent.

3. Logolounge.com

The resource is primarily valuable analytical articles. For example, "Trends in Logo Design 2009" . Interviews with designers, a lot of news from the world of identity will also be completely useful.

4. Logofaves.com

Smaller, but no worse than the Logopond.com gallery.

5. Logospire.com

Another gallery from the author Usabilitypost.com , Dmitry Fadeev .

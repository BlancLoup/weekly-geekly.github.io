Stress management in a burning tank

Stress - the body's reaction to any unusual irritant, physical or emotional. Stress biology


Hans Selye, the author of the concept of stress, defined it as the body's response to nonspecific effects - effects, in order to adapt to which the body must change. He also stated: the body experiences stress when adapting to any psychological or physical environmental factor. Grief and joy are diverse emotions, specific in their essence, but the body reacts to them in the same way.

In support of his hypothesis, Selye resorted to laboratory experiments. Rats were injected with toxic hormones, and, regardless of their type, the animals ‚Äôorganism produced the same reaction: intensive work of the adrenal cortex, atrophied or deformed thymus gland and lymph nodes, ulcers in the gastrointestinal tract. Later it turned out that exactly the same internal changes occur in conditions of sudden changes in temperature, injuries or infections.

About burning tank


General adaptation syndrome (JSC) - stress cycle, an algorithm that our body drives around in a circle in any unclear situation:

1. Surprise or anxiety due to a new stress factor (Danio is dark and he cannot find the keys in his pocket)
2. Resistance to the stress factor - the solution to the problem (Danio took a deep breath and tried to calm down, carefully looks at his feet and found the keys in his backpack);
3. Exhaustion - the end of emotional and hormonal "consumption", fatigue (After a hot soup and half the Breaking Bad series, Danio falls asleep without hind legs).

In essence, a person passes through these phases daily. A cup of espresso - and your body is ready to shove at the barricades.

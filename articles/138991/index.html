The FBI ordered the court to shut off 3,000 GPS bugs, now it cannot find them

A few days ago, the US Supreme Court ruled that the use of tracking GPS devices without a court order was unconstitutional and decided to stop this practice. Since the decision of the court entered into force immediately, the Federal Bureau of Investigation was forced on the same day to turn off signal reception from about 3,000 active GPS bugs.

The decision of the Supreme Court has caused serious consequences, which were told by the General Counsel of the FBI Andrew Weissmann (Andrew Weissmann) at a scientific conference at the University of San Francisco. It turns out that the FBI specialists had problems collecting the bugs they had installed. In some cases, Weissmann says, even had to ask the court to issue temporary warrants for surveillance, just to turn on the bug for a while.

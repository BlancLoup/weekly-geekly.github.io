Criminal liability will be imposed for stealing bank passwords.



Izvestia reports that a conclusion from the National Council of the Financial Market (NSFR) was sent to the Federation Council and the State Duma for a government bill amending the Criminal Code. The conclusion contains the appeal of a number of bankers and deputies about the inclusion in the Criminal Code of articles for phishing. It is proposed to set the maximum penalty for phishing up to 2 million rubles, and the maximum term of imprisonment - up to 10 years. This is even more severe punishment than for the most serious crime under the article "fraud." Now in the Criminal Code there is no mention, as such, of mentioning and punishment for phishing - so far only a bill has been introduced, adding responsibility for the manufacture and sale of skimming devices for ATMs.

According to reports from the Central Bank over the summer of 2014, about 220 million bank cards are used in Russia. Banks are constantly trying to improve the level of data and card protection. Even cards with a fingerprint scanner appear already. But users are still victims of theft, most often because of their negligence.

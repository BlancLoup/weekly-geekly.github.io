Stable overclocking of Samsung Galaxy S II up to 1.5 GHz

Friday evening is approaching inevitably, with which I congratulate everyone!

In order to stir up interest on the eve of the launch of Samsung Galaxy S II in sales, I present to your attention the result of coolbho3000 overclocker works from the well-known forum.xda-developers.com .

This comrade set out to find out how high the dual-core Exynos 4210 can fly. The processor with which our flagship smartphone came into existence with an initial clock frequency of 1 GHz. Shortly before launching sales in Korea, engineers from Samsung Electronics accelerated it to 1.2 GHz. Now, thanks to the efforts of coolbho3000, a new performance bar has been taken: 1.5 GHz!



Overclocker has kindly provided for everyone to view the source codes and instructions necessary to play this stable overclocking on the Galaxy S II.

Having overclocked the smartphone by 25% of its original frequency, coolbho3000 achieved really impressive benchmark results: 59 MFLOPS in Linpack and over 4,000 points in Quadrant!



According to the overclocker, Exynos processors have great potential, being the fastest on the market at the moment.

PS: Despite the excellent results of this processor overclocking, Samsung Electronics does not recommend repeating it on its own: there may be sad consequences.

How do they go to US universities


Articles about studying in the USA interested my wife so much that she decided to describe her thoughts and experience. She is not a blogger at all, but I hope the article turned out to be interesting:

I came to the States immediately after graduation, so I know about the life of a Russian student only by hearsay. Since I have already written about learning myself , I‚Äôll tell you

Applicants


As described in previous articles, there are two methods of admission. The first is the exam during the last year of school. If a person is fluent in English, it is best to pass the SAT , which includes a rather difficult level of English and mathematics. ‚ÄúStandardized Aptitude Test‚Äù is a test for testing the academic abilities of mathematics and English; it is necessary when entering universities in the USA and Canada. Americans at school rent it several times. Attempts begin at grade 10 (Pre-SAT) and end at grade 11 (recall that they have 12 grades at school).

I believe that the exam is the Russian equivalent of the SAT . At the beginning of the 12th year of schooling, students send their application forms to universities. A questionnaire is attached to the points, where the student talks about himself and his parents (general data), and also writes an essay on a given topic. Most often, the topic ‚ÄúWhy do you want to enter this university‚Äù . With this confusion essay, you have to compose for each university. Wanting to increase the chances of admission, I want to send to the maximum number of universities.
Be sure to attach the letters of recommendation that write and send the teacher. This means that the student himself will never know what the teacher wrote about him. Letters of recommendation are usually 2 - 3 pieces. Usually, students send out their questionnaires in the fall. The admission of questionnaires ends at each university in different ways, from December 1 to February 1 (rarely March 1). Consideration of the questionnaire costs money, an average of $ 50-100 per application.
The second option is admission - the person arrived from another country, where he had already graduated from school. He needs to transfer the school certificate and pass the TOEFL . Specialist companies are engaged in the transfer of certificates, and different universities accept transfers from different companies. They translate not only the text itself, but also the estimates into the ABCDF system, calculate the GPA. True, not everywhere so strictly, sometimes a simple translation with the seal of a translator and a notary certified is enough. Next, you need to send questionnaires to universities, and how everyone should wait for an answer. Naturally, the better the university, the higher the requirements for test results (and the higher the competition).

Centralized systems

In my case, it was PharmCAS . Instead of filling in separate forms for each university, the applicant is registered in the system. All data: transcript, test results (PCAT, MCAT, GRE ...), letters of recommendation, questionnaires, essays - are sent there or are filled in online. By the way, the professors' recommendations for me were also filled in online. PharmCAS then sends the data to the specified universities. Most universities participate in this system. I had to fill in only two separate application forms for universities that do not accept PharmCAS.
Similar systems exist for other specialties.

Choice of profession


Pupils who have chosen a profession already at school are purposefully preparing for admission to certain universities. Naturally, there are students who have not decided on the choice; they go where they take it. Often try to choose away from their parents;)
Such students will be able to decide in the profession during the first year. You ask how is it? Each university has a credit education system. Any profession requires the student to collect basic subjects such as biology, chemistry, mathematics, psychology, English, etc. Accordingly, during the first year, a student, visiting subjects, decides what he likes most. These students are called undecided . As for the more serious professions, such as medical professionals, lawyers, pharmacists, etc. (to those who study for many many years) - it is useful for them to be determined as early as possible. The advantage of free choice of subjects is the ease of changing specialty in the middle of the way. One friend studied for three years in economics, then I realized that it was ‚Äúnot his‚Äù and changed direction in the direction of medicine. He was credited with all the basic subjects, all that remained was to recruit only profile ones.

By the way, assessments when applying for a job do not matter, the school name is decisive. You have completed MIT on threes - employers will meet with open arms. Selection



After the initial screening of the questionnaires, approximately 2 people remain in place. Everyone is called for an interview where their fate is already decided. After the interview, a formal response comes within a month: accepted, not accepted, or queued. A queue means you can be accepted if space is made available. In case of success (you were accepted!), You must send an official response and a down payment of $ 500 - $ 2000.
All have probably noticed that the whole process takes place remotely by mail and online.

When applying for a doctor or lawyer, the competition may be much more serious or include additional requirements. For example, for admission to a doctor, they may additionally require 2000 hours of work as a volunteer in the medical field, for example, in a hospital. (for reference, 2000 hours = year of work for 8 hours a day, five days)

Also, when enrolling in specialized universities, most often it is necessary to pass additional tests, such as GRE (for PhD), MCAT (for physicians), PCAT (for pharmacists), etc. These tests, to put it mildly, are complex and require detailed knowledge of the subjects.

TESIS - Space Observatory online

News about the gigantic emissions of solar matter played the role of advertising for one very interesting site - TESIS - space observatory . On this site you can daily monitor the behavior of our star; It is very convenient that the experimental data are given in an almost original form - the flow of radio emission, photographs in various forms, etc. The site is primarily interesting for people who like to read the original source, and not the interpretation of journalists.

In general, I really like sites where you can see objective data, and not someone's personal opinion. Therefore, for example, when it comes to weather, I like to look at real images (or at least schematic representations) of fronts, directions and wind strength. And if according to the weather on our Internet, you can at least get something valuable (for example, from here ), then by space observations I have so far only seen either references to serious articles containing final conclusions, and sometimes for me very heavy or frankly yellow statements about that in the year 20NN we all will come to an end. In addition to the current state of affairs, the site can see the forecast of magnetic storms, the history of magnetic activity over the past 27 days and a rather useful encyclopedia of the sun.

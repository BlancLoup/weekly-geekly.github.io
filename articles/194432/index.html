External 3D / IR sensor for mobile devices collected $ 500k in 2 days


External 3D sensor breaks records on fees at Kickstarter . The planned $ 100,000 was collected in just 3.5 hours, and on the second day, half a million are already running. "Structural sensor", as the developers call it, is something like Kinect reduced and attached to the camera of a mobile device. The demo application attached to the sensor already allows you to:
  1. Scan objects - capture object models and export them to CAD or 3D printing, in OBJ, STL, or PLY format, which you can immediately upload to Shapeways.com for 3D printing.
  2. Build a model room - easy to capture a 3D model of a room, just spinning around. After that, touching any two points you can get the distance between them.
  3. Augmented reality - in the room in which you are located, you can settle a virtual pet that will interact with real objects


Two IR LEDs and an additional camera allow you to get information about the distance to objects

and turn on night vision

On the left is a normal photo, and on the right is IR vision with an IR flash.

Nutrition


Charge the battery (apparently the device has its own battery), enough for 4 hours of active use, or more than 1000 standby.

Sdk


For iOS developers, an SDK has already been developed that allows working with a sensor

Using a special hacker cable and drivers (Windows, Android, OS X, Linux), you can connect the sensor to almost any device, for example, to the Oculus Rift.

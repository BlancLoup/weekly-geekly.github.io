Statistics of the arbitration system. Open data



An important component of the activities of any state is the arbitration system. The quality of its functioning depends on the coherence of the work of the entire economy of the country. After all, it is in arbitration courts that the fate of large and small enterprises is decided, fundamental decisions are taken that shape judicial practice on such important issues as: collecting mandatory payments and sanctions, challenging administrative liability, bankruptcy cases and corporate disputes.

However, how to evaluate the quality of work of arbitration courts? How to see weak spots? How to predict possible problems?



So that experts, journalists and participants in the process could answer these questions, we created a service statistics service for arbitration courts - open data of the arbitration system.

What can you learn from this data?



Sums of claims


That about 15% of cases are considered in the amount of from 1,000 to 10,000 rubles. But the actual costs of each party, including the court, are much higher.



Total Claim by Region


That in the North-Western District they are considering disputes for 93 billion rubles, and in the Moscow region for 71 billion, although it would seem that all the money is ‚Äúspinning‚Äù in Moscow.



Number of cases reviewed by category of disputes


With most cases in 2009, was considered for violation of supply contracts. It may try to look for weaknesses in the customs, infrastructure and understand why such a huge number of deliveries is violated.



The number of appealed cases


That in the court of appeal, only 13% of cases are appealed. That is, these cases are of an exclusively formal nature, in which only the presence of a judicial fact is necessary.



The average load on the judge


That the average number of cases per first instance judge is about 40 cases per month. That is, on average, the judge must consider and decide on at least two cases per day.



You can make any other conclusions yourself by analyzing statistics and figures. If you have any new ideas on the application of this data, we will be happy to receive suggestions and wishes.

In turn, we will complement and improve the available data and the quality of statistical indicators. All data is calculated on the basis of a file of arbitration cases, updated daily. The service is available to all users of the Pravo.ru reference and legal system.

We believe that the statistics of any agency should be submitted, if not in this form, then at least in such a way that the citizens of their country can always see what their officials are doing.

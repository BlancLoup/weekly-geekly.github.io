Mobility as a trend: almost 50% of Russian IT specialists agree to move to other regions of the country for their career


Sources: iksmedia.ru

48% of Russian IT-specialists are ready to move to another region of the country. Such a conclusion was made in a joint study by the mayor‚Äôs office of Innopolis and the Russian recruiting company HeadHunter. 16% of respondents already have experience moving to other regions. But 13% of specialists consider moving only outside Russia.

The most preferred regions for relocation were: Moscow (29.2% of respondents), St. Petersburg (27.7%) and Krasnodar Krai (11.3%). Also in the top 10 are Moscow, Novosibirsk, Nizhny Novgorod, Kaliningrad, Leningrad, Samara regions and the Republic of Tatarstan. Among foreign countries, the most attractive were the USA (13.5%), Germany (11.4%), Australia (9.2%), Canada (8.1%), Great Britain (7.6%), Spain (5, 4%), cites data Firrma.ru.

For IT professionals, factors like prospects for family members (38%), comfortable urban environment (29%), health care (20%), technical equipment or innovativeness of the region (21%), leading universities (12%).

But the priority factors were wages (64%), work by profession (58%), interesting projects, career prospects, a higher position (57%).

When moving to another country, the priorities were distributed as follows: wages (54%), work by profession (47%), interesting projects and career prospects (43%).

According to a joint study of Mail.ru Group and the recruiting company Smart Start, future graduates of IT specialties overestimate their salary expectations by more than 30%. The survey involved 345 students from the best universities in Moscow. They should have indicated the desired salary immediately after graduation, after three and ten years.

After graduation, students plan to receive 50 thousand rubles a month. This figure is 25% higher than the market average. With three years of experience, they hope to receive 85 thousand rubles. A ten-year experience, in their opinion, guarantees an amount not lower than 160 thousand rubles.

More than 60% of IT-specialists would be interesting to work in a technopark, 10% have already worked or are working. Among the significant advantages for working in technoparks, applicants noted the exchange of experience or the opportunity to learn something new, advanced technologies and higher wages.

According to the respondents, the top 5 technoparks include the Skolkovo Innovation Center (Moscow), Academpark (Novosibirsk), Fiztekhpark (Moscow), IT Park and Innopolis (Tatarstan).

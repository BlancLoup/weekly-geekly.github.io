What is the Unified Registry of Banned Sites?

The registry was created in October 2012 by order of the Government of the Russian Federation. All sites that are blocked for viewing for publication of information prohibited in Russia are entered into it. Such information today includes:

Blocking sites by Roskomnadzor. Access to Internet resources is restricted on the basis of complaints from their visitors. First, experts check the content of sites for which complaints have been received. In the case of confirmation of the received signals, the hosting providers, on whose servers the sites are located, are sent notifications with the requirement to remove prohibited materials. If this does not happen within three days, the resource is entered in the "black list". Telecom operators are obliged to block access to the site from Russian IP-addresses.

Check for getting into the registry of prohibited sites by the Host-tracker service

After a citizen submits a complaint to Roskomnadzor, any site may be blocked already 6 days after its receipt. Moreover, not only resources that deliberately publish materials on the verge of a foul are threatened with blocking. The site may be included in the registry banned and by mistake. Simultaneously with this complaint, others were considered on more than 100 different sites. When locking the sites, the operator mistakenly ticked the VK site. As a result, the multimillion audience of the network for some time could not get on this resource.

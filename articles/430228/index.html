X-shaped marks, as a way to identify the radar by analyzing open data from two scientific satellites SENTINEL-1



The GIS analyst, Harel Dan, noticed that when he accidentally adjusted the noise levels while working with a database of images obtained from the SENTINEL-1 satellites, suddenly a lot of colored X-shaped markings appeared around the world.

Under the radar in the article refers to a tracking radar with a phased antenna array.

The SENTINEL-1 satellite constellation includes: the Sentinel-1A satellite (launched April 3, 2014) and the Sentinel-1B satellite (April 25, 2016). The mass of each satellite is 2300 kilograms, put into a sun-synchronous orbit with an altitude of 690 kilometers.



There are two main types of satellites for remote sensing: optical and radar. Each type can be further divided into subcategories based on aperture, orbit and ranges. At the moment, one of the most used of such complexes is the implementation of the stages of the European Space Agency (ESA) program called Copernicus using a pair of satellites from the Sentinel-1, S1A and S1B constellation, which will provide data with a combined average waiting time of 1.5 days best case scenario.

This method of obtaining high-resolution data with short update times, as well as providing open access to data from these satellites, now plays an important role in many areas of research, including data for emergency response, monitoring the marine and aquatic environment ( even floods ) , cover analysis and agricultural assistance, forest fire assessments and urban planning.

Satellite data can be freely downloaded and analyzed on many platforms, including: Copernicus Open Data Hub , Sentinel EO Browser and Google Earth Engine .

The mission of Sentinel-1 is based on the complex of two identical satellites Sentinel-1A and Sentinel-1B, rotating in orbits 180 degrees apart from each other around the Earth. Using these satellites allows you to create global ground coverage and the ability to deliver optimized data to Copernicus users.


Taking into account that there can be distortions in the data from optical images caused by interference from clouds and dust, using data from radar images you can mostly ‚Äúsee‚Äù through water vapor and other solid particles. On the other hand, data from radar images may be distorted due to interference from other sources on the ground and transmitting at the same wavelength.

Analyzing data from Sentinel-1, you encounter many types of noise, flares, twists, snaps, and waves in images, so you need to combine and filter data from multiple images so that you can create a clearer image and compensate for some, if not all, noise.

Dell Vostro 14 (5480) Laptop: Well Balanced Availability

It so happened that companies usually offer to review their most advanced and, as a result, expensive products. The logic is clear: a person reads about a chic flagship, gets excited from the heat of technical progress and goes to the store to look at the miracle of technology. Well, there it all depends on the talent of the seller and the thickness of the wallet. Maybe the hero of the review will buy, maybe that is easier. But, most likely, will buy. Yes, and the author of the review to write about the flagship is usually more interesting.



Dell Vostro 14 (5480) - the laptop is inexpensive. Even in modern times. It was not without budgetary constraints, but in some respects it can confidently compete with models one and a half to two times more expensive. So, this is one of the few compact devices on which you can play with pleasure. And in terms of battery life, Vostro 14 (5480) is comparable to the MacBook Pro.

From the very first seconds of dating, the laptop surprised with its weight. When the courier brought the box, it seemed so easy that I doubted - did you forget to put the device in it? A budget 14-inch laptop has always been a brick kilogram for two and a half, and then the whole box weighs less. Vostro 14 for its price category is really very easy - only one and a half kilograms. And this is taking into account the lid, lined with aluminum, without which the weight would be even lower. Even ultrabooks are harder. The thickness of the Dell laptop is not a record, 18.3 mm in the thickest place. But it is a bit. Carrying the Vostro with you is easy and convenient, and I involuntarily remembered - how many times I confidently stated that in the world of real laptops, mobility and affordable price do not overlap in one device.



The absence of the need to achieve compactness at any cost allowed us to place on the sides three USB 3.0 ports, a full-sized HDMI, a card reader and even a rarity on the current ones - an Ethernet port. The description of the Dell Vostro 14 (5480) mysteriously says that it uses a conventional hard disk and a small SSD to speed up the work. Actually, drive one is the Seagate ST500LM000. It belongs to the third-generation Seagate hybrid drives, and in addition to the usual 500 GB HDD with a spindle speed of 5400 revolutions per minute, it also contains an 8 GB flash drive. Does it affect performance? At linear velocity, obviously, no. But since the disk keeps track of what data the user uses most often, he quickly drags the operating system files to the flash. Therefore, loading from scratch and switching on after sleep happen quickly, almost like with an SSD. But miracles, of course, do not happen, and if you want to get the full performance of the solid-state drive, you have to buy it. The hard disk also changes very easily, but pay attention to the replacement thickness - it should not exceed 7 mm.

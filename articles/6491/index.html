Microsoft is worried about ODF in California

We already wrote that the US state of California may soon adopt ODF (Open Document Format for XML) as the official format for documents and other records. Earlier this was stated by the states of Texas and Minnesota.

Then, Microsoft, which was explicitly denied earnings, said it supported such a choice, although it did not approve of using only one document format. Now it ceases to arrange such a state of affairs.

The corporation began collecting signatures and campaigning the public in its defense, urging people to write letters to the California Assembly. The slashdot.org bloggers have access to sample emails sent by Microsoft .

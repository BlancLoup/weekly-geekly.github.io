Convenient accounting of working hours. UPD



Good afternoon, dear habra users!

Some time ago, I realized that the warm weather and the proximity of the sea in Thailand is beginning to relax me greatly and I can no longer work as much as I worked in a one-room Khrushchok cold winter days in our homeland. It seems to be working, working, and the effect is small.

I remembered the same condition while working as a freelancer and thought that I was not alone.

I tried to set up an experiment - I took my usual working day and started writing down a report on what I was doing and how much time. The numbers were not very pleasant. Over time, he began to write down such reports every day, and as it is not surprising, things went uphill. Even if no one sees these reports except me, I can see them perfectly and realize the problem. When it is spinning in your head, you can just take it and dismiss these thoughts. But when you read the reports for the whole month and see that things are really not going very well, then you cannot go anywhere and you begin to realize that you need to change something.

After some time, I started to keep reports in Emacs (org-mode). I used it, I studied it at a basic level and after some time I abandoned it - it seemed not very convenient, because these reports may need to be shared with superiors or customers, and exporting to the same HTML does not look so good.

Actually, something like this was the idea to make your time tracker with blackjack and user-friendly interface and detailed reports.



A little work in the evenings, a month of self-use to improve usability and was born timty.ru - the time-tracker of my dreams. From the very beginning I wanted to make it very simple and convenient. I hope that I did it.

And now a small review.

Projects and tasks

To get started, you need to add projects and tasks. This is done quite simply in this interface:



You can use the tasks as you wish. I use them as categories - Front-End, Back-End, Design, etc. You can use, for example, as parts of a project (for a city portal - posters, weather, etc.)

Time tracking

Accounting itself goes in this interface:



You choose a project and a task, enter a comment if necessary, and time tracking begins:



If desired, you can immediately fill in the field with time to skip the stage with the timer and just add a certain number of minutes.

And you can also choose not to choose a project and task, but write them immediately in the comment field with convenient autocompletion:



Below you will see a list of the time spent today, and you can continue working on each entry by clicking on the airplane icon.

Reports

After some time, you probably want to look at the results of your work and on the reports page you will see the following:



You can see the report for any month or week (for going back and forth, use the arrows in the title). Every day in the schedule is highlighted in one of three colors - red, green or yellow, depending on your diligence on this day.

In addition, you can see the story:



And of course, download the report in PDF format for the entire month or for certain projects.

Intel is no longer going to hold a forum for IDF developers



Today it became known that Intel is no longer going to hold an annual event that has become traditional: a forum for IDF developers. Intel first held this forum 20 years ago, in 1997. At that time, the Intel Developer Forum (IDF) had a relatively small scale. The first time he was held in Palm Springs (California, USA), then moved to San Francisco. In addition, the forum was held at different times in other countries and cities, including Tokyo, Beijing, Moscow.

Each year, IDF collected a large number of technicians who discussed Intel products and developments that were somehow related to the corporation. Sometimes Intel presented some new items that it considered important. Now, according to company representatives, the time has come to cancel IDF, since all the efforts of employees are aimed at developing new markets that are not related to hardware. Therefore, the forum format itself is not suitable for the presentation of most new Intel products.

Representatives of the company say that, instead of IDF, other events are likely to be held, their range will be expanded, but the subject, on the contrary, is narrowed. But what exactly it will be for the event, not reported.

What features should the iPhone take over from the N800?

The other day, Nokia released a correction for the N800. Now the N800 has Skype and Flash Player 9. The N800 is probably the first mobile device with Flash Player 9 support.


So, what should now be in the IPhone for complete happiness:
  • Skype (phone and chat)
  • Flash 9 (we can look at Flex applications, and generally all the functionality that Flash Player 9 provides)
  • GTalk (IM and video chat)
  • Games
  • Open to developers
  • Full browser support (Opera)
  • Media Player (local and streaming audio and video support)
  • Losing any YouTube video
  • Good battery. And also, the ability to change the battery at any time
  • Memory cards in the N800 have two slots, each slot can support 2/4/8 GB
  • The screen is bigger than the IPhone.
  • More intelligent interface.
  • Buttons for zooming - Zoom In / Zoom Out as well as Fullscreen. It is very convenient
  • FM radio
  • Reading Books (PDF)
  • Open source development environment - such as maemo.org
  • GPS via bluetooth
  • Optional external keyboard - bluetooth standard
  • Good wifi and fast connection
  • Etc.


Perhaps this year, we will see the answer of the iPhone on the N800.

Tips for Ties

Three young intelligent guys at the same time run up to the consultant in tuxedos for black bow ties. The consultant, at a loss, checking the number, informs the young people that there are only two blacks left, and one of them has already been ordered by another buyer.

On the proposal to take all the whites, young people begin to resent and argue with each other about who still gets a black tie. The consultant turns for help to the manager to solve such a delicate situation.

The manager, assessing the situation, offers the guys a quick-witted challenge.

1. If there are two black ties in a game, then one of the participants sees two black ties, and understands that he is wearing white. Once no one answered, it means one tie.
2. If the tie is one, then two participants see a black tie, and, applying the logic of paragraph 1, they understand that they have a white tie.
3. Since no one has yet answered, it means that there are no black ties in the game, and ties for all are white.

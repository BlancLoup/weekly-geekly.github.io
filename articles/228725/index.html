Veeam Technical Support Department

As a result of a discussion of my last article about the Veeam office in St. Petersburg, it turned out that readers would be interested to learn more details about our company and, moreover, to see how our specialists work.

We decided to tell more about the work of various departments and today the post about the pride of the company - the technical support department. Under the cut - photos and details



We have already written several times about the company in detail, so I will not stop here. Whoever missed this information can read ‚Äú One day at the office of Veeam Software ‚Äù and ‚Äú Where to start the billionth company ‚Äù.

So, ‚Äúwelcome to the Veeam Software technical support department. How can I help you?"

Our department is divided into 3 offices located in different parts of the world (Columbus, Sydney, St. Petersburg). They are united by one simple task - to provide uninterrupted service in the 24/7 format, accessible to customers from anywhere in the world.

Officially, the company provides support in English, French and German. It so happens that we have few Russian-speaking clients, so knowledge of a foreign language is a prerequisite for hiring. Unofficially, there is support in Italian, Spanish, Greek, and any other language that one of my colleagues knows. A person who understands IT and virtualization, and also speaks a foreign language is a potential candidate for our team.

The technical support department in the usual sense is the place where the load is distributed between the call center operators processing the application and the engineers actually working on this application. But we are working in the B2B sector, and therefore our average client is not a hysterical online player, an individual, but a system administrator who is at least responsible for backing up the virtual environment, and in most cases completely serving this virtual infrastructure. Of course, the level of communicative and technical competence of an employee of our department must correspond to the level of the client, so the requirements for candidates are quite high.

The team has a clear hierarchy. Customer requests can be transferred, if required, from the first level to the second, and possibly to the third. Levels are called T1, T2 and T3 (T = tier). At the same time, the 3rd level is a kind of QA department, where bugs are finally reportable and are considered together by developers.

Inside each of the teams there are experienced team leaders who themselves were similar specialists a year or so ago. That is, they themselves have gone all the way and will not allow their subordinates to be offended, they can, if necessary, stand by the machine to communicate with a difficult client. We also almost introduced gamification into the workflow (now the system is in beta testing). It will be possible to get virtual points and swing up to level 80 to get bonuses by executing the usual working tasks, and then exchange points accumulated for a bonus, time off, or a top-end video card. Actually, the pool of gifts is still not fully determined yet, but we are in the process.

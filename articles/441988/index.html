2019 cameras: stand alone, smart, autonomous



In addition to traditional processors and video cards, innovative modular and flexible screens, the main international consumer electronics exhibition CES this year pleased with the clear formulation of the technology agenda for home security and unexpected collaborations with manufacturers of video surveillance equipment.

In today's review, we‚Äôll tell you what significant features for cameras will be in demand in the near future, and how companies from outside the field of video surveillance use VSaaS technologies in their products.

New features: control zones, object recognition, scaring thieves




The Kasa autonomous surveillance camera system is designed for use as part of a smart home and is equipped with a hub that adds siren and audio recording features that mimic the presence of people.

Virtually every US equipment manufacturer has added a battery to its cameras or a solar battery power feature for battery life.



The Arlo Ultra camera scares the thieves with a real searchlight. You can at any time turn on a powerful lamp mounted in the camera body to scare away intruders. The searchlight also helps to illuminate dark areas of the room, so the camera records color images at night. Recall that the standard infrared illumination makes the image black and white.



California startup Deep Sentinel has equipped its futuristic camera with LiveSentinel predictive analytics module. Watching the activity around the house, the video surveillance system predicts situations that lead to theft, damage to property, penetration into the house. When a suspicious activity occurs in the security zone, the system automatically sends a notification to the owner or security service.



The trend of the year - voice assistants. Device manufacturers are fighting for the right to be the first to incorporate voice technology into their products. It is not only about the inclusion of voice assistants in new products, but also about the evolution of the speech recognition technology itself.

The Australian company Swann integrated the camera with a voice assistant Google. Now the basic functions available through the application can be called by voice. In the near future, we expect further development of this area - the cameras will learn to respond to the voice commands of the user and maintain a conversation.

Swann devices make the difference between mail delivery and its theft, pet and person running at night, an authorized user and a stranger. The smart system itself decides whether to send a push notification in a dangerous situation, or a false alarm.

Object Recognition




Nortek Security & Control has integrated the face recognition system into the ELAN smart home control panels. The new touch panel identifies a person as they get closer, and then displays the user options menu, sets the preferred lighting and temperature, turns on your favorite music. A similar solution was presented by the ADT company - their 7-inch wireless touch control panel is connected to third-party smart things.



A well-known manufacturer of chips for CCTV cameras Ambarella introduced the single-chip system CV2 with built-in support for object recognition technologies. CV2 cameras are able to recognize people, pets, cars directly on the device. CV2 provides efficient video encoding in AVC and HEVC formats to minimize cloud storage costs.



Startup UVeye introduced the technology of detecting security threats by shooting on the camera the bottom of the passing vehicles. A startup uses several synchronized high-resolution cameras to create a digital 360-degree model. Three seconds after the vehicle passes the ground-based UVeye device, the system processes the video data to create a 3D model of the chassis with a full-color high-resolution image.

The combination of object recognition technologies with the data obtained in the manufacture of vehicles, allows you to compare and track the characteristics of different car models. The service can recognize a foreign object the size of a USB drive. In this case, such a system is effectively used to detect leaks, identify wear of parts and any damage.

In the system of Sunflower Labs, drones with cameras operate in conjunction with high-tech garden lanterns. A drone, called a "bee", recharges its batteries in a hub or "hive". Sunflower flashlights with built-in motion and vibration sensors, which, according to the developers, distinguish between people, pets and cars.

Motorola Moto G - for beginners and budget

After Motorola was acquired by search giant Google in 2011, the new owner had no particular impact on the release of smartphones. But soon the company was incredibly quickly and rapidly changed. The first and very successful products of the updated Motorola became the Moto X and the DROID line for the Verizon service provider. The company further stated that it was going to start conquering markets that had not yet been fully formed. The first move in this direction was the release of the budget smartphone Moto G.



And if the Nexus 5 is more of a smartphone for geeks and fans of this line, and Moto X is mainly for people who cannot resist creating their own design, Moto G is for those who want a functional device at a reasonable price.

‚ñå TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS


First of all, Moto G is an inexpensive smartphone, but its filling is still cool! The device runs on a quad-core Snapdragon 400 (MSM8226) with a clock frequency of 1.2 GHz and Adreno 305 GPU. The chip is much better than the MediaTek MT6589 processor, which is also based on the ARM Cortex A7. Thanks to the latest version of Android and 1 GB of RAM, there are no lags with multitasking. The smartphone also supports two navigation systems - the Russian GLONASS and the American GPS.

‚ñå DESIGN, DIMENSIONS, CONTROL ELEMENTS


The front panel is completely covered with protective glass Gorilla Glass. The glass is completely flat, without bulges and slopes, surrounded by small sides around the perimeter. Thanks to the oleophobic coating, the screen is not very easily soiled and is easily wiped.



Above is a conversational speaker, a camera eye and an event indicator. Unfortunately, it shines only in white. So what type of notifications came - can not be determined.



On the right side are the volume and power buttons. All the keys are very thin, quite hard, under the fingers feel nice. From the side it looks like a kind of strict brick with rounded edges and a boat-shaped form.



Bottom - micro-USB connector for charging and synchronizing with a computer. Nearby is a microphone.



On the left side there are no controls. The case is only 11.6 mm thick, and it is in the thickest part, and in the thinnest one is only 6 mm. This thickness does not cause negative sensations.



On the upper side there is a 3.5 mm audio jack and a second microphone for noise cancellation. Commendable, given that the model budget.



The back surface is matte, without textures, the coating is made of soft-touch "soft-touch" plastic. Fingerprints and other marks the surface collects on itself rather abundantly, but they are erased easily. It looks beautiful on the back side, one dimple with a logo is worth something!



On the back of the device are located: a camera, a flash and a cut where the speakerphone is located.



The case of the Moto G is not monolithic: the back cover of the smartphone is removable, only in which case the battery can not be changed. And the yellow inscription warns us about this.



Under the cover you can also find a slot for microSIM-card, it is on the side. As you can see, the slot is named SIM 1, so it is logical that Motorola has released a version with support for two SIM cards. Now this version is sold only in Brazil, Australia, Singapore, Hong Kong, Malaysia, India, and so on. The timing of the appearance of a smartphone on the world market is not specified by the company.



As for the convenience of the smartphone, the Moto G due to the curved backrest is perfectly in the hand and is easily controlled. Of course, a smartphone can not be called thin by modern standards, because it is 3.01 mm thicker than the Nexus 5, but this does not play any role in the portability of the smartphone. The device impresses with its design and build quality - it seems as if you are holding Moto X in your hands.

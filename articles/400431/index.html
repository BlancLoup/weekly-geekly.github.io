How IBM Watson can change healthcare in the near future



IBM Watson is a cognitive system that helps conduct business, make weather forecasts, come up with original recipes for dishes, heal people. In fact, the service is used in much more spheres than indicated, but in this material we would like to touch on medicine.

So, Watson is gradually becoming an indispensable assistant to doctors and patients. But all this is no match for what Watson can do in the health sector in the near future. What exactly? Let's try to imagine, and not just fantasize - everything that is said in the sequel is quite realistic, and probably will not be realized in the not too distant future.

Watson is already working as a digital concierge at Alder Hey Children's Hospital in Liverpool. Soon, doctors want to teach the system to advise children on their diagnosis and upcoming procedures.

Providing detailed and relevant information about the patient to the attending physician. One of the key points in healthcare is the availability of a wealth of information about diseases and medical experiments. A person is simply unable to orient himself in all this information space, no matter how highly qualified a specialist he is.

Creating effective drugs. IBM Watson can help create new drugs by speeding up the development process. For example, the usual way to create a new medicine from scratch takes developers about 12 years. IBM Watson is able to increase the chances of getting an effective medicine while reducing the time of its introduction into circulation. Work on the creation of new drugs is already underway.

In particular, specialists from IBM Research have developed a macromolecule, an organic compound that allows you to fight against viruses that are resistant to traditional antiviral drugs. The molecule was developed by a joint team of IBM Research and the Singapore Institute of Bioengineering and Nanotechnology ( IBN ).

The new macromolecule contains three components that are able to suppress viruses. One component uses electrostatic coupling to block the source of viruses. In this case, the virus simply cannot infect healthy cells. The second tool helps to change the pH inside infected cells, which makes it harder for viruses to reproduce. And finally, the molecule contains mannose, an isomer of glucose, which enters the healthy cells of the immune system and provides a more effective fight against viruses, directing them to fight infection.

At the same time, Watson helps reduce the drug resistance of cancer cells.

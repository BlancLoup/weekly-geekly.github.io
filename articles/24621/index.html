Run Google News, Google Apps and 16 language support for iPhone and iPod touch

The official Google Mobile blog announces the launch of Google support for the iPhone and iPod touch by Google News and Apps services on May 1. In addition, the Google search page, adapted for iPhone and iPod touch, launched in January, now supports 16 languages.


Google News for iPhone will load automatically when it hits the home page of the service. Now, users of these devices will see the full-featured top page of Google News, get an improved search interface and the ability to view YouTube‚Äôs embedded videos if they go along with the news.

If you are a Google Apps user, now you can use your domain name also on the iPhone. To quickly go to one click it is necessary to make a bookmark like www.google.com/m/a/YOURDOMAIN , replacing the value YOURDOMAIN, respectively.

Google for iPhone today provides fairly convenient access to the main services of the search giant. This includes quick and convenient one-touch access to the results of news, images or web pages with support for prompting search phrases during typing. Emails from Gmail are automatically downloaded, which does not require updating the browser page, plus auto-completion of email addresses during input. In addition to all this, Google for iPhone provides an adapted service of Google Calendar and Picasa Web Albums.

Wrist Heart Rate Monitor Alpha Mio


Calling MIO Alpha a simple clock with a pulse measurement will not turn the tongue. Those people who do not just run around the stadium in the evening have an understanding of cardio workouts and know that good cardio monitoring has three characteristics: it is constant, accurate and comfortable.
Before MIO Alpha, heart rate monitoring was able to ‚Äúchoose two out of three‚Äù - either the clothespin on the finger, which constantly measures the pulse, but easily flies during the run, or a watch with a contact sensor that cannot measure the pulse continuously - to do this you need to stop and put your fingers on the sensor, or a two-component system - a monitor on the wrist and a chest sensor, which delivers a lot of inconvenience.

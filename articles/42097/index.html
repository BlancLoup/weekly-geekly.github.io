Beeline broadcasts a mobile base station

On October 8, VimpelCom (Beeline TM) held a press conference in Moscow, at which it presented a new type of equipment for providing communications in crowded places - a mobile base station (MBS).

A mobile base station is a set of radio transmitters mounted on a KAMAZ truck. When deployed, it forms a standard cellular radio telephone network of the GSM 900/1800 standard and UMTS 2100, providing uninterrupted radio communication to subscribers in conditions of insufficient capacity of fixed cellular networks.

inside the video about the performance characteristics of this beast
Mobile base station on KAMAZ chassis

According to Nikolai Nifontov, technical director of VimpelCom for the Moscow region, MBS is completely autonomous (the complex includes a diesel electric station), can use radio relay, laser and satellite equipment to connect to a cellular network, and its deployment time does not exceed an hour. The station adds approximately 200 additional voice channels to the network capacity.

BFM.Ru



Many companies in the wake of the global and local financial crisis lay off employees, close offices, curtail unprofitable and even balance-neutral initiatives. However, there are also projects that, in the spirit of general interest in the economy in particular, and in objective information, in general, on the contrary, are only emerging. One of them is the BFM.Ru website officially opened on the night of November 11-12 .
First of all, the abbreviation, of course, tells us that this resource is directly connected to Business FM radio, however, for her, a separate decoding was invented by Business For Me. I, as a person not burdened with serious knowledge in journalism and media, I can only say about the content of the site that all data are presented purely from the turn of information for reflection - unobtrusively and as fully as possible.

But personally, I would like to tell in more detail not about the content (which, of course, not much yet), but about the form, that is, about the site itself.


First of all, I would like to note that the site structure is very flexible - almost any information blocks (and if you believe promises, then soon any) can be moved closer or further to the top of the page based on your own interests.


Moreover, you can even add a unique news block from any site using your favorite RSS feed.

It is very obvious and convenient, and at the same time, a currency converter, one of the elements of the additional panel of various plates, is implemented quite powerfully in terms of functionality.


Plates can be collapsed, expanded, supplemented and trimmed.


In the same sidebar, the weather forecast block is available for any city you are interested in.


Without social elements even on a site with an editor and far from user-generated content in 2008, of course, it is impossible to live. On BFM.Ru there are "recommendations" and comments. Moreover, comments are not moderated and are available for adding here and now.


All materials are carefully ottedovany for the most convenient filling of gaps of knowledge and the possibility of studying stories from beginning to end, for those who do not like buttons (or just for those who do not use Firefox), an obvious font size adjustment scale has been made. Hitherto, I have seen similar only on some large and cumbersome German sites.


In general, the site is really made interesting, and even static (in other words, it does not stretch, not rubber) of the central column does not spoil the overall impression.

Good luck to developers and journalists.

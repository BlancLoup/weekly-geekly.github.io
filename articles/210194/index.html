Ross & Moor RM-997 Review: Huawei UltraStick Tablet

Recently, Huawei introduced the Huawei Ultrastick technology, which will allow equipping most of the tablets in the Chinese market with 3G support.

Many companies have taken up the production of devices with Ultra Stick technology, but the only model from Ross & Moor is officially represented on the Russian market.
A detailed review of the tablet Ross & Moor RM-997 - under the cut.



Device characteristics
  • Screen: 9.7 ", 2048x1536 288 dpi
  • Processor: Four-core RockChip RK3188 based on ARM Corex A9, 1.6 GHz
  • Video Accelerator: Mali 400
  • Operating system: Android 4.2.2 (Jelly Bean)
  • RAM: 2 GB
  • User Memory: 8 GB
  • Camera: front - 0.3 megapixel, rear -2 megapixel
  • Battery: 9500 mAh
  • Optional: Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, accelerometer, gyroscope, indicator, 3G Huawei ULtra Stick



The parameters of the modem Huawei UltraStick 3G:
  • HSPA / WCDMA: 900/2100 MHz
  • EDGE: 900/1800 MHz
  • Data transfer rate: HSDPA 14.4 Mbps, HSUPA: 5.76 Mbps
  • Modem weight <30 grams
  • Connector Size: 65 x 35 x 3.3 mm.



Equipment


The device package is quite standard for tablets:
  • Cable for connecting to a computer format microUSB-USB
  • 2.5 A / 5V charger with USB output
  • Cable to recharge the device
  • OTG cable for connecting external drives
  • Cable for connecting Ultra Stick modem to computer
  • Ultra Stick Modem
  • Napkin
  • Headphones




Appearance


Externally, the device has a standard for 10 inch tablets design that is difficult to distinguish from the iPad, even from less than 3 meters.


The back panel is metallic golden. At the top of the tablet is a plastic insert which has a slot under the Ultra Stick.


And in the lower part there are 2 speakers and a 2MP camera. The very location of the speakers is very doubtful, because sometimes when watching movies the hand closes the speakers and you have to turn the device over.


Charging connector, micro USB and headphone jack were grouped at the end of the tablet in its left side, next to the power button, microphone and slot for a memory card.

The front panel contains a 0.3 megapixel camera and a light sensor. The frame around the screen is white and quite wide.

Screen


The tablet Ross & Moor RMD-997 has a 10-touch high-definition display with a resolution of 2048x1536.
A ballad about viewing angles and juicy colors will be left for marketers.


Of the features I would like to note the possibility of switching the screen resolution through the settings. So, if you want a lower resolution, but for the same money, then just open the screen settings and switch the resolution to 1600x1200.


I did not notice much difference, although I said the same thing when I compared the screens of the iPad and the iPad 3. So, I think, for some, the difference will be noticeable.

Memory


Built-in memory is only 8Gb. Quite a strange decision to release a tablet with such a small amount of memory, but this problem can be solved by purchasing the 32GB MicroSD memory card. The blessing their cost in the market does not exceed 1000 rubles. The read speed of the internal memory is 10.6 MB / s, and the write speed is 11.2.


The memory itself is distributed in a standard way for Android devices. For example, 1.6 GB is allocated for applications, and a little more than 4 GB is available to the user.


Multimedia


The Ross & Moor RM-997 tablet reproduced all popular formats mkv, avi, mp4, wmv through a standard player without any problems


But for 2K and 4K video, I had to use the MX player.


Working hours


The tablet running time was impressive. The device was discharged from 97% to 16%, playing video in MP4 1080P format, in 8 hours and 15 minutes. That is, the battery should be enough for 9-10 hours of video viewing.

Huawei UltraStick



The tablet is equipped with a HUAWEI E122-2 modem, made using UltraStick technology. This technology was introduced on August 26 by HUAWEI.
According to the company in the future we should expect Ultrastick with 4G support. So it will be enough to purchase an additional modem, and not completely change the tablet. How it will be implemented at the software level is not entirely clear, since the RK3188 chip does not have 4G LTE hardware support
By the way, modems are not the only way to use this technology. In the future, it is planned to complete the device with RAM, a GPS receiver or, for example, two sim cards.

Mobile Testing.


According to the manufacturer, the modem can issue up to 14 Mbps for download, but it was not possible to achieve such results. The maximum speed given by the tablet did not exceed 5.5 Mb / s, which is also not a bad indicator.
In order to start working with a mobile network, it is enough to place a standard sim card in the corresponding slot on the modem, and insert the modem into the tablet.

After the manipulations done, the sim card is determined almost immediately, but using the Internet will only happen after 1 minute. The device does not need to reboot.


For the test, I used the Megaphone SIM card with the basic Internet tariff "All Inclusive" and MTC with the "Super Bit" tariff

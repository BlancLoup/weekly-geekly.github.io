10 fabulous speeches by Steve Jobs


Last week, something happened that I wanted to postpone for as long as possible, but that was still inevitable. The most innovative entrepreneur of America, and maybe the world, Steve Jobs left the post of CEO of Apple.
Some luckies in life are given the chance to work on one revolutionary device. Steve Jobs - a man who made several revolutions in the digital world, - can rightly be considered a successful man.
This article contains 10 of the most famous and symbolic speeches that characterize the life and career of the master.

1. First episode - Steve Jobs introduces the first Apple Macintosh computer in the world, 1984


Pay attention to the reaction of the public. At that moment, what Steve was showing seemed fantastic.


2. 1984 Apple Macintosh Advertising




3. Steve Jobs announces collaboration with Microsoft in Boston, 1997


In this talk, Steve announces Apple‚Äôs collaboration with Microsoft. The audience plunges into chaos and indignation. Steve is calm, he only thinks about how to bring Apple products to a new level, how to provide users with the best products available at the time.
Looking at this presentation, you understand that there are wars only between fans of companies, while the leaders of empires are always ready for cooperation and mutually beneficial deals. Steve Jobs and Bill Gates - bravo!


4. First iMac Presentation


iMac was the first product to set the standard that Apple follows to this day. The iMac computer model developed by Jonathan Yves was elegant, modern and affordable. It was not just the Macintosh of the 90s, it was something really new.


5. Presentation of the first iPod, 2001


About 10 years ago, Apple introduced the iPod. This device has changed not only Apple in the eyes of users, but also the way people listen to music on the planet, and the music industry as a whole.
All the advantages of an iPod can be described in one phrase: 1,000 songs in your pocket. This simple phrase has become a slogan that makes Apple stand out among the few competitors at the time.


6. Steve Jobs Introduces First ITunes Phone


After the success of the iPod in 2003-2004, rumors began to spread about the iPod phone. The first attempt was made by Motorola, presenting the Rokr phone, which used iTunes functionality. But the device did not become super-popular, even Steve Jobs hesitated to control when demonstrating the device.


7. Opening Speech by Steve Jobs at Stanford University 2005


I think this is the most popular performance of Steve. In it, he more than anyone else describes the life difficulties he had to face in order to achieve the fulfillment of his dream. This speech is incredibly motivating and inspiring.


8. Presentation to the public of the new device - iPhone


Personally, I think that this presentation should be viewed by any person who has to perform on stage. Steve Jobs is just a phenomenal speaker, and you can be sure of that by looking at any of his speeches, but in this episode he does something incredible with the public. He plays the whole show so that the viewer himself can go step by step through the evolution that Jobs himself has already gone through. He does not give ready-made answers and solutions, he allows everyone to become a part of the show.


9. Steve Jobs and Bill Gates


Steve Jobs and Bill Gates - the two most influential people in the personal computer industry - are interviewed on channel D5. The transfer was very interesting. Both company leaders behave very decently and constantly use their excellent sense of humor.


10. Steve Jobs Presents iPad


In this presentation, Steve Jobs demonstrates a new type of device - something between a phone and a laptop. No, not a netbook. The netbook is a primitive and cheap laptop. iPad is a new way to interact with the web, read books and watch videos.



Steve Jobs is one of the few who left a mark on history and completely changed the computer industry, who showed how much you can be dedicated and love work, how interesting and exciting you can make presentations of your products.

Bravo, Steve!

The new service will allow you to talk with the authors of blogs live.

The young company Jaxtr , founded a little over a year ago, starts testing a new service, thanks to which subscribers of cellular and landline telephone lines will be able to communicate live with the authors of pages in blogs and social networks.

The principle of the service is as follows. First, the page author, who wants to receive voice calls from visitors, needs to download a special application from the Jaxtr website. This application allows you to place on the page a link indicating the possibility of organizing a telephone connection. Subsequently, the visitor, by clicking on the link, receives an offer to score your phone number. Next, the Jaxtr service establishes a connection between two subscribers by automatically calling back to the number of the visitor of a blog or a page on a social network. At the same time, the author of the page should also have a landline or mobile phone, although its number will be classified for the caller.

It is noteworthy that the first time you click on the Jaxtr link, the Internet user will be offered a special number for direct communication with the Jaxtr subscriber without the need to use a computer. Moreover, this number allows you to make calls to bypass international service providers, providing cost savings in negotiations. Now "bypass" numbers are available for 29 countries.
Jaxtr subscribers will be able to determine from whom they want to receive calls and block annoying subscribers. Jaxtr link can also be placed in emails. Callers, in turn, will be able to leave voice messages to service subscribers who are currently unavailable. By the way, callers will not have to register with the Jaxtr system and download any software.
The new service is currently compatible with MySpace, Tagged, Friendster, Hi5, Xanga, and Blogger web services. Testing is conducted in private, and later on the first subscribers will be able to invite their friends to Jaxtr. Jaxtr plans to receive revenues through advertising and the provision of additional paid services.

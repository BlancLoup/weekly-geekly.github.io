Fuel cells as one of the sources of energy for the data center



We, KingServers, have been following the work of Bloom Energy for a long time. The fact is that it offers an interesting option for supplying the data center with energy. Namely - fuel cells on biogas. Clients of this company use proprietary technology in their fuel cells, which allows to obtain energy from atmospheric air and biogas. Waste - water and a very small amount of carbon dioxide.

Many telecommunication companies are going to meet their customers, who express their wishes to abandon the traditional energy supply in favor of "green" energy. Why this happens is a different question, but the trend is becoming increasingly noticeable. This is not always possible, but a number of hardware developers went to meet such wishes.

Of course, for the sake of its own reputation and the original commercial company can go to great lengths. But there is another reason. The fact is that biogas in many regions is a much cheaper source of energy than all other sources. In particular, in California many companies switched to biogas, abandoning traditional sources.

The fuel elements of Bloom Energy are used in North Carolina by Apple, Ebay, CenturyLink.



Now, Bloom Energy has decided to combine efforts with Power Secure solutions. It offers customers Power Secure Energy Storage Systems. As a result, customers receive universal solutions that can be used in the most inaccessible regions. You can use the solution from Bloom energy in the most difficult situations. The fact is that such a system has a modular architecture, plus software that tries to optimize the operation of the station. As a result, customers of a data center with such a power supply scheme achieve strong savings, which would otherwise be associated with the use of electricity.

Among other tasks, both companies provide the following:

  • Development and provision of power supply for all elements of distributed infrastructure;
  • Ability to work with loads of different types;
  • The level of reliability of data centers meets the requirements from the minimum to the needs of the most important elements of the infrastructure;
  • Achieving the required level of reliability: from the minimum to the needs of critical infrastructure elements;
  • Work with local fuel cell based generating facilities. According to some experts, the new car can provide effective work of sellers.

In addition, the developers aim to ensure, first of all, the provision of high-quality power supply to the distributed infrastructure elements;
Service of any degree of employment;
The need to achieve a level of reliability not lower than Tier III. There are plans to increase the level of protection of the system through the installation of solutions.

Now both companies are already taking pre-orders from customers. And the orders are not single. Soon it is planned to release the Bloom energy fuel cells from the finished warm water.

Bloom Energy and partners are already taking orders from potential customers. Bloom Energy offers this and other solutions to reduce data center costs, both direct and indirect.

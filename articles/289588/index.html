Lithuania as a top destination for Russian startups

The recent years have become extraordinary for the development of Lithuanian startup ecosystems. Local projects are growing, but even more attention of the business community is attracted by the redeployment of Russian business to Lithuania. Lithuania shows itself as an attractive business environment for many well-known Russian companies, and this is not by chance.

The last year was very significant for the largest platform Game Insight . The company moved its head office from Moscow to Vilnius. And also 15 more gaming companies from Russia and the CIS countries have chosen Vilnius. This business migration has had a huge impact on many Russian start-ups looking for investment opportunities and a good strategic location. A really favorable IT environment is what makes Lithuania attractive for foreign business. In the ranking of countries with favorable conditions for the business start ‚ÄúDoing Business‚Äù, Lithuania ranked 11th and far ahead of its closest competitors. Vilnius is considered the most active start-up platform. There are hundreds of themed events that bring together an energetic start-up community. Easy moving


Russian companies that have moved to Lithuania (GameInsight, Charlie Oscar) receive consulting support from leading Lithuanian institutions ( Invest Lithuania , StartupHighway ), assistance from law firms (SORAINEN) in transferring business, obtaining an EU Blue Card.

According to Invest Lithuania , the EU Blue Card is a special work and residence permit for highly qualified professionals; it also extends its effect to the applicant's close relatives. To obtain an EU Blue Card, a non-EU citizen must have a higher education, be employed by a Lithuanian company under a contract, with a salary exceeding the average salary from Lithuania at least 2 times. If the salary exceeds the average by three times or more, then the Blue Card can be issued under an accelerated procedure. To date, the average salary in Lithuania is approximately 700 euros.

In addition to receiving a Blue card, there are other possibilities for moving to Lithuania, such as a resident's permit based on working in a group, permanent work permit, permission based on management & investment, etc. Permission based on investment can be issued to a non-EU citizen who invested at least ‚Ç¨ 14,000 a Lithuanian company with a capital of 28,000 euros or more, employed at least 3 local employees and has been operating for at least six months (additional requirements may apply). In addition, there are many opportunities for obtaining residency permits for private businesses in need of development (of various start-ups), says Vite Danilechute , head of the SORAINEN Communications, Media & Technology (CMT) Sector Group in Lithuania.

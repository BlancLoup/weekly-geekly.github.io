New in 2013 already in Russia

Last Friday in Moscow, specifically for representatives of the IT-communities and digital editions, a closed presentation of new products in the field of TV and audio, in general Home Entertainment, was held.
I, as a habrary editor of the LG blog, took part in this event, and I want to share with you my impressions of the new LG products, which I managed to not only see, but also touch.




Of course, the main characters of the event were the 2013 LG Cinema 3D TVs. This year, the picture quality has become even better thanks to the new XD engine chipset. 3D is now not only an image, but also a sound, even without an assistive audio technology, although below I will tell a little more about a home theater with an incredible sound quality. And there is a special mention of the design of the new TV line, this year the framework has become even thinner, even in the most budget models.
I will write more about LG Cinema 3D 2013 in a separate review material, and today I would like to talk about several unique products that were presented at this event: a huge 3D TV with a diagonal of 84 inches and an incredible 4k (2160p) resolution, weightless OLED -TV (55 inches) and a new Blu-ray 3D home theater with a nine-channel sound (i.e., the sound is also 3D).

Let's start, perhaps, with the most interesting - with a TV, the cost of which exceeds some cars. In the showroom, he was hidden under a silk curtain, and it was difficult to imagine the diagonal of the device, but the fabric slipped off his body easily, and ... you know, the effect was amazing. Negotiating journalists and bloggers who have seen many different high-tech gizmos, immediately silenced and froze.



A thin frame, an elegant stand and a truly impressive-sized display with an amazingly clear image. Especially for 84LM960V (this is the name of this model), ULTRA HD content was prepared, which most fully revealed all the charms of the display. In terms of technology and quality of user electronics, the product is certainly a success.

The second impressive result of the creativity of South Korean engineers - ultra-thin 55-inch OLED TV, presented a few minutes later.

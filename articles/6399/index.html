To as closely as possible bring the result of the issue to the expectations of the search, the creators of search engines have come up with several tricks.
Link popularity - the total amount of external links that mention your page. The search engine thinks that the more sites on the network link to the document or domain on which it is located, the more important and authoritative the document.

There are several types of quantitative expression of link popularity - PageRank (Google), VIC and TCI (Yandex), IC (Aport).

Citation Index (IC) - the number of sites that link to this site. Links from sites located on free hosting, links from guest books, forums, and a number of directories are excluded from the total number of domains. Updated weekly.

Weighted citation index (VIC) of Yandex, taking into account the number of pages that link to the site, and the own VIC of these pages. Calculated for each page in the database of Yandex. Updated twice a week. It is one of the factors affecting the position of the page in Yandex search results.

Thematic Vic (CY) is a weighted Yandex citation index, calculated taking into account the correspondence between the themes of your site and the sites that link to your site. The algorithm is not disclosed.
PageRank (PR) is a measure of the ‚Äúimportance‚Äù of a page, according to Google search engine data. PR depends on the number of external links to this page and on their quality (in turn, the number of external links to referring pages).

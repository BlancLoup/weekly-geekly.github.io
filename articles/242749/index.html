The mechanics of informal communication of C # developers at the conference

Good afternoon, readers of Habr!

In order to emphasize the value of informal communication, we first share the opinion of how developer conferences affect the performance of the C # ecosystem and the personal income of each developer individually.

In Moscow, C # has an established labor market with a resistance level of 120-130,000 and a conditional ceiling of 180,000 rubles. At these levels, an adequate supply of quality labor with a limited number of positions. Soon this market will be redrawn due to changes in the dollar. How to quickly reach these levels and even overcome them?

To do this, it is important to understand that success in the labor market depends primarily on the combination of skills, and not on the depth of ownership of any one issue. It is important which way you choose, and not how fast you go. How to know where to go? Do I need to overtake colleagues or everyone can earn more?




C # cluster and developer conferences


The set of C # developers, companies, tools, open source projects is one interconnected cluster. And the success of the element inside, i.e. individual developer, is determined by the effectiveness of the cluster as a whole. The cluster works the more efficiently, the more communication and coordination occurs within the developer environment. If you share problems and solutions, combine to solve similar problems, the ecosystem will be more efficient and it is more profitable for everyone.

The development audience has a lot of needs: find out about updates, see a solution to a complex technical problem, find an employee or a job. There are many thematic niches and formats. At one extreme of the formats are conferences that broadcast content, and at the other are unconferences, in which the schedule and schedule are created by participants at the event itself. Events vary in level of training and volume of marketing. But no format can satisfy everyone. You can not combine the novelty of the conference DevCon and the solution of current practical problems. It is difficult to combine informal communication and deep technical reports. Therefore, we believe that there should be more different events.

In the Russian .Net world there are few events, but they are:
  • DevCon is a large conference from Microsoft, held annually, usually in May in the Moscow region and is usually devoted to technical innovations.
  • various events from Microsoft, for example the Russian App Day , which will be November 21
  • DotNetConf - regional conference on .Net in Chelyabinsk, which began to meet in 2010
  • DotNext - was held in St. Petersburg in April 2014 and will soon be held in Moscow on December 8, 2014, a ‚Äúhardcore‚Äù conference dedicated to the insides of the CLR, working with memory, profiling and performance
  • GoSharp - thematic conferences with short presentations and an emphasis on informal communication (November 15 is a day of personal effectiveness, and December 12 is a team leader day)

You can often hear C # developers at regional conferences, as well as large SECR conferences or mobile development events.

The more different events will take place from different organizers - the stronger will be the exchange of experience within the C # ecosystem. We are convinced that coordination between the organizers and the planning of such events will be useful for the community as a whole and will allow closing the spectrum of needs completely.

One of the upcoming events takes place with the information support of Luxoft and the participation of one of our trainers, Andrei Gordienkov violettape , who is a member of the organizing team and speaker in the Effective Code Writing section. We would like to share information about the mechanics of networking at such an event and ask for your advice. Further, the story goes on behalf of the organizers.

Informal communication on GoSharp


In order not only to hear ‚Äúwhere to dig‚Äù from the scene, but also to ask colleagues again, informal communication will be organized. It allows:
  • exchange information about tasks and corporate culture in companies
  • junior developers to ask mentors for advice
  • project managers and startups - to find people in a team for permanent and remote work
  • Startup managers - to build connections with colleagues who also have their own projects
  • just chat with colleagues to broaden your horizons

Often acquaintances begin with discussions in small groups around the speakers after the presentations, yes, or simply when sitting next to each other are exchanged with a couple of phrases. In order to have more piles, the number of speakers and experts is increased and they will be presented to the audience from the very beginning and involved in the discussion. The number and duration of breaks is increased - there will be five such time zones with a total duration of 3 hours.
But what is more important - a forced networking will be organized for a part of the audience. How exactly?
Four tools are stocked:
  • tags on cocktail tables (we can supply 30-40 pieces)
  • speed networking (10 tables)
  • Networking moderators who will work in the hall
  • transfer of discussions to negotiation.

Tags on cocktail tables - we have not seen this before at other events, so, of course, this is an experiment with a certain amount of risk. But surely many of you were annoyed that you could not find a colleague with similar interests at the conference. Therefore, why not try this method?



At each cocktail table there is a tag with a tag or name that indicates a sign of grouping. Then you can drink coffee with people who share your interests. For example, WPF, WinForms, Silverlight, EF Code First, SQL Server. Signs, Luxoft Alumni, Kaspersky Alumni or "C # in the regions" are also possible.

What would we like? To the arrangement of tables forced communication. For example, you can tables "freelancers", "looking for a contractor", "CTO" close to each other. Some tables let them gather the same participants. Some of them are suppliers and consumers of a certain type. How best to do it from a mathematical point of view?

Regarding discussions in negotiation. In them we will transfer conversations with speakers after the sections, if the discussion drags on and it is time to start the next section. Many speakers promised us to show their applications in a physical implementation - on various mobile devices that they specifically bring for the demonstration.
The speed-deating format will make it possible to establish such connections: the customer is a freelancer, the employer is a developer. Junior and Middle developers will be able to ask advice from those who are experts or simply work in a company with a strong development culture. And another format - dinner-afterparty.

Junior System Administrator


Many may wonder what the younger means. We put into this concept:
  • Independent administration of a small network (about 10 machines) or assistance in administering a large network (more than 100 machines).
  • Hardware maintenance (printers, mice, keyboards, etc.)
  • Advising users on simple questions.

Windows system administrator


The typical, average system administrator with some experience. Duties include:
  • Service and support of the park of cars (more than 30 machines), working under control of the Windows operating system.
  • Maintenance and support of server systems (up to 10 servers) running Windows Server.
  • Ability to deploy, administer, restore one or more DBMS: MS SQL Server, Oracle, PostgresSQL, etc.

Lead Windows System Administrator


Experienced system administrator with experience from 3 to 5 years. Compared to their smaller brothers, the leading system administrator performs an order of magnitude more various tasks, including:
  • Service and support of server systems (over 10 servers) running Windows Server and * nix.
  • Deployment, administration, backup, recovery of one or several DBMS (MS SQL Server, Oracle, PostgreSQL ...).
  • Creation, management and support of cluster, high-load and fault-tolerant systems.

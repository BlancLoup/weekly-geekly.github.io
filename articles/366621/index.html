Deleting Facebook usernames in Opera browser

Deleting a login to log in to Facebook in Opera browser is done in the drop-down menu of logins by simply clicking on the cross opposite the username entered in error. Of course, you need to make a distinction between the concepts of login and password. Facebook passwords in Opera are deleted in the Browser Settings in the ‚ÄúSecurity‚Äù section. But the function of deleting logins is not explicitly there. A lot of materials and links are devoted to password removal on the network, but deleting only logins is not enough, or the described actions affect the operations with clearing the cache and cookies. Of course, a simple action to remove a login right away in the drop-down menu is convenient, but not everyone knows about this feature and traditionally goes to look for the required functionality in the depths of the settings of the Opera browser.



How are extra logins generated? Everything is very simple, each user of social networks and forums has a lot of logins and passwords and he sometimes automatically enters them into the window of another portal. The browser remembers the logins automatically and if you do not clean this field, then a whole ‚Äúelevator‚Äù of unnecessary logins can accumulate. Deleting logins is also necessary from the point of view of security, since sometimes you can mistakenly enter a password in the login field, which will be saved in the browser along with a one-word login. Naturally, no one wants to show the password and such an erroneous login-password needs to be deleted.

We hope that the material will be useful and will save the users time to search for the obvious at first glance solution.

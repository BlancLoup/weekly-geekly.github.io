In 2014, 720,000 Android Wear devices were shipped, and Motorola became the market leader in smart watches.

Google rolled out the operating system for wearable Android Wear gadgets in March last year, and the first devices with it on board appeared in June. These were the LG G Watch and Samsung Gear Live. For six months, 720,000 devices were shipped on this version of Android - quite a bit if compared to sales of smartphones , but compared to other smart devices, this is a good result.



Analytical Agency Canalys published a report on the supply of wearable gadgets in 2014. A total of 4.6 million smart watches and bracelets were shipped. Of these, 720 thousand - Android Wear gadgets. Motorola has become a clear leader among device manufacturers. An example of Motorola's watch leadership shows the importance of a stylish look: Moto 360 is visually very different from its futuristic and square counterparts.



Pebble on December 31 reported on deliveries of millions of smart watches in the period from launch in 2013 to the end of 2014. From March to December 2014, the manufacturer shipped 600,000 hours of Pebble . Fitbit remained the leader in the bracelet market.

Samsung, as the most prolific company , has released six wearable devices in 14 months on different platforms. And Xiaomi again chose its own path and entered the market with the Mi Band , a simple and inexpensive bracelet. One day, 103,000 devices were sold.

In April, Apple Watch should be on sale. If Apple can sell at least several million devices in the first year, this gadget could become the most popular on the market.

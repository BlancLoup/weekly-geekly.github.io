Computer science: what to learn in school

Is the school capable of giving systematic knowledge, or can you become a good specialist only at a university? Do I need to learn in school fast printing? Which PL should be chosen for computer science lessons? Having tried to answer these questions from the topic , I realized that the answer would not fit in the comment.

1. A school in principle can provide “fairly systematic” knowledge - to train specialists. Unless, of course, in this judgment we oppose a school to a university, we understand all secondary education institutions as “school”, including all kinds of “lyceums”, “gymnasiums”, and “colleges”. You can only become a good specialist in practice - it is impossible to acquire enough skills of a good specialist in absentia, even in a “good university”.

2. At school, you need to learn not to type on a PC quickly, but type blindly , so as not to waste attention on your keys, mental energy. The speed here will be acquired later - when working with specific texts. After all, we do not even know in advance what language the graduate will need to then develop this speed. A lot also depends on the subject area: the mind and fingers memorize not "letters in general", but the specific, most frequent combinations of letters, words, typical expressions of speech.

How to save using smart home technology: thermostats and lighting control

The residence on Elton Road in Miami Beach is a house of 300 square meters and valued at 2.2 million dollars, which not only saves energy - it produces it for itself, and at the same time sells surplus to local public utilities.



This piece of architecture has gained platinum status in the LEED rating, a national certification program that supports eco-design and construction standards in the United States.

The smart stuffing at home was organized by a team of engineers from Florida-based MultiMedia Innovations (MMI), headquartered in Miami. Energy savings were organized using the Control4 system, which MMI developed in conjunction with the Florida Green Home Design Group and their SKLARchitecture partners.

As a result, this house, capable of providing itself with energy, received the highest LEED rating to date. Energy saving and optimization are facilitated by lighting the house, which is produced using LED lamps, the use of which gives a large number of LEED glasses, as well as Q-Motion motorized curtains and blinds, Jandy pool water purification system, audio and video sensors and Eragy electricity consumption monitoring system .



It is not difficult to guess that given the stated ability to reproduce energy and the location of the house, it is equipped with solar panels on photocells, which are also controlled by the Eragy and Control4 systems. All information on the operation of the systems is displayed on the touch panel and also on the iPad of the owner of the house.



Energy saving algorithms that provide these systems, maintain an optimal level of comfort in the house and save the owner from unnecessary running around and fuss with the inclusion and adjustment of appliances. Thus, a certain part of the lighting is automatically turned on and off in accordance with astronomical time. The system never turns on the lamps at full power, which in itself saves a lot of energy. Automatic blinds are closed in order to avoid overheating of the room with the bright Florida sun, in connection with which the geothermal cooling system of the house works in moderate mode.

Such a structure is a flagship of green technologies and a technological showroom with a demonstration of the possibilities of modern integration of smart home systems, a masterpiece of engineering thought that is not only expensive, but also not only pays off, but also brings profit - if, of course, maintenance costs and replacement of equipment this profit will not be eaten. But even this will already be good, because the initially set standards of comfort will be maintained in the house thanks to a system that feeds itself.

Although a common masterpiece of engineering and architectural thought from an ordinary user is more likely to remain in dreams, this does not mean at all that we cannot afford the technology of a smart home to save energy in everyday life.

Smart thermostat


This section of the economy is common in the United States, where homes are equipped with climate systems by default. In our country, saving on heating is more difficult in apartment buildings, but at the same time, technology can be introduced into private homes if you know about it in advance and allow for its use.



Previously, the case with smart thermostats was very bad: an unfriendly user interface and the need to set the parameters manually fought off any desire to do so. Now everything has become much more convenient. For example, the NEST thermostat does not just look like an element of an expensive design. For some time, he collects information about your preferences, after which, on the basis of this data, he builds the necessary schedule to maintain a cooler temperature when you sleep or are away from home, and warmer when you are at home.



Surprisingly, it can be connected to the Jawbone UP24 bracelet, which the thermostat will wake you up after the temperature is optimal for waking up. And the technology that Mercedes-Benz uses in its cars can transmit to the thermostat data about when you will be at home, so that it will start to warm or cool the house by your arrival.

By the way, the combination with the Jawbone bracelet is also planned in the new version of iRidium 3.0, the launch of which has already been reported to the developers .



According to manufacturers, this thermostat can save 10-12% of electricity for heating and about 15% for cooling rooms. Algorithms for maintaining temperature levels, as a rule, are created for each specific project located in different geographic areas. If the Smart Home is located on the Mediterranean coast, its requirements for energy saving are very different from the requirements of the house, located, for example, on the Baltic Sea coast. In addition, the requirements of the owner of the house are also purely individual. Someone is comfortable at home at a temperature of +22, someone is hot, someone is cold. All these parameters are closely monitored by NEST and learns those patterns that are optimal for the owner.

Technology


Most thermostats are easily connected via Wi-Fi. However, not all. There is also Z-Wave technology, for which you need an independent hub. When choosing equipment, look at this option, and if you are a beginner, then maybe you should stick with Wi-Fi.

Compatibility


Immediately indicate the range of devices that will be connected to your thermostat and their parameters. Note that if you later replace or add devices, you need the thermostat to still fit into your home's ecosystem. With smartphones, the problems are much less, rather than with the compatibility of devices with each other. See requirements.

Mobile access


All smart thermostats are equipped with this feature. Some of them can work with only one type of operating system - either Android or iOS. Therefore, pay attention to this aspect. If you have, for example, BlackBerry, then, perhaps, unfortunately, you will have to start by changing your gadget in this case.

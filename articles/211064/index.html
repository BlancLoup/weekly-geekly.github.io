How the Guardian newspaper destroyed computers with Edward Snowden files

On June 20, 2013, under the supervision of agents of the Center for Government Communications in the UK, three employees of the Guardian newspaper's London office, using Bulgarians, drills and electric grippers, literally powdered several computers that stored files obtained by Edward Snowden. Yesterday, January 31, for the first time a video was published that captured this absurd act. Despite the fact that Snowden's files are stored in several more places, and journalists from foreign departments of the Guardian, as well as other media with whom Snowden shared information, continue to work with them, the British authorities forced the Guardian to physically destroy several editorial computers.

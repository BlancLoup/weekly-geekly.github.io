New antibiotic opened for the first time in 25 years

The experimental antibiotic "teixobactin" can be a cure for infections that kill hundreds of thousands of people each year: it will help fight antibiotic-resistant Staphylococcus aureus and tuberculosis.



Staphylococcus aureus feels great in the nasal passages and axillary areas of healthy people. Its carriers are from 30% to 50% of children aged 4-6 years and 15-20% of adults. Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus in the United States alone kills more than 20 thousand people a year, about 30% of all patients.

Tuberculosis is a common infectious disease in the world that affects the lungs and other organs. This disease is transmitted from person to person through drops from the pharynx and lungs. One of the most difficult to treat varieties is multidrug-resistant tuberculosis : it does not respond to isoniazid and rifampicin, the two most powerful anti-tuberculosis drugs. In 2012, this species was diagnosed in 450,000 people. According to the World Health Organization, in 2013, 1.5 million people died from tuberculosis.

The problem of treatment is complicated by the fact that bacteria become resistant to antibiotics.

A new antibiotic that can change the pattern of mortality from these diseases, scientists have gotten practically out of the mud : in the course of studying bacteria on the grass fields of New England, Maine. It was obtained as a result of joint work of scientists from Germany, the UK and the USA. Teixobactin was isolated from the bacterium Eleftheria terrae.

Eleftheria terrae


Testing "teixobactin" on laboratory mice showed that it is able to cure an animal from a multi-resistant strain of Staphylococcus aureus, and no side effects were noted. Testing "in vitro", that is, "in glass", showed the ability of the antibiotic to destroy resistant strains of a number of infections, such as the anthrax pathogen, the diarrhea pathogen - clostridia, tuberculosis bacillus and staphylococcus.

With luck, an antibiotic in ampoules will appear by 2019.

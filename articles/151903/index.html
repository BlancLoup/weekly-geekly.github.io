Worldwide IP Surveillance Industry Leader Supported Ivideon



Sweden. The population is two times smaller than it currently lives in Moscow. But this did not prevent them from giving the world Ikea, H & M, Volvo, Saab, Ericsson and Carlson.

And also a wonderful company Axis Communications is from Sweden, or simply Axis . It does not need an introduction for those who are familiar with the IP video surveillance market. At least because in 1996, Axis released the world's first IP camera.



Then it was far from modern analogues. As technologically (analog cameras at that time gave significantly better quality), and by architecture. If now the vast majority of IP cameras use the embedded Linux version on board, then the world's first IP camera contained a very simplified custom implementation of a web server without using Linux. But be that as it may, this was the first and very important step in replacing analog video surveillance with IP. In my opinion, this is comparable with the appearance of the first digital camera, which marked the beginning of the end of the largest Kodak corporation, which stubbornly did not notice the digital revolution in progress. And, for sure, seeing the high cost and low quality of the first ‚Äúsoap cases‚Äù - underestimated the situation. And then, with a capitalization of almost $ 40 billion, it rapidly fell to zero.

Interestingly, the history of the Axis company itself begins in 1984. At first, she specialized in the development of print servers, which she successfully engaged in for more than 10 years. And then, since the release of the first IP camera in 1996, there was a shift towards the then new IP video surveillance market, which was partly created by Axis.



Modern Axis cameras are based on a proprietary chip designed specifically for working with video. This is a symbiosis of processors based on RISC architecture and DSP. By controlling the production of hardware and software, Axis engineers were able to achieve truly unparalleled image quality with maximum compression. Thanks to this, Axis confidently maintains its status as an industry leader, setting the trend for other manufacturers of IP-cameras and participating in the formation of standards.

And now, Axis cameras support working with our Ivideon cloud-based video surveillance service . Together with Axis, we managed to port our module for two generations of ARTPEC-3 and ARTPEC-4 processors used in cameras. So for their work with the cloud, Ivideon no longer needs a computer and an external IP address. Our application installed in the camera independently communicates with one of our data centers and makes the camera available in your Ivideon account or in Android and iOS applications.



Axis cameras have a very high quality motion detector and some video analytics functions. So in the video archive you will see only really important events, and not a huge number of false positives due to the matrix noise and low light. Naturally, Ivideon supports the function of pre-recording, which will allow you to see the moment of occurrence of the event, and not just what happened after it.



Some models have an SD card slot. So videotapes can be stored not only in our cloud, but also duplicated locally, in case of disconnection from the Internet. Thus, it turns out a hybrid and centralized and decentralized surveillance systems.

But the most important thing for the sake of which it is worth stopping your choice on Axis cameras is to stream live video on your website or in your blog using Ivideon . If you want to show a really high-quality image, especially in conditions of changing light, then you are unlikely to find better equipment than what Axis offers.

UPD: A question was asked in the comments. And what exactly is the "integration of Ivideon in camera firmware"?

The vast majority of IP cameras present in the market are poorly adapted to work via the Internet, that is, if you connect such a camera at home / in the office, it will take a lot of effort to watch it, for example, remotely on an iPad. Configure an external IP address on it (often, you need to buy one). If the camera is connected to the router - set the redirection of the dedicated port on the router to the camera. If the camera operates using the RTSP protocol (in fact, all cameras are encoded in H264 or MPEG4), then there is a possibility that it can function only over UDP (not all manufacturers implement over TCP). This means that your provider or other providers may ‚Äúcut‚Äù UDP traffic on their way to you and you will receive not a video, but a set of colored / gray squares. In addition, there is no question about any encryption. All traffic is in clear. And in general, it is not convenient to ‚Äúlog in‚Äù even to 2 cameras by IP address.

Therefore, we have created a special module - an application for IP cameras, which is installed directly into the firmware and serves as a client for our cloud service. After personalization (camera bindings to your personal account), our module itself establishes a secure SSL connection with the nearest datacenter and waits to receive further commands. These can be commands for requesting live video, a list of archived recordings (if there is an SD card in the camera), the archived recordings themselves, receiving information from the built-in motion detection or alarm inputs for recording to a remote archive and others. In fact, this is just an embedded version of Ivideon Server, which is available on our website for Windows, OS X and Linux platforms.

What is the complexity of integration? First of all, to solve this problem, we need the tools and the SDK to build our module for the specific chip used in the camera. Not all manufacturers are ready to provide such tools and access to the camera so that we can install our application in the firmware. Therefore, it is doubly great that the leader of the industry, not only quickly provided everything necessary, but also consulted and helped in every way during the development process. In addition, each manufacturer in its own way implements the interface of interaction with a motion detector built into the camera and for obtaining additional information. We needed to write an implementation for Axis in accordance with the documents they also provided.

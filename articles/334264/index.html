We read, listen, use. Guide on the sources for the development of Android-developer

A couple of weeks ago, I published a development roadmap for iOS developers . Now, as promised, - a similar selection of resources for those who work with Android. The important point is that there are many different sources, but I chose the ones that I constantly read myself, and what I find useful. So, regular mailings, blogs (personal, collective and in video format), podcasts, live chat rooms in Russian, telegram channels and fire events are all here, under the cut.



Blogs


Android Developers Blog
News from the heart of the Googleplex, warmed up by the California sun.

Google developers
Blog on the medium: written for engineers, written by engineers. Materials are written or supervised by Google employees, but this is not the company's voice, as one might think, but personal opinions of specialists. Google developers already read 30,000 subscribers from around the world.

Android stuff on news.realm.io
Many useful axes can be found on the Realm website under the corresponding tag. Here are regularly laid out records of reports on current topics. There are transcripts and notes in English.

Androiddev on reddit
Perhaps Reddit needs no introduction. The androiddev stream has 70,000 subscribers, so be sure to at least occasionally look in the top - there is the best of the best.

AndroidPub
Team blog with a good editorial and authoring team. 17,000 subscribers, a decent frequency of updates (almost every day), nice layout.

Androidhive
Blog developer from India. Many articles about material design, UI and UX. Updated irregularly.

Styling android
Blog Mark Alison , a developer for Android with more than seven years of experience. What is he talking about? Architectural components, reviews of updates in terms of design, backgrounds, scripts. Everything for those who love to be beautiful.

Android in faces
Vlog about android development. Approaches, solutions practices - all this in a conversational format of interviews with well-known developers around the world.

Telegram channels


Android Good Reads
The most interesting articles, videos and news related to Android-development, directly to the screen of your Android-smartphone. No more than three materials per day. The channel has 850 subscribers.

Android ResId
News and resources for android developers. Tags: android, testing, AndroidStudio, Java8, performance, Architecture, Kotlin, MultiWindow, RxJava, MVP, MVVM. 1350 followers.

pro.JVM
JVM and Android developer channel. Select bytecode and 1000 fans.

Newsletters


Android weekly
If you are an android developer and are still not subscribed to this newsletter, immediately correct this misunderstanding. A weekly portion of articles, tutorials, videos, podcasts and job offers - in short, all the latest information about Android from around the world, carefully selected by the community.

Kotlin weekly
This is a weekly dose of Kotlin, which will come to you in the mail. Community-supported newsletter with guides, new features, libraries, videos, and everything that might be interesting to those who use Kotlin. Recently, the anniversary 50th edition.

#ANDROIDDEV DIGEST
As the author of the digest announces, this is a ‚Äúhandmade mailing list‚Äù. And indeed, there are materials from corporate blogs, and carefully selected thematic posts from major portals, and info about small projects and libraries. In general, the pleasure of reading without searching for sources is already done for us. You can offer and your links.

Podcasts


Android Developers Backstage
Podcast from the creators of Android Developers Blog .

The context
Podcast about android-development from Artem Zinnatullin , Hannes Dorfmann and guests of their studio. Before creating the next issue, a branch is created where you can suggest topics for discussion. And in the comments to the already published material reviews, comments and questions on the topic are published. Updated irregularly.

Podlodka
Weekly podcast about mobile development in general. Architecture, patterns, libraries, development processes in various companies, current news, reviews of events and conferences and, of course, interesting guests, well-known in the community. Authors - Yegor Tolstoy , Stas Tsyganov , Gleb Novik . Frequency: weekly, Mondays.

Fragmented
Podcast on android development from Donna Felker and Kaushik Gopal . Once these guys ‚Äúgot tired of listening to cool podcasts about web and iOS development and made their own - about Android‚Äù. Entries are constantly updated since January 2015. Subscribers report that the guys are worthy of prizes in nominations such as ‚Äúthe best interviewer of the world about Android‚Äù. In general, I agree with them. In the notes inside a lot of useful links.

Android dev
Android Dev is a Russian-language podcast about Android development in all its aspects: from cutting a design to building your own firmware. All issues are themed. Leads podcasts Denis Neklyudov. Guests of the program are developers who remember Android in its Cupcake version.

Talking kotlin
A podcast about Kotlin (and not only) from JetBrains. It comes out twice a month.

Chats


Android Good Talks
Discussion of articles on the Android Good Reads. Disputes, holivary, that's all.

Android Developers - Russian-speaking community
Chatting on Android development, SDK, Kotlin, Realm, etc. 2500 participants.

Pro.jvm
Chat eponymous developer community. There is also a ‚Äúdaughter‚Äù community for beginners. 1700 participants.

kotlinlang.slack.com
Discussion of the Kotlin language in Android. 8600 participants to date. Chat is official and very popular. Invites issue here .

Developments


Mobius
Independent Russian conference of mobile application developers. In November of this year will be held in Moscow. No marketing, just technical reports, hardcore and a discussion of practical problems. Audience - developers, 80% of which are at the Middle and Senior level. By the way, today in Android Good Reads there was a promotional code for buying tickets for this event.
Organizers: JUG.ru.
Frequency: 2 times a year.

AppsConf
The conference is held as part of RIT ++ and is dedicated to Android and iOS technologies, cross-platform development, mobile application architecture, client-server interaction, and development and testing processes. Dedicated day for master classes.
Organizers: ontico.ru.
Periodicity: annually.

MBLTdev
The fourth conference of mobile developers MBLTdev 2017 is coming. You can hear both Russian and foreign speakers. Announce powerful reports on native and cross-platform development, stories about innovations and features of Android and iOS, as well as laboratory zones for everyone.
Organizers: e-Legion, RAEC.
Periodicity: annually.

MosDroid
Regular events for android developers who organize independent enthusiasts. Mitapy held at the sites of different companies. The guys promise to those present the exchange of knowledge, professional development and total socialization. This series of meetings comes from Moscow, but meetings also take place in St. Petersburg.
Organizers: Alexander Smirnov, Artem Kulakov, Grigory Dzhanelidze.
Periodicity: several times a year.

Shuffle rubric: review of the PocketBook 611 reader and dual-card Gigabyte Gsmart G1345 smartphone

Hello! Today we are pleased to introduce you to the new Droider Shuffle column, in which we will talk about two completely unconnected devices. And the guests of the first issue were a minimalist PocketBook Basic 611 reader and a smartphone with two SIM cards Gigabyte GSmart G1345 .



Let's start with the reader. In PocketBook Basic 611 there is practically nothing superfluous. The slim and stylish case consists of a 6-inch E-Ink screen, two buttons for browsing and a joystick. There is also a microSD connector and a microUSB port. Among the software there was a place for the browser (the device has WiFi ), calendar, rss-client and other simple applications.

There is no music player, but there is a pre-installed library with a large number of books in different languages. All major formats are supported: EPUB, PDF, TXT, DJVU, RTF, HTML, and FB2. Complementing the picture is the minimalist price of 5,690 p. Let's go to the smartphone.



Like many brothers in dvuhsimochnuyu class, Gsmart G1345 has a simple TFT screen with a diagonal of 3.5 inches and a resolution of 320x480. But the 800 MHz processor allocates the device among most competitors, the gadget works smoothly, despite the need to service two SIM cards. The Android version of version 2.3.4 is practically unchanged, but if you wish, you can install the update with the SPB Shell 3D shell.



The body of the smartphone consists mainly of glossy plastic and is not compact. But on the body of the gadget there was a place for the camera launch button, and inside - for the FM radio module. The price of the device is also quite acceptable in this class - 7,990 p .

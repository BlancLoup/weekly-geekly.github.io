Intel Compute Stick. 4 cores x86 + Windows 8 in the HDMI whistle



Another device, first shown by Intel at CES, is an HDMI-stick micro computer called the Intel Compute Stick . In a dense row of "whistles" the novelty stands out for its advancement. Judge for yourself: quad-core Intel Atom Z3735F (Bay Trail) processor with a maximum frequency of 1.8 GHz, 2 GB of RAM, 32 GB of internal storage, Wi-Fi 802.11, Bluetooth, expansion slot. Provides power from the USB port of the TV.

The device will be sold with Windows 8.1 preinstalled and Bing services. The cost of the Intel Compute Stick is $ 149, the start of sales is the current quarter.

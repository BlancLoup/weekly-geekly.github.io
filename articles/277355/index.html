To accommodate the equipment, we use several data centers and decided to create a series of materials about the life support system of one of them.

A bit about the DataPro project



The data center at Aviamotornaya 69 is the second data center built by DataPro, which specializes in providing services to commercial data centers.
International certification of Uptime Design and Facility means that the data center is designed and built in accordance with all applicable standards for the Tier III reliability category. The next level in 2016 will be Uptime Operations certification, confirming that all the procedures and procedures implemented in the data center comply with the requirements. Briefly about the levels of reliability
The higher the level of reliability of the data center, the more it costs during construction and operation. The cost of renting a rack in it cannot be small. However, such costs are not significant for business-critical applications and when choosing a data center, you need to pay attention to its certification. Only it can serve as a basis for statements that the data center really has a Tier III reliability level and actually corresponds to it. However, for many minor tasks, a data center with a Tier II level of reliability or even Tier I will suffice.

Data Processing Centers are designed to ensure the smooth operation of all of the company's critical IT processes. Underestimating and overestimating the effects of downtime have the same negative effect on costs, since financial losses are possible in both cases, either when the critical systems are idle and the business is stopped, or because of an unjustified overpayment for the service.


Tier I, according to the Uptime Institute, is a dedicated, non-redundant data center infrastructure. Usually this is one or two air conditioners and UPS without diesel. If something breaks down or not electricity - this is an emergency. Any service is easy to provide. Issue price of about $ 10,000 capital costs per rack 5 kW.

Tier II provides basic redundancy, that is, power and cooling systems have redundant components and powers. Usually there are several air conditioners in N + 1 mode, one industrial UPS and possibly diesel. Electricity is supplied through AVR from 1-2 inputs. Room - room or hangar, usually in the lease. Maintenance is often difficult or creates the danger of an accident during even routine maintenance. Issue price of about $ 20,000 capital costs per rack 5 kW.

Tier III means the reservation of all engineering subsystems, the possibility of carrying out any operations, scheduled repairs and equipment replacement without stopping services. This means N + 2 for air conditioners, 2 inputs per rack from different UPS systems, a reserve for diesel engines, two inputs and specially prepared premises in the property. The price of the issue is about $ 30,000 of the capital cost of a 5 kW rack without real estate prices.

Finally, Tier IV means fault tolerance, the ability to withstand a single unplanned emergency event, such as a fire or explosion.

If the data center of the first level does not provide for redundancy, then the second adds redundancy of the main engineering systems, but not the ‚Äúways of delivery‚Äù of the service ‚Äî electricity and data network channels. Reservation of the latter is implemented in the data center of the third level, which allows for maintenance work without stopping it. The fourth level assumes reservation (N + 1 and above) of all main systems and fault tolerance upon the occurrence of the worst event. The average estimated annual data center idle time varies from 28.8 hours for the first level to 0.8 hours for the fourth level data center, while the actual availability rate is from 99.67 to 99.99%.

Thus, in the data center with the Tier III reliability level, it is possible to simultaneously operate and maintain the data center, up to the replacement of the system components and the failed equipment, and the addition of systems. This makes it possible to carry out planned repair work and equipment replacement without stopping services ‚Äî imperceptible to customers.


The redundancy of all engineering subsystems (Concurrent Maintainability) significantly increases the reliability of the data center. In order to achieve this level, it is necessary to design and build two pipelines for the cooling system, to ensure that all equipment operates with spare capacity, taking into account the breakdown or preventive maintenance of the power supply system. In such a data center there are several ways of power distribution and cooling, but only one of them is active. In the Tier III data center, components are backed up using the ‚ÄúN + 1‚Äù scheme. As a result, the resiliency ratio is 99.982%, which is expressed in the possible downtime per year of 1.6 hours.

Data Center Power Supply


Power supply - the basis of reliable data center operation. It should be uninterrupted, for which uninterruptible power supplies (UPS) are traditionally used, and guaranteed, for which diesel generator sets (DGS) are used, supporting the work of the data center during a prolonged power outage.




The DataPro data center has two independent inputs of 10 kV from the Mosenergo substation, and the cables are laid in different collectors, provide the necessary electrical power to the facility. Power is supplied to consumers through a group of step-down transformers. These dry transformers are constantly in operation in the presence of power from the city substation.

Now the allowed and connected data center power is 6 MW. Another 8 MW of power is in the process of registration. The power supply of the data center is actually reserved under the 2N scheme. For example, even at a 100% load in the event of an accident of a power supply panel, transformer, DRUPS, the entire turbine hall will be powered from the second input / shield. Diesel rotary ups


Traditionally, in the event of the disappearance of the city network voltage, the UPS batteries must maintain the power supply of the equipment until the diesel generator set is fully started, which can provide the entire data center with electricity. This process usually takes from 20-30 seconds to 2 minutes, on which the power of the UPS and the capacity of the batteries are calculated.

The DataPro data center uses a different, more economical approach for its scale. The main components of the DataPro data center infrastructure were power supply solutions for diesel rotary UPSs manufactured by Hitec Power Protection (currently four units) and Euro-Diesel (two cars). A service contract with two different vendors allows you to optimize prices and compare services.

In essence, a diesel rotary or dynamic UPS (DRUPS) of these manufacturers is a diesel engine assembled coaxially on one shaft, an electromagnetic clutch, a synchronous electric machine and a dynamic flywheel. A decoupling choke connects the mains voltage input to the ‚Äúclean‚Äù voltage output of the UPS system. It provides a high degree of isolation between the input and output, blocking the passage of harmonics and transients.

If there is an external power supply, the UPS synchronous electric machine operates in the electric motor mode, rotating the rotor of the flywheel. When the parameters of the network outside the set limits, it goes into generator mode, maintaining a continuous sinusoidal voltage on the load. The source of energy during the transition process is the flywheel, thanks to which the stable frequency of rotation of the rotor shaft of the synchronous electric machine is maintained. A flywheel keeps up to one minute at full electric load without a diesel.

After the mains voltage fails, the DRUPS control system sends a signal to start the diesel engine. The kinetic energy of the flywheel, in addition to maintaining the output voltage, is used to output the diesel engine crankshaft to the operating frequency. After 50 ms after a power failure, the diesel engine begins to work, which within a few seconds goes into normal operation - the flywheel is already spinning.






Independent power supply of the facility in the event of a power failure from the substation is provided by diesel-rotary UPSs Hitec Power Protection of 1670 kVA with 4 / 3N redundancy scheme. And no batteries to power the IT load.

Moments of technical.

In the "Notes of the Military Topographical Depot" Part 10 of 1847. under the editorship of Major-General Tuchkov on p. 24, the highest approval (of April 2, 1845) on the compilation of a military topographic map of Russia was published. In this document, the projection parameters and three sheet sizes are indicated. This part freely lies in RuNet in the scans of the Munich Library - anyone can read this approval.

The projection of the three is a pseudo-conical isometric cartographic projection of Bonnet, the central meridian is Pulkovsky, the central parallel is 52 degrees. Sheet size is 23 x 16.5 inches. The spheroid is taken "... according to the findings of Professor Valbek from Abo, who defined (compressed) 302.78 and (the big semi-axis) 2988830,0024 ...", or how often it occurs in the "Notes of the VTD" - "For the figure of Valbek Earth taken for all the works of the Military Topographic Department ... the semi-axis = 2988853 fathoms, compression = 302.78 ".

The publication of map sheets was planned up to 28 pillars, but with the improvement of the quality of geodetic works at the beginning of the 20th century and the material base, the creation of new sheets was stopped, because the new standard of one-verst maps (Western Border Area) went into practice. The number of finished sheets for this time can be calculated on the Volarovich's form in 1909, indicating which sheets did not have time to do.

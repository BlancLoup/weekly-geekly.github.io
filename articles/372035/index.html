RoboAir - the world's first lawn mower robot rental service

Imagine how a lawnmower flies on the drone over the fields to mow a piece of land? Not? And soon it will be everywhere!

A major international manufacturer of robotic mowers Robomow has launched a new service RoboAir . The point is very simple: anyone can download the application and call a robot on their site, which will fly on a quadcopter, mow a piece and fly back. It looks like this:



The service works on the principle of AirBNB or Uber. It brings together two categories of users:

  • Those who need a Robomow, but for now there is no money (or just want to try);
  • Those who bought the robot, and do not mind it to earn extra money.

To get income from your robot, the owner of Robomow needs quite a bit:

  • Step 1. Register with the service and get a special drone from the company.
  • Step 2. Pay its rent in the amount of $ 10 per month + give 20% of your income (debited automatically).
  • Step 3. Relax. For passive earnings, nothing more is needed: now on its site a combat quadcopter is on duty around the clock, ready for departure.

The potential client only needs to download the application and create an order. When you make it, it denotes its plot by geotag in the calculator RoboMap . The program itself will determine the area of ‚Äã‚Äãthe site, find a suitable power in the district of the robot, calculate the cost of mowing and the estimated time of arrival. The required amount will be deducted from the linked bank card. It only remains to wait for the robot to arrive and turn the summer lawn into a nice English lawn.

The service has already been launched in the suburbs of Los Angeles and Miami, and by the end of the year it is planned to expand its geography to 34 US cities, including Washington's suburbs, and 9 European cities. In Russia, the launch of RoboAir will begin no earlier than the summer of 2017, but the Robomow lawn mowers themselves are already available in our country .

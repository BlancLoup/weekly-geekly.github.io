Iphone month


People who have not heard of the existence of the iPhone , with each such post is getting smaller. There is a lot of controversy about an innovative phone, or rather, a smartphone, from Apple , and relative opinions about devices too. Some people, whether dear Eldar Murtazin ( mobile-review.com ) or the less popular Anton Nosik ( dolboeb.livejournal.com ), are perplexed about the popularity of the device, calling the iPhone "a weak solution both programmatically and technically ." Most Mac users and iPods who understand the company's ideology still think differently. I have been using the iPhone for almost a month now and want to share my impressions with you.

Impressions


First and foremost, the longer you use your iPhone , the more you like it. This is not advertising, it is the most real universal impression. Usually the opposite is true - you buy a smartphone / communicator and the longer you use it, the more you become disappointed. IPhone is different.
When I just had a phone in my hands - I wanted to have more features, a communicator on Windows Mobile or a smartphone on Symbian replaced it, I wanted the phone to be just a phone, because most of the functions were poorly implemented. The phone should not slow down, build multi-level chains of menu items to access something simple. In the case of the iPhone - this is true. As they say, It just works .
An almost perfect combination of a multifunctional device and a phone, as well as an innovative method of device management, are two main components of iPhone success. Its potential is huge - we will all see it as soon as Steve Jobs reveals the promised SDK in February '08 and hundreds of new applications appear.

Equipment


The kit supplied with the device is simple and concise - just as much as is necessary for comfortable use of the device.
It includes, in fact, the iPhone itself, a wired headset (we'll talk about it separately), a Dock , a USB cable (the same as a regular iPod ), a USB Power Adapter for recharging from the mains, documentation, and a cloth to wipe the screen .
All you need to synchronize with a Mac / PC , as well as travel - fits in your pocket and is a kind of designer. Judge for yourself - you can plug a USB cable on one side, both on the iPhone and the Dock , and on the other, both on the USB port of a Mac / PC and on the USB Power Adapter . In case you just want to charge the iPhone of your choice there are 4 design options for recharging. Apple always leaves a choice

Appearance


Let's talk about the appearance of the device. Every familiar person who saw the iPhone , took it more than positive reviews. The iPhone is more than a regular phone, and in its dimensions tends to the communicator. The device is light ( 135 grams ) and thin ( 11.6 mm ), therefore communication with it causes only positive emotions. An important feature is that the iPhone case is monolithic, so it creates the impression of something finished and completed. No battery covers with backlash - Uncle Steve saved us from such unpleasant moments. Of course, the iPhone battery can not be changed independently (besides, it still needs to be bought, but for now it is unrealistic), but how long the replacement of the battery does not require depends on how to handle the device. Without a doubt, a year or even two years of normal life are provided. During this time, most likely, there will be more than one new version of the device, which will be queued for purchase. Due to the fact that the iPhone is not for sale in Russia, and also due to the lack of appropriate batteries in the service centers of Apple dealers, this can be considered an omission.

Working hours


The battery lasts an average of one and a half days, taking into account 10-20 minutes of calls, watching two or three episodes of the series, 3-4 hours of music and constantly on Wi-Fi . The result is quite decent, and if we take into account the fact that the iPhone can be recharged during synchronization, then you can almost forget about timely charging. The power saving system in the iPhone is implemented simply - the display turns off after a few seconds if the device is in lock mode, or it goes from standby to lock mode after the time specified by the user in the settings (1-5 minutes).

Display


On the front surface of the device you can find only a speaker, a display and the so-called " Home Button ", a button that returns the device to the main screen. Since the device was originally designed for use with the Multitouch technology , the control element of which are fingers, Apple very competently approached the screen protection by placing it under a special scratch-resistant glass.
The following video demonstrates the scratch resistance of a similar iPod Touch glass.

The display makes you happy from the first seconds of exploring it - the colors are bright and saturated, a diagonal of 3.5 inches and a resolution of 320x480 pixels make you feel comfortable, whatever you do - from surfing with Safari to watching movies with iPod .
Since the display above and below is surrounded by black, when watching a video, it seems that it is somewhat larger than it really is.

Headset

The included stereo headset is striking in its simplicity. It is very similar to ordinary iPod headphones, with the exception of the talk button. Immediately and do not pay attention to it, if you do not know what it is. In a small button about 2x0.5x0.25 cm in size, the headset microphone and control element are located. This button is responsible for the operations " Pick up / hang up ", " Play / Pause " in the iPod - parts of the phone, and also with a double click switches to the next song in the playlist 'e.
However, the ears themselves are not as good as we would like. The sound in them is far from the desired ideal, although not as bad as you might think. In addition, they always strive to fall out of the ears, at the slightest touch to the headset wires, which may be accidental.




With the help of simple manipulations with a soldering iron, they have recently been replaced by Koss Plugs, which are closer to the heart. Now the headset sits tight in the ears and provides decent sound quality. The noise of the subway remains somewhere far behind, thanks to the good sound insulation of the Plugs .

Software


While Steve Jobs is only announcing a full- featured SDK for the iPhone in February next year, many enthusiasts are already creating programs on their own. No matter how revolutionary the idea of Web 2.0 would have been, even Steve realized that online applications are limited to browser functionality and cannot compete with applications for OS X itself (as we remember, a special trimmed version of Leopard was installed on the iPhone ).
In order to run third-party applications on the iPhone , the first step is to do the so-called JailBreak (jailbreak), which will enable non- Apple programs to work through the holes found in the system. At the moment, JailBreak is feasible, both for the iPhone (even with ominous firmware 1.1.1) and for the iPod Touch .
After that, the AppInstaller application (or simply the Installer ) is installed on the iPhone , which independently, without the participation of a computer, allows you to select and install applications directly from the public repository via Wi-Fi or Edge . Of course, after installing third-party applications there may be malfunctions in the operation of the device, therefore, installing them, you take responsibility for yourself.
There is a lot of third-party software, but for the most part it is stupid. Of the total programs, I can only mention the Russian Keyboard (the global system hack from Slava Karpenko , which gives happiness to all Russian-speaking iPhone users), Terminal (which is somewhat limited in its functionality), the NES emulator (funny but uncomfortable to play), Books (scroll the book finger is very unusual)
and SummerBoard ( iPhone interface tuning).

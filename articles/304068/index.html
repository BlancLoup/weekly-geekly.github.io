"Cloud Technologies in Russia" - the first joint forum of Huawei and RUVDS was held in Moscow



Huawei and RUVDS , recently forging an increasingly close partnership in the field of cloud technologies, held the first joint thematic forum on June 23. During the event, participants and guests of the forum were able to discuss the present and the future of the cloud sphere in our country, the existing problems in the industry and the possible ways to solve them.

The Moscow Hotel Lotte Plaza, selected as a forum, accommodated more than a hundred guests - recognized IT experts, representatives of government-related structures, and businessmen.
‚ÄúWe set the task to create a platform where providers and consumers of cloud services could meet, technical specialists share experience in using the most advanced technologies, and business representatives to discover new opportunities for themselves,‚Äù said Nikita Tsaplin, managing partner of RUVDS, during the welcome speech.



The forum opened with a plenary session ‚ÄúThe development of cloud technologies in Russia. New reality: vectors of development and major problems ‚Äù, during which the speakers discussed the main trends in the development of cloud technologies, the benefits of application, as well as the features and difficulties of implementation in a number of business areas, in particular construction.

The representative of ZAO Dynamo CJSC, Alexander Kustarev, a company that is currently engaged in construction of VTB Arena Park, said that there is almost no software in Russia to solve the problems of the construction sector, they have to do a lot. Currently, VTB Arena Park has its own automated document management system, its own data center, where all contractors and subcontractors are registered, which increases the pace of construction several times. Also, Dynamo Management Company is developing a multi-service system for stadium maintenance, which it plans to replicate in the future. Operation, maintenance, information security of large stadiums, stations, airports and other large public facilities can not be transferred to the cloud storage, according to the resolution 10.53, to counter terrorism. Therefore, there are restrictions on the use of cloud storage in government structures.

The deputy director of MFD-InfoCenter, Konstantin Ivaylovsky said that if the market competition is low and the software maturity is also low, then the solution will have to be created by ourselves, if the competition is high and the software maturity is high, then definitely it needs to be bought, as there is a very low threshold to enter.

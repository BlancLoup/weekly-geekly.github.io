Ultrabook Review MicroXperts MXP U400


The next review of the next ultrabook will be a bit unusual. In Russia, they rarely produce independently, preferring to sell finished products. The process of self-assembly of imported components can be called production, and if so, this review will be devoted to the Russian production Ultra MicroXperts MXP U400-02. Instead of a boring story about a stylish white cardboard box in which an ultrabook is packed, I‚Äôll tell a little about the manufacturer. The MicroXperts trademark has been on the market for 25 years. Initially, it was registered in the United States and in 1993, one of the first assembly plants in Russia was opened in St. Petersburg. In the 90s, MicroXperts personal computers were sold in the largest stores in the Northern Capital. Many large government and commercial institutions were corporate brand customers. The story is silent about the reasons for the decline in the company's activities, but a new round of development began in 2009 after the brand was acquired by Ulmart. A wide range of computers produced recently replenished with portable devices, which will be discussed.

Externally, the ultrabook is not a cast, rather even strict, because it is made entirely in black. The design is non-unique, the platform is made by Lengda and is called X300V. The diagonal of the monitor is 14 inches.



The top cover, or surface A, is made of aluminum. Right in the middle, the MXP logo is applied on it using laser engraving.


Keyboard island type, the backlight is missing, the keys are quite convenient. It is worth noting the full-sized cursor keys, as habrayuders often in the comments complain about the small size of the cursor buttons. Russian and English letters are applied in one color - white. It‚Äôs hard to say whether it‚Äôs a plus or a minus, because the MacBook also has one color of letters. The movement of the buttons is soft, but pressing is clearly audible.



The following ports and connectors are located on the right side of the ultrabook: combo audio, USB 3.0, HDMI, LAN, power adapter jack. The LAN port is closed with a rubber plug, a slightly strange decision, because the ultrabook does not claim to be ‚Äúprotected‚Äù and the rest of the connectors are not covered by anything.


The left side of the MicroXperts MXP U400 ultrabook contains a VGA connector, two USB 2.0 ports and a card reader that can read SD / SDHC / MMC




The back side is reserved for the ventilation holes through which the cooling system ejects hot air.



The bottom side of the ultrabook is made of a single piece of plastic, and just get to the inside will not work. In total, the cover is fixed with 18 (!) Screws of different lengths. When assembling / disassembling, it is very important not to confuse them in places, otherwise the plastic of the surface C, the one where the keyboard is located, can swell significantly.


Package delivery is very modest: user manual and charger. Nishtyak lovers do not profit from anything, but on the price of the product is not reflected.



The power supply is not perfect in terms of dimensions, but it is already close to the best samples.



We dig inside. A good half of the insides is occupied by the battery. This fact gives hope for good battery life.


The system has an SSD disk from Intel. No compromises and hybrid storage systems, just pure performance.


The battery capacity is 7800 mAh. Another hint of "vitality."


The front panel is studded with LEDs like a Christmas tree. Here and the power indicator, and the hard drive activity indicator, and the charge indicator, and Caps Lock, and NumLock, and even the wireless activity indicator.

The cooling system consists of a heat pipe, uniform for the chipset and processor, and a fan manufactured by SEPA. This is where the minus lies - at high revs, read when the CPU is loaded, the roar of the fan becomes very noticeable.

An 8-gigabyte DDR3 memory module is installed in the only allocated slot on the motherboard. I'm not sure if it makes sense to install more than 8 gigabytes of RAM into a laptop, but with the advent of 16 gigabyte slats, the owners of MicroXperts MXP U400 will have a chance to try to double the memory.

The chipset on which the motherboard is built - Intel HM76, has a maximum heat dissipation of 4.1 W, that is, in general, the system emits 21.1 W of heat, a very modest figure, but not minimal.


Intel's SSD drive operates in SATA II mode, the presence of a hidden R drive: allows you to talk about the pre-installed system for restoring Windows.


The graphics core of Intel HD Graphics 4000 is well known, as it is found in all ultrabooks with integrated video.

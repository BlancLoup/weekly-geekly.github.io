SSD reliability statistics

Some time ago, I created a survey on the reliability of SSD. Despite the fact that it seems to me that there are too few results, there is not much point in waiting.

So, for a start, size statistics.

The picture shows that the most popular formats are 120, 80 and 60, which are produced only by Intel.
You may also note that 120/128 disks are especially popular among users of alternative operating systems (including OS X).

It is interesting here that we do not have so many results for different controllers, but nevertheless, the following conclusions can be made.
- Indilinx Barefoot is not very reliable
- SF-1200 is not very reliable
- The SF-2200 is much better, but the number of returns is nonetheless large.
- both Marvel and intel are relatively reliable

Recommendations to the buyer.
Which SSD is better to choose? It is worth saying that there is no "better choice". Some discs are better in some cases, some in others.
According to the results we can conclude that the drives on the SF 1200 and SF 2200 should not be taken. Indilinx discs are definitely not worth taking. The disks that are worth taking to rid yourself of nightmares are disks with Intel and Marvel controllers. These are the Intel X25-M, Intel 320, Intel 510, Crucial C300 and Crucial M4. Well, more discs for which there is not much data, Kingston V 100, 470 and Samsung. I recommend, nevertheless, to think anyway, and to take information from this topic only as a reference, since the sample is not large.

Enterprise applications also want to work on mobile devices. Chat, social networks and toys, although very common, have already saturated the market. Users do not care what kind of chip in their smartphone, and rightly so. But there are applications that do not care. They have always existed in the PC world, used the power of Core processors and felt at ease in 64-bit space. Imagine a large enterprise, as usual, with warehouses, invoices, taking into account incoming and outgoing written documentation, reports, protocols, and other logistic joys. And it all works under the control of solutions, for example, from SAP or Oracle. So, a huge amount of costs comes from semi-manual input of information, special and simple scanners, remote readers, etc. But all this functionality can be placed in the usual smart phone for $ 300, which would have each employee (camera and NFC-chip as sensors). It is enough to place on it a front-end application that will do all the work without special expensive devices, and all specialization will be determined by the software allowed on a particular phone, with access rights, protection and other necessary attributes.

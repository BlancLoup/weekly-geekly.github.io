YouTube has put comedy shows back into place.

The video hosting YouTube is free again hosted clips, rights to which belong to Comedy Central .

YouTube and Viacom (CBS Corporation) entered into an agreement whereby files containing excerpts from popular American television shows (such as South Park, Laguna Beach and SpongeBob SquarePants) can be freely shared by all users.

Recall that last week these files were deleted . Viacom lawyers sent a letter to YouTube owners with a request to remove materials, because their free placement infringes the copyright of Comedy Central.

Thus, the most popular video hosting has continued to follow the traditional policy - not to delete content, but to enter into agreements with holders of compensation payments.
